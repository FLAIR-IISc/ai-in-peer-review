- Proposes a hierarchical RL pipeline: pre-train a single stochastic neural-network (SNN) conditioned on a discrete latent to induce multimodal, temporally consistent skills, then learn a per-task high-level manager to select skills.  
- SNN uses bilinear integration of observations and latent codes, is trained with TRPO, and uses an MI bonus I(Z; C) on behaviorally relevant state features (e.g., CoM) to encourage diverse, interpretable skills.  
- Experiments on locomotion-with-mazes and gather tasks show broader exploration and improved sample efficiency vs strong intrinsic-motivation baselines, with code, videos, and ablations provided.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Key reason: Clear, well-motivated method combining multi-modal SNNs and a simple MI diversity bonus yields reusable, interpretable skills that improve exploration and sparse-task performance.  
- Key reason: Strong empirical support across multiple downstream tasks, including ablations (e.g., concatenation vs bilinear, MI strength, switch time).  
- Novelty: Compact multi-skill representation via one SNN with weight sharing, MI regularizer on behaviorally relevant features, and a practical two-stage pipeline for transfer.  
- Support: Qualitative visitation maps, quantitative learning curves with multiple seeds, and ablations back the stated claims within the studied domains.  
- Sample-efficiency claim: Training K skills in one SNN amortizes cost and gives K-fold savings compared to training K separate policies; managers reuse low-level policies without fine-tuning.  
- Concern: Diversity scaling is unclear—skill collapse or redundancy may grow as K increases beyond the small K tested (e.g., 6).  
- Concern: MI estimation relies on discretized CoM grids; sensitivity to grid resolution and applicability to high-dimensional continuous C are not fully explored.  
- Concern: Generality to complex morphologies is limited—instability on Ant suggests switching and embodiment sensitivity requiring extra mechanisms.  
- Concern: Proxy rewards (e.g., CoM speed) are hand-crafted and domain-dependent, so the “minimal domain knowledge” claim is overstated without broader evidence.  
- Feedback: Quantify skill diversity (MI estimates, coverage, pairwise distinguishability) and show how diversity scales with K and αH.  
- Feedback: Ablate MI estimation choices (grid resolution, density/classifier-based estimators) and report computational/stability trade-offs.  
- Feedback: Explore robust switching mechanisms (option termination, transition models, adaptive switch times) to mitigate instability for complex agents.  
- Feedback: Include an end-to-end fine-tuning study (e.g., Gumbel-Softmax) to evaluate benefits versus stability concerns.  
- Feedback: Demonstrate alternative proxy rewards and a non-locomotion domain (e.g., manipulation) to strengthen the minimal-knowledge claim.  
- Feedback: Report total samples for pre-training plus per-task training and quantify how many downstream tasks are needed for pre-training to “pay off.”  
- Question: How do performance and skill diversity change as K increases (e.g., K=10, 20), and how effectively does αH prevent mode collapse?  
- Question: Why discretize CoM for MI rather than use continuous estimators; how sensitive are results to grid resolution and count-smoothing?  
- Question: How do results change if C includes heading, velocities, or other features, and how should C be chosen in domains without low-dim summaries?  
- Question: Can simple, domain-agnostic proxy rewards produce reusable skills outside locomotion; what principles guide proxy reward design?  
- Question: For unstable agents (e.g., Ant), can failure modes at switches be quantified and improved by simple mitigations (warm-up, restricted switches, transition-aware managers)?  
- Question: What is the total pre-training budget relative to per-task gains and how many tasks are required before pre-training yields net sample savings?  
- Question: Have you tried a recurrent manager to leverage within-skill history, and does it improve long-horizon navigation performance?
