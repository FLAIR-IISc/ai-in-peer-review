- Summary: Proposes hierarchical RL with MI-regularized SNN pre-trained low-level skills and a high-level manager switching skills with fixed commitment time; uses bilinear latent integration and weight sharing; shows strong empirical results and transfer (e.g., Snake).

- Decision: Accept.

- Reason: Strong empirical performance on previously unsolved sparse tasks with a simple, general pre-train/transfer pipeline; MI-regularized SNN skill learning is elegant and useful.

- Supporting 1 (Problem): Tackles acquiring reusable, diverse low-level skills in minimal pre-training and leveraging them for long-horizon sparse downstream tasks.

- Supporting 2 (Placement): Well positioned relative to options/skill discovery and prior pre-training, but calling proxy-reward pre-training “intrinsic motivation” is misleading (task-aligned, similar to goal-conditioned pre-training).

- Supporting 3 (Evidence/rigor): Results are compelling and code is provided, but analysis is weak where it matters—MI often increases diversity but not performance on harder mazes; switching dynamics and S_agent/S_rest factorization are under-specified.

- Feedback 1: Clarify and formalize the S = S_agent ⊕ S_rest factorization per domain and list state variables for replication.

- Feedback 2: Provide deeper MI analysis: report MI/entropy metrics, posterior estimation details (grid resolution, smoothing), and sensitivity to α_H; explain why MI fails to help mazes 1–3.

- Feedback 3: Analyze switching behavior more thoroughly: distributions over skill usage, sensitivity to commitment time T, per-skill success/failure, and detailed failure modes (e.g., unstable agents).

- Feedback 4: Consider termination policies or transition-aware training to address switching-induced instability.

- Feedback 5: Avoid using “intrinsic motivation” unless the pre-training uses task-agnostic curiosity/novelty rewards.

- Question 1: Precisely which variables constitute S_rest in each task, and are there stronger-than-claimed interactions with S_agent?

- Question 2: How sensitive are results to choices of K, α_H, MI grid resolution, and bilinear vs. concatenation for latent integration?

- Question 3: Why does MI not improve performance on mazes 1–3—over-specialized turning skills or manager credit-assignment issues?

- Question 4: Can you report quantitative switching statistics (skill dwell times, usage entropy) and correlate them with task success?

- Question 5: Have you tried learned termination policies or end-to-end fine-tuning to mitigate switching instability (e.g., on Ant)?
