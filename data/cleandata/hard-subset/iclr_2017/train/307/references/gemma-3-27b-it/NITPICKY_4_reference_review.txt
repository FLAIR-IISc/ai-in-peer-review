This paper introduces a suite of tasks designed to evaluate end-to-end dialog systems in a goal-oriented setting, specifically restaurant reservation. The authors argue that existing evaluation methods are lacking and propose a controlled environment, grounded in a knowledge base, to systematically assess capabilities like dialog management, KB querying, and entity handling. They present results using Memory Networks, comparing performance on synthetic data and real human-machine dialogs, highlighting areas where end-to-end models still fall short compared to traditional slot-filling approaches.

**Reject.** While the motivation for a more rigorous evaluation of end-to-end dialog systems is sound, and the proposed tasks are a reasonable first step, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor yet distracting flaws in presentation, experimental detail, and analysis. These issues, while not individually devastating, collectively undermine confidence in the results and the overall contribution. The presentation feels rushed and lacks the polish expected for a top-tier conference like ICLR.

The primary issue lies in the sheer number of inconsistencies and omissions. For example, the description of the rule-based baseline is vague; what specific rules were implemented, and what was the precise optimization procedure on the validation set? The hyperparameter search space for the Memory Networks is not detailed, making it difficult to assess the robustness of the results.  Table 1 lists statistics for the datasets but lacks units for party size (is it a count, or a range?). The description of the “match type features” is convoluted and could benefit from a more intuitive explanation and illustration.  Moreover, the constant referral to "Appendix" without consistently providing complete information within the main text is frustrating. Several figures are referenced (e.g., Figure 1) but their relevance isn't always immediately clear from the text. The text refers to "Table 10 in Appendix D" but then proceeds to discuss it as if it's readily available in the main body of the paper.  The conclusion is weak; it restates observations without offering concrete future directions or a compelling argument for the significance of the work, beyond acknowledging current limitations. The formatting is also sloppy, with inconsistent spacing and punctuation throughout. There are several instances where the same information is repeated unnecessarily. 

The analysis feels superficial. While the authors observe that Memory Networks struggle with per-dialog accuracy, they don't delve deeply into *why*. Is it a failure of KB integration, dialog state tracking, or something else? The examples in Appendix B are helpful, but more systematic error analysis is needed. The claim that the synthetic tasks correlate well with real data (DSTC2 and the concierge dataset) is asserted but not rigorously demonstrated; a more detailed comparison of error patterns would strengthen this claim.  The paper states that the datasets are available at http://fb.ai/babi, but I was unable to locate the datasets there at the time of review.

**Additional Feedback (for improvement, not part of the rejection decision):**

*   **Clarity and Conciseness:** Rewrite the introduction to be more focused and less repetitive. Streamline the descriptions of the tasks and models.
*   **Experimental Details:** Provide a complete and precise description of all experimental settings, including hyperparameter search spaces, optimization algorithms, and evaluation metrics.
*   **Error Analysis:** Conduct a more thorough error analysis to identify the root causes of failures, particularly for the per-dialog accuracy metric.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Perform ablation studies to assess the contribution of each component of the Memory Network architecture, including the match type features.
*   **Dataset Availability:** Confirm the availability of the datasets at the provided link.
*   **Formatting:** Proofread the paper carefully to correct inconsistencies in formatting and punctuation.
*   **Appendices:** Either integrate key information from the appendices into the main text or ensure that the appendices are self-contained and clearly referenced.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a complete list of the rules implemented in the rule-based baseline and detail the optimization procedure used to tune their parameters?
2.  What was the hyperparameter search space for the Memory Networks, and how were the optimal hyperparameters selected?
3.  What specific error patterns were observed in the synthetic and real datasets, and how do they differ?
4.  Are the datasets truly available at http://fb.ai/babi? If so, could you provide instructions on how to access them?
5.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of vocabulary size and embedding dimension?
