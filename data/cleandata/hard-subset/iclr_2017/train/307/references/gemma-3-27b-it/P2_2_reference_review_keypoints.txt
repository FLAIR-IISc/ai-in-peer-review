- Introduces a five-task benchmark based on a restaurant reservation KB to evaluate goal-oriented end-to-end dialog systems, arguing existing datasets are insufficient for reproducible, scalable, interpretable evaluation.  
- Validates the benchmark by evaluating Memory Networks against rule-based and IR baselines and by testing on DSTC2 and a proprietary concierge dataset.  
- Core finding: end-to-end models show promise but underperform traditional methods on reliable goal completion, especially in KB integration and multi-turn reasoning.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — benchmark addresses an important gap and experiments offer clear insights and directions.  
- Motivation: Clearly articulates weaknesses of current datasets (noisy, state-focused, poor reproducibility).  
- Approach: Decomposes goal-oriented dialog into subtasks (API calling, KB interpretation) enabling targeted error analysis; simulated KB ensures control and reproducibility.  
- Experiments: Well-designed comparisons with strong baselines provide a clear picture of Memory Networks’ strengths and weaknesses.  
- Validation: Real-world dataset evaluation (DSTC2, proprietary) strengthens relevance of findings.  
- Results: Per-response accuracy is promising but per-dialog accuracy remains low; match-type features notably improve OOV performance.  
- Task complexity: Request for more detailed analysis of difficulty levels across tasks, particularly Task 5 (full dialogs).  
- Memory Network details: Ask for expanded hyperparameter tuning information (number of hops, embedding dimensions).  
- Error analysis: Recommend deeper error analysis with specific failure examples and categorization to explain per-dialog accuracy drops.  
- KB scalability: Suggest exploring effects of larger and more complex KBs to assess scalability.  
- Limitations: Request more comprehensive discussion of simulated-environment biases and generalizability to other domains.  
- Questions: Why prioritize Memory Networks over other architectures (e.g., Transformers)?  
- Questions: Please elaborate on match-type feature design and alternative ways to incorporate type information.  
- Questions: Can you quantify the effort to build competitive rule-based systems for DSTC2 versus the simulated tasks?  
- Questions: Is the benchmark generalizable to other goal-oriented domains or would significant modifications be needed?
