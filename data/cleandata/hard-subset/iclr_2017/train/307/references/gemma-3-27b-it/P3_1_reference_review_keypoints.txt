- Introduces a diagnostic benchmark of five tasks and two real-world datasets to evaluate goal-oriented end-to-end dialogue systems.  
- Provides a controlled, reproducible environment targeting skills like API querying, KB interpretation, and OOV handling.  
- Demonstrates the benchmark using Memory Networks and compares performance across tasks and datasets.  
- Highlights that per-response accuracy is reasonable but per-dialog accuracy remains low, revealing limits of current end-to-end models.  
- Intends the contribution as a diagnostic tool rather than a new state-of-the-art model.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to the benchmark’s clear value and reproducibility.  
- Well-defined problem statement addressing shortcomings of existing evaluation methods for goal-oriented dialogue.  
- Strong motivation for a task-level diagnostic benchmark to better understand model capabilities.  
- Solid experimental setup using both synthetic simulations and real datasets (DSTC2, concierge) with an insightful OOV test set.  
- Clear presentation and analysis, including comparisons across rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, and Memory Networks.  
- Authors prioritize reproducibility by releasing the dataset and detailing the experimental setup.  
- Dataset scale is relatively small; the paper should discuss training limitations and scaling directions.  
- Memory Network hyperparameters (e.g., number of hops) seem crucial and need a more detailed sensitivity analysis.  
- A more granular error analysis with concrete failure examples would improve actionable insights.  
- Discussion of alternative architectures (e.g., Transformers) is missing and would contextualize the choice of Memory Networks.  
- More details on the concierge dataset’s extraction and potential biases are needed.  
- Question: What are the main bottlenecks and plans for scaling the synthetic dataset?  
- Question: Can the authors provide concrete examples and categorization of common per-dialog failure cases?  
- Question: Why were Memory Networks chosen over Transformers—computational limits or specific hypotheses?  
- Question: What steps were taken to mitigate biases in the concierge dataset?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the quality, completeness, or noise in the knowledge base?
