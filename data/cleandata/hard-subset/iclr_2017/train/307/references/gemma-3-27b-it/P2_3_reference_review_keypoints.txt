- Introduces five synthetic tasks plus two real-world datasets (DSTC2 and a concierge dataset) to benchmark goal-oriented end-to-end dialog systems.  
- Motivates the need for standardized, reproducible benchmarks for goal-directed dialog beyond chit-chat.  
- Provides a controllable environment to diagnose dialog system skills like KB querying and dialog management.  
- Evaluates Memory Networks as a baseline, highlighting strengths and weaknesses and establishing a reference point for future work.  
- Synthetic tasks are well-designed, progressively increasing in complexity and include standard and out-of-vocabulary splits to test generalization.  
- Thorough experiments compare MemNNs to rule-based, IR, and supervised embedding baselines across synthetic and real datasets.  
- Results show end-to-end models struggle with complex reasoning and KB interpretation, with consistent performance trends across datasets.  
- Authors prioritize reproducibility by making datasets and tasks publicly available.  
- Analysis identifies specific failure modes (e.g., difficulty interpreting KB results), useful for guiding future research.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — valuable resource addressing a significant gap.  
- Suggestion: Provide clearer justification for task complexity and the chosen task sequence (1–5).  
- Suggestion: Discuss MemNN hyperparameter sensitivity and whether different tasks needed different configs.  
- Suggestion: Add deeper error analysis with concrete examples of incorrect predictions and causes.  
- Suggestion: Briefly compare MemNN performance to state-of-the-art slot-filling results on DSTC2, even if not directly comparable.  
- Question: Why were Memory Networks chosen as the primary end-to-end model and were architectures like Transformers considered?  
- Question: What anonymization techniques were used for the concierge dataset and could they introduce biases?  
- Question: How was the 1,000-example training set size determined and was an ablation on training size conducted?  
- Question: Can authors provide specific dialog examples where the Memory Network failed, indicating error types and likely reasons?
