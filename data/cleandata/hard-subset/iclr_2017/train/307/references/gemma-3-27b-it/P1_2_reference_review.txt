## Review of "Goal-Oriented Dialog Tasks"

**1. Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel benchmark for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in a goal-oriented setting – restaurant reservation. Recognizing the limitations of existing datasets (scale, noise, reproducibility) and the difficulty of directly comparing end-to-end and traditional slot-filling approaches, the authors propose a set of five synthetic tasks grounded in a knowledge base. These tasks progressively test critical capabilities like API querying, knowledge base interpretation, and dialog management.  Crucially, the authors also validate the synthetic tasks against two real-world datasets (DSTC2 and a proprietary concierge service) to demonstrate their correlation with real-world performance. They benchmark Memory Networks on these tasks, showing promising, but not complete, performance. The paper’s key contribution is providing a controllable, reproducible environment for analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of end-to-end dialog models in a goal-oriented context.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper addresses a significant gap in the evaluation of end-to-end dialog systems for goal-oriented tasks. The proposed benchmark is well-motivated, thoughtfully designed, and validated against real-world data, making it a valuable resource for the community. While the performance of the tested Memory Networks isn’t state-of-the-art, the paper’s focus is not on pushing performance boundaries but on creating a rigorous evaluation platform.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented scenarios. The argument for needing a controlled environment to isolate specific capabilities is compelling.
* **Strong Technical Contribution:** The designed tasks are well-defined, progressively complex, and cover essential aspects of goal-oriented dialog. The use of a knowledge base and the OOV split adds valuable complexity and realism.  The task breakdown allows for targeted error analysis.
* **Rigorous Evaluation:**  The validation against DSTC2 and the proprietary concierge data strongly supports the claim that the synthetic tasks correlate with real-world performance. The inclusion of both per-response and per-dialog accuracy provides a nuanced evaluation.
* **Reproducibility:**  Making the data and code publicly available (http://fb.ai/babi) is a major strength, fostering further research and comparison.
* **Well-written & Organized:** The paper is generally well-written, with a clear structure and logical flow. The detailed explanation of the tasks and experimental setup is helpful.



**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Discussion of Limitations:** While the paper acknowledges the limitations of the synthetic data, a more in-depth discussion of potential biases or unrealistic assumptions would be beneficial. For example, the deterministic order of questions in Task 1 might not fully reflect real user behavior.
* **Ablation Study:** While the results with and without match-type features are presented, a more comprehensive ablation study exploring the impact of different Memory Network components (e.g., number of hops, embedding size) would provide further insights.
* **Error Analysis:**  More detailed examples of error analysis beyond those in Appendix B would be valuable. Specifically, categorizing the types of errors the Memory Networks make (e.g., KB querying errors, interpretation errors, response generation errors) would inform future research directions.
* **Hyperparameter Search Details:** Providing more details on the hyperparameter search process (e.g., search space, optimization algorithm) would increase the reproducibility of the experiments.
* **Connection to POMDPs:** The introduction briefly mentions POMDPs. Expanding on how the proposed tasks relate to challenges in POMDP-based dialog systems would strengthen the motivation and contextualize the work.



**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* **Task Complexity and Realism:** You mention the deterministic question order in Task 1. How do you anticipate this simplifying assumption might affect the generalizability of results to more realistic scenarios? Do you have plans to introduce more variability in future iterations of the benchmark?
* **KB Size & Complexity:**  How sensitive are the results to the size and complexity of the knowledge base? Could increasing the number of restaurants, locations, and cuisine types impact the performance of the models?
* **Concierge Dataset Details:** Could you provide a bit more detail about the process of converting the concierge data into the task format? Specifically, how were API calls inferred from the human operator actions?
* **Negative Sampling Strategy:** In the Supervised Embedding models, what specific negative sampling strategy was used during training?
* **Future Work:** What future extensions or additions to this benchmark are you considering?  Could you envision incorporating tasks that require more complex reasoning or long-term planning?
