## ICLR 2024 Review

**Paper:** A Dataset for Evaluating Goal-Oriented Dialog Systems

**1. Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenge of evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented scenarios. The authors argue that existing datasets are either not suitable for end-to-end learning, lack reproducibility, or are difficult to interpret.  To address this, they propose a new, open-source dataset and a set of five tasks grounded in a restaurant reservation domain. These tasks are designed to test specific capabilities crucial for goal-oriented dialog, like KB querying, dialog management, and handling out-of-vocabulary entities. They also evaluate their tasks on real human-machine dialogs from DSTC2 and a proprietary concierge service, demonstrating that performance on the synthetic tasks correlates with real-world performance. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their framework by evaluating Memory Networks and establishing baselines.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a valuable contribution to the community by providing a well-motivated and systematically designed benchmark for evaluating goal-oriented dialog systems. The correlation with real-world datasets strengthens its usefulness.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the shortcomings of existing evaluation methods and datasets for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems. The motivation for creating a new benchmark is well-established.
* **Well-Designed Tasks:** The proposed tasks are thoughtfully designed to isolate and evaluate core dialog capabilities. The breakdown into subtasks facilitates error analysis and allows for targeted improvements to models. The inclusion of an OOV test set is particularly valuable, addressing a common challenge in real-world applications.
* **Strong Empirical Evaluation:** The authors perform a thorough evaluation of several methods, including rule-based systems, IR baselines, supervised embeddings, and Memory Networks. The comparison provides valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of different approaches.
* **Correlation with Real Data:** The evaluation on DSTC2 and the concierge dataset demonstrates that the synthetic tasks are a reasonable proxy for real-world performance, increasing confidence in the benchmark's validity and usefulness.
* **Open Resource:** Making the dataset and code publicly available is a significant contribution to the research community and promotes reproducibility.

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Discussion of Limitations:** While the paper acknowledges some limitations, a more thorough discussion of potential drawbacks of the synthetic environment would be beneficial. For instance, the simplified KB and natural language patterns may not fully capture the complexity of real-world interactions.
* **Ablation Studies:** While the results with and without match-type features are presented, providing a more detailed ablation study for the Memory Network architecture (e.g., varying the number of hops, embedding dimensions) could offer further insights.
* **Error Analysis:** A more in-depth error analysis of the Memory Network's failures would be helpful.  Specifically, pointing to concrete examples of types of requests where the model consistently struggles would guide future research.
* **Future Work:** Expanding on potential avenues for future work, such as incorporating more complex KB structures or exploring reinforcement learning techniques, would strengthen the paper.

**Questions for the Authors:**

* Regarding the choice of restaurant reservation as the domain, were other domains considered? If so, what were the trade-offs that led to the selection of this domain?
* The paper states that the synthetic data is designed to be reproducible. Could you elaborate on the specific steps taken to ensure reproducibility, especially given the stochastic nature of dialog generation?
* You mention limitations of the simplified KB. Do you have any plans to expand the KB to include more complex relationships or entities?
* Could you provide more detail on the process of converting the DSTC2 dataset into the task format?  Specifically, how were API calls and their outputs determined from the existing dialog states?
