- Introduces a public testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue focused on restaurant booking, decomposed into five synthetic tasks plus DSTC2 conversion and a Concierge dataset for validation.
- Evaluation uses retrieval-based next-utterance selection with per-response and per-dialog accuracy; benchmarks include IR, supervised embeddings, MemN2N, and a rule-based baseline.
- Proposes "match type" features to handle entities and OOV generalization.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Strength: Well-motivated, standardized testbed isolating key capabilities (state tracking, KB ops, sorting, OOV handling).
- Strength: Solid baseline study that exposes failure modes and provides pragmatic improvements (match types).
- Framing: Clearly positions gap between slot-filling and end-to-end neural approaches and offers a reproducible diagnostic path.
- Novelty: Fills a gap with a goal-oriented, KB-grounded multi-skill test suite and useful DSTC2 conversion linking synthetic and real settings.
- Methodology: Reasonable spectrum of baselines and ablations; match types address exact matching and OOVs effectively.
- Metrics: Reporting both per-response and per-dialog accuracy highlights degradation from response-level to dialog-level performance.
- Empirical findings: Models handle state-tracking/API issuance but fail on interpreting/sorting KB outputs in multi-turn settings.
- Empirical findings: Multi-hop MemNN improves performance; match types materially help OOV and KB attribute retrieval.
- Empirical findings: Relative rankings transfer from synthetic tasks to DSTC2 and Concierge; rule-based ceiling shows tasks are solvable.
- Significance: The testbed can steer research toward KB interfaces, copying/pointer mechanisms, and planning; negative results are informative.
- Concern: Simulator uses limited templates and deterministic policy, risking pattern overfitting and limited realism despite DSTC2/Concierge addition.
- Concern: Candidate set includes train+val+test responses, raising questions about modeling language vs. response-space design and OOV handling.
- Concern: KB/OOV setup assumes known KB halves during training; stricter zero-shot KB/entity regimes are not tested.
- Concern: Missing comparisons to entity-aware architectures (copy/pointer, key-value memories, seq2seq+pointer-generator) which could change conclusions.
- Concern: Per-dialog accuracy is a strict metric; task-success metrics (API correctness, booking success) would be more nuanced.
- Suggestion: Increase linguistic and policy variability (paraphrases, disfluencies, non-determinism) and provide multiple difficulty modes.
- Suggestion: Add stronger, entity-grounded baselines (copy/pointer, key-value memory networks) and ablations isolating sorting vs. retrieval errors.
- Suggestion: Refine evaluation with task-success rates, slot-level F1, and avoid or justify including test-only responses in candidate sets.
- Suggestion: Provide detailed error analysis (confusion matrices for slot updates, mis-sorted options, termination failures).
- Suggestion: Release DSTC2 conversion scripts, rule-based baseline, and an anonymized Concierge subset for reproducibility.
- Question: Do models see embeddings for tokens that only appear in OOV test candidates, and how is softmax over such candidates implemented without leakage?
- Question: Why allow access to both KB halves at training time for OOV evaluation; have stricter zero-shot KB experiments been tried?
- Question: On Task 3, are failures due to wrong ranking, failure to read ratings, or dialogue control errors?
- Question: How sensitive are results to adding ratings in DSTC2 conversion; is a variant without added ratings available?
- Question: How does performance scale with larger training sets and larger candidate sets; any overfitting signs to small synthetic data?
- Question: For MemNN, is training done with full softmax or negative sampling, what hop counts are optimal, and how stable are results across seeds?
