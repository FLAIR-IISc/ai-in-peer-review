- Introduces a public, bAbI-style benchmark suite for end-to-end goal-oriented restaurant dialogs with five synthetic tasks and two real datasets (reformatted DSTC2 and concierge).
- Decomposes dialog capability testing into API issuance, updates, option presentation/sorting, KB information provision, and full dialogs to probe implicit state tracking, KB querying, sorting, and OOV handling.
- Benchmarks rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, and MemN2N models, and proposes match-type features to mitigate entity/OOV issues.
- Finds MemN2N with match-types performs best overall, succeeds on API issuance and KB information tasks, but fails on option sorting and full-dialog per-dialog accuracy remains low.
- Dataset and analyses are designed to provide reproducible, diagnostic error analysis and to catalyze progress on goal-oriented dialog systems.
- Decision: Accept — paper offers a well-motivated, reusable benchmark with controlled diagnostics and OOV generalization tests.
- Decision rationale: Empirical analysis is careful, reveals concrete failure modes, and outlines a clear research agenda for end-to-end dialog.
- Problem relevance: Addresses a real gap between chit-chat and goal-oriented dialog, positioned well relative to POMDP slot-filling and end-to-end work, with DSTC2/concierge linkage for external validity.
- Methodological clarity: Tasks, KB schema, simulation details, and evaluation metrics (per-response and per-dialog) are clearly specified to promote reproducibility.
- Diagnostic value: Decomposition into targeted tasks yields insightful error analysis and consistent method rankings across synthetic and real data.
- Contribution: Match-type features are a simple, interpretable, and effective method for entity exact-match and OOV mitigation, especially on KB-reading tasks.
- Evaluation protocol concern: Clarify whether union of train/val/test candidates (including OOV) is available during training or only at inference, since candidate pool composition affects OOV robustness claims.
- Candidate-pool suggestion: Report results with candidate pools restricted to training-time candidates and include evaluations requiring composition/copying of unseen entities.
- Baseline coverage concern: Include stronger baselines (sequence models with copy/pointer, key-value memory networks, neural module nets, differentiable sorting or heuristic sort modules) to better bound performance on sorting and KB reasoning.
- Entity handling realism: Test with automatic NER/linking and noisy entity mentions to assess reliance on exact KB linking and robustness to typos/morphology.
- DSTC2 conversion: Analyze impact of added ratings for deterministic ranking and whether model performance depends on rating distributions or deterministic rules; compare to adapted RNN baselines from DSTC literature.
- Metrics recommendation: Complement harsh per-dialog accuracy with API field accuracy, entity F1, stop/proposal decision accuracy, and qualitative error breakdowns, especially for Tasks 3 and 5.
- Data realism suggestion: Expand synthetic language via paraphrase augmentation or crowd-sourced paraphrases to reduce lexical overfitting and test generalization.
- OOV-KB access concern: Report a stricter setting where OOV KB entries are hidden until test time to disentangle reliance on entity lexicon access during training.
- Question — candidate pool: Are test-only candidates used during training negative sampling or only at evaluation, and how does restricting the pool affect performance?
- Question — OOV setup: Do models have access to OOV KB lexicon during training or only at inference, and what are results when OOV items are hidden until test?
- Question — Task 3 sorting errors: What fraction of errors are incorrect top-1 selections versus failures in advancing/stop decisions, and do additional memory hops help specific sub-steps?
- Question — DSTC2 conversion consistency: How often do original bot responses violate deterministic ranking rules, and do such inconsistencies limit performance?
- Question — entity recognition: Are match-type features based on exact string matches or was normalization/NER applied, and how robust are results to spelling/variation?
- Question — concierge dataset: What fraction of turns are decision/action versus surface realization, and can tasks be decoupled (API-call decision vs. response ranking) to pinpoint model weaknesses?
