- Problem: Evaluates goal-oriented, task-completion end-to-end dialog systems where traditional slot-filling excels but E2E models are under-explored and hard to evaluate.
- Contribution: Lightweight, reproducible benchmark suite in a restaurant reservation domain with five synthetic tasks covering API calls, updates, option display/sorting, KB-grounded info, and full dialogs.
- Contribution: Explicit OOV test protocol via a split KB with disjoint cuisines/locations to assess generalization to unseen entities.
- Contribution: Conversion of DSTC2 into the same task/ranking format (Task 6) and evaluation on a real Concierge dataset to test transfer to noisy human data.
- Contribution: Baselines including IR (TF-IDF, NN), supervised embeddings, MemN2N (multi-hop), a rule-based system, and match-type features to handle entities/OOVs.
- Finding: MemN2N outperforms supervised embeddings and IR across tasks; match-type features substantially improve OOV and KB grounding.
- Finding: T1/T2 largely solved, T3/T5 remain challenging; DSTC2 and Concierge results mirror synthetic task ordering but per-dialog accuracy remains low.
- Claim: The dataset offers a controlled, interpretable, reproducible way to stress-test goal-oriented dialog capabilities and shows E2E models can learn some operations but fall short on reliable dialog completion.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason: Benchmark/resource is timely, well-motivated, and fills a clear evaluation gap for KB-grounded, OOV-stressed goal-oriented dialog.
- Reason: Empirical study is careful and insightful with multiple baselines, ablations, OOV split, and cross-dataset validation.
- Supporting point: Tasks decompose required abilities (state tracking, API issuing/updating, sorting/presenting options, KB answering) enabling diagnostic analysis.
- Supporting point: Well positioned relative to POMDP/slot-filling, non-goal E2E dialog, bAbI QA, and DSTC; existing corpora/evaluations are insufficient.
- Rigor: Clear task definitions, controllable simulation, explicit OOV protocol, and distinct per-response/per-dialog metrics.
- Rigor: Hyperparameter tuning on dev, analysis of multi-hop and match-type features, and inclusion of rule-based upper bounds and baselines.
- Cross-validation: Performance ordering and qualitative failures transfer from synthetic tasks to DSTC2 and Concierge, supporting task relevance.
- Novelty/impact: Dataset/framework should catalyze work on entity-centric reasoning, KB interaction, and OOV robustness; match-type augmentation is a practical mitigation.
- Suggestion: Clarify candidate ranking setup and justify including test candidates in the global candidate set or report results restricted to train/val candidates.
- Suggestion: Expand baselines to include sequence models with copy/pointer or key–value mechanisms to better assess entity/OOV handling.
- Suggestion: Provide granular error analysis for T3/T5 (e.g., sorting mistakes, stopping errors, grounding mismatches) and relate to architecture limits.
- Suggestion: Discuss realism/coverage—paraphrase/disfluency/mixed-intent diversity and how simulator could be extended (stochastic policies, more slots, non-determinism).
- Suggestion: Report statistical significance and variance via confidence intervals or multiple runs, especially given small (~1k) training sets.
- Suggestion: Reconsider KB visibility in OOV protocol—allowing OOV KB at training reduces difficulty; consider ablations without OOV KB access.
- Suggestion: Ensure reproducibility by releasing code, simulator, data generation scripts, and exact candidate lists.
- Question: Do results change if candidates are restricted to those seen in train/validation—can you report both?
- Question: How much OOV improvement comes from type lexicon visibility at train time vs. learned generalization—can you ablate “unknown KB at train”?
- Question: For T3 sorting/stopping, what is the typical options distribution and were architectures with explicit comparison/numeric embeddings tried?
- Question: For MemN2N, were position-/n-gram-aware encodings or key–value memory networks tested to better bind attributes to entities?
- Question: In DSTC2 conversion, do original system errors (ASR/NLU) contradict deterministic rules and cap achievable accuracy?
- Question: For Concierge, what is candidate set size/diversity, are there domain drifts (out-of-domain requests), and was delexicalization attempted?
- Question: Were hops and other hyperparameters tuned per-task on same-distribution dev sets and how well do single settings generalize across tasks?
