- Decision: Accept — paper addresses an important problem with a well-designed testbed and comprehensive evaluation.
- Presents a valuable goal-oriented dialog testbed focused on restaurant reservation.
- Identifies the gap between chit‑chat end-to-end models and their applicability to goal‑oriented dialog.
- Proposes structured tasks (T1–T5) and datasets to isolate capabilities like API calling, KB interaction, and state tracking.
- Includes OOV tests and supplements synthetic data with DSTC2 and an online concierge dataset for realism.
- Strong methodology: tasks are thoughtfully designed to dissect model strengths and weaknesses.
- Thorough experimental evaluation comparing Memory Networks to rule-based, IR, and supervised embedding baselines.
- Results show Memory Networks are promising but fall short of human‑level performance, especially on full dialogs.
- Ablation study demonstrates match‑type features substantially improve entity handling.
- Consistent patterns across synthetic and real datasets validate the testbed’s effectiveness.
- Authors release the dataset and tasks (http://fb.ai/babi), supporting reproducibility.
- Suggest providing per‑task difficulty analysis and more detailed error breakdowns.
- Recommend exploring limitations and generalization of match‑type features, particularly with larger KBs.
- Suggest discussing other end‑to‑end architectures (e.g., Transformers) within the testbed.
- Request more detail on the concierge dataset content and how anonymization was performed.
- Question: clarify design choices for the simulated user/bot utterance patterns and sensitivity to those patterns.
- Question: explain how OOV entities are represented to the model and whether embedding techniques are used.
- Question: report how match‑type feature performance scales as KB size and entity variety increase.
- Question: justify choosing Memory Networks as the primary model and whether other architectures were considered.
