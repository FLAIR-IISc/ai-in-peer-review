- Introduces a testbed for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems, focused on restaurant reservation.
- Argues chit-chat model successes don't directly transfer to goal-oriented tasks and proposes tasks to isolate capabilities like API calling and KB manipulation.
- Uses both synthetic and real-world datasets (DSTC2 and a concierge service) to enable controlled experiments and assess generalizability.
- Recommends accepting the paper as it fills a critical gap in evaluating where end-to-end dialog systems fail in goal-oriented contexts.
- Proposed tasks are well-motivated and experimental results demonstrate remaining challenges even for Memory Networks.
- Paper is well-structured with clear explanations of tasks, models, and results.
- Comparison of Memory Networks to simpler baselines (rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings) is insightful and highlights the testbed's value.
- Inclusion of an OOV setting is a strong design choice that forces generalization beyond memorization.
- Detailed appendices with hyperparameters and example predictions strengthen the paper.
- Discussion of match type features feels somewhat disconnected and needs better integration into the narrative.
- More analysis is needed on why Memory Networks struggle specifically with tasks 3 and 5.
- The paper should discuss potential biases introduced by the synthetic data in greater detail.
- Questions raised: impact of the language patterns used to generate simulated utterances on results.
- Questions raised: sensitivity of results to training dataset size and whether more data would yield significant gains.
- Questions raised: request for more failure-case examples of Memory Networks on tasks 3 and 5 to illustrate specific error modes.
