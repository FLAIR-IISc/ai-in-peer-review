- Paper introduces a 5-task testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog focused on restaurant reservation.
- Authors argue existing evaluations are unreproducible or poorly correlated with human judgment.
- Provide a synthetic simulator-generated dataset plus evaluations on DSTC2 and a proprietary concierge dataset.
- Compare Memory Network model against rule-based and information retrieval baselines.
- Memory Networks show promise but per-dialog accuracy remains low and reliable goal-oriented dialog is still challenging.
- Recommendation: Reject with invitation to revise due to incremental contribution and shallow analysis.
- Contribution identifies an important gap (standardized reproducible evaluation) but feels incremental.
- Synthetic dataset is a valuable resource but is underused for deep diagnostic analysis.
- Empirical analysis is superficial; paper shows systems struggle but doesn’t deeply investigate why.
- OOV test sets are a good idea, but results analysis is limited.
- Match type features help, but their impact isn’t thoroughly explored.
- Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and which configurations mattered.
- Connection between simulated tasks and real-world datasets is shown but not fully explained.
- Related work section is cursory and should better contextualize recent advances in knowledge-grounded dialog.
- Task design description lacks precision on how natural language patterns and KB entities are combined and potential biases.
- Suggestions: add detailed error analysis with qualitative examples.
- Suggestions: perform ablation studies to isolate model and feature contributions.
- Suggestions: explore hyperparameter sensitivity and report effects.
- Suggestions: analyze discrepancies between synthetic and real-world datasets and their impact on performance.
- Suggestions: compare against more recent state-of-the-art dialog models and expand related work coverage.
- Questions for authors: provide concrete error examples for low-accuracy tasks (e.g., Tasks 3 and 5).
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity to hyperparameters and computational resources required.
- Questions for authors: describe steps ensuring synthetic data represents real-world conversations and whether code/datasets will be released publicly.
