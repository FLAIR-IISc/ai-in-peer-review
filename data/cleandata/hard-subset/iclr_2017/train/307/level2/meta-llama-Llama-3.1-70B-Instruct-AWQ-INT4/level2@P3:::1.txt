**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications, specifically in the context of restaurant reservation. The authors design a set of five tasks that require manipulating sentences and symbols to conduct conversations, issue API calls, and use the outputs of such calls. They evaluate the performance of an end-to-end dialog system based on Memory Networks on these tasks and compare it to a hand-crafted slot-filling baseline on data from the second Dialog State Tracking Challenge. The results show that the end-to-end dialog system can reach promising, yet imperfect, performance and learn to perform non-trivial operations.
