This paper proposes a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications. The testbed consists of five tasks within the context of restaurant reservation, which require manipulating sentences and symbols to conduct conversations, issue API calls, and use the outputs of such calls. The authors also provide results on two additional datasets extracted from real interactions with users. The paper's main contribution is the introduction of this testbed, which allows for a systematic evaluation of end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented settings.

I decide to accept this paper with the following key reasons:

1. The paper addresses an important problem in the field of dialog systems, which is the lack of a systematic evaluation framework for end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented settings.
2. The proposed testbed is well-motivated and well-designed, covering various aspects of goal-oriented dialog, such as dialog management, querying knowledge bases, and interpreting results.

Supporting arguments for the reasons:

1. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented settings. They highlight the limitations of traditional evaluation methods, such as the lack of reproducibility and the difficulty of interpreting errors.
2. The proposed testbed is designed to address these limitations by providing a systematic and controlled evaluation framework. The five tasks cover various aspects of goal-oriented dialog, and the authors provide a clear explanation of each task and its requirements.
3. The authors also provide results on two additional datasets extracted from real interactions with users, which demonstrates the applicability of the testbed to real-world scenarios.

Additional feedback:

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the Memory Network architecture and its modifications. While the authors provide a brief overview of the architecture, it would be helpful to include more details about the implementation and the hyperparameters used.
2. The results section could be improved by including more detailed analysis of the results, such as error analysis and comparison with other state-of-the-art models.
3. The authors mention that the testbed is designed to be lightweight and easy to use, but it would be helpful to provide more information about the resources required to run the testbed and the expected computational cost.

Questions to the authors:

1. Can you provide more details about the implementation of the Memory Network architecture and the hyperparameters used?
2. How do you plan to extend the testbed to cover more goal-oriented dialog scenarios?
3. Can you provide more information about the resources required to run the testbed and the expected computational cost?
