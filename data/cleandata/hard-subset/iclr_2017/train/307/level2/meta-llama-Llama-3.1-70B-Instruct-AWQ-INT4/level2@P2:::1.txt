This paper proposes a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications, specifically in the context of restaurant reservation. The authors aim to break down the strengths and shortcomings of these systems by designing a set of five tasks that require manipulating sentences and symbols to conduct conversations, issue API calls, and use the outputs of such calls. The paper also compares the performance of an end-to-end dialog system based on Memory Networks to a hand-crafted slot-filling baseline on data from the second Dialog State Tracking Challenge.

The paper claims to contribute to the field by providing a systematic and controlled way to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog learning methods. The authors argue that existing measures of performance either prevent reproducibility or do not correlate well with human judgments, and that the breakdown in tasks will help focus research and development to improve the learning methods.

I decide to **accept** this paper, with the following key reasons:

1. The paper proposes a well-motivated and well-placed testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the tasks and the dataset, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed testbed using a variant of end-to-end Memory Networks, which proves to be an effective model on these tasks relative to other baselines. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the results, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of the model.
