**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The paper proposes a testbed to evaluate end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications. The authors aim to break down the strengths and shortcomings of these systems by designing a set of five tasks within the context of restaurant reservation. They evaluate the performance of Memory Networks, a type of end-to-end model, on these tasks and compare it to other baselines, including rule-based systems, classical information retrieval models, and supervised embedding models.

**Decision: Accept**

I decide to accept this paper based on two key reasons:

1. **Novelty and significance of the proposed testbed**: The paper introduces a new testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications, which is a significant contribution to the field. The testbed is well-designed, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of its effectiveness.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation of Memory Networks**: The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of Memory Networks, a type of end-to-end model, on the proposed testbed. The authors compare the performance of Memory Networks to other baselines and provide insights into its strengths and weaknesses.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their proposed testbed and evaluation methodology. The evaluation results are thorough and provide valuable insights into the performance of Memory Networks and other baselines.

The authors also demonstrate the effectiveness of their testbed by evaluating the performance of Memory Networks on real human-machine dialogs from the restaurant reservation dataset of the 2nd Dialog State Tracking Challenge. This provides additional evidence for the validity of their proposed testbed.
