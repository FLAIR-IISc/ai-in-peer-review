- Paper proposes a restaurant-reservation testbed of five tasks to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems and tests Memory Networks against several baselines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Novel and significant testbed design that meaningfully evaluates end-to-end dialog systems.  
- Reason 2: Comprehensive evaluation of Memory Networks with insightful analysis of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Well-written paper with clear methodology and thorough evaluation results.  
- Validation includes experiments on real human-machine dialogs from DSTC2, supporting testbed validity.  
- Suggestion: Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning for Memory Networks.  
- Suggestion: Compare Memory Networks to other end-to-end architectures (e.g., RNNs, transformers).  
- Suggestion: Evaluate on more diverse datasets and domains to test generality.  
- Question: How were optimal hyperparameters chosen for each task?  
- Question: How will the testbed be extended to multi-turn or multi-user scenarios?  
- Question: Can Memory Networks be compared to other end-to-end models on this testbed?
