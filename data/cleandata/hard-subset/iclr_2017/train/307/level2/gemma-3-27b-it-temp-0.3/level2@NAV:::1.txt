This paper introduces a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented tasks, specifically restaurant reservation. The authors argue that while end-to-end systems show promise in chit-chat, their performance in goal-oriented settings remains unclear. They propose a set of five tasks, grounded in a knowledge base, designed to assess capabilities like API querying and dialog management. The work also includes experiments on existing datasets (DSTC2 and a concierge service dataset) to validate the testbed’s relevance.

I recommend **accepting** this paper. The work addresses a crucial gap in evaluating end-to-end dialog systems – the lack of a focused, reproducible benchmark for goal-oriented tasks. The proposed testbed, with its breakdown into specific subtasks, provides a valuable tool for analyzing system strengths and weaknesses.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the limitations of current evaluation methods and the need for a more targeted approach. The experimental setup is thorough, comparing a Memory Network model to a rule-based baseline and classical IR methods on both synthetic and real-world data. The results demonstrate that while Memory Networks show some promise, significant challenges remain in achieving reliable goal completion, particularly in tasks requiring knowledge base integration. The inclusion of OOV testing is a particularly strong point, highlighting the difficulty of generalizing to unseen entities.

However, the paper could benefit from further clarification. The details of the KB structure and the natural language patterns used for utterance generation could be expanded. It would also be helpful to see a more detailed error analysis of the Memory Network’s failures, beyond the general observations provided. Additionally, the impact of different hyperparameters on performance could be discussed more thoroughly. Finally, the authors mention match type features, but a more intuitive explanation of how these features are implemented would be beneficial. 

Questions for the authors: Could you elaborate on the process of converting the DSTC2 data into your task format? What specific challenges did you encounter during this conversion? Also, could you provide examples of the types of errors the Memory Network makes when failing to complete a dialog, and how these errors relate to the specific subtasks?
