Introduces a new public testbed of five synthetic tasks in the restaurant domain to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog models.  
Validates the synthetic tasks by evaluating on real datasets (DSTC2 and an online concierge service), showing they proxy real-world challenges.  
Strong recommend to accept due to the creation of a well-designed, reproducible benchmark and thorough experimental analysis.  
Decomposes goal-oriented dialog into discrete, testable subtasks allowing fine-grained error analysis.  
Includes an OOV evaluation setting to test generalization to unseen entities.  
Provides a comprehensive empirical comparison of a strong end-to-end model (Memory Networks) against IR and rule-based baselines.  
Finds that performance patterns on synthetic tasks correlate with performance on real data, supporting benchmark utility.  
Suggests adding discussion on extending the ranking-based evaluation to generative models.  
Recommends adding qualitative error analysis with examples, especially for Task 3 (Displaying options) and Task 5 (Conducting full dialogs).  
Asks why match-type features caused slightly worse performance on Task 2 (Updating API calls).  
Asks for elaboration on the types of errors the rule-based baseline made that the neural model handled correctly on DSTC2.
