- Introduces a new restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, generated by a KB-driven simulator with rule-based NLG.
- Task defined as ranking candidate next-turn responses, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Memory Networks improve over bag-of-words baselines and can learn API-like operations and entity manipulation.
- Reviewer is skeptical about the utility of end-to-end systems in narrow domains where engineered, domain-specific systems may yield higher user satisfaction.
- Criticism that ranking responses (rather than generating) limits generalization and practicality for new domains.
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in real deployments; paper appears to rank from existing train/val/test responses.
- Simulator-based generation reduces linguistic variability despite enabling unlimited data.
- Natural language generation rules used in the simulator are not published; releasing them would aid reproducibility.
- Paper would benefit from a detailed discussion of how to generate diverse and relevant candidate responses in practice.
- Requests deeper error analysis to pinpoint the KB interactions and reasoning steps Memory Networks struggle with.
- Recommends comparisons to more recent seq2seq/attention models for a stronger baseline.
- Overall decision: Accept — valuable resource and study despite reservations about ranking approach and simulation reliance.
- Questions for authors: how to obtain candidates in new domains, willingness to release NLG rules, detailed error analysis, and comparisons with seq2seq/attention models.
