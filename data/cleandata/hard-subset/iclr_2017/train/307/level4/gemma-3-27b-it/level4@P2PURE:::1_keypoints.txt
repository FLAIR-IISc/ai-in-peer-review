- Introduces a new publicly available, synthetically generated dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue created via rule-based programs.  
- Tasks cover evaluation of capabilities from API calls and option presentation to full dialogue interactions.  
- Contribution is valuable and can help advance dialogue system research.  
- Concern that synthetic data may not demonstrate deep learning’s superiority over traditional/rule-based or shallow models due to large data needs and regularization effects.  
- Deterministic nature of tasks 1–5 limits evaluation of handling real-world noise, ambiguity, intent distributions, and dialogue repair.  
- Overall research direction is worthwhile despite these limitations.  
- Significant weakness: no baseline model that incorporates word-order information, potentially overstating neural network performance.  
- Recommend adding a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features) for fair comparison.  
- Including such a baseline would strengthen conclusions and could raise the reviewer’s score to 8.  
- Conclusion’s claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is inaccurate given prior end-to-end task-oriented metrics (e.g., Wen et al.).  
- Non-task-oriented dialogue can be evaluated with human subjects, as shown by Liu et al. (2016) and Yu et al.  
- Reviewer updated their score following new results added to the paper.
