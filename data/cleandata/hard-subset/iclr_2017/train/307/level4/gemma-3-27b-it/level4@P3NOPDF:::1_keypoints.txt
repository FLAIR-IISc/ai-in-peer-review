- Introduces a novel, publicly available, rule-based dataset and task suite for evaluating goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Provides a controlled environment that can accelerate research in task-oriented dialogue evaluation.  
- Methodology limitations: unclear whether deep learning is inherently suitable compared to rule-based or shallow methods.  
- Deep models may need much data, and observed architecture differences could reflect regularization rather than representation benefits.  
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic, preventing evaluation of handling real-world noise, ambiguity, intent inference, and dialogue repair.  
- Major omission: no baseline that incorporates word order, which may inflate perceived neural network performance.  
- Strong recommendation: add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, and match-type features).  
- Inclusion of such a baseline would substantially strengthen conclusions and justify deep models; reviewer would raise score to 8 if added.  
- Incorrect claim in conclusion: existing task-oriented systems do use well-defined performance measures (e.g., Wen et al.); non-task evaluations can use human studies (e.g., Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Update: after the authors added the suggested logistic regression baseline, results strengthened and the reviewer increased their score.
