- Paper gives a valuable, sobering reality check on chatbot capabilities and is highly significant for both dialog/NLP and ML communities.  
- Two key conjectures: (1) end-to-end ML is insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs; (2) synthetic-data performance predicts natural-data performance.  
- Main algorithmic contribution is a simple match-type addition to memory networks, first tested on goal-oriented dialog.  
- Experimental protocol is excellent and the paper is clear and accessible; appendix and hop-analysis tables are strong.  
- Current dialog evaluation metrics are fragile and somewhat arbitrary, highlighting need for better metrics.  
- Per-response accuracy is limited/misleading (success driven by correct API call and short dialog length).  
- Per-dialog accuracy might be better but is highly sensitive to experimental protocol (illustrated by T3 vs T5 differences).  
- Question raised about concierge data: define “correct” as “best” instead of within top-5—how would results change?  
- Proposes task-driven loss functions (e.g., revenue-weighted sum of API errors, turns, rejected options) for more meaningful evaluation—requires real users or realistic simulators.  
- Notes vocabulary/KB mismatch: match-type relies on exact KB tokens; models should learn mappings instead of depending on human-defined matches.
