- Introduces a new simulated dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant domain (finding availability and booking tables).  
- Dataset generation uses a knowledge base to create parameterized tuples (cuisine, price, etc.) and rule-based NLG to render natural language dialog.  
- Task is framed as ranking candidate responses per dialog turn; evaluation reported with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines.  
- Reviewer is skeptical about the value of end-to-end approaches in narrow domains where hand-engineered systems may be more effective.  
- Disappointment that the task focuses on ranking responses rather than generating them, despite ranking simplifying evaluation.  
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in practical deployment—authors appear to rank the full set seen in train/val/test.  
- Ranking from a predetermined response pool limits generalization to new domains; generation would enable better transfer—viewed as the paper's weakest point.  
- Simulation and rule-based NLG constrain variability of responses, a trade-off for unlimited data.  
- Reviewer could not find the list of NLG rules in the paper and recommends publishing them.  
- Despite reservations, the reviewer supports publication and updated their score after clarifications and new results.
