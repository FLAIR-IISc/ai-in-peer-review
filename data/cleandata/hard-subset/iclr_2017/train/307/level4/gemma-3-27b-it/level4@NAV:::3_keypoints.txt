- Introduces a novel dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant reservation domain using a KB-driven simulator and rule-based NLG.  
- Evaluation framework ranks candidate responses per turn and measures per-response and per-dialog accuracy; Memory Networks beat bag-of-words baselines.  
- Reviewer commends the contribution but doubts practicality of end-to-end training in narrowly defined domains where engineered systems can exploit domain knowledge.  
- Restricting the task to ranking (not generation) is limiting and raises questions about real-world sourcing of candidate responses.  
- Method seems to rely on ranking responses drawn from train/validation/test sets, which undermines scalability to new domains.  
- Response generation would better support generalization; ranking a pre-defined set of generic responses is a key weakness.  
- Simulation plus rule-based NLG expands parameter combinations but inherently limits response diversity; authors note this trade-off.  
- Reviewer requests publication of the specific NLG rules (e.g., as supplementary material) for full transparency.  
- Overall positive recommendation: interesting and worthwhile for conference presentation; score updated after clarifications and new results.
