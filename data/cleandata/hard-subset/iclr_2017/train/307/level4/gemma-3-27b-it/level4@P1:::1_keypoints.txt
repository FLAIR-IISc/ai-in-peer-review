- Presents a new public, rule-based synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Tasks range from issuing API calls to displaying options and conducting full dialogues.  
- Valuable contribution that provides a standardized testbed to advance dialogue research.  
- Concern: unclear whether Deep Learning models are better suited than rule-based or shallow models given data requirements.  
- Concern: performance differences between neural methods may stem from regularization rather than inherent model capability.  
- Limitation: Tasks 1–5 are entirely deterministic and may not capture noise, ambiguity, or dialogue repair needed in real-world interactions.  
- Initial paper omitted a baseline that incorporates word order, which potentially overstated neural advantages.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bigram features, and match-type features).  
- Authors responded and added a more competitive baseline, addressing the main concern.  
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is overstated—task-oriented dialogue has established metrics and evaluations.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: addresses an important community need and offers a useful standardized resource.  
- Key reasons: thorough experimental evaluation comparing multiple methods across tasks.  
- Key reasons: clear problem definition and well-articulated motivation.  
- Key reasons: authors were responsive to reviewer feedback and strengthened the paper.  
- Suggestion: include more detailed qualitative error analysis to understand failure modes.  
- Suggestion: investigate how models trained on synthetic data generalize to noisy, real-world dialogues.  
- Suggestion: evaluate effects of a more complex, realistic knowledge base on model performance.  
- Questions for authors: what are the computational costs for experiments, especially large models?  
- Questions for authors: plans to release additional datasets or expand current scenarios?  
- Questions for authors: have you considered adding user simulators to provide more realistic evaluation environments?
