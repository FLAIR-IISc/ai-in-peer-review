- Paper is a significant reality check on applying chatbots to goal-oriented dialog, with impact beyond Dialog/NLP into ML.
- Two central conjectures: (1) current end-to-end ML is insufficient for goal-oriented dialog, and (2) relative performance on synthetic data can predict performance on natural data.
- Algorithmic contribution: adding match type features to Memory Networks, first use of this architecture in goal-oriented dialog.
- Experimental protocol is strong and paper is clearly written; appendix and hop-count illustrations are concise and informative.
- Work exposes brittleness and arbitrariness of current evaluation metrics, suggesting need for more realistic validation.
- Per-response accuracy (next-utterance classification) can be misleading—correct API calls and short dialogs may still yield low accuracy.
- Per-dialog accuracy is sensitive to experimental protocol (e.g., number of candidate options), leading to large swings in reported performance.
- Question: report Concierge results when “correct” is defined as being among the top-5 responses rather than strictly the top-1.
- Suggest exploring alternative, application-driven metrics (e.g., weighted sum of API errors, turns, rejected options) tied to real-world objectives, which require human users or realistic simulators.
- Limitation: match type relies on exact vocabulary overlap with KB; vocabulary mismatch may break this approach.
- Recommendation: investigate methods that learn associations between user language and KB terms instead of relying on human-defined mappings.
- Decision: reviewer accepts the paper with a strong recommendation for publication.
- Questions for authors: (1) top-5 Concierge results, (2) strategies for addressing vocabulary mismatch/learning associations, (3) plans to explore application-specific evaluation metrics, (4) whether normalizing or weighting dialogs by task complexity (e.g., number of options) would yield more robust evaluation.
