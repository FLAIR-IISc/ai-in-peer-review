- Introduces a new simulated dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant domain using a KB and rule-based NLG.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses per turn; evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines.  
- Skepticism about the utility of fully end-to-end systems in narrow, well-defined domains where engineered solutions may work better.  
- Criticism that ranking pre-defined candidates (rather than generating) weakens the claim of end-to-end scalability to new domains.  
- Practical concern: unclear where candidate responses would come from in real deployments for new domains.  
- Argues generative models would offer better generalization and produce novel utterances compared to ranking.  
- Simulation and rule-based NLG limit variability of training data; the specific NLG rules are not disclosed.  
- Recommends publishing the NLG rules to improve reproducibility and reveal dataset biases.  
- Nonetheless, reviewer considers the dataset and initial Memory Network results a valuable contribution worthy of publication.
