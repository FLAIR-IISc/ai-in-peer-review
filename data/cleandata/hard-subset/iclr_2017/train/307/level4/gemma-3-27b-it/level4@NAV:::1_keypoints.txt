- Introduces a novel, publicly available, rule-generated dataset and tasks for evaluating goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Dataset enables controlled assessment of capabilities from API calls to full dialogue management.  
- Contribution is valuable and useful for future dialogue research.  
- Artificial construction raises concerns about whether deep learning actually outperforms rule-based or shallow methods.  
- Deep models’ advantage is unclear given deep learning’s data needs and possible differences due to regularization.  
- Deterministic nature of tasks 1–5 limits evaluation of key skills: handling noise, ambiguity resolution, inferring user intent distributions, and dialogue repair.  
- Missing baseline that incorporates word order inflates apparent neural-network performance.  
- Recommend adding a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, and match-type features) to fairly evaluate representation learning.  
- Inclusion of such a baseline would strengthen conclusions and could raise the paper’s rating to 8.  
- Claim that existing work lacks well-defined performance measures is inaccurate; prior end-to-end task-oriented systems and evaluation methods (Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.) already exist.
