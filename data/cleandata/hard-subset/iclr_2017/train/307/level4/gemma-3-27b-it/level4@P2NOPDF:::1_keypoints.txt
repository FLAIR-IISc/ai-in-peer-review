- Introduces a novel, publicly available synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue; a useful, controlled environment for research.  
- Synthetic, rule-based generation limits realism and may not reflect real-world dialogue complexity.  
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic and fail to assess ambiguity, noise, intent inference, and dialogue repair.  
- Unclear whether deep learning models truly outperform rule-based or simpler statistical approaches given data demands and regularization effects.  
- Major weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, which may artificially inflate neural model performance.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features) to fairly evaluate representation learning.  
- Paper’s claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is inaccurate; established metrics and human-evaluation methodologies already exist (e.g., Wen et al. 2016; Liu et al. 2016; Yu et al. 2017).  
- Reviewer updated their score based on new results added to the paper.
