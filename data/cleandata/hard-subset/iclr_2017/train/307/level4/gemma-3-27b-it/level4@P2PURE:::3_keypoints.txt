- Paper introduces a new dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogs in the restaurant reservation domain.  
- Dataset generated by simulation over a knowledge base, sampling parameter variations and applying rule-based NLG to produce utterances.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses per dialog turn, evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines.  
- Reviewer questions the value of end-to-end approaches in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered systems might better maximize user satisfaction.  
- Choosing to RANK instead of GENERATE responses is limiting; practical sourcing of candidate responses in novel domains is unclear.  
- Ranking appears to use all responses seen in train/val/test, which undermines claimed scalability to new domains.  
- Simulation and rule-based NLG likely limit variability of training responses, and the specific rules were not published.  
- Reviewer suggests publishing the NLG rules and notes generating responses would improve generalization; despite reservations, recommends publication and updated their score after clarifications.
