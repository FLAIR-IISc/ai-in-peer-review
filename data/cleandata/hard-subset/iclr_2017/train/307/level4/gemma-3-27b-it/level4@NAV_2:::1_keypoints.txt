- Introduces a novel dataset and tasks for evaluating goal-oriented dialogue systems, focused on restaurant reservation subskills (API calling, KB interpretation, multi-turn management).  
- Provides a controlled testbed to dissect end-to-end model capabilities beyond open-domain chit-chat.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Well-motivated—addresses limits of handcrafted systems and uncertain transferability from open-domain models.  
- Tasks are carefully designed and grounded in a practical scenario.  
- Experimental setup compares rule-based and embedding-based baselines and evaluates on DSTC2 and a concierge service dataset.  
- Results show strong per-response accuracy but poor per-dialog success, highlighting remaining challenges; includes out-of-vocabulary tests.  
- Suggested future work: add word-order information (e.g., bi-gram features) to baselines to better compare with neural models.  
- Suggested future work: perform a detailed error analysis of Memory Networks beyond low per-dialog accuracy.  
- Suggested future work: expand discussion of existing task-oriented dialogue metrics and clarify the new testbed’s advantages.  
- Questions for authors: (1) limitations of deterministic tasks 1–5 and generalization to non-deterministic settings.  
- Questions for authors: (2) whether match-type features were added to classical IR models (TF-IDF, NN) and rationale if not.  
- Questions for authors: (3) how the Concierge dataset complements simulated tasks given noisy language and variable dialogue structure.  
- Questions for authors: (4) request concrete examples of Memory Network errors in tasks T3 and T5 to explain low per-dialog accuracy.
