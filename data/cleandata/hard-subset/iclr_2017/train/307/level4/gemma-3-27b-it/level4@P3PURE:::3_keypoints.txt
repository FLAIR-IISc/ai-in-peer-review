- Introduces a new dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant reservation domain, generated by a KB-driven simulator and rule-based NLG.
- Task is framed as ranking candidate responses per dialog turn, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on the task.
- Reviewer is skeptical about end-to-end approaches’ utility in narrow domains compared to engineered, domain-aware systems.
- Disappointment that the task focuses on ranking rather than generating responses, which simplifies evaluation but limits applicability.
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in practical deployments or novel domains.
- Models appear to rank the complete set of responses from train/validation/test, raising concerns about generalization and the scalability argument.
- Simulation plus rule-based NLG inherently limits response variability; the specific NLG rules were not provided.
- Recommends publishing the NLG rule set to improve transparency and reproducibility.
- Overall positive about the contribution and recommends publication despite reservations; reviewer updated score after clarifications and new results.
