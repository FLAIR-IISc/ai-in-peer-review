- Paper gives a valuable, sobering assessment of chatbot capabilities and is highly significant for Dialog/NLP and ML communities.  
- Highlights need for more rigorous validation when applying ML to dialog systems.  
- Puts forward two conjectures: end-to-end ML is inadequate for goal-oriented dialogs, and synthetic-data performance predicts natural-data performance.  
- Algorithmic contribution is modest (single match-type addition) but this is the first evaluation of memory networks on a goal-oriented dialog task.  
- Experimental protocol is exceptionally well-designed and the paper is clear and accessible, with a concise informative appendix.  
- Demonstrates the fragility and arbitrariness of current dialog evaluation metrics and motivates more robust metrics.  
- Per-response accuracy (next-utterance classification) is problematic and can be misleading, as Table 3 shows.  
- Per-dialog accuracy might be preferable but is highly sensitive to experimental/task definitions (illustrated by T3 vs T5 disparity).  
- The concierge dataset’s 5-best-response metric raises questions about whether being among top-k suffices versus needing the single best response.  
- Recommends exploring alternative, application-driven loss functions (e.g., revenue-weighted errors, API-call correctness, turn cost), which require real users or realistic simulators.  
- Points out a representation-learning shortcoming: vocabulary mismatches between user input and KB require verbatim KB entries for the match-type to work.  
- Suggests ML should infer KB–user associations rather than relying on manual or obfuscated KB terminology.
