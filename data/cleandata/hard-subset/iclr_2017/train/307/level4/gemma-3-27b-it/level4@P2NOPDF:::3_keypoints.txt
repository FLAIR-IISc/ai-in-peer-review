- Introduces a novel simulated dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in restaurant booking, using a KB to generate parameter combinations and rule-based natural language rendering.
- Frames the task as ranking candidate responses per turn and reports per-response and per-dialog accuracy metrics.
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform simpler bag-of-words baselines.
- Reviewer questions the practical utility of end-to-end training in narrow domains where engineered, domain-knowledge systems may yield better user satisfaction.
- Focusing on ranking rather than generating responses is seen as limiting; unclear how candidate responses would be sourced in new domains, hurting generalization.
- The rule-based simulation restricts linguistic variability, and the authors did not disclose the specific rules, reducing reproducibility and understanding of dataset characteristics.
- Despite reservations, the reviewer finds the contribution valuable and recommends publication (score updated after author clarifications).
