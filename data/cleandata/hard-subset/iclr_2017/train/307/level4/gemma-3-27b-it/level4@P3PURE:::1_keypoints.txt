- Paper introduces a new publicly available, rule-based synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue covering API calls, option display, and full dialogue management.  
- The contribution is welcome and useful for advancing dialogue research.  
- Artificial dataset construction raises concerns about realism and generalizability to real-world dialogue.  
- Unclear whether deep learning is especially appropriate for these tasks versus rule-based or shallow models.  
- Neural performance differences may stem from regularization rather than superior representations, given data requirements of deep models.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, limiting evaluation of handling noisy/ambiguous interactions, goal inference, and dialogue repair.  
- Major weakness: no baseline models that incorporate word order, which could make neural models appear stronger than they are.  
- Strong recommendation to add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, and match-type features) to fairly assess benefits of representation learning.  
- The paper’s claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is incorrect; task-oriented systems have established metrics and non-task evaluation can use human studies (citations provided).  
- Reviewer updated their score after new results were added.
