- Introduces a new dataset and benchmark for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in restaurant reservations.  
- Dataset comprises five tasks generated by simulation over a knowledge base to test specific dialog capabilities (API querying, entity manipulation).  
- Demonstrates Memory Networks achieving promising but imperfect results, outperforming TF-IDF and supervised embeddings.  
- Validates findings on real-world data (DSTC and an online concierge), strengthening relevance of synthetic tasks.  
- Recommendation: Reject — limitations of ranking-based evaluation and synthetic data outweigh contributions.  
- Main critique: Ranking-based evaluation (pre-defined candidate responses) limits applicability and undermines the scalability argument for end-to-end systems.  
- Candidate response sourcing (drawing from train/val/test) is unrealistic for new domains and feels contrived.  
- Heavy reliance on synthetic data raises concerns about generalizability to diverse, noisy real-world conversations.  
- Positive: Systematic task design and clear dissection of required goal-oriented dialog capabilities.  
- Positive: Useful comparison between Memory Networks and baselines with commendable ablation studies and hyperparameter details.  
- Missing detail: Insufficient description of the natural language generation (NLG) rules used in simulation, affecting reproducibility and realism assessment.  
- Suggested improvement: Investigate generative models in addition to ranking models to better reflect end-to-end system capabilities.  
- Suggested improvement: Propose strategies for generating candidate responses in new domains without relying on existing curated datasets (e.g., paraphrasing/back-translation).  
- Suggested improvement: Expand and deepen analysis of real-world datasets, including detailed error analysis.  
- Suggested improvement: More explicit discussion of limitations of ranking evaluation and synthetic data.  
- Questions for authors: computational cost of synthetic data generation and sensitivity of results to different NLG rule sets.  
- Questions for authors: examples of Memory Network failure cases, rationale for selecting Memory Networks and whether other architectures were considered.  
- Question for authors: how API calls and KB outputs were reconstructed for the DSTC2 real-world dataset.
