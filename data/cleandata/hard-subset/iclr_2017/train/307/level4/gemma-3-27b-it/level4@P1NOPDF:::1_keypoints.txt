- Introduces a new publicly available dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, constructed with rule-based programs.  
- Dataset/tasks are useful for evaluating diverse dialogue capabilities (API calls, option presentation, dialogue management).  
- Valuable contribution likely to facilitate future dialogue research and system understanding.  
- Unclear whether deep learning is the appropriate approach versus traditional/rule-based or shallow models given data needs and regularization effects.  
- Tasks 1–5 are entirely deterministic, limiting evaluation of robustness to noisy, ambiguous real-world interactions and dialogue repair.  
- Major weakness: paper lacks a baseline that incorporates word order, which may inflate perceived neural performance.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-gram features, match-type features).  
- Including such a baseline would better demonstrate the benefits of representation learning and could increase the reviewer’s score.  
- Minor factual correction: claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is inaccurate—end-to-end task-oriented and human-evaluated non-task dialogue metrics exist (e.g., Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Reviewer updated their score after the authors added new results.
