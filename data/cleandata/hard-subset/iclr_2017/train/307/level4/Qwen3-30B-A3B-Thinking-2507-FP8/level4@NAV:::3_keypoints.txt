- Introduces a new synthesized dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog focused on restaurant reservations.  
- Dataset created by simulation using a knowledge base with parameterized samples and rule-based natural language transformations.  
- Task is to rank candidate responses at each dialog turn, evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy metrics.  
- Memory Networks outperform baseline bag-of-words approaches on this dataset.  
- Reviewer is skeptical about the practical utility of end-to-end systems in narrow domains where engineered solutions may better maximize user satisfaction.  
- Disappointed the work tackles ranking rather than response generation, which limits evaluation and generalization.  
- Questions how candidate responses would be sourced for new domains under a ranking approach; generation would scale better.  
- Simulation-based NL generation limits response variability, and the absence of the rule list hurts reproducibility.  
- Overall judged a valuable contribution worthy of publication pending author clarifications and additional results.
