- Introduces a simulated restaurant-reservation dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, using a KB and rule-based NLG; task is ranking candidate responses with per-response and per-dialog accuracy; Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines.

- Reviewer is skeptical about the utility of end-to-end training in narrow domains, arguing engineered systems using explicit domain knowledge may better ensure user satisfaction.

- Major criticism: the work focuses on ranking rather than generating responses, and it’s unclear how candidate responses would be sourced for new domains, limiting scalability and generalization.

- Rule-based NLG used to expand parameter tuples restricts response variability; the authors’ omission of the generation rules hurts reproducibility.

- Despite concerns, reviewer views the contribution as valuable and worthy of ICLR; score was updated after author clarifications.
