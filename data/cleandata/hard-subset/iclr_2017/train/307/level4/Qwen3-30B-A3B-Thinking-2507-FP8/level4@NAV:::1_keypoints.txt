- Introduces a new public dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, constructed via rule-based programs.  
- Dataset/tasks enable evaluation across diverse dialogue capabilities (API calls, options display, full dialogue management).  
- Valuable contribution that will facilitate future dialogue system development and analysis.  
- Major methodological concern: paper does not clarify how deep learning compares to traditional/rule-based or shallow models; observed gains may reflect regularization.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic and do not test handling of noisy or ambiguous interactions (e.g., user goal distributions, dialogue repair).  
- Critical omission: no baseline using word-order information; simple baselines (logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, match-type features) could outperform the neural models.  
- Including such a baseline would substantially strengthen claims; reviewer would raise score to 8 if added.  
- Minor correction: conclusion incorrectly claims no well-defined performance measures; prior end-to-end task-oriented systems and human-subject evaluation methods exist.  
- Reviewer updated their score after seeing new results in the revised paper.
