- Presents a novel open-source dataset and task suite for systematically evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Constructs a synthetic restaurant reservation environment that decomposes dialogue capabilities into subtasks (API call issuance, KB querying, option display, full conversational flow) for precise error analysis.  
- Recommends acceptance conditional on adding a baseline model that incorporates word order information.  
- Argues the absence of a word-order baseline likely overstates neural models’ advantages and creates uneven comparisons.  
- Identifies the lack of a baseline with word-order features (e.g., logistic regression with bi-grams and match-type features) as the most significant weakness.  
- Notes the synthetic task design enables controlled testing but limits assessment of handling noisy, ambiguous, real-world user interactions.  
- Praises the dataset as a useful contribution that will facilitate future research in task-oriented dialogue.  
- Criticizes the paper’s claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” as inaccurate, citing prior evaluation metrics (e.g., Wen et al., 2015).  
- Asks authors to explain why a word-order baseline was omitted and how that omission affects result interpretation.  
- Requests clarification of the conclusion regarding prior work’s evaluation measures in light of existing literature.
