- Introduces Restaurant-15K, a simulated restaurant-reservation dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue with KB sampling and rule-based NL transformations.  
- Evaluation framed as response ranking (per-response and per-dialog accuracy) with candidates drawn from train/val/test sets.  
- Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines on the dataset.  
- Narrow, well-understood domain raises doubts about the necessity and advantage of end-to-end learning versus hand-engineered dialog systems.  
- Focusing on ranking instead of generation limits real-world applicability and contradicts claims about scaling to new domains.  
- The ranking setup depends on pre-collected candidate responses, which is impractical for domain adaptation without manual curation.  
- NL generation rules used to create utterances were initially undisclosed, impairing reproducibility and raising concerns about linguistic diversity and simulator bias.  
- Authors addressed reviewer queries and added clarifications, improving the paper.  
- Dataset and simulation framework are valuable contributions and fill an evaluation gap.  
- Recommendation: Accept after minor revisions to clarify scope, limitations, and released rule details.
