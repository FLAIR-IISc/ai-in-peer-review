- Introduces a new public dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue; a valuable standardized benchmark for the community.  
- Tasks are fully deterministic, failing to evaluate noise, ambiguity, intent distributions, or dialogue-repair—fundamental omissions for real-world systems.  
- Lacks a strong non-neural baseline that incorporates word-order (e.g., n-grams, logistic regression with embeddings); without it neural superiority is unconvincing.  
- Current evaluation likely inflates neural performance and cannot isolate representation learning from regularization effects.  
- The paper’s claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures” is incorrect—prior task-oriented systems use metrics like slot-filling accuracy and success rate, and non-task systems use human evaluation.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent terminology ("rule-based programs" vs "rule-based systems") and insufficient discussion of how the dataset’s artificial/deterministic nature affects generalizability.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision—must add a robust feature-based baseline and clarify evaluation scope; reviewer would raise score to acceptance if addressed.
