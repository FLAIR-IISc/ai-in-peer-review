- Introduces a new simulated restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, with booking tasks using KB and rule-based NL generation.  
- Data is synthesized via simulation and rule-based natural language rendering, enabling scalable sample creation.  
- Task framed as per-turn candidate response ranking, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines on the benchmark.  
- Dataset construction is well-motivated and fills a need for standardized narrow-domain dialog evaluation.  
- Simulation approach generates diverse training samples and the evaluation framework is clearly defined.  
- Major limitation: focusing on ranking (with candidate sets drawn from training) undermines end-to-end scalability to unseen domains.  
- Ranking assumes pre-specified candidate lists, which is unrealistic in real-world deployment and limits practical relevance.  
- Generative models could better generalize to new domains without manual candidate curation; omission weakens the paper’s central claim.  
- Second limitation: rule-based NL generation restricts language diversity and rules are not published, harming reproducibility and transparency.  
- Lack of published generation rules leaves unclear how linguistic variation is achieved and whether data is overly constrained.  
- No comparison to strong hand-engineered rule-based baselines (e.g., slot-filling with templates), so end-to-end advantage is not fully established.  
- Evaluation metric (accuracy) may conflate task correctness with naturalness and user experience.  
- Despite concerns, the dataset is a valuable, structured benchmark with clear protocol and solid baselines.  
- Recommended revisions: discuss ranking vs. generation and scaling to unseen domains, publish NL generation rules, and add a rule-based baseline comparison.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; dataset is useful but needs clarifications to strengthen impact.
