- Introduces a new simulated dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog focused on restaurant reservations.  
- Data generation samples parameter combinations from a knowledge base and applies rule-based NL transformations to create utterances.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses per dialog turn, evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on the task.  
- Reviewer skeptical about the utility of end-to-end training in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered systems may better ensure user satisfaction.  
- Major concern that the work focuses on ranking rather than generating responses, limiting applicability and generalization to new domains.  
- Unclear how candidate responses would be obtained in practice for new domains, undermining the scalability motivation.  
- Simulation’s NL-generation rules likely constrain response variability, and those rules were not published, harming reproducibility and transparency.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and publishable contribution, but with important limitations and reservations.
