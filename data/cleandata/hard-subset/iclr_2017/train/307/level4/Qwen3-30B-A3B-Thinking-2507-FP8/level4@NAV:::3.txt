

SYNOPSIS:  
This paper presents a novel dataset for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems, focused exclusively on restaurant reservations. The dataset is synthesized via simulation using a knowledge base to generate parameterized samples (e.g., cuisine, price range), followed by rule-based natural language transformations. The task involves ranking candidate responses at each dialog turn, with evaluation metrics including per-response and per-dialog accuracy. The authors demonstrate that Memory Networks achieve superior performance over baseline bag-of-words approaches.  

THOUGHTS:  
I commend the authors for this intriguing contribution. Nevertheless, I remain skeptical about the practical utility of end-to-end trained systems in narrow-domain scenarios. While end-to-end methods are compelling for open-domain settings where manual state/response engineering is infeasible, narrow domains typically allow comprehensive domain understanding, making engineered solutions preferable for maximizing user satisfaction.  

I am also disappointed that the work focuses on *ranking* rather than *generating* responses, despite acknowledging this simplifies evaluation. Crucially, the method appears to rank responses from the training/validation/test sets (as noted in the final sentence before Section 3.2). Given the core motivation of end-to-end approaches—scalability to new domains without manual re-engineering—how would candidate responses be sourced for a new domain in practice? Response generation would enable superior generalization compared to ranking pre-collected generic responses, representing the work’s weakest aspect.  

Furthermore, the simulation-based data generation, while enabling unlimited synthetic data, inherently limits response variability through fixed NL generation rules. While this trade-off is acknowledged, the absence of the rule list impedes reproducibility; publishing these rules would strengthen the contribution.  

Despite these reservations, I consider the work a valuable contribution meriting publication following the authors’ clarifications and supplementary results.
