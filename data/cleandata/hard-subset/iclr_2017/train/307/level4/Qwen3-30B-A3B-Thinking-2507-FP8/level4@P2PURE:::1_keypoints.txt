- Introduces a novel, publicly available dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, built with rule-based programs to evaluate diverse dialogue behaviors.  
- Dataset is a valuable contribution for standardized evaluation in dialogue research.  
- Paper fails to show whether deep learning models outperform traditional or shallow methods, given deep models’ data needs and possible effects of regularization.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, preventing assessment of models’ handling of noisy, ambiguous interactions and dialogue repair.  
- The absence of a baseline that captures word order (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, match-type features) is a major weakness that may overstate neural models’ advantages.  
- Authors should include a non-neural benchmark; doing so would likely increase the reviewer’s rating to 8.  
- Minor correction: the claim that existing work lacks well-defined performance measures is inaccurate—end-to-end task-oriented systems and human-annotated evaluation frameworks exist.  
- Reviewer updated their score after the authors’ supplementary results.
