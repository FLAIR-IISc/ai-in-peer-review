- Introduces a valuable, reproducible benchmark of five synthetic restaurant-reservation tasks targeting specific dialog capabilities (API calls, updates, options, info).
- Tasks are grounded in a structured knowledge base and designed to facilitate error analysis and reproducibility.
- Validates the testbed by comparing multiple models (including Memory Networks) against baselines on synthetic data and real-world datasets (DSTC2 and an online concierge).
- Significant contribution: fills the gap of lacking standardized, reproducible benchmarks for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue systems.
- Synthetic approach provides controlled, systematic testing of capabilities while enabling consistent comparisons across models.
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions, noting substantial improvement since initial submission.
- Authors added a word-order aware baseline (logistic regression with bi-gram features), strengthening comparisons with neural models.
- Paper’s conclusion corrected to acknowledge existing well-defined performance measures (e.g., Wen et al., 2015).
- Supporting argument 1: Testbed decomposes goal-oriented dialogue into manageable subtasks for focused research.
- Supporting argument 2: Synthetic tasks serve as a reasonable proxy for real-world performance, showing consistent relative model patterns across datasets.
- Supporting argument 3: Memory Networks with match-type features perform well, especially on handling known OOV entities.
- Suggested improvement 1: Discuss limitations of synthetic tasks in capturing real-world ambiguity, noise, and non-determinism, and outline future bridging work.
- Suggested improvement 2: Expand discussion of the DSTC2 rule-based system (specific rules, failure modes) to clarify comparative strengths and weaknesses.
- Question 1: Which specific aspects of the synthetic tasks most closely mirror real-world dialog management and error types?
- Question 2: Why do match-type features not improve OOV performance on task T4, despite overall gains?
- Question 3: What specific rules and prioritization were used to optimize the DSTC2 rule-based system on the validation set?
