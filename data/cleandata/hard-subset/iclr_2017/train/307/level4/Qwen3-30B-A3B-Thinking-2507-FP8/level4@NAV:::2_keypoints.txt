- Paper provides an important reality check on overhyped chatbot applications and is highly significant for Dialog/NLP and ML venues.  
- Two key conjectures: end-to-end ML is insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs, and relative performance on synthetic data predicts performance on natural data when conditions are aligned.  
- Algorithmic contribution: modest extension (match type) to memory networks and first deployment/testing of memory networks in goal-oriented dialog.  
- Experimental protocol is exemplary and the paper is exceptionally clear and accessible beyond ML/dialog specialists.  
- Appendix summary of memory networks and tables on hop-count effects are particularly useful.  
- Paper establishes rigorous dialog-metric baselines but exposes the brittleness and arbitrariness of current metrics.  
- Per-response accuracy (next-utterance classification) is misleading—requires exact bot responses and can produce counterintuitive requirements.  
- Per-dialog accuracy is highly protocol-sensitive; task definitions can dramatically alter reported performance (e.g., T3 vs T5).  
- For concierge data, the definition of "correct" (best among options vs. top-k) needs further exploration.  
- Proposes more meaningful, real-world-aligned metrics (e.g., revenue-weighted by API errors, dialog turns, user rejections) but notes these need real users or realistic simulators.  
- Paper offers recommendations for future research on metrics rather than proposing immediate revisions.  
- Overlooked issue: representation learning and vocabulary mismatch between user input and KB entries—match type requires exact KB terms and fails when KB uses obfuscated terminology.  
- Argues ML should learn associations between user terms and KB concepts instead of relying on manual mapping or exact matches.
