- Proposes a reproducible testbed of five KB-grounded tasks in a restaurant domain to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Uses synthetic data plus real-world datasets (DSTC2, concierge) to validate results and demonstrate Memory Networks' abilities.  
- Shows Memory Networks achieve promising results on KB interactions but have limited per-dialog accuracy.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary weakness: insufficient baseline comparisons that overstate neural methods' advantages.  
- Missing critical baseline: logistic regression with bigram features to capture word order and sentence structure.  
- Neural models appear superior mainly because compared against weak/trivial baselines (rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings).  
- Second major weakness: core tasks are synthetic and deterministic, limiting generalizability to real-world dialogue challenges (ambiguity, ASR noise, dialogue repair).  
- Claim that synthetic tasks proxy real-data behavior is not sufficiently validated.  
- Recommendations: add a logistic regression bigram baseline, expand discussion of synthetic vs. real limitations, and clarify relation to existing evaluation methodologies.  
- Clarifying questions requested about omission of the bigram logistic baseline, generalization of deterministic tasks to noisy real dialogues, and comparison to standard DSTC2 slot-filling baselines.
