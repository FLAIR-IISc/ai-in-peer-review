- Introduces a novel public dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue systems; a valuable resource for the community.  
- Significant methodological concerns undermine the paper’s claims.  
- Fails to assess whether deep learning provides real advantages over traditional/rule-based or shallow models; observed gains may reflect regularization or data volume.  
- Tasks 1–5 are entirely deterministic and do not test robustness to noisy inputs, ambiguous intents, or dialogue repair.  
- Major omission: no non-sequential baseline that captures word-order—neural models only compared to bag-of-words baselines.  
- Recommendation: add a logistic regression baseline with word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features to isolate DL’s contribution.  
- Minor error: claim that “existing work has no well-defined performance measures” is incorrect—prior work uses metrics like success rate and slot accuracy and there are human-annotated benchmarks.  
- Overall: dataset is useful but evaluation lacks rigor; addressing missing baselines and deterministic tasks would substantially strengthen the paper (would raise score to 8).
