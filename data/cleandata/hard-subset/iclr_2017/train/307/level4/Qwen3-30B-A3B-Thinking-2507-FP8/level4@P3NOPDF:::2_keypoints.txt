- Provides a crucial reality check on chatbot capabilities, challenging overhyped claims and relevant to both Dialog/NLP and ML venues.  
- Main contribution is a rigorous experimental protocol for evaluating goal-oriented dialog, not algorithmic novelty (match type is simple).  
- Implicit conjectures: (1) end-to-end ML is still insufficient for practical goal-oriented dialog, and (2) relative performance on synthetic data predicts natural-data performance.  
- Experimental design and presentation are strong: clear appendix on memory networks and informative tables (e.g., impact of hops).  
- Core limitation: reliance on per-response accuracy that fails to capture real-world task success (example where a correct API call yields low accuracy).  
- Dialog-level accuracy is also flawed and extremely sensitive to task definition (e.g., T3’s 0.0% vs. T5’s 19.7%).  
- The paper notes metric shortcomings but does not propose alternative, goal-oriented evaluation metrics.  
- Realistic metrics should align with user/business goals (e.g., API-call accuracy, dialog length, rejection rates) and require real users or sophisticated simulators.  
- Major practical issue: match type depends on exact lexical matches between user input and KB, which is unrealistic given diverse phrasing and obfuscated KB terms.  
- The paper fails to address how models could learn lexical associations autonomously rather than relying on manual KB curation.  
- Overall: a landmark evaluation contribution that exposes critical gaps (metrics and lexical robustness) but stops short of proposing what should replace current practices.
