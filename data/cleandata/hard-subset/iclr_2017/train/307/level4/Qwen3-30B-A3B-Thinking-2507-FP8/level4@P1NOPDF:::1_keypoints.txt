- Introduces a valuable public dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue via rule-based program generation.
- Fails to adequately compare deep learning models with traditional/rule-based or shallow methods; observed differences may reflect regularization/data size rather than model superiority.
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic and do not assess handling of noisy, ambiguous interactions or dialogue repair, limiting real-world relevance.
- Major omission: no baseline that incorporates word order, which overstates the proposed neural models’ effectiveness.
- Recommend adding a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features); inclusion would substantially strengthen claims and increase score.
- Incorrectly claims that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance”; prior end-to-end task-oriented systems and evaluation methods exist and the paper should revise this statement.
