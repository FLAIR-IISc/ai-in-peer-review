- Introduces a simulated restaurant-reservation dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue evaluation.  
- Dataset generated from a knowledge base and rule-based NLG converting templates to natural language.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses per dialog turn; evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on this benchmark.  
- Reviewer appreciates the dataset contribution but is skeptical of end-to-end methods' practical utility in narrow, well-understood domains.  
- Argues hand-engineered, domain-tailored systems may better achieve high user satisfaction in narrow domains than end-to-end approaches.  
- Criticizes focus on ranking rather than generation: raises practicality questions about sourcing candidate responses in real deployments.  
- Notes ranking all responses from train/val/test limits scalability and undermines the claimed generalization advantage of end-to-end methods.  
- Points out rule-based NLG limits linguistic variability and the paper omits the full rule set, hindering reproducibility.  
- Recommends addressing the ranking vs. generation trade-off and releasing the NLG rules in future work.  
- Despite concerns, finds the dataset valuable for benchmarking and supports publication after clarifications.  
- Reviewer updated their score following authors’ clarifications and supplementary results.
