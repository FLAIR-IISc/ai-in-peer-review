- Introduces a valuable new public dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, aiding future evaluation and development.  
- Fails to clarify how deep learning compares to traditional methods; neural gains may reflect regularization/data rather than model capacity.  
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic, so they cannot evaluate handling of real-world noise, ambiguity, or dialogue repair strategies.  
- Critically missing a non-neural baseline that incorporates word-order (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, match-type features), which likely undermines the claimed neural advantage.  
- Reviewer would raise the paper’s rating to 8 if such a baseline were included.  
- Minor correction: the paper’s claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures” is inaccurate; prior end-to-end task-oriented and human-evaluation studies provide such metrics.
