- Provides a critical reality check on chatbot hype, relevant to both Dialog/NLP and ML venues.  
- Significant contribution as a necessary counterpoint to overoptimistic claims about current chatbots.  
- Two guiding conjectures: end-to-end ML is insufficient for goal-oriented dialog; aligned synthetic data can predict natural-data performance.  
- Core contribution is a rigorous evaluation/deployment of memory networks with a single "match type" tweak, not a novel algorithm.  
- Experimental protocol is exemplary and the paper is unusually clear; appendix and hop-count analyses are effective.  
- Highlights the fragility and subjectivity of current dialog evaluation metrics and calls for better validation.  
- Main critique: per-response accuracy is deeply flawed because it requires exact matches for all preceding responses even when the API call is correct.  
- Dialog-level accuracy is more meaningful but extremely sensitive to experimental protocol (e.g., T3 vs T5 discrepancies).  
- Concierge dataset: "best" response among all options would better reflect utility; recommends exploring revenue-driven metrics requiring humans or simulators.  
- Vocabulary/KB mismatch: match-type needs exact KB terms, forcing manual mappings and limiting scalability and learned associations.  
- Overall strengths outweigh limitations: sets a new benchmark for dialog evaluation, is transparent about metric limits, and merits strong acceptance with future-work opportunities.
