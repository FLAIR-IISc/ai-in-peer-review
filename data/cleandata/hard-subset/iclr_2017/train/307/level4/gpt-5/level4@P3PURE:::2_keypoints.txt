- Provides a necessary reality check on the 2016 trend of applying chatbots broadly; high significance for dialog/NLP and ML venues.  
- Two key conjectures: end-to-end ML methods remain insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs; relative performance on aligned synthetic data predicts performance on natural data.  
- Algorithmic contribution is a simple match-type addition to memory networks, first tested on goal-oriented dialog with an excellent experimental protocol.  
- Paper is clear and accessible, with a concise appendix on memory networks and informative tables showing effects of number of hops.  
- Evaluation metrics are brittle and somewhat arbitrary, limiting the interpretability of results.  
- Per-response accuracy is problematic (e.g., an API call outcome can be scored very low despite being the main goal).  
- Per-dialog accuracy is extremely sensitive to experimental protocol and task definitions (explains counterintuitive T3 vs T5 numbers).  
- For concierge data, ambiguity exists whether correctness should be top-1 versus top-5, affecting evaluation.  
- Recommends outcome-driven metrics (e.g., revenue or weighted loss combining API errors, dialog length, user rejections), which require real users or realistic simulators.  
- Vocabulary mismatch is not fully addressed: match-type relies on exact token matches in the KB, whereas ML should learn associations for obfuscated or domain-specific terms.  
- Overall strong, timely work whose main concerns (metrics and vocabulary handling) point to future research directions rather than grounds for rejection.
