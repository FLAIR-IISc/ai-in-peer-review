- Paper introduces a new simulated restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems (find availability and book tables).
- Data is produced by sampling KB parameter tuples and applying rule-based NL generation to create utterances.
- Task formulation is to RANK candidate responses per turn, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on this task.
- Reviewer is skeptical about the utility of fully end-to-end systems in narrow/constrained domains where engineered systems may yield higher user satisfaction.
- Reviewer is disappointed the task is ranking rather than generating responses, questioning where candidate response sets would come from in new domains.
- Constrained variability of training responses due to rule-based generation is a concern; trade-off between unlimited data and limited linguistic variety.
- Reviewer requests publication of the NL-generation rules used to create the data.
- Overall the reviewer finds the contribution interesting and worthy of publication and updated their score after clarifications and new results.
