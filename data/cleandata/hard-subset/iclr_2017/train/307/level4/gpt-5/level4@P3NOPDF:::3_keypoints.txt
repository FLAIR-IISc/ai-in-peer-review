- Introduces a simulated restaurant-reservation dataset with KB-driven slot sampling and rule-based NL generation for end-to-end, goal-oriented dialogue.
- Task framed as response selection (ranking a fixed candidate set) with per-response and per-dialog accuracy metrics.
- Memory Networks outperform simple bag-of-words baselines, showing benefit from dialogue context and KB interactions.
- Strength: provides a focused, reproducible benchmark for a canonical goal-oriented dialogue task.
- Strength: clear evaluation protocol (per-response and per-dialog accuracies).
- Strength: simulator allows scalable data generation with controllable complexity.
- Strength: empirical evidence that memory-augmented models improve performance over basic baselines.
- Concern: narrow domain weakens the motivation for end-to-end approaches versus modular, knowledge-driven systems; comparison to strong modular baselines is missing.
- Concern: framing as response ranking (not generation) limits generalization and linguistic variability, undermining the scalability claim.
- Concern: realism of the candidate-response set is unclear—uncertain how candidates would be constructed or covered in new domains.
- Concern: rule-based utterance generation likely limits linguistic variability (paraphrases, disfluencies), potentially inflating model performance.
- Concern: unclear how models generalize to OOV entities or unseen slot combinations; no OOV/KB expansion results.
- Suggestion: release full generation rules and simulator to increase transparency and enable extensions.
- Suggestion: evaluate generation-based models (e.g., seq2seq with copy) and report automatic and human-oriented metrics.
- Suggestion: clarify and propose realistic candidate-selection mechanisms for deployment (retrieval, templating, constrained generation).
- Suggestion: add stronger modular or rule-based baselines to compare engineering cost vs. performance.
- Suggestion: include robustness tests (OOV splits, paraphrase stress tests, human paraphrase augmentations).
- Suggestion: report per-dialog goal-completion success rates and perform error analyses highlighting common failure modes.
- Overall: valuable and publishable contribution, but fixed candidate ranking and limited linguistic variability reduce practical significance; addressing candidate construction, rule release, and additional baselines would strengthen the work.
