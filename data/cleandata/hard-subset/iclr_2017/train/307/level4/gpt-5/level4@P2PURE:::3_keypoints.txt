- Introduces a new dataset for end-to-end, goal-oriented restaurant dialog (availability/booking), evaluated via per-response and per-dialog accuracy; Memory Networks beat bag-of-words baselines.
- Reviewer is skeptical about the value of end-to-end training in narrow domains where engineered, domain-knowledge systems may yield higher user satisfaction.
- Disappointment that the task is RANKING candidate responses rather than GENERATING them, despite ranking simplifying evaluation.
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in new domains; models appear to rank the fixed set used in train/val/test, which seems impractical.
- Argues generation would generalize better than ranking a hand-collected list of generic responses; views ranking as the weakest aspect of the work.
- Data are produced by simulating KB tuples and rule-based NL generation, which limits response variability.
- The rule-based NLG rules are not listed; reviewer requests publishing them for transparency and reproducibility.
- Despite reservations, reviewer finds the contribution interesting and worthy of publication; score was updated after clarifications and new results.
