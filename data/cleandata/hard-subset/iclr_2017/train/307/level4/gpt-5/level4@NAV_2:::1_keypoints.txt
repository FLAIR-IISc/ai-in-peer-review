1. Introduces a public, rule-based benchmark for goal-oriented dialogue in restaurant booking.  
2. Decomposes tasks: API call issuance/updating, ranked option presentation, and KB use in multi-turn dialogues.  
3. Testbed designed for reproducibility and targeted error analysis; includes DSTC2 and concierge evaluations.  
4. Benchmarks several methods (rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, Memory Networks) showing strengths and limits of end-to-end models.  
5. Recommendation: Weak accept.  
6. Primary reasons: benchmark is timely/useful and empirical study improved, but non-neural baselines need strengthening.  
7. Core problem: rigorously evaluating end-to-end dialogue systems for symbol manipulation, KB interfacing, and few-turn task completion.  
8. Motivation/literature fit: end-to-end approaches are promising but under-tested; controlled decomposed tasks are appropriate.  
9. Results summary: MemNNs outperform IR and simple embeddings but struggle on option presentation and full-dialog success.  
10. Concern: Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, initially lacking word-order-aware non-neural baselines, muddying attribution of gains.  
11. Suggested improvement: add competitive logistic regression/SVM baselines using averaged embeddings, n-grams, and match-type features.  
12. Suggested improvement: explicitly state determinism limitations and propose non-deterministic variants (slot order, ASR-style noise).  
13. Suggested improvement: soften/refine claims about lack of evaluation metrics—prior task-oriented and human-eval work exists.  
14. Suggested improvement: report sensitivity analyses and ablations (data size, candidate-set construction, match-type variants).  
15. Suggested improvement: analyze error types unique to real data (ASR noise, user drift, non-standard entities) to bridge synthetic/real.  
16. Question: How do you prevent candidate-set pooling from leaking task-specific patterns and advantaging models via global frequency cues?  
17. Question: How sensitive are results to the fixed slot-question order; effects of randomized slot orders or stochastic users?  
18. Question: Clarify OOV handling and KB access at test time—do models see OOV KBs and how is dialogue-text leakage prevented?  
19. Question: Can you add a stronger word-order-aware non-neural baseline (n-grams + match-type) with per-dialog and per-response accuracy?  
20. Question: How do ASR errors and non-deterministic bot behavior in DSTC2 affect label construction and are there validation/alignment checks?  
21. Question: Have you tested robustness to synthetic noise (paraphrases, typos) or alternative word orders for MemNNs and baselines?  
22. Question: How do match-type features handle ambiguous surface forms across entity types and what heuristics disambiguate them?
