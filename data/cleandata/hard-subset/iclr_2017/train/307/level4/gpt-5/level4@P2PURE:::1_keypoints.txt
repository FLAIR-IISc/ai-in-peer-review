- Paper was recently posted to arXiv.
- Introduces a new public dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue.
- Dataset/tasks are artificially constructed via rule-based programs to isolate dialogue capabilities.
- Contribution is valuable and can facilitate future dialogue research.
- Unclear whether Deep Learning is appropriate versus traditional methods given data needs; reported differences may reflect regularization choices.
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic and do not evaluate handling of noisy/ambiguous interactions or dialogue repair.
- No baseline that incorporates word order, a significant weakness that may overstate neural models' advantage.
- Recommend adding a non-neural benchmark with word-order features (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bigrams, match-type); would increase reviewer score.
- Conclusion's claim that prior work lacks well-defined performance measures is inaccurate; prior papers (Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.) provide evaluation methods.
