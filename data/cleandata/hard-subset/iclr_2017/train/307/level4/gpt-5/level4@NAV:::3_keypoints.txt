- Paper introduces a new simulated restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue (booking/checking availability).
- Data are generated by sampling KB parameter tuples and applying rule-based NL generation to produce utterances.
- Task/objective is to rank candidate responses at each turn; evaluation uses per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform simple bag-of-words baselines.
- Reviewer is skeptical about end-to-end methods in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered, knowledge-driven systems may yield higher user satisfaction.
- Concern that ranking (not generation) is a weak objective; unclear how candidate response pools would be obtained in new domains.
- Generative approaches likely generalize better across domains than ranking from a hand-collected pool, which the reviewer views as the paper’s weakest aspect.
- Rule-based NLG constrains linguistic variability; reviewer requests release of the rule set used.
- Despite reservations, reviewer finds the contribution interesting and recommends acceptance.
