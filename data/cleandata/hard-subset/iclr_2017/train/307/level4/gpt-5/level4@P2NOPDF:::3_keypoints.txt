- Introduces a new restaurant-reservation dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, framed as ranking candidate responses with per-response and per-dialog accuracy; Memory Networks beat bag-of-words baselines.  
- Reviewer skeptical about the practical benefit of end-to-end methods in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered systems may be more effective.  
- Task is limited to ranking rather than response generation, which simplifies evaluation but reduces applicability and generalization.  
- Unclear how candidate response sets are obtained in practice and how this approach would scale to unseen domains; ranking from observed responses is a major weakness.  
- Data is produced by a simulator with rule-based NLG, so linguistic variability is constrained by the rule set.  
- The paper does not publish the generation rules/templates; releasing them would improve transparency, reproducibility, and allow bias assessment.  
- Recommends adding a response generation track and metrics to better evaluate generalization and portability.  
- Overall positive: setup is clear, metrics appropriate, empirical gains meaningful; reviewer considers the contribution worthwhile and updated their score.
