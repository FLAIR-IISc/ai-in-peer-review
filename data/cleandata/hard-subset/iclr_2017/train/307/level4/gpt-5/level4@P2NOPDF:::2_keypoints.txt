- Paper is a valuable, highly significant reality check on broad 2016 “chatbots for everything” claims and relevant to both dialog/NLP and ML venues.
- Two implicit conjectures for future work: (1) end-to-end ML is still insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs under these metrics; (2) relative performance on matched synthetic data can predict natural data performance.
- Main algorithmic contribution is a simple match-type augmentation to memory networks; notable as the first deployment/evaluation of memory networks on a goal-oriented dialog task.
- Experimental protocol is strong, with careful alignment of synthetic and natural settings and clear analysis (e.g., effect of number of hops).
- Paper is clearly written and accessible, with a concise appendix summarizing memory networks.
- Current dialog metrics are brittle and can be counterintuitive, risking mismeasurement of true system capability.
- Per-response accuracy (next-utterance classification over fixed response set) can massively undervalue goal completion when wording deviates earlier in the dialog.
- Per-dialog accuracy is highly sensitive to task design and small definition choices, which can dominate outcomes.
- For concierge data, reporting top-1 correctness rather than top-5 could substantially change comparative results and better reflect user expectations.
- More meaningful evaluation should model business value (API error severity, turns to completion, user rejections) and likely requires real users or high-fidelity simulators.
- Match-type mechanism depends on exact string matches and is brittle to vocabulary/KB mismatch; learned entity linking or synonym induction would be preferable.
- Overall assessment: high significance, modest algorithmic novelty, strong empirical quality, excellent clarity; paper should be published.
