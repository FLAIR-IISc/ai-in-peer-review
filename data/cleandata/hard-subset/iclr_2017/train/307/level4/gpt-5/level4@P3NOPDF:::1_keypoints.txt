- Introduces a new public dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue with rule-based task generation targeting specific capabilities.
- Clearly defined benchmark that decomposes dialogue capabilities into targeted tasks for systematic evaluation.
- Public release of data and tasks should standardize comparisons and accelerate community progress.
- Focus on goal-oriented dialogue complements existing open-domain chat research.
- Unclear whether deep models truly outperform simpler rule-based or shallow models given dataset size and sensitivity to training/tuning.
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic and do not test robustness to noise, ambiguity, user variability, or error recovery.
- Lacks a competitive non-neural baseline that captures word order, which may overstate neural model advantages.
- Recommend adding a strong order-aware baseline (e.g., logistic/linear classifier with word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features).
- Suggest augmenting tasks with stochastic user behaviors or noise (paraphrases, typos, ellipses, missing/conflicting info) to improve realism.
- Recommend analyses to separate data-size and regularization effects from architectural benefits (learning curves, parameter sweeps, ablations).
- Minor corrections: claim that prior work has no well-defined performance measures is inaccurate; cite Wen et al. and other evaluation studies.
- Minor proofreading needed for typos and consistency (e.g., duplicated words, “performance” vs. “performances”).
- Overall: valuable and timely contribution, but empirical claims are limited by deterministic tasks and missing order-sensitive baseline; addressing these would strengthen the paper.
