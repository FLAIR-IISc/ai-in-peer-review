- Introduces an open, reproducible testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue, decomposing restaurant booking into five synthetic subtasks plus DSTC2 and concierge real-data sets.  
- Framework supports per-turn and per-dialog evaluation and systematic error analysis.  
- Benchmarks rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, and Memory Networks (with/without match-type); MemNNs perform well but struggle with KB use and OOV entities.  
- Decision: Accept — dataset fills a community gap by providing a controlled, reproducible benchmark with synthetic decomposition and real-data validation.  
- Empirical study is carefully executed and exposes strengths and limits of end-to-end methods (e.g., match types help OOVs; option ranking and KB grounding remain hard).  
- Task breakdown is well motivated and aligns with prior work (bAbI, DSTC); per-dialog accuracy is an appropriate metric.  
- Claims are supported by comprehensive baselines and ablations; successes include state tracking and API call handling, failures include option ranking and robust KB grounding, with patterns corroborated on DSTC2 and concierge.  
- Limitation: Synthetic tasks are deterministic, so they don’t test robustness to noise, ambiguity, or repair strategies found in real-world settings.  
- Limitation: Initial baselines lacked a simple non-neural model with word-order features (authors later added word-order experiments).  
- Suggestion: Add non-neural baselines that include word-order (e.g., logistic regression/linear ranker with averaged embeddings, bigrams, match-type) to isolate deep-model benefits.  
- Suggestion: Soften claims about the absence of well-defined metrics and acknowledge prior task-oriented and human-in-the-loop evaluation work.  
- Suggestion: Report learning curves/data-efficiency to show how model performance scales with training size.  
- Suggestion: Introduce stochastic/noisy variants (ASR-like noise, paraphrasing, variable question order, probabilistic user simulator) to test robustness.  
- Suggestion: Discuss ranking vs. generation and report results with constrained decoding or copy/pointer mechanisms for OOV handling.  
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility — release simulator and DSTC2 conversion scripts, document candidate-set construction, and ablate match-type features.  
- Question: How does pooling all bot utterances/API calls as a candidate set affect realism and difficulty versus task-specific candidate pools?  
- Question: How sensitive are models to expanding/diversifying templates and to paraphrase augmentation?  
- Question: How does match-type OOV handling generalize when KB coverage is incomplete or entities appear only in user text?  
- Question: For option ranking, do errors arise from sorting by rating, tracking proposed options, or accept/reject state — any ablations to disentangle these?  
- Question: Does converting DSTC2 (adding ratings) alter original task semantics, and how often do converted labels disagree with original system behavior?  
- Question: Provide learning curves: how do MemNNs vs supervised embeddings compare across Tasks 1–5 and DSTC2, and are there regimes where simpler models win?  
- Reviewer notes appreciation for additional experiments and updated score; resource likely to have significant impact.
