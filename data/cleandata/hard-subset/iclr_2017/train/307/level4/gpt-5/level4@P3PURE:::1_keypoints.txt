- Introduces a new public, rule-based synthetic dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue to isolate and evaluate different system aspects—a welcome resource.
- Questions suitability of deep learning for these tasks versus rule-based/shallow models, noting DL needs many examples and reported differences may reflect regularization choices rather than real modeling gains.
- Points out Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, so they do not test handling of noisy or ambiguous interactions (e.g., inferring goal distributions or dialogue repair), which matters for real-world systems.
- Criticizes omission of a baseline that captures word order, calling it a major weakness since simple non-neural baselines (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bigrams, and match-type features) could be competitive; including one would raise the paper’s score.
- Notes the paper’s claim that existing work lacks well-defined performance measures is incorrect and cites prior end-to-end and human-evaluation studies (e.g., Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).
- Reviewer updated their score after the authors added new results.
