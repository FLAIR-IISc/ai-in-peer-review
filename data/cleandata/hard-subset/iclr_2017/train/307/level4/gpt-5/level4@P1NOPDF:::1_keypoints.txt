- Paper introduces a new public, artificially constructed dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue (API calls, displaying options, full dialogues), generated by rule-based programs.  
- Contribution is welcome and should facilitate future dialogue research.  
- Concern: unclear suitability of deep learning models versus traditional rule-based or shallow models for these synthetic tasks.  
- Deep models need many examples; observed performance gaps may reflect regularization differences rather than representational advantages.  
- Tasks 1–5 are completely deterministic, so they don’t evaluate handling of noisy/ambiguous interactions, goal distributions, or dialogue repair strategies.  
- Significant weakness: paper lacks a baseline that accounts for word order, making neural models seem unreasonably strong.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bigram features, and match-type features).  
- If such a word-order baseline is included, reviewer would raise the score to 8.  
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performances” is inaccurate—prior end-to-end task-oriented systems and human-evaluation methods exist (e.g., Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Reviewer updated their score after new results were added to the paper.
