- Introduces a new public dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue, useful for standardizing evaluation and spurring research.  
- Tasks are generated by rule-based programs to isolate specific capabilities from API calls to full multi-turn dialogues.  
- Unclear how suitable deep learning is relative to traditional rule-based or shallow models given data requirements and potential regularization effects.  
- Neural performance differences may reflect regularization choices rather than superior representations.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic and do not test handling of noisy, ambiguous interactions, user-goal distributions, or dialogue repair.  
- Overall direction is promising despite the above limitations.  
- Major weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, likely inflating neural model performance.  
- Recommend adding a non-neural baseline with word-order features (e.g., logistic regression using embeddings, bigrams, and match-type features); would increase reviewer score if included.  
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well defined measures” is inaccurate — end-to-end task-oriented systems and non-task dialogue have evaluative metrics and human-study methods; reviewer updated score after new results.
