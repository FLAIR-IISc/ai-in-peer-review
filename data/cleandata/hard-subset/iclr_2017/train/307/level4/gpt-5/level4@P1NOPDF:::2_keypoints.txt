- The paper is an important reality check on chatbots, relevant to dialog/NLP and ML venues and highly significant.  
- Two motivating conjectures: (1) end-to-end ML is insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs; (2) relative performance on well-aligned synthetic data predicts performance on natural data.  
- Algorithmic contribution: introduces a simple “match type” addition to memory networks and is the first evaluation of such models on goal-oriented dialog with a strong experimental protocol.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and accessible, with a helpful appendix and tables showing hop effects.  
- Current dialog metrics are brittle and can be misleading; per-response accuracy especially underestimates task success because it requires many intermediate responses to be exact.  
- Per-dialog accuracy is also sensitive to protocol definitions (illustrated by the surprising T3 vs T5 results).  
- Metric/design choices matter: e.g., for concierge data, should “correct” mean the single best option or inclusion in the top five?  
- Recommend goal-aligned loss functions that weight API-call errors, dialog length, and user-rejected options—requiring real users or realistic simulators to evaluate.  
- Representation-learning limitation: match-type needs exact token matches between user language and KB terms; ML should learn such associations instead of relying on hand-crafted mappings.
