- Introduces a new dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant domain.  
- Dialogs are simulated by sampling KB parameter tuples and using rule-based NLG to create utterances.  
- Task is to rank candidate responses per dialog turn; evaluation via per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform simple bag-of-words baselines on this benchmark.  
- Reviewer is skeptical about the value of end-to-end training in narrow domains where engineered systems leverage domain expertise better.  
- Disappointment that the task focuses on ranking rather than response generation, which may limit practical usefulness.  
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained for new domains, raising portability concerns.  
- Argues generative models may generalize better to unseen domains than systems that rank hand-collected responses.  
- Data variability is limited by rule-based NLG, so simulated utterances may lack natural-language diversity.  
- Requests publication of the NLG rules to improve transparency and reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and useful contribution with reservations; merits acceptance; score updated after clarifications.
