- Introduces a new publicly available, synthetically generated dataset and tasks for goal-directed dialogue evaluation.  
- Dataset/tasks enable evaluation across facets from API calls and option presentation to full dialogues.  
- Valuable contribution likely to facilitate future dialogue system development and analysis.  
- Concern: unclear if Deep Learning is appropriate versus rule-based/shallow models given data size and regularization effects.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, so they do not test noise, ambiguity, user-goal distributions, or dialogue repair.  
- Significant weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, which may inflate neural methods’ apparent advantage.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural baseline accounting for word order (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features).  
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performances” is inaccurate—prior end-to-end task-oriented and conversational evaluation work exists (e.g., Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Reviewer would raise their score if the suggested baseline is added and notes they updated their score after new results.
