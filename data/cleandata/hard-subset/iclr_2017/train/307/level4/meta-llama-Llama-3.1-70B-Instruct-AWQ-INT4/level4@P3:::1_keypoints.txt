- Introduces a new public dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue; accepted primarily for this contribution.  
- Dataset/tasks are artificially constructed using rule-based programs, enabling systematic evaluation of multiple dialogue aspects (API calls, options, full dialogues).  
- Artificial construction is double-edged: allows control but may not reflect noisy, ambiguous real-world dialogues.  
- Tasks 1–5 are completely deterministic, so evaluation won’t measure models’ ability to handle noise or ambiguity.  
- Reviewer doubts suitability of Deep Learning models for these tasks and notes neural nets may appear unreasonably strong.  
- Major weakness: no baseline model that incorporates word order, which could make simpler methods competitive or superior.  
- Recommendation: include an additional non-neural benchmark that captures word order to fairly assess representation learning.  
- Minor correction: statement that existing work lacks well-defined performance measures is incorrect; end-to-end task-oriented dialogue work (e.g., Wen et al.) has measures.  
- Requested authors to discuss limitations of artificially constructed dataset and its real-world representativeness.  
- Requested clarification of the conclusion’s claim about absence of well-defined performance measures.  
- Question: How will the authors address dataset/task limitations to make them more representative of real-world dialogues?  
- Question: Can the authors provide details on the baseline model with word order information they plan to include?  
- Question: How would results change if tasks 1–5 were not completely deterministic?
