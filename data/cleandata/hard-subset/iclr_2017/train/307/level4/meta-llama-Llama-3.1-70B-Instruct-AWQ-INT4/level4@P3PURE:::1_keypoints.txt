- Introduces a new publicly available, rule-based synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue covering API calls, option display, and full dialogues — a useful resource for the field.  
- Unclear whether deep learning models are appropriate compared to traditional or shallow methods, since they require large data and differences may reflect regularization.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, so evaluation won’t test model robustness to noise, ambiguity, goal distributions, or dialogue-repair strategies.  
- Major evaluation weakness: no baseline that incorporates word-order information, making neural models appear overly strong; simpler baselines may be competitive.  
- Strong recommendation to add a non-neural benchmark with word-order features (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, and match-type features); inclusion could raise the reviewer’s score to 8.  
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures” is inaccurate — prior work (e.g., Wen et al.) and human-evaluation studies (e.g., Liu et al., Yu et al.) provide evaluation measures.  
- Reviewer updated their score after the authors added new results.
