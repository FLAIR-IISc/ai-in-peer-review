- Introduces a novel public dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, constructed via rule-based programs covering API calls, option display, and full dialogue interactions.
- Provides a valuable contribution to dialogue literature that will facilitate future research.
- Unclear whether Deep Learning models are appropriate compared to rule-based or shallow models given DL’s need for large training data.
- Deterministic nature of tasks 1–5 means evaluations won’t test robustness to noisy or ambiguous interactions.
- Overall direction is interesting and worth exploring further.
- Major weakness: paper lacks a baseline that incorporates word order, which may unfairly favor neural models.
- Recommendation to add a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bigram features, and match-type features) for fair comparison.
- If the baseline issue is addressed, the reviewer would raise the rating to 8.
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performances” is inaccurate—end-to-end task-oriented systems and human-evaluation methods exist.
- Paper score was updated after the authors added new results.
