- Paper provides a crucial reality check on the 2016 trend of using chatbots, highlighting limitations of current systems and need for realistic ML validation on dialogs.  
- Timely and significant contribution for Dialog/NLP and ML venues by emphasizing practical shortcomings of chatbots.  
- Two conjectures likely to spur research: end-to-end ML is still insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs.  
- Second conjecture: relative performance on synthetic data can predict natural data performance if conditions are carefully matched.  
- Novel algorithmic contribution: a simple, effective "match type" addition to memory networks, first deployed on a goal-oriented dialog.  
- Experimental protocol is well-designed and the paper is very clear and accessible.  
- Appendix summary of memory networks and tables on number of hops are informative and valuable.  
- Paper exposes the brittleness and arbitrariness of common dialog evaluation metrics, urging future work on better metrics.  
- Per-response accuracy is problematic and can be misleading; per-dialog accuracy may be preferable but is sensitive to protocol.  
- Standard metrics may be suboptimal; propose task-weighted measures (e.g., weighted error sum reflecting API correctness, turns, rejected options) requiring real users or realistic simulators.  
- Paper insufficiently addresses vocabulary mismatch between user and knowledge base; match type relies on exact matches and underscores need for representation learning to associate words and concepts.
