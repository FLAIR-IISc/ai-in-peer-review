- Introduces a novel, publicly available synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue evaluation.  
- Tasks span from issuing API calls to displaying options and full multi-turn dialogues.  
- Contribution is a welcome addition that will facilitate future dialogue research.  
- Unclear whether deep learning models are the most suitable approach compared to rule-based or shallow methods.  
- Differences between neural models may reflect regularization or data volume effects rather than true superiority.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic and do not test handling of noisy/ambiguous interactions, user goal distributions, or dialogue repair strategies.  
- Missing baseline that incorporates word order makes neural models appear unreasonably strong.  
- Recommend adding a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram and match-type features); inclusion would raise the reviewer’s rating to 8.  
- Minor inaccuracy: claim that "existing work has no well-defined measures of performance" is incorrect — end-to-end task-oriented models have measures (Wen et al.), and non-goal systems can be evaluated with human studies (Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Reviewer updated their score after the new results added to the paper.
