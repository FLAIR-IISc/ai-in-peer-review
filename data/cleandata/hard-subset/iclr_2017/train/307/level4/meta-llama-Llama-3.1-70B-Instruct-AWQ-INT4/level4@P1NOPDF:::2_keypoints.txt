1. Paper provides a needed reality check on chatbot capabilities and assesses the current state of chatbots.  
2. Highly relevant and significant for Dialog/NLP and ML communities.  
3. Conjecture 1: End-to-end ML approaches remain insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs.  
4. Conjecture 2: Relative performance on synthetic data can predict performance on natural data when conditions are well matched.  
5. Authors carefully matched synthetic and natural conditions to support comparisons.  
6. Main algorithmic contribution is a simple "match type" addition to memory networks, first tested on goal-oriented dialog.  
7. Experimental protocol and paper clarity are excellent; appendix and tables effectively explain memory networks and hop effects.  
8. Paper advances rigorous dialog metrics but highlights their brittleness and arbitrariness.  
9. Per-response accuracy is problematic (it penalizes intermediate bot replies even when the critical API call is correct).  
10. Per-dialog accuracy might be a better metric; metrics are highly sensitive to task definitions and protocol (e.g., T3 vs T5 discrepancy).  
11. For concierge data, evaluating "best" rather than "in top-5" responses would be informative.  
12. Proposes revenue-aligned loss (weighted sum of API errors, dialog length, and rejected options) as a more meaningful metric, requiring humans or realistic simulators.  
13. Unaddressed: vocabulary mismatches between user language and KB terms—match type requires exact KB occurrences and doesn’t learn associations automatically.
