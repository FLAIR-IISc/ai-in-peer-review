- Introduces a new dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented restaurant dialog focused on finding availability and booking tables.
- Dataset is generated by a simulator that uses an underlying knowledge base and rule-based NL transformations for parameters like cuisine and price.
- Evaluation frames the task as ranking candidate responses per next turn, reported via per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on this ranking task.
- Reviewer appreciates the dataset contribution but questions the utility of end-to-end systems in narrow, well-understood domains.
- Argues that in narrow domains it may be better to incorporate domain knowledge and engineer optimal systems for user satisfaction.
- Major concern: focusing on ranking predefined responses rather than generating them limits practicality and scalability to new domains.
- Unclear how candidate responses would be obtained in real-world settings; ranking from train/val/test sets seems unrealistic.
- Simulation-based NL generation likely limits variability in training data, and the paper does not publish the NL-generation rules.
- Suggests publishing the simulator rules would benefit future research.
- Despite reservations, reviewer considers the paper an interesting contribution and recommends publication after clarifications.
