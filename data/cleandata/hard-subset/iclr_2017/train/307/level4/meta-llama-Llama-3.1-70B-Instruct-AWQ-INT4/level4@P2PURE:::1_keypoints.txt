- Introduces a new, publicly available, rule-based synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, a welcome contribution that will facilitate future research.
- Questions suitability of deep learning for these tasks versus traditional/rule-based or shallow models, noting neural models need large data and gains may stem from regularization.
- Notes tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, so they fail to evaluate models' ability to handle noisy/ambiguous interactions, user-goal distributions, or dialogue repair strategies.
- Identifies a major methodological weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, which may make the neural models appear unrealistically strong.
- Recommends adding a non-neural baseline that includes word order (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, match-type features); inclusion could raise the review rating.
- Challenges the paper's claim that existing work lacks well-defined performance measures, citing prior end-to-end task-oriented evaluation (Wen et al.) and human-evaluation studies for non-task dialogue (Liu et al., Yu et al.).
