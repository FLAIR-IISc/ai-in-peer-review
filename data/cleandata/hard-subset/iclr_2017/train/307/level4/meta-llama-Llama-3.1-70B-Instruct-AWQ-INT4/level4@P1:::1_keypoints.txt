- Introduces a new public, rule-based dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue covering API calls, option displays, and full dialogues.  
- Contribution is welcome and will facilitate future dialogue research.  
- Reviewer leans to accept because of a well-structured, systematic evaluation framework.  
- Paper provides thorough analysis of model strengths/weaknesses (Memory Networks, Supervised Embeddings, IR models).  
- Concern: tasks 1–5 are deterministic, so they do not test handling of noisy or ambiguous interactions.  
- Concern: no baseline model that incorporates word order, making neural models appear overly strong.  
- Strong recommendation to add a non-neural baseline with word-order features (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, match-type features).  
- Suggestion: provide a detailed comparison of the proposed tasks with existing dialogue datasets and highlight unique aspects.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of deterministic tasks and propose extensions to model noise/ambiguity.  
- Suggestion: run additional baseline experiments, including non-neural models with word-order information.  
- Suggestion: clarify the conclusion about lack of well-defined performance measures for end-to-end task-oriented dialogue models.  
- Questions for authors: detail how tasks were designed to evaluate different dialogue aspects.  
- Questions for authors: explain plans to extend tasks to handle noisy/ambiguous interactions.  
- Questions for authors: discuss potential real-world applications of the proposed tasks and models.  
- Overall: significant contribution that could become a valuable resource after revisions.
