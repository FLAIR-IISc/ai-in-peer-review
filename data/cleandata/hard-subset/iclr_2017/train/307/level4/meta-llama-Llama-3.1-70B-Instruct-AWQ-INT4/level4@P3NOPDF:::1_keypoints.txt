- Proposes a novel, publicly available, rule-based dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue covering API calls, option display, and full dialogues.  
- Dataset is a valuable contribution that will facilitate future dialogue-system research.  
- Unclear whether Deep Learning is suitable compared to traditional or shallow models, since DL needs large data and differences may stem from regularization.  
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, so they do not evaluate handling of noisy or ambiguous interactions and dialogue-repair capabilities.  
- Major evaluation flaw: no baseline that incorporates word-order information, making neural models appear overly strong.  
- Strong recommendation to add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression using word embeddings, bi-gram, and match-type features) to fairly assess representation learning.  
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures” is inaccurate—prior task-oriented and non-task evaluations (e.g., Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.) provide measurable evaluation methods.  
- Reviewer updated their score based on new results added to the paper.
