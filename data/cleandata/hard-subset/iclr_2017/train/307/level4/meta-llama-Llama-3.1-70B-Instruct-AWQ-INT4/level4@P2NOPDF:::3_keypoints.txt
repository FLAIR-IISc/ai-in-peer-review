- Introduces a novel restaurant-focused dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.  
- Dataset generated via simulation using a knowledge base and rule-based natural language descriptions.  
- Task formulation: rank candidate responses per dialog turn, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on the task.  
- Reviewer questions the utility of end-to-end approaches in narrow, well-understood domains versus engineered domain-specific systems.  
- Concern that ranking responses (rather than generating them) limits practicality and evaluation realism.  
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in new domains, challenging the claim of end-to-end scalability.  
- Simulation + rule-based NLG likely limits variability in training responses; publishing the rules would be beneficial.  
- Overall view: interesting and publishable contribution; reviewer raised concerns but updated score after authors’ clarifications and new results.
