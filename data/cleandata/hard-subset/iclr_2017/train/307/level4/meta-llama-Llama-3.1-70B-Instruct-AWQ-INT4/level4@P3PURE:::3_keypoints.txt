- Introduces a new dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented restaurant dialog systems focused on finding availability and booking tables.
- Data is generated by simulating dialogs using an underlying knowledge base and applying rule-based natural language transformations.
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses for each next turn, evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines.
- Reviewer skeptical about the utility of end-to-end systems in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered solutions may be more practical.
- Disappointment that the objective is response ranking rather than generative modeling, which simplifies evaluation but limits realism.
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in practice, especially when scaling to new domains.
- Ranking pre-collected responses weakens the main scalability argument for end-to-end approaches versus generating novel responses.
- Rule-based NL generation constrains variability in training data, a trade-off of simulator-driven unlimited data.
- The list of NL-generation rules used in data creation is not provided and should be published.
- Overall assessment: interesting and publishable contribution, with reviewer updating score after clarifications and new results.
