- Introduces a new public, artificially constructed dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue using rule-based programs.
- Dataset/tasks aim to evaluate different dialogue system aspects from API calls to option display and full dialogues.
- Reviewer welcomes the contribution as useful for future dialogue research.
- Paper accepted primarily because of the new dataset/tasks and extensive experimental evaluation.
- Concern that suitability of deep learning vs. traditional/rule-based or shallow models is unclear due to deep models' data needs and regularization effects.
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, so they do not test handling of noisy or ambiguous interactions (e.g., inferring user goal distributions or dialogue repair).
- Suggest adding a non-neural baseline that captures word order to better demonstrate deep models' utility.
- Concrete baseline suggestion: logistic regression using word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features.
- Pointed out that the claim "existing work has no well defined measures of performances" is inaccurate; cites Wen et al. (end-to-end task-oriented) and Liu et al. (human evaluation for non-goal-oriented).
- Asks authors to clarify how they will address deterministic-task limitations regarding noise and ambiguity.
- Requests more details on the proposed logistic regression baseline with word-order information.
- Requests the authors' response to the criticism about the statement on lack of well-defined performance measures.
