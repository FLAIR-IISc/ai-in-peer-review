- Introduces a novel simulated dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented restaurant dialog (table availability and booking).
- Dataset generation uses a knowledge base and rule-based natural language templates to expand parameter tuples (e.g., cuisine, price).
- Evaluation frames response selection as ranking candidate responses, reported with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on this task.
- Reviewer questions the utility of end-to-end training in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered systems may yield higher user satisfaction.
- Concern that focusing on ranking rather than response generation limits practical applicability and generalization to new domains.
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in real-world or new-domain settings; paper may rank over all train/val/test responses.
- Simulation and rule-based NL generation likely limit variability in training responses, reducing realism.
- The specific NL-generation rules used in simulation are not provided and should be made publicly available.
- Despite reservations, reviewer finds the work interesting and worthy of publication.
- Reviewer updated their score positively after clarifications and new results.
