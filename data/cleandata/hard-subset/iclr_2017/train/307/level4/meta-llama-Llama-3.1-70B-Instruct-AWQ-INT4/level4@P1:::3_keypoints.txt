- Introduces a new restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.  
- Data generated via simulation over a knowledge base and rendered to natural language with rule-based transformations.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate next-turn responses; evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines.  
- Reviewer skeptical about the utility of end-to-end training in narrow domains where engineered systems can leverage domain knowledge for higher user satisfaction.  
- Concern that focusing on RANK rather than GENERATE limits realism and practical utility.  
- Questions practicality of obtaining candidate response lists in new domains — paper appears to rank responses from train/val/test.  
- Argues generation would generalize better to new domains than ranking a fixed set of hand-collected responses.  
- Simulator-based NL rules limit variability of training responses, trading realism for limitless data.  
- Requests publication of the NL-generation rules used in the simulator.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting contribution and worthy of publication despite reservations.
