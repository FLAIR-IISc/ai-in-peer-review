This paper introduces a new dataset for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems, specifically in the restaurant setting where the goal is to find availability and eventually book a table based on parameters provided by the user to the bot as part of a dialog. The data is generated by running a simulation using an underlying knowledge base to generate samples for the different parameters, and then applying rule-based transformations to render natural language descriptions. The objective is to rank a set of candidate responses for each next turn of the dialog, and evaluation is reported in terms of per-response accuracy and per-dialog accuracy.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Memory Networks in improving over basic bag-of-words baselines, showcasing their potential in goal-oriented dialog systems. However, I have some reservations regarding the utility of end-to-end trained systems in narrow-domain settings. In such scenarios, domain knowledge can be leveraged to engineer high-quality systems, making the need for end-to-end training less compelling.

Furthermore, I am disappointed that the goal is to rank responses instead of generating them, which would allow for better generalization to new domains. The current approach relies on ranking a set of pre-collected generic responses, which may not be feasible in practice, especially when scaling to new domains. Generating responses would be a more robust and scalable approach.

Additionally, the data generation process using a simulation may constrain the variability in training responses, which could impact the model's ability to generalize. Although the authors mention that the simulator can generate unlimited data, it would be beneficial to publish the list of rules used in the simulation to provide more transparency.

Despite these concerns, I believe this is an interesting contribution worthy of publication. The authors have made a valuable effort in introducing a new dataset and demonstrating the potential of Memory Networks in goal-oriented dialog systems.

**Decision:** Accept

**Key reasons:**

1. The paper introduces a new dataset for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
2. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Memory Networks in improving over basic bag-of-words baselines.

**Supporting arguments:**

* The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed dataset and models, including comparisons with various baselines.
* The authors discuss the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future work.

**Additional feedback:**

* Consider exploring response generation instead of ranking to improve generalization to new domains.
* Provide more details on the simulation rules used to generate the data to increase transparency.
* Discuss the potential applications and implications of using end-to-end trained systems in narrow-domain settings.

**Questions for the authors:**

* Can you provide more information on how the candidate responses were obtained in practice?
* How do you plan to address the limitations of the ranking approach and explore response generation in future work?
* Can you elaborate on the potential benefits and drawbacks of using end-to-end trained systems in narrow-domain settings?
