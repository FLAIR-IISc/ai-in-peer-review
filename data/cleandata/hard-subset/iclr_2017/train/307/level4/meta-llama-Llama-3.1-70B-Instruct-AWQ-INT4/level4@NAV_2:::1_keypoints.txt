- Introduces a new public, artificially constructed dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue covering API calls, option displays, and full dialogues.  
- Recommends acceptance because the dataset/tasks will facilitate future dialogue-system research.  
- Concern: unclear whether Deep Learning is suitable compared to rule-based or shallow methods, since DL needs many examples and differences may reflect regularization.  
- Concern: Tasks 1–5 are completely deterministic, so they do not test handling of noisy or ambiguous interactions (e.g., inferring goal distributions or dialogue repair).  
- Suggests adding a non-neural benchmark that captures word order (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bigram features, and match-type features) to better justify DL utility.  
- Requests correction of the claim that “existing work has no well defined measures,” citing prior task-oriented evaluation (Wen et al.) and non-task evaluation approaches (Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Asks for clarification: (1) more details on construction of tasks 1–5 and their relation to real-world dialogue applications.  
- Asks for clarification: (2) plans to extend tasks to handle noisy and ambiguous interactions.  
- Asks for clarification: (3) details on the logistic regression baseline experimented with and how it compares to the Deep Learning models.  
- Overall: views direction as interesting and expects authors to address the raised concerns.
