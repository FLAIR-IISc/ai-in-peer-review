- Introduces a novel, simulated restaurant-domain dataset for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.  
- Dataset is generated from a knowledge base using rule-based transformations to create natural-language descriptions.  
- Task formulation focuses on ranking candidate responses per dialog turn, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on this dataset.  
- Reviewer appreciates the contribution but questions the utility of end-to-end training in narrow domains where engineered, domain-specific systems may be preferable.  
- Concern that focusing on response ranking (vs. generation) limits practicality and generalization to new domains.  
- Doubt about real-world availability of candidate response sets, since the method ranks responses drawn from the dataset itself.  
- Simulation-based data generation may restrict variability in training responses; reviewer requests publication of NL-generation rules.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting contribution and worthy of publication despite the noted limitations.
