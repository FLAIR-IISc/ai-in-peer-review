- Proposes a testbed to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems in a restaurant-reservation context.  
- Designs tasks targeting dialog management, knowledge-base querying, and output interpretation to dissect system abilities.  
- Evaluates multiple learning methods: rule-based, IR models, supervised embeddings, and end-to-end Memory Networks.  
- Claims a systematic, controlled evaluation that improves reproducibility and better aligns with human judgments than existing measures.  
- Argues the contribution is well-motivated and fills a gap in evaluation methodology for goal-oriented dialog.  
- Presents a thorough, comprehensive analysis of strengths and weaknesses across evaluated approaches.  
- Accepts the paper based on its motivation, placement in literature, and depth of evaluation.  
- Reservation: focuses on ranking candidate responses rather than generating responses, raising practical concerns.  
- Concern about how candidate responses would be obtained in real systems and how well the approach generalizes to new domains.  
- Notes variability in training responses caused by simulator-generated NL-expansions of (cuisine, price, ...) tuples.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed information about the NL-generation rules used to create training data.  
- Requests discussion on obtaining candidate responses in practice, handling training-response variability, and implications for evaluation and generalization.
