- Introduces a new dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant domain, a valuable contribution.
- Data is generated via simulation backed by a knowledge base, then rendered to natural language with rule-based transformations.
- Task is framed as ranking a set of candidate responses for each next turn, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines, showing promise for goal-oriented dialog systems.
- Reviewer questions the utility of end-to-end training in narrow domains where engineered domain knowledge can produce high-quality systems.
- Criticism that ranking pre-collected responses (vs. generation) limits generalization and scalability to new domains.
- Concern that simulation-based generation may constrain response variability and hurt generalization.
- Requests publication of the simulator’s rule list to improve transparency.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
- Supporting argument: paper provides thorough evaluation and comparisons with multiple baselines.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline avenues for future work.
- Suggested next steps: explore response generation instead of ranking to improve generalization.
- Suggested detail: clarify how candidate responses were obtained in practice.
- Questions for authors: plans to address ranking limitations, explore generation, and elaborate on pros/cons of end-to-end systems in narrow domains.
