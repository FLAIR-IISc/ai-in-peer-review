- Paper introduces a new publicly available, rule-based dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, enabling evaluation from API calls to full dialogues.  
- Contribution is welcome and should facilitate future research on dialogue systems.  
- Dataset/tasks are artificially constructed using rule-based programs, which may limit realism.  
- Unclear suitability of Deep Learning models vs traditional/rule-based or shallow models, given DL's large-data needs.  
- Reported neural performance differences may reflect regularization choices rather than true model superiority.  
- Tasks 1–5 are completely deterministic, so they don't test handling of noisy, ambiguous interactions or dialogue repair.  
- Major weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, making neural models appear unreasonably strong.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings + bi-gram + match-type features) to fairly assess representation learning.  
- Minor correction: claim that "existing work has no well-defined measures" is inaccurate—there are end-to-end task-oriented evaluation measures and human-evaluation methods for non-goal dialogues (Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Reviewer notes they updated their score after new results and would raise it to 8 if the additional baseline is included.
