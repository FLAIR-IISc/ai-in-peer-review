- Proposes a testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems in a restaurant reservation setting using a KB-driven simulator.
- Simulator generates data allowing creation of multiple tasks that probe different dialog capabilities.
- Evaluates several models: rule-based systems, information retrieval, supervised embeddings, and end-to-end Memory Networks.
- Memory Networks outperform other models but struggle with tasks requiring entity interpretation and result presentation.
- Reviewer questions the utility of end-to-end trained systems in narrow domains where engineered systems may be more effective.
- Focusing on ranking candidate responses rather than generating responses may limit generalizability to new domains.
- Simulation-based data generation enables unlimited data but may constrain variability in training responses.
- Reviewer requests the list of rules used to generate the simulated data for transparency.
- Reviewer asks for clarification on how candidate responses were obtained and suggests exploring alternative response-generation methods.
- Recommends more discussion of trade-offs between end-to-end and engineered systems in narrow-domain settings.
- Suggests additional analysis: error analysis and clearer discussion of strengths and weaknesses of each model.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions.
