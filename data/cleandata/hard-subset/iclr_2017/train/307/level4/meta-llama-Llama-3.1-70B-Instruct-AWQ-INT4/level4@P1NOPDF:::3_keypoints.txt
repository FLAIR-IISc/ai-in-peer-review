- Introduces a new simulated restaurant dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog evaluation.  
- Dataset generated from a KB by expanding parameter tuples and applying rule-based NL transformations.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate next-turn responses; evaluated via per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on this task.  
- Reviewer questions the utility of end-to-end methods in narrow domains where domain engineering may yield higher user satisfaction.  
- Concern that ranking responses (instead of generating them) weakens generalization and practical applicability to new domains.  
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in real-world new domains; ranking all responses from train/val/test seems unrealistic.  
- Simulator-based NL generation limits variability in training responses, potentially biasing models.  
- Reviewer requests publication of the NL-generation rules used to create the data.  
- Despite reservations, reviewer considers the contribution interesting and worthy of publication and raised their score after clarifications.
