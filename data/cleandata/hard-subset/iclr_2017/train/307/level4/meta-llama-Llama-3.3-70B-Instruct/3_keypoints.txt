- Paper introduces a novel simulated dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogs in a restaurant domain.
- Data is generated via a knowledge base and rule-based natural language transformations.
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses per dialog turn with per-response and per-dialog accuracy metrics.
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines.
- Reviewer questions the practicality of end-to-end training in narrow domains where domain knowledge can be hand-engineered.
- Concern that focusing on ranking (not generation) limits practical utility despite easier evaluation.
- Unclear how candidate responses are sourced in practice; appears models rank responses from train/val/test sets.
- Ranking pre-collected responses may not scale or generalize to new domains as well as generation would.
- Simulation-based NLG likely limits response variability in training data.
- The paper does not provide the list of NLG rules used; reviewer requests these be made public.
- Overall, reviewer finds the contribution interesting and adjusted their score upward after clarifications.
