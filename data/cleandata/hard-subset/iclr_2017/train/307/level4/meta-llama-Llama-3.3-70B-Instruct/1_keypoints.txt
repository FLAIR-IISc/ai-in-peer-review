- Introduces a novel, publicly available, rule-based dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue evaluation.  
- Dataset/tasks are artificially constructed via rule-based programs, enabling evaluation of API calls, option display, and full dialogues.  
- Contribution is valuable and could facilitate future dialogue-system research.  
- Unclear whether Deep Learning is suitable: neural gains may stem from regularization and DL needs large training sets.  
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic, so they don’t test handling of noisy/ambiguous interactions, goal inference, or dialogue repair.  
- Major weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, making neural models look overly strong and comparisons unfair.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams, and match-type features) to fairly assess representation learning.  
- Minor critique: claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures” is inaccurate — prior end-to-end task-oriented and human-evaluated non-task-oriented metrics exist (Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).
