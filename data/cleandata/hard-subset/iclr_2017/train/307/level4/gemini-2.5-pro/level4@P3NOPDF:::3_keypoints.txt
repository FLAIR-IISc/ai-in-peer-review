- New public dataset and benchmark for goal-oriented (restaurant booking) dialog systems.  
- Dataset created by simulating dialogs against a KB and rendering turns with rule-based NLG.  
- Task formulated as ranking candidate responses, evaluated by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Baselines include bag-of-words and Memory Networks; Memory Networks outperform simpler models.  
- Strength: clear experimental setup providing a solid starting point for future research.  
- Major concern: limited motivation for end-to-end systems in a narrow, well-defined domain where engineered systems may be preferable for control and reliability.  
- Major concern: framing as ranking (using all unique responses from splits as candidates) weakens scalability and the low-engineering argument compared to a generation approach.  
- Major concern: synthetic, rule-based NLG limits linguistic diversity and may not reflect real user language; publishing NLG rules is recommended.  
- Recommendation: despite conceptual issues, the paper is a valuable contribution and the reviewer recommends acceptance after clarifications and added results.
