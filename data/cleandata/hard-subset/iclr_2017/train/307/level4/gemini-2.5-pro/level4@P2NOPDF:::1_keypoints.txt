- Introduces a new publicly available, synthetically generated dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Synthetic, rule-based generation enables systematic evaluation from API calls to full end-to-end dialogues.  
- Valuable contribution likely to facilitate future dialogue research.  
- Synthetic nature limits realism and may not reflect challenges of real user noise, ambiguity, goal distributions, or dialogue repair.  
- Unclear how well deep learning models suit these tasks versus traditional or shallow methods; differences may stem from regularization rather than architecture.  
- Tasks 1–5 are entirely deterministic, so they don’t test robustness to real-world variability.  
- Major evaluation weakness: no baseline that incorporates word-order information, possibly overstating neural model performance.  
- Recommend adding a non-neural word-order-aware baseline (e.g., logistic regression with averaged embeddings, n-grams, and match-type features); reviewer would raise score if included.  
- Minor critique: the paper’s claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performances” is inaccurate; established evaluation methods exist (e.g., Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).
