- Introduces a new publicly available, synthetically generated dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Dataset/tasks are rule-based, enabling isolated evaluation of capabilities from API calls to full dialogues.  
- Contribution is valuable and likely to facilitate future dialogue research.  
- Unclear whether deep learning is inherently better than traditional methods for these tasks.  
- Neural performance differences may reflect regularization/data requirements rather than architectural advantages.  
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic and do not test handling of noise, ambiguity, goal distributions, or dialogue repair.  
- Evaluation lacks a non-neural baseline that incorporates word order, inflating reported neural performance.  
- Recommend benchmarking against a word-order-aware model (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-gram and match-type features).  
- Conclusion wrongly claims absence of well-defined performance measures; such measures exist for task-oriented and non-task-oriented dialogue.
