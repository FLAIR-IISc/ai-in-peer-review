- Introduces a new public, well-structured synthetic dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue systems.
- Dataset is rule-based/synthetic, allowing isolated evaluation of specific dialogue capabilities (API calls, option presentation, full dialogue).
- Positive impact: will facilitate future research and accelerate progress in dialogue systems.
- Major weakness: lacks a strong non-neural baseline that accounts for word order, making neural models appear artificially strong.
- Recommendation: include a logistic regression baseline with n-grams, pre-trained embeddings, and match-type features for fair comparison.
- Concern: synthetic/deterministic data may not suit deep learning and could favor differences in regularization over true language understanding.
- Limitation: deterministic tasks 1–5 cannot assess robustness to noise, ambiguity, user goal distributions, or dialogue repair strategies.
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performances” is an overstatement and should be revised with citations to prior work.
- Overall: valuable resource but experimental evaluation needed strengthening; reviewer raised score after new baseline experiments addressed the main concern.
