- Introduces a valuable public testbed of synthetically generated restaurant reservation tasks for goal-oriented dialog research.  
- Tasks systematically evaluate capabilities from issuing API calls and showing options to conducting full dialogs, broken into subtasks for fine-grained analysis.  
- Recommendation: conditional accept contingent on adding a stronger experimental baseline.  
- Primary concern: current baselines (TF-IDF, supervised bag-of-embeddings) are insensitive to word order, complicating evaluation of model gains.  
- Memory Network improvements may reflect sequence modeling rather than superior reasoning; needs comparison to sequence-aware baselines.  
- Suggest adding a competitive non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with n-gram features + supervised embeddings + match-type features).  
- Limitation: deterministic synthetic Tasks 1–5 are useful for control but do not test handling of real-world noise, ambiguity, user goal distributions, or dialog repair.  
- Inclusion of DSTC2 and Concierge is helpful but the evaluation still focuses mainly on synthetic tasks.  
- Critique of paper’s conclusion: the claim that existing performance measures prevent reproducibility or don’t correlate with human judgments is too strong; cite well-defined measures (e.g., success rate, task completion time) and human-judgment evaluations (e.g., Liu et al., 2016); recommend nuancing this claim.
