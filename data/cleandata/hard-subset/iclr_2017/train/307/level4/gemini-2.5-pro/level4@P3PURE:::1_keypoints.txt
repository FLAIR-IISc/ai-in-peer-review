- Introduces a new public dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue, built from rule-based programs to evaluate multiple dialogue behaviors.
- Useful and welcome contribution likely to facilitate future dialogue-system research.
- Unclear if deep learning is well-suited for these tasks; observed differences may reflect regularization rather than architectural advantages.
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic, so they do not test handling of real-world noise, ambiguity, user-goal distributions, or dialogue repair strategies.
- Major weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, which may make the presented neural models look deceptively strong.
- Recommendation: add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features) to fairly assess representation learning.
- Reviewer states that inclusion of such a baseline would raise their score to 8.
- Minor correction: the paper’s claim that existing work has no well-defined performance measures is incorrect; task-oriented dialogue has established evaluation metrics and there are methods for non-task dialogue evaluation.
- Reviewer updated their score after the authors’ new results.
