- Introduces a new open-source dataset and testbed for end-to-end, goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant reservation domain.
- Proposes five synthetic tasks that systematically test capabilities like API calls and handling OOV entities.
- Data is generated by a simulator using a knowledge base and rule-based natural language patterns.
- Frames the primary task as ranking candidate responses at each turn rather than generating responses.
- Evaluates several baselines; Memory Networks outperform IR and supervised embedding models.
- Per-dialog performance shows substantial remaining challenges and room for improvement.
- Includes grounding experiments on DSTC2 and a real-world concierge dataset to validate synthetic-task findings.
- Decision: Accept — benchmark fills a community need and the task decomposition aids analysis.
- Experimental analysis is comprehensive and provides strong baselines for future work.
- Conceptual concern: limited utility of purely end-to-end approaches in narrow domains where engineered systems may be more reliable.
- Major methodological concern: framing as ranking (not generation) weakens claims about end-to-end scalability and flexibility.
- Scalability issue: ranking requires a pre-defined exhaustive candidate response set, which is impractical for new domains.
- Generalization issue: fixed candidate sets prevent generative flexibility for new entities and conversational nuances.
- Suggestion: include the simulator’s rules and pattern lists in an appendix for transparency and reproducibility.
- Questions for authors: how would candidate responses be curated in deployment for new domains, and what are the advantages of end-to-end over engineered slot-filling/rule-based systems in narrow domains?
