- Novel dataset for end-to-end, goal-oriented restaurant dialogue (table finding/booking) presented.  
- Data synthetically generated via simulation using a knowledge base and rule-based natural language transformations.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses for each turn; evaluation via per-response and per-dialogue accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform basic bag-of-words baselines on the task.  
- Contribution is interesting and valuable to the field.  
- Reservations about the applicability of purely end-to-end systems in a constrained, well-understood domain where engineered solutions may be more practical.  
- Framing the task as response ranking rather than generation is disappointing despite simplifying evaluation.  
- Unclear how candidate response sets would be obtained in real-world deployment; using a global pool from train/val/test is problematic.  
- A generative approach would likely offer better generalization to new domains and is a major missing element.  
- Rule-based NLG in the simulation limits linguistic diversity; the specific generation rules were not provided.  
- Request to publish generation rules for transparency and reproducibility.  
- Overall suitable for publication but with noted reservations; reviewer revised score after authors’ clarifications and new results.
