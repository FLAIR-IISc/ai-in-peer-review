- Introduces a new public testbed for end-to-end, goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant domain with five tasks testing API calls, updates, and KB use.
- Decomposes dialog into distinct subtasks, enabling detailed error analysis and identification of specific model weaknesses.
- Validates the synthetic dataset on DSTC2 and a concierge service, showing consistent performance patterns with real-world data.
- Demonstrates Memory Networks outperform IR and supervised embedding baselines but highlights remaining challenges for complex dialogs.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Primary reason: Provides a well-structured, publicly available benchmark (including in-vocab and OOV tests) that will drive progress and analysis.
- Secondary reason/reservation: Ranking-based evaluation is a key limitation as it avoids the harder problem of response generation and scalability.
- Weakness 1: Framing the task as ranking from a fixed candidate pool limits realism and reintroduces domain-specific engineering.
- Weakness 2: Paper does not convincingly argue end-to-end advantages over high-performing, handcrafted slot-filling systems in narrow domains given low per-dialog accuracy.
- Suggested improvement: Release the 43 user and 20 bot generation patterns to increase transparency and reproducibility.
- Question 1: Why choose ranking-based evaluation over generation, and how would candidate response sets be sourced/maintained for new domains?
- Question 2: Please clarify the practical advantages of end-to-end systems versus handcrafted systems given the low task-completion rates observed.
