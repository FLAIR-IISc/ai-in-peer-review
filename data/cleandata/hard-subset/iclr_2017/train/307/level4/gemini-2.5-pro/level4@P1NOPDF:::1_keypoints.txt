- Introduces a new public, synthetically generated dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, enabling isolated evaluation of dialogue components.
- Synthetic, rule-based generation is useful but may limit realism and generalization to real-world dialogue.
- Unclear whether deep learning is the right paradigm here versus rule-based or shallow models, given data demands and regularization effects.
- Performance differences between neural architectures may reflect regularization rather than true architectural advantages.
- The first five tasks are deterministic, so they do not evaluate handling of noisy, ambiguous, or repair-prone real-world interactions.
- Major weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, which could make neural models look stronger than they are.
- Authors should add a non-neural baseline with word-order features (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, n-grams, and match-type features).
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performances” is inaccurate; there are established evaluation measures and human-evaluation approaches in prior work.
- Reviewer views the contribution as valuable and raised their score after the authors’ new results.
