- Introduces a new synthetic dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant booking domain.  
- Dataset created via a simulator + knowledge base, producing structured dialog states rendered to natural language with rule-based transformations.  
- Task formulation: rank a set of candidate responses at each dialog turn; evaluated using per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform simpler bag-of-words baselines on this task.  
- Contribution praised for providing a new large-scale resource and releasing it to the community.  
- Raises concern that end-to-end learning may be unnecessary in narrow domains where engineered systems can leverage domain knowledge for better user satisfaction.  
- Major limitation: framing as a ranking problem rather than generation; unclear how candidate response sets are obtained in practice.  
- Paper ranks over all responses from train/validation/test, which is not scalable and weakens the end-to-end motivation.  
- Reliance on a predefined candidate set limits generalization to new domains compared to generative models.  
- Data generation via fixed NLG rules constrains linguistic diversity of dialogs.  
- Lack of transparency about the specific generation rules; publishing them would aid reproducibility and community understanding.  
- Overall assessment: despite limitations, the dataset and baseline results are valuable and the paper merits publication; reviewer updated score after authors' clarifications.
