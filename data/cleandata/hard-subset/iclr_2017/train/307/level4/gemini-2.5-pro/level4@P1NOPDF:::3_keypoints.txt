- Introduces a new simulated dataset and benchmark for end-to-end, goal-oriented restaurant dialogue.  
- Task framed as ranking candidate responses at each dialogue turn, with data generated from KB-derived semantic tuples and rule-based NLG.  
- Evaluated using per-response and per-dialogue accuracy; Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines.  
- Reviewer finds the contribution interesting and timely but has reservations about practical utility.  
- Concern that end-to-end methods offer less clear advantage in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered systems can maximize user satisfaction.  
- Major limitation: framing the task as response ranking rather than generation simplifies evaluation but poses practical scalability issues.  
- Questioned how a comprehensive candidate response set would be sourced for new domains, undermining claims of reduced engineering.  
- Argues a generative approach would generalize better and be more aligned with building adaptable dialogue systems.  
- Data-generation via simulator and rule-based NLG likely limits linguistic diversity of the dataset.  
- Paper does not include the NLG rules; publishing them is recommended for reproducibility and transparency about dataset biases.  
- Overall positive: dataset/benchmark is valuable and worthy of publication; reviewer updated score after authors' clarifications.
