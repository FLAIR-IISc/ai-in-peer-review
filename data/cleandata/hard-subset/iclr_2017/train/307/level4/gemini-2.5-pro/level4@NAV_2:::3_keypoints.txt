- Paper introduces a new synthetic testbed and dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant reservation domain.  
- Tasks require gathering user parameters, querying a KB, and booking a table.  
- Dataset is generated by a simulator using rule-based natural language transformations, limiting linguistic variability.  
- Evaluation is framed as ranking candidate responses for each turn rather than response generation.  
- Baseline experiments show Memory Networks outperform classical IR and supervised embedding models.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, calling the resource well-structured and useful for reproducible research.  
- Main concern: unclear motivation for purely end-to-end approaches in narrow, well-understood transactional domains where engineered systems may be more practical.  
- Main concern: ranking-based evaluation and reliance on a predefined candidate set limit scalability and practical deployment; generative models might better demonstrate end-to-end advantages.  
- Suggested improvement: publish the simulator’s NLG rules for transparency and reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify how candidate response sets would be obtained in real-world deployments, since pooling from datasets is unrealistic.
