- Introduces a novel, publicly available synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue systems, generated by rule-based programs for structured evaluation.  
- Represents a valuable contribution likely to support future dialogue research.  
- Concern 1: Unclear whether deep learning is appropriate here versus traditional or shallow models; performance differences may reflect regularization, not architectural superiority.  
- Concern 2: Deterministic nature of tasks 1–5 prevents evaluating handling of noisy/ambiguous user inputs, inferring goal distributions, or dialogue repair strategies.  
- Major weakness: No baseline that accounts for word order was included, which may overstate the proposed neural models' strength.  
- Recommendation: Add a non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression using word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features) to fairly assess representation learning.  
- Reviewer note: Including such a baseline would raise the paper’s score (would increase rating to 8).  
- Minor comment: Claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performances” is inaccurate; end-to-end task-oriented models and evaluation methods exist (e.g., Wen et al.; human-evaluation studies by Liu et al. and Yu et al.).  
- Update: Reviewer revised their score after new results were added to the paper.
