- Presents a new synthetic restaurant-booking dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog.  
- Data generated by a simulator that creates KB-derived task parameter tuples and converts them to utterances via rule-based transformations.  
- Primary task: rank candidate responses per dialog turn; evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Memory Networks outperform simpler bag-of-words baselines on these benchmarks.  
- Reviewer questions the value of end-to-end approaches in narrow domains where engineered modular systems may be more practical and higher-performing.  
- Requests clearer justification of advantages of end-to-end methods over traditional modular pipelines in the restaurant-booking setting.  
- Criticizes framing the task as ranking (vs. generation) as simplifying evaluation but reducing real-world applicability and scalability.  
- Key practical concern: how to source the full candidate response set for new domains—this undermines portability claims.  
- Simulator’s rule-based NLG constrains linguistic diversity; reviewer recommends publishing the generation rules for assessment and reproducibility.  
- Despite concerns, reviewer finds the dataset and baselines valuable and raised their score after the authors’ clarifications and new experiments.
