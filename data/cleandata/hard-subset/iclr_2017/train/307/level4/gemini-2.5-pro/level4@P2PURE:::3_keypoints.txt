- Introduces a new simulated dataset for end-to-end, goal-oriented restaurant booking dialogs.
- Dataset generated by sampling a knowledge base and rendering attributes into utterances via rule-based NLG.
- Task framed as ranking a set of candidate responses per dialog turn, evaluated with per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines on this ranking task.
- Reviewer is skeptical about the utility of end-to-end systems for narrow, well-understood domains where engineered solutions may be preferable.
- Framing the task as ranking rather than generation is a major limitation, especially for practical application.
- It is unclear how the candidate response set would be sourced in new domains; reliance on training/validation/test responses undermines scalability.
- A generative approach would likely offer better generalization than the ranking setup.
- NLG rule-based expansion limits linguistic variability and the paper does not publish the generation rules, hurting reproducibility.
- Despite concerns, the reviewer finds the contribution interesting and worthy of publication and updated their score after clarifications.
