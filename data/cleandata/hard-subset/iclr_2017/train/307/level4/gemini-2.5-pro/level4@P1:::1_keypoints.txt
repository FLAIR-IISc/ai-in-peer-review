- Introduces a new public dataset and task suite (5 synthetic tasks + 2 real-world tasks: DSTC2, concierge) for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue evaluation.  
- Provides a reproducible, interpretable framework for analyzing dialogue model strengths and weaknesses and enabling targeted error analysis.  
- Decision: Weak Accept, with main reservation about insufficiently competitive baselines.  
- Baseline models (TF-IDF, Nearest Neighbor, Supervised Embeddings) use bag-of-words and ignore word order, potentially overstating Memory Network advantages.  
- Recommends adding a stronger non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression using summed embeddings, n-gram features, and match-type features) to fairly assess architecture benefits.  
- Synthetic tasks are deterministic, limiting assessment of models’ robustness to noise, ambiguity, and dialogue repair.  
- Training sets are relatively small, which may favor regularization effects over genuine architectural advantages for deep models.  
- Suggests tempering broad claims about existing evaluation measures and better contextualizing the contribution with prior work using success/efficiency metrics and human evaluation (e.g., Wen et al. 2016; Liu et al. 2016).
