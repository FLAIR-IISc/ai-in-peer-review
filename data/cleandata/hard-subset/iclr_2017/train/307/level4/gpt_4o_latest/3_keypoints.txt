- Paper introduces a simulated, restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, with tasks to rank candidate responses and metrics of per-response and per-dialog accuracy; Memory Networks beat bag-of-words baselines.

- Reviewer is skeptical about the practicality of end-to-end trained systems in narrow, well-understood domains where engineered domain knowledge might better maximize user satisfaction.

- The task evaluates RANKING of responses rather than GENERATING them, which the reviewer finds disappointing despite ranking simplifying evaluation.

- It is unclear how the candidate response set would be obtained in real-world or new-domain settings, since models appear to rank all responses from train/validation/test splits.

- The reviewer argues that generating responses (instead of ranking predefined ones) would support better generalization to new domains and views the ranking setup as the work’s main limitation.

- Data is simulation-generated by expanding KB tuples with rule-based NLG, which limits variability; the reviewer could not find the detailed NLG rules and requests their publication for reproducibility and transparency.

- Despite reservations, the reviewer finds the contribution intriguing and recommends acceptance, having updated their score after clarifications and extra results.
