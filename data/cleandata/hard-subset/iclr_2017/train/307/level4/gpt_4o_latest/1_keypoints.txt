- Introduces a novel, publicly available synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue systems.
- Dataset/tasks are generated by rule-based programs to evaluate API calling, option presentation, and full-dialogue handling.
- Contribution is valuable and can advance dialogue systems research.
- Unclear how Deep Learning models compare to traditional or shallow methods on these tasks.
- Deep models may require large data and performance differences could reflect regularization choices.
- Tasks 1–5 are fully deterministic, limiting evaluation of noisy/ambiguous interactions and dialogue-repair capabilities.
- Paper lacks a non-neural baseline that incorporates word-order, a significant shortcoming that could overestimate neural performance.
- Suggested baseline: logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram features, and match-type features.
- Adding the suggested baseline would likely increase the reviewer’s rating to 8.
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is inaccurate — task-oriented end-to-end models do have defined metrics (e.g., Wen et al.), and non-goal systems can be evaluated via human studies.
