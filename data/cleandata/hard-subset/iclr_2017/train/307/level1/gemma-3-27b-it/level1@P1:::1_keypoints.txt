- Introduces a testbed for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog (restaurant reservation) with five tasks and two datasets (DSTC2, Concierge).  
- Evaluates a Memory Network against a rule-based baseline and analyzes its strengths and weaknesses.  
- Decision: Reject — contribution not deemed groundbreaking for ICLR; confirms known limitations of end-to-end models without major advances.  
- Strength: Systematic task decomposition helps identify specific weaknesses in goal-oriented dialogs.  
- Strength: Uses both synthetic and real-world datasets, strengthening validity.  
- Strength: Comparison with a rule-based baseline provides a useful benchmark.  
- Strength: Detailed analysis and ablation (match-type features) and public release of data promote reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Memory Network performance is unimpressive; per-dialog accuracy remains low, showing limits in complex flows.  
- Weakness: Paper doesn’t justify choice of Memory Network or explore alternative architectures (Transformers, LSTMs, etc.).  
- Weakness: Empirical focus lacks theoretical explanation for why end-to-end models fail in these tasks; match-type features feel ad hoc.  
- Requested: Provide model choice justification and consideration of other architectures.  
- Requested: More detailed error analysis with concrete failure examples (API call/result interpretation).  
- Requested: Hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (learning rates, embedding dims, number of hops).  
- Requested: Clarify KB representation (list of facts vs. structured/graph) and its effect on performance.  
- Requested: Discuss generalizability to other goal domains (flights, hotels) and required modifications.  
- Requested: Break down per-dialog accuracy failures by conversation step (API formulation vs. result interpretation).  
- Requested: Provide more Concierge dataset details (training size, avg. length/complexity, anonymization and potential biases).
