- Proposes a systematic testbed for scaling end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems, focusing on restaurant reservation tasks.  
- Decomposes the task into five subtasks to evaluate dialog management, KB querying, and handling unseen entities.  
- Evaluates Memory Networks (MemNNs) against traditional slot-filling baselines and other methods.  
- Finds MemNNs outperform baselines on several subtasks but struggle with interpreting API call results and full-dialog complexity.  
- Validates findings on real-world datasets (DSTC2 and an online concierge service) to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Decision: Accept.
