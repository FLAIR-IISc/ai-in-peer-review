- Paper proposes a reproducible restaurant-reservation testbed decomposed into five tasks plus two datasets to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.  
- Evaluates IR, supervised embedding, and end-to-end Memory Network baselines (with match-type entity features), measuring per-response and per-dialog accuracy including OOV splits.  
- Claims: an open benchmark isolating dialog management, KB use, and OOV handling; MemNNs can learn API/entity operations but fail at full goal completion; synthetic tasks mirror realistic data patterns.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a reusable benchmark addressing OOV and KB reasoning and rigorous scientific analysis.  
- Problem framing and related work are clear and position the work well between chit-chat and transactional dialog needs.  
- Methodology is transparent: controlled synthetic data, explicit task design, disjoint-KB OOV split, and ranking-based evaluation to avoid generation confounds.  
- Results show MemNNs outperform IR and supervised embeddings; match-type features substantially improve OOV and KB fact retrieval (Task 4).  
- Per-dialog success remains low for complex tasks (T3/T5), indicating limitations despite progress on sub-tasks.  
- Consistent relative performance across synthetic tasks, DSTC2, and Concierge supports external validity.  
- Releasing tasks and data promotes reproducibility and community impact.  
- Clarify candidate-response pool choice and quantify potential realism inflation or leakage; consider per-task candidate pools.  
- Suggest adding copy/pointer or program-induction baselines to better handle sorting/display (T3) and OOV entities.  
- Recommend deeper error analysis for T3/T5 to separate sorting, state-tracking, and stopping-criterion failures.  
- Discuss generation-based evaluations and human judgments as complementary to ranking metrics.  
- Report statistical significance and confidence intervals for results.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to deterministic bot prompts in T1/T2 and to non-deterministic prompting orders?  
- Question: In DSTC2 conversion, do ASR noise and inconsistent bot policies create noisy labels that cap performance?  
- Question: Does the model truly learn sorting in T3 or exploit artifacts (e.g., rating distributions); can adversarial KB shuffles test this?  
- Question: What is the impact of restricting the candidate set to in-domain responses per task at test time?  
- Question: Can match-type features work without full KB access at test time (partial KB or schema-only), and how does that affect OOV handling?
