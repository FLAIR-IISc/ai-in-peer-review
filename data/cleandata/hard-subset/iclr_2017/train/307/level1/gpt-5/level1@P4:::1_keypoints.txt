- Introduces a controlled, reproducible restaurant reservation testbed decomposed into five synthetic subtasks, with OOV splits and real-data evaluations (DSTC2, Concierge).  
- Evaluates TF-IDF, nearest neighbor, supervised embeddings, and MemN2N (with/without match-type features) using per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Finds per-response accuracy can be high while per-dialog accuracy remains low, especially for KB reasoning (sorting) and full dialogues.  
- Identifies strengths: issuing/updating API calls and handling OOVs with type-match features.  
- Identifies weaknesses: interpreting KB outputs, multi-turn robustness, and tasks requiring reasoning over KB results.  
- Observes similar performance patterns across synthetic tasks and real datasets, supporting the synthetic suite as a useful proxy.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons: novel, well-constructed evaluation resource emphasizing reproducibility, diagnostics, and OOV challenges; sound empirical study with clear insights.  
- Problem addressed: assesses whether end-to-end neural dialog models can handle state tracking, API interaction, KB interpretation, and entity handling.  
- Motivation/placement: fills a gap beyond chit-chat and isolated state-tracking corpora; builds on MemN2N and dialog/QA literature.  
- Claims supported: clear task definitions, aligned metrics, validated hyperparameters, and ablations showing MemN2N > embeddings > IR and benefit of type features.  
- Acknowledged limitation: synthetic templated language and deterministic bot behavior reduce linguistic variability (partly mitigated by DSTC2/Concierge).  
- Evaluation caveat: retrieval-style candidate ranking differs from generative evaluation and may simplify the task.  
- Baselines caveat: missing contemporary seq2seq/attention/copy/pointer and key-value memory baselines relevant for entity-heavy tasks.  
- Suggested improvement: add generative baselines (seq2seq w/ attention, pointer/copy, key-value memory) and compare to MemN2N.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify candidate response set construction and consider generation-based or constrained-decoding evaluations.  
- Suggested improvement: increase user-side linguistic variability and non-deterministic bot policies to better test language understanding and policy learning.  
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper error analysis on Task 3 and Task 5, ablate type features per entity type, and report confidence intervals/variance across runs.  
- Suggested improvement: detail DSTC2 conversion procedure (ratings injection) and check mapping consistency and sensitivity to heuristics.  
- Suggested improvement: explore sample efficiency, multi-domain variants, and release code/data/scripts/models for reproducibility.  
- Question: confirm whether models see test-specific candidate utterances during training and how that affects generalization vs generation.  
- Question: how are DSTC2 restaurant ratings assigned and how sensitive are results to that heuristic?  
- Question: for OOV entities, are embeddings unknown and only type features used, or are subword/shared features employed; how many test responses use unseen entity strings?  
- Question: what specific failure modes drive Task 3 per-dialog failures (sorting, stopping, error propagation), and do more hops or attention supervision help?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to more templates, paraphrases, typos, or relaxed/different bot policies?  
- Question: were pointer/copy mechanisms or key-value memory networks attempted, and how did they compare to match-type features?  
- Question: provide per-entity-type generalization breakdown (location vs cuisine vs restaurant name) when testing OOV partitions.  
- Question: do per-dialog and per-response accuracies correlate with human judgments on DSTC2/Concierge; any human evaluation performed?
