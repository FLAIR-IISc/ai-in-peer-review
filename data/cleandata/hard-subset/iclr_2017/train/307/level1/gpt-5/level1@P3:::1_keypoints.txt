- Introduces an open, reproducible testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in restaurant reservations, decomposed into five synthetic sub-tasks plus DSTC2 and a proprietary Concierge dataset.

- Testbed targets capabilities: implicit state tracking, KB querying/grounding, sorting/selection, and handling OOV entities.

- Core contribution: a carefully designed, interpretable, reproducible benchmark and transparent empirical analysis for transactional dialogs.

- Provides baselines (TF-IDF, nearest neighbor IR, supervised embeddings) and a Memory Network variant with match-type features for better entity/OOV handling.

- Results: MemNNs outperform other baselines, especially with type features, but underperform on multi-step KB reasoning tasks (e.g., displaying sorted options) in per-dialog accuracy.

- Decision: Accept.

- Key reasons for accept: dataset/testbed is novel and fills a gap between chit-chat and goal-directed evaluation; empirical study is careful, insightful, and reproducible.

- Problem significance: The decomposition into subtasks offers diagnostic leverage for whether end-to-end models can replace slot-filling/POMDP systems; OOV splits are valuable.

- Literature placement: Well-situated relative to chit-chat corpora, DSTC, and POMDP approaches; testbed is a dialog-focused extension of bAbI-style diagnostics.

- Methodology: Simulation, KB schema, metrics (per-response vs per-dialog), and principled OOV splits are clearly specified; conversion of DSTC2 is justified.

- Evidence: Reported results are consistent and patterns transfer to DSTC2/Concierge, supporting the paper's claims.

- Caveat — synthetic bias/determinism: Templated language and deterministic policies may overestimate model progress compared to real dialogs.

- Caveat — ranking vs generation: Framing as candidate ranking over pooled candidates simplifies the problem and may mask generation/realization errors.

- Caveat — baselines: Missing comparisons to seq2seq with copy/pointer, delexicalized models, and key-value or entity-centric memories.

- Caveat — KB access for OOV types: Allowing full KB access during training blurs the meaning of OOV generalization (tests exact-match/type handling more than semantic generalization).

- Caveat — DSTC2 adaptation: Adding ratings to enforce deterministic ranking introduces a mismatch from original DSTC2 dynamics.

- Suggested improvements: add stronger neural baselines (seq2seq+copy, delexicalized, key-value memories), report/ablate candidate set sizes, and provide fine-grained error analysis and attention visualizations.

- Suggested robustness work: introduce noise/paraphrases, non-deterministic bot policies, cross-domain/generalization tests, and clarify release/licensing (or anonymized Concierge subset).

- Key questions for authors: exact candidate set sizes and pooling strategy; impact of disallowing KB access for OOVs; whether models truly learn sorting vs memorization; attempts with seq2seq/copy or key-value memories; support for RL fine-tuning; DSTC2 conversion biases; and breakdown of frequent T5 failure modes.
