This paper proposes a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications, specifically in the context of restaurant reservation. The authors aim to break down the strengths and shortcomings of these systems by designing a set of five tasks that test various abilities, such as dialog management, querying knowledge bases, and interpreting output. The paper also compares the performance of an end-to-end dialog system based on Memory Networks to a hand-crafted slot-filling baseline on data from the second Dialog State Tracking Challenge and an online concierge service.

I decide to accept this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the paper addresses a significant problem in the field of natural language processing, namely the lack of a systematic and controlled way to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog learning methods. The proposed testbed provides a valuable resource for the community to focus research and development on improving these methods. Secondly, the paper presents a thorough evaluation of various models, including Memory Networks, on the proposed tasks, providing insights into their strengths and weaknesses.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The testbed is designed to be lightweight and easy to use, making it accessible to a wide range of researchers. The evaluation of the models is comprehensive, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.

However, there are some areas that could be improved. For example, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed testbed and the potential biases in the evaluation metrics. Additionally, the authors could provide more insights into the performance of the Memory Networks model, particularly in terms of its ability to generalize to new entities and tasks.

To improve the paper, I would suggest the following:

* Provide more details on the design of the testbed, including the process of generating the tasks and the evaluation metrics.
* Discuss the limitations of the proposed testbed and the potential biases in the evaluation metrics.
* Provide more insights into the performance of the Memory Networks model, particularly in terms of its ability to generalize to new entities and tasks.
* Consider adding more baseline models to the evaluation, such as other end-to-end dialog systems or traditional dialog systems.
* Provide more details on the online concierge service dataset, including the process of collecting and annotating the data.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details on the design of the testbed, including the process of generating the tasks and the evaluation metrics?

* Can you provide more details on the online concierge service dataset, including the process of collecting and annotating the data?
