**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications. The testbed is designed to break down the strengths and shortcomings of end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented settings, specifically in the context of restaurant reservation. The authors argue that traditional dialog systems require a lot of domain-specific handcrafting, which hinders scaling up to new domains, and that end-to-end dialog systems can escape this limitation. However, the success of end-to-end dialog systems in chit-chat settings may not necessarily carry over to goal-oriented settings. The paper aims to provide an open resource to test end-to-end dialog systems in a way that favors reproducibility and comparisons.

I decide to **accept** this paper with some reservations. The key reasons for this choice are:

2. The paper provides a thorough evaluation of several learning methods on the proposed testbed, including rule-based systems, classical information retrieval models, supervised embeddings, and end-to-end Memory Networks.
