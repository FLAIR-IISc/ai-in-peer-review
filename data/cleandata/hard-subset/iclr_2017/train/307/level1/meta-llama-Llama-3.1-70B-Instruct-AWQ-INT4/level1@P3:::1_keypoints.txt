- Proposes a restaurant-reservation testbed of five tasks to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.
- Evaluates rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, and Memory Networks; Memory Networks outperform IR and embeddings but struggle with entity interpretation.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reason: Evaluation is limited to a single model (Memory Networks) without comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.
- Primary reason: Memory Networks show inconsistent performance and near-zero per-dialog accuracy on tasks T3 and T4.
- Supporting: Single-model evaluation may not represent broader end-to-end dialog approaches; seq2seq and graph-based models are missing.
- Supporting: Performance variance across tasks raises concerns about model and testbed effectiveness.
- Supporting: Discussion and analysis of results are limited; strengths and weaknesses of the testbed are not thoroughly examined.
- Feedback: Include broader model comparisons in the evaluation.
- Feedback: Provide a more detailed analysis of results and testbed limitations.
- Feedback: Improve conciseness of writing in some sections (e.g., introduction).
- Feedback: Add more examples of tasks and model outputs to illustrate the testbed.
