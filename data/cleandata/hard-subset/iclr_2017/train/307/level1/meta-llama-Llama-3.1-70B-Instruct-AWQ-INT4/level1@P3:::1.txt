**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a testbed to evaluate end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications. The authors design a set of five tasks within the context of restaurant reservation, which require manipulating sentences and symbols to conduct conversations, issue API calls, and use the outputs of such calls. They evaluate several learning methods on these tasks, including rule-based systems, classical information retrieval models, supervised embeddings, and end-to-end Memory Networks. The results show that Memory Networks outperform classical IR and supervised embeddings across all tasks, but still lack in some key areas, such as interpreting knowledge about entities to present results to the user.

**Decision**

Reject

**Reasons for Rejection**

1. The paper's primary contribution is the proposal of a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems, but the evaluation of the proposed tasks is limited to a single model, Memory Networks. The authors do not provide a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art models, which makes it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed testbed.
2. The paper's results are not entirely convincing, as the performance of Memory Networks is not consistently better than the other models across all tasks. For example, the per-dialog accuracy of Memory Networks is still close to 0 on tasks T3 and T4, which raises concerns about the model's ability to handle goal-oriented dialog.

**Supporting Arguments**

* The paper's evaluation is limited to a single model, Memory Networks, which may not be representative of the broader class of end-to-end dialog systems. A more comprehensive evaluation should include other state-of-the-art models, such as sequence-to-sequence models or graph-based models.
* The paper's results are not entirely consistent, as the performance of Memory Networks varies significantly across tasks. For example, the per-dialog accuracy of Memory Networks is 0 on tasks T3 and T4, which raises concerns about the model's ability to handle goal-oriented dialog.
* The paper's discussion of the results is limited, and the authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed testbed. A more detailed discussion of the results would help to clarify the effectiveness of the proposed testbed.

**Additional Feedback**

* The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed tasks, including a comparison with other state-of-the-art models.
* The authors should provide a more detailed analysis of the results, including a discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed testbed.
* The paper's writing is clear, but some sections, such as the introduction, could be more concise.
* The authors should consider adding more examples of the tasks and the output of the models to help illustrate the proposed testbed.
