- Proposes a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented settings (restaurant reservation).
- Motivates need: handcrafted dialog systems don’t scale; end-to-end approaches may help but chit-chat success may not transfer.
- Testbed aims to systematically break down strengths and shortcomings and favor reproducibility and comparisons.
- Decision: accept with reservations.
- Strength: well-motivated, well-placed testbed for controlled evaluation of goal-oriented dialogs.
- Strength: thorough evaluation of multiple methods (rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, Memory Networks).
- Presentation: clear, well-structured description and thorough comparative results.
- Limitation: testbed is restricted to the restaurant reservation domain; generalizability is unclear.
- Limitation: lacks clear explanation of how the testbed can be used to improve end-to-end dialog performance.
- Suggested improvement: provide details on using the testbed to identify method strengths/weaknesses and drive improvements.
- Suggested improvement: evaluate on additional domains to demonstrate generalizability and collect multi-domain data.
- Question: how will the testbed be extended to other domains and what challenges are anticipated?
- Question: provide more details on how the testbed can be used to improve end-to-end goal-oriented systems.
- Question: how will the restaurant-domain limitation be addressed?
- Question: provide details on the evaluation metrics used and the rationale for their choice.
- Question: how will the testbed and evaluation results be made accessible to the research community?
