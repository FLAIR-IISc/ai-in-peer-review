- Paper provides a reality check showing a large gap between current end-to-end models and requirements for robust goal-oriented dialog systems.  
- Progress in chit-chat does not automatically translate to task completion; authors provide a testbed to study this discrepancy.  
- Creation of synthetic tasks and evaluation on DSTC2 plus an online concierge service is a significant contribution.  
- Reviewer recommends accept.  
- Core arguments are supported by thorough experiments and insightful analysis.  
- Highlights brittleness of common dialog metrics and that per-dialog accuracy is highly sensitive to task definition.  
- Introduces a simple but effective "match type" addition to memory networks that helps handle out-of-vocabulary KB entities.  
- Strengths: rigorous experimental protocol, clear presentation, helpful appendix, strong synthetic-task analysis, and confirmation on real-world data.  
- Authors correctly identify vocabulary mismatch as a major challenge; match type is a sensible first step.  
- Weakness: vocabulary mismatch could be explored more deeply, especially learning semantic associations beyond exact matches.  
- Weakness: brief discussion of application-driven loss functions lacks detail on practical challenges (e.g., need for human feedback or realistic simulators).  
- Question: Did you try different concierge evaluation cutoffs (top-3, top-1) and how does stricter thresholds affect model weaknesses?  
- Question: How does match type handle synonyms or paraphrases of KB entities not explicitly in the KB?  
- Question: Can you provide a concrete example of an application-driven loss tied to a revenue metric and how it could be trained with simulation or limited human feedback?
