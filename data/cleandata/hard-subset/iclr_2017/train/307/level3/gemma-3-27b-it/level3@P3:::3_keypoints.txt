- Introduces a new simulated dataset and task set for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant domain, focusing on KB interaction and dialog management.  
- Core contribution is a simulated environment plus a ranking-based evaluation protocol to isolate and system capabilities.  
- Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines on the proposed tasks and results are validated on DSTC2 and a proprietary concierge dataset.  
- Controlled, reproducible simulation offers advantages over noisy real-world data for studying end-to-end dialog systems.  
- Tasks force key capabilities such as API querying and entity manipulation, and comparison to a rule-based baseline strengthens evaluation.  
- Choice of ranking (vs. generation) is a major limitation—restricts generalization and the ability to produce novel utterances; scalability of candidate lists is questionable.  
- Rule-based NL generation (43 user patterns, 20 bot patterns) limits linguistic variability and may harm generalization; publishing these rules is important.  
- Per-dialog accuracy remains low, and the paper lacks detailed error analysis identifying which subtasks fail most often.  
- Open questions include automatic candidate-response generation, access to NL generation rules, detailed error breakdowns, scaling with KB size/complexity, and hybrid ranking-plus-generation approaches.
