- Introduces a valuable publicly available rule-based synthetic dataset and task suite for goal-oriented dialogue evaluation.  
- Motivation is well articulated: scaling traditional systems is hard and chit-chat success may not transfer to goal-oriented settings.  
- Breaking goal-oriented dialogue into smaller tasks is a clever approach for focused analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- The dataset is a significant resource likely to benefit the research community.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks a strong non-neural baseline, weakening comparisons.  
- Suggest including a simple baseline (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings and bigram features) to better contextualize Memory Network performance.  
- Memory Network may appear unusually strong given deterministic tasks; deep models could be overkill and differences may stem from regularization.  
- Tasks are limited in scope and do not evaluate robustness to noisy or ambiguous interactions, user goal inference, or dialogue repair.  
- The claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is inaccurate and should acknowledge prior evaluation work (e.g., Wen, Liu, Yu).  
- Hyperparameter tables are useful but require more explanation about the tuning process.  
- Suggested revisions: add a non-neural baseline, qualify claims about evaluation metrics, discuss limitations on noisy interactions, and expand hyperparameter explanation.  
- Questions for authors: did you test simpler models beyond logistic regression, how was the synthetic user request distribution chosen, and how can the testbed be extended for goal uncertainty or dialogue repair?
