- Presents a new simulated restaurant-domain dataset with a KB and rule-based NLG for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.  
- Proposes a next-turn response ranking task to assess system performance.  
- Shows Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines on the proposed task.  
- Validates findings on DSTC2 and a proprietary concierge dataset, increasing confidence.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions, citing the value of a controlled, reproducible evaluation environment.  
- Argues the dataset fills a clear need for focused benchmarks in goal-oriented dialog research.  
- Notes the work is not a breakthrough in dialog generation but is a valuable methodological contribution.  
- Raises concern that ranking limits generalization and may hinder progress in true generative modeling.  
- Questions practicality of obtaining candidate response lists in novel domains without manual effort.  
- Criticizes rule-based NLG for restricting training data diversity and requests the full list of NLG rules.  
- Requests clearer discussion of ranking-task limitations, practicality of candidate lists, and alternative evaluations.  
- Asks for more detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analyses (candidate list size, NLG variability, choice of Memory Networks).
