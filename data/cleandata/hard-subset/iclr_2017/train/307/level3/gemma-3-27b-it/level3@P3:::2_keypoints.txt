- Demonstrates a significant gap between end-to-end learning approaches and the needs of robust, goal-oriented dialog systems.
- Provides a valuable, rigorous evaluation framework as a reality check against end-to-end model hype.
- Correctly notes that success in chit‑chat does not translate to goal‑oriented dialog tasks.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Core contribution: systematic evaluation of Memory Networks with a simple “match type” augmentation.
- Experimental setup is thorough and well-designed, using a controlled restaurant reservation scenario.
- Experimental protocol is excellent with clear explanations and useful supporting materials (e.g., hop-number analysis in Appendix).
- Inclusion of both synthetic and real-world datasets (DSTC2, concierge) strengthens the conclusions.
- Insightful critique of evaluation metrics, highlighting differences between per-response and per-dialog accuracy and sensitivity to task definitions.
- Identifies vocabulary mismatch as a critical limitation of the approach.
- Notes that the “match type” fix is superficial because it relies on exact KB occurrences rather than learned semantic associations.
- Points out limited ability to handle paraphrases and user language variation under the current approach.
- Suggests exploring external knowledge bases or more sophisticated embedding methods to address vocabulary mismatch.
- Observes the paper notes limits of current loss functions but lacks detailed discussion of practical challenges for application-driven losses.
- Asks why exact matching was chosen for “match type” and whether fuzzy/semantic matching methods were considered.
- Asks whether KB entities were explored as embeddings with attention mechanisms for better KB integration.
- Requests discussion of practical hurdles in quantifying costs for user rejections or incorrect API calls when designing loss functions.
- Questions whether the concierge dataset’s “correct” label should be single-best or within the 5-best and asks for the distribution of correct answers among the 5-best.
- Raises concern that Task 5 (combined task) may introduce unrealistic complexity and asks if its performance drop is disproportionate.
- Requests examples of OOV entities correctly handled after adding the “match type” feature.
