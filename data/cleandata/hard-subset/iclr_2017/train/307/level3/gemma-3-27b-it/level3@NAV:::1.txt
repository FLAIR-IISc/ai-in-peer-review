## Review of "A Testbed for Goal-Oriented Dialog"

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of goal-oriented dialogue systems by introducing a new, publicly available, rule-based synthetic dataset and a set of tasks designed to evaluate key capabilities like API calls and option display. The motivation for creating this testbed – the difficulty of scaling traditional systems and the uncertain transfer of success from chit-chat to goal-oriented settings – is well articulated and timely. The breakdown of goal-oriented dialogue into smaller, manageable tasks is a clever approach for focused analysis.

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. The dataset itself is a significant resource that will likely benefit the research community. However, the empirical evaluation lacks a crucial baseline, and some claims require qualification.

My primary concern is the lack of a strong non-neural baseline. While the authors compare to a hand-crafted slot-filling system and classical information retrieval methods, a simpler model like logistic regression with word embeddings and bigram features (as suggested by the reviewer update) would provide a more direct comparison and a clearer understanding of the value added by the Memory Network. The current results make the Memory Network appear unusually strong, and it’s important to demonstrate it genuinely outperforms simpler approaches. The authors correctly point out that these tasks are deterministic; therefore, complex deep learning models might be overkill, and performance differences could be primarily driven by regularization effects. Furthermore, the tasks, while well-defined, are limited in their scope. They do not address the complexities of noisy or ambiguous interactions—a core challenge in real-world dialogue. The absence of evaluation on handling user goal inference or dialogue repair is a notable limitation.

Additionally, the claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures of performance” is inaccurate. Several prior works on end-to-end and non-goal dialogue evaluation (Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.) have established metrics and methodologies. This should be acknowledged.

**To improve the paper, I suggest:**

*   **Include a non-neural baseline:** Logistic regression with appropriate features will provide a more compelling comparison.
*   **Qualify the claim about evaluation metrics:** Acknowledge existing work on dialogue evaluation.
*   **Discuss limitations regarding noisy interactions:** Explicitly address the lack of evaluation of robustness to ambiguity and dialogue repair.
*   **Provide more insight into hyperparameter tuning:**  The hyperparameters tables are helpful, but additional explanation of the tuning process would be beneficial.

**Questions for the authors:**

*   Given the deterministic nature of the tasks, did you investigate simpler models beyond logistic regression? What were the results?
*   What considerations were given to the distribution of user requests within the synthetic data? Was any effort made to reflect real-world distributions?
*   How do you envision this testbed being extended to include more complex scenarios, such as incorporating user goal uncertainty or allowing for dialogue repair?
