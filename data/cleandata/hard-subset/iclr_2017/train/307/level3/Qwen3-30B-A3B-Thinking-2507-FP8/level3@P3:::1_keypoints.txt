- Introduces a novel, public synthetic dataset and five rule-based restaurant reservation tasks for evaluating goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Dataset is deterministic and reproducible with an underlying knowledge base; an end-to-end Memory Network shows promising results and outperforms a slot-filling baseline on DSTC2 and concierge data.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions.  
- Strength: provides a controlled, reproducible testbed to isolate specific dialogue capabilities.  
- Major weakness: no non-neural baseline that preserves word order (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bi-grams), biasing comparisons toward neural methods.  
- Major weakness: inaccurate claim that prior work lacks well-defined evaluation metrics (Wen, Liu, Yu contradict this).  
- Major weakness: tasks are fully deterministic, so they do not test noise, ambiguity, partial information, or dialogue repair found in real-world systems.  
- Suggested fix: add a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram and match-type features) for fair comparison.  
- Suggested fix: clarify and acknowledge prior dialogue evaluation work and explain how this evaluation differs.  
- Suggested fix: discuss and/or extend tasks to include realistic noise and ambiguity and analyze the impact on evaluation.  
- Suggested fix: provide deeper error analysis of Memory Network failures, especially on Tasks 3 and 5.  
- Clarification requests: why traditional models wouldn’t be competitive given small deterministic datasets; how tasks would be modified to add realistic noise and evaluation challenges; why OOV test sets were excluded for the rule-based baseline and whether it can be extended to handle OOV; justification for claiming no well-defined performance measures despite cited prior work.
