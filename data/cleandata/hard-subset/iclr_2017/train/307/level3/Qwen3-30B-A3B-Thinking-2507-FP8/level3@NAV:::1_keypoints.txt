- Introduces a valuable synthetic dataset and tasks for evaluating goal-oriented restaurant dialogue systems.  
- Proposes five tasks covering API calls, option display, and full dialogues, with real-world dataset validation.  
- Contribution addresses need for standardized, reproducible evaluation of end-to-end dialogue systems.  
- Decision: Accept (with minor revisions).  
- Paper provides a well-structured testbed that isolates specific dialogue capabilities.  
- Memory Network model shows promise but has clear limitations.  
- Comparison with DSTC2 and Concierge strengthens real-world relevance.  
- Tasks are motivated by limitations of traditional slot-filling approaches.  
- Results: neural models handle API-call-related tasks but struggle with interpreting and displaying API results.  
- Suggest adding a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bigram and match-type features).  
- Current baselines (TF-IDF Match, Nearest Neighbor) are inadequate and may overstate neural performance.  
- Claim that “existing work has no well-defined measures” is inaccurate; cite Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.  
- Questions for authors: provide non-neural baseline results and assess impact of match-type features on comparisons.
