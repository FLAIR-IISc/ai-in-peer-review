- Introduces a synthetic testbed of five restaurant-domain tasks to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.  
- Provides a standardized, reproducible evaluation framework that fills an important gap.  
- Shows Memory Networks achieve promising performance and generalize to DSTC2 and an online concierge dataset.  
- Recommendation: Accept — testbed is a valuable contribution for future research and comparisons.  
- Strength: Well-motivated, systematically breaks down required dialog capabilities with rigorous experiments including OOV settings and real-data validation.  
- Major weakness: No non-neural baseline that incorporates word order and match-type features, making neural models appear unnaturally strong.  
- Incorrect claim: Statement that “existing work has no well-defined measures” is inaccurate—prior work (Wen, Liu, Yu et al.) has established evaluation metrics and should be acknowledged.  
- Suggested fix: Add a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with embeddings, bigrams, match-type features) because TF-IDF and nearest-neighbor baselines are too simplistic.  
- Limitation: Synthetic, deterministic tasks do not capture real-world noise, ambiguity, or dialogue repair and this should be discussed.  
- Questions for authors: Why omit a word-order non-neural baseline? Should the claim about prior evaluation work be revised? How will performance degrade on noisy real-world dialogs and what techniques would help?
