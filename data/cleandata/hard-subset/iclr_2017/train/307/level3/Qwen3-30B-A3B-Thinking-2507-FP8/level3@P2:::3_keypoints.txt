- Introduces a well-designed testbed with five restaurant-reservation tasks for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.
- Tasks are grounded in a restaurant KB and test dialog management, KB querying, and interpretation of API outputs.
- Creates a simulated dataset using rule-based natural language generation.
- Demonstrates Memory Networks can learn non-trivial operations on these tasks, with promising but imperfect performance.
- Validates synthetic-task findings on DSTC2 and real concierge data, showing the tasks are a good proxy for real-world performance.
- Decision: Accept — significant, reproducible contribution addressing an important evaluation gap.
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform IR and supervised embedding baselines and generalize patterns to real data.
- Error analysis: models handle implicit dialog state tracking and OOV entities but struggle to interpret KB results (Task 3).
- Concern that ranking-based candidate selection limits generalization and scalability to new domains without hand-crafted candidates.
- Suggest publishing the full rule-based NL generation rules to improve reproducibility and clarify synthetic-data limitations.
- Recommend more discussion of practical implications and why end-to-end approaches might be preferred over slot-filling in practice.
- Ask for clarification on how this testbed differs from bAbI (dialog-based vs question-answering) and how it could be extended to other domains.
