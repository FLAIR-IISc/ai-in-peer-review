- Paper studies transferability of adversarial examples (non-targeted and targeted) on ImageNet across multiple state-of-the-art models, comparing optimization-based and fast gradient methods.  
- Key findings: strong transferability for non-targeted attacks, difficulty producing transferable targeted labels with existing methods, and ensemble-based approaches improve transferable targeted attacks.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to insufficient comparison with prior work, particularly on targeted adversarial examples and incorrect claim about lack of existing performance measures.  
- Methodological concerns: very small test set (100 images) and insufficient diversity in chosen target labels.  
- Suggested improvements: broaden comparisons with existing targeted-attack work and discuss relative strengths/weaknesses.  
- Suggested improvements: increase test set size and label diversity, evaluate transferability across additional datasets and models, and analyze geometric properties of models related to transferability.  
- Clarification questions: provide more details on the ensemble-based methods and how they differ from prior approaches.  
- Clarification questions: explain how target labels were chosen to ensure diversity/representativeness.  
- Clarification questions: report whether the proposed approaches were evaluated against defenses like adversarial training or input preprocessing.
