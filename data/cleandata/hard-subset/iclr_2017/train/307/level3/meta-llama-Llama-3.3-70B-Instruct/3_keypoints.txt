- Introduces a new dataset for evaluating end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems in a restaurant domain.
- Task focuses on ranking candidate responses per turn rather than generating responses, which limits scope.
- Reviewer skeptical about the utility of end-to-end systems in narrow domains where engineered domain knowledge may perform better.
- Decision: accept—contribution is interesting and publication-worthy despite limitations.
- Paper offers a thorough evaluation and methods for generating adversarial examples, a valuable contribution.
- Limitation: ranking approach may not generalize well to new domains.
- Unclear how candidate responses would be obtained in practice or transferred to new domains without manual re-engineering.
- Data simulation constrains variability of training responses; reviewer requests publication of NL-generation rules for transparency.
- Suggestion: consider moving from ranking to response generation to improve generalization.
- Request for clarification on how ranked responses would be used in a real-world dialog system.
- Questions about handling out-of-vocabulary words and unseen scenarios.
- Inquiry about the potential impact of the approach on developing more robust dialog systems and requests for more simulation details.
