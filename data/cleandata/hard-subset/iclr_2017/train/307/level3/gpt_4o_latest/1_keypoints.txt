- Introduces a new public restaurant reservation dataset and task framework for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Breaks down dialogue tasks into subtasks (API calls, updates, displaying options) for error analysis and reproducibility.  
- Compares rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, and Memory Network models as baselines.  
- Decision: Reject — dataset and methodology have significant limitations preventing acceptance.  
- Dataset is fully deterministic, limiting realism and applicability to noisy, ambiguous real-world dialogues.  
- Missing non-neural baseline with word-order features (e.g., logistic regression + embeddings + bi-grams) biases comparisons toward neural models.  
- Unclear whether deep model performance differences stem from architecture or from regularization choices.  
- Incorrect claim that well-defined dialogue evaluation metrics do not exist; literature contains established measures.  
- Suggest adding noise/ambiguity to the dataset to better reflect real-world interactions.  
- Suggest including non-neural baselines with word-order information for fairer comparisons.  
- Suggest experiments to disentangle effects of regularization from model-specific capabilities.  
- Suggest revising the conclusion to acknowledge existing evaluation metrics and related literature.  
- Question: How will you address the dataset’s deterministic nature to improve real-world applicability?  
- Question: Why was a non-neural word-order baseline omitted and will you add one?  
- Question: Can you provide evidence separating task-specific model effects from regularization?  
- Question: How does this dataset complement existing resources like DSTC datasets?
