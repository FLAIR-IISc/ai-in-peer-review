- Introduces a novel dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in the restaurant reservation domain, generated via a KB-driven simulator and rule-based NLG.  
- Proposes five tasks targeting dialog management, API call generation, and knowledge interpretation.  
- Augments synthetic data with real-world examples from DSTC2 and an online concierge service to mix realism and control.  
- Evaluation uses per-response and per-dialog accuracy metrics.  
- Experiments show Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words and rule-based baselines.  
- Decision: Accept — dataset fills a critical gap by providing a controlled, reproducible, interpretable benchmark.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across baselines and models offers valuable insights into end-to-end dialog capabilities.  
- Practical relevance: narrow-domain focus plus real data helps bridge synthetic benchmarks and applied systems.  
- Well-motivated: addresses limitations of traditional slot-filling and scaling to new domains.  
- Scientific rigor: thorough experiments, inclusion of OOV test sets, and real-world data strengthen validity.  
- Transparency/reproducibility: framework is lightweight and open-access, but publishing the rule set would improve transparency.  
- Suggestion: ranking candidate responses limits generalization; consider response generation models.  
- Suggestion: rule-based NLG constrains linguistic diversity; expand rule set or use crowd-sourced data.  
- Suggestion: unclear how candidate responses are obtained for new domains, raising scalability concerns.  
- Question: please provide the complete list of rules used for generating the synthetic dataset.  
- Question: how would the framework extend to more complex or multi-domain scenarios and generalize?  
- Question: have you considered incorporating response generation models and what challenges do you anticipate?
