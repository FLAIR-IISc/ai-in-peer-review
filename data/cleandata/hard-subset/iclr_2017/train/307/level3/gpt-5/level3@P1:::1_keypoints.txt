- Introduces a lightweight, public testbed for goal-oriented dialog focused on restaurant booking with synthetic tasks, DSTC2 conversion, and a Concierge dataset.  
- Decomposes dialog into five deterministic synthetic tasks probing API calls, KB ranking, KB field retrieval, and full dialogs, plus real-data benchmarks.  
- Evaluates IR baselines, supervised embeddings, and end-to-end Memory Networks (with/without match-type features) using per-response and per-dialog accuracies, including an OOV entity setting.  
- Finds MemNNs (especially with match-type features) outperform simpler baselines on several behaviors but still fail on end-to-end dialog success and per-dialog accuracy.  
- The resource, task breakdown, and empirical analysis are valuable and likely to foster reproducible progress in goal-directed dialog.  
- Decision: Accept (weak) because the datasets/testbed are useful and the study surfaces important limitations and a roadmap for future work.  
- Problem addressed: assesses whether end-to-end neural dialog models can handle goal-oriented tasks requiring KB access and symbolic reasoning with a reproducible evaluation framework.  
- Motivation/placement: complements prior slot-filling and bAbI tasks and bridges synthetic and real-world scenarios; claim that evaluation measures are absent is overstated.  
- Results support claims broadly, with OOV and option-ranking analyses substantiating identified weaknesses; deterministic synthetic tasks limit robustness conclusions.  
- Suggest strengthening shallow baselines (linear classifiers with rich sparse features) to better calibrate neural gains.  
- Recommend adding a simple learned finite-state policy baseline that tracks slots and uses trained classifiers for actions.  
- Advise incorporating word-order/structured features (n-grams, positional weights) into baselines and surfacing bigram results in the main text.  
- Propose improving realism/robustness by adding noise (paraphrases, typos, ASR errors), user corrections, ambiguous turns, and reporting sample-complexity learning curves.  
- Evaluation suggestions: report slot-level accuracy, API-call exact match, top-k ranking/sorting correctness, and hits@k for surface variability.  
- Ask for discussion of fairness/practicality of candidate pools that include test-set utterances and clarify candidate pool construction implications.  
- Request clarity on data construction details: tie-breaking in sorting, acceptance probability, and potential brittleness from deterministic question order.  
- Question the realism of allowing models access to OOV KB during training and ask for results when OOV KB is withheld until test time.  
- Urge softening of claims about prior evaluation absence and citing relevant prior work (e.g., Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Specific methodological questions: impact of pooling candidates from train/val/test; effect of restricting candidate pool to train/val only.  
- Ask whether position-aware features for non-neural baselines were tried and how close they approach MemNN performance.  
- Question sensitivity of DSTC2 conversion choices (API calls/ratings) and potential biases from original dialog inconsistencies.  
- Request targeted error analysis for Tasks 3 and 5 to identify dominant failure modes (sorting, stopping, grounding, multi-hop retrieval).  
- Ask whether results generalize to other domains (hotels/flights) or show cross-domain transfer evidence.  
- Reviewer updated score after authors clarified baselines; addressing shallow baseline and OOV/candidate-pool issues would further strengthen the paper.
