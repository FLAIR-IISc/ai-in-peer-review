- Proposes an open, reproducible testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in restaurant reservations, decomposed into five interpretable subtasks plus DSTC2 and Concierge datasets.  
- Uses next-utterance ranking with per-response and per-dialog metrics focused on goal completion.  
- Shows a MemN2N baseline with match-type features outperforms IR and supervised embedding baselines and learns non-trivial operations like API calls and KB usage.  
- Demonstrates some OOV generalization when entity types are provided and finds synthetic task patterns mirror DSTC2/Concierge, supporting external validity.  
- Clarifies the goal: provide a controlled proxy to analyze end-to-end models, not to beat handcrafted slot-filling systems.  
- Decision: Accept (borderline/weak accept).  
- Key reason: Timely, well-motivated benchmark likely to become an important community resource for controlled, interpretable study of goal-oriented dialog.  
- Key reason: Careful, honest experimental analysis that reveals strengths and failures of end-to-end methods and connects synthetic to real data.  
- Addresses a literature gap: lack of an open, reproducible benchmark for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog compared to DSTC/ATIS limitations.  
- Well-situated relative to POMDP/slot-filling, chit-chat datasets, and bAbI-style QA tasks.  
- Design choices (simulator+KB, controlled subtasks, OOV splits, ranking evaluation) enable fine-grained error analysis while remaining lightweight.  
- Inclusion of DSTC2 and Concierge grounds tasks in realistic language and validates transfer of findings.  
- Empirical claims supported: MemN2N beats baselines; match-type features crucial for OOV and KB outputs; models can manage API calls but struggle with option presentation and dialog-level success.  
- Per-dialog accuracy is appropriate for measuring goal completion and highlights current model limitations.  
- Concern: Ranking-based evaluation sidesteps generation challenges and relies on precompiled candidate sets, weakening scalability claims.  
- Concern: Paper doesn't convincingly explain how candidate inventories would be constructed in new domains without handcrafting.  
- Concern: Simulator’s limited NLG templates may underrepresent linguistic diversity, potentially overestimating robustness despite DSTC2/Concierge mitigations.  
- Concern: Low per-dialog accuracy on complex tasks indicates a gap to practical reliability in narrow domains.  
- Suggestion: Release simulator code, KBs, NLG templates, and exact candidate lists to enable replication and extension.  
- Suggestion: Provide detailed statistics on candidate sets (size, distribution, overlap) and DSTC2/Concierge construction.  
- Suggestion: Add ablations (hops, memory size, question order, match-type granularity, sorting difficulty) to deepen analysis.  
- Suggestion: Consider adding a generation track (seq2seq with copy/pointer, delexicalization) evaluated by success-rate and slot F1.  
- Suggestion: Stress-test language variability via paraphrases, ASR-like noise, typos, and non-deterministic bot behavior.  
- Suggestion: Clarify OOV setup and realism of models matching unseen KB entities at test time.  
- Questions: How to assemble candidate lists in new domains and quantify performance dependence on candidate size/coverage?  
- Questions: Will the full simulator, templates, KBs, and candidate inventories be released?  
- Questions: Were generative baselines tried (copy/pointer or pointer-generator) and how do they compare on success metrics?  
- Questions: How robust is MemN2N to randomized missing-slot prompt order in Task 1?  
- Questions: How is sorting learned vs memorized in Task 3—any controlled tests with longer lists or ties?  
- Questions: Do models access the full KB (including unseen entities) at test time, and how would performance change without that access?  
- Questions: How sensitive are DSTC2 results to injected ratings used to force deterministic “best option”?  
- Questions: Can the authors provide learning curves to show data efficiency?  
- Questions: How consistent are Concierge API-use annotations and how is ambiguity handled?
