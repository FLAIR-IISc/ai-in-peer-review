- Introduces a public, rule-based synthetic dataset and suite of goal-oriented dialogue tasks in the restaurant domain that decompose capabilities (API calls, updating, ranking, end-to-end dialogue).  
- Testbed emphasizes reproducibility and controlled error analysis, includes OOV variants, and provides results on DSTC2 and a real-world concierge dataset.  
- Benchmarks IR baselines, supervised embeddings, and Memory Networks (with type-match features), clarifying strengths and weaknesses of end-to-end models.  
- Decision: Accept (weak accept) — dataset and task breakdown are impactful and enable standardized evaluation despite some methodological gaps.  
- Value and novelty: Fills a gap with a clean, modular benchmark; OOV setup and per-dialog accuracy are particularly useful.  
- Empirical thoroughness: Compares multiple model families, discusses match-type features and OOV generalization, and shows consistent patterns across synthetic and real data.  
- Concern: Comparisons blur advantages of deep vs shallow models due to lack of a competitive non-neural baseline that leverages word order and regularization.  
- Missing non-neural baselines (e.g., logistic regression with bag-of-embeddings, bigrams, match-type) may overstate neural models’ superiority, since tasks 1–5 are largely deterministic and compositional.  
- Tasks do not evaluate robustness to noise, ambiguity, or goal uncertainty (e.g., inferring goal distributions, dialogue repair), limiting real-world applicability.  
- Suggest adding a competitive non-neural benchmark (logistic regression or linear SVM with unigram+bigram, embedding averages, and match-type features).  
- Recommend clarifying when DL vs shallow models are suitable in small-data regimes and whether gains stem from capacity or type-matching regularization.  
- Minor correction: The claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performance” is too strong; cite prior task-oriented and non-goal dialogue evaluation work.  
- Suggest adding a noisy/ambiguous evaluation track (ASR noise, conflicting constraints, repair turns).  
- Question: Can you report results for a linear model with bigrams and match-type features on T1–T5 and OOV?  
- Question: How sensitive are Memory Network results to training set size — are there learning curves for sample efficiency?  
- Question: Do type-match features alone explain most OOV gains, or do multiple hops provide independent benefits?  
- Question: Could you release a variant with stochastic user policies and noisy KB returns to probe robustness?
