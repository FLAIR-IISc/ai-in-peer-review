- Introduces a reproducible end-to-end goal-oriented dialog testbed in a restaurant domain: simulated KB-grounded dataset with rule-based NLG, five subtasks, plus DSTC2 and concierge conversions.  
- Proposes a ranking-based evaluation protocol reporting per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Empirical finding: Memory Networks outperform TF-IDF/nearest-neighbor and supervised bag-of-embeddings, especially on multi-hop reasoning and KB use.  
- Well situated in literature, addressing the gap between chit-chat models and goal-directed dialog needs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main accept reasons: benchmark provides reproducible diagnostics for KB use, state tracking, and OOV handling; experiments are careful and informative.  
- Evidence: rigorous comparisons, OOV splits, and cross-dataset validation support claims and highlight strengths/weaknesses.  
- Limitation: reliance on ranking limits ecological validity versus generation.  
- Reviewer skepticism: end-to-end approaches may be less practical than engineered slot-filling in narrow domains, though valuable as a research probe.  
- Requested: release full rule-based NLG templates and coverage statistics due to limited linguistic variability.  
- Requested: include generative baselines (e.g., seq2seq/pointer-generator) to test portability beyond fixed candidate sets.  
- Practicality question: where candidate response inventories come from in new domains and sensitivity to candidate set size/quality.  
- Requested: detailed error analyses for Tasks 3/5 (sorting vs entity grounding vs discourse control).  
- Requested additional evaluations: human/task-completion metrics, calibration, and latency.  
- Clarify OOV assumptions: models see KB at train time—report performance when KB access is restricted or entities are partially unseen.
