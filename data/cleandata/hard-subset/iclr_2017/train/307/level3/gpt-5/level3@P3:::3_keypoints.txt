- Proposes a clean, reproducible restaurant-domain testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog grounded in a KB with a rule-based simulator generating user and system utterances.  
- Decomposes the problem into five subtasks (API call issuing/updating, option display with sorting, extra info retrieval, full dialogs) and converts DSTC2 and Concierge datasets.  
- Frames evaluation as next-response ranking over a global candidate set and reports per-response and per-dialog accuracy, including OOV splits to test unseen-entity generalization.  
- Benchmarks MemN2N against TF-IDF, nearest-neighbor IR, and supervised embeddings, adding match-type features for entities.  
- MemN2N outperforms bag-of-words/IR baselines, learns API/entity behaviors, and beats a rule-based system on DSTC2, but per-dialog accuracy stays low on hardest tasks.  
- Decision: Accept — dataset/task design is timely, well-constructed, reproducible, and includes a useful OOV regime.  
- Decision: Accept — empirical study is thorough and honest, showing MemN2N gains while exposing important gaps likely to spur future work.  
- Problem relevance: Fills a gap between chit-chat datasets and transactional dialog by providing a modular, analyzable proxy tied to real data.  
- Methodology: Tasks probe distinct capabilities (state tracking, KB use, sorting, OOV handling) with well-motivated OOV splits and dual metrics highlighting brittleness.  
- Empirical rigor: Uses multiple baselines, a competitive end-to-end model, entity-typing augmentation, and informative ablations; results generalize to DSTC2/Concierge.  
- Clarity/reproducibility: Paper is clear, provides model/training details, and positions dataset for open use with a natural breakdown for error analysis.  
- Reservation: Ranking vs. generation — ranking simplifies comparison but relies on a precompiled candidate set and hides fluency/control trade-offs.  
- Reservation: Narrow-domain value — hand-engineered slot-filling may still outperform end-to-end approaches in structured single-domain settings.  
- Reservation: Language variability — rule-based NLG limits diversity, so generalization to paraphrases and noisy ASR may be overestimated.  
- Feedback: Publish the simulator’s full NLG resources (user/bot templates and templating rules) to allow stress-testing and paraphrasing.  
- Feedback: Discuss practical pipelines for candidate construction in new domains (few-shot templates, retrieval from logs, controllable NLG).  
- Feedback: Add a generative track (copy+generate, constrained decoding, human evaluation) in addition to ranking to reflect deployment constraints.  
- Feedback: Include data-scaling curves and noise-robustness experiments (typos, paraphrases, disfluencies) to quantify brittleness.  
- Feedback: Provide fine-grained error analysis for Tasks 3 and 5 (sorting errors, option continuation/stop, entity grounding).  
- Feedback: Extend metrics to include goal-success with limited tolerance and dialog-efficiency measures (turns to completion).  
- Answer Q1: Problem is systematic evaluation of end-to-end dialog systems requiring KB access and symbolic ops, comparing strengths/limits to traditional methods.  
- Answer Q2: Motivation and placement are strong — fills a clear evaluation gap and connects to DSTC literature while leveraging MemNN advances.  
- Answer Q3: Results support claims — MemNN shows progress (with match types) but per-dialog gaps remain, validating conclusions.  
- Question: How would candidate construction translate to a brand-new domain without prior logs, and how does performance change if candidates are restricted to training-only utterances?  
- Question: How sensitive are DSTC2 results to adding ratings for deterministic option ranking, and can non-deterministic policies be supported?  
- Question: Does giving models access to both KBs at test time create leakage beyond type matching, and what if the test-only KB is partially hidden?  
- Question: Will you release exact NLG templates/rules for user and bot, e.g., in an appendix or supplementary material?  
- Question: Did you try pointer-generator or copy-augmented seq2seq baselines (e.g., reranking generated candidates), and what were the findings?  
- Question: Can you provide learning curves and sensitivity analysis on number of hops to show when multi-hop reasoning is essential?  
- Question: Any plans to validate per-dialog accuracy against human judgments of goal success and user satisfaction on DSTC2/Concierge?
