- Introduces a reproducible, lightweight testbed for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog in a controlled restaurant-reservation setting with five synthetic tasks and two real datasets (DSTC2, Concierge).  
- Evaluation framed as next-utterance ranking with per-response and per-dialog accuracy; benchmarks include rule-based, IR, supervised embeddings, and Memory Networks.  
- Memory Networks outperform strong baselines and learn non-trivial operations (API calls, OOV entity handling via match-type features) but still struggle on full end-to-end dialog completion.  
- Testbed positioned as an open, interpretable proxy for goal-oriented dialog research with an explicit OOV split to probe entity generalization.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Fills an important gap by providing reproducible, fine-grained evaluation for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog with clear tasks and built-in OOV generalization.  
- Key reason 2: Empirical study is thorough, well-situated in prior work, includes multiple baselines and ablations, and shows transfer from synthetic tasks to real datasets.  
- Motivation: Aims to test goal-completion skills (state tracking, KB querying, using structured outputs) that chit-chat benchmarks don’t probe.  
- Literature positioning: Correctly framed relative to slot-filling/POMDP systems, end-to-end chit-chat models, and QA-style toy tasks.  
- Claims vs. evidence: Methodology and metrics are clear; candidate-ranking is consistent; match-type ablation is informative; per-dialog metric supports cautious conclusions.  
- Rigor/reproducibility: Dataset, KB, tasks, hyperparameters, and OOV protocol are released and well-documented; concern that the candidate pool construction (aggregating splits) may be unrealistic and inflate results.  
- Suggestion: Add a generative baseline (e.g., attention-based seq2seq or pointer/copy model) or discuss how candidate lists would be obtained in new domains.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate candidate-set realism by restricting candidates to train-only utterances, varying pool sizes, and/or using retrieval-based prefiltering.  
- Suggestion: Publish simulator NL templates, deterministic question-order rules, and sampling seeds; report performance as user template variety increases.  
- Suggestion: Provide more detail and error analysis on DSTC2 conversion (ratings, ASR noise) and Concierge dataset quality.  
- Suggestion: Add stronger baselines such as seq2seq with copy and re-scoring, key–value memory reads over KB facts, and nearest-neighbor over dialog states.  
- Suggestion: Complement per-dialog accuracy with goal success rate under stochastic user acceptance and simulated user-ablation tests.  
- Question: How would candidate lists be operationalized in a new domain without hand-crafting; have retrieval-first pipelines been tried and how does candidate size affect results?  
- Question: What is the effect of restricting the candidate set to training utterances only—does performance or stability change?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to user template count and to relaxing deterministic question order?  
- Question: Beyond match-type features, were copy/pointer mechanisms or delexicalization–relexicalization approaches tried for OOV handling, and how do they compare?  
- Question: For Task 3, what are the failure modes—sorting, grounding ratings to options, or dialog control—and can you provide error breakdowns/attention visualizations?  
- Question: How were DSTC2 ratings assigned during conversion, could this bias option selection, and do MemNNs exploit injected ratings over language cues?  
- Question: Has the framework been tested in other domains (hotels, flights) to demonstrate portability?  
- Question: Any human evaluation for Concierge or DSTC2 to assess usefulness/appropriateness of selected responses?
