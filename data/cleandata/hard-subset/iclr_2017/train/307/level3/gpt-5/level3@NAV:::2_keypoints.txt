- Paper provides a reality check: end-to-end ML models that work for chit-chat are inadequate for reliably completing goal-oriented dialogs.
- Contributions: a clean, reproducible restaurant-domain task suite decomposing dialog skills (DM, API calls, KB use, sorting, OOV entities).
- Contributions: two conjectures — E2E ML remains insufficient for goal-directed scenarios; relative performance on synthetic tasks predicts natural-data performance.
- Contributions: first thorough deployment of Memory Networks for goal-oriented dialog with a useful “match type” augmentation.
- Experimental design, clarity, appendix, and tables (e.g., hop ablations) are excellent and reproducible.
- Decision: Accept for significance and careful experimentation despite modest algorithmic novelty.
- Key problem: Can E2E models replace traditional systems in transactional dialogs and what capabilities are missing?
- Motivation/placement: Fills a gap between POMDP/slot-filling and recent E2E dialog work by providing goal-oriented evaluation resources.
- Evidence: Results support both conjectures; per-dialog accuracy is low even when per-response looks good, and synthetic rankings transfer to DSTC2 and Concierge.
- Protocol and ablations (hops) and OOV splits are sound and informative.
- Metrics feedback: Common dialog metrics are brittle; recommend reporting API-call correctness and success-weighted cost metrics (API errors, dialog turns, user rejections).
- Concierge evaluation feedback: Clarify whether “correct” is single best vs top-5; report top-1 and Recall@k and justify choice.
- Vocabulary mismatch feedback: Match-type needs exact KB matches and misses semantic associations; suggest soft typing, entity linking, subword/char models, schema linking, or dual-encoder retrieval.
- Protocol sensitivity feedback: Provide sensitivity analyses for smaller, task-restricted candidate pools and show how definitions affect T3 vs T5 outcomes.
- Assumptions feedback: Discuss realism of granting access to both KBs at training for OOV tests and whether hiding the OOV KB at test changes results.
- Question 1: Report task success conditioned on API-call correctness and the advocated cost-based metric.
- Question 2: On Concierge, provide top-1 vs top-5 results and human judgments for a subset.
- Question 3: How does match-type perform with fuzzy matching or learned entity linking?
- Question 4: Could pointer or copy mechanisms improve T3 sorting/option presentation?
- Question 5: How robust are results to alternative candidate sets and stochastic variants of bot behavior?
