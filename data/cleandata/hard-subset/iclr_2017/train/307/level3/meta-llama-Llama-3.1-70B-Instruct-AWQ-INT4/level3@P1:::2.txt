**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications, specifically in the context of restaurant reservations. The authors argue that current end-to-end dialog systems are insufficient for goal-oriented dialogs and aim to provide a reality check on their performance. They introduce a set of five tasks that break down a goal-directed objective into several subtasks, allowing for a more nuanced evaluation of dialog systems. The paper also presents experimental results using various models, including Memory Networks, and provides a thorough analysis of their performance on the proposed tasks.

**Decision: Accept**

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of goal-oriented dialog systems. The proposed testbed is a valuable contribution to the field, and the experimental results provide a thorough evaluation of various models. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide helpful appendices and tables that support their claims.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **Consider alternative evaluation metrics**: The authors raise a valid point about the limitations of common dialog metrics, such as per-response accuracy. Consider exploring alternative evaluation metrics that better capture the nuances of goal-oriented dialogs.
2. **Address vocabulary mismatch**: The authors note that the match type feature requires exact KB occurrences and does not learn semantic associations automatically. Consider exploring ways to address this vocabulary mismatch, such as using word embeddings or other techniques.
3. **Explore more meaningful loss functions**: The authors suggest exploring more meaningful, application-driven loss functions that are tied to real-world revenue. Consider exploring this idea further and evaluating its impact on the performance of dialog systems.
