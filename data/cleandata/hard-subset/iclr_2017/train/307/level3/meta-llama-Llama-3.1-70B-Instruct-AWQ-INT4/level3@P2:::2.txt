**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel testbed for evaluating end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications, specifically in the context of restaurant reservations. The authors propose a set of five tasks that break down the strengths and shortcomings of end-to-end dialog systems, allowing for a more nuanced understanding of their capabilities. They also present a simple yet effective modification to Memory Networks, called "match type" features, which enables the model to better handle entities and out-of-vocabulary words. The paper provides a thorough evaluation of various models on these tasks, including rule-based systems, classical information retrieval methods, supervised embeddings, and Memory Networks. The results show that while end-to-end models can learn to perform non-trivial operations, they still fall short in handling goal-oriented dialogs, particularly in interpreting knowledge about entities and presenting results to the user.
