- Proposes a new simulated restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog using a KB and rule-based NL generation.  
- Task formulation: rank candidate next-turn responses with evaluation by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.  
- Demonstrates Memory Networks outperform bag-of-words baselines on this task.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance primarily because the paper introduces a valuable dataset/task and provides thorough model evaluation.  
- Contribution is significant: offers a systematic, controlled way to evaluate end-to-end goal-oriented dialog with well-chosen metrics and detailed analysis.  
- Reservation: end-to-end training may have limited value in narrow domains where hand-engineered systems could yield better user satisfaction.  
- Limitation: focusing on ranking rather than generation limits generalization to new domains due to reliance on pre-collected candidate responses.  
- Practicality concern: unclear how candidate response lists would be obtained for new domains without hand collection.  
- Concern: simulator’s rule-based NL generation restricts variability and may not reflect real-world conversation diversity.  
- Request for transparency: authors should publish the NL-generation rules used in the simulator for reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss end-to-end limitations and generalization, evaluate generation-based models, provide NL-rule details publicly, and address practicality of candidate lists.  
- Questions for authors: provide NL-generation rule details and design rationale; explain plans to generalize beyond narrow domains; report whether generation-based models were considered/evaluated.
