**Summary of the Paper's Contribution**

This paper introduces a novel, publicly available, rule-based synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue, covering API calls, option display, and full dialogues. The authors' primary contribution is a testbed to evaluate the strengths and shortcomings of end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications, specifically in the context of restaurant reservations. They propose a set of five tasks to test crucial capabilities that dialog systems should possess, such as performing dialog management, querying knowledge bases, and interpreting the output of such queries.

**Decision: Accept**

I decide to accept this paper because it provides a valuable contribution to the field of goal-oriented dialogue systems. The authors have created a well-motivated and well-placed testbed in the literature, which can facilitate future research in this area. The paper's approach is sound, and the results are correct and scientifically rigorous.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. The authors have identified a significant gap in the existing literature, where there is a lack of resources to train and test end-to-end models in goal-oriented scenarios. They have addressed this gap by creating a novel dataset and tasks that can be used to evaluate the performance of end-to-end dialog systems.
2. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of different models, including rule-based systems, classical information retrieval models, supervised embeddings, and end-to-end Memory Networks. The results show that Memory Networks outperform other models across most tasks, but still have limitations in handling certain aspects of goal-oriented dialogue.
3. The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their testbed by evaluating the performance of different models on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The results show that the testbed can be used to identify areas where end-to-end models need to improve before they can reliably handle goal-oriented dialogue.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:

1. Consider adding a non-neural benchmark, such as logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram, and match-type features, to provide a more comprehensive comparison of different models.
2. Clarify the claim that "existing work has no well-defined measures of performance" by providing more context and references to support this statement.
3. Provide more details about the hyperparameters used for each model and how they were optimized.
4. Consider adding more examples of predictions made by the Memory Network model to illustrate its strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details about the KB entity types used in the match type features and how they were selected?
2. How did you optimize the hyperparameters for each model, and what were the values used for the best-performing models?
3. Can you provide more insights into why the Memory Network model struggled with tasks T3 and T5, and how you plan to address these limitations in future work?
