**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper introduces a new simulated restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, using a knowledge base (KB) and rule-based natural language (NL) generation. The dataset is designed to test the strengths and weaknesses of end-to-end dialog systems in goal-oriented applications. The authors propose a set of five tasks within the goal-oriented context of restaurant reservation, which cover several dialog stages and test various abilities such as dialog management, querying KBs, and interpreting the output of such queries. The paper also presents results on two additional datasets extracted from real interactions with users, to confirm that the pattern of results observed in the simulated tasks is a good proxy for what would be observed on real data.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I decide to accept this paper, with the key reasons being:

1. The paper introduces a new and interesting dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of various models on the proposed tasks, including Memory Networks, which show promising results.

**Additional Feedback to Improve the Paper**

While the paper is well-written, there are a few areas that could be improved:

1. The authors could provide more details on the rule-based NL generation used in the simulation. For example, what are the specific rules used to generate the NL utterances?
2. The paper could benefit from more analysis of the results, particularly in terms of understanding why certain models perform better than others on certain tasks.
3. The authors could also provide more discussion on the limitations of the proposed dataset and tasks, and how they could be extended or modified to better capture the complexities of real-world goal-oriented dialog.
