- Proposes a novel simulated restaurant-domain dataset using a KB and rule-based NL generation for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog systems.
- Dataset aims for systematic, controlled evaluation to reveal strengths and shortcomings of end-to-end models.
- Empirical comparison showing Memory Networks versus classical IR and supervised embedding models.
- Provides a well-structured, reproducible evaluation framework with valuable insights into model performance.
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with detailed dataset/task descriptions and well-presented experiments/results.
- Concern: practical value of end-to-end training in narrow domains where hand-engineered systems may yield better user satisfaction.
- Concern: framing the task as ranking rather than generation limits generalization and relies on pre-collected candidates.
- Concern: obtaining candidate response lists in new domains may require significant manual effort and be impractical.
- Concern: rule-based NL generation in the simulator restricts training-data variability; requests publication of NL-generation rules for transparency.
- Decision: Accept.
- Acceptance reasons: novel dataset contribution, effective Memory Network comparisons, and clear/well-written presentation.
- Requested clarifications: publish NL-generation rules, explain plans to address end-to-end limitations in narrow domains, and discuss practicality of obtaining candidate response lists in new domains.
