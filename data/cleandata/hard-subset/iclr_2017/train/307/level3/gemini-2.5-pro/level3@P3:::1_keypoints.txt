- Introduces a new publicly available synthetic dataset and five rule-based subtasks for end-to-end goal-oriented dialogue in a restaurant domain.  
- Tasks probe specific capabilities: issuing API calls, handling updates, displaying KB options, and providing extra information.  
- Evaluates baselines (IR, supervised embeddings) and a Memory Network (MemNN), plus tests on DSTC2 and a real-world Concierge dataset.  
- MemNN outperforms simpler baselines but per-dialogue accuracy remains low, indicating significant challenges.  
- Main contribution: a lightweight, reproducible testbed that facilitates controlled, component-wise evaluation and error analysis.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Critical flaw: baselines all discard word order, making neural model gains appear stronger than they might be.  
- Recommendation: add a sequence-aware non-neural baseline (e.g., logistic regression/SVM with TF-IDF n-grams and match-type features).  
- Strength: inclusion of an OOV test set to evaluate generalization to unseen entities.  
- Evidence: performance patterns on synthetic tasks correlate with real datasets, supporting the testbed’s usefulness as a proxy.  
- Limitation: synthetic tasks are deterministic and do not model real-world noise, ambiguity, or dialogue repair.  
- Alternative explanation: MemNN’s advantage may stem from regularization or capturing simple sequential patterns rather than superior reasoning.  
- Suggestion: correct overstated claims about prior work on evaluation metrics and explicitly discuss dataset limitations in the paper.
