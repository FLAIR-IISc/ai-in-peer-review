- New dataset and 5 progressively harder goal-oriented dialog sub-tasks (restaurant reservation), generated by a simulator with OOV setting; baselines evaluated show MemNNs (with match-type features) outperform others but full-dialog performance is low; validated on DSTC2 and Concierge — useful contribution.

- Decision: Reject (encouraged resubmit).

- Primary reason 1: No strong non-neural baseline that captures word order was included, undermining the claim about end-to-end neural model strength.

- Primary reason 2: Synthetic tasks are deterministic and template-based, so they do not test noise, ambiguity, belief tracking, or error recovery central to real-world dialog.

- Main experimental weakness: Baselines (TF-IDF, NN, bag-of-embeddings) ignore word order, making comparisons unfair and possibly overstating the MemNN advantage.

- A simple strong baseline (e.g., logistic regression/SVM with unigrams, bigrams, and match-type features) could perform well on templated tasks and should be included.

- Deterministic simulator yields predictable user utterances from limited patterns, sidestepping core dialog challenges like ambiguity and dialogue repair.

- Although DSTC2 and Concierge validation is positive, limitations of the synthetic suite — the primary contribution — remain important and should be acknowledged.

- Small training sets (~1000 dialogs) raise overfitting concerns for deep models and leave open whether better-regularized simpler methods could match MemNNs; scalability with data is not analyzed.

- Recommended action: Add a non-neural strong baseline (e.g., logistic regression with n-grams and match-type features) to strengthen experimental claims.

- Recommended action: Soften overstated claims about lack of prior resources and evaluation metrics, and cite relevant prior work (e.g., Wen et al., 2015; Liu et al., 2016).

- Rebuttal questions to address: Why omit n-gram or RNN/LSTM baselines? How can you rule out MemNN gains being due to regularization/optimization on small data? Any plans to add stochasticity or linguistic variation to the simulator?
