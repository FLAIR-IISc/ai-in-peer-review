- Introduces a new simulated restaurant-domain dataset for end-to-end goal-oriented dialog, using a KB and rule-based NL generation.
- Dataset task is to rank candidate next-turn responses, with evaluation by per-response and per-dialog accuracy.
- Shows Memory Networks improve over bag-of-words baselines.
- Reviewer is skeptical about the value of end-to-end training in narrow domains where hand-engineered systems may yield better user satisfaction.
- Disappointed the task is ranking rather than generation, since ranking depends on pre-collected candidates and limits generalization to new domains.
- Questions practicality: where would candidate response lists come from in a new domain if not hand-collected?
- Simulator’s rule-based NL generation restricts variability in training data; reviewer requests the list of NL-generation rules be published.
- Overall considered an interesting contribution worthy of publication; score updated after clarifications.
