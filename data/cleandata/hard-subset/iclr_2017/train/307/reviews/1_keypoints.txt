- Introduces a new publicly available, rule-based synthetic dataset and tasks for goal-oriented dialogue covering API calls, option display, and full dialogues.  
- Useful and welcome contribution that can facilitate future dialogue-system research.  
- Unclear how suitable deep learning models are for these tasks versus traditional/rule-based or shallow models, since DL needs many examples and performance differences may reflect regularization.  
- Tasks 1–5 are deterministic, so they do not evaluate handling of noisy or ambiguous interactions (e.g., inferring distributions over user goals or dialogue repair).  
- Major weakness: no baseline that incorporates word order, making neural models appear unnaturally strong while simpler baselines might be competitive.  
- Recommendation: add a non-neural benchmark (e.g., logistic regression with word embeddings, bi-gram and match-type features); inclusion would likely increase the review score.  
- Minor correction: claim that “existing work has no well defined measures of performances” is inaccurate—prior end-to-end task-oriented and non-goal dialogue evaluation work exists (Wen et al., Liu et al., Yu et al.).  
- Reviewer updated their score after new results were added.
