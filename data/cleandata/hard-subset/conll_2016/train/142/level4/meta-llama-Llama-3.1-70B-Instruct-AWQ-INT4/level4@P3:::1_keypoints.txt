- Paper proposes using Gaussian Processes for MT Quality Estimation to model predictive uncertainty for real-world applications.
- Introduction well-structured and highlights limitations of traditional point-estimate QE methods.
- Authors provide clear explanations of GPs, kernels, and warping functions; research questions are well-posed.
- Use of asymmetric risk scenarios is a valuable and novel contribution for QE.
- Choice of evaluation metric (post-editing rates) needs justification versus alternatives like prediction (H)TER.
- Section 3.2 first paragraph is unclear and should be rephrased for clarity.
- Trends for fr-en and en-de are not apparent; the drastic decrease in NLL/NLPD with warped GPs is insufficiently explained.
- Paper lacks comparison to point-estimate baselines from existing QE work in the results tables.
- Interpretability of NLL and NLPD improvements is limited; qualitative examples would help illustrate significance.
- Strength: clear research questions addressing limitations of prior QE methods.
- Strength: paper is well-organized and easy to follow across introduction, methods, and results.
- Strength: proposing uncertainty-aware QE and asymmetric risk analysis is a meaningful contribution.
- Weakness: applying GPs to QE lacks substantial modeling novelty.
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to existing point-estimate QE methods weakens claims.
- Question: justify choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER.
- Question: how will authors improve interpretability of NLL and NLPD values in future work?
- Question: can authors add a point-estimate baseline from prior work to result tables?
- Minor: correct "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates".
- Minor: Figure 1 caption mislabels line colors (actually blue and green, not blue and red).
- Minor: cite the GP toolkit used if one was employed for modeling.
