The paper explores the application of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian processes, to predict post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of machine translation (MT) output. The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction that effectively highlights the limitations of point estimates in quality estimation (QE) for real-world applications. The description of different asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for estimator selection is particularly noteworthy.

For readers familiar with Gaussian processes (GPs), the paper dedicates considerable space to reviewing these concepts, but this effort is worthwhile, as it introduces these ideas to readers who may be less familiar with them. The GP approach, including the choice of kernels and the use of warping, is explained clearly and is easy to follow. The research questions addressed in this paper are interesting and well-formulated.

However, I have some questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, specifically with respect to Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:

- The choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER as the target variable is not explicitly justified. Since TER is a common metric in QE research, it would be beneficial to provide a clear explanation for this decision.
- In Section 3.2, the first paragraph is unclear. The text mentions a trend observed for fr-en and en-de that is not present for en-es, but the drastic decrease in NLL and NLPD with warped GPs is consistent across all three datasets.
- Although the paper explicitly states that it does not aim to advance the state-of-the-art (given the use of only the standard 17 baseline features), it would be helpful to include results from another point estimate model from existing work in the result tables. This would provide a better understanding of the overall quality of the models.
- Related to this point, the interpretation of NLL and NLPD values can be challenging. As the paper argues against relying solely on mean absolute error (MAE) for evaluation, it would be beneficial to provide a qualitative analysis with actual examples to illustrate the significance of drastic reductions in NLL/NLPD.
Section 4 is well-written and effectively explains the results in an intuitive manner.

Overall, I appreciate the paper's focus on the limitations of point estimates in QE, as additional information such as confidence intervals is crucial in this context. Although the submission does not advance the state-of-the-art or introduce significant novelty in terms of modeling (given the previous use of GPs), its research questions and goals are clearly stated and well-executed.

Minor issues:

- In Section 4, "over and underestimates" should be replaced with "over- and underestimates."
- The caption for Figure 1 incorrectly states that the lines are blue and red, when they are actually blue and green.
- If a specific toolkit was used for GP modeling, it would be beneficial to cite it in the final paper.
