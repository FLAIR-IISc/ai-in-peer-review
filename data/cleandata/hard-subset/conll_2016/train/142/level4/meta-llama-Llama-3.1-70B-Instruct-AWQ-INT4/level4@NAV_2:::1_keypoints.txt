- Paper applies Gaussian process probabilistic models to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Well-structured with a clear introduction that highlights the problem of QE point estimates in real-world applications.  
- Strong presentation of asymmetric risk scenarios and how they imply different estimators.  
- Provides a useful introduction to GPs for non-expert readers, justifying the space spent on background.  
- GP approach, kernel choices, and warping are clearly explained and easy to follow; research questions are well-phrased.  
- Question: choice of post-editing rates over predicting (H)TER is not justified and should be explained.  
- Section 3.2 is unclear: the claimed trend differences between fr-en & en-de versus en-es are not clearly described; NLL/NLPD reductions with warped GPs appear in all datasets.  
- Paper avoids SOTA claims (uses standard 17 baseline features) but should still include a point-estimate baseline model in result tables for context.  
- NLL and NLPD are hard to interpret in isolation; suggest qualitative examples to show what a drastic reduction means in practice.  
- Section 4 is very well written and explains results intuitively.  
- Overall contribution: highlights the limitations of point estimates in QE and the benefits of modeling predictive uncertainty with GPs.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in modeling (GPs have been used before) and does not advance state-of-the-art.  
- Minor wording issue: "over and underestimates" → "over- and underestimates".  
- Minor figure issue: Figure 1 caption lists incorrect line colors.  
- Minor suggestion: cite the GP toolkit used for modeling if applicable.  
- Questions for authors: justify post-editing rates vs HTER choice, address limitations/novelty and SOTA advancement, and provide GP implementation/toolkit details.
