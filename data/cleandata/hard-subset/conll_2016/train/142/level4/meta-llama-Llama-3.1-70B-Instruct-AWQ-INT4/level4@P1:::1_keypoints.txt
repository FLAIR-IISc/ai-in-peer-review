- Paper explores Gaussian processes for MT quality estimation by regressing post-editing time/rates and argues for using uncertainty over point estimates.
- Well-structured with a clear introduction and a good explanation of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators.
- GP approach, kernel choices, and warping are clearly explained and easy to follow.
- Research questions are interesting and well-phrased.
- For GP-savvy readers, some GP material is lengthy but justified to introduce concepts to a broader audience.
- Choice of post-editing rates over HTER is not justified — authors should explain this decision.
- Section 3.2 is unclear: need clarification on the claimed trend differences for fr-en & en-de versus en-es given NLL/NLPD decrease with warped GPs for all datasets.
- Would be helpful to include an existing point-estimate baseline in result tables to contextualize overall prediction quality.
- NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret; recommend qualitative analysis with examples showing what a “drastic” reduction means.
- Paper does not advance state-of-the-art or modeling novelty since GPs have been used before in QE.
- Strengths: clear goals, strong explanation of uncertainty and new evaluation metrics (NLPD), and intuitive discussion in Section 4.
- Minor issues: wording (“over and underestimates” → “over- and underestimates”), incorrect figure caption colors, and missing reference to the GP toolkit if used.
- Questions for authors: justify target choice (post-editing rates vs HTER), clarify Section 3.2 trends, include point-estimate comparison, and state the GP toolkit used.
