- Uses Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Well-structured paper with a clear introduction highlighting problems of point estimates in QE.  
- Noteworthy explanation of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators.  
- Thorough, accessible explanation of GPs, kernel choices, and warping.  
- Research questions are interesting and well-phrased.  
- Needs justification for choosing post-editing rates over common metrics like TER/HTER.  
- Section 3.2 contains a confusing contradiction about dataset-specific trends versus universal NLL/NLPD decreases with warped GPs.  
- Recommend adding a comparison to a point-estimate baseline in result tables to contextualize model quality.  
- Suggest providing qualitative examples to help interpret the magnitude and significance of NLL/NLPD reductions.  
- Section 4 is well-written and explains results intuitively.  
- Contribution lacks modeling novelty and does not advance SOTA, but the goals are clearly stated and executed.  
- Minor issues: fix wording to "over- and underestimates," correct Figure 1 color caption (blue/green), and cite any GP toolkit used.
