- Paper studies Gaussian Processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Main contributions: apply GPs to QE, evaluate full predictive distributions via NLPD, and show use in asymmetric loss scenarios.
- Strength: calls out dangers of relying only on point estimates and proposes probabilistic modeling as a remedy.
- Strength: provides a clear introduction to Gaussian Processes and their application to QE.
- Strength: investigates asymmetric risk scenarios, showing benefits of well-calibrated predictive distributions.
- Strength: writing and result presentation are generally clear.
- Weakness: choice of post-editing rates over HTER is not justified; no comparison with other target formulations.
- Weakness: NLPD values are hard to interpret and the paper lacks qualitative examples to illustrate them.
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—GPs have been used previously in quality estimation.
- Question: please justify the choice of post-editing rates rather than HTER.
- Question: how will you make NLPD interpretable, and can you add qualitative examples?
- Question: can you compare your models against existing point-estimate baselines?
- Minor: fix wording to "over- and underestimates" in Section 4.
- Minor: Figure 1 caption mislabels line colors (lines are blue and green, not blue and red).
- Minor: cite the GP toolkit/library used for modeling if applicable.
