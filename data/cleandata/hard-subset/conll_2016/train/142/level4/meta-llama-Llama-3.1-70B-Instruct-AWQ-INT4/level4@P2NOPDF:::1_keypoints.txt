- Paper applies Gaussian processes to predict post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation, with a clear introduction and motivation against relying on point estimates.  
- Asymmetric risk scenarios and their corresponding estimators are well explained and noteworthy.  
- Paper provides a helpful explanation of Gaussian processes, kernel choices, and warping, useful for readers unfamiliar with GPs.  
- Research questions are interesting and well-formulated.  
- Authors should justify predicting post-editing rates instead of standard metrics like HTER/TER.  
- Section 3.2 contains a contradictory description about NLL/NLPD trends across datasets and needs clarification.  
- Include a comparison to an existing point-estimate model in result tables to contextualize model quality.  
- NLL/NLPD results are hard to interpret; add qualitative examples or explain what constitutes a "drastic" reduction.  
- Section 4 is well-written and explains results intuitively.  
- Contribution is valuable for highlighting the importance of confidence information, though it does not advance state-of-the-art modeling.  
- Minor wording issue: use "over- and underestimates" instead of "over and underestimates."  
- Fix Figure 1 caption: lines are blue and green, not blue and red.  
- If a specific GP toolkit was used, add a reference to it in the final paper.
