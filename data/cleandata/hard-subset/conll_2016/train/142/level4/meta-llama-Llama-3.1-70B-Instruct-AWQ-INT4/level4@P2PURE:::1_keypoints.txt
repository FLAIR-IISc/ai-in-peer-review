- Applies Gaussian processes to predict post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Well-structured paper with a clear introduction highlighting limitations of point estimates in QE.
- Noteworthy discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and their impact on estimator selection.
- Useful GP background for non-experts; kernel choice and warping are clearly explained.
- Research questions are interesting and well-formulated.
- Unclear justification for choosing post-editing rates over the commonly used HTER as the target variable.
- Section 3.2’s first paragraph is unclear and contains an inconsistent description of trends across datasets.
- Warped GPs show drastic NLL/NLPD decreases across datasets, but this consistency isn’t clearly stated or discussed.
- Recommend including a point-estimate baseline from prior work in result tables to contextualize model quality.
- Suggest adding qualitative examples to illustrate the practical significance of NLL/NLPD reductions versus MAE.
- Section 4 is well-written and explains results intuitively.
- Paper does not advance state-of-the-art or modeling novelty (GPs used before) but has clear goals and solid execution.
- Minor wording issue: “over and underestimates” should be “over- and underestimates.”
- Minor figure issue: Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (says blue and red, but they are blue and green).
- Minor reproducibility note: cite the GP toolkit if a specific implementation was used.
