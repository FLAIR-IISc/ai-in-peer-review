This paper presents an investigation into the application of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian processes, to predict post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of machine translation output. 

The paper's structure is well-organized, with a clear and concise introduction that effectively highlights the limitations of point estimates in quality estimation for real-world applications. The discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and their corresponding estimators is particularly noteworthy. 

Although the paper dedicates considerable space to explaining Gaussian processes, this effort is justified as it provides a comprehensive introduction to these concepts for readers unfamiliar with them. The explanation of the Gaussian process approach, kernel selection, and warping is clear and easy to follow. The research questions posed by the paper are well-articulated and thought-provoking.

However, I have some suggestions and questions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, specifically the Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:

- The choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER warrants justification, as TER is a commonly predicted value in quality estimation research.
- The first paragraph of Section 3.2 is unclear, particularly the trend observed for fr-en and en-de that is not present in en-es. The drastic decrease in NLL and NLPD with warped GPs is consistent across all three datasets.
- Although the paper explicitly states that it does not aim to advance the state-of-the-art, incorporating a point estimate model from existing work in the result tables would provide a useful benchmark for evaluating the overall quality of the models.
- The interpretation of NLL and NLPD values is challenging, and the inclusion of MAE values in the tables would facilitate a better understanding of the predictions. A qualitative analysis with actual examples would help illustrate the significance of drastic reductions in NLL/NLPD.
- Section 4 is well-written and effectively communicates the results in an intuitive manner.

Overall, the paper is well-liked for highlighting the problematic use of point estimates in quality estimation, a challenging task where additional information such as confidence is crucial. Although the submission does not advance the state-of-the-art or provide significant novelty in terms of modeling, its research questions and objectives are clearly stated and effectively executed.

Minor issues include:

- In Section 4, "over and underestimates" should be replaced with "over- and underestimates."
- The caption for Figure 1 incorrectly states that the lines are blue and red, when they are actually blue and green.
- If a specific toolkit was used for Gaussian process modeling, it would be beneficial to include a reference to it in the final paper.
