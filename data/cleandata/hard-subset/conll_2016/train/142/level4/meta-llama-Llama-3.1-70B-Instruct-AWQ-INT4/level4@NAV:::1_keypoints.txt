- Paper investigates using Gaussian processes to predict post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Well-organized with a clear introduction that highlights limits of point estimates for real-world QE.
- Notable discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators.
- Detailed, justified exposition of Gaussian processes, kernel selection, and warping for readers unfamiliar with them.
- Research questions are well-articulated and thought-provoking.
- Justification needed for predicting post-editing rates instead of commonly used HTER/TER.
- Section 3.2 first paragraph is unclear about trends for fr-en and en-de versus en-es; warped GPs show consistent NLL/NLPD decreases across datasets.
- Recommend including a point-estimate baseline from prior work in result tables as a benchmark.
- NLL and NLPD interpretation is difficult; adding MAE values would aid understanding.
- Suggest providing qualitative examples to illustrate the practical impact of large NLL/NLPD reductions.
- Section 4 is well-written and communicates results intuitively.
- Overall: valuable emphasis on uncertainty over point estimates, clear execution but limited novelty and no state-of-the-art advance.
- Minor issues: replace "over and underestimates" with "over- and underestimates."
- Minor issues: Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (blue and red vs. blue and green).
- Minor issues: cite the Gaussian process toolkit used if one was employed.
