The paper explores the use of probabilistic models (Gaussian processes) to regress on the target variable of post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of machine translation (MT) output.

**Summary of the paper and main contributions:**

The authors investigate probabilistic methods for quality estimation that can provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates. They propose the use of Gaussian Processes (GPs) and evaluate them in terms of their full posterior predictive distributions. The main contributions of this work are: (1) the application of GPs for quality estimation, (2) the evaluation of these models using the Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) metric, which captures information on the whole predictive distribution, and (3) the demonstration of how information in the predictive distribution can be used in asymmetric loss scenarios.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper highlights the problem of relying solely on point estimates in quality estimation and proposes a solution using probabilistic models.
2. The authors provide a clear introduction to Gaussian Processes and their application to quality estimation.
3. The paper explores asymmetric risk scenarios and demonstrates how well-calibrated predictive distributions can be beneficial in these settings.
4. The writing is generally clear, and the results are well-presented.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER is not justified, and it would have been nice to include a comparison with other point estimate models.
2. The interpretation of NLPD values is not straightforward, and a qualitative analysis with actual examples would have been helpful.
3. The paper does not advance the state-of-the-art in terms of modeling, as Gaussian Processes have been used before in quality estimation.

**Questions to authors:**

1. Can you justify the choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of interpreting NLPD values, and can you provide a qualitative analysis with actual examples?
3. Can you compare the performance of your proposed models with other point estimate models from existing work?

**Minor problems:**

1. Section 4: "over and underestimates" should be "over- and underestimates".
2. Figure 1 caption: Lines are actually blue and green, not blue and red as stated in the caption.
3. If a certain toolkit was used for GP modeling, it would be great to refer to this in the final paper.
