The paper explores the application of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian processes, to predict the target variable of post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of machine translation (MT) output. 

The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction that effectively highlights the limitations of point estimates in quality estimation for real-world applications. The description of different asymmetric risk scenarios and their corresponding estimators is particularly noteworthy. 

While the paper dedicates considerable space to explaining Gaussian processes, this effort is justified by the need to introduce these concepts to readers who may be unfamiliar with them. The Gaussian process approach, including the choices of kernels and the use of warping, is clearly explained and easy to follow. The research questions addressed in the paper are interesting and well-phrased.

However, I have some questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, specifically in the context of Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:

- The choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER or TER, a common metric in quality estimation research, could be better justified. It would be beneficial to explain why post-editing rates were preferred.
- In Section 3.2, the first paragraph is unclear. The trend observed for fr-en and en-de, but not for en-es, is not explicitly stated. Furthermore, the drastic decrease in NLL and NLPD with warped GPs is consistent across all three datasets.
- Although the paper explicitly states that it does not aim to advance the state-of-the-art, given the use of standard 17 baseline features, it would be helpful to include results from another point estimate model from existing work in the tables. This would provide a better understanding of the overall quality of the models.
- Related to this, the interpretation of NLL and NLPD values can be challenging, making it tempting to rely on MAE values to gauge the difference in predictions. Since the paper argues against this approach, it would be beneficial to provide a qualitative analysis with actual examples to illustrate the significance of drastic reductions in NLL/NLPD.
- Section 4 is well-written and effectively explains the results in an intuitive manner.

Overall, I appreciate the paper's focus on the limitations of point estimates in quality estimation. The research questions and goals are clearly stated and well-executed. Although the submission does not advance the state-of-the-art or provide significant novelty in terms of modeling, as Gaussian processes have been used before, it highlights an important issue and demonstrates the value of confidence information in quality estimation.

Minor issues:

- In Section 4, "over and underestimates" should be replaced with "over- and underestimates."
- The caption for Figure 1 incorrectly states that the lines are blue and red, when they are actually blue and green.
- If a specific toolkit was used for Gaussian process modeling, it would be helpful to acknowledge this in the final paper.
