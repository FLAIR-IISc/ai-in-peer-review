- Applies Gaussian processes to predict post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear introduction highlighting limits of point estimates in real-world QE.  
- Strong description of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators.  
- Lengthy but justified GP background; kernels and warping choices are clearly explained.  
- Research questions are interesting and well-phrased.  
- Needs better justification for choosing post-editing rates over common metrics like HTER/TER.  
- Section 3.2 unclear: trends for fr-en and en-de vs en-es not explicitly stated; warped GPs consistently reduce NLL/NLPD across datasets.  
- Recommend adding a point-estimate baseline/model from prior work alongside the 17 standard features for comparison.  
- NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret—suggest qualitative examples to illustrate the practical significance of large NLL/NLPD reductions instead of relying on MAE.  
- Section 4 is well-written and explains results intuitively.  
- Does not advance state-of-the-art or modeling novelty (GPs are known), but effectively demonstrates the value of uncertainty/confidence information.  
- Minor wording: use "over- and underestimates" instead of "over and underestimates."  
- Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (says blue/red but are blue/green).  
- If a GP toolkit was used, acknowledge it in the final paper.
