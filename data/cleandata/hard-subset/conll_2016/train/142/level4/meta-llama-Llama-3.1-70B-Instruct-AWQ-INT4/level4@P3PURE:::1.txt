The paper explores the application of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian processes, to regress on the target variable of post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of machine translation (MT) output. The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction that effectively highlights the problem of point estimates in quality estimation (QE) in real-world applications. The description of different asymmetric risk scenarios and their corresponding estimators is particularly noteworthy.

For readers familiar with Gaussian processes (GPs), the paper devotes sufficient space to explaining these concepts, which is a worthwhile effort to introduce these ideas to the reader. The GP approach, including the choice of kernels and the use of warping, is explained clearly and is easy to follow. The research questions addressed in this paper are interesting and well-phrased.

However, I have some questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, specifically in the context of Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:

- The choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER as the target variable is not explicitly justified. Since TER is a common value to predict in QE research, it would be beneficial to provide a clear explanation for this choice.
- In Section 3.2, the first paragraph is unclear. The trend observed for fr-en and en-de, but not for en-es, is not well-defined. Furthermore, the statement that NLL and NLPD "drastically" decrease with warped GPs for all three datasets is not entirely accurate.
- Although the paper explicitly states that it does not aim to advance the state-of-the-art (given the use of only the standard 17 baseline features), it would be beneficial to include another point estimate model from existing work in the result tables. This would provide a better understanding of the overall quality of the models.
- Related to this point, the interpretation of NLL and NLPD values is challenging. To provide a clearer understanding, it would be helpful to include a qualitative analysis with actual examples to illustrate the significance of the reduction in NLL/NLPD values.
- Section 4 is well-written and effectively explains the results in an intuitive manner.

Overall, I appreciate the paper's contribution to highlighting the problematic use of point estimates in QE. The submission clearly states its research questions and goals, which are nicely executed. Although the paper does not advance the state-of-the-art or provide significant novelty in terms of modeling (since GPs have been used before), it addresses an important issue in QE.

Minor issues:

- In Section 4, "over and underestimates" should be replaced with "over- and underestimates."
- The caption for Figure 1 incorrectly states that the lines are blue and red, when in fact they are blue and green.
- If a specific toolkit was used for GP modeling, it would be beneficial to include a reference to this in the final paper.
