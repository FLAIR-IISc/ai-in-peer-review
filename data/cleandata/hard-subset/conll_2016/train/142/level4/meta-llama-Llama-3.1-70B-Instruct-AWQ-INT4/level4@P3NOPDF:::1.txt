The paper explores the use of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian processes, to regress on the target variable of post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of machine translation (MT) output. The paper is well-structured, with a clear introduction that highlights the problem of point estimates in quality estimation (QE) in real-world applications. The description of the different asymmetric risk scenarios and their corresponding estimators is particularly noteworthy.

The authors dedicate sufficient space to explaining Gaussian processes (GPs), which may be beneficial for readers unfamiliar with these concepts. The GP approach, including the choices for kernels and the use of warping, is explained clearly and is easy to follow. The research questions that the paper aims to answer are interesting and well-phrased.

However, I have some questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, particularly with respect to Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:

1. The choice of post-editing rates over prediction (H)TER or translation edit rate (TER) as the target variable warrants justification. TER is a common metric in QE research, and it would be beneficial to explain why post-editing rates were preferred.

2. The first paragraph of Section 3.2 is unclear. The authors mention a trend observed for fr-en and en-de datasets that is not present in the en-es dataset. However, the text states that the negative log-likelihood (NLL) and negative log predictive density (NLPD) "drastically" decrease with warped GPs for all three datasets, which seems contradictory.

3. Although the authors acknowledge that they do not aim to advance the state-of-the-art, it would be beneficial to include a comparison with another point estimate model from existing work in the result tables. This would provide a better understanding of the overall quality of the models.

4. The interpretation of NLL and NLPD values can be challenging. To facilitate a better understanding, it would be helpful to provide a qualitative analysis with actual examples to illustrate the significance of the reductions in NLL and NLPD.

Section 4 is well-written and effectively explains the results in an intuitive manner. Overall, I appreciate the paper's focus on the problematic use of point estimates in QE, highlighting the importance of additional information such as confidence in this context.

While the submission does not advance the state-of-the-art or provide significant novelty in terms of modeling (since GPs have been used before), its research questions and goals are clearly stated and nicely executed.

Minor issues:

- In Section 4, "over and underestimates" should be replaced with "over- and underestimates".
- The caption for Figure 1 incorrectly states that the lines are blue and red, whereas they are actually blue and green.
- If a specific toolkit was used for GP modeling, it would be beneficial to reference it in the final paper.
