- Paper studies probabilistic QE using Gaussian Processes to produce calibrated uncertainty estimates alongside point predictions.  
- Main contribution: proposes Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a superior metric to capture predictive uncertainty vs. standard point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson’s r).  
- Secondary contribution: shows warping functions (especially tanh-based) and Matérn kernels improve uncertainty modeling, particularly for noisy QE data.  
- Tertiary contribution: demonstrates practical utility in asymmetric risk scenarios where uncertainty-aware predictions outperform point estimates.  

- Strength: Strong motivation for uncertainty in real-world QE workflows; asymmetric risk scenarios concretely demonstrate decision-making benefits.  
- Strength: Effectively argues NLPD is necessary to distinguish models with identical point estimates but differing uncertainty (over/underconfidence).  
- Strength: Clear empirical validation that warped GPs (tanh) improve NLPD across fr-en and en-de; Matérn kernels help for some language pairs; insightful analysis of NLL overfitting on small datasets.  

- Weakness: Unjustified choice of normalized post-editing rates rather than standard QE targets like (H)TER, weakening relevance to prior work.  
- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative interpretation of NLPD improvements—no examples showing what a 0.2–0.5 NLPD reduction means in practice.  
- Weakness: No comparison to strong point-estimate baselines (e.g., SVR from WMT), making it hard to contextualize the impact of uncertainty modeling.  

- Question: Why use post-editing rates (normalized by sentence length) instead of (H)TER, and how would results differ with TER?  
- Question: Can you provide qualitative examples linking specific NLPD reductions to tangible decision benefits in asymmetric-risk scenarios?  
- Question: How do warping functions (tanh vs. log) interact with language pairs—why does log outperform tanh on small en-es while tanh wins on larger datasets?  

- Minor: Hyphenate "over- and underestimates."  
- Minor: Fix Figure 1 caption color reference ("red" → "green").  
- Minor: Specify the GP library/toolkit used (e.g., GPy, GPflow) for reproducibility.
