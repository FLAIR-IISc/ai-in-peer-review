- Strong, well-crafted introduction that clearly motivates asymmetric risk scenarios and the need for probabilistic QE beyond point estimates.  
- Clear, accessible exposition of GP fundamentals, kernel choices, and warping techniques that benefits readers unfamiliar with the methods.  
- Compelling and precisely formulated research questions focused on probabilistic regression for QE.  
- Primary contribution: advocating probabilistic predictions (GPs) for risk-sensitive QE is timely and important.  
- Need explicit justification for choosing post-editing rates over standard QE metrics like (H)TER to avoid appearing arbitrary.  
- Section 3.2 is confusing: reported dataset-specific trends conflict with the claim that NLL/NLPD "drastically" decrease for all datasets; authors must clarify and reconcile this.  
- Add a concise table or figure to clearly illustrate dataset-specific patterns and NLL/NLPD comparisons.  
- Provide a representative point-estimate baseline (e.g., strong linear regression or SVR) to contextualize GP performance and show practical gains beyond theory.  
- Interpret NLL/NLPD values with benchmarks or qualitative examples (e.g., example predictions showing meaningful confidence adjustments) so numerical drops are tangible.  
- Section 4 clearly translates technical results into practical uncertainty insights and strengthens the paper’s core thesis.  
- Minor corrections: change "over and underestimates" → "over- and underestimates", fix Figure 1 caption "blue and red" → "blue and green".  
- For reproducibility, state the GP library/toolkit used (e.g., which GP implementation) in the methodology.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written paper addressing a vital methodological issue; not novel in model architecture but makes a convincing case for moving beyond point estimates — recommend acceptance.
