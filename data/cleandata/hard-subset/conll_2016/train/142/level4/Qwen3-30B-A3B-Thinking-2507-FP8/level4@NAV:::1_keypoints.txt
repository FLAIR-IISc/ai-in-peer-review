- Paper applies Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Introduction clearly identifies limitations of point estimates and discusses asymmetric risk scenarios.  
- Thorough, justified explanation of GPs, kernel choices, and warping technique; methodology is clear and accessible.  
- Research questions are compelling and precisely articulated.  
- Justify using post-editing rates instead of standard HTER as the QE target.  
- Clarify Section 3.2: the claimed trend for fr-en and en-de being absent in en-es is not evident given NLL/NLPD reductions across all datasets.  
- Although not aiming for SOTA, include a prior point-estimate baseline in results tables for context.  
- Since the paper argues against relying on MAE, provide qualitative examples to interpret what a “drastic” reduction in NLL/NLPD means in practice.  
- Section 4 is exceptionally well-written and results are clearly conveyed.  
- Paper offers a valuable critical perspective on point-estimate pitfalls, but does not advance SOTA or introduce major modeling novelty.  
- Minor edits: change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”  
- Minor correction: Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (should be blue and green, not blue and red).  
- If a specific GP toolkit was used, cite it in the final paper.
