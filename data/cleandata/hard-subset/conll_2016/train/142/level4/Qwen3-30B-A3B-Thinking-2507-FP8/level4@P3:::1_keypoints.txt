- Paper proposes using probabilistic models (Gaussian Processes) for QE, adding Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs to model noise and strictly positive scores.  
- Main contributions: argue NLPD is a better metric for uncertainty, show uncertainty improves asymmetric-risk decisions, and provide evidence Warped GPs+Matèrn improve calibration.  
- Strength: Strong, practical motivation that point estimates are insufficient for real-world translation workflows and filtering/post-editing decisions.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical demonstration that NLPD distinguishes uncertainty profiles while MAE/Pearson do not (e.g., 15–20% NLPD gains).  
- Strength: Shows probabilistic uncertainty yields better decisions under asymmetric loss, with consistent AL loss improvements (e.g., 5–10%).  
- Weakness: No justification for using normalized post-editing rates instead of standard QE metrics (TER/BLEU), reducing comparability to prior work.  
- Weakness: Explanations of dataset-specific NLL vs NLPD trends are unclear and somewhat contradictory, especially for the small en-es dataset.  
- Weakness: Lacks strong point-estimate baselines (e.g., SVR from WMT) in result tables, making practical significance hard to judge.  
- Question: Why were post-editing rates chosen over standard QE metrics, and how does that affect generalizability?  
- Question: Please clarify why NLL is unreliable on en-es (n=858) and how that influences the interpretation of NLPD across datasets.  
- Question: Can the authors provide qualitative examples showing how Warped GPs’ uncertainty improves filtering/decision-making in asymmetric-risk scenarios?
