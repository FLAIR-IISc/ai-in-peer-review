- Convincing argument that point-estimate metrics are inadequate for QE decision-making, supported by multi-dataset analysis.
- Clear, well-executed demonstration of using predictive uncertainty in asymmetric-risk scenarios showing tangible benefits.
- Sound evaluation methodology that uses NLPD as the primary metric, aligning with probabilistic modeling best practices.
- Accessible and thorough explanation of GP variants and their relevance to QE for a broad NLP audience.

- Insufficient justification for using post-editing rates as the target variable instead of standard QE metrics like (H)TER.
- Confusing presentation of results in Section 3.2, especially dataset-specific NLL/NLPD trends, which weakens the NLPD superiority claim.
- NLPD values lack context because there is no comparison against established point-estimate models.
- No qualitative examples illustrating what the reported "drastic" NLPD improvements mean in practice.

- Please justify the choice of post-editing rates over (H)TER and explain generalization to other QE tasks.
- Clarify dataset-specific NLL/NLPD trends (notably the en–es anomaly) and why they matter for the paper’s main argument.
- Provide qualitative examples showing how model uncertainty would change decision-making in real translation workflows.
- Include a point-estimate performance comparison against at least one strong recent QE baseline to contextualize results.
