- Clear, well-structured paper framing limitations of point-estimate approaches for real-world QE.
- Strong discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for estimator choice.
- Good exposition of Gaussian processes for readers unfamiliar with GPs.
- GP methodology, kernel selection, and warping techniques are clearly and logically presented.
- Research questions are compelling, well-articulated, and address a meaningful QE gap.
- Results/Discussion require clarification about the choice of post-editing rates versus standard metrics like (H)TER.
- Section 3.2 contains a contradictory description of NLL/NLPD trends across datasets and needs resolution.
- Paper lacks a representative point-estimate baseline (e.g., recent SOTA) to contextualize performance.
- NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret; provide qualitative examples to show what reductions mean in practice.
- Section 4 is exceptionally well-written with intuitive explanations of uncertainty interpretation.
- Overall contribution is valuable for advocating nuanced uncertainty in QE despite not advancing GP SOTA.
- Minor corrections: fix "over and underestimates" → "over- and underestimates"; correct Figure 1 caption color; state the GP toolkit used for reproducibility.
