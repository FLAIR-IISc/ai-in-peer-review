- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to provide calibrated uncertainty estimates for Machine Translation Quality Estimation (QE) and argues uncertainty is important for real-world translation workflows.  
- Proposes evaluating QE with distribution-aware metrics (NLPD) instead of relying solely on point-estimate metrics (e.g., MAE).  
- Demonstrates applying uncertainty estimates to asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic/optimistic) in translation workflows.  
- Introduces and evaluates GP extensions (Matèrn kernels, warped GPs) tailored to QE data characteristics.  

- Strength: Clearly motivates limitations of point-estimate QE and the practical value of uncertainty, with well-motivated asymmetric risk use-cases.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation using NLPD shows uncertainty differences that point metrics miss; warped GPs perform well across datasets.  
- Strength: Presents accessible explanations of GP concepts and a well-developed warped GP framework.  
- Strength: Application to asymmetric risk scenarios is theoretically sound and practically relevant.  

- Weakness: Unjustified choice of post-editing rate as target instead of standard QE metric (H)TER, reducing relevance without explanation.  
- Weakness: Results in Section 3.2 are confusing—need clarification why en-es behaves differently from fr-en and en-de despite reported decreases in NLL/NLPD.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to strong point-estimate baselines (e.g., SVR) beyond the 17-feature baseline, making model quality hard to contextualize.  
- Weakness: NLPD/NLL values are uncontextualized; readers need qualitative examples to interpret practical significance relative to MAE.  

- Question: Why choose post-editing rate over (H)TER, and how would results change if (H)TER were used?  
- Question: Please clarify the discrepancy in Section 3.2 regarding trends for en-es versus other datasets and explain dataset-size effects.  
- Question: Can you provide qualitative examples that illustrate what a "drastic reduction" in NLPD/NLL means for prediction quality?  

- Minor: Hyphenate "over- and underestimates" in Section 4.  
- Minor: Figure 1 caption incorrectly labels line colors (should be blue and green).  
- Minor: If a GP toolkit was used, cite the specific implementation in the final paper.  

- Overall: Well-executed paper that highlights the importance of uncertainty estimation in QE and appropriate evaluation metrics, but needs target-metric justification, clearer dataset-result explanations, stronger baseline comparisons, and contextualization of probabilistic metrics.
