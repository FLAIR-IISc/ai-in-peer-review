- Investigates using Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Well-structured paper with a clear introduction highlighting limitations of point estimates for real-world QE.  
- Strong treatment of asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for estimator selection.  
- Justified, accessible introduction to Gaussian processes for non-expert readers.  
- GP approach, kernel choices, and use of warping are clearly and coherently explained.  
- Research questions are well-defined and compelling.  
- Clarify choice of post-editing rates over the standard TER target used in QE research.  
- Section 3.2’s first paragraph is unclear: the claimed differing trend for fr-en and en-de versus en-es is not evident given consistent NLL/NLPD decreases with warped GPs.  
- Include a point-estimate baseline from prior work in results tables to contextualize performance.  
- NLL and NLPD are hard to interpret; relying on MAE for intuition undermines the paper’s argument—provide qualitative examples showing the practical impact of NLL/NLPD reductions.  
- Section 4 is exceptionally well-written and intuitively explains the results.  
- Paper makes a meaningful contribution by highlighting pitfalls of point estimates in QE, though it does not advance state-of-the-art or introduce novel modeling.  
- Minor issues: fix "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates"; correct Figure 1 caption (line colors); cite any specific GP toolkit used.
