- Studies Gaussian processes for probabilistic regression of post-editing time/rate in QE; well-structured intro highlighting limitations of point-estimates and asymmetric risk.  
- Useful background on GPs for non-expert readers; methodology, kernel choices, and warping techniques are clearly described and research questions well-motivated.  
- Major omission: no justification for choosing post-editing rates over standard QE metrics like (H)TER — authors should explain this choice.  
- Section 3.2 is unclear/contradictory: reported dataset-specific trends conflict with the claim that NLL/NLPD “drastically” decrease across all datasets with warped GPs.  
- Lack of context: include a representative point-estimate baseline from prior work (even if not SOTA) to help interpret GP performance.  
- Interpretability of uncertainty gains is abstract: quantify or give qualitative examples (e.g., prediction intervals for specific MT outputs) to show what NLL/NLPD reductions mean in practice.  
- Section 4 is exceptionally well-written and provides intuitive explanations of results.  
- Contribution is valuable for emphasizing uncertainty in QE, though the GP modeling itself is not novel and the work does not advance SOTA.  
- Minor issues: wording ("over and underestimates" → "over- and underestimates"), Figure 1 caption color error, and cite any GP toolkit used (e.g., GPy, GPflow).
