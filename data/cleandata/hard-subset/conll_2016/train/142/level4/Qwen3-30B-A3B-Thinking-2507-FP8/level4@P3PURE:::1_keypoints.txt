- Uses Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Well-structured paper with a clear introduction highlighting limitations of point estimates and asymmetric risk scenarios.  
- Provides accessible, detailed explanations of Gaussian processes for broader readership.  
- GP methodology (kernel choices and warping) is clearly and coherently explained; research questions are interesting and well-formulated.  
- Question: paper must justify choosing post-editing rates over the common QE target HTER/TER.  
- Issue: Section 3.2 is unclear about observed trends for fr-en and en-de versus en-es; contradictory statements about NLL/NLPD reductions with warped GPs.  
- Suggestion: include an additional point-estimate baseline from prior work in result tables to contextualize model quality.  
- Concern: NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret; provide qualitative examples to illustrate meaningful reductions since MAE dominates reader interpretation.  
- Section 4 is exceptionally well written and intuitively explains the results.  
- Contribution: valuable emphasis on pitfalls of point estimates and the importance of uncertainty, though not SOTA or highly novel as GPs have prior use.  
- Minor issues: fix hyphenation to "over- and underestimates," correct Figure 1 caption (line colors), and cite the GP toolkit if used.
