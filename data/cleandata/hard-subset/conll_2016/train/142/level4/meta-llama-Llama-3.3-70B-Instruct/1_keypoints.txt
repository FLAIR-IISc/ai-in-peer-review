- Study applies Gaussian processes to predict post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Well-organized manuscript with a clear introduction highlighting limits of point estimates in real-world quality estimation.  
- Notable discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators.  
- Thorough (though somewhat lengthy) explanation of Gaussian processes useful for non-expert readers.  
- GP approach, kernel selection, and warping are clearly explained and easy to follow.  
- Research questions are intriguing and well-formulated.  
- Choice of post-editing rates over predicting HTER needs justification given HTER's common use.  
- Paragraph in Section 3.2 is unclear about observed trends for fr-en/en-de versus en-es despite consistent NLL/NLPD decreases.  
- Should include a point-estimate baseline (from existing research) in result tables to contextualize model quality using the standard 17 features.  
- NLL and NLPD are hard to interpret; the paper should include qualitative examples to illustrate the practical significance of reductions in these metrics.  
- Section 4 is well-written and provides an intuitive explanation of results.  
- Paper is valuable for emphasizing uncertainty and confidence intervals, though it does not advance state-of-the-art or introduce major modeling novelty.  
- Minor edits: change "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates"; fix Figure 1 caption color description (blue and green); cite any GP toolkit used.
