- Topic: Uses Gaussian Processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Strength: Well structured paper with a clear introduction framing the problem of relying on QE point estimates.
- Strength: Good discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for different estimators.
- Strength: GP background is thorough (though possibly long for GP-experts) and GP methodology (kernels, output warping) is clearly explained.
- Strength: Research questions are interesting, well posed, and Section 4 explains results intuitively.
- Concern: Need justification for choosing post-editing rates over the common QE target TER/HTER.
- Concern: Section 3.2 first paragraph is unclear—author should clarify the specific trends for fr–en and en–de versus en–es.
- Observation: Warped GPs drastically decrease NLL/NLPD across datasets, but this claim needs clearer presentation.
- Suggestion: Include at least one point-estimate model from prior work in results tables to contextualize model quality.
- Concern: NLL and NLPD are hard to interpret—readers may default to MAE; the paper should provide guidance on what constitutes a “drastic” NLL/NLPD reduction, ideally via qualitative examples.
- Overall assessment: Paper highlights the problem of relying on point estimates in QE and is well executed, but does not advance state-of-the-art and offers limited modeling novelty.
- Minor issue: Wording—“over and underestimates” should be “over- and underestimates.”
- Minor issue: Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (says blue and red, but lines are blue and green).
- Minor issue: If a specific GP toolkit was used, cite it in the final paper.
