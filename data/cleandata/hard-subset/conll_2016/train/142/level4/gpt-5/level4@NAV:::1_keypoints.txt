- Uses Gaussian Processes to regress post-editing time/rates for QE of MT output.  
- Well organized introduction that clearly frames limitations of QE point estimates and motivates asymmetric-risk estimators.  
- GP background is somewhat extensive but justified for readers less familiar with GPs.  
- GP methodology (kernels, warping) is clearly presented; research questions are interesting and well formulated.  
- Question: justify choice of post-editing rate over the commonly used (H)TER as the target variable.  
- Clarify Section 3.2: specify the observed trend for fr–en and en–de that differs from en–es, since NLL/NLPD seem to decrease with warped GPs across all datasets.  
- Suggest including a prior point-estimate model in result tables to contextualize overall model quality.  
- NLL/NLPD are hard to interpret; recommend calibrating what substantial reductions mean (e.g., qualitative examples) instead of relying on MAE.  
- Section 4 is very well written and provides intuitive explanations of results.  
- Overall: paper usefully highlights issues with point estimates and importance of uncertainty, but offers limited novelty and does not advance the state of the art; objectives are clearly executed.  
- Minor fixes: correct “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates,” fix Figure 1 caption color labels, and cite any GP toolkit used.
