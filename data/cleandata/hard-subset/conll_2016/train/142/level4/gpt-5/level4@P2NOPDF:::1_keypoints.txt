- Paper studies Gaussian process models to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Well organized with a clear introduction that motivates limitations of point estimates and asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Exposition on GPs is accessible for broad audiences but may be lengthy for GP-experienced readers.
- Modeling choices (kernel selection, target warping) are clearly explained and easy to follow.
- Research questions are well posed and interesting.
- Question: justify focusing on post-editing rates instead of the commonly used (H)TER for comparability with prior work.
- Section 3.2 is unclear: the claimed trend differences for fr–en and en–de versus en–es need clarification given similar NLL/NLPD reductions.
- Suggest including at least one strong point-estimate baseline from prior work in results tables to contextualize predictive quality.
- NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret alone—add qualitative examples showing how reductions affect decisions under asymmetric costs.
- Section 4 is very well written and provides an intuitive explanation of results.
- Overall positive: convincingly highlights pitfalls of relying on point estimates; limited modeling novelty and not SOTA, but goals are clear and executed thoughtfully.
- Minor issues: wording should be “over- and underestimates”; Figure 1 caption has incorrect color description; cite the GP toolkit if used.
