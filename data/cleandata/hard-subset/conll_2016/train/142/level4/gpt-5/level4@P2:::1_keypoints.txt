- Paper studies probabilistic QE with Gaussian Processes, focusing on calibrated uncertainty rather than only point predictions.  
- Evaluates posterior predictive distributions using NLPD and examines kernel and warping choices.  
- Shows how posterior predictive information supports decision-making under asymmetric risk via Bayes estimators.

- Demonstrates NLPD uncovers differences among QE models not seen by MAE or Pearson’s r, underscoring the need for calibrated uncertainty.  
- Applies and evaluates Warped GPs and Matérn kernels, reporting consistent NLL/NLPD gains and analyzing warping effects on uncertainty.  
- Presents a practical asymmetric-risk framework using Bayes estimators (quantiles for asymmetric linear loss; closed-form for linex) to adapt predictions without retraining.

- Strong motivation for uncertainty-aware QE with clear links to production workflows (post-editing, gisting).  
- Clear and accessible exposition of GPs, Matérn kernels, Warped GPs, and asymmetric loss decision rules.  
- Empirical evidence that warping substantially improves NLL/NLPD across datasets.  
- Practical relevance shown through asymmetric risk analysis connecting predictive uncertainty to operational decisions.

- Unclear rationale for predicting post-editing rates instead of standard (H)TER, limiting comparability with prior QE work.  
- Ambiguous interpretation in Sec. 3.2 about en‑es trends versus fr‑en/en‑de; claims of overfitting need clearer evidence.  
- Missing strong point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR or recent neural QE) to contextualize absolute performance.  
- NLL/NLPD improvements lack qualitative interpretation (predictive intervals, reliability plots, case studies) to judge practical impact.  
- Modeling novelty is modest since GPs and Warped GPs have been used in QE before; novelty is mainly in evaluation emphasis.

- Why choose post-editing rates over (H)TER, and how does this choice affect generality and comparability?  
- In Sec. 3.2, which specific trend differs for en‑es, and can you provide train/test NLL gaps, calibration plots, or variance analysis to support claims of overfitting?  
- Please provide qualitative analyses for NLPD improvements (coverage, reliability diagrams, illustrative sentence-level examples).  
- Can you add a strong point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR with RBF or a published QE system) to situate GP performance?  
- For the pessimistic linex setting, can you empirically show how predictive variance (or truncation at zero) affects linex loss and calibration/sharpness trade-offs?

- Use “over- and underestimates” in Section 4.  
- Fix Figure 1 caption: line colors are blue and green, not blue and red.  
- Cite any GP toolkit used (GPML, GPy), and provide hyperparameter optimization, warping, and quadrature details for reproducibility.  
- Add guidance on practical scales of NLL/NLPD changes (typical ranges) to improve interpretability.
