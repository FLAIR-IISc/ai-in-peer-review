- Paper investigates Gaussian Process–based probabilistic methods to predict post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Well organized manuscript with a clear introduction motivating limitations of point estimates in QE workflows.
- Commendation for treatment of asymmetric risk scenarios and derivation of different optimal estimators.
- Substantial GP background material recaps known content but is justified for accessibility.
- GP setup, kernel choices, and output warping are explained clearly and are easy to follow.
- Research questions are interesting and articulated well.
- Question: justify choice of post-editing rates over the standard (H)TER target in QE.
- Section 3.2 is unclear—need precise statement of the trend observed for fr-en and en-de that purportedly does not appear for en-es.
- Observation: NLL/NLPD drop with warped GPs appears substantial for all three datasets; clarify the reported trend.
- Suggestion: include a representative point-estimate baseline from prior work to contextualize overall performance.
- Suggestion: NLL/NLPD are hard to interpret alone—add qualitative examples showing what large reductions in NLL/NLPD look like in practice.
- Section 4 is excellently written and offers intuitive explanations of results.
- Overall: paper usefully highlights pitfalls of relying on point estimates in QE, but does not advance state of the art and offers limited modeling novelty.
- Minor issues: wording “over and underestimates” → “over- and underestimates”; Figure 1 caption colors incorrect (blue & green, not blue & red); cite any GP toolkit used.
