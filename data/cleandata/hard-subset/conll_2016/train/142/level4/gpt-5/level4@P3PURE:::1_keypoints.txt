- Uses Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for QE; well-structured with a clear intro highlighting limitations of point estimates.  
- Strong discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and how they require different estimators.  
- GP background is somewhat long but justified for a broad audience; kernel choices and response warping are clearly explained.  
- Research questions are interesting and well posed.  
- Ask for justification of choosing post-editing rates over (H)TER, a common QE target.  
- Section 3.2 is unclear—need clarification on the claimed trend differences for fr-en and en-de versus en-es given NLL/NLPD decreases for all.  
- Recommend including a point-estimate baseline from prior work in results tables to contextualize model quality.  
- NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret; suggest a qualitative analysis or examples to show what drastic reductions mean relative to MAE.  
- Section 4 is well written and explains results intuitively.  
- Overall: paper is good and clearly articulates pitfalls of point estimates in QE, but offers limited novelty and does not advance state of the art.  
- Minor fixes: change “over and underestimates” → “over- and underestimates”; correct Figure 1 caption color labels; cite the GP toolkit if one was used.
