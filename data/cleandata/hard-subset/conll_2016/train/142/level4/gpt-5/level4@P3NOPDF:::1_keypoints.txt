- Clear and compelling motivation to model predictive uncertainty in QE for asymmetric risk decision-making.
- Well-explained GP modeling choices (kernels, warping) with accessible exposition and pedagogical value.
- Paper has a coherent narrative and well-posed research questions.
- Empirical results show meaningful NLL/NLPD improvements with warped GPs, indicating benefits for heteroscedasticity and non-Gaussianity.
- Must justify choice of post-editing time/rates over (H)TER and discuss transferability to (H)TER.
- Section 3.2 needs clarification on the referred trend and why en-es behaves differently despite NLL/NLPD drops.
- Provide dataset statistics (variance, skewness, noise characteristics) that explain observed differences across language pairs.
- Include at least one strong point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR or tree-based) to contextualize absolute predictive quality.
- Make NLL/NLPD more interpretable by adding qualitative examples showing predicted means, intervals, and realized outcomes.
- Add calibration analyses (empirical coverage at 50/80/95%) to assess reliability of uncertainty estimates.
- Consider reporting an additional scoring rule (e.g., CRPS) that is easier to interpret alongside NLL/NLPD.
- Emphasize the paper’s main contribution as principled risk framing and uncertainty treatment rather than novel modeling.
- Discuss operational scenarios favoring probabilistic QE (triage, dynamic pricing) and limitations (data scarcity, heavy tails).
- Provide an ablation on kernel and warping choices to show when warping helps most (e.g., skewed/heavy-tailed labels).
- Comment on computational cost and scalability (training/inference time, dataset sizes) for deployability concerns.
- Minor fixes: hyphenation in “over- and underestimates,” correct Figure 1 caption colors, and cite the GP toolkit/version used.
- Overall: well-written and practically relevant contribution that improves uncertainty estimates, though not SOTA; clarify points above to strengthen the paper.
