- Paper studies probabilistic QE with Gaussian Processes to predict post-editing time/rates and advocates evaluating full predictive distributions instead of point estimates.  
- Contributions: uncertainty-aware evaluation using NLPD, demonstration of asymmetric risk handling with Bayes estimators (AL and linex), and modeling guidance favoring Matérn kernels and Warped GPs.  
- Strength: clear motivation and accessible exposition of GPs, kernels, and warping.  
- Strength: strong, well-motivated treatment of asymmetric risk with clean derivations and intuitive empirical results.  
- Strength: sensible modeling choices and transparent experimental protocol (three language pairs, 10-fold CV, Bayes estimators without retraining).  
- Strength: empirical finding that Warped GPs substantially improve NLL/NLPD and useful discussion on linex preferring higher-variance models in pessimistic regimes.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—GP techniques and features are established and feature set is restricted to 17 baseline features.  
- Weakness: target choice of post-editing rates over (H)TER is unjustified, hurting comparability with prior QE work.  
- Weakness: Section 3.2 is unclear about dataset-specific trends (fr-en/en-de vs en-es) and evidence of overfitting on en-es.  
- Weakness: NLL/NLPD are hard to interpret in isolation; readers will default to MAE—paper needs qualitative examples showing practical impact of NLL/NLPD reductions.  
- Weakness: lack of a strong point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR) in results limits contextualization of gains.  
- Question: why choose post-editing rates instead of (H)TER—please justify and discuss generality/comparability.  
- Question: clarify the exact trend in Section 3.2 for fr-en and en-de versus en-es and provide evidence for overfitting on en-es.  
- Question: include a representative prior point-estimate baseline in result tables for context.  
- Question: which GP toolkit/implementation was used—please specify for reproducibility.  
- Minor: wording—use “over- and underestimates.”  
- Minor: Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (says blue and red; appears blue and green).  
- Overall: paper convincingly advocates uncertainty-aware QE and is well executed, but needs clearer justification of targets, better contextual baselines, and clarifications in Section 3.2 to strengthen the submission.
