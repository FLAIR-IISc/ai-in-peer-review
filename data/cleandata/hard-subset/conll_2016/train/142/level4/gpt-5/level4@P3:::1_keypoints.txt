- Summary: Argues QE should predict calibrated distributions; applies Gaussian Processes (Warped GPs, Matérn kernels), evaluates with NLPD, and uses posterior Bayes estimators for asymmetric losses.  
- Contribution: Shows uncertainty-aware QE reveals differences missed by point metrics and enables principled decisions under asymmetric risk.

Strengths
- Clear, practical motivation: convincingly argues point estimates are insufficient when downstream decisions depend on uncertainty.  
- Good model choices: Warped GPs and Matérn kernels are well motivated for positive targets and relaxed smoothness.  
- Proper evaluation: Uses NLPD/test likelihood to assess accuracy and calibration; shows MAE/Pearson r can miss uncertainty differences across language pairs.  
- Insightful asymmetric-risk analysis: Correctly derives/apply Bayes estimators for AL and linex losses and reports practically relevant findings.  
- Reproducible setup: Uses standard QuEst features, cross-validation, and sensible hyperparameter tuning.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: GP components (warping, Matérn) have precedents; lacks broader baselines to contextualize performance and uncertainty.  
- Unclear Section 3.2: Claims about NLL vs NLPD trends (especially en-es) are ambiguous and need explicit evidence (e.g., train vs test likelihoods).  
- NLPD interpretability: Lacks calibration diagnostics (PIT, interval coverage) and qualitative examples to make NLPD/NLL improvements actionable.  
- Target justification missing: No explanation for choosing post-editing rate over standard targets like (H)TER.  
- Incomplete asymmetric-loss analysis: Warped GPs' worse performance under pessimistic linex is hypothesized but not analyzed (variance comparisons, sensitivity to w, quantile baselines).

Questions for authors
- Why choose post-editing rate instead of (H)TER, and how would conclusions transfer?  
- Please clarify the NLL vs NLPD discrepancy on en-es and provide train vs test likelihoods if overfitting is claimed.  
- Can you add calibration diagnostics (interval coverage, PIT histograms) to support “well-calibrated” claims?  
- For asymmetric scenarios, can you report sensitivity across more w values and provide examples where posteriors change decisions vs medians?  
- Which GP implementation/toolkit was used, and are code/hyperparameters available for reproduction?

Minor comments
- Typo: “over and underestimates” → “over- and underestimates.”  
- Figure 1 caption: colors mismatch (blue/green vs blue/red).  
- State GP software library and any non-default settings.  
- Consider reporting 50%/90% predictive interval coverage alongside NLPD.  
- If possible, add a qualitative case study showing segments with similar MAE but different posterior spreads affecting AL/linex decisions.

Overall recommendation
- Well-written and timely work advocating uncertainty-aware QE; addressing clarifications, adding calibration diagnostics, and broader baselines would substantially strengthen the paper.
