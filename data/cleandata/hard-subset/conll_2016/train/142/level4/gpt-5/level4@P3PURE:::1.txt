This paper explores probabilistic models—specifically Gaussian processes (GPs)—to regress post-editing time/rates for quality estimation (QE) of MT output. It is well structured, with a clear introduction that highlights the limitations of point estimates in real-world QE applications. I especially appreciated the discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and how they entail different estimators. The paper devotes considerable space to reviewing GPs; while readers already familiar with the topic may find this lengthy, it is worthwhile to introduce these concepts to the broader audience. The GP approach, including kernel choices and response warping, is explained clearly and is easy to follow. Overall, the research questions are interesting and well posed.

Questions and suggestions about the Results and Discussion, particularly the Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:
- Why were post-editing rates chosen over predicting (H)TER? TER is a common target in QE research, and it would be helpful to justify this choice in the paper.
- Section 3.2: I found the first paragraph difficult to follow. What exactly is the trend you observe for fr-en and en-de that you do not observe for en-es? NLL and NLPD “drastically” decrease with warped GPs for all three datasets, so the claimed difference is unclear.
- The paper explicitly does not aim to advance the state of the art (given the use of only the standard 17 baseline features), but it would still be useful to include another point-estimate model from existing work in the results tables to contextualize the overall quality of the models.
- Relatedly, NLL and NLPD values are hard to interpret, so readers are tempted to look at MAE in the tables to gauge “how different the predictions are.” Since the core message is that this is not the right focus, it would be valuable to provide some notion of what a drastic reduction in NLL/NLPD is worth—for example, via a qualitative analysis with actual examples.

Section 4 is very nicely written and explains the results intuitively.

Overall assessment:
I like the paper, particularly its clear articulation of the pitfalls of relying on point estimates in QE—a difficult task where additional information such as confidence is arguably very important. The submission does not advance the state of the art and offers limited novelty in modeling (since GPs have been used before), but its research questions and goals are clearly stated and well executed.

Minor issues:
- Section 4: “over and underestimates” → “over- and underestimates”
- Figure 1 caption: the lines are blue and green, not blue and red as stated.
- If a particular toolkit was used for GP modeling, please include a reference in the final paper.
