- Paper studies probabilistic modeling (Gaussian processes) to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Strong, well-structured introduction that highlights limitations of point estimates and discusses asymmetric risk scenarios.  
- GP background may be long for experts but is justified; GP setup, kernel choices, and output warping are clearly explained.  
- Research questions are interesting and well posed.  
- Major question: justify choice of post-editing rates over (H)TER and discuss trade-offs relative to (H)TER.  
- Clarify Section 3.2: explicitly state which trends differ for fr–en and en–de versus en–es and explain the cause.  
- Suggest adding at least one strong point-estimate baseline from prior work to contextualize absolute model quality.  
- NLL/NLPD are hard to interpret alone; provide guidance on what constitutes a practically meaningful reduction.  
- Recommend a small qualitative analysis with concrete examples (predictions and uncertainty intervals before/after warping) showing impacts under asymmetric loss.  
- Section 4 is clear and explains results intuitively.  
- Overall positive: effectively highlights pitfalls of relying on point estimates; limited novelty and not SOTA but goals are clear and study well executed.  
- Minor fixes: correct “over and underestimates” → “over- and underestimates,” fix Figure 1 caption colors, and cite any GP toolkit used.
