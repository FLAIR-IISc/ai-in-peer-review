- Paper argues point-estimate QE evaluation is insufficient and advocates probabilistic models that provide calibrated uncertainty (focus on GPs and Warped GPs for positive noisy targets).
- Proposes evaluating full predictive distributions with metrics like NLPD and demonstrates practical utility in asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Empirical result: Warped GPs give better-calibrated uncertainty (NLPD) and improved decision-making under asymmetric loss across three datasets.

Contributions:
- Argues for probabilistic QE evaluation using distributional metrics (e.g., NLPD).
- Presents a framework to derive optimal estimators for various asymmetric loss functions from a single probabilistic model without retraining.
- Empirically compares GP variants and shows Warped GPs effectively model strictly positive, noisy targets like post-editing time.

Strengths:
- Addresses a practical, decision-focused problem by emphasizing uncertainty in QE.
- Sound methodology: justified use of GPs, systematic exploration of extensions, and principled adoption of NLPD.
- Novel, insightful application of predictive uncertainty to asymmetric risk and on-the-fly Bayes estimators.
- Clear, well-written presentation accessible to non-experts in probabilistic modeling.

Weaknesses:
- Lacks comparison to a strong non-probabilistic baseline (e.g., SVR) to contextualize point-prediction performance.
- NLPD improvements are not made tangible with qualitative examples showing when better uncertainty helps.
- Choice of post-editing time as target is not justified and limits discussion of generalization to targets like HTER.

Questions to authors:
- Please clarify the differing NLL vs NLPD trends on the small en‑es dataset and whether model rankings change.
- Would Warped GPs offer the same benefits for non-strictly-positive targets such as HTER?
- Can you add a standard SVR baseline to the tables for MAE and Pearson’s r for context?
