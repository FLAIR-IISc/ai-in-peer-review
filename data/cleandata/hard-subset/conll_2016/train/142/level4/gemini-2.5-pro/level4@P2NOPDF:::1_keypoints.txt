- Well-structured and clearly motivated paper highlighting the limits of point estimates and importance of modeling uncertainty in QE.  
- Convincing discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios showing estimator choice should depend on downstream task costs.  
- Clear technical exposition of Gaussian Processes, including kernels and input warping, making the paper self-contained.  
- Research questions are relevant and the qualitative discussion (Section 4) provides intuitive explanations of results.  
- Main contribution is demonstrating how to model and leverage uncertainty rather than improving predictive accuracy.  
- Justification of the target variable is weak: need rationale for using post-editing rates instead of the more common (H)TER.  
- Results clarity issue (Section 3.2): claimed trends across language pairs conflict with table results for NLL/NLPD and need clarification.  
- Lack of a standard point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR or feed-forward network) makes it hard to contextualize performance and MAE comparisons.  
- Improvements in NLL/NLPD are not intuitively interpretable; include qualitative examples of predictive distributions to show practical benefits.  
- Typo: “over and underestimates” should be hyphenated as “over- and underestimates.”  
- Figure 1 caption mismatch: caption says blue and red but lines appear blue and green.  
- Reproducibility: cite the specific software toolkit/library used for Gaussian Process modeling.
