- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to regress post-editing time and rates for MT Quality Estimation.  
- Well-organized with a lucid introduction that clearly frames the limitations of point estimates.  
- Insightful discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and their impact on estimator choice.  
- Extensive GP background may be familiar to experts but helps make the work accessible to a broader audience.  
- Methodology (GP implementation, kernel selection, warping) is explained with exceptional clarity and is easy to follow.  
- Research questions are compelling and well-articulated.  
- Question: justify predicting post-editing rates instead of the standard (H)TER target.  
- Question: clarify Section 3.2’s first paragraph about trends for fr-en, en-de, and en-es given that NLL/NLPD seem to decrease for all with warped GPs.  
- Suggestion: include a comparison with a standard point-estimate model from the literature to contextualize results.  
- Suggestion: provide qualitative examples to interpret the practical significance of NLL/NLPD reductions (so readers can see what a “drastic” reduction means).  
- Section 4 analysis is exceptionally well-written and intuitive.  
- Overall assessment: valuable paper highlighting problems with point estimates in QE; not novel modeling but clearly executed and well-motivated.  
- Minor corrections: change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”  
- Minor corrections: fix Figure 1 caption (refers to blue and red but figure shows blue and green).  
- Minor corrections: cite the specific software toolkit used for the Gaussian Process modeling.
