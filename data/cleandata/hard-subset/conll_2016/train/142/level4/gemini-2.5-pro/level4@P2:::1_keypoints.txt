- Main contribution: Advocates and demonstrates uncertainty-aware evaluation for QE, showing NLPD reveals differences invisible to MAE/Pearson.
- Main contribution: Shows probabilistic QE is useful in asymmetric risk scenarios, using Bayes risk estimators to make optimal decisions without retraining.
- Strength: Strong motivation and framing that point estimates are insufficient for real-world QE decision-making.
- Strength: Practical, intuitive application of asymmetric risk (pessimistic vs optimistic workflows) that demonstrates real-world utility.
- Strength: Clear, accessible presentation of GPs (Matern kernels, Warped GPs), helpful for non-experts.
- Strength: Experimental design supports core claims by comparing GP variants and using both point-estimate and distribution-aware metrics (NLPD).
- Weakness: Results lack interpretability/context — NLPD/NLL values need qualitative examples to illustrate practical differences.
- Weakness: No standard non-probabilistic baseline (e.g., SVR) included to contextualize GP point-estimate performance.
- Weakness: Confusing analysis in Section 3.2 about NLL vs NLPD trends (en-es vs fr-en/en-de); explanation and support are unclear.
- Weakness: Experimental choice of post-editing time as target is not justified; relevance to common metrics like HTER is not shown.
- Question: Please clarify the claimed NLL/NLPD trend differences in Section 3.2 and how they indicate overfitting on en-es.
- Question: Provide 1–2 qualitative examples where Warped GP gives more useful uncertainty-aware predictions than a standard GP.
- Question: Why was post-editing time chosen over HTER, and would the benefits of probabilistic modeling generalize to HTER prediction?
- Additional: Fix minor text/figure issues ("over- and underestimates" wording; figure color-caption mismatch).
- Additional: State which GP/software toolkit was used to aid reproducibility.
