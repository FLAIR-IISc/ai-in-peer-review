- Proposes using Gaussian Processes for QE to produce full predictive distributions (uncertainty) alongside quality predictions, evaluated with point and probabilistic metrics and tested in asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Contribution: Strongly motivates incorporating predictive uncertainty into QE and frames practical decision-making via asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Contribution: Systematically compares GP variants using NLPD to assess quality of predictive distributions, showing modeling choices affect uncertainty estimates beyond MAE.
- Strength: Excellent motivation and framing, clearly arguing the limitations of point estimates and the relevance of uncertainty for real-world QE decisions.
- Strength: Clear exposition that introduces GPs accessibly and presents experiments and results logically, especially the asymmetric risk discussion.
- Strength: Well-designed experiments focused on uncertainty evaluation using NLPD and asymmetric loss functions, aligned with the paper's goals.
- Weakness: Section 3.2’s interpretation is unclear — claimed differences in NLL/NLPD trends for en-es vs fr-en/en-de are not evident from Table 1.
- Weakness: Lacks comparative baselines (e.g., SVR) to contextualize GP performance on MAE and Pearson’s r, making trade-offs unclear.
- Weakness: Practical significance of NLPD improvements is not concrete; needs qualitative examples to make uncertainty benefits tangible.
- Question: Please clarify the specific NLL/NLPD trend differences reported in Section 3.2 for fr-en/en-de versus en-es.
- Question: Justify predicting post-editing time instead of (H)TER and comment whether probabilistic modeling benefits would generalize to HTER prediction.
- Additional comment: Minor wording/format issues (hyphenation "over- and underestimates", Figure 1 color mismatch) and request to state which GP software/library was used for reproducibility.
