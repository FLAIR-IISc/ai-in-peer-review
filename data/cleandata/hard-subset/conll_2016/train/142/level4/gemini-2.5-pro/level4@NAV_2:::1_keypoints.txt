- Paper studies probabilistic Gaussian Process models for MT Quality Estimation, arguing point estimates are insufficient and uncertainty should be evaluated with metrics like NLPD.  
- Evaluates multiple GP variants (kernels, Warped GPs) and finds warping improves predictive distributions.  
- Shows how predictive distributions can be used in asymmetric risk scenarios (e.g., pessimistic post-editing vs optimistic gisting) via Bayes risk estimators without retraining.

- Contribution: Advocates uncertainty-aware evaluation (NLPD) over standard point-estimate metrics and demonstrates its importance for decision-making.  
- Contribution: Presents a practical application of uncertainty estimates to adapt a single model to different user risk preferences.  
- Contribution: Provides a thorough empirical comparison of GP configurations, highlighting the benefits of Warped GPs.

- Strength: Strong motivation and clear framing of why uncertainty matters in real-world QE workflows.  
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated, and practical exploration of asymmetric risk scenarios that strengthens real-world applicability.  
- Strength: Clear, accessible methodology and well-structured presentation of GPs, kernels, and experiments.

- Weakness: Missing comparison to standard non-probabilistic baselines (e.g., SVR) to contextualize performance and trade-offs on metrics like MAE.  
- Weakness: NLPD improvements are hard to interpret in practice; paper lacks qualitative examples or prediction visualizations to illustrate impact.  
- Weakness: Analysis claiming overfitting based on NLL vs NLPD trends (Section 3.2, en-es) is unclear and needs clarification.

- Question: Please clarify the NLL vs NLPD trends in Section 3.2 that lead to the claim that NLL is unreliable and indicates overfitting on smaller datasets.  
- Question: Why choose post-editing time as the target variable rather than other QE labels like HTER—please justify.  
- Question: Can you include a standard SVR baseline with the same features to provide a useful point of reference for the community?
