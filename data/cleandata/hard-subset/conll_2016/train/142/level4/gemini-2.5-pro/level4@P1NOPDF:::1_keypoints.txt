- Paper investigates Gaussian Processes to predict post-editing time/effort for MT quality estimation.  
- Strong, clear introduction highlighting limitations of point estimates and importance of asymmetric risk scenarios.  
- Background on GPs is lengthy but makes the work accessible to non-experts.  
- GP modeling choices (kernels, warping) and research questions are clearly explained.  
- Choice of post-editing rates as the target variable is not justified; (H)TER is a more common QE target and rationale is needed.  
- Section 3.2’s discussion is confusing: authors claim trends absent for en-es, but tables show NLL/NLPD reductions with warped GPs for all language pairs—clarification requested.  
- Although not claiming SOTA, paper should include a standard point-estimate baseline (from prior work) to contextualize performance against the 17-feature setup.  
- Need better interpretability of NLL/NLPD: explain what “drastic” reductions mean practically and add qualitative examples linking improved metrics to more reliable predictions.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and well-executed study that emphasizes the need for prediction confidence despite limited modeling novelty.  
- Extrinsic evaluation (Section 4) is well-written and intuitively demonstrates practical benefits.  
- Minor issues: fix “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 caption incorrectly mentions red/blue lines while the figure shows green/blue.  
- Reproducibility request: cite the specific GP software toolkit used.
