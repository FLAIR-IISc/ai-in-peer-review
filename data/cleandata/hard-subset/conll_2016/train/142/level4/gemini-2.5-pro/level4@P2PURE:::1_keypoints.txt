- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to predict post-editing time/effort for machine translation quality estimation.  
- Well-structured with a clear introduction highlighting limitations of point estimates in QE applications.  
- Thorough and useful description of asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for estimator selection.  
- Detailed GP background may be familiar to some readers but improves accessibility.  
- Methodology (GP approach, kernel choices, warping) is clearly and carefully explained; research questions are well-phrased.  
- Choice of post-editing rates as prediction target needs justification versus the common (H)TER target.  
- Claim that a trend exists for fr-en and en-de but not en-es is unclear and inconsistent with reported NLL/NLPD reductions across all language pairs.  
- Recommend adding a comparison to a standard point-estimate baseline from prior work to contextualize performance.  
- NLL and NLPD are hard to interpret alone; suggest qualitative examples showing what reductions mean in practice to support claims.  
- Section 4 discussion is well-written and provides intuitive explanations of results.  
- Contribution lies in framing and analysis advocating evaluation shifts in QE, though it does not advance state-of-the-art modeling novelty.  
- Minor issues: fix "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates"; correct Figure 1 caption color description; cite any GP toolkit used.
