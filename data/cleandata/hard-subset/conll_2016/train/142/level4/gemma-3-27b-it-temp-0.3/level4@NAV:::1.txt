This paper investigates the application of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian Processes, to the task of predicting post-editing time/rates for quality estimation (QE) of machine translation output. 

The paper is well-organized, beginning with a clear introduction that effectively frames the limitations of point estimates in real-world QE applications. I particularly appreciated the discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and how these considerations motivate the choice of different estimators. While the paper dedicates considerable space to reviewing Gaussian Processes, which may be familiar to some readers, I believe this thoroughness is justified to ensure accessibility. 

The GP methodology, including kernel selection and the use of warping, is explained in a lucid and easily understandable manner. The research questions posed are both interesting and well-defined.

However, I have several questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, specifically concerning the intrinsic uncertainty evaluation:

- The choice to focus on post-editing rates rather than (H)TER warrants further justification. Given that (H)TER is a frequently predicted metric in QE research, explaining this decision would strengthen the paper.
- Section 3.2 contains a paragraph that I found difficult to interpret. Could the authors clarify the observed trend for fr-en and en-de, which appears to differ from that of en-es? The claim of a ‘drastic’ decrease in NLL and NLPD with warped GPs seems to hold across all three datasets.
- While the authors acknowledge that this work does not aim to achieve state-of-the-art performance (due to the use of a standard feature set), including a point estimate model from existing literature in the results tables would provide valuable context for assessing the overall quality of the proposed models.
- Relatedly, interpreting NLL and NLPD values can be challenging. As the paper rightly argues against relying solely on point estimates like MAE, providing a qualitative analysis—perhaps with illustrative examples—to demonstrate the practical significance of observed reductions in NLL/NLPD would be beneficial.
Section 4 is exceptionally well-written and presents the results in an intuitive and insightful manner.

Overall, this is a valuable contribution that highlights the shortcomings of relying on point estimates in QE, a field where quantifying confidence is crucial. While the paper does not introduce significant modeling novelty (as GPs have been previously applied in this area), it clearly articulates its research questions and executes them effectively.

Minor issues:

- Section 4: “over and underestimates” should be “over- and underestimates”.
- Figure 1 caption: The lines are blue and green, not blue and red as stated.
- Specifying the toolkit used for GP modeling would be a helpful addition to the final version of the paper.
