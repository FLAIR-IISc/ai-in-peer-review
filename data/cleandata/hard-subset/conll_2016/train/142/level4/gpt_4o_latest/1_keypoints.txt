- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Manuscript is well-organized with a clear introduction on limits of QE point estimates.  
- Valuable discussion on asymmetric risk scenarios requiring different estimators.  
- Revisiting GP fundamentals is justified and makes the work accessible to non-experts.  
- GP methodology, kernel choices, and warping are thoroughly and clearly explained.  
- Research questions are compelling and well-articulated.  
- Question: justify choosing post-editing rates instead of the widely used (H)TER metric.  
- Clarification needed in Section 3.2 regarding trends for fr-en and en-de versus en-es and the reported NLL/NLPD decreases with warped GPs.  
- Suggestion: include a prior point-estimate baseline in result tables for clearer benchmarking.  
- Suggestion: add qualitative examples to illustrate what constitutes a meaningful reduction in NLL/NLPD and why MAE is misleading.  
- Section 4 is particularly well-written and provides intuitive explanations of results.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution highlighting limits of point estimates and importance of uncertainty, though not advancing SOTA or introducing major modeling innovations.  
- Minor edits: change "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates."  
- Minor edit: correct Figure 1 caption colors (lines are blue and green, not blue and red).  
- Minor suggestion: reference the GP toolkit used if any was employed.
