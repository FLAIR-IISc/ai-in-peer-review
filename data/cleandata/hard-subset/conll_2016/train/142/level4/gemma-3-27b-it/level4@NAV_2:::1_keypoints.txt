- Paper applies Gaussian process probabilistic models to predict post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.  
- Well-structured introduction that clearly motivates problems with QE point estimates.  
- Clear and useful description of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators.  
- Revisiting GP fundamentals is lengthy but justified for accessibility to non-expert readers.  
- GP methodology, kernel choices, and warping are explained clearly and are easy to follow.  
- Research questions are interesting and well-phrased.  
- Question: no justification given for choosing post-editing rates instead of the commonly used HTER.  
- Section 3.2 is unclear about the specific trend differences for fr-en and en-de versus en-es; NLL/NLPD reportedly decrease with warped GPs across all datasets.  
- Suggestion: include an existing point-estimate model baseline in results to contextualize overall model quality.  
- NLL and NLPD values are hard to interpret; recommend qualitative examples to show what constitutes a meaningful reduction.  
- Section 4 is well written and provides intuitive explanations of results.  
- Overall positive: paper highlights issues with relying on point estimates and the importance of uncertainty information.  
- Limitation: does not advance state-of-the-art nor present significant modeling novelty since GPs have been used before.  
- Minor issues: correct “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”  
- Minor issue: Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (says blue and red; actually blue and green).  
- Minor issue: cite the specific GP toolkit used if one was employed.
