- Studies Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Clear, well-structured introduction that highlights real-world issues with QE point estimates.
- Good explanation of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators.
- Paper revisits GPs at length — useful for non-expert readers though familiar readers may find it long.
- GP methodology, kernel choices, and warping are explained clearly and are easy to follow.
- Research questions are interesting and well-phrased.
- Question: justify choosing post-editing rates over predicting (H)TER, a common QE target.
- Section 3.2 is unclear about observed trends across French-English, English-German, and English-Spanish; NLL/NLPD decrease with warped GPs for all datasets.
- Since the paper does not aim for SOTA, include a baseline point-estimate model in results to contextualize performance.
- NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret; provide guidance on what constitutes a substantial reduction and add qualitative examples.
- Section 4 is well written and explains results intuitively.
- Overall: useful critique of reliance on point estimates in QE; lacks SOTA gains and major modeling novelty but executes its stated goals well.
- Minor fixes: change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates”; correct Figure 1 caption color labels; mention which GP toolkit was used.
