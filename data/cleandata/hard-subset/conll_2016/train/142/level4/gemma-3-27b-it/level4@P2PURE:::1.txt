This paper investigates the application of probabilistic modeling, specifically Gaussian Processes (GPs), to predict post-editing time/rates as a means of quality estimation (QE) for machine translation output.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear introduction that effectively highlights the limitations of point estimates in real-world QE applications. I particularly appreciated the discussion of different asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for estimator selection. 

While readers already familiar with GPs may find the background material somewhat extensive, I believe this detailed exposition is worthwhile for ensuring broader accessibility. The GP approach, including kernel choices and the use of warping, is explained clearly and is easy to follow. In general, the research questions posed are both interesting and well-defined.

However, I have several questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, specifically concerning the intrinsic uncertainty evaluation:

- The choice of post-editing rates as the target variable, rather than (H)TER, warrants further justification. (H)TER is a frequently predicted metric in QE research, and the rationale for prioritizing post-editing rates should be elaborated upon.
- Section 3.2’s first paragraph is unclear. Specifically, what trend is observed for fr-en and en-de that differs from en-es? The observed decreases in NLL and NLPD with warped GPs appear substantial across all three datasets.
- The paper acknowledges its aim is not to advance state-of-the-art, given the use of a standard 17-feature baseline. However, including a point estimate model from existing work in the results tables would provide valuable context for assessing the overall quality of the proposed models.
- Relatedly, the NLL and NLPD values are difficult to interpret in isolation. While the paper rightly cautions against relying on MAE, readers will naturally gravitate towards it for gauging predictive differences. Providing a qualitative analysis, with illustrative examples, to demonstrate the practical significance of observed reductions in NLL/NLPD would be highly beneficial.
Section 4 is exceptionally well-written and presents the results in an intuitive manner.

Overall, this is a strong paper that effectively points out the problematic reliance on point estimates in QE, a field where quantifying uncertainty is arguably crucial. While the submission does not represent a substantial advance in state-of-the-art performance or modeling novelty (given prior uses of GPs), its research questions are clearly articulated and well-addressed.

Minor issues:

- Section 4: “over and underestimates” should be “over- and underestimates.”
- Figure 1 caption: The lines are blue and green, not blue and red, as stated.
- It would be helpful to mention the specific toolkit used for GP modeling in the final version of the paper.
