- Investigates Gaussian Processes for QE, predicting post-editing time/rates instead of (H)TER.  
- Core argument: point estimates alone are insufficient — calibrated uncertainty is crucial for real-world decision-making under asymmetric risk.  
- Explores GPs with different kernels and warping functions to obtain and leverage uncertainty estimates.  
- Strength: clear structure and thorough methodological explanation, with an effective introduction motivating probabilistic QE.  
- Strength: detailed GP background improves accessibility even if familiar to some readers.  
- Strength: strong exploration of asymmetric risk scenarios and corresponding estimators with practical relevance.  
- Primary contribution: argues for evaluating QE beyond point-estimate metrics and demonstrates this with probabilistic GPs.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical comparison of GP configurations using NLPD, showing NLPD captures uncertainty aspects missed by MAE and Pearson’s r.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for choosing post-editing rates over the more common (H)TER.  
- Weakness: unclear discussion in Section 3.2 about NLL and NLPD trends; needs precise articulation and dataset comparisons (what is “drastic”).  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to a representative point-estimate QE model from literature to contextualize NLL/NLPD improvements.  
- Weakness: would benefit from qualitative examples illustrating how NLL/NLPD reductions impact post-editing scenarios in practice.  
- Questions to authors: justify using post-editing rates over (H)TER.  
- Questions to authors: elaborate on specific NLL/NLPD trends in Section 3.2 and compare datasets precisely.  
- Questions to authors: consider adding a comparison to a representative point-estimate QE model.  
- Questions to authors: provide qualitative examples showing tangible benefits of NLL/NLPD improvements in post-editing.  
- Additional minor comments: change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates”; fix Figure 1 caption color description (lines are blue and green); mention the GP toolkit used if applicable.
