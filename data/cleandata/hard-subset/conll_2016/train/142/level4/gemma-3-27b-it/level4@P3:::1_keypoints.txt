- Focuses on applying probabilistic models (Gaussian Processes) to MT quality estimation, emphasizing uncertainty for post-editing time/rates in asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Major contribution: comprehensive evaluation of probabilistic QE using full posterior predictive distributions and NLPD, showing distributional differences missed by MAE/Pearson.
- Secondary contribution: clear formulation and exploration of asymmetric loss scenarios and corresponding Bayesian risk estimators for QE workflows.
- Provides a thorough comparison of GP configurations (kernels and warping functions) useful for QE researchers.
- Strong motivation: argues convincingly that uncertainty information is crucial for practical QE deployment.
- Methods are clearly and accessibly explained, including GP basics, Matérn kernels, warping functions, and asymmetric loss connections to Bayes risk.
- Evaluation is comprehensive, using NLPD alongside traditional metrics and exploring asymmetric loss impacts.
- Experimental setup is well-defined and robust (standard datasets, features, and 10-fold cross-validation); choice to use post-editing rates is justified.
- Results are presented intuitively, with thoughtful discussion of limitations like overconfident GP predictions.
- Weakness: limited novelty in modeling—primarily evaluation and analysis rather than a new modeling approach.
- Weakness: NLPD/NLL improvements are hard to interpret without benchmarks or qualitative examples of predictive distributions.
- Weakness: comparison to stronger point-estimate baselines (more advanced features/SOTA) is limited, reducing context for performance.
- Weakness: linex loss analysis may be speculative and needs further justification about GPs’ behavior under pessimistic scenarios.
- Question: please provide example sentences showing predictive distribution differences between standard and warped GPs.
- Question: specify which GP toolkit was used to aid reproducibility.
- Question: elaborate on the claim that linex loss favors higher-variance models and consider alternative loss functions.
- Minor edits: fix “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates” and correct Figure 1 caption (lines are blue and green).
- Suggestion: add discussion of computational cost of GP models as dataset size grows.
- Overall verdict: solid, well-written paper with valuable evaluation and insights; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
