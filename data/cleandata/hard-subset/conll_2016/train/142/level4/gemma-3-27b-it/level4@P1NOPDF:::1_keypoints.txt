- Paper uses Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for machine translation quality estimation.
- Well-structured with a clear introduction highlighting limitations of point estimates in QE.
- Effective description of asymmetric risk scenarios and the need for different estimators.
- Background on GPs may be lengthy for experts but makes concepts accessible to a broader audience.
- GP methodology (kernel choices and warping) is clearly explained and easy to follow.
- Research questions are interesting and well-formulated.
- Question: justify choosing post-editing rates as the target variable instead of the commonly used (H)TER.
- Issue: Section 3.2 first paragraph is unclear—specify the claimed trend for fr-en and en-de versus en-es and clarify the “drastic” decrease claim since NLL/NLPD decrease for all datasets with warped GPs.
- Suggestion: include a point-estimate baseline from existing work in result tables for contextual comparison.
- Concern: NLL and NLPD values are hard to interpret—provide qualitative examples to illustrate what constitutes a “drastic” reduction versus MAE differences.
- Section 4 is well written and explains results intuitively.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution emphasizing uncertainty in QE, but not a major SOTA or modeling novelty given prior GP use.
- Minor issues: correct “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates,” fix Figure 1 caption colors (blue and green, not blue and red), and mention the specific GP toolkit used.
