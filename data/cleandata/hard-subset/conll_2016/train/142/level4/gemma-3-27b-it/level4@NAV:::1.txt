This paper investigates the application of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian Processes, to model post-editing time and rates for the purpose of quality estimation (QE) of machine translation output. 

The paper is well-organized and features a strong introduction that effectively motivates the limitations of point estimates in practical QE scenarios. I particularly appreciated the clear articulation of different asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for estimator selection. While the paper dedicates considerable space to reviewing Gaussian Processes for readers potentially unfamiliar with the technique, this thoroughness is justified to ensure accessibility.

The GP methodology, including kernel choices and the use of warping, is explained with commendable clarity and is easy to follow. The research questions posed are both interesting and well-defined.

However, I have several questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion, specifically concerning the intrinsic uncertainty evaluation:

- The choice of post-editing rates as the target variable, rather than the more common (H)TER, warrants further justification. While not necessarily a flaw, explaining this decision would strengthen the paper.
- Section 3.2 contains a paragraph that I found unclear. The described trend observed for fr-en and en-de, but not en-es, is not readily apparent, as NLL and NLPD demonstrate substantial decreases with warped GPs across all three datasets.
- The paper acknowledges its focus is not on achieving state-of-the-art results, given the use of a standard feature set. Nevertheless, including a point estimate model from existing QE literature in the results tables would provide valuable context regarding the overall performance of the presented models.
- Related to this, interpreting NLL and NLPD values can be challenging. As the paper advocates against relying on point estimates like MAE, providing a qualitative analysis illustrating the practical significance of observed reductions in NLL/NLPD – perhaps through concrete examples – would be highly beneficial.
Section 4 is exceptionally well-written, presenting the results in an intuitive and accessible manner.

Overall, this is a valuable contribution that highlights the shortcomings of relying solely on point estimates in QE, a critical issue where confidence measures are arguably essential. While the submission does not represent a novel modeling approach (as GPs have been previously applied in this field) nor achieve state-of-the-art performance, its research questions are clearly defined and effectively addressed.

Minor points for consideration:

- Section 4: “over and underestimates” should be “over- and underestimates.”
- Figure 1 caption: The lines are blue and green, not blue and red as stated.
- Specifying the toolkit used for GP modeling would be helpful.
