- Uses Gaussian Processes to regress post-editing time/rates for QE of machine translation.
- Well structured introduction that clearly motivates limits of point estimates and asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Intro may be overly long for GP experts but improves accessibility for broader readers.
- GP methodology (kernel choices and warping) is clearly explained and research questions are well-phrased.
- Requests justification for choosing post-editing rates over (H)TER as the target variable.
- Asks for clarification in Section 3.2: what specific trend differs for fr-en & en-de vs en-es, given NLL/NLPD drop with warped GPs across all datasets.
- Recommends adding a point-estimate baseline from prior work in result tables for contextual comparison.
- Notes NLL/NLPD are hard to interpret in isolation and suggests qualitative/illustrative examples to show practical significance of reductions.
- Section 4 is well written and presents results intuitively.
- Overall positive: valuable contribution highlighting the need for uncertainty in QE; not SOTA or highly novel but well executed.
- Minor: use “over- and underestimates” (hyphenation).
- Minor: Figure 1 caption incorrectly states line colors (should be blue and green, not blue and red).
