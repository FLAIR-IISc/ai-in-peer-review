- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to quality estimation by regressing on post-editing time/rates, arguing for uncertainty-aware predictions over point estimates.  
- Intro clearly motivates limitations of point estimates and articulates asymmetric risk scenarios requiring different estimators.  
- Exposition of GPs, kernel choices, and warping is thorough and accessible, though possibly long for GP-expert readers.  
- Choice of post-editing rates as the prediction target (instead of common HTER/TER) needs clearer justification.  
- Paragraph 1 of Section 3.2 is unclear: the claimed trend for fr-en and en-de (absent for en-es) is not evident.  
- Claim that NLL and NLPD “decrease drastically” with warped GPs across datasets requires quantitative substantiation and context.  
- Provide concrete, qualitative examples or real-world scenarios to illustrate what a “drastic” reduction in NLL/NLPD means in practice.  
- Although not aiming for SOTA, the paper should include a recent point-estimate QE baseline to help benchmark performance.  
- Section 4 is well-written and presents results clearly and intuitively.  
- Overall contribution is valuable for emphasizing uncertainty in QE, even if GPs themselves are not novel in related areas.  
- Minor issues: correct “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 caption misstates line colors (says blue/red but are blue/green).  
- Minor issues: specify the GP toolkit used to improve reproducibility.
