- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to predict post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of machine translation.
- Introduction is clear and effectively highlights limitations of point estimates and discusses asymmetric risk scenarios.
- GP background is somewhat lengthy but improves accessibility for non-expert readers.
- GP methodology (kernel choices and warping) is clearly explained; research questions are interesting and well-defined.
- The choice of post-editing rates instead of (H)TER as the target needs further justification.
- Section 3.2 is unclear about differing trends for fr-en and en-de versus en-es; decreases in NLL/NLPD with warped GPs appear substantial across datasets.
- Including a point-estimate baseline from prior work would help contextualize performance relative to the standard 17-feature GP baseline.
- NLL and NLPD values are hard to interpret in isolation; provide qualitative analysis or illustrative examples to show practical impact of reductions.
- Section 4 is exceptionally well-written and presents results intuitively.
- Overall assessment: strong paper emphasizing the importance of uncertainty in QE, though not a substantial SOTA or modeling novelty.
- Minor issues: correct “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates,” fix Figure 1 caption colors, and state the GP toolkit used.
