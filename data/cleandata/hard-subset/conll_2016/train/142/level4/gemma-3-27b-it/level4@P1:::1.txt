The paper explores the use of probabilistic models (Gaussian processes) to regress on the target variable of post-editing time/rates for quality estimation of MT output.

The paper is well structured with a clear introduction that highlights the problem of QE point estimates in real-world applications. I especially liked the description of the different asymmetric risk scenarios and how they entail different estimators.

For readers familiar with GPs the paper spends considerable space revisiting them, but I think it is worthwhile to introduce these concepts to the reader.

The GP approach and the choices for kernels and using warping are explained very clearly and are easy to follow. In general, the research questions that are addressed by this paper are interesting and well-phrased.

However, I do have some questions/suggestions about the Results and Discussion sections for Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:

- Why were post-editing rates chosen over prediction (H)TER? TER is a common value to predict in QE research and it would have been helpful to justify the choice made in the paper.
- Section 3.2: I found the first paragraph difficult to follow: What exactly is the trend observed for French-English and English-German that is not seen for English-Spanish? NLL and NLPD demonstrably decrease with warped GPs for all three datasets.
- The paper states that it does not aim to advance state-of-the-art (given the use of only the standard 17 baseline features), but it would have been valuable to include another point estimate model from existing work in the result tables, to provide context for the overall quality of the models.
- Related to this, it is difficult to interpret NLL and NLPD values, so one is naturally inclined to look at MAE in the tables to get a sense of how different the predictions are. Since the central argument of the paper is that this is not the correct approach, it would be beneficial to provide some indication of what constitutes a substantial reduction in NLL/NLPD; a qualitative analysis with illustrative examples would be helpful.
Section 4 is very nicely written and explains the results very intuitively!

Overall, I like the paper because it points out the problematic reliance on point estimates in QE. This is a challenging task in general where additional information such as confidence is arguably very important. The submission does not advance state-of-the-art and does not present significant modeling novelty (since GPs have been used previously), but its research questions and goals are clearly stated and well executed. 

Minor points:

- Section 4: “over and underestimates” -> “over- and underestimates”
- Figure 1 caption: The lines are actually blue and green, not blue and red as stated in the caption.
- If a specific toolkit was used for GP modeling, it would be helpful to mention this in the final paper.
