- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to model post-editing time/rates for machine translation quality estimation.  
- Strong, well-organized introduction that motivates limitations of point estimates and presents asymmetric risk scenarios.  
- Thorough review of Gaussian Processes is lengthy but justified for accessibility.  
- GP methodology (kernel choices and warping) is clearly explained and easy to follow.  
- Research questions are interesting and well-defined.  
- Choice of post-editing rates as target needs further justification versus common (H)TER.  
- Paragraph in Section 3.2 is unclear about trends across fr-en, en-de, and en-es datasets.  
- Paper should include a point-estimate model from existing QE literature in result tables for context.  
- NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret; provide qualitative analysis or concrete examples to show practical significance of reductions.  
- Section 4 is exceptionally well-written and presents results accessibly.  
- Contribution is valuable in highlighting limits of point estimates, though not novel or state-of-the-art.  
- Minor fixes: use “over- and underestimates,” correct Figure 1 caption color description, and specify the GP toolkit used.
