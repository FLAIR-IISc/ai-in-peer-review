- Paper applies Gaussian Processes to model post-editing time/rates for QE, focusing on informative uncertainty estimates rather than point predictions.  
- Well-structured, clearly motivated introduction that highlights limits of point estimates and discusses asymmetric risk and optimal estimators.  
- Thorough GP background and implementation details (kernels, warping); potentially redundant for experts but justified for accessibility.  
- Research questions are interesting, well-defined, and rigorously addressed.  
- Choice of post-editing rates as target (vs. HTER) needs further justification.  
- Paragraph 3.2 is unclear about observed trends for fr-en/en-de and their absence for en-es; claimed contrasts with NLL/NLPD reductions require clarification.  
- Suggest adding a comparable point-estimate baseline from QE literature to contextualize performance.  
- NLL/NLPD are hard to interpret for practitioners; include qualitative examples showing practical impact of reduced uncertainty (predictions with associated uncertainties).  
- Results and discussion (Section 4) are exceptionally well-written and intuitive.  
- Contribution is valuable for QE practice by emphasizing uncertainty, though not novel methodologically since GPs were used before.  
- Minor edits: change "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates", correct Figure 1 caption color from “blue and red” to “blue and green”, and mention the GP toolkit used for reproducibility.
