The paper explores the use of probabilistic models, specifically Gaussian Processes (GPs), to regress on post-editing time/rates for quality estimation (QE) of machine translation output. 

The paper is well structured, with a clear introduction that effectively highlights the limitations of point estimates in real-world QE applications. I particularly appreciated the description of different asymmetric risk scenarios and how these scenarios motivate the choice of different estimators. While readers familiar with GPs may find the introductory material somewhat extensive, I believe this detailed explanation is worthwhile to ensure accessibility for a broader audience. 

The GP approach, including the choices of kernels and the use of warping, is explained very clearly and is easy to follow. In general, the research questions addressed by this paper are interesting and well-phrased.

However, I have some questions and suggestions regarding the Results and Discussion sections, specifically concerning the Intrinsic Uncertainty Evaluation:
- Why were post-editing rates chosen as the target variable over (H)TER? TER is a common metric to predict in QE research, and the paper would benefit from a justification for this choice.
- Section 3.2: The first paragraph is unclear. What specific trend is observed for fr-en & en-de that differs from en-es? NLL and NLPD drastically decrease with warped GPs for all three datasets.
- The paper explicitly states that it does not aim to advance state-of-the-art, given the use of only the standard 17 baseline features. However, including a point estimate model from existing work in the result tables would provide valuable context for assessing the overall quality of the models presented.
- Related to this, NLL and NLPD values are difficult to interpret in isolation. As the paper argues against relying on point estimates like MAE, it would be beneficial to provide a qualitative analysis with illustrative examples to demonstrate the practical significance of observed reductions in NLL/NLPD – essentially, to convey what constitutes a "drastic" reduction.
Section 4 is very well written and presents the results intuitively.

Overall, I find this paper to be a valuable contribution, as it effectively points out the problematic nature of relying solely on point estimates in QE, where uncertainty information is arguably crucial. While the submission does not advance the state-of-the-art or introduce significant modeling novelty (given the prior use of GPs), its research questions are clearly defined and well executed.

Minor issues:
- Section 4: “over and underestimates” should be “over- and underestimates”.
- Figure 1 caption: The lines are blue and green, not blue and red, as stated.
- It would be helpful to mention the specific toolkit used for GP modeling in the final version of the paper.
