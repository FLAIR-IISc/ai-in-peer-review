Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper argues for modeling predictive uncertainty in sentence-level MT Quality Estimation (QE) via Gaussian Processes (GPs), focusing on calibrated predictive distributions rather than point estimates. It proposes using Matérn kernels and Warped GPs to better match data characteristics (positivity, non-smoothness), evaluates models with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD), and demonstrates how predictive posteriors enable decision-making under asymmetric loss (AL and linex), using Bayes estimators without retraining.
- As I see it, the primary contributions are:
  1) An empirical study applying Warped GPs and Matérn kernels to QE with NLPD-based evaluation, showing NLPD captures differences in uncertainty missed by MAE and Pearson’s r.
  2) A use case of predictive uncertainty for asymmetric risk, deriving and applying Bayes estimators (quantiles for AL, closed form for linex) to QE decisions.
  3) Practical observations about when training likelihood (NLL) can mislead compared to NLPD in small-data QE.

Strengths
- Addresses an important gap in QE: Decisions in workflows depend on confidence, not just point estimates. Centering evaluation on proper scoring rules (NLPD) is well-motivated and valuable to the community.
- Sensible modeling choices for QE data: positivity of post-editing rates and potential non-smoothness. Warped GPs are a principled way to enforce support on R+, and Matérn kernels can relax unrealistic smoothness assumptions.
- The asymmetric-risk section is useful and actionable. The Bayes estimator framing (no retraining for different operating points) is a sound and practical insight that could influence how QE outputs are consumed in downstream workflows.
- The finding that MAE and Pearson’s r largely fail to reflect uncertainty modeling differences is important for evaluation practice in QE and possibly other text regression tasks.
- Experimental protocol uses 10-fold cross validation across three language pairs and a standard feature set, providing some breadth and avoiding language-specific resources.

Weaknesses
- Serious technical errors and inconsistencies in equations and kernels:
  - RBF/EQ kernel: kEQ(x,x′) = σv exp(−r2) is nonstandard; usually amplitude is σ^2 (or σv^2) and exponent commonly includes 1/2 (or equivalently defines r appropriately). The paper defines r2 as a squared scaled distance but then uses r2 in places where r should appear.
  - Matérn kernels are incorrectly specified: Matern-3/2 should be σ^2(1+√3 r)exp(−√3 r); the paper uses r2 inside both the linear term and exponential. Matérn-5/2 should be σ^2(1+√5 r + 5r^2/3)exp(−√5 r); the paper again uses r2 and omits 1/3 factor. These are not minor typos; they change the kernel substantially.
  - Warped GP predictive density formula is wrong: the exponent is missing the negative squared term and the 1/2σ^2 factor; it should be exp(−(f(y*)−µ*)^2/(2σ*^2)), not linear in (f(y*)−µ*)/σ*.
  - Asymmetric linear (AL) Bayes estimator: for the stated loss L(ŷ,y)=w(ŷ−y) if ŷ>y, and (y−ŷ) otherwise, the optimal quantile is q=1/(1+w), not w/(w+1). With w>1 (pessimistic, penalizing overestimates), q should be <0.5, not >0.5 as implied. This undermines the asymmetric-risk experiments and conclusions.
- Evaluation gaps and limited baselines:
  - No comparison to alternative uncertainty approaches (e.g., quantile regression baselines for AL, expectile regression, Student-t likelihood GPs, heteroscedastic/noise models, bootstrap SVR, ensembling, or Bayesian NNs). Without such baselines, it is hard to attribute gains to warping versus general calibration improvements.
  - No calibration diagnostics (PIT histograms, reliability diagrams, CRPS). NLPD alone is insufficient to assess calibration vs sharpness trade-offs.
  - No statistical significance testing, confidence intervals, or variance across folds; claims about superiority (e.g., tanh vs log warping) are not quantified for robustness.
- Methodological details insufficient or potentially problematic:
  - Warping function selection (“up to 3 terms”): criteria for choosing I (and per-fold tuning) are unspecified; risk of overfitting and test leakage. No priors/regularization on warping parameters, no discussion of optimization stability.
  - Feature preprocessing (standardization, outlier handling) is not described; positivity vs zeros in post-editing rates is not discussed (log transform undefined at zero).
  - For the asymmetric experiments, the choice of weights is ad hoc, with acknowledged numerical instabilities (linex exploding); results are described as “inconclusive” for pessimistic linex, which weakens the claimed utility.
- Clarity, formatting, and referencing issues that impede reproducibility:
  - Tables and figure referenced (Table 1, Table 2, Figure 1) are not present in the submission; critical to judge claims.
  - Numerous typos and inconsistencies (“postediting” vs “post-editing”, “predicitive”, “an sentence”, “remaining of this paper”), incorrect diacritics and inconsistent kernel notation (Matèrn).
  - Footnotes and URLs are inlined poorly; references are severely malformed (missing years, “?”, odd escapes, inconsistent author punctuation, “Callison.burch”, “Hernández.Lobato”, “\Q2014\E”), which does not meet conference standards.
  - Notation inconsistencies (σv vs σv^2; σ∗ vs σ∗2; µ∗/σ∗ usage) add confusion.

Questions to authors
- Please correct and clarify the exact kernel definitions you used in code (RBF and both Matérn variants). Did experiments use the incorrect forms as written, or the standard forms? Provide the exact formulas implemented.
- Please correct the Warped GP predictive density expression and confirm the implementation. How did you compute the marginal likelihood and its gradients for warping parameters? Any regularization or priors?
- For the AL loss, which quantile did you actually use in experiments: q=1/(1+w) or q=w/(w+1)? If the latter, please explain the rationale and reconcile with standard derivations.
- Did any sentences have zero post-editing rates? How did you handle zeros for log-warp models? What was the warping term count I per dataset/fold, and how was it selected without peeking at test data?
- Can you add calibration diagnostics (e.g., PIT or reliability plots) and CRPS to support claims about “well-calibrated” uncertainty?
- Please include strong probabilistic baselines (quantile regression, Student-t GP, bootstrap SVR) and report statistical significance. Also, clarify preprocessing (standardization), optimization restarts per model, and whether hyperparameters were tuned within each training fold only.

Overall, while the problem is important and the high-level message is valuable, the manuscript, as written, contains multiple serious technical formula errors, missing artifacts (tables/figure), and incomplete evaluation and baselines. These issues need to be addressed for the contribution to be reliable.
