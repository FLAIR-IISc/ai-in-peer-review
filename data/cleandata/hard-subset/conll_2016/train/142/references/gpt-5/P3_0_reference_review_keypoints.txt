- Paper advocates modeling predictive uncertainty in sentence-level MT QE using Gaussian Processes (GPs), revisiting kernels and introducing Warped GPs for positive targets.
- Claims that NLPD (Negative Log Predictive Density) better captures uncertainty than MAE/Pearson r, with Warped GPs improving NLL/NLPD.
- Shows post-hoc, loss-aware MBR decision rules under asymmetric losses (asymmetric linear and linex) using GP posteriors, avoiding retraining.
- Provides a practical kernel/warping analysis across three QE datasets and 17 QuEst features, comparing Matérn vs. RBF and different warpings.
- Empirical contribution: predictive distributions and NLPD reveal information missed by point metrics; Warped GPs yield substantially better predictive likelihoods.
- Demonstrates quantile-based estimators for asymmetric L1 and closed-form linex estimators for asymmetric exponential loss.
- Observes that linex can favor high-variance models and penalize variance-suppressing warpings, affecting loss-aligned model choice.
- Clear, timely motivation linking uncertainty calibration to production QE workflows (post-editing, gisting) and decision-making.
- Sound probabilistic methodology: correct GP treatment, ARD, Matérn kernels, and monotone warpings are principled and well-explained.
- Proper evaluation with test NLPD highlights overfitting risks when relying on training NLL and shows point metrics’ limitations for uncertainty.
- Post-hoc MBR estimators are practical and useful for practitioners needing loss-aware decisions without retraining.
- Uses a simple, reusable protocol (ten-fold CV, unified features) that others can replicate or extend.
- Limited novelty: techniques are well-established in GP literature and the paper is largely an application/empirical study for QE.
- Missing probabilistic baselines: no comparison to quantile regression, heteroscedastic models, alternative likelihoods (gamma/log-normal), Bayesian NNs, or deep ensembles.
- Incomplete calibration assessment: lacks interval coverage, PIT histograms, CRPS, or other diagnostics to separate sharpness from calibration.
- Modeling under-explored: targets likely heavy-tailed/heteroscedastic but alternatives like heteroscedastic GPs or log-normal/gamma likelihoods are not tested.
- Kernel analysis is shallow and lacks discussion of ARD lengthscales or feature relevance.
- Evaluation is not decision-centric enough: no realistic accept/reject task, cost curves, or statistical significance testing reported.
- Reproducibility concerns: missing details on warping initialization, monotonicity enforcement, tanh-term selection, optimization settings, runtimes, and seed averaging; code/splits not released.
- Asks for calibration metrics (coverage, PIT, CRPS) to assess whether warped models yield better-calibrated intervals.
- Requests comparisons to quantile regression, log-normal/gamma GPs, heteroscedastic GPs, Bayesian NNs, and deep ensembles for contextualizing gains.
- Seeks details on warping implementation: monotonicity enforcement, initialization, regularization, Bayesian warps, runtimes, and numerical stability.
- Wants quantification of posterior variances and analysis of how linex estimators shift predictions and affect performance under pessimistic settings.
- Suggests decision evaluation: show utility/cost-sensitive accept/reject curves comparing warped vs. standard GP and point-estimate baselines.
- Questions generality with modern QE inputs: how results change with neural sentence representations (e.g., XLM-R) or newer QE datasets.
