- Paper argues for modeling predictive uncertainty in MT quality estimation using GPs and Warped GPs, evaluating with probabilistic metrics (NLPD) and enabling decision-theoretic predictions under asymmetric loss.

- Contribution: Empirical evidence that uncertainty-aware evaluation (NLPD) distinguishes QE models that look similar under point metrics, with Warped GPs improving predictive densities.

- Contribution: Decision-theoretic treatment showing Bayes-optimal predictors for asymmetric linear and linex losses can be derived from the same probabilistic model without retraining.

- Contribution: Modest modeling exploration comparing Matérn vs EQ kernels and parametric vs log warping, with analysis of when modeling choices matter.

- Strength: Clear motivation and correct use of proper scoring rules, highlighting limitations of point estimates and the reliability of held-out NLPD.

- Strength: Sound, well-grounded methodology (GP/Warped GP setups, ARD, marginal likelihood training, two-pass hyperparameter optimization, 10-fold CV).

- Strength: Practical decision-making angle mapping realistic QE scenarios to asymmetric losses and showing operational flexibility without retraining.

- Strength: Empirical evidence that distributional modeling (especially Warped GPs) matters beyond point accuracy for NLPD and asymmetric-loss performance.

- Strength: Approach generalizes to other text regression tasks with positive-valued or skewed targets.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty; techniques (Warped GPs, Matérn kernels, NLPD, Bayes risk) are established and the contribution is mainly empirical/advocacy.

- Weakness: Incomplete comparative baselines — no comparisons to quantile regression, Bayesian linear regression, bootstrapped ensembles, or modern neural uncertainty methods.

- Weakness: Calibration analysis is shallow; lacks diagnostics decomposing sharpness vs calibration (reliability diagrams, PIT, CRPS) to support linex behavior claims.

- Weakness: Narrow feature/modeling setting and dated QE setup (only 17 QuEst features, classical GPs) without stronger point-estimate baselines or modern QE representations.

- Weakness: Reporting lacks statistical rigor—no significance tests, confidence intervals, or effect sizes to support claims about overfitting or model differences.

- Question: Provide calibration diagnostics (calibration curves/PIT histograms, CRPS) to assess whether NLPD improvements stem from calibration or sharpness.

- Question: How do GP models compare to quantile regression (e.g., gradient boosting quantile regressor) and simple Bayesian linear regression for asymmetric loss scenarios?

- Question: What are the distributions of predictive variances across models/datasets, and do warped GPs consistently reduce variance correlating with linex outcomes?

- Question: Provide sensitivity analyses for warping complexity (number of tanh terms) and for kernel ARD vs isotropic settings, with possible regularization.

- Question: How robust are results to using more features or modern QE representations (e.g., pretrained sentence embeddings); does the uncertainty advantage persist?

- Overall assessment: Well-motivated, methodologically sound, and practically relevant empirical study that foregrounds uncertainty-aware QE, but limited by lack of broader baselines, deeper calibration analysis, and statistical rigor.
