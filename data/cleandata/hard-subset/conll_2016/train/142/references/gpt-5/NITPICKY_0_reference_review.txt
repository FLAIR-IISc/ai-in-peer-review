Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper argues for modeling predictive uncertainty in sentence-level MT Quality Estimation (QE) using Gaussian Processes (GPs), contrasting point estimates with full predictive distributions and evaluating with NLPD. It explores Matérn kernels and Warped GPs to better handle noisy data and positive-valued labels.
- It proposes evaluating QE models with NLPD in addition to MAE/ Pearson’s r, showing that point-estimate metrics miss uncertainty differences. It also demonstrates using predictive distributions to derive Bayes-optimal decisions under asymmetric losses (AL, linex) without retraining.
- Empirically, on three small QE datasets with 17 QuEst features, Warped GPs (esp. tanh warps) often improve NLL/NLPD; under asymmetric losses, warped models can help in optimistic scenarios for AL but underperform for pessimistic linex.

Strengths
- Clear motivation for uncertainty-aware QE. The post-editing and gisting scenarios are realistic, and the asymmetric risk framing is relevant for MT workflows where false positives/negatives have different costs.
- Sound high-level methodological choice. Leveraging GPs (including Matérn kernels and warping) for small-data text regression is appropriate; evaluating with a proper scoring rule (NLPD) is the right direction beyond MAE/r.
- Practical decision-theoretic angle. The exposition on computing Bayes estimators from predictive posteriors to adapt to changing asymmetric losses without retraining is useful in production settings.
- Sensible experimental protocol for small data. Using 10-fold CV and ARD kernels with multiple restarts is reasonable; choosing the median for MAE comparisons reflects the loss-optimal decision rule.

Weaknesses
- Significant technical/notation errors undermine correctness:
  - Warped GP predictive density (Sec. 2.3) is incorrect: the Gaussian exponent lacks the square and negative sign; should be exp(−(f(y*)−μ*)^2/(2σ*^2)), and the prefactor should include 1/σ* (and Jacobian f′(y*)).
  - AL quantile mapping (Sec. 4.1) appears wrong. For L = w(ŷ−y) if ŷ>y and y−ŷ otherwise, the Bayes estimator is the 1/(1+w) quantile, not w/(w+1). This inverts pessimistic/optimistic behavior and affects all asymmetric-AL results and interpretations.
  - Matérn/EQ kernel formulas mix r and r^2 and differ from standard forms (e.g., Matérn-5/2 typically uses r, with (1+√5 r + 5r^2/3)exp(−√5 r)). EQ amplitude and the 1/2 in the exponent are nonstandard/missing. This makes reproduction and interpretation difficult.
- Limited novelty. Warped GPs (Snelson et al., 2004), Matérn kernels, NLPD evaluation, and asymmetric-loss Bayes estimators are well-established. The paper’s novelty is primarily applying these to QE with small feature sets; methodological contribution is minimal.
- Insufficient empirical validation and missing baselines:
  - No numeric tables/figures (Tables 1–2, Figure 1) are present, preventing verification of effect sizes, variance across folds, and statistical significance. Claims of improvements are unverifiable.
  - No comparison to strong non-GP baselines (e.g., quantile regression, quantile random forests/GBMs, Bayesian linear regression, deep ensembles/MC dropout), nor to modern QE systems beyond the small 17-feature baseline. Even SVR point-estimate baselines are absent empirically.
  - No calibration analysis (e.g., coverage of credible intervals, reliability diagrams, PIT histograms, CRPS). NLPD alone is insufficient to claim “well-calibrated” uncertainty.
- Methodological clarity gaps:
  - Monotonicity constraints for the tanh warping are not specified; how are a,b,c constrained to ensure monotonic f and numerical stability? How is overfitting controlled when I=3?
  - Optimization details (priors/regularization, stopping, initialization ranges, handling local minima) are sparse; 10 random restarts may be inadequate for warped models.
  - Using the same training folds for marginal likelihood optimization and model selection (kernel choice, warping complexity) risks overfitting; no validation split or Bayesian model comparison evidence is provided.
- Presentation and formatting issues:
  - Numerous typos, inconsistent notation (e.g., r vs r2), and terminology (“postediting,” “enes”), and several malformed mathematical expressions.
  - References contain many errors (author spellings, years “?,” inconsistent styles, LaTeX artifacts, duplicates). This does not meet conference standards.
  - Claims such as “state-of-the-art” for GPs in QE are outdated relative to recent neural QE advances; related work omits modern neural uncertainty methods.

Questions to authors
- Please correct and clarify the AL quantile mapping and re-run asymmetric-AL experiments. Do results and conclusions change when using τ=1/(1+w)?
- Provide the exact kernel definitions used in code (EQ and Matérn 3/2, 5/2), including amplitude parameters and distance scaling, and reconcile with the equations in the paper.
- Specify constraints/enforcement for monotonic tanh warping (on a,b,c), initialization ranges, and any regularization/hyperpriors used to avoid non-monotonicity or overfitting. What proportion of restarts yielded invalid warps?
- Include full numeric results (means and std across folds) for NLL/NLPD/MAE/r and asymmetric losses, with statistical tests (paired tests across folds). Also report coverage of predictive intervals and CRPS.
- Add comparative baselines: (i) point-estimate SVR and a competitive neural QE baseline for MAE/r, (ii) probabilistic/quantile baselines (quantile regression/forests/GBMs, Bayesian linear regression), and (iii) at least one neural uncertainty method (e.g., deep ensemble or MC dropout).
- How sensitive are conclusions to the number of warping terms I and to the choice of kernel? Provide an ablation and model selection strategy (e.g., via marginal likelihood or cross-validated NLPD).
- For the linex loss pessimistic setting, can you disentangle the effect of variance scale by rescaling predictive variances (e.g., temperature scaling) and report calibration metrics to support claims of “well-calibrated” vs “high-variance but misspecified” models?

Overall recommendation
Despite a timely motivation, the submission suffers from multiple technical errors (notably in core equations and AL mapping), limited novelty, missing baselines, and absent quantitative results/figures. The presentation and references require substantial revision. I recommend rejection in its current form; a revised version addressing the correctness, experimental rigor, calibration analysis, and formatting issues could make a useful empirical study on uncertainty-aware QE.
