- Proposes modeling and evaluating predictive uncertainty in sentence-level MT QE using Gaussian Processes and Warped GPs, advocating NLPD over MAE/Pearson.
- Warped GPs substantially improve likelihood-based metrics and NLPD reveals uncertainty differences unseen by MAE.
- Derives and evaluates Bayes estimators for asymmetric losses (tilted absolute / quantile and linex) to exploit calibrated posteriors without retraining.
- Compares EQ vs Matérn kernels and log vs parametric tanh warps across three QE datasets using the QuEst17 feature set.
- Strength: Uses proper probabilistic evaluation (NLPD on test data), aligning with GP best practice.
- Strength: Warped GPs appropriately handle positive-only targets; change-of-variables predictive density and posterior median derivation are correct.
- Strength: Practical relevance via mapping QE use-cases to asymmetric risk and obtaining Bayes estimators for deployment.
- Strength: Fair experimental protocol—cross-validation, controlled features, and kernel/warp ablations isolate uncertainty modeling effects.
- Strength: Insightful negative result—linex pessimistic setting underperforms warped models due to variance dependence.
- Weakness: Limited baselines—omits GP log-link/log-normal, Gamma/Inverse-Gaussian, Student-t likelihoods, heteroscedastic GPs, and non-GP uncertainty methods (quantile regression, GBM quantiles, etc.).
- Weakness: Insufficient calibration diagnostics—missing coverage tests, PIT histograms, reliability diagrams, and sharpness-vs-calibration analysis.
- Weakness: Incomplete treatment of asymmetric losses—non-median quantiles for Warped GPs and linex estimators for non-Gaussian posteriors not fully specified; potential numerical/robustness issues.
- Weakness: Reporting lacks numeric results, variances, and statistical significance across folds; claims need quantified deltas and error bars.
- Weakness: Limited feature/dataset scope—only 17 QuEst features; lacks modern encoders, out-of-domain tests, and dataset-size ablations to support low-data GP motivation.
- Question: How were non-median AL quantiles computed for Warped GPs (numerical inversion, quadrature, sampling) and what is the computational cost?
- Question: For linex, was the normal-form formula used as an approximation for warped posteriors, and was bias assessed by Monte Carlo?
- Question: Why not compare to GP log-normal/Gamma/Student-t likelihoods and other robust baselines that also enforce positivity or heavy tails?
- Question: Can you report empirical coverage of predictive intervals and PIT histograms for each model to substantiate calibration claims?
- Question: What optimizer/regularization and constraints were used for warping parameters; how sensitive are results to number of tanh terms and random restarts?
- Suggestion: Consider adding CRPS as an additional proper scoring rule less sensitive to tail mis-specification.
- Suggestion: Discuss constraining or rescaling linex parameter w and/or tempering predictive variances to address instability.
- Minor: Include full numeric tables/figures with standard errors and fix reference/formatting issues.
- Overall: Useful, application-driven case for probabilistic QE with Warped GPs and proper scoring, but needs stronger baselines, fuller calibration diagnostics, and clearer treatment of asymmetric estimators.
