- Systematic uncertainty-centric evaluation of QE using NLPD, showing MAE/Pearson r can mask important differences in predictive distributions.  
- Application and empirical assessment of Warped GPs and Matérn kernels for positive-valued QE targets, with learned monotone warpings improving log-scores.  
- Decision-theoretic framing: use Bayes estimators for asymmetric losses (AL, linex) to adapt decisions post-hoc without retraining.  

- Clear motivation tying uncertainty in QE to downstream decisions (post-editing vs gisting) and practical Bayes estimator use.  
- Appropriate probabilistic modelling for small-data QE: GPs with ARD/Matérn and Warped GPs to enforce R+ support and avoid ad-hoc truncation.  
- Use of a proper scoring rule (NLPD) that reveals distributional differences unseen by MAE/Pearson r and cautions against relying on training NLL.  
- Decision-theoretic experiments demonstrate flexible post-hoc adaptation and highlight interesting linex effects that penalize low-variance models.  
- Solid experimental protocol: three language pairs, 10-fold CV, consistent features, two-pass kernel training with multiple restarts.  

- Limited methodological novelty: Warped GPs/Matérn kernels are standard and alternatives (log-normal/Gamma likelihoods, Student-t, heteroscedastic GPs) are not compared.  
- Incomplete calibration assessment: relies mainly on NLPD and lacks interval coverage, PIT, CRPS, sharpness vs calibration analyses.  
- Asymmetric loss experiments are self-referential and lack downstream task-level evaluation; linex behavior is hypothesized but not investigated.  
- Noted inconsistencies/typos and clarity issues (AL loss sign/definition, possible omission in Warped GP density equation, kernel equation inconsistencies).  
- Limited baselines and scope: no quantile regression, ensembles, Bayesian NN/ensemble baselines, or comparisons using learned representations.  

- Clarify AL loss sign convention and the mapping of “pessimistic” vs “optimistic.”  
- Report calibration diagnostics: predictive interval coverage (e.g., 50%/90%), PIT histograms, and CRPS across models.  
- Did you compare to GP with log-link/log-normal, Gamma likelihood, or truncated Gaussian for positive support?  
- Did you try Student-t likelihoods or heteroscedastic GPs to handle heavy tails/variable noise?  
- Can variance calibration (temperature scaling) or marginalizing warping parameters mitigate the linex pessimistic issue?  
- How was the number of tanh terms chosen and were warping parameters regularized or sensitivity-tested?  
- Are NLPD/AL improvements statistically significant across folds (report mean±std and paired tests)?  
- Will you release code, hyperparameters, and CV splits for reproducibility?  

- Overall: well-motivated and timely focus on predictive uncertainty in QE, but incremental modeling novelty and needs deeper calibration analysis and stronger baselines to strengthen claims.
