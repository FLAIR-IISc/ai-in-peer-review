- Paper proposes modeling predictive uncertainty in sentence-level MT QE using Gaussian Processes, with Matérn kernels and Warped GPs, evaluated with NLPD and decision-theoretic asymmetric losses.
- Contribution: Empirically shows Warped GPs give better-calibrated predictive distributions (lower NLL/NLPD) than standard GPs, improvements not visible in point metrics.
- Contribution: Demonstrates decision-theoretic use of predictive posteriors to optimize under asymmetric losses (AL, linex) and shows modeling choices interact with loss choice.
- Contribution: Discusses kernel selection (Matérn vs EQ) and the effect of small-data QE regimes on uncertainty estimation.
- Strength: Clear decision-centric motivation and use of a proper scoring rule (NLPD) to reveal differences invisible to MAE/Pearson r.
- Strength: GPs are appropriate baselines for small QE datasets; Warped GPs respect positivity and Matérn kernels relax EQ smoothness assumptions.
- Strength: Correct derivation/use of Bayes estimators for AL and linex, showing models can adapt to different risk profiles without retraining.
- Strength: Honest reporting of failure modes (puzzling linex results under pessimistic settings) with a plausible variance-sensitivity hypothesis.
- Weakness: Missing strong probabilistic baselines—no heteroscedastic GPs, Student-t likelihoods, simple log-normal/Box-Cox transforms, or Bayesian linear/kernel ridge with ARD.
- Weakness: Incomplete uncertainty evaluation—no interval coverage, PIT histograms, or complementary scores like CRPS to assess calibration and tail behavior.
- Weakness: Asymmetric loss experiments lack realistic grounding for chosen weights and miss comparisons to quantile regression (pinball loss) or conformal quantile methods.
- Weakness: Overfitting diagnosis and evaluation protocol are insufficient—single-layer CV, no clear nested validation for hyperparameter/model selection, and missing variability/statistical significance reporting.
- Weakness: Limited QE scope—uses 17 QuEst features and older WMT14–16 post-editing rate targets; lacks tests on diverse labels or neural feature encoders to show generality.
- Request: Report calibration diagnostics—empirical coverage (50/90%), PIT histograms, and CRPS to test variance-based explanations for linex behavior.
- Request: Report results (if tried) for heteroscedastic noise models or Student-t likelihoods compared to Warped GPs on NLPD and asymmetric losses.
- Request: Compare GP-based AL quantiles to standard quantile regression (pinball loss) and conformalized quantile regression for coverage and loss.
- Request: Evaluate simpler parametric warps (Box-Cox/Yeo–Johnson) as competitive monotone transforms to mitigate overfitting on small data.
- Request: Provide sensitivity analyses for number of tanh terms and priors/regularization on warping parameters, and evidence of overfitting (e.g., extreme warping behavior).
- Request: Report robustness across different labels and feature representations (sentence encoders, COMET-QE features) or preliminary results.
- Request: Test variance calibration methods (temperature scaling or isotonic calibration of predictive CDFs) to see if they reduce pessimistic-linex degradation.
