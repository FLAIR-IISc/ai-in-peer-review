- Paper argues for modeling predictive uncertainty in sentence-level MT QE and evaluates Gaussian Process variants that output full predictive distributions.
- Proposes using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate predictive distributions beyond point metrics.
- Explores EQ vs. Matérn kernels and Warped GPs to better model positive response variables (post-editing time/rate).
- Shows predictive distributions enable minimum Bayes risk decision-making for asymmetric losses (AL and linex) without retraining.
- Experiments on three language pairs (en–es, fr–en, en–de) with QuEst features and 10-fold CV; warped GPs improve NLPD and sometimes MAE.
- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that NLPD distinguishes models where MAE/Pearson fail and Warped GPs give better-calibrated densities than standard GPs.
- Contribution: Practical demonstration that Bayes estimators on predictive posteriors handle asymmetric decision costs (notably improving optimistic scenarios) without retraining.
- Contribution: Methodological exploration of enforcing positivity and relaxed smoothness via Warped GPs and Matérn kernels and analysis of train NLL vs. test NLPD.
- Strength: Clear, practical motivation linking uncertainty estimates to risk-sensitive QE decisions like post-editing and gisting.
- Strength: Appropriate probabilistic evaluation using NLPD and useful note that training NLL can mislead on small datasets.
- Strength: Well-motivated modeling choices (warping and Matérn kernels) with empirical gains on NLPD.
- Strength: Decision-theoretic approach for asymmetric risk is practical and operationally useful.
- Strength: Cross-dataset evaluation with 10-fold CV adds robustness to results.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—techniques are established and the work is mainly application-focused.
- Weakness: Incomplete baselines and ablations; missing uncertainty-oriented baselines (quantile regression, Student-t GP, heteroscedastic GP, ensembles).
- Weakness: Calibration analysis is shallow—no coverage probabilities, PIT, or CRPS to substantiate calibration claims.
- Weakness: Linex estimator use is problematic for non-Gaussian warped GP posteriors; mean/variance approximation may be invalid and explain failures in pessimistic scenarios.
- Weakness: Reporting and reproducibility issues—insufficient details on warping initialization/regularization, tanh terms, feature scaling, numerical methods, and no significance tests or released code/splits.
- Question: Did you use the normal-approximation linex estimator for Warped GPs or compute the exact Bayes estimator via log-MGF, and can you quantify the approximation error?
- Question: Can you provide calibration diagnostics (50%/90% coverage, PIT histograms, CRPS) to back calibration claims, and do Warped GPs improve coverage?
- Question: For AL loss, how did you compute predictive quantiles for warped posteriors—numerical inversion details and stability across folds?
- Question: Can you include quantile regression, Student-t GP, heteroscedastic GP, or deep ensembles as baselines to contextualize Warped GPs?
- Question: How were warping parameters initialized/regularized, were priors or constraints used for I=3 tanh terms, were features standardized, and will you release code and splits?
