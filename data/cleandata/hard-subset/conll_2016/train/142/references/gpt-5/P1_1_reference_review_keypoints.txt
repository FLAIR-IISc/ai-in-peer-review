- Paper argues for modelling predictive uncertainty in MT quality estimation (QE) using Gaussian Processes, especially Warped GPs and Matérn kernels, and evaluates uncertainty with NLPD alongside point metrics.  
- Claims Warped GPs substantially improve NLL/NLPD over standard GPs; Matérn kernels sometimes help; point metrics often miss these differences.  
- Shows how predictive distributions can be used for asymmetric loss decisions without retraining via Bayes estimators for AL and linex losses.  

- Contribution: Empirical evidence that NLPD reveals important model differences missed by MAE/Pearson and that Warped GPs give better-calibrated predictive distributions.  
- Contribution: Practical demonstration of decision-making under asymmetric risk using Bayes risk estimators (quantiles and linex) derived from the posterior.  
- Contribution: GP design recommendations for QE (Matérn kernels, monotonic output warping) with analysis of effects on uncertainty and performance.  

- Strength: Clear, relevant motivation tied to real QE workflows and realistic asymmetric loss scenarios.  
- Strength: Sound probabilistic treatment and appropriate use of NLPD to assess predictive distributions.  
- Strength: Sensible model choices (warping, Matérn), clear exposition on median vs mean, ARD, and optimization.  
- Strength: Practical value in enabling asymmetric decisions without retraining, useful for production QE.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation across three language pairs with 10-fold CV provides robustness and highlights dataset-dependent effects.  

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty — key components (Warped GPs, Matérn, NLPD, Bayes estimators) are established; contribution is largely application/analysis.  
- Weakness: Insufficient calibration analysis beyond NLPD (no PIT, interval coverage, CRPS, calibration error reported).  
- Weakness: Narrow feature set (17 QuEst features) and lack of strong/up-to-date QE baselines (e.g., neural QE, ensembles, quantile regression).  
- Weakness: Asymmetric loss experiments partly inconclusive and under-analyzed (linex pessimistic failures not deeply diagnosed; sensitivity to w not shown).  
- Weakness: Missing experimental details and reproducibility concerns (effect sizes, CIs, modelling choices, code/data splits not provided).  

- Question: Can you provide calibration diagnostics (PIT, 80/90% coverage, CRPS) to substantiate claims of better calibration by Warped GPs?  
- Question: Did you compare to alternative likelihoods or transforms (log-Gaussian, Gamma/LogNormal likelihood GPs) and how do they perform in NLPD/calibration?  
- Question: Did you try heteroscedastic GPs or input-dependent noise models, and how did they affect uncertainty and asymmetric decision performance?  
- Question: Can you provide sensitivity curves over w for AL and linex and posterior variance distributions to validate the “variance-driven” explanation for linex underperformance?  
- Question: Can you include uncertainty-aware baselines (quantile regression, bagged SVR, gradient boosting quantile regression, Bayesian neural nets) to contextualize gains?  
- Question: Do uncertainty improvements translate to practical downstream outcomes (reduced bad MT to post-editors, improved gisting precision/recall), any simulation or case study?  

- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept — worthwhile and well-argued but limited novelty and needs deeper empirical/diagnostic analysis and stronger baselines to be clearly above the bar.
