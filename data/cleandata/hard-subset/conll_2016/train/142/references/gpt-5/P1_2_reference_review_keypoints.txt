- Paper argues uncertainty-aware modeling (GPs producing predictive distributions) is valuable for sentence-level QE and evaluates models with NLPD plus point metrics, using Bayes estimators for asymmetric losses.
- Contribution: Empirical study showing Warped GPs notably improve predictive densities (NLPD/NLL) over standard GPs and that uncertainty differences are not captured by MAE/Pearson r.
- Contribution: Demonstrates using predictive posteriors to handle asymmetric risk via Bayes estimators (asymmetric linear and linex losses) without retraining, with mixed results across optimistic/pessimistic scenarios.
- Contribution: Smaller methodological/implementation points — Matérn vs EQ kernels, two-pass ARD optimization, and practical Warped GP details for text regression.
- Strength: Addresses a timely, practical need in QE workflows for calibrated uncertainty to support decision-making (post-editing vs discard).
- Strength: Solid probabilistic framing and use of NLPD as a proper scoring rule; shows point metrics can miss uncertainty differences.
- Strength: Warped GP adaptation for strictly positive labels is principled and yields consistent NLL/NLPD gains.
- Strength: Mapping asymmetric costs to Bayes-optimal estimators is useful and shows post-hoc tuning without retraining is feasible.
- Strength: Careful experimental protocol (three language pairs, 10-fold CV, controlled features, two-pass hyperopt) gives a clean testbed.
- Weakness: Limited novelty — methods (Warped GPs, Matérn kernels, NLPD, asymmetric losses) are established; main novelty is application to QE.
- Weakness: Claims about calibration are not supported by direct calibration diagnostics (coverage, PIT, CRPS, interval scores).
- Weakness: Narrow experimental scope and baselines — only 17 QuEst features and no comparisons to strong non-probabilistic ensembles or modern neural QE methods.
- Weakness: Inconsistent definitions/interpretations for asymmetric losses (AL quantile formula and linex sign interpretation appear incorrect).
- Weakness: Linex/pessimistic failures are inconclusive and explanations are speculative without ablations (posterior variance, heteroscedastic GP, truncated Gaussian).
- Weakness: Practical impact not quantified for motivating scenarios; no downstream decision-analytic evaluations (trade-offs, cost-benefit).
- Question: Please clarify asymmetric loss definitions and Bayes estimators (AL optimal quantile formula and linex sign convention).
- Question: Provide calibration diagnostics (predictive interval coverage, PIT histograms, CRPS/interval scores) to support calibration claims.
- Question: How sensitive are Warped GP results to number of tanh terms and regularization; evidence of overfitting with I=3 on small datasets?
- Question: Did you consider heteroscedastic GPs or heavy-tailed likelihoods (Student-t) as alternatives to warping to address variance/tail issues?
- Question: Can you provide decision-analytic evaluations for post-editing (threshold rules, throughput vs error trade-offs) using posterior moments/quantiles?
- Question: How would results change with stronger features (pretrained embeddings) or on standard QE labels (HTER/DA) to show broader applicability?
- Additional: Recommend code release and precise optimization details (software, seeds, priors/bounds) for reproducibility.
- Additional: Suggest considering truncated Gaussian likelihoods as a simpler alternative to warping.
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept — useful empirical guidance but needs clarifications on asymmetric losses and added calibration/decision analyses to strengthen the contribution.
