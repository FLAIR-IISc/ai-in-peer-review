- Paper argues for modeling and evaluating predictive uncertainty in sentence-level MT QE using Gaussian Processes, comparing standard and Warped GPs and Matern kernels, and evaluating with NLPD plus point metrics.  
- Contribution: Warped GPs substantially improve probabilistic fit (NLL/NLPD) over standard GPs, while MAE/Pearson often miss uncertainty improvements.  
- Contribution: Presents a practical framework using Bayes estimators for asymmetric linear and linex losses to handle pessimistic/optimistic decision settings without retraining.  
- Contribution: Empirical study of kernel/likelihood choices (Matern vs EQ; tanh vs log warping) across three QE datasets, noting dataset-size effects and potential overfitting (NLL vs test NLPD).  
- Strength: Strong practical motivation—point estimates are insufficient for operational QE; emphasis on predictive distributions and asymmetric risk is relevant.  
- Strength: Methodologically sound use of proper scoring (NLPD) showing warping improves uncertainty beyond point metrics.  
- Strength: Clear, principled use of Bayes estimators for asymmetric losses that decouple training from scenario-specific utilities.  
- Strength: Sensible modeling choices—warping for positive support and Matern for relaxed smoothness; insight that log-transform may suffice on smaller data.  
- Strength: Honest discussion of failure modes, e.g., linex pessimistic settings where variance-sensitive penalties favor standard GPs.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—methods are established; main contribution is empirical application to QE.  
- Weakness: Narrow baselines and missing calibration diagnostics (no quantile methods, heteroscedastic/Student-t GPs, ensembles, or interval/PIT/CRPS analyses).  
- Weakness: Limited datasets, small scale, restricted features (17 QuEst features) and unclear generalization to modern neural QE settings.  
- Weakness: Evaluation gaps—no statistical significance testing, limited sensitivity analysis of loss weights, numerical issues for linex, and no computational/latency analysis.  
- Weakness: Notational and clarity issues in kernel/NLPD formulas and some rough typesetting hindering reproducibility.  
- Question: Can you report calibration diagnostics (coverage, PIT, CRPS) to support the claim of better-calibrated uncertainty for Warped GPs?  
- Question: Why were uncertainty baselines like quantile regression, quantile random forests, heteroscedastic/Student-t GPs, or ensembles omitted?  
- Question: Can you demonstrate end-to-end decision outcomes (acceptance rates, recall/precision, cost curves) for post-editing and gisting scenarios?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of w for AL and linex, and can you explain linex numerical instability in pessimistic cases?  
- Question: Will you release code and splits, and provide optimizer/initialization/regularization details for Warped GP training?  
- Overall: Timely, practically motivated empirical study advocating probabilistic evaluation and asymmetric risk in QE, but limited methodological novelty, baseline/calibration coverage, and modern QE representation.
