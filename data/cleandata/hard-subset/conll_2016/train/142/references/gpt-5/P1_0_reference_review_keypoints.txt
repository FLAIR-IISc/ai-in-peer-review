- Paper argues for modeling predictive uncertainty in sentence-level QE using GPs, evaluates full predictive distributions (NLPD) and applies Matérn kernels and Warped GPs across three QE datasets.  
- Shows warped GPs improve NLL/NLPD, Matérn sometimes helps, and point metrics (MAE/r) often miss differences revealed by probabilistic scores.  
- Demonstrates using Bayes estimators to adapt predictions to asymmetric losses (pinball/AL and LINEX) at test time without retraining.

- Contribution: Empirically shows probabilistic evaluation (NLPD) reveals differences that MAE/r can miss and that warping to enforce positivity improves uncertainty.  
- Contribution: Applies Warped GPs and Matérn kernels to QE with careful training and uses Bayes estimators to handle asymmetric losses without retraining.  
- Contribution: Analyses optimistic/pessimistic use cases, showing interaction between predictive variance and asymmetric losses.

- Strength: Clear motivation linking uncertainty in QE to real downstream decision-making (e.g., post-editing filters) and asymmetric risk.  
- Strength: Uses proper probabilistic evaluation (NLPD) and finds informative behaviors (e.g., NLL vs NLPD differences on small data).  
- Strength: Reasonable GP modeling choices (Matérn, warping) justified for noisy text features and positive outputs.  
- Strength: Practical demonstration of Bayes estimators for asymmetric losses enables decision optimization without retraining.  
- Strength: Multi-dataset evaluation and 10-fold CV improve robustness of findings.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—techniques (Warped GPs, Matérn, NLPD, Bayes estimators) are established; contribution is mainly empirical.  
- Weakness: Lacks strong baselines for uncertainty/asymmetry (quantile regression, quantile RF/GBM, Bayesian linear/NN, deep ensembles).  
- Weakness: Calibration analysis is underdeveloped—no interval coverage, PIT, CRPS, or reliability diagnostics presented.  
- Weakness: Asymmetric evaluation may be tautological (optimizing and evaluating with same loss); task-level decision impact is not simulated.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited (only 17 QuEst features, no ablations on warping complexity/heteroscedasticity, no significance testing).

- Question: How was monotonicity of the tanh-sum warp enforced and how sensitive are results to number of terms I and optimization stability?  
- Question: Can you report predictive interval coverage, CRPS, or calibration plots to assess whether warped models are better calibrated?  
- Question: How do your models compare to quantile regression and quantile RF/GBMs for AL, and to Bayesian linear models or deep ensembles for uncertainty?  
- Question: Did you try heteroscedastic GPs or Student-t likelihoods to model input-dependent noise and possibly improve LINEX pessimistic performance?  
- Question: Can you simulate practical decision metrics (e.g., segments sent to PE, bad-pass rate, productivity) to show tangible gains from your approach?  
- Question: Did you compare posterior mean vs median for point metrics, and does that change conclusions on MAE/r or NLPD?  
- Question: Will code, hyperparameters, and feature extraction pipelines be released to ensure reproducibility?
