- Paper explores GP models for QE with emphasis on uncertainty estimation, proposing Matérn kernels and Warped GPs and advocating NLPD as an uncertainty-aware metric.  
- Contribution: argues NLPD better captures uncertainty than point-estimate metrics for practical QE decisions.  
- Contribution: proposes Matérn kernels and Warped GPs (tanh warping) to improve uncertainty estimates for QE.  
- Contribution: demonstrates use of predictive distributions in asymmetric risk decision scenarios, showing improved performance.  
- Strength: fills a gap by emphasizing uncertainty in QE evaluation beyond MAE/Pearson’s r.  
- Strength: builds on solid GP theory and proposes well-motivated practical extensions for noisy, positive QE scores.  
- Strength: empirical validation across three language pairs with multiple metrics provides convincing evidence for claims.  
- Strength: clear demonstration of practical utility for decision-making (post-edit vs. retranslate) under asymmetric risk.  
- Weakness: no comparison with modern neural-network QE state-of-the-art, limiting relevance.  
- Weakness: inadequate theoretical justification for preferring NLPD over other uncertainty metrics and little discussion of model misspecification effects.  
- Weakness: ignores GP computational complexity (O(n^3)) and does not address scalability or runtime impacts of proposed extensions.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases and dataset-specific behavior (notably the small en-es dataset).  
- Weakness: overlooks recent probabilistic deep learning methods (Bayesian NNs, ensembles, MC dropout) for uncertainty estimation.  
- Question: how would GP approaches compare to neural SOTA in point estimates and uncertainty, and can hybrid neural+GP approaches be used?  
- Question: why does tanh warping outperform log warping theoretically, and is this QE-specific or generalizable?  
- Question: are warping differences due to dataset size or distribution, and how would results scale to much larger datasets?  
- Question: what strategies do authors recommend to scale uncertainty-aware GPs to industrial-scale QE pipelines?  
- Question: how would using language-specific features or neural embeddings affect the probabilistic models’ performance?  
- Overall recommendation: weak acceptance conditional on addressing comparisons with modern QE approaches and scalability concerns.
