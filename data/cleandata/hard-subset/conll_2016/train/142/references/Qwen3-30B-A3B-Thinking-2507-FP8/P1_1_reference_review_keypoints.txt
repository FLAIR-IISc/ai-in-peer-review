- Paper argues point-estimate QE is insufficient and proposes Gaussian Processes (with Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs) to model predictive uncertainty for MT quality estimation.  
- Introduces NLPD (Negative Log Predictive Density) as a better metric than MAE/Pearson’s r when uncertainty matters.  
- Demonstrates using predictive distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic vs. optimistic) for practical decision-making (post-editing vs. gisting).  
- Shows standard metrics (MAE, Pearson’s r) fail to distinguish models with different uncertainty properties, while NLPD captures those differences.  
- Provides a practical demonstration that uncertainty-aware QE can improve decision-making via Bayes risk estimators.  
- Systematically evaluates GP extensions (Matèrn vs. EQ kernels, log vs. tanh warping) and gives implementation guidance.  
- Strong motivation and clear examples for why uncertainty estimation matters in real QE applications.  
- Convincing empirical evidence that traditional point-estimate metrics are insufficient for uncertainty modeling.  
- The asymmetric loss scenarios section effectively links uncertainty estimates to real-world translation workflows.  
- Thorough comparison of kernels and warping functions with dataset-dependent performance insights (tanh better on larger sets, log on smaller).  
- NLPD improvements are marginal (0.1–0.2), and the paper doesn’t show if such gains yield meaningful real-world benefits.  
- No calibration analysis provided to verify that predicted uncertainties are well-calibrated and thus reliable for decisions.  
- Experimental scope is limited to three relatively small datasets (en-es: 858, fr-en: 2,525, en-de: 2,828), raising concerns about generalization and scalability.  
- No analysis of computational cost or scalability of Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs compared to standard GPs.  
- Lacks guidance on how to set practical uncertainty thresholds for decision-making (e.g., when to forward for post-editing).  
- Question: Why do Warped GPs with tanh perform worse in pessimistic (linex loss) settings — limitation of warping or linex?  
- Question: Please provide calibration plots (reliability diagrams) to show whether uncertainty estimates are well-calibrated.  
- Question: How will model performance scale to larger datasets and other language pairs not included in the experiments?  
- Question: Can you quantify whether NLPD improvements translate to better downstream decision quality (e.g., filtering accuracy for post-editing)?  
- Question: In the pessimistic scenario, are poorer linex results due to warping reducing variance or an issue with applying linex to these datasets?
