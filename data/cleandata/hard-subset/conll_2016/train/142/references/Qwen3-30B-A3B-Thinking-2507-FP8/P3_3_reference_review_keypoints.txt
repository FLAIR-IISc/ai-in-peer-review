- Prediction uncertainty is critical for QE; point-estimate models are insufficient for real-world decisions.  
- Authors propose Gaussian Processes with Matérn kernels and warped GPs to better model uncertainty in QE.  
- NLPD is advocated and demonstrated as a superior evaluation metric that captures uncertainty missed by MAE/Pearson.  
- Predictive distributions can be leveraged to improve decisions in asymmetric risk scenarios (post-editing vs. gisting).  

- NLPD empirically captures uncertainty differences not reflected in traditional metrics (Table 1).  
- Matérn kernels and warped GPs are theoretically and empirically justified to handle noisy, non-Gaussian QE data.  
- Calibrated uncertainty estimates improve performance in practical asymmetric-cost decision tasks.  

- Experimental scope is limited to three language pairs with imbalanced dataset sizes, reducing generalizability.  
- No comparison with other probabilistic approaches (e.g., Bayesian neural networks, ensembles) to contextualize GP advantages.  
- Contradictory result: warped GPs performed worse than standard GPs under pessimistic linex loss without sufficient explanation.  
- Paper lacks discussion of computational/scalability challenges for warped GPs and guidance on choosing asymmetric loss parameters.  

- How will warped GPs with multiple tanh terms scale computationally in large-scale QE applications?  
- Why did warped GPs underperform standard GPs in the pessimistic linex scenario despite claimed benefits?  
- How should asymmetric loss parameter w be determined or dynamically adapted in production?  

- Overall: well-written and theoretically sound paper that advances uncertainty-focused QE (NLPD is a valuable contribution) but needs broader experiments, probabilistic baselines, and clearer explanations/implementation guidance to strengthen its impact.
