- Contribution: Shows NLPD is a better evaluation metric for probabilistic QE models than point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson's r).
- Contribution: Proposes GP extensions for QE (Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs with tanh warping) and demonstrates improved performance across language pairs.
- Contribution: Demonstrates practical use of uncertainty estimates in asymmetric risk scenarios (e.g., post-editing) and deriving Bayes risk estimators without retraining.

- Strength: Strong, practical motivation for uncertainty estimation in QE, especially for post-editing decision-making.
- Strength: Clear, accessible theoretical exposition of GPs, Matèrn kernels, and Warped GPs, with appropriate attention to likelihood assumptions.
- Strength: Convincing experimental evidence that NLPD captures differences in uncertainty modeling that MAE/Pearson’s r miss.
- Strength: Novel, relevant exploration of asymmetric risk applications and methods to use uncertainty in decision-making.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to other probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian Neural Networks, Bayesian SVR); claim that others only provide point estimates is too broad.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of dataset-specific behavior, notably why the small en-es dataset favors log warping while larger datasets favor tanh warping.
- Weakness: Superficial treatment of asymmetric risk results—poor pessimistic performance of Warped GPs (linex loss) is noted but not deeply investigated.
- Weakness: Lacks practical implementation guidance for deploying and calibrating uncertainty-aware QE models in real-world translation pipelines.

- Question: Why are GPs claimed to be uniquely suited for uncertainty estimation in QE, and why was there no comparison with Bayesian Neural Networks?
- Question: Can the authors explain the en-es dataset behavior and how findings generalize to much smaller datasets (e.g., <500 instances)?
- Question: Is Warped GPs' poor performance under pessimistic (linex) loss a fundamental limitation or addressable via warping/uncertainty calibration modifications?

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions; paper is valuable, well-written, and highlights NLPD’s importance despite a narrow GP focus.
