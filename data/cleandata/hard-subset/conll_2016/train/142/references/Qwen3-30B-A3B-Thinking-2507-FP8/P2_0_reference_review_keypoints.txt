- Paper argues that prediction uncertainty is crucial for practical QE and proposes Gaussian Processes (with Matérn kernels and warped GPs) to model it.  
- Introduces Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a metric that evaluates full predictive distributions and captures uncertainty differences missed by MAE/Pearson.  
- Shows how predictive uncertainty can be used in asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic/optimistic) to improve decision-making in QE.  
- Strong real-world motivation: uncertainty estimates help avoid poor post-editing/gisting decisions that point estimates alone can cause.  
- Well-justified novel evaluation: NLPD captures uncertainty modeling differences that point-estimate metrics miss.  
- Practical demonstration: better-calibrated uncertainty leads to improved decisions in asymmetric loss settings.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison showing GP models match state-of-the-art point-prediction performance (MAE/Pearson) — potential trade-off unquantified.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported for observed differences across cross-validation folds.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to other probabilistic approaches (e.g., Bayesian neural nets), so GPs may not be the best choice.  
- Question: Please compare GP point prediction performance (MAE, Pearson) against SVR and neural baselines.  
- Question: Explain why NLPD and MAE show some correlation and when uncertainty estimation provides benefits despite similar point predictions.  
- Question: How would results change using larger/more comprehensive QE feature sets common in state-of-the-art systems?  
- Question: How would the approach handle very small-data QE regimes (e.g., en-es dataset behavior)?  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable contribution but needs stronger comparisons and statistical validation to support claims.
