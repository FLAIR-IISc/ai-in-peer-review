- Traditional QE metrics (MAE, Pearson) fail to capture differences in uncertainty; NLPD reveals these differences.  
- The paper applies established GP techniques (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) to QE with domain-appropriate justification.  
- Predictive uncertainty is shown to be useful for decision-making under asymmetric risk (pessimistic vs. optimistic) using Bayes risk estimators.  
- Strong practical motivation: uncertainty estimates matter in real-world QE workflows like post-editing and can prevent bad translations reaching humans.  
- Compelling empirical evidence that models with similar point estimates can have very different uncertainty properties, with warping improving NLPD more than MAE.  
- Effective demonstration of using uncertainty for asymmetric losses (AL, linex) to guide production decisions.  
- Limited technical novelty: methods are existing GP extensions rather than new modeling contributions.  
- No comparison to other probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian neural nets, Bayesian linear models) to judge whether GPs are the best choice.  
- Explanation for poorer linex performance in pessimistic scenarios (warped models underperforming) is unclear and counterintuitive.  
- Insufficient discussion of GP computational costs and scalability (O(n^3)) for large-scale QE deployment.  
- Question: Provide a theoretical explanation for why point-estimate metrics do not capture uncertainty differences.  
- Question: Explain why log warping helps on the smallest dataset (en‑es) but not on larger datasets (fr‑en, en‑de).  
- Question: Clarify how using the median vs. mean under a Gaussian likelihood interacts with warping and affects quality-score predictions.  
- Question: Supply evidence (e.g., train vs. test NLPD) to support the claimed overfitting for the en‑es dataset.  
- Overall: Well-written, practically relevant paper that merits acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability, alternative models, and several explanatory gaps.
