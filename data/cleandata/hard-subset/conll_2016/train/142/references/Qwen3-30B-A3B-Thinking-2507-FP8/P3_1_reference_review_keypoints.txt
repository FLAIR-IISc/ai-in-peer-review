- Paper investigates prediction uncertainty in Machine Translation Quality Estimation (QE) and argues point estimates are insufficient for decision-making.
- Authors propose Gaussian Processes (with Matérn kernels and warped GPs) and recommend NLPD as a metric to capture uncertainty.
- Demonstrates leveraging uncertainty in asymmetric loss scenarios (pessimistic vs. optimistic decisions).

- Contribution: Shows standard QE metrics (MAE, Pearson) fail to distinguish models with differing uncertainty despite identical point estimates.
- Contribution: Proposes and validates NLPD as a better evaluation metric for probabilistic QE models.
- Contribution: Shows practical utility of uncertainty estimates in asymmetric decision-making, with warped GPs beneficial in optimistic scenarios.

- Strength: Highlights a critical flaw in standard QE evaluation and empirically shows NLPD distinguishes uncertainty-aware models.
- Strength: Connects theoretical uncertainty modeling to practical workflows via asymmetric loss experiments.
- Strength: Uses multiple datasets, 10-fold CV, kernel/warping ablations, and discusses small-dataset behavior, indicating rigorous evaluation.

- Weakness: No comparison with recent neural-network-based uncertainty methods (Bayesian NNs, ensembles), leaving a major gap.
- Weakness: Warped GPs perform worse under pessimistic linex loss, contradicting claimed advantages and lacking deep analysis.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of GP computational complexity and scalability; sparse GP methods cited but not evaluated.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis and mitigation strategies for small-dataset behavior (en-es) differences between NLL and NLPD.

- Question: Clarify why MAE is deemed insufficient when Table 1 shows MAE improvements for warped GPs.
- Question: Explain the counterintuitive worse performance of warped GPs under pessimistic linex loss and the role of variance.
- Question: How do the proposed GP methods compare to state-of-the-art non-probabilistic/neural QE approaches and how could those provide uncertainty?
- Question: Could feature choice (QuEst’s 17 features) affect uncertainty quality, and was feature selection for uncertainty considered?

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on addressing comparisons to neural methods and deeper analysis of linex/pessimistic results before camera-ready.
