- Paper argues prediction uncertainty is crucial in QE and proposes Gaussian Process extensions (Matérn kernels, warped GPs) plus NLPD as a better evaluation metric.  
- Traditional point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson) are inadequate for probabilistic QE; NLPD captures uncertainty differences.  
- Matérn kernels and warped GPs are proposed and validated to relax smoothness assumptions and handle strictly positive quality scores.  
- Predictive distributions are shown useful for asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic vs. optimistic) via asymmetric loss functions.  
- Strong practical motivation with realistic examples illustrating the cost of misestimating translation quality.  
- Well-justified evaluation: NLPD convincingly captures uncertainty information that point metrics miss.  
- Thoughtful model extensions with informative ablation studies comparing warping functions and kernels.  
- Practical demonstration that uncertainty estimates can guide application-specific decisions.  
- Limited dataset sizes (e.g., en‑es = 858) raise concerns about robustness and generalizability.  
- Insufficient comparison to other probabilistic approaches (e.g., Bayesian neural networks, Bayesian linear regression).  
- Superficial analysis of why warped GPs perform worse with linex loss in pessimistic scenarios.  
- No analysis of computational complexity or runtime impact of the proposed model extensions.  
- Overstated generalizability claim that methods apply to any text regression task without supporting evidence.  
- Request evidence that warped GPs reduce probability mass on negative values (e.g., histogram of predictions).  
- Ask for rationale behind chosen asymmetric loss weight values (empirical vs. theoretical basis).  
- Ask which dataset characteristics might explain the weak gains from Matérn kernels.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparisons with other probabilistic models and deeper analysis of asymmetric loss behavior.
