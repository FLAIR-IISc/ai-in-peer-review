- Paper investigates prediction uncertainty in MT Quality Estimation and proposes using Gaussian Processes with Matérn kernels and warped GPs, advocating NLPD as an evaluation metric and demonstrating use in asymmetric decision scenarios.

Main contributions:
- Shows NLPD distinguishes uncertainty differences missed by point-estimate metrics.
- Systematically compares Matérn vs EQ kernels and parametric tanh warping vs log warping for GPs.
- Demonstrates practical use of predictive distributions for asymmetric (pessimistic/optimistic) decision-making without retraining.

Strengths:
- Strong, practical motivation for modeling uncertainty in QE, illustrated with realistic post-editing scenarios.
- Appropriate and well-justified focus on NLPD as a superior evaluation metric for probabilistic models.
- Thorough empirical exploration of GP extensions with useful insights into model choices.
- Clear demonstration of applying predictive distributions to different risk preferences.

Weaknesses:
- No comparison to alternative probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian neural networks, Bayesian SVR), limiting assessment of GPs’ competitiveness.
- Insufficient analysis of how dataset size affects model performance, especially given inconsistent en-es results.
- Inconsistent and under-explained results for linex loss in pessimistic scenarios where warped GPs sometimes underperform.
- No ablation study on warping function complexity (up to 3 tanh terms), risking overfitting especially on small datasets.
- Limited discussion of computational costs of training and deploying the extended GP models.

Questions to authors:
- How do GP-based models compare to other probabilistic approaches like Bayesian neural networks or Bayesian SVR?
- Can you provide experiments or analysis showing performance scaling with dataset size?
- Why do warped GPs perform worse under linex loss in pessimistic scenarios—can you validate the reduced-variance hypothesis?
- Is the two-pass hyperparameter optimization necessary for warped GPs, or would single-pass ARD suffice, and what are the cost implications?
- Why do log-based models perform well with AL loss but not consistently with linex loss in optimistic scenarios?

Overall recommendation:
- Valuable contribution highlighting uncertainty in QE and NLPD use, but requires major revision to address missing model comparisons, explain inconsistent linex results, and provide dataset/ablation analyses.

Additional comments:
- Well-written with a strong section on asymmetric loss that is practically compelling.
- Limited dataset diversity and small sizes reduce generalizability; claims about broad applicability of Matérn/warped GPs may be overstated.
