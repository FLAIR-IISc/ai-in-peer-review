- Paper argues point-estimate QE is insufficient; proposes GPs (Matérn kernels, warped GPs) and advocates NLPD for uncertainty-aware evaluation.
- Shows MAE and Pearson's r fail to capture uncertainty differences while NLPD distinguishes models' predictive distributions.
- Proposes and empirically validates Matérn kernels and warped GPs as better suited for noisy, non-Gaussian QE data.
- Demonstrates practical benefit of uncertainty estimates in asymmetric decision scenarios (post-editing vs gisting).
- Strong motivation with clear, real-world examples illustrating why uncertainty matters in QE applications.
- Novel evaluation perspective: recommends NLPD over traditional point-estimate metrics for QE.
- Practical demonstrations show uncertainty can directly inform decisions under pessimistic and optimistic risk settings.
- Rigorous experimental design: multiple language pairs, multiple metrics, and 10-fold cross-validation.
- Clear theoretical grounding using established probabilistic models and asymmetric loss functions.
- Does not compare GPs to other probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian neural nets, Bayesian linear regression).
- Fails to address GP computational complexity (O(n^3)) and scalability relative to industry-standard SVR approaches.
- Inconsistent warped GP results across pessimistic vs optimistic asymmetric losses are unexplained.
- Limited discussion of practical implementation challenges (model selection, hyperparameter tuning, pipeline integration).
- No comparison of point-estimate performance against state-of-the-art QE systems to assess trade-offs.
- Question: How do computational requirements compare to standard SVR-based QE models and affect scalability?
- Question: Why do tanh-warped GPs perform poorly on linex loss in pessimistic scenarios and when are they preferable?
- Question: Have you evaluated different or larger feature sets beyond the 17 QuEst features?
- Question: Can data augmentation or regularization address overfitting on the smaller en-es dataset?
- Question: How would you integrate uncertainty estimation into production QE pipelines focused on point accuracy?
- Overall assessment: Well-written, timely paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address model comparisons and computational concerns.
