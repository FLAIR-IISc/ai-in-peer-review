- NLPD is shown to be a superior evaluation metric for probabilistic QE, distinguishing models with similar point estimates but different uncertainty behavior.
- Warped Gaussian Processes with tanh-based parametric warping outperform standard GPs and log-transformed approaches for modeling positive QE scores, especially on larger datasets.
- Predictive uncertainty is demonstrated to improve decision-making in asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic vs. optimistic post-editing), showing practical value.
- Strong justification and empirical evidence are provided for using NLPD to penalize overconfident models beyond MAE and Pearson’s r.
- The paper convincingly links uncertainty estimates to real-world translation workflows and business scenarios, improving practical relevance.
- Experimental setup is rigorous: three language pairs, multiple kernels and warping functions, standard features, and 10-fold cross-validation with statistical analysis.
- Methods have limited novelty since Warped GPs and Matérn kernels are established in GP literature and theoretical reasons for their specific suitability to QE are underdeveloped.
- No comparisons are made to other probabilistic approaches (e.g., Bayesian neural nets, Bayesian ridge), weakening claims about GPs being the best uncertainty model for QE.
- Results are inconsistent across datasets (notably the small en-es set); the paper attributes this to dataset size but lacks deeper analysis or mitigation strategies.
- Request for elaboration on why tanh warping is especially appropriate for QE versus other warping functions (polynomial, splines, etc.).
- Ask for evidence supporting the hypothesis that warped models’ lower variance explains worse linex performance in pessimistic scenarios (e.g., variance distribution comparisons).
- Request more analysis on why Matérn vs. EQ kernel choice shows inconclusive gains and whether dataset characteristics (especially en-es) affect kernel effectiveness.
- Overall recommendation: paper is well-written, practically relevant, and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty concerns and comparisons to alternative probabilistic models.
