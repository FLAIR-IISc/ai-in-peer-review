- Paper argues that probabilistic modeling (GPs with Matérn kernels and Warped GPs) better captures prediction uncertainty for MT Quality Estimation than point estimates alone.
- Proposes Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a superior evaluation metric that incorporates predictive uncertainty beyond MAE or Pearson's r.
- Shows that predictive uncertainty can be used directly in asymmetric risk scenarios (e.g., post-editing) to make better decisions without retraining models.
- Demonstrates that models with similar point-estimate performance can have very different uncertainty profiles, motivating uncertainty-aware evaluation.
- Evaluates models across three language pairs (en-es, fr-en, en-de), providing broader empirical support than single-dataset studies.
- Presents clear derivation and application of Bayes risk estimators for different loss functions, with empirical gains in specific decision scenarios.
- Strength: strong motivation and practical examples showing uncertainty matters for real-world QE workflows.
- Strength: convincing evidence that MAE and Pearson's r fail to capture meaningful differences in uncertainty modeling.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental setup across multiple language pairs to support generalizability.
- Strength: effective presentation of asymmetric risk application and concrete decision-making benefits.
- Weakness: limited novelty—Matérn kernels and Warped GPs are standard GP techniques and novelty for QE is not well established.
- Weakness: no comparison to other probabilistic approaches (e.g., Bayesian neural networks), limiting claims about GPs' superiority.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of overfitting and unreliable NLL on small datasets (e.g., en‑es) and lack of mitigation strategies.
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability and practical implementation challenges for large-scale or production environments.
- Recommendation: Accept—paper makes a valuable contribution by focusing on uncertainty estimation and NLPD despite modest model novelty.
- Questions to authors: why focus exclusively on GPs versus other probabilistic models; how to improve uncertainty estimation for small datasets; why Matérn kernels offer limited gains over EQ in QE datasets?
