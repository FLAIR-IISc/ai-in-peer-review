

# Review of "Exploring Prediction Uncertainty in Machine Translation Quality Estimation"

## Summary and Contributions
This paper investigates the importance of prediction uncertainty in Machine Translation Quality Estimation (QE) by focusing on Gaussian Processes (GPs) as a probabilistic modeling approach. The authors propose extensions to standard GP models (Matérn kernels and Warped GPs) and argue that NLPD (Negative Log Predictive Density) should be used as an evaluation metric that better captures uncertainty than traditional point estimate metrics. They demonstrate how predictive distributions can be leveraged in asymmetric risk scenarios.

As I see them, the main contributions are:
1. Application of Warped GPs and Matérn kernels to QE, with empirical evidence that these approaches better capture uncertainty.
2. Introduction of NLPD as a more suitable evaluation metric for probabilistic QE models.
3. Demonstration of how predictive uncertainty can be practically utilized in asymmetric loss scenarios.

## Strengths
1. The paper makes a compelling case for the importance of uncertainty estimation in QE, which is often overlooked in favor of point estimate metrics. The examples of post-editing and gisting scenarios are well-chosen and directly relevant to real-world applications.

2. The empirical results showing that NLPD captures differences in uncertainty modeling that MAE and Pearson's r miss are convincing and well-presented. The authors effectively demonstrate that traditional metrics fail to distinguish between models with different uncertainty properties.

3. The exploration of asymmetric loss scenarios is novel for QE and provides a practical application for uncertainty estimates. The paper clearly explains how different scenarios (pessimistic vs. optimistic) require different approaches to uncertainty handling.

## Weaknesses

1. **Critical technical error in NLPD definition**: The paper incorrectly defines NLPD as "NLPD(ŷ,y) = − 1/n ∑ log p(ŷi = yi|xi)" which is technically wrong. The correct definition should be -log(p(y_test|X_train)), not -log(p(ŷ_i = y_i|x_i)). This fundamental error undermines the paper's central argument about NLPD being a better metric for uncertainty.

2. **Inconsistent terminology and notation**: The paper uses inconsistent spellings of "Matèrn" vs. "Matérn" throughout. It also incorrectly refers to "log(y)" as a warping function when it's not a warping function as defined in Section 2.3 (which requires a sum of tanh functions). This creates confusion about the model architecture.

3. **Unsupported claims about dataset sizes**: The paper claims the en-es dataset is "considerably smaller" without providing actual dataset sizes for comparison. This weakens the argument about overfitting in small datasets, as the other datasets (fr-en and en-de) are reported as having 2,525 and 2,828 instances, respectively.

4. **Lack of justification for median as point prediction**: The paper states "Point predictions were fixed as the median of the distribution" but provides no justification for this choice, especially since the paper later discusses asymmetric loss functions where the mean or quantiles might be more appropriate.

5. **Inconsistent experimental reporting**: For the linex loss with w = -0.75 on en-de dataset, the paper notes "loss values on the order of 10^7" but doesn't explain how this was handled. Instead, it states "we report results using a higher w in this case for completeness," which is not scientifically rigorous.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper uses both "Matèrn" and "Matérn" throughout. Is this a typo or intentional? If it's a typo, please correct all instances.

2. The definition of NLPD provided in Section 3 is technically incorrect. Could you clarify the correct definition and how it was implemented in your experiments?

3. Why did you choose the median as the point prediction for Warped GPs, especially given that you later discuss asymmetric loss functions where the mean or quantiles might be more appropriate?

## Overall Recommendation

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of uncertainty in QE. However, the critical technical error in the NLPD definition, along with the numerous inconsistencies in terminology and notation, significantly undermine the paper's credibility. As a perfectionist reviewer who meticulously examines every aspect of a paper, I cannot recommend acceptance in its current form. The technical errors are too fundamental to be considered minor flaws, as they directly impact the paper's central argument.

The paper's core ideas about uncertainty estimation in QE are important and relevant, but the technical shortcomings require substantial revision before the paper can be considered for publication. I recommend rejection pending major revisions to address these technical issues.
