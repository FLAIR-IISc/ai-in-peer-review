This paper explores the use of Gaussian Processes (GPs) for Quality Estimation (QE), focusing on the modeling of predictive uncertainty. The primary contribution, as I see it, is a thorough empirical evaluation of different GP configurations (kernels, warping functions) and evaluation metrics (NLPD) within the QE context. A secondary, though potentially useful, contribution is the application of asymmetric loss functions to demonstrate the practical benefits of well-calibrated uncertainty estimates in risk-sensitive scenarios.

**Strengths:**

1. **Comprehensive Empirical Evaluation:** The paper presents a well-designed and executed set of experiments across multiple language pairs and datasets. The systematic comparison of different GP models, including Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs, is a significant strength. This offers practical insights into which model choices perform best in this domain.
2. **Justification for NLPD:** The authors convincingly argue for the superiority of Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as an evaluation metric for probabilistic QE models, highlighting its ability to capture uncertainty information missed by traditional point-estimate metrics like MAE and Pearson’s r. This is a valuable contribution to the field.
3. **Exploration of Asymmetric Risk:** The application of asymmetric loss functions demonstrates a practical motivation for modeling uncertainty in QE. By simulating optimistic and pessimistic scenarios, the paper illustrates how predictive distributions can be leveraged for improved decision-making.
4. **Clear and Detailed Methodology:** The paper provides a clear description of the experimental setup, including feature sets, cross-validation procedures, and hyperparameter optimization techniques. This reproducibility is commendable.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Methodology:** While the *application* of GPs to QE and the detailed empirical evaluation are valuable, the core methodologies themselves (GPs, Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) are not novel contributions. The paper largely applies existing techniques to a specific problem.
2. **Overly Detailed and Repetitive Descriptions:** The paper includes an excessive amount of detail regarding the mathematical formulations of GPs and related concepts. While clarity is important, some sections feel overly verbose and detract from the flow of the paper. For example, the repeated description of the mathematical definitions for each kernel type.
3. **Inconclusive Results Regarding Kernels:** The results concerning the Matèrn kernels are not definitive. The authors acknowledge that gains are “less conclusive” and that further experiments are needed, which weakens the impact of this exploration.
4. **Linex Loss Issues:** The inconsistent/inconclusive results when applying the linex loss in the pessimistic scenario are concerning. The explanation provided (variance differences) feels insufficient, and the authors should address this more thoroughly. The fact that the pessimistic linex case required different w values for meaningful results is itself a red flag.
5. **Formatting and Presentation Issues:** There are several minor formatting inconsistencies throughout the paper, including some inconsistent spacing and potentially improper use of mathematical notation (e.g., superscripting). The table captions are too verbose.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the specific reasons for the observed issues with the linex loss in the pessimistic scenario? Are there any potential limitations or biases inherent in this loss function that might explain the results?
2.  Given the limited novelty of the core methodologies, could you more strongly emphasize the practical implications of your findings and the potential benefits of using well-calibrated uncertainty estimates in real-world QE applications?
3.  Can you address the perceived redundancy in the mathematical descriptions? Perhaps condense these sections to focus on the aspects most relevant to your specific contributions?
4.  Would you be able to provide a concise summary of the most important lessons learned regarding the choice of kernels and warping functions for QE? What practical guidance can you offer to researchers or practitioners based on your experiments?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The bibliography formatting appears to be incomplete in places.
*   Consider adding a table summarizing the hyperparameter settings used for each model and dataset.
*   A brief discussion of computational cost associated with different GP configurations would be beneficial.
*   The title could be more concise. “Exploring Uncertainty in QE” might be more impactful.



Despite the strengths of this work, the incremental nature of the core methodology and the presence of several minor flaws (formatting, inconclusive results, excessive detail) lead me to recommend **rejection**. While the paper offers valuable empirical insights and a compelling argument for using NLPD, the overall contribution does not meet the high standards for acceptance at a conference like CoNLL. A major revision addressing the weaknesses outlined above might be considered for future submission.
