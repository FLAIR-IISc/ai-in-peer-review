## Review for "Exploring Prediction Uncertainty in Machine Translation Quality Estimation"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the use of Gaussian Processes (GPs) for Quality Estimation (QE) with a focus on modeling prediction uncertainty, rather than just point estimates. The authors argue that uncertainty estimates are crucial for real-world QE applications, particularly in scenarios like post-editing where informed decisions require confidence levels. They explore extensions to standard GPs (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) and propose using the Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a more appropriate evaluation metric than traditional point-estimate metrics like MAE and Pearson's r. They further demonstrate how predictive distributions can be leveraged in asymmetric risk scenarios using asymmetric loss functions.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Advocacy for Uncertainty-Aware QE:** The paper provides a strong argument for the importance of explicitly modeling and evaluating prediction uncertainty in QE, going beyond simply optimizing for point estimate accuracy. This is a valuable perspective shift for the field.
2. **Empirical Evaluation of GP Extensions for QE:** The systematic comparison of different GP extensions (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) and evaluation metrics (NLPD vs. traditional metrics) provides a useful empirical benchmark for future research.
3. **Demonstration of Asymmetric Risk Application:** Showing how predictive distributions can be used within an asymmetric loss framework offers a concrete application and demonstrates the practical benefits of uncertainty modeling.

**Strengths:**

1. **Relevance and Motivation:** The motivation for exploring uncertainty in QE is compelling and clearly articulated. The post-editing scenario is a strong example of where accurate uncertainty estimates would be highly beneficial. This makes the research highly relevant to practical applications.
2. **Thorough Exploration of GP Models:** The authors demonstrate a good understanding of GPs and explore relevant extensions in a systematic manner. The explanations of Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs are clear and concise.
3. **Sound Evaluation Methodology:** The use of cross-validation and multiple datasets is commendable. The introduction of NLPD as a primary evaluation metric is a significant contribution, as it directly addresses the goal of evaluating probabilistic models. The asymmetric loss experiments further strengthen the evaluation.
4. **Clear Presentation & Organization:** The paper is well-written and organized, making it relatively easy to follow the technical details and understand the main arguments. The structure logically progresses from motivation to methods, experiments, and conclusions.
5. **Practical Focus:** The discussion of asymmetric risk scenarios and the connection to real-world applications demonstrates a focus on practical impact, which is a strength.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Methods:** While the *application* of GPs to QE with a focus on uncertainty is valuable, the individual GP extensions (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) are not novel contributions. The authors acknowledge this, but the paper would benefit from a more nuanced discussion of how their work builds on existing GP literature specifically *within the QE context*.
2. **Inconclusive Results in Asymmetric Risk:** The results in Section 4.4 regarding the linex loss are inconclusive, with the standard GP outperforming warped GPs in the pessimistic scenario. This raises questions about the suitability of the linex loss for this application and could benefit from further investigation and discussion. The reasons cited (variance concerns) feel somewhat speculative and require stronger support.
3. **Feature Set Limitation:** The authors acknowledge the use of a relatively limited feature set for reproducibility. While understandable, it might limit the generalizability of the results. It would be helpful to briefly discuss the potential impact of using more complex feature sets.
4. **Limited Ablation Study:** While the comparison of Matèrn vs. EQ kernels is present, a more comprehensive ablation study exploring the impact of individual components of the Warped GP (e.g., number of tanh terms) would have been beneficial.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on how your work specifically builds upon existing GP research beyond simply applying these methods to the QE task?  What novel insights did you gain from applying these methods to QE data?
2.  Regarding the inconclusive results with the linex loss in the pessimistic scenario, can you provide more detailed analysis or hypotheses as to why standard GPs performed better? Have you explored other asymmetric loss functions?
3.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of features? Do you anticipate different conclusions with a more comprehensive feature set?
4.  You mention that NLPD is a more appropriate metric than MAE and Pearson’s r. However, you still report these metrics. Can you articulate a clearer rationale for *why* you still include them in your analysis, given your assertion about their limitations?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The references are very thorough.
*   The formatting is clean and consistent.
*   The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of computational costs associated with the different GP models.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper presents a valuable perspective on the importance of uncertainty modeling in QE and provides a solid empirical evaluation of relevant GP extensions and evaluation metrics. While the core methods themselves are not entirely novel, the application to QE, the comprehensive evaluation, and the demonstration of practical benefits are significant contributions. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly a more nuanced discussion of the limitations and inconclusive results, would further strengthen the paper.
