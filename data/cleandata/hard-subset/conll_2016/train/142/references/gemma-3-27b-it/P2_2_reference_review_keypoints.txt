- Investigates Gaussian Processes for QE with emphasis on modeling and evaluating prediction uncertainty rather than just point accuracy.  
- Proposes Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a superior metric for evaluating probabilistic predictions.  
- Explores Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs, showing these modeling choices affect uncertainty calibration.  
- Demonstrates leveraging well-calibrated predictive distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios using asymmetric loss functions.  
- Strong real-world motivation (e.g., post-editing workflows) for providing uncertainty estimates alongside predictions.  
- Provides a clear, accessible overview and justification for using GPs in QE.  
- Empirical results show NLPD captures differences missed by standard point-estimate metrics.  
- Practical demonstration includes optimistic and pessimistic scenarios and discussion of linex loss limitations.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Uses a limited feature set (17 QuEst features), which may constrain performance and generality.  
- Dataset size inconsistencies (notably en–es) suggest potential overfitting and limit confidence in some conclusions.  
- Inconclusive linex loss findings point to possible bias toward high-variance models and need deeper analysis.  
- Lacks comparison or contextual discussion with recent neural QE approaches.  
- Asks authors to elaborate on limitations of the chosen feature set and potential effects of richer features.  
- Requests evaluation on larger datasets to confirm NLL reliability.  
- Seeks further analysis of linex loss behavior and strategies to counteract variance bias.  
- Inquires about integrating GP-based uncertainty estimation with modern neural QE systems.  
- Recommends releasing code for reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-motivated, and valuable despite acknowledged weaknesses.
