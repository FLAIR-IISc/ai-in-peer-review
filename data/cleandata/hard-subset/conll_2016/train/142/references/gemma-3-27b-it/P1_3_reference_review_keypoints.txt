- Summary: Uses Gaussian Processes (including Matèrn and Warped GPs) to model uncertainty in MT Quality Estimation, advocates NLPD as an evaluation metric, and shows utility under asymmetric loss scenarios.

- Contribution: Argues for shifting QE from point estimates to calibrated probabilistic predictions for real-world decision making.

- Contribution: Empirically compares GP kernel and likelihood choices (EQ vs Matèrn, Gaussian vs Warped) within QE.

- Contribution: Demonstrates how probabilistic QE supports asymmetric-risk decisions (selective post-editing) via simulated linex and AL losses.

- Strength: Clear motivation and framing emphasizing the importance of uncertainty for MT workflows, especially post-editing.

- Strength: Thorough, well-explained exploration of GP models and relevant kernel/likelihood variants.

- Strength: Strong emphasis on proper evaluation using NLPD, showing point metrics miss uncertainty differences.

- Strength: Practical focus on asymmetric risk scenarios with relevant loss functions (linex, AL).

- Strength: Reproducibility via public WMT datasets and clear feature descriptions.

- Weakness: Empirical gains in point-estimate metrics are modest, questioning the complexity-cost tradeoff for GPs.

- Weakness: Results are sensitive to dataset size and inconsistent across datasets (e.g., en-es), raising generalizability concerns.

- Weakness: Linex loss results are inconclusive and sometimes worsen performance; the offered explanation (lower variance of Warped GPs) is speculative.

- Weakness: No comparisons to simpler uncertainty methods (e.g., bootstrapped SVR or ensembles) to establish baselines.

- Weakness: Limited discussion of computational cost and scalability of GP variants versus simpler models.

- Question: Why focus solely on GPs and exclude other uncertainty approaches (Bayesian NNs, ensembles)?

- Question: Can you analyze the linex loss failures more deeply, especially the relation between predictive variance and linex estimator performance?

- Question: Can experiments with larger datasets be run to test stability and generalizability of results?

- Question: How were Warped GP hyperparameters (e.g., number of tanh terms) selected and how sensitive are results to these choices?

- Question: What are the training and inference time costs for the proposed GP models compared to standard SVR?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and has an up-to-date reference list.

- Additional comment: Consider visualizing predictive distributions across models and scenarios to illustrate uncertainty differences.
