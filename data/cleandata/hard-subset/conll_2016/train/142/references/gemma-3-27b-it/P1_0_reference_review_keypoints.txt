- Paper investigates Gaussian Processes for Machine Translation Quality Estimation, emphasizing modeling prediction uncertainty instead of relying on point estimates.  
- Authors explore Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs and advocate using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate predictive distributions.  
- They demonstrate applying predictive distributions to asymmetric risk scenarios via asymmetric loss functions for practical QE decisions.

- Empirically shows NLPD reveals differences in uncertainty modeling missed by MAE and Pearson’s r.  
- Provides a practical exploration of GP extensions (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) for QE, outlining benefits and limitations.  
- Illustrates how asymmetric loss functions and Bayes risk estimators can tailor QE decisions to application-specific risk preferences.

- Strong motivation and clear problem statement on the need for uncertainty modeling in QE.  
- Thorough, systematic exploration and justification of different GP configurations.  
- Rigorous evaluation using NLPD as a principled metric for predictive distributions.  
- Practical relevance shown through asymmetric loss application examples.  
- Well-written, well-organized paper with clear mathematical notation.

- Limited methodological novelty since GPs, Matèrn kernels, and Warped GPs are established techniques.  
- Experiments limited to three language pairs, reducing generalizability across languages and MT systems.  
- Inconclusive and mixed results regarding the advantage of Matèrn over EQ kernel.  
- Concern about linex loss performing worse in pessimistic scenario, suggesting issues with loss choice or model variance.  
- Lacks comparison to stronger, recent baselines such as neural QE models.

- Why did the linex loss worsen performance in the pessimistic scenario—due to warped GP variance or other factors?  
- Are there plans to evaluate additional language pairs and MT systems, and which ones?  
- Have you compared to recent neural QE models, and can uncertainty modeling offset neural point-estimate strength?  
- How sensitive are Warped GP results to hyperparameters (e.g., number of tanh terms) and was a systematic search performed?

- Related work discussion could include more recent neural QE developments.  
- Figures (e.g., Figure 1) are well-designed and informative.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable, well-written contribution with minor revisions suggested.
