- Paper investigates Gaussian Processes for MT Quality Estimation with emphasis on modeling predictive uncertainty and advocates NLPD as a better evaluation metric than point estimates.  
- Argues that well-calibrated uncertainty is important for real-world MT workflows like human post-editing.  

Main contributions:
- Empirically shows standard point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson r) miss differences in uncertainty modeling between GP configurations.  
- Systematically explores GP variants (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) and offers practical guidance for QE.  
- Demonstrates using predictive distributions with asymmetric loss functions to handle different risk profiles in QE.

Strengths:
- Strong motivation and clear problem framing grounded in realistic post-editing scenarios.  
- Thorough experiments across multiple language pairs with 10-fold cross-validation for robustness.  
- Methodologically sound choice of GPs and thoughtful exploration of kernels/warping.  
- Persuasive argument and empirical support for NLPD as a superior metric for probabilistic QE.  
- Practical relevance shown by applying asymmetric loss functions to translate uncertainty into decisions.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: GP extensions used are established methods; novelty is mainly in their systematic application to QE.  
- Inconclusive and counterintuitive results for linex loss in the pessimistic scenario, suggesting further analysis is needed.  
- Possible overfitting on small datasets (en-es), causing inconsistent results across metrics.  
- Feature set is standard; exploring richer or language-specific features might improve QE performance.

Questions for authors:
- Why might linex loss penalize lower-variance models in the pessimistic scenario—could miscalibrated uncertainties be responsible?  
- Have you tried overfitting mitigations (regularization, data augmentation) for the small en-es dataset?  
- What are the challenges in adapting other probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian NNs) to produce well-calibrated QE uncertainties?  
- Would findings generalize to other QE targets like direct assessment (DA) scores beyond post-editing time?

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and cites relevant literature.  
- Including code or a repository link would improve reproducibility.

Overall assessment:
- Valuable contribution through thorough empirical evaluation and uncertainty analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
