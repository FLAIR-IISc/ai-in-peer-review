- Paper studies Gaussian Processes (GPs) for Machine Translation Quality Estimation (QE), emphasizing modeling prediction uncertainty rather than only point estimates.  
- Authors evaluate Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs and advocate Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a superior metric for probabilistic QE.  
- They demonstrate using predictive distributions with asymmetric loss functions and Bayes risk estimation for decision-making in QE (e.g., post-editing scenarios).  

- Contribution: Highlights the practical importance of uncertainty quantification in QE and argues point estimates are insufficient for human-in-the-loop workflows.  
- Contribution: Provides a systematic exploration of GP variants (Matèrn, Warped GPs) as a practical guide for QE researchers.  
- Contribution: Shows how asymmetric loss and Bayes risk can leverage predictive distributions to improve real-world QE decisions.  

- Strength: Strong motivation and clear problem framing, especially for post-editing and human-in-the-loop use cases.  
- Strength: Thorough, well-explained exploration of GP models and relevant extensions for QE.  
- Strength: Persuasive justification and empirical support for using NLPD over traditional point-estimate metrics.  
- Strength: Concrete, practical demonstration of asymmetric risk application enhancing real-world relevance.  
- Strength: Well-written, logically organized, and accessible despite complex statistical content.  

- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited—only three language pairs, a single MT system, and a fixed feature set—raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: Reliance on 10-fold cross-validation without a held-out test set limits clarity on out-of-sample performance.  
- Weakness: Results with linex loss (pessimistic scenario) are inconclusive; claimed variance-bias explanations need deeper analysis and evidence.  
- Weakness: No comparison to simpler uncertainty estimation methods (e.g., bootstrap SVR), so added value of GPs is not fully demonstrated.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion and empirical detail on computational cost and scalability of the GP variants.  

- Question: Can authors provide variance analyses, diagnostic plots, or statistical tests to support the claim that linex loss favors higher-variance models?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to tanh warp function parameters in Warped GPs—was sensitivity analysis or hyperparameter tuning performed?  
- Question: Please elaborate on computational costs (training time, memory) for the different GP models in practice.  
- Question: Do authors expect the trends to hold with different features or other MT systems, and are there plans to test more diverse scenarios?  

- Additional: References are comprehensive and well-organized.  
- Additional: Recommend adding a brief discussion of GP limitations (computational complexity) and potential remedies (sparse GP approximations).  
- Additional: Suggest including a summary table contrasting the GP variants explored.  

- Overall recommendation: A well-written, insightful paper that makes a valuable contribution to QE; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the raised concerns.
