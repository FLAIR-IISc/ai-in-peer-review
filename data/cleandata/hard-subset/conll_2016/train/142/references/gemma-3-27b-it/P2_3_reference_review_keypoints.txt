- Investigates Gaussian Processes for Quality Estimation in MT, emphasizing predictive uncertainty and decision-making under risk.
- Argues NLPD is a better evaluation metric than point-estimate metrics and demonstrates use of predictive distributions with asymmetric losses.

- Advocates for uncertainty-aware QE and ties it to practical post-editing workflows.
- Empirically compares GP extensions (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) for QE across datasets.
- Demonstrates applying predictive distributions to asymmetric loss functions for different risk profiles.

- QE relevance: shifting beyond point estimates addresses an important gap and practical needs.
- Solid theoretical exposition of GPs and relevant extensions.
- Comprehensive experiments across multiple language pairs with 10-fold cross-validation.
- Clear motivation and practical demonstration (post-editing, asymmetric risk).
- Thorough comparison to relevant baselines and GP configurations.

- Limited methodological novelty: GP extensions used are established techniques; contribution is mainly application-focused.
- Inconclusive kernel comparisons (Matérn vs EQ), suggesting kernel choice may be less critical.
- Problematic/ unexpected behavior with linex loss in pessimistic scenarios requires further analysis.
- Fixed 17-feature set may limit model performance and obscure differences between methods.
- Missing comparisons to recent neural QE models (e.g., transformer-based), weakening benchmarking.

- Why does linex loss behave unexpectedly in the pessimistic scenario; hypotheses about low-variance models performing poorly?
- Given limited kernel impact, are GP benefits mainly from uncertainty modeling rather than kernel choice?
- Have you considered neural QE baselines; what are challenges/benefits of comparing to them?
- How were warped GP parameters tuned, specifically the number of tanh terms?

- Writing is clear and well-organized.
- Well-referenced with good related-work coverage.
- Results presentation could benefit from more visual aids (e.g., predictive distribution plots).

- Overall: valuable contribution advocating uncertainty in QE; solid experiments and application, limited novelty — recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
