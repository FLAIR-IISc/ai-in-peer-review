- Paper investigates Gaussian Processes for MT Quality Estimation, emphasizing the need for calibrated uncertainty over point estimates.  
- Explores GP kernel choices (Matérn, warped GPs) and evaluates using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) alongside traditional metrics.  
- Shows practical benefit of probabilistic QE in asymmetric-risk scenarios (e.g., post-edit vs. discard) using asymmetric loss functions.  
- Main contribution: strong framing and advocacy for uncertainty quantification in QE for real-world workflows.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation of GP extensions across multiple language pairs and cross-validation folds.  
- Main contribution: demonstration that probabilistic predictions improve decisions under asymmetric losses.  
- Strength: clear motivation and relevance to post-editing workflows.  
- Strength: rigorous experimental setup with multiple datasets, consistent features, and 10-fold cross-validation.  
- Strength: sound methodological choices—GPs are appropriate for low-resource QE and uncertainty estimation.  
- Strength: appropriate evaluation metrics; NLPD effectively assesses uncertainty quality.  
- Strength: well-written, organized, and well-contextualized within related work.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—extensions are established GP techniques applied to QE rather than new algorithms.  
- Weakness: linex loss results in pessimistic scenario are inconclusive; explanation (reduced variance from warping) is speculative and needs investigation.  
- Weakness: use of a standard (but limited) feature set may constrain absolute performance and should be acknowledged.  
- Weakness: no comparison to alternative uncertainty estimation methods (e.g., bootstrapped SVR, Bayesian NNs); discussion or justification is missing.  
- Question: can authors clarify why linex loss fails in pessimistic settings—Is reduced variance the main cause or do warped posterior properties contribute?  
- Question: please discuss trade-offs between log and tanh warping functions, especially on smaller datasets (complexity vs. performance).  
- Question: beyond NLPD, are there other calibration or uncertainty metrics the authors considered or recommend?  
- Question: what adaptations and challenges would applying this GP-based uncertainty approach pose for other text regression tasks (e.g., sentiment, readability)?  
- Additional: Figure 1 is helpful; references are comprehensive; minor copyediting advised to remove redundancy.  
- Recommendation: solid paper with valuable contributions—accept with minor revisions.
