- Paper studies Gaussian Processes for Quality Estimation (QE) with emphasis on modeling prediction uncertainty rather than just point estimates.  
- Proposes using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a primary evaluation metric for probabilistic QE models.  
- Compares GP extensions (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) systematically and benchmarks them across datasets with cross-validation.  
- Demonstrates using predictive distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios (asymmetric loss functions) to show practical benefits of uncertainty estimates.  
- Strong motivation: uncertainty-aware QE is relevant for real-world tasks like post-editing where confidence matters.  
- Thorough exploration and clear explanations of GP models and their extensions.  
- Sound evaluation methodology: multiple datasets, cross-validation, and NLPD-focused assessment strengthen the empirical claims.  
- Clear presentation and logical organization that make the technical content accessible.  
- Practical focus: experiments on asymmetric risk highlight application-oriented impact.  
- Core methods (Matérn, Warped GPs) are not novel; contribution is mainly in application and evaluation within QE.  
- Inconclusive results for linex loss/asymmetric risk: standard GP sometimes outperforms warped GPs, with explanations needing stronger evidence.  
- Feature set is limited for reproducibility, which may constrain generalizability of results.  
- Ablation study is limited; more analysis of Warped GP components (e.g., number of tanh terms) would be beneficial.  
- Questions for authors: clarify novel insights beyond applying existing GPs to QE and detail why standard GP beat warped GPs under linex loss or whether other asymmetric losses were tested.  
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity to feature choices and whether conclusions hold with richer feature sets.  
- Questions for authors: justify continued reporting of MAE and Pearson’s r if NLPD is deemed more appropriate.  
- Additional notes: references are thorough and formatting is clean; include more discussion of computational costs of GP variants.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—valuable perspective and solid empirical work, but needs deeper discussion of limitations and inconclusive findings.
