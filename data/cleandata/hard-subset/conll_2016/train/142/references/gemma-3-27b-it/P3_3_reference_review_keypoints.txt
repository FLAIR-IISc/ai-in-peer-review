- Paper studies Gaussian Processes for Quality Estimation, emphasizing modeling predictive uncertainty (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs), advocating NLPD over MAE/pearson, and demonstrating asymmetric-risk applications.  
- Main contributions: strong case for uncertainty-aware QE; systematic exploration of GP extensions for QE; empirical evidence that NLPD captures uncertainty differences missed by point metrics.  
- Strengths: clear motivation and post-editing example; thorough GP technical background; rigorous multi-language experiments with cross-validation and intrinsic/extrinsic evaluations; practical focus on decision-making via Bayes risk; well-organized and readable.  
- Weaknesses: limited methodological novelty (no new GP algorithms); reliance on a small 17-feature QuEst set limits performance and generality; confusing linex loss results where standard GP beat Warped GPs needing deeper analysis; insufficient direct calibration analysis; missing comparisons to recent neural/transformer QE methods.  
- Questions for authors: explain why standard GP outperformed Warped GPs under pessimistic linex loss; discuss NLPD’s limitations and contexts where it may be less informative; potential effects of richer feature sets on results; computational/scalability costs of Warped GPs; sensitivity to the number of tanh terms (I).  
- Additional notes: figures and references are good; consider discussing combining uncertainty with other information sources (e.g., human feedback).  
- Overall: solid, well-executed paper with valuable empirical and practical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
