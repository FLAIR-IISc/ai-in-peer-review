- Paper investigates Gaussian Processes (GPs) for Quality Estimation (QE), focusing on uncertainty modeling and extensions (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs).  
- Argues point estimates are insufficient for decision-making QE; calibrated uncertainty is needed.  
- Systematically evaluates GP configurations on standard QE datasets with intrinsic metrics and an asymmetric-risk application.  

Main contributions:
- Emphasizes importance of uncertainty in QE systems used in downstream decision-making.  
- Provides a comparative evaluation of GP extensions (kernels and warping) tailored to QE.  
- Demonstrates an application using asymmetric loss and Bayes risk to leverage calibrated uncertainty.  

Strengths:
- Clear motivation linking uncertainty estimation to practical MT workflows.  
- Rigorous experimental setup: standard datasets, consistent features, and 10-fold CV across language pairs.  
- Correct emphasis on NLPD as a metric that captures probabilistic model quality beyond point estimates.  
- Comprehensive exploration of GP variants, including Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs.  
- Practical demonstration of asymmetric risk use-cases showing tangible benefits of calibration.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited methodological novelty: GP extensions are existing techniques applied to QE rather than new methods.  
- Dense, technical exposition of GP extensions may be inaccessible; relative benefits vs. simpler baselines unclear.  
- Inconclusive and potentially misleading linex-loss/pessimistic-scenario results; explanation (low variance) is hand-wavy.  
- Lacks comparison to simpler uncertainty methods (e.g., bootstrapped SVR) to justify GP complexity.  
- Insufficient discussion of GP computational cost and scalability trade-offs.  

Questions for authors:
- Why is bootstrapped SVR considered less ideal than GPs for uncertainty estimation in QE beyond calibration concerns?  
- How could the linex-loss inconclusive behavior be further investigated (different parameterizations, predictive distribution analysis)?  
- How does computational cost scale with training size, and what strategies could improve GP scalability?  
- How sensitive are results to using other quality metrics (e.g., human ratings, error tags) instead of post-editing time?  

Additional comments:
- Suggest adding a summary table comparing the GP models (EQ kernel, Matèrn, Warped GPs).  
- Recommend consistent notation (avoid reusing ‘w’ for different meanings).  

Overall assessment:
- Solid, well-motivated paper that meaningfully highlights uncertainty in QE and provides a thorough GP-based evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
