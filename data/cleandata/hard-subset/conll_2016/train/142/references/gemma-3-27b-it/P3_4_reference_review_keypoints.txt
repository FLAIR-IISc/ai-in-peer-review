- Paper studies Gaussian Processes (GPs) for Quality Estimation (QE), emphasizing the value of well-calibrated predictive uncertainty for MT workflows.
- Shows that point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson) miss differences in uncertainty modeling; probabilistic metrics (NLPD/NLL) are more appropriate.
- Explores Matern kernels and warped GPs for QE, providing practical insights into their effectiveness.
- Demonstrates practical use of predictive distributions in optimistic/pessimistic post-editing (asymmetric risk) scenarios.
- Strong motivation and clear problem framing around uncertainty in QE with a relevant post-editing use case.
- Rigorous experimental setup: multiple datasets, 10-fold cross-validation, standardized features.
- Persuasive analysis that conventional point-estimate metrics are inadequate and that NLPD is a better metric for probabilistic models.
- Thorough exploration of GP extensions, offering guidance on Matern kernels and warped GPs.
- Practical relevance shown through downstream asymmetric risk application, motivating uncertainty-aware QE.
- Limited novelty of components (GPs, Matern, warped GPs) — novelty is mainly in the application and evaluation.
- Over-reliance on a single standardized feature set may mask benefits of uncertainty modeling; richer features should be tested.
- Inconclusive or problematic results for the pessimistic scenario (linex loss); needs more investigation or explanation.
- Lacks comparison to modern neural QE approaches (e.g., transformer-based models) for context on point performance and calibration.
- Limited discussion of computational cost and scalability of GPs; practical adoption implications are underexplored.
- Question: Why did linex loss perform poorly in the pessimistic scenario—related to warped GP predictive variance?
- Question: How will these models scale to larger datasets; are sparse GP techniques required?
- Question: How do these GP models compare to state-of-the-art neural QE models in point performance and uncertainty calibration?
- Question: What are practical deployment considerations (hyperparameter tuning, retraining cost) for real-world MT workflows?
- Question: What other applications of well-calibrated uncertainty in QE beyond asymmetric risk scenarios are envisioned?
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written with informative figures and tables.
- Additional comment: Recommend adding a table summarizing computational costs (training/prediction time).
- Additional comment: Include brief discussion of GP limitations (e.g., sensitivity to kernel choice).
- Overall: Solid, valuable contribution with acceptance recommended after minor revisions addressing pessimistic scenario, neural comparisons, and computational/scalability details.
