- Paper investigates Gaussian Processes for Quality Estimation, focusing on modeling and evaluating predictive uncertainty rather than just point estimates.
- Explores GP extensions (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) and proposes Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a preferable evaluation metric.
- Demonstrates using predictive distributions in decision-making under asymmetric risk with asymmetric loss functions.

Main contributions:
- Systematic empirical evaluation of GP configurations for QE uncertainty, offering practical guidance.
- Argues convincingly for NLPD as a more informative metric sensitive to calibration.
- Shows practical benefits of uncertainty-aware decisions via asymmetric risk scenarios.

Strengths:
- Strong motivation and clear problem statement highlighting limits of point estimates for real-world QE.
- Thorough exploration and clear descriptions of GP techniques and extensions.
- Rigorous empirical setup with multiple datasets and 10-fold cross-validation.
- Insightful result analysis, including discussion of metric limitations and dataset size effects.
- Practical relevance demonstrated through application of asymmetric loss functions.

Weaknesses:
- Limited methodological novelty—the value is mainly in empirical analysis rather than new algorithms.
- Restricted feature set (17 features) may limit model performance and obscure uncertainty benefits.
- Inconclusive and problematic linex loss behavior in pessimistic scenarios needs deeper investigation.
- Lack of comparison to modern neural QE approaches or Bayesian variants to contextualize performance.

Questions for authors:
- What is the computational cost of Warped GPs vs. standard GPs as the number of tanh terms increases?
- Have you tried other methods to encourage higher predictive variance (e.g., kernel or likelihood modifications) to address linex issues?
- Any extrinsic evaluation results showing how uncertainty estimates affect downstream tasks like post-editing triage?
- Have you considered Monte Carlo Dropout or Deep Ensembles for obtaining predictive distributions from NQE models and comparing them to GPs?

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written, organized, and references are comprehensive.
- Table 1 should show standard deviations alongside means to indicate result variability.
- Visualizations of predictive distributions for example sentences would improve understanding.

Overall assessment:
- Solid and valuable contribution emphasizing NLPD and practical uncertainty use; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
