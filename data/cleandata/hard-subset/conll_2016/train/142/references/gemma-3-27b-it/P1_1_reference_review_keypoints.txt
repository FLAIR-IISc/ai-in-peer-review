- Paper investigates Gaussian Processes (GPs) for Quality Estimation (QE) in MT, emphasizing modeling prediction uncertainty.  
- Authors argue point estimates are insufficient for real-world MT decisions and propose GP extensions (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) to improve uncertainty calibration.  
- Introduces Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a superior metric for evaluating predictive distributions over point-estimate metrics.  
- Demonstrates use of predictive distributions with asymmetric loss functions to handle uneven misclassification costs in MT workflows.  
- Contribution: Advocates for uncertainty-aware QE and ties it to practical MT scenarios like post-editing.  
- Contribution: Empirically evaluates Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs within QE, providing insights on their effect on uncertainty calibration.  
- Contribution: Shows application of probabilistic QE outputs to asymmetric risk settings, illustrating optimistic vs. pessimistic scenarios.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and relevance, addressing a gap in QE research and connecting to practical workflows.  
- Strength: Thorough and systematic exploration of GP models and relevant extensions.  
- Strength: Strong evaluation framework using NLPD and experiments across multiple language pairs and datasets.  
- Strength: Practical demonstration of asymmetric loss application and thoughtful analysis of optimistic/pessimistic scenarios.  
- Strength: Well-written, structured paper with clear figures, tables, and related work.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—extensions used are established in GP literature and novelty is mainly in application.  
- Weakness: Inconclusive linex-loss results in the pessimistic scenario are not well explained and need deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: Simple feature set may limit model assessment; richer feature engineering could change outcomes.  
- Weakness: Over-reliance on 10-fold cross-validation; absence of a held-out test set risks overfitting, especially for small datasets.  
- Weakness: No comparison to other uncertainty estimation methods (e.g., bootstrap SVR, Bayesian NNs), limiting justification for GP focus.  
- Question: Why are linex-loss results inconclusive in the pessimistic scenario and what further analyses could clarify this behavior?  
- Question: Would more complex or language-specific features significantly improve performance and affect uncertainty estimates?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to training data size and would data augmentation or transfer learning help low-resource cases?  
- Question: What are the computational costs and scalability implications of Warped GPs (e.g., tanh warping) versus simpler methods like SVR?  
- Question: How do the arguments for uncertainty-aware QE and use of NLPD generalize to other ML methods for QE, such as neural networks?  
- Additional comment: Reference list is comprehensive; figures/tables are clear; recommend adding discussion of ethical considerations (bias, translator workload).
