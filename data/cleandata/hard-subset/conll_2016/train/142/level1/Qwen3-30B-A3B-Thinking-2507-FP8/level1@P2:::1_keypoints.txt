- Investigates Gaussian Process (GP) probabilistic models for Machine Translation Quality Estimation (QE) to provide calibrated uncertainty, introducing Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs.

- Proposes NLPD (Negative Log Predictive Density) as a better evaluation metric for probabilistic QE that captures uncertainty beyond point-estimate metrics.

- Demonstrates practical use of predictive uncertainty in asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic/optimistic) relevant to translation workflows.

- Addresses a gap in QE practice by emphasizing the need for uncertainty estimates to inform real-world decisions like post-editing vs. retranslating.

- Shows that NLPD can reveal differences between models with similar MAE/Pearson r but different uncertainty calibration.

- Provides empirical evidence that Warped GPs can improve recall in optimistic scenarios, linking modeling advances to practical gains.

- Lacks comparison with other probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian neural networks, Bayesian linear regression), weakening claims of GP superiority.

- Does not systematically analyze how dataset size affects model performance and the reliability of NLL/NLPD, despite noting possible overfitting on smaller sets.

- Reports inconsistent linex-loss results in the pessimistic scenario where Warped GPs underperform standard GPs, without a clear explanation.

- Asks for experiments analyzing the relationship between dataset size and NLL/NLPD reliability across sizes.

- Requests discussion or comparison of generalization to other probabilistic approaches like Bayesian neural networks.

- Seeks insight into why Warped GPs perform worse under linex loss in the pessimistic scenario and whether this is a model or loss characteristic.
