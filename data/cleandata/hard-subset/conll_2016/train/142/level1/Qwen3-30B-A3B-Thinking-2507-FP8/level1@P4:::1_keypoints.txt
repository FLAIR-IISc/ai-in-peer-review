- Proposes probabilistic QE using Gaussian Processes (Matèrn kernels and warped GPs) and promotes NLPD over point metrics for evaluating uncertainty.  
- Shows NLPD reveals differences in uncertainty modeling that MAE/Pearson miss, and argues it better penalizes overconfidence.  
- Empirically validates Matèrn kernels and warped GPs across multiple language pairs with 10-fold CV, claiming improved uncertainty estimation.  
- Demonstrates practical value of uncertainty estimates in asymmetric risk scenarios (e.g., post-editing) using asymmetric loss functions.  
- Provides a clear theoretical motivation for GPs in QE and explains how kernels/warping affect uncertainty.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive and consistent, with results highlighting NLPD’s sensitivity to uncertainty differences.  
- Good presentation and positioning within related work on probabilistic modeling, asymmetric loss, and QE.  
- Fails to compare point-estimate accuracy against state-of-the-art non-probabilistic QE systems to ensure no sacrifice in MAE/r scores.  
- Claim that Matèrn kernels outperform EQ kernels is not strongly supported; results are mixed and may depend on dataset size.  
- No quantitative analysis of computational complexity or runtime overhead for training and inference of proposed models.  
- Results for the pessimistic linex-loss scenario are inconclusive and warped GPs perform worse than standard GPs without explanation.  
- Lacks a thorough discussion of limitations, including assumptions about warping function forms and the choice of asymmetric loss functions.  
- Requests clarification on computational overhead (training/inference) compared to standard QE systems.  
- Asks for explanation why parametric tanh warping outperforms log warping on larger datasets and why warped GPs underperform with linex loss.
