- Paper argues prediction uncertainty matters in MT QE and that point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson) miss critical decision-relevant information.
- Proposes Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate full predictive distributions instead of just point estimates.
- Shows probabilistic models (Gaussian Processes with Matérn kernels and Warped GPs) can provide better uncertainty estimates.
- Contribution: Introduces NLPD as an uncertainty-aware evaluation metric for QE.
- Contribution: Demonstrates Warped GPs with tanh-based parametric warping outperform standard GPs in uncertainty estimation.
- Contribution: Applies predictive distributions to asymmetric risk scenarios, improving decision-making in post-editing workflows.
- Strength: Identifies an important gap in QE regarding uncertainty with clear practical implications for translation workflows.
- Strength: NLPD convincingly distinguishes models with similar point estimates but different uncertainty characteristics.
- Strength: The asymmetric risk demonstration effectively links improved uncertainty estimates to real-world decisions.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited to three language pairs with relatively small datasets, reducing generalizability.
- Weakness: No comparison against modern neural-network QE approaches, leaving competitiveness vs. SOTA unclear.
- Weakness: Asymmetric risk experiments are somewhat superficial and lack quantified practical implications (e.g., time/cost savings).
- Question: How will the authors address the limited comparison against state-of-the-art neural QE methods?
- Question: Can the authors provide concrete evidence of practical benefits, such as estimated translator productivity gains using their approach?
