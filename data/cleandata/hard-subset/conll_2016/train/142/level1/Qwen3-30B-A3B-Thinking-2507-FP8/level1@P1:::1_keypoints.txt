- Introduces NLPD to evaluate QE models' full predictive distribution, arguing point metrics (MAE/RMSE/Pearson) miss uncertainty.  
- Empirically shows Warped GPs with tanh warping outperform standard GPs and log-transform models for uncertainty calibration across multiple language pairs.  
- Demonstrates that leveraging predictive uncertainty improves outcomes in asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic/optimistic) relevant to translation workflows.  
- Identifies a critical gap in QE evaluation and positions NLPD as a meaningful alternative to traditional point-estimate metrics.  
- Provides clear practical demonstrations where uncertainty estimates aid decision-making in real-world translation tasks.  
- Conducts rigorous experiments across multiple datasets, metrics, kernels, warping functions, and uses 10-fold cross-validation with dataset-size analyses.  
- Core methodology lacks novelty: GPs, Matérn kernels, and Warped GPs are established techniques; contribution is domain application rather than new models.  
- Fails to compare probabilistic models against state-of-the-art point-estimate QE models (e.g., SVR, neural nets), leaving practical competitiveness uncertain.  
- Results for linex loss in the pessimistic scenario are inconclusive; the paper does not explain why Warped GPs sometimes underperform standard GPs.  
- Does not address practical scalability of GPs to much larger QE datasets or production deployment concerns.  
- Requests explanation of when and why standard GPs might be preferable to Warped GPs in asymmetric scenarios.  
- Asks authors to justify the practical value of probabilistic models without comparisons to top point-estimate QE methods.  
- Asks how the approach would scale to millions of examples and what practical solutions would enable real-world deployment.
