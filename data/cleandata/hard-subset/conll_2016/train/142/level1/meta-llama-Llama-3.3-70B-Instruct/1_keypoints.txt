- Use of Gaussian Processes for QE to provide predictive uncertainty estimates.
- Proposal of Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate probabilistic QE using the full posterior predictive distribution.
- Application of asymmetric loss functions (asymmetric linear and linex) to model asymmetric risk in QE.

- Well-motivated case for probabilistic methods, highlighting limits of MAE and Pearson.
- NLPD is well-justified and gives a more comprehensive measure of model performance.
- Novel use of asymmetric losses captures realistic over/underestimate consequences.
- Experiments are well-designed and include comparisons with traditional point-estimate metrics.

- Assumes GP uncertainty estimates are well-calibrated without demonstrating calibration.
- Uses only a single dataset per language pair, limiting representativeness and generalizability.
- No detailed analysis of computational cost or scalability for large-scale applications.
- Lacks deeper discussion of practical implications and deployment of QE systems.

- How will the authors ensure and evaluate calibration of GP uncertainty estimates?
- What are the computational costs and how can the methods be optimized for large-scale translation?
- How do the authors envision applying asymmetric loss functions in real-world translation scenarios, and what are the practical benefits and challenges?
