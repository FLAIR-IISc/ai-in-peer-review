- Main claim: QE should move from point estimates to probabilistic predictions to provide well-calibrated uncertainty for real-world decision-making (e.g., post-editing vs accept).

- Methods explored: systematic adaptation of Gaussian Process variants (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) to QE, finding tanh-based Warped GPs often outperform standard GPs.

- Evaluation argument: advocate using full-posterior metrics like NLPD instead of point-estimate metrics (MAE, RMSE, Pearson’s r) because they capture uncertainty differences.

- Practical demonstration: show how predictive distributions help in asymmetric risk scenarios (post-editing with differing false-positive/negative costs).

- Contribution summary: (1) strong case for probabilistic QE and posterior evaluation, (2) empirical comparison of GP extensions on QE, (3) concrete application to asymmetric risk decision-making.

- Strength — relevance: clearly motivated practical need for uncertainty in QE, with compelling post-editing example.

- Strength — technical treatment: thorough, clear exposition of GPs and extensions applied to QE.

- Strength — metrics: convincing evidence that NLPD reveals differences missed by traditional metrics.

- Strength — presentation: well-structured paper with logical flow from motivation to experiments.

- Strength — empirical validation: multi-dataset experiments (en–es, fr–en, en–de) showing consistent Warped GP gains.

- Weakness — novelty: methods themselves (GPs, Matérn, Warped GPs) are not novel; novelty lies in application to QE.

- Weakness — dataset sensitivity: performance and NLPD behavior vary with dataset size, implying unstable conclusions on small datasets.

- Weakness — linex loss issues: inconsistent/counterintuitive linex-loss results in pessimistic scenario need clearer explanation and analysis.

- Weakness — features: reliance on a fixed, limited feature set; authors did not explore richer, language-dependent feature engineering.

- Weakness — baselines: lack of comparison to modern non-GP QE systems (e.g., neural approaches) limits assessment of competitiveness.

- Questions for authors: explain poor linex-loss results and how GP predictive distribution characteristics cause this behavior.

- Questions for authors: report sensitivity to Warped GP hyperparameters (number of tanh terms) and details of hyperparameter search/optimal values per dataset.

- Questions for authors: given dataset-size sensitivity, recommend minimum data required to reliably evaluate probabilistic QE and NLPD.

- Questions for authors: would Warped GP benefits carry over to neural QE models or via warping their outputs?

- Additional suggestion: include a table of optimal hyperparameters per model and dataset.

- Additional suggestion: provide visualizations of predictive distributions from different models to illustrate uncertainty differences.

- Additional note: references are comprehensive and well-formatted.
