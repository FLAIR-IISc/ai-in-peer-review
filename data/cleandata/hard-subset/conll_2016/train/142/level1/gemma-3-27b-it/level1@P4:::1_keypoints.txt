- Comprehensive empirical evaluation of various GP configurations for QE, emphasizing uncertainty calibration with NLPD.
- Demonstration of leveraging predictive posterior distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios using AL and linex Bayes risk estimators.
- Rigorous experiments across datasets with 10-fold cross-validation and a standard feature set for fair comparison.
- Strong focus on uncertainty calibration, arguing NLPD better captures probabilistic prediction quality than point estimates.
- Practical application showing probabilistic QE benefits for decision-making under asymmetric risks.
- Detailed explanation and comparison of GP extensions (Matérn kernels, warped GPs) and their theoretical rationale.
- Clear, well-structured, and accessible writing and presentation.
- Limited novelty: approach builds on established GP methods and prior QE work (e.g., Cohn & Specia, 2013).
- Inconclusive behavior of linex loss in the pessimistic scenario with no definitive explanation or remedies offered.
- Use of a limited standard feature set may constrain model performance and hide potential differences with larger feature sets.
- Small dataset size (notably en-es) raises concerns about overfitting and unreliable NLL scores.
- Missing comparison to more recent QE approaches with uncertainty estimation (e.g., Bayesian neural nets, MC Dropout).
- Question: why did linex perform poorly in the pessimistic scenario and what remedies could mitigate variance effects?
- Question: were larger feature sets or feature selection considered to assess impacts on GP performance?
- Question: how do results vary with alternative normalization methods for post-editing time?
- Question: what are the computational costs and scalability implications of the GP variants, especially warped GPs with multiple tanh terms?
- Citation style is generally consistent and tables/figures effectively present results.
- Related work discussion is adequate but should be expanded to cover recent QE advancements.
- Suggest including explicit discussion of limitations and future research directions in the conclusion.
