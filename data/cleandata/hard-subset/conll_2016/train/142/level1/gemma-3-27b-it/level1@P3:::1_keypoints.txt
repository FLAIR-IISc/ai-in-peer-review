- Paper investigates probabilistic methods (Gaussian Processes) for Quality Estimation (QE), emphasizing well-calibrated uncertainty and asymmetric risk use-cases.  
- Argues traditional QE evaluation (point estimates) ignores predictive uncertainty and is inadequate for real-world decision-making.  
- Primary contribution: demonstrates standard point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson’s r) fail to evaluate uncertainty quality and recommends Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD).  
- Secondary contribution: explores GP configurations (kernels, warping functions) and their effects on uncertainty modeling.  
- Shows practical applications of uncertainty estimates in asymmetric risk scenarios (post-editing, gisting) and use of Bayes risk estimators to avoid retraining for new loss functions.  

Strengths:
- Tackles an important, overlooked aspect of QE: predictive uncertainty rather than just point predictions.  
- Well-justified methodological choice of GPs, with thoughtful exploration of kernels and warpings and a reproducible experimental setup.  
- Empirical results convincingly show point-estimate metrics miss differences in uncertainty; NLPD is a better indicator.  
- Clear, well-structured writing and presentation with strong motivation and related work coverage.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated via asymmetric risk scenarios and decision-theoretic usage of predictive distributions.  

Weaknesses:
- Exclusive focus on GPs; lacks discussion/justification relative to other probabilistic approaches (Bayesian NNs, MC Dropout).  
- NLPD may be unreliable on small datasets (e.g., en-es), raising concerns about generalizability under data scarcity.  
- Counterintuitive linex loss results in pessimistic scenarios need deeper analysis; possible bias toward high-variance models and limited sensitivity testing of weight w.  
- Small feature set may limit model performance and the ability to distinguish uncertainty estimation methods; larger feature experiments are lacking.  
- Missing detailed error analysis highlighting cases of good vs. poor uncertainty calibration and factors affecting it.  

Questions to authors:
- Why focus exclusively on Gaussian Processes; how might other probabilistic models apply to QE?  
- How robust is NLPD’s superiority across dataset sizes, and recommendations for low-resource uncertainty evaluation?  
- Can you explain the counterintuitive linex loss behavior in pessimistic scenarios and ways to mitigate it?  
- What impact would larger feature sets have on model performance and discrimination of uncertainty methods?  
- Will you release code and data to support reproducibility?  

Additional comments:
- Notation is mostly clear but could use more consistent mathematical symbol usage.  
- Related work is comprehensive but should focus more tightly on the most relevant probabilistic QE papers.  
- Suggest adding visualizations of predictive distributions for representative examples to illustrate model differences in uncertainty.
