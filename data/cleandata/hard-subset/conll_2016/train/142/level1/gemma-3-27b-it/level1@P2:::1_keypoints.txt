- Paper studies Gaussian Processes for QE, emphasizing the importance of modeling and evaluating predictive uncertainty rather than relying on point estimates.  
- Main contribution: empirical evidence that NLPD detects differences in uncertainty modeling that MAE and Pearson’s r hide.  
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates the use of asymmetric loss functions (e.g., for post-editing and gisting) to leverage predictive distributions for better decision-making.  
- Provides a clear, well-structured review of relevant GP techniques (Matérn kernels, warped GPs) tailored to QE.  
- Strength: novel evaluation focus—advocates NLPD for QE and shows it reveals critical differences among models.  
- Strength: practical relevance—framing QE tasks as asymmetric risk problems demonstrates real-world impact.  
- Strength: comprehensive GP overview helps readers understand methodological choices.  
- Strength: robust experimental setup with multiple language pairs, cross-validation, and consistent feature sets.  
- Weakness: narrow model scope—paper focuses almost exclusively on GPs and omits other probabilistic approaches (e.g., BNNs, ensembles).  
- Weakness: some technical sections are overly dense; need more intuition and streamlined presentation.  
- Weakness: linex loss results in the pessimistic scenario are inconclusive and explanations are speculative.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error-level analysis showing where probabilistic models succeed or fail.  
- Question: why were other probabilistic modeling techniques not considered?  
- Question: can authors clarify causes and any remedial experiments for the inconclusive linex results?  
- Question: were specific error types more frequent with particular models or loss functions—any examples?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to the chosen feature set and would larger feature sets change conclusions?  
- Additional: ensure consistent definition of notation throughout.  
- Additional: improve Figure 1 labeling and add a scale for loss magnitude.  
- Additional: include discussion of computational cost of different GP models for practical deployment.
