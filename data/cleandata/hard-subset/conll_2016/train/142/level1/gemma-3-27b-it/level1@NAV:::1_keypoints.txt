- Investigates the importance of modeling and evaluating prediction uncertainty in MT Quality Estimation, arguing point-estimate metrics are insufficient for decision-making.
- Proposes Gaussian Processes (GPs) to produce well-calibrated uncertainty estimates, evaluated via NLPD and extrinsically in asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Main contribution: shows MAE/Pearson’s r fail to capture uncertainty differences and advocates using NLPD for probabilistic QE.
- Second contribution: compares GP configurations (kernels, warping); warped GPs—especially tanh warping—generally outperform standard GPs.
- Third contribution: demonstrates how calibrated uncertainty can be leveraged in asymmetric risk scenarios relevant to real-world QE.
- Strength: clear motivation and well-defined problem framing.
- Strength: thorough experiments across multiple datasets and configurations with clear result presentation.
- Strength: insightful discussion of asymmetric risk and a strong methodological choice to use NLPD.
- Weakness: limited novelty, since applying GPs to QE has prior work.
- Weakness: asymmetric risk analysis is somewhat limited—needs more detailed study of different weighting schemes.
- Weakness: discussion of why linex loss performs poorly in the pessimistic scenario is underdeveloped.
- Question: How would results change with significantly larger datasets and would preferred GP configurations (e.g., Matérn kernels) differ?
- Question: What are the computational costs of the different GP configurations, particularly warped GPs with tanh, and how do they affect practicality?
- Question: What are potential downsides or limitations of relying heavily on uncertainty estimates in real-world MT workflows?
- Additional comment: Include a table summarizing computational training time for different GP models.
- Additional comment: References are extensive and well-formatted.
