- Addresses limitation of point-estimate QE by proposing probabilistic models (GPs) to provide uncertainty estimates for decision-making.  
- Uses Gaussian Processes (including Matérn kernels and Warped GPs) and recommends evaluating with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to capture full predictive distributions.  
- Presents a practical framework for asymmetric risk scenarios using Bayes risk estimators to adapt to different loss profiles without retraining.  
- Makes a strong empirical case that NLPD is a more appropriate metric than MAE or Pearson’s r for probabilistic QE.  
- Excellent motivation framing QE as decision-making and introducing pessimistic/optimistic asymmetric risk scenarios relevant to production.  
- Rigorous, multi-dataset experimental setup that supports the paper’s core thesis and demonstrates practical implications.  
- Limited methodological novelty since the work applies established models rather than developing new algorithms.  
- Benefits from Matérn kernels are marginal/inconsistent, and Warped GPs underperform under pessimistic linex loss without a clear explanation.  
- Reliance on a simple 17 hand-crafted feature set raises questions about generalization to state-of-the-art pre-trained representations.
