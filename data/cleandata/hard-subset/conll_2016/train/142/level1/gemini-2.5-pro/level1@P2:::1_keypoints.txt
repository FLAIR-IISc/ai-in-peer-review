- Proposes shifting QE from point estimates to probabilistic models that output full predictive distributions, emphasizing uncertainty for real-world use.
- Uses Gaussian Processes (including Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs) to model noisy, positive QE targets like post-editing time.
- Evaluates models with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to assess quality of uncertainty estimates beyond MAE/Pearson.
- Demonstrates risk-aware decision making via asymmetric loss (Bayes risk estimators), allowing one model to adapt to different downstream risk scenarios.
- Argues that QE should provide and be evaluated on predictive uncertainty, promoting NLPD as a superior metric for this purpose.
- Shows Warped GPs learn nonlinear target transformations and yield substantially better probabilistic fits (NLPD) than standard GPs.
- Presents asymmetric risk as a practical application that connects predictive distributions to realistic translation workflow decisions.
- Experiments are reproducible and well-designed (public datasets, standard features, 10-fold CV, hyperparameter tuning).
- Methods are largely applications of established techniques (GPs, Matèrn, Warped GPs, NLPD) rather than novel algorithmic contributions.
- Experiments use a small 17-feature baseline, leaving open whether gains remain with large, modern feature sets.
- Puzzling Linex loss result: standard GP outperforms Warped GPs in the pessimistic scenario, questioning the loss or estimator choice and weakening the narrative.
- Asks authors to explain the Linex loss counterintuitive results and whether alternative asymmetric losses might be better.
- Requests intuition on how results would scale with high-dimensional, powerful feature sets and whether NLPD gains would persist.
- Suggests reporting mean-based estimators (optimal for RMSE) in addition to median-based ones to see if Warped GPs improve point predictions under different losses.
