- Main claim: QE needs well-calibrated predictive uncertainty, not just point estimates, to be useful in real-world decision-making.
- Method: Investigate Gaussian Processes (GPs) and extensions (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) to model predictive distributions for QE.
- Metric argument: Standard point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson r) are insufficient; Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) better evaluates full predictive distributions.
- Practical contribution: Use GP predictive distributions with Bayes risk estimators to make optimal decisions under asymmetric costs without retraining for each loss.
- Empirical finding: Warped GPs better capture non-negative quality labels (e.g., post-editing time) and improve calibration visible in NLPD but not MAE.
- Strength: High relevance and practical impact—ties QE to real translation workflows and decision-making.
- Strength: Methodologically sound evaluation—robust experiments, multiple datasets, and dual evaluation (traditional + NLPD).
- Strength: Novel, insightful treatment of asymmetric risk and analysis of differing loss behaviors (AL vs. linex).
- Weakness: Limited model/feature scope—focuses only on GPs and a small 17-feature set, unclear generalization to modern neural QE approaches.
- Weakness: Matérn kernel results are inconclusive/marginal, weakening that modeling contribution.
- Weakness: Asymmetric loss weights are illustrative; paper lacks grounding/elicitation of real-world cost parameters for deployment.
- Questions raised: Does linex loss unjustly favor poorly calibrated high-variance models, and what are the practical barriers to adopting NLPD in shared tasks (e.g., requirement for probabilistic outputs)?
