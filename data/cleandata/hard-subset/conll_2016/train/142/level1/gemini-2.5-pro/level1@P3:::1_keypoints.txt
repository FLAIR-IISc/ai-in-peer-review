- Argues and empirically validates shifting QE evaluation from point estimates to probabilistic assessment using NLPD.  
- Proposes using Bayes risk estimators on probabilistic QE outputs to handle asymmetric decisions without retraining.  
- Provides a thorough empirical comparison of GP configurations (kernels and likelihoods) for QE.  
- Strong motivation and problem formulation: reframes QE as a probabilistic prediction problem for practical decision-making.  
- Sound methodology: GPs, Matérn kernels, Warped GPs and NLPD are well-justified and evaluated both intrinsically and extrinsically.  
- Clear experimental results: Warped GPs improve NLPD despite similar MAE, showing limits of point-estimate metrics; asymmetric risk analysis is insightful.  
- High-quality presentation: paper is well-written, clear, and logically structured.  
- Limited methodological novelty: work applies established methods and should explicitly state it is primarily an application/evaluation study.  
- Uses an outdated feature-based QE paradigm (17 handcrafted features) without connecting to modern neural QE approaches.  
- Lacks comparison to simpler uncertainty baselines (e.g., bagged SVRs or bootstrap ensembles) evaluated with NLPD.  
- Superficial analysis of a key negative result: insufficient investigation into why Warped GPs perform poorly under the pessimistic linex loss.  
- Question: How do these methods and evaluation principles transfer to end-to-end neural QE models (e.g., MC Dropout, Bayesian NNs, Deep GPs)?  
- Question: Could the standard GP’s high variance be “gaming” the pessimistic linex loss, suggesting linex may not be robust across models with different likelihood assumptions?  
- Question: Why omit an empirical comparison with bootstrapping/ensemble-based uncertainty baselines (e.g., bagging SVRs) on NLPD?
