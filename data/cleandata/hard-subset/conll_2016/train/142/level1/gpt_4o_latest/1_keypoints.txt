- Proposes probabilistic QE using Gaussian Processes to provide calibrated uncertainty estimates rather than only point predictions.  
- Introduces Matèrn kernels and Warped Gaussian Processes to handle noisy, skewed, non-Gaussian QE response variables.  
- Advocates evaluating QE with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to capture quality of uncertainty estimates missed by MAE/Pearson.  
- Demonstrates utility of predictive distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios (post-editing, gisting) using asymmetric loss functions.  
- Shows comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple datasets, cross-validation, and metrics supporting probabilistic approaches.  
- Provides theoretical grounding on Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs, and Bayes risk estimators for asymmetric losses.  
- Highlights practical industry relevance through exploration of asymmetric-risk workflows.  
- Strength: Novel focus on uncertainty modeling in QE, addressing an important gap for real-world applications.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments convincingly show advantages of uncertainty-aware models.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion and no proposed solutions for GP scalability on large datasets or high-dimensional features.  
- Weakness: Claims of generalizability to other text regression tasks lack supporting experiments or examples.  
- Weakness: Inconclusive and inconsistent linex-loss results in pessimistic scenarios, raising questions about model suitability there.  
- Asks authors to clarify plans for GP scalability, provide demonstration on other text regression tasks, and explain/rescue linex-loss issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept, conditional on addressing scalability concerns and clarifying generalizability.
