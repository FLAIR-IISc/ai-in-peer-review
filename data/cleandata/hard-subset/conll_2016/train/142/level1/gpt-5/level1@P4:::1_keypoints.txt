- Study proposes uncertainty-aware QE for MT, evaluating full predictive distributions (GPs, Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) with NLPD and decision-making under asymmetric risk.

- Contribution: Empirically shows Warped GPs and Matérn kernels yield better-calibrated predictive distributions (lower NLPD) than standard GPs, differences not captured by MAE or Pearson’s r.

- Contribution: Demonstrates transforming probabilistic model predictions into risk-sensitive decisions via Bayes estimators for asymmetric linear and linex losses, avoiding retraining.

- Contribution: Argues training NLL can be misleading on small data and advocates test-time NLPD as the preferred uncertainty-aware metric.

- Strength: Problem framing is clear and practically motivated, with realistic asymmetric-risk QE scenarios.

- Strength: Methodology is technically sound, using established GP/Warped GP formulations, kernel choices, ARD, and optimization appropriate for small data.

- Strength: Evaluation goes beyond point metrics using NLPD, showing Warped GPs reduce mass on negative outcomes and improve calibration for positive-only targets.

- Strength: Shows practical deployment benefit—switching risk preferences via estimators (quantiles/closed-form) without retraining.

- Strength: Multi-dataset, consistent protocol (three language pairs, 10-fold CV, fixed features) strengthens empirical claims.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—GPs, Warped GPs, and NLPD are established; main novelty is systematic QE application and asymmetric-loss demonstration.

- Weakness: Narrow baselines—omits quantile regression, Bayesian linear models, ensembles, Student-t GPs, and Bayesian neural baselines.

- Weakness: Calibration analysis incomplete—lacks PIT histograms, interval coverage, calibration curves, or CRPS to substantiate calibration claims.

- Weakness: Lacks statistical rigor—no confidence intervals or significance tests for differences across metrics or folds.

- Weakness: Methodological clarity missing for asymmetric estimators under Warped GPs—no details on computing y-space quantiles or linex expectations and their costs/accuracy.

- Question: How are AL quantiles computed under Warped GPs—numerical inversion in y-space or approximations in latent space; what solvers/tolerances and costs?

- Question: For linex under Warped GPs, are µy and σ2y approximated numerically (e.g., Gauss-Hermite), and how is applying Gaussian closed-form justified for non-Gaussian y posteriors?

- Question: Can the authors provide calibration diagnostics (predictive interval coverage, PIT histograms) to complement NLPD?

- Question: How do the models compare to quantile regression (SVR/GBM) and Student-t likelihood GPs for asymmetric losses and heavy-tailed noise?

- Question: Are NLPD/AL/linex differences statistically significant across folds—please report mean±std and tests?

- Question: Is there heteroscedasticity in residuals, and were heteroscedastic GPs considered to capture input-dependent noise?
