- Argues QE should provide calibrated predictive uncertainty (not just point estimates) to support real workflow decisions.
- Instantiates Gaussian Process models (Matern kernels and Warped GPs) and evaluates predictive distributions using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD).
- Shows how GP posteriors enable posterior-based decision-making for asymmetric risks via Bayes estimators for AL and linex losses.
- Experiments on three QE datasets indicate warping substantially improves likelihood-based metrics and NLPD uncovers differences hidden by MAE/Pearson’s r.

- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that predictive-distribution evaluation (NLPD) is more informative than point metrics for model selection and diagnosing overfitting.
- Contribution: Practical instantiation of posterior-based decision-making for asymmetric risk using Bayes estimators on GP posteriors, avoiding retraining.
- Contribution: Application-focused analysis of GP design choices (Matern vs EQ, warping vs log) providing actionable guidance for QE uncertainty modeling.

- Strength: Workflow-grounded motivation with realistic asymmetric decision scenarios (post-editing vs gisting) showing why uncertainty matters.
- Strength: Principled probabilistic treatment using GPs and NLPD as a strictly proper scoring rule for calibration and sharpness.
- Strength: Solid empirical evidence across three language pairs and 10-fold CV that warping improves NLL/NLPD; highlights small-data NLL pitfalls.
- Strength: Actionable decision layer demonstrating how Bayes estimators adjust to changing risk without retraining.
- Strength: Honest analysis of failure modes, noting linex pessimistic settings may favor higher-variance models.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—Warped GPs and NLPD are established; mainly an application to QE.
- Weakness: Narrow baselines—no comparisons to modern neural QE or uncertainty methods (deep ensembles, Bayesian nets).
- Weakness: Thin calibration analysis beyond NLPD; lacks PITs, reliability diagrams, or coverage diagnostics.
- Weakness: Feature and dataset limitations—uses 17 QuEst features and older/small datasets, raising scalability concerns.
- Weakness: Sparse exploration of GP variants—no heteroscedastic likelihoods or sparse GP approximations; kernel conclusions tentative.

- Question: Can you report calibration diagnostics (PIT histograms, interval coverage) alongside NLPD?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the number/parameterization of warping terms, and is regularization used to avoid overfitting?
- Question: Why not compare to quantile regression baselines for AL or deep ensemble/Bayesian neural QE for uncertainty?
- Question: Would heteroscedastic or input-dependent noise models mitigate the linex pessimistic failure case?
