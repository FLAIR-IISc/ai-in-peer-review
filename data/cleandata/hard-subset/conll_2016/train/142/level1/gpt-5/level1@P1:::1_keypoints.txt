- Paper argues point-estimate QE is limited for real workflows and advocates uncertainty-aware modeling/evaluation (Warped GPs, Matérn kernels) and using predictive densities (NLPD) plus Bayes estimators for asymmetric decision-making.
- Contribution 1: Proposes uncertainty-aware evaluation for QE using NLPD and calibrated predictive distributions, showing MAE/r can miss predictive uncertainty differences.
- Contribution 2: Adapts and validates GP variants (Warped GPs with learned tanh warpings, Matérn kernels with ARD) to better fit positive-valued QE targets and improve predictive densities.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates asymmetric-risk decision-making via Bayes estimators from predictive posteriors (quantiles for asymmetric linear loss; closed-form for linex) to adapt to changing costs without retraining.
- Strength: Clear, practical motivation showing uncertainty matters for production QE tasks (post-editing filtering, gisting) and that point metrics are insufficient.
- Strength: Principled probabilistic modeling choices (Warped GPs, Matérn kernels) that empirically reduce NLL/NLPD and better match target support/smoothness.
- Strength: Uncertainty-centric evaluation using NLL and out-of-sample NLPD, with useful guidance about NLL overfitting on small data and MAE/r failing to capture uncertainty.
- Strength: Decision-layer decoupling via Bayes estimators is pragmatic and shows warped models can yield better downstream decisions under optimistic scenarios.
- Strength: Experimental setup (datasets, 17 QuEst features, CV protocol, GP training) is described clearly enough for rough reproducibility.
- Weakness: Limited baselines — excludes other uncertainty-capable methods (quantile regression, Bayesian linear/kernel models, heteroscedastic/Student-t GPs, ensembles), limiting claims.
- Weakness: Calibration analysis is thin — lacks PIT, interval coverage, CRPS, and separate sharpness diagnostics to support "well-calibrated" claims.
- Weakness: Asymmetric-risk evaluation incomplete and sometimes inconclusive (pessimistic linex results unexplained, w values ad hoc, no mapping from workflow costs to loss parameters).
- Weakness: Small/dated feature set (17 QuEst) may not generalize to modern QE setups with richer/neural features or other target labels (HTER, DA) and document-level QE.
- Weakness: Statistical reporting is weak — no significance tests, effect sizes, variability across folds, or systematic hyperparameter/ablation studies (e.g., ν for Matérn).
- Weakness: Missing reproducibility/implementation details (priors/constraints on hyperparams, warping regularization, noise model choice, optimization stability, computational costs).
- Question: Did you assess calibration explicitly (posterior interval coverage, PIT, CRPS) to show warped GPs are better calibrated beyond likelihood gains?
- Question: How should users set w in AL/linex from operational costs — can you map realistic QE workflows to loss parameters and show sensitivity?
- Question: Can you quantify predictive variance and tail coverage differences across models to explain pessimistic linex behavior, and would heavier-tailed or heteroscedastic GPs help?
- Question: Did you compare to quantile regression (linear/SVR) for AL scenarios, Student-t GPs, or heteroscedastic GPs as baselines?
- Question: Do results generalize to other QE labels (HTER, DA), richer features/neural encoders, or word/document-level QE?
- Additional comment: Report CRPS alongside NLPD to capture calibration and sharpness in a single robust score.
- Additional comment: Include runtime and memory costs since latency matters for online QE deployment.
- Additional comment: Clarify whether MAE/r point predictions use medians and how median vs mean choices affect correlations under non-Gaussian posteriors.
