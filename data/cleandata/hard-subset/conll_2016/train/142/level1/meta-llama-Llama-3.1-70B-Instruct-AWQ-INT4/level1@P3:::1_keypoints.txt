- Proposes a probabilistic QE approach using Gaussian Processes to model prediction uncertainty.  
- Evaluated on three language pairs, claiming improved uncertainty estimation and decision-making.  
- Introduces Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as an uncertainty-aware evaluation metric.  
- Novel contribution: applying GPs for uncertainty-aware machine translation quality estimation.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across three language pairs with detailed result analysis.  
- NLPD is a useful tool for assessing probabilistic QE models.  
- Limited comparisons: does not benchmark against other probabilistic methods.  
- No analysis of how GP hyperparameters affect performance.  
- Insufficient discussion of practical, real-world applications and integration into MT workflows.  
- Asks for comparison to other probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian neural networks).  
- Requests analysis of GP hyperparameter impact on results.  
- Requests more details on real-world usage and deployment scenarios.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and valuable contribution despite noted limitations.
