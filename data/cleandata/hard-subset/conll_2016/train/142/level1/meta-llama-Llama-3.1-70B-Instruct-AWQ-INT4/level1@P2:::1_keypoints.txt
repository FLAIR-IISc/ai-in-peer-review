- Proposes using Gaussian Processes for Quality Estimation to produce well-calibrated predictive uncertainty.
- Evaluates models with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to account for full posterior predictive distributions.
- Investigates applying predictive distributions to asymmetric risk scenarios with different costs for over- vs underestimation.
- Main contributions: proposing probabilistic QE models (GPs), using NLPD for evaluation, and demonstrating asymmetric-risk applications.
- Strength: novel probabilistic approach that yields more informative, uncertainty-aware predictions.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including NLPD, MAE, and Pearson’s r.
- Strength: practical demonstration of utility in realistic asymmetric risk cases.
- Weakness: experiments cover only three language pairs, limiting representativeness.
- Weakness: small en–es dataset may cause overfitting and limit generalizability.
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of model hyperparameters could affect reported performance.
- Question: how will the authors address overfitting on small datasets?
- Question: can the authors provide more details on their hyperparameter selection and tuning?
- Question: how do the authors plan to scale or adapt the approach to additional language pairs and datasets?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written but would benefit from broader datasets/language pairs and more analysis of hyperparameters and overfitting.
