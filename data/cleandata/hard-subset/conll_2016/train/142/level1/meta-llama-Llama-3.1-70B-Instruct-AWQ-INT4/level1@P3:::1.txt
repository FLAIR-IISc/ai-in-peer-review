**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Exploring Prediction Uncertainty in Machine Translation Quality Estimation" proposes a new approach to machine translation quality estimation (QE) by incorporating uncertainty estimates into the prediction process. The authors argue that traditional point estimate metrics are not sufficient for QE, as they do not take into account the uncertainty of the predictions. To address this, they propose the use of probabilistic methods, specifically Gaussian Processes (GPs), to model uncertainty in QE. The authors evaluate their approach using three different language pairs and show that their method can provide better performance in terms of uncertainty estimation and decision-making.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Proposal of a probabilistic approach to QE**: The authors propose the use of GPs to model uncertainty in QE, which is a novel approach in the field.
2. **Evaluation of uncertainty estimation**: The authors evaluate their approach using three different language pairs and show that their method can provide better performance in terms of uncertainty estimation.
3. **Introduction of a new evaluation metric**: The authors introduce the Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) metric, which takes into account the uncertainty of the predictions.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to QE**: The authors propose a new approach to QE that incorporates uncertainty estimates into the prediction process, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Thorough evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach using three different language pairs and provide a detailed analysis of the results.
3. **Introduction of a new evaluation metric**: The authors introduce the NLPD metric, which is a useful tool for evaluating the performance of probabilistic models in QE.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited comparison to other methods**: The authors only compare their approach to traditional point estimate metrics and do not provide a comparison to other probabilistic methods.
2. **Lack of analysis of the impact of hyperparameters**: The authors do not provide an analysis of the impact of hyperparameters on the performance of their approach.
3. **Limited discussion of the practical applications**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of the practical applications of their approach and how it can be used in real-world scenarios.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a comparison of your approach to other probabilistic methods, such as Bayesian neural networks?
2. How do the hyperparameters of the GP model affect the performance of your approach?
3. Can you provide more details on how your approach can be used in real-world scenarios, such as in machine translation workflows?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed approach. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach using three different language pairs, which is a significant contribution to the field. However, there are some limitations to the paper, including the lack of comparison to other probabilistic methods and the limited analysis of the impact of hyperparameters. Overall, the paper is a valuable contribution to the field of machine translation quality estimation.
