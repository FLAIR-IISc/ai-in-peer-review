**Summary of the Paper**

This paper explores the use of probabilistic methods for machine translation quality estimation (QE), specifically Gaussian Processes (GPs), to provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates. The authors argue that traditional point estimate metrics do not capture the uncertainty of QE models, which can lead to poor decision-making in real-world translation environments. They propose the use of Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a metric to evaluate the performance of QE models, which takes into account the full posterior predictive distribution. The authors also demonstrate the usefulness of predictive distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios, where the cost of overestimation and underestimation are different.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Probabilistic QE models**: The authors propose the use of GPs for QE, which can provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates. They also explore various extensions of GPs, such as Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs, to improve the performance of QE models.
2. **NLPD as a evaluation metric**: The authors propose the use of NLPD as a metric to evaluate the performance of QE models, which takes into account the full posterior predictive distribution.
3. **Asymmetric risk scenarios**: The authors demonstrate the usefulness of predictive distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios, where the cost of overestimation and underestimation are different.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel application of GPs**: The authors apply GPs to QE, which is a novel application of this method. They also explore various extensions of GPs to improve the performance of QE models.
2. **Use of NLPD as a evaluation metric**: The authors propose the use of NLPD as a metric to evaluate the performance of QE models, which takes into account the full posterior predictive distribution. This is a more comprehensive evaluation metric than traditional point estimate metrics.
3. **Demonstration of usefulness in asymmetric risk scenarios**: The authors demonstrate the usefulness of predictive distributions in asymmetric risk scenarios, which is a common scenario in real-world translation environments.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited experimental evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their proposed methods on three datasets, which may not be representative of all QE scenarios.
2. **Lack of comparison to other probabilistic methods**: The authors only compare their proposed methods to traditional point estimate methods, but do not compare them to other probabilistic methods, such as Bayesian neural networks.
3. **Limited discussion of computational complexity**: The authors do not discuss the computational complexity of their proposed methods, which may be a concern for large-scale QE applications.
