- Summary: Investigates probabilistic MTQE using Gaussian Processes (Matérn, Warped GPs) to obtain calibrated uncertainty, evaluated with NLPD and applied to asymmetric risk scenarios.

- Contribution 1: Proposes GP-based probabilistic MTQE models that provide uncertainty estimates beyond point predictions.

- Contribution 2: Uses Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate full posterior predictive distributions rather than only point metrics.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates the value of probabilistic MTQE in asymmetric risk settings where over- and underestimation have different costs.

- Strength 1: Provides a comprehensive evaluation using multiple metrics (NLPD, MAE, Pearson’s r).

- Strength 2: Shows applicability to real-world scenarios such as post-editing and gisting.

- Strength 3: Supplies solid theoretical background and detailed explanation of GPs and their extensions.

- Weakness 1: Limited experimental setup — only three language pairs and few datasets.

- Weakness 2: Potential overfitting issues, particularly on smaller datasets.

- Weakness 3: Missing comparisons to state-of-the-art MTQE models, making impact unclear.

- Question 1: How will overfitting be addressed, especially for small datasets?

- Question 2: Can you provide a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art MTQE methods?

- Question 3: How will the approach be extended to additional language pairs and datasets?
