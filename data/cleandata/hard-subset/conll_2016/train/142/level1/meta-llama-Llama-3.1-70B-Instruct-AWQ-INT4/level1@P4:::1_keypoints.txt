- Paper explores Gaussian Processes and extensions (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) for Machine Translation Quality Estimation to obtain calibrated uncertainty estimates.
- Models are evaluated using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) and applied to asymmetric risk scenarios.

- Contribution: Introduces probabilistic GP-based methods to QE, a novel direction for the field.
- Contribution: Advocates NLPD as a more comprehensive evaluation metric than point-estimate metrics.
- Contribution: Demonstrates model utility under asymmetric costs of over/underestimation.

- Strength: Novel probabilistic approach that yields informative uncertainty estimates beyond point predictions.
- Strength: Use of NLPD provides a fuller assessment of predictive distributions.
- Strength: Practical demonstration of benefits in asymmetric risk scenarios.

- Weakness: Limited experimentation across kernel types and weighting schemes may reduce generality.
- Weakness: Inconclusive linex loss results in the pessimistic scenario need further investigation.
- Weakness: Lack of detailed discussion on computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.

- Question: Please provide details on computational complexity and strategies for scaling the models.
- Question: How will you address the inconclusive linex loss findings in the pessimistic scenario?
- Question: Can you include more experiments with varied kernels and weighting schemes to test robustness?
