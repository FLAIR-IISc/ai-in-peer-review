- Proposes Gaussian Processes (GPs) for QE to provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates.  
- Argues traditional point estimates miss model uncertainty and can harm real-world decisions.  
- Introduces Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as an evaluation metric using the full posterior predictive distribution.  
- Shows predictive distributions are useful in asymmetric risk scenarios with different costs for over/underestimation.  
- Contribution: applies GPs and GP extensions (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) to QE.  
- Contribution: advocates NLPD as a more comprehensive QE evaluation metric.  
- Contribution: demonstrates value of predictive distributions for asymmetric risk handling.  
- Strength: novel application of GPs to QE and exploration of GP variants.  
- Strength: NLPD provides a more complete evaluation than point-estimate metrics.  
- Strength: Practical demonstration of benefits in asymmetric risk contexts.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to only three datasets.  
- Weakness: No comparison to other probabilistic approaches (e.g., Bayesian neural networks).  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability concerns.  
- Question: How will methods scale to larger QE tasks and address computational complexity?  
- Question: Please provide detailed experimental settings (hyperparameters, optimization iterations).  
- Question: How do the authors plan to compare to other probabilistic methods like Bayesian neural networks?
