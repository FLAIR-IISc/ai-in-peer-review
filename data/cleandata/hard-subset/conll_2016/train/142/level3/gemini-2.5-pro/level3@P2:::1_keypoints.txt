- Paper applies probabilistic models (Gaussian Processes, Warped GPs, Matèrn kernels) to Machine Translation Quality Estimation, arguing uncertainty-aware outputs are needed for real-world decisions.  
- Advocates evaluating full predictive distributions using metrics like NLPD/NLL rather than only point estimates (MAE, Pearson).  
- Demonstrates using probabilistic predictions to derive optimal decision rules under asymmetric loss without retraining.  
- Strong motivation: framing QE as needing calibrated uncertainty for production/post-editing workflows.  
- Clear and accessible exposition of GPs, Matèrn kernels, and Warped GPs suitable for a broad NLP audience.  
- Well-designed asymmetric risk experiments distinguishing “pessimistic” and “optimistic” settings and showing practical benefits.  
- Limited methodological novelty: relies on established GP techniques rather than introducing new models.  
- Missing contextual baselines: no comparison to strong non-probabilistic methods (e.g., SVR), making point-estimate competitiveness unclear.  
- Interpretation of NLL/NLPD improvements is underexplained; lacks qualitative examples to show what constitutes a meaningful gain.  
- Question: clarify the specific trend difference for en-es versus fr-en and en-de regarding NLL/NLPD and the claimed overfitting.  
- Question: justify choosing post-editing time over HTER and comment on whether findings generalize to HTER prediction.  
- Minor issues: typo in Section 4 ("over and underestimates"), color mismatch in Figure 1 caption, and request to cite the GP software/library for reproducibility.
