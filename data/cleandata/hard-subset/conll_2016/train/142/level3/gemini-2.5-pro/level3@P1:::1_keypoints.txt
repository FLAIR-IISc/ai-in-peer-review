- Argues QE should model predictive uncertainty (using GPs) because point estimates lack decision-making information for real-world use.
- Investigates GP variants (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) to better capture quality-score distributions.
- Advocates evaluating QE with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to assess full predictive distributions rather than only MAE.
- Demonstrates using probabilistic predictions for asymmetric-risk decision-making via Bayes risk estimators for AL and linex losses.
- Reframes QE as probabilistic modeling and risk management, showing practical value in pessimistic vs. optimistic MT workflows.
- Empirically compares GP models with NLPD, highlighting limitations of point-estimate metrics like MAE and Pearson’s r for uncertainty.
- Strong motivation and clear framing that emphasizes the necessity of uncertainty-aware QE models for practical applications.
- Thorough and practical analysis of asymmetric risk, using decision theory to adapt a single model to different loss scenarios without retraining.
- Clear, accessible exposition of GPs, Matèrn kernels, and Warped GPs, with justified modeling choices for noisy and non-Gaussian scores.
- Limited technical novelty: applies existing methods (Warped GPs, NLPD, Bayes estimators) rather than proposing new architectures or SOTA advances.
- Lacks contextual baselines: no SVR or standard non-probabilistic comparisons, and NLPD/NLL absolute values are hard to interpret without qualitative examples.
