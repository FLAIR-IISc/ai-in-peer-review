- Applies Gaussian Processes to QE and argues for probabilistic predictions that model uncertainty rather than point estimates.
- Evaluates several GP variants (standard, Matérn, Warped) using distributional metrics like NLPD/NLL.
- Demonstrates the practical value of uncertainty estimates for asymmetric risk scenarios in translation workflows.
- Shows limitations of point-estimate evaluation and argues for uncertainty-aware decision-making in QE.
- Empirical results indicate Warped GPs yield better-calibrated uncertainty for post-editing rate prediction, not captured by MAE.
- Strong motivation and practical grounding for considering uncertainty and asymmetric risks in QE.
- Clear, accessible methodological exposition of GPs, kernels, and warping for an NLP audience.
- Experimental design effectively supports the thesis; NLPD provides convincing evidence of improved calibration.
- Lacks a strong non-probabilistic baseline (e.g., SVR) to contextualize performance and trade-offs.
- NLL/NLPD metrics are hard to interpret; paper needs to show what constitutes a meaningful improvement, e.g., via qualitative examples.
- Modeling contribution is limited to applying established GP techniques rather than proposing new methodology.
- Request justification for predicting post-editing rates instead of the common QE target HTER.
- Ask authors to clarify the reported NLL/NLPD trend discrepancy for the en-es dataset versus fr-en and en-de, given Table 1.
