- Uses Gaussian Processes for QE and argues QE should report uncertainty, not just point estimates.
- Explores kernels (EQ, Matérn) and warped GPs to model positive, non-Gaussian quality scores.
- Evaluates models with point metrics (MAE) and probabilistic metrics (NLPD/NLL) that use full predictive distributions.
- Demonstrates using predictive distributions to derive Bayes-optimal estimators for asymmetric loss without retraining.
- Main contribution: advocating probabilistic QE and emphasizing uncertainty for real-world decision-making.
- Shows practical application of a single GP’s predictive distribution to asymmetric risk scenarios (pessimistic/optimistic estimators).
- Empirical comparison indicates warped GPs improve probabilistic fit (NLPD) even when MAE/Pearson’s r don’t show it.
- Strength: strong motivation and clear framing linking academic QE to practical needs.
- Strength: clear, accessible methodological explanations of GPs, kernels, and warping for NLP/MT readers.
- Strength: insightful, well-explained analysis of asymmetric risk and why models differ under AL and linex losses.
- Strength: well-posed research questions that focus the evaluation and application of probabilistic models.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to strong non-probabilistic baselines (e.g., SVR) for point-estimate context.
- Weakness: probabilistic metrics (NLL/NLPD) are hard to interpret; needs intuition or qualitative examples for significance.
- Weakness: limited novelty in modeling—uses established GP/warping methods rather than new techniques.
- Question: why predict post-editing rates instead of HTER, and would findings generalize to HTER prediction?
- Question: clarify the claimed differing NLL/NLPD trend for en-es versus fr-en and en-de based on Table 1.
- Minor: hyphenate “over- and underestimates” for clarity.
- Minor: fix Figure 1 caption color mismatch (mentions blue/red but shows blue/green).
- Minor: cite the GP software/library used (e.g., GPy, GPflow) to aid reproducibility.
