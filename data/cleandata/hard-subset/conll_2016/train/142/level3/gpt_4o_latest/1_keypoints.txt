- Paper studies Gaussian Process models for Machine Translation Quality Estimation, emphasizing uncertainty estimation for real-world, asymmetric-risk applications.  
- Proposes using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate probabilistic QE models and explores warped GPs and Matèrn kernels to improve predictions.  
- Demonstrates utility of predictive distributions under asymmetric loss functions, arguing for moving beyond point estimates.  
- Contribution: detailed exploration of probabilistic QE models with an emphasis on uncertainty.  
- Contribution: introduction and empirical support for NLPD as a preferred evaluation metric in QE.  
- Contribution: novel application of asymmetric loss functions to showcase practical benefits of uncertainty-aware models.  
- Strength: clear motivation and problem framing, especially regarding limitations of point estimates and asymmetric risks.  
- Strength: accessible and educational explanation of Gaussian Processes, kernels, and warping.  
- Strength: meaningful evaluation in asymmetric scenarios, integrating AL and linex losses with predictive distributions.  
- Strength: well-written results section that links findings to research questions and practical implications.  
- Weakness: using post-editing rates as the response variable is not well-justified and may limit generalizability versus established metrics like HTER.  
- Weakness: trend analysis (Section 3.2) for fr-en, en-de, and en-es is unclear and difficult to interpret.  
- Weakness: no comparison to a state-of-the-art point-estimate baseline, weakening empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: NLPD/NLL metrics are hard to interpret without qualitative prediction examples.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—applies existing GP techniques without substantial modeling innovation.  
- Questions for authors: justify choice of post-editing rate vs HTER, provide qualitative examples/case studies for NLPD/NLL reductions, and compare to SOTA point-estimate models on accuracy and computational efficiency.  
- Additional comments: fix minor typos, incorrect figure caption, and missing GP toolkit references; clarify trend analysis for the en-es dataset.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the listed concerns.
