- Emphasizes the importance of modeling uncertainty in QE and advocates probabilistic models that provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates.
- Provides a comprehensive, accessible exploration of Gaussian Processes (kernel choices and warping) tailored to QE.
- Demonstrates using GP posterior distributions for asymmetric-risk decisions via asymmetric loss functions (pessimistic/optimistic scenarios).

- Clearly motivates the limitations of point estimates in real-world QE and the need for uncertainty quantification.
- Delivers a thorough GP primer useful for non-experts, with clear explanations of kernel choices and warping.
- Presents a well-written results section that links modeling choices to observed performance in asymmetric-risk scenarios.
- Uses multiple, varied datasets and 10-fold cross-validation for robust experimental design.
- Thoughtfully acknowledges limitations of point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson’s r) for evaluating uncertainty-aware models.

- Needs stronger justification for predicting post-editing rates instead of the more common HTER target.
- Unclear explanation for differing NLL/NLPD trends (fr-en & en-de vs en-es); attributing it to overfitting on en-es is speculative and needs deeper analysis.
- Lacks baseline point-estimate comparisons (e.g., SVR) to contextualize GP performance improvements.
- NLL and NLPD reported are hard to interpret in isolation; relate reductions to practical improvements for clarity.
- Inconclusive linex loss results in pessimistic scenarios suggest an issue needing further investigation.

- Please justify choosing post-editing rates over HTER as the target variable.
- Explain the NLL/NLPD trend discrepancy across language pairs and identify dataset characteristics of en-es that may cause it.
- What analyses or modifications would clarify the interaction between the linex loss and the predictive distributions in pessimistic scenarios?

- Change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates” throughout.
- Correct Figure 1 caption: line colors are blue and red, not blue and green.
- Cite the GP toolkit used (e.g., GPy) to improve reproducibility.

- Overall: valuable contribution highlighting uncertainty modeling in QE and practical use of GPs, though it would be strengthened by clearer target justification, deeper analysis of metric discrepancies, baseline comparisons, and investigation of linex results.
