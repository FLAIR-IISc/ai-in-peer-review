- Focus: Uses Gaussian Processes for Machine Translation Quality Estimation, emphasizing prediction uncertainty and decision-making under asymmetric risk.

- Contributions: Advocates uncertainty-aware evaluation (NLPD), explores Matérn and Warped GPs for QE, derives Bayes risk estimators for asymmetric loss scenarios, and provides an accessible GP primer.

- Strength — Motivation: Strong, practical motivation showing point estimates are insufficient for post-editing and gisting workflows.

- Strength — GP exploration: Thorough, well-explained treatment of GP methods, kernels, and warping that aids reproducibility.

- Strength — Evaluation metrics: Correctly promotes NLPD over MAE/Pearson’s r to capture full predictive distribution.

- Strength — Asymmetric risk analysis: Clear, insightful use of asymmetric loss functions (AL, Linex) and Bayes risk derivations for relevant MT scenarios.

- Strength — Writing: Clear, well-structured, and easy to follow.

- Weakness — Novelty: Modeling extensions (Matérn, Warped GPs) are incremental; main novelty is applying known GP techniques to QE and emphasizing uncertainty.

- Weakness — Result interpretation: Discussion of differing NLL/NLPD trends across language pairs (fr-en, en-de vs. en-es) is speculative and underexplained.

- Weakness — Baselines: Lacks comparison to strong non-probabilistic QE baselines (e.g., well-tuned SVR) to contextualize performance.

- Weakness — Linex loss results: Poor performance in pessimistic Linex scenario requires deeper investigation and justification.

- Weakness — Practicality: No discussion of computational cost for training/evaluating complex GP models and warping functions.

- Question: Why use post-editing rates as the response variable instead of the more common HTER, and how does this affect interpretation?

- Question: Please elaborate on the specific NLL/NLPD trend differences for fr-en and en-de vs. en-es and suggest further analyses.

- Question: Which GP toolkit was used to facilitate reproducibility?

- Minor comments: Fix “over and underestimates” → “over- and underestimates”; correct Figure 1 caption colors; optionally discuss computational complexity.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
