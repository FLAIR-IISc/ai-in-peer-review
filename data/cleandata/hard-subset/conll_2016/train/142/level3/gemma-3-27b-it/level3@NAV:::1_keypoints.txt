- Explores using Gaussian Processes to model prediction uncertainty in MT Quality Estimation beyond point estimates.
- Correctly highlights limitations of point estimates in real-world, asymmetric-risk scenarios.
- Well-structured paper with clear motivation and thorough, accessible explanation of GPs, kernels, and warping.
- Research questions are well-phrased and address a meaningful gap in QE research.
- Main contribution: emphasizes the importance of uncertainty-aware QE using GPs within an asymmetric risk framework.
- Empirical contribution: shows standard point-estimate metrics fail to capture nuances across GP configurations (kernels/warpings).
- Applies asymmetric loss functions to QE, demonstrating practical decision-making benefits.
- Weakness: does not advance state-of-the-art raw prediction accuracy.
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited, relying on established GP techniques.
- Weakness: insufficient justification for choosing post-editing rates over the more common HTER, harming generalizability.
- Weakness: Section 3.2 contains a confusing claim about differing NLPD/NLL trends (fr-en/en-de vs en-es) that needs clarification.
- Weakness: results lack a baseline point-estimate model (e.g., SVR) for comparison context.
- Weakness: NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret without scale or qualitative examples of what “drastic” reductions mean.
- Question to authors: justify using post-editing rates instead of HTER for QE targets.
- Question to authors: clarify/explain the NLPD/NLL discrepancy between language pairs with warped GPs.
- Question to authors: provide qualitative examples to interpret the magnitude of NLL/NLPD improvements.
- Additional: cite the specific GP toolkit used for modeling.
- Additional: correct Figure 1 caption — line colors are blue and green, not blue and red.
- Additional: change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates” throughout the paper.
