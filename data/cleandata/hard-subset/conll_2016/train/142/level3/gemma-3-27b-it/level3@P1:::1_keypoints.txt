- Paper studies Gaussian Processes (GPs) for Machine Translation Quality Estimation (QE) with emphasis on modeling prediction uncertainty for real-world workflows.
- Authors argue point-estimate metrics are insufficient for decision-aware QE, motivating probabilistic predictions and calibration.
- Propose evaluating probabilistic QE using metrics like Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) instead of only MAE/Pearson’s r.
- Demonstrate that differences in uncertainty modeling are not captured by standard point-estimate metrics but are revealed by NLPD/NLL.
- Explore GP design choices, comparing Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs, and discuss ARD and warping function rationale.
- Show practical application by deriving Bayes risk estimators for asymmetric risk scenarios, illustrating decision-aware benefits.
- Strength: Strong, user-centric motivation linking QE evaluation to human-in-the-loop post-editing workflows.
- Strength: Thorough and accessible exposition of GP methodology, kernels, and warping for readers unfamiliar with GPs.
- Strength: Rigorous experimental setup across multiple language pairs with nuanced analysis beyond headline metrics.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured writing with helpful illustrative examples and related-work discussion.
- Weakness: Core methodological novelty is limited—mainly systematic exploration of existing GP techniques rather than a new model.
- Weakness: NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret without benchmarks or qualitative impact examples.
- Weakness: Rationale for using post-editing rates over HTER needs stronger justification and discussion of trade-offs.
- Weakness: Asymmetric risk analysis may be biased—linex loss sometimes underperforms and causes unclear variance-related effects.
- Question: Please justify choosing post-editing rates over HTER and discuss impacts on results and generalizability.
- Question: Provide intuitive interpretation or practical implications of specific NLL/NLPD reductions (e.g., impact on post-editing time).
- Question: Investigate the relationship between model variance (especially warped GPs) and performance under linex/asymmetric losses.
- Additional: Clarify confusing paragraph in Section 3.2 about differing trends across language pairs.
- Additional: Include a baseline standard QE model (e.g., SVR) in result tables for context.
- Additional: Correct Figure 1 caption color descriptions, cite the GP toolkit/library used, and fix “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”
