- Paper examines Gaussian Processes (GPs) for MT Quality Estimation (QE) with focus on predictive uncertainty for decision-making.  
- Contributions: shows uncertainty helps in asymmetric risk scenarios, evaluates GP modeling choices (kernels, warping) using uncertainty-aware metrics, and argues for using uncertainty-aware metrics over point-estimate metrics.

- Strong motivation: highlights limitations of point estimates in real-world, asymmetric-risk translation workflows.  
- Clear GP methodology: accessible, well-structured explanation of GPs, Matèrn kernels, and warped GPs.  
- Practical demonstration: compelling, intuitive examples of leveraging predictive uncertainty (pessimistic vs. optimistic) and Bayes risk estimators.  
- Evaluation emphasis: convincingly argues NLPD/NLL are more appropriate than MAE/Pearson for uncertainty-aware models.

- Weakness: insufficient justification for using post-editing rates instead of the standard HTER target.  
- Weakness: confusing and possibly contradictory description of trends in Section 3.2 (warped GPs behavior for en-es vs. other datasets).  
- Weakness: missing point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR/WMT baselines) in result tables for contextual performance comparison.  
- Weakness: NLL/NLPD values are presented without interpretation or baseline to indicate what constitutes meaningful reductions.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in modeling—GP techniques are standard and improvements are incremental.

- Question: why choose post-editing rates over HTER as the target variable?  
- Question: please clarify the apparent contradiction about en-es trends in Section 3.2 versus Table 1.  
- Question: can a standard point-estimate model (e.g., SVR) be added to the results for context?

- Minor: change "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates".  
- Minor: fix Figure 1 caption color labels (should be "blue and green" not "blue and red").  
- Minor: cite the GP toolkit used (e.g., GPflow, GPy) for reproducibility.

- Overall: well-written, valuable paper highlighting importance of uncertainty in QE; useful application of existing probabilistic methods though lacking major modeling novelty; recommend minor revisions.
