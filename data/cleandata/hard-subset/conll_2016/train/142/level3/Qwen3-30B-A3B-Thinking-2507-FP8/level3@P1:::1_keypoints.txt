- Contribution: Shows standard point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson r) miss meaningful differences in uncertainty modeling.  
- Contribution: Proposes NLPD (Negative Log Predictive Density) as a superior metric for evaluating probabilistic QE models because it accounts for uncertainty.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates how predictive distributions can inform asymmetric risk decisions in translation workflows (pessimistic vs optimistic scenarios).  
- Strength: Makes a compelling practical case that uncertainty estimates matter for post-editing and decision-making.  
- Strength: Asymmetric risk framework is well-motivated, clearly explained, and illustrated with realistic examples.  
- Strength: Provides a thorough, accessible explanation of GP methodology (Matern kernels, warping) for NLP readers.  
- Strength: Replacing traditional metrics with NLPD yields insights about model behavior that point estimates hide.  
- Weakness: Does not improve state-of-the-art point estimate performance, which is the primary focus in QE research.  
- Weakness: Choice of post-editing rate as target over standard HTER is not adequately justified, hindering comparisons.  
- Weakness: Section 3.2 results (especially en–es) are confusing and show inconsistent NLL/NLPD trends across datasets.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to strong point-estimate baselines (e.g., SVR, neural models) for contextualization.  
- Weakness: Reported NLPD values lack interpretive context, making practical significance unclear.  
- Question: Why choose post-editing rate instead of the widely used HTER metric?  
- Question: Can authors provide qualitative examples showing how uncertainty changes asymmetric decision-making in practice?  
- Question: What is the practical impact of the observed NLPD improvements on real workflows (quantified gains)?  
- Question: How do authors explain inconsistent Matern kernel performance across language pairs?  
- Recommendation: Paper would be stronger with baseline comparisons to strong point-estimate models and clearer justification of methodological choices.  
- Recommendation: Show how probabilistic QE could be integrated into a production translation pipeline to demonstrate real-world utility.
