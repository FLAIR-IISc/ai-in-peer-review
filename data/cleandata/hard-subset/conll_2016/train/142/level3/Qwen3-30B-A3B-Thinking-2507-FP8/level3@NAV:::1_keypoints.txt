- Paper studies Gaussian Processes for machine-translation quality estimation, prioritizing calibrated uncertainty over point predictions.  
- Main contributions: (1) argues limitations of point estimates and value of uncertainty-aware QE models.  
- Main contributions: (2) systematically evaluates GP methods with probabilistic metrics (NLL, NLPD) that capture uncertainty.  
- Main contributions: (3) demonstrates how predictive distributions aid decision-making under asymmetric risk in post-editing workflows.  
- Strength: Strong motivation for uncertainty estimation in QE and practical relevance to real-world workflows.  
- Strength: Clear, well-explained asymmetric-risk section with intuitive, practical examples.  
- Strength: Well-structured, accessible yet rigorous exposition of GP methodology.  
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for using post-editing rates instead of the standard HTER metric, limiting comparability.  
- Weakness: Section 3.2 explanation of inconsistent trends across language pairs (fr-en, en-de vs. en-es) is confusing and needs clarification.  
- Weakness: No comparison to existing point-estimate baselines, hindering contextualization of model quality and gains.  
- Weakness: NLPD values reported without interpretation guidance, making practical significance unclear; requests qualitative examples.  
- Weakness: Paper does not advance modeling state-of-the-art or show large performance gains; contains minor errors (e.g., wording and Figure 1 caption).  
- Questions for authors: Clarify choice of post-editing rates over HTER, provide qualitative examples to contextualize NLPD improvements, and explain the en-es trend inconsistency.
