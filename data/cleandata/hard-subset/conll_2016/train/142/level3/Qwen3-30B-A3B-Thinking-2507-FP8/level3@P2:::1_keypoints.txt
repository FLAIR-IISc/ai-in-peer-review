- Argues for probabilistic QE models (Gaussian Processes) that provide calibrated uncertainty rather than only point estimates.  
- Proposes Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a superior evaluation metric that captures full predictive distributions missed by MAE/Pearson.  
- Shows predictive uncertainty can be leveraged in asymmetric risk scenarios (e.g., post-editing) to make better decisions, with probabilistic models outperforming point-estimate approaches.  
- Strong motivation and practical examples demonstrating why uncertainty matters in translation workflows (post-editing, gisting).  
- Empirically demonstrates that NLPD reveals meaningful performance differences (e.g., between warping functions) that point metrics miss.  
- Effectively applies asymmetric loss functions (AL, linex) to show models can adapt to risk preferences without retraining.  
- Fails to justify choice of target variable (post-editing time per word) over standard HTER, needing explanation of impact.  
- Lacks a strong point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR) in results, making it hard to gauge absolute point-prediction performance.  
- Presents NLPD values without contextual interpretation or qualitative examples to judge significance.  
- Offers methodological contributions but does not advance state-of-the-art point-estimate QE performance.  
- Question: Why choose post-editing time normalized by length instead of HTER, and do findings generalize to other QE metrics?  
- Question: Can you include a strong point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR) to contextualize probabilistic model performance?  
- Question: Can you provide context for interpreting NLPD values—what constitutes a meaningful improvement and comparisons to prior work?
