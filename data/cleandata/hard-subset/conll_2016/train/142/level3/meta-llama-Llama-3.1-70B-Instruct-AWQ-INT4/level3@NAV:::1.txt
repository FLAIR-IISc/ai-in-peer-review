**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Exploring Prediction Uncertainty in Machine Translation Quality Estimation" proposes the use of Gaussian Processes (GPs) to model uncertainty in machine translation quality estimation (QE). The authors argue that traditional point estimate metrics are not sufficient for evaluating QE models and propose the use of Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a metric that takes into account the full posterior predictive distribution. The paper also explores the use of asymmetric loss functions to evaluate QE models in scenarios where the cost of overestimation and underestimation are different.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes the use of GPs to model uncertainty in QE, which is a novel approach in this field.
2. The authors introduce the use of NLPD as a metric to evaluate QE models, which is a more comprehensive metric than traditional point estimate metrics.
3. The paper explores the use of asymmetric loss functions to evaluate QE models in scenarios where the cost of overestimation and underestimation are different.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a clear and well-structured introduction to the problem of QE and the limitations of traditional point estimate metrics.
2. The authors provide a thorough explanation of GPs and their application to QE, which is easy to follow even for non-experts.
3. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach using multiple datasets and metrics.
4. The authors provide a clear and concise discussion of the results and their implications for QE.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper does not advance the state-of-the-art in terms of modeling novelty, as the use of GPs is not a new approach in machine learning.
2. The authors do not provide a clear justification for choosing post-editing rates over the more common HTER prediction in QE research.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future work.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear justification for choosing post-editing rates over HTER prediction in QE research?
2. How do you plan to address the limitations of the proposed approach, such as the need for large amounts of training data and the potential for overfitting?
3. Can you provide more details on the implementation of the GP toolkit used in the paper?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work in QE and machine learning.
2. The authors should consider including more qualitative examples to illustrate the benefits of using NLPD as a metric.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results for QE and machine translation in general.
