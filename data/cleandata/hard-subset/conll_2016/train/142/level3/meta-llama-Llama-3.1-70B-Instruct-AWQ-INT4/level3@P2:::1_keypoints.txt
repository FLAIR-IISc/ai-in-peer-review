- Summary: The paper applies Gaussian Processes to Machine Translation Quality Estimation, emphasizing modeling prediction uncertainty and advocating NLPD as an evaluation metric, with experiments on asymmetric risk scenarios.

- Contribution 1: Proposes using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate probabilistic QE models that capture predictive uncertainty.

- Contribution 2: Shows how well-calibrated GP predictive distributions can yield Bayes risk estimators for asymmetric loss scenarios.

- Contribution 3: Provides a clear tutorial-style introduction to GPs for QE, including kernel choices and warping functions.

- Strength 1: Well-structured and clearly written, accessible to experts and non-experts.

- Strength 2: Strong motivation for probabilistic models in QE, highlighting limitations of point estimates.

- Strength 3: Thorough and clear exposition of GP methodology and modeling choices.

- Strength 4: Empirically demonstrates GPs’ effectiveness at modeling uncertainty and handling asymmetric risks.

- Weakness 1: Does not advance QE state-of-the-art, relying on existing GP methodology.

- Weakness 2: Lacks justification for choosing post-editing rates instead of the more common HTER target.

- Weakness 3: Missing a point-estimate baseline in result tables to contextualize performance.

- Weakness 4: Reported NLL/NLPD values are hard to interpret; paper needs guidance on what constitutes meaningful improvements and qualitative examples.

- Question 1: Please justify the choice of post-editing rates over HTER for QE prediction.

- Question 2: How will the authors address the lack of state-of-the-art improvement and what future work is planned to overcome this?

- Question 3: Provide more details on the GP toolkit and implementation used.

- Question 4: How do the authors plan to extend this GP-based approach to other NLP applications?

- Additional comment 1: Minor wording edit—use “over- and underestimates.”

- Additional comment 2: Fix Figure 1 caption colors (should be blue and green, not blue and red).

- Additional comment 3: Consider including a qualitative example to illustrate uncertainty modeling effectiveness.
