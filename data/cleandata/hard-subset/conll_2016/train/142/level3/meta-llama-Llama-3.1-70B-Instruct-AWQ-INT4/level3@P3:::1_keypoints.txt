- Applies Gaussian processes to machine translation quality estimation to model uncertainty and produce well-calibrated predictive distributions.
- Argues that point-estimate metrics are insufficient for real-world QE and that uncertainty information is crucial for decision-making.
- Paper is well-structured with a clear introduction to QE point-estimate problems and the importance of uncertainty.
- Strong, clear explanation of asymmetric risk scenarios and implications for different estimators.
- Provides a substantial GP introduction that may be long for experts but is helpful for general readers.
- GP methodology, kernel choices, and warping are explained clearly and are easy to follow.
- Research questions are interesting and well-phrased.
- Needs stronger justification for choosing post-editing rates instead of the more common HTER prediction.
- Section 3.2 is confusing about trends for fr-en and en-de versus en-es regarding NLL/NLPD decreases with warped GPs.
- Results would be better contextualized by including an existing point-estimate model baseline in tables.
- NLL and NLPD values are hard to interpret; authors should provide qualitative examples or thresholds for meaningful reductions.
- Section 4 is well-written and explains results intuitively.
- Paper highlights problems with point estimates and the importance of uncertainty but does not advance state-of-the-art or offer major modeling novelty.
- Minor edits: change "over and underestimates" to "over- and underestimates."
- Figure 1 caption needs correction for line colors (mentions wrong colors).
- Should cite the GP toolkit used for modeling, if applicable.
- Main contributions: GP application to QE, use of NLPD for probabilistic evaluation, and exploration of asymmetric risk/Bayes risk estimators.
- Key weaknesses: limited novelty, justification for target metric choice, interpretability of probabilistic metrics, and missing point-estimate baseline.
- Questions to authors: justify post-editing rate choice, provide qualitative examples for NLL/NLPD interpretability, include point-estimate baseline, and discuss extensions to other NLP tasks and challenges.
