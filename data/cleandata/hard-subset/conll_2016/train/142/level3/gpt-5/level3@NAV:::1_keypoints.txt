- Primary: Demonstrates that uncertainty-aware QE with Gaussian Processes (including warped GPs) yields better-calibrated predictive distributions and more appropriate decision rules in asymmetric risk settings, evaluated via NLPD and Bayes-risk-optimal estimators.  
- Secondary: Provides a clear, reproducible exposition of GP choices (Matérn kernels, ARD, warping) tailored to QE for practitioners.  
- Tertiary: Shows how predictive posteriors translate into actionable policies for post-editing and gisting using asymmetric linear and linex losses.  
- Strength: Strong motivation and framing arguing point estimates are insufficient and calibrated uncertainty matters for risk-sensitive QE workflows.  
- Strength: Clear methodology with well-explained kernel choices, warping, training protocol, and Bayes estimators linking theory to use cases.  
- Strength: Empirical insight that warped GPs consistently improve NLL/NLPD and often MAE, revealing uncertainty differences missed by point metrics.  
- Strength: Practical relevance of asymmetric loss analysis (especially AL) for translation pipeline design.  
- Strength: Well-written and structured introduction to GPs for non-specialists with intuitive result discussion.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—GPs, warping, and Matérn kernels are established; contribution is mainly evaluative/applicative rather than SOTA.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to strong point-estimate baselines (e.g., SVR, recent neural regressors) to contextualize accuracy.  
- Weakness: NLL/NLPD interpretability lacking—no qualitative examples, interval coverage, or calibration plots to show what constitutes a “drastic” reduction.  
- Weakness: Confusion in Section 3.2 about dataset trends—text claims trends for fr-en and en-de not seen for en-es, yet NLL/NLPD decrease with warping across datasets; needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Scope of targets unclear—justify using post-editing rates instead of the more standard HTER and discuss applicability implications.
