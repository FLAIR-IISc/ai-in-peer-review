- Paper studies uncertainty-aware MT quality estimation using Gaussian Processes and evaluates full predictive distributions with NLPD rather than just point metrics.
- Compares standard GPs, Warped GPs, and EQ vs. Matérn kernels across three post-editing time datasets using 17 QuEst features and 10-fold CV.
- Finds Warped GPs substantially improve NLL/NLPD while point metrics (MAE, RMSE, Pearson) barely differentiate models.
- Demonstrates decision-making under asymmetric losses (asymmetric linear and linex) using Bayes estimators from predictive posteriors.
- Contribution: Empirical evidence that probabilistic evaluation (NLPD) reveals model differences missed by point metrics.
- Contribution: Evaluation of Warped GPs and Matérn kernels for QE, showing learned warping helps predictive uncertainty and sometimes point accuracy.
- Contribution: Practical framework for asymmetric decision-making in QE using minimum Bayes risk estimators.
- Strength: Clear motivation that point estimates are inadequate for production QE and human-in-the-loop workflows.
- Strength: Sound probabilistic evaluation; highlights when NLL can mislead on small data while NLPD stays informative.
- Strength: Well-executed asymmetric risk analysis linking realistic workflows to interpretable Bayes estimators.
- Strength: Methodological exposition of GPs, Matérn kernels, and Warped GPs is clear and accessible.
- Strength: Broad, controlled experiments (three language pairs, standard features, 10-fold CV) that isolate modeling effects.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—Warped GPs and Matérn kernels are established; contribution is mainly empirical/guideline.
- Weakness: Missing strong non-probabilistic baselines (e.g., SVR or modern neural models) to contextualize point-prediction quality.
- Weakness: NLL/NLPD magnitudes lack interpretability without calibration diagnostics or qualitative illustrations.
- Weakness: Section 3.2 is unclear about which trends diverge across datasets beyond small-data overfitting claims.
- Weakness: Choice of post-editing rates over HTER is not justified, affecting generality and comparability.
- Question: Please justify using post-editing rates instead of HTER regarding availability, stability, and comparability.
- Question: Can you precisely state the trend differences in Section 3.2 and provide statistical tests relating NLL vs NLPD divergences to sample size?
- Question: Provide calibration diagnostics (PIT, reliability diagrams, interval coverage) or qualitative examples to interpret NLL/NLPD.
- Question: Which GP toolkit and optimization/settings were used, and will code be released?
- Question: Add or comment on results versus strong SVR/neural baselines to contextualize trade-offs.
- Question: For linex pessimistic settings, have you tried variance calibration (tempering/inflation) to test whether effects stem from variance magnitude?
- Comment: Minor edit—change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”
- Comment: Figure 1 caption: correct color description (blue and green, not blue and red).
- Comment: Briefly note limits of kernel exploration and rationale for omitting other kernels.
- Comment: Advise adding guidance on selecting the number of tanh terms in warping and overfitting safeguards for small datasets.
