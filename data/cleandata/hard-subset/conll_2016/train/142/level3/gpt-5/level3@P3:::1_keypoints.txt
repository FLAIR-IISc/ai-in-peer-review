- Summary: Models sentence-level QE uncertainty with GPs (Matérn, Warped), evaluates full predictive distributions via NLPD, and derives Bayes estimators for asymmetric AL and linex losses to support decision-making in post-editing/gisting.

- Contribution 1: Shows probabilistic QE (GPs, Warped GPs) yields better-calibrated predictive distributions than point-estimate baselines by NLPD, and that calibration matters for asymmetric decisions.

- Contribution 2: Formulates asymmetric risk scenarios practically, derives Bayes estimators for AL and linex losses, and demonstrates benefits of uncertainty-aware decisions under asymmetric losses.

- Contribution 3: Provides careful exposition of GP modeling choices (Matérn kernels, ARD, warping) with transparent training and evaluation protocols.

- Strength: Clear motivation and problem framing—argues convincingly that uncertainty is needed for real workflows and asymmetric decision thresholds.

- Strength: Strong methodological exposition—GP overview, kernel discussion, and warping treatment are accurate and accessible.

- Strength: Proper probabilistic evaluation—NLPD is used appropriately and shown to reveal differences missed by MAE/Pearson r.

- Strength: Insightful asymmetric-loss analysis—translates probabilistic outputs into actionable estimators without retraining.

- Strength: Solid experimental design—multiple language pairs, 10-fold CV, and standard feature set focus attention on uncertainty rather than feature engineering.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—the methods (GPs, Matérn, warping) are established and the main advance is evaluation/advocacy rather than new algorithms.

- Weakness: Missing strong point-estimate baselines in result tables (e.g., SVR-RBF or linear L2) to contextualize point accuracy vs uncertainty gains.

- Weakness: NLPD/NLL improvements are hard to interpret; paper should ground gains in intuitive metrics or qualitative cases where uncertainty changes decisions.

- Weakness: Section 3.2 trend discussion is unclear—claims about train NLL vs test NLPD trends (fr-en/en-de vs en-es) need concrete numbers/plots.

- Weakness: Outcome variable choice (post-editing rate) needs justification versus more common HTER and evidence that conclusions transfer.

- Weakness: Limited calibration diagnostics—should add PIT histograms, interval coverage, or CRPS beyond NLPD.

- Question: Why choose post-editing rate over HTER—clarify label quality, operational relevance, and transferability of conclusions.

- Question: Can you quantify/visualize the train NLL vs test NLPD trends per dataset (with error bars across folds) and diagnose overfitting?

- Question: Can you add a point-estimate baseline (e.g., SVR-RBF) and report how MAE/r of that baseline compare to GP variants and Warped GP?

- Question: Can you make NLPD changes more interpretable (average likelihood ratios, bits-per-sentence, or qualitative decision-flipping examples)?

- Question: Which software/toolkit was used (GPy/GPflow/scikit-learn)? Provide implementation details affecting reproducibility (optimizers, seeds, restarts, quadrature).

- Question: For the linex pessimistic case, did you examine posterior variance calibration (coverage)? Could recalibration (variance inflation/temperature scaling) mitigate bias toward high-variance models?
