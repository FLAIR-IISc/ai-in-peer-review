- Proposes Gaussian Processes with Matèrn kernels and Warped GP extensions for probabilistic QE modeling.  
- Advocates using NLPD (Negative Log Predictive Density) as a better evaluation metric than MAE or Pearson’s r for probabilistic QE.  
- Demonstrates leveraging predictive uncertainty in asymmetric risk scenarios (e.g., post-editing workflows) without retraining models.  

- Strong motivation for uncertainty estimation in QE, emphasizing real-world workflow costs and asymmetric error impacts.  
- Clear, well-chosen pessimistic and optimistic scenario examples illustrating practical value of uncertainty information.  
- Compelling argument and empirical evidence that NLPD reveals meaningful differences in uncertainty modeling that MAE misses.  
- Valuable demonstration that uncertainty can be used operationally under changing loss functions without model retraining.  

- Inconsistent Matèrn kernel performance across language pairs (better for fr-en but not en-es/en-de) without sufficient analysis or explanation.  
- Comparison limited to GPs; lacks other probabilistic baselines (Bayesian neural networks, ensembles), reducing generalizability.  
- Confusing linex-loss results: warped GPs underperform standard GPs in pessimistic scenario despite better uncertainty estimates, unexplained contradiction.  
- Needs additional experiments comparing to other probabilistic approaches and deeper analysis of Matèrn kernel behavior across datasets.  
- Authors should investigate and clarify the unexpected linex-loss outcomes to validate the main thesis about uncertainty benefits.  

- Question 1: How were statistical significance tests conducted to assess whether Matèrn vs EQ kernel performance differences are meaningful?  
- Question 2: Can authors analyze dataset characteristics (size, noise, feature distributions) to explain warping/kernel performance differences across language pairs?  
- Question 3: Why do warped GPs perform worse than standard GPs under linex loss in the pessimistic scenario despite better uncertainty estimates?
