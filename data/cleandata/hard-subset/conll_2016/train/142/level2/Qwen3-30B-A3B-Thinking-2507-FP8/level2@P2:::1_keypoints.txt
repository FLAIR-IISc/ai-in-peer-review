- Goal: study probabilistic QE that provides calibrated uncertainty, extending GPs (Matèrn kernels, warped GPs) and promoting NLPD for full predictive-distribution evaluation.
- Contribution: establish NLPD as a critical metric for evaluating uncertainty in QE.
- Contribution: show GP extensions (Matèrn, warped GPs) improve uncertainty calibration.
- Contribution: illustrate how predictive uncertainty enables better decision-making in asymmetric risk scenarios.
- Strength: strong motivation and relevance—uncertainty estimation matters for real-world QE (e.g., post-editing).
- Strength: compelling evidence that NLPD captures uncertainty differences missed by MAE/Pearson’s r (fr-en, en-de examples).
- Strength: practical demonstration in asymmetric risk settings where warped GPs improve outcomes in optimistic scenarios.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical grounding and sound use of established metrics and loss functions.
- Weakness: limited comparison with non-probabilistic baselines—no rigorous MAE comparison to SVR or neural QE models.
- Weakness: scalability concerns unaddressed—no analysis for larger datasets despite GPs’ O(n^3) complexity.
- Weakness: inconsistent results on the small en-es dataset with no proposed remedies for overfitting or low-resource settings.
- Weakness: lack of ablation studies—unclear whether gains derive from warping, Matèrn kernels, or both.
- Question: how will you address GP scalability for large-scale QE (e.g., sparse approximations, neural-GP hybrids)?
