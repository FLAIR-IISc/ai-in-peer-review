1. Contribution: Establishes NLPD as a superior metric for probabilistic QE by capturing uncertainty differences missed by point-estimate metrics (MAE, Pearson).
2. Contribution: Shows calibrated uncertainty enables better decision-making in asymmetric-risk workflows (e.g., post-editing filtering).
3. Contribution: Proposes model extensions (Matérn kernels, warped GPs) to improve uncertainty calibration in QE.
4. Strength: Argues practical necessity of uncertainty estimation with concrete workflow relevance.
5. Strength: Justifies and effectively demonstrates NLPD’s ability to reveal model differences that MAE misses (e.g., tanh vs. log warping).
6. Strength: Asymmetric-risk experiments link theory to practice, showing warped GPs excel in optimistic/AL-loss scenarios and explaining linex-loss behavior.
7. Weakness: Experimental scope limited to three language pairs and a fixed feature set; inconsistent Matérn benefits (e.g., fr-en vs en-es) are unexplained.
8. Weakness: Discussion of linex loss under pessimistic scenarios is underdeveloped; unclear if warped GPs’ poorer performance is fundamental or an artifact.
9. Weakness: Recommendation to prefer NLPD lacks actionable guidance on interpreting NLPD ranges across datasets and models.
10. Question: Provide ablation results on varying the number of tanh terms (e.g., 1, 2, 3) and their effect on NLPD and asymmetric-risk performance?
11. Question: Is there a correlation between dataset size and the optimal warping function, given log warping outperformed tanh on small en-es data?
12. Question: Can you quantify how NLPD penalizes overconfident vs. underconfident models, e.g., via a simple illustrative example?
