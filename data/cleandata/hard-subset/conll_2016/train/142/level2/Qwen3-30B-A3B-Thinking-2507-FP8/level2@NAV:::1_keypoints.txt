- Investigates probabilistic QE models that provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates, addressing a gap in point-estimate QE.  
- Contribution: Shows Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) is a superior evaluation metric for probabilistic QE versus MAE and Pearson's r because it captures uncertainty.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates warped Gaussian Processes (tanh warping) consistently outperform standard GPs in NLPD and asymmetric risk scenarios.  
- Contribution: Shows predictive distributions have practical utility in real-world asymmetric translation workflows (e.g., post-editing prioritization).  
- Strength: Strong real-world motivation for modeling uncertainty in translation workflows.  
- Strength: Robust experimental validation across three language pairs (en-es, fr-en, en-de) with 10-fold cross-validation.  
- Strength: Clearly demonstrates that uncertainty modeling differences are invisible to standard point-estimate metrics.  
- Strength: NLPD evaluation framework is theoretically grounded and connected to practical asymmetric loss examples.  
- Weakness: Small en-es dataset (858 samples) yields inconsistent results (log warping beating tanh), raising overfitting concerns not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to non-GP probabilistic models (e.g., Bayesian neural networks), despite claiming GPs are the natural choice.  
- Weakness: Unexplained, dataset-specific marginal gains from Matérn kernels (only for fr-en), indicating insufficient kernel exploration.  
- Weakness: Counterintuitive pessimistic linex loss results where warped GPs perform worse than standard GPs lack sufficient analysis.  
- Recommendation: Clarify en-es dataset limitations, expand comparisons to other probabilistic models, and investigate the Matérn kernel inconsistency.  
- Overall: Promising and valuable core contributions (NLPD metric and uncertainty utility) but needs deeper dataset analysis and broader benchmarking to strengthen claims.
