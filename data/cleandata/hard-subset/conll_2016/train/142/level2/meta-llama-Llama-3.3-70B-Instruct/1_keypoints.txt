- Paper proposes probabilistic QE using Gaussian Processes (including Matérn kernels and Warped GPs) to obtain well-calibrated uncertainty estimates.
- Models are evaluated with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to account for full posterior predictive distributions.
- Paper explores asymmetric loss functions (asymmetric linear, linex) for scenarios with asymmetric risk like post-editing and gisting.
- Main contribution: introducing probabilistic GP-based modeling for QE as an improvement over point-estimate methods.
- Main contribution: advocating NLPD as a more comprehensive evaluation metric for QE models.
- Main contribution: demonstrating application of asymmetric loss to practical asymmetric-risk scenarios, showing improved performance.
- Strength: novel approach that models uncertainty in QE rather than producing only point estimates.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation metric (NLPD) that captures predictive uncertainty.
- Strength: demonstration of practical applications (post-editing, gisting) where uncertainty-aware models can help.
- Weakness: limited experiments — few datasets and models, raising concerns about generality.
- Weakness: no comparison to other state-of-the-art QE methods, making relative performance unclear.
- Weakness: limited analysis and interpretation of results, lacking deeper insights and visualizations.
- Question: how do the proposed models compare quantitatively to other state-of-the-art QE methods?
- Question: can the authors provide more analysis/visualizations of predictive distributions and uncertainty estimates?
- Question: how easily can these probabilistic QE models be extended to other NLP tasks like MT and text classification?
