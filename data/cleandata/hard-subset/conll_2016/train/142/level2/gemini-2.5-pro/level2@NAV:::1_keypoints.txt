- Paper summary: Advocates probabilistic QE using Gaussian Processes (Warped GPs, Matérn kernels) to produce calibrated predictive distributions rather than point estimates.

- Contribution 1: Empirically demonstrates MAE is insufficient for probabilistic QE and promotes distribution-aware metrics like Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD).

- Contribution 2: Shows how uncertainty estimates can be used with Bayes risk estimators to adapt a single model for asymmetric decision scenarios without retraining.

- Strength 1: Strong motivation and clear framing of the importance of uncertainty for practical translation decision-making.

- Strength 2: Rigorous intrinsic evaluation showing models with similar MAE can have very different predictive distributions; NLPD is an effective discriminative metric.

- Strength 3: Practical and novel application to asymmetric losses, linking predictive distributions to actionable Bayes risk decisions.

- Weakness 1: Limited to Gaussian Processes; lacks comparison or discussion of uncertainty methods for modern neural QE models (e.g., MC dropout, ensembles).

- Weakness 2: Uses a basic 17-feature set, leaving open how methods scale or interact with richer representations from current high-performance QE systems.

- Weakness 3: Warped GPs underperform on pessimistic linex loss; explanation is underexplored and weakens generality claims.

- Question 1: Does the pessimistic linex penalty reveal a fundamental limitation of the models or instability of linex with non-Gaussian Warped GP posteriors?

- Question 2: How do GP-based uncertainty methods complement or compete with neural uncertainty techniques like deep ensembles or Bayesian neural networks in QE?
