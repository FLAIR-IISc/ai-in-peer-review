- Standard point-estimate QE metrics (MAE, Pearson) ignore prediction uncertainty; authors propose a probabilistic QE approach using Gaussian Processes (GPs).
- Advocate using Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as a primary metric to capture uncertainty and penalize overconfident errors.
- Show predictive distributions from a single probabilistic model can yield optimal estimators for different asymmetric loss functions without retraining.
- Empirically compare GP variants (standard GP, Warped GP, Matérn) and report Warped GPs provide better-calibrated predictive distributions by NLPD.
- Strong, practical motivation linking uncertainty-aware QE to real-world asymmetric risk decisions (e.g., post-edit vs. discard).
- Experimental design convincingly shows NLPD distinguishes models with similar point estimates but differing uncertainty quality across three datasets.
- Demonstrates flexible application of probabilistic models to optimistic/pessimistic post-editing via minimum Bayes risk estimators.
- Paper is well-written and logically structured, with clear presentation of models and asymmetric-use scenarios.
- Limited methodological novelty: relies on established methods (GPs, Warped GPs, Matérn, NLPD) and is primarily an application/evaluation study.
- Lacks comparison to simpler uncertainty baselines (e.g., SVR ensembles, dropout NNs), leaving unclear whether GPs are necessary for reported benefits.
- Warped GPs perform worse in the pessimistic linex-loss scenario; this counter-intuitive result is underanalyzed and weakens generality claims.
- Experiments use a simple 17-feature set; unclear if findings generalize to high-dimensional, state-of-the-art QE feature spaces.
- Question: Have the authors analyzed calibration (e.g., calibration plots) to explain poor Warped GP performance in the high-penalty tails?
- Question: Why was an ensemble SVR baseline omitted, and how would such simpler baselines be expected to perform on NLPD and asymmetric-risk tasks?
