- Argues point estimates in QE are insufficient and advocates probabilistic approaches that provide uncertainty measures.
- Investigates Gaussian Processes (Matérn kernels + warping) to produce well-calibrated predictive distributions for QE.
- Promotes NLPD over MAE to evaluate full predictive distributions and demonstrates Bayes risk estimators for asymmetric loss without retraining.

- Shows models with similar MAE can differ substantially in uncertainty quality, a difference captured by NLPD.
- Demonstrates adapting a single GP posterior to different asymmetric cost scenarios via Bayes risk estimators, avoiding retraining.
- Empirically finds Warped GPs and Matérn kernels (especially warping) yield better-calibrated models for positive, non-Gaussian targets.

- Strong, clear motivation with concrete real-world examples (post-editing filtering, gisting).
- Rigorous evaluation methodology; NLPD effectively distinguishes uncertainty quality beyond traditional metrics.
- Practical and elegant asymmetric risk analysis using Bayes risk estimators, with useful deployment implications.
- High clarity and readability; complex methods and results are well explained.

- Exclusive focus on GPs; lacks discussion/comparison with other probabilistic methods (Bayesian NNs, ensembles) and GP scalability.
- Experiments use a standard 17-feature set; limited discussion on generalization to high-dimensional pretrained representations.
- Unresolved finding: Warped GPs underperform standard GPs for pessimistic linex loss and the analysis remains speculative.

- Why focus solely on GPs; how does deriving Bayes risk estimators for GP posteriors compare in practice to Monte Carlo approaches for Bayesian NNs?
- Does the standard GP’s linex advantage reflect it being a worse, high-variance model (e.g., assigning mass to impossible negatives), and what does this imply about linex as a metric?
