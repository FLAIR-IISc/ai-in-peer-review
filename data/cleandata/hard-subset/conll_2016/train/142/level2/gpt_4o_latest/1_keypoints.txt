- Paper proposes Gaussian Processes (GPs) and Warped GPs (WGPs) to provide uncertainty estimates for Machine Translation Quality Estimation (QE).
- Uses Matèrn kernels and WGPs to better handle noisy, non-Gaussian QE data.
- Evaluates models with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to account for predictive uncertainty beyond point metrics like MAE and Pearson’s r.
- Demonstrates utility of probabilistic QE in asymmetric-risk workflows (post-editing, gisting) using AL and linex losses.
- Argues probabilistic QE improves decision-making and can generalize to other text regression tasks.
- Contribution: introducing probabilistic GPs/WGPs for QE to provide calibrated uncertainty estimates for real-world workflows.
- Contribution: advocating NLPD as a superior evaluation metric that penalizes miscalibrated confidence.
- Contribution: applying uncertainty-aware models to asymmetric loss scenarios relevant to translation workflows.
- Strength: novel and practically relevant focus on uncertainty modeling for QE.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation combining NLPD with traditional metrics for better insight into calibration.
- Strength: GPs are shown to scale well for typical small QE datasets, with practical kernel/warp extensions.
- Strength: exploration of asymmetric loss functions demonstrates application-specific benefits of uncertainty estimates.
- Weakness: experiments limited to three datasets and specific language pairs, reducing generalizability.
- Weakness: potential overfitting on small datasets (e.g., en-es) noted but not deeply mitigated or analyzed.
- Weakness: inconclusive and unexplained results for linex loss in pessimistic scenarios, especially for WGPs.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons with non-GP probabilistic or recent deep learning uncertainty approaches.
- Question: how do the proposed models scale to larger datasets or more complex feature sets given GP computational limits?
- Question: why did linex loss perform worse for warped GPs in pessimistic scenarios, and are alternative losses viable?
- Question: have the authors considered evaluating these methods on other NLP regression tasks to show broader applicability?
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance conditional on addressing dataset diversity, overfitting analysis, linex results explanation, and comparisons with other probabilistic models.
