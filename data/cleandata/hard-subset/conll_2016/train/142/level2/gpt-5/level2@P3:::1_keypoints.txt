- Empirical study: Frames QE with probabilistic GPs, compares standard vs. Warped GPs and EQ vs. Matérn kernels, and evaluates with NLPD in addition to point metrics.
- Decision-theoretic contribution: Derives Bayes estimators from predictive posteriors for asymmetric linear and linex losses to adapt to changing costs without retraining.
- Evaluation: Tests models on three QE datasets (en–es, fr–en, en–de) with standardized features and 10-fold CV, yielding practical insights (warping helps; kernel effects data-dependent; linex pessimistic is hard).
- Motivation: Clearly argues uncertainty is crucial for translation workflows and that asymmetric risk maps well to practical scenarios.
- Methodology: Uses appropriate Bayesian GP methods (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) for noisy, low-data QE and leverages calibrated posteriors for Bayes estimators.
- Evaluation approach: Employs proper scoring (NLPD) and shows uncertainty-aware metrics reveal differences missed by MAE/Pearson’s r.
- Experimental protocol: Transparent and reproducible-in-spirit setup (three datasets, QuEst features, 10-fold CV, two-pass kernel optimization).
- Analysis: Offers insightful interpretations on overfitting, why warping improves NLL/NLPD, and variance-driven linex issues.
- Limited novelty: Techniques are established; the paper is mainly an application/analysis rather than a methodological advance.
- Narrow uncertainty baselines: Lacks comparisons to non-GP uncertainty methods (e.g., bootstrapped SVR, quantile regression, Bayesian neural approaches).
- Weak calibration diagnostics: Reports NLPD but omits reliability diagrams, PIT, interval coverage/width, and CRPS for deeper calibration assessment.
- Limited feature scope: Uses only 17 QuEst features, which may limit conclusions for stronger QE setups with richer features or learned representations.
- Heteroscedastic/heavy-tail handling: Considers only homoscedastic Gaussian likelihoods (plus warping); doesn’t test Student-t or heteroscedastic GPs that might address linex issues.
- Calibration question: Are reliability diagrams, PIT, interval coverage, or CRPS consistent with the observed NLPD improvements?
- Linex sensitivity: How sensitive are results to the linex w parameter, and would heavier-tailed likelihoods or variance inflation mitigate bias?
- Heteroscedasticity question: Was a heteroscedastic noise model considered, and do residuals show input-dependent noise after warping?
- Baseline question: How do bootstrapped SVR quantiles or quantile regression perform on NLPD/CRPS and asymmetric losses for contextual comparison?
- Practicality question: What are training/inference times per fold/dataset and how many hyperparameters (including warping) were optimized; any optimization instabilities?
- ARD question: Do learned lengthscales give interpretable feature relevance across language pairs?
- Essential suggestion: Add calibration diagnostics (reliability/PIT, interval coverage, CRPS) to substantiate calibrated uncertainty claims.
- Essential suggestion: Include at least one non-GP uncertainty baseline (e.g., bagged SVR or quantile regression) to contextualize GP benefits.
- Optional suggestion: Experiment with heavy-tailed or heteroscedastic likelihoods (e.g., Student-t) to probe linex pessimistic behavior.
- Optional suggestion: Report sensitivity analyses over w for AL and linex and show performance curves rather than single points.
- Optional suggestion: Report ARD-derived feature relevance and discuss cross-lingual patterns.
- Optional suggestion: Release code and scripts to improve reproducibility.
- Overall: Timely and useful case for uncertainty-aware QE showing Warped GPs and NLPD benefits; strengthening calibration analysis and baseline comparisons would increase impact and generality.
