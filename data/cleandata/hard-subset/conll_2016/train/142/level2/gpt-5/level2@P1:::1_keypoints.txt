- Paper proposes using Gaussian Processes (GPs) and Warped GPs to produce calibrated predictive distributions for QE and evaluates them with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) in addition to point metrics.
- Shows that predictive distributions enable decision-making under asymmetric risk via Bayes risk estimators without retraining.

- Empirically, Warped GPs yield better-calibrated uncertainty (lower NLPD) than standard GPs, and MAE/Pearson’s r can miss uncertainty differences.
- Demonstrates application of Bayes risk decision rules to asymmetric losses (e.g., AL), showing practical benefits for QE workflows.
- Argues for adopting proper scoring rules (NLPD) and appropriate kernel/likelihood choices (Matérn, output warping) when modeling QE uncertainty.

- Motivation is clear and timely: point estimates are insufficient for practical QE tasks like post-editing and gisting.
- Probabilistic modeling choices are appropriate: GPs suit small QE datasets and Matérn kernels plus output warping fit noisy, positive-support targets.
- Introducing NLPD to QE is a substantive contribution and the paper properly distinguishes training NLL from test NLPD.
- The Bayes risk decision layer is practical and appealing for production where risk preferences change over time.
- Experimental protocol is careful: cross-validation across three language pairs, standard features, random restarts, and ARD refinement.

- Methodological novelty is limited: mainly applies established GP tools and scoring rules; novelty is stronger in evaluation perspective than modeling.
- Calibration analysis is underdeveloped: missing PITs, coverage, reliability diagrams, CRPS, and interval coverage to validate calibration claims.
- Linex estimator derivation appears mismatched to the stated loss; closed-form Gaussian solution may not apply for Warped GP’s non-Gaussian predictive distribution, likely explaining poor linex results.
- Missing non-GP uncertainty baselines (e.g., bootstrapped SVR, quantile regression, Bayesian linear regression) weakens claims that GPs are superior.
- No reported dispersion estimates or statistical tests across folds; significance, CIs, and sensitivity analyses (warping complexity, kernel, priors) are needed.
- Kernel findings are inconclusive and feature set is small; results may be dataset-specific and should be tested with richer features or other QE targets (e.g., HTER).

- Clarify linex loss definition and whether the Bayes estimator was computed exactly (ŷ = −(1/w) log E[exp(−w y)]) or approximated by μ − (wσ^2)/2.
- Explain how quantiles for AL loss were computed under Warped GP (analytical, numerical inversion, or Monte Carlo) and confirm τ = w/(1+w).
- Provide calibration diagnostics (coverage, PIT) to support the “well-calibrated” claim.
- Indicate whether robust/heteroscedastic likelihoods (e.g., Student-t, heteroscedastic GPs) were tried.
- Will code and hyperparameter settings (warping terms, priors/constraints) be released for reproducibility?

- Recommend adding CRPS as a complementary proper scoring rule less sensitive to extreme tails.
- Recommend reporting standard errors/CI over folds and conducting paired statistical tests for robustness.
- Suggest a learning-curve analysis to support claims about overfitting differences between NLL and NLPD.
- Note minor equation typos/formatting issues (e.g., kernel equations, ambiguous “ww+1” quantile) that need proofreading.
