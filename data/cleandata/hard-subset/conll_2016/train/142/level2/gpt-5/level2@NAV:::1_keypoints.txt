- Studies uncertainty for sentence-level MT QE using Gaussian Processes (GPs), Matérn kernels, and Warped GPs, evaluated with probabilistic metrics (NLPD).
- Shows posterior predictive distributions enable decision-making under asymmetric risks via Bayes-optimal estimators for asymmetric linear and linex losses.
- Empirically finds on three QE datasets that Warped GPs yield better-calibrated posteriors than standard GPs and that MAE/correlation can miss uncertainty quality differences.

- Clear motivation: argues point estimates are inadequate for QE workflows and motivates the need for calibrated uncertainty.
- Sound probabilistic evaluation: uses NLPD and NLL vs NLPD comparisons to emphasize calibration and diagnose overfitting.
- Practical decision-theoretic contribution: maps predictive posteriors to Bayes-optimal decisions for asymmetric losses with actionable insights.
- Careful modeling: justifies Warped GPs for positive targets, uses ARD and Matérn kernels, and analyzes when tanh warp beats log warp and dataset-size effects.
- Reproducible setup: cross-validation, QuEst17 features, and optimization details are clearly provided.

- Limited novelty: methods are established and the work is primarily an application study rather than a novel algorithm.
- Narrow baselines: lacks comparison to neural QE uncertainty methods (Bayesian NNs, MC dropout, ensembles) and heteroscedastic GPs.
- Missing calibration diagnostics: no reliability diagrams, PIT histograms, or CRPS to strengthen calibration claims.
- Incomplete asymmetric-loss analysis: linex pessimistic behavior is noted but not diagnosed or resolved with variance/tail analyses.
- Feature and scalability limits: uses 17 handcrafted features on small datasets with no discussion of computational cost or large-scale extensions.

- Request calibration plots (predictive interval coverage, PIT) and CRPS to substantiate calibration claims.
- Ask for comparisons of Warped GPs to neural QE methods with uncertainty (MC dropout, ensembles) on the same data.
- Inquire whether heteroscedastic or truncated/nonnegative likelihoods were tried to address linex pessimistic failures.
- Ask about sensitivity to number of tanh terms in the warp and to ARD priors/regularization.
