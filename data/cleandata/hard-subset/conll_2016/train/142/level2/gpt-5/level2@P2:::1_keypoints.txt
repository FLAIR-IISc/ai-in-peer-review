- Paper addresses uncertainty estimation for sentence-level MT QE using Gaussian Process models (Matérn, EQ, Warped GPs) and evaluates predictive distributions rather than only point metrics.
- Contribution: systematic empirical evaluation showing Warped GPs give better-calibrated posteriors (NLPD) than standard GPs.
- Contribution: demonstrates decision-theoretic use of predictive posteriors for asymmetric losses (AL and linex) and provides Bayes estimators for those losses.
- Contribution: shows conventional point metrics (MAE, Pearson’s r) miss important differences in uncertainty quality.
- Strength: timely and practically significant framing; uncertainty is crucial for real QE workflows like post-editing filtering.
- Strength: appropriate probabilistic modeling choices—Warped GPs for positive targets and Matérn kernels for less-smooth/noisy functions.
- Strength: use of proper scoring (NLPD) is correct and informative; warping improves generalization of uncertainty.
- Strength: asymmetric-risk experiments are useful and show how to adapt predictions without retraining; quantile-based estimator for AL is principled.
- Strength: careful experimental setup (multi-language, QuEst features, 10-fold CV, two-stage hyperparameter procedure) and honest discussion of inconclusive cases.
- Weakness: incorrect generality of linex Bayes estimator—formula using μ and σ² only holds for Gaussian predictive posteriors; Warped GP posteriors are non-Gaussian so estimator may be biased.
- Weakness: lack of calibration diagnostics beyond NLPD—no coverage, PIT, calibration curves, or sharpness vs. calibration analysis.
- Weakness: limited uncertainty baselines—no comparisons to Bayesian NNs, MC dropout, deep ensembles, quantile regression, random forests, or bootstrapped SVR.
- Weakness: sparse analysis of warping complexity and overfitting; no prior sensitivity/regularization study or comparison to simpler monotone transforms (Box–Cox, log-shift).
- Weakness: insufficient statistical reporting—no variance/SE across folds or significance tests to support key claims.
- Question: how was the linex Bayes estimator computed for Warped GP posteriors—Gaussian approximation or exact E[exp(−w y)] via integration/sampling?
- Question: how were predictive quantiles obtained for Warped GPs for AL—numerical inversion, Gauss–Hermite in latent space, or sampling?
- Question: were calibration diagnostics (coverage, PIT, reliability diagrams) evaluated; if so, please report them?
- Question: are there zeros in post-editing rates and how was log-based warping handled (log or log-shift) and applied consistently across models?
- Question: sensitivity to number of tanh terms, warp priors/regularization, and ARD lengthscales—any signs of overfitting beyond NLL/NLPD discrepancies?
- Suggestion: add calibration plots and interval coverage metrics to substantiate “well-calibrated” claims.
- Suggestion: include at least one alternative uncertainty baseline (e.g., MC dropout or deep ensemble) and a non-GP quantile method for context.
- Suggestion: provide a small end-to-end decision simulation (e.g., accept/reject MT with downstream utility) to make cost implications concrete.
- Suggestion: clean up references formatting and clarify code/data split release for reproducibility.
- Overall: solid, well-motivated paper that foregrounds uncertainty in QE; addressing the linex estimator issue and adding calibration diagnostics would substantially strengthen it.
