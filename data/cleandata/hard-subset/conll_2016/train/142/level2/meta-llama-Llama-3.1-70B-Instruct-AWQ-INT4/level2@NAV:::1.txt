**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Exploring Prediction Uncertainty in Machine Translation Quality Estimation" presents a novel approach to machine translation quality estimation (QE) by exploring prediction uncertainty using probabilistic methods. The authors argue that traditional QE models, which provide point estimates, are limited in their ability to capture uncertainty and make informed decisions in real-world translation environments. They propose the use of Gaussian Processes (GPs) and Warped GPs to model uncertainty and evaluate their performance using the Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) metric.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Probabilistic Modeling of QE**: The authors propose the use of GPs and Warped GPs to model uncertainty in QE, which allows for the estimation of predictive distributions and the calculation of uncertainty estimates.
2. **Evaluation Metric**: The authors introduce the NLPD metric as a more comprehensive evaluation metric for QE models, which takes into account the entire predictive distribution, rather than just point estimates.
3. **Asymmetric Loss Functions**: The authors demonstrate the use of asymmetric loss functions, such as the asymmetric linear (AL) loss and the linear exponential (linex) loss, to capture different risk scenarios in QE.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel Approach**: The paper presents a novel approach to QE by exploring prediction uncertainty using probabilistic methods, which is a significant departure from traditional QE models.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their proposed models using multiple metrics, including NLPD, MAE, and Pearson's r.
3. **Real-World Applicability**: The paper demonstrates the potential of the proposed approach in real-world translation environments, where uncertainty estimates can inform decision-making.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Experiments**: The authors only report results for three language pairs, which may not be representative of all possible QE scenarios.
2. **Small Dataset Size**: The authors acknowledge that the en-es dataset is small, which may lead to overfitting and affect the reliability of the results.
3. **Limited Exploration of Asymmetric Loss Functions**: The authors only explore two asymmetric loss functions, which may not be exhaustive, and more research is needed to fully understand their impact on QE.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to larger datasets and more language pairs?
2. Can the authors provide more insights into the choice of hyperparameters for the GP and Warped GP models?
3. How do the authors plan to address the issue of overfitting in small datasets, such as the en-es dataset?
