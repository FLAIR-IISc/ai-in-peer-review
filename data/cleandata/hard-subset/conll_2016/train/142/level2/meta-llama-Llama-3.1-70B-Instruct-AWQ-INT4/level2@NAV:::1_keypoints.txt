- Proposes probabilistic QE using Gaussian Processes and Warped GPs to model prediction uncertainty.  
- Evaluates models with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to assess full predictive distributions.  
- Demonstrates using asymmetric loss functions (asymmetric linear and linex) to capture different risk scenarios.  
- Novel departure from traditional point-estimate QE models by focusing on uncertainty estimation.  
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation using NLPD, MAE, and Pearson’s r.  
- Shows potential real-world applicability where uncertainty estimates inform decision-making.  
- Experiments are limited to only three language pairs, reducing generality.  
- Small en–es dataset raises concerns about overfitting and result reliability.  
- Limited exploration of asymmetric loss functions (only two types examined).  
- Seeks plans for extending approach to larger datasets and more language pairs.  
- Requests clarification on hyperparameter choices for GP and Warped GP models.  
- Asks how the authors will address overfitting on small datasets like en–es.
