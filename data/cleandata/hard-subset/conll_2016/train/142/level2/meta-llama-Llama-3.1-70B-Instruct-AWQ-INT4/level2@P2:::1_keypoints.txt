- Proposes probabilistic Quality Estimation (QE) for machine translation using Gaussian Processes (GPs) to obtain well-calibrated uncertainty estimates.  
- Investigates GP extensions (Matèrn kernels, Warped GPs) to improve QE performance.  
- Evaluates models with Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to account for full posterior predictive distributions.  
- Explores application of probabilistic QE in asymmetric risk scenarios (different costs for over/underestimation).  
- Strength: Addresses a well-motivated problem where uncertainty affects decision-making in MT.  
- Strength: Provides a thorough evaluation using NLPD, which considers full predictive uncertainty.  
- Strength: Demonstrates relevance to real-world asymmetric risk situations.  
- Weakness: Lacks comprehensive comparison with common QE methods like SVR and neural networks.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of results—insufficient exploration of kernel choices and hyperparameter effects.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of practical implications and how models would improve decision-making in deployment.  
- Question: How do the proposed models compare with SVR and neural networks in performance and computational cost?  
- Question: Can the authors provide detailed analysis on the impact of different kernels and hyperparameters?  
- Question: How can these models be applied in practice, and what are their real-world benefits and limitations?
