- Proposes probabilistic MTQE to provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates beyond point predictions.  
- Introduces Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) as an evaluation metric that accounts for model uncertainty.  
- Investigates Gaussian Processes (GPs) for MTQE and extends them with Matérn kernels and Warped GPs.  
- Demonstrates GP-based models in asymmetric risk scenarios where loss functions are non-symmetric.  

- Main contribution: advocating NLPD to evaluate probabilistic MTQE models.  
- Main contribution: adapting and evaluating GPs (Matérn kernels, Warped GPs) for MTQE.  
- Main contribution: showing applicability of these models under asymmetric risk/loss settings.  

- Strength: Thorough critique of traditional point-estimate metrics and motivation for uncertainty-aware evaluation.  
- Strength: Detailed investigation and adaptation of GP variants for the MTQE task.  
- Strength: Practical focus on asymmetric risk scenarios relevant to real-world translation.  

- Weakness: Assumes reader familiarity with GPs, which may limit accessibility.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to three datasets, raising generalization concerns.  
- Weakness: Lacks comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art MTQE models.
