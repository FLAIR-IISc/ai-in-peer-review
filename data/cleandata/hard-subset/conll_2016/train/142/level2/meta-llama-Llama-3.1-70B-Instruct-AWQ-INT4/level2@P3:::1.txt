**Summary of the Paper**

The paper explores the use of probabilistic methods for Machine Translation Quality Estimation (MTQE) to provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates. The authors argue that traditional point estimate metrics are not sufficient for real-world translation environments and propose the use of Negative Log Predictive Density (NLPD) to evaluate the performance of probabilistic MTQE models. They investigate Gaussian Processes (GPs) as a probabilistic framework for MTQE and propose extensions to the standard GP model, including the use of Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs. The authors also demonstrate the application of these models in asymmetric risk scenarios, where the loss function is not symmetric.

**Main Contributions**

1. The proposal of NLPD as a metric to evaluate the performance of probabilistic MTQE models, which takes into account the uncertainty estimates of the models.
2. The investigation of GPs as a probabilistic framework for MTQE, including the use of Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs.
3. The demonstration of the application of these models in asymmetric risk scenarios, where the loss function is not symmetric.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of traditional point estimate metrics in MTQE and proposes a more comprehensive evaluation metric, NLPD.
2. The authors provide a detailed investigation of GPs as a probabilistic framework for MTQE, including the use of Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs.
3. The paper demonstrates the application of these models in asymmetric risk scenarios, which is a common scenario in real-world translation environments.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the reader is familiar with GP models and their applications in MTQE, which may not be the case for all readers.
2. The experimental evaluation of the proposed models is limited to three datasets, and it is not clear how well the models will generalize to other datasets.
3. The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison of the proposed models with other state-of-the-art MTQE models.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the Matèrn kernels and Warped GPs were implemented in the GP framework?
2. How did you select the hyperparameters for the GP models, and what was the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the performance of the models?
3. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the preprocessing of the data and the evaluation metrics used?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of overfitting in the GP models, particularly in the case of small datasets?
5. Can you provide more details on the application of the proposed models in asymmetric risk scenarios, including the selection of the loss function and the evaluation metrics used?
