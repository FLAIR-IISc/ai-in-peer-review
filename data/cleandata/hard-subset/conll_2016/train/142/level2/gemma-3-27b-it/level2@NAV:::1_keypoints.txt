- Studies probabilistic methods (Gaussian Processes) for MT Quality Estimation with emphasis on well-calibrated uncertainty estimates.
- Argues that standard QE evaluation metrics ignore prediction uncertainty and proposes evaluating full posterior predictive distributions.
- Empirically shows that differences in uncertainty modeling are not captured by point-estimate metrics like MAE or Pearson’s r.
- Explores different GP configurations (kernels, warping functions) and their effects on uncertainty calibration.
- Demonstrates a practical application using asymmetric loss functions to leverage uncertainty in MT workflows (e.g., post-editing).
- Strength: rigorous experimental setup across multiple language pairs using established datasets.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including NLPD in addition to point-estimate metrics for a nuanced performance view.
- Strength: clear motivation tied to practical MT workflow needs.
- Strength: thorough and well-executed exploration of GP configurations.
- Weakness: discussion of asymmetric loss functions is underdeveloped and feels tentative.
- Weakness: linex loss results in the pessimistic scenario are inconclusive without a convincing explanation.
- Weakness: benefits of Matèrn kernels over EQ kernel are not consistently demonstrated.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational costs and scalability limitations of GP models.
- Questions for authors: elaborate on GP computational challenges for larger datasets; explain hypotheses and further experiments to clarify inconclusive linex loss results; detail how uncertainty-based active learning could be implemented in the proposed QE framework.
