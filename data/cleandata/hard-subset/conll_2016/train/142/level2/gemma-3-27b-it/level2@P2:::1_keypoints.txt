- Central claim: Gaussian Process (GP) probabilistic models provide well-calibrated uncertainty estimates that improve MT quality estimation (QE) decision-making beyond point estimates, especially in asymmetric risk scenarios.  
- Primary contribution: Thorough empirical evaluation of GP configurations (kernels, warping functions) using distributional metrics (NLPD) rather than just point metrics.  
- Secondary contribution: Demonstration of practical benefits of uncertainty-aware QE via asymmetric loss functions and Bayes risk estimation in post-editing/gisting scenarios.  
- Resource contribution: Clear, comprehensive overview of GP techniques useful for researchers new to probabilistic QE.  

Strengths:
- Novel evaluation perspective: Advocates for NLPD and distributional evaluation, showing traditional metrics miss uncertainty differences.  
- Comprehensive GP exploration: Systematic comparison of Matèrn kernels and warped GPs with good theoretical explanations.  
- Practical application: Shows tangible benefits of uncertainty estimates in asymmetric (pessimistic/optimistic) decision settings.  
- Presentation quality: Well-written, logically structured, and accessible explanations of complex GP concepts.  
- Reproducibility: Sufficient dataset, feature, and experimental detail to enable reproduction.

Weaknesses:
- Limited model scope: Focuses only on GPs; misses comparison to other probabilistic models (Bayesian NNs, deep ensembles) given modern advances.  
- Linex loss inconclusiveness: Pessimistic linex results are unclear and may bias toward higher-variance models, weakening general claims.  
- Feature limitations: Uses standard feature set; impact of modern/sophisticated features not explored.  
- Dataset dependency: Warping function performance (tanh vs log) appears dataset-dependent and needs broader testing.  
- Lack of error analysis: No detailed analysis of prediction errors or how uncertainty correlates with those errors.

Key questions for authors:
- Why is NLL unreliable on small datasets—can you formalize or further justify this observation?  
- For pessimistic linex, why might models not leverage variance effectively, and what fixes do you propose?  
- How sensitive are results to hyperparameters (especially tanh-warped GPs) and variability across random restarts?  
- What are the computational costs of training/evaluating the GP variants versus simpler point-estimate QE models?

Additional comments:
- Notation improvements recommended (e.g., define σv before use in the EQ kernel).  
- Figure 1 (asymmetric loss functions) is helpful.  
- Related work should include more recent uncertainty estimation advances in deep learning.
