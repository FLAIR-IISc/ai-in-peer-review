- Investigates Gaussian Processes for MT Quality Estimation with emphasis on well-calibrated uncertainty to improve decisions like post-editing vs. accept.
- Evaluates various GP models (kernels, likelihoods, warped GPs) using NLPD and an asymmetric risk scenario, showing uncertainty differences missed by MAE/Pearson.
- Primary contribution: thorough empirical study of probabilistic QE and strong argument for distribution-aware evaluation (NLPD).
- Contribution: explores asymmetric loss (pessimistic vs. optimistic) and Bayes risk estimation for practical decision-making.
- Contribution: comprehensive comparison of GP configurations serving as a useful resource for researchers.
- Strength: Addresses an important practical limitation (lack of uncertainty) with a clear post-editing motivation.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across datasets, GP variants, and both intrinsic/extrinsic metrics; good use of NLPD.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured technical exposition of GPs, Matérn kernels, and warped GPs with accessible math.
- Strength: Practical application focus—shows how uncertainty estimates can be operationalized via asymmetric risk and Bayes risk.
- Strength: Extensive and comprehensive related work situating the paper in the broader literature.
- Weakness: Focus exclusively on GPs without sufficient discussion of other probabilistic models or GP limitations (scalability, modeling complexity).
- Weakness: Inconclusive linex loss results in the pessimistic scenario need deeper analysis and investigation of alternatives.
- Weakness: Limited feature set (for comparability) may constrain model performance and influence uncertainty estimates.
- Weakness: Dataset size disparities (notably smaller en‑es) could affect robustness; needs formal analysis of dataset-size effects.
- Question: Can you discuss computational challenges scaling GPs and whether sparse/approximate GP methods were considered?
- Question: Have you tested alternative loss functions or approaches to address the linex loss issues with low-variance predictions?
- Question: How sensitive are results to feature choice; were larger feature sets tried for some language pairs?
- Question: Why does en‑es behave differently from fr‑en and en‑de, and how does that affect generalizability?
- Comment: Recommend adding a table summarizing tested GP configurations (kernel, likelihood, warping).
- Comment: Paper is well-written with consistent notation and terminology.
- Comment: Related work coverage and citations are thorough.
