- Paper applies Gaussian processes to regress post-editing time/rates for MT quality estimation.
- Well-structured paper with a clear introduction highlighting problems of QE point estimates in real applications.
- Strong explanation of asymmetric risk scenarios and their implications for different estimators.
- Provides a substantial introduction to GPs—may be lengthy for experts but useful for general readers.
- GP methodology, kernel choices, and warping are explained clearly and are easy to follow.
- Research questions are interesting and well phrased.
- Question: justify choosing post-editing rates over the more common HTER prediction in QE research.
- Confusion: Section 3.2’s first paragraph—unclear what trend is seen for fr-en & en-de that isn’t present for en-es (NLL/NLPD decrease with warped GPs across datasets).
- Suggestion: include an existing point-estimate model baseline in result tables to contextualize overall quality.
- Concern: NLL and NLPD values are hard to interpret—provide notion of what constitutes a drastic reduction, e.g., qualitative examples.
- Section 4 is very well written and explains results intuitively.
- Overall: paper usefully highlights problems with point estimates and importance of uncertainty, though it doesn’t advance SOTA or offer major modeling novelty.
- Minor edits: change “over and underestimates” to “over- and underestimates.”
- Minor fix: Figure 1 caption lists wrong line colors (blue and green, not blue and red).
- Minor suggestion: cite the GP toolkit used for modeling if applicable.
