- Paper targets broad-coverage semantic parsing requiring DAGs with multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities, using UCCA as a testbed.
- Authors compare conversion-based parsing (map UCCA to related formalisms) and a direct transition-based parser (BSP) that supports all three structural properties.
- Evaluation on UCCA corpora (including out-of-domain) shows competitive primary-edge performance and some ability to predict remote edges.
- Main contribution 1: Empirical evidence that transition-based methods, especially LSTM sequence models, can achieve reasonable primary-edge F-scores on UCCA via conversion and direct parsing.
- Main contribution 2: A direct transition system (BSP) for DAGs with discontinuities and non-terminals, plus feature design and dynamic-oracle training for UCCA.
- Main contribution 3: Conversion protocols between UCCA and discontinuous constituency/dependency representations to reuse existing parsers.
- Strength: Clear problem motivation and scope, framing three structural requirements useful beyond UCCA.
- Strength: Solid empirical coverage across three experimental settings and domains; LSTM dependency parser excels on primary edges; BSP robust and predicts some remotes.
- Strength: Transition-based approach is well justified, with thoughtful dynamic-oracle training and impactful features (e.g., ratio feature) supported by ablations.
- Strength: Promised public release of converters and parsers benefits the community.
- Strength: Clear explanation of UCCA background (Scenes, primary vs remote, phenomena) aids reader understanding.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty relative to existing DAG parsers; transition set largely generic and little UCCA-specific innovation.
- Weakness: Missing relevant DAG/AMR/SDP baselines (some available) — unclear why existing systems were not compared.
- Weakness: Incomplete baseline coverage for constituency conversion; other conversion-based methods (e.g., Fernández-González & Martins 2015) should be included.
- Weakness: Evaluation misaligned with SDP conventions; lack of standard SDP metrics weakens cross-task comparisons.
- Weakness: Surface dependency conversion is highly lossy (drops non-terminals and remotes), reducing informativeness of dependency track.
- Weakness: Insufficient error analysis and discussion of which phenomena (discontinuities, remotes, non-terminals) drive difficulty.
- Question: Define “grounded semantic” earlier in the paper.
- Question: Why not pursue a rich-constituency-only approach (enriched labels/traces) using strong PCFG parsers as baselines?
- Question: Clarify “implicit units with no textual correspondent” with examples/references and explain their exclusion.
- Question: How does UCCA handle quantifier scope or phenomena addressed by MRS/DRT; is such semantics underspecified and what are downstream implications?
- Question: Provide error analysis breaking down BSP failures across discontinuity, remotes, non-terminals, coordination, and MWEs.
- Minor: Clarify the paper’s primary narrative focus (parser, general model, or parsability of scheme).
- Minor: Add Fernández-González & Martins (2015) when discussing UPARSE and justify parser choices.
- Minor: Consider richer dependency conversions (e.g., SemEval SDP-style) to retain more semantics.
- Minor: Double-check references (spellings/years) and ensure consistency and completeness.
- Minor: For reproducibility, include available DAG parsers as baselines and cite provided baseline links.
- Overall recommendation: Accept for CoNLL discussion contingent on strengthening experimental comparisons and clarifying representational and evaluation choices.
