- Paper presents the first broad-coverage UCCA semantic parsers, highlighting differences from CoNLL semantic dependencies and AMR, and evaluates several parsing methods including a transition-based parser.
- Developing a UCCA parser is a reasonable goal given available data, but the introduction/background wrongly implies UCCA is the only graph-based SR worth studying.
- Reviewer disputes the three proposed criteria for semantic formalisms, questioning the claimed necessity of nonterminal nodes and the asserted advantages of “grounded” representations.
- Recommends avoiding the term “grounded” (suggests “anchored” or “lexicalized”) and urges the authors to rephrase and more carefully argue the introduction.
- Parser implementation appears sound, but evaluation is weak: MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and F-scores on remote edges are low.
- Notes prior work on tree approximations and reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could improve dependency-based baselines, reducing the apparent advantage of the BSP parser.
