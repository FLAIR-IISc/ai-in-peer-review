- Paper presents first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, contrasts UCCA with CoNLL dependency graphs and AMR, and proposes a transition-based parser with evaluation.  
- Given available UCCA corpora, building a UCCA parser is a reasonable and valuable goal.  
- Introduction/background overstates UCCA’s uniqueness and should be reframed and argued more carefully.  
- Reviewer disputes the three proposed criteria for semantic formalisms, questioning their generality and necessity.  
- Doubts that “nonterminal” nodes add real expressive power; richer edge labels might suffice for problematic cases like coordination.  
- Notes that discontinuity concerns don’t apply to ungrounded SRs and questions claimed benefits of “grounded” representations over AMR.  
- Suggests “grounded” is overloaded; recommends alternative terminology (e.g., “anchored” or “lexicalized”).  
- Parser design seems reasonable though not fully checked by the reviewer.  
- Evaluation is unconvincing: a standard MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and BSP’s remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Points out extensive prior work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) that could boost dependency-tree baselines and reduce the BSP parser’s apparent advantage.
