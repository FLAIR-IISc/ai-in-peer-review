- Introduces first broad-coverage UCCA semantic parsers, including a transition-based parser that builds UCCA graphs directly.
- Developing a UCCA parser is reasonable and could be a valuable contribution given available formalism and data.
- Introduction/background adopts an overconfident tone, implying UCCA is the only graph-based semantic representation worth pursuing—needs a more balanced framing.
- Disagreements with proposed design criteria: unclear that nonterminal nodes add true expressive power versus richer edge labels or alternative dependency structures.
- Discussion of discontinuity is muddled and partly orthogonal for ungrounded representations.
- Advantages of “grounding” over AMR-style representations are not convincingly argued; the term “grounded” is overloaded and alternatives like “anchored” or “lexicalized” might be clearer.
- Intro should more carefully distinguish representational convenience, expressivity, and learnability, and state if goals are improved learnability/annotation consistency rather than expressivity.
- Parsing methodology appears sound overall, though the reviewer did not verify all algorithmic details.
- Evaluation is weak: a MaltParser baseline outperforms the BSP parser on primary edges, and F-scores on remote edges are unconvincing.
- Prior work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction (e.g., Agić et al. 2015) could improve baselines and narrow the reported advantage of the BSP parser.
- Paper should include stronger baselines using tree-approximation and reconstruction methods to establish empirical benefits.
- Recommended fixes: adopt fairer framing, provide clearer representational justification, and run more competitive baseline comparisons to strengthen the paper.
