- Novelty claim questioned: transition system does not appear to exploit properties unique to UCCA, so claim of first semantically oriented parser is overstated.
- Proposed transitions (e.g., for remote edges) could be handled by existing graph parsers with enriched label schemes.
- The work reads as a general graph-parsing solution tied to UCCA mainly via feature templates, with count and unigram features dominating.
- Missing comparison to existing transition-based graph parsers (e.g., Almeida & Martins; Ribeyre et al.) despite public implementations [1,2].
- Phrase-based experiments should include another LCFRS-capable parser (e.g., Fernández-González & Martins 2015) as a stronger baseline; cite UParse when first introduced.
- Evaluation omits shared-task metrics used in SDP/SemEval, preventing direct comparison to related systems and weakening empirical claims.
- Recommend clearer definition and earlier introduction of “grounded semantics” (term used before being defined).
- Suggest adding a constituent-tree baseline (e.g., Berkeley parser with rich node labels and propagated traces) as a meaningful alternative.
- Current conversion to surface dependency trees loses too much information; use a richer conversion (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014) if comparing to dependency parsers.
- Clarify and exemplify the claim about “implicit units that have no correspondent in the text” or provide references.
- Question how abstract/underspecified UCCA is compared to MRS, DRT, DM, PCEDT — provide error analysis and discussion of how “parsable” and informative the scheme is in practice.
- The three principles motivating UCCA (graph-based, non-terminals, discontinuities) are broad/ad hoc and need a clearer, wider justification.
- Paper mixes goals (a UCCA parser, a generally semantic parser, and an argument about parsable semantic schemes) and should state its primary aim more clearly.
- Provide error analysis and more direct comparison points to strengthen empirical conclusions.
