- Paper introduces a transition-based graph parser for UCCA to handle graphs, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Novelty concern: the transition system does not clearly exploit UCCA-specific semantics and could be framed as general graph parsing.  
- Existing DAG-capable transition/higher-order parsers (e.g., Almeida & Martins; Ribeyre) should be used as comparative baselines.  
- Handling of remote edges may be achievable via labeling schemes rather than dedicated transitions.  
- Anchoring to UCCA appears to rely mainly on feature templates with limited shown impact beyond unigram/count features.  
- Phrase-structure experiments need stronger baselines (e.g., other LCFRS-capable parsers like Fernández-González & Martins); UParse reference is missing initially.  
- Dependency conversion is likely too lossy for UCCA; recommend richer conversion methods (e.g., Schluter et al., 2014).  
- Evaluation should include SDP-style/common metrics to enable cross-framework comparison and strengthen the empirical narrative.  
- Overall positioning is unclear: paper should state whether it aims to (a) parse UCCA, (b) propose a general semantic model, or (c) show UCCA is parsable.  
- Recommend adding targeted error analysis to determine whether low scores arise from annotation properties or modeling choices.  
- “Grounded semantics” should be defined when first introduced.  
- Clarify and exemplify UCCA’s implicit units (units without textual correspondents) with references/examples.  
- Discuss how UCCA handles quantifier scope relative to MRS/DRT/DM/PCEDT, quantify underspecification, and analyze its impact on parsing.  
- The three guiding principles (graphs, non-terminals, discontinuities) feel ad hoc and broadly applicable; argue more clearly what the paper is advocating.  
- Overall recommendation: include strong graph- and phrase-structure baselines, align evaluation with shared metrics, and provide clearer framing and error analysis before publication.
