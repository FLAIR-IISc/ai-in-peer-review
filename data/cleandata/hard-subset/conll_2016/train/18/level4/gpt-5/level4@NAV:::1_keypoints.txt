- Introduces a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and argues semantic schemes should be graph-based, allow non-terminals, and permit discontinuities.  
- Novelty claim is weak: the transition set does not appear tailored to UCCA’s specifics and remote edges might be representable via label modifications.  
- Fails to compare against prior transition-based graph parsers from SemEval 2014–15 (e.g., Almeida & Martins; Ribeyre), weakening the claim of novelty.  
- The model seems tied to a feature template, but count and unigram features appear more influential than scheme-specific features.  
- Phrase-based experiments should be compared to Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins’ LCFRS-like parser; a reference for Uparse is missing.  
- Converting UCCA to surface dependency trees loses too much information; richer conversions (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014 SDP) should be used.  
- Evaluation metrics should follow SDP shared-task standards to enable comparison with competing systems and datasets.  
- The paper lacks error analysis to show how informative and “parsable” UCCA is relative to DM, PCEDT, MRS, or DRT; UCCA may be more underspecified.  
- Clarify or exemplify the claim that “UCCA graphs may contain implicit units that have no correspondent in the text.”  
- The three proposed principles are generic and not uniquely semantic; the paper conflates goals (UCCA parser vs semantic-analysis model vs parsable semantic scheme).  
- Recommend adding baselines using available DAG/graph parsers (DAGParser, TurboParser) and a constituent-tree baseline (Berkeley parser).  
- Minor fixes: introduce “grounded semantic” earlier, provide missing references, and address other random-order editorial points.  
- Overall: interesting and discussion-worthy (e.g., CoNLL) but publication needs stronger methodological rigor and empirical comparisons.
