- Summary: Studies UCCA parsing (DAGs with multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities), comparing lossy conversions to constituency/dependency parsers vs. a new transition-based DAG parser (BSP), with a new evaluation measure and in-/out-of-domain results.

- Contribution 1: Empirical assessment of conversion-based parsing for UCCA, including converters and upper bounds quantifying information loss.

- Contribution 2: A direct transition-based parser (BSP) for DAGs with discontinuities/non-terminals, plus a dynamic oracle and targeted features.

- Contribution 3: An evaluation protocol for DAGs (yield- and label-matching) and the first reported UCCA parsing results including remote edges.

- Strength: Clear problem framing isolating three structural properties relevant to broad semantic representations.

- Strength: Broad experimental evaluation comparing conversion-based and direct DAG parsing with in-domain and out-of-domain tests and upper bounds.

- Strength: Evidence that stronger local classifiers (LSTM) improve conversion-based dependency results, guiding future BSP improvements.

- Strength: BSP is the only model evaluated that predicts remote edges, demonstrating feasibility of direct multi-parent parsing.

- Strength: Public release of converters and parsers promotes reproducibility and follow-up work.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty relative to existing DAG/discontinuous constituency parsers; transitions largely mirror prior work and novelty needs clearer positioning.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons with existing DAG/SDP/AMR parsers (e.g., TurboParser variants, Ribeyre et al.), weakening claims about effectiveness.

- Weakness: Conversion choices discard substantial structure (non-terminals, remotes), making dependency results hard to interpret and possibly unfair.

- Weakness: Lack of error analysis and qualitative insight into specific failure modes (e.g., coordination, MWEs, discontinuities, remote vs. primary).

- Weakness: Positioning and clarity issues: goals and key terms are mixed/underdefined and the guiding properties need better justification versus other formalisms.

- Question: Which parts of BSP (transitions, oracle, features) are novel beyond prior DAG/discontinuous systems, and could remote vs. primary be modeled via labels instead?

- Question: Why were existing DAG parsers (SDP/AMR) not included as baselines via conversions, and can these comparisons be added?

- Question: Can more structure-preserving reductions (e.g., LCFRS, enhanced dependencies) or SDP-style metrics on bi-lexical projections be evaluated for fairer comparison?

- Question: Please provide error analysis by construction and edge type to show where conversion-based models fail vs. BSP.

- Question: Expand on implicit units (definition, frequency, examples), and discuss UCCA limitations re: scope and quantification and their downstream impact.

- Question: Did you consider strong constituency baselines (e.g., Berkeley parser) or LCFRS-style parsing baselines such as Fernández-González and Martins (2015)?

- Question: Do you plan to replace BSP’s perceptron with neural classifiers (e.g., LSTMs), and what bottlenecks are expected?

- Overall: A timely, useful empirical study likely to stimulate work on broad-coverage semantic parsing, but needs stronger baselines, clearer novelty, richer conversions/metrics, and substantive error analysis; recommend acceptance if addressed.
