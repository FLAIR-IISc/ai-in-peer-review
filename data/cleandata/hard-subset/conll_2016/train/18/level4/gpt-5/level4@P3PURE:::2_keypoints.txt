- Paper presents first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and proposes several methods including a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA graphs.
- Contrasts UCCA with CoNLL semantic dependency graphs (nonterminal nodes) and AMR (claims UCCA is “grounded”/tokens as nodes).
- Developing a UCCA parser is a reasonable and worthwhile goal given available data.
- Introduction/background are biased, treating UCCA as the only important graph-based representation and need reframing.
- Reviewer disputes parts of the three proposed criteria: unclear that nonterminal nodes add unique expressive power versus richer edge labels.
- Questions the claimed advantages of “grounded” representations over AMR and recommends using terms like “anchored” or “lexicalized.”
- Parser design seems reasonable, though the reviewer did not verify all technical details.
- Empirical evaluation is not convincing: MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.
- Notes prior work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could improve baselines and weaken reported BSP advantages.
