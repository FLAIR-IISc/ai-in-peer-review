- Paper presents what authors claim are the first broad-coverage UCCA parsers, including a transition-based system and empirical evaluation.  
- Building a UCCA parser is a reasonable and potentially useful contribution.  
- Introduction reads biased toward UCCA as the only sensible graph-based representation and needs more balanced framing.  
- The three desiderata (nonterminal nodes, discontinuity, groundedness) are unconvincing: nonterminals don’t clearly increase expressivity and discontinuity isn’t a problem for ungrounded formalisms.  
- Benefits of “grounded” representations vs. AMR are not clearly justified; the term “grounded” is overloaded and a different label (e.g., “anchored” or “lexicalized”) is suggested.  
- Parser design seems reasonable, but empirical results are weak: straightforward MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Related work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (e.g., CoNLL 2014/2015, Agić et al. 2015) is overlooked; incorporating those techniques could strengthen baselines and reduce BSP’s apparent advantage.
