- Paper presents the first broad-coverage UCCA parsers, including a transition-based parser, with empirical evaluation.
- Topic is worthwhile and the work is technically competent; transition-based approach is a natural fit for UCCA.
- Positioning overclaims UCCA's superiority; introduction should present UCCA as a complementary semantic formalism, not the only sensible one.
- The three criteria for evaluating semantic formalisms are unconvincing and need clearer justification.
- Role of nonterminal nodes is unclear; authors should provide concrete examples where nonterminals encode information not representable by labeled terminal-only edges.
- Benefits of grounding (token-anchored graphs) versus unanchored formalisms like AMR are not substantiated; grounding must be precisely defined and justified.
- Terminology issue: “grounded” is ambiguous; consider using “anchored” or “lexicalized” and ensure consistent usage.
- Empirical results are not compelling: MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F1s are low.
- Baseline comparisons are incomplete—missing graph-to-tree conversion + edge reconstruction methods (e.g., Agić et al. 2015) which could strengthen baselines.
- Recommend including stronger baselines (tree-approximation + reconstruction, MaltParser with LSTM features) to fairly assess BSP's advantage.
- Methodological clarity lacking: need example-driven explanation of nonterminal creation/use and targeted phenomena (coordination, MWEs, shared arguments).
- Conversion protocol to trees must be specified, including what information is discarded/recovered and how evaluation aligns.
- Require more thorough error analysis for remote edges (which relations fail and why), statistical significance testing, and per-relation breakdowns.
- Suggested experiments: report primary vs. remote metrics, per-category F1, speed/complexity, and include ablation studies of transition system and features.
- Provide qualitative analyses showing where the parser helps or fails (constructions where nonterminals help) to guide future work.
- Overall: promising contribution but currently overreaches; recommend substantial revision to temper claims, clarify motivations, and strengthen evaluation before publication.
