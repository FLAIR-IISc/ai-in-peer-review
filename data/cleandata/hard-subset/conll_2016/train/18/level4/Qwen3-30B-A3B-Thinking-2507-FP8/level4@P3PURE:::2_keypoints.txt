- Presents the first broad-coverage UCCA semantic parsers (including a transition-based parser) and evaluates them.  
- Introduction/background wrongly implies UCCA is the only graph-based semantic formalism worth studying; this claim is misleading and unnecessary.  
- Disagrees with the three proposed criteria for semantic representations; the usefulness of nonterminal nodes is unclear and may be replaceable by richer edge labels.  
- Argues discontinuity concerns don't apply to non-grounded SRs, and the claimed advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR are unconvincing; recommends clearer terminology (e.g., "lexicalized" or "anchored").  
- Calls for substantial revision of the introduction to improve arguments and phrasing.  
- Parser appears technically sound, though the reviewer did not verify implementation details.  
- Evaluation is underwhelming: a simple MaltParser outperforms the proposed BSP on primary edges, and remote-edge F-scores are modest.  
- Notes prior work on tree approximations (CoNLL 2014/2015, Agić et al. 2015) for reconstructing deleted edges; incorporating such methods could boost baselines and reduce the BSP's apparent advantage.
