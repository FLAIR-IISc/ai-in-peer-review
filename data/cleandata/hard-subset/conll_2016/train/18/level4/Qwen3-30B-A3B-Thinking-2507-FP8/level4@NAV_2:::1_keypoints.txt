- Introduces a transition-based parsing approach for UCCA supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.
- Proposes two strategies: conversion-based parsing (map to constituency/dependency) and a direct Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP).
- Contribution: first parser for UCCA that directly supports all three structural properties.
- Contribution: thorough analysis of conversion-based approaches to assess adapting existing parsers.
- Contribution: empirical validation reporting nearly 70% labeled F-score on UCCA corpora.
- Strength: addresses a gap by supporting structures no existing parser handles simultaneously.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation with in-domain and out-of-domain tests plus detailed ablation studies.
- Strength: clearly situates UCCA relative to other semantic frameworks (AMR, SDP).
- Weakness: “first to propose” claim is questionable given prior graph parsing work (Ribeyre; Almeida & Martins, 2014–2015).
- Weakness: lacks comparison to existing graph parsers that could likely be adapted to UCCA.
- Weakness: conversion to dependency trees loses substantial information; more sophisticated conversions (e.g., SemEval SDP) would be preferable.
- Weakness: evaluation omits standard metrics from related semantic parsing tasks, complicating comparisons.
- Question: why use dependency-tree conversion instead of a more information-preserving method?
- Question: how does UCCA compare to MRS and DRT regarding quantifier scope and other semantic phenomena, and what is the parsing complexity impact?
- Question: provide an example of UCCA’s implicit units to clarify their nature and significance.
- Question: why not use the Berkeley parser with rich node labels as a baseline for constituency experiments?
