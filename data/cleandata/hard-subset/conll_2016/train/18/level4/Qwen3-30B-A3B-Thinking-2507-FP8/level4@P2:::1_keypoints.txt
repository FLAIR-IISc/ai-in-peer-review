- Summary: Introduces a transition-based parser for UCCA and two approaches—conversion-based parsing and a direct BSP parser—to handle multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Contribution 1: First systematic exploration of parsing UCCA structures that support all three target structural properties.
- Contribution 2: Shows transition-based methods achieve strong performance (nearly 70% labeled F-score) on UCCA parsing.
- Contribution 3: Proposes a novel BSP parser that directly supports multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Strength 1: Clear, thorough analysis of required structural properties and demonstration that no existing parser handles all three together.
- Strength 2: Comprehensive experimental evaluation comparing conversion-based and direct parsing approaches on UCCA corpora.
- Strength 3: Demonstrates adaptability of transition-based methods to complex semantic structures, advancing semantic parsing research.
- Weakness 1: Novelty claim is overstated; prior graph parsing work (e.g., SemEval 2014–2015) could be adapted and is not acknowledged.
- Weakness 2: Evaluation lacks meaningful comparisons with established graph parsing methods; baselines used are not graph-oriented.
- Weakness 3: Uses custom evaluation metrics instead of standard SemEval metrics, hindering comparability with related work.
- Question 1: How does the parser incorporate UCCA’s semantic label specificity (e.g., Process, State) beyond handling graph structure?
- Question 2: Why were existing graph parsing approaches (e.g., SemEval systems) not compared or adapted for UCCA to strengthen novelty claims?
- Question 3: Please provide an example of implicit units in UCCA graphs and explain their impact on parsing.
- Additional comment 1: Define “grounded semantic” early, as it’s used before being defined.
- Additional comment 2: Conversion to dependency trees appears to lose non-terminal information; a richer conversion (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014) would be preferable.
- Additional comment 3: Consider using the Berkeley parser with rich node labels as a stronger constituency-based baseline.
- Additional comment 4: Better justify why the three identified structural properties are necessary for broad-coverage semantic parsing with more linguistic motivation.
