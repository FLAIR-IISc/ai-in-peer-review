- Introduces a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and outlines three principles for semantic annotation (graph-based, non-terminals, discontinuity).  
- Experiments include phrase-based (Stanford/UParse conversion), dependency conversion, and direct UCCA graph parsing.  
- Claiming to be the "first parser for a semantically-oriented scheme" is problematic given prior graph parsers (e.g., Almeda & Martins 2014; Ribeyre et al.).  
- The parser’s contribution appears to be a scheme-specific feature template rather than novel transition rules or parsing innovations.  
- Key prior graph parsers cited were not used as baselines, undermining empirical comparisons and fairness.  
- Phrase-based experiments lack validation against comparable LCFRS approaches (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins 2015).  
- Evaluation omits standard shared-task metrics (e.g., Semeval SDP), leaving results ungrounded in the semantic parsing literature.  
- No error analysis or benchmarking against semantic parsers for MRS/DRT, so claims that UCCA parsing is "harder" are unsupported.  
- Novelty and goals are unclear: the three principles seem ad hoc and the paper conflates advocating for UCCA, a general semantic parser, and UCCA’s parseability.  
- Baseline and conversion methods are weak: dependency conversion discards semantic information; richer conversions (e.g., Schluter et al.) and stronger baselines (Berkeley Parser with enriched trees) should be used.  
- Technical ambiguities: claims about implicit units need examples/references; omission of quantifier scope modeling vs. MRS/DRT requires discussion of limitations.  
- Terminology issue: "grounded semantic" is used before being defined.  
- Recommendation: major revision—add relevant baselines, adopt shared-task metrics, clarify novelty and aims, and resolve technical/terminological ambiguities.
