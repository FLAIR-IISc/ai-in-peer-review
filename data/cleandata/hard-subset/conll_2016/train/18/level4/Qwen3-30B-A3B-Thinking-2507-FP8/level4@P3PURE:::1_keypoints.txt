- Paper presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and defines three core principles for semantic annotation: graph-based, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous elements.  
- Authors run three experiments: phrase-based parsing via UCCA-to-constituency conversion, dependency parsing via UCCA-to-dependency conversion, and a direct transition-based graph parser (core contribution).  
- Novelty is questionable: the transition system does not specifically address UCCA’s unique semantic features and remote-edge handling could be done with existing transitions/labeling.  
- Important prior work and baselines (post-2014 Semeval systems, Almeida & Martins, Ribeyre et al.; DAGParser, TurboParser) are not compared, undermining empirical claims.  
- Phrase-based baseline (uparse) lacks validation against comparable LCFRS work (Fernández-González & Martins 2015) and is missing an initial reference.  
- Evaluation metrics are not aligned with shared-task/SDP-style benchmarks, preventing meaningful cross-system comparisons.  
- Theoretical framing is unclear: the three principles appear ad hoc and the paper conflates being a UCCA parser, a general semantic parser, and a demonstration of UCCA’s parseability.  
- Terminology issue: "grounded semantic" is undefined before page 2 and needs definition.  
- Missing comparisons: why not evaluate against a constituent parser (e.g., Berkeley Parser) with rich node labels and trace propagation?  
- Conversion issue: the surface dependency conversion used is lossy; a richer conversion (e.g., Schlüter et al., 2014) should be considered.  
- Clarify UCCA’s implicit units (units without textual correspondence) with examples or references.  
- Address UCCA’s lack of explicit quantifier-scope modeling (vs. MRS/DRT) and provide an error analysis to explain why UCCA parsing appears harder than other schemes.  
- Overall: work is timely and worth conference discussion, but missing baselines and inadequate metrics leave the contribution under-justified.
