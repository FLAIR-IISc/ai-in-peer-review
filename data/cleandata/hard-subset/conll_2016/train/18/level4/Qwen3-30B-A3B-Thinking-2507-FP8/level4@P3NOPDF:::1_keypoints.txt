- Novelty claim is problematic: prior work (e.g., Almeda & Martins; Ribeyre et al.) has already extended transition-based graph parsers for non-projective/graph-structured data.  
- Missing comparisons to existing graph-parser baselines (TurboParser, DAGParser), undermining the paper’s claimed novelty and performance assessment.  
- The described feature template is less influential than parser architecture, so omitting architecture-strong baselines is a significant oversight.  
- Phrase-based evaluation omits relevant baselines (e.g., Fernandez-González & Martins 2015) and does not explore the Berkeley Parser.  
- Evaluation metrics are inappropriate: surface dependency conversions discard key semantic structure; should use shared-task metrics (e.g., Semeval SDP) or richer conversions (e.g., Schlüter et al. 2014).  
- Without SOTA baselines and standardized metrics, it’s impossible to gauge the parser’s relative performance or contribution.  
- The three proposed design principles are underdeveloped and not uniquely semantic, weakening the argument for UCCA’s necessity.  
- Contribution is ambiguous: unclear whether the paper targets UCCA parsing, a general semantic parser, or validation of UCCA’s parsability.  
- UCCA’s limitations are not discussed (e.g., lack of quantifier scope modeling), and claims about being “more abstract” or “less specified” need quantification.  
- No error analysis is provided to determine whether parsing difficulty arises from the annotation scheme or the parser.  
- The claim that UCCA contains implicit units has no examples or references and needs elaboration.  
- Minor issues: “grounded semantic” is used before definition, U*parse reference is missing, and some baseline choices are unexplored.  
- Recommendation: reject pending major revisions—add SOTA graph-parser baselines, adopt shared-task metrics and richer conversions, clarify the paper’s contribution, address UCCA’s limitations with examples, and better integrate the design principles.
