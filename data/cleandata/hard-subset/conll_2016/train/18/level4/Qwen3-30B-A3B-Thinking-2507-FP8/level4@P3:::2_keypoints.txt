- Presents the first semantic parser for UCCA, targeting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Contribution: conversion procedures mapping UCCA to dependency and constituency trees to leverage existing parsers.
- Contribution: Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA graphs.
- Strength: First parser for UCCA with open-sourced parsers and converters, filling a gap for a cross-linguistic formalism.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on UCCA corpora, establishing baselines (nearly 70% labeled F-score on primary edges).
- Strength: Clear, detailed algorithms for conversion between UCCA and other formalisms and analysis of trade-offs.
- Weakness: Overstates UCCA’s uniqueness—other formalisms (e.g., SDP) support some of the same structural properties, making the claim inaccurate.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for the necessity of non-terminal nodes; unclear what they add beyond richer edge labels or other features.
- Weakness: BSP underperforms conversion-based LSTM parser (62.4% vs. 73.2% F-score), questioning the benefit of the direct approach.
- Weakness: Claims about UCCA being “grounded” are vague and not shown to offer clear parsing or representation advantages over non-grounded formalisms.
- Question: Clarify how SDP differs from UCCA regarding non-terminal nodes and why SDP can’t be considered to support all three properties.
- Question: Provide a concrete example where non-terminal nodes are necessary for representing coordination and edge-label-only approaches fail.
- Question: Given conversion-based LSTM outperforms BSP, what practical advantages justify developing BSP despite its complexity?
- Question: Define “grounded” as used for UCCA and explain how this definition differs from other uses in semantic parsing literature.
