- Presents first broad-coverage UCCA semantic parsers and several parsing methods (including a transition-based parser) with evaluation.  
- Introduction overstates UCCA's uniqueness and implies it is the only viable graph-based SR formalism, which is unnecessary and misleading.  
- The three proposed SR criteria are questionable: necessity of nonterminal nodes for expressivity is unclear versus richer edge labels.  
- The discussion of discontinuity is misleading because it does not apply to non-grounded SRs.  
- Claimed advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR are unclear; the term "grounded" is inconsistently used and should be replaced with a clearer term (e.g., "lexicalized" or "token-anchored").  
- The introduction requires major revision to avoid overstating UCCA’s uniqueness and to clarify its theoretical positioning.  
- The parser implementation appears adequate (not independently verified).  
- Evaluation is underwhelming: a standard MaltParser baseline outperforms BSP on primary edges, and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Prior work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (CoNLL 2014/2015, Agić et al. 2015) could improve dependency-based baselines.  
- Given existing conversion/reconstruction techniques, the BSP parser’s novelty and contribution are less compelling than claimed.
