- Paper presents the first UCCA parser: a transition-based BSP that supports multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Also proposes a conversion-based approach mapping UCCA to dependency/constituency trees to leverage existing parsers; best system achieves ~70% labeled F1.
- Strength: fills a gap by providing the first dedicated parser for UCCA.
- Strength: conversion approach shows existing parsing technology can be adapted to UCCA with practical utility.
- Strength: provides an evaluation framework and promises to release parsers/converters to facilitate research.
- Weakness: overstates UCCA as the only graph-based semantic representation worth studying.
- Weakness: weak justification for the three structural properties, especially the necessity of non-terminal nodes versus richer edge labels.
- Weakness: "grounded" terminology is unclear and not well distinguished from other semantic formalisms.
- Weakness: BSP results are unimpressive—worse than standard dependency parsers on primary edges (62.4% vs. 63.9–73.2%) and very low remote edge scores (15.3%).
- Weakness: conversion-to-dependency approach overlaps with prior CoNLL semantic dependency work and lacks clear comparison or claimed improvements.
- Question: why are non-terminal nodes necessary for coordinated structures instead of using more informative edge labels?
- Question: what concrete advantages does the "grounded" representation provide over AMR-style representations?
