- Paper presents the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, a graph-based formalism with nonterminal nodes and grounded token nodes.  
- Authors propose multiple parsing methods, including a transition-based parser, and provide evaluations.  
- Introduction overstates UCCA’s uniqueness, claiming it is the only graph-based SR worth studying, which is unnecessary and undermines the paper.  
- The three proposed SR criteria are questionable: the claimed utility of nonterminal nodes is unclear and may not add expressivity.  
- The discontinuity criterion is irrelevant for non-grounded SRs and is presented without sufficient justification.  
- The claimed advantages of “grounded” representations over AMR are unconvincing and the term “grounded” is overloaded.  
- Recommend using clearer terms like “anchored” or “lexicalized” instead of “grounded” to avoid confusion.  
- The introductory argument requires substantial revision for precision, balance, and literature awareness.  
- Parser methodology seems sound in principle, though implementation details were not checked.  
- Evaluation results are underwhelming: a standard MaltParser outperforms the BSP on primary edges and remote edge F-scores are modest.  
- Established techniques (tree approximations and edge-reconstruction methods like Agic et al., 2015) could improve dependency-based baselines, reducing BSP’s perceived novelty.
