- Paper introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities, plus a conversion-based approach and systematic evaluation.  
- Strength: Novel parser architecture—BSP extends transition systems (LEFT/RIGHT-REMOTEX, SWAP) to handle remote edges and discontinuities.  
- Strength: Rigorous evaluation—defines DAG evaluation via yield sets and reports in-domain/out-of-domain results (BSP ~69.2% primary-edge F1).  
- Strength: Practical utility—LSTM classifier outperforms traditional parsers by 9.1% F1, showing transition-based methods are viable for UCCA.  
- Weakness: Lack of comparative baselines—no empirical comparison to existing graph/DAG parsers (e.g., TurboParser, DAGParser, Sagae & Tsujii 2008).  
- Weakness: Questionable conversion strategy—dependency conversion discards non-terminals and yields zero remote-edge F1; richer conversions were not explored.  
- Weakness: Underdeveloped motivation—BSS properties overlap with SDP/AMR literature and paper does not clearly distinguish or justify UCCA's unique advantages.  
- Q: Why choose a dependency-tree conversion that discards non-terminals over richer SemEval SDP–style conversions?  
- Q: How does UCCA’s “grounded” representation concretely address AMR limitations in applications like MT or summarization?  
- Q: Please provide an example/reference of UCCA “implicit units with no text correspondent” and explain how they affect parsing.  
- Q: How is BSP the first parser supporting all three BSS properties given prior dependency DAG parsers that handle multiple parents?  
- Comment: Define “grounded semantic” early (page 2) for clarity.  
- Comment: Include an error analysis to explain why remote edges remain challenging (13.3% F1).  
- Comment: Soften/precise the claim that “no existing parser supports all three properties” and contrast explicitly with SDP parsers.  
- Overall: Meaningful and technically sound contribution to UCCA parsing, but requires stronger baselines, improved conversion strategies, and clearer motivation before publication.
