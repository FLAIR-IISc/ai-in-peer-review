- Paper introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and a transition-based parser, evaluating multiple approaches.  
- Introduction makes an inaccurate and unnecessary claim that UCCA is the *only* graph-based SR worth studying.  
- Framing distracts from a valuable contribution; the paper should focus on UCCA’s merits without dismissing alternatives.  
- The three proposed criteria for SR formalisms lack convincing evidence and justification.  
- Claim that nonterminal nodes are essential is unsupported; enriched edge labels might suffice and no evidence is given that nonterminals are required.  
- The term and concept of “grounded” are problematic, ambiguously defined, and advantages over AMR are not compellingly argued.  
- “Grounded” should be replaced with clearer terms like “lexicalized” or “anchored.”  
- Parser appears technically sound, but implementation details are not discussed in depth.  
- Evaluation is weak: BSP is outperformed by a MaltParser baseline on primary edges.  
- Performance on remote edges is modest and not clearly superior.  
- Evaluation overlooks prior graph-to-tree conversion work (e.g., CoNLL 2014/2015, Agić et al. 2015) that could improve baselines.  
- Authors should compare against MaltParser enhanced with graph-to-tree reconstruction methods to fairly assess BSP.  
- Required revisions: remove the unsubstantiated exclusivity claim, rewrite the introduction to justify UCCA’s design choices, use clearer terminology, and strengthen the evaluation.  
- With those revisions the paper could be a meaningful contribution; as written the introduction’s overreach and weak evaluation weaken its impact.
