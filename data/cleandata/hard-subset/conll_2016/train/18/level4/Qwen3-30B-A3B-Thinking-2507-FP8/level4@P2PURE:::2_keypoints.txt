- Paper presents the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, a grounded graph SR with nonterminal nodes, and proposes several parsing methods including a transition-based parser.
- Developing a UCCA parser is reasonable and valuable given existing annotated data.
- Introduction/background incorrectly implies UCCA is the only graph-based SR worth studying, which is unnecessary and distracting.
- The three proposed criteria for SR formalisms are questionable; the necessity of nonterminal nodes is not convincingly demonstrated and might be replaceable by richer edge labels.
- Discontinuity concerns are irrelevant to non-grounded SRs, and claimed advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR-style formalisms are not convincingly established.
- The term "grounded" is ambiguous in prior work and should be replaced with a clearer term (e.g., "anchored" or "lexicalized").
- The entire introductory section requires substantial revision for greater precision and rigor.
- The parser itself appears sound, though technical details were not fully evaluated in the review.
- Evaluation results are unimpressive: a basic MaltParser outperforms the BSP parser on primary edges, and remote-edge F-scores are modest.
- Existing work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (CoNLL 2014/2015, Agic et al. 2015) could be applied to strengthen baselines and reduce the BSP parser’s claimed novelty.
