- Paper introduces first comprehensive semantic parsers for UCCA and evaluates several methods including a transition-based parser.  
- Developing a UCCA parser is justified given existing UCCA resources, but the paper over-advocates UCCA as the only graph formalism worth studying.  
- The three motivating criteria for semantic formalisms are not fully convincing and need stronger justification.  
- Claimed benefits of nonterminal nodes are unclear; it's uncertain what they express that richer edge labels could not.  
- The discussion of discontinuity is irrelevant for non-"grounded" representations and the claimed advantages of "grounded" over AMR-style formalisms are not demonstrated.  
- The term "grounded" is overloaded; authors should consider clearer alternatives (e.g., "anchored" or "lexicalized").  
- The parser implementation seems reasonable, but the paper lacks detailed examination of its mechanics.  
- Empirical results are unimpressive: a basic MaltParser outperforms the proposed BSP on primary edges.  
- F-scores on remote edges are low and not especially strong compared to what might be achievable.  
- Prior work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could likely boost MaltParser results, weakening the claimed advantage of BSP; these methods should be considered and cited.
