- Proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and presents three experiment sets (phrase-based, dependency, direct UCCA parsing).  
- Reviewer finds the methodology and empirical justification insufficiently argued.  
- Claim of being the first parser for such semantic schemes is questionable; existing graph parsers could be adapted.  
- Transition set does not appear to specifically address unique characteristics of UCCA graphs.  
- Lack of comparisons/baselines with existing graph parsers (e.g., Almeda & Martins, Ribeyre et al.) is a major limitation.  
- Phrase-based experiments using uparse should be validated with another parser (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzales & Martins).  
- Evaluation should include metrics from related shared tasks for more comprehensive comparison.  
- “Grounded semantic” concept should be introduced earlier in the paper.  
- Suggest using constituent trees with rich node labels and propagator traces (Berkeley parser) as an additional baseline.  
- Conversion to surface dependency trees may be unnecessary; recommend richer conversions (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014).  
- Authors should clarify and provide examples for “implicit units that have no correspondent in the text.”  
- Unclear whether the scheme captures quantifier scope; comparability to syntax-oriented schemes is limited.  
- The three principles presented seem ad-hoc and need stronger motivation in the introduction.  
- An error analysis is missing and would help understand parsing challenges and results.  
- Reviewer still finds the work interesting and suitable for discussion at a conference like CoNLL despite concerns.  
- Provides references to existing tools/parsers (DAGParser, TurboParser) that could be used for comparison.
