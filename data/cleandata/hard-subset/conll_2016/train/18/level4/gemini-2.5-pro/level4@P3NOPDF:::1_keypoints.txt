- Paper introduces a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and reports three experiments: phrase-based (via constituency conversion), dependency-based (via dependency conversion), and a direct UCCA parser.

- Reviewer finds the problem important but has major concerns about methodology, evaluation, and framing that preclude acceptance.

- Major omission of comparisons to existing graph parsers (e.g., SemEval 2014/2015 systems), leaving claims of novelty and superiority unsubstantiated.

- The transition set appears to address structural, not uniquely semantic, properties of UCCA, so novelty vs. general DAG parsing is unclear.

- Missing strong phrase-based baselines (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzales & Martins) that can produce LCFRS-style analyses.

- Converting UCCA to dependency trees is lossy and discards key structural information, making that baseline weak and uninformative.

- A constituency-based baseline (convert UCCA to enriched constituent trees and use a state-of-the-art parser like Berkeley) is missing.

- Evaluation metrics are not standardized; adopting SemEval SDP metrics would enable clearer comparison with other semantic graphbanks.

- The three “guiding principles” for semantic schemes are overly general and need better motivation and grounding.

- The paper’s central contribution is unclear (parser itself, a general semantic model, or a proof-of-concept), producing confusing framing.

- No detailed error analysis is provided; the paper should identify common error types and failures on UCCA phenomena (remote edges, implicit units, specific categories).

- Several terms and claims are undefined or under-explained (e.g., “grounded semantics,” examples/references for implicit units).

- UCCA’s semantic depth is unclear (e.g., lack of quantifier scope modeling); paper should position UCCA on the syntax–semantics spectrum.

- Conclusion: promising first step but requires substantial revision — add relevant baselines, stronger/meaning-preserving conversions, standardized metrics, clearer framing, and error analysis.
