- Introduces a transition-based parser for UCCA and compares conversion-based vs. direct (BSP) parsing strategies.  
- Main contributions: first UCCA-specific parser and a systematic comparison of direct vs. conversion-based approaches.  
- Strength: addresses parsing of a new broad-coverage semantic formalism combining graphs, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Strength: provides a systematic empirical comparison between a novel direct parser and conversion-based pipelines.  
- Strength: UCCA poses central challenges for current parsing research and the paper offers a useful baseline for the community.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—transition system mainly synthesizes existing techniques and lacks baselines against SemEval-style transition-based graph parsers.  
- Weakness: Conversion baselines are sub-optimal and highly lossy; richer SDP-style or constituency conversions and parsers were not tried.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses a custom metric without mapping results to established metrics from related tasks, limiting comparability.  
- Weakness: Framing is unclear—paper simultaneously argues for a new parser, a new scheme, and demonstrability, weakening focus.  
- Question: Provide an example of an "implicit unit" in UCCA and explain why parsing them is left for future work.  
- Question: How does UCCA handle phenomena like quantifier scope compared to MRS/DRT, and is poor performance due to structural or semantic difficulty (error analysis requested)?  
- Question: Did you consider converting UCCA to rich constituency trees (with labels/traces) and using strong constituency parsers (e.g., Berkeley) as a baseline?  
- Question: Why choose a lossy CoNLL-style dependency conversion (88.4% upper bound) instead of more structure-preserving SDP conversion methods?
