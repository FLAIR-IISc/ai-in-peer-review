1. Introduces a transition-based parser for Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS), applied to UCCA.  
2. Evaluates two approaches: converting UCCA to dependency/constituency formats and a direct transition-based BSP parser.  
3. Establishes first benchmarks for UCCA parsing, best conversion-based model achieves 69.2% F-score.  
4. Contribution: first empirical investigation and benchmark for UCCA parsing, creating a foundation for future work.  
5. Contribution: comparative analysis of conversion-based methods versus a direct parser, highlighting trade-offs.  
6. Strength: tackles a novel, relevant problem involving multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
7. Strength: comprehensive experiments including conversion and direct parsing, plus in- and out-of-domain evaluation.  
8. Strength: commits to publicly releasing converters and parsers for reproducibility.  
9. Weakness: missing comparisons to state-of-the-art transition-based graph parsers (e.g., SemEval SDP systems), weakening claims of novelty/effectiveness.  
10. Weakness: conversion baselines are methodologically weak and discard structure; new evaluation metric prevents easy comparison to existing work.  
11. Weakness: unclear framing and motivation—principles presented as novel are known challenges, and main contribution is ambiguous.  
12. Question: why were existing SemEval SDP transition-based graph parsers not used as baselines?  
13. Question: please provide an example or reference for "implicit units" with no surface correspondent.  
14. Question: clarify UCCA's limitations (e.g., lack of quantifier scope) and how it compares to deeper formalisms like MRS/DRT or Prague tectogrammatical layer.
