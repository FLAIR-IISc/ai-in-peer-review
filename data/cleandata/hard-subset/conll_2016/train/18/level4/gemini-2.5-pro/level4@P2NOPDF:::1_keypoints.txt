- Introduces a transition-based parser for UCCA but the three proposed semantic properties are general and not unique to UCCA.  
- Novelty is limited: transition system lacks mechanisms specifically for semantic labels (e.g., Process/State); main novelty appears in feature templates.  
- Remote-edge handling could be implemented via label modifications rather than new transition primitives.  
- Major omission: no comparison with state-of-the-art graph parsers from SemEval SDP (e.g., Ribeyre et al., Martins et al.), which are available and applicable to UCCA.  
- Phrase-based baselines are weak: misses comparison to LCFRS-style parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzales & Martins 2015).  
- Paper’s focus is diffuse—unclear if contribution is a UCCA-specific parser, a general semantic parsing model, or evidence that UCCA is parsable.  
- Dependency conversion baseline is overly simplistic and causes significant information loss; should use more sophisticated conversions (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014).  
- Evaluation metrics should align with SemEval SDP metrics to allow direct comparison and better contextualization.  
- Lacks error analysis: need to identify whether errors concentrate on re-entrancy, discontinuity, non-terminals, etc.  
- Constituency baseline could be strengthened by converting UCCA to rich constituent trees and using strong parsers (e.g., Berkeley parser).  
- Terminology issue: "grounded semantics" is used before being defined and should be clarified on first use.  
- Ambiguity about "implicit units" in UCCA—provide an example or reference.  
- Need clearer characterization of UCCA’s expressive power (e.g., quantifier scope) relative to MRS, DRT, DM, and PCEDT; UCCA may resemble abstract syntactic schemes more than logical-form representations.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and relevant work but undermined by lack of relevant baselines, unclear framing, and insufficient analysis; these must be addressed before publication.
