- Introduces the first parsing systems for UCCA and defines Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Explores two parsing strategies: lossy conversion to constituency/dependency with existing parsers, and a direct transition-based Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP).  
- Empirical results on English UCCA corpora show feasibility, with the best conversion-based LSTM achieving ~70% labeled F-score on primary edges.

- Contribution: first empirical study and benchmarks for parsing the UCCA semantic framework.  
- Contribution: comparative analysis of conversion-based versus direct parsing approaches, highlighting trade-offs and conversion upper bounds.  
- Contribution: development of BSP, a transition-based parser combining techniques for graphs and discontinuities.

- Strength: tackles an important, timely problem in broad-coverage semantic parsing using a cognitively grounded, cross-linguistic scheme.  
- Strength: provides a thorough methodological comparison between adapting mature parsers and building a direct parser, including upper-bound analyses.  
- Strength: establishes strong baselines (≈70% F-score) and shows BSP’s robustness in out-of-domain settings.

- Weakness: BSP’s novelty is limited—primarily an integration of existing transition-based techniques—and the paper omits comparison to relevant SDP parsers from SemEval 2014/2015.  
- Weakness: conversion-based experiments use lossy dependency conversions and omit stronger, structure-preserving conversion methods and discontinuous-capable constituency parsers.  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative error analysis and relies on a non-standard evaluation metric, impeding interpretation and comparison with related work.

- Question: What specific algorithmic innovations in BSP distinguish it from prior transition-based DAG parsers (e.g., those for SDP), and why were those systems not used as baselines?  
- Question: Have you considered more structure-preserving dependency conversions or stronger constituency parsers (e.g., Berkeley-style with traces) as baselines?  
- Question: Can you provide an example of UCCA implicit units that have no textual correspondent and explain the parsing challenges they create?  
- Question: How does UCCA handle phenomena like quantifier scope compared to formalisms such as MRS and DRT?  
- Question: Please provide a brief error analysis for BSP identifying the most common error types to guide future work.
