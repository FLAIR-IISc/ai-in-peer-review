- Presents the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and situates UCCA relative to other formalisms (nonterminal nodes vs CoNLL, grounded vs AMR).  
- Strong parsers for UCCA would be valuable without needing to claim superiority over other graph-based representations.  
- The three criteria offered to justify UCCA over alternatives are unconvincing and need stronger argumentation.  
- Unclear whether nonterminal nodes provide expressiveness that edge-labeling or other mechanisms couldn't achieve.  
- Framing discontinuity as a core motivator is weak because it mainly affects grounded/anchored representations.  
- The term "grounded" is overloaded; a more precise term like "anchored" or "lexicalized" is preferable.  
- The introduction needs more careful, nuanced positioning of UCCA’s role and contributions.  
- The proposed transition-based parser is conceptually sound but the empirical evaluation is not compelling.  
- A standard MaltParser outperforms the BSP parser on primary edges, while BSP’s remote-edge F-scores are modest.  
- Comparison to MaltParser is incomplete: prior work on tree-approximation and edge reconstruction (CoNLL 2014/2015, Agic et al. 2015) was not applied and could boost baseline graph scores.  
- Omitting reconstruction-enhanced baselines weakens the evidence that the proposed parser substantially outperforms strong, adapted baselines.
