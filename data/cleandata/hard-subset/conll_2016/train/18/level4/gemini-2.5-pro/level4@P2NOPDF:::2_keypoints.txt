- Paper presents first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, contrasts UCCA with dependency graphs and AMR, and proposes a novel transition-based parser.
- Framing is overly strong/biased toward UCCA, arguing implicitly it is the best representation; needs more balanced positioning.
- Introductory arguments and three criteria for representation design are not convincing and require more nuance.
- Unclear added expressive power of nonterminal (non-lexical) nodes — similar phenomena might be handled with richer edge labels on lexicalized graphs.
- Emphasis on handling discontinuity is mainly a problem for grounded/lexicalized formalisms and less relevant for unanchored representations like AMR.
- Benefits of being “grounded” versus AMR are not clearly articulated; term “grounded” is ambiguous — suggest using “anchored” or “lexicalized.”
- Technical parser design is sound, but empirical results are not compelling: the BSP parser loses to a MaltParser baseline on primary edges.
- Remote-edge (non-tree) parsing performance is weak (low F-scores), undermining the motivation for a graph-based parser.
- Baseline comparison is incomplete: paper ignores tree-approximation/post-processing literature (e.g., CoNLL 2014/2015, Agic et al. 2015) that can reconstruct non-tree edges and likely improve baselines.
- If stronger tree-approximation baselines were used, the relative advantage of the proposed BSP parser would likely diminish.
