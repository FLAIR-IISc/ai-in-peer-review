- Presents first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, including a transition-based parser that directly builds UCCA graphs.  
- Introduction/framing is problematic: authors imply UCCA is the only graph-based SR worth studying, which is unnecessary and unconvincing.  
- The three criteria proposed for evaluating semantic formalisms are unconvincing and inadequately justified.  
- The paper does not demonstrate that nonterminal nodes add expressive power beyond what richer edge labels could represent.  
- The discontinuity argument is irrelevant for non-grounded SRs, and advantages of UCCA’s “grounded” approach over AMR are not clearly articulated.  
- The term “grounded” is overloaded in the literature; a clearer term like “anchored” or “lexicalized” should be used.  
- Introductory sections need a more careful, nuanced motivation and presentation.  
- The parser seems technically sound but evaluation is weak: a simple MaltParser baseline outperforms BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Comparison to MaltParser is incomplete: tree-approximation and reconstruction methods (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could improve baselines, so BSP’s advantage on remote edges may be overstated.
