- Paper presents the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, a graph formalism with nonterminal nodes, multiple parents, and discontinuities.  
- Highlights differences: UCCA nonterminals vs CoNLL dependency graphs; UCCA is "grounded" (tokens appear as nodes) unlike AMR.  
- Proposes two parsing strategies: a conversion-based approach leveraging existing parsers and a novel transition-based parser (BSP) that directly builds full UCCA graphs.  
- BSP is designed to handle UCCA’s unique properties (nonterminals, discontinuous units, remote edges).  
- Evaluation is performed on UCCA-annotated corpora, comparing proposed parsers to existing systems.  
- Strength: novel, challenging approach to parsing a complex semantic formalism.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation including comparisons with prior parsers.  
- Strength: contributes to semantic parsing methods with practical NLP applications.  
- Weakness: introduction/background lacks a balanced overview of other semantic representation formalisms.  
- Weakness: evaluation results are not particularly strong, especially relative to baseline parsers like MaltParser.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of UCCA’s advantages and drawbacks, and of what “grounded” representation buys you.  
- Question: authors should clarify how UCCA’s nonterminal nodes increase expressive capacity and their practical benefits.  
- Question: need plans to improve BSP’s performance on remote edges and known limitations.  
- Question: authors should discuss real-world NLP applications where the proposed parsers would be beneficial.
