- Presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA, motivated by three principles: graph-based, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuity.  
- Notes those three principles are not specific to semantics and could apply to syntax or other layers.  
- Experiments: constituency conversion + Stanford parser, dependency conversion + dependency parser, and direct transition-based graph parsing (core proposal).  
- Claims of being the first semantic-scheme parser are overstated — existing graph-parsing work could be applied to UCCA.  
- The transition set does not appear to treat UCCA-specific structural/semantic peculiarities; remote edges could be handled via labels.  
- Lacks comparison/baselines with prior graph parsers (e.g., Ribeyre et al., TurboParser/DAGParser).  
- The model is mainly anchored by a feature template rather than by novel transition operations.  
- Phrase-based experiments should include other strong parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins 2015); uparse was not properly referenced.  
- Converting UCCA to surface dependency trees loses information; a richer SDP-style conversion (Schluter et al., SemEval) should be used.  
- Should evaluate with shared-task metrics used in related SDP work for comparability.  
- Overall lack of comparison points with other models/datasets weakens the empirical justification.  
- Request clarification/examples/references for “UCCA graphs may contain implicit units that have no correspondent in the text.”  
- UCCA’s inability to represent quantifier scope (vs. MRS/DRT) raises questions about its semantic expressiveness and how “parsable” it really is; an error analysis is needed.  
- The three principles seem ad-hoc and the paper’s main claim is unclear: a UCCA parser, a semantic parsing model, or an argument about scheme parsability.  
- Minor suggestions: introduce “grounded semantic” earlier, consider a Berkeley-parser constituent baseline with rich node labels and traces.  
- Provides relevant prior-tool references: DAGParser and TurboParser (links).
