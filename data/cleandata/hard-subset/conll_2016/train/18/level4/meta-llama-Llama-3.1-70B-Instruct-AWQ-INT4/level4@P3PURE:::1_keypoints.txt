- Paper presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and outlines desired properties for semantic annotation schemes (graph-based, non-terminals, discontinuities).  
- Reviewer questions novelty: authors’ claim of first parser for such schemes is not justified given prior graph-parsing work.  
- Transition set does not specifically address UCCA’s semantic peculiarities; remote edges could be handled differently via label changes.  
- Prior graph-parsing methods (SemEval 2014-2015; Almeida & Martins; Ribeyre et al.) should have been used as baselines or comparisons.  
- Phrase-based experiments using uparse lack validation against alternative parsers (e.g., Fernández-González & Martins 2015).  
- Experiments should use SDP shared-task metrics and include more comparison points given competing systems/datasets.  
- Converting UCCA to surface dependency trees is unnecessary and lossy; richer conversions (e.g., Schlüter et al. 2014) would be preferable.  
- Suggestion to try constituent-tree approach with rich node labels (Berkeley parser) as a baseline.  
- “Grounded semantics” is used before being defined; introduce the concept earlier.  
- Request expansion or reference for the claim that UCCA contains implicit units not corresponding to text.  
- UCCA appears not to model quantifier scope, making comparison to MRS/DRT and other syntax-oriented schemes unclear.  
- An error analysis is needed to clarify how UCCA’s underspecification compares to DM/PCEDT semantic levels.  
- The three proposed principles seem ad-hoc and need clearer motivation and presentation.  
- Unclear overall goal: whether the paper aims to present a UCCA parser, a semantic analysis model, or a general semantic-oriented scheme; presentation is dense and confusing.  
- Despite criticisms, reviewer finds the work interesting and worth discussion at ConLL.
