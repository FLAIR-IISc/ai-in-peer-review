- Paper introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, including a transition-based parser, and provides evaluation.  
- Introduction/background overstates UCCA as the sole graph-based formalism worth studying and should be rephrased; a good UCCA parser is valuable on its own.  
- Disagreement with the three criteria for semantic formalisms: the benefit of nonterminal nodes is unclear and may be replaceable by richer edge labels.  
- Discontinuity concerns don’t apply to non-"grounded" semantics, and claimed advantages of "grounded" over AMR are not well justified.  
- The term "grounded" is ambiguous; consider using "anchored" or "lexicalized" and argue terminology more carefully.  
- The parser implementation appears sound, though the reviewer did not verify all details.  
- Evaluation is underwhelming: a simple MaltParser outperforms the BSP on primary edges, and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Graph-to-tree conversion and reconstruction methods (e.g., tree approximations, Agic et al. 2015) are well-studied and could improve dependency-baseline scores, weakening the BSP’s apparent advantage.
