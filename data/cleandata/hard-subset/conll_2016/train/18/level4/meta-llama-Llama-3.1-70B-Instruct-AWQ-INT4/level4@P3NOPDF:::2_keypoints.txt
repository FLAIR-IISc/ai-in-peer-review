- Presents the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and evaluates several methods including a transition-based parser that directly builds UCCA graphs.
- Developing a UCCA parser is a reasonable and valuable contribution given existing UCCA data.
- The introduction/background overstates UCCA's uniqueness and should avoid implying it's the only graph-based formalism worth studying.
- The three criteria for semantic formalisms are unconvincing: the benefit of nonterminal nodes is unclear and may be replicated with richer edge labels.
- The claimed advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR are not clearly demonstrated; consider alternative terms like "anchored" or "lexicalized."
- The parser design seems sound, but the review did not deeply examine implementation details.
- Evaluation results are underwhelming: a simple MaltParser outperforms the proposed BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.
- Prior work on converting graphs to trees and reconstructing deleted edges (e.g., CoNLL 2014/2015, Agic et al. 2015) could improve baselines and weaken the BSP's apparent advantage.
- Overall, the paper contributes to UCCA parsing but needs clearer argumentation and stronger evaluation.
