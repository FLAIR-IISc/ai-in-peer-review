- Paper introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and highlights UCCA’s distinctive features (nonterminal nodes, grounded/anchored tokens).  
- Authors propose several parsing methods, including a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA parses, and evaluate them.  
- Given existing UCCA resources, building a UCCA parser is reasonable and a worthwhile contribution.  
- Introduction/background is biased toward UCCA as the only worthwhile graph-based formalism and needs tempering.  
- The three proposed criteria for evaluating semantic formalisms are unconvincing and require better justification.  
- It is unclear that nonterminal nodes add expressive power versus more informative edge labels.  
- The discussion of discontinuity is misplaced for non-grounded representations and the advantages of “grounded” over AMR are not clearly explained.  
- The term “grounded” is ambiguous; alternatives like “anchored” or “lexicalized” should be considered.  
- Parser design appears sound (not fully verified by reviewer), but evaluation results are underwhelming.  
- A basic MaltParser outperforms the BSP parser on primary edges, and remote-edge F-scores are unimpressive.  
- Prior work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (CoNLL 2014/2015, Agic et al. 2015) could improve dependency-tree baselines and weaken BSP’s advantage.
