- Introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parser for UCCA and evaluates it on UCCA-annotated data.  
- Notes key structural differences: UCCA allows nonterminal nodes (unlike CoNLL) and is grounded with tokens as nodes (unlike AMR).  
- Presents multiple parsing methods, including a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA graphs.  
- Main contributions: development of a broad-coverage UCCA parser, introduction of a transition-based construction method, and empirical evaluation.  
- Strength: novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing for a less-studied formalism.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation with comparisons to other parsing methods.  
- Strength: well-organized and clearly written presentation.  
- Weakness: introduction/background lacks a nuanced discussion of UCCA’s advantages and limitations.  
- Weakness: evaluation performance is unimpressive on "remote" edges.  
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed comparison with other parsing methods, including tree-approximation approaches.  
- Question: please clarify UCCA’s advantages and limitations compared to CoNLL semantic dependencies and AMR.  
- Question: how will you improve parser performance on remote edges and what challenges do you foresee?  
- Question: provide more details on comparisons with other parsers, especially tree-approximation baselines and relative performance.
