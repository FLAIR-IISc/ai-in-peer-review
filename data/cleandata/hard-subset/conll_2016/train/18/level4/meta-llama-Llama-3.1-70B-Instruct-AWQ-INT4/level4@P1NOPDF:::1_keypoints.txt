- Presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and outlines key semantic-scheme features: graph-based, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous elements.  
- Runs three experiment sets: phrase-based via Stanford/uparse, dependency via UCCA-to-dependency conversion, and direct transition-based graph parsing.  
- Reviewer finds the work interesting but raises methodological and empirical concerns that need addressing before publication.  
- Claims of novelty are questionable because the transition set does not specifically target UCCA’s semantically-oriented labels or underspecified arguments.  
- Remote-edge transitions seem redundant—could be represented via other transitions or label changes.  
- Prior post-2014 graph-parsing work (e.g., Almeida & Martins; Ribeyre et al.) should be used as baselines or comparisons.  
- The model is tied to UCCA mainly via a feature template, but count and unigram features dominate performance.  
- Phrase-based experiments should be validated with alternative parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins) for robustness.  
- Metrics should match related shared tasks (SDP) because UCCA abstracts differently from typical SDP treebanks.  
- Lack of comparisons to other systems, models, and datasets weakens the empirical contribution.  
- Paper is worth conference discussion (ConLL) as it bridges areas in the field.  
- Introduce the term “grounded semantic” before page 2 since it’s used without definition.  
- Suggest using rich-labeled constituent trees (with traces) and the Berkeley parser as an additional baseline.  
- Converting UCCA to surface dependency trees loses information; prefer richer conversions like Schluter et al. (2014).  
- Expand on or give examples/references for “implicit units” in UCCA that have no text correspondent.  
- Question whether UCCA models quantifier scope and how it compares to MRS/DRT; an error analysis would help clarify abstractness and underspecification.  
- The three guiding principles feel ad-hoc and the paper’s core argument (parser for UCCA vs. semantic model vs. semantic-oriented scheme) is unclear and needs sharpening.  
- Reviewer provides links to relevant tools/resources (DAGParser, TurboParser) for comparison.
