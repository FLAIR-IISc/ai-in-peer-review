- Presents the first broad-coverage UCCA semantic parsers, including a transition-based parser, and evaluates their performance.
- Building a UCCA parser is a reasonable contribution given available data, but the introduction needlessly portrays UCCA as uniquely superior among graph-based formalisms.
- Disagrees with the paper's three criteria: the necessity of nonterminal nodes is unclear, discontinuity concerns don't apply to non-grounded formalisms, and "grounded" is an ambiguous term—suggest "anchored" or "lexicalized."
- The introductory framing and arguments should be rephrased and made more careful.
- The proposed parser appears sound (not exhaustively checked by reviewer).
- Evaluation is underwhelming: a simple MaltParser beats the proposed parser on primary edges, and remote-edge F1 scores are low.
- Prior work on graph-to-tree conversion and reconstruction (CoNLL 2014/2015 and Agic et al. 2015) could improve baselines, further weakening the claimed advantage of the proposed parser.
