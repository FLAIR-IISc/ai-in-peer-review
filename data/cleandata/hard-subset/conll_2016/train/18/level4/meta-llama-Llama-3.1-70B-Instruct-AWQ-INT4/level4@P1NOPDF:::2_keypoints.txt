- Paper presents first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, highlighting nonterminal nodes and that UCCA is "grounded" (tokens as nodes) unlike AMR.
- Authors propose several parsing methods, including a transition-based parser, and report evaluations.
- Developing a UCCA parser is a reasonable contribution, but the intro overclaims UCCA’s uniqueness among semantic representation formalisms.
- The three criteria for SR formalisms in the introduction are unconvincing; the utility of nonterminal nodes for expressive capacity is unclear.
- The paper’s discussion of discontinuity and the claimed advantages of “grounded” representations over AMR-style ones is not convincingly argued.
- Recommend reconsidering the term “grounded” (suggest “anchored” or “lexicalized”) and rephrasing the introduction and arguments.
- The parser implementation seems sound, though the reviewer did not scrutinize all technical details.
- Evaluation results are underwhelming: BSP’s primary-edge performance is outperformed by a simple MaltParser.
- BSP’s F-scores on remote edges are low and not particularly impressive.
- Existing work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could likely boost Malt/LSTM-Malt baselines, weakening the claimed advantage of BSP.
