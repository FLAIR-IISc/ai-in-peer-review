- Paper presents the first comprehensive semantic parsers for UCCA, including a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA parses.  
- Developing a UCCA parser is justified given existing UCCA resources, but the paper overstates UCCA as the only viable graph-based formalism.  
- Intro/background make unsubstantiated claims about UCCA’s uniqueness; phrasing and argumentation need to be more careful.  
- The authors’ evaluation criteria for representation formalisms are questionable: nonterminal nodes do not necessarily increase expressivity and discontinuity is irrelevant for non-grounded SRs.  
- Advantages claimed for “grounded” representations over AMR-style ones are not clearly articulated; suggest alternative terms like “anchored” or “lexicalized.”  
- The parser design seems reasonable, though the reviewer did not verify implementation details.  
- Empirical results are underwhelming: the BSP parser is outperformed by a basic MaltParser on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Prior work on tree approximations (CoNLL 2014/2015) and edge-reconstruction methods (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could improve baseline performance and weaken the claimed gains of BSP.
