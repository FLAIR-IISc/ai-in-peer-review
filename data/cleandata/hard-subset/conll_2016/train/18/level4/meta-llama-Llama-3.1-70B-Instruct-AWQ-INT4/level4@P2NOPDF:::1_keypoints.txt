- Presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and outlines general principles for semantic annotation (multi-governors, non-terminals, discontinuities).  
- Positions work within broad-coverage semantic parsing and introduces the UCCA scheme.  
- Runs three experiment types: phrase-based (Stanford + UCCA→constituency), dependency via UCCA→dependency conversion, and direct transition-based UCCA parsing.  
- Reviewer finds the work interesting but raises methodological and empirical concerns needing resolution before publication.  
- Argues the proposed transition set does not specifically address UCCA’s underspecified structural arguments or semantically-oriented labels.  
- Claims existing transition-based graph parsers (e.g., Almeida & Martins; Ribeyre et al.) could be adapted and should have been used as baselines.  
- Criticizes omission of comparisons to other parsers/baselines (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins for LCFRS-like parsing).  
- Recommends using shared-task metrics for SDP-style evaluation due to UCCA’s abstract syntaxico-semantic nature.  
- Notes terminology issues (e.g., “grounded semantic” introduced too late) and requests clearer definitions earlier.  
- Suggests alternative baselines (Berkeley parser with rich node labels and propagated traces) and richer conversion methods (e.g., Schluter et al.) instead of surface dependency conversion.  
- Requests reference/example for UCCA implicit units and deeper analysis of comparators (MRS, DRT) regarding quantifier scope and informativeness.  
- Finds the three stated principles ad-hoc and urges clearer framing: is the contribution a UCCA parser, a semantic model, or a parseable semantic scheme?  
- Despite issues, believes the paper merits discussion at a venue like CoNLL for its contribution to semantic parsing.
