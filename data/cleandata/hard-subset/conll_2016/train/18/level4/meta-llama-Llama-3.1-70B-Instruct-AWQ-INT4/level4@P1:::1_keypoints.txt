- Presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA's rich semantico-cognitive representations.
- Argues semantic annotation should allow graph structures, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous elements.
- Performs three parsing experiments: phrase-based (Stanford), dependency (UCCA→dependency), and direct UCCA graph parsing.
- Contribution: a transition-based graph parser tailored to UCCA.
- Contribution: articulation of three principles for semantic-based annotation schemes.
- Contribution: empirical comparison of direct parsing vs conversion-based approaches.
- Strength: novel approach to parsing UCCA graphs with thorough evaluation.
- Strength: includes comparisons with other parsers and conversion methods.
- Strength: the three proposed principles are broadly applicable beyond UCCA.
- Weakness: Claim of being the first parser for such semantic schemes is not fully justified.
- Weakness: No clear justification for the chosen feature template and its relevance to UCCA.
- Weakness: Lacks direct, detailed comparisons with other models/parsers cited in the literature.
- Weakness: No error analysis provided to illuminate parser strengths and failure modes.
- Question: Please justify the chosen feature template and its relation to UCCA representations.

- Question: Provide comparisons with other referenced models/parsers to assess significance.
- Question: Provide an error analysis to clarify strengths and weaknesses of the parser.
