**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a transition-based approach for broad-coverage semantic parsing, focusing on the Universal Cognitive Conceptual Annotation (UCCA) scheme. The authors argue that a grounded semantic representation should support three structural properties: multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. They introduce a novel transition-based parser, Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), which supports these properties and achieves competitive results on the UCCA-annotated corpora.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Transition-Based Parser**: The authors propose a transition-based parser that can handle the complexities of UCCA graphs, including multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. **Conversion-Based Parsing Approach**: The authors also explore a conversion-based parsing approach, where they convert UCCA structures to constituency trees and dependency trees, and train existing parsers on these converted representations.
3. **Experiments on UCCA Parsing**: The authors conduct experiments on UCCA parsing, achieving competitive results and demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative Approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing, which can handle the complexities of UCCA graphs.
2. **Competitive Results**: The authors achieve competitive results on the UCCA-annotated corpora, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.
3. **Comparison to Existing Parsers**: The authors compare their approach to existing parsers, including UPARSE and MaltParser, and demonstrate its strengths and weaknesses.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Comparison to Other Models**: The authors do not compare their approach to other models, such as those proposed in the 2014-2015 Semeval shared tasks, which could provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their approach.
2. **Lack of Error Analysis**: The authors do not provide an error analysis, which could help to identify areas for improvement and provide a more nuanced understanding of their approach.
3. **Unclear Introduction**: The authors' introduction is dense and unclear, making it difficult to understand the context and significance of their work.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed comparison to other models, such as those proposed in the 2014-2015 Semeval shared tasks?
2. Can you provide an error analysis to help identify areas for improvement and provide a more nuanced understanding of your approach?
3. Can you clarify your introduction to provide a clearer understanding of the context and significance of your work?
