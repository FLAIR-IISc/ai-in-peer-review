- Paper proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and outlines three general principles (graph-based, non-terminals, discontinuity), though these principles are not specific to semantics.  
- Experimental setup includes three evaluations: constituency parsing via UCCA-to-constituency (Stanford parser), dependency parsing via UCCA-to-dependency, and direct transition-based graph parsing (the main contribution).  
- Major methodological and empirical justifications are insufficient and need addressing before publication.  
- Claiming novelty as the “first” parser for such semantic schemes is questionable; the transition set does not clearly target the unique properties of semantic graphs.  
- Remote-edge transitions may be unnecessary—similar effects could be achieved by changes in the label set rather than new transitions.  
- The paper omits comparisons to relevant post-2014 transition-based or higher-order graph parsing work (e.g., Almeida & Martins, Ribeyre et al.), which should be used as baselines.  
- The proposed feature template appears tied to this scheme, and model behavior seems dominated by count and unigram features rather than the template.  
- Uparse-based phrase experiments should be validated with alternative parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins 2015).  
- Evaluation metrics should align with shared-task/SDP standards to allow meaningful comparisons with other systems and datasets.  
- Lack of comparison points with existing models and parsers undermines the empirical contribution despite the paper’s interest.  
- Request to introduce the term “grounded semantics” earlier in the paper (before page 2).  
- Suggestion to include a baseline using constituent trees with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser.  
- Converting UCCA to surface dependency trees is lossy; a richer conversion (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014 SDP) would be more appropriate.  
- Need to clarify and provide examples/references for “implicit units” in UCCA that have no textual correspondent.  
- UCCA appears unable to represent quantifier scope like MRS/DRT, raising questions about its informativeness and comparability; an error analysis is needed.  
- The three stated principles seem ad-hoc and the paper should more clearly state its goal (UCCA parsing, semantic analysis model, or advocating a parsable semantic scheme), as the current presentation is dense and confusing.
