- Presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA to handle rich semantico-cognitive representations.  
- Authors propose three principles for semantic annotation: graph-based (reentrancy), non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous elements, noting these aren’t unique to semantics.  
- Paper runs three parsing experiments: phrase-based (Stanford + UCCA→constituency), dependency (UCCA→dependency), and direct transition-based UCCA parsing.  
- Main contribution: a transition-based graph parser tailored to UCCA graphs.  
- Main contribution: evaluation via three complementary parsing experiments.  
- Main contribution: claim of being the first parser to tackle this representation.  
- Strength: novel parser capable of handling UCCA’s rich structures.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across multiple conversion/parsing setups.  
- Strength: advancing the field by addressing a previously underexplored representation.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison with other existing parsers that could potentially handle UCCA.  
- Weakness: no error analysis provided to diagnose parser strengths and failure modes.  
- Weakness: the three proposed principles for semantic annotation appear ad-hoc and need clarification or expansion.  
- Question: please provide more thorough comparisons with other parsers that might handle UCCA.  
- Question: please provide an error analysis of parser performance.  
- Question: please clarify and expand the three principles for semantic-based annotation.  
- Question: how will implicit units in UCCA that lack textual correspondents be handled?  
- Question: how does the scheme model quantifier scope and how does that compare to syntax-oriented schemes?
