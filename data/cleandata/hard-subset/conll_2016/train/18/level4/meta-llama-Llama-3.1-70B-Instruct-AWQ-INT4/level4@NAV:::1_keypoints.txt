- Paper introduces a transition-based graph parser for UCCA-style semantico-cognitive annotations and outlines essential components of a semantic-based annotation scheme.
- The authors identify three required properties: graphs allowing multiple governors, non-terminal nodes for complex structures, and support for discontinuous elements.
- The paper notes these principles are not unique to semantic frameworks and could apply across representation layers.
- Three parsing experiments are presented: phrase-based (Stanford parser + UCCA-to-constituency), dependency-based (UCCA-to-dependency), and direct UCCA graph parsing with the proposed transition parser.
- Reviewer finds the work intriguing but raises methodological and empirical justification concerns.
- The claim of first proposing a parser for such semantic schemes is questioned; prior extensions/adaptations (Almeda & Martins; Ribeyre et al.) could apply.
- Transition sets in the paper do not specifically address unique graph characteristics like under-specified structural arguments or semantically-oriented labels.
- The reviewer objects to the lack of comparison to existing graph parsers as baselines.
- Phrase-based experiments using Uparse should have been validated with other parsers (e.g., Fernández-González & Martins).
- Evaluation should use metrics consistent with related shared tasks to better assess the abstract syntaxico-semantic view.
- Suggestions: introduce "grounded semantic" earlier, clarify implicit UCCA units with examples, and better articulate whether the paper is about a UCCA parser, a semantic model, or a parsable semantic-oriented scheme.
- Suggestions: use constituent trees with rich node labels (Berkeley parser baseline), employ richer dependency conversions (e.g., Schluter et al. Semeval SDP), and compare to formalisms like MRS/DRT including quantifier scope modelling.
- Despite concerns, the reviewer believes the paper is worth discussion at ConLL due to its relevance and timing in the field.
