- Introduces novel broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, including a transition-based parser, addressing a logical need given the formalism and data.  
- Introductory framing is problematic: claiming UCCA is uniquely valuable among graph-based representations is overstated and weakens the paper; a UCCA parser is a worthwhile contribution without sweeping claims.  
- The three proposed criteria for evaluating semantic formalisms are questionable: the benefit of nonterminal nodes is not justified and may be replicable via richer edge labels.  
- The concern about discontinuity is irrelevant for non-grounded semantic representations, so it should not be used as a general criterion.  
- Claimed advantages of a “grounded” approach over AMR are not convincingly demonstrated; the term “grounded” is overloaded and alternatives like “anchored” or “lexicalized” should be considered.  
- Parser implementations appear sound though the reviewer did not verify implementation details.  
- Evaluation results are unconvincing: MaltParser outperforms the Baseline Span Parser (BSP) on primary edges and remote-edge f-scores are modest.  
- Evaluation overlooks related work on approximating dependency graphs as trees (CoNLL 2014/2015) and reconstruction methods (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) that could improve MaltParser and reduce BSP’s apparent advantage.
