Paper presents the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, comparing conversion-based methods and a novel transition-based parser.  
Main contribution: a transition-based parser that directly handles UCCA complexities (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities); conversion methods are secondary.  
Strength — Novelty: introduces a dedicated transition-based parsing approach tailored to UCCA’s expressiveness.  
Strength — Resources: provides implementation, converters, and experimental baselines for future work.  
Strength — Comparative study: explores both conversion-based and direct parsing, highlighting conversion limitations.  
Weakness — Motivation framing: overassertive claims that UCCA is uniquely capable and insufficient justification for the three proposed criteria.  
Weakness — Performance & comparison: parser performance is not strong and lacks analysis explaining underperformance versus tools like MaltParser.  
Weakness — Error analysis: lacks detailed error analysis and examples of common parse failures.  
Weakness — Terminology & clarity: "grounded" is ambiguous and background sections are dense and need clearer presentation.  
Question — Error analysis request: please provide detailed analysis of UCCA structures/categories where BSP fails.  
Question — Conversion recovery techniques: have methods for reconstructing information lost in graph-to-tree conversion (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) been tried?  
Question — Criteria justification: provide concrete examples of phenomena that require non-terminal nodes or discontinuities vs. alternatives.  
Question — Efficiency: what are the training and parsing time costs of BSP compared to conversion-based approaches?  
Additional comment — References: reference list is extensive and well-maintained.  
Additional comment — Figures: figures effectively illustrate UCCA structures and parsing process.  
Additional comment — Terminology suggestion: consider replacing "grounded" with a clearer term such as "lexicalized" or "anchored."
