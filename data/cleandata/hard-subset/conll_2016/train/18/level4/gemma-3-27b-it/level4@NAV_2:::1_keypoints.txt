- Paper introduces a transition-based graph parser for UCCA but claims of novelty are overstated since transitions don’t fundamentally address UCCA-specific graph or semantic-label issues.
- Remote-edge handling could be implemented with existing transitions and label modifications, so the proposed transitions aren't clearly necessary.
- The authors omit comparison to prior transition-based graph parsers from SemEval-era work (e.g., Almeda & Martins; Ribeyre et al.), and these should be used as baselines.
- The model appears tied to a specific feature template, but count features and unigrams have greater influence than the template, questioning scheme-specificity.
- Phrase-based experiments using UPARSE need validation against other strong constituency parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins).
- Converting UCCA to surface dependency trees causes substantial information loss; a richer conversion (e.g., Schluter et al., SemEval SDP) would be preferable.
- Because UCCA is more abstract than most SDP treebanks, evaluation should use metrics from related shared tasks and include comparisons to competitive systems/datasets.
- The lack of comparisons with existing models and parsers weakens the empirical claims and overall contribution.
- The paper should define “grounded semantic” before first use.
- The authors should consider a baseline using constituent trees with rich node labels and propagated traces (e.g., Berkeley parser).
- Clarify or give examples/references for “implicit units” in UCCA that have no textual correspondent.
- Discuss whether UCCA can represent semantic phenomena like quantifier scope and how it compares to MRS, DRT, DM, or PCEDT in semantic expressivity.
- An error analysis is needed, since reported scores suggest parsing UCCA is challenging and informative diagnostics are missing.
- The three proposed principles are somewhat ad-hoc and should be grounded in a clearer, broader introduction.
- The paper must clarify its primary aim: proposing a parser for UCCA, a model for semantic analysis, or advocating a parsable semantic-oriented scheme.
