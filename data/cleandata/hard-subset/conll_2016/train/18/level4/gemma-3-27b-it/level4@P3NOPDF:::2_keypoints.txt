- Paper presents the first broad-coverage UCCA semantic parsers for Chinese and proposes a transition-based parser.  
- UCCA graphs differ from CoNLL dependency graphs by including nonterminal nodes and from AMR by being “grounded” (every token represented).  
- Introduction/background is overly critical of other semantic formalisms and frames UCCA as the only sensible approach, which is unsubstantiated.  
- The three criteria used to evaluate SR formalisms are unconvincing; the benefit of nonterminal nodes is not clearly demonstrated.  
- The discontinuity criterion is irrelevant for non-grounded SRs, and the advantages of “grounded” representations over AMR are not clearly articulated.  
- Recommend reconsidering the term “grounded” (suggestions: “anchored” or “lexicalized”) for clarity.  
- Parsing methods seem sound in principle, though the reviewer did not exhaustively check implementation details.  
- Evaluation is underwhelming: a MaltParser baseline outperforms the BSP on primary edges, and f-scores on remote edges are low.  
- Prior work on converting dependency graphs to trees and reconstructing edges (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) is relevant and could improve baselines and edge recovery.  
- Recommend substantial revision of the intro for balanced argumentation and strengthening the evaluation by incorporating graph-to-tree approximation techniques.
