- Paper introduces first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and compares UCCA’s nonterminal, grounded graphs to CoNLL dependencies and AMR.
- Authors evaluate several parsing approaches, including a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA parses.
- Intro/background framing is misleading by implying UCCA is uniquely the right graph-based SR to study; a strong UCCA parser is a valuable contribution without that claim.
- The three proposed evaluation criteria are questionable: the benefit of nonterminal nodes is unclear and may be replicable with richer edge labels.
- The discontinuity criterion is irrelevant for ungrounded SRs, and claimed advantages of grounded representations over AMR are not convincingly shown.
- The term “grounded” is loaded and ambiguous; consider alternatives like “anchored” or “lexicalized.”
- The introductory section needs substantial refinement and stronger, more careful argumentation.
- The parser implementation seems sound in principle, though not all implementation details were verified.
- Reported evaluation results are unimpressive: MaltParser outperforms the Beam-Search Parser on primary edges, and remote-edge f-scores remain low.
- Prior work on graph-to-tree “tree approximations” (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) is relevant; using such techniques could improve dependency-tree baselines and reduce the BSP’s apparent advantage.
