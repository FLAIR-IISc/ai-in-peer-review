- Presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA and situates it within broad-coverage semantic parsing.  
- Claims novelty as first parser for this semantically-oriented scheme, but transitions don't seem tailored to UCCA-specific graph properties.  
- Remote-edge transitions may be unnecessary since existing labeling schemes could handle them.  
- Prior graph-parsing work (SemEval 2014–2015 extensions) was not compared or used as a baseline despite applicability.  
- The model appears anchored more by feature templates (counts and unigrams) than by genuinely novel transition operations.  
- Phrase-based experiments rely on UPARSE; validation with other parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzales & Martins) is missing.  
- Dependency conversion to surface trees likely loses information; a richer conversion (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014) would be preferable.  
- Evaluation metrics should match related SDP shared tasks to enable fair comparison with existing systems.  
- Lack of comparisons with available graph parsers (DAGParser, TurboParser) weakens empirical justification.  
- Needs clarification and examples for claims like “UCCA graphs may contain implicit units that have no correspondent in the text.”  
- Paper raises but does not address whether UCCA models scope phenomena (quantifier scope) compared to MRS/DRT/DM/PCEDT.  
- An error analysis is needed to show how “parsable” and informative UCCA annotations are.  
- The three principles for semantic annotation (graph structure, non-terminals, discontinuities) are broad/ad-hoc and need clearer framing.  
- Suggested additional baselines: Berkeley parser with rich node labels and propagated traces.  
- Overall interesting and worth conference discussion (CoNLL), but methodological, comparative, and evaluative shortcomings must be addressed.
