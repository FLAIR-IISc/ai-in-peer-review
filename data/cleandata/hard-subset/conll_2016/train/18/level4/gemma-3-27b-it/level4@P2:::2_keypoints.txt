- Introduces the first parsers for UCCA, a graph-based semantic formalism with multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuities.  
- Presents both conversion-based parsing methods and a novel transition-based parser (BSP) tailored to UCCA.  
- Main contribution: designing and evaluating BSP to directly handle UCCA’s structural properties.  
- Provides empirical baselines for UCCA parsing, evaluated in-domain and out-of-domain.  
- Clear, accessible presentation of UCCA’s motivations and unique characteristics.  
- Commits to releasing code and models to support reproducibility and future work.  
- Claims of UCCA’s unique suitability for broad-coverage semantics are overstated and need a more balanced discussion.  
- BSP’s primary-edge performance is outperformed by simpler conversion-based methods (e.g., MaltParser), limiting evidence for BSP’s advantage.  
- BSP shows some gains on remote edges, but these are not large enough to clearly justify added complexity.  
- Paper omits engagement with related work on reconstructing graph information from tree conversions (e.g., semantic dependency parsing techniques).  
- Reconstructive/tree-approximation techniques could strengthen conversion baselines and the paper’s comparisons.  
- Terminology issue: the term “grounded” is vague and should be replaced with a more precise descriptor for textual anchoring.  
- Question: justify design choices for UCCA elements like non-terminal nodes with concrete examples comparing alternatives.  
- Question: what concrete avenues (features, pre-training, classifiers) do authors see for improving BSP given limited gains so far?  
- Question: have authors tried or considered methods for reconstructing lost graph information in conversions, and what were the results or reasons for not doing so?
