- Presents a transition-based graph parser for UCCA, arguing it can handle rich semantico-cognitive representations.  
- Defines three principles for semantic-based annotation (graph structure, non-terminal nodes, discontinuities) but these are not uniquely semantic.  
- Experiments: (i) phrase-based parsing via Stanford + UCCA→constituency conversion, (ii) dependency parsing via UCCA→dependency conversion, (iii) direct transition-based graph parsing (core contribution).  
- Reviewer finds the work interesting but raises substantial methodological and empirical concerns.  
- Claim of novelty is questionable because prior transition-based graph parsers and higher-order models (post-2014 SemEval) could likely be applied to UCCA.  
- The proposed transition set does not specifically address UCCA’s structural underspecification or semantically-oriented labels.  
- Remote-edge handling might be achievable with existing transitions plus label changes, so specialized transitions may be unnecessary.  
- The parser’s apparent scheme-specific anchoring is largely due to the feature template, whose impact seems smaller than count/unigram features.  
- Missing baselines: existing graph parsers (e.g., Ribeyre, Almeida & Martins, TurboParser) should be used for comparison.  
- Uparse-based phrase experiments should be validated against other parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzales & Martins) — reference missing.  
- Evaluation metrics: should use SDP/shared-task metrics to allow comparison with related work; current lack of comparable baselines is a major weakness.  
- Suggest introducing the term “grounded semantic” earlier (before page 2).  
- Why not use a constituent-tree approach with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser as a strong baseline?  
- Converting to surface dependency trees causes information loss; recommend richer conversion methods (e.g., Schluter et al. 2014).  
- Request clarification or examples for “implicit units” in UCCA that lack text correspondents.  
- Questions about expressivity: does UCCA model quantifier scope or other phenomena (MRS/DRT)? How underspecified/informative and “parsable” is the scheme? Error analysis is needed.  
- The three defining principles are somewhat ad-hoc and need clearer positioning; the paper’s goals (parse UCCA vs. semantic analysis model vs. parsable semantic scheme) are conflated and confusing.  
- Despite weaknesses, the paper merits discussion at a conference like CoNLL.
