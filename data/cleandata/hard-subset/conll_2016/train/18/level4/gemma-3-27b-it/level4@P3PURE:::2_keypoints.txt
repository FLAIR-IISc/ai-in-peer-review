- Presents the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, including a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA parses.
- Developing a semantic parser for UCCA is a reasonable and valuable contribution given existing UCCA data.
- Intro/background overstates UCCA’s uniqueness, arguing (unconvincingly) that UCCA is the only sensible graph-based semantic representation.
- The three criteria proposed for semantic representation formalisms are not fully convincing, particularly the claimed necessity of nonterminal nodes.
- The benefit of nonterminal nodes is unclear; information might be representable via richer edge labels instead.
- Discontinuity concerns are irrelevant for ungrounded formalisms, so using them as a general criterion is questionable.
- Advantages of “grounded” representations over AMR-style representations are insufficiently explained; the term “grounded” is ambiguous and alternatives like “anchored” or “lexicalized” should be considered.
- The introductory section needs more careful phrasing and stronger argumentation.
- The parser implementation seems sound (not examined in detail), but evaluation results are underwhelming.
- MaltParser outperforms the presented BSP parser on primary edges, and F-scores for remote edges remain low.
- Prior work on tree approximations and graph-to-tree reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could be integrated to improve baseline parsers and narrow the claimed advantages of BSP.
