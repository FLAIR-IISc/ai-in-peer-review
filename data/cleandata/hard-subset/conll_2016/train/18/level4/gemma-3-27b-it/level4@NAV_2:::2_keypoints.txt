- Paper presents first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, including a transition-based parser and conversion-based methods, with empirical evaluation.
- Developing a semantic parser for UCCA is reasonable given available annotations, but the paper's framing treats UCCA as the only sensible SR formalism, which is unnecessary.
- Introduction's three criteria for SRs are unconvincing; necessity of "nonterminal" nodes for expressivity is unclear and may be replaceable by richer edge labels.
- The discontinuity argument is weak because it does not apply to non-grounded SRs; advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR-style ones are not clearly articulated.
- The term "grounded" is overloaded; authors should consider alternatives like "anchored" or "lexicalized."
- Introductory section needs careful revision and more nuanced argumentation about UCCA's advantages.
- Parser implementation appears sound, though reviewer did not verify all technical details.
- Evaluation results are not compelling: a simple MaltParser achieves higher F-scores on primary edges than the proposed BSP.
- F-scores on remote edges remain low, reducing the empirical impact of the transition-based approach.
- The paper omits substantial related work on tree approximation and graph-to-tree conversion (e.g., Agic et al., 2015), which could improve conversion-based baselines.
- Integrating existing tree-approximation and edge-reconstruction methods could likely boost MaltParser/LSTM-Malt performance and narrow BSP's advantage.
- Strength: Novelty — first dedicated parsers for UCCA, leveraging available annotated data.
- Strength: Comprehensive approach — multiple parsing strategies explored, including a novel transition-based parser.
- Strength: Clear presentation of UCCA — concise, informative overview for unfamiliar readers.
- Weakness: Unconvincing motivation and framing that overstates UCCA's uniqueness or superiority.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation that fails to show clear superiority of the proposed parser over simpler baselines.
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to prior work on tree approximation and graph-to-tree reconstruction techniques.
- Question: Please clarify specific downstream advantages of UCCA's "grounded" nature compared to AMR-style representations.
- Question: How were features for the transition-based parser chosen and tuned?
- Question: Did you try tree-approximation methods (e.g., Agic et al., 2015) for the conversion-based baselines, and what were the results?
