- Presents a transition-based graph parser for directly parsing UCCA’s rich semantic graphs.  
- Argues three essential properties for broad-coverage semantic schemes: multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, discontinuities.  
- Provides empirical experiments: constituency conversion, dependency conversion, and direct UCCA graph parsing.  
- Demonstrates feasibility with labeled F1 ≈ 60–70% for the direct parser, establishing a baseline.  
- Highlights challenges of parsing representations with reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Releases dataset and implementation publicly to support reproducibility.  
- Uses conversion-based baselines (constituency and dependency) as comparison points.  
- Introduces a novel ‘ratio’ feature tailored to UCCA parsing.  
- Motivation is clear about needing semantic representations beyond syntax.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, evaluating multiple conversion and direct parsing strategies.  
- Public code availability is a strong community contribution.  
- Baseline performance is solid given UCCA complexity.  
- Claiming novelty is misleading due to prior graph parsers (e.g., Almeda & Martins, Ribeyre et al.) not compared.  
- Feature selection lacks detailed justification and ablation to show relative importance.  
- Dependency conversion is overly simplistic and causes information loss; richer conversions should be used.  
- No detailed error analysis to diagnose parser strengths and weaknesses.  
- Paper’s overall goal is ambiguous—mixes parser development, semantic modeling, and scheme validation.  
- The three proposed principles for semantic schemes feel ad hoc and need stronger theoretical justification.  
- Requests clearer explanation and justification of UCCA-specific BSP feature choices.  
- Asks for details on training hyperparameters (MINUPDATE, IMPORTANCE) and exploration of alternatives.  
- Requests an error analysis linking mistakes to UCCA-specific challenges.  
- Wants computational cost comparisons between BSP and conversion-based approaches.  
- Asks about UCCA limitations (e.g., handling quantifier scope) and need for complementary representations.  
- Suggests introducing “grounded semantic” before first use, expanding on implicit units, and considering richer constituent-tree baselines (e.g., Berkeley parser).
