- Paper presents first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA, including a transition-based parser, and evaluates them.  
- Building a UCCA parser is reasonable, but the introduction reads as argumentative, claiming UCCA is the only sensible SR formalism.  
- Reviewer questions the three criteria for SR formalisms; the claimed benefit of non-terminal nodes is not clearly justified.  
- Discontinuity concerns and advantages of grounded representations over AMR-style ones are not convincingly explained.  
- Suggest using alternative terminology to “grounded” (e.g., “anchored” or “lexicalized”) and rephrase the introduction more carefully.  
- The proposed parser seems sound in design, though details were not exhaustively checked.  
- Evaluation is underwhelming: a simple MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are modest.  
- Prior work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction (e.g., CoNLL 2014/2015, Agic et al. 2015) could boost dependency-tree baselines and reduce the novelty of BSP.
