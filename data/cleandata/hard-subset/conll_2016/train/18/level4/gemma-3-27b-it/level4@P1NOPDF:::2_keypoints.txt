- Paper introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and evaluates several methods including a transition-based parser.  
- UCCA differs from CoNLL semantic dependency graphs by allowing nonterminal nodes and from AMR by being token-grounded.  
- Developing a UCCA parser is sensible given available data, but the introduction overclaims UCCA as the only sensible graph-based SR.  
- Intro/background are unconvincing and require more careful, balanced phrasing and argumentation.  
- The three criteria for semantic representation formalisms are questionable; the benefit of nonterminal nodes for expressive capacity is unclear.  
- Discontinuity concerns mainly affect non-grounded SRs, and advantages of “grounded” over AMR-style representations are not clearly demonstrated.  
- The term “grounded” is overloaded; a clearer term such as “anchored” or “lexicalized” is preferable.  
- The parser design seems sound (implementation not examined), but evaluation results are underwhelming.  
- On primary edges, a baseline MaltParser outperforms the BSP parser, and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Relevant prior work on tree approximations and edge reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could improve MaltParser results and weaken claimed BSP advantages.
