- Introduces first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and evaluates multiple approaches, including a transition-based parser.  
- Notes UCCA graphs differ from other SRs by having nonterminal nodes and being "grounded" (tokens directly represented).  
- Developing a UCCA parser is a worthwhile contribution, but the paper overstates UCCA as the only SR formalism worth studying.  
- Disagrees with the three criteria for SRs in the introduction; nonterminal nodes may not add real expressive power.  
- Argues coordinated-head issues could be handled with richer edge labels, so nonterminals may be unnecessary.  
- Claims advantages of grounded representations over AMR are unclear and the term "grounded" is ambiguous; suggests alternatives like "anchored" or "lexicalized."  
- Recommends more careful phrasing and stronger argumentation in the introduction.  
- Parser implementation seems adequate though not independently verified.  
- Evaluation is unconvincing: BSP underperforms MaltParser on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Points out extensive prior work on tree approximations (CoNLL 2014/2015) and methods (e.g., Agić et al. 2015) for reconstructing edges removed during graph-to-tree conversion.  
- Suggests applying such reconstruction techniques could improve MaltParser/LSTM-Malt results and reduce the BSP parser’s claimed advantage.
