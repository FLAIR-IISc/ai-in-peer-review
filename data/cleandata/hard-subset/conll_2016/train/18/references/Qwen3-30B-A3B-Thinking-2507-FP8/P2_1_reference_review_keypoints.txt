- Proposal: First parser (BSP) supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units for broad-coverage semantic parsing.
- Method: Novel transition set and feature design (e.g., LEFT-REMOTEX, RIGHT-REMOTEX, SWAP, "ratio" feature) and comparison to conversion-based approaches.
- Evaluation: Comprehensive framework with conversion upper bounds, in-domain and out-of-domain tests; achieves 69.2% labeled F on primary edges and 60.6% OOD.
- Strength: Fills a significant gap by addressing all three structural properties missing from prior schemes.
- Strength: Technical innovation in extending transition-based parsing to handle BSS structures.
- Strength: Thorough experimental comparisons and robustness analyses.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for choosing UCCA over alternative semantic representations.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis (e.g., remote edges, coordination, discontinuous units) and concrete improvement paths.
- Weakness: Limited discussion and evidence of integration, scalability, and benefits for downstream applications.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparative analysis, error analysis, and application discussion.
- Question: Why was UCCA chosen and what specific advantages does it offer here?
- Question: Can the authors provide detailed error analysis, especially for remote edges and discontinuities?
- Question: How would this parser be integrated into NLP pipelines and what evidence shows benefits for tasks like MT or summarization?
