- Introduces first parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, via conversion methods and a novel transition-based BSP parser.  
- Primary contributions: first BSS-capable parser, BSP with UCCA-specific transitions/features, and comprehensive evaluation showing feasibility.

- Strength: Clearly identifies a gap—no existing parser supports all three BSS properties—with illustrative examples and literature grounding.  
- Strength: Technically well-motivated BSP design with extended transitions for remote edges and discontinuities and carefully chosen features.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including a new metric distinguishing primary vs remote edges, in-/out-of-domain tests, baselines, and ablations; strong primary-edge performance (69.2% F) and better handling of remotes than conversion baselines.  
- Strength: Strong technical execution with clear conversion algorithms, robust domain-transfer results, and detailed feature impact analysis.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons to related semantic parsers (e.g., AMR/other systems) and insufficient explanation of superiority or relation to those methods.  
- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative error analysis to explain failure modes (e.g., where BSP struggles).  
- Weakness: Vague discussion of practical impact and lack of concrete examples or empirical evidence for downstream benefits.  
- Weakness: Overstates novelty given prior SDP work (SemEval 2015) that supports multiple parents and discontinuities; distinction from SDP needs clarification.

- Question: How would BSP handle implicit units that were deferred to future work, and what are the technical challenges?  
- Question: Why wasn't an LSTM integrated into BSP given higher LSTM scores, and how might that affect remote-edge performance?  
- Question: Clarify how UCCA inter-annotator agreement (80–85% F) maps to the paper's evaluation metric and its use as an upper bound.  
- Question: Explain how the claimed novelty differs from SemEval 2015 SDP work (Oepen et al., 2015) that supports multiple parents and discontinuities.

- Overall: Valuable, well-executed contribution that demonstrates BSS parsing feasibility; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty, comparisons, error analysis, and practical impact.
