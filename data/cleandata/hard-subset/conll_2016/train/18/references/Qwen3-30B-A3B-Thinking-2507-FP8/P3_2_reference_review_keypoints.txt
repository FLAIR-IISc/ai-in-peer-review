- Introduces BSP, the first direct parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Proposes a novel transition-based parsing framework with specialized transitions and features tailored for UCCA parsing.  
- Shows BSP achieves ~70% labeled F-score on UCCA, demonstrating feasibility of direct BSS parsing.

- Correctly identifies the gap in semantic parsing: no existing parsers fully support all BSS properties, with clear motivation and examples.  
- Uses a well-motivated transition-based approach, introducing effective transitions (LEFT/RIGHT-EDGEX, REMOTEX) and useful features (ratio feature).  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation including conversion-baselines, in-/out-of-domain tests, and ablations, plus new metrics for BSS evaluation.  
- Clearly situates the work relative to UCCA, AMR, and SDP and motivates UCCA’s cross-linguistic and semantic stability benefits.

- Lacks meaningful comparison to state-of-the-art semantic parsers (e.g., top AMR parsers), making progress difficult to contextualize.  
- Omits detailed error analysis to show whether failures are concentrated on remote edges, non-terminals, or discontinuities.  
- Insufficient analysis of out-of-domain performance differences, particularly on the 20K Leagues corpus.  
- Overlooks related frameworks (e.g., Meaning-Text Theory, Prague Dependency Treebank) that may also support some BSS properties.

- How does BSP compare quantitatively to top existing semantic parsers on their metrics (e.g., AMR parser scores)?  
- Can the authors provide detailed error analysis, especially for remote edges and discontinuous units?  
- How much information is lost in conversion-based approaches—can this be quantified, particularly for remote edges?  
- Given UCCA inter-annotator agreement of 80–85% F-score versus parser’s 70%, is the parser nearing human ceiling or is there room for substantial improvement?

- Overall: significant and well-motivated contribution establishing feasibility of direct BSS parsing, with solid evaluation but gaps in comparisons and analyses.  
- Recommendation: Accept, pending minor revisions addressing comparison to SOTA, detailed error analysis, and out-of-domain/related-work clarifications.
