- Main contribution: First parser that directly supports all three BSS properties—multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Formalization: Provides a formal definition of Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures and identifies the three required structural properties.  
- Conversion baseline: Introduces a principled conversion-based approach to map BSS to dependency/constituency trees for comparison with existing parsers.  
- BSP parser: Proposes a novel transition-based parser (BSP) designed to directly handle BSS requirements.  
- Clear motivation: Strong problem definition with examples showing why existing representations (AMR, SDP) are insufficient for BSS.  
- Methodology: Balanced approach combining conversion baselines and a tailored transition system with well-motivated features.  
- Evaluation: Meaningful metric distinguishing primary vs. remote edges, upper bounds for conversions, and in-/out-of-domain evaluation.  
- Practicality: Provides detailed conversion algorithms and commits to releasing tools for community use.  
- Limited prior-work comparison: Insufficient comparison with parsers that handle some BSS properties (e.g., AMR/UCCA parsers).  
- Lack of qualitative examples: No illustrative parser outputs demonstrating handling of multiple parents, non-terminals, or discontinuities.  
- Sparse error analysis: Performance gap (69.2% vs. 80–85% IAA) is reported but lacks detailed analysis of error types and failure modes.  
- Underdeveloped impact discussion: Practical applications (MT, summarization) are mentioned but not concretely connected to BSS improvements.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify prior UCCA parsing attempts and why they don’t meet BSS criteria; explain how LSTM methods could integrate with BSP; diagnose primary reasons for the 69.2% vs. IAA gap.  
- Overall judgment: Valuable, well-motivated contribution that warrants acceptance with minor revisions to improve comparisons, qualitative examples, and error analysis.
