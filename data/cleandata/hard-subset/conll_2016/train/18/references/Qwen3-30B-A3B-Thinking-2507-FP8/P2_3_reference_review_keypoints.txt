- Introduces the first parser (BSP) that directly handles UCCA's three structural properties: multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Proposes a novel transition-based parser with specialized transitions (LEFT-REMOTEX, RIGHT-REMOTEX, SWAP) for BSS.  
- Presents a comprehensive evaluation framework for DAG-based semantic parsing, including conversion procedures and metrics.  

- Clearly identifies a significant gap: no existing parser supports all three BSS properties simultaneously, with strong motivating examples.  
- Provides a well-motivated, detailed description of UCCA and its linguistic grounding and cross-linguistic applicability.  
- Adapts transition-based parsing thoughtfully with remote features and a ratio regularizer.  
- Offers thorough evaluation (primary vs. remote edges, in-domain and out-of-domain) and upper bounds for conversion approaches.  

- Results are modest (69.2% F-score on primary edges) and far below inter-annotator agreement (80–85%) with insufficient analysis of the gap.  
- Limited comparison to prior semantic parsers (e.g., AMR) and inadequate justification for why those approaches cannot address BSS.  
- Insufficient analysis of why conversion-based approaches fail, and inverse conversion rules appear overly simplistic for reconstructing non-terminals.  
- Lacks case-level analysis showing where the parser succeeds or fails, especially on challenging remote edges.  

- Why does BSP reach only 69.2% F-score versus 80–85% inter-annotator agreement—detailed error analysis requested.  
- How does BSP compare empirically to state-of-the-art semantic parsers (e.g., AMR), and why can't those be adapted to UCCA?  
- Need clarification on specific challenges in the inverse conversion process that limit conversion-based performance.  

- Overall: Significant and well-designed first step addressing a clear gap, but modest results and limited analysis/comparisons weaken the empirical case.
