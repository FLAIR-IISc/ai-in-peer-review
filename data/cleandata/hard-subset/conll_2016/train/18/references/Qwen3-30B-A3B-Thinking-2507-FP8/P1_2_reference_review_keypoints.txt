- Proposes BSP, a transition-based parser and conversion-based methods for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) that handle multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.

Main contributions:
- First parser that directly supports all three BSS properties (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities).
- Introduces a comprehensive evaluation framework and a new metric distinguishing primary vs. remote edges.
- Analyzes trade-offs between conversion-based methods and direct parsing, showing conversion loses information (especially remote edges) while BSP can recover some.

Strengths:
- Clearly identifies a significant gap: no existing parser handles all three BSS properties simultaneously.
- Provides thorough evaluation and analysis across in-domain and out-of-domain settings, highlighting BSP’s ability to predict remote edges.
- Practical implementation with converters and parsers to be released, plus sufficient reproducibility details.

Weaknesses:
- Limited justification for the transition-based BSP given that classifier improvements (e.g., LSTM) may be the main bottleneck.
- Uses an ad hoc evaluation metric without demonstrating correlation with human judgments or established metrics.
- Lacks thorough comparison with alternative representations (AMR, SDP) and whether BSS properties generalize beyond UCCA.
- Insufficient discussion of practical implications, computational complexity, scalability, and whether 69.2% F-score is useful in applications.
- Weak treatment of domain adaptation; out-of-domain performance drop is reported but not deeply analyzed.

Questions to authors:
- How does UCCA inter-annotator agreement (80–85% F) compare to AMR/SDP, and does higher agreement justify BSP development?
- Why not combine an LSTM classifier with BSP—would this improve results and why wasn’t it explored?
- Have you tested BSP on non-English UCCA data, and what are the challenges for cross-linguistic adaptation?
- Could more sophisticated conversion procedures better preserve non-terminal information instead of the simple rule-based inverse conversion used?

Overall recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with major revisions to address justification for direct parsing, evaluation metric validity, comparisons, and domain-adaptation analysis.
