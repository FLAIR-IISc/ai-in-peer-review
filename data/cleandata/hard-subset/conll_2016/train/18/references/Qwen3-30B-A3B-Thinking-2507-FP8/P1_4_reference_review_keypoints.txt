- Formalizes Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.  
- Introduces the first transition-based parser (BSP) that directly supports BSS with novel transitions.  
- Provides conversion protocols between UCCA and standard dependency/constituency representations to enable baselines.  

- Clear problem definition and motivation with compelling illustrative examples.  
- Well-motivated transition-based approach and thorough explanation of new transitions (e.g., LEFT-REMOTEX, RIGHT-REMOTEX).  
- Thorough experimental evaluation including conversion-based baselines, out-of-domain testing, and upper bounds for baselines.  
- Practical contribution: plans to release code/data and detailed conversion protocols for reproducibility.  
- Clear comparison with related work (SDP, AMR) and explanation of advances.  

- Limited performance (69.2% F on primary edges) compared to other semantic parsing benchmarks and lack of LSTM-based results.  
- Missing qualitative error analysis and illustrative examples of parser failures and successes.  
- Conversion-based baselines are lossy (drop remote edges and non-terminals) with insufficient quantification of information loss.  
- No direct comparison with existing dependency DAG parsers despite their relevance and existence.  
- Uses unconventional edge yield–based metrics, raising comparability concerns and lacking validation of metric suitability.  

- How does parser performance compare to UCCA inter-annotator agreement (80–85% F) and what does that imply?  
- Can the authors provide preliminary LSTM-based results or deeper analysis supporting expected improvements?  
- Can the authors quantify information loss in conversion (e.g., percentage of edges lost) and its impact on evaluation?  
- Why was code for dependency DAG parsers unobtainable, and will BSP be released to facilitate future comparisons?  
- How does discarding linkage nodes/edges affect evaluation and overall parser performance?  

- Overall: well-written and important work formalizing BSS and providing the first parser, but needs stronger empirical analysis, qualitative error analysis, and comparisons to be more impactful.
