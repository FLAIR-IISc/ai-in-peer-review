- Paper introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA that supports three BSS properties: multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Claims to be the first parser supporting all three BSS properties simultaneously.  
- Also proposes conversion protocols from UCCA to dependency/constituency formalisms and parses with existing tools.  

- Main contributions: first broad-coverage BSS parser, development of BSP, and conversion protocols.  

- Strength: Addresses a clear gap by supporting necessary structural properties for broad-coverage semantic parsing.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel evaluation measure for DAGs that accounts for primary and remote edges.  
- Strength: Provides a practical, publicly released implementation with detailed transition sets and features.  
- Strength: Competitive results (69.2% labeled F-score on primary edges; 60.6% out-of-domain), showing robustness.  

- Weakness: Parser architecture has limited novelty, largely adapting existing transition-based techniques.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison with related work (e.g., SDP parsers, discontinuous constituency parsers).  
- Weakness: New DAG evaluation metrics are not fully justified against simpler alternatives (primary edges plus remote edges).  
- Weakness: Conversion-based approaches are lossy and the paper does not quantify how much loss causes performance drops.  
- Weakness: Remote edge prediction is weak (15.3% F-score) with little analysis of causes.  

- Question: Have there been any prior unpublished attempts at UCCA parsing (given Sulem et al. 2015 on IAA)?  
- Question: How does BSP concretely handle non-terminal nodes compared to SDP approaches that avoid them?  
- Question: Why wasn't the higher-performing LSTM-based parser integrated into BSP, despite its better primary-edge F-score?  
- Question: Can the authors separate the contribution of parser quality versus conversion loss to performance differences?  

- Overall: Significant and valuable contribution as the first BSS parser, though technical novelty is moderate; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve comparisons, quantify conversion loss, and analyze remote edges.
