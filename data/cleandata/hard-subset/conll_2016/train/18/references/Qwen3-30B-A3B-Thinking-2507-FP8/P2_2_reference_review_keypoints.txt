- Introduces BSS (Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures) supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Proposes two approaches: conversion-based mapping to trees and a novel transition-based BSP that directly handles BSS.  
- Claims the first parser to support all three BSS properties concurrently.  
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation of conversion-based methods, highlighting their limitations.  
- Develops a practical conversion protocol between UCCA and other formalisms.  
- Conducts thorough experiments on the UCCA corpus with in-domain and out-of-domain testing, reporting ~70% F-score on primary edges.  
- Commits to public release of converters and parsers to facilitate further research.  
- Clearly identifies an important gap in semantic parsing with convincing linguistic examples.  
- Experimental results suggest BSS parsing is feasible and provide useful upper bounds for conversion methods.  
- The BSP parser is largely incremental, building on existing transition-based and DAG parsing techniques.  
- Lacks direct comparison to existing DAG parsers that handle multiple parents, weakening claims of novelty.  
- Does not meaningfully compare results to other semantic frameworks (e.g., AMR, SDP) or standard benchmarks.  
- Provides insufficient analysis of failure cases and specific structures the parser struggles with.  
- Offers limited discussion and empirical analysis of linguistic coverage for challenging phenomena (coordination, MWEs, discontinuities).  
- Requests clarification on differences from prior DAG parsers, benchmarking against other formalisms, detailed failure analyses, and relation to UCCA inter-annotator agreement.  
- Overall: valuable problem identification and evaluation, but parser novelty is incremental; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing comparisons and error analysis.
