- Paper targets broad-coverage semantic parsing for representations that jointly support reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities, focusing on UCCA.  
- Proposes two approaches: lossily convert UCCA DAGs to constituency/dependency trees and back, and a new transition-based parser (BSP) that natively supports all three properties.  
- Experiments on in-domain (Wiki) and out-of-domain (20K Leagues) data show best primary-edge F1 from conversion+LSTM dependency parser (69.2); BSP is competitive on primary edges and uniquely predicts some remotes (13.3 F1 in-domain).  

Strengths:
- Addresses an underexplored gap by jointly supporting reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities for grounded semantics.  
- Solid empirical setup with multiple baselines and quantified conversion upper bounds that reveal information loss.  
- First end-to-end transition system combining discontinuous constituency and DAG dependency ideas with remote-edge handling.  
- Includes out-of-domain evaluation and informative feature ablations (notably the “ratio” feature).  
- Plans to release converters and parsers, enabling reuse and further research.  

Weaknesses:
- Main technical contribution (BSP) underperforms the conversion+LSTM baseline by ~10 F1 on primary edges and has low remote-edge F1 (~13).  
- Methodological novelty is limited: transition system composes known ingredients and the “ratio” feature is minor.  
- Evaluation omits linkage and implicit units, lacks per-label/error-type analyses, and doesn’t compare to broader SDP or bi-lexical DAG conversions.  
- Learning/search choices (greedy perceptron, no beam/neural classifier) likely limit BSP performance; neuralization is suggested but untested.  
- Some details are missing or underspecified (POS/preprocessing, distribution of remotes/discontinuities, concrete Label(·) rules, inconsistent references).  

Questions for authors:
- What proportions of edges/units are remote and discontinuous, and how many sentences contain each phenomenon?  
- Which tokenizer/tagger were used and are POS tags gold or predicted? Were pretrained embeddings used and are resources consistent across systems?  
- Can you provide per-label F1 and a brief confusion/error analysis for primary vs. remote edges?  
- Did you try beam search or a neural classifier for BSP, and what are expected computational trade-offs?  
- Please specify the Label(·) rules used in inverse conversion and their empirical impact (e.g., ablating to always Center).  

Additional comments:
- Clarify that the constituency-conversion upper bound of 100% on primary edges follows from dropping remotes to avoid misinterpretation.  
- Include a qualitative example contrasting a correct remote edge vs. the nearest-tree baseline.  
- Fix reference formatting and provide the conversion/inverse-conversion rule details in the main text or appendix.  

Overall:
- Useful task framing, practical baselines, and released artifacts make the work valuable, but BSP’s empirical underperformance and modest novelty limit its immediate impact.
