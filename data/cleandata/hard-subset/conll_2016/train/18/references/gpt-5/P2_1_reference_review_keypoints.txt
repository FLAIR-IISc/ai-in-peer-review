- Paper tackles broad-coverage grounded semantic parsing requiring reentrancies, non-terminals, and discontinuities, using UCCA as the testbed.
- Proposes two strategies: conversion-based parsing (to constituency/dependency) and a direct transition-based parser (BSP) that builds UCCA-style DAGs with discontinuities.
- Main contribution: first practical treatment and evaluation setup for jointly supporting reentrancies, non-terminals, and discontinuities in a single parser.
- Provides explicit conversion protocols between UCCA and constituency/dependency formats and quantifies information loss with upper bounds.
- Empirical findings: competitive primary-edge F-scores via conversion (69.2% with LSTM dependency parser) and a direct parser that predicts remote edges at low F.
- BSP extends prior transition systems with NODE and remote-edge transitions, SWAP for discontinuities, and a dynamic oracle tailored to UCCA.
- Methodology is sound: dual strategy, clearly defined forward/inverse conversions, and reporting of upper bounds to contextualize results.
- Evaluation is comprehensive, including in-domain and out-of-domain tests, and ablations showing feature importance and robustness.
- Reproducibility is strong: conversion algorithms, transition set, features, training regimen, and evaluation metric are specified and code release is promised.
- Weakness: BSP achieves very low labeled F on remote edges (about 13%), undermining practical handling of reentrancies.
- Weakness: Comparisons may be unfair—best primary-edge result uses a neural LSTM after lossy conversion while BSP uses a linear perceptron and greedy decoding.
- Weakness: Evaluation metric (yield-based labeled edge matching) can overestimate structural correctness and misses attachment/height distinctions.
- Weakness: Some conversions are simplistic and may bias outcomes; dependency inverse conversion uses heuristics that limit upper bounds.
- Weakness: Greedy decoding and linear training for BSP likely limit performance; beam search or neural classifiers were not explored sufficiently.
- Requests for clarification: proportion and per-category performance of remote edges and whether low F is due to recall or precision.
- Asks whether beam search or global training were tried for BSP and their impact on remote/discontinuous edges.
- Requests detailed error analysis identifying dominant failure modes and hardest UCCA categories.
- Asks how sensitive dependency inverse-conversion is to the Label(u) heuristic and whether learned labeling could improve upper bounds.
- Notes a likely typo in the data split description and asks for clarification on test splits and overlap.
- Overall judgment: timely, well-motivated work that opens an important line of research but needs clearer evaluation and stronger handling/discussion of remote edges; leans toward acceptance.
