- Targets broad-coverage grounded semantic parsing (UCCA) supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Proposes two strategies: conversion-based parsing (constituency/dependency mappings) and a novel transition-based parser (BSP) for DAGs with non-terminals and discontinuities.  
- Introduces label-driven tree↔dependency conversion algorithms, an evaluation protocol separating primary vs. remote edges, and in-/out-of-domain experiments on UCCA English.  

- Well-motivated problem with clear representational targets and UCCA as a practical testbed.  
- Novel, coherent transition inventory (NODEX, LEFT/RIGHT-EDGE, LEFT/RIGHT-REMOTE, SWAP) handling DAGs, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Dynamic-oracle perceptron training with UCCA-aware features and a regularizer to limit spurious node creation.  
- Strong complementary conversion baselines and upper-bound analyses that quantify conversion loss.  
- Empirical evaluation includes in-domain and out-of-domain robustness, showing BSP retains some remote prediction ability while conversion methods cannot predict remotes.  

- BSP’s primary-edge accuracy lags a strong conversion+Stack-LSTM baseline (59.0 vs. 69.2 F1).  
- Remote-edge F1 is very low (~13%), raising questions about practical utility and lacking detailed error analysis.  
- Evaluation metric may collapse important structural distinctions and lacks per-label, phenomenon-level, and prevalence analyses.  
- Training/search choices are limited (greedy perceptron); no beam/global normalization or stronger graph-based baselines are evaluated.  
- Conversion algorithm details and heuristics are under-specified in the main text, with material choices deferred to supplementary.  

- What proportion of edges are remote vs. primary, and per-label distribution and per-label F1 for remotes?  
- How sensitive are results to label priority order in Algorithm 1, and what happens under alternative or learned priorities?  
- Would adding a small beam (4–8) or global normalization improve primary and remote performance?  
- Did the authors try replacing perceptron features with neural encoders (bi-LSTM) in BSP, and if not, what are the hurdles?  
- What are runtime/complexity comparisons (tokens/sec) between BSP, UPARSE, and Stack-LSTM, and how does SWAP frequency affect time?  

- Provide qualitative error examples for remotes and discontinuities and report per-label precision/recall and phenomenon-level scores.  
- Consider simple neural features (embeddings) and biaffine scoring or joint/distillation training with converted trees to close the primary-edge gap.  
- Clarify impact of removing linkage/implicit nodes on data statistics and evaluation.  

- Overall: solid, timely paper defining a challenging target with principled modeling and baselines; leans toward acceptance contingent on stronger analysis and clarification of key design choices.
