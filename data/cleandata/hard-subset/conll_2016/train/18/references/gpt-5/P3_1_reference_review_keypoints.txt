- Paper addresses broad-coverage semantic parsing for graphs with reentrancies, non-terminals, and discontinuities (UCCA) and proposes conversion-based pipelines plus a new transition-based BSP parser.  
- Conversion approach maps UCCA DAGs to discontinuous constituency or bi-lexical dependency trees, uses off-the-shelf parsers, and maps back; best primary-edge F1 ≈ 69.2 (dependency + LSTM).  
- BSP transition parser introduces dedicated transitions for non-terminals, discontinuities (SWAP), and remote edges, with a perceptron + dynamic oracle and a “ratio” regularizer; BSP gets ≈59 F1 primary, ≈13 F1 remote.  
- Main contribution: a concrete transition system for grounded DAG parsing supporting non-terminals, discontinuities, and remote edges, with a practical feature set and dynamic oracle.  
- Main contribution: a systematic, measured conversion methodology between UCCA DAGs and discontinuous constituency / bi-lexical dependency formalisms, providing upper bounds and baselines.  
- Main contribution: a simple span-based labeled-edge evaluation for DAGs (primary vs. remote) and the first reported UCCA parsing results in and out of domain.  
- Strength: clear motivation that tree-only or bi-lexical dependency formalisms cannot capture UCCA phenomena like shared arguments, coordination without heads, and discontinuous MWEs.  
- Strength: well-designed transition inventory that explicitly enforces constraints (single primary parent) and handles reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities; dynamic oracle and setup are appropriate.  
- Strength: conversion baselines and upper-bound analysis are strong and fair, showing how far off-the-shelf parsers can go (notably stack-LSTM dependency parser).  
- Strength: empirical scope includes in-domain (Wikipedia) and out-of-domain (20K Leagues) evaluation; BSP shows modest out-of-domain degradation and informative feature ablations (ratio feature helpful).  
- Strength: practical relevance—parsing UCCA could benefit downstream tasks (MT, summarization) due to grounded, cross-linguistic spans.  
- Weakness: BSP underperforms conversion-based baseline on primary edges by ~10 F1 and has very low remote-edge recall; current results don’t show superiority of the bespoke system.  
- Weakness: evaluation metric matches edges by child yield and label only, ignoring parent identity, which can overestimate correctness for reentrancies and remote edges.  
- Weakness: conversion details (head-priority order, Label(u) reconstruction) are underspecified despite conversions producing the best results; sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis—no per-label/phenomenon breakdowns, qualitative remote-edge error analysis, or diagnostics to pinpoint error sources (search vs. features vs. data).  
- Weakness: scope/generalization claims are weak—experiments only in English, linkage/implicit units excluded; cross-linguistic claims need more evidence or discussion.  
- Question: quantify how often the metric counts an edge as correct despite incorrect parent attachment; can you report an alternative metric conditioning on both parent and child anchoring?  
- Question: provide exact label-priority order from Algorithm 1 and full Label(u) definition from Algorithm 2; how sensitive are results to these choices?  
- Question: how does the transition system ensure DAG acyclicity with remote edges—are cycles possible, and if not, why?  
- Question: what is the distribution of remote-edge labels and per-label F1s; which labels are most frequently missed—please provide error analysis for remote edges.  
- Question: did you try beam search, globally normalized training, or stronger classifiers (LSTM) for BSP; what gains are expected over greedy dynamic-oracle training?  
- Additional: ablate the “ratio” feature’s effect on non-terminal generation as a function of sentence length; it may be broadly useful.  
- Additional: report statistical significance and per-category scores (P/A/C/R etc.) and fix minor typos/formatting errors.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept—important, well-motivated work with solid baselines and a first transition system for UCCA-like DAGs, but BSP lags conversions, evaluation and conversion details need strengthening, and analysis should be deeper.
