- Paper proposes BSP: a transition-based parser that directly constructs UCCA-style DAGs with discontinuities and remote edges, plus conversions between UCCA DAGs and constituency/dependency trees and an empirical study including a new yield+label metric.  
- Primary contribution is the transition system and training setup that handle multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities; secondary contributions are conversion protocols and empirical positioning.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated problem and appropriate choice of UCCA as a testbed for broad-coverage semantic parsing with BSS properties.  
- Technically sound transition system extending SHIFT/REDUCE/NODE/SWAP with primary vs. remote edges and a dynamic oracle.  
- Conversion baselines are clearly specified with quantified upper bounds on recoverable structure, aiding interpretability.  
- Thorough empirical setup with in- and out-of-domain evaluation, strong baselines, remote-edge scores, ablations, and upper-bound analyses.  
- Clear presentation and reproducibility commitments (detailed transitions, features, algorithms, promised code/converters).

Weaknesses:
- BSP underperforms a strong conversion baseline on primary edges (best primary F1 via converted LSTM dependency parser ≈69.2 vs. BSP ≈59–60), and remote-edge F1 is very low (~13).  
- Modeling choices are limited: greedy averaged perceptron with hand-crafted features, no beam or neural classifier, leaving classifier capacity confounded with transition system potential.  
- Evaluation and analysis are coarse: yield+label metric misses attachment-height and fine-grained error patterns; lacks significance tests and runtime/efficiency reporting.  
- Comparisons lack DAG dependency parser baselines due to tool availability; inverse conversion heuristic (Label(u)) is underspecified and could affect results.  
- Dataset scope limited by discarding linkage, implicit, and inter-sentence edges, with limited analysis of their frequency or impact.

Questions for authors:
- What is the distribution and label breakdown of remote edges, and how does BSP perform by label/construction (e.g., control/raising, argument sharing)?  
- Have you tried adding a small beam or neural classifier (MLP/LSTM) to BSP, and do you have preliminary results?  
- Can you provide error analysis for discontinuous constructions and coordination to show where BSP gains/loses relative to conversions?  
- What are average transitions per sentence, runtime comparisons to UPARSE/dependency baselines, and average number of SWAPs per sentence?  
- How sensitive are results to the inverse conversion heuristic (Label(u)) and the priority order over UCCA labels?

Overall assessment:
- Novelty high for a direct parser covering all three BSS properties; soundness and clarity good; significance moderate because primary-edge quality lags conversion baselines and remote-edge performance is low, yielding a borderline accept recommendation contingent on stronger modeling and deeper analysis.
