- Paper introduces a transition-based parser (BSP) for UCCA-style DAGs supporting non-terminals, discontinuities, and reentrancy, with a dynamic oracle and tailored features.
- Proposes conversion protocols between UCCA DAGs and discontinuous constituency/dependency trees to reuse existing parsers, with explicit upper bounds on information loss.
- Defines a yield+label evaluation for BSS and reports in-domain and out-of-domain English UCCA results.
- Main contribution: joint transition system that handles non-terminals, discontinuities, and remote edges, including a dynamic oracle.
- Main contribution: practical conversion pipelines with quantified upper bounds showing representation loss.
- Main contribution: empirical finding that a strong neural dependency parser plus lossy conversion can outperform BSP on primary edges, while BSP uniquely predicts remote edges.
- Strength: clear problem formulation and motivation, situating UCCA as an appropriate testbed.
- Strength: novel transition system design (separate primary vs. remote, SWAP for discontinuities) and inclusion of a dynamic oracle.
- Strength: careful comparison via conversion upper bounds that contextualize downstream performance.
- Strength: broad and robust empirical evaluation across baselines and out-of-domain data, showing strong stack-LSTM dependency results.
- Strength: sensible, representation-aware evaluation separating primary and remote edges.
- Weakness: poor performance and limited analysis on reentrancy (low remote-edge F1 ~13), lacking qualitative error analysis.
- Weakness: BSP uses dated/underpowered training (greedy perceptron, sparse features, no beam), confounding whether bottleneck is transition system or classifier.
- Weakness: incomplete/comparability-limited evaluation (no unified DAG score, missing unlabeled F1 and per-phenomenon breakdowns).
- Weakness: heuristic conversion choices (high-attachment, label defaults) lack sensitivity/ablation analysis.
- Weakness: experimental clarity issues (typo in splits, vague hyperparameters, missing significance tests, partial feature ablation, linkage edge removal justification).
- Question: please report an aggregate DAG score combining primary and remote edges for fair comparison.
- Question: provide breakdown of remote-edge types recovered by BSP and common error patterns.
- Question: ablation for dynamic oracle / beam search / neural classifier for BSP?
- Question: sensitivity of conversion results to label priority and high-attach heuristics?
- Question: clarify train/dev/test split indices and confirm no document-level leakage.
- Suggestion: neuralize BSP (biLSTM or stack-LSTM controller) to close gap with conversion+LSTM baseline.
- Suggestion: provide error analysis focused on discontinuity, non-terminals, and reentrancy.
- Suggestion: report speed, complexity, and memory footprint for practical adoption.
- Suggestion: release code and converters to benefit the community.
- Overall: credible first attempt addressing an important task, but needs stronger modeling and deeper analysis; reviewer leans toward acceptance conditional on improved experimental clarity and evaluation.
