- Summary: Proposes conversion-based and a new transition-based (BSP) parser for UCCA DAGs with non-terminals and discontinuities; conversion+LSTM dependency parser yields best primary F1, BSP uniquely predicts remote edges.

- Contribution 1: New transition system and parser (BSP) supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities, dynamic-oracle training, and explicit REMOTE edges.

- Contribution 2: Systematic conversion framework between UCCA and constituency/dependency trees with algorithms, upper-bound analysis, and strong conversion-based baselines.

- Contribution 3: Empirical analysis including out-of-domain evaluation, feature ablations, and evidence that full BSS parsing is feasible with directions for improvement.

- Strength: First transition-based parser jointly supporting DAGs with multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities in grounded semantics.

- Strength: Careful conversion pipelines with upper bounds make comparisons interpretable and quantify information loss from conversion.

- Strength: Strong neural baseline (stack-LSTM dependency parser) achieves 69.2 primary F1, outperforming traditional parsers and informing BSP development.

- Strength: Out-of-domain results are close to in-domain, indicating reasonable cross-genre generalization.

- Strength: Use of dynamic oracle, informative feature set, and novel “ratio” regularization feature with ablation analysis.

- Weakness: BSP lags conversion baseline (∼59 vs. 69.2 F1) and remote-edge F1 is low (~13); greedy perceptron and lack of neural modeling likely limit performance.

- Weakness: Limited qualitative and fine-grained error analysis; no breakdown by UCCA phenomena or labels to identify bottlenecks.

- Weakness: Incomplete comparisons to related DAG/SDP parsers; absence of reimplementations or reported numbers leaves a gap.

- Weakness: Metric/analysis issues—yield-based P/R may obscure attachment errors, no significance testing, and remote-edge evaluation is limited.

- Weakness: Missing details and clarity in conversions and label priority; malformed references; absent runtime/complexity and SWAP statistics.

- Question: Provide label-wise and phenomenon-level breakdowns (coordination, MWEs, PPs, discontinuities) and distribution of remote-edge predictions?

- Question: What is the priority ordering over UCCA labels for head selection, and how sensitive are results to this ordering?

- Question: Describe the Label() function for inverse dependency→constituency conversion and its impact.

- Question: How often does SWAP fire, and what fraction of gold discontinuities does BSP recover; any discontinuity error statistics?

- Question: Have you tried beam search or neural encoders (BiLSTM) for BSP, and what performance gains do you expect?

- Question: Can you compare to SDP parsers via an SDP-style conversion of UCCA or discuss adapting BSP to bi-lexical DAGs?

- Question: Are there significance tests between BSP and baselines and between in-domain and out-of-domain results?

- Question: What is BSP’s runtime and memory profile versus baselines, and how does SWAP affect complexity on long sentences?

- Additional comment: Releasing converters and parser, plus adding per-label scores and qualitative error analysis, would substantially strengthen impact.
