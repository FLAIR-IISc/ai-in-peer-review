- Paper targets Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) requiring reentrancies, non-terminals, and discontinuities, using UCCA as the concrete formalism; conversion pipeline gets 69.2 F (primary), BSP gets 59.0 F (primary) and 13.3 F (remote).
- Contribution: clear problem formulation and positioning UCCA as a grounded, cross-linguistically motivated BSS target.
- Contribution: BSP, a transition-based parser that directly builds UCCA DAGs with explicit remote-edge transitions, non-terminals, and SWAP for discontinuities.
- Contribution: conversion protocols from UCCA DAGs to constituency and dependency trees with upper bounds, enabling reuse of existing parsers.
- Strength: well-motivated target and examples showing limitations of bi-lexical dependencies for coordination, MWEs, and PP attachment.
- Strength: concrete, reproducible methodology with explicit transition inventory, dynamic oracle training, feature templates, and released code.
- Strength: solid baselines and empirical scaffold showing conversion-based approaches (Stack-LSTM) are strong and quantifying loss through conversion.
- Strength: BSP directly handles reentrancies and discontinuities via LEFT/RIGHT-REMOTEX transitions and SWAP; introduces a useful “ratio” feature to regularize non-terminals.
- Strength: results are robust across domains, suggesting generality beyond Wikipedia.
- Weakness: BSP’s accuracy is limited—underperforms conversion pipeline on primary edges and yields very low remote-edge F-scores.
- Weakness: model choice (greedy averaged perceptron, hand-engineered features) underutilizes modern neural methods; lacks beam/global training to mitigate local errors.
- Weakness: acyclicity and well-formedness constraints are underspecified; unclear how cycles via remote edges are prevented during decoding.
- Weakness: conversion and evaluation details are incompletely described (Label(·) rules, head-priority order, and yield-based metric), leaving important impacts unexplored.
- Weakness: comparisons omit relevant baselines (SemEval SDP semantic dependency parsers, graph-based/neural DAG models) and task simplifications (removal of linkage/inter-sentential edges) are not quantified.
- Question: How is acyclicity guaranteed during BSP decoding—are cycle checks performed or structural constraints enforced?
- Question: What is the label priority order and full specification of Label(·) used in conversions, and how sensitive are results to it?
- Question: What proportion and phenomenon distribution of UCCA edges are remote, and can you provide an error analysis for remote-edge types?
- Question: How often is SWAP used and what is the rate of discontinuous units; do discontinuity features help in English and other languages like German?
- Question: Did you try beam search or neuralized features for BSP, and can you provide oracle/upper-bound analyses to estimate potential gains?
- Question: Can you report statistical significance and per-label F-scores to show where the parser struggles (P/A/C/R/N)?
- Overall: valuable and timely tooling and problem framing for UCCA/BSS, but the direct parser needs stronger modeling, clearer structural constraints, and deeper error analyses to be competitive.
