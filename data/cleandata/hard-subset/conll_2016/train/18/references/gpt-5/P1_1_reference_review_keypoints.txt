- Paper tackles UCCA parsing supporting reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities via conversion-based methods and a new transition-based parser (BSP).

- Contribution 1: BSP — a transition system with NODE, SWAP, LEFT/RIGHT-EDGE and LEFT/RIGHT-REMOTE, dynamic oracle, and training procedure to build UCCA-style DAGs.

- Contribution 2: Conversion protocols between UCCA and constituency/dependency formalisms to leverage existing parsers; strong dependency + reconstruction yields best primary-edge F1.

- Contribution 3: Labeled yield-based evaluation for DAGs (reduces to PARSEVAL on trees) and empirical baselines on in-domain and out-of-domain UCCA.

- Strength: Well-motivated problem framing showing necessity of handling all three structural properties and choice of UCCA as testbed.

- Strength: Thoughtful BSP design encoding primary-in-degree constraint, useful transitions, a dynamic oracle, and an effective ratio feature.

- Strength: Empirical benchmark and insights, notably that classifier strength (neural dep. parser) can outperform richer transition sets for primary edges.

- Strength: Transparent evaluation separating primary vs remote edges, reporting upper bounds for lossy conversions.

- Strength: Reproducibility intent with hyperparameters and promised code release.

- Weakness: BSP lags conversion baseline on primary edges (59.0 vs 69.2 F1) and has very low remote-edge F1 (~13), weakening immediate practical impact.

- Weakness: Conversion is lossy (drops non-terminals/reentrancies) and uses a simple Label heuristic, making comparisons partially apples-to-oranges and possibly biased.

- Weakness: Sparse analysis of remote edges — no breakdown by phenomenon or error diagnosis to explain low performance.

- Weakness: No formal proof or sketch of soundness/completeness for the transition system relative to the targeted UCCA graph class.

- Weakness: Experimental imbalance — BSP uses greedy sparse features while baselines use beam search/neural models; lacking beam/neural BSP ablation or stronger DAG-parsing baselines.

- Question: Can the authors provide a proof sketch/argument for BSP’s soundness and completeness, and any excluded UCCA structures?

- Question: What is the prevalence and type distribution of remote edges in the datasets, and how does BSP perform by remote-edge type?

- Question: How sensitive is BSP to beam size and neural scoring (e.g., BiLSTM features); were beam search or neural classifiers tried?

- Question: In dependency conversion, how often does the Label heuristic choose non-Center categories and how much could learned reconstruction improve results?

- Question: How much of BSP’s primary-edge error is due to discontinuities and does IMPORTANCE=2 for SWAP materially affect performance?

- Additional: Fix reference metadata typos, add qualitative/error analysis (coordination, PPs, MWEs), report parsing speed.

- Additional: Clarify impact of removing linkage nodes on sentence-level graph connectivity.
