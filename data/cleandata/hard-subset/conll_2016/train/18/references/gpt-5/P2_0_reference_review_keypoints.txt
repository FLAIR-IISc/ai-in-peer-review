- Originality: First transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities for UCCA; conversion protocols are less novel but useful.
- Soundness: Transition system and training are specified, but dynamic oracle optimality and label-priority selection lack full proofs/details.
- Significance: Moderate–high — practical parser and conversions can enable downstream work; direct parser uniquely predicts remote edges, albeit modestly.
- Empirical evaluation: Adequate (in-domain and OOD corpora, baselines, upper bounds, ablations) but missing deeper error analysis and stronger decoding/classification experiments.
- Clarity: Generally clear with concrete algorithms and features; some implementation details deferred to supplementary material.
- Reproducibility: Good — uses public corpora, promises to release code/converters, and employs standard baselines.
- Contribution 1: A transition-based Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP) for UCCA supporting remotes, non-terminals, and discontinuities, with features, a node-creation regularizer, and dynamic-oracle training.
- Contribution 2: Conversion pipelines between UCCA DAGs and discontinuous constituency/dependency formats, including inverse conversions and quantified information-loss upper bounds.
- Contribution 3: Empirical study on English UCCA corpora with a labeled-yield metric; conversion+dependency parsing achieves 69.2% primary F while the direct parser achieves comparable primary F and recovers some remotes.
- Strength: First end-to-end UCCA parser with full structural coverage, filling a gap for grounded semantic parsing research.
- Strength: Provides informative upper bounds via conversion pipelines (e.g., 88.4% primary F for dependency conversion), clarifying where information is lost.
- Strength: Demonstrates classifier choice matters — stack-LSTM on converted data substantially outperforms Malt variants.
- Strength: Shows OOD robustness and useful feature ablations (e.g., ratio feature improves recall).
- Strength: Practical evaluation design — labeled-yield metric and separate reporting for primary vs. remote edges.
- Weakness: BSP underperforms the best conversion baseline on primary edges (~59–60% vs. 69.2%) and has low remote edge F (~13%).
- Weakness: Modeling choices are dated (linear perceptron, hand-engineered features) and decoding is greedy; no neural/local classifier or beam search explored.
- Weakness: Key algorithmic components (dynamic oracle, label-priority order, Label(u) heuristic) are insufficiently detailed to assess correctness/impact.
- Weakness: Limited analysis and diagnostics — no breakdown by construction types, transition ablations, learning curves, or significance testing.
- Weakness: Scope limited to English and a single layer; excludes implicit units/linkage and lacks cross-linguistic evidence.
- Question: How many remote edges exist in the datasets, and what are precision/recall for remote parents versus a baseline that predicts none?
- Question: Clarify the dynamic oracle’s loss function, reachability guarantees for gold graphs after arbitrary mistakes, and any empirical oracle accuracy.
- Question: How is the label-priority ordering for head selection determined and how sensitive are conversion results to this ordering?
- Question: Were beam search or neural local classifiers tried for BSP (even small-beam or MLP), and are there preliminary results?
- Question: Provide statistics on discontinuities (percentage requiring SWAP) and the observed effect or ablation results for SWAP.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable first full-coverage UCCA parser and conversion pipelines, but needs stronger algorithmic detail and more comprehensive experiments.
