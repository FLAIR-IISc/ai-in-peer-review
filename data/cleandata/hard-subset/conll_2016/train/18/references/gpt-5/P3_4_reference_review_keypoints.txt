- Addresses UCCA semantic parsing with two strategies: lossy conversion to trees/dependencies + existing parsers, and a novel transition-based parser (BSP) supporting non-terminals, discontinuities, and multiple parents.

- Contribution: a transition system and dynamic oracle for UCCA/BSS that natively handles non-terminals, discontinuities, and remote edges.

- Contribution: a careful, reproducible conversion-based evaluation (with upper bounds) situating UCCA parsing within existing syntactic/semantic parsers.

- Contribution: empirical analyses (feature ablations, in-/out-of-domain results) and public release of converters and parsers.

- Strength: tackles a semantically rich formalism (UCCA) where standard dependency/SRL schemes struggle, addressing coordination, discontinuities, and shared arguments.

- Strength: principled transition inventory (NODE, SWAP, primary vs. remote edges) and a thoughtfully constructed dynamic oracle appropriate for greedy parsing.

- Strength: transparent conversion methodology with explicit head-selection heuristics and upper-bound reconstruction estimates, making comparisons interpretable.

- Strength: broad empirical evaluation (in-/out-of-domain, primary vs. remote separation, multiple baselines, feature ablations) showing cross-domain robustness.

- Strength: practical insights—Stack-LSTM conversion baseline outperforms Malt suggesting BSP could benefit from a stronger classifier; ratio feature helps non-terminal regularization.

- Weakness: BSP lags conversion baseline (primary F1 ~59–60 vs. 69.2) and predicts remotes poorly (F1 ~13), limiting practical impact given remotes’ centrality.

- Weakness: baseline comparability concerns—conversion loses non-terminals/remotes and inverse reconstruction is simplistic, so comparisons are skewed despite reported upper bounds.

- Weakness: modeling choices are dated/limited (greedy averaged perceptron, no beam or neural features), making it hard to assess the transition system’s full potential.

- Weakness: evaluation lacks deeper linguistic/structural breakdowns, error analysis, significance testing, and qualitative discussion of remote-edge errors.

- Weakness: key implementation details (edge-label priority, Label(·) mapping, cycle prevention, POS source) are deferred or omitted, affecting reproducibility.

- Question: provide exact label priority order for h(u) and full Label(·) mapping; how sensitive are dependency-conversion results to these choices?

- Question: are POS tags gold or predicted, are pre-trained embeddings used, and what are remotes/discontinuities statistics (e.g., % sentences with remotes, avg discontinuity length)?

- Question: how are acyclicity and UCCA well-formedness enforced during decoding—can the transition system generate invalid graphs?

- Question: did you try beam search, cost-augmented training, or neural feature representations in BSP, and what effects did they have on primary/remote edges?

- Question: which edge types dominate errors (Center, Participant, Relator, Linker), and how much of the primary F1 gap vs. Stack-LSTM is due to label vs. span/yield errors?

- Question: what proportion of remotes link Scenes vs. intra-Scene sharing, and did remote-specific features (e.g., s0R) trade precision for recall in ablations?

- Comment: report speed, memory, and statistical significance to contextualize trade-offs.

- Comment: consider stronger tree-to-graph reconstruction (learned attachment heights or latent non-terminals) or joint decoding to sharpen baselines.

- Comment: add per-phenomenon evaluation (coordination, MWEs, Scene linking) to show where BSP’s structural strengths help or fail.
