- Targets broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS) using UCCA, addressing reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Proposes two approaches: conversion-based parsing (UCCA ↔ constituency/dependency) and a new transition-based parser (BSP) that directly builds UCCA DAGs.
- Introduces explicit conversion algorithms with upper-bound analyses, a transition inventory (including remote-edge and SWAP), a dynamic oracle, and a novel global “ratio” feature.
- Experiments on in-domain (UCCA Wiki) and out-of-domain (20K Leagues) data; best primary-edge F1 is conversion + stack-LSTM dependency parser (69.2%).
- BSP achieves comparable primary-edge F1 to some baselines (Malt/UPARSE) but lower than the best and recovers few remote edges (~13% F1); out-of-domain trends similar.
- Contribution: first end-to-end parser that natively supports reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities for a grounded semantic scheme (UCCA).
- Contribution: well-specified, reproducible conversion procedures with informative upper bounds enabling strong conversion-based baselines.
- Contribution: empirical study across in- and out-of-domain data with feature ablation and comparisons across parser families.
- Strength: clear problem formulation and strong justification for using UCCA over bi-lexical dependencies.
- Strength: transition system and dynamic oracle are thoughtfully adapted and constitute a meaningful technical advance.
- Strength: solid conversion baselines and transparent reporting of information loss (e.g., 100% primary upper bound for constituency, 88.4% for dependency).
- Strength: competent experimental design with multiple parser families, domain evaluation, and transparent handling of punctuation and non-sentence edges.
- Strength: feature ablation provides useful insights; stack-LSTM’s strong gains suggest neural upgrades are promising for BSP.
- Weakness: BSP underperforms the strongest conversion-based neural baseline on the main metric (primary-edge F1 ~59–60 vs 69.2).
- Weakness: limited modeling choices for BSP (linear perceptron, greedy inference, no beam, no neural features) make its potential unclear.
- Weakness: sparse qualitative/quantitative analysis of remote edges and discontinuities despite their centrality to the task.
- Weakness: evaluation metric (yield+label matching) may conflate different DAG structures; statistical significance and learning curves are not reported.
- Weakness: missing or deferred comparisons (e.g., existing DAG parsers), some methodological details relegated to supplements, and minor clarity/formatting issues.
- Questions: why not add a beam or neural classifier to BSP, and would that yield similar improvements as seen for stack-LSTM?
- Questions: can the authors provide detailed error analysis for remote edges/discontinuities, sensitivity of Label() heuristic, preprocessing/POS usage, and metric robustness?
