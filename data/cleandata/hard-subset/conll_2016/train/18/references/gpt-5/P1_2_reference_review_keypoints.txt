- Paper addresses broad-coverage semantic parsing for grounded structures allowing reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities, focusing on UCCA.
- Proposes two strategies: conversion-based parsing to constituency/dependency and a new transition-based BSP parser that natively builds UCCA DAGs.
- Reports in-domain and out-of-domain experiments: conversion with LSTM dependency parser achieves primary-edge F1 up to 69.2; BSP achieves 59.0/13.3 (primary/remote).
- Contribution: practical transition system (BSP) supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities, with a dynamic oracle and tailored features.
- Contribution: bidirectional conversion procedures between UCCA graphs and dependency/constituency trees enabling reuse of existing parsers and providing upper bounds.
- Contribution: first empirical benchmark for UCCA parsing with in- and out-of-domain results and analyses showing strong local classifiers improve conversion performance.
- Strength: well-motivated problem and appropriate use of UCCA to handle headless constructs and reentrancy.
- Strength: clear, implementable transition system combining ideas from discontinuous constituency and DAG dependency parsing, with sensible engineering choices (dynamic oracle, ratio feature).
- Strength: principled conversion baselines and explicit upper bounds clarify information loss and parser potential.
- Strength: solid experimental setup with cross-domain evaluation and appropriate DAG metrics, showing moderate robustness OOD.
- Strength: empirical insight that a strong LSTM classifier substantially improves conversion-based primary-edge parsing.
- Weakness: low remote-edge performance and BSP primary-edge lag (~10 F1) behind LSTM conversion, leaving preferrability of direct approach unclear.
- Weakness: novelty is mainly compositional—many components have precedents and the new ratio feature is minor.
- Weakness: baseline comparability issues—BSP uses a greedy perceptron while the best baseline uses neural LSTM, confounding approach vs. classifier effects.
- Weakness: missing qualitative/error analyses by linguistic phenomena (coordination, MWEs, discontinuities) and unclear contribution of REMOTE transitions.
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps in conversion details (label-priority ordering, Label(u) function) which are deferred to supplements despite their importance.
- Question: what are the main sources of remote-edge errors—data sparsity, features, or search—and statistics on oracle vs. chosen REMOTE/EDGE transitions?
- Question: how sensitive are dependency conversion results to label-priority order and the Label(u) heuristic; were alternatives tried?
- Question: were beam search or neural classifiers tried for BSP, or can authors estimate potential gains based on LSTM conversion improvements?
- Question: how does the system ensure DAG acyclicity and constrain overgeneration of remote edges during parsing?
- Question: can authors provide performance breakdowns by UCCA phenomena (coordination, MWEs, phrasal verbs, PP attachments, discontinuities) and by unit size/height?
- Additional comment: report speed/memory and average transitions per sentence to assess practicality.
- Additional comment: small-scale experiments on another language would strengthen cross-linguistic generality.
- Additional comment: include exact commit/version identifiers for code/data release to ease replication.
- Overall: valuable, integrative work establishing a UCCA benchmark and promising neural-scoring avenues; leans toward acceptance pending clarified conversion details and finer-grained analyses.
