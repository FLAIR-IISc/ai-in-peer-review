- Contributions: BSP transition-based DAG constituency parser (NODE, REMOTE-EDGE, SWAP) with dynamic-oracle training and a “ratio” feature.  
- Contributions: Conversion and inverse procedures between UCCA DAGs and discontinuous constituency / dependency trees to leverage existing parsers and establish upper bounds.  
- Contributions: Empirical evaluation on English UCCA (in-domain and OOD) with a DAG-compatible yield-based labeled metric and feature ablations.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem framing showing the need for multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities and positioning UCCA as an appropriate testbed.  
- Strength: Technically sound, well-specified transition system extending prior discontinuous/DAG parsing work with reasonable constraints.  
- Strength: Explicit conversion algorithms and quantified upper bounds (e.g., 100 constituency, 88.4 dependency) that make conversion-based comparisons interpretable.  
- Strength: Solid empirical setup comparing direct and conversion-based approaches, including relevant baselines and separate primary vs remote edge reporting.  
- Strength: Carefully chosen feature set with useful ablations demonstrating the impact of UCCA-specific and “ratio” features.

- Weakness: BSP lags strong LSTM conversion baseline on primary F1 (~59.0 vs 69.2) and has very low remote-edge F1 (~13), questioning practical utility; greedy linear inference likely a bottleneck.  
- Weakness: Dynamic oracle is underspecified—no proof sketch or completeness guarantees for DAG+SWAP+REMOTE setting and no empirical ablation vs static oracle.  
- Weakness: Evaluation lacks per-phenomenon breakdowns (coordination, MWEs, PP attachment, phrasal verbs), per-label scores, error analysis, and statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: Inverse conversion relies on heuristic high-attachment and Label() rules (often Center); alternative learned or stronger reconstructions are not explored and may bias comparisons.  
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited to English with linkage/implicit/inter-sentential edges removed, reducing demonstrated cross-linguistic and full-UCCA coverage.

- Q: Please clarify the dynamic oracle’s optimality/completeness conditions and how it guarantees gold-reachability with REMOTE edges and SWAP when multiple parents must be created in different orders.  
- Q: How is the edge-label priority for head selection chosen, what is the exact Label(u) rule set (Algorithm 2), and how sensitive are results to these choices (ablation/variation)?  
- Q: Can you provide an error breakdown: proportion of errors due to missing remote edges vs wrong primary structure vs incorrect discontinuity handling, plus per-label or phenomenon-based F1s?  
- Q: What fraction of edges are remote and how often are they long-distance? Have you tried cost-sensitive training, curriculum learning, or other strategies to improve remote-edge recall?

- Additional: Suggest reporting unlabeled scores, per-length breakdowns, and statistical significance for comparability.
