- Paper presents a transition-based parser (BSP) for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (UCCA) and evaluates conversion-based baselines.
- BSP handles UCCA complexities: multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, achieving ~70% labeled F-score in-domain.
- Contribution: first transition-based parser specifically for BSS/UCCA enabling broader use of the formalism.
- Contribution: empirical evaluation of converting UCCA to dependency and constituency trees, showing information loss from conversions.
- Contribution: new UCCA-specific features (notably a ‘ratio’ feature) to improve parsing performance.
- Strength: addresses the critical lack of a UCCA parser, facilitating adoption and application.
- Strength: methodically compares conversion baselines and a dedicated parser, offering a robust evaluation strategy.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with reproducible experimental setup and detailed transition/feature explanations.
- Strength: promising experimental results (~70% labeled F-score) demonstrating feasibility.
- Strength: authors will release code and converters, aiding reproducibility and further work.
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent neural semantic parsers (e.g., LSTM/Transformer-based AMR parsers).
- Weakness: transition system is complex (separate primary vs. remote transitions) without ablation evidence for necessity.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on feature engineering; lacks exploration of learned features via neural models.
- Weakness: evaluation metrics are relatively simple; more nuanced metrics (AMR-inspired) could help.
- Weakness: notable out-of-domain performance drop, especially on remote edges, indicating domain sensitivity.
- Question: Have neural architectures (LSTMs/Transformers) or learned features been explored or why were they not pursued?
- Question: Can you provide an ablation for LEFTREMOTEX/RIGHTREMOTEX to show their impact on performance?
- Question: Can you analyze remote edge errors in out-of-domain data and propose robustness improvements?
- Question: How do UCCA and AMR differ and can state-of-the-art AMR techniques be adapted to UCCA?
- Additional comments: include supplementary material (Label definition) and discuss parser computational complexity.
- Overall: strong paper with minor revisions recommended to address comparisons, neural approaches, and ablations.
