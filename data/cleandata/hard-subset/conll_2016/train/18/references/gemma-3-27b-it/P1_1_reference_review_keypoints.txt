- Paper proposes BSP, a transition-based parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (UCCA), plus conversion-based baselines, achieving promising results (~62–70% labeled F-score) on English UCCA.
- Claimed contributions: first parser explicitly for BSS (BSP), exploration of conversion-based parsing, and concrete UCCA parsing experiments.
- Reviewer assessment: BSP is the primary novel contribution; the empirical UCCA evaluation is a valuable benchmark; conversion-analysis offers useful insights.
- Strength: tackles an important gap by handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Strength: technically well-motivated transition-based approach with reasonable extensions for BSS properties.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including in-domain and out-of-domain experiments and multiple baselines.
- Strength: clear presentation and effective illustrative figures.
- Strength: authors plan to release converters and parsers publicly, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: performance gains over conversion-based methods are limited (though remote edge prediction improves).
- Weakness: heavy reliance on hand-crafted features limits generalization and scalability.
- Weakness: lacks detailed qualitative error analysis to pinpoint failure modes.
- Weakness: proposed evaluation metric is coarse-grained and may penalize near-misses.
- Question: which ablated features were most impactful or redundant in performance?
- Question: have authors tried integrating LSTM/neural representations into BSP, and what were the challenges?
- Question: request for qualitative error analysis with concrete failure examples and difficult UCCA structures/labels.
- Question: how does BSP scale computationally with sentence length and very long inputs?
- Additional comment: include discussion of related work on other non-projective dependency formalisms beyond AMR.
- Additional comment: clarify rationale or tuning for MINUPDATE=5.
- Additional comment: provide the supplementary material describing the Label function.
- Overall: solid, valuable contribution that merits acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
