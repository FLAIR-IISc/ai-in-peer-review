## Peer Review: Broad-Coverage Semantic Parsing: A Transition-Based Approach

**Summary:**

This paper presents a transition-based parser (BSP) for Universal Cognitive Conceptual Annotation (UCCA), a semantic representation aiming for broad coverage through support for multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors acknowledge the lack of existing parsers for UCCA and address this gap by first exploring conversion-based parsing from UCCA to constituency and dependency trees. Subsequently, they introduce BSP, extending existing transition-based parsers with new transitions and features tailored for UCCA's unique characteristics. The paper reports experimental results on English UCCA data, achieving around 70% labeled F-score.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **First Transition-Based Parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS):** The primary contribution is the development and evaluation of BSP, the first parser capable of directly parsing UCCA structures, which inherently embody the complexities of BSS. This tackles a significant gap in the field.
2. **Empirical Exploration of UCCA Parsing:** The paper provides a valuable empirical baseline for UCCA parsing, offering insights into the challenges and potential of applying transition-based methods to this complex annotation scheme. The conversion-based experiments, while not novel in concept, establish a comparative benchmark.
3. **Identification of Key Features for BSS Parsing:** The authors investigate feature engineering for BSS parsing, specifically highlighting the importance of ratio features, which could potentially be beneficial for other similar tasks.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addressing a Critical Gap:** The lack of a dedicated parser for UCCA, a potentially powerful semantic representation, is a significant problem this work directly addresses. Creating a baseline parser is a crucial first step towards wider adoption and application of UCCA.
2. **Sound Methodological Approach:** The authors adopt a sensible approach by first exploring simpler, conversion-based methods to assess the viability of existing technology. This provides a baseline for comparison with the more complex BSP.  The extension of established transition-based parsing techniques is a logical starting point.
3. **Clear Presentation & Comprehensive Experiments:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the UCCA scheme, the proposed transitions, and the feature set. The experiments are well-defined, including both in-domain and out-of-domain evaluation, providing a more robust assessment of the parser’s performance.
4. **Reproducibility:** The commitment to making code publicly available is a strong positive point, fostering reproducibility and further research.
5. **Strong Baseline Performance:** Achieving nearly 70% labeled F-score is a good starting point for a novel parser on a complex representation, demonstrating the potential of the transition-based approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Parsing Technique:** While the adaptation of transition-based parsing to UCCA is valuable, the underlying parsing *technique* itself is not entirely novel. The core innovations lie in the specific transitions and features designed for UCCA, rather than a fundamentally new parsing paradigm.
2. **Limited Ablation Study Depth:** The ablation study (Table 3) is a good start, but could be more in-depth. It would be valuable to understand the *interaction* between feature sets and the relative importance of individual features more precisely. For example, how much does removing "disco" features *specifically* impact performance on discontinuous structures?
3. **Evaluation Metric Limitations:** The proposed evaluation metric, while reasonable given the lack of standard metrics for BSS, is relatively simplistic. It focuses solely on edge labels and doesn't fully capture the structural quality of the parsed graph. A more nuanced evaluation scheme, potentially incorporating structural similarity measures, would be desirable.
4. **Lack of Comparison with Stronger Baselines:** While the conversion-based approaches provide a baseline, comparing BSP against a more sophisticated AMR parser (even without direct conversion) could strengthen the analysis. This would provide a clearer understanding of where BSP excels and where it falls short relative to other semantic parsing approaches.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the specific challenges encountered when adapting existing transition-based parsing techniques to handle discontinuous units in UCCA? Specifically, what modifications to the stack management or transition selection were most crucial?
2.  The paper mentions that the ratio feature improves recall.  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of *why* this feature is effective? What aspect of the UCCA structure is it capturing?
3.  Are there plans to explore beam search during inference to potentially improve the parser's performance? If not, could you explain the rationale behind using greedy search?
4.  What are the biggest challenges to scaling up BSP to larger corpora and more complex languages?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The abbreviations used are generally clear, but a table listing them would be helpful.
*   The reference list is extensive.
