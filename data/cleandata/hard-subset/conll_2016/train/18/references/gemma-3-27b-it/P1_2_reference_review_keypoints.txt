- Paper proposes BSP, a transition-based parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (UCCA), with conversion strategies from dependency/constituency and ~70% labeled F-score including out-of-domain tests.
- Claimed contributions: first dedicated BSS parser; novel transition system and UCCA-specific features; conversion-based baseline and analysis.
- Reviewer view: primary contribution is a parser capable of BSS; transition-based extensions are sensible but incremental; conversion experiments effectively show information loss and justify a dedicated parser.
- Strength: addresses a clear gap in parsing non-terminal, discontinuous, multi-parent structures like UCCA.
- Strength: solid experimental setup—appropriate datasets (Wiki, 20K Leagues), labeled F-score metric, and out-of-domain evaluation.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 1).
- Strength: commits to releasing code, which benefits the community.
- Strength: conversion experiments provide insightful analysis of information loss from simpler formalisms.
- Weakness: BSP’s gains over conversion-based methods are limited—70% F-score isn’t a dramatic improvement given system complexity.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to stronger or state-of-the-art semantic parsing baselines (e.g., LSTM-based transition parsers trained on UCCA).
- Weakness: heavy reliance on hand-crafted features and head-word identification, which seems at odds with BSS goals of avoiding head-selection.
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis identifying where the parser fails (specific structures or edge types).
- Weakness: remote edge prediction performance remains low and under-analyzed.
- Question: Have you tried an LSTM-based transition parser directly on BSS/UCCA, and if not, why?
- Question: Can you provide a detailed error analysis highlighting recurring failure cases or troublesome UCCA structures?
- Question: What is the intuition for why the ratio feature improves recall?
- Question: Why include head-word features despite BSS goals, and were alternative feature representations considered?
- Question: How sensitive is BSP performance to the MINUPDATE parameter—did you explore more values?
- Additional comment: include a table summarizing counts of different UCCA edge labels to convey task complexity.
- Additional comment: report BSP’s computational cost compared to conversion-based methods.
