- Converts UCCA to constituency/dependency trees as baseline comparisons before proposing BSP.
- Contribution: first transition-based parser directly handling UCCA’s multi-parent, non-terminal, and discontinuous structures.
- Contribution: provides empirical baselines and comparative experiments for UCCA parsing, including conversion-based methods.
- Contribution: investigates feature engineering for BSS parsing, highlighting the utility of ratio features.

- Strength: methodologically sound—uses conversion baselines and extends established transition-based techniques.
- Strength: clearly presented with well-defined in-domain and out-of-domain experiments.
- Strength: commits to code release, supporting reproducibility.
- Strength: achieves strong baseline performance (~70% labeled F-score) on a complex representation.
