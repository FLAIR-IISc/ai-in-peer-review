- Presents BSP, the first transition-based parser for UCCA, and compares direct parsing with conversion-based approaches.
- Achieves competitive results (~70% labeled F-score in-domain) and reasonable out-of-domain performance.
- First transition-based parser for broad-coverage semantic structures, providing a baseline for UCCA parsing.
- Empirically evaluates conversion-based methods, showing information loss and limits of relying on simpler parsers.
- Identifies important feature sets (including a novel ratio feature) via ablation, guiding future model design.
- Tackles a significant gap in semantic parsing by supporting multi-parent, non-terminal, and discontinuous structures.
- Uses a solid experimental setup with in-domain and out-of-domain evaluation and sensible baselines (MaltParser, UPARSE, LSTM).
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and Figure 1 effectively illustrates BSP characteristics.
- Commits to releasing code, supporting reproducibility and follow-up work.
- Ablation study (Table 3) provides valuable insights into feature importance.
- Performance gains over conversion-based baselines are limited, raising questions about advantages of direct parsing.
- Lacks detailed error analysis and qualitative examples to identify specific failure modes (e.g., discontinuities, coordination).
- Conversion to dependency trees is overly simplistic; more sophisticated conversion could improve downstream results.
- Transition system exploration is limited; alternative operations tailored to UCCA complexities were not deeply investigated.
- Asks authors for detailed error analysis, clarification on LSTM vs BSP feature/training differences, and whether conversion/transition alternatives were tried.
- Notes references are comprehensive, notation mostly clear, and suggests adding visual predicted vs gold parses to illustrate errors.
- Overall: valuable baseline contribution with modest gains; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address error analysis and conversion/transition discussions.
