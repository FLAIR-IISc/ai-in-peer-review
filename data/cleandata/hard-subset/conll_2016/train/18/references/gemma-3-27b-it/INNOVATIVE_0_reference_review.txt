This paper introduces a transition-based approach to parsing Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS), specifically targeting the UCCA annotation scheme. The core contribution is the development of BSP, a novel parser designed to handle the complexities of BSS – multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units – which existing parsers struggle with. A secondary contribution lies in exploring conversion-based methods as a baseline and diagnostic tool, bridging the gap between established parsing techniques and the novel BSS framework. Finally, the release of the code and datasets will likely benefit the community.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of the Problem:** The attempt to parse UCCA structures – which inherently require handling complexities absent in standard dependency or constituency parsing – is genuinely novel. Existing semantic parsing approaches either lack the breadth of coverage or avoid grounding altogether. This work directly tackles a significant gap in the field.
2. **Bold Approach:** Directly applying a transition-based framework to BSS is a sensible and ambitious choice. Transition-based parsing has proven effective in related tasks, and extending it to handle these complexities demonstrates a clear and well-motivated research direction. The authors aren’t incrementally improving; they’re building something new.
3. **Comprehensive Feature Set:** The feature engineering appears thorough, drawing from successful features in related parsing tasks and incorporating novel features (like the ‘ratio’ feature) specifically tailored to UCCA.  The inclusion of features aimed at capturing remote dependencies is particularly commendable.
4. **Solid Experimental Setup:**  The use of both in-domain and out-of-domain datasets provides a reasonable assessment of the parser’s generalizability.  The exploration of conversion-based methods offers a useful point of comparison, illuminating the information loss inherent in attempting to map BSS to simpler representations.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Performance Gains:** While the parser achieves a respectable 62.4% F-score, it doesn't drastically outperform the conversion-based baselines, particularly considering the complexity of the task and the extensive feature engineering. The comparatively small gains suggest that the core transition system may not be fully exploiting the richness of the UCCA representation.
2. **Evaluation Metric Concerns:** The proposed evaluation metric, while understandable given the novelty of BSS, feels somewhat coarse-grained. Focusing solely on edge-level F-score overlooks potential errors in the structure and relationships within the graph, which are crucial aspects of semantic parsing.  A more holistic evaluation might be needed.
3. **Lack of Ablation Study Detail:** While a feature ablation study *is* presented, the results aren’t discussed in detail. It's unclear *why* certain features contribute more than others.  A deeper analysis of feature importance would provide valuable insights into the parser's behavior and guide future development.
4. **Limited Comparative Analysis:** The comparison to other parsers is somewhat limited. While the LSTM parser results are impressive, a more direct comparison to other state-of-the-art semantic parsers (even those operating on different representations) would help contextualize the results.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you elaborate on the design choices behind the transition system? Specifically, why were these transitions selected over others, and what attempts were made to explore alternative transition sets?
2.  What is the reasoning behind the weighting of the 'SWAP' transition during training? Does this indicate a specific weakness in the model's ability to handle discontinuities?
3.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the errors made by BSP? Are there specific types of UCCA structures that consistently lead to parsing failures?
4.  Are you planning to explore alternative evaluation metrics that capture the structural properties of BSS more comprehensively?



**Additional Comments:**

The paper’s writing is clear and well-organized. The inclusion of figures illustrating the UCCA structures is particularly helpful. The authors' commitment to releasing the code and datasets is greatly appreciated.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the modest performance gains, this paper presents a genuinely novel and ambitious attempt to tackle a challenging problem in semantic parsing. The proposed approach is well-motivated, and the experimental setup is reasonable. The limitations related to performance and evaluation don't detract significantly from the overall contribution, which lies primarily in establishing a baseline and demonstrating the feasibility of a transition-based approach to BSS parsing.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the questions raised above. The boldness of the approach and the potential for future work outweigh the current limitations.
