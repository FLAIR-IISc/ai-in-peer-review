- Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA, addressing multi-parent, non-terminal, and discontinuous units; achieves ~70% labeled F-score in-domain and reasonable OOD performance.  
- First dedicated parser for UCCA, filling a gap in practical tools for this semantic scheme.  
- Demonstrates transition-based parsing can be adapted to handle UCCA’s structural complexities (discontinuities, reentrancy).  
- Provides empirical comparison between conversion-based (constituency/dependency) and direct parsing approaches, establishing baselines for UCCA parsing.  
- Addresses an important problem and motivates broad-coverage semantic structures clearly.  
- Methodology is sound: combines conversion baselines with a novel, purpose-built parser and justifies the transition-based choice.  
- Experimental setup is solid, uses standard metrics, includes out-of-domain evaluation, and reports promising results.  
- Paper is clear and well-written, with helpful figures explaining UCCA and the approach.  
- Commits to releasing code, improving reproducibility and impact.  
- BSP shows limited improvement over constituency-conversion baselines, suggesting conversion retains much useful structure or BSP underutilizes UCCA richness.  
- Feature set is basic and lacks deeper UCCA-specific engineering beyond a noted "ratio" feature.  
- Use of a linear classifier is a limitation; LSTM integration could likely improve performance given conversion-based LSTM success.  
- Evaluation metric may not fully capture semantic parsing complexity; a more nuanced error-aware metric is desirable.  
- Requests elaboration on feature selection and whether UCCA-specific features were tried and discarded.  
- Asks about challenges and feasibility of integrating an LSTM into BSP and whether obstacles are engineering or fundamental.  
- Requests a detailed error analysis to identify common BSP error types for future guidance.  
- Asks for assessment of information loss during conversion to constituency trees and whether it affects recall or precision more.  
- Inquires about BSP scalability with sentence length and UCCA graph complexity.  
- Suggests broader related-work discussion (beyond AMR/SDP) and a brief comparison of UCCA to FrameNet/PropBank.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses and questions; sees substantial potential for future work.
