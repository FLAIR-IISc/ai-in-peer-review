- Introduces BSP, the first transition-based parser tailored for UCCA, handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Evaluates both direct parsing (BSP) and conversion-based methods, establishing baselines on English UCCA with ~70% labeled F-score.  
- Analyzes conversion strategies to dependency/constituency, highlighting information loss from simplification.  
- Addresses a gap by providing a practical parsing solution for the under-served UCCA formalism.  
- Uses a well-justified transition-based framework with sensible extensions for UCCA-specific phenomena.  
- Conducts comprehensive evaluation including in-domain/out-of-domain tests and feature ablation.  
- Clear presentation and commitment to release code/data, aiding reproducibility.  
- Conversion experiments thoughtfully demonstrate trade-offs and justify a dedicated parser.  
- BSP’s performance improvement over conversion baselines is modest, questioning the impact of UCCA-specific design choices.  
- Paper lacks a detailed error analysis to pinpoint common failure modes (e.g., remote edges, coordination).  
- Evaluation metric is coarse-grained and may not capture nuanced UCCA structure quality.  
- BSP uses a linear classifier; more powerful models (e.g., neural/LSTM) are not explored and could improve results.  
- Requests a detailed error breakdown and identification of crucial corrective features.  
- Asks whether LSTM layers were considered/integrated into BSP or why not, given LSTM success after conversion.  
- Inquires about sensitivity to the MINUPDATE parameter and its effects on performance/training time.  
- Questions whether the framework was tested on other UCCA-annotated languages/corpora and related challenges.  
- Suggests including visual examples of UCCA vs. converted trees to illustrate information loss.  
- Recommends expanding related work to include recent graph-based neural semantic parsers.  
- Requests clarification on whether the ratio feature is normalized (e.g., by corpus size).  
- Overall: valuable, technically sound contribution that establishes a foundation for UCCA parsing; recommend acceptance with minor revisions and public code/data release.
