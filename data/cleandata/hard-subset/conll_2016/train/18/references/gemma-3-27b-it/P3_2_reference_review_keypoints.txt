- Summary: Tackles UCCA broad-coverage semantic parsing, proposes a transition-based BSP and conversion-based baselines, achieving ~70% labeled F-score.

- Contribution 1: First transition-based parser for UCCA/BSS that handles multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuities.

- Contribution 2: Thorough exploration of converting UCCA to dependency and constituency trees, analyzing information loss and providing baselines.

- Contribution 3: Formalizes the key properties of Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuity) as an evaluation framework.

- Strength 1: Novel and well-motivated extension of transition-based parsing to UCCA’s structural complexities.

- Strength 2: Rigorous experimental setup with clear metrics and in-domain/out-of-domain evaluation and comparisons to conversion methods.

- Strength 3: Strong baseline results (~70% labeled F-score), showing BSP is competitive with conversion-applied parsers.

- Strength 4: Clear, comprehensive background and accessible exposition of UCCA and related work.

- Strength 5: Commitment to open-sourcing the code, aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Limited improvement over conversion-based methods; BSP does not dramatically recover conversion losses to fully justify its complexity.

- Weakness 2: Conservative feature engineering; lack of LSTM-based representations likely limits performance gains.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation relies on edge-level F-score which may miss structural and semantic errors; alternative metrics should be considered.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient detailed error analysis to identify systematic failure modes across UCCA categories or constructions.

- Weakness 5: Experiments use relatively small datasets, leaving scalability to larger corpora and complex phenomena unresolved.

- Question 1: How exactly would LSTMs be integrated into BSP—what features would they encode and how would they affect transitions?

- Question 2: Why does the 'ratio' feature substantially improve recall—what is the intuitive explanation?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide a detailed error analysis by UCCA categories or grammatical constructions?

- Question 4: What are the computational costs of BSP versus conversion-based methods, and is the added complexity justified?

- Question 5: What do performance differences between dependency and constituency conversions indicate about representational fit for UCCA?

- Additional comment 1: Include visual examples comparing BSP parses with gold-standard UCCA structures.

- Additional comment 2: Clarify which parts are novel contributions versus adaptations from existing parsing literature.

- Overall recommendation: Solid and valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
