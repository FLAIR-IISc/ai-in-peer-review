- Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA to handle multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities (BSS), achieving ~70% labeled F-score in-domain.
- Contribution: First transition-based parser for UCCA, establishing a baseline using a standard parsing paradigm.
- Contribution: Empirical comparison of conversion-based and direct parsing approaches for broad-coverage semantic structures.
- Contribution: Feature engineering and ablation study identifying important features for BSS parsing.
- Strength: Well-motivated task and clear explanation of UCCA advantages over other semantic representations.
- Strength: Solid experimental setup with standard metrics and both in-domain and out-of-domain evaluations.
- Strength: Thorough exploration of both conversion-based and direct parsing methods, enabling comparative insights.
- Strength: Detailed feature ablation (including the useful `ratio` feature) that highlights feature importance.
- Strength: Commitment to release code, aiding reproducibility and follow-up research.
- Weakness: BSP’s performance (62–63% F) only modestly improves over conversion methods, questioning the complexity trade-off.
- Weakness: Use of a linear classifier likely limits BSP’s performance; LSTM-based models show better results on conversion tasks.
- Weakness: Remote edge handling is simplistic with few dedicated features and low F-score.
- Weakness: Lacks in-depth error analysis to pinpoint parser failure modes (e.g., syntactic structures or label errors).
- Weakness: Limited comparison to other semantic parsing frameworks (e.g., AMR), reducing broader contextualization.
- Q: What were results when using more complex classifiers (e.g., LSTMs) for BSP, even preliminarily?
- Q: What additional or alternative features might improve remote edge prediction?
- Q: Can the authors provide examples of common parser errors and likely causes?
- Q: How does BSP performance vary with datasets that have different degrees of discontinuity?
- Additional: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and figures effectively illustrate UCCA and parsing steps.
- Additional: Citations are comprehensive and relevant.
- Overall: Valuable contribution that establishes a foundation for UCCA parsing; recommend acceptance with minor revisions, especially exploring stronger classifiers.
