## Review for "Broad-Coverage Semantic Parsing: A Transition-Based Approach"

**Summary:** This paper introduces a transition-based parser (BSP) for Universal Cognitive Conceptual Annotation (UCCA), a semantic representation scheme supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units – collectively termed Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS). The authors explore both conversion-based methods (to constituency and dependency trees) and a novel, direct transition-based parsing approach. They demonstrate the feasibility of BSS parsing, achieving around 70% labeled F-score on in-domain data.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **First Transition-Based Parser for UCCA:** This is the most significant contribution. The paper establishes a baseline for parsing UCCA structures with a well-motivated and standard parsing paradigm.
2. **Empirical Exploration of BSS Parsing:** The paper provides a solid empirical evaluation of both conversion-based and direct parsing approaches for BSS, providing valuable insights into the challenges and potential of this task.
3. **Identification of Key Features for BSS:**  The feature engineering process and ablation study highlight important features for capturing the complexities of BSS, which could be useful for future research in this area.

**Strengths:**

1. **Well-Motivated Task and Representation:** The paper convincingly argues for the need for BSS and provides a clear explanation of the UCCA scheme, highlighting its advantages over existing semantic representations. The rationale for addressing multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuity is strong.
2. **Solid Experimental Setup:** The authors employ a standard evaluation methodology, using clear metrics and a well-defined split of the UCCA corpus.  The inclusion of both in-domain and out-of-domain experiments strengthens the results.
3. **Thorough Exploration of Approaches:**  Investigating both conversion-based and direct parsing methods provides a comprehensive assessment of the feasibility of parsing BSS, allowing for a comparison of strengths and weaknesses.
4. **Detailed Feature Analysis:** The feature ablation study (Table 3) is well-executed and provides valuable insights into which features contribute most to parsing performance. The addition of the `ratio` feature is a nice touch and demonstrates thoughtful consideration.
5. **Code Release Commitment:** Promising to release the code is a substantial benefit for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Performance Gain over Conversion Methods:** While the direct parser (BSP) is a valuable contribution, its performance (around 62-63% F-score) isn't dramatically better than the conversion-based approaches, particularly when considering the complexity added. This raises questions about whether the gains justify the effort of developing a new parser.
2. **Suboptimal Classifier Choice:** The use of a linear classifier seems potentially limiting given the complexity of the task. The paper acknowledges this, noting the LSTM parser’s superior performance on conversion tasks, and this suggests a potential avenue for substantial improvement for BSP.  This is *the* biggest weakness.
3. **Simplistic Remote Edge Handling:** The treatment of remote edges feels somewhat tacked on, with only basic features dedicated to their prediction. The low F-score on remote edges suggests this aspect requires more attention.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** A deeper error analysis of the parser's mistakes would be highly beneficial. Understanding *where* the parser struggles could guide future improvements. (e.g., specific syntactic structures, edge label prediction errors).
5. **Limited Comparison to Other Semantic Parsing Approaches:** The comparison is largely limited to conversion baselines.  A brief discussion of how this approach compares to other, unrelated semantic parsing tasks (like AMR parsing, despite the differences) could provide useful context.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Classifier Exploration:**  What results did you obtain when experimenting with more complex classifiers (e.g., LSTMs) for BSP?  Even preliminary results would be very valuable.
2. **Remote Edge Features:**  Could you elaborate on potential improvements to the feature set for predicting remote edges?  Are there specific features that you believe would be particularly beneficial?
3. **Error Analysis:**  Can you provide some examples of the most common errors made by the parser, and discuss potential reasons for these errors?
4. **Impact of Discontinuity:**  How does the performance of BSP change when applied to datasets with varying degrees of discontinuity?



**Additional Comments:**

*  The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.
* The provided figures are helpful for understanding the UCCA scheme and the parsing process.
* The citations are comprehensive and relevant.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of semantic parsing. The development of the first transition-based parser for UCCA is a significant achievement, and the empirical evaluation provides useful insights into the challenges and potential of parsing broad-coverage semantic structures. While the performance gains over conversion methods are modest, the work lays a solid foundation for future research in this area. The paper is suitable for acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly exploring more powerful classifier options.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
