- Paper introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA, exploring conversion-based and direct parsing and reporting ~70% labeled F1 on English data.  
- Authors claim the first transition-based parser for broad-coverage semantic structures (UCCA) that handles multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Paper explores conversion-based parsing to dependency/constituency trees as baselines and feasibility study.  
- BSP proposes a novel transition system (LEFT-REMOTEX, RIGHT-REMOTEX) and UCCA-specific features.  
- Reviewer: main contribution is demonstrating feasibility of direct UCCA parsing with a transition-based approach.  
- Reviewer: provides a comprehensive comparison of conversion-based vs direct parsing strategies for BSS.  
- Reviewer: establishes a baseline with publicly released code for future UCCA parsing work.  
- Strength: addresses an important gap by targeting semantic representations beyond dependency/constituency parsing.  
- Strength: sound empirical methodology combining conversion-based and direct approaches for nuanced insights.  
- Strength: clear presentation and helpful figures explaining UCCA and the transition system.  
- Strength: shows reasonable robustness on out-of-domain data.  
- Strength: open-sourcing the code enhances reproducibility and follow-up research.  
- Weakness: novel remote transitions yield limited gains (remote edges ~15.3% F-score) and lack detailed failure analysis.  
- Weakness: feature engineering is conservative; richer features (embeddings, contextual signals) might improve performance.  
- Weakness: evaluation metric is simplistic and may not fully capture UCCA complexity; finer-grained metrics are needed.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to broader state-of-the-art semantic parsers (e.g., AMR/SDP) beyond conversion baselines.  
- Question: please provide detailed error analysis identifying common parsing errors and hard UCCA structures.  
- Question: were more sophisticated features (word embeddings, contextual features) tried and what were the results?  
- Question: elaborate on limitations of the chosen evaluation metric and potential alternative metrics.  
- Question: justify MINUPDATE=5 and IMPORTANCE=2 choices and report effects of varying them.  
- Question: why use greedy inference; would beam search help and is it computationally feasible?  
- Additional comment: discuss the relationship between UCCA and other semantic representations like AMR and SDP.  
- Additional comment: notation could be made more consistent.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address highlighted weaknesses.
