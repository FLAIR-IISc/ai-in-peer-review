- Paper introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA that handles multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities; compares direct parsing and conversion-based approaches and reports ~70% labeled F-score in-domain with reasonable out-of-domain performance.

- Contribution: first transition-based parser for UCCA/BSS, providing a concrete implementation and evaluation for these complex semantic structures.

- Contribution: empirical evaluation of conversion-based methods, showing information loss and providing useful baselines and error analysis.

- Contribution: introduction of UCCA-specific features (e.g., for remote edges and terminal-to-node ratio) to address UCCA parsing challenges.

- Strength: task novelty and importance — parsing UCCA/BSS fills a gap and advances expressive semantic representations.

- Strength: sound methodology — well-motivated transition-based approach and useful comparison between direct and conversion-based pipelines.

- Strength: clear presentation and thorough experiments, including in-domain/out-of-domain evaluation and feature ablations.

- Strength: strong baseline performance — nearly 70% labeled F-score is respectable for a first parser of this kind.

- Strength: resources released publicly (code and models), aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.

- Weakness: BSP’s improvement over conversion-based methods is limited, questioning the impact of introduced features and transitions.

- Weakness: evaluation metric (edge overlap) is crude and may not capture structural graph differences or overall coherence; more nuanced metrics suggested.

- Weakness: lack of detailed error analysis identifying specific UCCA categories or constructions where BSP fails.

- Weakness: reliance on feature engineering; suggests exploring end-to-end methods (e.g., pretrained LMs, GNNs) for larger gains.

- Question: what are the challenges and plans for integrating an LSTM-based classifier into the BSP framework?

- Question: will the authors explore more sophisticated graph-based evaluation metrics (e.g., graph edit distance)?

- Question: can the authors provide a detailed error analysis specifying common BSP errors and problematic UCCA categories?

- Question: is the current feature set a bottleneck given the strong LSTM performance on converted trees, and what new features would be prioritized?

- Additional comment: related work discussion is comprehensive and situates the paper well.

- Additional comment: explanation of the UCCA scheme is clear and well-motivated.

- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution (first transition-based UCCA parser); recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses.
