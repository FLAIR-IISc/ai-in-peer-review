- Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA that handles multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities, with ~70% labeled F-score and released code.
- Explores conversion-based parsing to constituency/dependency trees and a novel direct transition-based approach.
- First transition-based parser specifically targeting broad-coverage UCCA structures.
- Provides empirical analysis of conversion-based parsing effectiveness and its information loss.
- Establishes a baseline and experimental setup for future UCCA parsing research.
- Addresses an important gap in semantic parsing by supporting UCCA’s structural complexities.
- Investigates both conversion and direct methods, giving insights into limitations of existing parsers.
- Strong experimental evaluation including in-domain/out-of-domain testing and ablation studies.
- Public code release enhances reproducibility and potential impact.
- Paper is clear and well-written, with helpful UCCA diagrams.
- BSP shows only modest gains over conversion methods for primary edges, raising questions about added complexity.
- Remote edge prediction performance is weak and under-explored, suggesting the model struggles to learn them.
- Evaluation metrics (edge label precision/recall) may not fully capture overall graph quality or downstream utility.
- Heavy reliance on hand-engineered features; learned representations (e.g., neural nets) are not sufficiently explored.
- Requests clarification on planned LSTM-based classifier implementation and potential gains, and asks for preliminary results.
- Asks what makes remote edges hard to predict and whether specialized features/transitions were tried.
- Seeks discussion of evaluation metric limitations and alternative, more holistic metrics.
- Asks for intuition behind the helpfulness of the ratio feature.
- References and diagrams are well-curated and informative.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution and baseline for UCCA parsing; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
