This paper presents the first parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS), specifically targeting the UCCA annotation scheme. The authors propose a transition-based approach and explore both conversion-based parsing using existing constituency and dependency parsers and a novel, dedicated BSS parser (BSP). The main contributions are: 1) the first attempt at parsing UCCA structures with a dedicated parser; 2) a comprehensive experimental evaluation of conversion-based approaches, demonstrating the information loss inherent in simplifying BSS; and 3) the introduction of a transition system extended to handle multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, crucial for BSS parsing.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty and Importance of the Problem:** The paper tackles a genuinely novel and important problem – parsing semantic structures with characteristics that go beyond standard syntactic dependency or constituency frameworks. The UCCA scheme, and the need for a dedicated parser, are well-motivated and the challenges of representing multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities are clearly articulated. This addresses a gap in the current landscape of semantic parsing.

2. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors don’t solely rely on their novel parser. The thorough evaluation using conversion-based approaches provides a strong baseline and demonstrates the limitations of applying existing parsing technology to BSS. The results clearly highlight the information loss during conversion and justify the need for a dedicated parser.  The inclusion of both in-domain and out-of-domain experiments strengthens the evaluation and demonstrates some robustness.

3. **Well-Defined Transition System:** The extension of the transition-based parsing framework to accommodate the specific requirements of BSS (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities) is well-defined and logically presented. The new transitions (LEFT-EDGEX, RIGHT-EDGEX, LEFT-REMOTEX, RIGHT-REMOTEX) are clearly explained and appear appropriately designed to capture the complexities of UCCA structures. The feature set is also well-considered, building upon existing successful parsing frameworks while incorporating UCCA-specific features.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Performance Gap:** While the paper establishes a baseline and demonstrates the feasibility of BSS parsing, the performance achieved (around 60-70% F-score) is relatively modest. The comparison with the LSTM parser (73.2% F-score) obtained after conversion suggests that there's significant room for improvement in the proposed BSP model.  The paper acknowledges this, but a more in-depth analysis of error sources would be beneficial.

2. **Limited Analysis of Error Types:** The paper lacks a detailed error analysis. Understanding *where* the parser fails (e.g., in handling discontinuities, multiple parents, specific UCCA categories) would provide valuable insights for future research and improvements.  Simply stating that there is room for improvement is insufficient; concrete examples of failure cases are needed.

3. **Lack of Comparison with Stronger Baselines:** While conversion-based approaches are employed, the lack of comparison with a stronger, state-of-the-art AMR parser might be a limitation. Both UCCA and AMR aim to represent semantic meaning, and a direct comparison (even if challenging due to differences in representation) could offer valuable insights.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a detailed error analysis, categorizing the most frequent errors made by BSP?  Specifically, what types of UCCA structures (e.g., coordination, discontinuous units, specific relation types) does the parser struggle with the most?
2.  The LSTM parser, after conversion, outperforms BSP.  What steps have been taken (or are planned) to incorporate LSTM or similar neural network architectures into the BSP framework to potentially improve performance?
3.  The paper mentions that the ratio feature is a regularizer.  Could you elaborate on the rationale behind using this specific feature and provide some ablation results demonstrating its impact?
4. You mention that some feature choices were adopted from existing parsers. How did you select which features to transfer from other parsing frameworks and how did you determine the need for UCCA-specific features?

**Additional Comments:**

*   Figure 2 is visually cluttered and could be improved for readability.
*   The supplementary material mentioned in the text (regarding the definition of Label) should be included with the submission.
*   A brief discussion of the computational complexity of BSP would be beneficial.
