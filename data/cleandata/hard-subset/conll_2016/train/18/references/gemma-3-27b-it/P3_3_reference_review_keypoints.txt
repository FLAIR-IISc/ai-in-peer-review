- Summary: Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA/BSS, and shows competitive results (~70% labeled F-score) vs conversion methods.

- Contribution 1: First direct parser specifically designed for Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS)/UCCA, handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.

- Contribution 2: Empirical evaluation of direct BSS parsing and conversion-based baselines, including out-of-domain tests, establishing baselines for future work.

- Contribution 3: Extension of transition-based parsing with new transitions and features to support UCCA phenomena.

- Strength 1: Clear motivation and problem definition with helpful examples of UCCA structural properties.

- Strength 2: Solid experimental setup using standard dataset, clear metrics, comparisons to baselines, and out-of-domain evaluation.

- Strength 3: Well-reasoned transition system design with clear explanation of new transitions.

- Strength 4: Extensive, thoughtful feature engineering (including a novel "ratio" feature).

- Strength 5: Converters and parsers to be made publicly available.

- Weakness 1: Limited performance gains—BSP only modestly better than conversion methods and worse than the LSTM baseline.

- Weakness 2: Ablation study lacks granular analysis of individual feature contributions.

- Weakness 3: Conversion procedures are simplistic and may lose information, potentially underestimating conversion baselines.

- Weakness 4: Evaluation metric focuses on edges/labels and may miss important structural/semantic differences.

- Weakness 5: Missing detailed error analysis to identify common BSP failure modes.

- Question 1: Request details on the LSTM baseline architecture/training and how LSTM components could be integrated into BSP.

- Question 2: Why do remote edge transitions slightly decrease in-domain performance—data sparsity or other causes?

- Question 3: Have more sophisticated alignment or graph-transformation methods been considered to reduce conversion information loss?

- Question 4: Please provide qualitative examples of BSP successes and failures to illustrate error patterns.

- Question 5: Ask for evidence and elaboration that the ratio feature acts as a regularizer (e.g., learning curves).

- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive and relevant.

- Additional comment 3: UCCA figures effectively illustrate concepts.

- Overall: Solid, valuable contribution (first direct BSS parser); recommend acceptance pending deeper error analysis and further study of the LSTM baseline.
