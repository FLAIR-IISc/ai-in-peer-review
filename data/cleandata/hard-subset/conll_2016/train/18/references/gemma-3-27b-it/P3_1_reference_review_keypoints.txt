- Summary: Proposes a transition-based parser (BSP) for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (UCCA), compares conversion-based methods and evaluates in-domain and out-of-domain.

- Contribution 1: Presents the first dedicated parser for BSS that directly handles multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.

- Contribution 2: Systematically explores transition-based parsing techniques, introducing novel transitions and features for UCCA.

- Contribution 3: Provides empirical evaluation on UCCA with in-domain and out-of-domain experiments as a benchmark.

- Strength 1: Clearly motivates the task and explains UCCA properties and challenges.

- Strength 2: Uses a sound, well-justified transition-based methodology and compares conversion-based baselines.

- Strength 3: Introduces a comprehensive feature set, including a useful “ratio” regularization feature.

- Strength 4: Reports solid empirical results with competitive performance and important out-of-domain evaluation.

- Strength 5: Commits to releasing code, supporting reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: BSP shows substantially lower performance than a strong LSTM baseline on converted dependency trees.

- Weakness 2: Evaluation metric (edge-yield matching) may not adequately capture semantic correctness, especially edge labels/roles.

- Weakness 3: Ablation study is shallow; lacks systematic analysis of individual transitions and feature groups.

- Weakness 4: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art semantic parsers beyond conversion-based baselines (e.g., AMR systems).

- Question 1: Request for detailed error analysis identifying most challenging structures and common error types.

- Question 2: Ask about the impact of remote edge transitions on speed and memory and possible optimizations.

- Question 3: Inquire whether integrating LSTM layers into BSP was explored and with what results.

- Question 4: Ask if authors plan more sophisticated evaluation metrics that assess semantic roles/labels.

- Additional comment 1: Writing is clear and concise.

- Additional comment 2: Related work coverage is good.

- Additional comment 3: Figures effectively illustrate concepts and examples.

- Overall assessment: Valuable and novel contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses; recommendation: accept.
