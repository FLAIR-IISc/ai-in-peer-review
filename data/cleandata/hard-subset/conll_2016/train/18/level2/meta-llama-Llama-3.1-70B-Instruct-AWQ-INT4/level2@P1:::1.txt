**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing, which involves representing semantic structures with multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors propose two transition-based techniques for parsing such semantic structures: (1) converting them into related formalisms and using existing state-of-the-art parsers, and (2) constructing a parser that directly supports the full set of properties. They experiment with UCCA-annotated corpora and demonstrate the effectiveness of transition-based methods for the task.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of a novel parser**: The authors introduce a transition-based parser, called Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), which supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. **Conversion-based parsing approach**: The authors propose a conversion-based parsing approach to assess the ability of existing technology to address the task.
3. **Experimental results**: The authors present experimental results on UCCA-annotated corpora, demonstrating the effectiveness of transition-based methods for the task.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to semantic parsing**: The authors propose a novel approach to semantic parsing, which involves representing semantic structures with multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. **Effective use of transition-based methods**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of transition-based methods for the task, achieving high F-scores on primary edges.
3. **Robustness to domain variation**: The authors show that their approach is robust to domain variation, achieving similar results on in-domain and out-of-domain data.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation metrics**: The authors only evaluate their approach using labeled precision, recall, and F-score, which may not capture all aspects of the task.
2. **Comparison to existing parsers**: The authors only compare their approach to a limited set of existing parsers, which may not be representative of the state-of-the-art in semantic parsing.
3. **Lack of analysis of error types**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of error types, which could help to identify areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the conversion-based parsing approach, including the specific conversion procedures used and the trade-offs involved?
2. How do you plan to extend your approach to handle implicit nodes, which are currently deferred to future work?
3. Can you provide more information on the feature templates used in the BSP parser, including the motivation behind each feature and the impact of each feature on the results?

**Additional Comments**

The paper presents a novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing, which is an important task in natural language processing. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of transition-based methods for the task and provide a robust evaluation of their approach. However, there are some limitations to the evaluation, including the limited set of evaluation metrics and the comparison to only a few existing parsers. Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing.
