**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Broad-Coverage Semantic Parsing: A Transition-Based Approach" proposes a novel approach to parsing broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS) using a transition-based method. BSS requires representing structural properties beyond those commonly used for syntactic parsing, including multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors introduce a new parser, Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), which supports these criteria and achieves state-of-the-art results on the UCCA Wikipedia corpus and the UCCA Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea English-French parallel corpus.

**Main Contributions Claimed by the Authors**

1. A novel transition-based parser, BSP, that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. A conversion-based parsing approach that assesses the ability of existing technology to address the task of BSS parsing.
3. Experimental results on the UCCA Wikipedia corpus and the UCCA Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea English-French parallel corpus, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Contributions of this Work as Seen by the Reviewer**

1. The introduction of a novel parser, BSP, that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, which is a significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing.
2. The exploration of a conversion-based parsing approach, which provides a useful comparison with existing parsing methods.
3. The experimental results on two datasets, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach and provide a benchmark for future research.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to parsing BSS, which is a challenging task in the field of semantic parsing.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach using two datasets, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.
3. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with the field.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. The paper assumes a high level of familiarity with the field of semantic parsing and the UCCA annotation scheme, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by their parser, which would be useful for understanding the limitations of the approach.
3. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed approach can be applied to other semantic parsing tasks, which may limit its generalizability.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the BSP parser handles discontinuous units, and how it differs from other parsing approaches?
2. How do you plan to extend the proposed approach to other semantic parsing tasks, such as AMR parsing or SDP?
3. Can you provide more analysis on the errors made by the BSP parser, and how they can be improved in future work?
