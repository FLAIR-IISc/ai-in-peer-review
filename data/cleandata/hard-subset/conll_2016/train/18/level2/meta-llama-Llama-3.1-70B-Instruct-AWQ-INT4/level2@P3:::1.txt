**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a transition-based approach for broad-coverage semantic parsing, which involves parsing semantic structures that support multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors introduce a new parser, called Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), which is designed to parse Universal Cognitive Conceptual Annotation (UCCA) structures. The paper also explores a conversion-based parsing approach, where existing parsers are used to parse converted UCCA structures. The results show that BSP achieves a labeled F-score of 69.2% on primary edges, and 13.3% on remote edges, outperforming the conversion-based methods.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes a novel transition-based parser, BSP, which is designed to parse broad-coverage semantic structures that support multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. The authors introduce a new feature set, including UCCA-specific features, which improves the performance of BSP.
3. The paper explores a conversion-based parsing approach, where existing parsers are used to parse converted UCCA structures, and demonstrates its effectiveness.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing, which is a challenging task in natural language processing.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison with conversion-based methods.
3. The paper introduces a new feature set, which improves the performance of BSP.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on the UCCA annotation scheme, which may limit its applicability to other semantic representation schemes.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by BSP, which could provide insights into its limitations.
3. The paper does not explore the use of other machine learning models, such as neural networks, which could potentially improve the performance of BSP.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the UCCA annotation scheme and its advantages over other semantic representation schemes?
2. How do you plan to extend BSP to parse other semantic representation schemes, such as Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR)?
3. Can you provide more insights into the errors made by BSP, and how you plan to address them in future work?
