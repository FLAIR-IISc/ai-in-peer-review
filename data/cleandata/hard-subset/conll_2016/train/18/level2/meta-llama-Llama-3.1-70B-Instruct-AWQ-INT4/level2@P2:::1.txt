**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes two transition-based techniques for parsing broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS), which require support for multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors first assess the ability of existing technology to tackle the task by developing conversion protocols between UCCA structures and two related formalisms: dependency trees and discontinuous constituency trees. They then present a novel transition-based broad-coverage parser, Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors experiment with UCCA-annotated corpora and demonstrate the effectiveness of transition-based methods for the task.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Broad-Coverage Semantic Parsing**: The authors propose a novel transition-based broad-coverage parser, BSP, supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. **Conversion-Based Parsing**: The authors develop conversion protocols between UCCA structures and two related formalisms: dependency trees and discontinuous constituency trees, demonstrating the effectiveness of existing technology in tackling the task.
3. **UCCA Parsing**: The authors experiment with UCCA-annotated corpora, providing a first attempt at parsing this formalism.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel Approach**: The authors propose a novel transition-based broad-coverage parser, BSP, which supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. **Effective Conversion-Based Parsing**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of existing technology in tackling the task by developing conversion protocols between UCCA structures and two related formalisms.
3. **UCCA Parsing**: The authors provide a first attempt at parsing UCCA-annotated corpora, demonstrating the potential of this formalism for broad-coverage semantic parsing.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their approach on two corpora, which may not be representative of the entire range of semantic phenomena.
2. **Simple Evaluation Measures**: The authors use simple evaluation measures, which may not capture the full complexity of the task.
3. **Lack of Comparison to Other Formalisms**: The authors do not compare their approach to other formalisms, such as AMR or SDP, which may provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. **How do you plan to extend your approach to handle implicit units, which are not currently supported by your parser?**
2. **Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the errors made by your parser, and how you plan to address these errors in future work?**
3. **How do you think your approach compares to other formalisms, such as AMR or SDP, in terms of its ability to capture broad-coverage semantic phenomena?**
