- Proposes BSP, a novel transition-based broad-coverage parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Develops conversion protocols between UCCA structures and dependency / discontinuous constituency trees to leverage existing technology.  
- Presents the first experiments on parsing UCCA-annotated corpora to demonstrate feasibility.  
- Strength: introduces a novel transition-based approach that handles complex semantic phenomena (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities).  
- Strength: shows conversion-based parsing is effective for tackling UCCA by reusing existing formalisms.  
- Strength: provides an initial empirical exploration of UCCA parsing for broad-coverage semantics.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to only two corpora, possibly not covering the full range of semantic phenomena.  
- Weakness: uses relatively simple evaluation measures that may not reflect task complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other semantic formalisms (e.g., AMR, SDP) to contextualize strengths and weaknesses.  
- Question: how will the approach be extended to handle implicit units that the current parser does not support?  
- Question: request for a more detailed error analysis and plans to address common parsing errors.  
- Question: how does the approach compare empirically and representationally to other formalisms like AMR or SDP?
