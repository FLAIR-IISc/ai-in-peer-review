- Summary: Proposes BSP, a transition-based parser for UCCA that handles multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities; also evaluates a conversion-based approach; BSP gets 69.2% labeled F on primary edges and 13.3% on remote edges, outperforming conversion methods.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel transition-based parser (BSP) for broad-coverage semantic structures.

- Contribution 2: Proposes a new feature set (including UCCA-specific features) that improves BSP performance.

- Contribution 3: Explores and demonstrates a conversion-based parsing approach using existing parsers on converted UCCA structures.

- Strength 1: Presents a novel solution to the challenging problem of broad-coverage semantic parsing.

- Strength 2: Provides a thorough evaluation with comparisons to conversion-based baselines.

- Strength 3: Introduces effective features that yield performance gains.

- Weakness 1: Heavy reliance on the UCCA annotation scheme may limit applicability to other semantic formalisms.

- Weakness 2: Lacks a detailed error analysis to illuminate model limitations.

- Weakness 3: Does not explore other machine learning models (e.g., neural networks) that might improve results.

- Question 1: Request for more details on the UCCA annotation scheme and its advantages over other representations.

- Question 2: How can BSP be extended to parse other schemes such as AMR?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide insights into BSP’s errors and plans to address them?

- Additional comment 1: Evaluation is thorough but would benefit from more discussion of UCCA’s advantages.

- Additional comment 2: Suggests exploring neural-model approaches to potentially boost performance.
