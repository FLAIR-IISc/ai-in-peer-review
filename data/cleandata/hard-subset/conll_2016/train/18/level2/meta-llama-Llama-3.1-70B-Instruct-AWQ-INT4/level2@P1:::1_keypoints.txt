- Presents a novel broad-coverage semantic parsing approach supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Proposes two transition-based techniques: (1) convert structures to related formalisms and use existing parsers, and (2) build a parser that directly supports all properties.
- Introduces the Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP) that directly handles multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Uses a conversion-based parsing approach to evaluate how well existing technology can handle the task.
- Experiments on UCCA-annotated corpora demonstrate effectiveness of transition-based methods with high F-scores on primary edges.
- Shows robustness to domain variation with similar in-domain and out-of-domain performance.
- Evaluation is limited to labeled precision, recall, and F-score, potentially missing other important aspects.
- Comparisons are limited to a small set of existing parsers and may not reflect the full state of the art.
- Lacks a detailed error-type analysis that could guide further improvements.
- Requests clarification on conversion procedures and trade-offs involved in the conversion-based approach.
- Asks how the approach will be extended to handle implicit nodes currently left for future work.
- Requests more information about BSP feature templates, their motivations, and their individual impact on results.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper making a significant contribution despite evaluation limitations.
