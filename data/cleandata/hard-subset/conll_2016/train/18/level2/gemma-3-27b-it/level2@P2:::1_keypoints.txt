- Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser for Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) aimed at UCCA, handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.
- Proposes two strategies: convert UCCA to simpler formalisms (dependency/constituency) to use existing parsers, and build a dedicated BSP parser.
- Primary contribution: a novel transition system (including LEFT-REMOTEX, RIGHT-REMOTEX) and features to directly parse BSS.
- Secondary contribution: shows conversion-based parsing is feasible though lossy, and provides a UCCA corpus for experiments.
- Strength: Addresses an important gap in semantic parsing by targeting structures that tree-based parsers cannot represent.
- Strength: BSP is a novel extension of transition-based parsing with thoughtful new transitions and the `ratio` regularizer for node creation.
- Strength: Evaluation is thorough (in-domain and out-of-domain), compares against conversion baselines, includes ablation studies and upper bounds.
- Strength: Paper is generally clear with helpful figures and detailed conversion algorithm descriptions.
- Weakness: BSP’s performance (~62.4% F) is not a large improvement over conversion methods and is below an LSTM-based converted parser (73.2% F), questioning BSP’s effectiveness.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on feature engineering borrowed from other parsers; unclear if features are optimal for BSS and feature ablation is inconclusive.
- Weakness: The evaluation metric (edge matching/yield) is simplistic and may not capture semantic quality or graph coherence.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis to identify common BSP failure modes and guide improvements.
- Question: Have you considered an LSTM-based architecture for BSP or replacing the linear classifier with LSTM layers?
- Question: Can you provide a detailed error analysis identifying common BSP errors and problematic linguistic phenomena?
- Question: Please elaborate on the intuition, empirical effectiveness, and threshold sensitivity of the `ratio` feature.
- Question: How crucial are head-word features for BSP given UCCA lacks explicit heads, and could they be replaced by UCCA-specific features?
- Additional: Extending the parser to handle implicit units is an interesting future direction.
- Additional: Code and data should be well-documented and accessible to ensure reproducibility.
- Additional: Compare against simple baselines (e.g., random parser) to contextualize BSP’s performance.
