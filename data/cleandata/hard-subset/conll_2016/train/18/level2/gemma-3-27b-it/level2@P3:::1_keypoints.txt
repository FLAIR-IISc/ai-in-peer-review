- Introduces a transition-based approach for parsing Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) focused on UCCA.
- Explores two strategies: converting UCCA to dependency/constituency trees and a dedicated BSP transition-based parser.
- Demonstrates feasibility with nearly 70% labeled F-score on the English UCCA corpus.
- Addresses a significant gap by tackling structures with multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Comprehensively compares conversion-based and direct parsing approaches to show trade-offs.
- Provides solid experimental evaluation including in-domain/out-of-domain tests, an ablation study, and public code.
- Offers clear motivation and accessible background on BSS, UCCA, and related work.
- BSP’s transition system shows limited novelty, largely extending existing techniques.
- BSP underperforms compared to stronger models (e.g., LSTM parser achieving ~73.2%).
- The edge-matching evaluation metric may not fully capture semantic correctness.
- Lacks detailed error analysis to pinpoint BSP’s weaknesses on specific structures.
- Requests clarification on the design and linguistic motivation for LEFTREMOTEX/RIGHTREMOTEX transitions.
- Asks whether remote edge handling is especially challenging or of limited semantic impact.
- Requests examples of BSP parsing errors and explanations for them.
- Suggests exploring LSTM-based features within the BSP framework.
- Recommends expanding related work to include graph-based semantic parsing approaches.
- Suggests a more systematic feature ablation study and timely public release of code.
