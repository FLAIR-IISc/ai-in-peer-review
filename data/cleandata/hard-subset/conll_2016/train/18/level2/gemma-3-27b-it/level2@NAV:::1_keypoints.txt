- Problem addressed: parsing broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS) in UCCA, which include multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.  
- Proposed approaches: (1) conversion to simpler formalisms then parse, (2) direct transition-based parser (BSP).  
- Primary contribution: BSP — first parser designed to directly handle UCCA’s BSS complexities.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical analysis of conversion-based parsing as a baseline and diagnostic tool.  
- Strength: important, well-motivated problem using the valuable UCCA corpus.  
- Strength: solid experimental setup with clear metrics and comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Results: ~70% labeled F-score showing feasibility and competitive performance; novel features (e.g., “ratio”) are promising.  
- Weakness: ablation study (Table 3) lacks statistical significance testing, leaving robustness unclear.  
- Weakness: LSTM parser comparison not integrated into BSP, leaving potential improvements unexplored.  
- Weakness: conversion procedure description feels cursory; needs more detail on information loss.  
- Weakness: insufficient error analysis — deeper analysis of error types and handling long-range dependencies warranted.  
- Question: provide statistical significance tests for Table 3 results.  
- Question: what main error types were observed and which UCCA structures caused consistent errors?  
- Question: have you tried incorporating an LSTM-based classifier within the BSP framework?  
- Question: elaborate on specific information loss from conversion to dependency trees and its impact on parsing performance.
