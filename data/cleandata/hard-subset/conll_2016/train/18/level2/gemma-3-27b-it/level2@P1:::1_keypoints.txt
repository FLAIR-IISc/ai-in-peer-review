- Introduces a transition-based approach for parsing Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) with focus on UCCA, addressing multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Proposes two strategies: convert UCCA to simpler formalisms and reuse existing parsers, and build a novel transition-based BSS parser (BSP) supporting BSS properties.  
- Empirical evaluation on UCCA corpora shows effectiveness, reaching nearly 70% labeled F-score.  

Contributions:
- Clearly identifies BSS structural properties and the gap in existing parsers supporting all three simultaneously.  
- Develops BSP, a transition-based parser with new transitions/features to handle UCCA complexities.  
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation (in-domain and out-of-domain) and establishes baselines for future work.  

Strengths:
- Clear motivation and problem definition with a well-presented explanation of UCCA.  
- Sound methodology: justified transition-based approach and sensible conversion vs. direct parsing strategy.  
- Comprehensive experiments with clear metrics and out-of-domain testing.  
- Commits to public release of converters and parsers, aiding reproducibility.  
- Careful discussion of results and realistic suggestions for future improvements (e.g., LSTM classifiers).  

Weaknesses:
- Limited comparison to strong baselines, notably dependency DAG parsers that might handle multiple parents.  
- Feature set appears complex; needs more systematic feature selection or ablation studies.  
- Evaluation metric may not fully capture BSS parsing nuances; finer-grained error measures are needed.  
- Poor performance on remote edge prediction (13.3% F-score), indicating issues with long-range dependencies.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis to identify common failure patterns and guide improvements.  

Questions for authors:
- Why were existing dependency DAG parsers unavailable for comparison?  
- What are BSP’s computational costs compared to conversion-based approaches?  
- Have you tried alternative classifier architectures (e.g., LSTMs) within BSP?  
- Can you provide example errors, especially for remote edge prediction?  

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes helpful UCCA figures.  
- Reference list is comprehensive.  
- Consider briefly discussing potential applications of BSS parsing beyond research.
