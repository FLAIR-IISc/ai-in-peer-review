- Paper tackles UCCA parsing with reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities via conversion and a direct parser (BSP).
- Conversion approach maps UCCA to discontinuous constituency and dependency trees to use existing parsers and inverse-convert back.
- BSP is a transition-based parser natively supporting reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuity using extended transitions, a dynamic oracle, and UCCA features.
- Experiments on English UCCA (Wikipedia in-domain, 20K Leagues out-of-domain) use yield-based labeled F1 for primary and remote edges.
- Best primary-edge result comes from LSTM dependency conversion (69.2 F1); BSP uniquely predicts remote edges (13.3 F1 in-domain).
- Main contributions: direct transition system for semantic DAGs, conversion protocols with empirical upper bounds, and the first published UCCA parsing evaluation and baselines.
- Strength: well-motivated task with clear problem formulation and illustrative UCCA examples.
- Strength: methodological clarity and reproducibility—detailed transition set, training, features, conversion algorithms, and evaluation definitions.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical study with competitive baselines and contextualized results against conversion upper bounds.
- Strength: first evidence of UCCA parsing and remote-edge prediction; BSP establishes a baseline for reentrancy.
- Strength: useful analyses and feature ablations; BSP shows reasonable out-of-domain robustness.
- Weakness: BSP primary-edge accuracy lags behind conversion with neural LSTM baseline (59.0 vs. 69.2 F1).
- Weakness: remote-edge F1 is low (≈13) and lacks detailed error/label-wise analysis to guide improvements.
- Weakness: evaluation scope limited to English and excludes inter-sentential/implicit units; no SDP-style or DAG parser comparisons.
- Weakness: greedy decoding, perceptron, and sparse features limit performance compared to neural transition parsers.
- Weakness: some details are deferred or missing (Label(·) definition in supplement, runtime/memory stats, significance testing).
- Question: what is the full definition and empirical impact of the Label(·) heuristic in inverse conversion?
- Question: which remote-edge labels dominate errors; can per-label F1 or confusion matrices be provided?
- Question: how often is SWAP used and what discontinuity error types occur (e.g., pass vs. gap)?
- Question: what are BSP’s decoding speed, memory usage, and scalability to longer sentences?
- Question: was beam search tried for BSP; if not, why was it impractical?
- Question: are there constraints preventing cycles or duplicate primary parents, and were any violations observed?
- Essential suggestion: strengthen BSP with neural scoring (BiLSTM/stack LSTMs) and/or beam search to close the gap to LSTM baselines.
- Essential suggestion: provide targeted error analysis for remote edges and discontinuities and ablations specific to REMOTE features.
- Optional: add statistical significance tests for main comparisons.
- Optional: report efficiency metrics and include conversion heuristics inline rather than only in supplement.
- Optional: explore an SDP-style conversion and comparisons to public SDP parsers.
- Optional: include a small multilingual or cross-domain test to support cross-linguistic applicability claims.
- Overall: important, underexplored problem with clear technical contributions; conversion study is informative and BSP establishes a baseline, but improving learning/inference and deepening remote-edge analysis would substantially strengthen the work.
