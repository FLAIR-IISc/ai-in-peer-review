- Summary: Tackles broad-coverage semantic parsing for grounded DAGs with multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities; proposes conversion-based pipelines and a new transition-based parser (BSP); conversion + LSTM reaches 69.2% F on primary edges while BSP attains comparable primary scores and uniquely recovers some remote edges.

- Contribution 1: A direct transition system (BSP) for semantic DAGs supporting discontinuities, non-terminals, explicit REMOTE operations, SWAP, a dynamic oracle, and a global “ratio” regularizer.

- Contribution 2: Practical forward/inverse conversion protocols between UCCA graphs and constituency/dependency formats with upper-bound analyses showing where information is lost.

- Contribution 3: First systematic evaluation and metrics for UCCA parsing across in-domain and out-of-domain settings, with ablations illuminating impactful features.

- Strength: Addresses important structural needs beyond bi-lexical dependencies using UCCA, a scheme that supports the targeted properties.

- Strength: Sound methodology with clear algorithms for conversion, dynamic-oracle training, and principled evaluation including upper bounds.

- Strength: Empirical depth via comparisons across multiple parsers, in/out-of-domain tests, feature ablations, and primary vs. remote edge analysis.

- Strength: Practical insight that conversion + LSTM is strong and can guide improving BSP’s local model.

- Weakness: Poor performance on remote edges (BSP remote F ≈13%); conversion pipelines cannot predict remotes.

- Weakness: Competitiveness issues—best primary scores come from the converted LSTM pipeline; BSP uses a linear perceptron without beam/global search or neural features.

- Weakness: Limited generality—experiments limited to English UCCA, omit implicit units and inter-sentential phenomena, and do not evaluate other formalisms (SDP/AMR).

- Weakness: Evaluation/reporting gaps—no statistical significance tests, limited error analysis by phenomenon/label, and inverse-conversion heuristics may bias conclusions.

- Question: Can BSP benefit from a neural classifier (e.g., BiLSTM/stack-LSTM) or beam/global training to improve remote-edge recall?

- Question: How transferable is BSP to other semantic DAG formalisms (SDP, AMR with alignment) with minimal transition changes?

- Question: Please provide error breakdowns by UCCA label and phenomena (coordination, phrasal verbs, discontinuities).

- Question: How sensitive are results to the high-attachment heuristic in inverse conversion, and have you tried learned attachment reconstruction?
