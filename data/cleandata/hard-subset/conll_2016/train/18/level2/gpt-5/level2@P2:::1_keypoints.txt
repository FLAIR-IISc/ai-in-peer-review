- Contribution: BSP transition system that directly parses DAGs with non-terminals, discontinuities, and explicit remote (reentrant) transitions.  
- Contribution: Practical, explicit conversion procedures between UCCA and dependency/constituency formalisms with algorithmic detail and upper bounds.  
- Contribution: First empirical UCCA parsing study including evaluation metric, datasets, and feature-impact analyses.

- Strength: Clear problem framing showing UCCA is an appropriate testbed for structures beyond SRL/AMR.  
- Strength: BSP’s transition inventory is well-motivated, enforces UCCA constraints (single primary parent) and uses a dynamic oracle.  
- Strength: Feature design adapts prior work with UCCA-specific additions (ratio regularizer, remote-aware counts).  
- Strength: Conversion algorithms are explicit and reproducible, producing strong baselines (LSTM dependency parser achieves 69.2 primary F1).  
- Strength: Good out-of-domain robustness (20K Leagues results comparable to in-domain).  
- Strength: Commitment to release code/converters and sufficient algorithmic detail for replication.

- Weakness: BSP’s primary F1 (59.0) substantially lags the best conversion baseline (69.2); remote F1 is very low (~13%).  
- Weakness: Evaluation lacks fine-grained breakdowns by phenomena (coordination, MWEs, discontinuities, remotes) and error types.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to existing SDP/DAG-capable parsers, leaving baseline gaps.  
- Weakness: BSP uses greedy inference and averaged perceptron; stronger classifiers or beam/global training likely would improve results.  
- Weakness: The 100% constituency conversion upper bound is trivial (drops remotes); needs richer analysis of representational loss.

- Question: How does BSP ensure acyclicity when creating remote edges—are there explicit constraints/checks?  
- Question: What are counts/prevalence of remote edges, discontinuities, and coordination, and per-phenomenon F1 scores?  
- Question: Were beam search or LSTM-based classifiers tried inside BSP, and what were the results?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to SWAP weighting and MINUPDATE/IMPORTANCE hyperparameters; any ablation studies?  
- Question: For dependency inverse conversion, can the Label(·) heuristic losses be quantified or improved via alternative mappings or learned reconstruction?

- Suggestion: Report runtime and model sizes for BSP versus conversion pipelines to justify efficiency claims.  
- Suggestion: Provide error analyses on representative constructions (coordination heads, PP relators, phrasal verbs) to illustrate typical failures.  
- Suggestion: Consider multi-task or joint training with syntactic/SDP/AMR supervision to improve remote-edge generalization.  
- Suggestion: Proofread and correct minor typos in references/citation formatting; ensure released code includes trained models and reproduction scripts.

- Overall: Timely and well-motivated work with clear algorithms and useful baselines; BSP is a significant conceptual contribution but needs stronger learning/inference and deeper analyses to be practically competitive with conversion approaches.
