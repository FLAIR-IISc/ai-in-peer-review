- Summary: Proposes transition-based BSS parsing with two strategies—conversion to other formalisms and a novel BSP parser—and evaluates on UCCA corpora.  
- Contribution 1: Identifies and defines three structural properties required for broad-coverage semantic parsing (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities).  
- Contribution 2: Introduces the first parser (BSP) that directly supports all three structural properties.  
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates conversion procedures between UCCA and dependency/constituency formalisms.  
- Strength 1: Clearly motivates a meaningful gap in semantic parsing with solid examples and references.  
- Strength 2: Provides detailed, reproducible technical descriptions of conversions, transitions, and feature templates.  
- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation (in- and out-of-domain) showing feasibility and ~70% labeled F-score on primary edges.  
- Strength 4: Publishes code and models, facilitating reproducibility and future work.  
- Strength 5: Thoughtful discussion of trade-offs between conversion-based use of existing parsers and building specialized parsers.  
- Weakness 1: Lacks sufficient comparison to other semantic parsers that handle subsets of the required properties.  
- Weakness 2: Uses custom evaluation metrics rather than standard ones, hindering direct comparison to prior work.  
- Weakness 3: Fails to explain why an LSTM-based parser outperforms BSP for primary edges (73.2% vs 62.4% F-score).  
- Weakness 4: Omits detailed failure-case analysis to pinpoint specific parser weaknesses.  
- Weakness 5: Insufficient examples/discussion of information loss when converting UCCA to dependency trees.  
- Question 1: How does BSP compare quantitatively to SDP or AMR parsers that handle some structural properties?  
- Question 2: What are the inter-annotator agreement scores for the UCCA corpus to contextualize reported performance?  
- Question 3: Can the authors give concrete examples of structures that are particularly hard to convert (especially to dependency format)?  
- Overall: Meaningful and technically solid contribution, but weakened by limited comparative analysis and insufficient explanation of BSP’s performance gap.
