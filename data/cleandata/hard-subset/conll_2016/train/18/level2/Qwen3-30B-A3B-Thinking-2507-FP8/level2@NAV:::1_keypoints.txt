- Contribution: First parser supporting all three BSS properties (multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, discontinuous units).
- Contribution: Conversion-based approaches mapping UCCA structures to dependency/constituency trees.
- Contribution: Experimental validation on UCCA-annotated corpora with in-domain and out-of-domain testing; 69.2% labeled F-score on primary edges.
- Strength: Identifies a clear, well-motivated gap in semantic parsing overlooked by prior work.
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across domains and multiple methods.
- Strength: Provides a solid baseline and demonstrates practical relevance (e.g., MT, summarization).
- Weakness: Lacks analysis explaining why parser performance (69.2% F) is far below UCCA inter-annotator agreement (80–85%).
- Weakness: Acknowledged information loss from conversion (notably remote edges) is not thoroughly evaluated for downstream impact.
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison with other semantic parsing frameworks (AMR, SDP), leaving relative novelty unclear.
- Recommendation: Provide error analysis to identify specific failure modes and address the performance gap to IAA.
- Recommendation: Expand comparisons with existing semantic parsers to better establish novelty and trade-offs.
- Recommendation: Analyze when and why conversion-based methods fail to preserve full semantic structure, especially remote edges.
- Questions: How will you close the performance gap to IAA? Can you provide examples of parser failures (particularly remote edges)? How can the approach be extended to handle implicit units?
