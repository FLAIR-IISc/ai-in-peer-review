- Introduces Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) defined by three properties (multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, discontinuous units) and proposes conversion-based methods plus a new transition-based BSP parser to handle them.  
- Primary contribution: first parser that handles all three BSS properties simultaneously; secondary: conversion protocols; tertiary: experiments showing transition-based parsing works on UCCA.

- Strength: Clearly identifies a significant gap in existing semantic parsers and justifies focusing on UCCA with motivating examples and comparisons.  
- Strength: Provides practical, well-reasoned conversion protocols between UCCA and dependency/constituency trees, acknowledging and evaluating their lossy nature.  
- Strength: Demonstrates strong empirical results (BSP ~70% labeled F-score on primary edges) and notable out-of-domain robustness.

- Weakness: Evaluation metrics are newly defined, not sufficiently justified, and not compared to standard semantic-parsing metrics, making score interpretation difficult.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to alternative semantic representations and lack of exploration whether others (e.g., SDP) could be adapted to cover all three properties.  
- Weakness: Limited error analysis — paper reports F-scores but lacks breakdown of errors across remote edges, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Weakness: Underdeveloped discussion of scalability and cross-linguistic applicability; evaluation limited to English with no roadmap for other languages or larger datasets.

- Question: How do the proposed evaluation metrics compare to standard metrics used in AMR/SDP and other semantic tasks?  
- Question: How do other representations (e.g., MRS, DRT) handle the three properties, and could the conversion approach apply to them?  
- Question: What are the common error patterns for remote edges (BSP’s 15.3% F-score) and how could they be addressed in future work?
