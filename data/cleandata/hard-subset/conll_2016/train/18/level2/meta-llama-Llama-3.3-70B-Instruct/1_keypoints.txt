Summary: Proposes two transition-based techniques for broad-coverage semantic (BSS) parsing and shows effectiveness on UCCA corpora.

Contribution: Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.

Contribution: Proposes a conversion-based parsing approach to leverage existing parsers on converted representations.

Contribution: Presents the first UCCA parsing experiments, reporting a primary F-score of 69.2%.

Strength: Demonstrates effectiveness of transition-based methods for BSS parsing.

Strength: Introduces novel features and transitions (e.g., ratio feature, remote edge transitions) that improve performance.

Strength: Shows robustness to domain variation with out-of-domain results comparable to in-domain.

Weakness: Limited comparison set, only evaluated against parsers like UPARSE and MaltParser.

Weakness: No direct comparison to other semantic parsing frameworks such as AMR.

Weakness: Performance still leaves room for improvement, notably in predicting remote edges.

Question: How will the authors improve BSP’s prediction of remote edges?

Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of BSP to other semantic parsing approaches (e.g., AMR)?

Question: How do the authors plan to extend BSP to other semantic representation schemes (e.g., AMR, MRS)?
