- Addresses parsing Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) and proposes conversion-based methods plus a novel transition-based parser (BSP).  
- BSP is the first parser to support all three BSS structural properties (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuous units).  
- Experiments on UCCA corpora: BSP achieves ~70% labeled F-score on primary edges and 13.3% F-score on remote edges.  
- Authors evaluate conversion-based methods and provide upper bounds, showing their limitations.  
- Tools and code are publicly released, aiding reproducibility and future work.  
- Strength: Novelty and scope—fills a gap by directly supporting BSS properties in parsing.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation comparing BSP and conversion-based baselines in-domain and out-of-domain.  
- Strength: Practical impact through public release and applicability to cross-linguistic semantic tasks.  
- Strength: Clear writing and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Weakness: Poor performance on remote edges, limiting capture of phenomena like shared arguments.  
- Weakness: No benchmarking against state-of-the-art semantic parsers for related tasks (e.g., AMR, SDP).  
- Weakness: BSP uses a simple linear perceptron classifier; neural architectures (e.g., LSTMs/transformers) are unexplored.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis to pinpoint specific parsing challenges.  
- Questions: Consider integrating pre-trained language models or transformers to boost performance, especially for remote edges?  
- Questions: Can authors detail error types and differences between BSP and conversion-based method errors?  
- Questions: How does BSP generalize to languages other than English, given UCCA’s cross-linguistic focus?  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance—novel, well-executed, impactful work with suggested areas for future improvement.
