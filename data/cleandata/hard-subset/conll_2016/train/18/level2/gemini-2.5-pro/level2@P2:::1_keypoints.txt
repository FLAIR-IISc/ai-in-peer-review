- First paper to tackle parsing for Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS)/UCCA: defines task, metrics, baselines, and introduces a new parser.  
- Compares conversion-based and direct parsing strategies, finding a conversion+LSTM dependency parser outperforms the direct parser on core tree structure.  
- Proposes BSP, a transition-based parser that unifies discontinuous constituency and DAG dependency techniques to directly model UCCA.  
- Clear motivation and novelty: addresses a gap in parsing technology for a relevant semantic formalism (UCCA).  
- Strong experimental design: evaluates both conversion and direct approaches, uses a state-of-the-art LSTM baseline, and provides conversion upper bounds.  
- Reproducible methodology: simple, clearly described converters and a sensible BSP transition system, with planned code release.  
- Robust evaluation and insights: in-/out-of-domain tests show robustness and identify classifier choice (LSTM vs perceptron) as a major performance factor.  
- BSP is substantially outperformed on primary edge prediction (59.0% vs 69.2%), weakening the case for the direct approach.  
- Very low remote-edge performance (13.3% F) with little analysis of error types, causes, or data sparsity issues.  
- BSP’s technical novelty is incremental, mainly combining known transitions with REMOTEX actions rather than a fundamental new transition-system advance.  
- Question: how much of the BSP vs LSTM gap is due to the classifier vs features or transition system, and can an LSTM be integrated into BSP?  
- Question: please provide qualitative/quantitative error analysis of remote edges (e.g., distance, UCCA category correlations) to clarify current limitations.
