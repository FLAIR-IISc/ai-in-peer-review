- Tackles parsing Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (UCCA) with two strategies: conversion-based and a new direct parser (BSP), providing first empirical results and baselines.  
- Contribution: BSP — the first direct transition-based parser for UCCA that natively handles graph structure, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Contribution: Establishes a comprehensive benchmark systematically comparing direct and conversion-based methods using state-of-the-art parsers.  
- Contribution: Provides clear formalization of conversion algorithms between UCCA and tree-based representations to facilitate cross-formalism comparison.  
- Strength: Addresses a novel and important problem with a well-conceived approach and clear, well-written presentation.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation is thorough and rigorous, covering both in-domain and out-of-domain datasets.  
- Strength: Insightful analysis showing conversion methods lose structural info (e.g., remote edges) but powerful classifiers (LSTMs) can still excel on simplified tasks.  
- Strength: Code and converters will be publicly released, supporting reproducibility and future research.  
- Weakness: BSP underperforms the best conversion-based baseline by ~10 F1 on primary edges (59.0 vs. 69.2), limiting its practical appeal.  
- Weakness: Very low performance on remote-edge prediction (~13 F1) with no detailed error analysis to identify causes (data sparsity, features, or task difficulty).  
- Weakness: Comparison is confounded by differing classifiers (BSP uses a linear perceptron; baseline uses stack-LSTM), making it unclear whether gains are due to parsing strategy or classifier power.
