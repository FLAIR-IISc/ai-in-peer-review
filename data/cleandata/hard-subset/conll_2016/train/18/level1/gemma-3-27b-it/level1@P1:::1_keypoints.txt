- Introduces a transition-based approach for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) targeting UCCA, highlighting three challenging properties: multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.  
- Proposes two strategies: convert UCCA to dependency/constituency trees and build a novel transition-based parser (BSP) that directly supports BSS.  
- Experiments on UCCA corpora show transition-based methods work well, achieving nearly 70% labeled F-score.  
- Contribution: BSP is the first transition-based parser capable of directly handling BSS complexities.  
- Contribution: Empirical evaluation on UCCA provides a useful baseline and testbed for future BSS parsing work.  
- Contribution: Conversion-based parsing study reveals information loss and limitations of relying on existing parsers.  
- Strength: Clear problem definition with a strong rationale via the three key properties needing joint handling.  
- Strength: Transition-based parsing is well-motivated and argued to suit UCCA structure.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental setup including in-domain/out-of-domain tests, comparisons, and feature ablations.  
- Strength: Authors plan to release converters and parsers, benefiting the research community.  
- Strength: Achieves a solid baseline (~70% labeled F-score) on a complex semantic parsing task.  
- Weakness: Transition system novelty is limited—mainly an application/extension of existing techniques.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct comparison to state-of-the-art semantic parsers (e.g., AMR systems) for broader context.  
- Weakness: Relies on complex, manually engineered features; limited exploration of neural/automatic feature learning.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metric based on edge matching may not capture overall semantic coherence of parses.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks detailed error analysis identifying specific failure modes (e.g., remote edges, non-terminals).  
- Question: Please elaborate on the planned LSTM-based local classifier implementation and expected performance gains.  
- Question: How sensitive is BSP to the MINUPDATE and IMPORTANCE parameters and how extensively were they tuned?  
- Question: Can you provide a detailed breakdown of BSP’s error types and the most challenging UCCA categories/phenomena?  
- Question: Despite grounding differences, how does BSP qualitatively compare to AMR parsers and what hinders direct comparison?  
- Additional: References are extensive and well-organized.  
- Additional: Figures effectively illustrate concepts and examples.  
- Additional: Recommend adding a table comparing UCCA to other schemes (AMR, SDP) to clarify unique challenges.
