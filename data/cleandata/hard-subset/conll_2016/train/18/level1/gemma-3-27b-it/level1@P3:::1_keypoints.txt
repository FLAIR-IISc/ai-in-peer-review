- First systematic effort to parse UCCA/BSS with a transition-based approach, combining transition parsing and UCCA for the first time.  
- Explores both conversion-based (to dependency/constituency) and direct transition-based (BSP) strategies, enabling a useful comparison.  
- Empirically demonstrates feasibility: nearly 70% labeled F-score, establishing a strong baseline and validating the paradigm.  
- Provides a publicly available implementation of converters and the parser, facilitating further research.  
- Ablation studies give initial feature importance insights but are limited in scope.  

- Novel and significant problem: tackles semantic representations beyond syntax, addressing UCCA’s multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Solid experimental setup with in-domain/out-of-domain evaluation, reporting precision/recall/F-score for primary and remote edges.  
- Comprehensive approach by comparing conversion-based lower bounds with direct parsing, clarifying trade-offs.  
- Clear presentation, motivating the work and using helpful diagrams to illustrate BSS properties.  

- Limited performance gain of BSP over conversion-based LSTM parser, questioning the benefit of added model complexity given scarce UCCA data.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis categorizing failures on multiple parents, discontinuities, and non-terminal nodes; remote edge prediction is underexamined.  
- Feature set appears ad-hoc with incomplete justification; ablation is partial and the “ratio” feature is not well-motivated.  
- Limited comparisons to state-of-the-art semantic parsers (e.g., AMR parsers), reducing context for BSP’s performance.  

- Request detailed error analysis by structural property (multiple parents, discontinuities, non-terminals).  
- Ask which BSP modifications (architecture, richer features) authors expect would close the gap to conversion-based LSTM.  
- Was a global oracle tried vs. the dynamic oracle, and what were the results?  
- Request clearer rationale and evidence for the effectiveness of the “ratio” feature.  

- Make supplementary feature descriptions available during review.  
- Add a table summarizing UCCA corpus statistics (sentences, avg length, nodes, edges).  
- Improve consistency and readability of notation (subscripts/superscripts).
