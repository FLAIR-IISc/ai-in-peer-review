- Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser directly supporting UCCA’s multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuities.
- Proposes two strategies: conversion to dependency/constituency trees and direct parsing with BSP.
- Main novelty: first parser designed to fully support BSS/UCCA properties via new transitions and features.
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive experimental evaluation on UCCA corpora, providing baselines for future work.
- Provides comparative analysis showing limitations of conversion-based methods as stepping stones.
- Strength: Clearly identifies the gap in existing parsers and motivates the work well.
- Strength: BSP’s novel transitions (LEFTREMOTEX, RIGHTREMOTEX) and features for remote edges/non-terminals are solid engineering contributions.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation including in-domain/out-of-domain tests and feature ablation strengthens the results.
- Strength: Converters and parsers will be made publicly available, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: Performance gains over conversion-based methods are modest given BSP’s complexity.
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics (precision/recall/F-score on edges) are simplistic and may miss structural errors.
- Weakness: Paper lacks a detailed error analysis categorizing BSP’s common mistakes.
- Weakness: Limited comparison with other parsing paradigms suited for DAGs or discontinuities.
- Question: Request for a detailed error analysis specifying common incorrect predictions (labels, attachments).
- Question: Ask for elaboration on the “ratio” feature’s impact and interactions with other features.
- Question: Did the authors try beam search during BSP inference, and what were the effects?
- Question: Request discussion of BSP’s computational complexity versus conversion-based methods.
- Additional: Related work discussion is comprehensive.
- Additional: Figures and tables are clear and well-presented.
- Additional: Abstract is concise and informative.
- Additional: Notation is well-defined and consistent.
