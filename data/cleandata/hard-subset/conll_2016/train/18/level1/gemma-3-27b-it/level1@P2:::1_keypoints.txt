- Introduces the first transition-based parser for Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (UCCA), establishing a baseline for direct BSS parsing.  
- Systematically evaluates conversion-based strategies (UCCA→dependency/constituency), revealing trade-offs and information loss from simplifying UCCA.  
- Proposes UCCA-specific transitions (LEFTREMOTEX, RIGHTREMOTEX) and features (remote-edge handling, node/terminal ratio) tailored to UCCA’s complexities.  
- Addresses a real gap in UCCA tooling, tackling a significant bottleneck for semantic parsing research.  
- Presents a thorough experimental setup with in-domain/out-of-domain evaluation and ablation studies, comparing conversion-based and direct approaches.  
- Design choices for transitions and features are well-motivated by UCCA’s representational challenges; the ratio feature is an interesting regularizer.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful figures explaining UCCA and the parsing process.  
- Commits to releasing code and models, supporting reproducibility and future work.  
- Parser underperforms compared to conversion+LSTM baseline (62–69% vs. 73.2% F-score), indicating room for architectural/training improvements.  
- Ablation study lacks granularity—individual contributions of unigram/bigram/disco features are not separately assessed.  
- Inverse conversion uses a heuristic (high-attaching) that may introduce errors; a more sophisticated or learned conversion could help.  
- Evaluation metric focuses on edge matching and may not capture full structural or semantic graph quality.  
- No detailed error analysis provided to pinpoint frequent failure cases (e.g., specific remote-edge types).  
- Asks whether moving to a neural transition-based framework or other architectural/training changes could close the performance gap.  
- Requests clarification on the rationale for the high-attaching heuristic and whether alternative or learned conversions were considered.  
- Seeks discussion of evaluation metric limitations and potential alternative metrics for graph/semantic similarity.  
- Requests example error cases and which UCCA structures (coordination, discontinuities, remote edges) are most problematic.  
- Notes related work discussion is comprehensive and contextualizes the contribution well.  
- Suggests a deeper analysis of the ratio feature’s impact on performance.  
- Recommends adding a reference or brief explanation of the UCCA annotation guidelines for unfamiliar readers.
