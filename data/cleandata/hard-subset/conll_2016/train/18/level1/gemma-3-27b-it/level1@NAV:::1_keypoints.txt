- Tackles parsing Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) with multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities, using UCCA as the target scheme.
- Proposes two transition-based approaches: conversion to dependency/constituency trees and a direct BSS parser (BSP).
- Main contribution: initial exploration of transition-based parsing for UCCA, handling multiple parents and discontinuities.
- Secondary contribution: provides a conversion-based parsing baseline for comparison.
- Strength: correctly identifies a gap in parsing technology and selects UCCA as an appropriate testbed.
- Strength: sound methodology, introducing novel transitions to handle UCCA remote edges.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation (in-domain and out-of-domain) reporting precision, recall, and F-score for primary and remote edges.
- Strength: plans to release code and data, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited performance gains—BSP’s F-scores (~62–63%) only modestly better than conversion-based methods.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on features borrowed from other paradigms; the new “ratio” feature shows limited impact.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent neural state-of-the-art parsers (e.g., LSTM-based models).
- Question: what specific challenges during BSP development prevented higher performance versus conversion-based approaches?
- Question: what is the computational cost of BSP compared to conversion-based methods (training time, parsing speed)?
- Question: what is the distribution and properties of remote edges in the UCCA corpus, and what makes them hard to predict?
