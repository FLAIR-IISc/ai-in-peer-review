- Targets broad-coverage semantic parsing for structures with reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities, using UCCA as a grounded testbed.  
- Explores two approaches: conversion-based parsing (to constituency/dependency trees and back) and a new transition-based direct parser (BSP) for UCCA DAGs.  
- Evaluates on English UCCA corpora (Wikipedia in-domain, 20K Leagues out-of-domain) using yield-and-label matching for primary and remote edges.  
- Contribution: provides an empirical framework and strong baselines via conversion, showing a stack-LSTM dependency baseline achieves 69.2 primary F1.  
- Contribution: introduces BSP, a transition-based parser supporting non-terminals, discontinuities (SWAP), and remote edges, and is the only method predicting remotes (13.3 F1 in-domain).  
- Contribution: supplies tooling and an evaluation protocol for UCCA (converters, dynamic-oracle training, features, evaluation) to enable future work.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation, explicitly motivating the three structural requirements and UCCA’s role between SDP and AMR.  
- Strength: solid empirical side-by-side comparison of constituency conversion, dependency conversion, and direct parsing with informative upper bounds (e.g., 88.4 F1).  
- Strength: BSP is the first direct parser covering the full UCCA structural set, demonstrating feasibility for remote edge prediction.  
- Strength: careful feature engineering and training choices (UCCA-specific features, ratio regularizer, dynamic oracle, SWAP upweighting) with useful ablations.  
- Strength: reasonable domain robustness, with out-of-domain primary-edge results close to in-domain performance.  
- Weakness: BSP’s primary-edge F1 (59.0) lags the best conversion-based baseline (69.2), weakening BSP’s practical advantage.  
- Weakness: remote-edge performance is low (≈13.3 F1) and lacks detailed error analysis on phenomena like control/raising or shared arguments.  
- Weakness: conversion pipelines are lossy and under-specified (head-selection heuristics and label-priority order not fully detailed), harming reproducibility and error interpretation.  
- Weakness: the evaluation metric (yield+label matching) may obscure structural differences and offers limited diagnostic insight into reentrancy, span errors, and discontinuities.  
- Weakness: BSP uses a linear averaged perceptron with greedy decoding rather than stronger neural or beam-search methods, conflating modeling vs. representation limits.  
- Question: please specify the exact priority order over UCCA labels used in Algorithm 1 and analyze sensitivity of conversion/upper bound to this ordering.  
- Question: how is acyclicity guaranteed when creating multiple parents with LEFT/RIGHT-REMOTEX—are there explicit cycle checks?  
- Question: what proportion/types of remote edges exist, and what are the main error types for BSP’s remote predictions (missing vs. spurious, per-label F1)?  
- Question: what is the frequency/average distance of discontinuities, SWAP usage per sentence, and does SWAP upweighting change error profiles?  
- Question: have you tried beam search or neural scorers (e.g., stack-LSTM or biaffine) for BSP to narrow the gap with conversion baselines?  
- Question: please provide runtime and memory (tokens/sec) for BSP vs. conversion pipelines.  
- Additional: a qualitative comparison with SDP on phenomena like coordination would clarify costs/benefits of non-terminals.  
- Additional: an error analysis comparing UPARSE vs. dependency conversion would illuminate why dependency conversion + stack-LSTM outperforms constituency conversion.  
- Additional: releasing converters and code with full label-priority lists, Label(u) details, and hyperparameters is essential for community uptake.
