- Paper tackles broad-coverage semantic parsing for structures with multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities, proposing conversion-based and direct (BSP) transition-based approaches and evaluating on in-/out-of-domain UCCA data.

Contributions:
- Introduces BSP, a novel transition system that directly parses DAGs with non-terminals, discontinuities, and explicit remote-edge transitions, trained with a dynamic oracle.
- Provides a conversion-based evaluation framework with explicit UCCA→constituency/dependency and inverse algorithms, computing upper bounds to quantify information loss and benchmarking multiple parsers.
- Delivers the first comprehensive experimental study on UCCA parsing, including out-of-domain evaluation and feature ablation, establishing competitive baselines.

Strengths:
- Technical novelty: BSP minimal transition set principledly supports all target properties and separates primary vs. remote edges.
- Solid comparative framework: forward/inverse conversions and measurable upper bounds clarify where conversions lose information.
- Empirical breadth: multiple parsers and two domains yield coherent results and stable domain-shift behavior.
- Thoughtful feature engineering: ablation shows clear contributions from unigram/bigram and a novel ratio feature plus discontinuity-oriented features.
- Clarity and reproducibility: transitions, training protocol, conversions, and evaluation are specified and code/converters promised.

Weaknesses:
- BSP underperforms best conversion baseline on primary edges (59.0 vs. 69.2 F1) and yields low remote F1 (13.3), possibly due to linear perceptron and greedy decoding.
- Limited comparison to alternative DAG parsers and missing neural/beam variants for BSP weakens empirical claims.
- Evaluation/reporting limitations: yield-equivalence hides structural nuances; unlabeled scores, phenomenon-level breakdowns, and detailed error analysis are missing.
- Data scope: experiments only on English and exclude linkage/implicit units without analysis of their frequency or impact.
- Some conversion/reconstruction heuristics (head-priority order, Label(u)) are under-specified and can materially affect bounds and performance.

Questions for authors:
- Provide the exact priority order over UCCA edge labels for head selection (h and h*) and the full specification of Label(u); how sensitive are upper bounds to these choices?
- What are the distribution and frequency of remote edges and discontinuities in train/test, and can you add per-phenomenon performance (coordination, phrasal verbs, remote participants)?
- How does BSP perform with beam search and/or neural features (e.g., stack-LSTM) instead of the linear perceptron—do you expect similar gains as in dependency baselines?
- Can you report unlabeled primary/remote scores and per-length/span metrics to disentangle labeling vs. attachment errors?
- What decoding constraints ensure graph well-formedness (acyclicity, single primary parent), are there failure modes, and how are violations handled?

Overall:
- A timely, meaningful contribution introducing a direct DAG parser and careful conversion-based evaluation; main shortcomings are empirical (BSP’s accuracy and modeling choices) and analytical (lack of phenomenon-level analysis), which could be addressed by stronger classifiers and deeper error analysis.
