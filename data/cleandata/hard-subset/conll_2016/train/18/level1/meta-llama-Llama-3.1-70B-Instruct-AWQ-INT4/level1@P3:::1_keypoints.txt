- Proposes a transition-based approach for broad-coverage semantic parsing targeting the UCCA scheme.
- Identifies three required structural properties for semantic representations: multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Presents two parsing techniques: conversion-based parsing (convert UCCA to related formalisms) and a novel transition-based parser (BSP) that directly supports all properties.

- Contribution: Proposes a transition-based method specifically for UCCA parsing.
- Contribution: Develops BSP, a transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Contribution: Explores conversion-based approaches by converting UCCA to constituency and dependency trees and evaluating existing parsers.

- Strength: Addresses a significant gap by handling complex broad-coverage semantic structures.
- Strength: Provides thorough evaluation with comparisons to existing parsers and analysis of method strengths/weaknesses.
- Strength: Advances the UCCA scheme, potentially helping it become a standard for semantic annotation.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on UCCA, which may not be widely adopted by the community.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to one in-domain dataset (UCCA Wikipedia) and one out-of-domain dataset (20K Leagues).
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis to pinpoint parser failure modes and guide improvements.
