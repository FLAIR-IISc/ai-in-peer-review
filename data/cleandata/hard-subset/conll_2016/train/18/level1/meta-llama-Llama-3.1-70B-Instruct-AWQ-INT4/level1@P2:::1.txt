**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing, which involves parsing semantic structures that support multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors introduce a transition-based parser, called Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), that can handle these complex structures. They also explore a conversion-based parsing approach, where existing parsers are trained on converted representations of the semantic structures. The paper presents experimental results on the UCCA Wikipedia corpus and the English part of the UCCA Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea English-French parallel corpus.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP)**: The authors propose a novel transition-based parser that can handle multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. BSP is the first parser that supports these complex structures.
2. **Conversion-Based Parsing Approach**: The authors explore a conversion-based parsing approach, where existing parsers are trained on converted representations of the semantic structures. This approach allows for the evaluation of existing parsers on broad-coverage semantic parsing tasks.
3. **Experimental Results**: The paper presents experimental results on the UCCA Wikipedia corpus and the English part of the UCCA Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea English-French parallel corpus. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of BSP and the conversion-based parsing approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty of the Approach**: The paper proposes a novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing, which involves parsing semantic structures that support multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. **Effectiveness of BSP**: The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of BSP in parsing complex semantic structures.
3. **Comparison with Existing Parsers**: The paper provides a comparison of BSP with existing parsers, which highlights the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Evaluation Metrics**: The paper uses limited evaluation metrics, which may not fully capture the performance of the parsers.
2. **Lack of Error Analysis**: The paper does not provide a detailed error analysis, which could help to identify the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.
3. **Limited Comparison with Other Representations**: The paper does not provide a comparison with other semantic representations, which could help to evaluate the effectiveness of BSP and the conversion-based parsing approach.
