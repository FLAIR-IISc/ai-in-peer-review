- Proposes a transition-based approach for parsing broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS), identifies three required properties (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities), and evaluates on UCCA showing effectiveness.
- Introduces the BSS parsing task to represent complex semantic phenomena in text.
- Presents a transition-based parsing strategy that leverages similarities between UCCA and dependency schemes.
- Proposes BSP, a transition-based parser that directly supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Reports strong empirical results (69.2% labeled F-score on primary edges).
- Shows BSP is robust to domain variation with comparable out-of-domain performance.
- Introduces novel features (e.g., a ratio feature) that improve recall on primary edges.
- Evaluation is limited to labeled precision/recall/F-score and may not capture all semantic parsing aspects.
- Uses simple conversion procedures to map BSS to existing formalisms, which may be suboptimal.
- Comparison with existing parsers is limited, so overall performance context is incomplete.
- How will the parser be extended to handle implicit nodes currently deferred to future work?
- Please provide more details on the dynamic oracle used to train the local classifier.
- How do the authors plan to evaluate the parser with broader metrics (e.g., semantic role labeling, question answering)?
