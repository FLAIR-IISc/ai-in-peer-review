- Novel transition-based BSP parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units for broad-coverage semantic representation.
- Conversion-based parsing approach assessing existing parsers by converting UCCA structures into related formalisms.
- First experiments on UCCA parsing demonstrating the effectiveness of transition-based methods.

- Clear, comprehensive introduction to broad-coverage semantic parsing and the UCCA scheme.
- BSP is well-motivated and builds on advances in discontinuous constituency and dependency DAG parsing.
- Experimental results: BSP attains comparable F-scores to existing parsers and outperforms them on remote-edge prediction.

- Conversion-based approach is limited by reliance on existing parsers not optimized for UCCA and may miss semantic phenomena.
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis of BSP, reducing insight into parsing challenges and future improvements.
- Evaluation is limited to English despite UCCA’s multilingual use; cross-lingual performance is untested.

- How will implicit units in UCCA, currently unsupported by BSP, be handled?
- Please provide more details on the feature templates used in BSP and how they were selected.
- How do the authors plan to extend BSP to handle coreference, anaphora, and other complex semantic phenomena?

- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Tables and figures effectively present results.
- Comprehensive reference list for readers who want to explore the topic further.
