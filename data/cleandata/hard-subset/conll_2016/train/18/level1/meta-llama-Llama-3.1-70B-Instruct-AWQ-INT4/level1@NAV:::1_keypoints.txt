- Summary: Proposes two techniques for broad-coverage semantic parsing—conversion to other formalisms to use existing parsers and a direct transition-based parser—and evaluates them on UCCA corpora, showing transition-based methods work well.

- Contribution 1: Introduces Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), a transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units—the first to target broad-coverage semantic parsing.

- Contribution 2: Presents a conversion-based approach with two scenarios (to constituency trees and to CoNLL-style dependencies) to leverage existing parsing technology.

- Contribution 3: Evaluates approaches on UCCA-annotated corpora, the only corpus meeting the three criteria for broad-coverage semantic structures.

- Strength 1: Demonstrates effectiveness of transition-based methods with a primary F-score of 69.2%.

- Strength 2: Shows robustness to domain variation, with comparable out-of-domain performance.

- Strength 3: Presents a novel approach addressing a crucial NLP task.

- Weakness 1: Comparisons are limited to a small set of existing parsers and may not reflect state-of-the-art.

- Weakness 2: No detailed error analysis is provided to pinpoint failure modes.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation relies solely on labeled precision, recall, and F-score, which may be insufficient.

- Question 1: How will the parser be extended to handle implicit nodes and edges?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide a more detailed error analysis identifying areas for improvement?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to compare their parser with other state-of-the-art (e.g., neural) semantic parsers?
