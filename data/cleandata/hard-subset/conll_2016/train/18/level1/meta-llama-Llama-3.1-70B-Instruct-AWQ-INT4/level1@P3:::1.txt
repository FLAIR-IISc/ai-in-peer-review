**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a transition-based approach for broad-coverage semantic parsing, specifically for the Universal Cognitive Conceptual Annotation (UCCA) scheme. The authors identify three structural properties required for broad-coverage semantic representation: multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. They present two techniques for parsing such semantic structures: (1) conversion-based parsing, where UCCA structures are converted to related formalisms and existing parsers are applied, and (2) a novel transition-based parser, Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), that directly supports the full set of properties.

**Main Contributions**

1. The proposal of a transition-based approach for broad-coverage semantic parsing, specifically for the UCCA scheme.
2. The development of a novel transition-based parser, BSP, that directly supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
3. The exploration of conversion-based parsing approaches, including the conversion of UCCA structures to constituency trees and dependency trees, and the evaluation of existing parsers on these converted structures.

**Strengths**

1. The paper addresses a significant gap in the field of semantic parsing by proposing a parser that can handle the complexities of broad-coverage semantic representation.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison with existing parsers and an analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of their method.
3. The paper contributes to the development of the UCCA scheme, which has the potential to become a widely-used standard for semantic annotation.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on the UCCA scheme, which may not be widely adopted or accepted by the research community.
2. The evaluation of the paper is limited to a single dataset (UCCA Wikipedia corpus) and a single out-of-domain dataset (20K Leagues corpus).
3. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by their parser, which could help to identify areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the issue of inter-annotator agreement, which is a significant challenge in semantic annotation tasks?
2. Can you provide more details on the features used in the BSP parser, and how they were selected?
3. How do you plan to extend the BSP parser to handle other semantic representation schemes, such as AMR or SDP?
4. Can you provide more information on the computational resources required to train and run the BSP parser?
5. How do you plan to make the BSP parser and its training data publicly available, and what are the plans for maintaining and updating the parser in the future?
