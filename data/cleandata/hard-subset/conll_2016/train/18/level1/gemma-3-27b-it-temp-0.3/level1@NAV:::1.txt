## Review for Broad-Coverage Semantic Parsing: A Transition-Based Approach

This paper introduces a transition-based approach to parsing Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS), specifically focusing on the UCCA annotation scheme. The authors identify the need for parsers capable of handling multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units – features lacking in existing semantic parsers. They propose two strategies: converting UCCA structures to simpler formalisms (dependency and constituency trees) for parsing with existing tools, and developing a novel transition-based parser (BSP) directly supporting BSS features.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the **initial exploration of transition-based parsing for UCCA and, more broadly, for BSS**. While transition-based parsing is well-established for dependency parsing, applying it to structures with multiple parents and non-terminals is a novel step. The authors successfully adapt existing techniques and introduce new transitions to handle these complexities. A secondary contribution is the **empirical evaluation of conversion-based parsing as a baseline and proof-of-concept**. Demonstrating that existing parsers can achieve reasonable performance on converted UCCA data provides a valuable starting point for future research. Finally, the **public release of the converters and parsers** is a valuable contribution to the community, facilitating further work in this area.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addresses a clear gap:** The paper tackles a significant limitation in current semantic parsing technology – the inability to handle complex structural properties crucial for broad semantic coverage.
2. **Sound methodology:** The two-pronged approach – conversion-based parsing and a novel parser – provides a comprehensive evaluation and allows for comparison.
3. **Solid experimental setup:** The use of the UCCA corpus, both in-domain and out-of-domain, provides a robust evaluation environment. The ablation studies are helpful in understanding the contribution of different features.
4. **Practical contribution:** The release of code will accelerate research in BSS parsing.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited novelty of the parsing approach:** The BSP parser largely builds upon existing transition-based parsing techniques. While the adaptation for BSS is valuable, the core parsing mechanism itself isn’t groundbreaking.
2. **Evaluation metric limitations:** The proposed evaluation metric, based on edge matching, feels somewhat coarse-grained and doesn’t fully capture the nuances of semantic structure. A more comprehensive evaluation, potentially incorporating semantic similarity measures, would be beneficial.
3. **Lack of comparison to strong baselines:** While the conversion-based approach provides a baseline, comparing BSP against state-of-the-art AMR parsers (despite the differences in representation) could provide a more informative benchmark.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you elaborate on the design choices behind the novel features (e.g., the ratio feature)? What insights led you to believe these features would be beneficial?
2. The paper mentions the potential for applying LSTM-based classifiers to BSP. Have you explored this direction, and if so, what were the preliminary results?
3.  How sensitive is the performance of BSP to the MINUPDATE parameter? Did you perform a hyperparameter search to optimize this value?
