- Paper tackles parsing Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) — supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units — via conversion-based methods and a new transition-based parser (BSP), evaluated on UCCA corpora.  
- BSP: first transition-based parser that natively supports all three BSS structural properties.  
- Thorough evaluation of conversion-based approaches, analyzing trade-offs between using existing parsers and specialized solutions.  
- Authors will publicly release converters and parsers to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Novelty: BSP fills a gap in semantic parsing by handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Comprehensive evaluation includes both in-domain and out-of-domain performance, providing robust baselines.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: detailed transition set, features, training procedure, and conversion upper bounds provided.  
- Potential broader impact: focus on UCCA supports cross-linguistic applications (e.g., MT, summarization).  
- Weak performance on remote edges (13.3% F-score) highlights need for improvement.  
- BSP uses a linear classifier; LSTM-based dependency parser outperforms it, suggesting neural integration could help.  
- Dependency-format conversion loses substantial information for non-terminals and remote edges.  
- Custom evaluation metrics for BSS parsing are not widely validated, limiting comparability.  
- Question: How does BSP compare to state-of-the-art semantic parsers for AMR or SDP beyond UCCA?  
- Question: Could integrating neural architectures (LSTMs/transformers) improve BSP, especially for remote edges?  
- Question: Are there plans to extend BSP to other semantic annotation schemes, and what challenges are expected?  
- Overall: well-written and promising work that needs further improvements in performance and scalability; tool release is commendable.
