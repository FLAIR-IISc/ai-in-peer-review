- Proposes parsing broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS) that allow multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Introduces two transition-based techniques: conversion to related formalisms for existing parsers and a direct parser supporting full BSS properties.
- Main contribution: Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), a transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.
- Main contribution: A conversion-based parsing approach to evaluate existing parser technology on BSS.
- Main contribution: First experiments on UCCA parsing showing transition-based methods effective for BSS.
- Strength: Provides a clear, detailed explanation of transitions, features, and training procedures.
- Strength: BSP achieves comparable F-scores to state-of-the-art on primary edges and outperforms them on remote edges.
- Strength: Includes thorough analysis with feature ablation and error analysis to reveal strengths and weaknesses.
- Weakness: Conversion-based approach can lose information during conversion, hurting accuracy.
- Weakness: Method requires substantial annotated data, which is time-consuming and expensive to obtain.
- Weakness: Paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison with other semantic parsing approaches and trade-offs.
- Question: How will information loss during conversion be addressed?
- Question: Can the approach be applied to other representations like AMR or SDP?
- Question: How do the authors plan to improve parser accuracy, particularly on remote edges?
