- Introduces Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) using UCCA and frames parsing with non-terminals, discontinuities, and multiple parents.
- Proposes two parsing approaches: lossily converting UCCA to standard formalisms and a novel direct transition-based parser (BSP) that produces DAGs with remote edges.
- Experiments show an LSTM-based conversion approach bests BSP on the primary tree backbone, while BSP uniquely predicts remote (multi-parent) edges.
- Contribution: defines the UCCA parsing task, provides baselines, code, and converters to seed future work.
- Contribution: presents BSP, an integrated transition system combining discontinuous constituency and DAG dependency techniques.
- Contribution: provides an empirical comparison showing conversion-based methods can outperform specialized direct parsers on tree structure.
- Strength: tackles a novel, linguistically motivated and grounded semantic representation (UCCA) missing from prior work.
- Strength: thorough evaluation comparing multiple strong parsers, computing conversion upper bounds, and testing in- and out-of-domain.
- Strength: principled BSP design leveraging prior transition techniques and useful novel features (e.g., ratio feature).
- Weakness: BSP underperforms substantially on primary edges (59.0 vs. 69.2 F) and scores very low on remote edges (13.3 F), reducing its practical impact.
- Weakness: limited error analysis of remote edge prediction—no detailed breakdown of what remote phenomena are captured or missed.
- Weakness: dependency-to-constituency inverse conversion relies on simple heuristics (e.g., Label(u) defaults), with unclear impact and no ablation.
- Question: beyond using an LSTM classifier, could BSP’s transition system or feature set be causing the performance gap?
- Question: what kinds of remote-edge phenomena does BSP predict correctly versus fail on?
- Question: how sensitive are dependency-based results to the Label(u) heuristic and were alternative reconstruction strategies tested?
