- Task: Parsing Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) — UCCA with DAGs, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Methods: Two approaches — indirect conversion to standard formalisms for existing parsers, and a direct transition-based parser (BSP) for full BSS.  
- Main experimental finding: Conversion + LSTM achieves highest primary-edge performance; BSP is the only method that predicts remote edges but with low accuracy.  
- Contribution 1: Formal introduction of UCCA parsing as a new, challenging task and provision of first baselines.  
- Contribution 2: BSP — a novel transition-based parser designed to handle DAGs, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Contribution 3: Thorough empirical comparison of direct vs. indirect methods, including upper bounds for conversions.  
- Strength: High novelty and significance — tackles richly structured semantic representations beyond trees.  
- Strength: Comprehensive methodology — dual-pronged (conversion and direct) approach leveraging existing parsers.  
- Strength: Rigorous evaluation — in-/out-of-domain tests, new BSS metric, upper-bound analysis, and BSP feature ablation.  
- Strength: Clarity and reproducibility — clear exposition and commitment to releasing code and converters.  
- Weakness: BSP underperforms on primary edges (59.9% F) compared to conversion+LSTM (69.2% F) and has very low remote-edge F-score (13.3%).  
- Weakness: Core comparison confounded by classifier mismatch (perceptron for BSP vs. LSTM for indirect method), obscuring whether gains are due to approach or model.  
- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative/error analysis — little detail on failure cases (coordination, long-distance dependencies, headless constructions).  
- Question 1: Why use a perceptron for BSP instead of a stronger classifier (e.g., LSTM)? implementation simplicity or theoretical rationale?  
- Question 2: How much do back-conversion heuristics introduce artifacts or inflate LSTM-based scores by producing easy-to-convert structures?  
- Question 3: Can authors provide examples/patterns of successful vs. failed remote-edge attachments to guide feature/transition improvements?
