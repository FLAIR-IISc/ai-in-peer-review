- Introduces BSS parsing (DAGs, non-terminals, discontinuities) using UCCA and evaluates two approaches: conversion-based and a novel direct transition-based parser (BSP); conversion wins on core trees, BSP is only method handling remotes.
- Contribution: Formulates BSS parsing and develops the first direct parser (BSP) for it.
- Contribution: Provides the first comprehensive empirical benchmarking of UCCA parsing, comparing direct and conversion-based methods and computing conversion upper bounds.
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem definition grounded in UCCA with strong significance for parsing research.
- Strength: Methodologically sound dual approach (conversion + direct) with upper-bound analysis that isolates conversion error.
- Strength: Novel, well-engineered BSP design—principled transition set, comprehensive features, and a new `ratio` feature to regulate node creation.
- Strength: Thorough, reproducible experiments (in/out-of-domain tests, ablations) and promised code/converter release.
- Weakness: BSP is substantially outperformed on primary edges by a conversion+LSTM baseline and obtains very low remote-edge F-score, questioning the direct approach’s payoff.
- Weakness: Paper lacks in-depth error analysis to explain BSP’s poorer primary-edge performance and the characteristics of correctly predicted remote edges.
- Weakness: Understates the impact of the LSTM classifier—classifier choice appears critical and BSP’s perceptron may be a limiting factor.
- Weakness: Dependency-to-constituency inverse conversion relies on heuristics that are not fully explained in the main paper despite being crucial to the best-performing system.
- Question: Have you tried replacing BSP’s linear classifier with a stronger neural model (e.g., stack LSTM) to close the performance gap?
- Question: Can you provide a qualitative error analysis comparing BSP and the LSTM conversion parser, and characterize the remote edges BSP predicts correctly?
- Question: Please clarify the intuition behind the `ratio` feature and why it primarily boosts recall for non-terminal creation.
