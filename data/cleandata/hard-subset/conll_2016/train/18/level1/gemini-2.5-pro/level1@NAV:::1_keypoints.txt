- Introduces first parsing methods for Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) using UCCA as a case study.  
- Proposes two strategies: conversion-based (to constituency/dependency trees) and a novel direct transition-based parser (BSP) handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Experiments show conversion-based LSTM dependency parser achieves highest accuracy on primary structure; BSP uniquely recovers remote edges but with low accuracy.  
- Contribution: establishes the first comprehensive benchmark and strong baselines for UCCA parsing, enabling future research.  
- Contribution: provides an empirical comparison of direct vs. indirect parsing strategies, highlighting trade-offs between lossy conversion with strong classifiers and principled direct modeling.  
- Strength: novel and significant—pioneers parsing for UCCA and opens a new area in semantic parsing.  
- Strength: methodologically sound dual-strategy design leverages strong existing parsers and proposes a principled direct parser.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation with comparisons to strong baselines, upper-bound analysis, feature ablation, and in-/out-of-domain testing.  
- Weakness: BSP is substantially outperformed on primary edges by conversion-based methods and has very low remote-edge F-score, limiting practical utility.  
- Weakness: comparison is inconclusive because BSP uses weaker classifier technology (linear perceptron) versus conversion’s stack-LSTM, confounding strategy vs. classifier effects.  
- Weakness: limited qualitative error analysis—unclear which linguistic phenomena drive failures and why lossy conversions perform well.
