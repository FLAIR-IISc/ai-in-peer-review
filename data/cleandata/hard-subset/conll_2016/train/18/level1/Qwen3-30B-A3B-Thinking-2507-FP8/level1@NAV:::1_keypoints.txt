- Addresses gap in semantic parsing by targeting Broad-Coverage Semantic Structures (BSS) with multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Proposes two approaches: convert UCCA to dependency/constituency trees for existing parsers, and a novel transition-based BSP parser that directly handles BSS.  
- Main contributions: first feasible transition-based parsing for BSS, development of BSP with specialized transitions (e.g., LEFT-REMOTEX/RIGHT-REMOTEX), and conversion procedures between UCCA and other formalisms.  
- Strength: clearly motivated problem and demonstration that BSS parsing is feasible with current transition-based techniques.  
- Strength: BSP design includes novel transitions specifically for remote edges and non-standard structures.  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on UCCA corpus with promising results (69.2% primary F-score versus 80–85% IAA).  
- Weakness: Core approach builds heavily on prior transition-based work (limited novelty).  
- Weakness: Lacks meaningful comparison to state-of-the-art parsers for other representations (e.g., AMR), limiting contextualization.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metric (edge-yield–based) is not well-justified and deviates from standard semantic-parsing metrics.  
- Weakness: Direct BSP only marginally outperforms conversion-based LSTM, weakening the case for a specialized parser.  
- Question: How does BSP compare to recent state-of-the-art AMR parsers using similar transition-based methods?  
- Question: Can you provide detailed error analysis identifying which BSS properties (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuities) are most challenging?  
- Question: Why does conversion to dependency trees with LSTM match BSP’s primary F-score, and does that undermine the need for BSP?
