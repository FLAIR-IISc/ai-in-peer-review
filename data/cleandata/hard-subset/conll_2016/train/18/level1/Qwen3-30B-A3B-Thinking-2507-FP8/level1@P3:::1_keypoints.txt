- Paper targets parsing for broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS) supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, proposing conversion-based methods and a novel transition-based BSP parser.  
- Contribution: first parser claimed to directly support all three BSS properties.  
- Contribution: detailed conversion procedures between UCCA and dependency/constituency formalisms with trade-off analysis.  
- Contribution: shows transition-based methods can be adapted for BSS, reporting promising performance (69.2% primary F-score).  
- Strength: identifies an important theoretical gap—no prior parser supports all three BSS properties—and argues why they are necessary.  
- Strength: rigorous experiments on UCCA corpora with in/out-of-domain testing and detailed feature ablations.  
- Strength: practical impact via released converters/parsers and competitive results relative to UCCA inter-annotator agreement.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—BSP mainly adapts existing transition-based techniques and differences from prior work are not clearly emphasized.  
- Weakness: introduces an "edge yields" evaluation metric without clear justification or comparison to standard metrics.  
- Weakness: insufficient benchmarking against state-of-the-art semantic parsers for related formalisms (e.g., AMR).  
- Weakness: experiments limited to English UCCA corpora with sparse out-of-domain and no multilingual evaluation.  
- Question: how does the edge-yields metric relate to and compare with standard semantic parsing metrics (AMR/SDP)?  
- Question: how does this approach differ from prior dependency DAG parsing (e.g., Sagae & Tsujii 2008; Tokgöz & Eryiğit 2015) and what adaptations were necessary for full UCCA?  
- Question: why not apply LSTM-based classifiers directly to BSP given their stronger performance, and how would that affect results?  
- Question: is the gap to UCCA inter-annotator agreement (69.2% vs. 80–85%) due to parser limitations or annotation inconsistency, and how does UCCA IAA compare to AMR/SDP?
