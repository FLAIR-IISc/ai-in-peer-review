- Paper introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and evaluates several methods, including a transition-based parser.  
- UCCA differs from other SRs by allowing nonterminal nodes and being "grounded" (text tokens appear as nodes).  
- Developing a semantic parser for UCCA is a reasonable and potentially valuable contribution.  
- Intro/background overstates UCCA as the only sensible graph-based SR to study and should be more balanced.  
- Disagreement with the three proposed criteria for SRs: nonterminal nodes may not add expressive power; advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR are unclear.  
- The term "grounded" is overloaded; authors should consider alternatives (e.g., "anchored" or "lexicalized") and rephrase arguments carefully.  
- Parser implementation seems acceptable, but evaluation is unimpressive: MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.  
- Prior work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) could boost dependency-tree baselines and further weaken the claimed strength of the BSP parser.
