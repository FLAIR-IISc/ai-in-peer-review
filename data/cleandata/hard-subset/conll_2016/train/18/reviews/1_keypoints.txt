- Paper proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA’s rich semantico-cognitive representations.  
- Authors claim semantic annotation should be graph-based, include non-terminal nodes, and allow discontinuity; these principles are not specific to semantics.  
- Paper situates work within broad-coverage semantic parsing and UCCA corpora.  
- Three experimental setups: constituency-based (Stanford/uparse with UCCA→constituency), dependency-based (UCCA→dependency), and direct transition-based graph parsing (core contribution).  
- Reviewer finds the work interesting but raises methodological and empirical concerns.  
- Claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser is weak because the transition set doesn’t exploit UCCA-specific phenomena.  
- Remote-edge handling could be achieved via label differences rather than new transitions.  
- Prior graph-parsing work from SemEval (2014–2015) and existing DAG/Turbo parsers were not used as baselines or comparisons.  
- Phrase-based experiments should compare to Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins (2015); uparse reference is missing.  
- Evaluation should include metrics from related SDP shared tasks; lack of comparable baselines and metrics weakens the paper.  
- Suggest the paper is worth discussion at a venue like CoNLL despite issues.  
- Editorial/organization notes: introduce “grounded semantic” earlier.  
- Suggest using constituent trees with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser as an alternative baseline.  
- Converting to surface dependency trees loses information; recommend richer SDP-style conversions (Schlüter et al., 2014).  
- Request clarification/examples for UCCA implicit units that lack textual correspondents.  
- Note UCCA appears not to model quantifier scope (unlike MRS/DRT); asker requests error analysis to judge informativeness and parsability.  
- The three proposed principles are generic and somewhat ad-hoc; the paper needs a clearer, broader introduction and clearer statement of goals (UCCA parser vs. general semantic parser vs. parsable semantic scheme).  
- References/resources cited: DAGParser (Ribeyre) and TurboParser (André Martins) repos.
