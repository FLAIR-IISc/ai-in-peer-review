- Proposes a transition-based graph parser (BSP) for UCCA addressing multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuity.  
- Presents conversion-based baselines mapping UCCA to constituency and dependency trees.  
- Significant because UCCA uniquely supports all three structural properties and lacked a dedicated parser.  
- Strength: clearly identifies structural properties often overlooked in semantic parsing frameworks.  
- Strength: provides the first UCCA parser with reasonable performance (≈70% F-score for primary edges).  
- Strength: code is publicly released, and conversion experiments illuminate information loss between formalisms.  
- Weakness: claim of being the “first semantic-oriented parser” is unfounded; transition set does not exploit UCCA-specific phenomena.  
- Weakness: fails to compare against prior graph-parsing work (DAGParser, TurboParser, SemEval results), using only dependency/constituency baselines.  
- Weakness: evaluation omits standard SDP shared-task metrics, weakening cross-formalism comparison.  
- Weakness: the three structural principles are generic and ad-hoc; goals (UCCA-specific parser vs. general semantic parser) need clearer articulation.  
- Omission: paper neglects discussion that UCCA does not model quantifier scope and that implicit units lack textual anchors.  
- Omission: conversion to surface dependency trees leads to significant information loss; richer SDP-style conversions should be used.  
- Recommendation: authors should compare to appropriate graph-parsing baselines, clarify the scope of their contribution, and adopt better evaluation metrics.  
- Overall: a meaningful step toward broad-coverage semantic parsing that is valuable for discussion if revisions address these concerns.
