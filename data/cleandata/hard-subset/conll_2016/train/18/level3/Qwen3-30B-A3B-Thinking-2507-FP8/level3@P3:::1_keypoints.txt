- Proposes a transition-based graph parser (BSP) for UCCA that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Evaluates three approaches: UCCA→constituency (UPARSE), UCCA→dependency (MaltParser/LSTM), and direct BSP graph parser.  
- Provides conversion protocols between UCCA and standard representations and is the first experimental exploration of UCCA parsing.  
- Strong theoretical motivation arguing the necessity of multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuity with illustrative examples.  
- Practical evaluation shows ~70% primary edge F-score, promising given UCCA inter-annotator agreement of 80–85%.  
- Authors plan to release converters and parsers, aiding reproducibility and future work.  
- Novelty claim is weak: the transition set and parser are standard adaptations rather than a fundamentally new semantic parsing approach.  
- Inadequate baselines and evaluation: no comparison to prior graph parsers (DAGParser, TurboParser) and omission of standard SDP metrics; dependency conversion loses non-terminal information.  
- The three proposed principles are presented without strong theoretical grounding and the paper is unclear whether it targets UCCA specifically or general semantic parsing.  
- Paper notes UCCA’s lack of quantifier scope modeling but fails to provide error analysis on how this limits informativeness and parsability.  
- Request examples of implicit UCCA units lacking textual correspondents and how the parser handles them.  
- Ask how omission of quantifier scope affects sentences like “Every student read a book.”  
- Question why existing graph-parsing baselines weren’t used and how those methods would handle UCCA structures.  
- Ask how the custom evaluation metric compares to standard SemEval SDP metrics and why those weren’t used.  
- Overall: an interesting and valuable contribution that merits discussion but requires substantial revisions—clearer theoretical framing, stronger comparisons/metrics, and deeper analysis of UCCA limitations.
