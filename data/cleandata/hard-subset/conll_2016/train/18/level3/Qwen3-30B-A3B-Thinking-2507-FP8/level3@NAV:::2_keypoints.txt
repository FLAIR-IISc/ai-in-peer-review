- Introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parser for UCCA (BSP) handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units, and compares it to conversion-based dependency/constituency approaches.  
- Claims three main contributions: first UCCA parser enabling practical use, a transition-based parser tailored to UCCA, and a thorough evaluation framework for UCCA requirements.  
- Strength: timely work addressing an important gap in semantic parsing.  
- Strength: clear exposition of the structural properties required for broad-coverage semantic representations.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation performed on a substantial UCCA corpus.  
- Weakness: overstates UCCA as the only sensible graph-based semantic representation and fails to acknowledge alternative representations.  
- Weakness: claimed advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR are unsubstantiated with no evidence of improved parsing or downstream benefit.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is unimpressive—MaltParser beats BSP on primary edges (63.9% vs. 62.4%) and BSP's remote-edge F-score is very low (15.3%).  
- Weakness: the term "grounded" is overloaded and should be replaced with a clearer term such as "lexicalized" or "anchored."  
- Weakness: overlooks prior work on tree approximations (e.g., Agic et al. 2015) that could improve dependency-tree baselines and weaken BSP's claimed advantages.  
- Overall: contribution is potentially valuable but undermined by the above issues; BSP’s novelty and theoretical claims require stronger justification and empirical support before being considered a meaningful advancement.
