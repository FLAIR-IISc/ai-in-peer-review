- Paper tackles parsing semantic representations requiring multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units; proposes two approaches for UCCA.

- Contributions: presents the first UCCA parser enabling future work.

- Contributions: introduces a novel transition-based parser (BSP) that directly supports the three structural properties.

- Contributions: systematically evaluates conversion-based approaches between UCCA and dependency/constituency representations.

- Strength: First parser for UCCA with released code/data, promoting reproducibility and follow-up work.

- Strength: Clear, structured analysis of conversion and inverse-conversion methods, comparing direct vs conversion-based parsing.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on UCCA Wikipedia corpus including primary and remote edges and out-of-domain testing on 20K Leagues.

- Weakness: Claim that UCCA is uniquely able to support all three properties is overstated and not convincingly argued.

- Weakness: The necessity of the three structural criteria (especially non-terminal nodes and “groundedness”) is insufficiently justified.

- Weakness: BSP is outperformed by an LSTM-based dependency parser by 9.1% F on primary edges; remote edge F-scores (10–15%) are very low.

- Weakness: The term “grounded” is used inconsistently; clearer terminology like “anchored” or “lexicalized” is recommended.

- Question: How do authors respond to the critique that non-terminal nodes may be unnecessary, since MWEs can be handled via other mechanisms (e.g., head selection)?

- Question: What concrete evidence supports the claimed advantages of grounded representations over non-grounded ones like AMR beyond easier alignment?

- Question: Given BSP’s lower performance, what specific improvements would make BSP competitive with LSTM-based parsers while leveraging UCCA’s unique properties?

- Recommendation: Recommend rejection due to overstated claims and weak evaluation, though the UCCA parsing work is potentially valuable if claims, justification, and evidence are strengthened.
