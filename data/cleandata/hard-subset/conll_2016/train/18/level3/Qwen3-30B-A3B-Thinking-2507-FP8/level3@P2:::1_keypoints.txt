- Proposes a transition-based parser (BSP) and conversion-based methods for UCCA, aiming to support multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Claims the first parser supporting all three structural properties for broad-coverage semantic parsing.  
- Formalizes structural requirements for broad-coverage semantic representation and validates transition-based methods experimentally.

- Identifies a real gap: no prior parser supports all three required structural properties, making this contribution meaningful.  
- Clearly argues and exemplifies why the three structural properties are necessary for broad-coverage semantics.  
- Conversion-based approaches offer a practical way to leverage existing parsers and achieve reasonable performance.

- BSP’s transition set appears generic and does not sufficiently exploit UCCA-specific phenomena; remote-edge handling may have been achievable without new transitions.  
- Fails to compare against relevant prior graph-parsing work and SDP shared-task baselines (e.g., DAGParser, TurboParser), weakening the evaluation.  
- Uses nonstandard evaluation metrics and baselines instead of SDP shared-task metrics, preventing direct comparison with prior work.  
- The “first semantic-oriented parser” claim is questionable given the generic transition set and lack of comparison to prior graph parsers.  
- Unclear goal: whether this is a UCCA-specific parser, a general semantic parser, or a proposal for a new parsable semantic scheme; the three properties seem ad-hoc without stronger justification.

- Request examples of UCCA implicit units without textual correspondents and how they’d be parsed in practice.  
- Ask for error analysis on UCCA’s lack of quantifier scope modeling to assess impact on informativeness and parsability.  
- Ask why conversion to dependency trees loses non-terminal information and how this compares to richer SDP-style conversions.  
- Request clarification on how the parser specifically exploits UCCA semantics beyond a generic graph parser.

- Suggest introducing “grounded semantic” concepts earlier for clarity.  
- Phrase-based experiments should compare to Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins (2015) and reference the UPARSE parser.  
- Conversion to surface dependency trees loses significant information; recommend richer SDP-style conversions.  
- The paper’s “first” claim is overstated and lack of proper comparisons undermines contribution, but the work addresses a meaningful gap and merits discussion.
