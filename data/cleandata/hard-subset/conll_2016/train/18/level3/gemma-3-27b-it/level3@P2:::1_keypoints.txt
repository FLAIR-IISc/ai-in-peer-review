- Paper introduces a transition-based parser (BSP) and conversion strategies for UCCA, which supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous constituents.  
- Primary contribution is exploring direct transition-based parsing of UCCA graphs, an underexplored area.  
- Secondary contribution is empirical evaluation on UCCA corpora, providing a baseline including out-of-domain testing.  
- Authors clearly articulate structural requirements for broad-coverage semantics (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuity).  
- Strength: Novel application of transition-based methods to model complex UCCA graph structure with a concrete implementation.  
- Strength: Valuable empirical evaluation on the only widely available resource with the three desired structural properties.  
- Strength: Well-presented motivation and framing of UCCA’s structural properties, even if not unique.  
- Weakness: Core transition system shows limited novelty and special-case transitions (e.g., for remote edges) seem ad-hoc.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to relevant baselines and prior DAG/graph parsers (e.g., SemEval SDP work, Ribeyre’s DAGParser, TurboParser).  
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics and conversion procedures are not well-grounded in the broader semantic parsing literature and conversions lose information.  
- Question: Provide concrete examples of “implicit units” without textual correspondents and explain how the parser handles them.  
- Question: Show error analysis where UCCA’s lack of quantifier scope modeling impacts parsing and discuss potential extensions.  
- Question: Broader justification needed for requiring multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuity — clarify goals (UCCA parser vs general semantic parser).  
- Additional comment: Introduction should mention “grounded semantic” representation earlier for context.  
- Additional comment: Consider Berkeley parser with rich node labels as an alternative baseline and cite Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins (2015).  
- Additional comment: UPARSE reference is missing for constituency experiments; provide links to DAGParser and TurboParser repositories.
