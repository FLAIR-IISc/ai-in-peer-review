- Primary contribution: initial exploration of parsing UCCA/BSS, proposing conversion-based and a novel transition-based (BSP) parser and providing baseline experiments.
- Secondary contribution: experimental setup and baseline results with promised public release of converters and parsers.

- Strength: addresses a genuine gap—parsers that support multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuity.
- Strength: comprehensive methodology—evaluates both conversion-to-trees and direct parsing approaches.
- Strength: clear presentation of UCCA and helpful illustrative figures.
- Strength: implementation will be made publicly available, aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness: novelty and the necessity of BSS criteria (especially non-terminal nodes) are overstated and not convincingly justified.
- Weakness: terminology issues—“grounded” is ambiguous and weakens the argument; suggest alternatives like “anchored” or “lexicalized.”
- Weakness: experimental results are underwhelming—MaltParser beats BSP on primary edge F-score and remote edge F-scores are low.
- Weakness: limited comparison to related work on approximation/reconstruction techniques that could improve conversion baselines.

- Question: which concrete applications specifically benefit from UCCA/BSS structural properties?
- Question: why does MaltParser outperform BSP—feature design, training algorithm, or other causes?
- Question: how sensitive are results to the MINUPDATE training parameter and were alternatives tried?
- Question: can you quantify conversion-induced information loss (e.g., round-trip metric from UCCA → tree → UCCA)?

- Additional comment: feature templates are dense and need clearer explanation of rationale.
- Additional comment: include an error analysis to identify common parser failure modes.
- Additional comment: compare computational complexity of BSP versus conversion-based approaches.
