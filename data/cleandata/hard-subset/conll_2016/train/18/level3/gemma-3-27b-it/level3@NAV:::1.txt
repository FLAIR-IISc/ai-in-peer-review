## Review: Broad-Coverage Semantic Parsing: A Transition-Based Approach

This paper proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA’s rich semantico-cognitive representations, arguing for graph-based semantic annotation supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuity. While the problem addressed—parsing UCCA—is valuable and the attempt to apply transition-based methods is reasonable, the paper suffers from methodological limitations and lacks compelling empirical validation.

The primary contribution, as I see it, is the *application* of transition-based parsing to UCCA structures. This is a reasonable first step, but the proposed parser (BSP) doesn't fundamentally exploit UCCA-specific properties in its transition set. The claim of being the “first semantic-oriented parser” feels overstated; the transitions largely mirror dependency parsing, lacking dedicated mechanisms for UCCA’s unique characteristics. A stronger contribution would have been demonstrating how UCCA’s specific structures necessitate and benefit from novel transition types.

The experimental setup is concerning. The conversion-based baselines, while providing a comparison point, obscure the true potential of direct graph parsing. Moreover, the lack of comparison to existing graph parsing work (e.g., SemEval 2014-2015 SDP tasks, DAGParser, TurboParser) is a significant omission. The conversion to dependency trees loses substantial information, and the paper doesn’t adequately justify this choice beyond ease of leveraging existing parsers.  The evaluation metrics, while internally consistent, are not connected to the broader SDP research community, making it difficult to assess the paper's progress relative to the state-of-the-art.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addressing a relevant problem:** UCCA is a promising semantic representation, and a parser is a crucial component for its usability.
*   **Clear presentation of UCCA:** The paper provides a good introduction to the UCCA annotation scheme.
*   **Initial exploration of transition-based parsing:**  The work provides a starting point for applying transition-based methods to graph-based semantic parsing.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Weak claim of novelty:** The parser's transition set doesn’t leverage UCCA's unique characteristics effectively.
*   **Inadequate baseline comparisons:**  Missing key comparisons to relevant graph parsing systems.
*   **Limited evaluation:** Lack of standard SDP metrics hinders meaningful comparison.
*   **Questionable conversion strategies:** Converting to dependency trees loses important structural information.
*   **Generality of claimed principles:** The asserted principles (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuity) are not specific to semantics.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed justification for not including prior graph-parsing work in the baseline comparisons?
2.  Could remote edge handling be achieved more elegantly through edge labeling rather than adding separate transitions? Please explain why this approach was not explored.
3.  UCCA does not explicitly model quantifier scope. Can you provide an error analysis demonstrating how the parser handles sentences with complex quantifier scope, and what limitations it exhibits? 
4.  For UCCA implicit units that lack textual correspondents, can you provide concrete examples and explain how they are handled (or not handled) by the parser?



**Additional Comments:**

The introduction would benefit from a clearer articulation of the paper's goals. Is it a UCCA parser, a general semantic parser, or a demonstration of a parsable semantic scheme? Consider introducing "grounded semantic" earlier for clarity. The suggestion of using constituent trees with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser as a baseline is worth exploring.
