- Introduces the first semantic parsers for UCCA, addressing multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuity.
- Explores both conversion-based parsing and a novel transition-based parser (BSP) tailored to UCCA.
- Main contribution is establishing an initial benchmark and demonstrating feasibility of transition-based UCCA parsing.
- Public release of code and data for UCCA parsing.
- Analyzes key UCCA challenges, especially handling discontinuities and non-terminal nodes.
- Overstates UCCA as the definitive graph-based semantic representation and neglects alternative SRs.
- Theoretical justification for UCCA design choices (necessity of non-terminals, benefits of being “grounded”) is weak.
- The term “grounded” is overloaded and could be clearer (e.g., “anchored” or “lexicalized”).
- BSP’s performance is only marginally better than simpler conversion-based baselines.
- Low remote-edge F-scores show difficulty with UCCA’s core non-tree aspects.
- Evaluation doesn’t adequately consider the effect of conversion on baseline performance.
- Omits discussion of related methods (e.g., Agic et al., 2015) that could improve baselines via tree approximations and edge reconstruction.
- Question: which specific linguistic phenomena strictly require non-terminal nodes in UCCA?
- Question: what practical downstream advantages does a “grounded” UCCA representation have over more abstract formalisms like AMR?
- Question: how would incorporating Agic et al. (2015) techniques affect baseline dependency parser performance?
- Question: why was beam search not used during BSP inference, despite its common use in transition-based parsers?
