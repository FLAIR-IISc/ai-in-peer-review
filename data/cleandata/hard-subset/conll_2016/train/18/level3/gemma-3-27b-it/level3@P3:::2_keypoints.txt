- Overall: Introduces first UCCA semantic parsers and a new transition-based BSP, but overstates claims and reports unconvincing experimental results.

Contributions:
- BSP parser: Presents a transition-based parser (BSP) tailored to UCCA, providing a baseline for future work.
- Conversion-based study: Systematically evaluates converting UCCA to constituency/dependency trees, revealing information loss and adaptation challenges.
- Task & dataset: Establishes UCCA as a viable benchmark for broad-coverage semantic parsing and draws community attention.

Strengths:
- Novel task focus: Tackles the important gap of parsing graph-based semantic representations like UCCA.
- Reasonable methodology: Uses a well-justified transition-based approach with clear in-domain and out-of-domain evaluation.
- Reproducible implementation: Provides detailed descriptions of BSP transitions and feature engineering.
- Clear conversions: Well-explained conversion procedures with upper-bound analyses demonstrating limitations.

Weaknesses:
- Overstated claims: Introduction incorrectly frames UCCA as the only sensible graph formalism, ignoring AMR, SDP, etc.
- Weak theoretical justification: The three criteria for representation choice and the claimed necessity of non-terminal nodes are not convincingly defended.
- Terminology issues: Repeated use of "grounded" is ambiguous and potentially misleading.
- Underwhelming results: BSP’s overall F-scores are not substantially better than standard dependency parsers; remote-edge performance is poor.
- Incomplete baselines: Lacks comparison to stronger or more recent graph-capable baselines (e.g., GNNs).
- Shallow ablation: Ablation study is present but insufficiently detailed about specific features/transitions.

Questions to authors:
- Why focus on UCCA—what specific limitations of AMR/SDP motivated this choice?
- How do non-terminal nodes provide expressivity that dependency alternatives cannot?
- What parser or feature changes would you pursue to improve remote-edge performance?
- Have you tried graph neural networks for UCCA parsing, and what were the outcomes?
- Please clarify the rationale behind the MINUPDATE and IMPORTANCE training parameters.

Additional comments:
- Perform a more rigorous error analysis to identify common BSP mistakes.
- Include detailed feature templates and exact parser configurations in the supplement.
- Discuss the computational complexity of the BSP parser.
