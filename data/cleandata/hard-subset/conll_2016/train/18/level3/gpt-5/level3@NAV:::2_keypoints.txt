- Summary: Proposes broad-coverage UCCA parsing via (i) conversion to dependencies/constituency plus inverse conversion and (ii) a direct transition-based BSP supporting DAGs, nonterminals, and discontinuities; reports BSP primary-edge F1 ≈59 (LSTM baseline 69.2) and remote-edge F1 ≈13.

- Contribution 1: Direct transition system (BSP) for UCCA/BSS that handles DAGs, nonterminals, discontinuities, with a dynamic-oracle training setup.

- Contribution 2: Practical, bidirectional conversion pipelines (algorithms) between UCCA and constituency/dependency formalisms and an evaluation protocol separating primary and remote edges.

- Contribution 3: Empirical benchmark comparing multiple methods (in-/out-of-domain) and feature ablations on UCCA corpora.

- Strength 1: Clear problem formulation and engineering—BSP transition set, dynamic oracle, and features coherently adapt known techniques to UCCA’s needs.

- Strength 2: Useful conversion baselines and metrics that expose information loss and provide reproducible evaluation for future work.

- Strength 3: Empirical breadth and transparency—several parsers evaluated, domain generalization tested, and feature ablations reported.

- Strength 4: Community value—first systematic UCCA parsing study with released tools likely to catalyze further research.

- Weakness 1: Overstated framing—paper over-privileges UCCA as “the only sensible” graph-based SR and under-discusses alternatives like SDP and AMR.

- Weakness 2: Questionable desiderata—criteria conflate convenience with necessity; nonterminals’ expressive advantage and claims about “grounded” representations over AMR are not demonstrated; term “grounded” is overloaded.

- Weakness 3: Underwhelming results—BSP underperforms simple conversion baselines on primary edges and achieves very low remote-edge F1 (~13), limiting practical impact.

- Weakness 4: Missing stronger baselines—no comparison to tree-approximation + edge-reconstruction methods or modern DAG parsers/neuralized BSP variants.

- Weakness 5: Limited analysis—insufficient fine-grained error analysis by phenomena (coordination, discontinuities, remotes) to link design choices to outcomes.

- Question 1: Provide a more balanced comparison of UCCA vs. SDP/AMR and prefer “anchored/lexicalized” over “grounded”?

- Question 2: Do nonterminals add expressive power or mainly convenience—please illustrate phenomena unrepresentable with bilexical dependencies?

- Question 3: Can BSP be reported with a neural local classifier and/or beam search?

- Question 4: Can you include a tree-approximation + edge-reconstruction baseline (e.g., Agić et al., 2015) to contextualize BSP’s remote-edge performance?
