- Tackles broad-coverage semantic parsing for UCCA, focusing on reentrancies, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Compares conversion-based parsing (map UCCA→trees→parse→convert back) with a direct transition-based graph parser (BSP) supporting SWAP and REMOTE.  
- Reports best conversion-based primary-edge F1 69.2% (LSTM arc-standard); BSP gets ~59% primary F1 and ~13% remote-edge F1, with modest OOD degradation.  
- Provides conversion upper bounds and feature ablations.

- Contribution: empirical showing that conversion + off-the-shelf transition parsers are strong baselines for UCCA.  
- Contribution: a workable transition system and dynamic-oracle training for DAGs with non-terminals, discontinuities, and some remote edges.  
- Contribution: public converters and an evaluation protocol (yield- and label-based) for UCCA.

- Strength: Timely and relevant—first reproducible UCCA parsing tools and results for the community.  
- Strength: Sensible experimental design with in-domain and OOD evaluations, conversion upper bounds, and useful ablations.  
- Strength: Clear, implementable constituency and dependency conversion algorithms with reported upper bounds.  
- Strength: BSP directly predicts reentrancies (remote edges), showing DAG parsing feasibility.  
- Strength: Robustness to domain shift shown by modest OOD performance drops.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty; transition set largely mirrors prior work and REMOTE handling may be ad hoc.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to SemEval SDP systems and strong DAG/Turbo parsers; no SDP-metric reporting.  
- Weakness: Dependency conversion discards non-terminals and remotes, relying on simple heuristics that are lossy.  
- Weakness: Framing unclear—principles are general and relation to AMR/MRS/DRT or scope phenomena is underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative/error analysis by UCCA category, discontinuity type, remote-edge class, and omission of implicit units.

- Question: Why use dedicated LEFT/RIGHT-REMOTE transitions instead of predicting a remoteness attribute—please ablate.  
- Question: Can you re-evaluate with SDP metrics and include competitive SDP systems (TurboParser/DAGParser)?  
- Question: Can you provide richer conversions preserving reentrancies/non-terminal info or compare to parsing-as-reduction approaches?  
- Question: Please add per-category F1 (Scenes, Participants, Linkers, Centers, coordination, MWEs) and analyses for discontinuities and remotes.  
- Question: How will implicit UCCA units be handled in future parsing and what impact are they expected to have?  
- Question: Does UCCA model scope-sensitive phenomena in this layer; how is “broad-coverage” defined regarding scope/control/raising?

- Additional: Introduce “grounded semantic representation” early to clarify token-to-graph alignment.  
- Additional: Add missing UPARSE citation and consider a Berkeley parser baseline with richly labeled non-terminals.  
- Additional: Report dataset statistics per split (rates of discontinuity/remotes) and release preprocessing for reproducibility.  
- Overall: Paper is interesting and valuable for CoNLL but would benefit from stronger baselines, SDP metrics, clearer framing, and deeper analysis.
