- Presents first parsing approaches for UCCA: conversion-based (constituency/dependency) and a direct BSP transition-based parser; conversion LSTM-dependency gets 69.2% F on primary edges, BSP gets 59.0% and recovers some remote edges.  
- Contribution: first practical UCCA parser, enabling downstream use and future baselines.  
- Contribution: comparative study of direct vs. conversion-based parsing with a tripartite experimental setup.  
- Contribution: proposes BSP, a transition system combining mechanisms for non-terminals, discontinuities, and multiple parents.  
- Strength: addresses a timely and important problem by targeting a broad-coverage semantic formalism (UCCA).  
- Strength: BSP is technically sound, combining known techniques (e.g., SWAP, delayed REDUCE) and empirically viable.  
- Strength: thoughtful experimental design comparing three strategies, revealing trade-offs and conversion-imposed ceilings.  
- Strength: high potential impact—establishes a benchmark and makes UCCA computationally accessible.  
- Weakness: missing crucial baselines and comparisons (SemEval SDP tasks, TurboParser, DAGParser, stronger phrase-structure parsers).  
- Weakness: novelty claims are overstated; transition system largely recombines existing structural techniques and the motivation should be framed as a UCCA parser.  
- Weakness: conversion-based baselines are suboptimal—dependency conversion is lossy and constituency baseline could be stronger with richer labels or state-of-the-art parsers.  
- Weakness: unclear justification for four new remote-edge transitions instead of a simpler labeled-edge approach.  
- Question: provide a concrete example of UCCA implicit units with no text correspondent and explain parsing challenges.  
- Question: supply an error analysis for BSP—common error types (remote edges, deep structures, specific edge categories).  
- Question: why not handle remote edges by distinguishing labels (e.g., A-primary vs A-remote) instead of new transitions?  
- Question: why were SemEval SDP metrics and strong SDP baselines not included to better situate the results?
