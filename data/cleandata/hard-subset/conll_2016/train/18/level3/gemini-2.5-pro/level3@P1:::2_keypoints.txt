- Summary: First work on parsing UCCA, proposing conversion-based methods and a direct transition-based parser (BSP) and evaluating them on English UCCA corpora to establish benchmarks.

- Contribution 1: Introduces the first parsers for UCCA via conversion methods and a novel direct BSP parser, making UCCA accessible for automatic processing.

- Contribution 2: Provides an empirical comparison of conversion-based vs direct parsing strategies, establishing benchmarks and insights for future work.

- Strength 1: Tackles a novel, challenging parsing problem combining non-terminals, discontinuities, and DAGs.

- Strength 2: Thorough experimental design: multiple conversion approaches, several state-of-the-art parsers, and in-domain/out-of-domain evaluation.

- Strength 3: BSP is well-motivated and grounded in prior transition-based parsing work, with a clearly specified transition system and thoughtful feature set.

- Weakness 1: BSP is outperformed by a stack-LSTM conversion baseline (primary F 59.0 vs 69.2) and shows very low remote-edge F (13.3%), questioning its practical advantage.

- Weakness 2: Motivation overstates necessity of UCCA properties and presents an unbalanced critique of alternative formalisms like SDP and AMR.

- Weakness 3: Terminology is ambiguous—“grounded” is used non-standardly and should be replaced by clearer terms like “anchored” or “lexicalized.”

- Weakness 4: Missing important baselines and related work—specifically pipeline approaches that parse primary edges then predict remote edges, and prior semantic dependency parsing literature.

- Question 1: Have you considered integrating an LSTM-based classifier (like the stack-LSTM) into BSP to close the performance gap?

- Question 2: Can you clarify why non-terminal nodes represent a fundamental expressive limitation for dependency formalisms rather than an annotation/head-selection convention?

- Question 3: How does BSP compare to a pipeline approach that first parses high-accuracy primary edges (e.g., with the LSTM parser) then predicts remote edges with a separate classifier?
