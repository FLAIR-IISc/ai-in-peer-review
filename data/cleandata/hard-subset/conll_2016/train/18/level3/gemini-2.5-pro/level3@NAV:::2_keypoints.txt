- Introduces the first broad-coverage UCCA parsers using two strategies: conversion-to-tree and a novel transition-based BSP for direct graph parsing.
- Contribution: first empirical study and a publicly available parser for the under-resourced UCCA formalism.
- Contribution: comparative analysis of direct graph parsing versus lossy conversion-to-tree approaches.
- Strength: Task is novel and necessary—UCCA’s unique structural properties merit dedicated parsing work.
- Strength: Methodology is sound; BSP extends established transition-based techniques for discontinuities and DAGs.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is unconvincing—BSP is outperformed on primary edge F-score and achieves very low remote-edge F-score (~13%).
- Weakness: Framing overstates UCCA’s necessity; “groundedness” is overloaded/confusing and advantages over alternatives (e.g., AMR) aren’t clearly demonstrated.
- Weakness: Baselines may be weak; they could be strengthened using prior tree-approximation and edge-reconstruction techniques.
- Question: Why not incorporate a neural classifier (e.g., the LSTM used by the top dependency baseline) into BSP to enable a fairer comparison of transition systems?
