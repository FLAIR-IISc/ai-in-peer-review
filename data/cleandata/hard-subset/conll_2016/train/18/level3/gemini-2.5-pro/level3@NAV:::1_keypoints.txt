- Proposes BSP, the first direct transition-based parser for UCCA, and studies conversion-based alternatives.  
- Contribution 1: BSP implemented as a novel, reproducible baseline for UCCA parsing.  
- Contribution 2: Empirical comparison of direct vs. conversion-based parsing, highlighting information loss from conversions.  
- Strength: Tackles the challenging task of parsing UCCA’s semantic DAGs with non-terminals and discontinuities.  
- Strength: BSP combines and extends transition-based techniques to handle non-projectivity, DAGs, and non-terminals.  
- Strength: Experimental design compares direct parsing against two conversion strategies and reports respectable F-scores.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to relevant semantic dependency parsing (SDP) baselines and standard SDP metrics (e.g., SemEval 2014/2015 parsers).  
- Weakness: Conceptual framing/novelty claims are weak—transition set appears generic rather than exploiting UCCA-specific semantics.  
- Weakness: Conversion-based baselines could be stronger (richer SDP-inspired conversion, stronger constituency parser like Berkeley).  
- Question: Which parts of the parser (transitions, features) are specifically tailored to UCCA semantics versus generic graph structure?  
- Question: Why were established SDP graph parsers omitted as baselines, and how would BSP compare under standard SDP evaluations?  
- Question: Please give an example of an implicit unit in UCCA and explain the challenges it poses for the transition-based framework.
