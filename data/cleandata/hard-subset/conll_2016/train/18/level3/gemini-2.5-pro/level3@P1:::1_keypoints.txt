- Task: parses Broad-coverage Semantic Structures (BSS), focusing on UCCA, supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuity.  
- Approaches: (1) conversion-based—lossily convert UCCA to constituency/dependency trees to use existing parsers; (2) direct—novel transition-based Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP).  
- BSP: extends transition systems for discontinuous constituency and dependency DAG parsing and can recover remote (multiple-parent) edges.  
- Experimental result: stack-LSTM on converted dependency achieves highest F on primary edges; BSP is competitive and uniquely recovers remote edges.  
- Contribution: first parser and empirical evaluation for UCCA, released as an open-source baseline/tool.  
- Contribution: systematic comparison of direct graph parsing vs. conversion-based parsing, highlighting trade-offs and information loss.  
- Strength: tackles a timely, challenging problem of richer graph-structured semantic representations; UCCA is cross-linguistically motivated.  
- Strength: provides a valuable community resource by releasing the first UCCA parser.  
- Strength: thorough experimental setup (in-domain/out-of-domain comparisons, strong baselines, and feature ablation).  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to state-of-the-art semantic graph parsers (SemEval SDP systems, TurboParser) and related evaluation metrics, isolating the work.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—BSP mainly combines existing techniques and treats remote edges via new transitions rather than leveraging semantic-specific insights.  
- Weakness: conversion-based baselines are weak/lossy and omit stronger conversion methods and recent related work, undermining the baseline strength.  
- Weakness: unclear framing and motivation—three BSS principles are structural and not clearly positioned as uniquely semantic; overall goal is not crisply stated.  
- Question: Why were prominent graph-based semantic parsers (SemEval SDP systems, TurboParser) not included as baselines?  
- Question: Could remote edges be handled by adding a 'remote' feature to EDGE labels instead of new LEFT/RIGHT-REMOTEX transitions—why choose current design?  
- Question: Give an example of the "implicit units" from Section 2 and explain why they were excluded and what parsing challenges they pose.  
- Question: Provide an error analysis for BSP: what are the most common error types (attachment, label, discontinuity, etc.)?
