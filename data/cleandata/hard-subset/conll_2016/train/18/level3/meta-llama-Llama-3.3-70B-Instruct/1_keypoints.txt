- Contribution: Proposes a transition-based UCCA parser (BSP) supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Contribution: Explores a conversion-based approach by converting UCCA to dependency and discontinuous constituency trees and training existing parsers.  
- Contribution: Empirical results show UCCA can be parsed with 69.2% primary F-score.  

- Strength: Fills a gap by targeting UCCA, a widely used semantico-cognitive annotation scheme.  
- Strength: Provides a thorough evaluation, comparing approaches and analyzing results.  
- Strength: Emphasizes the need to handle structural properties like reentrancy, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  

- Weakness: Claim of being the first parser for a semantically-oriented scheme is questionable given related prior work.  
- Weakness: Feature-template design is tightly tied to UCCA, potentially limiting generalizability to other schemes.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison with other graph parsers, weakening the completeness of evaluation.  

- Suggestion: Include a more detailed comparison with existing graph parsers and alternative semantic representation schemes.  
- Suggestion: Introduce the notion of "grounded semantic" earlier to strengthen motivation.  
- Suggestion: Use a richer conversion to surface dependency trees to improve conversion-based accuracy.  
- Suggestion: Provide details on handling implicit units in UCCA graphs, which the parser currently ignores.  

- Question: How will implicit units in UCCA graphs be addressed by the authors?  
- Question: Can the authors provide detailed comparisons with other graph formalisms (e.g., MRS, DRT)?  
- Question: How do the authors plan to extend the approach to other languages, given UCCA's cross-linguistic aims?
