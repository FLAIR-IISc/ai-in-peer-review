- Proposes TBuckets to measure LDA topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic "buckets" and scoring based on the largest bucket.
- Implements three techniques: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.
- Evaluated on three public datasets and reported better performance than state-of-the-art methods.
- Contribution: Novel thematic-grouping-based metric (TBuckets) for topic coherence.
- Contribution: Two parameter-free variants (TBuckets-SVD, TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that outperform parameter-tuned baselines.
- Contribution: Empirical improvements on three datasets; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms on two of three.
- Strength: Effectively leverages word embeddings to compute semantic similarity among topic words.
- Strength: Simple, intuitive methodology that is easy to understand and implement.
- Strength: Demonstrates empirical performance gains over existing coherence measures.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to only three public datasets, limiting generalizability.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis explaining underlying principles and limitations.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on word embeddings, which may be unavailable or inaccurate in some domains/languages.
- Question: How will TBuckets be extended to domains or languages without reliable word embeddings?
- Question: Can the authors provide a theoretical analysis to clarify TBuckets' principles and limitations?
- Question: How do the authors plan to evaluate TBuckets on larger, more diverse datasets to demonstrate scalability and robustness?
