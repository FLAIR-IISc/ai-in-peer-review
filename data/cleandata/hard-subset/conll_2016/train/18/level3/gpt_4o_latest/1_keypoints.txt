- Introduces a transition-based graph parser (BSP) for UCCA and proposes three annotation principles: graph structures, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous elements.  
- Explores conversion-based approaches (to dependency/constituency trees) alongside a novel BSP that directly supports UCCA’s structures.  
- Reports empirical results on UCCA corpora achieving nearly 70% labeled F-score for primary edges.  
- Contribution: a novel transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.  
- Contribution: empirical evaluation comparing conversion methods and BSP with both in-domain and out-of-domain tests.  
- Contribution: argues that the three annotation principles generalize beyond UCCA to other representation layers.  
- Strength: addresses an important gap in semantic parsing by targeting UCCA’s unique structural properties.  
- Strength: extensive experiments and robustness checks strengthen empirical claims.  
- Strength: commitment to open-sourcing converters and parsers improves reproducibility and impact.  
- Strength: clear problem definition and motivation for a specialized parser.  
- Weakness: no comparisons to existing graph parsers (e.g., Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins 2015) as baselines.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis, lacking detailed breakdowns (e.g., implicit units, quantifier scope).  
- Weakness: the three proposed principles appear somewhat ad-hoc with weak theoretical grounding.  
- Weakness: conversion to dependency trees is lossy; richer conversion methods were not explored.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with other semantic frameworks (AMR, MRS, DRT) on issues like scope and implicit units.  
- Questions: how are implicit units without textual anchors handled and will BSP model them?  
- Questions: why were existing graph parsers omitted as baselines, and how does BSP fundamentally differ?  
- Questions: what is the theoretical or empirical motivation for the three annotation principles?  
- Questions: why were richer conversion methods for dependency trees not pursued?  
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance with revisions to address baseline comparisons, error analysis, and grounding of the principles.
