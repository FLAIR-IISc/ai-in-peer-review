- Introduces BSP, a novel transition-based parser for UCCA that handles multiple parents, nonterminal nodes, and discontinuous units.  
- Proposes conversion-based methods leveraging existing parsers alongside the direct BSP approach and evaluates both.  
- Releases parsing tools and conversion protocols to the community.

- BSP is novel in explicitly addressing UCCA’s structural features and attempts to handle remote edges.  
- Paper provides a comprehensive evaluation including out-of-domain experiments and comparisons to conversion-based baselines.  
- Public availability of code and data encourages further research and adoption.

- Introduction overstates UCCA as the sole viable graph-based semantic formalism, overlooking alternatives like AMR and SDP.  
- The term “grounded” is used ambiguously and its necessity is not convincingly justified; alternatives like “anchored” or “lexicalized” may be clearer.  
- BSP’s primary-edge performance is comparable to existing parsers and its remote-edge performance is low, weakening claims of superiority.  
- Prior work on dependency graph-to-tree conversion is underexplored and might improve results.

- Request clarification on practical advantages of UCCA’s “grounded” representation over non-grounded formalisms like AMR.  
- Ask whether LSTM-based classifiers were considered for BSP given LSTM parser success in experiments.  
- Query envisioned downstream uses of UCCA parsing (e.g., MT, summarization) and anticipated challenges.

- Overall: Valuable contribution but overstated claims, poor remote-edge performance, and limited comparative evidence mean revisions and parser improvements are needed for stronger impact.
