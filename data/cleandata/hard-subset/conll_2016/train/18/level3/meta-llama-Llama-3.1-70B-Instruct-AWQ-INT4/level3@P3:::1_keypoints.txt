- Proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Introduces a conversion-based parsing approach to evaluate existing technology on UCCA.
- Presents experiments on UCCA parsing showing transition-based methods are effective for the task.

- Novel contribution to semantic parsing by targeting UCCA’s semantico-cognitive representations.
- Thorough evaluation with comparisons to existing parsers and result analysis.
- Clear, well-written presentation and experimental setup.

- Claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser is weak because the transition set does not leverage UCCA-specific phenomena.
- Remote-edge handling may be achievable via label differences instead of new transitions.
- Important prior graph-parsing baselines (SemEval 2014–2015 work, DAG/Turbo parsers) were not used or compared.
- Phrase-based experiments lack comparison to Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins (2015); uparse reference is missing.
- Evaluation omits SDP shared-task metrics, reducing comparability to related work.
- The three proposed principles are generic/ad-hoc; the paper needs a clearer statement of goals (UCCA parser vs. general semantic parser vs. parsable semantic scheme).

- Request details on how the transition set was designed and why it omits UCCA-specific phenomena.
- Ask how remote-edge handling will be addressed in future work.
- Why were prior SemEval graph-parsing works and DAG/Turbo parsers not used as baselines/comparisons?
- How will the authors improve evaluation metrics and include more comparable baselines?
- Request clarification on the three principles and how they relate to the paper’s stated goals.

- Recommend a clearer introduction that sets context and significance of UCCA representations.
- Suggest adding visualizations of the approach and experimental results to aid understanding.
- Advise a more detailed/error analysis to pinpoint areas for improvement.
