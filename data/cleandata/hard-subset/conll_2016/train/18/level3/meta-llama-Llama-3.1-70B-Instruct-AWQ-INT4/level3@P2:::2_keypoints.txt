- Proposes two UCCA-focused parsing strategies: a conversion-based method and a novel transition-based broad-coverage parser (BSP) handling multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Evaluates approaches on UCCA corpora and shows BSP achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art parsers.
- Contribution: first broad-coverage semantic parser for UCCA supporting its unique structural properties.
- Contribution: systematic evaluation of conversion-based approaches to assess existing parser technology for broad-coverage semantic parsing.
- Contribution: introduces BSP with new transitions and features extending transition-based parsing to UCCA phenomena.
- Strength: presents a novel perspective on broad-coverage semantic parsing centered on UCCA.
- Strength: provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation with comparisons and result analysis.
- Strength: work has potential impact on semantic parser development and broad-coverage semantic representations.
- Weakness: paper may overemphasize UCCA’s importance and appear biased toward that scheme.
- Weakness: proposed criteria for broad-coverage semantic representations are arguable and not universally accepted.
- Weakness: evaluation shows BSP is comparable but not clearly superior to state-of-the-art; gains are modest.
- Weakness: BSP exhibits relatively low remote-edge F-scores, raising concerns for some applications.
- Question: How do authors respond to the critique that UCCA is not the only sensible graph-based semantic representation to study?
- Question: Can authors clarify the advantages of “grounded” representations over AMR and their impact on parsing?
- Question: How will authors address BSP limitations, especially low remote-edge F-scores, and have they considered alternative metrics or datasets for further evaluation?
