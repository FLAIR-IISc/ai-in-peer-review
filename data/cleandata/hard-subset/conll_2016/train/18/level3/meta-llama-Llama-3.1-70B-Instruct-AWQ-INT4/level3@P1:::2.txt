**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for Universal Conceptual Cognitive Annotation (UCCA) and evaluates several methods, including a transition-based parser. UCCA is a graph-based semantic representation that allows non-terminal nodes and is "grounded", meaning that text tokens appear as nodes. The paper proposes two complementary parsing strategies: a conversion-based approach and a novel transition-based broad-coverage parser, Broad-coverage Semantic Parser (BSP). The authors evaluate their methods on the English UCCA-annotated corpora and report results that suggest concrete paths for further improvement.

**Main Contributions**

1. **First broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA**: The paper introduces the first parsers that support multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, making a significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing.
2. **Evaluation of several methods**: The authors evaluate multiple parsing strategies, including a conversion-based approach and a novel transition-based parser, providing a comprehensive comparison of different methods for UCCA parsing.
3. **Development of a novel transition-based parser**: The paper presents a novel transition-based parser, BSP, which supports the full set of properties required for broad-coverage semantic representation.

**Strengths**

1. **Significant contribution to the field**: The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing by introducing the first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of multiple parsing strategies, including a conversion-based approach and a novel transition-based parser.
3. **Novel transition-based parser**: The paper presents a novel transition-based parser, BSP, which supports the full set of properties required for broad-coverage semantic representation.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Overstatement of UCCA's uniqueness**: The intro/background section overstates UCCA as the only sensible graph-based semantic representation to study, which may not be entirely accurate.
2. **Disagreement with proposed criteria**: The paper's proposed criteria for semantic representations, including non-terminal nodes and "grounded" representations, may not be universally accepted.
3. **Unimpressive evaluation results**: The evaluation results, particularly the low remote-edge F-scores, may not be as strong as expected.
4. **Lack of comparison to prior work**: The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison to prior work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more context on why UCCA is considered a more suitable semantic representation than other graph-based representations?
2. How do you respond to criticisms that non-terminal nodes may not add expressive power to semantic representations?
3. Can you elaborate on the advantages of "grounded" representations over Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR)?
4. How do you plan to improve the performance of the BSP parser, particularly in terms of remote-edge F-scores?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper could benefit from a more balanced discussion of the advantages and disadvantages of different semantic representations.
2. The authors may want to consider alternative terminology, such as "anchored" or "lexicalized", to describe the "grounded" nature of UCCA.
3. The evaluation results could be strengthened by including more baselines, such as prior work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction.
