- Paper introduces first broad-coverage semantic parsers for UCCA and evaluates multiple methods including a conversion-based approach and a novel transition-based parser (BSP).
- UCCA is a graph-based, grounded semantic representation allowing non-terminal nodes, multiple parents, and discontinuous units.

- Contribution: first parsers supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities for UCCA.
- Contribution: comprehensive evaluation of several parsing strategies for UCCA.
- Contribution: development of BSP, a novel transition-based parser supporting full broad-coverage properties.

- Strength: significant advance in semantic parsing by handling full UCCA properties.
- Strength: thorough empirical comparison of conversion-based and transition-based methods.
- Strength: novel transition-based parser (BSP) that operationalizes broad-coverage parsing requirements.

- Weakness: intro overstates UCCA’s uniqueness among graph-based semantic representations.
- Weakness: proposed criteria (non-terminals, groundedness) may be contentious and not universally accepted.
- Weakness: evaluation results are unimpressive in places, notably low remote-edge F-scores.
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparisons to prior work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction.

- Question: why is UCCA argued to be more suitable than other graph-based representations?
- Question: how do the authors respond to claims that non-terminal nodes do not add expressive power?
- Question: what are the concrete advantages of UCCA’s “grounded” representations over AMR?
- Question: how will BSP’s performance be improved, especially for remote-edge F-scores?

- Comment: paper should present a more balanced discussion of pros and cons of different semantic representations.
- Comment: consider alternative terms like “anchored” or “lexicalized” instead of “grounded.”
- Comment: strengthen evaluation by adding more baselines, e.g., tree-approximation and edge-reconstruction work.
