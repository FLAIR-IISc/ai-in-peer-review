**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a broad-coverage semantic parser for UCCA (Universal Conceptual Cognitive Annotation), a semantic representation scheme that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors propose two transition-based techniques for parsing UCCA structures: conversion-based parsing and a novel transition-based parser called BSP (Broad-coverage Semantic Parser). The paper evaluates the performance of these methods on UCCA-annotated corpora and reports promising results.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Introduction of a broad-coverage semantic parser for UCCA**: The authors propose the first parser that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, making it a significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing.
2. **Evaluation of conversion-based parsing approaches**: The paper assesses the ability of existing technology to tackle the task of UCCA parsing by developing conversion protocols between UCCA structures and related formalisms.
3. **Novel transition-based parser (BSP)**: The authors present a new transition-based parser that supports the three criteria of broad-coverage semantic structures, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Strengths of the Submission**

1. **Significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing**: The paper introduces a broad-coverage semantic parser for UCCA, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation of conversion-based parsing approaches**: The authors provide a thorough evaluation of conversion-based parsing approaches, which helps to understand the strengths and weaknesses of these methods.
3. **Novel transition-based parser (BSP)**: The paper presents a new transition-based parser that supports the three criteria of broad-coverage semantic structures, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

1. **Overemphasis on UCCA as the only sensible graph-based SR**: The introduction and background sections overstate UCCA as the only sensible graph-based semantic representation to study, which may not be entirely accurate.
2. **Disagreement with the three proposed criteria for SRs**: The authors' criteria for SRs may not be universally accepted, and the paper could benefit from a more nuanced discussion of these criteria.
3. **Unimpressive evaluation results**: The evaluation results for BSP are not as impressive as those for other parsers, such as the LSTM parser, which may indicate that BSP still has room for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the limitations of BSP, such as its lower performance compared to other parsers?
2. Can the authors provide more information on the UCCA annotation scheme and its advantages over other semantic representation schemes?
3. How do the authors plan to extend BSP to support other semantic representation schemes, such as AMR or SDP?
