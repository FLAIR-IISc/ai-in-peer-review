- Proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA that handles multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Introduces two parsing strategies: conversion of UCCA to related formalisms for existing parsers, and a direct transition-based parser supporting UCCA properties.
- Demonstrates effectiveness via experiments on UCCA-annotated corpora.
- Contribution: first parser claimed to support UCCA’s multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Contribution: adoption of a transition-based approach inspired by successful syntactic and semantic parsers.
- Contribution: exploration of conversion-based parsing to evaluate existing technology on UCCA.
- Strength: addresses an important and relevant problem with a novel solution.
- Strength: motivation is clear about limitations of existing parsers and the need to support UCCA-specific properties.
- Strength: includes experimental evaluation showing the proposed methods’ effectiveness.
- Weakness: methodological concerns — missing comparable baselines, metrics, and omission of relevant prior graph-parsing work (e.g., SemEval 2014–2015, DAG/Turbo parsers).
- Weakness: empirical contribution questioned — claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser is weak and transition set may not exploit UCCA-specific phenomena.
- Weakness: lack of clarity — introduction and statement of goals are vague and proposed principles seem generic/ad-hoc.
- Question: please clarify the transition set and how it captures UCCA-specific phenomena.
- Question: provide examples or clarification for UCCA implicit units without textual correspondents.
- Question: include an error analysis to assess parser informativeness and parsability.
- Question: consider richer SDP-style conversions (e.g., Schlüter et al., 2014) instead of converting to surface dependency trees that lose information.
- Additional: introduce the notion of “grounded semantic” earlier and clarify goals; consider using constituent trees with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser as an alternative baseline; cite DAGParser (Ribeyre) and TurboParser (André Martins) repositories.
