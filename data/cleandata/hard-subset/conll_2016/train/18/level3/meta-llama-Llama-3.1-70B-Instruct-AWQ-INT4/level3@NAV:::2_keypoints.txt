- Introduces a broad-coverage UCCA semantic parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units, proposing conversion-based methods and a new transition-based BSP with evaluation on UCCA corpora.
- Contribution: first parser that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units for UCCA.
- Contribution: evaluates conversion-based parsing by developing protocols between UCCA and related formalisms.
- Contribution: proposes a novel transition-based parser (BSP) designed for broad-coverage semantic structures.
- Strength: significant advance in semantic parsing by enabling broad-coverage UCCA parsing.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation of conversion-based approaches that clarifies their strengths and weaknesses.
- Strength: BSP is a novel transition system that attempts to meet the three broad-coverage criteria.
- Weakness: overemphasizes UCCA as the only sensible graph-based semantic representation.
- Weakness: the three proposed criteria for semantic representations are presented without sufficient nuance or broad justification.
- Weakness: BSP’s evaluation results are unimpressive compared to other parsers (e.g., LSTM parser), indicating room for improvement.
- Question: how will the authors address BSP’s limitations and performance gap with other parsers?
- Question: can the authors provide more detail on UCCA annotation and its advantages over other semantic schemes?
- Question: do the authors plan to extend BSP to other representation schemes (AMR, SDP), and if so how?
