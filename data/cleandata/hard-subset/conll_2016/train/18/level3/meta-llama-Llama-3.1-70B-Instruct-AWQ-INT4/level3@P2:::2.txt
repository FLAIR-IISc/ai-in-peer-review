**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing, focusing on the Universal Conceptual Cognitive Annotation (UCCA) scheme. The authors propose two complementary parsing strategies: a conversion-based approach, which leverages existing parsers to tackle the task, and a novel transition-based broad-coverage parser (BSP) that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The paper evaluates the effectiveness of these approaches on the UCCA-annotated corpora, demonstrating that BSP achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art parsers.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the first broad-coverage semantic parser for UCCA**: The paper presents a novel parser that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, making it a valuable contribution to the field of semantic parsing.
2. **Evaluation of conversion-based parsing approaches**: The authors assess the ability of existing technology to address the task of broad-coverage semantic parsing, providing insights into the strengths and limitations of these approaches.
3. **Proposal of a novel transition-based parser**: The BSP parser is a significant contribution, as it extends existing transition-based parsers with new transitions and features to support the unique characteristics of UCCA.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to broad-coverage semantic parsing**: The paper presents a fresh perspective on semantic parsing, focusing on the UCCA scheme and its unique characteristics.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approaches, including comparisons with state-of-the-art parsers and analysis of the results.
3. **Potential impact on the field**: The paper's contributions have the potential to impact the development of semantic parsers and the understanding of broad-coverage semantic representations.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Overemphasis on UCCA**: The introduction and background sections overstate the importance of UCCA, which may be perceived as biased.
2. **Disagreement with proposed criteria**: The authors' criteria for broad-coverage semantic representations may not be universally accepted, and alternative perspectives should be considered.
3. **Limited evaluation**: While the evaluation is comprehensive, the results are not overwhelmingly impressive, with BSP's performance being comparable to, but not significantly better than, state-of-the-art parsers.
4. **Low remote-edge F-scores**: The BSP parser's performance on remote edges is relatively low, which may be a concern for certain applications.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you respond to the criticism that UCCA may not be the only sensible graph-based semantic representation to study?
2. Can you provide more insight into the advantages of "grounded" representations over AMR, and how these advantages impact the parsing task?
3. How do you plan to address the limitations of the BSP parser, particularly with regards to remote-edge F-scores?
4. Have you considered exploring alternative evaluation metrics or datasets to further assess the performance of your approaches?
