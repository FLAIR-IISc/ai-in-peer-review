**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA's rich semantico-cognitive representations. The authors argue that semantic annotation should be graph-based, include non-terminal nodes, and allow discontinuity, and claim that these principles are not specific to semantics. They situate their work within broad-coverage semantic parsing and UCCA corpora, and present three experimental setups: constituency-based, dependency-based, and direct transition-based graph parsing.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel transition-based parser that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, which is a significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing.
2. They explore a conversion-based parsing approach to assess the ability of existing technology to address the task, which provides a useful comparison to their proposed parser.
3. The paper is the first to experiment on UCCA parsing, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to semantic parsing that addresses the limitations of existing parsers.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their proposed parser, including a comparison to existing parsers and an analysis of the results.
3. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed parser and the experimental setups.

**Weaknesses**

1. The claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser is weak because the transition set doesn't exploit UCCA-specific phenomena.
2. Remote-edge handling could be achieved via label differences rather than new transitions, which may be a more efficient approach.
3. The paper does not provide a clear statement of goals, and it is unclear whether the authors are proposing a UCCA parser, a general semantic parser, or a parsable semantic scheme.
4. The evaluation does not include metrics from related SDP shared tasks, which weakens the paper.
5. The paper does not provide a comparison to prior graph-parsing work from SemEval (2014-2015) and existing DAG/Turbo parsers, which may be useful baselines.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more information on how the proposed parser handles UCCA implicit units that lack textual correspondents?
2. How does the proposed parser model quantifier scope, and can you provide an error analysis to judge informativeness and parsability?
3. Can you clarify the goals of the paper and whether the proposed parser is intended for UCCA parsing, general semantic parsing, or parsable semantic schemes?
4. How does the proposed parser compare to prior graph-parsing work from SemEval (2014-2015) and existing DAG/Turbo parsers?
5. Can you provide more information on the conversion-based parsing approach and how it compares to the proposed parser?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper could benefit from a clearer introduction that explains the goals and motivations of the work.
2. The authors may want to consider using constituent trees with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser as an alternative baseline.
3. Converting to surface dependency trees loses information, and the authors may want to consider richer SDP-style conversions (Schlüter et al., 2014).
4. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including an error analysis to judge informativeness and parsability.
