**Paper Summary**

The paper "Broad-Coverage Semantic Parsing: A Transition-Based Approach" proposes a transition-based graph parser for parsing Universal Cognitive Conceptual Annotation (UCCA) structures. UCCA is a semantic representation scheme that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units. The authors argue that existing parsers do not support these properties, and therefore, they propose two transition-based techniques for parsing such semantic structures: (1) applying conversion procedures to map them into related formalisms and using existing state-of-the-art parsers on the converted representations, and (2) constructing a parser that directly supports the full set of properties. The authors experiment with UCCA-annotated corpora and demonstrate the effectiveness of transition-based methods for the task.

**Main Contributions**

1. **First parser for UCCA**: The paper proposes the first parser that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units, which are essential properties of UCCA structures.
2. **Transition-based approach**: The authors adopt a transition-based approach, which has been successful in syntactic dependency parsing and other semantic and syntactic settings.
3. **Conversion-based parsing**: The paper explores a conversion-based parsing approach to assess the ability of existing technology to address the task, by converting UCCA structures into related formalisms and using existing parsers on the converted representations.

**Strengths**

1. **Interesting and relevant work**: The paper addresses an important problem in semantic parsing and proposes a novel solution.
2. **Well-motivated**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their work, highlighting the limitations of existing parsers and the importance of supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The paper includes an experimental evaluation of the proposed parser, which demonstrates its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Methodological concerns**: The reviewer raises concerns about the methodological soundness of the paper, including the lack of comparable baselines and metrics, and the absence of prior graph-parsing work from SemEval (2014-2015) and existing DAG/Turbo parsers as baselines or comparisons.
2. **Empirical concerns**: The reviewer questions the empirical contribution of the paper, including the claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser, which is considered weak because the transition set does not exploit UCCA-specific phenomena.
3. **Lack of clarity**: The paper could benefit from a clearer introduction and statement of goals, as the three proposed principles are generic and somewhat ad-hoc.

**Questions to Authors**

1. **Clarify the transition set**: Can the authors provide more details on the transition set and how it exploits UCCA-specific phenomena?
2. **Provide examples for UCCA implicit units**: Can the authors provide examples or clarification on UCCA implicit units that lack textual correspondents?
3. **Error analysis**: Can the authors provide an error analysis to judge the informativeness and parsability of the proposed parser?
4. **Richer SDP-style conversions**: Can the authors consider richer SDP-style conversions (Schlüter et al., 2014) instead of converting to surface dependency trees, which loses information?

**Additional Comments**

1. **Editorial/organization notes**: The paper could benefit from introducing "grounded semantic" earlier and providing a clearer statement of goals.
2. **Alternative baseline**: The authors may consider using constituent trees with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser as an alternative baseline.
3. **References/resources**: The reviewer notes that the paper does not cite DAGParser (Ribeyre) and TurboParser (André Martins) repos, which may be relevant to the work.
