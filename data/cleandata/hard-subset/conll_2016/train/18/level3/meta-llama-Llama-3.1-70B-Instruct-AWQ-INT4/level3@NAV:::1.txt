**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a transition-based graph parser for parsing Universal Conceptual Cognitive Annotation (UCCA) structures, which represent broad-coverage semantic representations. The authors argue that existing parsers do not support the combination of multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units required for UCCA parsing. They propose two approaches: (1) conversion-based parsing, where UCCA structures are converted to constituency trees or dependency trees and parsed using existing parsers, and (2) a novel transition-based parser, Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP), which supports the three criteria of broad-coverage semantic structures. The authors experiment with UCCA-annotated corpora and report results on labeled precision, recall, and F-score for primary and remote edges.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Transition-Based Parser**: The authors propose a novel transition-based parser, BSP, which supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units required for UCCA parsing.
2. **Conversion-Based Parsing Approach**: The authors explore a conversion-based parsing approach, where UCCA structures are converted to constituency trees or dependency trees and parsed using existing parsers.
3. **Experiments on UCCA Parsing**: The authors experiment with UCCA-annotated corpora and report results on labeled precision, recall, and F-score for primary and remote edges.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty of the Approach**: The authors propose a novel transition-based parser, BSP, which supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units required for UCCA parsing.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach using a comprehensive set of metrics, including labeled precision, recall, and F-score for primary and remote edges.
3. **Comparison with Existing Parsers**: The authors compare their approach with existing parsers, including UPARSE and MaltParser, and report competitive results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Methodological Concerns**: The authors' claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser is weak, as the transition set does not exploit UCCA-specific phenomena.
2. **Lack of Baselines**: The authors do not use existing dependency DAG parsers as baselines or comparisons.
3. **Evaluation Metrics**: The authors' evaluation metrics are limited to labeled precision, recall, and F-score for primary and remote edges, and do not include metrics from related SDP shared tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the UCCA-specific features used in the BSP parser?
2. How do you plan to address the methodological concerns raised by the reviewer, particularly with regards to the transition set not exploiting UCCA-specific phenomena?
3. Can you provide more information on the conversion procedures used to convert UCCA structures to constituency trees or dependency trees?
4. How do you plan to extend the evaluation metrics to include metrics from related SDP shared tasks?
