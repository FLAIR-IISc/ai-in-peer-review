- Proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA supporting non-terminals, discontinuity, and multiple parents.
- Situates work in broad-coverage semantic parsing and compares constituency-, dependency-, and direct transition-based setups.
- Novel parser design that explicitly supports multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.
- Uses a conversion-based parsing approach to benchmark existing technology against the direct parser.
- First reported experiments on UCCA parsing.
- Strength: addresses limitations of existing parsers with a novel approach.
- Strength: provides a thorough evaluation including comparisons and result analysis.
- Strength: paper is well-written and explanations are clear.
- Weakness: claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser is weak because the transition set isn’t UCCA-specific.
- Weakness: remote-edge handling may be achievable via label differences rather than new transitions.
- Weakness: paper lacks a clear statement of goals (UCCA parser vs general semantic parser vs parsable scheme).
- Weakness: evaluation omits metrics from related SDP shared tasks.
- Weakness: no comparison to prior SemEval (2014–2015) graph-parsing work or DAG/Turbo parsers.
- Question: How does the parser handle UCCA implicit units that have no textual correspondents?
- Question: How is quantifier scope modeled, and can an error analysis show informativeness and parsability?
- Question: Clarify whether the intended goal is UCCA-specific parsing, general semantic parsing, or proposing a parsable scheme.
- Question: How does the parser compare empirically to SemEval/DAG/Turbo baselines?
- Question: Provide more details on the conversion-based parsing approach and its comparison to the proposed parser.
- Additional: Paper would benefit from a clearer introduction stating goals and motivations.
- Additional: Consider using constituent trees with rich node labels and the Berkeley parser as an alternative baseline.
- Additional: Converting to surface dependency trees loses information; consider richer SDP-style conversions (e.g., Schlüter et al., 2014).
- Additional: Include a more detailed error analysis to assess informativeness and parsability.
