This paper proposes a transition-based graph parser for UCCA's rich semantico-cognitive representations, which requires structural properties beyond those commonly used for syntactic parsing. The authors claim that semantic annotation should be graph-based, include non-terminal nodes, and allow discontinuity, and that these principles are not specific to semantics. The paper situates its work within broad-coverage semantic parsing and UCCA corpora.

The main contributions of this paper are:

1. A transition-based graph parser that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
2. A conversion-based parsing approach to assess the ability of existing technology to address the task.
3. Experiments on UCCA parsing, which demonstrate the effectiveness of transition-based methods for the task.

Strengths:

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to parsing UCCA's rich semantico-cognitive representations, which is a significant contribution to the field of semantic parsing.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison with existing parsers and an analysis of the results.
3. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and the experimental setup.

Weaknesses:

1. The claim of being the first semantic-oriented parser is weak because the transition set doesn't exploit UCCA-specific phenomena.
2. Remote-edge handling could be achieved via label differences rather than new transitions.
3. Prior graph-parsing work from SemEval (2014-2015) and existing DAG/Turbo parsers were not used as baselines or comparisons.
4. Phrase-based experiments should compare to Fernandez-Gonzalez & Martins (2015); uparse reference is missing.
5. Evaluation should include metrics from related SDP shared tasks; lack of comparable baselines and metrics weakens the paper.
6. The three proposed principles are generic and somewhat ad-hoc; the paper needs a clearer, broader introduction and clearer statement of goals (UCCA parser vs. general semantic parser vs. parsable semantic scheme).

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide more details on how the transition set was designed and why it doesn't exploit UCCA-specific phenomena?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of remote-edge handling in future work?
3. Can you explain why you didn't use prior graph-parsing work from SemEval (2014-2015) and existing DAG/Turbo parsers as baselines or comparisons?
4. How do you plan to improve the evaluation metrics and include more comparable baselines in future work?
5. Can you provide more details on the three proposed principles and how they relate to the goals of the paper?

Additional Comments:

* The paper could benefit from a clearer introduction that sets the context for the proposed approach and explains the significance of UCCA's rich semantico-cognitive representations.
* The authors should consider including more visualizations of the proposed approach and the experimental results to help illustrate the key points.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including an error analysis to identify areas for improvement.
