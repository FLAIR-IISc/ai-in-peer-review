Contributions:
- Introduces BSP, a transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units for broad-coverage semantic parsing.
- Evaluates several conversion-based parsing methods, including applying existing parsers to converted representations.
- Experiments on UCCA-annotated corpora to assess transition-based methods for broad-coverage semantic parsing.

Strengths:
- Novel parser that handles the three key phenomena (multiple parents, non-terminals, discontinuity).
- Thorough comparison of conversion-based approaches, clarifying strengths and weaknesses.
- Uses UCCA corpora, offering a valuable testbed for broad-coverage semantic parsing methods.

Weaknesses:
- Introduction/background overstates UCCA as the only sensible graph-based semantic formalism; discussion is unbalanced.
- Authors' rejection of the three proposed criteria (non-terminals, groundedness, discontinuity) is insufficiently justified.
- The term "grounded" is overloaded and should be reconsidered (e.g., "anchored" or "lexicalized").
- Parser implementation is acceptable but evaluation is unimpressive: MaltParser outperforms BSP on primary edges and remote-edge F-scores are low.
- Paper omits discussion of prior work on tree approximations and edge-reconstruction that could strengthen dependency-tree baselines.

Questions to authors:
- Can you detail the advantages and disadvantages of the three proposed criteria for semantic representations?
- How will you address the overloaded term "grounded" and possible alternative terminology?
- Can you provide more details on the BSP parser implementation and feature set?
- How do you plan to improve BSP evaluation, especially remote-edge F-scores?
- Can you discuss tree-approximation and edge-reconstruction prior work and its potential impact on results?

Additional comments:
- Paper needs a more detailed related-work discussion covering alternative semantic parsing approaches.
- Provide clearer explanation of the UCCA annotation scheme and how it differs from other representations.
- Include more comprehensive evaluation and error analysis to identify and address parser weaknesses.
