- Proposes a CNN classifier (based on Johnson & Zhang 2015) for in-domain data selection in SMT, yielding about +0.5 BLEU over LM-based selection and robustness with very small in-domain data.  
- Claims of +3.1 BLEU are misleading: large gains occur only when in-domain data is in the training set, and training only on in-domain data already gives +2.8 BLEU; recommend comparing to interpolated in-/out-domain models.  
- Second experiment (CNN effective with very little in-domain data) is the most compelling result but was run only on zh→en where in-domain data exists in training; question whether this holds for tasks without in-domain training data.  
- Recommend reporting results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to better support the small-data claim.  
- Raises concern about social-media-specific issues (high OOV rate, usernames, hashtags, emoticons) and asks whether any special preprocessing or filtering was applied.  
- Notes data selection cannot address OOVs and requests concrete examples showing what kinds of improvements data selection produces.  
- Section 3.2 needs clarification: how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined (an architecture illustration would help) and what the “designated loss function” was.  
- Section 5.2 should state how system weights were tuned for completeness.
