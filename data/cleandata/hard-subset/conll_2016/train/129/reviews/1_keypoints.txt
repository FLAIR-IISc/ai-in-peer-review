- Proposes MT training data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN classifier.
- Good comparison to prior continuous and n-gram LM approaches, but missing comparison to bilingual data selection (e.g., sum/difference of cross-entropies).
- Motivation for using a CNN over RNN/LSTM was unclear initially; paper argues CNN captures important sentence sections.
- No experiments demonstrating whether BOW, SEQ, or their combination is most important or why.
- Textual description of CNN (one-hot and pre-trained embeddings) is clear, but a layer diagram is missing.
- Minor writing issue: inconsistent/incorrect parenthetical citation style (\citet vs \citep).
- Unclear validation procedure for choosing number of selected in-domain sentences and CNN hyperparameter selection; sensitivity to these choices not reported.
- Table 2 should compare methods at the same number of selected sentences (Figure 1 suggests the proposed method still outperforms).
- Request experiment comparing methods when more in-domain data is available, not only the development set.
- Suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to support Section 5.4 claims.
- Recommend comparing to Axelrod (2015) that uses POS replacement to reduce LM sparsity to test word2vec advantage.
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and Lewis-Moore sum/difference of cross-entropies; recommend citing that work.
- Suggest extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to learn weights for combining source and target scores.
