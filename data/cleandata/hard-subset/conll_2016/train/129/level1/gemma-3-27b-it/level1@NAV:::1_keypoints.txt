- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to select in-domain sentence pairs for SMT using pre-trained word embeddings to compensate for tiny labeled in-domain data.  
- Primary contribution: demonstrates effective data selection with as few as 100 labeled in-domain sentences.  
- Secondary contribution: empirically validated across four language directions and multiple informal domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook), showing robustness.  
- Strength: yields consistent, significant BLEU improvements (up to 3.1) over strong baselines including LM-based methods.  
- Strength: directly addresses the critical problem of adaptation with extremely limited in-domain resources.  
- Strength: leverages pre-trained embeddings effectively to mitigate labeled-data scarcity.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation with multiple baselines and ablation studies varying in-domain data amounts.  
- Weakness: CNN architecture is relatively standard; main novelty is application to low-resource data selection rather than model design.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis explaining which sentence types are selected and why the method works.  
- Weakness: does not explore sensitivity to CNN hyperparameters or embedding dimensionality.  
- Question: What types of sentences or linguistic features does your method select compared to LM-based baselines?  
- Question: What is the computational cost of training the CNN selection model versus LM-based selection models?  
- Question: Have you tried alternative methods for combining source and target sentence scores beyond simple summation?
