- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN for SMT data selection using pre-trained word embeddings to handle extremely limited in-domain data.  
- Main contribution: practical domain adaptation method that achieves up to +3.1 BLEU with as little as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Secondary contribution: empirically shows CNNs with pre-trained embeddings can learn meaningful domain-classification representations from minimal labels.  
- Strength: tackles a significant, well-motivated low-resource domain adaptation problem.  
- Strength: strong empirical improvements across four language pairs and three domains, outperforming strong baselines.  
- Strength: demonstrates scalability and robustness to very small in-domain datasets; ablation study supports this.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured writing and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of CNN architecture choices and hyperparameter optimization.  
- Weakness: reliance on pre-trained embeddings may cause sensitivity to embedding quality and vocabulary coverage.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis to reveal types of remaining failures.  
- Weakness: baseline comparisons could include more recent neural data-selection methods beyond the RNNLM baseline.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to different pre-trained embeddings and missing vocabulary coverage?  
- Question: what is the computational cost of training the CNN versus the baseline language models?  
- Question: what was the hyperparameter optimization process (layers, filter sizes, etc.)?  
- Question: can the authors provide examples of misclassified sentence pairs and explanations?  
- Additional: reference list is comprehensive and significance testing using bootstrap resampling is appropriate.  
- Overall: strong, convincing paper with valuable contributions that warrant acceptance despite some areas for further analysis.
