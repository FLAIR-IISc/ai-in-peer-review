- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN using pre-trained word embeddings for data selection in SMT when only a tiny amount (as few as 100 sentences) of in-domain data is available.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over strong baselines across four language pairs and three test domains (SMS, Tweets, Facebook).
- Main contribution: effective domain adaptation with extremely limited in-domain data, enabling adaptation to niche/emerging domains.
- Main contribution: successful application of semi-supervised CNNs leveraging pre-trained embeddings to identify in-domain sentence pairs.
- Main contribution: strong empirical validation showing notable gains in low-resource scenarios.
- Strength: addresses a critical, common real-world problem of domain adaptation with minimal resources.
- Strength: novel combination of CNNs and pre-trained embeddings for SMT data selection.
- Strength: comprehensive, well-designed experiments with diverse domains and strong baselines.
- Strength: clear, concise presentation and well-documented experimental setup.
- Strength: method shows scalability and robustness, with reasonable stability in 100-sentence experiments.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of CNN architecture choices (filter sizes, pooling, depths) and their effects.
- Weakness: lacks qualitative error analysis to understand types of sentences misclassified and failure modes.
- Weakness: limited exploration/discussion of different pre-trained embeddings or embedding fine-tuning impacts.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to more recent data selection approaches (e.g., NMT-based or advanced representation learning).
- Question: how were negative samples selected for CNN training, and was active learning or hard negative mining used?
- Question: what is the computational cost of training the CNN model compared to LM-based data selection methods?
- Question: how was it verified that test domains had no in-domain data in the general training data, and was overlap checked?
- Request: provide example sentence pairs correctly/incorrectly classified by the CNN with explanations to illustrate model behavior.
- Additional suggestion: include discussion of limitations and future work beyond current points.
- Additional suggestion: provide a table of key CNN hyperparameters used in training.
