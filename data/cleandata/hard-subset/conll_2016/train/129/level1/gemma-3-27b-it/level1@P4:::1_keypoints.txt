- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier to score and select general-domain sentence pairs for SMT adaptation using pre-trained word embeddings.
- Main contributions: semi-supervised CNN for low-resource SMT data selection, effectiveness with as few as 100 in-domain sentences, and extensive evaluation across 4 language pairs and 3 domains.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results with consistent BLEU improvements over strong baselines (e.g., up to 3.1 BLEU on Zh→En sms).
- Demonstrates effectiveness with extremely limited in-domain data; ablation study supports the 100-sentence claim.
- Comprehensive cross-lingual and cross-domain evaluation, supporting generalizability.
- Sound, well-motivated methodology combining CNNs, pre-trained embeddings, and one-hot features; implementation details are adequate.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty in the CNN architecture itself; novelty is mainly in the application/context.
- Lacks a detailed error analysis explaining selection mistakes and model behavior.
- Baselines (LM-based) are somewhat dated; unclear comparison to newer neural data-selection methods.
- No sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (architecture, embedding size, window size).

Questions to authors:
- Please provide detailed error analysis: what sentence types are commonly misclassified and why?
- How sensitive are results to the choice of pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. fastText)?
- Have you compared against more recent neural data-selection baselines; if so, how does your method perform?
- Was summing source and target scores empirically validated as the best combination strategy; please provide experimental details.

Additional comments:
- Writing is clear but a few sentences could be rephrased for readability.
- References are comprehensive and up-to-date.
- Include a discussion of computational cost of the proposed approach versus baselines.
