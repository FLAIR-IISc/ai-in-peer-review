- Proposed method: semi-supervised CNN classifier to distinguish in-domain vs out-of-domain sentence pairs and select relevant general-domain data for SMT adaptation.  
- Core idea: train CNN with pre-trained word embeddings plus one-hot features to generalize from very small in-domain samples.  
- Primary contribution: demonstrates CNNs + semi-supervised learning can effectively perform data selection with extremely limited in-domain resources.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical finding that as few as 100 in-domain sentences can suffice to train a useful selection model.  
- Experimental validation: consistent improvements over strong baselines across four language directions and three domains (e.g., +3.1 BLEU on Chinese→English SMS).  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important practical problem: domain adaptation with severely limited in-domain data.  
- Strong empirical results with significance testing across multiple language pairs and domains.  
- Effective use of semi-supervised learning and pre-trained embeddings to generalize from scarce labeled data.  
- Demonstrates robustness down to very small in-domain datasets (≈100 sentences).  
- Comprehensive experiments varying in-domain data size and comparing several baselines.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited analysis of CNN architecture choices (layers, filter sizes, pooling) and rationale for hyperparameters.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis explaining which sentences are misclassified or why selection fails.  
- Heavy dependence on pre-trained word embeddings without experiments on embedding variation or sensitivity.  
- Baseline comparison omits recent NMT-based domain adaptation methods (e.g., fine-tuning pre-trained NMT), focusing on SMT.  

Questions to authors (key asks):
- Did you explore different CNN architectures (layers, filter sizes, pooling) and their effects?  
- Can you provide an error analysis: what sentence types are commonly misclassified?  
- How sensitive are results to the choice or training corpus of pre-trained embeddings?  
- How would your approach compare to state-of-the-art NMT domain adaptation (fine-tuning pre-trained NMT models)?  
- Is implementation/code available and are there more implementation details you can share?  

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written and method clearly explained.  
- Related work section provides a good overview of existing adaptation techniques.  
- Experimental setup and result presentation are clear and concise.  
- Consider adding visualizations (e.g., learned CNN filters) to provide insight into learned features.
