- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN framework that selects domain-relevant training data using as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Shows the method outperforms state-of-the-art language model–based data selection, giving up to +3.1 BLEU with only 3% of training data.  
- Validates effectiveness even when the general-domain corpus contains no in-domain component.  
- Addresses an important practical MT adaptation problem where in-domain data is scarce.  
- Provides compelling, statistically significant improvements across four language directions and multiple informal domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook).  
- Demonstrates value for fine-grained topic adaptation by working with only 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Lacks comparison to simpler text-classification baselines (e.g., TF-IDF or bag-of-words).  
- Does not show that the 100 in-domain sentences were randomly sampled, raising selection-bias concerns.  
- Focuses solely on SMT and omits comparisons or evaluations on neural machine translation systems.  
- Fails to justify why CNNs are necessary as opposed to other deep-learning architectures.  
- Question: Were the 100 in-domain sentences randomly sampled from the dev set to avoid bias?  
- Question: Have you considered applying or evaluating the approach on NMT systems?  
- Question: Can the method be extended to work with fewer than 100 in-domain sentences?
