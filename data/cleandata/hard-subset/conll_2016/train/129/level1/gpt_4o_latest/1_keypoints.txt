Introduces a semi-supervised CNN method to score and select domain-relevant general-domain sentences for SMT using minimal in-domain data.

Semi-supervised CNN leverages pretrained word embeddings with convolution and max-pooling to highlight domain-specific features for data selection.

Method yields up to 3.1 BLEU improvement and works with as few as 100 in-domain sentences, suiting fine-grained or emerging domains.

Validated across four language pairs and multiple domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook), outperforming baselines and LM-based selection methods.

Significant, statistically validated BLEU gains over strong baselines, particularly in low-resource settings.

Robust performance even with very small in-domain datasets (≈100 sentences).

Novel application of CNNs (with embeddings, convolution, and pooling) to SMT domain adaptation.

Thorough experimental evaluation with comparisons and significance testing.

Insufficient analysis of computational efficiency and runtime versus LM-based selection for large datasets.

Relies on high-quality pretrained word embeddings, limiting applicability to truly low-resource languages or domains.

No ablation studies to isolate the contributions of embeddings, convolution layers, and pooling.

Question: How does training and selection computational cost compare to LM-based methods for large datasets?

Question: Can the method be extended to settings without pretrained embeddings, and what is the expected performance impact?

Question: Were alternative architectures (e.g., transformers) explored and how do they compare to CNNs?

Addressing computational efficiency and architectural choices would further strengthen the work.
