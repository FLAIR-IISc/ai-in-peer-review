- Summary: Proposes a semi-supervised CNN that uses tiny in-domain positives and equal-number negatives from general-domain data to score and select general-domain sentence pairs for MT training, showing gains across four language directions and three domains.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a semi-supervised CNN data-selection method that leverages both labeled in-domain and unlabeled general-domain data.
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates substantial BLEU improvements using very limited in-domain data (as few as 100 sentences).
- Contribution 3: Shows robustness to significant domain mismatch by selecting suitable in-domain-like sentences from general-domain corpora.
- Strength 1: Leverages word embeddings trained on large general-domain data to capture semantic relationships for better selection.
- Strength 2: Handles out-of-vocabulary words via embeddings, improving generalization to unseen terms.
- Strength 3: Empirically outperforms strong baselines, including state-of-the-art language model–based selection methods.
- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to four language directions and three domains, leaving generalizability uncertain.
- Weakness 2: No comparison to other CNN-based data-selection approaches, so relative performance vs. similar methods is unclear.
- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter effects, making optimization guidance limited.
- Question 1: How will the method be extended and validated on additional language pairs and domains?
- Question 2: How do the authors plan to compare against other CNN-based data-selection techniques?
- Question 3: Can the authors provide a thorough analysis of hyperparameter impact on performance?
- Additional comment: Authors should report implementation details (specific hyperparameters, compute resources) and selection-quality metrics (precision, recall, F1) for the chosen sentences.
