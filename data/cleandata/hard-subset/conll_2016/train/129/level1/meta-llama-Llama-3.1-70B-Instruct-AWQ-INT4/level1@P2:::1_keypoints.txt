- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to select in-domain training data for SMT using few in-domain examples.  
- Trains CNN with small in-domain positive samples and equal-sized randomly sampled negatives from general-domain data.  
- Uses the trained CNN to score and select top-scoring sentence pairs from general-domain data for SMT training.  
- Evaluated on four language directions and three test domains, reporting up to 3.1 BLEU improvement.  
- Contribution: a semi-supervised CNN framework for data selection in SMT.  
- Contribution: demonstrates effectiveness even with tiny in-domain data (e.g., 100 sentence pairs).  
- Contribution: outperforms three state-of-the-art language model–based data selection methods by BLEU.  
- Strength: method effectively selects in-domain data and improves SMT performance.  
- Strength: approach is robust across different amounts of in-domain data.  
- Strength: provides thorough comparison against LM-based baselines.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope (only 4 language directions and 3 domains).  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the learned word embeddings and their role.  
- Weakness: no discussion of computational cost or scalability for large-scale SMT.  
- Question: please provide more details on the learned word embeddings and their contribution.  
- Question: how does the computational cost compare to other data selection methods?  
- Question: have you evaluated the approach on additional language pairs and domains?  
- Additional: paper is clearly written and experiments are well-analyzed.  
- Additional: would benefit from more discussion on embeddings and computational cost.
