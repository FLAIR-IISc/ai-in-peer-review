- Proposes semi-supervised CNNs for domain adaptation in SMT that use tiny amounts of in-domain data to select relevant sentence pairs from general-domain corpora.  
- Trains CNN classifier with few positive in-domain samples and randomly sampled negatives from general-domain data to rank similarity.  
- Uses pre-trained word embeddings from large general-domain data as input features to the CNN.  
- Selected similar sentence pairs are used to train an SMT system and evaluated on a test set.  
- Main contribution: semi-supervised CNN method enabling domain adaptation with very small in-domain datasets.  
- Main contribution: leveraging word embeddings learned from general-domain data to boost CNN classification.  
- Main contribution: a data selection pipeline that filters general-domain sentence pairs for SMT training.  
- Strength: effective with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs.  
- Strength: improves SMT performance over baseline systems trained on large general-domain data.  
- Strength: demonstrated robustness across multiple language pairs and domains, including social media.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a small set of language pairs and domains, possibly not generalizable.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other deep learning domain-adaptation approaches.  
- Weakness: no analysis of the learned word embeddings or their contribution to model performance.  
- Question: provide more details on how word embeddings are learned and integrated into the CNN.  
- Question: clarify hyperparameter selection (layers, neurons) and training choices for the CNN.  
- Question: provide broader analysis across language pairs/domains and error analysis of the system.
