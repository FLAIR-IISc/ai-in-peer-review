- Summary: Proposes semi-supervised CNNs to select in-domain training sentences for SMT by using few in-domain positives, equal random negatives, scoring general-domain data, and selecting top-scoring sentences for MT training.

- Contribution: Novel application of semi-supervised CNNs for in-domain data selection in statistical machine translation.

- Contribution: Demonstrates effective selection with very limited in-domain data.

- Contribution: Reports state-of-the-art BLEU performance versus language-model-based selection.

- Strength: Leverages pre-trained word embeddings to boost CNN performance.

- Strength: Method shown to be robust when in-domain data is scarce.

- Strength: Empirical BLEU improvements over LM-based baselines.

- Weakness: Evaluation only on four language directions, limiting generality.

- Weakness: No comparisons to other deep learning architectures (RNNs, transformers).

- Weakness: No analysis of computational complexity or scalability.

- Question: Provide more details on CNN hyperparameter tuning?

- Question: How will the approach be extended to other language pairs and domains?

- Question: Please provide a detailed analysis of computational complexity and resource requirements?
