Summary: Proposes a semi-supervised CNN that uses few in-domain positives and equal random negatives to score general-domain sentence pairs and select top examples to train SMT, yielding up to 3.1 BLEU gains across multiple languages/domains.

Main contribution 1: A semi-supervised CNN data-selection method effective when only a tiny amount of in-domain data is available.

Main contribution 2: Leverages word embeddings learned from large general-domain data to improve CNN classification and capture domain information.

Main contribution 3: Large-scale experimental evaluation across four language directions and three test domains, showing improvements over strong baselines.

Strength 1: Effectively selects small high-quality training subsets that can outperform systems trained on all general-domain data.

Strength 2: Delivers significant BLEU improvements over strong baselines (up to 3.1 BLEU on Zh→En SMS).

Strength 3: Demonstrates stability with very small in-domain sets (e.g., 100 pairs: avg BLEU 25.03, std 0.12).

Strength 4: Outperforms state-of-the-art language model–based data selection methods.

Strength 5: Provides extensive large-scale experimental validation across multiple languages and domains.

Weakness 1: Limited analysis of the CNN architecture and its component contributions.

Weakness 2: No comparison with other deep learning–based data-selection approaches.

Weakness 3: Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability.

Weakness 4: Evaluation is limited to SMT tasks; no experiments on other NLP tasks.
