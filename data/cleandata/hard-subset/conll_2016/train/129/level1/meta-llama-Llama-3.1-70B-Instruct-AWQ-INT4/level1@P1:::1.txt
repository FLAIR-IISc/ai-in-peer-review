**Summary**

The paper proposes a semi-supervised convolutional neural network (CNN) approach for selecting in-domain training data for statistical machine translation (SMT) when only a tiny amount of in-domain data is available. The approach uses a small amount of in-domain data as positive samples and randomly selects the same number of sentences from the general-domain training data as negative samples to train a CNN classification model. The trained CNN model is then used to classify the sentence pairs in the general-domain data, and the top-scoring sentence pairs are selected to train the SMT system. The authors evaluate their approach on four language directions with three test domains and compare it with strong baseline systems trained with large amounts of data. The results show that their approach can improve the performance of SMT systems by up to 3.1 BLEU.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Semi-supervised CNN approach for data selection**: The authors propose a novel approach for selecting in-domain training data for SMT using a semi-supervised CNN. This approach is particularly effective when only a tiny amount of in-domain data is available.
2. **Use of word embeddings for text classification**: The authors use word embeddings learned from a large amount of general-domain data to improve the performance of the CNN classifier. This is a key contribution, as it allows the CNN to capture important domain information from the words in the window.
3. **Experimental evaluation on large-scale SMT tasks**: The authors evaluate their approach on four language directions with three test domains and compare it with strong baseline systems trained with large amounts of data. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in improving the performance of SMT systems.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of limited in-domain data**: The authors' approach is able to select a small amount of training data that is used to create a system which outperforms baseline systems trained with all the general-domain data.
2. **Improved performance over strong baselines**: The authors' approach achieves significant improvements over strong baseline systems trained with large amounts of data, with up to 3.1 BLEU improvement on the Chinese-to-English sms task.
3. **Stability of the approach**: The authors demonstrate that their approach is stable even when the in-domain set is very small, with an average BLEU score of 25.03 and a standard deviation of 0.12 when using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to train the classification model.
4. **Comparison with state-of-the-art methods**: The authors compare their approach with state-of-the-art language model-based data selection methods and demonstrate that their approach achieves better performance.
5. **Large-scale experimental evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on four language directions with three test domains, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach in improving the performance of SMT systems.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited analysis of the CNN architecture**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the CNN architecture and its components, which could be useful in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
2. **Lack of comparison with other deep learning-based approaches**: The authors do not compare their approach with other deep learning-based approaches for data selection, which could be useful in understanding the relative strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
3. **Limited discussion of the computational complexity**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of the computational complexity of their approach, which could be useful in understanding the scalability of their approach.
4. **Limited evaluation on other tasks**: The authors only evaluate their approach on SMT tasks, and do not provide an evaluation on other tasks, such as text classification or sentiment analysis.
