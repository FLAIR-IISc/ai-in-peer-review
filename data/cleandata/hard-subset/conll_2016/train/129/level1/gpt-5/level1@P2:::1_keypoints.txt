- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to score/select in-domain-like sentence pairs from large general-domain parallel corpora using one-hot region reps plus word2vec-derived region embeddings, scoring source and target separately and combining scores.  
- Contribution 1: Semi-supervised CNN selection outperforms strong LM-based baselines and “train on all data” across 4 language directions and 3 social-media domains, giving up to +3.1 BLEU while using ~2.5–10% of data.  
- Contribution 2: Method remains effective in extreme small in-domain regimes (100–800 sentences), with low variance and clear advantage over LM methods and one-hot CNN.  
- Contribution 3: Provides a practical recipe using separate source/target CNNs, summed scores, reasonable hyperparameters, and public toolkits.  
- Strength: Strong, multi-setting empirical gains with careful significance testing and non-trivial BLEU improvements on realistic SMT tasks.  
- Strength: Convincing small-data experiments showing stability at very low in-domain sample sizes and tiny run-to-run variance.  
- Strength: Appropriate and broad baselines (n-gram, RNNLM, combinations) and a strong phrase-based SMT pipeline reduce chance of backbone artifacts.  
- Strength: Clear, plausible intuition for why convolution + pooling + pretrained embeddings capture domain-diagnostic patterns and generalize.  
- Weakness: Limited ablations — missing simpler discriminative baselines (e.g., logistic/SVM on BoW or char n-grams) and other architectural/component ablations.  
- Weakness: Possible confound in zh→en sms due to a 120K in-domain subset in the pool; overlap with selected data not quantified.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps — missing optimizer, LR, batch size, epochs, regularization, early stopping, class rebalancing details, and no released code/models.  
- Weakness: LM baselines may be weak/unfair — n-gram and RNNLM hyperparameters and stronger settings (e.g., Kneser-Ney, larger n-grams) not explored.  
- Weakness: Computational cost not reported — scoring millions of pairs with CNNs may be expensive; training/inference time and hardware absent.  
- Question: What held-out data were used to choose the number of selected sentences, and how was overfitting avoided if the dev set served as positive labels?  
- Question: For zh→en, how many selected sentences came from the 120K in-domain subset and what is performance if that subset is excluded?  
- Question: Please provide ablations for target-only vs source-only vs sum, disabling semi-supervised embeddings, region sizes other than 5, and a simple linear BoW classifier.  
- Question: What are the CNN training hyperparameters (optimizer, epochs, batch size, dropout/regularization), and how sensitive are results to them?  
- Question: How long does scoring the full corpus take per language direction and on what hardware?  
- Overall assessment: Important, practical problem addressed with a simple, well-motivated method and solid empirical gains, but needs ablations, dataset clarification, and reproducibility details to be a strong accept.
