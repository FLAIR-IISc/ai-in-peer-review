- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN pipeline that scores source and target sides to select parallel sentences for SMT domain adaptation with very small in-domain data.
- Trains CNN with limited labeled in-domain examples plus random negatives; top-ranked combined scores retrain a phrase-based SMT system.
- Shows consistent BLEU improvements across four language directions and three social-media-like domains, robust down to ~100 in-domain sentences.
- Claims up to +3.1 BLEU gains using only 2.5%–10% of data and stability analyses with extremely small labeled sets.
- Outperforms strong LM-based selection, giving 0.3–0.5 BLEU gains at ≥1600 sentences and up to +2.1 BLEU at 100 sentences.
- Evaluation is broad and convincing: multiple directions/domains, large pools (9–15M), and statistical significance testing.
- Methodologically clear and reproducible: concrete CNN hyperparameters, public toolkits (conText, word2vec), and a clear selection procedure.
- Ablation (one-hot CNN vs semi-supervised CNN) confirms benefit of leveraging unlabeled data via embeddings.
- Limited novelty: applies known CNN/embedding techniques to selection rather than introducing new modeling ideas.
- Possible confound: zh→en SMS task may contain an in-domain subset within general data, potentially inflating gains.
- Narrow baseline scope: comparisons only to LM-based selection in phrase-based SMT; no NMT or data-weighting/importance-sampling baselines.
- Tuning/selection budget chosen on held-out data raises overfitting concerns; computational cost and scalability of scoring entire corpora are not discussed.
- Asks about sensitivity to size/domain of unlabeled corpus and alternative embeddings (e.g., GloVe) or fine-tuning.
- Requests analysis of source-only vs target-only scoring and how score disagreements affect selection.
- Queries prevention of leakage/near-duplicate overlap between dev/test and selected training sentences.
- Wonders whether the approach transfers to NMT and if gains persist against NMT domain-adaptation baselines.
