- Proposes framing SMT domain data selection as a binary classification task using a semi-supervised CNN trained on tiny in-domain positives and random negatives, scoring general-domain sentences to select training data.
- Leverages pre-trained word embeddings to enable generalization from very limited in-domain data and selects top-scoring sentence pairs to train adapted SMT systems.
- Demonstrates significant BLEU improvements across four language pairs and three social media domains compared to strong baselines.
- Main contribution: an effective classification-based framework for data selection that works in extremely low-resource in-domain settings (as few as 100 sentences).
- Main contribution: a strong, comprehensive empirical evaluation showing consistent, statistically significant gains over multiple baselines and tasks.
- Strength: impressive and consistent empirical results, with BLEU gains up to 3.1 points and outperforming RNNLM-based selection.
- Strength: addresses a practical and relevant problem—adapting MT with very small in-domain data, applicable to emerging or fine-grained domains.
- Strength: thorough analysis of performance in the low-data regime, including stability checks and performance curves from 6,000 down to 100 sentences.
- Strength: clear writing and reproducibility, using publicly available toolkits and transparent experimental setup.
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis explaining why the CNN outperforms baselines and what types of sentences it selects differently.
- Weakness: no discussion of computational cost—scoring millions of sentences with a CNN may be much more expensive than LM-based methods.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to phrase-based SMT, with no exploration or discussion of implications for NMT adaptation.
- Question: request qualitative examples comparing sentences ranked highly by sscnn vs rnnlm and explanation of linguistic phenomena behind disagreements.
- Question: request details on computational requirements and timing for scoring large corpora with sscnn versus rnnlm.
- Question: ask how the method would integrate with NMT workflows and whether data selection could outperform simple fine-tuning on tiny in-domain sets.
