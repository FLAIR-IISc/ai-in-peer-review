- Summary: Reframes SMT data selection as semi-supervised CNN text classification using few in-domain seeds and pre-trained embeddings to score and pick in-domain sentence pairs from a large general corpus.

- Contribution 1: Strong empirical validation that semi-supervised classification for data selection outperforms LM-based methods, especially with extremely limited in-domain data (e.g., 100 sentences).

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates the effectiveness of semi-supervised CNNs and pre-trained word embeddings to mitigate data sparsity in domain classification for MT.

- Strength 1: Yields substantial, statistically significant BLEU improvements (up to 3.1) across four language pairs and three social media domains.

- Strength 2: Effectively addresses domain adaptation with tiny seed data; shows superior robustness and stability versus LM methods as seed size decreases.

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation with multiple baselines, significance testing, and analysis across varying seed-data amounts.

- Weakness 1: Limited technical novelty—semi-supervised CNNs for text classification are established; novelty is in the application to SMT selection.

- Weakness 2: Experiments are limited to phrase-based SMT, so applicability to modern NMT systems is untested and uncertain.

- Weakness 3: Lack of details on CNN hyperparameter tuning and empirical selection of optimal selected-data size reduces reproducibility and ease of application.

- Question 1: What is the computational overhead of CNN-based scoring compared to LM-based methods on large general-domain corpora?

- Question 2: How does using only source-side or only target-side scores compare to the combined scoring approach in final selection performance?
