- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN-based data selection method for SMT domain adaptation using word embeddings and limited in-domain data.  
- Model assigns domain relevance scores to general-domain sentences to select data for adapted SMT training.  
- Demonstrates up to 3.1 BLEU improvement over state-of-the-art LM-based methods across four language pairs and three domains.  
- Method remains effective with very small in-domain corpora (as few as 100 sentences).  
- Contribution: introduces a semi-supervised CNN framework for domain classification and data selection that outperforms LM-based baselines.  
- Contribution: shows robustness and strong BLEU gains with minimal in-domain data.  
- Contribution: validates applicability across multiple language pairs and social media domains, indicating real-world relevance.  
- Strength: consistent, statistically significant performance gains versus strong n-gram and RNN baselines.  
- Strength: novel use of CNNs (convolution + pooling) with embeddings to capture domain-specific features and generalize to unseen words.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including multiple languages, domains, baselines, and varying in-domain sizes.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational costs and training overhead compared to traditional LM methods.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of applicability to non-SMT NLP tasks and potential extensions.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed ablation studies to isolate effects of embeddings vs. CNN architecture.  
- Questions: how do CNN training costs compare to baseline LM methods on large-scale data?  
- Questions: can the method be extended to other domain-adaptation tasks (e.g., sentiment analysis, QA), and what modifications would be required?
