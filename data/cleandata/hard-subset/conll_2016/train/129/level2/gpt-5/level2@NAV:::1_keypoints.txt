- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier for SMT data selection that scores source and target sentences and uses top-scoring pairs to train an adapted SMT system.
- Main contributions: a practical semi-supervised CNN combining one-hot regions with embeddings and bilingual score aggregation for selection.
- Main contributions: thorough empirical study across four language directions and three social-media domains showing consistent, significant gains over strong LM baselines.
- Main contributions: analysis of the tiny-data regime showing effectiveness with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.
- Strength: Strong multi-setting empirical evidence, with up to +3.1 BLEU over training on all data and consistent improvements (significance tested).
- Strength: Addresses a high-impact practical scenario—tiny in-domain regimes common in applications—showing method works where LM-based selection degrades.
- Strength: Sound modeling choices—CNNs capture local domain cues and semi-supervised embeddings generalize to unseen domain terms.
- Strength: Sensible bilingual scoring—summing source and target scores is empirically useful.
- Strength: Reasonable reproducibility—public datasets, toolkits, key hyperparameters reported, and significance testing conducted.
- Weakness: Missing scalability analysis—no runtime or compute cost reported for classifying millions of sentences compared to fast LM-based scoring.
- Weakness: Limited ablations—does not isolate embeddings vs. one-hot, bow vs. seq regions, or source vs. target vs. sum sufficiently.
- Weakness: Selection-size tuning per task on held-out data risks overfitting and reduces plug-and-play usability; lacks automatic stopping criterion.
- Weakness: Modern applicability concern—evaluation limited to phrase-based SMT; no NMT experiments to demonstrate generality.
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps—no code or selected subsets released, and tokenization/normalization for noisy social media not detailed.
- Question: What is the end-to-end runtime for training the classifier and scoring the full corpus per domain/language, and what hardware was used?
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding source/quality (e.g., domain-mismatched embeddings)?
- Question: Can you report ablations for source-only vs. target-only vs. sum and for bow vs. seq regions?
- Question: Can you provide an automatic criterion for selecting N (e.g., score threshold calibrated on dev)?
