- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN for bilingual data selection using tiny in-domain seeds and pretrained embeddings to adapt phrase-based SMT.
- Trains separate source and target CNNs on in-domain positives and random negatives, scoring sentence pairs by summed source+target scores to select data.
- Demonstrates consistent, statistically significant BLEU improvements across 4 translation directions and 3 social-media domains, remaining effective with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.

- Strong empirical gains (up to +3.1 BLEU; average +2.1 BLEU with 100 sentences) and practical impact for low-resource adaptation.
- Solid baselines and broad evaluation: compares to Moore–Lewis, RNNLM, and combined baselines across large corpora (9–15M pairs) with proper significance testing.
- Clear, reproducible methodological framing: separate source/target CNNs, provided hyperparameters and toolkits.
- Robustness to very small seeds: CNNs outperform LM-based methods especially below ~800 sentences.
- Convincing qualitative rationale that convolution + max-pooling and pretrained embeddings capture local cues and lexical similarity for domain relevance.

- Possible data leakage: selection size N may be tuned on the same dev set used for CNN training and SMT tuning, risking overfitting and optimistic gains.
- Baseline parity concerns: unclear if LM baselines perform bilingual selection or if combination weights and RNNLM configs were optimally tuned; need matched selection sizes or BLEU vs. N curves.
- zh→en sms gains may be inflated by an existing 120k in-domain subset in the pool; need retrieval precision/recall analysis versus LMs.
- Missing ablations: unclear contributions of pretrained embeddings vs. CNN architecture and of source-only vs. target-only scoring; numeric support for summing scores is lacking.
- Reproducibility gaps: missing tokenization/casing, OOV/vocab handling, word2vec training details, score normalization, runtime/throughput, and negative sampling strategy.

- Which held-out set is used to choose N, and is it distinct from dev sets used for CNN training and SMT tuning?
- Did LM baselines perform bilingual selection and were combination weights tuned?
- How were word2vec embeddings trained (corpus, tokenization, vocab size, min-count) and are they shared across tasks?
- Can you quantify embeddings vs. CNN (one-hot CNN vs. pretrained) and source-only vs. target-only contributions across seed sizes?
- What are the computational training and inference costs of the selector and how does it scale with corpus size?

- Compare to other selection strategies (VSM/distributed VSM, submodular selection) and to data weighting approaches.
- Provide BLEU vs. selection size curves and qualitative analysis of selected sentences for all methods.
- Test applicability to NMT data selection if feasible.
- Fix reference formatting and typos (e.g., “GloV e”) to improve professionalism.
- Release code and selected sentence lists to aid reproducibility.

- Overall: a timely, practical contribution with strong empirical results; addressing evaluation protocol, baseline parity, and ablations would substantially strengthen the paper.
