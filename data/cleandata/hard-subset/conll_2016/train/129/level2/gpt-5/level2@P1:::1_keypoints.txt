- Proposal: semi-supervised CNN using pre-trained embeddings to score source/target sentences and select in-domain parallel data for SMT when only tiny in-domain samples exist.
- Empirical claim: consistent BLEU improvements across four language pairs (zh→en, ar→en, en→es, en→zh) and three social-media domains, outperforming one-hot CNN and LM-based baselines.
- Contribution 1: practical, effective data-selection method that yields sizable SMT gains even with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.
- Contribution 2: comprehensive empirical evaluation vs. strong LM baselines with significance testing and stability analysis.
- Strength: strong empirical gains in realistic low-data settings, including surpassing a known 120K in-domain subset in one task.
- Strength: robust performance with tiny labeled dev sets (e.g., 100 sentences) and low variance across samples.
- Strength: architecture/design aligns with prior CNN text-classification work, aiding plausibility and reproducibility.
- Strength: wide-scale evaluation (multiple languages/domains, large pools of sentence pairs) and appropriate significance testing.
- Strength: practical adoptability using small dev sets and publicly available tools (conText, word2vec).
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—application of established CNN techniques to selection rather than novel modeling.
- Weakness: baseline coverage is narrow; missing comparisons to mixture/instance-weighting, VSM/distributed methods, and simple discriminative baselines.
- Weakness: unclear protocol for dev/held-out splits and potential tuning/selection-size leakage.
- Weakness: limited ablations and missing quantitative numbers for source vs. target scoring, pretrained embeddings, region/window choices, and embedding size.
- Weakness: computational/resource reporting absent—no training time, scoring throughput, or hardware comparisons to LM methods.
- Weakness: incomplete reproducibility details (tokenization, vocab pruning, OOV handling, embedding corpora, potential data leakage).
- Questions: how was the in-domain dev set split for CNN training vs. selection-size tuning vs. MT tuning/testing?
- Questions: please provide quantitative ablations for source-only/target-only/sum, with/without pretrained embeddings, bow vs. seq, region sizes, and embedding dims.
- Questions: report runtime: training time per classifier, sentences/sec for scoring, hardware, and comparisons to Moore-Lewis/RNNLM selection.
- Questions: can you show qualitative examples of selected vs. rejected sentences and classifier precision/recall on a labeled holdout?
- Questions: were CNN scores calibrated given class imbalance, and are rankings robust across domains?
- Questions: are embeddings trained per language side, on which corpora, and was any bilingual embedding tried?
- Additional suggestion: explore using CNN scores as instance weights rather than hard selection and consider joint/bilingual (Siamese) CNNs for pair-level signals.
- Reproducibility recommendation: release code and selection lists for at least one language pair.
- Overall: solid, well-motivated empirical paper with practical impact; incremental modeling novelty but compelling results in the tiny in-domain regime.
