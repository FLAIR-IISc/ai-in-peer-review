- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN-based data selection method for SMT using as few as 100 in-domain sentences, evaluated across four language directions and multiple domains.
- Semi-supervised CNN framework effective for data selection with minimal in-domain data (~100 sentences).
- Shows significant BLEU improvements over LM-based selection methods (up to +3.1 BLEU on Chinese→English SMS).
- Validates effectiveness for fine-grained topic-dependent translation adaptation.
- Addresses the important problem of adapting MT to new domains with very limited parallel data.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with convincing ablation studies showing gains even with 100 sentences.
- Leverages word embeddings trained on large general-domain corpora to compensate for scarce in-domain data.
- Demonstrates robust selection of high-quality in-domain sentences even when general-domain corpora lack in-domain content.
- Focus on SMT limits relevance given the field’s shift to NMT.
- Comparisons use outdated LM-based baselines and omit recent neural data-selection methods.
- Lacks deeper analysis explaining why CNNs outperform language-model approaches.
- Omits discussion of computational cost and practical deployment considerations.
- Claims fine-grained topic adaptation but provides limited evidence beyond tested domains.
- How would this method perform or be adapted for NMT systems?
- Can you compare with recent neural data-selection approaches (e.g., neural LMs) to show relative advantages?
- What are the computational costs of training and using the CNN compared to LM-based baselines?
