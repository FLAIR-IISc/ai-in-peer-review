- Summary: Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to select in-domain sentences for SMT using as few as 100 labeled in-domain sentences plus general-domain negative samples and pretrained word embeddings.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a semi-supervised CNN framework for data selection that works with extremely limited in-domain data (≈100 sentences).

- Contribution 2: Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art LM-based data selection methods (ngram, rnnlm, comblm), especially when in-domain data is scarce.

- Contribution 3: Enables fine-grained, topic-dependent translation adaptation that was previously impractical due to the cost of collecting large in-domain datasets.

- Strength 1: Addresses a highly relevant MT problem—adapting to new domains with very limited data—and clearly motivates the need for fine-grained adaptation.

- Strength 2: Strong, well-documented experimental results across four language pairs and three test domains, with up to 3.1 BLEU gains and consistent improvements at 100-sentence scale.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates stability and reproducibility on tiny in-domain samples (100-sentence experiments repeated with low BLEU variance).

- Weakness 1: Lacks sufficient detail on CNN architecture and hyperparameter tuning (e.g., region size, stride, neuron count, pooling choice), hindering reproducibility.

- Weakness 2: Fails to explain why semi-supervised CNN outperforms supervised CNN beyond a generic claim about embeddings—no concrete examples or analysis of OOV/helped cases.

- Weakness 3: Does not discuss limitations or behavior under even smaller in-domain sizes (<100 sentences), computational costs, or scaling as in-domain data grows.

- Weakness 4: Negative sampling strategy is under-specified; random sampling from the general pool may produce poor negatives if that pool contains domain-specific data.

- Question 1: Please provide detailed CNN design rationale and hyperparameter selection process (region size, stride, neurons, pooling).

- Question 2: Provide concrete examples where pretrained embeddings enabled correct classification versus the supervised approach.

- Question 3: How does performance change below 100 in-domain sentences and what is the minimal effective in-domain sample size?

- Question 4: Please report computational costs and resource requirements for training the CNNs compared to LM-based baselines (training time, hardware).
