Proposes semi-supervised CNNs to select in-domain sentences from general-domain data using few positive in-domain samples and random negatives to improve SMT.

Novel domain-adaptation method using semi-supervised CNNs for in-domain data selection in SMT.

Demonstrates effectiveness across four language pairs and three domains, outperforming strong baselines.

Shows method works with very small in-domain samples (≈100 sentences) while still improving translation quality.

Well-motivated and clearly explained approach to SMT domain adaptation.

Thorough evaluation across multiple language directions and domains, showing robustness.

Highlights promise of semi-supervised CNNs for the challenging task of in-domain data selection.

Assumes availability of at least a small amount of in-domain data, which may not always be realistic.

Lacks analysis of computational resources and training cost for the CNN models at scale.

Does not explore alternative ML strategies (e.g., transfer learning, multi-task learning) for domain adaptation.

How will the authors obtain or handle cases with extremely scarce or unavailable in-domain data?

Can the authors provide details on computational resources, training time, and optimization for large-scale deployment?

Have the authors considered or compared transfer learning or multi-task learning approaches and their potential trade-offs?
