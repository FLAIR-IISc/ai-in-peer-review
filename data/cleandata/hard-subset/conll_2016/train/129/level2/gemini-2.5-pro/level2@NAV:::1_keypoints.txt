- Novel method: semi-supervised CNN for SMT data selection, trained with very small in-domain positive and general-domain negative examples to score and pick relevant sentences.
- Contribution: practical adaptation approach that yields significant gains even with extremely limited in-domain data (e.g., 100 sentences).
- Contribution: strong empirical evaluation across four language pairs showing consistent, significant improvement over language model–based baselines.
- Strength: Impressive empirical results—statistically significant BLEU gains up to 3.1 points across multiple language pairs and domains.
- Strength: High practical impact—enables rapid adaptation for new or niche topics with minimal data.
- Strength: Clear analysis of low-data scenarios—CNN approach remains robust as in-domain data shrinks, unlike LM methods.
- Weakness: Limited qualitative analysis—paper lacks concrete examples explaining why the CNN outperforms LMs.
- Weakness: Missing computational cost discussion—no comparison of training/inference time or scalability versus LM baselines.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—the CNN is standard; novelty lies mainly in its application to SMT data selection and should be framed accordingly.
