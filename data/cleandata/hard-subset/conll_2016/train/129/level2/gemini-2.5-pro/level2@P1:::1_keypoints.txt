- Novel semi-supervised CNN formulation for SMT data selection: train on few in-domain positives and general-domain negatives, score and select best general-domain pairs.
- Use of pretrained word embeddings as additional features to handle extremely limited in-domain data (as few as 100 sentences).
- Strong empirical gains across four language pairs and three domains, up to +3.1 BLEU over full-data and LM-based baselines.
- Novel and well-motivated shift from perplexity-based selection to a discriminative CNN approach with embeddings for low-resource scenarios.
- Consistently outperforms strong baselines—including an RNNLM—across multiple languages and social media domains.
- Addresses a practical and important low-resource domain adaptation problem, showing robustness when seed data is very small.
- Thorough evaluation with multiple baselines, statistical significance testing, and analysis of performance vs. in-domain seed size.
- Lacks qualitative analysis or examples illustrating what the CNN learns and why it outperforms LM methods.
- CNN architecture description is brief and unclear (uncertain how bow-regions and seq-regions and one-hot vs embedding inputs are combined), hindering reproducibility.
- No discussion or analysis of computational cost for training the CNN and scoring millions of sentences compared to LM baselines.
- Request for illustrative examples: sentences ranked highly by sscnn but low by rnnlm to build intuition.
- Request for more detailed CNN architecture details, specifically how different region types and input representations are combined.
- Request for analysis of computational overhead: training and scoring time of sscnn vs rnnlm.
