- Proposes a practical semi-supervised CNN for data selection in SMT when in-domain data is extremely limited.
- Leverages pre-trained word embeddings to boost semi-supervised learning effectiveness with tiny in-domain seeds.
- Provides comprehensive evaluation across four language pairs and three domains, showing consistent improvements.
- Addresses a critical problem: domain adaptation with very small in-domain datasets.
- Reports strong, statistically significant gains over strong baselines, including LM-based methods.
- Demonstrates scalability to low-resource scenarios, performing well with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and easy to follow.
- Experimental setup is thorough, using multiple languages, domains, and strong baselines.
- Lacks detailed analysis of the CNN architecture and missing ablation studies on components and meta-parameters.
- Insufficient exploration of negative sample selection strategies (e.g., hard negative mining).
- No detailed error analysis to explain when and why the CNN fails or which sentence types cause problems.
- Limited comparison/discussion of more recent domain-adaptation approaches (e.g., NMT, transfer learning).
- Request for authors to detail CNN architecture search and ablation results (layer sizes, pooling, impacts).
- Ask whether alternative negative sampling strategies (hard negative mining) were tried and their outcomes.
- Request for a brief error analysis highlighting common CNN mistakes and causes.
- Ask how the approach could be adapted or extended to NMT/transfer-learning settings.
- References are comprehensive.
- Releasing code would be a valuable addition.
- Recommend adding a table summarizing hyperparameter settings for each experiment.
