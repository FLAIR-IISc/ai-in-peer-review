- Problem: Adapting SMT systems with very limited in-domain data.  
- Method: Semi-supervised CNN classifier to select in-domain sentences from general-domain corpora, leveraging pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Contribution 1: Demonstrates effective data selection with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Contribution 2: Empirical gains up to 3.1 BLEU across four language pairs and three domains, outperforming strong baselines.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and well-defined experimental setup.  
- Strength: Sensible use of semi-supervised learning and pre-trained embeddings to mitigate scarce data.  
- Strength: Comprehensive baseline comparisons and a useful ablation study on in-domain data size.  
- Strength: Well-written paper with a clear explanation of methodology.  
- Weakness: Analysis of why the CNN performs well is shallow—lacks insight into learned features.  
- Weakness: No discussion of computational cost (training/inference overhead) for the CNN-based selection.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison or discussion relative to recent NMT adaptation techniques.  
- Questions: Request detailed feature analysis, computational cost figures, and comparisons to NMT fine-tuning with limited in-domain data.
