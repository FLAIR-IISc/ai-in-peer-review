- Contribution: Introduces a semi-supervised CNN for in-domain data selection that outperforms LM-based methods.  
- Contribution: Can select suitable in-domain sentences with very limited data (reportedly as few as 100 sentence pairs).  
- Contribution: Validated on four language directions and three test domains.  

- Strength: Shows significant improvements over LM-based selection, especially with limited in-domain data.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments across multiple language pairs and domains demonstrating robustness.  
- Strength: Tackles domain adaptation in SMT with limited in-domain data and offers a novel solution.  

- Weakness: The reported +3.1 BLEU gains are misleading because large gains occur only when in-domain data is in the training set; comparison to interpolated in-/out-domain models is needed.  
- Weakness: The small-data experiment (100 pairs) was run only on zh→en where in-domain data exists in training, so generality to cases without in-domain training data is unclear.  
- Weakness: Insufficient details on preprocessing/filtering of social media data (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons).  
- Weakness: Lacks concrete translation examples showing improvements from data selection.  

- Question: Please detail preprocessing/filtering of social media data and techniques for handling OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons.  
- Question: Please provide concrete examples (sentence pairs/phrases) where data selection improved translations.  
- Question: Clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the CNN and specify the designated loss function.  
- Question: Provide results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to support the small-data claim.  

- Comment: Section 3.2 needs clarification on feature combination and would benefit from an architecture illustration.  
- Comment: Section 5.2 should state how system weights were tuned.  
- Comment: Report computational resources (number of GPUs, training time) used for experiments.
