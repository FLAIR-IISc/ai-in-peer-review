- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN method to select in-domain SMT training data that works with very small in-domain samples.  
- Trains CNN using equal-sized positive (in-domain) and negative (random general-domain) samples, scores general-domain sentences, and selects top N pairs for SMT.  
- Shows significant improvements over strong baselines across four language directions and three test domains.  
- Demonstrates robustness to tiny in-domain sizes, reporting good performance with as few as 100 sentence pairs.  

- Strength: Effective selection approach even with minimal in-domain data.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple languages and domains.  
- Strength: Results show significant gains over large-data baselines.  

- Weakness: Motivation for using CNN instead of RNN/LSTM is not adequately justified.  
- Weakness: No comparison to bilingual/parallel data selection methods.  
- Weakness: Validation procedure for selecting N and tuning CNN hyperparameters is unclear.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis and example sentences to support claimed behaviors.  

- Question: Why was CNN chosen over RNN/LSTM for this task?  
- Question: How were the number of selected sentences and CNN hyperparameters chosen, and how sensitive are results to these choices?  
- Question: Can example sentences selected by different methods be provided to support Section 5.4 claims?  
- Question: Have you considered extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to combine source and target scores?  

- Comment: Compare the approach to other methods when more in-domain data is available.  
- Comment: Provide more detail on the CNN architecture and hyperparameter choices.  
- Comment: Minor citation and formatting issues need fixing.  
- Comment: Paper is well-organized but some sections could be more clearly labeled/separated.
