- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN method to select in-domain sentence pairs for statistical MT, effective with very small in-domain data.
- Trains CNN using a few in-domain positives and equal-number random negatives from general-domain data, then selects top-scoring general-domain pairs for MT training.
- Contribution: semi-supervised CNN data selection effective even with tiny in-domain corpora.
- Contribution: empirical comparison showing the CNN approach outperforms state-of-the-art language model–based selection methods.
- Contribution: demonstrates stability by repeating experiments with different in-domain sets and obtaining consistent results.
- Strength: selects a small, high-quality training subset that yields better MT systems than using all general-domain data.
- Strength: robust performance with extremely limited in-domain data (e.g., ~100 sentence pairs).
- Strength: thorough comparison to existing language model–based selection baselines.
- Weakness: no clear justification for choosing a CNN architecture over alternatives (e.g., RNN/LSTM).
