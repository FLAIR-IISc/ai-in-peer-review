- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN that uses few in-domain positives and random general-domain negatives to score and select top N sentence pairs for SMT, claiming up to 3.1 BLEU gain.
- Introduces a novel in-domain data selection method using semi-supervised CNN that can outperform baselines trained on all general-domain data.
- Shows the method remains effective with very small in-domain sets (as few as 100 sentence pairs).
- Compares against three state-of-the-art language model–based selection methods and reports superior performance.
- Effectively leverages semi-supervised learning to exploit large unlabeled corpora.
- Demonstrates robustness to very small in-domain training data, a key advantage over traditional methods.
- Provides thorough comparisons with existing data selection techniques to validate effectiveness.
- Claims of 3.1 BLEU gain are misleading because the large improvement depends on including the in-domain data in training; gains are much smaller with only general-domain data.
