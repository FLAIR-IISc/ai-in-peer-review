- Proposes a novel semi-supervised CNN classifier for MT training data selection, effective with very little in-domain data.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on 4 language directions and 3 test domains, outperforming strong baselines.  
- Shows a small selected in-domain subset can produce systems better than those trained on all general-domain data.  
- Clear, concise explanation of the method and in-domain selection process.  
- Strong empirical evaluation with detailed analysis and comparisons to continuous and n-gram LM approaches.  
- No comparison to bilingual data selection methods, which could be more effective.  
- Lacks justification for choosing CNN over RNN/LSTM for text classification.  
- Missing experiments to determine whether BOW, SEQ, or their combination drives performance.  
- No layer diagram of the CNN architecture provided.  
- Validation procedure for selecting number of in-domain sentences and CNN hyperparameters is not clearly explained.  
- Asks authors to compare to bilingual data selection methods.  
- Asks authors to explain motivation for CNN vs RNN/LSTM.  
- Requests experiments to isolate importance of BOW, SEQ, and their combination.  
- Requests a CNN layer diagram for clarity.  
- Requests clarification of validation procedure for selection size and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Suggests including example sentences selected by different methods to support claims.  
- Suggests comparing to Axelrod (2015) POS-replacement approach to test word2vec advantages.  
- Suggests extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to combine source and target scores.
