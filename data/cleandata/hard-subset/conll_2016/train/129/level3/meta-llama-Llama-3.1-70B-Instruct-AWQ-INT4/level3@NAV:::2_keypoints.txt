- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to select in-domain sentence pairs for SMT using tiny in-domain positives and sampled negatives from general-domain data, then selects top-scoring pairs to train SMT, evaluated on 4 language directions and 3 test domains with reported improvements.

- Contribution: Novel application of semi-supervised CNN for in-domain data selection effective with very small in-domain seed data.

- Contribution: Demonstrates significant improvements over strong baseline systems trained on large general-domain corpora.

- Contribution: Shows robustness of the approach when in-domain data is extremely limited.

- Strength: Uses word embeddings learned from large general-domain data to boost CNN performance.

- Strength: Method remains effective even with very limited in-domain examples.

- Strength: Achieves notable gains over strong baselines in reported experiments.

- Weakness: The reported +3.1 BLEU improvement is misleading because it depends on including the in-domain data in training; gains are much smaller otherwise.

- Weakness: No comparison to interpolated in-/out-domain models, leaving out a relevant baseline.

- Weakness: CNN architecture and loss function are not described clearly, hindering reproducibility.
