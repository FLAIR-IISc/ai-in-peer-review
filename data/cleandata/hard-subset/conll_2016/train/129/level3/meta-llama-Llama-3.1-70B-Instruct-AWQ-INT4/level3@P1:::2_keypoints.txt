- Proposed method: semi-supervised CNN selects in-domain sentence pairs using a few in-domain positives and equal-sized random negatives from general-domain data.
- Evaluation: tested on four language directions and three domains, showing improvements over strong baselines.
- Contribution: novel semi-supervised CNN-based approach for SMT domain adaptation.
- Contribution: method is robust with very small amounts of in-domain data.
- Contribution: outperforms LM-based selection methods, reporting up to +3.1 BLEU.
- Strength: introduces a novel approach for domain adaptation in SMT.
- Strength: demonstrates robustness when only tiny in-domain corpora are available.
- Strength: shows improved translation performance compared to strong baselines.
- Weakness: +3.1 BLEU claim is misleading because large gains occur mainly when in-domain data is included in training.
- Weakness: limited evaluation—the most compelling experiment was run only on zh→en where in-domain data existed in training.
- Weakness: Section 3.2 lacks clarity on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined.
- Weakness: Section 5.2 is incomplete—authors do not state how system weights were tuned.
