- Main contribution: Semi-supervised CNN for data selection yields large BLEU gains (up to 3.1) with very small in-domain datasets (as few as 100 sentences).  
- Secondary contribution: sscnn outperforms state-of-the-art LM-based data selection by about 0.5 BLEU on average.

Strengths:
- Method effective with extremely limited in-domain data (100 sentences) and shows low variance across runs (std 0.12).  
- Evaluation thorough across four language directions and diverse social-media domains, showing consistent improvements and sscnn best across tasks.  
- Semi-supervised design leverages large unlabeled corpora via embeddings and plausibly captures domain-specific features while ignoring general words.

Weaknesses:
- "Up to 3.1 BLEU" claim is misleading because largest gains stem from cases where in-domain data is already in training; need comparison to interpolated in-/out-domain models.  
- The 100-sentence experiment was only run on zh→en where in-domain examples exist in training; unclear if results generalize to the other three tasks.  
- Paper omits handling of social-media-specific issues (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons); preprocessing details are missing.  
- No concrete translation examples or analysis of how data selection handles OOVs, making qualitative improvements unclear.

Questions to authors:
- Please report results for all four language pairs using only 100 in-domain sentences.  
- How were social-media-specific tokens (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons) preprocessed or filtered?  
- Provide example sentences selected by the method and show how selection improved translation quality.  
- Clarify model architecture in Section 3.2: how embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined, and specify the CNN loss function.  
- How were SMT system weights tuned in Section 5.2 to ensure fair comparison with baselines?
