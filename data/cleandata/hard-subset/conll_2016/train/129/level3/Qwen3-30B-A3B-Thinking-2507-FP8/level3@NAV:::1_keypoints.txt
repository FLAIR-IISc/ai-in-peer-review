- Contribution: Proposes semi-supervised CNN-based data selection for MT when only tiny in-domain data is available.  
- Contribution: Shows CNN-based selection can adapt MT systems with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates semi-supervised CNNs (with pre-trained embeddings) outperform LM-based methods, especially with scarce in-domain data.  
- Contribution: Provides practical evidence of BLEU improvements up to 3.1 using only 2.5–10% of training data.  
- Strength: Tackles a meaningful practical problem (domain adaptation with very limited in-domain data).  
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence across four language directions and multiple test domains.  
- Strength: Method remains effective even with extremely limited in-domain data (100 sentences); experimental design generally sound.  
- Weakness: Fails to compare with bilingual data selection methods (e.g., sum/difference of cross-entropies).  
- Weakness: Motivation for using CNN over RNN/LSTM is unclear and unsupported by comparative evidence.  
- Weakness: No experiments isolating the importance of bag-of-words (BOW) vs. sequence (SEQ) features or their combination for CNN performance.  
- Weakness: Validation procedure for selecting number of in-domain sentences and hyperparameter tuning is unclear; lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Table 2 compares methods at different selection sizes (e.g., 300K vs 400K), hindering direct comparison.  
- Suggestion: Include a CNN layer diagram to clarify the architecture.  
- Suggestion: Provide example sentences selected by different methods to support qualitative claims.  
- Suggestion: Fix inconsistent citation style and compare/cite relevant prior work (Axelrod 2015; Lewis-Moore on cross-entropy differences).
