

**Review**

This paper proposes a semi-supervised CNN approach for in-domain data selection in SMT, claiming significant gains with minimal in-domain data. The primary contribution is demonstrating CNN-based data selection can effectively adapt translation systems with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs. The secondary contribution is the semi-supervised framework that leverages general-domain data to train word embeddings, mitigating the small-data challenge. The tertiary contribution is the robustness of CNN selection when in-domain data is scarce.

Strengths: The core contribution of achieving meaningful adaptation with extremely limited in-domain data (100 sentences) is valuable for real-world applications where collecting large in-domain datasets is impractical. The semi-supervised CNN framework effectively combines labeled in-domain data with unlabeled general-domain data to train robust classifiers. The experimental results consistently show improvements over LM-based baselines, particularly when in-domain data is limited.

Weaknesses: The claim of +3.1 BLEU improvement is misleading, as the largest gains occur when in-domain data is already present in the training set (training only on in-domain data yields +2.8 BLEU). The most compelling small-data result (100 in-domain sentences) was only tested on zh→en where in-domain data exists in training; it's unclear whether this generalizes to tasks without such data. The paper fails to address social-media-specific challenges (high OOVs, usernames, hashtags) and doesn't provide concrete examples of what improvements data selection actually produces. Section 3.2 lacks clarity on vector combination methods, and Section 5.2 omits details on system weight tuning.

Questions to Authors: 1) Can you report results for all tasks using exactly 100 in-domain sentence pairs to better support the small-data claim? 2) What preprocessing was applied to handle social-media artifacts like usernames, hashtags, and emoticons? 3) How were system weights tuned for the final models?
