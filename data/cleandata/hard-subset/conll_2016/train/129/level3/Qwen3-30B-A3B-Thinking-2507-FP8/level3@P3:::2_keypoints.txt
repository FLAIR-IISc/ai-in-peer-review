- Paper proposes semi-supervised CNNs for selecting in-domain sentences from general-domain corpora to adapt SMT with very small in-domain data.
- Authors claim CNN-based selection yields up to +3.1 BLEU and works with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs.
- Contribution: shows CNN-based selection can be effective with extremely limited in-domain data (e.g., 100 sentences).
- Contribution: uses semi-supervised CNNs with embeddings trained on general-domain data to mitigate tiny in-domain datasets.
- Contribution: provides empirical comparisons showing CNN selection outperforms LM-based selection when in-domain data is limited.
- Strength: addresses a practical and important problem—domain adaptation for scarce in-domain data, relevant to social media translation.
- Strength: the 100-sentence result is potentially impactful if validated across more tasks and languages.
- Strength: clear methodology that can be integrated into existing SMT pipelines with well-defined experiments and baselines.
- Weakness: the +3.1 BLEU claim is misleading because large gains come from tasks where in-domain data already exists in training; comparisons should include interpolated in-/out-domain models.
- Weakness: the 100-sentence effectiveness was only tested on zh→en where in-domain training data exists, so generalizability to other language pairs is unproven.
- Weakness: paper fails to address social-media-specific issues (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons) or describe any special preprocessing.
- Weakness: lacks concrete before/after translation examples to illustrate the nature of the improvements from data selection.
- Weakness: Section 3.2 is unclear about how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined and omits the specific loss function; an architecture diagram is needed.
- Weakness: Section 5.2 does not explain how system weights were tuned, reducing reproducibility.
- Question: clarify how the method differs from simply using the in-domain subset and provide fair comparisons to interpolated models.
- Question: provide results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to support the small-data claim.
- Question: describe any social-media-specific preprocessing applied (handling OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons).
- Question: provide concrete translation examples showing improvements from the data selection method.
- Question: clarify the CNN architecture in Section 3.2—how features are combined and what loss function was used.
- Question: explain how system weights were tuned in Section 5.2 for completeness.
