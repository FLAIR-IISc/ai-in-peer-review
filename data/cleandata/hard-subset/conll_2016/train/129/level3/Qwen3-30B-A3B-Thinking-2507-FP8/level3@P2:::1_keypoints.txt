- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN framework to score general-domain sentences for selecting in-domain training data, enabling SMT domain adaptation with very small in-domain sets.  
- Demonstrates effective adaptation with as few as 100 in-domain sentences and reports up to 3.1 BLEU improvement over strong baselines.

- Addresses a practical industry problem of domain adaptation with limited in-domain data, enabling topic-specific translation for new domains.  
- Provides strong, comprehensive empirical results across four language directions and multiple domains, outperforming n-gram and RNNLM baselines.  
- Shows robustness to extremely limited in-domain data with stable performance across random samples.

- Lacks comparison to bilingual data selection methods (e.g., Moore and Lewis 2010) that use cross-entropy differences.  
- Does not justify why CNNs are preferable to RNNs/LSTMs or include direct CNN vs. RNN comparisons.  
- Fails to clearly explain the validation/selection procedure for the number of selected sentences and sensitivity to equal-data comparisons.  
- Omits ablation studies to determine the contributions of BOW, SEQ, or their combination and to support the claim about summing source and target scores.

- Please compare your method against Moore and Lewis (2010) bilingual selection using LM difference to better contextualize results.  
- Clarify the relationship between your score-summing approach and the Lewis-Moore cross-entropy difference, and cite that work.  
- Provide example sentences selected by your method versus LM-based methods to illustrate domain-specific capture.  
- Clarify whether the “100 in-domain sentences” are from dev or a separate in-domain set.  
- Report sensitivity analyses for the number of selected sentences (N) and CNN hyperparameters (e.g., neuron counts).
