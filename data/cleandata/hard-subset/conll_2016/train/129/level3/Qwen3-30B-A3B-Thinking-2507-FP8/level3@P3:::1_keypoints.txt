- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to select relevant general-domain sentences for SMT adaptation using as few as 100 in-domain sentences.
- Trains the CNN with small labeled in-domain positives and randomly sampled general-domain negatives, then selects top-scoring general sentences to retrain the SMT system.
- Demonstrates consistent BLEU improvements across four language directions (zh→en, ar→en, en→es, en→zh) and three domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook).
- Main contribution: shows practical domain adaptation is possible with minimal in-domain data (orders of magnitude fewer sentences than prior work).
- Main contribution: introduces a semi-supervised framework leveraging pre-trained word embeddings to exploit large unlabeled general-domain corpora.
- Main contribution: empirically outperforms state-of-the-art LM-based data selection methods, with up to 3.1 BLEU gain.
- Strength: addresses a real-world problem (scarce in-domain data) relevant for emerging domains like social media.
- Strength: strong, comprehensive experiments across multiple languages and domains supporting the method’s effectiveness.
- Strength: method shows robustness with very small in-domain sets (100 sentences) and low variance across random samples.
- Strength: effective use of semi-supervised learning and pre-trained embeddings to overcome limited labeled data.
- Weakness: omits comparison to standard bilingual selection baselines (e.g., Lewis-Moore sum/difference of cross-entropies).
- Weakness: insufficient justification or empirical comparison for choosing CNN over RNN/LSTM architectures.
- Weakness: lacks ablation studies to disentangle the contributions of BOW, SEQ, or their combination within the CNN.
- Weakness: validation procedure for selection sizes and CNN hyperparameters is unclear and sensitivity is not reported.
- Weakness: Table 2 comparisons should control for equal numbers of selected sentences as in Figure 1.
- Question: please compare to Lewis-Moore bilingual cross-entropy sum/difference methods.
- Question: please justify CNN choice and provide RNN/LSTM comparisons or ablations.
- Question: please provide example sentences selected by different methods to illustrate domain-specific capture.
- Question: how does the method perform when in-domain data is a test set rather than a dev set?
