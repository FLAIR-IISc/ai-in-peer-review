- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN method to select in-domain SMT training data, effective with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Claims the CNN-based selection significantly outperforms state-of-the-art language model-based selection, especially when in-domain data is scarce.  
- Addresses a relevant MT problem for low-resource or niche domains (e.g., social media) and shows practical improvements.  
- Empirical results are strong: up to 3.1 BLEU gain over strong baselines using only 3% of training data.  
- Method works with just 100 in-domain sentences, yielding a notable 2.1 BLEU improvement.  
- Ablation studies indicate stability across in-domain sizes, with low variance (std 0.12 BLEU) over repeated 100-sentence samples.  
- Lacks comparison to bilingual data selection methods (e.g., Lewis-Moore) that directly compare source and target LM scores.  
- Motivation for choosing CNN over RNN/LSTM is underdeveloped and not empirically justified for this text-classification task.  
- Does not compare different CNN input representations (BOW vs SEQ vs combined), leaving model behavior unclear.  
- Validation procedure for selecting sentence counts and hyperparameters is unclear; Table 2 comparisons may be misleading without fixed selection sizes.  
- Claims about word embeddings aiding adaptation to unseen words (e.g., "Lenovo") are unverified; no ablation with/without pre-trained embeddings.  
- No justification for summing source and target classification scores versus established cross-entropy-based bilingual combinations (Lewis-Moore).  
- Asks authors to: compare with Lewis-Moore bilingual selection; provide evidence for embedding benefits on unseen words; clarify validation/hyperparameter selection; and report performance with larger in-domain sets (e.g., 1,000 sentences).
