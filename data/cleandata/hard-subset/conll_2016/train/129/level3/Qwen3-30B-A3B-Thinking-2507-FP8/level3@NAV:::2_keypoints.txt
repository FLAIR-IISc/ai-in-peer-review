- Proposed a semi-supervised CNN for in-domain data selection in SMT, claiming significant gains with minimal in-domain data.  
- Primary contribution: show CNN-based data selection can adapt translation systems with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs.  
- Secondary contribution: semi-supervised framework leverages general-domain data to train word embeddings, mitigating small-data issues.  
- Tertiary contribution: claim of robustness of CNN selection when in-domain data is scarce.  
- Strength: Achieves meaningful adaptation using only 100 sentences, valuable when large in-domain datasets are impractical.  
- Strength: Effectively combines labeled in-domain and unlabeled general-domain data to train robust classifiers.  
- Strength: Experimental results consistently improve over LM-based baselines, especially with limited in-domain data.  
- Weakness: The reported +3.1 BLEU is misleading because largest gains occur when in-domain data is already in training (training only on in-domain yields +2.8 BLEU).  
- Weakness: The 100-sentence small-data result was tested only on zh→en where in-domain data exists in training, so generalization is unclear.  
- Weakness: Paper does not address social-media-specific challenges (high OOVs, usernames, hashtags) or provide concrete examples of improvements.  
- Weakness: Section 3.2 is unclear about vector combination methods; Section 5.2 omits details on system weight tuning.  
- Question: Please report results for all tasks using exactly 100 in-domain sentence pairs to support the small-data claim.  
- Question: What preprocessing was applied to handle social-media artifacts (usernames, hashtags, emoticons)?  
- Question: How were system weights tuned for the final models?
