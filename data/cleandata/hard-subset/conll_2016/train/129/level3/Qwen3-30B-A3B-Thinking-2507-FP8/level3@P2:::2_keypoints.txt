- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN for in-domain sentence selection in SMT using as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Shows CNN-based selection can outperform LM-based methods, enabling fine-grained topic adaptation with tiny in-domain data.  
- Demonstrates improvements even when no in-domain data exists in the general-domain training set.  

- Key strength: effective adaptation with extremely limited in-domain data (100 sentences), addressing a practical data-collection challenge.  
- Key strength: systematic evaluation across four language directions and multiple social-media domains with consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Key strength: well-motivated semi-supervised CNN that leverages word embeddings to overcome limited in-domain data.  

- Weakness: +3.1 BLEU on zh→en sms is misleading because a large in-domain subset was already present in the training data.  
- Weakness: Claim of effectiveness with 100 in-domain sentences tested only on zh→en, not validated for the other three tasks.  
- Weakness: No concrete examples of selected sentence pairs or analysis showing how selection improved translation quality.  
- Weakness: No discussion of handling social-media-specific issues (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons) or any special preprocessing.  

- Q: Please provide results for all four tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs.  
- Q: For tasks without in-domain data in training, what specific improvements occurred and can you give example selected sentence pairs and their translation gains?  
- Q: How were social-media features (usernames, hashtags, emoticons) handled—was any filtering or normalization applied?  
- Q: Clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the semi-supervised CNN (diagram or brief description requested).  

- Suggestion: Compare against baselines that include interpolated in-domain/out-domain models rather than only the "alldata" baseline.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly state how system weights were tuned in Section 5.2 for completeness.
