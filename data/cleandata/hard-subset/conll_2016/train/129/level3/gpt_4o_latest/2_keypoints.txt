- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN for in-domain sentence selection using word embeddings plus one-hot vectors to improve SMT with limited in-domain data.  
- Outperforms LM-based methods (up to 3.1 BLEU, ~0.5 BLEU on average) and is robust with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs across four language pairs and three domains.  
- Contributions: novel semi-supervised CNN framework, demonstrated robustness in extremely low-resource settings, and ability to select relevant in-domain data without domain-specific knowledge.  
- Strength: Strong performance and robustness, particularly in very low-resource scenarios (as few as 100 sentences).  
- Strength: Novel and practical application of CNNs for domain classification in SMT, relevant for noisy domains like social media.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple language pairs/domains with comparisons and analysis over varying in-domain data sizes.  
- Weakness: Limited evidence of generalizability; need results for all language pairs at the 100-sentence setting.  
- Weakness: Missing comparison to interpolation methods that combine in-domain and out-of-domain models.  
- Weakness: Lacks preprocessing/filtering details for social media text (OOVs, special characters), hurting reproducibility and applicability claims.  
- Weakness: Architectural details are unclear—insufficient explanation/illustration of how embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined and what the designated loss function is.  
- Question: Provide results for all language pairs with only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to validate robustness.  
- Question: How does the proposed method compare to systems interpolating in-domain and out-of-domain models?  
- Question: What preprocessing/filtering was applied for social media text to handle OOVs and special character sequences?  
- Question: Clarify and illustrate how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the CNN architecture.  
- Question: Which "designated loss function" was used and how were system weights tuned in Section 5.2?  
- Recommendation: Promising and effective approach but needs clearer methodological details and stronger generalizability evidence to fully justify impact.
