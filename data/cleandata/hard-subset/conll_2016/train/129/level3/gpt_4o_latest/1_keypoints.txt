- Novel semi-supervised CNN for domain classification and in-domain data selection using word embeddings from unlabeled data.
- Method performs well with very little in-domain data (as few as 100 sentences).
- Extensive empirical validation across multiple language pairs and domains showing robustness and generalizability.
- Novel and practical approach, well-suited for low-resource and emerging-domain scenarios.
- Consistently outperforms LM-based baselines (n-gram and RNN) with statistically significant gains.
- Clear and detailed textual description of the CNN architecture aiding reproducibility.
- Demonstrated robustness to extreme data scarcity, outperforming LM approaches in such settings.
- No comparison to bilingual data selection methods (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences), limiting assessment of relative performance.
- No visual diagram of the CNN architecture to aid reader understanding.
- Unclear experimental details: how many sentences were selected and how CNN hyperparameters were tuned.
- Missing comparison to Axelrod (2015) POS-based method for reducing LM sparsity.
- No experimental exploration of bag-of-words versus sequential representations to justify representation choices.
- Question: How does the method compare to bilingual data selection (sum of cross-entropy differences)?
- Question: Please clarify the sentence-selection process and CNN hyperparameter tuning procedure.
- Question: Have you considered extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting and what challenges would that entail?
- Minor citation formatting issues to fix (\citet vs. \citep).
- Suggestion to include example sentences selected by different methods for qualitative support.
- Suggest adding a Lewis–Moore reference on sum of cross-entropy differences in related work.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor revisions; suitable for acceptance.
