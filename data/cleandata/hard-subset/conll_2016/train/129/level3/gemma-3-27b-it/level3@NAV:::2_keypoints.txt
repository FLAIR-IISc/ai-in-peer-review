- Proposes a CNN classifier to distinguish in-domain vs out-of-domain sentence pairs and use it to select general-domain data for SMT retraining.  
- Reports substantial BLEU improvements (up to +3.1) and claims effectiveness with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates effective data selection with extremely limited in-domain resources, useful for low-resource or rapid adaptation.  
- Experimental strength: evaluated on four language pairs and multiple test domains.  
- Novelty caveat: data selection itself is not new; novelty is mainly the strong performance under tiny in-domain data.  
- Misleading BLEU claim: the reported +3.1 gain depends on in-domain data already present in the general-domain training set.  
- Fair-baseline issue: a baseline interpolating in- and out-of-domain data would be a more appropriate comparison.  
- Small-data experiment limitation: the 100-pair experiment was only run for zh→en and used in-domain examples already present in training.  
- Generalization concern: unclear whether the 100-pair approach works when no in-domain data exists in the initial training set.  
- Missing preprocessing details: no clarity on handling social-media-specific issues (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons) or normalization/filtering steps.  
- Lack of qualitative analysis: paper does not show example sentences selected by the method or what kinds of in-domain relevance are captured.  
- Architectural ambiguity: combination of word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors is not clearly explained; a diagram is needed.  
- Training details missing: the “designated loss function” in Section 3.2 is unspecified.  
- Experimental details missing: how system weights were tuned in Section 5.2 is not described.  
- Requested additional experiments: provide results using 100 in-domain sentence pairs for all tested language pairs.
