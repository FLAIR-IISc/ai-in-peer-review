- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier using pre-trained word embeddings for data selection to adapt SMT with very small amounts of in-domain parallel data.  
- Main claim: CNN-based selection yields meaningful SMT improvements even with as few as 100 labeled in-domain sentences.  
- Secondary claim: Pre-trained embeddings enable effective semi-supervised learning for the CNN classifier.  
- Experiments cover four language pairs and multiple test domains (including social media), supporting generalizability.  
- Strength: Demonstrates compelling performance gains in extremely low-resource in-domain settings.  
- Strength: Thorough comparisons against strong baselines (n-gram and RNN LM-based selection) showing consistent gains.  
- Strength: Broad experimental setup across languages and domains increases confidence in results.  
- Strength: Clear problem definition and strong motivation for alternative data selection techniques.  
- Weakness: Reported large BLEU gains (up to +3.1) are misleading because much improvement comes from in-domain data already present in the general-domain set; better baselines (e.g., interpolated data) are needed.  
- Weakness: Key low-data result may not generalize—strongest effect seen for Chinese→English where in-domain examples existed in training data.  
- Weakness: Insufficient ablation/discussion of preprocessing for noisy social media data (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons).  
- Weakness: Lacks illustrative examples showing which sentences the CNN selected versus baseline methods.  
- Question: Provide results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentences to validate low-resource claims.  
- Question: Detail preprocessing/filtering steps applied to social media data (normalization, handling of usernames/hashtags/emoticons).  
- Question: Supply illustrative examples of sentences selected by the CNN that improved translation quality versus baselines.  
- Question: Clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined (architecture diagram) and specify the “designated loss function.”  
- Question: Clarify specifics of system weight tuning in Section 5.2.  
- Additional comment: References are thorough; discussion should include recent NMT domain-adaptation advances and potential extension of methods to NMT.
