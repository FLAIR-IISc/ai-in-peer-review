- Paper proposes TBuckets, which groups topic words into thematic "buckets" and scores topic coherence using properties of the largest bucket, with three variants (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).
- Evaluated on three public datasets, TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art on two datasets and aids weakly supervised text classification.

- Novel contribution: a new bucket-based topic coherence measure.
- Improved performance: beats prior methods on two of three datasets.
- Parameter-free variants: TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg require no parameter tuning.

- Strength: provides an effective metric for topic coherence.
- Strength: demonstrates utility for improving weakly supervised text classification.
- Strength: parameter-free techniques reduce computational overhead and practical tuning.

- Weakness: insufficient detail on preprocessing and filtering, especially for noisy social media text.
- Weakness: unclear description of how different vectors are combined and of the loss function; needs illustrations/details.
- Weakness: no discussion of system weights tuning or its effect on performance.

- Q to authors: please provide detailed preprocessing and filtering steps used.
- Q to authors: how will TBuckets be extended to applications beyond text classification?
- Q to authors: clarify system weights tuning process and its impact on results.
