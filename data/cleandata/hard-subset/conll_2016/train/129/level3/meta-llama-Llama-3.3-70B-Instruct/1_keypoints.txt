- Proposes TBuckets: groups topic words into thematic buckets and scores topic coherence from the largest bucket using clustering, SVD, and SVD-with-reorg variants.
- Evaluated TBuckets on three public datasets and shown effective at measuring topic coherence and aiding weakly supervised text classification.
- Contribution: Introduces a novel bucket-based method for measuring topic coherence.
- Contribution: Provides empirical evaluation demonstrating TBuckets’ effectiveness.
- Contribution: Demonstrates TBuckets’ utility for selecting high-quality topics in weakly supervised classification.
- Strength: Simple and effective, outperforming state-of-the-art on two of three datasets.
- Strength: SVD-based TBuckets variants require no parameter tuning, increasing practicality.
- Strength: Proven useful in improving weakly supervised text classification performance.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification explaining why TBuckets works.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—only three datasets, possibly not representative.
- Weakness: No comparison with other approaches (e.g., word-embedding–based methods).
- Question: Can the authors provide theoretical justification for TBuckets’ effectiveness?
- Question: How does TBuckets perform across datasets with varying size, domain, and language?
- Question: Can TBuckets be compared against word-embedding–based techniques, with pros/cons discussed?
