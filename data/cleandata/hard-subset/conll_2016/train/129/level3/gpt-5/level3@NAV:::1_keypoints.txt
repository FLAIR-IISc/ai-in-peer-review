- Summary: Proposes a semi-supervised CNN that combines one-hot and pre-trained embeddings to score source and target sentences independently (summed) for SMT data selection, outperforming LM-based baselines across four language directions and working with as few as ~100 in-domain sentences.

- Contribution 1 (empirical): Simple, scalable CNN selection pipeline giving consistent BLEU gains in extremely low in-domain regimes (down to 100 sentences) across multiple language pairs/domains.

- Contribution 2 (practical insight): Semi-supervised embeddings improve robustness over one-hot CNNs and LM-based selection under severe data scarcity.

- Contribution 3 (validation): Broad cross-lingual experiments (zh↔en, ar→en, en→es) with statistically significant improvements, indicating generality.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical gains (up to +3.1 BLEU over alldata) and stable performance at 100-sentence supervision.

- Strength 2: Cross-language and domain robustness increases external validity.

- Strength 3: Clear, reproducible textual description of the CNN and semi-supervised setup.

- Strength 4: Sensible design to score source and target separately and combine, aligning with bilingual selection intuition.

- Weakness 1: Missing key baselines such as bilingual cross-entropy difference/sum (Moore–Lewis/Lewis–Moore) and Axelrod-style source+target comparisons.

- Weakness 2: Limited ablations—no isolation of BOW vs SEQ contributions or justification for CNN over RNN/LSTM.

- Weakness 3: Under-specified experimental protocol for selecting N sentences and CNN hyperparameters; lacking sensitivity curves to N and embedding choices.

- Weakness 4: Fairness concerns—Table 2 does not hold the number of selected sentences constant across methods; same-K comparisons needed.

- Weakness 5: Presentation issues—missing architecture diagram and inconsistent citation/parenthetical style.

- Question 1: Please report same-K comparisons for Table 2 and sensitivity to N (and to hyperparameters/embedding types).

- Question 2: Provide ablations isolating BOW vs SEQ vs combined inputs and compare CNN to RNN/LSTM.

- Question 3: Compare to bilingual cross-entropy sum/difference (source, target, combined) and to Axelrod (2015) POS-replacement LMs.

- Question 4: Include qualitative examples of sentences selected by different methods to support claims in Section 5.4.

- Question 5: Clarify connection to Lewis–Moore sum/difference and consider a bilingual CNN that jointly learns source/target weighting.
