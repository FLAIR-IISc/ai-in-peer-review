- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based (semi-supervised) classifier to score bilingual sentence pairs for in-domain relevance and select SMT training data, showing consistent BLEU gains across four language directions and social-media domains.

- Contribution 1: A practical semi-supervised CNN for bilingual data selection that yields small but consistent gains (~+0.5 BLEU avg.) over strong LM baselines.

- Contribution 2: Evidence that the CNN remains effective with very small in-domain seed data (down to ~100 sentence pairs) in one setting (zh→en).

- Contribution 3: A thorough comparative evaluation against three LM-based selection baselines under large-data conditions.

- Strength: Consistent, statistically significant improvements across four directions using only 2.5–10% of available data.

- Strength: Robustness to small in-domain samples demonstrated convincingly for zh→en (stable gains when seed data shrink).

- Strength: Sensible architecture design (convolution + max-pooling for n-gram signals; combines one-hot regions with embeddings from unlabeled data).

- Strength: Broad, challenging evaluation on social-media domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook).

- Strength: Clear selection protocol (score source and target separately, sum scores; tune selection sizes on held-out data).

- Weakness: Headline +3.1 BLEU on zh→en is misleading because a large in-domain subset exists; training on that subset alone already yields most of the gain.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons to in-domain-only and interpolated/mixed baselines (e.g., phrase-table/LM interpolation, instance weighting).

- Weakness: Small-data robustness claim only validated for one task (zh→en), threatening generality across other directions.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of social-media challenges (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons, noisy tokenization) and no details on preprocessing/normalization.

- Weakness: Architectural and optimization details under-specified (how one-hot and embedding streams are combined, loss function, regularization, dimensions).

- Weakness: Incomplete SMT system details (how log-linear weights were tuned, tuning algorithm, dev set usage, stopping criteria).

- Question: Clarify Section 3.2—exact construction and combination of wl(x) and ul(x), segment/window definitions, loss function, output layer, and optimization method.

- Question: In zh→en, how much selected data overlaps with the 120K in-domain subset, and BLEU for (a) 120K alone, (b) 120K + top-k OOD, (c) excluding 120K from pool?

- Question: Please compare to standard interpolated/mixture baselines (in-domain/out-of-domain LM interpolation, mixture phrase tables, instance weighting).

- Question: Can the “100 in-domain sentences” experiment be repeated for all four directions to support generality?

- Question: Describe social-media preprocessing (normalization/filtering of usernames, hashtags, URLs, emojis, casing, tokenizer) and report OOV rates.

- Question: Provide concrete examples showing what selection improves (source, selected pairs, translations before/after; lexical choice vs. reordering vs. noise reduction).

- Additional: Specify SMT tuning method (MERT/MIRA/PRO), dev set usage, and whether classifier positives overlap with tuning data to avoid overfitting.

- Additional: Report per-task OOV rates, consider subword models or transliteration for social media robustness.

- Additional: Include ablation studies (source-only vs. target-only vs. sum; one-hot vs. embeddings vs. both; selection size sensitivity).

- Additional: Add an architecture figure and complexity/runtime comparison versus LM baselines for clarity.
