- Paper proposes a semi-supervised CNN to score general-domain sentence pairs for in-domain relevance and use top-ranked pairs to train phrase-based SMT.  
- CNN uses one-hot regions plus region embeddings from word2vec; trained with tiny in-domain positives and equal-sized random negatives.  
- Selection applied independently to source and target; scores combined for final selection.  
- Experiments on zh→en, ar→en, en→es, en→zh across sms, tweets, Facebook; compares to Moore-Lewis, RNNLM, and combos.  
- sscnn consistently outperforms LM-based baselines, with up to +3.1 BLEU and average ~+0.5 BLEU over best LM.  
- Shows substantial training set reduction (2.5–10% of full data) while improving BLEU and efficiency.  
- Demonstrates robust small-data behavior for zh→en down to 100 in-domain sentences with low variance.  
- Strength: addresses practical domain adaptation with tiny in-domain data using simple, scalable tools.  
- Strength: strong comparative evaluation across multiple languages, large corpora, and test domains with statistical significance.  
- Strength: ablations show combining source and target selection is best and provide practical guidance on selection sizes.  
- Weakness: headline +3.1 BLEU is misleading because a 120K in-domain subset in training largely explains the gain; CNN adds only ~+0.3 BLEU margin.  
- Weakness: small-data robustness claim is shown only for zh→en where in-domain data exist in training, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: methodological details of architecture and training (feature combination, embedding fine-tuning, loss, regularization, optimization, negative sampling) are unclear.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of social-media preprocessing (tokenization, normalization, Chinese segmentation) and OOV handling; no OOV/perplexity or translation examples.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental; missing comparisons to simpler strong baselines (logistic/SVM/FFNN) and ablations isolating one-hot vs embedding contributions.  
- Request: run 100-sentence experiment for other directions (or at least one without in-domain data) to test robustness.  
- Request: for zh→en report “in-domain-only,” mixed in/out interpolation, and LM-selected subsets approximating the 120K to quantify marginal sscnn benefit.  
- Request: clarify model architecture: how wl(x) and ul(x) combine, whether embeddings are updated, loss, optimizer, learning rate, regularization, early stopping, class handling, negative sampling.  
- Request: detail preprocessing for social media, vocabulary sizes, and OOV rates before/after selection.  
- Request: specify SMT tuning method (MERT/MIRA), dev sets, stopping criteria, tuning iterations, and whether weights were re-tuned per subset size.  
- Additional suggestions: add ablations one-hot vs embeddings vs combined, compare to linear classifier baselines, report training/selection runtime, and provide qualitative selected/non-selected examples.  
- Presentation note: add CNN schematic and move hyperparameter details to appendix to improve reproducibility and readability.
