- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier to score sentence pairs for in-domain SMT data selection using one-hot and embedding-based region representations and combined source/target scores.  
- Primary empirical contribution: modest but consistent gains over strong LM-based selection (+~0.5 BLEU avg) and robustness with tiny in-domain seeds (reported down to 100 sentences).  
- Secondary contribution: a practical selection pipeline demonstrated across four language pairs and social-media test domains.

Strengths:
- Clear empirical advantage over LM selection, with significance testing across four directions.  
- Strong small-data robustness on zh→en, stable down to 100 in-domain pairs with low variance.  
- Sensible modeling choices (source+target score combination), reproducible setup with public toolkits and standard SMT pipelines.  
- Ablation studies reasonably explore selection size and supervision levels, supporting CNN benefits when supervision is scarce.

Weaknesses:
- Headline +3.1 BLEU on zh→en is overstated since in-domain data already exist in the training pool; training on in-domain subset alone gives +2.8 BLEU.  
- No fair comparison to interpolated/mixed in-/out-of-domain systems (phrase-table/LM interpolation) is provided.  
- Small-data claim is compelling only for zh→en; 100-sentence experiments are not reported for the other three tasks, limiting generality.  
- Social-media specifics (high OOVs, usernames/hashtags/emoticons, tokenization/normalization) are under-addressed with no preprocessing/filtering details.  
- Data selection cannot directly fix OOVs; paper lacks qualitative analysis/examples showing what error types improved (lexicon, reordering, named entities).  
- Section 3.2 is underspecified regarding how one-hot, segment embeddings, and architecture are combined, and the exact loss/objective and training schedule.  
- Section 5.2 omits how system (log-linear) weights are tuned and how selected size N was chosen without overfitting.

Questions for authors:
- Please compare to interpolated in-/out-domain models (phrase-table and LM interpolation); does CNN selection still outperform?  
- Report 100-sentence in-domain experiments for all tasks or simulate with proxy domains if direct in-domain overlap is infeasible.  
- What social-media preprocessing was applied (tokenization, casing, hashtag/username segmentation, emoji handling)? Any filtering?  
- Provide qualitative examples of selected sentences and resulting translation improvements and quantify OOV coverage changes.  
- Clarify Section 3.2: exact network topology, fusion of one-hot and segment vectors, loss function, optimization hyperparameters, and whether embeddings are fine-tuned.  
- Specify weight-tuning details in 5.2: optimizer, objective, development set, stopping criterion, and how N (selected size) was chosen.
