- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier that scores source and target sentences to select in-domain parallel data for SMT using as few as 100 labeled sentences.

- Augments one-hot CNN inputs with pre-trained word2vec embeddings to improve robustness and handle unseen semantically similar words.

- Selection ranks sentence pairs by the sum of source and target CNN domain scores and yields SMT gains over LM-based and all-data baselines across multiple language pairs and social-media domains.

- Contribution: a simple, effective semi-supervised CNN selection method that works in extremely low in-domain data regimes and yields substantial BLEU gains (up to +3.1).

- Contribution: extensive empirical validation across four language directions and three social-media domains, showing robustness and low variance with tiny seeds (e.g., +2.1 BLEU with 100 sentences).

- Contribution: practical finding that combining source- and target-side scores outperforms using either side alone.

- Strength: strong improvements over “all data” and LM selection baselines with significance testing, especially in very low-resource settings.

- Strength: broad, convincing evaluation with large out-of-domain pools (9–15M pairs) across multiple domains and languages.

- Strength: practical, reproducible pipeline using off-the-shelf embeddings and standard SMT training.

- Strength: useful analysis explaining why CNN+pooling and embeddings help capture informative n-grams and semantic similarity.

- Weakness: unclear or missing fair comparisons to bilingual LM selection baselines (e.g., source+target cross-entropy, Axelrod POS-replacement).

- Weakness: Table 2 compares methods at different selected-data sizes, confounding conclusions; controlled fixed-budget comparisons are needed.

- Weakness: lacking ablations and empirical rationale for CNN vs RNN/LSTM and for BOW vs SEQ, embeddings vs one-hot contributions.

- Weakness: unclear hyperparameter tuning and selection-budget procedure; sensitivity to these choices in low-data regimes is not reported.

- Weakness: limited qualitative evidence—no example sentences showing differences in selections between methods.

- Question: Do LM baselines implement bilingual selection (source+target cross-entropy or Axelrod 2015 POS-replacement)? If yes, detail score combination and tuning.

- Question: How is N (number of selected pairs) chosen per method and task, which set tunes it, and is it disjoint from dev/tuning sets? Provide BLEU vs N sensitivity plots.

- Question: What is the exact validation protocol for CNN hyperparameters and is nested validation used to avoid overfitting with tiny in-domain data?

- Question: Are in-domain dev sentences reused for both classifier training and SMT tuning (double-dipping)? If so, how is overfitting mitigated?

- Question: What is the computational cost of CNN training and corpus ranking versus LM-based selection (throughput/compute numbers)?

- Additional: add an architecture diagram showing one-hot and embedding region feature combination.

- Additional: include an ablation table (BOW-only, SEQ-only, BOW+SEQ; one-hot vs embeddings vs both; ReLU variants; RNN/LSTM baseline).

- Additional: report results at matched selection sizes in the main table and best-N per method in an appendix.

- Additional: explicitly relate source+target score summation to Moore-Lewis/Axelrod bilingual combinations and discuss sum vs difference choices.

- Additional: consider a bilingual CNN that ingests aligned sentence pairs to learn source–target combination end-to-end.

- Minor: fix inconsistent citation styles, smoothing footnote, and complete/format references.
