- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier to select in-domain training data for SMT using tiny in-domain samples and unlabeled data for embeddings.  
- CNN scores general-domain sentence pairs by source, target, or combined scores; top-ranked pairs train adapted SMT systems.  
- Semi-supervised variant augments one-hot region features with word2vec-derived region embeddings from large unlabeled corpora.  
- Experiments on four translation directions and social-media domains show consistent BLEU gains over all-data and LM-based selection methods.  
- Method is especially robust with very small in-domain samples, showing gains even with a few hundred or ~100 sentences.  
- Contribution: a practical semi-supervised CNN data selection method that outperforms strong n-gram/RNNLM baselines by ~0.5 BLEU on average.
