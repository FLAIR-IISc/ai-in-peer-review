- Proposed semi-supervised CNN classifier that uses a tiny in-domain seed set plus pre-trained word embeddings to score and select sentences from a large general-domain pool for SMT domain adaptation.
- Contribution: Empirically shows CNNs work well for data selection with very small (≈100) in-domain seeds, outperforming LM-based methods in low-resource scenarios.
- Contribution: Semi-supervised CNN yields consistent SOTA improvements (~0.5 BLEU over best LM-based methods) across four language pairs and three social-media domains.
- Strength: Strong, statistically significant empirical results on a challenging, practical domain-adaptation task.
- Strength: Demonstrates robustness to tiny seed sizes—CNN performance remains stable as seed size shrinks while LM methods degrade.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation using multiple language pairs, large-scale corpora, and several established baselines.
- Weakness: +3.1 BLEU headline on zh→en is misleading because a known 120K in-domain subset already explains most of the gain; the method adds only ~0.3 BLEU over that oracle.
- Weakness: Key claim about tiny-data effectiveness is validated only on zh→en (where a clean in-domain block exists), so generalizability to other pairs is unproven.
- Weakness: Methodological details are unclear—how one-hot and embedding-based inputs are combined, which loss function is used, and SMT tuning details are omitted; an architectural diagram is needed.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of social-media-specific issues (OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons) and no qualitative examples showing which translation errors are corrected.
- Question: Please report results for the other three language pairs using only 100 in-domain sentences to test generalization of the tiny-data claim.
- Question: For zh→en, please compare to interpolation of a model trained on the 120K in-domain subset with a general-domain model to clarify the method’s contribution.
- Question: Clarify the semi-supervised CNN architecture—how wl(x) and ul(x) are combined (Equation 2) and which loss function was used.
- Question: Describe any domain-specific preprocessing for social media data and provide concrete translation examples illustrating improvements over the alldata baseline.
