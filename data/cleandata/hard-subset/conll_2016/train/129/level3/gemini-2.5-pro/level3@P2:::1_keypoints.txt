- Proposes framing SMT training-data selection as text classification using a semi-supervised CNN with tiny in-domain seeds and pre-trained embeddings.  
- Novel contribution: applies semi-supervised CNNs to data selection, moving beyond perplexity-based approaches.  
- Empirical claim: outperforms n-gram and RNNLM selection across four language pairs, notably with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Strength: Demonstrates substantial BLEU gains (up to 3.1 points) while often using much less selected data.  
- Strength: Particularly effective and robust in extremely low-resource in-domain settings where LM methods fail.  
- Strength: Broad evaluation across 4 language directions and 3 social-media domains with multiple baselines.  
- Weakness: Uncontrolled comparisons—Table 2 compares methods using different selected-data amounts, preventing fair isolation of selection quality.  
- Weakness: Missing key bilingual baselines (e.g., Moore & Lewis cross-entropy difference, Axelrod et al.), and insufficient positioning relative to bilingual selection heuristics.  
- Weakness: Lacks ablation and qualitative analyses (e.g., bow-regions vs seq-regions, example sentences, architecture diagram) to explain CNN advantages.  
- Question: Please clarify validation procedure for selecting optimal sentence counts and tuning CNN hyperparameters, and sensitivity of SMT performance.  
- Question: How does the CNN method perform versus LM baselines with much larger in-domain corpora (e.g., 30k sentences)?  
- Question: Have you considered a bilingual CNN that ingests sentence pairs jointly instead of summing independent source/target scores?
