- Proposed method: frame SMT data selection as a classification task using a semi-supervised CNN that leverages pre-trained embeddings to score and select sentence pairs from a general-domain pool.
- Main contribution: novel application of a semi-supervised CNN for SMT data selection, effective especially in very low-resource settings.
- Empirical contribution: strong BLEU improvements (up to +3.1) across four language pairs and three social-media domains, outperforming n-gram and RNNLM selection methods.
- Strength: tackles a practical low-resource domain-adaptation problem relevant to social media and fine-grained topics.
- Strength: compelling analysis showing robustness of the CNN methods (especially sscnn) with very small seed sets (100–800 sentences).
- Strength: solid empirical comparison to widely-used monolingual baselines (n-gram LM and RNNLM) with consistent, significant gains.
- Strength: clear and well-explained methodological description of one-hot CNN and semi-supervised extension using pre-trained embeddings.
- Weakness: missing comparison to bilingual selection baselines (e.g., cross-entropy difference, Moore & Lewis) despite conceptual similarity to summing source/target scores.
- Weakness: experimental setup may be unfair because different methods select different numbers of sentences; tuning and validation procedures for selection sizes and CNN hyperparameters are not described, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: no justification for choosing a CNN over other encoders (RNN/LSTM) and no ablation showing the importance of bow-regions vs seq-regions.
- Weakness: presentation issues—no CNN architecture diagram and inconsistent citation formatting.
- Question: why were bilingual LM-based selection methods not included as baselines given the similarity to summed source/target scores?
- Question: please clarify the validation procedure for selecting sentence counts and CNN hyperparameters, and report sensitivity or comparisons with a fixed selection size.
- Question: how does the method perform with much larger in-domain sets (e.g., 10k–50k); does the semi-supervised advantage diminish?
- Question: compare against Axelrod et al. (2015) POS-replacement LM approach to disentangle benefits of embeddings vs the CNN architecture.
- Question: provide qualitative examples of sentences ranked highly by sscnn but not by LM-based methods to illustrate why the method works.
