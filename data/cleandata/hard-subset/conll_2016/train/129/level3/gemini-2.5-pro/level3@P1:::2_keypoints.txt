- Summary: Proposes a semi-supervised CNN that uses a few in-domain sentences + pre-trained embeddings to score and select domain-relevant sentence pairs from a large general corpus to train adapted SMT; tested on 4 language pairs in social-media domains, outperforming LM-based baselines and remaining effective with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.

- Contribution: Demonstrates that a CNN-based semi-supervised classifier can outperform LM-based data selection for SMT domain adaptation, especially in very low-resource in-domain settings.

- Strength — tiny-data performance: Method remains effective with extremely limited in-domain data (e.g., 100 sentences), showing substantial BLEU gains in zh->en.

- Strength — consistent empirical results: sscnn consistently beats strong LM-based baselines across four language pairs and three social-media domains with statistically significant improvements.

- Strength — methodological novelty: Novel use of a neural (CNN) classifier with pre-trained embeddings for SMT data selection, removing need for hand-engineered features.

- Weakness — headline result may mislead: The large +3.1 BLEU zh->en gain largely stems from a 120K clean in-domain subset in the training pool; the true incremental gain over that in-domain-only system is only +0.3 BLEU.

- Weakness — limited validation of tiny-data claim: The 100-sentence robustness is only demonstrated for zh->en (the task with the hidden in-domain subset), so generalizability to other language pairs is unproven.

- Weakness — missing analysis of social-media phenomena: Paper lacks discussion/examples on handling OOVs, usernames/hashtags, nonstandard spellings, and whether preprocessing or special handling was used.

- Weakness — unclear model/experimental details: Section 3.2 is hard to follow (combination of wl(x) and ul(x) unclear), loss function unspecified, and SMT tuning data/process (possible dev set overlap) not clarified.

- Question 1: Compare against an interpolated baseline combining a model trained on the 120K in-domain subset with one trained on remaining data.

- Question 2: Provide results for the other three language pairs when the classifier is trained with only 100 in-domain sentences.

- Question 3: Provide qualitative translation examples and analysis showing how selected data improves social-media translation (lexical choice, fluency, handling of OOVs/hashtags/usernames).

- Question 4: Clarify the sscnn architecture (diagram), specify the loss function used, and state which data were used to tune SMT weights for each condition.
