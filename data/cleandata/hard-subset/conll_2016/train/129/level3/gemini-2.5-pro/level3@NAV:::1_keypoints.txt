Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier trained on small in-domain and general-domain data to score and select sentence pairs for MT domain adaptation, claiming strong low-resource gains.

Introduces a CNN-based discriminative data-selection method that outperforms perplexity-based approaches.

Shows a semi-supervised setup with pre-trained word embeddings is effective for domain adaptation with very limited in-domain seeds.

Addresses the practical problem of MT domain adaptation when only minimal parallel data is available.

Presents strong empirical gains (up to 3.1 BLEU) across four language pairs, outperforming n-gram and RNNLM-based baselines.

Demonstrates robustness in extreme low-data regimes (as few as 100 seed sentences), useful for fine-grained or emerging domains.

Experimental issues: varying amounts of selected data complicate fair comparisons, and the procedure for choosing selection size and CNN hyperparameters lacks detail and sensitivity analysis.

Omits comparisons to relevant bilingual selection baselines (e.g., sum/difference of cross-entropies) and LM sparsity-reduction techniques.

Insufficient architectural motivation and analysis: no empirical CNN vs RNN/LSTM comparison, no ablation for bag-of-words vs sequential components, and no network diagram.
