- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to select in-domain sentence pairs for SMT adaptation using few labeled in-domain examples and pre-trained embeddings.

- Contribution: novel application of a semi-supervised CNN (Johnson & Zhang-style) for bilingual data selection, effective in very low in-domain seed settings (100–800 sentences).

- Contribution: empirical claim that CNN-based selection outperforms strong LM-based methods, showing modest average gains (~+0.5 BLEU) across four language pairs.

- Strength: tackles a practical domain-adaptation problem relevant for low-resource/new domains like social media.

- Strength: strong low-resource result on zh→en, with sscnn stable and yielding +2.1 BLEU over alldata using only 100 seeds.

- Strength: compares against strong, relevant baselines (n-gram cross-entropy difference and RNNLMs).

- Weakness: misleading headline on zh→en—a 120K in-domain subset alone yields 25.7 BLEU, so the proposed method only adds +0.3 BLEU over that strong baseline.

- Weakness: limited evidence of generalizability—the tiny-seed robustness is demonstrated mainly on zh→en and not replicated on the other language pairs.

- Weakness: unclear model description—how one-hot vectors and segment embeddings are combined in Eq.(2) is not explained.

- Weakness: unspecified training details—the "designated loss function" used for the CNN classifier is never specified.

- Weakness: missing SMT tuning details—how final system weights were tuned (e.g., MERT) is omitted.

- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis showing what translation improvements selected data yields (lexical choice, structure); no translation examples provided.

- Weakness: no mention of domain-specific preprocessing or handling of social-media phenomena (hashtags, mentions, emoticons, OOVs).

- Question: given the strong 120K in-domain baseline on zh→en, how should the contribution be reframed and how does your method compare to interpolating models trained on all data and the 120K subset?

- Question: can you replicate the low-seed (e.g., 100 sentences) robustness experiment for ar→en, en→es, and en→zh to support generalizability?

- Question: please clarify the architecture in Section 3.2—how are one-hot and pre-trained segment vectors combined—and specify the CNN loss function.

- Question: did you apply any special preprocessing or filtering for social-media phenomena in training or test data?
