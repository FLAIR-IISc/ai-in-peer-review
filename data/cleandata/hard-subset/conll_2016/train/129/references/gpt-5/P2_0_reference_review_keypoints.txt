- Paper addresses SMT domain adaptation with very little in-domain parallel data using a CNN sentence-level domain classifier.  
- Semi-supervised variant augments one-hot inputs with pre-trained word2vec embeddings and scores source/target separately, summing scores to select sentence pairs.  
- Empirical contribution: sscnn selection outperforms strong LM-based selection (Moore-Lewis n-gram, RNNLM, combo) across four language directions, with notable BLEU gains using only 2.5–10% data.  
- Methodological contribution: shows CNNs with pre-trained embeddings remain robust down to ~100 labeled sentences, beating LM-based selection and supervised one-hot CNN.  
- Practical contribution: demonstrates source+target score summation works best and provides sensitivity analysis across labeled sample sizes with significance testing.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on realistic, difficult social-media domains and multiple language pairs, with statistically significant improvements.  
- Strength: careful experimental design including two CNN variants, per-side classification, controlled labeled-data ablation (6k→100), and bootstrap significance testing.  
- Strength: clear motivation linking CNN/local n-gram features and pre-trained embeddings to robustness with tiny labels.  
- Strength: high practical relevance for rapid adaptation in settings with many topics and scarce in-domain data.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—architecture and embedding use follow prior work (e.g., Johnson & Zhang 2015); novelty mainly in application/protocol.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to simple, strong discriminative baselines (TF-IDF/BM25 centroid, linear SVM/LogReg on bag-of-ngrams, averaged-embedding cosine).  
- Weakness: incomplete ablations/reporting—no detailed results for source-only vs target-only scoring, region-type effects, embedding fine-tuning vs frozen, quantified negative-sampling impact, or runtime/resource costs.  
- Weakness: possible data-usage ambiguity and overfitting risk—positives drawn from dev set commonly used for SMT tuning; unclear if tuning/selection used separate held-out data.  
- Weakness: scope limited to phrase-based SMT; no discussion of applicability to NMT or modern neural training scenarios.  
- Question: are pre-trained embeddings fine-tuned during CNN training, and what regularization was used with tiny labels?  
- Question: how is selection size N chosen for each method, which held-out set is used, and does classifier training dev overlap with SMT tuning?  
- Question: please provide ablations for source-only, target-only, weighted combinations, and report correlation between source/target scores.  
- Question: can authors add baselines TF-IDF/BM25 cosine, averaged word-embeddings + cosine, and linear SVM/LogReg on bag-of-ngrams?  
- Question: report training/inference times and computational resources for sscnn vs RNNLM/ngram selection on a representative language pair.  
- Overall assessment: originality moderate, soundness generally good but needs additional baselines/ablations, significance high, clarity good with some ambiguities, empirical evaluation strong but missing simple baselines and runtime analysis.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance contingent on clarifying data usage and adding or discussing the missing baselines and ablations.
