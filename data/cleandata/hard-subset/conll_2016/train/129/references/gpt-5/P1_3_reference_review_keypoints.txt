- Tackles SMT domain adaptation with very small in-domain labels by training sentence-level CNN classifiers (one-hot and semi-supervised with pretrained embeddings).  
- Classifier scores are used to select top-scoring sentence pairs from general-domain bitext to train adapted SMT systems.  
- Experiments on zh→en, ar→en, en→es, en→zh in social-media domains show semi-supervised CNN outperforms training on all data and LM-based selection (Moore-Lewis, RNNLM).  
- Substantial BLEU gains achieved using only 2.5–10% of training data; method effective down to ~100 labeled in-domain sentences.  
- Contribution: empirical finding that semi-supervised CNN with pretrained embeddings enables effective selection under extreme label scarcity, outperforming LM baselines.  
- Contribution: practical recipe combining source- and target-side CNN scores to select small subsets that beat full-data phrase-based SMT at scale.  
- Contribution: thorough analysis of robustness to labeled-sample size (6k → 100), showing graceful degradation and advantage over LM methods in low-label regimes.  
- Strength: Strong, consistent BLEU improvements on small selected subsets, notably +3.1 BLEU on zh→en (sms) relative to all-data and beating an in-domain oracle.  
- Strength: Addresses realistic low-resource social-media adaptation setting, requiring only a dev set for positive labels and leveraging unlabeled text for embeddings.  
- Strength: Solid comparisons to competitive baselines with significance testing (Moore-Lewis, RNNLM, combination) and held-out tuning for N.  
- Strength: Simple and reproducible methodology using standard toolkits (word2vec, public CNN code) and modest architecture choices.  
- Strength: Informative labeled-sample-size analysis showing CNN methods outperform LM methods even with 100–800 labels.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—model is a direct application of prior text-CNN work; contribution is primarily empirical/engineering.  
- Weakness: Missing baselines and ablations (tf-idf logistic/SVM, modern neural LMs like LSTM/GRU, instance weighting, embeddings-only vs. one-hot vs. both).  
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps—omitted optimizer, batch size, epochs, learning rate, regularization, early stopping, and word2vec hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to phrase-based SMT; no exploration of NMT, modest gains on some language pairs, and no runtime/cost comparisons to LM methods.  
- Weakness: Potential confound in zh→en due to a hidden 120k in-domain subset in the pool; CNN may simply rediscover that subset—needs deeper analysis.
