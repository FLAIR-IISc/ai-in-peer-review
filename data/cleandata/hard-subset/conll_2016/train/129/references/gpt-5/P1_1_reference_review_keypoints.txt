- Task: Domain adaptation for phrase-based SMT with extremely small in-domain supervision (~100 sentences) using monolingual CNN classifiers and selection of top-N parallel sentence pairs.
- Core method: Train semi-supervised CNNs (one-hot + pre-trained embeddings) on source and target sides, sum scores to rank parallel sentences, train SMT on selected subset.

Contributions:
- Empirical finding: sscnn enables highly effective data selection with tiny supervision, outperforming Moore-Lewis, RNNLM, and their combo across 4 language directions, up to +3.1 BLEU.
- Practical recipe: Combining source- and target-side monolingual CNN scores with large-scale experiments (9–15M pairs), significance testing, and stability analysis for tiny supervision.
- Selection efficiency: Small subsets (2.5–10% of data) can beat training on all data and sometimes exceed large “in-domain-only” subsets.

Strengths:
- Addresses an important, realistic low-resource adaptation setting with consistent gains across languages and social-media domains.
- Beats strong, relevant LM-based baselines with statistical significance and low variance even with only 100 labeled sentences.
- Simple, scalable, and easy-to-deploy approach requiring only a small in-domain dev set and general-domain unlabeled data.
- Design choices (source+target scoring, pre-trained embeddings) are sensible and empirically supported; ablation shows embeddings help.
- Clear qualitative motivation explaining why CNNs+embeddings help in tiny-data regimes.

Weaknesses:
- Limited methodological novelty: straightforward application of known CNN classification techniques without new bilingual-specific modeling.
- Missing ablations and diagnostics: lacks source-only vs target-only comparisons, finer-grained embedding analyses, region/filter experiments, and distributional diagnostics (lexical coverage, OOVs, length/style).
- Potential confound in zh→en: a known 120K in-domain subset in the pool may inflate gains; overlap and exclusion analyses are missing.
- Reproducibility issues: sparse details on preprocessing, tokenization, vocab/OOV handling, word2vec and classifier training hyperparameters, and N tuning procedure.
- Scope limited to phrase-based SMT and hard selection; no evaluation on NMT, weighting strategies, or integration into phrase-table weighting.

Questions for authors / requested analyses:
- Report BLEU for selection by source-only, target-only, and summed scores; clarify if source/target CNNs are trained independently and whether bilingual models were tried.
- Provide overlap analysis for zh→en: fraction of selected sentences from the known 120K and results when that subset is removed.
- Give detailed ablations on embeddings (sscnn vs ohcnn), region types (bow vs seq), filter/window sizes, embedding hyperparameters, and randomly initialized vs pre-trained embeddings.
- Explain how N is tuned in real settings without test references; show BLEU vs N curves for more tasks and describe validation metric.
- Provide preprocessing details: tokenization/segmentation (esp. Chinese), casing, punctuation normalization, max sentence length, vocab sizes, and OOV handling.
- Report whether classifier scores were tried as instance weights instead of hard selection, and how weighting compares.
- Provide efficiency comparisons: training/inference cost of CNN classifiers vs LM- and RNNLM-based selection on the reported datasets.
