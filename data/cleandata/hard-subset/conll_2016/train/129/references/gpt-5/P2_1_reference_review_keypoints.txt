- Tackles SMT domain adaptation with tiny in-domain data by using a sentence-level CNN to select in-domain-like subsets from large general corpora for phrase-based SMT.
- Primary empirical contribution: a semi-supervised CNN (pretrained embeddings + one-hot inputs) outperforms strong LM-based selection (Moore–Lewis n-gram, RNNLM, combination) across four directions, often using only 2.5–10% of data, with up to +3.1 BLEU and effectiveness down to 100 labeled sentences.
- Secondary contribution: a practical recipe of scoring source and target sides independently with the CNN and summing scores, with demonstrated stability across tiny labels and multiple social-media domains.

- Strong, practical empirical gains under realistic constraints, improving BLEU over training on all data and LM baselines across large corpora.
- Addresses a timely and challenging scenario (social media adaptation) and shows robustness even with extremely small labeled sets (100 pairs).
- Semi-supervised design (pretrained embeddings) plausibly improves stability; experiments show low variance across random tiny-label samples.
- Comparisons to strong LM baselines with significance testing and specification of toolkits/hyperparameters aid interpretability.
- Method is conceptually simple and computationally efficient in training data used, enabling practical deployment.

- Limited methodological novelty; architecture largely follows prior work (Johnson & Zhang 2015) with minor twists and minimal ablations.
- Potential data leakage/unclear validation protocol: dev used as positives but selection-size tuning claims a “separate validation set” without clear splits.
- Reproducibility gaps: SMT pipeline, tokenization/segmentation (esp. Chinese), tuning algorithm, LM training data, vocabulary handling, and code/selected-sentence lists are not fully specified.
- Baseline breadth and diagnostics limited: no simple discriminative baselines (tf-idf+LR/SVM), no random-selection control, missing classifier metrics and overlap/analysis details.
- Computational costs unreported: runtime, memory, and throughput for scoring millions of pairs are not provided nor compared to LM-based selection.

- How was the “separate validation set” constructed for selection-size tuning, and were dev/test splits handled to avoid leakage?
- What exact SMT setup was used (toolkit, tuning algorithm, tokenization/truecasing, Chinese segmentation, normalization, LM data) for each direction?
- What are domain classifier metrics (accuracy/AUC) on held-out validation and how do they correlate with BLEU vs. selected set size?
- For zh→en, how much of the known 120K in-domain subset is recovered by top CNN selections versus simply rediscovering it?
- Were simpler discriminative baselines and a random selection baseline at matched sizes evaluated, and how do they compare?
- Are embeddings fixed or fine-tuned during CNN training, and how sensitive are results to embedding dimension and region/pooling hyperparameters?
- What are the computational costs (CNN training time, scoring throughput) compared to LM-based selection?

- Novelty: Moderate (application-focused with limited model innovation).
- Significance: High practical impact for low in-domain data scenarios with consistent multi-directional gains.
- Soundness: Generally sound but needs clarification on validation protocol and additional diagnostics.
- Clarity: Mostly clear, with some typos and missing methodological/data-split details.
- Reproducibility: Partial; some toolkits/hyperparams given but key SMT/preprocessing details and code are missing.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying validation protocol and adding a simple discriminative or random-selection baseline.
