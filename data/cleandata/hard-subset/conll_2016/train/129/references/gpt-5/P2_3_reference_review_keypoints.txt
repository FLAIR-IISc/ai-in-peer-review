- Paper proposes semi-supervised CNN sentence classifier using pre-trained embeddings to select general-domain parallel sentences for SMT domain adaptation.
- Main empirical claim: CNN-based selection outperforms n-gram and RNNLM selection across four language directions and social-media domains with up to +3.1 BLEU using 2.5–10% of data.
- Shows robustness in very low-label regimes: with as few as 100 in-domain sentences the method still improves BLEU significantly and with low variance.
- Provides a practical selection recipe (CNN hyperparams, word2vec pretraining, source+target score combination) intended to be easy to implement and scale.
- Strong large-scale evaluation across four directions, realistic domains, comparisons to competitive LM baselines, and statistical significance testing.
- Directly addresses the challenging tiny in-domain labeled-data scenario with progressive reduction experiments down to 100 sentences.
- Method is simple and plausible, leveraging CNN local n-gram feature extraction and embeddings for semantic generalization.
- Practical pipeline uses standard tools (word2vec, conText) and straightforward positive/negative data construction, lowering adoption barriers.
- Limited novelty: approach mainly adapts established CNN classification and semi-supervised embedding ideas to data selection.
- Ambiguity in methodological description and reproducibility: unclear how “segment embeddings” are learned, architecture details, freezing vs training, training regimes, and validation set construction.
- Baselines could be broader: missing comparisons to discriminative classifiers (SVM/LogReg with n-grams/embeddings) and neural sequence models (BiLSTM/GRU).
- Bilingual information is underused: source and target scored independently and summed, ignoring cross-lingual interactions and joint bilingual encoders.
- Claim that sum of source and target scores is best lacks quantitative ablation evidence in the paper.
- Potential selection bias: zh→en sms strong gain may be inflated by a hidden 120K in-domain subset in the training pool, raising generalization concerns.
- Request clarification of semi-supervised architecture: how ul(x) segment embeddings are computed and which parameters are frozen vs tuned.
- Ask how selection size N is chosen and details of the “separate validation set” to avoid test-domain tuning.
- Request quantitative comparison of source-only, target-only, and source+target scoring to substantiate the “sum is best” claim.
- Ask for training/inference cost comparisons (wall-clock time, hardware) between ohCNN/ssCNN and LM-based selection at scale.
- Recommend adding non-CNN discriminative baselines (e.g., linear SVM on n-grams with embeddings) and/or BiLSTM to test CNN necessity.
- Overall assessment: practical and effective application with strong evidence in low-label regimes, but incremental novelty and reproducibility/coverage gaps.
