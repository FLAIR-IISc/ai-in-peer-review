- Paper proposes a semi-supervised CNN to classify sentence pairs as in-domain vs out-of-domain for SMT adaptation with very limited in-domain data.
- Uses pre-trained word embeddings to compensate for scarce in-domain labels and selects data for SMT training.
- Experiments on four language pairs and multiple informal domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook) show improvements over baseline data selection methods.
- Empirically demonstrates CNN effectiveness down to as few as 100 in-domain sentences for domain adaptation.
- Combines pre-trained embeddings with CNNs to leverage large unlabeled corpora for semantic representation.
- Presents a thorough experimental evaluation across languages and domains to support generalizability.
- Reports strong BLEU gains (up to 3.1) and outperforms RNNLM-based data selection baselines.
- Addresses a practically relevant problem of low-resource domain adaptation where labeled data is expensive.
- Methodology is clearly explained and uses standard, reproducible tools (word2vec, SMT toolkits).
- Includes a valuable ablation study showing robustness of CNNs versus language-model methods with tiny data.
- Uses bootstrap resampling for statistical significance testing, strengthening result reliability.
- CNN architecture itself is relatively standard, so architectural novelty is limited.
- Lacks detailed error analysis explaining why certain sentences are selected or misclassified.
- Success depends on quality of general-domain pre-trained embeddings; no sensitivity analysis provided.
- Framed in SMT only; broader applicability to NMT is not explored, limiting impact.
- Asks authors to provide example sentence pairs where the CNN succeeds/fails compared to baselines.
- Requests experiments or results using different pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe).
- Asks whether the approach was tried with NMT and what the outcomes were.
- Seeks clarification on computational cost of training and applying the CNN at large scale.
- Paper is well-written with comprehensive, up-to-date references.
- Suggests adding a table summarizing CNN hyperparameters per language pair.
- Overall assessment: strong, practical contribution with solid empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
