- Paper proposes a semi-supervised CNN to classify sentence pairs and select general-domain data for SMT when only tiny in-domain parallel data is available, using pre-trained word embeddings.
- Shows significant BLEU improvements across four language pairs and multiple social-media domains, e.g., +3.1 BLEU for Chinese→English SMS with 3% in-domain data.
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that CNNs are effective for data selection in very low-resource domain adaptation.
- Provides a practical system and strong empirical results versus strong baselines (LM-based selection).
- Offers analysis explaining why the CNN works: convolution captures domain-specific cues and pre-trained embeddings bolster learning.
- Strength: addresses a highly relevant, practical low-resource domain adaptation problem.
- Strength: strong experimental design—multiple languages, domains, and comparisons to established baselines.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with reproducible experimental details.
- Strength: substantial BLEU gains despite minimal in-domain data.
- Strength: thorough ablation study on varying amounts of in-domain data demonstrating robustness.
- Weakness: CNN architecture is fairly standard; novelty is mainly its application to this problem, not model innovation.
- Weakness: lacks qualitative error analysis of misclassified sentence pairs to explain selection failures.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning (search ranges, held-out set size, selection criteria).
- Weakness: dependency on pre-trained embeddings not evaluated for sensitivity to embedding source or quality.
- Question: request qualitative error analysis with examples of misclassified sentences and explanations.
- Question: request detailed hyperparameter tuning process and value ranges explored.
- Question: ask whether different pre-trained embeddings were tested and how sensitive results are to them.
- Question: ask about computational cost of CNN training and selection versus LM-based methods for large datasets.
- Question: ask about potential applicability and benefits of this semi-supervised CNN approach for NMT domain adaptation.
- Additional comments: introduction could be more concise; related work is comprehensive; references, tables, and figures are clear.
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution with robust evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing error analysis and hyperparameter details.
