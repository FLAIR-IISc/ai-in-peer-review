- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN with pre-trained word embeddings to select in-domain data for SMT adaptation with very small labeled sets (down to 100 sentences).
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements over baseline data selection across four language pairs and social-media domains (SMS, Tweets, Facebook).
- Practical, reproducible pipeline using standard tools (word2vec, SMT toolchains) and public datasets.
- Strength: thorough experiments with ablation studies and statistical significance testing.
- Strength: effective use of pre-trained embeddings to compensate for scarce in-domain supervision.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and adequate related work context.
- Strength: reproducibility due to use of standard tools and detailed experimental setup.
- Strength: high practical impact for real-world MT domain adaptation.
- Weakness: limited novelty—individual components (CNNs, embeddings, data selection) are established; novelty is primarily in the application/combination.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on CNN architecture choices (layers, filter sizes, activations, pooling); lacks diagram/hyperparameter table.
- Weakness: lacks error analysis—no investigation of misclassified sentences or challenging linguistic phenomena.
- Weakness: limited comparison/discussion relative to recent neural MT domain-adaptation techniques.
- Question: please provide detailed CNN architecture (layers, filter sizes, activations, pooling) and rationale for choices.
- Question: can you show examples of correctly/incorrectly classified sentences and error patterns?
- Question: how would this approach compare to NMT adaptation methods (e.g., fine-tuning pre-trained NMT on small in-domain data)?
- Question: how does the 120K in-domain overlap in the zh→en task affect generalizability when such overlap is absent?
- Additional comment: related work discussion should more directly address recent SMT and NMT domain-adaptation advances.
- Additional comment: include a table summarizing key hyperparameters used in experiments.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address CNN architecture details and error analysis.
