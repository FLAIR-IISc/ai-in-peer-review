- Summary: Semi-supervised CNNs with pre-trained word embeddings are used to select in-domain sentence pairs for SMT when only tiny labeled in-domain data is available, improving translation quality.  
- Contribution 1: Demonstrates effective use of CNNs for data selection in low-resource domain adaptation.  
- Contribution 2: Empirically validated across four language pairs and multiple social-media domains.  
- Contribution 3: Method remains effective with extremely small labeled sets (as few as 100 sentences).  
- Strength 1: Strong empirical results with significant BLEU gains and significance testing.  
- Strength 2: Clear problem definition and well-motivated use of CNNs and semi-supervision.  
- Strength 3: Well-structured, readable paper with thorough experimental setup.  
- Strength 4: Rigorous comparisons to multiple strong baselines and ablation studies.  
- Strength 5: Insightful discussion linking results to CNNs and pre-trained embeddings.  
- Weakness 1: CNN architecture itself is not novel; application is the main novelty.  
- Weakness 2: Limited detail and analysis of hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity.  
- Weakness 3: Lacks in-depth error analysis of misclassified or harmful selected data.  
- Weakness 4: Focuses on sentence-level selection; phrase/word-level alternatives are unexplored.  
- Question 1: Please provide details on hyperparameter ranges and observed sensitivities (window size, stride, neuron counts).  
- Question 2: Have you conducted error analysis to characterize misclassified sentences?  
- Question 3: Did you try alternative CNN architectures or attention mechanisms, and what were the outcomes?  
- Question 4: What is the computational cost of training/selecting with the CNN vs. LM-based methods?  
- Additional comment 1: References are well-cited.  
- Additional comment 2: Use of publicly available datasets is commendable.  
- Additional comment 3: Please mention computational resources used for experiments.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-evaluated paper suitable for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
