- Paper addresses SMT domain adaptation with extremely limited in-domain data using a semi-supervised CNN-based sentence-pair classifier.
- Method: train CNNs with a few in-domain examples plus out-of-domain negatives, leveraging pre-trained word embeddings to select relevant general-domain sentence pairs for SMT training.
- Shows empirical improvements over LM-based data selection, notably a 3.1 BLEU gain on Chinese→English SMS and gains even with only 100 in-domain sentences.
- Contribution 1: Demonstrates CNNs can effectively identify in-domain data in very low-resource settings as an alternative to LM-based methods.
- Contribution 2: Combines semi-supervised learning with pre-trained embeddings to boost performance when in-domain data is scarce.
- Contribution 3: Provides experiments across four language pairs and multiple informal domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook), supporting generalizability.
- Strength: Tackles a practical, widely relevant problem in MT deployment where in-domain data is limited.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with statistically validated BLEU improvements.
- Strength: Clear, well-defined experimental setup and fair baselines, including bootstrap resampling for significance testing.
- Strength: Well-written paper with logical flow and clear motivation.
- Strength: Insightful discussion linking CNN benefits to embeddings and domain indicator capture.
- Weakness: CNN architecture is fairly standard; novelty is mainly in application rather than model design.
- Weakness: Paper lacks detailed error analysis showing what sentence types are misclassified and why.
- Weakness: Sparse details on hyperparameter tuning (search space, selection criteria), hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: Experiments use only public datasets; performance on proprietary/unique domains is untested.
- Weakness: Unclear rationale and analysis for combining source and target classification scores (sum vs. other methods).
- Question: Provide detailed hyperparameter search space and selection procedure for CNN training.
- Question: Share examples and analysis of common misclassification types and underlying causes.
- Question: Why sum source and target in-domain scores; were alternatives (avg, max) tried and with what results?
- Question: What is the computational cost of CNN-based selection compared to LM-based selection?
- Question: How would online dynamic adaptation be implemented and what are the expected challenges?
- Additional comment: Some citations could be more specific (e.g., include years for Axelrod et al.).
- Additional comment: Visualizing learned embeddings could help illustrate domain-specific term clustering.
- Overall recommendation: Solid, practical contribution with strong results; recommend acceptance pending clarifications on weaknesses.
