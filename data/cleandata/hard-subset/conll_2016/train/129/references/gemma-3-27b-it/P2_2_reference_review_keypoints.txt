- Addresses SMT domain adaptation with extremely limited in-domain data using a semi-supervised CNN classifier for sentence selection.  
- Leverages pre-trained word embeddings to compensate for scarce in-domain supervision.  
- Uses the CNN classifier to select a subset of general-domain data for SMT training.  
- Demonstrates improvements across four language pairs and three domains, especially in very low-resource settings.  
- Practical approach effective in challenging, low-resource domain adaptation scenarios.  
- Shows CNNs + embeddings can differentiate in-domain vs out-of-domain sentences for data selection.  
- Comprehensive experimental validation with multiple languages, domains, and strong baselines.  
- Strong empirical gains (e.g., >3 BLEU on Chinese→English SMS) and useful ablation showing benefits with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Clear problem motivation and relevance to emerging domains like social media.  
- Methodology is well-grounded using established CNNs and pre-trained embeddings in a semi-supervised framework.  
- Thorough experimental setup including multiple baselines and detailed analysis.  
- Limited novelty: individual components (CNNs, embeddings) are not new; novelty is in their application to this task.  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning (search space and selection criteria) and possible generalization issues.  
- CNN remains a “black box”; more interpretation (filter visualization, influential words/phrases) would be valuable.  
- Focuses mainly on data selection; does not explore further SMT adaptation (e.g., fine-tuning on selected data).  
- Questions requested: details of hyperparameter search and metrics, techniques for CNN interpretability, experiments on post-selection SMT fine-tuning, and performance when larger in-domain corpora are available.  
- Writing, related work coverage, and experiment presentation are clear; code/script references are appreciated.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed paper with strong empirical support; recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions to address weaknesses and questions.
