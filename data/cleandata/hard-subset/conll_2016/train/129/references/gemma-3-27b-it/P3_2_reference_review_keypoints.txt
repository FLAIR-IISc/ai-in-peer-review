- Addresses SMT domain adaptation with very limited in-domain data using a semi-supervised CNN to select in-domain sentence pairs from large general corpora; effective even with ~100 in-domain sentences.
- Empirically demonstrates CNNs can guide data selection for low-resource domain adaptation in SMT.
- Shows benefit of semi-supervision via pre-trained word embeddings and unlabeled general-domain data.
- Provides comprehensive experiments across four language pairs and multiple social-media domains.
- Strong experimental results, e.g., +3.1 BLEU on zh→en SMS and gains with only 100 in-domain sentences.
- Well-motivated problem relevant to social media MT and clearly presented methodology.
- Thorough comparison to multiple strong baselines, including LM-based selection methods.
- Robustness analysis showing performance as a function of in-domain training size.
- Methodological components (CNNs, embeddings, data selection) are not individually novel; novelty is mainly in their combination.
- Lacks qualitative/error analysis explaining why selected data improves translation and which errors are reduced.
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity (window sizes, embedding dims, pooling).
- Implicit domain assumptions: selected test domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook) limit claims about truly unknown domains.
- Request for more detail on hyperparameter search strategies and sensitivity of results.
- Request for example sentences selected (both helpful and unhelpful) and analysis of their characteristics.
- Clarification requested about the 120K in-domain subset in zh→en: whether it was known a priori and used for tuning.
- Ask whether different CNN architectures (deeper nets, alternative pooling) were explored.
- Minor issues: inconsistent citation style across the paper.
- Suggest adding discussion of computational cost relative to baselines.
- Overall: strong empirical paper with practical significance; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
