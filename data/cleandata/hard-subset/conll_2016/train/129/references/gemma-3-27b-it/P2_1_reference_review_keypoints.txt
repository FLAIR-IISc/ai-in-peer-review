- Paper proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier using pre-trained word embeddings to select in-domain training data for SMT when only tiny in-domain corpora are available.  
- Evaluated across four language pairs and social-media domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook), showing robust generalization.  
- Demonstrates significant BLEU improvements (up to +3.1), especially in very low-resource settings (as few as 100 in-domain sentences).  
- Main contribution: effective application of CNNs + embeddings for low-resource data selection rather than novel model components.  
- Strong experimental results that consistently outperform strong baselines in low-resource adaptation.  
- Well-motivated problem addressing practical MT adaptation needs with scarce in-domain data.  
- Comprehensive and diverse empirical evaluation enhances credibility.  
- Clear, concise writing and well-presented experimental setup and tables.  
- Insightful analysis attributing gains to convolutional feature extraction and pre-trained embeddings.  
- Limited novelty of individual components; novelty is primarily in application and empirical validation.  
- Lacks detailed ablation study to quantify contributions of components (e.g., embeddings vs. semi-supervised learning vs. CNN).  
- Insufficient details on hyperparameter tuning (search space and optimization strategy) for reproducibility.  
- Dependence on quality/coverage of pre-trained embeddings could limit applicability; needs discussion/mitigation.  
- Questions: request more hyperparameter details, impact of embedding vocabulary size, alternative score-combination methods, and CNN training cost vs. baselines.  
- Suggests comparing to more recent neural data-selection methods beyond the RNNLM baseline and fixing inconsistent citation style.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses.
