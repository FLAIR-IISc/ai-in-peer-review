- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN framework for domain adaptation that works with extremely limited in-domain data (as few as 100 sentences).  
- Evaluated across four language pairs and social-media domains, showing consistent BLEU gains up to 3.1, especially when in-domain data is scarce.  
- Argues and analyzes that CNNs with pre-trained word embeddings capture semantic similarity (e.g., brand names) enabling effective classification with little in-domain data.  

- Tackles a practical MT problem and shows real-world applicability by improving translation with tiny in-domain datasets (e.g., 2.1 BLEU with 100 sentences).  
- Presents strong, well-documented experimental results and ablation studies across multiple languages and domains.  
- Provides a clear technical rationale: convolutional layers capture local domain features and embeddings support semantic generalization.  
- Compares against multiple strong baselines with statistical significance testing, showing sscnn outperforms them.  

- Lacks sufficient implementation details (e.g., exact model settings and how variable sentence lengths are handled), hindering reproducibility.  
- Baseline comparison is limited and dated (primarily Duh et al. 2013); missing comparisons to more recent domain-adaptation methods.  
- Real-world applicability concern: using the development set as in-domain data may not reflect practical scenarios where representative dev data is unavailable.  
- Omits analysis of computational cost and training time, which is important for large-scale and multi-domain systems.  
- Shows inconsistent gains across language pairs (notably stronger Zh→En than En→Zh) without explanation.  

- How are variable sentence lengths processed before feeding into the CNN?  
- What explains the large performance gap between Chinese→English and English→Chinese tasks?  
- Can you compare against more recent state-of-the-art domain-adaptation techniques (e.g., NMT-based or newer data-selection methods)?  
- How would the method perform if no representative development set is available for the test domain?  

- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions to address reproducibility, stronger/broader baselines, real-world applicability, and computational-cost analysis.
