- Addresses domain adaptation in SMT with tiny in-domain data (100–1000 sentences) using semi-supervised learning.
- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier for selecting relevant out-of-domain training sentences by leveraging large unlabeled corpora for word embeddings.
- Evaluated on four language pairs (ZH→EN, AR→EN, EN→ES, EN→ZH) and social-media domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook).
- Reports significant BLEU improvements (up to 3.1) using about 3% of training data and remains effective with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.
- Main contribution: novel semi-supervised CNN framework for translation domain adaptation with minimal in-domain data.
- Main contribution: comprehensive experiments showing consistent BLEU gains across languages and domains.
- Main contribution: demonstrates effectiveness even when no in-domain examples exist in the general-domain corpus.
- Strength: tackles a highly relevant practical problem of adapting MT to new domains with scarce labeled data.
- Strength: strong and convincing experimental results and ablation studies demonstrating robustness to tiny in-domain sizes.
- Strength: well-motivated use of semi-supervised learning and word embeddings to bridge limited labeled data.
- Strength: thorough analysis of behavior as in-domain data varies, showing CNN advantage with less data.
- Strength: practical applicability — works with standard SMT toolkits and small in-domain datasets.
- Weakness: evaluation focuses on SMT while NMT is now the dominant paradigm; lacks NMT comparisons.
- Weakness: missing discussion of computational cost for CNN training versus baseline LM methods.
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases and extreme domain shifts.
- Weakness: no comparisons to newer text classification methods (e.g., transformers) or advanced CNN variants.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and search procedures, hindering reproducibility.
- Questions: how does CNN training/use cost compare to LM baselines under resource constraints?
- Questions: can the approach be applied or adapted to NMT systems, and how?
- Questions: provide more hyperparameter tuning details (region size, stride, neuron counts).
- Questions: what specific limitations were observed and how could they be addressed?
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated paper with compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address cost and hyperparameter details.
