- Proposes a CNN classifier for MT training data selection and thoroughly compares to continuous/n-gram LM baselines, but lacks comparison to bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of differences in cross-entropies).  
- Motivation for using a CNN over RNN/LSTM is eventually convincing: CNNs better identify important sentence sections.  
- No experimental evidence showing whether BOW, SEQ, or combined representations are most important or why.  
- Textual description of the CNN (one-hot vs. pre-trained embeddings) is clear, but a visual diagram of CNN layers would improve clarity.  
- Paper is well-written overall but citation styles are inconsistently applied.  
- Unclear how the number of selected in-domain sentences is determined from a separate validation set and what validation data was used.  
- Hyperparameter selection and sensitivity for the CNN models are not described.  
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 hints the proposed method still wins, but explicit comparisons would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Suggest running experiments with more in-domain data beyond the development set.  
- Recommend showing example sentences selected by different methods to support claims in section 5.4.  
- Propose comparing against Axelrod (2015) (POS-tag replacements) to test whether word2vec embeddings add value over surface-form abstraction.  
- Note that summing source and target classification scores is similar to Lewis-Moore LM selection (sum of cross-entropy differences); add that reference.  
- Suggest exploring a bilingual/parallel extension of the CNN to learn weights for combining source and target classification scores.
