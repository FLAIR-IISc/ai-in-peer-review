- Paper proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT, building on Johnson and Zhang (2015).  
- Method yields ~0.5 BLEU improvement over language model–based data selection and shows robustness with very small in-domain data.  
- Authors report a 3.1 BLEU improvement, but large gains appear only when in-domain data are included in training.  
- Training exclusively on in-domain data already gives a +2.8 BLEU gain, so comparison to model interpolation (separate in-/out-domain models) is needed.  
- Second experiment is convincing: the CNN classifier works with minimal in-domain data, but it was tested only on zh→en where in-domain data existed in training.  
- Question whether the minimal-data result holds when no in-domain data are present in training; en→es and en→zh dev sets suggest it might.  
- Recommend reporting results for all tasks with training limited to just 100 in-domain sentence pairs to strengthen the claim.  
- Social-media domain has high OOVs and token types (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); ask whether any preprocessing/filtering was applied.  
- Data selection cannot fix OOVs alone; request concrete translation examples to show the nature of adaptation improvements.  
- Section 3.2 needs clarity on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined; an architecture illustration would help.  
- Clarify what the “designated loss function” in Section 3.2 refers to.  
- Section 5.2 should state how system weights were tuned for completeness.
