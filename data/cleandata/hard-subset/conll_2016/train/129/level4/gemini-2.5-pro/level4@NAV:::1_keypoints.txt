- Proposes CNN-based MT training data selection and compares favorably to n-gram and continuous-space LM baselines.  
- Unclear whether method was compared to bilingual data selection techniques (e.g., summed cross-entropy difference).  
- Reasoning for choosing CNN over RNN/LSTM is argued (focus on sentence segments), but initial motivation was not obvious.  
- Missing ablation study to quantify the contributions of Bag-of-Words (BOW) vs. sequential (SEQ) representations or their combination.  
- CNN model description (one-hot and pre-trained embeddings, architecture) is clear and detailed.  
- Paper would benefit from a diagram of the network architecture and data flow for clearer presentation.  
- Well-written overall but minor citation/formatting issues (use of \citet vs. \citep).  
- Experimental results support claims, but the procedure for choosing the number of selected sentences (validation set) is unspecified.  
- The dataset used for CNN hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity to validation choices are not described.  
- Table 2 should show results for all methods using the same number of selected sentences for a fairer comparison.  
- Recommend experiments with more in-domain data (beyond dev set) and qualitative examples of selected sentences.  
- Suggest comparisons to Axelrod (2015) and Moore & Lewis (2010), and consideration of learning weights when combining source and target scores in a bilingual CNN extension.
