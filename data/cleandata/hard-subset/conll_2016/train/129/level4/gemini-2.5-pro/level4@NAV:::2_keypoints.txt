- Presents a CNN-based in-domain data selection method for SMT that leverages Johnson and Zhang (2015).  
- Method outperforms language model–based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and stays effective with very small in-domain data.  
- Authors claim a 3.1 BLEU improvement, but much of that gain occurs because in-domain data is present in training; training only on in-domain gives +2.8 BLEU.  
- Recommend comparing to model interpolation between separately trained in-domain and out-of-domain systems.  
- Second experiment’s key finding is CNN effectiveness with minimal in-domain data, but it was run only on zh-en where genuine in-domain data exists in training.  
- Question generalizability: unclear if small-data effectiveness holds for other tasks lacking in-domain training data; en-es and en-zh dev results hint it might.  
- Request reporting results for all tasks when using only 100 sentence pairs for classifier training to strengthen the central claim.  
- Social media translation has high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); ask whether specialized preprocessing or filtering was applied.  
- Data selection does not solve OOVs—ask for detailed analysis or illustrative examples showing the nature of adaptation improvements.  
- Section 3.2: clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the model and provide an architecture diagram.  
- Section 3.2 & 5.2: specify the exact “designated loss function” used and describe how system weights were tuned for reproducibility.
