1. Proposes a semi-supervised CNN to score and select general-domain sentence pairs for SMT domain adaptation when only tiny in-domain data is available.  
2. Uses the CNN classifier to rank sentences and selects top-scoring pairs to train an adapted SMT system.  
3. Novel application of semi-supervised CNN for bilingual data selection, effective with as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
4. Strong empirical evaluation across four language pairs and three domains (SMS, tweets, Facebook) showing significant BLEU gains using only 3–10% of data.  
5. Method consistently outperforms strong baselines and three LM-based selection methods, with improvements up to +3.1 BLEU.  
6. Well-motivated problem and clear reasoning for CNN use to capture position-invariant domain-specific n-grams/phrases.  
7. Clear methodological description and intuition that pre-trained embeddings mitigate limited labeled in-domain data.  
8. Unclear details on hyperparameter tuning and the separate validation set, hindering reproducibility and sensitivity assessment.  
9. Main results table compares methods using different numbers of selected sentences, making direct comparisons unfair.  
10. Missing comparison to a key bilingual baseline (cross-entropy difference sum methods like Moore & Lewis / Axelrod et al.).  
11. Lacks qualitative examples illustrating sentences selected by the CNN but missed by LM-based methods to support claimed advantages.  
12. Question: clarify what data constituted the separate validation set and how it was kept independent from dev/test.  
13. Question: did the authors try a joint bilingual CNN that processes source and target together to learn optimal score combination?  
14. Question: how does the CNN advantage change as in-domain seed size increases (e.g., 10k or 50k)—does the gap to LM methods persist or shrink?
