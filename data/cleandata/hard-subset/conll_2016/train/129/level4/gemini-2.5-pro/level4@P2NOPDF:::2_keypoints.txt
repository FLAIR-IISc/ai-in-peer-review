- Proposes a CNN-based in-domain data selection method for SMT that outperforms LM-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and is robust with very limited in-domain data.  
- Reported improvement "up to 3.1 BLEU" is unclear because an in-domain-only baseline already yields +2.8 BLEU, so the selection method's net gain is ~0.3–0.5 BLEU.  
- Lacks comparison to standard domain-adaptation techniques (e.g., interpolated language/translation models) to better situate the contribution.  
- Most compelling result: the CNN classifier remains effective with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs.  
- The 100-sentence experiment was only on Chinese→English, which may be easier because the general-domain set already contains some in-domain data.  
- Recommend replicating the 100-sentence selector experiment across all language pairs, especially English→Spanish and English→Chinese (purely out-of-domain).  
- Targets the social media domain with high OOV/slang/hashtag rates but does not state any special preprocessing or filtering applied.  
- Data selection alone cannot fix OOVs; include qualitative analysis and example translations to show what error types the selected data corrects.  
- Model architecture description is unclear about how embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined—an architectural diagram would help.  
- The paper mentions a "designated loss function" but does not specify which loss (e.g., cross-entropy); please clarify.  
- SMT tuning method (e.g., MERT, PRO) is not specified—report this for completeness and reproducibility.
