- Proposes a CNN classifier for in-domain data selection in SMT that outperforms LM-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and is robust with very small in-domain training data.
- Reported gains up to 3.1 BLEU are misleading because the baseline lacked any in-domain training; an in-domain-only system already gives +2.8 BLEU, so the benefit may mainly come from including in-domain data rather than the selection method.
- The paper should compare the proposed method to a stronger, commonly used baseline—interpolating separate in-domain and out-of-domain models.
- The strongest claim is that the CNN works with very little in-domain data, but this was only shown for zh→en where the general corpus contains some in-domain data, possibly simplifying the task.
- To strengthen that claim, run the small-data experiment (e.g., training the classifier on 100 sentence pairs) for en→es and en→zh as well and report results for all tasks.
- Social media text has high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); clarify whether any specialized preprocessing or filtering was applied.
- Since data selection doesn’t directly fix OOVs, provide qualitative translation examples (baseline vs adapted) to show the specific types of improvements achieved beyond BLEU.
- Section 3.2 needs clarification on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined—an architectural illustration would help.
- Specify the “designated loss function” mentioned in Section 3.2.
- In Section 5.2, describe how system component weights were tuned (e.g., MERT, PRO).
