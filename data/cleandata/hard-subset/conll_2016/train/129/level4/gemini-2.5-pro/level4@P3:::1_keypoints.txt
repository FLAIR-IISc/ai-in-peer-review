- Novel semi-supervised CNN using pre-trained word embeddings to select in-domain sentence pairs for SMT domain adaptation with very small seed data.  
- Classifier scores a large general-domain corpus and top-scoring sentence pairs are used to train adapted SMT systems, outperforming baselines across 4 language pairs and 3 social media domains.  
- Contribution: an effective data-selection method that works with as few as 100 in-domain seed sentences, leveraging pre-trained embeddings to generalize.  
- Contribution: strong empirical evaluation showing consistent BLEU improvements (up to 3.1 points) and analysis of performance vs. seed size.  
- Strength: excellent, statistically significant empirical results beating full-data and LM-based selection baselines.  
- Strength: well-motivated CNN architecture—max-pooling detects domain n-grams and embeddings generalize to semantically similar words.  
- Strength: addresses a practical problem of domain adaptation when in-domain parallel data is scarce, useful for social media and many fine-grained topics.  
- Weakness: unclear experimental protocol for choosing the number of selected sentences and potential overlap/ambiguity between validation and dev sets; data-size differences hinder fair comparisons.  
- Weakness: missing architectural ablations and visualizations to justify design choices (e.g., bag-of-words vs. sequential region inputs and how inputs are combined).  
- Weakness: limited comparison to bilingual selection methods; unclear if LM baselines used bilingual scoring comparable to the proposed source+target sum.  
- Question: which separate validation set was used to determine optimal selection sizes and were CNN hyperparameters tuned on disjoint data?  
- Question: did the ngram/rnnlm baselines use bilingual selection (summing source and target scores) as the proposed method does?  
- Question: have authors considered a truly bilingual CNN (shared final layer combining source and target) to produce a joint classification score?
