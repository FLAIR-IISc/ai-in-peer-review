- Proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT following Johnson & Zhang (2015), outperforming LM-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and remaining robust with very small in-domain training sets.

- Paper claims a 3.1 BLEU improvement, but most of this (+2.8 BLEU) is explained by the fact that the out-of-domain set already contained in-domain data; a simple baseline on that in-domain subset nearly matches the gain.

- Recommend comparing against model interpolation of separately trained in-domain and out-of-domain systems to better isolate the method’s contribution.

- Second experiment shows CNN effectiveness with very little in-domain data, but it was only run on zh-en where in-domain examples exist in training data, possibly making selection easier.

- Claim of robustness to low-resource in-domain training would be strengthened by reporting results for all tasks using very small training sizes (e.g., 100 sentence pairs).

- Social media translation has high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); the paper should clarify any special preprocessing/filtering applied.

- Data selection does not directly solve OOVs; authors should provide qualitative analysis and translation examples to show what types of improvements adaptation yields.

- Section 3.2: clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined; include an architecture illustration for clarity.

- Section 3.2: explicitly state the “designated loss function” used for classifier training.

- Section 5.2: describe how SMT system weights were tuned for completeness.
