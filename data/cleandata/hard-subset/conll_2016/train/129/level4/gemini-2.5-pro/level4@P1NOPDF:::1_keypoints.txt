- Proposes CNN-based classifier to score/rank general-domain sentences for MT training data selection.
- Comparison to n-gram and continuous-space language model methods is well-executed.
- Missing comparison to bilingual data selection techniques (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences).
- Motivation for choosing CNN over RNN/LSTM not immediately clear, though authors argue CNNs identify domain-indicative sentence parts.
- No experimental analysis determining whether BOW, SEQ, or combined representations is most effective or why.
- CNN architecture description (one-hot vectors, pre-trained embeddings) is clear but would benefit from a diagram.
- Paper is well-written overall but has minor citation/formatting issues (e.g., \citet vs \citep).
- Experiments broadly support claims, but methodology concerns remain about validation data and hyperparameter tuning.
- No sensitivity analysis for model hyperparameters and selection of number of sentences to include.
- Table 2 should report results using the same number of selected sentences for all methods for a fairer comparison.
- Suggest evaluating baselines with a larger in-domain training set rather than only development data.
- Recommend showing example sentences selected by the proposed method versus baselines to support abstraction claims.
- Propose adding a baseline like Axelrod (2015) that reduces sparsity via POS tagging to test if gains stem from word2vec beyond simple abstraction.
- Note analogy between summing source/target scores and Moore & Lewis (2010); recommend citing that work.
- Suggest future work to extend the CNN to a bilingual setting to learn optimal weights for combining source and target scores.
