- Proposes semi-supervised CNN-based data selection for SMT domain adaptation by training a classifier on small in-domain (positive) and general-domain (negative) sets to score and select relevant sentences.
- Claims effectiveness with extremely limited in-domain data (as few as 100 sentences) and robustness compared to LM-based methods.
- Provides strong empirical evaluation across four language pairs and three social media domains, outperforming n-gram and RNN LM baselines.

- Robust performance in very low-resource settings: sscnn maintains gains where LM methods degrade sharply, e.g., +2.1 BLEU from 100 sentences.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results, consistently and significantly outperforming strong data selection baselines across tested language pairs.
- Thorough experiments across multiple language directions and noisy social media domains demonstrate broad applicability.

- The reported +3.1 BLEU for zh→en may be misleading because a large in-domain subset already exists in the general training data, so the method may be mainly locating that data.
- The key low-resource claim is validated mainly on the zh→en task (which contains in-domain data), and should be replicated on other tasks without such in-domain subsets.
- Model architecture and training details are insufficiently described (e.g., how one-hot and embedding representations are combined, loss function, SMT tuning method); an architectural diagram is needed.
- Paper lacks analysis of social-media-specific translation challenges (OOVs, nonstandard spellings, usernames, hashtags) and qualitative examples to show what types of improvements occur.

- Clarify how wl(x) (one-hot) and ul(x) (embeddings) are combined for convolution and specify the CNN loss function.
- Provide Figure 1-style low-resource results for the other language pairs to validate generalization of robustness claims.
- Describe any text normalization/preprocessing applied to social media data and provide translation examples illustrating the method's improvements.
