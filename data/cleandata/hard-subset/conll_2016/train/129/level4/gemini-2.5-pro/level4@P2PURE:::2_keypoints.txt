- Presents a CNN-based in-domain data selection method for SMT that outperforms an LM-based baseline by ~0.5 BLEU and is robust with very little in-domain training data.  
- The reported 3.1 BLEU improvement is misleading because large gains occur when in-domain data is already in the general-domain training set; a system trained only on in-domain data already yields +2.8 BLEU.  
- A more informative baseline would be interpolation of separately trained in-domain and out-of-domain models rather than the presented comparison.  
- The compelling result that the CNN works with very limited in-domain data was only shown for Chinese→English (which contains in-domain data), so robustness across other tasks without in-domain overlap is unclear.  
- Recommend reporting results for all language pairs when the classifier is trained on a similarly small set (e.g., 100 sentence pairs) to substantiate the robustness claim.  
- Social-media translation poses high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); the paper should clarify any special preprocessing or filtering applied.  
- Since selection does not solve OOVs, include qualitative translation examples to illustrate specific improvements (lexical choice, phrasing) from the adaptation method.  
- Section 3.2 needs clearer description of how embeddings/segment/one-hot vectors are combined, a model architecture diagram, and specification of the “designated loss function.”  
- Section 5.2 should describe the method used for tuning system weights.
