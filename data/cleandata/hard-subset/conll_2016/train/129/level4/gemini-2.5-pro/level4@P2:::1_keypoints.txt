- Proposed method: semi-supervised CNN to score/rank general-domain sentence pairs for SMT domain adaptation, targeting very low-resource in-domain seed data.  
- Contribution: highly effective data selection for low-resource adaptation, yielding significant BLEU improvements over LM baselines with only a few hundred in-domain sentences.  
- Contribution: strong empirical validation across four language pairs and three social media domains, with robustness analysis as in-domain seed size decreases.  
- Strength: novel and well-motivated application of semi-supervised CNNs that achieves substantial, statistically significant gains (e.g., +2.1 BLEU with 100 zh-en sentences).  
- Strength: convincing rationale for CNN architecture—convolution + max-pooling captures salient domain-specific n-grams irrespective of position, explaining advantages over sequential models.  
- Strength: well-motivated semi-supervised use of pre-trained embeddings to generalize from tiny seed sets and recognize semantically similar words.  
- Weakness: unclear experimental methodology—unspecified validation set used to choose the number of selected sentences and to tune CNN hyperparameters, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: inconsistent comparisons in main results table—different methods use different amounts of selected data, preventing direct apples-to-apples evaluation (fixed-size comparison should be standard).  
- Weakness: absence of qualitative analysis—no example sentences showing what the CNN selects versus LM baselines to substantiate claims about model behavior.  
- Question: which dataset was used as the validation set for tuning CNN hyperparameters and selecting the optimal number of sentences (held-out dev or separate dataset)?  
- Question: did you compare against bilingual LM-based selection methods that sum cross-entropy differences, analogous to summing source and target scores?  
- Question: how does your embedding-based approach compare to sparsity-mitigation methods like Axelrod et al. (2015) that replace words with POS tags and do not rely on pre-trained embeddings?  
- Question: have you considered learning combination weights for source and target scores or extending the CNN to jointly model bilingual sentence pairs instead of summing scores?
