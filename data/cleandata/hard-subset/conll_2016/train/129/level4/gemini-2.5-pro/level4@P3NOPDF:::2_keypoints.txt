- Proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection (following Johnson & Zhang) and outperforms an LM-based baseline by ~0.5 BLEU.  
- Key strength: method remains effective with very small in-domain training sets.  
- Small-data robustness experiment was only run on zh-en, which may be easier due to in-domain data present in the general training set.  
- Recommend replicating the small-training-data experiment (e.g., 100 sentences) across en-es and en-zh to validate robustness.  
- The reported up-to-3.1 BLEU gain is misleading because an in-domain-only baseline already gives +2.8 BLEU; selection’s incremental gain is ~0.3–0.5 BLEU.  
- Suggest comparing the method to other adaptation approaches (e.g., interpolating separately trained in-domain and out-of-domain models).  
- Social media domain has high OOV rates (slang, usernames, hashtags); paper doesn’t state whether special preprocessing/filtering was applied.  
- Data selection alone cannot address OOVs—provide qualitative translation examples showing what types of errors are corrected (lexical choice, phrasing, style).  
- Methodology clarity needed: explain precisely how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the CNN (a diagram would help).  
- Specify the “designated loss function” used in training and state the SMT tuning method (MERT, MIRA, PRO) for reproducibility.
