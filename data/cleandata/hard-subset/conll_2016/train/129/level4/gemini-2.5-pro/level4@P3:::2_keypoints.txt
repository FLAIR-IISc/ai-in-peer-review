- Proposes semi-supervised CNN to score and select general-domain sentences for SMT domain adaptation using few in-domain positives and pre-trained embeddings.  
- Trains CNN on small in-domain set vs. random general-domain negatives, then selects top-scoring sentence pairs to adapt SMT.  

- Contribution: Shows CNN-based data selection remains effective with very scarce in-domain seed data (as few as 100 sentences).  
- Contribution: Adapts a semi-supervised CNN text-classification framework successfully to SMT data selection across multiple language pairs and social-media domains.  

- Strength: Addresses practical low-resource domain adaptation, useful for fine-grained or rapidly changing domains.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains over three LM-based baselines across four language pairs (0.3–1.0 BLEU improvements, statistically significant).  
- Strength: Demonstrates robustness as seed data decreases, with notable gains even at 100 seed sentences (e.g., +2.1 BLEU vs. alldata).  

- Weakness: “Up to 3.1 BLEU” headline for zh-en is misleading without contextualizing that a 120K in-domain subset in training accounts for much of the gain.  
- Weakness: Key claim about tiny-data robustness is supported mainly by the zh-en task; missing replication of the 100-sentence experiment on the other tasks.  
- Weakness: Model description lacks clarity (combination of wl(x) and ul(x), no architectural diagram, unspecified loss function).  

- Question: Can the authors report results with only 100 in-domain sentences for the other three tasks (ar-en, en-es, en-zh)?  
- Question: What text normalization/preprocessing was applied for noisy social-media data, and can the authors show qualitative translation examples?  
- Question: How were SMT weights tuned for final evaluation—was the same dev set/MERT tuning used across all conditions and baselines?
