

The paper presents a convolutional neural network classifier for in-domain data selection in SMT, extending the framework of Johnson and Zhang (2015). It achieves approximately 0.5 BLEU point improvement over language model-based data selection and demonstrates robustness when only minimal in-domain data is available. However, the claimed 3.1 BLEU point improvement appears overstated, as training exclusively on in-domain data yields +2.8 BLEU, suggesting the incremental gain from data selection is smaller. A comparison with a model interpolation approach (separate in- and out-domain models) would provide valuable context.  

The most compelling result concerns the CNN's effectiveness with scarce in-domain data, though the second experiment is limited to the zh2en task—which inherently includes in-domain data in training, potentially simplifying the selection task. It is unclear whether this holds for other tasks lacking in-domain data in training (e.g., en2es and en2zh, where development sets contain only hundreds of sentence pairs). The claim would be strengthened by reporting results across all tasks with 100 training sentence pairs.  

For social media translation, which features high OOV rates (due to unconventional orthography in Latin scripts) and special elements (usernames, hashtags, emoticons), the review questions whether specific preprocessing or filtering was applied. Since data selection cannot mitigate OOV issues, further details on the *nature* of improvements (e.g., through concrete examples) would clarify its impact.
