- Proposes a CNN classifier for in-domain SMT data selection (based on Johnson & Zhang 2015), achieving ~0.5 BLEU over LM-based selection and robust with very limited in-domain data.  
- Claims a 3.1 BLEU improvement, but that gain appears only when in-domain data is already in the training set; training exclusively on in-domain yields +2.8 BLEU.  
- Should compare against interpolating separate in-domain and out-of-domain models as a stronger baseline.  
- Most compelling result: CNN works well with minimal in-domain data (second experiment), but this experiment is limited to zh→en where in-domain data is present in training, possibly simplifying selection.  
- Unclear if minimal-data robustness generalizes to tasks without in-domain training data; en→es and en→zh dev sets hint this needs investigation.  
- Recommend reporting results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain training sentence pairs to better support the claim.  
- Social-media translation has high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); ask whether any specialized preprocessing or filtering was applied.  
- Data selection cannot resolve OOVs—request concrete examples showing what specific improvements adaptation achieves.  
- Section 3.2: Clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined; include an architectural diagram.  
- Section 3.2: Specify the "designated loss function."  
- Section 5.2: For completeness, state how system weights were tuned.
