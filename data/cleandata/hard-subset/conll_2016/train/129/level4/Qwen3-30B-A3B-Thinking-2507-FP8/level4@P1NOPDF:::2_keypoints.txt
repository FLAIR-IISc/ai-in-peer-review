- Paper proposes a CNN for in-domain data selection (extending Johnson & Zhang 2015) and reports +0.5 BLEU over LM-based selection and claimed robustness with minimal in-domain data.  
- The reported +3.1 BLEU improvement is likely overstated since training only on in-domain data yields +2.8 BLEU, suggesting gains come from in-domain data rather than the selection method.  
- Recommend comparing against interpolated separate in-domain and out-of-domain models to isolate the selection method’s true contribution.  
- The strong result with only 100 in-domain sentences is shown only for zh→en, where in-domain data is already in the training set, likely simplifying selection.  
- Request reporting the 100-sentence experiment for en→es and en→zh (dev sets with few hundred pairs) to confirm generality of the “minimal data” claim.  
- Paper omits preprocessing/filtering details for social media data, despite high OOV rates; data selection cannot address OOVs alone.  
- Ask for concrete examples showing what the method improves (e.g., handling domain-specific terms vs. overall fluency).  
- Section 3.2: the combination of word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors is unclear—include a diagram and specify the “designated loss function” and its rationale.  
- Section 5.2: briefly describe how system weights were tuned for completeness.  
- Overall: CNN approach is promising but evaluation needs stronger evidence for the minimal-data claim and clearer demonstration of the practical impact of selected data.
