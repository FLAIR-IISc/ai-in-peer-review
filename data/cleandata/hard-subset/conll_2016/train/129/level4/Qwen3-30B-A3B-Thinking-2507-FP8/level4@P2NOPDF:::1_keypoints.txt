- Proposes a CNN-based method for scoring/ranking general-domain sentences for MT data selection; comparison to continuous/n-gram LMs is thorough but unclear if bilingual selection methods (e.g., Lewis & Moore) were compared.  
- Motivation for using CNNs over RNNs/LSTMs is initially unclear but the paper argues CNNs better capture localized, salient sentence features.  
- No experimental evaluation or justification for choosing Bag-of-Words (BOW), Sequence (SEQ), or combined representations.  
- Textual description of the CNN (one-hot inputs, semi-supervised embeddings) is clear and precise; a diagram of the layer architecture and input combination is recommended.  
- Paper is well-written overall, but citation formatting (e.g., `\citet` vs `\citep`) should be standardized.  
- Experimental concerns: validation data source and CNN hyperparameter tuning are unspecified; sensitivity analysis of these choices is needed.  
- Table 2 should compare all methods using identical numbers of selected sentences, as suggested by Figure 1.  
- Recommend evaluating baselines when more in-domain data is available (beyond the development set).  
- Suggest including example sentences selected by each method to illustrate selection quality concretely.  
- Recommend comparing to Axelrod et al. (2015) (POS-tag substitution) to assess whether word2vec embeddings yield additional gains over POS-based sparsity mitigation.  
- Recommend citing Lewis & Moore (2011) when discussing the sum-of-scores approach, as it parallels their sum-of-cross-entropy-differences method.  
- Suggest exploring a bilingual extension where the CNN jointly learns weights for source and target classification scores.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and technically robust contribution, but addressing validation methodology, representation comparison, and additional baselines would significantly strengthen the paper.
