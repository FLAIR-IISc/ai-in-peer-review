- Summary: Semi-supervised CNN-based data selection for SMT domain adaptation using extremely limited in-domain data (as few as 100 sentences), outperforming LM methods and scalable to many topics.  
- Strength: Tackles a critical, underexplored real-world problem with meaningful gains (e.g., 2.1 BLEU with 100 sentences).  
- Strength: Robust empirical validation across four language pairs and three domains; advantage increases as in-domain data shrinks.  
- Strength: Practically scalable — needs only a small dev set enabling rapid adaptation for hundreds of topics.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to bilingual data selection methods (e.g., Axelrod et al., 2015), weakening novelty claims.  
- Weakness: Validation methodology unclear — unclear held-out/dev procedure, sentence-selection criteria, and hyperparameter tuning/sensitivity.  
- Weakness: Unjustified choice of representations (BOW vs SEQ); missing ablation to identify which representation drives results.  
- Question: How does your method compare empirically to Axelrod et al. (2015) POS-tagged bilingual selection?  
- Question: Please clarify the validation set used for selecting sentence counts and provide sensitivity analysis for CNN hyperparameters (depth, embedding size).  
- Comment: The claim that CNNs capture domain-relevant phrases better than LMs needs concrete evidence (e.g., example sentences selected by each method).  
- Comment: The abstract’s "3.1 BLEU improvement" is ambiguous — clarify whether it’s absolute or relative to baseline.  
- Comment: Citation style is inconsistent; standardize to conference guidelines.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on adding key bilingual comparisons and clarifying methodology and representation ablations.
