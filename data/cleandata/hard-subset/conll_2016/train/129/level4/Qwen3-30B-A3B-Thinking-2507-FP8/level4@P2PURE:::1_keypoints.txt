- Proposes MT training data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN classifier.  
- Comparison to prior continuous/n-gram LM baselines is strong, but unclear if bilingual data-selection methods (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences) were compared.  
- Motivation for CNN over RNN/LSTM is improved by the claim that certain local segments matter more, but no experimental comparison to BOW vs sequential representations (or their combination).  
- CNN architecture and use of one-hot vs. pre-trained embeddings are clearly described, but a diagram of layer structure and input combination would help.  
- Paper is well-written overall, though some citation commands misuse \citet vs. \citep.  
- Experiments support claims, but the method for choosing the number of selected in-domain sentences (via a validation set) is insufficiently described.  
- Unclear which validation data and which CNN hyperparameters are tuned on the validation set, and how sensitive results are to those choices.  
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same fixed number of selected sentences (Figure 1 suggests the proposed method still wins under that condition).  
- Suggest an experiment comparing the method against baselines with larger amounts of in-domain data (beyond the dev set).  
- Recommend including example sentences selected by different methods to strengthen qualitative claims (Section 5.4).  
- Propose adding Axelrod (2015)-style POS-tag-based baseline to test whether word2vec embeddings improve over abstracting surface forms.  
- Note conceptual similarity to source+target LM selection (sum of cross-entropy differences / Lewis-Moore); include that reference and consider extending the CNN to learn bilingual/parallel weighting of source and target scores.
