

The paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) classifier for in-domain data selection in SMT, adopting the framework introduced by Johnson and Zhang (2015). It achieves approximately 0.5 BLEU points higher performance than language model-based data selection and remains robust even when only extremely limited in-domain data is available. 

The paper claims a 3.1 BLEU point improvement. However, the results indicate that such gains are observed only when in-domain data is already present in the training set—training exclusively on in-domain data yields +2.8 BLEU. It would be valuable to compare this approach against a system interpolating separate in- and out-domain models. 

The most compelling result lies in the second experiment, demonstrating the CNN classifier's effectiveness with minimal in-domain data. However, this experiment is limited to the zh2en task, which inherently includes in-domain data in the training set, potentially simplifying the selection task. It remains unclear whether this holds for other tasks lacking in-domain data in the training set. The en2es and en2zh results already suggest this direction, as their development sets contain only a few hundred sentence pairs. The claim would be better substantiated by reporting results for all tasks using just 100 training sentence pairs.  

Social media translation often involves distinct challenges compared to standard domains, notably a high OOV rate due to non-conventional spelling (particularly for Latin scripts) and special character sequences like usernames, hashtags, or emoticons. Were any specialized preprocessing or filtering steps applied to the data?  

Since data selection cannot resolve OOV issues, it would be insightful to detail the specific improvements achieved through adaptation, perhaps via concrete examples.
