- Proposes a CNN classifier (extending Johnson & Zhang 2015) for in-domain data selection and reports ~0.5 BLEU gain over language-model-based selection and robustness with minimal in-domain data.  
- The claimed +3.1 BLEU improvement is likely overstated: training only on in-domain data yields +2.8 BLEU, so the incremental benefit from data selection is small.  
- A comparison to model interpolation (separate in- and out-domain model combination) is missing and would give useful context.  
- The CNN’s strength with scarce in-domain data is promising, but the second experiment is limited to zh→en (which includes in-domain data in training), so results may not generalize to tasks without in-domain training data.  
- Recommend reporting results for all tasks with only 100 in-domain training sentence pairs to validate the scarce-data claim.  
- For social-media translation, high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons) raise questions about preprocessing/filtering; data selection cannot address OOVs and example-driven analysis of improvements is needed.  
- Section 3.2 needs clarification: how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are integrated (an architectural diagram would help) and specify the “designated loss function.”  
- Section 5.2 should report how system weights were tuned for completeness.
