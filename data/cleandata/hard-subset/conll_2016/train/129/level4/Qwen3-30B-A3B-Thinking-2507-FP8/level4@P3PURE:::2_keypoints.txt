- Paper proposes a CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT, reporting ~0.5 BLEU improvement over LM-based selection and robustness with very small in-domain data.  
- The claimed 3.1 BLEU improvement is overstated because similar gains occur only when in-domain data are included in training; training solely on in-domain data already yields +2.8 BLEU.  
- Authors should compare against interpolation of separate in-domain and out-of-domain models to contextualize reported gains.  
- The strongest result is the CNN’s effectiveness with minimal in-domain data, but this experiment is limited to zh→en and uses in-domain data in training, possibly biasing selection.  
- It is unclear whether the minimal-data result generalizes to other language pairs/tasks lacking in-domain data in training; en→es and en→zh dev-set sizes hint it might, but more evidence is needed.  
- To strengthen claims, report results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain training sentence pairs.  
- Social media translation has high OOV rates and special character patterns; the paper must clarify if any specialized preprocessing or filtering was applied.  
- Since data selection cannot fix OOVs, the authors should provide concrete example outputs illustrating the specific types of improvements achieved by adaptation.  
- Section 3.2: the combination mechanism for word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors needs a clearer explanation, preferably with an architectural diagram.  
- Section 3.2: explicitly name and justify the “designated loss function.”  
- Section 5.2: briefly describe the method used for tuning system weights for completeness.
