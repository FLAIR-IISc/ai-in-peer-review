- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN for domain adaptation in SMT that ranks and selects general-domain sentences using as few as 100 labeled in-domain sentences and pretrained embeddings.  
- Trains a CNN classifier to distinguish in-domain vs out-of-domain sentences and uses it to select data to adapt translation systems.  
- Contributions: a novel data selection method enabling adaptation with extremely limited in-domain data.  
- Contributions: a semi-supervised CNN framework leveraging large unlabeled corpora to overcome small in-domain data.  
- Contributions: empirical validation showing significant BLEU improvements over strong LM-based baselines, especially in low-data regimes.  
- Strength: novel application showing CNNs capture local domain-relevant features better than LMs for scarce in-domain data.  
- Strength: strong, stable empirical gains across four language directions and three test domains, including up to +3.1 BLEU and consistent wins with only ~3% training data.  
- Strength: clear technical description of architectures and rationale for using general-domain embeddings.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparison to relevant bilingual data-selection baselines (e.g., Axelrod et al.), weakening the novelty claim.  
- Weakness: no ablation study isolating the impact of input representations (BOW vs SEQ) to justify design choices.  
- Weakness: unclear validation procedure for selecting number of sentences and hyperparameter tuning; potential bias if validation not independent.  
- Questions for authors: compare to Axelrod et al. (2015) and POS-based methods; provide example sentences selected by sscnn vs LM baselines; and discuss joint bilingual CNN models vs summed source/target scores.
