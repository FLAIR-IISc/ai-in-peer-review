- Main claim of a 3.1 BLEU improvement is overstated: the gain occurs only when in-domain data is present in training and training on in-domain data alone already yields +2.8 BLEU.  
- Missing baseline: authors must compare against interpolated separate in-domain and out-domain models to isolate the benefit of data selection.  
- Minimal-data (100-sentence) experiment is under-validated: tested only on zh-en where in-domain data exists in training, so generalizability is unclear.  
- Lack of cross-task validation: need results for en-es and en-zh (and all tasks) with 100 in-domain sentences to support robustness claims.  
- OOVs in social media are not addressed: no preprocessing/filtering for non-standard spellings, usernames, hashtags, emoticons, yet these strongly affect SMT and aren’t resolved by selection.  
- Empirical clarity needed: provide concrete examples/statistics showing what specific improvements (e.g., OOV reduction) in practice result from in-domain selection.  
- Section 3.2 is insufficiently detailed: clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined, include a schematic, and explicitly name the loss function.  
- Section 5.2 lacks hyperparameter-tuning details: specify how system weights were optimized (development set vs. cross-validation) and the tuning procedure.  
- Overall assessment: CNN-based selection is promising for low-resource adaptation but current evidence and missing comparisons limit the claimed novelty and generalizability; addressing the above would substantially strengthen the paper.
