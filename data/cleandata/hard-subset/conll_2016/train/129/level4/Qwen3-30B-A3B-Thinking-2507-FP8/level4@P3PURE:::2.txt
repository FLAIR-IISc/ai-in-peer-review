

The paper presents a convolutional neural network (CNN) approach for in-domain data selection in SMT, extending the framework of Johnson and Zhang (2015). It reports an approximate 0.5 BLEU point improvement over language model-based data selection and highlights robustness when only a very small in-domain dataset is available.  

However, the claimed 3.1 BLEU point improvement appears overstated. As shown in the results, this magnitude of gain is only achieved when in-domain data is present in the training set—training exclusively on in-domain data already yields +2.8 BLEU. The authors should compare their method against a system that interpolates separate in-domain and out-domain models to better contextualize their results.  

The most compelling finding is the second experiment, demonstrating the CNN's effectiveness with minimal in-domain data. Yet this experiment is limited to the zh2en task, which includes actual in-domain data in the training set—potentially facilitating selection. It remains unclear whether this result generalizes to other tasks without in-domain data in the training set. The en2es and en2zh tasks, where development sets contain only a few hundred sentence pairs, already suggest this might be the case. To strengthen the claim, results for all tasks using only 100 training sentence pairs should be reported.  

Social media translation introduces distinct challenges, notably high OOV rates due to non-conventional spelling (particularly for Latin scripts) and special character sequences (e.g., usernames, hashtags, emoticons). The review requires clarification on whether any specialized preprocessing or filtering was applied to the data.  

Since data selection cannot mitigate OOV issues, the authors should provide concrete examples to detail the specific types of improvements achieved through adaptation.  

Specific section comments:  
**Section 3.2:**  
- The combination mechanism for word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors requires clearer explanation. An architectural diagram would be highly beneficial.  
- The "designated loss function" should be explicitly named and justified.  

**Section 5.2:**  
For completeness, the method for tuning system weights should be briefly described.
