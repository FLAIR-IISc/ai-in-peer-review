- Novel semi-supervised CNN framework for domain classification that leverages limited in-domain data and large unlabeled general-domain data.  
- Empirical claim that the method outperforms language model–based selection with as few as 100 in-domain sentences, achieving up to +2.1 BLEU.  
- Demonstrates feasibility of fine-grained topic-dependent adaptation previously unattainable due to data constraints.  
- Robust performance with minimal in-domain data: substantial gains (up to +2.1 BLEU) with only 100 sentences and low variance (std 0.12).  
- Superior performance on challenging domains: outperforms all baselines (including RNNLM) across four language directions, e.g., +3.1 BLEU on zh→en SMS.  
- Practical applicability: needs no domain-specific preprocessing or resources and can select useful data from very large general-domain corpora.  
- Overstated BLEU improvements: the reported +3.1 BLEU relies on a training set with a large in-domain subset (120K), making the absolute gain less clear.  
- Limited evaluation of the "tiny data" scenario: 100-sentence experiment shown only for zh→en, not for ar→en, en→es, en→zh, limiting generalizability.  
- Insufficient discussion of social-media challenges: no details on preprocessing or OOV handling, so improvements might reflect noise handling rather than true domain adaptation.  
- Question: How much of the 0.3 BLEU gain (sscnn 26.0 vs indata 25.7) on zh→en SMS is due to the CNN selecting better sentences than the in-domain subset?  
- Question: For the 100 in-domain sentence condition, what are the results for ar→en, en→es, and en→zh?  
- Question: How does performance change when the in-domain reference is reduced to 100 sentences sampled from the dev set rather than using the full dev set?  
- Question: How were OOVs handled during translation, and how do you separate effects of OOV handling from domain-adaptation gains?
