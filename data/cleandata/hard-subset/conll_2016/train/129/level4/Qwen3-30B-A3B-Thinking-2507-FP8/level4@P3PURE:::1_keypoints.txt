- Proposes MT training data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN classifier.  
- Thorough comparison to prior continuous and n-gram LM methods, but unclear if bilingual data selection baselines (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences) were compared.  
- CNN motivation (capturing important sentence segments vs RNN/LSTM) becomes a strength in the paper’s argument.  
- No experiments or analysis to determine whether BOW, SEQ, or combined representations are most important, nor rationale for the choice.  
- Textual description of the CNN (one-hot or pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed; a diagram of layer structure and input combination would improve clarity.  
- Paper is well written overall, but citation formatting (unnecessary parentheses, \citet vs \citep) should be fixed.  
- Experimental method for selecting the number of in-domain sentences (using a separate validation set) is concerning and under-specified.  
- Unclear what validation data was used; CNN hyperparameter selection and sensitivity to hyperparameters are not examined.  
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 indicates the proposed method still outperforms under that condition.  
- Recommend an experiment comparing the method against baselines with more in-domain data (beyond the development set).  
- Suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to substantiate claims in Section 5.4.  
- For abstraction-from-surface-forms claims, compare to Axelrod (2015) which uses POS replacement to reduce LM sparsity to evaluate added value of word2vec embeddings.  
- Note that summing source and target classification scores parallels the Lewis-Moore LM data selection method (sum of cross-entropy differences); add that reference.  
- Propose extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setup to learn weights for combining source and target scores.
