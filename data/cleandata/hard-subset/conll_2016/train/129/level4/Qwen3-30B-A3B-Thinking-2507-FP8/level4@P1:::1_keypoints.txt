- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN-based data selection method for SMT adaptation that works well with very small in-domain datasets.  
- Demonstrates the CNN method significantly outperforms language model–based selection, yielding up to 3.1 BLEU improvement using only 3% of training data and as few as 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Addresses an important practical problem (domain adaptation with scarce labeled data), showing consistent gains across four language directions and social media domains.  
- Justifies the use of CNNs over RNNs/LSTMs by arguing CNNs capture position-independent local features and leverages pre-trained word embeddings to mitigate limited in-domain data.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including multiple domains, language pairs, and an ablation study highlighting performance with only 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Lacks comparison to bilingual sentence-pair selection methods (e.g., sum/difference of cross-entropies), weakening claims of superiority over all relevant baselines.  
- Insufficient detail on how the optimal number of selected in-domain sentences was determined: unclear validation data, hyperparameter selection, and sensitivity to these choices.  
- No experimental comparison between BOW and SEQ representations (or their combination) to show which representation matters more and why.  
- Requests clarification on what validation data was used and sensitivity of results to that choice.  
- Asks for illustrative examples of sentences selected by the proposed method versus baselines to demonstrate selection quality.  
- Queries how performance scales with more abundant in-domain data (e.g., 1000+ sentences) and whether the CNN advantage diminishes.  
- Requests a reference and clarification regarding the relation to Lewis-Moore LM data selection and whether the sum of source/target scores is equivalent.  
- Suggests exploring learning weights for source and target scores in the CNN rather than simply summing them to potentially improve performance.
