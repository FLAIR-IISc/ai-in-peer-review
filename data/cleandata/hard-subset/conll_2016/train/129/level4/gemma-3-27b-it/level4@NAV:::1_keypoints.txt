- Introduces a CNN-based method for MT training data selection, scoring and ranking sentences from general-domain corpora.
- Thorough comparison to continuous and n-gram LM-based selection methods, but omits discussion of bilingual selection approaches like sum of cross-entropy differences.
- Initial choice of CNN over RNN/LSTM was unclear, though paper later argues CNNs better identify important sentence segments.
- Lacks experimental evidence to determine whether bag-of-words, sequential, or combined representations drive performance and why.
- CNN architecture and use of one-hot vs. pre-trained embeddings are clearly described, but a diagram of layer interactions and inputs would aid understanding.
- Paper contains inconsistent citation formatting (\citet vs. \citep) in places (e.g., line 385).
- Experimental results support claims but selection procedure for number of in-domain sentences (line 443) relies on an undescribed validation set.
- Composition/source of the validation data and the CNN hyperparameter selection process are not detailed; no sensitivity analysis is provided.
- Table 2 should report scores normalized to the same number of selected sentences for direct comparison, though Figure 1 indicates the proposed method still outperforms baselines.
- Recommend experiments with larger volumes of in-domain data beyond the development set to assess scalability.
- Suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to illustrate differences and support Section 5.4 claims.
- Propose comparing abstraction-from-surface-forms approach to Axelrod (2015)’s POS-tagging method to evaluate word2vec benefits.
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Moore-Lewis sum-of-cross-entropy-differences method; add a reference and discussion.
- Encourage extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to learn weights for combined source and target classification scores.
