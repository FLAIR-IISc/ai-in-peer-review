- Proposes MT data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN classifier.
- Comparison to prior continuous and n-gram language model methods is strong, but unclear if bilingual data selection methods (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences) were compared.
- Initial motivation for choosing CNN over RNN/LSTM is unclear, though the paper argues CNNs capture that some sentence parts matter more.
- Paper does not experimentally compare bag-of-words (BOW) vs sequential (SEQ) representations or combinations to show which matters more.
- Textual description of the CNN architecture (one-hot vs pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed.
- Adding a diagram of how inputs are combined across CNN layers would improve clarity.
- Writing quality is generally good, but some parenthetical citation styles are unnecessary.
- Experiments support claims, but the method for selecting the number of in-domain sentences via a separate validation set raises concerns.
- It’s unclear what validation data was used, how CNN hyperparameters were chosen, and how sensitive results are to those choices.
- Table 2 should compare approaches using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 suggests the proposed method still outperforms under that adjustment.
- Suggest running experiments with more in-domain data (beyond the dev set) to test robustness.
- Recommend including example sentences selected by different methods to substantiate claims in section 5.4.
- Propose comparing to Axelrod (2015) (POS-based abstraction) to evaluate whether word2vec embeddings offer additional advantage.
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore sum-of-difference cross-entropy LM selection; recommend citing that work.
- Suggest extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to learn weights for combining source and target classification scores.
