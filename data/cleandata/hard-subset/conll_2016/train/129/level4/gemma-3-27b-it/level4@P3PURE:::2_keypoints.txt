- Proposes a CNN classifier for in-domain data selection for SMT (following Johnson & Zhang 2015), outperforming LM-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and showing robustness with limited in-domain data.  
- Claims up to +3.1 BLEU, but large gains occur only when in-domain data is already in the initial training set; training solely on in-domain yields +2.8 BLEU.  
- Recommend comparing against interpolation of separate in-domain and out-of-domain models.  
- Second experiment shows CNN remains effective with very small in-domain sets, but it’s limited to zh→en where in-domain data is present in training, so generalization to other tasks is unclear.  
- en→es and en→zh results (small dev sets of a few hundred pairs) suggest the reported small-data robustness may not generalize; recommend reporting results when training is limited to 100 sentence pairs for all tasks.  
- For social-media translation, high OOVs and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons) are a concern—ask whether specific preprocessing/filtering was applied and request illustrative translated examples to show what adaptation improves.  
- Section 3.2 needs clarification on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined (an architectural diagram would help) and must specify the “designated loss function.”  
- Section 5.2 should state how system weights were tuned.
