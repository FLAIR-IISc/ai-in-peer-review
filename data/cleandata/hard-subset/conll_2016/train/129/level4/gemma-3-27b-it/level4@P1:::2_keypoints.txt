- Proposes a CNN classifier for in-domain data selection (builds on Johnson & Zhang 2015) that outperforms language-model-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and stays robust with very limited in-domain data.  
- The claimed improvements up to 3.1 BLEU are largely driven by cases where in-domain data is already in the training set; training solely on that in-domain data yields +2.8 BLEU.  
- Recommend comparing to an interpolated in-domain/out-of-domain model to contextualize the large gains.  
- The strongest claim is CNN effectiveness with minimal in-domain data, but the experiment is limited to zh→en where in-domain data exists in training, so results may be simplified.  
- Need to test whether the minimal-data robustness generalizes to tasks without pre-existing in-domain examples (en→es and en→zh).  
- Request reporting results for all tasks when training with only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to strengthen the claim.  
- Ask for details on preprocessing/filtering applied to social-media data to mitigate high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons).  
- Note that data selection alone cannot solve OOVs and ask for illustrative examples showing specific improvements from adaptation via data selection.  
- Section 3.2: Clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the model and provide an architecture diagram.  
- Section 3.2: Specify the “designated loss function” used during training.  
- Section 5.2: Describe how system weights were tuned.
