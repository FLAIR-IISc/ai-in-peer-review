- Paper proposes an in-domain data selection method for SMT using a CNN classifier in a framework similar to Johnson and Zhang (2015).  
- Method yields ~0.5 BLEU improvement over language model–based data selection and shows robustness with limited in-domain training data.  
- Reports improvements up to 3.1 BLEU, but these gains largely depend on having in-domain data in the initial training set.  
- Training only on the in-domain data already gives a substantial +2.8 BLEU, questioning the marginal benefit of the selection method.  
- Recommend comparing against an interpolated system combining separate in- and out-of-domain models for clearer context.  
- Second experiment is the most compelling, demonstrating CNN effectiveness with minimal in-domain data.  
- That experiment is limited to zh→en and includes in-domain data in training, so generalization to tasks without initial in-domain data is unclear.  
- en→es and en→zh results hint at possible generalization given small dev sets, but claims would be stronger with uniform small-data experiments (e.g., 100 sentence pairs) across all tasks.  
- For social media translation, clarify preprocessing/filtering steps given high OOV rates, non-standard orthography, and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons).  
- Note that data selection alone cannot resolve OOV issues; provide examples or analysis of the specific types of improvements achieved.  
- Section 3.2 needs clarification on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined; include an architecture diagram.  
- Specify the “designated loss function” used in the model.  
- Section 5.2 should briefly describe the method used to tune system weights.
