- Proposes a CNN classifier for in-domain data selection in SMT, extending Johnson & Zhang (2015).  
- Method is robust: outperforms LM-based data selection by ~0.5 BLEU and retains performance with very limited in-domain data.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates CNN effectiveness for low-resource domain adaptation, achieving gains with only 100 in-domain sentences.  
- Overall improvement over strong baselines is modest, but low-data effectiveness is the key advantage.  
- The reported 3.1 BLEU gain is misleading because much of it depends on in-domain data already present in the general training set.  
- Training only on the in-domain portion yields ~2.8 BLEU, so a comparison with interpolated in-/out-of-domain models is needed.  
- The second experiment’s strength is limited to zh→en, which had in-domain data in training and may simplify selection.  
- Unclear whether the 100-sentence results generalize to en→es and en→zh; authors should report the low-data experiment for all tasks.  
- For social-media adaptation, clarify preprocessing/filtering for high OOV rates, nonstandard spelling, usernames, hashtags, and emoticons.  
- Provide analysis beyond BLEU: example selected sentences and evidence of how selection addresses OOVs and improves translation quality.  
- Section 3.2 is unclear: explain how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined, state the loss function, and include a CNN architecture diagram.  
- Section 5.2 lacks detail: fully specify the methodology used for tuning system weights.
