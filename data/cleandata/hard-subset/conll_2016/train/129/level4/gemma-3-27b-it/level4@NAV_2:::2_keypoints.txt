- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN classifier for in-domain data selection for SMT, building on Johnson and Zhang (2015).  
- Achieves ~0.5 BLEU improvement over language-model-based selection and remains robust with limited in-domain data.  
- Primary contribution: CNN-based data selection that improves translation quality, especially when in-domain resources are scarce.  
- Secondary contribution: Shows effective selection can work with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs, useful for low-resource/adaptation scenarios.  
- Empirical validation across four language directions and three domains, with gains up to 3.1 BLEU in some settings.  
- Strength: Strong, consistent empirical results across multiple language pairs and domains, including up to +3.1 BLEU.  
- Strength: Demonstrated robustness to extremely limited in-domain data (100 sentences).  
- Strength: Novel application of semi-supervised CNNs with pre-trained embeddings to mitigate data sparsity.  
- Weakness: The reported large improvements (e.g., +3.1 BLEU) may be inflated by pre-existing in-domain data in the training set rather than the selection method alone.  
- Weakness: The key 100-sentence low-data experiment is only run on zh→en (which contains in-domain data), limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Weakness: No error analysis or discussion of OOVs and social-media-specific challenges (non-standard spelling, hashtags, usernames), which data selection alone cannot address.  
- Question: Please illustrate the network architecture and how embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined.  
- Question: What loss function was used to train the CNN classifier?  
- Question: Please report the 100-sentence training results for all four language pairs (zh→en, ar→en, en→es, en→zh).  
- Question: What preprocessing or filtering was applied to the social media data (handling non-standard spelling, special characters, usernames, hashtags)?
