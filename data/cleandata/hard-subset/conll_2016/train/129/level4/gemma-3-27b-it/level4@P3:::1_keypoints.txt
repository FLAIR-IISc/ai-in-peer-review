- Core idea: Use semi-supervised CNNs to classify sentence pairs as in-domain vs out-of-domain for SMT data selection under extremely limited in-domain data.
- Main contribution: Effective application of CNN-based data selection that yields significant BLEU improvements across multiple language pairs and domains.
- Empirical strength: Consistently outperforms strong baselines, including LM-based selection, with statistically significant gains.
- Low-resource impact: Demonstrates reasonable performance with as few as 100 in-domain sentences, enabling fine-grained topic adaptation.
- Evaluation breadth: Thorough experiments across four language directions and three domains, strengthening result generality.
- Novelty: Novel use of CNNs for MT data selection, justified by their ability to capture important local sentence features.
- Missing ablation: No systematic ablation study probing architecture choices (one-hot vs embeddings, BOW vs sequence, pooling).
- Validation dependence: Selection procedure relies on a separate validation set with insufficient detail and no sensitivity analysis.
- Baseline gap: Lacks comparison to bilingual data selection methods (e.g., difference of cross-entropies).
- Limited qualitative insight: No qualitative examples of selected sentences to illustrate method behavior versus baselines.
- Hyperparameter clarity: Hyperparameter tuning procedure, search space, and computational cost are vaguely described.
- Q1: Provide details on the validation set size, composition, and creation method used to pick selection size.
- Q2: Supply a sensitivity analysis showing performance variation with different CNN hyperparameters.
- Q3: Explain why BOW was chosen and compare it to sequence embedding representations.
- Q4: Provide example sentences selected by the proposed method and by baselines to illustrate differences.
- Q5: Show how performance changes with larger amounts of in-domain data (e.g., 1k or 5k sentences) versus baselines.
