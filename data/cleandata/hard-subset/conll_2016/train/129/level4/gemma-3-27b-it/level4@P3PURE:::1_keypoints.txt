- Proposes MT data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN and compares well to continuous and n-gram LM baselines, but it's unclear if bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences) were compared.  
- Initial motivation for using a CNN over RNN/LSTM was weak, later justified by CNNs’ ability to focus on important sentence sections, but no experimental evidence distinguishes BOW vs sequential (SEQ) representations or their combination.  
- CNN architecture description (one-hot or pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed, but a diagram of layers and input combinations would improve understanding.  
- Paper is generally well written, though some parenthetical citations are unnecessary and the distinction between \citet and \citep should be addressed.  
- The method for choosing the number of selected in-domain sentences relies on a validation set, but the review asks which validation data was used and how CNN hyperparameters were chosen and how sensitive results are to them.  
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 suggests the proposed approach still outperforms baselines under this constraint.  
- Recommend an experiment testing the technique when more in-domain data is available (beyond the development set).  
- Suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to concretely support claims in section 5.4.  
- Recommend adding Axelrod (2015) as a baseline that replaces words with POS tags to address LM sparsity and to test the incremental benefit of word embeddings.  
- Note conceptual similarity between summing source and target classifier scores and the Lewis-Moore sum-of-cross-entropy-differences approach; a reference around line 435 is appropriate.  
- Proposes learning weights for the sum of source and target scores by extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting.
