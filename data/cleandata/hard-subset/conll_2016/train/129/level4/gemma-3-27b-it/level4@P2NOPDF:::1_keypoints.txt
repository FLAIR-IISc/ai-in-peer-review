- Proposes a novel CNN classifier to score and rank general-domain sentences for MT data selection and compares it thoroughly to continuous and n-gram LM methods.  
- Missing comparison to bilingual data selection techniques (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences).  
- Initially unclear why CNN was chosen over RNN/LSTM; authors later argue CNNs better identify salient sentence regions.  
- No experimental evidence comparing bag-of-words, sequential, or combined representations, nor explanation for which is best and why.  
- Textual description of CNN architecture (one-hot, semi-supervised embeddings) is clear and detailed.  
- A visual diagram of the CNN layer structure and input combination would improve clarity.  
- Some citation-style inconsistencies (\citet vs. \citep) that should be streamlined.  
- Experimental evaluation supports claims, but selection of number of in-domain sentences via a validation set needs clarification (which validation data, hyperparameter selection criteria, sensitivity analyses).  
- Table 2 should report scores for a fixed number of selected sentences to allow direct comparison (Figure 1 already suggests superiority under fixed conditions).  
- Recommend testing the method with larger amounts of in-domain data beyond the development set.  
- Suggest including example sentences selected by each method to provide concrete evidence of selection quality.  
- Compare against Axelrod (2015) POS-tag-based method to evaluate whether word2vec embeddings meaningfully abstract away surface forms.  
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore sum of cross-entropy differences; add a citation and discussion.  
- Propose extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel model with learned weights for combining source and target scores as future work.
