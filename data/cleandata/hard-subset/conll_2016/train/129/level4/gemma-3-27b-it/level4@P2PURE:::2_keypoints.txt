- Presents CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT following Johnson & Zhang (2015).
- Method outperforms language model–based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and reports up to +3.1 BLEU improvements.
- Key advantage: maintains robustness with limited in-domain training data, unlike other approaches.
- Many gains depend on having in-domain data in the training set; training only on in-domain data already gives +2.8 BLEU.
- Recommend comparison to interpolating separate in-domain and out-of-domain models to contextualize gains.
- Claim of CNN effectiveness with very small in-domain data is mainly supported by zh→en, which includes in-domain training data and may simplify selection.
- Unclear if results generalize to tasks without any in-domain examples; en→es and en→zh have very small dev sets, raising concerns.
- Suggest reporting results when training is limited to only 100 sentence pairs for all tasks to strengthen the claim.
- For social media application, request details on preprocessing/filtering for high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons).
- Note that data selection alone cannot solve OOV problems; ask for concrete examples illustrating the nature of improvements from adaptation.
- Section 3.2: clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined and provide an architectural diagram.
- Section 3.2: specify the “designated loss function.”
- Section 5.2: report how system weights were tuned.
