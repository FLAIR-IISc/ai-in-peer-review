- Proposes a CNN-based in-domain data selection method for SMT (building on Johnson & Zhang 2015) that yields ~0.5 BLEU improvement over LM-based selection and is robust with limited in-domain data.  
- Claim of up to +3.1 BLEU is misleading: much of the gain relies on having in-domain data in training, and training only on in-domain data already gives +2.8 BLEU.  
- Recommend comparison to interpolation of separate in-domain and out-domain models as a more direct baseline.  
- strongest contribution is the experiment showing CNN effectiveness with minimal in-domain data, but that experiment is limited to zh→en where training contains in-domain examples.  
- Question of generalization to en→es and en→zh (which lack in-domain training examples); current results are only preliminary due to very small dev sets.  
- Request to report results for all tasks when trained with only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to strengthen the claim.  
- Social-media SMT has high OOV and special-token issues; clarify what preprocessing or filtering (if any) was applied for usernames, hashtags, emoticons, and non-standard spelling.  
- Data selection alone cannot fix OOVs; provide concrete translation examples before/after selection to illustrate types of improvements achieved.  
- Section 3.2: Clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined; include a diagram of the CNN architecture.  
- Section 3.2: Specify the “designated loss function.”  
- Section 5.2: Describe the method used to tune system weights (briefly for completeness).
