- Introduces a novel CNN-based classifier for MT training data selection and compares it thoroughly to LM- and n-gram–based baselines.  
- Missing comparison to bilingual data selection methods (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies).  
- Initial motivation for choosing a CNN over RNN/LSTM was unclear, though the paper later argues CNNs better identify salient sentence parts.  
- Lacks experimental evidence on whether Bag-of-Words (BOW), sequential (SEQ), or combined representations are most effective—and why.  
- CNN architecture description (one-hot and semi-supervised pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed.  
- A diagram of the CNN layers and input combination would greatly improve clarity and accessibility.  
- Writing is generally good but contains unnecessary parenthetical citations that should be simplified.  
- Experimental results support claims, but the method for choosing the optimal number of selected in-domain sentences (via a separate validation set) needs clarification—what data comprises this set?  
- Missing CNN hyperparameter details and sensitivity analysis on hyperparameter impact.  
- Table 2 should include comparisons of approaches using the same number of selected sentences.  
- Figure 1 indicates the proposed approach consistently outperforms baselines despite the table issue.  
- Suggested experiment: evaluate performance with a larger in-domain corpus to assess scalability.  
- Suggested analysis: show example sentences selected by each method to substantiate claims in section 5.4.  
- Recommend comparison to Axelrod (2015) POS-based abstraction to judge whether word2vec embeddings give an incremental advantage.  
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore sum-of-difference-of-cross-entropies technique—add that reference.  
- Suggested extension: learn weights for combining source and target scores by adapting the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to potentially improve performance.
