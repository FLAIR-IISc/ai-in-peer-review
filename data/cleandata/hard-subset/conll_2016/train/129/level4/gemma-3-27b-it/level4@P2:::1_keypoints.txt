- Proposes a CNN-based data selection method for SMT using semi-supervised pre-trained embeddings to rank general-domain sentences for in-domain training.  
- Demonstrates the CNN approach outperforms strong language-model baselines across multiple language pairs and domains.  
- Shows effective data selection is possible with extremely small in-domain sets (as few as 100 sentences).  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results: consistent BLEU gains across four language directions and three domains, up to +3.1 BLEU on Chinese→English SMS.  
- Effective semi-supervised learning: pre-trained embeddings enable the CNN to beat a supervised one-hot CNN.  
- Low-resource robustness: method yields significant gains and stable performance with varying tiny in-domain samples.  

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient analysis of CNN architecture choices, particularly bag-of-words vs sequential representations.  
- Dependence on a validation set for selecting number of sentences without detailing its size/source or sensitivity, raising reproducibility concerns.  
- Limited comparison to bilingual data-selection methods (e.g., cross-entropy difference approaches).  

Questions to authors:
- What is the validation set’s size, source, and representativeness used to choose the number of selected in-domain sentences?  
- How sensitive are results to hyperparameters (window size, stride, number of neurons); can you provide an ablation/sensitivity study?  
- Can you show example sentences selected by the semi-supervised CNN versus a baseline (e.g., random) to illustrate selection quality?  

Additional comments:
- Consider referencing Lewis and Moore on summed cross-entropy differences.  
- Suggest extending the model to directly handle bilingual data by learning source/target weights.  
- Compare to POS-based abstraction methods (e.g., Axelrod et al., 2015) as a benchmark.  
- Include a visual diagram of the CNN architecture to improve clarity.
