- Proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT building on Johnson & Zhang (2015).
- Method outperforms language-model-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and is robust with limited in-domain data.
- Reports overall improvements up to 3.1 BLEU, but large gains depend on having some in-domain data in initial training.
- Training only on in-domain data yields +2.8 BLEU, implying the selection step provides modest additional benefit when in-domain data is present.
- Recommend comparison against interpolating separate models trained on in- and out-of-domain data.
- The second experiment shows CNN effectiveness with very scarce in-domain data but is limited to zh→en, which had in-domain data in training.
- Unclear if scarce-data results generalize to en→es and en→zh (which lack in-domain examples); suggest reporting results using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs for all tasks.
- Application to social media translation is relevant; request clarification on preprocessing/filtering for high OOV rates, nonstandard spelling, usernames, hashtags, emoticons.
- Note data selection cannot directly solve OOVs; request concrete details or examples showing how selected data improves translation quality.
- Section 3.2 needs clarification on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined; a model diagram is suggested.
- Define the "designated loss function" explicitly (Section 3.2).
- Section 5.2 should detail the method used to tune system weights.
