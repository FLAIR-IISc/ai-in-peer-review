- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN for in-domain data selection in SMT that outperforms traditional LM-based selection.  
- Method remains effective with very small in-domain datasets, enabling fine-grained topic-dependent adaptation.  
- Validated on four language directions and three test domains, including social media.  
- Approach is well-motivated and clearly explained for domain adaptation with limited data.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over strong baselines, including state-of-the-art LM-based methods.  
- Provides thorough analysis and discussion of results across different scenarios.  
- Shows robustness: good performance even with very tiny in-domain datasets.  
- Lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, such as separate in-/out-domain model approaches.  
- Minimal-in-domain experiment is limited to zh→en and not run across all tasks.  
- Insufficient detail on preprocessing and filtering steps, especially for social media data.  
- Limited insight/examples illustrating the specific types of translation improvements from adaptation.  
- Requests more detail on preprocessing/filtering for social media texts.  
- Asks how the OOV problem in social media translation is addressed.  
- Requests examples showing improved translations due to data selection.  
- Asks whether separate in- and out-domain models were considered and how the proposed method compares.
