- Paper proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT, building on Johnson and Zhang (2015), yielding ~0.5 BLEU improvement over LM-based selection.
- Method is robust with very limited in-domain data.
- Authors' claimed 3.1 BLEU gain is misleading: large gains occur only when in-domain data is included in training.
- Training only on in-domain data already gives ~2.8 BLEU improvement, so comparison to model interpolation (separate in-/out-of-domain models) is needed.
- The second experiment (effectiveness with minimal in-domain data) is the most compelling result.
- That experiment was run only on zh→en, which includes in-domain data and may make selection easier; generality to other tasks is uncertain.
- en→es and en→zh dev-set results hint at positive trends, but results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs should be reported.
- Social media text has high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); the paper should state any special preprocessing or filtering applied.
- Data selection cannot solve OOVs alone; the paper should provide concrete translation examples to show what improvements adaptation achieves.
- Section 3.2 needs clarification on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined—an architectural illustration would help.
- The nature of the mentioned "designated loss function" in Section 3.2 is unclear and should be specified.
- Section 5.2 should report how system weights were tuned for completeness.
