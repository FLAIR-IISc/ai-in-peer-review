- Summary: Proposes semi‑supervised CNNs for in‑domain data selection in SMT, using few in‑domain positives and equal random negatives to score and select general‑domain sentence pairs for adaptation.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel semi‑supervised CNN selection method that outperforms language‑model‑based data selection.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates effectiveness even with very small in‑domain datasets.

- Contribution 3: Shows the classifier can select in‑domain bilingual sentences to train an adapted SMT system under limited-data conditions.

- Strength 1: Novel and effective approach useful for SMT when in‑domain data is scarce.

- Strength 2: Thorough evaluation including comparisons with state‑of‑the‑art LM‑based methods.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates robustness of the semi‑supervised CNN with minimal in‑domain data.

- Weakness 1: Lacks detailed analysis/examples showing how selected data concretely improves translation quality.

- Weakness 2: No clear illustration of the semi‑supervised CNN architecture or how word embeddings, segment, and one‑hot vectors are combined.

- Weakness 3: Omits discussion of preprocessing/filtering steps, which matters for social‑media text, high OOV rates, and non‑conventional spelling.

- Question 1: Please provide concrete examples or analyses of translation improvements from the selected/adapted data.

- Question 2: Please include an architectural illustration of the semi‑supervised CNN clarifying vector combination.

- Question 3: Were any special preprocessing or filtering steps applied, particularly for noisy social‑media text and OOV handling?
