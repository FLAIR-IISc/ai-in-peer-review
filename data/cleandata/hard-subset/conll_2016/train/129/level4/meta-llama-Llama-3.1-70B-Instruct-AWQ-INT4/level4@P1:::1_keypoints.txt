- Proposes semi-supervised CNNs for selecting in-domain SMT training data, claiming up to +3.1 BLEU with very little in-domain data.  
- Main contribution: semi-supervised CNNs for domain classification enabling effective data selection with limited in-domain data.  
- Main contribution: shows the method can outperform state-of-the-art LM-based data selection even with tiny in-domain data.  
- Main contribution: evaluates the method across multiple language directions and test domains.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated, and clearly explained approach to data selection for SMT.  
- Strength: thorough comparison to prior continuous and n-gram LM-based selection methods.  
- Strength: experimental results support claims and include detailed analysis.  
- Weakness: no experimental comparison of CNN input representations (BOW vs SEQ).  
- Weakness: unclear explanation of how the number of selected in-domain sentences is determined from a validation set.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of CNN hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Question: provide details on the validation data used to choose the number of selected sentences.  
- Question: how sensitive are CNNs to hyperparameters and how were they selected?  
- Question: can the technique be compared to baselines when more in-domain data is available (beyond dev set)?  
- Comment: recommend running experiments with larger amounts of in-domain data for comparison.  
- Comment: suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to support claims.  
- Comment: ask for more detailed analysis of effectiveness across language directions and domains.  
- Comment: note similarity between summed source+target classification scores and Lewis-Moore LM selection; recommend citing that work.  
- Comment: propose extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to learn weights for combining source and target scores.
