- Proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT, building on Johnson and Zhang (2015).  
- Reports ~0.5 BLEU improvement over language model–based data selection and robustness with small in-domain sets.  
- Claimed 3.1 BLEU improvement appears overstated because including in-domain data in training already yields ~2.8 BLEU.  
- Suggests comparing to an interpolated in-domain + out-of-domain model to better assess effectiveness.  
- Highlights the second experiment (CNN effective with minimal in-domain data) as the most significant result.  
- Notes that the second experiment was only run on zh→en, which had in-domain data in training, possibly making selection easier.  
- Recommends testing other tasks (en→es, en→zh) and reporting results with only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to strengthen the claim.  
- Points out high OOV rates and special character sequences in social media text; asks whether special preprocessing/filtering was applied.  
- Observes that data selection alone cannot solve OOV issues and requests examples/case studies to illustrate adaptation gains.  
- Section 3.2 needs clearer explanation/illustration of how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined.  
- Section 3.2 should explicitly state the “designated loss function” used.  
- Section 5.2 should report how system weights were tuned for completeness.
