- Proposes an MT training data selection method using a CNN classifier to score and rank general-domain sentences.
- Provides thorough comparison with prior continuous or n-gram LM-based selection methods, but doesn’t clarify whether bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies) were considered.
- Justifies using a CNN over RNN/LSTM by arguing that certain sentence sections matter more, but lacks experimental comparison to BOW vs SEQ (or combined) representations to support this.
- Textual description of the CNN (one-hot and semi-supervised with pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed.
- Paper would benefit from a visual diagram of the CNN layers to show how inputs are combined.
- Generally well-written, but some citation formatting in parentheses is unnecessary (e.g., \citet vs. \citep).
- The method for choosing the number of selected in-domain sentences via a validation set is unclear; the source of this validation data and hyperparameter selection process are not specified.
- Unclear how sensitive the model performance is to validation/hyperparameter choices.
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 suggests the proposed approach still outperforms baselines under equal-size selection.
- Would be interesting to test the technique with more in-domain data beyond the development set.
- Include example sentences selected by different methods to support claims in section 5.4.
- Suggests comparing abstraction-from-surface-forms claim to Axelrod (2015) who used POS-tag replacement to reduce LM sparsity to see if word2vec adds value.
- Notes similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore sum of difference of cross-entropies method; recommends adding that reference around line 435.
- Recommends exploring learned weights for source/target score sums by extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting.
