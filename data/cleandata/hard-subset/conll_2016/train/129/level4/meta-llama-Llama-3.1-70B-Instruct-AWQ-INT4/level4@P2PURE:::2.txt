The paper proposes a method for in-domain data selection for Statistical Machine Translation (SMT) using a convolutional neural network (CNN) classifier, which follows the framework introduced by Johnson and Zhang (2015). This approach outperforms language model-based data selection by approximately 0.5 BLEU points and demonstrates robustness even with very limited in-domain data.

However, the paper's claim of a 3.1 BLEU point improvement appears to be overstated. Upon closer inspection of the results, it becomes evident that this magnitude of improvement is only achieved when in-domain data is present in the training set, as training solely on in-domain data already yields a 2.8 BLEU point gain. A more comprehensive comparison would involve evaluating the proposed method against a system that interpolates separate in-domain and out-domain models.

A more compelling result, in my opinion, is the second experiment, which showcases the effectiveness of the CNN classifier even with minimal in-domain data. Nevertheless, this experiment is only conducted on the zh2en task, where in-domain data is present in the training set, potentially making data selection easier. It would be valuable to determine whether this result holds for the other tasks, which lack in-domain data in the training set. The development set results for the en2es and en2zh tasks, with only a few hundred sentence pairs, suggest that this might be the case. To strengthen the claim, it would be beneficial to report results for all tasks using only 100 sentence pairs for training.

When translating social media text, unique challenges arise, including a high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) rate due to non-standard spellings and special character sequences such as usernames, hashtags, or emoticons. Was any special preprocessing or filtering applied to the data to address these issues? As data selection alone cannot mitigate the OOV problem, it would be interesting to explore the types of improvements achieved through adaptation via data selection, potentially by providing concrete examples.
