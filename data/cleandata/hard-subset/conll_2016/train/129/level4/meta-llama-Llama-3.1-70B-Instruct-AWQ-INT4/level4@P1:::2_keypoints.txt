- Proposed semi-supervised CNN method for in-domain data selection in SMT, outperforming language model–based selection.
- Method can adapt to new domains with very limited in-domain data (as few as 100 sentence pairs).
- Experiments conducted on four language directions with different test domains.
- Addresses the important challenge of domain adaptation with limited in-domain data.
- Method is robust and effective even with very small in-domain datasets.
- Experimental results are comprehensive and show superiority over LM-based methods.
- Claimed 3.1 BLEU improvement is misleading—this only occurs when in-domain data are in the training set; actual gain over LM-based selection is ~0.5 BLEU.
- Second experiment demonstrating effectiveness with very little in-domain data is run only on zh→en and should be replicated across all tasks with 100 pairs.
- Paper lacks detailed analysis or examples showing how adaptation via data selection produced translation improvements.
- Request for more details on preprocessing and filtering steps for the social media text data.
- Ask how the out-of-vocabulary (OOV) problem for social media text translation is addressed.
- Request clarification and details on the “designated loss function” used in the semi-supervised CNN.
- Ask how system weights were tuned in the experiments.
- Section 3.2 needs clearer explanation of how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined; an architectural illustration would help.
- For completeness, system weight tuning details should be mentioned in Section 5.2.
