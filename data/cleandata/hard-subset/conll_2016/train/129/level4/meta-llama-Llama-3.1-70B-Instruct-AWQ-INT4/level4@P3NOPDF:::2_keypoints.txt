- Paper proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT, building on Johnson and Zhang (2015), and reports a 0.5 BLEU gain over LM-based selection.  
- Claimed 3.1 BLEU improvement depends on including in-domain data in training; training only on in-domain data already gives a 2.8 BLEU gain.  
- Incremental benefit of the proposed method may be smaller than reported; compare against interpolation of separate in-/out-domain models.  
- Second experiment shows CNN classifier is robust with very limited in-domain data, which is a notable result.  
- That robustness experiment is only on zh→en (which includes in-domain data), so results may not generalize to tasks with no in-domain training data.  
- Suggest running all tasks with only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to validate the limited-data claim.  
- Unclear whether special preprocessing/filtering was applied for social media text (usernames, hashtags, emoticons, non-standard spellings).  
- Data selection alone cannot solve high OOV rates in social media; need clarity on what types of improvements adaptation achieved.  
- Recommend providing qualitative examples or error analysis to illustrate the improvements from data selection.  
- Section 3.2: clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined—an architectural illustration would help.  
- Section 3.2: the “designated loss function” is not explicitly defined and needs a detailed explanation.  
- Section 5.2: for completeness, report how system weights were tuned.  
- Overall: interesting and promising approach, but requires additional experiments, clarifications, and analyses to strengthen the claims.
