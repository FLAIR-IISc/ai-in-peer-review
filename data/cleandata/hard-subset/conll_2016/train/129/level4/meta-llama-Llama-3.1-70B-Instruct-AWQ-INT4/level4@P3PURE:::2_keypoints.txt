- Paper proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT, improving ~0.5 BLEU over LM-based selection and showing robustness with very limited in-domain data.
- The reported 3.1 BLEU improvement is misleading: such gains occur only when in-domain data is present in training, and training only on in-domain data already yields a ~2.8 BLEU gain.
- Recommend comparison to interpolating separate in-domain and out-of-domain models to contextualize the claimed gains.
- Second experiment’s main contribution is CNN effectiveness with minimal in-domain data, but it’s only shown for zh→en where in-domain data exists in training.
- Unclear if the minimal-data result generalizes to other tasks without in-domain training data; en→es and en→zh dev results hint positively.
- Suggest reporting results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to better support the minimal-data claim.
- Social-media translation raises high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); the paper should state any preprocessing/filtering applied.
- Data selection cannot solve OOVs alone; the paper should provide concrete examples showing what types of improvements adaptation via data selection yields.
- Section 3.2 needs clearer explanation and an architecture diagram showing how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined.
- The “designated loss function” in the model is not explicitly specified and should be stated.
- Section 5.2 should report how system weights were tuned for completeness.
