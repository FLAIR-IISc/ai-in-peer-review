- Proposes a novel CNN classifier to score and rank general-domain sentences for MT training data selection.  
- Comparison to continuous/n-gram language model baselines is solid, but unclear if bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies) are compared.  
- Motivation for choosing CNN over RNN/LSTM was initially unclear, though later argued that CNNs capture important sentence sections.  
- No experimental comparison between bag-of-words (BOW), sequence (SEQ), or combined representations to justify representation choice.  
- CNN architecture and embedding choices are clearly described; a layer-structure diagram would improve intuition.  
- Paper is well-written, with minor citation formatting issues (unnecessary parenthetical citations).  
- Unclear how the number of selected in-domain sentences is determined: validation data source, hyperparameter selection, and sensitivity are not specified.  
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 indicates the proposed method still outperforms baselines under that condition.  
- Suggest running experiments with more in-domain data (beyond the development set).  
- Recommend including example sentences selected by different methods to support claims in section 5.4.  
- Propose an additional baseline replacing words with POS tags (Axelrod 2015) to test whether word2vec embeddings add value beyond reducing sparsity.  
- Note similarity between summing source/target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore LM sum-of-difference cross-entropies approach; add a reference.  
- Ask whether weights for combining source and target scores could be learned by extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting.
