- Proposes MT training-data selection using a CNN classifier to score and rank general-domain sentences and compares to continuous or n-gram LM baselines; unclear if bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies) were considered.  
- Initial CNN vs RNN/LSTM motivation was unclear but paper argues CNN captures important sentence sections; no experiments assess BOW vs SEQ (or combined) representations or explain which matters more.  
- Textual description of the CNN (one-hot vs pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed.  
- A visual diagram of the CNN layers showing input combination would improve clarity.  
- Paper is well-written overall but has minor citation-format issues (unnecessary parentheses like \citet vs. \citep).  
- Experiments support claims but the method for selecting the number of in-domain sentences (using a validation set) is unclear—origin and composition of that validation data are not described.  
- It’s unclear how CNN hyperparameters were chosen and how sensitive results are to those choices.  
- Table 2 should compare approaches at the same number of selected sentences (Figure 1 suggests the proposed method still outperforms baselines under this constraint).  
- Run experiments comparing the technique to baselines when more in-domain data (beyond the dev set) is available.  
- Include example sentences selected by different methods to substantiate claims in Section 5.4.  
- Compare the approach of abstracting away from surface forms to Axelrod (2015)’s POS-tag replacement to assess whether word2vec embeddings give an advantage.  
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore sum of difference of cross-entropies method; add a reference and consider learning weights for source/target scores by extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting.
