- Proposes MT training data selection using a CNN classifier to score/rank general-domain sentences; compares well to continuous and n-gram LM baselines but unclear if bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies) were compared.  
- Initial motivation for using a CNN over RNN/LSTM was unclear, though the paper argues CNNs capture important sentence sections.  
- Paper lacks experimental investigation comparing bag-of-words (BOW), sequential (SEQ), or combined representations to justify the chosen architecture.  
- Textual description of the CNN (one-hot and semi-supervised pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed.  
- A diagram illustrating the CNN layer structure and input combination would be highly beneficial.  
- Writing is overall good, but some citation formatting (e.g., \citet vs \citep) is unnecessary.  
- Experiments support claims but it’s unclear how the number of selected in-domain sentences was chosen, what validation data was used, and how CNN hyperparameters were selected.  
- Recommend reporting hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for the CNN models.  
- Table 2 should compare approaches using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 suggests the proposed method still outperforms baselines in that setting.  
- Suggest evaluating the method when more in-domain data is available beyond the development set.  
- Recommend showing example sentences selected by different methods to support claims in Section 5.4.  
- Propose comparing to Axelrod (2015) POS-tag substitution baseline to test whether word2vec embeddings add advantage.  
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore LM selection (sum of difference of cross-entropies); recommend citing this work.  
- Suggest extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to learn weights for combining source and target scores.
