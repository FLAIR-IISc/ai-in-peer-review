- Proposes a CNN-based MT training data selection method that scores and ranks general-domain sentences with thorough comparison to continuous and n-gram LMs.
- Does not compare to bilingual data selection methods (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies / Lewis-Moore).
- Provides a convincing argument for CNNs over RNNs/LSTMs due to their ability to emphasize important sentence regions.
- Fails to experimentally compare bag-of-words (BOW) vs. sequential (SEQ) representations or combinations thereof.
- Textual description of the CNN architecture is clear, but a diagram of the layer structure and input combination is missing.
- Generally well-written, though some citation formatting is inconsistent.
- Unclear how the number of selected in-domain sentences was determined and what validation data was used.
- Hyperparameter selection for the CNN and the models’ sensitivity to these choices are not reported.
- Table 2 should include comparisons with the same number of selected sentences across approaches.
- Recommend testing the method with more in-domain data beyond the development set.
- Suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to support qualitative claims.
- Propose an additional baseline that replaces words with POS tags (Axelrod 2015) to address LM sparsity and evaluate word2vec benefits.
- Recommend acknowledging and citing the similarity to source/target LM cross-entropy selection methods.
- Suggest extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting or learning weights to combine source and target classification scores.
