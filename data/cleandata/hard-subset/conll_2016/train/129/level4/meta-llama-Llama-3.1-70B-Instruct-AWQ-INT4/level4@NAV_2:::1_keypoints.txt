- Proposes semi-supervised CNNs to compute domain relevance scores and select top general-domain sentence pairs for SMT adaptation with very limited in-domain data.
- Introduces a semi-supervised CNN-based data selection method that outperforms state-of-the-art language model-based selection.
- Shows that a small selected subset can outperform systems trained on all general-domain data, effective even with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs.
- Evaluates the approach across four language pairs and three test domains, demonstrating broad effectiveness.
- Well-motivated, clearly explained approach addressing SMT adaptation with scarce in-domain data.
- Thorough empirical evaluation including comparisons to LM-based baselines and hyperparameter analysis.
