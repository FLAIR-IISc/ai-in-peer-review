- Proposes a CNN classifier for in-domain data selection in SMT (based on Johnson & Zhang 2015), outperforming LM-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and robust with very limited in-domain data.  
- The reported 3.1 BLEU improvement is likely overstated — that gain occurs only when in-domain data is already in the training set, and training solely on in-domain data gives ≈2.8 BLEU.  
- A fairer comparison would include an interpolated in-domain/out-domain model baseline.  
- The second experiment showing CNN effectiveness with minimal in-domain data is promising but only run on zh→en (which has in-domain training data); results may not generalize to tasks without in-domain training data.  
- Recommend reporting results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to strengthen the claim.  
- Social-media translation has high OOV and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); the paper should state any preprocessing/filtering used and show concrete examples of improvements, since data selection alone cannot solve OOVs.  
- Section 3.2 needs clarity on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined and would benefit from an architecture illustration; the specific "designated loss function" is not stated.  
- Section 5.2 should state how system weights were tuned.
