- Paper proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT, showing ~0.5 BLEU improvement over LM-based selection and robustness with minimal in-domain data.
- Authors’ headline claim of 3.1 BLEU gains is misleading—large gains occur only when in-domain data is included in training.
- Training solely on in-domain data already yields ~2.8 BLEU, so comparisons should control for inclusion of in-domain data.
- Recommend comparing against an interpolated model that combines separate in-domain and out-domain systems.
- Second experiment’s key positive result is CNN effectiveness with very little in-domain data, but it’s limited to zh→en where in-domain data exists in training.
- Unclear if the limited-data result generalizes to tasks with no in-domain training data; suggest testing other tasks or reporting results using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs across all tasks.
- Social media translation has high OOV and special tokens; paper should state any preprocessing/filtering steps applied.
- Data selection does not solve OOV issues—provide concrete examples of improvements from adaptation via data selection to illustrate what changed.
- Section 3.2 needs clarity on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined (architectural diagram suggested) and must specify the “designated loss function.”
- Section 5.2 should report how system weights were tuned for completeness.
