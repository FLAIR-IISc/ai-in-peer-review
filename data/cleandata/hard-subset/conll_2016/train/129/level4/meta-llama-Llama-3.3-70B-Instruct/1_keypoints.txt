- Proposes a CNN classifier to score/rank general-domain sentences for MT data selection; comparison to LM-based baselines is thorough but unclear if bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies) were evaluated.  
- Argues CNN is preferable to RNN/LSTM because it captures important local sections, but lacks experiments comparing bag-of-words (BOW) vs sequential (SEQ) representations or their combination.  
- Textual description of the CNN and embedding choices (one-hot vs pre-trained) is clear, but a layer diagram showing how inputs are combined is missing.  
- Paper is well written overall, but citation formatting is inconsistent and some parenthetical citations are unnecessary.  
- Experiments support claims, yet it is unclear what validation data was used to choose the number of selected in-domain sentences and how CNN hyperparameters were chosen; sensitivity to hyperparameters is not discussed.  
- Table 2 should present comparisons with the same number of selected sentences (Figure 1 suggests the proposed method still outperforms baselines under equal-size selection).  
- Recommend an experiment comparing the method to baselines with more in-domain data beyond the development set.  
- Suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to substantiate claims in section 5.4.  
- Propose comparing to Axelrod (2015)’s POS-replacement approach to evaluate whether word2vec embeddings provide additional benefit in abstracting surface forms.  
- Note the similarity between summing source and target classification scores and the Lewis-Moore sum-of-difference-of-cross-entropies method; recommend citing that prior work.  
- Suggest exploring learned weights for combining source and target scores by extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting.
