- Proposes CNN-based data selection for SMT scoring sentence pairs by in-domain relevance using tiny labeled in-domain set vs. random negatives; semi-supervised variant uses pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Evaluated on four translation directions and three social-media domains; selecting 2.5–10% of data yields higher BLEU than using all data and beats n-gram/RNNLM baselines.  
- Main contribution 1: semi-supervised CNN with region/segment embeddings is a strong selector under very limited in-domain supervision, outperforming LM-based selectors.  
- Main contribution 2: shows effective adaptation possible with as few as 100 in-domain sentences (demonstrated for zh→en).  
- Main contribution 3: proposes a practical bilingual selection strategy that combines source- and target-side scores.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains across all tasks, up to +3.1 BLEU and consistent 0.7–1.4 BLEU improvements; significance tested via bootstrap resampling.  
- Strength: robustness in tiny-supervision regime (100 sentences) with notable BLEU gains, addressing the motivating low-resource scenario.  
- Strength: clear experimental setup and reproducible design choices (CNN hyperparameters, embedding training, data splits, selection sizes).  
- Strength: method is computationally reasonable and scalable; pretrained embeddings leverage unlabeled data effectively.  
- Weakness: largest +3.1 BLEU gain may be confounded by presence of large in-domain subset in pool; unclear how much selection vs. domain presence contributes.  
- Weakness: 100-sentence robustness shown only for zh→en, limiting generalization to other language pairs without in-domain pool components.  
- Weakness: model description lacks clarity on combining one-hot and embedding-based region vectors, and the designated loss function is unspecified.  
- Weakness: preprocessing and OOV handling for social media (usernames, hashtags, emojis, spelling variants) are underdescribed.  
- Weakness: missing baseline comparisons such as interpolation of in-domain and out-of-domain SMT models or instance/mixture weighting.  
- Question: can the 100-sentence experiment be reported for en→es, en→zh, and ar→en (or 100/400/800 variants) to test generality?  
- Question: how does sscnn compare to interpolation between in-domain-only and full out-of-domain models, and to mixture/instance weighting?  
- Question: clarify architecture for combining wl(x) and ul(x), the loss used (logistic/hinge), and whether class weights are applied.  
- Question: provide optimization details—regularization, early stopping, batch size, optimizer, hyperparameter selection and sensitivity.  
- Question: detail social-media preprocessing (normalization, token handling) and how tokens are represented in embeddings (UNK vs. surface forms).  
- Question: are classifier outputs calibrated probabilities; what ranking/thresholding strategy and selection-size tuning were used; provide source-only vs. target-only vs. summed-score ablation.  
- Question: how were SMT weights tuned (MERT/MIRA/etc.); were the same dev sets used for labeling and tuning and how is selection bias avoided?  
- Overall: solid empirical paper with practical method and convincing gains; main novelty is empirical validation rather than methodological, and addressing the listed clarifications and baselines would strengthen the paper.
