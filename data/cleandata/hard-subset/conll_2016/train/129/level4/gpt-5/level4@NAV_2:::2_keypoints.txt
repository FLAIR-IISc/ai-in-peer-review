- Proposes a CNN classifier for SMT in-domain data selection (following Johnson & Zhang 2015), yielding ≈+0.5 BLEU vs. LM-based selection and robust with very small in-domain sets.  
- Reports gains up to 3.1 BLEU, but large gains occur only when in-domain material is present in the general training pool; training solely on in-domain data already gives ≈+2.8 BLEU.  
- Recommend comparison to an interpolated in-domain + out-of-domain model baseline.  
- Most compelling evidence is the second experiment showing CNN effectiveness with limited in-domain data, but it’s only on zh→en which may be easier due to genuine in-domain content in the pool.  
- Question whether the robustness holds for other tasks where the training pool lacks identifiable in-domain material; en→es and en→zh dev sets are very small and suggest possible limitations.  
- Ask for results for all tasks when classifier training is restricted to 100 in-domain sentence pairs to strengthen the claim.  
- Request details on preprocessing/filtering for social media (handling high OOV rates, non-standard spellings, usernames, hashtags, emoticons).  
- Note that data selection doesn’t directly address OOVs and ask for illustrative examples of the types of improvements achieved by this adaptation.  
- Section 3.2: Clarify how word embeddings, segment embeddings, and one-hot vectors are combined in the model; an architecture figure is requested.  
- Section 3.2: Specify the “designated loss function” used during training.
