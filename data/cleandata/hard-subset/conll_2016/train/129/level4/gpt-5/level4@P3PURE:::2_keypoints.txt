- Proposes CNN-based in-domain data selection for SMT (following Johnson & Zhang 2015), outperforming LM-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU and remaining effective with very small in-domain data.  
- Claimed 3.1 BLEU gain appears to depend on including in-domain data in training; training only on in-domain already yields +2.8 BLEU.  
- Request to compare against interpolation of independently trained in-domain and out-of-domain models.  
- Second experiment (CNN effective with limited data) is compelling but only shown on zh→en where in-domain data are in training; unclear if result generalizes to tasks without in-domain training data.  
- Ask to report results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs for training to strengthen the limited-data claim.  
- Raise domain-specific concerns for social media: high OOV rates, nonstandard spellings, usernames/hashtags/emoticons—ask whether special preprocessing or filtering was applied.  
- Note data selection alone cannot fix OOVs; request concrete examples illustrating what types of improvements data selection yields.  
- Section 3.2: clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the model and provide an architectural illustration.  
- Section 3.2: specify the “designated loss function” used during training.
