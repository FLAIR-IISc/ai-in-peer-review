- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN using tiny in-domain seeds and random negatives to score/select bilingual sentence pairs for SMT adaptation.
- Combines source and target sentence scores (summed) to rank and select top general-domain data for retraining phrase-based SMT.
- Demonstrates consistent BLEU gains over n-gram and RNNLM selection across four language pairs and social-media domains.
- Shows robustness when in-domain supervision is extremely small, reporting meaningful gains even with ~100 in-domain sentences (reported for zh→en).
- Provides implementation details (embeddings, region sizes, pooling) and a reproducible recipe for resource-constrained adaptation.
- Large empirical gains (e.g., +3.1 BLEU zh→en sms using ~3% data) and statistically significant improvements across tasks.
- Stability under tiny supervision indicated by repeated sampling (mean BLEU ~25.03, sd 0.12) in the reported experiment.
- Leverages pre-trained embeddings and convolution+max-pooling to generalize from sparse domain cues better than LM-based methods.
- Clear baseline comparisons and significance testing against standard LM-based selection methods.
- Confound in largest reported gain: zh→en training pool contains an in-domain subset, so selection improvement may partly reflect availability of genuine in-domain material.
- The 100-sentence robustness claim is only demonstrated on zh→en (which has overlap), limiting generalization to other language pairs/domains.
- Missing ablations and architectural clarity: no quantified gap for source-only vs target-only scoring or detailed breakdown of one-hot vs embedding contributions.
- Incomplete training/optimization details: classifier loss, class weighting, regularization, early stopping, and SMT tuning method (MERT/MIRA/PRO) are unspecified.
- Social-media preprocessing and OOV handling are not described, leaving unclear how noisy text was normalized or how OOV rates changed after selection.
- No runtime or memory cost analysis comparing CNN scoring to LM-based selection for large corpora.
- Request to replicate 100-sentence experiment on a task without in-domain overlap (e.g., ar→en or en→es) to validate tiny-supervision robustness.
- Ask for a diagram/explanation of how one-hot and embedding-derived segment vectors are combined, plus the classifier loss and class-imbalance handling.
- Request ablations for source-only vs target-only vs summed scores, one-hot vs semi-supervised CNN, region types, and pooling strategies.
- Ask for details on SMT weight tuning (algorithm, dev set) and confirmation that tuning was separate from classifier training/selection data.
- Ask for social-media preprocessing steps and analysis of OOV/vocabulary coverage changes after selection.
- Ask for computational cost and practical guidance on runtime and memory for training/scoring with the CNN.
- Overall: solid, practical contribution to SMT adaptation under limited supervision, but needs to address the zh→en confound, extend tiny-supervision experiments, and clarify architecture/training/preprocessing to strengthen claims.
