- Paper proposes MT training-data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN classifier.
- Comparison to prior continuous or n-gram LM-based selection is solid, but unclear whether bilingual selection methods (e.g., sum of differences of cross-entropies) were evaluated.
- Initial motivation for preferring CNN over RNN/LSTM is weak, though the paper argues CNNs capture the most informative sentence parts.
- Experiments do not clarify the relative importance of BOW vs SEQ representations or their combination, nor explain why.
- Textual description of the CNN (one-hot vs semi-supervised embeddings) is clear and detailed.
- A figure of the CNN architecture showing how inputs are combined would be very helpful.
- Writing is overall good, but some parenthetical citation styles are unnecessary/inconsistent.
- Experimental methodology is supported but the procedure for choosing the number of selected in-domain sentences (via a validation set) is unclear.
- It is unspecified which validation data and which data were used to choose CNN hyperparameters, and model sensitivity to these choices is not reported.
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 suggests the proposed method still outperforms under that constraint.
- Request experiments comparing the proposed technique to baselines when more in-domain data is available (beyond the development set).
- Suggest including example sentences selected by different methods to support claims in Section 5.4.
- Recommend adding Axelrod (2015)-style baseline (word→POS replacement) to test whether word2vec embeddings add benefit beyond reduced LM sparsity.
- Note that summing source and target classification scores is similar to Lewis–Moore LM data selection; add that reference near line 435.
- Suggest extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting to learn weights for combining source and target classification scores.
