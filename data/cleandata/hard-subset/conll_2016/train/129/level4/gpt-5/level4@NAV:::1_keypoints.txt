- Paper proposes MT training-data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN classifier; comparisons to continuous and n-gram LM baselines are well executed.
- It’s unclear whether the paper compared against bilingual data selection (e.g., sum of difference of cross-entropies).
- The CNN choice over RNN/LSTM is persuasively motivated by locality of informative sentence parts.
- The paper lacks empirical ablation showing the relative importance of BOW vs SEQ representations or their combination.
- The textual description of the CNN (one-hot vs pre-trained embeddings) is clear and detailed.
- A diagram of the CNN layers and how inputs are combined is needed for clarity.
- Writing is generally good, but some parenthetical citation formats are unnecessary (e.g., \citet vs. \citep).
- Concern about the procedure for choosing the number of selected in-domain sentences: what validation data was used and how hyperparameters were chosen?
- Need analysis of model sensitivity to validation data and hyperparameter choices.
- Table 2 should compare approaches using the same number of selected sentences (Figure 1 suggests the method still outperforms baselines under equal conditions).
- Request experiments with more in-domain data (beyond the development set) to test robustness.
- Recommend including example sentences selected by different methods to support Section 5.4 claims.
- Suggest comparing against Axelrod (2015) or similar baselines that reduce LM sparsity (e.g., POS replacements) to test whether word2vec offers extra benefit.
- Note similarity between summing source and target classification scores and source+target Lewis-Moore LM selection (sum of difference of cross-entropies); add a reference and consider learning weights or extending the CNN to a bilingual setting.
