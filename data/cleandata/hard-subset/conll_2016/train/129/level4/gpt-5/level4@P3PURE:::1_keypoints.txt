- Proposes CNN-based MT training data selection that scores/ranks general-domain sentences to capture localized domain-relevant spans.  
- Thorough comparison to prior continuous and n-gram LM-based selection methods.  
- Strong motivation for using CNNs to capture that some sentence sections are more important than others.  
- Clear textual description of CNN variants (one-hot vs. semi-supervised with pre-trained embeddings) and design choices.  
- Experiments/evaluation generally support claims and show the method outperforming baselines (per Figure 1).  
- Missing comparison to bilingual data selection baselines (e.g., sum of differences of cross-entropies).  
- No ablation isolating contributions of BOW vs. SEQ representations or explaining which drives gains.  
- Lack of a model diagram illustrating layer connections and input combination reduces clarity.  
- Inconsistent/unnecessary citation formatting (e.g., \citet vs. \citep).  
- Unclear validation procedure for selection size: unspecified validation data source, domain, and size.  
- Unclear how CNN hyperparameters were chosen and how sensitive results are to these choices.  
- Table 2 should compare methods with the same number of selected sentences for a fair comparison.  
- Request experiments with more in-domain data and qualitative examples of sentences selected by different methods.  
- Suggest adding Axelrod (2015) POS-abstraction baseline and citing Lewis-Moore-style bilingual selection; propose learning weighted source/target scores via a bilingual CNN.  
- Overall: well-written paper with clear method and supporting results, but needs comparability fixes, clearer validation/hyperparameter reporting, BOW/SEQ analysis, bilingual baseline comparison, and improved figures/examples.
