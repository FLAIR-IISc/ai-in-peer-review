- Proposes a CNN-based classifier for scoring/ranking general-domain sentences for MT training-data selection; comparisons to monolingual LM baselines are generally solid.  
- Missing comparison to bilingual data-selection baselines (e.g., Moore–Lewis style sum of source+target cross-entropy differences).  
- CNN choice is argued to capture informative subsequences, but initial motivation was weak and needs empirical support.  
- Paper does not disentangle contributions of BOW vs. SEQ representations (or their combination); an ablation is needed.  
- Model description (one-hot vs. pre-trained embeddings) is clear, but an architectural schematic would improve readability.  
- Writing/presentation are good, but citation style inconsistencies (\citet vs. \citep) should be standardized.  
- Unclear validation protocol: need to specify validation data for selection size and hyperparameter tuning and report sensitivity to choices to avoid test leakage.  
- Fixed-budget comparisons are missing; Table 2 should report results under identical numbers of selected sentences.  
- Must add explicit comparison/discussion of bilingual selection methods and cite relevant literature around line 435.  
- Include ablations isolating BOW vs. SEQ and with/without pre-trained embeddings to attribute gains to architecture vs. embeddings.  
- Empirically compare against alternative sequence models (lightweight RNN/LSTM or pooled bag-of-ngrams) to justify CNN inductive bias.  
- Evaluate robustness across data regimes by testing with more in-domain data than just the dev set.  
- Add qualitative examples of sentences selected by different methods to make advantages concrete.  
- Test abstraction baselines (e.g., POS-tagging per Axelrod 2015) to see if embeddings outperform surface-form abstraction.  
- Consider learning source/target combination weights or extending to a bilingual/parallel CNN instead of uniform summation.  
- Minor actionable items: add an architectural figure, standardize citation formatting, and clarify/report the selection-size procedure consistently in tables.  
- Strengths: clear task formulation, reasonable CNN motivation for capturing spans, solid monolingual LM comparisons, clear model description, promising results.  
- Weaknesses: ambiguous validation/hyperparameter protocol, lack of fixed-budget comparisons, missing key ablations and bilingual baselines, limited qualitative analysis.  
- Recommendation: promising paper that would be significantly improved by fixing experimental design, adding ablations and bilingual comparisons, and including a schematic and qualitative examples.
