- Proposes a semi-supervised CNN for in-domain SMT data selection using tiny labeled in-domain sets, unlabeled region embeddings, and scoring to pick top-N sentence pairs.
- Evaluated across four translation directions and three social-media domains, outperforming LM-based selection by ~0.5 BLEU on average and robust with very small in-domain data.
- Shows effectiveness down to 100 in-domain sentence pairs in a zh→en experiment.

- Contribution: practical semi-supervised CNN framework that improves over strong LM-based selection and is robust with scarce in-domain data.
- Contribution: empirical finding that adaptation can work with as few as 100 in-domain sentence pairs (shown for zh→en).
- Contribution: multi-direction social-media evaluation showing 2.5–10% selected data can outperform training on all data.

- Strength: Maintains robust gains even with extremely small labeled in-domain sets where LM-based methods falter.
- Strength: Compared against strong, established LM-based baselines in realistic settings.
- Strength: Small selected subsets (2.5–10%) yield better BLEU than training on all data, implying efficiency.
- Strength: Clear, reproducible strategy to score and sum source+target for selection improves performance.

- Weakness: Headline +3.1 BLEU depends on zh→en overlap in the general pool; in-domain-only training already gives +2.8 BLEU, weakening the generality of the claim.
- Weakness: 100-sentence experiment is only on zh→en (the overlapping case); unclear if result generalizes to other directions.
- Weakness: Missing architectural and training details (how one-hot and embeddings combine, loss function, optimization).
- Weakness: Social-media preprocessing (handling OOVs, usernames, hashtags, emoticons, spelling normalization) is not described.
- Weakness: No SMT tuning procedure details (MERT/MIRA/PRO, tuning set, iterations) for reproducibility.
- Weakness: Limited qualitative analysis and examples of selected sentences and translation improvements.

- Question: Provide results for a standard in-/out-domain interpolation baseline to contextualize the +3.1 BLEU zh→en claim?
- Question: Will you run the 100 in-domain sentence experiment for the other translation directions or justify why zh→en generalizes?
- Question: Clarify the exact CNN architecture: combination of wl(x) and ul(x), number of conv layers, pooling, loss function, and optimizer?
- Question: What SMT tuning procedure was used (algorithm, tuning set, iterations, stopping criteria)?
- Question: What preprocessing was applied to social-media text (tokenization, normalization of usernames/hashtags/URLs, emoji handling, spelling normalization, subword segmentation)?
- Question: Can you include example selected sentences and corresponding translation improvements to illustrate the nature of the gains?
