- Proposes MT training-data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN; comparison to LM-based baselines is solid, but unclear if bilingual selection (e.g., sum of cross-entropy differences) is compared.

- Initial motivation for CNN over RNN/LSTM is unclear, though paper argues CNNs capture informative sentence regions.

- No empirical analysis showing whether BOW, SEQ, or their combination is more important, nor explanation for their relative contributions.

- Descriptions of CNN variants (one-hot vs. semi-supervised with pre-trained embeddings) are clear and detailed.

- A figure illustrating the CNN architecture and how inputs are combined would greatly improve clarity.

- Writing is generally good, but some parenthetical citations are unnecessary; prefer \citet to \citep in places (e.g., line 385).

- Experimental setup concern: selection size is chosen via a separate validation set—unclear what validation data and which data tune CNN hyperparameters; sensitivity to these choices is not analyzed.

- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences; Figure 1 suggests the proposed method likely still wins under that control.

- Request experiments with more in-domain data (not just the development set) to test robustness.

- Request showing example sentences selected by different methods to substantiate claims in Section 5.4.

- Suggest adding Axelrod (2015) POS-abstraction baseline to test whether word2vec-style embeddings provide additional advantage over surface-form abstraction.

- Note that summing source and target classification scores is similar to Moore–Lewis LM selection (sum of cross-entropy differences); add citation/discussion around line 435.

- Suggest learning weights for source vs. target classification scores by extending the CNN to a bilingual/parallel setting, which may improve selection quality.
