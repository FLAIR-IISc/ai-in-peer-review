- Method summary: CNN-based in-domain data selection following Johnson and Zhang (2015) yields ~0.5 BLEU gain over LM-based selection and is reported to be robust with very small in-domain data.

- BLEU-claim discrepancy: the reported 3.1 BLEU improvement appears to depend on including in-domain data in training; training only on in-domain data already gives +2.8 BLEU.

- Missing baseline: need a comparison to interpolating separate in-domain and out-of-domain models (or other simple baselines) to contextualize gains.

- Robustness claim limited: the strong result for very small in-domain data is shown mainly on zh→en where in-domain training data exist, so generality to tasks without in-domain training data is unclear.

- Suggested experiment: report results for all tasks when training on only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to validate the small-data claim.

- Social-media specifics: social media has high OOV rates and special tokens (usernames, hashtags, emoticons); clarify whether any special preprocessing/filtering was applied.

- Impact analysis needed: data selection alone cannot fix OOVs—show what kinds of improvements adaptation via selection produces, ideally with illustrative translation examples.

- Model details requested: clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined in the CNN (a diagram would help).

- Training objective: specify what the “designated loss function” is.

- Tuning details: state how system weights were tuned in Section 5.2.
