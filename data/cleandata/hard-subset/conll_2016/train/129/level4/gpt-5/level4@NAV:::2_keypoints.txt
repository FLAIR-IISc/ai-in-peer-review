- Paper proposes a CNN classifier for in-domain data selection (following Johnson & Zhang 2015) achieving ~0.5 BLEU over LM-based selection and robustness with very small in-domain sets.  
- Reported 3.1 BLEU gain largely depends on including in-domain data in training; training only on in-domain already gives +2.8 BLEU.  
- Compare against an interpolated system that combines separate in-domain and out-domain models to better contextualize gains.  
- Second experiment shows CNN effectiveness with limited in-domain data but is only on zh→en (which has in-domain data); unclear if this generalizes to tasks without in-domain data.  
- Recommend reporting results for all tasks when using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs to strengthen the claim.  
- Ask whether any special preprocessing/filtering was applied for social-media text (OOVs, nonstandard spellings, usernames, hashtags, emoticons).  
- Since data selection doesn’t fix OOVs, provide concrete examples or analysis of what types of improvements adaptation yields.  
- Clarify model details in Section 3.2: how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined and specify the “designated loss function”; include an architecture schematic.  
- In Section 5.2, state how system weights were tuned.
