- Proposes in-domain data selection for SMT using a CNN classifier (Johnson & Zhang framework) and shows consistent improvements over LM-based selection (~+0.5 BLEU).
- Method appears robust with very small in-domain supervision and targets noisy social-media text, a practically important domain.

Strengths:
- Simple yet effective application of CNNs for data selection.
- Consistent BLEU improvements over LM-based selection across conditions.
- Notable robustness in low-data supervision scenarios.
- Timely focus on social-media text for real-world MT.

Main concerns and suggestions:
- Clarify the claimed +3.1 BLEU: separate gains from selected out-of-domain data versus in-domain-only training and report net contribution over (i) all data and (ii) in-domain only.
- Add comparison to standard model interpolation (separate in-/out-domain LMs/TMs with tuned mixture weights) as a strong baseline.
- Small-data experiment generalization: replicate limited-supervision experiments for en→es and en→zh where in-domain sentences are absent, and test with very small dev sets to confirm robustness.
- Analysis of improvements: detail preprocessing/normalization (tokenization, masking, hashtag/emoticon handling, spelling normalization, etc.) and provide qualitative examples/error analysis plus OOV stats before/after selection.
- Report statistical significance (e.g., paired bootstrap) and variance (mean/std across runs or seeds), especially in small-data settings.

Clarity and reproducibility:
- Clarify how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined (concatenation, post-convolution, gating).
- Provide an architecture diagram and full model specs: embedding dims/initialization, convolution widths/filters, pooling, FC layers, activations, regularization.
- Specify loss function, optimizer, learning rate schedule, batch size, epochs/early stopping, and any class balancing.
- State whether classifier uses source-only, target-only, or bilingual features and how sentence pairs are represented for scoring.
- Detail data/selection protocol: sizes of in-domain supervision, candidate pool, selection budget, and how selected data are integrated (replace/augment/weight; iterative or not).
- Describe SMT tuning: method (MERT/PRO/MIRA), tuning set size/domain, iterations, and whether re-tuning occurred after selection.
- Reporting: include baselines for in-domain only, out-of-domain only, all data, and interpolated models, plus selection precision/recall proxies (average domain score of selected vs nonselected).

Overall assessment:
- Promising, practically useful approach with notable gains, but needs disentangling selection effects, interpolation comparison, broader small-supervision validation, and clearer methodological details and analysis to be fully convincing.
