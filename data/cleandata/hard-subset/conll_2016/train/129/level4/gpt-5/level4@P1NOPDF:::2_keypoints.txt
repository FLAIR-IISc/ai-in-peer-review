- Presents an in-domain data selection method for SMT using a CNN classifier (based on Johnson & Zhang 2015) that gives ~+0.5 BLEU over LM-based selection and stays robust with very little in-domain data.  
- Claims an overall improvement of 3.1 BLEU, but those large gains appear only when the training set already contains in-domain data.  
- Notes that training solely on the in-domain subset already yields +2.8 BLEU, so the 3.1 BLEU claim is potentially misleading.  
- Recommends comparing to an interpolated system combining separate in-domain and out-of-domain models.  
- Finds the second experiment (CNN effective with very limited in-domain data) the most compelling, but it’s reported only for zh→en where in-domain data exists.  
- Asks whether the limited-data robustness holds for other tasks without in-domain training data and suggests reporting results for all tasks using only 100 in-domain sentence pairs.  
- Points out social-media translation challenges (high OOVs, nonstandard spelling, usernames/hashtags/emoticons) and asks whether any special preprocessing/filtering was applied.  
- Notes that data selection does not directly fix OOVs and requests qualitative examples or analysis showing what kinds of improvements adaptation via data selection produces.  
- Section 3.2: asks for clarification on how word embeddings, segment vectors, and one-hot vectors are combined—recommends an architectural diagram.  
- Section 3.2: asks what the “designated loss function” was.  
- Section 5.2: requests a statement of how the system weights were tuned.
