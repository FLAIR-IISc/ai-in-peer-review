- Proposes MT training data selection by scoring/ranking general-domain sentences with a CNN classifier.  
- Solid comparison to LM-based baselines, but unclear if bilingual/parallel selection baselines (e.g., Moore–Lewis/Axelrod) were included.  
- Initial choice of CNN over RNN/LSTM was under-motivated, though authors argue CNNs capture informative local spans.  
- No ablation study to disentangle contributions of BOW vs. SEQ (or their combination).  
- Textual description of CNN and embedding choices is clear, but adding an architecture figure would improve accessibility.  
- Paper is generally well written, though some citation style issues (e.g., \citet vs. \citep).  
- Experimental claims supported, but choice of number of in-domain sentences (selected via validation) is insufficiently described.  
- Unclear what validation data was used and on which data CNN hyperparameters were tuned; sensitivity to hyperparameters not analyzed.  
- Table 2 should compare methods using the same number of selected sentences for fair comparison.  
- Suggest evaluating scalability/robustness with more in-domain data beyond the development set.  
- Request to include example sentences selected by different methods for qualitative support of claims.  
- Suggest a baseline that reduces LM sparsity (e.g., POS replacement) to test whether embeddings add benefits beyond surface-form abstraction.  
- Combining source and target scores by summation parallels bilingual Moore–Lewis; cite that and consider learning weights or jointly extending the CNN to bilingual setting.  
- Recommended improvements: clarify inclusion of bilingual baselines, add BOW/SEQ ablations, detail validation/hyperparameter selection and sensitivity, match selection sizes in comparisons, and provide qualitative examples.
