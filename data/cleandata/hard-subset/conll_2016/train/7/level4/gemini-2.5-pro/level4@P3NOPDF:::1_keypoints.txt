- Proposes a novel, much faster dimensionality-reduction method than neural-network approaches, motivated by preserving PMI-based distributional properties.
- Strength: high computational efficiency for training compared to popular neural embedding models.
- Strength: method has theoretical grounding in preserving specific properties of the original distribution.
- Major weakness: no direct empirical comparison to existing dimensionality reduction or word-embedding baselines.
- Major weakness: lack of quantitative results makes practical utility and relative performance impossible to assess.
- Major weakness: unclear motivation and justification for requiring vectors to be transformable with PPMI.
- Methodological concern: evaluation uses unconventional datasets and omits standard semantic benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999).
- Methodological concern: inconsistent use of correlation metrics—optimizes a non-standard Kendall’s tau but evaluates with Spearman’s rho without justification.
- Detailed comment: the paper’s definition of Kendall’s tau appears non-standard and needs citation or explanation.
- Detailed comment: authors should explain why Spearman’s rank correlation wasn’t used during optimization.
- Detailed comment: Figure 1 caption mentions three methods but the plot shows only two lines—needs correction/clarification.
- Typo: "large extend" should be corrected to "large extent."
