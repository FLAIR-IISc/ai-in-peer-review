- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP): a random projection with non-negative entries (E(R) > 0) that enables applying PPMI in low-dimensional space.  
- Two-stage pipeline: efficient dimensionality reduction via PoP followed by fast PPMI weighting to get high-quality embeddings.  
- Evaluated on the MEN semantic relatedness task, achieving competitive performance (Spearman ≈ 0.75).  
- Contribution: makes PPMI computation practical by performing it in a low-dimensional projected space, reducing time and memory costs.  
- Contribution: proposes a novel non-negative random projection scheme argued to permit PPMI where zero-mean projections do not.  
- Strength: offers strong efficiency and scalability compared to applying PPMI on full co-occurrence matrices.  
- Strength: demonstrates strong empirical results on the MEN dataset, competitive with heavier neural methods.  
- Strength: novel insight linking the projection matrix’s expected value to the feasibility of PPMI weighting.  
- Weakness: exposition lacks clarity and is not self-contained; key claims (why E(R)=0 prevents PPMI) are under-explained.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation—results reported on a single dataset (MEN), so generalizability to other benchmarks is unclear.  
- Weakness: insufficient theoretical grounding—no formal analysis (error bounds or preserved geometry) of PoP’s properties.  
- Question: please clarify with a concrete example why zero-mean random projections make PPMI infeasible (e.g., why projected column sums become zero).  
- Question: why were other benchmarks (SimLex-999, WordSim, analogies) not included, and how might performance or optimal hyperparameters change across tasks?  
- Question: explain why increasing dimensionality m beyond ~2000 can reduce unweighted PoP performance—does this indicate a fundamental trade-off or instability?
