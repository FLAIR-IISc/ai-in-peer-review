- Introduces PoP, a random-projection-based word embedding with a positive-expected-value projection matrix, claimed to enable PPMI and offer computational efficiency.
- Primary reservation: the paper lacks clarity overall.
- Does not clearly explain the core technical difference between PoP and standard random projections or substantiate the PPMI claim.
- Argumentation is difficult to follow from the introduction onward.
- Several claims are unsupported and logical links (e.g., "in addition," "since," "thus") are unjustified in the text.
- Assumes specialized knowledge in random projections, making the motivation and logic inaccessible to general vector-space model readers.
- Contains numerous grammatical and stylistic errors that hurt readability.
- Experimental evaluation is weak, relying only on the MEN dataset and lacking comparisons across standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999) or justification for the limited evaluation.
- Recommendation: substantially improve presentation and broaden/strengthen experimental methodology before resubmission.
