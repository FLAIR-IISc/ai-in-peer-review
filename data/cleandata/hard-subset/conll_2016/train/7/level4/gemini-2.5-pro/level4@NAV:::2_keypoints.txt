- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection with positive expected value claiming compatibility with PPMI and retained computational efficiency.  
- Main weakness: overall lack of clarity prevents proper assessment of contributions.  
- Fails to clearly distinguish PoP from existing random projection methods, leaving novelty and claimed benefits uncertain.  
- Poor logical flow throughout the paper, beginning with the introduction, making the argumentation hard to follow.  
- Several key arguments are unsubstantiated and contain logical leaps (examples cited at lines 114, 137, 154).  
- Lacks sufficient background and motivation; assumes specialized knowledge of random projections instead of being self-contained.  
- Numerous grammatical and stylistic errors that harm readability and professionalism.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient—only evaluated on the MEN dataset with no justification and missing standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999).  
- Recommendation: reject in current form and resubmit after substantial revisions to clarity, argumentation, and experimental rigor.
