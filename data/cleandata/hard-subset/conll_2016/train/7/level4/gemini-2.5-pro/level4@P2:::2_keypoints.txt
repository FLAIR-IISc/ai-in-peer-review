- Introduces PoP: a non-zero-mean, positive-only random projection enabling PPMI on low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Claims PoP+PPMI yields efficient, high-performing embeddings comparable to state-of-the-art on MEN.  
- Contribution: novel random projection scheme with positive expected value to allow downstream weighting.  
- Contribution: provides a computationally inexpensive alternative to neural/SVD methods for producing embeddings.  
- Strength: high computational efficiency by building vectors directly in low-dimensional space and applying PPMI there.  
- Strength: strong empirical result on MEN (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.75–0.78), competitive with more costly methods.  
- Strength: novel combination of random projections and PPMI, opening avenues for other low-dimensional transformations.  
- Weakness: key technical claim that RI makes PPMI inapplicable (row/column sums = 0) is under-explained and unproven.  
- Weakness: evaluation is narrow—only tested on MEN—so generalizability to other similarity/analogy tasks is unclear.  
- Weakness: presentation issues and unclear argumentation, plus grammatical errors that hurt readability.  
- Question: please provide a formal proof/derivation that RI-constructed models have zero row/column sums.  
- Question: please justify evaluating solely on MEN or provide results on other benchmarks (SimLex, WordSim, analogies).  
- Question: does the effectiveness stem from enabling PPMI (and subsequent Gaussian-like normalization) rather than PoP itself?
