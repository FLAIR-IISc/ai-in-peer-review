- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method using matrices with positive expected value to build low-dimensional word vectors.  
- Claims PoP allows subsequent PPMI weighting because PoP vectors have non-zero sums, aiming to combine random-projection efficiency with PPMI performance.  
- Contribution: proposes an incremental, computationally efficient technique for creating word embeddings.  
- Strength: addresses a relevant problem—fast, high-quality semantic representations—and is well-motivated.  
- Strength: conceptually interesting idea to design random projections compatible with PPMI as an alternative to expensive SVD or neural methods.  
- Weakness: core method and novelty are unclear; paper fails to explain why E(R) > 0 fundamentally enables PPMI or how PoP differs from prior random-projection methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical evaluation—results reported only on the MEN dataset rather than a diverse benchmark suite (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999, downstream tasks).  
- Weakness: contains unsupported assertions and logical leaps (e.g., claim that E(R)=0 prevents PPMI, broad claims about incremental efficiency, and runtime statements without evidence).  
- Weakness: poor writing and frequent grammatical errors reduce clarity and accessibility.  
- Recommendation: cannot recommend acceptance; needs major revisions to clarify technical claims, expand experiments, and improve presentation.
