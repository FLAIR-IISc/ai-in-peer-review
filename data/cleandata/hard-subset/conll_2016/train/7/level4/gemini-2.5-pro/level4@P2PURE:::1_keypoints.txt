- Motivation partly convincing: training speed is an advantage and it preserves some distributional properties, but criticality of transforming vectors with PPMI is not justified.
- Major weakness: no direct empirical (quantitative) comparison to related work or state-of-the-art methods.
- Kendall’s tau definition appears non-standard and its origin should be clarified or replaced with the standard formulation.
- Authors must justify using Kendall’s tau over Spearman’s rank correlation, especially since Spearman is more common and used in the evaluation.
- Evaluation uses non-standard datasets; authors should also report results on widely used benchmarks for semantic relatedness to enable direct comparison.
- Figure 1 seems to be missing a line (caption/text refers to three results but only two are shown).
- Paper offers qualitative statements about related work comparisons but lacks quantitative experiments against baselines.
- Typo: “large extend” should be “large extent.”
