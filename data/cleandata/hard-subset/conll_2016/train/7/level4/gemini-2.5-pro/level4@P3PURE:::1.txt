The motivation for the proposed method is partially compelling. In particular, the significant speed-up in training time compared to neural network-based approaches is a notable advantage. The method's ability to preserve certain distributional properties during dimensionality reduction is also a valuable quality.

However, the paper does not adequately justify why the ability for vectors to be transformable with PPMI is a critical property. Most importantly, the work is weakened by a lack of direct empirical comparison to related work, which makes it difficult to assess the practical contributions of the proposed method.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   p.3: The definition of Kendall's tau used by the authors is unconventional. This does not appear to be the standard formula, and its origin is not cited. The authors should clarify this definition and justify its use over the original.
*   p.3: Given that Spearman's correlation is a standard metric for semantic tasks (and is indeed used by the authors at evaluation time), it is unclear why the non-standard Kendall's tau was used instead for this part of the analysis.
*   The datasets chosen for evaluation are not the standard benchmarks for semantic relatedness typically used in the NLP community. While the inclusion of different datasets can be informative, the authors are strongly encouraged to also report results on standard benchmarks to allow for a direct comparison with prior work.
*   Figure 1 appears to be incomplete. The text seems to refer to three lines, but only two are visible in the plot.
*   The paper lacks a direct, quantitative comparison to related work. The claims would be much stronger if supported by empirical results against established baselines, rather than general statements.
