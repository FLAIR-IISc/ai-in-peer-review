- Introduces PoP, a positive-expected-value random projection for word embeddings claiming PPMI compatibility and preserved computational efficiency.  
- Unclear contribution: paper does not clearly distinguish PoP from existing random projection methods or establish novelty.  
- Difficult exposition: weak motivation and poor logical flow make the method hard to follow.  
- Unsupported arguments: several claims are presented without sufficient justification or logical support.  
- Not self-contained: assumes specialized knowledge and lacks necessary background explanations.  
- Language and presentation problems: numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasings hamper readability.  
- Weak experimental evaluation: evaluated only on the MEN dataset, which is insufficient for a new embedding method.  
- No justification for limited evaluation scope or absence of standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999).  
- Recommendation: cannot accept; requires substantial revision to clarify contributions, improve presentation, and broaden experiments.
