- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel random projection using non-negative projection matrices to allow post-projection weighting.  
- Claims PoP enables applying PPMI after dimensionality reduction, addressing a limitation of standard random indexing.  
- Presents an efficient, incremental pipeline combining PoP with PPMI as a fast alternative to SVD and neural embeddings.  

- Computationally efficient and scalable; models can be built in minutes versus hours/days for alternatives.  
- Simple, elegant technical idea that permits traditional count-based weighting in reduced space.  
- Strong empirical performance on MEN relatedness (average ρ = 0.75, max 0.787) showing competitive embeddings.  

- Lacks direct, controlled comparisons to key baselines such as PPMI+SVD and neural embeddings (word2vec/GloVe) on the same data.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single task/dataset (MEN); broader benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, analogy tasks) are missing.  
- Justification for using Kendall’s τb internally (vs. Spearman’s ρ or cosine) is weak, and the claim that PPMI yields Gaussian-like distributions needs more evidence.  

- How does PoP+PPMI perform versus PPMI+SVD in direct head-to-head experiments on the same corpus?  
- Why choose Kendall’s τb for PoP similarity, and what advantages does it have over Spearman’s ρ or cosine similarity?  
- Have you evaluated PoP embeddings on other standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, analogy datasets)?  

- Figure 1 legend lists three methods but only two lines are plotted—please clarify.  
- Minor typo: "large extend" should be "large extent".
