The motivation for this work is partially compelling. The proposed method offers a significant training speed advantage over neural network approaches and is designed to preserve key distributional properties during dimensionality reduction.

However, the paper's central claims are not fully substantiated. The motivation for why vectors should be transformable via PPMI is not clearly articulated. Most importantly, the experimental section lacks a direct comparison with relevant prior work, which significantly weakens the paper's conclusions.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   **p.3, Kendall's Tau:** The authors present an unconventional definition of Kendall's tau. This does not appear to be the standard formula, and its origin is unclear. The authors should clarify where this definition comes from and justify its use over the standard version.

*   **p.3, Choice of Correlation Metric:** It is unclear why the authors use this version of Kendall's tau instead of the more standard Spearman's rank correlation coefficient, which is widely used for semantic tasks. This choice is particularly puzzling given that the authors themselves use Spearman's correlation in their final evaluation. An explanation for this inconsistency is needed.

*   **Evaluation Datasets:** The datasets selected for evaluation are not the standard benchmarks for semantic relatedness (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999). While the exploration of new datasets is appreciated, including results on these standard benchmarks is crucial for contextualizing the performance of the proposed method and enabling direct comparison with existing literature.

*   **Figure 1:** The figure appears to be incomplete. The caption refers to three methods, but only two lines are visible in the plot. Please clarify this discrepancy.

*   **Comparison to Related Work:** The paper lacks a direct empirical comparison to related work. The authors state that their method is competitive, but this claim is not substantiated with comparative results against any baseline or state-of-the-art methods in the experimental section. This is a major omission that makes it difficult to assess the practical value of the proposed method.
