- Summary: Introduces Positive-Only Projections (PoP), an incremental random-projection method with E(R) > 0 that enables post-projection PPMI weighting to produce low-dimensional word embeddings, evaluated on MEN.
- Contribution 1: Proposes PoP, a random projection scheme that yields dense vectors compatible with PPMI weighting—unlike traditional zero-mean Random Indexing.
- Contribution 2: Presents an incremental, computationally efficient pipeline that avoids costly neural training or SVD by operating directly in low-dimensional space.
- Strength 1: Highly computationally efficient and scalable compared to neural embedding training or SVD on large corpora.
- Strength 2: Novel and technically sound combination of random projections and PPMI via the positive-only projection insight.
- Strength 3: Empirical results on MEN (PoP+PPMI+Pearson) are strong (ρ ≈ 0.75), supporting the method’s effectiveness in that setup.
- Weakness 1: No direct empirical comparisons to standard strong baselines (word2vec, GloVe, SVD+PPMI) on the same corpus/evaluation.
- Weakness 2: Insufficient motivation for why PPMI compatibility is a crucial goal given competitive predictive models that do not require PPMI.
- Weakness 3: Evaluation is limited to one dataset (MEN); broader benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex, analogies) are needed to assess generality.
- Question 1: The Kendall’s τb definition in Section 2.2 seems nonstandard—please clarify the source or correct a possible typo.
- Question 2: Why use Kendall’s τb for unweighted PoP similarity when Spearman’s ρ is used for final evaluation, and were alternative measures tested?
- Question 3: Figure 1’s caption mentions three models but the plot appears to show only two distinct lines—please clarify which line represents which model.
