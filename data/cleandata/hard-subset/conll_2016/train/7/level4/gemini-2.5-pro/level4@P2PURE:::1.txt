The motivation for the proposed method is partially convincing. Its training speed is a clear advantage over neural network-based approaches, and it effectively preserves certain distributional properties during dimensionality reduction. However, the paper does not sufficiently justify why the ability to transform vectors with PPMI is a critical property.

Most importantly, a major weakness of the paper is the lack of any direct empirical comparison to related work.

Detailed Comments:
- p.3: The definition of Kendall's tau provided appears to be non-standard. It does not match the original formulation, and its origin is unclear. The authors should clarify this or use the standard definition.
- p.3: The authors should justify the choice of Kendall's tau over Spearman's rank correlation, especially since Spearman's is more common for semantic tasks and is, in fact, used by the authors during evaluation.
- The evaluation is conducted on datasets that are not standard benchmarks for semantic relatedness. While experimenting with new datasets can be valuable, the authors should also report results on widely-used, standard datasets to allow for a direct comparison with prior work.
- Figure 1 appears to be missing a line. The caption or text seems to refer to three results, but only two lines are visible in the plot.
- The paper lacks a direct empirical comparison to related work, offering only a qualitative statement instead of quantitative results against state-of-the-art methods.

Typos:
