- Summary: Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), an incremental random-projection method with positive expected values that preserves count-space properties to allow post-hoc weighting (PPMI); evaluated on MEN with claimed competitive performance and low cost.  
- Contribution 1: Proposes PoP, a fast projection that enables PPMI weighting by using a positive-only projection matrix, avoiding training or SVD.  
- Contribution 2: Empirically shows a simple PoP+PPMI pipeline can bridge efficient count-based models and high-performing predictive models.  
- Strength: Computational efficiency and scalability—builds embeddings directly in low dimensions without iterative training or full matrix factorization.  
- Strength: Novel methodological idea—designing random projections to permit subsequent PPMI weighting merges RI efficiency with PPMI discriminative power.  
- Strength: Thorough internal analysis—ablation of dimensionality and sparsity parameters (Figures 4–5) provides practical guidance.  
- Weakness: No direct comparisons with state-of-the-art baselines (word2vec, GloVe, PPMI+SVD) trained on the same UKWaC corpus, undermining competitiveness claims.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—results reported only on MEN; lacks broader benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999) to assess generality.  
- Weakness: Non-standard/unclear methodological choices—Kendall’s τb formula appears non-standard and uncited.  
- Weakness: Unclear rationale for using Kendall’s τb internally but Spearman’s ρ for final evaluation.  
- Weakness: Figure 1 presentation unclear—described as three lines but appears to show two, reducing interpretability.  
- Question 1: Please add a comparison table of PoP+PPMI vs established baselines (word2vec, GloVe, PPMI+SVD) trained on the same UKWaC and evaluated on MEN.
