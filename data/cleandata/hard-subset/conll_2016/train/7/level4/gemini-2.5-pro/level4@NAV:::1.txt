I appreciate certain aspects of the motivation presented; the proposed method offers a significant reduction in training time compared to neural network approaches. Furthermore, its ability to preserve certain distributional properties during dimensionality reduction is a notable advantage.
However, the manuscript does not sufficiently justify why the ability for vectors to be transformed with PPMI is a critical property.
More significantly, a direct empirical comparison to related work is absent from the paper.

Detailed comments:
- p.3: The formulation of Kendall's tau used by the authors appears non-standard. It does not match the conventional definition, and its origin should be clarified.
- p.3: Could the authors justify why Spearman correlation was not used, given that it is a standard metric for semantic tasks and is later employed in the evaluation phase?
- The datasets selected for evaluation are not the benchmarks commonly accepted by the NLP community for measuring semantic relatedness. While results on new datasets are welcome, the inclusion of standard sets is recommended to properly situate the work.
- In Figure 1, only two of the three expected lines are visible. Please clarify the location of the third line.
- The paper lacks a direct, quantitative comparison to prior art, offering only a general statement instead.

Minor corrections:
- "large extend" should be "large extent"
