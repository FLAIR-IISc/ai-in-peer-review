- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a scalable random projection that yields vectors with positive expected values to permit post-projection weighting like PPMI.  
- Contributions: proposes PoP (incremental, efficient, PPMI-amenable) and shows PoP+PPMI with Pearson achieves strong results on MEN.  
- Strength: Highly computationally efficient and scalable, enabling PPMI to be applied to low-dimensional vectors rather than the full count matrix.  
- Strength: Novel projection design (E[R] > 0) that overcomes Random Indexing’s zero-centered vectors and allows PPMI weighting.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance on MEN (Spearman’s ρ ≈ 0.75) with analysis of dimensionality and sparsity effects.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art models (e.g., word2vec, GloVe, SVD) on the same dataset/corpus.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to a single task (MEN); broader benchmark validation and stronger motivation for post-projection PPMI are needed.  
- Weakness: Some methodological choices and presentation are unclear (choice of Kendall’s τb, Figure 1’s curves not clearly visible).  
- Question: Please provide a comparison table of PoP+PPMI on MEN against published CBOW/Skip-gram/GloVe/SVD results trained on UKWaC or comparable corpora.  
- Question: Please justify using Kendall’s τb for raw PoP vectors instead of the more common Spearman’s ρ used later.  
- Question: Please cite or clarify the source/derivation of the Kendall’s τb formula presented on page 3.
