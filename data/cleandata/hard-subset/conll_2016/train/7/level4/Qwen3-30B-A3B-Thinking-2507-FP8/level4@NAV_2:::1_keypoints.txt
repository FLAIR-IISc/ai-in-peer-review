- Summary: Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with E(R)>0 that enables PPMI weighting in low-dimensional semantic spaces and achieves competitive MEN performance (0.75) with much lower compute than neural methods.
- Contribution 1: Presents a highly computationally efficient method for building semantic spaces in seconds or minutes versus hours/days for neural embeddings.
- Contribution 2: Shows PoP preserves distributional properties (unlike Gaussian RPs), enabling application of weighting techniques like PPMI.
- Contribution 3: Enables constructing low-dimensional spaces (few hundred dims) so PPMI weighting can be applied quickly, avoiding prohibitive cost on high-dimensional count models.
- Strength 1: Major practical advantage in computational efficiency for resource-constrained settings.
- Strength 2: Technical advance in making PPMI compatible with random-projection-based spaces.
- Strength 3: Empirical results are competitive (0.75 on MEN) while requiring far less computation.
- Weakness 1: No direct comparison to related RP methods, leaving novelty and relative performance unclear.
- Weakness 2: Uses a nonstandard Kendall’s τb definition without justification or literature references.
- Weakness 3: Evaluation relies on MEN rather than standard benchmarks (WordSim353, SimLex-999), limiting generalizability.
- Weakness 4: Figure 1 appears to show only two lines (count-based and RI) despite claiming three (count-based, RI, PoP), obscuring comparisons.
- Question 1: Please justify the modified Kendall’s τb definition and provide references for its use.
- Question 2: Why was MEN chosen over more standard semantic similarity benchmarks like WordSim353 or SimLex-999?
- Question 3: Provide direct empirical and conceptual comparisons to RI, RRI, ISA, RMI and clarify how PoP differs/improves.
- Question 4: Clarify and substantiate the claim that being transformable with PPMI is a critical advantage over methods that do not support PPMI.
