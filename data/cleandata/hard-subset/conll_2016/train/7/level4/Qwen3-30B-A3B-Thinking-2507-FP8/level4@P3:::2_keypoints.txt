- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), enabling PPMI weighting on random-projection embeddings by using projections with expected value > 0.  
- Demonstrates PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance (0.75 on MEN) while being far more computationally efficient than neural methods.  
- Analyzes how vector distribution affects appropriate similarity measures, recommending Kendall's τb for non-Gaussian and Pearson's r after PPMI.  

- Novel technical contribution: resolves the E(R)=0 limitation preventing PPMI application in prior random projection methods.  
- Compelling empirical results: competitive accuracy with orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains (models built in seconds/minutes).  
- Insightful analysis linking distributional properties to choice of similarity metric.  

- Poor presentation and clarity: grammatical errors, unclear explanations, and weak organization obscure key differences from prior work like RI.  
- Inadequate evaluation: tested only on MEN and lacks results on other standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim353, SimLex-999, RareWords).  
- Lacks theoretical justification: no error bounds or theoretical analysis (e.g., for δ) expected for random projection methods.  
- Unclear experimental details: parameter choices (m, s, etc.) and sensitivity analysis are insufficient for reproduction.  

- Clarify mathematically and with examples how PoP differs from RI and why E(R)>0 enables PPMI.  
- Explain why evaluation was limited to MEN and provide evidence of generalization to other similarity benchmarks.  
- Provide concrete timing measurements on standard hardware for corpora of varying sizes (e.g., 10M, 100M, 1B words).  
- Give a quantitative comparison of time and memory costs for applying PPMI to PoP spaces versus count-based models.  

- Overall: promising and interesting technical insight but recommend rejection in current form due to poor presentation, limited evaluation, and missing theoretical guarantees; reconsider after substantial revision.
