- Summary: PoP uses random projections with E(R)>0 to enable PPMI weighting, achieving MEN 0.75 with much lower computation.

- Contribution: PoP makes random projection spaces compatible with PPMI, producing discriminative semantic spaces.

- Contribution: PoP is computationally efficient, building models in seconds/minutes versus hours/days for neural methods.

- Strength: Shows RI vectors have undefined PPMI due to zero column sums while PoP yields positive sums allowing PPMI (Sec.2.3, Eq.3).

- Strength: PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves MEN 0.75 comparable to neural methods with minimal computation.

- Strength: Incremental construction with dimensionality m controls capacity; performance plateaus at m=1000, supporting scalability.

- Weakness: Novelty is underexplained—paper does not clearly differentiate PoP from RI/RMI beyond the E(R)>0 condition.

- Weakness: Several key claims (need for transformations, efficiency savings) are asserted without supporting data or quantification.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to MEN only and omits standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, RG-65), hurting generalizability.

- Question: How does the E(R)>0 distribution change mathematical properties (distortion bounds, convergence, error guarantees δ) compared to RI’s Gaussian?

- Question: Please provide MEN results for baselines (RI, count-based) and add at least one additional benchmark (e.g., WordSim-353) to contextualize improvements.
