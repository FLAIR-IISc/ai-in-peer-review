

The paper presents a method that trains significantly faster than neural networks while preserving key distributional properties during dimensionality reduction. I appreciate these aspects of the contribution. However, the necessity of transforming vectors via PPMI remains unclear. The authors do not convincingly justify why this specific transformation is critical to the approach’s success.

Most critically, the work lacks a direct comparison to relevant prior methods. A standalone statement that "related work is less effective" is insufficient without concrete performance benchmarks. This omission substantially weakens the claim of novelty and advantage.

Detailed comments:  
- Page 3: The definition of Kendall’s tau used is unconventional and diverges from the standard formulation. Its origin and rationale require clarification.  
- Page 3: Spearman correlation is the conventional metric for semantic relatedness tasks (and is used in the evaluation section); the rationale for deviating from this standard should be justified.  
- Evaluation datasets: The chosen benchmarks are not the standard semantic relatedness resources (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999). While exploring alternative datasets is valuable, results on established benchmarks are essential for meaningful comparison.  
- Figure 1: Only two lines are visible; the third line referenced in the caption is missing.  
- Typos: "large extend" → "large extent" (page 3).
