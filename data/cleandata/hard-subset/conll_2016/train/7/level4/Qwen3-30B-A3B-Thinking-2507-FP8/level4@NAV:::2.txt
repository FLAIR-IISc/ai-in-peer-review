

The paper introduces a Positive-only Projection (PoP) word embedding method, a random projection technique employing a matrix with a positive expected value. The authors contend this enables PPMI application—unfeasible with zero-mean projections—and leverages the computational efficiency inherent to random projection methods.  

My primary concern pertains to clarity:  
1. The core distinction between this method and prior random projection approaches remains unclear, making it difficult to assess whether the claimed advantages are valid.  
2. The paper's logical flow is problematic from the introduction onward.  
3. Key arguments lack sufficient support:  
   - Line 114: "in addition" initiates a sentence without contextual justification.  
   - Line 137: "Since" introduces a premise without evidence.  
   - Line 154: "thus" draws a conclusion unsupported by prior reasoning.  
4. While vector space modeling is a common area of study, the paper fails to provide a self-contained explanation of the research rationale, hindering comprehension for readers with standard field experience.  
5. The manuscript contains multiple English errors (e.g., Line 86: "To a large extend"; Line 142: "such PPMI").  

Additionally, evaluation restricted solely to the MEN benchmark is inadequate. Standard practice requires testing against multiple datasets (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex), and omitting these without justification undermines the method's validity.  

I recommend significant revisions to enhance clarity, justification, and presentation, followed by resubmission to a different venue.
