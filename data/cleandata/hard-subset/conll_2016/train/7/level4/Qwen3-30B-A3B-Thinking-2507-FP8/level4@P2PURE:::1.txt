

I find the motivation partially convincing: the proposed method trains significantly faster than neural networks while preserving certain distributional properties when reducing dimensionality. However, I remain unconvinced of the necessity for vectors to be transformable via PPMI. Most critically, the paper lacks direct comparisons to related work.  

Detailed comments:  
- p.3: The definition of Kendall's tau used by the authors is non-standard and differs from the original formula; its derivation and source are unclear.  
- p.3: Why not employ Spearman correlation, the standard metric for semantic tasks (and the metric used in evaluation)?  
- The evaluation datasets are not the standard benchmarks for semantic relatedness preferred by the NLP community. While exploring alternative datasets is acceptable, results on standard benchmarks should also be included.  
- Figure 1 shows only two lines; the third line is missing.  
- The claim that "no direct comparison to related work" is stated without evidence.

- "large extend" → "large extent"
