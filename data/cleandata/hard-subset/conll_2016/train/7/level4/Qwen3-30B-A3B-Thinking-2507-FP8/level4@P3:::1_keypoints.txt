- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with E(R) > 0 that enables PPMI weighting on projected spaces.  
- Claims efficient incremental construction of semantic spaces in seconds/minutes versus hours/days for neural methods.  
- Demonstrates PoP+PPMI achieves competitive semantic similarity results (0.75 MEN) without neural network training.

- Computational efficiency: PoP builds models much faster than neural embeddings, beneficial for resource-constrained settings.  
- PPMI compatibility: PoP’s positive-expected projections allow applying PPMI to random-projection spaces, yielding strong discriminative power.  
- Empirical validation: Thorough experiments show sensitivity to dimensionality and sparsity and report an improved 0.78 score with tuning.

- Lack of direct comparison to state-of-the-art baselines (GloVe, FastText, Levy & Goldberg PPMI) and omission of common benchmarks weakens competitiveness claims.  
- Misuse/non-standard definition of Kendall’s τb (Equation 4) and its unconventional use as a similarity measure without justification.  
- Omission of results on standard datasets like SimLex-999 and WordSim-353 limits evaluation completeness.  
- Ambiguity in Figure 1: claimed three lines but only two visible, obscuring baseline comparisons.
