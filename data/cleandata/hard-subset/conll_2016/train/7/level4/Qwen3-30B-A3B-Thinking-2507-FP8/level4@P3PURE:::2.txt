

The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method, a random projection technique employing a matrix with a positive expected value. The authors contend this enables the application of PPMI—unfeasible with a zero-mean projection—and highlight the method's computational efficiency as a random projection approach.  

My primary concern is the paper's lack of clarity. Specifically:  
1. The core distinction between this method and prior random projection techniques remains unclear, making it impossible to assess whether the claimed advantages hold.  
2. The paper's argument flow is difficult to follow from the introduction onward.  
3. Several arguments lack sufficient support:  
   - Line 114: Sentence begins with "In addition."  
   - Line 137: Sentence begins with "Since."  
   - Line 154: Sentence begins with "Thus."  
4. Despite my experience in vector space modeling, the paper fails to adequately justify the research direction for readers with moderate expertise in the field. A self-contained paper should be accessible to such readers without requiring specialized knowledge of random projections.  
5. The paper contains multiple grammatical errors (e.g., line 86: "to a large extent"; line 142: "such as PPMI").  

Furthermore, the exclusion of standard benchmarks (MEN, WordSim, SimLex) is problematic. Evaluating solely on MEN without justification is insufficient for a novel method; comprehensive comparison across established datasets is expected.  

I recommend substantial revisions to clarify the methodology, strengthen the argumentation, correct grammatical errors, and expand the empirical evaluation. The paper should be resubmitted to a different venue after these improvements.
