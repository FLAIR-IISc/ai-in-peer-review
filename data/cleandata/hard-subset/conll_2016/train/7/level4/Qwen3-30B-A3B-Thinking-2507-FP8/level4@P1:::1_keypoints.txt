- Summary: Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), using random projections with E(R)>0 to enable weighting (e.g., PPMI) and claims competitive semantic relatedness performance with much lower compute than neural methods.

Main contributions:
- PoP allows application of weighting techniques like PPMI to random-projection semantic spaces, overcoming the E(R)=0 limitation of prior methods.
- The method achieves dramatic computational efficiency, building semantic spaces in seconds/minutes versus hours/days for neural training.
- PoP attains competitive performance on relatedness tasks (0.75 average on MEN) without neural network training.

Strengths:
- Computational efficiency is convincingly demonstrated and practically significant.
- Enabling PPMI weighting on random projections is a novel and meaningful insight.
- Reported results are competitive with state-of-the-art, showing strong non-neural performance.

Weaknesses:
- Lacks direct comparisons to other random-projection methods and to state-of-the-art neural or PPMI-weighted count-based models.
- Uses a non-standard definition of Kendall's τb without justification, and choice over Spearman is unexplained.
- Evaluation omits standard datasets (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim353), limiting comparability.
- Figure 1 apparently omits the PoP line, preventing assessment of the claimed performance.

Questions to authors:
- Please clarify and justify the Kendall's τb definition used in the paper.
- Why choose Kendall's τb instead of the standard Spearman correlation for semantic similarity?
- Can you provide results on standard datasets like SimLex-999 or WordSim353?
- Please fix Figure 1 and provide the missing PoP performance data.

Minor corrections:
- "large extend" should be "large extent".
- Claim about transforming vectors into spaces discriminative for semantic similarity needs direct comparisons to other methods.
