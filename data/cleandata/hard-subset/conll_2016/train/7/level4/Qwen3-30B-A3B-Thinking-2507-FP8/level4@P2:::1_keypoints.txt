- Summary: Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), random projections with E(R)>0 enabling PPMI weighting; claims competitive MEN (0.75) performance with much lower compute than neural models.

- Contribution: Novel random projection method that permits PPMI weighting, filling a limitation of existing random-projection techniques.

- Contribution: Demonstrates PoP+PPMI attains competitive semantic similarity performance while requiring orders-of-magnitude less computation than neural approaches.

- Contribution: Provides empirical analysis of PoP behavior across different parameter settings.

- Strength: Strong, well-supported claim of computational efficiency (models built in seconds/minutes vs hours/days).

- Strength: Enabling PPMI on random projections is a genuine technical contribution combining efficiency with discriminative weighting.

- Strength: Empirical MEN results are competitive, indicating viability for semantic similarity tasks.

- Weakness: Lacks direct comparisons to other relevant methods (other random-projection variants like RRI/ISA/RMI and count-based PPMI baselines).

- Weakness: Uses a non-standard variant of Kendall’s tau without explanation or justification, undermining methodological rigor.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to MEN and omits other standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999), limiting generalizability.

- Weakness: The claimed importance of PPMI transformability is not sufficiently justified or explained.

- Question: Please provide direct comparisons of PoP+PPMI to GloVe, word2vec, fastText and on benchmarks like WordSim-353 and SimLex-999.

- Question: Why choose the presented Kendall’s tau variant over the more standard Spearman correlation, especially given Spearman is used elsewhere?

- Question: How does the ability to apply PPMI specifically benefit semantic similarity tasks, and what would be lost without it?

- Comment: Typo noted — "large extend" should be "large extent."

- Comment: The MEN score claim (0.75) lacks context to judge competitiveness with state-of-the-art.

- Comment: Assertion that PoP outperforms RI on semantic similarity is not properly substantiated with comparative results.

- Comment: Discussion of random projection theory is insufficient; lack of mathematical error-bound analysis (δ) weakens the theoretical foundation.
