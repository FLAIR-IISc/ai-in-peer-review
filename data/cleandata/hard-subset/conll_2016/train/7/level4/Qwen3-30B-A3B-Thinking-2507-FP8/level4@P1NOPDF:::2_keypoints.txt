- Proposes positive-only projection (PoP) for word embeddings using random projections with positive expected value, claiming it enables PPMI use and is computationally efficient.
- Unclear novelty: paper does not clearly distinguish PoP from prior random projection methods; claimed advantages may be restatements.
- Poor exposition: argument flow is difficult to follow and key concepts lack context and motivation.
- Unsupported claims: sentences begin with logical connectors without established premises (e.g., lines 114, 137, 154).
- Insufficient self-containment: assumes specialized knowledge of random projections not explained for typical field readers.
- Multiple grammatical errors (e.g., "To a large extend", "such PPMI") further impede understanding.
- Limited evaluation: experiments only on MEN dataset; omits standard benchmarks like WordSim-353 and SimLex-999.
- Core technical claims are interesting but unconvincing due to presentation flaws and lack of empirical support.
- Computational efficiency claim is not demonstrated convincingly or distinguished from existing approaches.
- Recommended revisions: clearly define PoP’s novelty, improve logical flow, provide self-contained technical rationale, fix grammar, and evaluate on multiple standard benchmarks.
- Final judgment: recommend rejection until fundamental issues are addressed and the paper is substantially revised.
