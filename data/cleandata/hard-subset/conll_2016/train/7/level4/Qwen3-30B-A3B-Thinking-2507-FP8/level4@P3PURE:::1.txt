

The motivation for this work is partially compelling: the proposed method trains significantly faster than neural networks while retaining key distributional properties when reducing dimensionality. However, I remain unconvinced of the necessity for vectors to be transformable via PPMI. Most critically, the paper lacks a direct comparison to relevant prior work.  

Detailed comments:  
- p.3: The definition of Kendall's tau employed by the authors is non-standard and diverges from the original formula; its derivation and source are unclear.  
- p.3: Why not adopt Spearman correlation, the standard metric for semantic tasks (and the metric used in the evaluation section)?  
- The evaluation datasets are not the standard benchmarks for semantic relatedness in NLP; while exploring alternative datasets is valuable, results on established benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999) should be included for proper context.  
- Figure 1 only displays two lines; the third line referenced in the caption or text is missing.  
- Typos: "large extend" should be "large extent".
