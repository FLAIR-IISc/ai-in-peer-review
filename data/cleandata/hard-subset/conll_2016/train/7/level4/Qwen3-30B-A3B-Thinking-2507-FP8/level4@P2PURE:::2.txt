

The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) method for word embeddings, a random projection technique employing a matrix with a positive expected value. The authors contend that this property enables the use of PPMI—a technique infeasible with standard random projections (which have an expected value of 0—and that the method's random projection nature ensures computational efficiency.  

My primary concern is the paper's lack of clarity, specifically:  
1. The core distinction between PoP and prior random projection methods is unclear, making it impossible to verify whether the claimed advantages hold.  
2. The argument flow is difficult to follow from the introduction onward.  
3. Several assertions lack support:  
   - Line 114: Sentence begins with "in addition."  
   - Line 137: Sentence begins with "Since."  
   - Line 154: Sentence begins with "thus."  
4. Despite my experience with vector space modeling, I struggled to grasp the paper's rationale for this research direction. A self-contained paper should be accessible to readers with foundational expertise in the field.  
5. The paper contains numerous English errors (e.g., Line 86: "To a large extend"; Line 142: "such PPMI").  

Additionally, the evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset alone. Given multiple standard benchmarks (MEN, WordSim, SimLex, etc.), evaluating only one dataset without justification is insufficient for a novel method.  

I recommend substantial revisions to improve presentation and justification, followed by resubmission to another conference.
