This paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method, which relies on a random projection matrix with an expected positive value. The authors argue that this approach enables the application of Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI), which is not feasible with an expected value of 0, and that the computational efficiency of their method is a result of being a random projection technique.

My primary concern with this paper is its lack of clarity, specifically in the following areas:

1. I struggled to understand the core differences between the proposed method and previous random projection methods, which made it difficult to grasp how (and whether) the advantages argued by the authors hold.

2. The paper's arguments, starting from the introduction, were challenging to follow.

3. Some of the paper's arguments lack supporting evidence:
   - Line 114: The sentence begins with "in addition" without a clear connection to the preceding context.
   - Line 137: The sentence starting with "Since" appears to be unsubstantiated.
   - Line 154: The sentence beginning with "thus" seems to be a non-sequitur.

4. Although I have experience with vector space modeling, I am not an expert in random projections and have not utilized them in my research. I found it difficult to comprehend the underlying logic of this research avenue based on the paper alone. In my opinion, a paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with some background in the field.

5. The paper contains numerous English errors (e.g., line 86: "To a large extend", line 142: "such PPMI").

Furthermore, I question the decision to evaluate the method solely on the MEN dataset. Given the existence of standard benchmarks (MEN, WordSim, SimLex, and others), I believe it is insufficient to evaluate a new method on only one dataset without providing a compelling justification.

I recommend that the authors substantially improve the presentation in the paper and consider resubmitting to another conference.
