Summary: PoP is a random-projection word embedding with a positive expected projection that enables PPMI weighting and is computationally efficient.

Contribution 1: Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel random-projection method with positive expected values for constructing semantic spaces.

Contribution 2: Claims PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient for large-scale NLP.

Contribution 3: Shows PoP can be PPMI-weighted—unlike prior random-projection models—and achieves competitive semantic similarity results.

Strength 1: Introduces a novel, efficient technique for semantic space and word-embedding construction.

Strength 2: Provides evaluation on MEN and UKWaC demonstrating effectiveness in semantic similarity.

Strength 3: Emphasizes that positive expected-value random projections permit PPMI weighting and competitive performance.

Weakness 1: Paper clarity and presentation are weak, especially in explaining differences from prior random-projection methods.

Weakness 2: Some arguments are insufficiently supported and lack explicit links between claims and evidence.

Weakness 3: Evaluation is limited (primarily MEN); needs more standard benchmarks to demonstrate generalizability.

Weakness 4: Several English language errors reduce overall quality.

Question 1: Please clarify core differences between PoP and previous random-projection methods, particularly regarding expected values and PPMI implications.

Question 2: How will you address non-orthogonal index vectors in PoP, and what are the performance implications?

Question 3: Can you provide additional evaluations on standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim, SimLex) to demonstrate generalizability?

Question 4: How will you improve presentation and clarity to make the paper more accessible to experienced readers?
