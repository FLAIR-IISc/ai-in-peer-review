The proposed paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP is based on random projections and is highly scalable and computationally efficient. The method uses a random projection matrix R with E(R) > 0, which allows for weighting methods such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to be applied to the constructed spaces.

**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions:**

The paper proposes a new method for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, called Positive-Only Projection (PoP). The main contributions of the paper are:

1. The introduction of a novel random projection technique that preserves the distribution of the original data in the lower-dimensional space.
2. The ability to apply weighting methods such as PPMI to the constructed spaces, which enhances the discriminatory power of the models.
3. The demonstration of the effectiveness of the PoP method in semantic similarity assessment tasks, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.
