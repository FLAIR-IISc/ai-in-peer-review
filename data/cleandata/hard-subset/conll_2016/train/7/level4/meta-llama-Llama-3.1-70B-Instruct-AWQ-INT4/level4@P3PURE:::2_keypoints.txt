- Paper proposes a positive-only projection (PoP) embedding using a random projection matrix with expected positive values to enable PPMI and claim computational efficiency.  
- Unclear core differences between PoP and prior random projection methods, making claimed advantages hard to assess.  
- Overall presentation and argumentation are difficult to follow from the introduction onward.  
- Several statements lack supporting evidence or logical connection (e.g., lines 114, 137, 154 flagged as non-sequiturs or unsubstantiated).  
- The paper is not self-contained for readers unfamiliar with random projections and is hard to follow even for those with vector-space modeling experience.  
- Numerous English language errors reduce clarity (examples: line 86 "To a large extend", line 142 "such PPMI").  
- Evaluation is weak—method tested only on the MEN dataset without justification or use of standard benchmarks like WordSim or SimLex.  
- Recommendation: substantially improve presentation, clarity, and empirical evaluation before resubmission.
