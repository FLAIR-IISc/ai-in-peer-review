- Introduces PoP: a new random projection method with positive expected value that enables PPMI weighting.  
- PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient, achieving competitive semantic similarity results without heavy computation.  
- Provides a new perspective on using non-Gaussian random projections for semantic space construction.  
- Evaluation is clear and well-structured, using the MEN relatedness test set and the UKWaC corpus.  
- Offers a thorough performance analysis, including parameter sensitivity and comparisons to other methods.  
- Uses Kendall’s τb correlation appropriately given the non-Gaussian distribution of projected spaces.  
- Needs clearer explanation of core differences and theoretical underpinnings compared to prior random projection methods.  
- Some claims are insufficiently supported; stronger links between evidence and assertions are needed.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset; more benchmarks are required to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Paper contains several English mistakes that detract from clarity and professionalism.  
- Requests a detailed theoretical comparison between PoP and previous random projection methods.  
- Asks how authors will address evaluation limitations and which additional datasets/benchmarks they will use.  
- Seeks more information on potential NLP applications of PoP and how it could be combined with other techniques.
