The paper presents a novel technique called positive-only projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. This method is based on random projections, making it highly scalable and computationally efficient. The authors claim that the PoP method is an improvement over previous random projection methods, as it allows for the application of weighting techniques such as positive pointwise mutual information (PPMI).

**Main Contributions:**

1. The introduction of the PoP method, which enables the application of PPMI to random projection-based models.
2. The demonstration of the efficiency and effectiveness of the PoP method in constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings.
3. The evaluation of the PoP method on the MEN relatedness test set, showing competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, which is both efficient and effective.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the PoP method, including comparisons with other methods and an analysis of the method's parameters.
3. The paper highlights the potential of the PoP method for improving the performance of distributional natural language analyses.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The paper's clarity and organization could be improved, particularly in the introduction and background sections.
2. Some of the arguments and sentences in the paper are not well-supported or clear, making it difficult to follow the logic of the research.
3. The paper's evaluation is limited to a single dataset (MEN), which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the method's generalizability.
4. The paper contains several English errors, which detract from its overall quality.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a clearer explanation of the core difference between the PoP method and previous random projection methods?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of evaluating the PoP method on multiple datasets to demonstrate its generalizability?
3. Can you provide more details on the theoretical accounts for the PoP method, which are currently lacking?

**Additional Comments:**

1. The paper's supplemental material, including code and resulting embeddings, will be useful for reproducing the results and exploring the method further.
2. The authors may consider providing more background information on random projections and their application in natural language processing to make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.
