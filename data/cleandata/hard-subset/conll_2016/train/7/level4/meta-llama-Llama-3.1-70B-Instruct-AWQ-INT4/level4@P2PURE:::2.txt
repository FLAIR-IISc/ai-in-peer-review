The paper proposes a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method, which is a random projection approach utilizing a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors argue that this method enables the application of Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI), which is not feasible with an expected value of 0, and that its computation is efficient due to its random projection nature.

My primary concern with this paper is its clarity. Specifically, I have several reservations:

1. The core difference between the proposed method and previous random projection methods is not clearly explained, making it difficult to understand how (and whether) the authors' argued advantages hold.
2. The paper's arguments, starting from the introduction, are hard to follow.
3. Some arguments are not adequately supported, such as those presented in lines 114, 137, and 154, which begin with transitional phrases ("in addition," "Since," and "thus") without providing sufficient context.
4. As someone who has worked on vector space modeling but lacks expertise in random projections, I found it challenging to grasp the underlying logic of this research avenue from the paper alone. I believe that a paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with some experience in the field.
5. The paper contains numerous English errors (e.g., "To a large extend" in line 86 and "such PPMI" in line 142), which detract from its overall clarity.

Furthermore, I question the decision to evaluate the method solely on the MEN dataset. Given the existence of multiple standard benchmarks (MEN, WordSim, SimLex, and others), it seems insufficient to assess a new method on only one dataset without providing a compelling justification.

I recommend that the authors substantially improve the paper's presentation and consider resubmitting to another conference.
