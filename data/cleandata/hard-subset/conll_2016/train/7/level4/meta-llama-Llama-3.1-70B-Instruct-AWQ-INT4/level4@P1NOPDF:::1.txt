I find some of the motivations compelling, particularly the proposed method's faster training time compared to neural networks, as well as its ability to preserve certain distribution properties when reducing dimensionality.

However, I am not entirely convinced by the emphasis on the importance of transformability with Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The justification for this requirement could be more thoroughly explained.

Most significantly, the paper lacks a direct comparison to related work, which is a notable omission.

Detailed comments:

- p.3: The definition of Kendall's tau used by the authors appears to be non-standard. The formula does not match the original, and its origin is unclear. It would be helpful to provide more context or a reference for this alternative definition.
- p.3: Given that the authors use Spearman correlation for evaluation, it is unclear why they do not employ it consistently throughout the paper. Spearman correlation is the standard metric in semantic tasks, making it a more intuitive choice.
- The datasets selected for evaluation deviate from the standard benchmarks commonly used in the NLP community to assess semantic relatedness. While exploring alternative datasets is valuable, it would be beneficial to also include results from the standard datasets for a more comprehensive comparison.
- Figure 1 appears to be missing a third line. It is possible that the line is not visible due to scaling or formatting issues, but it is essential to ensure that all relevant information is accurately represented.
- The paper lacks a direct comparison to related work, instead only stating that the proposed method is faster to train. A more thorough comparison, including both quantitative and qualitative analyses, would strengthen the paper's contributions and impact.
