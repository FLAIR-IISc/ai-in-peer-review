**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP uses random projections with a positive expected value, allowing for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors claim that PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient, making it a viable alternative to neural network-based approaches. The paper evaluates the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set and reports competitive results.

**Main Contributions**

1. The introduction of PoP, a new technique for constructing semantic spaces that allows for the application of weighting techniques such as PPMI.
2. The evaluation of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set, demonstrating competitive results with state-of-the-art approaches.
3. The proposal of a new similarity measure, Kendall's τb, for computing similarities in non-Gaussian spaces.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel and efficient technique for constructing semantic spaces, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. The evaluation of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set demonstrates its potential as a viable alternative to neural network-based approaches.
3. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the performance of PoP under different parameter settings, which is useful for understanding the behavior of the technique.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks a direct comparison to related work, which makes it difficult to evaluate the significance of the proposed technique.
2. The choice of datasets for evaluation is not standard in the NLP community, which may limit the generalizability of the results.
3. The paper contains some typos and formatting issues, which can be distracting.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed comparison of PoP with related work, such as neural network-based approaches and other dimensionality reduction techniques?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of selecting optimal parameters for PoP, such as the dimensionality of the projected index vectors and the number of non-zero elements?
3. Can you provide more information on the computational resources required to train PoP models, and how they compare to neural network-based approaches?

**Additional Comments**

* The definition of Kendall's tau used in the paper is not the original formula, and it would be helpful to provide more information on its origin and motivation.
* The use of Spearman correlation as a standard measure in semantic tasks is a good point, and it would be interesting to see how PoP performs using this measure.
* The paper would benefit from more detailed explanations of the mathematical concepts and techniques used, such as the random projection matrix and the PPMI transformation.
