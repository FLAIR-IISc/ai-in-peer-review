I am partially convinced by the motivation behind the proposed method, specifically its faster training time compared to neural networks, as well as its ability to preserve certain properties of the distribution when reducing dimensionality.

However, I remain unconvinced about the importance of vectors being transformable with Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors could strengthen their argument by providing a clearer justification for this requirement.

A major limitation of the paper is the lack of direct comparison to related work. This omission makes it challenging to assess the proposed method's performance and contributions relative to existing approaches.

Detailed comments:

- Page 3: The definition of Kendall's tau used by the authors appears to be non-standard. It would be helpful to clarify the origin and rationale behind this definition, as it deviates from the original formula. 
- Page 3: Given that the authors use Spearman correlation for evaluation, it would be more consistent to use it instead of Kendall's tau in their analysis, as it is the standard metric in semantic tasks.
- The datasets chosen for evaluation are not the standard benchmarks used by the NLP community to measure semantic relatedness. While exploring alternative datasets is valuable, I recommend including results on the standard datasets for a more comprehensive evaluation.
- Figure 1 appears to be incomplete, showing only two lines. The third line is missing, which may be a formatting issue.
- As mentioned earlier, a direct comparison to related work is necessary to contextualize the proposed method's performance and significance. A simple statement acknowledging related work is insufficient.
