I can appreciate some of the motivations behind the proposed method, particularly its ability to train faster than a neural network while preserving certain properties of the distribution when reducing dimensionality.

However, I am not entirely convinced about the importance of ensuring that vectors are transformable with Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors could strengthen their argument by providing more context or evidence to support this requirement.

A major limitation of the paper is the lack of direct comparison to related work. This omission makes it challenging to assess the proposed method's performance and contributions relative to existing approaches.

Here are some detailed comments to further improve the manuscript:

* On page 3, the definition of Kendall's tau used by the authors appears unconventional. It does not align with the original formula, and its origin is unclear. The authors should clarify the rationale behind this definition or adopt the standard formula.
* Also on page 3, the authors might consider using Spearman correlation, which is the standard metric in semantic tasks and is also employed in the evaluation section. This would enhance consistency throughout the paper.
* The datasets selected for evaluation deviate from the standard benchmarks commonly used in the NLP community to measure semantic relatedness. While exploring alternative datasets can be valuable, I recommend including results on the standard datasets for a more comprehensive evaluation.
* Figure 1 appears to be incomplete, with only two lines visible. The authors should ensure that the figure is complete and accurately represents the results.
* As mentioned earlier, the lack of direct comparison to related work is a significant omission. Instead of simply stating that the proposed method differs from existing approaches, the authors should provide a thorough comparison to demonstrate its advantages and limitations.

Additionally, I noticed a few typos that should be corrected, including "large extend" which should be replaced with "large extent."
