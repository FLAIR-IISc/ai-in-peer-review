I am partially convinced by the motivation behind the proposed method, which offers a significant advantage in terms of training speed compared to neural networks. Additionally, it preserves certain properties of the distribution when reducing dimensionality.

However, I remain unconvinced about the importance of vectors being transformable with Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). This aspect of the method requires further justification.

Most critically, the paper lacks a direct comparison to related work, which is a significant omission.

Detailed comments:

- Page 3: The definition of Kendall's tau used by the authors is unconventional and does not align with the original formula. Its origin and rationale are unclear.
- Page 3: Given the standard use of Spearman correlation in semantic tasks (including the authors' own evaluation), it is unclear why it was not used consistently throughout the paper.
- The datasets chosen for evaluation deviate from the standard benchmarks used in the NLP community to measure semantic relatedness. While exploring alternative datasets is valuable, I recommend including results on the standard datasets for a more comprehensive evaluation.
- Figure 1 appears to be incomplete, as only two lines are visible; the third line is missing.
- The paper only mentions related work in passing, without providing a direct comparison. This omission makes it difficult to assess the proposed method's contributions and advantages.

Minor corrections:
- "large extend" should be corrected to "large extent".
