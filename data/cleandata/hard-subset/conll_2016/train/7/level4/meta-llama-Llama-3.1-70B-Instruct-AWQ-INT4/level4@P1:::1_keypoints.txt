- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for constructing semantic spaces using projection matrices with positive expected value.  
- PoP enables application of weighting methods like Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to projected vectors.  
- Method is highly scalable and computationally efficient, suited for large-scale NLP tasks.  
- Evaluated on the MEN relatedness test set and UKWaC corpus, yielding competitive results versus state-of-the-art.  
- Main contribution: introduction of PoP allowing transformation of semantic spaces with PPMI.  
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that PoP achieves competitive semantic similarity performance.  
- Main contribution: provision of a fast, efficient approach for building semantic spaces.  
- Strength: strong scalability and computational efficiency for large corpora.  
- Strength: compatibility with weighting schemes (e.g., PPMI) that can boost performance.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation using a standard dataset and corpus.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparisons to related work, obscuring novelty and impact.  
- Weakness: unclear justification for why transformability with PPMI is important.  
- Weakness: use of Kendall’s tau as similarity measure is not well-justified versus alternatives like Spearman.  
- Question: please explain why vectors being transformable with PPMI matters for semantic similarity tasks.  
- Question: how does PoP compare to related methods, and what are its key differences/advantages?  
- Question: please justify the choice of Kendall’s tau and its relevance over other correlation measures.
