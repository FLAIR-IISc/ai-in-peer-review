I find the proposed method's faster training time compared to neural networks and its ability to preserve certain distribution properties when reducing dimensionality to be compelling motivations. However, I am not entirely convinced of the importance of vectors being transformable with Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI).

Most significantly, the paper lacks a direct comparison to related work, which is a crucial aspect of any research paper.

Detailed comments:

- On page 3, the authors' definition of Kendall's tau appears unconventional. It does not match the original formula, and I am unsure of its origin or basis.
- Also on page 3, I question the use of Kendall's tau when Spearman correlation is the standard metric in semantic tasks, as the authors themselves employ it during evaluation.
- The chosen datasets for evaluation deviate from the standard ones typically used to measure semantic relatedness in the NLP community. While exploring alternative datasets is welcome, I recommend including results from the standard datasets for a more comprehensive evaluation.
- Figure 1 only displays two lines; the third line is not visible.
- A minor typo: "large extend" should be corrected to "large extent".

Additionally, I suggest addressing the lack of direct comparison to related work with more than just a statement, providing concrete evidence to support the proposed method's contributions.
