The paper introduces a novel word embedding method called positive-only projection (PoP), which utilizes a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors claim that this approach enables the application of Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI), a method not feasible with traditional random projection techniques that have an expected value of zero. Moreover, they argue that their method is computationally efficient due to its random projection nature.

However, I have several major concerns regarding the paper's clarity and overall quality. Firstly, I struggled to understand the core differences between the proposed method and existing random projection techniques, which made it challenging to grasp the advantages claimed by the authors. This lack of clarity is evident in several areas, including the introduction, where the authors' arguments are difficult to follow.

Furthermore, some of the paper's arguments are not adequately supported. For instance, the sentences starting with transitional phrases on lines 114 ("in addition"), 137 ("Since"), and 154 ("thus") seem to lack clear connections to the preceding text. As someone who has worked on vector space modeling but is not an expert in random projections, I found it hard to comprehend the underlying logic of this research avenue. In my opinion, a paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with a basic understanding of the field.

Additionally, the paper contains several English errors, such as "To a large extend" (line 86) and "such PPMI" (line 142), which detract from its overall quality. I also question the decision to evaluate the proposed method solely on the MEN dataset, as there are several standard benchmarks (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex) that are commonly used in the field. Unless a compelling justification is provided, it is insufficient to evaluate a new method on only one dataset.

In light of these concerns, I recommend that the authors substantially revise the presentation of their work and resubmit it to another conference. Addressing the issues mentioned above will be essential to improving the paper's clarity, coherence, and overall impact.
