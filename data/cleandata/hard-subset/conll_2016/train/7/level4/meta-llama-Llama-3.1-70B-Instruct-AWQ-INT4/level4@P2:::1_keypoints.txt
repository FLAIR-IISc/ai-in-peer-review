- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel technique for constructing semantic spaces using random projections with a positive expected value.  
- PoP enables application of weighting techniques such as PPMI to projected vectors.  
- Claims PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient, offering an alternative to neural network-based embeddings.  
- Evaluates PoP on the MEN relatedness test set and reports competitive results.  
- Introduces PoP as a main contribution for semantic-space construction.  
- Demonstrates PoP’s performance on MEN as evidence of competitiveness with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Proposes using Kendall’s τb as a similarity measure for non-Gaussian semantic spaces.  
- Strength: novel and efficient technique that could impact NLP representation methods.  
- Strength: empirical results suggest PoP can be a viable alternative to neural approaches.  
- Strength: provides detailed analysis of performance under varying parameter settings.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparisons to related work and baseline methods.  
- Weakness: uses non-standard datasets, limiting generalizability of results.  
- Weakness: manuscript contains typos and formatting issues that distract readers.  
- Asks authors for more detailed comparisons with neural and other dimensionality-reduction methods.  
- Requests clarification on selecting optimal PoP parameters (dimensionality, number of non-zero elements).  
- Requests information on computational resources required to train PoP versus neural models.  
- Notes Kendall’s tau definition differs from the original and asks for origin/motivation.  
- Suggests reporting Spearman correlation as a standard semantic-task measure.  
- Recommends clearer mathematical explanations of the random projection matrix and the PPMI transformation.
