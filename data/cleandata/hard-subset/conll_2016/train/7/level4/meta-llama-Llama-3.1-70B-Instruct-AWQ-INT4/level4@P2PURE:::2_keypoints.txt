- Paper proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection embedding method with a positive-expected-value projection matrix enabling PPMI and claiming computational efficiency.
- Core difference from prior random projection methods is not clearly explained, making claimed advantages hard to validate.
- Overall argumentation and exposition (starting from the introduction) are hard to follow.
- Specific claims are unsupported or lack context (notably around lines 114, 137, and 154).
- Paper is not self-contained or accessible to readers familiar with vector space modeling but not expert in random projections.
- Numerous English language errors reduce clarity and readability (examples: "To a large extend", "such PPMI").
- Evaluation is inadequate—only the MEN dataset is used despite multiple standard benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex, etc.), with no justification for this choice.
- Recommendation: substantially improve presentation and clarity before resubmission.
