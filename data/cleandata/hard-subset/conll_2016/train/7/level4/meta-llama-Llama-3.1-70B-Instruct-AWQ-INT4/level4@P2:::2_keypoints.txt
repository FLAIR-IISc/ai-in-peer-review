- Presents positive-only projection (PoP), a novel, scalable random-projection-based method for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings.  
- Claims PoP combined with PPMI yields competitive semantic similarity results comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  

Contributions:
- Introduces PoP to enable application of PPMI to random-projection models, improving semantic similarity performance.  
- Demonstrates PoP’s efficiency and effectiveness for building semantic spaces usable in NLP tasks.  
- Evaluates PoP on the MEN relatedness dataset and reports competitive performance.  

Strengths:
- Novel and computationally efficient method for constructing semantic spaces.  
- Thorough evaluation on MEN showing competitiveness with top algorithms.  
- Emphasizes the importance of data distribution in semantic space construction.  

Weaknesses:
- Lacks clarity in explaining core differences from prior random projection methods.  
- Introduction and several arguments are under-supported and unclearly explained.  
- Multiple English language errors that reduce paper quality.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (MEN), raising concerns about generalizability.  

Questions to authors:
- Please clarify the core technical difference between PoP and previous random projection methods.  
- How will you address PoP’s sensitivity to noise and the requirement for careful context selection?  
- Can you provide additional evaluations on other benchmarks (e.g., WordSim, SimLex) to show generalizability?  
- How do you plan to improve presentation and clarity for readers experienced in the field?
