The paper introduces a novel word embedding method based on positive-only projection (PoP), which utilizes a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. According to the authors, this approach enables the application of Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI), a possibility not afforded by methods with an expected value of 0. Furthermore, as a random projection method, it offers efficient computation.

However, I have several concerns regarding the paper's clarity and presentation:

1. The distinction between the proposed method and previous random projection techniques remains unclear, making it challenging to evaluate the validity of the authors' claims regarding the advantages of their approach.
2. The paper's arguments, starting from the introduction, are difficult to follow.
3. Some statements lack supporting evidence:
   - Line 114: The sentence beginning with "in addition" requires further justification.
   - Line 137: The sentence starting with "Since" needs more context or explanation.
   - Line 154: The sentence beginning with "thus" is not adequately supported.
4. As someone familiar with vector space modeling but not an expert in random projections, I found it difficult to grasp the underlying logic and motivation behind this research. I believe the paper should be more self-contained and accessible to readers with some background in the field.
5. The paper contains numerous English errors (e.g., "To a large extend" on line 86 and "such PPMI" on line 142).

Additionally, I question the decision to evaluate the method solely on the MEN dataset. Typically, new methods are assessed on multiple standard benchmarks (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex). Unless the authors provide a compelling justification, evaluating their method on only one dataset seems insufficient.

To strengthen the paper, I recommend that the authors significantly improve the presentation, address the concerns mentioned above, and resubmit the revised manuscript to another conference.
