**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP is based on random projections and is highly scalable and computationally efficient. The method uses a transformation matrix with a non-zero expected value, allowing for weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to be applied to the constructed spaces. The authors evaluate their method using the MEN relatedness test set and the UKWaC corpus, achieving competitive results compared to state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Random Projection Method**: The authors propose a new random projection method, PoP, which is specifically designed to preserve the distribution of the original data when reducing dimensionality.
2. **Efficient Construction of Semantic Spaces**: PoP enables the efficient construction of semantic spaces, making it possible to apply weighting techniques such as PPMI to the constructed spaces.
3. **Competitive Results**: The authors demonstrate that their method achieves competitive results in semantic relatedness tasks, comparable to state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficiency**: The proposed method is much faster to train than neural networks, making it a promising approach for large-scale natural language processing tasks.
2. **Preservation of Distribution**: PoP preserves the distribution of the original data when reducing dimensionality, which is important for certain applications.
3. **Flexibility**: The method allows for the application of weighting techniques such as PPMI to the constructed spaces, which can enhance the performance of semantic relatedness tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: The authors do not provide a detailed mathematical account for their method, which may limit its adoption and understanding.
2. **Limited Evaluation**: The evaluation of the method is limited to a single dataset and task, which may not be representative of its performance in other scenarios.
3. **No Direct Comparison to Related Work**: The authors do not provide a direct comparison to related work, making it difficult to assess the novelty and impact of their contribution.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind using PPMI weighting in your method?
2. How do you plan to address the lack of theoretical justification for your method in future work?
3. Can you provide additional evaluation results on standard datasets and tasks in the field of semantic relatedness?

**Additional Comments**

* The definition of Kendall's tau used in the paper is not the original formula. It would be helpful to clarify the source of this definition.
* The use of Spearman correlation as a standard measure in semantic tasks is not consistent throughout the paper. It would be better to use a single measure consistently.
* The datasets chosen for evaluation are not the standard ones in the field. It would be helpful to include results on standard datasets to facilitate comparison with related work.
