- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection technique for semantic spaces and word embeddings.  
- Uses a random projection matrix R with E(R) > 0, enabling weighting schemes like PPMI on the projected space.

- Contribution: novel random projection that preserves the original data distribution in lower dimensions.  
- Contribution: supports applying weighting methods (e.g., PPMI) to enhance model discrimination.  
- Contribution: shows competitive performance on semantic similarity tasks versus state-of-the-art.

- Strength: much faster to train than neural-network-based methods, suitable for large-scale NLP.  
- Strength: preserves distributional properties of the original data in the reduced space.  
- Strength: compatibility with PPMI increases discriminatory power.

- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to related work, hindering assessment of novelty and impact.  
- Weakness: evaluation datasets are non-standard for semantic relatedness, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: paper contains typos and formatting issues that need fixing.

- Question: provide a more detailed comparison to related work, including pros/cons of PoP.  
- Question: include results on standard semantic relatedness datasets in addition to current ones.  
- Question: clarify the definition and use of Kendall's tau and its relation to the original formula.
