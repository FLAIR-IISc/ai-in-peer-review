- Introduces PoP (positive-only projection), enabling PPMI weighting in random projection-based semantic models.  
- Shows PoP is scalable and computationally efficient for building semantic spaces and embeddings.  
- Evaluates PoP on the MEN relatedness test and reports competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Novel, efficient, and effective approach to constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings.  
- Thorough evaluation including method comparisons and parameter analysis.  
- Highlights PoP’s potential to improve distributional NLP analyses.  
- Paper’s clarity and organization need improvement, especially in introduction and background.  
- Some arguments and sentences lack adequate support or clarity, hindering logical flow.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (MEN), raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Contains several English errors that detract from quality.  
- Request clearer explanation of the core difference between PoP and prior random projection methods.  
- Ask for plans to evaluate PoP on multiple datasets to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Request more theoretical justification or accounts for the PoP method.  
- Supplemental material (code and embeddings) is useful for reproducibility and further exploration.
