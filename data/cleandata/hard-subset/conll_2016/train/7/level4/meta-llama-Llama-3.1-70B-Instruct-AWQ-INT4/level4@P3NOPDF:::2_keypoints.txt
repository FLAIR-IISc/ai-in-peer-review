- Proposes PoP word embedding: a random projection method with a positive-expected projection matrix enabling application of PPMI and claimed computational efficiency.
- Major concerns about overall clarity and presentation of the paper.
- Unclear core difference from prior random projection methods, making the claimed advantages hard to assess.
- Arguments are difficult to follow and need clearer, more intuitive explanations and better logical flow.
- Several specific claims lack sufficient justification (e.g., lines 114, 137, 154).
- Paper is not self-contained for readers unfamiliar with random projections; explanation of the research logic is insufficient.
- Contains English language errors that reduce clarity (e.g., lines 86 and 142).
- Evaluation is weak—only tested on the MEN dataset; should include additional benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex, etc.) or justify limited evaluation.
- Recommendation: substantially improve presentation, address issues, and consider resubmission to another conference.
