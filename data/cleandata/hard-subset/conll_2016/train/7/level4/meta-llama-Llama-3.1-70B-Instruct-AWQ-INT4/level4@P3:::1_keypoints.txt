- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel random-projection method for building semantic spaces and embeddings.  
- PoP uses a transformation matrix with non-zero expectation, enabling weighting like PPMI and offering high scalability and computational efficiency.  
- Evaluated on MEN relatedness and UKWaC corpus, showing competitive results versus state-of-the-art.  
- Claims PoP preserves the original data distribution when reducing dimensionality.  
- Enables efficient construction of semantic spaces and the application of weighting schemes (e.g., PPMI).  
- Much faster to train than neural-network approaches, making it attractive for large-scale NLP.  
- Preservation of distribution is emphasized as important for certain applications.  
- Lacks detailed theoretical/mathematical justification for the method.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset/task, reducing confidence in generality.  
- No direct comparisons to related work, hindering assessment of novelty and impact.  
- Requests clarification on the motivation for using PPMI with PoP.  
- Asks how authors will address the theoretical justification in future work.  
- Requests additional evaluations on standard datasets and tasks for broader validation.  
- Notes the Kendall’s tau definition used differs from the original and should be clarified.  
- Recommends consistent use of Spearman correlation as the standard measure across the paper.  
- Suggests including results on standard datasets to facilitate comparison with related work.
