The paper presents a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method, which is a random projection technique that utilizes a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors argue that this approach enables the application of Pointwise Mutual Information (PMI) with positive values, a possibility not afforded by methods with an expected value of 0. Furthermore, as a random projection method, the authors claim that their computation is efficient.

My primary concern with this paper is its clarity. Specifically, I have several reservations:

1. I struggled to discern the core differences between the proposed method and previous random projection techniques. Consequently, I found it challenging to understand how (and whether) the advantages argued by the authors hold.
2. The paper's arguments, starting from the introduction, were difficult to follow.
3. Some of the paper's arguments lack support. For instance, the sentences starting with "in addition" (Line 114), "Since" (Line 137), and "thus" (Line 154) do not provide clear justifications for the claims made.
4. Although I have worked on vector space modeling, I am not an expert in random projections and have not utilized them in my research. I found it challenging to grasp the underlying logic of this research avenue from the paper alone. I believe that a paper should be self-contained and accessible to researchers with some experience in the field.
5. The paper contains numerous English errors (e.g., "To a large extend" on Line 86 and "such PPMI" on Line 142).

Additionally, I am perplexed by the authors' decision to evaluate their method solely on the MEN dataset. There are several standard benchmarks for evaluating word embeddings, including MEN, WordSim, SimLex, and others. In my opinion, it is insufficient to evaluate a new method on only one dataset without providing a compelling justification.

I recommend that the authors substantially improve the presentation in the paper and resubmit it to another conference.
