Here's a paraphrased version of the review:

I find the proposed method somewhat compelling, particularly in terms of its training efficiency, which significantly outperforms neural network training. Additionally, it preserves certain distributional properties when reducing dimensionality.

However, I remain unconvinced about the necessity of transformability with PPMI for vectors. Moreover, a direct comparison with related work is noticeably absent.

Detailed comments:

- On page 3, the definition of Kendall's tau appears unconventional and does not align with the original formula. Its origin and derivation are unclear.
- Also on page 3, it would be more standard to use Spearman correlation for semantic tasks, as is commonly practiced in the NLP community and even adopted by the authors during evaluation.
- The chosen evaluation datasets deviate from the standard benchmarks typically used to assess semantic relatedness in the NLP community. While exploring alternative datasets is commendable, I recommend supplementing the results with those from the standard datasets.
- Figure 1 only displays two lines; the third line is missing.
- A direct comparison with related work is lacking, with only a statement acknowledging its existence.

Minor errors:
- "large extend" should be corrected to "large extent".
