- Proposes a positive-only projection (PoP) random-projection word embedding using a random matrix with positive expected value.  
- Claims PoP enables applying PMI with positive values, unlike zero-mean projection methods.  
- Claims computational efficiency due to being a random projection method.  
- Primary concern: paper lacks clarity overall.  
- Reviewer could not discern core differences between PoP and prior random projection techniques.  
- Arguments throughout the paper (starting from the introduction) are difficult to follow.  
- Several specific claims are unsupported (e.g., sentences starting “in addition” (L114), “Since” (L137), and “thus” (L154)).  
- Paper is not self-contained for readers without random projection expertise; underlying logic is hard to grasp.  
- Contains numerous English errors (examples: “To a large extend” L86, “such PPMI” L142).  
- Evaluation is inadequate: method tested only on MEN despite available standard benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex, etc.).  
- Recommendation: substantially improve presentation and resubmit to another conference.
