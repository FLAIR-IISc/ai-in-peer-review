This paper presents a Positive-Only Projection (PoP) word embedding method. It is a random projection method where the expected value of a random projection matrix is positive. The authors argue that this enables PPMI, which is not possible with an expected value of 0, and that it is computationally efficient because it is a random projection method.

My main objection to this paper is its clarity. Specifically:

1. I did not understand the key difference between the method proposed in the paper and previous random projection methods. Therefore, I did not understand the benefits the authors have achieved (and whether they have achieved them at all).
2. It was difficult to follow the arguments in the paper, from the introduction onwards.
3. Some of the arguments in the paper are not supported:
- Line 114: The sentence begins with "in addition"
- Line 137: The sentence begins with "Since"
- Line 154: The sentence begins with "thus"
4. Although I have worked with vector space modeling (who hasn't?), I am not an expert in random projections and have not used them in my research. It was difficult for me to understand the reasoning behind this research path from the paper. I believe a paper should be self-sufficient and should be possible to follow for people with some experience in this field.
5. There are many English errors in the paper (86: "To a large extend", 142: "such PPMI").

Also, I do not understand why the paper is only evaluating in MEN. There are several standard benchmarks (MEN, WordSeim, SimLex, and a few more) - if you present a new method, I think it is not enough to only evaluate in one dataset, unless you provide a good justification.

I recommend that the authors significantly improve the presentation of the paper and resubmit it to another conference.
