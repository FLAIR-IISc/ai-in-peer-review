- Paper proposes a positive-only projection (PoP) random projection method enabling PPMI use by using a projection matrix with positive expected value.  
- Main concern: overall lack of clarity impedes assessment of the method and its claimed benefits.  
- Unclear how PoP fundamentally differs from existing random projection techniques, so claimed advantages are not validated.  
- Writing and argumentation are hard to follow starting from the introduction.  
- Several transitions and logical flows are inappropriate or misleading (examples cited at Lines 114, 137, 154).  
- Paper insufficiently motivates and explains the research for readers not expert in random projections; not self-contained.  
- Multiple grammatical and wording errors throughout (examples at Lines 86, 142).  
- Evaluation is too limited—only the MEN dataset used; standard benchmarks like WordSim and SimLex should be included or justified.  
- Reviewer recommendation: substantially revise presentation and consider resubmission to another conference.
