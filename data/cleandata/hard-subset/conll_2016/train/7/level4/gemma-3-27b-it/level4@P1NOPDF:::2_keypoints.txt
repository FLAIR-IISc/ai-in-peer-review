- Paper proposes a Positive-Only Projection (PoP) random projection method for word embeddings.  
- Authors claim PoP enables use of PPMI (incompatible with zero-expected-value methods) while retaining random projection efficiency.  
- Key distinctions between PoP and existing random projection techniques are not clearly explained.  
- Overall presentation and logical flow are hard to follow, starting from the introduction.  
- Several arguments/transitions are unsupported or unclear (e.g., lines 114 “in addition,” 137 “Since,” 154 “thus”).  
- Paper does not adequately motivate the research direction or provide background for readers not specialized in random projections.  
- Numerous grammatical and typographical errors reduce readability (examples at lines 86, 142).  
- Evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset; broader benchmarking (WordSim, SimLex, etc.) is needed or a strong justification for the limited evaluation.  
- Recommendation: substantially revise the presentation and experimental evaluation and resubmit.
