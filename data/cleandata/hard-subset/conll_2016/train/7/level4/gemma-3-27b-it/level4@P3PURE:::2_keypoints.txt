- Paper proposes a positive-only projection (PoP) random projection for word embeddings, claiming a positive expected value allows use of PPMI while retaining random projection efficiency.
- Core distinction between PoP and existing random projection techniques is unclear, making claimed advantages hard to assess.
- Overall clarity is poor; the paper’s arguments are difficult to follow starting from the introduction.
- Several arguments lack logical flow and contain awkward sentence constructions (examples cited at lines 114, 137, 154).
- Paper fails to contextualize the motivation and background for readers who are not random-projection specialists; not self-contained for a general audience.
- Numerous grammatical errors throughout the manuscript (examples cited at lines 86 and 142).
- Evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset, which is insufficient to justify a novel method.
- Reviewer recommends broader evaluation on standard benchmarks (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex-998) to strengthen claims.
- Recommendation: substantially revise presentation and resubmit to a future conference.
