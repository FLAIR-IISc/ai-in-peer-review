- Paper proposes a dimensionality-reduction method focused on preserving distributional properties and faster training than neural networks — a compelling motivation.  
- The centrality and significance of PPMI-based transformability as a design principle are not fully justified.  
- No direct comparisons to established related methods; the paper needs a detailed comparative analysis.  
- Kendall’s tau is defined inconsistently with the standard formula; the origin and correctness must be clarified.  
- Training uses a non-standard Kendall’s-tau variant while evaluation uses Spearman correlation; this inconsistency isn’t justified and harms comparability.  
- Evaluation omits common benchmark datasets for semantic relatedness, limiting assessment against prior work.  
- Figure 1 appears incomplete (shows two lines when a third is expected) and should be fixed.  
- Typo: “large extend” should be “large extent.”
