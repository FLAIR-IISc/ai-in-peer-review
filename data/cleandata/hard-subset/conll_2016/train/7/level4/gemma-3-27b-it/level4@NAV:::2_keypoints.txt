- Introduces PoP: a positive-only random projection for word embeddings with positive expected value, claimed to enable PPMI while keeping random-projection efficiency.  
- Core distinction between PoP and existing random projection methods is unclear, making claimed advantages hard to assess.  
- Overall paper clarity is poor; the line of reasoning is difficult to follow starting from the introduction.  
- Several arguments lack clear logical connections and appear unsupported (unnecessary transitional phrases noted at lines 114, 137, 154).  
- Motivation and background for using positive-only/random projections are insufficiently explained for non-specialist readers; needs a stronger self-contained explanation.  
- Numerous English language errors throughout (examples: line 86 “To a large extend”, line 142 “such PPMI”).  
- Evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset; testing only one benchmark is inadequate without strong justification—should include WordSim, SimLex, etc.  
- Recommendation: significantly revise presentation and resubmit to a future conference.
