The paper presents a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method. This is a random projection method utilizing a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors argue that this enables the application of PPMI weighting, which is not possible with a matrix having an expected value of 0, and that, being a random projection method, their approach is computationally efficient.

My main reservation about this paper concerns its clarity. Specifically:

1. I found it difficult to understand the core difference between the method proposed in the paper and existing random projection methods. Consequently, I struggled to evaluate how—and whether—the advantages claimed by the authors hold.
2. The arguments presented in the paper were difficult to follow, beginning with the introduction.
3. Some arguments lack smooth transitions: 
   - Line 114: The sentence begins with “In addition” without clear connection to the preceding text.
   - Line 137: The sentence begins with “Since” without establishing a preceding condition.
   - Line 154: The sentence begins with “Thus” without a clear preceding consequence.
4. While I have experience with vector space modeling, I am not an expert in random projections and have not used them in my research. The paper made it difficult to grasp the rationale behind this research direction. I believe a paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with some familiarity with the field.
5. The paper contains numerous English errors (e.g., 86: “To a large extend,” 142: “such PPMI”).

In addition, I question the evaluation strategy, which relies solely on the MEN dataset. Several standard benchmarks exist (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex) – if a new method is presented, I believe evaluation on multiple datasets is necessary, or a strong justification for focusing on a single one should be provided.

I recommend that the authors substantially improve the presentation of the paper and consider resubmitting to another conference.
