- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection with positive expected value enabling post-projection weighting like PPMI.  
- Shows that applying PPMI to PoP embeddings yields competitive results on the MEN semantic relatedness task.  

- Strength: Random projections offer computational efficiency and faster model construction than neural methods.  
- Strength: Novel combination allowing PPMI weighting after projection, bridging efficient dimensionality reduction with post-hoc weighting.  
- Strength: Empirical demonstration that PPMI improves PoP embeddings on MEN.  
- Strength: Method described in sufficient detail to support potential reproducibility.

- Weakness: Poorly motivated and unclear explanation of why positive expected value enables PPMI; introduction is dense.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to the MEN dataset; lacks broader baselines and metrics to establish true value.  
- Weakness: Stylistic and grammatical issues plus disorganized presentation hinder readability.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical justification and superficial discussion of related randomized-algorithm work.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to parameters (s and dimensionality) is noted but not analyzed or explained.

- Question: Please provide an intuitive explanation or illustration of why positive expected value permits PPMI.  
- Question: What is PoP’s computational complexity compared to RI, RRI, and PPMI-applied count models?  
- Question: How does PoP perform across different languages and datasets; what data characteristics affect effectiveness?  
- Question: Have other weighting schemes beyond PPMI been explored with PoP?

- Minor comments: Several wording errors (e.g., "To a large extend") and phrasing fixes suggested.  
- Minor suggestion: Add a table comparing PoP performance to multiple baselines on MEN.  
- Overall recommendation: Interesting idea but requires major revision for clarity, broader evaluation, and stronger theoretical/empirical analysis.
