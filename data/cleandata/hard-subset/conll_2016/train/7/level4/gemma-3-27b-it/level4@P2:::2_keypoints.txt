- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP): a random projection matrix with positive expected value enabling post-hoc weighting like PPMI.  
- Main contribution: modified random projection that allows PPMI-style weighting, trading off efficiency and discriminative power.  
- Secondary contribution: PoP+PPMI achieves competitive semantic relatedness performance near neural embeddings with lower computational cost.  
- Provides detailed parameter analysis (dimensionality m, sparsity s) and sensitivity/optimal configuration insights.  
- Strength: novel combination—positive expected value in random projections to permit weighting schemes.  
- Strength: emphasizes and (presumably) demonstrates substantial computational efficiency over neural methods.  
- Strength: thorough exploration of parameter effects and trade-offs (sparsity, orthogonality).  
- Strength: clear problem framing on efficient, discriminative semantic space construction.  
- Weakness: lacks conceptual clarity on why positive expectation fundamentally changes the vector space properties.  
- Weakness: disorganized and dense presentation, especially the introduction, hindering narrative coherence.  
- Weakness: numerous grammatical and editorial errors that impede readability.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation—only MEN dataset used, reducing claims of generalizability.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to standard Gaussian/random projection baselines with potential weighting workarounds.  
- Question: please provide an intuitive geometric explanation of how positive expectation alters the space versus standard projections.  
- Question: what modifications (if any) would allow PPMI on standard Gaussian random projections and what limits does PoP overcome?  
- Question: why was MEN the sole evaluation dataset, and will evaluations be expanded to other benchmarks (e.g., WordSim353, SimLex-999)?  
- Question: clarify how dimensionality (m) and sparsity (s) affect PoP construction and PPMI computational complexity.  
- Additional: improve notation/readability (e.g., Equation 3) and streamline theoretical bounds discussion (δ).  
- Additional: supplemental material promising code and embeddings is a positive for reproducibility.
