The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method, a random projection technique employing a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors posit that this characteristic enables the application of Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI), which is incompatible with random projections having an expected value of zero, while maintaining computational efficiency inherent to random projection methods.

My primary concern with this paper lies in its clarity. Specifically:

1. The core distinction between the proposed method and existing random projection techniques remains unclear. Consequently, I found it difficult to assess the validity of the claimed advantages.
2. The paper’s arguments are challenging to follow, beginning with the introduction.
3. Several arguments lack clear logical flow, evidenced by awkward sentence constructions: line 114 begins with “in addition,” line 137 with “Since,” and line 154 with “thus.”
4. While I possess a background in vector space modeling, I am not a specialist in random projections and have not utilized them in my own research. The paper did not sufficiently contextualize the research motivation for readers without specialized knowledge in this area. A strong paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with general expertise in the field.
5. The paper contains numerous grammatical errors (e.g., line 86: “To a large extend,” line 142: “such PPMI”).

Furthermore, the evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset, which is insufficient justification for a novel method without a compelling rationale. A more comprehensive evaluation using standard benchmarks such as MEN, WordSim, and SimLex-998 would strengthen the paper’s claims.

I recommend that the authors substantially revise the presentation and resubmit to a future conference.
