The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method, a random projection technique employing a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors posit that this allows for the application of PPMI, which is not feasible with methods having an expected value of zero, while maintaining computational efficiency inherent to random projections.

However, my primary concern lies with the paper’s clarity. Specifically:

1. The core distinction between the proposed method and existing random projection techniques remains unclear, hindering my ability to assess the validity of the claimed advantages.
2. The paper’s line of reasoning is difficult to follow, beginning with the introduction.
3. Several arguments lack clear logical connection and appear unsupported: 
    - Line 114 begins with an unnecessary transitional phrase ("in addition").
    - Line 137 begins with an unnecessary transitional phrase ("Since").
    - Line 154 begins with an unnecessary transitional phrase ("thus").
4. Although I have experience with vector space modeling, I am not a specialist in random projections. The paper does not adequately explain the motivation behind this research direction, and a stronger self-contained explanation is needed to make it accessible to readers with general expertise in the field.
5. The paper contains numerous English language errors (e.g., Line 86: “To a large extend”, Line 142: “such PPMI”).

Furthermore, the evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset. Given the availability of established benchmarks such as MEN, WordSim, and SimLex, evaluation on a single dataset feels inadequate without a compelling justification. 

I recommend that the authors significantly revise the presentation and resubmit to a future conference.
