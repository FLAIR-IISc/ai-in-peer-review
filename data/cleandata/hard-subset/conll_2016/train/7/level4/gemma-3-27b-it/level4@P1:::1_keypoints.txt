- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a novel, scalable random-projection method for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings with E(R) > 0.  
- PoP uses sparse, positive-integer index vectors and incremental update (like RI), producing low-dimensional vectors (≈100–5000 dims) efficiently.  
- Because E(R) > 0, PoP-constructed vectors can be weighted post hoc with PPMI, unlike standard zero-mean random projections.  
- Uses Kendall’s τb to measure similarity in PoP’s non-Gaussian spaces; after PPMI (log) transformation, Pearson/cosine may be preferable.  
- PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves competitive performance (avg. 0.75 on MEN), comparable to state-of-the-art neural and count+SVD methods.  
- PoP outperforms RI and count-based models even without weighting, and converges faster in experiments.  
- PPMI on PoP is faster than on full count matrices due to PoP’s low dimensionality, mitigating PMI’s typical computational cost.  
- Performance depends on hyperparameters (dimension m, sparsity s, number of non-zeros); recommended s > 0.002 for direct PoP and 1–2 non-zeros when using weighting.  
- Empirical patterns: increasing m helps PPMI-weighted models (reduces variance); overly large m or inappropriate s can degrade unweighted PoP.  
- PoP is randomized with no formal error bounds δ yet; empirical behavior aligns with other sparse random projections and variance scales ~1/m.  
- Advantages: fast model building (seconds–minutes), low resource use, amenable to further weighting/stabilization and integration with neural methods.  
- Evaluation used MEN relatedness and UKWaC corpus with Spearman’s ρ; the review text was edited for clarity and minor errors corrected.
