I am persuaded by the motivation for this work: the proposed method offers a significantly faster training process compared to neural networks, while also preserving distributional properties during dimensionality reduction. However, I remain unconvinced regarding the critical importance of PPMI transformability. Furthermore, a key weakness is the lack of direct comparison to closely related existing methods.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   **Kendall’s τ Calculation:** The presented definition of Kendall’s τ (p.3) is unconventional and its origin is unclear. This raises concerns about the validity of the similarity measurements.
*   **Correlation Measure Choice:** Given its widespread use in semantic tasks, the rationale for not employing Spearman correlation—particularly given its application during evaluation—is unclear.
*   **Evaluation Datasets:** While exploring alternative evaluation datasets is valuable, inclusion of results on standard semantic relatedness benchmarks is crucial for establishing a clear point of reference within the NLP community.
*   **Figure 1 Clarity:** Figure 1 currently displays only two lines, hindering proper interpretation of the results. The missing line should be clarified or corrected.
*   **Missing Related Work Comparison:** The paper lacks a direct comparative analysis against relevant existing techniques. The authors merely state differences without providing empirical evidence of performance contrasts.
*   **Typo:** "large extend" should be "large extent."

**Contributions (as I see them):**

1.  **A Novel Random Projection Technique:** The primary contribution lies in the introduction of Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a new random projection method utilizing matrices with a positive expected value. This is a distinct approach compared to existing random projection methods, potentially offering advantages in specific scenarios.
2.  **PPMI Compatibility:** The method’s ability to facilitate the application of PPMI weighting after projection is a noteworthy characteristic. While its importance is debatable, it expands the possibilities for post-processing and refinement of the resulting semantic spaces.
3.  **Efficiency:** The inherent scalability and computational efficiency of PoP, stemming from its reliance on random projections, represent a practical benefit, particularly when dealing with large datasets.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Computational Efficiency:** The method’s speed is a significant advantage. The authors clearly demonstrate the potential for rapid model construction compared to computationally intensive neural embedding techniques. This is particularly valuable for resource-constrained environments or scenarios requiring quick iteration.
2.  **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a reasonable theoretical grounding for the proposed method, linking it to existing concepts in distributional semantics and random projections. The discussion regarding the limitations of traditional random projections and the benefits of positive-valued projections is insightful.
3.  **Novelty of Approach:** PoP introduces a unique perspective on random projection, departing from the conventional use of zero-mean matrices. This departure opens up possibilities for exploring new properties and applications of random projections in semantic space construction.
4.  **Clear Explanation of Method:** The authors present the PoP method in a clear and understandable manner, detailing the mathematical foundations and the two-step construction process. This clarity facilitates comprehension and potential replication of the work.
5.  **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on the MEN relatedness test demonstrate that PoP, combined with PPMI, achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art algorithms. This empirical validation lends credibility to the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Lack of Comparative Evaluation:** The most significant weakness is the absence of a comprehensive comparison against directly related methods, such as standard random indexing (RI) and related techniques. Demonstrating performance gains—or identifying scenarios where PoP excels—relative to existing approaches is crucial.
2.  **Unclear Motivation for PPMI:** The authors assert the importance of PPMI compatibility, but fail to adequately justify why this is a critical requirement. A more compelling argument for the benefits of applying PPMI to PoP-constructed spaces is needed.
3.  **Unconventional Kendall's τ Implementation:** The use of a non-standard formula for Kendall’s τ raises concerns about the validity of the similarity measurements and potentially impacts the reported results.
4.  **Limited Evaluation Datasets:** Relying solely on the MEN relatedness test limits the generalizability of the findings. Including results on more established benchmark datasets would provide a more robust evaluation.
5.  **Figure 1 Incompleteness:** The incomplete nature of Figure 1 hinders a clear understanding of the experimental results and the relative performance of the different methods.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed justification for the importance of PPMI transformability, outlining the specific benefits it offers in the context of PoP-constructed semantic spaces?
2.  What is the origin of the Kendall’s τ formula used in your paper, and how does it differ from the standard definition?
3.  Will you include a direct comparative evaluation against standard random indexing (RI) and other related methods in a revised version of the paper?
4.  Do you plan to evaluate the performance of PoP on other established semantic relatedness benchmark datasets to assess its generalizability?
5.  Could you clarify the missing line in Figure 1 and provide a complete and accurate representation of the experimental results?
