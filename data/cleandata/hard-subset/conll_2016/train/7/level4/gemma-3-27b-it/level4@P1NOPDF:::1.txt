I find some of the motivation compelling: the proposed method trains considerably faster than a neural network. Furthermore, it preserves certain distributional properties when reducing dimensionality. 

However, I’m not convinced of the significance of requiring vectors to be transformable using PPMI. 

Most critically, the paper lacks a direct comparison to related work.

Detailed comments:

- p.3: The authors’ definition of Kendall's tau is unusual. This does not correspond to the standard formula, and its origin is unclear.
- p.3: Why was Spearman correlation not used, given its prevalence in semantic tasks (and its use by the authors during evaluation)?
- The datasets chosen for evaluation are not the standard benchmarks preferred by the NLP community for measuring semantic relatedness. While exploring alternative datasets is valuable, I recommend including results on established benchmarks as well.
- Figure 1 appears to show only two lines; where is the third line referenced in the text?
- The paper does not offer a direct comparison to related work, only a descriptive statement.

- Typo: "large extend" should be "large extent".
