- Paper proposes faster dimensionality reduction preserving semantic properties of embeddings but lacks full justification for core idea.  
- Main methodological concern: no clear rationale for enforcing PPMI transformability or its advantage over other constraints.  
- Insufficient comparison to prior work; needs quantitative benchmarks against PCA, SVD, t‑SNE, and other embedding compression methods.  
- Non‑standard definition of Kendall’s tau is used without explanation, raising validity and comparability concerns.  
- Training uses a non‑standard Kendall variant while evaluation uses Spearman correlation; this inconsistency is unexplained.  
- Evaluation datasets are non‑standard for semantic relatedness; results should include benchmarks like WordSim353, SimLex‑999, and RG‑65.  
- Figure 1 appears incomplete (text mentions three lines but only two shown), reducing clarity.  
- Related work section lacks a dedicated, quantitative comparative analysis to establish the method’s strengths/weaknesses.  
- Typo: “large extend” should be “large extent.”  
- Overall: paper needs clearer justification for PPMI constraint, consistent metrics, standard benchmarks, and direct comparisons to prior methods.
