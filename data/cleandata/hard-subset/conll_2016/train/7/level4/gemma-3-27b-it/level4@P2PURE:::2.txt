The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method, a random projection technique employing a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors posit that this allows for the application of Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI), which is incompatible with methods having an expected value of zero, while maintaining computational efficiency due to the inherent properties of random projections.

My primary concern with this paper centers on its lack of clarity. Specifically:

1. The core distinction between the proposed method and existing random projection techniques remains unclear. Consequently, the validity of the claimed advantages is difficult to assess.
2. The paper’s arguments are difficult to follow, beginning with the introduction.
3. Several arguments within the paper lack logical flow, as evidenced by inappropriate transitional phrasing: (e.g., Line 114 begins with “in addition,” Line 137 with “Since,” and Line 154 with “thus”).
4. While I possess experience in vector space modeling, I am not a specialist in random projections. The paper does not sufficiently explain the rationale behind this research direction for readers without prior expertise in the area. A strong paper should be self-contained and accessible to those with a reasonable background in the field.
5. The paper contains numerous grammatical errors (e.g., Line 86: “To a large extend,” Line 142: “such PPMI”).

Furthermore, the evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset, which is insufficient. Given the availability of standard benchmarks like MEN, WordSim, and SimLex, a more comprehensive evaluation is expected for a novel method, or a strong justification for focusing on a single dataset is required. 

I recommend that the authors significantly revise the paper’s presentation and consider resubmission to another conference.
