- Introduces PoP (positive-only projection) using a random projection matrix with positive expected value to enable PPMI while keeping random projection efficiency.
- Claims PoP allows effective use of PPMI, which standard zero-mean random projections cannot support.
- Primary concern: paper lacks clarity on core contributions and how PoP differs from existing random projection methods.
- Argumentation is hard to follow from the introduction onward, with awkward transitions and disjointed flow.
- Several instances of poor phrasing and grammatical/typographical errors that reduce readability (example lines cited).
- Paper does not provide sufficient background or motivation for readers unfamiliar with random projections.
- Evaluation is limited: only the MEN dataset is used, which is insufficient to demonstrate generalizability.
- Recommends broader evaluation on standard benchmarks (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex) or justification for focusing solely on MEN.
- Overall recommendation: substantial revision for clarity, argumentation, grammar, and more thorough evaluation before resubmission.
