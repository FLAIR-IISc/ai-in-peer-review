This paper presents an interesting approach to dimensionality reduction with a focus on maintaining distributional properties and training efficiency. The motivation – achieving faster training times compared to neural networks, alongside preservation of distributional characteristics during dimensionality reduction – is compelling. However, the paper doesn't fully justify the significance of PPMI-based transformability as a central design principle. A key weakness is the lack of direct comparison to established, related work in the field.

Here are more detailed comments:

* **Kendall's Tau Definition (p.3):** The presented definition of Kendall's tau deviates from the standard formula. Its origin and correctness are unclear and require clarification.

* **Correlation Metric (p.3):** The choice to employ a non-standard variant of Kendall’s tau during training, when Spearman correlation is commonly used and *is* applied during evaluation, is not justified. Using Spearman correlation throughout would improve consistency and facilitate comparison with other studies.

* **Evaluation Datasets:** While exploring non-standard datasets is valuable, the evaluation lacks results on commonly used benchmark datasets for semantic relatedness. Including these standard evaluations would strengthen the paper and allow for a more comprehensive assessment of the method’s performance within the broader NLP community.

* **Figure 1:** Figure 1 appears incomplete, displaying only two lines when a third is expected. This should be addressed.

* **Related Work Comparison:** The paper lacks a direct, comparative analysis with relevant existing methods. Simply stating the existence of related work is insufficient; a detailed comparison demonstrating the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach relative to alternatives is crucial.

* **Typos:** "large extend" should be corrected to "large extent."
