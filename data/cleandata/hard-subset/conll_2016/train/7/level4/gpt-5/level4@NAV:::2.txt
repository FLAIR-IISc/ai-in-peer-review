The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) approach to word embeddings. It is a random projection technique in which the random projection matrix has a positive expectation. The authors argue that this enables the use of PPMI (which is not feasible when the expectation is 0) and that, as a random projection method, it is computationally efficient.

My main concern is the paper’s clarity. Specifically:
1. I could not discern the core distinction between the proposed method and prior random projection approaches. As a result, I could not evaluate how (or whether) the claimed advantages actually follow.
2. The argumentation is hard to follow starting from the introduction.
3. Several claims are not adequately supported:
- Line 114: Sentence starts with "in addition"
- Line 137: Sentence starts with "Since"
- Line 154: Sentence starts with "thus"
4. While I have experience with vector-space modeling, I am not a specialist in random projections and have not used them in my work. The paper did not make the rationale behind this research direction clear. A paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with relevant background.
5. The manuscript contains numerous English errors (86: "To a large extend", 142: "such PPMI").

In addition, I do not understand why the evaluation is restricted to MEN. There are several standard benchmarks (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex, and others); for a new method, it seems insufficient to test on a single dataset without a strong justification.

I recommend that the authors significantly improve the presentation and resubmit to another conference.
