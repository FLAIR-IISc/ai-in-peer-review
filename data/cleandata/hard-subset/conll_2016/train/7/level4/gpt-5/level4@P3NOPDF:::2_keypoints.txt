- Summary: Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection word embedding with projection matrix positive in expectation to enable PPMI after projection and claim efficiency vs. matrix factorization.

- Strength: Aligning random projection with PPMI weighting is an interesting, potentially lightweight alternative to heavier factorization methods.

- Strength: Emphasis on computational efficiency is timely and could be useful for large vocabularies if validated.

- Major concern 1 (clarity/novelty): Core methodological difference from prior random-projection methods is unclear; ambiguity whether entries are strictly nonnegative, positive in expectation, or rectified, and how that satisfies PPMI’s nonnegativity needs.

- Major concern 2 (PPMI workflow): Workflow is underspecified—unclear if PPMI is applied before or after projection, how marginals/smoothing/normalization are defined post-projection, and which PMI properties are preserved.

- Major concern 3 (theory/empirics): Several consequential claims lack proofs or citations, and efficiency assertions need formal complexity analysis plus runtime/memory comparisons to baselines.

- Major concern 4 (accessibility): Paper assumes projection knowledge without offering necessary background or intuition for readers from distributional semantics.

- Major concern 5 (experiments): Evaluation only on MEN is insufficient; needs many more intrinsic datasets, analogy and extrinsic tasks, stronger baselines (SVD-PPMI, SGNS, GloVe, Random Indexing), statistical tests, and ablation studies (positivity, sparsity, dimensionality, seed sensitivity).

- Major concern 6 (writing): Numerous grammatical errors and unclear sentences impede comprehension and leave many claims dangling without context.

- Clarification requested (projection distribution): Precisely define the projection matrix distribution—strictly nonnegative or nonnegative in expectation—and how individual projected counts meet PPMI requirements.

- Clarification requested (PPMI location): Specify where PPMI is computed in the pipeline and how projected marginals and normalization are handled.

- Clarification requested (formal properties): State formal properties preserved by PoP (e.g., approximate dot-product preservation or expected PMI relations).

- Clarification requested (comparisons): Compare theoretically and empirically to nonnegative matrix factorization and projections with Bernoulli {0,1} or {1,2} distributions.

- Clarification requested (complexity): Provide time/space complexity relative to baselines and empirical wall-clock measurements.

- Revision suggestion (intro/method): Rework the introduction to state the precise contribution and why positivity matters, and add a clear method section with notation, algorithm, and a proposition or derivation linking projection distribution to valid PPMI estimation.

- Revision suggestion (experiments/detailing): Expand experiments to multiple benchmarks and baselines, include ablations, hyperparameter details, sensitivity analyses, and statistical error bars.

- Revision suggestion (organization/claims): Ensure each claim is supported by citation/derivation/experiment and move informal intuition to its own subsection.

- Revision suggestion (proofreading): Thoroughly proofread for grammar and clarity or use professional editing.

- Overall recommendation: Do not recommend acceptance; the idea has potential but needs substantially clearer exposition, stronger theoretical justification, and a much more comprehensive empirical evaluation.
