- Novelty unclear: The essential distinction between PoP and existing random projection methods is not made explicit, so claimed advantages (PPMI applicability, efficiency) are unsupported.

- Lack of precise comparisons: Need a detailed comparison showing what changes when the expected projection is positive versus zero and how that affects outcomes.

- Poor exposition and logical flow: Key ideas, assumptions, and implications are not explained with sufficient intuition or formal justification.

- Unsupported claims: Several statements (lines 114, 137, 154) assert conclusions or premises without derivations, evidence, or citations.

- Insufficient background/accessibility: The paper lacks a concise overview of relevant random projection theory, an explanation of why positive expectation enables PPMI, and a clear description of how PoP integrates with PMI/PPMI and improves efficiency.

- Language and style issues: Numerous English errors and awkward phrasings impede readability and require thorough proofreading.

- Limited empirical evaluation: Experiments are restricted to MEN only; evaluation should include multiple standard benchmarks and strong baselines or justify the narrow focus.

- Revision recommendations: Authors should clearly state methodological novelty, provide formal support for claims, expand empirical comparisons, and fix grammatical and presentation problems before resubmission.
