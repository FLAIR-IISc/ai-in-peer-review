I accept part of the motivation: the approach trains far more quickly than neural networks and appears to preserve aspects of the original distribution after reducing dimensionality. However, I remain unconvinced about the necessity of ensuring that the projected vectors can be post-hoc transformed with PPMI. Most critically, the paper lacks a direct, head-to-head comparison with closely related methods.

Detailed comments:
- p.3: The provided definition of Kendall’s τb is unusual; it does not match the standard formulation. Please clarify the source and justification for this variant.
- p.3: Why not use Spearman correlation, which is conventional in semantic similarity/relatedness evaluations (and is what you report at evaluation time)?
- The evaluation datasets are not the typical benchmarks favored by the NLP community for semantic relatedness. Exploring alternative sets is fine, but please also include results on the standard ones.
- Figure 1 appears to show only two curves; where is the third method’s curve?
- There is no direct empirical comparison to competing approaches; the paper offers only general claims of competitiveness without side-by-side results.
