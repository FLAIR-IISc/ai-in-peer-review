The submission introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP) for constructing word embeddings. PoP is a random projection approach whose projection matrix has a positive expected value, which the authors claim permits applying PPMI post hoc—unlike zero-mean random projections—and, being a random projection method, is computationally efficient.

My primary concern is clarity. Specifically:
1. The paper does not make the fundamental distinction between PoP and prior random projection methods clear, so it is difficult to assess whether the stated advantages are valid and how they arise.
2. The argumentation is hard to follow starting from the introduction.
3. Several claims appear unsupported; for example:
   - Line 114: sentence beginning “in addition …”
   - Line 137: sentence beginning “Since …”
   - Line 154: sentence beginning “thus …”
4. Although I have experience with vector space models, I am not a specialist in random projections. The paper does not sufficiently motivate or explain the core logic behind this line of work to a reader with general background, and it should be self-contained for such readers.
5. The manuscript contains numerous English errors (e.g., line 86: “To a large extend”; line 142: “such PPMI”).

In addition, the evaluation is restricted to MEN. There are several standard benchmarks (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex, among others). For a new method, evaluating on a single dataset is inadequate unless compelling justification is provided.

I recommend substantial improvements to the presentation and clarity of the paper, followed by resubmission to another venue.
