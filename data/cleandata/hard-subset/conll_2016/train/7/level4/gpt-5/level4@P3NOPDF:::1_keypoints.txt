- Proposes a fast, non-neural method for learning word vectors aimed at preserving distributional properties under dimensionality reduction and being PPMI-transformable.  
- Strength: clear motivation on substantially faster training speed than standard neural embeddings.  
- Strength: interesting focus on preserving distributional structure during dimensionality reduction.  
- Major concern: Limited justification for why PPMI transformability is practically important; needs concrete downstream scenarios and empirical demonstration.  
- Major concern: No direct comparisons to strong baselines; must include SVD on PPMI, SGNS (word2vec), GloVe, and relevant non-neural methods, reporting both accuracy and training time.  
- Major concern: Uses non-standard semantic relatedness datasets; should report results on community benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, MEN, SimLex-999, RG-65, Rare-Word).  
- Major concern: Ambiguity in correlation metrics—Kendall’s tau definition is non-standard; cite or rename the variant, and consider using/reporting Spearman’s rho for consistency.  
- Major concern: Figure 1 is flawed—third line missing; legend, colors, and line styles must be distinguishable in print/grayscale.  
- Major concern: “Preserved properties” claim is under-quantified; specify which properties (neighborhoods, rank correlations, etc.) and provide targeted diagnostics.  
- Request: Clarify specific applications that depend on PPMI transformability or state if the benefit is primarily theoretical/interop with legacy pipelines.  
- Request: Provide sensitivity analysis or ablation on hyperparameters (dimensionality, smoothing/weighting choices).  
- Request: Report training-time hardware, corpus sizes, wall-clock times, and memory usage for fair, reproducible speed claims.  
- Minor: Use standard definitions for Kendall’s tau or provide clear reference; consider Spearman’s rho for semantic evaluations.  
- Minor: Fix Figure 1 so the third line appears and verify readability in grayscale.  
- Minor: Typos and phrasing errors (e.g., “large extend” → “large extent”); conduct careful proofreading.  
- Overall: Promising emphasis on efficiency and distributional preservation, but currently lacks empirical rigor, baseline comparisons, standard evaluations, and clear PPMI motivation; addressing these would strengthen the paper.
