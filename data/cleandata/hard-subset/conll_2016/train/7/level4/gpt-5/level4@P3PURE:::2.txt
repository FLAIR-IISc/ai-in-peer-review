This paper proposes a positive-only projection (PoP) method for word embeddings: a random projection approach in which the projection matrix has a positive expected value. The authors argue that this design enables the use of PPMI (which they claim is not feasible when the projection has zero expected value) and that, as a random projection method, PoP is computationally efficient.

Major concerns about clarity and presentation:
1) Novelty and core differences are unclear. I could not discern the essential difference between PoP and prior random projection methods. As a result, I cannot assess how—or whether—the stated advantages (e.g., the ability to apply PPMI and improved efficiency) actually hold relative to existing approaches.
2) The exposition is difficult to follow from the outset. The logical flow of the introduction and subsequent sections made it hard to understand the paper’s argument and contributions.
3) Several claims appear unsupported or insufficiently justified. For example:
   - Line 114: Sentence begins with “In addition” but does not clearly tie to a prior established point.
   - Line 137: Sentence begins with “Since” without providing an explicit, supported premise.
   - Line 154: Sentence begins with “Thus” without a clear preceding argument to conclude from.
4) Accessibility and self-containment are lacking. While I have experience with vector space modeling, I am not a specialist in random projections, and the paper did not provide enough background or intuition for me to follow the reasoning behind this research direction. A conference paper should be self-contained and understandable to readers with general experience in the field.
5) The manuscript contains numerous English errors (e.g., line 86: “To a large extend”; line 142: “such PPMI”), which further impede readability.

Evaluation concerns:
- The empirical evaluation is restricted to MEN. There are multiple widely used benchmarks (e.g., MEN, WordSim, SimLex, and others). For a new method, evaluating on only one dataset is insufficient unless a strong justification is provided.

Recommendation:
I recommend substantial revisions to improve clarity, rigor, and writing quality, along with a broader evaluation, and resubmission to another venue. Specifically, the paper should:
- Clearly articulate how PoP differs from existing random projection methods and why those differences enable PPMI where zero-mean projections do not.
- Provide explicit, well-supported arguments for the claimed advantages, with theoretical and/or empirical evidence.
- Improve organization and flow from the introduction onward, making the paper self-contained for readers not specializing in random projections.
- Correct the English throughout.
- Expand the experimental evaluation to multiple standard benchmarks or justify the narrow focus.
