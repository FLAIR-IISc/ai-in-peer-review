- Paper proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection with positive expected projection matrix, claiming it enables use of PPMI and is computationally efficient.
- Main clarity concern: key differences between PoP and prior random projection methods are not clearly explained.
- As a result, it's unclear how the claimed advantages (e.g., applicability of PPMI, computational benefits) actually follow.
- Overall argumentation is difficult to follow from the introduction onward.
- Several statements appear unsupported or poorly signposted (examples: lines 114, 137, 154).
- Paper is not sufficiently self-contained for readers familiar with vector space models but not random projections; underlying rationale is not made clear.
- Numerous English errors and awkward phrasing (examples: line 86 “To a large extend”; line 142 “such PPMI”).
- Evaluation is inadequate—only tested on MEN; standard additional benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex, etc.) are missing or unjustified.
- Recommendation: substantially improve presentation, clarity, experiments, and resubmit.
