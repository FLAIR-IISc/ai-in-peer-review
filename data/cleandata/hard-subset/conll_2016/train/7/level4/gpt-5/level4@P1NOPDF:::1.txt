I agree with parts of the motivation: the proposed method trains much faster than neural networks and appears to preserve certain distributional properties when reducing dimensionality. However, I am not convinced that making the vectors transformable using PPMI is particularly important. Most critically, the paper offers no direct empirical comparison to related work.

Detailed comments:
- p.3: The definition of Kendall’s tau used in the paper is unusual. It is not the original formula; please clarify what variant this is, where it comes from, and why it is appropriate here.
- p.3: Why not use Spearman correlation consistently, as is standard in semantic evaluation tasks and as the authors themselves do at evaluation time?
- The datasets chosen for evaluation are not the standard ones typically used by the NLP community for measuring semantic relatedness. Exploring additional datasets is fine, but please also include results on the commonly used benchmarks.
- Figure 1: I can only see two lines. Where is the third line?
- There is no direct comparison to related work; the paper offers statements but no head-to-head empirical comparisons.
