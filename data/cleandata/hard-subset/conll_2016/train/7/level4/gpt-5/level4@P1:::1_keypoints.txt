- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse, positive-only random-projection method enabling post-hoc PPMI weighting in low-dimensional semantic spaces.  
- Recommends Kendall’s τb for similarity in unweighted PoP space and reports competitive MEN performance (ρ ≈ 0.75) with efficiency gains.  

Contributions:
- Introduces a simple incremental positive-only random projection that preserves distributional structure for efficient post-hoc PPMI in reduced space.  
- Claims PoP+PPMI is faster to construct than neural embeddings while achieving comparable MEN performance.  
- Proposes using Kendall’s τb for unweighted PoP and Pearson’s r after PPMI.

Strengths:
- Lightweight, incremental, and computationally efficient alternative to neural embeddings and full PPMI computation.  
- Empirical gains over classical Random Indexing and raw counts on MEN, especially with small windows.  
- Provides practical parameter studies and observations on stability/variance.  
- Demonstrates feasibility and speed of post-projection PPMI in reduced space.

Weaknesses:
- No direct controlled comparisons to strong baselines (SGNS/word2vec, GloVe, or PPMI+SVD) on the same corpus/preprocessing.  
- Insufficient ablations to show the specific importance of “PPMI-transformable” projections versus other factors.  
- Kendall’s τb is advocated but the paper’s definition/derivation is unclear and lacks empirical comparison to cosine, Pearson, and Spearman.  
- Evaluation is limited to MEN and UKWaC; lacks additional datasets and downstream tasks, and missing confidence intervals/variability across runs.  
- Presentation issues: ambiguous Figure 1 (expected three lines but shows two), unclear legends/captions, and several typos.

Questions for authors:
- Provide the exact τ variant used (τa/τb/τc), clarify tie handling, fix the formula on p.3, and empirically justify τb over cosine/Spearman.  
- Report results and runtime/memory comparisons against SGNS and GloVe trained on UKWaC with matched preprocessing, and against PPMI+SVD at similar dimensionality.  
- Ablate how much performance stems from PoP vs PPMI (e.g., PPMI on counts + SVD at matched dims).  
- Justify why E[R] > 0 is necessary versus re-centering/shifting zero-mean projections and compare alternatives.  
- Clarify Figure 1’s missing third line and ensure legend/caption accuracy.

Additional comments:
- Fix minor grammatical errors (e.g., “large extend” → “large extent”) and other typos.  
- Since MEN is a relatedness dataset, report both Spearman ρ and Pearson r for all methods for comparability.  
- Recommend releasing code, exact preprocessing steps, seeds, and confidence intervals to facilitate replication.
