Summary and contributions
This paper proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for building low-dimensional semantic spaces. Unlike prior random indexing variants that use zero-mean projection matrices, PoP uses sparse, positive-only random matrices (E[R] > 0), which the authors argue enables post-hoc weighting with PPMI in the reduced space. They recommend Kendall’s τb for similarity in the non-Gaussian projected space and claim competitive MEN performance (ρ ≈ 0.75) with substantial efficiency gains.

Contributions as I see them:
1) A simple, incremental random projection scheme (positive-only, sparse) that preserves enough distributional structure to allow efficient post-hoc PPMI weighting in a low-dimensional space.
2) An empirical claim that PoP (especially PoP+PPMI) is faster to construct than neural embeddings while achieving comparable performance on MEN.
3) A proposal to use Kendall’s τb for similarity in the unweighted PoP space, with Pearson’s r after PPMI.

Strengths
- Efficiency and simplicity: The approach is lightweight, incremental, and avoids expensive training. This is a compelling practical advantage over neural methods and over computing PPMI on full count matrices.
- Empirical improvement over RI and raw counts: The reported gains over classical Random Indexing and naive count-based baselines on MEN (especially with small windows) support the claim that positive-only projections plus post-hoc weighting can be beneficial.
- Practical guidance: The paper provides parameter studies (m, sparsity s, number of non-zeros) and observations about stability/variance, which are valuable for practitioners.
- Compatibility with standard weighting: The key idea that post-projection PPMI becomes feasible (and fast) in the reduced space is interesting and potentially useful for building large-scale models quickly.

Weaknesses
- Lack of direct comparison to strong baselines: There is no head-to-head evaluation against standard, high-performing baselines on the same corpus and preprocessing, such as SGNS/word2vec, GloVe, or SVD on PPMI-weighted counts (with common tweaks as in Levy et al., 2015). The claim of “comparable to state-of-the-art” is hard to assess without these controlled comparisons, including runtime and memory.
- Unclear importance of “PPMI-transformable” projections: The claim that enabling PPMI after projection is crucial is not convincingly demonstrated. Please add ablations that (i) compare PoP+PPMI to PPMI on the original counts with SVD truncation at matched dimensionality; (ii) test whether a simple shift/normalization of zero-mean projections truly prevents meaningful PMI-like weighting; and (iii) disentangle how much of the gain comes from PoP vs from PPMI.
- Similarity measure choice and definition issues: The paper advocates Kendall’s τb for similarity in PoP spaces, but the definition given (p.3) does not appear to match the standard τb formula, and the derivation/notation is confusing (ties, nc/nd/n0/n1/n2). Moreover, Spearman correlation is standard for evaluating semantic relatedness and is already used at evaluation time. A clear empirical justification comparing τb, cosine, Pearson’s r, and Spearman (on both unweighted PoP and PoP+PPMI) is needed.
- Limited evaluation: MEN and UKWaC alone are insufficient. MEN is widely used, but please include additional standard datasets (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999, RG-65, Rare Words) and, ideally, at least one downstream task (e.g., analogy, categorization, or a simple text classification/semantic similarity task) to substantiate generality. Report confidence intervals or variability across runs for all setups.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 appears to show only two lines, although the text suggests three (counts, RI, PoP). Legends and captions should make all curves unambiguous. There are also several typos.

Questions to authors
- Kendall’s τb: Please provide the exact standard τb definition you use (τa/τb/τc?), clarify tie handling, and fix or justify the formula on p.3. Why is τb preferable to cosine or Spearman for unweighted PoP? Show empirical comparisons.
- Baselines: Can you report results against SGNS and GloVe trained on UKWaC with matched preprocessing, and against PPMI+SVD at similar dimensionality? Include training time and peak memory comparisons.
- Role of PPMI: How much of the final performance is attributable to PoP versus PPMI? Would PPMI on counts with SVD (at 3000 dims) match or exceed PoP+PPMI+Pearson? Please include an ablation.
- Projection mean: Is there a principled reason (beyond enabling Equation 3) that E[R] > 0 is necessary? Could re-centering or shifting zero-mean projections permit analogous weighting, and how would that compare?
- Figure 1: Where is the third line? Please clarify and ensure the legend/caption matches the plot.

Additional comments
- Please correct “large extend” to “large extent.” There are a few other minor grammatical issues throughout.
- Since MEN is a relatedness dataset and you emphasize Pearson’s r post-PPMI, reporting both Spearman ρ and Pearson r for all methods would aid comparability.
- Consider releasing code and exact preprocessing steps to facilitate replication, including seeds for randomized components and confidence intervals across multiple runs.
