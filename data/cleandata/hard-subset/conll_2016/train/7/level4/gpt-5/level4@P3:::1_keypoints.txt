- Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse nonnegative random projections that allow post-hoc PPMI on low-dimensional vectors for efficiency gains over PPMI on full counts.
- Empirically shows PoP+PPMI+Pearson attains ~0.75 on MEN, with analysis of how dimensionality and sparsity affect performance.
- Recommends Kendall’s τb as a similarity measure for non-Gaussian PoP spaces.

- Efficient and incremental: computationally light, streaming-friendly, and keeps vectors low-dimensional.
- Enables applying PPMI after projection by using positive-only projections, addressing a limitation of zero-mean random projections.
- Competitive MEN results without neural training, suggesting a favorable accuracy–efficiency trade-off.
- Provides sensitivity analysis over dimension m and sparsity s to guide hyperparameter choices.
- Transparently acknowledges lack of theoretical error bounds.

- Missing direct head-to-head comparisons with SGNS/word2vec, GloVe, PPMI+SVD, and stronger random-indexing baselines under matched settings.
- Evaluation is limited to MEN and should include additional benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, Rare Words, RG-65, SemEval).
- Incorrect/inconsistent Kendall’s τb definition (uses consecutive indices instead of all pairwise comparisons); needs correction.
- Weak motivation for Kendall over Spearman or cosine and lacking empirical ablations to justify the choice.
- Insufficient controlled evidence that post-projection PPMI matches PPMI-on-counts or feature-hashing alternatives beyond speed.
- Reproducibility details are thin: code only promised on acceptance, missing wall-clock times, hardware, preprocessing, seeds, run variance, and significance testing.

- Provide controlled comparisons on the same corpus with SGNS, GloVe, and PPMI+SVD across MEN, WordSim-353, and SimLex-999.
- Report wall-clock time, memory usage, and throughput for PoP, count-based PPMI, PPMI+SVD, and SGNS on the same hardware.
- Justify Kendall’s τb versus Spearman or cosine and include empirical ablation studies.
- Clarify relation to feature hashing and Count-Sketch and whether PoP offers distinct advantages.
- Fix Figure 1 so the third curve is present and legible.

- Correct τb definition to standard pairwise (i<j) comparisons and cite Knight’s O(n log n) algorithm if used.
- Use consistent notation for the projection matrix (R vs T) and for expectation statements.
- Fix minor typos and spacing/punctuation errors.
- Release code and preprocessing scripts to facilitate verification.

- Overall: promising practical idea with solid efficiency potential, but needs stronger controlled empirical comparisons, corrected statistical definitions, and clearer justification for similarity measures before acceptance.
