- Proposal: Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with strictly non-negative projection entries and E(R) > 0 to construct word embeddings.  
- Practical claim: PoP enables post-hoc weighting (e.g., PPMI) on low-dimensional representations, unlike prior zero-mean random projections.  
- Efficiency claim: PoP supports incremental construction, avoids heavy training, and with PPMI achieves competitive MEN scores (~0.75) in low dimensions.  
- Methodological claim: Recommend Kendall’s τb for similarity on raw PoP vectors and Pearson’s r after PPMI transforms.  

- Strength: Addresses a real computational pain point with a simple, incremental alternative to heavy embedding/training pipelines.  
- Strength: Novel idea of preserving “counts-like” structure via positivity to enable weighting after projection.  
- Strength: Empirical parameter exploration showing how dimensionality and sparsity affect performance and variance; PoP outperforms RI on MEN and PoP+PPMI approaches strong baselines.  

- Weakness: No rigorous justification that PPMI is meaningful after random projection; positivity of entries alone doesn’t make projected values valid probabilities or PMI surrogates.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is narrow (mostly MEN); lacks other similarity benchmarks, analogy/downstream tasks, controlled comparisons with SGNS/GloVe, and statistical significance/variance reporting.  
- Weakness: Poor positioning against prior random-projection methods (RI/RRI/ISA/RMI); unclear what properties PoP preserves or trades off relative to inner-product/L1 preservation or nonnegative random features.  
- Weakness: Choice of Kendall’s τb on raw PoP vectors is unconventional and not systematically justified across datasets or metrics.  
- Weakness: Presentation and language issues (grammar, imprecise statements, notation) hinder comprehension and need tightening.  

- Question: Provide formal justification or empirical/theoretical evidence that PPMI on PoP preserves PMI ordering or approximates co-occurrence statistics better than alternatives.  
- Question: Evaluate on additional benchmarks (WordSim353, SimLex-999, Rare Words, analogy/downstream) and compare to SGNS/GloVe trained on the same corpus with significance testing.  
- Question: Report wall-clock runtimes and peak memory for count+PPMI+SVD, RI, SGNS/GloVe, and PoP/PoP+PPMI at multiple m and s on the same hardware.  
- Question: Systematically compare similarity metrics (cosine, Pearson, Spearman, Kendall τb) for PoP with/without PPMI across datasets to show when τb helps.  
- Question: Provide theoretical or partial results on how positivity and sparsity of the projection affect distortion, neighborhood preservation, and estimator variance; relate to nonnegative random features/JL variants.  
- Question: Improve reproducibility by providing full preprocessing details, hyperparameters, number of runs, and code or pseudocode now rather than upon acceptance.
