Overall assessment:
- I appreciate some aspects of the motivation: the proposed method appears substantially faster to train than neural networks and seems to preserve certain distributional properties when reducing dimensionality.
- However, I am not convinced of the importance of requiring vectors to be transformable with PPMI.
- Most critically, the paper lacks direct, quantitative comparisons to related work.

Detailed comments:
- p. 3: The definition of Kendall’s tau used here is unusual and does not match the standard formulation. Please justify this choice and provide a citation, or revert to the conventional definition.
- p. 3: Why not use Spearman correlation, which is standard for semantic evaluation (and which you use at evaluation time)? The inconsistency between the methodological choice and the evaluation metric is confusing.
- Datasets: The evaluation does not use the standard semantic relatedness benchmarks commonly employed in NLP. Exploring alternative datasets is fine, but please also report results on the standard benchmarks for comparability.
- Figure 1: I can only see two lines. Where is the third line mentioned in the caption/text?
- Related work: There is no direct, quantitative comparison to prior approaches; the paper offers only general claims without baseline numbers or head-to-head evaluations. This makes it difficult to assess the contribution.

Minor issue:
- Typo: “large extend” → “large extent.”
