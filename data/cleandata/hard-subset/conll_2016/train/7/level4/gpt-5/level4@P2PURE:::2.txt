The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) method for word embeddings: a random projection whose projection matrix has a positive expected value. The authors claim this allows the use of PPMI (which they argue is not applicable when the expected value is zero) and that, as a random projection approach, it is computationally efficient.

My main concern is the paper’s clarity, specifically:
1. I could not discern the key differences between the proposed approach and prior random projection methods. Consequently, it is unclear how—or whether—the claimed advantages actually follow.
2. The argumentation is difficult to follow from the introduction onward.
3. Several statements appear unsupported. For example:
   - Line 114: sentence begins with “In addition …”
   - Line 137: sentence begins with “Since …”
   - Line 154: sentence begins with “Thus …”
4. Although I have worked on vector space models, I am not a specialist in random projections and have not used them in my research. The paper does not make the underlying rationale of this research direction sufficiently clear. A paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with relevant background in the broader area.
5. There are numerous English errors (e.g., line 86: “To a large extend” → “extent”; line 142: “such PPMI”).

In addition, I do not see why the evaluation is limited to MEN. There are several standard benchmarks (MEN, WordSim, SimLex, and others). For a new method, evaluation on a single dataset is insufficient unless convincingly justified.

I recommend that the authors substantially improve the presentation and resubmit to another conference.
