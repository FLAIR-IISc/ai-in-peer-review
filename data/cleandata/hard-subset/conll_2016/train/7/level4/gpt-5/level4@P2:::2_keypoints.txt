- Paper proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse, nonnegative random projection matrices (E(R) > 0) allowing post-projection PPMI and efficient incremental embedding construction.
- Authors use Kendall’s τb in the unweighted projected space and report PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves competitive MEN scores (~0.75) with much lower compute.

Main contributions:
- Empirical finding: positive-only projections enable effective post-hoc PPMI at low dimensionality with competitive MEN performance and low cost.
- Practical recipe: fast, incremental embedding construction with parameter study over dimension m and sparsity s.
- Proposal to use Kendall’s τb for similarity measurement in the non-Gaussian projected (unweighted) space.

Strengths:
- High efficiency and simplicity: no expensive training, runs in seconds–minutes, suitable for large-scale or incremental use.
- Novel bridging of random indexing and count-based weighting by enabling PPMI after projection, reducing cost of full co-occurrence PPMI computation.
- Useful parameter exploration showing how m and s affect performance and variance.

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient clarity and novelty relative to prior random-projection methods (RI, RRI, ISA, RMI, feature hashing); formal differences are not clearly proven.
- Exposition is dense and sometimes meandering, making key ideas hard to follow for non-specialists.
- Several claims are asserted without derivations, evidence, or references.
- Evaluation is too narrow (only MEN); missing standard benchmarks and matched baselines (count+PPMI(+SVD), SGNS/CBOW, GloVe) and significance testing.
- Similarity-measure rationale and ablations (Kendall vs Pearson vs cosine before/after PPMI) are incomplete.
- Sensitivity and stability issues: performance varies with m and s and shows counterintuitive behaviors without clear guidance.
- Writing quality has grammatical errors and awkward phrasing that impede comprehension.

Questions / requests to authors:
- Provide a precise, formal contrast to RI/RRI/ISA/RMI and feature hashing and prove how E(R)=0 vs E(R)>0 affects PPMI denominators and expectations.
- Clarify whether PoP is equivalent to positive-only feature hashing or what essential difference exists.
- Expand evaluation to multiple benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, RG-65, RW, MTurk, SimVerb), matched preprocessing, and strong baselines with runtime/memory comparisons.
- Add ablations: PoP without PPMI (cosine vs Kendall), PPMI+SVD at matched dimensionality, PMI vs PPMI, and context/window/vocabulary effects; report variance and significance.
- Provide theoretical justification or error/concentration bounds (JL-style) for positive-only projections and dependence on m and s.
- Ensure reproducibility: release code, preprocessing details, hyperparameters, and seeds for review.
- Substantially rewrite the paper for clarity, early statement of contributions, and copyediting.

Overall recommendation:
- Promising and practically useful idea but currently lacks clarity, rigorous comparison to prior work, theoretical grounding, and broad evaluation; not recommended for acceptance in current form.
