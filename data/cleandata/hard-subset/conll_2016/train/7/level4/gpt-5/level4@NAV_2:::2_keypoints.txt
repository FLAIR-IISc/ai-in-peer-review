- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection with positive expected values, claimed to allow PPMI post hoc and to be computationally efficient.
- Major clarity issue: paper fails to clearly distinguish PoP from prior random projection methods, so claimed advantages are hard to assess.
- Overall argumentation is hard to follow starting from the introduction.
- Several specific claims are unsupported (lines 114, 137, 154) and need justification.
- Paper does not sufficiently motivate or explain the core logic for readers unfamiliar with random projections; it is not self-contained.
- Manuscript contains numerous English errors (examples: line 86, line 142).
- Evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset; additional standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim, SimLex) or a strong justification are required.
- Recommendation: substantially improve presentation, clarity, and evaluation before resubmission.
