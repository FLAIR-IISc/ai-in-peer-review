This paper presents a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding method. It is a random projection method with a random projection matrix whose expected value is positive. The authors argue that this enables the application of PPMI, which is not possible with an expected value of 0, and because it is a random projection method, their calculations are efficient.

My main objection to this paper relates to its clarity. Specifically:

1. I could not understand the fundamental difference between the method proposed in the paper and previous random projection methods. Therefore, I could not understand how the benefits claimed by the authors apply (and what they are).
2. The paper's arguments, starting from the introduction, were difficult to follow.
3. Some of the paper's arguments are not supported:
    - Line 114: The sentence begins with "in addition to".
    - Line 137: The sentence begins with "since".
    - Line 154: The sentence begins with "that".
4. While I have worked on vector space modeling (who hasn't?), I am not an expert in random projections and have not used them in my research. It was difficult for me to understand the reasoning behind this research path from the paper. I believe a paper should be self-contained and followable by people with some experience in the field.
5. There are many English errors in the paper (86: "to a large extend", 142: "search" PPMI).

Furthermore, I do not understand why the paper is only evaluating on MEN. There are some standard benchmarks (MEN, WordSeim, SimLex, and a few others) - if you present a new method, I think it is insufficient to evaluate only on one dataset unless you provide a good justification.

I recommend that the authors significantly improve the presentation in the paper and submit it again to another conference.
