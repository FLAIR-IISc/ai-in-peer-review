The paper introduces a positive-only projection (PoP) word embedding technique, a random projection method utilizing a random projection matrix with a positive expected value. The authors posit that this allows for the application of PPMI, which is incompatible with methods having an expected value of zero, while maintaining computational efficiency inherent to random projections.

However, my primary concern lies with the paper’s clarity. Specifically:

1. The fundamental distinction between the proposed method and existing random projection techniques remains unclear. Consequently, the claimed advantages of the approach are difficult to assess and their validity is uncertain.
2. The paper’s argumentation is difficult to follow, beginning with the introduction.
3. Several arguments lack logical connection and flow:
    - Line 114: The sentence begins with an unnecessary transitional phrase ("in addition").
    - Line 137: The sentence begins with an unnecessary transitional phrase ("Since").
    - Line 154: The sentence begins with an unnecessary transitional phrase ("thus").
4. While I possess experience in vector space modeling, I am not a specialist in random projections. The paper does not adequately contextualize the research motivation for those unfamiliar with the field. A strong paper should be self-contained and accessible to readers with relevant, but not necessarily specialized, expertise.
5. The manuscript contains numerous grammatical and typographical errors (e.g., Line 86: "To a large extend", Line 142: "such PPMI").

Furthermore, the evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset. Given the existence of established benchmark datasets (including MEN, WordSim, and SimLex, among others), evaluating on a single dataset feels insufficient without strong justification. A more comprehensive evaluation is expected for a novel method.

I recommend the authors significantly revise the paper’s presentation and consider resubmission to another venue.
