- Paper proposes a Positive-only Projection (PoP) random-projection word embedding whose projection matrix has a positive expected value, enabling PPMI use and claiming computational efficiency.  
- Main reservation: the paper lacks clarity overall.  
- Unclear how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and whether the claimed advantages hold.  
- Arguments are hard to follow from the introduction onward.  
- Specific unsupported/poorly framed statements noted (Line 114 starts with "in addition"; Line 137 starts with "Since"; Line 154 starts with "thus").  
- Paper is not self-contained for readers unfamiliar with random projections; rationale and logic are hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Numerous English language errors that hurt readability (examples: Line 86 "To a large extend", Line 142 "such PPMI").  
- Evaluation is insufficiently broad — only tested on MEN; should include other standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim, SimLex) or justify the single-dataset choice.  
- Overall recommendation: substantially improve presentation and clarity before resubmission.
