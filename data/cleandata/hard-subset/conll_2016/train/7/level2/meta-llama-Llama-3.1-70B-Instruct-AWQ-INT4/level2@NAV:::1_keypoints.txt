- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a randomized random-projection method that enables PPMI-style weighting to produce efficient word embeddings competitive with neural methods on MEN without training.
- Introduces PoP combining random projections with a projection matrix design that permits PPMI weighting.
- Demonstrates PoP achieves competitive performance on the MEN relatedness test (average ~0.75) versus neural embeddings.
- Analyzes the impact of PoP hyperparameters (index vector dimensionality, number of non-zero elements) on performance.
- PoP is an efficient, scalable randomized algorithm suitable for large datasets.
- PoP achieves competitive empirical performance on MEN compared to neural embedding techniques.
- PoP is flexible and allows application of weighting schemes like PPMI to resulting vectors.
- Paper lacks a detailed theoretical/mathematical justification of the PoP technique.
- PoP’s performance is sensitive to hyperparameters, requiring careful tuning.
- Evaluation is limited to the MEN relatedness test, so generalizability is uncertain.
- Request for a more detailed mathematical account and underlying assumptions of PoP.
- Question about plans to address PoP’s parameter sensitivity and practical implications.
- Request to evaluate PoP on additional tasks/datasets to demonstrate robustness and generalizability.
