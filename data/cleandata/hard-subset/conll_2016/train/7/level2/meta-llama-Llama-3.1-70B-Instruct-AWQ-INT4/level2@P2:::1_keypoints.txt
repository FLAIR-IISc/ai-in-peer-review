- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP) that enables applying weighting techniques to random projection-based models.  
- Demonstrates PoP combined with PPMI achieves competitive semantic similarity results comparable to neural models.  
- Proposes Kendall’s τb as a similarity measure for non-Gaussian vector distributions.  

- Novel, scalable, and computationally efficient approach to constructing semantic spaces and embeddings.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and parameter analysis.  
- Kendall’s τb provides a more robust alternative to cosine for non-Gaussian data.  

- Lacks detailed mathematical/theoretical analysis of PoP’s properties and limitations.  
- Heavy reliance on empirical results without sufficient theoretical grounding.  
- Insufficient comparison with and discussion of other random projection-based methods.  

- Request for a more detailed mathematical analysis of PoP, including properties and limitations.  
- Ask how derandomization will be addressed and its potential impact on performance.  
- Request a more comprehensive comparison with other random projection approaches and clarification of differences.  

- Well-written and clearly presented with helpful figures/tables and comprehensive references, but needs more theory and related-work discussion.
