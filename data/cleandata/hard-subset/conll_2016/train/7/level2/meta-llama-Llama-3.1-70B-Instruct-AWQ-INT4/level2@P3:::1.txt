**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. The PoP method is based on random projections and uses a transformation matrix with a positive expected value, allowing for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to the resulting vectors. The authors evaluate the performance of the PoP method using the MEN relatedness test set and the UKWaC corpus, and demonstrate that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the PoP method**: The authors propose a new technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, which is based on random projections and uses a transformation matrix with a positive expected value.
2. **Application of PPMI to PoP-constructed models**: The authors demonstrate that the PoP method allows for the application of weighting techniques such as PPMI to the resulting vectors, which enhances the performance of the model in semantic similarity assessment tasks.
3. **Evaluation of the PoP method**: The authors evaluate the performance of the PoP method using the MEN relatedness test set and the UKWaC corpus, and demonstrate that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques.

**Strengths**

1. **Scalability and efficiency**: The PoP method is highly scalable and computationally efficient, making it suitable for large-scale natural language processing tasks.
2. **Competitive performance**: The PoP method achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques, without the need for time-consuming training of neural nets.
3. **Flexibility**: The PoP method allows for the application of various weighting techniques, such as PPMI, to the resulting vectors, which enhances the performance of the model in semantic similarity assessment tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of theoretical justification**: The authors acknowledge that a detailed mathematical account for specifying the error caused by the randomized algorithm is not provided, which may be a limitation of the paper.
2. **Dependence on parameter settings**: The performance of the PoP method is influenced by several parameters, such as the dimensionality of the projected index vectors and the number of non-zero elements in index vectors, which may require careful tuning.
3. **Limited evaluation**: The evaluation of the PoP method is limited to a single dataset and task, which may not be representative of its performance in other settings.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the theoretical justification of the PoP method and its relationship to other random projection-based methods?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of parameter tuning in the PoP method, and what are the implications of different parameter settings on the performance of the model?
3. Can you provide more information on the computational resources required to train and evaluate the PoP method, and how it compares to other neural embedding techniques?
