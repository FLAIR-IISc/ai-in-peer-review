**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

The paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. The PoP method is based on random projections and uses a transformation matrix with a positive expected value (E(R) > 0). This allows for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to the resulting vectors. The authors claim that PoP-constructed models, combined with PPMI, achieve competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks, comparable to state-of-the-art neural network-based approaches.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. The introduction of the PoP method, which enables the application of weighting techniques to random projection-based models.
2. The demonstration of the effectiveness of PoP-constructed models in semantic similarity assessment tasks, particularly when combined with PPMI.
3. The proposal of a new similarity measure, Kendall's τb, for non-Gaussian distributions, which is used to compute similarities between vectors in PoP-constructed models.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, which is both scalable and computationally efficient.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the PoP method, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art approaches and an analysis of the method's parameters and their impact on performance.
3. The use of Kendall's τb as a similarity measure for non-Gaussian distributions is a valuable contribution, as it provides a more robust alternative to traditional measures such as cosine.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks a detailed mathematical account of the PoP method, including a theoretical analysis of its properties and limitations.
2. The authors rely heavily on empirical evaluations, which, while informative, do not provide a complete understanding of the method's behavior.
3. The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work, including a comparison with other random projection-based methods and an analysis of the differences between PoP and these approaches.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed mathematical analysis of the PoP method, including a discussion of its properties and limitations?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of derandomization in the PoP method, and what implications might this have for the method's performance?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive comparison with other random projection-based methods, including an analysis of the differences between PoP and these approaches?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the PoP method and its evaluation. The use of figures and tables to illustrate the results is helpful, and the references are comprehensive. However, as noted above, the paper could benefit from a more detailed mathematical analysis and a more comprehensive discussion of related work.
