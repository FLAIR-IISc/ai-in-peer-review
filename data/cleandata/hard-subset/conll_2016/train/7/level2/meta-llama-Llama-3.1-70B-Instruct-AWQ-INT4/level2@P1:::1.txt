**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

The paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. The PoP method is based on random projections, making it highly scalable and computationally efficient. The main contributions of this work are:

1. The proposal of a new random projection matrix R with E(R) > 0, which allows for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to the constructed semantic spaces.
2. The development of an incremental semantic space construction method that can be used to build models efficiently and with low computational resources.
3. The demonstration of the effectiveness of the PoP method in semantic similarity assessment tasks, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficient and Scalable**: The PoP method is highly efficient and scalable, making it suitable for large-scale natural language processing tasks.
2. **Flexibility**: The method allows for the application of various weighting techniques, such as PPMI, to the constructed semantic spaces, which can enhance the performance of the models.
3. **Competitive Results**: The PoP method achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques in semantic similarity assessment tasks, without the need for time-consuming training of neural nets.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: The paper lacks a detailed mathematical account for specifying the error caused by the randomized algorithm, which may raise concerns about the reliability of the method.
2. **Sensitivity to Parameters**: The performance of the PoP method is influenced by several parameters, such as the dimensionality of the projected index vectors and the number of non-zero elements in the index vectors, which may require careful tuning.
3. **Limited Evaluation**: The evaluation of the PoP method is limited to a single dataset (MEN relatedness test set) and a single task (semantic similarity assessment), which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the generalizability of the method.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the sensitivity of the PoP method to its parameters and how they affect the performance of the method?
2. How do you plan to address the lack of theoretical justification for the PoP method, and what future work do you propose to provide a more solid mathematical foundation for the method?
3. Can you provide more information about the computational resources required to build and train the PoP models, and how they compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
