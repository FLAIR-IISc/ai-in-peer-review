Summary: Introduces PoP — random projections with E(R)>0 that allow weighting (e.g., PPMI); claims competitive accuracy (0.75 on MEN) with much lower compute than neural methods.

Contribution 1: Proposes a novel random projection method enabling application of weighting techniques like PPMI to random-projection semantic spaces.

Contribution 2: Shows much faster model construction (seconds/minutes) versus neural methods (hours/days) while achieving competitive performance.

Contribution 3: Provides theoretical justification for using Kendall’s τb as a similarity measure in non-Gaussian spaces.

Strength 1: Identifies and addresses a real limitation (E(R)=0 preventing PPMI use) with a simple, well-motivated solution.

Strength 2: Compelling claim of computational efficiency supported by incremental construction and low dimensionality arguments.

Strength 3: Clear empirical comparisons showing PoP outperforms RI and count-based models across context windows; PoP+PPMI reaches 0.75 on MEN.

Strength 4: Sound and well-explained theoretical rationale for Kendall’s τb in these spaces.

Weakness 1: Novelty claim is weak — lacks comprehensive comparison to other random-projection variants to prove PoP’s uniqueness.

Weakness 2: Insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art embeddings (e.g., GloVe, fastText, PPMI+SVD) to contextualize the 0.75 MEN score.

Weakness 3: Computational-efficiency claims lack concrete runtime and memory measurements against baselines.

Weakness 4: Insufficient detail on how PPMI is applied to PoP vectors and quantitative support for PPMI being faster on low-dimensional PoP vectors.

Question 1: Provide a detailed comparison of PoP to other random-projection methods (RI, RRI, ISA, RMI), especially regarding E(R)>0 usage.

Question 2: Supply actual runtime and memory measurements for PoP versus methods like GloVe, fastText, and PPMI+SVD on the same hardware.

Question 3: Include a comparison table showing PoP+PPMI on MEN alongside recent state-of-the-art results (e.g., Chen & de Melo 2015, Tsvetkov et al. 2015).

Question 4: Elaborate the PPMI implementation on PoP vectors and explain how PoP’s low dimensionality affects PPMI computation.

Overall: Interesting and potentially useful technique with solid core idea and promising efficiency, but needs stronger novelty claims, comprehensive SOTA comparisons, and concrete empirical evidence of efficiency.
