- Summary: Proposes PoP — random projections with E(R)>0 to allow PPMI weighting, reporting 0.75 MEN with much lower compute.  
- Contribution 1: Novel random projection (PoP) enabling weighting techniques like PPMI on resulting semantic spaces.  
- Contribution 2: Efficient semantic space construction achieving competitive performance without neural network training.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical demonstration that PoP+PPMI matches state-of-the-art performance while being computationally much more efficient.  
- Strength 1: Correctly identifies limitation of E(R)=0 in prior random projection methods and offers a clear fix (E(R)>0).  
- Strength 2: Strong evidence for major computational efficiency gains (models built in seconds/minutes vs hours/days).  
- Strength 3: Thorough empirical validation across parameters showing consistent improvement over RI and count-based models, especially with PPMI.  
- Strength 4: Method is well-motivated by addressing heavy-tailed language distributions, a known challenge in distributional semantics.  
- Weakness 1: Novelty appears overstated — using E(R)>0 is a simple change and paper fails to contextualize differences from prior positive/sparse RPs.  
- Weakness 2: Performance claims lack comparison to contemporary neural methods (e.g., GloVe, word2vec), so SOTA claim is not well supported.  
- Weakness 3: Theoretical justification is weak; authors do not provide error bounds or formal analysis grounded in JL-type results.  
- Weakness 4: Choice of similarity measure (Kendall's τb vs Pearson) is not well justified and lacks empirical comparison.  
- Question 1: How does PoP+PPMI compare to GloVe and word2vec on MEN and other benchmarks?  
- Question 2: Provide exact MEN scores and parameter settings for the state-of-the-art comparisons cited.  
- Question 3: What is the theoretical basis for PoP preserving the original distribution (Figure 3d) and how does this differ from other random projection methods?
