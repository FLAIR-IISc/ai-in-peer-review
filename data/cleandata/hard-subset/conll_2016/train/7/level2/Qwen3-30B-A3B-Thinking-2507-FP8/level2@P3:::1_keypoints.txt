- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection technique with positive expectation (E(R) > 0) that permits application of weighting methods like PPMI.  
- Main contributions: PoP enabling PPMI, competitive empirical performance (0.75 on MEN) with much lower computation, and analysis of parameter effects.  
- Identifies and addresses a clear limitation of existing random projection methods (E(R)=0 prevents PPMI).  
- Computational efficiency claim is well-supported: models built in seconds/minutes instead of hours/days.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing PoP to Random Indexing and count-based models across many parameter settings with consistent improvements.  
- Choice of Kendall’s τb over cosine is well-motivated given non-Gaussian PoP spaces.  
- Demonstrates that PoP+PPMI can reach performance comparable to neural methods, a meaningful practical result.  
- Theoretical justification for why E(R)>0 specifically enables the PPMI calculation is underdeveloped and needs clearer mathematical explanation.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art neural methods is limited and lacks direct, up-to-date baselines to substantiate “comparable performance” claims.  
- The drop in performance for unweighted PoP models above 2000 dimensions is counterintuitive and requires deeper analysis.  
- Reproducibility details are insufficient: randomness handling, run variance, and experimental seeds are not fully reported despite averaging over 10 runs.  
- The claim of parity with top-performing algorithms is unsubstantiated without specific recent scores for direct comparison.  
- Requests for authors: provide detailed comparisons to recent (2015–2016) neural methods, full fine-tuning procedures that produced 0.78, and clarification on how PoP compares to methods achieving 0.80+ on MEN.
