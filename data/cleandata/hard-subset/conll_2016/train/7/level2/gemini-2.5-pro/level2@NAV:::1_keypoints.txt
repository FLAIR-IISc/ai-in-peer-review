- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection with positive-expected entries that preserves non-negative count vectors.  
- Enables applying PPMI weighting directly to low-dimensional random projections, previously infeasible.  
- Shows PoP+PPMI is extremely fast and attains competitive performance on the MEN semantic relatedness benchmark.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces PoP to bridge efficient random projections and discriminative weighting like PPMI.  
- Contribution 2: Empirically demonstrates a computationally cheap method that competes with more expensive models.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and novelty—simple, insightful fix to a known limitation of random indexing.  
- Strength: Thorough, well-structured empirical analysis exploring dimensionality and sparsity effects.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written with well-explained intuition and experimental rationale.  
- Weakness: Lacks formal theoretical analysis (no error bounds or JL-type guarantees).  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a single intrinsic semantic relatedness task, leaving downstream utility untested.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to neural state-of-the-art are indirect (benchmarks from other papers rather than head-to-head on the same corpus).
