- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP): random projections with non-negative entries (E(R) > 0) enabling PPMI-style weighting in low-dimensional space.
- Contribution: a novel random projection method (PoP) that permits post-hoc weighting like PPMI on projected vectors.
- Contribution: an efficient, incremental pipeline combining PoP and PPMI to produce competitive embeddings much faster than high-dimensional alternatives.
- Contribution: empirical analysis of similarity metrics showing Kendall’s τb suits raw PoP spaces while Pearson works better after PPMI.
- Strength: conceptually elegant idea that bridges efficient random projections and discriminative PPMI weighting.
- Strength: strong practical utility—makes PPMI computations tractable on large corpora by projecting to low dimensions.
- Strength: solid empirical results on MEN (Spearman’s ρ ≈ 0.75) with thorough hyperparameter analysis and informative visualizations.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical grounding or guarantees (no Johnson–Lindenstrauss–style analysis) making it feel heuristic.
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope—only one task/dataset (MEN), no similarity, analogy, or downstream task tests.
- Weakness: indirect comparison to neural baselines—no controlled training of word2vec/GloVe on the same corpus for fair comparison.
- Weakness: unclear, non-principled guidance for hyperparameter tuning (dimensionality, sparsity) due to complex interactions.
- Question: clarify SVD results—was SVD applied to full high-dimensional PPMI or to low-dimensional PoP+PPMI, and could that explain negligible improvements?
- Question: provide more theoretical intuition or connections to non-Gaussian random projections and why PoP preserves information for PPMI.
