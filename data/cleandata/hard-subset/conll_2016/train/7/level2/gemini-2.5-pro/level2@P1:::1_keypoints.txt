- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with non-negative projection matrix allowing post-projection weighting like PPMI on low-dimensional vectors.  
- Claims PoP+PPMI yields competitive embeddings (Spearman 0.75 on MEN) while being much more computationally efficient than traditional PPMI+SVD or neural methods.  
- Contributions: novel PoP compatible with post-projection weighting, an incremental scalable pipeline that defers PPMI until after dimensionality reduction, and strong empirical results with analysis of similarity measures.  
- Strength: simple, novel idea—making the projection matrix positive-only enables PPMI on compressed vectors and addresses a limitation of Random Indexing.  
- Strength: computationally efficient and scalable—single-pass incremental construction and fast PPMI on reduced vectors avoids SVD/training bottlenecks.  
- Strength: well-designed experiments and analyses (hyperparameters, similarity metrics) showing clear gains over raw counts and RI.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical grounding—no formal analysis of what PoP preserves (unlike Johnson–Lindenstrauss guarantees for Gaussian projections).  
- Weakness: limited evaluation—only tested on MEN/relatedness, missing broader benchmarks like WordSim, SimLex, or analogy tasks.  
- Weakness: no direct controlled comparison to neural embeddings (e.g., SGNS trained on same corpus) despite motivating efficiency vs. neural methods.  
- Question: why does PoP+PPMI performance degrade as index-vector sparsity decreases—could uncontrolled distortions or loss of orthogonality explain this?  
- Question: why choose the b 1Uα c distribution with α=0.5 for non-zero entries—was this empirically tuned and were other distributions tested?  
- Question: request for an intuitive explanation of what PoP preserves (e.g., long-tailed distribution) and how that relates to viewing PoP as randomized feature sampling and using Kendall’s τb.
