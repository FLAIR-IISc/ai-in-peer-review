- Summary: Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with non-negative entries enabling post-projection PPMI weighting, yielding efficient low-dimensional embeddings competitive on MEN.  
- Contribution 1: Novel random projection that produces positive-expected-value vectors, making post-projection weighting (e.g., PPMI) feasible.  
- Contribution 2: Efficient, scalable PoP+PPMI pipeline that avoids large PPMI matrix factorization and lengthy neural training while maintaining competitive performance.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical analysis recommending similarity measures (Kendall's τb for raw PoP, Pearson after PPMI) based on vector distributional properties.  
- Strength 1: High computational efficiency and simplicity—enables PPMI on compact vectors, drastically reducing time and memory.  
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results with clear stepwise improvements and competitive MEN performance (ρ ≈ 0.75).  
- Strength 3: Clear exposition and insightful analyses (e.g., histograms explaining why PoP enables PPMI).  
- Strength 4: Novel and practically significant—fills gap between efficient RI and expensive PPMI/SVD or neural embeddings.  
- Weakness 1: Lacks formal theoretical grounding or distortion analysis analogous to Johnson–Lindenstrauss results.  
- Weakness 2: Limited evaluation—only tested on MEN and one corpus, missing broader intrinsic and downstream tasks.  
- Weakness 3: Ambiguity in hyperparameter selection (dimensionality m and sparsity s) with interactions that need clearer heuristics.  
- Question 1: Request concrete wall-clock time and memory comparisons between PoP+PPMI and baselines (SVD on PPMI, word2vec) on UKWaC.  
- Question 2: Ask for quantitative evidence that PPMI makes vectors “Gaussian-like” (e.g., normality tests like Shapiro–Wilk) to justify Pearson use.  
- Question 3: Inquire whether PoP has been tested as low-dimensional input to neural embedding methods to accelerate or improve neural training.
