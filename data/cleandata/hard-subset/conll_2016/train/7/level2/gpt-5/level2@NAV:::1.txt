Summary: The paper proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for constructing word semantic spaces using sparse, nonnegative projection matrices (E[R] > 0). This design purportedly enables post-hoc weighting (PPMI) on low-dimensional projected representations, and uses Kendall’s τb for similarity in the raw non-Gaussian space. On MEN, PoP+PPMI+Pearson reaches ~0.75 (up to 0.78 with tuning), while unweighted PoP exceeds RI and count-based baselines.

Contributions (as I see them, strongest first):
1) A nonnegative randomized projection scheme that preserves compatibility with PPMI after projection, enabling efficient weighting in low dimensions.
2) An empirical study showing PoP (with/without PPMI) outperforming RI and count-based baselines on MEN, with parameter analyses over m and sparsity.
3) The observation that Kendall’s τb is suitable pre-weighting, while Pearson’s r is superior post-PPMI.

Strengths:
- Practical efficiency angle: Moving weighting to a low-dimensional space is compelling and could reduce the cost of classic PPMI pipelines substantially; the approach is incremental/streaming-friendly.
- Clear empirical comparisons to count-based and RI baselines, with consistent gains for PoP, and sensible analyses of parameters (m, number of nonzeros, variance across runs).
- Sensible similarity choices: τb for non-Gaussian raw PoP and Pearson after PPMI are justified and supported empirically.
- Accessibility: Uses public corpora and benchmarks; method description is simple enough to reimplement.

Weaknesses:
- Limited evaluation scope: Only MEN is reported; no tests on SimLex-999, WordSim353, RareWords, or downstream tasks. No significance testing or ablations isolating PPMI vs projection effects.
- Efficiency claims lack quantitative evidence: No runtime/memory benchmarks versus count+PPMI+SVD or neural baselines.
- Comparative omissions: No comparison to feature hashing/nonnegative hashing + PPMI, SGNS/GloVe trained on the same corpus and settings, or SVD-truncated PPMI at similar dimensions.
- The probabilistic meaning of PPMI on projected pseudo-counts is under-theorized; while numerically valid, its relation to true co-occurrence probabilities is unclear.
- The claim that RI cannot support PPMI because row/column sums are zero needs clarification/qualification.

Questions to authors:
- Can you report wall-clock time, peak memory, and throughput versus (a) dense PPMI, (b) hashing+PPMI, (c) SGNS/GloVe on UKWaC?
- How does PoP+PPMI compare to PPMI+SVD at matched dimensions and compute budgets?
- Is PPMI-after-projection theoretically related to PPMI-before-projection? Any bounds on distortion or consistency?
- Can you evaluate on additional relatedness/similarity sets and a downstream task to validate robustness?
