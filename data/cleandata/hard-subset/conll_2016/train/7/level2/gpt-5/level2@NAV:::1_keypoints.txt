- Summary: PoP uses sparse, nonnegative random projections to enable post-hoc PPMI weighting in low-dimensional word spaces, uses Kendall’s τb pre-weighting, and achieves ~0.75–0.78 on MEN while outperforming RI and count baselines.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a nonnegative randomized projection scheme compatible with PPMI, enabling efficient weighting in low dimensions.

- Contribution 2: Empirical study shows PoP (with/without PPMI) outperforms RI and count-based baselines on MEN, with parameter analyses over dimensionality and sparsity.

- Contribution 3: Finds Kendall’s τb effective for raw PoP and Pearson’s r superior after applying PPMI.

- Strength: Efficiency/engineering angle—moving weighting to low-dim space is practical, incremental, and streaming-friendly.

- Strength: Clear empirical comparisons and sensible parameter analyses (m, nonzeros, run variance).

- Strength: Similarity choices are justified and supported empirically.

- Strength: Reproducible setup using public corpora and a simple-to-implement method.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation—only MEN reported; no other intrinsic sets, downstream tasks, significance tests, or ablations isolating effects.

- Weakness: Efficiency claims lack quantitative runtime/memory/throughput benchmarks against dense PPMI, hashing, or neural methods.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons to feature hashing/nonnegative hashing+PPMI, SGNS/GloVe on same data, and truncated PPMI+SVD at matched dimensions.

- Weakness: Theoretical grounding of applying PPMI to projected pseudo-counts is underdeveloped; relation to true co-occurrence probabilities is unclear.

- Weakness: Claim that RI cannot support PPMI due to zero row/column sums requires clarification.

- Question: Please report wall-clock time, peak memory, and throughput versus dense PPMI, hashing+PPMI, and SGNS/GloVe on UKWaC.

- Question: How does PoP+PPMI compare to PPMI+SVD at matched dimensions and compute budgets?

- Question: Is there a theoretical relation or bounds connecting PPMI-before-projection and PPMI-after-projection?

- Question: Can you evaluate on additional similarity/relatedness sets and at least one downstream task to validate robustness?
