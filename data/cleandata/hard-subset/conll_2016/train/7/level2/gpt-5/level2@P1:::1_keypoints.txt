- Proposal: Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse random projection with non-negative projection vectors enabling PPMI after projection and using Kendall’s τb pre-PPMI, Pearson post-PPMI.
- Two methodological twists: enforce E[R]>0 for projections and switch similarity metric from Kendall’s τb (non-Gaussian) to Pearson’s r after PPMI (more Gaussian).
- Incremental, lightweight construction akin to random indexing, claimed faster to build and suitable for streaming updates.
- Main contribution 1: Empirical claim that PoP+PPMI+Pearson attains competitive relatedness (≈0.75 Spearman on MEN) at much lower cost than dense PPMI or neural embeddings.
- Main contribution 2: Careful empirical study of hyperparameters (projection dimensionality m, sparsity k) and similarity choices, reporting variance across random seeds.
- Main contribution 3: Practical incremental scheme with reported superiority over standard Random Indexing on MEN.
- Strength: Simple, practical, and easy-to-implement method requiring no training and supporting streaming/incremental updates.
- Strength: Solid empirical gains over RI and count baselines; stability analyzed across 10 runs.
- Strength: Reasoned similarity choices (Kendall’s τb pre-PPMI, Pearson post-PPMI) supported by distributional evidence.
- Strength: Parameter exploration provides guidance on m and nonzero counts and highlights collision risks.
- Strength: Clear motivation linking heavy-tailed counts to the need for weighting and enabling PPMI after projection.
- Weakness: Evaluation is narrow—results only on MEN with UKWaC windows, lacking other benchmarks, downstream tasks, and statistical tests.
- Weakness: Novelty unclear relative to feature hashing / Count-Min Sketch; lacks discussion/comparison to those methods and known error bounds.
- Weakness: Thin theoretical analysis—no bounds or formal characterization of error/bias from projection/collisions.
- Weakness: Reproducibility and methodological clarity issues (e.g., exact sampling/discretization of tij, choice of s, complexity of τb, interpretation of PPMI marginals).
- Weakness: Potential tuning/overfitting concerns—many hyperparameters appear tuned on MEN without held-out development or significance reporting.
- Key question: How does PoP differ from feature hashing/CMS and can baselines using those sketches + PPMI be added?
- Key question: Precisely how are tij sampled/discretized (range, α, expected k) and were alternatives tried?
- Key question: How should marginals over m pseudo-features be interpreted and how large is bias from collisions on PMI/PPMI?
- Key question: Can authors report wall-clock time and memory comparisons (build PPMI, SVD) vs dense PPMI and SGNS on same hardware/corpus?
- Key question: How scalable is Kendall’s τb for large vocabularies and were faster similarity approximations or NN methods considered?
- Overall assessment: Promising, practical approach with useful empirical insights but requires broader evaluation, clearer ties to sketching literature, and stronger theoretical/reproducibility support.
