- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP): a sparse, non-negative random projection enabling PPMI weighting in a low-dimensional compressed space.  
- Argues Kendall’s τb is appropriate for raw PoP similarities and Pearson/cosine after PPMI due to distributional shape changes.  
- Empirical claim: PoP outperforms RI and counts on MEN; PoP+PPMI+Pearson reaches ~0.75 Spearman’s ρ with sensitivity analyses over m and sparsity s.  

- Strength: PoP retains random-indexing benefits (streamable, simple, fast) and offers a practical way to apply PPMI without dense high-dimensional matrices.  
- Strength: Experiments include multiple runs and ablations (m, non-zeros, similarity measures) showing informative trends and variance reduction with larger m.  
- Strength: Provides clear intuition linking representation distributions to choice of similarity metric.  
- Strength: Method is simple, training-free, and useful as a compact preprocessing sketch.  

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to MEN and lacks head-to-head comparisons with strong baselines trained on the same corpus (SGNS, GloVe, PPMI+SVD, HPCA).  
- Weakness: Omits comparisons and citations to established sketching/hashing techniques (Count-Min Sketch, feature hashing) that are conceptually similar.  
- Weakness: The statistical/semantic meaning of PPMI after random aggregation is unclear; no analysis of bias/variance from collisions or parameter effects (α, s).  
- Weakness: Kendall’s τb is computationally expensive (O(m log m) per pair); paper lacks timing, retrieval, and ANN strategy for large vocabularies.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical grounding; some questionable statements (e.g., RI row/column sums exactly zero) and unexplained choices for weight distribution (α=0.5).  

- Question: Compare PoP empirically/conceptually to Count-Min Sketch/feature hashing + PPMI (and SVD); justify the chosen random weight distribution vs binary hashing.  
- Question: Can you formalize bias in PMI estimates due to random collisions and motivate α=0.5 or other weight choices?  
- Question: Provide wall-clock time and memory for count+PPMI, RI, PoP, and PoP+PPMI (including similarity search) on the same hardware/corpus.  
- Question: How does PoP generalize to other benchmarks (WS353, SimLex-999, RW, SimVerb-3500), analogy tasks, or downstream applications?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to preprocessing (stopword removal, frequency cutoffs) and to using syntactic contexts?  

- Suggestion: Add stronger baselines on the same corpus (SGNS, GloVe, PPMI+SVD, HPCA) with significance tests across runs.  
- Suggestion: Clarify mathematical properties of the projection matrix (expectation, variance, collision probability) and relate to JL-type bounds where possible.  
- Suggestion: Ablate PPMI-after-projection vs PPMI-before-projection-then-project to isolate effects.  
- Suggestion: Report cosine vs Pearson for PoP+PPMI and adopt standard ANN methods for scalable similarity search.  
- Suggestion: Release code and exact hyperparameter/settings (m, s, α, context defs) for reproducibility.  

- Overall: Idea is practical and promising but requires broader empirical comparisons to sketching and word-embedding baselines, clearer theoretical justification, and runtime/retrieval scalability evidence.
