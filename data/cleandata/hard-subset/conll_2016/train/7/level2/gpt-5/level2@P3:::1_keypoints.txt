- Contribution (method): Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), using nonnegative, sparse random projection vectors to enable post-hoc PPMI weighting in low-dimensional space.  
- Contribution (empirical): Shows PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves MEN ρ ≈ 0.75–0.787, is incremental, faster than training neural embeddings, and includes parameter studies (m, s, nonzeros).  
- Contribution (similarity rationale): Argues PoP’s raw coordinates are non-Gaussian (use Kendall’s τb) while PPMI makes coordinates approximately Gaussian (justify Pearson’s r).  

- Strength: Elegant, simple, and practical idea—E[R] > 0 resolves RI’s incompatibility with PMI/PPMI and enables cheap discriminative weighting.  
- Strength: Solid empirical improvements over RI and raw counts on MEN, with averaging over runs and variance analysis.  
- Strength: Useful parameter analysis offering actionable defaults (e.g., 1–2 nonzeros for PPMI, larger m reduces variance).  
- Strength: Coherent statistical perspective that PPMI acts as a variance-stabilizing transform, supported by histograms and experiments.  
- Strength: Efficient and incremental; computes PPMI on small m (100–5000) rather than full context space.  

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MEN on UKWaC; lacks other intrinsic benchmarks, downstream tasks, and controlled comparisons to SGNS/CBOW/GloVe/PPMI+SVD on the same data.  
- Weakness: Theoretical foundations are thin—no error bound or conditions when projected PPMI approximates count-space PPMI.  
- Weakness: Claim that RI yields row/column sums “always 0” (making PPMI undefined) is too strong and needs refinement or empirical support.  
- Weakness: Scalability of Kendall’s τb (O(m log m) per pair) vs. cosine (O(m)) is not benchmarked; no wall-clock or memory comparisons.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity about PPMI semantics after projection—mixing of context features and probabilistic interpretability is under-discussed.  

- Question: Clarify Eq. 2 — what is the exact distribution for tij, why α = 0.5, sensitivity to α, and are integer magnitudes essential?  
- Question: Provide controlled baselines on the same corpus (SGNS, CBOW, GloVe, PPMI+SVD) and compare scores and resources with matched settings.  
- Question: Report runtime and peak memory for model building and similarity computation (PoP, PoP+PPMI, RI, count+PPMI, SGNS) for representative vocabularies and m.  
- Question: Justify or refine the RI/PPMI claim — are row/column sums provably zero under common RI settings, or is the issue numerical instability?  
- Question: Compare similarity measures (cosine, Spearman) in both raw PoP and post-PPMI regimes and discuss accuracy/latency trade-offs for retrieval.  

- Essential suggestion: Expand evaluation to additional intrinsic benchmarks and at least one downstream task, and add controlled comparisons to SGNS/CBOW/GloVe and count+PPMI(+SVD) on the same corpus.  
- Essential suggestion: Add efficiency benchmarks (build time, query time, memory) and analyze τb vs. cosine scalability.  
- Essential suggestion: Strengthen or empirically demonstrate the RI vs. PPMI argument and clarify PPMI interpretation after projection.  
- Optional suggestion: Provide partial theoretical analysis (moments, variance vs. m/s, links to feature hashing/JL) and evaluate robustness on another corpus (e.g., Wikipedia).  
- Optional suggestion: Release code and exact preprocessing to facilitate replication.  

- Overall assessment: Simple, novel, and practically appealing method with clear gains over RI, but empirical scope is too narrow to claim parity with state-of-the-art; broader evaluation, controlled baselines, and clarified claims would make the contribution stronger.
