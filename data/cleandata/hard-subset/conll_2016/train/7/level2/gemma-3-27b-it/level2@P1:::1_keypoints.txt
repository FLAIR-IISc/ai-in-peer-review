- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP): use random projection matrices with positive expectation to enable post-projection PPMI weighting.
- Demonstrates PoP+PPMI achieves competitive results on the MEN relatedness test with high computational efficiency.
- Primary contribution: novel random projection technique (PoP) that allows post-projection weighting like PPMI.
- Contribution: empirical validation showing PoP+PPMI performs comparably to state-of-the-art on MEN.
- Contribution: extensive parameter analysis (dimensionality, non-zero elements) offering practical guidance.
- Strength: novel and insightful idea bridging random projections and discriminative weighting.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation with comparisons and reporting of averages and ranges.
- Strength: clear, detailed methodological explanation and comparison to other projection techniques.
- Strength: practical implications due to computational efficiency versus neural embeddings.
- Strength: valuable parameter exploration for practitioners.
- Weakness: evaluation scope is limited to MEN; lacks diverse tasks/datasets (e.g., similarity, analogy).
- Weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical justification and explicit error bounds; connection to Johnson–Lindenstrauss needs clarity.
- Weakness: sensitivity to parameter settings may require extensive tuning; more guidance needed.
- Weakness: comparisons should include more recent/stronger baselines (newer neural and count-based methods).
- Weakness: switching from Kendall’s τb to Pearson’s r post-PPMI feels ad-hoc and needs stronger justification.
- Question: plan for formal theoretical analysis, error bounds, and convergence properties?
- Question: guidance on selecting optimal parameters (dimensionality, sparsity) for different datasets/tasks?
- Question: detailed computational cost breakdown compared to neural embeddings and SVD methods?
- Question: will optimal parameters vary significantly across domains/corpora and how to adapt them?
- Question: have other weighting schemes besides PPMI been tried, and how do they perform?
- Additional: paper is well-written and includes useful supplemental material.
- Additional: fix minor grammatical issues for clarity.
- Additional: providing example code or a public implementation would be beneficial.
