- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection method using matrices with E(R) > 0 to enable weighting like PPMI.  
- Demonstrates PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance on the MEN relatedness test while being much more efficient than neural embeddings.  
- Provides detailed empirical analysis of parameters (dimensionality, sparsity/non-zero elements) and method behavior.  
- Novelty: E(R) > 0 random projections allow application of weighting schemes, addressing limitations of RI/RRI.  
- Efficiency/scalability: Claims models can be built in seconds/minutes vs hours/days for neural methods, useful for resource-constrained settings.  
- Empirical validation: Results on MEN and comparisons to established methods lend credibility to the approach.  
- Clear explanation and motivation: Well-structured connection to distributional semantics and benefits of weighting.  
- Parameter analysis: Thorough exploration of sparsity, orthogonality, and their impact on performance.  
- Limited evaluation: Experiments confined to MEN; needs broader semantic tasks and downstream evaluations.  
- Limited baseline comparison: Requires comparisons to other random-projection methods with weighting to isolate the effect of E(R) > 0.  
- Theoretical justification lacking: Needs more rigorous mathematical analysis of PoP’s properties and relation to JL-style guarantees.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Performance appears sensitive to s (non-zero elements); guidance for parameter selection across datasets is missing.  
- Presentation issues: Figures (esp. Fig.1 and Fig.4) need clearer axis labels, captions, and consistent visuals.  
- Question on metrics: Have alternative similarity measures for non-Gaussian distributions been explored beyond Kendall’s τb and Pearson’s r?  
- Question on derandomization: Can derandomization techniques improve PoP and are there any preliminary results?  
- Question on α: Was the weighting parameter α (fixed at 0.5) tuned or tested for sensitivity?  
- Question on scalability: How does PoP performance and behavior degrade with very large vocabularies?  
- Question on PPMI cost: What is the computational cost of building PPMI for PoP vs traditional count-based models — please quantify?  
- Citation note: Referencing Jurafsky & Martin (2015) for PMI explanation seems unusual.  
- Clarification needed: The phrase "P6⊥" in Figure 6’s caption is unclear and should be explained.  
- Overall: PoP is a promising, efficient approach with competitive results but requires broader evaluation and stronger theoretical support to fully establish its contribution.
