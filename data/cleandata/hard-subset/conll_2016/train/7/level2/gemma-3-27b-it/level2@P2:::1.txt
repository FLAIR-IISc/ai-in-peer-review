## Review of "Random Positive-Only Projections: PPMI-Enabled Incremental Semantic Space Construction"

**Summary:** This paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel method for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings based on random projections. Unlike traditional random projection methods that use matrices with an expected value of 0, PoP utilizes matrices with a positive expected value, enabling the application of weighting techniques like PPMI after projection. The authors demonstrate that combining PoP with PPMI achieves competitive performance on the MEN relatedness test, comparable to state-of-the-art algorithms, but with significantly improved computational efficiency.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Novel Projection Technique (PoP):** The core contribution is the introduction of the PoP method itself – utilizing random projections with a positive expected value. This is a conceptually simple yet effective modification to existing random projection techniques.
2. **PPMI Compatibility:** Demonstrating that PoP enables the application of PPMI weighting *after* the projection step is a significant improvement. This allows for leveraging the benefits of both random projection efficiency and the discriminative power of PPMI weighting, something previously impossible with standard random projection methods.
3. **Competitive Performance with Efficiency:**  Achieving results on par with state-of-the-art methods on the MEN test, coupled with a substantial reduction in computational cost, is a valuable contribution. The speed of model construction (seconds/minutes vs. hours/days) is a particularly compelling aspect.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation & Problem Statement:** The paper effectively articulates the limitations of existing methods (count-based, standard random projections, and neural embeddings) and positions PoP as a solution that addresses these drawbacks – namely, scalability, efficiency, and the inability to combine random projections with weighting techniques.
2. **Sound Technical Approach:** The mathematical formulation of PoP is clear and well-explained.  The reasoning behind using a positive expected value in the projection matrix to enable PPMI weighting is logical and compelling.  The use of Kendall’s τb for initial similarity measurements is also appropriately justified given the non-Gaussian nature of the projected spaces.
3. **Empirical Validation:** The paper provides empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of PoP. The comparison with RI and the demonstration of comparable performance to more complex methods on the MEN test provide strong support for the proposed approach. The inclusion of experiments varying dimensionality and non-zero element counts adds depth to the analysis.
4. **Detailed Analysis & Parameter Exploration:**  The extensive exploration of PoP's parameters (dimensionality, number of non-zero elements) and the discussion of their impact on performance are valuable.  Figures 4, 5, and 6 provide insightful visualizations of this analysis. The discussion regarding the trade-offs between sparsity and orthogonality is particularly strong.
5. **Practical Significance:** The computational efficiency of PoP is a significant practical advantage, making it suitable for large-scale applications where training time and resource constraints are major concerns.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** While the MEN test is a standard benchmark, evaluating the method on a wider range of semantic similarity tasks (e.g., word analogy, semantic relatedness datasets beyond MEN) would strengthen the claims of generalizability.  Evaluating performance on downstream NLP tasks (e.g., text classification, sentiment analysis) would demonstrate the practical utility of the generated embeddings.
2. **Theoretical Justification:** The paper acknowledges the lack of a strong theoretical foundation for PoP. While providing empirical justification is valuable, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the projection’s properties (e.g., distortion bounds, error analysis) would significantly enhance the work. Exploring connections to related work in random projection theory (e.g., Johnson-Lindenstrauss lemma) would be beneficial.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The discussion of parameter sensitivity (s, dimensionality) is thorough, but it also reveals a potential fragility of the method. Optimal performance appears to be quite dependent on parameter tuning. A more robust method that is less sensitive to parameter choices would be desirable.
4. **Clarity of Notation:** While generally clear, the notation could be improved in places. For instance, clarifying the meaning of 's' and its relation to the distribution of 'tij' in Equation 2 more explicitly would enhance readability.
5. **Presentation:** The paper is rather long and contains some repetitive phrasing. Condensing the text and streamlining the presentation would improve its overall impact. The numerous references to previous works throughout the text sometimes disrupt the flow of argumentation.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Generalization:** Have you investigated the performance of PoP embeddings on tasks beyond semantic relatedness, such as word analogy or downstream NLP applications? If not, what are the expected challenges in applying PoP to these tasks?
2. **Theoretical Analysis:** Are you planning to pursue a more rigorous theoretical analysis of PoP? Specifically, can you provide any preliminary thoughts or ongoing work regarding distortion bounds or error analysis for the projection?
3. **Parameter Tuning:** Do you have any recommendations or heuristics for selecting the optimal values of 's' and dimensionality 'm' for different datasets or tasks? Could an automated parameter tuning approach be used?
4. **Orthogonality:** The discussion of non-orthogonality and its impact on performance is intriguing. Could you elaborate on how the level of non-orthogonality affects the quality of the resulting embeddings?

**Additional Comments:**

* The supplemental material mentioned at the end of the paper would be extremely helpful to reviewers and the research community.
* The authors mention using the Apache Commons Mathematics Library for the Kendall’s τb implementation. Including the specific version number would be good practice.
* A brief comparison of PoP’s performance to other fast embedding methods (e.g., FastText) would provide valuable context.



Overall, this is a strong and promising paper that presents a novel and effective approach to semantic space construction. The combination of theoretical justification, empirical validation, and detailed analysis makes a compelling case for the benefits of PoP. Addressing the identified weaknesses would further strengthen the paper and solidify its contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
