- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel random projection method with E(R) > 0 enabling post-projection weighting like PPMI.  
- PoP achieves competitive results on the MEN relatedness benchmark, comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency by applying PPMI in low-dimensional projected spaces.  
- Empirical evaluation includes ablation studies on dimensionality and sparsity, showing parameter effects on performance.  
- Method is clearly explained with helpful mathematical formulation and design rationale.  
- Lacks a complete theoretical justification linking PoP to existing random projection theory.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single benchmark (MEN), reducing evidence of generality across tasks/datasets.  
- Performance is sensitive to parameters (s and dimensionality) without principled tuning guidance.  
- Initial justification for using Kendall’s τb is weak and needs stronger explanation.  
- Questions for authors: clarify theoretical connections to RP theory, report results on additional semantic benchmarks, and propose automatic strategies to tune the 's' parameter.  
- Overall recommendation: promising contribution worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to address theory, broader evaluation, and parameter selection guidance.
