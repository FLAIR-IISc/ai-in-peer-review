- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection method with positive expected values enabling post-projection weighting.  
- PoP allows applying PPMI after projection, combining random projection efficiency with PPMI’s discriminative power.  
- PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance on the MEN relatedness test while dramatically reducing computation time.  

- Clear motivation: addresses scalability, efficiency, and inability to weight standard random projections.  
- Sound technical approach: well-explained math and logical rationale for positive expected values; Kendall’s τb choice justified.  
- Empirical validation: comparisons with RI and state-of-the-art on MEN support effectiveness.  
- Thorough parameter exploration: analyzes effects of dimensionality and sparsity with informative visualizations and trade-off discussion.  
- Practical significance: much faster model construction suitable for large-scale applications.  

- Limited evaluation scope: only MEN tested; lacks broader semantic tasks and downstream NLP evaluations.  
- Weak theoretical foundation: needs rigorous analysis (distortion bounds, error analysis, links to JL lemma).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on careful tuning of s and m, indicating potential fragility.  
- Notation clarity issues: some symbols (e.g., s and tij) need clearer definitions.  
- Presentation length/repetition: paper is long, sometimes repetitive, and references can disrupt flow.  

- Have you tested PoP on other tasks (word analogy, downstream NLP) and what challenges do you expect?  
- Any plans or preliminary work on theoretical analysis (distortion bounds, error analysis)?  
- Recommendations or heuristics for choosing s and m, or automated tuning strategies?  
- Can you expand on how non-orthogonality levels affect embedding quality?  

- Supplemental material would be useful for reproducibility.  
- Specify the version of Apache Commons Math used for Kendall’s τb.  
- Compare PoP’s performance to other fast embedding methods (e.g., FastText) for context.  

- Overall recommendation: strong, promising paper with acceptance suggested pending addressing weaknesses.
