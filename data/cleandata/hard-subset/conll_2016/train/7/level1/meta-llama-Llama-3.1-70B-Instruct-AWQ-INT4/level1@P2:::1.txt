**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a novel technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings called Positive-Only Projection (PoP). The PoP method is based on random projections, making it highly scalable and computationally efficient. Unlike previous methods that use random projection matrices with an expected value of 0, the proposed method uses matrices with an expected value greater than 0. This allows for the application of weighting methods such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to PoP-constructed spaces. The authors evaluate their method using the MEN relatedness test set and demonstrate that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the PoP method**: The authors propose a new technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings that is based on random projections with an expected value greater than 0.
2. **Application of PPMI to PoP-constructed spaces**: The authors demonstrate that PoP-constructed spaces can be weighted using PPMI, which enhances the discriminatory power of the models and improves their performance in semantic similarity assessment tasks.
3. **Evaluation of the PoP method**: The authors evaluate their method using the MEN relatedness test set and demonstrate that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Strengths**

1. **Scalability and efficiency**: The PoP method is based on random projections, making it highly scalable and computationally efficient.
2. **Flexibility**: The PoP method allows for the application of weighting methods such as PPMI to PoP-constructed spaces, which enhances the discriminatory power of the models.
3. **Competitive performance**: The authors demonstrate that the PoP method achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art algorithms in semantic similarity assessment tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of theoretical justification**: The authors do not provide a detailed mathematical account for the PoP method, which may limit its applicability and interpretability.
2. **Randomized nature**: The PoP method is a randomized algorithm, which may result in different results for the same input and may require careful tuning of parameters.
3. **Dependence on parameter settings**: The performance of the PoP method depends on the choice of parameters such as the dimensionality of the projected index vectors and the number of non-zero elements in index vectors.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the theoretical justification of the PoP method and how it relates to other random projection-based methods?
2. How do you plan to address the randomized nature of the PoP method and ensure that it produces consistent results for the same input?
3. Can you provide more insights on how to choose the optimal parameters for the PoP method, such as the dimensionality of the projected index vectors and the number of non-zero elements in index vectors?

**Additional Comments**

The authors provide a clear and concise description of the PoP method and its evaluation. However, some minor typos and formatting issues were found in the paper. Additionally, the authors may consider providing more details on the implementation of the PoP method and the experimental setup to facilitate reproducibility.
