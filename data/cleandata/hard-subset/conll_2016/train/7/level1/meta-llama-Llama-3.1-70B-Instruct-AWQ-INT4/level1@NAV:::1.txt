**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP is based on random projections and uses a transformation matrix with a positive expected value, allowing for the application of weighting methods such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors evaluate the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set and show that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques, but with much lower computational resources.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the PoP method**: The authors propose a new technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, which is based on random projections and uses a transformation matrix with a positive expected value.
2. **Application of PPMI weighting to PoP-constructed models**: The authors show that PoP-constructed models can be weighted using PPMI, which enhances their performance in semantic similarity assessment tasks.
3. **Evaluation of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set**: The authors evaluate the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set and show that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficient construction of semantic spaces**: PoP is a highly scalable and computationally efficient method for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings.
2. **Competitive performance**: PoP achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art neural embedding techniques on the MEN relatedness test set.
3. **Flexibility**: PoP-constructed models can be weighted using PPMI, which enhances their performance in semantic similarity assessment tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of theoretical justification**: The authors do not provide a detailed mathematical account for the PoP method, which may limit its applicability and interpretability.
2. **Randomized algorithm**: PoP is a randomized algorithm, which may lead to variability in its performance and results.
3. **Dependence on parameter settings**: The performance of PoP is influenced by several parameters, such as the dimensionality of the projected index vectors and the number of non-zero elements in index vectors.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed mathematical account for the PoP method, including its theoretical justification and bounds for the error caused by replacing deterministic decisions with random ones?
2. How do you plan to address the variability in the performance of PoP due to its randomized nature?
3. Can you provide more insights into the optimal values for the parameters of PoP, such as the dimensionality of the projected index vectors and the number of non-zero elements in index vectors?
