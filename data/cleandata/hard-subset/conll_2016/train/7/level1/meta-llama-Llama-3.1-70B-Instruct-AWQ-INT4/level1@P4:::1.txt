**Summary**

This paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. The PoP method is based on random projections and uses a transformation matrix with a non-zero expected value, allowing for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors evaluate the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test and demonstrate that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art methods, including neural network-based approaches.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel random projection technique**: The authors propose a new random projection method, PoP, which uses a transformation matrix with a non-zero expected value, enabling the application of weighting techniques such as PPMI.
2. **Efficient and scalable semantic space construction**: PoP is shown to be highly scalable and computationally efficient, allowing for the construction of semantic spaces in a matter of seconds or minutes.
3. **Competitive performance on semantic similarity tasks**: The authors demonstrate that PoP achieves competitive results on the MEN relatedness test, comparable to state-of-the-art methods, including neural network-based approaches.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficient and scalable**: PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient, making it a promising approach for large-scale semantic space construction.
2. **Flexibility in weighting techniques**: The use of a non-zero expected value transformation matrix allows for the application of various weighting techniques, including PPMI.
3. **Competitive performance**: PoP achieves competitive results on the MEN relatedness test, demonstrating its effectiveness in semantic similarity tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of theoretical justification**: The authors acknowledge that a detailed mathematical account for specifying the error caused by the random projection is not provided, which may raise concerns about the method's theoretical soundness.
2. **Randomized algorithm**: PoP is a randomized algorithm, which may lead to variations in performance across different runs.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors note that the choice of hyperparameters, such as the dimensionality of the index vectors and the number of non-zero elements, can significantly impact the performance of PoP.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more insights into the theoretical justification of the PoP method, including the expected error caused by the random projection?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of hyperparameter tuning in PoP, and what are the implications of different hyperparameter settings on the method's performance?
3. Can you discuss the potential applications of PoP beyond semantic similarity tasks, such as in other natural language processing tasks or domains?
