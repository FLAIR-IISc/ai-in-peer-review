**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a novel technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, called Positive-Only Projection (PoP). The PoP method is based on random projections and is designed to be highly scalable and computationally efficient. Unlike previous methods that use random projection matrices with an expected value of 0, the PoP method uses matrices with an expected value greater than 0. This allows for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to the resulting vectors. The authors evaluate the performance of the PoP method using the MEN relatedness test set and demonstrate that it achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art methods.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the PoP method**: The authors propose a new technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, which is based on random projections and allows for the application of weighting techniques such as PPMI.
2. **Evaluation of the PoP method**: The authors evaluate the performance of the PoP method using the MEN relatedness test set and demonstrate its competitiveness with state-of-the-art methods.
3. **Analysis of the PoP method's parameters and random behavior**: The authors study the performance of the PoP method under different parameter settings and analyze its random behavior.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficiency**: The PoP method is highly scalable and computationally efficient, making it a promising approach for large-scale natural language processing tasks.
2. **Competitive performance**: The authors demonstrate that the PoP method achieves competitive results compared to state-of-the-art methods, making it a viable alternative for semantic similarity assessment tasks.
3. **Flexibility**: The PoP method allows for the application of weighting techniques such as PPMI, which can enhance its performance in certain tasks.
4. **Theoretical analysis**: The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the PoP method's parameters and random behavior, which can help to improve its performance and robustness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of theoretical justification**: The authors acknowledge that they do not provide a detailed mathematical account for specifying the error caused by the PoP method, which can make it difficult to understand its limitations and potential biases.
2. **Dependence on hyperparameters**: The performance of the PoP method depends on the choice of hyperparameters, such as the dimensionality of the index vectors and the number of non-zero elements. The authors do not provide a clear guideline for choosing these hyperparameters, which can make it difficult to replicate their results.
3. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate the performance of the PoP method using the MEN relatedness test set, which may not be representative of all semantic similarity assessment tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the PoP method's error and its dependence on the dimensionality of the index vectors and the number of non-zero elements?
2. How do you plan to address the lack of theoretical justification for the PoP method, and what are the potential implications of this limitation?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the PoP method using multiple datasets and tasks, and how do you plan to extend its evaluation to other areas of natural language processing?
