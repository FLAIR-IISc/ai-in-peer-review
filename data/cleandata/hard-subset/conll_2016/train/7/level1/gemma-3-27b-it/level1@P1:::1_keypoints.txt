- Summary: Proposes PoP, a random projection with E(R) > 0 that allows post-projection weighting (PPMI), yielding competitive MEN scores with much lower computational cost.

- Contribution: Introduces PoP, enabling application of weighting schemes after dimensionality reduction by using a projection matrix with positive expected value.

- Contribution: Demonstrates PoP+PPMI matches state-of-the-art relatedness performance on MEN while being orders of magnitude faster.

- Contribution: Provides empirical exploration of key parameters (dimensionality m and sparsity s) and their effects on performance.

- Strength: Novel idea that bridges random projections and count-based weighting, overcoming a limitation of standard random projection methods.

- Strength: Strong empirical validation on MEN with ablation studies that support the method’s effectiveness.

- Strength: Major computational efficiency advantages over neural and SVD-based embedding methods, useful for large-scale or resource-limited settings.

- Strength: Detailed parameter analysis (m, s) with helpful visualizations (e.g., weight distributions) offering practical guidance.

- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with sufficient background and technical explanation.

- Weakness: Lacks formal theoretical justification or bounds on error (δ), limiting assessment of robustness and generalizability.

- Weakness: Evaluation scope is narrow, focused mainly on MEN; needs broader benchmarks and downstream task tests.

- Weakness: Heavy focus on PPMI — limited exploration of PoP with other weighting schemes or without weighting.

- Weakness: Discussion of non-orthogonality and its impact feels disconnected and could better explain how s manages this effect.

- Question: What are the authors’ plans for theoretical analysis and error bounds for PoP?

- Question: Have the authors tested PoP on other semantic similarity datasets or downstream NLP tasks?

- Question: Were alternative weighting schemes explored besides PPMI, and what were the outcomes?

- Question: How does corpus size affect optimal parameters m and s, and is there a relationship between corpus size and required dimensionality?

- Question: Can the authors suggest methods to explicitly control index-vector orthogonality beyond tuning s?

- Additional comment: Supplemental material is appreciated and references are comprehensive.

- Additional comment: Clarify notation (e.g., “P6⊥”) and consider adding an explicit limitations section.
