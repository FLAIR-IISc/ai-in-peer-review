- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection method with a positive bias enabling subsequent weighting transforms.
- Core idea: use a random projection matrix with positive expected value to make PPMI-like weighting compatible with random projections.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on the MEN relatedness test, validating the approach empirically.

- PoP enables applying PPMI weighting to projected spaces, achieving results comparable to state-of-the-art methods.
- Extensive experiments explore dimensionality and the 's' parameter, providing practical guidance and insights into method behavior.
- Empirical results show PoP is faster than neural embeddings and SVD-weighted count models while maintaining comparable performance.
- Analysis includes investigation of sparsity, orthogonality of index vectors, and trade-offs between sparsity and performance.
- Clear, well-structured presentation with appropriate mathematical notation and explanation of evaluation metrics.
- Authors acknowledge the lack of formal theoretical error bounds and the need for further theoretical analysis.

- Evaluation limited to a single dataset (MEN), reducing confidence in generalizability to other tasks.
- Missing comparisons to simpler baselines (e.g., count-based embeddings with PCA) to contextualize benefits of PoP.
- Some sections are repetitive and overly detailed, hurting readability.
- The role and intuition of the 's' parameter need clearer, more intuitive explanation.

- Have you evaluated PoP on other downstream NLP tasks (e.g., analogy, classification)?
- How does PoP’s performance scale with corpus size; are there diminishing returns?
- Which alternative similarity measures beyond Pearson and Kendall’s τb were tested and which worked best after PPMI?
- Can you provide a formal computational complexity comparison of PoP versus RI, SVD, and neural embeddings?

- Figure 3 effectively visualizes PPMI’s effect; public implementation availability is valuable.
- Clarify that shaded areas in Figure 4 represent variance, not absolute worst/best outcomes.
- Emphasize the publicly available implementation in the paper.

- Overall: strong, innovative paper with solid empirical validation and practical value; recommend strong accept pending addressing noted weaknesses.
