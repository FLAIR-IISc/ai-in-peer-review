- PoP introduces positive-only random projections with non-zero expectation to enable post-projection weighting like PPMI.  
- Shifts random projection focus from distance preservation to compatibility with weighting techniques.  
- PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance on the MEN relatedness benchmark while being computationally efficient.  
- Empirical exploration of parameters (dimensionality m and sparsity s) provides practical guidance for applying PoP.  
- Clearly identifies and addresses the limitation of traditional random projections being incompatible with post-projection weighting.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation with comparisons to RI and count-based models and ablation studies.  
- Clear, mathematically grounded description linking PoP to PPMI and discussing Kendall’s τb distance.  
- Demonstrated efficiency makes PoP appealing for resource-constrained settings (faster model construction vs. neural embeddings).  
- Thoughtful discussion of randomness, sources of variance, and connections to Johnson-Lindenstrauss concepts.  
- Evaluation is limited mainly to the MEN dataset, reducing evidence of generalizability across tasks.  
- Results lack formal statistical significance testing to confirm advantages over baselines.  
- Section 2.3 is overly detailed and somewhat tangential, with δ justifications deferred to future work.  
- Parameter sensitivity: optimal s and m vary by dataset/task and recommendations appear inconsistent.  
- Comparisons omit recent strong embedding methods (e.g., fastText, GloVe), limiting contemporary benchmarking.  
- Question: How does PoP perform on other semantic similarity datasets (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim-353)?  
- Question: Can the authors provide statistical significance tests (e.g., t-tests) for performance claims?  
- Question: Request for practical guidance or principled methods to choose s and m for new datasets/tasks.  
- Question: Ask for concrete computational cost estimates (training time) across parameter settings and dataset sizes.  
- Question: How does PoP compare to fastText/GloVe in both performance and computational cost?  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with useful supplemental material.  
- Recommend shortening Section 2.3 and making the Kendall’s τb discussion more concise.
