- Paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with positive-mean matrices enabling post-projection weighting like PPMI and claiming efficiency comparable to neural embeddings.  
- Experiments on MEN show competitive performance when PoP is combined with PPMI and evaluated with Kendall’s τb or Pearson correlation.

- Main contribution: using positive-expected-value random projections to allow discriminative post-projection weighting.  
- Main contribution: demonstrating that PPMI can be successfully applied to PoP-constructed semantic spaces.  
- Main contribution: thorough empirical exploration of parameters (dimensionality, sparsity) and similarity measures.

- Strength: PoP is novel and potentially impactful, offering computationally cheap alternatives to neural embeddings.  
- Strength: Method and math are clearly explained, with a good rationale for choices like Kendall’s τb.  
- Strength: Experiments and ablation studies are thorough and informative.  
- Strength: Practical relevance due to speed advantages for resource-constrained settings.  
- Strength: Authors honestly discuss limitations and parameter impacts.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to stronger non-neural baselines (e.g., fastText, large-scale word2vec).  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to MEN and UKWaC, reducing claims of generalizability.  
- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical justification and missing rigorous error bounds for PoP; parameter ‘s’ guidance is hand-wavy.  
- Weakness: Overemphasis on PPMI and similarity measures sometimes overshadows the core PoP contribution.  
- Weakness: Some figures (e.g., Figure 3) are hard to interpret due to small fonts and unclear labels.

- Q: Have you compared PoP+PPMI to fastText or other strong non-neural baselines trained on large corpora?  
- Q: Have you evaluated PoP on datasets beyond MEN (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim353), and if not, do you expect differences?  
- Q: What efforts are underway to provide rigorous theoretical justification, error bounds, and characterization of PoP spaces?  
- Q: Can you provide practical guidelines for selecting parameter ‘s’ based on corpus characteristics (sparsity, vocab size)?

- Additional: Paper is well-written but could use minor edits for clarity and conciseness.  
- Additional: Supplemental Material should explicitly state contents (code, pre-trained embeddings).  
- Additional: The link between Kendall’s τb choice and non-Gaussian projected distributions is good but could be expanded.

- Overall: Novel, well-explained, and promising approach with strong practical value; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen baselines and theoretical discussion.
