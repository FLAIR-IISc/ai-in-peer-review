- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection method with E(R) > 0 enabling PPMI weighting of projected spaces.  
- Claims PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves competitive performance (0.75 on MEN) with much lower computational cost than neural embeddings.  
- Contribution: PoP method that allows PPMI on projected spaces.  
- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that PoP+PPMI+Pearson outperforms unweighted PoP and traditional RI.  
- Contribution: Explanation of why traditional random projections (E(R)=0) cannot use PPMI.  
- Strength: Practical computational advantage—PoP+PPMI is much faster than neural training while retaining competitive performance.  
- Strength: Clear technical insight on the incompatibility of zero-mean random projections with PPMI.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation across window sizes, dimensionality, sparsity and effective visualizations.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification and formal error bounds (δ) for the PoP method.  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons with modern state-of-the-art neural embedding methods (e.g., GloVe, FastText, transformers).  
- Weakness: Weak justification for using Kendall’s τb over cosine similarity beyond the authors’ experiments.  
- Weakness: Insufficient scalability analysis and no concrete runtime measurements across corpus sizes.  
- Weakness: No ablation studies to isolate the contributions of PoP, PPMI, and the similarity measure.  
- Question: Provide runtime comparisons of PoP+PPMI vs neural training (e.g., GloVe) on the same hardware and corpus.  
- Question: How does PoP perform on other semantic tasks (word analogy, STS) beyond MEN relatedness?  
- Question: Provide computational complexity analysis comparing PPMI on count-based models vs PoP-constructed models.  
- Question: Supply specific comparisons with recent state-of-the-art methods (post-2015) to support the “comparable” claim.  
- Question: Compare PoP+PPMI+Pearson at m ≥ 3000 to standard PPMI+SVD/GloVe baselines at similar dimensionality.
