- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection method with E(R) > 0 that enables application of weighting techniques like PPMI to projected spaces.  
- PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance on the MEN relatedness test (0.75 average) comparable to neural methods while requiring far less computation.  
- Provides analysis of PoP behavior across parameters (dimensionality, sparsity), showing fast model construction (seconds/minutes).  
- Novel technical insight: PoP overcomes limitation of prior random projections (E(R)=0) that made PPMI inapplicable.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive comparisons and ablations demonstrate PoP+PPMI+Pearson outperforms count-based models and RI.  
- Computational efficiency: PoP enables much faster PPMI transformation and model building than high-dimensional count-based models.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification and formal error bounds (δ) for PoP's performance.  
- Weakness: Limited benchmarking against recent state-of-the-art neural methods (e.g., ELMo, BERT) and broader count-based baselines.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous parameter selection; recommendations (e.g., s > 0.002, 1–2 non-zero elements) are not strongly justified or systematically optimized.  
- Question: Which specific parameter settings produced the reported 0.75 MEN score and how do they compare to the 0.78 fine-tuned settings?  
- Question: What are the time/memory costs for building models at m=3000 versus smaller dimensions, and how does scaling behave with corpus size?  
- Question: What concrete NLP tasks beyond semantic relatedness demonstrably benefit from PoP?  
- Additional comment: Using Kendall’s τb for similarity in non-Gaussian spaces is reasonable but would benefit from more citations and stronger theoretical grounding.
