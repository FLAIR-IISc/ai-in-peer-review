- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP): random projections with positive elements (E(R)>0) to build semantic spaces.
- Main contribution: enables PPMI weighting on projected spaces, which previous zero-centered random projections could not support.
- PoP+PPMI achieves 0.75 on MEN, claimed competitive with neural methods while requiring far less computation.
- Empirical analysis of PoP parameters showing how dimensionality and sparsity affect performance.
- Shows PoP+PPMI produces Gaussian-like distributions suitable for Pearson correlation.
- Strength: strong computational-efficiency claim — PoP models can be built in seconds/minutes vs hours/days for neural embeddings.
- Strength: clear theoretical justification for why PoP enables PPMI, addressing a prior limitation.
- Strength: empirical results outperform Random Indexing (RI) and count-based models, and are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical foundations and formal error bounds (δ) for the random projection method.
- Weakness: the 0.75 MEN score is not compared against recent SOTA contextual embeddings (e.g., transformers), limiting assessment of competitiveness.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to other efficient, high-performing methods like SGNS, GloVe, or FastText, weakening the unique value claim.
- Question: how does PoP+PPMI compare to FastText or SGNS on standard benchmarks?
- Question: what exact parameters produced the 0.75 MEN score to enable reproducibility?
- Question: what is the runtime comparison between PoP+PPMI and state-of-the-art neural methods on the same dataset size?
