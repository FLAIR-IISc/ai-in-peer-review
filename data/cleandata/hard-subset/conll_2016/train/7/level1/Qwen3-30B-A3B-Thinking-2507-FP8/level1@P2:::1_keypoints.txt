- Paper introduces PoP: random projections with E(R)>0 to enable PPMI weighting on projected semantic spaces, claiming competitive accuracy with much faster computation.  
- Contribution: novel PoP method that allows application of weighting techniques like PPMI to projected spaces.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration that PoP+PPMI yields competitive semantic relatedness (0.75 on MEN) with dramatically lower compute time.  
- Contribution: analysis of PoP vector distributional properties and compatibility with PPMI.  
- Strength: Addresses a genuine limitation of existing random projection methods (E(R)=0) and shows PoP permits PPMI where others cannot.  
- Strength: Strong, practically significant empirical results showing PoP+PPMI outperforms count-based models and RI, with large speed advantages.  
- Strength: Clear explanation of why PoP enables PPMI and appropriate use of Kendall’s τb for non-Gaussian PoP spaces, supported by distributional analyses.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification and formal error bounds (δ) for PoP; no JL-style guarantees presented.  
- Weakness: Insufficient direct comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., fastText, GloVe, other 2015–2016 neural methods).  
- Weakness: Inadequate justification/evidence for choice of similarity measure (Kendall’s τb) over alternatives for untransformed PoP spaces.  
- Question: What are the authors’ plans for providing theoretical justification and error bounds for PoP?  
- Question: Can the authors provide direct comparisons to recent SOTA (e.g., fastText, GloVe) to substantiate competitiveness claims?  
- Question: Can the authors provide evidence that Kendall’s τb is superior for untransformed PoP spaces, rather than an arbitrary choice?  
- Overall: Novel, practical, and efficient approach with valuable insight (E(R)>0 → PPMI), but needs stronger theoretical grounding and SOTA comparisons; recommend rejection with major revisions, reconsider upon addressing weaknesses.
