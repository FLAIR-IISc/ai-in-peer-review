- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with positive-expected entries enabling post-projection weighting like PPMI.  
- Shows PoP+PPMI achieves competitive semantic similarity performance (Spearman 0.75 on MEN) with lower computational cost than neural embeddings.  
- Provides empirical analysis of PoP parameters (dimensionality, sparsity, weighting) and their impact on performance.  
- Highly scalable and computationally efficient, suitable for resource-constrained settings.  
- Compatible with weighting techniques, bridging random projections and count-based models.  
- Strong empirical validation with comparisons to RI and count-based models and detailed parameter studies.  
- Methodology and implementation are clearly explained and accessible.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis or bounds on the randomization error δ, limiting interpretability.  
- Evaluation is limited (primarily MEN); additional benchmarks and downstream tasks are needed.  
- Performance is sensitive to parameter choices with insufficient practical guidance for selection.  
- No direct comparisons to specific neural models (e.g., Word2Vec, GloVe) on the same datasets.  
- Requests theoretical bounds for δ and comparisons to RI.  
- Asks for broader evaluation on other benchmarks and downstream NLP tasks.  
- Inquires about integration with neural embeddings (initialization/preprocessing) and choice of Kendall’s τb versus alternatives.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing theoretical justification and expanding evaluation.
