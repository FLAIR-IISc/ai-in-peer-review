- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP): a random projection with non-negative entries that allows PPMI weighting on low-dimensional vectors.  
- PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance on MEN relatedness (0.75) while being significantly more computationally efficient.  
- Contribution: a random projection method that produces low-dim vectors amenable to post-hoc weighting, bridging random indexing and PPMI.  
- Contribution: demonstrates a cheap two-stage pipeline that rivals resource-intensive methods (SVD on PPMI, neural nets).  
- Strength: Novel and practical—simple, effective solution to a known limitation of existing random projection methods.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation with analysis of parameters (dimensionality, sparsity) that aids practical use.  
- Strength: Clear exposition and sound methodological choices (e.g., appropriate similarity measures).  
- Weakness: Lacks formal theoretical grounding/error bounds comparable to Gaussian projection theory.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—only one intrinsic English task (MEN); no downstream or multilingual tests.  
- Weakness: Indirect comparison to SOTA—no direct baseline training (e.g., fastText/GloVe) on the same corpus for a fair efficiency-performance comparison.
