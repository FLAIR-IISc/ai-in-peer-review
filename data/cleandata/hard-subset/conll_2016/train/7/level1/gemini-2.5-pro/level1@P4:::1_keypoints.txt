- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a sparse positive random projection enabling low-dimensional incremental word embeddings.
- PoP's positive-valued projections allow applying PPMI weighting directly in the reduced space, unlike standard Random Indexing.
- Authors recommend Kendall’s τb for raw PoP (non-Gaussian) and find Pearson’s r better after PPMI makes distributions more Gaussian-like.
- PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance (0.75 on MEN) while being significantly more computationally efficient than SVD or neural methods.
- Main contribution: a simple positive-only random projection that enables post-projection weighting like PPMI.
- Main contribution: an efficient, scalable PoP+PPMI framework combining random projection speed with PPMI discriminative power.
- Main contribution: comprehensive empirical analysis on parameter effects, baselines, and similarity metrics.
- Strength: high novelty and practical significance by bridging random projections and PPMI weighting.
- Strength: strong empirical results and informative ablation/visualization analyses supporting claims.
- Strength: clear writing, reproducible methodology, and sensible choice of similarity measures tied to vector distributions.
- Weakness: lacks formal theoretical analysis or guarantees about what PoP preserves compared to JL-style projections.
- Weakness: evaluation is restricted to the single MEN relatedness benchmark, limiting claims of generality.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., sparsity s and α) with no principled selection strategy provided.
- Question: does the low optimal s imply PoP functions mainly as a low-dimensional sketch/feature hash rather than preserving geometry?
- Question: have you tested PoP+PPMI on other tasks (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim-353, analogies) to assess generalization beyond relatedness?
- Question: why choose Kendall’s τb for raw PoP, and how does cosine similarity compare to τb on unweighted PoP vectors in your experiments?
