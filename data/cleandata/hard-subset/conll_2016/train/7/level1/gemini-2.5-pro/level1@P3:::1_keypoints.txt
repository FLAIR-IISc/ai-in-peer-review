- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with non-negative entries that permits PPMI weighting on low-dimensional vectors.
- Shows PoP+PPMI matches state-of-the-art relatedness performance (MEN) while being far more computationally efficient.

Contributions:
- Introduces a simple way to combine random projections with PPMI by enforcing non-negativity in the projection matrix.
- Empirically demonstrates an efficient, incremental embedding that achieves competitive Spearman ρ on MEN without expensive SVD or neural training.

Strengths:
- Core idea is novel and conceptually significant, bridging scalable random projections with discriminative PPMI weighting.
- Excellent performance-efficiency trade-off: strong MEN results at a fraction of the computation of SVD/word2vec/GloVe.
- Well-structured empirical analysis, including appropriate baselines, distributional analyses, and hyperparameter studies.

Weaknesses:
- Lacks theoretical grounding: no formal characterization of what geometric/statistical properties PoP preserves.
- Evaluation limited to a single task (MEN), leaving generality to other semantic tasks untested.
- Insufficient comparison with non-negative methods (e.g., NMF) that also produce additive, interpretable non-negative embeddings.

Questions for authors:
- Are PPMI marginals computed from the low-dimensional PoP matrix or from the original high-dimensional co-occurrence counts, and what is the impact of that choice?
- Why does PPMI-weighting benefit from extremely sparse index vectors while unweighted models prefer denser projections?
- Have you tested PoP+PPMI on analogy or synonym tasks (e.g., Google analogies, TOEFL) to assess relational/linear properties?
