- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with non-negative projection matrix enabling PPMI weighting on low-dimensional vectors and achieving competitive MEN results.

- Main contribution: introduces PoP to make low-dimensional vectors amenable to count-based weighting (PPMI), bridging random projections and explicit weighting.

- Main contribution: demonstrates an efficient PoP+PPMI pipeline that builds embeddings much faster than SVD or neural methods while yielding strong semantic accuracy.

- Main contribution: analyzes similarity measures, recommending Kendall’s τb for raw PoP and Pearson after PPMI when distributions become more Gaussian-like.

- Strength: simple, novel, and effective idea—positive-only projections unlock PPMI in reduced space and conceptually unite two modeling families.

- Strength: major computational efficiency—incremental construction and low-dim PPMI avoid expensive sparse PPMI+SVD or long neural training.

- Strength: strong empirical results (Spearman ρ=0.75 on MEN) with useful ablations on dimensionality and index sparsity.

- Strength: clear exposition and helpful visualizations (e.g., weight distributions) that clarify the method.

- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification or formal analysis explaining why positive projections preserve semantic structure.

- Weakness: limited evaluation scope—only MEN word relatedness tested, so generalization to other tasks or downstream uses is unknown.

- Weakness: insufficient comparisons to strong, well-tuned baselines (e.g., word2vec/SGNS and PPMI+SVD) on the same data/preprocessing.

- Question: why choose the specific projection distribution (U^-0.5) and how do simpler positive distributions (binary, Poisson) compare?

- Question: how does computing high-dimensional PPMI then applying standard zero-mean random projection compare to PoP-then-PPMI in performance and cost?

- Question: why does unweighted PoP degrade beyond 2000 dims and how does the interaction between dimensionality and sparsity (s) affect stability and tuning?
