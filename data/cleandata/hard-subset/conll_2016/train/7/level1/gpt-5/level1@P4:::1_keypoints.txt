- PoP is a sparse random projection with nonnegative entries and positive mean that allows applying PPMI after projection to build low-dimensional semantic spaces.
- The paper uses Kendall’s τb for raw PoP (non-Gaussian) and Pearson after log/PPMI (more Gaussian), reporting PoP+PPMI+Pearson ≈0.75–0.78 ρ on MEN with UKWaC.

- Contribution: Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP) to combine random-projection efficiency with post-hoc PPMI weighting at low dimensionality.
- Contribution: Empirically shows PoP > RI ≈ raw counts on MEN and that PoP+PPMI+Pearson approaches strong PMI/PPMI baselines, with parameter sensitivity analyses.
- Contribution: Proposes matching similarity measures to induced distributions—τb for unweighted PoP and Pearson after PPMI.

- Strength: Simple, well-motivated modification of RI that enables PPMI on projected vectors.
- Strength: Preserves random-projection benefits (streaming construction, small m, no expensive factorization), making scalability claims plausible.
- Strength: Demonstrates empirical effectiveness and stability trends (PoP gains and reduced variance with larger m), reaching ~0.75 Spearman on MEN.
- Strength: Thoughtful justification for using τb in non-Gaussian projected spaces and switching to Pearson post-PPMI.
- Strength: Uses public datasets (UKWaC, MEN) and promises code, supporting reproducibility.

- Weakness: No theoretical derivation or distortion/error bounds for PoP; Eq.(2) is insufficiently specified.
- Weakness: Evaluation is narrow (only MEN/UKWaC) with no matched comparisons to strong baselines (SGNS, GloVe, SPPMI+SVD) or significance testing.
- Weakness: Efficiency claims lack empirical runtime, memory, or throughput measurements versus alternatives.
- Weakness: Overstates RI’s incompatibility with PPMI (mischaracterizes the zero-sum argument) and ignores simpler fixes like shifting/rectifying RI.
- Weakness: Notational/typesetting ambiguities and unclear probabilistic meaning of PPMI on projected non-count values hinder reproducibility.

- Q: Precisely specify Eq.(2) for tij, its distribution, expected value/variance, and how α and s are chosen to ensure nonzeros per row.
- Q: Provide wall-clock time and memory for PoP construction, τb/PPMI computation, and querying versus (a) count+PPMI(+SVD) and (b) SGNS/GloVe with matched vocab/window.
- Q: Report MEN (and ideally SimLex-999, WordSim-353) results for SGNS, GloVe, and SPPMI+SVD with matched preprocessing and significance tests versus PoP+PPMI.
- Q: Clarify the probabilistic interpretation of PPMI on PoP matrices and whether PPMI(PoP(C)) can be related or bounded relative to PPMI(C).
- Q: Compare PoP empirically to simple RI modifications (e.g., rectifying/shifting to nonnegative) to rule out these baselines.
- Q: Provide empirical comparisons of τb vs cosine vs Pearson on unweighted PoP across multiple datasets beyond the distributional argument.
