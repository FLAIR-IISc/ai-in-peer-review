- PoP: a positive-only random-projection scheme that yields nonnegative compressed co-occurrence counts, enabling PPMI weighting after projection.  
- Recommendation: use Kendall’s τb for raw PoP (non-Gaussian) and Pearson after PPMI (more Gaussian-like).  
- Empirical claim: PoP outperforms count-based and RI baselines unweighted and PoP+PPMI+Pearson reaches ~0.75–0.78 ρ on MEN, competitive with neural embeddings and more efficient.  
- Parameter study: empirical sweeps over dimension m, sparsity s, and nonzero count show variance behavior, effects of non-orthogonality, and trade-offs with sparsity.  

Main contributions:
- Practical: simple, incremental, efficient random projection allowing post-hoc PPMI in compressed space with competitive MEN performance.  
- Empirical analysis: ablations comparing counts, RI, PoP (with/without PPMI), similarity measures, and parameter sweeps.  
- Methodological observation: advocate Kendall’s τb for non-Gaussian projected spaces and switching to Pearson after PPMI.  

Strengths:
- Clear practical efficiency story avoiding high-dimensional counting and expensive model training.  
- Solid, multifaceted empirical comparisons and averaging over runs.  
- Competitive accuracy (~0.75–0.78 on MEN) with simple randomized pipeline.  
- Inherently incremental/streaming-friendly construction.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited evaluation: only MEN reported; missing other similarity/analogy benchmarks and downstream tasks.  
- Weak theoretical grounding: no clear theory for projection error or for validity of PPMI on projected counts.  
- Efficiency claims lack concrete timing/memory comparisons against dense PPMI, SVD-PPMI, SGNS/GloVe on same data/hardware.  
- Some design choices are ad-hoc (nonzero value distribution, Kendall choice, recommended sparsity) and key baselines like feature hashing/count-min sketch are missing.  
- Incomplete ablations tying non-orthogonality/collision rates to downstream accuracy.  
- Reproducibility gaps: code/data/settings/hyperparameters, seeds, and significance measures are incomplete or promised only if accepted.  

Questions for authors:
- Provide exact wall-clock time and memory usage for each method/config (counts, RI, PoP, PoP+PPMI, SVD-PPMI) on the same hardware and corpus.  
- Clarify statistical meaning of PPMI on projected counts: are projected counts unbiased or monotone-preserving for true co-occurrences?  
- Compare against feature hashing or count-min sketch with PPMI, and against SPPMI+SVD and SGNS/GloVe trained on the same UKWaC setup.  
- Report vocabulary/context dimensionality, frequency cutoffs, stop-word handling, and other corpus preprocessing used for main results.  
- Show sensitivity to window weighting, subsampling, positional/decay schemes, and dependency-based contexts.  
- Provide additional benchmarks (SimLex-999, WordSim-353, RW), variance across 10 runs (stddev/confidence intervals), and significance tests.  

Overall assessment:
- Novel, practical idea with strong empirical support on MEN but limited by narrow evaluation, missing baselines/timings, and insufficient theoretical justification; promising if expanded and better documented.
