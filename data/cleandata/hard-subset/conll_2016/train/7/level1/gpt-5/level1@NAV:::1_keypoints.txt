- Proposal: Positive-only Projections (PoP), a sparse random projection with strictly positive entries enabling post-hoc PPMI in low-dimensional projected co-occurrence spaces.  
- Performance claim: Achieves up to 0.75 Spearman ρ on MEN using tiny vectors, computed incrementally and with low compute.  
- Similarity strategy: Uses Kendall’s τb in raw non-Gaussian space and Pearson’s r after applying PPMI.  
- Primary contribution: Practical, streaming random projection pipeline that allows efficient PPMI weighting in reduced dimensions.  
- Secondary contribution: Empirical characterization of correlation measures and parameter regimes (m, s), with distributional diagnostics and guidance on using τb vs Pearson.  
- Tertiary contribution: A positive-only random indexing variant that trades zero-mean projections for post-hoc weighting—methodologically incremental.  
- Strength: Simple, scalable two-step construction (index vectors + additive updates) that is memory-frugal and supports incremental updates for streaming corpora.  
- Strength: Competitive accuracy vs efficiency—outperforms RI and count baselines and reaches strong relatedness scores with very small vectors.  
- Strength: Insightful empirical analysis justifying the τb→Pearson switch and providing actionable parameter sweeps (e.g., small s for PPMI).  
- Strength: Practical benefit of enabling PPMI in low dimensions, addressing dense PMI computational bottlenecks.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—only MEN on UKWaC, no downstream tasks, multilingual tests, or comparisons to SGNS/GloVe/fastText with matched settings.  
- Weakness: Theoretical gaps—no error bounds, bias analysis, or clear statistical interpretation of PMI computed on projected/collided counts.  
- Weakness: Unquantified efficiency claims—no wall-clock, memory, or throughput comparisons to RI, PPMI+SVD, or neural baselines.  
- Weakness: Metric inconsistency—using τb for PoP and cosine for baselines confounds method comparisons; same similarity metric should be used.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—code release conditional on acceptance and experimental details (preprocessing, hyperparameters) are insufficient.  
- Requested experiments/questions: Report end-to-end runtime, memory, hardware and comparisons to SGNS/GloVe/PPMI+SVD on same data.  
- Requested experiments/questions: Clarify relation to Count-Min Sketch and feature hashing (signed vs unsigned) and characterize PPMI bias on projected counts.  
- Requested experiments/questions: Add evaluations on SimLex-999, WordSim353, MTurk-771 and at least one downstream task.  
- Requested experiments/questions: Analyze sensitivity to α, nonzero-value distribution, stopword handling, and streaming dynamics (e.g., OOV growth).
