- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP): sparse, nonnegative random index vectors enabling post-hoc PPMI on compressed word spaces.  
- Argues Kendall’s τb is better for raw PoP and Pearson after PPMI, motivated by distributional properties.  
- Reports PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves Spearman ρ ≈ 0.75 on MEN (UKWaC), competitive with baselines and fast/incremental.  
- Main contribution: simple, incremental random projection that preserves ability to apply PPMI without materializing co-occurrence matrices.  
- Contribution: empirical claim that Kendall’s τb outperforms cosine on raw PoP and Pearson is preferable after PPMI.  
- Contribution: parameter study of dimensionality (m) and sparsity (s) with qualitative analysis of variance and sensitivity.  
- Strength: practical simplicity and online, lightweight construction suitable for streaming or resource-limited settings.  
- Strength: enabling post-hoc PPMI on compressed representations is an interesting engineering advantage.  
- Strength: competitive MEN performance with relatively small vectors; unweighted PoP outperforms RI in reported experiments.  
- Strength: clear discussion of distributional choices and similarity measures with supportive histograms.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—approach closely resembles feature hashing/Count-Min Sketch (CMS) and lacks citations/comparisons.  
- Weakness: questionable theoretical validity of applying PPMI to projected bins, since bins lack true context marginals.  
- Weakness: narrow evaluation — only MEN on UKWaC, missing other similarity/analogy datasets and downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: missing scalability evidence and runtime/memory comparisons on realistic large vocabularies (~100k–1M types).  
- Weakness: ad hoc index-vector choices (e.g., ⌊U^−α⌋ with α=0.5) and no theoretical analysis of error, collisions, or bias/variance.  
- Key question: Were PoP vectors built only for the 751 MEN lemmas or for the full corpus vocabulary; please report large-vocab experiments and resource usage.  
- Key question: How does PoP differ theoretically/empirically from CMS/feature hashing—please compare directly.  
- Key question: Can you justify theoretically or empirically that PPMI on projected bins approximates PPMI on original counts?  
- Key question: Provide SGNS, SVD-PPMI, and GloVe baselines trained on the same data and report wall-clock and memory comparisons.  
- Key question: Are the similarity-measure findings (Kendall’s τb vs. Pearson/cosine) consistent across other datasets beyond MEN?  
- Key question: Justify α=0.5 and heavy-tailed index weights; do {0,1} updates or small integers perform similarly?  
- Additional: Improve presentation, release code, report confidence intervals across runs, and compare to other compression/sketching schemes.
