- Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse nonnegative random projection allowing PPMI weighting in reduced space and competitive MEN performance.
- Contributions claimed: practical nonnegative sparse random projection + PPMI post-projection; empirical study of m, sparsity, similarity; qualitative distributional analysis motivating Pearson after PPMI.
- Strength: simplicity and efficiency — reduces co-occurrence matrix before weighting, enabling cheaper PPMI on m≈3k and incremental/streaming construction.
- Strength: competitive intrinsic performance (~0.75 Spearman on MEN) with minimal machinery, matching strong count-based and some neural baselines.
- Strength: clear exposition and ablations showing effects of dimensionality, sparsity, and variance across runs.
- Strength: positions PoP as a potential bridge between sketching/hashing methods and PMI-style weighting.
- Weakness: no theoretical justification or statistical analysis for computing PPMI on the projected (pseudo-context) matrix; mutual-information interpretation breaks.
- Weakness: limited evaluation — only MEN, single corpus, bag-of-words windows; missing many intrinsic and extrinsic benchmarks.
- Weakness: incomplete baselines — lacking comparisons to SPPMI+SVD, GloVe, well-tuned SGNS, feature hashing, and Count-Min Sketch pipelines.
- Weakness: efficiency claims lack quantitative runtime, memory, and throughput measurements or asymptotic/constant-factor comparisons.
- Weakness: RI vs PPMI critique is conflated — negativity vs undefined marginals; alternatives like signed hashing or separating signs are not considered.
- Weakness: methodological choices (e.g., heavy-tailed index weights, α=0.5, τb choice) are ad hoc and under-motivated with limited ablation.
- Question: formal objective — does PPMI(P) approximate PPMI(C) in any provable sense; any sketching-style error bounds or bias/variance analysis?
- Question: empirical comparison to Count-Min Sketch or feature hashing applied to co-occurrence matrices followed by (S)PPMI and (optionally) randomized SVD?
- Question: please report runtime and memory for PoP, PPMI-on-PoP, and count-based PPMI/SVD on same hardware and corpus across m, including build, similarity, and peak memory.
- Question: clarify RI/PPMI claim — are PMI marginals truly undefined with RI, and could shifted-PMI or positive/negative separation mitigate issues?
- Question: motivation and sensitivity of index weight distribution t_ij = floor(1/U^α) with α=0.5; were signed or uniform weights or multiple hashes tried?
- Question: add evaluations on additional intrinsic sets (SimLex-999, WordSim-353, Rare Words) and at least one extrinsic task, with statistical significance across runs.
- Additional: fix notation inconsistencies (x vs m) and provide exact hyperparameters for best results (m, s, nonzeros).
- Additional: discuss derandomization or data-dependent projections and whether learning sparsity per feature helps.
- Additional: release code, exact preprocessing, and reproducible scripts to validate claims.
