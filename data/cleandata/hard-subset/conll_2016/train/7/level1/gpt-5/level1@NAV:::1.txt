Summary: The paper proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP), a sparse random projection scheme with strictly positive entries (E[R] > 0) that enables post-hoc application of PPMI to low-dimensional projected co-occurrence spaces. Similarities are computed with Kendall’s τb in the raw non-Gaussian space and with Pearson’s r after PPMI, yielding up to 0.75 Spearman ρ on MEN while being incremental and computationally light. The authors provide parameter studies over dimension and sparsity.

Contributions (as I see them):
- Primary: A practical, streaming random projection pipeline that allows efficient PPMI weighting in reduced dimensions, achieving competitive relatedness scores with substantially lower computational cost.
- Secondary: Empirical characterization of correlation measures and parameter regimes (m, s) for PoP, including distributional diagnostics (histograms) and guidance on when τb vs Pearson should be used.
- Tertiary: A positive-only variant of random indexing that trades zero-mean projections for post-hoc weighting; methodologically incremental relative to RI/feature hashing.

Strengths:
- Simplicity and scalability: The two-step construction (index vectors + additive updates) is easy to implement, memory-frugal, and supports incremental updates—valuable for large, streaming corpora.
- Competitive accuracy vs efficiency: Demonstrates clear gains over RI and count-based baselines and reaches 0.75 on MEN with tiny vectors, suggesting an attractive accuracy/compute trade-off.
- Insightful empirical analysis: The switch from τb to Pearson after PPMI is well-motivated and supported by distributional evidence; parameter sweeps provide actionable guidance (e.g., small s for PPMI).
- Practical benefit of post-hoc weighting: Bringing PPMI to low-dimensional projections addresses the computational bottleneck of dense PMI on full co-occurrence matrices.

Weaknesses:
- Limited evaluation: Results are restricted to MEN on UKWaC; no downstream tasks, multilingual tests, or comparisons to strong baselines (SGNS/GloVe/fastText) trained on the same corpus with matched settings.
- Theoretical gaps: No error bounds or bias analysis, especially for computing PPMI on projected (collided) counts; the statistical meaning of PMI after projection is unclear.
- Unquantified efficiency claims: No wall-clock, memory, or throughput comparisons to RI, PPMI+SVD, or neural baselines; “seconds/minutes” claims need evidence.
- Metric inconsistency: Using τb for PoP and cosine for baselines confounds comparisons; same similarity function should be applied across methods.
- Reproducibility: Code availability is contingent on acceptance; experimental detail (preprocessing, hyperparameters) is insufficient for exact replication.

Questions to Authors (optional):
- Please report end-to-end runtime, memory, and hardware, and compare against SGNS, GloVe, and PPMI+SVD on the same data.
- How does PoP relate to Count-Min Sketch and feature hashing (signed vs unsigned)? Can you characterize the bias of PPMI on projected counts?
- Can you add evaluations on SimLex-999, WordSim353, MTurk-771, and at least one downstream task?
- How sensitive are results to α, the nonzero-value distribution, stopword handling, and streaming updates (e.g., OOV growth)?
