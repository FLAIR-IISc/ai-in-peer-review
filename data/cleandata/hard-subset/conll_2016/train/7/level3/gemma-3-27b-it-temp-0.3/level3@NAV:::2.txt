## Review of “Random Positive-Only Projections: PPMI-Enabled Incremental Semantic Space Construction”

This paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection technique aiming for computational efficiency and compatibility with weighting methods like PPMI. The core idea – using a random projection matrix with a positive expected value – is potentially interesting, but the paper suffers from significant clarity and presentation issues that currently hinder its evaluation.

The primary contribution, as I see it, lies in the *attempt* to bridge the gap between random projection methods and count-based weighting techniques. Previous random projection methods, with their zero-mean matrices, preclude the direct application of PPMI. PoP attempts to address this, and the claim of enabling PPMI application is the most novel aspect. A secondary contribution is the empirical demonstration, albeit limited, that PoP combined with PPMI achieves competitive results on the MEN dataset. Finally, the exploration of the method’s sensitivity to parameters like dimensionality and sparsity offers some initial insight into its behavior.

However, the paper is significantly weakened by a lack of clarity. The arguments are difficult to follow, even for someone familiar with the field. The introduction meanders without clearly establishing the motivation or positioning PoP within the existing landscape of word embedding techniques. Statements are often abruptly introduced ("in addition," "since," "thus") without sufficient context or justification. The paper lacks self-containment; readers unfamiliar with random projections will struggle to grasp the rationale behind PoP. Numerous English language errors further impede readability. 

The evaluation is also a major weakness. Testing solely on the MEN dataset is insufficient. The authors should include standard benchmarks like WordSim or SimLex to demonstrate the generalizability of their approach, or provide a strong justification for focusing solely on MEN. The parameter exploration is a good start, but lacks depth and a clear theoretical grounding. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty of Approach:** The attempt to create a random projection method compatible with PPMI weighting is a potentially valuable contribution.
*   **Competitive Performance:** Achieving results comparable to state-of-the-art methods on the MEN dataset suggests promise.
*   **Parameter Exploration:** Initial investigation into the impact of dimensionality and sparsity provides some insight.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of Clarity:** The paper is poorly written and difficult to understand. Arguments are not well-developed, and statements are often unsupported.
*   **Insufficient Evaluation:** Testing on only one dataset limits the generalizability of the findings.
*   **Missing Theoretical Foundation:** The paper lacks a rigorous theoretical analysis of PoP's properties and limitations.
*   **English Language Errors:** Numerous grammatical and stylistic errors detract from readability.



**Questions to Authors:**

*   What is the theoretical justification for choosing a positive-only projection matrix? What properties does this confer compared to standard random projections?
*   How does PoP compare to other methods that attempt to combine random projections with weighting schemes (if any)?
*   Why was the MEN dataset chosen as the sole evaluation benchmark? What are the limitations of evaluating solely on this dataset?
*   Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of the 's' parameter on the performance and properties of PoP?



**Additional Comments:**

The authors should consider a thorough rewrite to improve clarity and address the weaknesses outlined above. A more comprehensive evaluation and a stronger theoretical foundation are also essential for resubmission.
