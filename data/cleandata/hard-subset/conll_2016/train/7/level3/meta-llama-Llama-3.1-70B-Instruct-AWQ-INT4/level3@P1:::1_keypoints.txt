- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for constructing semantic spaces that permits PPMI-style weighting.
- PoP models evaluated on the MEN relatedness test and reported as competitive with neural and count-based baselines.
- Demonstrates that PPMI weights can be computed efficiently for PoP-constructed models.
- Strength: PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient for large-scale NLP tasks.
- Strength: Despite simplicity, PoP achieves competitive performance on the MEN benchmark.
- Strength: Efficient incorporation of PPMI weighting is a notable practical contribution.
- Weakness: No direct, comprehensive comparison to related work (neural nets, weighted count models, matrix factorization).
- Weakness: Uses a non-standard definition of Kendall’s tau without explanation.
- Weakness: Inconsistency in use of Spearman correlation — used for evaluation but not for computing PoP similarities, with no justification.
- Weakness: Evaluation relies on a non-standard dataset, limiting claims of generalizability.
- Question: Please provide direct comparisons to neural net approaches and weighted/matrix-factorized count models.
- Question: Explain the motivation for using a non-standard Kendall’s tau definition.
- Question: Justify why Spearman correlation was not used for computing similarities in PoP models.
- Question: Provide additional evaluations on standard semantic relatedness benchmarks.
- Additional comment: The paper is well-written and clearly explains PoP and its evaluation.
- Additional comment: The use and efficient computation of PPMI for PoP is an important contribution that merits further validation on standard benchmarks.
