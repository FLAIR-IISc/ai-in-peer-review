**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP is based on random projections and is designed to be highly scalable and computationally efficient. The method uses a transformation matrix with a non-standard distribution, which allows for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to the resulting vectors. The authors evaluate the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set and demonstrate its competitiveness with state-of-the-art neural network-based approaches.

**Main Contributions**

1. The introduction of the PoP technique, which enables the construction of semantic spaces and word embeddings using random projections with a non-standard distribution.
2. The demonstration of the effectiveness of PoP in combination with PPMI weighting for achieving competitive performance in semantic relatedness tests.
3. The evaluation of the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set, which shows that it can achieve an average score of 0.75, comparable to many neural embedding techniques.

**Strengths**

1. The PoP method is reportedly much faster to train than neural networks, making it a promising approach for large-scale natural language processing tasks.
2. The method preserves some distributional properties in dimensionality reduction, which is a desirable property for semantic space construction.
3. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of PoP in combination with PPMI weighting, which is a widely used technique in natural language processing.

**Weaknesses**

1. The motivation for using transformability with PPMI is unclear, and the authors do not provide a clear explanation for why this is an important property.
2. The paper lacks a direct comparison to related work, which makes it difficult to evaluate the novelty and impact of the proposed method.
3. The definition of Kendall's tau used in the paper is non-standard and unexplained, which may cause confusion for readers.
4. The authors do not justify the use of Kendall's tau over Spearman correlation, despite using the latter at evaluation time and it being a standard measure in semantic tasks.
5. The evaluation dataset choice is non-standard, and the authors should consider including standard semantic relatedness benchmarks to demonstrate the generalizability of their approach.
6. Figure 1 has a formatting issue, with only two lines visible, and the third line seems to be missing.
7. There is a minor typo in the text ("large extend" should be "large extent").

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear explanation for why transformability with PPMI is an important property for semantic space construction?
2. How does the PoP method compare to other dimensionality reduction techniques, such as PCA or t-SNE?
3. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the performance of PoP on different datasets and tasks?
4. How does the choice of similarity measure (Kendall's tau vs. Spearman correlation) affect the performance of the PoP method?
5. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the non-standard definition of Kendall's tau used in the paper?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its evaluation. However, there are several areas where the paper could be improved, including the addition of a direct comparison to related work, a clearer explanation of the motivation for using transformability with PPMI, and a more detailed analysis of the performance of the PoP method on different datasets and tasks.
