**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel technique called Positive-only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP uses random projections with a positive expected value, allowing for the application of weighting methods such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors claim that PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient, achieving competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks without the need for heavy computations. The paper evaluates the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set and reports an average score of 0.75, comparable to state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of PoP**: The paper introduces a new technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, which is based on random projections with a positive expected value.
2. **Enabling PPMI use**: PoP allows for the application of weighting methods such as PPMI, which is not possible with previous random projection methods.
3. **Computational efficiency**: The authors claim that PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient, achieving competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks without the need for heavy computations.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new technique for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings, which is based on random projections with a positive expected value.
2. **Competitive results**: The authors report an average score of 0.75 on the MEN relatedness test set, which is comparable to state-of-the-art algorithms.
3. **Computational efficiency**: The paper demonstrates the efficiency of PoP in achieving competitive results without the need for heavy computations.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of clarity**: The paper lacks clarity overall, making it difficult to follow the arguments and understand the proposed technique.
2. **Unclear differences from previous methods**: The paper does not clearly explain how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and whether the claimed advantages hold.
3. **Insufficient evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the performance of PoP on the MEN relatedness test set, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate its effectiveness.
4. **Numerous English language errors**: The paper contains numerous English language errors, which hurt readability and make it difficult to understand the proposed technique.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear explanation of how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and why the claimed advantages hold?
2. How do you plan to address the lack of clarity in the paper and make it more accessible to non-experts?
3. Can you provide additional evaluation results on other standard benchmarks, such as WordSim and SimLex?
4. How do you plan to address the numerous English language errors in the paper and improve readability?

**Additional Comments**

The paper has potential, but it requires significant improvements in presentation and clarity before resubmission. The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above and provide additional evaluation results to demonstrate the effectiveness of PoP.
