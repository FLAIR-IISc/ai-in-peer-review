- Summary: Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based, scalable method for word embeddings that supports PPMI weighting and is competitive on MEN relatedness.
- Contribution 1: Introduces PoP, a novel random projection approach using a non-standard transformation distribution.
- Contribution 2: Shows PoP combined with PPMI yields strong semantic relatedness performance.
- Contribution 3: Evaluates PoP on the MEN test set, reporting an average score of 0.75 comparable to neural embeddings.
- Strength 1: PoP trains much faster than neural-network-based embedding methods, beneficial for large-scale tasks.
- Strength 2: The method preserves some distributional properties during dimensionality reduction.
- Strength 3: Demonstrates effective integration of PoP with widely used PPMI weighting.
- Weakness 1: Motivation for requiring transformability with PPMI is unclear and inadequately justified.
- Weakness 2: Lacks direct comparisons to related dimensionality-reduction and embedding methods.
- Weakness 3: Uses a non-standard, unexplained definition of Kendall’s tau that may confuse readers.
- Weakness 4: No justification for preferring Kendall’s tau over standard Spearman correlation, which is used in evaluation.
- Weakness 5: Evaluation dataset choice is non-standard; standard semantic relatedness benchmarks are missing.
- Weakness 6: Figure 1 has a formatting issue with a missing or partially visible third line.
- Weakness 7: Minor typo present (“large extend” should be “large extent”).
- Question 1: Why is transformability with PPMI an important property for semantic space construction?
- Question 2: How does PoP compare empirically to other dimensionality-reduction techniques like PCA or t-SNE?
- Question 3: Can the authors provide more detailed performance analyses of PoP across different datasets and tasks?
- Question 4: How does the choice of similarity measure (Kendall’s tau vs. Spearman) affect PoP’s performance?
- Question 5: Please clarify and justify the non-standard definition of Kendall’s tau used in the paper.
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but would benefit from clearer motivation, direct comparisons to related work, and broader empirical evaluation.
