- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for semantic spaces that supports PPMI weighting.
- Claims PoP is highly scalable and computationally efficient, offering an alternative to neural network embeddings.
- Reports competitive performance (average 0.75 on the MEN relatedness test).
- Proposes using Kendall's τb as a new similarity measure for non-Gaussian spaces.
- Strength: PoP is reportedly much faster than neural-network approaches, suitable for large-scale NLP.
- Strength: Method preserves some distributional properties important for semantic similarity.
- Strength: Demonstrates competitive semantic similarity performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness: No direct comparison to related work, making novelty and impact hard to assess.
- Weakness: Uses a non-standard, poorly explained definition of Kendall's τb and does not justify its choice over alternatives like Spearman.
- Weakness: Inconsistent evaluation metrics—Spearman is used for evaluation but not adopted as the method's similarity measure.
- Weakness: Evaluation relies on non-standard datasets that may not reflect broader semantic relatedness benchmarks.
- Question: Provide more detailed motivation for using PPMI weighting with PoP.
- Question: How will the authors address methodological issues (non-standard Kendall's τb, inconsistent metrics)?
- Question: Provide a comprehensive comparison to neural-network and other dimensionality-reduction baselines.
- Question: How will PoP be extended theoretically and combined with other weighting techniques to address current limitations?
