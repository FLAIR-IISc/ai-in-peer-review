- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for building semantic spaces with a projection matrix of positive expected value.  
- Claims PoP enables applying weighting methods like PPMI and yields competitive semantic similarity results.

- Primary contribution: introduction of PoP with a positive-expected-value projection matrix enabling PPMI on projected spaces.  
- Contribution: scalable, incremental, and computationally efficient construction of semantic spaces via random projections.  
- Contribution: PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance on the MEN relatedness test.

- Strength: PoP offers efficient, scalable construction of semantic spaces.  
- Strength: Demonstrated competitive results on the MEN relatedness benchmark.  
- Strength: Novel application of PPMI to projected spaces enabled by PoP’s design.  
- Strength: Produces low-dimensional embeddings suitable for efficient computation.

- Weakness: Paper writing is unclear and hard to follow, reducing accessibility.  
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation of how PoP differs from prior random projection methods.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to MEN; lacks other standard benchmarks like WordSim and SimLex.  
- Weakness: Numerous English language errors that impair readability.

- Question: Please clarify how PoP materially differs from previous random projection approaches and what specific advantages it provides.  
- Question: How will you address distortions caused by non-orthogonal index vectors?  
- Question: Can you provide evaluations on additional benchmarks (e.g., WordSim, SimLex) for a more comprehensive assessment?  
- Question: How will you improve the paper’s readability and fix language issues?

- Additional comment: Paper has potential but needs significant improvements in presentation, clarity, and evaluation before resubmission.
