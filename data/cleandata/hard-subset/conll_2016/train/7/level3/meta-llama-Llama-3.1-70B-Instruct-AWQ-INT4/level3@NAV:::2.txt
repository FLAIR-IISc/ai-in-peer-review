**Paper Review**

**Paper Title:** Random Positive-Only Projections: PPMI-Enabled Incremental Semantic Space Construction

**Summary:** The paper proposes a novel technique called Positive-only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP uses random projections with a positive expected value, enabling the application of weighting methods such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors claim that PoP-constructed models achieve competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks, comparable to state-of-the-art neural network-based approaches, without requiring heavy computations.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Novel Random Projection Method**: The paper introduces a new random projection method, PoP, which uses a positive expected value, allowing for the application of PPMI weighting.
2. **Efficient Semantic Space Construction**: PoP enables the construction of semantic spaces without requiring prior computation of embeddings, making it a scalable and computationally efficient approach.
3. **Competitive Results**: The authors demonstrate that PoP-constructed models achieve competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks, comparable to state-of-the-art neural network-based approaches.

**Strengths:**

1. **Efficient Approach**: PoP is a scalable and computationally efficient approach for constructing semantic spaces, making it a promising method for large-scale natural language processing tasks.
2. **Competitive Results**: The paper demonstrates that PoP-constructed models achieve competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks, comparable to state-of-the-art neural network-based approaches.
3. **Novelty**: The paper introduces a new random projection method, PoP, which uses a positive expected value, allowing for the application of PPMI weighting.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Clarity**: The paper lacks clarity overall, making it difficult to follow the arguments and understand the proposed method.
2. **Unclear Differences**: It is unclear how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and whether the claimed advantages hold.
3. **Insufficient Evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the proposed method on the MEN dataset, which is insufficient to demonstrate its generalizability.
4. **Numerous English Language Errors**: The paper contains numerous English language errors, which hurt readability and make it difficult to understand the proposed method.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and what advantages it offers?
2. How do you plan to address the lack of clarity in the paper and make it more accessible to non-experts?
3. Can you provide more evaluation results on other standard benchmarks, such as WordSim and SimLex, to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method?
4. How do you plan to address the numerous English language errors in the paper and improve its readability?
