- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for semantic spaces and word embeddings.
- PoP uses random projections with a positive expected value, enabling application of weighting methods like PPMI.
- Claims high scalability and computational efficiency while achieving competitive semantic similarity performance.
- Reports an average MEN relatedness score of 0.75, comparable to state-of-the-art.
- Main contribution: introduction of PoP as a novel projection technique for embeddings.
- Main contribution: enabling use of PPMI with random projections.
- Main contribution: emphasis on computationally efficient, scalable construction of semantic spaces.
- Strength: novel approach extending random projection methods.
- Strength: competitive empirical results on MEN.
- Strength: demonstrated computational efficiency without heavy computation.
- Weakness: paper lacks clarity and is hard to follow.
- Weakness: does not clearly explain differences from prior random projection methods or justify claimed advantages.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset and may be insufficient to prove effectiveness.
- Weakness: numerous English language errors hurt readability.
- Question: please clarify how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and why advantages hold.
- Question: how will you improve clarity and accessibility for non-experts?
- Question: provide additional evaluations on standard benchmarks (e.g., WordSim, SimLex).
- Question: how will you address the English language errors and improve readability?
- Additional comment: paper has potential but requires significant improvements in presentation, clarity, and broader evaluation before resubmission.
