**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces a novel technique called Positive-Only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP is based on random projections and uses a transformation matrix with a non-zero expected value, allowing for the application of weighting techniques such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors evaluate the performance of PoP-constructed models in the MEN relatedness test and report competitive results compared to neural net-based approaches and combinations of count-based models with weighting and matrix factorization.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of PoP technique**: The authors propose a new method for constructing semantic spaces, which is based on random projections and allows for the application of weighting techniques such as PPMI.
2. **Evaluation of PoP-constructed models**: The authors evaluate the performance of PoP-constructed models in the MEN relatedness test and report competitive results compared to state-of-the-art methods.
3. **Efficient computation of PPMI weights**: The authors demonstrate that PoP-constructed models can be efficiently weighted using PPMI, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.

**Strengths**

1. **Scalability**: The PoP method is highly scalable and computationally efficient, making it suitable for large-scale natural language processing tasks.
2. **Competitive performance**: The authors report competitive results for PoP-constructed models in the MEN relatedness test, which is a significant achievement considering the simplicity of the method.
3. **Efficient computation of PPMI weights**: The authors demonstrate that PoP-constructed models can be efficiently weighted using PPMI, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of direct comparison to related work**: The authors do not provide a direct comparison to related work, which makes it difficult to evaluate the significance of the contributions.
2. **Methodological issues**: The authors use a non-standard definition of Kendall's tau, which is not explained in the paper.
3. **Inconsistency**: The authors use Spearman correlation for evaluation but do not justify why they did not use it for computing similarities in the PoP-constructed models.
4. **Non-standard evaluation dataset**: The authors use a non-standard dataset for evaluation, which may not be representative of the performance of the method in other tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a direct comparison to related work, such as neural net-based approaches and combinations of count-based models with weighting and matrix factorization?
2. Can you explain the motivation behind using a non-standard definition of Kendall's tau?
3. Can you justify why you did not use Spearman correlation for computing similarities in the PoP-constructed models?
4. Can you provide additional evaluation results on standard semantic relatedness benchmarks?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the PoP technique and its evaluation.
* The use of PPMI weights is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing, and the authors demonstrate its efficiency in computing PPMI weights for PoP-constructed models.
* The paper could benefit from additional evaluation results on standard semantic relatedness benchmarks to demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
