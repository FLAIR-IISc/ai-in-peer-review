- Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection-based method for building semantic spaces and word embeddings, evaluated on the MEN relatedness set.  
- Introduces a novel random projection method (PoP) that preserves some distributional properties during dimensionality reduction.  
- Provides an efficient dimensionality reduction approach that avoids forcing the data distribution toward Gaussian with mean zero.  
- Enables application of weighting techniques (e.g., PPMI) to random-projection vectors, unlike prior methods.  
- Achieves competitive relatedness results compared to neural embeddings while training much faster.  
- Preserves distributional properties important for semantic similarity tasks.  
- Offers computationally efficient dimensionality reduction without expensive computations.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparisons to related work, hindering contextual evaluation.  
- Uses a non-standard definition of Kendall's tau without sufficient explanation.  
- Inconsistency in using Spearman correlation for evaluation but not for computing similarities in PoP models.  
- Evaluation relies on non-standard datasets, risking limited generality to other tasks.  
- Requests direct comparisons to neural embedding techniques and other random-projection methods.  
- Asks for clarification of the non-standard Kendall's tau definition used.  
- Questions the rationale for using Spearman correlation for evaluation but not for similarity computation.  
- Requests additional experiments on standard semantic relatedness benchmarks to better assess PoP.
