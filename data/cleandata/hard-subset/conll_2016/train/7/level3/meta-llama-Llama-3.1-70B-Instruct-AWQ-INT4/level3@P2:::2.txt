**Paper Summary and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel technique called Positive-only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. PoP is based on random projections, making it highly scalable and computationally efficient. The main contribution of PoP lies in its ability to use a projection matrix with a positive expected value, enabling the application of weighting methods such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI). The authors claim that PoP-constructed models, combined with PPMI, achieve competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks, comparable to state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Contributions (ordered by strength)**

1. **Introduction of PoP with a positive expected value projection matrix**: This is the primary contribution of the paper, as it enables the application of PPMI and other weighting methods to the projected spaces.
2. **Efficient construction of semantic spaces**: PoP's random projection approach allows for incremental construction of semantic spaces, making it a computationally efficient method.
3. **Competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks**: The authors demonstrate that PoP-constructed models, combined with PPMI, achieve competitive results in the MEN relatedness test.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficient construction of semantic spaces**: PoP's random projection approach makes it a computationally efficient method for constructing semantic spaces.
2. **Competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks**: The authors demonstrate that PoP-constructed models, combined with PPMI, achieve competitive results in the MEN relatedness test.
3. **Novel application of PPMI to projected spaces**: The authors show that PoP's positive expected value projection matrix enables the application of PPMI to the projected spaces, which is a novel contribution.
4. **Low-dimensional representation**: PoP-constructed models have a low-dimensional representation, making them suitable for tasks that require efficient computation.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of clarity and readability**: The paper suffers from poor writing, making it difficult to follow the arguments and understand the proposed method.
2. **Unclear differences from previous random projection methods**: The authors do not clearly explain how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and whether the claimed advantages hold.
3. **Insufficient evaluation**: The paper only evaluates PoP on the MEN relatedness test, which is not sufficient to demonstrate its effectiveness. Other standard benchmarks, such as WordSim and SimLex, should be included to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
4. **Numerous English language errors**: The paper contains several English language errors that hurt readability and make it difficult to understand the proposed method.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of how PoP differs from previous random projection methods and what advantages it offers?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of non-orthogonal index vectors, which can lead to distortions in similarities?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive evaluation of PoP on other standard benchmarks, such as WordSim and SimLex?
4. How do you plan to improve the readability and clarity of the paper to make it more accessible to non-experts?

**Additional Comments**

The paper has potential, but it requires significant improvements in presentation and clarity before resubmission. The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of PoP to demonstrate its effectiveness.
