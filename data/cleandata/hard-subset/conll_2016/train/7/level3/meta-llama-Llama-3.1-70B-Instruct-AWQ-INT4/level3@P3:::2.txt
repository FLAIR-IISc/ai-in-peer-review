This paper proposes a novel technique called Positive-only Projection (PoP) for constructing semantic spaces and word embeddings. The PoP method is based on random projections and is claimed to be highly scalable and computationally efficient. The authors argue that their method can be used for incremental semantic space construction and can be combined with weighting methods such as Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI) to achieve competitive results in semantic similarity assessment tasks.

**Summary of the Paper**

The authors introduce the PoP method as a dimensionality reduction technique that uses a random projection matrix R with a positive expected value (E(R) > 0). This allows for the application of weighting methods such as PPMI to the transformed spaces. The authors claim that their method is more efficient than neural embedding techniques and can achieve comparable results in semantic relatedness tests.

**Main Contributions**

1. The proposal of a new random projection method called PoP, which uses a projection matrix R with a positive expected value.
2. The demonstration of the applicability of weighting methods such as PPMI to PoP-constructed models.
3. The evaluation of the PoP method on the MEN relatedness test set, showing competitive results with neural embedding techniques.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel technique that addresses the limitations of previous random projection methods.
2. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of their method in achieving competitive results in semantic relatedness tests.
3. The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the PoP method, including an analysis of its parameters and performance.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks clarity overall, making it difficult to follow the arguments and understand the contributions of the work.
2. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the PoP method differs from previous random projection methods and whether the claimed advantages hold.
3. The paper contains numerous English language errors that hurt readability.
4. The evaluation of the PoP method is insufficiently broad, as it only tests on the MEN dataset and does not include other standard benchmarks.
5. The authors do not provide a detailed mathematical account of the PoP method, relying instead on empirical results.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clearer explanation of how the PoP method differs from previous random projection methods and what advantages it offers?
2. How do you plan to address the limitations of the PoP method, such as the lack of a detailed mathematical account and the reliance on empirical results?
3. Can you provide additional evaluation results on other standard benchmarks to demonstrate the robustness of the PoP method?
4. How do you plan to extend the PoP method to address other natural language processing tasks and applications?

**Recommendation**

I recommend that the authors substantially improve the presentation and clarity of the paper before resubmission. The paper has potential, but the lack of clarity and the limited evaluation make it difficult to assess the contributions of the work. With revisions to address these weaknesses, the paper could make a stronger contribution to the field of natural language processing.
