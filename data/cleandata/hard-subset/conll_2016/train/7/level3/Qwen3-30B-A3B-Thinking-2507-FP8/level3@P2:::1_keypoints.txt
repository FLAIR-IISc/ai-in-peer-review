- Introduces PoP: random projections with E(R) > 0 enabling PPMI weighting for random-projection semantic spaces.  
- Claims PoP is computationally efficient, training in seconds/minutes rather than hours/days.  
- Reports competitive performance: PoP+PPMI achieves ~0.75 on the MEN dataset.  
- Strength: Demonstrated computational efficiency useful for resource-constrained applications.  
- Strength: Novel insight that E(R) > 0 enables subsequent PPMI weighting unlike prior RI methods.  
- Strength: Empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art without neural training.  
- Weakness: No direct comparisons to relevant baselines (RI, other random projections, or count-based PPMI models).  
- Weakness: Unjustified use of Kendall's τb for similarity measurement instead of standard Spearman ρ.  
- Weakness: Inadequate evaluation scope—only MEN used; omits common benchmarks like WordSim-353 and SimLex-999.  
- Weakness: Figure 1 appears to show only two lines despite claiming three methods, suggesting a plotting error.  
- Weakness: Methodological inconsistency—calls spaces "non-Gaussian" yet uses Pearson correlation after PPMI without explanation.  
- Question: Why is transformability with PPMI important beyond the reported performance—what specific advantages does it provide?  
- Question: Why use Kendall's τb for similarity measurement rather than the standard Spearman's ρ?  
- Question: Why not include additional benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999) to demonstrate generality?  
- Question: Please clarify and correct Figure 1, which appears to show only two methods.  
- Additional comment: Minor typo ("large extend" → "large extent") and an unsubstantiated claim about heavy-tailed distributions.  
- Additional comment: The paper needs stronger methodological justification for PoP versus existing PPMI-weighted count+SVD approaches.
