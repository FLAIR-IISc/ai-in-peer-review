- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP): random projections with E(R) > 0 that permit applying PPMI after projection for incremental semantic space construction.
- Claims main contributions: (1) novel projection enabling post-projection weighting like PPMI, (2) PoP+PPMI achieves competitive MEN performance (0.75) with much lower compute, (3) analysis of PoP behavior across dimensionality and sparsity.
- Strength: Claims strong computational efficiency — PoP models built in seconds/minutes vs. hours/days due to low dimensionality and fast PPMI application.
- Strength: Technical novelty — enabling PPMI after projection addresses a limitation of E(R)=0 methods and has practical implications.
- Strength: Competitive empirical results — reported MEN score (~0.75) comparable to neural methods without neural training.
- Weakness: Lacks direct comparison to related efficient baselines (e.g., count+PPMI+SVD) to show relative performance/efficiency.
- Weakness: Motivation for PPMI transformability is unclear — paper asserts importance but doesn’t demonstrate clear performance/utility advantage over other approaches.
- Weakness: Methodological inconsistency — uses Kendall’s τb for similarity computation but evaluates with Spearman’s ρ without justification.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation — only uses MEN and omits standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, RG-65), making result assessment difficult.
- Weakness: Figure 1 appears to miss PoP curve (shows only count-based and RI), undermining empirical credibility.
- Question: Can authors provide quantitative runtime comparisons between PoP+PPMI and standard count-based+PPMI+SVD pipelines?
- Question: Why choose Kendall’s τb for similarity computation instead of the standard Spearman correlation, and why not use the same measure for computation and evaluation?
- Question: Under what conditions does PoP outperform RI without PPMI (e.g., context window sizes), and why does that advantage vanish with larger windows?
