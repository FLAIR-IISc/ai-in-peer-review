- Paper proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP): use random projections with E(R) > 0 so PPMI weighting can be applied, claiming faster construction and competitive semantic similarity performance.
- Contribution: Introduces PoP method enabling PPMI on random-projection semantic spaces (previous methods use zero-mean projections).
- Contribution: Empirically shows PoP+PPMI achieves competitive results on the MEN relatedness test (0.75 average score).
- Contribution: Claims substantial computational efficiency—models can be built in seconds/minutes vs. hours/days for alternative methods.
- Strength: Enabling PPMI via positive-mean random projections addresses a real limitation of standard random projection approaches.
- Strength: Demonstrates promising computational efficiency, valuable for resource-constrained settings since no neural training is required.
- Strength: Empirical MEN results are promising—PoP+PPMI outperforms RI and count-based baselines and benefits from PPMI.
- Weakness: Paper lacks clarity and fails to clearly distinguish PoP from existing random projection methods in implementation or theory.
- Weakness: Evaluation is narrow—only MEN reported; missing other standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, RG-65).
- Weakness: Numerous English language errors and poor organization reduce readability and accessibility.
- Weakness: Not self-contained—assumes prior knowledge of random projections and omits necessary context for non-experts.
- Weakness: Claims of parity with state-of-the-art lack proper contextualization and direct comparisons to specific top-performing methods.
- Question: How exactly does PoP differ in implementation from RI (beyond E(R) > 0)?
- Question: Why was MEN the only benchmark used, and how would PoP perform on other standard similarity datasets?
- Question: What hardware and procedures were used for timing measurements supporting the seconds/minutes claim, and how do they compare to neural baselines?
- Question: Can the authors provide quantitative numbers showing PoP’s improvement over RI on semantic similarity tasks?
- Question: What final vector dimensionality was used for the MEN results, and what is meant by “very low dimensionality”?
