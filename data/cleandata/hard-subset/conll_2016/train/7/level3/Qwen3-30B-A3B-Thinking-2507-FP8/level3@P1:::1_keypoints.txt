- Summary: Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), using random projections with E(R) > 0 to allow PPMI weighting; claims PoP+PPMI gets 0.75 on MEN with far lower compute than neural nets.

- Contribution: Shows E(R) > 0 random projection matrices enable weighting transformations like PPMI that standard zero-mean random projections cannot.

- Contribution: Demonstrates practical efficiency—PoP+PPMI achieves competitive performance with dramatically reduced computational cost versus neural approaches.

- Contribution: Empirically validates that PoP-constructed spaces can be effectively transformed using standard weighting techniques.

- Strength: Computational efficiency claim is well-supported; PoP models can be built in seconds/minutes versus hours/days for neural models.

- Strength: Theoretical insight about the importance of E(R) > 0 for enabling PPMI is novel and clearly explained.

- Strength: Reported performance (0.75 on MEN) is competitive with state-of-the-art neural methods despite no training.

- Weakness: No direct comparisons to standard count-based PPMI baselines (e.g., co-occurrence + PPMI + SVD) to contextualize PoP’s advantage.

- Weakness: Inconsistency between using Kendall’s τb for similarity computation and Spearman’s ρ for evaluation without justification.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MEN; missing results on standard benchmarks like WordSim353, SimLex-999, or RareWords.

- Weakness: Figure 1 is misleading/problematic—only two lines visible though PoP is claimed, making comparisons unclear.

- Weakness: Experimental setup lacks sufficient detail (exact dimensionalities, s parameters) hindering reproducibility.

- Question: Why was Kendall’s τb used for similarity computation while Spearman’s ρ was used for evaluation, and what evidence supports τb’s superiority?

- Question: Can you provide direct comparisons between PoP+PPMI and standard count-based PPMI models (e.g., with SVD truncation)?

- Question: Is the reported 0.75 MEN score a mean across runs or the best single run, and please provide standard deviations?

- Question: Why was MEN chosen as the primary evaluation dataset instead of more established benchmarks like WordSim353 or SimLex-999?

- Question: Please fix Figure 1 so it properly shows all three lines (count-based, RI, PoP) as claimed.
