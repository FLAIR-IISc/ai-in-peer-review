Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with E(R) > 0 that enables applying PPMI to projected embeddings.  
Authors claim PoP is computationally efficient while retaining competitive semantic-similarity performance.  
Primary contribution: PoP enabling PPMI on random-projection spaces; secondary: PoP+PPMI achieves ≈0.75 MEN score comparable to neural methods.  
Novelty is unclear because the paper fails to clearly distinguish PoP from existing random-projection techniques (e.g., RI, RMI).  
Strength: Empirical demonstration of PoP’s performance on the MEN dataset.  
Strength: Logical connection argued between positive projection matrices and PPMI applicability.  
Strength: Some parameter analysis showing how dimensionality affects performance.  
Weakness: Paper lacks clarity throughout and arguments are hard to follow from the introduction onward.  
Weakness: Poorly framed key statements and stylistic issues that impair comprehension.  
Weakness: Not self-contained—requires prior knowledge of random projection methods to understand rationale.  
Weakness: Evaluation is insufficiently broad, testing only on MEN and omitting standard benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex) without justification.  
Weakness: Numerous English language errors further hinder readability.  
Weakness: Computational-efficiency claims are vague and unsupported by concrete evidence.  
Overall recommendation: Substantial revision needed to clarify novelty, improve presentation, and provide broader empirical validation before reconsideration.
