- Proposal: Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with E(R) > 0 to enable applying PPMI to low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Claim: PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves 0.75 average Spearman’s ρ on MEN, rivaling state-of-the-art with lower computational needs.  
- Contribution 1: PoP as a novel random projection scheme that yields positive-expected projections.  
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that PoP spaces can be PPMI-weighted to improve semantic similarity estimates.  
- Contribution 3: Claims computational efficiency via compact, low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Strength: Conceptually interesting idea—using positive projections to permit PPMI weighting.  
- Strength: Competitive empirical result on MEN suggesting potential utility.  
- Strength: Provides empirical analysis of parameter effects (dimensionality, sparsity).  
- Weakness: Paper is poorly written and unclear, making the method’s rationale hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Weakness: Insufficient differentiation from prior random-projection methods (RI, RRI, ISA, RMI); novelty not well justified.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MEN only; lacks results on additional benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999) and comparisons to other PPMI-based methods.  
- Weakness: No theoretical grounding or error-bound justification for PoP; mathematical differences from existing methods are not established.  
- Weakness: Unsubstantiated claims of scalability and efficiency—no complexity analysis or runtime measurements provided.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify mathematical/implementation differences from RI/RRI/ISA/RMI and why PoP is superior to shifting RI for PPMI.  
- Questions for authors: Explain dataset choice (why only MEN) and provide additional benchmark results.  
- Questions for authors: Provide runtime comparisons vs count-based PPMI and matrix factorization models to support efficiency claims.  
- Questions for authors: Justify distribution choice and the specific α = 0.5 parameter in Equation 2 and its effect on performance.  
- Recommendation: Interesting idea but fundamentally flawed in clarity, evaluation, and novelty proof; requires substantial revision, more comparisons, broader evaluation, and runtime/theoretical evidence before acceptance.
