- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection method with positive expected values that enables applying PPMI to embeddings, claiming efficiency and competitive semantic performance.

- Contribution: novel PoP technique enabling PPMI on projected spaces, addressing limitations of prior random projections.

- Contribution: demonstrates PoP+PPMI achieves 0.75 on the MEN relatedness test.

- Contribution: claims substantial computational efficiency (models built in seconds/minutes vs hours/days).

- Strength: insight that positive expected-value projections enable PPMI is potentially valuable and addresses a real limitation of RI-like methods.

- Strength: potential practical significance if the computational efficiency claims hold.

- Strength: appropriate use of Kendall’s τb for similarity in non-Gaussian embedding spaces.

- Weakness: paper is poorly written and unclear, with grammatical errors and insufficient background for readers unfamiliar with random projections.

- Weakness: evaluation is insufficient—only MEN was used and results are below state-of-the-art; no benchmarks like WordSim353 or SimLex-999 included.

- Weakness: unclear how PoP fundamentally differs from existing methods (RI, RRI, ISA, RMI) beyond E(R) > 0 and lacks mathematical justification.

- Weakness: unsupported claims of competitiveness without direct comparisons to relevant baselines and top-performing methods.

- Weakness: results presentation is confusing (e.g., Figure 1), and claims about faster convergence lack clear evidence.

- Question: provide a clear mathematical distinction showing why E(R) > 0 enables PPMI where others cannot.

- Question: why only MEN was used, and what evidence shows results generalize to other semantic benchmarks?

- Question: provide detailed comparisons with other count-based PPMI methods (e.g., Levy & Goldberg 2014) to show PoP+PPMI’s advantage.

- Overall: idea has potential but paper is unsuitable for publication in current form due to clarity, evaluation, and comparison weaknesses; major revisions required.
