## Review of “Random Positive-Only Projections: PPMI-Enabled Incremental Semantic Space Construction”

This paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection technique for constructing word embeddings with a positive expected value, aiming to enable the application of PPMI weighting and achieve computational efficiency. While the core idea has potential, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues and lacks a convincing demonstration of novelty and advantage over existing methods.

The primary contribution, as I see it, is the *attempt* to bridge the gap between random projection methods and PPMI weighting. Existing random projection techniques, due to their zero-mean projections, are incompatible with PPMI. PoP aims to address this, and if successful, would offer a computationally efficient path to incorporating PPMI into random projection workflows. However, the paper doesn’t fully establish this success or convincingly demonstrate why existing work hasn't already explored this direction. A secondary, less significant contribution is the empirical evaluation, though limited in scope, showing comparable performance to state-of-the-art methods on the MEN dataset.

Despite these potential contributions, the paper's presentation is a major weakness. The arguments are difficult to follow, beginning with a convoluted introduction. Statements are often unsupported or awkwardly phrased (e.g., lines 114, 137, and 154). The rationale behind PoP and its advantages are not clearly articulated, especially for readers unfamiliar with random projections, making it inaccessible to a broader audience. The paper lacks sufficient background and context to understand the significance of using a positive-valued projection matrix. 

Furthermore, the evaluation is insufficient. Testing only on the MEN dataset is a significant limitation. The authors should include standard benchmarks like WordSim and SimLex, or provide a strong justification for their choice. The parameter sensitivity analysis is a good addition, but feels somewhat disconnected from the core claim of PoP's inherent advantage. Numerous English language errors impede readability and detract from the professionalism of the presentation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Potential for Efficient PPMI Integration:** The core idea of enabling PPMI weighting within a random projection framework is potentially valuable.
*   **Comparable Performance:**  Achieving results comparable to state-of-the-art methods on MEN suggests some merit to the approach.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity Analysis:** The exploration of parameter settings (dimensionality and non-zero elements) provides some insight into the method's behavior.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of Clarity:** The paper is poorly written and difficult to understand. Arguments are not well-structured, and key concepts are not explained clearly.
*   **Unclear Novelty:** The paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate that this approach differs significantly from previous random projection methods or why this specific positive-only projection hasn't been explored before.
*   **Insufficient Evaluation:** The evaluation is limited to a single dataset and lacks comparison to appropriate baselines.
*   **Poor Writing Quality:** Numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing hinder readability.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How does PoP fundamentally differ from simply applying a positive scaling factor to the elements of a standard random projection matrix?
2.  What are the theoretical justifications for choosing α = 0.5 in Equation 2?
3.  Why was the MEN dataset the *only* dataset used for evaluation? What steps were taken to ensure the results generalize beyond this specific dataset?
4.  Can you provide a more intuitive explanation of why a positive expected value in the projection matrix allows for the application of PPMI weighting?

**Additional Comments:**

*   A more thorough literature review on existing random projection methods and their limitations is needed.
*   Consider adding a visual illustration of the projection process to aid understanding.
*   The paper would significantly benefit from a revision by a native English speaker.



**Overall Recommendation:** Substantially improve presentation and clarity before resubmission. The core idea has potential, but the current manuscript is too difficult to understand and lacks sufficient evidence to support its claims.
