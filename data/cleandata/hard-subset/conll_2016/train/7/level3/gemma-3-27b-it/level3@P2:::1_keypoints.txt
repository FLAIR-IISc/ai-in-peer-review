- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection matrix with positive expectation that enables post-projection PPMI weighting.
- Shows PoP matches state-of-the-art on MEN and outperforms standard Random Indexing even without PPMI.
- Provides thorough exploration of parameters (dimensionality m, sparsity s) and their impact on performance.

- Claims strong computational efficiency, promising much faster model construction than neural embedding methods.
- Novel idea of using non-zero-mean random projections to retain compatibility with count-based weighting.
- Detailed parameter analysis gives useful practical insights for future work.
- Attempts to situate PoP within theoretical dimensionality-reduction literature.

- Lacks comparisons to standard, well-tuned baselines (e.g., PPMI + SVD), so the added value of projection vs. PPMI is unclear.
- Motivation for applying PPMI specifically after projection is not convincingly justified.
- Use of Kendall’s τb is non-standard and poorly motivated, especially given evaluation with Spearman correlation.
- Evaluation is limited to the MEN dataset and Figure 1 has visibility issues; broader benchmarks are needed.
- Minor writing errors and occasional redundancy reduce polish.

- Provide a direct PoP+PPMI vs. PPMI+SVD comparison on identical data and protocol.
- Clarify the rationale for choosing Kendall’s τb over Pearson correlation.
- Offer theoretical/error bounds on information loss introduced by PoP and trade-offs with dimensionality.
- Report whether PoP+PPMI performance holds using Pearson correlation instead of Kendall’s τb.

- Include all code and detailed experimental setup in the supplementary material.
- Investigate effects of different PPMI normalization schemes.
