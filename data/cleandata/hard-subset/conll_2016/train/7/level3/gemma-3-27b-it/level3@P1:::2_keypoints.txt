- Introduces PoP, a random projection method with positive expected value enabling post-projection PPMI weighting.
- Main contribution: attempt to combine random projection efficiency with PPMI-style weighting by altering projection matrix expectation.
- Secondary contribution: shows competitive performance on MEN relatedness (score ~0.75) when PoP is paired with PPMI.
- Strength: promises computationally efficient, scalable embedding construction as an alternative to resource-heavy neural methods.
- Strength: novel projection-matrix design (E(R) > 0) that purportedly allows post-projection weighting.
- Weakness: paper lacks clarity and motivation; does not clearly explain why E(R) > 0 enables PPMI.
- Weakness: insufficiently differentiates PoP from existing methods (RI, RRI, ISA) and lacks rigorous justification of claimed advantages.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to MEN; needs additional benchmarks (e.g., WordSim353, SimLex-999) to demonstrate generality.
- Weakness: poor writing and numerous grammatical/stylistic errors that impede understanding.
- Weakness: not self-contained; assumes reader familiarity with random projections and omits clear background explanation.
- Question: request formal explanation of why E(R) > 0 allows PPMI while E(R) = 0 does not.
- Question: ask for analysis of how sparsity parameter 's' affects PoP performance and selection guidelines.
- Question: request detailed computational complexity/comparison with RI, RRI, ISA, Word2Vec, and GloVe.
- Question: ask for theoretical justification for using Kendall’s τb in PoP space and its relation to vector distributions.
- Additional: supplemental code and embeddings would be valuable if well-documented and reproducible.
- Additional: discussion should be streamlined and include a table summarizing differences between PoP and other random projection methods.
- Overall: interesting and potentially valuable idea but requires substantial rewriting, stronger theoretical justification, and broader experiments; current recommendation: reject with encouragement to resubmit after revisions.
