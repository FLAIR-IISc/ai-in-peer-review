- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP) allowing post-projection weighting (e.g., PPMI) by using positive-only random projections.
- Main contribution: identifies zero-mean constraint of traditional random projections and proposes a simple positive-only modification to overcome it.
- Empirical claim: PoP combined with PPMI achieves competitive MEN relatedness results with substantially faster training times.
- Strength — computational efficiency: much faster than neural embeddings, suitable for limited resources and rapid prototyping.
- Strength — novelty: clever, simple fix to enable post-projection weighting not possible with standard random projections.
- Strength — preserves distributional properties, enabling application of weighting techniques after projection.
- Weakness — motivation unclear: paper doesn't convincingly argue why enabling PPMI after projection is inherently valuable beyond improved results.
- Weakness — lacks comparisons: no direct, quantitative head-to-head comparison with other fast, non-neural methods (e.g., RI, SVD variants).
- Weakness — non-standard Kendall’s tau: uses an undefined variant without justification or clear specification.
- Weakness — inconsistent correlation measure: uses Spearman without explaining why Pearson wasn’t used after PPMI transformation.
- Weakness — dataset choice: relies on non-standard datasets; should include standard benchmarks (WordSim353, SimLex-999).
- Minor issue: Figure 1 is missing the third line.
- Question: please justify why enabling PPMI after projection is a desirable goal beyond improved performance.
- Question: provide direct quantitative comparisons of PoP against RI, SVD-based, and other fast semantic-space methods.
- Question: clarify which Kendall’s tau variant was used and justify that choice.
- Question: explain why Spearman correlation was chosen instead of Pearson given the use of PPMI.
