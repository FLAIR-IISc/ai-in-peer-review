- Main contribution: proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP) to enable PPMI weighting in random projection-based word embeddings.
- Potential benefit: PoP could offer a computationally efficient way to incorporate PPMI into random projection workflows.
- Empirical result: Shows comparable performance to state-of-the-art on the MEN dataset.
- Parameter analysis: Includes sensitivity study (dimensionality and non-zero elements), giving some insight into behavior.
- Clarity issues: Paper is poorly written, arguments are convoluted, and key concepts are not clearly explained.
- Accessibility problem: Rationale and intuition behind PoP and positive-valued projections are not made accessible to non-experts.
- Novelty unclear: Authors fail to convincingly show PoP is distinct from or unexplored relative to existing random projection methods.
- Evaluation insufficient: Experiments are limited to a single dataset (MEN) and lack comparisons on standard benchmarks and appropriate baselines.
- Question on design: Unclear how PoP differs from simply applying a positive scaling to standard random projections.
- Theoretical question: No justification given for choosing α = 0.5 in Equation 2.
- Generalization concern: No explanation why MEN was the sole dataset or evidence results generalize beyond it.
- Request for intuition: Authors should provide a clearer, intuitive explanation of why positive expected projections enable PPMI weighting.
- Literature and presentation suggestions: Need a more thorough related-work review, visual illustrations of the method, and native-English revision.
- Recommendation: Major revision required—improve clarity, provide stronger empirical and theoretical evidence before resubmission.
