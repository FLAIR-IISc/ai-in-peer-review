- Core contribution: introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection that enables applying PPMI weighting to embeddings.  
- Claim: PoP’s positive-expected projection matrix allows PPMI to work with random projections, offering competitive performance and improved efficiency.  
- Potential value: PoP could provide a faster alternative to neural embeddings or applying PPMI to high-dimensional count models if efficiency claims hold.  
- Addresses known limitation: identifies incompatibility of zero-mean random projections with weighting schemes like PPMI and proposes a fix.  
- Empirical support: reports respectable MEN relatedness score (0.75), suggesting the approach has merit.  
- Clarity/motivation issues: introduction is dense and hard to follow; motivation and conceptual differences from existing methods are unclear.  
- Technical justification lacking: insufficient explanation for using a positive-expected matrix and no rigorous justification for PPMI compatibility; α = 0.5 choice is unexplained.  
- Insufficient evaluation: experiments limited to MEN only; needs additional benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999) or justification for using only MEN.  
- Presentation and language problems: numerous grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and weak organization that hinder readability.  
- Missing baselines: comparison limited (only RI); should include more recent/random-projection baselines and simpler alternatives.  
- Questions for authors: clarify mathematically why E(R)=0 projections are incompatible with PPMI; justify and analyze sensitivity of α=0.5.  
- Questions for authors (cont.): provide concrete computational cost comparisons versus other projection methods and direct PPMI on counts.  
- Questions for authors (cont.): elaborate on the mentioned "derandomization techniques" and how they integrate with PoP.  
- Additional comment: supplemental material/code is useful but paper must stand alone; better integration of related work discussions needed.  
- Recommendation: Reject with major revisions — idea is interesting but requires clearer motivation, stronger justification, broader evaluation, and careful editing.
