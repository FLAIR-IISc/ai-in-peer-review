- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP): random projection matrix with positive expected value enabling post-hoc PPMI weighting for semantic spaces.  
- Main contribution: exploration of non-standard random projections (positive expectation) compatible with PPMI weighting.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical demonstration that PoP performs competitively on the MEN dataset with much lower computational cost.  
- Investigates the interplay between dimensionality, sparsity, and weighting techniques.  
- Strength: Computational efficiency—PoP achieves competitive results with far lower computation than complex neural models.  
- Strength: Novelty—using positive-expected-value random projections to enable post-hoc weighting is a new design space.  
- Strength: Parameter exploration—analysis of effects of dimensionality (m) and proportion of non-zeros (s) with discussed trade-offs.  
- Strength: Distributional considerations—acknowledges heavy-tailed counts and connects PPMI, Gaussian assumptions, and similarity measures.  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons—lacks direct head-to-head evaluation against standard pre-trained embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe, fastText).  
- Weakness: Unclear motivation for emphasizing PPMI compatibility and why it’s essential versus alternative weightings.  
- Weakness: Choice of Knight (1966) Kendall’s τb implementation is unexplained and conflicts with later use of Spearman’s rho.  
- Weakness: Narrow evaluation—reliance on MEN only; should include SimLex-999, WordSim-353, RG-65 for broader benchmarking.  
- Weakness: Figure 1 is unclear—one method’s line appears truncated/obscured, hindering interpretation.  
- Question: Provide direct comparisons of PoP+PPMI with Word2Vec/GloVe/fastText on standard semantic similarity benchmarks?  
- Question: Elaborate the specific advantages of PPMI for PoP and limitations if alternative weighting schemes were used?  
- Question: Why choose Knight (1966) Kendall’s τb, and how does it compare to other implementations or Spearman’s rho?  
- Question: How sensitive is PoP to the choice of α (fixed at 0.5); were other α values tested and with what results?  
- Question: Provide a more detailed theoretical explanation for performance gains as dimensionality m increases.
