- Proposed TBuckets method groups topic words into thematic buckets and scores topic coherence using properties of the largest bucket.
- TBuckets implements three techniques: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.
- Evaluated on three public datasets and reported better performance than state-of-the-art methods.
- Contribution: novel bucket-based approach to measure topic coherence.
- Contribution: two parameter-free variants (TBuckets-SVD, TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that avoid extensive tuning.
- Contribution: empirical improvements, with TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforming prior methods on two of three datasets.
- Strength: leverages word embeddings effectively to compute semantic similarity for grouping words.
- Strength: simple and intuitive method that is easy to understand and implement.
- Strength: demonstrated improved empirical performance across datasets.
- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to related work, making significance unclear.
- Weakness: uses non-standard datasets that may not represent the broader topic modeling community.
- Weakness: Figure 1 contains an error (shows two lines instead of three), indicating attention-to-detail issues.
- Weakness: paper contains typos and unclear sections that hinder readability.
- Author question: provide a direct comparison to related work and discuss relative strengths/weaknesses.
- Author question: justify use of non-standard datasets and address representativeness concerns.
- Author question: explain the error in Figure 1 and how it will be corrected.
