- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping LDA topic words into thematic buckets (TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, TBuckets-SVD-Reorg), evaluated on three public datasets; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms state-of-the-art on 2/3 datasets and aids weakly supervised text classification.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel bucket-based approach to measure topic quality.

- Contribution 2: Evaluated on three public datasets, showing TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior methods on two datasets.

- Contribution 3: TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are parameter-free, avoiding tuning.

- Strength 1: Empirically effective in measuring topic quality.

- Strength 2: No parameter tuning required, increasing practicality.

- Strength 3: Demonstrated utility for weakly supervised text classification.

- Weakness 1: Insufficiently clear explanation of how the method differs from previous random projection approaches.

- Weakness 2: Paper arguments are hard to follow and some claims lack supporting evidence.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation is limited to only three datasets and lacks broader benchmarking.

- Weakness 4: Numerous English language errors hinder readability.

- Question 1: Please clarify the core difference between TBuckets and previous random projection methods.

- Question 2: How will you address limited evaluation and which additional datasets will you use?

- Question 3: Provide more details on the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg reorganization step and why it improves performance.
