- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method for building semantic spaces claiming efficiency and scalability.  
- Claims PoP preserves non-Gaussian distributions, enabling weighting transforms like PPMI.  
- Reports PoP+PPMI achieves competitive semantic similarity performance (e.g., MEN) without expensive training.  
- Strength: computationally efficient and suitable for resource-constrained settings.  
- Strength: compatible with PPMI, unlike traditional random projection methods.  
- Strength: empirical result: PoP+PPMI+Pearson attains Spearman 0.75 on the MEN relatedness test.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification and analysis of error bounds and variance.  
- Weakness: unclear importance and justification of PPMI transformability versus other weighting techniques.  
- Weakness: missing direct comparisons to other dimensionality-reduction methods (SVD, PCA) and neural embeddings.  
- Weakness: inconsistent evaluation metrics — Kendall’s τb used without justification and Spearman not applied consistently.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies mainly on the MEN dataset; omits standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999).  
- Weakness: presentation problems (incomplete Figure 1, typo "large extend").  
- Question: clarify why PPMI transformability is critical and how it compares to other weightings.  
- Question: justify choice of Kendall’s τb and report how it compares to Spearman correlation.  
- Question: include comparisons with other dimensionality-reduction and neural embedding methods in revisions.  
- Conclusion: promising and efficient approach but needs stronger theory, broader comparisons, and consistent evaluation — recommend major revision.
