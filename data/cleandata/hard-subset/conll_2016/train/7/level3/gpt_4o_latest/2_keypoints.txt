- Introduces PoP: a random projection method with positive expected value (E(R) > 0) enabling PPMI weighting.  
- Claims PoP is computationally efficient, scalable, and compatible with PPMI where prior random projection methods were not.  
- Empirical result: PoP achieves competitive performance on the MEN dataset while requiring much less computation.  
- Strength: PoP offers a scalable, efficient alternative suitable for resource-constrained settings.  
- Strength: Empirical validation on MEN supports PoP’s feasibility as a lightweight alternative to neural embeddings.  
- Strength: Paper outlines potential extensions (theoretical analysis, integration with neural embeddings).  
- Weakness: Novelty is unclear; distinction from prior methods (e.g., Reflective Random Indexing) is not well explained.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to MEN, omitting other standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999), reducing generalizability.  
- Weakness: Presentation and clarity issues—poorly explained key concepts and numerous English errors hinder readability.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification or mathematical foundation, weakening the validity of claims.  
- Weakness: Overemphasizes PPMI without exploring or justifying alternative weighting methods.  
- Question: Please clarify how PoP fundamentally differs from prior random projection methods beyond E(R) > 0.  
- Question: Why was evaluation restricted to MEN, and why were other standard benchmarks excluded?  
- Question: How does PoP perform versus neural embeddings or other projection methods on tasks beyond similarity (analogy, classification)?  
- Recommendation: Major revision required—improve clarity, provide theoretical support, and expand evaluation before resubmission.
