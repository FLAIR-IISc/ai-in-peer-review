- Paper proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse, nonnegative random projections enabling incremental, low-dimensional semantic spaces and post-hoc PPMI weighting.
- Authors advocate Kendall’s τb for similarity in raw PoP and Pearson after PPMI, reporting MEN ≈ 0.75 for PoP+PPMI+Pearson and large speed advantages over neural/dense pipelines.

Contributions:
- Engineering: simple positive-only random projection scheme enabling fast, incremental construction and PPMI after projection.
- Empirical observation: τb performs better on raw PoP (non-Gaussian), Pearson improves after PPMI (more Gaussian).
- Practical parameter study: exploration of dimensionality (m) and sparsity (s) effects on variance and performance.

Strengths:
- High efficiency and scalability with minimal computation (random vectors + additions), suitable for resource-constrained settings.
- Enables post-hoc PPMI at low dimensionality, achieving competitive MEN scores with lower compute.
- Reasonable link between distributional shape and choice of similarity measure, supported by empirical evidence.
- Useful parameter exploration and variance reporting offering practical guidance.

Weaknesses:
- No controlled, same-corpus comparisons to strong baselines (SGNS/word2vec, GloVe, fastText, PPMI+SVD), undermining claims of comparability.
- Unclear motivation/justification for PPMI “transformability”; missing direct comparisons to pipeline counts→PPMI→projection or PPMI-before-projection.
- Incorrect/unclear definition of Kendall’s τb (uses adjacent pairs); choice of Kendall not compared to Spearman or cosine while evaluation metric is Spearman ρ.
- Limited evaluation focused mainly on MEN and a single corpus (UKWaC), lacking standard benchmarks and robustness checks.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 missing a curve, minor language/formatting typos hindering readability.

Questions for authors:
- Provide controlled comparisons (accuracy, runtime, memory) on the same corpus to SGNS/word2vec/fastText, GloVe, PPMI+SVD, and counts→PPMI→random projection with matched preprocessing/hyperparameters.
- Correct and fully specify Kendall’s τb computation; did you run Spearman and cosine as alternative similarities for PoP (before/after PPMI)? Report results.
- Clarify the argument that RI cannot be combined with PPMI post-projection and compare to applying PPMI before projection (e.g., Achlioptas-style).
- Provide comprehensive end-to-end timing, memory usage, and throughput vs. the baselines.
- Extend evaluation to additional benchmarks (WS-353, SimLex-999, RW, MTurk, RG-65, MC-30) and other corpora.

Additional comments:
- Fix Figure 1 plotting/legend to show all referenced curves.
- Typo: “large extend” → “extent.”
- Recommend releasing code/embeddings and specifying random seeds for reproducibility.
