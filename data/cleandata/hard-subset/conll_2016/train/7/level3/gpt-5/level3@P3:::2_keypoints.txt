- Summary: Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse nonnegative random-projection method for incremental word embeddings, claiming post-projection PPMI applicability and improved similarity metrics (Kendall’s τb pre-PPMI, Pearson’s r post-PPMI) with competitive MEN results.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple sparse, nonnegative random projection for word co-occurrence data enabling post-hoc PPMI weighting in projected space.

- Contribution 2: Empirically studies PoP hyperparameters (dimension, sparsity, similarity metric) and compares preliminarily to RI and count-based models.

- Contribution 3: Claims efficiency benefits: fast incremental construction and cheaper weighting versus full co-occurrence PPMI.

- Strength: Supports streaming/incremental construction by assigning random index vectors to contexts and accumulating word vectors online, avoiding full co-occurrence matrices.

- Strength: Provides clear empirical diagnostics on dimensionality and sparsity trade-offs, including helpful histograms of distributions before/after PPMI.

- Strength: Sensible use of Kendall’s τb for pre-PPMI and Pearson’s r for post-PPMI, backed by distributional reasoning and results.

- Strength: Achieves competitive MEN performance (~ρ=0.75) with a simple, training-free pipeline (PoP+PPMI).

- Weakness: Novelty and positioning unclear; relation to feature hashing, Count-Min Sketch, nonnegative random projections, and RI variants is not well delineated.

- Weakness: Applying PPMI after projection is not well justified—PPMI’s meaning after dimension mixing, bias/collision effects, and comparison to PPMI-before-projection are unaddressed.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation breadth and baselines — experiments only on MEN and lacking comparisons to SGNS/CBOW, GloVe, PPMI+SVD (tuned), RI variants, hashing/CMS, and multiple benchmarks or downstream tasks.

- Weakness: Efficiency claims lack empirical measurements — no wall-clock time, memory, or throughput comparisons versus baselines.

- Weakness: Clarity and organization issues — definitions, assumptions, and key arguments are scattered, imprecise, and sometimes unsupported.

- Weakness: Language and grammar issues reduce readability and require careful proofreading.

- Question: How does PoP theoretically and empirically differ from feature hashing or Count-Min Sketch applied to the word–context matrix?

- Question: Why is PPMI preferable after projection rather than before, and what bias do collisions introduce in PoP+PPMI?

- Question: Can you provide wall-clock time, memory, and throughput comparisons for model construction, PPMI computation, and similarity computation against relevant baselines, with variance?

- Question: How sensitive are results to corpus size and preprocessing choices, and can you report results on additional datasets and benchmarks?

- Question: Is there any theoretical guarantee or bound on expected inner product distortion for the nonzero-mean sparse projections, or at least empirical characterization of rank-order preservation?

- Additional comment: Include a concise algorithm box or pseudocode for PoP (inputs, parameters, update rules).

- Additional comment: Define and use notation consistently; ensure equations introduce variables before use.

- Additional comment: Report multiple random seeds, confidence intervals, and significance testing for comparisons.

- Additional comment: Improve the introduction to clearly state contributions, scope, and hypotheses and move background material to a related work section.

- Recommendation: Recommend rejection in current form; paper has potential but needs clearer novelty, stronger evaluation (including efficiency), better justification for post-projection PPMI, and substantial rewriting.
