- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse random-projection matrix with nonnegative entries to enable post-hoc PPMI weighting on low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Claims computational savings while achieving competitive MEN relatedness scores (~0.75), especially when using Pearson after PPMI.  

- Empirical finding: sparse nonnegative random projection + PPMI + Pearson yields competitive MEN performance at low cost.  
- Introduces PoP as a random-projection variant with positive expectation to preserve nonnegativity for PPMI.  
- Explores Kendall’s τb as a similarity metric for the non-Gaussian pre-PPMI projected space.  

- Emphasizes practical efficiency: build low-dim embeddings first, then apply PPMI to reduce weighting/factorization cost.  
- Shows reasonable empirical MEN performance (~0.75) with simple PoP+PPMI+Pearson pipelines.  
- Method is easy to implement and suitable for streaming due to incremental construction like RI.  
- Provides some parameter sensitivity analysis (dimensionality m, number of nonzeros) giving initial guidance.  

- Paper is hard to follow; key arguments (e.g., necessity of E(R)>0 for PPMI) are asserted rather than demonstrated.  
- Novelty vs prior random projections (RI, RRI, ISA, RMI) is unclear; differences and advantages are insufficiently substantiated.  
- Evaluation is too narrow (only MEN); lacks other benchmarks and comparisons to strong baselines (SGNS, GloVe, PPMI+SVD) and runtime/memory metrics.  
- Theoretical grounding is thin: no distortion guarantees, formal complexity analysis, or clear claims about cosine vs correlation.  
- Not self-contained for readers unfamiliar with random projections; background and rationale are sparse and some statements are imprecise.  
- Numerous English errors and unclear sentences harm readability.  

- Request formalization of the projection distribution T, its expectation/variance, and a clear argument why E(R)>0 is necessary/sufficient for valid PPMI post-projection.  
- Ask for wall-clock time and memory comparisons against baselines (count+PPMI, SGNS, GloVe, RI) on the same hardware.  
- Request additional benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999, RG-65, Rare Words, SimVerb) and ablations: PoP without PPMI, PoP+cosine, RI+PPMI (if feasible), PoP+SVD.  
- Ask for clarification and supporting statistics on the role and benefit of Kendall’s τb vs Pearson/cosine given distributional assumptions.  

- Overall recommendation: substantially improve presentation, clarity, theoretical grounding, and evaluation breadth before resubmission.
