- Summary: Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method producing nonnegative low-dimensional vectors that allow post-projection PPMI and report ~0.75 MEN with claimed speed/memory gains.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple incremental random projection using sparse positive index vectors enabling post-projection weighting (PPMI) at low dimensionality.

- Contribution 2: Empirically observes that Kendall’s τb works better on raw PoP vectors while Pearson’s r is better after PPMI, with a brief study of dimensionality (m) and sparsity (s) and runtime advantages.

- Contribution 3: Provides a practical, fast recipe for semantic space construction that approaches competitive MEN performance without neural training or SVD.

- Strength: Very efficient and simple—PoP only needs sparse positive index vectors and summation, suitable for streaming/incremental construction.

- Strength: Competitive lightweight performance—PoP+PPMI+Pearson reaches ~0.75 on MEN, approaching neural methods without training costs.

- Strength: Clear algorithmic description and ablations—reproducible two-step method with reported variability and hyperparameter exploration.

- Strength: Insightful connection between distribution shape and similarity metric choice (τb for non-Gaussian, Pearson after Gaussianizing PPMI).

- Strength: Well-suited for incremental/online corpora and dynamic vocabularies due to compositionality and low retraining needs.

- Weakness: Lacks strong, controlled baselines and direct comparisons to SGNS/word2vec, GloVe, fastText, and PPMI+SVD; claims of state-of-the-art are weakened without this.

- Weakness: Motivation for post-projection PPMI is underjustified—no comparison to PPMI-before-projection or PPMI→projection pipelines to show superiority.

- Weakness: Similarity metric definition issues—Kendall’s τb definition appears nonstandard and metric choices are inconsistently applied without a principled protocol.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation—only MEN on lemmatized UKWaC; missing other benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex, RareWord, SimVerb), downstream tasks, OOV handling, and significance testing.

- Weakness: Thin theoretical grounding—distributional claims and variance arguments lack formal bounds or concentration analyses specific to PoP.

- Weakness: Presentation issues—figures/legends (Figure 1) and minor language/notation errors need fixing.

- Question: Provide controlled comparisons with SGNS, GloVe, PPMI/SPPMI+SVD, and randomized SVD including training time, memory, and variance.

- Question: Report end-to-end runtimes and memory for PoP+PPMI versus count-based+PPMI+SVD at comparable performance.

- Question: Compare PoP+PPMI to PPMI-before-projection and PPMI+randomized SVD to clarify whether PoP’s advantage is computational or quality-driven.

- Question: Clarify Kendall’s τb definition/implementation and present head-to-head results for τb, cosine, and Pearson before and after PPMI across m and s.

- Question: Explain hyperparameter selection for m and s—were they tuned on MEN dev, fixed a priori, and how sensitive are results to s at large m?

- Question: Discuss generality—can PoP handle contextualized features (subword/dependency contexts) and does positivity constrain expressivity or induce bias?
