Summary and Contributions
- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse random projection with E(R) > 0 that supports post-hoc weighting (e.g., PPMI) in low-dimensional spaces.
- Empirically demonstrates competitive MEN relatedness performance (≈0.75) with far lower compute than neural embeddings by applying PPMI after projection.
- Observes that PoP preserves some heavy-tailed/distributional properties pre-weighting, enabling a later Gaussianization step via PPMI and Pearson.

Strengths
- Efficiency and simplicity: The method is computationally light (no expensive training or factorization) and incremental, offering a compelling alternative to neural embeddings for rapid, large-scale construction.
- Distribution-aware pipeline: The idea to retain non-Gaussian structure via positive-only projections and then switch to Pearson after PPMI is principled and practically effective.
- Parameter exploration: The paper includes useful ablations (m, sparsity s, number of nonzeros), highlighting stability/variance trends and giving practitioners actionable guidance.
- Practical viability: Achieving ~0.75 on MEN with small vectors and minimal resources is attractive for constrained settings and for rapid iteration.

Weaknesses
- Missing strong baselines and direct comparisons: No controlled, same-corpus comparisons to SGNS, GloVe, SVD(PPMI), or fastText. Claims of competitiveness therefore remain unsubstantiated.
- Metric inconsistency and unclear motivation: The paper uses Kendall’s τb for similarities pre-weighting and Pearson post-PPMI, yet evaluates with Spearman ρ on MEN. The choice to avoid Spearman for similarities (which is standard in semantic tasks) is not justified, and the motivation for PPMI-after-projection (vs. weighting before projection) lacks theoretical support.
- Nonstandard and under-explained Kendall definition: The τb definition appears to compare only adjacent pairs rather than all pairs, which is nonstandard; this calls into question similarity scores and complexity claims.
- Limited evaluation: MEN only. Standard relatedness/similarity benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, SimVerb-3500, MTurk, RG-65, RareWords) and downstream tasks are missing.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 appears to show only two curves although three methods are discussed. Minor typo: “large extend” → “extent.”

Questions to Authors
- Please clarify your exact definition and implementation of Kendall’s τb (pairs considered, handling of ties) and reconcile with Knight’s algorithm.
- Why is PPMI-after-projection theoretically meaningful? What properties are preserved so that PPMI on PoP approximates PPMI on counts?
- Provide same-corpus, same-preprocessing comparisons to SGNS, GloVe, and SVD(PPMI), with wall-clock time and memory.
- Justify the choice of similarity metrics; why not Spearman for vector similarities?

Additional Comments
- Please fix Figure 1 (missing line/legend) and the typo noted above. Include results on standard benchmarks.
