- Paper proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse, non-negative random projections to preserve long-tailed distributions and enable post-hoc PPMI weighting on low-dimensional vectors.  
- Central empirical claim: PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves ≈0.75 Spearman ρ on MEN and is much faster than training neural embeddings or computing PPMI on full counts.  

Contributions:
- Introduces a non-negative, sparse random-projection scheme that preserves structure for downstream PPMI weighting.  
- Empirical study showing competitive MEN performance and analysis of PoP hyperparameters (dimension m, sparsity s) and similarity choices.  
- Practical framing for incremental/streaming semantic-space construction without heavy training.  

Strengths:
- Method is simple, fast, and practical for large corpora and frequent updates.  
- Clear empirical improvement over classic RI and raw counts on MEN; PoP+PPMI+Pearson is competitive on that benchmark.  
- Provides insightful parameter exploration and qualitative distributional analyses supporting method choices.  
- Conceptual observation that E[R] > 0 enables count-based weighting after projection, which may inspire hybrids.  

Weaknesses:
- Lacks controlled comparisons to strong baselines (SGNS/CBOW, GloVe, PPMI+SVD) on the same corpus and preprocessing.  
- Ambiguity and possible nonstandard use of Kendall’s τb; insufficient ablation vs cosine/Pearson across settings.  
- Evaluation is too narrow (mainly MEN); missing standard benchmarks (WordSim, SimLex, Rare Words, SimVerb, analogies).  
- Minimal theoretical justification: no formal analysis or bounds on how PoP affects co-occurrence statistics or distortion with m and s.  
- PPMI semantics after projection are unclear because projected “counts” mix many contexts; no justification for PPMI’s validity post-projection.  
- Missing diagnostics and reproducibility details: no runtime/memory baselines, significance tests, full hyperparameter/preprocessing/seeds, and some figure/reporting issues.  

Questions for authors (requested in rebuttal):
- Provide a precise, standard definition of Kendall’s τb used, how ties are handled, and an ablation comparing τb vs cosine vs Pearson for PoP and PoP+PPMI across m and s.  
- Add controlled comparisons on the same UKWaC setup to SGNS/GloVe (tuned), PPMI+SVD (typical truncations 200–500), and PPMI variants; report Spearman ρ on MEN, WordSim-353 (Sim/Rel), and SimLex-999.  
- Report actual runtime and peak memory for building PoP, computing PPMI on PoP, and baselines (full counts+PPMI, SGNS, GloVe) with wall-clock times.  
- Explain interpretation of PPMI computed on PoP-projected counts and whether/how it approximates true PPMI; compare PoP+PPMI vs true PPMI+SVD on a smaller tractable corpus.  
- Ablate necessity of E[R] > 0: compare to biased {−1,+1} R with positive bias or adding a constant offset to zero-mean projections.  
- Clarify preprocessing (windowing, POS, stopwords, frequency thresholds), chosen m and s for best score, random seeds, and provide code/embeddings.  

Additional comments:
- Fix Figure 1: three described curves (counts, RI, PoP) but only two visible; clarify legend/visuals.  
- Minor typo and grammatical issues (e.g., “to a large extend” → “to a large extent”); recommend proofreading.  
- Complexity note: τb computation may be costly for large vocabularies; nearest-neighbor retrieval may need approximate indexing or pruning.  
- Clarify how PPMI marginals are computed over PoP matrices and whether smoothing is applied.
