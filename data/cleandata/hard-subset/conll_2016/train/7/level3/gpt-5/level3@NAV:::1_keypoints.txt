- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse random projection with E(R) > 0 that supports post-hoc weighting (e.g., PPMI) in low-dimensional spaces.
- Empirically demonstrates competitive MEN relatedness (~0.75) with far lower compute than neural embeddings by applying PPMI after projection.
- Claims PoP preserves heavy-tailed/distributional properties pre-weighting, enabling Gaussianization via PPMI and Pearson.

- Efficiency and simplicity: computationally light, incremental, no expensive training or factorization.
- Distribution-aware pipeline: retains non-Gaussian structure via positive-only projection, then applies Pearson after PPMI effectively.
- Parameter exploration: includes ablations (m, sparsity s, number of nonzeros) showing stability and variance trends.
- Practical viability: achieves ~0.75 on MEN with small vectors and minimal resources, useful for constrained settings.

- Missing strong baselines and direct comparisons to SGNS, GloVe, SVD(PPMI), or fastText on the same corpus/preprocessing.
- Metric inconsistency and unclear motivation: uses Kendall’s τb pre-weighting, Pearson post-PPMI, and evaluates with Spearman ρ without justification; unclear theoretical case for PPMI-after-projection vs. before.
- Nonstandard and under-explained Kendall definition that appears to compare only adjacent pairs, raising questions about similarity scores and complexity claims.
- Limited evaluation: only MEN reported; other standard benchmarks and downstream tasks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, SimVerb-3500, etc.) are missing.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 seems to show two curves despite three methods discussed; minor typo “large extend” → “extent.”

- Clarify exact definition and implementation of Kendall’s τb (which pairs are considered, tie handling) and reconcile with Knight’s algorithm.
- Explain why PPMI-after-projection is theoretically meaningful and what properties are preserved so PPMI on PoP approximates PPMI on counts.
- Provide same-corpus, same-preprocessing comparisons to SGNS, GloVe, and SVD(PPMI), including wall-clock time and memory.
- Justify the choice of similarity metrics and explain why Spearman was not used for vector similarities.

- Fix Figure 1 (missing line/legend) and the noted typo.
- Include results on standard relatedness/similarity benchmarks and downstream evaluation.
