- Proposal: Positive-only Projections (PoP) — sparse, non-negative random projections to build low-dimensional word embeddings that allow post-hoc PPMI weighting.
- Key claim: PoP preserves long-tailed count structure so PPMI in low-dim yields competitive relatedness (≈0.75 Spearman on MEN) with high efficiency and incremental construction.
- Similarity rationale: Use Kendall’s τb in raw (non-Gaussian) PoP space and Pearson after PPMI (Gaussianized).
- Contribution 1: Practical observation that positive-only, sparse random projections enable efficient post-projection PPMI and competitive MEN performance at low cost.
- Contribution 2: Simple, incremental semantic space construction combining positive-only random index vectors, rank-based similarity pre-weighting, and Pearson after PPMI.
- Contribution 3: Empirical parameter study over dimensionality m and sparsity s, including guidance that 1–4 non-zero entries often suffice.
- Strength: Computational efficiency and practicality — avoids heavy training and large dense matrices; suitable for large-scale or streaming settings.
- Strength: Coherent intuition for similarity choices with empirical support for τb vs Pearson depending on distributional assumptions.
- Strength: Useful parameter exploration showing trade-offs between orthogonality and distortion.
- Strength: Achieves competitive MEN result with straightforward data and tuning, indicating viability for relatedness tasks.
- Weakness: Clarity and organization are poor — exposition has logic jumps, insufficient background, and many language errors needing substantial rewriting.
- Weakness: Novelty vs prior random projection methods is underdeveloped; lacks rigorous positioning against RI, ISA, RMI, feature hashing, and Achlioptas-style projections.
- Weakness: Theoretical claims are weak — no analysis showing how PoP preserves joint/marginal statistics or bounds on approximation to PMI.
- Weakness: Evaluation is too narrow — only MEN tested; needs more benchmarks (WordSim353, SimLex-999, RG-65, RareWords), analogies, downstream tasks, and comparisons to strong baselines.
- Weakness: Missing ablations and controls — unclear contributions of PPMI vs positive-only projection vs similarity choice; need direct comparisons and systematic similarity tests.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees or error bounds; connection to known results on non-negative/sparse projections is insufficient.
- Question: How does PoP differ in principle and practice from prior non-negative/binary sparse projection schemes and feature hashing?
- Question: What does PPMI computed on the projected matrix estimate — does PPMI(PoP) approximate PPMI(counts) in any quantifiable sense?
- Question: What conditions on s and m guarantee non-zero marginals in practice, and how do collisions affect PPMI reliability?
- Question: Can you add results on additional benchmarks and compare to count+PPMI(+SVD) and SGNS on the same corpus, reporting means, stddevs, and significance?
- Question: Provide wall-clock and memory comparisons versus count+PPMI(+SVD) and SGNS on UKWaC, including preprocessing and PPMI times at different m.
- Additional comment: Improve writing, define notation consistently, support claims or label as conjectures, and release code/embeddings for reproducibility.
- Additional comment: Include cosine alongside τb/Pearson and report sensitivity to context window and stopword handling.
- Recommendation: Reject for now — promising idea but requires substantial improvements in clarity, positioning, theoretical grounding, and broader evaluation.
