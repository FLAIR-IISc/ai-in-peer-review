- Proposal: Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection with E(R)>0 to enable PPMI weighting on low-dimensional word vectors, evaluated on MEN (ρ ≈ 0.75).  
- Contribution 1: Proposes PoP to allow PPMI weighting after dimensionality reduction, merging random projections with count-based weighting.  
- Contribution 2: Empirically demonstrates PoP+PPMI can be competitive on the MEN relatedness benchmark.  
- Strength 1: Novel concept — designing random projections to preserve properties needed for PPMI is original and well-motivated.  
- Strength 2: Computational advantage — computing PPMI on low-dimensional vectors and incremental projection suggests substantial efficiency gains.  
- Strength 3: Promising initial performance — reported results on MEN are comparable to many contemporary state-of-the-art systems.  
- Weakness 1: Severe clarity issues — paper is hard to follow, argumentation is confusing, and key claims lack justification or proof.  
- Weakness 2: Unsupported technical claims — assertions like "E(R)=0 prevents weighting" and exact zero row/column sums are stated without clear mathematical justification.  
- Weakness 3: Insufficient evaluation — results shown only on MEN, no tests on standard similarity/analogy benchmarks (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim-353, TOEFL).  
- Weakness 4: Language and formatting errors throughout degrade readability and professionalism.  
- Question 1: Please clarify the claim that RI-built models have row/column sums equal to zero — is this exact or asymptotic, and why does E(R)=0 force this?  
- Question 2: Provide theoretical intuition or a sketch showing why E(R)>0 preserves distributional information for PPMI while E(R)=0 does not.  
- Question 3: Why restrict evaluation to MEN, and can you report results on standard similarity benchmarks (SimLex-999, WordSim-353, etc.) to substantiate state-of-the-art claims?
