- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection with E(R) > 0 that preserves counts for post-hoc weighting.  
- PoP enables applying count-based weighting (e.g., PPMI) to low-dimensional vectors, unlike standard random indexing.  
- Demonstrates an efficient pipeline (PoP + PPMI) that trains models in seconds/minutes and achieves strong MEN performance (0.75 Spearman’s ρ).  
- Primary contributions: a novel projection method enabling post-hoc weighting and an efficient, effective embedding pipeline.  
- Strength: very high computational efficiency and scalability by applying expensive weighting on small vectors.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated modification of random projections to preserve sums needed for weighting.  
- Strength: strong empirical result on MEN with a useful ablation study over dimensionality and sparsity.  
- Weakness: no direct quantitative comparisons to standard baselines (PPMI+SVD, SGNS, GloVe) trained on the same UKWaC corpus.  
- Weakness: unconventional and unclearly specified use of Kendall’s τb for vector similarity, undermining unweighted PoP results.  
- Weakness: narrow evaluation limited to a single dataset (MEN) instead of a broader suite (WordSim, SimLex, analogy tasks).  
- Weakness: presentation/figure clarity issues (Figure 1 legend/caption ambiguous about methods shown).  
- Questions for authors: clarify how Kendall’s τb is computed between two vectors and rationale for its choice over other rank correlations.  
- Questions for authors: provide baseline results (PPMI+SVD, SGNS, GloVe) trained on the same corpus to contextualize performance.  
- Questions for authors: report whether Spearman’s ρ was used internally instead of Kendall’s τb for PoP and how that affected results.
