- Proposal of Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection with E(R) > 0 that lets post-projection weighting (e.g., PPMI) be applied to low-dimensional vectors.  
- PoP differs from Random Indexing (RI) by using a non-negative-expected projection matrix so projected vectors retain properties (non-negativity) needed for weighting.  
- Pipeline: create low-dimensional embeddings via PoP, then apply PPMI to those dense vectors for efficient weighted embeddings.  
- Empirical claim: PoP+PPMI is computationally efficient and achieves competitive performance on the MEN semantic relatedness task (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.75–0.78).  
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated idea that bridges efficient random projections and weighted count models by enabling post-projection weighting.  
- Strength: Significant potential for computational savings by applying PPMI in low-dimensional dense space rather than on large sparse matrices.  
- Strength: Strong preliminary results on MEN suggest the method can match more complex models on relatedness.  
- Weakness: Paper is poorly written and hard to follow; key arguments and implications of E(R)>0 are not clearly explained.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is narrow (only MEN); lacks broader benchmarks like WordSim-353, SimLex-999, and analogy tasks to show generality.  
- Weakness: Numerous grammatical and formatting errors that reduce clarity and polish.  
- Question: Please clarify why RI-generated vectors (with zero-centered R) have column/row sums of zero and thus cannot support PPMI — provide an intuitive explanation.  
- Question: Justify the switch to Kendall’s τb for PoP-projected space and why cosine similarity (effective in count spaces) is insufficient.  
- Question: Explain why unweighted PoP performance decreases beyond 2000 dimensions (hypotheses about non-zero elements, projection instability, or parameter sensitivity).
