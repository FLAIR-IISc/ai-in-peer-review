- Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection with positive expected values enabling PPMI-style weighting on low-dimensional word embeddings and reports competitive results on MEN.  
- Key contribution: proposing a projection matrix with positive expectation to make low-dimensional spaces compatible with PPMI and similar weighting schemes.  
- Secondary contribution: preliminary empirical evidence that PoP+PPMI performs well on a standard semantic relatedness task.  
- Strength: conceptually novel — addresses a limitation of traditional zero-centered random projections and could bridge scalable projection methods with PPMI-style count-based strengths.  
- Strength: promises substantial computational efficiency by allowing PPMI computations in low-dimensional space instead of large sparse co-occurrence matrices.  
- Weakness: severe lack of clarity and poor presentation; explanations and design rationale are hard to follow and inaccessible to non-experts.  
- Weakness: contains unsupported assertions and weak logical flow with apparent unsubstantiated leaps in reasoning.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is too narrow (only MEN); broader benchmarking (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999) or justification for single-dataset use is required.  
- Weakness: many grammatical and stylistic errors that impede readability and obscure technical content.  
- Conclusion: not ready for publication; requires substantial revision to improve clarity, strengthen arguments, and broaden empirical evaluation.
