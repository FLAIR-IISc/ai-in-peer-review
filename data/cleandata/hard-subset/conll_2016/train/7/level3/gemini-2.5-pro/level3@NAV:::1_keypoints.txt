- Introduces Positive-Only Projections (PoP), a random projection with only positive entries that preserves distributional properties in low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Main contribution: enables applying PPMI weighting efficiently in low-dimensional space, combining random projection speed with PPMI benefits.  
- Secondary contribution: shows the hybrid PoP+PPMI approach can achieve competitive results on a semantic relatedness task.  
- Strength: Very fast training—PPMI applied after dimensionality reduction reduces model construction time from hours/days to minutes/seconds.  
- Strength: Novel technical insight—E(R) > 0 in PoP preserves applicability of weighting schemes unlike standard random indexing.  
- Weakness: No direct quantitative comparison to established baselines (word2vec, GloVe, PPMI+SVD), so claimed competitiveness is unverified.  
- Weakness: Methodological issues—non-standard definition/use of Kendall’s tau and inconsistent choice of similarity metrics (Spearman used for final eval but not internally).  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—experiments confined to the MEN dataset; broader benchmarks (e.g., SimLex-999, analogy tasks) are needed.  
- Question: Please provide a table comparing best PoP+PPMI MEN scores to word2vec (CBOW/Skip-gram), GloVe, and PPMI+SVD with citations.  
- Question: Clarify why a non-standard implementation of Kendall’s tau was used and why Spearman wasn’t used consistently.
