Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random projection with E(R) > 0 that enables post-hoc PPMI weighting on low-dimensional embeddings.  
Claims computational efficiency by applying PPMI after dimensionality reduction while retaining high semantic similarity performance.  
Evaluated on the MEN dataset, achieving competitive Spearman’s ρ ≈ 0.75–0.78.  
Contribution: proposes PoP to preserve count-like structure for PPMI via a non-zero-mean projection matrix.  
Contribution: empirically demonstrates PoP+PPMI+Pearson yields strong results with an incremental, efficient pipeline.  
Strength: novel idea of designing random projections compatible with PPMI weighting.  
Strength: computationally efficient—avoids computing PPMI on very high-dimensional sparse co-occurrence matrices.  
Strength: promising empirical performance on MEN.  
Weakness: paper is unclear and poorly presented, making arguments and novelty hard to follow.  
Weakness: evaluation is too narrow (only MEN), lacking broader benchmarks and downstream tasks.  
Weakness: numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing; needs thorough proofreading.  
Question: clarify theoretical differences between PoP and other sparse random projections beyond E(R) > 0 and how PoP preserves information for PPMI.  
Question: justify use of Kendall’s τb for base vectors and Pearson’s r for PPMI vectors—report normality tests and sensitivity to similarity metric.  
Question: why limit evaluation to MEN; provide results or justification for other standard benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, Google analogy).
