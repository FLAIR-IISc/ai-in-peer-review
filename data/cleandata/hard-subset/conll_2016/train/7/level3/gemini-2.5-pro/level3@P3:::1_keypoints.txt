- Introduces Positive-Only Projections (PoP), a random projection with non-negative entries that preserves distributional properties and enables PPMI on low-dimensional vectors.  
- Contribution: a novel random projection method (PoP) that permits post-hoc weighting (e.g., PPMI) by preserving count-space distributional information.  
- Contribution: demonstrates an efficient pipeline (PoP+PPMI+Pearson) achieving strong results (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.75 on MEN) with much lower computational cost than SVD or neural training.  
- Strength: computationally efficient and scalable—avoids SVD/training and enables rapid embedding construction suitable for resource-constrained settings.  
- Strength: methodologically novel and sound—positive-only projections retain long-tailed count distributions, enabling effective PPMI transformation.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance on MEN (0.75, up to 0.78 with tuning), showing competitive quality for a lightweight method.  
- Weakness: lacks direct, controlled comparisons to standard strong baselines (word2vec/SGNS, GloVe, PPMI+SVD) trained on the same corpus, so competitiveness claims are unverified.  
- Weakness: unclear and inconsistent methodological choices—non-standard Kendall’s τb definition, unclear justification for similarity metrics, and inconsistency using Pearson internally while evaluating with Spearman.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope—only the MEN dataset is used; missing other similarity and analogy benchmarks to assess generalizability.  
- Weakness: presentation and clarity issues—Figure 1’s legend/overlapping lines are ambiguous and the paper could better motivate why PPMI-transformability is the key research direction.  
- Question: please provide direct, controlled comparisons of PoP+PPMI+Pearson against word2vec (SGNS), GloVe, and PPMI+SVD on the same UKWaC→MEN setup.  
- Question: please clarify the definition and derivation/citation of Kendall’s τb used in Section 2.2 and how pairs of observations are formed.  
- Question: why wasn’t Spearman’s ρ used as the similarity function for PoP and PoP+PPMI vectors given it is the evaluation metric and robust to non-Gaussianity?  
- Question: do the count-based and RI lines in Figure 1 perfectly overlap—if so, please state this explicitly in the caption.
