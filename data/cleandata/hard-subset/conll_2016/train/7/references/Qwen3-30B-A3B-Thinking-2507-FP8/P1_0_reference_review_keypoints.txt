- Proposal of Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection using only positive integers that enables PPMI weighting.  
- Empirical claim that PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves competitive MEN performance (~0.75) with much lower computational cost than neural embeddings.  
- Comprehensive empirical analysis of PoP parameters (dimensionality, number of non-zeros) and their impact on performance.  
- Strength: addresses a real gap by enabling PPMI on random-projection models that previously used zero-mean matrices.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation on MEN with clear parameter studies and practical guidance.  
- Strength: outperforms RI and count-based baselines, achieving high ρ with smaller context windows.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification and error bounds for PoP’s semantic preservation.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to modern SOTA neural methods (e.g., BERT-based models achieving ≈0.85 on MEN).  
- Weakness: vague claims about computational efficiency with no concrete timing or comparisons to fast methods like word2vec.  
- Weakness: inconsistent statements about dimensionality (claims “few hundred” but experiments use m=1000–5000).  
- Question: clarify what “incremental” means for PoP and how it differs from other incremental methods.  
- Question: how does the reported 0.75 compare directly to current SOTA on MEN and which neural methods are being referenced?  
- Question: reconcile the claim that 1–2 non-zero elements are optimal with Figure 5 showing better performance with more non-zeros.  
- Overall recommendation: weak acceptance conditional on providing timing comparisons, direct SOTA comparisons, theoretical clarification, and resolving dimensionality inconsistencies.
