- Summary: Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP) enabling PPMI weighting with positive random vectors, claiming PoP+PPMI+Pearson matches neural embeddings on MEN (0.75) with far lower computation time.

- Contribution 1: Introduces PoP to allow PPMI on random-projection models by using positive integer random vectors.

- Contribution 2: Empirically shows PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves competitive MEN performance (0.75) while being computationally efficient (seconds/minutes).

- Contribution 3: Offers a theoretical argument that Pearson correlation outperforms Kendall's τb after PPMI transformation.

- Strength 1: Correctly identifies a real limitation of prior random-projection methods (zero-mean Gaussian matrices cannot support PPMI).

- Strength 2: Demonstrates strong computational efficiency and practical incremental construction at low dimensionalities.

- Strength 3: Provides a well-structured empirical evaluation against count-based models and RI, showing clear gains from PPMI+Pearson.

- Weakness 1: Overstates novelty by insufficiently engaging with prior positive-sparse projection work (e.g., Baroni et al. 2007 ISA).

- Weakness 2: Lacks rigorous theoretical foundation and error bounds for non-Gaussian random projections, despite citing Johnson–Lindenstrauss.

- Weakness 3: Superficial comparison to neural methods, missing direct benchmarks against modern SOTA (GloVe, FastText, BERT).

- Weakness 4: High parameter sensitivity (s, sparsity) with only vague practical guidance, limiting reproducibility and adoption.

- Weakness 5: Incomplete explanation of why PoP+PPMI+Pearson outperforms count-based PPMI and whether positive projections themselves are the key factor.

- Question 1: How does PoP materially differ from Baroni et al.'s 2007 ISA and what concrete improvements does it offer?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide direct comparisons of PoP+PPMI+Pearson with modern SOTA (GloVe, FastText) on MEN and other benchmarks?

- Question 3: How does PoP’s incremental construction differ from and improve on RI’s incremental approach?

- Question 4: Why use Kendall's τb for PoP without PPMI and Pearson for PoP+PPMI—what theoretical justification supports this switch?

- Overall assessment: Useful practical method with clear efficiency gains but novelty is overstated, theoretical support is weak, and comparisons to SOTA are insufficient.
