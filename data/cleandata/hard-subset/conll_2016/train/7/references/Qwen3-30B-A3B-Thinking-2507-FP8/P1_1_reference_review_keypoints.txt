- Introduces Random Positive-Only Projections (PoP) to enable PPMI weighting for incremental semantic space construction.  
- Claims PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves competitive MEN performance with much lower computation than neural embeddings.  
- Provides parameter sensitivity and behavioral analysis for PoP.

- Novel solution addressing inability of zero-mean random matrices to undergo PPMI weighting.  
- Strong empirical evaluation showing advantages over RI and count-based models and fast training times.  
- Clear and compelling computational efficiency claims versus neural embeddings.

- Lacks theoretical justification or error bounds (no δ analysis) for the new random projection technique.  
- Evaluation limited to the MEN relatedness test; no broader semantic or downstream task results.  
- Insufficient direct comparisons to recent state-of-the-art neural methods (e.g., BERT/ELMo).  
- Unclear novelty relative to other positive-weight random projection approaches; distinction from prior positive methods is not well articulated.

- Clarify how PoP differs from existing "positive random indexing" or similar positive-only methods.  
- Explain mathematically why zero-mean random matrices prevent PPMI application but PoP allows it.  
- Add specific MEN performance comparisons to recent neural embeddings and a broader evaluation suite.

- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on stronger theoretical analysis, broader evaluation, and clearer comparisons to state-of-the-art.
