- Paper proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method that enables PPMI weighting for incremental semantic space construction and reports ρ=0.75 on MEN.  
- Contribution: novel random projection technique allowing PPMI, empirical competitiveness with neural embeddings, and large computational speedups (seconds/minutes vs hours/days).  

- Strength: Strong computational-efficiency claim with empirical evidence that PoP+PPMI+Pearson is fast and achieves high MEN correlation.  
- Strength: Technical insight that non-zero-mean (positive-only) projections permit PPMI weighting; authors give clear mathematical justification why RI-type zero-mean projections cannot.  
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical analysis of parameter sensitivity (m, sparsity, s) with informative visualizations of distribution and performance effects.  

- Weakness: Insufficient novelty positioning — PoP is a simple modification of existing random/sparse projection methods and not well distinguished from prior work like RI.  
- Weakness: Limited baselines — missing comparisons to fastText, GloVe, and SVD/PPMI approaches, so claimed competitiveness is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Weakness: Theoretical gap — no error bounds or Johnson–Lindenstrauss–style guarantees for PoP; only informal justification is provided.  
- Weakness: Modest empirical gains over RI and unclear advantage over standard PPMI+SVD; performance claims may be overstated.  

- Q1: Please compare PoP+PPMI+Pearson to fastText and GloVe (default settings) on MEN.  
- Q2: Clarify how PoP handles incremental vocabulary growth and updates for new words.  
- Q3: Explain motivation for using Kendall’s τb for similarity and why Pearson ultimately performed better in experiments.  

- Overall: Method is technically sound and practically useful for efficiency, but novelty and competitive claims are overstated due to limited baselines and weak theoretical grounding; recommend rejection pending stronger comparisons and theory.
