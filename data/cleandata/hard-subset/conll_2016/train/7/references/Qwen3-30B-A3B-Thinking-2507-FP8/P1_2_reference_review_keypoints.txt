- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method using positive integer vectors to enable PPMI weighting while retaining computational efficiency.
- Claims PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves competitive performance (e.g., 0.75 on MEN) comparable to neural embeddings but with dramatically lower compute time.
- Key contributions: novel PoP method, empirical demonstration of strong performance and speed, and comprehensive parameter-sensitivity analysis.
- Practical innovation: addresses incompatibility of PPMI with mean-zero random projections and offers a straightforward, useful solution for practitioners.
- Strong empirical validation: thorough comparisons to RI and count-based models across parameters, with clear performance and weight-distribution analyses.
- Thorough parameter analysis: provides guidance on dimensionality, sparsity, and recommended settings for different transformations and metrics.
- Major weakness: lacks theoretical justification and error bounds (δ), limiting understanding of method limits and generalization.
- Limited comparison to modern SOTA neural embeddings (e.g., FastText, GloVe); claims of competitiveness are vague without direct comparisons.
- Reproducibility concerns: code/embeddings promised later and some hyperparameters are only partially specified, hindering replication.
- Question: provide direct comparisons of PoP fine-tuned results (0.78/0.787) to recent literature and efficient neural baselines.
- Question: report actual timing and scaling (e.g., UKWaC build time) compared to RI and neural methods.
- Question: clarify how the model ensures vector element sums >0 in practice and handle the edge case of sum = 0 for PPMI.
- Overall recommendation: interesting and practical contribution; recommend acceptance with major revisions for added theory, SOTA comparisons, and reproducibility details.
