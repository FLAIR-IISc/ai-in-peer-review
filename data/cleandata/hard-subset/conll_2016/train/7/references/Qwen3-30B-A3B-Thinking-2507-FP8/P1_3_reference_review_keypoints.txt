- Paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method using positive integers that enables PPMI weighting and fast semantic-space construction.
- Main contribution 1: PoP method enabling PPMI weighting for random projections.
- Main contribution 2: Empirical result that PoP+PPMI+Pearson reaches 0.75 on MEN with much lower computational cost than neural embeddings.
- Main contribution 3: Detailed empirical analysis of PoP parameters (dimensionality, sparsity, non-zero counts).
- Strength 1: Novel and practical—solves zero-mean limitation of prior random projections allowing PPMI weighting.
- Strength 2: Strong empirical validation showing competitive performance and clear parameter studies.
- Strength 3: Large computational efficiency advantage—models built in seconds/minutes vs. hours/days for neural embeddings.
- Weakness 1: Lacks theoretical justification or mathematical analysis explaining why PoP works.
- Weakness 2: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., fastText, GloVe); "competitive" claim is vague without SOTA numbers.
- Weakness 3: Insufficient practical guidance on parameter choice; recommendations (e.g., s, non-zero counts) feel ad hoc.
- Questions for authors: provide SOTA MEN comparisons, concrete timing comparisons versus count-based PPMI, and plans/ideas for developing a theoretical foundation.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept conditional on adding theoretical analysis and broader SOTA comparisons.
