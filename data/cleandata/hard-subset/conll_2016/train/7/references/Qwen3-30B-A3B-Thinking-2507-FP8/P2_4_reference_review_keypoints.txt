- Main contribution: Introduces Positive-only Projection (PoP), enabling PPMI weighting on random-projection-based semantic spaces and showing competitive MEN performance with much lower compute cost.

- Practical advantage: PoP constructs semantic spaces in seconds/minutes versus hours/days for neural embeddings, addressing resource constraints.

- Technical insight: Positive-only random projections allow PPMI where zero-mean projections (e.g., RI) cannot, a novel methodological gap fill.

- Empirical validation: Experiments and ablation studies show PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves ~0.75 Spearman on MEN and reveal parameter sensitivity.

- Weakness — theory: Lacks a detailed mathematical account (e.g., justification for parameter δ) explaining why PoP works or outperforms others.

- Weakness — comparisons: Insufficient comparison with recent SOTA (fastText, GloVe, BERT, etc.), so "competitive" claims are unsubstantiated.

- Weakness — ablation depth: Does not isolate PoP’s contribution from PPMI or compare PoP+PPMI vs. count-based+PPMI to quantify novelty.

- Weakness — implementation detail: Missing specifics on parameter selection (s, m, sparsity), scaling to larger corpora, and what “careful selection” entails.

- Question: Provide direct comparisons of PoP+PPMI+Pearson to fastText, GloVe, BERT on MEN for both accuracy and computational cost.

- Question: Offer empirical or theoretical evidence on how index vector dimensionality affects memorization and semantic capacity.

- Question: Explain why PPMI works better with PoP than with count-based models and what PoP properties enable this advantage.

- Question: Compare performance and computational cost of PoP+PPMI (m ≥ 3000) versus count-based PPMI+SVD and detail PPMI application costs.

- Overall assessment: Novel, practical method with promising empirical results but weakened by theoretical gaps and missing SOTA comparisons.

- Recommendation: Accept with major revision to add theoretical justification and comprehensive comparisons with contemporary methods.
