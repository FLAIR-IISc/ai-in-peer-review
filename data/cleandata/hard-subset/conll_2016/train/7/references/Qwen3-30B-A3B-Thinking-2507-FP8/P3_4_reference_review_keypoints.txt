- Paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method with strictly positive elements to enable PPMI weighting and build semantic spaces.
- Authors claim PoP achieves competitive semantic similarity performance while using dramatically less computation than neural embeddings.

- Primary contribution 1: Novel concept of positive-only random projections enabling PPMI.
- Primary contribution 2: Empirical demonstration of PoP’s efficiency and competitive performance on the MEN test.
- Primary contribution 3: Partial theoretical justification for PoP’s effectiveness (incomplete).

- Strength 1: Correctly identifies limitation of zero-mean random projections for PPMI and proposes a novel technical fix.
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results showing PoP+PPMI+Pearson reaches ~0.75 on MEN with large efficiency gains.
- Strength 3: Clear, convincing explanation and distributional analysis for why PoP enables PPMI.

- Weakness 1: Lacks theoretical error bounds/comprehensive theoretical grounding comparable to Johnson-Lindenstrauss results.
- Weakness 2: Limited comparisons to state-of-the-art (no direct comparisons to fastText, GloVe, etc.), weakening performance claims.
- Weakness 3: Inconsistent methodology reporting (claims few-hundred dimensions but experiments use m=1000–5000), undermining efficiency claims.
- Weakness 4: Evaluation scope limited to MEN; needs additional datasets (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim353) for generalizability.

- Question 1: How can the authors justify error bounds (δ) for PoP and relate them to JL lemma for Gaussian projections?
- Question 2: Can the authors provide direct comparisons to modern embeddings (fastText, GloVe) on MEN and other similarity datasets?
- Question 3: Why claim PoP dimensionality is “a few hundred” when experiments use 1000–5000, and how does that affect efficiency?
- Question 4: Clarify the claim that PoP outperforms RI without weighting, given Figure 1 shows similar performance.

- Overall: Novel and practically promising approach but weakened by lack of theory, limited comparisons, and inconsistent methodology.
- Recommendation: Weak reject, with encouragement to revise addressing theory, broader evaluation, and clearer methodology.
