- Summary: Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP) — random projections with strictly positive integers — enabling PPMI weighting while remaining computationally efficient and yielding competitive semantic similarity scores.

- Contribution: Introduces a positive-valued random projection method that allows PPMI weighting, addressing a limitation of standard zero-mean random projection techniques.

- Contribution: Demonstrates strong computational efficiency for building PoP models (seconds/minutes) compared to neural embedding training (hours/days).

- Contribution: Shows competitive performance on semantic similarity (MEN correlation ~0.75) when combined with PPMI and Pearson.

- Strength: Correctly identifies and contextualizes the limitation that zero-mean random matrices prevent PPMI weighting.

- Strength: Provides convincing empirical evidence of large runtime advantages over neural methods using standard datasets (MEN, UKWaC).

- Strength: Achieves practically competitive semantic similarity scores versus many neural embedding approaches.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification or bounds for PoP (no mathematical account for parameter choices or why positive projections work).

- Weakness: Inadequate comparisons to recent state-of-the-art and efficient methods (e.g., FastText, GloVe, contextual models like BERT/ELMo).

- Weakness: Claims about quantitative runtime advantages lack concrete measurements or comparative benchmarks versus count-based PPMI+factorization.

- Weakness: Novelty may be overstated; PoP appears as a minor variant of random indexing rather than a fundamentally new approach.

- Weakness: No ablation study to isolate the contributions of positive projections, dimensionality, and PPMI weighting.

- Question: Provide specific runtime comparisons (e.g., building 100K-word models) between PoP+PPMI and count-based PPMI+factorization.

- Question: Provide direct comparisons on MEN to recent neural methods (FastText, GloVe, etc.) for verification.

- Question: Explain why increasing m over 2000 decreases unweighted PoP performance and rule out hyperparameter artifacts.

- Question: Compare PoP to recent sparse/random projection embedding work (e.g., Gao et al. 2022) and similar approaches.

- Overall: Technically sound with practical value for efficiency, but lacking theory, thorough SOTA comparisons, runtime quantification, and ablation analyses; needs significant revisions for a conference expecting both theoretical and practical contributions.
