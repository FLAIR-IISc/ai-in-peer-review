- Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse, nonnegative random projections enabling PPMI weighting in reduced spaces.
- Empirical study of PoP’s hyperparameters (dimensionality m, sparsity s) and similarity measures showing competitive MEN results (~0.75–0.78 Spearman).
- Argues that performing weighting after projection yields major speed/memory benefits and shows qualitative distributional effects pre/post-PPMI.
- Strength: simple, incremental, low-memory method suitable for practitioners without large-model resources.
- Strength: core idea unlocks PPMI after projection, matching PPMI-on-counts empirically at lower compute.
- Strength: thorough parameter study including variance across runs and effects of non-orthogonality as s increases.
- Strength: clear argument for potential speedups over dense PMI/SVD and neural embeddings.
- Strength: authors acknowledge lack of theoretical guarantees and frame the work as empirical.
- Weakness: method is similar to feature hashing/Count-Min Sketch and lacks citation/comparison to those sketching techniques and related nonnegative projections.
- Weakness: Eq. (2) definition of tij is ambiguous (notation like “1Uα c” unclear); needs precise specification and reproducibility.
- Weakness: evaluation limited and uneven—only MEN used, missing other benchmarks and downstream tasks; baselines (PPMI+SVD, SGNS/GloVe) are incomplete or not reported quantitatively.
- Weakness: mixed similarity metrics (Kendall’s τb, Pearson) create apples-to-apples fairness issues; metric consistency is needed.
- Weakness: conceptual gap—PPMI semantics change after projection and no theoretical/empirical bounds on distortion are provided.
- Weakness: efficiency claims lack concrete evidence—no wall-clock times, memory usage, or comparative benchmarks.
- Weakness: several reproducibility details are underspecified (preprocessing, window weighting, stopword handling, index-vector sampling, seeds); code pending acceptance.
- Question: precisely define Eq. (2) and state what distribution and choices (ceil/floor, α) were used in experiments.
- Question: clarify differences from feature hashing/Count-Min Sketch and provide empirical/theoretical comparisons.
- Question: add comparisons to SGNS/GloVe and PPMI+SVD with numbers; report consistent metrics (cosine and Pearson) across methods.
- Question: provide justification that PPMI on projected counts approximates PPMI on original counts (correlations or distortion bounds).
- Question: provide runtime and memory benchmarks (end-to-end) vs dense PPMI and SGNS on UKWaC, including variance across runs.
- Question: report robustness across datasets, at least one more similarity dataset and a downstream task, and check different context types (e.g., syntactic).
- Comment: correct phrasing about RI sums—expectations may be zero but actual entries are not, which affects PMI applicability.
- Comment: justify use of Kendall’s τb and include cosine/ρ results to rule out metric-specific improvements.
- Comment: verify and detail SVD setup if claiming negligible gains, since this contradicts prior work.
