1) Contribution: Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP), a sparse nonnegative random projection that permits incremental construction of semantic spaces.  
2) Contribution: Enables post-sketch weighting (PPMI) and recommends Kendall’s τb for raw PoP and Pearson’s r after PPMI.  
3) Contribution: Empirical study showing PoP (especially PoP+PPMI+Pearson) outperforms RI and counts on MEN-3000 with small models (ρ ≈ 0.75).  

4) Strength: Simple, practical, streaming two-line update; no training required and suitable for low-resource or rapid prototyping.  
5) Strength: Empirical improvements over RI and raw counts; competitive MEN scores comparable to some neural baselines.  
6) Strength: Demonstrates that post-weighting compressed sketches (PPMI) substantially boosts accuracy and that Pearson is preferable after PPMI.  
7) Strength: Thoughtful hyperparameter exploration (m, sparsity s) with actionable recommendation of very sparse signatures (1–2 nonzeros).  
8) Strength: Potential efficiency gains in time and memory; τb cost is small for moderate m (≈10^3).  

9) Weakness: Limited novelty—method is close to Count-Min Sketch/feature hashing applied to co-occurrence counts and lacks positioning against those baselines.  
10) Weakness: Insufficient evaluation—only MEN-3000 reported; missing other similarity datasets and extrinsic tasks and no direct SGNS/GloVe/PPMI+SVD comparisons on the same corpus.  
11) Weakness: Theoretical/methodological gaps—Equation (2) underspecified, incorrect claims about RI row sums, and unrigorous cosine–Pearson equivalence argument.  
12) Weakness: No analysis of collision bias/variance or of PMI estimation error induced by sketching.  
13) Weakness: Scalability concerns—Kendall’s τb is heavier than cosine for large-scale nearest-neighbor search and only 3k pairs evaluated.  
14) Weakness: Overstated claims about intractability of PPMI and limited SVD gains without fair runtime/memory comparisons to modern sparse implementations.  
15) Weakness: Reproducibility issues—code/embeddings not yet released and best hyperparameters, seeds, hardware, and timing are under-specified.  

16) Question: How does PoP theoretically and empirically differ from Count-Min Sketch or unsigned feature hashing followed by PPMI; please include that baseline and collision analysis.  
17) Question: Please formalize Equation (2): exact pmf of tij, whether s is fixed count or probability, and motivation for α=0.5; do integer magnitudes matter versus all-ones?  
18) Question: What is the theoretical relationship between PMI on compressed buckets and PMI on the original matrix; under what collision assumptions does PoP+PPMI approximate full PPMI?  
19) Question: Provide concrete efficiency comparisons—memory, build time, PPMI computation for PoP vs sparse counts+PPMI and vs SGNS/GloVe on same hardware, plus similarity/NN costs.  
20) Question: Add evaluations on standard datasets (WS-353, SimLex-999, SimVerb-3500, RW) and apples-to-apples comparisons to PPMI+SVD, SGNS, and GloVe on UKWaC.  
21) Question: Why use τb—have you compared cosine and Spearman on PoP and PoP+PPMI, and how will τb be made tractable for large-scale retrieval?  
22) Question: Was RI fairly tuned—did you try unsigned/nonnegative RI or RI combined with weighting/normalization for comparison?  

23) Overall: Idea is practical and promising on MEN but novelty is weakened by missing connections to established sketching methods, limited datasets, and lack of theoretical and empirical analysis of collisions and efficiency.
