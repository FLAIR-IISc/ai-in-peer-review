Summary and claimed contributions
- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse random projection with strictly non-negative integer entries, enabling incremental construction of low-dimensional word vectors that can subsequently be reweighted with PPMI.
- Advocates Kendall’s τb for similarity in the unweighted PoP space (non-Gaussian), and Pearson’s r after PPMI (approximately Gaussian).
- Presents empirical results on MEN (using UKWaC) showing PoP > RI and count-based for small windows, and PoP+PPMI+Pearson ≈ strong count-based PPMI baselines at far lower cost; includes small parameter studies (m, sparsity s).

Strengths
- Simplicity and efficiency: The idea to use a nonnegative random projection so that PPMI remains well-defined is elegant and practical. The incremental construction is attractive for streaming settings.
- Empirical clarity on parameter sensitivity: The multiple-run averages and shading for min/max across 10 runs are good practice; the exploration of m and number of non-zero entries is informative.
- Practical observation on similarity metrics: The switch from Kendall to Pearson post-PPMI is sensible and empirically motivated.
- Potential usefulness: If validated, PoP could offer a cheap alternative to heavy-weight count-based PPMI+SVD or neural embeddings in resource-constrained settings.

Weaknesses
- Theoretical and statistical claims are weak or inaccurate:
  - The statement that in RI “the sum of values of row and column vectors … are always 0” and thus PPMI is undefined is incorrect. RI typically uses sparse ternary {−1,0,+1} (or Gaussian) projections; sums are not identically zero (only zero-mean in expectation). PMI requires nonnegative counts (probabilities); the real obstacle is negative entries, not zero sums. One could, e.g., split positive/negative parts or use absolute values for a fairer comparison.
  - Assertions that RI produces “perfect Gaussian” distributions and PoP “preserves long tails” are overgeneralized; with very sparse projections and heavy-tailed inputs, coordinate distributions will not be exactly N(0,1).
  - The “guarantee” that every row has a nonzero in T with tiny s is probabilistic, not guaranteed; no bound is given. Also, no normalization of T is discussed; without scale control, column norms can explode (floor(1/U^α) heavy tails) and distort distances.
  - References to Johnson–Lindenstrauss–type bounds are gestural; no derivation specialized to the proposed discrete heavy-tailed positive-only projection is provided. The claim σ^2_δ ≈ 1/m is asserted without proof.
- Ambiguous/incorrect methodology for Kendall’s τb:
  - The definition compares (x_j, y_j) with (x_{j+1}, y_{j+1}) in the text but then uses n0 = m(m−1)/2, implying all pairs. This needs precision. Using τb across feature coordinates of word vectors is nonstandard; please justify rigorously and compare to cosine/Pearson on the same unweighted PoP vectors.
- Evaluation is too narrow and lacks strong baselines:
  - Only MEN is used. No results on SimLex-999, WordSim-353, Rare-Word, SimVerb-3500, or extrinsic tasks (e.g., analogy, downstream classification).
  - No comparison against well-tuned PPMI+SVD with standard context selection (stopwords, subsampling, dynamic windows), or against fastText/word2vec/GloVe trained on UKWaC with controlled settings. Claims of competitiveness rely on numbers from other papers with different corpora and preprocessing.
  - No significance testing across the 10 runs; only averages and min/max bands.
- Efficiency claims lack evidence:
  - No wall-clock times, hardware specs, memory footprints, or asymptotic costs beyond high-level statements. PMI on PoP is claimed “incredibly faster,” but no timing vs. count-based PPMI+SVD with standard pruning is shown.
- Reproducibility gaps:
  - Code/data “if accepted” is inadequate. Many hyperparameters are under-specified (exact s, α, non-zero count per row, normalization of T, random seed handling, context vocabulary, frequency thresholds). Figures are referenced but not enough detail is provided to reproduce them.
- Notation/formatting issues that impede clarity:
  - Inconsistent notation for matrices (T vs. R; dimensions n×x vs. n×m) and expectations E(R) vs. E = (0).
  - The distribution in Eq. (2) is garbled: “1Uα c” presumably means floor(U^−α); define it explicitly and give its expectation/variance.
  - Multiple typos and grammar: “To a large extend” → extent; “count-baseds”; “concern- ing”; missing inequality signs (x n?); inconsistent η/α norms; footnotes spill into main text; “Ibid” usage is unclear.
- Conceptual gap in PPMI after projection:
  - PMI is defined on co-occurrence counts of target-context pairs; after projection, columns are pseudo-contexts (mixtures). You compute PPMI on P directly, which is not equivalent to projecting a PPMI(C). Please analyze bias/consistency: does PPMI(P) approximate any principled objective? Provide a comparison to PPMI(C) followed by linear projection (where feasible) or a theoretical argument.

Questions to Authors
- Precisely define Eq. (2): What is the distribution of non-zero tij? Is it tij = floor(U^−α) with U∼U(0,1]? What are E[tij], Var[tij], and the expected squared norm per column? Do you normalize T (e.g., by column norms) to control scale?
- How do you ensure every row of T has at least one nonzero with small s? Do you resample until nonzero appears, or deterministically plant one index? Provide the exact algorithm.
- Clarify the Kendall τb computation: Are all O(m^2) pairs used (with Knight’s O(m log m)), or adjacent pairs only? Why is τb preferable to cosine/Pearson for unweighted PoP? Please include ablations.
- PPMI on projected matrices: What target/context marginals are you modeling after projection? Can you relate PPMI(P) to a known estimator of PMI(C) under random hashing? How different are results from applying PPMI to the full C with standard pruning (top-k contexts, cutoff) and optionally SVD?
- Provide comprehensive baselines on MEN, SimLex-999, WS-353, RW, and SimVerb-3500 using the same corpus and preprocessing: (a) strong PPMI+SVD, (b) SGNS/fastText/GloVe. Include statistical significance and runtime/memory comparisons.
- Report wall-clock times, memory usage, and throughput for building C, building P, and computing PPMI, for several m and s, with hardware details.
- Many claims about RI (sums zero, Gaussian distributions) seem inaccurate. Please correct or justify with derivations/experiments.

Overall, the idea has promise, but the paper in its current form suffers from theoretical inaccuracies, ambiguous definitions, limited evaluation, and numerous presentation issues that undermine confidence. I encourage a substantial revision addressing the above points, with clearer math, stronger baselines, and reproducible experiments.
