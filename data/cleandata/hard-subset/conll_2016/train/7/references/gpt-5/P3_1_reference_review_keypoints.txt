- Summary: Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP), a very sparse nonnegative randomized projection that permits PPMI weighting in compressed space and shows strong MEN performance with low compute.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple randomized sketch that preserves nonnegativity to enable PPMI on compressed co-occurrence representations.

- Contribution 2: Empirically studies PoP hyperparameters (dimension m, sparsity s) and the effect of similarity metrics (Kendall vs Pearson after PPMI).

- Contribution 3: Observes rank-based similarity helps unweighted PoP, while PPMI makes projected distributions more Gaussian-like and favors Pearson.

- Strength: Conceptual simplicity and practicality—easy to implement, incremental, and bridges RI and dense/neural models.

- Strength: Empirical gains over RI and raw counts on MEN with clear parameter sweeps and sensitivity analysis.

- Strength: Shows PPMI applied in compressed space yields competitive relatedness (~0.75 on MEN) with small vectors and no heavy training.

- Strength: Claims incrementality and efficiency suitable for streaming updates and avoiding expensive SVD/neural training.

- Weakness: Missing key sketching baselines—no comparisons or citations for Count-Min Sketch, feature hashing, or Count-Sketch variants.

- Weakness: Limited and potentially biased evaluation—only MEN on one corpus, metric mismatches (Kendall for PoP vs cosine for others), and possibly uneven preprocessing/tuning.

- Weakness: Incomplete modern baselines—no SGNS/fastText trained on UKWaC or tuned SPPMI(+SVD) setups per Levy & Goldberg.

- Weakness: Ad hoc theoretical/design choices—no justification for tij = floor(1/U^α) with α=0.5, or analysis of bias/variance and collision effects.

- Weakness: Efficiency claims unsubstantiated—no wall-clock, memory, or asymptotic comparisons versus sparse PPMI, CMS, or SGNS.

- Question 1: Compare PoP to Count-Min Sketch and feature hashing baselines (varying width/depth) and discuss theoretical connections/differences.

- Question 2: Report Kendall’s τb and Pearson/cosine for all methods (RI, counts, PoP) to decouple representation from similarity-metric effects.

- Question 3: Add benchmarks (SimLex-999, WordSim-353, RareWords) and at least one downstream task; report SGNS/fastText on UKWaC and tuned SPPMI(+SVD).

- Question 4: Provide runtime and memory benchmarks (construction, PPMI, similarity) vs baselines across m and vocab sizes and show scaling behavior.

- Question 5: Justify the projection distribution (1/U^0.5 and flooring), report expected L1/L2 norms, and give guidance on choosing s to control collision rates.

- Question 6: Clarify the statistical meaning of PPMI over random bucket dimensions and analyze/experimentally show regimes where it approximates true-context PPMI.

- Question 7: Confirm whether preprocessing and hyperparameter tuning in the improved setup were identical across all baselines; provide fair side-by-side comparisons.

- Additional comment: Nuance the claim that RI “cannot be weighted”—RI produces signed entries so PMI is inapplicable but other weightings exist.

- Additional comment: Request code, seeds, error bars, and ablations on α and unit vs heavy-tailed positive weights.

- Overall: Interesting and potentially useful idea, but needs stronger sketching and modern baselines, broader evaluation, principled analysis, and concrete efficiency measurements to be publication-ready.
