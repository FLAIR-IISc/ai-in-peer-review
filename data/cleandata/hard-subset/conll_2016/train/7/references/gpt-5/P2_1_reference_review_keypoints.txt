- Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse non-negative integer random projections to allow incremental low-dimensional distributional spaces and enable post-projection weighting (PPMI).
- Recommends using Kendall’s τb for similarity on non-Gaussian projected vectors and switching to Pearson after PPMI.
- Empirical claim: PoP (especially PoP+PPMI+Pearson) approaches strong baselines on MEN with much lower computational cost; includes parameter study over dimension m and sparsity s.

Strengths
- Practical idea addressing a real bottleneck: apply weighting after an aggressive projection to speed up PPMI-like transforms.
- Thorough empirical exploration of m and s, reporting variance across seeds and sensible behavior.
- Thoughtful discussion linking distributional properties to choice of similarity metric (Kendall vs Pearson).
- Simple, incremental, training-free method suitable for streaming/rapid prototyping.

Weaknesses
- Evaluation is narrow: single dataset (MEN), small target set (751 lemmas), no harder similarity benchmarks, rare words, analogy, downstream tasks, or larger vocabularies.
- Missing comparisons to tuned modern baselines (SGNS/word2vec, GloVe, PPMI+SVD with CDS/subsampling) trained on the same corpus.
- Efficiency/speed claims lack empirical wall-clock time, memory usage, and scaling experiments; Kendall’s τb complexity for large-vocab retrieval is unaddressed.
- Conceptual justification for applying PPMI to projected, non-interpretable features is weak; need ablations comparing PPMI-before vs PPMI-after vs other transforms (log/Box-Cox).
- Novelty and relation to prior sparse/nonnegative random projections, hashing, and RI literature are insufficiently positioned; JL-type or preservation guarantees not discussed.
- No theoretical analysis of bias/variance, expected scaling, marginals, or collision effects in PoP; basic formal properties are missing.
- Clarity and reproducibility issues: unusual distribution for magnitudes, unclear normalization, tangled notation, and missing preprocessing/vocabulary/random-seed details.

Questions / Requests
- Provide wall-clock time and peak memory comparisons end-to-end vs count+PPMI(+SVD) and SGNS/GloVe on the same corpus/vocabulary.
- Clarify probabilistic interpretation of PPMI on projected columns and add ablations: PPMI-before-projection, project-then-PPMI, and log-only/other transforms.
- Report performance on additional benchmarks (e.g., WordSim-353, SimLex-999, Rare Words) and at least one downstream task with tuned baselines trained on UKWaC.
- Explain index-vector normalization choices and justify heavy-tailed magnitudes vs binary/ternary entries; show what breaks with {0,1} entries.
- Release code and exact preprocessing details now to enable verification.

Overall assessment
- Simple and potentially useful idea but current evidence is too limited; recommend rejection pending broader experiments, solid efficiency measurements, ablations clarifying PPMI’s role, theoretical grounding, and clearer positioning vs prior work.
