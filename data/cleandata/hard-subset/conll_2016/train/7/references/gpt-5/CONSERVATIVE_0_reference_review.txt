Summary and contributions
- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a random-projection scheme with non-negative, sparse index vectors that allows incremental construction of low-dimensional distributional spaces and enables post-projection weighting with PPMI.
- Introduces use of Kendall’s τb (and Pearson after PPMI) as similarity measures for PoP due to non-Gaussian vs. Gaussian-like distributions, respectively.
- Reports empirical results on MEN showing PoP > RI and count-based on small windows, and PoP+PPMI+Pearson ≈ dense PPMI+cosine (≈0.75), with claims of substantial computational savings.

Strengths
- Simplicity and potential efficiency: The method is easy to implement (random sparse, positive index vectors with additive updates) and amenable to streaming/incremental construction, which could be practically useful when building spaces on-the-fly.
- Empirical exploration of parameters: The paper systematically varies m and sparsity s, and analyzes instability and non-orthogonality effects. This is useful for practitioners and gives initial intuition for trade-offs.
- Attempts to align similarity measures to distributional assumptions: The rationale for using τb on non-Gaussian raw projections and Pearson after PPMI is coherent at a high level, and the plots (e.g., histograms) provide some intuition.
- Aims to bridge efficiency and accuracy: The core idea—enabling PPMI-like weighting after projection—targets a known bottleneck (the cost of computing PPMI on huge count matrices).

Weaknesses
- Limited and non-standard evaluation: Only a single intrinsic dataset (MEN) is used; no tests on SimLex-999, WordSim-353, RG-65, or downstream tasks (e.g., similarity in STS, classification, retrieval). No statistical significance testing across runs. This is insufficient to assess robustness or generality.
- Baselines and comparisons are not apples-to-apples: Comparisons mix similarity measures across methods (τb for PoP vs. cosine for RI/count; Pearson after PPMI for PoP vs. cosine for dense PPMI). To isolate the effect of the projection, each method should be evaluated with the same similarity measures when possible. Moreover, key baselines are missing: SGNS/fastText/GloVe on the same corpus/settings; PPMI+SVD with randomized SVD; hashing/count-min sketch-based baselines; non-negative/sign-consistent random projections.
- Questionable probabilistic grounding of PPMI post-projection: After random projection, dimensions correspond to aggregates of many original contexts and do not have a clear interpretation as joint events. Applying PPMI in the projected space lacks theoretical justification. The claim that RI cannot be PPMI-weighted because “row/column sums are always 0” is not accurate (they are not identically zero; the issue is the presence of negatives and the loss of probabilistic semantics). The paper should either provide a principled derivation (e.g., showing PoP approximates count-min sketches of co-occurrence counts preserving marginals) or clearly state the heuristic nature and its limitations.
- Lack of theoretical analysis: The paper explicitly states no bounds or JL-style guarantees for PoP and offers only empirical observations. While empirical methods are acceptable, the paper makes strong claims about preserving distributions and enabling valid PPMI that require at least some theoretical support or connections to established results (e.g., count-min sketch, sign-consistent JL transforms, database-friendly/sparse projections).
- Incomplete reporting and reproducibility: No wall-clock timing, memory usage, or throughput comparisons are reported, despite efficiency being a main motivation. Important experimental details are underspecified (vocabulary size, whether vectors were built only for MEN lemmas, context vocabulary pruning/stopwords, smoothing for PMI, handling of zeros/rare events). Code “if accepted” is not sufficient for reproducibility.

Questions to Authors
- PPMI after projection: How do you interpret projected dimensions probabilistically to justify PPMI? Are your “joint” and “marginal” terms computed over projected bins that mix unrelated contexts? Can you relate PoP formally to count-min sketch (Cormode & Muthukrishnan) or count-sketch, and derive bias/variance of joint and marginal estimates?
- Apples-to-apples similarity: What happens if you use the same similarity measure across methods (e.g., cosine and Pearson for RI/count/PoP; τb for RI as well)? Please provide controlled comparisons to isolate the effect of the projection from the choice of similarity metric.
- Baselines: Please report results for (i) SGNS/fastText/GloVe trained on the same UKWaC with standard hyperparameters, (ii) PPMI+SVD with randomized SVD, and (iii) hashing/count-min sketch baselines with PPMI, all on the same preprocessing. Where does PoP stand then?
- Scope of training data: Did you build vectors only for the 751 MEN lemmas, or for a large vocabulary? If only MEN lemmas, how does performance and runtime scale when building the full vocabulary (e.g., 100k+ types)?
- Efficiency claims: Please provide wall-clock times and peak memory for (a) building count matrices, (b) PoP construction, (c) PPMI computation in both spaces, and, if applicable, (d) SVD vs. PoP variants, on the same machine. Without this, it is hard to substantiate the claimed advantages.
- Parameterization: How sensitive is performance to s at fixed m when using PPMI? Your plots suggest increasing s hurts PPMI performance; can you give a principled guideline (or an adaptive procedure) for choosing s?
- Theoretical connections: Can you provide any bounds (even loose) or references to sign-consistent/non-negative JL transforms or sketching theory that would support your claims about δ scaling with 1/m and about preserving information needed by PPMI?

Additional comments
- The statement that cosine equals Pearson in Gaussian RP spaces because E=0 is imprecise; cosine and correlation coincide after centering and appropriate normalization, not merely due to zero mean in expectation.
- Please discuss related work on non-negative/randomized projections and hashing (Achlioptas; Li et al.; feature hashing; sign-consistent JL; count-min/sketching) more thoroughly, and clarify novelty beyond choosing positive-only entries.
- The “incremental” property is not unique to PoP; count models and SGNS can be streamed. Clarify the practical advantage.
- Release code and full preprocessing details to enable verification. Without stronger theory, broad and rigorous empirical validation is essential.

Overall, the idea is intriguing, but the current evidence and grounding are insufficient for publication at CoNLL. A more rigorous, apples-to-apples empirical study across diverse benchmarks, clear efficiency measurements, corrected claims, and theoretical or sketching-based justification for PPMI after projection are needed.
