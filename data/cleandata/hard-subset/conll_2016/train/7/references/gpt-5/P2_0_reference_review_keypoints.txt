- Proposes Positive-only Projection (PoP), a sparse nonnegative integer random projection enabling post-hoc weighting like PPMI on compressed co-occurrence matrices.  
- Recommends Kendall’s τb for raw PoP space and Pearson/cosine after PPMI due to distributional changes.  
- Empirically on MEN: PoP outperforms RI and raw counts; PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves ~0.75 Spearman, with claimed efficiency gains; includes ablations on m and s.  
- Main contribution 1: Practical online, low-memory sketch for co-occurrence matrices that supports PPMI and incremental construction.  
- Main contribution 2: Empirical evidence that PoP+PPMI+Pearson is competitive on MEN, with basic parameter and metric studies.  
- Main contribution 3: Qualitative discussion linking pre/post-PPMI distributions to choice of similarity measures.  
- Strength: Simple, practical, and suitable for low-resource or streaming settings if PPMI in compressed space is accurate.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical advantage of PoP over vanilla RI due to support for post-projection weighting.  
- Strength: Helpful parameter exploration (m, s), variance across runs, and interaction with similarity metrics.  
- Strength: Thoughtful treatment of similarity metric choice (rank-based vs parametric) tied to observed distributions.  
- Weakness: Novelty unclear—closely related to feature hashing/Count-Min Sketch and prior sparse/nonnegative projection work without adequate citations or differentiation.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis (distortion/bias/variance guarantees) and derivation of claimed σ²_δ ≈ 1/m.  
- Weakness: PMI semantics after projection are questionable—no justification that PoP yields unbiased/low-bias marginals or comparison to true PPMI weights.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—only MEN, no significance tests, no controlled comparisons to neural baselines, and no measured runtime/memory figures.  
- Weakness: Clarity and reproducibility issues—notation inconsistencies, missing algorithmic details, editorial problems, and promise to release code only if accepted.  
- Question: How does PoP differ theoretically/practically from feature hashing/CMS, and can you compare empirically (accuracy vs space)?  
- Question: What is the bias/error of PoP joint/marginal estimates vs true counts; report correlations between dense PPMI and PoP+PPMI vectors.  
- Question: Provide wall-clock time and peak memory for dense PPMI, PPMI+SVD, RI, PoP, SGNS, and GloVe on same corpus/hardware, including m and s effects.  
- Question: Did you try simple RI nonlinearities (abs/shift) before PPMI and how do they compare to PoP?  
- Question: Add robustness tests on SimLex-999, WordSim-353, RW, downstream tasks, and significance testing across multiple runs.  
- Assessment: Originality moderately low; soundness mixed (empirical but lacking theory); clarity fair but needs polishing; significance potentially practical but limited by evaluation; reproducibility currently weak.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline reject—method is promising but needs clearer positioning vs hashing/CMS, theoretical or empirical validation of PPMI approximation, broader rigorous evaluation with compute metrics, and improved clarity/reproducibility.
