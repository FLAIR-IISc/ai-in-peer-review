- Proposal: Positive-only Projections (PoP), a sparse random-projection variant with strictly positive index vectors to enable PPMI weighting in low-dimensional spaces.  
- Empirical claim: PoP+PPMI+Pearson matches dense PPMI and approaches neural embeddings on MEN while being simpler and faster to build.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple random-indexing variant using positive-only sparse vectors to permit efficient PPMI in projected space.  
- Contribution 2: Provides empirical evidence on MEN that PoP+PPMI attains similar accuracy to dense PPMI and analyzes effects of projection dimension m and sparsity s.  
- Contribution 3: Offers pragmatic guidance on similarity measures—Kendall’s τb pre-PPMI and Pearson post-PPMI—based on distributional considerations.  
- Strength: Practical, well-motivated engineering tweak that enables weighted count-based modeling in streaming/incremental settings.  
- Strength: Empirical gains on MEN with minimal machinery; PoP outperforms unweighted RI and matches dense PPMI after PPMI.  
- Strength: Parameter sweeps show sensible behavior (larger m improves accuracy/reduces variance; s trades off collisions).  
- Strength: Efficiency argument is compelling in principle—PPMI on m-dimensional matrices should save time/memory versus full co-occurrence matrices.  
- Strength: Thoughtful choice of similarity metrics grounded in statistical rationale about distributions.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—results only on MEN and a single corpus (UKWaC), no controlled comparisons to SGNS/GloVe/SPPMI+SVD trained on the same data.  
- Weakness: No empirical runtime or peak-memory measurements to substantiate efficiency claims.  
- Weakness: Theoretical framing is underdeveloped; no distortion bounds or precise characterization of bias/variance and collision probabilities.  
- Weakness: Methodological clarity issues—confusion in Kendall’s τb presentation, incorrect wording about RI sums being “always 0,” and missing reproducibility details (preprocessing, hyperparameters, seeds).  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental; nonnegative projection idea is straightforward and missing connections to related prior work.  
- Questions: Provide runtime/memory comparisons for dense PPMI+cosine, RI, and PoP+PPMI and scaling with m/corpus size.  
- Questions: Clarify precise computation of Kendall’s τb (all pairs vs adjacent pairs) and implementation details.  
- Questions: Provide controlled baselines on UKWaC (SGNS, GloVe, SPPMI(+SVD)) trained identically for fair comparison.  
- Questions: Supply full reproducibility details—preprocessing, vocab/context selection, window weighting, final hyperparameters, and random seeds.  
- Questions: Can RI be adapted to support PMI-like weighting via shifts/rectification or splitting signs, and how does that compare to PoP?  
- Questions: Any additional evaluations beyond MEN (e.g., SimLex, WordSim, downstream tasks) and significance testing across runs?  
- Overall assessment: Practical and promising but currently limited by evaluation scope, missing runtime evidence, and clarity issues—borderline paper leaning toward rejection pending stronger empirical validation and clearer methodology.
