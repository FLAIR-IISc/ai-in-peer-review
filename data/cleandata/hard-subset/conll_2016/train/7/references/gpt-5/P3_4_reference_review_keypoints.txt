- Paper proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse, nonnegative integer random index vectors enabling PPMI-style weighting in the projected space.
- Authors claim PoP outperforms Random Indexing (RI) on MEN, PoP+PPMI matches some neural baselines at low dimension, and different similarity metrics behave differently pre/post-PPMI.
- Claimed contributions: a simple incremental nonnegative projection scheme, an empirical study of similarity measures and projection parameters, and an efficiency claim for low-dimensional competitive performance without neural training.
- Strength: method is simple, incremental, easy to implement, and supports streaming updates like RI.
- Strength: empirical gains shown over RI and raw counts on MEN with parameter sweeps and seed stability analyses.
- Strength: performing PPMI in projected space reduces cost and storage for large vocabularies; nonnegativity makes information-theoretic weighting plausible.
- Strength: sensible empirical discussion showing Kendall’s τb helps in pre-weighted spaces while Pearson improves after PPMI.
- Weakness: novelty relative to prior sketching (feature hashing, Count-Min Sketch, CountSketch) is unclear and not discussed or compared to that literature.
- Weakness: Equation (2) appears incorrect/ambiguous (floor(U^α) ≈ 0); sampling distribution of index values must be fixed and justified.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis (distortion bounds, collision bias) and gives only heuristics; reproducibility endangered by unclear sampling.
- Weakness: inaccurate explanation for why RI cannot use PPMI—problem is negative entries, not zero sums; argument should be corrected.
- Weakness: limited/outdated evaluation using only MEN; needs multiple benchmarks (SimLex, WS-353, SimVerb, Rare-Word, SCWS, analogies) and comparisons to strong baselines (SGNS, fastText, GloVe, PPMI+SVD).
- Weakness: efficiency claim lacks timing and memory numbers; statement that neural training takes months is outdated for UKWaC-scale corpora.
- Weakness: ambiguity about PMI computed on projected bins — collisions change marginals and introduce bias/variance that should be analyzed relative to CMS theory.
- Weakness: using Kendall’s τb on vector coordinates is unusual and may discard magnitude information; justification linking cosine/Pearson is loose.
- Question: please fix/clarify Eq. (2) and specify the sampling distribution and sensitivity to α.
- Question: explicitly compare and distinguish PoP from feature hashing/Count-Min Sketch and include sketching literature and direct empirical/theoretical comparisons.
- Question: report wall-clock time and memory for RI, count+PPMI(+SVD), SGNS, and PoP(+PPMI) on UKWaC with same hardware and include index-vector construction cost.
- Question: explain how PPMI is maintained incrementally — do global normalizers get recomputed or approximated online?
- Question: provide results beyond MEN with multiple seeds and significance testing, and compare to SGNS/GloVe/PPMI+SVD with strong hyperparameters on the same corpus.
- Question: correct the RI/PPMI claim and, if relevant, compare to a nonnegative sketch baseline.
- Question: provide a small ablation on cosine vs Pearson vs τb in PoP(+PPMI).
- Question: analyze collision rates versus s and m, relate them to performance, and give guidance on choosing s given C’s sparsity.
