- Proposal: Positive-only Projections (PoP), a sparse random projection with positive integer entries enabling post-projection PPMI weighting.  
- Empirical claim: PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves ~0.75–0.78 Spearman on MEN, outperforming RI and raw counts and approaching neural baselines.  
- Efficiency claim: Computing PPMI in low-dimensional projected space is much faster than on full count matrices (no concrete runtimes given).

- Strength: Simple, practical modification of random indexing that preserves nonnegativity and supports incremental one-pass construction.  
- Strength: Clear empirical improvement over RI and raw counts on MEN, especially when combined with PPMI.  
- Strength: Reasonable distributional analysis linking vector shapes to choice of similarity (Kendall’s τb vs Pearson) with informative histograms and parameter sweeps.  
- Strength: Parameter sensitivity analyses and variance reporting across runs help demonstrate robustness.  
- Strength: Potential to bridge sketching/random projection efficiency with established weighting methods if validated.

- Weakness: Narrow evaluation—only MEN on UKWaC; missing strong baselines (SGNS, GloVe, PPMI+SVD) and additional benchmarks or downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: Lack of theoretical justification for PPMI-after-projection and no analysis of bias/variance introduced by sketching collisions.  
- Weakness: Some claims are overstated or imprecise (e.g., RI sums always zero, cosine equals Pearson) and τb’s appropriateness is under-justified.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to related sketching methods (feature hashing, nonnegative RP) and ablations isolating positive-only/heavy-tailed effects.  
- Weakness: Efficiency assertions lack wall-clock/memory profiling and comparison on the same hardware; τb’s O(m log m) cost may be nontrivial.  
- Weakness: No concrete guidance or heuristics for choosing sparsity s at scale.

- Request: Provide end-to-end runtime and memory benchmarks vs RI, PPMI+SVD, SGNS, and feature hashing across realistic vocabularies.  
- Request: Ablations comparing PPMI-before vs PPMI-after, feature hashing (±signs), and SVD/random-projection pipelines.  
- Request: Evaluate on additional word-similarity datasets (SimLex-999, WordSim-353, RW) and at least one downstream task.  
- Request: Clarify similarity function choices and sensitivity to cosine vs τb in raw PoP space, and normalization used before Pearson.  
- Request: Provide a small-scale empirical comparison where both PPMI-before-projection and PPMI-after-PoP are computable to assess approximation quality.  
- Request: Explain how s is set in practice for large vocabularies and any heuristics based on co-occurrence sparsity.

- Additional: Tighten exposition, correct imprecise statements, expand related work (feature hashing, nonnegative RP, sketching for co-occurrence).  
- Additional: Releasing code and scripts is important for reproducibility.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject due to narrow evaluation, missing baselines/analysis, and overstated claims; promising idea if expanded and validated.
