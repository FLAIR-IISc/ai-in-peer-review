- Proposal: Positive-only Projections (PoP), a sparse non-negative integer random projection enabling incremental low-dimensional word-context vectors and permitting PPMI weighting in the reduced space.
- Similarity choices and results: Advocates Kendall’s τb for unweighted PoP and Pearson after PPMI; reports MEN/UKWaC ρ≈0.75 for PoP+PPMI+Pearson, comparable to some neural embeddings with faster construction.
- Empirical studies: Explores effects of dimensionality m and sparsity (nonzeros) on performance and collision/orthogonality, claiming practical guidance (e.g., 1–2 nonzeros if followed by PPMI).
- Strength — practicality/simplicity: PoP is conceptually simple, incremental, and attractive for streaming or constrained environments where full counts or neural training are impractical.
- Strength — PPMI compatibility: Computing PPMI in the low-dimensional projected space is much cheaper and PoP+PPMI+Pearson is competitive with strong count-based baselines without requiring SVD.
- Strength — better than RI/counts: Empirically outperforms Random Indexing and unweighted counts, especially with small context windows.
- Strength — sensible similarity discussion: Provides rationale and empirical visuals for choosing Kendall vs Pearson pre- and post-PPMI.
- Strength — parameter guidance: Investigates m and nonzero count trade-offs, offering actionable recommendations to practitioners.
- Weakness — limited evaluation: Only evaluates on MEN and UKWaC, with no downstream tasks, no significance testing, and indirect/neural comparisons from other papers.
- Weakness — missing baselines/prior art: Fails to cite or compare to non-negative sketching methods (feature hashing, Count-Min Sketch) and misstates RI/PPMI issues, threatening novelty claims.
- Weakness — lack of theory: No distortion/concentration bounds or analysis of bias introduced in PMI estimates after projection.
- Weakness — fairness of similarity choices: Uses different similarity measures for PoP vs baselines, possibly advantaging PoP; cross-method fairness not demonstrated.
- Weakness — reproducibility and clarity: Key implementation choices (e.g., t_ij = ceil(U^α), α=0.5), preprocessing, figures, and numerical reporting are incomplete; code only promised upon acceptance.
- Requested comparisons: Provide controlled baselines on UKWaC with SGNS/GloVe, PPMI+SVD, and non-negative sketching/feature-hashing/CMS, with and without PPMI.
- Requested runtime/memory reporting: Report wall-clock time and peak memory for all methods (construction and weighting) across m and nonzero settings for MEN vocabulary on UKWaC.
- Requested theory/analysis: Clarify relationship to sketching schemes and provide bias/variance or recovery bounds for PMI after projection.
- Requested sensitivity analyses: Quantify sensitivity to α and nonzero weight distributions (including binary indices) and measure Pearson vs cosine and Kendall vs cosine benefits across baselines.
- Requested broader evaluation: Test on additional benchmarks (WordSim353, SimLex-999), analogy tasks, and downstream tasks with standard preprocessing to validate generality.
- Overall assessment: PoP is a practical, potentially useful idea, but the paper needs stronger empirical baselines (especially non-negative sketching), runtime/memory evidence, theoretical justification, and clearer exposition to substantiate its claims.
