- Proposal: PoP — sparse, nonnegative integer random projections enabling post-projection PPMI by preserving nonnegativity and positive expectation.
- Empirical claim: PoP with Kendall’s τb (pre-PPMI) and Pearson (post-PPMI) outperforms RI and simple counts on MEN, reaching Spearman ρ ≈ 0.75 with low compute.
- Experiments: Parameter studies over dimensionality m, sparsity s, and randomization effects are reported.

Main contributions:
- Practical incremental pipeline: projects counts into a low-dimensional nonnegative space and applies PPMI, achieving competitive MEN results cheaply.
- Empirical study: PoP outperforms RI and count baselines on MEN, with analyses of m and s.
- Conceptual argument: advocates correlation-based similarities (τb pre-PPMI, Pearson post-PPMI) given induced distributions.

Strengths:
- Simple, incremental, and low-compute method that avoids expensive training or decompositions.
- Clear empirical improvements over RI and raw co-occurrence, especially with smaller windows.
- Sensible parameter exploration and discussion of non-orthogonality/collision effects.
- Transparent about theoretical gaps and randomized behavior.

Weaknesses:
- Novelty/positioning weak: closely related to Count-Min Sketch/feature hashing and sparse projections; lacks citations/comparisons to that literature.
- Narrow and potentially unfair evaluation: only MEN on UKWaC, no controlled SGNS/GloVe/PPMI-SVD comparisons, no other datasets, no significance testing; inconsistent similarity metrics across methods.
- Missing runtime/memory evidence despite efficiency claims — no wall-clock timings or memory/complexity comparisons.
- Imprecise mathematical claims:
  - Incorrect/oversimplified statement that RI row/column sums are always 0; PMI applicability concerns are more nuanced.
  - Eq.(2) heavy-tailed weight distribution is poorly motivated; choice of α and comparison vs binary weights absent.
  - Argument for τb over cosine/Pearson is ad hoc and under-supported.
- Reproducibility/detail gaps: code/data withheld until acceptance, insufficient preprocessing and hyperparameter details, figures lack error bars and consolidated settings.

Questions for authors:
- How does PoP differ from Count-Min Sketch/feature hashing applied to co-occurrence matrices; provide conceptual/empirical comparisons and discuss known CMS error bounds?
- Why evaluate PoP with τb while using cosine for baselines; please report consistent metrics (cosine, Pearson, τb) across all methods pre- and post-PPMI.
- What justifies treating projected entries as pseudo-counts for PPMI given destroyed marginals; can you measure correlation between original-space PPMI and projected-space PPMI in a small setting?
- Provide wall-clock time and peak memory for building counts, PoP projection, PPMI on dense counts vs PoP, and inference, on the same hardware; compare to SGNS/GloVe on UKWaC.
- Why use heavy-tailed positive weights in Eq.(2) instead of binary 1s; include an ablation vs 1s and clarify α and s selection guidelines.
- Can you show robustness on additional benchmarks (WordSim-353, SimLex-999, RW, analogy) and report statistical significance/variance across runs?

Overall verdict:
- Promising, simple idea with practical potential but needs stronger related-work positioning, broader/fairer evaluation, and concrete efficiency evidence before acceptance.
