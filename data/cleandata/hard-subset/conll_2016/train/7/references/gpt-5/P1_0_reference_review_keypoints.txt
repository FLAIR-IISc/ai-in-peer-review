- Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP): sparse, non-negative random projections enabling PPMI-style weighting in low-dimensional space.
- Argues Kendall’s τb on raw PoP vectors and Pearson’s r after PPMI are appropriate; reports PoP > RI and PoP+PPMI ≈ 0.75 ρ on MEN/UKWaC.
- Provides parameter studies (dimension m, sparsity/number of nonzeros) and qualitative distributional analyses; claims efficiency and incrementality.

- Contribution 1: Practical sketching-style RI variant using non-negative sparse projections to permit PPMI in projected space.
- Contribution 2: Empirical study on when Kendall vs Pearson works and how PoP parameters affect performance and variance.
- Contribution 3: Claims fast, incremental construction and weighting on low-dimensional projections enabling heavy weightings without full matrices.

- Strength: Conceptually simple and practical — non-negative sparse projections solve the problem of applying PPMI to sketches.
- Strength: Empirical improvements over RI and raw counts on MEN; PoP+PPMI approaches classical baselines.
- Strength: Reports averages and variance, explores parameter effects and sparsity-related non-orthogonality.
- Strength: Reasonable choice of similarity measures with observed support for τb pre-PPMI and Pearson post-PPMI.
- Strength: Naturally incremental/streaming and promising computational savings in principle.

- Weakness: Lacks connection to sketching literature (feature hashing, Count-Min/Count Sketch); novelty unclear without comparisons.
- Weakness: Limited, dated evaluation (only MEN/UKWaC); missing strong baselines like SGNS, GloVe, PPMI+SVD and more datasets.
- Weakness: Computing PPMI on sketched counts is potentially biased; unclear whether PPMI(P) approximates PPMI(C).
- Weakness: Method details unclear (distribution of non-zero entries in T, preprocessing, smoothing, etc.).
- Weakness: No runtime or memory benchmarks to substantiate efficiency claims or streaming costs.
- Weakness: Some overstatements about RI/PPMI compatibility and incremental claims without empirical backing.

- Question: How does PoP differ theoretically/empirically from feature hashing and Count-Min Sketch on word–context matrices?
- Question: Do you view PPMI(P) as an approximation to PPMI(C)? Can you quantify bias from sketched PMI?
- Question: Clarify Equation (2) and the distribution of non-zero entries in T — are non-zeros effectively all 1?
- Question: Provide wall-clock time and memory comparisons vs raw PPMI, SGNS/GloVe, and hashing/CMS; show incremental update costs.
- Question: Can you report results on more intrinsic benchmarks (SimLex-999, SimVerb-3500, WS353, RW) and at least one downstream task?

- Additional comment: Ablate Kendall τb against cosine and Spearman on raw PoP vectors.
- Additional comment: Tighten notation and claims (e.g., “retain distances” vs inner products; RI mean approximations).
- Additional comment: Release code and preprocessing details for reproducibility.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject pending stronger positioning against sketching methods, broader/stronger evaluation, clarified methods, and efficiency measurements.
