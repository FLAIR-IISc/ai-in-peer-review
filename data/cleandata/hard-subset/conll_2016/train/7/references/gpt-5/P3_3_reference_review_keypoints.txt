- Summary: Proposes Positive-only Projections (PoP), a sparse random projection giving nonnegative index vectors so word vectors are summed counts enabling PPMI weighting; argues Kendall’s τb for raw PoP and Pearson after PPMI; evaluated only on MEN with UKWaC against raw counts and Random Indexing (RI).

- Contribution 1: Simple, streaming-friendly random projection variant that preserves nonnegativity (E[R]>0) allowing PPMI in compressed space.

- Contribution 2: Empirical study of similarity metrics (Kendall vs Pearson) and parameter effects (dimension m, sparsity s) for PoP.

- Contribution 3: Evidence that PoP+PPMI+Pearson achieves MEN performance comparable to common embeddings while being computationally light.

- Strength 1: Practicality and simplicity — easy to implement, incremental, deployable in streaming settings with natural count-based weighting.

- Strength 2: Empirical improvements — PoP (especially with PPMI) outperforms RI and unweighted counts on MEN for small/moderate windows, with informative parameter sweeps.

- Strength 3: Sensible treatment of similarity measures — τb for non-Gaussian coordinates and Pearson after PPMI is reasonable and supported by results.

- Strength 4: Useful parameter analysis — exploration of m and non-zero counts per index vector and their effect on variance is informative.

- Weakness 1: Novelty/positioning — method is very close to existing sketching techniques (Count-Min/Count-Sketch, feature hashing); paper omits key citations (e.g., Cormode & Muthukrishnan 2005, Weinberger et al. 2009) and related nonnegative sparse projections.

- Weakness 2: Overstated uniqueness — claim that PoP uniquely enables PPMI is unsupported without comparison to hashing/sketching + PPMI; positive-onlyness is not clearly novel.

- Weakness 3: Incorrect/unclear RI/PPMI claim — statement that RI “cannot be adjusted” for PPMI due to zero sums is inaccurate; the real issue is negativity, not guaranteed zero sums, and RI could be rectified.

- Weakness 4: Narrow evaluation and weak baselines — only MEN on UKWaC tested; missing other intrinsic datasets (SimLex-999, WordSim-353, RareWords), analogy tasks, downstream tasks, and comparisons to PPMI+SVD, SGNS, GloVe, and hashing+PPMI baselines.

- Weakness 5: Missing runtime/memory and statistical rigor — no wall-clock/memory measurements, scaling evidence, or statistical tests to support efficiency and claimed improvements.

- Weakness 6: Lack of theoretical grounding — no bias/variance or collision/error analysis akin to Count-Min/Count-Sketch guarantees; choice of tij distribution (floor(1/U^α), α=0.5) lacks justification.

- Weakness 7: Similarity-definition and methodology issues — incorrect exposition of Kendall’s τb (adjacent pairs vs all pairs) and unusual use of rank correlation that discards magnitude; should compare cosine on PPMI and Pearson/cosine across methods.

- Weakness 8: Reproducibility gaps — missing preprocessing details (stopwords, cutoffs), vocabulary sizes, exact m/s values per experiment, and precise runtime/memory to substantiate “seconds or minutes” claims.

- Question 1: Relation to sketching baselines — how does PoP differ theoretically and empirically from Count-Min/Count-Sketch applied to co-occurrence matrices followed by PPMI; provide comparisons and error guarantees.

- Question 2: On RI and PPMI — do authors agree the issue is negativity not zero sums, and could simple rectifications enable PPMI on RI comparable to PoP?

- Question 3: Nonzero distribution choice — why use floor(1/U^α) with α=0.5 instead of binary ones or other distributions; provide ablation showing benefit of heavy-tailed positive integers.

- Question 4: Similarity measures — clarify τb implementation (all pairs) and report cosine on PPMI spaces since cosine is common with PPMI.

- Question 5: Evaluation breadth/fairness — provide results on additional intrinsic datasets and at least one downstream task, plus baselines PPMI+SVD, SGNS, GloVe, and hashing/sketching+PPMI on UKWaC.

- Question 6: Efficiency claims — report wall-clock times, memory footprints, and scaling comparisons between PoP+PPMI, full PPMI, and SGNS/GloVe on same hardware.

- Question 7: Theoretical analysis — connect PoP to known distortion/error bounds (e.g., Count-Min analysis) and characterize bias in PMI estimates due to collisions.

- Overall: Idea is practical and useful, but novelty over established sketching/hashing is unclear, some claims are inaccurate, and evaluation/baselines/theory need substantial strengthening.
