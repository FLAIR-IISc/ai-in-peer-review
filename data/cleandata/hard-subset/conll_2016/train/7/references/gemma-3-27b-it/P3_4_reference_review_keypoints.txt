- Summary: Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), using random positive integer projections to enable PPMI weighting and achieve competitive MEN results with much lower compute.

- Contribution: Practical, efficient alternative to traditional random projections that supports weighting transformations.

- Contribution: Demonstrates that combining PPMI with random projections yields strong performance without heavy neural training.

- Contribution: Provides empirical validation with ablation studies and parameter sensitivity analyses on MEN.

- Strength: Directly addresses the known incompatibility of weighting transformations with prior random projection methods.

- Strength: Strong empirical results—PoP+PPMI is comparable to neural embeddings while being significantly faster.

- Strength: Thorough analysis of dimensionality, the 's' parameter, and non-orthogonality improves understanding of the method.

- Strength: Clear, accessible exposition aided by appropriate matrix notation.

- Strength: Highly practical and scalable; models can be built in seconds or minutes.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to the MEN test, reducing generalizability across datasets and languages.

- Weakness: Lacks a formal theoretical analysis and error bounds for δ and the effect of 's'.

- Weakness: The switch from Kendall’s τb to Pearson’s correlation after PPMI is inadequately justified.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to strong baselines like word2vec or GloVe under similar resource constraints.

- Question: Have you evaluated PoP on other semantic similarity datasets (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim353) or other languages?

- Question: Do you plan to investigate theoretical properties, error bounds, and the impact of 's'?

- Question: Can you justify why PPMI makes Pearson’s correlation appropriate after initially using Kendall’s τb?

- Question: Have you compared PoP+PPMI to word2vec/GloVe with comparable dimensionality and training time?

- Additional: Figures and references are informative; sharing code and embeddings will aid reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with clear contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address theoretical and evaluation gaps.
