- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), random positive-integer vectors that enable weighting like PPMI for semantic spaces.  
- PoP + PPMI achieves competitive MEN semantic similarity performance while being computationally cheaper than neural embeddings.  
- Paper analyzes effects of dimensionality and sparsity, offering practical implementation guidance.  
- Novel method addresses limitation of traditional zero-centered random projections and enables weighting transformations.  
- Technique is technically sound and well-situated within matrix factorization/random projection literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough on MEN, includes comparisons to RI and count-based baselines and ablation studies.  
- Detailed theoretical and empirical discussion, including weight distribution analysis and limitations.  
- Significant efficiency gains over neural embeddings and SVD-based methods, useful for resource-constrained settings.  
- Evaluation scope is limited—needs testing on additional benchmarks (SimLex-999, WordSim353) and other tasks (analogies, classification).  
- Missing or inconsistent statistical significance testing for reported performance differences.  
- Theoretical justification (error bounds, convergence, link to Johnson–Lindenstrauss) is underdeveloped.  
- Parameter sensitivity: optimal dimensionality/sparsity appear task-dependent and require more study or automatic tuning methods.  
- Requests for authors: report results on more datasets, provide statistical tests, pursue formal theoretical analysis, advise on parameter tuning, and give detailed computational cost breakdowns.  
- Minor writing issues: some verbosity; suggest streamlining for clarity.  
- Appreciate promise to release code and embeddings; suggest moving detailed Kendall’s τb/Pearson discussion to appendix.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and efficient approach with important contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
