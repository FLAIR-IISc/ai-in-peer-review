- Summary: Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random-projection method using an all-positive random matrix to enable weighting transforms like PPMI, achieving competitive semantic similarity results with lower cost.

- Contribution 1: Introduces PoP to combine random projection efficiency with compatibility for weighting schemes (e.g., PPMI).

- Contribution 2: Empirically shows PoP+PPMI performs competitively on MEN relatedness compared to heavier methods.

- Contribution 3: Analyzes effects of sparsity parameter s and dimensionality, offering practical guidance.

- Strength 1: Addresses a clear limitation of traditional random projections by enabling weighting transformations.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results showing PoP outperforms RI and benefits from PPMI, with favorable speed/cost trade-offs.

- Strength 3: Thorough ablation studies and parameter exploration demonstrating method behavior and trade-offs.

- Strength 4: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures and notation.

- Strength 5: High practical relevance for resource-constrained settings due to reduced computation/build time.

- Weakness 1: No direct, in-paper head-to-head comparison to strong neural baselines (e.g., Word2Vec, GloVe) on MEN.

- Weakness 2: Evaluation limited to one dataset/task (MEN semantic similarity), limiting claims of generality.

- Weakness 3: Lacks rigorous theoretical justification or formal error bounds connecting PoP to JL-type results.

- Weakness 4: Excessively detailed discussion of Kendall's τb that could be streamlined.

- Weakness 5: Discussion of non-orthogonality effects (Figure 6) feels disconnected from performance implications or remedies.

- Q1: Request for direct performance/time/resource comparison to standard pre-trained embeddings on MEN.

- Q2: Ask about sensitivity to α (fixed at 0.5) and whether other values were tested.

- Q3: Ask how UKWaC corpus size affects results and optimal parameters.

- Q4: Request concrete examples of derandomization techniques applicable to PoP.

- Q5: Ask what happens if alternative normalization/weighting techniques are used instead of PPMI.

- Additional comment 1: Supplemental material is appreciated.

- Additional comment 2: Frequent citations aid context but sometimes disrupt flow.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, and practical work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, broader evaluation, and theoretical clarity.
