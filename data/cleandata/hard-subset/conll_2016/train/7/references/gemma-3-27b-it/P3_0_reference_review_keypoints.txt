- Summary: Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), enabling PPMI weighting with random projections to build efficient semantic spaces and shows competitive results on MEN vs. neural/SVD methods.

- Contribution 1: Proposes PoP, using positive-only random vectors to allow PPMI weighting in random projection frameworks.

- Contribution 2: Empirically shows PoP+PPMI matches more expensive embeddings on MEN and outperforms basic Random Indexing, with helpful ablations.

- Contribution 3: Provides analysis of randomness, parameter effects, and theoretical discussion of properties and extensions.

- Strength 1: Addresses the trade-off between computational cost and semantic accuracy with a practical alternative.

- Strength 2: Method is clearly and mathematically presented, making reproduction and understanding straightforward.

- Strength 3: Strong empirical results on MEN with ablation studies and performance variation reporting.

- Strength 4: Thorough theoretical discussion on sparsity, dimensionality, orthogonality, and appropriate correlation metrics.

- Strength 5: Practically significant due to low computational cost, useful for resource-constrained settings.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation limited to MEN; generalizability to other tasks and datasets is unclear.

- Weakness 2: Parameter sensitivity (e.g., sparsity s, dimensionality) lacks concrete tuning guidelines and systematic exploration.

- Weakness 3: Lacks comparisons to other efficient alternatives like LSH or FastText in both performance and cost.

- Weakness 4: Initial justification for using Kendall’s τ is convoluted and could be more directly linked to PoP distributions.

- Weakness 5: Observation that increasing dimensionality can hurt performance without PPMI is noted but not deeply investigated.

- Question 1: Have you evaluated PoP beyond MEN, and if so, what are the results on other tasks/datasets?

- Question 2: Can you provide concrete guidance for selecting 's' based on corpus characteristics like size and sparsity?

- Question 3: Have you compared PoP to FastText, LSH, or other efficient methods in terms of accuracy and computational cost?

- Question 4: Why does dimensionality growth degrade performance without PPMI—are there theoretical reasons or empirical diagnostics?

- Question 5: Will code and trained models be released for reproducibility?

- Additional Comment 1: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and supplemented with promised materials.

- Additional Comment 2: Discussion of algorithm randomness and attempted error bounds is commendable though incomplete.

- Additional Comment 3: Visualizations effectively support method explanation and results.

- Overall Assessment: Solid, novel contribution that merits acceptance with minor revisions to address evaluation breadth, parameter guidance, and comparative baselines.
