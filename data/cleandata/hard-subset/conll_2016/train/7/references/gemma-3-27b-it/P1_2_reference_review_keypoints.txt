- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP): random projections with strictly positive values enabling post-projection weighting like PPMI.  
- PoP circumvents the zero-centering limitation of traditional random indexing, allowing PPMI after projection.  
- Achieves competitive semantic similarity performance (MEN dataset) vs. neural embeddings and SVD-based count methods.  
- Offers significantly reduced computational cost, suitable for resource-constrained or rapid model-building scenarios.  
- Empirically analyzes key parameters (dimensionality m and sparsity s) and their trade-offs for performance and distortion.  
- Investigates orthogonality of projection vectors and its relation to semantic performance.  
- Well-written, clear mathematical notation and a helpful step-by-step method description.  
- Practical implications: useful for fast semantic space construction and possible preprocessing for neural embeddings.  
- Evaluation limited to a single dataset (MEN); broader tasks and downstream evaluations are missing.  
- Lacks a detailed theoretical justification or error bounds (δ) for PoP’s behavior.  
- Results show sensitivity to parameter s; more guidance or automatic tuning methods are needed.  
- Different optimal s values observed depending on whether PPMI is applied—needs further elaboration.  
- Comparisons to stronger, recent baselines (e.g., fastText, optimized GloVe) are absent.  
- Requests clarification on generalizability to other semantic tasks and downstream NLP applications.  
- Asks whether the authors will pursue theoretical analysis of error bounds and projection distributions.  
- Seeks practical guidance on selecting s relative to corpus/vocabulary size and PPMI usage, and on auto-tuning.  
- Suggests providing a formal computational complexity comparison to SVD and neural embedding training.  
- Requests deeper explanation of how non-orthogonality degree affects performance post-PPMI.  
- Notes Figure 3 as informative and endorses the idea of PoP as a preprocessing step; supplemental material appreciated.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with promising results; recommends acceptance pending addressing theoretical analysis, broader evaluation, and parameter guidance.
