- Summary: Proposes Positive-Only Projection (PoP) that uses strictly positive random projections so PPMI weighting can be applied, yielding competitive semantic similarity performance with much lower computation.

- Contribution 1: Introduces PoP to enable PPMI-like weighting in random-projection semantic spaces.

- Contribution 2: Empirically shows PoP+PPMI matches neural and SVD-based baselines on the MEN similarity benchmark.

- Contribution 3: Emphasizes substantial speed and efficiency advantages for rapid or resource-constrained model construction.

- Strength 1: Directly addresses a notable limitation of existing random-projection methods (incompatibility with PPMI) with a simple, elegant fix.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical validation with baseline comparisons and ablation studies (dimensionality, PPMI effects).

- Strength 3: Clear, well-written exposition with helpful matrix notation and equations.

- Strength 4: Repeated and convincing emphasis on the method’s speed and practical utility.

- Strength 5: Useful analysis of parameter effects (m and s) and interesting observations about orthogonality.

- Weakness 1: Core idea’s novelty is limited—the paper needs deeper positioning relative to prior random-projection work.

- Weakness 2: Lacks theoretical analysis (error bounds, formal properties), and the JL analogy is superficial.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation is narrow (only MEN); broader datasets and downstream tasks are missing.

- Weakness 4: Justification for using Kendall’s τb over cosine is not fully convincing or clearly motivated.

- Weakness 5: Insufficient explanation of why increasing s can harm performance and the link between s, orthogonality, and distortion.

- Question 1: What are the main challenges and possible frameworks for a formal theoretical analysis of PoP (e.g., error bounds)?

- Question 2: What experiments motivated choosing Kendall’s τb before PPMI, and were other nonparametric measures tried?

- Question 3: Have you tested PoP beyond MEN or on downstream NLP tasks, and what are the results or plans?

- Question 4: Any heuristics or guidance for selecting s based on corpus sparsity?

- Question 5: Can you further explain how index-vector orthogonality, s, and similarity distortion interact?

- Additional comment 1: Providing code and embeddings as supplemental material would be valuable.

- Additional comment 2: A summary table of key parameters and their performance impact would improve clarity.

- Additional comment 3: Expand related work to include more recent random-projection advances.

- Overall assessment: Solid, practically useful contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focused on theory, broader evaluation, and clearer explanations.
