## Peer Review: Random Positive-Only Projections: PPMI-Enabled Incremental Semantic Space Construction

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel method for constructing semantic spaces based on random projections. The key idea is to use random vectors containing only positive integers, allowing for the application of weighting transformations like PPMI, which is problematic with traditional random projection methods that center around zero. The authors demonstrate that PoP, especially when combined with PPMI, achieves competitive results on semantic similarity assessment (specifically the MEN test) while being significantly faster and less computationally expensive than neural embedding approaches and traditional count-based methods followed by SVD.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A novel random projection technique (PoP) enabling weighting transformations:** The core contribution is the design of a random projection method specifically engineered to be compatible with weighting schemes like PPMI, overcoming a limitation of existing techniques. This opens up avenues for combining the efficiency of random projections with the performance gains of weighting.
2. **Empirical demonstration of competitive performance with significant efficiency gains:** The authors convincingly show that PoP+PPMI achieves results comparable to more computationally intensive methods (neural embeddings, SVD-based approaches) on a standard benchmark, while being much faster to construct.
3. **Insightful analysis of the method's parameters and behavior:** The paper includes a good exploration of the impact of dimensionality and sparsity on performance, providing practical guidance for using PoP.



**Strengths:**

1. **Addresses a clear and significant problem:** Existing random projection methods struggle to integrate with weighting techniques, hindering their ability to achieve state-of-the-art performance. PoP directly addresses this limitation.
2. **Strong empirical results:** The results on the MEN test are compelling, demonstrating that PoP+PPMI is a viable alternative to more complex methods. The speed comparison is a particularly strong point, highlighting the practical benefits of the approach.
3. **Thorough experimentation and analysis:**  The authors provide a detailed exploration of the method's behavior with varying parameters, including dimensionality and sparsity, offering valuable insights for users. The inclusion of results for both PPMI-weighted and unweighted PoP, along with comparisons to RI, strengthens the evaluation.
4. **Clear and well-structured presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanation of the method is concise and logical.
5. **Justification of design choices:** The authors provide good rationale for their design choices, such as using Kendall's τb initially and then transitioning to Pearson’s correlation after PPMI weighting.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited scope of evaluation:** The evaluation is currently limited to a single benchmark (MEN). While MEN is a standard test, it would be beneficial to see results on a more diverse set of semantic similarity tasks and potentially on downstream NLP applications.
2. **Lack of a comprehensive theoretical analysis:** The paper acknowledges the absence of a rigorous theoretical justification for PoP. While the authors mention related work on random projections, a more detailed analysis of the statistical properties of PoP and its relationship to other methods would significantly strengthen the paper. Specifically, a deeper exploration of the impact of the positive-only constraint on the resulting embedding space would be valuable.
3. **Sensitivity to parameters:** The results demonstrate that PoP’s performance is sensitive to parameters like dimensionality and sparsity. While the authors explore these parameters, a more systematic parameter tuning process, perhaps using a validation set, would be beneficial.  It is unclear how robust these optimal parameter settings are across different datasets.
4. **Comparison to simpler baselines:** While comparisons to RI and SVD are included, it would be useful to compare PoP to even simpler baselines, like directly using raw counts or TF-IDF, to better isolate the benefits of the random projection and PPMI combination.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Broader Evaluation:** Could you provide results on additional semantic similarity datasets beyond MEN?  (e.g., SimLex-999, WordSim353)
2. **Theoretical Justification:**  What are the key challenges in providing a theoretical analysis of PoP?  Could you outline potential avenues for future theoretical work?
3. **Parameter Robustness:** How robust are the optimal parameter settings (dimensionality and sparsity) observed in your experiments? Have you investigated the impact of these parameters on different datasets or corpora?
4. **Computational cost breakdown:**  Can you provide a more detailed breakdown of the computational cost (time and memory) for PoP, PPMI weighting, and comparison methods? This would further emphasize the efficiency benefits of your approach.
5. **Choice of α:**  Why was α = 0.5 chosen? Was this parameter tuned, and what impact does changing it have on performance?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The discussion section is strong and effectively summarizes the contributions and potential future directions.
*   The inclusion of supplemental material for code and embeddings is appreciated.
*   Figure 3 is excellent for visually illustrating the distributional differences between the various methods.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. While the lack of a comprehensive theoretical analysis and the limited scope of evaluation are weaknesses, the novelty of the PoP method, the strong empirical results demonstrating competitive performance with significant efficiency gains, and the thorough exploration of the method's parameters make this a valuable contribution to the field. Addressing the questions above during the author response period would further strengthen the paper.
