## Peer Review: Random Positive-Only Projections: PPMI-Enabled Incremental Semantic Space Construction

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a novel method for constructing semantic spaces. The core idea is to use random projections with strictly positive values, enabling the application of weighting techniques like PPMI *after* the projection – something traditional random indexing methods prevent due to their zero-centered projections. The authors demonstrate that PoP achieves competitive performance on semantic similarity tasks (MEN dataset) compared to neural embeddings and count-based methods with SVD, but with significantly reduced computational cost. They also explore the impact of parameters like dimensionality and the number of non-zero elements in the projection vectors.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A Novel Random Projection Scheme:** The primary contribution is the PoP method itself. By ensuring positive values in the projection matrix, the authors circumvent a limitation of existing random projection techniques, opening the door to applying weighting schemes like PPMI, enhancing the quality of the resulting semantic space.
2. **Efficient Semantic Space Construction:** PoP offers a computationally efficient alternative to both traditional count-based methods and neural embeddings, achieving comparable performance in a fraction of the time. This is a significant benefit for resource-constrained environments or tasks requiring rapid model building.
3. **Empirical Evaluation & Parameter Analysis:** The paper provides a thorough empirical evaluation of PoP, comparing it to relevant baselines and systematically analyzing the influence of key parameters. This helps to understand the strengths and limitations of the method.



**Strengths:**

1. **Addresses a Clear Limitation of Existing Methods:** The paper effectively highlights the constraint of zero-centering in traditional random projection methods and presents a well-motivated solution.  The inability to apply weighting techniques post-projection *is* a significant limitation, and PoP directly tackles this.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The performance on the MEN dataset is compelling, demonstrating that PoP can achieve accuracy comparable to more computationally expensive methods. The provided figures clearly illustrate the performance gains and the impact of various parameters.
3. **Detailed Parameter Exploration:** The analysis of dimensionality (m) and the number of non-zero elements in the projection vectors (s) is valuable.  The insights gained regarding the trade-offs between performance, computational cost, and potential for distortion are helpful for practitioners.  The analysis of orthogonality and its relation to performance is particularly insightful.
4. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations are easy to follow. The step-by-step breakdown of the PoP method is particularly helpful.
5. **Practical Implications:** The potential for rapid model building and reduced computational costs makes PoP attractive for applications where speed and resource efficiency are critical.



**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Scope of Evaluation:**  While the MEN dataset provides a good starting point, the evaluation is limited to a single task. Demonstrating performance on a wider range of semantic similarity tasks (e.g., word analogy, different datasets) would strengthen the claims of generalizability.
2. **Lack of Theoretical Justification:** The authors acknowledge the lack of a detailed theoretical account for specifying the error bounds (δ) of the PoP method. While providing references to related work, a deeper theoretical analysis would be highly beneficial. Understanding *why* PoP works, beyond the empirical results, is crucial for building confidence in the method.
3. **Sensitivity to Parameter Settings:** The results indicate a sensitivity to parameters like 's' (number of non-zero elements). More guidance on how to choose appropriate values for 's' in different scenarios would be valuable. A more robust parameter selection strategy or a method for automatically tuning 's' would be a significant improvement.  The discussion suggests a different optimal 's' value depending on whether PPMI weighting is applied – this is a crucial point that needs further elaboration.
4. **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** While the comparison to RI and count-based methods is useful, a direct comparison to more recent and advanced word embedding techniques (e.g., fastText, GloVe with optimized parameters) would provide a stronger benchmark for evaluating PoP's performance.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Generalizability:**  Beyond the MEN dataset, have you evaluated PoP on other semantic similarity tasks or downstream NLP applications? If so, what were the results?
2. **Theoretical Analysis:**  Are you planning to investigate the theoretical properties of PoP further, particularly concerning the error bounds (δ) and the impact of different projection matrix distributions?
3. **Parameter Tuning:**  Can you provide more detailed guidance on selecting appropriate values for the parameter 's', considering factors like corpus size, vocabulary size, and the use of PPMI weighting? Have you explored any automatic parameter tuning methods?
4. **Computational Complexity:**  While you mention the efficiency of PoP, a more formal analysis of its computational complexity (e.g., compared to SVD or neural embedding training) would be helpful.
5. **Orthogonality and Performance:** The discussion of orthogonality and its impact on performance is intriguing. Could you elaborate on the relationship between the degree of non-orthogonality and the observed performance, particularly in the context of the PPMI transformation?



**Additional Comments:**

*   Figure 3 is very helpful in visualizing the effect of PoP and PPMI on the distribution of weights.
*   The suggestion of using PoP as a pre-processing step for neural embeddings is an interesting and potentially valuable idea.
*   The supplemental material promise is appreciated.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel contribution and promising results.  While some theoretical analysis and a broader evaluation would strengthen the work, the demonstrated performance and efficiency of PoP make it a valuable addition to the field of semantic space construction. I recommend **Acceptance**, with the caveat that the authors address the weaknesses raised during the author response phase.
