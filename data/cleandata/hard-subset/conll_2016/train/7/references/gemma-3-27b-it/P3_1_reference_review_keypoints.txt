- Presents Positive-Only Projections (PoP), using positive-only random vectors to enable PPMI weighting for constructing word embeddings.  
- Claims PoP+PPMI yields competitive semantic similarity performance while being faster and less resource-intensive than neural embeddings.  
- Main contribution: a practical, efficient alternative to neural embedding models for rapid prototyping and low-resource settings.  
- Main contribution: bridges random projections and weighting techniques by using positive-only projections to allow PPMI application.  
- Main contribution: provides empirical validation and detailed parameter analysis on the MEN benchmark.  
- Strength: novel and insightful idea that addresses a limitation of traditional random projections.  
- Strength: strong empirical results showing PoP+PPMI rivals more complex methods on MEN and outperforms RI.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of parameters and randomness, including effects of dimensionality and sparsity (s).  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and accessible mathematical notation.  
- Strength: practically relevant and computationally efficient—models can be built in seconds or minutes.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to MEN and a single corpus (UKWaC), reducing evidence of generalizability.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed quantitative comparison to strong pre-trained baselines like GloVe or fastText.  
- Weakness: theoretical justification is weak; no formal analysis of error bounds or convergence.  
- Weakness: performance appears sensitive to PPMI parameter choices, suggesting dataset-specific tuning.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and systematic exploration of hyperparameter tuning (e.g., s and m).  
- Question: provide results on additional downstream tasks (word analogy, text classification) to test generalizability?  
- Question: how does PoP+PPMI compare quantitatively to GloVe/fastText on MEN in performance and time?  
- Question: what progress exists toward a theoretical analysis of error bound (δ) and convergence properties?  
- Question: what strategy was used to tune PPMI parameters—grid search or other optimization?  
- Question: how does context window size affect PoP+PPMI and what is the optimal window size?  
- Additional: figures and supplemental material are well-presented and informative.  
- Additional: discussion linking Kendall’s τb and Pearson correlation is insightful.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with a compelling performance-efficiency trade-off; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address evaluations, baselines, and theoretical gaps.
