- Introduces PoP, a positive-only random projection method enabling weighting transformations like PPMI that traditional zero-centered projections cannot use.  
- PoP+PPMI achieves competitive MEN semantic similarity results while being much faster and less computationally expensive than neural embeddings and SVD-based count methods.  
- Paper provides thorough empirical exploration of PoP’s parameter effects (dimensionality, sparsity) and practical guidance for use.  
- Strong motivation: addresses the limitation of existing random projection methods in integrating weighting schemes.  
- Convincing speed and efficiency comparisons highlight practical benefits of PoP.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with precise notation and rationale for design choices (e.g., correlation metrics).  
- Lacks broad evaluation — results shown only on MEN; additional datasets and downstream tasks are needed.  
- Little theoretical analysis — no rigorous justification of PoP’s statistical properties or the effect of the positive-only constraint.  
- Performance is sensitive to parameters; robustness across datasets and need for systematic tuning remains unclear.  
- Missing comparisons to simpler baselines (e.g., raw counts, TF-IDF) to isolate PoP+PPMI benefits.  
- Requests for authors: provide additional evaluations (SimLex-999, WordSim353), outline avenues for theoretical analysis, and report parameter robustness across datasets.  
- Requests a detailed computational cost (time and memory) breakdown for PoP, PPMI, and competing methods.  
- Asks why α = 0.5 was chosen, whether it was tuned, and how varying it affects performance.  
- Supplemental materials and Figure 3 are appreciated; discussion section effectively summarizes contributions and future directions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing theoretical gaps and expanding empirical evaluation.
