- PoP introduces positive-only random projections that enable applying weighting schemes like PPMI.
- PoP+PPMI yields competitive MEN benchmark performance compared to neural embeddings and SVD while being far less computationally expensive.
- The paper presents parameter sensitivity analysis and practical guidelines on dimensionality and sparsity.
- PoP directly addresses the incompatibility of PPMI with standard Random Indexing (RI).
- Strong empirical results and ablation studies demonstrate the impact of dimensionality and sparsity.
- The method claims large computational efficiency gains, with model construction in seconds or minutes.
- Authors acknowledge the lack of formal theoretical guarantees and discuss related theoretical considerations.
- Paper insightfully links distribution shape to choice of similarity measures (Kendall’s τb vs Pearson).
- Detailed figures and discussions provide practical guidance for users.
- Evaluation is limited to MEN and the UKWaC corpus, reducing claims of generalizability.
- Lacks comparison to more recent or stronger baselines such as fastText or lightweight neural embeddings.
- The manuscript does not fully explain why positive-only projections permit PPMI application at a mathematical level.
- Potential sensitivity to random initialization is noted but not formally analyzed (e.g., standard deviations).
- Hyperparameter search strategy (for sparsity s and dimensionality m) is under-specified, affecting reproducibility.
- Reviewer asks about computational cost and plans to evaluate PoP on other tasks (e.g., analogy, classification).
- Reviewer requests adding comparisons to fastText or similar neural baselines.
- Reviewer requests a more detailed mathematical explanation of why PoP allows PPMI while RI does not.
- Reviewer asks for reporting performance variability (standard deviation) across multiple random seeds.
- Reviewer requests elaboration on the hyperparameter optimization process used.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and the paper is well-motivated.
- Inclusion of supplemental materials (code, embeddings) would be highly valuable.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
