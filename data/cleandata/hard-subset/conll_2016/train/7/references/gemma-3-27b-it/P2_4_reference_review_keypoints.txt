- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; novel, valuable technique with promising empirical results but needs stronger theory.
- Core idea: Introduces Positive-Only Projections (PoP) for random projection vectors containing only positive values.
- Main contribution: Enables applying PPMI weighting after projection, combining random projection efficiency with PPMI effectiveness.
- Empirical claim: PoP outperforms RI without weighting and matches neural/SVD-weighted methods with PPMI at far lower computational cost.
- Secondary contribution: Detailed empirical analysis of PoP’s parameters and performance characteristics.
- Strength — Novelty: PoP fills a gap by allowing post-projection weighting previously impossible with standard random projections.
- Strength — Efficiency: Demonstrates substantial runtime savings by projecting first into low-dimensional space before weighting.
- Strength — Competitive performance: Solid results on the MEN dataset, comparable to stronger baselines with much less compute.
- Strength — Empirical depth: Thorough investigation of dimensionality (m), sparsity (s), and similarity measures with insightful observations.
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, clearly explained methodology with helpful equations and diagrams.
- Weakness — Theory: Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis and clear error bounds; JL-lemma connection feels superficial.
- Weakness — Evaluation scope: Experiments only on MEN dataset, limiting claims about generalizability.
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Performance depends on m and s with seemingly ad-hoc parameter selection guidelines.
- Weakness — Baselines: Missing comparisons to simple baselines like PPMI without dimensionality reduction.
- Question — Theory: Request for avenues and existing random matrix results to derive formal bounds on PoP’s error.
- Question — Generalizability: Ask for experiments or plans to test PoP on datasets beyond MEN.
- Question — Parameter tuning: Request details on how m and s were selected and whether a validation set was used.
- Question — Ablation: Request direct comparison to simple PPMI without dimensionality reduction to quantify PoP’s benefit.
- Minor comments: Improve formatting of Figure 3 and verify reference completeness.
- Overall conclusion: Strong submission; addressing theoretical grounding and expanding evaluation would increase impact.
