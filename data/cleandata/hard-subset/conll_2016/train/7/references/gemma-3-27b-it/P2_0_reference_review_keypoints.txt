- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — PoP is novel, technically sound, and shows promising improvements over existing random projection methods, though writing is dense and evaluation limited.
- Main contribution: Positive-Only Projection (PoP) — a random projection matrix with only positive values that allows post-projection PPMI weighting.
- Secondary contributions: empirical demonstration that PoP+PPMI is competitive with neural embeddings while being much faster, plus detailed parameter/behavior analysis.
- Strength: novelty & technical soundness — clear justification for why standard random projections preclude post-projection weighting and why PoP enables it.
- Strength: practical efficiency — PoP builds models in seconds/minutes versus hours/days for neural/SVD methods.
- Strength: empirical validation — PoP+PPMI outperforms RI and count-based baselines on MEN; ablation studies strengthen claims.
- Strength: detailed parameter exploration — analysis of dimensionality (m), sparsity (s), orthogonality, and their effects is thorough and informative.
- Strength: clear motivation & literature connection — situates PoP well within distributional semantics and motivates the approach.
- Weakness: limited evaluation — experiments only on MEN; needs broader tasks/datasets to demonstrate generalizability.
- Weakness: writing clarity & density — complex sentences, jargon, and parentheticals impede readability and need simplification and examples.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to simpler baselines — should include comparisons like PCA-reduced count models to clarify PoP’s advantages.
- Weakness: theoretical gap — lacks a detailed mathematical account for PoP error bounds (δ) and fuller theoretical properties.
- Questions for authors: plans to evaluate on more tasks/datasets (analogy, sentence similarity, etc.)?
- Questions for authors: are there other influential parameters (e.g., α) and can their sensitivity be analyzed?
- Questions for authors: provide a detailed computational cost breakdown versus neural embeddings and PPMI on different corpus sizes?
- Questions for authors: how does reduced orthogonality from higher s relate to performance drops, and are there mitigation techniques beyond lowering s?
- Additional comments: Figure 3 axes are too small and hard to read.
- Additional comments: add a table summarizing key parameters and recommended ranges.
- Additional comments: simplify and clarify the discussion of Kendall’s τb and Pearson’s r.
