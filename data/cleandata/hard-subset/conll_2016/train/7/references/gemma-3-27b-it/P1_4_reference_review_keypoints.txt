- Introduces Positive-Only Projection (PoP), a random projection method enabling PPMI-style weighting for efficient semantic space construction.
- PoP’s projection matrix design allows application of weighting transforms previously incompatible with RI/RRI, combining efficiency with representational power.
- Empirically, PoP with PPMI achieves competitive performance on the MEN semantic similarity benchmark while reducing training time.
- Provides thorough analysis of PoP behavior across dimensionality and sparsity, offering practical insights and ablation results.
- Addresses the computational cost of building embeddings, offering much faster model construction suitable for rapid prototyping and constrained environments.
- Method is clearly and mathematically well-explained with intuitive connections to existing techniques like RI.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive on MEN, including comparisons with and without PPMI and parameter studies.
- Discusses theoretical implications and connections to randomized algorithms, PPMI, Gaussian assumptions, and correlation measures.
- Reports substantial speed gains (seconds/minutes vs hours/days), increasing practical relevance.
- Evaluation limited to a single benchmark (MEN), reducing evidence of generalizability to other semantic tasks.
- Lacks comparisons to strong, recent baselines such as fastText or GloVe to contextualize performance.
- Parameter sensitivity (m and s) appears dataset-dependent with limited guidance on tuning and trade-offs.
- Theoretical guarantees and rigorous analysis of the PoP projection matrix are still lacking.
- Justification for switching from Kendall’s Tau to Pearson after PPMI feels ad-hoc and needs more rigorous statistical discussion.
- Requests results on additional datasets and downstream tasks (e.g., word analogy, text classification) to demonstrate generalizability.
- Asks for direct comparisons to fastText and GloVe in terms of accuracy and training time.
- Seeks concrete guidelines for selecting m and s and clarity on PPMI parameter impacts.
- Encourages theoretical investigation into distortion bounds and Johnson-Lindenstrauss–style guarantees for PoP.
- Queries scalability to very large vocabularies/corpora and any practical memory or runtime limits.
- Appreciates offer of supplemental material and requests code/embeddings for reproducibility.
- Notes Figure 3’s usefulness in showing PPMI weight distributions.
- Finds discussion of derandomization techniques an interesting direction for future work.
- Overall recommendation: promising method with clear explanation and competitive results; accept with minor revisions contingent on broader evaluation and stronger baselines.
