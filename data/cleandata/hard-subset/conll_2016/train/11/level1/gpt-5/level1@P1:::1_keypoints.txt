- Task: Revisit “main concept” (MC) resolution in Wikipedia — identify mentions referring to the article’s main concept.
- Method: Construct small OntoNotes-style Wikipedia dataset (WCR) and train two linear SVMs for pronominal vs non-pronominal mentions.
- Features: Use Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived attributes (title, redirects, inferred/KB types, gender/number, etc.) to compare mentions to the MC.
- Results: Large gains over CR baselines (≈+13–15 F1 on MC task) and +4 CoNLL F1 when adding MC classifier as an early sieve in Dcoref.
- Contribution 1: A high-accuracy, Wikipedia-targeted MC-resolution classifier that exploits curated links and KB attributes.
- Contribution 2: A small annotated Wikipedia coreference dataset (WCR) and ablation studies showing which Wikipedia/KB features matter.
- Contribution 3: A simple, effective method to inject MC decisions into a full CR pipeline (Dcoref sieve), yielding consistent gains.
- Strength: Clear, practically useful task definition for Wikipedia-centric NLP (OIE, IE, entity analytics) and demonstration that news-trained CR fails on Wikipedia.
- Strength: Effective exploitation of Wikipedia structure and KB attributes to avoid linker trade-offs and cascading errors.
- Strength: Strong empirical improvements on targeted MC task (F1 ≈88.8) and pronominal mentions, and consistent CoNLL gains when integrated into Dcoref.
- Strength: Sensible engineering choices (separate pronoun/non-pronoun models, well-motivated feature families, normalized frequency/positional cues).
- Strength: Authors intend to release WCR, features, and outputs to promote reproducibility.
- Weakness: CV methodology may inflate performance if folds are not document-level, enabling train/test leakage of article-specific features.
- Weakness: Small dataset (30 docs, ~60k tokens, 1.8k MC mentions) with limited diversity; full-CR gains reported only on WCR limit generality claims.
- Weakness: Baselines may be too weak — a stronger deterministic, KB/string-rule baseline (title/redirects/first-sentence type + pronoun rules) is not reported.
- Weakness: Dependency on discontinued/sparse Freebase; no coverage stats or analysis of robustness with Wikidata or without KB attributes.
- Weakness: Incomplete reporting/clarity (missing tables referenced, limited error analysis, confusion cases like org vs location not deeply explored).
- Question: How were CV folds constructed — document-level or random mention split; please report leave-one-document-out CV.
- Question: What is the coverage of Wikipedia/Freebase attributes (percent of mentions/MCs with link or Freebase data) and performance when KB attributes are ablated or removed?
- Question: Please report results for a stronger deterministic baseline using title/redirect matching, first-sentence inferred type, and simple pronoun rules.
- Question: How is “inferred type” extracted from the first sentence; what are its accuracy, coverage, and precision/recall vs Freebase types?
- Question: For Dcoref++, how many errors stem from preventing later sieves from adding mentions to the MC chain; would relaxing constraints improve recall?
- Comment: Consider replacing Freebase with Wikidata and report coverage/performance parity.
- Comment: Provide inter-annotator agreement and full annotation protocol details (appositives, copular, attribution).
- Comment: Include topic-wise breakdown (persons/orgs/locations/events) and macro-averaged scores across articles for robustness assessment.
