Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper defines and evaluates a focused subtask of coreference in Wikipedia: identifying all mentions of the article’s main concept (MC). The authors build a feature-based binary classifier (separate models for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions) that exploits Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived attributes, and show sizable gains over baselines derived from state-of-the-art CR systems.
- A small Wikipedia coreference dataset (WCR) is used to benchmark both standard CR systems and the proposed MC classifier; the authors report that generic CR systems degrade markedly on Wikipedia.
- Integrating the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Stanford’s Dcoref improves overall CR performance on WCR.

Contributions (as assessed)
1) A practical, Wikipedia-specific MC mention identification method leveraging article markup and external KB attributes, with clear empirical gains on the defined task.
2) An empirical testbed and evidence that off-the-shelf CR systems underperform on Wikipedia, plus a simple integration into Dcoref that yields measurable end-to-end CR gains.
3) A dataset (WCR) tailored to Wikipedia CR (though current availability details are unclear under anonymity).

Strengths
- Strong empirical improvements on the defined task: The proposed classifier achieves 88.79 F1 overall, with 88.99 F1 on pronominals and 88.67 F1 on non-pronominals (Table 4), clearly outperforming all baselines constructed from Dcoref and Scoref outputs (best baselines ~75.77 F1 overall). This indicates the designed features capture Wikipedia-specific cues effectively.
- Sensible use of Wikipedia markup to avoid error-prone entity linking: The method maps mentions to attributes using human-authored hyperlinks within the same article, and augments the MC with title, redirects, aliases, and types. This design is technically sound for the Wikipedia setting and plausibly higher precision than pipelined NEL, aligning with prior observations about cascading errors.
- Feature family analysis and ablations (for pronominals) provide evidence of where gains originate: Progressive inclusion of MC coarse attributes, positional, POS, and distance features lifts F1 from 74.11 to 88.99 (Table 2), demonstrating the complementary value of structural and syntactic cues in Wikipedia articles.
- End-to-end CR impact: Incorporating the classifier as a first sieve into Dcoref yields a +3.34 CoNLL F1 improvement (51.77 to 55.11, Table 5) and gains across MUC, B3, and CEAFφ4. This supports the claim that MC decisions can beneficially constrain downstream sieves in a full CR pipeline.
- Clear demonstration that genre mismatch matters: Multiple recent CR systems suffer 10–17 CoNLL F1 degradation on WCR relative to OntoNotes (Table 1), reinforcing the need for genre-adapted methods and datasets.

Weaknesses
- Data size and availability limit generality and reproducibility: WCR contains only 30 articles (~60k tokens). Details are deferred to a “non-anonymized version,” and the release URL is a placeholder. Without immediate access, it is difficult to verify sampling, annotation quality, and the exact evaluation protocol. Conclusions may not generalize across Wikipedia’s diversity.
- Potential cross-validation leakage and unit of splitting unclear: The paper reports 10-fold cross-validation but does not state whether folds are at the document level. If folds split mentions rather than documents, training and test could share the same article. Because features heavily exploit article-specific markup (title, redirects, internal links, frequencies), cross-article contamination would inflate performance. This is a critical methodological detail.
- Labeling of automatically added singleton mentions is ambiguous: The training pool merges WCR-annotated mentions with ~13.4k automatically extracted mentions, but WCR lacks singletons. It is unclear how positives/negatives were assigned to these added mentions (e.g., assumed negatives unless matched to MC?), and whether mislabeled singletons introduce bias. Clarification is needed to assess soundness of supervision and reported metrics.
- Baseline strength and fairness: The strongest non-CR baseline is B4 (substring aggregation with pronoun heuristic). However, given the method’s reliance on title/aliases/redirects, a strong string/alias-only supervised baseline (e.g., logistic regression over string/position/frequency features) or a rule-based baseline using MC title, redirects, infobox types, and section structure would be informative. Current baselines depend on CR outputs that underperform on Wikipedia and may understate the difficulty.
- External KB dependence and portability: The approach relies on Freebase attributes for types, gender, and number. The paper does not quantify the contribution of Freebase versus Wikipedia-only features, nor robustness when KB attributes are missing or noisy. This affects applicability to non-English Wikipedias or settings without reliable KB coverage.

Questions to authors
- How were the 10-fold splits constructed? Were folds at the document level to prevent train/test contamination within the same article?
- How were labels assigned to the ~13.4k automatically added mentions (singletons)? Were any of these positive MC mentions, and if so, how identified without gold singleton annotations?
- Which Wikipedia/Freebase snapshots were used? What is the coverage of Freebase attributes for mentions/MCs in WCR? Can ablations quantify the impact of removing Freebase-derived features?
- Do the features use only content from the target article (including its internal links and redirects to the MC) or also information from linked articles? Please clarify the exact scope of information available at inference time.
- Can the authors provide an ablation table for non-pronominal features analogous to Table 2?

Additional comments
- Consider adding a purely Wikipedia-only variant (no Freebase) and a Wikipedia-only-at-inference setting (in case KB is unavailable) to assess robustness.
- Reporting per-topic performance (persons, organizations, locations, events) on WCR would help understand where the method excels or struggles.
- Clarify whether the Dcoref++ sieve prevents incorrect additions to the MC chain from later sieves at the cost of recall; error breakdowns by link type (pronoun, nominal, named) would be useful.
