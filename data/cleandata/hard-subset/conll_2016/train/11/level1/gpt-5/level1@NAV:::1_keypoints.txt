- Task: identify all mentions referring to a Wikipedia article’s main concept (MC) and evaluate MC resolution on a specialized testbed (WCR).
- Method: feature-based SVM classifiers (separate pronominal and non-pronominal models) using Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes, compared to heuristics over CR systems.
- Pipeline: insert MC classifier as a high-precision leading sieve in Dcoref, showing gains on full-document coreference.

- Contribution: a task formulation and WCR testbed with evaluation protocol and baselines for Main Concept Resolution.
- Contribution: a simple, high-precision MC mention classifier leveraging Wikipedia/Freebase signals that markedly outperforms adapted CR baselines.
- Contribution: evidence that injecting MC decisions into a coreference pipeline (Dcoref++) yields notable CoNLL F1 improvements on Wikipedia.

- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem focus with practical utility for Wikipedia-specific IE and entity-centric tasks.
- Strength: strong empirical gains—classifier achieves ~89 F1 on MC identification, beating Dcoref/Scoref baselines by ~13–15 F1 with sensible ablations.
- Strength: demonstrated pipeline benefit—adding MC sieve to Dcoref improves CoNLL F1 by about 4 points.
- Strength: robust design using human-curated Wikipedia structure (titles, redirects, links) instead of noisy entity linking, which boosts precision.

- Weakness: small dataset scope—WCR contains only 30 documents (~60k tokens), limiting statistical confidence and generalization claims.
- Weakness: possible cross-fold leakage—10-fold CV may be at the mention level rather than document level, risking inflated scores.
- Weakness: dependence on Freebase (deprecated) raises reproducibility and longevity concerns; coverage and redirect noise noted in errors.
- Weakness: baselines are heuristics on older CR systems; no comparison to stronger modern neural CR/domain-adaptation methods or to supervised MC models without KB features.

- Question: confirm whether cross-validation is document-level (no article in both train and test); if not, report doc-level CV results.
- Question: how sensitive are results to removing Freebase features, and can Wikidata substitute without performance loss?
- Question: what is the failure profile by page type (biography vs organization vs location) and by link density—please report per-category metrics?
- Question: will the authors release the WCR dataset and code (anonymized) at review time to support reproducibility?
