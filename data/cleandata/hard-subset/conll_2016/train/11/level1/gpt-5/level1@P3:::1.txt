Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper defines and evaluates “Main Concept (MC) Resolution” for Wikipedia: identifying all mentions in an article that refer to the page’s main subject. The approach uses two SVM classifiers (pronominal vs. non-pronominal) with features derived from Wikipedia markup (titles, redirects, anchors), Freebase attributes (types, gender/number), POS/position cues, and simple lexical statistics. It also integrates the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref to improve full CR on Wikipedia.
- Main contributions as I see them:
  1) A focused, Wikipedia-specific MC mention identification model that substantially outperforms baselines derived from state-of-the-art CR systems and improves a full CR pipeline when injected as a sieve.
  2) An empirical testbed (WCR) and analysis showing a sizable domain gap between news-trained CR systems and Wikipedia, plus an informative feature ablation for pronominal/non-pronominal mentions.
  3) A pragmatic exploitation of Wikipedia markup and Freebase to avoid expensive entity linking while still obtaining high-quality attributes for mentions and the MC.

Strengths
- Clear task formulation with practical utility: Isolating the main subject mentions is both meaningful in Wikipedia and beneficial downstream (e.g., OIE). Unlike general CR, the task admits sharp signals from page structure; the work leverages those effectively.
- Strong empirical gains and informative ablations: The proposed classifier yields large F1 improvements over baselines for both pronouns and non-pronouns, with a compelling ablation that quantifies the incremental value of title, inferred type, variants, and entity type features. The pronoun model’s stepwise improvements (base → +main → +position → +tag → +distance) demonstrate careful feature engineering grounded in Wikipedia’s discourse structure.
- Sensible use of markup to sidestep noisy EL: Using internal links and redirects to propagate attributes yields high-precision cues without full wikification; this is a solid design choice for Wikipedia specifically.
- Integration into a full CR pipeline: The “Dcoref++” sieve instantiation is a concrete demonstration that MC resolution can materially help general CR on Wikipedia, improving CoNLL F1 by 4 points on WCR. This is an impactful connection between the niche task and general CR.
- Insight on domain mismatch: The head-to-head comparison of several well-known CR systems on WCR vs. OntoNotes underscores the genre shift issue and motivates specialized approaches; the observation that deterministic sieves fare comparatively better on Wikipedia is noteworthy.

Weaknesses
- Dataset size and split methodology: WCR contains only 30 documents (~60k tokens). It is unclear whether the 10-fold cross-validation is by document or by mention. If folds are not strictly document-disjoint, per-document features (counts, positions) and MC-specific attributes can leak across train/test, inflating scores. Given the small number of documents, a leave-one-document-out or document-level CV should be specified and used. Without this, the reported 89 F1 could be optimistic.
- Baseline fairness and training mismatch: The main baselines rely on off-the-shelf CR systems trained on OntoNotes newswire, then apply heuristics (B1–B4) to extract MC chains. The proposed classifier is trained on WCR; the CR baselines are not adapted or retrained. This is not a level playing field. A stronger baseline would include: (a) re-training a CR system (or a simple binary classifier) on WCR to tag MC mentions; (b) a supervised mention-level classifier using only surface features (no markup/Freebase) to isolate the contribution of Wikipedia-specific information.
- Incomplete labeling and potential noise: WCR excludes singletons, yet the training set adds ~13.4k automatically extracted mentions. How were labels obtained for these added mentions? Treating all added singletons as negatives risks label noise if some are genuine MC mentions omitted by the original annotation. The proportion of positives and negatives in these added mentions, and any filtering, should be clarified.
- Reproducibility and anonymity issues: Critical resource details are deferred to a “nonanonymized version,” and promised releases are not available for review. Precise definitions of features (e.g., how “inferred type” is extracted from the first sentence), the exact fold assignment, and code/data availability are essential. As written, the work is difficult to reproduce faithfully.
- Potential circularity/overfitting to markup: The method assigns attributes to mentions by matching surface forms to internal links in the same article. This may create optimistic conditions by allowing supervision-like signals from human-curated links without explicitly modeling unlinking bias (most mentions in Wikipedia are not linked). The impact of link sparsity and noise (e.g., redirects like “Johnston and Sand Islands”) is acknowledged but not quantified. A controlled ablation removing internal-link-based attributes would better isolate gains from markup vs. generic features.
- Limited scope and generalization: The approach depends on Freebase (now deprecated) and English Wikipedia’s markup conventions. It is unclear how well this method adapts to other languages, Wikidata in place of Freebase, or non-encyclopedic genres. While the task is Wikipedia-specific, the paper would benefit from a cross-lingual or non-Freebase experiment or at least a discussion and a plan for migration (e.g., to Wikidata).

Questions to authors
- Cross-validation protocol: Are folds document-disjoint? If not, please re-run with document-level CV and report results.
- Labeling of added mentions: How did you obtain gold labels for the 13.4k automatically extracted mentions? Were any heuristics used to filter noisy negatives/positives?
- Inferred type extraction: What exact algorithm extracts the MC type from the first sentence (patterns, POS constraints, fallback behavior)? What is its accuracy?
- Baseline retraining: Can you re-train Scoref or Berkeley on WCR (even with limited data) or train a simple mention-level classifier using only surface features to better assess the added value of Wikipedia/Freebase features?
- Feature ablation: Please report performance when removing internal-link-derived attributes and/or Freebase attributes to quantify reliance on external knowledge. Also, how often are MC mentions proper-name vs. nominal vs. pronominal in WCR?
- Resource plan: Given Freebase’s deprecation, can the approach use Wikidata? What modifications are needed?

Additional comments
- Please report statistical significance across folds and include per-document variance to assess robustness.
- Clarify whether evaluation counts only mentions present in WCR annotation or includes added singletons; if the latter, ensure consistent gold labels and report mention detection impact.
- Some feature definitions (Wu & Palmer similarity between strings without sense disambiguation) need clarification.
- Writing is clear overall; a few footnotes and references (e.g., WCR URL, “www.somewhere.country”) should be replaced with stable, anonymized links for review.
