- Paper defines “Main Concept (MC) Resolution” for Wikipedia and uses two SVMs (pronominal vs non-pronominal) with Wikipedia/Freebase/POS/lexical features, plus a Dcoref sieve integration.
- Claimed contributions: a focused Wikipedia-specific MC model that outperforms baselines and improves full CR when used as a sieve.
- Claimed contributions: creation of WCR testbed showing a domain gap between news-trained CR systems and Wikipedia, with informative pronominal/non-pronominal feature ablations.
- Claimed contributions: pragmatic use of Wikipedia markup and Freebase to obtain high-quality mention attributes without full entity linking.
- Strength: Clear, practical task formulation that leverages Wikipedia page structure for sharp signals useful downstream.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains and careful ablations showing incremental value of title, inferred type, variants, and entity-type features.
- Strength: Sensible use of internal links/redirects to get high-precision cues without noisy full wikification.
- Strength: Concrete integration into a full CR pipeline (Dcoref++) demonstrating a meaningful CoNLL F1 improvement on WCR.
- Strength: Useful insight on genre/domain mismatch — deterministic sieves perform relatively well on Wikipedia compared to news.
- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 documents, ~60k tokens) and unclear CV split; possible leakage if folds are not document-disjoint, inflating reported scores.
- Weakness: Baselines are off-the-shelf systems trained on OntoNotes and not retrained on WCR, producing an unfair comparison to a model trained on WCR.
- Weakness: Labeling of ~13.4k automatically extracted mentions is unclear; treating added singletons as negatives may introduce noisy labels.
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues and deferred non-anonymous resources; feature extraction, fold assignments, and data/code are not sufficiently specified.
- Weakness: Potential circularity/overfitting from relying on internal-link matching (human-curated links) without quantifying link sparsity or unlinking bias.
- Weakness: Limited generalization due to reliance on Freebase and English Wikipedia markup; unclear portability to Wikidata, other languages, or non-encyclopedic genres.
- Question: Are cross-validation folds document-disjoint, and can results be re-run with document-level CV (or leave-one-document-out)?
- Question: How were gold labels obtained for the 13.4k auto-extracted mentions and were any heuristics used to filter noise?
- Question: What exact algorithm extracts the MC type from the first sentence (patterns, POS constraints, fallback) and what is its accuracy?
- Question: Can baselines (Scoref/Berkeley) be retrained on WCR or can a supervised mention-level classifier with only surface features be trained for a fairer comparison?
- Question: Please report performance without internal-link-derived and/or Freebase-derived attributes and provide the distribution of proper/nominal/pronominal MC mentions in WCR.
- Question: Given Freebase’s deprecation, can the approach migrate to Wikidata and what modifications would be needed?
- Additional comment: Report statistical significance across folds and per-document variance to assess robustness.
- Additional comment: Clarify whether evaluation includes only original WCR annotations or also added singletons and ensure consistent gold labels.
- Additional comment: Clarify some feature definitions (e.g., Wu & Palmer similarity without sense disambiguation) and replace unstable/non-anonymized links.
