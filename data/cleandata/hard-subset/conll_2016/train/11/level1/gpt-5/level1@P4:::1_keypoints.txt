- Paper defines the task of identifying all mentions of a Wikipedia article’s main concept (MC) and proposes feature-based binary classifiers (separate pronominal/non-pronominal) using Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes.
- Authors introduce a small Wikipedia coreference dataset (WCR) to benchmark MC identification and show generic CR systems degrade markedly on Wikipedia.
- Integrating the MC classifier as a high-precision first sieve into Stanford Dcoref improves end-to-end CR performance on WCR.
- Contribution: practical, Wikipedia-specific MC mention identification method leveraging article markup and external KB attributes with clear empirical gains.
- Contribution: empirical evidence and testbed showing off-the-shelf CR systems underperform on Wikipedia and that a simple integration into Dcoref yields measurable gains.
- Contribution: release of a WCR dataset tailored to Wikipedia coreference (availability unclear under anonymity).
- Strength: Strong empirical results—classifier achieves ~88.8 F1 overall versus ~75.8 F1 for best baselines, indicating effective feature design.
- Strength: Sensible use of Wikipedia markup (internal links, title, redirects, aliases, types) to avoid error-prone entity linking pipelines.
- Strength: Feature family analysis and ablations (for pronominals) show cumulative gains from MC attributes, positional, POS, and distance features.
- Strength: End-to-end impact demonstrated—a +3.34 CoNLL F1 improvement when adding the MC sieve to Dcoref, with gains across MUC, B3, and CEAFφ4.
- Strength: Clear evidence that genre mismatch matters—recent CR systems drop 10–17 CoNLL F1 on WCR vs. OntoNotes.
- Weakness: WCR is very small (30 articles, ~60k tokens) and dataset availability is unclear, limiting generality and reproducibility.
- Weakness: 10-fold cross-validation details are unclear—if folds are not at the document level, article-level feature leakage could inflate performance.
- Weakness: Labeling of ~13.4k automatically added mentions (singletons) is ambiguous, raising questions about supervision quality and possible label noise.
- Weakness: Baselines may be weak/unfair—stronger string/alias-based supervised or rule-based baselines using title/redirects/infoboxes are missing.
- Weakness: Dependence on Freebase attributes without quantified ablation or robustness analysis limits portability to other Wikipedias or KB-poor settings.
- Question: Were the 10-fold splits constructed at the document level to prevent train/test contamination within the same article?
- Question: How were labels assigned to the ~13.4k automatically added mentions; were any positives present and how were they identified?
- Question: Which Wikipedia and Freebase snapshots were used, and what is Freebase attribute coverage for WCR mentions/MCs?
- Question: Do features use only content from the target article or also information from linked articles at inference time?
- Question: Can the authors provide an ablation table for non-pronominal features analogous to the pronominal Table 2?
- Suggestion: Add a purely Wikipedia-only variant (no Freebase) and an inference-time KB-unavailable setting to assess robustness.
- Suggestion: Report per-topic performance (person, organization, location, event) to show where the method succeeds or fails.
- Suggestion: Clarify whether the Dcoref++ sieve trades recall for preventing incorrect MC additions and provide error breakdowns by mention type (pronoun, nominal, named).
