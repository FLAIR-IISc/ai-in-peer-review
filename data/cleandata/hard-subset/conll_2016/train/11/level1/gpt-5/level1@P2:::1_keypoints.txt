- Task: Identifies mentions in a Wikipedia article that refer to the article’s main concept (MCR) using a feature-based classifier with separate models for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.
- Method: Leverages Wikipedia markup (titles, redirects, internal links) and Freebase attributes, avoiding full NEL, and integrates as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref.
- Dataset: Introduces a small Wikipedia coreference testbed (WCR) of 30 documents (~60k tokens) for evaluation.
- Main empirical claim: The MCR classifier substantially outperforms adapted Dcoref/Scoref baselines (≈13–15 F1 gain; ~89 F1 overall).
- Downstream effect: Adding the MCR sieve to Dcoref (Dcoref++) improves end-to-end Wikipedia coreference by +4 CoNLL F1 on WCR.
- Motivation: Shows state-of-the-art CR systems trained on OntoNotes degrade on Wikipedia, motivating a Wikipedia-specific MCR approach.
- Strength — empirical gains: Consistent large improvements across pronouns and non-pronouns versus thoughtful baselines, with practical significance for Wikipedia IE.
- Strength — feature design: Clever use of human-curated Wikipedia signals and Freebase to increase precision and reduce NEL errors; ablations identify important feature families.
- Strength — relevance: Demonstrates practical utility of MCR for Wikipedia-centric IE and that early high-precision MC decisions help pipelines.
- Strength — evaluation practices: Uses predicted mentions, sensible feature descriptions, linear SVMs, and (assumed) document-level CV to mitigate overfitting.
- Weakness — dataset scale: WCR’s 30-doc size may limit generalization and make reported gains sensitive to sampling; no held-out larger or silver-label evaluations shown.
- Weakness — baselines: Missing stronger MCR-specific baselines (e.g., simple learned string-feature baseline or NEL-based wikification baseline) to isolate feature family value.
- Weakness — dependence on external knowledge: Reliance on Freebase and link density may hurt robustness; paper doesn’t quantify coverage or degradation on low-link articles.
- Weakness — attribute propagation ambiguity: Preprocessing that propagates attributes by surface match can conflate homonyms; lack of error analysis on mispropagation.
- Weakness — reproducibility gaps: Some implementation details and CV protocol under-specified and dataset anonymized at review time, limiting immediate reproducibility.
- Question — CV protocol: Are the 10 folds split at the document level to avoid leakage?
- Question — attribute conflicts: How are conflicting attributes handled when the same surface form links to different pages within an article?
- Question — Freebase coverage: What fraction of mentions receive Freebase attributes and how does performance change when Freebase features are ablated or on low-link-density articles?
- Question — robustness by type: Can MCR performance be broken down by MC type (PER/ORG/LOC/other) and article structure (biography vs organization)?
- Question — simple learned baseline: How would a learned baseline using only title/variant/type overlaps perform to gauge incremental value?
- Question — sieve interaction trade-offs: Does preventing later sieves from adding mentions to the MC chain harm recall or cause errors in other chains?
- Overall assessment: Well-motivated, practically effective contribution with notable gains but reservations about dataset scale, robustness, and missing competitive baselines; likely acceptable if dataset/code released and robustness clarified.
