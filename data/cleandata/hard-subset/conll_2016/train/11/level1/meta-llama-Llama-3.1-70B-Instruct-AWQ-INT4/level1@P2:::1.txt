**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, focusing on identifying all mentions of the main concept being described. The authors design a classifier that leverages Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases like Freebase to acquire useful information about entities. They demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on this task and show its benefits in a full coreference resolution pipeline applied to Wikipedia texts.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Adapting coreference resolution to Wikipedia**: The authors revisit the task of identifying main concept mentions in Wikipedia articles, which has received little attention in recent years. They propose a testbed for evaluating systems designed for this task and compare several state-of-the-art systems on this testbed.
2. **Classifier design**: The authors design a classifier that exploits carefully crafted features extracted from Wikipedia markup and characteristics, as well as from Freebase. They demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art generic coreference resolution engines on this task.
3. **Integration with a full coreference resolution pipeline**: The authors show that integrating their classifier into a state-of-the-art rule-based coreference system improves the detection of coreference chains in Wikipedia articles.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia**: The authors propose a new approach to coreference resolution that is specifically designed for Wikipedia articles. This approach leverages the unique characteristics of Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases like Freebase.
2. **State-of-the-art performance**: The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on the task of identifying main concept mentions in Wikipedia articles.
3. **Well-designed experiments**: The authors conduct thorough experiments to evaluate their approach, including a comparison with several state-of-the-art systems and an analysis of the impact of each feature family on the performance of their classifier.
4. **Useful features and insights**: The authors provide useful insights into the design of their classifier and the features that contribute to its performance. They also highlight the importance of exploiting Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases like Freebase.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset**: The authors use a relatively small dataset (WCR) to evaluate their approach, which may not be representative of the entire Wikipedia corpus.
2. **Overfitting**: The authors use a simple greedy approach to select the most informative features, which may lead to overfitting.
3. **Lack of error analysis**: The authors do not provide a detailed error analysis of their classifier, which would be useful to understand the types of errors it makes and how to improve its performance.
4. **Limited generalizability**: The authors' approach is specifically designed for Wikipedia articles, and it is unclear how well it would generalize to other types of text.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the WCR dataset, including its size, composition, and annotation guidelines?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of overfitting in your feature selection approach?
3. Can you provide a more detailed error analysis of your classifier, including the types of errors it makes and how to improve its performance?
4. Have you considered applying your approach to other types of text, such as news articles or social media posts? If so, what are the challenges and opportunities in adapting your approach to these domains?
