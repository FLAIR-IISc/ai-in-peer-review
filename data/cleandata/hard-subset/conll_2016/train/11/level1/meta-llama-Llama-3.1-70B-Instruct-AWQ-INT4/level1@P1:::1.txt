**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel approach for coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, focusing on identifying mentions of the main concept described in an article. The authors design a classifier that leverages features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to classify mentions as coreferent or not. They evaluate their approach on a dedicated dataset, WCR, and show that it outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems. The authors also demonstrate the benefits of integrating their classifier into a full coreference resolution pipeline.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach for coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles**: The authors propose a new approach for coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, focusing on identifying mentions of the main concept described in an article. This approach is specifically designed to exploit the unique characteristics of Wikipedia articles.
2. **Classifier design and evaluation**: The authors design a classifier that leverages features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to classify mentions as coreferent or not. They evaluate their approach on a dedicated dataset, WCR, and show that it outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.
3. **Integration with full coreference resolution pipeline**: The authors demonstrate the benefits of integrating their classifier into a full coreference resolution pipeline, showing an improvement of 4% in F1 score on a fully fledged coreference task.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a new approach for coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, which is specifically designed to exploit the unique characteristics of Wikipedia articles.
2. **Effective use of features**: The authors design a classifier that effectively leverages features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to classify mentions as coreferent or not.
3. **Strong evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on a dedicated dataset, WCR, and show that it outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.
4. **Integration with full coreference resolution pipeline**: The authors demonstrate the benefits of integrating their classifier into a full coreference resolution pipeline, showing an improvement of 4% in F1 score on a fully fledged coreference task.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset**: The authors use a relatively small dataset, WCR, which may not be representative of all Wikipedia articles.
2. **Simple classifier design**: The authors use a simple classifier design, which may not be robust to variations in the data.
3. **Lack of error analysis**: The authors do not provide a detailed error analysis, which would help to identify areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the WCR dataset, such as the selection criteria for the articles and the annotation process?
2. How do you plan to extend your approach to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?
3. Can you provide more details on the features used in the classifier, such as the specific features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of cascading errors in the pipeline, which can occur when inaccuracies in the named-entity linking or coreference resolution steps affect the overall performance of the system?
