- Proposes a novel Wikipedia-focused coreference approach that targets mentions of the article's main concept.  
- Designs a classifier that leverages features from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to label mentions as coreferent.  
- Evaluates on the WCR dataset and reports outperforming state-of-the-art coreference systems.  
- Integrates the classifier into a full coreference pipeline and reports a ~4% F1 improvement.  
- Approach is tailored to exploit unique structural and content characteristics of Wikipedia.  
- Effective feature usage from Wikipedia markup and Freebase appears to drive performance gains.  
- Strong empirical evaluation on a dedicated dataset supports the method's effectiveness.  
- Demonstrated end-to-end pipeline benefits when incorporating the specialized classifier.  
- Weakness: WCR is relatively small and may not represent the full diversity of Wikipedia articles.  
- Weakness: Classifier design is simple and may lack robustness to data variation.  
- Weakness: No detailed error analysis provided to identify failure modes or improvement areas.  
- Question: Request for more details on WCR selection criteria and annotation process.  
- Question: How will the approach be extended to identify all coreference relations in an article?  
- Question: Request for specifics on the exact features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase.  
- Question: How will cascading errors from named-entity linking or upstream steps be addressed?
