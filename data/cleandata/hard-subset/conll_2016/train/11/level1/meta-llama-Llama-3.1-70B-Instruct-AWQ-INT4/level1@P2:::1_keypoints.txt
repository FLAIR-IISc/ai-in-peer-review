- Paper proposes a new classifier for identifying all mentions of the main concept in Wikipedia articles using Wikipedia markup and Freebase.  
- Authors show their method outperforms state-of-the-art coreference systems on this Wikipedia-focused task and improves a full coreference pipeline.  

- Contribution: Revisit and formalize the task of identifying main concept mentions in Wikipedia and provide a testbed for evaluation.  
- Contribution: Design a classifier that uses features from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to improve mention identification.  
- Contribution: Integrate the classifier into a rule-based coreference system and demonstrate end-to-end improvements.  

- Strength: Novel, Wikipedia-specific approach that leverages unique markup and external KBs.  
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the target task.  
- Strength: Well-designed experiments, including comparisons and feature ablation.  
- Strength: Provides useful feature design insights and highlights the value of Wikipedia/Freebase signals.  

- Weakness: Evaluation uses a relatively small WCR dataset that may not represent full Wikipedia.  
- Weakness: Feature selection via a simple greedy method risks overfitting.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks a detailed error analysis to characterize failure cases.  
- Weakness: Method’s generalizability beyond Wikipedia (e.g., news, social media) is unclear.  

- Question: Provide full details on the WCR dataset size, composition, and annotation guidelines.  
- Question: How will you mitigate overfitting in the feature selection process?  
- Question: Can you include a detailed error analysis describing common classifier mistakes?  
- Question: Have you tested or considered adapting the approach to other domains (news, social media), and what challenges do you foresee?
