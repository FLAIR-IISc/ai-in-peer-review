**Summary of the Paper**

This paper revisits the task of coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, specifically identifying all mentions of the main concept being described. The authors propose a novel approach that leverages Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases like Freebase to acquire useful information on entities. They design a classifier that outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on this task. The approach is evaluated on a dedicated dataset, WCR, and shows significant improvements over baselines built on top of existing coreference resolution systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles**: The authors propose a new approach that exploits Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases to identify mentions of the main concept.
2. **Effective use of features**: The authors design a set of features that capture information from Wikipedia and Freebase, which are shown to be effective in improving coreference resolution performance.
3. **Improved performance on a dedicated dataset**: The authors evaluate their approach on a dedicated dataset, WCR, and show significant improvements over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of leveraging Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases to improve coreference resolution performance.
2. **Well-designed features**: The authors design a set of features that capture relevant information from Wikipedia and Freebase, which are shown to be effective in improving coreference resolution performance.
3. **Significant improvements over state-of-the-art systems**: The authors demonstrate significant improvements over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on a dedicated dataset.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset**: The authors evaluate their approach on a relatively small dataset, WCR, which may not be representative of all Wikipedia articles.
2. **Simple classifier**: The authors use a simple Support Vector Machine classifier, which may not be the most effective choice for this task.
3. **Limited generalizability**: The authors focus on identifying mentions of the main concept, which may not be generalizable to other coreference resolution tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend this work to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?
2. Can you provide more details on the features used in the classifier and how they were selected?
3. How do you plan to evaluate the effectiveness of this approach on a larger dataset or on other types of text?
