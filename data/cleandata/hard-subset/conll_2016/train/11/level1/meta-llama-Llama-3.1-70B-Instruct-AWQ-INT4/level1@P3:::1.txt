**Summary and Main Contributions**

This paper presents a novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, focusing on identifying mentions of the main concept being described. The authors design a classifier that leverages Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases like Freebase to acquire useful information on entities. They demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on this task and show its benefits when integrated into a full coreference resolution pipeline.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. A novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, specifically designed to identify mentions of the main concept.
2. A classifier that effectively leverages Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases like Freebase to improve coreference resolution performance.
3. A thorough evaluation of the proposed approach, including comparisons to state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems and an analysis of the impact of different feature families.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles**: The authors tackle a specific and challenging task in coreference resolution, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach in identifying mentions of the main concept in Wikipedia articles.
2. **Effective use of Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases**: The authors cleverly leverage Wikipedia markup and Freebase to acquire useful information on entities, which significantly improves coreference resolution performance.
3. **Thorough evaluation and analysis**: The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their approach, including comparisons to state-of-the-art systems and an analysis of the impact of different feature families.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset size**: The WCR dataset used in this study is relatively small, consisting of only 30 documents. While the authors argue that the dataset is representative, a larger dataset would provide more robust results.
2. **Over-reliance on Wikipedia markup and Freebase**: The authors' approach heavily relies on Wikipedia markup and Freebase, which may not be available or reliable for all Wikipedia articles. This limitation may impact the generalizability of the approach.
3. **Lack of error analysis**: While the authors provide some examples of errors, a more thorough error analysis would help to identify areas for improvement and provide insights into the limitations of the approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limited dataset size and ensure that the results are generalizable to a larger set of Wikipedia articles?
2. Can you provide more details on how the classifier handles cases where Wikipedia markup or Freebase information is incomplete or unreliable?
3. Have you considered using other external knowledge bases or resources to supplement Wikipedia markup and Freebase?
4. Can you provide a more detailed error analysis to help identify areas for improvement and provide insights into the limitations of the approach?
