**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, focusing on identifying mentions of the main concept being described. The authors design a classifier that leverages features extracted from Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases like Freebase. They demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on a dedicated Wikipedia dataset (WCR) and show its benefits in a full coreference resolution pipeline.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel task and dataset**: The paper introduces a new task, main concept resolution, and a dedicated dataset (WCR) for evaluating coreference resolution systems on Wikipedia articles.
2. **Effective features and classifier**: The authors design a classifier that effectively leverages features from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to identify mentions of the main concept, outperforming state-of-the-art systems.
3. **Improved coreference resolution pipeline**: The paper demonstrates the benefits of incorporating the proposed classifier into a state-of-the-art rule-based coreference system, leading to improved performance on a full coreference resolution task.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-motivated task**: The paper addresses a relevant and challenging task in coreference resolution, with significant implications for various NLP applications.
2. **Effective use of Wikipedia markup and Freebase**: The authors demonstrate the value of exploiting Wikipedia's structured data and external knowledge bases to improve coreference resolution.
3. **Strong experimental evaluation**: The paper presents a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach, including comparisons with state-of-the-art systems and an analysis of the impact of different feature families.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset size**: The WCR dataset, although carefully curated, is relatively small compared to other coreference resolution datasets.
2. **Over-reliance on Wikipedia markup**: The paper's approach relies heavily on Wikipedia's structured data, which might limit its applicability to other domains or text genres.
3. **Simple classifier**: The authors use a relatively simple classifier (SVM with a linear kernel), which might not be the most effective choice for this task.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limited size of the WCR dataset, and what strategies will you employ to ensure that the proposed approach generalizes to other datasets?
2. Can you discuss the potential limitations of relying on Wikipedia markup and Freebase, and how you might adapt the approach to other domains or text genres?
3. Have you considered using more advanced machine learning models or techniques, such as deep learning or transfer learning, to further improve the performance of the proposed classifier?
