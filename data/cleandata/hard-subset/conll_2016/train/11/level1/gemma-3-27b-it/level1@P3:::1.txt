This paper addresses the task of main concept resolution in Wikipedia articles, a relatively underexplored area within coreference resolution. The authors revisit this task, framing it as a binary classification problem and demonstrating that a classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup and external knowledge (Freebase) significantly outperforms baselines and improves a rule-based coreference system.

**Contributions:**

1. **A Focused Dataset and Evaluation:** The creation and use of the WCR dataset (though details are promised in the non-anonymized version) is arguably the most significant contribution. The lack of dedicated datasets for coreference in Wikipedia is a clear limitation in the field, and this resource, even at 30 documents, provides a valuable benchmark for future research. The careful evaluation using standard CoNLL metrics is also commendable.
2. **Effective Feature Engineering for Wikipedia:** The authors demonstrate that incorporating features specific to Wikipedia's structure (markup, links) and leveraging Freebase enrichments leads to substantial performance gains. The feature set, while described at a high level, appears thoughtfully designed and effectively captures relevant information for the task.  The analysis of feature importance (Table 2) is helpful.
3. **Demonstrated Improvement to a Coreference Pipeline:** Successfully integrating the classifier into the Dcoref system and achieving performance gains in full coreference resolution is a practical and encouraging result.  This suggests a viable pathway for improving existing systems by specializing them for the nuances of Wikipedia text.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of adapting coreference resolution to Wikipedia, highlighting the shortcomings of systems trained on news-like texts and the unique characteristics of Wikipedia articles.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The quantitative results consistently demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over strong baselines, including state-of-the-art coreference systems. The improvements are substantial and statistically significant, judging from the reported F1 scores.
3. **Thorough Ablation Study:** The feature ablation study (Table 2) provides valuable insights into the contribution of different feature families, allowing for a deeper understanding of the model's behavior. This is critical for identifying the most impactful features and guiding future improvements.
4. **Practical System Integration:** Demonstrating the effectiveness of the classifier within a full coreference pipeline (Dcoref++) adds practical relevance and showcases the potential for real-world applications.
5. **Attention to Detail in Baselines:** The authors don't simply use out-of-the-box coreference systems. The baselines are thoughtfully constructed, attempting to adapt existing systems to the specific task, making the comparison more fair and informative.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Dataset Size and Representativeness:** The WCR dataset, with only 30 documents, is a significant limitation. While the authors acknowledge this, the generalizability of the results is questionable.  It's crucial to understand the criteria for document selection and whether the 30 articles adequately represent the diversity of topics and writing styles found in Wikipedia. The limited size also raises concerns about the reliability of the cross-validation results.
2. **Lack of Detailed Dataset Description:** The paper states that detailed dataset information will be provided in the non-anonymized version. This is unacceptable. A thorough description of the dataset—annotation guidelines, inter-annotator agreement, topic distribution, document length statistics—is essential for evaluating the work and reproducing the results. The current paper feels incomplete without this information.
3. **Feature Set Description is High-Level:** While the authors list feature families, the specific features within those families are not fully detailed.  A more precise description would facilitate understanding and replication. For example, the “semantic relatedness score” is mentioned but the specific method (Wu & Palmer, etc.) is only specified in passing.
4. **Limited Error Analysis:** While the paper touches upon error analysis (Figure 3), it’s relatively superficial.  A more systematic error analysis, categorizing common error types and identifying the underlying causes, would provide valuable insights for future research. The examples provided highlight some issues, but aren’t comprehensive.
5. **Over-reliance on External Resources:** The approach heavily relies on Freebase.  The availability and quality of information in Freebase can be a concern, and the performance of the system may be affected by inconsistencies or missing data.  This dependency should be acknowledged more explicitly.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dataset Details:** Please provide a complete and detailed description of the WCR dataset, including annotation guidelines, inter-annotator agreement, topic distribution, document length statistics, and a justification for the selection of the 30 documents.
2. **Feature List:** Can you provide a more comprehensive list of the specific features used in each feature family?
3. **Error Analysis:** Could you provide a more systematic error analysis, categorizing common error types and discussing potential solutions?
4. **Freebase Dependency:** How sensitive is the performance of the system to the quality and completeness of the Freebase data? Have you investigated alternative knowledge bases?
5. **Scalability:** What is the computational cost of feature extraction, particularly the Freebase lookups? How well does the system scale to larger Wikipedia articles or a larger dataset?

**Additional Comments:**

* The reference to "somewhere.country" for data availability is not helpful. A concrete URL or repository location is needed.
* Consider adding a discussion of related work on knowledge graph completion or entity linking, as these techniques could potentially improve the robustness of the system.
* The paper would benefit from a brief discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential avenues for future research.
