This paper revisits coreference resolution in the specific context of Wikipedia articles, focusing on identifying mentions of the "main concept" (MC) of an article. The authors argue that existing CR systems, trained primarily on news data, perform poorly on Wikipedia, and propose a classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase knowledge to improve MC mention identification. They demonstrate significant improvements over baselines built on top of state-of-the-art CR systems, and show further gains when integrating their classifier into a rule-based CR pipeline.

**Contributions:**

1. **A focused task and dataset:** The paper clearly defines a valuable and under-explored task – identifying mentions of the main concept within a Wikipedia article.  The creation of the WCR dataset (details promised in the non-anonymized version) is a significant contribution, providing a benchmark for evaluating systems designed for this specific task. While the size (30 documents) is relatively small, the annotation following OntoNotes guidelines is commendable.
2. **Effective feature engineering:** The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of relevant features for this task, combining information from Wikipedia markup (links, redirects) and Freebase to represent mentions and the main concept. The feature extraction process is well-described, and the ablation study (Table 2) convincingly shows the importance of different feature families, particularly the MC attributes.
3. **Improved performance on MC identification:**  The proposed classifier achieves substantial improvements over strong baselines on the WCR dataset (F1 gain of 13 points over Dcoref), demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. The integration into Dcoref further highlights the practical benefits, improving overall coreference resolution performance. 

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates a relevant and specific problem – adapting coreference resolution to the unique characteristics of Wikipedia articles. This focus distinguishes it from broader CR research.
2. **Strong Empirical Evaluation:** The experimental setup is thorough. The authors compare against multiple baselines, including state-of-the-art CR systems, and perform a detailed ablation study to understand feature importance. The use of 10-fold cross-validation adds robustness to the results.
3. **Leveraging Wikipedia Structure:** The authors effectively exploit the rich structural information available in Wikipedia (links, markup) as a source of valuable features. This is a key strength of the work, and a logical approach given the domain.
4. **Practical Impact:** Demonstrating improvement on a full coreference resolution pipeline (Dcoref++) shows the real-world potential of the proposed classifier.  
5. **Well-written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and follows a logical structure, making it easy to understand the proposed approach and its evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Dataset Size:** The WCR dataset, consisting of only 30 documents, is a significant limitation. While the authors acknowledge this, it raises concerns about the generalizability of the results. The performance gains might be specific to the characteristics of these 30 articles.
2. **Baseline Dependency:** The performance gains are demonstrated relative to specific baselines (Dcoref and Scoref). It would be beneficial to compare against other, potentially more competitive baselines, or to analyze performance variance across different CR systems.
3. **Limited Error Analysis:** The error analysis, while mentioning a few examples, is relatively superficial. A more in-depth analysis of the types of errors made by the classifier (e.g., common confusions, difficult cases) would provide valuable insights for future improvement.  The example errors (Figure 3) are helpful but limited.
4. **Freebase Dependency:** The reliance on Freebase introduces a dependency on an external knowledge base that may not be available or reliable for all domains. A discussion of the potential impact of Freebase inaccuracies on the results would be useful.
5. **Lack of Generalizability discussion:** While the authors motivate the work by pointing out the differences between news and Wikipedia, they do not adequately discuss *which* aspects of Wikipedia’s structure and style contribute to the performance differences. A discussion about the potential applicability of their features to other domain-specific corpora would be valuable.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dataset Details:** Could you provide more details about the composition of the WCR dataset, specifically the distribution of topics and the average article length? This would help assess its representativeness of Wikipedia as a whole.
2. **Error Analysis:** Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, categorizing the types of errors the classifier makes and identifying common patterns?
3. **Freebase Robustness:** Have you investigated the impact of missing or inaccurate Freebase data on the performance of your classifier? 
4. **Feature Importance:** Were any feature selection techniques used beyond the greedy approach described in Section 6.3? If so, could you share the results?
5. **Generalizability:** Do you have preliminary results or hypotheses about how well the proposed features would transfer to other domain-specific corpora beyond Wikipedia?



**Additional Comments:**

* The paper references a "non-anonymized version" containing details of the WCR dataset. It is crucial that this version is made publicly available upon publication to ensure reproducibility.
* The link to the promised data dump (www.somewhere.country) is a placeholder and needs to be updated.




Overall, this is a solid paper with a clear contribution. The authors have identified a relevant problem, proposed an effective solution, and provided a strong empirical evaluation. The main limitation is the small size of the dataset, but the authors’ strong results and detailed analysis make this a worthwhile contribution to the field of coreference resolution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
