- Paper focus: revisits coreference resolution for Wikipedia, targeting identification of all mentions of an article’s main concept with a tailored classifier.  
- Primary contribution: a feature-rich binary classifier that significantly outperforms baseline coreference systems on the WCR task.  
- Secondary contribution: creation of the WCR evaluation dataset and demonstration of gains when integrating the classifier into a rule-based coreference pipeline.  
- Strength — Novelty: addresses an underexplored problem of adapting coreference to Wikipedia’s characteristics.  
- Strength — Empirical results: large F1 gains (up to ~15 points) over Dcoref/Scoref and a clear ablation study showing which feature families matter.  
- Strength — Feature design: effective use of Wikipedia markup (links, redirects) and external KB attributes (Freebase) with well-motivated MC features.  
- Strength — Practical integration: incorporating the classifier into Dcoref yields a ~4% overall F1 improvement, showing real-world applicability.  
- Weakness — Dataset size/generalizability: WCR is small (30 articles, ~7k mentions), raising concerns that results may not generalize to broader Wikipedia.  
- Weakness — Freebase dependency: heavy reliance on Freebase for attributes is risky given Freebase is no longer maintained.  
- Weakness — Limited error analysis: error analysis is sparse; more detailed qualitative breakdown of error types is needed.  
- Weakness — Baselines: comparisons omit more recent end-to-end neural coreference models, weakening evaluation against state-of-the-art.  
- Question — Dataset expansion: do you plan to expand WCR and how will you ensure diversity and representativeness of articles?  
- Question — Freebase workaround: how will you address Freebase dependency — alternatives like Wikidata/DBpedia or attribute inference?  
- Question — Error breakdown: can you provide a detailed breakdown of common error types and which features cause them?  
- Question — Neural comparison: will you evaluate against recent transformer-based end-to-end coreference systems and how do you expect them to perform on WCR?
