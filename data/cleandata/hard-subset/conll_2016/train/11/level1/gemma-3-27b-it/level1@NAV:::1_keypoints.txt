- Frames main-concept identification in Wikipedia as a binary classification task using Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived features.
- Releases the WCR dataset and a dump of main-concept mentions from English Wikipedia as a research resource.
- Classifier substantially outperforms baseline coreference systems on the constructed WCR dataset.
- Demonstrates positive impact when integrating the classifier into the rule-based Dcoref system.
- Addresses a relevant problem: adapting coreference resolution to Wikipedia where news-trained models underperform.
- Feature engineering is well-motivated, leveraging Wikipedia-specific markup, links, and external knowledge.
- Includes an extensive feature ablation study that clarifies feature importance.
- Experiments are well-conducted with clear baseline comparisons and substantial improvements reported.
- Dataset is very small (30 articles), limiting generalizability and raising statistical-significance concerns.
- Core novelty is incremental; using Wikipedia markup and KBs for coreference is not fundamentally new.
- Presentation issues: Table 5’s long list of systems makes direct comparison difficult.
- Requests more detail on statistical significance given the small dataset.
- Asks for elaboration on WCR creation process and inter-annotator agreement.
- Requests description of the mention-dump’s format and scale (number of articles/mentions).
- Notes "Dcoref++" is used without prior definition and should be defined earlier.
