- Contribution: Defines MC identification task and introduces the WCR dataset (30 documents, OntoNotes-style annotation).
- Contribution: Strong feature engineering combining Wikipedia markup (links, redirects) and Freebase; ablation shows MC attributes are crucial.
- Contribution: Classifier yields substantial gains (≈13 F1 over Dcoref) and improves a full CR pipeline when integrated.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem focusing on adapting coreference resolution to Wikipedia.
- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation with multiple baselines, ablation study, and 10-fold cross-validation.
- Strength: Effective exploitation of Wikipedia’s structural information (links/markup) for features.
- Strength: Demonstrated practical impact by improving an end-to-end coreference system (Dcoref++).
- Strength: Well-written and well-organized presentation.

- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 articles) raises concerns about generalizability.
- Weakness: Comparisons limited to specific baselines (Dcoref, Scoref); broader baseline analysis is needed.
- Weakness: Error analysis is superficial and lacks detailed categorization of common failure modes.
- Weakness: Reliance on Freebase creates external-knowledge dependency and potential robustness issues.
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of which Wikipedia-specific traits drive the improvements and feature transferability to other domains.

- Question: Please provide WCR dataset composition details (topic distribution, average article length).
- Question: Can you provide a more detailed, categorized error analysis of classifier failures?
- Question: Have you evaluated sensitivity to missing or inaccurate Freebase entries?
- Question: Were other feature-selection methods tried beyond the described greedy approach?
- Question: Do you have results or hypotheses about feature transferability to other domain-specific corpora?

- Additional comment: The non-anonymized version and the WCR dataset should be made publicly available for reproducibility.
