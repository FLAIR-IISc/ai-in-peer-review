## Review of "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

This paper revisits the task of identifying mentions of the main concept within a Wikipedia article, a surprisingly under-explored area given Wikipedia’s prevalence in NLP. The authors present a binary classification approach leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase links to determine coreference to the main concept, demonstrating significant performance gains over baseline coreference systems and showing improvements when integrated into a rule-based system.

The primary contribution of this work is the creation and evaluation of a dedicated approach for main concept coreference resolution in Wikipedia. While the core idea of utilizing Wikipedia’s structure isn’t entirely novel, the specific feature engineering – combining markup, Freebase data, and careful consideration of pronominal vs. non-pronominal mentions – is well-executed and demonstrably effective. A second, valuable contribution is the WCR dataset, though details are deferred to a non-anonymized version. The experimental results clearly show the limitations of general-purpose coreference systems when applied to Wikipedia and highlight the benefits of a tailored approach. Finally, the integration of the classifier into an existing coreference pipeline and the resulting performance improvement is a positive outcome.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents compelling experimental results demonstrating a substantial improvement (up to 15 F1 points) over strong baselines. The ablation studies, showing the contribution of different feature sets, are particularly insightful.
2. **Practical Approach:** The method leverages readily available information within Wikipedia and Freebase, making it easily replicable and applicable to a large corpus of articles. The avoidance of complex named entity linking pipelines is a smart design choice.
3. **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly defines the task of main concept coreference resolution and justifies its importance within the context of NLP applications utilizing Wikipedia.
4. **Dataset Contribution:** The creation of the WCR dataset, even with details deferred, is a valuable contribution to the community, providing a benchmark for future research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Detail:** The description of the WCR dataset is insufficient. Key details like size, annotation scheme specifics, and selection criteria are missing. This hinders assessment of the dataset's representativeness and potential biases.
2. **Feature Engineering Complexity:** While effective, the feature set is quite extensive. A more thorough analysis of feature importance and potential redundancy would be beneficial. The justification for some feature choices feels somewhat ad-hoc.
3. **Error Analysis Depth:** The error analysis, while present, is limited. A more detailed examination of the types of errors made by the system could reveal areas for further improvement. The examples provided are helpful, but a quantitative analysis of error patterns would be stronger.
4. **Generalizability:** The focus is solely on English Wikipedia. The extent to which the approach generalizes to other languages or other structured knowledge sources is unclear.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Please provide a detailed description of the WCR dataset, including its size, annotation guidelines, and the criteria used for selecting articles.
2. Can you provide a feature importance ranking or analysis to justify the inclusion of all the features used in your model?
3. Could you elaborate on the types of errors the system makes, perhaps categorizing them and providing quantitative data on their frequency?
4. Have you considered evaluating the approach on other languages or knowledge sources beyond English Wikipedia and Freebase?
