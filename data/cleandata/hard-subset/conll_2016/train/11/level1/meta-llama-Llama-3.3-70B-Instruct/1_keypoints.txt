- Paper revisits identifying all mentions of the main concept in a Wikipedia article using a binary classification approach.  
- Uses Wikipedia markup and external KBs (e.g., Freebase) to classify mentions as coreferent or not.  
- Proposes a classifier that outperforms state-of-the-art coreference systems on this task and shows benefits in a full Wikipedia coreference pipeline.

- Contribution: Adapts coreference resolution to Wikipedia’s large, multilingual, and highly structured setting.  
- Contribution: Effectively leverages Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases to improve performance.  
- Contribution: Presents a simple yet powerful classification method achieving state-of-the-art results for main-concept mention identification.

- Strength: Achieves significant improvement over existing coreference systems on the target task.  
- Strength: Demonstrates effective use of Wikipedia markup and external KBs to boost resolution accuracy.  
- Strength: Method is simple, efficient, and easily integrable into existing pipelines.  
- Strength: Authors release datasets and resources to support reproducibility and further research.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to a relatively small dataset that may not represent full Wikipedia diversity.  
- Weakness: No comparison to other Wikipedia-specific coreference systems that might be competitive.  
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on Wikipedia markup and external KBs, which may be unavailable or inaccurate in some cases.

- Question: How will the approach be extended to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?  
- Question: Can the authors provide more details on the released datasets and resources, including size and characteristics?  
- Question: How do the authors plan to mitigate reliance on Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases?
