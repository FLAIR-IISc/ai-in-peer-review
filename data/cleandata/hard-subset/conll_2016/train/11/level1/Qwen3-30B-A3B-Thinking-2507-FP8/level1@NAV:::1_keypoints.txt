- Paper introduces Main Concept Resolution (MCR): identify all mentions of an article's central concept in Wikipedia as a binary classification task using Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes.
- Contribution: Novel task definition addressing poor performance of news-trained coreference systems on Wikipedia.
- Contribution: Feature-rich classifier exploiting links, redirects, and Freebase, achieving 89% F1 (13–15 pts above baselines).
- Contribution: Integration into a coreference pipeline yields a 4-point overall F1 improvement.
- Strength: Identifies a meaningful and practical research gap for NLP applications using Wikipedia.
- Strength: Well-justified feature engineering that leverages Wikipedia’s structural elements unavailable in generic text.
- Strength: Robust experimental gains over multiple baselines and demonstrated practical utility in a pipeline.
- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 documents, 60k tokens, 7k mentions), limiting statistical significance and generalizability.
- Weakness: Baselines are overly simplistic; lacks comparison to stronger, modern approaches.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of limitations and failure cases beyond a few examples.
- Question: How will the approach scale to the full Wikipedia corpus (millions of articles)?
- Question: Has the method been evaluated on non-English Wikipedia articles?
