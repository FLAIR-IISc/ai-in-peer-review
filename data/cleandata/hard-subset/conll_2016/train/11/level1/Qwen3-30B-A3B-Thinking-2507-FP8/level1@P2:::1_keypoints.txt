- Paper addresses main-concept (MC) resolution in Wikipedia using a classifier with Wikipedia-specific features (markup, links, Freebase).
- Identifies that coreference systems trained on news degrade substantially on Wikipedia.
- Introduces WCR3, a testbed/dataset for Wikipedia main-concept resolution.
- Shows Wikipedia-specific features yield a 13–15 point F1 improvement over baselines.
- Empirically demonstrates the problem is meaningful: state-of-the-art coref F1 drops from ~60–63% (OntoNotes) to 46–51% (WCR).
- WCR3 is carefully constructed (30 articles, ~60k tokens, 7k non-singleton mentions, 1.8k MC mentions) and will be shared.
- Feature engineering effectively leverages Wikipedia markup, internal links, and Freebase attributes for large gains.
- WCR3 is very small, limiting robustness and generalizability of results despite 10-fold CV.
- The task is narrowly defined (MC resolution) and not clearly justified versus full coreference resolution.
- Baselines are simplistic heuristics, so reported gains may partly reflect weak comparisons.
- Question: How does the small dataset size affect generalizability, and what is the dataset’s diversity (topic, length, link density)?
- Question: Why does Lee et al.’s rule-based system outperform ML systems on WCR—because of Wikipedia structure or other reasons?
- Question: Please provide concrete examples showing how integrating the MC classifier improves full coreference beyond a 4-point CoNLL F1 boost.
- Question: Which NLP applications concretely benefit more from MC resolution than from full coreference?
