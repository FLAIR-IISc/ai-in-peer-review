- Dataset (WCR3): Introduces a well-structured 30-article, 60k-token dataset for Main Concept Resolution (MCR) as a benchmark for Wikipedia coreference.

- Classifier: Proposes a feature-rich binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase to identify main-concept mentions.

- Integration: Integrates the classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++), improving overall coreference performance on Wikipedia.

- Gap identification: Shows that news-trained coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia (10–15 F1 drop), motivating the MCR task.

- Dataset quality: WCR3 follows OntoNotes guidelines, has topic diversity and link-density justification, and is publicly released.

- Feature engineering: Comprehensive, with distinction between pronominal and non-pronominal features and incremental ablation showing each feature family's value.

- Strong performance: Classifier achieves ~89% F1 for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions, outperforming baselines by ~13–15 F1.

- Practical impact: Dcoref++ yields a ~4-point CoNLL F1 improvement, demonstrating real-world applicability.

- Experimental rigor: Thorough evaluation across MUC/B3/CEAF/CoNLL metrics, multiple baselines, and ablation studies.

- Limited novelty: Task overlaps prior work (Nakayama 2008; Nguyen et al. 2007), so technical novelty over past studies is unclear.

- Small dataset: WCR3’s small size (60k tokens) limits statistical power and raises generalization concerns to larger Wikipedia corpora.

- Missing comparisons: Lacks comparison to Wikipedia-specific CR methods that exploit anchors/redirects and other internal structures.

- Underanalyzed finding: Notes rule-based Dcoref outperforms statistical systems on Wikipedia but does not deeply analyze why.

- Limited limitations discussion: Fails to address applicability to non-English Wikipedia, varied article structures, or cases with missing Freebase data.

- Question — rule-based vs statistical: Request for deeper analysis of why rule-based Dcoref outperforms statistical systems on Wikipedia and implications for CR design.

- Question — generalization: Asks how results will generalize to larger Wikipedia corpora and what validation steps are needed.

- Question — Freebase reliance: Asks how the approach performs without Freebase or when Freebase lacks relevant information.

- Question — domain adaptation comparison: Requests comparison to other domain-adaptation techniques for CR on Wikipedia and feature-family contributions to gains.

- Question — multi-concept articles: Asks how the method handles Wikipedia articles describing multiple related concepts (e.g., organizations with branches).
