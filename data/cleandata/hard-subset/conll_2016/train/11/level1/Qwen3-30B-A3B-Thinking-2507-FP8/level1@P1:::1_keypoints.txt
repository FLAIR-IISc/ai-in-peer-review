- Standard coreference systems trained on news perform poorly on Wikipedia (F1 drops from ~60 to ~50).
- Contribution: a new WCR dataset (30 Wikipedia articles, ~60k tokens) annotated for main concept resolution.
- Contribution: a binary classifier for identifying mentions of the main concept using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.
- Contribution: integrating the MC classifier into a CR pipeline (Dcoref++) yields a ~4-point F1 improvement on Wikipedia.
- Strength: clearly identifies an important gap—Wikipedia differs from news and needs domain-specific CR solutions.
- Strength: carefully engineered Wikipedia-specific features (title, inferred type, links, name variants) that substantially improve performance.
- Strength: demonstrates practical utility by improving end-to-end coreference when integrated into an existing pipeline.
- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 articles, 60k tokens), limiting statistical power and generalizability.
- Weakness: task scope is narrow—focuses only on the single main concept rather than full coreference chains.
- Weakness: lacks analysis of why standard CR systems fail on Wikipedia (genre, annotation, or other causes).
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other state-of-the-art CR systems, weakening claims of superiority.
- Question: How were the 30 WCR articles selected and how representative/diverse are they?
- Question: How would the approach generalize to non-English Wikipedias and what challenges would arise?
- Question: How does the method handle articles where the title does not clearly represent a single main concept?
- Question: Does integrating the MC classifier affect performance on standard CR datasets (e.g., OntoNotes) or other genres?
