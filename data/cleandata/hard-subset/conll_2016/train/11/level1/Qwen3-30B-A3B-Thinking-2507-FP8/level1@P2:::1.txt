

# Review for "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper addresses a specific subtask of coreference resolution: identifying all mentions of the main concept (MC) in Wikipedia articles. The authors propose a classifier that uses Wikipedia-specific features (markup, internal links, Freebase attributes) to distinguish MC mentions from other mentions. The main contributions, as I see them, are:

1. Identification of a significant gap in the literature: coreference resolution systems trained on news text perform poorly on Wikipedia, a widely used NLP resource, and the authors provide evidence for this.
2. Creation of a well-structured testbed (WCR3 dataset) specifically for evaluating systems on Wikipedia main concept resolution.
3. Demonstration that Wikipedia-specific features significantly improve performance on this specific task, with a 13-15 point F1 improvement over baselines.

## Strengths

1. **Addressing a meaningful gap in the literature**: The paper makes a compelling case that Wikipedia, a crucial resource for NLP, has been largely overlooked for coreference resolution research. The authors provide strong empirical evidence that state-of-the-art coreference systems (Dcoref, Scoref, etc.) perform dramatically worse on Wikipedia (F1 scores dropping from ~60-63% on OntoNotes to 46-51% on WCR). This is a significant contribution as it identifies a clear need for research on adapting coreference resolution to this important text genre.

2. **Well-constructed testbed with clear evaluation**: The WCR3 dataset is carefully constructed with 30 Wikipedia articles annotated using OntoNotes guidelines, containing 7000 non-singleton mentions (1800 referring to the MC). The authors provide detailed evaluation metrics across multiple baselines, demonstrating clear performance improvements (89% F1 for their approach vs. 75-84% for baselines). The dataset is a valuable resource for future research in this area, and the authors plan to share it.

3. **Effective use of Wikipedia-specific features**: The authors' approach of leveraging Wikipedia's markup structure (internal links, redirects) and Freebase attributes as features is novel and well-executed. The paper demonstrates that these features significantly improve performance (Table 2 shows a 15-point F1 improvement when adding all features to the simplest model). The feature engineering is thoughtful and directly addresses the unique characteristics of Wikipedia text.

## Weaknesses

1. **Insufficient dataset size for robust evaluation**: The WCR3 dataset is very small (30 articles, ~60k tokens, 7000 non-singleton mentions). This is a significant limitation for a machine learning approach that requires sufficient data for training and evaluation. The authors acknowledge this ("WCR is far smaller than the OntoNotes one") but don't adequately address how this affects the reliability of their results. The 10-fold cross-validation is a good attempt to mitigate this, but the small size makes it difficult to draw strong generalizations.

2. **The task is narrowly defined and not fully justified**: The paper focuses on "main concept resolution" rather than full coreference resolution, but the paper's title and abstract suggest it's about coreference resolution in Wikipedia. The authors don't clearly articulate why this specific subtask (identifying MC mentions) is more important or relevant than full coreference resolution. The paper states this task is useful for information extraction (citing Nguyen et al., 2007), but doesn't demonstrate broader applicability or provide convincing justification for why this narrow task should be the focus.

3. **Weak baselines and comparison**: The baselines (B1-B4) are simplistic heuristics based on coreference system outputs, not state-of-the-art approaches specifically designed for Wikipedia. The paper claims "drastically outperforms fair baselines," but the baselines are not truly competitive. For example, B4 (which aggregates chains containing a substring of the MC title) already achieves 75.77 F1, while the authors' approach achieves 88.79 F1. This gap seems more attributable to the simplicity of the baselines than to the novelty of the authors' approach.

## Questions to Authors

1. How does the small dataset size (30 articles) impact the generalizability of your results? Could you provide more details on the diversity of the Wikipedia articles in terms of topic, length, and internal link density?

2. The paper states that "the rule-based system of (Lee et al., 2013) works better than the machine-learning based systems on the WCR dataset." Could you elaborate on why this might be the case? Is it because Wikipedia text has more structured information that aligns better with rule-based approaches, or is there another factor?

3. The paper mentions that "the integration of our classifier into the state-of-the-art rule-based coreference system of Lee et al. (2013) improves the detection of coreference chains." Could you provide more specific examples of how this improvement manifests in the full coreference resolution pipeline, beyond just the CoNLL F1 score increase of 4 points?

4. The authors state that "the main concept resolution task is a prerequisite for many NLP tasks." Could you provide concrete examples of NLP applications where this specific task (identifying MC mentions) is more valuable than full coreference resolution?
