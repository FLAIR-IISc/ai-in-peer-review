- Task/contribution: Introduces main-concept (MC) resolution in Wikipedia and presents a new WCR dataset, a classifier using Wikipedia markup/Freebase features, and integration into a rule-based CR system.

- Dataset: WCR contains only 30 articles (~60k tokens), much smaller than standard CR corpora.

- Performance: Classifier achieves ~89% F1 on pronominal and non-pronominal mentions, a 13–15 point absolute gain over the best baselines.

- Downstream impact: Integrating the classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++) yields a ~4-point absolute improvement on CoNLL F1.

- Strength — problem identification: Correctly highlights that state-of-the-art CR systems trained on news perform poorly on Wikipedia, with up to 17-point F1 drops.

- Strength — features: Clever exploitation of Wikipedia markup, internal links, redirects, and Freebase avoids error propagation from cascaded pipelines.

- Strength — feature engineering: Thoughtful distinctions between pronominal and non-pronominal mentions and careful preprocessing strengthen the approach.

- Weakness — dataset size: The tiny WCR dataset severely limits statistical significance and representativeness of results.

- Weakness — narrow task: MC resolution is a specific subtask and the paper does not show generalizability to full CR or other genres.

- Weakness — weak baselines: Baselines are simplistic heuristics and omit stronger domain-adaptation or sophisticated CR comparisons, possibly inflating gains.

- Weakness — error analysis: Lacks systematic error analysis across article types, mention types, or features beyond a few examples.

- Questions: How does the approach scale to much larger Wikipedias (1,000+ articles), what is the broader impact on tasks like KB population or QA, and how would domain-adaptation/transfer methods compare?
