- New WCR dataset for coreference on Wikipedia showing standard systems underperform on this genre.  
- Framed Main Concept (MC) resolution as binary classification with two SVMs (pronominal and non-pronominal) using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.  
- Classifier yields large gains (+13 F1) over strong baselines.  
- Integrating the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref improves full coreference (~+3.3 CoNLL F1).  
- Clear motivation and problem definition: MC framing is practical and highlights Wikipedia-specific challenges.  
- Strong empirical evaluation including ablation studies and an extrinsic test demonstrating utility in a full pipeline.  
- Clever exploitation of Wikipedia structure (internal links, redirects, Freebase) to obtain high-quality semantic features and avoid noisy entity linking.  
- Commitment to releasing the WCR dataset and a dump of identified MC mentions for English Wikipedia.  
- Novelty lies mainly in engineering and feature design rather than new ML methods.  
- WCR dataset is very small (30 articles), limiting generalizability and possibly vulnerable for less-covered Freebase concepts.  
- Baselines are weaker than ideal; missing a direct in-domain trained coreference baseline without Wikipedia-specific features.  
- Minor clarity/reproducibility issues: missing Table 3 and ambiguous description of the Dcoref++ sieve merge behavior.
