- Proposes a feature-driven SVM classifier for Main Concept Resolution (MCR) in Wikipedia, exploiting Wikipedia markup and Freebase, and outperforms strong baselines.  
- Introduces WCR, a 30-document annotated Wikipedia corpus, and shows existing CR systems perform poorly on this genre.  
- Demonstrates that adding the MCR classifier as a high-precision sieve to Dcoref improves full coreference performance (+4 F1 on WCR).  
- Strong empirical results: MCR classifier reaches 88.8% F1 and yields +13 F1 over best baselines, with thorough ablations and error analysis.  
- Effective use of Wikipedia hyperlinks to access Freebase attributes, avoiding a noisy full entity-linking pipeline.  
- Well-motivated problem focus: MCR is tractable and valuable for downstream IE, RE, and QA tasks.  
- Provides a useful benchmark showing a 10–17 F1 drop for SOTA CR systems on Wikipedia vs. OntoNotes, and that rule-based Dcoref can outperform learning-based systems here.  
- Methodological novelty is limited: relies on classic SVM and heavy hand-crafted features rather than end-to-end neural models.  
- Heavy dependence on Freebase (now defunct) threatens long-term reproducibility; no discussion of migration to Wikidata.  
- Small dataset (30 docs) raises concerns about generalization across diverse Wikipedia articles; larger-scale evaluation needed.  
- Integration into Dcoref is narrow: unclear why not integrated into learning-based systems like Scoref, and the hard sieve constraint may hurt recall in ambiguous cases.  
- Question: Can the MCR classifier be integrated as features or constraints into learning-based systems like Scoref, and would that yield similar or greater gains?  
- Question: Have you considered migrating the feature extraction from Freebase to Wikidata, and do you expect comparable feature quality?  
- Question: Can you provide examples of noisy redirects that harm precision in the name-variants feature and suggest filtering strategies to mitigate this?
