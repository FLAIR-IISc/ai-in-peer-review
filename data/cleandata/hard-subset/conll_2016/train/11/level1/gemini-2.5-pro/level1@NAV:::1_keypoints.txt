- Paper frames Main Concept Resolution (MCR) for Wikipedia as binary classification and trains pronominal/non-pronominal SVMs using Wikipedia markup/links and Freebase, achieving ~89% F1.
- Releases and analyzes the WCR dataset (30 documents) to show general-domain coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia.
- Integrates the MCR classifier as a high-precision sieve into a rule-based coreference pipeline, improving downstream F1 by ~4 points.
- Strong empirical gains: over 13-point absolute F1 improvement vs. baselines, with convincing ablation studies showing the importance of Wikipedia-specific features.
- Clear problem motivation: evaluation on WCR highlights a performance gap and justifies a Wikipedia-specific approach.
- Demonstrated practical utility: the MCR model improves full coreference resolution when integrated into Dcoref.
- Dataset limitations: WCR is small (30 docs) and currently anonymized/inaccessible, raising reproducibility and generalizability concerns.
- Dependency on deprecated Freebase with no discussed migration path to a maintained KG (e.g., Wikidata), limiting long-term viability.
- Methodological datedness: reliance on feature-engineered SVMs without discussion of integrating features into modern end-to-end neural coreference models.
- Question: Have you considered adapting feature extraction to Wikidata and what challenges would that entail?
- Question: What was the sampling strategy for the 30 WCR documents to address generalizability concerns?
- Question: Could high-precision MCR predictions be used to improve statistical/neural systems (e.g., as hard constraints or features for Scoref)?
