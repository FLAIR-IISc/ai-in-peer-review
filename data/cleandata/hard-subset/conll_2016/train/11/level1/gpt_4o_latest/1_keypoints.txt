- Paper tackles identifying all mentions of the main concept (MC) in Wikipedia articles, a neglected coreference resolution task.  
- Proposes a binary classification approach using Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived features for mention classification.  
- Introduces WCR, a new dataset for evaluating coreference on Wikipedia texts.  
- Classifier achieves large gains (≈13 F1 points) over state-of-the-art CR baselines.  
- Integrating the classifier into the Dcoref pipeline improves overall CR performance (≈4 CoNLL F1 points).  
- Uses rich features from Wikipedia markup, Freebase attributes, semantic types, and positional information.  
- Demonstrates particular improvement on pronominal and non-pronominal MC mentions, backed by ablation studies.  
- Leverages external knowledge effectively while avoiding some named-entity linking cascade errors.  
- Shows practical utility by enhancing an established rule-based CR system.  
- Approach may not generalize well beyond Wikipedia due to reliance on wiki-specific markup and Freebase.  
- Heavy reliance on hand-crafted features raises scalability and adaptation concerns for other domains.  
- Evaluation centers on MC identification and its effect on Dcoref; broader CR implications are underexplored.  
- Raises questions about generalization to non-wiki genres, dependence on the now-unmaintained Freebase, and extending to secondary concepts.  
- Overall positive contribution (novel task, dataset, performance gains) tempered by generalizability and feature-engineering limitations.
