The paper proposes a Wikipedia-focused reformulation of coreference: identify the chain corresponding to the entity the article is about (the “main concept,” MC). The authors annotate 30 articles with complete coreference, with roughly 25% of all mentions belonging to the MC. They evaluate simple baselines and a supervised classifier that outperforms them, and they integrate this classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system, yielding substantial gains over state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia.

Overall assessment: This is a well-motivated and timely task with clear practical value for information extraction. Unlike work that defines a niche evaluation to showcase a system tweak, this setting is genuinely important, and standard systems’ poor performance is informative rather than contrived. The task also offers a natural bridge between coreference and entity linking.

On the task: MC resolution provides a useful discourse anchor for coreference and aligns closely with IE scenarios where a document primarily concerns a single entity (e.g., biographies, briefings, event reports, clinical narratives). Standard coreference includes many mentions that are often irrelevant for downstream IE (e.g., generic mentions). By contrast, the MC chain contains the mentions that matter most. Wikipedia is an especially promising domain for this work: it enables leveraging structured knowledge (e.g., links, categories, KB attributes) in ways that are harder to exploit broadly, making it a fertile testbed for methods that could eventually strengthen general coreference. Unlike other “slices” of coreference (e.g., Winograd schemas), this work moves overall coreference metrics in a domain of broad interest to the ACL community.

On the techniques: The methods are not the main contribution, but they appear effective. The feature set is sensible; it would be useful to know whether the authors explored conjunctions or feature interactions, which might yield additional gains. The paper should also clearly state early on that the primary MC resolution model is a binary classifier; as written, this is implicit and the learning setup is underspecified during the feature description.

Methodological observations: While the MC notion provides a strong anchor, the results suggest that substantive effort is still needed to surpass baselines, especially for non-pronominal mentions. This is encouraging: it indicates that the task is nontrivial and that gains likely come from meaningful modeling rather than just exploiting priors.

Minor comments and presentation:
- Organization: Consider introducing the dataset immediately after Related Work so that Baselines and Approach can be grounded in concrete statistics and examples.
- Terminology/citations: When referring to Dcoref and Scoref, cite the corresponding Stanford coreference papers or explicitly state that these are Stanford’s rule-based systems; many readers may not recognize the abbreviations.
- “Candidate list” terminology: The phrase “candidate list” is vague in the sentence “We leverage the hyperlink structure... we look through the candidate list for all mentions that match the surface string of the link.” Please clarify that the candidate list is the set of mentions in the article that could be assigned to the MC chain. The intended mechanism—importing shallow semantic attributes from hyperlinks (e.g., if a mention links to a page whose Freebase entry indicates female, mark that mention as female)—is useful; clearer terminology would help.
- Consistency between sections: Section 6.1 states that you “consider the union of WCR mentions and all mentions predicted by the method described in (Raghunathan et al., 2010).” Section 4.1, however, seems to suggest these sets align. Please clarify where additional WCR mentions arise and how the union differs from the set derived via Raghunathan et al. (2010).

Strengths:
- Clear, practically motivated task tightly connected to IE needs.
- Demonstrated improvements over strong baselines and state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia.
- Potential to catalyze deeper integration between coreference and entity linking, and to encourage knowledge-aware coreference methods.

Weaknesses and opportunities:
- The modeling section would benefit from a crisper description of the learning setup (binary classifier), an ablation of feature interactions, and a discussion of errors, especially on non-pronominal mentions.
- The dataset is small (30 documents); while sufficient for proof-of-concept, a larger release or a plan for scaling would strengthen the paper’s impact and reproducibility.

Recommendation: Accept. The task is compelling, the empirical improvements are meaningful within an important domain, and the work opens a promising avenue that could re-energize coreference research and connect it more directly to entity-centric IE and linking. Clarifying the learning setup, terminology, and section inconsistencies would further improve the paper.
