- Introduces "main concept" (MC) coreference for Wikipedia: identify the coreference chain corresponding to the article’s main entity.
- Annotated a corpus of 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference; ~25% of mentions refer to the MC.
- Proposes simple baselines and a learned mention-level classifier that outperforms them.
- Integrates the MC classifier into Stanford’s rule-based coreference system, producing substantial gains on Wikipedia.
- Task is well-motivated and practically relevant for IE domains centered on a salient entity (encyclopedias, biographies, clinical notes).
- MC provides a useful discourse anchor and exposes meaningful headroom beyond simple heuristics, especially for non-pronominal mentions.
- Leveraging Wikipedia-specific structured signals (hyperlinks, Freebase) is effective and makes Wikipedia a strong testbed.
- Unlike narrow pronoun-focused tasks, this approach yields tangible improvements on overall coreference metrics in the Wikipedia domain.
- The poor performance of standard systems on MC appears to reveal a substantive gap rather than a contrived task.
- Techniques are straightforward but effective; feature design is sensible and improves over baselines.
- Paper should state early that the core model is a binary mention-level classifier and clarify the decision function/training objective.
- Recommend exploring feature conjunctions/interaction features, which may improve performance on non-pronominal mentions.
- Clarity issues: move dataset description earlier, and explicitly cite/identify Stanford systems when referring to Dcoref and Scoref.
- Define “candidate list” more clearly in the context of hyperlink-derived semantic attributes.
- Resolve discrepancy between Sections 4.1 and 6.1 regarding the union of WCR mentions and Stanford-detected mentions.
- Evaluation is convincing that the MC classifier helps and that integrating it improves Wikipedia coreference; error analysis shows room for improvement, especially for named/nominal mentions.
- Suggested additions: early classifier specification, ablation/sensitivity analyses for feature groups (especially hyperlink features), clearer terminology, and explicit citations for system nicknames.
- Overall: a clean, useful reframing bridging coreference and entity linking with clear empirical benefits; minor exposition and experimental clarifications would strengthen the paper.
