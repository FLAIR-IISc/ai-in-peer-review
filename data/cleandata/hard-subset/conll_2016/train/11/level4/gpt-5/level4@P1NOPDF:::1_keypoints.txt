- Introduces a new task—main concept (MC) resolution on Wikipedia—to identify the coreference chain for an article’s main entity; dataset: 30 annotated documents, ~25% mentions refer to MC.  
- Proposes simple baselines and a binary classifier that outperforms them, and integrates this classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system, yielding substantial gains on Wikipedia.  
- Task is compelling for information extraction and can bridge coreference and entity linking.  
- Reviewer initially skeptical of “new task + tweak” papers but finds this task well-motivated because standard systems fail in a practically important setting.  
- Focusing on MC filters out irrelevant generic mentions and provides a discourse anchor that should help coreference performance.  
- Significant headroom remains, especially on non-pronominal mentions, so the problem is not saturated by the baselines.  
- Working on Wikipedia enables richer background-knowledge features, making it a useful domain/testbed for broader coreference advances.  
- Techniques/features are sensible and effective but are not the main novelty; additional feature conjunctions might help.  
- Paper should state earlier that the primary MC-resolution module is a binary classifier (model is not made explicit soon enough).  
- Organizational suggestion: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work to better motivate baselines and the approach.  
- When referring to Dcoref and Scoref, explicitly cite or state use of the Stanford coreference system for reader clarity.  
- Clarify terminology and procedure around “candidate list” and how hyperlink structure imports semantic attributes from Wikipedia links.  
- Resolve the apparent inconsistency about “union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010)”—explain where additional WCR mentions originate.
