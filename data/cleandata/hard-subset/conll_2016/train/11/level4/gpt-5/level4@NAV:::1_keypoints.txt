- Introduces a Wikipedia-specific coreference task: identify the single coreference chain corresponding to the article’s subject (the “main concept”).
- Annotates 30 Wikipedia articles and finds about 25% of mentions refer to the main concept.
- Proposes simple baselines and a lightweight classifier; the classifier outperforms the baselines.
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system and observes sizable gains over prior state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.
- The task is well-motivated and practical for information extraction, focusing on the central entity and filtering irrelevant/generic mentions.
- Framing the main concept as a discourse anchor is helpful, though there remains notable room for improvement, especially on non-pronominal mentions.
- Operating on Wikipedia enables richer background-knowledge features, making the domain a promising testbed for broader coreference advances.
- Methods are not highly novel but are effective; features are sensible and could benefit from additional feature conjunctions.
- The paper should state earlier that the primary MC-resolution approach is a binary classifier for clarity.
- Organizational suggestion: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work so Baselines can present concrete numbers sooner.
- When referring to Dcoref and Scoref, the paper should cite/clarify these are the Stanford coreference systems.
- Clarify the term “candidate list” (it likely means the article’s set of mentions that could corefer with the MC) and how hyperlink structure enriches mentions.
- Resolve ambiguity about Section 6.1’s “union of WCR mentions and all mentions predicted by (Raghunathan et al., 2010)” versus Section 4.1, which implies they are identical.
