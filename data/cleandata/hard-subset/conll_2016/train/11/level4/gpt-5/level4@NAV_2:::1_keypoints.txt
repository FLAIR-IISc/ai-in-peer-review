- Introduces "main concept resolution" for Wikipedia: identify the article's primary entity; dataset of 30 fully annotated articles with ~25% of mentions referring to the main concept (MC).
- Proposes simple baselines and a classifier that outperforms them; embedding the classifier into the Stanford deterministic coref system yields notable gains on Wikipedia.
- Task is compelling for information extraction and could reconnect coreference work with entity linking; the domain is well-motivated and shows current systems underperform where it matters.
- The MC acts as a discourse anchor that aids coreference, but there remains substantial room for improvement, especially on non-pronominal mentions.
- Using Wikipedia enables richer background knowledge and provides a strong testbed for coreference advances that are harder to validate on general datasets.
- Methods are not novel but are effective; feature design is sensible and additional feature conjunctions/ablations might further improve results.
- The paper should state earlier that the MC resolver is a binary classifier, since the model is under-specified during the featureization description.
- Organizational suggestion: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work so its statistics can be referenced in Baselines and motivate the Approach.
- When referencing Dcoref and Scoref, cite the Stanford papers or explicitly note they are Stanford systems for reader clarity.
- Clarify the ambiguous term "candidate list" (it should mean the set of mentions in the article that could corefer with the MC) and explain how Wikipedia links import semantic attributes.
- Resolve the inconsistency about WCR mentions: explain where the additional WCR mentions in Section 6.1 come from versus the description in Section 4.1.
