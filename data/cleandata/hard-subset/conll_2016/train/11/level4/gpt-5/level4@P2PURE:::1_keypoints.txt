- Introduces “main concept resolution”: identify the coreference chain for the entity an article is about (Wikipedia domain).  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia documents with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a basic classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system and reports substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Task is compelling for information extraction and for bridging coreference with entity linking by focusing on mentions that matter.  
- The main concept provides a useful discourse anchor and Wikipedia enables richer use of external knowledge.  
- The work moves the needle on coreference metrics in a domain of interest to the ACL community, rather than carving off a narrow toy problem.  
- Methods are effective but not the primary contribution; feature set is sensible though more feature conjunctions might help.  
- The paper should state earlier that the primary main-concept system is a binary classifier; the model is underspecified during feature description.  
- Organizational suggestion: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work so Baselines can reference concrete results.  
- Clarify references to Dcoref and Scoref by citing Stanford coreference papers or explicitly naming them as Stanford systems.  
- Clarify the term “candidate list” (the set of mentions in the article) and explain how Wikipedia link structure enriches mention attributes.  
- Resolve the apparent discrepancy between Section 4.1 and 6.1 about the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010).
