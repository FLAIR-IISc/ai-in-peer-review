- Reframes coreference on Wikipedia as "main concept resolution" and builds a small annotated dataset (30 articles) for the task.
- Trains separate binary classifiers for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions using Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes.
- Classifier substantially outperforms coreference-derived baselines and, as a high-precision sieve in Dcoref, improves end-to-end CR on Wikipedia.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated task with clear information-extraction utility for documents centered on a single entity.
- Large empirical gains: ~13–15 absolute F1 over baselines on the main concept task and ~4 CoNLL F1 improvement when integrated into Dcoref.
- Smart use of Wikipedia structure (links, redirects) and Freebase to avoid noisy NEL pipelines and obtain reliable features.
- Bridges coreference and entity-linking communities, encouraging knowledge-aware CR methods.
- Highlights genre transfer issues and shows targeted adaptation can recover performance on Wikipedia.

Weaknesses:
- Limited methodological novelty: mainly a linear SVM with feature engineering and few model innovations.
- Very small dataset (30 articles) raises robustness and generalization concerns across topics, languages, and sparse linking.
- Exposition and clarity issues: binary-task framing, Dcoref/Scoref citations, and "candidate list" definition should be clarified earlier.
- Confusing description of mention extraction vs. union with predicted mentions; unclear what is gold vs. predicted in training/testing.
- Insufficient analysis of error modes (redirect noise, location/organization conflation) and sensitivity to Freebase coverage.
- Evaluation details missing: need article-level cross-validation, variance reporting, and clarity about pronominal baseline heuristics.

Questions for authors:
- Please state upfront that the task is binary classification with separate pronoun/non-pronoun models.
- Are cross-validation folds at the article level, and can you report variance across folds or topics? Plans to expand/release WCR?
- Did you try feature conjunctions or alternative classifiers (logistic, tree-based, neural) and report comparisons?
- Can the pipeline use Wikidata instead of deprecated Freebase, and how sensitive are results to missing links or sparse markup?
- Can you ablate Wikipedia-only vs. Freebase-only feature sets to quantify each source's contribution?
- Did you try integrating the classifier into Scoref or other statistical CR systems, and if not, why?

Additional comments:
- Move the dataset section earlier (after Related Work) to motivate baselines and approach with concrete statistics.
- Define or rename "candidate list" to "candidate mention set" when first used.
- When first mentioning Dcoref/Scoref, state they are Stanford systems and add citations.
- Reconcile and clarify the inconsistency between Sections 4.1 and 6.1 regarding mention extraction and union with gold mentions.
