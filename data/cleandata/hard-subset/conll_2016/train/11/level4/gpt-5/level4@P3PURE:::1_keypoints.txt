- Introduces the new task of main concept (MC) resolution on Wikipedia and annotates 30 articles; ~25% of mentions refer to the MC.  
- Presents simple baselines and a binary classifier that outperforms them and, when integrated into the Stanford rule-based system, yields substantial gains over SOTA on Wikipedia.  
- Task is compelling and practically useful for IE because it targets the entity-centric mentions most relevant to downstream extraction.  
- MC acts as a discourse anchor that can aid coreference, though substantial work remains to improve non-pronominal mention resolution.  
- Doing coreference directly on Wikipedia enables richer background-knowledge use and is a strong testbed bridging coreference and entity linking.  
- The methods are not the primary novelty but are effective; features are sensible and could benefit from additional conjunctions.  
- The paper should explicitly state earlier that the primary MC-resolution model is a binary classifier.  
- Recommend reorganizing to present the dataset immediately after Related Work to better ground baselines and motivate the approach.  
- Clarify terminology and provide citations when referring to Dcoref and Scoref as components of the Stanford coreference system.  
- Define “candidate list” clearly as the set of mentions in the article considered for MC coreference and explain how hyperlink-derived semantic attributes are applied.  
- Resolve and clarify the apparent discrepancy between Section 6.1 and Section 4.1 about the source/union of WCR mentions versus those from Raghunathan et al. (2010).
