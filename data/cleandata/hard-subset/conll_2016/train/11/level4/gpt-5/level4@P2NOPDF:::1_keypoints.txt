- Paper proposes MC (main concept) coreference: identify the chain for the article’s central entity (Wikipedia-focused).
- Authors annotated 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference; ~25% of mentions belong to the MC.
- Evaluated simple baselines and a supervised binary classifier that outperforms them.
- Integrated the classifier into Stanford’s rule-based coreference, yielding substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.
- Task is well-motivated, practically valuable for IE, and bridges coreference with entity linking.
- Wikipedia is a promising domain because of exploitable structured knowledge (links, categories, KB attributes).
- The methods/features are sensible and effective, though modeling is not the main novelty.
- Paper should explicitly state early that the MC model is a binary classifier and clarify the learning setup.
- Authors should report whether they tried feature conjunctions/interactions (potential further gains).
- Results show the task is nontrivial, especially for non-pronominal mentions, indicating real modeling challenges.
- Minor presentation/comments: reorganize to introduce dataset earlier for grounding baselines/approach.
- Clarify terminology/citations (e.g., Dcoref/Scoref as Stanford systems) and the meaning of “candidate list.”
- Explain the mechanism for importing attributes from hyperlinks (e.g., Freebase gender) more clearly.
- Resolve inconsistency between Sections 4.1 and 6.1 about WCR mentions and the union with Raghunathan et al. (2010).
- Weaknesses: need crisper learning description, ablation of feature interactions, and error analysis on non-pronominal mentions.
- Dataset is small (30 docs); scaling or a larger release would improve impact and reproducibility.
- Recommendation: Accept — compelling task, meaningful empirical gains, and promising direction for coreference and IE.
