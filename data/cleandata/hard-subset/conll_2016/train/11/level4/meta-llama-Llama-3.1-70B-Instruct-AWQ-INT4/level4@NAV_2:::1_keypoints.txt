- Task: Introduces "main concept resolution" for Wikipedia — identify the coreference chain for the entity the article is about.  
- Dataset: Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles with all coreference chains; ≈25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Baselines & model: Proposes simple baselines and a basic classifier that outperforms them.  
- System integration: Integrates the classifier into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system and achieves substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Motivation: Task is well-motivated for information extraction where documents center on a single entity (bios, dossiers, clinical records).  
- Impact: Could revive coreference research and bridge coreference with entity linking literature.  
- Not a fishing exercise: Reviewer finds the task genuinely important rather than an artificial domain chosen to show improvements.  
- Discourse anchor: Framing a "main concept" provides a useful discourse anchor for improved coreference.  
- Challenges: Significant remaining overhead to improve beyond baselines, especially for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Knowledge use: Wikipedia domain enables leveraging external knowledge (e.g., hyperlinks, Freebase), which the authors begin to exploit.  
- Techniques: Techniques are effective but not novel or the paper’s strongest aspect.  
- Features: Features are sensible; reviewer suggests exploring additional feature conjunctions and more experimentation.  
- Model clarity: The paper should state earlier that the primary MC resolution system is a binary classifier — model not defined early enough.  
- Terminology: "Candidate list" is unclear and should be explicitly defined as the set of mentions in the article that could corefer with the MC.  
- Citations: When mentioning Dcoref/Scoref, cite Stanford coreference papers or clarify those names for unfamiliar readers.  
- Section inconsistency: Possible discrepancy between Section 4.1 and 6.1 about whether WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. mentions are the same or different.  
- Organization suggestion: Move dataset description earlier (after Related Work) so baselines/results can be presented with concrete context.  
- Questions for authors: Request details on feature conjunction experiments, clarification on candidate list/WCR mention extraction, and discussion of real-world IE applications.
