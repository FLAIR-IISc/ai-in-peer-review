- Proposes a novel "main concept resolution" task on Wikipedia: identify the coreference chain for the article's primary entity.  
- Annotates 30 Wikipedia documents with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.  
- Describes simple baselines and a basic classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system, yielding substantial gains over state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia.  
- Task is well-motivated, bridges coreference and entity linking, and addresses a real shortcoming of standard systems in an important setting.  
- The focus on the primary entity is useful for information extraction and avoids many irrelevant/generic mentions.  
- The Wikipedia domain is a promising testbed for research that could improve coreference more broadly.  
- Results show promise but there is room for improvement, especially for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Techniques and features are sensible but not the paper’s strongest contribution; feature conjunctions might improve performance.  
- The authors should state earlier that the primary MC resolution system is a binary classifier.  
- Paper organization could be improved: introduce the dataset sooner (after Related Work) so baselines/results are more concrete.  
- When referencing Dcoref and Scoref, cite or clarify these as the Stanford coreference system for readers unfamiliar with the terms.  
- Clarify the term "candidate list" (it likely means the set of mentions in the article) in the hyperlink/mention-matching description.  
- Resolve the apparent discrepancy between Sections 6.1 and 4.1 about whether WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) are the same or unions, and where extra WCR mentions come from.
