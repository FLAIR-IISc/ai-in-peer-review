**Summary of the Paper**

The authors present a new version of the coreference task tailored to Wikipedia, where the goal is to identify the coreference chain specifically corresponding to the entity that the Wikipedia article is about. They annotate 30 documents with all coreference chains, of which roughly 25% of the mentions refer to the "main concept" of the article. They then describe some simple baselines and a basic classifier which outperforms these. Moreover, they integrate their classifier into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system and see substantial benefit over all state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Main Concept Resolution Task**: The authors propose a new task, Main Concept Resolution, which is an intriguing task from an Information Extraction (IE) perspective. This task has the potential to revitalize and shake up coreference research and bridge the gap between coreference literature and entity linking literature.
2. **Effective Classifier**: The authors develop a simple yet effective classifier that accurately identifies all the mentions that co-refer to the concept being described in a Wikipedia article. Their approach is composed of a preprocessor and a feature extractor that compares the representations of each mention and the main concept.
3. **Improved Performance**: The authors show that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art generic coreference resolution engines on this task. They also demonstrate that the integration of their classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system improves the detection of coreference chains in Wikipedia articles.

**Strengths**

1. **Interesting Twist on Coreference**: The authors propose an interesting twist on coreference that makes good sense from an IE perspective. This task has the potential to revitalize and shake up coreference research.
2. **Effective Use of Knowledge**: The authors illustrate the effective use of knowledge in coreference, particularly in the Wikipedia domain.
3. **Improved Performance**: The authors show that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art generic coreference resolution engines on this task.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Techniques**: The techniques used in the paper are not particularly novel or strong. The features seem pretty sensible, but it's unclear whether the authors did any experimentation with additional conjunctions of these features.
2. **Model Definition**: The authors should state earlier in the work that their primary MC resolution system is a binary classifier. This is not explicitly stated early enough, and the model is left undefined throughout the description of featurization.
3. **Minor Details**: There are some minor details that need to be clarified, such as the use of the term "candidate list" and the extraction of additional WCR mentions.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Did the authors experiment with additional conjunctions of features to improve the performance of their classifier?
2. Can the authors provide more details on how they extracted the additional WCR mentions?
3. How do the authors plan to extend their work to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?

**Additional Comments**

1. The organization of the paper could be improved by introducing the dataset immediately after "Related Works".
2. The authors should make it clear that the "candidate list" is the set of mentions in the article that are possible candidates for being coreferent with the MC.
3. The authors should provide more details on the Stanford papers or make it clear that it's the Stanford coreference system (many will be unfamiliar with the Dcoref/Scoref names).
