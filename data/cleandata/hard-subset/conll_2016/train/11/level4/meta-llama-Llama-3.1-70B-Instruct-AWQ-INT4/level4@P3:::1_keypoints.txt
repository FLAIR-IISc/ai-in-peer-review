- New task: Main Concept Resolution (identify coreference chain for the entity an article is about) applied to Wikipedia.
- Dataset: 30 annotated Wikipedia articles with all coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.
- Methods: Simple baselines and a binary classifier (preprocessor + feature extractor comparing mention vs. main concept).
- Integration: Classifier integrated into the Stanford rule-based coreference system yields substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.
- Contribution: Proposes an IE-relevant twist on coreference that can bridge coreference and entity linking research.
- Contribution: Presents a simple yet effective classifier that accurately identifies mentions coreferent with the main concept.
- Contribution: Demonstrates improved performance versus generic coreference engines and benefits from integration into an existing system.
- Strength: Interesting and potentially influential task formulation for coreference research.
- Strength: Effective use of Wikipedia knowledge/features in coreference resolution.
- Strength: Empirical improvement over prior systems on the Wikipedia-focused task.
- Weakness: Techniques and features are sensible but not particularly novel or strongly engineered.
- Weakness: Paper fails to state early that the primary MC resolution model is a binary classifier.
- Weakness: Minor clarifications needed (e.g., definition of “candidate list”, extraction of additional WCR mentions).
- Question: Did the authors try feature conjunctions or other feature combinations to boost classifier performance?
- Question: Please provide details on how additional WCR mentions were extracted.
- Question: How will the approach be extended to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?
- Comment: Reorganize paper to introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work.
- Comment: Clearly define “candidate list” as the set of mentions that could be coreferent with the main concept.
- Comment: Clarify references to the Stanford coreference system (explicitly name Dcoref/Scoref vs. “Stanford coreference system”).
