- Introduces "main concept" coreference task for Wikipedia: identify the coreference chain corresponding to the article's subject.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a basic binary classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system and achieves substantial improvements over state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia.  
- Task is well-motivated for information extraction and bridges coreference and entity linking research.  
- Domain (Wikipedia) is a useful testbed that enables use of external knowledge and may stimulate robust coreference research.  
- The task reduces noise from generic/irrelevant mentions, focusing evaluation on mentions that matter for IE.  
- Techniques/features are sensible and effective but not especially novel or complex.  
- There appears to be remaining difficulty improving beyond baselines, especially for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Unclear whether the authors tried feature conjunctions or more extensive feature engineering.  
- The paper fails to state early enough that the primary MC resolution model is a binary classifier; the model is not well-defined during feature discussion.  
- Reviewer finds the task impactful for Wikipedia-domain coreference metrics and overall ACL interest.  
- Questions for authors: report experiments with feature conjunctions; justify choosing a binary classifier; detail how the classifier was integrated into the Stanford system.
