- Introduces "main concept resolution": a Wikipedia-tailored coreference task focusing on the article’s primary entity, motivated by information extraction needs.
- Annotated 30 documents; about 25% of mentions refer to the main concept, indicating strong impact potential.
- Proposes simple baselines and a binary classifier that outperforms them.
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system, yielding substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.
- Positions the task as a bridge between coreference and entity linking research.
- Task is well-motivated and addresses real-world limitations of standard coreference systems.
- The "main concept" serves as a useful discourse anchor, but improving beyond baselines is costly, especially for non-pronominal mentions.
- Using Wikipedia enables incorporation of background knowledge and interesting future directions.
- Techniques and features are sensible and effective but not particularly novel.
- Unclear whether feature conjunctions were explored; more experimentation may be needed.
- The paper should state earlier that the primary system is a binary classifier.
- Recommend reorganizing to present the dataset immediately after Related Work to better motivate baselines and approach.
- Need to define the term "candidate list" clearly.
- Clarify in Section 6.1 how WCR mentions relate to mentions predicted by Raghunathan et al. (2010).
- Overall: interesting, well-motivated work with potential to revitalize coreference research despite limited methodological novelty.
