- Introduces a novel "main concept resolution" task for Wikipedia: identify the coreference chain corresponding to the article's central entity.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia documents with all coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a basic classifier that outperforms them.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford coreference system and shows significant improvements over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Task is an intriguing, useful variation linking coreference and entity linking, with potential to revive research in both areas.  
- Main concept resolution is valuable for information extraction and applicable to documents like biographies, clinical records, and event briefings.  
- The task helps filter irrelevant mentions and provides a useful discourse anchor for coreference.  
- Current methods perform well but need improvement, particularly on non-pronominal mentions.  
- Working directly on Wikipedia enables incorporation of external knowledge, which is promising.  
- Features used are sensible but experiments with feature conjunctions could strengthen results.  
- Authors should state earlier that the primary system is a binary classifier.  
- Minor revisions: move dataset description after Related Work, clarify Stanford system citations, define "candidate list" as potential mentions, and ensure method description (Sec. 6.1) aligns with implications in Sec. 4.1.
