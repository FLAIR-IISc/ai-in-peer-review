- Proposes a novel "main concept resolution" task focused on identifying the coreference chain for the Wikipedia article’s main entity.  
- Introduces a small annotated dataset of 30 Wikipedia documents with all coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a binary classifier that outperforms them and improves results when integrated into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system.  
- Task is valuable for information extraction because it isolates mentions that matter (avoids many generic/irrelevant mentions).  
- Could bridge coreference and entity linking research and serve as a useful Wikipedia testbed for knowledge-rich coreference ideas.  
- The notion of a "main concept" provides a useful discourse anchor, but substantial work remains to improve beyond baselines, especially for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Techniques are effective but not especially novel; features are sensible but could benefit from additional conjunctions or feature engineering.  
- The primary MC resolution system is a binary classifier but this is not stated early enough and the model is underdefined during feature description.  
- Paper organization could be improved (introduce dataset earlier to motivate baselines and approach).  
- Need clearer citations/explanation when referring to Dcoref/Scoref (identify as the Stanford coreference system).  
- Terminology is unclear: clarify that "candidate list" means the set of mentions in the article considered as possible coreferents.  
- Clarify ambiguity about Section 6.1 vs. Section 4.1 regarding the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010).
