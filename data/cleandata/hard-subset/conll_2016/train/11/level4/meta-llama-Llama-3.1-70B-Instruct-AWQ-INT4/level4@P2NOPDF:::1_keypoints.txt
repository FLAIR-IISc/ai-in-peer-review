- Introduces a novel main-concept (MC) coreference task focused on the Wikipedia article’s central entity.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia documents with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a basic binary classifier that outperforms them.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system, yielding substantial gains on Wikipedia.  
- Task is a meaningful and motivating twist on coreference that bridges coreference and entity linking research.  
- Standard coreference systems perform poorly in this setting, highlighting the task’s importance for information extraction.  
- The MC task reduces irrelevant mentions and provides a useful discourse anchor, aiding extraction-focused applications.  
- Domain (Wikipedia) is a promising testbed for methods that could more broadly improve coreference.  
- Techniques are effective but not a strong contribution; feature engineering could be deeper (e.g., feature conjunctions).  
- The paper should state earlier that the main MC resolution model is a binary classifier; model details are under-specified.  
- Organization could be improved: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work to motivate baselines and approach.  
- Clarify references to Dcoref/Scoref as the Stanford coreference system and add proper citations.  
- Clarify the term “candidate list” (it means the set of mentions in the article that could be coreferent with the MC).  
- Resolve ambiguity about Section 6.1 vs 4.1 regarding the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010).
