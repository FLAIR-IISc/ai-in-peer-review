- Paper summary: introduces “main concept” coreference task on Wikipedia and a 30-document annotated dataset, showing a tailored classifier improves coreference and boosts a rule-based system to state-of-the-art on Wikipedia text.

- Contribution — Novel task: defines and motivates main concept resolution as a practical, focused subproblem of coreference for information extraction.

- Contribution — New dataset: releases WCR, a manually annotated dataset for evaluating main concept resolution.

- Contribution — Improved performance: demonstrates significant gains on Wikipedia by integrating a custom classifier with a strong rule-based coreference system.

- Strength — Task motivation: convincingly argues the task is relevant and useful for real-world IE scenarios (e.g., biographies, reports, clinical records).

- Strength — Dataset quality: provides a focused annotated resource sufficient to show meaningful improvements despite modest size.

- Strength — Impactful results: classifier integration yields substantial performance gains and surpasses prior systems on Wikipedia.

- Strength — Cross-domain connection: work bridges coreference resolution and entity linking, encouraging method exchange between areas.

- Strength — Practical applicability: addresses a common IE bottleneck, making the approach directly useful in applied pipelines.

- Weakness — Feature analysis: lacks detailed ablation or assessment of individual feature importance and feature combinations (e.g., conjunctions).

- Weakness — Classifier description clarity: does not clearly state up front that the main concept resolver is a binary classifier, causing potential confusion.

- Weakness — Ambiguous terminology: terms like “candidate list” are used unclearly, especially in the hyperlink enrichment discussion.

- Weakness — Data discrepancy: possible inconsistency about whether additional mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) were added to WCR; needs clarification.

- Weakness — Limited error analysis: paper gives only cursory error discussion and would benefit from deeper analysis of failure modes.

- Question — Feature conjunctions: did the authors experiment with feature conjunctions, and what were the outcomes?

- Question — Candidate list details: please provide a clearer, more detailed explanation of the “candidate list” in Section 4.1.

- Question — WCR augmentation: clarify whether and how additional mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) were incorporated into the WCR dataset.

- Question — Error analysis: provide a more detailed error analysis describing common error types and likely causes.
