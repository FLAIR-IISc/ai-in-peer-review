The authors present a novel coreference task focused on identifying the coreference chain corresponding to the main entity described in a Wikipedia article. They introduce a dataset of 30 annotated documents with approximately 25% of mentions referring to the “main concept.” They demonstrate that a basic classifier outperforms simple baselines and significantly improves performance when integrated into the Stanford rule-based coreference system, achieving state-of-the-art results on this task.

I believe this paper proposes an interesting and motivating adaptation of coreference resolution, relevant to information extraction. The task addresses a practical need—isolating mentions pertaining to the central entity in a document—and contrasts with previous work that often focuses on improving general coreference performance without specific application in mind. This work feels less like “task-fishing” and more like identifying an important sub-problem where standard systems underperform, particularly in a domain—Wikipedia—of broad interest to the ACL community.

THE TASK: Main concept resolution is a valuable task from an information extraction perspective. It addresses the issue of irrelevant coreference chains in standard coreference by focusing on mentions crucial to understanding the central entity of a document. This has potential applications in areas like biographical document analysis, event summarization, and clinical record processing. The problem also provides a useful discourse anchor for coreference, potentially enabling the development of more robust and targeted systems. 

From a methodological standpoint, the paper provides an interesting testbed for coreference techniques, offering an environment where improvements may be more readily apparent than in general-domain coreference. Unlike some specialized coreference tasks, this work demonstrates a tangible impact on overall coreference metrics within the Wikipedia domain.

THE TECHNIQUES: While the techniques themselves are not groundbreaking, they are effective. The features are sensible and well-motivated, leveraging Wikipedia-specific characteristics and external knowledge from Freebase. However, the paper would benefit from explicitly stating earlier that the primary MC resolution system is a binary classifier. The model's architecture is somewhat obscured in the description of feature engineering. Further exploration of feature conjunctions could also be beneficial, though the authors do not explicitly mention experimenting with this.

MINOR DETAILS:
Organization: Reordering the paper to introduce the dataset immediately after the Related Work section (i.e., making it Section 3) would provide a clearer structure and allow for more immediate motivation of the baselines and proposed approach.
Citations: When referencing Dcoref and Scoref, clarify that these are components of the Stanford coreference system, as many readers may be unfamiliar with these specific names. Explicit citations to the Stanford papers would be helpful.
Terminology: The term “candidate list” is unclear. Clarify that it refers to the set of mentions within the article that are potential coreference candidates for the main concept. Ensure clear language regarding the import of semantic information from Wikipedia’s hyperlink structure.
Data Preparation: Section 6.1 states that the dataset consists of the union of WCR mentions and mentions predicted by Raghunathan et al. (2010). However, Section 4.1 suggests these are the same. Clarify how additional WCR mentions are extracted.
