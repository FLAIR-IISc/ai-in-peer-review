- Introduces "main concept resolution": identify the coreference chain corresponding to the Wikipedia article’s subject.

- Annotated 30 Wikipedia documents; roughly 25% of mentions refer to the article’s main concept.

- Presents simple baselines and a binary classifier that outperforms those baselines.

- Integrating the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system yields substantial gains over prior state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.

- Task is well-motivated for information extraction and focuses on mentions that matter, potentially bridging coreference and entity linking.

- Wikipedia as a domain is a valuable testbed for leveraging external knowledge and for advancing coreference research.

- Techniques are effective but not the paper’s strongest contribution; features are sensible but could be combined more thoroughly.

- Significant room for improvement on non-pronominal mentions.

- Must state earlier that the primary system is a binary classifier; the model is not defined early enough.

- Recommend reorganizing: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work to motivate baselines and approach.

- When referring to Dcoref/Scoref, cite the original Stanford coreference papers or clarify that these are Stanford system components.

- Clarify the term “candidate list” as the set of mentions in the article that could be coreferent with the main concept.

- Clarify Section 6.1 vs 4.1: explain how WCR mentions relate to mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) and how additional WCR mentions are extracted.
