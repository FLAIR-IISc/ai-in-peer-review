- Introduces a novel “main concept” coreference task for Wikipedia, targeting mentions referring to the article’s entity.  
- Presents a new annotation of 30 documents, finding ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Demonstrates a binary classifier that outperforms simple baselines on this task.  
- Integrating the classifier with the Stanford rule-based coreference system yields substantial improvements over SOTA.  
- Task is well-motivated and practically important for information extraction (e.g., biographies, clinical records).  
- Focusing on main-concept mentions provides a useful discourse anchor and a cleaner testbed for coreference improvements.  
- Applying the task to Wikipedia enables incorporation of external knowledge resources.  
- The task avoids being a trivial new benchmark and shows measurable impact on overall coreference metrics in an important domain.  
- Technical contributions are effective but modest; features are sensible and could be combined conjunctively for further gains.  
- The paper should clarify earlier that the main-concept system is a binary classifier to avoid model-definition confusion.  
- Suggest reorganizing to place the dataset description earlier (after Related Works) to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- Cite original Stanford coreference papers when referring to Dcoref and Scoref or explicitly identify them as Stanford components.  
- Clarify the term “candidate list” as the set of mentions in the article that could corefer with the main concept.  
- Resolve the apparent inconsistency about WCR mentions vs. mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) and explain where additional WCR mentions come from.
