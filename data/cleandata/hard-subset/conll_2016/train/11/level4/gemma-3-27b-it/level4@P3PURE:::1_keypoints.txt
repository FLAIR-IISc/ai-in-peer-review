- Introduces "main concept resolution" on Wikipedia: annotate 30 articles, ~25% of mentions refer to the article’s main entity, and integrating the proposed classifier into Stanford coref yields state-of-the-art results.  
- Task is compelling and practically important for information extraction, bridging coreference and entity linking.  
- Framing the main concept as a discourse anchor is useful and the Wikipedia domain is a promising testbed for coreference research.  
- The paper could revitalize coreference work by focusing on mentions that matter for downstream IE, not generic mentions.  
- Techniques are effective but not novel; features are sensible and might benefit from feature conjunctions.  
- The primary model is a binary classifier, but this is not stated early enough and the model is underdefined during feature description.  
- Performance still needs substantial improvement beyond baselines, especially on non-pronominal mentions.  
- Organizational suggestion: present the dataset immediately after Related Work so baselines and motivations are clearer.  
- Clarify references to Dcoref and Scoref (cite or state these are Stanford coreference components).  
- Define "candidate list" clearly as the set of mentions in the article potentially coreferent with the main concept and explain hyperlink-derived semantic enrichment.  
- Resolve inconsistency between Section 6.1 and 4.1 about the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010); explain where additional WCR mentions come from.
