- Introduces a new coreference task: identifying coreference chains for the article’s “main concept” in Wikipedia articles.  
- Provides a new annotation of 30 Wikipedia documents, with ~25% of mentions labeled as relating to the main concept.  
- Shows a basic binary classifier that outperforms simple baselines on this task.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvement to the Stanford rule-based coreference system when the classifier is integrated.  
- The task is well-motivated and addresses a real information-extraction need for resolving references to a document’s primary entity.  
- Focusing on the main concept reduces noise from generic mentions and offers a useful discourse anchor, though non-pronominal mentions still need improvement.  
- Wikipedia is a promising, knowledge-rich testbed for this task and relevant to the ACL community.  
- Techniques used are sensible and effective, though they are not the main novelty; exploring feature conjunctions could yield further gains.  
- The paper should state earlier and more clearly that the primary MC resolution model is a binary classifier.  
- Organization suggestion: move the dataset description after Related Work to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- Clarity suggestion: cite or explicitly name the Stanford system when referencing Dcoref and Scoref.  
- Terminology fix: clarify “candidate list” as the set of mentions in the article potentially coreferent with the main concept.  
- Consistency issue: reconcile Sections 4.1 and 6.1 about whether WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. mentions are identical or unioned, and clarify where extra WCR mentions come from.
