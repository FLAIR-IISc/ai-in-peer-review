- Introduces a novel task: identify mentions of the main concept in a Wikipedia article; dataset of 30 annotated documents with full coreference chains (≈25% of mentions refer to the main entity).

- Presents baselines and a binary classification approach that outperforms them; integrating this classifier into the Stanford coreference system yields state-of-the-art results on Wikipedia coreference.

- Task is well-motivated and useful for information extraction from biographies, reports, and clinical records by focusing on mentions relevant to the core entity.

- Framing coreference around a “main concept” provides a helpful discourse anchor and a promising, focused testbed distinct from broader coreference challenges.

- Wikipedia domain enables incorporation of external knowledge resources (e.g., Freebase) and may produce more robust gains than general coreference settings.

- Methodological gains are real but limited; substantial room remains for improvement, notably on non-pronominal mentions.

- Technical contributions are sensible but not the paper’s strongest element; further feature combinations could yield additional gains.

- The paper should explicitly state earlier that the primary MC resolution model is a binary classifier rather than leaving it implicit.

- Organizational improvements: move dataset description immediately after Related Work (new Section 3) to better motivate baselines and approach.

- Citation/clarity: when referencing Dcoref and Scoref, cite original Stanford coreference papers or explicitly identify these as Stanford components.

- Terminology: clarify “candidate list” (it refers to the set of mentions within the article considered as potential main-concept coreferents) and make the hyperlink/semantic-information usage explanation unambiguous.

- Inconsistency: reconcile Section 6.1 and Section 4.1 regarding whether the system uses the union of WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. (2010) mentions or if they are the same set; explain any additional WCR mentions.
