The authors introduce a novel coreference task focused on Wikipedia, specifically identifying coreference chains relating to the entity a Wikipedia article describes. They present a new annotation of 30 documents, identifying roughly 25% of mentions as referring to this “main concept.” They demonstrate strong performance with a binary classifier, exceeding simple baselines, and achieve substantial improvements over state-of-the-art systems when integrating this classifier with the Stanford rule-based coreference system. This work proposes a compelling refocusing of coreference research with clear relevance to information extraction. It has the potential to stimulate new research directions and bridge connections between coreference and entity linking. While I am often critical of work that defines a new task merely to demonstrate shortcomings of existing systems, this task is inherently motivating: standard coreference systems perform poorly in a practically important setting.

THE TASK: Main concept resolution is an intriguing task from an information extraction perspective. Many documents center on a specific entity—biographical texts, dossiers, clinical records—and extracting information relevant to *that* entity is paramount. Conventional coreference resolution often involves a high proportion of mentions largely irrelevant to most IE applications (e.g., generic references). This task, conversely, focuses on mentions that are demonstrably significant. 

Methodologically, defining a “main concept” provides a valuable discourse anchor for coreference, although considerable effort remains to surpass baseline performance, particularly on non-pronominal mentions. Applying coreference directly to Wikipedia also facilitates the incorporation of knowledge resources, as the authors illustrate. This domain is likely to prove a valuable testbed for improving coreference generally, offering a context where improvements are less diluted by the complexity of broader coreference challenges. 

Importantly, unlike previous work that focused on isolated aspects of coreference (e.g., Winograd schema), this paper delivers a measurable impact on overall coreference evaluation metrics within a domain – Wikipedia – of significant interest to the ACL community.

THE TECHNIQUES: The technical contributions are not the primary strength of this paper, though they are effective. The features employed are sensible, and exploring conjunctions of these features may yield further gains. The authors should clarify earlier in the paper that their main concept resolution system is a binary classifier; this is not immediately apparent and leaves the model definition unclear during the featurization description. 

MINOR DETAILS:

Organization: Consider moving the dataset description immediately after the “Related Works” section (i.e., making it Section 3) to present concrete results in “Baselines” and further motivate the “Approach” section.

When Section 4 refers to Dcoref and Scoref, cite the original Stanford papers or explicitly identify these as components of the Stanford coreference system, as many readers will not recognize these names.

The term “candidate list” is unclear, particularly in the following passage: “We leverage the hyperlink structure of the article in order to enrich the list of mentions with shallow semantic attributes. For each link found within the article under consideration, we look through the candidate list for all mentions that match the surface string of the link.” Please clarify that the “candidate list” refers to the set of mentions within the article that are potential coreferents of the main concept. While readers will likely infer the module’s purpose—to import semantic information from Wikipedia’s link structure (e.g., gender information from Freebase)—clear terminology is essential.

Section 6.1 states, “we consider the union of WCR mentions and all mentions predicted by the method described in (Raghunathan et al., 2010).” However, Section 4.1 implies that these are identical. Please clarify where additional WCR mentions are extracted.
