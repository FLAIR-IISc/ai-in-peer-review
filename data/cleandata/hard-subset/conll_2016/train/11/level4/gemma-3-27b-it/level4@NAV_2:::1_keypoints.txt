- Paper introduces a novel “main concept” coreference task and dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles (~25% of mentions are main-concept mentions).  
- A simple binary classifier outperforms basic baselines and yields state-of-the-art performance when integrated into the Stanford rule-based coreference system.  
- The task is practically motivated and useful for information extraction (biographies, summarization, clinical records) and provides a focused testbed where improvements are more evident than in general coreference.  
- Techniques are effective though not novel; features are sensible and leverage Wikipedia-specific signals and Freebase.  
- The paper should state earlier that the primary MC resolution model is a binary classifier; the model architecture is currently obscured by the feature discussion.  
- Further exploration of feature conjunctions and more detailed feature engineering would strengthen the methodological contribution.  
- Paper organization could improve by moving the dataset description earlier (e.g., Section 3) to motivate baselines and approach.  
- Clarify that Dcoref and Scoref are components of the Stanford coreference system and add explicit citations to the Stanford papers.  
- Define “candidate list” clearly as the set of mentions in the article that could corefer with the main concept and clarify how Wikipedia hyperlink semantics are used.  
- Resolve ambiguity in data preparation: clarify whether WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) are identical or how additional WCR mentions are extracted.
