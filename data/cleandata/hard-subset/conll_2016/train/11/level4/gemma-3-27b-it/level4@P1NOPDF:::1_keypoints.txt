- Introduces a new task: main concept resolution for Wikipedia articles—identify coreference chains corresponding to the article’s central entity.  
- Dataset: 30 Wikipedia documents annotated for all coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the article’s main concept.  
- Baselines and model: simple baselines are presented and a basic binary classifier outperforms them.  
- Integration result: integrating the classifier into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system yields substantial improvements over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Motivation/value: the task is well-motivated for information extraction, bridges coreference and entity linking, and could reinvigorate coreference research.  
- Discourse insight: treating a “main concept” as a discourse anchor is methodologically useful and enables leveraging Wikipedia knowledge.  
- Performance gaps: there remains significant room for improvement, especially on non-pronominal mentions.  
- Methods critique: techniques are effective but not novel or strong; features are sensible and feature conjunctions could help.  
- Clarity issue: the paper should explicitly state earlier that the primary system is a binary classifier.  
- Organization suggestion: move the dataset description immediately after Related Work to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- Citation/terminology: clarify and cite that Dcoref and Scoref refer to the Stanford coreference system and provide relevant references.  
- “Candidate list” ambiguity: clarify that “candidate list” means the set of mentions in the article and explain how hyperlink semantics are imported.  
- Section inconsistency: resolve ambiguity between Section 6.1 and 4.1 about the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010).
