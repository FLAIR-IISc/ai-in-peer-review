- Introduces a novel, valuable task: identifying coreference chains for a Wikipedia article’s main concept; annotated 30 docs with ~25% mentions and a classifier that outperforms baselines and yields SOTA when integrated into the Stanford system.  
- Reviewer finds the task inherently motivating for information extraction and not a contrived domain to expose system weaknesses.  
- Task is practically important for documents centered on a single entity (e.g., biographies, clinical records) where focusing on the primary subject matters.  
- Main concept resolution reduces irrelevant mentions for downstream IE and enables leveraging external Wikipedia knowledge; provides a useful testbed for coreference improvements.  
- The work stands out from other focused coreference efforts by producing significant impact on coreference metrics in the widely studied Wikipedia domain.  
- Technical contributions are effective but not the paper’s main strength; features are sensible and well-motivated.  
- Recommend exploring feature combinations further and clarifying the extent of that exploration.  
- The paper should explicitly state early that the primary MC resolution system is a binary classifier; current presentation leaves the model undefined during featurization.  
- Minor organizational/clarity fixes: move dataset description after Related Work to motivate baselines and approach.  
- In Section 4, clarify or cite that Dcoref and Scoref refer to the Stanford coreference system.  
- Define “candidate list” explicitly as the set of mentions within the article that are potential coreferents of the main concept.  
- Resolve the apparent discrepancy between Section 6.1 (union of WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. mentions) and Section 4.1 (which implies they are identical) by clarifying where additional WCR mentions are extracted.
