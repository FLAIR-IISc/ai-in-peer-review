- Introduces a novel “main concept” coreference task for Wikipedia: identify the coreference chain corresponding to the entity described by the article.
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia documents with full coreference chains and found ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.
- Proposes simple baselines and a basic classifier that outperforms them.
- Integrating their classifier into the Stanford coreference system yields substantial improvements over state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia.
- The task bridges coreference and entity linking and could revitalize research by focusing extraction on a document’s central entity.
- The “main concept” acts as a useful discourse anchor, but performance, especially on non-pronominal mentions, needs improvement beyond the baselines.
- Wikipedia as a domain enables leveraging external knowledge and is a promising testbed, though generalizability and evaluation may be challenging.
- Methods and features are sensible and effective but not highly innovative; unclear whether richer feature conjunctions were explored.
- Minor issues to fix: introduce the dataset earlier, clarify citations for the Stanford system, define “candidate list” terminology, and resolve discrepancy between Sections 4.1 and 6.1 on WCR mention extraction.
- Overall assessment: compelling new task with significant empirical gains and a valuable contribution to the field.
