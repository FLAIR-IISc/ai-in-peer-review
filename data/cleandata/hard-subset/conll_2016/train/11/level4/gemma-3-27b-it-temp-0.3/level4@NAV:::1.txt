The authors introduce a novel coreference task focused on Wikipedia articles, specifically identifying coreference chains relating to the article’s main subject. They present a new annotation of 30 documents, identifying approximately 25% of mentions as referring to the “main concept” of each article. They demonstrate the effectiveness of a basic classifier, outperforming simple baselines, and significantly improve state-of-the-art coreference performance on Wikipedia when integrated with the Stanford rule-based system. 

This work presents a compelling and well-motivated adaptation of the coreference problem. It addresses a practical need in information extraction, where identifying mentions specifically related to a central entity is crucial (e.g., in biographical documents or clinical records). Unlike some task-focused papers, this work doesn’t simply seek a domain where existing systems fail; it highlights a genuinely important scenario where current coreference systems underperform. The task’s focus on the “main concept” provides a useful discourse anchor, and the Wikipedia setting allows for leveraging external knowledge resources. This domain offers a promising testbed for coreference research, potentially enabling more robust improvements than are typically achievable in broader settings. Furthermore, this work impacts coreference evaluation metrics on a domain of significant interest to the ACL community, unlike previous work that focused on isolated subproblems.

The technical contributions, while effective, are not the primary strength of the paper. The features employed are reasonable, and exploring conjunctions of these features could be beneficial. The authors should explicitly state earlier that their core “main concept” (MC) resolution system is a binary classifier, as the model remains undefined during the featurization description. 

**Minor Points:**

*   **Organization:** Consider moving the dataset description to immediately after the “Related Works” section (as a new Section 3) to provide concrete results earlier and better motivate the subsequent sections.
*   **Citations:** When referencing Dcoref and Scoref in Section 4, either cite the relevant Stanford papers or clarify that these refer to the Stanford coreference system, as these names may be unfamiliar to some readers.
*   **Terminology:** The term “candidate list” is unclear. Specifically, clarify that this list refers to the set of mentions within the article potentially coreferent with the MC. While the intention of importing semantic information from Wikipedia’s hyperlink structure is understandable, maintaining clear terminology is important.
*   **Consistency:** Section 6.1 states considering the union of WCR mentions and mentions predicted by (Raghunathan et al., 2010), while Section 4.1 suggests these are identical. Clarify where additional WCR mentions are extracted.
