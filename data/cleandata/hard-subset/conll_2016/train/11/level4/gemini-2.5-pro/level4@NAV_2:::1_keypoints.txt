- Introduces "Main Concept Resolution": identify all mentions referring to the primary entity in a Wikipedia article.  
- New annotated dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions are main-concept mentions.  
- Establishes baselines and a feature-rich classifier that substantially outperforms them; integrating this classifier into a rule-based coreference system yields notable gains.  
- Well-motivated, practically useful task that exposes domain-adaptation limits of newswire-trained coreference systems.  
- Provides a promising testbed to integrate external knowledge and bridge coreference with entity linking research.  
- Strong empirical results: +13–15 F1 on the task and +4 CoNLL F1 when the classifier is added to a full system.  
- Limited methodological novelty: approach is a standard binary SVM with straightforward features.  
- Model description lacks clarity early on—classification framing is not stated upfront, reducing readability.  
- Insufficient detail on feature engineering and no thorough ablation or analysis of feature combinations.  
- Question: what exactly is the "candidate list" in Section 4.1 — all article mentions or another entity collection?  
- Question: clarify the relationship between gold WCR mentions and automatically detected mentions used for training (is WCR a superset or a distinct set?).
