- Introduces a novel "main concept" (MC) coreference task for Wikipedia Articles.  
- Builds a new dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles; MC mentions are ~25% of all mentions.  
- Presents simple baselines and a classifier-based approach that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrates the MC classifier into the Stanford rule-based system, yielding substantial improvements on the Wikipedia dataset.  
- Task is well-motivated for information extraction and focuses on mentions most relevant to downstream IE.  
- The MC provides a natural discourse anchor, making the domain a promising testbed for new coreference techniques.  
- Wikipedia domain allows effective use of external knowledge (e.g., hyperlinks, Freebase) as demonstrated.  
- Technical methods are effective but not the main novelty; feature set is sensible.  
- Additional feature conjunctions may yield further gains; unclear if these were explored.  
- The paper should explicitly state early that the MC resolution model is a binary classifier.  
- Organization could improve by introducing the dataset earlier (after Related Work) to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- Cite the relevant papers for Dcoref and Scoref when referring to the Stanford coreference system.  
- Clarify the term "candidate list" — it refers to all extracted mentions considered for MC coreference.  
- Resolve the apparent contradiction about mention extraction sources (union of WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. vs. implication that Raghunathan et al. is the sole method).
