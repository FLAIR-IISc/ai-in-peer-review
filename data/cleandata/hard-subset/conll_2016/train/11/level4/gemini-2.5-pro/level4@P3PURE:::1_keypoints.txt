- Introduces Main Concept Resolution: identify the coreference chain for the primary entity of a Wikipedia article.  
- New dataset: 30 Wikipedia articles annotated with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.  
- Presents baselines and a classifier-based approach that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrating the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system yields substantial improvements over state-of-the-art on this Wikipedia dataset.  
- Task is well-motivated from an information extraction perspective and bridges coreference and entity linking.  
- Focusing on the main concept reduces irrelevant/generic mentions and provides a useful discourse anchor.  
- Wikipedia domain allows integration of external knowledge and is a promising, controlled testbed for new techniques.  
- The task has broader impact on overall coreference performance in an important domain, unlike very narrow benchmarks.  
- Technical contribution is effective but modest; features are sensible rather than novel.  
- The paper should state much earlier that the primary system is a binary classifier; model architecture is described too late.  
- It is unclear whether additional feature conjunctions were explored; these might yield further gains.  
- Organizational suggestion: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- Authors should cite or clarify Dcoref and Scoref as components of the Stanford coreference system.  
- Define the term "candidate list" explicitly (all mentions in the article considered for coreference with the main concept).  
- Clarify the apparent contradiction between Sections 4.1 and 6.1 regarding whether WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. mentions are identical or a union.
