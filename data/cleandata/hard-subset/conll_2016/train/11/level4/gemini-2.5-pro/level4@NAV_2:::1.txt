### Summary and Contributions

This paper introduces a variation of the coreference resolution task, termed "Main Concept Resolution," specifically for Wikipedia articles. The objective is to identify all mentions that refer to the primary entity described in a given article. The authors contribute a new dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles annotated with full coreference chains, demonstrating that a significant portion of mentions (approximately 25%) pertains to the main concept. They establish several baselines using state-of-the-art systems and propose a feature-based classifier that substantially outperforms them on this task. Finally, they show that integrating their classifier into a leading rule-based coreference system results in significant performance gains on the full coreference task for Wikipedia data.

The main contributions of this work are:
1.  **The introduction and formalization of "Main Concept Resolution"**: a practical and well-motivated sub-task of coreference resolution focused on identifying the most salient entity chain in a document, with clear applications for information extraction.
2.  **A new annotated dataset for Wikipedia coreference**: This resource serves as a valuable benchmark and effectively highlights the significant domain adaptation challenge faced by current state-of-the-art systems.
3.  **An effective, knowledge-rich classification model**: The authors demonstrate that a simple classifier, enhanced with features derived from Wikipedia's structure and linked knowledge bases, can dramatically improve performance on this task and also boost a full coreference system's accuracy in this domain.

### Strengths

1.  **Well-motivated and impactful task definition.** The paper identifies a compelling and highly relevant problem. Rather than simply applying existing methods to a new domain, the work defines a task that focuses on the most crucial entity chain in a document, which is directly useful for many downstream applications. The authors convincingly argue that standard coreference systems, despite their strong performance on newswire, are ill-suited for this important and widely-used setting.
2.  **Potential to stimulate new research directions.** By focusing on a single, anchored entity within a knowledge-rich environment like Wikipedia, this work provides an excellent testbed for exploring the integration of external knowledge into coreference models. This could help bridge the gap between coreference resolution and entity linking research and may lead to new techniques that could generalize to the broader coreference problem.
3.  **Strong and convincing empirical results.** The paper provides a thorough evaluation, clearly demonstrating the shortcomings of multiple state-of-the-art systems on the new dataset. The proposed approach achieves a very large performance gain (+13-15 F1 points) over strong baselines. Crucially, the authors also show that their specialized classifier can be successfully integrated into a full coreference system, leading to a significant improvement (+4 CoNLL F1 points) on the general coreference task, which underscores the practical value of the contribution.

### Weaknesses

1.  **Limited methodological novelty.** The technical approach, while effective, is based on a standard binary SVM classifier with well-chosen but largely straightforward features. The paper's main strength lies in its novel task formulation, dataset, and analysis, rather than in a new machine learning method or model architecture.
2.  **Clarity of the model description.** The paper could be clearer about the proposed model from the outset. The fact that the core system is a binary classifier is not explicitly stated until later in the paper, leaving the reader to infer the model's precise nature during the detailed description of the features. Defining the classification task and model more explicitly at the beginning of Section 4 would improve readability.
3.  **Lack of detail on the feature engineering process.** The paper presents several families of features but offers limited insight into their development. For example, it is unclear whether different feature combinations or conjunctions were explored. A more detailed analysis or ablation study of the feature space could further strengthen the paper's technical contribution.

### Questions to Authors

1.  In Section 4.1, you describe using a "candidate list" to enrich mentions with semantic attributes from hyperlinks. Could you please clarify what this list consists of? Is it the set of all mentions extracted from the article, or another collection of entities?
2.  There seems to be a potential ambiguity between Section 4.1 and Section 6.1 regarding the mention sets. Section 4.1 implies that the mention detection algorithm is the same one used in the baseline systems. However, Section 6.1 states that the training data is the "union of WCR mentions and all mentions predicted by" that same method. Are the gold "WCR mentions" a manually annotated superset of what the automatic method finds, or are they two distinct sets? Please clarify the exact relationship between the gold mentions in WCR and the automatically detected mentions used for training.
