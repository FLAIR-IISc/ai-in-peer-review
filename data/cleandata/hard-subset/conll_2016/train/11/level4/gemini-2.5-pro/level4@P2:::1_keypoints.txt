- Introduces and formalizes Main Concept Resolution (MCR) for Wikipedia: identify all mentions referring to an article's main topic.  
- Creates and releases a 30-article WCR dataset with full coreference chains as a testbed.  
- Shows state-of-the-art coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia, motivating domain-specific approaches.  
- Proposes a binary SVM MCR classifier using Wikipedia structure (hyperlinks) and Freebase-derived features.  
- MCR classifier substantially outperforms strong baselines and boosts Dcoref when used as a high-precision sieve.  
- Contribution: formalizing the MCR task and providing the WCR dataset for future research.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that newswire-trained coreference models degrade on Wikipedia.  
- Contribution: effective, knowledge-rich feature set and practical integration into a full coreference pipeline (Dcoref++).  
- Strength: well-motivated, impactful task that targets document-primary-entity extraction and links coreference with entity linking.  
- Strength: thorough analysis showing performance drops of five recent systems on the new dataset.  
- Strength: strong empirical results (MCR F1=88.79, >13 point gain; Dcoref++ +3.34 F1 on full task) with ablation studies.  
- Weakness: limited technical novelty—the approach is a standard SVM with hand-engineered features.  
- Weakness: model description and experimental setup lack clarity (late disclosure of binary classifier, ambiguous terms like “candidate list”).  
- Weakness: paper organization could be improved by presenting the dataset and baseline evaluations earlier.  
- Question: clarify the distinction between the WCR mention set and mentions predicted by Raghunathan et al. (2010)—does WCR include mentions that that method misses?  
- Question: did you try feature conjunctions (e.g., partial string match + entity type) for the non-pronominal model to strengthen signals?
