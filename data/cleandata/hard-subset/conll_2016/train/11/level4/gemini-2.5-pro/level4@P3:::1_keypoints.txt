- Introduces and formalizes Main Concept Resolution (MCR) to identify all mentions referring to an article's main subject.  
- Creates and annotates a 30-document WCR dataset and demonstrates SOTA coreference systems fail on Wikipedia (domain gap).  
- Proposes a feature-based binary classifier using Wikipedia markup, hyperlinks, and Freebase that outperforms baselines and improves a full coreference system when integrated.  

- Well-motivated, high-impact task that isolates the central entity chain useful for downstream IE.  
- Provides a valuable new WCR testbed and strong benchmarking showing the need for domain-specific coreference methods.  
- Strong empirical gains: ~+13 F1 on MCR and >+3 CoNLL F1 when integrated into a state-of-the-art system.  

- Limited methodological novelty: primarily a standard SVM with engineered features.  
- Model and feature descriptions lack clarity and should state the binary-classifier design earlier and detail feature interactions/conjunctions.  
- Baselines may be suboptimal; re-training a statistical system on WCR would better isolate the value of Wikipedia-specific features.  

- Clarify whether WCR annotations are gold mentions or based on the automatic mention detector (are WCR mentions distinct from auto-detected ones?).  

- Suggest introducing WCR earlier (after Related Work) so baselines report concrete numbers on the new dataset.  
- Clarify the term "candidate list" and explicitly state/cite that Dcoref and Scoref refer to the Stanford and Berkeley coreference systems.
