- Introduces "main concept resolution" task for Wikipedia-focused coreference.
- Creates a new dataset of 30 fully annotated documents; ~25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.
- Presents simple baselines that are outperformed by the authors' basic classifier.
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system, yielding significant gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia data.
- Task is highly motivated for information extraction, focusing on high-importance mentions and reducing irrelevant generic mentions.
- Defining a main concept provides a useful discourse anchor and enables integration of external knowledge.
- Wikipedia is a promising testbed to develop coreference ideas that may generalize beyond niche evaluations.
- The contribution has broader impact than narrow problems (e.g., Winograd), affecting overall coreference metrics in a relevant domain.
- Technical novelty is limited compared to the task formulation, but the methods are effective.
- Feature set is logical; unclear whether feature conjunctions were explored and could boost performance.
- The manuscript should state earlier that the main concept model is a binary classifier.
- Recommend reorganizing the paper to present the dataset immediately after Related Work to better motivate baselines and approach.
- Section 4 should cite or clarify "Dcoref" and "Scoref" as Stanford coreference components or add proper references.
- Define "candidate list" explicitly as the set of mentions in the article that are candidates for being coreferent with the main concept.
- Clarify inconsistency between Sections 6.1 and 4.1 about whether the evaluation uses the union of mention sets or identical sets.
