- Introduces Main Concept Resolution (MCR), a new coreference task focused on identifying the main entity in Wikipedia articles.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia documents with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrating the classifier into the Stanford rule-based system yields substantial performance gains on the Wikipedia domain.  
- Task is well-motivated and practical for information extraction, potentially bridging coreference and entity linking research.  
- MCR reduces noise from irrelevant mentions by focusing on highly salient, document-central references.  
- Using the main concept as a discourse anchor is promising, but significant challenges remain, especially for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Grounding the task in Wikipedia enables leveraging external knowledge and makes the domain a good testbed for novel coreference methods.  
- Technical contributions are effective but modest; feature set is sensible.  
- Unclear whether feature conjunctions were explored; this could improve performance.  
- The model should be explicitly introduced earlier as a binary classifier before feature descriptions.  
- Suggest reorganizing the paper: move dataset description after Related Work to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- First mentions of Dcoref and Scoref should include citations and clarify they are Stanford Coreference components.  
- The term "candidate list" is used unclearly and should be explicitly defined as all mentions in the article considered for coreference with the main concept.  
- Clarify contradiction between Section 4.1 and 6.1 about whether WCR annotations equal the mentions extracted by Raghunathan et al. (2010) or include additional mentions.
