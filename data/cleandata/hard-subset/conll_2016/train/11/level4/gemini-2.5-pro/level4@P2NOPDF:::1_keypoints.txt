- Introduces Main Concept Resolution (MCR): identify all mentions of the central entity in a Wikipedia article.  
- New dataset: 30 Wikipedia articles annotated with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Proposes baselines and a classifier-based approach; the classifier outperforms baselines.  
- Integrating the classifier into the Stanford coreference system yields significant improvements over state-of-the-art on this Wikipedia dataset.  
- Task is well-motivated and relevant to information extraction, bridging coreference and entity linking.  
- Main concept chains are high-value for downstream IE compared to many generic coreference chains.  
- The main concept serves as a useful discourse anchor; substantial room for improvement remains, especially for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Wikipedia enables incorporating external knowledge, making MCR a valuable testbed for new coreference ideas.  
- Technical weakness: contribution is stronger in task formulation than in modeling; classifier uses standard features.  
- Suggest exploring feature conjunctions for potential performance gains.  
- Clarity issue: authors should state earlier that the MCR system is a binary classifier and better define the model before feature discussion.  
- Organization suggestion: present the dataset immediately after Related Work so Baselines can show concrete results that motivate the Approach.  
- Citation suggestion: clarify and cite that Dcoref and Scoref are components of the Stanford coreference system.  
- Terminology issue: clarify the meaning of “candidate list” as the set of all mentions in the article that could be part of the main concept chain.  
- Clarification needed on mention sets (Section 6.1): explain the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) and where additional WCR mentions come from.
