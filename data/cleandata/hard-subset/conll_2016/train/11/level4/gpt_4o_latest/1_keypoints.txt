- Introduces "main concept resolution" on Wikipedia: identify the coreference chain for the article's primary entity.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Provides simple baselines and a binary classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system, yielding large improvements over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Task is meaningful for information extraction and bridges coreference with entity linking research.  
- Focuses on mentions directly relevant to IE, avoiding many generic mentions in standard coreference datasets.  
- Wikipedia domain enables leveraging external knowledge (e.g., hyperlinks, Freebase) for coreference.  
- The dataset and task can serve as a useful testbed to advance coreference methods, though general robustness remains challenging.  
- Techniques are effective but not especially novel; more feature conjunctions might improve results.  
- The main concept resolver is a binary classifier and should be stated earlier in the paper.  
- Performance still needs improvement, notably for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Organizational suggestion: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- Clarify terminology and cite Stanford coreference papers when referring to Dcoref and Scoref.  
- Specify that "candidate list" means the set of mentions in the article and clarify how hyperlink-derived semantic attributes are applied.  
- Resolve the confusion in Section 6.1 about the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010).
