- Introduces a novel Wikipedia-focused coreference task: identify coreference chains tied to the article's central entity ("main concept").  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles; ~25% of mentions belong to the main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a binary classifier that outperforms baselines.  
- Successfully integrates the classifier into Stanford's rule-based coreference system, yielding substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Task is well-motivated for information extraction where documents center on a single salient entity (e.g., biographies, clinical records).  
- Addresses a real limitation of standard coreference systems that handle irrelevant/generic mentions poorly.  
- The "main concept" provides a useful discourse anchor with potential to improve coreference robustness and link to entity linking.  
- Methodological contribution is modest but effective; the binary classifier is sensible though not groundbreaking.  
- Paper lacks early, clear model definition when describing features, making methodology harder to follow.  
- Does not explore feature conjunctions, which could potentially improve performance.  
- Minor clarity/organization fixes suggested: move dataset description to Section 3, define "candidate list" and "shallow semantic attributes," cite Stanford Coref papers for Dcoref/Scoref, and resolve inconsistency about WCR mentions.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful, well-justified task with strong empirical results; recommendation: Accept.
