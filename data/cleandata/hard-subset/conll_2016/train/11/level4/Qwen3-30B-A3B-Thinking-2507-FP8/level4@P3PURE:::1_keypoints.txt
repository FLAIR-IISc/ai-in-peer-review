- Introduces a novel "main concept" (MC) coreference task for Wikipedia: identify coreference chains for the article's central entity.  
- Annotated 30 documents with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions are to the MC.  
- Presents simple baselines and a classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrating the classifier into the Stanford rule-based coreference system yields substantial improvements over all state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia.  
- The task aligns well with information extraction (IE) needs and can bridge coreference and entity linking research.  
- The task is genuinely motivating and practically important, not just a contrived domain for tweaking systems.  
- MC resolution is valuable because many documents center on one entity; focusing on MC excludes irrelevant mentions and leverages Wikipedia/knowledge resources.  
- The methodology is effective but not the paper's strongest point; feature set is sensible.  
- It’s unclear whether feature conjunctions and related experiments were explored and reported.  
- The MC system is a binary classifier; this should be stated explicitly early in the paper.  
- Organizational suggestion: introduce the dataset immediately after Related Work to motivate baselines and approach.  
- Terminology needs clarification: explicitly cite/label Dcoref and Scoref as the Stanford Coreference System.  
- Clarify "candidate list" definition and precise use of hyperlink-derived features (e.g., inferring gender from linked Freebase entities).  
- Resolve inconsistency between Sections 6.1 and 4.1 about whether WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) are identical or unioned.
