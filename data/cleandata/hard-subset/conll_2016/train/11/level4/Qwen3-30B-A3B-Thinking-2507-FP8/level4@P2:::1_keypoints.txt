- Summary: Proposes main-concept coreference task for Wikipedia, releases WCR dataset (30 articles), and presents a binary classifier that outperforms baselines and improves Dcoref.

- Contribution: Well-motivated task and WCR dataset addressing main-concept identification in Wikipedia articles.

- Contribution: Binary classifier achieves ~88.99% F1 (pronominal) and ~88.67% F1 (non-pronominal), outperforming baselines by 13–15 F1.

- Contribution: Integrating the classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++) yields a +4 absolute CoNLL F1 improvement on Wikipedia texts.

- Strength: Task focuses on practically relevant mentions for information extraction, beneficial for entity-focused domains like biographies and clinical records.

- Strength: Strong empirical evidence that general-purpose coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia, justifying the new task.

- Strength: Demonstrated practical gains by integrating the classifier into an existing state-of-the-art coreference system.

- Weakness: Lacks sufficient implementation and training details (feature selection, hyperparameter tuning, class imbalance handling).

- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 articles, ~60k tokens), limiting statistical significance and generalizability.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of limitations and error sources (e.g., noisy Wikipedia redirects) and how to mitigate them.

- Question: How was the negative class weight of 2.0 chosen and was systematic hyperparameter tuning performed?

- Question: Clarify the difference between WCR mentions and the mentions from Raghunathan et al. (2010) and how the latter were generated.

- Question: Why does Dcoref++ perform worse than the standalone classifier on pronominal mentions (85.46% vs 88.99%)?

- Overall: Effective and meaningful contribution that bridges coreference and entity linking, but constrained by dataset size and missing methodological details.
