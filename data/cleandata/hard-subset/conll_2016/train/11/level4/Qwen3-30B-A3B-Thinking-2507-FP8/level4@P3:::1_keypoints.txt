- Introduces Main Concept Resolution (MCR), a task to identify all mentions of an article's central entity in Wikipedia.  
- Releases WCR3: 30 annotated Wikipedia articles with ~7,000 mentions (1,800 MC mentions) for MCR evaluation.  
- Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes to detect mentions co-referent with the main concept.  
- Integrates the classifier into a rule-based coreference system (Dcoref++), improving Wikipedia coreference by 4 absolute F1 points.  
- Strong motivation: MCR targets mentions relevant to information extraction, addressing limitations of standard coreference on Wikipedia.  
- High-quality dataset and evaluation showing news-trained coreference systems underperform on Wikipedia.  
- Significant empirical gains: ≈89% F1 for pronouns and non-pronominals, 13–15 point improvement over baselines.  
- Lack of clear task definition early in the paper; binary classification framing appears too late.  
- Limited exploration and reporting of feature engineering choices, combinations, and failed configurations.  
- Insufficient discussion of generalizability beyond Wikipedia and reliance on Wikipedia/Freebase-specific features.  
- Ambiguous description of the “candidate list” preprocessing step and its precise definition.  
- Question: Is the main concept defined by the article title or inferred from content, and how is that distinction handled?  
- Question: How is the inferred type (common noun from the first sentence) determined, and how are conflicts with the title resolved?  
- Question: Why do statistical systems rank differently on this dataset vs. OntoNotes, and what does that imply about processing Wikipedia vs. news?  
- Question: Which features were borrowed from prior work versus newly proposed, and what motivated the chosen feature set?  
- Overall recommendation: paper is valuable, well-motivated, and recommended for acceptance with clarifications.
