- Introduces a new main-concept (MC) coreference task for Wikipedia: identify all mentions of the article's primary concept.  
- Creates WCR3 dataset: 30 Wikipedia articles, ~60k tokens, 1,800 MC mentions out of ~7,000 non-singleton mentions.  
- Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes that outperforms state-of-the-art coreference baselines.  
- Contributions: formalizes MC resolution task, releases WCR3 dataset, and presents a classifier that improves MC and full-coreference performance.  
- Strength: Task is highly relevant for information extraction, focusing on conceptually significant mentions rather than generic coreference noise.  
- Strength: Classifier yields substantial gains (88.79% F1 vs. 75.77% baseline), showing Wikipedia-specific features are effective.  
- Strength: Integrating the classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++) improves full coreference F1 by ~4 points, demonstrating practical utility.  
- Weakness: Feature engineering lacks novelty and detailed analysis of feature interactions or higher-order combinations.  
- Weakness: Dataset is small (30 articles, 60k tokens), raising concerns about statistical significance and generalizability.  
- Weakness: The use of a binary classifier is not clearly stated early, causing methodological ambiguity.  
- Question: How does the classifier scale to a larger, more diverse Wikipedia sample (e.g., 100+ articles)?  
- Question: Can the authors provide qualitative examples of top misclassifications for non-pronominal mentions (e.g., "Houston" vs. "Houston Texans")?
