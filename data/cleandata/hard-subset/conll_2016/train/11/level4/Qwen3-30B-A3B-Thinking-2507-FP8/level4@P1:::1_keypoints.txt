- Introduces Main Concept Resolution (MCR), a coreference task to identify mentions of the main Wikipedia article concept rather than all chains.  
- Creates the WCR dataset: 30 Wikipedia articles, ~60k tokens, 7,000 mentions (25% main-concept), annotated with OntoNotes guidelines.  
- Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features to detect main-concept mentions, outperforming baselines by up to 15 F1 points.  
- Integrates the classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++), yielding a 4-point CoNLL F1 improvement on Wikipedia texts.  
- Strength: Task is practically motivated and addresses poor performance of news-trained coreference systems on Wikipedia.  
- Strength: WCR dataset is carefully annotated and sampled across diverse articles, enabling domain-specific evaluation.  
- Strength: Demonstrated end-to-end utility by improving a full coreference pipeline, not just the auxiliary task.  
- Weakness: Baselines (heuristics like longest chain or title match) are simplistic and don’t fairly represent state-of-the-art coreference systems.  
- Weakness: Small dataset (30 articles) limits statistical significance and generalizability of results.  
- Weakness: Feature exploration is incomplete — only a subset of "a few hundred features" reported and more complex models not explored.  
- Question: How would a full coreference model trained on MCR compare to the proposed binary classifier approach?  
- Question: Provide more details on the WCR sampling strategy to justify representativeness across Wikipedia.  
- Question: Have the authors considered training a dedicated Wikipedia coreference system on WCR instead of integrating a classifier into existing pipelines, and what challenges would that entail?
