- Introduces a novel "main concept" coreference task for Wikipedia that identifies coreference chains tied to the article’s primary entity.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles and found ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a basic binary classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrating the classifier into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system yields significant improvements over state-of-the-art systems on Wikipedia data.  
- Task is well-motivated by IE needs and addresses the problem of irrelevant/generic mentions in standard coreference.  
- The main-concept formulation is valuable for domains like biographies, clinical records, and event briefings and can bridge coreference and entity linking research.  
- Wikipedia provides a promising testbed for integrating external knowledge, though non-pronominal mentions remain a substantial challenge.  
- Techniques are effective but not the paper’s strongest contribution; the feature set is sensible yet under-explored (e.g., feature conjunctions).  
- The primary system is a binary classifier but this is not stated early enough and the model is not clearly defined during featurization.  
- Organizational suggestion: move the dataset description earlier (after Related Works) to better motivate baselines and approach.  
- Citation/terminology fixes needed: clarify that "Dcoref" and "Scoref" refer to Stanford coreference papers, explicitly define "candidate list," and resolve inconsistency between Sections 4.1 and 6.1 about mention unions.
