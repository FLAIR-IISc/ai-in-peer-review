- Proposes a novel Wikipedia-tailored coreference task: identify the coreference chain for the article’s main entity (MC).  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the MC.  
- Introduces simple baselines and a binary classifier; the classifier outperforms baselines.  
- Integrating the classifier into the Stanford coreference system yields significant improvements over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Strong IE-motivated task: focuses on mentions that matter for information extraction in single-entity documents (e.g., biographies, clinical notes).  
- Task closes a meaningful gap where standard coreference systems include many irrelevant/generic mentions.  
- Wikipedia domain is valuable: hyperlinks provide discourse anchors and enable richer knowledge integration (e.g., Freebase gender).  
- Demonstrates substantial gains on overall coreference metrics within a broadly relevant domain, not just a narrow subtask.  
- Methodology and features are sensible but not the paper’s strongest contribution; more complex feature combinations might help.  
- Presentation issue: the primary MC system is a binary classifier but this is not stated early enough.  
- Minor clarity suggestions: move dataset description earlier, clarify "Dcoref"/"Scoref" references, define "candidate list" explicitly, and resolve WCR mention consistency between sections.  
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, impactful task design that refocuses coreference on entity-relevant mentions and could spur new research bridging coreference and IE.
