- Introduces Main Concept Resolution (MCR), a Wikipedia-tailored coreference task to identify mentions referring to the article’s central entity.  
- Annotates 30 Wikipedia articles; roughly 25% of mentions correspond to the main concept.  
- Proposes a binary classifier for MCR that outperforms simple baselines.  
- Integrates the classifier into Stanford CoreNLP, producing substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Task is well-motivated: focuses coreference evaluation on IE-relevant mentions and avoids noisy/irrelevant chains.  
- Wikipedia’s hyperlink structure naturally supports the task and helps bridge coreference and entity linking.  
- Primary contribution is task design and dataset, not novel modeling techniques.  
- Clarity issue: binary classification formulation of the model is not stated early enough.  
- Methodological gap: lacks exploration of feature conjunctions and deeper feature analysis.  
- Integration gap: underutilizes Wikipedia’s knowledge graph (e.g., Freebase attributes) beyond surface matching.  
- Minor revision requests: move dataset description to Section 3, clarify “candidate list” terminology, properly cite CoreNLP systems, and resolve ambiguity about WCR mention sets/union.  
- Overall assessment: important, practical contribution likely to influence coreference research despite modest technical novelty.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
