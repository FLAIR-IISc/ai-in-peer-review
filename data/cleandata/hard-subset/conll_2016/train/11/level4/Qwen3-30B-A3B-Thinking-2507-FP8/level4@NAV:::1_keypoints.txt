- Introduces a novel Wikipedia-specific coreference task: identify coreference chains for the article’s primary entity (Main Concept, MC).  
- Annotates 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions relate to the MC.  
- Presents simple baselines and a basic classifier; the classifier outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford (rule-based) coreference system, achieving significant improvements over prior state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Task is a compelling coreference refinement with strong information-extraction relevance and potential to invigorate coreference research.  
- Provides a meaningful bridge between coreference and entity linking literatures.  
- Addresses a real failure mode of standard coreference systems: documents centered on a single salient entity (e.g., biographies, clinical records).  
- MC resolution focuses on IE-relevant mentions and reduces noise from generic references common in standard coreference.  
- Wikipedia’s structured knowledge enables novel knowledge use, making it a promising testbed for coreference advances.  
- Significant challenges remain, especially improving beyond baselines for non-pronominal mentions.  
- Methodology is weaker: features are sensible but under-explored (e.g., lacking feature conjunctions).  
- The MC resolver is a binary classifier but this detail is not stated early enough and the model is not fully defined during featurization.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: move dataset description to Section 3, clarify "Dcoref" and "Scoref" as Stanford components with citations, explicitly define "candidate list," and resolve ambiguity about the union of WCR mentions vs. mentions predicted by Raghunathan et al. (2010).
