- Task: formalizes "main concept" coreference on Wikipedia — find the full coreference chain for the entity an article is about.
- Dataset: introduces WCR, 30 annotated Wikipedia articles; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.
- Model: binary classifier (SVM) using Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived features to identify main-concept mentions.
- Performance: classifier outperforms strong baselines by a large margin (≈13 F1 points).
- Integration: incorporating the classifier into Stanford Dcoref yields substantial end-to-end coreference improvements on this domain.
- Contribution 1: presents a novel, practically motivated task and a benchmark dataset useful for IE and KB population.
- Contribution 2: empirically shows domain-specific features substantially outperform general-purpose coreference models on encyclopedic text.
- Strength: high practical motivation—useful for biography generation, KB population, and filtering salient entity mentions.
- Strength: provides a valuable resource (WCR) for coreference research beyond newswire despite modest size.
- Strength: thorough experimental setup with sensible baselines and a closed-loop system validation.
- Weakness: limited technical novelty—the approach is a standard binary SVM with established feature types.
- Weakness: clarity and organization problems—model details appear late and dataset description should precede the approach.
- Weakness: ambiguity in data preparation—unclear how the ~20,000 training mentions are derived from the annotated ~7,000 non-singleton mentions.
