- Introduces "Main Concept Resolution" (MC) as a binary task to identify mentions referring to an article's central entity and creates the WCR dataset (30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference annotations).
- Demonstrates that state-of-the-art coreference systems trained on newswire perform poorly on Wikipedia, motivating domain-specific approaches.
- Proposes a feature-based SVM using Wikipedia markup and Freebase links that substantially outperforms strong baselines (~89% F1).
- Shows that adding the high-precision MC classifier as a sieve to Stanford Dcoref improves full coreference resolution by ~4 CoNLL F1 points.
- Task is well-motivated and practically relevant for downstream IE tasks by focusing on the primary entity and filtering less relevant mentions.
- The WCR dataset and benchmarking provide a valuable resource and clear baselines for Wikipedia/coreference research.
- Strong experimental results and ablation studies convincingly show the contribution of each feature family.
- Methodological novelty is limited: core approach is a standard SVM with established feature types.
- Paper clarity and organization need improvement: model is not defined early, dataset description appears late, and some terms (e.g., "candidate list") are undefined.
- Ambiguity in data preparation: unclear how 7,000 gold WCR mentions and ~13,400 predicted mentions form the reported 20,362 training instances and how labels are assigned.
- Question: please clarify construction of the 20,362 mentions and label assignment in Section 6.1.
- Question: did you experiment with richer feature conjunctions or interaction terms to boost performance?
- Question: please detail the hyperlink-to-mention matching heuristic (e.g., headword overlap, string inclusion) used for enriching mentions.
