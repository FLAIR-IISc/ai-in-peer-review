- Introduces the "Main Concept Resolution" task, frames it as binary classification, and creates the WCR dataset using Wikipedia and Freebase features.  
- Presents a knowledge-rich SVM classifier that achieves nearly 89% F1 and outperforms state-of-the-art coreference baselines.  
- Shows that adding the classifier as a high-precision sieve to Dcoref yields a ~4-point absolute CoNLL F1 improvement on WCR.  
- Well-motivated, practically useful problem formulation with strong relevance to downstream tasks like IE and summarization.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation: large gains over baselines (e.g., +13 F1 vs. best Dcoref baseline) and clear ablation analyses.  
- Provides a new community resource (WCR dataset), highlighting domain shift from newswire to encyclopedic text.  
- Limited technical novelty: method is mainly an SVM with engineered features inspired by prior work.  
- Paper organization/clarity issues: binary-classifier framing and dataset description are presented too late.  
- Ambiguity in data preparation: unclear how WCR gold mentions are combined with automatically detected mentions for training/testing.  
- Question: Please clarify the exact procedure for constructing the mention set used to train/test the classifier (gold boundaries vs. automatic candidates).  
- Question: Have you analyzed the impact of preventing later sieves from adding mentions to the MC chain, which could hurt recall if your classifier misses difficult mentions?
