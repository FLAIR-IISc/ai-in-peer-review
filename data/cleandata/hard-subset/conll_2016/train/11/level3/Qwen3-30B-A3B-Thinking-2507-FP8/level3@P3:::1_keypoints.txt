- Summary: Proposes "main concept resolution" for Wikipedia, annotates 30 articles (~25% mentions are main concept), and presents a binary classifier that outperforms baselines and improves a Stanford coref pipeline.

- Contribution 1: Defines a practically motivated task focusing on primary-entity mentions relevant to information extraction.

- Contribution 2: Provides WCR, a 30-article annotated Wikipedia dataset showing a gap between standard coref systems and Wikipedia text.

- Contribution 3: Offers a simple classifier that outperforms baselines and improves end-to-end coreference when integrated (Dcoref++).

- Strength 1: Task is genuinely important and well-motivated by real-world applications (biographies, dossiers, clinical records).

- Strength 2: Clearly demonstrates a substantial performance drop of standard coref systems from OntoNotes to WCR.

- Strength 3: Integration yields substantial practical improvement (≈4-point CoNLL F1 gain).

- Weakness 1: Technique lacks novelty — a straightforward binary SVM using existing features without deeper exploration.

- Weakness 2: Paper organization and clarity are poor; model definition and dataset description are misplaced and sections contain ambiguities.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient citations (e.g., Dcoref, Scoref) and unclear explanation of the candidate list and hyperlink-derived semantic attributes.

- Question 1: Please clarify the specific failure modes of standard coreference systems that justify the new task.

- Question 2: Resolve the ambiguity about where the "extra WCR mentions" come from (Sections 4.1 vs 6.1).

- Question 3: Revise the paper to state the binary classification approach earlier (e.g., in the introduction).

- Question 4: Would the authors explore richer feature conjunctions or more sophisticated models to improve performance?
