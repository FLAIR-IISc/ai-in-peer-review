- Task: Introduces a new Wikipedia coreference task — identify mentions of the article's main concept rather than full coreference chains.
- Dataset: Creates WCR, 30 Wikipedia articles (~60k tokens) annotated with coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.
- Model: Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features to detect main-concept mentions.
- Results: Classifier outperforms baselines built on state-of-the-art coreference systems and improves Dcoref by ~4 F1 points on Wikipedia.
- Contribution 1: Defines a focused, practical coreference task (main concept identification) useful for information extraction.
- Contribution 2: Provides a new WCR dataset as a testbed for Wikipedia-specific coreference evaluation.
- Contribution 3: Offers a simple, effective classifier that integrates into full coreference pipelines.
- Strength 1: Addresses an important, underexplored task with clear practical applications and a meaningful discourse anchor.
- Strength 2: Fills a resource gap by adapting coreference resolution to the Wikipedia genre with annotated data.
- Strength 3: Demonstrates substantial empirical gains (13–15 F1 over baselines) and real-world utility when integrated into Dcoref.
- Weakness 1: Limited novelty — features and approach (using markup/Freebase) are similar to prior work and the classifier is simple.
- Weakness 2: Insufficient distinction between main-concept resolution and standard coreference, causing potential confusion about the tasks' relationship.
- Weakness 3: Small dataset (30 articles) limits statistical significance and generalizability across Wikipedia.
- Question 1: Is main-concept resolution fundamentally different from full coreference or merely a subset requiring different modeling?
- Question 2: How were the Raghunathan et al. (2010) mentions generated and why were they added to WCR?
- Question 3: What were the annotation guidelines for determining the main concept, and how was consistency ensured across articles?
- Question 4: How does the classifier interact with rule-based (Dcoref) versus statistical systems, and would gains transfer to statistical systems?
