- Introduces a new coreference task on Wikipedia: identify all mentions of the article's main concept (MC) rather than all coreference chains.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the MC.  
- Proposes a binary classifier that outperforms baselines built on Dcoref and Scoref by 13–15 F1 points.  
- Integrating the classifier into Dcoref yields a 4 F1 point improvement in Wikipedia coreference resolution.  
- Primary contribution: a practically motivated task addressing real-world needs (e.g., biographies, clinical records).  
- Secondary contribution: WCR dataset as a useful, carefully annotated testbed for Wikipedia text.  
- Tertiary contribution: effective integration into a full coreference pipeline showing tangible gains.  
- Strength: focuses on filtering generic mentions to prioritize what matters for information extraction.  
- Strength: dataset is well-constructed and suitable for future research.  
- Strength: approach shows significant empirical improvements over strong baselines.  
- Weakness: methods/features are sensible but not technically novel—application of existing techniques.  
- Weakness: binary classifier is not introduced early enough, hindering initial comprehension.  
- Weakness: organizational and clarity issues—reorder dataset description, add citations for Dcoref/Scoref, clarify terms like “candidate list.”  
- Overall: promising new research direction with novelty mainly in task definition; authors should clarify classifier early and fix organizational issues before publication.
