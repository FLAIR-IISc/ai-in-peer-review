- Introduces a novel coreference task for Wikipedia: identify all mentions of the article's main concept (MC).

- Creates a new WCR dataset: 30 Wikipedia articles, ~7,000 coreferent mentions, ~25% of mentions refer to the MC.

- Proposes a binary SVM classifier using Wikipedia/Freebase features that outperforms baselines by ~13–15 F1 on MC identification.

- Integrates the classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++), yielding a 4-point absolute F1 improvement on Wikipedia texts.

- Practical motivation: focusing on the MC filters out irrelevant mentions and benefits IE applications (biographies, dossiers, clinical records).

- Dataset strengths: annotated per OntoNotes, diverse article selection, and sufficient size for experiments.

- Claims of novelty are overstated—task and feature use are incremental relative to prior work (Nguyen et al. 2007; Nakayama 2008; Ratinov & Roth 2012).

- Paper lacks sufficient technical detail about the classifier architecture and buries model description too late.

- Feature engineering is sensible but not particularly innovative; no exploration of more advanced models (e.g., neural nets).

- Ambiguities in dataset/feature extraction: unclear origin/selection of the ~13,400 automatically extracted singletons and an ill-defined "candidate list."

- Question: How does the MC task differ from prior "entity-centric coreference" work and what new insights are provided?

- Question: How would the approach perform versus systems specifically trained/fine-tuned on Wikipedia (e.g., models trained on WCR)?

- Question: Can the method be extended to identify multiple key concepts/discourse anchors rather than only the primary MC?
