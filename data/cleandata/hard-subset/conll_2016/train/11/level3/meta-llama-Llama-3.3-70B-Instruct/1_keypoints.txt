- Proposes a Wikipedia-tailored coreference task focused on identifying the article’s main-entity coreference chains.
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia documents to create a testbed for this main concept resolution task.
- Develops a basic classifier that outperforms simple baselines and existing systems on Wikipedia.
- Argues that main concept resolution can bridge coreference and entity linking and serve as a discourse anchor for IE applications.
- Contribution: new task formulation tailored to Wikipedia with potential to improve coreference state-of-the-art.
- Contribution: basic classifier demonstrating effectiveness on the proposed task.
- Contribution: curated dataset of 30 annotated documents for future research.
- Strength: novel approach with potential impact on coreference research.
- Strength: classifier effectiveness relative to baselines and prior systems on Wikipedia.
- Strength: publicly useful annotated dataset as a testbed.
- Strength: potential downstream impact on entity linking, QA, and information extraction.
- Weakness: annotated dataset is small and may not represent the full Wikipedia corpus.
- Weakness: classifier is simple and may not capture complex coreference phenomena.
- Weakness: insufficient comparisons with other state-of-the-art coreference systems.
- Question: how will the annotated dataset be scaled to cover more of Wikipedia?
- Question: what specific features were used in the classifier and how were they chosen?
- Question: how do the authors plan to improve classifier performance and address limitations?
