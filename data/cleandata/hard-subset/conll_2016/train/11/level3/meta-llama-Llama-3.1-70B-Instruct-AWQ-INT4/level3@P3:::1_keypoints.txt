- Summary: Proposes the task of identifying all mentions of a Wikipedia article's main concept and builds a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase, evaluated on 30 articles with improvements over state-of-the-art systems.

- Contribution 1: Introduces the "Main Concept Resolution" task aimed at practical IE applications like biographies and dossiers.

- Contribution 2: Develops a binary classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase features, trained on 30 annotated articles, showing strong performance.

- Contribution 3: Integrates the classifier with the Stanford deterministic system, yielding a ~4% F1 improvement on full coreference.

- Strength 1: Novel, practically motivated task definition relevant to the NLP community.

- Strength 2: Effective exploitation of Wikipedia markup and Freebase improves coreference performance.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates significant gains over existing coreference systems.

- Strength 4: Evaluation is well-designed, using 10-fold cross-validation and multiple metrics.

- Weakness 1: Dataset is very small (only 30 annotated articles).

- Weakness 2: Techniques employed are effective but not highly novel.

- Weakness 3: Paper lacks a detailed error analysis to explain failures and limitations.

- Weakness 4: Unclear generalizability to other domains or text genres; further evaluation needed.

- Question 1: How will the authors address limited dataset size and improve generalizability?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide a more detailed error analysis and characterize common error types?

- Question 3: How could the approach be extended to identify all coreference relations in an article?

- Question 4: Have the authors considered evaluation on other datasets or genres to test generalizability?
