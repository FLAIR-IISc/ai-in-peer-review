- Introduces a new "main concept" coreference task: identifying all mentions of the main concept in a Wikipedia article.  
- Constructs a dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles as a testbed for this task.  
- Proposes a simple method using two binary classifiers (pronominal and non-pronominal) leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.  
- Shows significant performance gains over baselines built on state-of-the-art coreference systems.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, producing substantial improvements on Wikipedia.  
- Practical motivation: task is useful for information extraction (biographies, dossiers, clinical records).  
- Approach is simple yet effective, outperforming several strong baselines.  
- Effective integration with existing systems demonstrates real-world applicability.  
- Novelty concern: task creation may capitalize on scenarios where standard systems perform poorly.  
- Technique concern: methods and features are not novel; richer feature conjunctions might further help.  
- Organization/clarity issues: paper would benefit from clearer structure (e.g., move dataset description earlier, clarify "candidate list").  
- Clarification requested on ambiguity between Section 6.1 and 4.1 about union of WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. mentions.  
- Asks how the approach will be extended to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article.  
- Requests more detail on the Wikipedia markup and Freebase features and their contributions to performance.  
- Overall: useful contribution with notable empirical gains, but needs clearer presentation and responses to novelty/technique concerns.
