- Paper introduces a new coreference task: identifying all mentions of the main concept within a Wikipedia article.
- Authors propose a binary classification approach using features from Wikipedia markup/characteristics and Freebase.
- Method is evaluated on a dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles, reporting significant improvement over state-of-the-art coreference systems.
- Contribution: new task tailored to Wikipedia coreference resolution.
- Contribution: a feature-driven binary classification model leveraging Wikipedia and Freebase signals.
- Contribution: creation and use of a 30-article Wikipedia dataset for evaluation.
- Strength: achieves substantial performance gains compared to existing coreference systems on the presented dataset.
- Strength: features are carefully designed to capture Wikipedia-specific and Freebase characteristics.
- Strength: evaluation conducted on a challenging dataset relevant to the task.
- Weakness: techniques (binary classification and feature extraction) lack methodological novelty.
- Weakness: dataset is small (30 articles) and may not represent the full Wikipedia corpus.
- Weakness: generalizability is unverified; further evaluation on larger/diverse datasets is needed.
- Questions: provide selection criteria and more details about the 30-article dataset.
- Questions: plans for extending the approach to identify all coreference relations in an article?
- Questions: clarify the feature extraction process and rationale for chosen features.
