**Summary**

The paper "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution" proposes a novel task of identifying all mentions of the main concept in a Wikipedia article. The authors develop a binary classifier that exploits features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to classify mentions as coreferent or not. They evaluate their approach on a dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles and demonstrate significant improvements over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the Main Concept Resolution task**: The authors propose a new task that focuses on identifying mentions that refer to the main concept of a Wikipedia article. This task is practically motivated for Information Extraction (IE) applications, such as biographies, dossiers, and clinical records.
2. **Development of a binary classifier**: The authors develop a binary classifier that exploits features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase to classify mentions as coreferent or not. The classifier is trained on a dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles and achieves significant improvements over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.
3. **Integration with a rule-based coreference system**: The authors integrate their classifier with the Stanford deterministic rule-based system (Lee et al., 2013) and demonstrate an improvement of 4% in F1 score on a fully fledged coreference task.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel task definition**: The authors propose a new task that is practically motivated and relevant to the NLP community.
2. **Effective use of Wikipedia markup and Freebase**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of exploiting Wikipedia markup and Freebase features to improve coreference resolution performance.
3. **Significant improvements over state-of-the-art systems**: The authors demonstrate significant improvements over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems, including the Stanford deterministic rule-based system.
4. **Well-designed evaluation methodology**: The authors use a well-designed evaluation methodology, including a 10-fold cross-validation procedure and multiple evaluation metrics.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset size**: The dataset used in the study is relatively small, consisting of only 30 annotated Wikipedia articles.
2. **Lack of novelty in techniques**: The authors' techniques, while effective, are not particularly novel or groundbreaking.
3. **Need for more detailed error analysis**: The authors could benefit from a more detailed error analysis to better understand the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
4. **Limited generalizability**: The authors' approach may not generalize well to other domains or text genres, and further evaluation is needed to determine its applicability.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limited dataset size and lack of generalizability of your approach?
2. Can you provide more details on the error analysis and the types of errors that your approach is prone to?
3. How do you think your approach can be extended to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?
4. Have you considered evaluating your approach on other datasets or text genres to determine its generalizability?
