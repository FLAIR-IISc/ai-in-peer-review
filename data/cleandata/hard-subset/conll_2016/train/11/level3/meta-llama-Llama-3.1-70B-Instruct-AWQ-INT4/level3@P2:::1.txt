**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a new task in coreference resolution, "main concept" coreference, which involves identifying all mentions of the main concept in a Wikipedia article. The authors create a dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles and develop a simple yet effective approach to tackle this task. Their method uses two binary classifiers, one for pronominal and one for non-pronominal mentions, which exploit features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase. The authors evaluate their approach against several baselines built on top of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems and demonstrate significant improvements in performance. They also integrate their classifier into the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, leading to substantial gains over the state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the "main concept" coreference task**: The authors define a new task in coreference resolution, which is practically motivated for information extraction applications such as biographies, dossiers, and clinical records.
2. **Creation of a dataset**: The authors create a dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles, which provides a useful testbed for evaluating coreference methods that leverage knowledge.
3. **Effective approach**: The authors develop a simple yet effective approach to tackle the "main concept" coreference task, which outperforms several baselines built on top of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.

**Strengths**

1. **Practical motivation**: The "main concept" coreference task is practically motivated for information extraction applications, making it a useful contribution to the field.
2. **Effective approach**: The authors' approach is simple yet effective, demonstrating significant improvements in performance over several baselines.
3. **Integration with existing systems**: The authors integrate their classifier into the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, leading to substantial gains over the state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Novelty pattern**: The authors create a new task where standard systems perform poorly, which may raise concerns about novelty patterns.
2. **Techniques**: The techniques used by the authors are not novel, and richer feature conjunctions might help improve performance.
3. **Organization and clarity**: The paper could benefit from organizational and clarity suggestions, such as moving the dataset description earlier and clarifying the term "candidate list".

However, the paper could benefit from organizational and clarity suggestions, and the authors should address concerns about novelty patterns and the novelty of their techniques.
