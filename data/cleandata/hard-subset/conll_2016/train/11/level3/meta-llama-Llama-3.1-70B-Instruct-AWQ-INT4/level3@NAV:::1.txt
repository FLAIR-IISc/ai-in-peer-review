**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution" introduces a new task in coreference resolution, specifically designed for Wikipedia articles. The task involves identifying all mentions of the main concept being described in a Wikipedia article. The authors propose a binary classification approach to solve this task, using a set of carefully designed features extracted from Wikipedia markup and characteristics, as well as from Freebase. They evaluate their approach on a dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles, achieving a significant improvement over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of a new task**: The paper introduces a new task in coreference resolution, specifically designed for Wikipedia articles, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Development of a new approach**: The authors propose a binary classification approach to solve this task, using a set of carefully designed features extracted from Wikipedia markup and characteristics, as well as from Freebase.
3. **Evaluation on a new dataset**: The authors evaluate their approach on a dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Strengths**

1. **Significant improvement over state-of-the-art systems**: The authors' approach achieves a significant improvement over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on the Wikipedia dataset.
2. **Carefully designed features**: The authors' features are carefully designed to capture the specific characteristics of Wikipedia articles and Freebase.
3. **Evaluation on a challenging dataset**: The authors evaluate their approach on a challenging dataset, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of novelty in techniques**: The authors' approach is based on existing techniques, such as binary classification and feature extraction, which may not be novel.
2. **Limited dataset**: The authors' dataset is limited to 30 Wikipedia articles, which may not be representative of the entire Wikipedia corpus.
3. **Need for further evaluation**: The authors' approach needs to be evaluated on a larger dataset and in different settings to demonstrate its generalizability.
