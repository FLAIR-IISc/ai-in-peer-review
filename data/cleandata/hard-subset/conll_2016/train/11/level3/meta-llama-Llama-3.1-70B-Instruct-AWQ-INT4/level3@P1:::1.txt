**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel task, "main concept" coreference resolution, which involves identifying all mentions of the main concept in a Wikipedia article. The authors create a dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles and develop a simple yet effective approach to tackle this task. They design a binary classifier that outperforms several baselines built on top of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems. The classifier is then integrated into the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, leading to significant improvements in coreference resolution performance on Wikipedia articles.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of the "main concept" coreference task**: The paper proposes a new task that focuses on identifying the coreference chain for the entity the article is about. This task is practically motivated for information extraction applications, such as biographies, dossiers, and clinical records.
2. **Annotated dataset**: The authors create a dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains, where approximately 25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.
3. **Simple yet effective approach**: The paper presents a basic classifier that outperforms several baselines built on top of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.
4. **Integration with Stanford's rule-based coreference system**: The classifier is integrated into the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, leading to substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.

**Strengths**

1. **Practically motivated task**: The "main concept" coreference task is genuinely important and has practical applications in information extraction.
2. **Effective approach**: The proposed classifier outperforms several baselines and achieves significant improvements in coreference resolution performance on Wikipedia articles.
3. **Integration with existing systems**: The classifier can be integrated with existing coreference resolution systems, such as the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, to improve their performance on Wikipedia articles.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of novelty in techniques**: The paper's techniques are effective but not novel, and the features used are sensible but not particularly innovative.
2. **Organization and clarity**: The paper could benefit from organizational and clarity suggestions, such as moving the dataset description earlier, citing Stanford/Dcoref/Scoref explicitly, and clarifying the term "candidate list" and how hyperlinks add semantic attributes.
3. **Ambiguity in Section 6.1 vs 4.1**: The paper could clarify the ambiguity between Section 6.1 and 4.1 regarding the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the classifier is integrated with the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, and what specific modifications were made to the system?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of cascading errors in the pipeline, which can be caused by inaccuracies in the classifier's output?
3. Can you provide more information on the "candidate list" and how hyperlinks add semantic attributes to mentions?
4. How do you plan to extend this work to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article, and what challenges do you anticipate in this task?
