- Proposes a new "main concept" coreference task: identify all mentions of the article's main concept in a Wikipedia article.  
- Creates a dataset of 30 fully annotated Wikipedia articles; ~25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Develops a simple binary classifier that outperforms baselines built on state-of-the-art coreference systems.  
- Integrates the classifier into the Stanford deterministic rule-based system, yielding substantial performance gains on Wikipedia articles.  

- Introduces a practically motivated task useful for information extraction (e.g., biographies, dossiers, clinical records).  
- Provides an annotated dataset to support the new task.  
- Demonstrates an effective, easily integrable approach that improves existing coreference systems.  
- Shows concrete gains when combined with Stanford’s rule-based coreference system.  

- Task is practically important and well-motivated for real-world IE applications.  
- The classifier is effective and outperforms several strong baselines.  
- The method integrates well with existing coreference systems to improve Wikipedia performance.  

- Techniques and features are sensible and effective but lack novelty.  
- Paper organization and clarity need improvement (dataset placement, explicit citations, clearer terms).  
- Ambiguity between Sections 6.1 and 4.1 about the union of WCR mentions and Raghunathan et al. mentions.  

- Request details on how the classifier was integrated into the Stanford deterministic rule-based system and specific modifications made.  
- Ask how the authors will handle cascading errors in the pipeline from classifier inaccuracies.  
- Request clarification on the "candidate list" and the role of hyperlinks adding semantic attributes.  
- Ask how the approach could be extended to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article and anticipated challenges.
