- Introduces a new task: identifying mentions of the article’s “main concept” in Wikipedia, filling a gap in Wikipedia-specific coreference work.  
- Presents WCR, a new annotated dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains as a focused benchmark.  
- Demonstrates existing coreference systems perform poorly on this task; proposed classifier significantly outperforms strong baselines.  
- Integrates Wikipedia-specific features (markup, hyperlinks) and Freebase signals, producing substantial performance gains.  
- Ablation study shows the contribution of each feature family to overall performance.  
- Dataset sampling considered article size, topic, and link density; roughly 25% of mentions refer to the main concept.  
- Dataset size is small (30 articles), limiting generalizability and necessitating future expansion.  
- Feature novelty is modest—main contribution is applying and combining known features in a Wikipedia context rather than novel feature design.  
- Methodology presentation issue: the binary-classifier framing isn’t made explicit early enough (only clarified in Section 4).  
- Data preparation ambiguity: unclear source and annotation status of the “extra” mentions referenced to Raghunathan et al. (2010).  
- Question: plans for expanding WCR and releasing annotation guidelines to enable community contributions?  
- Question: clarify article selection criteria—were specific topics or characteristics prioritized?  
- Question: define the “candidate list” and explain how it’s used to enrich mention representations with semantic attributes.  
- Question: have you considered filtering or weighting noisy Wikipedia redirects to mitigate precision loss?  
- Additional comments: add explicit citations for Stanford CoreNLP, Dcoref, and Scoref; include an example contrasting full coreference chains vs the main-concept chain.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper with significant gains and clear recommendation for acceptance.
