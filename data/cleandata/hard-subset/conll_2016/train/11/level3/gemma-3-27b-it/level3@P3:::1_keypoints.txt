- Introduces a focused task: identify mentions of an article’s main concept in Wikipedia and integrates a binary classifier with a rule-based coreference system.  
- Creates and releases the WCR dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles for main-concept coreference (≈25% of mentions refer to main concept).  
- Shows a Wikipedia-trained binary classifier outperforms baselines and boosts Stanford Dcoref, yielding a ~4% F1 gain on full coreference resolution.  
- Argues that focusing on the document’s main concept is practical and useful for IE tasks like biography extraction and KB construction.  
- Empirically demonstrates the task’s importance and that exploiting Wikipedia structure and external knowledge improves performance.  
- Leverages Wikipedia hyperlink structure and Freebase-derived semantic attributes to enrich mention representations.  
- Provides strong empirical results with feature analysis and a 10-fold cross-validation evaluation using multiple standard metrics (MUC, B3, CEAF, CoNLL).  
- Successfully integrates the classifier into an existing complex rule-based system, showing practical utility.  
- Primary contribution is the WCR dataset and task framing rather than novel modeling techniques.  
- Core method (SVM classifier and engineered features) is incremental and largely inspired by prior work.  
- Paper delays stating the core method is a binary classifier until Section 4, reducing early clarity.  
- Organizational and clarity issues: dataset description should appear earlier, key systems should be cited on first mention, “candidate list” is poorly defined, and hyperlink-semantic-attribute explanation is thin.  
- Ambiguity in Section 6.1 about adding mentions from Raghunathan et al.; origin and impact of “extra WCR mentions” is unclear.  
- Requests more detailed annotation process information, including inter-annotator agreement scores.  
- Asks for criteria and effects of including mentions from Raghunathan et al. into WCR.  
- Requests a concrete, working URL for the released dataset and dumps.  
- Asks whether an error analysis was performed to categorize common failure types.  
- Suggests discussing limitations such as reliance on accurate mention detection and errors in Freebase data.  
- Recommends discussing extensions to handle complex documents or multiple main concepts.
