## Review for CONLL_2016 - Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

This paper introduces a novel and practically motivated coreference resolution task focused on identifying mentions of the “main concept” within a Wikipedia article. The authors address a gap in the literature – a dedicated focus on coreference in the specific context of Wikipedia, despite its widespread use in NLP applications. They present a new annotated dataset (WCR) of 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains, demonstrate that existing coreference systems perform poorly on this task, and propose a classifier that significantly outperforms baselines and improves a state-of-the-art rule-based system.

**Contributions:**

The primary contribution of this work is the framing and evaluation of the “main concept” coreference resolution task on Wikipedia. While coreference in Wikipedia has been previously touched upon in the context of information extraction, this paper provides a focused testbed and benchmark for evaluating systems specifically designed to identify mentions referring to the article’s central topic. The creation of the WCR dataset, though relatively small, is a significant contribution as it provides a resource for future research in this area. A secondary contribution lies in demonstrating the effectiveness of incorporating Wikipedia-specific features (markup, hyperlinks to Freebase) into a coreference system, achieving substantial gains over existing approaches when applied to this task.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty and Relevance of the Task:** The task of identifying mentions of the main concept is genuinely valuable. Unlike general coreference resolution, this task filters out many irrelevant mentions, focusing on the core entity described within the document. This is particularly beneficial for downstream applications such as information extraction (building biographies, dossiers, or clinical records) where precision regarding the central entity is crucial. The paper clearly articulates this motivation.

2. **Well-Designed Dataset:** The WCR dataset, while modest in size, is a valuable resource. The annotation following OntoNotes guidelines and the deliberate sampling of articles across various characteristics (size, topic, link density) suggest careful consideration was given to dataset construction. The statistic that roughly 25% of mentions relate to the main concept highlights the challenge and relevance of the task.

3. **Significant Performance Gains:** The results demonstrate a clear improvement over strong baselines, including adaptations of state-of-the-art coreference systems (Dcoref and Scoref). The reported F1 score improvement of 13 points over Dcoref, and the further gains achieved through integration into Dcoref++, are substantial and indicative of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

4. **Effective Feature Engineering:** The feature set leverages the unique characteristics of Wikipedia (markup, hyperlinks) and incorporates external knowledge from Freebase. While the individual features aren't revolutionary, their combination and thoughtful design appear to be key to the system’s performance. The ablation study (Table 2) effectively demonstrates the contribution of each feature family.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** The dataset of 30 articles is relatively small, potentially limiting the generalizability of the results. While the authors acknowledge this, it’s a crucial consideration, and further work with a larger dataset is necessary to confirm the robustness of the findings.

2. **Modest Feature Novelty:** The features themselves, while sensible and well-motivated, are largely based on existing approaches in coreference resolution. The primary novelty lies in their application to this specific task and the combination of Wikipedia-specific features. A deeper exploration of potentially more innovative features could further improve performance.

3. **Model Definition Placement:** The paper frames the approach as a classifier but doesn’t explicitly state that it’s a binary classification problem until Section 4. This definition should be presented earlier to provide a clearer understanding of the methodology.

4. **Ambiguity in Data Preparation:** The description of the data preparation process in Section 6.1 is somewhat ambiguous. It's unclear where the "extra" mentions added to the WCR dataset come from – the reference to Raghunathan et al. (2010) requires further clarification. It's also unclear if these added mentions were annotated as referring to the main concept.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. What are the plans for expanding the WCR dataset? Would you consider making the annotation guidelines publicly available to facilitate community contribution?
2. Could you provide more details about the criteria used for selecting the articles included in the WCR dataset? Were there specific topics or characteristics prioritized?
3. The phrase "candidate list" appears a few times without explicit definition. Could you clarify what constitutes this list and how it's used in the process of enriching mention representations with semantic attributes?
4.  The paper mentions that some noisy redirects in Wikipedia led to reduced precision.  Have you considered techniques to filter or weight these redirects to mitigate their impact?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from more explicit citations of the Stanford CoreNLP toolkit, Dcoref, and Scoref (especially in the introduction or related work sections).
*   Consider adding a brief example illustrating the difference between identifying *all* coreference chains versus the specific coreference chain relating to the “main concept.” This would further clarify the task's unique challenges.



Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a well-motivated and well-executed study. The proposed task and dataset have the potential to stimulate further research in coreference resolution, particularly in the context of knowledge-rich resources like Wikipedia. The weaknesses are primarily related to dataset size and incremental novelty, but the significant performance gains and clear articulation of the problem make this a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
