- Introduces a focused coreference task: identifying mentions of a Wikipedia article’s main concept.  
- Creates a new annotated dataset (WCR) of 30 Wikipedia articles as a key contribution.  
- Demonstrates practical utility for information extraction and tasks like biography summarization.  
- Shows standard coreference systems perform poorly on this main-concept task, motivating specialization.  
- Empirically validates that a simple classifier integrated into Stanford’s Dcoref substantially improves performance.  
- Leverages Wikipedia structure (hyperlinks, redirects) to enrich mention representations and avoid full NEL.  
- Presents the task as a useful discourse anchor and a testbed for knowledge-enhanced coreference methods.  
- Technical novelty of the classifier is limited; features are sensible but incremental.  
- Paper would benefit from exploring more complex feature interactions or advanced ML techniques.  
- Core classifier description is introduced too late and should appear earlier for better flow.  
- Clarity issues: explicitly cite Stanford/Dcoref/Scoref and better explain the “candidate list” and hyperlink-derived semantics.  
- Unclear connection between the union of WCR mentions and those from Raghunathan et al. in Section 6.1; needs clarification.  
- Questions about WCR’s limitations and selection bias from only 30 articles and how that affects generalizability.  
- Question on adapting/integrating the approach with other coreference systems, especially neural architectures.  
- Question on sensitivity of classifier performance to the quality of information extracted from Wikipedia and Freebase.
