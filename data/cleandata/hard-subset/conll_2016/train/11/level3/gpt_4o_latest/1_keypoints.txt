- Introduces the Main Concept (MC) coreference task for Wikipedia and creates the WCR dataset (30 documents).  
- Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features, achieving 89% F1 on MC resolution.  
- Integrates the classifier into Stanford Dcoref, improving overall CoNLL F1 on Wikipedia texts by 4%.  
- Contributions: task/dataset creation, a tailored classifier, and bridging coreference resolution with entity linking.  
- Strength: novel, practical task and a valuable WCR dataset for future research.  
- Strength: substantial empirical gains—13-point F1 increase for MC resolution and 4-point CoNLL F1 boost.  
- Strength: practical relevance for information extraction and seamless integration with existing systems.  
- Weakness: very small dataset (30 docs) raises generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on handcrafted, Wikipedia/Freebase-specific features limits cross-domain applicability.  
- Weakness: unclear methodology around “candidate lists” and Freebase feature integration, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited (feature engineering + SVM); main novelty is task/dataset.  
- Questions: does the classifier generalize beyond Wikipedia or to other semi-structured genres?  
- Questions: request clearer details on candidate list construction during preprocessing.  
- Questions: how were redirects and aliases from Wikipedia/Freebase filtered to avoid noisy features?  
- Additional: paper is well-motivated but needs better organization/terminology; expanding the dataset and using more generalizable techniques would strengthen impact.
