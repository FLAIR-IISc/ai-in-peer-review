- Summary: Defines Main Concept Resolution (MCR) for Wikipedia, introduces WCR dataset (30 articles), builds baselines from SOTA coreference outputs, and proposes a Wikipedia/Freebase-informed binary classifier that improves MCR and yields +4 CoNLL F1 when added to Dcoref.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a focused MCR task, a WCR testbed (30 docs, ~60k tokens), and shows newswire-trained CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia.

- Contribution 2: Proposes a knowledge-informed binary classifier (separate pronominal vs. non-pronominal models) using Wikipedia links/redirects and Freebase attributes, outperforming strong baselines by ~13–15 F1.

- Contribution 3: Integrates the classifier as a high-precision sieve in Dcoref, producing a substantial end-to-end improvement on Wikipedia text.

- Strength: Clear practical motivation—MCR filters the entity that “matters” in a Wikipedia article and is useful for IE pipelines.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains—the classifier reaches ~89 F1 on MCR and adds ~4 CoNLL F1 as a sieve to Dcoref.

- Strength: Sensible use of Wikipedia markup and Freebase to avoid brittle entity linking and provide robust features.

- Strength: Careful baseline construction and analysis using multiple heuristics across two CR systems, highlighting failure modes (especially pronouns).

- Strength: Provides useful domain-shift evidence comparing WCR vs. OntoNotes to motivate specialized approaches.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—the core method is a straightforward binary classifier with standard features and SVMs.

- Weakness: Small dataset and reproducibility concerns—WCR has only 30 documents, CV splits are small, and data release is unclear.

- Weakness: Clarity and organization issues—the main model and dataset description appear late and some terms/processes need clearer definition.

- Weakness: Ambiguity about mention sets—unclear how the union of WCR and Raghunathan et al. mentions is used and whether gold spans are involved.

- Weakness: Potential feature leakage—propagating hyperlink/alias attributes by surface match may bias performance; stronger ablation baselines are needed.

- Question: Are CV splits document-level and will you report standard deviations and release fixed train/dev/test splits?

- Question: At train/test time, what fraction of mentions are gold vs. automatically detected, and how are mention-boundary errors handled?

- Question: How are hyperlink-derived attributes propagated to mentions, how is disambiguation handled for ambiguous surface forms, and how are substrings avoided?

- Question: How is the inferred noun “type” extracted from the first sentence, how often does it conflict with Freebase, and which source is trusted?

- Question: How are pronominal compatibility/distance features computed using Freebase and what is the coverage when attributes are missing?

- Additional comment: Move task and model definition earlier and explicitly state two binary SVMs (pronoun vs. non-pronoun).

- Additional comment: Report versions of Dcoref/Scoref, SVM hyperparameters (C, class weights), feature normalization, and whether features are standardized.

- Additional comment: Provide ablations for non-pronominal features and include more failure cases (e.g., redirects/aliases and ambiguous place/organization names).
