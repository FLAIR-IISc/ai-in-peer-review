- Summary: Formalizes “main concept” (MC) resolution in Wikipedia and trains a Wikipedia-aware classifier to identify mentions referring to the article entity, comparing to adapted CR baselines and integrating it as a sieve in Dcoref.

- Result highlight: On a 30-article WCR dataset the MC classifier reaches ~89 F1, outperforms CR baselines, and improves Dcoref CoNLL F1 by +4 when integrated.

Contributions
- Strong Wikipedia-aware MC mention identification system (linear SVM with markup/KB features) with thorough ablations showing large gains over baselines.
- Effective integration of the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve in a full CR pipeline (Dcoref++), yielding notable end-to-end CoNLL F1 improvements.
- Provides a compact, annotated Wikipedia testbed and evaluation protocol exposing genre mismatch of news-trained CR systems.

Strengths
- Clear empirical gains on an underexplored, meaningful task with detailed feature ablations for pronouns and non-pronouns.
- Practical, effective exploitation of Wikipedia structure (titles, redirects, links) and Freebase attributes without full entity linking.
- Demonstrated end-to-end benefit: MC sieve improves precision and overall CoNLL metrics across multiple measures.
- Crisp task framing and testbed that reveal fragility of news-trained systems on Wikipedia.
- Thorough feature exploration for pronominal resolution showing stepwise improvements from positional and discourse cues.

Weaknesses
- Limited modeling novelty: relies on handcrafted features and a straightforward linear SVM; modern neural baselines are missing.
- Small dataset (30 articles, ~60k tokens) raises concerns about generalizability across Wikipedia and other genres/languages.
- Baselines could be stronger/fairer: missing simple markup-only and links-only heuristics and an EL-based baseline.
- Dependence on deprecated tooling/KB (Freebase, WikipediaMiner) without clarified coverage or ablations using alternatives like Wikidata.
- Clarity/organization issues: core model and dataset description are placed late, and some processes (candidate list, attribute transfer, singleton handling) are under-specified.

Questions to authors
- Data splits: Is 10-fold CV organized by article to avoid leakage, and are preprocessing resources article-specific which might leak across folds?
- Baselines: Can you add markup-only and links-only baselines and compare their performance to your classifier?
- KB reliance: What is Freebase attribute coverage in WCR, and how does performance change without Freebase or using Wikidata?
- Error analysis: Can you quantify main error types for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions and explain where Dcoref++ loses recall?
- Generality: How does performance vary with link density, article length, MC type (PER/ORG/LOC/EVENT), and on low-link or disambiguation pages or other languages?

Additional comments / suggested improvements
- Present the binary classifier setup earlier and move the dataset section forward to ground the approach.
- Clarify candidate list construction, hyperlink surface matching, and how coarse attributes are assigned/transferred.
- Explicitly cite exact Dcoref/Scoref configurations used.
- Clarify mention inventory and singleton handling: explain how the ~13.4k predicted singletons are added and interact with gold mentions during training and evaluation.
