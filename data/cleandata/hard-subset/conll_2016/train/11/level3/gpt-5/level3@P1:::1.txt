Summary
The paper revisits and formalizes “main concept” (MC) resolution in Wikipedia: given an article, identify all mentions (proper, nominal, pronominal) that refer to the entity the article is about. The authors build a Wikipedia-specific classifier that leverages markup (titles, redirects, internal links) and Freebase-derived attributes, compare it against baselines constructed from state-of-the-art coreference systems (Dcoref, Scoref), and integrate their classifier as a sieve in Dcoref. On a 30-article Wikipedia dataset (WCR), the MC classifier achieves about 89 F1, substantially outperforming adapted CR baselines, and improves end-to-end CR on Wikipedia when plugged into Dcoref (+4 CoNLL F1).

Contributions (as I see them)
1) A strong, Wikipedia-aware MC mention identification system that substantially outperforms fair baselines and provides clear ablations. The simple SVM with markup/KB features reaches ~89 F1 (+13–15 over baselines) and is well analyzed via feature families for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.
2) An effective integration of the MC classifier into a full CR pipeline (Dcoref++), yielding a notable CoNLL F1 improvement on Wikipedia (+4 absolute), indicating the MC chain as a high-precision discourse anchor benefits downstream sieves.
3) A compact, annotated Wikipedia testbed and evaluation protocol that expose the fragility of news-trained CR systems in this genre and motivate genre- and resource-aware CR.

Strengths
- Clear empirical gains on a meaningful, underexplored task. The classifier substantially outperforms baselines derived from Dcoref and Scoref across pronouns and non-pronouns (e.g., overall 88.79 F1 vs 75.77 best Dcoref baseline), with ablations that quantify the contribution of title/variants/types and positional/pronoun-specific features.
- Practical exploitation of Wikipedia structure without full entity linking. Using human-curated links, redirects, and Freebase attributes avoids the ambiguity and cascading errors of generic EL pipelines, a known issue in CR pipelines. The design is simple yet effective and well-justified for Wikipedia.
- Demonstrated end-to-end benefit in a CR system. Incorporating the MC sieve as the first, high-precision step in Dcoref improves CoNLL F1 from 51.77 to 55.11, with consistent gains across MUC, B3, CEAF, suggesting the MC chain decision improves later sieves and overall precision.
- Useful task framing and testbed. Framing MC resolution as binary mention classification is crisp, measurable, and relevant to applications (IE from encyclopedic text). The WCR dataset reveals strong genre mismatch: all state-of-the-art systems drop markedly on Wikipedia vs OntoNotes, motivating the approach.
- Thorough feature exploration, especially for pronouns. The pronominal feature families (base, main, position, tag, distance) yield stepwise improvements up to 88.99 F1, highlighting that Wikipedia discourse structure and local compatibility cues help pronoun resolution tied to MC.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty in modeling; reliance on handcrafted features. The technical method is a straightforward linear SVM with fairly standard string/KB/positional features. The novelty lies more in task focus and resource exploitation than in learning or inference. Stronger or more modern baselines (e.g., neural mention ranking) are not considered.
- Small dataset and unclear generalizability. WCR includes only 30 articles (60k tokens). While varied topics are claimed, it is hard to assess robustness across the breadth of Wikipedia or other encyclopedic genres/languages. Cross-article CV helps but may still overfit to Wikipedia conventions and link density.
- Baseline strength and fairness could be improved. The adapted baselines rely on existing CR outputs plus chain-length/title heuristics. Missing are: (i) a strong heuristic using title/redirect/alias matching alone (with pronoun heuristics), (ii) a direct “link-only” baseline that marks all mentions whose surface matches any internal-link surface pointing to the page, and (iii) an EL-based baseline to test the claimed advantage of avoiding EL.
- Dependence on Freebase and specific tooling. The system uses Freebase attributes and WikipediaMiner; Freebase is deprecated, and availability/coverage may vary. It would help to clarify performance when Freebase attributes are missing or replaced (e.g., Wikidata), and to report coverage statistics and ablations without KB attributes.
- Clarity and organization issues. The core model (binary classification for pronominal/non-pronominal mentions) could be stated earlier. The dataset description comes late relative to baselines and approach. Some terms/processes are under-specified: the “candidate list” and how hyperlink surfaces are matched to mentions; how coarse attributes are assigned; and the relation between Section 4.1 preprocessing and Section 6.1’s union of WCR mentions with predicted mentions (especially the role of added singletons).

Questions to Authors
- Data and splits: How is the 10-fold CV organized—by article (i.e., no sentence/mention leakage across folds)? Are any resources (e.g., redirects, variants) article-specific and thus at risk of leakage via preprocessing across folds?
- Baselines: Can you add a “markup-only” baseline that marks mentions matching any title/redirect/alias/name-variant (plus the pronoun expansion heuristic)? Likewise, a “links-only” baseline that projects internal-link surfaces to mentions, without CR output. How do these compare?
- KB reliance: What is the coverage of Freebase attributes for mentions and MCs in WCR? What is the performance without Freebase (Wikipedia-only), or replacing Freebase with Wikidata?
- Error analysis: Beyond a few illustrative cases, can you quantify main error types for non-pronominal and pronominal mentions (e.g., metonymy, near-synonyms, section-local entities overshadowing MC)? Where does Dcoref++ lose recall?
- Generality: How does performance vary with link density, article length, and MC type (PER/ORG/LOC/EVENT)? Any preliminary results on other languages or on disambiguation pages/low-link articles?

Additional comments
- Present the binary classifier setup at the end of the introduction to set expectations. Move the dataset section earlier to ground baselines and features. Clarify “candidate list” construction and the process of transferring attributes from links to mentions. Explicitly cite the exact Dcoref/Scoref configurations used. Clarify the mention inventory: WCR excludes singletons; you add ~13.4k predicted singletons—please detail how these interact with gold mentions during training and evaluation.
