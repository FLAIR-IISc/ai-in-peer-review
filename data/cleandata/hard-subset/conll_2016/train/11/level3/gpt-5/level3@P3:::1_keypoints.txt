- Defines the "main concept" (MC) coreference task on Wikipedia and annotates 30 pages (WCR); ~25% of mentions are MC.  
- Frames MC detection as binary classification over mentions using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features, with separate linear SVMs for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.  
- The proposed classifier substantially outperforms adapted CR baselines (Dcoref, Scoref) with ~13–15 F1 gains for MC detection.  
- Integrating the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref yields ~+4 CoNLL F1 on Wikipedia, showing practical end-to-end benefit.  
- Main contribution: formalizing MC resolution and releasing a carefully annotated evaluation resource (30 docs, ~7k non-singleton mentions).  
- Main contribution: a simple, knowledge-informed binary classifier that clearly beats strong baselines.  
- Main contribution: evidence that MC classification improves full coreference resolution by providing a discourse anchor.  
- Strength: task is practically important and aligns with real IE needs (biographies, dossiers, clinical narratives).  
- Strength: strong empirical gains with fair, informative baselines and illuminating ablation studies.  
- Strength: sensible exploitation of Wikipedia structure (links, redirects) and Freebase notable types to inject knowledge robustly.  
- Strength: shows broader CR impact by improving downstream clustering when used as an early sieve.  
- Strength: offers a path to bridge coreference resolution and entity linking via KB-derived attributes.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited — core technique is a straightforward binary classifier with basic features.  
- Weakness: small dataset (30 articles) raises generalization concerns across topics, languages, and low-link pages.  
- Weakness: clarity and organization issues — model and dataset details are presented too late and some terms are underspecified.  
- Weakness: ambiguity about the construction of the mention set (union of WCR and Raghunathan et al. mentions) and labeling of extra mentions.  
- Weakness: paper emphasizes tailored success on WCR but needs stronger evidence of transfer or cross-genre impact.  
- Question: clarify how the final mention inventory is created, how predicted mentions are labeled, and how overlaps/conflicts are resolved.  
- Question: define "candidate list" matching to hyperlinks — span/head/substring matching, nested mentions, inflections, and mapping rules.  
- Question: explain hyperlink-to-attribute propagation and how homonyms, ambiguous redirects, and cross-section noise are handled.  
- Question: provide details on baseline heuristics (e.g., pronoun "add-all") and report their isolated precision/recall impact.  
- Question: confirm cross-validation protocol and whether knowledge-derived features risk fold leakage or global tuning.  
- Suggestion: move dataset description earlier and state upfront that the model is a mention-level binary classifier with feature families.  
- Suggestion: add a running example to illustrate hyperlink-to-attribute propagation and precisely define the candidate list.  
- Suggestion: consider simple feature conjunctions and local syntactic/contextual features for pronoun errors.  
- Suggestion: report results by page type (PER/ORG/LOC) and hyperlink density and discuss replacing Freebase with a maintained KB.
