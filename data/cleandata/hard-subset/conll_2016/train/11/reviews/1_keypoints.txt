- Introduces "main concept" coreference task on Wikipedia: identify the coreference chain for the entity the article is about.  
- Annotated 30 Wikipedia articles with full coreference chains; ~25% of mentions refer to the article's main concept.  
- Presents simple baselines and a basic classifier that outperforms those baselines.  
- Integrates the classifier into Stanford's rule-based coreference system and achieves substantial gains over state-of-the-art on Wikipedia.  
- Task is practically motivated for IE (biographies, dossiers, clinical records) because it focuses on mentions that matter and filters out generic/irrelevant mentions.  
- The "main concept" provides a useful discourse anchor and a promising testbed for coreference methods that leverage knowledge.  
- Work could bridge coreference and entity linking research and revitalize coreference research on meaningful domains.  
- Concern about novelty pattern: creating a new task where standard systems perform poorly, but here the task itself is genuinely important.  
- Techniques are effective but not novel; features are sensible although richer feature conjunctions might help.  
- The primary system is a binary classifier but this is not stated early enough; model definition should appear sooner.  
- Organizational and clarity suggestions: move dataset description earlier, cite Stanford/Dcoref/Scoref explicitly, and clarify the term "candidate list" and how hyperlinks add semantic attributes.  
- Clarify Section 6.1 vs 4.1 ambiguity about the union of WCR mentions and mentions from Raghunathan et al. — it's unclear where extra WCR mentions come from.
