- Introduces "Main Concept Resolution" for Wikipedia and frames it as identifying mentions of an article's primary subject rather than full coreference chains.
- Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia-specific features (markup, Freebase attributes) and releases a new WCR dataset plus classifier outputs.
- Shows Wikipedia poses unique coreference challenges: state-of-the-art systems suffer a 15–17 point F1 drop on WCR vs. OntoNotes.
- Binary Main Concept classifier yields substantial gains (13–15 F1 over baselines); pronominal classifier reaches 88.99% F1.
- Feature ablation demonstrates the strong contribution of title and main-attribute features.
- Integrating the classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++) produces a practical 4-point CoNLL F1 improvement.
- WCR dataset is very small (30 articles, ~60k tokens), limiting statistical significance and generalizability.
- Comparison to prior Wikipedia coreference work is incomplete—no direct results reported for Nguyen et al. (2007) or Nakayama (2008) on WCR.
- Error analysis is minimal (only a single example) with no systematic examination of failure modes.
- Baseline comparisons are limited to two systems (Dcoref, Scoref) despite mentioning four state-of-the-art systems.
- Questions for authors: How was WCR sampled to ensure representativeness across article types?
- Questions for authors: What evidence supports claims of representativeness given only 30 articles?
- Questions for authors: Why were Nguyen (2007) and Nakayama (2008) not directly compared on the WCR/Main Concept Resolution task?
