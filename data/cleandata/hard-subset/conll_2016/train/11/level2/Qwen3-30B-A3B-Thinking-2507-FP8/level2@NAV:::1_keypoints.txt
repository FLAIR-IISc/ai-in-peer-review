- Paper frames identifying mentions of an article's main concept in Wikipedia as a binary classification task and proposes a classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase, outperforming coreference baselines.  
- Identifies a meaningful gap: news-trained coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia despite Wikipedia's importance for NLP.  
- Provides a structured dataset (WCR3) of 30 Wikipedia articles for the task.  
- Classifier design is well-motivated, exploiting Wikipedia structure to avoid cascading errors from named-entity linking.  
- Integration into Dcoref (Dcoref++) yields a practical 4-point F1 improvement in coreference resolution.  
- Dataset is extremely small (30 articles, 60k tokens), limiting generalizability and statistical significance.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with prior work specifically targeting Wikipedia coreference, making novelty unclear.  
- Does not address how the approach would scale to the full Wikipedia corpus, limiting practical applicability.  
- Overall: well-executed within scope but constrained by dataset size; authors should acknowledge this and propose scaling paths.  
- Questions for authors: how will you address the small dataset and demonstrate generalization to larger corpora?  
- Questions for authors: what specific characteristics of Wikipedia text make coreference harder than news beyond existing literature?
