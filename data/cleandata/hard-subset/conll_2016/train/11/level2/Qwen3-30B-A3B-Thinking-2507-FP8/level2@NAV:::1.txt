

Summary: This paper revisits the task of identifying all mentions of the main concept in Wikipedia articles, framing it as a binary classification problem where the goal is to determine if a mention refers to the main concept. The authors propose a classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase information, demonstrating it outperforms baselines built on state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.

Strengths: The paper identifies a meaningful gap in the literature—coreference resolution systems trained on news data perform poorly on Wikipedia, yet Wikipedia is a widely used resource in NLP. The authors provide a well-structured dataset (WCR3) with 30 Wikipedia articles, enabling meaningful evaluation of their task. Their classifier design is well-motivated, exploiting Wikipedia's rich structure to avoid cascading errors common in other approaches that rely on named-entity linking. The integration of their classifier into Dcoref (creating Dcoref++) demonstrates practical utility by improving coreference resolution performance by 4 F1 points.

Weaknesses: The dataset size (30 articles, 60k tokens) is extremely small, limiting the generalizability of results and raising concerns about statistical significance. The paper lacks a thorough comparison with prior work specifically designed for Wikipedia coreference, making it difficult to assess true novelty beyond what's mentioned in the introduction. The paper doesn't adequately address how the approach would scale to the full Wikipedia corpus, which is a significant limitation for practical application.

The work is well-executed for its scope but constrained by the small dataset. The authors should acknowledge this limitation more prominently and provide clearer pathways for scaling their approach. The task itself is valuable for Wikipedia-based NLP applications, but the results require validation on a larger corpus before being considered robust.

Questions to Authors: How do you plan to address the small dataset size limitation in future work, and what evidence do you have that your approach would generalize well to larger Wikipedia corpora? Could you elaborate on the specific characteristics of Wikipedia text that make coreference resolution particularly challenging compared to news text, beyond what's already established in the literature?
