- Paper introduces MC-resolution for Wikipedia: mention-level binary classification to detect mentions of the article’s main concept using two SVMs and Wikipedia/Freebase features.  
- Contributions: small annotated WCR dataset, an MC classifier that outperforms strong baselines, and improved full CR by adding a high-precision MC sieve to Dcoref.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical gains: MC classifier achieves ~88.8 F1, ≈13 F1 improvement over best Dcoref-derived baseline.  
- Feature design leverages Wikipedia markup and Freebase effectively, with interpretable ablative contributions for pronouns.  
- Integrating MC-resolution as an early sieve improves full CR (Dcoref++), yielding +3.3 to +4 CoNLL F1 on WCR.  
- Baselines and context are solid: careful adaptation of state-of-the-art CR systems and demonstration that off-the-shelf systems drop on Wikipedia.  
- Paper includes illustrative examples, qualitative error analysis, and greedy feature-addition experiments.

Weaknesses:
- Dataset is very small (30 articles, ~60k tokens), risking instability and overfitting; no variance/confidence intervals or significance tests reported.  
- Missing quantitative ablations for non-pronominal mentions (Table 3 absent), so key feature contributions are unverifiable.  
- Possible feature leakage: using intra-article hyperlink surface forms to derive attributes may unfairly advantage the model over baselines.  
- Baselines lack simple mention-level, Wikipedia-informed non-learning comparators to isolate classifier gains.  
- Reliance on Freebase raises portability concerns; unclear how results transfer to Wikidata-only setups.

Questions for authors:
- Are CV folds split by article to prevent per-article feature leakage between train and test?  
- Please provide the missing non-pronominal feature ablation table to quantify each feature family’s effect.  
- What is performance without Freebase features (Wikipedia-only), and how much gain comes solely from Freebase?  
- Are reported improvements stable across random splits and statistically significant?  
- Can you provide a breakdown of remaining major error types and the roles of redirects vs. inferred types in errors?

Additional suggestions:
- Add a simple high-precision baseline using exact matches to title/redirects/anchor texts to calibrate classifier value.  
- Clarify singleton labeling and class balance handling during training to assess class-skew impacts.  
- Report per-article performance variance and consider adding more articles to reduce sampling noise.  
- Analyze MC-chain-specific precision/recall changes vs. non-MC chains to ensure overall gains are not offset elsewhere.  
- Discuss mapping Freebase features to Wikidata to improve method longevity.
