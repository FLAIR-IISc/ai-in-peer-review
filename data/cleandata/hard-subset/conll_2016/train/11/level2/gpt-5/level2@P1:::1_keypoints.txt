- Contribution: Proposes a simple, effective MC resolution model for Wikipedia using Wikipedia structure and Freebase, yielding strong gains over baselines.
- Contribution: Presents a small annotated Wikipedia dataset (WCR) and shows off-the-shelf coreference models degrade on this genre.
- Contribution: Integrates MC resolution into a Dcoref pipeline (Dcoref++) and reports a +4 CoNLL F1 improvement on WCR.

- Strength: Clear task formulation and sensible binary classifiers by mention type, with substantial empirical gains (~+13–15 F1 on MC, overall ~89 F1; +4 CoNLL when injected).
- Strength: Leverages high-precision Wikipedia signals (links, redirects, first-sentence types) and simple contextual cues without full NEL.
- Strength: Practical and relevant — MC resolution helps downstream IE and improves a full CR sieve pipeline.
- Strength: Genre-focused evaluation highlights consistent degradation of recent CR systems on Wikipedia vs OntoNotes, motivating adaptation.

- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 documents ~60k tokens) and ten-fold CV with no held-out test or significance testing risks high-variance and unclear generalization.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Wikipedia links and Freebase; paper lacks quantification of missing/noisy attributes and alternatives like Wikidata.
- Weakness: Baselines may be weak—no pure alias/redirect/head-matching baseline reported and pronoun-chain heuristics might distort recall comparisons.
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues—feature inventory, exact predicates/thresholds, hyperparameter/model selection details, calibration, and IAA are not fully specified.
- Weakness: Limited end-to-end evaluation — only on WCR, prevents later sieves from appending to MC chain, and no comparison to retraining CR models or adding MC cues as features.

- Question: What fraction of mentions/MCs lacked Freebase entries or link-derived attributes, and how does performance change for those cases?
- Question: Can you report MC resolution performance by MC type (PER/ORG/LOC/MISC) and by mention type (proper/nominal/pronoun) per MC type?
- Question: Have you tried Wikidata as a replacement for Freebase, and were there differences?
- Question: Can you add a stronger alias-based baseline using title+redirects+link-anchored variants+head matching, and a simple pronoun majority baseline?
- Question: Did you try allowing later high-precision sieves to append mentions or using MC predictions as features, and how sensitive are results to sieve order and classifier confidence threshold?
- Question: Were significance tests run, what is per-fold variance, and are there results on a larger held-out set?

- Additional: Please release WCR, annotation guidelines, IAA, code, and an immutable URL with clear licensing.
- Additional: An error taxonomy and per-topic analysis (e.g., section-initial pronouns, noisy redirects, ambiguous heads) would clarify limitations.
- Additional: Scaling the dataset and replacing Freebase with Wikidata would increase the durability and impact of the contribution.
