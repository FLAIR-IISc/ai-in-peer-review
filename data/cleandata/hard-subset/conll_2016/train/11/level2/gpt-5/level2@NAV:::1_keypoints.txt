- Paper addresses "main concept (MC) resolution" in Wikipedia: identify mentions referring to an article’s main concept using two SVMs (pronominal/non-pronominal) with Wikipedia and Freebase features.  
- Authors compare against heuristic adaptations of SOTA coreference systems and report sizable gains on a new Wikipedia Coreference (WCR) dataset.  
- They integrate the MC classifier as a sieve into Dcoref, improving end-to-end coreference performance.

- Contribution: Focused MC resolution formulation with feature-based classifiers leveraging Wikipedia structure and Freebase, yielding strong gains over heuristic baselines.  
- Contribution: Empirical evidence of domain mismatch for coreference systems on Wikipedia and a practical sieve integration that improves a full CR pipeline (Dcoref++).  
- Contribution: Release of a specialized Wikipedia coreference dataset (WCR) and analysis/ablation of feature families.

- Strength: Clear empirical gains — ~89 F1 on MC mentions and +4 CoNLL F1 when integrated into Dcoref.  
- Strength: Effective use of Wikipedia/Freebase signals (titles, redirects, types, markup) that boost precision and recall.  
- Strength: Task is useful for downstream Wikipedia-centric NLP (IE, summarization, EL) and sieve integration shows practical impact.  
- Strength: Thoughtful ablation for pronouns and qualitative error analysis.

- Weakness: Small dataset (WCR = 30 documents) with annotation details and agreement deferred; no statistical significance reported.  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons rely on heuristic adaptations; missing stronger learned baselines (e.g., learned string/EL classifiers or end-to-end EL).  
- Weakness: Dependence on deprecated Freebase and markup may limit reproducibility and portability; robustness to sparse/noisy markup not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: Experiments are English-only and Wikipedia-only, with no evidence of transfer to other domains or broader CR tasks.

- Question: Please report annotation guidelines, inter-annotator agreement, train/dev/test splits, and significance testing.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to missing/noisy links or redirects; can you quantify markup ablation?  
- Question: Can Freebase features be replaced by Wikidata, and do results hold?  
- Question: Could you include learned “title/variants-only” and EL-based baselines to strengthen comparisons?
