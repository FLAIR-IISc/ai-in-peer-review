- Paper proposes a supervised per-mention “main concept” (MC) classifier for Wikipedia using Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes.  
- Authors release a small WCR coreference dataset and evaluate against CR-derived baselines (Dcoref, Scoref).  
- Classifier is integrated as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref, yielding improved CR metrics on Wikipedia.

Strengths:
- Clear focus on Wikipedia-specific MC resolution and demonstration of a genre gap between news-trained CR systems and Wikipedia.  
- Effective exploitation of Wikipedia/KB signals (hyperlinks, redirects, Freebase), with informative ablations showing feature contributions.  
- Strong empirical gains on WCR (≈88.8 F1, +13–15 points over baselines).  
- Downstream benefit shown: Dcoref++ improves MUC/B3/CEAF/CoNLL F1 on WCR.  
- Commitment to releasing WCR and Wikipedia-wide MC predictions to support reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- WCR is small (30 docs, ~7k non-singleton mentions), raising robustness and overfitting concerns; need clarity that folds are by document.  
- Baselines may omit strong Wikipedia-specific heuristics (title/redirect/anchor matches + article-level pronoun filtering).  
- Potential feature leakage: assigning types from intra-article hyperlinks may act like oracle linking for repeated surface forms.  
- Downstream gains tested only on small WCR; unclear impact on non-MC chains, longer/denser articles, or larger Wikipedia samples.  
- Missing reproducibility details: exact fold construction, feature scaling, Freebase coverage, and string-similarity thresholds; anonymized dataset link blocks review.

Key questions for authors:
- Were 10-fold splits constructed by document or by mention? Please re-run with document-level folds if needed.  
- What fraction of positive non-pronominal MCs are exact title/redirect/name-variant matches, and classifier F1 when excluding them?  
- How often do mention surface strings appear as intra-article MC anchors, and what is performance when removing attributes assigned via those anchors?  
- Does the Dcoref++ sieve prevent later sieves from correctly adding non-MC mentions to non-MC chains—any per-entity error analysis?  
- What is the coverage of Freebase notable types/coarse attributes in WCR, and how does performance change when Freebase is absent/removed?

Suggestions for improvement:
- Add a high-precision Wikipedia-specific string/link heuristic baseline and ablate direct title/redirect/anchor matches.  
- Expand WCR or add a larger held-out Wikipedia test set; ensure document-level splits and report learning curves.  
- Provide full feature specifications, code/configs for extraction and training, and results without Freebase to quantify KB dependence.  
- Extend Dcoref++ evaluation with per-chain diagnostics and experiments on a broader Wikipedia sample to assess scalability and side effects.
