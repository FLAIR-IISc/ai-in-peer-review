- Proposes a novel binary classification approach to identify mentions of the main concept in Wikipedia articles for coreference resolution.  
- Leverages Wikipedia markup and links to external KBs (e.g., Freebase) to extract features for the classifier.  
- Evaluates on a dedicated WCR dataset and reports outperforming state-of-the-art coreference systems on that dataset.  
- Integrates the classifier into a rule-based coreference system and improves detection of coreference chains in Wikipedia articles.  
- Contribution: adapts coreference resolution specifically to Wikipedia and provides a testbed for this task.  
- Contribution: exploits Wikipedia’s structured markup and external knowledge bases to boost performance.  
- Contribution: introduces a simple yet effective binary classification model for the task.  
- Strength: effective use of Wikipedia structure and external KBs demonstrates clear gains.  
- Strength: model simplicity with high reported performance and superiority over prior systems on WCR.  
- Strength: practical improvement when combined with rule-based coreference pipelines.  
- Weakness: WCR dataset is relatively small compared to standard coreference corpora.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Wikipedia markup and Freebase links may limit generality and robustness.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a single dataset without broader cross-dataset comparisons.  
- Question: how will the authors address WCR’s limited size and potential biases?  
- Question: can the method be adapted to other domains/datasets, and what would that require?  
- Question: what are the plans to evaluate and compare the approach on additional datasets and against other state-of-the-art systems?
