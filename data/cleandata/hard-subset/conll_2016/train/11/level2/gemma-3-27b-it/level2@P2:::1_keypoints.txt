- Contribution: Creates and releases the WCR dataset for main concept resolution in Wikipedia (30 documents) and extracts mentions for public use.  
- Contribution: Proposes a binary classification approach using Wikipedia markup and Freebase links to identify main-concept mentions.  
- Contribution: Shows up to 13 F1 improvement over baselines and moderate gains when integrated into a rule-based coreference system (Dcoref++).

- Strength: Clearly defines and motivates the main concept resolution task for Wikipedia.  
- Strength: Compares method against strong baselines (rule-based Dcoref and statistical Scoref).  
- Strength: Provides a detailed ablation study showing the contribution of different feature families.  
- Strength: Demonstrates practical integration into a larger coreference pipeline (Dcoref++).  
- Strength: Commits to releasing dataset and extracted mentions for reproducibility.

- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 documents), limiting generalizability and robustness of results.  
- Weakness: Method depends heavily on Wikipedia-specific markup and links, raising portability concerns to other genres.  
- Weakness: Feature engineering is ad-hoc and high-dimensional; lacks systematic feature selection or dimensionality reduction.  
- Weakness: Error analysis is limited; more comprehensive analysis needed, especially for errors requiring broader context.  
- Weakness: Related work section is incomplete—misses recent work on entity linking and knowledge-graph-enhanced coreference.

- Question: Do authors plan to expand WCR and can the annotation scale to many more Wikipedia articles?  
- Question: How robust is the approach to noisy or missing Wikipedia markup and absent Freebase data?  
- Question: What feature selection or dimensionality-reduction techniques were used, if any?  
- Question: What is the computational cost of feature extraction and how does it scale with document size?  
- Question: How does performance vary across Wikipedia article categories (e.g., biographies vs. scientific topics)?

- Additional: Recommend discussing ethical considerations and biases in using knowledge bases like Freebase.  
- Additional: Suggest improving Table 4 formatting for readability.  
- Additional: Note typo—use “coreferent” consistently instead of “co-referent.”
