- Revisits the under-explored task of identifying main-concept mentions in Wikipedia articles.  
- Frames the task as a binary classification problem enriched with Wikipedia markup and Freebase, yielding strong gains over baseline coreference systems.  
- Main contribution: adapts coreference techniques to Wikipedia by leveraging structure (links, redirects) and external knowledge, improving task performance.  
- Created and released the WCR dataset, a valuable resource for future research.  
- Shows practical utility by integrating the classifier into Dcoref and improving overall coreference resolution.  
- Strengths: clear problem definition, rigorous experiments, and comprehensive feature engineering; ablation studies validate feature contributions.  
- Results: compelling improvements over baselines and state-of-the-art systems; authors note limitations like noise from redirects.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient error analysis and some redundant result tables; dataset/resource links should be provided directly.  
- Questions for authors: detail error types and mitigation, report computational costs and scalability of Wikipedia/Freebase feature extraction, and supply direct dataset links.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; theoretically sound, practically relevant, and likely to inspire further research.
