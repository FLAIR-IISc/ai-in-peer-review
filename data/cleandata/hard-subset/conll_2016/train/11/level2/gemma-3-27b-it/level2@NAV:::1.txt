## Review of "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

This paper revisits the task of identifying mentions of the main concept within a Wikipedia article, a surprisingly under-explored area despite Wikipedia’s prevalence in NLP. The authors demonstrate a strong performance improvement over baseline coreference systems by leveraging Wikipedia’s markup and external knowledge from Freebase. The core idea – framing the task as a binary classification problem and enriching features with Wikipedia-specific information – is sound and well-executed.

The primary contribution is the effective adaptation of coreference techniques to the unique characteristics of Wikipedia text. The authors successfully demonstrate that incorporating features derived from Wikipedia’s structure (links, redirects) and external knowledge bases significantly boosts performance on this specific task. A second, significant contribution is the creation and release of the WCR dataset, providing a valuable resource for future research in this area. Finally, showing that integrating their classifier into an existing rule-based system (Dcoref) improves overall coreference resolution further demonstrates the practical utility of their approach.

The strengths of this paper lie in its clear problem definition, rigorous experimental setup, and comprehensive feature engineering. The ablation studies (Table 2) effectively demonstrate the value of each feature family. The results (Table 4 & 5) are compelling, showcasing a clear advantage over established baselines and state-of-the-art systems. The authors also acknowledge limitations, such as potential noise from Wikipedia redirects. 

However, the paper could be improved by providing a more in-depth discussion of error analysis, particularly focusing on the types of errors made by the classifier. The presentation of results could also be streamlined; several tables feel redundant. Further, while the authors mention the availability of datasets, a direct link would be beneficial. 

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details on the types of errors your system makes, and any potential strategies for mitigating them?
2.  What are the computational costs associated with feature extraction from Wikipedia and Freebase, and how scalable is the approach to larger datasets?
3. Could you provide a direct link to the datasets and resources mentioned in the conclusion?

Overall, this is a well-written and insightful paper with a clear contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance, with minor revisions to address the points raised above. The work is both theoretically sound and practically relevant, and has the potential to inspire further research in adapting NLP techniques to specialized text genres like Wikipedia.
