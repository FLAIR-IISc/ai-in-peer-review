- Frames main-concept resolution in Wikipedia as a binary classification task using Wikipedia markup and Freebase to outperform baseline coreference systems.
- Provides a new WCR dataset and resources, and shows benefits of integrating the classifier into a full coreference pipeline.

Strengths:
- Novel task focus: targeting main-concept mentions in Wikipedia highlights gaps in generic coreference systems.
- Effective feature engineering: Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived features are well-motivated and improve performance; strong ablation study.
- Clear experimental setup: solid baselines, evaluations on WCR and CoNLL 2012, and practical integration into Dcoref.
- Resource contribution: releasing WCR and extracted mention data aids reproducibility and future work.

Weaknesses:
- Dataset size/representativeness: WCR is small (30 documents) and selection criteria/generalizability are underexplored.
- Limited error analysis: only a few error examples; lacks systematic analysis of failure cases and mitigation strategies.
- Classifier detail lacking: insufficient SVM hyperparameter tuning and class-weight/penalty specification for reproducibility.
- Dcoref++ improvement nuance: the 4% F1 gain is modest and lacks deeper analysis of how the sieve integration changes errors or precision/recall.

Questions to authors:
- What were the selection criteria for the 30 WCR documents and how representative are they of Wikipedia?
- What hyperparameter values and tuning process were used for the SVM classifiers?
- Can you provide a more detailed error analysis and potential mitigation strategies for common failure modes?
- Which evaluation metrics (MUC, B3, CEAF, CoNLL F1) are most appropriate for main-concept resolution and why?
