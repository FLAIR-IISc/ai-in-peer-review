**Summary of the Paper**

This paper revisits the task of identifying all mentions of the main concept being described in a Wikipedia article, a task that has been seldom studied in the context of coreference resolution. The authors propose a novel approach that exploits the Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases such as Freebase to acquire useful information on entities. They design a classifier that outperforms fair baselines built on top of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems. The authors also demonstrate the benefits of their classifier in a full coreference resolution pipeline applied to Wikipedia texts.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles that leverages Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases.
2. A classifier that outperforms fair baselines built on top of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on the task of identifying all mentions of the main concept being described in a Wikipedia article.
3. An evaluation of the benefits of the proposed classifier in a full coreference resolution pipeline applied to Wikipedia texts.

**Strengths**

1. The paper tackles a specific and important task in the context of coreference resolution, namely identifying all mentions of the main concept being described in a Wikipedia article.
2. The authors propose a novel approach that leverages Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases, which is a significant contribution to the field.
3. The paper presents a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach, including a comparison with fair baselines built on top of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems.
4. The authors demonstrate the benefits of their classifier in a full coreference resolution pipeline applied to Wikipedia texts, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases, which may not be available in other types of text.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by their classifier, which could provide insights into how to improve the approach.
3. The paper does not discuss the potential applications of the proposed approach beyond coreference resolution, which could be an interesting area of future research.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend your approach to other types of text, where Wikipedia markup and links to external knowledge bases may not be available?
2. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the errors made by your classifier, and how you plan to address these errors in future work?
3. How do you think your approach could be applied to other NLP tasks beyond coreference resolution?
