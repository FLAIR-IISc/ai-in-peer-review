**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents a novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles. The task is to identify all mentions of the main concept being described in a Wikipedia article. The authors propose a binary classification approach, where a classifier is trained to decide whether a detected mention refers to the main concept. The classifier exploits features extracted from Wikipedia markup and characteristics, as well as from Freebase. The authors also measure the benefits of this classifier in a full coreference resolution pipeline applied to Wikipedia texts.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles**: The paper proposes a new approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, which is different from traditional coreference resolution systems.
2. **Effective use of Wikipedia markup and Freebase**: The authors show that exploiting Wikipedia markup and Freebase can provide useful information for coreference resolution.
3. **Improved performance over state-of-the-art systems**: The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on the task of identifying main concept mentions.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-designed features**: The authors propose a set of well-designed features that are effective in identifying main concept mentions.
2. **Effective use of external resources**: The authors show that exploiting external resources such as Wikipedia markup and Freebase can improve coreference resolution performance.
3. **Improved performance on a challenging task**: The authors demonstrate that their approach can improve performance on a challenging task such as coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset**: The authors use a relatively small dataset (WCR) to evaluate their approach.
2. **Limited generalizability**: The authors' approach may not generalize well to other domains or text genres.
3. **Simple classifier**: The authors use a simple Support Vector Machine classifier, which may not be the best choice for this task.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to identify all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the features they used and how they were extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase?
3. How do the authors plan to address the issue of limited generalizability of their approach to other domains or text genres?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and the results are well-presented. However, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the limitations of the approach and potential future work.
