**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution" presents a novel approach to coreference resolution specifically designed for Wikipedia articles. The authors focus on identifying all mentions of the main concept being described in a Wikipedia article, which they refer to as the "main concept" (MC). They propose a binary classification approach that exploits features extracted from Wikipedia markup and characteristics, as well as from Freebase. The authors evaluate their approach on a dedicated dataset, WCR, and compare it to state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems adapted to this task.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel approach to coreference resolution specifically designed for Wikipedia articles, which achieves an F1 score of 89% on the WCR dataset.
2. A comprehensive evaluation of state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on the WCR dataset, which highlights the need for adapting these systems to new text genres.
3. A simple yet effective extension of the Stanford deterministic rule-based system (Lee et al., 2013) that incorporates the proposed approach, leading to an improvement of 4% in F1 score on a fully fledged coreference task.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a well-motivated and well-defined problem, which is relevant to the NLP community.
2. The authors propose a novel and effective approach to coreference resolution specifically designed for Wikipedia articles.
3. The evaluation is comprehensive and well-executed, with a clear presentation of results and comparisons to state-of-the-art systems.
4. The authors provide a detailed description of their approach, including feature extraction and classification, which facilitates reproducibility.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on Wikipedia-specific features and markup, which may limit its applicability to other text genres.
2. The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, WCR, which may not be representative of all Wikipedia articles.
3. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by their approach, which could help to identify areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the WCR dataset, including its size, composition, and annotation guidelines?
2. How do you plan to adapt your approach to other text genres, such as news articles or social media posts?
3. Can you provide more insights into the errors made by your approach, including examples of difficult coreference cases?
