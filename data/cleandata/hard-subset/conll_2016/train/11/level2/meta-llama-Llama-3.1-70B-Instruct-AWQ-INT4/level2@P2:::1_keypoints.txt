- New task: define coreference resolution in Wikipedia as identifying all mentions of the article’s main concept.  
- Novel method: leverage Wikipedia markup and external KBs (e.g., Freebase) for coreference resolution.  
- Empirical claim: classifier outperforms state-of-the-art coreference systems on this task.  

- Strength: introduces a novel, relevant task with thorough analysis of its challenges.  
- Strength: effectively exploits Wikipedia markup to improve coreference resolution.  
- Strength: provides strong experimental results and comprehensive comparisons.  

- Weakness: limited scope may reduce generalizability to other coreference tasks.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on Wikipedia markup limits applicability in other domains.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses standard metrics but lacks deeper error analysis.  

- Question: how will the approach extend to identifying all coreference relations in an article?  
- Question: how are cases of incomplete or unreliable Wikipedia markup handled?  
- Question: how will robustness be evaluated against errors (e.g., POS tagging, NER)?  

- Additional: paper is clear and well-structured with well-designed experiments.  
- Additional: authors should provide more detail on limitations and directions for future work.
