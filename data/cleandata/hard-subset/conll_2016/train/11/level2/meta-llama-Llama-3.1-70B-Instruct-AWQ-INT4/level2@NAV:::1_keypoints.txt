- Paper proposes binary classification for identifying an article's "main concept" mentions in Wikipedia using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.
- Evaluated on the WCR dataset and compared to state-of-the-art coreference systems adapted for this task.
- Reports 89% F1 on WCR for the proposed approach.
- Extends Stanford deterministic rule-based system with the approach, yielding a 4% F1 improvement on a full coreference task.
- Well-motivated, clearly defined problem relevant to NLP.
- Novel and effective Wikipedia-specific coreference method.
- Comprehensive, well-executed evaluation with clear results and comparisons.
- Detailed method description and feature extraction that aids reproducibility.
- Heavy reliance on Wikipedia-specific features/markup may limit generalizability.
- Evaluation restricted to a single dataset (WCR), which may not represent all Wikipedia articles.
- Lacks detailed error analysis to identify failure modes and improvement areas.
- Requests more details about the WCR dataset (size, composition, annotation guidelines).
- Asks how the approach could be adapted to other genres (news, social media).
- Seeks examples and insights into difficult or erroneous coreference cases.
