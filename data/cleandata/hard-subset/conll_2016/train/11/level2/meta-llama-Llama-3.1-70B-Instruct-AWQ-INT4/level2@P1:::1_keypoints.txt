- Summary: Revisits identifying all mentions of an article's main concept on Wikipedia by exploiting markup and external KB links; proposes a classifier that outperforms baselines and improves a full coreference pipeline.

- Contribution 1: Novel approach leveraging Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases for coreference in Wikipedia articles.

- Contribution 2: Classifier that outperforms fair baselines built on state-of-the-art coreference systems for the target task.

- Contribution 3: Evaluation showing benefits of the classifier when integrated into a full coreference resolution pipeline on Wikipedia text.

- Strength 1: Tackles a specific, important coreference subtask—identifying all mentions of the main concept in an article.

- Strength 2: Introduces a novel use of Wikipedia markup and KB links, providing useful entity information.

- Strength 3: Presents a thorough evaluation with comparisons to fair, strong baselines.

- Strength 4: Demonstrates real pipeline-level gains when applying the classifier to Wikipedia texts.

- Weakness 1: Heavy reliance on Wikipedia markup and KB links limits applicability to other text types.

- Weakness 2: Lacks a detailed error analysis of classifier mistakes that could guide improvements.

- Weakness 3: Does not discuss potential applications beyond coreference resolution or broader impact.

- Question 1: How will the approach be extended to texts lacking Wikipedia markup and KB links?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide a detailed error analysis and plans to address common failure modes?

- Question 3: How might this approach be applied to other NLP tasks beyond coreference resolution?
