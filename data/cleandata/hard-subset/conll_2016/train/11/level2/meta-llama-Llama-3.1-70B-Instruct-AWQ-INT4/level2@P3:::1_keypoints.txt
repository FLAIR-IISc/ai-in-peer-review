- Proposes a binary classification approach to identify main-concept mentions in Wikipedia articles.
- Classifier exploits features from Wikipedia markup and Freebase and is evaluated in a full coreference pipeline.
- Contribution: a novel approach different from traditional coreference systems.
- Contribution: effective exploitation of Wikipedia markup and Freebase.
- Contribution: outperforms state-of-the-art systems on identifying main concept mentions.
- Strength: well-designed features effective for identifying main concept mentions.
- Strength: effective use of external resources (Wikipedia markup, Freebase).
- Strength: demonstrable improvement on a challenging Wikipedia coreference task.
- Weakness: evaluation uses a relatively small WCR dataset.
- Weakness: approach may have limited generalizability to other domains or genres.
- Weakness: relies on a simple SVM classifier that may not be optimal.
- Question: How will the approach be extended to identify all coreference relations in an article?
- Question: Request for more details on features and how they were extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase.
- Question: How will the authors address limited generalizability to other domains/genres?
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but needs more discussion of limitations and future work.
