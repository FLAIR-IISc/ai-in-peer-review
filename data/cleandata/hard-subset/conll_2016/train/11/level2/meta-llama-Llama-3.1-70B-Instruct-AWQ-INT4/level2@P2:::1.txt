**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, focusing on identifying all mentions of the main concept being described. The authors design a classifier that leverages Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases like Freebase to improve coreference resolution. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A new task definition for coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles, which involves identifying all mentions of the main concept.
2. A novel approach that exploits Wikipedia markup and external knowledge bases to improve coreference resolution.
3. A classifier that outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on this task.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel task definition**: The authors introduce a new task that is relevant to many NLP applications, and provide a thorough analysis of the challenges involved.
2. **Effective use of Wikipedia markup**: The authors demonstrate the value of exploiting Wikipedia markup to improve coreference resolution, which is a key contribution of this work.
3. **Strong experimental results**: The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their approach, comparing it to state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems and demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited scope**: The authors focus on a specific task, which may limit the generalizability of their approach to other coreference resolution tasks.
2. **Dependence on Wikipedia markup**: The authors' approach relies heavily on Wikipedia markup, which may not be available or reliable in other domains.
3. **Simple evaluation metrics**: The authors use standard evaluation metrics, but do not provide a more in-depth analysis of the errors made by their approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend your approach to other coreference resolution tasks, such as identifying all coreference relations in a Wikipedia article?
2. Can you provide more details on how you handle cases where Wikipedia markup is incomplete or unreliable?
3. How do you plan to evaluate the robustness of your approach to different types of errors, such as errors in part-of-speech tagging or named entity recognition?

**Additional Comments**

The authors provide a clear and well-structured paper that effectively communicates their contributions. The writing is clear, and the experiments are well-designed and thoroughly evaluated. However, the authors could benefit from providing more details on the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future work.
