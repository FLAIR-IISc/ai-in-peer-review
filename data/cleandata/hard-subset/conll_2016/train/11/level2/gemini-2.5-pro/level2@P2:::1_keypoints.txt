- Paper frames Main Concept Resolution in Wikipedia as per-mention binary classification and uses two SVMs (pronominal / non-pronominal) with features from Wikipedia and Freebase.
- Contribution: A simple, effective feature-based model leveraging Wikipedia structure and Freebase that substantially outperforms strong baselines.
- Contribution: A new annotated WCR dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles highlighting the need for genre-specific coreference methods.
- Contribution: Proof-of-concept integration of the MC resolver into Dcoref, improving full coreference performance on Wikipedia.
- Strength: Strong empirical results — 88.79 F1 on MCR, >13-point absolute gain over well-constructed state-of-the-art baselines.
- Strength: Well-motivated use of Wikipedia hyperlinks/redirects to access curated semantic info and avoid noisy entity linking.
- Strength: WCR and genre analysis show a 10–17 F1 drop for newswire-trained systems, motivating domain adaptation.
- Strength: Thorough ablation studies and qualitative examples that identify key feature contributions (e.g., title, inferred type).
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on deprecated Freebase, with no discussion of migration to Wikidata or reproducibility implications.
- Weakness: Very limited evaluation scale (only 30 articles), raising concerns about generalizability and robustness.
- Weakness: Methodology is feature-engineered and somewhat dated; experiments only integrate with the older Dcoref system rather than modern neural models.
- Question: Can the approach be adapted to Wikidata and do equivalent attributes (types, aliases, gender) exist there?
- Question: Did the authors try integrating MC predictions into a learning-based system (e.g., Scoref) as features or clustering constraints?
- Question: What is the topical composition of the 30-document WCR corpus (distribution of people, organizations, locations, events)?
