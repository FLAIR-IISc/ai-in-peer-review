- Task: Identify all mentions of the article's Main Concept (MC) in Wikipedia articles via binary classification for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.  
- Key idea: Use Wikipedia structure (hyperlinks, redirects) and Freebase features to avoid a separate entity-linking step.  
- New dataset: Introduces WCR, an annotated dataset for Wikipedia coreference and benchmarks existing systems.  
- Contribution 1: WCR dataset and empirical benchmark showing domain-adaptation challenges for newswire-trained systems.  
- Contribution 2: Feature-engineered SVM classifiers for MC resolution that exploit Wikipedia/Freebase signals and yield large performance gains.  
- Contribution 3: Integration of the MC classifier into a rule-based pipeline improves end-to-end coreference performance on WCR.  
- Strength: Well-motivated focus on the important sub-problem of MC resolution, enabling thorough analysis.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results with >13 point absolute F1 gain over baselines and detailed feature ablation.  
- Strength: WCR is a valuable resource and reveals that an older rule-based system (Dcoref) outperforms recent statistical systems on this domain.  
- Strength: Clever use of Wikipedia markup to map mentions to entities and avoid cascading entity-linking errors.  
- Weakness: Integration experiment limited to one rule-based system (Dcoref); unclear applicability to modern statistical/neural systems.  
- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 documents), raising concerns about robustness and generalizability.  
- Weakness: Reliance on deprecated Freebase; reproducibility would benefit from migration guidance to Wikidata.  
- Question 1: Clarify whether the Dcoref++ integration hard-blocks any additions to the MC cluster or only prevents moving identified MC mentions—were softer integration alternatives considered?  
- Question 2: Was any effort made to fine-tune statistical models (e.g., Scoref) on WCR to assess whether in-domain training could close the performance gap?
