**Summary of the Paper**
This paper addresses the task of Main Concept Resolution (MCR) in Wikipedia, which involves identifying all mentions within an article that refer to the article's main subject. The authors argue that standard coreference resolution systems, trained primarily on newswire text, perform poorly on this domain. They frame MCR as a binary classification problem and propose a feature-rich Support Vector Machine (SVM) classifier. The key idea is to leverage Wikipedia’s internal markup (e.g., hyperlinks, redirects) and linked external knowledge from Freebase to generate highly informative features, thereby avoiding the need for a separate, potentially noisy entity linking step. The authors demonstrate that their approach significantly outperforms several strong baselines built upon state-of-the-art coreference systems. Furthermore, they show that integrating their classifier as a high-precision sieve into a rule-based system improves performance on the full coreference resolution task for Wikipedia articles.

**Contributions**
1.  The primary contribution is a simple yet highly effective classification method for Main Concept Resolution in Wikipedia. The strength lies in its novel feature engineering, which directly exploits domain-specific signals from Wikipedia's structure and linked Freebase data to achieve state-of-the-art results on this specific task.
2.  A secondary contribution is the creation of a new annotated dataset, WCR, for coreference resolution on Wikipedia. By benchmarking existing systems on this dataset, the authors provide compelling evidence for the domain adaptation problem and offer a valuable resource for future research.

**Strengths**
1.  **Impressive Empirical Performance:** The proposed method achieves a substantial improvement in performance, with an absolute F1 gain of over 13 points compared to the strongest baseline on the MCR task. This large margin clearly demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach.
2.  **Thorough Evaluation:** The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation, including well-designed baselines, a detailed ablation study that justifies the contribution of each feature family, and an extrinsic evaluation showing the classifier's utility in a downstream full coreference resolution pipeline.
3.  **Methodologically Sound Use of Domain Knowledge:** The approach smartly leverages the unique structure of Wikipedia to gather high-quality entity information without relying on a general-purpose wikification system, thus bypassing a common source of cascading errors in similar pipelines.

**Weaknesses**
1.  **Small Dataset Size:** The evaluation is conducted on the newly created WCR dataset, which contains only 30 documents. While the authors state care was taken in sampling, this small size raises concerns about the generalizability of the results across the vast and diverse content of Wikipedia. The model could be overfitting to the specific characteristics of these 30 articles.
2.  **Reliance on a Defunct Knowledge Base:** The system's features are heavily dependent on Freebase, which is no longer maintained. This limits the long-term viability and reproducibility of the work. The paper would be stronger if it addressed the potential for migration to a current knowledge graph like Wikidata.
3.  **Limited Integration Experiment:** The authors demonstrate the utility of their classifier by integrating it into a single, rule-based coreference system (Dcoref). It is unclear whether similar gains would be achieved with more recent neural systems, which might be less amenable to such sieve-based modifications.
