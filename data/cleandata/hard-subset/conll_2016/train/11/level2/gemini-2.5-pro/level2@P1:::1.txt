### **Summary and Contributions**

This paper addresses the task of coreference resolution in Wikipedia, a domain where standard systems trained on newswire data perform poorly. The authors focus on a specific sub-problem: identifying all mentions that refer to the article's "main concept" (MC). They propose a system based on two SVM classifiers (for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions) that use a rich set of features derived from Wikipedia's internal markup and the Freebase knowledge base. The approach is evaluated on a newly created dataset of 30 annotated Wikipedia articles (WCR), on which it is shown to significantly outperform strong baselines constructed from state-of-the-art coreference systems. Finally, the authors demonstrate that integrating their MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into a rule-based coreference system (Dcoref) improves its performance on the full coreference resolution task for this domain.

In my view, the main contributions of this work are:

1.  **A new benchmark dataset and analysis for coreference on Wikipedia:** The paper introduces the WCR dataset and provides a valuable analysis demonstrating that state-of-the-art coreference systems struggle on this genre. This highlights a clear need for domain-specific research and provides a resource to facilitate it.
2.  **An effective, feature-engineered classifier for Main Concept Resolution:** The proposed system substantially outperforms several well-reasoned baselines. The ablation studies clearly justify the utility of the feature set, which cleverly exploits Wikipedia's unique structure (e.g., hyperlinks, redirects) to gather semantic information without relying on a separate, potentially noisy, entity linking step.
3.  **A practical demonstration of the utility of MC resolution for full coreference:** The paper shows that solving this sub-task can concretely improve a full coreference system on Wikipedia text, leading to a new state-of-the-art performance on the WCR dataset.

### **Strengths**

1.  **Well-motivated and clearly defined problem:** The paper convincingly argues that coreference on Wikipedia is an important and under-explored problem and that existing systems are not well-suited for it. The sub-task of "Main Concept Resolution" is a practical and well-defined simplification of the full problem, and its utility is clearly demonstrated.
2.  **Thorough and convincing empirical evaluation:** The experiments are well-designed. The baselines are fair and varied, covering different heuristics applied to both rule-based and statistical systems. The comparison against multiple SOTA systems on the new WCR dataset (Table 1) effectively establishes the difficulty of the domain and is an interesting finding in its own right. The feature ablation studies (Tables 2 and 3) are clear and show the contribution of each component of the model.
3.  **Creation of a valuable new resource for the community:** The introduction and promised release of the WCR dataset is a significant contribution. It enables reproducible research and provides a testbed for future work on coreference resolution beyond newswire text.
4.  **Clever use of domain-specific information:** A key strength of the proposed method is its effective use of Wikipedia's unique properties. Using internal hyperlinks to connect mentions to Freebase entities is an excellent way to obtain high-precision semantic information while bypassing the need for a separate entity linking component, thus avoiding a potential source of cascading errors.

### **Weaknesses**

1.  **Limited novelty of the machine learning method:** The core of the approach is a linear SVM with hand-crafted features. While the feature engineering is excellent and well-suited to the domain, the machine learning method itself is standard. The paper would be stronger if it discussed why this classical approach was chosen over more contemporary neural methods, especially since one of the main comparators (Scoref) is an early neural model. It is possible the dataset size is a limiting factor, but a short discussion of this would be welcome.
2.  **Dependency on a deprecated resource (Freebase):** The approach relies heavily on features extracted from Freebase, which is no longer maintained and has been superseded by Wikidata. For the work to have long-term impact and be easily reproducible, the authors should comment on the portability of their approach to Wikidata.
3.  **Brief analysis of the full coreference system integration:** The integration of the classifier into Dcoref to create Dcoref++ is a nice proof of concept, but the analysis is brief. The new sieve is placed first and strictly prevents other sieves from adding mentions to the MC chain. This is a strong, hard constraint. An analysis of the impact of this choice would be valuable. For instance, what kinds of errors does this design introduce, and what would performance look like if this constraint were relaxed?
4.  **Small dataset size:** The WCR dataset consists of only 30 documents. Although the authors state they sampled for variety, this is a small number, which raises some concern about how well the findings and the learned classifier will generalize to the full diversity of Wikipedia articles. While the use of 10-fold cross-validation is appropriate for a dataset of this size, the conclusions would be more robust if validated on a larger corpus.

### **Questions to Authors**

1.  Could you comment on the choice of an SVM classifier over more recent neural models for this task? Was this choice primarily motivated by the size of the WCR dataset, or were there other factors?
2.  Your approach relies on Freebase, which has been deprecated. Have you considered the feasibility of migrating your feature extraction pipeline to use Wikidata? Do you anticipate any major challenges in doing so?
3.  In the Dcoref++ system, you prevent other sieves from adding mentions to the MC chain after it has been formed by your classifier. Could you elaborate on the impact of this design choice? For example, what is the performance if you allow later, lower-precision sieves to modify the MC chain?
