- Task: Main Concept Resolution (MCR) in Wikipedia—identify mentions referring to the article's main subject.
- Approach: Frame MCR as binary classification using a feature-rich SVM exploiting Wikipedia markup and Freebase links.
- Avoids entity linking by directly using Wikipedia structure and Freebase-derived features.
- Empirical result: Significantly outperforms strong coreference baselines, with an absolute F1 improvement of over 13 points.
- Integration: Adding the classifier as a high-precision sieve improves performance in a rule-based full coreference pipeline.
- Contribution: Proposes a simple, effective MCR method driven by novel domain-specific feature engineering.
- Contribution: Creates and releases WCR, a new annotated Wikipedia coreference dataset for benchmarking.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation including strong baselines, detailed ablation studies, and extrinsic pipeline testing.
- Strength: Methodologically sound use of Wikipedia structure to obtain high-quality entity signals without noisy wikification.
- Weakness: Very small evaluation dataset (30 documents), raising concerns about generalizability and potential overfitting.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on Freebase, which is defunct, limiting long-term viability and reproducibility.
- Weakness: Integration tested only with a single rule-based system (Dcoref); unclear if gains hold with modern neural coreference models.
