- Task and dataset: Introduces the novel task of identifying "main concept" (MC) mentions in Wikipedia and provides a new WCR dataset annotated with OntoNotes guidelines.  
- Classifier approach: Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived features to identify MC mentions.  
- Integration: Shows that incorporating the classifier into Stanford Dcoref improves full CR performance on Wikipedia articles.  
- Empirical gains: Reports strong results — ~13-point F1 gain for MC identification and ~4-point CoNLL F1 gain when integrated into Dcoref.  
- Feature engineering: Thorough exploration and ablation of linguistic, positional, and semantic features from Wikipedia and Freebase.  
- Motivation and novelty: Well-motivated focus on Wikipedia CR, leveraging Wikipedia-specific signals not previously exploited.  
- Robustness: Improvements are robust across pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.  
- Reproducibility: Authors commit to releasing the WCR dataset and classifier outputs.  
- Limited dataset size: WCR is small (30 articles, ~60k tokens), raising generalizability concerns and lacking scaling strategies.  
- Limited error analysis: Error analysis is shallow; more discussion of ambiguous mentions and noisy redirects is needed.  
- Baseline scope: Baselines are existing CR systems not optimized for MC; additional tailored or rule-based baselines would strengthen evaluation.  
- Limited system integration testing: Only integrated with Dcoref; impact on other CR systems (e.g., Scoref) is untested.  
- Generalization question: Unclear how the classifier performs on other genres (news, blogs) or beyond Wikipedia.  
- Data expansion question: Suggests exploring semi-supervised/unsupervised methods to enlarge WCR.  
- Redirects question: Proposes filtering or weighting noisy Wikipedia redirects to mitigate negative effects.  
- Recommendation: Paper is a solid contribution and is recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
