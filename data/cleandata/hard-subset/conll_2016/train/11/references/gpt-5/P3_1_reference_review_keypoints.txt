- Task: Defines Main Concept (MC) Resolution in Wikipedia as identifying all mentions (proper, nominal, pronominal) referring to a page’s main concept and frames it as binary mention-level classification.
- Method: Proposes two lightweight Wikipedia-aware linear SVMs (pronoun vs non-pronoun) using Wikipedia markup, Freebase attributes, inferred lead-sentence type, and local/context cues.
- Data/results: Releases a small WCR dataset (30 articles), shows OntoNotes-trained CR systems degrade on this genre, and that adding the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve to Dcoref improves end-to-end coreference F1 on WCR.

- Strength: Well-motivated, practical task that leverages Wikipedia structure to avoid generic NEL ambiguity.
- Strength: Simple, interpretable models give substantial gains (+13–15 F1 on MC identification; +4 CoNLL F1 when integrated into Dcoref).
- Strength: Effective, high-precision use of Wikipedia signals (titles, redirects, internal links, lead-sentence hypernyms).
- Strength: Diagnostic comparison highlights genre mismatch between WCR and OntoNotes and shows deterministic system advantage on WCR.
- Strength: Initial ablation and error analysis for pronoun features with illustrative examples.

- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 docs) and 10-fold CV risks high variance/overfitting; no statistical significance testing or broader robustness checks.
- Weakness: Unclear whether all systems are evaluated on the same mention inventory; possible recall bias in baseline comparisons.
- Weakness: Baselines depend on upstream CR quality and may be weak; lacks CR-independent rule baselines and a Wikipedia-only learning baseline.
- Weakness: Reliance on deprecated Freebase and noisy attribute propagation from anchors without quantitative analysis of noise/coverage.
- Weakness: Methodological details are incomplete (inferred-type extraction, W&P similarity for names, attribute coverage, per-type performance, non-pronominal ablation table).
- Weakness: Dcoref++ modifications may affect other chains; evaluation only on WCR so broader impact is unknown.

- Q: Do all methods classify the same mention set at evaluation, and can results be provided with identical mention inventories?
- Q: What exact heuristic extracts the inferred type from the first sentence, and what is its coverage/accuracy/backoff behavior on WCR?
- Q: How are synsets assigned for Wu & Palmer similarity, especially for proper names, and what proportion of comparisons yield valid scores?
- Q: How are attributes assigned from internal links when anchors are ambiguous, and what are the precision/recall statistics for these coarse attributes?
- Q: What fraction of mentions/MCs lack Freebase attributes, and what is the performance drop when using only Wikipedia-derived features?
- Q: Do results generalize with more/diverse pages, by entity type, article length, or link density, and are Dcoref++ gains statistically significant beyond WCR?

- Suggestion: Replace Freebase with Wikidata and use infobox types as complementary signals.
- Suggestion: Add a stronger CR-independent rule baseline and a full non-pronominal ablation table.
- Overall: Timely and practical work that needs clarifications and stronger evaluation to be a solid contribution.
