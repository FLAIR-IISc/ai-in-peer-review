Summary and claimed contributions
This paper targets “Main Concept” (MC) resolution in Wikipedia: given an article, identify all mentions (proper, nominal, pronominal) that refer to the article’s subject. The authors frame MC resolution as per-mention binary classification, engineer feature families leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes, train separate SVMs for pronominal vs. non-pronominal mentions, and compare against baselines derived from two coreference systems (Dcoref, Scoref). They also add an MC-oriented sieve to Dcoref (Dcoref++) and show gains on a small Wikipedia coreference dataset (WCR).

Contributions as I see them (in order of significance):
1) A practical and effective MC resolution model that exploits Wikipedia and Freebase signals, achieving large improvements over adapted CR baselines on the MC task, especially for pronouns.
2) An evaluation testbed (WCR) and empirical evidence that state-of-the-art CR models trained on OntoNotes underperform on Wikipedia, motivating domain adaptation.
3) A simple integration of MC predictions as a high-precision sieve in Dcoref, improving CoNLL-style coreference scores on WCR.

Strengths
- Well-scoped task with clear formulation and strong empirical gains: Framing MC resolution as binary classification per mention is simple yet effective. The reported F1 of ~89 on all mentions, with especially high pronominal performance (F1 ~89), substantially outperforms baselines built from two strong CR systems (13–15 F1 absolute gains on the MC task). The greedy ablation for pronouns is informative and shows consistent gains from position, POS, and local distance features.
- Sensible use of Wikipedia markup and Freebase: The approach leverages redirects, anchor text, inferred types from lead sentences, notable types, and coarse attributes (gender/number/NER type) without relying on a full wikification pipeline. This reduces ambiguity and cascading errors, aligning with prior observations that Wikipedia’s human-curated links are high-precision. The MC representation is thoughtfully constructed and contributes materially to performance (e.g., title, inferred type, and variants ablations).
- Useful diagnostic evidence on domain mismatch: The side-by-side evaluation of multiple CR systems (rule-based and statistical) on WCR vs. OntoNotes highlights substantial degradation on Wikipedia and changes in system ranking. This is a valuable result for the community and underscores the need for domain-targeted solutions.
- Integration into a full CR pipeline yields measurable improvements: The added sieve that consolidates MC mentions improves CoNLL average F1 for Dcoref by ~4 points on WCR, with consistent gains across MUC, B3, and CEAF. This suggests that early high-precision MC decisions can beneficially constrain later sieves.
- Reproducibility intent: The paper commits to sharing datasets and predictions, which is essential for this niche task and encourages follow-up work.

Weaknesses
- Dataset size and generalizability: WCR is very small (30 documents, ~60k tokens, ~1.8k MC mentions). Results are all from 10-fold CV on this small set, which risks overfitting and limits confidence in robustness across topics and styles within Wikipedia. No evaluation on a larger held-out Wikipedia set, other languages, or across time.
- Limited baselines for the specific task: While the CR-derived baselines are reasonable, more direct MC-specific baselines are missing. For example: (a) a simple alias/redirect/anchor dictionary baseline that labels all spans matching MC aliases; (b) a mention-ranking model trained on WCR; (c) an off-the-shelf high-precision wikifier/EL system used to decide MC membership; (d) a logistic regression or neural classifier with only lexical/syntactic features to quantify the marginal value of Wikipedia/Freebase signals.
- Potential leakage from intra-article links and markup: The method enriches mentions by matching surface forms to internal links present in the same article and assigning attributes from the linked pages. Since human editors selectively link mentions (often the first occurrence), this could provide unusually strong supervision signals not available in other settings (e.g., non-Wikipedia or less richly linked corpora). It is unclear whether self-links to the article’s own MC are excluded and whether results depend heavily on link density.
- Details of the Dcoref++ modification and broader impact: The new sieve both merges MC chains and prevents other sieves from appending mentions to the MC chain. While it improves precision on WCR, it may reduce recall or adversely affect non-MC chains; there is no analysis of trade-offs nor evaluation on OntoNotes or other domains to demonstrate no harm. Statistical significance testing is not reported.
- Incomplete analysis: Beyond the pronominal ablation, quantitative ablations for non-pronominal features are only described qualitatively. There is no breakdown by article topic, mention type, or ambiguity class (e.g., organization vs. location names like “Houston”). Error analysis is thin for a specialized task.

Questions to authors
- Cross-validation protocol: Are folds split by article (document-level CV)? Please confirm no mention-level leakage across folds.
- Link usage: Do you exclude self-links to the MC within the article? How do you handle cases where the MC is not linked in its own article (typical Wikipedia style)? How sensitive is performance to intra-article link density?
- Baselines: Can you add an alias/redirect/anchor dictionary baseline that labels all spans matching MC name variants (with/without stopword filtering), and a wikifier/EL-based baseline? These seem directly competitive for MC resolution.
- Feature ablations: Please provide a full quantitative ablation table for non-pronominal features (title, inferred type, variants, entity type, main) analogous to the pronominal table, and a study without Freebase to assess reliance on external KBs.
- Dcoref++ generality: Can you report whether the added sieve harms performance on OntoNotes (or at least does not degrade substantially)? Also, what is the effect of allowing later sieves to append to the MC chain (precision/recall trade-off)?
- Statistics: Are improvements over baselines statistically significant (e.g., bootstrap or randomization tests) on the MC task and for Dcoref++?

Additional comments and suggestions
- Consider training a supervised end-to-end CR model on WCR to test whether the MC signal can be learned jointly and to contextualize the gains from adding a dedicated sieve.
- Provide per-article performance and topic-wise breakdown to gauge robustness. A larger, more diverse Wikipedia set would substantially strengthen the paper.
- Clarify the procedure for inferring the MC type from the first sentence (rules used, failure modes) and release this component.
- If release is central to the contribution, ensure the dataset, code, and predictions are available upon publication, with documentation on constructing features from Wikipedia dumps.

Overall recommendation
Weak reject. The task is interesting and the approach is well-engineered with solid gains on a bespoke dataset. However, the dataset is very small, the baselines for MC resolution are incomplete, and generalization beyond the narrow setting is insufficiently demonstrated. Addressing the baseline and evaluation concerns, expanding the dataset, and strengthening analysis would make this a strong contribution.
