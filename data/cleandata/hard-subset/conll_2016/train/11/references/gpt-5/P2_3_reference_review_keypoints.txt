- Paper addresses Wikipedia main-concept (MC) resolution as binary mention-level classification using Wikipedia/Freebase features with separate SVMs for pronouns and non-pronouns.  
- Introduces WCR, a small annotated dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles (~60k tokens), and shows news-trained CR systems perform poorly on it.  
- Builds baselines by heuristically selecting/aggregating chains from existing CR systems and reports MC identification F1 ≈ 89.  
- Adding an MC classifier as a high-precision sieve to Dcoref yields +4 CoNLL F1 on WCR.  
- Contribution: a practical, high-precision MC resolver leveraging Wikipedia/Freebase signals with clear empirical gains over adapted baselines.  
- Contribution: an empirical study showing cross-genre degradation of CR systems on Wikipedia and a simple sieve to improve a rule-based system.  
- Contribution: the WCR annotated Wikipedia resource for coreference and MC evaluation (pending release).  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem formulation exploiting Wikipedia structure relevant for downstream tasks.  
- Strength: strong empirical improvements with informative feature ablations, especially for pronouns.  
- Strength: successful integration of MC signal into a full CR pipeline with nontrivial end-to-end gains.  
- Strength: sensible use of human-curated hyperlinks and Freebase to reduce noisy entity linking.  
- Strength: useful qualitative examples showing successes and common failure modes.  
- Weakness: very small dataset (30 documents) limits representativeness, training robustness, and generalization claims.  
- Weakness: potential evaluation leakage if 10-fold cross-validation is done by mention rather than by document, risking inflated performance.  
- Weakness: baselines may be weak for the task; a stronger rule-based Wikipedia-aware baseline is missing.  
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps and reliance on deprecated Freebase; preprocessing and hyperparameter details are under-specified.  
- Weakness: limited quantitative error analysis and no statistical significance testing given small data.  
- Question: were the 10 CV folds split by document or by mention — can you rerun with document-level folds to rule out leakage?  
- Question: can you compare to a purely rule-based MC resolver using title/redirects/aliases and simple pronoun heuristics?  
- Question: how do you handle ambiguity when propagating attributes from hyperlink targets (e.g., Houston city vs. team), and what is the precision of this step?  
- Question: can the method port to Wikidata (which fields map to notable types, gender, profession)?  
- Question: can you provide per-topic performance and variance across the 30 articles, and breakdowns by entity type (person/org/location)?  
- Additional comment: include the non-pronominal feature ablation table for transparency.  
- Additional comment: report statistical significance (bootstrap/permutation) for Dcoref++ gains on WCR.  
- Additional comment: add a small held-out Wikipedia evaluation beyond WCR to support generalization.
