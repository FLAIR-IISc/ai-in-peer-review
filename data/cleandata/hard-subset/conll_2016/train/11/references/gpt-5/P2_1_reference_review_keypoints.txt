- Frames “main concept (MC) resolution” in Wikipedia as a binary mention-level task, introduces WCR dataset, and shows news-trained CR systems underperform on it.
- Uses two linear SVMs (pronoun vs non-pronoun) with Wikipedia-native signals and Freebase attributes plus simple document/positional/POS features to outperform adapted CR baselines.
- Integrating the MC classifier as a high-precision first sieve in Dcoref yields a +4 CoNLL F1 improvement on WCR, showing downstream CR benefits.
- Well-motivated, practically useful task with clear scope for Wikipedia-centric IE and evidence that newswire CR models degrade on Wikipedia.
- Strong empirical gains with a simple, interpretable, deployable modeling approach (~89 F1; +13–15 F1 over baselines).
- Effectively exploits Wikipedia markup (internal links, redirects, title/type cues) to reduce ambiguity and avoid low-recall NER/EL pipelines.
- Demonstrates downstream benefit to full CR by improving Dcoref when MC resolution is solved early.
- Provides solid analysis, multiple chain-selection baselines, pronoun heuristics, and qualitative error examples.
- WCR is very small (30 documents, ~60k tokens), raising concerns about robustness and generalization across topics, lengths, languages, and styles.
- Possible evaluation leakage: 10-fold CV appears to be over mentions rather than documents, which could leak document-level signals into training and inflate performance.
- Baselines may be insufficiently strong/fair—simple string-based heuristics or a lightweight learned baseline using title/redirect signals are missing.
- Reliance on Freebase is problematic for replication; unclear reproducibility and need for a Wikipedia-only feature ablation.
- Methodological novelty is incremental—primarily feature engineering and SVMs; novelty is mainly in assembling features and a new testbed.
- Requests confirmation that CV folds are split by document and reporting of mean±std across folds.
- Asks whether WCR, feature code, and trained models will be released and whether a Wikipedia-only feature set and results will be provided.
- Requests performance of simple heuristics that tag mentions matching title/redirects, and of a logistic/linear model using only string-match features.
- Asks for breakdowns/generalization across article types (people/orgs/locations), lengths, and additional held-out Wikipedia slices or learning curves.
- Seeks integration details: recall delta on MC mentions when preventing later sieves from appending to MC chain, impact on non-MC chains, and whether softer merging could improve recall.
- Overall: useful and practically impactful contribution with solid empirical gains but concerns about dataset size, CV clarity, and Freebase dependence; would favor acceptance if authors confirm document-level splits, release resources or provide Wikipedia-only alternatives, and strengthen baselines.
