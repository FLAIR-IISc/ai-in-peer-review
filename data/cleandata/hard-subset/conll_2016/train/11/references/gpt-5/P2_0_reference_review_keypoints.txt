- Defines "Main Concept (MC) Resolution" for Wikipedia: identify all mentions (proper, nominal, pronominal) referring to the article subject and frames it as a binary mention-level classification with separate SVMs for pronouns and non-pronouns using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.
- Introduces WCR, a 30-article Wikipedia coreference dataset with OntoNotes-style annotations, shows news-trained CR systems underperform on this genre, and provides baselines extracting MC chains from Dcoref/Scoref.
- Reports large gains on the MC task (~+13–15 F1 over baselines) and a +4 CoNLL F1 improvement on full coreference when integrating the MC classifier into Dcoref.
- Clear, focused task with practical utility for IE and downstream applications that need reliable in-article references to the page topic.
- Effective feature design leveraging Wikipedia structure (anchors, redirects, title variants) and Freebase types/gender without full entity linking; intuitive and useful pronominal features (position, tag, distance).
- Strong empirical performance: ~89 F1 on 10-fold CV vs mid-70s baselines, with consistent gains for pronouns and non-pronouns and informative incremental feature analysis.
- Integration as a high-precision sieve yields practical benefits to a competitive CR system, improving downstream sieves and full-system CoNLL scores on WCR.
- Provides a dataset and genre analysis documenting the performance drop of news-trained systems on Wikipedia, supporting the need for genre-adaptive methods.
- Dataset is small (30 documents) and evaluation may suffer from train/test leakage if cross-validation is not done at the document level; fold unit is not specified.
- Reproducibility concerns: anonymized dataset release location unclear, missing preprocessing details (matching policies, Freebase coverage), and a referenced ablation table (Table 3) is absent.
- Baselines may be weak or unfairly chosen given the paper’s reliance on Wikipedia signals; missing comparisons to simple Wikipedia-aware heuristics (bolded lead, infobox, lead aliases).
- Methodological novelty is limited—main contribution is engineering and resource creation rather than novel algorithms.
- Class-weighting description is confusing (claims to penalize negatives despite class imbalance); needs correction or justification.
- Request clarification on cross-validation protocol: were folds document-level to avoid leakage?
- Request exact class weights used for pronoun and non-pronoun models and clarification if positives were upweighted.
- Ask for coverage/error rates of hyperlink→Freebase attribute propagation and applicability/error rate of the pronoun distance feature.
- Ask whether Wikipedia-specific heuristics (bolded name variants, infobox fields, lead aliases) were tried as baselines and how they compare to B1–B4.
- Inquire whether Dcoref integration gains are robust to sieve ordering and whether preventing later sieves from adding to MC chains trades off recall or affects MUC/B3/CEAF differently.
- Request statistical significance tests for the improvements reported in Tables 4 and 5.
- Suggest detailing the extraction and accuracy of inferred common-noun "type" from the first sentence.
- Ask for the missing non-pronominal ablation results, more failure cases, and clarification of baseline pronoun propagation heuristics.
- Recommend commenting on portability from Freebase to Wikidata or other KBs given Freebase deprecation.
- Overall judgment: well-motivated and clearly executed with strong empirical results on a useful Wikipedia-specific subtask, but concerns remain about evaluation rigor, dataset size/availability, and baseline strength.
