Summary
The paper focuses on Main Concept (MC) Resolution in Wikipedia articles: identifying all proper, nominal, and pronominal mentions referring to the article’s subject. The task is framed as binary classification over detected mentions. The authors design features leveraging Wikipedia markup (titles, redirects, internal links) and Freebase attributes, with separate models for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions using linear SVMs. They introduce a small annotated Wikipedia dataset (WCR), show that standard coreference systems degrade on this domain, compare against baselines derived from two state-of-the-art CR systems (Dcoref and Scoref), and report substantial F1 improvements on MC identification. Integrating their MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref yields a 4-point CoNLL F1 gain on WCR. Resources are promised for release.

Main contributions (as assessed)
1) A focused evaluation setup for MC resolution in Wikipedia, including a small annotated dataset (WCR) and evidence that off-the-shelf CR systems underperform on this genre.
2) A simple, effective classifier for MC mention identification that exploits Wikipedia/Freebase-derived features and outperforms baselines derived from strong CR systems.
3) A practical integration of the MC classifier into a deterministic CR pipeline (Dcoref), improving end-to-end coreference on Wikipedia.

Strengths
- Clear empirical gains on the target task: The proposed classifier achieves ~89 F1 on MC identification, improving over the best baselines by 13–15 absolute F1 points (Table 4). Gains hold for both pronominal and non-pronominal mentions. Integration into Dcoref yields a 4-point CoNLL F1 improvement (Table 5), demonstrating downstream utility.
- Effective use of Wikipedia structure without full entity linking: Assigning attributes via human-authored links and exploiting titles/redirects/aliases provides high-quality signals while avoiding low-recall named-entity linking pipelines, aligning well with the Wikipedia genre and mitigating ambiguity.
- Baselines grounded in strong CR systems: The authors adapt outputs from Dcoref and Scoref into multiple heuristics (longest chain, title matching, aggregation with substring heuristics and pronoun propagation). This is a reasonable and challenging set of baselines for the MC task and highlights where generic CR systems fall short in Wikipedia.
- Feature analysis and ablations: The paper reports incremental contributions of feature families (notably for pronominal mentions), showing how coarse MC attributes, positional cues, and local compatibility features each add value. For non-pronominal mentions, qualitative examples illustrate cases where inferred types and name variants help beyond frequency.

Weaknesses
- Limited dataset size and potential generalization concerns: WCR comprises 30 documents (~60k tokens), with ~1,800 MC mentions. Ten-fold cross-validation over such a small sample risks optimistic estimates and limits statistical confidence. The work would benefit from larger-scale evaluation and reporting of variance across folds.
- Heavy reliance on Wikipedia/Freebase signals and English-specific markup: Features depend on internal links, redirects, and Freebase attributes. Coverage and quality of such signals vary across articles and languages. The paper does not quantify coverage (e.g., proportion of mentions benefiting from link-derived attributes) or robustness when these signals are sparse or absent.
- Potential task simplification and baseline gaps: Many MC mentions will overlap with the title/redirects or straightforward type cues. While baselines B2–B4 partially capture this, the paper does not include stronger non-learning rule baselines combining title/alias/type heuristics and pronoun filtering by MC attributes, nor simple ML baselines (e.g., logistic regression on a minimal feature set). A constraint-only or heuristic upper bound would further contextualize the observed gains.
- Evaluation details and clarity: It is not entirely clear whether MC identification is evaluated over gold non-singleton mentions only or over the union with automatically detected singletons; the metric definition and mention set for precision/recall computation should be explicit. Some tables/labels are inconsistent (e.g., references to Tables 2–4 and narrative about non-pronominal ablations without a clear table), and hyperparameters are sparsely specified (e.g., feature counts for non-pronominal families).
- Risk of document-internal information leakage: The preprocessing assigns attributes to all mention spans matching any surface form that is linked somewhere in the same article. Given Wikipedia’s guideline to link only first occurrences, this may propagate high-precision attributes from one linked mention to many unlinked ones, which is realistic for deployment but may inflate performance; an analysis of performance on mentions without direct title/alias matches or without link-propagated attributes is missing.

Questions to Authors
- Coverage analysis: What proportion of non-pronominal MC mentions exactly match the title or its variants/redirects? What is performance when title/variant features are ablated, and when link-propagated attributes are disabled?
- Attribute propagation: How often do mention spans receive attributes via internal-link matching within the same article? What fraction of mentions remains without such attributes, and how does the classifier perform on those?
- Evaluation set and metrics: Are precision/recall computed over all predicted mentions (gold + added singletons) or only gold-annotated mentions? Please clarify the exact mention inventory used for scoring and whether evaluation is micro- or macro-averaged across documents.
- Baselines: Could you include a stronger heuristic baseline combining title/alias exact/substring matches, inferred type matches, and pronoun filtering by MC gender/number/NER type, without learning? This would better isolate the added value of the SVM.
- Generalization: How does the approach perform on Wikipedia pages where the MC is not a person or where aliases are scarce? Any preliminary results on other languages or with Freebase absent?
- Resource release: Please specify the planned release details (dataset splits, annotation schema, code for feature extraction/classification, and the mention dump), to ensure reproducibility.
