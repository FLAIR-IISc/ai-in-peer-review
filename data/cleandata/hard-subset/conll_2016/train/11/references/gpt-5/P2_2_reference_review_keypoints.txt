- Defines "Main Concept (MC) Resolution": identify all mentions (proper, nominal, pronominal) in a Wikipedia article that refer to the article subject, treated as binary classification per mention.  
- Leverages Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes with separate models for pronominal vs non-pronominal mentions.  
- Introduces WCR, a small Wikipedia coreference dataset (30 articles, ~60k tokens) annotated with OntoNotes guidelines.  
- Shows that state-of-the-art CR systems trained on news perform poorly on Wikipedia.  
- MC classifier achieves large gains over Dcoref/Scoref-derived baselines on MC detection (F1 ~88.8 vs ~75.8).  
- Integrating the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref yields +3.34 CoNLL F1 on WCR (51.77 → 55.11).  
- Task is well-motivated and practically useful for downstream tasks like OIE and entity-centric analysis.  
- Method is simple and effective, pragmatically exploiting article title, redirects, anchors, lead-sentence types, and Freebase attributes.  
- Ablations (especially for pronouns) provide insight into useful features such as position, POS, and local distance.  
- Strong empirical improvements are consistent across precision and recall for both pronominal and non-pronominal cases.  
- Authors intend to release WCR, features, and a Wikipedia-wide dump of predicted MC mentions for reproducibility.  
- WCR is very small and limited in diversity, raising concerns about robustness, generalization, and variance reporting.  
- Baselines are limited and possibly unfair; no strong markup-only baseline and CR systems were not retrained on WCR.  
- Heavy reliance on Wikipedia/Freebase signals limits generality, and the paper does not quantify performance on low-link-density pages or migration to Wikidata.  
- Some methodological details are missing (e.g., Wu & Palmer similarity computation, non-pronominal ablation table, lack of statistical significance testing).  
- Risk of label leakage and optimistic estimates due to title/redirect features and small dataset; no external held-out validation presented.  
- Requested clarifications: provide a link-only baseline, report fraction of mentions with Freebase types and performance by link density, explain Wu & Palmer implementation for strings, present non-pronominal ablation table and significance tests, and report results or challenges of retraining CR models on WCR.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is promising and practically useful; leans toward acceptance contingent on clarifying baselines and analyzing dependency on Wikipedia/Freebase signals.
