- Task: Proposes Wikipedia-specific "main concept" (MC) resolution as binary classification over mentions, with separate SVMs for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.  
- Dataset: Introduces WCR, a small annotated Wikipedia coreference corpus (30 docs, ~60k tokens).  
- Performance: MC classifier outperforms baselines from general CR systems and yields high MC F1 (88.79 vs. baselines ~73–76).  
- Integration: Adding MC classifier as a high-precision sieve to Dcoref improves end-to-end coreference (CoNLL F1 +3.34).  
- Feature design: Leverages Wikipedia-specific signals (internal links, redirects, first-sentence type) and Freebase attributes effectively.  
- Pronoun gains: Large improvements for pronominal MC identification (pronoun F1 up to 88.99).  
- Ablations: Provides thorough comparisons and feature ablations showing cumulative contributions of feature families.  
- Domain mismatch evidence: Demonstrates general CR systems degrade on Wikipedia (CoNLL F1 from ~64 on OntoNotes to ~46–52 on WCR).  
- Pipeline benefit: Dcoref++ with MC sieve improves MUC, B3, and CEAFφ4 metrics, showing early MC decisions help later sieves.  
- Small dataset concern: WCR size (30 docs, ~1.8k MC mentions) raises generalization and overfitting concerns; no significance tests reported.  
- Heavy wiki/KB dependence: Approach relies on Wikipedia markup and deprecated Freebase, limiting portability to other domains or wikis.  
- Potential trivial cues: Possible leakage from anchor-linked mentions and redirect-derived name variants may inflate performance; controls not shown.  
- Baselines limited: Missing stronger/supervised or entity-linker baselines to isolate benefits of proposed features.  
- Method detail gaps: Key implementation details (inferred type extraction, pronoun distance feature specifics, mention-detection handling, fold stratification) are insufficiently described.  
- Evaluation controls requested: Need analysis excluding anchor-linked MC mentions to quantify dependence on markup.  
- Clarify inferred type: Request details on first-sentence pattern rules, coverage, error rates, and conflict resolution with Freebase.  
- Pronoun compatibility: Ask how distance/compatibility is determined (type/gender/number/profession) and reliance on Freebase for nearby mentions.  
- Cross-validation concerns: Ask whether folds are document-stratified to avoid redirect/alias leakage and whether significance testing was performed.  
- Dcoref++ side effects: Request analysis of how the MC sieve affects recall/precision for non-MC chains and per-chain error analysis.  
- Freebase replacement: Ask if authors tried Wikidata/DBpedia and what proportion of features would remain available.  
- Suggested extensions: Release larger WCR, report results by topic/article length, add significance tests, and separate analyses for linked vs. unlinked and mention types.  
- Suggested baselines: Add supervised baseline without KB features and/or a lightweight entity linker to better isolate Wikipedia/Freebase signal contributions.  
- Portability discussion: Recommend discussing migration to Wikidata and applicability to other encyclopedic or domain-specific wikis.
