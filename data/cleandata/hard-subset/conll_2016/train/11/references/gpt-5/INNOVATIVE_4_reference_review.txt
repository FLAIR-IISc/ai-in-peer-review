Summary and contributions
- The paper revisits the “main concept (MC)” resolution task in Wikipedia: identify all mentions (proper, nominal, pronominal) that refer to the article’s subject. It frames this as binary mention classification and leverages Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived attributes to build feature-rich SVM classifiers (separate for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions).
- It introduces a Wikipedia Coreference (WCR) dataset (30 articles, ~60k tokens) annotated with OntoNotes-style mentions and links to Freebase, and shows that state-of-the-art CR systems trained on news (Dcoref, Berkeley, Cort, Scoref) underperform substantially on this genre.
- It demonstrates strong gains over baselines derived from SOTA CR outputs for MC identification (F1 ~89 vs. 73–76), and shows that integrating the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref improves full CR on WCR by ~4 CoNLL F1.

Strengths
- Clear, focused problem with practical value and under-explored genre. Wikipedia is ubiquitous in NLP, yet most CR models target newswire; the paper quantifies the domain mismatch (large drops on WCR compared to OntoNotes across four recent systems). Defining and evaluating MC resolution as a first-class task is a useful, concrete step for Wikipedia-centric IE/OIE pipelines and downstream KB population.
- Clever, lightweight use of Wikipedia structure instead of full NEL. Exploiting internal links, redirects, and Freebase attributes as high-precision signals avoids the low-recall pitfalls of noisy entity linking pipelines and fits the Wikipedia setting particularly well. The feature design is sensible, and the ablations are informative: MC coarse attributes and position cues markedly help pronouns; title/inferred type/name variants/entity type/main attributes cumulatively boost non-pronominal performance.
- Strong empirical gains with simple models. Despite using linear SVMs and straightforward features, the method outperforms robust baselines by large margins (13–15 F1 points on MC identification), especially on pronouns (88.99 F1 vs. ~77–84). The improvements are consistent across both Dcoref- and Scoref-based baselines.
- Demonstrated impact on full CR. The integration as the first sieve in Dcoref (“Dcoref++”) yields meaningful improvements (≈4 CoNLL F1 on WCR), indicating that early, accurate identification of the MC chain can beneficially constrain downstream sieves. This strengthens the claim that MC resolution is not only intrinsically interesting but also useful in general CR for Wikipedia.
- Dataset and planned release. An OntoNotes-style Wikipedia coreference dataset with Freebase links, even if small, addresses a clear need; committing to release the data and the large-scale MC mention dump would provide a valuable community resource.

Weaknesses
- Dataset size and availability at review time. WCR includes only 30 articles, and details/URL are anonymized. With 10-fold cross-validation over 30 documents, risks of overfitting and optimistic estimates are non-trivial. The strength of the conclusions would be much higher with a larger, public dataset or at least an additional held-out set. Please also clarify whether folds are by document and whether any Wikipedia/Freebase-derived resources can introduce fold leakage (e.g., redirects/aliases computed globally but used in test folds).
- Heavy reliance on Wikipedia-specific markup and Freebase. The approach’s strength is also its limitation: features depend on internal links, redirects, title variants, and Freebase attributes, which may make the task substantially easier on Wikipedia than elsewhere. The generality of the method to other encyclopedic corpora (or to Wikipedia without certain markup) is unclear. Moreover, Freebase is deprecated; a pragmatic path to Wikidata or other KBs should be discussed.
- Baseline design leaves gaps. The baselines are derived from off-the-shelf CR outputs plus heuristics (B1–B4 and pronoun expansion), which are reasonable but may understate what a learned in-article mention classifier could achieve without Wikipedia-specific features. A stronger learned baseline (e.g., SVM/logistic regression with only non-Wikipedia linguistic features, or a simple neural mention classifier) would help isolate the incremental value of Wikipedia/Freebase features. Statistical significance testing of the main gains is also missing.
- Methodological clarity and controls. Several implementation details are glossed over: the exact number and definitions of “hundreds” of features; how “inferred type” is extracted from first sentences; how pronoun expansion affects fairness of baselines; whether within-article anchors to the MC are allowed as features for test mentions (potentially encoding near-gold signals); and whether any normalization avoids trivial string-match leakage. Some error analyses are provided, but a deeper breakdown (per-mention type, per-topic, per-article length/link density) would be helpful.
- Scope and positioning relative to prior MC work. While the paper cites Nguyen et al. (2007) and Nakayama (2008), the novelty is primarily in building a testbed and learning a classifier with Wikipedia features. A more explicit discussion of how this surpasses prior rule-based MC heuristics (beyond better numbers) and what new insights/general principles emerge would strengthen the contribution.

Questions to authors
- Data splits: Are CV folds strictly by document? Are Wikipedia-derived resources (redirect lists, aliases, notable types) built only from training folds or globally? How do you prevent leakage of MC variants from training into test for the same article/topic?
- Anchor usage: Do you allow anchor text that links to the MC’s page within the test article to influence features for test mentions? If so, can you report results when intra-article anchors to the MC are masked at test time?
- Dcoref++ analysis: Beyond aggregate CoNLL F1, how does the new sieve affect non-MC chains (precision/recall), and does freezing the MC chain harm recall for legitimate late attachments? Any observed regressions?
- Freebase: Can the pipeline be swapped to Wikidata with comparable coverage? Any preliminary results?
- Release: Will WCR and code be released at camera-ready? Please provide a precise plan and license.

Overall recommendation: Weak accept. The paper pushes a useful, under-explored direction (Wikipedia-specific coreference via MC resolution) with clear empirical gains and practical integration, despite limitations in dataset size and some methodological clarity. The contribution would be solid if the dataset/code are released and leakage controls are clarified.
