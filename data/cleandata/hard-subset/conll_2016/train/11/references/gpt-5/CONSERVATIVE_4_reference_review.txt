Summary and contributions
- The paper defines Main Concept Resolution (MCR) in Wikipedia: identifying all mentions that refer to the article’s main concept (MC). It frames this as a binary classification problem over mentions and builds SVM classifiers (separate for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions) using features derived from Wikipedia markup and Freebase, plus standard lexical/syntactic cues.
- It introduces a small Wikipedia coreference dataset (WCR) and reports that several state-of-the-art coreference systems trained on news perform poorly on WCR. The proposed MCR classifier outperforms heuristics derived from two coreference engines (Dcoref, Scoref) for MC identification. Integrating the classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref improves CoNLL F1 on WCR by ~4 points.
- The main contributions as I see them are: (1) a focused formulation and featureized approach for MC mention detection leveraging Wikipedia-specific signals; (2) a testbed and empirical evidence that off-the-shelf CR systems degrade on Wikipedia; (3) a simple integration into a rule-based coref pipeline that yields measurable gains on WCR.

Strengths
- Clear, well-motivated problem in a high-impact domain. Wikipedia is widely used, and the notion that CR models trained on news underperform on Wikipedia is plausible and worth documenting. The paper articulates a concrete subtask (MCR) that is useful for downstream IE/NEL pipelines and potentially simpler than full CR.
- Sensible exploitation of Wikipedia-native signals. Avoiding a noisy NEL pipeline by directly using internal links, redirects, and first-sentence type heuristics is reasonable in this domain. The feature design is grounded in prior work and easy to reproduce conceptually (string matches to title/variants/types, position cues, coarse attributes).
- Empirical improvements on the task. The proposed classifier substantially outperforms the baselines derived from Dcoref/Scoref on MCR (notably for pronouns), and the incremental feature ablations for pronouns are informative, showing consistent gains from position/tag/distance features.
- End-to-end integration into an established system. Incorporating the MC sieve into Dcoref and reporting standard MUC/B3/CEAF/CoNLL metrics demonstrates some impact on full CR beyond the bespoke MCR task, with a 4-point absolute CoNLL F1 gain on WCR.
- Useful diagnostic observation. The comparison table highlighting the drop of several leading CR systems on WCR (vs OntoNotes) is a helpful reminder of genre sensitivity in CR.

Weaknesses
- Dataset size, accessibility, and validation rigor. WCR contains only 30 documents, and key details are deferred to a “nonanonymized version” with anonymized URLs. This is too small to support strong conclusions about system rankings or generalization; statistical significance is not reported. The promise to release is not sufficient for reproducibility at review time.
- Potential leakage and unfair baselines. The model uses Wikipedia internal links and Freebase-derived attributes to annotate mentions, but the baselines (B1–B4) are constructed on top of CR outputs without access to the same rich markup/KB signals. This creates an uneven comparison; a stronger, purely rule-based baseline exploiting the same title/redirect/first-sentence type and Freebase attributes would be appropriate. Furthermore, using anchors from the article to annotate all matching surface forms can bake in human-curated entity identity; the paper should quantify how often mention attributes are derived from links and whether those links include the mention instance being evaluated.
- Cross-validation protocol unclear and likely optimistic. The 10-fold cross-validation appears to split by mention, not by document. Since features include article-level MC representations and distributional counts, placing mentions from the same article in both train and test folds risks substantial leakage and overestimation of performance. A leave-one-article-out (by document) setup is the minimum acceptable protocol here; results should be recomputed accordingly.
- Limited novelty and narrow scope. The learning approach is a straightforward linear SVM with standard features; the MC task is not new (Nguyen 2007; Nakayama 2008). The main novelty is feature engineering specific to Wikipedia markup. Given the small dataset and potential leakage, the methodological contribution feels incremental.
- Integration evaluation is constrained and lacks ablation. The Dcoref++ improvement is reported only on WCR, with no test on other Wikipedia articles or larger sets. It is unclear how much of the gain comes from preventing later sieves from adding mentions to the MC chain versus the classifier’s precision. No analysis is provided of the impact on non-MC chains, error propagation, or robustness when internal links are sparse.

Questions to authors
- What is the unit of cross-validation? Are folds created at the document level (i.e., no article appears in both train and test)? If not, please re-run with leave-one-article-out and report results.
- For how many mentions do you successfully assign attributes from internal links/Freebase? Please report coverage rates separately for pronouns, proper, and nominal mentions, and the proportion of evaluated mentions that are exact linked anchors.
- Can you provide a strong rule-based baseline that uses the same information available to your model (title + redirects + first-sentence type + Freebase attributes + simple pronoun heuristics), without ML? This would help isolate the value of learning vs heuristics.
- How sensitive are your results to the availability of Wikipedia links? Please evaluate a setting where link-based attributes are masked for mentions not explicitly linked, or where all internal links are removed, to estimate dependency on human-curated markup.
- For Dcoref++, please report per-metric statistical significance and an ablation where you add the new sieve but do not block later sieves from appending to the MC chain, to disentangle effects.

Additional comments
- Please clarify whether Freebase notable types and gender/number are available for all MCs and mentions; Freebase coverage is uneven and now deprecated. Consider a version using only Wikipedia-native signals.
- The paper would benefit from releasing the dataset and code at submission time (even under anonymous links) and expanding WCR beyond 30 documents to support stronger claims.
