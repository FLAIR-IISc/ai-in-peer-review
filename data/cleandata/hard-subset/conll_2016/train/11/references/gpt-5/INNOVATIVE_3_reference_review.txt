Summary and main contributions
- The paper introduces Main Concept (MC) Resolution: given a Wikipedia article, identify all mentions coreferring to the article’s main concept. The task is framed as binary classification for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.
- It builds a specialized feature-based approach that leverages Wikipedia internal links and Freebase to assign coarse semantic attributes to mentions without a full entity linking pipeline, and shows large gains over strong CR-based baselines.
- It integrates the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into a rule-based coref system (Dcoref), improving overall coref on a Wikipedia dataset (WCR). The authors also promise to release WCR and a large dump of MC mentions across English Wikipedia.

Strengths
- Clearly articulated new task and direction. Focusing on MC resolution in Wikipedia is a crisp, useful subproblem with clear downstream value (IE, OIE, summarization) and pushes coref beyond newswire. The work convincingly shows that state-of-the-art CR systems degrade on Wikipedia, motivating genre-specific solutions.
- Smart exploitation of Wikipedia structure. Avoiding full-blown entity linking and instead leveraging human-curated internal links to assign attributes is a bold, practical idea. The feature families (title, inferred type from first sentence, name variants, types from Freebase) are well-justified for Wikipedia and lead to strong empirical gains.
- Strong empirical improvements on the MC task. The proposed classifiers reach ~89 F1, improving 13–15 absolute points over baselines derived from Dcoref/Scoref. The pronominal ablation study is informative: each feature family adds value, culminating in high precision/recall.
- Impact on full coref within the genre. Incorporating the MC sieve as the first, high-precision operation in Dcoref yields a 4-point CoNLL F1 gain on WCR across MUC, B3, and CEAF, suggesting that early, reliable decisions about the MC help subsequent sieves.
- Resource promise. If delivered as stated, WCR plus a large-scale dump of MC mentions across English Wikipedia would be a valuable artifact for the community and could catalyze further work on Wikipedia-specific coref and IE.

Weaknesses
- Scale and representativeness of WCR. The dataset is only 30 articles (~60k tokens, ~7k non-singleton mentions). This limits the robustness of conclusions and raises concerns about overfitting to Wikipedia idiosyncrasies (e.g., redirects, link density). Please provide evidence that results generalize across topic types and article lengths.
- Reliance on Freebase and potentially fragile resources. Many features hinge on Freebase types and attributes; Freebase is deprecated. The paper should discuss migration to Wikidata/DBpedia and report coverage/ablation without Freebase. Current dependence may hinder reproducibility and long-term utility.
- Baselines and comparisons. The baselines are chain-heuristic variants of CR outputs; they are sensible but limited. Missing are comparisons to: (a) a straightforward entity-linking-assisted baseline (e.g., using Wikifier/GLOW or modern Wikidata linkers), (b) a simple neural mention classifier trained on the same features or contextual embeddings, and (c) a strong “title/redirect/alias string matching” heuristic to measure how far learned features go beyond surface cues.
- Possible information leakage and overuse of markup. The method uses internal links to assign attributes to mentions whose surface matches a link elsewhere. This gives the model access to human-curated linking signals that a generic CR system would not have, blurring the line between coref and distant supervision. Clarify at test time how often mention attributes are inferred from other links and whether this creates optimistic estimates.
- Evaluation limitations and missing details. Inter-annotator agreement for WCR is not reported. Only a pronominal ablation is shown; analogous non-pronominal ablation (noted qualitatively) should be quantified. The Dcoref++ improvement is measured only on WCR, not on a larger Wikipedia sample. It is unclear how the union of gold and predicted mentions is used at test time for fair evaluation of the MC classifier.

Questions to authors
- WCR details: annotation protocol, inter-annotator agreement, topic distribution, article length/link density statistics, and how articles were sampled. Will you release train/dev/test splits?
- Mention attributes: At test time, for an unlinked mention span, how do you decide it “matches the surface string of the link” and avoid mismatches to different entities sharing strings? What proportion of mentions receive attributes via this mechanism?
- Freebase reliance: What is coverage of Freebase types/attributes on WCR? Can you replace Freebase with Wikidata, and how would performance be affected? Please report an ablation without Freebase-derived features.
- Generalization: Have you tried evaluating Dcoref++ on a larger random sample of Wikipedia pages or across specific domains (PER/ORG/LOC)? Can you report performance by MC type and article length?
- Baselines: Can you add a wikification-based baseline (e.g., Ratinov et al., or a modern Wikidata linker) and a stronger string-match-only baseline using title/redirects/aliases without learning?

Overall recommendation
Leaning accept. The paper proposes a focused, novel task with a clean formulation, leverages Wikipedia’s unique structure in a creative way, and shows clear, sizable gains on both the MC task and downstream coref within the genre. While the dataset is small and some comparisons/resources are dated, I see this as a valuable new direction for coref in non-news domains, especially if the promised resources are released and Freebase dependence is addressed.
