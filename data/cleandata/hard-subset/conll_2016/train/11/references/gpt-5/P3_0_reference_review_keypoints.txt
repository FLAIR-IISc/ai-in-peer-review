- Task & contributions: Introduces Main Concept (MC) Resolution on Wikipedia, builds a 30-article WCR testbed with OntoNotes-style and Freebase links, proposes SVM classifiers for pronominal/non-pronominal MC detection, and shows gains when adding an MC sieve to Dcoref.

- Strength: Well-scoped, practical task with clear downstream utility and evidence that news-trained coreference models underperform on Wikipedia.

- Strength: Effective use of high-precision, low-cost Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived attributes to avoid full entity linking; informative ablation analyses.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains on MC identification (13–15 absolute F1 over baselines), with high pronoun recall and non-pronominal precision, plus qualitative success/error examples.

- Strength: Integrating the MC sieve into Dcoref improves end-to-end CoNLL F1 by ~4 points on WCR, showing early MC decisions help downstream sieves.

- Strength: Promised release of a new Wikipedia-genre resource and MC-mention dump to facilitate follow-up work.

- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 documents, ~60k tokens) and unclear CV protocol; mention-level folds may leak per-article features and inflate performance.

- Weakness: Baselines may be weak/incomplete; no CR-independent baselines (e.g., title/alias overlap or simple lexical/positional classifiers) despite title features driving gains.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Freebase and internal links limits portability and may bias evaluation; coverage for linked vs unlinked mentions not reported.

- Weakness: Some methodological details underspecified (construction of name variants, use of Wu–Palmer similarity, feature dimensionality and regularization), raising overfitting concerns.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of effects on non-MC chains; freezing MC chain may harm other chains and the breakdown of gains vs collateral effects is not shown.

- Question: How are CV folds constructed—are they document-level? If not, can leave-one-article-out results be reported for MC classification and Dcoref++?

- Question: What exactly comprises “name variants”? Are same-article anchor texts included, and if so, how is circularity avoided?

- Question: What fraction of mentions are linked vs unlinked in WCR, and can performance be reported separately for these groups by mention type?

- Question: How is Wu–Palmer similarity computed between MC fields and mention spans (sense inventory/disambiguation), and what is its ablation contribution?

- Question: Can the authors add CR-independent baselines: (i) pure title/redirect/alias overlap, (ii) simple first-NP heuristics, and (iii) local lexical+position classifier without KB features?

- Question: For Dcoref++, were classifier decisions produced on held-out folds per article? Provide error analysis of mislabeling-caused merges and an oracle experiment fixing the true MC chain.

- Comment: Replace Freebase with Wikidata and test sensitivity to KB coverage; report results with/without KB features.

- Comment: Scale WCR beyond 30 articles, add per-topic splits, and report statistical significance to strengthen claims.

- Comment: Release code and data with clear licensing to maximize impact.
