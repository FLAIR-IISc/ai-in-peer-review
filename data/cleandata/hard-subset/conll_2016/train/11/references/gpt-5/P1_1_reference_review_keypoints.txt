- Task: MCR in Wikipedia framed as mention-level binary classification with two SVMs (pronoun vs non-pronoun) using Wikipedia markup, inferred first-sentence type, and Freebase attributes.  
- Dataset: Introduces WCR (30 docs, ~60k tokens, ~7k non-singleton mentions, ~1.8k MC mentions) and shows off-the-shelf CR systems perform poorly on it.  
- Baselines: Builds B1–B4 by post-processing Dcoref and Scoref outputs to isolate/aggregate MC chains.  
- Main result: Classifier achieves ~89 F1 vs best baselines ~76, and adding it as a high-precision sieve to Dcoref yields +4 CoNLL F1 on WCR.  
- Contribution: Strong empirical MCR approach exploiting Wikipedia-specific signals and Freebase attributes yielding large gains over chain-based baselines.  
- Contribution: Empirical evidence that OntoNotes-trained CR systems underperform on Wikipedia and that an MCR module can improve full CR on Wikipedia.  
- Contribution: Curated WCR dataset and planned release of MC-labeled mentions across English Wikipedia.  
- Strength: Clear, practically meaningful problem formulation with sizable absolute F1 improvements and sensible ablation analyses.  
- Strength: Effective use of in-article Wikipedia supervision instead of full NEL; inferred first-sentence type is an insightful low-cost signal.  
- Strength: Thorough baseline design, use of predicted mentions, and 10-fold CV for robustness.  
- Strength: Integration into a full CR pipeline demonstrates downstream utility (+4 CoNLL F1).  
- Strength: Highlights genre mismatch effects between OntoNotes and Wikipedia, motivating domain-specific CR work.  
- Weakness: WCR is very small (30 docs), raising overfitting/variance concerns and lacking statistical significance tests, error bars, or learning curves.  
- Weakness: Potential leakage/bias from using in-article links and copied Freebase attributes; needs quantification of linked vs unlinked positives and attribute coverage.  
- Weakness: Baseline fairness — missing stronger baselines that use the same Wikipedia/Freebase cues or prior Wikipedia-specific heuristics to isolate SVM value.  
- Weakness: Missing methodological details (exact inferred-type algorithm, full ablation numbers, hyperparameters, fold-disjointness/tuning procedures).  
- Weakness: Limited generality — tight coupling to Wikipedia/Freebase (Freebase deprecated), only integrated into Dcoref, and limited error analysis by type/topic/linked status.  
- Question: What fraction of MC-positive mentions are exact title/redirect matches, covered by in-article hyperlinks, or unlinked, and how often are attributes copied from links to the MC page?  
- Question: Provide the exact inferred-type extraction algorithm (patterns, POS constraints, handling appositives/parentheticals) and its precision/recall on a gold set.  
- Question: Are MCR gains statistically significant across folds? Please report variance/confidence intervals.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to mention detection (gold mentions vs predicted), and what is the mention detector’s coverage/error rate on WCR?  
- Question: In Dcoref++, were later sieves allowed to append mentions to the seeded MC chain (vs freezing it), and can the sieve be integrated into Scoref or other systems?  
- Question: Given Freebase is discontinued, will you release derived features/attributes or provide scripts to migrate to Wikidata, and clarify the content/licensing of the planned data release?
