- Targets "Main Concept (MC) Resolution" on Wikipedia: binary classification over mentions with separate SVMs for pronominal vs non-pronominal mentions using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.
- Builds heuristic baselines atop Dcoref and Scoref and shows large improvements on a new Wikipedia Coreference Resource (WCR).
- Integrates the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref, improving overall coreference performance (+4 CoNLL F1).
- Introduces WCR: 30 Wikipedia articles (~60k tokens) with OntoNotes-style coreference annotations, MC labels, and Freebase links.

Main contributions:
- A simple, effective Wikipedia-aware MC mention classifier with strong gains and practical integration into a full CR pipeline.
- A focused evaluation showing a genre gap where OntoNotes-trained CR systems degrade on Wikipedia, motivating genre-specific approaches.
- A small Wikipedia-genre dataset (WCR) annotated for coreference and linked to Freebase.

Strengths:
- Clear problem formulation with practical downstream utility and avoidance of brittle global clustering.
- Effective exploitation of Wikipedia/KB signals (title, inferred type, redirects, Freebase types) with ablation evidence, especially strong on pronouns (pronominal F1 up to 88.99).
- Strong empirical gains: best classifier ≈89 F1 and +13–15 absolute F1 over Dcoref/Scoref-based baselines.
- Demonstrated benefit when integrated into a full CR system (+4 CoNLL F1 across MUC, B3, CEAFφ4).
- Diagnostic analysis showing which features help and qualitative error examples.

Weaknesses:
- WCR is very small (30 docs); no variance/significance testing reported, raising overfitting and generality concerns.
- Baselines are limited and potentially weak; missing stronger rule-based and minimal supervised baselines and prior-heuristics comparisons.
- Heavy dependence on Freebase and Wikipedia-wide signals; unclear robustness when KB coverage or markup is sparse and reproducibility issues given Freebase deprecation.
- Reproducibility and anonymization gaps: WCR withheld, placeholder release link, and missing detailed feature/SVM/hyperparameter specifications.
- Unclear labeling procedure and noise for ~13.4k automatically extracted singletons that dominate negative examples.

Questions to authors:
- Provide per-fold variances/confidence intervals and explain fold construction (by-article? topic stratification?).
- Report ablations: (a) without Freebase, (b) only local-page markup (no global anchors/redirects), (c) string-only performance.
- Add stronger baselines: rule-based using title/redirects/lead-sentence types, a minimal supervised baseline, and comparisons to Nguyen/Nakayama heuristics.
- Clarify how the 13.4k auto singletons were labeled, estimated noise rate, and sensitivity of results to that augmentation.
- Does adding the MC sieve harm non-MC chains (over-merging or blocking later corrections)? Provide per-chain/entity-type and precision/recall breakdowns.

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written; releasing data/code or processed features would greatly improve impact.
- Discuss limits of MC resolution for non-entity main concepts (events, works, abstracts) to clarify scope.
