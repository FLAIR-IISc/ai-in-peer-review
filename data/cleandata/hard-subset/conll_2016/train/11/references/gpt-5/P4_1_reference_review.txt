Summary and contributions
The paper targets a Wikipedia-specific variant of coreference: identifying all mentions in an article that refer to the article’s main concept (MC). The authors frame this as a binary classification problem over mentions, using two linear SVMs (separate models for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions) with features derived from Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes, plus standard lexical and positional features. They introduce a small, Wikipedia-annotated dataset (WCR; 30 articles, ~60k tokens, ~7k non-singleton mentions, ~1.8k MC mentions). The proposed classifier substantially outperforms baselines induced from state-of-the-art CR systems (Dcoref, Scoref) adapted to MC identification, and, when integrated as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref, improves end-to-end CR on WCR by +4 CoNLL F1.

Contributions (as assessed):
1) A task formulation and evaluation testbed for Main Concept Resolution in Wikipedia, including an annotated dataset and baselines derived from established CR systems (primary).
2) A supervised, feature-based MC-detector leveraging Wikipedia internal links/redirects and Freebase attributes, yielding large gains over strong baselines (secondary).
3) A simple integration of the MC-detector into a deterministic CR pipeline (Dcoref) that yields consistent gains on full CR in Wikipedia (tertiary).

Strengths
- Clear empirical gains on the target task: On MC identification, the proposed classifier achieves 88.8 F1 averaged over all mentions, outperforming the best Dcoref/Scoref-based baselines by 13–15 absolute F1 points. Gains hold for both pronominal and non-pronominal mentions (e.g., 89.0 F1 for pronouns vs. 84.2 for the best baseline), indicating effectiveness across mention types.

- Effective use of Wikipedia-native signals without full entity linking: The approach sidesteps noisy EL by exploiting human-curated internal links, redirects, and title/type cues, and augments with Freebase attributes when available. Ablations on pronominal mentions show systematic improvements from base features through MC attributes, positional, tag, and distance features, culminating in 89.0 F1, supporting the design.

- Demonstrated domain gap and practical benefit to end-to-end CR: The paper provides a useful diagnostic: state-of-the-art CR systems trained on OntoNotes degrade notably on WCR (e.g., Scoref CoNLL F1: 46.4 vs. 63.6 on OntoNotes). Integrating the MC-detector as a high-precision sieve in Dcoref improves CoNLL F1 from 51.8 to 55.1 on WCR, showing that MC identification can positively influence broader CR decisions.

- Task and resources are relevant and timely: Wikipedia is widely used in NLP; an MC-focused resolver is directly useful for IE and summarization, and the proposed dataset/baselines provide a starting point for community work on non-newswire CR.

Weaknesses
- Limited dataset size and incomplete description: WCR contains only 30 articles. While diverse topics are claimed, the size limits statistical power and may inflate gains. Details on annotation protocol, inter-annotator agreement, article selection criteria, and train/test splits are minimal, and the dataset is “to be described in the non-anonymized version,” hindering current reproducibility assessment.

- Potential over-reliance on Wikipedia markup and Freebase; unclear generalization: The method’s strongest features are Wikipedia-specific (title, redirects, name variants, internal links) and Freebase attributes. This narrows applicability to English Wikipedia (or similarly rich encyclopedias) and may not transfer to other domains or languages. The paper does not quantify the marginal contribution of Freebase vs. Wikipedia-only features nor provide results when Freebase coverage is missing.

- Baselines may not fully stress-test the approach: All baselines are post-hoc aggregations of chains from Dcoref/Scoref, plus simple title/substring heuristics and a pronoun-propagation trick. There is no supervised baseline trained directly on the same binary task using only shallow features (e.g., string/position) without knowledge bases, nor modern mention-ranking adaptations trained on WCR. This makes it difficult to isolate the true lift from the proposed knowledge features.

- Methodological clarity gaps: Several feature definitions are underspecified. Examples: how Wu-Palmer relatedness is computed outside WordNet; exact “compatibility” criteria used in pronoun “distance” features; how “inferred type” from the first sentence is extracted and normalized; how internal links are matched to mentions (case sensitivity, morphological variants). It is also not explicitly stated that cross-validation is by document to avoid leakage from article-level signals.

- Limited analysis and significance testing: The paper provides an ablation only for pronominal mentions; a corresponding breakdown for non-pronominal mentions is discussed qualitatively but not tabulated. There is no statistical significance testing of improvements. Error analysis is brief and anecdotal, with few insights into failure modes by entity type, section position, or link density.

Questions to authors
- Cross-validation protocol: Are folds defined at the document/article level to prevent any leakage of article-level features (e.g., redirects, name variants) across train/test? Please clarify the exact splitting procedure.

- Feature details: 
  • How is Wu-Palmer similarity computed for arbitrary strings/phrases? Which ontology/sense inventory is used? 
  • How is the “inferred type” extracted from the first sentence (patterns, POS/NP head rules), and how is it normalized? 
  • For the pronoun “distance” features, what precise compatibility checks (NER type, gender, number, Freebase attributes) are applied?

- Freebase dependency: What is the Freebase coverage on WCR, and how do results change when Freebase attributes are unavailable? Can you report an ablation with Wikipedia-only features?

- Baselines: Can you include a supervised binary classifier baseline that uses only document-internal string/positional features (no Wikipedia/Freebase) to better quantify the value of external knowledge?

- Dcoref++ integration: Beyond the new sieve, were any other sieves modified (e.g., to prevent later sieves from attaching to the MC chain)? How sensitive are the gains to sieve ordering?

- Dataset and reproducibility: Please provide details on annotation guidelines, inter-annotator agreement, and article/topic selection; and ensure that the planned release includes train/dev/test splits and code to reproduce features.
