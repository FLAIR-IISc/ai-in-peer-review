- Paper introduces Wikipedia Coreference Resolution (WCR) dataset and shows off-the-shelf CR models trained on newswire underperform on Wikipedia.
- Proposes a feature-based binary classifier (separate pronominal/non-pronominal models) leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase attributes to detect page main concept (MC) mentions.
- Integrates the classifier as an early high-precision sieve in Dcoref, improving CoNLL F1 on WCR (~3.3–4 points).
- Contribution: new Wikipedia-focused CR dataset and empirical demonstration that newswire-trained models degrade on Wikipedia.
- Contribution: practical high-performing MCR model exploiting title/aliases/redirects, inferred types, Freebase attributes, and positional features.
- Contribution: simple integration into a rule-based CR pipeline that yields consistent metric gains across MUC/B3/CEAFφ4.
- Strength: well-motivated, crisp binary formulation of MC resolution with strong end-to-end utility (F1 ≈ 88.8 vs ≈ 75 for baselines).
- Strength: sensible feature design using Wikipedia structure and KB signals; ablations show complementary benefits of feature families.
- Strength: solid empirical comparisons and ablation studies with error examples illustrating failure modes.
- Strength: classifier improves full-document CR when used as an early sieve; reproducibility intent to release data and predictions.
- Weakness: WCR is very small (30 docs, ~60k tokens), raising concerns about generality and stability of reported gains.
- Weakness: unclear labeling procedure and potential bias from adding ~13.4k automatically extracted singletons; risk of training/test leakage if cross-validation isn't document-level.
- Weakness: baselines do not fully exploit the same knowledge (redirects/aliases/types/Freebase), so learning benefit vs. knowledge use is not isolated.
- Weakness: reliance on Freebase and Wikipedia markup raises robustness and longevity concerns; Freebase is deprecated and signals can be noisy/missing.
- Weakness: limited analysis—missing deeper breakdowns (mention types, entity classes, link density), and no statistical significance testing.
- Question: Are cross-validation folds split by document to prevent within-article leakage? If not, rerun with document-level splits.
- Question: How were the ~13.4k automatic singleton mentions labeled for training given WCR lacks singleton annotations?
- Question: Can the authors provide a rule-based baseline using exactly the same knowledge sources (titles, redirects, types, Freebase) to isolate learning gains?
- Question: How robust is the model to removing/replacing Freebase (e.g., with Wikidata) and to missing/noisy internal links or redirects?
- Question: Do pronominal vs non-pronominal performance gains hold across entity categories (PER/ORG/LOC/MISC) and across pages with different link densities?
- Additional: Clarify whether pronoun-propagation heuristics were applied only to baselines and ensure parity in comparisons.
- Additional: Report per-article variance and correlation with page link density/length; add significance tests for main comparisons.
- Additional: Note Freebase deprecation and discuss substituting Wikidata for future robustness and longevity.
