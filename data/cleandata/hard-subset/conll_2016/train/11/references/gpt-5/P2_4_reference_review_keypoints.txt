- Paper formalizes “main concept (MC) resolution” in Wikipedia and introduces the WCR testbed annotated under OntoNotes guidelines.
- Shows state-of-the-art coreference systems trained on newswire suffer large performance drops on Wikipedia.
- Proposes two linear SVM classifiers (pronominal and non-pronominal) using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features to solve MC resolution as per-mention binary classification.
- Integrates the MC classifier as a high-precision sieve into Dcoref, improving end-to-end CoNLL F1 on WCR.
- Clear problem definition and strong motivation for a dedicated Wikipedia-focused MC subtask.
- Demonstrates large domain shift from newswire to Wikipedia, justifying the new approach.
- Strong empirical gains on MC identification (about 89 F1) with 13–15 absolute F1 improvements over Dcoref/Scoref baselines.
- Informative feature ablations showing title, inferred type, alias, and coarse attribute features each contribute value.
- Effective, simple use of Wikipedia structure (links, redirects, lead sentence) to infer entity types without full entity linking.
- Adding the MC sieve yields ~4 CoNLL F1 improvement on WCR, suggesting early high-precision MC decisions benefit downstream sieves.
- System design, features, baselines, and some qualitative error analysis are described and authors commit to releasing data/outputs.
- WCR is very small (30 documents, ~60k tokens, ~7k non-singleton mentions), raising concerns about training robustness and high-variance CV.
- Unclear whether cross-validation folds are document-level; potential leakage from article-specific features could inflate results.
- Baselines may be mismatched or too favorable; missing deterministic rule baselines leveraging the same Wikipedia signals as the model.
- Reliance on Freebase is problematic (deprecated); no exploration of alternatives like Wikidata or an ablation removing Freebase features.
- Generalization is unclear: improvements reported only on WCR, with no analysis of effects on non-MC chains or other genres (e.g., OntoNotes).
- Requests clarification on splits/leakage: whether folds are document-level and whether MC attributes are computed solely from test articles.
- Asks for a deterministic rule baseline using title/redirects/aliases/inferred type and simple heuristics for comparison.
- Requests Freebase ablation and/or substitution with Wikipedia-only features or Wikidata to assess robustness.
- Wants analysis of MC-chain vs. non-MC-chain F1 and error modes introduced by early MC decisions.
- Requests public release of WCR, code, and mention dump at camera-ready for reproducibility.
- Recommends reporting statistical significance and runtime/efficiency details.
- Suggests a short discussion on portability to other encyclopedic corpora and replacing Freebase to increase longevity.
- Overall: well-motivated and effective within-Wikipedia study, but limited by small dataset, leakage risks, missing heuristic baseline, and Freebase dependence; clarifications and additional experiments could make it acceptable.
