- Task & approach: Define "main concept resolution" (MC) for Wikipedia pages and cast it as binary mention classification using engineered Wikipedia/Freebase features and two linear SVMs (pronouns vs non-pronouns).
- Dataset: Introduce WCR, a small 30-document Wikipedia coreference corpus annotated with OntoNotes-style mentions and Freebase links.
- Empirical claims: Show state-of-the-art news-trained CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia; their MC classifier achieves ~89 F1 on WCR and improves Dcoref when used as an early high-precision sieve.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem formulation that is useful for Wikipedia-centric IE/OIE and cleanly framed as mention-level classification.
- Strength: Effective, pragmatic use of Wikipedia structure (redirects, aliases, in-article links, inferred type from first sentence) to get high-precision signals without full entity linking.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains on WCR with interpretable feature ablations (title/inferred type for nominals; attributes/position/compatibility for pronouns).
- Strength: Demonstrated positive impact on full coreference resolution by integrating the MC sieve into Dcoref (CoNLL F1 +3–4 on WCR).
- Strength: Resource contribution by releasing WCR and an MC mention dump to aid reproducibility and future work.

- Weakness: Very small dataset (30 docs, ~60k tokens) risks high variance and topic leakage; results may not generalize across Wikipedia without larger or leave-one-out evaluations.
- Weakness: Baselines may be weak—no deterministic mention-level rule baseline using the same Wikipedia signals to fairly compare against the classifier.
- Weakness: Possible feature leakage/bias from in-article hyperlinks and imported Freebase types; need to quantify reliance on link-derived features versus title/alias/type-only.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation of Dcoref++ — no statistical significance testing, no out-of-domain checks, and unclear precision/recall stability across topics.
- Weakness: Dependency on deprecated Freebase and several underspecified implementation details (how inferred type is extracted, W&P similarity setup, pronoun-compatibility test, hyperparameter tuning).

- Q: Provide a rule-based mention-level baseline using title/aliases/redirects/inferred type/coarse attributes and report its performance.
- Q: Precisely describe how the MC type is inferred from the first sentence, with patterns, coverage, accuracy, and handling of appositives/atypical leads.
- Q: Ablate link-derived features (in-article hyperlinks and imported attributes) to quantify their contribution versus title/alias/type-only features.
- Q: Evaluate Dcoref++ on OntoNotes or other non-Wikipedia genres (or at least WCR-like vs non-WCR portions) to check for harm to general-domain CR.
- Q: Report statistical significance across folds for the MC classifier and Dcoref++ (e.g., stratified bootstrap over documents).
- Q: Explain hyperparameter selection (C, class weights), and confirm no tuning occurred outside the cross-validation loop.

- Suggestion: Replace Freebase with Wikidata (or DBpedia) and document the mapping to improve longevity and impact.
- Suggestion: Provide per-genre/topic and per-mention-type breakdowns in WCR (people/orgs/locations/events; named/nominal/pronoun) to show strengths and weaknesses.
- Suggestion: Discuss failure cases involving metonymy (e.g., team vs city) and possible mitigations (contextual cues, section headers).
- Minor clarification: Explicitly state whether gold mention boundaries are used during training/inference and confirm evaluation is on predicted mentions only.
