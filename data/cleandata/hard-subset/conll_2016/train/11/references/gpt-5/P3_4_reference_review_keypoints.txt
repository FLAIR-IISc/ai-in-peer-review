- Task & contributions: Proposes supervised mention-level classifiers (separate for pronouns/non-pronouns) using Wikipedia markup and Freebase, builds a small WCR dataset, and integrates the classifier as a high-precision sieve in Dcoref improving CoNLL F1.

- Strength — task formulation: Well-scoped and practical framing of “main concept resolution” as binary mention classification for Wikipedia-centric IE/OIE.

- Strength — use of Wikipedia structure: Effectively leverages titles, redirects, internal links, first-sentence heuristics, and Freebase/Wikipedia types for high-precision supervision.

- Strength — empirical gains: Large F1 improvements on WCR over baselines and state-of-the-art CR systems, especially for pronouns; informative pronoun feature ablations.

- Strength — end-to-end benefit: Adding the classifier early in Dcoref and freezing the MC chain yields measurable CoNLL F1 gains, showing practical impact on coreference pipelines.

- Strength — genre diagnostic: Demonstrates that OntoNotes-trained CR systems degrade on Wikipedia genre, motivating genre-specific adaptation.

- Weakness — dataset size/representativeness: WCR is small (30 documents, 60k tokens) which limits robustness and generalizability of supervised results.

- Weakness — potential leakage: 10-fold CV protocol not described at document level; article-level features could leak across folds and inflate performance.

- Weakness — weak baselines: Baselines B1–B4 underuse available markup; a stronger deterministic baseline using title/alias matches, type inference, and simple pronoun rules is missing.

- Weakness — incomplete analysis/reporting: Non-pronominal feature ablations missing, no breakdown by MC type or link density, no significance testing, and limited error analysis.

- Weakness — reliance on deprecated resources: Heavy dependence on Freebase and Wikipedia-specific signals limits portability and long-term maintainability.

- Weakness — Dcoref++ design brittleness: Freezing the MC chain may block correct later attachments; softer mechanisms or analysis of prevented attachments are not provided.

- Question — CV protocol: Are folds at document level to avoid leakage? If not, results may be invalid and need re-running with doc-level splits.

- Question — stronger rule baseline: How does a deterministic matcher using title/redirects/aliases, inferred types, and simple pronoun heuristics perform?

- Question — ablations & coverage: Request non-pronominal feature ablations and coverage stats for internal-link matches, inferred types, and Freebase types.

- Question — robustness analyses: Provide performance by MC NER type and by article internal link density to assess sparsely linked pages.

- Question — resource substitution: Can the pipeline operate with Wikidata instead of Freebase, and what performance change occurs if Freebase features are removed?

- Question — significance & variance: Are reported improvements statistically significant across CV folds?

- Overall assessment: Moderate accept pending clarification/mitigation of leakage and addition of stronger baselines and ablations; task and method are promising but empirical case needs strengthening.
