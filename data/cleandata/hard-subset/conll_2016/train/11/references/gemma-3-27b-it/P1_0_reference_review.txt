## Peer Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the task of identifying mentions of the “main concept” (MC) within Wikipedia articles, a task the authors argue is underexplored despite Wikipedia’s widespread use in NLP. The authors frame this as a binary classification problem (MC mention vs. not MC mention) and propose a system leveraging features extracted from Wikipedia markup, Freebase, and existing coreference resolution systems. They demonstrate improved performance over state-of-the-art generic CR engines and show that integrating their classifier into a rule-based CR system (Lee et al., 2013) yields further gains.  The authors also provide a new dataset (WCR) for this task.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **A Dedicated Evaluation Dataset (WCR):** The creation and release of the WCR dataset is the strongest contribution.  A resource specifically designed for evaluating MC resolution systems is invaluable, and fills a clear gap in the field.
2. **Demonstration of Domain Specificity:** The paper clearly demonstrates that generic coreference resolution systems perform poorly on Wikipedia text, reinforcing the need for domain adaptation.
3. **Effective Feature Engineering:** The combination of features from Wikipedia markup, Freebase, and existing CR systems shows a practical approach to tackling the problem and achieves strong results.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper articulates a well-defined and relevant problem – adapting coreference resolution to the unique characteristics of Wikipedia. The motivation is strong, emphasizing the importance of Wikipedia as a knowledge source and the limitations of existing systems.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over strong baselines.  The performance gains reported are significant, especially considering the challenging nature of the task.
3. **Comprehensive Feature Set:** The feature engineering process appears well-considered and thorough. The combination of lexical, syntactic, and knowledge-based features provides a robust representation of mentions. The analysis of feature importance (Table 2) is insightful.
4. **Integration with Existing System:** The successful integration of the classifier into an existing rule-based system (Dcoref++) shows the practical applicability of the work and suggests a pathway for improving existing CR pipelines.
5. **Dataset Availability:**  The commitment to release the dataset and associated resources is commendable and will foster further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** The WCR dataset, while valuable, is relatively small (30 documents). This limits the generalizability of the findings and raises concerns about the robustness of the results.  The authors acknowledge this, but it remains a significant limitation.
2. **Over-reliance on Wikipedia/Freebase:** While leveraging these resources is sensible for Wikipedia data, the approach’s performance on other domains with less structured knowledge is questionable. The paper doesn't address this potential limitation.
3. **Classifier Complexity:** The description of the feature extraction process (Section 4.2) is quite dense and difficult to follow. A clearer explanation of the feature interactions and their rationale would be beneficial.  A table summarizing the feature categories and their counts would be helpful.
4. **Baseline Limitations:**  The baselines, while reasonable, are somewhat simplistic. Exploring more sophisticated baselines (e.g., fine-tuning a pre-trained language model for the binary classification task) would provide a stronger benchmark for comparison.
5. **Lack of Error Analysis:** The paper mentions some error examples (Figure 3), but a more comprehensive error analysis would provide valuable insights into the system’s weaknesses and guide future improvements.  



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dataset Expansion:** Do you have plans to expand the WCR dataset? If so, what is the strategy for selecting additional articles to ensure diversity and representativeness?
2. **Generalizability:**  How do you anticipate your approach would perform on other text genres (e.g., news articles, scientific papers) where Wikipedia/Freebase aren’t readily available? Have you explored any strategies for adapting the system to such scenarios?
3. **Feature Interaction:** Could you provide a more detailed explanation of how the different feature families interact with each other to influence the classification decision?  Are there any specific feature combinations that are particularly effective?
4. **Error Analysis Details:**  Could you elaborate on the types of errors your system makes?  Specifically, are there any patterns in the incorrect classifications that could inform future development? (e.g., are there specific types of mentions or contexts that consistently cause errors?)
5. **Computational Cost:** What is the computational cost of your approach, particularly the feature extraction process?  Is it scalable to large-scale Wikipedia datasets?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a clearer visual representation of the system architecture (e.g., a diagram illustrating the preprocessing, feature extraction, and classification stages).
*   The references are generally well-cited, but some are a bit dated.  Including more recent work on coreference resolution and knowledge integration would strengthen the paper.
*   The writing is generally clear, but some sentences are overly complex and could be simplified.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that addresses an important problem with a well-motivated approach. The main strength lies in the creation of the WCR dataset, which will be a valuable resource for the research community.  While the dataset size and complexity of the features are weaknesses, the strong empirical results and clear presentation make this a worthwhile contribution. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
