## Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the task of identifying mentions of the “main concept” (MC) within Wikipedia articles – a specific coreference resolution (CR) problem tailored to the characteristics of this unique text genre. Recognizing that generic CR systems struggle with Wikipedia data, the authors propose a binary classification approach, leveraging features extracted from Wikipedia markup (links, redirects) and Freebase, to determine if a given mention refers to the MC. They evaluate their approach against existing CR systems and demonstrate performance improvements, further showing that integrating their classifier into a rule-based system boosts overall coreference chain detection.  The authors also release a dataset (WCR) for this task.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **A Dedicated Evaluation Dataset (WCR):**  The creation and release of the WCR dataset is arguably the most significant contribution.  Existing CR datasets are heavily skewed towards news-like text. A specifically curated dataset for Wikipedia, even at a moderate size, is valuable for advancing research in this area.
2. **A Feature-Rich Binary Classification Approach:** The authors present a well-motivated and systematically designed approach for identifying MC mentions. The careful selection of features derived from Wikipedia's structure and linked knowledge bases (Freebase) demonstrates a good understanding of the domain.
3. **Empirical Demonstration of Wikipedia-Specific CR Improvement:** The paper convincingly demonstrates that their approach outperforms generic CR systems on the WCR dataset and improves a strong rule-based system when integrated, highlighting the need for genre-specific adaptation.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of adapting CR systems to Wikipedia, highlighting the shortcomings of existing solutions and the unique challenges posed by this text genre. The rationale for focusing on the main concept is well-explained and relevant.
2. **Strong Feature Engineering:** The feature set is comprehensive and thoughtfully designed. The inclusion of features leveraging Wikipedia’s markup (redirects, links) and knowledge base integration (Freebase) is a key strength.  The separation of features for pronominal vs. non-pronominal mentions shows a nuanced understanding of the task.
3. **Rigorous Evaluation and Baseline Comparison:** The authors establish strong baselines using state-of-the-art CR systems and thoroughly evaluate their approach using standard CoNLL metrics.  The ablation studies (Table 2) effectively demonstrate the contribution of each feature family.
4. **Practical Integration and Results:** The integration of the classifier into the Dcoref system and the subsequent performance improvement demonstrate the practical utility of the proposed approach beyond just a standalone classification task. 
5. **Data Release:**  The commitment to releasing the WCR dataset is a significant benefit to the research community.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** While the WCR dataset is a valuable contribution, its size (30 documents) is relatively small. This limits the statistical power of the experiments and raises concerns about the generalizability of the results. Further expansion of the dataset would significantly strengthen the work.
2. **Lack of Error Analysis Depth:**  While the paper mentions some error cases (Figure 3), a more detailed and systematic error analysis would be highly beneficial. Understanding *why* the system fails on specific examples would offer valuable insights for future improvements.  For instance, further analysis of the errors stemming from ambiguous pronouns would be worthwhile.
3. **Classifier Choice and Hyperparameter Tuning:** The choice of SVM with a linear kernel and fixed hyperparameters feels somewhat arbitrary. A more thorough exploration of different classifiers and hyperparameter tuning strategies (e.g., using cross-validation) could potentially yield further performance gains. While the authors mention trying other configurations, the specifics aren't detailed.
4. **Limited Scope of Integration:** The integration with Dcoref focuses solely on using the classifier as a sieve for the MC coreference chain. Exploring more sophisticated integration strategies, such as incorporating the features into Dcoref’s rule set or using the classifier’s output as confidence scores for existing rules, might be fruitful.
5. **Over-reliance on Freebase:** While leveraging Freebase is a good idea, the reliance on its quality and completeness introduces a potential vulnerability. Missing or inaccurate Freebase data could negatively impact performance. The paper briefly acknowledges this but doesn't extensively discuss mitigation strategies.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dataset Expansion:** What are your plans for expanding the WCR dataset?  Have you considered active learning or crowd-sourcing strategies to accelerate dataset creation?
2. **Error Analysis Details:** Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, categorizing the types of errors (e.g., pronoun ambiguity, coreference to related but incorrect entities, spurious mentions) and quantifying their frequency?
3. **Classifier Justification:** What motivated the choice of SVM with a linear kernel? Did you experiment with other classifiers, such as Random Forests or Neural Networks? If so, what were the results?
4. **Freebase Robustness:** How sensitive is your approach to the quality and completeness of Freebase data? Have you investigated strategies for handling missing or inaccurate Freebase information (e.g., using alternative knowledge bases, fallback mechanisms)?
5. **Integration Alternatives:** Did you explore alternative integration strategies with Dcoref beyond using the classifier as a sieve? If so, what were the results?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*   The related work section provides a good overview of relevant research.
*   The code and dataset should be made readily available to facilitate reproducibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and valuable contribution to the field of coreference resolution, particularly for the specific domain of Wikipedia. The creation of the WCR dataset is a major asset, and the proposed approach demonstrates promising results. While the relatively small dataset size and limited error analysis are weaknesses, the overall strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the questions and weaknesses raised above.
