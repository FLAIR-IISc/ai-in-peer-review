## Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

**Summary:** This paper addresses the task of identifying mentions of a Wikipedia article’s main concept (MC) – a task neglected by standard coreference resolution (CR) systems geared towards news-like text. The authors revisit this task, framing it as a binary classification problem and proposing a system leveraging features from Wikipedia markup and Freebase. They demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art generic CR engines and improves a rule-based CR system when integrated. A new dataset, WCR, is introduced for evaluating performance on this specific task.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **A Dedicated Testbed (WCR):** The creation and release of the WCR dataset is the most significant contribution. This provides a much-needed resource for evaluating CR systems specifically on the unique characteristics of Wikipedia text.
2. **Effective Feature Engineering for MC Resolution:** The authors demonstrate the power of leveraging Wikipedia-specific features (markup, links, Freebase information) for identifying mentions of the main concept. This represents a practical and effective approach to adapting CR to a new domain.
3. **Empirical Demonstration of Domain Adaptation:** The paper clearly demonstrates that generic CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia data and that a targeted approach, utilizing the proposed features, yields substantial improvements.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper effectively articulates the problem of applying standard CR techniques to Wikipedia and justifies the need for a dedicated approach. The introductory section provides a strong rationale for the work.
2. **Comprehensive Feature Set:** The feature engineering is well-considered and draws inspiration from related work while cleverly incorporating Wikipedia’s unique structural elements. The separation of features for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions is a sound design choice.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results are convincing. The consistent outperformance of the proposed system over established baselines, on both the MC resolution task and integrated into a rule-based CR system, demonstrates its effectiveness.  The ablation study (Table 2) is particularly insightful, showcasing the contribution of individual feature families.
4. **Practical Integration & Impact:** Demonstrating improvement within an existing rule-based system (Dcoref++) shows the practical applicability of the work and its potential to improve real-world CR performance in Wikipedia-related applications.
5. **Resource Contribution:** The authors explicitly state they will release the dataset and a dump of identified MC mentions, furthering the reproducibility of their work and encouraging future research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:**  While WCR is a valuable contribution, its small size (30 documents) raises concerns about the generalizability of the results. The reported performance gains might be overstated due to the limited variability of the data. A larger, more diverse dataset would significantly strengthen the conclusions.
2. **Baseline Selection & Justification:** The choice of baselines, while including state-of-the-art systems, could be more thoroughly justified. The performance disparities between Dcoref and Scoref are substantial. Exploring *why* Dcoref performs better on WCR would be valuable. A deeper analysis of baseline errors is missing.
3. **Lack of Error Analysis Depth:**  While Figure 3 provides some illustrative examples of errors, a more systematic error analysis would be beneficial. Understanding the types of errors the system makes (e.g., ambiguity, long-distance coreference, complex sentence structure) could guide future improvements.
4. **Classifier Complexity & Explainability:** While SVMs are a reasonable choice, the paper doesn't delve into the most important features driving the classifier’s decisions.  Highlighting key feature weights or providing examples of instances where specific features are crucial would increase understanding and trust in the results.
5. **Limited Discussion of Scalability:** The authors mention utilizing WikipediaMiner and Jena for accessing data. However, they don’t discuss the computational cost or scalability of their approach for processing the entire English Wikipedia.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dataset Expansion:**  What are your plans for expanding the WCR dataset?  Are there any existing, larger Wikipedia corpora that could be leveraged for creating a more robust evaluation resource?
2. **Baseline Analysis:**  Can you provide a more in-depth analysis of *why* Dcoref outperforms Scoref on the WCR dataset? What specific characteristics of Wikipedia text make it more suitable for rule-based systems in this case?
3. **Error Analysis Detail:** Could you provide a more quantitative error analysis, categorizing the types of errors made by your system (e.g., errors related to pronominal resolution, errors due to ambiguous mentions, etc.)?
4. **Feature Importance:** What are the most important features contributing to the classification performance, for both pronominal and non-pronominal mentions? Could you provide specific examples demonstrating how these features influence the classifier’s decisions?
5. **Scalability Concerns:** What is the computational complexity of your system? How does it scale with the size of the Wikipedia corpus, and what optimizations could be implemented to improve scalability?



**Additional Comments:**

* The writing is clear and well-organized.
* The related work section is adequate, though could benefit from a more detailed discussion of recent advances in knowledge-enhanced coreference resolution.
* The authors' commitment to releasing the dataset and data dump is commendable.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and valuable contribution to the field of coreference resolution. While the limited dataset size and lack of detailed error analysis are weaknesses, the creation of the WCR dataset and the demonstration of effective feature engineering for MC resolution outweigh these concerns. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above. The paper has the potential to stimulate further research in adapting CR techniques to specialized domains like Wikipedia.
