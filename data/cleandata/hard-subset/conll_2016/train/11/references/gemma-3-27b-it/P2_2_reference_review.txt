## Review of "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the task of identifying mentions of the main concept (MC) within Wikipedia articles. Recognizing the limitations of coreference resolution (CR) systems trained on news-like text when applied to Wikipedia, the authors propose a binary classification approach to determine if a given mention refers to the MC. They leverage features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase, and demonstrate improved performance compared to state-of-the-art generic CR engines and an improvement when integrated into a rule-based system. A new dataset, WCR, is also introduced for evaluating systems on this specific task.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Dedicated Evaluation Dataset (WCR):** The creation and release of the WCR dataset is the most significant contribution.  It provides a valuable resource for researchers specifically targeting coreference within the unique context of Wikipedia, filling a clear gap in existing resources.
2. **Task Formulation & Feature Engineering:** Framing the MC resolution problem as binary classification and exploring Wikipedia-specific features (markup, Freebase links) is a solid contribution. The thoughtful feature engineering, drawing inspiration from existing CR work but tailored for Wikipedia, is commendable.
3. **Demonstrated Performance Improvement:**  The paper demonstrates that their approach outperforms existing CR systems on the WCR dataset and improves a rule-based system’s performance. This provides empirical evidence for the benefit of a dedicated approach for Wikipedia coreference.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of adapting CR to Wikipedia and convincingly motivates the need for a dedicated approach, given the performance gaps observed with systems trained on other genres.
2. **Well-Designed Experiments:** The experimental setup is robust, including comparisons to multiple baselines and state-of-the-art systems.  The use of 10-fold cross-validation adds credibility to the results.
3. **Comprehensive Feature Set:** The feature engineering is thorough and demonstrates an understanding of the characteristics of Wikipedia text.  The detailed description of the features (Sections 4.2.1 & 4.2.2) allows for easy replication and extension.
4. **Positive Results & Integration:** The performance gains shown, especially the improvement to the Dcoref system, are encouraging and indicate the practical applicability of the proposed approach. Demonstrating integration with an existing system is a strong point.
5. **Resource Contribution:** The authors’ commitment to releasing the dataset and potentially a mention-resolution dump will benefit the NLP community.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** The WCR dataset, with 30 documents, is relatively small. While the authors acknowledge this, it raises concerns about the generalizability of the results. Performance gains on a small dataset might not translate well to larger-scale applications.
2. **Baseline Limitations:** The baseline systems, while representing state-of-the-art CR engines, are not necessarily optimized *for* the main concept resolution task.  A more carefully tuned baseline specifically designed for this task might provide a stronger comparison.  The reliance on simply applying existing systems and then attempting to extract the MC chain feels somewhat ad-hoc.
3. **Lack of Error Analysis:** While the authors mention observing errors (e.g., Figure 3), a more systematic error analysis would be valuable.  Identifying the *types* of errors the system makes (e.g., ambiguity, coreference distance, etc.) would provide insights for future improvement.  
4. **Classifier Details:** While the choice of SVM with a linear kernel is justified, the parameter selection process (e.g., C value) is not fully explained.  Further detail on the training procedure would increase confidence in the results.
5. **Presentation & Redundancy:** The paper suffers from some redundancy. For example, the descriptions of related work and the motivation for the study are repeated in slightly different ways in the introduction and Section 2.  A more concise presentation would enhance readability.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dataset Expansion:** Do you have plans to expand the WCR dataset? If so, what are the target size and annotation guidelines?
2. **Baseline Tuning:**  Were any attempts made to specifically tune the baselines (Dcoref, Scoref) *for* the main concept resolution task, beyond the heuristics described in Section 3?  If not, why?
3. **Error Analysis Details:** Can you provide a more detailed error analysis?  What are the most common types of errors made by your system?
4. **Feature Importance:**  Which features proved to be most predictive in your experiments?  Was a feature selection process employed, and if so, what were the results?
5. **Generalizability:** Given the small dataset size, what steps have you taken to assess the generalizability of your approach to unseen Wikipedia articles? (e.g. different topics)



**Additional Comments:**

*   The link to the dataset availability (www.somewhere.country) should be replaced with a valid URL upon submission.
*   Consider including a table summarizing the feature set in an easily digestible format.
*   The paper would benefit from a discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential future directions (e.g., handling more complex coreference scenarios, dealing with long-range dependencies).



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of coreference resolution, specifically in the context of Wikipedia. The creation of the WCR dataset is a significant asset, and the proposed approach demonstrates promising results. While the small dataset size and limited baseline tuning are weaknesses, the strengths of the paper – clear motivation, robust experiments, and a well-engineered feature set – outweigh these concerns.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the points raised in the weaknesses and questions.
