- Task: Identify all mentions of a Wikipedia article’s “main concept” using a binary classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase, and evaluate on a new WCR dataset.  
- Contribution: Release of the WCR dataset for main-concept-focused coreference evaluation.  
- Contribution: Demonstration that Wikipedia-specific features (hyperlinks, redirects) and Freebase greatly improve this task.  
- Contribution: Practical integration showing the classifier improves a rule-based system (Dcoref → Dcoref++).  
- Strength: Well-motivated problem framing and clear justification for focusing on the main concept.  
- Strength: Rigorous evaluation against strong baselines, including 10-fold cross-validation and OntoNotes context.  
- Strength: Detailed, well-reasoned feature engineering with distinction between pronominal and non-pronominal features and feature importance analysis.  
- Strength: Practical applicability demonstrated by improving a full coreference system.  
- Strength: Commitment to releasing dataset and identified mentions for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 documents), raising generalizability and representativeness concerns.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of why the classifier succeeds; error analysis is shallow.  
- Weakness: Baselines lack a targeted heuristic baseline specifically for main-concept resolution.  
- Weakness: No formal statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values) reported for improvements.  
- Weakness: Presentation of Dcoref++ results (Table 5) is confusing and improvements aren’t clearly delineated.  
- Question: What are the plans and strategies for expanding WCR to ensure diversity and representativeness?  
- Question: Can the authors provide a more thorough error analysis with more examples and common error types?  
- Question: Have the authors conducted a detailed ablation study to quantify each feature group's contribution?  
- Question: Can the authors report statistical significance (p-values) for the reported improvements?  
- Question: Please elaborate on Dcoref++ implementation details, especially how the new sieve interacts with other sieves.  
- Comment: The introduction could be more concise and focus faster on problem and contributions.  
- Comment: References are comprehensive and demonstrate good literature understanding.  
- Comment: Writing is generally good but some sentences need clearer phrasing.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, worthwhile paper with valuable dataset and practical improvements; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset size, error analysis, baselines, significance testing, and clearer Dcoref++ presentation.
