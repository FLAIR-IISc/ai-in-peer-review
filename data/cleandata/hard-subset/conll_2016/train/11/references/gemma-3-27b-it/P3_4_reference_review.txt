## Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the task of identifying mentions of a Wikipedia article's main concept (MC) – a crucial step for many NLP applications leveraging Wikipedia’s knowledge base. Recognizing the performance gap between coreference resolution (CR) systems trained on news data and their application to Wikipedia, the authors propose a binary classification approach to identify MC mentions. They leverage features extracted from Wikipedia markup, Freebase, and existing CR systems, and demonstrate superior performance compared to state-of-the-art systems, both on the MC resolution task and when integrated into a rule-based CR system. A new dataset, WCR, is introduced for evaluation.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Dedicated Evaluation Dataset (WCR):** The creation and release of the WCR dataset is the most significant contribution. A focused dataset for evaluating MC resolution in Wikipedia is a valuable resource for the community, filling a clear gap in available benchmarks.
2. **Empirical Demonstration of Domain Adaptation Challenge:** The paper provides a strong empirical demonstration that existing CR systems significantly underperform on Wikipedia text, highlighting the need for domain-specific approaches.
3. **Effective Feature Engineering for MC Resolution:**  The authors successfully demonstrate the utility of leveraging Wikipedia-specific features (markup, links, Freebase) for MC resolution, outperforming generic CR systems.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of adapting CR to Wikipedia, justifying its importance with the widespread use of Wikipedia in NLP.
2. **Comprehensive Baseline Comparison:** The authors rigorously compare their approach against several strong baselines, including state-of-the-art CR systems (Dcoref, Scoref) and well-designed heuristics. This provides a robust assessment of their method’s effectiveness.
3. **Strong Performance Gains:**  The reported performance improvements, particularly on the MC resolution task, are substantial and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.  The ablation study (Table 2) provides valuable insight into the contributions of different feature sets.
4. **Practical Integration & Improvement:**  The successful integration of their classifier into Dcoref, leading to further improvements in overall CR performance, shows the practical applicability of their work.
5. **Publicly Available Resources:**  The commitment to release the WCR dataset and identified MC mentions is commendable and will facilitate further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size & Scope:** While WCR is a valuable contribution, its relatively small size (30 documents) raises concerns about the generalizability of the results.  The dataset's composition (selection criteria for the articles) isn't fully detailed, potentially introducing bias.
2. **Feature Engineering Complexity & Limited Analysis:** The paper describes a substantial number of features. While the ablation study is helpful, a more detailed analysis of which features are *most* important for different types of MC mentions (e.g., pronouns vs. noun phrases) would be beneficial.  A discussion of feature redundancy would also be valuable.
3. **Classifier Choice & Hyperparameter Tuning:**  The choice of SVM with a linear kernel and fixed hyperparameters feels somewhat arbitrary.  A more thorough exploration of different classifiers and hyperparameter optimization techniques might yield further improvements.
4. **Over-reliance on Freebase:**  The approach heavily relies on Freebase for entity type and attribute information.  Given Freebase’s known issues with data quality and completeness, this is a potential limitation. The paper acknowledges this implicitly but doesn't adequately address strategies for mitigating its impact (e.g., alternative knowledge bases, handling missing data).
5. **Limited Error Analysis:** While example errors are provided (Figure 3), a more systematic error analysis would be helpful to identify common failure modes and guide future research.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dataset Details:**  Can you provide more details about the selection process for the articles in the WCR dataset?  What criteria were used to ensure diversity in terms of topic, length, and complexity?  What is the distribution of article types (e.g., biographical, geographical, historical)?
2. **Freebase Dependency:** How robust is your approach to missing or inaccurate information in Freebase? Have you experimented with alternative knowledge sources or strategies for handling missing data?
3. **Feature Importance:**  Beyond the ablation study, can you provide a more detailed ranking of feature importance? Which features contribute the most to the improvement in performance, particularly for different types of MC mentions (e.g., pronouns)?
4. **Scalability:**  How computationally expensive is your approach, particularly the feature extraction process?  Can it be scaled to process large corpora of Wikipedia articles efficiently?
5. **Generalizability:** Do you have any preliminary results on applying your approach to Wikipedia articles in languages other than English?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*   The references are comprehensive and relevant.
*   The authors should consider explicitly mentioning the limitations of their approach in the conclusion.
*   A discussion of potential ethical considerations, such as bias in Freebase or Wikipedia, would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of coreference resolution and Wikipedia processing. The creation of the WCR dataset is particularly noteworthy. While the paper has some limitations, the strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the questions and concerns raised above. The authors have clearly demonstrated the importance of adapting CR techniques to the specific characteristics of Wikipedia text and provided a solid foundation for future research in this area.
