- Summary: Proposes binary classification for identifying a Wikipedia article’s main concept (MCR), using Wikipedia/Freebase features and combining existing CR systems; introduces WCR dataset and outperforms CR baselines.

- Contribution 1: Introduces WCR, a focused dataset and evaluation setup for Main Concept Resolution in Wikipedia.

- Contribution 2: Empirically shows that general-purpose coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia text.

- Contribution 3: Builds a feature-rich classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup, redirects, links, and Freebase to improve MCR.

- Strength 1: Addresses a well-defined, relevant task with clear applications in IE and knowledge graph construction.

- Strength 2: Provides strong experimental evaluation with 10-fold cross-validation and comparisons to several baselines.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates effective feature engineering and ablation analysis to show feature contributions.

- Strength 4: Shows practical utility by improving the rule-based Dcoref system when integrating the classifier.

- Strength 5: Commits to releasing the dataset and extracted resources for community use.

- Weakness 1: WCR dataset is small (30 docs, ~60k tokens), limiting generalizability and raising overfitting concerns.

- Weakness 2: Classifier uses hundreds of features; needs dimensionality reduction/feature selection and clearer presentation of features.

- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed qualitative error analysis beyond superficial examples.

- Weakness 4: Baselines are limited to existing CR systems; more tailored or simple rule-based baselines for MCR would be informative.

- Weakness 5: Claim of “outperforming state-of-the-art CR engines” is overstated since results are task-specific.

- Question 1: What are the plans and challenges for expanding the WCR dataset?

- Question 2: Have you tried feature selection or dimensionality reduction, and what were the outcomes?

- Question 3: Can you provide a systematic error analysis linking error types to features?

- Question 4: How well would the approach generalize to other KBs or encyclopedic texts, and what changes are needed?

- Question 5: What is the computational cost of training and applying the classifier given many features?

- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written but could use more visual aids (e.g., diagrams of feature extraction).

- Additional comment 2: Related work on Wikipedia-specific NLP could be cited more comprehensively.

- Additional comment 3: Consider discussing ethical issues, such as bias in Freebase data.

- Overall assessment: Solid paper with a significant dataset contribution and promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing dataset size, classifier complexity, and error analysis.
