## Peer Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the task of identifying all mentions of the “main concept” (MC) within a Wikipedia article – essentially coreference resolution focused on a single, predefined entity. Recognizing the limitations of general coreference resolution systems when applied to Wikipedia text, the authors propose a binary classification approach leveraging features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase. They establish a testbed (WCR dataset) and demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art generic coreference engines and improves a rule-based system (Dcoref) when integrated.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **A Focused Coreference Resolution Dataset (WCR):** The creation and release of a dedicated dataset for evaluating systems designed to identify mentions of a Wikipedia article's main concept is a valuable contribution. This fills a gap in available resources and enables targeted evaluation.
2. **Demonstration of Wikipedia-Specific Feature Effectiveness:** The paper convincingly demonstrates that features derived from Wikipedia’s inherent structure (hyperlinks, redirects) and external knowledge sources like Freebase are highly effective for this specific coreference task.
3. **Practical Improvement to a Rule-Based System:** Showing a performance gain by integrating the classifier into an existing coreference system (Dcoref) suggests a practical pathway for applying the research to real-world applications.



**Strengths:**

1. **Well-Motivated Problem:** The paper clearly articulates the need for adapting coreference resolution techniques to specific genres, using Wikipedia as a relevant and important example. The justification for focusing on the “main concept” is logical and useful for many downstream NLP tasks.
2. **Rigorous Evaluation:** The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation against strong baselines, including state-of-the-art coreference systems. The inclusion of comparison with the OntoNotes dataset provides helpful context. The 10-fold cross-validation provides statistically sound results.
3. **Detailed Feature Engineering:** The description of the feature set is thorough and well-reasoned.  The clear distinction between features for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions and the analysis of feature importance are particularly strong points. The exploration of feature combinations is a good practice.
4. **Practical Integration:** The work doesn’t stop at classification accuracy; the authors show a concrete improvement in a complete coreference resolution system (Dcoref++), suggesting practical applicability.
5. **Commitment to Resource Sharing:** The authors explicitly state their intention to release the dataset and a dump of identified mentions, facilitating reproducibility and future research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** The WCR dataset, while valuable, is relatively small (30 documents).  This raises concerns about the generalizability of the results. A larger dataset would be crucial for ensuring the robustness of the findings. It’s unclear how representative the 30 documents are of the vast breadth of Wikipedia articles.
2. **Classifier-Centric Approach:**  While the classifier performs well, the paper lacks a deeper exploration of *why* it works so well.  A more detailed error analysis, beyond the few examples in Figure 3, could provide valuable insights into the model's strengths and weaknesses.  For example, understanding the types of errors the classifier makes would illuminate potential avenues for improvement.
3. **Baseline Limitations:** The baselines are largely built on existing systems, and their performance is understandably lower.  However, a more targeted baseline specifically designed *for* the main concept resolution task, perhaps using simpler heuristics leveraging Wikipedia structure, could provide a stronger point of comparison.
4. **Lack of Statistical Significance Testing:** While 10-fold cross-validation is used, the paper lacks formal statistical significance testing to demonstrate that the improvements over the baselines are not due to chance. Reporting p-values would strengthen the conclusions.
5. **Presentation of Dcoref++ Results:**  The presentation of the Dcoref++ results (Table 5) is somewhat confusing. The metrics are numerous and the improvements aren’t always clearly delineated. A more focused comparison with the original Dcoref would be helpful.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Dataset Expansion:** What are your plans for expanding the WCR dataset?  What strategies will you employ to ensure its diversity and representativeness of the wider Wikipedia corpus?
2. **Error Analysis:** Can you provide a more detailed error analysis of your classifier, identifying common error types and potential causes? Include examples beyond those presented in Figure 3.
3. **Ablation Study:** Have you performed an ablation study to determine the relative importance of different feature groups? While briefly touched upon, a more thorough analysis of feature contributions would be beneficial.
4. **Statistical Significance:**  Can you provide p-values for the improvements reported in the experimental results, demonstrating their statistical significance?
5. **Dcoref++ Implementation Details:** Can you elaborate on the specific implementation details of the Dcoref++ integration, particularly how the sieve interacts with the other sieves in the system?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a more concise introduction. The initial paragraphs could be streamlined to focus more directly on the problem and contributions.
*   The references are well-cited, demonstrating a good understanding of the relevant literature.
*   The writing quality is generally good, but some sentences could be rephrased for clarity.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with a clear focus and a valuable contribution in the form of the WCR dataset. While the limited dataset size and lack of detailed error analysis are weaknesses, the strong evaluation, rigorous feature engineering, and practical integration make this a worthwhile contribution to the field of coreference resolution and NLP for Wikipedia.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
