- Paper proposes a binary classification approach to identify all mentions of the main concept (MC) in Wikipedia articles and introduces the WCR dataset for this task.
- Uses Wikipedia markup (links, redirects) and Freebase-derived features to improve MC mention identification over general CR systems and a rule-based baseline.
- Main contribution: creation of the WCR dataset, filling a gap for MC coreference evaluation on Wikipedia.
- Main contribution: shows that Wikipedia-specific adaptations yield better performance than general-purpose coreference systems.
- Main contribution: effective feature engineering combining markup, Freebase, and grammatical features for MC recognition.
- Strength: clear problem definition and strong motivation for focusing on main concept resolution in Wikipedia.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art CR systems and 10-fold cross-validation for reliability.
- Strength: well-designed feature set with an informative ablation study showing each feature family’s contribution.
- Strength: integrating the classifier into Dcoref improved overall coreference performance, indicating practical applicability.
- Strength: authors plan to share the dataset and annotations, facilitating further research.
- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 documents), raising concerns about generalizability and scalability to diverse Wikipedia articles.
- Weakness: brief description of the classifier and limited hyperparameter/tuning details; deeper error analysis is lacking.
- Weakness: baselines are relatively simple and could be strengthened by incorporating feature insights for more challenging comparisons.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of systematic error sources, e.g., effects of ambiguous mentions or complex sentence structures.
- Question: Do the authors plan to expand the WCR dataset and how will they ensure diversity and representativeness?
- Question: Why was a linear SVM chosen, and were other models experimented with and compared?
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed error analysis categorizing classifier errors (false positives/negatives, cross-sentence issues)?
- Question: Beyond ablation, can the authors report relative feature importance (e.g., SVM feature weights)?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written and references are comprehensive.
- Additional comment: data-sharing link is currently a placeholder and should be updated before submission.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with a valuable dataset and performance gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing dataset size and error analysis.
