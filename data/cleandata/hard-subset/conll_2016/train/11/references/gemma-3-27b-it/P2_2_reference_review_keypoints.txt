- Contribution: Introduces and releases the WCR dataset for main-concept coreference in Wikipedia.
- Contribution: Frames main-concept resolution as a binary classification task tailored to Wikipedia.
- Contribution: Leverages Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived features to improve MC resolution.
- Contribution: Shows empirical gains over generic coreference systems and improves a rule-based system when integrated.
- Strength: Clearly motivates the need for a Wikipedia-specific coreference approach.
- Strength: Robust experimental setup with comparisons to multiple baselines and 10-fold cross-validation.
- Strength: Comprehensive, well-documented feature engineering specific to Wikipedia.
- Strength: Demonstrates practical applicability by integrating with and improving Dcoref.
- Strength: Resource contribution (dataset and potential mention-resolution dump) benefits the community.
- Weakness: WCR dataset is very small (30 documents), raising concerns about generalizability.
- Weakness: Baselines were not specifically tuned for MC resolution, making comparisons potentially weak.
- Weakness: Lacks a systematic error analysis detailing error types and causes.
- Weakness: Insufficient details on classifier training and parameter selection (e.g., SVM C value).
- Weakness: Paper contains redundant descriptions that hurt presentation clarity.
- Question: Are there plans to expand WCR, and what are the target size/annotation guidelines?
- Question: Were baselines (Dcoref, Scoref) specifically tuned for MC resolution beyond simple heuristics?
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed error analysis and common error types?
- Question: Which features were most predictive, and was feature selection performed?
- Question: What steps were taken to assess generalizability to unseen Wikipedia articles/topics?
- Comment: Replace placeholder dataset URL with a valid link upon submission.
- Comment: Include a summary table of the feature set for readability.
- Comment: Add discussion of limitations and future directions (complex coreference, long-range dependencies).
- Overall: Valuable and promising work with dataset contribution and solid methods; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses.
