- Introduces WCR, a new dataset for identifying mentions of a Wikipedia article's main concept (MC).
- Shows that CR systems trained on news data underperform on Wikipedia, demonstrating a domain adaptation gap.
- Proposes a binary classification approach leveraging Wikipedia markup, links, Freebase, and CR system outputs for MC resolution.
- Reports superior performance over state-of-the-art CR systems and heuristics on the MC task and when integrated into a rule-based CR system.
- Provides an ablation study showing the contribution of different feature sets.
- Creates practical improvements by integrating the classifier into Dcoref, boosting overall coreference performance.
- Commits to releasing WCR and identified MC mentions as publicly available resources.
- Clearly motivates the problem and its importance for NLP applications using Wikipedia.
- Compares against multiple strong baselines, providing a rigorous empirical evaluation.
- Demonstrates substantial performance gains, with evidence from quantitative results and ablations.
- Dataset size is small (30 documents), raising concerns about generalizability and potential selection bias.
- Uses many engineered features but lacks detailed analysis of feature importance, redundancy, and per-mention-type effects.
- Chooses a linear SVM with fixed hyperparameters without thorough exploration of other classifiers or tuning.
- Relies heavily on Freebase, which has known quality and coverage issues, and lacks robust mitigation strategies.
- Provides limited and mostly anecdotal error analysis rather than a systematic breakdown of failure modes.
- Requests more detail on WCR article selection, diversity, and article-type distribution.
- Asks how the method handles missing/inaccurate Freebase data and whether alternative KBs were tested.
- Seeks a more detailed ranking of feature importance, especially for different MC mention types like pronouns.
- Questions computational cost and scalability of feature extraction for large-scale Wikipedia processing.
- Inquires about generalizability to non-English Wikipedia articles.
- Notes the paper is well-written with comprehensive references and recommends explicitly stating limitations.
- Suggests discussing ethical considerations such as biases in Freebase and Wikipedia.
- Overall assessment: a strong contribution centered on WCR and domain-specific MC resolution; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
