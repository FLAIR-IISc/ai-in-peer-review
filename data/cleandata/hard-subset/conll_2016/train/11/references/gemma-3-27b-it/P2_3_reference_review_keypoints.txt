- Summary: Proposes binary classification to identify a Wikipedia article’s main concept (MC), introduces WCR dataset, and integrates the classifier into Dcoref to improve MC resolution.

- Strength: Addresses an important gap by focusing on Wikipedia-specific coreference and domain adaptation issues.

- Strength: Sensible, well-motivated approach using Wikipedia structure and Freebase features for MC classification.

- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation showing clear improvement over existing CR systems and baselines.

- Strength: Practical applicability demonstrated by improving the rule-based Dcoref via classifier integration.

- Strength: Contribution of the WCR dataset as a resource for future Wikipedia coreference work.

- Weakness: Core method lacks novelty—standard SVM classifier with engineered features rather than methodological innovation.

- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 documents); concerns about representativeness and selection criteria.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Freebase, raising concerns about long-term viability and need for alternative KBs.

- Weakness: Limited and shallow error analysis; needs deeper investigation of error types and causes.

- Weakness: Ablation study is brief and lacks detailed per-feature impact within feature families.

- Question: Please provide detailed WCR dataset info—selection criteria, average article length, annotation stats and inter-annotator agreement, and release plans.

- Question: What alternative knowledge bases were considered and how easily can the approach be adapted away from Freebase?

- Question: Can you supply a comprehensive error analysis categorizing common misclassifications and their sources?

- Question: Provide finer-grained feature importance—identify individual features with strongest positive or negative impact.

- Question: How does the approach scale to larger articles or full Wikipedia processing; any computational bottlenecks?

- Additional: Writing is clear and the paper is well-organized.

- Additional: References are relevant and up-to-date.

- Additional: Paper should discuss related work more thoroughly, especially knowledge-based coreference and domain adaptation.

- Overall: Solid, well-motivated paper with useful contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset, Freebase dependency, error analysis, and ablation details.
