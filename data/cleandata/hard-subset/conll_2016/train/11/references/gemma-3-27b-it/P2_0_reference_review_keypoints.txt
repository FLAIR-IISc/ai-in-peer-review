- Paper tackles identifying mentions of the article’s main concept (MC) in Wikipedia using a binary classifier tailored to Wikipedia text.
- Introduces WCR, a focused dataset for Main Concept Resolution in Wikipedia.
- Proposes a feature-rich binary classification approach using Wikipedia markup, links, and Freebase features.
- Shows the classifier outperforms generic coreference systems and improves the Dcoref rule-based pipeline.
- Clear problem definition and motivation for adapting CR to Wikipedia’s characteristics.
- Strong empirical evaluation with comparisons to baselines and 10-fold cross-validation.
- Resource contribution: planned release of the WCR dataset and processed results.
- Comprehensive feature engineering and an ablation study showing feature family impacts.
- Well-written, well-structured paper with clear explanations of design choices.
- WCR dataset is small (30 documents), raising concerns about generalizability and statistical power.
- Baselines are somewhat simplistic and aggregation strategies (B3/B4) may introduce noise.
- Classifier is basic (linear SVM); lacks exploration of advanced models and deeper explainability.
- Limited error analysis; need systematic categorization of error types and failure modes.
- Dependence on Freebase risks portability and robustness when external knowledge is missing or incorrect.
- Questions for authors: plans to expand WCR, use of automatic annotation, detailed error analysis, feature importance, Freebase robustness, and generalizability.
- Additional comments: replace placeholder data URL with a concrete link; temper the “significant improvement” claim given dataset limits.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution, especially WCR; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand dataset and deepen error/feature analysis.
