- Task and approach: Identify mentions of the "main concept" (MC) in Wikipedia using a binary classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase.
- Dataset: Introduces WCR, a manually annotated dataset for MC resolution in Wikipedia.
- Performance: Classifier outperforms existing CR systems on WCR and improves a rule-based system when integrated.
- Contribution 1: Creation of the WCR dataset fills a resource gap for MC evaluation.
- Contribution 2: Rich feature set combining Wikipedia structure (hyperlinks, redirects) and external knowledge (Freebase).
- Contribution 3: Empirical validation shows effectiveness both as a standalone classifier and when integrated into Dcoref++.
- Strength 1: Task is novel and relevant, exploiting Wikipedia’s unique structure.
- Strength 2: Strong and fair comparisons to state-of-the-art CR baselines.
- Strength 3: Thorough and well-justified feature engineering with ablation insights.
- Strength 4: Practical applicability demonstrated via integration and performance gains in a real system.
- Strength 5: Authors commit to releasing dataset and resources for the community.
- Weakness 1: WCR is small (30 documents, ~60k tokens), raising generalizability and statistical significance concerns.
- Weakness 2: Choice of linear SVM seems basic given feature complexity; lack of justification or experiments with richer models.
- Weakness 3: Error analysis is shallow; more qualitative insight into common failure modes is needed.
- Weakness 4: Evaluation scope is limited to identifying MC mentions rather than full-coreference chaining of the MC.
- Question 1: Plans for expanding WCR and releasing annotation guidelines to enable community contributions?
- Question 2: Why choose a linear SVM over more complex classifiers, and were other models tried?
- Question 3: Can you provide a deeper error analysis detailing frequently misclassified mention types and causes?
- Question 4: Have you evaluated end-to-end coreference chaining for the MC, and what are the challenges?
- Additional: Paper is well-written, related work is comprehensive, and data/code release is positive.
- Overall assessment: Solid, valuable contribution centered on WCR and feature-rich classification; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
