This paper addresses the task of identifying mentions of the main concept (MC) within Wikipedia articles, a specific instance of coreference resolution (CR) often overlooked in favor of news-like texts. The authors revisit this task, providing a testbed (WCR dataset) and comparing several CR systems, including baselines built on existing engines and their own feature-rich classifier. The primary contribution is the creation and release of the WCR dataset, along with a comprehensive evaluation of both existing and their proposed method on this dataset. A secondary contribution lies in demonstrating the benefit of integrating their classifier into a rule-based CR system (Dcoref), improving overall coreference resolution performance. A third contribution is the detailed feature engineering specifically tailored for Wikipedia text, leveraging markup and external knowledge sources like Freebase.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel Dataset & Task Focus:** The introduction of the WCR dataset is a significant contribution. Wikipedia’s unique structure and content distinguish it from typical CR datasets (newswire, etc.).  Focusing specifically on MC resolution within Wikipedia is a well-defined and valuable task. The authors convincingly argue that existing CR systems perform poorly on this genre, necessitating a dedicated resource and evaluation. The reported statistics regarding the dataset’s characteristics (number of mentions, percentage relating to MC) provide useful context.

2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors conduct a thorough evaluation, comparing several state-of-the-art CR systems and their baselines. Reporting performance on both the WCR dataset *and* the more common OntoNotes dataset allows for a useful comparison of genre-specific performance.  The inclusion of multiple evaluation metrics (MUC, B3, CEAFφ4, CoNLL F1) provides a robust assessment.

3. **Detailed Feature Engineering:** The authors demonstrate a good understanding of Wikipedia’s characteristics and leverage them effectively through feature engineering.  Exploiting markup (redirects, internal links), Freebase, and part-of-speech tags demonstrates a thoughtful approach to adapting CR techniques to this specific domain. The ablation study (Table 2) clearly illustrates the impact of different feature families, providing valuable insights into their effectiveness. The separation of features for pronominal vs. non-pronominal mentions is also a positive aspect.

4. **Integration & Improvement of Existing System:**  Demonstrating an improvement in coreference resolution performance by integrating their classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++) is a strong result. This practical demonstration of applicability strengthens the paper's impact. The reported 4% F1 score increase is substantial.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** While the WCR dataset is a valuable contribution, its size (30 documents, ~60k tokens) is relatively small, especially considering the complexity of coreference resolution. This limits the statistical significance of the results and the generalizability of the findings.  The authors acknowledge this but it remains a concern.

2. **Baseline Limitations:** The baselines, while reasonable, are relatively simple heuristics based on existing CR systems. A more sophisticated baseline specifically tailored for MC resolution in Wikipedia could provide a stronger point of comparison.  The discussion of the baselines feels somewhat brief.

3. **Over-Reliance on Freebase:** The approach heavily relies on Freebase for attribute extraction. Freebase is no longer actively maintained, raising concerns about the long-term viability and reproducibility of the method. The authors do not adequately address this dependency or suggest alternative knowledge sources.

4. **Lack of Error Analysis Depth:**  While the authors provide examples of error cases (Figure 3), the error analysis is relatively superficial. A more in-depth analysis of the types of errors made by the system and their root causes would be beneficial for guiding future research.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. What steps have been taken to ensure the quality and consistency of the annotations in the WCR dataset?  Inter-annotator agreement scores would be helpful.
2. Given the limitations of Freebase, are there plans to explore alternative knowledge sources or methods for attribute extraction?
3. Could you elaborate on the limitations of using the existing CR systems 'out of the box' on this task, beyond simply stating that they perform poorly? What specific characteristics of Wikipedia text cause the most difficulties for these systems?
4.  The integration with Dcoref focuses on using your classifier as a sieve. Have you experimented with other integration strategies, such as incorporating the classifier's features directly into Dcoref's rule set?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The writing is generally clear and well-organized.
*   The references are appropriately cited.
*   The paper would benefit from a discussion of related work on knowledge graph completion or entity linking, as these areas are relevant to the task of identifying MC mentions.
*   Providing access to the WCR dataset and code will be crucial for facilitating future research in this area.
