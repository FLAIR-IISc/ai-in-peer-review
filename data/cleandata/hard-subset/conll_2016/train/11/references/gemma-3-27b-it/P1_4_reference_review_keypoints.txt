- Addresses identifying mentions of a Wikipedia article’s main concept (MC) using a binary classifier with Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.  
- Proposes and releases the WCR dataset tailored for MC resolution in Wikipedia.  
- Shows genre-specific adaptation (Wikipedia structure + external knowledge) yields significant gains over general CR systems.  
- Demonstrates effective feature engineering that leverages Wikipedia structure and Freebase.  
- Compares against strong baselines (Dcoref, Scoref) with rigorous 10-fold cross-validation and CoNLL metrics.  
- Provides an ablation study that clarifies the contribution of different feature families.  
- Successfully integrates the classifier into Dcoref, improving coreference chain detection (Dcoref++).  
- Commits to publicly releasing dataset and data dump to support reproducibility.  
- Clear problem definition and motivation with relevance to downstream tasks like OIE.  
- Experimental setup is rigorous and well-executed.  
- Detailed feature analysis helps explain why the model works.  
- Writing, references, and figures are clear and helpful.  
- WCR dataset size is small (30 documents), limiting generalizability and statistical strength.  
- Approach heavily relies on well-structured Wikipedia features, raising concerns about transfer to less structured text.  
- Classifier complexity and many features hurt explainability; more error analysis and examples are needed.  
- Baseline selection could be broader, including more Wikipedia-specific approaches.  
- Questions raised: plans to expand WCR and challenges in scaling annotation.  
- Questions raised: assessment of generalizability to less structured genres and steps to improve it.  
- Questions raised: request for detailed error analysis with misclassification examples and contributing features.  
- Questions raised: whether feature selection was considered to simplify the model.  
- Questions raised: robustness to Freebase changes/unavailability and fallback mechanisms.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with valuable dataset and genre-specific gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address concerns.
