- Tackles identifying mentions of a Wikipedia article’s main concept (MC) as a binary classification task and releases the WCR dataset.  
- Demonstrates that general coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia text and motivates a Wikipedia-specific solution.  
- Proposes a practical system leveraging Wikipedia markup, Freebase, and engineered features as a competitive baseline.  
- Shows empirical improvements over strong CR baselines and integration into a rule-based system boosts overall CR performance.  
- Releases a focused dataset (WCR) and a mention database, enabling reproducibility and further research.  
- Clear problem definition and strong motivation for focusing on Wikipedia as an important knowledge source.  
- Well-designed experiments, ablation study, and useful benchmark comparisons (including CoNLL).  
- Practical feature choices avoid complex entity linking by exploiting readily available Wikipedia information.  
- Improvement demonstrated by integrating the classifier into an existing rule-based CR system.  
- WCR dataset is small (30 documents), raising concerns about generalizability and statistical significance.  
- Methodological novelty is limited—approach is standard binary classification with engineered features.  
- Error analysis is shallow; lacks detailed breakdowns of false positives/negatives and error causes.  
- Baselines are somewhat limited; more comparisons to Wikipedia- or KG-focused methods would be helpful.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive; presentation could be more concise and focused.  
- Questions about plans to expand WCR, potential crowdsourcing, and experiments on larger unseen data.  
- Requests for a deeper error analysis with examples and analysis of frequently misclassified mention types.  
- Asks for explanations of why each feature family is effective (linguistic or domain-specific justification).  
- Wants information on computational cost and feature extraction expense compared to baselines.  
- Suggests including a data release URL, discussing Freebase limitations (and Wikidata alternatives), and renaming “Dcoref++.”  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on more error analysis and clarification of generalizability.
