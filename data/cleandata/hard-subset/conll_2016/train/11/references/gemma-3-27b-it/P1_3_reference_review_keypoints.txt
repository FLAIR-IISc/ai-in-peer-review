- Summary: Paper tackles identifying "main concept" mentions in Wikipedia via a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase, and presents the WCR dataset.

- Contribution 1: Defines a focused MC identification subtask and releases the WCR dataset, filling a resource gap for Wikipedia-specific CR.

- Contribution 2: Shows the classifier outperforms generic coreference systems on WCR, and helps a rule-based CR system when integrated.

- Contribution 3: Proposes Wikipedia- and knowledge-graph-aware features (markup, links, Freebase) with an ablation study highlighting their importance.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated—addresses domain mismatch of newswire-trained CR systems on Wikipedia.

- Strength 2: Sound experimental setup with multiple baselines, cross-validation, standalone and integrated evaluations.

- Strength 3: Thorough feature description and helpful ablation study clarifying feature contributions.

- Strength 4: Commits to releasing WCR and identified MC mentions, aiding reproducibility and future work.

- Strength 5: Clear writing and organization, making methods and results easy to follow.

- Weakness 1: WCR is very small (30 documents), limiting generalizability and statistical significance.

- Weakness 2: Baselines are limited—poor baseline performance on WCR may inflate gains; stronger adapted baselines needed.

- Weakness 3: Lacks systematic error analysis to characterize common failure modes (e.g., ambiguity, article sections).

- Weakness 4: Binary classification framing may be too simplistic for inherently relational coreference tasks.

- Weakness 5: Scalability concerns—feature extraction (e.g., Freebase queries) may be expensive at Wikipedia scale.

- Question 1: Plans for expanding WCR—will you release more data and how large/diverse will it be?

- Question 2: Have you tried adapting/retraining baseline CR systems for MC identification (e.g., on Wikipedia)?

- Question 3: Can you provide a detailed error analysis categorizing common error types and contexts?

- Question 4: What is the computational cost of feature extraction and how would it scale to full Wikipedia?

- Question 5: Have you considered alternatives to binary classification, such as modeling inter-mention coreference relations?
