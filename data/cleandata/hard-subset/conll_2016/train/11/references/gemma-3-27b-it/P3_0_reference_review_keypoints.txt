- Paper addresses identifying "main concept" mentions in Wikipedia using a binary classifier leveraging Wikipedia markup and Freebase; authors release the WCR dataset.
- Contribution: released WCR, a domain-specific coreference dataset for Wikipedia.
- Contribution: designed a feature-rich binary classification approach using Wikipedia links/redirects and Freebase; separates pronominal vs non-pronominal features.
- Contribution: empirically outperforms generic CR systems on WCR and improves a rule-based system (Dcoref) when integrated.
- Strength: clear problem definition and strong motivation for Wikipedia-specific coreference adaptation.
- Strength: comprehensive and well-motivated feature engineering exploiting Wikipedia structure and knowledge-base links.
- Strength: rigorous evaluation with strong baselines, CoNLL metrics, and informative ablation studies.
- Strength: demonstrated practical utility by improving Dcoref via classifier integration.
- Strength: commitment to releasing data and code to the community.
- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 documents), limiting statistical power and generalizability.
- Weakness: error analysis is shallow; needs more systematic categorization (e.g., pronoun ambiguity).
- Weakness: classifier choice (linear SVM) and hyperparameter tuning are underexplored and appear arbitrary.
- Weakness: limited integration scope with Dcoref; more sophisticated integration strategies were not thoroughly explored.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Freebase introduces vulnerability to missing/inaccurate KB data and lacks mitigation strategies.
- Question: What are the plans and methods (e.g., active learning, crowdsourcing) for expanding WCR?
- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed error taxonomy and frequency counts for common failure modes?
- Question: Why choose linear SVM; were other classifiers (Random Forests, neural nets) and tuned hyperparameters tested and what were results?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to Freebase quality and have fallback strategies or alternate KBs been evaluated?
- Question: Were alternative integration methods with Dcoref (e.g., using classifier confidence in rules) explored and what were the outcomes?
- Additional: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and has a solid related work section.
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution (especially WCR); recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset size, error analysis, classifier justification, and Freebase reliance.
