## Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the task of identifying mentions of a Wikipedia article's main concept (MC) within that article – a focused coreference resolution problem. Recognizing that generic CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia text, the authors propose a binary classification approach leveraging features extracted from Wikipedia markup, Freebase, and existing CR systems. They establish a testbed (WCR dataset) and demonstrate that their method outperforms state-of-the-art systems on this task, and that integrating it into a rule-based CR system improves overall performance.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **A Dedicated Evaluation Testbed (WCR):** The creation of the WCR dataset is the most significant contribution.  A focused, annotated dataset for MC coreference resolution in Wikipedia is a valuable resource for the community.
2. **Demonstration of Wikipedia-Specific Adaptation:** The paper convincingly demonstrates that adapting coreference resolution techniques to the specific characteristics of Wikipedia text yields improved performance compared to applying generic CR systems.
3. **Effective Feature Engineering:** The authors demonstrate the utility of incorporating features derived from Wikipedia’s structure (markup, links) and external knowledge (Freebase) for the targeted coreference task.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of adapting coreference resolution to the unique characteristics of Wikipedia and motivates the need for a specialized approach.  The rationale for focusing on the main concept is well explained.
2. **Thorough Experimental Evaluation:** The authors present a comprehensive experimental evaluation, comparing their approach against several strong baselines, including state-of-the-art CR systems. The use of cross-validation and reporting on multiple metrics (P, R, F1) adds to the robustness of the evaluation.
3. **Well-Justified Feature Set:** The features chosen are intuitively relevant to the task and build on existing work in coreference resolution, while smartly exploiting the unique properties of Wikipedia.  The ablation study (Table 2) effectively demonstrates the contribution of each feature family.
4. **Positive Impact on Existing System:** Showing that the proposed classifier improves the performance of a rule-based CR system (Dcoref++) is a strong indication of its practical value.
5. **Availability of Resources:**  The authors’ commitment to releasing the dataset and extracted data is highly commendable and will significantly benefit the research community.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** While the creation of WCR is a significant strength, its relatively small size (30 documents) raises concerns about the generalizability of the results. The performance gains observed might be specific to the chosen articles and may not hold across a larger, more diverse sample of Wikipedia articles.
2. **Over-reliance on Existing Systems for Mention Detection:**  The paper relies on the mention detection algorithms embedded in Dcoref and Scoref.  The performance of the proposed method is thus directly influenced by the accuracy of these mention detectors.  A more robust evaluation would involve assessing the performance with different mention detection modules, or ideally, evaluating the entire pipeline end-to-end with a dedicated mention detection component.
3. **Limited Error Analysis:**  While the paper includes a few illustrative examples of errors (Figure 3), a more thorough error analysis is needed to understand the specific challenges that remain. For example, are errors more frequent with specific types of main concepts (e.g., abstract concepts vs. people)? Are there systematic biases in the errors?
4. **Baseline Selection:** The baselines chosen, while reasonable, don’t fully represent the spectrum of existing approaches.  Exploring simpler baselines (e.g., a frequency-based baseline) could provide a stronger point of comparison.
5. **Lack of Statistical Significance Tests:** The performance improvements reported are not accompanied by statistical significance tests.  This makes it difficult to determine whether the observed gains are statistically significant or due to random chance, especially given the relatively small dataset size.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Dataset Expansion:** What are your plans for expanding the WCR dataset?  Will you be releasing more annotated data in the future?
2.  **Mention Detection Sensitivity:** How sensitive is your approach to the performance of the underlying mention detection system?  Have you experimented with different mention detectors?
3.  **Error Analysis Details:** Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, categorized by error type (e.g., incorrect pronoun resolution, incorrect entity type assignment)?
4.  **Statistical Significance:**  Can you provide statistical significance tests to support the reported performance improvements?
5. **Generalizability:** Have you considered evaluating your approach on Wikipedia articles in languages other than English?  Are there language-specific challenges that you anticipate?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The writing is generally clear and concise.
*   The related work section is well-structured and provides a good overview of the relevant literature.
*   The paper would benefit from a brief discussion of the limitations of the study.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **Acceptance** with minor revisions.  The WCR dataset is a valuable contribution to the community, and the paper demonstrates a clear improvement over existing methods for this specific task. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above – particularly expanding the dataset, analyzing error types, and performing statistical significance testing – would strengthen the paper further.
