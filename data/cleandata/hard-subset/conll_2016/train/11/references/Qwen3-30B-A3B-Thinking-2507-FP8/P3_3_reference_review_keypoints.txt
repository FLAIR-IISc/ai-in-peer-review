- Paper identifies gap in coreference resolution on Wikipedia and defines task of identifying all mentions of the article's "main concept" (MC), presenting WCR dataset, a binary MC classifier using Wikipedia-specific features, and Dcoref integration.

Strengths:
- Demonstrates large drop in SOTA CR performance on Wikipedia vs. news, motivating Wikipedia-specific approaches.
- Cleverly leverages Wikipedia markup (redirects, links, Freebase) to extract robust features without relying on error-prone NEL pipelines.
- Strong empirical validation and ablation studies; classifier reaches 89% F1 and integration into Dcoref yields a 4-point F1 boost.

Weaknesses:
- WCR dataset is very small (30 articles), raising serious concerns about generalizability and statistical support.
- No comparison to prior specialized Wikipedia CR systems (e.g., Nguyen et al. 2007, Nakayama 2008), a critical omission for a "revisiting" claim.
- Task formulation (binary MC classification) may be artificial for full coreference resolution and the paper lacks evidence it addresses core Wikipedia CR challenges beyond simple filtering.

Questions to authors:
- How do you justify using only 30 articles and what evidence shows this sample represents broader Wikipedia?
- Why was there no comparison to specialized Wikipedia CR work (Nguyen et al., Nakayama)?
- How would you fix errors like Figure 3(f) where "it" is misassociated with the MC, and what effect would corrections have on overall F1?

Overall assessment:
- Promising and well-motivated exploitation of Wikipedia structure but limited by tiny dataset and missing comparisons; recommendation: Weak Accept pending major revisions (expand dataset and comparative evaluation).
