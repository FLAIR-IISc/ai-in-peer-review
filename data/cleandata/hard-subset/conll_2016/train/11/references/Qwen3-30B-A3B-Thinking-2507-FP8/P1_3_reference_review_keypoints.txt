- Summary: Paper targets poor coreference performance on Wikipedia by proposing a binary classifier to identify all mentions of the article's main concept, introducing a new WCR dataset, and reporting large F1 gains over baselines and a Dcoref++ integration.

Main contributions:
- Created WCR, the first dataset for evaluating coreference on Wikipedia (30 articles, ~60k tokens).
- Demonstrated that CR systems trained on news drop 10–15 F1 on Wikipedia.
- Proposed a simple binary classification method using Wikipedia markup/Freebase that outperforms baselines by 13–15 F1 and improves Dcoref when integrated.

Strengths:
- Clearly identifies and quantifies a meaningful gap in CR performance on Wikipedia.
- Provides a valuable new resource (WCR) with diverse sampling across article types.
- Uses a simple, effective approach leveraging Wikipedia-specific signals without heavy feature engineering.
- Shows practical impact by improving existing pipelines (Dcoref++ yields reported CoNLL F1 gains).

Weaknesses:
- WCR is small (30 articles, ~60k tokens), limiting generalizability of results.
- Baseline comparison is limited to two systems (Dcoref and Scoref), omitting other state-of-the-art models.
- Feature selection/engineering process is underexplained (greedy addition process not detailed).
- Claim of "outperforming state-of-the-art" is misleading since comparisons are to baselines built on top of, not the full, systems.
- No discussion of limitations such as few-link articles, non-English Wikipedia, or robustness of extensions to full-coreference.

Questions for authors:
- How confident are you that results generalize beyond the 30-article WCR, and will you expand the dataset?
- Why focus only on Dcoref and Scoref baselines rather than a broader set of state-of-the-art systems?
- Please clarify the reported CoNLL F1 improvement (paper says 4 points but table arithmetic shows 3.34).
- How do you plan to extend this approach to identify all coreference relations in Wikipedia, beyond main concept mentions?

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset size, baseline breadth, feature-details, and limitation discussions.
