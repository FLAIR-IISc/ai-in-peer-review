- Addresses coreference in Wikipedia by identifying all mentions of the article’s "main concept" (MC), arguing news-trained CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia.  
- Proposes a binary classifier that identifies MC mentions using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.  
- Evaluates against baselines built on Dcoref and Scoref and shows the method outperforms them.  
- Demonstrates integrating the classifier into Dcoref improves coreference performance on Wikipedia.  

- Contribution: provides a testbed and new WCR dataset for main concept resolution in Wikipedia.  
- Contribution: introduces a simple, effective classifier leveraging Wikipedia-specific structural features (links, redirects, Freebase).  
- Contribution: shows practical integration of a domain-specific classifier into an existing CR system to boost performance.  

- Strength: clearly motivated task highlighting a real domain shift; shows 4–17 F1 drop for SOTA CR systems on Wikipedia vs. news.  
- Strength: releases a carefully annotated WCR dataset (30 articles) and thorough evaluation of multiple systems on it.  
- Strength: practical utility demonstrated by a 4-point absolute F1 gain when integrating the classifier into Dcoref.  

- Weakness: Task scope is narrow (MC identification) and the paper does not convincingly justify its primacy over full coreference resolution.  
- Weakness: Fails to compare against prior Wikipedia-specific CR work (Nguyen et al., 2007; Nakayama, 2008), leaving novelty unclear.  
- Weakness: Small WCR dataset (30 docs, ~60k tokens) raises concerns about statistical power and overfitting/generalizability.  
- Weakness: Framing is unclear—paper reads as a binary MC task rather than a clear advancement of general coreference literature.  
- Weakness: Insufficient feature analysis/ablation beyond pronominal mentions; unclear contribution of different feature families.  

- Question: Why does the rule-based Dcoref outperform ML systems on WCR—dataset size issue or fundamental limitation of statistical methods?  
- Question: How do MC resolution gains translate to broader coreference improvements beyond the reported 4-point CoNLL F1 increase?  
- Question: How representative and unbiased is the small WCR dataset across Wikipedia topics, structures, and sizes?  
- Question: Details requested on how the classifier integrates with Dcoref and whether integration generalizes to other CR systems.  

- Overall recommendation: Accept with major revisions—paper is valuable and well-motivated but needs stronger positioning, comparisons to prior Wikipedia work, larger/more representative data or analysis, and better feature/ablation studies.
