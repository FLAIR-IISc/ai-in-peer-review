

# Peer Review: "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper addresses a significant gap in coreference resolution (CR) research: the poor performance of existing CR systems on Wikipedia text. The authors propose a task-specific approach to identify all mentions of the "main concept" (MC) in a Wikipedia article (e.g., all mentions of "Chilly Gonzales" in the article about Chilly Gonzales). They frame this as a binary classification problem to determine whether a mention refers to the MC, leveraging Wikipedia's unique structural features (links, redirects) and Freebase for feature extraction. 

The authors' main contributions, ordered by strength, are:

1. **The creation of a new dataset (WCR) specifically for evaluating CR on Wikipedia articles**, containing 30 annotated articles (60k tokens) with detailed coreference annotations. This fills a critical gap in the literature, as there are few resources for evaluating CR systems on Wikipedia.

2. **A novel binary classification approach** that leverages Wikipedia's markup and Freebase to identify MC mentions, which outperforms baselines built on top of state-of-the-art CR systems by 13-15 F1 points.

3. **Demonstration that integrating their classifier into a rule-based CR system (Dcoref++) improves full coreference resolution performance by 4 F1 points** on the WCR dataset.

## Strengths

1. **Addresses a genuine gap in the literature**: The paper compellingly demonstrates that state-of-the-art CR systems trained on news data perform poorly on Wikipedia (Table 1 shows F1 scores dropping from 63.61 on OntoNotes to 46.39 on WCR). This is a significant contribution to the field, as Wikipedia is a widely used resource in NLP that has been neglected in CR research.

2. **Leverages Wikipedia's unique structural features effectively**: The authors cleverly exploit Wikipedia's internal links, redirects, and Freebase integration rather than relying on error-prone named-entity linking pipelines. This approach is more robust and better suited to Wikipedia's characteristics than previous methods (Section 4.1).

3. **Provides a valuable new dataset (WCR)**: The authors have created a carefully constructed dataset with attention to article diversity (size, topic, link density), which will be highly valuable for future research. The paper makes the dataset and classifier results publicly available, enhancing reproducibility.

4. **Clear experimental validation**: The paper thoroughly compares their approach to multiple baselines and state-of-the-art systems, demonstrating both the effectiveness of their specific approach for the MC identification task and the broader impact of integrating their classifier into a full CR system (Table 5).

5. **Practical contribution**: The authors demonstrate that their approach can be integrated into existing CR systems to improve performance, making it immediately useful for practitioners.

## Weaknesses

1. **Small dataset size limits statistical significance**: The WCR dataset contains only 30 articles (60k tokens), which is quite small for NLP evaluation. While the authors acknowledge this, it significantly limits the confidence in their results. The paper would be stronger with a larger, more diverse dataset, especially given the claims about Wikipedia's "multi-domain" nature.

2. **Unclear connection to prior work**: The paper claims to "revisit" the task from Nakayama (2008), but doesn't clearly explain how their approach differs or improves upon it. They mention that Nakayama's work was "dedicated to extract tuples from Wikipedia articles" and "did not spend much efforts in designing a fully-fledged CR," but don't explicitly compare their method to Nakayama's.

3. **Limited justification for the main concept identification task**: While the paper mentions this task is useful for information extraction, it doesn't provide concrete examples of how this specific task improves downstream applications. The authors could better justify why identifying MC mentions is valuable beyond the specific examples they provide.

4. **Lack of comparison to Wikipedia-specific CR approaches**: The paper only compares to baselines built on top of general CR systems, not to approaches specifically designed for Wikipedia. This makes it difficult to assess the novelty of their approach, as they don't benchmark against other Wikipedia-focused CR methods.

5. **Insufficient discussion of limitations**: The paper doesn't thoroughly address the limitations of their approach, such as how it handles ambiguous cases where multiple concepts share similar mentions (e.g., "Houston" referring to both the city and the football team, which they acknowledge as a challenge in Example (g)).

## Questions to Authors

1. The WCR dataset contains only 30 articles. Could you provide more details about how these articles were selected (e.g., categories, sizes, link densities) to ensure they represent the diversity of Wikipedia? How might results change with a larger dataset?

2. In Table 4, your classifier achieves 88.99 F1 on pronominal mentions, but the baselines (like B4) achieve 84.17. How do you explain this large gap, and why do the baselines perform so poorly on pronouns?

3. The paper states that Wikipedia is "a large, multilingual, highly structured, multi-domain encyclopedia," but the evaluation is limited to English Wikipedia. How would your approach generalize to multilingual Wikipedia, and have you considered this aspect?

4. The authors claim their approach "outperforms state-of-the-art generic coreference resolution engines on this task," but the task is specifically identifying MC mentions, not full coreference resolution. Could you clarify how your approach relates to full coreference resolution, and why this specific task is a meaningful proxy for CR performance?

5. In Figure 3, Example (g) shows a case where the classifier incorrectly identifies "Houston" as referring to the football team rather than the city. Given that this is a common challenge in Wikipedia (multiple concepts with similar names), how does your approach handle such ambiguous cases?

## Overall Recommendation

This paper makes a valuable contribution by addressing the underexplored problem of adapting coreference resolution to Wikipedia and providing a new dataset for this purpose. The approach is well-motivated and effectively leverages Wikipedia's unique structural features. However, the small dataset size and limited comparison to Wikipedia-specific approaches are significant weaknesses that need addressing. With more thorough evaluation and clearer justification of the task's importance, this paper would be a strong contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses noted above.
