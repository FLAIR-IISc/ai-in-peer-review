

# Review of "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

## Summary of the Paper

This paper introduces a novel task called "Main Concept Resolution" for coreference resolution in Wikipedia articles. Instead of attempting full coreference resolution, the authors focus on the specific task of identifying all mentions of the main concept (MC) described in a Wikipedia article (e.g., all mentions of "Chilly Gonzales" in the article about Chilly Gonzales). They frame this as a binary classification problem (mention refers to MC or not), leveraging Wikipedia's unique markup structure and Freebase information to create features that outperform existing baselines. The authors demonstrate that their approach significantly outperforms baselines (13-15 F1 point improvement) and that integrating it into a rule-based coreference system improves overall coreference resolution performance.

## Primary Contributions (in order of strength)

1. **Novel Task Definition**: The paper identifies a clear gap in coreference resolution research - systems trained on news data perform poorly on Wikipedia, yet Wikipedia is a widely used resource in NLP. The authors propose a specific, well-defined subtask (main concept resolution) that leverages Wikipedia's unique structure.

2. **Task-Specific Approach**: The authors develop a binary classification approach specifically designed for Wikipedia, using the article's internal links, redirects, and Freebase information as features. This avoids the error propagation issues common in named-entity linking pipelines.

3. **Demonstrated Value**: The paper provides strong empirical evidence that their approach works significantly better than baselines for this specific task (89% F1 score), and that integrating it into a rule-based CR system improves coreference resolution performance (4 F1 point improvement).

## Strengths

1. **Significant Novelty**: The paper identifies a genuinely new research direction - rather than trying to adapt general CR systems to Wikipedia (which has been attempted before), the authors define a specific subtask that is well-suited to Wikipedia's structure. This is a bold conceptual shift that prioritizes novelty over incremental improvement.

2. **Effective Use of Wikipedia's Unique Features**: The authors make excellent use of Wikipedia's internal structure (hyperlinks, redirects, Freebase integration) to create high-quality features without relying on error-prone named entity linking. This is a creative and well-justified approach that directly addresses the limitations of existing methods.

3. **Clear Empirical Demonstration**: The paper provides compelling evidence of the approach's value with a 13-15 point F1 improvement over baselines and a 4-point improvement when integrated into a full CR system. The feature ablation study (Table 2) effectively demonstrates the contribution of each feature family.

4. **Reproducibility Commitment**: The authors plan to release their dataset and classifier outputs, which is a significant contribution to the community and will facilitate future research in this area.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Scope of the Task**: While the main concept resolution task is novel, it's a very specific subtask of coreference resolution. The paper doesn't convincingly argue why this specific task is more important or useful than general coreference resolution for Wikipedia-based applications.

2. **Small Dataset Size**: The WCR3 dataset is quite limited (30 documents, 60k tokens), which is a significant limitation for a machine learning approach. While the authors mention they tried to cover various characteristics, the small size makes it difficult to draw strong conclusions about generalizability.

3. **Lack of Comparison to Prior Wikipedia-Specific Work**: The paper mentions Nguyen et al. (2007) and Nakayama (2008) as prior work but doesn't compare to their systems. This is a missed opportunity to position the current work more clearly in the literature.

4. **Unclear Practical Utility**: The paper doesn't sufficiently explain why identifying mentions of the main concept is more useful than full coreference resolution for Wikipedia-based NLP applications. For example, in Open Information Extraction, wouldn't full coreference resolution be more valuable?

## Recommendation

**Accept**

This paper introduces a genuinely novel research direction that addresses a real gap in the literature. While the specific task is limited in scope, the conceptual shift from general coreference resolution to a task specifically designed for Wikipedia's structure is bold and valuable. The empirical results are strong for the specific task, and the approach effectively leverages Wikipedia's unique features.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold approaches, I find this paper compelling. The main contribution is the novel task definition and the demonstration that this specific subtask can be solved effectively with a Wikipedia-specific approach. The paper doesn't claim to solve the general coreference problem, but rather identifies a meaningful subtask that is well-suited to Wikipedia, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The weaknesses noted are important but don't outweigh the novelty and clear empirical contribution of the paper. The small dataset size is a limitation, but the paper acknowledges this and the results are still statistically significant for the task.

This paper makes a valuable contribution by identifying a new direction for coreference resolution research that is specifically tailored to Wikipedia, and demonstrates that this direction is fruitful. I recommend acceptance for this significant conceptual contribution.
