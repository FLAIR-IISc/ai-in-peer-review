

# Review of "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

## Summary of the Paper
The paper addresses the problem of coreference resolution (CR) specifically for Wikipedia articles, noting that existing CR systems trained on news-like texts (e.g., ACE, OntoNotes) perform poorly on Wikipedia. The authors introduce the "Main Concept Resolution" (MCR) task, which focuses on identifying all mentions of the main concept (MC) described in a Wikipedia article. They propose a binary classification approach to determine if a mention refers to the MC, leveraging features derived from Wikipedia markup and Freebase. The authors create a new dataset (WCR) of 30 Wikipedia articles annotated with coreference information, and evaluate their approach against several baselines built on state-of-the-art CR systems.

## Main Contributions (as I see them)
1. **A new dataset (WCR) for Main Concept Resolution**: The authors created a 30-article Wikipedia dataset with 60k tokens annotated according to OntoNotes guidelines, specifically designed for evaluating MCR systems. This fills a gap in the literature for CR evaluation on Wikipedia texts.

2. **A binary classification approach for MCR**: The authors propose a simple yet effective classification approach using features specifically designed for Wikipedia's structure (markup, redirects, Freebase links), which outperforms baselines by 13-15 F1 points.

3. **Demonstration of improved coreference resolution**: The authors show that integrating their MCR classifier into an existing rule-based CR system (Dcoref) improves overall CR performance on Wikipedia articles by 4 F1 points, demonstrating practical utility beyond the specific MCR task.

## Strengths

1. **Novel task definition with practical relevance**: The paper identifies a clear gap in the literature (lack of CR systems designed for Wikipedia) and defines a meaningful task (MCR) that is directly applicable to Wikipedia-based NLP systems. The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms existing CR systems on Wikipedia texts by a significant margin (13-15 F1 points), addressing an important practical problem.

2. **Effective use of Wikipedia-specific features**: The authors demonstrate a thoughtful approach to feature engineering that leverages Wikipedia's unique characteristics (markup, redirects, links to Freebase) rather than relying on generic NLP pipelines. The feature selection is well-motivated and directly tied to the structure of Wikipedia, with clear evidence of their effectiveness (e.g., the title feature family dramatically increases precision).

3. **Demonstrated practical impact**: The authors show that their MCR approach can be integrated into an existing CR system (Dcoref) to improve overall coreference resolution performance on Wikipedia by 4 F1 points. This demonstrates clear utility beyond just the MCR task, showing how their work can benefit broader CR applications.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited dataset size**: The WCR dataset is quite small (30 articles, 60k tokens), which is significantly smaller than standard CR datasets (e.g., OntoNotes has over 1 million tokens). This small size limits the statistical significance of the results and raises questions about how generalizable the findings are to larger Wikipedia corpora. The authors acknowledge this limitation but don't provide sufficient justification for why this small dataset is sufficient for their claims.

2. **Lack of comparison with prior Wikipedia-specific work**: The authors mention two prior works on Wikipedia coreference (Nguyen et al., 2007; Nakayama, 2008), but they do not compare their approach to these systems. Since these works are specifically designed for Wikipedia, not comparing to them makes it difficult to assess how much of an improvement their approach represents over prior work for this specific task.

3. **Unclear relationship to general coreference resolution**: While the paper focuses on identifying mentions of the main concept, it doesn't clearly explain how this relates to or improves general coreference resolution. The authors show that integrating their classifier into Dcoref improves overall CR performance, but they don't provide detailed analysis of how the MCR task contributes to the CR process, making it difficult to assess the broader impact of their work.

## Questions to Authors

1. The authors state they sampled Wikipedia articles of various characteristics but don't provide details about the sampling strategy. Could they provide more information about how the 30 articles in the WCR dataset were selected (e.g., criteria for topic diversity, size, internal link density)?

2. Given the small size of the WCR dataset (30 articles), the authors don't provide statistical significance testing for their results. How confident are they that the observed improvements (13-15 F1 points) are statistically significant given the limited data?

3. The paper mentions two prior works on Wikipedia coreference (Nguyen et al., 2007; Nakayama, 2008), but the authors don't compare their approach to these systems. Could they explain why they chose not to include these as baselines and how their approach differs from these prior works?

4. The authors claim their approach outperforms state-of-the-art generic CR systems on Wikipedia, but they don't provide a detailed analysis of why Wikipedia texts pose such challenges for existing CR systems. Could they elaborate on the specific characteristics of Wikipedia that cause these performance drops?
