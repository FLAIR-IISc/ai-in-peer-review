- Summary: Proposes Main Concept Resolution (MCR) to identify all mentions of an article’s main concept in Wikipedia, builds a classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase, creates the WCR dataset, and integrates into Dcoref to form Dcoref++ with improved performance.

- Contribution: Defines the MCR task and releases a new WCR evaluation dataset for Wikipedia-specific coreference.

- Contribution: Designs Wikipedia-specific features (links, redirects, categories, Freebase) to avoid NEL cascading errors.

- Contribution: Integrates MCR into a CR pipeline (Dcoref++) and shows end-to-end CR improvements on Wikipedia.

- Strength: Clearly identifies and quantifies a real performance gap of existing CR systems on Wikipedia text.

- Strength: Effectively leverages Wikipedia’s unique structure for feature engineering, outperforming generic CR features.

- Strength: Demonstrates practical utility by improving a full CR system (consistent gains across metrics).

- Weakness: Fails to compare experimentally with prior Wikipedia-specific CR work (Nguyen 2007; Nakayama 2008).

- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 articles, ~60k tokens) and possibly unrepresentative and English-only, limiting generalizability.

- Weakness: Limited theoretical novelty—mainly engineering using existing resources rather than new coreference theory.

- Weakness: Insufficient and shallow error analysis; lacks breakdown by error types and mention categories.

- Question: Provide details on article selection for WCR—how many were considered and what criteria ensured diversity?

- Question: Clarify annotation guidelines and inter-annotator agreement for labeling main concept mentions.

- Question: Explain the apparent arithmetic discrepancy in reported Dcoref++ CoNLL F1 improvement (3.34 vs. stated 4 points).

- Question: State which feature family (title, inferred type, name variants, entity type, main) most drives performance and why the feature set outperforms baselines.

- Question: Provide the actual URL for shared datasets and mention dumps (placeholder currently in paper).

- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing dataset size/representativeness, comparisons to prior Wikipedia CR work, and more thorough evaluation/analysis.
