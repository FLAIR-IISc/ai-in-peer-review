- Created WCR: a 30-article Wikipedia dataset for main concept resolution.  
- Proposed a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features to detect main-concept mentions.  
- Showed that integrating their classifier into a rule-based CR system (Lee et al., 2013) improves CR on Wikipedia.  

- Clearly identifies the gap that news-trained CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia and provides empirical evidence.  
- Leverages Wikipedia-specific signals (links, redirects, Freebase) to build effective, domain-aware features.  
- Achieves strong results (≈89% F1 on pronoun and non-pronoun mentions) and improves CoNLL F1 by 4 points when integrated into Dcoref.  
- Releases a useful resource (WCR dataset and classifier) for future research.  

- WCR is very small (30 articles, 60k tokens), and dataset selection/annotation details are insufficient.  
- Fails to compare directly with prior Wikipedia-focused coreference work (e.g., Nguyen 2007, Nakayama 2008).  
- Evaluation is limited to the binary MC task and lacks demonstration of benefit for downstream NLP applications.  
- Inadequate discussion of limitations (e.g., low-link articles, other languages, implicit main-concept mentions).  
- Missing systematic ablation of feature families and details of unsuccessful configurations.  

- How were the 30 WCR articles selected and do they represent diverse article types and link densities?  
- What was the annotation protocol, number of annotators, inter-annotator agreement, and criteria for main-concept mentions?  
- Why focus on main concept resolution rather than full CR, and how are articles with multiple important concepts handled?  
- How would the approach generalize to non-English Wikipedias and what adaptations would be required?  

- Overall recommendation: valuable and practical contribution addressing a clear gap; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing dataset size, annotation details, comparisons to prior Wikipedia-specific work, and limitations.
