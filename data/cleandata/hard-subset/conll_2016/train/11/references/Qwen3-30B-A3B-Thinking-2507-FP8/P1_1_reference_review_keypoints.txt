- Summary: Proposes a binary main-concept (MC) resolution task for Wikipedia, creates WCR dataset, uses Wikipedia/Freebase features to train a classifier that outperforms standard CR systems and improves Dcoref.

- Contribution: Introduces WCR, the first curated dataset for Wikipedia coreference evaluation.

- Contribution: Defines a practically useful MC-resolution task distinct from full coreference resolution.

- Contribution: Proposes novel feature engineering leveraging Wikipedia links, redirects, and Freebase for effective MC classification.

- Strength: Significant dataset contribution showing standard CR systems drop in performance on Wikipedia.

- Strength: Well-motivated, practically relevant task with empirical evidence that existing systems fail on it.

- Strength: Effective feature engineering that avoids heavy reliance on named-entity linking; ablation studies show feature importance.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains (13–15 F1 on MC task; +4 CoNLL F1 when integrated into Dcoref) with statistical support.

- Strength: Practical utility and reproducibility via released code and data.

- Weakness: Extremely small WCR size (30 articles, 60k tokens), limiting generalizability and raising statistical concerns.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to prior Wikipedia-specific CR work (Nguyen 2007; Nakayama 2008), relying instead on generic baselines.

- Weakness: Evaluation framed as binary MC F1 rather than standard CR metrics, potentially misrepresenting scope.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of why Wikipedia differs from news domains and how that impacts CR.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of failure cases and error types produced by the classifier.

- Question: Can the authors scale WCR (e.g., semi-automatic annotation) to address dataset size and generalizability?

- Question: Why were Nguyen (2007) and Nakayama (2008) not implemented as baselines, and how does this approach differ from them?

- Question: How does MC detection fit into a full coreference pipeline beyond improving MC mentions?

- Question: Why does the rule-based Dcoref outperform ML systems on WCR, and does that suggest different CR strategies for Wikipedia?

- Question: How does the Dcoref integration improvement compare to other adaptation methods for Wikipedia CR?

- Recommendation: Paper is well-written and valuable; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset size and contextual comparisons.
