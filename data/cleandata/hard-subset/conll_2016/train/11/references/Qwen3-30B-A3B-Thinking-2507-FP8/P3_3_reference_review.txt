

# Peer Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

## Summary and Contributions
This paper addresses a critical gap in coreference resolution (CR) systems, which are predominantly trained on news text but perform poorly on Wikipedia articles. The authors define a specific task: identifying all mentions of the "main concept" (MC) in a Wikipedia article (e.g., all mentions of "Chilly Gonzales" in its article). Their key contributions are: (1) Creation of the WCR dataset (30 Wikipedia articles, 60k tokens) annotated for coreference; (2) A binary classification approach for identifying MC mentions using Wikipedia-specific features; (3) Demonstration that their approach outperforms baselines by 13-15 F1 points and improves full CR when integrated into Dcoref.

## Strengths

1. **Compelling identification of a significant gap**: The paper convincingly demonstrates that state-of-the-art CR systems (e.g., Scoref) drop dramatically in performance on Wikipedia (46.39 vs. 63.61 F1 on OntoNotes), confirming the need for Wikipedia-specific approaches. This addresses a meaningful problem that has been overlooked despite Wikipedia's prominence in NLP.

2. **Thoughtful exploitation of Wikipedia's unique structure**: The authors wisely leverage Wikipedia's rich markup (redirects, internal links, Freebase connections) to extract high-quality features without relying on error-prone named-entity linking pipelines. This avoids cascading errors that plague previous approaches (Section 2), making their feature extraction method particularly elegant for this text genre.

3. **Strong empirical validation**: The ablation study (Table 2) clearly demonstrates the incremental value of each feature family, with the most complex classifier achieving 89% F1. The integration into Dcoref (Table 5) showing a 4-point F1 improvement is particularly compelling evidence of practical utility.

## Weaknesses

1. **Insufficiently representative dataset**: The WCR dataset is extremely small (30 articles), making generalizability questionable. The authors acknowledge this but provide no evidence that their results generalize beyond this tiny sample. While they mention "various characteristics" (size, topic, link density), this claim lacks statistical support. A dataset of 30 articles is inadequate for meaningful evaluation of CR systems, especially given the complexity of the task.

2. **Lack of comparison to specialized Wikipedia CR systems**: The authors mention Nguyen et al. (2007) and Nakayama (2008) as previous work but fail to compare their approach to these specialized Wikipedia CR systems. This is a critical omission since their contribution claims to "revisit" this task. Without such comparison, it's unclear whether their approach represents a meaningful improvement over prior specialized work.

3. **Questionable task formulation**: The binary classification task (is this mention the MC?) is somewhat artificial for coreference resolution. The paper doesn't adequately address how identifying MC mentions translates to full coreference resolution. While they show integration into Dcoref improves CR performance, the paper doesn't demonstrate that their approach specifically addresses the core challenges of Wikipedia CR beyond a simple filtering mechanism.

## Questions to Authors

1. How do you justify using such a small dataset (30 articles) for evaluation? What evidence do you have that this sample is representative of the broader Wikipedia corpus?

2. Why didn't you compare your approach to the specialized Wikipedia CR systems by Nguyen et al. (2007) and Nakayama (2008), which were explicitly designed for this text genre?

3. In Figure 3(f), your classifier incorrectly associates "it" with the MC instead of "Johnston Atoll Safeguard C mission." How would you address this specific error case, and what would be the impact on your overall F1 score?

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a valuable contribution by highlighting a significant gap in CR systems for Wikipedia and providing a well-motivated approach to address it. The exploitation of Wikipedia's unique structure is particularly strong. However, the extremely small dataset size and lack of comparison to prior specialized work substantially weaken the contribution claims. While the results are promising, they are limited by the scale of the evaluation. With a larger, more representative dataset and comparison to prior specialized systems, this work could make a substantial contribution to the field. As it stands, the paper demonstrates a promising direction but lacks sufficient evidence to claim significant advancement.

**Recommendation: Weak Accept** (with major revisions addressing dataset size and comparative evaluation)
