- New dataset (WCR): 30 Wikipedia articles (~60k tokens) annotated for Main Concept Resolution using OntoNotes guidelines.  
- Task defined: Main Concept Resolution (MCR) — identify all mentions of the article’s main concept.  
- Method: Binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features to decide if a mention is the main concept.  
- Performance: Classifier outperforms state-of-the-art CR systems on Wikipedia by ~13–15 F1 points (89% vs 76% best baseline).  
- Integration: Adding the classifier to Dcoref improves overall coreference resolution on Wikipedia by ~4 F1.  
- Strength: Clearly identifies and evidences a significant gap — newswire-trained CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation and feature analysis showing incremental feature value.  
- Strength: Clever use of Wikipedia’s markup (redirects, internal links) as a reliable substitute for NEL.  
- Strength: Demonstrated practical utility via integration into an existing CR system.  
- Weakness: Small dataset size and limited diversity (30 docs) raise concerns about statistical significance and bias.  
- Weakness: Narrow task scope — MCR is less general than full coreference resolution and broader relevance is not fully justified.  
- Weakness: No direct experimental comparison to earlier Wikipedia-focused CR work (e.g., Nguyen 2007, Nakayama 2008).  
- Weakness: Unclear how well the approach generalizes to other genres or less-structured text.  
- Question: Provide per-document distribution of the ~1,800 MC mentions and variation across article types (people vs organizations, etc.).  
- Question: Show specific examples where the classifier changes coreference chain detection compared to the baseline.  
- Question: Explain why the rule-based system (Lee et al., 2013) outperforms ML systems on WCR and whether Wikipedia requires different CR approaches.  
- Overall: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset size concerns and clarify generalizability and broader implications.
