

# Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

## Summary
This paper addresses a critical gap in coreference resolution (CR) research: the poor performance of existing CR systems on Wikipedia text, which is widely used in NLP but under-studied for CR. The authors propose a specialized task of identifying all mentions of the "main concept" (MC) in a Wikipedia article (e.g., all references to "Chilly Gonzales" in an article about him). They introduce the WCR dataset (30 Wikipedia articles, 60k tokens) and a binary classification approach using features derived from Wikipedia markup and Freebase. Their method significantly outperforms baselines and improves a state-of-the-art CR system when integrated.

## Main Contributions (as I see them)
1. **WCR dataset**: The first dedicated dataset for evaluating coreference resolution on Wikipedia articles, specifically annotated for main concept mentions.
2. **Wikipedia-specific CR approach**: A novel classification method leveraging Wikipedia's unique structural features (links, redirects, Freebase) rather than generic CR features.
3. **Demonstrated value of genre-specific CR**: Clear evidence that Wikipedia requires different handling than news text, with significant performance gains for Wikipedia-specific approaches.

## Strengths

1. **Clear identification of a significant research gap**: The paper effectively establishes that existing CR systems trained on news data (ACE, OntoNotes) perform poorly on Wikipedia. The authors' empirical demonstration (Table 1 showing 10-15 point F1 drops on WCR vs. OntoNotes) is convincing and well-justified. This is a strong motivation for their work.

2. **Valuable new dataset (WCR)**: Creating a specialized Wikipedia coreference dataset is a substantial contribution. The authors provide detailed annotation guidelines (OntoNotes-based), document the dataset's characteristics (diverse article sizes, topics, link densities), and make it publicly available. This dataset fills a critical gap in the field and will enable future research on Wikipedia coreference.

3. **Effective use of Wikipedia-specific features**: The authors' approach is smartly designed to leverage Wikipedia's unique structure. By using hyperlinks to extract attributes (entity type, gender, etc.) instead of relying on error-prone named-entity linking pipelines, they achieve higher precision. Their feature engineering (e.g., using title, name variants, inferred types) is well-motivated and directly addresses Wikipedia's characteristics.

4. **Significant performance gains**: The results are compelling - their classifier achieves 88.79% F1 on all mentions, a 13-15 point absolute gain over the best baseline. The integration with Dcoref (Dcoref++) also shows a 4-point F1 improvement on the full CR task, demonstrating practical value beyond the specialized main concept task.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited dataset size and scope**: The WCR dataset is quite small (30 articles, 60k tokens), which limits the statistical significance of the results. The authors acknowledge this but don't adequately address how this small size affects the reliability of their claims. With such a small dataset, it's difficult to determine whether the improvements are consistent across different types of Wikipedia articles.

2. **Insufficient comparison to Wikipedia-specific baselines**: While the authors compare to generic CR systems (Dcoref, Scoref), they don't compare to any existing Wikipedia-specific CR approaches. The only prior work cited (Nguyen et al., 2007; Nakayama, 2008) didn't focus on CR but on information extraction. A proper comparison to any existing Wikipedia CR system would strengthen their claim that their approach is better suited for Wikipedia.

3. **Lack of analysis on why Wikipedia is different**: The paper states that Wikipedia requires different CR approaches but doesn't thoroughly analyze *why* this is the case. A deeper linguistic or structural analysis of Wikipedia text (e.g., more frequent pronoun usage, different sentence structures, more explicit entity mentions) would strengthen the motivation and help explain their approach's success.

4. **Limited error analysis**: The paper provides examples of classification errors (Figure 3), but doesn't systematically analyze these errors to understand the limitations of their approach. A more thorough error analysis would help identify where their method fails and suggest future improvements.

## Questions to Authors

1. Given the small size of the WCR dataset (30 articles), how confident are you that the 13-15 point F1 improvement over baselines is statistically significant? Could you provide confidence intervals for your results?

2. The paper mentions that Wikipedia articles are "well-formed, grammatical and meaningful compared to ordinary internet documents." Could you elaborate on specific linguistic or structural features that make Wikipedia different from news text in ways that affect coreference resolution?

3. The authors state that "the integration of our classifier into the state-of-the-art rule-based coreference system of Lee et al. (2013) improves the detection of coreference chains." Could you clarify whether this improvement is specific to the main concept chains or extends to all coreference chains in Wikipedia articles?

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a valuable contribution by addressing a significant gap in coreference resolution research for Wikipedia. The creation of the WCR dataset is particularly noteworthy and will be useful for future work. The authors' approach is well-motivated and demonstrates clear performance gains over baselines. However, the small dataset size and lack of comparison to existing Wikipedia-specific CR approaches are notable limitations.

The paper's strengths outweigh its weaknesses, and it represents an important step toward genre-specific coreference resolution. I recommend acceptance with the understanding that future work should expand the dataset and provide deeper analysis of Wikipedia's linguistic characteristics. The authors' decision to make their dataset and classifier results publicly available is commendable and will facilitate further research in this area.

**Overall Rating: Strong Accept**
