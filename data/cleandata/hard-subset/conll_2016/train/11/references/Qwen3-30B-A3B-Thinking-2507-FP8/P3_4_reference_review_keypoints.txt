- Task: Defines "main concept resolution" (MCR)—identify all mentions of an article's central concept in Wikipedia.  
- Dataset: Introduces WCR3, 30 Wikipedia articles annotated for main concept mentions (~60k tokens, ~7k mentions).  
- Method: Proposes a binary classifier using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features to detect main-concept mentions.  
- Strength: Addresses a real domain-adaptation gap—news-trained CR systems perform poorly on Wikipedia.  
- Strength: Effectively leverages Wikipedia structure (links, redirects, Freebase) to avoid cascading NER/linking errors.  
- Strength: Clear task framing as binary classification with appropriate precision/recall/F1 evaluation.  
- Weakness: Dataset is very small and not clearly representative, limiting generalizability and strength of claims.  
- Weakness: MCR is a narrow subtask with limited demonstrated practical impact on full coreference resolution (only ~4 F1 improvement).  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons are limited and omit stronger or recent domain-adaptation/Wikipedia-specific CR approaches.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks detailed error analysis and examples to explain remaining failures (e.g., pronominal errors).  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—approach builds on prior use of external knowledge (Ratinov & Roth, Hajishirzi et al.) without clear differentiation.  
- Question: How do you justify the small 30-article dataset and how would you scale or validate broader applicability?  
- Question: How does the modest improvement when integrated into Dcoref compare to other domain-adaptation methods for Wikipedia CR?  
- Question: How does integrating this classifier compare to simply retraining a standard CR system on Wikipedia data?  
- Overall assessment: Valuable first step leveraging Wikipedia structure, but insufficient evidence (small data, limited comparisons, modest impact) to claim significant advances.
