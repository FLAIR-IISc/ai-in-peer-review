- Paper identifies a gap: newswire-trained coreference systems perform poorly on Wikipedia and proposes Main Concept Resolution (MCR) — a binary task to detect mentions of an article’s central entity using Wikipedia markup and Freebase.  
- Contributions: defines the MCR task, releases the WCR dataset (30 articles, ~60k tokens), and shows integration into Dcoref yields +4 F1 on Wikipedia.  
- Strength: strong problem identification with empirical evidence of performance drop on Wikipedia.  
- Strength: high-quality, publicly released WCR dataset with OntoNotes-style annotation and diverse article selection.  
- Strength: practical impact demonstrated by improving an existing CR system (Dcoref++) on Wikipedia text.  
- Weakness: misalignment between claims and task — MCR is a narrower binary problem, yet framed and compared as full coreference resolution.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to prior Wikipedia-specific CR work (e.g., Nguyen et al., 2007) and lack of clarity on novelty.  
- Weakness: small dataset (30 articles) raises concerns about statistical significance and generalizability; 10-fold CV may overestimate performance.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of why Wikipedia-specific features help; feature ablations are shallow and don’t explain mechanisms (e.g., redirects, links).  
- Weakness: some claims overstate novelty and implications given task scope and prior work differences.  
- Key questions: why frame as binary MCR instead of full CR; how handle articles with multiple key entities; why no direct comparison to Nguyen et al. (2007); why rule-based methods beat ML on WCR; what features fix pronoun errors like the “it” example?  
- Recommendation: weak accept with major revisions — clarify MCR vs full CR, compare to Wikipedia-specific prior work, enlarge or better justify dataset, and provide deeper analysis of Wikipedia-specific features.
