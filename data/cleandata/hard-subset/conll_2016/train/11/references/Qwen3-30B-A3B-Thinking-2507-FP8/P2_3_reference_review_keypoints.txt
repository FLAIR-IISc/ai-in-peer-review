- Paper introduces the task of identifying all mentions of the "main concept" in a Wikipedia article and proposes solutions for it.
- Introduces WCR: a new dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles (~60k tokens) annotated for main concept mentions.
- Proposes a Wikipedia-specific binary classification approach using Wikipedia markup and Freebase-derived features.
- Demonstrates that Wikipedia-specific features outperform generic CR features and avoid error-prone NER/linking pipelines.
- Achieves strong results: 88.79% F1 on all mentions, 13–15 point gain over baselines.
- Integration of the classifier into Dcoref yields a ~4-point F1 improvement on full CR chains.
- Clearly motivates the need for genre-specific CR by showing large performance drops when applying news-trained systems to Wikipedia.
- Strength: provides detailed annotation guidelines and publicly releases the dataset for future work.
- Strength: feature engineering effectively leverages links, redirects, titles, and inferred types from Wikipedia.
- Weakness: WCR dataset is small, limiting statistical reliability and generalizability of results.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to any existing Wikipedia-specific coreference approaches.
- Weakness: paper doesn't deeply analyze the linguistic/structural reasons Wikipedia differs from news for CR.
- Weakness: error analysis is limited and not systematic enough to diagnose failure modes.
- Questions: request for statistical significance/confidence intervals given small dataset.
- Questions: asks for elaboration on specific linguistic/structural differences between Wikipedia and news.
- Questions: asks whether the Dcoref integration improvement applies only to main concept chains or to all coreference chains.
- Overall assessment: valuable, well-motivated contribution with public dataset release; recommend acceptance but urge dataset expansion and deeper analyses.
