- Paper addresses poor coreference resolution on Wikipedia by identifying all mentions of an article's "main concept" (MC) via binary classification using Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.  
- Contribution: created WCR, a new dataset of 30 Wikipedia articles (60k tokens) for evaluating coreference on Wikipedia.  
- Contribution: proposed a binary classification approach leveraging Wikipedia links, redirects, and Freebase that outperforms baselines by 13–15 F1 on MC identification.  
- Contribution: integrating the classifier into a rule-based CR system (Dcoref++) yields a 4 F1 improvement on WCR.  
- Strength: clearly demonstrates that state-of-the-art CR systems trained on news perform poorly on Wikipedia, highlighting a genuine gap.  
- Strength: effectively leverages Wikipedia's structural features (links, redirects) instead of error-prone NEL pipelines.  
- Strength: provides a valuable, publicly released dataset with attention to article diversity and reproducibility.  
- Strength: thorough experimental comparisons to multiple baselines and integration experiments validate effectiveness.  
- Strength: practical applicability shown by improving existing CR systems when integrated.  
- Weakness: WCR dataset is small (30 articles), limiting statistical significance and confidence in results.  
- Weakness: unclear distinction from or detailed comparison to prior work (e.g., Nakayama 2008).  
- Weakness: limited justification of the MC identification task's downstream utility beyond a few examples.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other Wikipedia-specific coreference approaches, raising questions about novelty.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations, especially handling ambiguous mentions referring to multiple concepts.  
- Question: provide more details on article selection criteria (categories, sizes, link densities) and expected effects of scaling the dataset.  
- Question: explain the large pronominal performance gap (classifier 88.99 F1 vs baselines ~84.17) and why baselines struggle on pronouns.  
- Question: clarify generalization to multilingual Wikipedia and whether the approach extends beyond English.  
- Question: justify how MC identification relates to full coreference resolution and why it is a meaningful proxy for CR performance.  
- Question: explain handling of ambiguous mentions (e.g., "Houston" city vs. football team) and how the approach resolves such cases.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution addressing Wikipedia CR with a novel approach and dataset, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset size, comparisons, and task justification.
