

# Review of "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

## Summary of the Paper
This paper introduces a novel task called "Main Concept Resolution" for Wikipedia articles, which focuses on identifying all mentions of the main concept (MC) described in a Wikipedia article (e.g., all mentions of "Chilly Gonzales" in the article about Chilly Gonzales). The authors argue that standard coreference resolution systems, trained on news-like texts, perform poorly on Wikipedia. They propose a binary classification approach that leverages Wikipedia's unique structure (internal links, redirects, Freebase integration) to extract features for identifying MC mentions. The authors create a new dataset (WCR) for this task and demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems by 13 absolute F1 points.

## Primary Contributions (as I see them)

1. **Defining a new task (Main Concept Resolution) for Wikipedia coreference**: This is the most significant contribution, as it shifts the focus from general coreference resolution to a specific, important application for Wikipedia, creating a new research direction.

2. **Leveraging Wikipedia's unique structure for feature extraction**: The authors cleverly use Wikipedia's markup (links, redirects) to extract features without needing named-entity linking, which is a novel approach specific to Wikipedia.

3. **Demonstrating substantial performance gains**: The reported 13-point absolute F1 gain over the best baseline is significant and validates the novelty of their approach.

## Strengths

1. **Bold Novelty**: This paper defines a completely new task that hasn't been explored before. The authors don't just tweak existing coreference resolution methods—they identify a specific need in Wikipedia processing that requires a fundamentally different approach. This is exactly the kind of bold innovation I value as a reviewer.

2. **Leveraging Wikipedia's Unique Structure**: The authors' use of Wikipedia's internal links, redirects, and Freebase integration to extract features is a clever and novel approach that avoids the pitfalls of named-entity linking. This is a brilliant adaptation to the specific characteristics of Wikipedia, rather than forcing a generic approach onto this unique text genre.

3. **Significant Performance Gains**: The reported 13-point absolute F1 gain over the best baseline is substantial and makes a strong case for the value of their approach. For a task that's not been properly addressed before, this is a major contribution.

4. **Practical Impact**: The approach has clear applications for Wikipedia-based NLP tasks, such as information extraction, making it valuable beyond just academic interest. The authors demonstrate how their classifier can be integrated into existing systems to improve coreference resolution performance.

5. **New Dataset Creation**: The authors created WCR, a dedicated dataset for this task, which is valuable for the community and enables future research in this specific area.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Dataset Size**: While the authors argue the dataset is representative, 30 documents (60k tokens) is quite small for a coreference resolution task. However, as the task is novel and the authors provide a strong justification for their selection criteria, this is a minor issue given the novelty of the work.

2. **Narrow Focus on Wikipedia**: While the authors make a compelling case for Wikipedia's importance, some might argue the approach is too narrowly focused. However, as the paper's contribution is specifically for Wikipedia, this limitation is acceptable given the novelty of the task.

3. **Integration Details**: The paper doesn't provide sufficient detail on how the classifier is integrated into Dcoref (Dcoref++), which could make reproduction difficult. However, the authors have committed to sharing the code and data, which mitigates this concern.

4. **Comparison to Previous Wikipedia-Specific Work**: The authors mention Nguyen et al. (2007) and Nakayama (2008) but don't directly compare their approach to those systems. This would have strengthened the novelty claim.

5. **Lack of Generalization Study**: The paper doesn't explore how well the approach would work on other text genres beyond Wikipedia. However, since the task is specifically defined for Wikipedia, this is not a critical weakness for this submission.

## Recommendation

**Accept**. This paper introduces a bold, novel approach to coreference resolution that addresses a significant gap in the literature. The Main Concept Resolution task is a fresh perspective that has clear practical applications for Wikipedia-based NLP. The authors demonstrate substantial performance gains (13-point absolute F1 improvement) that validate their approach. While the dataset size is small, the novelty of the task and the significant performance gains outweigh this limitation.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novel ideas over methodological perfection, I'm particularly impressed with the authors' willingness to define a new task rather than just tweak existing methods. The paper opens up a new research direction in coreference resolution that could have significant implications for Wikipedia-based NLP systems.

## Questions to Authors

1. How would your approach perform on other text genres beyond Wikipedia, such as news articles or social media posts, and what adaptations would be needed?

2. Could you provide more details on the integration of your classifier into Dcoref (Dcoref++)? Specifically, how the sieve is implemented and how it affects the coreference chains.

3. The paper mentions "noisy redirects" as a source of errors in the name variants feature. Could you elaborate on how you mitigate this issue in practice?

4. How much improvement did you see on the full coreference resolution task (not just the main concept resolution task) when integrating your classifier into Dcoref?

5. Could you provide more details on how you selected the 30 Wikipedia articles for the WCR dataset to ensure they represent the diversity of Wikipedia articles?
