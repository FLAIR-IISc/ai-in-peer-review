

# Review of "Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution"

## Summary of the Paper

This paper introduces a novel subtask of coreference resolution called "Main Concept Resolution" (MCR) for Wikipedia articles. The task involves identifying all mentions of the main concept (the central entity described in a Wikipedia article) rather than performing full coreference resolution. The authors propose a binary classification approach that determines whether a mention refers to the main concept, leveraging features extracted from Wikipedia markup and Freebase. They demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on this specific task, and they show how integrating their classifier into an existing rule-based system (Dcoref) improves full coreference resolution performance on Wikipedia articles.

## Main Contributions (in order of strength)

1. **Novel Task Definition**: The paper identifies and formally defines "Main Concept Resolution" as a distinct task, addressing a gap in the literature where Wikipedia-specific coreference has been studied only in the context of information extraction, not as a standalone coreference task.

2. **Wikipedia-Specific Adaptation**: The authors propose a method that directly leverages Wikipedia's unique structural features (hyperlinks, redirects, Freebase connections) rather than relying on generic NER/NEL pipelines, which is a bold and clever approach to the problem.

3. **Significant Performance Gains**: The proposed approach achieves substantial improvements over state-of-the-art coreference resolution systems on the specific task of identifying main concept mentions (13-15 absolute F1 points), demonstrating the practical value of the novel task framing.

## Strengths

1. **High Novelty of Task Framing**: The paper's most compelling contribution is the clear identification and definition of "Main Concept Resolution" as a distinct problem. While coreference resolution on Wikipedia has been mentioned in prior work (Nguyen et al., 2007; Nakayama, 2008), this is the first paper to frame it as a specific task with its own evaluation metrics, rather than just a component of information extraction. This is a significant conceptual contribution that opens up new research directions.

2. **Leveraging Wikipedia's Unique Structure**: The authors' approach to using Wikipedia's markup (hyperlinks, redirects, Freebase connections) as a source of features is elegant and directly addresses the problem of adapting CR to Wikipedia. This is a bold approach that demonstrates deep understanding of Wikipedia's unique characteristics, and it's far more effective than generic approaches that rely on external NER/NEL pipelines.

3. **Practical Impact Demonstrated**: The paper doesn't just present a new task; it shows concrete benefits by integrating the classifier into an existing CR system (Dcoref++) and demonstrating improved performance on the full coreference resolution task. This shows that the work has real-world applicability beyond just the MCR task.

4. **Resource Contribution**: The authors provide a new dataset (WCR) specifically for evaluating main concept resolution, which is a valuable contribution to the community. This dataset will enable future work on this specific task.

5. **Clear Demonstration of Wikipedia-Specific Challenges**: The paper effectively demonstrates that Wikipedia text poses unique challenges for generic coreference resolution systems, with results showing a significant performance drop (10-15 points F1) compared to standard datasets like OntoNotes.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Scope of the Task**: While the task is novel, it's quite specific to Wikipedia articles. The paper doesn't sufficiently argue why this specific subtask is more important than full coreference resolution for Wikipedia-based NLP applications, or how broadly applicable this approach is beyond Wikipedia.

2. **Small Dataset Size**: The WCR dataset consists of only 30 articles (60k tokens), which is quite small for a coreference resolution study. While the authors acknowledge this, the limited size makes it difficult to claim that the results are representative of the full diversity of Wikipedia articles.

3. **Overstated Novelty Compared to Related Work**: The paper cites prior work (Ratinov and Roth, 2012; Hajishirzi et al., 2013) that also used external knowledge sources for CR, but doesn't sufficiently differentiate how their approach is novel in this context. The use of Wikipedia-specific features is innovative, but the general approach of using external knowledge for CR is not entirely new.

4. **Lack of Analysis of Failure Cases**: The paper identifies some examples where the classifier fails (e.g., Figure 3), but doesn't provide a thorough analysis of why these failures occur or how they could be addressed. This limits the understanding of the approach's limitations.

5. **Limited Generalization to Other Domains**: The paper doesn't explore how this approach might be adapted to other text genres or domains beyond Wikipedia, which would strengthen the case for the broader significance of the work.

## Questions to Authors

1. How do you envision the main concept resolution task being integrated into larger NLP pipelines beyond Wikipedia-specific applications? What other text genres or domains might benefit from this specific approach?

2. The paper mentions that the rule-based system (Dcoref) outperforms statistical systems on the WCR dataset, but the performance gap is relatively small (51.77 vs. 49.52 F1). How much of this performance difference is due to the specific characteristics of Wikipedia, and how much might be due to the specific implementations of the systems?

3. Could you provide more details on the diversity of the 30 Wikipedia articles in the WCR dataset? How were the articles selected to ensure they represent the full spectrum of Wikipedia content?

4. The authors claim that the main concept resolution task is "a prerequisite for many NLP tasks," but don't provide concrete examples of NLP tasks where this specific approach would be more beneficial than full coreference resolution. Could you elaborate on this?

5. How scalable is your approach to the full Wikipedia corpus? What are the computational challenges of applying your classifier to all Wikipedia articles?

## Overall Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** for this paper. While the paper has some weaknesses, particularly regarding the limited scope of the task and the small dataset size, its core contribution—the novel framing of Main Concept Resolution as a distinct task and the demonstration of a Wikipedia-specific adaptation approach—is significant and aligns with the values of novelty and boldness that I prioritize as a reviewer.

The paper identifies a real gap in the literature, proposes a clear and innovative solution that directly leverages Wikipedia's unique structure, and demonstrates substantial performance gains. The integration of the classifier into Dcoref to improve full coreference resolution further demonstrates practical value. While the dataset size is limited, the paper's main contribution is conceptual (defining a new task and demonstrating its feasibility), which outweighs the methodological limitations in this case.

The paper's novelty and clear demonstration of a Wikipedia-specific adaptation approach make it a valuable contribution to the field, and I believe it will inspire future work on adapting NLP systems to domain-specific text structures.
