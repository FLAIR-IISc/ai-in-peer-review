

# Review: Coreference in Wikipedia: Main Concept Resolution

## Summary and Main Contributions

The paper addresses the specific task of identifying mentions of the "main concept" (MC) in Wikipedia articles - the concept being described by the article itself. The authors note that existing coreference resolution (CR) systems, trained on news-like texts, perform poorly on Wikipedia, and propose a focused solution for this specific subtask rather than general CR.

The primary contributions of this work are:
1. Creation of a new dataset (WCR3) specifically for evaluating main concept resolution in Wikipedia, consisting of 30 documents (60k tokens) annotated with OntoNotes guidelines.
2. A binary classification approach for identifying mentions of the main concept using features derived from Wikipedia markup and Freebase, with separate models for pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.
3. Demonstration that their approach outperforms baselines on the main concept resolution task (88.99% F1 for pronominal mentions, 88.67% for non-pronominal) and that integrating it into an existing CR system (Dcoref) improves overall coreference resolution performance by 4% absolute F1.

## Strengths

1. **Clear identification of a specific gap in the literature**: The paper effectively demonstrates that state-of-the-art CR systems trained on news data perform significantly worse on Wikipedia (Table 1), with F1 scores dropping from 60+ on OntoNotes to around 50 on WCR. This establishes a clear need for Wikipedia-specific CR approaches.

2. **Well-constructed dataset and evaluation**: The WCR3 dataset is properly annotated with OntoNotes guidelines, includes a representative sample of Wikipedia articles across different topics and structures, and is shared with the community. The evaluation framework is comprehensive, comparing against multiple baselines and demonstrating both task-specific performance and impact on broader CR.

3. **Technical soundness of the approach**: The feature engineering is well-motivated and leverages Wikipedia's unique characteristics (markup, redirects, internal links) effectively. The authors carefully distinguish between pronominal and non-pronominal features, and the incremental feature addition (Table 2) demonstrates clear improvements in performance with each added feature family.

4. **Demonstrated practical utility**: The paper shows that the main concept resolution approach can be integrated into existing CR systems (Dcoref++) to improve overall coreference resolution performance on Wikipedia, with a 4% absolute F1 gain (Table 5). This demonstrates tangible value beyond just the specific subtask.

5. **Strong experimental validation**: The paper provides thorough ablation studies (Tables 2-3) showing the contribution of each feature family, and the results are consistent across multiple evaluation metrics (precision, recall, F1) for both pronominal and non-pronominal mentions.

## Weaknesses

1. **Narrow scope of the task**: The paper focuses on a very specific subtask (identifying mentions of the main concept) rather than addressing general coreference resolution in Wikipedia. This limits the broader applicability of the work, as the main concept resolution task is a highly constrained problem that doesn't solve the full coreference resolution challenge.

2. **Small dataset size**: The WCR3 dataset is quite small (30 documents, 60k tokens, 1800 main concept mentions out of 7000 annotated mentions). While the authors acknowledge this limitation, they don't sufficiently justify why this small dataset is sufficient for their claims, especially given the statistical significance concerns with such limited data.

3. **Lack of clear differentiation from related work**: The paper cites Nguyen et al. (2007) and Nakayama (2008) as prior work on main concept resolution, but doesn't clearly articulate how their approach differs from or improves upon these studies. The novelty claim is somewhat weak given these prior studies.

4. **Limited analysis of failure cases**: While the paper mentions that most errors are difficult coreference cases, it doesn't provide in-depth analysis of these cases or discuss why the approach fails in specific situations. More detailed error analysis would strengthen the paper's claims.

5. **Insufficient discussion of broader applicability**: The paper doesn't adequately address how the approach would generalize to other languages or Wikipedia structures beyond English. The focus on English Wikipedia limits the perceived contribution to the broader NLP community.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper claims their approach "outperforms state-of-the-art generic coreference resolution engines on this task," but doesn't clearly explain how this specific task (main concept resolution) differs from the general coreference resolution task. Can the authors clarify the relationship between their task and the standard CR task, and why this specific subtask is valuable for the broader NLP community?

2. Given the small dataset size (30 documents), how do the authors justify that the reported performance gains (e.g., 13-15 points F1 over baselines) are statistically significant and not due to dataset-specific quirks? What additional validation would be needed to strengthen these claims?

3. The paper mentions that their approach uses Wikipedia markup and Freebase, but doesn't discuss potential limitations when these resources are incomplete or when Wikipedia structure changes. How would the approach handle cases where Wikipedia redirects or Freebase entries are missing or inaccurate?

4. The authors state that "the integration of our classifier into the state-of-the-art rule-based coreference system of Lee et al. (2013) improves the detection of coreference chains." Can the authors provide specific examples of articles where this integration led to meaningful improvements in coreference chain detection?

5. The paper focuses exclusively on English Wikipedia. How would the authors expect their approach to generalize to other language Wikipedias, particularly those with different structural conventions or markup patterns?
