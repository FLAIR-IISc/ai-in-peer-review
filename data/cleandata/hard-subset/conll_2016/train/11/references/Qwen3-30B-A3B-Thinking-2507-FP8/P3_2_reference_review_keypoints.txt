- Paper identifies poor performance of standard coreference systems on Wikipedia and defines the new task "Main Concept Resolution" (MCR) to find all mentions of an article's main concept.
- Authors create the WCR dataset (30 Wikipedia articles, OntoNotes-style annotations) for MCR evaluation.
- Propose a binary classifier using Wikipedia-specific features (markup, redirects, Freebase) to distinguish main-concept mentions.
- Classifier outperforms Dcoref and Scoref baselines by 13–15 F1 points on MCR and improves Dcoref by 4 F1 when integrated.
- Novel, well-motivated task addressing practical NLP needs and a documented gap in CR research on Wikipedia text.
- WCR dataset is carefully constructed and publicly released, showing state-of-the-art CR systems perform poorly on it.
- Demonstrates practical integration of MCR into an existing CR pipeline, yielding measurable improvement.
- Strong feature engineering that leverages Wikipedia structure and external knowledge; incremental feature analysis provided.
- High reported F1 scores for pronoun and non-pronoun mentions, indicating substantial empirical gains.
- WCR dataset is very small (30 articles, ~60k tokens), limiting training power and generalizability of results.
- Paper omits direct comparison to prior Wikipedia-specific CR work (Nguyen et al., 2007; Nakayama, 2008), weakening baseline claims.
- Lacks systematic error analysis to characterize failure modes and challenging mention types.
- Heavy reliance on Wikipedia-specific features raises concerns about applicability to other domains and languages.
- No direct comparisons to other rule-/knowledge-based Wikipedia approaches that could serve as baselines.
- Question: How were the 30 WCR articles selected and how representative are they of Wikipedia's diversity?
- Question: Why were Nguyen et al. (2007) and Nakayama (2008) not used as baselines and how does the new approach compare to them?
- Question: Why does the rule-based system (Lee et al., 2013) outperform ML systems on WCR, and is this a general Wikipedia pattern?
- Question: In the Dcoref++ integration, how much of the 4-point CoNLL F1 gain is specifically due to the MCR component?
- Question: How would the approach adapt to current knowledge bases given Freebase's discontinuation?
- Suggestion: Clarify how MCR relates to and aids standard coreference resolution.
- Suggestion: Provide the actual URL for the released dataset in the final version.
- Suggestion: Analyze why rule-based methods work better on WCR to inform future system design.
- Suggestion: Address handling of articles with multiple main concepts or related-concept structures.
- Suggestion: Clarify what makes the approach "simple yet powerful" (e.g., parameter count, data needs).
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — promising and practical idea but limited by small dataset and inadequate baselines; needs dataset expansion, direct comparisons, and deeper error analysis.
