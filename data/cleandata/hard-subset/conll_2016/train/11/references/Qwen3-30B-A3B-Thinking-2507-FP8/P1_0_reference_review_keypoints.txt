- Proposes "main concept resolution" (MCR) for Wikipedia: identify mentions referring to an article's main concept using a binary classifier with Wikipedia markup and Freebase features.
- Contribution: introduces the WCR dataset for Wikipedia coreference resolution.
- Contribution: presents an MCR approach that outperforms state-of-the-art CR baselines by ~13–15 F1 points on the task.
- Contribution: integrating the classifier into Lee et al. (2013) yields a ~4 F1 point improvement in full coreference resolution on Wikipedia.
- Strength: clearly identifies a significant gap—state-of-the-art CR systems trained on news perform poorly on Wikipedia.
- Strength: effectively leverages Wikipedia structure (links, redirects, Freebase) to extract robust features and avoid NER pipeline errors.
- Strength: provides a clear task setup, dataset, metrics, multiple baselines, and a useful feature ablation study.
- Strength: commits to releasing dataset and results, aiding reproducibility and future work.
- Weakness: WCR is small (30 articles, 60k tokens), limiting statistical significance and representativeness across Wikipedia.
- Weakness: task definition (MCR) is narrow and not clearly justified as superior to adapting full CR systems for Wikipedia.
- Weakness: lacks comparison with prior Wikipedia-specific CR work (e.g., Nguyen 2007, Nakayama 2008), weakening novelty claims.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—insufficient examination of specific failure modes and confusion types.
- Weakness: scalability concerns—reliance on Freebase and markup, unclear computational cost and applicability to all of Wikipedia.
- Question: why is the small WCR dataset size sufficient for supporting the paper's claims?
- Question: is the ~4-point CoNLL F1 improvement in Dcoref++ practically significant given baseline performance?
- Question: how can the claim of outperforming CR systems be reconciled when evaluated on a different task (MCR vs standard CR)?
- Question: where and how will the dataset and classifier results be made available for reproducibility?
