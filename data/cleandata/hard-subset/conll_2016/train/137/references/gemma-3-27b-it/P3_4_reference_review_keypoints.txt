- Paper studies typed vs. untyped contextual features and higher-order dependency paths for detecting compositionality in noun-noun compounds.
- Uses Reddy et al. (2011) dataset to compare APT-based integration of composition and post-hoc composition over pre-trained embeddings.
- Main claim: dependency-informed, richer contexts better represent phrases—including non-compositional ones—improving discrimination.

- Contribution: Systematic comparison of typed vs. untyped dependency features and higher-order paths clarifying the role of syntactic information.
- Contribution: Demonstrates APT-based compositional models (with aligned+unaligned paths) outperform standard word-embedding approaches on compositionality detection.
- Contribution: Replicates and extends Reddy et al. (2011) with thorough parameter exploration and stronger baselines.

- Strength: Strong empirical results with higher Spearman correlations and robust parameter tuning/cross-validation.
- Strength: Well-motivated, linguistically grounded rationale for using typed dependencies and higher-order paths.
- Strength: Thorough experimental setup with reproducible preprocessing, multiple baselines, and dataset-appropriate evaluation.
- Strength: Clear presentation, helpful illustrations (e.g., Table 1), and useful discussion of limitations and future directions.

- Weakness: Limited exploration of compositional functions—primarily additive and intersective approaches only.
- Weakness: Results demonstrated only on ukWaC corpus, raising generalizability concerns across domains, sizes, and parsing qualities.
- Weakness: Hybrid aligned+unaligned approach lacks a fully developed rationale and analysis of when unaligned features help.
- Weakness: Missing detailed error analysis identifying which compounds are misclassified and why.

- Question: Have you tested model performance on other corpora or considered variations due to corpus characteristics?
- Question: Can you specify scenarios or phrase types where unaligned features in the hybrid model provide most benefit?
- Question: Did you try other compositional functions beyond additive/intersective, or can you discuss potential alternatives?
- Question: Can you provide examples of consistently misclassified compounds and analyze the causes?

- Additional: Suggest adding discussion linking compositionality detection to tasks like idiom detection and metaphor recognition.
- Additional: Include the suppressed reference in Section 3 for completeness.

- Overall: Strong, well-executed paper with valuable contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the questions and suggestions.
