- Summary: Evaluates typed vs untyped contextual features and higher-order dependency paths for detecting non-compositional phrases, showing dependency-aware APTs improve over embeddings and untyped models.

Main contributions:
- Empirically validates that incorporating syntactic dependency information improves compositionality detection compared to naive embedding composition.
- Provides an extensive exploration of the APT composition method, including alignment and weighting strategies.
- Replicates and extends prior work (Reddy et al., 2011), establishing stronger baselines and improvements.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results with statistically significant gains for typed dependency and hybrid APT models.
- Comprehensive experimental setup exploring PPMI smoothing, composition ops, and alignment strategies.
- Clear linguistic motivation linking dependency grammar and formal semantics to the task.
- Transparent reporting of corpus, tools, and parameter settings, aiding reproducibility.
- Well-written and logically structured presentation.

Weaknesses:
- Limited generalizability: evaluation restricted to one noun-noun dataset, unclear performance on other phrase types or languages.
- APT model complexity with many parameters and alignment choices reduces accessibility and ease of implementation.
- Lack of systematic ablation on dependency path order to quantify contributions beyond order 1.
- Insufficient error analysis identifying types of non-compositional phrases the model still fails on.

Questions to authors:
- Why does the hybrid aligned+unaligned APT consistently perform best, and which phrase types benefit from unaligned features?
- Are the optimal h and q values ([0.3,0.5]) stable across different experimental setups and PPMI settings?
- Why does shifting PMI hurt performance here despite its utility in word embedding training—could low compound frequency explain this?
- What is the computational cost and scalability of APTs versus simpler methods like vector addition?

Additional comments:
- Rephrase the sentence about APT overlap to emphasize differences in feature weighting rather than lack of overlap.
- Add a visual example illustrating APT construction and alignment.

Overall assessment:
- Strong paper with thorough evaluation and clear contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing questions and clarifications.
