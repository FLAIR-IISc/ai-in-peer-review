- Summary: Evaluates typed vs. untyped contextual features and higher-order dependency paths using APTs to improve detection of non-compositional compound nouns versus neural embeddings.
- Contribution 1: Empirically analyzes how typed (syntactic) vs. untyped contextual features affect compositionality detection.
- Contribution 2: Shows APT models, especially a hybrid aligned/unaligned approach, achieve state-of-the-art on the compound noun dataset, outperforming neural embeddings.
- Contribution 3: Systematically explores compositional operations (addition, intersection) and APT parameter settings, yielding practical configuration insights.
- Strength 1: Strong, well-controlled empirical evaluation with relevant baselines and cross-validation.
- Strength 2: Clear motivation and problem framing for compositionality in distributional semantics.
- Strength 3: Novel hybrid APT approach that effectively leverages syntactic information.
- Strength 4: Thorough and clear explanation of the APT method, aided by helpful examples.
- Strength 5: Experimental details and preprocessing enhance reproducibility.
- Weakness 1: Evaluation restricted to compound nouns, limiting claims about generalizability to other phrase types.
- Weakness 2: Insufficient theoretical justification for chosen parameter ranges and optimal values.
- Weakness 3: Potential corpus-induced bias from using web-derived ukWaC without corpus-effect analysis.
- Weakness 4: Limited comparison to recent SOTA neural compositional models (e.g., transformer-based approaches).
- Weakness 5: Some redundancy and verbosity in tables and results presentation.
- Question 1: How well would findings generalize to non-nominal phrases, and which phrase types might favor or disfavor APTs?
- Question 2: What linguistic or cognitive rationale supports the chosen parameter ranges and optimal settings?
- Question 3: Have the authors compared APTs to modern transformer-based compositional models, or can they speculate on expected differences?
- Question 4: Can the authors supply qualitative error analysis showing which non-compositional phrases are mispredicted and why?
- Question 5: What are the computational training and deployment costs of APTs compared to neural embeddings?
- Additional comment 1: Resolve the reference suppression in section 3.
- Additional comment 2: Include a clearer visual illustration of the APT concept.
- Additional comment 3: Minor editing to reduce verbosity and improve clarity is advised.
- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper with strong empirical and methodological contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
