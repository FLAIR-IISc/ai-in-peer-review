- Empirically investigates how contextual feature definitions (typed vs. untyped, higher-order dependencies) affect detection of non-compositional phrases.
- Compares APT-based models with standard compositional operations on pretrained embeddings using Reddy et al. (2011) dataset.
- Finds untyped dependency features with a hybrid alignment strategy and pointwise addition yield best performance, outperforming prior state-of-the-art.

- Rigorous experimental setup replicating Reddy et al. (2011) with 3-fold cross-validation and detailed parameter tuning.
- Thorough exploration of model configurations: PPMI smoothing, composition ops (INT vs. UNI), alignment strategies, and feature orders.
- Clear, well-structured presentation with concise descriptions of APT model and experiments.
- Insightful interpretation of results, especially regarding why some configurations outperform others.
- Directly addresses non-compositionality, improving understanding of phrase-level meaning combination.

- Evaluation limited to a single dataset of compound nouns, raising questions about generalizability to other phrase types or languages.
- Section 3’s APT model description is dense and may be hard to follow without more intuitive explanation or visuals.
- Lacks in-depth error analysis to identify specific non-compositional phrase types that remain problematic.
- Omits discussion of computational cost for constructing and using APT representations given parsing and high-dimensional features.
- Improvement over prior work is incremental; practical significance of the gains is not fully discussed.

- How do gains generalize to other phrase types beyond compound nouns and other datasets?
- Can the authors provide a simplified example showing the alignment/offsetting process?
- What are the time and memory costs of constructing and using APTs compared to neural embeddings?
- Have the authors attempted explicit identification/handling of highly idiomatic phrases within the APT framework?
- Why do untyped features outperform fully typed ones despite theoretical advantages of dependency relation info?

- Provide full citation suppressed in Section 3.
- Include brief discussion of related work on idiom detection and handling.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with rigorous experiments and valuable insights; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
