- Paper studies how typed vs. untyped and higher-order dependency contextual features affect distributional models' ability to detect non-compositional noun-noun phrases.  
- Finds that incorporating typed dependency information, especially a hybrid of aligned and unaligned features, yields the best compositionality detection performance.  

Main contributions:
- Empirical comparison of contextual feature types (typed/untyped, higher-order dependencies) for compositional distributional semantics.  
- Identification of a hybrid aligned+unaligned dependency-path approach as superior for modeling phrase meaning.  
- Replication and improvement over Reddy et al. (2011) baseline, providing a solid comparative baseline.

Strengths:
- Clear motivation and problem framing around compositional vs. non-compositional phrases.  
- Rigorous experimental setup following prior work with appropriate cross-validation.  
- Detailed and nuanced analysis of results and parameter effects (e.g., h and q).  
- Strong empirical gains, notably with the hybrid approach and PPMI smoothing.  
- Well-connected to existing literature in distributional and compositional semantics.

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation limited to a small dataset (90 noun-noun compounds), raising generalizability concerns.  
- Computational complexity and scalability issues from dependency parsing and higher-order path construction are not adequately addressed.  
- Limited exploration of alternative compositional functions beyond additive/intersective methods.  
- Lack of qualitative error analysis and examples illustrating model failures/successes.

Questions to authors:
- How well would the method generalize to other phrase types (adjective-noun, verb-noun) and other languages?  
- What are the computational costs for building the APT lexicon and composing with higher-order paths at scale, and can scalability be improved?  
- Can you provide examples of phrases the model struggles with and analyze the linguistic causes?  
- What is the intuition behind the hybrid alignment strategy and how is the optimal weighting between aligned/unaligned features determined?

Additional comments:
- Suppressing the APT reference hinders reproducibility and understanding.  
- Provide a clearer explanation of the reduction process in Equation (1).  
- Include a more thorough discussion of PPMI limitations.

Recommendation:
- Well-written and insightful paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
