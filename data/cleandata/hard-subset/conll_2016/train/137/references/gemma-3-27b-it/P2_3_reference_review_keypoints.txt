- Paper studies how typed vs. untyped contextual features and higher-order dependency paths affect compositional distributional semantics for noun-noun compounds.  
- Compares naive composition of pre-trained embeddings with building composition into the model using Anchored Packed Trees (APTs).  
- Shows that richer, dependency-typed contextual features better capture phrase compositionality.  

- Systematic exploration of typed contextual features and higher-order paths is a significant empirical contribution.  
- Empirically validates APTs for compositionality detection, achieving state-of-the-art on Reddy et al. (2011).  
- Reframes evaluation emphasis onto detecting compositionality rather than phrase similarity.  

- Strong empirical results: APT models consistently outperform baselines, including neural embeddings.  
- Careful and thorough experimental setup, replicating Reddy et al. (2011) and exploring many parameters.  
- Well-motivated linguistically: syntactic structure via dependency parsing is appropriate for compositional semantics.  
- Clear explanation of APTs with helpful illustrative tables.  
- Insightful finding that aligned and unaligned APTs capture complementary aspects and a hybrid is most effective.  

- Evaluation limited to a single dataset of noun-noun compounds, so generalizability to other phrase types or languages is unclear.  
- APTs with higher-order paths likely have high computational cost; scalability is not adequately addressed.  
- Many hyperparameters; results may be sensitive and would benefit from more systematic optimization.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis identifying which compounds or structures fail.  
- Hybrid model’s improvement over unaligned APTs is modest and needs stronger justification.  

- Request for detailed analysis of computational cost of building/using APTs versus neural embeddings.  
- Ask for insight into why the hybrid (aligned+unaligned) model outperforms individual approaches and which compounds benefit most.  
- Question about sensitivity to choice of dependency parser and parsing settings.  
- Inquiry about plans to evaluate on other datasets and phrase types (verb phrases, adj-noun, other languages).  
- Clarification requested on the role and theoretical justification of PPMI smoothing parameter α = 0.75.  

- Paper is well-written, organized, and has a comprehensive related work section.  
- One suppressed citation in section 3 should be included.  

- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper with compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address generalizability, scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, and error analysis.
