- Paper studies compositionality detection for noun–noun compounds using typed, higher-order dependency features encoded as Anchored Packed Trees (APTs), comparing aligned, unaligned, and hybrid composition regimes against neural additive baselines.
- Evaluated on Reddy et al. (2011) dataset (90 compounds), APT methods achieve higher Spearman correlations with human judgments (up to ρ=0.79) than prior work (0.71) and neural baselines.
- Provides analysis of design choices (alignment vs. no alignment, intersection vs. union composition, PPMI smoothing, modifier vs. head weighting) showing modifiers and unaligned typed features are slightly more informative and that shifted PMI/aggressive subsampling hurt.

- Clear empirical gains on a standard benchmark with careful comparisons; APT hybrid consistently outperforms Reddy baseline and tuned word2vec CBOW/SG.
- Methodology is well-motivated linguistically: offset-and-reduction alignment is principled and the hybrid finding is plausible given overlap of first- and second-order contexts.
- Experimental design mitigates leakage by re-tokenizing compounds and preventing compound-derived contexts from seeding constituent vectors.
- Reports actionable analyses on weighting and smoothing (e.g., α≈0.75 helps, optimal h and q ≈0.3–0.5), useful for practitioners.
- Baselines include frequency and neural embeddings; frequency baseline (ρ=0.63) reveals dataset frequency–compositionality correlation and all methods beat it.

- Limited evaluation scope and possible overfitting due to small dataset (90 items) and repeated 3-fold cross-validation; stability of hyperparameters is uncertain.
- Missing strong count-based dependency baselines (first-order typed dependency addition or lexical-function models), making it hard to isolate APT-specific gains from typed-dependency effects.
- Statistical testing and reporting lack clarity (unspecified resampling protocol, unclear definition of "error" and significance thresholds).
- Potential confound from phrase frequency and observed phrasal representations; need partial correlations controlling for log frequency and stratified analyses by frequency.
- Reproducibility/presentation gaps: suppressed prior reference, incomplete hyperparameter details (pruning, path-depth), minor table/notation inconsistencies; code/settings release needed.

- Please add a count-based typed dependency baseline using first-order PPMI vectors with weighted addition and a second-order variant without alignment to isolate APT contributions.
- Provide per-compound occurrence counts in ukWaC after preprocessing, stratified performance by frequency bins, and partial correlations controlling for frequency.
- Evaluate on an additional dataset (e.g., phrasal verbs or larger noun-compound sets) or provide leave-one-domain-out analysis to reduce overfitting concerns.
- Clarify the resampling protocol for error/significance estimation (number of repetitions, fold drawing, whether tuning is nested) and report confidence intervals for main comparisons to CBOW and Reddy.
- Report sensitivity to maximum path order and to reduction with ablations: first-order only, first+second without reduction, and with reduction.
- Provide breakdown of aligned vs. unaligned vs. hybrid performance for compounds where NMOD is not the dominant relation.

- Overall: solid, timely paper with convincing empirical gains and useful insights into typed higher-order dependency features for compositionality detection, but would be strengthened by broader baselines, clearer statistics, and expanded evaluations.
