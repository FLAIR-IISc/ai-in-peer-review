- Paper studies compositionality detection for English noun–noun compounds using typed dependency APTs and compares aligned, unaligned, and hybrid composition to neural/count baselines on Reddy et al. (2011).
- Main empirical claim: hybrid APT composition achieves substantial improvement (ρ=0.79) over CBOW (0.74) and prior count-based baselines (0.71).
- Methodological contribution: explicit dependency-path alignment (offset/reduction) for compound composition and a tunable hybrid (q) mixing aligned and unaligned signals.
- Analytical observations: practical recommendations on PPMI smoothing (α≈0.75), additive vs intersective composition choices, and modifier/head weighting (h≈0.3–0.5).
- Strength: reimplementation of competitive baselines with parameter sweeps and statistically significant gains on the target dataset.
- Strength: empirical evidence that typed, higher-order dependency paths capture complementary structural information (aligned vs unaligned views).
- Strength: thorough ablation studies within the APT family (composition modes, path smoothing, composing counts vs PPMI).
- Strength: careful preprocessing to avoid leakage (pre-tokenizing compounds and excluding phrasal contexts from constituent vectors).
- Strength: interpretable linguistic insight that modifiers contribute slightly more than heads.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single very small dataset (90 compounds), raising generalizability and overfitting concerns.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to dependency-based neural embeddings (e.g., Levy & Goldberg SGNS on deps) and more recent compositionality detectors.
- Weakness: potential frequency confound—frequency baseline correlates strongly (ρ=0.63) and no partial-correlation or stratified analyses are reported.
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps—unclear exact h/q per condition, CV repetition count, dimensionality/pruning thresholds, and code/resource release plans.
- Weakness: practicality and scalability concerns—APT dimensionality (80k–230k) and alignment ops may be computationally heavy versus low-dimensional neural models; no runtime/memory costs reported.
- Questions for authors: report partial correlations controlling for compound frequency and performance stratified by frequency buckets.
- Questions for authors: compare APT models to dependency-based embeddings to isolate typed-context vs APT-specific benefits.
- Questions for authors: provide exact optimal h and q per variant, stability across CV folds, and any relation-type trends (e.g., NMOD).
- Questions for authors: state how many 3-fold CV repetitions were run and provide confidence intervals/standard deviations per model.
- Questions for authors: provide computational cost (memory, training/inference time) for APT construction/composition versus CBOW/SG.
- Questions for authors: did you test higher-than-second-order paths or ablate the reduction operation to see if gains saturate?
