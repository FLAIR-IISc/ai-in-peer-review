- Summary: Evaluates compositionality detection for noun–noun compounds using APTs (typed higher-order dependency paths) and three composition settings (aligned, unaligned, hybrid), finding APT hybrid achieves ρ=0.79 on Reddy et al. (90 compounds) vs 0.71 (Reddy baseline) and 0.74 (best CBOW).

- Contribution 1: Empirical finding that typed higher-order dependency features via APTs substantially improve compound compositionality detection over untyped models and neural baselines.

- Contribution 2: Methodological adaptation of APT composition to noun compounds, including offset alignment and a hybrid mixture to combine aligned and unaligned features.

- Contribution 3: Systematic ablations exploring PPMI smoothing, composition operators (add vs min), head/modifier weighting, and effects of subsampling.

- Strength: Clear empirical improvements with multiple baselines and justification that typed dependency information adds value beyond frequency and untyped similarity.

- Strength: Careful experimental protocol and ablations, including varied composition operators and repeated 3-fold CV to tune parameters.

- Strength: Corpus preprocessing prevents trivial leakage by collapsing compounds into single tokens for observed phrase representations.

- Strength: Linguistically motivated features and interpretable examples that show how higher-order dependency alignment works.

- Strength: Useful ancillary findings (modifier slightly more informative than head; frequency correlates with compositionality).

- Weakness: Limited novelty—APT alignment and related techniques are prior work; new elements are mainly application to compounds and a simple hybrid mixture.

- Weakness: Narrow evaluation on a single small dataset (90 compounds), making statistical claims and generalization questionable.

- Weakness: Missing a strong baseline of dependency-based embeddings (e.g., Levy & Goldberg) to isolate the effect of typed contexts.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why unaligned outperforms aligned APTs and why the hybrid is best; needs case studies and feature-level analysis.

- Weakness: Reproducibility and presentation gaps—suppressed prior work details, missing implementation specifics, messy references, and no code/data release plan.

- Question: Clarify exact novelty relative to prior APT work (is the hybrid or other technical detail new?).

- Question: Can dependency-based word embeddings be added as baselines to test whether typed contexts alone explain gains?

- Question: Report CV repetitions, variance/confidence intervals, and justify the 0.005 significance threshold for Spearman ρ.

- Question: Provide qualitative/quantitative analysis of aligned vs unaligned features, error breakdowns by POS/relations/frequency.

- Question: Will you release code, preprocessing scripts, and full implementation details (feature vocab sizes, pruning, hyperparameter grids)?
