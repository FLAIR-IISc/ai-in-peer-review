- Paper studies compositionality detection for noun–noun compounds using typed higher-order dependency contexts and Anchored Packed Trees (APTs) with dependency-path offsetting/alignment.
- Main empirical contribution: hybrid aligned+unaligned APT model achieves state-of-the-art correlation (ρ up to 0.79) on Reddy et al. (2011), outperforming cbow (ρ=0.74) and earlier count-based methods (ρ=0.71).
- Methodological insight: typed higher-order dependency features and path-based alignment improve composition; combining aligned and unaligned signals is best.
- Experimental analysis: investigates PMI smoothing (α=0.75), composition operators (min vs add), and constituent/head weighting with careful corpus preprocessing to avoid leakage.
- Strength: Clear, consistent empirical gains with robust controls (3-fold CV, small standard errors) and outperforming a strong frequency baseline.
- Strength: Well-motivated use of typed dependency contexts and a principled offsetting/reduction alignment mechanism.
- Strength: Thoughtful corpus preprocessing to prevent phrasal contexts contaminating constituent vectors.
- Strength: Useful comparative analysis showing additive composition tends to beat intersective and identifying optimal weighting ranges.
- Strength: Transparent discussion of PMI choices and their effects on low-frequency compounds.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—APTs and higher-order typed features largely pre-existing; main advance is application and hybrid combination.
- Weakness: Narrow evaluation on a small 90-item dataset that correlates with frequency, limiting generality claims.
- Weakness: Baselines are weak/unfairly matched—no dependency-based embeddings or typed PPMI+SVD comparisons included.
- Weakness: Possible overfitting and hyperparameter tuning on a tiny dataset; method is weakly supervised by human ratings.
- Weakness: Reliance on parse quality and dominant relation δ selection; sensitivity to parsing errors and δ choice not quantified.
- Question: Please compare against dependency-based SGNS and typed PPMI+SVD baselines to isolate APT-specific gains.
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of δ and parser-derived relations—please report an ablation.
- Question: Provide partial correlations controlling for frequency to show signal beyond frequency effects.
- Question: Report robustness to parse errors and performance by compound frequency and parsing confidence.
- Question: Do results generalize to other constructions (adj–noun) and larger compositionality datasets—any preliminary results?
- Additional comment: Reproducibility would improve with released code, APT construction details, and compound lists with δ assignments.
- Additional comment: Ablation including second-order similarity after composition would clarify the impact of restricting to first-order features.
- Additional comment: Ensure proper anonymized citations for prior APT work to assess novelty.
