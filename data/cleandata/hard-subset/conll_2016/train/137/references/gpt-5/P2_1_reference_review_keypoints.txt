- Study of noun–noun compositionality using Anchored Packed Trees (APTs) with aligned, unaligned, and hybrid composition compared to PPMI and neural embeddings.
- Empirical claim: hybrid APT achieves state-of-the-art correlation (~ρ=0.79) on Reddy et al. (2011), beating weighted-add baselines and CBOW.
- Evidence that typed dependency features and combining aligned+unaligned views provide complementary signals for compositionality detection.
- Methodological care to prevent leakage: collapsing compounds and removing compound contexts from constituent vectors for fair evaluation.

- Strength: Consistent empirical improvements over strong baselines with careful cross-validation and parameter exploration.
- Strength: Clear formalization and motivation of typed higher-order features, path operations, and alignment mechanism.
- Strength: Insightful analysis of design choices (PPMI smoothing, intersects vs union-like composition, effects of subsampling).
- Strength: Attention to leakage and evaluation correctness increases credibility.
- Strength: Practical relevance for MWE handling and downstream tasks; shows syntax-aware contexts help.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—APTs are prior work and contribution is mostly application/empirical.
- Weakness: Narrow evaluation on a small 90-compound Reddy dataset; risk of overfitting with many tuned hyperparameters.
- Weakness: Missing stronger baselines (dependency-based embeddings like dep2vec, count-based dependency vectors, lexical-function/tensor models).
- Weakness: Methodological clarity gaps—similarity computed on 1st-order features only and several preprocessing choices lack empirical justification.
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues—implementation/pruning/dimensionality/training details and seeds under-specified; no code released.

- Question: Please compare to dependency-based embeddings (e.g., dep2vec) and count-based dependency vectors with weighted addition to isolate benefits of alignment vs typed contexts.
- Question: How sensitive are results to compound-collapsing heuristics (majority dependency label, ignoring modifier-internal paths)? Report alternatives.
- Question: Provide ablations on h and q without tuning (e.g., fixed h=0.5, q in {0,1}) and quantify hybrid gains beyond best single view.
- Question: Include per-compound/category analysis (idiomatic vs semi-compositional; frequent vs rare) to show where APT helps or hurts.
- Question: Evaluate on additional datasets and MWE types (other noun-compound sets, phrasal verbs, adjective–noun) to demonstrate generality.

- Assessment: Originality moderate; quality generally sound but needs broader baselines/ablations; clarity good though some implementation details missing.
- Assessment: Significance promising but limited by narrow evaluation; reproducibility medium pending more details and code.
- Overall recommendation: Lean weak accept if space permits, but broader baselines, ablations, and evaluations are encouraged.
