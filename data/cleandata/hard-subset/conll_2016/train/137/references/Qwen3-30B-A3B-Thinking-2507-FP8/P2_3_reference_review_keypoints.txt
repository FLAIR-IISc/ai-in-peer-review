- Addresses confounding between compositionality and phrase similarity; proposes Anchored Packed Trees (APT) with typed dependency-path alignment, achieving Spearman’s ρ = 0.79 vs. prior 0.71.  
- Contribution: principled compositional modeling using typed dependency paths with proper alignment, significantly outperforming previous methods.  
- Contribution: argues compositionality detection is a valuable, less-confounded evaluation task for compositional models.  
- Contribution: shows syntactic structure in dependency paths is highly informative; APT outperforms neural embeddings.  
- Strength: Significant empirical improvement over prior work with careful experimental control (same corpus/dataset).  
- Strength: Strong linguistic/theoretical motivation for using typed dependency paths and alignment; insightful analysis of feature-type differences.  
- Strength: Clear conceptual link that compositionality detection can evaluate compositional models without confounding by phrase similarity.  
- Strength: Rigorous experimental design with parameter sweeps, 3-fold cross-validation, and statistical testing.  
- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical justification for using compositionality detection as the primary evaluation metric and no demonstrated translation to improved phrase similarity models.  
- Weakness: Limited generalizability—evaluation confined to noun compounds (90 examples), no evidence for other phrase types.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of why APT outperforms neural embeddings and which dependency features drive gains.  
- Weakness: Overstated claims that compositionality detection will lead to improved compositional models without empirical support.  
- Question: Which specific dependency features (e.g., NMOD, AMOD, NSUBJ) contribute most to APT’s performance?  
- Question: How would APT perform on non-noun compounds (verb phrases, adverbial phrases), and can it be extended?  
- Question: Can you give a concrete example of how insights from compositionality detection could improve a compositional phrase-similarity model?  
- Overall: Meaningful, well-motivated, and rigorously evaluated contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to temper claims and improve analysis/generalizability.
