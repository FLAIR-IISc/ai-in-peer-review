- Paper tackles failure of compositional methods on non-compositional phrases by using Anchored Packed Trees (APTs) with typed higher-order dependency paths to better model compositionality.  
- Main contribution: a novel compositional method (APTs) that aligns vector spaces via typed dependency paths.  
- Main contribution: a new evaluation framework measuring how well compositional models predict observed phrase representations, avoiding phrase-similarity confounds.  
- Main contribution: strong empirical improvement over prior state-of-the-art (0.79 Spearman vs. 0.71).  
- Strength: clarifies a fundamental evaluation flaw in prior work and proposes a more appropriate compositionality evaluation methodology.  
- Strength: statistically significant, consistently better empirical results across APT configurations with clear experimental details.  
- Strength: solid linguistic/theoretical motivation emphasizing continuum of compositionality and the role of syntactic dependency structure.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to 90 noun compounds, raising questions about generalization to other phrase types.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to more recent compositional methods (e.g., Berant & Liang 2014, Hashimoto et al. 2014).  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation/detailing (handling ambiguous parses, ukWaC processing) hindering reproducibility.  
- Question: why focus only on noun compounds and are there plans to extend to verbs, adjectives, or other MWEs?  
- Question: can the authors provide concrete examples or preliminary results showing compositionality detection improving compositional methods?  
- Question: why does the unaligned APT outperform the aligned model despite theoretical motivation for alignment, and does this generalize?  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; accept with minor revisions to improve reproducibility and expand discussion of scope.
