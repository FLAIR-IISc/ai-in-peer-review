- Paper proposes an Anchored Packed Trees (APT) method using typed dependency paths to detect compositionality in compound nouns, evaluated on Reddy et al. (2011) data.
- Main contribution: showing syntactic structure (typed dependency paths) substantially improves compositionality detection (Spearman 0.79 vs 0.71).
- Main contribution: systematic comparison of composition operations (intersection vs union) and alignment strategies, finding a hybrid approach works best.
- Main contribution: finding the modifier is slightly more informative than the head for this task (optimal weights h and q in [0.3,0.5]).
- Strength: significant empirical advance with robust experimental validation and statistically significant improvement over prior work.
- Strength: clear evidence that incorporating syntactic structure is crucial for accurate compositionality detection.
- Strength: thorough exploration of composition strategies, providing actionable insights for the community.
- Strength: clear implementation details and reproducible methodological contribution (reduction operation, handling dependency paths).
- Weakness: evaluation scope is limited to noun compounds and may not generalize to other phrase types (verb/adjectival phrases).
- Weakness: insufficient comparisons to more recent compositional models post-2011 (e.g., lexical function models in Dinu et al. 2013).
- Weakness: no computational complexity analysis or comparison to cheaper methods like neural embeddings.
- Weakness: theoretical justification for why typed dependency paths help is underdeveloped and needs deeper linguistic motivation.
- Question: how does the method handle compositionality as a continuum versus categorical judgments in practice?
- Question: why does unaligned APT composition outperform aligned composition despite theoretical motivation for alignment?
- Question: how does the method handle ambiguity in dependency relations for compounds parsed with multiple relations (e.g., NMOD vs AMOD)?
- Question: can the authors elaborate on the proposed feedback loop where compositionality detection improves compositional methods, with any preliminary evidence?
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant empirical contribution and recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scope and theoretical discussion.
