- Addresses detection of compositional vs. non-compositional phrases using Anchored Packed Trees (APT) with typed dependency paths.  
- Evaluated on Reddy et al. (2011) 90 compound-noun dataset, achieving Spearman's rho = 0.79 vs prior SOTA 0.71.  
- Proposes an APT-based method that uses typed higher-order dependency paths instead of untyped features.  
- Key empirical finding: unaligned composition outperforms aligned composition for compositionality detection.  
- Provides a systematic comparison of composition strategies (additive, multiplicative, aligned, unaligned, hybrid).  
- Reports a significant empirical improvement over previous work and neural embedding baselines.  
- Argues compositionality detection is a valuable evaluation that avoids conflating compositionality with similarity judgments.  
- Experimental design is clear and well-executed (ukWaC corpus, 3-fold CV, fair comparisons, appropriate metrics).  
- Demonstrates typed dependency features and syntactic structure are informative for compositionality detection.  
- Fails to provide a theoretical explanation for why unaligned composition outperforms aligned composition.  
- Evaluation is limited to noun-noun compounds, leaving generalization to other phrase types untested.  
- Comparison to neural methods is inadequate—only basic cbow/skip-gram used, not modern neural composition models.  
- Paper title ("Red Herring") is misleading about the nature of non-compositionality.  
- Hybrid method (aligned + unaligned) performs best but lacks theoretical justification and explanation for the weighting parameter q.  
- Asks authors to explain the theoretical basis for the unaligned-vs-aligned result and its relation to syntactic structure.  
- Requests evaluation or expectations for other phrase types and broader datasets (e.g., McCarthy et al. 2003).  
- Asks why shifted PMI / negative sampling harmed results and whether low compound frequency is a factor.  
- Encourages comparison to modern neural composition architectures (e.g., tensor methods, LSTMs, transformers/attention).  
- Seeks theoretical rationale for why combining aligned and unaligned components helps and why q ∈ [0.3,0.5] is optimal.  
- Overall: strong empirical contribution but requires major revisions to address theoretical gaps and broaden evaluation.
