- Paper studies predicting compound compositionality by comparing compound vectors to composed component vectors using cosine similarity and Spearman correlation.
- Experiments compare neural embeddings vs syntactic-context count vectors, and use of "aligned" vs plain vectors for dependents.
- Main finding: syntactic-context count vectors outperform embeddings; aligned vectors alone hurt, but a tuned mix of aligned+unaligned gives slight gains.
- Innovation unclear: aligned vectors seem previously published by the authors (suppressed reference).
- Dataset issues: uses Reddy et al.’s 90 compounds but selection criteria, frequency ranges, and number of human judges are not reported.
- Dataset small and possibly biased; marginal gains on a small, poorly described dataset make findings fragile.
- Concern about ties in human scores and impact on Spearman correlation not addressed.
- Terminology/confusion: new name "packed anchored tree" seems unnecessary and unclear; what are "elementary APTs"?
- Section 3 unclear about discarded features/order >3 and incompatible types—needs examples and clarification.
- Section 4: need frequencies for compounds and components, selection process, and clearer use of term "constituent" vs "component".
- Request for running examples to clarify "compound phrase token" vs constituent representations.
- Section 5.1: definition of "elementary APTs" and whether "shifted PMI" corresponds to eq. (3) is unclear.
- Several sentences are confusing/contradictory (e.g., about low positive PMI and "these phrases"); pronoun references need fixing.
- Alignments explanation unclear: what "not properly aligned" means and why alignment should increase overlap, especially for NMOD.
- Paragraphs 575–580 and 558–563 are puzzling and need clearer argumentation.
- Methodological inconsistency: paper uses higher-order dependency features generally, but similarity measurement uses only first-order features—insufficient justification (note 3 unclear).
- Paragraph explaining unreliability of 2nd-order paths in composed representations is not convincing; needs clarification and examples.
- Section 6: claimed small positive effect of PPMI smoothing (α=0.75) not clearly supported by table 3.
- Hyperparameters h and q (eqs. 8–9) and how much hybridity contributes to gains are not reported.
- Table 4 legend ambiguous; unclear whether "add" combination was used.
- Request to include results using word2vec vectors for compound phrases (not just components).
- Baseline FREQ intuition unclear and possibly indicates dataset bias—why should frequent compounds be more compositional?
- Paper’s readability suffers in places; recommend rephrasing with running examples and clearer exposition.
