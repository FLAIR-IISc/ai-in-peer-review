- Paper compares composed (additive neural and count-based APT) vs observed compound representations to predict compositionality on Reddy et al. (2011).
- Main empirical contribution: higher-order typed dependency count vectors (hybrid APT) outperform neural embeddings and prior SOTA (ρ≈0.79 vs 0.74 vs 0.71).
- Secondary contribution: hybrid mixture of aligned and unaligned APTs slightly outperforms either alone; modifiers have slightly more weight than heads.
- Tertiary contribution: broad parameter sweep for PPMI and neural embeddings offering practical guidance.

- Strength: Strong empirical improvement on a standard benchmark with consistent APT gains (hybrid 0.79, unaligned 0.78, aligned 0.75).
- Strength: Systematic comparison across typed vs untyped contexts, alignment vs no-alignment, and composition methods plus neural hyperparameter grid.
- Strength: Useful qualitative findings (unaligned dependency features informative for NMOD, modifier > head, cbow subsampling tip).
- Strength: Careful preprocessing to avoid leakage by collapsing compounds and excluding compound-derived contexts.

- Weakness: Limited novelty; APT and alignment appear prior work and novelty is mainly applying them to one small dataset and mixing variants.
- Weakness: Small, potentially biased evaluation (Reddy dataset has 90 compounds); frequency may confound results and is not controlled for.
- Weakness: APT and alignment concepts are insufficiently defined with no concrete end-to-end examples.
- Weakness: Inconsistency: approach emphasizes higher-order features but similarity uses only first-order features without empirical justification.
- Weakness: Reporting/reproducibility gaps: unclear statistical testing details, missing optimal h/q values, unclear repeatability and some table legends/method details absent.
- Weakness: Neural holistic baselines (compound neural vectors) are not fully reported as comparison baselines.

- Question: Provide precise definitions and a full running example of elementary APTs, alignment, reduction, zero-th order features, and incompatible types.
- Question: Justify evaluating with first-order features only; report ablations with 1st-order, 1st+2nd-order, and all orders.
- Question: Report best h and q per variant and ablations for q∈{0,0.25,0.5,0.75,1} to quantify hybrid gains and per-compound alignment effects.
- Question: Clarify handling of ties in human scores, number of distinct ranks, number of CV repetitions, and the statistical test behind the 0.005 significance threshold.
- Question: Provide per-compound frequency stats, correlations with compositionality, and results controlling for frequency (partial ρ or regression).
- Question: Report results using holistic neural compound vectors as observed targets and composed neural vectors compared to those holistics.

- Additional: Many passages are hard to follow; add careful editing and a running example for readability.
- Additional: Quantify the claimed PPMI smoothing effect (α=0.75) and show where it helps or hurts.
- Additional: Recommend releasing code, preprocessing scripts, and parameter files to improve reproducibility.
