- Proposes TBuckets, which groups topic words into thematic buckets and scores topic coherence using properties of the largest bucket (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg variants).
- Evaluated TBuckets on three public datasets and reported better performance than state-of-the-art methods.
- Demonstrates TBuckets’ utility for selecting high-quality topics in weakly supervised text classification.
- Main contribution: novel bucket-based topic coherence measure.
- Main contribution: improved performance on two of three datasets, with SVD variants needing no parameter tuning.
- Main contribution: application to aid human annotation in weakly supervised classification.
- Strength: innovative, conceptually new approach to measuring topic coherence via thematic grouping.
- Strength: empirical improvements over existing methods on multiple datasets.
- Strength: practical SVD-based methods that avoid parameter tuning.
- Weakness: some sections lack clarity and need rephrasing and running examples.
- Weakness: limited evaluation on only three datasets, raising generalizability concerns.
- Weakness: reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is unclear and needs better explanation.
- Questions: request more dataset details (selection of compounds, frequency ranges, judgment procedures).
- Questions: ask for clarification and examples of the SVD-Reorg reorganization step and its effectiveness.
- Questions: ask how TBuckets could be extended beyond text classification and what challenges/limitations may arise.
