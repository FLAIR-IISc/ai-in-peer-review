- Paper compares observed phrase representations with composed constituent representations for detecting compositionality in compound nouns; evaluated on Reddy et al.'s 90-compound dataset.  
- Contribution: syntactic-contextual distributional features significantly outperform neural embeddings for compositionality detection.  
- Contribution: novel hybrid approach combining aligned and unaligned APT representations yields marginal improvements.  
- Contribution: detailed analysis showing dependency structure (higher-order dependency paths) affects compositional modeling and modifier contributes slightly more than head.  
- Strength: clear problem framing—compositionality detection is a better testbed than phrase similarity for compositional methods.  
- Strength: meaningful performance improvement (Spearman 0.79 vs prior 0.71).  
- Strength: insightful analysis linking syntactic structure to compositionality.  
- Weakness: questionable novelty—method appears a refinement of authors’ prior work with insufficient differentiation.  
- Weakness: dataset description is incomplete (selection criteria, frequency ranges, number of judges, tie handling missing).  
- Weakness: methodological inconsistency—uses higher-order dependency features in model but restricts similarity computation to first-order without justification.  
- Weakness: unaddressed baseline issue—FREQ baseline outperforms RAND, suggesting possible dataset bias unexamined.  
- Weakness: readability and clarity problems—unclear terminology, contradictory explanations, insufficient examples make replication difficult.  
- Question: clarify novelty relative to prior APT/alignment work and why this is a significant advance.  
- Question: provide full details about the 90 compounds (selection criteria, frequency ranges, number of human judges, tie handling).  
- Question: justify using only first-order features for similarity when higher-order features are used in the model.  
- Question: explain why FREQ baseline outperforms RAND and whether dataset bias links frequency to compositionality.  
- Question: provide a running example showing how compound phrase token representations differ from constituent representations and define "elementary APTs."  
- Overall recommendation: meaningful contribution but plagued by novelty, dataset, methodological, and clarity issues—borderline accept with major revisions.
