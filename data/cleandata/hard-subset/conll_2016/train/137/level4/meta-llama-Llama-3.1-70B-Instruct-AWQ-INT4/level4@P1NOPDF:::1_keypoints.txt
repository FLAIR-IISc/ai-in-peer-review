- Paper studies predicting compound compositionality using cosine similarity between compound vectors and composed component vectors, evaluated by Spearman correlation with human judgments.

- Main experimental variables: neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors, and use of "aligned" vs. plain vectors for dependents.

- Finding: syntactic-context count vectors outperform embeddings; aligned vectors alone underperform, but a tuned hybrid of aligned and unaligned vectors gives slight improvement.

- Innovation is limited: aligned vectors were published previously by the authors, so novelty over Reddy et al. is unclear.

- Dataset is small (90 compounds), poorly described, and selection/frequency ranges of compounds and components are not reported.

- Number of human judges per compound and ties in compositionality scores are not reported, raising concerns about reliability of Spearman rankings.

- Results look fragile because improvements are marginal on a small, possibly biased dataset with unclear compound selection.

- Paper clarity issues: sections (notably 5.1) are hard to read and would benefit from running examples and clearer terminology.

- Terminology problems: the new term "packed anchored tree" is unclear and seems to be just dependency-path extraction; "elementary APTs" is confusing.

- Need to explain why the structure is called a "tree" and to define "elementary APTs" clearly (likely a triplet of target word, dependency path, and context word).

- Table 2 and feature handling are unclear: did the authors discard features of order >3 or eliminate incompatible types? Provide examples and explanations.

- Clarify why higher-order dependency features are used for representations but only first-order features are used when measuring similarity; Note 3's explanation is unclear.

- Explain the alignment procedure and what is meant by "not properly aligned" vs. "unaligned"; clarify why overlap might be considerable for NMOD relations.

- Define "shifted PMI" and confirm if it refers to PMI as in equation (3); clarify contradictory sentences about low positive PMI and phrase observation counts.

- Request running example to clarify statements like the one about elementary representations lacking contextual features of the compound phrase token.

- Provide dataset statistics: frequency ranges for compounds and components, how compounds were chosen, and how many compounds share compositionality scores.

- Report results using word2vec vectors for compound phrases (compounds as tokens) as requested.

- Clarify the FREQ baseline intuition: why would higher compound frequency indicate greater compositionality, and whether this reflects dataset bias.

- Report hyperparameter choices and effects: optimal values for α in PPMI smoothing, and for h and q in equations (8) and (9) that control hybridity.

- Include combination method (e.g., "add") used in reported results in table legends and describe combination procedures.

- Provide clearer explanations in Section 3/5 why APT overlap and alignment matter, and why certain paths/features are considered unreliable in composed representations.
