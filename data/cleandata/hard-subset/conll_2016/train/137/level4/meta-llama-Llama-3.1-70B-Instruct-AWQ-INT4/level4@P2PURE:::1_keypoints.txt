- Paper predicts compound compositionality using cosine similarity between compound vectors and composed component vectors, evaluated with Spearman correlation on a 90-compound dataset.  
- Experiments compare neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors and test "aligned" vs. unaligned vectors for dependents.  
- Syntactic-context count vectors outperform neural embeddings; aligned vectors alone underperform, but a tuned hybrid of aligned+unaligned gives slight gains.  
- Introduction is well-written, but other sections (e.g., 5.1) are hard to read and would benefit from running examples.  
- Main novelty (aligned vectors) appears to be previously published by the authors, limiting originality.  
- Dataset is very small (90 compounds) and insufficiently described, raising concerns about result robustness.  
- Frequency ranges of compounds and components are not reported but likely affect results.  
- Improvements from alignment are marginal on a small, unclearly selected dataset, making findings fragile.  
- The term "packed anchored tree" seems unnecessary and confusing; it appears to be path extraction in a dependency tree.  
- Questioned why the structure is called a "tree" and what "elementary APTs" mean.  
- Table 2: unclear if features of order >3 were discarded or if incompatible types were eliminated—needs clarification and examples.  
- Section 4: must explain how the 90 compounds were selected and report frequency ranges for compounds and components.  
- Section 4: number of human judgments per compound is unspecified; many ties in scores could affect Spearman ranking.  
- Suggest replacing "constituent" with "component" to avoid syntactic ambiguity.  
- The intuition that a constituent's literal usage implies shared co-occurrences is true for heads but not dependents (example: "spelling bee").  
- Section 5: several statements need running examples; unclear what "compound phrase token" refers to.  
- Section 5.1: "elementary APTs" likely refer to (w, path, w') triplets—term is confusing and should be clarified.  
- Clarify whether "shifted PMI" equals the PMI in equation (3).  
- A sentence about removing low-positive-PMI features is contradictory and needs rewriting for clarity.  
- Clarify what "not properly aligned" means and whether it simply means "not aligned at all."  
- Paragraphs spanning lines 558–563 and 575–580 are unclear and need reworking.  
- Explain why NMOD relation should yield considerable overlap after alignment.  
- Justify why higher-order dependency features are used broadly but only first-order features are used for the key similarity metric; Note 3’s explanation is unclear.  
- Section 6: claim that PPMI smoothing with α=0.75 has a small positive effect is not evident from Table 3.  
- Report optimal h and q values for equations 8 and 9, as they are crucial for hybridity gains—include them in Table 4’s legend.  
- Provide results using word2vec vectors for the compound phrases.  
- The FREQ baseline is unintuitive (why would frequent compounds be more compositional?) and suggests potential dataset bias.
