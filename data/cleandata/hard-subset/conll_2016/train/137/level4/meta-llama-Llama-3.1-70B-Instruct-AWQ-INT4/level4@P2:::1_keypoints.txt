- Experiments predict compositionality of 90 English compounds with human judgments, comparing neural embeddings and syntactic-context count vectors, and testing aligned vectors for the dependent component.
- Syntactic-context count vectors outperform neural embeddings for compositionality detection.
- Using aligned vectors for the dependent component yields a slight improvement over non-aligned vectors.
- Novelty is limited: aligned vectors approach was previously proposed by the authors.
- Dataset is small (90 compounds) and not well-described, raising generalizability concerns.
- Improvements from aligned vectors are marginal and may not justify the approach.
- Clear and well-written introduction to the compositionality detection problem.
- Thorough evaluation using the 90-compound dataset with human judgments.
- Significant finding that syntactic-context count vectors beat neural embeddings.
