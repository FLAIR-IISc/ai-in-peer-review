- Task/method summary: Predict compound compositionality by cosine similarity between compound vectors (as single tokens) and composed component vectors, evaluated by Spearman correlation against human judgments on a 90-compound dataset.

- Experimental factors: Compare neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors, and whether to use "aligned" vectors for the dependent component.

- Main results: Syntactic-context count vectors outperform embeddings; aligned vectors alone underperform unmodified vectors; a tuned hybrid of aligned+unaligned yields only slight improvement.

- Writing clarity: Introduction is good, but many sections (notably 5.1) are hard to read; recommend rephrasing with running examples.

- Novelty concern: The claimed innovation (aligned vectors) appears previously published by the authors, reducing novelty.

- Dataset concerns: The dataset is small (90 items) and insufficiently described, making results fragile.

- Missing dataset details: Need explanation of how compounds were selected, frequency ranges of compounds and components, number of human judgments per compound, and prevalence of tied scores affecting Spearman.

- Terminology issues: Terms like "packed anchored tree," "tree," and "elementary APTs" are unclear/confusing; recommend clearer names and definitions and using "component" instead of "constituent."

- Table 2/method details: Clarify whether features of order >3 are discarded and explain/elaborate on "incompatible types" with examples.

- Section 5/5.1 specifics: Provide a running example; define "compound phrase token"; clarify "shifted PMI" and contradictory phrasing about low positive PMI and phrase-context frequency.

- Alignment explanation lacking: Clarify what "not properly aligned" means, why overlap should be considerable for NMOD, and why alignment behaves as claimed.

- Feature-order inconsistency: Explain why higher-order dependency features are used generally but similarity measures use first-order features only, and why 2nd-order paths in composed representations are unreliable.

- Section 6/statistics: The benefit of PPMI smoothing (α=0.75) is not clearly supported by Table 3; report optimal h and q values to assess hybrid gains.

- Baselines and reporting: Provide results for word2vec compound vectors, justify the FREQ baseline (why frequency correlates with compositionality), and clarify table legends (e.g., that Table 4 uses the add composition).
