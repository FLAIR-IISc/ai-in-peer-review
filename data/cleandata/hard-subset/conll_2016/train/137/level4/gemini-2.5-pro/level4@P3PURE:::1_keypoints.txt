- Paper predicts compositionality of 90 English compounds using cosine similarity between compound vectors and composed component vectors, evaluated by Spearman correlation with human ratings.  
- Uses neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors; syntactic-context count vectors outperform neural embeddings.  
- Introduces "aligned" vectors for the dependent component; aligned vectors alone perform worse, but a tuned hybrid of aligned and unaligned vectors yields slight improvement.  
- Introduction is clear, but other sections (notably 5.1) are hard to follow and would benefit from running examples.  
- Novelty concern: aligned vectors were introduced in a previously suppressed reference by the authors, reducing the paper’s originality.  
- Dataset concern: the 90-compound dataset is small and poorly described; critical details like frequency ranges of compounds and components are missing.  
- Fragility of findings: improvements are marginal, demonstrated on a small/unclearly selected dataset, making results potentially fragile.  
- Section 3 terminology: the new term "packed anchored tree (PAT)" seems unnecessary and resembles standard dependency path extraction.  
- Section 3 clarity: unclear why PAT is called a "tree" and what "elementary APTs" are; these need explicit definitions.  
- Section 3 feature selection: unclear whether features of order >3 are discarded or if some types are eliminated—this process needs an illustrated example.  
- Section 4 dataset selection: the selection process for the Reddy et al. dataset is not described; selection criteria and frequency ranges should be reported.  
- Section 4 annotation details: number of human judgments per compound is unspecified; many tied scores could impact Spearman correlation.  
- Terminology: recommend using "component" instead of "constituent" to avoid syntactic ambiguity.  
- Section 4 intuition: the claim that a constituent’s literal use implies shared co-occurrences is stronger for heads than dependents and needs qualification.  
- Section 5 clarity: the sentence about elementary representations lacking contextual features is hard to parse; a running example is needed and clarify "compound phrase token."  
- Section 5.1 definition: suggest clarifying that "elementary APTs" appear to be (target word, dependency path, context word) triplets.  
- Section 5.1 PMI: clarify whether "shifted PMI" refers to the PMI in Equation (3).  
- Section 5.1 wording: the sentence about removing low-PMI features is contradictory and its referents ("these phrases"/"things") must be clarified.  
- Section 5.1 alignment: define "not properly aligned" (does it mean "unaligned"?) and clarify why feature overlap is expected to be considerable for NMOD relations.  
- Section 5.1 methodological inconsistency: the paper builds on higher-order dependency features but computes similarity using only first-order features—this inconsistency requires justification.  
- Footnote 3: the explanation that second-order paths are "not reliable" is unconvincing and needs elaboration.  
- Section 6 smoothing: the claim that PPMI smoothing with α=0.75 helps is not clearly supported by Table 3.  
- Section 6 hybridity: optimal hyperparameter values for h and q should be reported to assess the hybrid model’s contribution.  
- Section 6 results reporting: Table 4 appears to use additive composition—this should be explicitly stated in the caption.  
- Section 6 additional baselines: the authors should report results using word2vec vectors for compound phrases as the observed vector.  
- Section 6 FREQ baseline: the intuition linking compound frequency to compositionality is unclear and may indicate dataset bias.
