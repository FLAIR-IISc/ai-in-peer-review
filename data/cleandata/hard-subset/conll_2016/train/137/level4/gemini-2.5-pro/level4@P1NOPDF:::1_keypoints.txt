- Paper studies predicting English noun-compound compositionality by correlating cosine similarity between observed compound vectors and composed component vectors with human judgments (Spearman).
- Compares neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors; count vectors outperform neural embeddings.
- Authors investigate an "alignment" method for dependent components (from their prior work); alignment alone performs worse than unmodified vectors.
- A highly-tuned hybrid of aligned and unaligned vectors yields only a slight improvement.
- Primary novelty (aligned vectors) is diminished because the method was introduced by the authors in a previous suppressed publication.
- The dataset is small (90 compounds) and inadequately described, undermining confidence in the results.
- Critical missing dataset details: selection criteria, frequency ranges of compounds and components, number of human judgments per item, and prevalence of ties in scores.
- Small dataset, marginal improvements, and unclear compound selection make the paper's conclusions fragile.
- Presentation issues: introduction is strong but other sections (e.g., 5.1) are hard to follow and would benefit from running examples.
- Terminology concerns: new term "packed anchored tree" (PAT) is unclear and seems equivalent to simple dependency paths; "elementary APTs" needs clarification.
- Table 2 ambiguity: unclear why a feature is missing (feature-order cutoff vs. elimination of incompatible types); examples and explicit statements needed.
- Section 4 wording: prefer "component" over "constituent" to avoid syntactic confusion; the intuition about shared co-occurrence depends on whether a component is head or dependent.
- Section 5 clarity: unclear references (e.g., "compound phrase token"); concrete examples should illustrate objects discussed.
- Section 5.1 specifics: clarify whether "elementary APTs" are (target, path, context) triplets and whether "shifted PMI" matches Equation (3).
- Several sentences in Section 5.1 are confusing or contradictory (e.g., feature removal description, "not properly aligned" vs. unaligned), requiring rephrasing.
- Reasoning gaps: unclear why component feature overlap should be "considerable" for NMOD relations and why higher-order dependency features are ultimately ignored when measuring similarity.
- Footnote justification for using only first-order features in similarity computation is unconvincing; explanation of reliability of second-order paths is needed.
- Section 6: claim that smoothing PPMI (α=0.75) helps is not clearly supported by Table 3; expand or justify.
- Missing hyperparameter details: report the optimal h and q values used for the hybrid model to assess tuning influence.
- Table 4 ambiguity: specify in the caption that results shown are for the additive combination model.
- Additional experiments requested: report results using pre-trained word2vec vectors for observed compound phrase vectors.
- FREQ baseline unclear: need justification for expecting more frequent compounds to be more compositional and discussion of possible frequency bias in the dataset.
