- Experiments predict compositionality of English noun-noun compounds using cosine similarity between compound vectors and composed component vectors, evaluated with Spearman correlation on the Reddy et al. dataset (N=90).  
- Two main variables: neural word embeddings vs syntactic count-based vectors, and for count-based vectors: standard vs "aligned" modifier vectors (alignment from prior work).  
- Primary findings: syntactic count-based vectors outperform neural embeddings; aligned vectors alone underperform unaligned, but a tuned hybrid of aligned+unaligned gives slight improvement.  
- Presentation is uneven: introduction clear but sections like 5.1 are hard to follow and would benefit from running examples.  
- Novelty concern: main technical contribution (aligned vectors) was published previously by the authors, so novelty of current work is unclear.  
- Dataset size and description inadequate: N=90 is small and frequency ranges of compounds/components are not reported.  
- Robustness concern: marginal improvements on a small, under-specified dataset make conclusions fragile.  
- PATs terminology issue: "packed anchored tree" label is unclear and seems to be standard dependency path extraction; calling it a "tree" is not justified.  
- Clarify definition of "elementary APTs" and whether higher-order dependency paths (>3) were discarded; give examples of "incompatible types."  
- Dataset selection questions: how were the 90 compounds chosen and how do results vary across frequency bands?  
- Annotation details missing: number of human judgments per compound, distribution of compositionality scores, and prevalence of tied scores affecting Spearman.  
- Terminology suggestion: use "component" instead of "constituent" to avoid syntactic ambiguity.  
- The intuition that literal constituent use implies shared co-occurrences is not universally true, particularly for modifiers (e.g., "spelling" vs "spelling bee").  
- Section 5 clarity: the statement about "elementary representation" lacking contextual features is hard to parse and needs a running example.  
- Section 5.1 clarity: specify if "elementary APTs" are triplets (w, dependency path r, context w') and whether "shifted PMI" equals Equation (3).  
- Rephrase confusing claims about removing low-PMI features and define what it means for APTs to be "not properly aligned."  
- Explain why NMOD relation is expected to have high APT overlap and justify using only first-order features for similarity despite higher-order representations; Footnote 3 is insufficient.  
- Section 6: the claim that PPMI smoothing α=0.75 has a small positive effect is not obvious from Table 3—highlight supporting comparisons.  
- Report optimal hyperparameters h and q for Equations (8) and (9) to allow reproducibility and assess hybrid model contribution.  
- Table 4 caption should state that results are for the "add" composition function.  
- Provide a baseline using word2vec vectors for whole compound phrases (not just components) for a fuller comparison.  
- Clarify the intuition behind the FREQ baseline and discuss whether its strong performance indicates a frequency bias in the dataset.
