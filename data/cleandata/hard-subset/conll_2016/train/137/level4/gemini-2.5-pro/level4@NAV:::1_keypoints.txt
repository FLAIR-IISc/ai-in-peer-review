- Paper predicts compositionality of 90 English compounds by correlating cosine similarity between compound vectors and composed component vectors with human ratings.  
- Dataset: 90 compounds from Reddy et al., but selection criteria, annotator counts, and frequency statistics are not provided.  
- Main variables tested: neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors; aligned vs. unaligned count vectors; hybrid combinations.  
- Results: syntactic-context count vectors outperform neural embeddings.  
- Aligned vectors alone underperform unaligned vectors; a tuned hybrid of aligned+unaligned gives only marginal improvement.  
- Novelty concern: aligned-vector technique was developed in the authors' prior (suppressed) work, reducing the paper’s originality.  
- Dataset size and lack of description make results fragile and likely non-generalizable.  
- Presentation: introduction is clear, but sections (notably 5.1) are hard to follow and need running examples.  
- Terminology concern: the new term "packed anchored tree" is unjustified and resembles standard dependency-path extractions; calling it a “tree” is unclear.  
- Request to define “elementary APTs” (term used but not explained).  
- Table 2 ambiguity: unclear whether features of order >3 were discarded or if absent features are due to “incompatible types”; needs explicit clarification and examples.  
- Reddy dataset needs fuller description: selection method, frequency distributions of compounds and components, and annotator counts.  
- Possible evaluation issues: score collision in human ratings could undermine Spearman rank correlation.  
- Suggest using “component” instead of “constituent” to avoid syntactic ambiguity.  
- The co-occurrence intuition applies mainly to heads, not dependents (e.g., “spelling” vs. “spelling bee”); this should be qualified.  
- Section 5 contains a convoluted sentence about elementary representations and compound phrase tokens; a concrete example is needed.  
- Clarify whether “compound phrase token” means the compound’s single-token vector.  
- In Section 5.1, confirm whether “elementary APTs” are (target word, dependency path, context word) triplets.  
- Clarify that “shifted PMI” refers to the PMI in Equation (3) and rephrase confusing sentences about low positive PMI and what “these phrases” refers to.  
- Define “APTs which have not been properly aligned” — does it mean unaligned vectors or something else?  
- Explain why NMOD relations would produce considerable potential overlap between component representations.  
- Justify why higher-order dependency features are used in composition but similarity is measured with only first-order features; footnote 3’s explanation is insufficient.  
- Section 6 claim that PPMI smoothing with α = 0.75 helps is not clearly supported by Table 3.  
- Report optimal hyperparameter values for h and q (Equations 8 and 9) used by the hybrid model.  
- Table 4 should state explicitly that results are for the “add” combination method.  
- Provide a baseline using word2vec vectors for compound phrases treated as single tokens.  
- Explain the intuition behind the FREQ baseline; frequent compounds being more compositional is not obviously justified and may indicate dataset bias.
