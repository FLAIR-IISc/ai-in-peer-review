- Task: Predict compositionality of 90 English compounds by comparing compound vectors to composed constituent vectors using cosine similarity and Spearman correlation with human ratings.  
- Data: Dataset is small (90 compounds) from Reddy et al.; selection process, frequency ranges, and number of human judgments per item are not reported.  
- Main methods: Compare neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors; test “aligned” vs. unaligned vectors for the dependent component.  
- Key result: Syntactic-context count vectors outperform neural embeddings.  
- Aligned vectors: Using aligned vectors alone performs worse than unaligned; a tuned combination of aligned and unaligned yields only slight improvement.  
- Novelty concern: Primary innovation (aligned vectors) was previously presented by the authors in a suppressed reference, weakening novelty.  
- Fragility concern: Small dataset, unclear selection/frequency biases, and marginal gains make the findings fragile.  
- Evaluation concern: Many compounds may share identical scores; this could affect ranking and Spearman correlation—details unreported.  
- Terminology: “Constituent” is misleading; reviewer suggests “component” for NN compounds.  
- Presentation praise/suggestion: Introduction is well-written; other sections (e.g., 5.1) are hard to follow and would benefit from running examples.  
- APT terminology: “Packed anchored tree” and “elementary APTs” are unclear and seem unnecessary—need clarification and justification.  
- Table 2 query: Unclear if features of order >3 were discarded or incompatible types eliminated—example needed to clarify omissions.  
- Section 5 clarification: Ambiguity about “compound phrase token” and which element lacks contextual features—running examples requested.  
- Section 5.1 confusion: “Elementary APTs” likely triplets (w, r, w'); need clearer terms and confirmation that “shifted PMI” equals PMI in eq. (3).  
- PMI/PPMI phrasing: Several sentences about PMI/PPMI and low positive PMI are contradictory or unclear and require rewording.  
- Alignment overlap: Unclear what “not properly aligned” means and why NMOD relations would produce considerable overlap—needs explanation.  
- Feature order issue: Why higher-order dependency features are used generally but similarity is measured with first-order features—clarify reliability concerns for second-order paths.  
- Section 6 questions: Effect of PPMI smoothing (α=0.75) not clearly shown; optimal values for h and q are not reported, making hybridity claims unclear.  
- Results/reporting: Caption should state Table 4 uses additive combination; request results for word2vec vectors trained on compound phrases.  
- Baseline rationale: FREQ baseline rationale is unclear—why would frequent compounds be more compositional, and does this indicate dataset bias?
