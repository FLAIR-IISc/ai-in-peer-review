- Experiments predict English compound compositionality using cosine similarity between compound vectors and composed component vectors.  
- Dataset: 90 compounds from Reddy et al. with 1–5 compositionality scores, but number of human judges unspecified.  
- Method compares neural embeddings vs. syntactic-context count vectors and tests “aligned” vectors for the dependent component.  
- Alignment strategy models semantic shift from dependent to head and was introduced previously in an unpublished work.  
- Evaluation uses Spearman correlation between cosine similarities and human judgments.  
- Results: syntactic-context count vectors outperform neural embeddings.  
- Results: aligned vectors alone perform worse than unaligned vectors; a tuned combination yields modest improvement.  
- Presentation: introduction is clear, but other sections (notably Section 5.1) are hard to follow and need illustrative examples.  
- Novelty concern: main novelty (aligned vectors) was presented earlier by the authors in unpublished work, reducing originality.  
- Data concerns: small dataset size and limited description raise questions about result robustness and compound selection.  
- Frequency effects: impact of differing term/component frequencies on results is unclear and unreported.  
- Annotation details: number of judgments per compound and score distribution (ties) are not provided, affecting Spearman correlation reliability.  
- Terminology: “constituent” should be replaced with “component” for NN sequences.  
- Claim caveat: co-occurrence intuition holds mainly when the component is the head, not for dependents (e.g., “spelling bee”).  
- Section 3: term “packed anchored tree” is unclear and seems to be dependency-path extraction; rationale for calling it a “tree” is not given.  
- Section 3: “elementary APTs” is undefined and confusing.  
- Table 2: omission of higher-order (>3) features is not stated and needs clarification or examples.  
- Section 4: selection criteria and frequency ranges for the 90 compounds and components are missing.  
- Section 4: need clarification of how many compounds share identical compositionality scores.  
- Section 5: unclear reference in sentence about elementary representations and “compound phrase token”; concrete example requested.  
- Section 5.1: define “elementary APTs” explicitly (is it a triplet w, r, w'?) and clarify “shifted PMI” wording.  
- Section 5.1: ambiguous sentences about low-positive PMI and “these phrases” require rewriting for clarity.  
- Section 5.1: explain what “not properly aligned” means and why overlap should be low; paragraphs 558–563 are confusing.  
- Section 5.1: justify why NMOD relations should show considerable overlap between component APTs.  
- Section 5.1: explain why higher-order dependency features are used generally but only first-order features are used for the crucial similarity measure.  
- Section 6: smoothing effect of α = 0.75 on PPMI is not clearly supported by Table 3.  
- Section 6: report optimal values for h and q to assess the contribution of hybridity to improvements.  
- Table 4: legend must state results are for the additive combination method.  
- Section 6: provide results for word2vec vectors for the compound phrases as well.  
- Baseline: motivation for the FREQ baseline is unclear—why would frequent compounds be more compositional, and does this indicate dataset bias?
