- Experiments estimate compositionality of 90 English compound nouns by comparing cosine similarity of compound vectors with composed component vectors, evaluated via Spearman correlation with human ratings.
- Syntactic-context count vectors outperform neural embeddings; aligned modifier vectors alone underperform, while a tuned mix of aligned and unaligned vectors gives a small additional gain.
- Novelty concern: alignment mechanism appears not new and was previously introduced by the authors (suppressed reference).
- Dataset concerns: only 90 items, selection procedure unclear, and small size makes empirical conclusions fragile.
- Dataset under-described: no information on annotator counts, frequency ranges of compounds/components, or prevalence of tied scores (which could affect Spearman).
- Readability issues: sections beyond the introduction (notably 5.1) are hard to follow; more continuous worked examples would improve clarity.
- Terminology/naming issues: the new name “anchored packed tree” is unclear—why call it a “tree” and what exactly are “elementary APTs”?
- Request explicit definition/format of “elementary APTs” (e.g., triple: target word, dependency path, context word) to avoid confusion.
- Clarify whether “compound phrase token” means the merged token for the compound and provide a running example to track objects throughout Section 5.
- Table 2 ambiguity: state whether features were truncated above order 3 or excluded due to “incompatible co-occurrence types,” and give an explicit incompatibility example.
- Clarify whether “shifted PMI” is PME with an additive shift as in Levy et al. (2015).
- Rewrite and clarify the sentence about removing low-positive-PMI features—specify whether “these phrases” refers to compounds or contexts and explain the causal mechanism.
- Terminology preference: use “component” rather than “constituent” to avoid syntactic ambiguity.
- Challenge the intuition that modifiers share contexts with compounds (modifier distributions often differ from the compound, e.g., “spelling” vs “spelling bee”).
- Explain “not properly aligned”: do you mean partially aligned, misaligned, or not aligned at all?
- Justify the claim that NMOD shows considerable overlap without alignment despite general claims of little overlap across unaligned APTs.
- Explain why similarity is computed using only first-order features when higher-order contexts are modeled throughout, and expand on footnote 3 about unreliability of second-order paths.
- Section 6 claim that PPMI smoothing with α = 0.75 helps is not clearly supported by Table 3; either point to consistent evidence or temper the claim.
- Report the optimal h and q values from equations (8) and (9) to quantify the hybridization’s contribution beyond the unaligned model.
- Indicate that Table 4 uses additive composition in the caption (and ensure composition method is explicit).
- Request results treating compounds as tokens in word2vec (i.e., learning compound embeddings directly) as an additional baseline.
- Question the FREQ baseline’s intuition: explain why more frequent compounds should be more compositional and discuss possible dataset bias if frequency correlates with compositionality.
