1. Proposes an APT-based compositional method to detect non-compositional noun compounds, evaluated on 90 compounds with human judgments and compared to neural embedding composition baselines.  
2. Introduces Anchored Packed Trees (APTs) encoding dependency paths from target words to contexts for finer-grained composition.  
3. Finds typed dependency features in contextual representations capture syntactic relations better than untyped features.  
4. Proposes a hybrid composition model combining aligned and unaligned APTs that outperforms each alone.  
5. Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on compositionality detection, beating previous methods.  
6. Offers clear linguistic and computational theoretical foundations for the approach.  
7. Conducts a comprehensive evaluation against multiple baselines and prior methods.  
8. Method is computationally intensive, particularly for constructing and composing APTs.  
9. Approach is specifically designed for noun compounds, so generalization to other phrase types is uncertain.  
10. Heavily depends on high-quality preprocessing (tokenization, POS tagging, dependency parsing).  
11. Question: How will the approach be extended to other types of phrases or linguistic phenomena?  
12. Question: What computational resources are required and how can the method be optimized for larger-scale applications?  
13. Question: How will the authors address limited generalizability and improve robustness?
