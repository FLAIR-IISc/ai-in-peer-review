- Proposes a new compositionality detection approach for compound nouns using Anchored Packed Trees (APTs) versus ad-hoc composition on neural embeddings.  
- Introduces APTs that incorporate syntactic structure into word contextual features for composition.  
- Shows APTs outperform state-of-the-art neural embeddings and other compositional methods on compound-noun compositionality detection.  
- Finds typed dependency features and higher-order dependency paths are highly informative for determining compositionality.  
- Provides thorough evaluation on a standard dataset with comparisons to competing methods.  
- Offers detailed analysis of parameter settings and the effects of different composition operations.  
- Emphasizes the importance of incorporating syntactic structure into compositional models.  
- Focuses mainly on compound nouns, leaving generalization to other phrase types unclear.  
- Does not include a detailed analysis of APT computational complexity for large-scale applications.  
- Assumes dependency parses are accurate and does not assess robustness to parsing errors.  
- Asks how APTs can be extended to adjective-noun and verb phrases.  
- Requests a more detailed computational-complexity comparison with other compositional methods.  
- Seeks clarification on handling dependency-parse errors and their expected impact on performance.
