- Summary: Proposes Anchored Packed Trees (APTs), a compositional distributional model using typed higher-order dependency paths to detect non-compositional compound nouns, evaluated on 90 compounds with human judgments.  
- Summary: Experiments compare composition operations and parameters (normalized counts, PPMI) against neural word embeddings using Spearman correlation.  
- Contribution: Introduces APTs as an integrated compositional distributional approach using typed dependency paths.  
- Contribution: Evaluates APTs on a 90-compound dataset with human compositionality scores, reporting state-of-the-art results.  
- Contribution: Shows APTs outperform state-of-the-art neural word embeddings for detecting non-compositional phrases.  
- Strength: Presents a novel, linguistically motivated approach to compositional semantics.  
- Strength: Provides thorough evaluation across composition operations and parameter settings.  
- Strength: Achieves strong empirical performance on a challenging dataset.  
- Weakness: Relies on extensive linguistic annotation that may not be available for many languages or datasets.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of the linguistic implications of the results.  
- Weakness: Needs deeper comparison to other compositional semantics approaches to better situate the work.  
- Question: Please detail the linguistic annotation used and how it was obtained.  
- Question: How will the approach be extended to other phrase types (e.g., verb phrases, sentences) and what are the expected challenges?  
- Question: Can you provide more analysis relating the results to existing theories of compositional semantics?
