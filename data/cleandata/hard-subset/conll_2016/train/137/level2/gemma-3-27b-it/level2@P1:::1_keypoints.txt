- Main contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation of compositional methods for non-compositional phrase detection using the Reddy et al. (2011) dataset.  
- Main contribution: showing that typed dependency information (higher-order dependency paths) in APTs improves performance over standard neural embedding baselines.  
- Main contribution: detailed ablation study (aligned vs unaligned APTs, INT vs UNI composition) offering insights into model behavior.

- Strength: thorough, systematic empirical evaluation across parameter settings (PPMI smoothing, composition ops, alignment).  
- Strength: strong, reproducible baseline using word2vec and clear experimental setup; surpasses Reddy et al. (2011) state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: insightful ablation study revealing that a hybrid of aligned and unaligned features performs best.  
- Strength: sensible framing of non-compositionality detection as a testbed for compositional semantics.  
- Strength: clear presentation and illustrative example of the Anchored Packed Trees (APT) model.
