- Paper studies detection of non-compositional phrases using compositional distributional methods, comparing integrated APT-style models with composition applied to neural embeddings.
- Core investigation: how context definition (typed vs untyped) and higher-order dependency paths affect compositional modeling.
- Evaluation uses the Reddy et al. (2011) compositionality detection dataset.
- Primary contribution: thorough empirical evaluation of APT-based models with varied alignment and composition functions against strong neural embedding baselines.
- Secondary contribution: demonstration that dependency-informed models (especially the hybrid aligned/unaligned approach) outperform untyped co-occurrence models.
- Contribution: replication and extension of Reddy et al. (2011), providing a clear benchmark and improved performance over prior work.
- Strength: comprehensive, systematic exploration of APT parameters (PPMI, alignment, composition) and comparisons to baselines.
- Strength: clear emphasis and empirical evidence for the benefit of incorporating syntactic/dependency information.
- Strength: clear presentation and illustration of the Anchored Packed Trees (APT) model.
- Strength: thoughtful, insightful analysis explaining observed trends (e.g., PPMI smoothing, when unaligned features help).
- Weakness: limited scope of neural baselines; lacks comparison to more recent architectures (e.g., transformer or GNN-based compositional models).
- Weakness: small dataset (90 compound nouns) raises questions about generalizability to other phrase types and domains.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter optimization procedure and rationale for certain cbow settings.
- Weakness: lack of detailed error analysis showing which non-compositional phrases are problematic and why.
- Weakness: hybrid approach motivated intuitively but lacks rigorous theoretical justification for why combined aligned/unaligned features work well.
- Question: please provide detailed hyperparameter optimization process, search space, and optimization algorithm.
- Question: have transformer-based or other recent neural compositional models been evaluated as baselines, or what would their expected impact be?
- Question: can you show example phrases where the model fails and analyze the causes of those errors?
- Question: what is the computational cost of the APT model versus neural embedding baselines, and is the complexity justified by performance gains?
- Additional comment: make composition operation notation (e.g., ⊔INT, ⊔UNI) more consistent.
- Additional comment: resolve the "reference suppressed" issue in Section 3.
- Additional comment: include a table summarizing key dataset characteristics from related work for reader clarity.
