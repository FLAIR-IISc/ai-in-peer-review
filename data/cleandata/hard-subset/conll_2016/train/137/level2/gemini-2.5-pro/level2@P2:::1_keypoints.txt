- Paper studies noun-compound compositionality, comparing an APT (sparse, syntactically-typed high-dim) model to dense word2vec composition, evaluated on Reddy et al. (2011).
- APT models (especially a hybrid combining aligned and unaligned features) achieve a new state-of-the-art Spearman’s rho (0.79), outperforming strong word2vec baselines (0.74).

- Contribution: Shows that explicit, high-dimensional syntactic features (APT) can substantially outperform standard dense-embedding composition for noun-compound compositionality.
- Contribution: Provides a thorough empirical study with multiple model variants and a stronger updated word2vec baseline, establishing a new benchmark.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel hybrid composition method that combines aligned and unaligned features, indicating complementary information.

- Strength: Achieves large and statistically significant empirical gains (rho 0.79 vs. prior 0.714), and uses a strong updated baseline.
- Strength: APT is linguistically motivated and interpretable, leveraging typed dependency paths and alignment to model syntactic structure.
- Strength: Evaluation is rigorous and transparent (same corpus, cross-validation, parameter search, significance testing, multiple baselines).
- Strength: Paper is clearly written with helpful examples and replicable methodology.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to a single task/dataset (noun compounds), so generalizability to other compositional phenomena is unclear.
- Weakness: Practicality and scalability concerns are not addressed—APT uses very high-dimensional sparse vectors (~230k dims) with unknown memory/time costs versus compact embeddings.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why the hybrid (aligned+unaligned) model works—more intuition or error analysis is needed.

- Question: Please report practical trade-offs—memory and computation for building/using APTs versus word2vec.
- Question: Can you elaborate why unaligned features complement aligned ones, and provide error analysis showing which compounds benefit from the unaligned component?
- Question: Regarding word2vec subsampling, did the higher threshold help mainly for low-frequency items, or did it improve performance across frequency ranges?
