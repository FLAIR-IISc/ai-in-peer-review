- Paper compares typed, higher-order dependency APT composition to additive composition on neural embeddings for noun–noun compositionality detection, reporting up to ρ = 0.79 on Reddy et al. (2011) using ukWaC.

- Main contribution 1: Empirically shows APTs with typed, higher-order dependency features outperform untyped window-based representations (including word2vec) on the Reddy dataset.

- Main contribution 2: Introduces a hybrid APT that linearly combines aligned and unaligned APTs, showing complementary benefits and best performance.

- Main contribution 3: Provides controlled comparisons of composition operators, PMI smoothing, and constituent weighting, offering insights into modeling choices and robustness.

- Strength: Clear empirical gains over strong baselines on the canonical dataset, with consistent trends and significance discussion across parameter settings.

- Strength: Well-motivated use of syntactic structure; APTs offer interpretability and linguistic plausibility via typed dependency paths and alignment.

- Strength: Experimental design mitigates information leakage by merging compounds and computing similarity with first-order phrasal features, supporting claims that composition predicts phrasal behavior.

- Strength: Thoughtful analysis of modeling choices (PMI α, composition operators, PPMI timing, weighting/mixing), with practical findings for practitioners.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope — only one small dataset (90 items) and only noun–noun compounds, so generalizability is untested.

- Weakness: Baseline coverage is narrow — missing dependency-based embeddings, GloVe, lexical function/tensor models, and phrase-level neural models trained on the same corpus.

- Weakness: Frequency effects not fully addressed — no partial correlations or per-frequency-bin analyses despite strong correlation between frequency and compositionality.

- Weakness: Statistical reporting lacks detail (repetition counts, exact tests, confidence intervals); claim that differences >0.005 are significant is under-specified given small N.

- Weakness: Methodological/clarity gaps — dense notation, incomplete references, missing table references, unspecified cross-validation repetitions, and absent runtime/memory trade-off discussion for large APT dims.

- Weakness: Sensitivity to parsing and preprocessing not quantified — reliance on Malt parses and compound-merging may affect results, and alignment underperforms on NMOD compounds without full explanation.

- Questions for authors: Provide performance by compound frequency (partial correlations or frequency-bin breakdowns) and report repetitions/tests used for significance.

- Questions for authors: Evaluate dependency-based embeddings with additive composition and report computational cost (time/memory) and any pruning or sparsity strategies.

- Questions for authors: Report robustness to parser choice and parsing errors, include error analysis showing where APTs help or fail, and illustrate aligned vs unaligned feature contributions.
