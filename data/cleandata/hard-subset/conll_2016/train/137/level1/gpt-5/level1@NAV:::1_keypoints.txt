- APT-based composition (typed, higher-order dependency features) outperforms naive additive neural embeddings on Reddy et al. (2011) compounds, achieving ρ = 0.79 vs. 0.74 (CBOW) and 0.71 (prior SOTA).  
- Primary contribution: empirical evidence that typed, higher-order dependency features substantially improve compositionality detection on the Reddy benchmark.  
- Secondary contribution: a hybrid composition scheme combining aligned and unaligned APTs, showing complementary signals from offset (structure-aware) and direct (structure-agnostic) modifier features.  
- Tertiary contribution: careful experimental design to avoid leakage (re-tokenizing compounds, separating constituent contexts) plus diagnostics on PPMI smoothing, subsampling, and constituent weighting.  
- Strength: strong quantitative gains with small error bars and a clear significance threshold (>0.005).  
- Strength: methodological rigor in preprocessing to prevent contextual overlap between compounds and constituents.  
- Strength: insightful ablations (aligned vs. unaligned vs. hybrid, ⊔INT vs. ⊔UNI, PPMI α) and finding that modifiers slightly outweigh heads (optimal h, q ∈ [0.3,0.5]).  
- Strength: clear comparison to strong baselines (word2vec with parameter sweeps and frequency baselines), arguing typed paths add information beyond untyped co-occurrences.  
- Weakness: narrow evaluation — only 90 noun compounds from one dataset, so generalization to other constructions and datasets is untested.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to dependency-based embeddings (e.g., Levy & Goldberg), GloVe, or learned composition functions for embeddings.  
- Weakness: limited novelty claim — core APT ideas appear in prior work (e.g., Weeds et al., 2014); novelty mainly in task/application and hybridization.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to parser errors and low-frequency compounds is only qualitatively discussed; robustness to noise is unclear.  
- Question: How does APT compare to dependency-based word2vec or GloVe—could those close the performance gap?  
- Question: Do results generalize to other compositionality datasets or phrase types (V–N, Adj–N, phrasal verbs)?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to parse quality and maximum path order (any ablation on order-1 vs. order-2)?  
- Question: Will the authors release code and preprocessing to reproduce the leakage-avoidance steps?
