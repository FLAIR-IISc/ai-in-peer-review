- Task: Detecting compositionality in noun compounds.  
- Proposed model: Anchored Packed Trees (APT) using sparse, high-dimensional vectors from typed, higher-order dependency paths with an alignment transform.  
- Key mechanism: Linguistically-motivated alignment (offsetting) of feature spaces before composition.  
- Empirical result: New state-of-the-art on Reddy et al. (2011) with Spearman’s ρ = 0.79.  
- Baseline comparison: APT significantly outperforms previous best and tuned word2vec baselines (baseline ρ = 0.74).  
- Contribution 1: Strong empirical SOTA result on noun compound compositionality detection.  
- Contribution 2: Introduction and evaluation of a structured compositional mechanism (alignment) and finding that a hybrid of aligned and unaligned features is best.  
- Strength: Thorough, rigorous evaluation with multiple APT variants and careful tuning of baselines and parameters.  
- Strength: Linguistically-grounded approach using typed dependency paths and syntactic-role alignment.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a single, small dataset (90 compounds), raising questions about generalization to other phrase types and downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: Scalability and efficiency concerns due to very high-dimensional (~230k) sparse vectors and no practical cost analysis.  
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation for why the hybrid/unaligned composition performs so well and why unaligned alone is nearly as strong (ρ = 0.78).
