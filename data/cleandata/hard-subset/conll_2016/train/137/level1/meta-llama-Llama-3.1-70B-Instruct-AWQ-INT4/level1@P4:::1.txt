**Summary of the Paper**

This paper investigates the detection of non-compositional phrases using compositional methods. The authors compare an integrated compositional distributional approach with the ad-hoc compositional approach of applying simple composition operations to state-of-the-art neural embeddings. The proposed approach uses sparse high-dimensional representations and typed contextual features to improve the detection of non-compositional phrases. The authors evaluate their approach on a dataset of 90 compound nouns with human judgments of compositionality and report significant improvements over previous state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Proposal of an integrated compositional distributional approach**: The authors propose a new approach to compositional semantics that integrates syntactic structure within the contextual features of words, which is then central to the compositional process.
2. **Evaluation of the approach on a dataset of compound nouns**: The authors evaluate their approach on a dataset of 90 compound nouns with human judgments of compositionality and report significant improvements over previous state-of-the-art results.
3. **Comparison with ad-hoc compositional approaches**: The authors compare their approach with ad-hoc compositional approaches that apply simple composition operations to state-of-the-art neural embeddings and report significant improvements.

**Strengths**

1. **Significant improvements over previous state-of-the-art results**: The authors report significant improvements over previous state-of-the-art results on the task of detecting non-compositional phrases.
2. **Novel approach to compositional semantics**: The authors propose a new approach to compositional semantics that integrates syntactic structure within the contextual features of words.
3. **Thorough evaluation and comparison**: The authors evaluate their approach on a dataset of compound nouns and compare it with ad-hoc compositional approaches, providing a thorough understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited scope**: The authors only evaluate their approach on a dataset of compound nouns, which may not be representative of all types of non-compositional phrases.
2. **Dependence on high-quality syntactic parses**: The authors' approach relies on high-quality syntactic parses, which may not always be available or accurate.
3. **Computational complexity**: The authors' approach may be computationally expensive due to the use of sparse high-dimensional representations and typed contextual features.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend your approach to other types of non-compositional phrases, such as idiomatic expressions or phrasal verbs?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of computational complexity, and what are the potential trade-offs between accuracy and efficiency?
3. How do you plan to evaluate the robustness of your approach to different types of noise or errors in the syntactic parses or contextual features?
