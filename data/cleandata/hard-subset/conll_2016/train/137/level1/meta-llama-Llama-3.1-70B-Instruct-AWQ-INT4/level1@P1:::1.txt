This paper presents a comprehensive study on the detection of non-compositional phrases using compositional methods. The authors compare an integrated compositional distributional approach with a more traditional ad-hoc compositional approach using neural embeddings. The main contributions of this work are:

1. The proposal of an integrated compositional distributional approach using sparse high-dimensional representations, which outperforms the traditional ad-hoc approach.
2. The use of a new dataset of 90 compound nouns with human judgments of compositionality to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
3. The comparison of different composition operations, including pointwise addition and multiplication, and the evaluation of their impact on the performance of the model.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The authors provide a thorough review of the related work in the field of distributional semantics and compositionality detection.
2. The proposed approach is well-motivated and grounded in linguistic theory, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the underlying assumptions and principles.
3. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed, with a clear description of the methodology and results.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

1. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed approach can be applied to more complex phrases or sentences, beyond simple noun compounds.
2. The evaluation of the model is limited to a single dataset, and it is unclear how well the approach would generalize to other datasets or domains.
3. Some of the technical details, such as the implementation of the composition operations and the optimization of the model parameters, are not fully explained.

Questions to authors:

1. Can you provide more details on how the proposed approach can be applied to more complex phrases or sentences?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of data sparsity, which is a common problem in distributional semantics?
3. Can you provide more information on the optimization of the model parameters, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the optimization algorithm used?

Additional comments:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work on compositionality detection, including the strengths and limitations of existing approaches.
* The authors could provide more information on the preprocessing steps, such as the tokenization and part-of-speech tagging of the corpus.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including a discussion of the errors and limitations of the model.
