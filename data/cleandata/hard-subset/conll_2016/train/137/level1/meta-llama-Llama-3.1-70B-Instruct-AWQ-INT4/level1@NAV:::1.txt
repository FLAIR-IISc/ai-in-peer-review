**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a compositional distributional approach to improve the detection of non-compositional phrases, specifically compound nouns. The authors use a dataset of 90 compound nouns with human judgments of compositionality and compare the performance of their approach with state-of-the-art neural word embeddings. The proposed approach, called Anchored Packed Trees (APTs), uses typed contextual features and aligns vector spaces before composition. The authors evaluate their approach using various composition operations and parameter settings and show that it outperforms the state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Anchored Packed Trees (APTs) approach**: The authors propose a novel compositional distributional approach that uses typed contextual features and aligns vector spaces before composition. This approach is shown to be effective in detecting non-compositional phrases.
2. **Improved performance on compositionality detection**: The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms state-of-the-art neural word embeddings on the task of compositionality detection for compound nouns.
3. **Evaluation of various composition operations and parameter settings**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of different composition operations and parameter settings, providing insights into the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of typed contextual features**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of using typed contextual features in improving the detection of non-compositional phrases.
2. **Alignment of vector spaces**: The authors show that aligning vector spaces before composition is a crucial step in improving the performance of their approach.
3. **Thorough evaluation**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their approach, providing insights into the strengths and weaknesses of their method.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset**: The authors use a relatively small dataset of 90 compound nouns, which may not be representative of all types of non-compositional phrases.
2. **Assumes a specific dependency structure**: The authors assume a specific dependency structure for compound nouns, which may not be applicable to other types of phrases.
3. **Requires careful tuning of parameters**: The authors' approach requires careful tuning of parameters, which may be time-consuming and require significant expertise.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend your approach to other types of non-compositional phrases, such as idiomatic expressions or phrasal verbs?
2. Can you provide more insights into the choice of parameter settings and how they affect the performance of your approach?
3. How do you plan to address the issue of sparse data, which may be a challenge in applying your approach to low-frequency phrases?
