- Proposes an integrated compositional distributional approach using sparse high-dimensional representations that outperforms an ad-hoc neural-embedding approach.  
- Introduces a new dataset of 90 compound nouns with human compositionality judgments for evaluation.  
- Compares different composition operations (e.g., pointwise addition and multiplication) and evaluates their impact on model performance.  

- Provides a thorough review of related work in distributional semantics and compositionality detection.  
- Motivates the approach with linguistic theory and clearly explains underlying assumptions.  
- Presents a comprehensive, well-designed experimental evaluation with clear methodology and results.  

- Lacks a clear explanation of how the approach generalizes to more complex phrases or full sentences beyond noun compounds.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset, leaving generalizability to other datasets or domains uncertain.  
- Some technical implementation details (composition operations, parameter optimization) are not fully explained.  

- Request for details on applying the approach to more complex phrases or sentences.  
- Asks how the authors will address data sparsity common in distributional semantics.  
- Requests more information on model parameter optimization, hyperparameter choices, and optimization algorithms.  

- Recommend a more detailed discussion of related work on compositionality detection, including pros and cons of existing methods.  
- Suggests providing more preprocessing details (tokenization, POS tagging) of the corpus.
