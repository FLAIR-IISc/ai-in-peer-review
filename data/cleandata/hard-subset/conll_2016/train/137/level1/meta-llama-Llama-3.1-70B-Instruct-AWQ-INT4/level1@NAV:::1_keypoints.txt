- Proposes Anchored Packed Trees (APTs): a compositional distributional model using typed contextual features and aligned vector spaces for compound noun compositionality detection.
- Evaluated on 90 compound nouns with human compositionality judgments and compared to neural embeddings; APTs outperform state-of-the-art.
- Contribution: introduces APTs with typed contextual features and vector-space alignment before composition.
- Contribution: shows improved performance over neural word embeddings on compound noun compositionality detection.
- Contribution: provides a systematic evaluation of composition operations and parameter settings.
- Strength: effective use of typed contextual features to detect non-compositional phrases.
- Strength: vector-space alignment prior to composition is shown to be crucial.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation that analyzes method strengths and weaknesses.
- Weakness: small dataset (90 compounds) limits representativeness and generalizability.
- Weakness: assumes a specific dependency structure for compounds, which may not generalize to other phrase types.
- Weakness: requires careful parameter tuning, which is time-consuming and expertise-dependent.
- Question: how will the approach be extended to other non-compositional phrases (idioms, phrasal verbs)?
- Question: can the authors provide more insight into parameter choice and its effect on performance?
- Question: how will the method address sparse data and low-frequency phrase challenges?
