- Paper investigates compositionality detection for noun compounds using anchored packed trees (APTs) with typed dependency paths versus naive composition of neural embeddings.  
- Contribution: novel integrated APT compositional approach that achieves state-of-the-art (Spearman 0.79 vs. 0.71).  
- Contribution: systematic exploration of composition operations (aligned vs. unaligned, intersective vs. union) and parameter settings.  
- Contribution: shows typed dependency features in APTs encode syntactic structure crucial for compositionality detection.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated evaluation framework that correctly favors compositionality detection over phrase-similarity tasks.  
- Strength: technically sound explanation of vector space alignment and why simple operations (e.g., addition) and hybrid strategies work.  
- Strength: compelling empirical results with significant improvement and thorough parameter analyses (PPMI smoothing, composition ops, alignment).  
- Strength: insightful observation that frequency correlates with compositionality (FREQ baseline outperforms RAND).  
- Strength: well-executed comparison between integrated APTs and naive neural embedding composition, providing a clear benchmark.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to noun compounds, with no evidence of generalization to other phrase types.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis explaining why unaligned features outperform aligned features.  
- Weakness: no discussion of computational complexity or practical costs of high-dimensional sparse APTs versus dense neural embeddings.  
- Weakness: limited parameter search for neural embeddings; not shown that tested settings were near-optimal.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of why the hybrid (aligned + unaligned) approach yields the best performance.  
- Question: request for analysis of what contexts unaligned features capture that aligned features miss.  
- Question: ask for explanation why modifier weight (h and q in [0.3,0.5]) is slightly more informative than head for noun compounds.  
- Question: ask how APTs would scale to larger corpora and generalize to phrase types beyond noun compounds.
