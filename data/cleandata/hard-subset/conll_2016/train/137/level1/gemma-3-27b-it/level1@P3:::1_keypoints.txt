- Investigates detection of non-compositional phrases using APTs (sparse high-dimensional, dependency-typed) vs compositional operations on neural embeddings.  
- Main contribution: empirical evaluation of incorporating typed dependency information via APTs for compositionality detection, compared to strong neural baselines.  
- Secondary contribution: careful experimental setup using Reddy et al. (2011) dataset and extensive parameter tuning.  
- Key result: a hybrid model combining aligned and unaligned APT features yields the best performance (incremental but meaningful improvement).  
- Strong empirical evaluation with 3-fold cross-validation and systematic exploration of PPMI smoothing, composition ops, and weighting schemes.  
- Novelty in the Anchored Packed Trees representation and the alignment/reduction strategy for dependency-path-based feature spaces.  
- Valuable direct comparison to neural baselines; cbow outperforms skip-gram here and subsampling requires task-specific tuning.  
- Insight that combining dependency-structured and general co-occurrence information improves compositionality detection.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with helpful illustrations (e.g., Table 1) and contextualized related work.  
- Evaluation limited to compound nouns and a single dataset, raising questions about generalizability to idioms, phrasal verbs, or other languages.  
- APT approach likely more computationally expensive due to high-dimensional dependency-path encoding; runtime/memory costs not reported.  
- Dependence on dependency parser accuracy is a concern; parsing errors (not fully analyzed) could introduce noise.  
- Lack of detailed error analysis to identify which non-compositional phrase types or dependency relations cause failures.  
- Suppressed reference for the APT method harms reproducibility and complicates assessment of novelty.  
- Question: provide computational cost comparisons (training time, memory) between APT and neural baselines.  
- Question: assess sensitivity to parser accuracy and quantify parser error impact on results.  
- Question: identify specific subsets or types of non-compositional phrases where the method underperforms.
