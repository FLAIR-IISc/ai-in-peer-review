- Paper shows connection between PoS tags and word embeddings and includes a strong detailed error analysis of outliers.  
- Fails to address why corpora like the BNC were PoS-tagged (for NLP tasks such as parsing); should reframe as whether embedding distinctions help parsing compared to original tags/UPOS.  
- Omits substantial related work in distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012), so results in Section 5 lack novelty.  
- Claim that distributional vectors contain PoS information is not novel given prior clustering/induction work.  
- Statement that embedding relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect; prior work (Mikolov et al., CoVe, etc.) shows substantial syntactic information—contradicts the paper’s own Section 2.  
- Questionable decision to switch to UPOS—fine-grained original tag distinctions may be more interesting.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear: ambiguous whether reported failed attempts were by the authors or others, what the failure criteria were, and no discussion of Brown clusters which align well with UPOS.  
- The claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns is trivialized by function-word distributions (e.g., presence of “the”) and needs more justification.  
- Analysis focuses on frequent word/tag pairs; paper should examine low-frequency tail words and the classifier’s errors there.  
- Suggest exploring alternatives to pure distributional and lemmatization-based morphological features to better generalize PoS for rare words.  
- Minor formatting issue: change sentential citation style to \newcite{}.
