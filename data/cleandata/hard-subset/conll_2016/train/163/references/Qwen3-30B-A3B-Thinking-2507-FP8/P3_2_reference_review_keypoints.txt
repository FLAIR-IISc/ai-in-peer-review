- Paper investigates whether word embeddings encode PoS information and uses classifier error analysis to reveal linguistic boundaries and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Contribution: method for identifying linguistic boundaries via error analysis of distributional PoS classifiers.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized.  
- Contribution: a simple, context-free PoS classifier potentially useful for low-resource languages.  
- Strength: insightful error analysis that exposes annotation inconsistencies (e.g., currency amounts labeled as nouns).  
- Strength: well-supported finding that PoS information is distributed across at least 100 dimensions in 300D embeddings.  
- Strength: robustness across corpora, achieving F-score 0.99 on the Universal Dependencies Treebank.  
- Weakness: limited practical relevance—context-free classifier accuracy (0.84 on UD) lags far behind state-of-the-art contextual taggers.  
- Weakness: methodological issue training on "word + PoS tag" combinations, an artificial setup not reflective of real tagging.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation for why distributional (semantic) models capture syntactic PoS information.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of whether misclassified boundary cases reflect true linguistic phenomena or annotation errors.  
- Question: how does this approach compare to existing methods for detecting PoS annotation errors, and what advantages does it offer?  
- Question: how could this classifier be integrated with state-of-the-art taggers to improve overall performance?  
- Question: can the authors elaborate why stochastic distributional models learn PoS clusters despite PoS being primarily syntactic?
