- Paper goal: test whether distributional word embeddings encode meaningful part-of-speech (PoS) information by training skip-gram vectors on the BNC and using logistic regression to predict PoS, then analyzing misclassifications.

- Main claimed contributions: (1) strong evidence that embeddings contain robust PoS information, (2) using misclassification analysis to identify annotation issues and fuzzy PoS boundaries, (3) showing PoS information is distributed across many vector dimensions.

- Strength: rigorous methodology with multiple experiments (classifier training, error analysis, cross-corpus validation) and high reported performance (F1 0.979 train, 0.91 BNC test, 0.99 on UD).

- Strength: insightful outlier analysis revealing annotation inconsistencies and practical cases (e.g., currency amounts as numerals, sparse proper noun examples).

- Strength: convincing evidence that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (at least ~100 of 300), supported by feature-ablation/accuracy curves.

- Weakness: insufficient benchmarking against state-of-the-art contextual PoS taggers; unclear practical advantage of the context-free approach.

- Weakness: authors overstate lack of contextual knowledge—skip-gram training uses local context, so the model does contain contextual information.

- Weakness: linguistic analysis is superficial; the paper does not deeply explore theoretical implications for PoS categories or how findings challenge linguistic theory.

- Question: how does this approach compare quantitatively and qualitatively to recent contextual neural taggers (e.g., BERT), and what advantages in low-resource settings?

- Question: what theoretical framework do the authors propose for the claim that PoS boundaries are not strict, and how do findings refine linguistic theory?

- Question: do the ~9% misclassifications cluster in specific linguistic phenomena, and can those clusters inform a more nuanced PoS classification?

- Overall assessment: valuable and methodologically sound contribution showing embeddings encode PoS and can flag annotation issues, but it needs stronger SOTA comparisons, clearer claims about contextual information, and deeper linguistic interpretation.
