- Paper studies whether distributional word embeddings reveal meaningful PoS class information, training a PoS classifier on the BNC and analyzing misclassifications.  
- Classifier achieves 97.9% F-score on a 16-class PoS task; a tagger without pre-existing tags reaches 84% accuracy on the UD Treebank.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration that embeddings encode substantial PoS information beyond semantics.  
- Contribution: insightful error analysis showing systematic misclassifications that indicate fluid PoS boundaries.  
- Contribution: a practical, data-driven tool for detecting and correcting PoS-tagging inconsistencies in corpora.  
- Strength: robust empirical results with visualized prototypical PoS vectors and strong replication on UD (F-score 0.99).  
- Strength: error analysis uncovers linguistic patterns and genuine boundary cases (e.g., subordinate conjunctions vs. adverbs).  
- Strength: demonstrates utility for corpus quality control by detecting annotation errors in the BNC.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited since prior work has shown PoS information in embeddings.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to modern state-of-the-art PoS taggers (e.g., neural contextual models).  
- Weakness: outlier/misclassification analysis is insufficiently systematic (no clustering or deeper linguistic feature analysis).  
- Question: How does the distributional PoS predictor compare quantitatively to state-of-the-art supervised taggers on UD?  
- Question: Why does the model fail on certain proper nouns (e.g., 'luftwaffe', 'stonehenge') despite mostly correct proper noun classification?  
- Question: How does the finding that PoS affiliation is distributed across many embedding components relate to literature on embedding interpretability?  
- Overall: valuable empirical and linguistic contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to include SOTA comparisons and more systematic misclassification analysis.
