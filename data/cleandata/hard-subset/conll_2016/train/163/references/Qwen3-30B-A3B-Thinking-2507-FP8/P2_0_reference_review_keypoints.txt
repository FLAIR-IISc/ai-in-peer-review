- Paper tests whether word embeddings encode PoS info by training skip-gram on the BNC and using logistic regression to predict PoS, analyzing misclassifications for fuzzy boundaries and annotation errors.
- Main contributions: empirical proof that PoS info is embedded in vectors; identification of boundary phenomena via error analysis; a method to detect annotation inconsistencies in PoS-tagged corpora.
- Strength: strong empirical evidence—high F-scores (0.979 train, 0.91 unseen) and PoS signals distributed across many vector dimensions.
- Strength: error analysis yields insightful linguistic cases (e.g., ambiguous items like "white-collar", annotation errors like "plain", contextual issues like "£70", proper/common noun boundaries).
- Strength: presents a clear, reproducible methodology (embeddings + classifier + misclassification analysis) useful for corpus linguists and NLP researchers.
- Weakness: limited novelty—the core method builds on prior work and the "redefining" claim overstates the contribution.
- Weakness: not a competitive PoS tagger—unseen accuracy ~0.84 vs. state-of-the-art >0.97, and the paper insufficiently emphasizes its role as an analytical tool rather than a practical tagger.
- Weakness: limited scope—analysis is English-only and largely confirms known linguistic facts without cross-linguistic evidence.
- Question: how will authors justify the "redefining" label given they do not propose new PoS categories but highlight ambiguities?
- Question: how does the classifier outperform simple heuristics (e.g., frequency-based PoS prediction), and what evidence shows added value of the distributional approach?
- Overall: valuable empirical and methodological contribution for analyzing PoS in embeddings and corpus annotation, but novelty is limited and claims should be toned down; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
