- Contribution: Shows word embeddings contain robust PoS information; classifier achieves 0.979 F-score on a balanced test set.  
- Contribution: Analyzes misclassifications to identify genuine linguistic boundary cases (e.g., currency amounts as numerals, "white-collar" as noun).  
- Contribution: Demonstrates PoS information is distributed across many vector dimensions (at least 100 of 300).  

- Strength: Strong empirical evidence that embeddings encode PoS information, validated on BNC and Universal Dependencies.  
- Strength: Insightful error analysis revealing annotation inconsistencies and genuine boundary phenomena useful for corpus linguists.  
- Strength: Important finding that PoS signals are distributed across dimensions, challenging localization assumptions.  

- Weakness: Circular methodology—embeddings trained on PoS-tagged tokens (word_POS) then used to predict PoS, inflating results.  
- Weakness: Title/claims overstate impact; paper identifies boundary cases but does not actually redefine PoS classes.  
- Weakness: Lacks meaningful baselines or comparisons to state-of-the-art taggers and simpler methods.  
- Weakness: Insufficient linguistic justification for treating model labels as correct (e.g., claim about "white-collar" being a noun).  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of practical implications or concrete applications for PoS tagging or linguistic theory.  

- Question: How would results differ if embeddings were trained on untagged data (no PoS concatenation)?  
- Question: Can you provide additional linguistic evidence to justify reclassifying cases like "white-collar" as nouns?  
- Question: How can these findings be integrated into practical PoS tagging systems or linguistic theory beyond error detection?  

- Overall: Valuable contribution showing embeddings reveal PoS boundary phenomena, but methodological circularity and overstated claims need revision.  
- Overall: With experiments on untagged data and stronger linguistic/empirical justification, the paper could make a stronger impact.
