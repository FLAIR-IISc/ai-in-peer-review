- Embeddings contain strong PoS information: classifier reaches 97.9% F-score on training and 84% accuracy without context.
- Misclassification analysis reveals genuine PoS boundary cases (proper vs. common nouns, noun vs. adjective, verb vs. adjective).
- PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (at least 100 of 300), not concentrated in a few features.
- Practical utility: method can detect potential annotation errors in corpora, aiding corpus maintenance.
- Robustness: approach validated on multiple datasets (BNC, UD), showing consistent performance.
- Methodological novelty is limited: using embeddings for PoS classification is not new.
- Claim of "redefining" PoS classes is overstated; no new taxonomy or theoretical framework is proposed.
- Lack of comparison to contextualized SOTA (e.g., BERT-based taggers ~97%+) weakens the significance of the 84% result.
- Insufficient discussion of limitations, especially handling words with multiple PoS in different contexts.
- Question: how would the approach handle polysemous words that take different PoS in context (e.g., "run")?
- Question: how to reconcile the claim of distributed PoS info with a single embedding component (94) apparently specific to conjunctions?
- Question: how should detected annotation errors be resolved and what additional linguistic evidence would be used?
- Question: does stochastic training help capture fluid PoS boundaries, and how does this relate to Manning (2015)?
- Overall: insightful error analysis and practical contribution, but limited novelty and overstated claims; recommend accept with minor revisions.
