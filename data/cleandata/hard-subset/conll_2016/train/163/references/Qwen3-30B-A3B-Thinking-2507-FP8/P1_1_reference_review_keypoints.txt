- Demonstrates that word embeddings contain robust PoS information: logistic regression on Skip-gram vectors predicts Universal PoS tags with high accuracy (F=0.979 train, 0.91 test).

- Uses misclassified words ("outliers") to reveal fuzzy PoS boundaries and possible annotation errors in corpora.

- Shows PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (at least ~100 of 300), not concentrated in a few features.

- Novel application: uses distributional models to analyze PoS boundary fuzziness, offering data-driven linguistic insights.

- Practical utility: method can detect annotation inconsistencies (e.g., adverbs tagged as adjectives) and could improve corpus annotation.

- Insight on embeddings: provides evidence that linguistic information (PoS) is broadly encoded across vector space dimensions.

- Limited novelty: core idea (embeddings containing PoS info) has precedent in prior work; main addition is outlier analysis.

- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art PoS taggers, so it's unclear if the approach is competitive (reported 0.84 on UD without benchmarks).

- Outlier analysis is somewhat superficial and catalog-like, lacking deeper linguistic interpretation or systematic reclassification proposals.

- Question: Provide performance comparisons with modern PoS taggers (accuracy, efficiency, handling ambiguity) on same dataset.

- Question: Reconcile finding of distributed PoS information with prior work claiming interpretable/specific embedding dimensions.

- Question: Explain how the model distinguishes proper names from common nouns in contexts lacking strong cues like "My name is."

- Overall: Valuable demonstration and annotator tool potential, but needs stronger novelty, deeper outlier analysis, and benchmarks against current taggers.
