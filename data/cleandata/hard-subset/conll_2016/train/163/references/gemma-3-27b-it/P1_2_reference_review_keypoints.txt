- Summary: Shows word embeddings encode PoS information, trains skip-gram on BNC, builds a PoS classifier, analyzes misclassifications, and demonstrates distributional-only PoS tagging.

- Contribution: Empirically demonstrates that embeddings capture lexical category (PoS) information via a successful classifier.

- Contribution: Analyzes misclassified tokens to reveal linguistic boundary cases and annotation inconsistencies in the BNC.

- Contribution: Shows feasibility of building a PoS tagger using only distributional information, suggesting utility for low-resource settings.

- Strength: Clear motivation and well-articulated research question about distributional models revealing PoS insights.

- Strength: Robust experimental setup using BNC, skip-gram, baseline comparisons, and frequency-weighted training to address class imbalance.

- Strength: Insightful error analysis that explains model errors and identifies annotation issues.

- Strength: Demonstrated applicability and analysis of component importance in the embedding-based tagger.

- Weakness: Methodological novelty is limited; approach applies established embedding+classification techniques.

- Weakness: Reliance on existing BNC tags creates circularity; lacks fully unsupervised discovery of PoS categories.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art contextual PoS taggers, leaving unclear performance trade-offs.

- Weakness: Visualizations (prototypical vectors) are not sufficiently informative or well-explained.

- Question: How would this approach be adapted practically to low-resource languages without PoS-tagged data?

- Question: Have you quantified the extent of annotation inconsistencies found across the entire BNC?

- Question: Were hyperparameters (vector size, negatives, window) subject to ablation to assess sensitivity?

- Question: Is the 100-dimensional threshold for important components hard or gradual, and can you show detailed F-value rankings?

- Question: Were there notable differences when using the original BNC tagset versus the Universal Dependencies tagset?

- Additional: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and effectively anonymized.

- Additional: References are comprehensive and related work discussion is thorough.

- Recommendation: Solid, insightful paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
