- Paper shows word embeddings encode significant PoS information, enabling PoS prediction and outlier detection.
- Authors train skip-gram embeddings on the BNC, build a PoS classifier, analyze misclassifications, and create a context-free PoS tagger.
- Contribution: Empirically demonstrates distributional models capture traditional PoS categories.
- Contribution: Analysis of misclassifications reveals annotation inconsistencies and fuzzy PoS boundaries (e.g., adverb/adjective, participles).
- Contribution: Demonstrates feasibility of a context-free PoS tagger based solely on distributional information.
- Strength: Clear hypothesis, logical methodology, and use of standard corpora (BNC, UD) for evaluation.
- Strength: Strong empirical results (97.9% train accuracy, ~91% on unseen data) across datasets.
- Strength: Insightful error analysis offering linguistic interpretation of misclassifications and annotation issues.
- Strength: Novel exploration of context-free tagging with potential low-resource applications or pipeline use.
- Strength: Investigation of which embedding components contribute to PoS prediction.
- Weakness: Methodology (train embeddings + classifier) is incremental and not fundamentally novel.
- Weakness: Experiments limited to English; unclear generalization to typologically different languages.
- Weakness: Classifier lags behind contextualized models (e.g., BERT); integration or complementarity not fully explored.
- Weakness: Weighting training instances by corpus frequency seems ad hoc and lacks rigorous justification.
- Weakness: Visualizations are unimpressive; more informative plots (t-SNE/PCA colored by PoS) would help.
- Question: Will results generalize to languages with free word order or richer morphology?
- Question: How could this approach integrate with contextualized models (BERT) for augmentation, initialization, or error correction?
- Question: Were alternative weighting schemes tested besides corpus frequency, and what were the outcomes?
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding hyperparameters (vector size, window, negative sampling)?
- Question: Can authors provide more qualitative misclassification examples with linguistic explanations?
- Additional: Writing is generally good with minor awkward phrasing; references are comprehensive and organization is clear.
- Additional: Should state computational resources used for training models.
