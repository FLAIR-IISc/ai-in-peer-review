- Paper shows word embeddings encode PoS information and can predict PoS with high accuracy, including a tag-free training variant.
- Contribution: Demonstrates embeddings inherently capture traditional PoS categories, enabling accurate PoS prediction.
- Contribution: Analyzes misclassifications to identify linguistic outliers and potential annotation inconsistencies.
- Contribution: Shows feasibility of training a PoS classifier without initial PoS tags, useful for low-resource settings.
- Strength: Strong empirical results (e.g., 97.9% weighted F-score train, 91% test, 99% on UD) with cross-validation and multi-dataset evaluation.
- Strength: Insightful error analysis highlighting systematic misclassifications (e.g., proper vs. common nouns, verbs vs. adjectives).
- Strength: Clear, logical organization and accessible writing.
- Strength: Reproducibility aided by releasing BNC-to-Universal tag mappings.
- Strength: Tag-free experiment opens applications for unsupervised or low-resource PoS induction.
- Weakness: Methodology (train embeddings → train classifier → analyze errors) is relatively standard; novelty is primarily in application and analysis.
- Weakness: Limited, somewhat anecdotal exploration of which embedding dimensions encode PoS information.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on BNC and UD annotations as ground truth without deep critique of their biases.
- Weakness: Lacks direct comparison of the tag-free classifier to state-of-the-art supervised PoS taggers for contextual benchmarking.
- Question: Can the authors elaborate on attempts to identify specific embedding dimensions correlated with PoS classes and the techniques used?
- Question: Can the authors provide concrete examples of problematic BNC annotation choices uncovered and suggestions to address them?
- Question: What specific low-resource scenarios and minimal annotation amounts do the authors envision for the tag-free approach?
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding hyperparameters (vector size, window, negative sampling) and were alternatives tested?
- Additional: References are relevant and figures effectively support the results.
- Additional: Paper should briefly discuss limits of distributional semantics for PoS (e.g., syntactic/morphological aspects).
- Overall: Valuable, well-written paper with strong analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
