- Summary: Paper shows word embeddings encode PoS information, enabling PoS prediction and identification of distributional outliers via logistic regression on BNC and UD data.  
- Contribution 1: Empirically validates that distributional semantic models implicitly encode PoS classes beyond mere syntactic context reflection.  
- Contribution 2: Proposes a novel error-analysis methodology that uses misclassified words to detect annotation inconsistencies and ambiguous cases in corpora.  
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates zero-shot PoS prediction capability using distributional vectors with reasonable accuracy.  
- Strength 1: Strong empirical support with thorough experiments on BNC and UD, achieving high accuracy scores.  
- Strength 2: Insightful, interpretable error analysis uncovering patterns like proper noun issues and nominal-adjectival ambiguities.  
- Strength 3: Methodological rigor including frequency weighting, 10-fold cross-validation, and baseline comparisons.  
- Strength 4: Clear, logical presentation that makes the experimental approach easy to follow.  
- Strength 5: Relevant contribution linking distributional semantics and traditional linguistics, informing PoS debates.  
- Weakness 1: Core methodology (embeddings + classifier) is well-established, limiting novelty of techniques.  
- Weakness 2: Superficial analysis of which embedding dimensions drive PoS distinctions; deeper dimension-level study is lacking.  
- Weakness 3: Overreliance on UD as a gold standard risks conflating annotation differences with errors.  
- Weakness 4: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art PoS taggers and discussion of trade-offs (zero-shot vs. context-aware).  
- Question 1: What proportion of flagged BNC items are true annotation errors after manual verification versus annotation-convention differences?  
- Question 2: How would the method adapt to languages with rich morphology or freer word order?  
- Question 3: Can you provide more analyses (visualizations/correlations) of embedding dimensions informative for PoS distinctions beyond the conjunction example?  
- Question 4: Were other embedding models (CBOW, FastText) tried and did they change results?  
- Additional: Figures and references are helpful and formatting is good.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, insightful paper with minor-addressable weaknesses — recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
