- Paper tests whether word embeddings encode part-of-speech (PoS) information by training a classifier on BNC-derived embeddings.  
- Authors analyze misclassifications to identify ambiguous or inconsistent PoS cases and propose embedding-driven PoS refinements.  
- Demonstrates a context-free PoS tagger trained solely on word embeddings, suggesting usefulness in low-resource or compute-limited settings.  

- Empirically shows embeddings contain strong signals for PoS, achieving high accuracy from embeddings alone.  
- Uses error analysis to reveal linguistic ambiguities (e.g., noun–adjective boundary) and BNC tagging inconsistencies.  
- Presents feasibility of building PoS taggers without contextual features, offering practical advantages.  

- Experimental setup is rigorous: large corpus (BNC), weighted training, cross-validation, and sensible baselines.  
- Error analysis is thorough and linguistically insightful, with clear illustrative examples.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful visualizations of vector representations.  
- Novel application: using distributional models to refine PoS definitions and create a context-free tagger.  
- Work is relevant to both theoretical linguistics and practical NLP.

- Embedding method (Continuous Skipgram, standard params) is not methodologically novel; newer models (e.g., BERT) could offer more insight.  
- Heavy dependence on BNC annotations risks circularity since analysis assumes BNC tags are mostly accurate.  
- No comparison to state-of-the-art contextual PoS taggers, leaving trade-offs unclear.  
- Analysis of informative embedding components is superficial; deeper semantic interpretation is needed.

- Have you tried contextualized embeddings (BERT/transformers) and would they change performance or error patterns?  
- Have findings been validated on other tagged corpora beyond UD to test generalizability of annotation inconsistencies?  
- Can you more thoroughly interpret the most informative embedding components and the features they capture?  
- How sensitive is the context-free tagger to vocabulary size and the selection of ‘unambiguous’ lemmas?

- Insights about BNC tagging limitations for currency amounts and years are valuable.  
- Conclusion summarizes findings well and outlines sensible future work.  
- Anonymization of the submission is correctly handled.

- Overall: well-executed, insightful, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
