- Summary: Authors show word embeddings capture PoS information by training skip-gram on BNC, building a PoS classifier from embeddings, and analyzing misclassifications to refine PoS boundaries and build a distributional-only tagger.

- Contribution 1: Empirical evidence that unsupervised word embeddings encode significant PoS information, demonstrated by high classifier accuracy.

- Contribution 2: Error analysis reveals linguistic insights and annotation ambiguities (e.g., participles vs. adjectives, currency as numerals).

- Contribution 3: Shows feasibility of a PoS tagger based solely on distributional information, suggesting utility in low-resource settings.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results (high F-scores on training, test, and UD Treebank) supporting the hypothesis.

- Strength 2: Thorough, interpretable error analysis that connects mistakes to linguistic/annotation issues.

- Strength 3: Clear methodology with reproducibility details and released mappings between BNC and Universal tags.

- Strength 4: Novel application of distributional models to refine PoS boundaries and identify annotation inconsistencies.

- Strength 5: Demonstrated robustness via successful transfer to UD Treebank.

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty—technique (embeddings + classifier) is established; novelty is mainly in application.

- Weakness 2: Reliance on gold-standard tags—paper doesn’t solve bootstrapping in a truly unsupervised setting.

- Weakness 3: Superficial analysis of which vector components encode PoS; findings (e.g., component 94) are isolated rather than systematic.

- Weakness 4: No comparison of the distributional-only tagger with state-of-the-art supervised PoS taggers to contextualize performance.

- Question 1: How could one bootstrap a distributional-only PoS tagger in a fully unsupervised setting?

- Question 2: Can you provide a more systematic analysis/visualization of embedding components correlated with PoS tags?

- Question 3: How sensitive are results to embedding hyperparameters (vector size, window, negative sampling), and were alternatives explored?

- Question 4: Are the BNC annotation inconsistencies observed also present in other corpora like the Penn Treebank?

- Additional comment 1: Writing is clear and well-organized.

- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive.

- Additional comment 3: Figures and tables are helpful.

- Overall assessment: Recommend acceptance—strong empirical results and insightful analysis outweigh minor weaknesses.
