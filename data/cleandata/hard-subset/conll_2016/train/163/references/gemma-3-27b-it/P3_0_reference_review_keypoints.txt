- Authors show word embeddings encode PoS information and train a classifier on BNC (F-scores >0.9), with tests on UD Treebank supporting the claim.  
- Misclassification analysis reveals PoS boundary ambiguities and potential annotation inconsistencies in the BNC.  
- The paper demonstrates feasibility of a context-free PoS tagger based solely on embeddings, useful for low-resource settings.  
- Strong empirical validation: high accuracy across datasets and use of instance weighting to handle class imbalance.  
- Error analysis is linguistically insightful (e.g., numerals vs. nouns, passive participles) rather than mere error reporting.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with sufficient methodological detail for reproducibility.  
- Authors released BNC-to-Universal tag mapping, aiding replication.  
- Novel perspective in exploring context-free distributional approaches to PoS tagging, even if not state-of-the-art.  
- Limited methodological novelty: using embeddings for PoS tagging is a fairly standard approach; novelty is mainly analytic.  
- Evaluation depends on existing PoS annotations; the approach flags inconsistencies but cannot independently resolve them without an external gold standard.  
- Only one set of embedding hyperparameters explored; sensitivity to parameter choices is not analyzed.  
- No direct performance comparison with standard/state-of-the-art PoS taggers, leaving practical utility unclear.  
- “Outlier” definition is ambiguous—misclassification may not always indicate linguistically meaningful outliers.  
- Question: propose a systematic procedure for using the model to improve existing annotations (auto-retagging vs. flagging for manual review?).  
- Question: how sensitive are results to lemma quality and potential lemmatization errors?  
- Question: request deeper exploration/visualization of the most informative embedding components and their semantic content.  
- Question: whether incorporating morphological features (prefixes/suffixes) was considered to improve the context-free tagger.  
- Question: request a full similarity matrix of average POS embeddings to visualize inter-PoS relationships.  
- Comment: figures of prototypical vectors need clearer axis labeling.  
- Comment: add discussion of limitations of the distributional hypothesis for PoS tagging (e.g., synonyms with different PoS).  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed study offering valuable empirical and analytic contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
