- Paper shows word embeddings inherently encode substantial part-of-speech (PoS) information via classifiers trained on BNC embeddings.  
- Authors analyze misclassifications to identify boundary cases between PoS categories and potential BNC annotation errors.  
- Demonstrates a proof-of-concept that embeddings trained without PoS labels can still predict PoS reasonably well, useful for low-resource settings.  
- Empirical evidence strengthens the link between distributional semantics and traditional linguistic PoS categories.  
- Error analysis highlights linguistically meaningful misclassifications (e.g., proper nouns vs. common nouns, currency amounts as numerals).  
- Classifier trained on BNC embeddings generalizes well to UD Treebank, indicating robustness.  
- Experimental setup is rigorous: BNC corpus, Skip-gram embeddings, logistic regression, 10-fold CV, and frequency weighting to address imbalance.  
- Combines quantitative metrics (accuracy, F-scores) with qualitative linguistic interpretation for well-rounded evaluation.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with logical section flow and motivation.  
- Methodological novelty is limited—the contribution is mainly in application and analysis rather than new techniques.  
- Results are currently limited to English; cross-linguistic generalization is not explored.  
- Use of a simple classifier (logistic regression) leaves potential gains from more complex models unexplored.  
- Embedding hyperparameters (e.g., vector size) are fixed; the impact of parameter choices on PoS discrimination is not analyzed.  
- Heavy reliance on the BNC restricts generalizability; additional corpora for training/evaluation would help.  
- Request for details on OOV rates in UD evaluation and how missing vocabulary affects the reported 99% F-score.  
- Suggestion to quantify annotation inconsistencies in BNC (e.g., percent of frequently misassigned tags).  
- Ask for more systematic analysis of embedding dimensions/features relevant to PoS beyond isolated examples.  
- Curious about cross-corpus tag-free performance (e.g., training on BNC, testing on UD Treebank).  
- Figures need higher resolution and larger size for clarity; references formatting is inconsistent.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with valuable insights into embeddings and PoS; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
