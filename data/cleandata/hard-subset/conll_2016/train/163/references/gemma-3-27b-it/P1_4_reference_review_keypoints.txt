- Hypothesis and method: paper tests whether unsupervised word embeddings encode PoS information by training a classifier on embeddings and analyzing misclassifications.
- Misclassification analysis: examining errors reveals words on PoS boundaries and potential BNC annotation inconsistencies.
- Proof-of-concept tagger: demonstrates a reasonably accurate PoS tagger built solely from embeddings without contextual features.
- Contribution—PoS in embeddings: provides empirical evidence that embeddings encode substantial PoS information.
- Contribution—boundary cases & annotations: identifies fuzzy PoS boundaries and highlights corpus annotation issues useful for linguistics/NLP.
- Contribution—resource-poor potential: suggests embedding-only tagging could help low-resource languages or serve as preprocessing.
- Strength—evaluation: uses large BNC corpus, cross-validation, unseen-data testing, and comparison to a standard tagger with clear metrics.
- Strength—error analysis: in-depth error analysis gives linguistic insights and flags annotation errors.
- Strength—clarity: paper is well-organized and easy to follow.
- Strength—relevance: addresses an active intersection of distributional semantics and syntax.
- Strength—reproducibility: authors release BNC-to-Universal tag mapping to aid replication.
- Weakness—method novelty: methodology (train embeddings + classifier) is standard and not methodologically novel.
- Weakness—performance gap: 84% accuracy is lower than state-of-the-art contextual taggers and the paper lacks comprehensive comparison or improvement avenues.
- Weakness—English focus: experiments are limited to English, so generalizability to other languages is unclear.
- Weakness—UD reliance: using Universal Dependencies adds potential annotation mismatch issues between BNC and UD.
- Question—instance weighting: request for details and justification of the class-imbalance weighting scheme used during training.
- Question—quantifying annotation errors: ask whether authors can quantify BNC annotation inconsistencies and their effect on results.
- Question—distributed PoS info: ask for hypotheses why PoS information is spread across many embedding dimensions.
- Question—other languages: ask which languages will be studied next and expected typological differences in results.
- Question—computational resources: request details on hardware and training time for embeddings and classifier.
- Comment—figures clarity: prototypical vector figures need clearer axis labels or explanations.
- Comment—tagset mapping: mapping Penn Treebank tags to Universal Tagset and its description should be expanded.
- Recommendation: overall accept with minor revisions addressing listed weaknesses.
