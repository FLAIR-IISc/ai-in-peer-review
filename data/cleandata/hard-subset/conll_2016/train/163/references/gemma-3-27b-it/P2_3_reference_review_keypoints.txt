- Embeddings encode substantial PoS information, enabling accurate PoS prediction from distributional semantics.  
- Misclassifications reveal linguistic boundary cases and possible annotation inconsistencies in PoS schemes.  
- Zero-shot PoS prediction using word embeddings (no context) is surprisingly effective.  
- Strong empirical evaluation using BNC and cross-validation, plus testing on UD Treebank.  
- Detailed error analysis provides linguistic insights beyond performance metrics.  
- Clear, logical presentation and well-explained methodology.  
- Novel use of distributional models to probe the nature of PoS classes rather than just tagging.  
- Results generalize well to Universal Dependencies, showing robustness of representations.  
- Methods are standard (Skipgram, logistic regression), so technical novelty is limited.  
- Analysis of which vector components matter for PoS is shallow and inconclusive.  
- Relying on UD as a gold standard may bias validation and overlooks annotation scheme differences.  
- No direct comparison to state-of-the-art context-aware PoS taggers (e.g., BERT), limiting contextualization of results.  
- Request for clarification on BNC vs UD annotation differences contributing to observed discrepancies.  
- Suggestion to use feature selection or dimensionality reduction to better identify informative vector components.  
- Ask about sensitivity of results to Skipgram hyperparameters and whether different settings were tested.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent citation formatting and unclear figures (1 & 2).  
- Overall assessment: solid, insightful contribution with thorough evaluation and error analysis; recommend acceptance.
