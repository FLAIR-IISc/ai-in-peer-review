- Summary: Trains a classifier on BNC-derived word embeddings to show embeddings encode PoS information, analyzes misclassifications, and demonstrates reasonable zero-shot PoS prediction.

- Contribution: Empirical validation that distributional embeddings capture traditional PoS distinctions without PoS supervision.

- Contribution: Analysis of misclassified words highlights linguistic outliers and potential annotation inconsistencies.

- Contribution: Shows feasibility of zero-shot PoS prediction, useful for low-resource settings.

- Strength: Solid experimental setup using BNC, Skip-gram, robust evaluation, and comparisons to standard taggers and UD Treebank.

- Strength: Insightful, detailed error analysis revealing linguistic phenomena (currency amounts, participles, noun–adjective overlap).

- Strength: Clear, logical presentation with well-explained methods and results.

- Strength: Novel exploration into how PoS information is distributed in embedding components, suggesting directions for future work.

- Strength: Practical implications for corpus linguistics, tagset refinement, and cross-lingual PoS tagging.

- Weakness: Methodological novelty limited—similar ideas exist in prior work; main value is in analysis rather than a new method.

- Weakness: Results depend heavily on the quality and consistency of the BNC tagset; impact of other tagsets not explored.

- Weakness: Zero-shot accuracy (~84%) is notably lower than supervised taggers; limitations and causes need deeper discussion.

- Weakness: Analysis of embedding components is superficial; more in-depth linguistic interpretation of components is needed.

- Question: How do limitations of the BNC tagset influence the results?

- Question: What techniques (e.g., subword info / fastText) could improve zero-shot PoS accuracy?

- Question: Have identified outliers been compared to errors from other PoS taggers to confirm annotation issues?

- Question: Why is PoS information distributed across many embedding components—distributional hypothesis or training artifact?

- Additional: Discuss relevance of contextualized embeddings (BERT/ELMo) in related work even if not used.

- Additional: Figures need labeled axes for clarity.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with valuable analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
