- Paper investigates whether distributional word embeddings encode PoS and can detect category-boundary outliers and annotation errors via skip-gram models on BNC and a lemma-only PoS predictor on UD.
- Contribution: empirical error analysis showing embeddings surface annotation inconsistencies and boundary phenomena (e.g., participles as ADJ-like, years as NOUN-like, numerals as NUM-like).
- Contribution: lemma-only, context-free PoS predictor achieving ~0.84 token accuracy on UD, suggesting strong PoS signal in embeddings without context.
- Contribution: exploratory analysis indicating PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (ANOVA ranking, learning curves).
- Strength: clear, data-driven qualitative insights highlighting linguistically plausible gray areas and corpus-specific inconsistencies, cross-checked with UD.
- Strength: simple methodology yields strong signals even with small window and no order information.
- Strength: practical proposal to use classifier disagreement to triage corpus annotations for human review.
- Strength: attempt to quantify feature distribution (ANOVA, top-k dimension analysis) to argue against single-dimension explanations.
- Weakness: circularity—using lemma+PoS tokens to build embeddings and predict PoS at inference relies on gold PoS, making the reported 0.99 F1 misleading.
- Weakness: unfair baseline—Stanford tagger was run on isolated tokens without context, making the comparison invalid.
- Weakness: missing critical baselines for the lemma-only predictor (lemma→majority-PoS lexicon, suffix/morphology heuristics, count-based distributional features) and missing per-tag/coverage/OOV stats.
- Weakness: unreliable component-level interpretations due to rotational invariance of embeddings; single-dimension claims risk overinterpretation.
- Weakness: reporting and reproducibility gaps—missing/poorly described tables, corrupted references, unclear UD/BNC mapping, random seeds, subword handling, and odd frequency-weighting choices.
- Question: provide a lemma→majority-PoS baseline from UD training and report token/per-tag performance for ambiguous vs unambiguous lemmas.
- Question: reframe or rerun the lemma+PoS experiment without using PoS in embedding keys (or train lemma-only embeddings) to avoid artifactually high quantitative claims.
- Question: provide a fair context-free baseline using PPMI vectors and/or Brown clusters to show embeddings specifically drive effects.
- Question: report stability of component-level findings across random seeds/hyperparameters and whether top-k features align after alignment (e.g., Procrustes).
- Question: include a small human validation of flagged annotation errors to quantify precision of the error-detection signal.
- Question: test larger windows, position-sensitive contexts, and subword models to see if error profiles or lemma-only performance change.
- Recommendation: remove misleading quantitative claims from lemma+PoS experiment, add strong fair baselines, report per-tag metrics/coverage, and validate error-detection with a manual study.
