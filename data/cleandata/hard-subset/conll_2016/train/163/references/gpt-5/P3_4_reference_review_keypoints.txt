- Paper studies whether distributional embeddings encode PoS info and can detect boundary/outlier words and annotation inconsistencies via skip-gram on lemma_POS, logistic regression, misclassification analysis, and a prototype predictor on untagged embeddings (0.84 on UD).

- Contribution: empirical evidence that type-level embeddings carry strong PoS signal and that misclassifications surface genuine boundary phenomena and corpus inconsistencies.

- Contribution: a contextless, prototype-based PoS predictor trained on untagged embeddings with potential for low-resource bootstrapping and corpus QA.

- Contribution: exploratory probing of embedding dimensions correlated with PoS and analysis of prototypical PoS centroid similarities.

- Strength: clear, data-driven error analysis highlighting linguistically meaningful boundary cases (nouns/adjectives, participles, numerals, time expressions) and concrete corpus QA discoveries.

- Strength: cross-corpus testing (BNC↔UD) increases credibility and shows generalization; high type-level F-scores for seen vocabulary.

- Strength: simple, reproducible methodology isolating the signal using multinomial logistic regression on static embeddings.

- Strength: practical utility for triaging annotation errors and informing tagset/corpus maintenance.

- Strength: quantitative feature probing (ANOVA F-scores) indicating PoS information is distributed across many embedding components.

- Weakness: circularity risk from training embeddings on lemma_POS strings—tagged contexts may directly encode PoS regularities, weakening semantic-discovery claims.

- Weakness: potential train/test leakage in 10-fold CV if splits were over instances rather than disjoint types, inflating reported performance.

- Weakness: missing baseline comparisons (lemma-majority PoS, nearest-centroid, Brown clusters, bag-of-contexts) to contextualize results.

- Weakness: Stanford tagger comparison is an unfair/inapt baseline since it’s constrained to tokenless inputs in evaluation.

- Weakness: component-level interpretability claims are fragile due to rotation non-identifiability and lack of stability checks across seeds/rotations or multivariate diagnostics.

- Weakness: no quantitative human validation of flagged annotation errors (precision/recall), so unclear how many flags are true issues vs. quirks/sparsity.

- Weakness: limited engagement with prior PoS induction literature and absence of quantitative clustering/induction baselines.

- Q: Was the 10k-type classifier cross-validation performed with disjoint word types or over repeated instances allowing same types in train/test?

- Q: Can the outlier analysis be repeated with embeddings trained on untagged lemmas to rule out label-induced structure, and do the same patterns emerge?

- Q: For the UD prototype predictor, how does performance compare to (a) lemma-majority PoS from UD training and (b) nearest-centroid classification—please report accuracy and per-class scores?

- Q: How stable are identified PoS-correlated dimensions across random seeds or after orthogonal rotations; do findings replicate over multiple runs?

- Q: Can you provide blinded human validation counts (true vs false positives) for the claimed BNC annotation errors (e.g., adverb vs adjective)?

- Q: Why choose window size = 2 and no subsampling; were hyperparameters (window, subsampling, negatives) explored for their effect on PoS separability?

- Q: Please provide per-class precision/recall for the 17k held-out type evaluation and the UD token-level test to show which classes drive performance.

- Additional: strengthen related-work coverage on PoS induction and include fair quantitative baselines (e.g., Brown clusters).

- Additional: report standard errors over multiple runs to assess robustness.

- Additional: a more systematic validation of embedding-based outlier detection would make the corpus-QA claim much stronger.
