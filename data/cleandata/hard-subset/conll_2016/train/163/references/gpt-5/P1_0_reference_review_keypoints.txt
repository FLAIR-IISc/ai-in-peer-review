- Paper studies how static distributional embeddings encode PoS and uses them to probe PoS boundaries and build a context-free PoS predictor.
- Method: train Skip-gram on BNC with lemma_POS tokens, fit multinomial logistic regression to predict PoS, analyze misclassifications.
- Second experiment: train lemma-only embeddings and a small “prototypical” lexicon-based type-level classifier achieving ~0.84 token accuracy on UD without context.
- Claim: static embeddings robustly encode PoS and misclassification patterns reveal genuine boundary phenomena and annotation inconsistencies.
- Claim: a simple context-free PoS predictor trained from a small lexicon attains competitive accuracy and could help bootstrapping/auditing in low-resource settings.
- Claim: PoS signal is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated in a few.
- Strength: Clear, data-driven error analysis that uncovers linguistically meaningful phenomena and probable BNC tagging errors.
- Strength: Practical utility as an auditing tool to flag systematic annotation inconsistencies.
- Strength: Simple, transparent, and reproducible methodology (logistic regression over pre-trained embeddings; ANOVA for feature importance).
- Strength: Cross-resource check (applied to UD) and discussion of annotation guideline differences is informative.
- Weakness: Methodological leakage/circularity from training on lemma_POS tokens — PoS is overtly encoded in contexts, weakening claims about learning PoS from semantics.
- Weakness: 10-fold CV likely inflated because many instances duplicate only ~10k unique vectors, risking same vector appearing in train and test; type-level splits needed.
- Weakness: UD evaluation with F=0.99 is unclear/potentially artifactual — need clarification on which vectors represent UD lemmas at test time.
- Weakness: Baselines are limited or unfair (e.g., degrading a contextual tagger to use isolated tokens); missing comparisons like type-level majority, lemma-only probes, or suffix-based baselines.
- Weakness: Scope limited to English and a single embedding configuration; no robustness checks across windows, models, languages, or seeds.
- Weakness: Presentation and reproducibility issues — missing/garbled references, omitted figures/tables, anonymized links, and missing implementation details (OOV handling, priors, regularization).
- Question: How exactly was the UD F=0.99 evaluation performed and which vector represented UD lemmas when multiple lemma_POS variants exist?
- Question: For the 10-fold CV, was it ensured that the same word type never appears in both train and test folds; can you report type-level CV?
- Question: Can you replicate the outlier analysis with lemma-only embeddings and a linear probe to rule out reliance on explicit tag signals in contexts?
- Question: How sensitive are results (e.g., key discriminative dimensions) to window size, subsampling, negative sampling, model variant, and random seeds?
- Question: For the lemma-only predictor, how does accuracy change with the size and dominance threshold of the prototypical lexicon?
- Suggestion: Release code, mappings, and outlier lists; clarify evaluation splits, OOV rates, and regularization for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Reframe main claim more modestly as an auditing/analysis tool rather than a general tagging method.
- Suggestion: Fix references, include legible figures/tables, and supply missing experimental details.
