- Proposes using a simple classifier over word embeddings to identify PoS-boundary words and surface annotation inconsistencies.  
- Trains skip-gram embeddings on BNC with lemma+UD-PoS types, then analyzes logistic-regression misclassifications as boundary cases and corpus errors.  
- Trains lemma-only embeddings and a context-free PoS predictor from unambiguous seeds, achieving 0.84 accuracy on UD test tokens.  
- Shows compelling qualitative error analyses that reveal linguistically meaningful boundary phenomena and systematic annotation issues (e.g., adjectival nouns, participles, adverb mislabels).  
- Uses a simple, transparent multinomial logistic regression over type-level embeddings that attains high F on held-out BNC types and near-perfect F for in-vocabulary UD lemmas.  
- Demonstrates cross-corpus portability: classifier trained on BNC performs well on UD lemmas present in the model.  
- Provides empirical evidence that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions, supported by ANOVA feature ranking and ablation curves.  
- Offers a potentially useful diagnostic pipeline (BNC-to-UD mapping and workflow) for corpus builders and tagset designers.  
- Main experiment leaks supervision by training on lemma_POS tokens, possibly inflating PoS signal and making findings partly artifacts of label injection.  
- Core outlier/annotation-error analysis is not replicated with truly unsupervised (lemma-only) embeddings, weakening claims about unsupervised embeddings.  
- Lacks baselines and ablations (lemma-majority baseline, Brown clusters, raw co-occurrence/PCA, dependency-based embeddings, morphological features) to contextualize the 0.84 result.  
- Compares to a standard tagger in an unfair regime (isolated tokens without context), producing a misleading benchmark.  
- Scope limited to English/BNC; no replication on larger or typologically different languages to support generalization.  
- Reproducibility concerns: insufficient preprocessing details (casing, digits, multiword tokens, punctuation) and unclear availability of mappings/code/models.  
- Asks authors to replicate outlier analysis with untagged-lemma embeddings to quantify persistence of highlighted cases.  
- Requests the lemma-majority baseline accuracy on UD (and on the unambiguous subset) and comparisons to Brown clusters or co-occurrence classifiers.  
- Requests clarification whether context tokens in the first experiment were lemma+PoS and how PoS-labeled neighbors' contribution is separated from lexical cues.  
- Asks about sensitivity to hyperparameters and context types (window size, negative sampling, subsampling, dependency-based contexts).  
- Requests release of BNC-to-UD mapping, code, and trained models for replication.  
- Overall: useful diagnostic perspective with strong linguistic analyses, but conclusions are weakened by supervision leakage, missing baselines, and limited generalization; addressing these would improve rigor and impact.
