- Paper investigates whether distributional word embeddings encode PoS information and can surface PoS-boundary cases and annotation errors.
- Method: train Skip-gram on BNC (lemma+PoS and lemma-only), fit multinomial logistic regression to predict PoS, analyze misclassifications.
- Finding: many classifier “errors” correspond to genuine PoS boundary phenomena and apparent BNC tagging mistakes.
- Second experiment: lemma-only embeddings + classifier on prototypical UD lemmas achieves 0.84 token accuracy without context.
- PoS signal is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that off-the-shelf embeddings encode robust PoS signals and can help find boundary cases and corpus errors.
- Contribution: a simple, context-free PoS predictor trained on unambiguous lemmas demonstrates strong PoS signal generality.
- Contribution: preliminary analysis of which embedding dimensions contribute to PoS, suggesting a distributed signal.
- Strength: insightful error analysis that surfaces linguistically meaningful grey areas and systematic tagging errors.
- Strength: simple, reproducible pipeline (Skip-gram + logistic regression) with clear intuition.
- Strength: classifier trained on BNC generalizes to UD vocabulary, showing cross-corpus robustness.
- Strength: quantitative and qualitative results align with linguistic expectations (e.g., PoS vector similarities).
- Strength: practical potential for corpus QA and bootstrapping PoS resources in low-resource settings.
- Weakness: methodological leakage—first experiment uses lemma+PoS tokens, possibly making PoS separability artificially easy.
- Weakness: cross-validation protocol likely inflates CV score by repeating identical word vectors across folds; type-level split needed.
- Weakness: unfair comparison to a supervised sequence tagger (Stanford tagger) when evaluated on single tokens.
- Weakness: limited scope—only English and few ablations; lacks analysis of hyperparameters and alternative representations.
- Weakness: shallow dimension-importance analysis without deeper probing or regularization studies.
- Weakness: clarity and reproducibility issues—missing figure/table details, incomplete references, and missing experimental specifics.
- Question: can the first experiment be re-run with embeddings trained on plain lemmas to address leakage?
- Question: how were cross-validation folds constructed; were identical word types kept in the same fold?
- Question: for the lemma-only predictor, what is class distribution and how does it compare to a majority/lexicon baseline?
- Question: how sensitive are results to window size and subsampling?
- Question: can the authors quantify the BNC “error” findings and provide a verifiable list?
- Additional comment: include stronger baselines (bag-of-context, char n-grams, clustering) to isolate embedding benefits.
- Additional comment: report per-class performance, confusion matrices, ambiguous vs unambiguous lemma results, and OOV handling.
- Additional comment: probe distributed PoS signal with modern probing methods (lasso probes, CKA/CCA) and control for frequency.
- Additional comment: clean up references/figures and release code, models, and flagged annotation issues.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept—interesting and useful insight but requires methodological fixes and clearer presentation.
