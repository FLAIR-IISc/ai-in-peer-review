Summary: Paper studies how static Skip-gram embeddings encode coarse PoS, using lemma+PoS embeddings and a multinomial logistic classifier to surface PoS boundary cases and annotation errors; also trains on raw lemmas to build a context-free PoS predictor (0.84 acc) and finds PoS signal distributed across many embedding dimensions.

Contribution 1: Empirical and qualitative evidence that static embeddings encode robust coarse PoS signals and that misclassifications reveal PoS boundary phenomena and corpus annotation issues.

Contribution 2: A simple context-free PoS predictor trained on raw lemmas and a probing-style analysis showing PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions.

Contribution 3: A practical, lightweight methodology for corpus auditing/outlier discovery using distributional vectors and a linear classifier.

Strength 1: Provides clear, interpretable qualitative examples (e.g., participles, currency/percent, years/decades, SCONJ vs ADV) that align with linguistic intuition and tagging pain points.

Strength 2: Simple, reproducible methodology with strong linear-classifier performance and convincing analyses (prototypical PoS vectors, feature-importance curves) showing robust distributed signal.

Strength 3: Cross-corpus probing on UD strengthens generality and grounds discussions about tokenization/tagging differences.

Strength 4: Practical value for pre-annotation or error mining, especially in low-resource settings using small seed lists.

Weakness 1: Circularity/leakage: training embeddings on lemma+PoS bakes label information into contexts, potentially biasing classification and error analyses.

Weakness 2: Unfair baseline: comparing to a sentence-less Stanford tagger is not a fair baseline; better baselines (lexicon prior, tagger with context) are needed.

Weakness 3: Limited novelty relative to prior probing/embedding literature; paper mainly adds an error-mining workflow and case studies without strong positioning.

Weakness 4: Incomplete/inconsistent evaluation: unclear UD F1 reporting, missing details on vocabulary matching, and no quantitative validation of surfaced annotation errors.

Weakness 5: Presentation/reproducibility issues: messy references, under-described figures/tables, and unclear resource release.

Question 1: How were UD lemmas mapped to embedding vectors—by constructing lemma+gold-tag tokens or using plain lemmas?

Question 2: What fraction of surfaced outliers are true annotation errors vs acceptable ambiguity; can you provide a double-annotated adjudication for precision?

Question 3: Can you replicate the outlier analysis with embeddings trained on raw lemmas to quantify tag leakage effects?

Question 4: How does the context-free classifier compare to (i) frequency-based lemma priors, (ii) simple shape/capitalization models, and (iii) fastText/subword embeddings; and how sensitive are results to window size and negative sampling?

Question 5: Will you release the BNC-to-UD tag mapping, outlier lists with predictions/confidences, and code to reproduce analyses?

Overall assessment: Interesting and useful qualitative insights but weakened by methodological circularity, limited novelty versus existing probing work, and evaluation gaps; replication on tag-free embeddings and a small adjudication would substantially strengthen the paper.
