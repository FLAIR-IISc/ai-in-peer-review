1. Paper studies how much PoS information is encoded in word embeddings by training word2vec on BNC (lemma+PoS) and classifying PoS with logistic regression, analyzing misclassifications as boundary phenomena.  
2. Contribution: a diagnostic method using embedding-based PoS prediction errors to surface PoS boundary cases and corpus annotation inconsistencies.  
3. Contribution: a simple, type-level, context-free PoS predictor trained on unsupervised embeddings that achieves ~0.84 accuracy.  
4. Contribution: evidence that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions, plus a prototype PoS vector analysis.  
5. Strength: strong qualitative error analysis showing linguistically plausible boundary cases and useful corpus QA examples.  
6. Strength: clear, simple, and reproducible methodology using standard models and corpora, with transparent hyperparameters.  
7. Strength: cross-corpus analysis (BNC and UD) that highlights annotation policy differences.  
8. Strength: empirical support (feature-ranking) that PoS signals are distributed across dimensions.  
9. Weakness: label leakage—training on lemma_POS tokens injects PoS into contexts, making PoS prediction partially tautological and inflating results.  
10. Weakness: potential circularity in UD evaluation if UD gold PoS were appended to look up vectors, making the 0.99 F-score meaningless.  
11. Weakness: unfair baseline—evaluating a contextual tagger (Stanford) on isolated tokens handicaps it and biases comparisons.  
12. Weakness: limited novelty given prior work showing embeddings encode PoS and linguistic properties; main novelty is the corpus-diagnostics angle.  
13. Weakness: methodological/reporting gaps including frequency-weighting leakage (duplicated vectors), risk of CV leakage, missing stronger baselines, absent per-class metrics, and no human validation of flagged errors.  
14. Weakness: limited evaluation scope—only English, no robustness checks to hyperparameters, incomplete references, and missing figures/tables.  
15. Requested clarification: explain how UD lemma vectors were obtained—were UD gold PoS appended to lemmas to retrieve vectors?  
16. Requested experiment: repeat outlier analysis with embeddings trained on raw lemmas (no PoS) to rule out label leakage and check if boundary cases persist.  
17. Requested baselines/ablation: compare to Brown clusters, PPMI+SVD, bag-of-context, subword-aware embeddings, and report per-class metrics and ablations (window size, corpus size, downsampling).  
18. Requested analysis: provide per-class scores for the 0.84 predictor and quantify effects of frequency, ambiguity, and orthographic cues (digits, capitalization).  
19. Requested validation: run a small human study to confirm whether flagged BNC “errors” are actual annotation mistakes and report precision/recall.  
20. Overall verdict: useful diagnostic and qualitative analysis but major methodological flaws (label leakage, possible circular evaluation, weak baselines); recommend rejection unless key analyses are redone, clarifications provided, and validations strengthened.
