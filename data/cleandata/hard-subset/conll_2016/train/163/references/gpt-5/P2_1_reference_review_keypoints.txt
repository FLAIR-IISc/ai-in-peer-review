- Paper studies whether distributional embeddings encode PoS and uses that to predict PoS and analyze misclassifications as PoS-boundary/corpus-annotation phenomena.  
- Two workflows: (A) train Skip-gram on lemma_POS tokens then probe with linear classifier; (B) train on unlabeled lemmas and learn a classifier from a small set of prototypical UD lemmas for a context-free tagger.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that embeddings encode substantial PoS information recoverable by a simple linear probe and identification of PoS-informative dimensions.  
- Contribution: qualitative error analysis suggesting embeddings surface PoS boundary cases and potential BNC annotation inconsistencies.  
- Contribution: proof-of-concept context-free PoS predictor trained from few unambiguous lemmas achieving 0.84 accuracy on UD tokens as a possible low-resource bootstrap.  
- Strength: linguistically motivated, convincing analyses linking misclassifications to known PoS grey zones and annotation policy choices.  
- Strength: simple, transparent, interpretable methodology (linear probe, feature ranking, prototypical vectors).  
- Strength: potential practical diagnostic for corpus quality by flagging systematic tagging issues.  
- Strength: plausible low-resource angle showing corpus-wide distributional info can yield a decent tag prior.  
- Weakness: major methodological circularity—training embeddings on lemma_POS injects PoS into contexts, making PoS separability largely tautological.  
- Weakness: UD cross-corpus result (F1=0.99) is unclear or possibly inflated due to mapping/label leakage between UD and BNC lemma+PoS space.  
- Weakness: unfair baseline—the Stanford tagger was tested on isolated tokens, disadvantaging a context-dependent system versus the embedding model.  
- Weakness: missing key baselines for context-free tagger (e.g., majority-PoS-per-lemma, frequency/morphology heuristics) to judge 0.84 accuracy.  
- Weakness: limited validation of error-flagging claims—no quantitative manual re-annotation to confirm flagged cases are genuine annotation errors.  
- Weakness: reproducibility and clarity gaps (UD version, mapping procedure, class distributions, per-class metrics) and bibliography/ code-release unclear.  
- Question: clarify how the UD 0.99 F-score was obtained—input type, mapping to embedding space, coverage, and per-class/confusion metrics.  
- Question: how do outlier analysis results change if embeddings are trained on unlabeled lemmas (no PoS) to address circularity?  
- Question: what is the majority-PoS-per-lemma baseline accuracy on the UD test under the same filtering and per-class comparison to the 0.84 result?  
- Question: how robust are top features/dimensions (e.g., conjunction dimension 94) across random seeds, window sizes, and subsampling?  
- Question: was any manual verification done for suspected BNC errors (e.g., “plain/clear/sharp”); if not, can counts and contexts be provided?  
- Overall: interesting and linguistically grounded work with a simple methodology, but serious confound from label leakage, unclear cross-corpus claims, missing baselines, and reproducibility issues—needs methodological tightening to be convincing.
