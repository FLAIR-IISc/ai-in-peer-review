- Paper studies whether distributional word embeddings encode PoS and uses that signal to (i) surface PoS-boundary/outlier lexical items and annotation inconsistencies and (ii) build a context-free PoS predictor.  
- Method: skip-gram embeddings on BNC with lemma+PoS and lemma-only variants, multinomial/prototype logistic classifiers, ANOVA feature ranking for dimensions.  
- Contribution 1: Corpus auditing via embeddings to reveal PoS boundary phenomena and potential BNC tagging errors.  
- Contribution 2: A lightweight, type-level context-free PoS predictor using prototype-based logistic regression achieving 0.84 token accuracy on UD.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical claim that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated.  
- Strength: Linguistically meaningful error analysis highlighting plausible boundary cases and concrete BNC inconsistencies.  
- Strength: Simple, reproducible methodology with specified hyperparameters and released mappings.  
- Strength: Clear separation of experiments to test whether PoS is encoded without explicit tags in training text.  
- Strength: Practical implication of using unambiguous prototypes to bootstrap PoS tagging in resource-poor settings.  
- Weakness: Methodological leakage—training embeddings on lemma+PoS tokens makes the task easier and may conflate semantics with tags.  
- Weakness: Cross-validation likely splits by instances rather than by type, allowing identical vectors in train and test and inflating performance.  
- Weakness: UD generalization result (F≈0.99) is unclear and possibly circular if vectors include PoS information; inputs need clarification.  
- Weakness: Unfair baseline—Stanford tagger evaluated on isolated tokens is not a meaningful comparison for diagnostics.  
- Weakness: Limited scope—only English; claims about PoS boundaries/generalizability need typologically different languages.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to alternative representations and baselines (PPMI+SVD, GloVe, Brown clusters, morphological/character features).  
- Weakness: No ablation on embedding hyperparameters (window size, subsampling, negatives) to support robustness claims.  
- Weakness: Novelty is limited given prior work on embeddings/PoS and unsupervised PoS induction; corpus-auditing angle underdeveloped and missing citations.  
- Weakness: Dimension analysis is under-evaluated—no held-out performance vs top-k features or stability checks across runs.  
- Questions: Clarify CV protocol (type-based or instance-based) and exact input vectors used for UD evaluation (lemma vs lemma+PoS).  
- Questions: Report performance specifically on ambiguous lemmas/tokens and accuracy by tag ambiguity.  
- Questions: Provide results with alternative embeddings/clusters and a character n-gram classifier, plus ablations on window/subsampling.  
- Questions: Provide held-out accuracy vs top-k ANOVA features and assess stability of high-F dimensions across random inits.  
- Overall: Thoughtful and promising for corpus auditing, but empirical claims are weakened by evaluation design, limited baselines, and scope; leans toward rejection unless major clarifications and additional experiments are provided.
