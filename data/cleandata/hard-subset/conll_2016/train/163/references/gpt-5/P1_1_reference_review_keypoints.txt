- Paper investigates whether distributional word embeddings encode PoS information and uses that signal to (i) surface boundary/annotation cases and (ii) build a simple, context-free PoS predictor.
- Methodology: train skip-gram embeddings on lemma+gold-PoS tokens, fit multinomial logistic regression, inspect systematic misclassifications as outliers.
- Contribution: practical pipeline to audit PoS annotations and identify borderline cases via classifier misclassifications.
- Contribution: evidence that static embeddings carry a strong PoS signal enabling a context-free PoS classifier trained on ~1.5k prototypical lemmas (0.84 accuracy on UD).
- Contribution: initial feature analysis indicating PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions.
- Strength: strong empirical signal—high F-scores (0.979 CV on frequent BNC types; 0.91 on held-out types) and solid context-free baseline given no sentence context.
- Strength: insightful error analysis revealing linguistically plausible boundary/annotation issues (e.g., participles, numerals vs nouns, adv/adj confusions).
- Strength: distributional confusion analysis complements tagger-based audits by surfacing different high-coverage misclassification types.
- Strength: low data requirement and potential utility for low-resource bootstrapping; reproducibility-friendly experimental choices.
- Major weakness: methodological confound—training embeddings on lemma+PoS injects supervision into representations, weakening claims about unsupervised encoding.
- Weakness: limited novelty—prior work already shows PoS signals in embeddings; the main novelty (corpus auditing) is narrower than the paper’s broader claims.
- Weakness: missing baselines and comparisons (PPMI+SVD, Brown clusters, fastText, ELMo/BERT, suffix/char n-gram baselines) and absence of per-tag metrics.
- Weakness: evaluation concerns—reported 0.99 F-score with 27% OOV excluded is suspicious and needs detail on coverage and per-tag performance.
- Weakness: generalization limited to English/BNC; claims about low-resource or typological generality are speculative without multilingual evidence.
- Weakness: feature analysis is shallow (ANOVA ranking), lacks hyperparameter robustness checks, and anecdotal single-dimension findings are unconvincing.
- Weakness: unfair comparison with Stanford tagger—running a sentence-level tagger on isolated tokens is a strawman; should compare tagger confusion on full sentences.
- Rebuttal request: replicate outlier-detection with embeddings trained on raw lemmas (no PoS in tokenization) and report whether same boundary cases emerge.
- Rebuttal request: provide per-tag precision/recall and confusion matrix for UD context-free classifier and compare to character n-gram/suffix baseline and fastText; report prototypes-per-tag needed.
- Rebuttal request: report sensitivity to embedding hyperparameters (window, subsampling, vector size) and justify window=2 and no subsampling.
- Rebuttal request: quantify how many flagged BNC cases are clear annotation errors vs debatable boundary cases (manual verification proportion).
- Rebuttal request: confirm release of code, embeddings, and BNC-to-UD mapping and specify license.
- Additional comments: fix incomplete/garbled references, clarify dataset splits and OOV handling, tone down “redefining PoS” claims, and consider adding multilingual experiments and stronger baselines.
