- Paper studies how word embeddings encode PoS, using a simple classifier to surface boundary lexical items and annotation inconsistencies; also trains a lemma-only PoS predictor from untagged embeddings.
- Contributions: example-rich analysis linking misclassifications to fuzzy PoS boundaries and annotation issues.
- Contributions: probing setup showing PoS information is robust and distributed across many embedding dimensions (ANOVA-based ablation).
- Contributions: lemma-only PoS predictor achieves reasonable accuracy, suggesting low-resource utility.
- Strength: linguistically grounded, detailed error analysis that identifies realistic annotation and boundary-category problems.
- Strength: clear evidence that PoS signals are distributed across embedding dimensions rather than localized.
- Strength: methodologically simple and reproducible (skip-gram + multinomial logistic regression; sensible frequency-weighting).
- Strength: some cross-corpus robustness shown by applying predictor to UD English with high type-level performance.
- Strength: practical diagnostic utility for corpus QA and bootstrap tagging.
- Weakness: limited novelty—encoding of PoS in embeddings is known; main novelty is incremental (outlier-driven annotation analysis).
- Weakness: methodological circularity—embeddings trained on lemma_POS tokens may leak supervision, inflating probe performance.
- Weakness: evaluation/reporting issues—cross-validation on duplicates, omission of UD types, and ablation reported only on training set weaken claims.
- Weakness: unfair baseline—comparing to a contextual tagger given only lemma inputs disadvantages the comparator and weakens conclusions.
- Weakness: limited scope—English-only, single algorithm/hyperparameters, no sensitivity study or multilingual validation.
- Weakness: presentation problems—malformed references, missing figures/tables, anonymized footnotes/links hinder clarity and reproducibility.
- Question: can the authors replicate outlier/error analyses with embeddings trained on untagged text to rule out supervision leakage?
- Question: how sensitive are results and surfaced outliers to embedding hyperparameters and model choice (window size, negative sampling, CBOW/SG, fastText, GloVe)?
- Question: please provide per-class precision/recall/F1 for UD with and without OOVs, and test-set ablation curves for feature importance.
- Question: how many tokens/lemmas are ambiguous by the authors’ criterion in UD, and how does ambiguity affect performance (breakdown closed vs open classes)?
- Question: can the authors release the BNC→Universal tag mapping and code to reproduce analyses?
- Overall: a clear, thoughtful, and useful diagnostic study with incremental technical novelty and concerns about circularity, evaluation rigor, and generality that should be addressed to strengthen the paper.
