- Paper trains SGNS on lemma_POS tokens, fits multinomial logistic regression to predict PoS, and treats misclassifications as outliers highlighting PoS boundaries and annotation issues.  
- Authors analyze prototypical PoS vectors and feature importance, claiming PoS signal is distributed across many embedding dimensions.  
- Present a lemma-only PoS predictor using UD English supervision on unambiguous lemmas, achieving 0.84 accuracy without sentence context.  
- Contribution: a practical corpus-diagnostics method using embedding-based PoS prediction errors to surface PoS boundary phenomena and tagging issues.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that PoS information is present but distributed across many embedding dimensions via proto-vector analysis and ANOVA ranking.  
- Contribution: proof-of-concept lemma-only PoS prediction from global contexts as a potential tool for preliminary tagging or lexicon induction.  
- Strength: insightful error analysis revealing linguistically meaningful boundary cases and likely annotation errors in BNC.  
- Strength: held-out evaluation by word type (frequency slices) supports generalization beyond top vocabulary and non-trivial outlier analysis.  
- Strength: lemma-only predictor’s 0.84 accuracy shows substantial PoS signal in global co-occurrence patterns without sentence context.  
- Strength: feature ranking counters claims that PoS is localized to a few embedding axes and highlights interesting idiosyncrasies.  
- Strength: approach is simple, reproducible in spirit, and potentially valuable for annotation audits.  
- Weakness: supervision leakage—training on lemma_POS injects gold PoS into embeddings, undermining claims about unsupervised PoS capture.  
- Weakness: UD evaluation unclear—uncertain how vectors for UD lemmas were obtained if embeddings are lemma_POS-based.  
- Weakness: cross-validation likely inflated because folds may include identical word vectors unless split by word type; type-level CV needed.  
- Weakness: unfair baseline—running the Stanford tagger on isolated tokens removes sentence context and yields meaningless comparisons.  
- Weakness: limited breadth—English-only, no multilingual/low-resource experiments, and missing sensitivity analyses for model family, window size, dimensionality, subword/casing.  
- Weakness: feature analysis reports training accuracy without test curves or statistical tests to support generalization claims.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—insufficient preprocessing details (lemmatizer, casing, tokenization, OOV handling), anonymized mapping, missing figures, and under-specified classifier hyperparameters.  
- Question: How was the UD evaluation conducted—were lemma_POS gold tags used to select vectors at test time?  
- Question: For 10-fold CV on top 10k words, were folds split by word type to prevent identical vectors in train and test?  
- Question: Can you rerun the Stanford tagger comparison on full sentences and report resulting confusion patterns?  
- Question: What proportion of test tokens are ambiguous lemmas, and how does lemma-only accuracy vary by PoS and frequency; would surface forms help?  
- Question: How sensitive are findings to embedding hyperparameters, model family (CBOW/GloVe/PPMI-SVD), and window sizes; does distributed PoS signal persist?  
- Suggestion: Avoid supervision leakage by training embeddings on raw lemmas/wordforms and learning PoS centroids from a small seed before outlier detection.  
- Suggestion: Provide a ranked list of high-confidence outliers with examples and release code/data for independent corpus auditing.  
- Suggestion: Replicate experiments on a typologically different language and a smaller corpus to support generality and low-resource claims.  
- Suggestion: Report held-out accuracy vs. number of features (k) and include test curves/statistical tests to substantiate the “distributed signal” claim.  
- Overall: Useful and timely diagnostic perspective but requires key methodological clarifications and leakage-free evaluations to support central claims.
