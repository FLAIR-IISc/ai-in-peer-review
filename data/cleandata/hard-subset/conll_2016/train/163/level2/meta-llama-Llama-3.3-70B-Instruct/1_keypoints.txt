- Paper summary: Trains a classifier on British National Corpus word embeddings to predict PoS tags and analyzes errors to study PoS boundaries.
- Contribution: Shows word embeddings contain PoS information enabling high-accuracy PoS prediction.
- Contribution: Demonstrates distributional models can detect systematic annotation or tagging inconsistencies via error analysis.
- Contribution: Finds PoS affiliation is distributed across many embedding components (around a hundred), not localized.
- Strength: Proposes a novel distributional approach to PoS tagging and annotation error detection.
- Strength: Classifier achieves high accuracy, validating the approach.
- Strength: Provides evidence that PoS boundaries are gradient/non-categorical.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to English, so cross-linguistic generalization is unclear.
- Weakness: Results depend on the quality of the corpus used to train embeddings.
- Weakness: More research needed on which embedding components encode PoS and on hyperparameter effects.
- Question: How do results generalize to languages with different grammatical structures?
- Question: Can this approach improve PoS tagging in low-resource languages lacking annotated corpora?
- Question: How do distributional-model hyperparameters affect classifier performance and what are the optimal settings?
