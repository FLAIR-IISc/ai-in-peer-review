- Summary — The paper studies how word embeddings encode part-of-speech information by training a classifier on embeddings, analyzing annotation inconsistencies, and examining PoS signal distribution across vector components, proposing a distributional PoS tagger.

- Contribution — Shows embeddings contain PoS affiliation and can reveal clusters matching traditional PoS classes.

- Contribution — Demonstrates the method can detect systematic annotation errors and inconsistencies.

- Contribution — Proposes a PoS tagging method that assigns tags based on proximity of word vectors to prototypical PoS vectors.

- Strength — Introduces a novel distributional-semantic approach to PoS tagging distinct from traditional methods.

- Strength — Provides a comprehensive evaluation, including error analysis and annotation inconsistency inspection.

- Strength — Offers insights into the fuzzy nature of PoS boundaries and annotation challenges.

- Weakness — Focuses mainly on English, limiting claims about cross-linguistic generality.

- Weakness — Methodological limits: approach may not suit all text types and hyperparameter effects need further exploration.

- Weakness — Lacks detailed comparison to state-of-the-art PoS taggers to contextualize performance.

- Question — How will the approach be extended to other languages and what challenges are expected?

- Question — Please provide more details on distributional model training and hyperparameter selection.

- Question — How will you address limitations like reliance on large annotated corpora?
