**Summary of the Paper**

This paper explores the relationship between word embeddings and part-of-speech (PoS) tags. The authors train a distributional semantic model on the British National Corpus (BNC) and use the resulting word embeddings to predict PoS tags. They find that the word embeddings contain information about PoS affiliation and that this information is distributed among several components of the word embeddings. The authors also analyze the errors made by the predictor and find that they often reveal inconsistencies in the annotation process or guidelines.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Demonstration of the relationship between word embeddings and PoS tags**: The authors show that word embeddings can be used to predict PoS tags with high accuracy, and that this information is distributed among several components of the word embeddings.
2. **Analysis of errors made by the predictor**: The authors analyze the errors made by the predictor and find that they often reveal inconsistencies in the annotation process or guidelines.
3. **Proposal of a new approach to PoS tagging**: The authors propose a new approach to PoS tagging that uses word embeddings as input to a classifier.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to PoS tagging**: The authors propose a new approach to PoS tagging that uses word embeddings as input to a classifier.
2. **Thorough analysis of errors**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of the errors made by the predictor, which reveals inconsistencies in the annotation process or guidelines.
3. **Use of a large and representative corpus**: The authors use the British National Corpus (BNC), which is a large and representative corpus of English language.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their approach on a single corpus (BNC) and do not provide a comprehensive evaluation on multiple corpora.
2. **Lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The authors do not compare their approach to state-of-the-art methods in PoS tagging.
3. **Limited discussion of the implications of the results**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of the implications of their results for the field of natural language processing.

**Questions to Authors**
