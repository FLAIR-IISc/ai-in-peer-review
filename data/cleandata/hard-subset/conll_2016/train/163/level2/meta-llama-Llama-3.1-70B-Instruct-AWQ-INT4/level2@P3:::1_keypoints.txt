- Paper studies how word embeddings trained on the BNC encode PoS information and uses them to predict PoS tags, finding information distributed across embedding components and errors that expose annotation inconsistencies.
- Main contribution: empirically demonstrates embeddings contain PoS signals and that this signal is distributed across components.
- Main contribution: analyzes predictor errors, showing they often reflect annotation or guideline inconsistencies.
- Main contribution: proposes a PoS tagging approach that feeds word embeddings into a classifier.
- Strength: introduces a novel embedding-based approach to PoS tagging.
- Strength: provides thorough error analysis that uncovers annotation issues.
- Strength: uses the large, representative British National Corpus for training and evaluation.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a single corpus (BNC), lacking cross-corpus validation.
- Weakness: no comparison to state-of-the-art PoS tagging methods.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the broader implications of the findings for NLP.
- Question: Do the results generalize to other languages and corpora?
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed error analysis linking mistakes to annotation guidelines?
- Question: How does the proposed approach compare empirically to state-of-the-art PoS taggers?
- Additional: Promising approach but needs broader evaluation and comparisons to be more convincing.
