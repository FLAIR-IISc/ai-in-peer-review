- Paper investigates how BNC-trained distributional word embeddings encode PoS by training a multinomial classifier on word-type vectors and using misclassifications to probe soft category boundaries and annotation inconsistencies.
- Claimed contribution: a practical audit methodology that surfaces boundary cases and systematic PoS annotation issues in BNC (e.g., ADJ/ADV confusions, NUM vs NOUN for years/currencies).
- Claimed contribution: empirical evidence that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized to a few.
- Claimed contribution: a contextless PoS predictor trained on ~1.5k unambiguous lemmas achieving 0.84 accuracy on UD tokens, suggesting utility for low-resource bootstrapping.
- Strength: strong empirical signal — classifier achieves 0.979 F-score (10-fold CV), 0.91 on held-out types, and UD application reports 0.99 F-score on covered items.
- Strength: insightful error analysis — outlier audit surfaces linguistically meaningful boundary phenomena and compelling corpus tagging issues (e.g., ~600 ADJ/ADV inconsistencies).
- Strength: methodological clarity — simple, reproducible models (skip-gram + logistic regression), frequency weighting, and ANOVA-based feature study showing signal plateau around k≈100.
- Strength: potential applicability — seed-based predictor’s contextless performance is promising for preliminary low-resource tagging.
- Weakness: possible circularity — training on lemma_POS tokens then predicting POS may conflate lexeme sense splits with learned contextual regularities, and UD “0.99” evaluation procedure is unclear.
- Weakness: baseline fairness — Stanford tagger is evaluated on isolated tokens (no context), which is outside its intended operating regime and may confound comparisons.
- Weakness: limited scope and robustness — single language/corpus (English/BNC) and single embedding configuration (window=2); no ablations across corpora, hyperparameters, or embedding families, and no per-class stats or significance tests.
- Weakness: lack of validation for flagged errors — examples are persuasive but no systematic human adjudication or quantitative precision/recall estimate for detected annotation issues.
- Question: how was the UD “0.99” evaluation conducted given the embeddings used lemma_POS vocabulary — were UD lemmas concatenated with tags for lookup, potentially leaking labels?
- Question: please report per-class precision/recall and confusion matrices for all evaluations, and sensitivity to the frequency weighting scheme.
- Question: how stable are the findings across embedding hyperparameters and alternative models (e.g., CBOW, GloVe, fastText with subword features)?
- Question: was any human adjudication performed to quantify the accuracy of the detected BNC annotation issues?
