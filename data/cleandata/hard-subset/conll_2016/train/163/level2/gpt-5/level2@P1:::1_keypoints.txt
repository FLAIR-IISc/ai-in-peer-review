- Paper trains PoS classifiers on BNC embeddings to surface boundary phenomena, annotation inconsistencies, and graded category membership.  
- Main contributions: (i) error-analysis framework using PoS-from-embeddings classifiers, (ii) evidence that PoS info is distributed across many embedding dimensions (plateau ~100 dims), (iii) a lemma-only, context-free PoS predictor achieving 0.84 token accuracy on UD.  
- Clear, data-driven analysis linking embedding misclassifications to linguistic observations and annotation issues (e.g., participles, numerals vs years, proper vs common nouns).  
- Strong empirical results: high F-scores for lemma+PoS model (0.979 weighted F on CV, 0.91 F held-out types) and good UD lemma coverage (F≈0.99 for types in model).  
- Practical utility for corpus diagnostics: identifies concrete BNC tagging issues and tokenization/tagging conventions needing harmonization with UD.  
- Evidence that PoS cues are distributed across many embedding dimensions; no small subset suffices, performance plateaus around ~100 top-ranked dims.  
- Reproducibility-oriented reporting of core hyperparameters (Skip-gram, 300d, window=2, negatives=10, epochs=5) and type selection/frequency-weighting details.  
- Potential circularity: using lemma+PoS tokens to train embeddings may inflate PoS signal and bias the outlier analysis.  
- Limited validation of claimed annotation errors: mostly anecdotal examples and counts; systematic manual evaluation is needed.  
- Methodologically weak baseline: running Stanford tagger on isolated tokens is unfair; lexicon-level or comparable embedding baselines would be stronger.  
- Scope/generalizability claims are speculative for low-resource languages and sensitive hyperparameters (window size, vector size, subword modeling) are unexplored.  
- Evaluation ambiguity: lemma-based predictor reported at token level despite token ambiguity and OOVs (860/21759 dropped); breakdown by ambiguity/frequency/OOV is needed.  
- Question: do findings persist if embeddings are trained on lemma-only tokens for the outlier analysis?  
- Question: can you quantify annotation error rate with a manual sample (e.g., 100 predicted errors with correction proportion and IAA)?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to window size and model type (CBOW vs Skip-gram, subword models); does larger window reduce PoS separability?  
- Question: are ranked PoS-relevant dimensions stable across random seeds/retrainings (is a specific component consistently linked to a PoS)?  
- Question: can you compare against a lexicon-level baseline using simple orthographic/morphological features to isolate embedding benefits?  
- Clarify capitalization handling and whether orthographic cues (e.g., proper-noun capitalization) leak into embeddings.  
- Recommend releasing mappings, models, and flagged outlier lists with contexts to support corpus-diagnostic adoption.  
- Note: the proper vs common noun distributional distinction (cosine ~0.67) is interesting and merits deeper contextual analysis.  
- Suggestion: ablate numerals/symbols during training to test whether “year-as-noun” effects arise from tokenization/tagging conventions or true distributional behavior.
