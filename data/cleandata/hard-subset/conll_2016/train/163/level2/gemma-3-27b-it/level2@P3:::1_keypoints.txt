- Paper shows word embeddings trained on large corpora implicitly encode PoS information that can be extracted via classification and misclassification analysis.
- Main contribution: demonstrating PoS signals in embeddings and using misclassifications to reveal annotation errors and boundary cases.
- Secondary contribution: analysis showing PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated in few.
- Presents a baseline PoS tagger relying solely on distributional information (no contextual features).
- Strength: Novel use of distributional semantics to re-examine linguistic categories and annotation consistency rather than just improve tagging performance.
- Strength: Thorough error analysis that uncovers plausible annotation issues (e.g., currency amounts, adjective/adverb confusions).
- Strength: Empirical evidence supporting distributed representation of PoS across vector components.
- Strength: Classifier shows robustness and transferability by performing well on UD Treebank beyond the BNC.
- Weakness: Embedding-only PoS tagger achieves ~84% accuracy, well below state-of-the-art contextual taggers; limitations and improvement paths are under-discussed.
- Weakness: Treating UD as a gold standard when evaluating BNC annotations risks conflating annotation differences with errors.
- Weakness: No direct comparison to existing corpus-auditing or error-detection methods to demonstrate relative advantage.
- Weakness: Analysis of specific vector components (e.g., component 94) is superficial and lacks linguistic interpretation.
- Question: Please clarify how this method would be applied to resource-poor languages, required amounts of tagged data, and expected performance.
- Question: Please discuss in more detail why BNC and UD annotations differ, considering differing guidelines and linguistic philosophies.
- Question: Provide more detail on the manual mapping process between BNC and Universal tags and how ambiguities were resolved.
- Additional: Paper is well-structured and clearly written.
- Additional: References are relevant and current.
- Additional: Releasing code and data would improve reproducibility.
- Additional: Include a table summarizing key annotation errors and their implications for guidelines.
