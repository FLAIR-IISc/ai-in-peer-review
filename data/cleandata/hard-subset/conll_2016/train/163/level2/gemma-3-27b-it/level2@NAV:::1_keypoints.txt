- Paper shows word embeddings trained on BNC contain PoS information and can predict PoS tags via classifiers.  
- Authors use embeddings to identify words whose distributional patterns deviate from their annotated PoS, revealing annotation issues or “soft” PoS affiliations.  
- Finding that PoS information is distributed across many vector components, not localized to a few.  
- Clear motivation and relevant background on fuzzy PoS boundaries and distributional semantics.  
- Empirical rigor: large corpus (BNC), Skip-gram embeddings, in-sample/out-of-sample testing, and comparison with a standard tagger.  
- Interesting findings: identification of potential annotation errors and insightful analysis of misclassified/outlier words.  
- Reproducibility: authors plan to release mappings between BNC and Universal tags.  
- Limited novelty: core technique (using embeddings for classification) is well-established and no new embedding or classifier method is proposed.  
- Error analysis is somewhat shallow; deeper exploration of specific vector components for misclassified words is needed.  
- Comparison is limited: lacks benchmarking against recent state-of-the-art PoS taggers, especially those using contextualized embeddings like BERT.  
- Missing discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and its effect on results.  
- Question: Which specific vector components indicate the identified errors and what linguistic properties do they represent?  
- Question: How do results change with different embedding dimensions (e.g., 100, 600)?  
- Question: Were contextualized embeddings (e.g., BERT) considered and how do their results compare?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and the code/mappings would be a valuable public resource.
