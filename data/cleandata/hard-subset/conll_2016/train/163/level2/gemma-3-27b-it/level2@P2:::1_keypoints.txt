- Shows word embeddings trained on the BNC encode PoS information, supported by high classifier accuracy.
- Uses logistic regression to predict PoS tags from embeddings with strong cross-validated and unseen-data performance.
- Error analysis of misclassifications reveals annotation inconsistencies and fuzzy PoS boundaries (e.g., currency amounts, adverb/adjective confusions).
- Demonstrates PoS information is distributed across embedding dimensions rather than localized to few components.
- Empirical results are solid (97.9% training, 91% unseen) and validated with 10-fold CV and UD Treebank testing.
- Detailed misclassification analysis provides linguistic insights and compares errors to the Stanford tagger.
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and includes useful examples.
- Uses established corpora (BNC, UD Treebank) and documents tagset mappings for reproducibility.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and propose future research directions.
- Core finding (embeddings encode PoS) has limited novelty relative to prior work; novelty mainly in misclassification analysis and distributed-representation exploration.
- Reliance on simple logistic regression may obscure gains from more complex models; baseline comparisons are weak.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity of embeddings (window size, dimensions, negative sampling) is not explored.
- Manual mapping between BNC and Universal tags raises scalability concerns for other languages/corpora.
- Asks why logistic regression was chosen and whether other models were considered.
- Suggests quantifying BNC annotation inconsistencies and their impact on results.
- Queries interpretability of top-ranked vector components and relation to linguistic features.
- Requests discussion of challenges when extending the approach to languages with different morphology/syntax.
- Notes figures are hard to interpret without clearer axis explanations.
- Recommends adding brief discussion of potential downstream applications (e.g., MT, information extraction).
