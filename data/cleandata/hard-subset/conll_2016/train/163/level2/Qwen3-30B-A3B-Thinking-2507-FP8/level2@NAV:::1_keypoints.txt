- Paper investigates whether word embeddings encode meaningful PoS information and can reveal PoS annotation inconsistencies.
- Method: logistic regression on BNC embeddings, achieving 97.9% weighted F-score on training and 91% on held-out test data.
- Analysis of misclassified words is used to identify potential annotation inconsistencies in the corpus.
- Contribution: Demonstrates PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions, not concentrated in a few.
- Contribution: Reveals annotation inconsistencies in the BNC (e.g., proper nouns labeled as common nouns, verbs labeled as adjectives).
- Contribution: Shows distributional models can detect systematic annotation errors that standard PoS taggers may miss.
- Strength: Clear empirical demonstration of distributed PoS information (Figure 3).
- Strength: Valuable linguistic insights from misclassification analysis, including discovery of systematic errors.
- Strength: Robust evaluation across multiple datasets, including the Universal Dependencies Treebank.
- Insight: Highlights “soft” PoS boundaries that challenge traditional categorical PoS approaches.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art PoS taggers (which typically exceed 97% accuracy).
- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations, notably handling of words absent from the embedding vocabulary (OOVs).
- Weakness: Inadequate exploration of how embedding hyperparameters affect PoS discrimination.
- Weakness: Claim of revealing “hidden inconsistencies” would be stronger with more detailed linguistic analysis of specific error cases.
- Recommendation: Clarify the practical significance relative to existing PoS taggers and how the method could be integrated into annotation pipelines.
- Recommendation: Provide more explicit discussion of how findings could inform future PoS tagging guidelines.
- Overall assessment: Solid contribution bridging distributional semantics and linguistic theory; acceptable for the conference with modest revisions.
