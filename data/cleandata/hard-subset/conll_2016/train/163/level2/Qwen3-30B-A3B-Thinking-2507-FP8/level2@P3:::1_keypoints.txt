- Contribution: Demonstrates PoS information is robustly encoded in word vectors (classifier F-score 0.979).  
- Contribution: Reveals annotation inconsistencies in the BNC via outlier analysis (e.g., proper nouns labeled as common nouns, numerals mislabeled).  
- Contribution: Shows PoS affiliation is distributed across many vector dimensions (~100 features needed for optimal performance).  
- Strength: Rigorous analysis of annotation errors, with outliers (e.g., "1804") exposing systematic BNC inconsistencies.  
- Strength: Cross-validation on Universal Dependencies Treebank supports results (F-score 0.99).  
- Strength: Challenges the “one or two key features” assumption by demonstrating PoS knowledge spans numerous vector dimensions (Fig. 3).  
- Strength: Provides a practical, scalable method to detect corpus annotation errors without manual inspection (identifies mislabeled adjectives/adverbs).  
- Weakness: Manual BNC-to-Universal tag mapping is insufficiently justified and lacks validation metrics, risking mapping-induced errors.  
- Weakness: Classifier’s 0.84 accuracy on UD Treebank is far below modern taggers (>0.97) and the paper does not adequately explain whether this is due to the no-context constraint, embedding quality, or other factors.  
- Weakness: The claim of “soft” PoS boundaries is descriptive and not tied to established theoretical frameworks (e.g., Hanks’ graded categories).  
- Question: How was the BNC-to-Universal tag mapping validated—provide inter-annotator agreement or error-rate statistics?  
- Question: What specific linguistic phenomena (e.g., morphological ambiguity, discourse context) account for the 0.84 accuracy gap, and how should this inform future embedding design?
