- Paper analyzes BNC-trained word embeddings and PoS boundaries by training classifiers on embeddings to detect outliers and annotation inconsistencies.
- Demonstrates embeddings encode PoS information across vector components, with high classifier F-scores (0.91–0.99).
- Uses misclassified words to identify annotation inconsistencies in the BNC (e.g., adjectives tagged as adverbs).
- Analyzes misclassifications to reveal graded PoS boundaries and overlap between classes (e.g., participles behaving like verbs and adjectives).
- Proposes bootstrapping PoS tagging for resource-poor languages using a small manually tagged seed and distributional models.
- Novel analytical use of embeddings to study PoS boundaries and detect annotation errors.
- Robust experimental design using multiple datasets (BNC, UD) and rigorous evaluation; strong performance from distributional features alone.
- Practical implications for improving PoS tagging in low-resource settings and refining annotated corpora.
- Detailed error analysis provides linguistic validation and insights into PoS boundary phenomena.
- Methodological novelty is limited—training classifiers on embeddings is not new; novelty is mainly in analysis.
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion of limitations, such as reliance on high-quality embeddings and annotated corpora.
- Experiments focus only on English, limiting claims of generalizability to other languages.
- Sparse comparison with state-of-the-art PoS taggers, making practical utility unclear.
- Question: How does performance compare to state-of-the-art PoS taggers when contextual information is included?
- Question: How do embedding hyperparameters (vector size, window size) affect the results?
- Question: Can the method handle ambiguous words that take multiple PoS tags in different contexts?
- Overall: Interesting and well-executed study that should address limitations and provide more evidence of applicability to other languages and real-world tagging.
