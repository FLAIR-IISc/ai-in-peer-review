- Paper investigates how distributional word embeddings encode PoS by training a classifier on BNC embeddings and analyzing its errors to find words that deviate from canonical PoS.  
- Contribution: Proposes a methodology that uses classifier misclassifications to discover linguistically meaningful words on fuzzy PoS boundaries and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Contribution: Provides empirical evidence that PoS membership is graded rather than strictly categorical, with identified outliers being linguistically motivated.  
- Strength: Thorough, concrete qualitative analysis showing meaningful error types (e.g., NOUN-ADJ, VERB-ADJ) and comparisons to a standard PoS tagger.  
- Strength: Sound experimental design with separate train/test splits, frequency weighting, validation on UD Treebank, and a context-free tagger experiment.  
- Strength: Clear practical application as a tool for probing annotations, finding systematic corpus errors, and exploring syntactic properties.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty at a high level since it confirms well-known facts that embeddings encode syntactic info and PoS boundaries are fuzzy.  
- Weakness: Unusual choice to train embeddings on concatenated lemma_PoSTag strings is insufficiently justified and may conflate lexical and tag information.  
- Weakness: Title “Redefining part-of-speech classes” overstates the contribution; the paper probes PoS boundaries but does not propose a new formal PoS framework.  
- Question to authors: Why train embeddings on lemma_PoSTag strings, and how would results differ if embeddings were trained on raw lemmas with PoS used only as classifier labels?
