- Summary: Studies how PoS information is encoded in word embeddings by training classifiers on BNC embeddings and qualitatively analyzing classifier errors; also shows a simple classifier trained on unambiguous words can predict PoS without context.

- Contribution 1: Uses distributional outliers to reveal graded category membership and systematic PoS annotation inconsistencies in the BNC, demonstrating embeddings as a tool for corpus exploration and annotation refinement.

- Contribution 2: Empirically confirms PoS information is robustly encoded and distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated in a few.

- Strength 1: Offers an insightful qualitative analysis of errors that uncovers fuzzy category boundaries and annotation artifacts, strengthened by comparison with a standard PoS tagger.

- Strength 2: Employs a clear, reproducible methodology (simple logistic regression) that keeps focus on embeddings and presents a logical two-part experimental narrative.

- Strength 3: Provides sufficient detail on data, preprocessing, and hyperparameters to allow replication, with justified design choices like frequency-weighted training.

- Strength 4: Presents empirical evidence that syntactic information is distributed across dimensions, showing accuracy increases smoothly with more features up to ~100 dimensions.

- Weakness 1: Experimental design trains embeddings on lemma_PoS strings, introducing circularity by encoding gold PoS into vocabulary and making separability hard to interpret without stronger justification.

- Weakness 2: Generalizability concern: PoS predictor is trained on a small curated set of "unambiguous" lemmas, raising questions about performance on rarer or more ambiguous vocabulary.

- Weakness 3: Potential inconsistency about feature importance: an idiosyncratic strong signal in one component (conjunctions) vs. the claim of broadly distributed PoS information needs a more unified account.
