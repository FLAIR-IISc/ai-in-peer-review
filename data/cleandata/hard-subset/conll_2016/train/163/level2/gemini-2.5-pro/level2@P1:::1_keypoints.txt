1. Novel method: use errors of an embedding-based PoS classifier to detect linguistic ambiguities and corpus annotation inconsistencies (e.g., noun–adjective continuum, currency amounts, years).  
2. Empirical finding: PoS information is encoded in embeddings and accumulates as more dimensions are used rather than being localized to a few components.  
3. Proof-of-concept tagger: a context-independent PoS tagger using only embeddings trained on few prototypes performs reasonably and may help low-resource settings.  
4. Strength — methodology: the classifier-error lens is a principled, data-driven tool for flagging outlier words and refining PoS boundaries/annotations.  
5. Strength — qualitative analysis: Section 4 provides compelling linguistic explanations for error patterns (e.g., participles-as-adjectives, currency-as-numeral, years-as-nouns).  
6. Strength — experiments: complementary experiments (lemma_POS vectors and standard embeddings) and comparison with Stanford Tagger show robustness of findings.  
7. Weakness — nonstandard training: primary experiments use concatenated lemma_POS tokens, conflating tag information in context windows without fully discussing implications or comparing to standard lemma-trained embeddings.  
8. Weakness — limited baseline: PoS predictor evaluation lacks simple baselines (e.g., most-frequent-tag per lemma) to contextualize the reported 0.84 accuracy.  
9. Weakness — apparent contradiction: a single strong component for conjunctions (component 94) seems at odds with the claim that PoS information is broadly distributed, without reconciliation.  
10. Question — lemma_POS choice: why train on lemma_POS tokens vs. training on lemmas and averaging by gold PoS, and would that yield similar outlier detections?  
11. Question — baseline results: please provide a majority-class (most-frequent-tag-per-lemma) baseline to contextualize the 0.84 accuracy.  
12. Question — component analysis: is the conjunction “killer feature” unique to conjunctions or closed-class words, and is it an artifact of lemma_POS training versus lemma-only embeddings?
