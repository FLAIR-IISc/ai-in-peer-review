- Method: train a classifier to predict PoS from pre-trained embeddings and use its misclassifications to identify words at PoS boundaries and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Contribution 1: Proposes a novel error-analysis methodology that reveals graded PoS membership and corpus tagging issues (e.g., passive participles, years/currencies).  
- Contribution 2: Shows context-free PoS prediction from embeddings is viable (baseline ~0.84 accuracy) and that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions.  
- Strength 1: Insightful error analysis produces linguistic insights and uncovers systematic BNC tagging errors (e.g., ‘plain’, ‘clear’ tagged as adverbs).  
- Strength 2: Sound experimental design—using lemma_POS, training on lemmas, and validating on UD Treebank—supports the claims’ robustness.  
- Strength 3: Clear, well-supported conclusions with high initial classifier F-score (0.979), solid context-free tagger performance, and convincing feature-ablation results.  
- Weakness 1: Initial setup (training on lemma_POS strings) is not explained clearly and makes high performance partly expected; authors should stress the goal is prototype-based analysis, not raw accuracy.  
- Weakness 2: Core premise (embeddings contain PoS info) is well-known; the paper should emphasize the novelty of the error-analysis methodology rather than restating that finding.  
- Weakness 3: Comparison to the Stanford tagger is unfair—feeding isolated tokens removes its context advantage; a more appropriate baseline would be a comparable context-free or embedding-based tagger.
