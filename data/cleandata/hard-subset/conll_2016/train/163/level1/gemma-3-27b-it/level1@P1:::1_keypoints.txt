- Embeddings trained on BNC encode PoS information, supported by high predictor accuracy (97.9% train F-score, 91% unseen), though not fundamentally novel.
- Systematic error analysis uncovers annotation inconsistencies (noun/adjective, numeral/noun, adverb/noun boundaries) and suggests using distributional evidence to refine annotations.
- PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (at least 100) rather than localized to a few components.

- Strong empirical evaluation using BNC, Universal POS, 10-fold CV, and UD Treebank test increases credibility.
- Clear, well-structured presentation with detailed methodology and insightful, example-driven error analysis.
- Practical methodology for identifying potential annotation errors, benefiting corpus linguistics and NLP pipelines.
- Mapping between BNC and Universal tags released, enhancing reproducibility.
- Investigation into how PoS is represented in vector space adds valuable analytical depth.

- Limited novelty: confirms known embedding–PoS link without introducing a new technique.
- Heavy reliance on BNC/UD Treebank as gold standards; cross-corpus validation is lacking.
- Error discussion is somewhat superficial and could delve deeper into semantic ambiguity and original BNC guidelines.
- No experimental exploration of hyperparameter effects (vector size, window, training algorithm).
- Table 2 is hard to interpret; should present errors as percentages of all words for clarity.
