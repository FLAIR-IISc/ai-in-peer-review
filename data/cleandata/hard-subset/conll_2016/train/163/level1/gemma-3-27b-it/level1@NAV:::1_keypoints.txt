- Core idea: Word embeddings trained on the BNC implicitly encode PoS information; a classifier on embeddings plus error analysis can reveal annotation inconsistencies and soft PoS boundaries.  
- Primary contribution: Demonstrates and systematically analyzes PoS-related inconsistencies in a well-established, automatically tagged corpus (BNC), with concrete examples (e.g., adjectives used as adverbs, mis-tagged currency amounts).  
- Secondary contribution: Shows PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated in a few features.  
- Additional result: A reasonably accurate PoS classifier can be trained from distributional vectors alone, without contextual information.  
- Strength — practical significance: Uses embeddings to pinpoint annotation errors, offering concrete utility for corpus linguistics and NLP resource improvement.  
- Strength — robustness & validation: Classifier performs well on training data and an external test set (UD Treebank), supporting general robustness beyond BNC.  
- Strength — representation analysis: The finding that PoS cues are spread across vector components challenges simplistic interpretations of embeddings.  
- Strength — reproducibility: Data processing, model training, and evaluation are clearly described, facilitating replication.  
- Weakness — limited novelty: The core premise that embeddings encode syntactic information is well-established; novelty is mainly in application and error analysis.  
- Weakness — language/corpus specificity: Results are tied to English and the BNC, leaving generalizability to other languages and annotation schemes unclear.  
- Weakness — shallow treatment of annotation rationale: The paper flags tagging issues but lacks deep analysis of original annotation guidelines or a strong case for revising them.  
- Question: Please elaborate on the manual mapping between BNC tags and the Universal Tagset and any systematic inconsistencies found.  
- Question: How sensitive are the error-analysis results to embedding hyperparameters (vector size, window, negative sampling)? Any sensitivity analysis?  
- Question: Given distributed PoS signals, could dimensionality reduction improve interpretability or classification performance?
