- Paper shows word embeddings encode PoS information and can predict PoS with high accuracy (97.9% F-score) and identify distributional “outliers.”
- Main contribution is analysis of misclassified/outlier words as indicators of annotation inconsistencies or flexible PoS boundaries.
- Secondary contribution: PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions, not localized to a few.
- Strength: Detailed error analysis revealing annotation issues (e.g., currency amounts, adjectives tagged as adverbs) and linking errors to linguistic observations.
- Strength: Convincing empirical demonstration that embeddings capture syntactic PoS cues, validated on UD Treebank for robustness.
- Strength: The distributed-representation finding counters simplistic dimension-to-feature mappings and is a useful empirical observation.
- Strength: Potential practical application for low-resource languages by training PoS predictors with few annotated examples.
- Weakness: Core finding (embeddings encode PoS) lacks novelty; contribution is more analytical than methodological.
- Weakness: Use of logistic regression feels simplistic; lacks comparison to stronger PoS tagging models (BiLSTM/transformers).
- Weakness: No quantitative analysis of annotation inconsistency prevalence or manual validation of misclassified samples.
- Weakness: Analysis of vector components is underdeveloped; more detail needed on which dimensions drive PoS discrimination.
- Question: What specific criteria and filters defined the “unambiguous” lemmas from UD used for training?
- Question: Was manual validation considered to quantify true annotation errors versus linguistic ambiguity among misclassifications?
- Question: Can the authors detail which embedding dimensions correlate most with particular PoS tags and the linguistic features they capture?
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding hyperparameters (vector size, window, negative sampling) and were ablation studies run?
- Additional: Recommend clearer visualizations (e.g., PCA plots) of prototypical vectors across PoS tags.
- Additional: Related work discussion should better address contextualized embeddings (BERT, RoBERTa) and their PoS implications.
- Additional: References formatting is inconsistent and should be standardized.
