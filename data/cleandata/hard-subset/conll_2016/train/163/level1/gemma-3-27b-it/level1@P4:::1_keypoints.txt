- Word embeddings encode PoS information: a classifier trained on embeddings predicts PoS tags with high accuracy, linking distributional semantics to grammatical categories.
- Classifier error analysis uncovers cases where distributional patterns suggest different PoS tags than BNC annotations, offering a method to identify annotation inconsistencies.
- PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions, supporting a distributed representation of PoS features.

- Strong empirical results: classifier F-scores (0.979 train, 0.91 unseen) robustly support the central claim.
- Novel error-analysis approach: using classifier misclassifications to flag potential corpus annotation issues.
- Robust experimentation across BNC and UD Treebank demonstrates generalizability.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with logical methodology.
- Quantified analysis of error types and misclassification patterns provides deeper insight.

- Limited novelty: the core idea that distributional semantics capture syntactic info is largely incremental given prior work.
- Dependence on BNC annotations: identified "errors" may reflect genuine ambiguity or stylistic choices rather than annotation mistakes.
- No direct comparison to state-of-the-art PoS taggers to contextualize performance.
- Superficial exploration of which embedding dimensions correspond to specific PoS features.
- Unclear implications for annotation guidelines and no concrete recommendations for revisions.
