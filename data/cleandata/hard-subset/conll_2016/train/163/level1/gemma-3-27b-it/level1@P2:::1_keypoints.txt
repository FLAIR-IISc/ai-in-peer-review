- Paper shows word embeddings encode PoS information and can refine PoS class definitions.
- Identifies lexical outliers whose embeddings conflict with annotated PoS, revealing annotation issues or nuanced behavior.
- Presents a PoS predictor based solely on word embeddings, demonstrating feasibility without contextual input.

- Empirical validation: logistic regression on embeddings yields high accuracy (97.9% train, 91% test), supporting distributional-semantic capture of PoS.
- Strong error analysis uncovers BNC annotation inconsistencies (numerals, adj/adv, proper/common nouns) and supports graded PoS affiliations.
- Embedding-only PoS prediction achieves ~84% accuracy and works with limited labeled data, promising for low-resource settings.
- Robustness shown by testing on UD Treebank, suggesting generalizability beyond BNC.
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and situates work within prior linguistic literature.

- Core method (train embeddings + classifier) lacks novelty; main contribution is application and error analysis.
- Classifier simplicity (logistic regression) may limit discovery of subtler embedding–PoS relationships; stronger models not explored.
- Study limited to English; cross-linguistic generalizability is unclear.
- Lacks quantitative comparison of classifier errors against standard PoS taggers for a stronger evaluation.

- Questions: why 300-d embeddings and were other sizes tested?
- Questions: impact of weighting training instances by corpus frequency vs. unweighted?
- Questions: will authors report BNC annotation issues to corpus maintainers?
- Questions: what are computational costs for training embeddings and the classifier, especially for low-resource languages?

- Figures and references are helpful; related work section could include more recent advances.
- Overall: solid study with insightful error analysis; recommends acceptance.
