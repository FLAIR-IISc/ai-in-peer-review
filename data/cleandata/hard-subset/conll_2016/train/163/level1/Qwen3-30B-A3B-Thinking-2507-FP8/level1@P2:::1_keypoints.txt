- Embeddings encode PoS information across many vector dimensions, enabling high-accuracy PoS prediction (97.9% F-score).  
- Classifier misclassifications ("outliers") can highlight potential PoS annotation inconsistencies for corpus improvement.  
- Results support graded/soft PoS boundaries, with words showing distributional overlap across categories.  
- Strong empirical evidence that PoS signal is distributed rather than concentrated in few dimensions (accuracy vs. components analysis).  
- Practical utility demonstrated for corpus linguists through identification of systematic tagging errors with concrete examples.  
- Robust evaluation across datasets (BNC and UD) showing generalizable performance (91% on unseen BNC, 99% on UD).  
- Methodological circularity: training on vectors paired with PoS tags may bias outlier detection and confound true annotation errors.  
- Missing comparison to modern contextual PoS taggers (>97% accuracy) weakens claims about practical usefulness.  
- Overstated novelty: builds on prior work and mainly applies existing techniques without clearly delineating new insights.  
- Question: How will authors address circularity and would a classifier trained without PoS-tag features yield similar outliers?  
- Question: What precisely is meant by "dozens" of components, given the smooth accuracy increase in Figure 3?  
- Question: How to reconcile proper noun misclassification claims with the reported 88% correct classification rate, and what does this imply distributionally?  
- Overall: Empirically valuable contribution linking semantic and grammatical information in embeddings, but needs clearer handling of circularity and stronger comparisons to existing PoS tagging methods.
