- PoS information is distributed across many embedding components (at least ~100 of 300), not concentrated in a few features.  
- A method to detect annotation inconsistencies by training classifiers on embeddings and analyzing misclassifications.  
- A PoS predictor built from embeddings plus 1,564 manually tagged lemmas achieves 84% accuracy on the UD test set.  

- Novel application linking distributional semantics to linguistic theory on PoS boundary sharpness/softness.  
- Analysis uncovers concrete BNC annotation inconsistencies (e.g., "white-collar", "1804") that suggest guideline revisions.  
- Strong empirical evidence that PoS signal is distributed across dimensions, with accuracy improving smoothly as more features are used.  

- Limited engagement with existing linguistic literature explaining the identified phenomena.  
- Insufficient comparison to established PoS taggers and unclear practical utility given the 84% accuracy vs ~97% SOTA.  
- Study restricted to English with no analysis of generalization to morphologically richer or typologically different languages.
