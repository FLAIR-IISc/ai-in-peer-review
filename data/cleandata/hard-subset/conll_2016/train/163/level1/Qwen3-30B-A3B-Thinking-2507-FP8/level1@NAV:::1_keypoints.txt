- Demonstrates PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (at least 100 of 300) rather than localized.  
- Outlier analysis reveals annotation inconsistencies in the BNC (e.g., proper nouns tagged as common nouns, adjectives as adverbs).  
- A simple classifier on embeddings can predict PoS with high accuracy (F1=0.979), enabling systematic exploration of PoS boundaries.  
- Challenges the common assumption that linguistic features are encoded in specific vector components.  
- Outlier-finding method is useful for corpus quality assessment by pinpointing meaningful annotation errors.  
- Cross-corpus validation on the Universal Dependencies Treebank shows findings are robust beyond the BNC.  
- Error-detection approach may be circular since the classifier is trained on the same corpus it analyzes, risking reinforcement of existing biases.  
- PoS prediction relies on a simple logistic regression and is not competitive with state-of-the-art taggers, limiting practical impact.  
- Lacks adequate comparison of its error-analysis method with standard approaches (e.g., confusion matrices from traditional taggers), weakening claims of novelty.  
- How do you distinguish genuine linguistic phenomena from artifacts of the embedding training process?  
- What specific annotation guideline changes do you recommend and how would you implement them in large-scale corpus creation?
