- Contribution: Trains a logistic regression on Skip-gram embeddings to predict PoS tags and analyzes misclassifications as "outliers."
- Contribution: Shows PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (at least ~100 of 300).
- Contribution: Uses distributional models to detect systematic annotation inconsistencies and builds a PoS classifier from minimal manual annotation (1564 words) achieving 84% on UD test.

- Strength: Proposes a novel, data-driven method to detect systematic PoS annotation errors, revealing different error patterns than traditional taggers.
- Strength: Empirically supports that PoS information is distributed across multiple embedding components, with accuracy improving as components increase.
- Strength: Demonstrates practical utility for resource-poor languages by training an effective classifier with minimal manual tagging.
- Strength: Methodology, preprocessing, and evaluations are well-documented and comparisons with a traditional tagger (Stanford) are provided.
- Strength: Provides concrete examples of annotation inconsistencies (e.g., numerals, currency, adjectives/adverbs) that can inform guideline improvements.

- Weakness: Main claim about distributed PoS information is incremental relative to prior work linking embedding dimensions to linguistic features.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison with state-of-the-art PoS taggers on the UD Treebank, making the 84% figure hard to contextualize.
- Weakness: Claim that information is spread over dozens of components is overstated given the large accuracy jump between 2 and 3 components.
- Weakness: No verification that identified "annotation errors" are true errors rather than mistakes by the distributional classifier.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to English, so cross-lingual generalizability is untested.

- Question: Why is there a large accuracy jump between 2 and 3 components, and how does that align with the claim of distributed PoS information?
- Question: How does the 84% UD Treebank accuracy compare to current state-of-the-art PoS taggers on the same dataset?
- Question: How did you validate that examples flagged as annotation errors are genuine annotation mistakes rather than classifier errors?
- Question: Which specific vector components distinguish proper nouns from common nouns, given reported similarity and highlighted components for conjunctions?
- Question: How does the stochastic, nondeterministic nature of training affect the consistency of PoS-related patterns across different embedding runs?
