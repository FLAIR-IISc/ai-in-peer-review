- Word embeddings encode PoS information and can be used to predict PoS tags with high accuracy.
- The authors identify outlier words whose distributional patterns suggest alternative PoS affiliations or annotation inconsistencies.
- PoS-related information is distributed across dozens of embedding components rather than being localized.
- Robust experimental design using a large corpus and multiple evaluation metrics.
- Detailed analysis of outliers and errors that illuminates complexities of PoS boundaries.
- Potential applications include improving PoS taggers for low-resource languages and detecting annotation errors.
- Experiments are limited to English, so cross-linguistic generalizability is unclear.
- Results may be sensitive to the specific hyperparameters used for training the distributional model.
- No comparison to state-of-the-art PoS taggers, leaving relative effectiveness unassessed.
- How will the method be extended to other languages and what challenges are anticipated?
- Please provide detailed hyperparameters and explain how they were chosen.
- How will the authors address annotation errors and what strategies will improve PoS tagging accuracy?
