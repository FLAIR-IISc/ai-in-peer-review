- Summary: Trains embeddings on BNC (lemma+PoS and lemma-only) to predict PoS, analyzes misclassifications to reveal graded PoS boundaries/annotation inconsistencies, and uses ANOVA to study PoS information across embedding dimensions.

- Contribution 1: Shows embeddings encode PoS strongly enough to recover PoS for most words and to identify systematic boundary cases and likely annotation issues.

- Contribution 2: Proposes a simple contextless PoS predictor trained on a small set of prototypical lemmas that attains reasonable accuracy on UD tokens, useful for low-resource bootstrapping.

- Contribution 3: Provides evidence that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized to a few.

- Strength: Clear, data-driven outlier analysis that surfaces interpretable linguistic boundary phenomena and plausible corpus tagging inconsistencies.

- Strength: Demonstrates robustness across datasets and settings with strong cross-validation, held-out BNC test-set, and high coverage on UD lemmas.

- Strength: Method is simple and reproducible; lemma-only embeddings plus logistic regression yield competitive contextless tagging (≈0.84 token accuracy) with minimal supervision.

- Strength: Feature-ranking results align with expectations—accuracy rises with more top dimensions and plateaus around ~100, supporting distributed PoS cues and identifying interpretable dimensions (e.g., conjunctions).

- Strength: Comparative analysis shows complementary error profiles to a mainstream tagger, arguing for diagnostic value beyond raw accuracy.

- Weakness: Primary experiment uses lemma+PoS tokens, which may inflate separability and complicate claims about discovering PoS from semantics; outlier analysis should be repeated with lemma-only embeddings.

- Weakness: Cross-validation may overestimate performance if folds are instance-level (not type-level) due to frequency-weighted repetitions; fold construction needs clarification and type-level CV.

- Weakness: Comparison to Stanford tagger is unbalanced since the tagger is evaluated on isolated tokens and with tagset mapping; a fairer baseline is needed.

- Weakness: Novelty is modest given prior work on embeddings clustering by PoS; main novelty is systematic misclassification analysis and lightweight predictor, which is incremental.

- Weakness: Limited validation of claimed annotation errors and feature analysis—manual validation and test-set feature-ranking curves are needed to rule out overfitting.

- Question: Can you replicate outlier/error analyses using lemma-only embeddings and recover similar boundary cases and inconsistencies?

- Question: How were cross-validation folds constructed—by type or by repeated instances—and can you report type-level CV results?

- Question: Can you report test-set accuracy for the feature-ranking (top-k dimensions) experiments rather than training accuracy?

- Question: How sensitive are findings to embedding hyperparameters (window size, dimension, negative samples) and preprocessing choices?

- Question: Why use a 1/2 dominance threshold to define “unambiguous” lemmas for the lemma-only predictor, and how does accuracy change with stricter thresholds?

- Question: Can you provide manual validation or external evidence for claimed BNC inconsistencies (e.g., adjectives tagged as adverbs like “plain/clear/sharp”)?

- Overall: Paper is clearly written and technically sound with useful analytical insights, but would be strengthened by lemma-only outlier analysis, CV clarifications, fairer baselines, and manual validation of annotation-error claims.
