- Paper studies how BNC-trained word embeddings relate to PoS by training multinomial logistic classifiers on lemma+PoS embeddings and on lemma-only embeddings.
- Authors analyze systematic misclassifications to reveal PoS boundary phenomena and annotation inconsistencies, and show PoS info is distributed across many embedding dimensions.
- Contribution: systematic error analysis showing distributional representations can surface borderline PoS cases and corpus tagging errors (e.g., ADJ vs ADV, numerals vs nouns).
- Contribution: empirical evidence that PoS information is broadly distributed across embedding dimensions via a top-k feature study.
- Contribution: a simple context-free PoS predictor trained from a small set of prototypical lexemes that achieves reasonable accuracy and could bootstrap low-resource settings.
- Strength: insightful, data-driven analysis that yields linguistically interpretable patterns (e.g., passive participles leaning toward ADJ; nominal modifiers trending ADJ).
- Strength: identification of systematic BNC tagging errors strengthens case for using this method for corpus auditing.
- Strength: cross-resource evaluation on UD English reduces overfitting concerns and shows similar tendencies beyond BNC.
- Strength: methodological simplicity and transparency using skip-gram embeddings and logistic regression.
- Strength: frequency-weighting to balance closed vs open classes is reasonable and practically motivated.
- Strength: contrastive baseline with a mainstream tagger suggests complementary auditing value, despite limitations.
- Strength: feature-importance study (ANOVA ranking) shows performance grows with more features and plateaus around ~100 dimensions.
- Weakness: methodological circularity/data leakage when training embeddings on lemma_POS tokens—contexts carry gold PoS, inflating separability and undermining high CV F-score.
- Weakness: 10-fold CV is over instances of the same vector type (weighted by frequency), causing identical vectors to appear in train and test folds.
- Weakness: UD evaluation protocol is unclear—unclear whether UD inputs were lemma_POS or bare lemmas, raising OOV and invalid evaluation concerns.
- Weakness: Stanford tagger baseline is incomparable because it was run on isolated tokens without sentence context, making the comparison unfair.
- Weakness: outlier lists lack human validation and quantitative precision/recall measures; analysis is largely anecdotal.
- Weakness: narrow scope—results only on English BNC/UD with one embedding config; lacks ablations (window size, subsampling) and non-English tests.
- Weakness: top-k feature study reports training accuracy only; generalization vs k should be reported.
- Question: can the main outlier analysis be redone with embeddings trained on lemmas only to remove circularity and confirm findings?
- Question: clarify exact form of inputs for UD evaluation when classifier was trained on lemma_POS embeddings.
- Question: report leave-lexeme-out cross-validation to avoid identical-vector leakage and provide resulting performance.
- Question: show dev/test performance vs k for feature selection and whether ANOVA ranking on different splits changes results.
- Question: consider a small-scale human validation of sampled flagged outliers to quantify detection precision.
- Additional: fix citation/bibliography formatting and clarify figure references.
- Additional: releasing mapping from BNC tags to Universal tags, trained models, and misclassified word lists would improve reproducibility and impact.
