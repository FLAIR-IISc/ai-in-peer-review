- Paper shows embeddings can predict PoS with high accuracy; misclassifications reveal graded category/boundary phenomena and annotation inconsistencies; PoS signal is distributed across many embedding dimensions.  
- Main contributions claimed: (1) linguistically informed error analysis exposing boundaries and corpus issues, (2) a simple interpretable probing method showing recoverable PoS info, (3) a lemma-only no-context classifier useful for low-resource bootstrapping.  
- Strength: Novel diagnostic use of misclassifications to highlight PoS boundaries and BNC annotation problems (e.g., numerals, ADJ/NOUN alternations, mis-tagged adverbs).  
- Strength: Robust empirical performance across datasets (high F-scores on unseen BNC band and UD lemmas) indicating stable distributional cues.  
- Strength: Clear, interpretable probing setup (logistic regression over embedding dims), frequency-weighting to handle class imbalance, and prototypical class vector analyses.  
- Strength: Comparison to a standard tagger shows complementary error patterns, highlighting different diagnostic value.  
- Strength: Preliminary ANOVA-based analysis indicates PoS signal spreads across many (~100+) dimensions rather than a few.  
- Weakness: Circularity/leakage: training embeddings on lemma+PoS tokens bakes gold tags into representations, making the main experiment unsurprising.  
- Weakness: Cross-validation likely inflated by instance-level folds that allow the same word type in train and test; type-level splits are required.  
- Weakness: Missing strong baselines (majority-tag-per-lemma, bag-of-context counts, char n-grams, Brown clusters/SVD) to quantify embedding gains.  
- Weakness: Interpretability claims about individual dimensions are fragile under rotations; need rotation-invariant or sparse-probe analyses (orthogonal rotations, L1 probes, CCA).  
- Weakness: Stanford tagger comparison is unfair because a contextual tagger fed isolated tokens is not a meaningful baseline and tagset conversion complicates interpretation.  
- Weakness: Limited engagement with classic and recent PoS induction and probing literature; related work coverage should be expanded.  
- Question: Can the main BNC experiment be redone with plain-token embeddings (no PoS in token identity) to remove leakage concerns?  
- Question: Were CV splits done by word type? If not, please rerun type-level CV and report updated scores.  
- Question: What is the majority-tag-per-lemma baseline performance for the UD token evaluation compared to the reported 0.84?  
- Question: Are lemmas lowercased, and could capitalization explain PROPN vs NOUN performance? Please control for case.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters (window size, subsampling, dimensionality); any ablation results?  
- Question: Can you test rotation robustness of the distributed-signal claim (random orthogonal rotations and re-running k-best feature curves)?  
- Additional suggestion: Release code and ranked lists of suspect items per tag to aid corpus repair and reproducibility.  
- Additional suggestion: Extending experiments to typologically diverse, morphologically rich languages would strengthen generality of graded PoS findings.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept — valuable linguistic diagnostics and lemma-only probe but methodological issues (PoS-augmented embeddings, CV leakage, missing baselines) must be addressed.
