- Paper examines how pre-trained word embeddings encode PoS by training a logistic classifier on embeddings and analyzing its errors to find linguistic and annotation phenomena.
- Misclassified words are treated as "distributional outliers" revealing PoS boundary cases, annotation inconsistencies, and systematic corpus errors.
- Secondary experiment shows a context-independent PoS tagger using embeddings achieves reasonable accuracy; analysis shows PoS signals are distributed across many embedding dimensions.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel diagnostic methodology using a simple classifier to detect corpus annotation issues via distributional models.
- Contribution: Provides detailed qualitative linguistic insights from distributional outliers (e.g., passive participles, numerals vs. nouns).
- Contribution: Empirically demonstrates PoS information is highly distributed in embeddings, with accuracy improving smoothly up to ~100 dimensions.

- Strength: Excellent, in-depth qualitative error analysis linking model errors to real linguistic phenomena and annotation mistakes.
- Strength: Sound experimental design choices (lemma_POS tokenization, frequency-weighted training) to handle polysemy and class imbalance.
- Strength: Novel framing that repurposes classification as a tool for linguistic discovery and annotation quality control.
- Strength: Useful comparison with the Stanford Tagger showing complementary error profiles and added value of the method.

- Weakness: Potential circularity because embeddings are trained on lemma_POS tokens, so neighbors include PoS tags and may bias classifier performance and outlier interpretation.
- Weakness: Context-independent tagger experiment is underdeveloped—selection of "unambiguous" lemmas is insufficiently justified and lacks a simple baseline comparison.
- Weakness: Paper omits important implementation and reproducibility details (classifier hyperparameters, mapping decisions from BNC to Universal tags).

- Question: Do the authors believe training on lemma_POS tokens causes the model to learn tag co-occurrence patterns, and how would results differ with embeddings from untagged text?
- Question: Can the authors report a baseline that assigns each lemma its most frequent tag to contextualize the 0.84 accuracy in Section 5?
- Question: Based on findings, what concrete recommendation would the authors make to improve BNC annotation guidelines (e.g., tagging of years, currency, passive participles)?
