- Novel methodology: use embedding-based classifier errors to audit PoS tagsets and find words with atypical distributional behavior.  
- Qualitative analysis: identifies linguistically interesting boundary cases and systematic annotation inconsistencies in the BNC.  
- Strength — innovative framing: repurposes classification failures as a tool for linguistic discovery, with compelling error analysis.  
- Strength — thorough comparison: contrasts findings with a conventional PoS tagger and evaluates on multiple datasets (BNC and UD Treebank).  
- Strength — clarity and reproducibility: experiments are well-described, justified, and clearly written.  
- Weakness — limited novelty: encoding of PoS in embeddings is already established in prior work.  
- Weakness — potential confound: training embeddings on lemma+PoS (e.g., love_VERB) may bake PoS distinctions into representations and simplify the task.  
- Weakness — narrow scope: experiments are limited to English, reducing claims about broader applicability to other languages.
