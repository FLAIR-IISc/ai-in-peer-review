**Summary**

This paper explores the relationship between word embeddings and part-of-speech (PoS) classes. The authors train a classifier on word embeddings to predict PoS tags and analyze the errors to identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS boundaries. They find that word embeddings contain information about PoS classes and can be used to detect inconsistencies in annotation and to identify words with unique distributional patterns.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel application of word embeddings to PoS prediction**: The authors demonstrate that word embeddings can be used to predict PoS tags with high accuracy, even when the classifier is trained on a limited set of words.
2. **Identification of words with unique distributional patterns**: The authors analyze the errors made by the classifier and identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS boundaries, highlighting the complexity of PoS classification.
3. **Insights into PoS annotation inconsistencies**: The authors show that their approach can be used to detect inconsistencies in PoS annotation, including errors and inconsistencies in the annotation of specific word types.

**Strengths**

1. **Robust experimental design**: The authors use a robust experimental design, including a large dataset and multiple evaluation metrics, to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
2. **Insightful error analysis**: The authors provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by the classifier, which provides insight into the complexity of PoS classification and the limitations of traditional annotation schemes.
3. **Novel application of word embeddings**: The authors demonstrate a novel application of word embeddings to PoS prediction, which has the potential to improve the accuracy of PoS taggers.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation metrics**: The authors primarily evaluate their approach using accuracy and F-score, which may not capture the full complexity of PoS classification.
2. **Limited comparison to existing approaches**: The authors do not provide a detailed comparison of their approach to existing PoS taggers, which makes it difficult to evaluate the relative effectiveness of their approach.
3. **Limited discussion of implications**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of the implications of their findings, including the potential applications and limitations of their approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to other languages and datasets?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the specific features of the word embeddings that are most informative for PoS prediction?
3. How do the authors plan to address the limitations of their approach, including the potential for overfitting and the need for more robust evaluation metrics?
