- Summary: Trains word embeddings on the BNC to predict PoS tags and detect words that deviate from traditional PoS categories, using error analysis to reveal annotation issues.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates that distributional semantic models can predict PoS affiliation with high accuracy and expose ambiguous cases.

- Contribution 2: Uses embeddings to identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS categories, aiding annotation refinement.

- Contribution 3: Analyzes model errors to uncover inconsistencies in annotation guidelines and improve tagging quality.

- Strength 1: Proposes a novel embedding-based approach to PoS tagging.

- Strength 2: Reports high predictive accuracy, including on ambiguous or inconsistently annotated items.

- Strength 3: Offers actionable insights into annotation inconsistencies that can inform guideline improvements.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to English; cross-linguistic applicability is not explored.

- Weakness 2: Method relies on high-quality BNC annotations, which may not exist for other corpora or languages.

- Weakness 3: Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art PoS tagging models, hindering assessment of relative performance.

- Question 1: How will the approach be extended to other languages and what challenges are anticipated?

- Question 2: How exactly will the insights be used to refine annotation processes or guidelines?

- Question 3: How will the method be compared to state-of-the-art PoS models and which evaluation metrics will be used?
