**Summary of the Paper**

This paper explores the relationship between word embeddings and part-of-speech (PoS) tags in natural language processing. The authors train a distributional semantic model on the British National Corpus (BNC) and use the resulting word embeddings to predict PoS tags for words. They find that the word embeddings contain information about PoS affiliation and can be used to identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS categories. The authors also analyze the errors made by the model and find that they often reveal inconsistencies in the annotation process or guidelines.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Demonstrating the effectiveness of distributional semantic models in predicting PoS tags**: The authors show that word embeddings can be used to predict PoS tags with high accuracy, even in cases where the traditional annotation is inconsistent or ambiguous.
2. **Identifying words that do not conform to traditional PoS categories**: The authors use the model to identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS categories, which can be useful for refining the annotation process or guidelines.
3. **Analyzing the errors made by the model**: The authors analyze the errors made by the model and find that they often reveal inconsistencies in the annotation process or guidelines, which can be useful for improving the accuracy of PoS tagging.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to PoS tagging**: The authors propose a novel approach to PoS tagging that uses distributional semantic models to predict PoS tags.
2. **High accuracy**: The authors achieve high accuracy in predicting PoS tags, even in cases where the traditional annotation is inconsistent or ambiguous.
3. **Insights into annotation inconsistencies**: The authors provide insights into annotation inconsistencies and guidelines, which can be useful for improving the accuracy of PoS tagging.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited scope**: The authors only focus on English language and do not explore the applicability of their approach to other languages.
2. **Dependence on high-quality annotation**: The authors rely on high-quality annotation of the BNC, which may not be available for other corpora or languages.
3. **Lack of comparison with state-of-the-art models**: The authors do not compare their approach with state-of-the-art models for PoS tagging, which makes it difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Questions to Authors**

2. Can you provide more details on how you plan to refine the annotation process or guidelines based on the insights gained from your approach?
3. How do you plan to compare your approach with state-of-the-art models for PoS tagging, and what evaluation metrics do you plan to use?
