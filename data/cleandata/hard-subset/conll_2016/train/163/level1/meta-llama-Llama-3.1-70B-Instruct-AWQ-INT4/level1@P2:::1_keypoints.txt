Summary: BNC-trained word embeddings predict PoS tags, reveal words that cross PoS boundaries, and expose annotation inconsistencies.

Contribution 1: Shows word embeddings encode PoS affiliation usable for PoS prediction.

Contribution 2: Uses classifier outputs to identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS categories, aiding annotation refinement.

Contribution 3: Error analysis demonstrates classifier mistakes can flag inconsistencies in corpus annotation.

Strength 1: Introduces a novel embedding-based approach to PoS tagging.

Strength 2: Validated with large-scale experiments on the BNC.

Strength 3: Provides in-depth error analysis that yields linguistic and annotation insights.

Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to accuracy and F-score, omitting broader metrics.

Weakness 2: No comparison to state-of-the-art PoS tagging methods, hindering effectiveness assessment.

Weakness 3: Hyperparameter choices and tuning are underanalyzed and underreported.

Question 1: How will the approach be extended to other languages and corpora?

Question 2: Can the authors provide full details of hyperparameters and their tuning?

Question 3: How will the authors address limited evaluation metrics and the absence of SOTA comparisons?
