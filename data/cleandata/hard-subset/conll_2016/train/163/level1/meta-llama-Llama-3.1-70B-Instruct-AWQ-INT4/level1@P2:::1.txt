**Summary of the Paper**

This paper explores the relationship between word embeddings and part-of-speech (PoS) tags. The authors train a distributional semantic model on the British National Corpus (BNC) and use the resulting word embeddings to predict PoS tags. They demonstrate that the word embeddings contain information about PoS affiliation and can be used to identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS boundaries. The authors also analyze the errors made by their classifier and show that they can be used to identify inconsistencies in the annotation process.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Demonstration of PoS information in word embeddings**: The authors show that word embeddings trained on a large corpus can capture information about PoS affiliation, which can be used to predict PoS tags.
2. **Identification of words that do not conform to traditional PoS boundaries**: The authors use their classifier to identify words that do not conform to traditional PoS boundaries, which can be used to improve the annotation process.
3. **Analysis of errors made by the classifier**: The authors analyze the errors made by their classifier and show that they can be used to identify inconsistencies in the annotation process.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to PoS tagging**: The authors propose a novel approach to PoS tagging that uses word embeddings to capture information about PoS affiliation.
2. **Large-scale experimentation**: The authors conduct large-scale experimentation on the BNC, which demonstrates the effectiveness of their approach.
3. **In-depth analysis of errors**: The authors provide an in-depth analysis of the errors made by their classifier, which provides insights into the limitations of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation metrics**: The authors only report accuracy and F-score as evaluation metrics, which may not provide a complete picture of the performance of their approach.
2. **Lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The authors do not compare their approach to state-of-the-art methods in PoS tagging, which makes it difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of their approach.
3. **Limited analysis of hyperparameters**: The authors do not provide an in-depth analysis of the hyperparameters used in their approach, which may affect the performance of their method.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to other languages and corpora?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameters used in their approach and how they were tuned?
3. How do the authors plan to address the limitations of their approach, such as the limited evaluation metrics and lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods?
