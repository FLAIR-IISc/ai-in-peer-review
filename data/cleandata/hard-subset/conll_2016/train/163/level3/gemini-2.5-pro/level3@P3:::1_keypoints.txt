- Summary: Trains Skip-gram on a lemmatized BNC with UD POS tags and uses logistic regression to predict POS, focusing on classifier errors to argue for graded POS membership and showing small unambiguous-word classifiers can predict POS without context.  
- Contribution: Uses classifier errors as a tool to empirically probe POS class boundaries, providing data-driven evidence (e.g., years as nouns, passive participles as adjectives, BNC annotation inconsistencies).  
- Contribution: Quantitatively grounds the notion of "graded" POS membership by identifying distributionally ambiguous words and operationalizing POS continua in embedding space.  
- Strength: Insightful linguistic analysis that treats misclassifications as meaningful, with detailed, convincing examples relevant to corpus-based linguistics.  
- Strength: Sound and clearly presented experiments (BNC, cross-validation, out-of-domain testing) with lucid writing and well-organized results.  
- Strength: Novel application of a standard technique—repurposing embeddings+logistic regression to reveal POS boundary cases complementary to standard taggers.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—uses standard Skip-gram + logistic regression and reconfirms known that vectors encode POS, so novelty is mainly analytical.  
- Weakness: Insufficient engagement with related work; literature review misses recent embedding-aware POS induction and tagging studies.  
- Weakness: Potentially confusing initial experimental setup (training on lemma_POS strings); motivation and implications need clearer justification.  
- Question: Are the identified outlier phenomena robust across other embedding architectures and hyperparameter settings (e.g., GloVe, FastText), or specific to the Skip-gram model used?
