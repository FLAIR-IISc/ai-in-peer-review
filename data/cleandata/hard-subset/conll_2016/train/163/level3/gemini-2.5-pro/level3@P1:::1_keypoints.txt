- Goal: probe how much POS information is encoded in word embeddings by training a classifier and analyzing its errors.  
- Method: train Skip-gram embeddings on a lemmatized BNC (lemma_POS) and use logistic regression to predict POS tags.  
- Key finding: classifier errors expose linguistic ambiguities, annotation inconsistencies in the BNC, and POS boundary words.  
- Additional experiment: training on raw-lemma embeddings shows a context-agnostic tagger still achieves reasonable performance.  
- Conclusion: POS information is robustly present but diffusely distributed across many embedding dimensions.  
- Contribution 1: detailed qualitative error analysis evidencing graded/soft POS membership and identifying outlier words and corpus issues.  
- Contribution 2: empirical evidence that POS affiliation is distributed across many vector dimensions, supporting distributed representation.  
- Strength: experiments are simple, clear, well-executed, and methodologically sound for an analysis-focused study.  
- Strength: deep, insightful qualitative analysis (Section 4) yielding concrete linguistic observations and useful comparisons to the Stanford Tagger.  
- Strength: provides solid empirical grounding for the idea that POS categories form a continuum and a method to find boundary cases.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty for a venue like CONLL—uses standard word2vec + logistic regression pipeline.  
- Weakness: related work section is sparse and omits key contemporary neural POS-tagging literature that uses embeddings.  
- Weakness: narrow empirical scope—single corpus (BNC), language (English), and model (Skip-gram), limiting generalizability.  
- Question: clarify the paper’s specific novelty and how the linguistic analysis advances NLP research for CONLL readers.  
- Question: explain how the isolated-embedding analysis relates to and could inform context-aware, embedding-based sequence taggers.  
- Question: provide details on why clustering word vectors failed and why a supervised classifier is a better probing method.
