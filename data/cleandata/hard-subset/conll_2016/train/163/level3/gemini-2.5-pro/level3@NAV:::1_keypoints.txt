- Paper studies how distributional word embeddings encode POS by training vectors on the BNC and using logistic regression to predict POS, arguing embeddings reflect graded POS affiliation and reveal annotation inconsistencies.
- Contribution: strong empirical analysis using a simple classifier to provide quantitative evidence that POS categories are fuzzy/continuous, with detailed examination of outlier words (e.g., passive participles).
- Contribution: demonstrates using distributional models as a practical tool for corpus error analysis, identifying systematic inconsistencies in the BNC.
- Strength: insightful, linguistically-grounded error analysis that interprets misclassifications to reveal phenomena like adjectival behavior and boundary-straddling words.
- Strength: paper is clear, well-written, and reproducible, with transparent methodology and effective presentation of results.
- Strength: provides empirical support for linguistic theory that POS categories exist on a continuum, operationalized via distributional models.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—using standard embeddings plus a simple classifier is unsurprising given prior work showing embeddings encode syntactic information.
- Weakness: insufficient literature review—fails to cite and discuss relevant recent work on POS tagging/induction with embeddings (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).
