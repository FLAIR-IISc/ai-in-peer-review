- Paper analyzes how pre-trained word embeddings encode PoS by training a logistic classifier to predict PoS from BNC-trained vectors and qualitatively examining misclassifications as "outliers."

- Contribution: Detailed qualitative error analysis showing misclassifications reveal fuzzy PoS boundaries and annotation inconsistencies in the BNC.

- Contribution: Demonstrates that distributional classifiers uncover different annotation errors than sequence-based taggers, offering a complementary tool for corpus analysis.

- Strength: Strong, linguistically insightful qualitative analysis identifying phenomena like passive participles and currency expressions causing PoS ambiguity.

- Strength: Novel and useful comparison to the Stanford PoS Tagger showing qualitatively different error patterns between distributional and context-aware models.

- Strength: Clear experimental setup with high performance (F-score 0.979 train, 0.91 test), supporting the claim that embeddings encode PoS information.

- Weakness: Omits related work on unsupervised PoS induction and clustering (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al., Blunsom & Cohn, Yatbaz et al.), making Section 5 appear non-novel.

- Weakness: Lacks connection to downstream tasks; no evidence that distributionally-refined PoS categories improve applications like parsing or NER.

- Weakness: Methodological issues—mischaracterizes embeddings as "mostly semantic," focuses only on frequent words ignoring the low-frequency tail, and potentially loses detail by mapping to coarse Universal PoS tags.

- Question: Please situate the work within unsupervised PoS induction literature and clarify differences from prior distributional clustering approaches.

- Question: Expand on the failed clustering attempts noted in Footnote 3—methods tried, failure criteria, and reasons for favoring supervised classification.

- Question: Can distributionally-derived PoS categories improve downstream tasks (e.g., dependency parsing); please provide evaluation or discussion.
