- Trains Skip-gram on BNC with lemma_POS tokens and probes embeddings with logistic regression to analyze PoS encoding, focusing on classifier errors as evidence for graded PoS and annotation issues; also shows a seed-based tagger can work on untagged text.

- Main contribution: qualitative, data-driven analysis linking distributional semantics to graded PoS membership via systematic error inspection.

- Main contribution: using misclassifications to detect corpus annotation inconsistencies (e.g., currency amounts, years) in the BNC.

- Strength: clear, focused writing and a straightforward experimental setup that emphasizes linguistic analysis over performance.

- Strength: thorough, insightful error analysis with concrete examples and effective summary tables.

- Strength: comparison with the Stanford PoS tagger demonstrates complementary error patterns and the method’s unique utility for corpus analysis.

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—relies on standard Skip-gram and logistic regression without new algorithms.

- Weakness: insufficient engagement with relevant related work on embeddings for PoS tagging (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).

- Weakness: experimental design potentially confounds results because training on lemma_POS strings injects PoS information, raising questions about whether findings generalize to embeddings trained on raw text.

- Question: how would the set of outliers and linguistic insights change if embeddings were trained on raw (untagged) lemmas—was this analysis done?

- Question: how do the paper’s findings complement or differ from insights from neural PoS-tagging models, and what distinct value does the simpler probing approach provide?
