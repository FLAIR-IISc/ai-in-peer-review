- Paper studies how pre-trained word embeddings encode PoS information using a logistic regression classifier trained on BNC vectors.  
- Finds embeddings contain substantial PoS signal with high classification accuracy and focuses on detailed error analysis of misclassified "outliers."  
- Error analysis uncovers graded PoS membership (e.g., passive participles as adjectives), annotation inconsistencies in the BNC, and systematic subclass differences (e.g., years vs. numerals).  

- Contribution: Provides a thorough qualitative error analysis that uses classification errors to probe fuzzy PoS boundaries and uncovers linguistically interesting phenomena.  
- Contribution: Proposes a simple, replicable method using distributional models to identify annotation inconsistencies and complements standard tagger error analyses.  

- Strength: Insightful, detailed error analysis that reveals nuanced syntactic behavior and treats errors as linguistically informative.  
- Strength: Use of a simple logistic regression as a transparent analytical probe, avoiding confounds from complex architectures.  
- Strength: Valuable comparison with the Stanford PoS Tagger showing complementary error patterns.  

- Weakness: Fails to engage key prior work on unsupervised/distributional PoS induction, overstating novelty (re-implements prior ideas without citation).  
- Weakness: Misleading framing and weak motivation—does not connect graded PoS findings to downstream task utility and mischaracterizes prior work on embeddings.  
- Weakness: Methodological limitations—coarse UPOS mapping may obscure finer distinctions, focus on frequent words misses long-tail challenges, and some claims (e.g., proper vs. common nouns) lack deeper justification.  

- Question: Clarify footnote on failed clustering attempts—who ran them, which algorithms, and what constituted failure; did you try Brown clustering?  
- Question: Explain the decision to map BNC tags to coarse UPOS despite aiming to find nuanced, graded distinctions.  
- Question: Clarify the novel contribution of the Section 5 PoS predictor given the existing literature on distributional PoS induction.  

- Additional comment: Use conventional in-text citation commands (\citet/\newcite) instead of the current sentential citation style for readability.
