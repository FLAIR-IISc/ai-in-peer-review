- Explores PoS information in distributional embeddings by training a classifier on BNC and analyzing classification errors to find atypical words.
- Main contribution is a detailed qualitative error analysis revealing graded class membership (e.g., participles as adjectives) and annotation inconsistencies (years, currency).
- Strength: Qualitative error analysis effectively treats misclassifications as probes to uncover linguistically meaningful patterns and fuzzy PoS boundaries.
- Strength: Paper is well-written with clearly described experiments; the classifier approach is straightforward and useful for generating outlier data.
- Weakness: Omits substantial prior work on unsupervised/distributional PoS induction, undermining novelty claims about embeddings containing PoS information.
- Weakness: Lacks motivation tied to downstream applications (e.g., parsing) and does not show practical utility of embedding-derived distinctions.
- Weakness: Analysis issues include an inaccurate claim that embeddings are “mostly semantic,” focus on frequent words only, and loss of fine-grained distinctions by mapping to UPOS.
