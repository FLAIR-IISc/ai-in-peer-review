- Paper studies how pre-trained word embeddings encode PoS by training a logistic regression to predict PoS and analyzing classifier errors to find distributional outliers.
- Main contribution is detailed qualitative error analysis showing graded PoS membership, annotation inconsistencies, and interesting linguistic subclasses (e.g., participles, years/currencies).
- Secondary contribution: attempt to build a standalone distributional PoS predictor, but presented without adequate engagement with prior work.
- Strength: strong qualitative error analysis that uncovers meaningful linguistic patterns and annotation issues.
- Strength: use of a simple, interpretable logistic regression probe is appropriate and replicable for corpus exploration.
- Strength: comparison with a standard tagger (Stanford PoS Tagger) shows distributional disagreements are complementary to sequence-tagging errors.
- Weakness: major omission of related work on unsupervised/distributional PoS induction (e.g., Brown clustering, Christodoulopoulos et al., Blunsom & Cohn, Yatbaz et al.).
- Weakness: no extrinsic evaluation showing downstream utility (e.g., parsing improvements) from correcting annotation outliers.
- Weakness: incorrect or oversimplified claims about embeddings being “mostly semantic” and questionable methodological choices (coarse UPOS mapping, overclaiming insights like proper vs. common noun separation).
- Weakness: focus on top 10k frequent words ignores long-tail/low-frequency vocabulary where PoS induction is harder.
- Weakness: unclear dismissal of clustering attempts (Footnote 3) and citation style/presentation issues.
- Questions: position Section 5 relative to extensive prior PoS induction literature and clarify novel contribution.
- Questions: clarify which clustering algorithms were tried, why they “failed,” and whether methods like Brown clustering were considered.
- Questions: consider/explain extrinsic evaluation — would downstream models (e.g., parsers) benefit from relabeling outlier words per the classifier?
