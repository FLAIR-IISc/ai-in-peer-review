- Claims: shows embeddings contain PoS information.  
- Claims: error analysis identifies annotation inconsistencies ("outliers").  
- Claims: PoS information is distributed across multiple embedding dimensions.  
- Strength: thorough error analysis that uncovers real annotation inconsistencies (e.g., proper vs. common nouns, verbs vs. adjectives).  
- Strength: cross-corpus validation on the Universal Dependencies Treebank adds credibility.  
- Strength: plausible finding that PoS information is distributed across vector dimensions.  
- Weakness: does not contextualize why the BNC was PoS-tagged (e.g., for parsing), making comparisons to parsing performance questionable.  
- Weakness: central claim lacks novelty given prior work showing embeddings encode PoS/syntactic information.  
- Weakness: claim that embeddings are “mostly semantic” contradicts prior evidence of substantial syntactic content.  
- Weakness: unjustified switch to UPOS; abandoning fine-grained tags may miss linguistically interesting distinctions.  
- Weakness: analysis restricted to high-frequency words, ignoring low-frequency tail where PoS ambiguity is likely greater.  
- Weakness: omission of key related work undermines the paper’s novelty and positioning.  
- Question: Why was the BNC PoS-tagging process not contextualized within its NLP/parsing purpose?  
- Question: How do these findings concretely improve parsing performance compared to traditional PoS tags?  
- Question: Why switch to UPOS without justification when fine-grained tags might better reveal linguistic phenomena?
