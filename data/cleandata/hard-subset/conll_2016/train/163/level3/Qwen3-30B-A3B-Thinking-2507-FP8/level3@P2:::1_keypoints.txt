- Empirical demonstration that BNC-trained word embeddings encode POS information, supporting graded POS boundaries rather than strict categories.
- Detailed error analysis revealing linguistic phenomena and annotation inconsistencies (e.g., proper/common noun confusions, participles behaving like adjectives, currency/number mislabels).
- Evidence that POS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (at least ~100 of 300), not localized to a few features.
- Strength: Insightful error analysis uncovers meaningful linguistic patterns and annotation issues beyond classifier performance.
- Strength: Convincing analysis showing POS signals are distributed across vector components (accuracy improves gradually with more features).
- Strength: Results validated on independent corpora (BNC test set and UD Treebank) demonstrating robustness.
- Weakness: Methodology is straightforward and lacks novelty relative to prior work using classifiers on embeddings.
- Weakness: Inadequate literature review—several relevant POS induction/embedding papers are not cited or discussed.
- Weakness: Limited practical relevance—no clear path shown for improving state-of-the-art POS taggers using the findings.
- Question: How does this work differentiate itself from existing POS induction research (e.g., Liu et al. 2016, Tsvetkov et al. 2015)?
- Question: Why is the finding that POS information is widely distributed across dimensions significant for understanding semantic vs. grammatical encoding?
- Question: How do you explain the remaining performance gap between your classifier (trained on BNC embeddings) and state-of-the-art POS taggers (>97% accuracy)?
