- Task and method: Trains a logistic classifier on word embeddings from a lemmatized BNC (UD POS tags) to predict POS categories.  
- Results: Achieves very high accuracy (F1 = 0.979 on cross-validation).  
- Contribution 1: Empirical validation that distributional vectors encode POS information.  
- Contribution 2: Shows outlier analysis on embedding-based predictions can reveal annotation inconsistencies.  
- Contribution 3: Finds POS information is distributed across multiple embedding dimensions, not localized.  
- Strength: Provides clear empirical evidence supporting POS as recoverable from distributional semantics, relevant to linguistic theory.  
- Strength: Evaluation is thorough and includes multiple corpora (BNC test set, UD Treebank).  
- Strength: Outlier analysis gives useful insights for improving POS-tagged corpora.  
- Weakness: Methodological novelty is limited; similar experiments exist (e.g., Tsvetkov et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015).  
- Weakness: Literature review omits key related work on POS induction with embeddings, weakening context.  
- Recommendation: Authors should add missing citations (Lin/Ling/Plank et al.) and clarify how findings extend prior work beyond empirical confirmation.
