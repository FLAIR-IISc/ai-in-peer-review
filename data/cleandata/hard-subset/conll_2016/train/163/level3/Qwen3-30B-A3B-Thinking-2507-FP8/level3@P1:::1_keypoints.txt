- The paper tests whether distributional word embeddings encode POS category information by training classifiers on BNC embeddings and analyzing errors and vector dimensions.
- Contribution: Shows embeddings robustly encode POS information, distributed across many vector dimensions rather than single features.
- Contribution: Uses misclassification analysis to expose linguistic inconsistencies and "soft" or graded POS boundaries in annotations.
- Contribution: Demonstrates a simple POS tagger can be built from static embeddings without explicit syntactic context.
- Strength: Thorough error analysis of 1,558 misclassified words reveals meaningful annotation issues (e.g., proper/common noun confusion, passive participles).
- Strength: Evaluation is comprehensive and consistent across datasets, achieving 0.91 F-score on unseen BNC and 0.99 on UD Treebank.
- Strength: Empirically shows POS information is spread over at least ~100 vector components, supporting the notion of distributed encoding of POS.
- Weakness: Methodology is straightforward and not novel, relying on standard embeddings and logistic regression.
- Weakness: Literature review is incomplete, omitting relevant prior work on embeddings for POS tagging (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).
- Weakness: The constructed POS tagger is basic and not competitive with state-of-the-art, limiting practical impact.
- Question: How would results change using contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT) instead of static embeddings?
- Question: Can the identified annotation inconsistencies be validated by linguistic experts to confirm true annotation errors?
- Question: How does the distribution of POS information across vector dimensions behave in other languages, especially morphologically complex ones?
