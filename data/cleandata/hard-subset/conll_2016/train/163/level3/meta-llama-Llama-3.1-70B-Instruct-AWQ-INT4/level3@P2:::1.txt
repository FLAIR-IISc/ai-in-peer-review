**Summary of the Paper**

This paper explores the relationship between word embeddings and part-of-speech (POS) tags. The authors train word embeddings on the British National Corpus (BNC) and use a logistic regression classifier to predict POS tags. They evaluate the performance of the classifier on the same corpus and on other corpora, and analyze the results to identify patterns and inconsistencies in POS tagging.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Empirical grounding for the notion of POS**: The paper provides empirical evidence that word embeddings contain information about POS tags, and that this information can be used to predict POS tags with high accuracy.
2. **Analysis of POS tagging inconsistencies**: The authors identify inconsistencies in POS tagging in the BNC and other corpora, and show that these inconsistencies can be detected using a distributional predictor.
3. **Robustness of POS information in word embeddings**: The paper demonstrates that POS information is distributed among many components of the word embeddings, and that this information is robust to different corpora and evaluation settings.

**Strengths of the Submission**

1. **Clear and well-written paper**: The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.
2. **Strong evaluation**: The authors evaluate the performance of the classifier on multiple corpora, and provide detailed analysis of the results.
3. **Interesting insights**: The paper provides interesting insights into the nature of POS tagging and the inconsistencies that can arise in different corpora.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

1. **Lack of novelty**: The paper uses straightforward experiments with no new NLP/ML methods, which may limit its impact on the field.
2. **Limited contribution to NLP/ML**: While the paper provides empirical grounding for the notion of POS, it does not contribute significantly to the development of new NLP/ML methods or techniques.
3. **Missing literature**: The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of the literature on POS tagging and induction using embeddings.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend this work to other languages, and what challenges do they anticipate in doing so?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the preprocessing steps used to prepare the data for training the word embeddings and classifier?
3. How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the current approach, such as the lack of novelty and limited contribution to NLP/ML?
