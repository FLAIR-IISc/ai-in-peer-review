- Investigates how word embeddings encode POS by training on BNC and using logistic regression to predict POS, evaluated on BNC and other corpora with analysis of tagging patterns and inconsistencies.  
- Empirical evidence that embeddings contain POS information and can predict POS tags with high accuracy.  
- Detects POS tagging inconsistencies in the BNC and other corpora using a distributional predictor.  
- Shows POS information is distributed across many embedding components and is robust across corpora and evaluation settings.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and well-organized.  
- Strong evaluation: tested on multiple corpora with detailed result analysis.  
- Provides interesting insights into POS nature and corpus inconsistencies.  
- Lacks novelty, relying on straightforward experiments without new NLP/ML methods.  
- Limited contribution to NLP/ML in terms of developing new methods or techniques.  
- Missing or incomplete literature review on POS tagging and induction using embeddings.  
- Question: How will the approach be extended to other languages and what challenges are anticipated?  
- Question: Request for more details on preprocessing for training embeddings and the classifier.  
- Question: How do the authors plan to address limitations such as lack of novelty and limited ML/NLP contribution?
