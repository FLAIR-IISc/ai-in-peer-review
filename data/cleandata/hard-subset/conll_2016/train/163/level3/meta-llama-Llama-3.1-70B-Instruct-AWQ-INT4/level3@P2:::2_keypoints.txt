- Paper trains a classifier on word embeddings to predict PoS tags and analyzes errors to find annotation inconsistencies and atypical words.
- Error analysis of outliers reveals annotation inconsistencies and words whose distributional patterns conflict with their PoS tags.
- Demonstrates that distributional vectors can predict PoS tags without needing pre-existing tag hints.
- Shows PoS information is distributed across embedding components, making it robust for prediction.
- Detailed error analysis helps understand limitations and biases of the approach.
- Novel application of distributional vectors for PoS prediction without tag supervision.
- Results are robust across datasets and evaluation metrics, increasing confidence in findings.
- Section 5 lacks novelty and omits substantial related work on distributional PoS induction.
- Incorrect claim that embedding relations are “mostly semantic,” ignoring evidence of syntactic information in embeddings.
- Unjustified switch to UPOS raises concerns about its suitability for the task.
- Analysis focuses on frequent word/tag pairs and lacks examination of low-frequency tail words.
- Request justification for switching to UPOS and its impact on results.
- Ask how authors will address limitations like reliance on frequent word/tag pairs.
- Request details on failed clustering attempts to match number of PoS tags.
- Ask how authors will explore alternatives to pure distributional and lemmatization-based morphological features for rare words.
- Paper is well-written with interesting results but has minor formatting issues and needs more discussion of implications and NLP applications.
