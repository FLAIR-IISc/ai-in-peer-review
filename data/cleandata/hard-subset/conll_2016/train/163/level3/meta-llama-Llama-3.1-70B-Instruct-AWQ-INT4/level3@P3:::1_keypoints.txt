- Paper investigates whether word embeddings encode POS information and can predict POS tags.  
- Uses BNC with UD POS tags; trains Continuous Skipgram embeddings and a logistic classifier to predict POS from word vectors.  
- Results: weighted F-score 0.979 on training set and 0.91 on unseen-word test set.  
- Classifier trained without sentence context achieves 0.938 training accuracy and 0.84 test accuracy.  
- Error analysis uncovers annotation inconsistencies and systematic patterns (e.g., proper vs common nouns, adjectives mislabeled as adverbs).  
- Shows distributional models can detect systematic errors in POS-tagged corpora.  
- Finds POS affiliation is distributed across many embedding components, not localized to a few features.  
- Main contribution: empirical grounding for POS categories via distributional word vectors.  
- Main contribution: using embeddings to reveal annotation errors and inconsistencies.  
- Main contribution: demonstrating distributed encoding of POS in embeddings.  
- Strength: clear, well-written paper with thorough discussion.  
- Strength: results clearly presented with robust evaluation (cross-validation and testing on other corpora).  
- Weakness: no new NLP/ML methods; experiments are relatively straightforward.  
- Weakness: incomplete citation of related embedding-based POS work (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).  
- Question: how will this approach generalize to other languages and corpora?  
- Question: can the authors detail which embedding components correspond to specific POS affiliations?  
- Question: how can this be applied practically, e.g., for POS tagging in resource-poor languages?  
- Additional comment: paper should discuss implications for linguistic theory and the nature of POS categories.  
- Additional comment: authors could explore other prediction models (e.g., RNNs or alternative ML methods).
