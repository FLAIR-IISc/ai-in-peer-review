- Paper investigates how word embeddings encode POS by training embeddings on the BNC and using logistic regression to predict POS tags; achieves high accuracy and error analysis reveals annotation inconsistencies.  
- Main contribution: strong empirical evidence that word embeddings contain POS information.  
- Main contribution: classifier error analysis uncovers patterns and ambiguity at POS boundaries.  
- Main contribution: POS prediction is robust even with limited training data.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and accessible.  
- Strength: empirical results are strong and validated across metrics and datasets.  
- Strength: error analysis provides novel insights into POS boundary complexity.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited; no new NLP/ML methods introduced.  
- Weakness: overall contribution to advancing NLP/ML methods is modest.  
- Weakness: relevant literature on POS tagging and induction with embeddings is under-cited.  
- Question: how will the approach generalize to other languages and what challenges are expected?  
- Question: request for more detail on POS boundary analysis and links to linguistic theory.  
- Question: how can these findings be used to improve POS tagging and induction in NLP applications?
