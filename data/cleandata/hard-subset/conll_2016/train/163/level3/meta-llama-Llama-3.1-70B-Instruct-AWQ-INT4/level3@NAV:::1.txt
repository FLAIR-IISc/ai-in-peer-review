**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Redefining part-of-speech classes with distributional semantic models" explores the relationship between word embeddings and part-of-speech (POS) tags. The authors train a Continuous Skipgram embedding model on the British National Corpus (BNC) and use a logistic regression classifier to predict POS tags from the word embeddings. The results show that the distributional vectors contain information about POS affiliation, and the classifier achieves high accuracy on both the training and test sets. The authors also analyze the errors made by the classifier and find that they often reveal interesting patterns and inconsistencies in the annotation process.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Empirical grounding for POS classes**: The paper provides empirical evidence that distributional semantic models can capture information about POS classes, which is a fundamental concept in linguistics.
2. **Robustness of word embeddings**: The authors demonstrate that word embeddings can be used to predict POS tags with high accuracy, even when the classifier is trained on a limited set of data.
3. **Analysis of errors**: The paper provides a detailed analysis of the errors made by the classifier, which reveals interesting patterns and inconsistencies in the annotation process.

**Strengths**

1. **Clear and well-written**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and methodology.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors provide strong empirical evidence to support their claims, including high accuracy results on both the training and test sets.
3. **Interesting analysis of errors**: The paper provides a detailed analysis of the errors made by the classifier, which reveals interesting patterns and inconsistencies in the annotation process.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of novelty**: The paper's methodology and results are not particularly new or surprising, as similar experiments have been conducted in the past.
2. **Limited generalizability**: The paper's results are based on a single corpus (BNC) and may not generalize to other corpora or languages.
3. **Overemphasis on accuracy**: The paper focuses primarily on the accuracy of the classifier, without exploring other important aspects of POS tagging, such as the impact of different hyperparameters or the use of different machine learning models.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend their research to other languages and corpora?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the analysis of errors and how they plan to use this information to improve the annotation process?
3. How do the authors think their results can be used to inform the development of more accurate POS taggers?
