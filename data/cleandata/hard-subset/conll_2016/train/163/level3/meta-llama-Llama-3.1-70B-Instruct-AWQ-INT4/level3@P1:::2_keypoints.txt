- Summary: The paper links PoS tags and word embeddings by training a classifier on embeddings to predict PoS, analyzing errors to find annotation outliers, and showing PoS information is distributed across embedding components.

- Contribution 1: Detailed error analysis identifies outliers and systematic inconsistencies in PoS annotations useful for improving tagging.

- Contribution 2: Empirical investigation shows PoS information is spread across multiple embedding components rather than confined to a single dimension.

- Contribution 3: Development of a PoS tagger that uses word embeddings as features, offering an alternative to traditional taggers.

- Strength 1: Provides a thorough error analysis that can help detect and correct annotation and tagging errors.

- Strength 2: Offers insight into how syntactic information (PoS) is represented in distributional embeddings.

- Strength 3: Builds and evaluates an embedding-based PoS tagger, demonstrating practical utility.

- Weakness 1: Results lack novelty, echoing prior work on the relationship between embeddings and PoS tagging.

- Weakness 2: Omits substantial related work in distributional PoS induction, weakening contextualization of contributions.

- Weakness 3: The switch to UPOS tags is questionable since finer-grained original tag distinctions may be more informative.

- Weakness 4: Minor formatting issues (e.g., citation style) distract from presentation quality.

- Question 1: Please justify the claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns beyond differences in function-word distributions.

- Question 2: How will you address classifier errors on low-frequency tail words?

- Question 3: Can you explore alternatives to pure distributional and lemmatization-based morphological features to better generalize PoS for rare words?

- Question 4: How do you plan to extend experiments to typologically different languages beyond English?
