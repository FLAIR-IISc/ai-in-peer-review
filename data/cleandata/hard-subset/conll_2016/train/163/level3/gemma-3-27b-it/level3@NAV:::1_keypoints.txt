- Study goal: investigates how distributional semantic models (word embeddings) relate to POS tags by predicting POS from embeddings trained on lemmatized BNC annotated with UD tags.  
- Method: train embeddings on BNC, use a logistic classifier to predict POS, evaluate with cross-validation and tests on independent corpora.  
- Main result: embeddings encode POS information and can identify words whose distributional patterns disagree with their POS labels.  
- Primary contribution: empirical validation and quantification of the link between distributional semantics and grammatical categories.  
- Secondary contribution: detailed error analysis revealing annotation inconsistencies and ‘soft’ boundaries between POS categories.  
- Additional observation: POS information is distributed across many embedding components rather than concentrated in a few.  
- Strength — evaluation: robust experimental setup with F-score metrics, cross-validation, and testing on multiple corpora.  
- Strength — presentation: paper is well-written with clear explanations and logical flow.  
- Strength — error analysis: misclassification analysis provides valuable linguistic insights and highlights annotation issues.  
- Strength — empirical grounding: supports the view that POS categories are often continuum-like rather than discrete.  
- Weakness — novelty: core methodology (train embeddings + classifier) is straightforward and not methodologically novel.  
- Weakness — data dependence: results rely on BNC and UD annotations; tagging conventions and quality may affect findings.  
- Weakness — component analysis: limited exploration of which specific vector components are most informative for POS.  
- Question: clarify the limitations of using BNC’s CLAWS4-based tagging as a gold standard given observed inconsistencies.  
- Question: discuss challenges of applying this approach to low-resource languages, especially obtaining initial manually tagged examples.  
- Question: speculate which linguistic features are captured by the most informative embedding components highlighted in Section 5.  
- Additional comment: include citations to Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank et al. (2016) for relevant context.
