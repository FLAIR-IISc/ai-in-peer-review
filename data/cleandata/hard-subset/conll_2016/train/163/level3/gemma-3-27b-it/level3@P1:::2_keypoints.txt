- Paper studies how BNC-trained word embeddings relate to part-of-speech (PoS) tags via classifiers and misclassification analysis.
- Main contribution: detailed error analysis uncovering annotation inconsistencies and PoS ambiguities in the BNC.
- Secondary contribution: a simple classifier on distributional vectors achieves reasonable PoS prediction (≈84%) without contextual information.
- Empirical finding: PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized to a few.
- Strength: thorough error analysis reveals linguistic phenomena and potential annotation errors (e.g., noun/adjective ambiguity).
- Strength: demonstrates that distributional embeddings encode PoS information even without explicit tagging during training.
- Strength: provides empirical evidence and feature-ablation exploration showing distributed representation of PoS information.
- Strength: clear methodology and reproducibility (data processing, embedding training, tag mappings provided).
- Weakness: limited novelty—prior work has already shown embeddings contain PoS information and PoS induction methods exist.
- Weakness: framing and motivation are weak; the paper doesn’t connect findings to practical downstream improvements.
- Weakness: contradictory claim that embeddings are “mostly semantic,” which conflicts with cited syntactic evidence.
- Weakness: questionable decision to convert BNC tags to Universal Dependencies, possibly losing finer-grained distinctions.
- Weakness: analysis biased toward frequent word/tag pairs; low-frequency “long tail” behaviors are underexplored.
- Question: why convert BNC tags to the Universal Dependencies tagset and was information loss considered?
- Question: how does the classifier’s performance compare to state-of-the-art PoS taggers on BNC?
- Question: provide details on failed attempts (footnote 3), methods tried, failure criteria, and whether Brown clusters were considered.
- Question: how do the methods and findings materially differ from prior distributional PoS induction work?
- Additional: change sentential citation style to \newcite{}.
- Additional: discuss limitations of relying solely on distributional information and benefits of combining morphological/contextual features.
- Additional: investigate which embedding components contribute most to each PoS tag.
