- Primary contribution: Empirical demonstration that word embeddings trained on the BNC encode significant POS information, supported by classifier results and error analysis.  
- Secondary contribution: Detailed error analysis revealing annotation inconsistencies (e.g., noun–adjective and numeral boundaries) and "soft" POS boundaries.  
- Finding: POS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized to a few key components.  
- Strength — Strong empirical results: High classifier performance (weighted F-score 0.979 train, 0.91 unseen) and successful transfer to UD (F-score 0.99).  
- Strength — Thorough error analysis: Misclassification analysis uncovers linguistic phenomena (proper vs common nouns, passive participles, currency handling).  
- Strength — Clear presentation: Well-written, logically organized paper with effective tables/figures and links to linguistic theory.  
- Strength — Demonstration of distributed representation: Experiments varying component count support the distributed nature of POS signals in embeddings.  
- Weakness — Limited novelty: Methodology (embeddings + classifier for POS) is not novel; contribution is mainly empirical thoroughness and analysis.  
- Weakness — Reliance on existing annotation: Evaluation depends on BNC/UD annotations; paper identifies but does not resolve annotation errors or ambiguity.  
- Weakness — Limited linguistic scope: Error analysis focuses on BNC inconsistencies; broader comparison to other resources or grammatical theories is lacking.  
- Question: Provide a more comprehensive literature review relating this work to Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), Plank et al. (2016), and clarify differentiators.  
- Question: Elaborate on the impact of annotation errors on classifier performance and strategies to mitigate them (e.g., re-annotation, data augmentation).  
- Question: Discuss practical challenges and benefits of applying this approach to resource-poor languages and minimal annotation needs.  
- Question: Report statistical significance testing (if any) for the ~100-dimensional threshold finding.  
- Additional comments: Fix nonstandard bibliographic citation for the BNC user guide; remove unnecessary anonymization statement.  
- Overall assessment: Well-executed, insightful study that justifies acceptance pending addressing weaknesses and questions.
