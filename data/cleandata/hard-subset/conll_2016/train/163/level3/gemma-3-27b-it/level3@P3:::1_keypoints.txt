- Summary: Trains embeddings on lemmatized BNC with UD POS tags and uses a logistic classifier to show embeddings encode POS information; error analysis reveals annotation inconsistencies and “soft” POS cases.

- Contribution 1 (Empirical validation): Provides a large-scale supervised demonstration that distributional embeddings contain significant POS information.

- Contribution 2 (Error analysis as diagnostic): Uses misclassifications to identify potential annotation errors and borderline POS cases in the BNC.

- Contribution 3 (Soft POS boundaries): Supplies quantitative evidence that POS categories can be gradient rather than strictly discrete.

- Strength 1 (Clarity): Paper is well-written with clear methodology, examples, and helpful visualizations.

- Strength 2 (Rigorous evaluation): Uses 10-fold cross-validation, external UD Treebank testing, and weighted metrics to address class imbalance.

- Strength 3 (Insightful error analysis): Detailed examination of misclassified tokens uncovers linguistically meaningful patterns and compares to Stanford tagger errors.

- Strength 4 (Reproducibility): Releases mappings between BNC and UD tags to facilitate replication.

- Strength 5 (Robustness): Classifier performance generalizes to a different human-annotated corpus (UD Treebank).

- Weakness 1 (Limited novelty): Core finding that embeddings capture POS is not entirely new given prior work; methodological novelty is limited.

- Weakness 2 (Supervised focus): Reliance on logistic regression is conservative; more advanced or unsupervised approaches could add value.

- Weakness 3 (Embedding parameter exploration): Paper reports parameters but lacks sensitivity analysis across vector sizes/training algorithms.

- Weakness 4 (UD tagset specificity): Using UD aids comparability but may limit applicability to corpora with different tagsets.

- Question 1: Have you considered quantitatively assessing annotation inconsistencies by applying the classifier to the full BNC to find systematic misannotations?

- Question 2: For resource-poor languages, what are the practical challenges and strategies for creating a small manually tagged lexicon for initial training?

- Question 3: Can you provide linguistic interpretations of the most salient vector components and any correlations with known features (morphology, syntax)?

- Additional comment 1: The reference list appears incomplete.

- Additional comment 2: Visualizing top-ranked ANOVA components and their linguistic interpretations would be helpful.

- Additional comment 3: Consider adding a discussion on limitations of the distributional hypothesis in this POS context.
