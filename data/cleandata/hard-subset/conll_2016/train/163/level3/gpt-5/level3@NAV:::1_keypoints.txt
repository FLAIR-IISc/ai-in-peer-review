- Summary: Tests whether distributional word embeddings encode POS using lemmatized UD-annotated BNC, logistic classifier, cross-validation, transfer to UD English, and error analysis; includes a lemma-only embedding follow-up.

- Contribution 1: Empirical evidence that POS information is robustly present in distributional embeddings and distributed across many dimensions.

- Contribution 2: Practical error-analysis framework that exposes graded POS boundaries and corpus annotation inconsistencies via embedding-based POS prediction.

- Contribution 3: Releases resources/protocols (BNC→UD tag mapping) and simple, reproducible baselines for context-free POS prediction.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results (F≈0.98 CV; 0.91 held-out BNC; 0.99 UD lemmas; 0.84 token-level with lemma-only embeddings) supporting the central claim.

- Strength 2: Insightful error analysis revealing linguistically meaningful boundary cases (participles, noun–adjective ambiguity, numerals vs years/currency) and annotation issues.

- Strength 3: Transparent, simple, interpretable methodology (word2vec + logistic regression, frequency weighting, prototypical vectors, feature ablation) showing POS signal spread over ≥100 dims.

- Strength 4: Well written and replicable with preprocessing, hyperparameters specified and mappings released.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—methods are straightforward and mainly offer empirical confirmation rather than new modeling or theory.

- Weakness 2: Potential circularity—training embeddings on lemma+POS tokens injects gold POS into representations, weakening unsupervised claims; lemma-only experiment should be more central.

- Weakness 3: Incomplete related work—missing key POS induction/tagging references (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016) and stronger positioning against that literature.

- Weakness 4: Missing baselines and controls—no surface-feature (capitalization, affixes, digits) or frequency-only baselines, no non-linear classifier comparisons, and no statistical tests or hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness 5: Limited generalization—evaluation is English-only; broader applicability claims would benefit from other domains or languages.

- Question 1: How do results change when embeddings are trained strictly on raw tokens/lemmas (no POS in training) for all experiments?

- Question 2: Can you quantify the contribution of surface cues (capitalization, digits) and frequency versus distributional context?

- Question 3: Did you control for potential leakage between BNC-derived embeddings and UD evaluation (e.g., shared lemmatization/vocabulary artifacts)?

- Question 4: Will you release code, trained models, and the BNC–UD mapping?
