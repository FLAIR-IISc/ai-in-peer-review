- Paper studies how lemmatized, UD-tagged BNC word embeddings correlate with PoS using simple classifiers to predict PoS and detect tag divergences, and shows PoS cues are distributed across many embedding dimensions.
- Contribution: Demonstrates embeddings surface “graded” PoS affiliations and highlight annotation inconsistencies and boundary cases (e.g., years vs. numerals, participles).
- Contribution: Provides cross-corpus evidence that embeddings encode robust PoS signals, enabling simple context-free classifiers to achieve strong accuracy (high BNC/UD results).
- Contribution: Quantitatively shows PoS information is distributed across many dimensions via ANOVA-based feature ranking and top-k analyses.
- Strength: Linguistically meaningful error analysis that uncovers fuzzy PoS boundaries and systematic BNC annotation inconsistencies useful for corpus curation.
- Strength: Clear, reproducible experimental setup with sensible baselines, cross-validation, held-out frequency checks, and explicit corpus/embedding details.
- Strength: Simple methodology (word2vec + logistic regression) isolates distributional signal effectively, with frequency weighting improving class coverage.
- Strength: Interpretable feature-distribution analysis supports the claim that PoS cues are spread across dozens of embedding dimensions.
- Strength: Paper is well written with explicit motivations, caveats, and informative examples.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—approach is straightforward and prior work has shown similar PoS/morphosyntax encoding in embeddings.
- Weakness: Potential circularity because best results use embeddings trained on lemma+PoS tokens, conditioning vectors on gold tags and limiting claims about pure distributional learning.
- Weakness: Incomplete baselines and metrics—should include stronger baselines (Brown clusters, BoC, char features, MLP/SVM) and report macro-F1 and per-class results.
- Weakness: Unfair comparison to a contextual tagger by feeding single tokens; needs more apples-to-apples context-free baselines for error-profile comparison.
- Weakness: Limited robustness analysis—no seed variance, hyperparameter sensitivity, or dev-set top-k feature evaluation; UD evaluation drops many types without coverage analysis.
- Question: Please report macro-F1, per-class precision/recall, and confusion matrices for BNC and UD, and performance on closed vs. open classes.
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding hyperparameters and random seeds—please provide means and standard deviations over multiple runs?
- Question: For tag-free UD experiment, how many unambiguous training lemmas per class, and how does varying the dominance threshold affect accuracy?
- Question: Can you provide a fairer, context-free baseline (e.g., Brown clusters, averaged contextual counts, char n-grams) and compare it to your embeddings?
- Question: Did you quantify BNC inconsistency claims via manual sampling or a small annotation study to solidify the argument?
- Question: Will you release lemmatization/UD mapping and code (anonymized) to facilitate replication?
- Additional comment: Expand related work on embedding-based PoS induction/probing (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016) to clarify novelty.
- Additional comment: Consider discussing applicability to low-resource settings (needed prototypical seeds per class) and cross-lingual transfer.
