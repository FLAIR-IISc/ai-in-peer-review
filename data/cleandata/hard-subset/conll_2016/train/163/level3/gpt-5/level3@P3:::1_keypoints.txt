- Paper studies whether distributional word embeddings encode POS information and uses probes to analyze POS boundaries, outliers, and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Uses lemmatized BNC with UD tags, trains skip-gram embeddings and a multinomial logistic classifier to predict POS; strong cross-validated and cross-corpus performance.  
- Follow-up: trains embeddings on lemmas only and probes with a small prototype list, achieving reasonable tagging without sentence context.  
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that embeddings robustly encode POS with extensive evaluations and analyses on BNC and UD.  
- Secondary contribution: methodology for surfacing POS outliers revealing graded category membership and annotation issues (e.g., PROPN/NOUN, VBN-as-ADJ).  
- Tertiary contribution: evidence POS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions and a low-resource probing setup that works without context.  
- Strength: clear empirical grounding and thorough analysis, with confusion patterns and linguistically informative qualitative insights.  
- Strength: outlier-driven error analysis effectively exposes real annotation inconsistencies and borderline POS cases.  
- Strength: simple, reproducible methodology (skip-gram + logistic regression) and useful BNC→UD tag mapping.  
- Strength: cross-corpus validation on UD reduces concerns about overfitting to BNC idiosyncrasies.  
- Strength: top-k feature analysis supports claim that POS information is distributed rather than localized.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—mostly empirical characterization using standard models.  
- Weakness: potential circularity because embeddings were trained on lemma+POS tokens, possibly leaking POS into contexts.  
- Weakness: narrow scope—English-only, BNC/UD corpora; would benefit from more languages or corpora.  
- Weakness: limited engagement with prior related work on tagging/induction and alternative embeddings/probing methods.  
- Weakness: probing methodology could be stronger (held-out evaluation for ablations, alternative probes, mutual information).  
- Question: can main outlier analyses be replicated with embeddings trained on lemmas only to rule out context-tag leakage?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to embedding hyperparameters and context definitions (window size, subword, dependency contexts)?  
- Question: did you examine per-class precision/recall for the lemma-only probe on UD and compare to simple baselines for ambiguous lemmas?  
- Question: can you provide full confusion matrices and top outlier lists to aid replication, and are signature dimensions stable across seeds?  
- Additional comment: add missing related work (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016; Levy & Goldberg 2014).  
- Additional comment: consider evaluating on another English corpus (ukWaC/Wikipedia) and at least one other UD language for generality.  
- Additional comment: paper is clearly written and well-suited to an empirical linguistics venue; contribution is primarily empirical insight rather than novel algorithms.
