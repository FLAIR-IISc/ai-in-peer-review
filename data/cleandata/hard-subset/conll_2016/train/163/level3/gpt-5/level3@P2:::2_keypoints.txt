- Paper studies how BNC-trained word embeddings encode PoS by training a multinomial classifier, analyzing misclassifications, building a lemma-level PoS predictor, and measuring informative embedding dimensions.  
- Contribution: data-driven error analysis revealing linguistically interesting outliers and likely BNC annotation inconsistencies (e.g., adjective/adverb confusions, year/currency nouns).  
- Contribution: empirical finding that PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (on the order of 100+), not concentrated in a few.  
- Contribution: a simple prototype-based lemma PoS predictor trained from small UD seed sets achieves reasonable context-free accuracy, useful for low-resource scenarios.  
- Strength: insightful outlier analysis with concrete corpus-backed examples and cross-checks against UD, demonstrating value for corpus auditing.  
- Strength: strong empirical signals that embeddings encode PoS (classifier: 0.979 CV on BNC, 0.91 on held-out frequency band; 0.99 F on UD subset).  
- Strength: clear evidence that PoS signal is broadly distributed across embedding dimensions via ablation and ANOVA rankings.  
- Strength: practical prototype-based PoS classifier reaches 0.84 token accuracy without context, offering a credible baseline for resource-poor languages.  
- Strength: reproducibility and methodological clarity with training details and released tag mappings.  
- Weakness: limited novelty given prior distributional PoS induction/clustering literature; key related work (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al., Blunsom & Cohn, Yatbaz et al.) is omitted.  
- Weakness: lacks evaluation of downstream utility—no experiments showing embedding-derived distinctions improve tasks like parsing or dependency labeling.  
- Weakness: coarse UPOS mapping and lemmatization may hide fine-grained phenomena; original BNC tags could reveal more nuanced patterns.  
- Weakness: unclear description of clustering attempts (“inescapably failed”)—missing methods, metrics, and quantitative failure criteria; no comparison to Brown clusters.  
- Weakness: claims about PROPN vs NOUN separability need tighter quantification beyond obvious function-word effects.  
- Weakness: frequency bias—study focused on frequent words (train ≥500; test 100–500) and neglects rare-word generalization and remedies (morphology, subwords).  
- Question: compare PoS-from-lemma predictor to Brown clusters and standard PoS induction baselines (HMM, Bayesian) and to dependency-based or positional contexts.  
- Question: detail clustering methods tried (algorithms, k selection, metrics) and provide quantitative failure analysis or partial successes.  
- Question: justify UPOS prioritization over original BNC tags and replicate outlier findings at finer tag granularity.  
- Question: quantify which features separate PROPN from NOUN and show results aren’t driven by a few function-word contexts.  
- Question: report performance for frequency bins below 100 and test whether adding character n-grams or suffix features helps rare-word tagging.  
- Additional: rephrase claims that embeddings are “mostly semantic” to acknowledge strong syntactic/functional signals.  
- Additional: suggest reporting results on non-lemmatized tokens to assess morphological contribution to PoS prediction.  
- Additional: minor fixes—use sentential citation style, clean references/figure captions, and clarify availability of mappings, code, and models.
