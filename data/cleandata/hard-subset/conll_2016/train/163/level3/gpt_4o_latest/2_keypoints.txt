- Paper analyzes how PoS tags relate to word embeddings trained on the BNC, using classifiers, error analysis, and distributional probing.  
- Contributions: detailed error analysis revealing graded PoS boundaries and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Contributions: shows PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated.  
- Contributions: suggests distributional models can detect annotation errors and help refine PoS-tagged corpora.  
- Strength: thorough, insightful analysis of misclassifications (e.g., proper nouns vs. common nouns/adjectives).  
- Strength: empirical validation across multiple datasets, including Universal Dependencies, showing robustness.  
- Strength: practical implications for improving corpora and bootstrapping tagging in low-resource settings.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — presence of PoS information in embeddings is well-established in prior work.  
- Weakness: related work coverage is insufficient; key references (e.g., Tsvetkov et al. 2015) are missing.  
- Weakness: missed opportunity to discuss whether embeddings aid parsing more than traditional PoS tags.  
- Weakness: Section 5 results are robust but not a significant advancement over prior methods.  
- Weakness: unclear methodological choices, notably use of UPOS instead of the original fine-grained BNC tagset.  
- Questions: why use UPOS rather than fine-grained BNC tags, and could that affect findings?  
- Questions: clarify the role and failure criteria for Brown cluster vectors mentioned in Footnote 3.  
- Questions: have you analyzed low-frequency words and classifier errors in the tail?  
- Additional comment: the observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and needs better framing.  
- Additional comment: compare findings more explicitly to state-of-the-art PoS taggers and practical applications.  
- Additional comment: update sentential references to conference formatting (\newcite{}) as required.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to limited novelty and related-work gaps, but encourage revision and resubmission.
