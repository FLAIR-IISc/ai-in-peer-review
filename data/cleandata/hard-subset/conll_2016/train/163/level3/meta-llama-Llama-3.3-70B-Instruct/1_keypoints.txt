- Presents a novel approach to parsing broad-coverage semantic DAGs that allow multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuities.  
- Proposes two complementary strategies: conversion-based parsing (to constituency/dependency) and a direct transition-based parser.  
- Contribution: a new transition-based Broad-Coverage Semantic Parser (BSP) that supports the three broad-coverage criteria.  
- Contribution: explores conversion-based parsing to assess existing technologies’ applicability to the task.  
- Contribution: provides the first experiments on UCCA parsing, a cross-linguistically applicable semantic representation.  
- Strength: clear and well-written description of the problem and proposed solutions.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation, including comparisons with existing parsers and feature-set analysis.  
- Strength: achieves high F-score on primary edges and predicts some remote edges, improving over prior parsers.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in ML/NLP methods—primarily builds on existing transition- and conversion-based techniques.  
- Weakness: inadequate literature review on related POS tagging and POS induction work using word embeddings.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis and concrete strategies for improving parser performance.  
- Question: how will the authors improve performance on remote edges, which lags behind primary-edge performance?  
- Question: please provide more details on the parser’s feature sets and their selection process.  
- Question: how do the authors plan to extend the approach to other semantic representations such as AMR or MRS?
