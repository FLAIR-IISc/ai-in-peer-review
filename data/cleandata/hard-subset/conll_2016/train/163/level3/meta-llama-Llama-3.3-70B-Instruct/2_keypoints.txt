- Paper explores connection between PoS tags and word embeddings, finding consistency between tags and vectors.  
- Fails to acknowledge original PoS tagging purpose (supporting NLP tasks like parsing) in analyses of corpora such as the BNC.  
- Missing related work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al., Blunsom and Cohn) and non-DNN approaches.  
- Results in Section 5 are not novel; similar systems with comparable outcomes already exist.  
- Abstract claim that distributional vectors contain PoS information is not new given prior distributional PoS induction work.  
- Claim that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect; prior work shows vectors also encode syntactic information.  
- Questionable move to UPOS tags — finer-grained original tagset distinctions may be more informative.  
- Observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and partly explained by function words (e.g., "the").  
- Analysis of most frequent word/tag pairs is useful but limited; tail-of-distribution behaviour is not examined.  
- Does not explore alternatives to pure distributional features that could improve PoS generalization.  
- Main contributions claimed: PoS–embedding exploration, frequent word/tag pair analysis, and two transition-based techniques for semantic parsing.  
- Strengths: thorough analysis of PoS–embedding connection, proposal of two novel transition-based semantic parsing techniques, and detailed evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: lacking related work, Section 5 results not novel, and overall contribution weakened by missing citations and context.  
- Questions for authors: how they will address missing related work, increase novelty of Section 5, and explore non-distributional features for PoS generalization.  
- Minor issues include formatting/sentential reference fixes (e.g., change to \newcite{}) and need for deeper related-work discussion and result implications.
