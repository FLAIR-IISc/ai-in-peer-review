- Paper studies PoS tags vs. word embeddings and includes strong, detailed error analysis and outlier identification.  
- Overlooks the main motivation for PoS tagging (supporting downstream tasks like parsing); should evaluate whether distributional distinctions help parsing more than original or Universal tags.  
- Lacks a comprehensive review of distributional PoS induction literature (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012); reported results are not clearly novel given prior comparable systems.  
- Claim in the abstract that distributional vectors encode PoS information is not new, especially for English.  
- The assertion that vector relations are mostly semantic is incorrect; prior work (e.g., Mikolov, Collobert) shows vectors capture substantial syntactic information, contradicting statements in section 2.  
- Moving to Universal PoS tags is questionable because the fine-grained original tagset may yield more interesting distinctions.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear about who attempted what and what "failed" means; Brown cluster vectors’ alignment with Universal PoS tags should be acknowledged.  
- The finding that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be trivial, possibly driven by high-frequency function words like "the."  
- Recommend analyzing low-frequency tail items and classifier error types, and exploring features beyond pure distributional and morphological ones for better generalization to rare words.  
- Minor formatting issue: use \newcite{} for sentential references (e.g., "Mikolov et al. (2013b) showed").
