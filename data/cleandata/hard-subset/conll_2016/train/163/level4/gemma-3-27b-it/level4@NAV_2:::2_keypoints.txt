- Paper investigates the link between part-of-speech (PoS) tags and word embeddings, showing embeddings reflect PoS information.
- Main contribution 1: Error analysis of a PoS classifier reveals inconsistencies and potential annotation errors in the BNC.
- Main contribution 2: Empirical confirmation that distributional vectors encode PoS-related information distributed across many dimensions.
- Main contribution 3: Re-examination of PoS boundaries, identifying words where distributional patterns suggest alternative tags.
- Strength: Detailed error analysis uncovers patterns (e.g., proper nouns, currency, adjective/verb ambiguity) and explains classifier mistakes.
- Strength: Classifier achieves high accuracy without context, supporting the intertwining of syntactic and semantic information.
- Strength: Identification of boundary cases highlights the fuzziness of PoS categories and annotation challenges.
- Strength: Results are robust across datasets (BNC and UD Treebank), reducing concern about overfitting.
- Weakness: Paper lacks framing in terms of downstream NLP tasks (e.g., parsing) and practical impact of findings.
- Weakness: Important related work on distributional PoS induction (Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012) is omitted.
- Weakness: Some findings (that embeddings contain PoS info and proper vs. common noun distinction) are unsurprising and not novel.
- Weakness: Converting to UPOS is not well-justified; original BNC tagset’s finer distinctions may be more informative.
- Question: How would refining PoS tags using distributional insights affect parsing performance?
- Question: How does the classifier compare to existing distributional PoS induction methods, especially non-logistic-regression approaches?
- Question: What causes misclassification of low-frequency (‘tail’) words and which features could improve their tagging?
- Question: Provide details on the failed classification attempts mentioned in the footnote and criteria for failure.
- Additional: Replace \cite{} with \newcite{} for citation style in the final version.
- Additional: Analyze classifier performance by subsets (word frequency, morphological complexity) to reveal biases.
- Additional: Explore more sophisticated ML models beyond logistic regression to potentially improve accuracy and robustness.
