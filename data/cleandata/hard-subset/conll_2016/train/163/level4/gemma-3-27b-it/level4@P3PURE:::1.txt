The aim of this paper is to demonstrate that distributional information encoded in word vector models contains information about part-of-speech (POS) labels. The authors utilize a version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies POS tags, in which words have been replaced by their lemmas. They train word embeddings on this corpus and subsequently use the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier for POS prediction. Evaluations are performed on the same corpus – employing cross-validation – as well as on other corpora. Results are clearly presented, thoroughly discussed, and extensively analyzed. 

The paper is clear and well-written. However, its primary limitation is a lack of novelty in terms of NLP or machine learning. The work describes a set of straightforward experiments without introducing any new NLP or ML ideas or methods. While the results are interesting, insofar as they provide empirical support for the connection between distributional semantics and POS categories, this contribution would be well-suited for publication in a (quantitative/empirical) linguistics venue. 

Furthermore, the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings should be cited more comprehensively. Relevant works include, for instance, Lin, Ammar, Duer and Levin (2015); Ling et al. (2015, EMNLP); and Plank, Søgaard and Goldberg (2016).
