- Strong empirical investigation linking PoS tags and word embeddings with detailed error analysis as a strength.  
- Paper needs clearer articulation of why corpora like Brown/BNC were originally PoS-tagged (primarily for downstream tasks like parsing) and should evaluate benefits for parsing vs traditional/UPOS tags.  
- Major omission: insufficient review of distributional PoS induction literature (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012).  
- Results in Section 5 appear incremental given prior work that achieved comparable performance with limited external knowledge.  
- Claim that “distributional vectors do contain information about PoS affiliation” is not novel for English; prior studies already show this via cluster purity.  
- The statement that word vectors are “mostly semantic” is inaccurate—prior work (Mikolov, Collobert) shows substantial syntactic information and this contradicts the paper’s own earlier comments.  
- Unclear motivation for switching to UPOS tags; original finer-grained tagsets may provide more informative distinctions.  
- Footnote 3 is vague about who made the “failed attempts” and what failure criteria were; also need to address Brown clusters’ near-perfect alignment with UPOS.  
- The proper vs common noun dissimilarity observation is unsurprising and may be driven by the high frequency of “the.”  
- Recommend expanding analysis to long-tail words and classifier errors and explore alternative feature combinations to improve low-frequency generalization.  
- Minor editorial: use \newcite{} for sentential references consistently.
