This paper investigates whether distributional information captured in word vector models encodes information about part-of-speech (POS) tags. The authors train word embeddings on a lemmatized version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies POS tags, and subsequently use these embeddings to train a logistic classifier for POS prediction. The approach is evaluated both on the training corpus (using cross-validation) and on external datasets. The results are clearly presented, thoroughly discussed, and comprehensively analyzed.

The paper is well-written and easy to understand. However, a significant limitation is its lack of novelty in terms of NLP or machine learning techniques. The work consists of a set of direct experiments that do not introduce new NLP or ML methods or concepts. While the results are interesting, providing empirical support for the link between distributional semantics and POS tags, they primarily offer empirical validation rather than a breakthrough. As such, publication would be more suitable in a quantitative or empirical linguistics venue. 

Finally, the paper would benefit from a more extensive review of existing literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings, including relevant work such as Lin, Ammar, Duer and Levin (2015); Ling et al. (2015, EMNLP); and Plank, Søgaard and Goldberg (2016).
