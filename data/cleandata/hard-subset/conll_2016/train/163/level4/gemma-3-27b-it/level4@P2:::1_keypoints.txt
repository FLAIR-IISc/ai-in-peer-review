- Primary contribution: Empirical demonstration that POS information is encoded in distributional word embeddings, with robust experiments and detailed analysis.
- Secondary contribution: Identification of potential POS annotation inconsistencies in the BNC via analysis of misclassified words.
- Observation: POS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized to a few.

Strengths:
- Well-defined, reproducible experimental setup using lemmatized/UD-tagged BNC, cross-validation, and external testing on UD Treebank.
- Detailed error analysis that highlights linguistic patterns and annotation issues (e.g., currency amounts, years).
- Strong empirical validation: high classifier accuracy even on unseen data and with limited context.
- Insightful finding about vector-space structure: hundreds of components contribute to POS prediction.
- Reproducibility enhanced by releasing mappings between BNC and Universal tags.

Weaknesses:
- Methodology lacks novelty—standard pipeline of training embeddings and using them for POS prediction.
- Incomplete discussion of related work; several relevant prior studies are not cited.
- Some interpretations of misclassifications appear speculative and over-claimed as annotation errors.
- Heavy reliance on a single corpus (BNC), limiting generalizability.

Questions to authors:
- Why choose logistic regression; were other classifiers tested and how did they compare?
- How were misclassified examples selected for detailed discussion (frequency, error type, etc.)?
- How sensitive are results to training corpus size and embedding dimensionality?
- How do the findings compare in detail to prior work on POS tagging/induction with embeddings?

Additional comments:
- Reference list is incomplete and formatting is inconsistent.
- Suggest adding a table summarizing most frequent misclassifications with examples.
- Formatting of references should be fixed.
