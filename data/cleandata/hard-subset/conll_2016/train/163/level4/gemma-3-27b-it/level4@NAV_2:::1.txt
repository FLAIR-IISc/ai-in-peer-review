The aim of this paper is to demonstrate that distributional information stored in word vector models contains information about POS labels. They utilize a version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags, where words have been replaced by their lemmas. Word embeddings are trained on this corpus and subsequently used to train a logistic regression classifier to predict word POS tags. Evaluations are conducted on the same corpus (using cross-validation) and on external corpora. Results are clearly presented, discussed, and analyzed in detail.

The paper is well-written and clearly articulated. The primary limitation of this work is its lack of novelty in terms of NLP or Machine Learning techniques. The experiments are straightforward, lacking new NLP or ML methods or ideas. However, the results are interesting as they provide empirical support for the notion of POS as a distributional phenomenon. In this respect, the work would be a valuable contribution to a (quantitative/empirical) linguistics forum.

Furthermore, the existing literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings should be more comprehensively cited (e.g., Lin, Ammar, Duer, and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg 2016).
