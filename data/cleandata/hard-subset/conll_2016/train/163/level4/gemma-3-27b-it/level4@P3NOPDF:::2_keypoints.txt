- Strength: Detailed error analysis is a key strength and shows distributional vectors encode PoS information.
- Missing framing: Paper should acknowledge Brown/BNC were designed for NLP/parsing, not primarily as linguistic resources for morphosyntax.
- Reframe motivation: Investigate whether distributional distinctions are more useful for parsing than original or UPOS tags.
- Related work omitted: Substantial prior work on distributional PoS induction is overlooked; Christodoulopoulos et al. (2010) should be supplemented with Blunsom & Cohn (2011), Yatbaz et al. (2012), and other non-DNN approaches.
- Novelty concern: The claim that embeddings contain PoS information is not novel for English given 15 years of induction work showing similar cluster purity results.
- Inaccurate claim: Statement that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect—prior work (e.g., Mikolov et al., Collobert et al.) shows embeddings encode syntactic information.
- Results incremental: Section 5’s results appear incremental compared to existing systems that achieve comparable or better performance with less external knowledge.
- UPOS choice questioned: Moving to UPOS may lose informative fine-grained distinctions from the original tagset; those distinctions deserve analysis.
- Clarify footnote: Footnote 3 must state whether “failed attempts” are the authors’ experiments or cited reports and specify failure criteria.
- Baseline suggestion: Include Brown cluster vectors as a baseline because they align closely with UPOS tags.
- Analysis depth: Observation about proper vs common nouns is obvious; extend analysis to the long tail of infrequent words and classifier error types, and consider supplementing distributional/morphological features to improve generalization.
- Formatting: Revise in-text citations to use \newcite{} consistently (e.g., \newcite{Mikolov2013b}).
