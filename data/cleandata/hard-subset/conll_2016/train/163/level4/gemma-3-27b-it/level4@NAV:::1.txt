This paper investigates whether distributional information captured within word vector models reflects part-of-speech (POS) tag information. The authors leverage a lemmatized version of the BNC corpus, annotated with Universal Dependencies POS tags, to train word embeddings. These embeddings are then used to train a logistic classifier for POS prediction, with evaluations conducted via cross-validation on the original corpus and further tested on external datasets. The results are presented transparently and subjected to thorough analysis and discussion. 

The paper is well-written and easily understood. However, a primary concern is the lack of novelty in terms of NLP or machine learning contributions. The work consists of a series of direct experiments employing established techniques without introducing new methodologies or concepts. While the results are interesting, confirming an empirical link between distributional semantics and POS categories, this connection is not a new finding. As such, publication would be more appropriate in a venue focused on quantitative or empirical linguistics.

Finally, the paper would benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work. Specifically, relevant studies on POS tagging and POS induction utilizing word embeddings – such as Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank et al. (2016) – should be discussed.
