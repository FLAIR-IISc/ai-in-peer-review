- Shows a clear connection between word embeddings and PoS tags; detailed error analysis of misclassified instances is a strength.  
- Fails to acknowledge that PoS corpora (e.g., BNC) were created for downstream tasks like parsing; paper should reframe to ask if distributional distinctions help parsing or UPOS.  
- Lacks a comprehensive review of distributional PoS induction literature (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012); Section 5 results are only marginally novel.  
- Claim that embeddings contain PoS information is unremarkable given ~15 years of prior cluster-purity/results in PoS induction.  
- Assertion that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is inaccurate; prior work shows substantial syntactic information in embeddings, and the paper appears to contradict itself.  
- Questionable decision to focus on UPOS—finer-grained original tag distinctions may yield more interesting findings.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear about who ran the failed experiments and failure criteria; the strong alignment of Brown clusters with UPOS is not discussed.  
- The claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and likely driven by surface cues like the definite article “the.”  
- Analysis is biased toward high-frequency word/tag pairs; the paper should examine the low-frequency tail and classifier error types to motivate alternatives to purely distributional/morphological features.  
- Minor formatting issue: use \newcite{} consistently for sentential references.
