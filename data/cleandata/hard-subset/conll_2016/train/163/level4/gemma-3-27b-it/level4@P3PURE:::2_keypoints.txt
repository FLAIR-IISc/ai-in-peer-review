- Strength: paper explores PoS–embedding connections and provides useful error analysis focusing on classification outliers.  
- Major oversight: ignores that PoS corpora (e.g., BNC) were created for NLP/parsing, so paper should evaluate whether distributional vectors better serve parsing than original tags or UPOS.  
- Related work missing: must review distributional PoS induction literature (Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012) and more recent non‑DNN work.  
- Novelty concern: results (sec. 5) are scarcely novel—prior systems with stricter constraints reach comparable performance.  
- Abstract claim that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not new for English given past induction/cluster‑purity results.  
- Incorrect assertion that vector relations are “mostly semantic”: embeddings also encode significant syntactic information (contradicts sec. 2 and Mikolov et al.).  
- Questionable choice of UPOS: finer‑grained original tagset may be more informative than UPOS.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear: who attempted the failed methods, what were failure criteria, and why exclude Brown clusters which align well with UPOS.  
- Comment that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and may be driven by high‑frequency function words (e.g., “the”).  
- Suggestion: analyze the long tail (low‑frequency words and classifier errors) and consider features beyond distributional/morphological to improve generalization.  
- Minor formatting issue: use \newcite{} consistently for sentential references.
