- Strength: Detailed error analysis is a notable strength, showing expected links between distributional vectors and PoS.
- Framing: Paper should be reframed as evaluating whether distributional distinctions help downstream tasks (e.g., parsing) more than original or UPOS tags.
- Related work missing: Add foundational and non-DNN references (Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012) and expand the survey of distributional PoS induction.
- Novelty concern: Claim that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not new for English and has been shown by prior induction systems over the last 15 years.
- Incremental results: Section 5’s outcomes appear incremental given prior systems achieved comparable results with less external knowledge.
- Inaccuracy: Statement that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect—substantial syntactic information in embeddings is established by prior work.
- Internal contradiction: The paper contradicts itself regarding syntactic information in vectors (lines 79–80 vs. lines 107–108 and cluster purity discussion).
- UPOS shift unclear: Moving to UPOS is not justified—finer-grained original tags may be more informative for the analysis.
- Footnote 3 unclear: Clarify whether “failed attempts” were by the authors or reported elsewhere, what failure criteria were, and the performance of Brown cluster vectors.
- Proper vs. common nouns: The observed dissimilarity between proper and common nouns may be driven by function words (e.g., “the”), questioning its significance.
- Long-tail analysis needed: Recommend investigating low-frequency words and classifier errors to identify alternative features beyond distributional and morphological ones.
- Minor issue: Use \newcite{} for sentential references (e.g., Mikolov et al. (2013b)).
