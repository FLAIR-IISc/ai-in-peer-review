- Paper studies relationship between PoS tags and word embeddings using classifiers and error analysis.  
- Authors train a classifier on embeddings to predict PoS and analyze classifier errors to find annotation outliers.  
- Paper examines how PoS information is distributed across embedding components and its use for PoS tagging.

- Contribution: detailed analysis of PoS–embedding relationship, identifying annotation inconsistencies and outliers.  
- Contribution: shows PoS information is distributed across multiple embedding dimensions rather than localized.  
- Contribution: demonstrates embeddings can identify prototypical PoS examples and detect annotation errors.

- Strength: thorough error analysis that helps reveal annotation inconsistencies and outliers.  
- Strength: provides insights into fuzzy boundaries between PoS classes and linguistic nuance.  
- Strength: suggests practical potential of embeddings to improve PoS tagging and error detection.

- Weakness: limited novelty because embeddings encoding PoS information has been reported before.  
- Weakness: evaluation confined to BNC and UPOS, limiting generalizability to other corpora and tagsets.  
- Weakness: methodological concerns—use of simple logistic regression may be suboptimal, especially for low-frequency words.

- Question: how do these results relate to prior work on distributional PoS induction and what new insights are offered?  
- Question: provide details on failed clustering attempts of vectors into PoS classes and the evaluation criteria used.  
- Question: how will low-frequency words, poorly represented in embeddings, be addressed?  
- Question: provide more details on the annotation process and guidelines for creating the UPOS tagset.  
- Question: how do the authors plan to extend the study to other languages and corpora, and what challenges are expected?
