Summary: Analyzes the relationship between PoS tags and word embeddings by training a classifier to predict PoS and evaluating on the BNC and UD English Treebank.

Contribution 1: Shows word embeddings encode PoS information enabling high-accuracy PoS prediction.

Contribution 2: Provides a detailed error analysis highlighting classifier vs. gold-standard disagreements.

Contribution 3: Demonstrates distributional models can detect inconsistencies and errors in PoS-tagged corpora.

Strength 1: Thorough analysis of the embeddings–PoS relationship with detailed error analysis.

Strength 2: Clear, well-structured presentation of results.

Strength 3: Emphasizes potential of distributional models for corpus error detection.

Weakness 1: Lacks clear motivation for why the embedding–PoS connection matters for downstream NLP tasks.

Weakness 2: Insufficient review of related work on distributional PoS induction.

Weakness 3: Some findings (that embeddings contain PoS information) are not novel.

Question 1: Please clarify the practical importance of the embedding–PoS link for NLP and the paper’s contribution.

Question 2: How will the approach be extended to other languages and corpora?

Question 3: Provide details on failed attempts to cluster vectors into PoS and how that informs the current work.

Additional comment 1: Include a more thorough related-work review on distributional PoS induction.

Additional comment 2: Analyze classifier performance on low-frequency words and test alternative features for better generalization.

Additional comment 3: Add more visualizations/plots to illustrate results, especially in the error analysis.
