- Paper shows connection between PoS tags and word embeddings with useful error analysis, but results are somewhat straightforward.
- Strong point: detailed analysis of outlier errors in classification.
- Missing critical perspective: PoS-tagged corpora (e.g., BNC) were created to support downstream NLP (especially parsing); paper should evaluate whether distributional distinctions help parsing more than original/UPOS tags.
- Literature review is incomplete for distributional PoS induction; cite Christodoulopoulos et al. (2010) and more recent non-DNN work (Blunsom & Cohn 2011, Yatbaz et al. 2012).
- Results in Section 5 lack novelty given prior systems with comparable performance under similar constraints.
- Abstract claim that “distributional vectors contain information about PoS affiliation” is not new for English.
- Incorrect assertion that vector relations are “mostly semantic”; prior work (Mikolov, Collobert, etc.) shows substantial syntactic information in embeddings.
- Paper contains contradictory statements about semantic vs syntactic content of vectors (lines cited).
- Unclear rationale for moving to UPOS—fine-grained original tag distinctions may be more informative.
- Footnote 3 is unclear: specify whether failed attempts were by the authors or others and the failure criteria; discuss Brown clusters which align closely with UPOS.
- Observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be trivial (function words like “the” could explain separation).
- Analysis focuses on frequent word/tag pairs; should examine tail (low-frequency words) and classifier errors there, and consider features improving generalization for rare words.
- Minor: change sentential references to \newcite{} format (e.g., “Mikolov et al. (2013b) showed”).
