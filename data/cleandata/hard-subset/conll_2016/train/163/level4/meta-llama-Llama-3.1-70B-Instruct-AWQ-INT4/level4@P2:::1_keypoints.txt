- Paper shows word embeddings can reveal POS information, enabling insights into grammar and syntax.
- Empirical finding: embeddings contain POS-class information that allows accurate POS prediction even with limited training words.
- POS information is distributed across many embedding components rather than concentrated in a few features.
- Provides evidence for "soft" or graded POS affiliations, indicating flexible POS boundaries in natural language.
- Clear, well-written presentation of experiments and results.
- Evaluation performed on multiple corpora (British National Corpus, Universal Dependencies), demonstrating approach effectiveness.
- Error analysis offers useful insights into POS boundary nature and method limitations.
- No novel NLP/ML methods introduced; relies on established techniques like logistic regression and word embeddings.
- Literature on POS tagging and POS induction with embeddings is under-cited, limiting broader relevance.
- Limited discussion of implications for linguistic theory and POS annotation in corpora.
- Question: How will the analysis extend to other languages and what challenges are expected?
- Question: Which specific embedding components drive POS affiliation and how do they interact?
- Question: How could this approach aid POS annotation in corpora, and what are its benefits and limits?
