- Paper investigates how word embeddings relate to part-of-speech (PoS) tags and uses embeddings to analyze tagging consistency.
- Authors train a classifier on word embeddings to predict PoS and evaluate its performance, identifying outliers and annotation inconsistencies.
- Results indicate embeddings encode PoS information and the classifier works even for low-frequency words.

- Contribution: shows embeddings can detect PoS tagging inconsistencies and outliers in annotations.
- Contribution: demonstrates high classifier accuracy for PoS prediction, including low-frequency words.
- Contribution: emphasizes the role of distributional word patterns in PoS tagging.

- Strength: provides a thorough analysis linking embeddings and PoS tags, with detailed error analysis.
- Strength: effectively uses embeddings for PoS tagging, benefiting low-frequency items.
- Strength: highlights importance of distributional context for PoS decisions.

- Weakness: lacks clear motivation for choosing embeddings over other PoS methods and comparative analysis.
- Weakness: insufficient literature review on distributional PoS induction and related work.
- Weakness: results are not especially novel given prior distributional PoS studies.

- Question: please explain why embeddings were chosen and how this approach compares to alternatives.
- Question: how will the method generalize to other languages and what challenges are expected?
- Question: provide details on failed clustering attempts of vectors into PoS and the evaluation criteria used.
