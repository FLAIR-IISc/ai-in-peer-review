This paper aims to demonstrate that word vector models contain distributional information that can be used to predict part-of-speech (POS) labels. The authors utilize a version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags, where words have been replaced by their lemmas. They train word embeddings on this corpus and subsequently employ the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier for POS prediction. The evaluations are conducted on the same corpus using cross-validation, as well as on other corpora.

The paper is well-written and clearly presents its results, which are thoroughly discussed and analyzed. However, the main limitation of this paper is that it does not introduce any novel concepts or methods in the fields of natural language processing (NLP) or machine learning (ML). The experiments described are relatively straightforward and lack innovative NLP or ML ideas. Nevertheless, the results are interesting in that they provide empirical evidence for the notion of POS. In this regard, the paper would be suitable for publication in a quantitative or empirical linguistics venue.

One notable omission in the paper is the lack of extensive citations to existing literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings. For instance, relevant studies such as Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank et al. (2016) should be referenced to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the research context.
