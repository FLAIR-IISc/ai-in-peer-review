- Shows word embeddings trained on the BNC encode part-of-speech information and support a 'soft' or graded POS affiliation view.
- Trains a classifier on embeddings to predict POS tags with high accuracy, finding POS information distributed across vector components.
- Provides empirical evidence that embeddings contain POS cues, reinforcing graded POS affiliations.
- Introduces a method to detect outlying words whose distributional patterns differ from their POS class, useful for finding annotation inconsistencies.
- Demonstrates classifier robustness across corpora, including evaluation on the Universal Dependencies English Treebank.
- Clear, well-structured argument with thorough explanations and supporting evidence.
- Well-designed experiments with clearly presented and discussed results.
- Offers new insights into the relationship between embeddings and POS boundaries, advancing linguistic understanding.
- Does not propose novel NLP/ML methods, relying on straightforward use of existing techniques.
- Literature review is limited and could better cover POS tagging and POS induction with embeddings.
- Findings may be unsurprising to specialists, serving mainly as empirical validation of known intuitions.
- Questions: plans for extending to typologically different languages; details on which embedding components correspond to POS affiliations; potential real-world applications like preliminary corpus tagging or improving POS taggers.
