**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Redefining part-of-speech classes with distributional semantic models" explores the relationship between word embeddings and part-of-speech (POS) labels. The authors use a version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags and train word embeddings on this corpus. They then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier to predict the word's POS. The evaluations are performed on the same corpus using cross-validation as well as on other corpora.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Demonstration of POS information in word embeddings**: The paper shows that word embeddings contain information about POS labels, which can be used to predict the POS of a word.
2. **Analysis of POS boundaries**: The authors analyze the errors made by the classifier and show that they can reveal inconsistencies in the annotation process or guidelines.
3. **Robustness of the approach**: The paper demonstrates the robustness of the approach by evaluating the classifier on different corpora and showing that it can generalize well.

**Strengths**

1. **Clear and well-written paper**: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and results.
2. **Interesting results**: The paper provides interesting insights into the relationship between word embeddings and POS labels.
3. **Robust evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on multiple corpora, which demonstrates the robustness of their method.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of novelty**: The paper does not present any new NLP or ML ideas or methods. The approach is straightforward and has been used in previous work.
2. **Limited literature review**: The paper could benefit from a more extensive review of the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings.
3. **Comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The paper does not compare the performance of their approach to state-of-the-art POS tagging methods.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the preprocessing steps used to create the version of the BNC used in the paper?
2. How do you plan to extend this work to other languages and corpora?
3. Can you provide more insights into the analysis of the errors made by the classifier and how they relate to inconsistencies in the annotation process or guidelines?
