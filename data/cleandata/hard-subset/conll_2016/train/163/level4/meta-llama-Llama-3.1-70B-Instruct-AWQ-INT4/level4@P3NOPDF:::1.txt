This paper aims to demonstrate that distributional information stored in word vector models contains information about part-of-speech (POS) labels. The authors utilize a version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags, where words have been replaced by their lemmas. They train word embeddings on this corpus and subsequently use the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier to predict word POS. Evaluations are performed on the same corpus using cross-validation, as well as on other corpora.

The paper is clear and well-written, presenting results in a transparent and easily understandable manner. The authors provide an in-depth discussion and analysis of their findings, which is commendable. However, the primary concern with this paper is that it does not introduce any novel concepts or methods in the fields of natural language processing (NLP) or machine learning (ML). The experiments described are straightforward and lack innovative ideas or approaches.

Despite this limitation, the results are interesting in that they provide empirical evidence for the notion of POS. In this regard, the paper would be suitable for publication in a quantitative or empirical linguistic venue, as it contributes to the understanding of POS through experimental means.

One notable omission in the paper is the lack of extensive citations from the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings. For instance, relevant works such as Lin, Ammar, Duer, and Levin (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg (2016) should be referenced to provide a more comprehensive context for the research. Incorporating these citations would strengthen the paper by situating it within the existing body of research and highlighting its contributions more effectively.

Overall, while the paper is well-executed and provides valuable insights into the relationship between word vector models and POS labels, its lack of novelty and limited engagement with the broader literature are notable drawbacks.
