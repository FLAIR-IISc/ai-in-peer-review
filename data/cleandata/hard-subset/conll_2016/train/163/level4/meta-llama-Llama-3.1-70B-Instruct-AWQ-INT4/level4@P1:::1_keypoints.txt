1. Aim: demonstrate that distributional information in word vectors encodes POS labels using logistic classification on a lemmatized UD-annotated BNC.  
2. Evaluation: experiments use cross-validation on the BNC and tests on other corpora; results are clearly presented and discussed.  
3. Contribution: distributional word vectors can predict POS labels with high accuracy.  
4. Contribution: the method uncovers words that do not conform to traditional POS categories, revealing annotation inconsistencies.  
5. Contribution: the approach can detect systematic POS tagging errors.  
6. Strength: the paper is clear and well-written with a straightforward experimental description.  
7. Strength: provides a thorough analysis of results and implications for understanding POS categories.  
8. Strength: highlights the value of distributional semantics for linguistic analysis.  
9. Weakness: no novel NLP/ML methods are proposed; experiments are straightforward.  
10. Weakness: limited citation of prior work on POS tagging and POS induction with embeddings.  
11. Question: how will this approach be extended to other languages with different grammatical properties?  
12. Question: which specific features of the embeddings drive POS prediction?  
13. Question: how will annotation inconsistencies be addressed when the model disagrees with the gold standard?
