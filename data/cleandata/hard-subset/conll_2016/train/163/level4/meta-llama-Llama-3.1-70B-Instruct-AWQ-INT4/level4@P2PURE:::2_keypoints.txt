- Paper investigates PoS tags vs. word embeddings and includes strong, detailed error analysis.
- Missing reframing: corpora like BNC were built for downstream NLP (e.g., parsing); evaluate whether distributional vectors help parsing more than original/UPOS tags.
- Lacks a comprehensive related-work review on distributional PoS induction; should cite Christodoulopoulos et al. (2010), Blunsom & Cohn (2011), Yatbaz et al. (2012) and more recent non-DNN work.
- Results in Section 5 are not novel given prior systems that achieve comparable performance with limited external knowledge.
- Claim that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not new for English and has been reported in many prior studies.
- Statement that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect—word vectors also encode substantial syntactic information (contradicted by Mikolov, Collobert, and cluster-purity findings).
- Unclear motivation for switching to UPOS tags; the fine-grained original tagset might be more informative.
- Footnote 3 is vague about who attempted the failed experiments and what counts as failure; Brown clusters and their UPOS alignment are not discussed.
- The remark that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be uninteresting or trivially explained by high-frequency function words.
- Recommend analyzing low-frequency (tail) words and classifier errors to explore generalization and consider additional features beyond distributional/morphological ones.
- Minor: change sentential citation format to \newcite{} (e.g., “Mikolov et al. (2013b) showed”).
