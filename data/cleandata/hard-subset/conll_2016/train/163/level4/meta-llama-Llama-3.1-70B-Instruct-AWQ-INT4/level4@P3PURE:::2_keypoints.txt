- Paper studies link between PoS tags and word embeddings and includes strong error analysis of outliers.
- Missing discussion of original purpose of corpora (e.g., BNC) for downstream NLP tasks like parsing; should reframe whether embedding distinctions help parsing more than original/UPOS tags.
- Lacks comprehensive related work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012) and ignores more recent non-DNN work.
- Results in Section 5 are not novel; prior systems with stricter external-knowledge constraints achieve comparable performance.
- Abstract claim that embeddings contain PoS information is not new for English and repeats established findings (cluster purity/many-to-one results).
- Claim that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect and contradicted elsewhere in the paper; embeddings also encode substantial syntactic information.
- The switch to UPOS tags is insufficiently justified; fine-grained original tag distinctions deserve exploration.
- Footnote 3 is unclear about who attempted and failed and what failure criteria were used; clarify and report attempts.
- Should discuss Brown clusters, which align closely with UPOS and are relevant to the analysis.
- Statement that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be trivial and potentially explained by high-frequency function words affecting vectors.
- Analysis focuses on frequent word/tag pairs; paper should examine tail of distribution and classifier errors on low-frequency words.
- Recommend exploring alternative features to improve generalization of PoS tagging for rare words.
- Minor: enforce consistent citation style (use \newcite{} for sentential references).
