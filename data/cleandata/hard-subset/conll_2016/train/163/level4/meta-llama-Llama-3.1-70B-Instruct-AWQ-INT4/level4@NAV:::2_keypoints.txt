- Paper shows embeddings reflect PoS information and provides strong error analysis of classification outliers.  
- Missing discussion of original purpose of corpora (e.g., BNC) and whether distributional vectors help parsing more than original tags/UPOS.  
- Lacks a comprehensive review of prior distributional PoS induction work; recommend including Christodoulopoulos et al. (2010), Blunsom & Cohn (2011), Yatbaz et al. (2012), etc.  
- Results (section 5) are not particularly novel given prior systems with similar constraints achieving comparable performance.  
- Abstract's claim that distributional vectors contain PoS information is not new for English.  
- Claim that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect and contradicted later; vectors also encode substantial syntactic information.  
- Unclear rationale for using UPOS instead of the more fine-grained original tagset.  
- Footnote 3 is ambiguous: state who attempted the experiments and what failure criteria were used; note Brown clusters largely align with UPOS.  
- The finding that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be unsurprising and possibly driven by function-word frequency effects (e.g., “the”).  
- Recommend analyzing low-frequency word/tag tail and vector representations to identify features improving PoS generalization for rare words.  
- Minor formatting issue: replace sentential references with \newcite{} (e.g., “Mikolov et al. (2013b) showed”).
