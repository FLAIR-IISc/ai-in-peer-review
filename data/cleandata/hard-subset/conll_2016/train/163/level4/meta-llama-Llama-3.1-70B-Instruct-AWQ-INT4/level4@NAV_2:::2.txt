**Summary of the Paper**
The paper explores the relationship between part-of-speech (PoS) tags and word embeddings. The authors use word embeddings to analyze the consistency of PoS tags and the connection between word vector representations and PoS. They train a classifier to predict PoS tags based on word embeddings and evaluate its performance on a test set. The results show that word embeddings contain information about PoS affiliation, and the classifier can detect outliers and inconsistencies in the annotation process.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper demonstrates that word embeddings can be used to analyze the consistency of PoS tags and detect outliers and inconsistencies in the annotation process.
2. The authors show that the classifier trained on word embeddings can achieve high accuracy in predicting PoS tags, even for words with low frequency.
3. The paper highlights the importance of considering the distributional patterns of words in the context of PoS tagging.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a thorough analysis of the relationship between word embeddings and PoS tags, including a detailed error analysis of the classifier's performance.
2. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of using word embeddings for PoS tagging, particularly for words with low frequency.
3. The paper highlights the importance of considering the distributional patterns of words in the context of PoS tagging.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks a clear motivation for why the authors chose to use word embeddings for PoS tagging, and how this approach compares to other methods.
2. The authors do not provide a comprehensive review of related work in the context of distributional PoS induction.
3. The paper's results are not particularly novel, as other studies have shown similar results using distributional methods for PoS induction.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more context on why you chose to use word embeddings for PoS tagging, and how this approach compares to other methods?
2. How do you plan to extend this work to other languages, and what challenges do you anticipate in doing so?
3. Can you provide more details on the failed attempts to cluster word vectors into PoS tags, and what criteria were used to evaluate their success?
