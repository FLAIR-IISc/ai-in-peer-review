This paper investigates the presence of part-of-speech (POS) label information in word vector models by leveraging distributional data. The authors utilize a modified version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags, where words are replaced with their lemmas. They train word embeddings on this corpus and subsequently employ the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier for predicting word POS. The evaluation is conducted on the same corpus using cross-validation, as well as on other corpora, with results presented and analyzed in detail.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. However, the primary limitation is the lack of novelty in terms of natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) contributions. The experiments described are relatively straightforward and do not introduce new NLP or ML concepts or methods. Nevertheless, the findings are noteworthy, as they provide empirical evidence supporting the notion of POS. In this regard, the paper is suitable for publication in a quantitative or empirical linguistics venue.

Notably, the authors should provide a more comprehensive review of the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings, citing relevant studies such as Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank et al. (2016).
