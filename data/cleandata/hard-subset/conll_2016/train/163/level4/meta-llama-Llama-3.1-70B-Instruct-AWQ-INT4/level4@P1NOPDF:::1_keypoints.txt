- Paper aims to show that word vectors encode distributional information relevant to POS labels.  
- Uses a version of the British National Corpus annotated with UD POS tags where words are replaced by lemmas.  
- Trains word embeddings on this corpus and uses them to train a logistic classifier for POS prediction.  
- Evaluations are performed via cross-validation on the same corpus and on other corpora.  
- Results are clearly presented, discussed, and analyzed, providing empirical support for POS distinctions.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Main concern: no novel NLP/ML methods or conceptual contributions—experiments are straightforward.  
- Recommendation: more appropriate for a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue than a methods-focused ML/NLP conference.  
- Notable omission: insufficient citations to prior work on POS tagging/induction with embeddings (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015 EMNLP; Plank et al. 2016).
