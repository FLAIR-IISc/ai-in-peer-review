This paper investigates whether distributional information captured in word vector models encodes information about part-of-speech (POS) tags. The authors leverage a lemmatized version of the BNC corpus annotated with Universal Dependencies POS tags, training word embeddings on this data. These embeddings are then used to train a logistic classifier for POS prediction, with evaluation performed via cross-validation on the original corpus and on additional datasets. The results are presented clearly, with thorough discussion and analysis.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. However, a primary concern is the lack of novelty in terms of NLP or machine learning techniques; the work consists of a set of direct experiments employing established methods without introducing new approaches. While the results are interesting and offer empirical support for the connection between distributional semantics and POS categories, they are perhaps better suited for publication in a linguistics-focused venue with a quantitative or empirical emphasis. 

Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a more comprehensive review of existing literature on POS tagging and POS induction utilizing word embeddings, including relevant works such as Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank et al. (2016).
