- Paper examines how well skip-gram word embeddings trained on a lemmatized, PoS-annotated BNC align with traditional PoS categories by using embeddings to predict PoS tags via logistic regression.
- Core analysis inspects classifier misclassifications to identify distributionally atypical words and argue for graded/soft PoS membership and annotation inconsistencies.
- Contribution: provides a detailed qualitative analysis using classifier errors to reveal linguistically interesting phenomena (e.g., participles and attributive nouns behaving like adjectives).
- Contribution: offers empirical, data-driven support for the notion that PoS categories are continuous rather than discrete by identifying outlier clusters.
- Strength: exceptionally clear presentation and well-explained experimental setup, with thorough error analysis and compelling examples.
- Strength: yields valuable linguistic insights and a practical method for spotting possible corpus annotation errors and fuzzy class boundaries.
- Strength: appropriate use of a simple logistic regression probe, which keeps results interpretable and links distributional patterns to PoS ambiguity.
- Weakness: lacks methodological novelty—uses standard word2vec embeddings and a basic classifier without advancing NLP methods.
- Weakness: literature review is incomplete and should discuss recent relevant work on PoS tagging/induction using embeddings (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).
- Weakness: venue fit is questionable since the paper focuses on linguistic analysis rather than technical innovation typical for CONLL.
- Question: please clarify how this work differs from or complements recent studies on PoS induction and tagging that use embeddings.
- Question: have you considered alternative classifiers (e.g., nearest-neighbor to PoS prototype vectors) and whether they yield different or more nuanced insights?
