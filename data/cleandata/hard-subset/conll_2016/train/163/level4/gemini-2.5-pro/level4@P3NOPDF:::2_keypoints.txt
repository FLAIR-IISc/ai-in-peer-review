- Strength: detailed error analysis and examination of classification outliers is a valuable contribution.  
- Weakness — Framing/Motivation: paper lacks connection to downstream NLP tasks (e.g., parsing) and should ask whether distributional vectors are more useful than manual tags for such tasks.  
- Weakness — Related Work & Novelty: fails to engage key POS induction literature (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012), so results appear not novel.  
- Abstract critique: claim that distributional vectors contain POS information is well-established and not a novel contribution.  
- Claim critique (Lines 79-80): incorrect to assert vector relations are “mostly semantic”—vectors also encode substantial syntactic information.  
- Choice of UPOS: mapping fine-grained BNC tags to coarse UPOS is unjustified and may oversimplify important distinctions.  
- Footnote 3: ambiguous mention of “failed attempts” lacks context (who attempted what, failure criteria) and omits Brown clusters as a relevant baseline.  
- Lines 331–332 critique: observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns is predictable (e.g., determiner distribution) and not insightful.  
- Analysis limitation: focus on high-frequency words misses the long tail; must analyze low-frequency words and consider non-distributional features (e.g., morphology) for generalization.  
- Minor issue: fix sentential citation formatting (use “Author (Year)” style).
