- Paper studies relation between word embeddings and PoS tags, finding embeddings reflect PoS consistency — interesting but largely expected.  
- Strong point: thorough error analysis, especially examination of classification outliers.  
- Missing motivation: PoS-tagged corpora like the BNC were created mainly to support downstream tasks (e.g., parsing); paper should ask whether distributional distinctions help parsing more than manual tags.  
- Omits substantial prior work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012), which reduces novelty.  
- Abstract claim that distributional vectors encode PoS affiliation is not novel given longstanding many-to-one/cluster-purity results.  
- Claim that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is inaccurate; embeddings also encode syntactic information and the paper contradicts this elsewhere.  
- Unclear why authors map to coarse-grained UPOS instead of analyzing the original fine-grained tags for more nuanced insights.  
- Footnote 3 is vague about whose “failed attempts” are referenced and what failure criteria were used; Brown clusters — relevant to UPOS alignment — are omitted.  
- The note that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and likely attributable to distributional context (e.g., determiners).  
- Analysis focuses only on frequent word/tag pairs, missing the long tail; examining low-frequency words and their error types would be valuable.  
- Should discuss alternative features beyond distributional/morphological (given lemmatization) to improve generalization for rare words.  
- Minor formatting issue: in-text bibliographic references should use \newcite{} rather than parenthetical citations.
