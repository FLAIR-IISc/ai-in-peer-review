- Goal: Show that distributional information in word vectors predicts POS labels.  
- Data: Uses a lemmatized British National Corpus annotated with Universal Dependencies POS tags.  
- Method: Train word embeddings on the corpus, then use logistic regression to predict POS from vectors.  
- Evaluation: Performed via cross-validation on the training corpus with additional tests on external corpora.  
- Presentation: Results are clear and supported by extensive discussion and analysis.  
- Writing: Manuscript is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Main weakness: Lacks methodological novelty in NLP or ML, relying on straightforward experiments without new techniques or conceptual insights.  
- Contribution: Still interesting empirically, providing strong evidence for the syntactic nature of POS categories.  
- Recommendation: More suitable for a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue than a methods-focused ML/NLP venue.  
- Prior work: Literature review on POS tagging/induction with embeddings is incomplete; should cite and discuss works such as Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015 EMNLP), and Plank et al. (2016).
