- Paper studies how much POS information is encoded in distributional word vectors.  
- Method: train embeddings on a lemmatized BNC annotated with Universal Dependencies POS tags, then train a logistic regression to predict POS.  
- Evaluation is thorough: cross-validation on training corpus plus tests on multiple out-of-domain corpora.  
- Results are clearly presented with extensive discussion and analysis.  
- Paper is clear and well-written.  
- Main weakness: limited methodological novelty—straightforward experiments without new techniques or theoretical contributions.  
- Findings are interesting and provide strong empirical support for a distributional basis of POS categories; suitable for quantitative/empirical linguistics venues.  
- Related work section is weak and should cite key prior work (e.g., Lin, Ammar, Duer and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank, Søgaard and Goldberg 2016).
