- Contribution: Qualitative analysis of classifier outliers revealing BNC annotation inconsistencies and evidence for graded PoS membership.
- Contribution: Empirical finding that PoS-related information is distributed across many embedding dimensions, not concentrated in a few.

- Strength: Insightful error analysis that uncovers annotation errors and linguistically interesting boundary cases between word classes.
- Strength: Clear, focused methodology—training a classifier on embeddings to isolate distributional-prototype mismatches.
- Strength: Reproducibility—experiments use public resources (BNC, UD) and standard tools (word2vec, logistic regression) with clear setup.

- Weakness: Lacks extrinsic motivation and evaluation linking findings to downstream tasks (e.g., parsing), so practical impact is unclear.
- Weakness: Insufficient engagement with prior unsupervised/semi-supervised PoS induction literature, weakening novelty claims.
- Weakness: Overstates novelty—presence of PoS/syntactic information in distributional vectors is already well established.
- Weakness: Analysis restricted to high-frequency words; ignores long-tail low-frequency words where PoS tagging is most challenging.

- Question: Why map BNC tags to coarse-grained UPOS instead of analyzing the original fine-grained BNC tagset?
- Question: Please clarify the “naive clustering” failures—what experiments and failure criteria were used (surprising given methods like Brown clustering)?
- Question: Could the distributional dissimilarity between proper and common nouns be largely driven by determiner distributions (e.g., “the”) rather than deeper lexical distinctions?
