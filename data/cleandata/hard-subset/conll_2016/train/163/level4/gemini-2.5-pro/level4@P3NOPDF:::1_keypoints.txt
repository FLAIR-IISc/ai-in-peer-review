- Paper studies how much POS information is encoded in distributional word embeddings by training embeddings on a lemmatized UD-annotated BNC and classifying POS with logistic regression.  
- Evaluation uses cross-validation on BNC and additional out-of-domain corpora to test generalization.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and experiments are described in sufficient detail.  
- Results and analysis are transparent and provide valuable insights into distributional vs. syntactic category relationships.  
- Main weakness: limited methodological novelty—pipeline (train embeddings → simple classifier) is standard and not a new technique.  
- Contribution is primarily empirical/confirmatory, validating a linguistic hypothesis rather than advancing NLP methods.  
- Recommendation: better suited to a linguistics-focused venue than a top-tier NLP conference like CONLL.  
- Related work section is sparse and should engage with key POS-tagging/induction papers using embeddings (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).
