- Paper studies PoS information in word embeddings and provides a thorough error analysis, especially of classification outliers (strength).

- Conclusions are interesting but largely straightforward and not clearly novel.

- Paper fails to consider the original purpose of PoS corpora (e.g., BNC) — namely aiding downstream tasks like parsing — and should reframe to ask whether distributional vectors are more useful for parsing than BNC or UPOS tags.

- Overlooks substantial prior work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012), making Section 5 results seem lacking in novelty.

- Claim in the abstract that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not a novel contribution and has been established for many years.

- Assertion that word-vector relations are “mostly semantic” is inaccurate; vectors also encode significant syntactic information, and the paper internally contradicts this.

- Motivation for mapping original tags to UPOS is unclear; analyzing fine-grained original tag distinctions would be more informative.

- Footnote about “failed attempts” is ambiguous — unclear who attempted them, what “failure” means, and alternative representations (e.g., Brown clusters) should be considered.

- The comment that proper nouns differ from common nouns is trivial and likely explained by contextual co-occurrence patterns (e.g., determiners).

- Analysis focuses on frequent word/tag pairs; the paper should examine low-frequency (long-tail) words and classification errors to reveal limitations of distributional and morphological features.

- Citation formatting errors need correction (use proper LaTeX citation commands).
