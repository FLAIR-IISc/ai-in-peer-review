This paper investigates whether distributional information captured by word embeddings is predictive of Part-of-Speech (POS) tags. The authors train word embeddings on a lemmatized version of the British National Corpus (BNC), which has been annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags. These embeddings are then used as features to train a logistic regression classifier for POS prediction. The model is evaluated using cross-validation on the training corpus, as well as on other corpora. The results are clearly presented and analyzed in detail.

The paper is clear and well-written. Its primary weakness, however, is a lack of methodological novelty. The work describes a set of straightforward experiments that, while sound, do not introduce new ideas or techniques to the fields of NLP or machine learning. The findings are nonetheless interesting, as they provide strong empirical grounding for the distributional basis of POS categories. In this regard, the paper might be better suited for a publication in a quantitative or empirical linguistics venue.

Additionally, the discussion of related work could be expanded. The authors should more extensively cite the literature on POS tagging and induction using word embeddings, including works such as Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank et al. (2016).
