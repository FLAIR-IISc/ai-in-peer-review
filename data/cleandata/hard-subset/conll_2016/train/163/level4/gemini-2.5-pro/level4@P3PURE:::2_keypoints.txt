- Paper shows link between PoS tags and word embeddings and provides strong error analysis of classification outliers.
- Strength: detailed error analysis of classifier outliers.
- Major oversight: ignores that PoS-tagged corpora (e.g., BNC) were created to support downstream tasks like parsing—motivation should be reframed around parsing utility.
- Related-work omission: fails to cite key distributional PoS induction literature (Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012).
- Novelty concern: results in Section 5 lack novelty given prior non-DNN systems with stricter constraints achieving comparable performance.
- Incorrect claim: the abstract’s contribution that embeddings contain PoS information is well-established and not novel for English.
- Incorrect/contradictory assertion: claiming word-vector relations are “mostly semantic” ignores established syntactic information encoded in embeddings.
- Unclear choice: mapping to coarse-grained UPOS is not well-motivated; analyzing fine-grained original tags would be more informative.
- Footnote 3 is unclear about whether failures are the authors’ attempts or prior reports and should acknowledge successful representations like Brown clusters.
- Observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and explainable by distributional context differences (e.g., determiners).
- Analysis scope limitation: focusing only on frequent word/tag pairs omits long-tail, low-frequency words—analysis of these could motivate additional features beyond distributional/morphological ones.
- Minor issue: use textual/in-sentence citations (e.g., \newcite{}) instead of parenthetical sentence-start citations.
