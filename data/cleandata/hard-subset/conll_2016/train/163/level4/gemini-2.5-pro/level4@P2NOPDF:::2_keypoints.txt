- Paper analyzes relationship between PoS tags and word embeddings and provides a strong, detailed error analysis of classification outliers.
- Motivation is weak: reframe toward downstream tasks (e.g., parsing) to test whether distributional distinctions are more useful than gold or UPOS tags.
- Insufficient engagement with prior work on unsupervised/distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012), which reduces novelty claims.
- Claim that embeddings “contain information about PoS affiliation” is not novel—this has been shown repeatedly in the literature.
- Incorrect or misleading statement that vector relations are “mostly semantic”; vectors also capture substantial syntactic information and the paper contradicts itself.
- Unclear motivation for mapping fine-grained tags to UPOS; authors should justify the loss of fine-grained distinctions or analyze them directly.
- Footnote 3 is ambiguous about whether “failed attempts” refer to the authors’ experiments or prior work and lacks criteria for failure; other representations (e.g., Brown clusters) should be considered.
- The claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns in embeddings is likely explained by simple distributional cues (e.g., determiner co-occurrence), not a novel finding.
- Analysis is limited to frequent word/tag pairs; authors should examine low-frequency (long-tail) words and consider adding morphological features to improve generalization.
- Minor formatting issue: ensure citations follow the conference style (use appropriate citation commands).
