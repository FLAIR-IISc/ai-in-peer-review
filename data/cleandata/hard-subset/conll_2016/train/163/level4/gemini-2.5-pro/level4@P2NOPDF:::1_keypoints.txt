- Studies how much POS information is encoded in distributional word embeddings.
- Method: train embeddings on a lemmatized, UD-tagged BNC and use logistic regression on embeddings to predict POS.
- Evaluation via cross-validation on the BNC and out-of-domain corpora; results are clearly presented with thorough analysis.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Main weakness: limited methodological novelty—follows the standard embeddings + simple classifier paradigm, no new techniques.
- Results are linguistically valuable, offering empirical evidence that distributional semantics capture core grammatical properties and quantify POS.
- Likely a better fit for quantitative/computational linguistics venues than a major AI conference.
- Literature review is weak and needs stronger engagement with recent POS tagging/induction work using embeddings.
- Should contextualize and cite relevant papers such as Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), and Plank et al. (2016).
