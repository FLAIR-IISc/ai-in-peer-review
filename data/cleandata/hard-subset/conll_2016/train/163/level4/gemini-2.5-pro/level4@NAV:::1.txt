This paper aims to demonstrate that distributional information encoded within word vector models is predictive of Part-of-Speech (POS) labels. To this end, the authors use a lemmatized version of the British National Corpus (BNC) that has been annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags. They first train word embeddings on this corpus and subsequently use these vectors to train a logistic regression classifier for POS prediction. The evaluation is performed using cross-validation on the training corpus, with additional experiments on external corpora. The results are presented clearly and are supported by extensive discussion and analysis.

The manuscript is well-structured and written with clarity. The principal weakness of this work is its lack of methodological novelty from either an NLP or a Machine Learning standpoint. It presents a series of straightforward experiments without introducing new techniques or conceptual insights to these fields. The results are nonetheless interesting, as they provide strong empirical grounding for the syntactic nature of POS categories. For this reason, the paper would be a suitable submission for a publication venue focused on quantitative or empirical linguistics.

On a separate point, the review of prior work on POS tagging and induction that leverages word embeddings should be more comprehensive. The authors are encouraged to cite and discuss relevant literature more extensively (for example, Lin, Ammar, Duer and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Søgaard and Goldberg 2016).
