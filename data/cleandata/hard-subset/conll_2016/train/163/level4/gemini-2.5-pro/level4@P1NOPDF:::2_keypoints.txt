- Strength: The paper links PoS tags and word embeddings and provides a detailed error analysis, especially of classification outliers.
- Missing motivation: The paper fails to consider that PoS-tagged corpora (e.g., BNC) were created for downstream NLP tasks (not just linguistic theory), so it should ask whether distributional vectors help tasks like parsing more than the original tagset.
- Reframing suggestion: Evaluate whether distinctions captured by vectors are more beneficial for parsing (or other downstream tasks) than the original or UPOS tagsets.
- Related work omission: The paper neglects significant prior work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010 survey, Blunsom & Cohn 2011, Yatbaz et al. 2012), reducing novelty claims.
- Novelty concern: Results in Section 5 are of limited novelty given existing non-neural systems that achieve comparable performance under stricter constraints.
- Abstract overclaim: The claim that "distributional vectors do contain information about PoS affiliation" is not novel and has been shown repeatedly in prior work.
- Incorrect claim about semantics: The statement that relations between word vectors are "mostly semantic" is inaccurate—vectors encode substantial syntactic information, as prior work shows.
- UPOS mapping unclear: The motivation for mapping original fine-grained tags to coarse-grained UPOS is unclear; analyzing original fine-grained distinctions would be more informative.
- Footnote ambiguity: Footnote 3 is confusing—clarify whether "failed attempts" are the authors’ experiments or prior work and define failure criteria; also discuss methods like Brown clustering.
- Proper noun finding unsurprising: The observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns is expected and largely explained by distributional context (e.g., determiner patterns).
- Long-tail analysis missing: Focusing on frequent word/tag pairs is practical but the paper should analyze low-frequency words to motivate features beyond distributional/morphological ones.
- Minor editorial: Use the appropriate LaTeX citation commands (e.g., \newcite{} or \citet{}) when the reference is part of the sentence.
