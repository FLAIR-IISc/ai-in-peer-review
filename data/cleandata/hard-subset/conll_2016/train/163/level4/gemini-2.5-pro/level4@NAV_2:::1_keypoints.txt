- Paper studies how distributional word embeddings encode PoS by training Skip-gram on lemmatized BNC and using a logistic regression PoS classifier, analyzing classifier errors to find non-prototypical words.
- Contribution: data-driven analysis of outlier words showing graded PoS membership and potential BNC annotation inconsistencies.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that PoS information is robustly distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than confined to a few.
- Strength: in-depth linguistic analysis that uses model errors to reveal fuzzy PoS boundaries and interesting word groups (e.g., years as nouns, passive participles as adjectives).
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with a compelling qualitative analysis.
- Strength: sound experimental design using multiple test sets and comparisons to standard PoS tagger confusion matrices to demonstrate robustness.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty — standard pipeline of classifier on top of embeddings without new algorithms.
- Weakness: related work section is incomplete and misses key literature on PoS induction/tagging with distributional methods.
- Weakness/Potential issue: focus on linguistic analysis over technical innovation may make the paper a better fit for computational/quantitative linguistics venues than an ML/AI conference.
