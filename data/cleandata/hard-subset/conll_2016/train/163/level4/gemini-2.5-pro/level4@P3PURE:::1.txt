This paper investigates whether the distributional information captured in word vector models is predictive of Part-of-Speech (POS) labels. The authors train word embeddings on a lemmatized version of the British National Corpus (BNC), which has been annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags. These word vectors are then used as features to train a logistic regression classifier for POS prediction. The model is evaluated using cross-validation on the training corpus, as well as on other corpora. The results are presented clearly and are supported by extensive discussion and analysis.

The paper is clear and well-written. Its primary weakness, however, is a lack of methodological novelty in the fields of Natural Language Processing or Machine Learning. It describes a set of straightforward experiments without introducing new techniques or theoretical insights. The results are nonetheless interesting, as they provide a strong empirical grounding for the distributional nature of POS categories. In this regard, the paper seems well-suited for publication in a quantitative or empirical linguistics venue.

A secondary concern is that the literature review could be more comprehensive. Specifically, the paper would benefit from citing and discussing more related work on POS tagging and induction using word embeddings (e.g., Lin et al., 2015; Ling et al., 2015; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg, 2016).
