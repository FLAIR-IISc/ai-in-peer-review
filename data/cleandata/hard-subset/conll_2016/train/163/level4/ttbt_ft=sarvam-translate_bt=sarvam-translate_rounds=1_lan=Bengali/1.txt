The purpose of this paper is to demonstrate that distributed information stored in a word vector model contains information about POS tags. They use a version of BNC annotated by UD POS and where words have been replaced with lemmas. They train word embedding in this corpus, then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier to predict word POS. Assessments are conducted in the same corpus (using cross-validation) and in other corpus as well. The results are clearly presented and discussed and analyzed in detail.

The paper is clear and well-written. The main problem with this paper is that it does not offer anything new in the field of NLP or ML. It describes simple tests without any new NLP or ML concepts or methods. The results are indeed interesting, in the sense that they provide an empirical basis for the concept of POS. In that regard, it is certainly worthy of publication in a (quantitative/empirical) linguistic space.

Another point is that the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embedding should be more extensively cited (for example, Lin, Ammar, Duer, and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg 2016...).
