- Paper shows links between PoS tags and word embeddings and provides a strong error analysis of classification outliers.  
- Fails to acknowledge that corpora like the BNC were tagged mainly to support downstream tasks (e.g., parsing); should evaluate whether distributional distinctions help parsing more than original tags/UPOS.  
- Insufficient engagement with prior work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012), so Section 5 offers limited novelty.  
- Claim that "distributional vectors contain PoS information" is hardly novel for English given many prior cluster-purity/many-to-one results.  
- Statement that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is inaccurate—previous work (e.g., Mikolov, Collobert) shows substantial syntactic encoding, and this contradicts Section 2.  
- Questionable choice to switch to UPOS; the fine-grained original tagset may provide richer, more informative distinctions.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear about whether failed attempts were the authors’ experiments or prior work, what failure criteria were used, and why Brown clusters were not discussed despite aligning with UPOS.  
- The note that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and likely driven by co-occurrence with function words like “the.”  
- Analysis focuses on frequent word/tag pairs; should examine low-frequency tail items to understand vector behavior and classifier errors and consider features beyond distributional/morphological signals.  
- Minor formatting issue: update citation style to use \newcite{} formatting.
