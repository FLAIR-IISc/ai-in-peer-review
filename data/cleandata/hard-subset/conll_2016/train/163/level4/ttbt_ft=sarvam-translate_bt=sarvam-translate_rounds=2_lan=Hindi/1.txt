This paper aims to demonstrate that the distribution information stored in a word vector model contains information about POS labels. They use a version of BNC annotated with UD POS and in which words are replaced with lemmas. They train word embeddings on this corpus, then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier to predict word POS. Evaluation is performed on the same corpus (using cross-validation) as well as other corpora. The results are clearly presented and discussed and analyzed in detail.

This paper is clear and well-written. The main problem with this paper is that it contains nothing new in terms of NLP or ML. It simply describes a set of experiments without any new NLP or ML ideas or methods. The results are indeed interesting, to the extent that they provide an empirical basis for the notion of POS. In that regard, it is certainly worthy of publication in a (quantitative/empirical) linguistic venue.

One more thing, the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings should be more widely cited (e.g., Lin, Ammar, Dure, and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Sogaard, and Goldberg 2016 ...).
