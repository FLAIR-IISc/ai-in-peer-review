The purpose of this paper is to demonstrate how distributed information stored in a word vector model can be used to represent information about POS tags. They use a version of BNC annotated by UD POS, where words are replaced by lemmas. They train word embedding in this corpus, then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier to predict POS. Assessments are conducted in the same corpus (using cross-validation) and in other corpus as well. The results are clearly presented and discussed and analyzed in detail.

This paper is clear and well-written. The main problem with this paper is that it does not offer anything new in the field of NLP or ML. It describes simple experiments without any new NLP or ML concepts or methods. The results are indeed interesting, in the sense that they provide an empirical basis for the POS concept. In that regard, it is certainly worthy of publication in a (quantitative/empirical) linguistic space.

Another point is that the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embedding should be more extensively cited (for example, Lin, Ammar, Duar, and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Sogaard, and Goldberg 2016...).
