- Paper studies how distributional word embeddings relate to PoS categories via a classifier, misclassification-driven outlier analysis, and a contextless PoS tagger.
- Main contributions: data-driven error analysis surfacing boundary cases and corpus tagging issues.
- Main contributions: empirical evidence that PoS signal is distributed across many embedding dimensions.
- Main contributions: a simple embedding-only PoS predictor that achieves moderate accuracy without context.
- Strength: insightful error analysis revealing interpretable patterns (participles as adjectives, NUM/NOUN confusions, SCONJ/ADP/ADV overlaps) and likely systematic BNC tagging errors.
- Strength: clear empirical setup with frequency-weighted training, held-out tests, UD transfer, and useful prototypical-vector visualizations.
- Strength: strong evidence (ANOVA/top-k curves) that PoS discrimination uses many embedding features, not just one or two.
- Strength: practical minimal-supervision baseline for resource-poor settings despite lacking sentence context.
- Weakness: weak framing—paper does not evaluate whether embedding-induced distinctions help downstream tasks like parsing or tagging.
- Weakness: limited novelty—existence of PoS signal in embeddings and many prior PoS induction results are not adequately acknowledged.
- Weakness: missing related work and inaccurate blanket claims that embeddings are “mostly semantic” despite known syntactic content.
- Weakness: methodological confound—training embeddings on lemma+PoS tokens leaks PoS into the vocabulary and biases results.
- Weakness: switch from CLAWS to UPOS loses finer-grained distinctions without justification or analysis of what is lost.
- Weakness: focus on frequent items ignores long-tail words where many boundary cases occur; tail/morphology/subword signals not analyzed.
- Weakness: some observations are unsurprising or under-analyzed (e.g., proper vs common noun differences may reflect determiners).
- Question: why prioritize UPOS over CLAWS and can the analysis be replicated at finer granularity?
- Question: can outlier analysis be repeated with embeddings trained on plain lemmas to rule out leakage?
- Question: what clustering experiments failed (algorithms, cluster counts, evaluation metrics) and how do results compare to Brown clusters?
- Question: how do you reconcile the “mostly semantic” claim with prior evidence of syntactic information in embeddings—can you report syntactic vs semantic probe results?
- Question: can you quantify and release the systematic tagging errors found to support corpus maintenance?
- Question: does redefining PoS via embeddings improve downstream parsing or tagging—can you run a small parsing experiment?
- Additional: add PoS induction and early embedding/tagging literature (Christodoulopoulos et al., Blunsom & Cohn, Collobert & Weston, Tsuboi).
- Additional: clarify stronger baselines (e.g., Brown clustering + classifier) and consider dependency-based embeddings or larger windows for robustness checks.
- Additional: explore tail performance, OOV handling, and subword models; fix citation placeholders and provide CLAWS→UPOS mapping and resource links.
