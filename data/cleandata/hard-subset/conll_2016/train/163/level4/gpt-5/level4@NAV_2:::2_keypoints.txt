- Studies PoS vs. word embeddings on BNC with simple classifiers; outlier/error analysis is a clear strength revealing annotation inconsistencies and graded category membership.  
- Sidesteps central motivation: PoS tagsets were engineered for downstream NLP (e.g., parsing); should evaluate whether embedding-induced distinctions improve downstream tasks vs. original tags/UPOS.  
- Omits substantial related work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012); Section 5’s novelty is limited given prior non-neural systems.  
- Abstract claim that embeddings contain PoS information is not novel for English; prior work already shows high many-to-one scores/cluster purity.  
- Claim that embedding relations are “mostly semantic” is inaccurate—embeddings also capture syntactic regularities (Mikolov, Collobert); the manuscript is internally inconsistent on this point.  
- Question the decision to map to UPOS—BNC/CLAWS fine-grained distinctions could enable richer analyses.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear: specify whether clustering failures were authors’ experiments, the failure criteria, and compare to Brown clusters (which often align with UPOS).  
- The separation of proper nouns from common nouns is unsurprising and may be driven by high-frequency function words.  
- Focusing on frequent word/tag pairs is practical but the paper should probe the long tail: behavior for rare types, classifier errors there, and possible augmentations beyond distributional/morphological features.  
- Minor formatting: replace sentential citations with \newcite{}.
