- Paper studies PoS–word embedding relations and provides a strong, detailed error analysis of classification outliers.  
- Main critique: authors ignore why corpora like the BNC were PoS-tagged (to aid downstream tasks such as parsing) and should reframe results around parsing usefulness.  
- Important related work on distributional PoS induction is missing (start with Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; include Blunsom & Cohn 2011, Yatbaz et al. 2012, etc.).  
- Section 5 offers limited novelty given prior non-neural systems with comparable performance under stricter knowledge constraints.  
- Claim in the abstract that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not new for English—many prior induction systems show this.  
- Statement that “relations between vectors are mostly semantic” is incorrect or overstated; prior work shows substantial syntactic content in embeddings.  
- Question the switch to UPOS: fine-grained original tagset may be more informative for downstream parsing and analysis.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear: specify whether failed attempts are authors’ or prior work, criteria for failure, and consider Brown-cluster-based vectors as a strong baseline.  
- The claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be trivial (high-frequency function words like “the” can drive differences).  
- Recommend analyzing low-frequency (tail) word–tag pairs and classifier errors for rare items to assess generalization and motivate features beyond distributional/morphological ones.  
- Minor: fix citation formatting (use \newcite{} for sentential references).
