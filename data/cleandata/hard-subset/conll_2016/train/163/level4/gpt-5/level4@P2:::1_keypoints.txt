- Paper shows skip-gram embeddings trained on lemmatized, UD-tagged BNC reliably encode POS information, enabling a simple classifier to predict POS without sentence context.
- Demonstrates high in-domain and out-of-domain (UD English) POS prediction accuracy from word vectors alone.
- Provides detailed error/outlier analysis revealing graded POS membership and annotation inconsistencies (e.g., numerals vs. nouns, adverbs vs. adjectives, PROPN vs. NOUN).
- Shows practical utility for corpus diagnostics and guideline refinement by flagging systematic tagging inconsistencies.
- Finds POS cues are distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than concentrated in a few, via feature ranking and ablation.
- Clear, well-organized experimental setup described sufficiently for replication (BNC preprocessing, embedding training, classifier design).
- Strong, insightful confusion and outlier analyses that support linguistic claims about soft POS boundaries.
- Demonstrates robustness: high F-scores with a basic logistic classifier and small context windows, performing reasonably without sentence context.
- Transparent reporting about where information resides in embeddings and empirical characterization of distributed representations.
- Limited methodological novelty—uses established techniques (skip-gram, logistic regression); contributions are primarily empirical/analytical.
- Related work engagement is incomplete; more discussion of prior embedding-based POS/tagging studies is needed and differentiation clarified.
- Main setup may be confounded by training on lemma+POS concatenations, risking memorization of tag-conditioned contexts rather than pure discovery of POS from embeddings.
- Outlier analysis is partly anecdotal and would benefit from systematic quantification (frequency effects, confidence calibration, significance tests).
- Comparisons and baselines could be unfair (e.g., token-level Stanford tagger without context); should include standard contextual baselines or clearly frame as a lexical probe.
- Requests for additional analyses: quantify frequency effects on misclassifications (stratify by frequency deciles).
- Ask for sensitivity checks to embedding hyperparameters and preprocessing choices (window size, negative samples, downsampling, lemmatization/casing).
- Request more ablations for the tag-free setting (expand prototypical lexicon, vary ambiguity thresholds, training size vs. accuracy).
- Ask for more systematic evidence for distributed-information claim (cumulative variance, mutual information per feature, regularized sparsity measures).
- Suggest release of BNC-to-UD mapping, trained models, and code to aid replication and corpus diagnostics.
- Overall: clear, careful, and empirically valuable paper that substantiates POS information in embeddings and offers useful analysis for corpus methodology, though novelty is analytical rather than methodological and related work should be expanded.
