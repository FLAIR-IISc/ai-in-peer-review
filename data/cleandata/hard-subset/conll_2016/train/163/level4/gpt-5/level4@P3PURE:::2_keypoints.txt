- Paper studies PoS tag information in word embeddings and includes a strong error analysis of classification outliers.  
- Missing critical motivation: PoS-tagged corpora like the BNC were created for downstream NLP (e.g., parsing), so reframing around parsing utility is needed.  
- Missing substantial related work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012) and more recent non-DNN studies.  
- Novelty concern: results (Section 5) are not clearly novel given prior systems with similar performance under stricter constraints.  
- Abstract claim that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not new for English and has been shown by many clustering/induction studies.  
- Incorrect assertion that vector relations are “mostly semantic”; prior work shows embeddings encode substantial syntactic information, contradicting the paper.  
- Questioning the switch to UPOS: original fine-grained tagset may be more informative and interesting for analysis.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear: were the failed attempts by the authors or prior work, what were the failure criteria, and why not consider Brown clusters?  
- The claimed distinction between proper and common nouns may be trivial (e.g., driven by function-word contexts like “the”) and not particularly insightful.  
- Recommend analyzing low-frequency tail words and classifier errors, and exploring non-distributional (or morphological) features to improve generalization.  
- Minor issue: use \newcite{} for sentential references (e.g., “Mikolov et al. (2013b) showed”).
