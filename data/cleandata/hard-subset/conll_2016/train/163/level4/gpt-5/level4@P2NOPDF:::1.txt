This paper investigates whether distributional information encoded in word vectors captures part-of-speech (POS) categories. The authors use a lemmatized version of the British National Corpus annotated with Universal Dependencies POS tags, train word embeddings on this corpus, and then fit a logistic regression classifier to predict POS from the learned vectors. Evaluation is conducted via cross-validation on the same corpus and on additional corpora to assess generalization. The experimental setup, results, and analyses are clearly presented and discussed in depth.

The paper is clear and well written. The main limitation is a lack of methodological novelty for NLP/ML: the pipeline—training embeddings and applying logistic regression to predict POS—is straightforward and does not introduce new ideas, models, or algorithms. While the results are indeed interesting and provide empirical support for the distributional basis of POS categories, the contribution is primarily empirical rather than technical. As such, the work seems better suited to a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue than to a venue emphasizing novel computational methods.

The related work section should be expanded to more thoroughly cover POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings (e.g., Lin, Ammar, Duer, and Levin 2015; Ling et al., EMNLP 2015; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg 2016). Including these would better situate the contribution within existing research.
