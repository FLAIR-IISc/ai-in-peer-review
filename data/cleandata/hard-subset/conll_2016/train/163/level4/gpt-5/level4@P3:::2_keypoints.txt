- Paper studies how distributional word embeddings encode PoS by training a multinomial classifier on lemmatized BNC mapped to UPOS, finding strong in-domain and cross-corpus performance.
- Error analysis uncovers “outlier” words whose distributional behavior diverges from annotations, revealing graded PoS membership and BNC annotation inconsistencies.
- Shows PoS-relevant information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized to a few.
- Presents a low-resource PoS predictor trained on untagged embeddings plus a small prototypical lexicon with reasonable accuracy.
- Main contribution: detailed, data-driven error analysis that can guide corpus auditing and reveals systematic tagging issues.
- Empirical contribution: robust evidence across BNC and UD that embeddings reliably encode PoS information.
- Analytical contribution: ANOVA-based feature ranking demonstrating distributed PoS cues across embedding components.
- Practical contribution: a simple diagnostic tool complementary to mainstream taggers for surfacing different annotation issues.
- Strength: linguistically insightful investigation of misclassified cases (e.g., participles, noun–adjective alternations, numerals).
- Strength: method generalizes across datasets, supporting broader applicability beyond the training corpus.
- Strength: supports “soft” PoS category boundaries consistent with linguistic descriptions.
- Weakness: novelty overstated—prior PoS induction work already documents strong PoS signals in distributional representations.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to classic baselines (e.g., Brown clusters, sparse context vectors) and established PoS induction systems.
- Weakness: limited extrinsic evaluation—no assessment of downstream impact (e.g., parsing) when using embedding-informed or corrected tags.
- Weakness: collapsing to UPOS loses fine-grained distinctions relevant to graded-category claims; need justification and analyses on original tags.
- Weakness: insufficient methodological detail on failed clustering attempts and frequency-weighting effects; class-wise and tail analyses missing.
- Key question: Which clustering algorithms, Ks, initializations, and evaluation metrics were tried and how did they fail compared to Brown clusters?
- Key question: Can you add baselines using Brown clusters, sparse context vectors, and existing PoS induction methods?
- Key question: Why prefer UPOS over original BNC tags, and do core findings hold on the original tagset?
- Key question: How does classifier performance vary with word frequency; what are error patterns in the low-frequency tail and can morphology help?
- Key question: Can a small parsing experiment compare standard tags, predicted tags, and corrected tags to test downstream utility?
- Additional comments: temper the claim that embeddings are “mostly semantic”; cite PoS induction literature (Christodoulopoulos et al., Blunsom & Cohn, Yatbaz et al.).
- Additional comments: clarify footnote 3 and provide full clustering experiment details; consider manual validation of detected inconsistencies and fix typos/references.
- Overall: well-executed, useful study with compelling error analysis, but needs stronger positioning against prior work, baseline comparisons, tagset justification, and extrinsic validation to reach publication standard.
