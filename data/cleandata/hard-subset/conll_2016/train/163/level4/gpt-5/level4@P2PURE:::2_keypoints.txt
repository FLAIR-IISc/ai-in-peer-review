- Paper studies PoS–embedding relationship and includes a strong, detailed error analysis of misclassifications.  
- Paper misses a key motivation: corpora like the BNC were tagged to support downstream tasks (e.g., parsing), so the framing should consider whether distributional distinctions help parsing more than the original tagset/UPOS.  
- Substantial prior work on distributional PoS induction is omitted; add reviews and non-DNN approaches (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012).  
- Given the prior literature, results in Section 5 show limited novelty because comparable performance exists from systems with stricter external-knowledge constraints.  
- Claim that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not new—long-established by distributional PoS induction and cluster purity results.  
- Statement that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is inaccurate; embeddings capture substantial syntactic information as shown by prior work (Mikolov et al., Collobert et al.).  
- Unclear rationale for switching to UPOS; fine-grained original tagset distinctions may be more informative and interesting.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear: specify whether failed attempts are yours or prior work, by what criteria they failed, and address Brown cluster vectors which align closely with UPOS.  
- Observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be unsurprising (e.g., driven by presence of “the”) and not especially noteworthy.  
- Recommend analyzing low-frequency tail words and classifier error types to identify alternatives to purely distributional (and morphological/lemmatized) features for better generalization.  
- Minor: use sentential citation style (e.g., “Mikolov et al. (2013b) showed …”).
