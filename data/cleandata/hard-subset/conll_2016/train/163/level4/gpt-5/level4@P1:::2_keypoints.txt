- Paper studies how BNC-trained word embeddings relate to PoS classes, using a classifier to find outlier words, argue for graded PoS membership, and show PoS info is distributed across many embedding dimensions.  
- Contribution: error analysis showing embeddings reveal boundary cases and annotation inconsistencies, with PoS information spread over many components.  
- Contribution: empirical confirmation across BNC and UD that embeddings encode PoS info well enough to separate many classes, including subtle distinctions.  
- Contribution: a preliminary context-free PoS predictor trained on ~1.5k prototypical words achieving reasonable accuracy.  
- Strength: insightful error analysis that identifies linguistically meaningful boundary cases, scheme mismatches, and potential annotation issues.  
- Strength: clear, simple, and robust methodology with consistent results on BNC and UD vocabularies.  
- Strength: practical value for corpus auditing and tagset refinement by flagging likely annotation errors.  
- Strength: compact supervision showing a small prototypical lexicon can yield a useful weak tagger for low-resource settings.  
- Weakness: missing central motivation about downstream utility—unclear if embedding-derived distinctions help parsing or other structured tasks.  
- Weakness: incomplete related work coverage on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al., Blunsom & Cohn, Yatbaz et al.), weakening novelty claims.  
- Weakness: overstated/incorrect claim that vector relations are “mostly semantic,” inconsistent with prior work showing syntactic regularities.  
- Weakness: unclear rationale for mapping to UPOS instead of preserving finer-grained BNC/CLAWS distinctions that could enrich analysis.  
- Weakness: methodological opacity about failed clustering attempts and lack of direct comparison to Brown clusters as baselines.  
- Question: can embedding-induced PoS distinctions improve downstream parsing or other structured tasks versus BNC tags/UPOS?  
- Question: how does this approach compare quantitatively to classical PoS induction methods under standard evaluations?  
- Question: please reconcile the “mostly semantic” characterization of embeddings with evidence of strong syntactic regularities.  
- Question: why choose UPOS over the original BNC tagset, and would finer-grained tags improve outlier analyses?  
- Question: clarify footnote 3—what clustering algorithms, cluster counts, and evaluation metrics were tried, and what specifically “failed”?  
- Question: could proper/common noun dissimilarity be driven by articles like “the”? Please run an ablation removing articles.  
- Question: analyze low-frequency types—how do vector quality and classifier errors behave, and would subword/morphological features help generalize?  
- Minor: fix citation formatting (use \newcite{}) and replace placeholder citations like “<MIKOLOV or COLOBERT>” with proper references.
