The aim of this paper is to show that distributional information captured by word vector models encodes information about POS labels. The authors use a version of the BNC annotated with UD POS tags, in which words have been replaced by their lemmas. They train word embeddings on this corpus and then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic regression classifier to predict a word’s POS. Evaluations are performed on the same corpus (using cross-validation) as well as on other corpora. The results are clearly presented and thoroughly analyzed.

The paper is clear and well written. The main issue is that it offers no substantial novelty in NLP or ML. It describes a set of straightforward experiments without introducing new ideas or methods. The results are interesting in that they provide empirical evidence for the linguistic validity of POS categories, showing that distributional semantics robustly encodes POS distinctions. In that sense, the contribution is primarily empirical and seems better suited to a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue.

The related work on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings should be cited more extensively (e.g., Lin, Ammar, Dyer, and Levin, 2015; Ling et al., 2015, EMNLP; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg, 2016, among others).
