- Clear research question: probes whether distributional word embeddings encode POS using lemmatized UD-BNC and a logistic classifier.  
- Experiments are clearly presented with thorough analysis and cross-corpus evaluation.  
- Empirical support for the hypothesis that POS categories are reflected in distributional patterns.  
- Limited novelty: standard embeddings + linear probe, contribution seen as incremental for CoNLL.  
- Potential circularity: embeddings and labels derived from the same corpus may overstate generalization; domains for representation learning, classifier training, and evaluation need clearer separation.  
- Type-level prediction ignores token-level syntactic ambiguity, making linguistic interpretation difficult; analysis by ambiguity class is needed.  
- Lemmatization removes morphological cues that predict POS, complicating interpretation; compare lemma vs surface-form or subword embeddings.  
- Baselines under-specified: need comparisons to count-based context vectors, Brown clusters, and frequency/majority-tag baselines.  
- OOV and coverage issues for out-of-domain evaluation are not fully described (unseen types, vocabulary overlap, tagset harmonization).  
- Reproducibility details missing: embedding algorithm, hyperparameters, dimensionality, window size, negative sampling, classifier regularization, class balance, and evaluation metrics.  
- Suggest strengthening related work coverage, citing prior POS/embedding and probing literature.  
- Suggest adding competitive baselines and ablations (count-based vectors, Brown clusters, shuffled/random embeddings, frequency baselines, CBOW vs skip-gram, window/dim variations, lemma vs surface).  
- Suggest clarifying evaluation design (separate in-/out-of-domain tests, train classifier on different corpora, report coverage/OOV handling, confidence intervals, significance tests).  
- Suggest deeper linguistic analyses (breakdown by frequency and ambiguity, confusion matrices, per-class scores, token-level diagnostics).  
- Recommend releasing code, trained embeddings, and annotated BNC splits for reproducibility.  
- Overall: clear and informative but lacking methodological novelty and stronger baselines; recommend rejection for CoNLL unless significantly improved or submitted to a more appropriate venue.
