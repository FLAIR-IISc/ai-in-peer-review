The goal of this paper is to demonstrate that distributional information encoded in word vector models contains cues about POS labels. The authors use a version of the BNC annotated with UD POS tags and with words replaced by lemmas. They train word embeddings on this corpus and then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic classifier to predict POS. Evaluation is performed on the same corpus using cross-validation, as well as on additional corpora. The results are clearly presented, with thorough discussion and analysis.

The paper is clear and well written. The main limitation is the absence of novelty in NLP or ML: it reports a set of straightforward experiments without introducing new ideas or methods. The results are nevertheless interesting in that they provide empirical support for the notion of POS. In this respect, the work would be well suited for publication in a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue.

On a related note, the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings should be cited more broadly (see, for example, Lin, Ammar, Duer, and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015, EMNLP; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg 2016).
