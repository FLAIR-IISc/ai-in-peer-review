This paper aims to demonstrate that distributional information captured by word vector models encodes part-of-speech (POS) information. The authors use a version of the BNC annotated with UD POS tags in which tokens have been replaced by lemmas. They first train word embeddings on this corpus, then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic regression classifier to predict POS. Evaluation is conducted on the same corpus via cross-validation, as well as on additional corpora. The results are clearly presented and extensively analyzed.

The paper is clear and well written. The primary concern, however, is the lack of novelty in NLP or ML: the work reports a series of straightforward experiments without introducing new ideas or methods. The findings are nonetheless interesting in that they provide empirical grounding for the concept of POS. In this respect, the paper would be suitable for publication in a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue.

Additionally, the literature on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings should be cited more comprehensively (see, for example, Lin, Ammar, Duer and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Søgaard and Goldberg 2016).
