This paper aims to demonstrate that distributional information captured by word vector models encodes part-of-speech (POS) labels. The authors use a lemmatized version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags. They train word embeddings on this corpus and then use the resulting vectors to train a logistic regression classifier to predict POS. Evaluations are conducted both on the same corpus via cross-validation and on additional corpora. The results are clearly presented and analyzed in depth.

The paper is clear and well written. The main issue, however, is the lack of novelty in NLP or ML: the work reports a set of straightforward experiments without introducing new ideas or methods. The findings are nonetheless interesting, as they provide empirical grounding for the notion of POS. In that respect, the work would be well suited to a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue.

The related work on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings should be cited more extensively (see, for instance, Lin, Ammar, Duer and Levin 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Søgaard and Goldberg 2016).
