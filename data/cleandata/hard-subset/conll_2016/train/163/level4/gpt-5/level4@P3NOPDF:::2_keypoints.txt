- Paper shows strong correlation between word embeddings and PoS categories with a detailed outlier/error analysis.  
- Outlier analysis is a clear strength, giving useful insights into distributional method successes and failures.  
- Missing motivation: corpora were PoS-tagged to support downstream tasks (e.g., parsing), so the central question should be utility for those tasks, not just whether embeddings encode PoS.  
- Reframe needed: compare parsing/tagging performance using gold tags, UPOS, and embedding-driven PoS predictions/clusters.  
- Related work undercited, especially PoS induction literature (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010) and non-neural methods (Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012).  
- Findings in Section 5 are not especially novel relative to prior systems with similar resources and clustering performance.  
- Abstract claim that embeddings contain PoS information is overstated for English and should be calibrated or clarified as adding finer-grained diagnostics or novel analysis.  
- Inaccurate/incomplete claim that vector relations are “mostly semantic”; prior work shows substantial syntactic regularities—reconcile and cite relevant literature.  
- Choice of UPOS tagset is unjustified; either motivate UPOS clearly or provide parallel analyses with original fine-grained tags.  
- Footnote 3 unclear about failed attempts—specify whether these were the authors’ experiments or prior work, and detail settings and failure criteria; include Brown clusters as a baseline.  
- Observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns is expected; control for determiners/capitalization to test the effect.  
- Head-focused analysis neglects the tail: analyze low-frequency words, classifier errors, and consider subword features to improve rare-word generalization.  
- Suggested experiments: feed predicted tags/clusters into a parser or run ablations mixing gold vs embedding-based cues.  
- Expand evaluations and metrics to standard PoS induction measures (many-to-one, V-measure, cluster purity) and report comparable numbers.  
- Add baselines and controls: Brown clusters, window-size ablations, different embedding types (skip-gram, CBOW, GloVe), and static vs subword-aware embeddings.  
- Clarify novelty: explicitly state what is new (depth of error analysis, specific tag-confusion diagnostics, cross-corpus robustness).  
- Improve interpretability by linking outliers to linguistic phenomena (polysemy, named entities, morphology, MWEs) and quantifying their prevalence.  
- Minor fixes: standardize citation format, correct author name spellings, add full citations for missing works, define abbreviations at first use, and state preprocessing choices.  
- Overall: strong empirical setup and outlier analysis, but limited novelty, incomplete related work, unjustified UPOS choice, and missing downstream utility evaluation.
