- The paper shows static word embeddings trained on BNC encode robust PoS information via a logistic classifier and misclassification analysis.  
- It proposes using distributional outliers to audit PoS annotations, exposing meaningful boundary cases and corpus-specific inconsistencies.  
- It provides evidence that PoS signal is distributed across many embedding dimensions, not concentrated in a few.  

- Clear, reproducible empirical setup with transparent training on BNC lemmas and strong reported metrics (F≈0.98 frequent, ≈0.91 mid-frequency, ≈0.99 UD lemmas).  
- Insightful error analysis linking classifier confusions to linguistically meaningful boundaries and annotation errors (e.g., participles/adjectives, adverb/adjective confusions).  
- The distributional auditing method highlights different, linguistically informative confusions compared to context-aware taggers, useful for corpus maintenance.  
- ANOVA and feature-ablation analyses support a distributed representation of PoS information across many embedding features.  
- The “proper predictor” trained on ≈1.5k unambiguous lemmas offers a practical context-free baseline (~0.84 accuracy) for low-resource lexicon induction.

- Limited methodological novelty: uses standard embeddings and logistic regression; main contribution is empirical analysis rather than new algorithms.  
- Risk of CV leakage: 10-fold CV at the instance level may let identical word vectors appear in both train and test, inflating high accuracy; grouped CV by type is needed.  
- Limited generality: embeddings trained only on BNC and UD evaluation excludes ~27% of types, introducing selection bias and limiting external validity.  
- Component-level findings may be unstable across seeds, algorithms, and hyperparameters; robustness checks are missing.  
- Comparison baseline is imperfect: evaluating standard taggers on isolated tokens is artificial; alternatives like lexicon lookup, Brown clusters, or POS induction would be fairer baselines.

- Did you use grouped cross-validation by word type to prevent identical vectors appearing in train and test?  
- How sensitive are results (especially salient components and PoS similarity patterns) to seeds, embedding algorithms, window sizes, and corpora?  
- How does classifier performance vary with lemma ambiguity; can you quantify accuracy as a function of PoS ambiguity?  
- Was any manual adjudication done to confirm claimed annotation inconsistencies?  
- Can you report UD evaluation results with simple OOV handling (subword embeddings or backoff lexicon) to assess end-to-end coverage?

- Suggested related work and baselines to cite/include: Lin et al. (2015), Ling et al. (2015), Plank et al. (2016), Collobert et al. (2011), and earlier clustering approaches such as Brown clusters.

- Overall: a clear, well-executed empirical study with valuable linguistic insights and auditing utility; recommend acceptance conditional on stronger validation (grouped CV, robustness checks) and broader evaluation.
