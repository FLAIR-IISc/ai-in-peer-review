- Paper studies links between PoS tags and word embeddings, with interesting but somewhat straightforward conclusions and a strong error analysis.
- Strength: thorough classification outlier/error analysis.
- Missing overarching motivation: BNC-style PoS tags were created to support downstream tasks (e.g., parsing); paper should reframe around whether embeddings capture distinctions more useful for parsing than original tags/UPOS.
- Significant related work on distributional PoS induction is omitted (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010 review; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012); this limits novelty claims.
- Claim that “distributional vectors contain PoS information” is not novel for English given prior PoS induction work.
- Assertion that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is incorrect—previous work shows embeddings encode substantial syntactic information.
- Inconsistency: the paper reverses the semantic/syntactic claim later (Section 2).
- Questionable choice to switch to UPOS—fine-grained original tagset may convey more information.
- Footnote 3 is unclear: clarify whether reported failed attempts are the authors’ or prior work, failure criteria, and how Brown clusters compare (they align closely with UPOS).
- Claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns may be trivially explained by presence/absence of “the” and is not especially insightful.
- Limiting analysis to most frequent word/tag pairs omits the tail—examining low-frequency words and classifier error types could suggest improvements beyond distributional/morphological features.
- Minor formatting suggestion: use sentential citations (e.g., \newcite{}) rather than parenthetical ones.
