The paper’s objective is to demonstrate that distributional information in word embedding models carries signal about POS categories. The authors use a lemmatized version of the BNC annotated with Universal Dependencies POS, train word embeddings on this corpus, and then fit a multinomial logistic classifier to predict POS from the resulting vectors. They evaluate via cross-validation on the same corpus and also on external corpora. The results are clearly reported and accompanied by substantial analysis and discussion.

The manuscript is clear and well written. The primary concern is the lack of novelty in NLP/ML: the work consists of a series of straightforward experiments without introducing new methodological ideas or modeling techniques. The findings are nevertheless interesting in that they empirically substantiate the connection between distributional representations and POS. In that sense, the contribution seems well suited to a quantitative/empirical linguistics venue.

Separately, the related work on POS tagging and POS induction with embeddings should be covered more comprehensively (e.g., Lin, Ammar, Duer and Levin 2015; Ling et al., 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg, 2016).
