- Shows distributional vectors encode PoS affiliation; contributions: (1) finds annotation inconsistencies via classifier error analysis, (2) identifies words with graded/soft PoS affiliations, (3) demonstrates PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions.  
- Classifier outliers reveal genuine PoS annotation inconsistencies and illuminate fluid part-of-speech boundaries.  
- PoS information is distributed across numerous vector components (at least ~100 of 300), a notable technical insight about embeddings.  
- Strong classifier performance (F-score 0.91 on unseen data, 0.99 on UD Treebank) indicates practical utility for PoS tasks and low-resource scenarios.  
- Paper does not address the primary purpose of PoS tags for NLP tasks (e.g., parsing) or evaluate whether embeddings improve downstream parsing compared to original tags.  
- The claim that embeddings contain PoS information is not novel given extensive prior distributional PoS induction literature.  
- Important related work (Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012) is omitted, weakening the novelty of Section 5.  
- Why was the Universal POS tagset (UPOS) used instead of the more fine-grained original BNC tagset?  
- Please clarify the failed "one-feature baseline classifier" (footnote 3) and why Brown cluster vectors were not considered.  
- How would including low-frequency tail words affect the analysis and reveal different PoS behavior?
