- Paper introduces a method to detect lexical outliers whose distributional patterns conflict with assigned PoS tags, revealing annotation inconsistencies in BNC.  
- Finds real annotation errors (e.g., adjectives/adverbs mislabeled in BNC) supported by distributional evidence.  
- Shows PoS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions (≥100), not confined to a few features.  
- Demonstrates a distributional classifier achieves very high accuracy on UD Treebank (F-score 0.99) using embeddings without contextual features.  

- Strength: Error-driven outlier analysis effectively uncovers actionable corpus annotation issues.  
- Strength: Classifier generalizes across corpora (BNC embeddings → high UD performance), reducing corpus-specific concern.  
- Strength: Empirical evidence that PoS information is spread over many dimensions offers a nuanced view of PoS boundaries.  

- Weakness: Core claim (embeddings encode PoS) lacks novelty given prior work (e.g., Liu et al. 2016; Tsvetkov et al. 2015); main novelty is the error analysis.  
- Weakness: Paper does not address BNC’s original tagging purpose or whether distributional distinctions improve parsing or practical downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: Related work incomplete; classifier baseline (accuracy 0.84) and claims of novelty are questioned given prior clustering/tagging methods (e.g., Brown clustering).  

- Question: How do you reconcile PoS being distributed across many dimensions with conjunctions showing a single dominant feature—exception or context-dependent?  
- Question: Why use UPOS instead of BNC’s fine-grained tags, which might better reveal annotation nuances?  
- Question: With classifier accuracy at 0.84 (contextless), how would this method integrate into full tagging pipelines or low-resource scenarios?  

- Comment: Correct the overbroad claim that vector relations are “mostly semantic”—prior work shows syntactic regularities too.  
- Comment: Focusing on frequent words ignores tail cases—how would low-frequency outliers be handled?  
- Comment: Clarify footnote about “failed attempts”: whose experiments, and what were the failure criteria (e.g., one-feature baseline F-score)?  
- Comment: Fix citation formatting for consistency (e.g., Mikolov et al. 2013b).
