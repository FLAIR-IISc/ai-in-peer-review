

The paper investigates whether distributional information within word vector models encodes part-of-speech (POS) label information. Utilizing a version of the British National Corpus (BNC) annotated with Universal Dependencies (UD) POS tags and lemmatized words, the authors train word embeddings on this corpus and subsequently employ these vectors to train a logistic regression classifier for POS prediction. Evaluations are conducted both on the same corpus (via cross-validation) and on additional corpora. The results are presented clearly, thoroughly discussed, and analyzed in depth.

The paper is well-written and clear. However, the primary limitation is the absence of novel contributions to NLP or machine learning. The work describes a series of straightforward experiments without introducing new NLP or ML methods or conceptual insights. While the results are empirically interesting—providing tangible evidence supporting the concept of POS—they do not advance the field methodologically. Consequently, the paper is suitable for publication in a quantitative or empirical linguistics venue.

Additionally, the related work on POS tagging and POS induction using word embeddings requires more comprehensive citation (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015 [EMNLP]; Plank, Søgaard, and Goldberg 2016).
