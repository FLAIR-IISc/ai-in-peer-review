- Paper shows a clear correspondence between distributional vectors and PoS categories; detailed error analysis of classification outliers is a strength.  
- Paper ignores the original purpose of corpora like the BNC (designed for downstream NLP/parsing), so discussion should assess whether distributional vectors aid parsing versus traditional PoS/UPOS.  
- Missing important related work: must cite Christodoulopoulos et al. (2010) and include recent non-DNN approaches (e.g., Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012).  
- Results in Section 5 lack novelty because prior systems with stricter external-knowledge constraints reach comparable performance.  
- Claim that distributional vectors encode PoS information is not new for English—many PoS induction systems have already shown similar findings.  
- Assertion that vector relations are “mostly semantic” mischaracterizes the literature; prior work shows substantial syntactic information in embeddings, and the paper contradicts itself.  
- Switching to UPOS is unjustified; the original fine-grained tagset may be more linguistically meaningful.  
- Footnote 3 is unclear about who attempted the failed experiments and what failure criteria were; note that Brown cluster vectors align well with UPOS.  
- The observation that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and partly driven by high-frequency function words like “the.”  
- Analysis should be extended to rare words and their error patterns to explore alternatives for improving PoS generalization beyond distributional/morphological features.  
- Minor formatting issue: replace in-text citations with \newcite{} formatting.
