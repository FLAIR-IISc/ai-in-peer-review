- Study question: whether distributional word vectors encode POS information using a lemma-processed BNC with UD tags.
- Method: train word embeddings on the corpus and use a logistic regression classifier to predict POS.
- Evaluation: cross-validation on BNC and testing on external corpora; results are clearly presented and analyzed.
- Writing and structure: paper is well-structured and clearly written.
- Main limitation: lacks methodological novelty—experiments are straightforward and use standard methods.
- Empirical contribution: confirms that distributional semantics correlates with POS, offering useful validation for linguistic theory.
- Venue suitability: better matched to quantitative/empirical linguistics than CONLL, which expects methodological innovation.
- Related-work gap: fails to adequately cite and situate itself relative to prior work (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).
- Incrementality: omission of related work makes the contribution appear incremental given existing embedding-based POS studies.
- Suggested improvements: introduce novel models/architectures or provide deeper analysis linking POS correlations to specific linguistic phenomena.
- Overall verdict: useful baseline and confirmatory study but does not advance the state of the art for NLP.
