- Paper studies PoS tags vs. word embeddings with expected conclusions; detailed error analysis of outliers is a strong contribution.  
- Fails to consider that PoS-tagged corpora were created for parsing; should evaluate whether distributional vectors are more useful for parsing than original tags/UPOS.  
- Omits essential related work on distributional PoS induction (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012); given this literature, Section 5’s results aren’t novel.  
- Abstract’s claim that distributional vectors contain PoS information is neither new nor surprising for English.  
- Incorrectly claims relations between vectors are mostly semantic; prior work (Mikolov et al. 2013b, Collobert et al. 2011) shows substantial syntactic information, and the paper later contradicts itself.  
- Unclear rationale for preferring UPOS over the linguistically more meaningful original fine-grained tagset.  
- Footnote 3 is ambiguous about who conducted the “failed attempts” and what failure criteria were used; Brown clusters reportedly align well with UPOS.  
- The claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and likely driven by high-frequency function words (e.g., “the”).  
- Analysis focuses on frequent word/tag pairs; should extend to low-frequency items to assess vector quality and motivate alternatives for rare words.  
- Minor formatting request: replace sentential citations with \newcite{}.
