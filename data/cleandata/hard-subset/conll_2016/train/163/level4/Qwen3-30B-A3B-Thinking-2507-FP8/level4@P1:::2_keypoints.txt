- Contribution: Shows PoS information is distributed across multiple embedding dimensions rather than concentrated in a few features.  
- Contribution: Uses classifier outliers to reveal annotation inconsistencies in the BNC.  
- Contribution: Provides evidence for "soft" or gradient PoS boundaries via misclassification patterns.  

- Strength: Systematic outlier analysis uncovers concrete annotation edge cases (e.g., participles, currency terms) challenging rigid PoS boundaries.  
- Strength: Logistic regression classifier achieves high accuracy on BNC and UD, demonstrating PoS information is present in distributional vectors alone.  
- Strength: Empirical results show PoS affiliation improves steadily with more features, supporting distributed/gradient category claims.  

- Weakness: Paper lacks framing about the practical purpose of PoS tags for NLP and whether distributional vectors offer downstream advantages.  
- Weakness: Important prior PoS induction work (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011) is omitted, calling novelty into question.  
- Weakness: Misstates embedding semantics by claiming relations are mostly semantic, contradicting evidence that embeddings capture syntactic patterns.  
- Weakness: Unjustified shift from fine-grained BNC tags to coarse UPOS, potentially discarding linguistically relevant distinctions.  

- Question: Is the "one-feature baseline classifier" F-score 0.22 the authors' result or cited from prior work—please clarify the source.  
- Question: Why replace BNC fine-grained tags with UPOS, and would the findings replicate using original tags?  
- Question: How does this work compare to Brown clustering (Yatbaz et al. 2012) which reportedly aligns well with UPOS?  
- Question: How does the distributional-only classifier handle low-frequency words, and could morphological features improve robustness?
