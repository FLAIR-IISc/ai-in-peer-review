- Strength: Detailed error analysis of classification outliers is a significant contribution.  
- Missing foundational context: POS-tagged corpora like the BNC were created for downstream NLP (e.g., parsing), not purely semantic prototype analysis.  
- Reframe needed: Evaluate whether distributional vector distinctions are more useful for parsing than original tags/UPOS.  
- Related work omitted: Add distributional POS induction literature (e.g., Christodoulopoulos et al. 2010; Blunsom & Cohn 2011; Yatbaz et al. 2012).  
- Novelty concern: Results in Section 5 show limited novelty given prior non-DNN systems with similar constraints/performance.  
- Overclaimed result: The abstract's claim that "distributional vectors contain information about POS affiliation" is not novel for English.  
- Incorrect/contradictory claim: Statement that vector relations are “mostly semantic” is wrong—prior work shows substantial syntactic information in embeddings.  
- Tagset choice questioned: Why use UPOS instead of the more linguistically informative fine-grained original tagset?  
- Footnote 3 unclear: Specify who attempted the failed experiments, failure criteria, and how Brown cluster vectors (which align well with UPOS) relate.  
- Uninformative observation: The claim that proper nouns differ from common nouns is unsurprising and likely explained by frequency effects.  
- Analysis scope limited: Focus on frequent word/tag pairs is pragmatic but exploring tail distributions and low-frequency error patterns would be more insightful.  
- Suggestion for future work: Tail analysis could motivate features beyond distributional/morphological signals to improve POS generalization for rare words.  
- Minor formatting: Replace sentential references with \newcite{} citation formatting.
