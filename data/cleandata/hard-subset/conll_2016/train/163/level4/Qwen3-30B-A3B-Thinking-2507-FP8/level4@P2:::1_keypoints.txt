- Paper investigates whether distributional word embeddings encode POS information by training embeddings on a lemmatized, POS-tagged BNC and using logistic regression for POS prediction.  
- Contributions: shows embeddings carry robust POS signals beyond semantics.  
- Contributions: analyzes misclassifications to uncover POS annotation inconsistencies and boundary cases.  
- Contributions: finds POS information is distributed across many embedding dimensions rather than localized.  
- Strength: strong empirical result (97.9% F-score on BNC) suggesting POS membership is often gradient/soft.  
- Strength: misclassification analysis reveals systematic annotation errors and linguistically meaningful borderline cases.  
- Strength: demonstrates that many vector dimensions (≈100/300) are needed to capture POS information, with some components being highly indicative for specific classes.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—uses standard skip-gram embeddings and logistic regression without new algorithms.  
- Weakness: literature review is incomplete; key related works (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016) are missing.  
- Weakness: paper under-argues the practical significance for NLP applications and advantages over existing POS taggers.  
- Question: how does this approach compare to prior POS induction methods using embeddings and what advantages does it offer?  
- Question: how does the reported 84% accuracy on UD Treebank without context compare to standard neural POS taggers, and what are implications for low-resource languages?  
- Question: what are the implications of POS being distributed across ≥100 dimensions for future embedding design and interpreting embedding geometry?
