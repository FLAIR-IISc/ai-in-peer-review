- Paper investigates whether distributional word vectors encode POS using a lemma-based BNC annotated with UD POS tags.
- Authors train word embeddings on this corpus and use them to train a logistic regression POS classifier, evaluated with cross-validation and external datasets.
- Results are clearly presented and show that word vectors reliably capture POS-related patterns.
- The study is well-written, methodologically sound, and has a transparent experimental design with robust evaluation.
- Contribution is primarily empirical validation of the linguistic intuition that POS correlates with distributional similarity.
- Core limitation: no novel NLP/ML methodology—standard embedding training plus a simple classifier.
- The paper fails to adequately engage with extensive prior work on embeddings for POS tagging/induction (e.g., Lin et al. 2015; Ling et al. 2015; Plank et al. 2016).
- Omitting and not comparing to these prior studies risks misrepresenting the work’s novelty and value.
- Given its lack of methodological innovation, the paper is not a good fit for CONLL’s focus on cutting-edge NLP research.
- The work would be better targeted to venues emphasizing empirical linguistics or resources (e.g., Computational Linguistics, Language Resources and Evaluation).
- Required revisions: substantially strengthen the related work, explicitly compare/contrast with prior efforts, and clarify the theoretical significance.
- Summary: methodologically rigorous and informative for linguistics, but offers little new for NLP/ML; limited suitability for an NLP venue without major revisions.
