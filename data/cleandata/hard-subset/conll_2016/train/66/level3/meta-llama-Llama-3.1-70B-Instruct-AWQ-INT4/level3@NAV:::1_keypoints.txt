- Summary: Transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs, with greedy linear-time inference and competitive results on CoNLL 2008/2009.

- Contribution: Joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing via a learned representation of the entire algorithm state using stack LSTMs.

- Contribution: Greedy inference algorithm with linear time complexity for efficient parsing.

- Contribution: Empirical competitiveness on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks.

- Strength: Novel integration of state representation for joint parsing using stack LSTMs.

- Strength: Efficient, practical inference due to greedy linear-time algorithm.

- Strength: Competitive empirical performance relative to existing joint systems.

- Strength: Well-written paper with clear explanations and a strong related-work section.

- Strength: Sound and well-justified methodology with clear system description and hyperparameter choices.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty given reliance on prior work (Henderson et al., Dyer et al.).

- Weakness: Dependence on pretrained embeddings, which may limit applicability across languages/domains.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons to other joint parsing paradigms (e.g., graph-based or pipeline approaches).

- Question: How will the model be extended to incorporate morphological features used by other state-of-the-art systems?

- Question: Please provide training details (number of epochs, convergence criteria). 

- Question: How do the authors plan to address potential overfitting in the joint model?
