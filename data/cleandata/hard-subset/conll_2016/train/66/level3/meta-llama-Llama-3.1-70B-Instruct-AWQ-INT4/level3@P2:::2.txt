**Summary**

The paper presents a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser that uses a transition-based approach with stack long short-term memories (LSTMs) to learn a representation of the entire parser state. The parser is evaluated on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks, and the results show that it outperforms previous joint parsing models. The paper also presents a multilingual evaluation on the CoNLL 2009 dataset, where the parser's performance is comparable to the top systems.

**Contributions**

1. **Joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing**: The paper presents a novel approach to joint parsing that uses a transition-based approach with stack LSTMs to learn a representation of the entire parser state.
2. **Greedy inference algorithm**: The paper proposes a greedy inference algorithm that has linear time complexity, including feature extraction.
3. **Competitive results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009**: The paper presents competitive results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks, outperforming previous joint parsing models.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to joint parsing**: The paper presents a novel approach to joint parsing that uses a transition-based approach with stack LSTMs to learn a representation of the entire parser state.
2. **Fast inference algorithm**: The paper proposes a greedy inference algorithm that has linear time complexity, including feature extraction.
3. **Competitive results**: The paper presents competitive results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks, outperforming previous joint parsing models.
4. **Well-written paper**: The paper is well-written and builds substantially on recent LSTM techniques applied to Henderson et al.'s framework.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Simple greedy algorithm**: The paper uses a simple greedy algorithm, which limits comparability to other works. Beam-search results should be reported to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
2. **Lack of clarity in Section 2**: Section 2 needs clarification on the presence/role of both A1 and C-A1 links for task understanding.
3. **Lack of clarity in Section 3.3**: Section 3.3 should clarify why predicate word and disambiguated sense vectors are concatenated, as this appears redundant.
4. **Confusing organization in Section 4**: The organization of subsections 4.1/4.2 is confusing with respect to multilinguality. The paper should clarify the scope of CoNLL-2008 vs CoNLL-2009.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide beam-search results to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the parser's performance?
2. Can you clarify the presence/role of both A1 and C-A1 links in Section 2 for task understanding?
3. Can you clarify why predicate word and disambiguated sense vectors are concatenated in Section 3.3?
4. Can you reorganize Section 4 to clarify the scope of CoNLL-2008 vs CoNLL-2009?

**Additional Comments**

The paper presents a novel approach to joint parsing that uses a transition-based approach with stack LSTMs to learn a representation of the entire parser state. The results are competitive with previous joint parsing models, and the paper is well-written and builds substantially on recent LSTM techniques applied to Henderson et al.'s framework. However, the paper has some limitations, including the use of a simple greedy algorithm and a lack of clarity in some sections. Addressing these limitations will strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the parser's performance.
