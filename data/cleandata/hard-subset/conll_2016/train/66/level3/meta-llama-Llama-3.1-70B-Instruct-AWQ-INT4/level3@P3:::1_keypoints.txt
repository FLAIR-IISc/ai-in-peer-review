- Summary: Proposes a Stack LSTM parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies, uses greedy linear-time inference, and achieves top joint-model results on CoNLL 2008/2009 English.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser that represents the entire algorithm state with stack LSTMs.
- Contribution 2: Obtains the best parsing performance among models that jointly learn syntax and semantics on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates the effectiveness of stack LSTMs for joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing.
- Strength 1: Novel approach differing from feature-engineered or pipeline methods by leveraging stack LSTMs.
- Strength 2: Achieves state-of-the-art results among joint models on standard English benchmarks.
- Strength 3: Uses an efficient greedy inference algorithm with linear-time complexity including feature extraction.
- Strength 4: Well-written paper with clear explanations and a strong related-work section.
- Weakness 1: Limited methodological originality, largely building on Henderson et al. and Dyer et al.
- Weakness 2: Heavy reliance on pretrained embeddings for Chinese results, which may not generalize to all languages.
- Weakness 3: Comparisons are limited to a small set of previous joint models and lack comprehensive benchmarking against other state-of-the-art systems.
- Question 1: How will the parser be extended to incorporate morphological features used by other top systems?
- Question 2: Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and choices?
- Question 3: How do the authors plan to address overfitting, especially evident on the out-of-domain Brown test set?
