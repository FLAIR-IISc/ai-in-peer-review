- Transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs, learns global algorithm-state representation, uses greedy linear-time inference, and reports top joint-model results on CoNLL 2008–2009.  
- Combines Henderson et al.'s joint syntactic/semantic transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM to enable learning of full algorithm-state representations and competitive parsing performance.  
- Demonstrates stack LSTMs effectively capture long-range dependencies and replace expensive hand-crafted features in parsing.  
- Proposes a greedy inference algorithm with linear-time complexity (including feature extraction) for efficiency and scalability.  
- Achieves competitive CoNLL 2008–2009 results, outperforming existing joint systems.  
- Provides a clear system description, justified hyperparameters, and a strong related-work discussion.  
- Presents an efficient inference approach that is scalable in practice.  
- Limited methodological novelty—largely integrates prior work (Henderson and Dyer) without major new innovations.  
- Reliance on pretrained embeddings (notably for Chinese) may account for a significant portion of performance gains.  
- Evaluation is limited to CoNLL 2008–2009, leaving generalizability to other languages and tasks uncertain.  
- How will the model incorporate morphological features used by top systems for highly inflective languages (e.g., Czech)?  
- Can the authors provide comparisons of the greedy inference algorithm’s effectiveness versus beam search or other inference methods?  
- How will the authors address overfitting, given the Joint model’s strong in-domain but weak out-of-domain performance?
