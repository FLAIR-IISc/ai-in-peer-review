- Novel transition-based parser that jointly learns syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs and representations of the full algorithm state instead of expert features.
- Demonstrated state-of-the-art performance among joint models on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks.
- Integration of Henderson et al.’s transition approach with Dyer et al.’s stack LSTM architecture to create the new joint parser.
- Competitive experimental results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English benchmarks validating the approach.
- Parser is efficient and scalable with linear time complexity and ~177.6±18s end-to-end runtime on the CoNLL 2009 English test set (single core).
- Well-designed architecture that effectively combines stack LSTMs with joint syntactic-semantic parsing.
- Strong empirical results supporting the model’s effectiveness.
- Demonstrated runtime efficiency and scalability.
- Limited methodological originality since the work builds on Henderson et al. and Dyer et al.
- Results (especially on Chinese) may rely heavily on pretrained embeddings rather than model innovation.
- Comparisons to other state-of-the-art approaches are limited and could be expanded.
- Question about addressing potential overfitting to the WSJ domain given Hybrid model’s Brown test performance.
- Request for details on preprocessing and oracle conversion when converting semantic parses into transitions.
- Inquiry about plans to incorporate morphological features for highly inflective languages like Czech.
