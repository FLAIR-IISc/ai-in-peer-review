- Summary: Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using transition-based stack LSTMs, evaluated on CoNLL-2008/2009 with competitive or superior results.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel transition-based joint parsing approach that learns whole parser-state representations via stack LSTMs.
- Contribution 2: Proposes a greedy inference algorithm with linear time complexity including feature extraction.
- Contribution 3: Reports competitive English results on CoNLL-2008/2009, outperforming prior joint models and comparable multilingual performance on CoNLL-2009.
- Strength 1: Novel application of stack LSTMs to joint syntactic–semantic parsing.
- Strength 2: Fast greedy inference with linear-time complexity.
- Strength 3: Strong empirical results on benchmark datasets.
- Strength 4: Well-written paper that builds on recent LSTM techniques and prior frameworks.
- Weakness 1: Reliance on a simple greedy algorithm limits comparability; beam-search results are missing.
- Weakness 2: Section 2 is unclear about the role/presence of both A1 and C-A1 links.
- Weakness 3: Section 3.3 lacks justification for concatenating predicate word and disambiguated sense vectors.
- Weakness 4: Section 4’s organization is confusing regarding multilingual experiments and CoNLL-2008 vs CoNLL-2009 scope.
- Question 1: Please provide beam-search results for a more comprehensive evaluation.
- Question 2: Please clarify the presence/role of both A1 and C-A1 links in Section 2.
- Question 3: Please explain why predicate word and sense vectors are concatenated in Section 3.3.
- Question 4: Please reorganize/clarify Section 4 to delineate CoNLL-2008 vs CoNLL-2009 experiments.
- Additional comment: The approach is promising and well-presented but would be strengthened by clarifications and broader inference experiments.
