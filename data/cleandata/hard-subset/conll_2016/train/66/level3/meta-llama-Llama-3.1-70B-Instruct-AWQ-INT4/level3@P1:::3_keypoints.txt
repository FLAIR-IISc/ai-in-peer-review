- Presents a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using a stack LSTM to represent full algorithm state, trained on CoNLL 2008/2009 and achieving SOTA among joint models.
- Introduces a new transition system that improves on Henderson et al. (2008) by leveraging stack LSTM state representations.
- Empirically demonstrates neural networks’ effectiveness for joint syntax–semantics parsing where linear models struggled.
- Attributes success to automatic feature learning, removing reliance on expert-crafted features.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English tasks.
- Offers efficient parsing (≈177.6±18 s to parse CoNLL 2009 English test on a single core).
- Claims flexibility of the transition system to integrate with other neural architectures (e.g., global beam-search) and orthogonal improvements.
- Limited comparison scope: evaluates only against other joint models, not pipeline models with expert features.
- Performance dependence on pretrained word embeddings, notably for Chinese.
- Lacks handling of morphological features, which may hurt performance on highly inflective languages.
- Asks how morphological features will be incorporated and what benefits are expected.
- Requests details on integrating the transition system with other neural architectures such as global beam-search.
- Inquires about plans to derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system and anticipated benefits.
- Overall: significant and impressive contribution with clear limitations and sensible avenues for future work.
