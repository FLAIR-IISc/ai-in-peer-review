- Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire parser state, with greedy linear-time inference and evaluation on CoNLL 2008/2009 showing competitive results without full hand-crafted syntactic features.  
- Main contribution: joint syntactic and semantic parsing via stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic features for semantic role labeling.  
- Main contribution: a greedy inference algorithm with linear-time complexity (including feature extraction) for faster, more efficient parsing.  
- Main contribution: competitive empirical results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks.  
- Strength: the greedy inference algorithm is faster and more efficient than prior beam-search approaches.  
- Strength: the model requires no hand-crafted features of the full syntactic parse, improving flexibility and adaptability.  
- Strength: reported results are competitive with older systems on standard CoNLL benchmarks.  
- Weakness: use of a simple greedy algorithm limits comparability to works that use beam search.  
- Weakness: paper does not report beam-search results, leaving evaluation incomplete.  
- Weakness: subsections 4.1 and 4.2 are organized confusingly regarding multilinguality and lack a clear comparison to Henderson et al.  
- Question: please provide beam-search results for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Question: how will you address limited comparability to beam-search-based methods?  
- Question: can you clarify the organization of subsections 4.1 and 4.2 with respect to multilinguality?  
- Question: please summarize the differences between your transitions and those in Henderson et al. to clarify novelty.  
- Question: why concatenate predicate word and disambiguated sense vectors in Section 3.3, and does this outperform a single vector representation?
