- Joint syntactic and semantic transition-based parser using a stack LSTM that models the full algorithmic state and achieves state-of-the-art on CoNLL 2008/2009 English.
- Authors attribute success to neural networks’ automatic feature learning and greater parameter efficiency versus linear models.

- New transition system improving on Henderson et al. (2008) that leverages stack LSTMs to capture long-range dependencies and contextual information.
- Empirical demonstration that neural networks can successfully handle joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing where linear models struggled.
- Automatic feature learning obviates the need for expert-crafted features.

- Achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming previous joint models with hand-crafted features.
- More parameter-efficient modeling leading to potential speedups in training and inference.
- Transition system appears flexible and likely compatible with other neural architectures (e.g., global beam-search).

- Comparison is limited to previous joint models with expert features and lacks broader benchmarking against other state-of-the-art approaches.
- No theoretical analysis of the transition system or network architecture to explain why the model works.
- Reliance on pretrained word embeddings may limit applicability across languages or domains without such resources.
