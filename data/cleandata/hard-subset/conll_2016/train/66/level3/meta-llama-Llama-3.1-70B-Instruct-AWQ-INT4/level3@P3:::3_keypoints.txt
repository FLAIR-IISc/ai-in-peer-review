- Introduces a new transition system for joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing improving on Henderson (2008).  
- Proposes learning representations for the entire parser algorithmic state rather than only top-stack items.  
- Uses a stack LSTM to capture distant stack information and inform parsing decisions.  
- Employs entirely greedy inference instead of beam search.  
- Provides empirical evidence that neural networks succeed where linear models struggled for joint parsing.  
- Outperforms previous joint parsing models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English shared tasks.  
- Achieves multilingual results comparable to top CoNLL 2009 systems.  
- Argues neural models are more parameter-efficient than linear models for joint tasks.  
- Strength: clear innovation in transition system design.  
- Strength: strong empirical demonstration of neural approaches for joint parsing.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis and breakdown of model mistakes.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison to other neural-network-based joint parsing approaches.  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed error analysis, compare to other neural methods on specific tasks/datasets, and evaluate alternative architectures (RNNs/transformers).
