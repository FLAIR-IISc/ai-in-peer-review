- Presents a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs, achieving state-of-the-art on CoNLL 2008/2009 English.  
- Proposes a new transition system improving on Henderson et al. (2008) to enable joint parsing.  
- Demonstrates that neural network–based joint parsing outperforms previous linear models.  
- Achieves linear-time complexity and fast runtime (~177.6±18 s to parse CoNLL 2009 English on one core).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks.  
- Efficient and practical due to speed and linear complexity.  
- Eliminates need for expert-crafted features, reducing feature engineering.  
- Adapts easily to non-English languages and is competitive on the CoNLL 2009 multilingual task.  
- Shows potential overfitting to training data, notably on CoNLL 2009 English.  
- Relies on greedy inference, which may be suboptimal in some cases.  
- Performance for non-English languages may depend on availability of high-quality pretrained word embeddings.  
- Questions for authors: how to address overfitting, how OOV words/unseen predicates are handled, and whether other architectures (e.g., graph-based models) were considered?
