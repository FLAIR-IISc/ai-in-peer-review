- Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire parser state.  
- Employs a greedy inference algorithm with linear-time complexity, including feature extraction.  
- Does not require expert-crafted features, improving flexibility and adaptability.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks; outperforms prior joint models and is competitive with top systems.  
- Fast and efficient due to the greedy linear-time algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach and advantages.  
- Comparison set is limited; a more comprehensive comparison to other works is needed.  
- No beam search results are reported, making comparisons to beam-based systems difficult.  
- Lacks morphological features, which may hurt performance on highly inflective languages.  
- Performance depends on reasonably large training datasets, which may not be available for all languages/domains.  
- Question: How would performance change using beam search instead of greedy inference?  
- Question: Can morphological features be incorporated to improve performance on inflective languages?  
- Question: How does performance vary with different word embeddings (e.g., character-based)?  
- Question: Can the parser be extended to other languages and domains, and how would performance change?
