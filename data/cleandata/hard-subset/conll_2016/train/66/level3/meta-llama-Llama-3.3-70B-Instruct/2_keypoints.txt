- Presents BSP, a transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units; evaluated on UCCA Wikipedia.  
- Proposes a transition-based parser that can handle broad-coverage semantic structures, a notable advancement.  
- Introduces a novel feature set specifically designed to capture complexities of broad-coverage semantics.  
- Evaluates the parser on the UCCA Wikipedia corpus, showing its effectiveness.  
- Achieves competitive primary-edge labeled F-score of 69.2% on UCCA Wikipedia.  
- Novel and significant approach addressing a long-standing challenge in semantic parsing.  
- Provides thorough evaluation including analysis of feature sets and their contributions.  
- Low performance on remote edges (labeled F-score 13.3%).  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with other semantic parsing approaches.  
- Evaluation is limited to the UCCA Wikipedia corpus, reducing evidence of robustness.  
- Asks how authors will address and improve low remote-edge performance.  
- Requests more details on the feature sets and their contributions to results.  
- Questions plans to evaluate on other datasets (e.g., CoNLL 2008/2009) to demonstrate robustness and enable comparisons.
