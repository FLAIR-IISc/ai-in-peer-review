- Proposes BSP, a transition-based parser that handles multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.  
- Investigates a conversion-based approach by mapping broad-coverage semantics to constituency/dependency formalisms and reusing existing parsers.  
- Provides extensive evaluation on the UCCA Wikipedia corpus, reporting strong performance.  
- Methodology is well-written and clearly explains BSP and the conversion approach.  
- Evaluation is thorough, including analysis of feature sets and their effects.  
- Demonstrates the parser’s effectiveness with impressive UCCA results.  
- Novelty concerns: approach builds on prior work and may lack originality.  
- Conversion-based method may be suboptimal; more sophisticated conversion procedures were not explored.  
- Analysis is missing a detailed error analysis of both the parser and conversion approach.  
- Asks authors how they will further improve parser performance and future research directions.  
- Requests more detail on the feature sets used and their specific impact on performance.  
- Asks how authors will address conversion-based limitations and what alternative approaches they propose.
