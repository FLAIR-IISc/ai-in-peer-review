- Novel approach to parsing BSS using both conversion-based and a direct transition-based parser (BSP) to handle multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuities.
- Contribution: a new transition system for BSS parsing that supports multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units.
- Contribution: successful application of neural networks to BSS parsing, outperforming linear models.
- Contribution: introduction of a novel feature, "ratio", as a regularizer for creating new nodes.
- Strength: thorough analysis of limitations in existing semantic parsing approaches motivating the new method.
- Strength: the proposed transition system and parser are well-designed and show effective empirical performance.
- Strength: detailed comparisons with existing parsers and conversion-based approaches illuminate strengths and weaknesses.
- Weakness: conversion-based approach may be suboptimal due to lossy conversions between representation formats.
- Weakness: missing detailed error analysis of the parser to identify specific failure modes and improvements.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single English dataset, raising questions about generalizability to other languages/datasets.
- Question: how will the approach be extended to handle implicit nodes and edges that are currently discarded?
- Question: please provide more details on the dynamic oracle used for training and how it is computed.
- Question: how do the authors plan to evaluate and adapt the approach to other languages and datasets, and what challenges do they expect?
