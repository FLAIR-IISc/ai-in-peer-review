- Proposes a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent full parser state.
- Claims two main contributions: vectorial state representations learned by stack LSTMs and a simple, fast greedy inference algorithm.
- Evaluated on CoNLL-2008/2009; outperforms Henderson et al. (2013) but lags behind recent feature-rich pipeline systems.
- Effectively applies stack LSTMs to capture long-range and structural information for semantic role labeling without hand-crafted features.
- Demonstrates a fast, feature-free joint parser with linear-time greedy decoding and impressive runtime.
- Elegantly integrates modern neural techniques, validating architecture by improving over Henderson et al. baseline.
- Eliminates need for discrete hand-crafted features, improving simplicity and multilingual generalizability.
- Offers practical efficiency suitable for downstream applications due to greedy, linear-time inference.
- Paper is clearly written and well-presented, with thorough explanation of the transition system and experiments.
- Major weakness: exclusive reliance on greedy inference limits comparability to prior beam-search models and may understate achievable performance.
- Needs clarification in Task Definition (Section 2) about semantic dependency types and examples (e.g., A1 vs. C-A1, nominal predicates).
- Should explicitly summarize differences between this transition system and Henderson et al. (2013), including new/modified transitions like M-SELF and S-SHIFT behavior.
- Questionable design choice in predicate composition (Section 3.3): concatenating word and sense vectors may be redundant and requires justification.
- Experimental section organization (Sections 4.1/4.2) is confusing; hyperparameters and ablation model descriptions should be reorganized for clarity.
- Requests to authors: report beam-search results to separate search vs. representation effects and enable fairer comparisons.
- Requests clarification on why predicate composition concatenates lemma-containing sense vectors with word vectors.
- Requests an explicit list of transition-system differences from Henderson et al. (2013).
- Requests brief clarification of core arguments vs. continuation arguments (e.g., A1 vs. C-A1) and whether they can co-occur.
