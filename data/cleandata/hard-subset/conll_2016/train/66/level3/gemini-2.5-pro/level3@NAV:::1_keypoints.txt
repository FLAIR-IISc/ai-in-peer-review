- Greedy transition-based parser combining Henderson et al. (2013) synchronized transitions with Dyer et al. (2015) stack LSTMs for joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing.  
- Learns dense vector representations of the entire parser state (stacks + buffer) without hand-crafted features; evaluated on CoNLL 2008/2009 and achieves competitive, state-of-the-art joint-model performance.  
- Contribution: strong empirical result showing stack LSTM architecture attains SOTA among joint syntactic–semantic parsers on standard benchmarks.  
- Contribution: a successful synthesis of two prior lines of work that provides a valuable proof-of-concept for neural state representations, despite no novel algorithmic components.  
- Strength: impressive empirical gains, significantly outperforming the Henderson et al. baseline and validating learned algorithmic-state representations.  
- Strength: exceptionally well-written with a clear methodology, sound experimental setup, and insightful ablation studies isolating joint-model effects.  
- Strength: appears highly reproducible, with clear system/hyperparameter descriptions and planned open-source release.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—primarily an application/combination of existing techniques rather than a new approach.  
- Weakness: potential confound from pre-trained embeddings (notably structured skip-gram for Chinese), leaving unclear how much gains come from architecture versus embedding quality.
