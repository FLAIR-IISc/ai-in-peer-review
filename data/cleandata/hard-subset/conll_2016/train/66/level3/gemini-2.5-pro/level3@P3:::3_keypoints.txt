- Greedy transition-based parser jointly models syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs to learn dense parser-state representations without hand-crafted features.
- New transition system extends Henderson et al. (2013), interleaving syntactic and semantic actions and adding an M-SELF action for self-referential semantic arcs.
- Achieves best published results among joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 and is competitive with strong pipeline systems while being significantly faster.
- Demonstrates that neural representation learning (stack LSTMs) makes joint transition-based parsing competitive and reduces reliance on feature engineering.
- Learns compact, parameter-efficient distributed representations that capture high-order syntax–semantic interactions.
- Employs a greedy, linear-time inference algorithm that attains strong accuracy without beam search, making it practical for downstream use.
- Proposed transition system and model are orthogonal to other advances and could be combined with global normalization, beam search, or character-level representations.
- Training uses a static oracle, introducing exposure bias because the model never trains on states resulting from its own errors.
- Paper provides limited analysis of what the learned joint representations actually encode; lacks visualizations or probing experiments.
- Reviewer asks whether a dynamic oracle (e.g., adapting Goldberg & Nivre 2012) could be developed for the transition system to mitigate exposure bias.
