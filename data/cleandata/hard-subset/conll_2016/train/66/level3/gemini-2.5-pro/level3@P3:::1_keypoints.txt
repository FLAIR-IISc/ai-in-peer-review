- Greedy transition-based joint syntactic and semantic parser combining Henderson et al.'s transition system with stack LSTMs to learn parser state without hand-crafted features, achieving SOTA among joint parsers on CoNLL 2008/2009 and fast linear-time inference.  
- Successfully applies stack LSTMs to joint parsing, demonstrating a unified representation learning approach that obviates manual feature engineering.  
- Strong empirical results: new state-of-the-art among joint models, competitive with pipelined systems, with thorough ablations and fast runtime.  
- Exceptional clarity and methodological soundness; model description, composition functions, and training details support reproducibility.  
- Timely and valuable integration of existing ideas showing representation learning can effectively model joint parser state.  
- Limited methodological originality since the work mainly combines existing transition system and neural architecture without introducing a new algorithm or theory.  
- Unclear contribution of pretrained "structured skip-gram" embeddings (notably for Chinese); controlled comparisons with standard embeddings are needed.  
- Joint model appears to overfit to in-domain data, performing worse than a hybrid/pipelined model on out-of-domain (Brown) data, with no explored mitigations.  
- Question: Why might joint training be more susceptible to domain overfitting than pipelined approaches, and would additional regularization methods help?  
- Question: How does model performance change when using common pretrained embeddings (GloVe, fastText) or when comparing systems using the same embeddings to isolate architectural effects?
