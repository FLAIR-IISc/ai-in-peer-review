- Proposes a joint syntactic-semantic transition-based dependency parser using stack LSTMs to learn a single parser-state representation and a greedy classifier, replacing hand-crafted features with an end-to-end neural architecture.  
- Demonstrates that stack LSTMs can learn comprehensive representations sufficient for joint syntactic-semantic analysis, removing the need for manual feature engineering.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates contributions relative to prior work in transition-based parsing and neural methods.  
- Greedy inference makes the model very fast, offering practical efficiency advantages over beam-search or global-inference systems.  
- Major omission: evaluation uses only greedy search and lacks a beam-search baseline, so it’s unclear if performance gaps stem from the model or the search strategy.  
- Clarity issues: Section 2 should explicitly state differences from Henderson et al., and roles of specific semantic links (e.g., A1 vs. C-A1) need clearer explanation.  
- Design/organization concerns: unclear motivation for concatenating predicate word and sense vectors, and the experimental setup (multilingual evaluation across CoNLL-2008/2009) is confusing.
