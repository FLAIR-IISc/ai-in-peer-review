

This paper presents a transition-based joint parser for syntactic and semantic dependency parsing using neural networks. The primary contribution is a novel transition system that improves upon Henderson (2008) by learning representations for the entire algorithmic state rather than just top stack items. The secondary contribution demonstrates that neural networks successfully achieve joint parsing where linear models previously failed, with the key insight being that neural networks provide more parameter-efficient representation learning for joint tasks than linear models. The tertiary contribution shows the parser's efficiency (177.6s for CoNLL 2009 English test data) compared to state-of-the-art pipelines.

Strengths include: 1) Clear empirical demonstration that neural networks can achieve joint parsing without expert-crafted features, surpassing previous joint models on CoNLL 2008-9; 2) The transition system's compatibility with other neural architectures (e.g., global beam search) suggests potential for further improvements; 3) Significant runtime advantage over competing systems (2.5x faster than Lei et al. 2015).

Weaknesses include: 1) The lack of a dynamic oracle for the transition system, which would clarify theoretical limits and aid comparison with other approaches; 2) Performance still lags behind state-of-the-art systems that use global inference and expert-crafted features; 3) Limited exploration of how to incorporate morphological features or character-based embeddings that could improve low-resource language performance.

The authors should address whether their transition system's performance would improve with a dynamic oracle, and whether their approach could be combined with global inference techniques to close the performance gap with state-of-the-art systems. The paper would benefit from a more detailed analysis of the parameter efficiency claim, including actual comparison of feature/class weights between their neural approach and previous linear models.

The work makes a valuable contribution to joint parsing, demonstrating that neural networks can successfully handle this challenging task without expert features, while maintaining computational efficiency.
