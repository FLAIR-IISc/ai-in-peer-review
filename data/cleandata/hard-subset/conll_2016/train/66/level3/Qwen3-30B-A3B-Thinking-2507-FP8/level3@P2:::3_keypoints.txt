- Transition-based parser using stack LSTMs jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies, achieving SOTA on CoNLL 2008-9 English without expert features.
- Novel application of stack LSTMs showing neural representations can replace hand-designed features in joint parsing.
- Practical linear-time greedy parser with fast runtime (~177.6±18s on CoNLL 2009 English single core).
- Empirical validation that joint learning without full syntactic tree features can achieve competitive semantic parsing performance.
- Demonstrates neural networks can match/exceed prior joint models that relied on expert-crafted features.
- Runtime efficiency is strong (~2.5x faster than Lei et al. pipeline under identical settings), making it suitable for real-world use.
- Clean, simple model using stack LSTMs to represent entire parser state, with a clear ablation study (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid).
- Lacks a dynamic oracle for the transition system, potentially limiting training robustness and handling of crossing arcs.
- Does not explore combining the approach with pipeline systems that use full syntactic parses, which have higher performance.
- Insufficient analysis of what specific features or representations the neural model learns compared to prior linear models.
- Question: How would you derive a dynamic oracle for your transition system and how might it improve performance?
- Question: Could combining your model with pipeline systems using full syntactic trees yield better results while maintaining efficiency, and how does your model compare in parameters/training time to prior linear models?
