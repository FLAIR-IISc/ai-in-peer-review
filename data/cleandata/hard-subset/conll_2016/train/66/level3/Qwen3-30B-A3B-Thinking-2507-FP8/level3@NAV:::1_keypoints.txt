- Presents a stack LSTM parser integrating Henderson et al.'s joint syntactic/semantic transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM architecture.  
- Main contributions: integration eliminating expert-crafted features; competitive CoNLL 2008/2009 English results surpassing prior joint models; efficient greedy parsing with linear time complexity.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art among joint parsing systems on CoNLL 2008/2009, removing need for expensive syntactic features.  
- Strength: methodology is well-justified with clear system description, thorough hyperparameter analysis, and comprehensive ablation studies (semantics-only, syntax-only, hybrid).  
- Strength: highly efficient implementation — processes CoNLL 2009 test set in ~177.6±18 seconds on a single core.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty, largely combining prior works without new algorithmic concepts.  
- Weakness: Chinese results may be driven by pretrained embeddings, raising questions about generalizability.  
- Weakness: does not surpass broader state-of-the-art systems that use richer features or global inference (e.g., Zhao et al., 2009).  
- Requests for clarification: whether Chinese performance would change without pretrained embeddings, and how the model could integrate with feature-rich approaches to improve performance.  
- Overall: strong empirical results and clear presentation, but the novelty gap should be addressed in revision.
