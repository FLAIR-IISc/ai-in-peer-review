- Contributions: integrates Henderson et al.'s joint parsing framework with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTMs to remove expert-crafted features.  
- Contributions: introduces a greedy inference algorithm with linear-time complexity including feature extraction.  
- Contributions: achieves strong empirical results, outperforming previous joint parsing models on CoNLL 2008–2009 (English).  
- Strength: demonstrates effectiveness of learning whole-parser-state representations over hand-crafted features.  
- Strength: robust, state-of-the-art performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks.  
- Strength: highly efficient parsing (≈177.6±18 seconds for CoNLL 2009 English test on one core).  
- Strength: paper is very well-written with clear technical exposition, justified hyperparameters, and solid related work.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—primarily a combination of existing techniques rather than a major conceptual advance.  
- Weakness: Chinese results seem heavily dependent on high-quality pretrained embeddings, raising concerns about generality.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation for why greedy inference outperforms beam search in this setting.  
- Weakness: does not investigate combining learned representations with expert-crafted features to close gap with SRL state-of-the-art.  
- Questions for authors: why does greedy inference outperform beam search here; how could expert-crafted features be integrated without losing greedy efficiency; details on the pretrained Chinese embeddings and performance with standard embeddings?
