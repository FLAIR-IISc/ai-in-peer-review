- Primary contribution: novel transition system that learns representations for the entire algorithmic state, improving on Henderson (2008).
- Secondary contribution: shows neural networks enable joint syntactic+semantic parsing where linear models failed, via more parameter-efficient representation learning.
- Tertiary contribution: demonstrates parser efficiency (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 English), outperforming some pipeline systems.
- Strength 1: Empirical evidence neural nets can achieve joint parsing without expert-crafted features, surpassing prior joint models on CoNLL 2008–9.
- Strength 2: Transition system is compatible with other neural architectures (e.g., global beam search), allowing further improvements.
- Strength 3: Significant runtime advantage (≈2.5× faster than Lei et al. 2015).
- Weakness 1: No dynamic oracle for the transition system, limiting theoretical analysis and fair comparison.
- Weakness 2: Overall performance still behind state-of-the-art methods that use global inference and expert features.
- Weakness 3: Limited investigation of morphological or character-based embeddings that could help low-resource languages.
- Recommendation 1: Assess whether a dynamic oracle improves performance and clarifies limits of the transition system.
- Recommendation 2: Explore combining the approach with global inference techniques to close the performance gap.
- Recommendation 3: Provide a more detailed, quantitative analysis of the claimed parameter efficiency versus previous linear models.
