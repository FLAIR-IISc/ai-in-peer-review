

**Review**

This paper presents a stack LSTM parser that integrates Henderson et al.'s joint syntactic/semantic transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM architecture. The main contributions are: (1) a novel integration of stack LSTMs with joint parsing that eliminates the need for expert-crafted features; (2) competitive results on CoNLL 2008/2009 English shared tasks that surpass previous joint models; and (3) a highly efficient greedy parsing approach with linear time complexity.

The paper's strengths are compelling. First, the empirical results demonstrate that the integrated model achieves state-of-the-art performance among joint parsing systems on both CoNLL 2008 and 2009, outperforming all prior joint approaches while eliminating the need for expensive syntactic features. Second, the methodology is exceptionally well-justified with clear system description, thorough hyperparameter analysis, and comprehensive ablation studies (including semantics-only, syntax-only, and hybrid variants). Third, the implementation is remarkably efficient—processing the entire CoNLL 2009 test set in 177.6±18 seconds on a single core, making it highly practical for real-world applications.

The primary weakness is the lack of methodological novelty—this work builds directly on Henderson et al. and Dyer et al. without introducing fundamentally new algorithmic concepts, though the integration was valuable and long-awaited. Second, the Chinese results raise concerns: the paper acknowledges that Chinese embeddings may drive performance more than the model innovation itself, which weakens the claim of generalizability. Third, while the approach is competitive among joint parsers, it does not surpass state-of-the-art systems that use more comprehensive feature sets or global inference (e.g., Zhao et al., 2009), suggesting the model's limitations compared to the broader state of the art.

The authors should clarify: (1) whether the Chinese performance would improve without pretrained embeddings (as suggested in the paper), and (2) how their model might integrate with existing feature-rich approaches to further improve performance. The paper's strong empirical results and clear presentation make it valuable for the community, though the novelty gap should be carefully addressed in revision.
