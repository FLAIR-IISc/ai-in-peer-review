- Presents a transition-based joint syntactic-semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs that learns full parser-state representations, removing the need for hand-crafted features and achieving competitive CoNLL 2008/2009 results with greedy, linear-time inference.

- Strong empirical results: outperforms prior joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 and shows generalizability across languages.

- Efficient inference: greedy algorithm yields linear time complexity and fast runtimes (e.g., ~178s on CoNLL 2009 English).

- Clear and comprehensive presentation: well-written methodology, helpful figures, and replicable description.

- Thorough related work coverage and thoughtful discussion of limitations and future directions.

- Sound experimental methodology: standard datasets, described hyperparameter tuning, and informative ablation studies.

- Limited methodological novelty: components (transition parsing, stack LSTMs) are not new; novelty is in their integration.

- Dependence on pretrained embeddings: multilingual gains (especially Chinese) may largely reflect embedding quality rather than model improvements.

- Comparison to state-of-the-art: still lags behind best SRL systems using full parses/reranking; trade-offs between accuracy and efficiency need more discussion.

- Oracle transition conversion issues: imperfect conversion affects ~1.5% of CoNLL 2009 English and may bias results, impact not quantified.

- Question: quantify contribution of pretrained embeddings (e.g., compare to random initialization).

- Question: estimate impact of imperfect oracle transitions on LAS and F1 scores.

- Question: have character-level embeddings or morphological features been tested, especially for morphologically rich languages?

- Additional: discuss model memory requirements as stack LSTMs scale; code availability is a notable positive.

- Overall recommendation: strong paper, worthwhile contribution despite limited novelty — recommend acceptance.
