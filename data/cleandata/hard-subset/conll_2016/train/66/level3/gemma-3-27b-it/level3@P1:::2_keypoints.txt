- Novel use of stack LSTMs to encode the entire parser state, capturing long-range dependencies without hand-crafted features.  
- Purely greedy decoding yields strong runtime efficiency (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 English) while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Competitive baseline performance on CoNLL 2008/2009, nearing pipeline systems and showing promising multilingual results.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with good connection to prior work and illustrative examples.  
- Reliance on greedy decoding may limit exploration of the search space; no comparison to beam search is provided.  
- Section 2 lacks clarity about semantic transitions, particularly the roles and handling of A1 vs C-A1 links.  
- Concatenation of predicate word vectors and disambiguated sense vectors appears redundant and lacks justification or ablation.  
- Experimental sections (4.1/4.2) are disorganized and unclear about dataset scope and multilingual coverage.  
- Question: Have you tried beam search decoding, and how does its performance compare to greedy decoding?  
- Question: Please clarify the definition and handling of A1 and C-A1 links in your transition system.  
- Question: What motivates concatenating predicate word and sense vectors, and is there ablation evidence of its impact?  
- Question: Clarify which datasets and languages are used in Sections 4.1 and 4.2 and the scope of each experiment.  
- Suggest briefly explaining "pseudo-projectivization" for unfamiliar readers.  
- Suggest discussing potential error propagation from provided POS tags and lemmatization on parser performance.
