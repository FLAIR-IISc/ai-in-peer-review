- Uses stack LSTMs to encode full parser state, removing need for hand-crafted features and capturing long-range dependencies.
- Greedy decoding yields linear-time parsing and fast runtime (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 English), offering practical speed advantages.
- Paper is well-written, clearly connects to prior work, and describes the transition system and model for reproducibility.
- Thorough evaluation on CoNLL 2008/2009 (including multilingual experiments) provides a robust benchmark and comparisons to existing systems.
- Performance is competitive with older systems but lags behind state-of-the-art models that use richer features, optimal decoding, or extra data.
- Limited comparative analysis with stronger baselines (e.g., Lei et al. 2015); more discussion of trade-offs between features, decoding, and runtime is needed.
- Exclusive reliance on greedy decoding is unjustified; no beam-search experiments are reported to assess accuracy vs. speed trade-offs.
- Description of transition details (especially A1 and C-A1 in the SRL graph) is unclear and needs clearer comparison to Henderson et al. (2013).
- Question: why concatenate word and disambiguated sense vectors for predicates—could the sense vector alone suffice?
- Question: clarify organization and scope of Sections 4.1/4.2 and whether CoNLL-2008 experiments include multilingual results.
- Question: discuss limitations and mitigation strategies for applying the model to morphologically rich languages (e.g., character-based embeddings).
- Suggestion: include a hyperparameter table for different models to aid reproducibility.
- Suggestion: provide the supplementary material (e.g., example run) referenced in the text.
- Positive note: code availability is a significant plus and enhances reproducibility.
- Overall: solid contribution demonstrating stack LSTMs for joint parsing with strong speed/representation benefits; addressing decoding justification and stronger comparisons would strengthen impact.
