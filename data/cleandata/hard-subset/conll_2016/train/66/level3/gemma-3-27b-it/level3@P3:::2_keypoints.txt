- Stack LSTMs effectively encode the parser state for joint syntactic-semantic parsing, capturing long-range dependencies without engineered features.
- The model achieves competitive results on CoNLL-2008 and CoNLL-2009 without hand-crafted features, demonstrating the strength of learned representations.
- Greedy decoding yields significantly faster inference than beam-search approaches, enabling practical real-time use.

- Paper is well written and clearly connects to prior work (Henderson et al., 2008).
- Empirical evaluation is solid, covering CoNLL-2008 and CoNLL-2009 including multilingual data.
- Reported runtime (177.6±18s on CoNLL-2009 English test) demonstrates efficiency from greedy decoding.
- Effective integration of stack LSTM techniques improves joint parsing performance.
- Authors plan to open-source the implementation, aiding reproducibility.

- Exclusive reliance on greedy decoding limits comparability to beam-search-based state-of-the-art; beam search results are required.
- Section 2 lacks a clear explanation of the roles and impact of A1 and C-A1 semantic links.
- The claimed novelty in transitions is not substantiated—differences from Henderson et al. (2008) are not clearly summarized.
- Concatenation of predicate word and disambiguated sense vectors appears redundant and is not justified.
- Subsections 4.1 and 4.2 are confusing about which results apply to CoNLL-2008 versus CoNLL-2009.

- Please provide results using beam search and compare them to greedy decoding and other state-of-the-art systems.
- Clarify the role and effect of A1 and C-A1 links in the semantic representation.
- Provide a clear summary or table of how your transitions differ from Henderson et al. (2008).
- Explain the rationale and empirical benefit (if any) of concatenating predicate word and sense vectors.
- Restructure sections 4.1 and 4.2 to explicitly delineate CoNLL-2008 and CoNLL-2009 results.

- Expand the related work to include more recent neural dependency parsing advances.
- Investigate different LSTM architectures or hyperparameters as potential avenues for improvement.
