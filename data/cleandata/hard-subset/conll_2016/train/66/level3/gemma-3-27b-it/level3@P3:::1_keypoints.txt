- Effective integration of stack LSTMs into a joint syntactic-semantic parser, showing representation learning can replace heavy feature engineering.  
- Establishes a strong, fast, and simple baseline for joint parsing with linear runtime and no complex decoding.  
- Thorough evaluation on CoNLL 2008/2009 with ablations and multilingual results, offering insights into model behavior and syntax–semantics interaction.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with detailed motivation, methodology, and experimental setup.  
- Sound methodological choices and reasonable hyperparameter justification, supported by ablation studies.  
- Competitive performance: state-of-the-art among joint models and comparable to strong pipelined systems, with runtime advantages.  
- Strong and comprehensive related work section situating the paper within joint parsing and neural SRL literature.  
- Planned open-source release of the implementation benefits the research community.  
- Limited methodological novelty—mainly integrates existing techniques rather than proposing new methods.  
- Possible over-reliance on high-quality pre-trained embeddings (notably for Chinese), which may partly explain gains.  
- Performance appears sensitive to training data size, with weaker results on smaller datasets (e.g., Japanese).  
- Use of greedy decoding may limit accuracy; beam search or richer decoding could improve results at computational cost.  
- Request for analysis of sensitivity to pre-trained embeddings (suggests experiment with random initialization).  
- Request for details on attempts to mitigate overfitting (e.g., WSJ/Brown domain differences and Hybrid model effects).  
- Request for elaboration on oracle transition conversion limitations and any analysis of conversion failures.  
- Question about expected impact of incorporating morphological features, especially for morphologically rich languages.  
- Suggest adding discussion of recent end-to-end neural SRL models (e.g., transformer-based) that bypass explicit parsing.  
- Minor suggestion to improve Table 1 formatting for readability.
