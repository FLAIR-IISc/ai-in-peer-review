- Revisits joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing using a transition-based Stack LSTM approach.  
- Achieves competitive results on CoNLL 2008/2009, outperforming prior joint models and nearing pipeline system performance.  
- Main contribution: successful application of Stack LSTMs to jointly model syntax and semantics without hand-engineered features.  
- Main contribution: introduces M-SELF transition to handle self-dependencies, improving performance.  
- Main contribution: empirical evidence that neural models can succeed at joint parsing where linear models struggled.  
- Strength: clear presentation and solid experimental results.  
- Strength: fast, efficient implementation (177.6s for CoNLL 2009).  
- Strength: detailed ablation studies (Syntax-only, Semantics-only, Hybrid) elucidating syntactic–semantic interactions.  
- Strength: fair and informative comparisons to state-of-the-art systems.  
- Weakness: transition system is incremental rather than fundamentally novel; needs deeper justification for transition choices.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis to show where the system fails and guide improvements.  
- Weakness: mentions greedy decoding limitations but does not explore mitigation strategies.  
- Concern: potential overfitting of the Joint model on out-of-domain Brown data; requests error-type analysis vs. Hybrid model.  
- Question: whether other transition modifications were considered and why M-SELF was chosen.  
- Question: requests details on computational/training cost and memory usage of Stack LSTMs for larger datasets or models.
