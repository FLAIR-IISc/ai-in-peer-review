- Proposes a novel transition-based joint syntactic/semantic parser using stack LSTMs that eliminates hand-crafted features by representing the entire parser state.  
- Introduces a fully greedy inference algorithm with linear runtime complexity.  
- Achieves competitive results on CoNLL 2008–2009, surpassing prior joint models and nearing state-of-the-art pipeline systems.  
- Demonstrates neural networks’ parameter efficiency in joint learning and suggests extensibility to other neural architectures.  
- Novel transition system is a significant advancement over Henderson (2008) by encoding full parser state via stack LSTMs.  
- Joint model is efficient and fast (177.6±18 s on CoNLL 2009 English test) with an open-source implementation, making it practical.  
- Shows generalizability and multilingual adaptability in experiments.  
- Lacks a dynamic oracle, which may limit robustness and error recovery during training.  
- Joint parser appears to overfit WSJ in-domain data and underperforms on out-of-domain (Brown) data, indicating need for regularization or domain adaptation.  
- Limited comparison to recent neural SRL models (e.g., Zhou & Xu 2015), leaving contextual performance unclear.  
- Does not incorporate morphological or character-based features, potentially reducing performance on morphologically rich languages like Czech.  
- Asks whether the authors considered a dynamic oracle and what challenges arose.  
- Requests comparison to neural SRL models in both performance and computational efficiency.  
- Asks for elaboration on extending the transition system to other architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with practical utility; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
