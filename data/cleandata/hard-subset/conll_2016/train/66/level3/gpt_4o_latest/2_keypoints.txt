- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs and a greedy search algorithm.  
- Uses stack LSTMs to capture large-scoped syntactic features (e.g., predicate–role filler paths) without hand-crafted features.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual), achieving competitive performance and promising an open-source release.  
- Contribution: novel application of stack LSTMs to joint parsing, removing need for hand-crafted features.  
- Contribution: a fast, greedy parsing algorithm that yields linear-time parsing and competitive benchmark results.  
- Contribution: shows joint parsing can approach pipeline performance while remaining simple and efficient.  
- Strength: innovative use of stack LSTMs for representing entire parser state and long-range dependencies.  
- Strength: practical efficiency with impressive runtime (177.6s for CoNLL 2009 English test).  
- Strength: well-written, clear presentation with detailed method and experimental setup.  
- Strength: commitment to releasing open-source implementation.  
- Weakness: greedy algorithm trades accuracy for speed, limiting comparability to state-of-the-art global or beam methods.  
- Weakness: lags behind recent SRL systems using global optimization or extra annotated data; no clear integration roadmap.  
- Weakness: Section 2 unclear about A1 and C-A1 links and differences from Henderson et al.  
- Weakness: questionable redundancy in concatenating predicates with disambiguated senses (Section 3.3).  
- Weakness: confusing organization of multilingual results across Sections 4.1 and 4.2.  
- Questions: clarify advantages of stack LSTMs over other neural architectures for joint parsing.  
- Questions: justify choice of greedy algorithm over beam search and plans to mitigate its limitations.  
- Questions: explain rationale for concatenating predicates with disambiguated senses.  
- Questions: how are cases handled where oracle conversion of semantic parses fails (e.g., 1.5% in CoNLL 2009)?  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity issues and justify design choices.
