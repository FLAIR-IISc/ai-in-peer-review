- Summary: Proposes a joint syntactic–semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs over full parser state, with greedy linear-time decoding; evaluated on CoNLL-2008/2009, competitive with older systems but behind recent feature-rich/global methods.

- Contribution (primary): Introduces a joint transition system conditioned on vector summaries of full algorithmic state via stack LSTMs, removing hand-crafted features and enabling long-range cues for SRL.

- Contribution (secondary): Provides a fast, practical greedy joint parser and systematic ablations (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) to elucidate where joint modeling helps.

- Contribution (tertiary): Offers broad empirical evaluation across CoNLL-2008/2009 including multilingual experiments, showing portability and competitiveness with earlier joint models.

- Strength: Strong design—stack LSTMs encode S, M, B and action history to capture predicate–argument paths and long-range cues without feature engineering.

- Strength: Empirical clarity—the ablation studies substantiate the utility of joint training and trade-offs for in/out-of-domain performance.

- Strength: Practicality—greedy decoding yields very fast inference (~178s on CoNLL-2009 English test, single core) with competitive accuracy.

- Strength: Writing and positioning—the paper is clear, well-organized, and situates itself against Henderson-style joint parsers and contemporary SRL systems.

- Weakness: Search comparability—relying solely on greedy decoding prevents separation of modeling gains from search effects; beam/global inference results are needed.

- Weakness: Methodological clarity—Section 2 fails to crisply enumerate differences from Henderson et al. (copy vs move, new M-SELF, interleaving policy), hindering novelty assessment.

- Weakness: Labeling clarity—the roles of A1 vs C-A1 (continuation) links are not explained, impeding task understanding and error analysis.

- Weakness: Redundancy concern—concatenating predicate lemma and sense vectors may be redundant since senses are lemma-specific; an ablation or rationale is needed.

- Weakness: Experimental organization—Sections 4.1/4.2 blur CoNLL-2008 predicate-identification vs 2009 pre-identified predicates and multilingual details; per-language specifics would help.

- Question: Please report beam-search results across beam widths and quantify speed–accuracy trade-offs.

- Question: Enumerate all transition/system differences from Henderson et al. and report their measured impact.

- Question: Clarify the semantics and representation of A1 vs C-A1 and how continuation links are modeled in the transition set.

- Question: Justify predicate lemma+sense concatenation and provide ablations (e.g., sense-only, gating).

- Question: Specify which languages are included in 4.2, what embeddings/morphological features are used, and how multilingual hyperparameters were tuned.

- Question: How does imperfect semantic oracle conversion bound performance—provide upper-bound estimates or diagnostics.
