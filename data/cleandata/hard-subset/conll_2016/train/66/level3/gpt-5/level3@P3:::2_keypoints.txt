- Summary: Proposes a greedy, joint transition-based parser using stack LSTMs to build syntactic dependency trees and SRL graphs simultaneously, achieving competitive accuracy and high speed on CoNLL-2008/2009 English and multilingual data but trailing recent globally-decoded SRL systems.

- Contribution 1: Introduces learned neural representations of the full joint parser state with stack LSTMs, reducing reliance on hand-crafted long-range syntactic features.

- Contribution 2: Presents a simple, fast greedy joint decoder with linear-time inference and no hand-crafted features that outperforms earlier joint models and matches older pipelines.

- Contribution 3: Extends Henderson-style transitions with M-SELF for NomBank self-arguments and a copy-based arc-eager adaptation to support interleaved syntax/semantics on a shared buffer.

- Strength: Elegant integration of stack LSTMs that summarize S, M, B, and action history to capture long-range dependencies without explicit feature extraction.

- Strength: Solid empirical validation showing consistent improvements over prior Henderson-style joint parsers on CoNLL-2008/2009 English.

- Strength: Thorough controlled experiments (Syntax-only, Semantics-only, Hybrid, Joint) that isolate joint-modeling benefits and domain behavior.

- Strength: Practical efficiency with a greedy decoder that is memory- and runtime-friendly, claiming a 2–3× speedup over a strong pipeline baseline.

- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation of the transition system, composition functions, and training procedure.

- Weakness: Only greedy decoding is evaluated; lack of beam search or dynamic-oracle results limits comparability and headroom assessment.

- Weakness: Incomplete ablation of modeling choices (predicate embedding concatenation, action-history LSTM, label/pretrained embeddings) and limited error analysis, especially OOD.

- Weakness: Transition system description lacks a concise summary of differences from Henderson et al., and treatment/evaluation of continuation roles (A1 vs C-A1) is unclear.

- Weakness: Confusing evaluation organization across CoNLL-2008 vs 2009 and English vs multilingual settings; effect of non-derivable gold transitions not quantified.

- Weakness: Falls short of recent globally-decoded SRL systems and does not test simple add-ons (beam, dynamic oracle, mild global scoring, char/morph features) that might reduce the gap.

- Question: Please report beam search (e.g., beam=4/8) and/or dynamic-oracle training results and runtime/accuracy trade-offs — does beam help the semantic graph?

- Question: Why concatenate predicate word and sense embeddings after disambiguation; would the sense vector alone or a gated composition suffice? Provide an ablation.

- Question: Are syntax and semantics label embeddings shared or separate, and what is the effect of sharing vs separating embeddings?

- Question: Provide a concise bullet list of differences from Henderson et al. (copy vs move, interleaving policy, constraints, M-SELF, SWAP treatment, initial buffer, legality changes).

- Question: How are A1 and C-A1 continuation edges predicted and evaluated in the dependency-SRL setting; provide an example and generation explanation.

- Question: What fraction of training instances have semantic structures not derivable by the transitions, and what upper bound or handling is applied during training?

- Question: For multilingual experiments, were hyperparameters and pretrained embeddings fixed across languages, and were character/morphological features tried for morphologically rich languages?

- Additional comment: Add error analysis contrasting Joint vs Hybrid on WSJ vs Brown to substantiate overfitting and identify roles benefiting from joint modeling.

- Additional comment: Highlight and illustrate the copy-vs-move deviation from arc-eager earlier in the paper with a small motivating diagram.

- Additional comment: Report per-role F1 (core vs adjunct) and arc-length breakdowns to support claims that stack LSTMs capture long-range features.

- Additional comment: Minor notational clarifications: unify action-history notation, clarify At dependencies, and state whether 2008 predicate identification uses the same embeddings as parsing.
