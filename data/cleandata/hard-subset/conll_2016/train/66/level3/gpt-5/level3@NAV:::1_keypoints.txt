- Summary: Presents a greedy, linear-time joint syntactic–semantic parser combining Henderson et al.’s synchronized transitions with stack LSTMs, achieving top joint-model results on English CoNLL 08/09 and competitive multilingual performance with fast runtime and clear exposition.

- Contribution 1: Practical integration of joint syntactic–semantic transitions with stack LSTMs yielding a strong, feature-light parser that sets a new bar among joint systems on English.

- Contribution 2: Careful empirical study (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid, joint) clarifying when joint modeling helps and when it overfits (WSJ vs Brown).

- Contribution 3: Efficient implementation with substantial speedups over competitive pipeline approaches and modest memory use.

- Strength 1: Competitive accuracy with a simple linear-time greedy approach, outperforming prior joint systems and validating full-state representations.

- Strength 2: Strong ablation experiments showing semantics-only lags and joint/hybrid setups improve performance, indicating mutual reinforcement of syntax and semantics.

- Strength 3: Excellent clarity and reproducibility: transition inventory, constraints, composition functions, and hyperparameters are precisely described and justified.

- Strength 4: Practical runtime (~178s on CoNLL 2009 English) and modest memory footprint, making the method deployment-friendly.

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty—mainly combines established components (Henderson transitions + Dyer stack LSTMs) rather than introducing new concepts.

- Weakness 2: Multilingual gains (esp. Chinese) appear driven by high-quality pretrained embeddings; without them performance is merely “on par,” tempering model-driven claims.

- Weakness 3: Greedy inference may limit ceiling performance; lacks comparisons to beam search or global training/inference and risks error propagation with crossing semantic arcs.

- Weakness 4: Oracle/convertibility limitations for semantic transitions (crossing arcs) introduce noisy supervision, but the impact on final accuracy is not quantified.

- Question 1: Can you quantify the contribution of pretrained embeddings (especially Chinese) via controlled with/without pretraining experiments across languages?

- Question 2: How would beam search or dynamic-oracle/global training affect accuracy/runtime trade-offs in this joint setting?
