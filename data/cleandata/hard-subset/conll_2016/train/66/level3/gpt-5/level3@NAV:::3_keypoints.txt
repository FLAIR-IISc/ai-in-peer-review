- Revisits joint syntactic–semantic dependency parsing with a neural, stack-LSTM transition parser that performs greedy, linear-time inference and shows strong CoNLL 2008–2009 results.  
- Empirical contribution: demonstrates that full-state neural representations substantially outperform prior joint models that used local/linear features and are more parameter-efficient.  
- Algorithmic contribution: introduces a refined joint transition system (interleaved syntax/semantics, M-SELF) enabling efficient greedy decoding and compatibility with other neural architectures.  
- Strong empirical gains over prior joint systems, with ablations (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) that clarify where joint modeling helps.  
- Engineering strength: linear-time greedy decoding with end-to-end speed competitive with or faster than pipeline approaches.  
- Architectural strength: full-state stack-LSTM encoding removes need for expert-crafted features while preserving context and improving parameter efficiency.  
- Broad applicability: transition system likely compatible with global/beam training and other improvements (char/morphology, contextual embeddings, cost-augmented decoding).  
- Limited training-signal exploration: static supervision only, risking exposure bias and showing out-of-domain overfitting (Hybrid > Joint on Brown).  
- Sparse transition-system analysis: unclear contribution of individual additions (e.g., M-SELF, interleaving); no transition inventory ablations.  
- Indirect comparisons to strong modern baselines and no beam/global training experiments, leaving unclear whether gains come from representation or search.  
- Question: can the authors derive and include a dynamic oracle for their transition set (given similarity to arc-eager actions)?  
- Question: how compatible is the system with global normalization/beam search (e.g., Andor 2016), and are there preliminary results?  
- Question: can the authors provide ablations quantifying the contributions of M-SELF and the syntax–semantics interleaving to accuracy and speed?
