- Summary: Joint syntactic–semantic dependency parser using Henderson-style transitions and stack LSTMs for greedy, linear-time inference; introduces M-SELF and learns vector compositions for partial structures, achieving top joint-model results on CoNLL 2008/2009 English and competitive SRL with much faster runtime.

- Primary contribution: Fast, greedy joint parser that models full parser state via stack LSTMs and attains state-of-the-art performance among joint syntax–semantics models with strong efficiency.

- Secondary contribution: Empirical analysis comparing semantics-only, syntax-only, and hybrid variants to study when joint training helps or overfits across domains.

- Tertiary contribution: Practical transition-system extensions for joint parsing, including interleaved syntactic/semantic actions, M-SELF for predicate self-arcs, and integrated predicate sense disambiguation.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance among joint models and competitive with pipeline SRL while using a simpler unified approach.

- Strength: Runtime efficiency — roughly 2.5× faster end-to-end than a strong SRL pipeline under comparable settings.

- Strength: Clear ablation studies and experimental design that reveal joint vs. hybrid trade-offs (joint better in-domain; hybrid better out-of-domain).

- Strength: Well-justified modeling choices, clear system description, and reproducible training/hyperparameter details.

- Strength: Well-written paper with good related-work coverage and clear exposition of the transition system.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—mainly an integration of known techniques with incremental practical additions (e.g., M-SELF).

- Weakness: Multilingual gains may be confounded by pretrained embeddings (notably Chinese) with no ablation across languages.

- Weakness: Joint model appears to overfit out-of-domain data (Brown) with no explored remedies like domain adaptation or small beams.

- Weakness: Still lags best-in-class SRL pipelines that use global inference, richer features, or extra annotations; lacks experiments integrating orthogonal improvements.

- Weakness: Imperfect semantic oracle and transition coverage (~1.5% of sentences not encodable) are noted but not deeply analyzed for downstream impact.

- Question: How sensitive are results to pretrained embeddings across languages; can authors report full with/without pretraining deltas?

- Question: Did authors try small beams or dynamic oracles to mitigate greedy errors while retaining speed?

- Question: Can authors quantify the impact of the imperfect semantic oracle on final SRL F1 and characterize affected phenomena?

- Question: For CoNLL 2008, how is predicate identification integrated and what is the error propagation from PI to SRL?

- Question: What is training stability across random seeds and variance in joint model performance?

- Additional comment: Multilingual experiments would benefit from morphological features or character-based embeddings for morphologically rich languages.

- Additional comment: A brief qualitative error analysis showing typical joint wins/losses vs. hybrid would strengthen the paper.

- Overall recommendation: Solid, well-executed, and timely work with strong joint-model results and efficiency; leans toward acceptance despite modest novelty.
