- Revisits joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing with a transition-based neural model using synchronized transitions and stack LSTMs, achieving competitive accuracy and much faster runtime on CoNLL 2008–2009 English.

- Empirical finding: full-state neural representations enable effective greedy joint parsing, surpassing earlier linear-feature joint models.

- Transition-system design: refines Henderson-style synchronized transitions (copy vs. move, M-SELF, constraints) to support linear-time greedy parsing.

- Practicality evidence: competitive against pipelined systems with notably faster end-to-end runtime, suitable for large-scale or low-latency use.

- Algorithmic simplicity and speed: greedy linear-time parser with automatic feature learning yields strong accuracy at ~2.5× the speed of a competitive pipeline.

- Semantics-specific refinements: copy semantics, M-SWAP for crossings, and M-SELF for NomBank self-arcs make the system align with SRL graph needs while remaining learnable.

- Generality and extensibility: stack-LSTM state representation and transition inventory are compatible with stronger neural methods (global normalization, pretrained encoders, beam search).

- Parameter efficiency: neural shared encoder is more parameter-efficient than large linear cross-product feature sets for joint tasks.

- Limited novelty in learning machinery: stack-LSTMs, local normalization, and greedy inference follow prior work; main novelty is transition adaptation and empirical gains.

- Exposure bias and lack of global training: static oracle and greedy decoding likely cause out-of-domain weaknesses; dynamic oracle/global normalization could improve robustness.

- Incomplete ablations and error analysis: missing analyses on pretrained embeddings, per-label benefits, and the impact of M-SELF/M-SWAP or long-distance/crossing errors.

- Oracle/transition fidelity issues: oracle conversion cannot perfectly represent some semantic graphs (~1.5% English sentences); impact on training/eval is unquantified.

- Predicate handling concerns: defaulting unseen predicates to “.01” may conflate joint-model gains with a strong prior; more thorough predicate sense analysis is needed.

- Question: Can a dynamic oracle be derived for the joint transition system to reduce exposure bias?

- Question: Have you tried globally normalized training or beam search with the interleaved/copy semantics, and if so what obstacles were found?

- Question: What are the effects of ablations on key transition tweaks (copy vs. move, removing M-SELF, disabling M-SWAP, changing interleaving)?

- Question: Can you compare parameter counts or degrees of freedom with a strong linear joint baseline to support the parameter-efficiency claim?

- Question: How often do gold semantic edges become unreachable due to conversion, and what is the accuracy impact on those cases?

- Overall: Accept — practical and impactful empirical advance for joint syntactic–semantic parsing with fast, clean modeling, despite limited architectural novelty and room for improved training/analysis.
