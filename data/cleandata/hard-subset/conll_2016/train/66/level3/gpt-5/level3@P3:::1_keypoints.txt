- Introduces a greedy, linear-time joint syntactic–semantic dependency parser combining Henderson et al.’s transition system with Dyer et al.’s stack LSTM, learning continuous full-parser-state representations without hand-crafted features or beam search.  
- Achieves strongest reported performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English, competitive multilingual CoNLL 2009 results, and is substantially faster than strong pipelines; includes ablations (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid).  
- Adds a small practical transition (M-SELF) to handle semantic self-dependencies and provides an efficient, modest-memory implementation with planned open-sourcing.

- Empirically strong: surpasses prior joint systems on English and matches many top systems on CoNLL 2009 English; syntax-only variant slightly improves over Dyer et al. (2015) arc-eager LAS.  
- Methodology and description are clear and reproducible: well-specified transition system, neural architecture, composition functions, and justified hyperparameters.  
- Fast, practical decoding: linear-time greedy inference yields ~2.5× speedup over a strong pipeline in matched settings, suitable for real-time or large-scale use.  
- Informative ablations: show syntax helps SRL; joint training helps in-domain but can overfit out-of-domain (hybrid sometimes outperforms joint).  
- Well-written with thorough related work and fair discussion of limitations (no global reranking or exact DP inference).

- Limited methodological originality: mainly integrates two existing components, with only modest novel contribution (M-SELF).  
- Heavy reliance on pretrained embeddings in some languages (notably Chinese), making it unclear how much gains stem from embeddings vs the model itself.  
- Greedy inference without beam or global training may limit accuracy; lacks experiments (small beams or global objectives) to quantify headroom.  
- Oracle/transition limitations and error analysis are underexplored; ~1.5% of English sentences cannot be encoded exactly and impact is not quantified.  
- Comparability and significance concerns: lacks statistical significance testing and clearer controls for pretraining corpus overlap and hyperparameter tuning across languages.

- How much improvement remains without pretrained embeddings (train word vectors from scratch), especially for English?  
- For Chinese (and German/Spanish), can you quantify performance delta attributable to pretrained embeddings?  
- Did you try small-beam decoding or globally normalized training, and what's the speed–accuracy tradeoff?  
- What fraction of errors stem from the oracle’s inability to encode gold graphs; can you report an oracle upper bound and subset performance?  
- How often is M-SELF used and what is its marginal impact (ablation without M-SELF)?  
- Are syntax-only improvements over Dyer et al. (2015) statistically significant, and what explains the gain?  
- Can you release exact pretraining details (vocab cutoff, tokenization, casing) and confirm no evaluation text was in pretraining for non-English languages?

- Suggestion: incorporate character/subword embeddings or explicit morphology to improve results on morphologically rich languages.
