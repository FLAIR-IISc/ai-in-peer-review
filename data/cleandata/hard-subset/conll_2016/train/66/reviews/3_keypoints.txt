- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using neural networks; joint transition-based models are promising.  
- Two contributions: a new transition system (improvement over Henderson 2008) and empirical demonstration that neural networks succeed where linear models struggled.  
- Authors credit success to automatic feature learning; reviewer adds that neural nets are more parameter-efficient for joint tasks than linear models (fewer feature/class weights).  
- Transition system likely compatible with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search); many orthogonal improvements could yield state-of-the-art results.  
- Suggestion to derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system (appendix or follow-up); M-S, M-D, L, R actions appear similar to arc-eager oracle in which specific semantic arcs are excluded.
