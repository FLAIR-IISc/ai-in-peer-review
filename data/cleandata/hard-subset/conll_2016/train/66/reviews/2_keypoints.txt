- Paper proposes a joint syntactic+semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Authors claim two main novelties: using vectorial representations for the entire parser state and employing a plain greedy search.  
- Central idea: stack LSTMs let the parser state encode long-range syntactic features useful for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–filler paths).  
- System evaluated on CoNLL-2008 (English) and CoNLL-2009 (multilingual) datasets.  
- Results are competitive with older 2008/2009 systems but lag behind more recent proposals.  
- Advantages highlighted: no hand-crafted features required and fast inference due to greedy decoding.  
- Paper is well written and builds substantially on recent LSTM techniques applied to Henderson et al.’s framework.  
- Major concern: using a simple greedy algorithm limits comparability to other works; beam-search results should be reported.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on the presence/role of both A1 and C-A1 links for task understanding.  
- Section 2 should summarize differences between the transitions used here and those in Henderson et al. to clarify novelty.  
- Section 3.3: question why predicate word and disambiguated sense vectors are concatenated—this appears redundant.  
- Section 4: organization of subsections 4.1/4.2 is confusing with respect to multilinguality; clarify CoNLL-2008 vs CoNLL-2009 scope.
