- Core contribution: transition-based joint syntactic and semantic parser using stack LSTMs and greedy inference that achieves strong results without expert-crafted features.  
- Secondary contribution: fast greedy inference with linear-time complexity and practical runtime (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 English).  
- Minor contribution: introduction of an M-SELF transition to handle self-dependencies in CoNLL 2009.  

- Strength: Strong empirical results — state-of-the-art among joint parsers on CoNLL 2008/2009, outperforming prior joint models.  
- Strength: Efficiency — linear-time greedy parsing provides a clear speed advantage over more expensive pipeline approaches.  
- Strength: Novel state representation — stack LSTMs encode full parsing state, enabling richer contextual features for greedy parsing.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and useful ablation studies (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid).  

- Weakness: Limited novelty — core components (transition parsing, stack LSTMs, greedy inference) are largely incremental and build heavily on prior work.  
- Weakness: Performance gap to pipeline systems — accuracy still lags the best pipelines, raising questions about the speed–accuracy trade-off.  
- Weakness: Limited error analysis — the paper lacks a detailed breakdown of error types and difficult constructions.  
- Weakness: Incomplete hyperparameter details — insufficient description of tuning procedure and search space, hindering reproducibility.  

- Question: Please provide a detailed error analysis comparing model errors to those of pipeline systems.  
- Question: What were the exact stack LSTM hyperparameters (layers, hidden size, dropout) and the hyperparameter search space?  
- Question: How often is the M-SELF transition used during parsing, and what is its impact on performance?  
- Question: Which languages in the multilingual CoNLL 2009 set benefited most or least from the joint approach?  

- Additional note: Reference to Lei et al. (2015) GitHub is helpful for reproducibility.  
- Additional note: Inclusion of example transition sequences in the supplement is good practice.  
- Additional note: Related work section could be improved by discussing recent SRL advances using pre-trained language models.  

- Overall: A solid, practical contribution demonstrating effective joint parsing and speed, but somewhat incremental and held back by an accuracy gap to top pipeline systems.
