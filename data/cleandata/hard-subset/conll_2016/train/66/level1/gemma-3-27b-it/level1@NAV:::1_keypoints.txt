- Proposes a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs.  
- Learns full parsing-state representations without hand-crafted features via stack LSTMs.  
- Achieves strong performance on CoNLL 2008/2009, competitive or state-of-the-art among joint models.  
- Uses a greedy, linear-time inference algorithm for computational efficiency.  
- Provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation, including multilingual datasets.  
- Establishes a strong baseline for future joint parsing work.  
- Core algorithmic novelty is limited—builds incrementally on established transition-based parsing frameworks.  
- Does not match some SOTA systems that use richer features and optimal decoding; comparisons and ablation analyses are insufficient.  
- Shows dependence on pretrained embeddings (notably hurting Chinese results); the role of embedding quality is underexplored.  
- Requests an ablation study to quantify contributions of model components (stack LSTMs, composition functions).  
- Asks for analysis of different embedding initialization strategies for multilingual models.  
- Requests concrete examples/elaboration of cases where joint learning of syntax and semantics is crucial.
