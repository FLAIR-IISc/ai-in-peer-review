- Primary contribution: a greedy, fully data-driven stack LSTM parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies, outperforming prior joint models and approaching pipeline systems.
- Secondary contribution: successful application of stack LSTMs to joint parsing, extending their benefits from syntactic parsing to the joint task.
- Tertiary contribution: release of a fast, open-source implementation enabling reproducibility and further research.
- Strength — Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art among joint parsers on CoNLL 2008/2009 English, clearly outperforming prior joint approaches.
- Strength — Novel use of stack LSTMs: effective, feature-free representation learning of the full parser state for joint syntactic and semantic parsing, supported by ablations.
- Strength — Efficiency: greedy inference with stack LSTMs yields a very fast parser (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 test), making it practical.
- Strength — Clear presentation: well-written paper with clear transition system description, architecture illustration, and relation to prior work.
- Weakness — Limited novelty in core parsing: underlying transition system (arc-eager) and transition-based parsing approach are established; main novelty is in representation learning.
- Weakness — Performance gap to pipeline systems: still lags behind state-of-the-art pipeline systems with engineered features and optimal decoding.
- Weakness — Limited error analysis: paper lacks deep analysis of error types and linguistic phenomena causing failures.
- Weakness — Sparse hyperparameter tuning details: brief description of tuning without clear search space or methodology.
- Question — Error analysis request: please provide detailed analysis of recurring error types and challenging linguistic constructions for the model.
- Question — Morphology handling: have you tried character-based word embeddings or other methods to capture morphological features, and what were the results?
- Question — Pseudo-projectivization impact: how much does pseudo-projectivization affect performance, and are alternatives viable for non-projective dependencies?
- Question — Semantic self-dependencies: how does the model perform on sentences without M-SELF arcs, and how does the M-SELF transition affect overall performance?
- Additional comment — Verify runtime citation: the reference to Lei et al. (2015) runtime needs verification as the linked repo may not provide comparable measurements.
- Additional comment — Expand related work: related-work discussion should include more recent neural SRL advancements beyond those compared in results.
- Additional comment — Training resources: include details on computational resources required for training the model.
