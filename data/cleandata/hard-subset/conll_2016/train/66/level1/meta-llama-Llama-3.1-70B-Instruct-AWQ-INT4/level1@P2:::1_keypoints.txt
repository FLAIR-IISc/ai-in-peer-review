- Contribution: Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using a stack LSTM to represent the entire algorithm state.
- Contribution: Uses a greedy inference algorithm with linear-time complexity (including feature extraction).
- Contribution: Achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English joint parsing tasks.
- Strength: Novel joint parsing approach that avoids expert-crafted features and pipelining.
- Strength: Demonstrates strong empirical performance (SOTA) on benchmark datasets.
- Strength: Efficient, practical inference due to linear-time greedy algorithm.
- Strength: Authors plan to release an open-source implementation.
- Weakness: Limited comparisons to other state-of-the-art models.
- Weakness: Reliance on pre-trained word embeddings may limit applicability across languages/domains.
- Weakness: Evaluation reports a limited set of metrics.
- Weakness: No error analysis provided to identify failure modes.
- Question: How are out-of-vocabulary words handled and how does that affect performance?
- Question: Can the authors provide more comprehensive comparisons to models using expert features or pipelines?
- Question: How does performance vary across different languages and domains?
- Question: Can the authors provide an error analysis to highlight areas for improvement?
- Additional comment: Paper is promising and practically useful but constrained by comparison, embedding dependence, and limited evaluation.
