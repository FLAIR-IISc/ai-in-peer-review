- Novel neural architecture: extends stack LSTMs to joint syntactic–semantic parsing with separate stacks encoding full parser state without hand-crafted features.
- Strong empirical evidence: a greedy, feature-free model outperforms previous joint systems on CoNLL 2008/2009, supporting representation learning over manual features/search.
- Fast linear-time parser: greedy inference yields linear complexity and practical speedups (~2.5x), making the model efficient for downstream use.
- Elegant model design: four LSTMs (syntax, semantics, buffer, action history) provide a unified, principled parser-state representation.
- State-of-the-art for joint models: achieves new SOTA among joint systems on CoNLL 2008/2009 and is competitive with many pipelined systems.
- Insightful ablation study: Syntax-only/Semantics-only/Hybrid/Joint comparisons show joint benefits in-domain and that Hybrid generalizes better out-of-domain.
- High practicality: linear-time efficiency, speed comparisons, and planned open-source release increase real-world applicability.
- Incremental novelty: core technique (stack LSTM) is adapted from prior work, so conceptual novelty is limited.
- Performance gap vs top pipelined systems: ~2 F1 deficit on semantic F1 versus best pipelined SRL systems, indicating global features/inference still help.
- Transition-system limitation: oracle conversion fails for ~1.5% of sentences, placing an upper bound on performance without analysis of those cases.
- Hyperparameter clarity: several hyperparameters were set "intuitively" without systematic tuning or sensitivity analysis, reducing reproducibility.
- Question on overfitting: what specifically causes the Joint model to overfit WSJ and were regularizers beyond dropout considered?
- Question on predicate identification: why is predicate ID handled by a separate BiLSTM rather than integrated as a parser transition for a fully joint system?
- Question on M-SELF transition: please quantify its impact on self-dependency accuracy and overall semantic F1 when removed.
