- Paper proposes a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependency graphs using four Stack LSTMs to encode parser state, with greedy linear-time inference; evaluated on CoNLL 2008/2009 and beats prior joint models.

- Contribution: applies Stack LSTMs to a joint syntactic-semantic parsing task, learning unified parser-state representations instead of hand-crafted features.

- Contribution: introduces a fast, simple, and accurate joint parser with greedy inference achieving linear-time complexity and a strong speed/accuracy tradeoff.

- Contribution: provides thorough empirical evaluation (Syntax-only, Semantics-only, Hybrid, Joint) on standard benchmarks, analyzing strengths and drawbacks including out-of-domain behavior.

- Strength: strong empirical performance, setting a new state of the art among joint parsers on CoNLL 2008/2009 and outperforming Henderson et al. (2013).

- Strength: model simplicity and efficiency—end-to-end neural model replacing feature engineering, reporting fast runtime (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 test).

- Strength: clear, technically sound presentation with well-designed experiments and planned code release for reproducibility.

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty since Stack LSTMs and composition functions largely follow Dyer et al. (2015); novelty is mainly application/engineering to the joint setting.

- Weakness: does not reach overall state-of-the-art compared to feature-rich pipeline systems, notably lagging on semantic F1.

- Weakness: under-explored generalization issues—Joint model underperforms Hybrid on out-of-domain data, attributed to overfitting without deeper analysis.

- Question: why does the Hybrid model outperform the Joint model out-of-domain—can authors provide a detailed hypothesis and does this temper claims about joint models improving generalization?

- Question: how significant is the 1.5% oracle limitation where the transition system cannot reconstruct gold semantic graphs—does it affect particular semantic structures and could an alternative transition system fix it?

- Question: have the authors tried beam search (instead of greedy) to determine whether the performance gap with top pipeline systems is due to search strategy or limitations of the learned representations?
