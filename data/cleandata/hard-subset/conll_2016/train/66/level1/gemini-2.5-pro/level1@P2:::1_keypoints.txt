- Presents a transition-based parser that jointly learns syntactic and semantic dependency structures using Stack LSTMs to represent the full parser state, yielding a fast greedy linear-time parser with strong CoNLL 2008/2009 results.

- Contribution: Introduces a learned, holistic state representation for joint parsing by encoding syntactic stack, semantic stack, buffer, and action history with separate Stack LSTMs to replace hand-crafted features.

- Contribution: Proposes a state-of-the-art greedy joint parser that outperforms previous joint models on CoNLL benchmarks while avoiding complex search methods.

- Contribution: Delivers an efficient, practical system with linear-time complexity and reported substantial speedups (≈2.5x) over contemporary pipelined models.

- Strength: Novel and effective architecture—extends Stack LSTMs from syntax-only to joint parsing and captures long history information without manual features.

- Strength: Strong, well-positioned empirical results that surpass prior joint systems and honestly acknowledge limits relative to top pipelined systems.

- Strength: Excellent ablation and comparative analysis (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid) that yields insights about joint modeling and overfitting.

- Strength: High practicality and efficiency, with concrete speed comparisons and a promise to release open-source code.

- Weakness: Incremental technical novelty since the core Stack LSTM machinery is adopted from prior work, making this primarily an application/extension paper.

- Weakness: Unexplained performance gap with top pipelined systems—paper lacks detailed error analysis to pinpoint what structural information the joint model misses.

- Weakness: Limited hyperparameter optimization; some settings were chosen intuitively, leaving uncertainty about sensitivity and peak performance.

- Question: Did you try stronger regularization (e.g., L2, increased dropout) to reduce overfitting and improve Joint model out-of-domain performance on Brown?

- Question: What is the impact of the M-SELF transition for NomBank nominal predicates—what are F1 on self-dependencies and overall semantic F1 if disabled?

- Question: Have you considered sharing parameters between syntactic and semantic composition functions (e.g., Zs and Zm) to learn more generalized headed-dependency representations?
