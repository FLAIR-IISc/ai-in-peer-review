- Greedy transition-based joint syntactic-semantic parser using Stack LSTMs to represent the full parser state, removing hand-crafted features.
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008/2009 and multilingual data, achieving new state-of-the-art among joint models while being highly efficient.
- Successful extension of the Stack LSTM parsing paradigm to a complex joint task, showing representation learning can replace feature engineering.
- Thorough empirical analysis with insightful ablation studies that validate effectiveness and efficiency.
- Strength: Elegant, impactful feature-free approach that outperforms previous joint models on standard benchmarks.
- Strength: Comprehensive, convincing evaluation across datasets and systems; ablations effectively isolate joint-learning benefits.
- Strength: Greedy, linear-time inference is substantially faster than beam-search or global methods, making it a strong practical baseline.
- Weakness: Still behind top pipelined systems that use rich syntactic features and global inference, suggesting missing long-distance structural information.
- Weakness: Methodological novelty is incremental—direct adaptation of prior Stack LSTM and transition-system work.
- Weakness: Out-of-domain analysis is underdeveloped; joint model appears more prone to overfitting (e.g., Brown corpus) than the Hybrid pipeline.
- Question: Please clarify why the Joint model is more susceptible to out-of-domain overfitting — does joint training learn domain-specific syntactic-semantic correlations that fail to generalize?
