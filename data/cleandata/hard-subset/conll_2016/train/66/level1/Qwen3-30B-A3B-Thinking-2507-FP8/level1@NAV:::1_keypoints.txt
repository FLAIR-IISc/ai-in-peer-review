- Neural networks can learn effective representations for joint syntactic and semantic parsing without expert-crafted features.  
- Introduces a linear-time greedy inference algorithm that achieves competitive performance.  
- Demonstrates joint parsing can match pipeline approaches in accuracy while being significantly faster.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008–9 English, outperforming previous joint models.  
- Practical speed advantage: linear-time inference (~177.6±18 s single-core) is ~2.5× faster than comparable pipeline systems.  
- Eliminates need for expert-crafted features, showing learned representations suffice.  
- Falls short of top expert feature-based systems that use optimal decoding or additional data.  
- Shows domain overfitting to WSJ; Hybrid model outperforms on out-of-domain Brown corpus.  
- Unclear robustness for low-resource languages—Japanese results indicate dependence on training set size.  
- Question: How would performance change without pretrained embeddings for non-English languages?  
- Question: Can overfitting to WSJ be mitigated via additional data sources or regularization?  
- Question: What is the training computational cost compared to other joint models and how does it scale to larger datasets?
