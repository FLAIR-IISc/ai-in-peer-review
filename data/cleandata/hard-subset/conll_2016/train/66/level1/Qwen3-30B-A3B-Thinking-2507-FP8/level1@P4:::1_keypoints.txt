- Summary: Transition-based joint syntactic-semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent full parser state, claiming feature-free learning, SOTA among joint models, linear-time greedy inference, and much faster runtime than pipeline approaches.

- Contribution 1: Use of stack LSTMs to learn representations of the entire parser state, removing need for expert-crafted features.

- Contribution 2: Demonstration that greedy inference (no beam search) can achieve state-of-the-art results for joint parsing.

- Contribution 3: High efficiency—reported 177.6±18s to parse CoNLL 2009 English test data on a single core and ~2.5x speedup over pipeline models.

- Strength 1: Well-executed technical innovation showing elimination of expert features while improving over prior joint models.

- Strength 2: Strong practical efficiency improvements making the model suitable for real-world use.

- Strength 3: Good multilingual performance across languages, generally on par with top systems (except Chinese without pretrained embeddings).

- Strength 4: Clear, well-structured description of the parsing algorithm and transition system.

- Strength 5: Thorough evaluation with relevant baselines and detailed ablation studies (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid).

- Weakness 1: Does not outperform best expert-crafted feature-based systems that use full syntactic parses and optimal decoding.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient treatment of non-projective syntactic structures and limited details on handling crossing arcs and oracle conversion errors.

- Weakness 3: Multilingual evaluation lacks depth and analysis of scaling with training data size, especially for small-data languages like Japanese.

- Weakness 4: No clear comparison of model performance versus amount of training data required relative to prior systems.

- Weakness 5: Claim of "linear time, including feature extraction" lacks a detailed time-complexity analysis of feature extraction.

- Question 1: How could expert-crafted features from full syntactic parses be incorporated into the model to close the performance gap?

- Question 2: How do oracle conversion imperfections and crossing arcs affect performance, and can the model handle non-projective structures without pseudo-projectivization?

- Question 3: What is the specific impact of pretrained embeddings on Chinese performance, and can alternative embedding strategies help low-resource languages?
