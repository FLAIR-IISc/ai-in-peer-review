- Introduces a transition-based joint syntactic-semantic parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire parser state, removing expert-crafted features.  
- Claims three contributions: stack LSTM-based joint parsing, greedy linear-time inference, and state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008–9 for joint models.  
- Primary novelty is effective application of stack LSTMs to replace expert features in joint parsing.  
- Secondary contribution is demonstrating efficient joint parsing without expensive handcrafted features.  
- Linear-time greedy inference is practically useful but not highly novel.  
- Strong technical contribution in representation learning, with stack LSTMs learning full parser-state representations.  
- Solid empirical validation on CoNLL 2008–9 with clear metrics and ablation studies (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid).  
- Significant runtime advantage over competing systems, demonstrating practical efficiency.  
- Fails to integrate or analyze combination with top feature-based systems that still outperform it.  
- Parsing algorithm shows limited novelty, being largely based on Henderson et al. (2013) with minor changes.  
- Insufficient analysis of limitations, including reasons for WSJ overfitting and lack of failure-case discussion.  
- Question: How can the model be combined with expert-crafted features and which features would help most?  
- Question: How does the model perform without pretrained embeddings (e.g., low-resource languages) and what strategies can improve that?  
- Question: What specific WSJ domain characteristics cause overfitting and how can it be mitigated?  
- Overall: Sound and valuable advance demonstrating stack LSTMs for joint parsing with strong results and efficiency; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address integration and limitation analyses.
