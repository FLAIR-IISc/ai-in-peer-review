- Transition-based joint syntactic and semantic parser using stack LSTMs to replace expert-crafted features.  
- Greedy linear-time inference algorithm that avoids beam search.  
- Practical, fast parser implementation achieving top performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008-9 with 177.6±18s end-to-end runtime.  

- Practical performance and efficiency: substantially faster (≈2.5x) than Lei et al. while maintaining competitive accuracy, highlighting a speed–accuracy trade-off.  
- Novel representation learning: shows stack LSTMs can learn whole-parser-state representations, reducing need for manual feature engineering.  
- Clear architecture and evaluation: well-described model, transitions, training, and convincing comparisons to prior joint parsing work.  

- Limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art expert-crafted systems and no demonstration of how to integrate those features.  
- Overfitting concerns: Joint model underperforms Hybrid on out-of-domain (Brown) data with no analysis of causes.  
- Lack of analysis of learned representations: no ablation studies, visualizations, or probing tasks to show what the stack LSTM captures.  

- Which specific features/techniques from top systems (e.g., Roth & Woodsend 2014; Lei et al. 2015) could be integrated and how would that affect performance?  
- What changes would reduce the Joint model’s overfitting to WSJ and how would those changes impact runtime?  
- Can you provide evidence (visualization, probing, ablation) that the stack LSTM representations capture meaningful linguistic information beyond simpler representations?
