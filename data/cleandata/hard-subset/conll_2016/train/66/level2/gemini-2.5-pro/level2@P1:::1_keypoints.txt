- Summary: Transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to encode full parser state, enabling greedy, feature-free decisions; strong, fast results on CoNLL2008/2009.

- Main contribution 1: Extends stack LSTM architecture to joint parsing, replacing hand-crafted indicator features with learned state representations.

- Main contribution 2: Presents a fast, linear-time joint parser that sets a new state-of-the-art for joint models and is practically efficient.

- Main contribution 3: Provides thorough experiments and an ablation study (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) that illuminate the effects of joint learning.

- Strength 1: Novel and effective model architecture that avoids tedious feature engineering and yields strong empirical gains over prior joint models.

- Strength 2: Thorough and honest evaluation against joint and pipeline baselines, with clear discussion of design trade-offs and limitations.

- Strength 3: High efficiency and practicality due to greedy inference and minimal feature extraction, demonstrated by runtime comparisons.

- Strength 4: Clear writing and insightful analysis, including a useful ablation comparing joint, hybrid, and single-task models in-domain and out-of-domain.

- Weakness 1: Limited algorithmic novelty—the transition system is largely adopted from prior work with only a minor addition.

- Weakness 2: Noticeable performance gap versus top pipelined systems, suggesting learned representations may miss some long-range structural cues provided by hand-crafted features.

- Weakness 3: Predicate identification is pipelined (BiLSTM) for CoNLL2008, undermining the claim of a fully joint model.

- Question 1: Why does the Hybrid model generalize better out-of-domain—does joint learning cause overfitting or spurious domain-specific correlations?

- Question 2: Can you characterize the 1.5% of sentences with imperfect oracle semantic transition sequences and whether this is a hard ceiling for transition-based models?

- Question 3: Can you provide qualitative error analysis—e.g., does the model fail on long-range semantic dependencies where pipeline, feature-rich systems excel?
