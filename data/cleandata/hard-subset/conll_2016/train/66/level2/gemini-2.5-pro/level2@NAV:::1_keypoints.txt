- Greedy transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent parser state without hand-crafted features; evaluated on CoNLL 2008/2009 with strong performance.

- Contribution: Effective application of stack LSTMs to joint parsing, showing learned representations can replace complex feature engineering.

- Contribution: State-of-the-art results among joint parsing models on English benchmarks, with comprehensive experiments and ablations validating the approach.

- Contribution: Highly efficient, linear-time parser that is practical for downstream NLP and contrasts with slower pipeline systems.

- Strength: Excellent empirical performance — new joint-model SOTA on CoNLL 2008/2009 and robust multilingual results.

- Strength: Elegant and principled model that simplifies and generalizes previous work by learning parser-state representations instead of manual features.

- Strength: High practicality and impact due to greedy linear-time complexity and potential open-source implementation.

- Strength: Thorough analysis with ablation studies (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) and transparent discussion of limitations.

- Weakness: Performance gap remains versus top pipelined systems that leverage richer syntactic features and more complex decoding.

- Weakness: Incremental novelty since the work combines existing transition system (Henderson et al.) and stack LSTM architecture (Dyer et al.) rather than introducing wholly new core components.

- Question: Have you experimented with regularization methods beyond dropout to improve the Joint model's generalization and reduce overfitting?

- Question: Can you provide an ablation or analysis of the impact of the novel M-SELF transition on performance?
