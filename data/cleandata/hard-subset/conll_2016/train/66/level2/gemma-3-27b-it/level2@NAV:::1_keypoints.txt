- Proposes a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs to learn parser state without hand-crafted features.  
- Core motivation: avoid error propagation from pipelined systems by using end-to-end learned representations.  
- Primary contribution: a purely learned, greedy joint syntactic-semantic parser achieving state-of-the-art among similar joint models on CoNLL-2008/2009.  
- Secondary contributions: comprehensive experimental comparisons (pipelined and joint), ablation analyses (syntax-only, semantics-only), and demonstration of efficient linear-time greedy inference.  
- Strength: compelling experimental results, especially on English CoNLL 2008/2009, outperforming prior joint models.  
- Strength: clear contextualization within prior work and articulation of advantages of the approach.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of different model configurations showing interplay between syntax and semantics.  
- Strength: reported runtime efficiency is a practical advantage.  
- Weakness: comparison to state-of-the-art systems using expert-crafted features and optimal decoding is somewhat dismissive and lacks exploration of integration possibilities.  
- Weakness: multilingual performance is comparable but lacks deeper language-specific analysis.  
- Weakness: the effect and role of the pseudo-projectivization step are not clearly discussed.  
- Question: how could expert-crafted features be incorporated without losing end-to-end learning benefits?  
- Question: how to handle unseen predicates beyond defaulting to sense “01”?  
- Question: please provide Stack LSTM hyperparameters (layers, hidden size) and rationale for their selection.
