- Greedy linear-time transition-based parser jointly predicts syntactic dependency trees and SRL graphs using stack LSTMs to encode full parser state, avoiding hand-crafted features.  
- Joint transition system adapted with interleaved syntactic/semantic actions, a new M-SELF for NomBank self-dependencies, and learned composition functions.  
- Empirically outperforms prior joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English, is competitive multilingually on CoNLL 2009, and is notably fast.  
- Strong empirical gains with controlled Syntax-only / Semantics-only / Hybrid / Joint comparisons isolating the benefit of joint modeling.  
- Linear-time inference and feature extraction yield substantially faster end-to-end speed than a competitive pipeline SRL system.  
- Clean, reproducible modeling of joint state via four stack LSTMs and recursive composition functions, reducing reliance on engineered features.  
- Sound transition-system extensions (M-SELF, interleaving constraints) that preserve linear complexity and account for semantic graph properties.  
- Sensible multilingual evaluation and practical choices (pretrained embeddings, small-memory CPU training, explicit hyperparameters) for replicability.  
- CoNLL 2008 predicate identification is external (biLSTM), breaking the joint claim and limiting end-to-end learning.  
- No reported oracle upper bound or quantification of gold structures lost due to non-encodable transitions, leaving training/evaluation headroom unclear.  
- Greedy decoding with static oracle training lacks ablations with beam search or globally/dynamic training, risking exposure bias (noted OOD drop on Brown).  
- Incomplete ablations — contributions of pretrained embeddings, action-history LSTM, composition functions, M-SELF/MSWAP, and interleaving constraints are not isolated.  
- Comparison fairness and significance issues: some baselines use extra resources, statistical significance not reported, and speed reported only for English.  
- Request to report oracle upper bounds (LAS, SRL F1, Macro F1) and fraction of gold arcs lost by transition set on CoNLL 2008/2009.  
- Request ablations: remove pretrained embeddings; remove action-history stack; replace recursive composition with head-dependent concatenation; disable M-SELF/MSWAP; try beam search.  
- Question why predicate identification wasn’t integrated into the transition process for CoNLL 2008 and whether joint training was attempted.  
- Ask whether improvements over Henderson et al. and differences among Semantics-only/Hybrid/Joint are statistically significant on WSJ and Brown.  
- Request analysis of main error categories for SRL (e.g., long-distance arguments, adjuncts vs core roles, crossing arcs, nominal predicates) and syntax (non-projectivity) and whether M-SWAP/M-SELF reduce specific errors.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos and clarify notation (e.g., copy vs move for S-RIGHT/S-SHIFT).  
- Report training details: number of epochs/updates, random seeds, and variance over multiple runs.  
- Consider adding character-level/morphological features for morphologically rich languages and report their effect.  
- Suggest globally normalized training, dynamic oracles, or small beam/imitation learning to mitigate exposure bias.  
- Provide multilingual runtime, memory usage, and per-sentence complexity vs sentence length to strengthen practicality claims.
