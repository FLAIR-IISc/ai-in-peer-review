- Fast, greedy transition-based joint parser using stack LSTMs to learn parser-state representations, interleaving syntactic and semantic transitions and adding M-SELF for self-dependencies.
- Claims best reported accuracy among joint syntax–semantics models on CoNLL 2008–2009 English, competitive multilingual performance, and linear-time decoding with strong runtime efficiency.
- Controlled variant analysis (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid, joint) used to compare joint modeling versus pipelining and semantics without syntax.

- Eliminates hand-engineered features by representing full algorithmic state with stack LSTMs, unifying transition systems and representation learning.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms prior joint systems on English CoNLL benchmarks and is competitive across languages; controlled comparisons support benefits of joint modeling.
- Practical efficiency: greedy linear-time inference with on-the-fly feature computation yields much faster end-to-end runtime (~178s) than a strong pipeline baseline.
- Well-designed transition system: simple interleaving over a shared buffer, M-SWAP for crossing arcs, and M-SELF to handle NomBank-style self-dependencies.
- Good multilingual generalization with minimal language-specific engineering, relying mainly on pretrained embeddings.

- Methodological novelty is incremental since core idea adapts Dyer et al. (2015) stack LSTMs; main additions are joint extension and a few transitions.
- Uses greedy decoding with static oracles only, lacking comparisons to beam search, dynamic oracles, or learning-to-search approaches that could reduce error propagation.
- Ablations are coarse; missing finer-grained analyses isolating pretrained embeddings, action-history stack, composition functions, interleaving constraints, and M-SELF effects.
- Oracle conversion limitations are noted but not quantified; no reported upper bounds (LAS/SRL F1) or frequency of infeasible oracle paths.
- Limited qualitative error analysis and robustness studies (predicate types, long-distance arguments, crossing arcs, OOVs, low-resource languages); overfitting to WSJ is observed but not diagnosed.

- Were dynamic oracle training, scheduled sampling, or small beams tried, and what are their accuracy/runtime trade-offs?
- For multilingual experiments, were pretrained embeddings comparable across languages and how sensitive are results to embedding quality or morphological/character features?

- Add statistical significance testing for key comparisons.
- Provide error analysis by dependency length, argument type, and predicate POS to guide improvements.
- Include accuracy-vs-latency curves (e.g., greedy vs small beam) to help practitioners choose settings.
