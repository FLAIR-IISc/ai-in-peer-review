- Summary: Proposes a transition-based parser that jointly predicts syntactic and semantic dependencies using a stack LSTM state representation, with greedy linear-time inference; evaluated on CoNLL 2008/2009 English and reports top joint-model performance.

- Contribution: Introduces joint syntactic/semantic dependency parsing via a stack LSTM that models the entire algorithm state.

- Contribution: Proposes a greedy inference algorithm with linear time complexity (including feature extraction) for efficient, scalable parsing.

- Contribution: Achieves state-of-the-art results among joint models on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks.

- Strength: Novel joint parsing approach enabling simultaneous syntax and semantics prediction through a stack LSTM.

- Strength: Efficient, scalable greedy inference with linear-time complexity.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance, outperforming previous joint parsing models on benchmark tasks.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to English CoNLL 2008/2009, reducing evidence of cross-lingual or cross-domain generality.

- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to other joint-model families (e.g., graph-based or pipeline approaches).

- Weakness: Dependence on pretrained word embeddings may limit applicability to languages or domains without such resources.

- Question: How will the model incorporate morphological features important for highly inflective languages?

- Question: Please provide more implementation details of the stack LSTM and the composition functions used.

- Question: What are the plans for evaluating the model on other languages or tasks, such as the CoNLL 2009 multilingual shared task?
