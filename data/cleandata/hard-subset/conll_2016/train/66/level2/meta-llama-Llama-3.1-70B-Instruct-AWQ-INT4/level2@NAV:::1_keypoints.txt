- Summary: Transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using a stack LSTM to represent full parser state; trained on CoNLL 2008/2009 and reports state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution: Proposes a novel joint syntactic–semantic parsing approach that conditions decisions on a learned representation of the entire algorithmic state.

- Contribution: Introduces the use of a stack LSTM to encode the parser state for decision making.

- Contribution: Presents a greedy inference algorithm with linear-time complexity for efficient parsing.

- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing (CoNLL 2008/2009 English).

- Strength: Uses an efficient, linear-time greedy inference algorithm suitable for practical applications.

- Strength: Avoids expert-crafted full-parse features, increasing generality and robustness.

- Weakness: Relies on pretrained embeddings, which may not be available or effective across all languages/domains.

- Weakness: Performance may degrade on small training datasets (e.g., Japanese).

- Weakness: Does not incorporate morphological features, limiting effectiveness on highly inflective languages.

- Question: How will the model be extended to incorporate morphological features?

- Question: Please provide more training details (number of epochs, learning rate schedule, etc.).

- Question: How do you plan to address performance issues on small training datasets such as Japanese?
