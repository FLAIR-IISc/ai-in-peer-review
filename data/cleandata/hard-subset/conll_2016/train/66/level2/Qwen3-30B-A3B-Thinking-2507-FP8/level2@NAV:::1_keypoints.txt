- Summary: Transition-based joint syntactic-semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs for greedy, linear-time inference.  
- Contribution: Novel application of stack LSTMs to jointly model syntax and semantics without expert features.  
- Contribution: State-of-the-art performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008–9 English.  
- Contribution: Highly efficient implementation (177.6s single-core on CoNLL 2009 test set).  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation — SOTA joint parsing with 1.5–2.3% semantic F1 gains and statistical significance.  
- Strength: Compelling efficiency — ~2.5× speed advantage over pipeline systems with end-to-end runtime reporting.  
- Strength: Clear novelty — full-state stack LSTMs address limitations of prior local-feature approaches.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to pipeline SOTA SRL systems and lack of quantified gap or error analysis, especially on Brown out-of-domain data.  
- Weakness: Missing ablation study isolating the stack LSTM’s contribution versus a local-feature baseline.  
- Weakness: Greedy inference trade-offs unexplored — no comparison to beam search or justification of performance-speed trade-off.  
- Question: How would adding expert-crafted features (e.g., syntactic paths) affect out-of-domain Brown performance?  
- Question: Can you provide an ablation comparing full-state stack LSTM to a local-feature variant?  
- Question: What strategies do you propose to improve generalization given underperformance on Brown (possible overfitting)?
