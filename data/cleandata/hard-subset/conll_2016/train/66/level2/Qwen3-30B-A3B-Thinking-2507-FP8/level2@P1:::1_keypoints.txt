- Summary: Proposes a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs, with greedy inference and linear-time parsing that outperforms prior joint models on CoNLL 2008–9 English.

- Strength 1: Demonstrates stack LSTMs can replace expert-crafted features in joint parsing and achieve competitive performance versus previous joint models.

- Strength 2: Offers a practical linear-time parser (177.6±18 s on CoNLL 2009 English test on a single core), showing clear efficiency benefits.

- Strength 3: Provides a clear, detailed description of the transition algorithm, stack LSTM architecture, implementation, and experimental setup aiding reproducibility.

- Strength 4: Thorough comparison to related work, situating the contribution relative to both joint and pipeline approaches.

- Weakness 1: Lacks sufficient analysis explaining why the joint model outperforms or competes with pipeline approaches that use full syntactic parses and expert features.

- Weakness 2: Ablation studies are limited and do not convincingly isolate the contribution of the stack LSTM representation versus other architectural choices.

- Weakness 3: Multilingual results are weak—Chinese performance depends heavily on pretrained embeddings without clear explanation or mitigation strategies.

- Weakness 4: Does not explore integration with top-performing expert-crafted feature systems, missing an opportunity to show complementary benefits.

- Question 1: Clarify why the joint model is competitive with pipeline systems (e.g., Zhao et al., 2009) despite not using the same expert-crafted features.

- Question 2: Provide details on how pretrained embeddings are used for Chinese and whether alternatives for low-resource languages were explored.

- Question 3: Show more specific evidence of how the claimed “entire algorithm state” representation differs from and improves upon Henderson et al. (2008).

- Question 4: Analyze common error types and where the model fails relative to top expert-feature-based systems.
