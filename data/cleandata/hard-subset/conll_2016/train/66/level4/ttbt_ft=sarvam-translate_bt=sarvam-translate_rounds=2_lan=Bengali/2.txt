General Comments
================
This paper presents a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser, inspired by the joint parser of Henderson et al. (2008).

The authors claim two main differences:

- Vector representation is used for the entire parser state, rather than the top element/last parser configuration of the stack.
- The algorithm is a simple greedy search.

The main idea is to take advantage of stack LSTMs so that the vector representing the parser state remembers potentially large scoped syntactic features, which are known as defining features for semantic role labeling (e.g., paths between the predicate and the candidate role-fulfilling head).

The system has been tested on the CoNLL 2008 dataset (English) and the multilingual CoNLL 2009 dataset.

The authors compare their system's performance with previously published performances,

finding their system to be better than the 2008/2009 system, but less than recent proposals (see Table 3 below).

However, they emphasize that no manual feature is required for the proposed system,

and that it is fast due to the simple greedy algorithm.

The paper is well-written and describes enough work on the recently popular LSTMs applied to the Henderson et al. algorithm,

which now seems somewhat distant.

I have some doubts about the choice of the simple greedy algorithm:

It does not give comparable results with some of the work cited.

The instance beam-search performance did not require too much extra work or space.

Further Comments / Questions
==================================

Section 2:
A comment on the presence of both A1 and C-A1 links will help to better understand the paper's goal.

A summary of the differences between the set of transitions used in this work and Henderson et al. should be provided.

In its current form, it is difficult to say what has been reused directly from Henderson et al. and what is new/slightly modified.

Section 3.3
Why do you need the addition of the predicate word and its unambiguous meaning?

It seems unnecessary since the unambiguous meaning is specific to a predicate?

Section 4
The organization of Section 4.1 / 4.2 is confusing about multilingualism.

CoNLL 2008 focused on the English language and CoNLL 2009 expanded the widely used work to several more languages.
