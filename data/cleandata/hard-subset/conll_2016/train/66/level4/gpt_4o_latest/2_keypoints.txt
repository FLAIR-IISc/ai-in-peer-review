- Paper introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Two main differences: use vectorial representations for the entire parser state instead of top-of-stack or last configurations.  
- Second difference: parsing uses a plain greedy search (no beam).  
- Central innovation: use stack LSTMs to build parser-state vectors that capture wide-ranging syntactic features.  
- These long-range features are particularly useful for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument path).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Performance is competitive with 2008/2009 systems but lags behind more recent methods (see Table 3).  
- Advantage: does not rely on hand-crafted features and is highly efficient due to greedy decoding.  
- Paper is well-written and applies stack LSTMs effectively to the Henderson et al. algorithm.  
- Concern: reliance on greedy search limits comparability; including beam-search results would strengthen evaluation.  
- Section 2 needs explanations for both A1 and C-A1 links and a concise summary of differences from Henderson et al.  
- Section 3.3 questions necessity of concatenating predicate with its disambiguated sense (seems redundant).  
- Section 4 should clarify the multilingual setup—CoNLL 2008 is English, CoNLL 2009 is multilingual.
