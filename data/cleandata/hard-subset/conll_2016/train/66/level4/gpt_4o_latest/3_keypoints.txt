- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models; joint models are promising for transition-based neural parsers.
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system improving on Henderson (2008), and evidence that neural networks outperform historical linear models on this task.
- Reviewer suggests an additional advantage: neural networks are more parameter-efficient than linear models because they avoid learning a weight for every feature/class pair in joint learning.
- The proposed transition system would likely perform well with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).
- Many orthogonal improvements could be explored and extensions might achieve state-of-the-art results.
- Deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system would be valuable and should be included in an appendix or future work.
- The system appears analogous to the arc-eager oracle: M-S excludes semantic arcs between buffer-start and semantic-stack words, M-D excludes arcs between stack-top and buffer words, and L/R exclude the reverse arc only.
