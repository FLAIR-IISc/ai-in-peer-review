This paper presents a stack LSTM parser based on the combined syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) and the stack LSTM syntactic parsing of Dyer et al. (2015). Comparing the use of the transition system from the former and the stack LSTM from the latter shows interesting results compared to the joint system on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I really liked this paper because it is well-written, well-explained, the related work is good, and the results are very interesting. The methodology is solid (with a minor concern about Chinese embedding, which makes me think that very good embeddings might be more informative than a very clever model...).

Furthermore, the system description is clear, the hyperparameters are reasonable, and the discussion is interesting.

I would only say that the proposed system lacks originality, in the sense that Henderson et al.'s work laid the foundation for semi-synchronized joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parsing many years ago, and Dyer et al. came up with stack LSTM last year, so it is not a new method in itself.

But in my opinion, we were waiting for such a parser to be designed, and so I am glad it was done here.
