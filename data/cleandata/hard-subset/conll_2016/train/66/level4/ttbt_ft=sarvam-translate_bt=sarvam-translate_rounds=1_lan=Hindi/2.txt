General Notes
================
This paper presents a combined syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser, inspired by the joint parser of Henderson et al. (2008).

The authors claim two main differences:

- Instead of configuring the top elements of the stack / the final parser, vector representations are used to represent the entire parser's state.
- The algorithm is a simple greedy search.

The main idea is to leverage stack LSTMs so that the vector representing the parser's state potentially holds memory of syntactic features with a large scope, which are considered crucial features for semantic role labeling (e.g., the path between the predicate and the candidate role filler head).

The system has been tested on the CoNLL 2008 data set (English) and the multilingual CoNLL 2009 data set.

The authors compare their system's performance with previously reported performances, showing that their system performs well compared to the 2008/2009 systems, but less than recent proposals (see table below 3).

However, they emphasize that the proposed system does not require any hand-crafting features and is fast due to the simple greedy algorithm.

This paper is well-written and describes a substantial amount of work based on recently popular LSTMs, which has been applied to the Henderson et al. algorithm, which now seems somewhat far-sighted.

I have reservations about the choice of the simple greedy algorithm:

- It does not present results comparable to some of the cited works.
- There will not be much additional work or space for instance beam-search-optimized performance.
