

General Comments  
================  
This paper introduces a joint syntactic-semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008). The authors highlight two key innovations: (1) the parser’s entire state is represented via vector embeddings rather than solely the top stack elements or recent configurations, and (2) the algorithm employs a straightforward greedy decoding strategy. The core insight leverages stack LSTMs to encode long-range syntactic dependencies—critical for semantic role labeling (e.g., paths between predicates and role fillers)—within the state vector. Evaluated on CoNLL-2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL-2009, the system achieves competitive results against 2008/2009 baselines but lags behind recent state-of-the-art approaches (Table 3). The authors emphasize the parser’s advantages: no hand-crafted features and efficient greedy decoding. The paper is well-structured and demonstrates meaningful work applying LSTM techniques to a previously visionary framework.  

However, the choice of greedy decoding warrants concern. It renders the results incomparable to several cited works that use beam search, which is a standard practice for such parsers. Given the minimal computational overhead of reporting beam-search results (e.g., 1–2 additional lines in the experiments), this omission weakens the comparative analysis. The authors’ claim of "simplicity" as a key strength is undermined without this context.  

Detailed Comments  
================  
**Section 2:**  
Clarify the distinction between A1 and C-A1 links, as their coexistence in the target task is unclear. A brief explanation would help readers understand the semantic labeling objectives. Additionally, explicitly summarize the transition set differences relative to Henderson et al. (2008). Currently, it is difficult to discern which transitions are reused versus modified, reducing transparency.  

**Section 3.3:**  
The need for concatenating the predicate word with its disambiguated sense appears redundant. Since disambiguated senses are inherently predicate-specific, this concatenation likely adds no new information. Please justify this design choice or clarify its purpose.  

**Section 4 (Organization):**  
The discussion of multilingualism in Sections 4.1–4.2 is confusing. CoNLL-2008 was English-only, while CoNLL-2009 expanded to multiple languages. The paper should explicitly acknowledge this data split to avoid misrepresenting the scope of multilingual evaluation. For instance, clarify whether the "multilingual" results in Section 4.2 stem solely from CoNLL-2009 or include additional languages beyond the 2009 task.  

**Additional Clarifications Needed:**  
- The paper states the parser "does not require hand-crafted features," yet it uses word embeddings and POS tags. Specify whether these are considered *hand-crafted* or standard pre-processing.  
- The "simple greedy algorithm" claim requires elaboration: Is this solely due to the decoding strategy, or are other components (e.g., feature engineering) also simplified?  
- Table 3 compares against "recent proposals" but lacks specific baselines (e.g., "Zhang et al. 2015" instead of "more recent works"). Include these citations for reproducibility.  

**Overall Assessment:**  
The work is technically sound and builds coherently on existing literature. The stack-LSTM approach is well-motivated for capturing long-range dependencies relevant to semantic parsing. However, the lack of beam-search results and the ambiguous multilingual evaluation framework limit the paper’s impact. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the contribution. The writing is clear, but minor revisions to Sections 2, 3.3, and 4 are essential for precision.  

**Recommendation:**  
**Weak Accept** (with minor revisions). The core idea is valuable, but the omitted beam-search results and methodological ambiguities prevent a stronger endorsement. The authors should:  
1. Report beam-search results (even if brief) for fair comparison.  
2. Clarify transition set modifications and the A1/C-A1 distinction.  
3. Resolve the multilingual evaluation description in Section 4.  
4. Justify the predicate-sense concatenation or remove it.  

The paper would benefit from tighter integration of its technical claims with the evaluation methodology. With these revisions, it would make a solid contribution to the field.
