- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural models.  
- Joint modeling is a promising approach underpinning the success of transition-based neural parsers.  
- Contribution 1: a novel transition system that appears superior to Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: neural network models succeed on this joint task where linear models previously failed.  
- Additional noted advantage: neural networks are more parameter-efficient; linear models incur quadratic parameter growth when jointly modeling multiple tasks.  
- The proposed transition system will likely generalize to other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Orthogonal improvements could yield state-of-the-art performance.  
- Suggestion: derive a dynamic oracle for this transition system; it appears analogous to an arc-eager oracle with M-S, M-D, and L/R action exclusion behaviors.
