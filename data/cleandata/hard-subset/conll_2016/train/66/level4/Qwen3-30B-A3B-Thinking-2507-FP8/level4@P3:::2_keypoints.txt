- Transition-based parser jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs representing the full parser state.  
- Novel full-state representation with stack LSTMs removes need for hand-crafted features and enables learning long-range dependencies.  
- Demonstrated top performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English without relying on full syntactic parses or expert-crafted features.  
- Significant computational efficiency: greedy inference runtime 177.6±18s on CoNLL 2009 English, ~2.5× faster than pipeline alternatives.  
- Strong empirical validation across CoNLL 2008/2009 and multilingual data with ablation studies comparing syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid, and joint models.  
- Joint full-state model consistently outperforms prior approaches and achieves state-of-the-art on CoNLL 2009 among comparable systems.  
- Weakness: no beam-search comparisons provided, limiting assessment against beam/optimal-decoding systems.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why the model underperforms top SRL systems that use expert-crafted features.  
- Weakness: confusing presentation and lack of context for multilingual results (Section 4.2 and Table 4).  
- Question: provide beam-searched performance (even small beam) to better contextualize greedy results.  
- Question: specify which expert-crafted features the model lacks and whether they can be added without large computational cost.  
- Question: clarify whether Chinese gains come from pretrained embeddings and if character-based embeddings would suffice for resource-poor languages.  
- Question: explain how the model handles morphological richness (e.g., German/Czech) and whether highly inflective languages pose limitations.
