

This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that builds on Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) for semi-synchronous joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) for stack LSTM syntactic parsing. The integration of these components yields compelling results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks, demonstrating the viability of this approach.  

I found the paper highly compelling: it is clearly written, thoroughly explained, and supported by strong related work. The methodology is sound, with well-justified hyperparameters and an insightful discussion. A minor caveat pertains to the Chinese embeddings—this suggests that exceptionally high-quality embeddings might provide more value than an exceptionally complex model, though this does not undermine the paper’s core contribution.  

The primary limitation is a lack of novelty. Henderson et al. established the foundation for semi-synchronous joint parsing years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM framework last year. Thus, the proposed system is not a fundamentally new method. However, this implementation fills a gap we have been anticipating: a practical, unified parser combining these established components. The authors deliver precisely what the community needed, making this work valuable despite its incremental nature.
