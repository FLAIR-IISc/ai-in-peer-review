- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Key innovation: represent the full parser state with vector embeddings (stack LSTMs) rather than relying only on top stack elements or last configuration.  
- Key innovation: uses plain greedy search decoding.  
- Stack LSTMs allow retention of long-distance syntactic features important for semantic role labeling.  
- Evaluated on English CoNLL 2008 and multilingual CoNLL 2009; competitive with older 2008/2009 systems but below more recent approaches.  
- System requires no hand-crafted features and is fast due to greedy decoding; paper is well written and builds on recent LSTM work.  
- Major concern: choice of greedy search makes results hard to compare to prior work using beam search; adding beam-search results would be straightforward and strengthen the evaluation.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on the roles of A1 vs C-A1 links and a clear summary of which transitions are reused or modified from Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Section 3.3 should justify concatenating the predicate word with its disambiguated sense, which seems redundant.  
- Section 4 should be reorganized to clearly distinguish that CoNLL 2008 is English-only while CoNLL 2009 is multilingual.
