- Introduces a joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs, representing full parser state as vector embeddings and using greedy decoding.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL-2008 (English) and CoNLL-2009 (multilingual); competitive with 2008/2009 baselines but behind recent state-of-the-art.  
- Claims advantages of no hand-crafted features and efficient greedy decoding.  
- Major concern: omission of beam-search results makes comparisons to beam-based parsers unfair and weakens the "simplicity" claim.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on the distinction between A1 and C-A1 links and explicit statements of which transitions differ from Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Section 3.3: concatenating predicate words with disambiguated senses appears redundant—needs justification or removal.  
- Section 4: multilingual evaluation is confusing—must clarify CoNLL-2008 vs CoNLL-2009 data splits and exactly which languages/tasks are included.  
- Clarify whether word embeddings and POS tags count as "hand-crafted features" or standard preprocessing.  
- Explain whether "simple greedy algorithm" refers only to decoding or to other simplifications (e.g., feature engineering).  
- Table 3 should cite specific recent baselines (e.g., named papers like Zhang et al. 2015) for reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well-motivated use of stack-LSTMs, but lack of beam-search results and evaluation ambiguities limit impact.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept (minor revisions); required fixes include reporting beam-search results, clarifying transition changes and A1/C-A1, resolving multilingual description, and justifying/removing predicate-sense concatenation.
