

This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that combines Henderson et al.'s (2008, 2013) joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing framework with Dyer et al.'s (2015) stack LSTM architecture for syntactic parsing. The integration of these components yields promising results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks when compared to prior joint parsing systems. I find this paper particularly strong: it is well-written, clearly explained, and features a thorough related work discussion. The results are compelling, the methodology is sound (with a minor concern regarding Chinese embeddings, leading me to believe that very good embeddings can be more informative than a very clever model), and the system description is precise, with well-justified hyperparameters and an insightful discussion. The sole limitation is the lack of originality in the methodological approach; Henderson et al. established the foundation for semi-synchronized joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM last year, meaning the proposed system is not fundamentally new per se. However, I believe the field has been awaiting such a parser, and I am pleased it has been implemented here.
