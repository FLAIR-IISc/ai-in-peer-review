- Presents a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic parser using stack LSTMs to represent full parser state, removing need for expert features.  
- Claims contributions: novel transition system (M-SWAP, M-SELF), neural full-state representations for joint parsing, and efficient greedy parsing with strong empirical performance.  
- Primary empirical contribution: full-state stack LSTM enables competitive joint parsing without expensive feature engineering.  
- Practical advantage: greedy parser is computationally efficient (≈177.6±18s on CoNLL 2009 English test, ~2.5× faster than a pipeline baseline).  
- Ablation study: thorough comparison of Syntax-only, Semantics-only, Hybrid, and Joint models showing mutual benefits of joint learning and Hybrid's out-of-domain promise.  
- Strength: novel state-representation approach convincingly removes need for hand-crafted features and achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008–9.  
- Strength: clear computational efficiency gains with direct comparison to other systems.  
- Strength: careful ablation experiments that substantiate the value of joint training for both tasks.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to top SRL systems and no evaluation of integrating orthogonal advances (e.g., interaction features, exact inference) to quantify potential gains.  
- Weakness: insufficient handling/discussion of non-projective sentences (≈1.5% in CoNLL 2009 English) and their effect on results.  
- Weakness: absence of a dynamic oracle for the transition system, leaving limits of the transition system vs. learning approach unclear.  
- Question: quantify impact of the 1.5% non-projective sentences and performance if handled correctly.  
- Question: specify which orthogonal improvements would most boost performance and plans to integrate them.  
- Question: provide quantitative parameter-count comparison between the neural model and comparable linear models.  
- Question: explain why pretrained embeddings were used only for Chinese and implications for generalizability to languages without large pretrained resources.
