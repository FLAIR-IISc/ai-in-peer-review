- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Two main distinctions: uses vectorial representations for the entire parser state and employs a simple greedy search algorithm.  
- Core innovation: stack LSTMs maintain a state vector that captures long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–filler paths).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Results are competitive with 2008/2009 systems but fall short of more recent approaches (see bottom of Table 3).  
- Authors emphasize lack of hand-crafted features and the efficiency of the greedy algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and effectively applies modern LSTMs to Henderson et al.’s framework.  
- Reviewer objects to the greedy search choice because it reduces comparability with other works; beam-search results should have been included.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on the roles of A1 vs. C-A1 links and a concise comparison of transition sets to Henderson et al. (2008) to separate reused vs. novel elements.  
- Section 3.3 should explain why word predicates are concatenated with disambiguated senses, which seems redundant.  
- Section 4’s organization around multilingualism is confusing; clarify that CoNLL 2008 was English-only while CoNLL 2009 covered multiple languages.
