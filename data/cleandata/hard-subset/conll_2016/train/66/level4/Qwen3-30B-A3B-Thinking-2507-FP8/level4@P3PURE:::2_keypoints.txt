- Paper presents a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Two claimed innovations: full parser state represented as vectorial embeddings and use of a simple greedy search strategy.  
- Uses stack LSTMs to retain wide-scope syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument paths).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Results are competitive with 2008/2009 systems but worse than more recent approaches.  
- Emphasizes no hand-crafted features and efficiency gains from greedy inference.  
- Reviewer praises writing and effort but questions the choice of a purely greedy algorithm and lack of beam-search comparisons.  
- Section 2 needs brief explanation of A1 and C-A1 links and a clear summary of which transitions are reused vs. modified from Henderson et al.  
- Section 3.3 questions the necessity of concatenating the predicate with its disambiguated sense as potentially redundant.  
- Section 4’s organization is confusing about multilingual coverage; clarify the progression from CoNLL 2008 (English) to CoNLL 2009 (multilingual).
