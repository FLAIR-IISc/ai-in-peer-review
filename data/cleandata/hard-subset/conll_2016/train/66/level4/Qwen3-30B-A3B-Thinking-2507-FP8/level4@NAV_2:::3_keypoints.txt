- Summary: Introduces a transition-based joint syntactic-semantic parser using stack LSTMs that learns whole-algorithmic-state representations, uses greedy inference, runs in linear time, and attains SOTA on CoNLL 2008–2009 without expert-crafted full-parse features.

- Contribution 1: Novel transition-based method that models the entire algorithmic state (not just stack tops), removing the need for expert-crafted features.

- Contribution 2: Shows neural networks can successfully handle joint parsing without expensive feature engineering, outperforming previous joint models.

- Contribution 3: Presents a greedy, linear-time inference algorithm that is substantially faster than prior joint parsing systems.

- Strength 1: Empirically demonstrates neural models can replace complex feature engineering for joint parsing, simplifying the pipeline while maintaining performance.

- Strength 2: Achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 without costly features or complex decoding, improving practical applicability.

- Strength 3: Delivers a significant speedup (e.g., 177.6±18 s vs. 439.9±42 s on CoNLL 2009 English), making it more suitable for real-world use.

- Weakness 1: Fails to explain or provide evidence for the claim that neural models are more parameter-efficient for joint learning than linear models.

- Weakness 2: Does not provide a dynamic oracle for the transition system, leaving theoretical properties and potential performance gains unexamined.

- Weakness 3: Does not investigate whether the transition system generalizes to other neural architectures (e.g., beam search), limiting claims of generality.

- Question 1: Please provide detailed evidence or analysis supporting the parameter-efficiency claim—is it theoretical or empirical?

- Question 2: Can a dynamic oracle be derived for your transition system, and how would it differ from the arc-eager oracle given semantic transition constraints?

- Question 3: Have you tried the transition system with other architectures (e.g., global beam-search models like Andor 2016) to test architecture-agnostic benefits?
