- Timely, valuable revisit of joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural networks.
- Neural models succeed where prior linear models struggled, enabled by a refined transition system building on Henderson (2008).
- Core contribution 1: a new transition system that outperforms Henderson’s, especially on complex semantic dependencies.
- Core contribution 2: neural architectures achieve state-of-the-art results by automatically learning relevant features.
- Paper overlooks and should highlight neural models’ parameter efficiency versus linear models’ combinatorial feature-weight explosion.
- The proposed transition system is modular and likely generalizes to other neural frameworks (e.g., global beam-search models).
- Suggested extensions: incorporate richer contextual embeddings and multi-task learning to potentially improve results further.
- Minor but important recommendation: derive a dynamic oracle to formalize and strengthen theoretical guarantees (clarify M-S and M-D exclusions).
- Empirical evaluation is thorough (e.g., CoNLL-2009) and ablation studies convincingly isolate the transition system and neural features’ impacts.
- Recommend expanding discussion comparing handcrafted features to learned neural features to emphasize practical advantages.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, well-executed, suited for CONLL_2016; accept with minor revisions.
