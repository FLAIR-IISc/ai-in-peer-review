- Introduces a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser that learns entire-algorithm-state representations using stack LSTMs.  
- Combines Henderson et al.'s synchronized transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM technique.  
- Eliminates the need for expert-crafted features while maintaining competitive performance on CoNLL 2008 and 2009.  
- Key contribution: learning whole-state representations enables a greedy parser to match or exceed prior joint parsing models.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008 English and comparable performance on CoNLL 2009 without expensive feature engineering.  
- Implementation is efficient: end-to-end runtime 177.6±18 seconds on CoNLL 2009 English (single core), ~2.5× faster than comparable pipeline systems.  
- Thorough ablation study shows value of joint learning: Semantics-only underperforms Joint on in-domain data.  
- Ablation also shows Hybrid model outperforms Joint on out-of-domain data.  
- Strength: compelling empirical case that whole-state learning can replace expert-crafted features.  
- Strength: implementation is practical and much faster than state-of-the-art pipelines.  
- Strength: experimental evaluation is thorough (multilingual results, ablations, multiple baselines) and writing is clear.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—primarily a combination of existing techniques without new algorithmic or architectural innovations.  
- Weakness: Chinese results heavily rely on high-quality pretrained embeddings, limiting low-resource applicability.  
- Weakness: inadequate handling of unseen predicate senses at test time, defaulting to "01" may not generalize.  
- Missing comparison to recent pipeline systems using similar features with exact inference (e.g., Täckström et al., 2015).  
- Questions for authors: how to handle unseen predicate senses for low-resource languages; impact of pretrained-embedding reliance on applicability; why recent exact-inference pipeline systems were not compared.  
- Overall: well-written and practically valuable despite limited novelty; minor concerns about embeddings and predicate-sense handling do not undermine main contributions.
