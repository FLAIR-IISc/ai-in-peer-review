

This paper introduces a Stack LSTM parser that integrates Henderson et al.'s (2008, 2013) joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing framework with Dyer et al.'s (2015) Stack LSTM architecture for syntactic parsing. The combination of these established components yields compelling results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks when compared to prior joint systems. I find this paper highly compelling: it is exceptionally well-written and clearly explained, with thorough related work coverage, highly interesting results, and a sound methodology. The system description is lucid, hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is insightful. A minor concern exists regarding the Chinese embeddings, suggesting that exceptionally high-quality embeddings may provide greater utility than an exceptionally sophisticated model. The only reservation is that the approach lacks significant novelty, as Henderson et al. established the foundation for semi-synchronised joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the Stack LSTM framework recently. Nevertheless, I believe this implementation fills a valuable gap, and I am pleased it has been realized here.
