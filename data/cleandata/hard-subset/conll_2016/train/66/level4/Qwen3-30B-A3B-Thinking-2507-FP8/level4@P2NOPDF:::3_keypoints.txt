- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural networks.  
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system improving on Henderson (2008) and empirical proof that neural models succeed where linear models failed.  
- Neural models reduce the parameter burden in joint learning by implicitly capturing feature interactions, enabling parameter-efficient joint modeling.  
- The transition system appears promising and likely independent of the specific neural architecture.  
- The system could be combined with other state-of-the-art neural parsing techniques (e.g., global beam-search) and extended to reach state-of-the-art with richer features or multi-task learning.  
- A dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system is a valuable future direction to provide theoretical foundation and fairer comparisons.  
- The system resembles arc-eager parsing with key semantic-arc constraints, suggesting that deriving a dynamic oracle may be feasible.  
- Evaluation is thorough, showing consistent improvements over strong baselines on CTB and SemEval-2015; neural models outperform linear ones.  
- The paper should more explicitly link transition design (e.g., M-S/M-D constraints) to neural feature extraction—an ablation study would help.  
- Minor issues: avoid vague phrasing, quantify comparative claims, and acknowledge prior work on neural feature selection (e.g., Zhang et al., 2015).  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper with a practical transition system and a noteworthy parameter-efficiency insight that should be emphasized.
