- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.
- Emphasizes joint parsing frameworks as central to transition-based neural parser success.
- Introduces a novel transition system that significantly outperforms the Henderson (2008) baseline.
- Shows neural networks succeed on this joint task where linear models previously failed.
- Attributes neural success to automatic feature learning and, more fundamentally, to parameter efficiency versus combinatorial weight explosion in linear models.
- Suggests the transition system should generalize to other neural architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016 global beam-search).
- Notes the approach is compatible with many orthogonal improvements and could reach state-of-the-art with extensions; calls for more exploration of these avenues.
- Identifies a notable omission: no dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system.
- Describes the system’s oracle-like constraints (M-S, M-D, L, R action exclusions) and recommends formalizing a dynamic oracle to clarify theory and improve training efficiency.
