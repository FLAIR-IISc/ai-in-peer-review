- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using neural networks, noting joint modeling’s promise for transition-based parsers.
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system improving on Henderson (2008) and showing neural networks succeed on this task where linear models failed.
- Neural networks offer greater parameter-efficiency versus linear models, avoiding per-feature-per-class weight explosion via structured feature representations.
- The proposed transition system should pair well with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016 global beam-search) and could be extended toward state-of-the-art with orthogonal improvements.
- Recommend deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system (appendix or future work); initial analysis indicates similarity to an arc-eager oracle with specific exclusion behaviors for M-S, M-D, L, and R actions.
