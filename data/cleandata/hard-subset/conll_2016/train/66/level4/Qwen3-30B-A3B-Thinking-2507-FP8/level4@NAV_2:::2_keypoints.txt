- Proposes a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire parser algorithmic state.
- Claims stack LSTMs enable greedy inference to achieve competitive performance without expert-crafted features.
- Presents a fast end-to-end system with reported runtime ~177.6±18 seconds on CoNLL 2009 English (single core).

- Demonstrates stack LSTMs can replace expensive feature engineering and capture syntactic path features via algorithmic state.
- Provides strong empirical validation across CoNLL 2008 and 2009, outperforming previous joint parsing models while avoiding pipeline architectures.
- Implements a greedy linear-time parser (including feature extraction) that is nearly 2.5× faster than comparable pipeline approaches.

- Omits beam search results, impeding direct comparison with prior work that uses beam search.
- Section 2 lacks a detailed description/comparison of transition set modifications relative to Henderson et al. (2008).
- Sections 4.1–4.2 are confusing about multilingual evaluation and do not clearly distinguish CoNLL 2008 (English) from CoNLL 2009 (multiple languages).

- Why concatenate word predicate representations with disambiguated sense representations instead of using sense representations alone?
- Please provide beam search results (e.g., beam=5) for direct comparison to prior work.
- Clarify Chinese results' reliance on pretrained embeddings, expected performance without them, and alternatives for low-resource languages.
