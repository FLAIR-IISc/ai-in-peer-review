- Introduces a joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parser using vector representations of entire parser states rather than only top stack elements.  
- Uses stack LSTMs to encode long-distance syntactic dependencies important for semantic role labeling.  
- Employs a simple greedy search algorithm instead of beam search.  
- Evaluated on English CoNLL 2008 and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Achieves competitive results against 2008/2009 systems but underperforms compared to more recent approaches.  
- Requires no hand-crafted features and claims computational efficiency due to greedy decoding.  
- Paper is well-structured and effectively builds on Henderson et al. (2008) with LSTM techniques.  
- Concern that using greedy search makes results hard to compare with works using beam search; adding beam results would strengthen evaluation.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on the roles of A1 and C-A1 links and explicit differences from Henderson et al.’s transition set.  
- Section 3.3 should justify concatenating the predicate with its disambiguated sense as potentially redundant.  
- Section 4’s organization is confusing about multilingual support; clarify how the system handles the CoNLL 2008 (English) vs CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) distinction.
