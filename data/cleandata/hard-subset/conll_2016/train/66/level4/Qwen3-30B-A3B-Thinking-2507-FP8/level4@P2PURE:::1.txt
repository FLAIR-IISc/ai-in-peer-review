

This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that integrates Henderson et al.'s (2008, 2013) joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing framework with Dyer et al.'s (2015) stack LSTM architecture for syntactic parsing. The combination of these established components yields compelling results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks compared to prior joint systems. I find this paper highly commendable for its clear writing, thorough explanations, strong related work coverage, and insightful results. The methodology is sound, though I note a minor concern regarding Chinese embeddings, suggesting that exceptionally high-quality embeddings may provide more informative features than an exceptionally sophisticated model. The system description is precise, hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is engaging. The sole limitation is the lack of methodological novelty: Henderson et al. established the foundation for semi-synchronous joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM recently, meaning the proposed approach is not a fundamentally new method per se. Nevertheless, I am pleased that this long-awaited parser has been implemented here.
