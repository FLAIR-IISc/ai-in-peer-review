- Presents a joint syntactic+semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs to encode the entire parser state and uses plain greedy decoding.  
- Key innovations: vector representations of the whole parser state and a feature-agnostic, efficient greedy decoder.  
- Empirical: competitive with 2008/2009 baselines on CoNLL 2008/2009 but lags recent SOTA (Table 3).  
- Main concern: greedy decoding undermines comparability with many recent systems that use beam search or structured prediction.  
- Reviewer argues beam search would likely boost performance with minimal computational cost and should be included (even k=5).  
- Evaluation gap: comparisons in Table 3 mix greedy and beam-based systems, weakening claims about performance.  
- Section 2 ambiguity: unclear distinction between A1 and C-A1 links and unclear which transitions are reused vs. modified from Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Section 3.3 critique: concatenating predicate word with its disambiguated sense seems redundant; authors should justify or drop it.  
- Section 4 confusion: organization conflates CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); clarify which languages/results are included and how multilingual training differs from English-only.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-written contribution demonstrating stack LSTMs capture long-range dependencies, but evaluation and presentation ambiguities limit impact.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions — specifically add beam-searched results, clarify task and transition-system details, justify/remove predicate-sense concat, and correct multilingual experiment descriptions.
