- Proposes a Stack LSTM parser that combines Henderson et al.'s joint syntactic-semantic transition framework with Dyer et al.'s Stack LSTM architecture.  
- Achieves strong results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks, showing the effectiveness of the combination.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.  
- Methodology is rigorously presented with thorough hyperparameter justification.  
- Experimental analysis is transparent, insightful, and well-argued.  
- Related work coverage is strong and provides a solid foundation for the contribution.  
- Minor concern: reliance on pre-trained Chinese embeddings—domain-specific lexical resources might offer larger gains than model tweaks.  
- Novelty is limited: no fundamentally new parsing mechanism, since core components originate from prior work.  
- Value lies in a timely, effective synthesis of established techniques rather than architectural innovation.  
- Contribution is significant for providing a unified, efficient parser for joint syntactic-semantic analysis.  
- Results are compelling and argue for the approach’s practical viability; paper merits publication.
