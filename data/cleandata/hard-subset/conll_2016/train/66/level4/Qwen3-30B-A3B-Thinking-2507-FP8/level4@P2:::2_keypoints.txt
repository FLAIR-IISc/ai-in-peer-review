- Transition-based joint syntactic-semantic parser building on Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Innovation: stack LSTM representing the entire parser state to replace hand-crafted features and capture long-range dependencies.  
- Innovation: greedy, linear-time inference for faster parsing than beam-search alternatives.  
- Contribution: competitive multilingual performance without language-specific resources.  
- Strength: stack LSTM state representation outperforms prior joint models on CoNLL 2008 (highest semantic F1 and LAS among joint learners).  
- Strength: greedy parser is fast and practical (~2.5× speedup vs. pipeline SOTA) with open-source code.  
- Strength: robust across languages (top-3 on several non-English CoNLL 2009 datasets; Chinese helped by pretrained embeddings).  
- Weakness: greedy inference prevents direct comparison to beam-search baselines and weakens claims of superiority.  
- Weakness: does not integrate orthogonal SRL improvements (reranking, full-parse syntactic features) and trails SOTA that use them.  
- Weakness: ambiguous task definition—unclear mapping of semantic labels to CoNLL 2009 schema and handling of edge cases.  
- Question: please provide a beam-search baseline (e.g., beam=1) to isolate the effect of greedy inference vs. representation.  
- Question: is lower out-of-domain (Brown) performance due to greedy inference overfitting or lack of syntactic features?  
- Question: why not integrate external syntactic features or reranking within the greedy framework to improve performance?
