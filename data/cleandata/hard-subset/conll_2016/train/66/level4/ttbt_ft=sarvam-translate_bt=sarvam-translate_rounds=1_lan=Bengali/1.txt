This paper presents a stack LSTM parser based on the joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) and the stack LSTM syntactic parsing of Dyer et al. (2015). The use of the previous transition system and the latter stack LSTM shows interesting results compared to the joint systems in the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I really like this paper because it is well-written, well-explained, the related work is good, and the results are very interesting. The method is reliable (with a slight concern about the Chinese embedding, which leads me to believe that very good embedding can be more informative than a very clever model...).

Moreover, the description of the system is clear, the hyperparameters are fair, and the discussion is interesting.

I would just say that the proposed system lacks originality, in the sense that Henderson et al.'s work laid the foundation for semi-synchronized joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parsing a few years ago, and Dyer et al. came up with stack LSTM last year, so it is not a new method.

However, in my opinion, we were waiting to design such a parser, and so I am happy that it has been done here.
