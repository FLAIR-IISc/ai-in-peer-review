This paper presents a stack-LSTM parser that combines the joint syntactic–semantic transition system of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) with the stack-LSTM architecture of Dyer et al. (2015). Leveraging the former’s transition framework and the latter’s neural stack yields strong results compared to prior joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

The paper is well written and clearly explained. The related work is thorough, and the empirical results are compelling. The methodology is sound, with one minor reservation about the Chinese experiments: the improvements appear to depend heavily on the choice of embeddings, suggesting that high-quality embeddings may contribute as much as, or more than, the modeling innovations in that setting. The system is described in sufficient detail, the hyperparameters are well justified, and the discussion is thoughtful.

My main critique concerns originality. The core ideas—semi-synchronized joint syntactic–semantic transition-based parsing (Henderson et al.) and the stack-LSTM mechanism (Dyer et al.)—are established; this work primarily integrates them rather than introducing a new paradigm per se. Nevertheless, such an integration has been overdue, and the paper delivers it convincingly and with care.
