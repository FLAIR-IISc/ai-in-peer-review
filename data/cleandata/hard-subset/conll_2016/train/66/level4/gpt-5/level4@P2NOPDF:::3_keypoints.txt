- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural transition-based models; joint modeling is a major strength.  
- Contribution 1: introduces a new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: shows a neural network succeeds on the joint task where linear models have struggled.  
- Authors credit success to automatic feature learning.  
- Reviewer note: neural models also benefit from parameter sharing, making joint learning more parameter-efficient than linear models.  
- The proposed transition system should work with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016 globally normalized beam-search); orthogonal enhancements could push performance to SOTA.  
- Suggestion: derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system; initial inspection suggests arc-eager–like action exclusions (M-S, M-D, L, R).  
- Overall: clear, well-motivated paper demonstrating promise of neural joint parsing and a path for further improvements.
