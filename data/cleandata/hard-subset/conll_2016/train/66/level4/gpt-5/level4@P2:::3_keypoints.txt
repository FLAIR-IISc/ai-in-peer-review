- Revisits joint syntactic–semantic dependency parsing using a transition-based neural approach in a fast, greedy setting.  
- Contribution 1: Proposes a revised transition system improving on Henderson et al. (2008), empirically more effective than prior joint variants.  
- Contribution 2: Shows neural representations enable joint parsing where linear models struggled, offering automatic feature learning and parameter economy via shared hidden representations.  
- The proposed transition system should work with other training/inference regimes (e.g., globally normalized beam search) and can be improved with orthogonal enhancements (global normalization, reranking, richer pretraining, better encoders).  
- Suggests adding a dynamic oracle (even partial/approximate); M-SHIFT, M-REDUCE, M-LEFT/M-RIGHT behaviors hint an arc-eager–like oracle is feasible and could reduce error propagation.  
- Overall: a timely, valuable paper presenting a simple, fast parser that outperforms previous joint systems and provides a strong platform for further improvements.
