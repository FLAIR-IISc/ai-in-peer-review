- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Main distinctions: uses vector representations for the entire parser state (via stack LSTMs) and employs a straightforward greedy decoding algorithm.  
- Stack LSTMs let the state vector capture large-scope syntactic information important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument path).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); compares to prior systems.  
- Results: competitive with earlier 2008/2009 systems but worse than more recent methods (see bottom of Table 3).  
- Claims advantages of requiring no hand-crafted features and being fast due to greedy decoding.  
- Paper is clearly written and substantial, effectively applying LSTMs to the Henderson et al. algorithm.  
- Major reservation: reliance on a purely greedy algorithm makes comparisons to some cited works problematic; reviewer suggests adding beam-search results.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on why both A1 and C-A1 links are present and what parts of the transition set differ from Henderson et al.  
- Section 3.3 questions concatenating the predicate word with its disambiguated sense as potentially redundant.  
- Section 4’s organization is confusing regarding multilinguality—clarify the CoNLL 2008 (English) vs CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) distinction.
