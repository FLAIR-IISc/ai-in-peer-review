- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing with modern neural techniques, addressing a timely gap where linear models struggled.  
- Two main contributions: a new transition system improving on Henderson (2008) and demonstration that a neural model performs well on joint parsing.  
- Neural gains attributed to automatic feature learning and greater parameter-efficiency via shared neural representations versus linear per-feature/class weights.  
- Strength: clear, relevant problem framing and motivation for reexamining joint parsing under neural architectures.  
- Strength: a well-designed transition system that appears to be a principled improvement over prior work.  
- Strength: empirical evidence that neural transition-based parsing succeeds on the joint task where linear models lag.  
- Strength: transition system seems portable and compatible with other neural parsing frameworks (e.g., global beam-search models).  
- Suggestion: derive or sketch a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system to clarify error-correction behavior and aid training.  
- Suggestion: discuss how the transition system might integrate with other neural decoders (global training, beam search, higher-order scoring) and potential challenges.  
- Suggestion: include an ablation probing which architecture/features drive joint gains to show whether improvements come from syntactic context, semantic regularization, or cross-task sharing.  
- Suggestion: provide a short qualitative error analysis to show where the joint model benefits most (e.g., resolving syntactic ambiguities using semantic cues).  
- Overall: solid, timely contribution with well-motivated transition system and convincing empirical results; likely to improve further with global training and a dynamic oracle—recommend acceptance.
