- Revisits joint syntactic and semantic dependency prediction, applying recent neural modeling insights to transition-based joint parsers.  
- Contribution 1: Proposes a revised transition system that appears to improve over Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates a neural model succeeds on this joint task where linear models struggled, attributing gains to automatic feature induction.  
- Additional advantage noted: neural architectures share representations and are more parameter-efficient than linear models that require weights for every feature/class pair.  
- The proposed transition system should pair well with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 globally normalized beam-search) and can accept many orthogonal enhancements toward state-of-the-art.  
- Recommendation: derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system (even as an appendix or follow-up), likely analogous to the arc-eager oracle.  
- Oracle observations: M-S would rule out semantic arcs between the buffer-front and semantic-stack items; M-D would rule out semantic arcs between the stack-top and remaining buffer; L and R seem to exclude only the reverse arc.
