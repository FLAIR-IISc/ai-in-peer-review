- Proposes a joint syntactic+semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).
- Claims two main novelties: encoding the entire parser state as a vector and using purely greedy search.
- Uses stack LSTMs to capture large-scope syntactic features crucial for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–filler paths).
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); competitive with 2008/2009 systems but worse than more recent approaches.
- Requires no hand-crafted features and is fast due to the simple greedy algorithm.
- Well written and substantial work applying LSTMs to the Henderson et al. algorithm, which is noted as visionary.
- Reviewer concerned that greedy decoding limits comparability to some cited work and suggests adding beam-search results.
- Section 2 needs a brief explanation of why both A1 and C-A1 links appear to clarify the target task.
- Section 2 should summarize differences between this transition set and Henderson et al.’s to clarify reuse vs. innovation.
- Section 3.3: question why predicate word is concatenated with its disambiguated sense, which seems redundant.
- Section 4: organization of Sections 4.1 and 4.2 is confusing with respect to multilinguality (CoNLL 2008 vs CoNLL 2009).
