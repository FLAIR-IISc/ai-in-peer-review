- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).
- Two main differences: represents the entire parser state with vectors (not just top of stack) and uses purely greedy decoding.
- Leverages stack LSTMs to retain long-range syntactic information important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument paths).
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) and compared to prior work.
- Results are competitive with 2008/2009 shared-task systems but lag behind more recent approaches (see Table 3).
- Model uses no hand-crafted features and is fast due to its simple greedy search.
- Paper is clearly written and substantively builds on recent LSTM advances applied to the Henderson framework.
- Main reservation: purely greedy algorithm limits comparability; reporting beam-search results (even small beams) is recommended.

- Section 2: summarize how the transition inventory differs from Henderson et al. (2008) to show what is reused versus new.
- Section 3.3: explain why predicate form is concatenated with its disambiguated sense, since senses are predicate-specific and this seems redundant.
