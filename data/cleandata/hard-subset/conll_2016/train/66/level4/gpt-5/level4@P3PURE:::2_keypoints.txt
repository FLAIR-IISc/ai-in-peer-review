- Proposes a joint syntactic–semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Claims two main differences from prior work: vector representations for the entire parser state and use of plain greedy search.  
- Key idea: leverage stack LSTMs so the parser state encodes large-scope syntactic features crucial for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–filler path).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL-2008 (English) and CoNLL-2009 (multilingual) datasets with comparisons to previously reported systems.  
- Performance: competitive with original 2008/2009 systems but lags behind more recent proposals.  
- Advantages: requires no hand-crafted features and is fast due to greedy decoding; paper is well written and substantial.  
- Major concern: reliance on a purely greedy algorithm reduces comparability to other work; reviewer suggests reporting beam-search results.  
- Section 2 needs a brief explanation of simultaneous A1 and C-A1 links to clarify the target task.  
- Section 2 also needs a concise summary of differences between the paper’s transition set and Henderson et al.’s to show what is reused vs. novel.  
- Section 3.3 question: why concatenate the predicate word with its disambiguated sense, which seems redundant.  
- Section 4 organization is confusing about multilinguality; clarify that CoNLL-2008 is English while CoNLL-2009 covers multiple languages.
