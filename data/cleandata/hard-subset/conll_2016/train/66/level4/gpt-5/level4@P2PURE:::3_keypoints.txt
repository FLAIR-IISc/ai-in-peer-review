- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.
- Joint models remain a promising aspect of transition-based neural parsers’ success.
- Contribution 1: introduces a new transition system that appears to improve on Henderson (2008).
- Contribution 2: shows a neural network performs well on joint parsing where linear models struggled.
- Success attributed to automatic feature learning by networks.
- Additional advantage: networks are more parameter-efficient than linear models because they share representations across tasks.
- The new transition system should work with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search) and could lead to state-of-the-art with orthogonal enhancements.
- Recommendation: derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system (possibly in an appendix or future work).
- Initial dynamic-oracle observations: M-S seems to exclude semantic arcs between buffer front and semantic stack; M-D excludes semantic arcs between stack top and buffer; L and R exclude only the reverse arc.
