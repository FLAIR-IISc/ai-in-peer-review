- Presents a greedy, transition-based joint syntax–semantics parser using four stack LSTMs to encode full algorithmic state, eliminating hand-crafted features and achieving linear-time inference with strong CoNLL 2008–2009 English results.  
- Contribution: a fast, feature-light joint parser with extended transitions (M-SELF) and integrated predicate sense disambiguation.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that full-state neural representations let joint parsing match leading pipelines and yield substantial runtime gains.  
- Contribution: ablations (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) quantify syntax’s effect on SRL and in- vs out-of-domain behavior.  
- Strength: clear, reproducible methodology with detailed formulation, hyperparameters, and implementation notes.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and convincing comparisons/ablations showing benefits of joint modeling in-domain.  
- Strength: high efficiency and practicality—linear-time greedy parser with notable runtime speedups over strong pipelines.  
- Strength: thoughtful design choices for joint parsing (interleaved transitions, action history, M-SELF).  
- Strength: multilingual experiments indicate the approach generalizes beyond English.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—main ideas are combinations of prior work rather than new learning/inference methods.  
- Weakness: lags behind state-of-the-art SRL systems that use full syntax and global/structured inference.  
- Weakness: joint model’s gains diminish or reverse out-of-domain, suggesting possible overfitting and limited generalization analysis.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on pretrained embeddings with limited analysis of their contribution, especially for multilingual results.  
- Weakness: incomplete oracle conversion for some semantic graphs (crossing arcs) and limited error analysis quantifying its impact.  
- Question: How much do M-SELF and the copy-vs-move arc-eager modification contribute—please provide ablations?  
- Question: Did you try small beams or exploration (dynamic oracles) to approach globally optimized SRL while keeping speed—any trends?  
- Question: Can you quantify pretrained embeddings’ contribution on English (in-/out-of-domain) and each non-English language?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to hyperparameters (action-history size, composition dims, dropout) and are there training stability issues?  
- Question: Please provide error analysis showing where joint modeling helps (e.g., long-distance arguments) and where it harms versus the hybrid.  
- Comment: An ablation for the default “01” sense fallback would clarify its impact on SRL performance.  
- Comment: Character-level or morphological features could reduce reliance on embeddings and help morphologically rich languages—sensible future work.  
- Comment: Quantify how often oracle mismatches for semantics occur per language and their measurable impact on training/evaluation.  
- Overall: Well-executed, clearly written paper that delivers a fast, effective joint parser with modest novelty but strong empirical/speed contributions—recommend accept.
