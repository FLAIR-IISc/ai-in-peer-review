- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.  
- Introduces a new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008).  
- Demonstrates a neural model succeeds on this joint task where linear models previously struggled.  
- Attributes neural success to automatic feature learning and greater parameter-efficiency in joint modeling.  
- Transition system likely works with other architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search) and can incorporate orthogonal improvements, potentially reaching state-of-the-art.  
- Recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system; initial analysis suggests oracle behavior similar to arc-eager with specific exclusions for M-S, M-D, L, and R actions.
