- Paper proposes a joint syntactic+semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs, inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).
- Authors claim two main differences from prior work: vector representations for the entire parser state and a purely greedy search algorithm.
- Key idea: stack LSTMs capture long-range syntactic information important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument paths).
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); results are competitive with older 2008/2009 systems but worse than more recent approaches.
- Strengths: no hand-crafted features and fast inference due to greedy decoding.
- Major concern: relying solely on greedy search limits comparability to systems using search; reporting beam-search results would strengthen claims.
- Request to clarify handling of continuation roles (presence of both A1 and C-A1) and precise target task definition.
- Ask for a concise summary of differences between the paper’s transition set and Henderson et al. (2008) to delineate reused vs. novel components.
- Question why predicate representations concatenate word and disambiguated sense, which seems redundant.
- Section organization is confusing about multilingual vs. English settings; ask to clearly separate datasets, preprocessing, and evaluation protocols for CoNLL 2008 vs. 2009.
