This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser building on Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) for joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) for stack LSTM syntactic parsing. Combining the transition system from the former with the stack LSTM from the latter yields interesting results compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I like this paper a lot: it is well written and clearly explained; the related work is solid; and the results are very interesting. The methodology is sound, with a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings, which leads me to believe that very strong embeddings can be more informative than a very clever model. Moreover, the system description is clear, the hyperparameters are justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My only reservation is originality. The work of Henderson et al. laid the basis for semi-synchronised joint syntax–semantic transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM last year, so this is not a new method per se. That said, I believe the community has been waiting for such a parser to be designed, and I am glad to see it realized here.
