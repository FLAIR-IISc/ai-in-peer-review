This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser building on Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) for joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) for stack LSTM syntactic parsing. It combines the transition system from the former with the stack LSTM from the latter, yielding interesting results compared to prior joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I like this paper a lot: it is well written and clearly explained, the related work is strong, and the results are very interesting. The methodology is sound (with a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings, which leads me to believe that very good embeddings can be more informative than a very clever model). The system description is clear, the hyperparameters are justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My only criticism is that the proposed system lacks originality: Henderson et al. laid the foundation for semi-synchronized joint syntax-semantics transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM last year, so it is not a new method per se. That said, I think the community has been waiting for such a parser to be built, and I am glad to see it realized here.
