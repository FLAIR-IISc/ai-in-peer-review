- Paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that combines Henderson et al.'s joint syntactic/semantic transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM, applied to joint parsing.
- Achieves interesting/competitive results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks compared to prior joint systems.
- Paper is well written, clearly explained, and places the work in strong related-work context.
- Methodology is sound; system description is clear and hyperparameters are justified.
- Minor concern that high-quality Chinese embeddings may contribute more to performance than model innovations.
- Main criticism: the approach lacks originality because it directly combines prior methods (Henderson et al. and Dyer et al.).
- Nevertheless, the implementation is timely and valuable because the community has been waiting for such a parser.
