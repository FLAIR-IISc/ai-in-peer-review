- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent neural models, noting joint models are a promising outcome of transition-based neural parsers.
- Two main contributions: a new transition system that appears superior to Henderson (2008), and demonstration that neural networks handle the joint task well where linear models struggled.
- Neural success attributed to automatic feature learning and shared representations, which are more parameter-efficient than separate feature–class weights in linear models.
- The proposed transition system would likely perform well with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016’s globally normalized beam-search).
- Many orthogonal improvements are possible; extensions of this approach could reach state-of-the-art results.
- Recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system (could be provided in appendix or follow-up work); notes the system seems analogous to the arc-eager oracle and describes how M-S, M-D, L, and R actions constrain semantic arcs.
