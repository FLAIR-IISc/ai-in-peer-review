This paper presents a Stack-LSTM parser that builds on Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) for joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) for stack-LSTM syntactic parsing. It adopts the transition system from the former and the stack-LSTM architecture from the latter, achieving strong results relative to prior joint systems on the CoNLL-2008 and CoNLL-2009 shared tasks.

I like this paper a lot. It is clearly written, well explained, and grounded in solid related work, and the results are compelling. The methodology appears sound, with one minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings: the improvements suggest that very strong embeddings can sometimes be more informative than architectural innovation. That said, the system description is clear, the hyperparameters are well justified, and the discussion is thoughtful and informative.

My main reservation is about originality. Henderson et al. laid the foundation for semi-synchronized joint syntax–semantics transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM last year. In that sense, this is not a novel method per se, but rather a careful and effective integration of existing ideas. Nevertheless, I believe the community has been waiting for precisely this combination to be implemented and evaluated, and I am glad to see it realized here.
