- Presents a joint syntactic–semantic dependency parser combining Henderson et al.'s synchronized transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM, using greedy inference.
- Primary contribution: empirical demonstration that this combination yields strong performance on CoNLL 2008 and 2009.
- Secondary contribution: clear, complete system description and experimental details that aid reproducibility.
- Strength: paper is well written, organized, and has thorough related work.
- Strength: methodologically sound integration of transition system and stack LSTMs with standard training/evaluation.
- Strength: transparent model description showing how full parser state is represented and used.
- Strength: hyperparameters are reported and justified, supporting reproducibility.
- Strength: strong empirical results suggest learned full-state representations can replace expert-crafted features.
- Weakness: limited originality—core ideas are direct combinations of prior work.
- Weakness: reliance on high-quality pretrained embeddings (notably for Chinese) may drive improvements more than the model itself.
- Question: please quantify the impact of pretrained embeddings (e.g., ablations with/without embeddings) across languages.
- Question: clarify the key methodological or technical insight beyond combining existing components to address originality concerns.
