- Introduces a joint transition-based parser for syntax and semantics building on Henderson et al. (2008).
- Two primary differences: encode entire parser state as a vector (not just top-of-stack) and use purely greedy inference.
- Uses stack LSTMs to retain broad contextual information approximating wide-scope syntactic cues important for SRL (e.g., predicate–argument head path).
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.
- Strong performance relative to contemporaneous 2008/2009 systems but trails more recent approaches (see Table 3).
- Emphasizes avoidance of hand-engineered features and efficiency due to greedy search.
- Paper is clear and presents a substantial application of LSTM techniques to the Henderson et al. algorithm.
- Main concern: exclusive use of greedy search limits comparability; adding beam-search results would strengthen the empirical story.
- Ask to clarify why A1 and C-A1 relations can coexist in the target representation.
- Request a concise summary of how the transition set differs from Henderson et al. to separate reused vs novel components.
- Question the necessity of concatenating the word with its disambiguated sense—please justify this design choice.
- Suggest clarifying the delineation between Sections 4.1 and 4.2 regarding CoNLL 2008 (English) versus CoNLL 2009 (multilingual).
