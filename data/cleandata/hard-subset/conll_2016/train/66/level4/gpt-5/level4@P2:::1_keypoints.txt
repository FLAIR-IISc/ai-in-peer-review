- Proposes a Stack-LSTM–based transition parser for joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing.
- Integrates Henderson et al.'s synchronized transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM and yields a greedy, linear-time parser.
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.
- Main contribution: learning full parser-state representations without hand-crafted features.
- Secondary contribution: clear, reproducible system description and training setup (hyperparameters, runtime).
- Methodology is technically sound with justified choices for training, constraints, and action interleaving.
- Empirical results are competitive or superior among joint models on English CoNLL 2008/2009.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—primarily a compelling combination of existing components.
- Weakness: Chinese results appear to rely heavily on high-quality pretrained embeddings, questioning gains from the model itself.
- Overall: well-executed, clear, efficient, and practically valuable joint parser despite limited novelty.
