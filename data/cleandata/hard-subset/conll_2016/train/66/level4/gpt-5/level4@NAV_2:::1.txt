This paper introduces a Stack LSTM parser that jointly predicts syntactic and semantic dependencies, building on Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) for the transition system and Dyer et al. (2015) for stack LSTMs. Combining the former’s synchronized joint parsing with the latter’s representation learning yields compelling results relative to prior joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I found the paper very strong: it is clearly written, well motivated, with thorough related work and notably interesting results. The methodology appears solid (with a minor caveat regarding the Chinese embeddings, which suggests that high-quality embeddings may sometimes contribute more than architectural sophistication). The system description is easy to follow, hyperparameter choices are justified, and the discussion is thoughtful.

My main reservation concerns novelty: the foundations for semi-synchronized joint syntax–semantics transition-based parsing were laid by Henderson et al., and the stack LSTM architecture was introduced by Dyer et al., so the method itself is not brand new. That said, a parser unifying these ideas has been overdue, and I am pleased to see it realized effectively here.
