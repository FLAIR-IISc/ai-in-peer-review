This paper introduces a stack LSTM parser that builds on Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) for joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) for stack LSTM syntactic parsing. Using the transition system from the former together with the stack LSTM from the latter yields promising results compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I like this paper a lot because it is clearly written and well-explained; the related work is thorough and the results are very compelling. The methodology is sound (with a minor concern about the Chinese embeddings, which suggests that very strong embeddings can sometimes be more informative than a very sophisticated model). Moreover, the system description is clear, the hyperparameters are well justified, and the discussion is engaging.

The only caveat is that the proposed system is not especially original: Henderson et al. laid the groundwork for semi-synchronised joint syntax–semantic transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM last year, so this is not a new method per se. That said, I believe the community has been waiting for such a parser to be built, and I am glad to see it realized here.
