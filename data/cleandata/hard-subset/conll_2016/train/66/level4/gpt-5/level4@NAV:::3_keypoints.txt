- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural models.  
- Joint modeling is a compelling strength of transition-based neural parsers.  
- Contribution 1: a new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: a neural network model that succeeds where linear models previously struggled.  
- Authors credit automatic feature learning; reviewer adds that neural nets share representations and are more parameter-efficient for joint tasks.  
- The new transition system would likely work well with other architectures (e.g., Andor 2016) and can accept many orthogonal enhancements toward state-of-the-art.  
- Reviewer suggests deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system (as appendix or future work).  
- Notes on oracle similarity: the system resembles arc-eager; M-S and M-D rule out multiple semantic arcs between buffer/stack items, while L and R only rule out the reverse-direction arc.
