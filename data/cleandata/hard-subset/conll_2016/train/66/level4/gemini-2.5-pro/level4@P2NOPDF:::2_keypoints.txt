- Presents a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).
- Claims two main contributions: vector representations for the entire parser state and a simple greedy search decoder.
- Uses Stack LSTMs to encode whole-parser state, capturing long-distance syntactic features important for SRL.
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); competitive with 2008/2009-era systems but below recent SOTA.
- Strengths: no hand-crafted features and computational efficiency from greedy decoding.
- Main reservation: greedy decoding limits comparability to other systems using beam search; recommends reporting beam search results.
- Section 2 query: asks authors to explain presence of both A1 and C-A1 links in the data to clarify the target task.
- Section 2 request: asks for an explicit summary of which transitions are reused, modified, or new versus Henderson et al. (2008).
- Section 3.3 question: questions the motivation for concatenating predicate word embeddings with disambiguated sense embeddings as seemingly redundant.
- Section 4 comment: organization is confusing about multilingual evaluation; requests clearer distinction that CoNLL 2008 is English-only and CoNLL 2009 is multilingual and suggests reordering results.
