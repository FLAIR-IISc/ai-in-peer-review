- Proposes a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependency graphs by adapting Henderson et al.’s synchronized transition system and using stack LSTMs.  
- Learns dense representations of the full parser state (stacks, buffer, action history) enabling greedy, linear-time inference without hand-engineered features.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English benchmarks and achieves strong performance, establishing a new state-of-the-art among joint parsing models.  
- First successful extension of the stack LSTM framework from syntactic parsing to joint syntactic–semantic parsing, demonstrating the architecture’s flexibility.  
- Empirically strong results obtained with a simple greedy algorithm and no manual features, highlighting effective state representation learning.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and thorough, with comprehensive related work, detailed system description, and insightful result discussion.  
- Methodology is sound: rigorous evaluation, ablation studies (Syntax-only, Semantics-only, Hybrid), and justified hyperparameters that aid reproducibility.  
- Honest analysis acknowledging the model’s shortcomings compared to pipelined systems using global features or extra resources.  
- Main weakness is limited methodological novelty—essentially a straightforward combination of prior transition systems and stack LSTMs rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Question for authors: clarify sensitivity to pretrained word embeddings (notably Chinese)—quantify how much gains come from embeddings versus the model architecture.
