- Revisits joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing with modern transition-based neural networks; timely and relevant.  
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system that improves on Henderson et al. (2008), and a neural model that achieves strong performance where prior linear models struggled.  
- Neural network success is attributed to automatic learning of feature representations.  
- Additional advantage (suggested): neural nets offer parameter efficiency in joint learning via shared compact representations, avoiding combinatorial weight explosion of linear models.  
- The proposed transition system is well-designed and flexible, likely integrable with other neural parsing architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016).  
- Future extensions and orthogonal improvements could push this approach toward state-of-the-art results.  
- Recommendation: derive a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system to strengthen theoretical foundations.  
- Informal analysis: M-S and M-D actions appear to preclude specific semantic/syntactic arcs, and L/R actions exclude reverse arcs—formal dynamic-oracle analysis is needed.
