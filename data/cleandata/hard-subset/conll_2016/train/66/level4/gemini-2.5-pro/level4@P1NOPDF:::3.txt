This paper revisits the important and overdue problem of joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing, applying recent advances from neural network models. The development of such joint models is one of the most promising directions enabled by the recent success of transition-based neural parsers.

The paper makes two main contributions. First, the authors present a new transition system that appears to improve upon the Henderson (2008) system on which it is based. Second, they demonstrate that a neural network model can succeed at this task, where previous linear models have struggled. The authors attribute this success to the neural network's ability to learn feature representations automatically. However, I believe there is another advantage to the neural network approach that is worth mentioning. In a linear model, a weight must be learned for each feature-class pair. Consequently, when jointly modeling two tasks, the parameter space grows considerably. Neural networks are much more parameter-efficient in this respect, as they can learn shared representations for both tasks.

The proposed transition system seems robust, and I expect it could be successfully integrated with other neural network architectures, such as the global beam-search model of Andor (2016). There are many other orthogonal improvements that could be made, and given the promising results, I expect that extensions to this method could achieve state-of-the-art performance.

The paper could be further strengthened by an attempt to derive a dynamic oracle for this transition system, perhaps in an appendix or as a direction for future work. At first glance, the oracle appears to have similarities to the standard arc-eager oracle. For instance, the M-S action seems to preclude all semantic arcs between the word at the front of the buffer and the words on the semantic stack, while the M-D action precludes all semantic arcs between the word at the top of the stack and the words remaining in the buffer. The L and R actions, in turn, each appear to exclude only the reverse arc.
