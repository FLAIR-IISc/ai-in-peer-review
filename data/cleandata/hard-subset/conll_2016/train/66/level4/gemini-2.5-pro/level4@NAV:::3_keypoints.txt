- Timely re-examination of joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent neural-network advances.  
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system (improvement over Henderson 2008) and demonstrating neural-network success on a task where linear models struggled.  
- Authors attribute success to neural automatic feature extraction.  
- Reviewer notes an additional advantage: neural models are more parameter-efficient than linear models that require per-feature-class weights, which is important for joint learning.  
- Proposed transition system should be compatible with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Many complementary improvements could be integrated, so future extensions may achieve state-of-the-art results.  
- Suggestion to formulate a dynamic oracle for the transition system (appendix or future work).  
- Preliminary dynamic-oracle analysis: M-S precludes semantic arcs between buffer head and words on semantic stack; M-D precludes arcs between stack top and buffer words; L and R exclude only the reverse arc.
