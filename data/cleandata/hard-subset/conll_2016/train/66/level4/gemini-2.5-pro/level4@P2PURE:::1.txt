This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that combines the joint syntactic-semantic transition system of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) with the stack LSTM architecture of Dyer et al. (2015). By integrating the transition system from the former with the neural architecture from the latter, the authors achieve compelling results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

This is a strong, well-executed paper. The submission is well-written, the methodology is sound, and the system is explained clearly. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, justify their hyperparameter choices, and offer an insightful discussion of the results. A minor concern relates to the Chinese word embeddings; their significant impact on performance suggests that high-quality embeddings may be as influential, or even more so, than the model's architectural innovations.

The primary weakness of the work is its limited novelty, as the proposed system is a direct combination of two existing lines of research. The foundational semi-synchronized, joint parsing framework was introduced by Henderson et al. several years ago, while the stack LSTM was proposed by Dyer et al. more recently. However, this is a minor criticism. The development of such a parser is a logical and important step for the field, and a contribution that many have been anticipating. This paper successfully implements this combination and delivers strong empirical results.
