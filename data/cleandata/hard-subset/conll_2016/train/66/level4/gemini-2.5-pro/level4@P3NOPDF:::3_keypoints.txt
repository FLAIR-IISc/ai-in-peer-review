- Timely revisitation of joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing using modern neural methods.  
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system and evidence that a neural model succeeds where linear models struggled.  
- Neural advantage attributed to automatic feature learning; additionally, neural networks provide compact shared representations that avoid parameter explosion in joint tasks.  
- Proposed transition system is general and modular, likely compatible with other neural parsing architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016).  
- Recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system; preliminary analysis outlines how M-S, M-D, and syntactic L/R actions restrict semantic/syntactic arcs.  
- Developing a dynamic oracle could reduce error propagation and further improve parser performance.  
- Overall verdict: strong paper addressing an important problem with a well-designed, effective method and promising avenues for future work.
