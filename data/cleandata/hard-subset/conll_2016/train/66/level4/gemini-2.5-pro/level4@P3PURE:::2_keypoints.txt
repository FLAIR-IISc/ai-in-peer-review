- Paper presents a joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs, inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).
- Two main differences from Henderson et al.: vectorial representations for the entire parser state and a simple greedy search algorithm.
- Stack LSTMs enable long-range syntactic feature memory important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–role filler paths).
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) datasets with comparisons to prior work.
- System performs well relative to 2008/2009 shared-task systems but is less competitive with more recent approaches.
- Strengths: no hand-crafted features and fast inference due to greedy search.
- Reviewer praises clear writing and substantial work, noting Henderson et al. was visionary.
- Major reservation: use of a simple greedy algorithm complicates fair comparison; beam search results would strengthen the paper.
- Request clarification/explanation for presence of both A1 and C-A1 links.
- Ask for a clear summary of differences between this transition set and Henderson et al. (2008).
- Question why predicate representation concatenates the word with its disambiguated sense, which may be redundant.
- Suggest reorganizing Sections 4.1/4.2 to clearly distinguish CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) setups and results.
