This paper presents a transition-based parser that integrates the joint syntactic/semantic transition system of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) with the stack LSTM architecture of Dyer et al. (2015). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks, achieving interesting results in comparison to previous joint parsing systems.

This is an excellent paper: it is well-written and well-explained, with a thorough review of related work and compelling results. The methodology is sound, the system description is clear, the hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is insightful. A minor concern relates to the Chinese embeddings; their high quality may suggest that superior embeddings can be more informative than a more sophisticated model architecture.

The primary weakness of the paper is its limited originality. The proposed system is a direct combination of two established lines of research: the semi-synchronized joint parsing framework from Henderson et al. and the stack LSTM model from Dyer et al. As such, it does not introduce a fundamentally new method. However, this is a logical and important synthesis. An effective implementation of this combined approach is a welcome contribution, and one that the community has been anticipating. The authors have done a very good job designing and evaluating such a system.
