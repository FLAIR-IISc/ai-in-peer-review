- Proposes a Stack LSTM model for joint syntactic and semantic parsing by combining Henderson et al.'s semi-synchronized transition framework with Dyer et al.'s Stack LSTM.
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 and 2009, achieving strong results relative to previous joint parsing systems.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explained, with a thorough related-work review.
- Methodology is sound, system description detailed, and hyperparameter choices are well-justified, aiding reproducibility.
- Discussion of results is insightful and execution produces significant empirical findings.
- Primary weakness: limited conceptual novelty—an incremental integration of existing approaches rather than a new paradigm.
- Nonetheless, the integration is non-trivial engineering that validates Stack LSTM for complex joint parsing and sets a strong benchmark.
- Minor concern: Chinese results depend heavily on high-quality pre-trained embeddings, highlighting the influence of input representations over model architecture.
