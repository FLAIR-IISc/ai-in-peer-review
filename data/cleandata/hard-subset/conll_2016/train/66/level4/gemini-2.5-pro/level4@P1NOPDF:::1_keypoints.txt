- Presents a Stack LSTM parser that jointly handles syntactic and semantic dependency parsing by combining Henderson et al.'s semi-synchronous transition system with Dyer et al.'s Stack LSTM architecture.
- Applies this neural architecture to joint parsing and achieves strong, competitive results on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and methodologically thorough.
- Provides a comprehensive related-work review that properly situates the contribution.
- System description is detailed, hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion offers useful insights.
- Minor concern: Chinese results suggest word embedding quality may sometimes matter more than model architecture.
- Main weakness: limited novelty, as the approach is an effective but straightforward integration of existing methods.
- Nonetheless, the integration is logical, timely, well-implemented, and represents a valuable contribution to the field.
