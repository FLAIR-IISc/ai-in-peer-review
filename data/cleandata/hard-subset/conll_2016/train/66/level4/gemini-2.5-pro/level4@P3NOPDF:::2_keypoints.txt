- Proposes a transition-based joint syntactic parser and SRL using stack LSTMs and a greedy decoder.  
- Two main innovations: full parser-state vectorization via stack LSTMs and adoption of a simple greedy search.  
- Argues stack LSTMs capture long-range syntactic features (e.g., predicate-argument paths) crucial for SRL.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL-2008 (English) and CoNLL-2009 (multilingual); competitive with older systems but behind recent feature-rich or beam-based methods.  
- Strength: well-written, substantial work that modernizes Henderson et al. (2008) using contemporary neural techniques.  
- Strength: no hand-crafted features, improving generality across languages.  
- Strength: greedy decoding yields a fast, computationally efficient system.  
- Weakness: exclusive reliance on greedy search likely limits performance and makes fair comparison to beam-based state-of-the-art difficult.  
- Weakness: omission of even small-beam experiments weakens claims about the model's full potential.  
- Comment: paper should briefly explain core vs continuation SRL arguments for less familiar readers.  
- Comment: needs an explicit summary differentiating the transition set from Henderson et al. (2008) — which transitions are reused, modified, or new.  
- Question: why concatenate predicate and disambiguated-sense embeddings given apparent redundancy; request justification.  
- Comment: reorganize Sections 4.1/4.2 to clearly distinguish CoNLL-2008 (English) from CoNLL-2009 (multilingual) experiments.
