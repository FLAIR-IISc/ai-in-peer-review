- Primary contribution: applies stack LSTMs to learn a full parser-state representation for joint syntactic and semantic parsing, replacing hand-crafted features.  
- Secondary contribution: shows a fast, greedy, feature-free parser can outperform previous joint models on CoNLL benchmarks.

- Strength: elegant, modern modeling that extends neural stack-LSTM parsing to joint syntax–semantics and removes manual feature engineering.  
- Strength: achieves new state-of-the-art among joint parsing models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English data.  
- Strength: simple and efficient—greedy linear-time inference avoids expensive feature extraction and is practical for real use.

- Weakness: exclusive reliance on greedy inference limits fair comparison to systems using beam/global search and may understate representational capacity.  
- Weakness: unclear delineation of algorithmic novelty versus Henderson et al. (2013); needs explicit comparison of transition differences.  
- Weakness: still lags behind pipelined state-of-the-art systems, raising questions about lost syntactic information without full parses.

- Question: please clarify core vs. continuation semantic role distinctions (e.g., A1 vs. C-A1) for non-expert readers.  
- Question: motivate the composition gd(v,p) of predicate word and sense—could using only the sense embedding p suffice?  
- Question: reorganize experimental sections for clarity—group English experiments and present multilingual setup/results more coherently.
