- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.  
- Joint models are a promising application of transition-based neural parsers.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces a new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: Shows a neural network can successfully solve the joint task where linear models struggled.  
- Neural success attributed to learned feature representations and greater parameter efficiency vs. linear models (avoids separate weights per feature-class pair).  
- The proposed transition system is likely model-agnostic and could integrate with other architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016 global beam-search).  
- Many orthogonal improvements are possible, so extensions could reach state-of-the-art performance.  
- Future work: derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system (could be included in an appendix or follow-up).  
- Observational note: M-S and M-D actions seem to preclude certain semantic arcs and L/R actions exclude reverse arcs, resembling an arc-eager oracle.
