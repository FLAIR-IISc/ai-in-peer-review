- Demonstrates stack LSTMs can model the full parser state for joint syntactic-semantic parsing, yielding strong empirical results.  
- Proposes a fast, greedy, linear-time transition-based parser that requires no hand-crafted features and sets a new joint-model baseline.  
- Timely and significant application of modern neural methods (stack LSTMs) to a challenging joint parsing task.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance among joint parsing models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English benchmarks.  
- Efficient, simple, feature-free design suitable for practical use due to greedy linear-time inference.  
- Provides a solid architecture that can be extended (e.g., beam search, better embeddings) to potentially close the gap with pipeline systems.  
- Presents insightful ablation experiments (Joint vs Syntax-only vs Semantics-only vs Hybrid) that clarify benefits of joint learning.  
- Limited algorithmic novelty: transition system is largely an extension of prior work, with main novelty in using stack LSTMs and an M-SELF action.  
- Does not match state-of-the-art pipeline systems that use global features, optimal decoding, and external resources.  
- Missing discussion of the oracle used to generate gold transition sequences; oracle design is important for interleaved syntactic/semantic actions.  
- Asks authors to clarify oracle conditions (e.g., whether M-SHIFT or M-REDUCE forbid future semantic arcs) and whether a static or dynamic oracle was used.
