- Tackles joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent transition-based neural network models.  
- Two main contributions: a new transition system (improvement over Henderson 2008) and showing neural models succeed where linear models struggled.  
- Success attributed to the neural network’s ability to automatically learn feature representations.  
- Neural networks are more parameter-efficient than linear models when learning two tasks jointly.  
- The proposed transition system is strong and likely to integrate well with other NN architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016 global beam-search).  
- The approach enables many orthogonal improvements and could reach state-of-the-art with future extensions.  
- Suggested future work: derive and provide a dynamic oracle for the new transition system (appendix or follow-up).  
- Initial oracle observations: M-S excludes semantic arcs between buffer front and semantic stack; M-D excludes semantic arcs between stack top and buffer; L and R exclude only the reverse arc.
