- Greedy, transition-based parser jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependency graphs using a synchronized transition system and stack LSTMs.  
- Learns representations of entire parser state, removing need for hand-crafted features.  
- Linear-time, greedy inference yields a fast parser that achieves state-of-the-art results among joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English.  
- Demonstrates that stack LSTM representations can supplant engineered features for complex joint syntactic-semantic parsing.  
- Produces a high-performance, efficient joint parser useful for practical downstream applications.  
- Provides a thorough experimental evaluation with well-designed ablation studies (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid).  
- Exceptionally well-written and methodologically sound presentation situating work in related research.  
- High reproducibility and impact potential, with authors committing to open-source release.  
- Limited methodological novelty: combines existing transition system (Henderson et al.) and stack LSTM architecture (Dyer et al.).  
- Does not surpass the best pipeline systems that leverage full syntactic parses for semantic role labeling.  
- Strong multilingual gains (e.g., Chinese) appear to rely heavily on high-quality pretrained embeddings, raising robustness questions.  
- Request: provide Chinese results without pretrained embeddings to quantify their contribution.  
- Request: analyze the impact of the novel M-SELF transition (accuracy on self-dependencies and effect on overall semantic F1 if removed).
