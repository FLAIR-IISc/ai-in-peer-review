- Introduces a transition-based dependency parser for joint syntactic and semantic analysis inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Two primary departures from Henderson: vectorial representations for the complete parser state, and a simple greedy search algorithm.  
- Core contribution is using stack LSTMs to let the parser state retain broad-scoped syntactic features important for semantic role labeling.  
- Evaluated on English CoNLL 2008 and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Performance is competitive with 2008/2009-era systems but not with more recent state-of-the-art; advantages include no hand-crafted features and greedy search efficiency.  
- Paper is clearly written and modernizes Henderson’s approach with LSTM architecture.  
- Main reservation: reliance on a simple greedy algorithm makes comparisons to systems using more complex search methods difficult; beam search results should be reported.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on the distinction and concurrent presence of A1 and C-A1 links for task clarity.  
- Section 2 should include a summary contrasting the transition set used here with Henderson et al. to identify adopted versus novel/modified transitions.  
- Section 3.3 asks for the rationale behind concatenating predicate word representations with their disambiguated senses, which appears redundant.  
- Sections 4.1–4.2 are confusing about multilingual experiments; the paper should more clearly separate discussion of CoNLL 2008 (English-only) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual).
