- Presents a greedy, transition-based parser that jointly models syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs to represent parser state.  
- Integrates Henderson et al.'s joint transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM architecture, avoiding hand-crafted features.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English benchmarks.  
- First successful application of stack LSTMs to joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing, showing a learned holistic parser-state representation is viable.  
- Achieves strong empirical results with linear-time, greedy inference that is significantly faster than other top systems.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and sufficiently detailed to facilitate reproducibility of the model and training.  
- Evaluation is thorough with insightful ablation studies and multilingual experiments, and authors honestly discuss limitations like out-of-domain drops.  
- Main methodological novelty is limited: the work is an effective combination of existing techniques rather than a fundamentally new parsing method.  
- Unclear how much performance gains come from pretrained embeddings versus the stack LSTM architecture, especially for Chinese.  
- Requests more detail on the performance gap with/without pretrained embeddings and suggests a simpler baseline using the same embeddings with a feed-forward classifier to isolate architectural contributions.
