- Presents a transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs.  
- Builds on Henderson et al.'s semi-synchronous joint transition system and Dyer et al.'s Stack LSTM syntactic parser.  
- Main contribution is adapting Stack LSTM architecture to joint syntax–semantic parsing.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 datasets, achieving strong, competitive results.  
- Paper is clear, thorough, and well-written with a lucid system description.  
- Methodology and hyperparameter choices are sound and well-justified.  
- Related work discussion is comprehensive and situates the contribution effectively.  
- Empirical analysis is insightful and supports the system's performance claims.  
- Contribution is largely an effective integration of existing techniques rather than a fundamentally new method.  
- Minor concern: the role of pre-trained word embeddings (especially for Chinese) may drive much of the performance and warrants brief discussion.  
- Overall: solid engineering and empirical work that produces a timely, state-of-the-art joint parser despite incremental conceptual novelty.
