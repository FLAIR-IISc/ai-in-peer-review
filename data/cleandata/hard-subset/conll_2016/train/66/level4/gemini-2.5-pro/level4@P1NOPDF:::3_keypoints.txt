- Revisits the important problem of joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent neural-network advances.
- Two main contributions: a new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008), and a neural model that succeeds where prior linear models struggled.
- Authors credit success to learned feature representations; reviewer adds neural nets also offer parameter efficiency via shared representations across tasks.
- Proposed transition system appears robust and likely compatible with other architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).
- Many orthogonal improvements are possible; extensions could achieve state-of-the-art performance.
- Paper would be strengthened by deriving a dynamic oracle (suggested for an appendix or future work).
- Initial oracle observations: M-S seems to preclude semantic arcs between the buffer front and semantic stack; M-D precludes arcs between stack top and buffer; L and R each exclude only the reverse arc.
