- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser using stack LSTMs, inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Two main departures from Henderson: vector representations for the entire parser state and a simple greedy search algorithm.  
- Leverages stack LSTMs to encode long-range syntactic features (e.g., syntactic path between predicate and candidate argument) important for SRL.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); competitive with 2008/2009 systems but below more recent models.  
- Emphasizes speed and lack of hand-crafted features due to the greedy algorithm.  
- Main reservation: using greedy search hinders direct comparison with prior work that uses stronger search; adding beam-search results would strengthen claims.  
- Request clarification on the presence and role of both A1 and C-A1 links to clarify the target task.  
- Ask for a summary of differences between this transition set and Henderson et al. (2008) to show what is reused vs. new/modified.  
- Question why predicate representations concatenate word and disambiguated sense, since sense is specific to the predicate.  
- Sections 4.1/4.2 organization is confusing for multilingual evaluation; recommend clarifying CoNLL 2008 = English vs CoNLL 2009 = multilingual.
