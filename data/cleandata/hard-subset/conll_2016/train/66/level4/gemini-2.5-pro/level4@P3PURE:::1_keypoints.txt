- Presents a transition-based parser combining Henderson et al.’s joint syntactic/semantic transitions with Dyer et al.’s stack LSTM architecture.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 tasks with competitive results versus prior joint parsers.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and contains a thorough related-work review.  
- Methodology and system description are sound, hyperparameters justified, and discussion insightful.  
- Minor concern: exceptionally good Chinese embeddings suggest embeddings quality may drive gains more than model architecture.  
- Primary weakness: limited originality—system is a direct combination of two existing methods rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Nonetheless, the synthesis and effective implementation/evaluation are valuable and anticipated by the community.
