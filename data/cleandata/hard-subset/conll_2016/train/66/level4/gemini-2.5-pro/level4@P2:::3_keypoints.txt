- Applies stack LSTM representation learning to joint syntactic-semantic parsing, removing hand-crafted features and modeling the full parser state.
- Demonstrates a greedy, linear-time parser using the learned state that outperforms previous joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009.
- Tackles an important joint syntax–semantics problem, providing a strong modern neural baseline to reduce pipeline error propagation.
- Presents strong empirical results and informative ablation studies that clarify benefits and trade-offs of joint vs. pipelined/single-task variants.
- Offers a simple, efficient model that avoids complex feature engineering and enables fast inference for practical use.
- Lays a solid foundation for future extensions (global inference, advanced embeddings, external knowledge) that could further improve results.
- Fails to discuss parameter efficiency and how shared neural representations reduce parameter growth compared to linear models.
- Limited architectural novelty—the stack LSTM is adapted from prior work—and the paper could better justify this choice over alternatives (e.g., global beam-search models).
- Asks for clarification on the static oracle logic (e.g., handling M-SHIFT and M-REDUCE commitments) and whether a dynamic oracle was considered for training.
