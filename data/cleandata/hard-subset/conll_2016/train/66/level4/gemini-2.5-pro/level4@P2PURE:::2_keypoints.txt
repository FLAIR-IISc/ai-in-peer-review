- Proposes a transition-based parser for joint syntactic and semantic parsing inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Key differences: represents the entire parser state vectorially (via stack LSTMs) and uses a simple greedy search.  
- Uses stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009; competitive with 2008/2009-era systems but outperformed by newer methods.  
- Claims advantages of no hand-crafted features and fast inference due to greedy search.  
- Well-written and builds effectively on LSTM models and Henderson’s algorithm.  
- Main reservation: greedy decoding limits comparability with cited works that use more complex search; beam search results should be included.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on the distinction between A1 and C-A1 links.  
- Section 2 should summarize which transitions are reused, modified, or novel compared to Henderson (2008).  
- Section 3.3 should clarify why predicate word is concatenated with its disambiguated sense (seems redundant).  
- Section 4 (4.1/4.2) should more clearly separate the CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) experimental setups.
