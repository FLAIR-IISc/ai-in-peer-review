- Proposes a dependency parser that jointly integrates syntactic and semantic transitions, inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Key distinctions: uses vectorial representations for the entire parser state (not just top stack elements) and employs a simple greedy search.  
- Core idea: stack LSTMs let the state vector retain long-range syntactic features (e.g., predicate–argument path) beneficial for semantic role labeling.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Results are competitive with older 2008/2009 systems but lag behind more recent proposals (see bottom of Table 3).  
- Advantage: no hand-crafted features and computationally efficient due to greedy decoding.  
- Major concern: use of a simple greedy algorithm makes comparisons with some cited works unfair; beam search results should have been reported.  
- Request for clarification on A1 versus C-A1 links to better define the target task.  
- Ask for a clear summary of differences between this paper’s transition set and Henderson et al.’s, to show what is reused versus new/modified.  
- Question the need to concatenate predicate and disambiguated sense representations, since senses are predicate-specific and this may be redundant.  
- Section organization confusion: clarify multilingual treatment because CoNLL 2008 is English-focused while CoNLL 2009 includes multiple languages.
