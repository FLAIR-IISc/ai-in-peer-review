- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.  
- Emphasizes joint parsing as a key factor in the success of transition-based neural parsers.  
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system improving on Henderson (2008) and demonstrating neural networks’ effectiveness on this task.  
- Attributes NN success to automatic feature learning and notes an additional advantage: parameter efficiency versus linear models.  
- The proposed transition system could be integrated with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Notes many orthogonal improvements are possible that might yield state-of-the-art results.  
- Recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the new transition system as a valuable addition or future work.  
- Observes the transition system initially resembles an arc-eager oracle: M-S and M-D constrain semantic arcs in specific directions, while L and R exclude reverse arcs.
