This paper introduces a Stack LSTM parser, building upon prior work by Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) in joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. Combining the transition system of the former with the stack LSTM architecture of the latter yields compelling results when evaluated on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks. 

I found this to be a strong paper, characterized by clear writing, thorough explanations, a comprehensive review of related work, and highly interesting results. The methodology is robust, though I have a minor reservation concerning the Chinese embeddings – suggesting that high-quality embeddings may contribute significantly to performance, potentially even exceeding the benefits of a sophisticated model. The system description is lucid, hyperparameter choices are well-justified, and the discussion is insightful.

While the proposed system doesn’t present fundamentally novel methods, given the foundational work of Henderson et al. on semi-synchronised joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al.’s development of the stack LSTM, its implementation feels timely and valuable. Indeed, a parser of this design was a logical next step, and this work successfully delivers on that expectation.
