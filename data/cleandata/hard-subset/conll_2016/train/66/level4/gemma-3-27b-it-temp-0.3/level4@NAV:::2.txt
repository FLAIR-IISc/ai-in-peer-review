Review- General comments
================
This paper introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser, drawing inspiration from the work of Henderson et al. (2008). The authors highlight two key distinctions: the utilization of vectorial representations for the entire parser state, rather than focusing solely on stack top elements or recent configurations, and the implementation of a straightforward greedy search algorithm. The core concept revolves around leveraging stack LSTMs to maintain a vector-based representation of the parser's state, effectively capturing potentially long-range syntactic features crucial for semantic role labeling – particularly the dependency path between a predicate and its potential role filler. 

The system’s evaluation is conducted on the English CoNLL 2008 dataset and the multilingual CoNLL 2009 dataset. Performance comparisons against previously published results demonstrate competitive scores relative to systems from 2008 and 2009, although more recent approaches achieve higher performance (as noted in Table 3). The authors emphasize the system’s advantage of requiring no manually engineered features and its speed, stemming from the simplicity of the greedy algorithm. The paper is well-written and presents a significant body of work, building upon the increasingly popular LSTM architecture and revisiting the Henderson et al. algorithm, which now appears particularly insightful.

However, I have concerns regarding the choice of a simple greedy algorithm, as it limits direct comparability with some of the cited research. Including results from a beam search, even briefly, would have been a valuable addition.

More detailed comments / questions
==================================
Section 2:
Clarification regarding the inclusion of both A1 and C-A1 links would aid in understanding the specific task addressed by this paper. Furthermore, a concise summary outlining the differences between the transition system employed here and that of Henderson et al. would be beneficial. Currently, it is unclear which components are directly adopted from Henderson et al. and which have been newly introduced or modified.

Section 3.3
The rationale behind concatenating the word predicate with its disambiguated sense is unclear, as the sense is inherently predicate-specific and thus appears redundant.

Section 4
The organization of sections 4.1 and 4.2 is somewhat confusing with respect to multilinguality. CoNLL 2008 was focused on English, while the CoNLL 2009 shared task expanded the scope to include several additional languages.
