This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser building on the work of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) regarding joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing, and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. Combining the transition system from the former with the stack LSTM from the latter yields promising results when compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks. 

I found this to be a strong paper – it is well-written and clearly explained, with a thorough related work section and very interesting results. The methodology is sound, although I have a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings. It seems plausible that high-quality embeddings could provide more benefit than even a sophisticated model. Furthermore, the system is described clearly, the hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My primary observation is that the proposed system doesn’t introduce a fundamentally novel method. Henderson et al. laid the groundwork for semi-synchronous joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM relatively recently. However, I believe a parser of this design was a natural and welcome progression in the field, and I am pleased to see it implemented here.
