This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser building upon the work of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. Combining the transition system from the former with the stack LSTM architecture of the latter yields promising results when compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks. 

I found this paper to be strong overall; it is well-written, clearly explained, and demonstrates a solid understanding of related work. The reported results are particularly interesting. The methodology is sound, although I have a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings – it seems plausible that high-quality embeddings could provide more informative representations than even a sophisticated model. Furthermore, the system description is clear, hyperparameter choices are justified, and the discussion is insightful.

My primary observation is that the proposed system’s originality is somewhat limited, as the foundational work on semi-synchronised joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing was established by Henderson et al. several years prior, and the stack LSTM was introduced by Dyer et al. relatively recently. Thus, the approach does not represent a fundamentally new method *per se*. However, in my opinion, a parser of this design was a natural and valuable next step, and I am pleased to see it implemented here.
