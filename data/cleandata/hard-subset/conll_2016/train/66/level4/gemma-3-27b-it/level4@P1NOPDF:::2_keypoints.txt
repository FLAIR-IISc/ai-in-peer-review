- Presents a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Key differences: uses vectorial representations for the entire parser state instead of just top stack elements, and employs a simple greedy search.  
- Core idea: leverage stack LSTMs so state vectors retain long-range syntactic info crucial for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–filler paths).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual).  
- Performance is competitive with 2008/2009 shared-task systems but lower than more recent approaches (see bottom of Table 3).  
- Strengths: requires no hand-crafted features and is fast due to greedy decoding.  
- Paper is well-written and substantial, applying LSTMs to the Henderson et al. algorithm effectively.  
- Main concern: using a simple greedy algorithm limits comparability to other systems; author should report beam search results.  
- Section 2 needs an explanation of the presence of both A1 and C-A1 links.  
- Section 2 should summarize differences between the paper’s transition system and Henderson et al.’s to clarify what’s reused vs. modified.  
- Section 3.3: clarify why predicate word and disambiguated sense representations are concatenated, which appears redundant.  
- Section 4: reorganize/clarify sections 4.1 and 4.2 with respect to multilinguality and CoNLL 2008 vs 2009 distinctions.
