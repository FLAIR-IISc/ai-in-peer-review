This paper introduces a Stack LSTM parser building upon the foundations laid by Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) in their work on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing, and Dyer et al. (2015) regarding stack LSTM syntactic parsing. Combining the transition system from the former with the stack LSTM architecture of the latter yields promising results when evaluated on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks, particularly in comparison to existing joint systems. 

I found this paper to be very strong, exhibiting clear writing, thorough explanations, a comprehensive related work section, and genuinely interesting results. The methodology is robust, though I have a minor reservation concerning the Chinese embeddings – the performance suggests that high-quality embeddings can be more impactful than even a sophisticated model. Furthermore, the system is described clearly, hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My primary observation is that the proposed system’s novelty is somewhat limited, as Henderson et al. previously established the groundwork for semi-synchronous joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM architecture relatively recently. However, I believe the development of such a parser was a natural and valuable step, and this paper successfully achieves that.
