- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser building on Henderson et al. (2008).
- Two key distinctions: vectorial representations for the entire parser state and a purely greedy search algorithm.
- Leverages stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–filler paths).
- Evaluated on English CoNLL 2008 and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets with comparisons to prior systems.
- Competitive with 2008–2009 systems but outperformed by more recent approaches (see bottom of Table 3).
- Requires no hand-crafted features and benefits from the speed of greedy inference.
- Paper is well-written and effectively applies LSTM advances to Henderson et al.’s algorithm.
- Concern: choice of a simple greedy algorithm limits comparability; beam search results would be a useful, low-effort addition.
- Section 2 needs clarification on including both A1 and C-A1 links and a summary of transition differences from Henderson et al.
- Section 3.3 questions rationale for concatenating the predicate with its disambiguated sense (possible redundancy).
- Section 4 organization is confusing about multilingual evaluation—CoNLL 2008 is English-focused while CoNLL 2009 covers multiple languages.
