- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural architectures — an important research problem.  
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system (building on Henderson 2008) and a neural-network approach that succeeds where linear models struggled.  
- Neural nets’ automatic feature learning explains gains; additionally they are more parameter-efficient than linear models for joint tasks, aiding learning with limited data.  
- The proposed transition system appears well-designed and likely beneficial beyond the specific neural architecture used.  
- Recommend evaluating the transition system with other neural parsing models (e.g., Andor 2016’s global beam-search).  
- Many orthogonal improvements are possible; extensions could yield state-of-the-art results.  
- Suggest deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system to strengthen the paper and improve reproducibility.  
- Initial oracle observations: M-S prevents semantic arcs from buffer head to semantic stack; M-D prevents semantic arcs from stack top to buffer; L and R only exclude the reversed immediate dependency arc.  
- Overall: clearly written, convincing experiments, and a significant, well-executed contribution that lays a strong foundation for future work.
