- Paper presents a Stack LSTM joint syntactic/semantic parser combining Henderson et al.’s transition system with Dyer et al.’s stack LSTMs, showing strong results on CoNLL 2008/2009.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance; paper is well-written, clearly explained, and thoroughly situated in related work.
- Methodology is sound with a lucid system description and well-justified hyperparameters.
- Primary contribution: effective integration of stack LSTMs into joint parsing, achieving competitive performance without manual features.
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates strong performance using a simple, greedy inference algorithm, offering speed and practical benefits.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple datasets and languages, with detailed analysis of performance variations.
- Limitation: approach does not introduce fundamentally novel methods, mainly integrates existing techniques.
- System does not match some state-of-the-art methods that use more complex features and decoding schemes.
- Request for clarification on the pretrained embeddings used for Chinese and a version of results without them to show their impact.
- Request for deeper analysis of why the Semantics-only model underperforms compared to Hybrid and Joint, including specific error patterns.
- Minor comment: the reviewer’s remark about embeddings vs. model cleverness is a personal opinion, not a formal criticism.
- Suggestion to add a table summarizing running time of the proposed system compared to relevant alternatives.
