This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser building upon the foundations laid by Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) in their work on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing, and Dyer et al. (2015) regarding stack LSTM syntactic parsing. By integrating the transition system from Henderson et al. with the stack LSTM architecture of Dyer et al., the authors demonstrate compelling results when compared to existing joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I found this paper to be particularly strong. The writing is clear and concise, the methodology is well-explained, the related work is thoroughly reviewed, and the results are both interesting and significant. The methodological approach is sound; however, I have a minor concern regarding the impact of embedding quality. While the model demonstrates strong performance, it raises the question of whether highly informative embeddings could potentially yield greater gains than even a sophisticated model architecture. 

The system description is notably clear, facilitating a strong understanding of the implementation details. The authors provide robust justification for their hyperparameter choices, and the subsequent discussion of the results is insightful and well-reasoned.

A potential limitation lies in the system's incremental nature. The core concepts – semi-synchronous joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing (Henderson et al.) and the stack LSTM architecture (Dyer et al.) – were established in prior work. The paper doesn’t present a fundamentally novel method *per se*. However, I believe the combination presented here was a logical and valuable step forward. The field has arguably been anticipating the development of a parser of this design, and this work successfully delivers on that expectation. The integration and evaluation within a robust framework are significant contributions. 

In summary, this is a well-executed and insightful paper that effectively combines existing techniques to produce a strong parsing system. The minor concern regarding embedding quality does not detract from the overall positive assessment. I recommend this paper for acceptance.
