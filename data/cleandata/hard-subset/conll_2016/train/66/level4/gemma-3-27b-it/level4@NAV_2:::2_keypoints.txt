- Paper introduces a transition-based parser that jointly models syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs.  
- Two main innovations: learning vector representations of the entire parser state and using greedy decoding for inference.  
- Stack LSTMs capture long-range syntactic features, reducing the need for manually engineered features.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual), showing competitive joint-parsing performance but below pipeline state-of-the-art.  
- Major strengths: speed and avoidance of hand-crafted features; paper is well-written and applies representation learning to a classic parsing framework.  
- Key reservation: exclusive use of greedy decoding limits comparability; adding beam-search results would strengthen evaluation.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on distinction between A1 and C-A1 links.  
- Section 2 should more explicitly summarize differences from Henderson et al., noting which parts are inherited versus novel.  
- Section 3.3 questions the benefit of concatenating word and predicate-sense representations given lexeme-specific predicate senses.  
- Section 4’s organization is confusing about multilingual evaluation; clarify CoNLL 2008 (English) vs CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) distinctions.
