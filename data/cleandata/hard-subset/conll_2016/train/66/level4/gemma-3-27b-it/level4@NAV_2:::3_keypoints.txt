- Revisits joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.  
- Argues joint modeling remains promising for improving transition-based neural parsers.  
- Introduces a novel transition system that builds on and improves Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Demonstrates a neural network approach that achieves strong performance where linear models struggled.  
- Authors attribute success to automatic feature learning; reviewer notes parameter efficiency in joint learning may also be important.  
- The transition system likely benefits from the chosen neural architecture but should be compatible with other methods (e.g., beam-search approaches like Andor 2016).  
- Several orthogonal improvements could be integrated, and future extensions could reach state-of-the-art results.  
- Recommends developing a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system as supplemental material or future work.  
- Initial oracle analysis suggests similarities to the arc-eager oracle with specific constraints on semantic actions.  
- Notes specific constraints: M-SHIFT restricts semantic arcs involving the buffer head; M-REDUCE limits arcs concerning the stack top; LEFT and RIGHT forbid their reverse counterparts.
