This paper introduces a Stack LSTM parser that builds upon prior work in transition-based joint syntactic/semantic parsing (Henderson et al., 2008, 2013) and stack LSTM syntactic parsing (Dyer et al., 2015). Combining the transition system from the former with the stack LSTM architecture of the latter yields compelling results when evaluated on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks. 

I found this paper to be exceptionally strong in its presentation and clarity. The related work is thoroughly discussed, and the results are both noteworthy and well-explained. The methodology is robust, although I have a minor reservation regarding the potential impact of embedding quality – specifically, whether highly informative embeddings could outweigh the benefits of a sophisticated model. The system description is clear and comprehensive, hyperparameter choices are well-justified, and the discussion is insightful. 

While the proposed system doesn’t introduce fundamentally novel methods – building as it does on the foundations laid by Henderson et al.’s semi-synchronous joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al.’s stack LSTM – its implementation feels timely and valuable. Indeed, a parser of this design has been anticipated, and this work successfully delivers on that expectation.
