- Presents a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser building on Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Introduces two main changes: vectorial representations of the entire parser state and a simple greedy search algorithm.  
- Uses stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic context and dependency paths for semantic role labeling.  
- Evaluated on English CoNLL 2008 and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Achieves competitive performance vs. original 2008/2009 systems but slightly below more recent approaches (see Table 3).  
- Advantages: eliminates hand-crafted features and is efficient due to greedy decoding.  
- Paper is well-written and effectively applies modern LSTM techniques to the Henderson algorithm.  
- Major concern: reliance on purely greedy search limits comparability; reviewer suggests reporting beam search results as well.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on inclusion of both A1 and C-A1 links and a concise summary of differences from Henderson et al. (what’s inherited vs. new).  
- Section 3.3: unclear why predicate word is concatenated with disambiguated sense—this representation choice needs justification.  
- Section 4: organization is confusing about multilingual evaluation; clarify that CoNLL 2008 is English-only and CoNLL 2009 is multilingual and restructure accordingly.
