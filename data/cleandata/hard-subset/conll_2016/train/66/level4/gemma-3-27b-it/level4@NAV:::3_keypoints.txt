- Revisits joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing using recent neural network advances.  
- Argues that joint models partly explain the success of transition-based neural parsers.  
- Contribution 1: Proposes a novel transition system that appears to improve on Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that neural networks effectively handle the joint parsing problem where linear models struggled.  
- Reviewer notes parameter efficiency of neural nets (fewer parameters than linear models that need weights per feature/class) likely contributes to their success.  
- Claims the new transition system should work with other NN architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search) and can be extended with orthogonal improvements toward state-of-the-art.  
- Recommends adding a dynamic oracle for the transition system; initial observations show strong resemblance to the arc-eager oracle with action constraints (M-S forbids semantic arcs from buffer head; M-D forbids arcs targeting buffer words from semantic stack; L and R each forbid their corresponding reverse arc).
