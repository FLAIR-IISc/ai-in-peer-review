- Paper presents a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using Henderson et al.'s transition system combined with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTMs.

- Primary contribution: demonstrates stack LSTMs effectively applied to joint syntactic-semantic parsing, learning full parser-state representations without manual features.

- Secondary contribution: strong empirical results on CoNLL 2008/2009, surpassing prior joint models and approaching pipeline system performance.

- Provides a clear, well-documented implementation with a fast, linear-time greedy inference algorithm suitable for practical use.

- Strength — Strong empirical performance: achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on CoNLL 2008/2009, outperforming previous joint models.

- Strength — Effective use of stack LSTMs: avoids extensive feature engineering and potentially improves generalization.

- Strength — Clear and thorough explanation: paper is well-written and facilitates understanding and reproducibility.

- Strength — Efficient inference: linear-time greedy decoding with reported fast runtime (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 test).

- Weakness — Limited novelty: core components (transition-based joint parsing and stack LSTMs) are not novel, primarily an application of known techniques.

- Weakness — Dependence on pretrained embeddings: multilingual performance (notably Chinese) appears heavily reliant on embedding quality.

- Weakness — Comparison to state-of-the-art: still lags behind the best systems that use more complex features, optimal decoding, or extra training data.

- Weakness — Constraints of greedy decoding: greedy inference may limit search and prevent achieving higher accuracy.

- Question — How sensitive is performance to choice of pretrained embeddings across languages and what are results without/different embeddings?

- Question — Were beam search experiments conducted, and what were the performance/runtime trade-offs?

- Question — What expert-crafted features enable top pipeline systems to outperform this model, and can some be integrated?
