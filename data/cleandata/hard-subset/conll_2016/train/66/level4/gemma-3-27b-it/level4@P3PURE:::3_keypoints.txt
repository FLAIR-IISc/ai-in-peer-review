- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing with neural networks; joint modeling is key for transition-based parsers.
- Contribution 1: introduces a novel transition system that appears to outperform Henderson (2008).
- Contribution 2: shows a neural network approach succeeds on this difficult task where linear models struggled, credited to automatic feature learning.
- Reviewer note: neural networks also offer greater parameter efficiency than linear models for joint tasks, reducing parameters needed for feature/class pairs.
- The transition system should work well with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search) and supports many orthogonal improvements toward state-of-the-art.
- Recommendation: investigate a dynamic oracle for the transition system; observed action constraints (M-S, M-D, L, R) make it conceptually similar to an arc-eager oracle.
