- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing with neural networks, returning to transition-based methods.
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system (based on Henderson 2008) and a neural model that succeeds where linear models struggled.
- Neural network advantage attributed to automatic feature learning and greater parameter efficiency compared to linear models.
- Results are promising and the transition system could be adapted to other architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).
- Numerous orthogonal improvements are possible; extensions may achieve state-of-the-art performance.
- Recommend developing a dynamic oracle for the transition system to strengthen analysis and aid evaluation.
- Initial inspection: M-S prevents semantic arcs from buffer head to semantic stack words; M-D prevents arcs between stack top and buffer words; L and R block only the immediate reverse arc.
- Exploring the oracle’s relation to arc-eager would provide both theoretical insight and a practical tool.
