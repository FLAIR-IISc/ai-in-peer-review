- Presents a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Two main novelties: vectorial representations for the entire parser state (via stack LSTMs) and a simple greedy search algorithm.  
- Core idea: state vectors capture long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling, especially predicate–argument paths.  
- Evaluated on English CoNLL-2008 and multilingual CoNLL-2009; performance competitive with original shared-task systems but below more recent methods.  
- System requires no hand-crafted features and benefits from the speed of greedy decoding.  
- Well-written and builds sensibly on LSTM frameworks, but the greedy algorithm choice limits comparability to other work; beam-search results would strengthen evaluation.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on A1 vs C-A1 links and a concise summary of differences from Henderson et al. (what is inherited vs novel).  
- Section 3.3 questions the motivation for concatenating predicate word and its disambiguated sense representations as potentially redundant.  
- Section 4’s presentation of multilingual experiments is confusing; requires clearer separation of CoNLL-2008 (English) and CoNLL-2009 (multilingual) setup and results.
