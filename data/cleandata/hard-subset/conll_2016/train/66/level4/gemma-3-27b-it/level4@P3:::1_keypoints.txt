- Presents a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs, building on prior work.
- Primary contribution: effective combination of a semi-synchronized joint parsing transition system with stack LSTMs to avoid hand-crafted features.
- Demonstrates that a greedy, linear-time inference algorithm can yield strong results in joint parsing.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance among jointly learned models on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English datasets.
- Provides thorough experimental evaluation including multilingual datasets and reports runtime performance.
- Strong empirical performance validates competitiveness with more complex, feature-engineered systems.
- Novelty lies in integrating known techniques (transition-based parsing + stack LSTMs) within a joint parsing framework.
- Efficiency and scalability are notable due to linear-time greedy decoding and practical runtimes.
- Paper is clearly written with well-justified design choices and comprehensive related work discussion.
- Core components are not fundamentally new; novelty is mainly in their application and combination.
- Performance is dependent on pretrained embeddings (notably for Chinese), limiting learning from scratch.
- Does not surpass systems using extensive feature engineering, optimal decoding, or extra data; lacks deeper trade-off comparisons.
- Limited error analysis; more detailed breakdown of errors would strengthen the work.
- Requests more analysis on the impact of different pretrained embedding strategies across languages.
- Asks how performance scales with training data size and whether there is evidence of overfitting on WSJ.
- Seeks clarification on limitations of greedy inference and potential gains/costs from beam search or other decoding methods.
- Notes that the open-source implementation and authors’ candid discussion of limitations are valuable contributions.
