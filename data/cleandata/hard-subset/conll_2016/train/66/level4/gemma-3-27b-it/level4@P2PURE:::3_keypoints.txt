- The paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural architectures, noting joint models are key for transition-based NN parsers.  
- Two main contributions: a novel transition system (improving on Henderson 2008) and empirical demonstration that neural networks work well on this challenging joint task.  
- Authors credit learned features for NN success; reviewer adds that NNs are also far more parameter-efficient than linear models in joint modeling.  
- The proposed transition system should be compatible with other NN approaches (e.g., global beam-search/Andor 2016) and amenable to many orthogonal improvements with potential for state-of-the-art results.  
- Future work should derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system; initial analysis suggests similarities to an arc-eager oracle and describes how M-S, M-D, L, and R actions constrain semantic arcs.
