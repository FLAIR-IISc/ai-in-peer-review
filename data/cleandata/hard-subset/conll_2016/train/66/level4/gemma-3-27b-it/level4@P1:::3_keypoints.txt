- Paper revisits joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing using recent neural-network advances; joint models are a key strength of transition-based parsers.  
- Contribution 1: Proposes a new transition system that appears to improve on Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates a successful neural-network approach where previous linear models struggled.  
- Authors credit automatic feature learning for success; reviewer notes neural nets are also more parameter-efficient than linear models for joint tasks.  
- The proposed transition system could likely benefit other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search) and other orthogonal improvements, with potential to reach state-of-the-art.  
- Reviewer suggests investigating a dynamic oracle for the transition system, noting similarities to the arc-eager oracle and describing which actions exclude which semantic arcs (M-SHIFT, M-REDUCE, LEFT, RIGHT).  
- Questions for authors: please elaborate on model parameter efficiency versus linear models, and comment on whether a dynamic oracle has been considered or how one might be developed.
