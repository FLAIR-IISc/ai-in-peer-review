- Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Uses vectorial representations of the entire parser state via stack LSTMs instead of relying only on top stack elements or recent configurations.  
- Employs a plain greedy search algorithm for decoding.  
- Claims stack LSTMs capture long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument paths).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Performance is competitive with 2008/2009 systems but falls short of more recent approaches (see Table 3).  
- No hand-crafted features required and benefits from the speed of greedy decoding.  
- Paper is well-written and makes a substantial contribution by applying LSTMs to Henderson et al.’s algorithm.  
- Major reservation: use of simple greedy search limits comparability to other works; beam search results should be reported.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on A1 vs C-A1 links and a clear summary of differences from Henderson et al.’s transition system (what is reused vs modified).  
- Section 3.3: unclear why the predicate token and its disambiguated sense are concatenated—sense seems redundant in the representation.  
- Section 4: organization around sections 4.1/4.2 is confusing regarding multilinguality—clarify CoNLL 2008 (English) vs CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) distinctions.
