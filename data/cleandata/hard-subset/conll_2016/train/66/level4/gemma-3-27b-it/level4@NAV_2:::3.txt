This paper revisits the problem of joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing, leveraging recent advances in neural network models. Joint modeling remains a promising avenue for improving transition-based neural network parsers.

The primary contributions of this work are twofold. First, the authors introduce a novel transition system, building upon and improving the Henderson et al. (2008) system. Second, the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of a neural network approach to this problem, achieving strong performance where linear models previously struggled. The authors posit that this success is due to the network's capacity to automatically learn relevant features. However, a potentially significant factor is the parameter efficiency of neural networks compared to linear models in joint learning scenarios – reducing the number of parameters needed when learning across multiple tasks.

While the proposed transition system likely benefits from the chosen neural network architecture, its potential compatibility with other models (e.g., beam-search approaches like Andor, 2016) suggests its broader applicability. Furthermore, several orthogonal improvements could be integrated, and future extensions of this work are likely to yield state-of-the-art results.

Developing a dynamic oracle for this transition system would be a valuable addition, potentially in supplemental material or future work. Initial analysis suggests similarities to the arc-eager oracle, but with specific constraints: the M-SHIFT action restricts semantic arcs involving the buffer’s head, while M-REDUCE limits arcs concerning the stack’s top element. The LEFT and RIGHT actions appear to preclude their reverse counterparts, and no others.
