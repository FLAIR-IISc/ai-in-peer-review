- Novel transition system for joint syntactic and semantic parsing built on and improving Henderson et al. (2008).
- Neural approach (Stack LSTMs) effectively learns features and may benefit from parameter efficiency over linear models.
- Strong empirical results on CoNLL 2008/2009, competitive with joint and pipeline systems and showing multilingual generalizability.
- Efficient implementation with linear runtime and fast inference (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 English test).
- Stack LSTMs capture parser state without manual feature engineering, simplifying modeling and improving generalization.
- Presented transition system is clear and interpretable, aiding analysis and future extensions.
- Limited novelty: transition system largely derived from Henderson et al. (2008); main innovation is applying Stack LSTMs within it.
- Does not match state-of-the-art systems that use expert features, optimal decoding, or extra training data, indicating room for improvement.
- Reliance on greedy inference may limit accuracy compared to beam search or optimal decoding methods.
- Paper lacks discussion or preliminary analysis on deriving a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system.
- Question: What specific challenges or modifications are needed to integrate optimal decoding or external datasets into this framework?
- Question: Can the authors provide a formal description or justification for the M-S and M-D transition constraints?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to LSTM hyperparameters (hidden size, layers) and was extensive hyperparameter tuning performed?
