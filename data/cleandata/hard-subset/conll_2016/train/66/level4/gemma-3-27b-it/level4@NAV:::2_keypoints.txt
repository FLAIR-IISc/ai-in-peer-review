- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser using stack LSTMs for full parser-state vector representations.  
- Key innovations: vectorial representation of entire parser state (not just stack top) and a simple greedy decoding algorithm.  
- Leverages stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., dependency paths).  
- Evaluated on English CoNLL 2008 and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.  
- Results are competitive with 2008/2009 systems but slightly below more recent approaches (Table 3).  
- Achieves results without hand-engineered features and benefits from faster greedy decoding.  
- Paper is clearly written and successfully applies recent LSTM advances to the Henderson et al. framework.  
- Concern: use of a simple greedy algorithm limits comparability to some cited works; including beam search results would strengthen evaluation.  
- Request for clarification: whether both A1 and C-A1 links are included and how that affects the task definition.  
- Suggestion: provide a concise summary of differences between this transition system and Henderson et al. (2008) to clarify adopted vs. modified elements.  
- Question: rationale for concatenating word predicate with its disambiguated sense, which seems redundant.  
- Comment: organization of Sections 4.1/4.2 is confusing regarding multilinguality—CoNLL 2008 vs. CoNLL 2009 scope should be clarified.
