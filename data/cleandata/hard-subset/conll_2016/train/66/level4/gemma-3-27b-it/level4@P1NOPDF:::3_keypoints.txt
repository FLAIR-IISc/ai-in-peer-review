- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent neural-network advances.  
- Joint modeling has been crucial for transition-based neural parsers’ success.  
- Contribution 1: a new transition system that appears to outperform Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: a neural-network approach that succeeds where linear models struggled.  
- Authors credit success to neural networks’ ability to automatically learn relevant features.  
- Reviewer notes an additional advantage: neural networks are more parameter-efficient than linear models when jointly learning two tasks.  
- The proposed transition system likely performs well with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Many orthogonal improvements could be explored; extensions may reach state-of-the-art.  
- Reviewer recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the transition system (appendix or future work).  
- Empirical observation: M-S prevents semantic arcs between buffer head and semantic stack; M-D prohibits arcs between stack top and buffer; L and R exclude only the reverse arc.
