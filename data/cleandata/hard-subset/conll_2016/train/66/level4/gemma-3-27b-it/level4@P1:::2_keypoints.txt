- Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Differs from prior work by using vectorial representations of the entire parser state rather than just top stack elements, and by employing a simple greedy search.  
- Leverages stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument paths).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009; performance is competitive with older 2008/2009 systems but lags behind more recent approaches.  
- Requires no hand-crafted features and gains speed from the greedy algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and makes a substantial contribution by integrating LSTMs and refining Henderson et al.’s algorithm.  
- Reviewer concern: the use of a greedy algorithm limits comparability with some cited works; recommend reporting beam-search results (even in supplementary material).  
- Section 2 request: add a brief explanation of A1 vs C-A1 links and summarize differences between this transition system and Henderson et al.’s to clarify what is reused vs novel.  
- Section 3.3 question: why concatenate the word predicate with its disambiguated sense—this appears redundant since senses are predicate-specific.  
- Section 4 critique: organization about multilinguality is confusing; clarify that CoNLL 2008 is English-focused while CoNLL 2009 is multilingual.
