- Summary: Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the full parser state, with a plain greedy search, evaluated on CoNLL 2008/2009 and obtaining competitive results.

- Contribution 1: Introduces stack LSTMs to learn a global algorithm-state representation that captures large-scope syntactic features relevant for semantic role labeling.

- Contribution 2: Develops a joint syntactic-semantic parser that achieves competitive performance without hand-crafted features.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates the approach across multiple languages (including English and Chinese) on CoNLL shared tasks.

- Strength 1: Well-written paper that builds on recent LSTM advances.

- Strength 2: Competitive empirical performance on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 datasets.

- Strength 3: Stack LSTMs enable capturing long-range syntactic information useful for SRL.

- Strength 4: System is fast due to a simple greedy decoding algorithm.

- Weakness 1: Greedy search may limit performance versus more advanced search/inference methods.

- Weakness 2: Lacks detailed comparisons to other state-of-the-art systems, especially those using advanced search.

- Weakness 3: Experimental section organization could better reflect the multilingual scope of CoNLL 2009.

- Question 1: Can you provide more detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art systems, particularly those with advanced search algorithms?

- Question 2: Do you plan to address the greedy algorithm limitation or integrate more advanced search strategies?

- Question 3: Please clarify preprocessing for handling out-of-vocabulary words and unseen predicates at test time.
