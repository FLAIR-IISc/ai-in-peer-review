This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that builds upon the work of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. By combining the transition system from the former and the stack LSTM from the latter, the authors achieve interesting results compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

I found this paper to be well-written, clearly explained, and thoroughly grounded in related work, making it a compelling read. The results are particularly noteworthy, and the methodology is sound, with the exception of a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings. This concern leads me to believe that high-quality embeddings can be more informative than even the most sophisticated models. The system description is clear, the hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My only criticism is that the proposed system lacks originality, as it draws heavily from established work. Henderson et al. laid the groundwork for semi-synchronised joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM last year. However, in my opinion, the creation of such a parser was long overdue, and I am glad to see it has been undertaken in this work.
