- Proposes a Stack LSTM parser that combines Henderson et al.'s joint syntactic/semantic transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM architecture.  
- Learns a representation of the full algorithm state and uses a greedy, linear-time inference algorithm including feature extraction.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008–2009 English tasks and outperforms other joint syntax–semantics models.  
- Main contributions: joint syntactic/semantic Stack LSTM parser, greedy linear-time inference, and state-of-the-art joint results on CoNLL 08–09.  
- Strength: clear, well-written methodology that is easy to follow.  
- Strength: thorough related-work discussion that situates the novelty of the approach.  
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating effectiveness of the proposed parser.  
- Strength: system description is clear and hyperparameters are justified.  
- Weakness: limited originality, largely building on prior work by Henderson et al. and Dyer et al.  
- Weakness: reliance on pretrained Chinese embeddings may inflate performance and obscure the model’s true contribution.  
- Question: how will the authors address concerns about the use of pretrained Chinese embeddings and what alternatives exist?  
- Question: provide more details on hyperparameter tuning and justification for chosen values.  
- Question: how is the parser envisioned to be used in practical applications, and what are its benefits and limitations?
