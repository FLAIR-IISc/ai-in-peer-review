- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent neural-network advances.
- Proposes a new transition system that appears to improve on Henderson (2008).
- Demonstrates neural networks can solve this joint parsing problem where linear models struggled, attributed to automatic feature learning.
- Reviewer adds neural nets are also more parameter-efficient for joint tasks versus linear models needing a weight per feature/class pair.
- Suggests the proposed transition system could be paired with other neural models (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).
- Notes many orthogonal improvements are possible and extensions could achieve state-of-the-art results.
- Recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system (appendix or future work).
- Observes the system resembles the arc-eager oracle: M-S/M-D actions exclude specific semantic arcs; L/R actions exclude the reverse arc.
