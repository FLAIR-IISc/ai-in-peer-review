- Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs combining Henderson et al.'s transition system with Dyer et al.'s stack LSTM approach.  
- Uses a greedy inference algorithm with linear time complexity, including feature extraction.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English shared tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and easy to follow.  
- Related work is well-covered and the system description is clear.  
- Results are interesting and demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Methodology is sound and hyperparameters are justified.  
- Lacks originality since it builds heavily on prior work (Henderson et al., Dyer et al.).  
- Use of pretrained Chinese embeddings raises concerns that embeddings, not the model, drive performance and may affect generalization.  
- Question: How will authors address the originality concern given reliance on existing research?  
- Question: Can authors provide deeper comparisons to other state-of-the-art systems, especially those using expert-crafted features?  
- Question: How do authors plan to incorporate morphological features to improve performance on highly inflective languages?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-structured and discussed, but the originality issue should be addressed in the authors' response.
