This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that builds upon the work of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. By combining the transition system from the former with the stack LSTM from the latter, the authors achieve interesting results compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

The paper is well-written, clearly explained, and provides a thorough overview of related work. The results are also noteworthy, making this a compelling contribution to the field. The methodology is sound, although I do have a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings, which suggests that high-quality embeddings can be more informative than a sophisticated model. The system description is clear, the hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My primary criticism is that the proposed system lacks originality, as it combines existing ideas from Henderson et al. and Dyer et al. However, I believe that the creation of such a parser was overdue, and I am glad that the authors have undertaken this effort. The fact that the underlying concepts have been established in previous work does not detract from the value of this paper, which provides a timely and effective implementation of these ideas.
