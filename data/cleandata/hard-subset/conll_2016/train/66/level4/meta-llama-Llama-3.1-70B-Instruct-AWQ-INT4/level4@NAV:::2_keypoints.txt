- Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).
- Key differences: uses vectorial representations of the entire parser state and a simple greedy search.
- Leverages stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic features important for semantic role labeling.
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and multilingual CoNLL 2009 datasets.
- Results are competitive with earlier 2008/2009 systems but lag behind more recent proposals.
- Strengths: no hand-crafted features and fast inference due to greedy decoding.
- Paper is well-written and effectively applies LSTMs to the Henderson et al. algorithm.
- Concern: choice of simple greedy algorithm limits comparability; beam search results should be reported.
- Section 2 needs clarification on inclusion of both A1 and C-A1 links and explicit differences from Henderson et al.’s transitions.
- Section 3.3: concatenating predicate and its disambiguated sense appears redundant.
- Section 4: organization is confusing regarding multilinguality; clarify that CoNLL 2008 is English and CoNLL 2009 includes multiple languages.
