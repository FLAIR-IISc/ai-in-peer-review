- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent neural network advances.  
- Two main contributions: a new transition system and demonstrating neural networks' effectiveness for this joint task.  
- New transition system appears to improve on Henderson (2008).  
- Neural networks succeed where linear models struggled by automatically learning relevant features.  
- Neural networks are also more parameter-efficient than linear models when jointly learning two problems.  
- Proposed transition system should be compatible with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Several orthogonal improvements could be explored to potentially reach state-of-the-art results.  
- Recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system (even as appendix/follow-up).  
- Transition system shows resemblance to arc-eager behavior.  
- M-S action excludes semantic arcs between the buffer start word and words on the semantic stack; M-D excludes semantic arcs between stack top and buffer words.  
- L and R actions appear to only exclude the reverse arc with no additional exclusions.  
- Overall the approach is promising and has potential to advance joint semantic-syntactic parsing.
