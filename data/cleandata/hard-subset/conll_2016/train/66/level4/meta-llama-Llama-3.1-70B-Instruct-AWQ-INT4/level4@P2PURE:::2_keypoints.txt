- Proposes a joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs and a greedy search algorithm.
- Two main differences vs. Henderson et al.: full parser-state vector representations (not just top stack elements) and plain greedy decoding.
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); competitive with older systems but behind more recent proposals.
- Advantage: no hand-crafted features and computational efficiency due to greedy decoding.
- Major concern: greedy search choice limits comparability to other works; reviewer asks for beam-search results.
- Section 2 needs clarification on A1 vs C-A1 links and a concise summary of which transitions are reused vs modified from Henderson et al.
- Section 3.3: unclear necessity of concatenating predicate word with its disambiguated sense (appears redundant).
- Section 4: organization around multilinguality is confusing; clarify that CoNLL 2008 is English while CoNLL 2009 covers multiple languages.
