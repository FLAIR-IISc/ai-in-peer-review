This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that combines the work of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. The integration of the transition system from the former and the stack LSTM from the latter yields promising results compared to the joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

**Summary of the Paper:**
The paper introduces a novel joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser that learns a representation of the entire algorithm state using stack long short-term memories (LSTMs). The parser employs a greedy inference algorithm with linear time complexity, including feature extraction. The authors evaluate their model on the CoNLL 2008-9 English shared tasks and achieve the best parsing performance among models that jointly learn syntax and semantics.

**Main Contributions:**
1. The development of a Stack LSTM parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies.
2. The use of a greedy inference algorithm with linear time complexity.
3. The achievement of state-of-the-art results on the CoNLL 2008-9 English shared tasks.

**Strengths:**
1. The paper presents a well-written and well-explained methodology, making it easy to follow and understand.
2. The authors provide a thorough discussion of related work, highlighting the novelty of their approach.
3. The results are interesting and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed parser.
4. The description of the system is clear, and the hyperparameters are justified.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The proposed system lacks originality, as it builds upon existing work by Henderson et al. and Dyer et al.
2. The use of pretrained Chinese embeddings may be more informative than the model itself, which could be a concern.

**Questions to Authors:**
1. How do you plan to address the concern regarding the use of pretrained Chinese embeddings, and what alternatives could be explored?
2. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and the justification for the chosen values?
3. How do you envision the proposed parser being used in practical applications, and what are the potential benefits and limitations?
