- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing, building on recent neural network advances.  
- Introduces a novel transition system that appears to improve on Henderson (2008).  
- Shows neural networks succeed where linear models struggled, thanks to automatic feature learning.  
- Notes neural models are more parameter-efficient than linear models when jointly learning two tasks.  
- The proposed transition system should be compatible with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Many orthogonal improvements are possible; extensions could reach state-of-the-art performance.  
- Recommends deriving a dynamic oracle for the new transition system (e.g., in an appendix or follow-up).  
- Observes the system resembles an arc-eager oracle: M-S and M-D preclude specific semantic arcs, while L and R preclude the reverse arcs.
