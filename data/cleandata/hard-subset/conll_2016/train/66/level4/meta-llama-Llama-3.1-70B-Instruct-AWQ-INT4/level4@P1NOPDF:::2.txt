General comments
================

The paper proposes a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser, inspired by the joint parser of Henderson et al. (2008). The authors highlight two key differences between their approach and the original work: (1) the use of vectorial representations for the entire parser state, rather than just the top elements of the stack or the last parser configurations, and (2) the employment of a plain greedy search algorithm. The core idea is to leverage stack LSTMs to capture potentially large-scoped syntactic features that are known to be crucial for semantic role labeling, such as the path between the predicate and the candidate role filler head. The system is evaluated on the CoNLL 2008 dataset (English) and the multilingual CoNLL 2009 dataset. The authors compare their system's performance to previously reported results, demonstrating that it performs well compared to the 2008 and 2009 systems but less so compared to more recent proposals (cf. the bottom of Table 3). They emphasize, however, that their system does not require any hand-crafted features and is fast due to the simple greedy algorithm.

The paper is well-written and describes a substantial amount of work, building on the recently popular LSTMs and applying them to the Henderson et al. algorithm, which now appears to have been somewhat visionary. However, I have reservations regarding the choice of the simple greedy algorithm, as it renders the results not directly comparable to some of the cited works. It would not have been too much additional work or space to provide, for instance, beam-searched performance.

More detailed comments / questions
==================================

Section 2:
A brief explanation of the presence of both A1 and C-A1 links would facilitate a better understanding of the paper's target task. Additionally, a summary of the differences between the set of transitions used in this work and that of Henderson et al. would be beneficial. In its current form, it is challenging to discern what is directly reused from Henderson et al. and what is new or slightly modified.

Section 3.3:
The need for representations that concatenate the word predicate and its disambiguated sense seems redundant, as the disambiguated senses are specific to a predicate.

Section 4:
The organization of Sections 4.1 and 4.2 is confusing with respect to multilinguality. CoNLL 2008 focused on English, while the CoNLL 2009 shared task extended it to a few other languages.
