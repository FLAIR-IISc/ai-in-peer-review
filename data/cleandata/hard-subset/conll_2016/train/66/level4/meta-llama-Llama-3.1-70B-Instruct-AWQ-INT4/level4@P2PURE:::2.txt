General comments
================
The paper presents a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser, inspired by the joint parser of Henderson et al. (2008). The authors highlight two primary differences between their approach and the original: the use of vectorial representations for the entire parser state, rather than just the top elements of the stack or the last parser configurations, and the employment of a plain greedy search algorithm. The core idea is to leverage stack LSTMs to enable the parser state vector to capture potentially large-scoped syntactic features, known to be crucial for semantic role labeling, such as the path between the predicate and the candidate role filler head. The system is evaluated on the CoNLL 2008 dataset (English) and the multilingual CoNLL 2009 dataset. The authors compare their system's performance to previously reported results, demonstrating its competitiveness with 2008 and 2009 systems, although it falls short of more recent proposals (cf. the bottom of Table 3). Notably, the authors emphasize that their system does not require hand-crafted features and is efficient due to the simple greedy algorithm. The paper is well-written and describes a substantial amount of work, building on the recently popular LSTMs and applying them to the Henderson et al. algorithm, which now appears visionary. However, I have reservations about the choice of the simple greedy algorithm, as it renders the results incomparable to some of the cited works. Providing beam-searched performance, for instance, would not have been a significant additional effort or space requirement.

More detailed comments / questions
==================================
Section 2:
A comment on the presence of both A1 and C-A1 links would facilitate a better understanding of the paper's target task. A summary of the differences between the set of transitions used in this work and those of Henderson et al. would be beneficial. In its current form, it is challenging to discern what is directly reused from Henderson et al. and what is new or slightly modified.

Section 3.3:
The need for representations that concatenate the word predicate and its disambiguated sense is unclear, as the disambiguated senses are specific to a predicate, making this seem redundant.

Section 4:
The organization of Sections 4.1 and 4.2 is confusing regarding multilinguality. It would be helpful to clarify that CoNLL 2008 focused on English, while the CoNLL 2009 shared task extended it to a few other languages.
