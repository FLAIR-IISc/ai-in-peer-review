- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.
- Presents two contributions: a new transition system improving on Henderson (2008) and a neural approach that makes the task tractable.
- Neural networks automatically learn relevant features, enabling success where linear models struggled.
- Neural approach is more parameter-efficient for joint learning compared with linear models that require a weight per feature/class pair.
- Strong empirical evaluation on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks with thorough analysis and discussion of results.
- Weakness: no dynamic oracle provided for the proposed transition system; deriving one would be a valuable addition.
- The transition system seems similar to an arc-eager oracle; exploring this connection could yield insights.
- Primary contribution is argued to be demonstrating neural networks’ effectiveness for joint parsing; the new transition system is secondary.
- Related work coverage is thorough but lacks deeper comparison to other neural models, e.g., Andor (2016) global beam-search.
- Questions for authors: provide dynamic-oracle derivation and relation to arc-eager; discuss extensions (morphology, char embeddings); give in-depth comparison to Andor (2016).
