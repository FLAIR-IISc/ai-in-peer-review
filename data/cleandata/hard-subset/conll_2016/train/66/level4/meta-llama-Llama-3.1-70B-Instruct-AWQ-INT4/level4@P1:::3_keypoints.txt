- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using neural networks; joint models are promising for transition-based parsers.
- Contribution: new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008).
- Contribution: shows neural networks outperform linear models by automatically learning relevant features.
- Neural networks also offer parameter-efficiency advantages when jointly learning multiple tasks.
- The proposed transition system could be combined with other NN models (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search); many orthogonal improvements are possible.
- Future work: derive a dynamic oracle for the transition system; system appears similar to an arc-eager oracle with specific action exclusions.
- Evaluation: outperforms previous joint models and is competitive with state-of-the-art on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks.
- Design choices (greedy inference + stack LSTMs to represent algorithmic state) are well-motivated and contribute to efficiency.
- Limitation: lacks detailed analysis on predicate sense disambiguation and semantic role labeling performance.
- Limitation: could discuss broader implications for natural language processing more thoroughly.
- Strength: novel transition system that advances prior work.
- Strength: neural approach learns compact representations of algorithmic state and relevant features.
- Weakness: absence of a dynamic oracle and deeper per-task performance analysis.
- Questions to authors: provide NN architecture details (layers, vector dimensionalities, activations) and hyperparameter selection/impact.
- Questions to authors: clarify preprocessing (OOV handling, POS tags) and plans/expected impact for incorporating morphological features.
