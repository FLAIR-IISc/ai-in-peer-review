- Well-motivated problem: revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using recent neural models.  
- Contribution 1: proposes a new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008).  
- Contribution 2: shows neural networks effectively handle joint parsing where linear models struggled.  
- Neural advantage: neural nets automatically learn features and require fewer parameters than linear models for joint tasks.  
- Transition system is flexible and can be combined with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Many orthogonal improvements/extensions are possible and likely to push performance to state-of-the-art.  
- Suggestion for future work: derive a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system (possibly as appendix/follow-up).  
- Analysis note: transitions (M-S, M-D, L, R) resemble arc-eager behavior and merit further investigation to clarify exclusions and limitations.
