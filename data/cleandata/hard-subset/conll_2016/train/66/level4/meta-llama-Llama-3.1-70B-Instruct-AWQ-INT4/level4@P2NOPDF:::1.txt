This paper proposes a Stack LSTM parser that combines the joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing approach of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) with the stack LSTM syntactic parsing method of Dyer et al. (2015). The integration of these two approaches yields interesting results, particularly when compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

The paper is well-written, clearly explained, and thoroughly grounded in related work. The results are also noteworthy, and the methodology is sound, with the exception of a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings. This concern suggests that high-quality embeddings can be more informative than a sophisticated model.

The system description is clear, and the hyperparameters are well-justified. The discussion is also engaging and provides valuable insights. However, the proposed system lacks originality, as it builds upon the semi-synchronized joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing framework established by Henderson et al. several years ago, and the stack LSTM approach introduced by Dyer et al. last year.

Despite this, the creation of such a parser has been anticipated, and its development is a welcome contribution to the field. Overall, the paper's strengths outweigh its limitations, making it a valuable addition to the existing body of research.
