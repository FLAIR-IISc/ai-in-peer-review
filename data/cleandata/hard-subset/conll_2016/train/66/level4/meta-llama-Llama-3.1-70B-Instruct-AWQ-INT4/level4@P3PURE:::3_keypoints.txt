- Paper revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models.  
- Joint models are a key factor in the success of transition-based neural parsers.  
- Contribution 1: proposes a new transition system that appears to improve on Henderson (2004).  
- Contribution 2: shows neural networks effectively tackle this joint parsing problem where linear models struggled.  
- Neural networks automatically learn relevant features and are more parameter-efficient than linear models for joint learning.  
- The proposed transition system could likely integrate with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor et al. 2016 global beam-search).  
- Many orthogonal improvements could further enhance the method; extensions may reach state-of-the-art.  
- Reviewer suggests deriving a dynamic oracle for the new transition system (possibly in an appendix or follow-up).  
- Initial oracle-like observations: M-S and M-D actions exclude certain semantic arcs (buffer/start and stack/top vs buffer), while L and R exclude the reverse arc.
