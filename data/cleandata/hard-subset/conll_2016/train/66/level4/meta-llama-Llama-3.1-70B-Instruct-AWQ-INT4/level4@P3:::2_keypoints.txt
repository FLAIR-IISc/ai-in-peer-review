- Paper introduces a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs.  
- Two claimed novelties: vectorial representations for the entire parser state, and a plain greedy search algorithm.  
- Key idea: use stack LSTMs to capture large-scoped syntactic features important for SRL.  
- Tested on CoNLL-2008 and CoNLL-2009 datasets and shows competitive performance.  
- System requires no hand-crafted features and is computationally fast due to greedy decoding.  
- Strength: well-written and substantial work leveraging recent LSTM techniques.  
- Strength: clear explanation of approach and distinctions from prior work.  
- Strength: competitive results despite no manual feature engineering.  
- Weakness: greedy algorithm may limit performance and hinder direct comparison to some cited systems.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison of the transition set with Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Weakness: experimental sections (4.1/4.2) are confusing; multilingual aspects of CoNLL-2009 need clarification.  
- Question: provide a detailed comparison of the transition sets used here and in Henderson et al.  
- Question: why concatenate word predicate and its disambiguated sense—does this redundancy have justification?  
- Question: can beam-search results be provided to enable fairer comparisons with other work?  
- Additional comment: include a concise summary of transition-set differences and reorganize experiments to clarify multilingual evaluation.
