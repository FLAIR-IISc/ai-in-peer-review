- Proposes a novel joint syntactic–semantic transition-based dependency parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Two main differences from Henderson: uses vectorial representations of the entire parser state and employs a plain greedy search.  
- Leverages stack LSTMs to capture large-scoped syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–argument paths).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); results are competitive with 2008/2009 systems but slightly below more recent approaches.  
- System requires no hand-crafted features and is fast due to greedy decoding.  
- Paper is well-written and substantial, effectively applying LSTMs to the Henderson algorithm.  
- Major concern: choice of greedy algorithm may limit comparability to other works; adding beam-search results is recommended.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on A1 vs C-A1 links and a concise summary of which transitions are reused or modified from Henderson et al.  
- Section 3.3: unclear why predicate and disambiguated sense are concatenated; justification needed since senses are predicate-specific.  
- Section 4: organization about multilingual evaluation is confusing; recommend reorganizing to clearly separate CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (other languages) comparisons.
