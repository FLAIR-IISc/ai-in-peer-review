- Presents a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based parser inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).
- Claims two main differences from Henderson: vectorial representations for the entire parser state and use of a plain greedy search.
- Uses stack LSTMs to capture large-scoped syntactic features important for semantic role labeling (e.g., predicate–filler path).
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual), showing strong results versus older 2008/2009 systems but lagging behind more recent proposals.
- Highlights advantages of no hand-crafted features and fast inference due to greedy decoding.
- Paper is well-written and substantial, effectively applying LSTMs to the Henderson algorithm.
- Reviewer concerned that using a simple greedy algorithm limits comparability; beam-search results should be provided (would be easy to add).
- Section 2 needs clarification on A1 vs C-A1 links and a summary of differences between this work's transitions and Henderson et al.’s to clarify what is reused vs new.
- Section 3.3 questions the need to concatenate the word predicate with its disambiguated sense, which seems redundant.
- Section 4’s organization is confusing about multilinguality—CoNLL 2008 was English-focused, CoNLL 2009 extended to other languages.
