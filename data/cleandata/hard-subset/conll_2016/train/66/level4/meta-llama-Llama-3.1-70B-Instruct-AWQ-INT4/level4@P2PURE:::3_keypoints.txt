- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using neural-network models; joint models are crucial for transition-based parsers.  
- Two main contributions: a new transition system (improvement over Henderson 2008) and demonstration that neural nets succeed where linear models struggled.  
- Success attributed to neural nets' automatic feature learning.  
- Neural networks are more parameter-efficient than linear models for joint learning (no weight per feature/class pair).  
- The proposed transition system should be compatible with other neural architectures (e.g., Andor 2016 global beam-search).  
- Many orthogonal improvements and extensions are possible; expected to reach state-of-the-art with further work.  
- Recommended future work: derive a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system (appendix or follow-up).  
- Observed oracle-like behavior: M-S excludes semantic arcs between buffer start and semantic stack; M-D excludes arcs between stack top and buffer; L and R exclude only the reverse arc.
