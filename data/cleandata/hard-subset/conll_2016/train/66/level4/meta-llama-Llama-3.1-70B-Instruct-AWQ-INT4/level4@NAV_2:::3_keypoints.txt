- Revisits joint semantic and syntactic dependency parsing using modern neural network models; joint models are promising for transition-based parsers.
- Main contribution 1: a new transition system that improves on Henderson (2008).
- Main contribution 2: shows neural networks effectively solve this joint parsing problem where linear models struggled, partly due to automatic feature learning.
- Additional NN advantage: neural models offer parameter efficiency when jointly learning multiple tasks.
- The transition system is well-designed and could integrate with other neural models (e.g., global beam-search models).
- Many orthogonal improvements are possible and likely to yield state-of-the-art results.
- Future work: derive and evaluate a dynamic oracle for the proposed transition system.
- The transition system appears similar to an arc-eager oracle: M-S and M-D actions exclude certain semantic arcs, and L/R actions exclude reverse arcs.
- Strength: presents a well-designed transition system improving prior work.
- Strength: empirically demonstrates neural networks’ effectiveness on this problem.
- Strength: highlights parameter efficiency benefits of neural joint learning.
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of the transition system and its relation to other oracles.
- Weakness: does not derive or evaluate a dynamic oracle for the system.
- Weakness: could discuss potential orthogonal improvements and their expected impact.
- Question: please explain design decisions behind the transition system and how it improves previous work.
- Question: clarify how automatic feature learning by the neural network drives its success here.
- Question: are there plans to derive and test a dynamic oracle in future work?
