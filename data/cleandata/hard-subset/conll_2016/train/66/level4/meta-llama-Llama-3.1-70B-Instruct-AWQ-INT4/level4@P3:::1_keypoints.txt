- Presents a transition-based parser that jointly predicts syntactic and semantic dependencies using a stack LSTM to encode the full algorithm state, building on Henderson et al. and Dyer et al.  
- Applies the model to CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks and reports competitive results versus other joint systems.  
- Contribution: a joint syntactic/semantic dependency parser using stack LSTM state representations.  
- Contribution: empirical evaluation on CoNLL 2008/2009 showing competitive performance.  
- Contribution: demonstrates effective parsing without expert-crafted features.  
- Strength: clear, well-explained methodology.  
- Strength: thorough related-work discussion situating the contributions.  
- Strength: interesting and competitive experimental results.  
- Strength: informative discussion of parser strengths and limitations.  
- Weakness: limited originality since it combines prior work from Henderson et al. and Dyer et al.  
- Weakness: reliance on pre-trained Chinese embeddings may limit comparability to other systems.  
- Question: How will the parser be extended to incorporate morphological features commonly used in other systems?  
- Question: Can the authors provide results or analysis on out-of-domain performance and comparisons to other systems?  
- Question: How do the authors address concerns about the paper's originality given its reliance on existing methods?
