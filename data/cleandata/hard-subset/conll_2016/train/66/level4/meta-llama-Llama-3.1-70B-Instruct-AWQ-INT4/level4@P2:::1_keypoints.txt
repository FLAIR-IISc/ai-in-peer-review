- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the full parser state.  
- Builds on transition-based joint parsing (Henderson et al.) combined with stack LSTM architectures (Dyer et al.).  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks and achieves state-of-the-art among joint syntax–semantics models.  
- Novelty claim: applying stack LSTMs to joint syntactic/semantic transition parsing.  
- Proposes a transition-based algorithm that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results compared to prior joint systems.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive and informative.  
- Methodology and hyperparameter choices are sound and justified.  
- Discussion provides useful insights into model strengths and limitations.  
- Lacks originality since it heavily builds on Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) and Dyer et al. (2015).  
- Relies strongly on pre-trained embeddings, limiting applicability to languages or domains without such embeddings.  
- Requests more implementation details on stack LSTMs and state representation.  
- Asks how the authors will mitigate WSJ-domain overfitting.  
- Seeks comparison insights versus feature-rich or reranking state-of-the-art models.
