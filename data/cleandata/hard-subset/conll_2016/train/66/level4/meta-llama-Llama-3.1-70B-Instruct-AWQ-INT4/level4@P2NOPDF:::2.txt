**General Comments**

The paper proposes a joint syntactic and semantic transition-based dependency parser, drawing inspiration from Henderson et al. (2008). The authors highlight two primary differences between their approach and the original joint parser: the use of vectorial representations for the entire parser state, rather than just the top elements of the stack or last parser configurations, and the employment of a plain greedy search algorithm. The core idea is to leverage stack LSTMs to capture potentially large-scoped syntactic features, known to be crucial for semantic role labeling, such as the path between the predicate and the candidate role filler head. The system is evaluated on the CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual) datasets, with performance comparisons to previously reported results. Although the system performs well against 2008 and 2009 systems, it lags behind more recent proposals (as seen in Table 3). The authors emphasize that their system does not require hand-crafted features and is fast due to the simple greedy algorithm. The paper is well-written and describes a substantial amount of work, building upon the popular LSTMs and applying them to the Henderson et al. algorithm, which now appears visionary. However, I have reservations about the choice of the simple greedy algorithm, as it renders the results incomparable to some of the cited works. Providing beam-searched performance would not have required significant additional work or space.

**More Detailed Comments and Questions**

**Section 2**

A comment on the presence of both A1 and C-A1 links would enhance understanding of the paper's target task. A summary of the differences between the set of transitions used in this work and those of Henderson et al. would be beneficial. In its current form, it is challenging to distinguish between what is directly reused from Henderson et al. and what is new or slightly modified.

**Section 3.3**

The use of representations concatenating the word predicate and its disambiguated sense seems redundant, as disambiguated senses are specific to a predicate. The authors should clarify the necessity of this approach.

**Section 4**

The organization of Sections 4.1 and 4.2 is confusing regarding multilinguality. CoNLL 2008 focused on English, while CoNLL 2009 extended the shared task to a few other languages. The authors should reorganize these sections to improve clarity and consistency.
