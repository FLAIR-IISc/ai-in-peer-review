- Proposes a joint syntactic+semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs and vectorial representations of the entire parser state, with a plain greedy search.  
- Leverages stack LSTMs to capture long-range syntactic features (e.g., predicate–filler paths) important for SRL.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); competitive with 2008/2009 systems but behind more recent approaches.  
- Strength: requires no hand-crafted features and is fast due to greedy decoding.  
- Major concern: use of plain greedy search limits comparability to other work; beam-search results should be reported.  
- Reviewer notes providing beam-search performance would be relatively easy and expected.  
- Section 2 needs a clearer explanation of A1 vs C-A1 links and a summary of which transitions differ from Henderson et al.  
- Unclear delineation of what is reused from Henderson et al. versus what is new or modified.  
- Section 3.3: concatenating predicate with its disambiguated sense representation appears redundant.  
- Section 4: organization is confusing about multilinguality—clarify CoNLL 2008 (English) vs CoNLL 2009 (multilingual).  
- Overall assessment: well-written, substantial work applying LSTMs to a visionary prior algorithm, but methodological clarifications and beam-search comparisons are needed.
