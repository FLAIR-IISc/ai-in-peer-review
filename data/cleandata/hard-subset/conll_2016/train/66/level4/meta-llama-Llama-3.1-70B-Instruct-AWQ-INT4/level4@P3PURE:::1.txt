This paper presents a Stack LSTM parser that builds upon the work of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. The combination of the transition system from the former and the stack LSTM from the latter yields interesting results compared to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written, clearly explained, and thoroughly researched, with a comprehensive overview of related work. The results are also noteworthy. The methodology is sound, although I do have a minor concern regarding the Chinese embeddings, which suggests that high-quality embeddings can be more informative than a sophisticated model. The system description is clear, the hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My primary criticism is that the proposed system lacks originality, as it combines existing ideas from Henderson et al. and Dyer et al. However, I believe that this parser was a necessary development, and I am glad to see it implemented here. The work of Henderson et al. laid the foundation for semi-synchronised joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing several years ago, and Dyer et al. introduced the stack LSTM last year, so while the method itself is not new, its application in this context is still valuable.
