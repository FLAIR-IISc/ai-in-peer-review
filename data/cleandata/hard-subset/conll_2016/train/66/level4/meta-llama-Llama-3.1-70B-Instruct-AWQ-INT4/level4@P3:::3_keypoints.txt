- Presents a transition-based parser that jointly produces syntactic and semantic dependencies using stack LSTMs and greedy linear-time inference.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 and 2009 English tasks, achieving best performance among jointly learned syntax–semantics models.  
- Introduces a new transition system based on Henderson (2008) that appears to improve over the prior system.  
- Shows neural networks outperform linear models here by automatically learning effective feature representations.  
- Demonstrates the effectiveness of joint learning, reaching competitive performance with state-of-the-art systems.  
- Novel neural approach to joint semantic and syntactic parsing, a promising research direction.  
- Thorough evaluation on standard benchmarks supports the model’s effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the model and components.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the model’s limitations and failure modes.  
- Does not compare against other state-of-the-art systems using different joint parsing approaches.  
- Missing a deeper discussion of implications for downstream NLP applications.  
- Requests more training details (hyperparameters and optimization algorithm).  
- Asks how out-of-vocabulary words are handled and the impact on performance.  
- Questions about applicability to other languages/datasets and associated challenges/opportunities.  
- Overall: well-organized, thorough evaluation, and a promising contribution to joint parsing research.
