- Proposes a joint syntactic-semantic transition-based parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire parser state and a plain greedy search, inspired by Henderson et al. (2008).  
- Claims strengths: no hand-crafted features and high speed due to greedy decoding.  
- Evaluated on CoNLL 2008 (English) and CoNLL 2009 (multilingual); competitive with older systems but behind more recent methods (Table 3).  
- Paper is well-written and applies LSTMs to the Henderson algorithm effectively, but the greedy decoder choice raises comparability concerns.  
- Reviewer recommends reporting beam-search results, as adding them would be straightforward and improve comparability.  
- Section 2 needs clarification on A1 vs C-A1 links and a clear summary of differences between this work’s transitions and Henderson et al.’s to distinguish reused versus novel elements.  
- Section 3.3: concatenating predicate word with its disambiguated sense appears redundant; authors should justify this design choice.  
- Section 4: organization around multilinguality is confusing—restructure Sections 4.1/4.2 for clarity given CoNLL 2008 is English and CoNLL 2009 is multilingual.
