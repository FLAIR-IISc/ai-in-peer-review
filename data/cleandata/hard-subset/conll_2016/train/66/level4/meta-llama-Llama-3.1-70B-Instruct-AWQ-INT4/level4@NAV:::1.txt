This paper proposes a Stack LSTM parser, drawing inspiration from the works of Henderson et al. (2008, 2013) on joint syntactic/semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al. (2015) on stack LSTM syntactic parsing. By combining the transition system from the former and the stack LSTM from the latter, the authors present intriguing results in comparison to joint systems on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks.

Overall, I found this paper to be well-written, clearly explained, and thoroughly grounded in related work, with very interesting results. The methodology is robust, although I do have a minor reservation regarding the Chinese embeddings, which suggests that high-quality embeddings can be more informative than even the most sophisticated models. The system description is clear, the hyperparameters are well-justified, and the discussion is engaging.

My only criticism is that the proposed system may lack originality, as it builds upon the foundational work of Henderson et al. on semi-synchronised joint syntax-semantic transition-based parsing and Dyer et al.'s introduction of the stack LSTM. However, in my opinion, the development of such a parser was long overdue, and I appreciate the authors' efforts in bringing this concept to fruition.
