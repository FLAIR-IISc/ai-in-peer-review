- End-to-end greedy joint syntactic+semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs that outperforms prior joint systems on CoNLL 2008/2009 and is fast.

- Transition system largely follows Henderson et al. with modest extensions (buffer-copying, M-SELF, M-SWAP) and an interleaving protocol enabling linear-time greedy parsing.

- Uses four stack LSTMs (syntax S, semantics M, buffer B, action history A) and recursive composition without hand-crafted features or beam search.

- Empirical strengths: consistent gains over prior joint systems, competitive with pipelines, and strong runtime (≈178s single-core on CoNLL09 EN).

- Careful transition engineering: copying buffer items preserves syntax info for semantics; M-SELF handles NomBank self-arguments; M-SWAP supports crossing semantic arcs.

- Controlled variants (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid) provide useful analysis: Joint > Hybrid in-domain but Hybrid > Joint out-of-domain.

- Syntax-only LAS slightly surpasses Dyer et al. (2015), indicating joint modeling does not harm syntactic accuracy.

- Practicality: implementation details, hyperparameters, pretrained embeddings, and promised code release aid reproducibility.

- Limited methodological novelty: main innovations are applying stack LSTMs to an existing joint transition system and small transition tweaks.

- Reliance on greedy decoding and static supervision without beam/dynamic oracle/global normalization raises exposure-bias and robustness concerns, likely causing OOD degradation.

- Semantic oracle is imperfect (~1.5% of English sentences unreachable), but impact on training/evaluation is not quantified.

- Missing ablations on key representation choices (action-history, buffer, recursive composition, pretrained embeddings, M-SELF, copy vs move) weakens claims about which components drive gains.

- Comparability/statistical rigor issues: no significance testing or run variance; multilingual gains may be confounded by pretrained embeddings (e.g., Chinese).

- Limited error analysis (by role label, arc length, predicate type) prevents understanding where joint modeling helps or fails.

- Questions for authors: quantify oracle coverage and its effect; results with small beam/dynamic oracle; ablation impacts of representation components; sensitivity to predicate identification and breakdown of end-to-end scores; quantify copy vs move effect on accuracy/time.

- Additional requested clarifications: add statistical significance and standard deviations, clarify learning rate decay schedule, report breakdowns by semantic label/arc length/predicate POS.

- Suggestion for multilingual evaluation: include runs without pretrained embeddings and consider adding character/morphology features.

- Overall: solid, practical paper showing a fast, feature-lean greedy joint parser with strong empirical results, but limited novelty, brittle greedy training, and insufficient ablations/analysis.
