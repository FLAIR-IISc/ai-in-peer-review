- Paper proposes a greedy transition-based joint parser for syntax + SRL using stack LSTMs, interleaving transitions and training end-to-end without hand-crafted features.
- Contribution: unified greedy transition system with learned full-state representations that outperforms prior joint models without beam search.
- Contribution: synchronized syntax–semantics design over a shared buffer, new M-SELF transition for self-dependencies, and copy-vs-move design choices.
- Contribution: strong empirical results — SOTA among prior joint systems on CoNLL 2008/2009 English, competitive multilingual performance, and large speedups vs pipelines.
- Strength: principled use of stack LSTMs to summarize full parser state and replace manual long-distance features, handling asynchrony via copy and constraints.
- Strength: consistently better than older joint systems while using a simpler, faster greedy decoder with compelling runtime numbers.
- Strength: thoughtful experimental design with semantics-only, syntax-only, and hybrid baselines that elucidate when joint training helps.
- Strength: practical SRL-specific transitions (M-SELF, M-SWAP) and constraints to cover NomBank and non-projective semantics without infinite loops.
- Strength: reproducible engineering choices (pretrained embeddings, sensible hyperparams), low compute/memory footprint, and public code promised.
- Weakness: limited novelty beyond Dyer et al. (2015); core idea reuses stack LSTMs and adds task-specific transitions, so conceptual advance is incremental.
- Weakness: missing ablations for key components (action history, composition functions, embeddings, dropout, copy-vs-move) and no beam/global normalization comparisons.
- Weakness: imperfect oracle conversion (~1.5% of sentences not encodable) with no quantification of lost gold arcs or training bias.
- Weakness: signs of overfitting — hybrid beats joint on Brown OOD set — with no discussion of domain adaptation or stronger regularization.
- Weakness: limited comparison to strongest SRL systems and no significance testing or lightweight integrations (small beams, rerankers) attempted.
- Question: for the 1.5% of unencodable sentences, how many gold semantic arcs are lost and how does excluding them affect dev/test scores; can the transition set be extended?
- Question: did you try small beams or globally normalized objectives, and how much accuracy is sacrificed by pure greediness, especially OOD?
- Question: please provide ablations: remove action history, pretrained embeddings, replaced composition functions, M-SELF ablation, move vs copy, and without pseudo-projectivization.
- Question: how sensitive are results to predicate-sense backoff to “01”; did you try lemma-based or character-based sense backoff for rare predicates?
- Question: error analysis requested: main SRL error types (attachment vs labeling, long-distance vs local) and how syntax errors influence SRL.
- Question: multilingual preprocessing details (OOV, tokenization, Chinese) and whether character-level embeddings were considered to reduce pretrained vector reliance.
- Additional comment: report statistical significance for main improvements.
- Additional comment: clarify whether SRL graph acyclicity or other structural constraints are enforced during decoding.
- Additional comment: publish code, pretrained models, configs, and random seeds to improve reproducibility and impact.
