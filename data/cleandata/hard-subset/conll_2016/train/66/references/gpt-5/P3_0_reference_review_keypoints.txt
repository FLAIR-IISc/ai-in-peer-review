- Presents a greedy, transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using four stack LSTMs to learn dense parser-state representations, avoiding hand-crafted features.
- Introduces a transition system interleaving syntax and semantics, adds M-SELF for NomBank self-dependencies, and M-SWAP to handle non-projective semantic arcs.
- Claims superior CoNLL 2008/2009 English joint performance, competitive multilingual results, and fast linear-time decoding.
- Provides ablation experiments (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) and uses pretrained embeddings to scale across languages.

- Methodological clarity and novelty: compact four-stack-LSTM state representation replaces sparse engineered long-range features.
- Well-integrated composition functions for recursive syntactic and semantic fragments.
- Very fast practical parser: fully greedy inference yields strong wall-clock speed (~178s on CoNLL 2009 En).
- Thoughtful transition design addressing SRL idiosyncrasies (M-SELF, allowing multi-headed dependents, M-SWAP, interleaving with copy).
- Broad empirical evaluation and ablations across in/out-of-domain and multilingual datasets.
- Reproducible: hyperparameters, training details, embeddings specified and code promised.

- Transition system cannot encode gold joint parses for ~1.5% of CoNLL 2009 sentences, limiting oracle supervision and evaluation upper bound.
- Fully greedy decoding likely causes exposure bias and compounding errors, possibly explaining weaker out-of-domain performance; no beam, dynamic oracle, or global training explored.
- Lacks character/morphological features important for inflected languages, making multilingual comparisons to morphology-aware systems imperfect.
- Interleaving/scheduling choices (e.g., forcing semantics after S-SHIFT/RIGHT) are under-justified and unanalyzed; potential for blocking useful attachments and unclear cycle/termination guarantees.
- Missing statistical significance testing and detailed error analyses (by role, predicate type, distance, crossings); key ablations (embeddings, action-history LSTM, composition/label embeddings) are absent.
- Default handling of unseen predicate senses ("01") may distort sense accuracy; impact not analyzed.

- What proportion of gold semantic arcs/sentences are unreachable with your transition system, and what patterns cause this?
- Did you try beam search, dynamic oracle training, scheduled sampling, or globally normalized objectives, and what were speed vs accuracy trade-offs?
- Have you experimented with alternative interleaving policies for syntax/semantics, and how do they affect in/out-of-domain performance?
- Do you enforce acyclicity or other global semantic-graph constraints, and how often do cycles occur in predictions?
- What is the contribution of pretrained embeddings versus learned-only, the action-history LSTM, label embeddings, and M-SELF for nominals?
- For multilingual experiments, were external resources restricted per shared-task rules, and can you report results without pretrained embeddings for all languages?

- Overall appraisal: timely and elegant approach showing learned state representations can replace feature engineering for fast joint parsing.
- Suggested improvements: address oracle gap, add modest global training or small beam, and inject char/morph features to close gaps to feature-rich pipelines.
- Minor requests: include significance tests, qualitative error analysis, and clarify evaluation handling of M-SELF edges.
