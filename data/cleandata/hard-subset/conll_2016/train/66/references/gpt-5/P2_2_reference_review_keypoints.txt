- Presents a greedy, transition-based joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs, interleaved transitions, an M-SELF action for nominal self-arguments, and no hand-crafted features.  
- Empirically outperforms prior joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English, gives competitive multilingual results, and is much faster than strong pipelines.  
- Ablations quantify contributions of joint learning and syntax to SRL and compare syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid, and joint setups.

- Contribution: a practical, fast, feature-free joint parser encoding full algorithmic state with stack LSTMs and greedy inference.  
- Contribution: an effective interleaved transition and composition design (including M-SELF) that enables joint parsing without beam/global optimization.  
- Contribution: comprehensive experiments across CoNLL 2008/2009 (English and multilingual) with informative ablations and runtime comparisons.

- Strength: Consistent empirical gains over prior joint transition-based models, showing benefits of full-state representations.  
- Strength: Very fast and low-memory implementation, suitable for large-scale or latency-sensitive use.  
- Strength: Clear, complete methodological description (transitions, constraints, composition functions) and sensible handling of NomBank self-arguments.  
- Strength: Broad evaluation across tasks and languages with well-documented hyperparameters and training details.  
- Strength: Ablations clearly isolate the impact of joint learning and reveal realistic generalization trade-offs (joint better in-domain, hybrid better OOD).

- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—stack LSTMs and interleaving ideas are inherited; main new pieces are their combination, M-SELF, and greedy inference.  
- Weakness: Greedy inference with local decisions may cause overfitting and OOD degradation; no beam/global training experiments reported.  
- Weakness: Imperfect semantic oracle and pseudo-projectivization introduce an unquantified upper bound and training noise.  
- Weakness: Limited error/robustness analysis (arc length, label-wise, domain, sentence length) and missing ablations (pretrained embeddings, M-SELF, pseudo-projectivization), plus no variance across random restarts.  
- Weakness: Comparisons show a remaining gap to top global/reranked SRL systems and lack of morphology/character features likely hurts some languages.

- Q: What fraction of gold graphs are unencodable by the oracle for CoNLL 2008 and each 2009 language, and how much performance loss does this cause?  
- Q: How much do pretrained embeddings contribute per language (please ablate, especially Chinese)?  
- Q: What is the variance across random seeds (mean±std) for key metrics and are reported differences significant?  
- Q: Were small beams, global normalization, or dynamic oracle training tried, and how do they affect speed/accuracy?  
- Q: How often are unseen predicates defaulted to “01” at test time, and what error is attributable to predicate sense disambiguation vs argument labeling?

- Originality: Moderate—useful integration of known components with small additions.  
- Quality: Good—sound method, appropriate baselines, solid results, but missing some analyses and oracle quantification.  
- Clarity: Good—transitions, architecture, and training are well explained.  
- Significance: Good—practical and fast advancement for joint parsing compared to prior joint systems.  
- Reproducibility: Reasonable—hyperparameters/data specified and code promised, but reporting variance and oracle impact would improve it.
