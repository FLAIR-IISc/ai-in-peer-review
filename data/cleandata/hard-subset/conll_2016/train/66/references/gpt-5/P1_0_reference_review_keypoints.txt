- Presents a greedy joint syntactic + semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent full parser state and adds M-SELF for semantic self-dependencies.
- Replaces expert-crafted features and beam search with learned state representations, achieving strong empirical performance and faster runtime.
- Main claimed contributions: a practical greedy neural joint parser, an empirical study of when joint modeling helps (incl. multilingual), and a transition-system refinement plus interleaving policy.
- Strength: principled use of stack LSTMs to summarize joint state, integrating syntactic/semantic composition and action-history conditioning.
- Strength: Strong empirical results, outperforming prior joint transition-based systems and competitive with top CoNLL-2009 English systems despite greedy decoding.
- Strength: Fast and memory-light implementation (∼178s on CoNLL-2009 En test single core), appealing for practical use.
- Strength: Informative ablations (semantics-only, syntax-only, hybrid) that quantify joint learning benefits and show semantics does not harm syntax.
- Strength: Competitive multilingual performance with minimal language-specific engineering and discussion of pretrained embeddings/morphology role.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—the method adapts known ingredients (stack LSTMs, Henderson et al.’s system) with modest task-specific tweaks.
- Weakness: Uses static oracles and locally normalized greedy decoding without exploration or global training, possibly limiting performance and robustness.
- Weakness: Does not quantify oracle/upper-bound mismatches from conversion/pseudo-projectivization and their impact on attainable accuracy.
- Weakness: Comparative claims lack statistical significance reporting and timing comparisons could mix hardware/software stacks.
- Weakness: Limited deeper analysis—more error analysis and architectural ablations (action history, composition choices, interleaving) are needed.
- Question: Did you try dynamic oracles, exploration-based training, or global objectives, and would they reduce out-of-domain drop?
- Question: Can you quantify oracle-imposed upper bounds across languages and effects of pseudo-projectivization on LAS?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the interleaving policy; were alternative schedules or unified classifiers tried?
- Question: What are contributions of action-history LSTM, composition functions, and buffer copying vs. moving; any beam-search experiments for headroom?
- Question: What is variance across random seeds and are reported scores averages or single runs?
- Comment: Adding character-level/morphological features could improve non-English performance and should be discussed or briefly tested.
- Comment: Release code to aid reproducibility of timing and accuracy; clarify handling of unseen predicates and the 91.22% “01” default across languages.
- Overall: Solid engineering and strong empirical value with moderate conceptual novelty.
