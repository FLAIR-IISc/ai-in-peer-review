- Proposes a greedy, transition-based joint syntactic+semantic parser using stack LSTMs to encode full parser state and adds an M-SELF transition for nominal self-arguments.
- Empirically outperforms prior joint models on CoNLL-2008/2009 English, is competitive multilingualy, and runs much faster than pipeline baselines.
- Demonstrates learned full-state representations can replace hand-engineered global syntactic features while preserving accuracy and improving speed.

- Clear, well-motivated modeling advance: full-state stack-LSTM encoding and shared buffer with interleaving constraints are principled and coherent.
- Strong empirical validation: wins over prior joint systems, informative ablation suite (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid, joint) clarifying syntax’s utility.
- Simplicity and speed: greedy inference yields ~2.5x speedup over strong pipeline with low memory and single-thread CPU feasibility.
- Methodologically sound: transition inventory, composition functions, scoring, and training objective are clearly specified and appropriate.
- Relevant and extensible: framework can incorporate morphology, global inference, reranking, multi-resource training, and generalize to other joint tasks.

- Not SOTA in SRL: lags behind best pipelines using global features and exact inference; lacks fine-grained comparisons (per-label, long-distance).
- Limited error analysis and design ablations: missing breakdowns by predicate type/distance/crossing arcs and little probing of architectural choices.
- Oracle mismatch and learning biases: imperfect semantic oracle conversion affects training and no exploration of dynamic oracles or scheduled sampling.
- Generalization concerns and overfitting: hybrid beating joint out-of-domain suggests overfitting; lacks regularization sweeps, domain robustness tests, and significance/variance reporting.
- Multilingual setup under-specified: sensitivity to pretrained embeddings and omission of morphology analysis (important for languages like Czech) undermine diagnostic value.

- Oracle coverage: what fraction of training arcs/sentences cannot be perfectly encoded, and how does removing/approximating them affect performance?
- Dynamic oracle/training: were dynamic oracles or exploration strategies tried to mitigate exposure bias from greedy training, and with what results?
- Design ablations requested: report performance deltas for (a) action-history LSTM, (b) interleaving constraint, and (c) copy vs move behavior on the buffer.
- Pretrained embeddings: quantify per-language drop without pretrained embeddings, sensitivity to embedding size/corpus, and viability of character-based embeddings for morphology-rich languages.
- Robustness and variance: report variance across random seeds (accuracy and runtime) and statistical significance of improvements over prior joint systems.

- Overall: originality moderate; soundness good though limited by oracle issues and lack of global training; significance good for speed/accuracy trade-off.
- Clarity and reproducibility: good — method and hyperparameters described and code release promised (should include training scripts and pretrained resources).
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance for clean modeling, solid joint-system gains, and practical speed impact, with encouragement to strengthen analysis and robustness.
