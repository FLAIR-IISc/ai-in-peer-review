- Presents a fast, greedy joint syntactic+semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs and an adapted Henderson synchronized transition system.  
- Claims empirical gains over prior joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English, competitive multilingual CoNLL 2009 results, and substantially faster runtime than pipeline SRL systems.  
- Introduces a new M-SELF transition for NomBank self-arguments and a copy-based arc-eager variant plus an interleaving schedule to keep buffer items visible.  
- Empirical strength: consistent accuracy improvements over earlier joint models without hand-crafted features.  
- Engineering strength: simple greedy model with compelling single-core speed (≈178s on CoNLL 2009 English) and justified design choices for efficiency.  
- Experimental strength: clear ablations (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) that diagnose where joint learning helps and OOD behavior.  
- Multilingual strength: competitive results across languages with sensible analysis of embedding/data limitations.  
- Methodological strength: clear specification of transition system, stack-LSTM architecture, training details, and promised open-source release.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is incremental—largely combines known components with small task-specific tweaks.  
- Weakness: reliance on greedy decoding with no exploration of beams or dynamic oracles leaves potential performance headroom unquantified.  
- Weakness: lacks statistical rigor—no significance testing, confidence intervals, or variance across runs reported.  
- Weakness: acknowledged oracle mismatch and ~1.5% unreachable gold states are not analyzed for training impact or error patterns.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to SOTA due to feature differences and no strong hybrid/morphology-aware baselines for multilingual claims.  
- Key questions: sensitivity to pretrained embeddings across languages and English ablation without them.  
- Key questions: whether beam search or dynamic oracle experiments were attempted and the speed/accuracy tradeoffs.  
- Key questions: how unreachable gold states are handled in training and their contribution to SRL errors at test time.  
- Key questions: ask for an ablation quantifying the copy-versus-move arc-eager modification’s contribution.  
- Key questions: request variance across runs and significance testing versus strongest prior joint baselines.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed empirical paper with moderate novelty; leans toward acceptance if evaluation and analysis concerns are addressed.
