- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser that represents the entire parser state with stack LSTMs, removing the need for expert-crafted features.  
- Demonstrates that greedy inference can be effective for joint parsing without beam search.  
- Shows strong empirical results on CoNLL 2008/2009 English tasks and significantly faster runtime than pipeline approaches.  
- Clear technical contribution extending stack LSTMs to represent the whole parser state, building on Dyer et al. (2015).  
- Empirical comparisons and ablation studies are appropriate and informative (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid).  
- Practical efficiency advantage: ~177.6±18s to parse CoNLL 2009 English test data versus ~439.9±42s for a competing pipeline.  
- Multilingual evaluation (Chinese, Japanese, German, Spanish) shows the approach is competitive and generalizable.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental, relying heavily on prior work (Henderson et al.) with limited novelty beyond using stack LSTMs.  
- Incomplete comparison to state-of-the-art systems and lack of exploration of how to integrate expert features or optimal decoding.  
- No analysis of the specific features or linguistic phenomena the model actually learns, leaving questions about whether it generalizes or memorizes.  
- Limited comparison to recent neural joint parsing approaches (e.g., graph-based neural models), making attribution of gains unclear.  
- Request: provide detailed analysis of learned features and how they compare to expensive expert-crafted features.  
- Request: clarify which orthogonal advances (feature types, decoding techniques) would most improve the model and integration challenges.  
- Request: compare architecture and performance to Zhou and Xu (2015) and other recent neural joint parsers.  
- Request: explain reliance on pretrained embeddings for Chinese and potential impact of character-based embeddings.  
- Overall: solid, practically relevant contribution with strong results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons and feature analysis.
