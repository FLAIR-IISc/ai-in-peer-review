- Presents a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire algorithmic state and greedy inference.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance among joint models on CoNLL 2008/2009 and much faster runtime (≈177.6±18s single-core on CoNLL 2009 English).  
- Main contribution: novel neural full-state representation via stack LSTMs that removes hand-crafted features.  
- Main contribution: shows greedy parsing with full-state representations can match or beat prior beam-search joint parsers.  
- Main contribution: practical, efficient parser achieving competitive performance without expensive feature engineering.  
- Strength: full-state stack LSTM representation is a meaningful advance that reduces reliance on linguistic feature engineering.  
- Strength: demonstrable runtime efficiency and practical utility, notably faster than comparable recent work.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical comparison and useful ablation studies (Syntax-only, Semantics-only, Hybrid).  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art SRL systems and lack of detailed analysis where the model falls short.  
- Weakness: superficial treatment of why greedy inference outperforms beam search; lacks analysis of trade-offs.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of multilingual results and language-specific factors (e.g., Chinese/Japanese dependencies, low-resource issues).  
- Question: request for detailed breakdown showing exactly where the Joint model underperforms SOTA SRL systems and remedies.  
- Question: ask for explanation why the Joint model overfits WSJ while Hybrid generalizes better and how to improve generalization efficiently.  
- Question: ask how the model would perform for low-resource languages and recommended strategies when pretrained embeddings are scarce.  
- Conclusion: significant, well-executed contribution advocating full-state representations for joint parsing; recommend acceptance despite noted analysis gaps.
