- Introduces a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser that learns representations for the entire transition-based parser state using stack LSTMs.  
- Key contributions: full-state stack LSTM representation removing expert features; a greedy inference method outperforming prior joint models; a practical, faster system.

- Strong empirical results with comprehensive comparisons showing improvements on CoNLL 2008/2009 and good multilingual generalization.  
- Practical runtime advantage: ~2.5x faster than a comparable pipeline model, making it suitable for real-world NLP pipelines.  
- Clear technical presentation of the transition system, stack LSTM architecture, and composition functions, justifying the main claim.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge limitations and identify orthogonal advances for future integration.

- Lacks comparison to more recent state-of-the-art (e.g., transformer-based models from 2016–2018 onward).  
- No ablation study to isolate the impact of learning full parser-state representations versus other components.  
- Limited qualitative error analysis to explain model failure modes and differences from prior models.  
- Multilingual results are underanalyzed, especially dependency on pretrained embeddings and implications for low-resource languages.

- Questions: How does the model compare to newer transformer-based joint parsing systems?  
- Questions: Can you provide ablation studies isolating the full-state representation’s contribution?  
- Questions: What specific error types does the model make compared to previous joint parsers?  
- Questions: How can reliance on pretrained embeddings (e.g., for Chinese) be mitigated for low-resource languages?

- Overall: Solid, practically valuable contribution with strong results and speedups, but needs comparisons to recent work and deeper analyses (ablation/error/multilingual) before final publication.
