- Paper proposes a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire parser state and performs greedy inference, reporting competitive CoNLL 2008/2009 results and fast runtime.

- Main contributions: applying stack LSTMs to joint parsing for full-state representation; showing greedy inference with full-state rep can be competitive; comprehensive multilingual evaluation.

- Strength: solid methodological foundation building on Dyer et al. (2015), with a well-designed transition system and sound implementation details.

- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation across CoNLL 2008/2009 and multilingual datasets, with a convincing runtime advantage over prior work.

- Strength: authors clearly state limitations and suggest orthogonal improvements (e.g., exact inference, additional datasets).

- Weakness: novelty claim is overstated—model still uses embeddings, POS tags, and linguistic features, so “no expert-crafted features” is misleading.

- Weakness: lacks comparative analysis (error analysis and ablation studies) to show which components (full-state rep vs. greedy inference) drive improvements.

- Weakness: insufficient justification or experiments for greedy inference; no direct comparison with beam search using their model.

- Weakness: multilingual analysis is shallow—e.g., Chinese benefits from pretrained embeddings but the reasons and implications for low-resource languages are not explored.

- Question: reconcile the “no expert-crafted features” claim with using embeddings, POS tags, and other linguistic features and compare to prior feature sets.

- Question: provide ablation comparing greedy vs. beam search on this model to isolate the effect of inference strategy.

- Question: explain why the joint model overfits WSJ (in-domain) compared to Hybrid out-of-domain and how to mitigate it.

- Conclusion: a valuable, practical, and fast contribution that advances joint parsing, but novelty is limited and the paper needs stronger comparative analyses and feature-clarity to be a fuller contribution.
