- Introduces a joint syntactic-semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs to represent the entire parser state, removing hand-crafted features.
- Claims two innovations: learning whole-parser-state representations and using fully greedy inference (no beam search).
- Novel representation learning approach captures entire algorithmic state, outperforming previous joint parsing models on CoNLL 2008/2009 English.
- Highly efficient parser runtime (≈177.6±18s on CoNLL 2009 English single-core), much faster than comparable pipeline models.
- Shows joint parsing can be competitive without expensive features from full syntactic parses.
- Strong empirical results across multiple datasets with statistically organized comparisons.
- Demonstrates practical computational advantages important for real-world use.
- Lacks comparisons to more recent neural joint parsing work (post-2015), weakening the "outperforms all previous" claim.
- Provides limited analysis of why the learned representations work or which parts of parser state are most informative.
- Multilingual results are weaker than claimed; Chinese performance heavily depends on pretrained embeddings, questioning generalizability.
- No ablation study comparing greedy inference to beam search, leaving the sufficiency of greedy decoding unvalidated.
- Insufficient discussion of limitations, e.g., behavior on longer sentences or complex syntactic constructions.
- Requests from authors: compare to post-2015 neural models, provide examples/analysis of features replaced by learned representations, and report performance without pretrained embeddings for languages like Chinese.
- Overall: novel, efficient, and well-executed work recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, analysis, and limitations.
