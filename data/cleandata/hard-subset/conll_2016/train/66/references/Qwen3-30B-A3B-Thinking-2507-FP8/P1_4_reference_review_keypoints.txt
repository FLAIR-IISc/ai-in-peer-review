- New joint syntactic+semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs that represents the entire parser state, avoiding expert features and beam search.  
- Claims competitive results on CoNLL 2008/2009 English and a substantial speed advantage (≈2.5× faster).  
- Demonstrates that neural representations can replace expensive, manually designed features (e.g., semantic paths).  

- Strong empirical results with substantial speed gains (competitive accuracy; 177.6±18s on CoNLL2009 test single-core).  
- Novel architectural insight: extending stack LSTMs to learn representations for the entire parser state improves joint parsing.  
- Clear demonstration that the model learns useful features, reducing reliance on manual feature engineering.  

- Limited novelty: extension of Dyer et al. (2015) to joint parsing not clearly argued as a major advance.  
- No analysis explaining why the model trails top expert-crafted feature systems or which specific features are missing.  
- Inadequate comparison with more recent (post-2015) state-of-the-art joint parsing work.  
- Lack of detailed failure-mode/error analysis to show where the model struggles.  
- Multilingual results underexplored; heavy reliance on pretrained embeddings for Chinese is not explained or mitigated.  

- Request analysis of which expert-crafted features/capabilities the model lacks compared to top systems.  
- Please update comparisons to include post-2015 state-of-the-art approaches.  
- How can dependency on pretrained embeddings (especially for Chinese) be reduced and what is performance without them?  

- Overall: practical and efficient approach with valuable insight; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing comparisons, performance-gap explanation, and deeper error analysis.
