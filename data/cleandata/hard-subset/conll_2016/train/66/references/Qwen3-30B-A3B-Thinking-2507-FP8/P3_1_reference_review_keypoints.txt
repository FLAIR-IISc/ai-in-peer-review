- Novel joint parser using stack LSTMs to learn representations of the parser state, replacing hand-crafted features.  
- Practical greedy parsing system that is ~2.5× faster than a prior pipeline model and outperforms previous joint models.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies and evaluations across CoNLL 2008/2009 (English and multilingual) demonstrating strong empirical results.  
- Methodological contribution: learning full parser-state representations (extends Dyer et al. 2015) with evidence that syntax–semantics interaction helps.  
- Efficiency strength: parses CoNLL 2009 English test set in 177.6±18 seconds on a single core, arguing suitability for real-world use.  
- Evaluation strength: thorough multi-dataset comparisons showing better performance than earlier joint parsing models.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to top expert-crafted SRL systems and lack of analysis on why performance lags or how to combine approaches.  
- Weakness: no justification or empirical comparison for using greedy inference instead of beam search and unclear impact on performance.  
- Weakness: overstates learning of the “entire algorithmic state” since representation is limited to stacks and buffer without evidence of using additional state info.  
- Question: how could the learned representations be integrated with high-performing expert features (e.g., Lei et al. 2015) to improve results?  
- Question: please provide greedy vs. beam-search comparisons (effects on labeled attachment and semantic F1) on a subset of data.  
- Question: clarify the role of pretrained embeddings and training-data size in the multilingual results (e.g., Chinese and Japanese).  
- Overall: well-executed, solid empirical and efficiency contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing comparisons to SOTA, inference justification, and state-claim clarity.
