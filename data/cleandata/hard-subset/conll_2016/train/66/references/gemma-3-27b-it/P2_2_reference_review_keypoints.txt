- Summary: Joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs for greedy, incremental parsing, eliminating hand-crafted features and improving CoNLL 2008/2009 results with much faster runtime.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates Stack LSTMs can learn parser-state representations sufficient for joint parsing without manual features.

- Contribution 2: Shows competitive performance using a purely greedy inference approach, balancing simplicity, speed, and accuracy.

- Contribution 3: Provides strong baseline and ablation studies (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) clarifying each component’s contribution.

- Strength 1: Clear motivation and connection to prior work, arguing representation learning over pipeline limitations.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results across CoNLL datasets, outperforming prior joint models and competitive with pipeline systems, including multilingual evaluations.

- Strength 3: Efficient implementation with notably fast runtime (177.6s on CoNLL 2009 test set).

- Strength 4: Thorough experimental setup with detailed hyperparameters, training procedures, and ablations.

- Strength 5: Commitment to open-source release, aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty—Stack LSTMs themselves were introduced previously; novelty is in the application.

- Weakness 2: Comparisons to top state-of-the-art systems are limited and do not deeply explore integrating expert features or optimal decoding.

- Weakness 3: Results depend on pre-trained embeddings; limited exploration of embedding strategies or OOV handling.

- Weakness 4: Lacks detailed error analysis to identify specific failure modes (dependency types, long-range dependencies, rare senses).

- Question 1: How might the model integrate expert-crafted features or more advanced decoding to close the SOTA gap?

- Question 2: How sensitive are results to pre-trained embedding quality/size and were other initialization strategies tried?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide error analysis identifying common error patterns and challenging dependency types?

- Question 4: Were additional transition constraints considered and discarded—if so, why?

- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Additional comment 2: Example transition sequences and supplementary material aid understanding.

- Additional comment 3: Paper organization and figures are clear and informative.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contributions in applying Stack LSTMs to joint parsing and achieving efficient, competitive results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
