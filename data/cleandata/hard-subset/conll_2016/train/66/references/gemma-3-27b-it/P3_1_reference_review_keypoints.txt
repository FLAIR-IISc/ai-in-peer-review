- Summary: Joint syntactic-semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs to represent full parser state, avoiding hand-crafted features, with strong CoNLL 2008/2009 results and fast runtime.

- Contribution 1: Shows effective joint parsing without hand-crafted features via learned representations.

- Contribution 2: Extends Stack LSTMs to capture interactions between syntax and semantics in a joint parser.

- Contribution 3: Achieves efficient, competitive performance, outperforming prior joint models and running much faster than pipeline systems.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results and well-designed ablation studies that illuminate syntax–semantics interactions.

- Strength 2: Clear motivation and rationale for using Stack LSTMs over feature engineering.

- Strength 3: Thorough, detailed description of the transition system, architecture, and training, including transition constraints.

- Strength 4: Practical efficiency (fast runtime) and promise of open-source code increase usability.

- Strength 5: Comparable multilingual performance suggests cross-lingual applicability.

- Weakness 1: Core methodology is incremental rather than novel—builds on established transition-based parsing literature.

- Weakness 2: Comparisons to strongest pipeline baselines are uneven; more direct comparisons or integrations would help.

- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed error analysis to pinpoint failure modes (e.g., specific semantic role errors).

- Weakness 4: Performance appears to rely on pretrained embeddings, raising concerns about dependence on external resources.

- Weakness 5: Limited discussion of hyperparameter tuning and its influence on results.

- Question 1: Provide a detailed error analysis identifying common model failures (predicate ID, argument labeling, long-range errors).

- Question 2: Report experiments or sensitivity analysis on Stack LSTM configurations (layers, hidden size).

- Question 3: Would integration of external knowledge sources (WordNet/FrameNet) improve performance and has it been considered?

- Question 4: Have alternative decoding strategies (beam search) been explored to trade speed for accuracy?

- Question 5: How much does pseudo-projectivization preprocessing affect performance, and can non-projective dependencies be handled directly?

- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.

- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive.

- Additional comment 3: Figures illustrating the parsing process are helpful.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper demonstrating valuable progress; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses and author questions.
