- Presents a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs with greedy, incremental decoding and no hand-crafted features.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008/2009 and competitive performance with pipeline systems while maintaining fast runtime.  
- Key contribution: effective application of stack LSTMs to jointly model syntax and semantics, reducing reliance on feature engineering.  
- Key contribution: strong performance using a purely greedy decoding strategy, showing learned representations compensate for simpler inference.  
- Key contribution: practical runtime (177.6s for CoNLL 2009 English) and promise of real-world applicability.  
- Strength: strong empirical results across English and multilingual benchmarks, outperforming prior joint models.  
- Strength: clear motivation and connection to prior work, with well-explained rationale for design choices.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments including ablations (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid) and comparison to pipeline systems.  
- Strength: attention to practical reproducibility (hyperparameters, compute) and plan to open-source code.  
- Strength: solid, clear technical description of the transition-based procedure and interleaving of transitions.  
- Weakness: limited algorithmic novelty beyond Henderson et al. (2013); main innovation is representation learning.  
- Weakness: comparisons to absolute SOTA are uneven and lacking deeper analysis of why performance lags in some areas.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter search and sensitivity; more reporting needed.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed error analysis identifying problematic relations or structures.  
- Questions for authors: provide hyperparameter tuning details, evaluate limited-horizon beam search or alternatives to greedy decoding, show common error types and examples, and report any experiments with morphological features.  
- Additional notes: writing and formatting are good; include an example algorithm run (§A); Lei et al. (2015) code link is appreciated.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address limitations and add analyses.
