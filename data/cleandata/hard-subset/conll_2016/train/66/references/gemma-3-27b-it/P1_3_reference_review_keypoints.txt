- Proposes a joint syntactic-semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs and greedy decoding, avoiding hand-crafted features and beam search.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on CoNLL 2008/2009, outperforming prior joint models and matching pipeline systems while being much faster.  
- Contribution: shows a purely greedy, representation-learning approach can be competitive, challenging the need for complex decoding and feature engineering.  
- Contribution: successfully extends Stack LSTMs from syntactic to joint syntactic-semantic parsing.  
- Contribution: provides a strong, efficient empirical baseline on standard datasets for future joint parsing work.  
- Strength: the paper is clear and well-written; transition algorithm and Stack LSTM architecture are explained concisely.  
- Strength: achieves significant performance gains over previous joint models with a simple approach.  
- Strength: computationally efficient—reported runtime of 177.6s on CoNLL 2009 English test set.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation including ablation studies and multilingual results.  
- Strength: authors plan to release an open-source implementation.  
- Weakness: core transition system largely follows Henderson et al. (2013), so algorithmic novelty is limited.  
- Weakness: comparisons to state-of-the-art are uneven; some stronger pipeline systems using reranking or external data are not directly compared.  
- Weakness: paper lacks detailed error analysis to pinpoint failures of the greedy approach.  
- Weakness: heavy dependency on pretrained embeddings; performance with random or dataset-trained embeddings is not explored.  
- Question: why was this specific transition system chosen and were alternatives considered?  
- Question: can authors provide examples of typical errors and strategies to fix them?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to pretrained embedding quality; results with random or target-trained embeddings?  
- Question: in which scenarios does greedy decoding fail and how might more sophisticated decoding be added while keeping efficiency?  
- Question: can the authors give a more detailed multilingual analysis and identify language-specific challenges?  
- Additional: Table 1 notation is dense and needs clarification.  
- Additional: verify the cited runtime for Lei et al. (2015); the stated number seems low.  
- Additional: exploring morphological features and character-based embeddings is a promising future direction.  
- Recommendation: overall strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address questions and suggestions.
