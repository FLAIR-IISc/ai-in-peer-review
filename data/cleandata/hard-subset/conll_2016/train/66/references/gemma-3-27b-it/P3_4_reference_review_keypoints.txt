- Proposes a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs that avoids hand-crafted features and uses greedy interleaved incremental parsing, achieving strong CoNLL 2008/2009 results with fast runtime.

- Demonstrates that learned representations via Stack LSTMs can effectively replace engineered features for joint parsing, simplifying model design.

- Shows competitive, often state-of-the-art, performance using a purely greedy decoding strategy, highlighting practical speed advantages.

- Provides comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and baselines, demonstrating generalizability.

- Empirically outperforms previous joint parsing models on CoNLL datasets, notably without hand-crafted features.

- Achieves significant runtime efficiency (e.g., 177.6s on CoNLL 2009), making it suitable for low-latency applications.

- Presents clear, detailed methodology enabling reproducibility, including transition examples and constraint discussions.

- Situates the work well within prior literature and compares effectively to both pipeline and joint systems.

- Includes informative ablation studies (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid) and analysis of joint training benefits and component contributions.

- Core algorithmic framework (arc-eager with interleaved transitions) is largely based on prior work, so novelty mainly lies in representation learning.

- Comparison to top-performing but expensive systems (e.g., Zhao et al., Lei et al.) is not fully convincing; lacks deeper analysis of why those systems outperform this model.

- Heavy reliance on pretrained embeddings, especially multilingually, introduces dependency on external resources and lacks analysis of alternative embedding strategies.

- Possible overfitting indicated by in-domain gains versus out-of-domain (Brown) performance drop; needs deeper analysis and regularization discussion.

- Requests quantification of the impact of imperfect oracle conversion of semantic parses to transitions (how many sentences are incorrectly encoded).

- Asks for specifics on challenges to integrating path/features from top pipeline systems into the Stack LSTM framework.

- Requests detailed error analysis to identify consistently mispredicted syntactic or semantic dependencies.

- Suggests exploring other architectures (e.g., Transformers) for state representation and discussing their potential benefits/drawbacks.

- Recommends clearer annotations on the Stack LSTM figure to explain information flow.

- Suggests tightening the related work discussion to focus on the most relevant contributions.

- Overall: strong paper with valuable contribution and compelling greedy+representation-learning approach; recommend acceptance despite limited algorithmic novelty.
