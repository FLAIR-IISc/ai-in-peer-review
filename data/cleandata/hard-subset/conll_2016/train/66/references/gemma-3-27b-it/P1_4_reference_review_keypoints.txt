- Summary: Introduces a joint syntactic-semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs to learn full parser-state representations and greedy inference, showing strong CoNLL 2008/2009 English results and fast runtime.

- Contribution 1: Shows learned whole-state representations with Stack LSTMs can replace hand-crafted features for joint parsing.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates competitive performance using a purely greedy inference algorithm.

- Contribution 3: Presents a fast, practical system with significantly lower runtime (177.6s on CoNLL 2009).

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results that outperform prior joint models and compete with pipeline systems.

- Strength 2: Clear, well-motivated approach advocating fewer expert features and greedy parsing.

- Strength 3: Thorough ablation study (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid) illuminating component contributions.

- Strength 4: Practicality and reproducibility via open-source implementation and detailed experimental setup.

- Strength 5: Multilingual evaluation on CoNLL 2009 showing model adaptability.

- Weakness 1: Limited comparison to the current state-of-the-art SRL systems and lack of integration discussion.

- Weakness 2: Missing detailed error analysis to identify specific failure modes.

- Weakness 3: Simplistic handling of out-of-vocabulary predicate sense (defaulting to “01”) may bias results.

- Weakness 4: Heavy reliance on Henderson et al.’s transition system leaves open whether gains come from Stack LSTMs or transition refinements.

- Question 1: Request for a detailed comparison with current state-of-the-art SRL systems and ways to bridge performance gaps.

- Question 2: Ask for categorized failure-case examples to clarify limitations on argument types or structures.

- Question 3: Inquire about sensitivity to pre-trained word embeddings and experiments with different embeddings.

- Question 4: Question impact of imperfect oracle conversion for the arc-eager algorithm on reported results.

- Question 5: Ask whether training time is competitive compared to similar approaches.

- Additional: Presentation is clear and organized; Figure 1 is helpful; open-source reference is important.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper recommending acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, error analysis, predicate sense handling, and attribution of gains.
