- Presents a greedy, joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs that forgoes hand-crafted features.
- Achieves strong performance on CoNLL 2008/2009, outperforming prior joint models and matching pipeline systems while running much faster.

Main contributions:
- Shows effective joint parsing without extensive feature engineering, easing adaptation to new languages.
- Uses Stack LSTMs to represent full parser state rather than only stack top, improving context capture.
- Demonstrates a fast, practical parser via greedy inference with competitive empirical results.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results across multiple datasets, including multilingual benchmarks.
- Innovative feature representation using holistic Stack LSTM state vectors.
- Practical efficiency and much faster runtime than competitive pipeline systems.
- Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful diagrams.
- Commitment to open-source implementation to facilitate further research.

Weaknesses:
- Lacks detailed error analysis to identify specific failure modes or linguistic phenomena causing errors.
- Comparisons to top pipeline systems are limited; deeper trade-off analysis between speed, accuracy, and feature engineering is missing.
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter sensitivity and absence of ablation studies probing component contributions.
- Heavy reliance on pre-trained embeddings, which may limit generalization to low-resource languages.

Questions to authors:
- Can you provide examples and analysis of common errors and the linguistic phenomena involved?
- What was the hyperparameter tuning procedure and how sensitive is performance to settings?
- Have you run ablation studies to quantify the contributions of Stack LSTMs and learned embeddings?
- What is the model’s memory footprint and how does performance scale with sentence length?
- How does performance vary with training-data size across languages and what’s the minimal data needed for reasonable results?

Additional comments:
- Supplementary Figure 5 was referenced but unavailable at review time.
- Discuss limitations of greedy inference and potential gains from beam search or other decoding strategies.
- Expanding on how morphological features could be incorporated would strengthen future work.

Recommendation:
- Strong accept: solid contribution with clear results and practical benefits; addressing weaknesses would further improve the work.
