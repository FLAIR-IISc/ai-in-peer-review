## Peer Review for CoNLL Paper: Joint Syntactic and Semantic Dependency Parsing

**Summary:**

This paper presents a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser leveraging stack LSTMs to learn representations of the parser’s state, eschewing traditional expert-crafted features. The parser utilizes a greedy, incremental approach, building both syntactic and semantic dependencies simultaneously. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on the CoNLL 2008 and 2009 shared tasks, outperforming prior joint models and achieving competitive performance with pipeline-based systems, all while maintaining a relatively fast runtime.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Application of Stack LSTMs to Joint Parsing:** The successful application of stack LSTMs to jointly model syntactic and semantic dependencies is a key contribution. This demonstrates the power of representation learning in reducing reliance on hand-engineered features in a complex parsing task.
2. **Competitive Performance with a Greedy Approach:** Achieving strong performance with a purely greedy decoding approach is notable.  Most SOTA systems employ more complex decoding strategies (beam search, exact inference) indicating the model learns surprisingly effective representations.
3. **Practical Runtime:** The reported runtime of 177.6 seconds for CoNLL 2009 English is a valuable asset, potentially enabling real-world applications where speed is critical.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates clear improvements over previous joint parsing models on standard datasets.  Consistently outperforming prior joint approaches, and reaching competitiveness with pipeline approaches is a compelling result.  The comprehensive reporting across CoNLL 2008, 2009 English, and multilingual settings strengthens this claim.
2. **Clear and Well-Motivated Approach:** The authors clearly articulate the motivations behind their work – addressing the error propagation issues inherent in pipelines and the limitations of feature engineering in greedy models. The connection to previous work (Henderson et al.) is well-established and the rationale for the changes (state representation learning, greedy decoding) is well-explained.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Setup:** The experiments are well-designed, including ablation studies (Semantics-only, Syntax-only, Hybrid) that provide valuable insights into the contributions of syntactic and semantic information.  The comparison to a state-of-the-art pipeline system (Lei et al., 2015) helps contextualize the performance.
4. **Practical Considerations:**  The emphasis on runtime performance and the promise of an open-source implementation are significant strengths, increasing the potential impact of the work. The inclusion of details regarding hyperparameter tuning and computational resources facilitates reproducibility.
5. **Solid Technical Description:** The explanation of the transition-based procedure, including the interleaving of syntactic and semantic transitions, is clear and concise.  The use of Table 1 is particularly helpful in understanding the transitions.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Algorithm:** While the application of stack LSTMs is effective, the underlying transition-based parsing algorithm is largely derived from Henderson et al. (2013). The core algorithmic innovation appears limited to the representation learning aspect. This isn't a fatal flaw, but the paper could benefit from more clearly articulating the distinct contributions *beyond* the successful application of a known technique.
2. **Comparison to State-of-the-Art is Uneven:**  While comparisons are made to strong baseline systems, the authors acknowledge that their performance doesn't match the very best, which leverage features and decoding strategies not employed in this work. The discussion of these limitations could be more substantial, perhaps with a more detailed analysis of why the proposed approach falls short and potential avenues for future improvement. It's unclear whether the performance gap is due to the choice of a greedy search, missing features, or other factors.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions careful tuning of hyperparameters, but doesn’t provide extensive details on the search process.  A more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, and perhaps a table summarizing the impact of different settings, would strengthen the results.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a detailed error analysis. Understanding *where* the model struggles (e.g., specific types of semantic relations, long-distance dependencies) would provide valuable insights for future work.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process?  Specifically, what search strategies were used, and how did different settings impact performance?
2.  Can you elaborate on the limitations of the greedy decoding approach?  Have you experimented with limited-horizon beam search or other strategies to mitigate the potential drawbacks of greedy decoding?
3.  What types of errors does the model make most frequently? Is there a particular subset of semantic relations or syntactic structures that consistently pose challenges for the parser?  Can you provide examples?
4.   You mention the potential for incorporating morphological features.  Have you explored this avenue, even in a preliminary fashion? If so, what were the results?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The formatting and writing quality are generally very good.
*   The inclusion of an example run of the algorithm (referenced in §A) would be beneficial.
*  The link to the Lei et al. (2015) code is appreciated.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of joint syntactic and semantic dependency parsing. The successful application of stack LSTMs to this task, coupled with the competitive performance and practical runtime, makes this work deserving of acceptance. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly with respect to a more detailed discussion of the limitations and error analysis, would further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
