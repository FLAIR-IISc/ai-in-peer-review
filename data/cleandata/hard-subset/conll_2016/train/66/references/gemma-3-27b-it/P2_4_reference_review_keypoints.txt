- Summary: Presents a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs and greedy inference, achieving strong CoNLL 2008/2009 results and fast runtime.
- Contribution 1: Demonstrates effective use of Stack LSTMs to represent full parser state, removing need for hand-crafted features.
- Contribution 2: Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive empirical performance for joint parsing on CoNLL benchmarks.
- Contribution 3: Provides a fast, practical parser with runtime advantages over prior systems.
- Strength 1: Novel combination of Stack LSTMs and greedy inference applied to joint parsing.
- Strength 2: Strong empirical validation, outperforming previous joint models and competitive with pipelines, including multilingual experiments.
- Strength 3: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful examples and justified design choices.
- Strength 4: Practical significance due to fast runtime and planned open-source release.
- Strength 5: Thorough ablation study (syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid) illuminating component contributions.
- Weakness 1: Limited comparison to the very best baselines using optimal decoding, expert features, or external data.
- Weakness 2: Lacks detailed error analysis to identify systematic failure modes or missing information in learned representations.
- Weakness 3: Greedy inference may sacrifice accuracy; trade-offs and alternatives (e.g., beam search) are not empirically explored.
- Weakness 4: Dependence on pretrained embeddings, raising concerns for low-resource languages without high-quality embeddings.
- Question 1: How could optimal decoding, richer features, and external data be integrated with the Stack LSTM framework and what are the challenges?
- Question 2: Can the authors provide example error cases and discuss what additional information would help?
- Question 3: Have limited-beam or other non-greedy search strategies been tried, and what are the speed/accuracy trade-offs?
- Question 4: How sensitive is model performance to pretrained embedding quality, especially for low-resource languages?
- Additional comment 1: Code availability is a significant positive.
- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive and situate the work well in prior literature.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with novel, effective approach and compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing comparisons, error analysis, and inference trade-offs.
