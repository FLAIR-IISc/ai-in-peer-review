- Joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using stack LSTMs in a greedy, transition-based framework; reports strong CoNLL 2008/2009 results and fast runtime; authors plan to open-source.  
- Main contribution: effective adaptation of stack LSTMs to joint parsing, reducing hand-crafted feature needs.  
- Main contribution: strong empirical performance competitive with pipeline systems and superior to prior joint models.  
- Main contribution: fast and practical system (notably low runtime).  
- Strength: novel learned feature representations reduce reliance on expert-designed features.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including syntax-only, semantics-only, hybrid variants, and multilingual experiments.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful transition system and architecture descriptions.  
- Strength: practical impact due to speed and planned open-source release.  
- Strength: demonstrates scalability across multiple languages (CoNLL 2009).  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis to identify common syntactic/semantic failure modes.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on pre-trained word embeddings without exploring alternatives or joint learning.  
- Weakness: simplistic out-of-vocabulary handling (random initialization) may hurt morphologically rich or low-resource languages.  
- Weakness: comparisons to top systems are caveated; needs deeper discussion of accuracy vs. speed vs. complexity trade-offs.  
- Question: please provide detailed error analysis identifying common error patterns.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to choice of pre-trained embeddings; were alternatives or joint learning tried?  
- Question: what OOV handling strategies were considered beyond random initialization and why were they rejected?  
- Question: have other syntactic parsing algorithms been investigated and how might they affect joint performance?  
- Question: which specific advances from other systems would be most promising to integrate next?  
- Additional comment: "Figure 1" is referenced but missing from the provided text.  
- Additional comment: the M-SELF transition description is unclear and needs a more intuitive explanation.  
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative, and practical paper; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the above points.
