- Paper presents a joint syntactic and semantic dependency parser using Stack LSTMs to learn parser state without hand-crafted features.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2008/2009 English and competitive multilingual performance, with fast parsing speed.  
- Main contribution: effective application of Stack LSTMs to joint parsing, removing the need for extensive feature engineering.  
- Main contribution: strong empirical results on established benchmarks.  
- Main contribution: practical speed and efficiency suitable for real-world use.  
- Strength: eliminates feature engineering in a complex joint model via learned representations.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across datasets and ablation variants, with wide comparisons to prior work.  
- Strength: clear, well-written paper with example runs and detailed transition descriptions aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength: practical advantages—reported runtime is competitive and open-source release is promised.  
- Strength: authors acknowledge limitations and outline future directions.  
- Weakness: improvements over previous joint models are incremental and gap with pipeline systems isn't fully explained.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis identifying consistent error types or challenging phenomena.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning process and sensitivity (dropout, LSTM size) are underreported.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of greedy inference trade-offs and whether beam search might help.  
- Weakness: handling of unseen predicate senses by defaulting to "01" may introduce bias.  
- Question: Which specific linguistic phenomena or argument types cause the model to lag behind pipeline systems?  
- Question: What hyperparameter ranges were explored and how sensitive are results to them?  
- Question: Were beam search experiments attempted, and if not, why?  
- Question: How does performance vary across syntactic structures (e.g., projectivity, tree depth)?  
- Question: What are the biases from defaulting unseen predicates to "01" and were alternatives considered?  
- Additional comments: citation style inconsistencies and a request for a table summarizing model parameters.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed work deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses and questions.
