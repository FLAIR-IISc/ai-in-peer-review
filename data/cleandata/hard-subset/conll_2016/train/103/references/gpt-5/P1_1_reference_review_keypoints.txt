- Proposes TBuckets: group top-N topic words into semantic “buckets” using pre-trained embeddings; coherent topics should have one dominant bucket.  
- Introduces three variants: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative with MaxDist), TBuckets-SVD (assign words to top SVD directions), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (parameter-free post-hoc reorganization).  
- Coherence scored from largest bucket size and optionally weighted by word order, eigenvector similarity, and top singular value; evaluated via Pearson correlation with human ratings on NYT, 20NG, Genomics and for topic selection in weakly supervised classification.  
- Main contributions claimed: simple interpretable SVD bucketing, competitive TBuckets-SVD-Reorg correlations with humans, and some downstream classification benefit from filtering by TBuckets.  
- Strength: Conceptual simplicity and interpretability—largest bucket = dominant theme, yields human-interpretable artifacts.  
- Strength: Mostly parameter-free SVD variants, attractive compared to many hyperparameter-dependent coherence measures.  
- Strength: Competitive empirical performance—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms reported baselines on NYT and 20NG and approaches them on Genomics (correlations ~0.83–0.87).  
- Strength: Practical utility—filtering topic runs by TBuckets can improve or maintain classification performance under the same annotation budget in some cases.  
- Strength: Clear use of off-the-shelf GloVe embeddings, making approach easy to reproduce in principle and avoiding corpus-based co-occurrence counting.  
- Weakness: Novelty incremental—embedding-based coherence and pairwise cosine/NPMI baselines already exist; SVD-based idea not strongly justified relative to simpler baselines.  
- Weakness: Methodological gaps—choice of V vs U for SVD, normalization and sign handling of eigenvectors, and exact similarity computation are not explained.  
- Weakness: Reorganization rule and threshold formula are ad hoc, under-explained, possibly mis-parenthesized, with no sensitivity analysis or ablation.  
- Weakness: Weighting by the square of the top singular value lacks justification and may reward variance unrelated to semantic coherence.  
- Weakness: Evaluation omissions—no statistical significance testing for correlation differences; modest gains may be non-significant with ~100 topics.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to common coherence metrics (e.g., CV) and simple embedding baselines (pairwise cosine, k-means, spectral clustering).  
- Weakness: Mixed and inconsistent classification results (some low-coherence sets outperform high-coherence ones), with no error bars or multiple runs to support claims.  
- Weakness: Domain mismatch—using general-domain GloVe for Genomics likely hurts performance; no experiments with domain-specific embeddings or OOV handling.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues—missing details on normalization, linkage/max-dist choices, topic-word N, OOV treatment; no code/data links; incomplete references.  
- Questions for authors: Do you L2-normalize embeddings/eigenvectors and how do you handle eigenvector sign ambiguity? Why use V instead of U or PCA on words?  
- Question: Clarify exact reorganization threshold formula, provide intuition, and report ablation/sensitivity to this step.  
- Question: Compare to simple baselines (average pairwise cosine, k-means/spectral) and include CV/NPMI computed on the same corpora for fairness.  
- Question: Are gains statistically significant (e.g., Steiger’s test) and how stable across runs? What is N and how do results vary with N? How are OOV words handled and have you tried domain-specific embeddings for Genomics?  
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject—promising, interpretable idea but limited novelty, insufficient justification for key choices, missing baselines/ablation/significance tests, and mixed downstream evidence; needs stronger experiments and clearer exposition.
