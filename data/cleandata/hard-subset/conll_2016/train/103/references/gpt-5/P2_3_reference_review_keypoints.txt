- Proposes TBuckets: embedding-based topic coherence measures that form buckets of semantically related words and score topics by the size/quality of the largest bucket.  
- Presents three realizations: agglomerative clustering on embeddings, SVD-based buckets from topic embedding matrices, and a parameter-free reorganization step moving words based on dynamic thresholds.  
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human coherence judgments on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics datasets; small downstream study on weakly supervised classification.  

Contributions:
- Introduces a simple, computationally cheap, largely parameter-free coherence measure (TBuckets-SVD and -SVD-Reorg) achieving state-of-the-art or better correlations on two benchmarks.  
- Proposes a heuristic reorganization procedure that modestly improves the SVD variant without external tuning.  
- Shows that coherence-based selection of topic-model runs can sometimes substantially improve weakly supervised classification performance.  

Strengths:
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg attains higher Pearson r than prior best on NYT and 20NG using off-the-shelf embeddings and no tuning.  
- Methods are lightweight and practical (SVD on ~10×300 matrices).  
- Bucketization is intuitive and interpretable, aligning with human notions of coherence.  
- Paper includes reasonable ablations/variants and qualitative bucket examples.  
- Downstream relevance demonstrated with improvements in some macro-F1 classification tasks.  
- Uses standard datasets and public GloVe embeddings, facilitating reproducibility.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: builds on prior embedding-based coherence work and effectively measures alignment with principal components without strong new theory.  
- Lacks comparison to simple baselines (average pairwise cosine, centroid similarity, PC1 explained variance, largest connected component).  
- Reported improvements may not be statistically significant; no confidence intervals or tests for dependent correlations provided.  
- Sensitivity to top-N, embedding choice, and preprocessing not examined; domain mismatch (poor Genomics result) unexplored.  
- Reorganization step is ambiguous, ad hoc, and under-justified; formula and impact poorly validated.  
- Baseline coverage incomplete and fairness concerns (possible domain-aligned embeddings advantage).  
- Downstream study under-specified (annotator details, topic-model settings) and yields inconsistent results without statistical testing.  
- Some SVD notation/shapes and linking of singular values to scores are imprecise; minor typos and formatting issues.  

Questions for authors:
- Are the gains over Röder et al. statistically significant? Provide confidence intervals and dependent-correlation tests.  
- How does TBuckets-SVD(-Reorg) compare to average pairwise cosine, centroid similarity, PC1 explained variance, and largest connected component baselines?  
- Clarify the exact reorganization threshold formula, its rationale, sensitivity, and how often words move.  
- Do results hold with different embeddings (word2vec, fastText), varying top-N words, and in-domain embeddings for Genomics?  
- Provide full downstream study details: annotator count/IAA, K/α/β/seeds, annotation protocol, and statistical tests; can gains be replicated under controlled labeling budgets?  

Overall assessment:
- Presents a simple, fast, and interpretable approach with competitive correlations, but conceptual novelty is moderate, key baseline comparisons and statistical rigor are missing, and the reorganization step is under-explained; promising but not yet fully convincing.
