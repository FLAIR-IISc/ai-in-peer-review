Summary and contributions
This paper proposes TBuckets, a family of topic coherence measures that group the top-N words of a topic into “buckets” (thematic groups) using word embeddings. Three instantiations are explored: (i) agglomerative clustering (TBuckets-Clustering); (ii) SVD-based assignment of words to right singular vectors of the word-embedding matrix (TBuckets-SVD); and (iii) an SVD variant with an automatic reorganization step to move words into/out of the largest bucket based on adaptive similarity thresholds (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg). Topic coherence is then computed primarily from properties of the largest bucket (size, LDA-order-weighted size, and similarity to the corresponding eigenvector), with SVD-based variants additionally weighted by the square of the largest singular value. Evaluations on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics topic sets (100 topics each, top-10 words, human scores) show Pearson correlations competitive with or exceeding the best configurations reported by Röder et al. (2015) on two datasets. A small downstream study suggests that selecting topic sets by TBuckets coherence can modestly improve weakly supervised text classification.

As I see them, the main contributions are:
1) A simple, embedding-based bucketing framework for topic coherence that yields competitive human correlation, with a parameter-free SVD variant.
2) An automatic reorganization heuristic that adaptively refines SVD assignments via word–word and word–eigenvector similarities, improving correlation on some datasets.
3) A small downstream demonstration that coherence-based selection of topic sets can improve topic-labelling-based classification.

Strengths
- Intuitive, interpretable formulation: The bucketing idea matches how humans informally judge topicality—grouping semantically related words and inspecting the size of the dominant theme. This framing is easy to understand and implement with widely available embeddings.
- Competitive results with minimal tuning: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves r = 0.837 on NYT and up to 0.869 on 20NG, slightly outperforming the best Röder configurations (0.806 and 0.859, respectively), while being parameter-free. This is appealing given the heavy configuration space of prior coherence measures.
- Lightweight and practical: Computing SVD on a 10×300 matrix per topic and a few cosine computations is fast. The method’s resource requirements (pretrained embeddings; top-N topic words) are minimal and broadly available.
- Useful qualitative analyses: The paper illustrates bucket assignments and reorganization behavior with concrete topics (e.g., TV-related words where “news/fox/series” are moved into the main bucket), which clarifies the intuition behind the gains.
- Downstream signal plausibility: In a topic-labelling classification setting, selecting the top-5 runs by TBuckets coherence (from 10 runs) generally improves macro-F1 over averaging 5 runs without selection (e.g., PC-MAC 66.98→68.47; POL-SCI 95.95→96.19) and shows clear separation between “best” and “worst” sets on three of four tasks, suggesting the coherence scores carry useful information.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty and theoretical grounding: Using distributional semantics for topic coherence and clustering top-N words is not new (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson, 2013; Röder et al., 2015). The specific SVD-based assignment—using top-n right singular vectors of the word-embedding matrix as bucket identifiers—and the choice to prioritize the largest bucket are interesting but not theoretically justified. It is unclear why eigenvector directions should correspond to human-interpretable sub-themes, especially with n as small as 10.
- Evaluation scope and rigor: The improvements over Röder et al. are small (e.g., 0.859→0.869 on 20NG) and no statistical significance tests are reported. The datasets are limited (three collections, 100 topics each, top-10 words). Sensitivity to N (top words per topic), different embedding types, and random seeds is not studied. The Genomics drop (0.773→~0.69) highlights domain mismatch, but there is no experiment with domain-specific embeddings.
- Design choices appear ad hoc or under-specified: 
  - Coherence is driven by the largest bucket, which may penalize legitimately multi-faceted—but still interpretable—topics. No cluster-separation/within-cluster cohesion terms are considered.
  - The reorganization threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES) is ambiguous (missing parentheses) and may be negative; its behavior and stability are not analyzed.
  - The weighting by the square of the top singular value lacks justification; ablations isolating its effect are missing.
  - Score normalization to [0,1] for SVD-Reorg is mentioned but not specified.
- Baseline comparisons and downstream study design: The main baseline is the “best settings” reported by Röder et al. rather than re-computed baselines under the same resources; this can be unfair. In the downstream study, S2 runs LDA 10 times and selects the top 5 by TBuckets coherence, whereas S1 uses only 5 runs, conflating more runs with better selection. There is no comparison to selection by standard c_v/c_npmi, no variance bars, and one task degrades (POL-REL 85.61→85.23), suggesting results may be within noise.
- Presentation and clarity issues: There are typos and inconsistencies (e.g., an example bucket trace shows “storm, weather” where it should not), and the references contain placeholders (“?”) and incomplete fields. Several algorithmic details (normalization, pseudocode, handling OOV words) are missing.

Questions to authors
- SVD-based bucketing: Why should right singular vectors (of ATA) serve as reliable sub-theme directions with n=10 words? Did you consider clustering in the word space directly as the main method and SVD as an ablation? How sensitive are results to N (e.g., 5, 15, 20)?
- Reorganization threshold: Please clarify the exact formula (parentheses), prove or argue that it yields nontrivial thresholds across datasets, and report its empirical distribution. How sensitive are results to this step?
- Weighting by σ1^2 and EVS: What is the rationale for squaring σ1? Can you provide ablations showing the incremental gains of EVS and of σ1^2 weighting?
- Baselines and significance: Can you recompute strong baselines (e.g., c_v, c_npmi, UMass) under the same setup and report significance tests? Also report sensitivity to embedding choice (GloVe vs word2vec vs fastText) and to domain-specific embeddings for Genomics.
- Downstream experiment design: Can you (i) equalize the number of LDA runs across S1/S2, (ii) compare selection by TBuckets to selection by c_v/c_npmi, and (iii) report mean±std over multiple repetitions?

Overall, the paper presents a clear and practical idea with competitive results and an appealing parameter-free variant. However, the methodological justification is thin, the novelty over prior embedding-based coherence measures is limited, and the evaluation lacks rigor. Strengthening theory, clarifying algorithms, expanding and tightening the empirical study, and improving presentation would substantially raise the paper’s impact.
