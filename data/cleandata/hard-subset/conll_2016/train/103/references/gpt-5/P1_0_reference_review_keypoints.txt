- Paper proposes TBuckets: bucket topic words via pretrained embeddings; coherent topics form one large bucket; three implementations (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) and scoring focused on largest-bucket properties.  
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves higher Pearson correlation with human ratings than Röder et al. on NYT and 20NG, close on Genomics; shows utility for selecting topic models for annotation.  

Main contributions:
- A simple, largely parameter-free SVD bucketing method with an automatic reorganization heuristic.  
- Empirical evidence of competitive correlation with human judgments and a demo improving weakly supervised classification.  
- A coherent operationalization of the “largest-bucket” idea combining word-order and spectral weighting.  

Strengths:
- Practical, fast, and easy to deploy (only pretrained embeddings and topic word lists required).  
- Intuitive largest-bucket heuristic aligns with human topic inspection.  
- Competitive empirical results on two datasets and reasonable performance despite domain mismatch.  
- Low parameter requirements; reorg threshold computed from data.  
- Demonstrated application value for triaging topic models before annotation.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty and weak theoretical grounding for using principal directions as semantic sub-themes.  
- Many scoring variants and no statistical significance tests or prespecified primary metric; small sample size (100 topics) may make small r differences unreliable.  
- Domain sensitivity: poor Genomics performance likely due to GloVe domain mismatch, undermining “parameter-free” claim.  
- Missing/ambiguous methodological details (similarity functions, normalization, OOV handling, SVD specifics, malformed reorg formula) and unclear code availability.  
- Missing stronger embedding-based baselines (e.g., average pairwise cosine, simple clustering, embedding-based NPMI) limiting interpretability of gains.  

Questions for authors:
- Do you use cosine similarity to assign words to V columns, are V-columns normalized, and why use the first n eigenvectors instead of selecting k by variance or stability? How sensitive are results to k?  
- Clarify the exact reorganization threshold formula, its motivation, alternatives considered, typical number of moved words, and per-dataset reorg impact.  
- Provide exact clustering distance/linkage details, how MaxDist was chosen/tuned, and whether tuning used test topics.  
- How are OOV words handled and what preprocessing was applied; were domain-specific embeddings tried for Genomics and with what effect?  
- Provide confidence intervals/statistical tests for Pearson r (e.g., bootstrap) to assess significance of improvements.  
- For the classification application: how many topics/runs, annotators, labeling protocol and IAA, significance of improvements, and annotation overhead of TBuckets-based selection?
