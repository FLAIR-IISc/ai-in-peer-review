- Proposal: TBuckets groups a topic’s top-N words into “buckets” (sub-themes) and scores coherence by the dominance of a single large bucket; three variants: clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg.  
- Evaluation: Pearson correlations with human coherence ratings on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics, plus a small case study showing model selection improves weakly supervised classification.  
- Contribution 1: A simple, largely parameter-light, embedding-based bucketization approach (notably SVD/Reorg) for topic coherence estimation.  
- Contribution 2: Empirical evidence that selecting topic models by TBuckets coherence can improve downstream weakly supervised classification.  
- Contribution 3: Practical diagnostic output—bucket assignments and largest-bucket metrics—as interpretable coherence signals.  
- Strength: Competitive performance—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves high correlations (r≈0.837 NYT, r≈0.869 20NG) and beats reported baselines on 2/3 datasets.  
- Strength: Parameter-light and efficient—operates on small matrices without external co-occurrence statistics and minimal tuning.  
- Strength: Clear, intuitive heuristic (“single big bucket”) with worked examples that mirror human inspection.  
- Strength: Produces interpretable diagnostic artifacts (bucket memberships) useful for topic debugging.  
- Strength: Demonstrates downstream utility in a classification case study, improving performance on harder distinctions.  
- Weakness: SVD design choices are under-justified—using V (feature eigenvectors), choosing first n eigenvectors, and treating eigenvectors as sub-theme proxies are ad hoc.  
- Weakness: Reorganization heuristic is heuristic-heavy and insufficiently analyzed—threshold derivation, stability, negative/edge cases, and ablation for move types are missing.  
- Weakness: Comparisons may be unfair—baselines are cited from prior work rather than re-run in the same environment, and no significance tests or CIs are reported.  
- Weakness: Domain sensitivity and OOV handling are weak—performance drops on Genomics likely due to general-domain embeddings, but domain embeddings or OOV strategies weren’t tested.  
- Weakness: Multiple design choices lack ablation (N=10, weightings, singular-value weighting, similarity metrics, linkage settings), undermining the “parameter-free” claim.  
- Questions: Why use V instead of U for bucket IDs, did you try clustering U rows or selecting k by explained variance, and how sensitive are results to k?  
- Questions: What exact similarity/normalization is used between words and eigenvectors (cosine? normalized eigenvectors? centering/whitening of embeddings)?  
- Questions: Please clarify the Reorg threshold derivation, possible negative/unstable values, and provide ablations quantifying Reorg’s contribution and move types.  
- Questions: Can you re-run strong baselines (C_v/NPMI variants) on the same topic sets and provide 95% CIs or statistical tests (e.g., Steiger’s) for correlation differences?  
- Questions: Did you try domain-specific embeddings or OOV handling for Genomics, and how do embedding source/dimension affect results?  
- Questions: How robust are results to N (number of top words), alternative word-order weightings, and would Spearman correlations tell a different story than Pearson?  
- Reproducibility request: Please release code, preprocessing details, and exact topics to facilitate replication.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline reject—idea and results are promising and pragmatic, but the work needs stronger theoretical justification, thorough ablations, fair baseline re-evaluation, and domain-robust experiments.
