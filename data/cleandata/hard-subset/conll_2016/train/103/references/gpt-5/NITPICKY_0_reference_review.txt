Summary and main contributions
- Proposes TBuckets, a family of topic-coherence measures that bucket the top-N words of a topic using pretrained word embeddings, with three variants: (i) agglomerative clustering over word vectors (TBuckets-Clustering), (ii) SVD-based bucketing where right singular vectors act as “bucket identifiers” (TBuckets-SVD), and (iii) a heuristic reorganization step that moves words into/from the largest bucket using similarity-based rules (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg).
- Evaluates on the Röder et al. (2015) datasets (NYT, 20NG, Genomics; 100 topics each, top-10 words), reporting Pearson correlations with human judgments; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg exceeds the best Röder setting on NYT and 20NG and is competitive on Genomics; claims parameter-free for SVD variants. Shows a downstream use case where selecting topic-model runs by TBuckets’ scores modestly improves weakly supervised text classification in 3/4 tasks.

Strengths
- Practical, simple, and computationally light: Uses only top topic words and off-the-shelf embeddings; no external corpora or co-occurrence counting. Scales trivially since n=10 words/topic.
- Competitive empirical results: On two datasets, SVD-Reorg yields higher correlations than the best setting in Röder et al. (2015); the clustering variant matches on Genomics under a particular threshold. This suggests the “single-large-bucket” intuition captures a meaningful facet of human interpretability.
- Interpretability: The bucket assignments offer intuitive qualitative diagnostics (e.g., TV/news topic), potentially useful to practitioners inspecting topics and curating models.
- Downstream utility: The run-selection experiment demonstrates a plausible use of coherence to reduce annotator burden/noise; the gap between best and worst runs on difficult splits (e.g., PC vs MAC) is suggestive.

Weaknesses
- Methodological clarity and correctness (SVD-based bucketing). Key details are missing or questionable:
  - You construct A ∈ R^{n×d} (n words, d=300). The right singular vectors V are directions in R^d. You “use the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers” and assign each word to its most similar eigenvector. It is unclear whether you use cosine or dot-product, whether similarity is signed or absolute (negative cosines?), and why using exactly n directions (rather than rank(A) or a data-driven K) is principled. Without sign disambiguation, words aligned with −v may be penalized. The connection between principal directions of A^T A and “themes” is asserted but not justified.
  - For WL*EVS, you never define whether this is a product or another aggregation. Likewise, the rationale for “weighting with the square of the highest singular value” is not justified.
- Reorganization heuristic underspecified and error-prone:
  - The threshold formula appears malformed: “AWES − AWPSAWES” likely intends AWES − (AWPS/AWES). Please confirm and derive. No analysis of sensitivity, stability, or failure cases is provided.
  - Definitions of all similarities (cosine vs other), handling of negatives, and ties are missing. The decision rules LBPW > NBPE and OBPW > max(NBPW, NBPE) are ad hoc; no ablations quantify the contribution of reorg vs plain SVD.
  - The “parameter-free” claim is overstated: the heuristic introduces a nontrivial, dataset-dependent thresholding formula.
- Baselines and comparisons are insufficient:
  - You compare to a single “best setting” number from Röder (2015) per dataset, but skip several strong and relevant baselines: pairwise average cosine with embeddings (Aletras & Stevenson, “C_w2v”-style), c_v/c_npmi with standard settings, and other embedding-based coherence from Röder’s framework. Without these, it’s hard to assess whether TBuckets improves over simpler embedding-based measures.
  - No statistical significance testing (e.g., Fisher r-to-z) is reported; your improvements on 20NG (0.869 vs 0.859) may be within noise.
- Reproducibility gaps:
  - Preprocessing: tokenization, lowercasing, stopwords, lemmatization, and OOV handling for GloVe are unspecified; several topic words (e.g., “000”, names) may be OOV—how were they treated?
  - Clustering: distance metric (cosine vs Euclidean), exact definition of “Max Dist” (values in Table 1 parentheses—units?), and selection protocol (tuned on test labels?) are unclear. Figure 1 lacks numeric axes and ranges.
  - Normalization: You state TBuckets-SVD-Reorg scores are normalized to [0,1], but do not describe the transformation; Pearson r is scale-sensitive to non-linear transforms.
  - Code/data and parameter settings are not released; exact topic-word count is fixed at 10 without justification or analysis of N.
- Writing, formatting, and citation quality:
  - Numerous typos and inconsistencies (e.g., incorrect bucket trace “Word Seen: official. Bucket-1: {storm, weather} …”, malformed equations, inconsistent author names, missing years, dangling punctuation), which reduce confidence.
  - Equation formatting for CUMASS is garbled; multiple references have “?” instead of years; inconsistent diacritics (Röder/Röder); table layout unclear; axes and units missing in Figure 1.
- Conceptual limitations not addressed:
  - The “largest bucket” heuristic may penalize legitimate multi-faceted but still interpretable topics. No analysis of such cases, nor calibration of how often this occurs. Performance degradation on Genomics underscores domain-sensitivity of general-purpose embeddings; no mitigation beyond future work.

Questions to authors
- SVD details: Do you use cosine similarity to eigenvectors? Are cosines taken with sign or absolute value? How do you handle the sign ambiguity of singular vectors? Why exactly n eigenvectors, and what happens if rank(A) < n?
- Reorganization threshold: Is the intended formula threshold = AWES − (AWPS/AWES)? Please derive and justify this choice. How sensitive are results to this threshold and to using alternatives (e.g., fixed margins)?
- Clustering variant: What distance metric and linkage implementation were used? What are the units of “Max Dist” shown in Table 1 parentheses, and how was it selected without peeking at the test labels?
- Baselines and significance: How does TBuckets compare to (i) average pairwise cosine between topic words, (ii) c_v, c_npmi, and (iii) word2vec-based coherence variants in Röder (2015)? Are your gains statistically significant (Fisher r-to-z)?
- Preprocessing and OOV: How did you handle tokens not in GloVe, capitalization, multiword entities, numbers, and stopwords? What fraction of topic words per dataset were OOV?
- Normalization and aggregation: Precisely define WL, EVS, and WL*EVS (product or other), and the linear/nonlinear normalization to [0,1]. Why weight by the square of the top singular value?
- Classification setup: Number of topics per run, annotator protocol, inter-annotator agreement, number of labels per topic, and variance across runs? Are improvements significant?
