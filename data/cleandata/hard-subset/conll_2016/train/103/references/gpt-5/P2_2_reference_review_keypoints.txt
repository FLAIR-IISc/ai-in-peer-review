- Paper proposes TBuckets: grouping top-N topic words into semantic “buckets” via pre-trained embeddings; hypothesis is coherent topics yield one dominant bucket; three instantiations: clustering, SVD bucketing, and SVD with dynamic reorganization.
- Evaluation on NYT, 20NG, Genomics shows TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al.’s best setting on 2/3 datasets and is used to select topic runs that sometimes improve weakly supervised classification.
- Contribution 1: A simple, largely parameter-free SVD-based bucketing heuristic with dynamic reorganization that yields competitive coherence correlations.
- Contribution 2: An interpretable “largest-bucket” scoring perspective operationalizing human grouping behavior as an alternative to PMI/NPMI-style measures.
- Contribution 3: A preliminary downstream application showing coherence-based run selection can improve topic-labeled classification in some cases.
- Strength: Empirical competitiveness — TBuckets-SVD-Reorg beats Röder et al. on NYT (0.837 vs 0.806) and 20NG (0.869 vs 0.859) with consistent ablations.
- Strength: Parameter-light and efficient — avoids corpus-wide co-occurrence estimation, relies on pre-trained embeddings and SVD of a small n×d matrix.
- Strength: Interpretability — bucket outputs are easy to inspect and examples show face validity and intuitive reorganization rationale.
- Strength: Sensible failure analysis — Genomics underperformance plausibly due to domain/coverage issues with generic embeddings; authors note domain adaptation as future work.
- Strength: Downstream utility signals — selecting higher-coherence topic sets improved classification in some difficult settings.
- Weakness: Incremental novelty — embedding-based coherence is well-established; main novelty (SVD bucketing and reorg) is interesting but not major.
- Weakness: Reorganization rule under-specified and ambiguous (formula unclear, normalization/OOV/stability details missing), hurting reproducibility.
- Weakness: Statistical rigor lacking — no significance testing or confidence intervals for dependent correlations; some gains are modest.
- Weakness: Incomplete baselines and ablations — missing direct comparisons to UMass/UCI/C_v, simple embedding baselines (mean pairwise cosine, centroid), varied N, different embeddings, and weight ablations.
- Weakness: Mixed and inconsistent downstream results and limited sample size for run-selection experiments; need larger experiments and statistical testing.
- Question: Clarify exact reorganization formula (is it AWES − (AWPS/AWES) or (AWES − AWPS)/AWES?), intuition, numeric ranges, and sensitivity.
- Question: Please compare against simple embedding-based coherences like average pairwise cosine or centroid similarity as ablations.
- Question: Report sensitivity to top-N (e.g., 5/10/15/20), different pre-trained embeddings (word2vec, fastText), OOV handling, and whether domain-specific embeddings improve Genomics.
- Question: Are differences vs Röder et al. statistically significant on NYT and 20NG? Provide confidence intervals and appropriate tests for dependent correlations.
- Question: Provide implementation details: cosine computation/normalization, tie-breaking for eigenvector assignment, behavior when singular vectors collapse, and rationale for weighting by squared top singular value.
- Overall recommendation: Lean reject in current form due to moderate novelty, underspecified reorganization, incomplete baselines, and missing statistical analysis, but paper could be competitive after clarifications, stronger baselines, significance testing, and sensitivity/domain analyses.
