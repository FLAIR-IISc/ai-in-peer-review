- Proposes TBuckets: grouping top-N topic words into semantic “buckets” using pretrained embeddings to measure coherence by dominance of a single bucket.  
- Presents three variants: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg with a reorganization step.  
- Coherence scored mainly via largest-bucket properties (size, LDA rank weighting, eigenvector similarity), sometimes scaled by top singular value.  
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human ratings on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics; small demo shows selecting high-coherence topics can modestly improve weakly supervised classification.  
- Contribution: simple, parameter-light SVD framework using embeddings, competitive or better than Röder et al. (2015) on two datasets.  
- Contribution: novel SVD-based bucketing plus automatic topic-specific reorganization heuristic.  
- Contribution: small downstream application demonstrating practical utility for topic-model selection.  
- Strength: practical and lightweight—no external corpus counts or heavy tuning, easy integration and computationally cheap.  
- Strength: competitive empirical performance (r up to .837 NYT, .869 20NG) and reasonable Genomics results given domain mismatch.  
- Strength: clear intuition and interpretability—dominant-bucket mirrors human topic inspection and yields human-interpretable buckets.  
- Strength: useful downstream demo showing coherence-based filtering helps labelling-based classification workflows.  
- Weakness: SVD bucketing rationale weak—right singular vectors are not cluster centroids, assignment to axes may be unintuitive and brittle.  
- Weakness: arbitrary choice of number of candidate buckets equal to number of words risks overfragmentation.  
- Weakness: reorganization threshold formula underspecified/ambiguous and may be negative; similarity metrics and embedding normalization unclear.  
- Weakness: scaling coherence by squared largest singular value may conflate anisotropy/norm with coherence and hampers comparability across topics.  
- Weakness: evaluation lacks statistical significance testing, small sample (100 topics), and comparisons not rerun under identical preprocessing.  
- Weakness: no experiments with in-domain embeddings for Genomics to validate domain-mismatch explanation.  
- Weakness: metric focuses heavily on largest bucket and ignores full bucket-size distribution; lacks ablations isolating components.  
- Weakness: presentation and reproducibility issues—typos, unclear definitions, missing implementation details (OOV handling, normalization, clustering linkage), and unclear code availability.  
- Questions: why use right singular vectors rather than PCA/clustering/spectral methods or different vector choices, and are empirical comparisons available?  
- Questions: why set bucket count to n (number of words), observed singleton buckets, and any eigenvalue-based bucket selection tried?  
- Questions: clarify threshold expression/scale, frequency of negative values, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Questions: are embeddings L2-normalized, is cosine used consistently, and were centering/debiasing considered to mitigate hubness/anisotropy?  
- Questions: are Pearson r differences vs Röder et al. significant (Fisher z) and can you provide confidence intervals?  
- Questions: how does TBuckets perform across other topic models (NMF/neural) and different N (top 5/15/20)?  
- Suggestions: add bucket-distribution measures (normalized entropy/Gini), ablations (SVD vs SVD-Reorg, into vs from reorg, WL vs EVS, singular-value weighting), and in-domain embeddings for Genomics.  
- Suggestions: include significance testing, rerun strong Röder baseline under same preprocessing, fix typos/definitions, and release code for reproducibility.  
- Overall: interesting, practical, and interpretable approach with promising results but needs stronger SVD justification, clearer method details, and more rigorous evaluation; reviewer leans borderline accept pending improvements.
