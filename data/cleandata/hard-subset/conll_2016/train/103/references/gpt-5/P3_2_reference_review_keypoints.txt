- Paper proposes TBuckets: grouping top-N topic words into semantically related “buckets” using embeddings, with three variants (clustering, SVD bucketing, SVD with reorganization) and coherence scored from the largest bucket.
- Empirical claim: best TBuckets variant correlates with human judgments better than Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG, worse on Genomics; also used to select topic runs that modestly improve weakly-supervised classification.

Main contributions:
- A simple, largely parameter-free embedding-based coherence scoring paradigm that operationalizes human “bucketing” intuition via SVD directions and a reorganization heuristic.
- Empirical evidence of competitive or better Pearson correlations with human coherence judgments versus a strong baseline on two datasets.
- An application showing TBuckets-guided selection of topic runs can modestly improve topic-labeling-based classification.

Strengths:
- Conceptually simple and interpretable: largest-bucket-as-single-theme is easy to understand and inspect.
- Largely parameter-free SVD variants avoid global tuning and the SVD-Reorg variant performs best on two datasets.
- Competitive empirical performance on NYT and 20NG with high Pearson correlations.
- Efficient and practical: uses only top-10 words and pre-trained 300d embeddings, avoiding co-occurrence infrastructure.
- Downstream relevance: coherence-based selection shows noticeable gains in some classification settings and highlights gaps between best/worst runs.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical grounding of SVD bucketing is unclear and appears ad hoc (sign indeterminacy, choice of eigenvectors, alternative representations unexplored).
- Reorganization threshold is underspecified and ambiguous mathematically, with no sensitivity analysis or ablation isolating its effects.
- Multiple scoring variants reported and best-per-dataset picks risk post-hoc cherry-picking; no primary pre-specified metric or statistical tests provided.
- Fairness concerns in comparisons and tuning: clustering thresholds and optional singular-value weighting are tuned/selected whereas a controlled selection procedure or dev set is missing.
- Domain robustness issues: sharp performance drop on Genomics and no experiments using domain-specific embeddings to address it.
- Missing contemporary baselines: limited comparison to embedding-based coherence measures and contextual-embedding baselines.
- Reproducibility gaps: missing details on similarity metric, normalization, OOV handling, tokenization, eigenvector sign handling, and N selection.
- Downstream evaluation thin: modest/mixed gains, no significance tests, and unclear annotation protocol and generalizability.

Questions for authors:
- SVD bucketing specifics: do you use cosine similarity and absolute values for eigenvector sign; why the first n eigenvectors; have you tried U S or clustering in projected space?
- Reorganization heuristic: please clarify exact formula with parentheses, rationale, sign/non-negativity, sensitivity, and ablation of into/from moves.
- Scoring and selection: which single scoring variant is primary; were dataset-specific best variants chosen post hoc; provide statistical significance (e.g., bootstrap).
- Fairness and baselines: can you compare against standard C_v/NPMI and other embedding-based coherences under fixed configurations?
- Domain robustness: what are results on Genomics using domain-specific embeddings; do TBuckets close the gap?
- Reproducibility: how do you handle OOVs, multiword tokens, normalization, and will you release code and splits?

Overall verdict:
- Idea is appealing and results promising, but SVD justification, threshold heuristic, scoring choice, and evaluation rigor are insufficient; reviewer leans toward rejection but suggests a clear revision path for acceptance.
