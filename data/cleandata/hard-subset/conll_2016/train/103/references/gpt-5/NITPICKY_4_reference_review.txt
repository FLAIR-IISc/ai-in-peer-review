Summary and main contributions
The paper proposes TBuckets, a family of topic-coherence measures that group the top-N words of a topic into “buckets” using word embeddings. Coherence is then computed from properties of the largest bucket (size, word-order weighting, eigenvector similarity), with three instantiations:
1) TBuckets-Clustering: agglomerative clustering of word embeddings with a distance threshold.
2) TBuckets-SVD: compute SVD of the topic’s word-embedding matrix and assign words to buckets corresponding to leading eigenvectors.
3) TBuckets-SVD-Reorg: post-hoc reorganization of assignments into/from the largest bucket via a dynamic threshold based on average similarities.
They evaluate Pearson correlation with human judgments on three established datasets and provide a small downstream study selecting “high-coherence” topic sets to improve weakly supervised text classification.

As I see them, the contributions are:
- A simple, embedding-based “dominant-bucket” coherence heuristic with a parameter-free SVD variant and a dynamic reorganization step.
- Empirical comparison on standard datasets showing small improvements over a Röder et al. (2015) best configuration on 2/3 datasets.
- A preliminary demonstration that filtering topic runs by coherence can modestly improve topic-labeling-based classification.

Strengths
- Intuitive, low-overhead design. The core idea—coherence ≈ existence of a dominant semantically tight cluster among top words—is easy to understand and implement. The SVD variants do not require tuning a dedicated number of clusters.
- Competitive performance on public benchmarks. The SVD-Reorg variant attains slightly higher correlations than a strong Röder et al. baseline on NYT and 20NG. Using pre-trained embeddings lowers the bar to practical use.
- Practical downstream angle. The selection experiment for topic labeling (best vs worst coherence runs) is a reasonable and useful application-oriented sanity check, especially on difficult 20NG subsets (e.g., PC-MAC).
- Attempt at parameter avoidance. The “dynamic threshold” in SVD-Reorg is an interesting attempt to avoid manual tuning (though see concerns below).

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty and weak theoretical grounding. Clustering topic words in embedding space to assess coherence is well-trodden (e.g., distributional/co-occurrence-based coherence, embedding-based measures). The SVD variant is under-justified: using V (feature-space eigenvectors of ATA) as “bucket identifiers” is unintuitive; if sub-themes are word-level structures, U (left singular vectors) would be the natural choice. Why should cosine to a feature-space eigenvector be meaningful for word assignment? This requires justification or evidence.
- Methodological clarity gaps and questionable choices.
  - SVD details: “use the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers” (n = number of words) leads to as many buckets as words, likely inducing many singletons and making “largest bucket size” the dominant factor. No rationale provided for the choice of n or any alternative (e.g., choose k by explained variance).
  - Reorganization: The threshold formula is ambiguous (AWES − AWPSAWES likely means AWES − AWPS/AWES). You compare word-word similarities (LBPW, NBPW, OBPW) to word–eigenvector similarities (NBPE), which are not necessarily on comparable scales; the corrective threshold is ad hoc and not validated. No ablation shows the effect of this step vs alternatives (e.g., reassign by projection magnitudes).
  - Weighting by σ1^2: Multiplying topic coherence by the largest singular value is not motivated; it changes the scale across topics and can affect correlation non-trivially. Why is this desirable?
  - WL weighting 1/log(i+1) is ad hoc, discontinuous at i=0, and increases above 1 at i=1. No sensitivity analysis is provided.
- Evaluation rigor and fairness.
  - The reported gains over Röder et al. are small (e.g., 0.869 vs 0.859). No significance tests are provided for differences in correlations across only 100 topics per dataset.
  - The baseline set is limited to Röder et al.’s best; no direct comparison to other embedding-based coherence (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson; Lau et al. NPMI-embedding hybrids) is reported.
  - Genomics results are poor; no attempt to control for domain shift (e.g., in-domain embeddings) or to analyze failure cases beyond a one-line note.
- Reproducibility/experimental detail omissions.
  - Similarity metric (cosine vs Euclidean), normalization of vectors/eigenvectors, OOV handling, tokenization, number of topic words N, and exact implementation details are missing.
  - For TBuckets-Clustering, the distance metric and a principled rule for Max Dist selection are not provided; the parenthetical numbers in Table 1 seem to be thresholds but are unexplained. Figure 1 is referenced but not described in sufficient detail.
  - The combination WL*EVS is not formally defined.
  - Code/data for TBuckets are not provided, making results difficult to verify.
- Presentation and correctness issues. The paper has numerous typos and inconsistencies (e.g., in the non-coherent topic dry-run, Bucket-1 suddenly contains “storm, weather”; inconsistent author names/dates; malformed references with “?”, backslashes, and odd titles; uneven notation), which undermine confidence.

Questions to authors
- SVD choice: Why V (feature-space) instead of U (word-space) for defining sub-themes? Please report results with U-based buckets and justify your final choice empirically/theoretically.
- Exact reorganization threshold: Please give the precise formula (with parentheses) and explain why mixing word-word and word–eigenvector similarities is valid. Are all vectors L2-normalized? Are eigenvectors normalized before cosine?
- TBuckets-Clustering: What similarity/distance and linkage were used, and how was Max Dist selected in each dataset? Do the parenthetical numbers in Table 1 denote Max Dist? Are thresholds fixed across datasets?
- Score construction: How exactly is WL*EVS computed? Why multiply by σ1^2 across topics, and how does this affect correlation? Please show an ablation: size only, +WL, +EVS, with/without σ1^2, with/without Reorg.
- Statistical testing: Are the correlation improvements over Röder et al. significant? Please include confidence intervals or bootstrap tests.
- Genomics: What is OOV coverage with GloVe? Have you tried in-domain embeddings? Does this change conclusions?
- Classification experiment: How many topics per run, how many annotators, labeling protocol, and inter-annotator agreement? Is S2’s improvement robust across random seeds? Any analysis of annotation time saved?

Additional comments
- Fix the dry-run bucket error (“storm, weather” in the second example), define N explicitly, and ensure consistent dataset naming.
- Clarify whether N=10 always; discuss sensitivity to N.
- Cite and compare against embedding-based coherence beyond Röder et al.’s framework.
- Clean up references (author order, years, titles) and formatting throughout.
- Consider topics with multiple legitimate sub-themes; your “single large bucket” criterion may systematically penalize such topics—please analyze this limitation. Overall, the idea is promising but the paper, in its current form, has too many methodological ambiguities, weak justifications, missing analyses, and presentation issues to recommend acceptance.
