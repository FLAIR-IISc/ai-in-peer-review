Summary and main contributions
This paper proposes TBuckets, a family of automatic topic-coherence measures that group the top-N words of a topic into “buckets” using semantic similarity from pre-trained word embeddings. The core hypothesis is that a coherent topic yields one dominant bucket. Three variants are presented: (i) TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering with a distance threshold), (ii) TBuckets-SVD (assign words to the nearest right singular vector of the topic’s word-embedding matrix), and (iii) TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (a post-hoc reorganization step that moves words into/out of the largest bucket using a dynamically computed threshold). Coherence scores are derived primarily from the size of the largest bucket, optionally weighted by LDA word-order and eigenvector similarity. Evaluation on three 100-topic datasets (NYT, 20NG, Genomics) reports Pearson correlations with human coherence labels, with TBuckets-SVD-Reorg slightly outperforming Röder et al. (2015)’s best reported setting on 2/3 datasets. As an application, they show small improvements in weakly supervised text classification by selecting topic runs with higher TBuckets coherence.

As I see it, the contributions are:
1) A simple, embedding-based coherence heuristic based on “largest bucket” size, with SVD-derived bucket identifiers and a reorganization heuristic.
2) Empirical comparison to Röder et al. (2015) on three datasets, showing competitive or slightly improved correlations, and a small downstream use case (selecting topic runs for labeling).

Strengths
- Intuitive idea and simplicity: The “largest-bucket” intuition is clear and easy to implement. Using off-the-shelf embeddings makes the method broadly applicable, and SVD on 10×d matrices is computationally trivial.
- Competitive correlations without extensive parameter tuning: TBuckets-SVD/Reorg reaches or exceeds Röder et al. (2015)’s reported best correlations on 20NG and NYT, with very modest complexity and (claimed) few tunables. Even the clustering variant performs reasonably.
- Downstream validation: The application to weakly supervised text classification, while limited, shows that selecting topic runs by TBuckets coherence can be beneficial, especially on harder subsets (e.g., POLITICS vs SCIENCE).
- Clear contrast to classic co-occurrence-based coherence: The paper positions TBuckets as an embedding-based alternative to PMI/NPMI/LCP-style measures and cites relevant literature.

Weaknesses
- Methodological ambiguities and ad hoc design choices: The SVD mapping is not well-justified. A is n×d (n words, d=300). Assigning words to the nearest right singular vector (V) is tantamount to assigning by nearest principal axis in embedding space; this is unusual, sensitive to sign/orientation, and not clearly aligned with semantic sub-themes. The choice to use the first n right singular vectors (rather than a data-driven k or rank(A)) is arbitrary. It is unclear whether cosine is used, whether eigenvectors/word vectors are normalized, whether absolute projections are considered, and how ties/empty buckets are handled. The WL*EVS combination and “weighting by square of the largest singular value” are unexplained and risk scale sensitivity if embeddings are not normalized.
- Reorganization heuristic is unclear and under-motivated: The “dynamic threshold” formula is given as AWES − AWPSAWES (missing parentheses). If intended as AWES − (AWPS/AWES), the rationale remains opaque. The criteria use maxima (NBPW, LBPW, OBPW), which amplifies noise/outliers, and no convergence procedure, update order, or oscillation handling is described. The “parameter-free” claim is weakened by these design choices masquerading as internal parameters.
- Evaluation is thin and lacks statistical rigor: Only three datasets with 100 topics each are reported; differences vs Röder et al. are often small (e.g., 0.869 vs 0.859), and no significance testing is provided. It is unclear whether “Röder best” is recomputed under identical preprocessing or copied from their paper; the exact configuration is not named. No comparisons to other embedding-based coherence measures (e.g., pairwise cosine variants, Aletras & Stevenson’s distributional approach) are given, despite being highly relevant baselines.
- Missing implementation details and reproducibility concerns: Tokenization/normalization (e.g., stemming, multi-word expressions), OOV handling (e.g., 000), choice of similarity metric, embedding normalization, and distance metric for clustering are unspecified. For TBuckets-Clustering, the values in parentheses in Table 1 (e.g., 0.63) presumably denote the MaxDist used, but this is never explained. Code/data availability is not stated.
- Writing and formatting issues: Numerous typos, inconsistent notation, and citation errors (e.g., “Blei.,? 2012”; broken hyphenation; an obvious copy error in the bucket example “Bucket-1: {storm, weather}” under the Afghanistan topic; inconsistent section agreement “Sections 3 describes”). Table 1 mixes metrics and unexplained parentheses; Table 2 mixes normalized scores with non-normalized human scores (correlation is scale-invariant, but normalization procedure should be stated). Figure 1 is referenced but not meaningfully discussed.
- Limited generalization: Performance drops on Genomics, likely due to domain vocabulary not covered by GloVe; this limitation is acknowledged only in future work. The downstream classification gains are small and not statistically tested; no comparison against alternative run-selection heuristics (e.g., perplexity, NPMI) is provided.

Questions to authors
- SVD mapping details: Do you L2-normalize word vectors before SVD? Are right singular vectors compared via cosine similarity? Do you use absolute projections (to avoid sign issues)? Why choose the first n right singular vectors rather than rank(A) or a data-driven k?
- Reorganization details: Please clarify the exact threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES?). How are AWES and AWPS computed (cosine on normalized vectors?) Do you update words synchronously or sequentially, and do you iterate to convergence? How do you prevent oscillations?
- Scoring: Why multiply by the square of the top singular value? How sensitive are results to this choice? For WL*EVS, is it a product or a weighted sum? How are these scores normalized before correlation?
- Clustering baseline: What distance metric and vector normalization are used? What do the parentheses in Table 1 denote? How was MaxDist selected per dataset?
- Baselines and significance: Did you recompute Röder et al. (2015)’s best configuration under your preprocessing? Please report which configuration it is. Provide statistical tests for r differences and compare against embedding-based pairwise-cosine coherence baselines.
- Preprocessing/OOV: How do you handle tokens absent from GloVe (e.g., numbers, domain terms)? Are multiword expressions or stemming used in topic models, and how are they mapped to embeddings?
- Reproducibility: Will you release code and exact topic lists used for evaluation?
