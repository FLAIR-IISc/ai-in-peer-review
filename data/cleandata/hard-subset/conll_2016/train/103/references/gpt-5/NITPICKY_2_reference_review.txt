Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper proposes TBuckets, a family of topic-coherence measures that group the top-N words of a topic into “buckets” using semantic similarity from pre-trained word embeddings. Three variants are presented: (i) agglomerative clustering with a distance threshold (TBuckets-Clustering), (ii) SVD-based bucketing by assigning words to the most similar right-singular vectors (TBuckets-SVD), and (iii) SVD plus an automatic reorganization step that moves words into/from the largest bucket based on several similarity-based heuristics (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg).
- The authors compute topic-coherence scores from properties of the largest bucket (size, position-weighted size, and similarity to eigenvectors, optionally weighted by the top singular value), and evaluate by Pearson correlation with human judgments on three standard datasets (NYT, 20NG, Genomics). They report improvements over Röder et al. (2015) on 2/3 datasets.
- As an application, they show that selecting topic sets with higher TBuckets coherence before human labeling can improve weakly supervised text classification.

Strengths
- Intuitive, human-aligned idea: The “bucket” metaphor reflects how humans often assess topic coherence (grouping semantically related words). The focus on the largest bucket as a proxy for a dominant theme is simple and compelling.
- Competitive empirical results with low tuning: The SVD-based variants achieve equal or better Pearson r than Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG (e.g., up to 0.837 vs. 0.806 on NYT and 0.869 vs. 0.859 on 20NG), while claiming to be largely parameter-free. This is notable given the popularity of NPMI-based measures.
- Reorganization step addresses a known failure mode: The SVD-Reorg method explicitly corrects for words that should belong in/out of the largest bucket. The idea of dynamic, per-topic thresholds derived from topic-internal statistics is interesting and could reduce manual tuning.
- Practical downstream motivation: The classification experiment illustrates a plausible end-to-end benefit of automatically filtering low-coherence topic sets prior to annotation, with improvements on 3/4 binary 20NG tasks (albeit modest on two of them).

Weaknesses
- Methodological clarity and correctness issues: Several core choices are under-specified or questionable.
  - Similarity measures are not consistently defined. You mention cosine as suitable, but do not state definitively which similarity is used in each step (clustering, SVD assignment, NBPW/OBPW/LBPW computations).
  - For SVD, you take the first n right singular vectors (columns of V) as bucket identifiers, where n is the number of topic words (typically 10). This is an arbitrary design choice; there is no justification versus, e.g., choosing k by explained variance. Additionally, right singular vectors are feature-space directions; mapping words to “closest eigenvector” is unusual and sign-ambiguous for cosine similarity.
  - The reorganization threshold formula “AWES − AWPSAWES” is ambiguous (likely AWES − AWPS/AWES). The text refers to a “ratio” and a “subtraction” to correct ranges but provides no derivation or sensitivity analysis.
  - You weight scores by the square of the top singular value, but give no rationale beyond intuition. An ablation is needed; such a weighting can change rankings even though Pearson correlation is scale-invariant within a topic.
- Evaluation and fairness concerns:
  - No statistical significance testing is reported. Most gains over Röder et al. (2015) are small (e.g., 0.869 vs 0.859), and without confidence intervals or tests it is hard to assess robustness.
  - TBuckets-Clustering requires a distance threshold and you vary it per dataset (values in parentheses). It is not clear whether these were tuned on the test sets, which would inflate results. A clear train/dev/test protocol is missing.
  - Only one embedding model (GloVe 300d) is used. Results on Genomics are markedly worse (≈0.68 vs 0.773). This undermines the generality claim; domain-mismatch is the likely culprit, but the paper stops at acknowledging this in future work rather than testing domain-specific embeddings.
  - Sensitivity to the number of topic words N is not explored. You fix N=10 (as in Röder et al.) but TBuckets’ bucket-size logic may be sensitive to N.
- Errors and inconsistencies that reduce confidence:
  - In the “non-coherent topic” dry run, the line “Word Seen: official. Bucket-1: {storm, weather}” is clearly a copy-paste error and breaks the example.
  - The threshold formula notation is malformed. Several variable names (NBPW vs NBPE) and their use in inequalities could be more carefully defined; currently, it is easy to misinterpret.
  - Table/reporting inconsistencies: TBuckets-SVD vs SVD-Reorg numbers are close and sometimes cross; the text claims parameter-free methods outperform SOTA, but Genomics is substantially worse. The claims should be tempered and better qualified.
- Downstream study design is weak:
  - In S2 you run 10 topic modelings but annotate only the best 5 by TBuckets. This increases compute/selection compared to S1 (5 runs, annotate all) and confounds gains with selection bias; a fair baseline would select 5 random out of 10, or the top 5 by held-out likelihood, etc.
  - Improvements are small for two tasks (PC-MAC +1.5, MED-SPACE +0.16) and mixed for MED-SPACE (lowest-coherence sets outperform highest). No significance testing or annotator agreement is provided.

Questions to authors
- Please precisely define the similarity used in each step (clustering linkage metric and distance, SVD word-to-eigenvector similarity, NBPW/LBPW/OBPW/NBPE). Are vectors normalized?
- Clarify the reorganization threshold formula: is it AWES − (AWPS/AWES)? Provide justification, ranges, and a sensitivity/ablation showing impact versus simpler fixed thresholds.
- Why use the first n right singular vectors (V) as bucket identifiers? Did you try U (word-space) or selecting k based on explained variance? How do you handle eigenvector sign?
- How do you handle OOV topic words in GloVe (e.g., numerics like “000”, proper nouns, misspellings)? What fraction of topic words across datasets were OOV?
- For TBuckets-Clustering, how were Max Dist values chosen (numbers in parentheses)? Was there a dev set, or were thresholds tuned on the evaluation topics?
- Did you test alternative embeddings (word2vec, fastText, domain-specific embeddings for Genomics) or contextual embeddings (static averages)? If not, can you at least report Genomics with domain embeddings?
- Please fix the erroneous dry-run example (“storm, weather” under the Afghanistan topic) and provide a reproducible, unambiguous algorithmic description of SVD-Reorg, including pseudocode and all constants.
- Provide statistical significance (e.g., bootstrap) for Pearson r differences, and an ablation of the sigma1^2 weighting and WL vs EVS components. Also report Spearman correlation for robustness.
- For the classification study, control for selection bias by comparing “top 5 of 10 by TBuckets” with “random 5 of 10” and report annotation effort/time and inter-annotator agreement.

Overall, while the idea is appealing and results are promising on two datasets, the methodological ambiguities, evaluation fairness issues, errors in examples, and lack of robust analysis make the work not yet ready. I encourage a revision addressing the above.
