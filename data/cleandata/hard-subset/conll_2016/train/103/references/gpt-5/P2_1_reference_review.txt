Summary
The paper proposes TBuckets, a family of topic coherence measures that group the top-N words of a topic into “buckets” using pre-trained word embeddings. Three variants are presented: agglomerative clustering (TBuckets-Clustering), an SVD-based assignment to right singular vectors (TBuckets-SVD), and a parameter-free reorganization heuristic on top of SVD (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg). Coherence is computed primarily from properties of the largest bucket (size, LDA-order weighting, eigenvector similarity), optionally weighted by the square of the top singular value. On three datasets with human coherence annotations (NYT, 20NG, Genomics), TBuckets-SVD-Reorg reportedly outperforms the best settings from Röder et al. (2015) on 2/3 datasets, and a small application study shows that filtering topic-model runs by TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification in most settings.

Main contributions (as I see them)
1) A simple, embedding-driven method to estimate topic coherence via bucketing, including an SVD-based, nominally parameter-free variant that aligns words to orthogonal directions as “sub-themes” and scores the largest bucket.
2) An empirical comparison showing competitive or better correlations with human judgments than prior state-of-the-art configurations on two datasets, and a small downstream application demonstrating potential utility for selecting topic-model runs for labeling.
3) A reorganization heuristic that moves words into/out of the largest bucket using a dynamic threshold derived from word–word and word–eigenvector similarities.

Strengths
- Intuitive formulation aligned with how humans group words. The “largest coherent theme” intuition is clear and the use of off-the-shelf embeddings avoids expensive corpus-specific co-occurrence statistics.
- Competitive empirical results. On NYT and 20NG, the reported Pearson correlations exceed the best from Röder et al. (2015), despite the SVD variants requiring no tuning; TBuckets-Clustering performs reasonably with only one parameter.
- Practicality. The methods are easy to implement, do not require external reference corpora or large co-occurrence computations, and the downstream classification study suggests the scores can help prioritize topic-model runs for annotation.
- Parameter economy. The SVD-based variants are effectively parameter-free, which is attractive given the heavy configuration space in prior work (e.g., Röder’s framework).

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to embedding-based coherence. Prior work already uses distributional semantics/embeddings for coherence (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson, Lau et al.). TBuckets largely reduces to geometric clustering/PCA over word embeddings and measuring dominance of the largest group. The use of right singular vectors as bucket directions is not theoretically justified, and could be approximated by simpler baselines (e.g., k-means, average pairwise cosine).
- Methodological clarity and reproducibility gaps. Key details are missing: whether embeddings/eigenvectors are centered or L2-normalized, whether cosine or dot-product is used, why the first n right singular vectors are appropriate as bucket identifiers, tie-breaking rules, and computational complexity. The reorganization threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES) lacks derivation and sensitivity analysis; mixing word–word and word–eigenvector similarities necessitates clearer normalization. No code or precise hyperparameters are provided.
- Evaluation shortcomings. The comparison to Röder et al. uses “best-performing settings” without clarifying whether those numbers were recomputed under the exact same topics/corpora; no statistical significance tests or confidence intervals are provided, and several deltas are modest. Baselines are incomplete: missing simple embedding baselines (e.g., mean pairwise cosine, centroid similarity), k-means cluster-based measures, and more recent coherence variants. The poor Genomics results underscore domain mismatch, but no in-domain embeddings or OOV handling are explored.
- Scoring design may reward single-theme dominance and penalize legitimate multi-faceted topics. Using only the largest bucket (and further up-weighting by the top singular value squared) could systematically favor narrow topics while underestimating interpretable but broader topics. There is limited qualitative error analysis to assess such failure modes.
- Presentation issues. There are several typos and inconsistencies (e.g., incorrect bucket contents in the step-by-step example), incomplete citations, and unclear tables/figures, which hinder clarity and reproducibility.

Questions to authors
- SVD pipeline: Do you center embeddings before SVD? Are word and eigenvector vectors L2-normalized, and is cosine or dot similarity used for assignment? Why choose the first n right singular vectors from V rather than using U (which directly involves the words) or principal components after centering?
- Reorganization heuristic: Please justify the threshold formula theoretically or empirically. Provide ablations: no reorg vs reorg; fixed zero threshold vs dynamic threshold; effect of each term (WL, EVS, singular value weighting). Are gains statistically significant?
- Baselines: How does TBuckets compare to simple baselines such as average pairwise cosine among topic words, cosine to topic centroid, and k-means cluster size-based metrics? Can you include comparisons to widely used embedding-based coherence measures beyond those in Röder’s space?
- Embedding robustness: How sensitive are results to the choice of embeddings (word2vec, fastText, in-domain embeddings) and OOV handling? For Genomics, do in-domain embeddings close the gap with Röder et al.?
- Classification study: Please report inter-annotator agreement, annotation time saved, and statistical significance of F1 differences. Since S2 annotates 5 of 10 runs vs S1 annotating 5 runs, what is the net labeling effort difference? Could the gains stem from selecting runs with more single-theme topics (which your metric favors)?
- Reproducibility: Will you release code, the exact topic lists used, and scripts to compute the reported correlations?

Overall assessment
The paper proposes a simple and practical embedding-based coherence family with competitive results and a clear intuition. However, the methodological novelty is modest, and important details and baselines are missing, making it difficult to judge robustness and significance. Strengthening the empirical comparison, clarifying the SVD/reorg mechanics, adding ablations and significance tests, and improving presentation would substantially improve the work.
