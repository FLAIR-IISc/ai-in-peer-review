- Proposes TBuckets: grouping top-N topic words into buckets via distributional semantics; coherent topics should have a dominant bucket.  
- Introduces three instantiations: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative on embeddings), TBuckets-SVD (assign by principal directions), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (heuristic dynamic-threshold reorganization).  
- Evaluates Pearson correlation with human ratings on 20NG, NYT, Genomics and reports improvements over Röder et al. (2015) on 2/3 datasets; shows a modest downstream gain for selecting topic runs in weakly supervised classification.  
- Claims contribution: simple embedding-based bucketing emphasizing largest-bucket size/quality as coherence proxy.  
- Claims contribution: SVD-based, largely parameter-free variant with a reorganization heuristic.  
- Claims contribution: empirical study against a popular benchmark and a small application to classification.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple and interpretable—bucket lens aligns with human topic reasoning and largest bucket is an intuitive coherence proxy.  
- Strength: Computationally efficient and easy to reproduce using standard GloVe embeddings without external co-occurrence corpora.  
- Strength: Competitive empirical results—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg matches or exceeds prior bests on NYT and 20NG; clustering is on par for Genomics.  
- Strength: Demonstrates downstream utility—coherence-based run selection improves or matches classification in most subsets and shows clear best-vs-worst gaps on hard tasks.  
- Strength: SVD-based variants require no manual per-dataset tuning.  
- Weakness: SVD bucketing lacks rigorous justification—ignores sign/magnitude of loadings, orthogonal axes may not map to semantic sub-themes, and choice of axes count is arbitrary.  
- Weakness: Reorganization heuristic is ad hoc and underspecified (ambiguous formula, uncalibrated scales, no sensitivity analysis or ablations).  
- Weakness: Comparisons missing modern baselines recomputed on the same data (e.g., C_v, C_npmi, embedding-based measures); reported gains are small and lack confidence intervals/significance testing.  
- Weakness: Possible leakage in clustering—Max Dist values may be tuned on test labels without held-out tuning, inflating performance.  
- Weakness: Domain dependence and OOV handling underreported—Genomics underperforms, but OOV treatment, token normalization, and domain-specific embeddings aren’t detailed.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation breadth—only Pearson r reported, no Spearman, no per-topic error analysis, limited model and top-N robustness checks.  
- Weakness: Text classification study lacks experimental controls and statistical tests; unclear annotation effort, topic counts labeled, and inter-annotator agreement.  
- Question: Clarify SVD assignment—do you use cosine similarity to V columns, how handle axis sign/negative loadings, and why pick first n axes instead of tuning k or using explained variance?  
- Question: Clarify reorganization threshold formula, its range, sensitivity, and ablation results for into-only/from-only/no-threshold.  
- Question: Did you re-compute C_v, C_npmi, and embedding-based baselines on the same topics/corpora, and can you add confidence intervals/significance tests?  
- Question: Were clustering Max Dist values chosen on a held-out dev set or optimized on test labels; can you re-run with proper tuning separation?  
- Question: Report embedding coverage/OOV rates per dataset, how OOVs handled, and results with domain-specific embeddings for Genomics.  
- Question: How do results vary with different top-N (e.g., 5/15/20) and do Spearman correlations agree with Pearson findings?  
- Additional comment: Consider evaluating contextualized-type embeddings (e.g., SIF or BERT-aggregates) versus GloVe.  
- Additional comment: Provide code, data splits, and fix typos/inconsistencies to aid reproducibility.
