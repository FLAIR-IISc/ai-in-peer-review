- Proposes TBuckets: grouping top-N topic words into embedding-based “buckets” and scoring primarily the largest bucket; three variants (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).  
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg reportedly outperforms prior best settings on 2/3 datasets (NYT, 20NG) and helps select topic-model runs for weakly supervised classification.  
- Main contribution: a simple, embedding-driven coherence estimator with an SVD-based, largely parameter-free variant that treats singular vectors as sub-theme directions.  
- Empirical contribution: competitive correlations with human judgments on two datasets and a small downstream classification application.  
- Method contribution: a reorganization heuristic that moves words into/out of the largest bucket using a dynamic threshold based on word–word and word–eigenvector similarities.  
- Strength: Intuitive formulation matching human grouping and uses off-the-shelf embeddings, avoiding heavy co-occurrence stats.  
- Strength: Competitive empirical results on NYT and 20NG, with SVD variants needing little/no tuning.  
- Strength: Practical and easy-to-implement methods that don’t require external reference corpora.  
- Strength: Parameter economy—SVD variants are effectively parameter-free compared to prior heavy configuration spaces.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—similar to existing embedding-based coherence and reducible to geometric clustering/PCA; right singular vectors lack theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: Missing methodological details (centering/normalization, cosine vs dot, tie-breaking, complexity) hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Reorganization threshold (AWES − AWPS/AWES) is unexplained and lacks sensitivity analysis or normalization justification.  
- Weakness: Evaluation issues—unclear if comparisons to Röder et al. use identical topics/corpora, no significance tests or confidence intervals, and some improvements are small.  
- Weakness: Incomplete baselines—omits simple embedding baselines (mean pairwise cosine, centroid similarity), k-means baselines, and recent coherence measures.  
- Weakness: Domain mismatch on Genomics not addressed—no in-domain embeddings or OOV handling explored.  
- Weakness: Scoring favors single-theme dominance and may penalize genuinely multi-faceted but interpretable topics; limited qualitative error analysis.  
- Weakness: Presentation problems—typos, inconsistencies, incomplete citations, and unclear figures/tables.  
- Question: Do you center embeddings before SVD, L2-normalize words/eigenvectors, and use cosine or dot similarity; why use V (right singular vectors) instead of U or centered PCA?  
- Question: Please justify and ablate the reorganization heuristic (no reorg vs reorg, fixed vs dynamic threshold, term-level effects) and report significance.  
- Question: How does TBuckets compare to simple baselines (average pairwise cosine, centroid similarity, k-means) and other embedding-based coherence measures?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding choice (word2vec, fastText, in-domain) and OOV handling; do in-domain embeddings help Genomics?  
- Question: In the classification study, report inter-annotator agreement, annotation time saved, statistical significance of F1 differences, and net labeling effort.  
- Question: Will you release code, exact topic lists, and scripts to reproduce reported correlations?  
- Overall: A simple, practical embedding-based coherence family with competitive results, but modest novelty, missing details, incomplete baselines, and insufficient analysis of robustness and significance.
