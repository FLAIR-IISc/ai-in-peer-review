- Proposes TBuckets, a family of topic-coherence measures that group top topic words into semantic “buckets” with three variants: Clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg.  
- Computes coherence primarily from the largest bucket’s size and optional weightings (LDA word order, eigenvector similarity) with SVD variants scaled by the top singular value squared.  
- Reports correlations with human coherence ratings on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics, outperforming Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG but underperforming on Genomics.  
- Shows a small application where selecting topic-model runs by TBuckets modestly improves weakly supervised text classification in some subsets.  
- Contribution: a simple embedding-based operationalization of “single-theme dominance” via largest-bucket size, including a parameter-light SVD variant.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence of good correlation with human ratings on two datasets and utility for selecting models for human labeling.  
- Contribution: a reorganization heuristic to correct SVD assignment mismatches without manual tuning.  
- Strength: conceptually simple and intuitive—largest semantically coherent group as a proxy for topic coherence.  
- Strength: practical and efficient—uses off-the-shelf embeddings, small SVDs, minimal hyperparameters, and no PMI/external corpora.  
- Strength: competitive empirical results, with TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieving higher Pearson r on NYT and 20NG than reported baselines.  
- Strength: cross-task utility—coherence-based run selection can improve downstream classification in several cases.  
- Strength: reorganization idea addresses a realistic failure mode of SVD-based word assignments.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—embedding-based clustering for coherence has prior work and the use of V (right singular vectors) is ad hoc without theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: lacks ablations comparing V-based assignments to simpler baselines (e.g., U, k-means, centroid or average pairwise cosine).  
- Weakness: evaluation rigor concerns—modest r improvements, only 100 topics per dataset, no confidence intervals or significance tests, and limited error analysis.  
- Weakness: tuning/fairness issues—TBuckets-Clustering’s MaxDist and SVD-Reorg’s dynamic threshold selection are unclear and may risk using test labels; design choices appear arbitrary without ablation.  
- Weakness: reproducibility and clarity problems—missing details on similarity metric, OOV handling and rates (especially Genomics), top-N choice, and ambiguous threshold notation; several typos/inconsistencies.  
- Weakness: limited baselines—no comparison to simple embedding-based coherence measures or more recent metrics; classification results show contradictory patterns (e.g., MED-SPACE).  
- Question: Why use right singular vectors V as bucket identifiers instead of U or projecting/clustering word vectors on principal components?  
- Question: How were MaxDist values chosen for TBuckets-Clustering—via a development split or using test human ratings?  
- Question: Are the reported Pearson r differences statistically significant—please provide confidence intervals or permutation tests?  
- Question: Clarify the dynamic threshold formula (AWES − AWPSAWES ambiguous), its range, sign, and sensitivity.  
- Question: What similarity metric was used (cosine?), how are OOV topic words handled, and what fraction of topic words are OOV per dataset; were domain-specific embeddings tried for Genomics?  
- Question: Why weight scores by the square of the top singular value, and please provide ablations for WL, EVS, WL*EVS, singular-value weighting, and reorganization.  
- Question: Explain contradictory classification outcomes (MED-SPACE, POLITICS vs RELIGION), clarify selection protocol (number of topics/runs/annotator consistency), and provide statistical testing for Table 3.
