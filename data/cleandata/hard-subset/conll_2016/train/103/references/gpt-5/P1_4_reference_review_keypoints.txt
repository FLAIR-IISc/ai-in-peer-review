- Proposes TBuckets: group top-N topic words into embedding-based “buckets” and score coherence by dominance of the largest bucket.  
- Implements three variants: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD plus heuristic reorganization).  
- Scores use largest bucket properties (size, LDA word-order weighting, eigenvector similarity) optionally weighted by top singular value.  
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human ratings on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics, and applied to weakly supervised topic-labeling classification.  

Contributions:
- Introduces a largely parameter-free, embedding-driven “bucketization” coherence framework operationalizing a dominant-theme intuition.  
- Shows TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. (2015) in Pearson correlation on 2/3 datasets and helps select high-coherence runs for classification.  
- Proposes a heuristic reorganization that adjusts SVD assignments to favor a coherent largest bucket.  

Strengths:
- Conceptually simple and interpretable: largest-bucket-as-coherence is intuitive and inspectable.  
- SVD-based approach is elegant and largely hyperparameter-free compared to prior methods.  
- Competitive empirical performance on NYT and 20NG, demonstrating viability without co-occurrence stats.  
- Demonstrates practical utility: selecting high-coherence runs improves macro-F1 in several classification subsets.  
- Minimal reliance on external resources using off-the-shelf GloVe embeddings.  
- Clear presentation of three techniques and multiple scoring variants for exploration.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty over prior embedding-based coherence; main novelty is the largest-bucket proxy and SVD assignment.  
- Reorganization is heuristic and under-specified, with ambiguous formulas and many derived quantities lacking justification.  
- No ablation quantifying the impact of reorg and sigma1^2 weighting; choice of top-n eigenvectors is not justified.  
- Evaluation lacks statistical significance tests, confidence intervals, and Spearman correlations; classification gains are small/inconsistent.  
- Poor Genomics performance (worse than Röder et al.), likely due to domain-mismatch in embeddings, raising generalization concerns.  
- Methodological/reproducibility details missing: vector normalization, similarity metric, eigenvector scaling, OOV handling, and clustering parameter selection.  
- Metric may unfairly penalize coherent multi-aspect topics by rewarding single dominant buckets.  

Questions for authors (key clarifications requested):
- Clarify reorganization threshold formula and sensitivity to threshold choices; provide ablations with/without reorg.  
- Justify using the first n eigenvectors (n = topic words) versus other criteria and handling when n > rank or duplicate mappings.  
- Specify similarity computations: cosine vs dot-product, normalization of word vectors, and whether eigenvectors are scaled.  
- Describe OOV handling for GloVe and report OOV rates (especially for Genomics) and their effect on results.  
- Report statistical significance tests and Spearman correlations for reported improvements.  
- Explain how Max Dist in clustering was chosen and whether it was tuned on test data.  

Additional suggestions:
- Provide ablations for scoring components (size vs WL vs EVS, with/without sigma1^2 weighting).  
- Compare against more recent coherence measures using the same reference corpora.  
- Release code and configurations to improve reproducibility.
