- Proposes TBuckets, grouping a topic’s top words into embedding-based “buckets” and scoring coherence from properties of the largest bucket.  
- Introduces three variants: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD (assign via eigenvectors), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (post-process moving words into/out of largest bucket).  
- Scores incorporate bucket size, LDA word-order weights, eigenvector similarity, and optional top singular value weighting.  
- Evaluated on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics: SVD-Reorg slightly outperforms Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG but underperforms on Genomics.  
- Demonstrates modest downstream utility: selecting higher-TBuckets runs improves weakly supervised classification accuracy on some 20NG splits.  
- Contribution 1: Simple, interpretable bucketing coherence proxy with an SVD instantiation and automatic reorganization.  
- Contribution 2: Empirical comparison to Palmetto-style measures showing competitive correlations with human judgments on two general-domain datasets and a small application.  
- Contribution 3: Provides human-interpretable diagnostics (bucket distributions) for qualitative topic analysis.  
- Strength: Conceptual simplicity and interpretability—largest-bucket intuition aligns with human coherence judgments and yields useful diagnostics.  
- Strength: Competitive performance without dataset-specific tuning (SVD variants use off-the-shelf embeddings and achieve high Pearson r on NYT/20NG).  
- Strength: Elegant use of pre-trained embeddings (GloVe), avoiding PMI tables and generalizing across domains in some settings.  
- Strength: Practical relevance shown by topic-selection experiment improving labeling choices and classification results.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—embedding-based grouping for coherence is well-explored; SVD-based bucketing and largest-bucket score seem incremental.  
- Weakness: Methodological clarity issues—unusual use of eigenvectors for assignment, sign ambiguity, ad-hoc reorganization thresholds, and unexplained weighting choices.  
- Weakness: Evaluation gaps and statistical rigor—modest correlation gains, no significance testing for dependent correlations, multiple comparisons without correction, and only three datasets used.  
- Weakness: Baselines insufficient—missing embedding-based baselines (average pairwise cosine, k-means/silhouette, PCA projections) and more recent coherence measures.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—missing details on OOV handling, tie-breaking, rank/dimensionality issues, exact clustering settings, runtime, and code release.  
- Weakness: Topic-labeling experiment lacks detail on topics per run, labeling protocol, annotator variance, and statistical significance; reported improvements are small on several splits.  
- Request: Justify using cosine similarity to A^T A eigenvectors versus conventional projection/loadings or clustering in projected space, and report if alternatives were tried.  
- Request: Clarify the reorganization threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES vs (AWES−AWPS)/AWES), its numeric range, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Request: Explain handling of eigenvector sign indeterminacy and normalization of eigenvectors/word vectors.  
- Request: Report results when restricting to k < n components (e.g., k=2–3) and justify using n≈10 components for n topic words.  
- Request: Provide significance tests for Pearson r differences versus Röder et al. (2015) and across TBuckets variants.  
- Request: For Genomics, evaluate with domain-specific embeddings (e.g., PubMed/BioWordVec) to test dependence on representation quality.  
- Request: Provide full details for the classification experiment (topics per run, labels per topic, annotator blinding) and statistical significance of reported gains.  
- Overall: Clear, interpretable, and practically motivated work with promising results, but novelty is limited and the paper needs stronger empirical comparisons, methodological justification, ablations, and reproducibility details.
