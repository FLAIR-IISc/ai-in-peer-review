- Paper proposes TBuckets: embed top-N topic words into “buckets” and score coherence by dominance of the largest bucket; three variants: clustering, SVD assignment, and SVD with automatic reorganization.

- Claimed contributions: a simple largely parameter-free SVD bucketing method, a per-topic automatic reorganization heuristic, and a demonstration that coherence-based run selection can aid weakly supervised classification.

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves competitive/higher Pearson correlation with human judgments on 2/3 datasets using only pre-trained GloVe and minimal tuning.

- Strength: Bucket outputs are interpretable and mirror human grouping of topic words, providing qualitative insight into subthemes.

- Strength: Approach is well-motivated by distributional semantics; SVD as principal directions captures potential orthogonal subthemes.

- Strength: Practical utility shown—selecting high-coherence topic runs improved weakly supervised labeling in most tested subsets.

- Weakness: Incremental novelty—embedding-based coherence and clustering have prior work; SVD assignment is only modestly new.

- Weakness: Several methodological definitions are ambiguous or underspecified, hindering reproducibility (e.g., reorganization threshold formula and scoring composition).

- Weakness: Scoring pipeline lacks precise combination rules and normalization details (WL, EVS, weighting by singular value), and tie-breaking/negative similarity handling are unclear.

- Weakness: Ad hoc design choices (e.g., using first n right singular vectors) are not justified or sensitivity-tested.

- Weakness: Examples contain apparent errors, reducing confidence in results and presentation.

- Weakness: Evaluation compares against reported best settings rather than re-running baselines under the same setup and omits comparison to other embedding-based baselines.

- Weakness: No significance testing, confidence intervals, or sensitivity analyses (to N, embedding choice/dimension, OOV handling); small reported gains may be insignificant.

- Weakness: Performance falls on Genomics dataset (likely due to embedding domain mismatch) and authors did not test in-domain embeddings to probe this issue.

- Weakness: Classification application lacks comparison to selecting topics by other coherence measures, so advantage over alternatives is unproven.

- Weakness: No ablation of reorganization steps or other design choices; contribution of each component is not quantified.

- Weakness: Writing and presentation suffer from typos, inconsistent notation, missing pseudocode/complexity discussion, and unspecified code/data availability.

- Key request: Provide exact formulas and unambiguous definitions for the reorganization threshold, AWPS/AWES computation, and final scoring composition and normalization.

- Key request: Justify choice of number of singular vectors (n = topic size) and report sensitivity to k or variance-based selection.

- Key request: Report OOV handling and fraction of OOV topic words for GloVe across datasets.

- Key request: Report statistical significance/CIs for improvements over Röder et al. and perform robustness analyses.

- Key request: Compare against at least one strong embedding-based baseline under the same experimental setup.

- Key request: Evaluate Genomics with in-domain embeddings to assess domain robustness.

- Overall assessment: Moderate originality and practical promise, but plausibility is undermined by underspecification, limited baselines, lack of robustness/significance tests, and poor clarity.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject pending clearer specification, stronger baselines and analyses, ablations, and improved presentation.
