- Proposes TBuckets: group top-N topic words into semantic “buckets” via pre-trained embeddings; coherent topics have one dominant bucket.  
- Introduces three variants: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative on word vectors), TBuckets-SVD (assign to right singular vectors), TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (post-process SVD buckets with dynamic threshold).  
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human coherence on 20NG, NYT, Genomics; claims outperforming Röder et al. on 2/3 datasets.  
- Small application: selecting coherent topic sets can improve weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strength: Intuitive, inspectable “bucket” framing that matches human topic interpretation.  
- Strength: Uses off-the-shelf embeddings (GloVe), avoiding costly co-occurrence counts and capturing semantic relatedness.  
- Strength: SVD variants largely parameter-free, reducing per-dataset tuning compared to prior work.  
- Strength: Empirical gains on NYT and 20NG for SVD-Reorg; clustering competitive on Genomics.  
- Strength: Practical utility demonstrated for topic selection in annotation/classification workflows.  
- Weakness: Unjustified SVD assignment—principal axes are variance directions, not cluster prototypes; key details missing (centering, normalization, sign handling).  
- Weakness: Questionable choice of using the first n eigenvectors (n = topic words) versus selecting k by variance or eigengap.  
- Weakness: Reorganization threshold formula is unclear, possibly negative, and lacks motivation; algorithmic details (iterations, convergence, tie-breaking) are unspecified.  
- Weakness: Scoring reduces coherence to largest-bucket mass, which may penalize legitimately coherent multi-facet topics; no failure-mode analysis or qualitative comparisons.  
- Weakness: Evaluation lacks statistical significance tests for dependent correlations, omits Spearman rank, and may not fairly compare baselines (unclear if recomputed).  
- Weakness: Dependence on external embeddings and domain mismatch (Genomics drop) undermines “parameter-free” claim; no sensitivity analyses across embeddings/dimensions.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues—missing implementation details (clustering thresholds, vector normalization, weighting rationale), textual errors, and no released code.  
- Question: Were word vectors centered/standardized before SVD, and are similarities computed with normalized vectors? How is eigenvector sign ambiguity handled?  
- Question: Why use exactly the first n eigenvectors rather than selecting k by explained variance/eigengap?  
- Question: Clarify the reorganization threshold formula (parentheses), its possible negativity, number of passes, convergence criteria, and sensitivity.  
- Question: Why weight coherence by the square of the top singular value; does this conflate variance with coherence? Have you tried centering/removing the first component?  
- Question: Did you recompute Röder et al.’s baselines on the same corpora or reuse published numbers? Please report Spearman correlations and significance tests, plus TBuckets-Clustering variability over seeds.  
- Question: Provide results with domain-specific embeddings for Genomics and sensitivity to embedding model/dimension.  
- Question: Provide qualitative failure examples where TBuckets-SVD-Reorg disagrees with human ratings, especially for two-theme topics.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline reject—idea is promising but requires clearer algorithmic specification, stronger theoretical justification for SVD/reorg choices, significance testing, and robustness/sensitivity analyses.
