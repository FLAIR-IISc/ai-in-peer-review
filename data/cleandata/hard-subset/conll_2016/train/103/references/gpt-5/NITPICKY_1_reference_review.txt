Summary and claimed contributions
This paper proposes TBuckets, a family of topic coherence measures that group the top-N topic words into “buckets” (clusters) using word embeddings. Three variants are presented: (i) TBuckets-Clustering via agglomerative clustering with a maximum-distance threshold; (ii) TBuckets-SVD, which uses the right singular vectors (eigenvectors of A^T A) of the topic’s word-embedding matrix as “bucket identifiers” and assigns each word to its most similar eigenvector; and (iii) TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, which post-processes assignments using dynamic thresholds derived from average word–word and word–eigenvector similarities. Coherence is computed from properties of the largest bucket (size, word-order weighting, eigenvector similarity), optionally scaled by the largest singular value. The methods are evaluated by Pearson correlation with human judgments on three datasets and a downstream topic-labeling classification scenario.

As I see them, the contributions are:
1) A bucket-based topic coherence measure leveraging word embeddings, with an SVD-based, ostensibly parameter-light instantiation and a reorganization heuristic.
2) An empirical comparison against selected configurations from Röder et al. (2015) showing competitive-to-better correlations on two datasets.
3) A small application study that uses TBuckets to select “high-quality” topic sets for weakly supervised text classification.

Strengths
- Intuitive formulation and simplicity: The central intuition—coherent topics should concentrate words in one dominant semantic cluster—is easy to grasp, implement, and explain to practitioners. The case studies illustrate how bucket distributions reflect perceived coherence.

- Competitive empirical correlations without extensive external tuning: On NYT and 20NG, the SVD-based variants reach or slightly exceed the best numbers reported by Röder et al. (2015), while avoiding explicit tuning over large configuration spaces. This is appealing for practical adoption.

- Parameter-light design choices: Aside from the clustering threshold (for TBuckets-Clustering), the SVD-based versions avoid explicit hyperparameter sweeps. Using off-the-shelf embeddings keeps inference fast.

- Potential utility for pre-screening topics in labeling workflows: The classification experiment indicates that selecting runs by the proposed coherence can improve average macro-F1 in three of four 20NG subsets, suggesting value as a filter for human annotation.

- Clear reporting of multiple scoring variants: Reporting size, WL, EVS, and their combinations provides some transparency into which factors drive the correlations.

Weaknesses
- Methodological ambiguities and ad hoc choices in SVD assignment and reorganization: The SVD variant uses the first n right singular vectors (n = number of topic words) as “bucket identifiers,” then assigns each word to the most similar vector. Conceptually, these are principal directions in embedding space, not clusters; mapping words to eigenvectors can produce unintuitive partitions. The approach is sensitive to sign flips and negative cosine similarities, which are not discussed. The reorganization step relies on a dynamic threshold defined as “AWES − AWPSAWES” (missing parentheses and likely meant as AWES − AWPS/AWES); the derivation is heuristic, its range/behavior (negative, >1) is unclear, and clipping is not specified. These choices look fragile and under-justified.

- Scoring inconsistencies and unclear formulation: The word-order weighting WL uses LDA Order Weight(w_i) = 1/log(i+1) for i>0 and 1 for i=0. This makes the 2nd word receive weight >1 (1/log 2 ≈ 1.44), exceeding the top word, and is non-monotonic early on; the base of log is unspecified. The multiplication/aggregation “WL*EVS” is not formally defined, nor is the rationale for squaring the top singular value as a global multiplier. No ablation or sensitivity analyses are provided to justify these design choices.

- Evaluation protocol and significance: Correlations are reported on 100 topics per dataset without confidence intervals, statistical tests, or analysis of variance across random seeds. The reported improvements over Röder et al. (e.g., 0.806→0.837 on NYT; 0.859→0.869 on 20NG) may not be statistically significant. TBuckets underperforms on Genomics, and no attempt is made to use domain embeddings (despite acknowledging this limitation), undermining the “parameter-free” claim’s practical utility.

- Clustering baseline fairness and threshold selection: For TBuckets-Clustering, it appears the “Max Dist” threshold is selected per dataset (numbers in parentheses in Table 1) but the selection procedure is not described; if tuned on the test topics, reported correlations may be optimistic. Figure 1 is referenced but not clearly explained, and exact thresholds are not justified. Linkage choice is treated as a free variable, again raising fairness concerns in comparisons.

- Limited novelty relative to prior embedding-based coherence: Grouping words via embeddings to measure coherence has precursors (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson’s distributional measures, centroid similarity among topic words). The main novelty—using SVD directions and largest-bucket size—is incremental and hinges on heuristics that are not theoretically grounded. The “single large bucket = high coherence” assumption penalizes multi-faceted yet semantically coherent topics and is not stress-tested.

- Reproducibility and presentation issues: Important details are missing: OOV handling for embeddings, text preprocessing, cosine similarity normalization/sign handling, precise formulas for combined scores, and code availability. Numerous typographical and formatting errors (hyphenation artifacts, inconsistent citation formats with “?,” misformatted equations, inconsistent capitalization of “topic modelling/modeling,” etc.) detract from clarity. Some references are malformed (e.g., CTM citation). Table/figure referencing is inconsistent, and the normalization ranges (Table 2 vs. Table 1) are confusing.

Questions to authors
- SVD assignment:
  • Do you use cosine similarity to signed eigenvectors, or absolute cosine? How do you handle sign indeterminacy of singular vectors?
  • Why select the first n right singular vectors rather than, e.g., k determined by explained variance? How sensitive is performance to this choice?

- Reorganization threshold:
  • Please provide the exact formula with parentheses and ranges. Is the threshold clipped to [0,1]? Under what conditions does it become negative, and what happens then?
  • Did you evaluate alternatives (e.g., margin-based thresholds, calibration by bootstrapping)?

- WL and aggregation:
  • What is the log base in WL? Why should the second word be weighted more than the first? Was a monotonic decreasing weighting considered?
  • How exactly are WL and EVS combined (“WL*EVS”)? Why square the top singular value?

- Clustering baseline:
  • How were Max Dist and linkage selected? Was any validation split used, or were choices made on the test set?
  • Can you provide CIs for the correlations in Table 1 and significance tests vs. Röder et al.?

- General:
  • How do you handle OOV words in embeddings? Lowercasing/token normalization?
  • Can you report per-dataset ablations (size vs. WL vs. EVS) and error analyses (cases where TBuckets fails) and release code?

Recommendation: I lean toward rejection due to methodological opacity, several questionable/under-justified design choices, lack of statistical rigor, and numerous presentation issues, despite promising correlations on two datasets and a clear, useful intuition. A revised version with clarified formulations, stronger evaluation, and improved writing could be much more compelling.
