- Proposes TBuckets: embedding-based topic coherence by clustering top words into thematic “buckets” and scoring topics by the largest bucket’s size/quality.  
- Introduces three variants: agglomerative clustering on embeddings, SVD-based assignment to orthogonal directions, and SVD plus an automatic reorganization step.  
- Evaluated via Pearson correlation with human ratings on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG.  
- Small downstream study shows selecting high-coherence runs can improve weakly supervised text classification.  

- Contribution: a simple, largely parameter-free bucket-based coherence measure using pre-trained embeddings with effective SVD and reorganization variants.  
- Contribution: empirical benchmarking against Röder et al. (2015), qualitative bucket analysis, and a small classification application.  
- Contribution: frames coherence as dominance of a single thematic group, offering interpretability and efficiency.  

- Strength: achieves non-trivial empirical gains over a strong, widely-used baseline on two datasets.  
- Strength: simple, efficient, interpretable outputs using off-the-shelf embeddings and light-weight computations.  
- Strength: parameter-light SVD methods and data-derived reorganization threshold reduce hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: qualitative examples show reorganization can correct obvious mis-assignments.  
- Strength: downstream utility demonstrated by coherence-based run selection improving/maintaining macro-F1 in several cases.  

- Weakness: SVD formulation is underspecified and conceptually questionable (uses V instead of U, unclear number of directions, unclear assignment rule).  
- Weakness: reorganization threshold formula is ambiguous and lacks statistical justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—similar to prior embedding/clustering coherence work and lacks comparison to simple embedding baselines (e.g., average pairwise cosine) or Aletras & Stevenson (2013) with modern embeddings.  
- Weakness: evaluation risks ex post selection among multiple TBuckets metrics, lacks confidence intervals/significance tests, and omits Spearman correlations.  
- Weakness: preprocessing/OOV handling not discussed, possibly explaining Genomics underperformance and hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: downstream experiment under-specified (topic counts, annotation details, statistical testing) and selection procedure may introduce bias.  
- Weakness: design favors single-theme topics and may penalize legitimate multi-faceted topics; no analysis of multi-theme behavior.  

- Question: clarify SVD details—why use V not U, exact number of eigenvectors used, full assignment algorithm including normalization.  
- Question: provide exact reorganization threshold formula with derivation, sensitivity analysis, and ablation results (without reorg and with alternatives).  
- Question: compare TBuckets to simple average pairwise cosine baseline and Aletras & Stevenson (2013) with GloVe to isolate bucketization value.  
- Question: detail preprocessing and OOV handling, specify GloVe model used, and consider re-running Genomics with domain-specific embeddings.  
- Question: report statistical significance/confidence intervals or permutation tests for Pearson r differences and include Spearman correlations.  

- Overall recommendation: borderline reject due to under-motivated/underspecified methodological choices, limited novelty against embedding baselines, and insufficient statistical rigor and baselines—work could improve into a solid contribution with clearer algorithms, stronger baselines, significance testing, and domain-appropriate embeddings.
