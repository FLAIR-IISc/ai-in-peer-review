- Proposes TBuckets: group topic words into thematic buckets and use bucket properties (largest bucket size) to measure topic coherence.
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative on embeddings), TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
- Evaluated on three datasets using Pearson correlation with human coherence scores; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior reported results on two of three datasets.
- Shows practical utility by using coherence scores to filter topics and improve weakly supervised text classification.

- Main contribution: a novel, intuitive coherence metric based on thematic grouping of words into buckets.
- Main contribution: parameter-free SVD-based implementations (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that avoid tuning required by prior methods.
- Main contribution: demonstrates downstream benefit for text classification by selecting high-quality topics.

- Strength: conceptually novel and human-intuitive; aligns with how humans judge topic coherence.
- Strength: parameter-free SVD methods offer strong practical advantages for practitioners.
- Strength: strong empirical results with notable Pearson r improvements on NYT and 20NG.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including multiple metrics, linkage ablation, and a classification application.

- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why bucketing and largest-bucket properties correlate with human judgments.
- Weakness: comparisons rely on reported prior results (Roder et al. 2015) rather than direct implementations of state-of-the-art; statistical significance unclear.
- Weakness: reorganization dynamic threshold (AWES - AWPS*AWES) is heuristically presented without justification or alternatives.
- Weakness: no scalability or computational complexity analysis for SVD-based methods on large topic sets.
- Weakness: inadequate ablation to isolate the specific contribution of the reorganization step versus SVD alone.

- Question: why does TBuckets-SVD-Reorg underperform on Genomics relative to other datasets; which dataset characteristics influence performance?
- Question: how was the dynamic threshold formula derived and why is it preferable to fixed or alternative thresholds?
- Question: what is the computational complexity and runtime comparison to existing coherence measures for typical topic-model sizes (e.g., 100 topics × 10 words)?
- Question: how much annotator time or effort is actually saved in the text classification application by filtering high-coherence topics?
- Question: how sensitive is TBuckets-Clustering to its maximum distance threshold and why are SVD methods considered fully parameter-free while clustering is not?

- Overall recommendation: weak accept with major revisions to add theoretical justification, direct state-of-the-art comparisons, clarification of the reorganization step, and scalability analysis.
