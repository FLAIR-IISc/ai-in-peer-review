- Paper proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets so coherent topics form one dominant bucket while incoherent topics spread across many.  
- Introduces three techniques: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganization improves coherence).  
- Evaluated on 20NG, NYT, and Genomics by correlating scores with human annotations; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms baselines on NYT and 20NG.  
- Shows TBuckets can improve weakly supervised text classification by selecting high-quality topics.  

Main contributions:
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg: parameter-free coherence measure with strong empirical gains on two datasets.  
- TBuckets-SVD: parameter-free SVD-based method to discover thematic sub-themes.  
- Practical application: using TBuckets for topic selection improves classification.

Strengths:
- Novel bucket concept aligns with human intuition and is well-exemplified.  
- SVD-based methods are parameter-free, offering practical advantages over tuned baselines.  
- Strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements on two datasets.  
- Clear methodology and motivating examples, especially for the reorganization step.  
- Uses standard GloVe embeddings, aiding reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Comparison limited to Roder et al. (2015); misses more recent strong baselines like Lau et al. (2014) and Meng et al. (2018).  
- Insufficient theoretical justification for why SVD discovers meaningful thematic dimensions.  
- Poor performance on the Genomics dataset (worse than baseline), with no analysis of the failure mode.  
- No ablation studies to isolate the contribution of the reorganization step versus SVD.  
- Lacks computational complexity or scalability analysis.

Questions for authors:
- Have you compared TBuckets to newer coherence measures (e.g., Lau et al., Meng et al.)?  
- Is the reorganization step truly parameter-free given the dynamic threshold based on AWES and AWPS?  
- Why does TBuckets-SVD-Reorg underperform on Genomics—what dataset characteristics cause this?  
- What is the computational complexity and scalability of your methods relative to existing approaches?

Overall assessment:
- Novel, well-written paper with a strong parameter-free method and practical utility; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, theoretical justification, and Genomics performance.
