

# Review: Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets

## Summary
This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach for measuring topic coherence in LDA-based topic models. The core idea is to group topic words into semantic buckets (thematic groups), where a single large bucket indicates high coherence. The authors present three techniques for bucket formation: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering), TBuckets-SVD (using SVD to discover sub-themes), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganizing buckets from SVD for improved coherence). The method is evaluated on three standard datasets, with TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforming state-of-the-art coherence measures while requiring no parameter tuning.

## Main Contributions (in order of strength)
1. The TBuckets framework provides a novel, intuitive approach to topic coherence measurement that aligns with human judgment of topic quality.
2. The parameter-free nature of TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg represents a significant practical advantage over existing methods requiring extensive parameter tuning.
3. The application to text classification demonstrates tangible benefits of using TBuckets for selecting high-quality topics, improving classification performance.

## Strengths

**1. Novel and Intuitive Approach:** The paper's core insight—humans assess topic coherence by grouping words into thematic buckets—is both novel and intuitively compelling. The examples provided (weather topic vs. non-coherent topic) clearly illustrate how the bucketing process captures coherence. This approach differs fundamentally from existing methods that rely on statistical measures of word co-occurrence or semantic similarity without explicit thematic grouping.

**2. Strong Empirical Results:** The evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed. TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves the highest correlation with human judgments on two out of three datasets (NYT and 20NG), outperforming the state-of-the-art. The parameter-free nature of the SVD-based methods is particularly impressive given that previous state-of-the-art methods require careful tuning of multiple parameters. The text classification application in Section 4.3 provides strong evidence of practical utility.

**3. Practical Advantages:** The authors correctly emphasize that TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are completely parameter-free, which is a major practical advantage over existing methods. As noted in Table 1, the state-of-the-art methods require tuning multiple parameters (e.g., "single-linkage, size" has a parameter value in parentheses), while the SVD-based methods have no parameters to tune. This makes the approach more accessible and easier to deploy in real-world applications.

**4. Clear Explanations and Examples:** The paper is exceptionally well-written with clear examples of the bucketing process (Section 3). The step-by-step walkthrough of how the weather topic and non-coherent topic are bucketed makes the approach immediately understandable, which is crucial for a method that relies on an intuitive human-like process.

## Weaknesses

**1. Lack of Theoretical Foundation:** While the approach is intuitive and empirically effective, the paper lacks a theoretical justification for why bucketing based on semantic similarity correlates with human judgment of coherence. The authors present compelling empirical results but don't provide deeper insights into the underlying principles that make this approach work.

**2. Limited Comparison with Recent Work:** The paper compares only with Roder et al. (2015), but doesn't address more recent work on topic coherence (e.g., work by Lau et al. 2014, or more recent approaches). This omission weakens the claim that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is state-of-the-art.

**3. Insufficient Analysis of Failure Cases:** The paper focuses on showing the method's success, but doesn't analyze cases where the method performs poorly. Understanding the limitations would strengthen the paper and provide direction for future work.

**4. Reproducibility Concerns:** The paper mentions using GloVe word embeddings but doesn't specify which version (6B, 400B, 840B). Given that different embedding models can affect results, this omission limits reproducibility. The paper should clarify this detail.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper claims TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is "completely parameter free," but the reorganization threshold is computed using AWPS and AWES. Could you clarify how this is truly parameter-free? Is the threshold calculation based on theoretical principles or empirical observations?

2. The paper doesn't discuss computational complexity. How do the computational requirements of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg compare with state-of-the-art methods? Is the reorganization step significantly more expensive than the basic SVD approach?

3. The evaluation uses only topics with 10 words. How would the method perform with topics having more words (e.g., 20 or 30), which are common in some topic modeling applications?

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper with a novel, intuitive approach to topic coherence measurement that outperforms existing methods while being simpler to use (no parameter tuning required). The empirical results are compelling, and the practical application to text classification demonstrates real-world utility. While the lack of theoretical foundation and limited comparison with recent work are weaknesses, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field. The intuitive approach, strong empirical results, and practical advantages make this a valuable contribution to topic modeling research.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the reproducibility concerns and clarify the parameter-free claim. The paper's core contribution—TBuckets—represents a meaningful advance in topic coherence measurement that aligns well with human judgment and has clear practical benefits.
