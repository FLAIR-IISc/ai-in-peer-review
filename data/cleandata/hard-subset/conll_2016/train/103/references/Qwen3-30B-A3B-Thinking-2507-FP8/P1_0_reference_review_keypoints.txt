- Introduces TBuckets, a thematic "word bucketing" approach to measure topic coherence for LDA topics.  
- Proposes three bucketing techniques: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Claims parameter-free SVD-based methods and shows application benefit for weakly supervised text classification.

- Novel human-aligned perspective: bucketing models how humans group topic words and captures intuitive coherence differences.  
- Parameter-free SVD variants perform strongly, outperforming prior methods on two of three datasets without tuning.  
- Demonstrates practical utility: selecting high-coherence topics with TBuckets improves weakly supervised classification.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with transparent experimental setup and illustrative examples.  
- Thoughtful design and analysis of the reorganization step, with a principled, parameter-free reorg method.

- Insufficient differentiation from existing semantic-similarity coherence measures (e.g., CUSheffield).  
- Evaluation limited to three standard datasets, raising questions about generalizability to other domains.  
- Lacks comparison with more recent coherence measures post-2015, weakening state-of-the-art claims.  
- Reorganization threshold formula (AWES-related) is poorly justified and appears arbitrary.  
- No ablation study to isolate contributions of clustering, SVD, and reorganization components.

- How is the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg threshold (AWES/AWPS) computed in practice and why is it truly parameter-free?  
- Can the authors compare TBuckets with newer topic coherence methods from 2016–2023?  
- Why does TBuckets perform worse on Genomics, and is this due to domain-specific characteristics?  
- How should the Max Dist parameter for TBuckets-Clustering be chosen for new datasets in practice?  
- How were human coherence scores normalized to 0–1 in Table 2, and what normalization formula was used?

- Overall: well-written paper with meaningful contributions and practical gains, but needs clearer differentiation from related work, broader evaluation, justification for the reorg threshold, and ablation analyses.
