

# Peer Review: "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

## Summary
The paper proposes a novel approach called TBuckets for measuring topic coherence in topic models. The core idea is that a coherent topic should have words that naturally group into a single dominant theme (one large bucket), while an incoherent topic will have multiple distinct thematic groups (multiple medium-sized buckets). The authors propose three techniques for creating these buckets: clustering-based (TBuckets-Clustering), SVD-based (TBuckets-SVD), and SVD with reorganization (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg). The approach is evaluated on three standard datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics), with results showing that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms state-of-the-art methods on two out of three datasets while being completely parameter-free.

## Main Contributions (in order of strength)
1. **TBuckets-SVD-Reorg**: A completely parameter-free coherence measure that outperforms state-of-the-art on two of three datasets, demonstrating significant practical value.
2. **Human-Centric Topic Coherence Insight**: The paper provides a clear, intuitive explanation of how humans assess topic coherence through thematic grouping, which aligns with human judgment better than purely statistical measures.
3. **Demonstrated Practical Utility**: The paper successfully demonstrates the application of their coherence measure in improving weakly supervised text classification, showing tangible benefits in real-world applications.

## Strengths

1. **Novel and Intuitive Approach**: The paper's core insight that topic coherence can be measured through thematic grouping of words is highly intuitive and aligns perfectly with how humans evaluate topics. The examples provided (e.g., the weather topic vs. the Afghanistan/Wikileaks topic) clearly illustrate this insight, making the paper's contribution immediately understandable. This human-centric perspective is a significant advancement over purely statistical coherence measures that often fail to correlate with human judgment.

2. **Strong Empirical Validation**: The evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed, using three standard datasets and comparing with the best results from Roder et al. (2015). The results show that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms state-of-the-art on 2 out of 3 datasets, which is significant given that the SVD-based techniques require absolutely no parameter tuning. This parameter-free aspect is a major practical advantage over previous methods that require significant parameter tuning.

3. **Practical Application Demonstration**: The paper successfully bridges theoretical contribution with practical application by showing how their coherence measure improves weakly supervised text classification. Table 3 demonstrates that using TBuckets to select high-quality topics for human annotation significantly improves classification performance, particularly for difficult datasets like PC vs MAC. This demonstrates the real-world value of their method beyond just academic metrics.

## Weaknesses

1. **Insufficient Novelty Differentiation**: While the paper claims novelty, it doesn't clearly differentiate its approach from Roder et al. (2015), which proposed a unifying framework for topic coherence measures. The paper states that their method "outperforms state-of-the-art" but doesn't explicitly explain how TBuckets fits into or improves upon Roder's framework. A clearer comparison would strengthen the novelty claim.

2. **Lack of Discussion on Method Limitations**: The paper doesn't adequately discuss the limitations of their approach. For instance, how does TBuckets handle topics with very few words (e.g., topics with only 5-6 words)? How does it perform on topics that genuinely have multiple distinct themes (like "sports and politics" topics)? The paper should acknowledge these potential limitations to provide a more balanced view of the method's applicability.

3. **Insufficient Explanation of Reorganization Mechanism**: The reorganization step (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) is described in detail, but the paper doesn't sufficiently explain *why* the initial SVD approach sometimes misplaces words. While the examples help, a more general explanation of the underlying reason for these misplacements would strengthen the paper's theoretical foundation.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper states that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is "completely parameter free," but the dynamic threshold calculation (AWES - AWPS*AWES) involves calculations that could be considered parameters. Could you clarify why this is considered parameter-free, especially since it depends on topic-specific calculations?

2. In Table 3, the "MEDICAL vs SPACE" dataset shows higher classification accuracy with low coherence topics (S2 Lowest 5) than with high coherence topics (S2 Highest 5). This contradicts the paper's main claim about coherence being important for classification. Could you explain this anomaly?

3. The paper uses GloVe word embeddings trained on Wikipedia and Gigaword. How would the performance of TBuckets change if different word embeddings (e.g., trained on domain-specific corpora) were used? The authors mention future work on domain-specific word similarities but would a preliminary discussion of this be helpful?

## Overall Assessment
The paper presents a compelling and well-executed approach to topic coherence measurement that aligns with human intuition and demonstrates practical value. The TBuckets-SVD-Reorg method's parameter-free nature and strong performance make it a significant contribution to the field. While the paper could benefit from clearer differentiation from Roder et al.'s framework and a more thorough discussion of limitations, the core contribution is strong and well-supported by empirical results. The demonstrated application in text classification significantly enhances the paper's impact beyond just a coherence measure.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the questions above and clarifying the novelty claim. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field of topic modeling by providing a human-centric coherence measure that is both effective and practical to implement.
