- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic "buckets" and using bucket properties (especially largest bucket size) to measure topic coherence.
- Introduces three bucketing methods: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD plus reorganization).
- Evaluates on 20NG, NYT, and Genomics using Pearson's r with human judgments; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg beats state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG.
- Claims SVD-based methods are parameter-free, avoiding manual tuning required by prior methods.
- Demonstrates practical utility: using TBuckets to select topics improves weakly supervised text classification performance.

- Contribution: novel coherence framework based on word bucketing and largest-bucket-based coherence metric.
- Contribution: parameter-free coherence measurement via SVD-based TBuckets methods.
- Contribution: shows effectiveness both for coherence evaluation and improving downstream text classification.

- Strength: intuitive, well-motivated approach with examples aligning to human coherence judgments.
- Strength: significant practical advantage from the claimed parameter-free implementation.
- Strength: strong empirical results with clear presentation and appropriate metrics/tables.
- Strength: validated practical gains in a text classification task using high-quality TBuckets topics.
- Strength: well-structured paper with helpful examples illustrating bucketing.

- Weakness: insufficient differentiation and discussion relative to prior embedding-based coherence work (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson, Lau et al.).
- Weakness: incomplete comparison with the most recent state-of-the-art coherence measures (e.g., NPMI/Lau et al., 2014).
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why largest-bucket size correlates with human coherence judgments.
- Weakness: limited dataset diversity (only 3 corpora); unclear generalization to other domains like social media or medical text.
- Weakness: "parameter-free" claim is questionable due to threshold computations (AWES/AWPS) derived from data without clear explanation.

- Question: Clarify whether TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is truly parameter-free given AWES/AWPS threshold computation.
- Question: Why does TBuckets perform worse on Genomics, and what properties of Genomics topics affect generalization?
- Question: How does TBuckets handle inherently multi-themed topics (e.g., technology + business) where multiple buckets may be meaningful?
- Question: Why were more recent coherence measures (e.g., Lau et al., 2014/NPMI) not included in comparisons?
- Question: How would TBuckets represent topics with multiple equally important themes and how would that affect coherence scores?

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revision—novel, useful, and empirically strong but needs clearer distinction from prior work, broader comparisons, and clarification of the parameter-free claim.
