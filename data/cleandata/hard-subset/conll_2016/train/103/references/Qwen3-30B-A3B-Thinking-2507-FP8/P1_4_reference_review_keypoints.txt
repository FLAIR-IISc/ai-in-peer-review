- Proposal: TBuckets groups topic words into thematic "buckets" (TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) and measures coherence by word distribution across buckets (single large bucket => high coherence).

- Main contributions: intuitive word-bucketing aligned with human judgments; parameter-free SVD-based techniques; demonstrated utility in improving weakly supervised text classification.

- Strength: Strong motivation and intuitive approach with clear examples showing bucket distributions correlate with human coherence judgments.

- Strength: Parameter-free SVD methods are practical (no tuning) and outperform prior best results on NYT and 20NG datasets.

- Strength: Solid experimental evaluation using the same datasets/metrics as prior work and an additional text classification experiment demonstrating utility.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—approach resembles existing distributional semantics methods (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson 2013) and other dimensionality-reduction techniques.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison with more recent topic-coherence methods post-2015 (e.g., Cao et al. 2018; Zhao et al. 2019).

- Weakness: Misleading "parameter-free" claim—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg uses a dynamic threshold based on topic-specific AWPS/AWES values.

- Weakness: Lack of theoretical justification for why the SVD-based approach should outperform simpler coherence measures.

- Question: Please clarify why TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is considered "parameter-free" despite using dynamic thresholds and how this differs from other dynamic-threshold methods.

- Question: How does TBuckets fundamentally differ from Aletras & Stevenson (2013) CUSheffield, and what specific improvements does it offer?

- Question: Why were more recent coherence methods not included in comparisons, and how would TBuckets perform against them?

- Overall assessment: Well-written, empirically strong paper with practical advantages and real-world utility; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the parameter-free claim and better differentiate from prior work.
