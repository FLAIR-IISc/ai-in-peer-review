- Contribution: Introduces TBuckets, a human-centric method that groups topic words into thematic "buckets" and scores coherence by the dominance of a single bucket.
- Contribution: Proposes three bucket-creation techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD (parameter-free SVD), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganization for improved coherence).
- Contribution: Presents a parameter-free SVD-based method that outperforms state-of-the-art on two of three datasets.

- Strength: Novel, intuitive bucketing view of topic coherence that matches human interpretation and differs from semantic-similarity approaches.
- Strength: TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are parameter-free, easing practical use and avoiding costly tuning.
- Strength: Strong empirical results — improved Pearson correlation on two datasets and better weakly-supervised text classification when selecting high-quality topics.
- Strength: Clear, detailed, step-by-step examples that make the bucketing procedure easy to understand.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why bucket distributions (e.g., largest bucket size) correlate with human coherence judgments.
- Weakness: Limited comparisons — only evaluates against Roder et al. (2015) and omits other recent coherence measures (e.g., Lau et al., Aletras & Stevenson).
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why the method works and why SVD outperforms clustering.
- Weakness: Evaluation scope is narrow (three text corpora), leaving generalizability to specialized domains unclear.
- Weakness: No ablation studies to quantify the contribution of the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.

- Question: Provide comparisons with other coherence measures (e.g., CUCI, CUSheffield) beyond Roder et al. (2015)?
- Question: Explain which properties of topic word distributions make the bucket approach effective versus semantic-similarity measures?
- Question: How does the method perform on specialized domains (medical, legal); any preliminary results or analysis?
- Question: Provide ablation studies showing how much the reorganization step improves over TBuckets-SVD and when it helps most?

- Overall: A solid, practical, and novel contribution with strong empirical results but needing stronger theoretical grounding, broader comparisons, and deeper analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions to address theoretical justification and broader comparisons).
