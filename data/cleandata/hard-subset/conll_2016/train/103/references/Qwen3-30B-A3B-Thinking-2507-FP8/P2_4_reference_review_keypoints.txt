- Paper proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into semantic "buckets" to measure topic coherence, with larger single buckets indicating higher coherence.  
- Three bucket-formation methods: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganized SVD buckets).  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves highest correlation with human judgments on NYT and 20NG.  
- TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are parameter-free, avoiding the tuning required by prior methods.  
- Demonstrated practical benefit: using TBuckets to select topics improves text classification performance.  
- Novel and intuitive idea: thematic bucketing aligns with human judgments and contrasts with co-occurrence/semantic-similarity measures.  
- Strong empirical results and comprehensive evaluation support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Clear explanations and illustrative examples make the bucketing process easy to understand.  
- Lack of theoretical justification for why bucketing correlates with human coherence judgments.  
- Limited comparison set: paper compares mainly to Röder et al. (2015) and omits some recent coherence methods.  
- Insufficient analysis of failure cases or limitations where the method underperforms.  
- Reproducibility concerns: unspecified GloVe embedding version may affect results.  
- Question on "parameter-free" claim: reorganization threshold uses AWPS/AWES—clarification needed on whether this is truly parameter-free.  
- Question on computational cost: need comparison of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg complexity versus other methods, and cost of reorganization step.  
- Question on generality to longer topics: evaluation limited to 10-word topics—performance on 20–30 word topics is unknown.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, practically useful contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address reproducibility and the parameter-free claim.
