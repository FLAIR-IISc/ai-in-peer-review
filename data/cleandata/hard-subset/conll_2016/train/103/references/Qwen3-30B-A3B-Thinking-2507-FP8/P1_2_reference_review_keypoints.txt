- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into semantic "buckets" with three methods (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg); SVD-Reorg outperforms prior work on 2/3 datasets and helps weakly-supervised classification.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel, intuitive bucket-based topic coherence measure aligned with human interpretation.

- Contribution: Proposes parameter-free TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg techniques requiring no tuning.

- Contribution: Demonstrates that selecting high-coherence topics with TBuckets improves weakly-supervised text classification.

- Strength: Novel and well-motivated approach that mirrors human thematic grouping, illustrated with clear examples.

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves superior performance without parameter tuning on NYT and 20NG datasets.

- Strength: Practical utility shown via improved classification accuracy when selecting high-coherence topics.

- Weakness: Limited baseline comparisons—only contrasts with Roder et al. (2015) and omits more recent methods (e.g., Zhao et al. 2017, Ding et al. 2019).

- Weakness: Reorganization step lacks clear justification and analysis; no ablation showing its isolated contribution.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of limitations—handling of very short topics, semantically related-but-distant words, domain-specific topics, and polysemy is unclear.

- Weakness: Inconsistent "parameter-free" claim because the reorganization uses a dynamic threshold dependent on AWPS and AWES, which may be dataset-sensitive.

- Question: Please compare TBuckets with more recent state-of-the-art coherence methods (e.g., Zhao et al. 2017, Ding et al. 2019).

- Question: Why use the specific dynamic threshold formula (AWES - AWPS*AWES), why is it parameter-free, and how sensitive are results to it?

- Question: Why is the MEDICAL vs SPACE classification improvement minimal—are those topics inherently more coherent or is another factor responsible?

- Question: Are GloVe embeddings appropriate for all datasets, especially Genomics, or would domain-specific embeddings be needed?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written with helpful examples and standard experiments; recommend revisions to include newer comparisons, clearer limitation analysis, and clarity on the parameter-free claim.
