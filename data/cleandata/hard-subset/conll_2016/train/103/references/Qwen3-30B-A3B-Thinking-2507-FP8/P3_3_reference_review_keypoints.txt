- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets with three methods (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) to measure topic coherence.  
- Main contributions: TBuckets framework; parameter-free TBuckets-SVD / SVD-Reorg that beat SOTA on 2/3 datasets; practical utility in improving weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strength: Intuitive, human-aligned measure—single dominant bucket correlates with human coherence judgments.  
- Strength: Parameter-free SVD variants reduce tuning and show superior performance on NYT and 20NG.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation across three datasets and a real application (1–2% classification accuracy gains).  
- Strength: Clear writing and illustrative examples of bucket formation.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why bucket count/size should map to human coherence.  
- Weakness: Does not compare to some recent baselines (e.g., Lau et al.’s NPMI and other newer coherence methods).  
- Weakness: Underperforms on Genomics (0.68 vs. 0.773) with no analysis of failure modes or dataset-specific causes.  
- Weakness: Reorganization threshold formula (AWES - AWPS*AWES) seems ad-hoc and its parameter-free claim is ambiguous.  
- Question: Why does TBuckets-SVD-Reorg underperform on the Genomics dataset—what dataset/topic properties cause this?  
- Question: How exactly are AWES and AWPS computed, and does their use introduce implicit parameters contrary to the parameter-free claim?  
- Question: How sensitive is TBuckets to topic model settings (K, hyperparameters) and to underlying model quality when selecting high-coherence topics?  
- Overall: Strong, well-validated, and practically useful contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address theoretical grounding, broader comparisons, and failure-case analysis.
