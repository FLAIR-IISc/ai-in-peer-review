- Proposes TBuckets: measure topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic buckets; coherence indicated by one large bucket plus few small ones versus many medium buckets.
- Introduces three bucket-creation methods: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
- Main contributions: novel human-interpretable TBuckets concept, parameter-free SVD-based techniques, and application improving weakly supervised text classification.
- Strength: Intuitive, well-motivated, and human-interpretable approach aligning with how people assess topic coherence.
- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves strong empirical results, outperforming prior methods on NYT and 20NG while being parameter-free.
- Strength: Demonstrated practical utility by filtering high-quality topics to improve weakly supervised classification performance.
- Weakness: Limited comparison scope—only compared to best-performing methods from Röder et al. (2015), not the full state of the art.
- Weakness: No ablation studies to isolate the effect of the reorganization step versus SVD alone.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to three relatively homogeneous datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics), questioning generalizability.
- Weakness: Reorganization threshold (AWES - AWPS*AWES) appears ad-hoc and lacks theoretical justification.
- Question: Clarify whether comparisons included all methods from Röder et al. (2015) or only the top performers reported there.
- Question: Provide ablation studies for TBuckets-SVD without reorganization to quantify reorg contribution.
- Question: How does the approach perform on larger topics (e.g., 20 words instead of 10)?
- Overall assessment: Well-written, novel, and practically valuable method with strong results; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions addressing scope and ablation concerns.
