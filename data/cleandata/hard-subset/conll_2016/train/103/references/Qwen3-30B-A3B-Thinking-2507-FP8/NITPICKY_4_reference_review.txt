

# Review of "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

## Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions

The paper proposes a novel approach called TBuckets for measuring topic coherence in topic modeling. The core idea is to group topic words into thematic buckets (logical groups) and use the distribution of words across these buckets as a measure of topic coherence. The authors present three techniques for creating these buckets: (1) TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering), (2) TBuckets-SVD (using singular value decomposition), and (3) TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (a reorganization step for the SVD-based technique).

As I see them, the main contributions of this work are:
1. A novel bucketing approach for topic coherence measurement that aligns with human interpretation of topic coherence
2. Three specific techniques for implementing the bucketing approach, with the SVD-based methods being claimed as parameter-free
3. Experimental validation showing superior performance compared to state-of-the-art on three standard datasets

## Strengths

1. **Intuitive and Human-Aligned Approach**: The core idea of grouping topic words into thematic buckets aligns well with how humans assess topic coherence. The examples provided (like the weather topic vs. the Afghanistan/Wikileaks topic) effectively demonstrate the concept and its relevance to human judgment.

2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The paper provides a thorough evaluation across three standard datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics) and compares against state-of-the-art techniques. The evaluation includes both coherence measurement and an application to text classification, demonstrating practical utility.

3. **Novelty in Parameter-Free Approach**: The claim that the SVD-based techniques are parameter-free is potentially significant, as most topic coherence measures require careful parameter tuning. This could simplify the application of topic modeling in practical settings.

## Weaknesses

1. **Fundamental Technical Error in SVD Explanation**: The paper states "we use the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers" (Section 3.1). This is mathematically incorrect. For an n × d matrix A, SVD produces a V matrix of size d × d (not n × n), containing only d eigenvectors. The paper claims n eigenvectors, which would require n ≤ d, but in topic modeling, n (number of words) is typically much larger than d (dimensionality of word embeddings). This fundamental misunderstanding of SVD undermines the core methodological contribution and raises serious doubts about the validity of the TBuckets-SVD approach.

2. **Mathematically Incorrect Threshold Formula**: The threshold calculation "AWES − AWPSAWES" (Section 3.1) is ambiguous and mathematically incorrect. The expression is unclear whether it's AWES - (AWPS × AWES) or AWES - (AWPS/AWES), but as written, it's ambiguous and likely incorrect. This is a critical flaw in the reorganization step that could lead to incorrect bucket assignments.

3. **False Claim of Parameter-Free Techniques**: The paper repeatedly claims the SVD-based techniques are "completely parameter free," but the reorganization step involves a threshold that's topic-dependent and calculated using topic-specific values (AWPS and AWES). This is not truly parameter-free; it's topic-dependent, which means the method still requires a parameter (the threshold calculation) that varies per topic. This misrepresentation of the method's characteristics is a significant issue.

4. **Numerous Formatting and Citation Errors**: The paper contains numerous formatting inconsistencies and citation errors throughout, including:
   - Inconsistent use of hyphens (TBuckets-Clustering vs. TBucketsClustering)
   - Inconsistent citation formats (e.g., "Röder et al.,? 2015" instead of "Röder et al., 2015")
   - Typos ("im-proving" instead of "improving")
   - Inconsistent capitalization ("Word Buckets" vs. "word buckets")
   - Mathematical notation errors (e.g., "LDA Order Weight(wi) = 1 log(i+ 1) if i > 0" instead of "1/log(i+1)")

5. **Unexplained Normalization in Results**: The paper states "TBuckets-SVD-Reorg coherence scores are normalized to lie between 0 and 1" (Table 2), but the human scores are between 1 and 3. The examples in Table 2 show human scores like ".542" and "2.3125," which are inconsistent with the 1-3 scale. This lack of clear explanation about the normalization process undermines the interpretability of the results.

## Questions to Authors

1. The SVD explanation states "we use the first n eigenvectors" for an n × d matrix. Since SVD of an n × d matrix produces only d eigenvectors (d < n), how does this work in practice? Can you clarify this fundamental misunderstanding?

2. The threshold calculation is written as "AWES − AWPSAWES." Is this meant to be AWES - (AWPS × AWES) or AWES - (AWPS/AWES)? The current notation is ambiguous and mathematically incorrect.

3. The paper claims the SVD-based techniques are "completely parameter free," but the reorganization step involves a threshold calculated from topic-specific values. How is this considered parameter-free? Can you clarify this apparent contradiction?

## Additional Comments

The paper contains numerous minor but distracting errors that detract from its professionalism. For example, the coherent topic example contains the step "Word Seen: storm" when processing the topic {storm, weather, wind, ...}, which is inconsistent with the topic order. Such errors suggest a lack of careful proofreading.

The claim of "completely parameter free" techniques is particularly problematic given the reorganization step. The threshold calculation depends on topic-specific values (AWPS and AWES), making it a parameter that varies per topic, which contradicts the claim of being parameter-free.

The fundamental error in the SVD explanation is especially concerning, as it indicates a misunderstanding of a core mathematical concept that the paper relies on. Without a correct understanding of SVD, the entire TBuckets-SVD technique is questionable.

## Recommendation

**Reject**

While the paper addresses an important problem in topic modeling and presents an intuitive approach, the fundamental technical error in the SVD explanation, the mathematically incorrect threshold formula, and the false claim of parameter-free techniques are substantial flaws that undermine the paper's credibility. These issues are not minor technicalities but rather fundamental misunderstandings of the core methodology.

The numerous formatting and citation errors further diminish the paper's professionalism. Given the stringent standards of CoNLL, these issues collectively warrant rejection, despite the paper's interesting conceptual approach.
