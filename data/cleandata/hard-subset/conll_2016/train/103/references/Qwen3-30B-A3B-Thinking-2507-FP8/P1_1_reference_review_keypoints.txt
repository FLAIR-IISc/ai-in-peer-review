- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, measuring topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic "buckets" and judging coherence by a dominant bucket presence; evaluates three bucket-creation techniques (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) on three datasets and for weakly supervised classification.

- Contribution 1: Introduces the TBuckets concept — coherent topics form a single dominant bucket while incoherent ones form multiple smaller buckets.

- Contribution 2: Proposes parameter-free SVD-based techniques (TBuckets-SVD, TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that avoid tuning required by prior measures.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification via selecting high-quality topics for human annotation.

- Strength: Novel and intuitive idea that aligns with human perception of topic coherence, with clear motivating examples.

- Strength: Parameter-free SVD approaches are a major practical advantage over methods requiring careful parameter tuning.

- Strength: Strong empirical results — TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG in Pearson correlation with human judgments.

- Strength: Practical application shows improved classification accuracy (3/4 datasets) and notable gains on difficult tasks (up to 5% F1).

- Strength: Clear, standard evaluation methodology (Pearson correlation with human annotations) enabling direct comparison.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification explaining why bucket structure should correlate with coherence or why it outperforms PMI/semantic methods.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons — only compared to Roder et al. (2015) and not to more recent coherence measures (e.g., Liu et al., Wang et al.).

- Weakness: Ambiguity in "parameter-free" claim — SVD-Reorg uses a data-dependent threshold (AWES - AWPS*AWES), which resembles an implicit parameter.

- Weakness: Limited analysis on why the approach works — no breakdown of topic types benefiting most or why SVD outperforms clustering.

- Weakness: Potential domain limitations — using general GloVe embeddings may be suboptimal for specialized domains like Genomics; domain-specific embeddings not explored.

- Question 1: How is the reorganization threshold considered parameter-free given it depends on computed topic values; can it be made truly parameter-free?

- Question 2: Can the authors compare TBuckets to more recent coherence methods (e.g., Liu et al. 2015, Wang et al. 2016) to better establish state-of-the-art standing?

- Question 3: Would domain-specific embeddings (e.g., biomedical) improve performance on datasets like Genomics?

- Question 4: Please describe the human annotation process for topic evaluation and how selecting high-quality topics reduced annotation effort.

- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the "parameter-free" claim and broaden comparisons to recent work.
