- Proposes TBuckets: measure topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic “buckets” where coherent topics form one dominant bucket and incoherent ones form multiple medium-sized buckets.  
- Introduces three bucket-creation methods: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Evaluated on 20NG, NYT, and Genomics datasets; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior methods on two of three datasets.  
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is claimed to be completely parameter-free.  
- Main contribution: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg as a parameter-free, high-performing coherence measure.  
- Main contribution: Human-centric insight that coherence corresponds to thematic grouping of words.  
- Main contribution: Demonstrated practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification through topic selection.  
- Strength: Novel, intuitive approach that aligns well with human judgments and is clearly illustrated with examples.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation across standard datasets and competitive comparison to Roder et al. (2015).  
- Strength: Practical application shown—selecting high-quality topics improves classification performance on difficult datasets.  
- Weakness: Insufficiently differentiated from Roder et al. (2015); novelty relative to that framework is not clearly explained.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks discussion of limitations (e.g., very short topics or genuinely multi-themed topics).  
- Weakness: Reorganization step (SVD-Reorg) is described but not theoretically explained why SVD misplaces words.  
- Questions for authors: clarify why dynamic thresholding is parameter-free; explain anomaly where low-coherence topics beat high-coherence ones in MEDICAL vs SPACE; discuss effects of different/domain-specific embeddings.  
- Overall: Well-executed, human-aligned, and practically valuable contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify novelty, limitations, and the parameter-free claim.
