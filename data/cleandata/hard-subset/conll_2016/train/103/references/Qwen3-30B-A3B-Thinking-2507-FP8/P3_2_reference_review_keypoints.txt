- Proposes TBuckets: measure topic coherence by clustering topic words into semantic “buckets” and preferring topics with a single dominant bucket.  
- Introduces three variants: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (the latter is parameter-free and best).  
- Evaluated on 20NG, NYT, and Genomics against human annotations; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior methods on NYT and 20NG.  
- Main contribution: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art coherence measurement without parameter tuning.  
- Main contribution: Provides a human-centered interpretation of coherence via thematic word grouping.  
- Main contribution: Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification when filtering topics by TBuckets.  
- Strength: Bucket concept is intuitive and aligns with human topic interpretation, supported by illustrative examples.  
- Strength: Parameter-free nature addresses real-world usability by eliminating tuning of common coherence parameters.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments and comparisons (notably to Röder et al., 2015) across three datasets.  
- Strength: Clear description of the reorganization step, including dynamic thresholding based on AWPS/AWES.  
- Strength: Good situating within existing literature and benchmarking against prior work.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why number/size of buckets should correlate with human coherence judgments.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to more recent coherence methods (e.g., contextualized embeddings and 2022–2023 work).  
- Weakness: Reorganization threshold formula (AWES - AWPS*AWES) and calculation of AWPS/AWES are ambiguously explained.  
- Weakness: Paper does not analyze why TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms others or provide concrete examples where reorganization helps.  
- Weakness: Potential overfitting to three well-studied datasets; unclear generalization to larger/diverse datasets or non-LDA models.  
- Question: How are AWPS and AWES computed, why that threshold formula, and how would it change with different embeddings?  
- Question: Is performance sensitive to embedding choice (GloVe vs. contextual/BERT embeddings)?  
- Question: Can authors give specific topic examples where TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al.?  
- Question: Any user studies validating that bucket grouping matches human interpretation beyond coherence scores?  
- Question: Is the method dependent on LDA-specific word ordering, and how does it apply to topic models without word order?  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical grounding, update comparisons, clarify reorganization details, and test generalization.
