- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic buckets (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) where coherence = one dominant bucket; evaluated on 20NG, NYT, Genomics and claims SVD-Reorg is parameter-free and outperforms prior work.

- Contribution — perspective: Introduces a novel bucket-based conceptual framework for measuring topic coherence distinct from pairwise similarity methods.

- Contribution — parameter-free: Offers SVD-based methods claimed to require no parameter tuning, easing practical use.

- Contribution — application: Demonstrates utility by improving text classification when filtering high-quality topics for annotation.

- Strength: Strong empirical improvements on NYT and 20NG (higher Pearson r), using standard datasets and metrics.

- Strength: Practicality of methods that purportedly avoid parameter tuning compared to traditional coherence measures.

- Strength: Real-world validation via improved text classification performance using TBuckets-selected topics.

- Strength: Clear, illustrative analysis of bucketing behavior for coherent vs. incoherent topics.

- Weakness: Claim of being "completely parameter free" is misleading because the Reorg step uses a topic-dependent threshold (AWPS/AWES).

- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to more recent coherence measures (e.g., NPMI/Lau et al. 2014) — may not truly be state-of-the-art comparison.

- Weakness: Lacks deeper theoretical justification for why a single dominant bucket should indicate coherence.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of failure cases (e.g., multi-themed but coherent topics).

- Weakness: Human annotation protocol is under-specified (no inter-annotator agreement reported), calling evaluation reliability into question.

- Question: Clarify the "parameter-free" claim vis-à-vis the topic-dependent reorganization threshold (AWPS/AWES).

- Question: How does TBuckets compare to more recent coherence measures (e.g., Lau et al. 2014 NPMI and post-2015 work)?

- Question: Why does TBuckets-SVD-Reorg perform substantially worse on Genomics (0.68 vs. 0.773 state-of-the-art)?

- Question: Why does EVS (eigenvector-based score) add little value compared to other metrics, and is it necessary?

- Question: Explain why MED-SPACE of 20NG is easier for classification and how that relates to topic coherence.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify parameter-freeness and include more recent baseline comparisons; core idea and empirical utility are valuable.
