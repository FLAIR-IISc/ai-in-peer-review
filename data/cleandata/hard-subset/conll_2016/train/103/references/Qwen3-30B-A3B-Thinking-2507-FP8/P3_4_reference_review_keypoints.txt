- Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic buckets to measure coherence, with three methods: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Contributions: introduces a human-centered bucketing framework, offers parameter-free SVD variants that outperform tuned baselines, and demonstrates practical gains in weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strength: novel conceptual framework aligning with human interpretation of topics via word grouping.  
- Strength: TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg perform well without parameter tuning, shown by strong Pearson correlations (e.g., NYT r=0.837).  
- Strength: strong empirical validation across datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics) and improved downstream classification in most cases.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification linking bucket size distribution to coherence; risks being seen as heuristic.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited to Röder et al. (2015) and omit more recent methods (e.g., Gao 2020, Wang 2022) that may outperform.  
- Weakness: “parameter-free” claim is ambiguous because the reorganization step uses a dataset-dependent dynamic threshold.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations, e.g., handling multi-theme topics or non-lexical/conceptual coherence.  
- Question: How does TBuckets handle topics with multiple equally important themes—split naturally or forced into one bucket?  
- Question: How does the dynamic threshold (AWES - AWPS*AWES) reconcile with the claim of being parameter-free given its dataset dependence?  
- Question: Why is SVD needed on top of GloVe embeddings—what’s the theoretical necessity for orthogonal dimensions?  
- Overall assessment: strong, practically valuable paper with solid empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address theory, comparisons, and clarify parameter claims.
