This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach to evaluating topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic buckets. The core idea—that a coherent topic should result in a single, large bucket—is intuitively appealing and represents a refreshing shift from existing coherence measures. The authors explore three instantiation techniques (clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization) and demonstrate, through correlation with human judgment, that TBuckets, particularly the SVD-based version with reorganization, outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on several datasets. A particularly strong point is the parameter-free nature of the SVD-based techniques. The application to weakly supervised text classification, demonstrating improved performance via topic quality filtering, further solidifies the practical relevance of the work.

**Contributions:**

1. **Novel Coherence Metric:** The TBuckets approach introduces a fundamentally new way to assess topic coherence, moving beyond simple word-level statistics and embracing a more holistic, thematic evaluation.
2. **Effective SVD-Based Implementation:** The SVD-based TBuckets, especially with reorganization, demonstrates superior performance to established coherence measures, offering a compelling alternative for topic evaluation.
3. **Practical Application:** The successful application of TBuckets to improve weakly supervised text classification highlights its utility beyond mere topic assessment and showcases its potential impact on downstream NLP tasks.

**Strengths:**

1. **Bold and Innovative Idea:** The concept of grouping words into buckets to represent thematic coherence is genuinely novel. Most existing coherence measures rely on variations of word co-occurrence or semantic similarity, while TBuckets captures a more nuanced understanding of topic structure.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:**  The paper consistently demonstrates statistically significant improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods, particularly with the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg technique. Outperforming established baselines is a strong indicator of the method's effectiveness.
3. **Parameter-Free Advantage:** The SVD-based approaches, requiring no parameter tuning, are a significant advantage. This simplifies usage and reduces the risk of overfitting or suboptimal performance due to parameter selection.
4. **Demonstrated Practicality:** The successful application to text classification underscores the practical value of the proposed metric. Showing that better coherence, as measured by TBuckets, translates to improved downstream task performance is compelling.
5. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The methodology is clearly explained, and the experimental setup is well-defined.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Error Analysis:** While the results are positive, the paper lacks detailed error analysis. Understanding *why* TBuckets succeeds in some cases and fails in others would provide valuable insights and guide future improvements. Are there specific topic characteristics that favor or hinder the performance of TBuckets?
2. **Reorganization Threshold Heuristic:** The dynamic threshold for reorganization in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, while clever, feels somewhat ad-hoc. While the authors justify the components (AWPS and AWES), the specific formula isn't deeply motivated. A more thorough ablation study exploring different thresholding strategies would be beneficial.
3. **Dependence on Word Embeddings:** The performance of TBuckets is inherently tied to the quality of the underlying word embeddings. The paper relies on pre-trained GloVe embeddings. Investigating the sensitivity of TBuckets to different embedding models or training data would strengthen the robustness claims.
4. **Scope of Evaluation:** The experiments are primarily focused on comparing TBuckets against existing coherence measures. Exploring its correlation with other aspects of topic model quality (e.g., perplexity, topic diversity) could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide examples of topics where TBuckets-SVD-Reorg *failed* to accurately capture coherence (i.e., low correlation with human judgment)? What characteristics did these topics share?
2.  Could you elaborate on the rationale behind the specific formula used for the dynamic reorganization threshold? Did you explore alternative weighting schemes or thresholding approaches?
3.  Have you investigated the sensitivity of TBuckets to different pre-trained word embeddings or to embeddings trained on different corpora?
4.  Beyond text classification, are there other downstream tasks where you envision TBuckets being particularly useful?



**Additional Comments:**

The paper is a strong contribution to the field of topic modeling. The novelty of the approach and the promising empirical results outweigh the minor weaknesses. While further investigation into the error analysis, thresholding strategy, and embedding dependence would be valuable, the current work is solid and deserves acceptance. The authors have a clear vision and a promising new metric for evaluating topic quality.
