## Review of "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, for evaluating the coherence and quality of topics generated by Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). The core idea is to group topic words into thematic "buckets" based on semantic similarity, arguing that a coherent topic will exhibit a single large bucket with smaller supporting ones. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation – clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization – and demonstrate improved correlation with human judgments compared to state-of-the-art methods on several datasets. A particularly noteworthy claim is the parameter-free nature of their SVD-based approaches. The paper also briefly explores applying the coherence scores to improve weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Novel Coherence Measure:** The TBuckets approach offers a conceptually intuitive and potentially effective way to quantify topic coherence by focusing on the *structure* of word relationships within a topic, rather than relying solely on pairwise word similarity or statistical co-occurrence.
2. **Effective SVD-Based Implementation:** The SVD-based TBuckets techniques, especially TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, achieve strong performance *without* requiring parameter tuning, which is a significant advantage over many existing coherence measures.
3. **Demonstrated Practical Benefit:**  The paper demonstrates that utilizing TBuckets coherence scores to pre-select high-quality topics for weakly supervised classification can lead to improved results, showcasing a potential real-world application.



**Strengths:**

1. **Intuitive Concept:** The idea of judging topic coherence by how naturally words group into themes resonates with how humans evaluate topics. The bucket analogy is clear and well-motivated.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The SVD-based TBuckets techniques (especially with reorganization) consistently outperform or match the state-of-the-art on the tested datasets, which is a strong indicator of the method's effectiveness. The outperformance on NYT and 20NG is particularly encouraging.
3. **Parameter-Free Advantage:** The lack of parameter tuning for TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is a major practical benefit.  This makes the method readily applicable without needing extensive experimentation to find optimal settings.
4. **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and explains the approach clearly, including helpful examples to illustrate the bucketing process. The experimental setup and results are presented in a straightforward manner.
5. **Relevant Application:** The application to weakly supervised text classification demonstrates the practical utility of the proposed coherence measure, showing that it can be used to improve downstream tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the *combination* of ideas in TBuckets is novel, the individual components (word embeddings, SVD, clustering) are well-established techniques.  The novelty lies primarily in *how* these are combined and applied to topic coherence evaluation. This weakens the claim of a completely new methodological breakthrough.
2. **Sensitivity to Word Embeddings:**  The performance of TBuckets heavily relies on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings. The authors only used GloVe embeddings. The results might be significantly different with different embedding models (e.g., Word2Vec, fastText) or embeddings trained on a different corpus, and this sensitivity is not sufficiently discussed. 
3. **Reorganization Heuristic:** The reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, while effective, relies on somewhat arbitrary thresholds based on average word pair similarity and eigenvector similarity. The justification for this dynamic threshold, while explained, feels ad-hoc and could potentially be further refined.  More analysis on the impact of different thresholding strategies would be valuable.
4. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper presents correlation coefficients but lacks a more in-depth analysis of *where* TBuckets succeeds and fails.  Examining specific topics where TBuckets' score diverges significantly from human judgments would provide valuable insights.
5. **Dataset Limitations:** Evaluating on only three datasets limits the generalizability of the findings. Exploring the performance of TBuckets on a wider range of datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different domain, different topic sizes) is important.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Embedding Sensitivity:** How sensitive are the results to the choice of word embeddings? Have you experimented with other pre-trained embeddings (e.g., Word2Vec, fastText) or embeddings trained on a different corpus?  Could you provide a table showing the performance with different embedding models?
2.  **Reorganization Threshold:**  What is the impact of different settings for the dynamic threshold used in the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg step?  Did you explore alternative methods for determining this threshold?  Is there a risk of overfitting the threshold to the specific datasets used?
3.  **Error Analysis:** Could you present a few examples of topics where TBuckets' coherence score significantly differs from human judgment? What are the characteristics of these topics, and why might TBuckets be failing to accurately assess their coherence?
4. **Computational Cost:** What is the computational complexity of each TBuckets technique? How does the runtime scale with the number of words in a topic and the size of the vocabulary? Is this a barrier to applying TBuckets to very large datasets?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The related work section is fairly comprehensive, but could benefit from briefly mentioning recent advances in neural topic models and their impact on topic coherence evaluation.
*   The application to text classification is a good starting point, but exploring other downstream tasks where topic coherence is important (e.g., topic model interpretability, topic model comparison) would strengthen the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a promising new approach to topic coherence evaluation. The TBuckets method, particularly the SVD-based variations, demonstrates strong empirical performance and offers a practical advantage in terms of parameter-free operation. While the novelty of the individual components is limited, the combination and application are insightful.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly regarding embedding sensitivity and the reorganization heuristic, would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.
