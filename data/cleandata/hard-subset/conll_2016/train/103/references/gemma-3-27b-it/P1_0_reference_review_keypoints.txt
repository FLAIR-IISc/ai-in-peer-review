- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic "buckets" (clustering/SVD/SVD-reorg) to measure LDA topic coherence and shows SVD-reorg correlates best with human judgments and helps weakly supervised classification.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel, intuitive coherence metric (TBuckets) that mimics human grouping of topic words beyond PMI-style statistics.

- Contribution 2: Presents effective SVD-based implementations, with the parameter-free SVD-reorg variant achieving strong empirical performance.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates practical utility by using TBuckets to select high-quality topics for weakly supervised text classification.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results showing TBuckets-SVD-Reorg consistently yields higher correlation with human judgments across datasets.

- Strength 2: Intuitive and well-motivated approach that aligns with human assessments of topic coherence.

- Strength 3: Parameter-free SVD methods improve usability and reproducibility compared to many coherence metrics.

- Strength 4: Comprehensive evaluation using established datasets and Pearson correlation, plus a practical application example.

- Strength 5: Clear, well-structured writing that makes the methodology and experiments easy to follow.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty in individual components (embeddings, SVD, clustering); the paper must better justify the uniqueness of their integration.

- Weakness 2: Reorganization step appears ad-hoc with arbitrary AWPS/AWES thresholding and lacks theoretical grounding.

- Weakness 3: Reliance on pre-trained GloVe embeddings raises concerns about generalizability across embedding types or domains.

- Weakness 4: Limited error analysis—paper lacks detailed examination of why TBuckets outperforms or fails on specific topics.

- Weakness 5: Narrow application scope; classification demonstration is limited and other uses (model selection, hyperparameter tuning) are unexplored.

- Question 1: Please justify the theoretical motivation for AWPS/AWES thresholding in SVD-Reorg and whether alternative strategies were considered.

- Question 2: How sensitive are results to AWPS/AWES values—can you provide a sensitivity analysis across datasets?

- Question 3: Did you test other embeddings (Word2Vec, FastText) or domain-trained embeddings, and how did performance vary?

- Question 4: Can you supply a qualitative error analysis showing topic examples where TBuckets helps or fails compared to baselines?

- Question 5: Beyond classification, what other applications do you foresee for TBuckets and will you explore them?

- Additional comment 1: Include discussion of computational complexity for the different TBuckets techniques.

- Additional comment 2: Add visualizations of SVD-Reorg buckets for example topics to illustrate thematic groupings.

- Additional comment 3: Fix missing publication details in references (e.g., Aletras and Stevenson, 2013).
