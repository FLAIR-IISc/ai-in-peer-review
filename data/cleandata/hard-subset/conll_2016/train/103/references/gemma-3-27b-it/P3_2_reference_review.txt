## Review for "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces TBuckets, a novel approach to evaluating the coherence of topics generated by Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). The core idea revolves around grouping topic words into thematic "buckets," with the rationale being that a coherent topic will exhibit a single large bucket and few smaller ones. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation – clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization – and demonstrate, through correlation with human annotations, that their method outperforms state-of-the-art approaches on several datasets. A key claim is that the SVD-based methods are parameter-free, offering a significant advantage. The authors also showcase the utility of TBuckets in improving weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A conceptually intuitive approach to topic coherence assessment:**  The 'bucket' metaphor offers a readily understandable way to evaluate topic quality, mirroring how humans assess coherence. This is a valuable contribution beyond purely mathematical measures.
2. **Empirical demonstration of superior performance:**  The reported results, particularly for the SVD-Reorg method, showing outperformance on NYT and 20NG datasets compared to existing methods, are noteworthy and suggest a real improvement in coherence measurement.
3. **Parameter-free SVD-based methods:** The simplicity and lack of tuning required for the SVD-based approaches represent a practical advantage over methods requiring parameter optimization.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear and well-motivated approach:** The paper clearly explains the intuition behind TBuckets and provides compelling examples to illustrate how the bucketing process reflects topic coherence. The motivation, stemming from how humans interpret topics, is strong.
2. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors evaluate their methods on three datasets, using a well-established evaluation metric (Pearson’s correlation) and comparing against relevant state-of-the-art methods.  The inclusion of the text classification application adds practical value.
3. **Strong Results:** The reported correlation coefficients demonstrate a clear advantage of the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg method, particularly on the NYT and 20NG datasets.  The consistently good performance across datasets is encouraging.
4. **Detailed explanation of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg:** The paper provides a thorough explanation of the reorganization step, including the rationale behind the dynamic threshold calculation. This level of detail is essential for reproducibility and understanding.
5. **Practical Application:** Demonstrating the use of TBuckets for improving weakly supervised text classification enhances the practical relevance of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Underlying Techniques:** While the combination and application of existing techniques (word embeddings, SVD, clustering) to topic coherence is clever, the individual components themselves aren't novel. The primary novelty lies in *how* these techniques are combined and applied to the bucketing problem. This should be better acknowledged in the paper.
2. **Sensitivity to Word Embeddings:** The performance of TBuckets heavily relies on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings. The paper doesn't explore the impact of different embedding models or domain-specific embeddings on the results. This could be a significant limitation.  A brief ablation study varying the embeddings would strengthen the work.
3. **Dynamic Threshold in Reorganization - Justification Limited:** While the explanation for the dynamic threshold in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is detailed, the justification for the specific formula (AWES - AWPS / AWES) feels somewhat arbitrary.  More theoretical or empirical justification for this specific calculation is needed.  Why does this ratio appropriately capture the flexibility needed?
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:**  The paper would benefit from a qualitative error analysis. Showing examples of topics where TBuckets performs well *and* poorly, and analyzing *why* it struggles in certain cases, would provide valuable insights.
5. **Comparison Limited to Roder et al. (2015):**  While comparing to the state-of-the-art is important, the field of topic coherence evaluation is broader.  A brief discussion of how TBuckets compares to *other* recent approaches (e.g., those based on neural topic models) would be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of word embeddings? Have you experimented with different pre-trained embeddings or considered training embeddings specific to the datasets used?
2.  Can you provide more theoretical justification for the formula used to calculate the dynamic threshold in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?  What alternatives did you consider, and why was this particular formula chosen?
3.  Could you provide examples of topics where TBuckets performs poorly, and discuss the reasons for the failure?  What characteristics of these topics make them difficult for TBuckets to evaluate?
4.  How does the performance of TBuckets compare to more recent approaches to topic coherence evaluation, particularly those leveraging neural topic models?
5.  The description of "LDA Order Weight" is a bit unclear. How does this weight specifically contribute to the coherence score, and why is this weighting scheme chosen?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*   The use of a consistent notation throughout the paper is appreciated.
*   The inclusion of the source code would further enhance the reproducibility of the results.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a novel and promising approach to topic coherence evaluation. The strengths of the paper – a clear conceptual framework, strong empirical results, and practical application – outweigh the weaknesses.  While the individual components aren’t ground-breaking, the combination and application to the “bucket” metaphor provide a valuable contribution.  Addressing the weaknesses raised above, particularly regarding sensitivity to word embeddings and justification of the dynamic threshold, would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
