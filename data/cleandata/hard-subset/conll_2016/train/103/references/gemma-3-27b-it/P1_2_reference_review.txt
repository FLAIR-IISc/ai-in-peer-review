## Peer Review: Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, for evaluating the quality of topics generated by Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). The core idea is to group topic words into thematic "buckets" – a process mirroring human assessment of topic coherence. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), and SVD with reorganization. They evaluate their methods on three datasets, correlating coherence scores with human annotations and demonstrating improved performance compared to existing state-of-the-art methods, particularly highlighting the parameter-free nature of their SVD-based techniques.  They also showcase the utility of TBuckets in improving text classification performance with weakly supervised learning.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Novel Topic Coherence Metric:**  The TBuckets approach offers a conceptually intuitive and seemingly effective method for quantifying topic coherence, grounded in how humans assess topic quality. This provides a valuable addition to the existing suite of topic coherence measures.
2. **Effective SVD-based Implementation:** The SVD-based techniques (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) are particularly strong, demonstrating competitive performance *without* requiring parameter tuning, a significant advantage over many existing methods.
3. **Demonstrated Practical Application:**  Successfully applying TBuckets to improve weakly supervised text classification showcases the method’s practical utility beyond simply being a topic quality metric.



**Strengths:**

1. **Intuitive Approach:** The underlying principle of grouping words into thematic buckets closely aligns with human intuition regarding topic coherence. This makes the methodology easy to understand and motivates its potential effectiveness.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates consistent improvement over state-of-the-art coherence measures on multiple datasets, particularly with the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg technique. The correlation with human judgment provides solid evidence of its validity.
3. **Parameter-Free Advantage:** The SVD-based methods' lack of parameter tuning is a significant strength, reducing the burden on users and making the approach more accessible and reproducible.  This is clearly highlighted and justified.
4. **Practical Application & Validation:**  The demonstration of TBuckets' utility in improving text classification performance strengthens the practical relevance of the work.  The experimental design, comparing different strategies for using the coherence scores, is well-considered.
5. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The explanation of the TBuckets process, and particularly the SVD-based techniques, is clear and concise.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the combination of ideas is novel, the individual components – word embeddings, SVD, clustering – are well-established techniques.  The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of how TBuckets represents a *distinct* methodological advance beyond simply applying these techniques in a new combination.
2. **Reorganization Step Complexity:** The TBuckets-SVD-Reorg technique, while performing well, introduces a level of complexity with the dynamic threshold calculation. The justification for this specific threshold formula, while explained, feels somewhat ad-hoc and could benefit from more rigorous justification or experimentation.  The dependence on AWPS and AWES is potentially sensitive and might not generalize well to different datasets.
3. **Limited Error Analysis:**  The paper lacks detailed error analysis. Understanding *where* TBuckets struggles, and why, would provide valuable insights for future improvement.  For example, are there specific types of topics (e.g., highly abstract concepts) where the method performs poorly?
4. **Dataset Limitations:** The evaluation relies on datasets from Roder et al. (2015). While standard, exploring performance on a wider range of corpora with varying characteristics (e.g., different domain specificity, document length) would strengthen the generalizability claims.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Novelty:** Could you elaborate on the key innovation of TBuckets beyond the combination of existing techniques? What specific aspect of the methodology differentiates it most significantly from prior work in topic coherence measurement?
2. **Reorganization Threshold:** What sensitivity analysis was performed on the dynamic threshold calculation in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg? How robust are the results to variations in the AWPS and AWES values? Could you explore alternative thresholding strategies?
3. **Error Analysis:**  Can you provide any examples of topics where TBuckets performs poorly, and discuss the potential reasons for this? Are there specific characteristics of topics that seem to negatively impact the method's performance?
4. **Computational Cost:** What is the computational complexity of the different TBuckets techniques (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg)?  Is the SVD-Reorg technique computationally demanding for large topics or corpora?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper cites a lot of previous work, which is good, but some citations are a bit stale. Incorporating more recent advances in topic modeling and coherence measures would be beneficial.
*   The figures in the paper are clear and effective.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of using human judgments as the gold standard for coherence. Subjectivity in human assessment is a known issue.




Overall, this is a promising and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of topic modeling.  Addressing the weaknesses identified above, particularly regarding the novelty and justification of the reorganization step, would significantly strengthen the submission. I recommend acceptance, pending satisfactory responses to the questions raised above.
