## Review: Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a new approach, TBuckets, for measuring topic coherence in Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) models. The core idea is to group topic words into “buckets” based on semantic similarity, with the rationale that a coherent topic will result in a distribution with a single large bucket and few smaller ones.  Three techniques are explored for bucket creation: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.  The authors evaluate their methods by correlating coherence scores with human annotations on three datasets and demonstrate performance comparable to or exceeding state-of-the-art methods. A key claim is the parameter-free nature of the SVD-based techniques. The paper also briefly explores the application of TBuckets in improving text classification performance.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Novel Topic Coherence Metric:** The TBuckets approach presents a conceptually intuitive and potentially valuable method for assessing topic quality, moving beyond purely statistical measures.
2. **Effective SVD-Based Implementation:** The SVD-based TBuckets techniques offer a compelling advantage by being parameter-free, simplifying application and reducing the risk of overfitting during coherence assessment.
3. **Demonstrated Empirical Performance:**  The experimental results showing competitive performance against existing state-of-the-art methods provides initial evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.



**Strengths:**

1. **Intuitive and Well-Motivated Approach:** The core idea of grouping words into thematic buckets aligns with how humans intuitively assess topic coherence. The paper clearly articulates this intuition and provides a concrete methodology for implementing it. The example illustrating bucket creation for coherent and incoherent topics is particularly helpful.
2. **Parameter-Free SVD Methods:**  The SVD-based techniques are a significant strength. Many existing coherence measures require careful parameter tuning, and a parameter-free approach offers a practical advantage.  This is explicitly highlighted and validated in the experimental results.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors evaluate their methods on three publicly available datasets, using a standard evaluation metric (Pearson’s r) and comparing against a strong baseline. The inclusion of datasets with varying characteristics (20NG, NYT, Genomics) strengthens the generalizability of the findings.
4. **Practical Application Demonstrated:** The brief exploration of using TBuckets to improve text classification accuracy provides a compelling application of the proposed metric and showcases its potential real-world utility. This adds value beyond simply a coherence assessment technique.
5. **Clear Presentation and Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it relatively easy to follow the proposed methodology and experimental results.



**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Idea:** While the implementation is novel, the underlying concept of assessing coherence by grouping semantically similar words isn't entirely new.  Existing approaches (e.g., using distributional semantics) already capture this idea. The paper needs to more explicitly position itself within the broader landscape of topic coherence metrics and clearly articulate *how* TBuckets offers a distinct advantage beyond these existing methods.
2. **Reorganization Step’s Heuristics:** The TBuckets-SVD-Reorg technique, while improving performance, relies on somewhat ad-hoc heuristics for determining when to move words between buckets. The threshold calculation based on AWPS and AWES feels empirically motivated without strong theoretical justification.  Further investigation into the sensitivity of this threshold and alternative approaches would be beneficial.
3. **Limited Analysis of Bucket Structure:** The paper primarily focuses on the size of the largest bucket as a coherence indicator.  A more detailed analysis of the *distribution* of bucket sizes (e.g., entropy, Gini coefficient) could potentially provide richer insights into topic coherence and lead to more nuanced metrics.  
4. **Lack of Ablation Study:**  While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, it doesn’t offer an ablation study to clearly isolate the contribution of the reorganization step. Quantifying the performance improvement specifically attributable to the reorganization would strengthen the analysis.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the computational complexity of each TBuckets technique (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg)? How does this scale with the number of topic words and the dimensionality of the word embeddings?
2.  The reorganization step relies on similarity thresholds. Can you provide a sensitivity analysis demonstrating how varying the AWPS-AWES ratio affects the performance of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?
3.  Beyond simply using the size of the largest bucket, have you explored other metrics based on the distribution of bucket sizes (e.g., entropy, Gini coefficient) to assess topic coherence? If so, what were the results?
4. You state that the SVD techniques are "completely parameter free," but the clustering approach requires a maximum distance threshold. Is there a strategy for selecting this threshold, or is it always chosen empirically?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The references section is extensive and well-organized.
*   Consider adding a visualization of example bucket structures for both coherent and incoherent topics to further illustrate the TBuckets approach.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper presenting a promising new approach to topic coherence assessment. The intuitive nature of TBuckets, coupled with the practical advantage of parameter-free SVD techniques and demonstrated competitive performance, make it a worthwhile contribution. Addressing the identified weaknesses, particularly clarifying the novelty and providing more justification for the reorganization heuristics, would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
