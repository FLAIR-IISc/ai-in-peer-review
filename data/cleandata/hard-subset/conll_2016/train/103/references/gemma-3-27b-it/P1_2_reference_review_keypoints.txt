- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic “buckets” to measure LDA topic coherence.  
- Explores three bucket-creation methods: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.  
- Evaluates on three datasets, showing improved correlation with human judgments vs. prior coherence measures.  
- Highlights parameter-free SVD-based methods as a practical advantage.  
- Demonstrates TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification, showing practical utility.  
- TBuckets is conceptually intuitive and aligns with human assessment of topic coherence.  
- Empirical results consistently outperform state-of-the-art, especially TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- SVD-based techniques require no parameter tuning, enhancing accessibility and reproducibility.  
- Practical application and experimental design for using coherence scores are well-considered.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the TBuckets procedures.  
- Individual components (embeddings, SVD, clustering) are established techniques, so novelty of components is limited.  
- The reorganization step (dynamic threshold) appears ad-hoc and may be sensitive to AWPS/AWES, needing stronger justification.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis identifying where TBuckets fails or topic types that challenge it.  
- Evaluation limited to Roder et al. (2015) datasets; broader corpora would strengthen generalizability claims.  
- Asks authors to clarify TBuckets’ key innovation beyond combining existing methods.  
- Requests sensitivity analysis for the SVD-Reorg dynamic threshold and exploration of alternate thresholding.  
- Asks for examples and discussion of topics where TBuckets performs poorly to inform improvements.  
- Requests computational complexity analysis for clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg, especially on large corpora.  
- Notes some citations are dated and suggests including more recent advances in topic modeling/coherence.  
- Recommends discussing limitations of using human judgments as the coherence gold standard.  
- Overall: promising, well-executed work recommended for acceptance pending satisfactory responses to weaknesses/questions.
