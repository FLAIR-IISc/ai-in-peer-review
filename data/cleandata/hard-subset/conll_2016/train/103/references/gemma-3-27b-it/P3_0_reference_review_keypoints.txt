- TBuckets groups topic words into semantic “buckets”; coherent topics produce one large bucket and few small ones.  
- The paper compares three bucket-creation methods: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with reorganization; SVD-Reorg performs best.  
- SVD-based techniques are effectively parameter-free, reducing the need for tuning.  
- Empirical results show TBuckets-SVD-Reorg matches or outperforms state-of-the-art coherence measures on standard datasets.  
- Ablation study with scoring methods (size, WL, EVS) gives useful insight into TBuckets behavior.  
- Demonstrated practical utility: filtering topics by TBuckets scores improves text classification performance.  
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and provides illustrative examples aligning with human judgments.  
- Heavy reliance on pre-trained embeddings (GloVe) may limit effectiveness in specialized domains; testing other embeddings is needed.  
- The reorganization dynamic threshold (AWES - AWPS / AWES) appears ad hoc and needs stronger justification or alternatives.  
- Analysis is limited to largest-bucket size; deeper examination of within-bucket similarity and inter-bucket relationships is missing.  
- SVD can be computationally expensive for large word sets; complexity analysis and scalability optimizations are required.  
- Key author questions: sensitivity to embedding choice, justification/sensitivity of threshold formula, analyzing bucket relationships, and computational complexity.  
- Suggestion to add a visual diagram of bucket formation to improve intuition.  
- Overall assessment: novel, promising, strong empirical support; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
