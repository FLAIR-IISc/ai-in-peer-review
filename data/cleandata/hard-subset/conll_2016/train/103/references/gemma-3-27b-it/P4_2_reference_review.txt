This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach for measuring topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets. The core idea is that coherent topics will result in a single large bucket, while less coherent topics will yield multiple smaller buckets. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with reorganization. They evaluate these techniques by correlating their coherence scores with human annotations and demonstrate improved performance compared to the state-of-the-art on several datasets. The SVD-based methods are highlighted as parameter-free.

**Contributions:**

1. **TBuckets Framework:** The primary contribution is the TBuckets framework itself, which provides a new perspective on topic coherence evaluation by focusing on the thematic grouping of words within a topic. This shifts the evaluation from solely considering word relationships to considering how well words coalesce into distinct themes.
2. **SVD-Based Methods:** The development of the SVD-based techniques (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) is a significant contribution. The parameter-free nature of these methods offers a practical advantage over existing approaches that require extensive parameter tuning. The reorganization step further improves the coherence measurement.
3. **Empirical Validation & Application:** The comprehensive experimental evaluation on multiple datasets and the demonstration of improved correlation with human judgments provide strong empirical support for the proposed framework.  Furthermore, illustrating the usefulness of the coherence scores in improving text classification performance adds practical value to the work.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Approach:** The TBuckets approach offers a novel and intuitive way to assess topic coherence. Unlike methods relying on statistical co-occurrence or semantic similarity of individual word pairs, TBuckets focuses on identifying underlying thematic structures within topics.
2. **Parameter-Free Methods:** The SVD-based techniques are a significant strength. The lack of tunable parameters simplifies the application of the method and reduces the risk of overfitting to specific datasets.  This is a practical benefit for researchers and practitioners.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results demonstrate that TBuckets consistently outperforms the state-of-the-art on the evaluated datasets. The high correlation with human annotations validates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
4. **Practical Application:** The demonstration of how TBuckets can be used to improve text classification performance showcases the practical relevance of the research.  This extends the value of the work beyond a purely theoretical contribution.
5. **Clear Explanation & Implementation Details:** The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the TBuckets framework, including the different techniques for bucket creation and the coherence score calculation. The inclusion of implementation details enhances reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Computational Cost:** While parameter-free, SVD can be computationally expensive, especially for large topics with many words. The paper doesn’t adequately address the computational complexity and scalability of the SVD-based methods.
2. **Sensitivity to Word Embeddings:** The performance of TBuckets relies heavily on the quality of the word embeddings used. The paper acknowledges using pre-trained embeddings but does not explore the impact of different embedding models or domain-specific embeddings.
3. **Reorganization Heuristics:** The reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg relies on dynamically computed thresholds based on average word similarity. While the authors motivate this approach, the heuristic nature of the threshold calculation could be a potential source of instability or suboptimal performance. A more rigorous analysis of the threshold selection process would strengthen the paper.
4. **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** The evaluation is primarily focused on datasets used in previous work. While this allows for direct comparison, it limits the generalizability of the findings. Evaluating TBuckets on more diverse and challenging datasets would provide a more comprehensive assessment of its performance.
5. **Lack of Ablation Study:** The paper does not include an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of each component of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (e.g., the impact of the reorganization step alone). Such an analysis would provide a better understanding of the effectiveness of the individual components.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. What is the computational complexity of the SVD-based techniques, and how does it scale with the number of words in a topic?
2. Have you explored the impact of using different word embedding models on the performance of TBuckets?
3. Could you provide a more detailed justification for the dynamic threshold calculation in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?  What is the sensitivity of the results to changes in this threshold?
4. Have you considered evaluating TBuckets on datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different domain, different topic sizes)?
5. Could you provide an ablation study to assess the individual contributions of the different components of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD, Reorganization)?
