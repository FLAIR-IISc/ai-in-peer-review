## Review for "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, for evaluating the coherence of topics generated by Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). The core idea is to group topic words into thematic buckets, with the assumption that coherent topics will result in a single large bucket and few smaller ones. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation – clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization – and demonstrate improved correlation with human judgment compared to state-of-the-art methods on three datasets. A key claim is that the SVD-based approaches are parameter-free, offering an advantage over existing techniques.  The paper also briefly explores an application of TBuckets for improving text classification performance.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Novel Topic Coherence Metric:** The TBuckets approach offers a conceptually sound and intuitively appealing method for evaluating topic quality.  The idea of grouping words based on semantic relatedness to assess overall topic coherence is a valuable contribution.
2. **Effective SVD-based Implementation:** The SVD-based techniques (especially with reorganization) demonstrate competitive performance, achieving state-of-the-art results on some datasets, and importantly, do so *without* parameter tuning, which is a significant practical advantage.
3. **Demonstration of Practical Application:** The brief exploration of using TBuckets for quality control in weakly-supervised text classification showcases a potential real-world application of the proposed method.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:**  The paper provides compelling experimental results showing that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on two of the three datasets tested. This validates the approach and suggests it is a promising alternative for topic coherence evaluation.
2. **Parameter-Free Advantage:** The parameter-free nature of the SVD-based methods is a significant strength. Many existing coherence metrics require careful parameter tuning, making them less accessible and potentially sensitive to specific datasets. The lack of tuning simplifies the application of TBuckets.
3. **Clear Explanation and Motivation:** The paper clearly explains the motivation behind TBuckets, grounding it in the human process of interpreting topics. The illustrative examples of bucket formation for coherent and incoherent topics are particularly helpful in understanding the approach.
4. **Thorough Experimentation:** The use of three datasets (20NG, NYT, and Genomics) and comparison to a recent state-of-the-art method (Roder et al., 2015) contribute to the robustness of the evaluation.
5. **Practical Application Demonstrated:** The brief application to text classification, showing improvement through topic quality filtering, highlights the potential practical utility beyond just topic evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Underlying Techniques:** While the combination of SVD and word embeddings within the TBuckets framework is novel, the individual components (SVD, word embeddings, clustering) are well-established. The primary contribution lies in the *application* of these techniques to topic coherence rather than groundbreaking algorithmic innovation.
2. **Complexity of Reorganization Step:** The TBuckets-SVD-Reorg method, while achieving the best results, introduces complexity through the dynamic threshold calculation. Although the authors aim to avoid parameter tuning, the dynamic threshold itself relies on computed values (AWPS, AWES) and the rationale behind those specific calculations could be further elucidated.
3. **Application Section is Brief:** The discussion of the text classification application is rather limited. A more extensive evaluation with different datasets and classification algorithms would strengthen the argument for the practical usefulness of TBuckets.  The improvements shown in Table 3 are promising but not particularly dramatic.
4. **Reliance on GloVe Embeddings:** The results are tied to the GloVe word embeddings.  It's unclear how performance would vary with different embedding techniques (e.g., Word2Vec, fastText) or domain-specific embeddings.  



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Threshold Calculation:** Could you provide a more intuitive explanation of why the dynamic threshold for reorganization is calculated as `AWES − AWPS / AWES`? What are the potential drawbacks of this specific formulation?
2. **Embedding Sensitivity:** Have you experimented with different word embedding techniques (e.g., Word2Vec, fastText) and, if so, how did they impact the performance of TBuckets?
3. **Computational Cost:**  What is the computational complexity of the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg algorithm, particularly for large topics with a significant number of words?  Is it scalable to very large datasets?
4. **Error Analysis:** Can you provide examples of topics where TBuckets performs poorly, and discuss possible reasons for the discrepancy between the automated score and human judgment?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.
*   The references are comprehensive.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach (e.g., sensitivity to the quality of the underlying word embeddings).




**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper.  While the individual techniques used aren’t entirely novel, the TBuckets framework provides a compelling and effective approach to topic coherence evaluation. The strong empirical results, the parameter-free nature of the SVD-based methods, and the potential for practical applications make this a valuable contribution to the field of topic modeling. Addressing the weaknesses and answering the questions raised during the author response period will further strengthen the paper.
