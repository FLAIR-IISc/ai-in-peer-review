## Review for "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces TBuckets, a novel approach for evaluating the coherence of topics inferred from Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) models. The core idea is to group topic words into “buckets” representing sub-themes, with the rationale being that a coherent topic will exhibit a dominant, large bucket and smaller, less populated ones. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), and a reorganization step applied to the SVD method (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg). They evaluate their methods on three datasets, comparing their correlation with human judgements to state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A conceptually intuitive approach to topic coherence:** The “bucket” metaphor provides a clear and understandable way to reason about topic quality, aligning with how humans naturally interpret topics.
2. **Empirically strong performance of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg:** The SVD-based method, particularly with reorganization, achieves competitive or superior results compared to existing methods on the tested datasets. This is especially noteworthy given its parameter-free nature.
3. **Demonstration of practical utility:** The paper shows that utilizing TBuckets to pre-select high-quality topics can improve the performance of weakly supervised text classification.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty and Intuition:** The TBuckets approach offers a fresh perspective on topic coherence evaluation.  Moving beyond purely statistical measures like PMI, it attempts to capture the semantic structure *within* a topic in a more human-aligned way. The bucket metaphor is easy to grasp and justifies the method logically.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents solid experimental results, demonstrating that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms the state-of-the-art on two out of three datasets.  The claim of being parameter-free is a significant advantage, as parameter tuning can be a bottleneck in many topic modeling evaluations.
3. **Practical Application:**  The demonstration of improved classification performance when using TBuckets to filter topics is a valuable addition. It showcases the real-world applicability of the proposed method and adds weight to its utility.
4. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the TBuckets approach and its different variations. The example runs with the coherent and non-coherent topics in Section 3 effectively illustrate the underlying idea.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of SVD Component:** While the TBuckets framework is novel, the application of SVD itself is not groundbreaking.  The contribution lies in *how* it's used within the bucket formation and reorganization steps. The paper doesn't fully emphasize what’s novel *within* the SVD application; more discussion on *why* SVD is well-suited for this task would be beneficial.
2. **Reorganization Step Complexity:** While effective, the reorganization step (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) introduces a degree of complexity with its dynamic threshold calculation. The explanation, while present, could be streamlined for better readability. The reliance on AWPS and AWES as parameters, while designed to be dynamic, might be perceived as introducing a form of parameterization.
3. **Dataset Limitations:** The evaluation is based on three specific datasets.  The generalizability of the approach to other domains and types of text data remains unclear. Testing on a more diverse set of datasets would strengthen the conclusions.
4. **Missing Ablation Study:**  The paper would be strengthened by an ablation study examining the individual contributions of the various components of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (e.g., the reorganization step itself).  This would help to understand which aspects are most crucial for performance.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on why SVD is particularly well-suited for identifying the "sub-themes" within a topic, as opposed to other dimensionality reduction techniques?
2.  The dynamic threshold for reorganization seems complex. Could you provide a more intuitive explanation of how it works and why it's effective?  Perhaps a simplified example?
3.  Have you considered evaluating TBuckets on datasets beyond those used in Roder et al. (2015)?  If so, what were the results? If not, what are the challenges of applying TBuckets to different types of corpora?
4.  Could you perform an ablation study to quantify the individual contribution of the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and promising paper that introduces a conceptually appealing and empirically effective method for topic coherence evaluation.  While the SVD component isn't entirely novel, the TBuckets framework and the SVD-Reorg method offer a valuable contribution to the field.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly the ablation study and discussion of the SVD component's novelty, would further strengthen the paper. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.
