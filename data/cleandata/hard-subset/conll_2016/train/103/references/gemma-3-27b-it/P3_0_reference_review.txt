## Review of "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, for evaluating the coherence of topics generated by Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). The core idea is to group topic words into thematic “buckets” based on semantic similarity, with the assumption that coherent topics will result in a distribution with a single large bucket and few small ones. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with reorganization. They demonstrate that their SVD-based approach, particularly with reorganization, outperforms state-of-the-art topic coherence measures on standard datasets.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A novel and intuitive coherence metric:** The TBuckets approach offers a new perspective on topic coherence, moving beyond simple word co-occurrence statistics to a more holistic assessment based on thematic grouping. This aligns well with how humans intuitively judge topic quality.
2. **Effective SVD-based implementation:** The SVD-based techniques (particularly with reorganization) provide a parameter-free method for estimating coherence, a significant advantage over many existing approaches that require careful parameter tuning.
3. **Empirical validation and competitive performance:** The authors demonstrate through rigorous experimentation that TBuckets, especially the SVD-Reorg variant, achieves competitive or superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art topic coherence measures.

**Strengths:**

1. **Intuitive and Human-Aligned Approach:** The core idea of grouping words into thematic buckets directly reflects how humans assess topic coherence. This makes the metric interpretable and potentially useful for guiding topic model development. The examples provided in Section 3 effectively illustrate this intuition.
2. **Parameter-Free SVD Methods:** The SVD-based techniques represent a significant advantage. Eliminating the need for parameter tuning simplifies the process of evaluating topic models and reduces the risk of sub-optimal results due to poor parameter selection.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation using standard datasets and evaluation protocols (following Röder et al., 2015). Achieving state-of-the-art or comparable results demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  The ablation study with different scoring methods (size, WL, EVS) within TBuckets-SVD-Reorg provides helpful insight.
4. **Practical Application Demonstrated:** The application to text classification, showing improved performance by filtering topics based on TBuckets coherence scores, strengthens the practical relevance of the work. This highlights a tangible benefit beyond simply measuring coherence.
5. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it easy to follow the proposed methodology and experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Reliance on Pre-trained Word Embeddings:** The performance of TBuckets is heavily reliant on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings (GloVe). While GloVe is a strong baseline, the metric's effectiveness could be limited when applied to specialized domains where pre-trained embeddings are less effective.  The authors should address this limitation and potentially explore the impact of different embedding models.
2. **Complexity of Reorganization Step:** While the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg improves performance, the methodology for dynamic threshold calculation (AWES - AWPS / AWES) appears somewhat *ad hoc*.  A more rigorous justification for this formula, or exploration of alternative thresholding methods, would strengthen this aspect of the work.  The authors mention it removes the need for parameter tuning, but the formula itself feels like a tunable element.
3. **Limited Analysis of Bucket Characteristics:**  Beyond simply measuring the size of the largest bucket, the paper doesn't delve deeply into the *characteristics* of the buckets themselves.  Analyzing the semantic similarity within buckets, or the relationships between different buckets, could provide further insights into topic coherence and potentially lead to improved coherence measures. For instance, investigating the semantic overlap between the largest bucket and other smaller buckets could reveal more nuanced information about topic quality.
4. **Computational Cost:** While parameter-free, SVD can be computationally expensive, especially for large topic word sets. A discussion of the computational complexity of the TBuckets approach, and potential optimizations, would be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the performance of TBuckets to the choice of word embeddings? Have you experimented with different embedding models (e.g., Word2Vec, FastText) and what impact did they have on the results?
2.  Can you provide a more detailed justification for the formula used to calculate the dynamic threshold in the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg step?  Have you explored alternative thresholding methods or performed a sensitivity analysis to assess the impact of this formula on performance?
3.  Have you considered analyzing the semantic relationships *between* buckets, in addition to just the size of the largest bucket? Could this provide additional insights into topic coherence?
4.  What is the computational complexity of the TBuckets approach, particularly for the SVD-based methods? Are there any potential optimizations to improve scalability?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The discussion of related work is thorough.
*   The presentation of experimental results is clear and well-organized.
*   Consider adding a visual representation of the bucket formation process for a more intuitive understanding of the TBuckets approach (e.g., a diagram illustrating the grouping of words in a topic).



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and promising approach to topic coherence evaluation. The TBuckets metric is intuitively appealing, the SVD-based implementation is practical, and the experimental results are compelling. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall contribution of the work.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the questions and concerns raised above.
