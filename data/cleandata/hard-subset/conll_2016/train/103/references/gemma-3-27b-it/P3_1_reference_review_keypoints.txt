- Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into semantic "buckets" to measure topic coherence, with larger single buckets indicating higher coherence.
- Explores three bucket-creation methods: clustering, SVD, and reorganized SVD, showing improved correlation with human judgments on benchmarks.
- Highlights parameter-free SVD-based approaches (particularly TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) as practically advantageous.
- Demonstrates TBuckets' utility by improving weakly supervised text classification performance.
- Motivates the method with a clear, intuitive "bucket" analogy and illustrative examples.
- Empirical results show TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms state-of-the-art on two of three datasets and is competitive on the third.
- SVD-based methods require no hyperparameter tuning, easing practical deployment.
- Thoroughly evaluates multiple bucket creation techniques, supporting the choice of SVD variants.
- Applying the metric to a downstream task strengthens the paper’s practical relevance.
- Novelty is limited to framing and combination; underlying techniques (SVD, embeddings, cosine similarity) are established.
- The reorganization step uses heuristic thresholds (average similarity) that lack strong theoretical justification and need sensitivity analysis.
- Datasets are standard benchmarks (20NG, NYT, Genomics) but may limit generalizability to broader real-world domains.
- Paper lacks discussion of computational cost and scalability of per-topic SVD on large topics.
- Limited error analysis; more examples where TBuckets succeeds and others fail would clarify strengths.
- Requests clarification on theoretical justification for thresholding and exploration of alternative strategies.
- Asks for computational complexity analysis and testing on very large topics to assess scalability.
- Seeks evidence on TBuckets' generalizability across domains, topic lengths, and noise levels.
- Questions sensitivity to choice of embeddings and whether different embedding models were tested.
- Paper is well-written, has comprehensive related work, and helpful example buckets.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with strong empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses.
