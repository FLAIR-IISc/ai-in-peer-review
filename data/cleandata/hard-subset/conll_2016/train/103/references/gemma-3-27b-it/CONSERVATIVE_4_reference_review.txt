This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach to measure topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic buckets. The core idea is that a coherent topic will result in a single large bucket, while less coherent topics will yield multiple smaller buckets. Three techniques – clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization – are presented for creating these buckets, and the SVD-based approach is highlighted for being parameter-free. The authors evaluate TBuckets on three datasets, demonstrating performance competitive with, and in some cases superior to, the state-of-the-art. A secondary application to weakly supervised text classification is also presented.

The primary contribution, as I see it, is the exploration of a conceptually intuitive approach to topic coherence evaluation. The idea of grouping words into thematic buckets aligns with how humans assess topic quality. However, this intuition is not particularly novel – the underlying principle of semantic grouping is inherent in many existing coherence measures. 

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the TBuckets approach, along with the three implementation techniques. The examples used to illustrate the bucketing process are helpful in understanding the core concept.
2. **Competitive Performance:** The experimental results demonstrate that TBuckets, particularly the SVD-Reorg variant, achieves competitive results against established coherence measures. While the improvement isn't overwhelming, it's a positive sign.
3. **Parameter-Free Variant:** The SVD-based approach’s claim of being parameter-free is noteworthy, as many coherence metrics require careful parameter tuning. This could make TBuckets more accessible to researchers.
4. **Application to Text Classification:** Demonstrating the usefulness of the coherence measure in a downstream task like text classification provides a practical validation of its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty:** While the concept of grouping words is intuitive, the core innovation feels incremental. Existing coherence measures (PMI-based, distributional semantics) already capture aspects of semantic relatedness and topic interpretability. The bucketing approach doesn’t fundamentally alter how these concepts are assessed.
2. **Insufficient Justification for SVD:** The rationale for applying SVD to this problem isn’t deeply explored. While SVD can identify underlying dimensions in a word embedding space, the connection to *thematic* grouping isn't fully explained, and feels somewhat arbitrary. Why is the eigenvector representation inherently suited to forming coherent buckets?
3. **Overstated Claims:** The claim of "outperforming the state-of-the-art" needs to be qualified. The performance gains are relatively small and not consistently observed across all datasets.  Furthermore, the state-of-the-art is a moving target, and the comparison is based on a specific, potentially dated, baseline (Roder et al., 2015).
4. **Limited Ablation Study:**  The paper lacks a detailed ablation study to understand the contribution of the “reorganization” step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg. How much of the performance gain is attributable to the reorganization process, and what are the specific scenarios where it is most beneficial? The dynamic threshold calculation also feels ad-hoc and requires more justification.
5. **Reliance on Pre-trained Embeddings:** The performance is heavily dependent on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings. The paper doesn’t explore the sensitivity of the approach to different embedding models or discuss potential issues with out-of-vocabulary words.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed explanation of why SVD is a suitable technique for identifying thematic groupings of words in the context of topic coherence?
2.  What is the computational complexity of the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg algorithm, and how does it scale with the number of words in a topic and the dimensionality of the word embeddings?
3.  Please provide a more rigorous comparison against a broader range of state-of-the-art topic coherence measures, including more recent approaches.
4.  Can you present a more thorough ablation study to isolate the impact of the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg? What is the sensitivity of the dynamic threshold to variations in AWPS and AWES?
5.  How does the performance of TBuckets change when using different pre-trained word embedding models or when dealing with datasets containing a significant number of out-of-vocabulary words?



**Additional Comments:**
The citation style is inconsistent. Some references include the year while others do not. Also, the description of the evaluation datasets in section 4.1 is sparse; providing more context on the characteristics of each dataset would be helpful.
