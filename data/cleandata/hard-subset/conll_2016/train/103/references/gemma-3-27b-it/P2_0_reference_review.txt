## Review for "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, for evaluating the coherence of topics generated by Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). The core idea is to group topic words into thematic "buckets" – a process mirroring human judgment of topic quality. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), and SVD with reorganization.  They demonstrate that their SVD-based approach, particularly with reorganization (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg), outperforms state-of-the-art methods on several datasets when correlated with human judgments of topic coherence. A key advantage highlighted is the parameter-free nature of the SVD techniques. The paper also demonstrates the utility of TBuckets in improving text classification performance with weak supervision.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A novel and intuitive approach to topic coherence evaluation:** The "bucket" metaphor effectively captures the notion of thematic unity within a topic, providing a potentially more interpretable and human-aligned metric than existing statistical approaches.
2. **Effective SVD-based implementation:** The SVD-based techniques, especially with the reorganization step, achieve strong performance compared to existing methods while avoiding the need for parameter tuning—a significant practical advantage.
3. **Demonstration of practical utility:**  Showing that TBuckets can improve text classification performance by filtering low-quality topics highlights the real-world value of improved coherence assessment.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides convincing experimental evidence demonstrating that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms the state-of-the-art on two of the three datasets tested. This is a substantial result, showing the method is not merely theoretical.
2. **Parameter-Free Advantage:** The fact that the SVD-based techniques require no parameter tuning is a significant strength. Many existing coherence measures require careful parameter selection, making them less practical. This ease of use is a major selling point.
3. **Intuitive and Well-Motivated Approach:** The "bucket" concept is readily understandable and closely aligns with how humans intuitively assess topic coherence. This clarity makes the approach more appealing and easier to adopt. The included example runs showing bucket creation for coherent and non-coherent topics are excellent illustrations.
4. **Practical Application:** Demonstrating the value of TBuckets in a downstream task (weakly supervised text classification) strengthens the paper’s impact and shows that improved coherence measurement translates to tangible benefits.
5. **Thorough Related Work:** The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing topic coherence measures, placing TBuckets within the broader research landscape.



**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Diversity:** While the paper uses three datasets, they all stem from the same general domain (textual data with associated human judgements). Exploring the performance of TBuckets on more diverse data types (e.g., topics extracted from images, code, etc.) would strengthen the generalizability claim.
2. **Reorganization Step Complexity:** While effective, the reorganization step (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) introduces additional complexity with its dynamic threshold calculation.  The explanation is somewhat dense and could benefit from further clarification, potentially including pseudocode. The reliance on AWPS and AWES might also be sensitive to specific topic representations.
3. **Reliance on Pre-trained Word Embeddings:** The performance is dependent on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings (GloVe).  It’s not discussed how the choice of different embedding models might affect the results. A brief exploration of the impact of different embeddings would be beneficial.
4. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a detailed analysis of *why* TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms other methods. Identifying specific types of topics where TBuckets excels, and cases where it struggles, would provide valuable insights.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide pseudocode illustrating the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg algorithm, particularly the dynamic threshold calculation? This would significantly improve the clarity of the method.
2.  Have you experimented with different pre-trained word embeddings, and if so, how did their performance compare?
3.  Could you elaborate on the computational cost of TBuckets, especially TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, compared to existing coherence measures?
4.  Can you provide examples of topics where TBuckets performs poorly or gives counterintuitive results, and discuss potential reasons for these outcomes?
5.  How sensitive is the performance of the reorganization step to the choice of semantic similarity metric used for comparing word embeddings?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is generally well-written and clear, though some sections could benefit from further elaboration (e.g., the explanation of SVD).
*   The formatting of Table 1 is a bit cramped, and could be improved for readability.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a promising new approach to topic coherence evaluation. The TBuckets method, particularly the SVD-based variant, demonstrates strong performance and offers practical advantages over existing methods. While some aspects of the method could benefit from further clarification and exploration, the work represents a valuable contribution to the field of topic modeling.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
