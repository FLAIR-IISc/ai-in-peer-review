1. Proposes TBuckets: group topic words into thematic "buckets"; coherent topics have one large bucket and few small ones.  
2. Implements three bucket-creation methods: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization (SVD-Reorg).  
3. Claims SVD-based methods are parameter-free, simplifying application.  
4. Reports TBuckets correlates better with human annotations and outperforms prior methods on NYT and 20NG.  
5. Shows TBuckets can improve weakly supervised text classification.  
6. Provides an intuitive, human-aligned "bucket" metaphor for topic coherence.  
7. Conducts comprehensive evaluation on three datasets using Pearson correlation against relevant baselines.  
8. Presents especially strong empirical results for TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
9. Gives a detailed explanation of the SVD-Reorg reorganization step and dynamic threshold for reproducibility.  
10. Demonstrates practical applicability through a text-classification use case.  
11. Novelty is limited: uses existing components (embeddings, SVD, clustering); main novelty is their combination/application.  
12. Method appears sensitive to choice/quality of pre-trained word embeddings; no ablation across embeddings was provided.  
13. The dynamic threshold formula (AWES - AWPS / AWES) lacks clear theoretical or empirical justification.  
14. Paper lacks qualitative error analysis showing examples where TBuckets succeeds or fails.  
15. Comparisons are mainly against Röder et al. (2015); missing broader comparisons to recent/neural topic-coherence approaches.  
16. Questions raised: embedding sensitivity, justification/alternatives for threshold formula, examples of failure cases, comparison to neural methods, and clarity on "LDA Order Weight."  
17. Additional notes: paper is well-written with consistent notation; releasing code would aid reproducibility.  
18. Overall assessment: solid, promising work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing embeddings and threshold justification.
