- Paper introduces TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic "buckets" to measure topic coherence.  
- Explores three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with reorganization.  
- SVD-based TBuckets (especially SVD-Reorg) outperforms existing coherence measures on 3 datasets.  
- SVD-based methods are parameter-free, a practical advantage.  
- Demonstrates TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification, showing practical utility.  
- Strong empirical results with good correlation to human judgments and comparison to Roder et al. (2015).  
- Methodology is clearly motivated and well-explained, with useful example walk-throughs.  
- Paper includes a thorough review of related coherence measures and positions TBuckets well.  
- Performance depends heavily on pre-trained word embeddings; generalization to other embeddings/domains is unclear.  
- The reorganization threshold (AWES - AWPS / AWES) lacks strong theoretical justification and seems arbitrary.  
- Paper lacks analysis of failure cases or topics where TBuckets poorly aligns with human judgments.  
- Evaluation uses standard but limited datasets; broader and larger corpora are needed for generalizability.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for correlation improvements or classification gains.  
- Questions raised: sensitivity to different embeddings, justification/alternatives for reorg threshold, examples of poor-performing topics, computational complexity of methods, and significance testing for classification results.  
- Additional notes: paper is well-written, examples are effective, suggest adding bucket visualizations; references are comprehensive.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing justification, embedding sensitivity, failure analysis, dataset breadth, and significance testing.
