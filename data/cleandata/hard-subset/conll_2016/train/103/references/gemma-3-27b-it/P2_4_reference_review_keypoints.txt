- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into semantic “buckets” (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) to measure coherence; evaluated by correlating with human judgments and shown competitive, with SVD methods claimed parameter-free and a brief text-classification application.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel, intuitive bucket-based topic coherence metric beyond purely statistical measures.

- Contribution 2: Presents SVD-based TBuckets implementations that are effectively parameter-free, simplifying use and reducing tuning.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates empirical performance comparable to or exceeding state-of-the-art on three datasets.

- Strength 1: Intuitive, well-motivated idea with clear methodology and helpful illustrative examples.

- Strength 2: Parameter-free SVD methods reduce need for hyperparameter tuning and potential overfitting.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across diverse datasets with standard metrics and strong baselines.

- Strength 4: Shows practical utility by applying TBuckets to improve text classification.

- Strength 5: Clear, well-organized presentation.

- Weakness 1: Core idea has limited novelty since grouping semantically similar words overlaps with existing distributional approaches; positioning versus prior work is weak.

- Weakness 2: SVD-Reorg relies on ad-hoc heuristics (AWPS/AWES threshold) lacking strong theoretical justification.

- Weakness 3: Analysis focuses on largest-bucket size; lacks deeper investigation of bucket-size distributions (e.g., entropy, Gini).

- Weakness 4: No ablation study isolating the contribution of the reorganization step.

- Question 1: What is the computational complexity and scalability of Clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg with respect to topic word count and embedding dimensionality?

- Question 2: Can you provide a sensitivity analysis showing how varying the AWPS–AWES ratio affects SVD-Reorg performance?

- Question 3: Have you tested other bucket-distribution metrics (entropy, Gini) beyond largest-bucket size, and what were the results?

- Question 4: For clustering, how should the maximum distance threshold be selected — is there a principled strategy or is it empirical?

- Additional Comment 1: References are extensive and well-organized.

- Additional Comment 2: Recommend adding visualizations of bucket structures for coherent vs. incoherent topics.

- Overall Assessment: Solid, well-executed, recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing novelty framing and justification/sensitivity of reorganization heuristics.
