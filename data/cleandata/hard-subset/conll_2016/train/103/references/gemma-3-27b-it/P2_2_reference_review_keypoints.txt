- Proposes TBuckets: group topic words into thematic buckets; coherent topics form one large bucket and few small ones.  
- Implements three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization; SVD variants show improved correlation with human judgments.  
- Key claim: SVD-based approaches are parameter-free, offering practical advantages over existing metrics.  
- Contribution: TBuckets provides an intuitive, conceptually sound metric by assessing semantic grouping of topic words.  
- Contribution: SVD-based (especially SVD-Reorg) achieves state-of-the-art results on some datasets without parameter tuning.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates a practical application—using TBuckets for quality control to improve weakly-supervised text classification.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results — TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior methods on two of three datasets.  
- Strength: Parameter-free SVD methods reduce need for dataset-specific tuning and simplify use.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and explanations with illustrative bucket-formation examples.  
- Strength: Thorough experimentation across 20NG, NYT, and Genomics, compared to Roder et al. (2015).  
- Strength: Practical utility shown via topic-quality filtering that modestly improves classification.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty — uses well-established components (SVD, embeddings, clustering) in a new application.  
- Weakness: Reorganization step adds complexity; dynamic threshold calculation (AWPS/AWES) needs clearer justification.  
- Weakness: Application section is brief and improvements in classification are not dramatic; needs broader evaluation.  
- Weakness: Results rely on GloVe embeddings; sensitivity to other embeddings or domain-specific vectors is unclear.  
- Question: Why is the dynamic threshold defined as AWES − AWPS / AWES, and what are its potential drawbacks?  
- Question: Were other embeddings (Word2Vec, fastText) tested, and how do they affect TBuckets’ performance?  
- Question: What is the computational complexity and scalability of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg for large topics/datasets?  
- Question: Can authors provide error analysis—examples where TBuckets disagrees with human judgments and why?  
- Additional: Paper is well-written with comprehensive references.  
- Additional: Recommend adding discussion of limitations, e.g., sensitivity to embedding quality.  
- Overall: Recommend acceptance — TBuckets is a compelling, effective framework for topic coherence despite limited low-level novelty.
