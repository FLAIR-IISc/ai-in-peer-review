- Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into semantic "buckets" to measure coherence, arguing coherent topics show one large bucket with smaller supporting ones.  
- Explores three bucket-creation techniques: clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization, claiming improved correlation with human judgments.  
- Claims SVD-based methods (especially SVD-Reorg) perform well and are effectively parameter-free.  
- Demonstrates practical benefit by using TBuckets to pre-select high-quality topics for weakly supervised text classification.  
- Intuitive idea: coherence judged by thematic grouping of words aligns with human evaluation.  
- Strong empirical results: SVD-based TBuckets often outperform or match state-of-the-art on tested datasets (notably NYT and 20NG).  
- Parameter-free advantage: TBuckets-SVD and SVD-Reorg require no tuning, easing practical use.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with illustrative examples and straightforward experiments.  
- Relevant application showcased in weakly supervised classification, indicating downstream utility.  
- Individual components (embeddings, SVD, clustering) are standard; novelty is mainly in their combination.  
- Method performance likely sensitive to choice of pre-trained embeddings; only GloVe was tested.  
- Reorganization step uses ad-hoc dynamic thresholds based on averages, needing stronger justification and analysis.  
- Limited error analysis: paper reports correlations but lacks examples of major divergences from human judgments.  
- Evaluation limited to three datasets, reducing evidence of generalizability across domains and topic sizes.  
- Asks for embedding sensitivity experiments comparing different embeddings and corpora.  
- Requests analysis of the impact and alternatives for the reorganization dynamic threshold to rule out overfitting.  
- Requests examples and analysis of topics where TBuckets diverges from human scores to understand failure modes.  
- Asks for computational complexity and runtime scaling details for each TBuckets technique on large vocabularies/topics.  
- Suggests adding brief discussion of neural topic models in related work and exploring other downstream tasks beyond classification.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focusing on embedding sensitivity and reorganization heuristic.
