The paper proposes TBuckets, a new approach to evaluating topic coherence by grouping topic words into “buckets” representing thematic groupings. Three techniques are presented for bucket creation: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization. The authors claim TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art methods and, crucially, the SVD-based approaches are parameter-free. The primary contribution is a novel coherence measure and its implementation. A secondary contribution is the demonstration of parameter-free topic coherence evaluation using SVD. Finally, applying this coherence measure to improve weakly supervised text classification is presented.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of the TBuckets Concept:** The idea of explicitly grouping topic words to assess coherence is reasonably novel. Existing methods often rely on statistical relationships or distributional semantics without directly mirroring how humans assess topic coherence—by identifying overarching themes.
2. **Parameter-Free SVD Approach:** The absence of parameter tuning in the SVD-based methods is a practical advantage. Many existing coherence measures require careful parameter selection, making them less accessible and potentially prone to overfitting.
3. **Empirical Results:** The experimental evaluation demonstrates competitive performance on several datasets, with the SVD-Reorg method exceeding state-of-the-art results on two out of three datasets. This provides initial evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
4. **Application to Text Classification:** The demonstration of TBuckets' usefulness in improving weakly supervised text classification is a valuable addition, illustrating a potential real-world application of the method.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Rigorous Justification for Bucket Creation:** While the paper *motivates* bucket creation based on human intuition, the specific methodology for determining bucket boundaries and assigning words to buckets feels somewhat arbitrary. The semantic similarity threshold used isn’t deeply justified. A more formal and theoretically grounded approach to bucket formation would strengthen the paper.
2. **Overly Complex Reorganization Step:** The TBuckets-SVD-Reorg method introduces a complex reorganization procedure with several parameters (NBPW, NBPE, LBPW, OBPW) and a dynamically calculated threshold. Despite the authors’ claim of no parameter *tuning*, the dynamic threshold calculation *is* a parameterization, albeit a data-driven one. It's unclear how sensitive the performance is to the specific threshold calculation. The explanation of this process is convoluted and difficult to follow.
3. **Limited Analysis of Bucket Characteristics:** The paper presents limited analysis of the formed buckets themselves. Beyond stating that a single large bucket indicates coherence, there's a lack of exploration of bucket size distributions, semantic relationships within buckets, or how bucket characteristics correlate with human judgements of coherence. Providing such an analysis would offer deeper insights into the method’s effectiveness.
4. **Formatting and Writing Issues:** The paper suffers from several minor formatting inconsistencies (e.g., inconsistent use of boldface) and stylistic issues (e.g., repetitive phrasing, overly verbose sentences). The writing is not always clear and concise, making it difficult to understand some of the more complex concepts. Specifically, the explanation of the SVD process is overly detailed and assumes a level of mathematical expertise not necessarily possessed by all readers.
5. **Dependence on Pre-trained Word Embeddings:** The method relies heavily on the quality of pre-trained word embeddings. The authors mention using GloVe embeddings, but do not discuss the potential impact of different embedding models or the limitations of relying on embeddings trained on a general corpus.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed justification for the semantic similarity threshold used in the clustering-based approach and the reorganization step? How sensitive are your results to different threshold values?
2. Can you provide a more intuitive explanation of the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg method, simplifying the description of the reorganization parameters and the dynamic threshold calculation?
3. Have you conducted any analysis of the formed buckets themselves (e.g., bucket size distributions, semantic relationships within buckets)? If so, could you present those findings?
4. How robust are your results to different pre-trained word embedding models? Have you considered using domain-specific word embeddings?

**Additional Comments:**

*   Please carefully review the paper for formatting inconsistencies and stylistic issues.
*   Consider providing a visual illustration of the bucket creation process with a concrete example.
*   The reference list is extensive, but could benefit from more consistent formatting.



Overall, while the paper presents a novel idea and demonstrates promising results, the lack of rigorous justification for certain methodological choices, the complexity of the reorganization step, and the writing quality raise concerns. I recommend **rejection** at this time, pending major revisions addressing these weaknesses. The potential of the concept is evident, but significant improvements are needed to elevate the paper to an acceptable standard.
