This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach to measuring topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets. The core idea is that coherent topics will result in a single large bucket, while less coherent topics will yield multiple smaller buckets. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation – clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization – and demonstrate improved performance compared to state-of-the-art methods on several datasets.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **The TBuckets Framework:** The central contribution is the TBuckets framework itself – a conceptually simple yet potentially powerful way to assess topic coherence by analyzing the structure of word groupings within a topic.  This provides a novel lens for evaluating topic quality beyond existing methods based on PMI or distributional semantics.
2. **SVD-based Bucket Creation:**  The authors’ implementation of bucket creation using SVD is a strong point. It’s parameter-free, and their reorganization step (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) demonstrably improves performance.  The method elegantly leverages the dimensionality reduction properties of SVD to identify underlying themes.
3. **Empirical Validation & Application to Text Classification:** The comprehensive empirical evaluation on multiple datasets, coupled with the demonstration of TBuckets’ utility in improving text classification accuracy, strengthens the paper's claims.  Showing practical benefits beyond simply a coherence score is valuable.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Approach:** The TBuckets approach offers a distinct perspective on topic coherence. While existing methods often focus on pairwise word relationships (PMI) or distributional similarity, TBuckets analyzes the *structure* of word groupings, which aligns well with human intuition about topic coherence.
2. **Parameter-Free SVD Method:** The TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg techniques are a significant advantage. The lack of parameter tuning simplifies the application of the method and reduces the risk of overfitting. This is highlighted in the results and is a strong selling point.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results consistently show that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms state-of-the-art methods on two out of three datasets. This provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
4. **Clear Explanation and Dry Runs:** The explanation of the TBuckets idea is very clear, and the inclusion of dry runs with example topics helps the reader understand the core concept.  The detailed explanation of the reorganization step is also appreciated.
5. **Application to Downstream Task:** Demonstrating the benefit of improved coherence scores by using them to improve text classification is a pragmatic addition to the evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Theoretical Justification:** While the intuition behind TBuckets is sound, the paper lacks a deeper theoretical grounding. It would be beneficial to connect the TBuckets framework to existing theoretical frameworks of topic coherence or information theory. Why does a single large bucket *necessarily* indicate a coherent topic? A more formal analysis would strengthen the paper.
2. **Sensitivity to Word Embeddings:** The performance of TBuckets relies heavily on the quality of the word embeddings used. The authors use pre-trained GloVe embeddings, which is reasonable, but the paper doesn’t discuss the potential impact of different embeddings or the sensitivity of the results to the embedding choice.  A brief ablation study varying the embedding dimensionality or training corpus could be valuable.
3. **Reorganization Threshold Heuristic:** While the dynamic threshold for reorganization in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is clever, it relies on several intermediate calculations (AWPS and AWES). The rationale for *this* specific formula isn't fully explained. It’s presented as an attempt to avoid arbitrary parameter tuning, but more justification for the components of the formula would be helpful.
4. **Limited Dataset Diversity:** The datasets used (20NG, NYT, Genomics) are relatively standard in topic modeling research. Evaluating TBuckets on more diverse datasets – e.g., datasets with different document lengths, writing styles, or domain-specific vocabulary – could further validate its generalizability.
5. **Comparison Depth:** While the authors compare against Roder et al. (2015), a more comprehensive comparison with recent coherence measures (e.g., those based on BERT embeddings) would be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more theoretical justification for why a single large bucket indicates a coherent topic? Are there any information-theoretic principles that support this idea?
2. Did you experiment with different pre-trained word embeddings (e.g., Word2Vec, FastText) or embedding dimensionality? How sensitive are your results to the choice of embeddings?
3. Can you elaborate on the rationale behind the specific formula used to calculate the dynamic threshold for reorganization in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg? Why those specific components?
4. Do you anticipate any challenges when applying TBuckets to datasets with very short documents or highly specialized terminology?
5. Could you provide an example of a topic where TBuckets fails to accurately assess coherence, and discuss the potential reasons for this failure?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*   The related work section is thorough and provides a good overview of the existing literature on topic coherence.
*   Consider adding a visualization of the buckets formed for a few example topics to further illustrate the TBuckets approach.
*   The reference list is extensive and properly formatted.



**Overall:**

This is a solid paper that presents a novel and promising approach to measuring topic quality. The TBuckets framework, particularly the SVD-based implementation, offers a compelling alternative to existing methods. While the paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical foundation and a broader empirical evaluation, the strong experimental results and clear presentation make it a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
