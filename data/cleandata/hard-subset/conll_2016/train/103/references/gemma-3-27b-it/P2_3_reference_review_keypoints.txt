- Overall assessment: Interesting, valuable TBuckets approach; clear writing and sound experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary: TBuckets groups topic words into thematic "buckets" (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) and shows SVD-Reorg correlates well with human judgments.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces the "bucket" metaphor as a new, intuitive framework for measuring topic coherence.  
- Contribution 2: Proposes a parameter-free SVD-based TBuckets-SVD-Reorg method that performs competitively.  
- Contribution 3: Provides extensive empirical validation across multiple datasets and comparisons to established methods.  
- Strength 1: Intuitive and well-motivated approach that aligns with human topic assessment.  
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg matches or exceeds state-of-the-art correlations without tuning.  
- Strength 3: Thorough experimental design using established datasets, Pearson’s r, and ablation studies.  
- Strength 4: Clear, well-organized presentation that is easy to follow.  
- Strength 5: Demonstrated downstream benefit—improves text classification via better topic selection.  
- Weakness 1: Novelty is incremental; relies on existing techniques combined rather than a fundamental algorithmic advance.  
- Weakness 2: Heavy reliance on pre-trained GloVe embeddings; sensitivity to other embeddings or domains not explored.  
- Weakness 3: Limited evaluation scope—only three datasets; broader corpora would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness 4: Reorganization step uses an ad-hoc threshold (AWES − AWPS/AWES) lacking theoretical justification.  
- Question 1: How sensitive are results to different word embeddings (Word2Vec, FastText, or corpus-trained embeddings)?  
- Question 2: What is the rationale and theoretical basis for the dynamic threshold in the reorganization step, and were alternatives tried?  
- Question 3: What is the computational complexity and scalability of TBuckets, especially the SVD step for larger vocabularies or corpora?  
- Additional comment 1: Tables of results are well formatted and easy to understand.  
- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive; consider adding a limitations discussion in the conclusion.
