## Review of "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces TBuckets, a novel approach for measuring topic coherence in Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) models. The core idea is to group topic words into thematic "buckets" based on semantic similarity, with the intuition that coherent topics will result in a single large bucket and few smaller ones. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation – clustering, SVD, and a reorganized SVD – and demonstrate improved correlation with human judgements of topic coherence compared to existing state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets. A key selling point is the parameter-free nature of the SVD-based approaches. Finally, they show application of their coherence metric for improved text classification in a weakly supervised setting.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A Novel Coherence Metric:** The TBuckets approach offers a conceptually intuitive and promising alternative to existing topic coherence measures, framing coherence in terms of thematic grouping. This is a valuable addition to the toolkit for evaluating topic models.
2. **Effective SVD-based Implementation:** The SVD-based methods (particularly TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) show strong performance without requiring parameter tuning, a significant advantage in practical applications where hyperparameter optimization can be expensive.
3. **Demonstrated Utility in Weakly Supervised Learning:**  The application of the metric to improve text classification highlights the potential practical benefit of more accurate topic coherence assessment.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear and Intuitive Motivation:** The paper begins with a strong and relatable example illustrating the difference between coherent and incoherent topics, effectively motivating the need for better coherence metrics. The “bucket” analogy is easy to grasp and provides a clear conceptual framework.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms state-of-the-art methods on two out of three datasets, with competitive performance on the third. This provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
3. **Parameter-Free Advantage:** The complete absence of parameters for the SVD-based methods is a notable strength, making the approach readily applicable in various scenarios without the need for time-consuming optimization.
4. **Thorough Exploration of Techniques:** The paper explores three different bucket creation techniques, providing a comprehensive analysis of the approach and highlighting the benefits of the SVD-based methods.
5. **Application to Downstream Task:** Demonstrating the utility of TBuckets in improving a downstream task (weakly supervised text classification) further strengthens the value of the contribution.



**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Underlying Techniques:** While the *combination* of SVD and word embeddings to form buckets is a novel application, the individual components (SVD, word embeddings, cosine similarity) are well-established techniques. The core innovation lies in the framing of coherence measurement rather than groundbreaking algorithmic advances.
2. **Reorganization Step is Ad-hoc:** The reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, while improving performance, relies on dynamically calculated thresholds based on average similarity scores. The justification for this specific threshold calculation feels somewhat ad-hoc and lacks a strong theoretical grounding. More rigorous justification or sensitivity analysis would be beneficial.
3. **Dataset Limitations:** The datasets used (20NG, NYT, Genomics) are standard benchmarks, but their relevance to real-world applications beyond these domains may be limited. Exploring performance on more diverse datasets would strengthen the generalizability of the findings.
4. **Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the parameter-free nature is a plus, the computational cost of performing SVD on the word embedding matrix for each topic is not discussed.  For very large topics, this could become a bottleneck, and a discussion of scalability would be valuable.
5. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper would benefit from a deeper analysis of *why* TBuckets performs well, and specifically where existing methods struggle. Providing illustrative examples of topics where TBuckets accurately identifies coherence issues, but other methods fail, would enhance understanding.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Threshold Justification:** Can you provide a more theoretically grounded justification for the dynamic threshold used in the reorganization step (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg)?  Have you explored other thresholding strategies, and if so, what were the results?
2. **Scalability:** What is the computational complexity of TBuckets-SVD (and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) as a function of the number of words in a topic and the dimensionality of the word embeddings?  Have you tested the performance on topics with a very large number of words?
3. **Generalizability:** How do you anticipate TBuckets performing on datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different domain, different topic lengths, different levels of noise)? Have you considered experimenting on more diverse datasets?
4. **Impact of Embedding Choice:** How sensitive are the results to the choice of word embeddings? Did you experiment with different embedding models (e.g., Word2Vec, fastText)?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*   The related work section is comprehensive.
*   The inclusion of example topic buckets in Table 2 is helpful for understanding the TBuckets approach.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the TBuckets approach.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid contribution to the field of topic modeling evaluation. While the individual techniques are not entirely novel, the framing of coherence measurement in terms of thematic buckets, combined with the strong empirical results and parameter-free advantage of the SVD-based methods, makes this a valuable and promising approach. The weaknesses are relatively minor and addressable through further experimentation and analysis. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses and questions raised above.
