- Introduces TBuckets: group topic words into “buckets” representing sub-themes; coherent topics show one dominant bucket.  
- Proposes three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD with reorganization).  
- Evaluated on three datasets and compared to human judgments; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is competitive or superior.  
- Contribution: intuitive “bucket” metaphor aligning with human topic interpretation.  
- Contribution: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves strong empirical performance and is effectively parameter-free.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification via topic pre-selection.  
- Strength: Novel, human-aligned perspective moving beyond purely statistical coherence measures like PMI.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results, outperforming state-of-the-art on two of three datasets.  
- Strength: Practical application shown, supporting real-world usefulness.  
- Strength: Clear writing and illustrative examples that explain the approach well.  
- Weakness: SVD component itself is not novel; paper lacks deep discussion of what is novel about its SVD usage.  
- Weakness: Reorganization step adds complexity; dynamic thresholding and AWPS/AWES may be perceived as parameterization.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to three datasets; generalizability to other domains is unclear.  
- Weakness: No ablation study to isolate contributions of reorganization and other components.  
- Question: Why is SVD particularly well-suited for identifying sub-themes versus other dimensionality-reduction methods?  
- Question: Can the authors provide a clearer, more intuitive explanation and a simple example of the dynamic reorganization threshold?  
- Question: Have TBuckets been tested beyond the datasets from Röder et al. (2015); if not, what are the challenges?  
- Question: Can the authors perform an ablation study to quantify the reorganization step’s contribution?  
- Overall: Solid, promising paper recommending acceptance with minor revisions (add ablation and better SVD discussion).
