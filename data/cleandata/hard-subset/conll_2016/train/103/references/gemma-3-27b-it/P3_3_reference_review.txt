## Review of "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces TBuckets, a novel approach to measuring topic coherence in Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) models. The core idea revolves around grouping topic words into thematic "buckets" – a process mirroring human intuition for assessing topic quality. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), and SVD with reorganization. They demonstrate, through correlation with human annotations on three datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics), that TBuckets, particularly the SVD-based methods, outperforms existing state-of-the-art coherence measures. A key highlight is the parameter-free nature of the SVD-based techniques. The paper further demonstrates the utility of TBuckets in improving weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Intuitive Framework for Topic Coherence:**  The concept of "word buckets" provides a conceptually simple and human-interpretable way to quantify topic coherence.  This is a valuable contribution as many existing measures are less intuitive.
2. **Effective SVD-based Implementation:** The SVD-based TBuckets method, and especially its reorganized variant, demonstrate strong empirical performance, surpassing existing state-of-the-art measures on several datasets.  The parameter-free nature of this approach is a significant practical advantage.
3. **Demonstrated Application to Weakly Supervised Learning:**  Showing that the TBuckets coherence score can improve performance in a downstream task (weakly supervised text classification) adds practical relevance and strengthens the motivation for the work.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The consistent outperformance of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg on two out of three datasets is compelling. The correlation with human judgments is a strong indicator of its effectiveness.  The comparison to Roder et al. (2015), a recent and comprehensive study, provides a valuable benchmark.
2. **Parameter-Free Approach:** The lack of parameter tuning required for TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is a significant advantage, simplifying application and reducing the risk of overfitting or sub-optimal performance due to parameter selection.  This is explicitly highlighted and well-justified.
3. **Clear and Well-Motivated Methodology:** The paper clearly explains the motivation behind the TBuckets approach – mimicking human topic evaluation – and the details of each technique are presented in a logical and understandable manner. The example walk-throughs are particularly helpful.
4. **Practical Application Demonstrated:** The inclusion of the text classification experiment grounds the work in a practical application and demonstrates the utility of the coherence measure beyond just a diagnostic tool.
5. **Thorough Related Work Review:** The paper provides a comprehensive overview of existing topic coherence measures, effectively positioning TBuckets within the landscape of current research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Sensitivity to Word Embeddings:** The performance heavily relies on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings. While GloVe is a good choice, the results may not generalize to other embedding spaces or domains with vastly different vocabulary. This dependency isn’t sufficiently discussed; the impact of different embeddings should be explored.
2. **Reorganization Step Justification:** While the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg demonstrably improves performance, the rationale behind the specific threshold calculation (AWES - AWPS / AWES) feels somewhat arbitrary and lacks a deeper theoretical justification. The authors mention avoiding empirical tuning, but a more principled approach to defining this threshold would be valuable.
3. **Limited Analysis of Failure Cases:** The paper focuses primarily on showcasing successes.  A discussion of cases where TBuckets *fails* to correlate well with human judgments (or produces counter-intuitive results) would provide a more balanced and insightful evaluation.  What types of topics does it struggle with?
4. **Evaluation Dataset Limitations:** While the datasets are standard, their size and representativeness are limited.  Evaluating on larger and more diverse corpora would further strengthen the generalizability of the findings.
5. **Lack of Statistical Significance Testing:** The paper reports correlation coefficients but doesn't provide statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values) to assess whether the improvements over the state-of-the-art are statistically significant.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the performance of TBuckets, especially TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, to the choice of word embeddings? Have you experimented with different embedding spaces (e.g., Word2Vec, fastText) or embeddings trained on different corpora?
2.  Could you provide a more detailed justification for the threshold calculation used in the reorganization step of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg? What are the theoretical underpinnings of this formula, and are there alternative approaches you considered?
3.  Can you provide examples of topics where TBuckets performs poorly according to human judgment, and analyze the reasons for this discrepancy?
4.  What is the computational complexity of each of the TBuckets techniques (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg)?  This would be helpful for understanding the scalability of the approach.
5.  Regarding the text classification experiments, did you perform any statistical significance testing to determine whether the improvements achieved using TBuckets are statistically significant?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
*   The use of examples to illustrate the TBuckets process is particularly effective.
*   Consider adding a visualization of the "buckets" formed for a representative topic to further enhance understanding.
*   The reference list is comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper presenting a novel and promising approach to topic coherence evaluation. The empirical results are compelling, and the parameter-free nature of the SVD-based techniques is a significant advantage. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly providing a more robust justification for the reorganization step and analyzing failure cases, would further strengthen the contribution of this work.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.
