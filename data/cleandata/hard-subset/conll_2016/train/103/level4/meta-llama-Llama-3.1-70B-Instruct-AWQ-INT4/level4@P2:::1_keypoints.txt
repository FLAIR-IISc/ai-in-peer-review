- Proposes TBuckets, a novel topic-quality measure that groups topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings.
- Implements three bucketing techniques: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.
- Evaluates TBuckets on three public datasets and compares to state-of-the-art methods.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on two of the three datasets.
- Method is parameter-free, simplifying use and interpretation.
- Provides a thorough evaluation and result analysis, including application to weakly supervised text classification.
- Performance is inconsistent across datasets; SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Does not explicitly handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, which may hurt performance on some datasets.
- Fails to discuss the potential impact of word-embedding method parameters on results.
- Asks authors how they will address inconsistent cross-dataset performance.
- Requests details on OOV handling and its performance impact.
- Suggests trying other word-embedding methods or techniques to improve performance.
- Recommends a more detailed related-work discussion (e.g., lexical chaining literature).
- Advises acknowledging implicit embedding parameters and discussing their potential effects.
- Notes Figure 1 graphs are too small and should be enlarged or reformatted for readability.
