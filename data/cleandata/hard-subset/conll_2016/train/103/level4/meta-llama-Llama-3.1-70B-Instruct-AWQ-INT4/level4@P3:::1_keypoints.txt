- Proposes TBuckets: evaluates topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings.  
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.  
- Experimental evaluation on three public datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art on two out of three.  
- Leverages word embeddings to capture semantic relationships, a natural fit for topic evaluation.  
- Provides a thorough comparison with prior techniques and analysis of results.  
- Inconsistent performance across datasets: SVD-based methods perform poorly on the Genomics dataset, raising robustness concerns.  
- Paper does not discuss handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, which may affect results.  
- Method depends on pre-trained embeddings, introducing many implicit hyperparameters/assumptions.  
- Reviewer asks how authors will address cross-dataset inconsistencies and improve robustness.  
- Reviewer requests details on OOV handling and its impact on the method.  
- Reviewer asks how the method could be extended to domains with limited vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.  
- Notes similarity to lexical chaining literature and suggests exploring those connections.  
- Figures (Figure 1) are too small/readability issues and should be enlarged or reformatted.  
- Challenges the paper’s assumption that embedding vector length is unimportant in practice.
