- Presents a novel, logical method for topic-quality evaluation using word-embedding similarities and matrix factorization with strong results on standard datasets.  
- Results are inconsistent across datasets; methods do not reliably outperform state of the art in all cases.  
- SVD-based methods perform particularly poorly on the genomics dataset, raising concerns about robustness.  
- Likely cause of SVD failure is high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term proportion; this hypothesis is not analyzed in the paper.  
- It may be possible to predict best-performing method from vocabulary match with pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe), but this is not explored.  
- The approach is similar to lexical chaining literature, which the authors should acknowledge and integrate.  
- Claim of being "parameter-free" is misleading because it depends on word-embedding models that have many implicit parameters.  
- The paper fails to explain how OOV terms are handled and the impact of OOVs on performance.  
- Incorrect/oversimplified claim about vector length irrelevance for cosine similarity given non-unit-length embeddings and post hoc normalization.  
- Figures (Figure 1) are too small to read, impeding result interpretation.
