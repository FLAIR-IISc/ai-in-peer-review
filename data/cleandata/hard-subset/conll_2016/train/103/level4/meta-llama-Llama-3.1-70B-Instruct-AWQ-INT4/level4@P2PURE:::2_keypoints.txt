- Introduces a novel evaluation method that clusters the top-n words of each topic into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Basic method assigns words to existing buckets or creates new ones based on a cosine similarity threshold.  
- Two additional, more complex variants use eigenvectors and reorganization strategies.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task, matching or surpassing Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method appears effective despite its straightforward, somewhat ad-hoc design.  
- No new theoretical justification is provided for measuring topic quality via word-word embedding similarity, given differing contextual definitions of topics and embeddings.  
- Reviewer requests significance tests for the results reported in Table 1.  
- Writing is generally good but Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear presentation of design choices.  
- Suggests explaining each method in separate subsections and condensing less informative material to improve clarity.  
- Asks about sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec) and how results would change with different embeddings.
