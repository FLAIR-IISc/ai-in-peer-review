- Proposes a novel topic-model evaluation method that clusters top-n topic words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with simple, eigenvector-based, and reorganization variants; evaluated on three datasets and weakly supervised classification, matching or exceeding Röder et al. (2015).  
- Core idea is straightforward but feels ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification linking word-word embedding similarity to topic quality given differing context definitions (document vs. sequential).  
- Empirical results look promising, but significance tests (e.g., for Table 1) are needed to strengthen claims.  
- Paper is generally well-written but has language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design choices—recommend clearer phrasing, dedicated subsections, and condensing the uninformative trace.  
- Need analysis of sensitivity to different word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe) to assess robustness and generalizability.  
- Overall assessment: interesting approach that could make a valuable contribution after addressing the noted concerns.
