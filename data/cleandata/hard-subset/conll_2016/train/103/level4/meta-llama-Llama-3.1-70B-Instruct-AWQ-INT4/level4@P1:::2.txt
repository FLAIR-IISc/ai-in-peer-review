This paper proposes a novel method for evaluating topic models by partitioning the top n words of each topic into clusters or "buckets" based on the cosine similarity of their associated word embeddings. In the simplest setup, the words are considered one by one, and each is either put into an existing "bucket" – if its cosine similarity to the other words in the bucket is below a certain threshold – or a new bucket is created for the word. Two more complex methods based on eigenvectors and reorganization are also suggested. The method is evaluated on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text classification setting, outperforming or being on par with the state of the art (Röder et al., 2015).

The basic idea behind the paper is straightforward and has a certain ad hoc flavor. The authors do not offer new explanations for why topic quality should be measurable in terms of word-word similarity. It is not obvious why this should be the case, given that topics and word embeddings are defined with respect to two rather different notions of context (document vs. sequential context). Nevertheless, the proposed method seems to work quite well. Some significance tests for Table 1 would be a welcome addition.

The paper is clearly written, although there are some language issues. The description of the techniques in Section 3 is somewhat hard to follow, mainly due to the authors' use of passive voice ("the threshold is computed as") in places where they were actually making a design choice. The authors should try to explain the different methods more clearly, with one subsection per method. There is room for this, as the authors did not completely fill the 8 pages of content, and they could easily downsize the rather uninformative "trace" of the method on page 3.

One question that arises is how sensitive the proposed technique is to different word embeddings. For example, how would the scores be if the authors had used word2vec instead of GloVe?

**Summary of the Paper:**
The paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, for measuring topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic groups (buckets). The method uses three techniques: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD)-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. The approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets and compared to state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions:**

1. A novel approach for measuring topic quality using word embeddings and bucketing.
2. Three techniques for creating buckets of words: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
3. Evaluation on three standard datasets and comparison to state-of-the-art results.

**Strengths:**

1. The proposed method outperforms or is on par with the state of the art in topic quality evaluation.
2. The method is relatively simple and easy to implement.
3. The authors provide a clear evaluation of their approach on three standard datasets.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The basic idea behind the paper has an ad hoc flavor and lacks a clear explanation for why topic quality should be measurable in terms of word-word similarity.
2. The description of the techniques in Section 3 is somewhat hard to follow.
3. The authors do not provide significance tests for Table 1.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. How sensitive is the proposed technique to different word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe)?
2. Can the authors provide more insight into why topic quality should be measurable in terms of word-word similarity?
3. How do the authors plan to address the potential issues with the ad hoc nature of their approach?
