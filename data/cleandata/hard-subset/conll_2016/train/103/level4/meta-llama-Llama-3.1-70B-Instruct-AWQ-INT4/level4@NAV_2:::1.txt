This paper proposes a method for evaluating topic quality based on using word embeddings to calculate similarity, achieving impressive results over standard datasets. The proposed method represents a natural but important next step in the evolutionary path of research on topic evaluation.

**Summary of the paper**: The authors propose a novel approach, TBuckets, to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. The method groups topic words into thematic groups (buckets) using word embeddings and employs three techniques: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD), and SVD with reorganization. The approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets and outperforms state-of-the-art techniques.

**Main contributions**:

1. A novel approach, TBuckets, for measuring topic quality using word embeddings and bucketing techniques.
2. The proposal of three techniques for creating buckets of words: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
3. The evaluation of the proposed methods on three publicly available datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results.

**Strengths**:

1. The proposed method achieves impressive results on standard datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art techniques.
2. The approach is based on a natural and intuitive idea of grouping topic words into thematic groups.
3. The method has the potential to be useful for various applications, including text classification.

**Weaknesses**:

1. The results are inconsistent across datasets, with no single method consistently beating the state of the art.
2. The SVD-based methods perform notably badly over the genomics dataset, which may be related to the proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms.
3. The method does not explicitly address how to deal with OOV terms, which may impact its performance.

**Questions to authors**:

1. How do you plan to address the inconsistency in results across datasets, and what factors contribute to the poor performance of SVD-based methods on the genomics dataset?
2. Can you discuss the impact of OOV terms on the method and propose a solution to handle them?
3. How do you plan to extend the method to handle domain-specific vocabularies and applications?
