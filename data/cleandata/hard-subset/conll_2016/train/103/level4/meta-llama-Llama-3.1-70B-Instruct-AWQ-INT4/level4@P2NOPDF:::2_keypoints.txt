- Proposes a novel topic-model evaluation method that clusters the top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Introduces three variants: a simple incremental bucketing method, an eigenvector-based method, and a reorganization-based method.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task, achieving performance on par with or better than Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method works empirically but is somewhat ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification tying word-word embedding similarity to topic quality.  
- Raises concern that topic co-occurrence (document context) and word embeddings (sequential context) are different, so effectiveness is not obvious.  
- Requests significance tests for Table 1 to confirm claimed performance gains.  
- Writing is generally good with minor language issues, but Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear presentation of design choices.  
- Recommends clearer organization: explain each method in a separate subsection and shrink the uninformative "trace" example.  
- Asks for analysis of sensitivity to choice of embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs word2vec) to assess robustness and generalizability.
