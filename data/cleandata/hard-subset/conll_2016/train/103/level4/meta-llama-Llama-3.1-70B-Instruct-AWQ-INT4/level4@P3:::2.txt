This paper proposes a novel approach to evaluate topic models by partitioning the top n words of each topic into clusters or "buckets" based on cosine similarity of their associated word embeddings. The authors present three techniques for creating these buckets: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg. The method is evaluated on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text classification setting, outperforming or matching the state of the art (Röder et al., 2015).

**Summary of the paper and main contributions:**

The paper's primary contribution is the proposal of the TBuckets approach, which groups topic words into thematic groups (buckets) to measure topic coherence. The authors evaluate their techniques by correlating their estimated coherence scores with human-annotated scores and demonstrate better performance than the state-of-the-art results. Additionally, the techniques TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg require no parameter tuning, making them more efficient than existing methods.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel approach:** The TBuckets approach offers a fresh perspective on measuring topic coherence by leveraging word embeddings and clustering techniques.
2. **Improved performance:** The proposed techniques outperform or match the state of the art on three standard datasets, demonstrating their effectiveness.
3. **Efficiency:** TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg require no parameter tuning, making them more efficient than existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of theoretical justification:** The authors do not provide a clear explanation for why topic quality should be measurable in terms of word-word similarity, given the different notions of context used in topic models and word embeddings.
2. **Sensitivity to word embeddings:** The paper does not investigate how sensitive the proposed technique is to different word embeddings, such as word2vec instead of GloVe.
3. **Clarity of technique descriptions:** The description of the techniques in Section 3 could be improved for better understanding, with one subsection per method.

**Questions to authors:**

1. Can you provide a theoretical justification for why topic quality should be measurable in terms of word-word similarity?
2. How sensitive is the proposed technique to different word embeddings, such as word2vec instead of GloVe?
3. Can you elaborate on the design choices behind the threshold computation in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?
