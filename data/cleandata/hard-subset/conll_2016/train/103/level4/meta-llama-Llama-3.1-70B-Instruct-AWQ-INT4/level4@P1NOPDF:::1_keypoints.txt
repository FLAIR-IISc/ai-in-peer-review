- Proposes a topic-quality evaluation method using word embeddings (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) with strong results on standard datasets.  
- Results show large inconsistencies across datasets; no proposed method consistently outperforms prior work.  
- SVD-based variants perform poorly on the genomics dataset, raising reliability concerns for arbitrary datasets.  
- Likely cause for SVD failure is high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term proportion; paper omits analysis of this.  
- Missing discussion on predicting the best method automatically (e.g., based on vocabulary overlap with GloVe).  
- Method is similar to prior work in lexical chaining literature, which the authors should cite and integrate.  
- Claim of having “no parameters” is misleading because used word embeddings have many implicit hyperparameters.  
- Paper does not explain how OOV terms are handled (ignored or otherwise) and what impact that has.  
- Incorrect/unstated assumption that vector length is irrelevant for cosine similarity—pretrained word2vec vectors are not unit-length unless normalized.  
- Figures (Figure 1) are too small and unreadable; need resizing/reformatting for clarity.  
- Recommend adding discussions of limitations, failure modes, and potential application scope to strengthen the contribution.
