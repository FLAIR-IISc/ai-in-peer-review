- Proposes embedding-based topic quality evaluation (direct or via matrix factorization) with strong results; a natural next step for topic evaluation research.  
- Large inconsistencies in method performance across the three datasets; no proposed method consistently outperforms prior state of the art.  
- SVD-based approaches perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset, raising concerns about generalizability to arbitrary datasets.  
- Likely cause of poor SVD performance is high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term proportion; paper omits analysis of this.  
- Suggestion to predict the best method per dataset based on vocabulary match with GloVe is missing from the paper.  
- Method is similar to prior work in the lexical chaining literature, which the authors should explore and cite/incorporate.  
- Claim of “no parameters” is misleading because word-embedding methods have many implicit parameters; this should be acknowledged.  
- Paper does not specify how OOV terms are handled (e.g., ignored) and does not assess the impact on performance, especially for genomics.  
- Technical point: Section 2.1 wrongly implies vector length is unimportant—word2vec outputs non-unit-length vectors and normalization should be discussed.  
- Presentation issue: Figures (Figure 1) are too small to be readable, hindering result interpretation.  
- Overall: Promising approach but requires more analysis, discussion, and presentation fixes before publication.
