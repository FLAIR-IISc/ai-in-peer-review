- Proposal: cluster top-n topic words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings; present a simple greedy method plus eigenvector and reorganization variants.  
- Empirical results: evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, matching or outperforming Röder et al. (2015).  
- Methodic concern: approach is straightforward but ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification for using word-word similarity as a proxy for topic quality.  
- Context mismatch: unclear why word embeddings (sequential context) should reliably reflect topic coherence (document-level context).  
- Statistical support: request significance tests for the results in Table 1 to validate reported improvements.  
- Clarity: paper is mostly clear, but Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design choices—recommend one subsection per method and clearer explanations.  
- Page usage: authors underutilized the 8-page limit and can condense uninformative material to expand methodological description.  
- Robustness question: need analysis of sensitivity to embedding choice (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec).
