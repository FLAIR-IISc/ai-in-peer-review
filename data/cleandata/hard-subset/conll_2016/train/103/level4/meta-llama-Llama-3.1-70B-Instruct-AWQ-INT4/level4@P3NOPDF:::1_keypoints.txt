- Paper proposes a word-embedding-based topic quality evaluator (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) and reports strong results on standard datasets.
- Method is a natural and important incremental step in topic evaluation research.
- Results show large inconsistencies across methods and datasets; no proposed method reliably outperforms state-of-the-art in all cases.
- SVD-based methods perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset, raising concern about off-the-shelf applicability.
- Reviewer suspects SVD failure may be due to a high proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms for pre-trained GloVe vectors, but the paper does not analyze this.
- Suggestion to investigate whether vocabulary match with pre-trained embeddings can predict which method will work best.
- Paper should discuss connections to prior lexical chaining literature, as the proposed method strongly resembles that work.
- Authors claim "no parameters," but the word embeddings used have many implicit hyperparameters that should be acknowledged.
- Paper lacks a clear explanation of how OOV terms are handled and what their impact is on performance.
- Technical detail: assumption that vector length is unimportant for cosine similarity is problematic—pre-trained vectors were normalized post hoc and word2vec outputs non-uniform lengths.
- Presentation issue: figures (Figure 1) are too small and unreadable, hindering result interpretation.
- Overall: promising approach but needs these analyses, clarifications, and presentation fixes to improve reliability and applicability.
