This paper proposes a novel method for evaluating topic models by partitioning the top n words of each topic into clusters or "buckets" based on the cosine similarity of their associated word embeddings. In the simplest setup, the words are considered one by one, and each is either added to an existing "bucket" – if its cosine similarity to the other words in the bucket is below a certain threshold – or a new bucket is created for the word. Two more complex methods based on eigenvectors and reorganization are also suggested. The method is evaluated on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text classification setting, outperforming or matching the state of the art (Röder et al., 2015).

The basic idea behind the paper is straightforward and has a somewhat ad hoc flavor. The authors do not provide new explanations for why topic quality should be measurable in terms of word-word similarity. It is not immediately clear why this approach should be effective, given that topics and word embeddings are defined with respect to two distinct notions of context (document vs. sequential context). Nevertheless, the proposed method appears to work well. However, I would like to see some significance tests for Table 1 to further support the results.

Overall, the paper is clearly written, despite some minor language issues. I found the description of the techniques in Section 3 somewhat challenging to follow, which I attribute to the authors' use of passive voice ("the threshold is computed as") in places where they were actually making design choices. I suggest that the authors explain the different methods more clearly, dedicating one subsection to each method. There appears to be sufficient space for this, as the authors did not fully utilize the 8-page content limit, and they could easily condense the somewhat uninformative "trace" of the method on page 3.

One question I had was how sensitive the proposed technique is to different word embeddings. For example, how would the scores be affected if the authors had used word2vec instead of GloVe?
