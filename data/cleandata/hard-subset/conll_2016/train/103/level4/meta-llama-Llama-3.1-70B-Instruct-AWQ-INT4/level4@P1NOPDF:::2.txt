This paper proposes a new method for evaluating topic models by partitioning the top n words of each topic into clusters, or "buckets," based on the cosine similarity of their associated word embeddings. In its simplest setup, the method considers each word individually and either assigns it to an existing bucket – if its cosine similarity to the other words in the bucket is below a certain threshold – or creates a new bucket for the word. The authors also suggest two more complex methods based on eigenvectors and reorganization. The method is evaluated on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text classification setting, where it outperforms or is on par with the state of the art (Röder et al., 2015).

The basic idea behind the paper is straightforward and has a somewhat ad hoc flavor. The authors do not provide any new explanations for why topic quality should be measurable in terms of word-word similarity. Given that topics and word embeddings are defined with respect to two distinct notions of context (document vs. sequential context), it is not immediately clear why this approach should be effective. Nevertheless, the proposed method appears to work well in practice. However, I would like to see some significance tests for Table 1 to further support the results.

Overall, the paper is clearly written, despite some minor language issues. I found the description of the techniques in Section 3 somewhat challenging to follow, primarily due to the authors' use of passive voice ("the threshold is computed as") in places where they were actually making design choices. I believe the authors should strive to explain the different methods more clearly, dedicating one subsection to each method. There appears to be sufficient space for this, as the authors did not fully utilize the 8-page content limit and could easily condense the rather uninformative "trace" of the method on page 3.

One question I had was how sensitive the proposed technique is to different word embeddings. For instance, how would the scores change if the authors had used word2vec instead of GloVe?
