1. Proposes TBuckets: partition top-n topic words into thematic "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings to evaluate topic models.  
2. Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task, matching or outperforming prior methods.  
3. Core idea is simple and intuitive but lacks a clear justification for why word–word similarity should measure topic quality given differing context definitions (document vs. sequential embeddings).  
4. Empirical results are promising, suggesting practical utility despite the theoretical gap.  
5. Paper is generally well-written but contains some language issues.  
6. Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear presentation of design choices; needs reorganization into clearer subsections per method.  
7. Authors should add statistical significance tests (e.g., for Table 1) to strengthen claims.  
8. Recommend testing sensitivity to different embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe) to assess robustness.  
9. Main claimed contributions: the TBuckets method, strong performance in weakly supervised classification, and evaluation across multiple datasets.  
10. Strengths: novel, intuitive, easy-to-implement approach with thorough empirical evaluation.  
11. Weaknesses: conceptual justification gap between topic definitions and embeddings, clarity issues in method description, and missing significance testing.  
12. Questions for authors: clarify why word–word similarity reflects topic quality, report sensitivity to different embeddings, and provide significance tests for reported results.
