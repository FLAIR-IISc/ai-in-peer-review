- Proposes a word-embedding-based topic quality evaluation method (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) with impressive results; a natural next step in topic evaluation research.
- Achieves state-of-the-art on three datasets but shows notable inconsistencies across methods and datasets.
- None of the proposed methods consistently outperforms prior work in practice; SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Inconsistency raises concerns about robustness and applicability to arbitrary datasets.
- Poor SVD performance may be due to out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms; no analysis of vocabulary match or method selection based on GloVe coverage is provided.
- Strong similarity to lexical chaining literature is not discussed; authors should explore and incorporate that work.
- Claim of “no parameters” ignores the large number of implicit parameters in the underlying word-embedding methods.
- Paper fails to explain how OOV terms (e.g., genomics-specific words absent from pre-trained GloVe) are handled and what impact they have.
- Technical inaccuracy: Section 2.1 downplays vector length despite word2vec producing non-unit-length vectors unless normalized; this should be acknowledged.
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 graphs are too small/unreadable, reducing clarity of results.
- Overall: method is promising but needs to address these methodological, technical, and presentation concerns to strengthen practical utility.
