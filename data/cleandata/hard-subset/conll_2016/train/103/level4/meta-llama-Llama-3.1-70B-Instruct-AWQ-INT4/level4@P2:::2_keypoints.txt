- Proposes TBuckets: evaluate topic models by partitioning top-n topic words into buckets via cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-with-reorganization.  
- Evaluates on three public datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task, reporting performance on par with or better than Röder et al. (2015).  
- Strength: method is simple, intuitive, and easy to implement.  
- Strength: leveraging word embeddings and bucketing yields a more nuanced measure of topic quality.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation across datasets and a downstream task demonstrates effectiveness.  
- Weakness: idea is relatively simple and may not provide deep new insights into why topic quality aligns with word-word similarity.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear theoretical explanation for why topics and word embeddings—derived from different context notions—should be related.  
- Weakness: Section 3’s method descriptions are sometimes hard to follow and use passive voice that obscures design choices.  
- Question: How sensitive is TBuckets to different embedding types (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe)?  
- Question: Can authors clarify the theoretical or empirical reasons topics and embeddings relate despite differing context definitions?  
- Question: How will authors address potential limitations like the method’s simplicity and reliance on pre-trained embeddings?
