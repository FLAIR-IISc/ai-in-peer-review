- Proposes a new topic-model evaluation method that clusters top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings (simple greedy, plus eigenvector and reorganization variants), evaluated on three datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, matching or beating Röder et al. (2015).

- Method is straightforward but somewhat ad hoc, with no new theoretical justification for measuring topic quality via word–word embedding similarity.

- Raises conceptual concern that topic definitions (document context) differ from embedding contexts (sequential context), so it's unclear why embedding similarity should reflect topic quality.

- Empirically promising, but Table 1 needs statistical significance tests to strengthen the results.

- Paper is clearly written overall, though there are minor language issues.

- Section 3 is hard to follow because of passive voice and unclear presentation of design choices; recommend dedicating a subsection to each method and removing the uninformative "trace" to use available page space better.

- Asks about sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., how results change using word2vec instead of GloVe).
