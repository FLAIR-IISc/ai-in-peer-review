- Proposes TBuckets: partition top-n topic words into clusters using cosine similarity of word embeddings to measure topic coherence.  
- Introduces three bucket-creation methods: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, matching or outperforming Röder et al. (2015).  
- TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg require no parameter tuning, improving efficiency.  

Strengths:
- Novel use of embedding-based clustering to assess topic coherence.  
- Empirically outperforms or matches state-of-the-art coherence measures.  
- Two variants are parameter-free, increasing practicality and efficiency.  

Weaknesses:
- No theoretical justification for measuring topic quality via word–word similarity given differing context notions.  
- Missing analysis of sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec).  
- Technique descriptions (Section 3) lack clarity and could be better structured.  

Questions to authors:
- Request theoretical justification for why word–word similarity should reflect topic quality.  
- Ask for sensitivity analysis across different word embeddings (e.g., word2vec).  
- Ask for elaboration on design choices for threshold computation in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
