- Proposes evaluating topic quality using word embeddings (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) and reports impressive results on standard datasets.
- Represents a natural and important next step in topic evaluation research.
- Results show significant inconsistencies across methods and datasets; no method reliably outperforms the state of the art in all cases.
- SVD-based methods perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Likely cause of poor SVD performance is high proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms; paper doesn’t analyze OOV handling or its impact.
- Authors could potentially predict the best method based on vocabulary match with GloVe, but this analysis is absent.
- Method is similar to prior work in the lexical chaining literature; related work is not discussed.
- Claiming “no parameters” is misleading because pre-trained word embeddings involve many implicit hyperparameters.
- Technical oversight: authors assume vector length is unimportant, but this only holds for unit-length vectors; word2vec vectors are not unit-length unless normalized.
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 graphs are too small and unreadable, hindering result interpretation.
- Overall assessment: promising approach but requires further refinement, analysis of inconsistencies, and clearer discussion of technical assumptions and OOV handling.
