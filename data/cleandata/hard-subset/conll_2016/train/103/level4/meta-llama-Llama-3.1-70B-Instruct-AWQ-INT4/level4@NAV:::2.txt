This paper introduces a novel approach for evaluating topic models by grouping the top n words of each topic into clusters, or "buckets," based on the cosine similarity of their corresponding word embeddings. The simplest configuration involves processing words individually, either assigning them to an existing bucket if their cosine similarity to the other words in the bucket falls below a predetermined threshold, or creating a new bucket for the word. Two additional, more complex methods leveraging eigenvectors and reorganization are also proposed. The method's performance is assessed using three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification setting, yielding results that either surpass or match the state-of-the-art (Röder et al., 2015).

While the underlying concept of the paper is straightforward, it has an ad hoc quality and lacks a clear explanation for why topic quality should be quantifiable in terms of word-word similarity. Given the distinct contextual definitions of topics and word embeddings (document-based vs. sequential context), it is not immediately apparent why this approach should be effective. Nonetheless, the proposed method demonstrates promising results. (However, I would appreciate seeing significance tests for Table 1.)

The paper is generally well-written, despite some language issues. I found the description of the techniques in Section 3 somewhat challenging to follow, primarily due to the authors' use of passive voice in certain instances where design choices were being made. I recommend that the authors provide clearer explanations of the different methods, ideally dedicating one subsection to each method. There appears to be sufficient space for this, as the authors did not fully utilize the 8-page content limit, and the relatively uninformative "trace" of the method on page 3 could be condensed.

One question that arose during my review was the sensitivity of the proposed technique to different word embeddings. For instance, how would the scores be affected if the authors had used word2vec instead of GloVe?
