- Proposes a novel evaluation for topic models that clusters top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings (simple incremental method plus two more complex eigenvector/reorganization variants).  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task, reporting results that match or beat the state-of-the-art (Röder et al., 2015).  
- Method is conceptually simple but feels ad hoc and lacks a clear theoretical justification linking topic quality to word–word embedding similarity.  
- Raises concern about the potential mismatch between topic definitions (document-based) and word embeddings (sequential context), casting doubt on why similarity should measure topic quality.  
- Requests statistical significance testing for the results reported in Table 1.  
- Paper is generally well written but has some language issues.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear explanation of design choices; recommends clearer, dedicated subsections for each method.  
- Notes authors underused the 8-page limit and suggests condensing the uninformative "trace" on page 3 to make room for clearer method descriptions.  
- Asks about sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., how results would change using word2vec instead of GloVe).
