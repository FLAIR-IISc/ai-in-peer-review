- Proposes TBuckets: evaluate topic models by clustering the top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Introduces three bucketing techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task, matching or outperforming Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method is simple and easy to implement.  
- Empirical results are strong, but Table 1 lacks reported significance tests.  
- Core idea feels ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification connecting word-word similarity to topic quality.  
- Concern that embeddings and topics use different contexts (document vs. sequential), so similarity may not reflect topic coherence.  
- Sensitivity to choice of embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec) is not addressed.  
- Paper is clearly written overall but has some language issues.  
- Section 3’s method descriptions are hard to follow, partly due to passive voice and unclear design choices.  
- Recommend clearer exposition: one subsection per method and removal of uninformative trace content.  
- Asks authors to explain why topic quality should be measurable via word-word similarity and how they will address the approach’s ad hoc nature.
