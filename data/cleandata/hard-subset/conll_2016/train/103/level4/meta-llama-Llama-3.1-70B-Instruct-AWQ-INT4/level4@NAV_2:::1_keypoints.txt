- Proposes TBuckets, a word-embedding-based method to evaluate LDA topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets.  
- Introduces three bucketing techniques: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Evaluated on three public datasets and reports state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks.  
- Achieves impressive results on standard datasets, outperforming existing techniques.  
- Uses an intuitive idea of grouping topic words into thematic clusters via embeddings.  
- Potentially useful for downstream applications such as text classification.  
- Results are inconsistent across datasets; no single method dominates across all benchmarks.  
- SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset, possibly due to many out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms.  
- Method does not explicitly address handling of OOV terms, which may hurt performance.  
- Question: How will authors address result inconsistency across datasets and factors behind SVD failures on genomics?  
- Question: How do OOV terms affect the method and what solutions can be proposed to handle them?  
- Question: How can the method be extended to handle domain-specific vocabularies and applications?
