- Introduces a novel topic-quality assessment using word embeddings and matrix factorization with strong results on standard datasets.
- Methods show inconsistent effectiveness across datasets; not all methods beat the state of the art everywhere.
- No single proposed method consistently outperforms the state of the art across all datasets.
- SVD-based methods perform particularly poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Poor SVD performance may be linked to high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term rates, but this is not analyzed.
- The paper does not explore predicting the best method based on vocabulary overlap with pretrained embeddings.
- Method resembles lexical chaining techniques; prior literature should be reviewed and incorporated.
- Claim of being "parameter-free" is misleading because pretrained word-embedding models contain many parameters.
- Handling of OOV terms (e.g., in genomics) is not specified—unclear if they are excluded and how that affects results.
- Minor: discussion of cosine similarity omits that vector lengths matter unless vectors are normalized.
- Minor: Figure 1 graphs are too small and not legible.
