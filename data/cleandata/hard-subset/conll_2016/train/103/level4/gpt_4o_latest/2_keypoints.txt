- Introduces a novel topic-model evaluation: cluster top-n words into "buckets" by cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Presents a simple sequential bucket-assignment algorithm and two advanced variants using eigenvectors and reorganization.  
- Tested on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised classification task; matches or outperforms Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method is straightforward but feels ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification for assessing topic quality via word–word similarity.  
- Raises concern that topics (document-level context) and word embeddings (sequential context) come from different notions of context.  
- Recommends reporting statistical significance tests for the results in Table 1.  
- Paper is generally well-written but has minor language issues.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow (overuse of passive voice); suggest clearer, dedicated subsections for each method.  
- Authors appear to have unused space in the 8-page limit; recommend condensing or removing the uninformative "trace" on page 3.  
- Queries sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., how results change using word2vec vs GloVe).
