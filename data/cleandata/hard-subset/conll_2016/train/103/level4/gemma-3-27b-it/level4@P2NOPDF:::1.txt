This paper presents a novel approach to topic quality evaluation, leveraging word embeddings to quantify similarity – both directly and through matrix factorization. The results demonstrate strong performance across standard datasets, representing a logical and valuable progression in topic evaluation research.

However, a significant concern arises from the inconsistency in performance across different methods and datasets. While the proposed methods achieve state-of-the-art results on individual datasets, no single method consistently outperforms existing approaches. Notably, the SVD-based methods perform comparatively poorly on the genomics dataset. This variability poses a practical challenge for users seeking a readily applicable solution for arbitrary datasets. I hypothesize that the lower SVD performance on genomics may be linked to a higher proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms. Exploring the possibility of automatically predicting optimal method selection based on vocabulary overlap with the pre-trained embedding space (e.g., GloVe) could be beneficial, but this is not discussed in the paper.

Further points to consider:

*   **Related Work:** The proposed method bears strong resemblance to techniques found in the lexical chaining literature. The authors are encouraged to investigate this related work and incorporate a discussion into future revisions.
*   **Parameterization:** The paper highlights the lack of parameters within the proposed method itself. However, the underlying word embedding models contain a substantial number of parameters. While not a critical flaw, acknowledging this implicit parameterization would provide a more complete picture.
*   **Out-of-Vocabulary Terms:** The paper does not address the handling of OOV terms, specifically those encountered in datasets like genomics that may not be present in the pre-trained GloVe embeddings. Clarification on whether these terms are ignored, and an analysis of their potential impact on the method’s performance, is needed.

Minor issues:

*   **Word Embedding Normalization:** Section 2.1 describes word embeddings without explicitly stating the importance of vector length. While cosine similarity effectively measures similarity between unit-length vectors, standard word2vec implementations do not inherently produce normalized vectors. The pre-trained vectors are typically normalized *post hoc*, and running word2vec directly results in varying vector lengths. This detail, though minor, is relevant for complete accuracy.
*   **Figure Readability:** The graphs presented in Figure 1 are too small and lack sufficient detail for clear interpretation. Increasing their size would significantly improve their usability.
