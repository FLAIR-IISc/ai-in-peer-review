- Proposes evaluating topic models by clustering top-n topic words into buckets via cosine similarity of word embeddings, with simple, eigenvector, and reorganization variants; evaluated on three datasets and weakly supervised classification, matching/exceeding Röder et al. (2015).

- Core idea is straightforward but feels ad hoc; authors do not justify why word-to-word similarity should measure topic quality given differing contexts of topics (documents) vs. embeddings (sequential).

- Request to report significance tests for the results in Table 1.

- Paper is generally clear but has language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and needs clearer, possibly subsectioned, explanations and removal/condensing of an uninformative method trace.

- Raises key question about sensitivity to choice of embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec) and how scores would change with different embeddings.
