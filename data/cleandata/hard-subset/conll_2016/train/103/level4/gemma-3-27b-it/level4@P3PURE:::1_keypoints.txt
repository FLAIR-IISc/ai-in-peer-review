- Proposes evaluating topic quality using word embeddings (directly or via matrix factorisation) and reports strong results on standard datasets.  
- Major concern: inconsistent method performance across datasets; relative rankings vary and no single method consistently outperforms others.  
- SVD-based variants perform poorly on the genomics dataset.  
- This lack of robustness is a practical limitation for applying the method to new datasets.  
- Suspected cause: high proportion of OOV terms in genomics; authors should analyze vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe) to predict best method.  
- Paper fails to discuss how OOV terms are handled or the impact of ignoring them.  
- Method closely resembles techniques from the lexical chaining literature and should be acknowledged/compared.  
- Claim that the method has “no parameters” is misleading because underlying word-embedding models have many parameters; this should be noted.  
- Minor clarity issue: discussion of cosine similarity assumes unit-length vectors, which is not guaranteed for all embedding sources.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 graphs are too small to be easily readable.
