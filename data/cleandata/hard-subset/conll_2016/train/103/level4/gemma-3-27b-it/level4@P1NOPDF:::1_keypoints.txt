- Proposes an embedding-based topic-quality metric (direct similarity or via matrix factorisation/SVD) with strong results on standard datasets.
- Results are inconsistent across datasets: no method consistently beats the state of the art, and SVD methods perform particularly poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Likely cause for SVD's poor genomics performance is high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term rate; paper does not explore predicting the best method from vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.
- The method has strong parallels with lexical chaining approaches and should acknowledge/prior work in that literature.
- Claiming the method has "no parameters" is misleading because underlying pre-trained word embeddings embed many implicit parameters that affect performance.
- The paper fails to specify how OOV terms are handled (e.g., ignored) and what effect that handling has on results.
- Minor: Section 2.1 glosses over vector length assumptions—cosine similarity assumes unit-normalised vectors, but word2vec does not inherently produce those.
- Minor: Figure 1 graphs are too small and hard to read.
