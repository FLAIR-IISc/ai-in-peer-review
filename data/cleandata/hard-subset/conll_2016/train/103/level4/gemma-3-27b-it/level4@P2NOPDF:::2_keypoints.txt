- Introduces a novel topic-model evaluation method that clusters the top-n words per topic using cosine similarity of word embeddings, assigning words to "buckets" by a similarity threshold.
- Proposes two advanced variants using eigenvector analysis and bucket reorganization.
- Evaluated on three benchmark datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, achieving comparable or better performance than Röder et al. (2015).
- Method is simple but feels ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification linking word–word embedding similarity to topic quality (document-level vs sequential context mismatch).
- Empirical results are promising but need statistical significance tests (e.g., for Table 1).
- Writing is generally good with minor language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear organization.
- Recommend restructuring Section 3 into dedicated subsections for each method and condensing the low-value "trace" on page 3 to use the page budget better.
- Suggest future work examining sensitivity to different word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe) to test robustness and generalizability.
