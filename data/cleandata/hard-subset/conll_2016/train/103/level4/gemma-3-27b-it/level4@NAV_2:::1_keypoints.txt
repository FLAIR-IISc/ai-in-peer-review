- Proposes TBuckets: embedding-based topic quality evaluation using direct similarity or SVD-based matrix factorization, showing strong results on standard datasets.  
- Results are state-of-the-art overall but inconsistent across datasets; no single method consistently dominates.  
- SVD-based techniques perform notably worse on the genomics dataset, raising concerns about robustness.  
- Suspected cause for poor genomics performance: high proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms; paper does not analyze this.  
- Lack of guidance for selecting the optimal TBuckets variant for a given dataset limits practical applicability.  
- Recommend investigating connections to lexical chaining literature and incorporating related techniques.  
- Claim of being “parameter-free” is misleading because pre-trained word embeddings have many implicit parameters.  
- Paper fails to state how OOV terms are handled and how their treatment affects performance.  
- Section 2.1 overlooks that cosine similarity’s irrelevance to vector length assumes unit-normalized vectors, which Word2Vec does not guarantee.  
- Figure 1 is too small and hard to read; figures should be enlarged for clarity.  
- Main contributions: TBuckets framework, empirical demonstration (esp. SVD variants) outperforming baselines, and an application to weakly supervised text classification.  
- Key questions to authors: impact of OOV terms on SVD approaches, relation between vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings and TBuckets performance, and connection to lexical chaining.  
- Additional request: acknowledge embedding pre-training when claiming parameter-free method and increase figure sizes for readability.
