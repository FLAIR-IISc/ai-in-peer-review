- Proposes evaluating topic models by clustering each topic’s top-n words into embedding-based “buckets” (simple per-word assignment plus eigenvector and reorganization variants).  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised classification task, matching or exceeding Röder et al. (2015).  
- Core idea is straightforward but feels somewhat ad hoc and lacks novel justification for using word-to-word similarity as a measure of topic quality.  
- Raises concern that word embeddings (sequential/contextual) and topic models (document-level) reflect different contexts, so alignment is not obviously valid.  
- Empirical results are strong, but significance tests (e.g., for Table 1) should be reported.  
- Overall clear writing, with minor language issues.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and presentation of design choices; recommend clearer explanations and separate subsections for each method.  
- Authors have not fully used the 8-page limit; suggest condensing the low-value “trace” on page 3 and expanding method descriptions.  
- Request analysis of sensitivity to different embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe) and how scores would change.
