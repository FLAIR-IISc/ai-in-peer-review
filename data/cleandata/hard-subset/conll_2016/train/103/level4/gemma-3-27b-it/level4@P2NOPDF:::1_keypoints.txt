- Presents a novel topic-quality evaluation using word embeddings and matrix factorization with strong results on standard datasets.  
- Performance is inconsistent across methods and datasets; no single method consistently outperforms others.  
- SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset, posing a practical issue for general applicability.  
- Hypothesis: poor SVD performance may stem from high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) rates; propose exploring automatic method selection based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.  
- Related work: approach resembles lexical chaining techniques and should be discussed and cited.  
- Parameterization: though the method claims few parameters, underlying pre-trained embeddings introduce substantial implicit parameters that should be acknowledged.  
- OOV handling: the paper does not state how OOV terms are treated or analyze their impact, especially for domain-specific datasets like genomics.  
- Word embedding normalization: the manuscript should note vector-length importance and that common implementations (e.g., word2vec) do not output normalized vectors by default.  
- Figure readability: Figure 1 is too small and lacks detail; enlarge and clarify graphs.
