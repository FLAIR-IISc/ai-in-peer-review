- Proposes TBuckets: evaluate topic quality by grouping topic words via word-embedding similarity (direct or via matrix factorization); shows strong results but inconsistent across datasets.  
- Frames topic coherence as formation of thematic groups within topic word sets (novel conceptual perspective).  
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG without parameter tuning.  
- SVD-based methods are parameter-free, offering practical simplicity over tuned baselines.  
- Demonstrates downstream utility: TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification.  
- Inconsistent performance: no single method consistently outperforms baselines across all datasets.  
- SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset, raising robustness concerns.  
- Paper lacks analysis of causes for dataset-specific discrepancies (e.g., vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings).  
- No exploration of methods to predict which TBuckets variant is optimal for a given dataset.  
- Fails to compare or position TBuckets relative to prior lexical chaining work.  
- Overlooks implicit dependence on pre-trained embedding parameters (GloVe) despite “parameter-free” claim.  
- Does not describe handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, problematic for specialized domains like genomics.  
- Reviewer asks authors to investigate causes of inconsistent performance, including vocab overlap with embeddings.  
- Reviewer asks how OOV terms are handled and their expected impact in specialized domains.  
- Reviewer requests a detailed analysis of TBuckets vs. lexical chaining and key differentiators.  
- Reviewer asks whether dataset characteristics can predict the best TBuckets technique.  
- Misc: Figure 1 is too small; clarify statement about embedding vector length and customary normalization.
