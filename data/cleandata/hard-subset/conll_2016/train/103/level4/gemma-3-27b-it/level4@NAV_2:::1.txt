This paper proposes a method for evaluating topic quality based on leveraging word embeddings to calculate similarity – either directly or indirectly through matrix factorization – and achieves compelling results across standard datasets.

The proposed method represents a natural and important advancement in the ongoing research of topic evaluation. While the results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on all three datasets, inconsistencies exist in which methods excel and underperform. Specifically, no single proposed method consistently surpasses the state-of-the-art, and the SVD-based techniques demonstrate notably weaker performance on the genomics dataset. This variability raises concerns for practical application, as a user seeking an out-of-the-box solution may find performance inconsistent across diverse datasets. I suspect the lower SVD results on genomics may be linked to the proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, and it may be possible to predict optimal method selection based on vocabulary overlap with GloVe, but this is not discussed within the paper.

Further considerations:
- The proposed method shares similarities with techniques found in the lexical chaining literature, which I recommend the authors explore and incorporate into future revisions.
- While the method is presented as parameter-free, the underlying word embedding models inherently possess a large number of parameters. Acknowledging this implicit dependency would be beneficial.
- The paper does not address how the method handles OOV terms, such as those present in the genomics dataset. Clarification on whether these terms are ignored and the potential impact on performance is needed.

Minor points:
- Section 2.1 implicitly assumes the irrelevance of vector length when discussing cosine similarity. While valid for unit-length vectors, word2vec does not inherently produce them. This nuance should be acknowledged.
- The graphs in Figure 1 are too small for clear readability.

The main contributions of this work, as I see them, are: (1) a novel framework (TBuckets) for evaluating topic coherence that leverages word embeddings to group topic words into thematic buckets, and (2) a demonstration that this framework – particularly the SVD-based techniques – can outperform existing state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets, despite being parameter-free. The application to weakly supervised text classification, showing practical benefit, is a third, though less substantial, contribution.

Questions to Authors:
1. Could you discuss the potential impact of OOV terms on the performance of your method, particularly the SVD-based approaches?
2. Have you explored the relationship between vocabulary overlap with the pre-trained word embeddings (e.g., GloVe) and the performance of different TBuckets techniques?
3. Could you elaborate on how the proposed method relates to existing work in lexical chaining?

Additional Comments:
Please consider increasing the size of the figures for improved readability. Also, while emphasizing the lack of *method* parameters, acknowledge the pre-training involved in the word embeddings.
