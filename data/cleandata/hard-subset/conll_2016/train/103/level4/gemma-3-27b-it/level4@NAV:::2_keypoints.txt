- Introduces a novel topic-model evaluation that clusters top-n topic words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Core algorithm: assign each top word to an existing bucket if similarity to bucket words meets a threshold, otherwise create a new bucket.  
- Proposes two refinements using eigenvectors and topic reorganization.  
- Evaluated on three benchmark datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task, matching or surpassing Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method is simple and heuristic but demonstrates practical effectiveness.  
- Lacks theoretical justification linking topic quality to word-to-word embedding similarity; contextual mismatch between document-level topics and sequential-context embeddings is unclear.  
- Recommends reporting statistical significance for results in Table 1.  
- Writing is generally good but Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice; suggest reorganizing into dedicated subsections per method.  
- Suggest condensing trace information (page 3) and expanding methodological exposition, as the paper underuses the allotted space.  
- Recommends evaluating sensitivity to different embedding models (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe).
