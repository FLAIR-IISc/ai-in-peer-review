- Novel, promising method using word embeddings and matrix factorization for topic quality evaluation with impressive results on standard datasets.
- Achieves state-of-the-art on three datasets but shows inconsistent performance across datasets.
- No single proposed method consistently outperforms existing approaches; SVD-based methods perform notably worse on the genomics dataset.
- Hypothesis: SVD’s lower genomics performance may be due to higher out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term rates; paper lacks automatic method selection based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.
- Recommend exploring automatic prediction of the optimal method from vocabulary overlap with embeddings (e.g., GloVe).
- Method is similar to prior work in the lexical chaining literature; the paper should discuss these connections.
- Authors claim no explicit parameters, but the underlying word embedding models have many implicit parameters that should be acknowledged.
- OOV handling is not described (e.g., ignored or otherwise); paper should clarify impact and mitigation strategies.
- Technical note: cosine similarity assumes normalized vectors; pre-trained vectors are often normalized post hoc while self-trained vectors may not be unit length—this should be discussed.
- Presentation issue: graphs in Figure 1 are too small and should be enlarged for readability.
