- Proposes a novel bucket-based evaluation for topic models that clusters top-n topic words by cosine similarity of word embeddings.
- Presents a simple greedy bucket method plus two more sophisticated variants using SVD and reorganization.
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text-classification setting, matching or exceeding Röder et al. (2015).
- Core idea is straightforward but somewhat ad hoc; no novel theoretical justification for measuring topic quality via word-to-word embedding similarity.
- Raises concern that embeddings and topics capture different contexts (sequential vs. document), questioning the validity of the similarity-based measure.
- Requests reporting of statistical significance for the results (e.g., Table 1).
- Writing is generally clear but Section 3 descriptions are hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design choices.
- Suggests clarifying each method with dedicated subsections and reducing/condensing the lengthy walkthrough on page 3.
- Wants analysis of sensitivity to different word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe).
