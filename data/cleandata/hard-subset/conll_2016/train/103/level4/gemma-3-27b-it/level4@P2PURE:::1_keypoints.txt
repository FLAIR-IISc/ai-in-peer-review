- Presents a topic quality evaluation method using word embeddings (direct similarity or via matrix factorisation) and reports strong performance on standard datasets.  
- Results are inconsistent across the three datasets: method rankings vary and no single proposed method consistently outperforms state-of-the-art.  
- SVD-based methods notably underperform on the genomics dataset, indicating a lack of robustness for general use.  
- Hypothesis: poor SVD performance on genomics may stem from high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term rates; predicting optimal method via vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe) is suggested but not explored.  
- Methodology overlaps substantially with lexical chaining literature; relevant citations and discussion should be added.  
- Claim of being parameter-free is misleading because the approach depends on pre-trained word embeddings, which themselves have many parameters and should be acknowledged.  
- The paper does not describe how OOV terms are handled (e.g., ignored) or analyze their impact, especially important for domains like genomics.  
- Minor: Section 2.1 implicitly assumes vector length is irrelevant for cosine similarity, but pre-trained/self-trained embeddings may not be unit-length—this subtlety should be addressed.  
- Minor: Figures (Figure 1) are too small and not easily readable.
