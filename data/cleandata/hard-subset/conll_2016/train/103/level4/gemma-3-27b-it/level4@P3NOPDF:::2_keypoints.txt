- Proposes a novel bucket-clustering method that groups top-n topic words by cosine similarity of their word embeddings.  
- Assigns words to existing buckets if similarity exceeds a threshold, otherwise creates a new bucket; two variants use eigenvector analysis and bucket reorganization.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task, often matching or outperforming Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method is straightforward but somewhat ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification for using word-to-word embedding similarity to assess topic quality.  
- Conceptual gap: topic models define context at document level while embeddings capture local sequential context, so the connection is not fully motivated.  
- Results are promising but Table 1 should include statistical significance tests to strengthen claims.  
- Writing is generally clear but has minor language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and presentation.  
- Recommend restructuring Section 3 into distinct subsections per method and condensing the descriptive “trace” on page 3 to use space better.  
- Important open question: sensitivity of results to the choice of embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs word2vec) and overall robustness.
