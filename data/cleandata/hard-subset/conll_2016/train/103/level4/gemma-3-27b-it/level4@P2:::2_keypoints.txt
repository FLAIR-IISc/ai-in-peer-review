- Introduces TBuckets: groups top-n topic words into buckets via cosine similarity of word embeddings to evaluate topic models.
- Proposes three techniques: basic clustering, SVD-based method, and SVD with reorganization.
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and achieves competitive or superior results to Röder et al. (2015) in weakly supervised classification.
- Core idea (measuring topic quality via word-word similarity) is straightforward but somewhat ad hoc and lacks strong theoretical justification.
- Empirical results are promising, but Table 1 needs statistical significance testing to strengthen claims.
- Paper is generally well-written, though Section 3’s explanations are convoluted and would benefit from separate subsections per method.
- Authors overuse passive voice; clearer descriptions of design choices are needed.
- The illustrative example is overly detailed and should be streamlined to save space.
- Important limitation: sensitivity to choice of word embeddings not explored (only GloVe used); testing word2vec or others is recommended.
- Contribution: a novel embedding-based approach to topic coherence evaluation (TBuckets).
- Contribution: empirical validation showing competitive performance across datasets.
- Contribution: demonstration of practical usefulness in weakly supervised text classification.
- Strength: clear presentation overall and parameter-free SVD techniques.
- Strength: strong empirical performance with comprehensive comparison to prior work.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical underpinning linking embedding similarities to human topic coherence.
- Weakness: clarity issues in methodological description and use of passive constructions.
- Weakness: missing statistical significance analysis for reported results.
- Weakness: limited exploration of embedding model robustness.
- Questions for authors: provide statistical significance tests for Table 1, report sensitivity to different embeddings, and explain design choices for TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (dynamic threshold).
