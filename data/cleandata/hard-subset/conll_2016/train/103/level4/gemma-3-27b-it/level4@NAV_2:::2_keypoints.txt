- Proposes a novel topic-model evaluation method that clusters top-n topic words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with three variants (basic clustering, SVD, refined SVD) and evaluation on three datasets plus weakly supervised classification, achieving competitive or better results than Röder et al. (2015).

- Core idea is conceptually simple but feels somewhat ad hoc, lacking novel justification for using word–word similarity to measure topic quality.

- Raises validity concerns because topics are defined at the document level while embeddings capture sequential word context, creating a potential mismatch.

- Results appear effective, but the reviewer requests statistical significance tests for the results reported in Table 1.

- Paper is generally well written with minor language issues.

- Presentation in Section 3 is hard to follow (overuse of passive voice); recommend dedicated subsections per method and clearer explanation of design decisions.

- Suggest streamlining the illustrative “trace” on page 3 to make room for more detailed explanations.

- Questions sensitivity to choice of word embeddings—asks how results would change using word2vec instead of GloVe.
