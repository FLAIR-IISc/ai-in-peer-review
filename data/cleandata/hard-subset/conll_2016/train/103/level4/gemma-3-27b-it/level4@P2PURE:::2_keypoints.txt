- Proposes a novel topic-model evaluation method that clusters each topic’s top-n words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with two advanced variants (eigenvectors and topic reorganisation); evaluated on three benchmarks and a weakly supervised classification task, achieving comparable or better results than Röder et al. (2015).
- Core algorithm: assign each word to an existing bucket if its cosine similarity to the bucket’s words exceeds a threshold, otherwise create a new bucket.
- Approach is straightforward but feels ad hoc; the paper lacks justification for using word–word embedding similarity to assess topic quality given the document-based nature of topics versus sequential-context embeddings.
- Despite conceptual concerns, the method performs well; the reviewer requests significance testing for the results in Table 1.
- Paper is generally well written but needs linguistic polishing; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design decisions—recommend separate subsections clarifying each method.
- Suggests trimming the uninformative “trace” on page 3 and using the available page space to better explain methods.
- Recommends testing sensitivity to different word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe) to assess robustness of the scores.
