- Introduces a novel topic-quality evaluation method using word embeddings and similarity measures, achieving strong results on benchmark datasets.  
- Method is a logical and valuable advancement in topic evaluation research.  
- Performance varies across datasets; no single method consistently outperforms others.  
- SVD-based methods perform significantly worse on the genomics dataset, raising practical concerns for diverse/unseen data.  
- Discrepancy may relate to out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term proportion; authors do not explore predicting best method based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.  
- Methodology has notable similarities to lexical chaining literature and should engage that prior work.  
- Paper claims lack of explicit parameters but does not acknowledge inherent parameters in underlying word embedding models.  
- Treatment of OOV terms (e.g., ignoring them) is unclear and needs clarification, especially for domains like genomics.  
- Minor: Section 2.1 should note that cosine similarity assumes normalized vectors since standard word2vec outputs are not unit-length by default.  
- Minor: Figure 1 graphs are too small and hinder readability.
