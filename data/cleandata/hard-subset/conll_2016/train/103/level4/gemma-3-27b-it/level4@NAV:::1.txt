This paper introduces a novel approach to topic quality evaluation, leveraging word embeddings and similarity measures – both direct and via matrix factorization – to achieve strong performance on established datasets. The work represents a logical and valuable progression within the field of topic evaluation research. 

However, a key concern arises from the variability in performance across the datasets. While the proposed method attains state-of-the-art results, the relative ranking of methods is inconsistent; no single approach consistently outperforms existing techniques. Specifically, the SVD-based methods demonstrate significantly weaker performance on the genomics dataset, which raises practical concerns for users intending to apply this method to diverse, unseen datasets. It is plausible that this discrepancy relates to the proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, and exploring the potential for predicting optimal method selection based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe) could be beneficial, but this is not discussed.

Further considerations:

- The proposed methodology shares notable similarities with existing work in lexical chaining, and the authors are encouraged to engage with this literature.
- While the paper highlights the lack of explicit parameters, the inherent parameters within the underlying word embedding models are not acknowledged. This is a minor point, but merits recognition.
- The treatment of OOV terms, particularly in datasets like genomics where they may be prevalent, requires clarification. The impact of simply ignoring such terms on the method’s effectiveness should be addressed.

Minor points:

- Section 2.1’s discussion of word embeddings implies that vector length is irrelevant when using cosine similarity. Although valid for unit-length vectors, standard word2vec implementations do not inherently produce normalized vectors, and this nuance should be noted.
- The visual presentation of the graphs in Figure 1 hinders readability due to their small size.
