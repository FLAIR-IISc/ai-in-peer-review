- Proposes evaluating topic quality using word embeddings (direct similarity or via matrix factorization), showing strong results on standard datasets.  
- Method is a natural and important next step in topic evaluation research.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on three datasets but performance varies considerably across methods and datasets.  
- No single proposed method consistently outperforms prior work; SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset.  
- Inconsistency raises concerns about reliability for practitioners seeking an off-the-shelf solution.  
- Lower SVD results may stem from out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms; paper does not investigate predicting best method based on vocabulary overlap with resources like GloVe.  
- Should acknowledge similarities to lexical chaining literature and explore that connection.  
- Claim of being “parameter-free” is misleading given reliance on pre-trained embeddings that contain many parameters.  
- Paper omits details on handling OOV terms (e.g., ignored or otherwise) and the impact on performance.  
- Minor: cosine-similarity discussion ignores that word2vec vectors are not necessarily unit-length.  
- Minor: figures (Figure 1) are too small to read easily.
