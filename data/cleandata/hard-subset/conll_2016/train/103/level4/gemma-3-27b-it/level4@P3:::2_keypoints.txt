- Proposes TBuckets: cluster top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with three methods (basic clustering, SVD, SVD+reorganization).  
- Evaluated on three datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task, showing competitive or superior performance to Röder et al. (2015).  
- Core contribution: TBuckets as a new metric for topic coherence that correlates with human judgment and aids downstream tasks.  
- Secondary contribution: SVD-based variants are parameter-free, reducing need for tuning.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by improving topic selection in weakly supervised classification.  
- Empirical strength: Outperforms state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG, suggesting real improvement (Table 1).  
- Usability strength: Parameter-free SVD methods increase accessibility for practitioners.  
- Practical application strength: Using TBuckets for topic selection improves classification accuracy (Table 3).  
- Presentation strength: Core idea is clear and intuitively explained, with helpful examples.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification linking embedding-derived similarity to topic coherence.  
- Weakness: Method descriptions (Section 3) are convoluted and often passive, hurting clarity.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance tests reported for the performance differences in Table 1.  
- Weakness: Limited ablation study; unclear contributions of SVD-Reorg components (size, WL, EVS).  
- Question: Provide p-values or confidence intervals for Table 1 to show statistical significance.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to different word embeddings (e.g., word2vec)?  
- Question: Please supply an ablation study for TBuckets-SVD-Reorg showing each component’s contribution.  
- Question: Justify the reorganization step’s heuristics and specify its controlling parameters.  
- Additional comment: Discuss computational complexity of the TBuckets variants.  
- Additional comment: Use more active voice (especially Section 3) to improve readability.  
- Additional comment: Consider replacing the informal term “buckets” with a more technical name.
