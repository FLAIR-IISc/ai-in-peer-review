- Proposes a novel word-embedding-based method (direct comparison or matrix factorization) that achieves state-of-the-art results on standard topic evaluation datasets.  
- Major concern: inconsistent performance across datasets — the method does not reliably outperform existing approaches.  
- SVD-based variant performs notably poorly on the genomics dataset, raising robustness concerns.  
- Likely cause: high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term rates in genomics; handling of OOVs is unclear and unreported.  
- Paper lacks analysis on automating method selection (e.g., based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings like GloVe).  
- Substantial conceptual overlap with lexical chaining approaches; relevant literature engagement is missing.  
- Claiming “no parameters” is misleading because pre-trained embedding models (e.g., GloVe) contain implicit parameters that must be acknowledged.  
- Technical oversight: cosine similarity is claimed to apply generally but assumes unit-length vectors — pre-trained vectors may be normalized while self-trained vectors may not.  
- Minor issue: Figure 1’s graphs are too small for clear interpretation.
