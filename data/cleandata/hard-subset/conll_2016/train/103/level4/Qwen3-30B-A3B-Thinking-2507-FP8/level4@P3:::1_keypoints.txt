- Introduces TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets to measure topic coherence, where a single large bucket indicates high coherence.  
- Proposes three bucket-formation techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD (SVD for orthogonal sub-themes), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganization to improve assignments).  
- Claims TBuckets correlates well with human judgments and can be used to select high-quality topics for downstream tasks.  
- TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are presented as parameter-free alternatives that outperform prior methods on two of three benchmarks.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification when selecting high-quality topics (example: improved POLITICS vs RELIGION accuracy).  
- Conceptually sound and interpretable: bucket formation aligns with human perception of topic coherence.  
- Parameter-free SVD-based methods reduce the need for manual tuning, making them more deployable for practitioners.  
- Strong empirical results on NYT and 20NG datasets, especially for TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Inconsistent performance across datasets: SVD methods perform poorly on Genomics compared to NYT and 20NG.  
- Paper lacks analysis of why performance drops on Genomics and guidance for method selection across domains.  
- Claim of being parameter-free is misleading because the approach depends on pre-trained word embeddings with implicit parameters.  
- OOV handling is not specified; failure to address out-of-vocabulary terms may explain Genomics results and affect domain-specific performance.  
- Requests authors investigate OOV rates in Genomics and consider domain-specific embeddings as mitigation.  
- Asks authors to clarify the “parameter-free” claim given embedding dependencies and to detail reproducibility implications.  
- Suggests expanding related work discussion (lexical chaining, topic coherence), clarifying embedding normalization for cosine similarity, and improving figure readability (larger graphs).
