- Introduces TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets with three methods (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).  
- Contribution: human-interpretability–grounded framework for measuring topic coherence.  
- Contribution: parameter-free SVD-based techniques that outperform prior methods.  
- Contribution: empirical validation showing topic quality improves weakly supervised text classification.  

- Strength: Strong empirical results — TBuckets-SVD-Reorg beats prior work on 2/3 datasets without hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: Parameter-free methods are practically advantageous for real-world use.  
- Strength: Demonstrated downstream utility with notable classification improvements.  
- Strength: Conceptually clear, human-centered motivation aligning with interpretability literature.  

- Weakness: No theoretical justification for relying on word embeddings to measure topic coherence (context mismatch concern).  
- Weakness: Insufficient robustness analysis — evaluation uses only GloVe, no comparisons to other embeddings like word2vec.  
- Weakness: Technical description (especially TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) is ambiguous and hard to replicate.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope — only three datasets from Röder et al., no non-English, domain-specific, or non-LDA tests.  

- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of word embedding model (GloVe vs. word2vec)?  
- Question: Are the performance differences in Table 1 statistically significant (p-values/confidence intervals)?  
- Question: Why is a dynamic threshold needed for reorganization instead of a fixed threshold?  

- Additional comment: Minor grammatical issues and prose could be tightened.  
- Additional comment: Section 3 should be streamlined with concise algorithmic summaries; 8-page limit is underutilized.  

- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance pending responses to the questions; strong empirical and practical contribution despite theoretical gaps.
