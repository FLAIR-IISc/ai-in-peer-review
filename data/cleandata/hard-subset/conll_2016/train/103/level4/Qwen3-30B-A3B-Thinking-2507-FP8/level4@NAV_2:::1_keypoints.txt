- Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic buckets; primary method TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is parameter-free and outperforms SOTA on NYT and 20NG by better correlating coherence scores with human judgments.
- Secondary contributions: TBuckets-SVD (parameter-free) and TBuckets-Clustering (single-parameter) also perform well and filtering by coherence improves weakly supervised classification.
- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves high Pearson correlations (0.837 NYT, 0.863 20NG) without parameter tuning, offering practical advantages over hyperparameter-heavy methods.
- Strength: Filtering topics by coherence yields 1.5–5.1% higher classification accuracy in weakly supervised text classification on 20NG subsets.
- Strength: Evaluation uses three diverse datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics) with standardized human-annotated coherence scores, supporting generalizability.
- Weakness: Underperforms on Genomics (0.68 vs. SOTA 0.773) with no explanation or analysis of domain-specific issues like OOV terms.
- Weakness: The "parameter-free" claim is misleading because methods rely on pre-trained GloVe embeddings, which carry implicit hyperparameters.
- Weakness: Omits engagement with lexical chaining literature (e.g., Radev et al., 2004), weakening novelty claims given conceptual similarities to bucketing.
- Weakness: No practitioner guidance on method selection (when to use TBuckets-Clustering vs. TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) despite inconsistent dataset performance.
- Question: How are OOV terms in the Genomics dataset handled and what effect do they have on coherence scores?
- Question: Could the Genomics performance gap be due to vocabulary mismatch with GloVe, and what mitigation strategies are recommended for domain-specific applications?
- Question: How do the authors reconcile the "parameter-free" claim with implicit parameter dependencies from using pre-trained embeddings?
