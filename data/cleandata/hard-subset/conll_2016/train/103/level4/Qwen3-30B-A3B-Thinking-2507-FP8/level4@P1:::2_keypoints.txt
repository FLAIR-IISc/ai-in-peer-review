- TBuckets introduces a parameter-free SVD-based coherence metric (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that outperforms prior methods on two of three datasets.  
- Proposes three bucket-formation techniques (clustering, SVD, SVD+reorganization), with the latter two being parameter-free.  
- Demonstrates practical benefit: selecting high-coherence topics with TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification.  

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art results on NYT and 20NG while avoiding hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: Empirical demonstration that coherence-ranked topic sets improve classification accuracy (e.g., best 5 sets 85.61% vs worst 5 sets 82.85%).  
- Strength: Robust experimental design using three standard datasets, human-annotated coherence, Pearson's r, and ablation studies.  

- Weakness: No theoretical justification connecting word-word semantic similarity (embeddings) to human-perceived topic coherence derived from document context.  
- Weakness: Method descriptions (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) are technically dense and unclear; key design choices are not well motivated or organized.  
- Weakness: Missing sensitivity analysis to choice of word embeddings (e.g., word2vec, fastText vs GloVe).  
- Weakness: Results lack statistical significance testing, so reported improvements may not be meaningful.  

- Question: How does TBuckets-SVD-Reorg perform with alternative embeddings (word2vec, fastText) instead of GloVe?  
- Question: Can you provide an intuitive explanation for why embedding-based semantic similarity aligns with human topic coherence given differing contexts?  
- Question: How does the dynamic threshold behave for topics with very low word-word similarity (highly fragmented topics)?
