- Introduces a novel topic-model evaluation that clusters top words by cosine similarity of word embeddings, with sequential bucket assignment and two extensions (eigenvector-based, reorganization).  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, achieving comparable or better results than Röder et al. (2015).  
- Foundational rationale is weak: the paper assumes word–word similarity measures topic coherence but fails to justify why embedding-based similarity aligns with topic interpretability.  
- Authors should link embedding semantics to topic quality or cite prior work on embedding–topic relationships to provide theoretical grounding.  
- Presentation issues: Section 3 is unclear, uses excessive passive voice, and makes the three method descriptions hard to follow.  
- Recommend restructuring Section 3 with active voice, one dedicated subsection per method, explicit rationale and parameters, and removal of redundant traces.  
- Missing statistical validation: results (e.g., Table 1) need significance tests, p-values, or confidence intervals to confirm improvements are non-random.  
- Robustness concern: sensitivity to the choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec) is not tested; include an ablation comparing embedding models.  
- Method is conceptually simple, practically implementable, and empirically effective, especially in the weakly supervised task.  
- Technical contribution is modest but useful; however the paper requires substantial revision on theory, clarity, and robustness to avoid appearing ad hoc.  
- Key revisions needed: (1) theoretical justification for using embeddings, (2) clearer exposition of methods, (3) statistical significance testing, (4) robustness checks across embedding choices.
