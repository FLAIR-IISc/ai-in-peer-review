- Proposes a novel evaluation: cluster top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings with a sequential assignment algorithm and two variants (eigenvector, reorganization).  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets plus a weakly supervised classification task, achieving results that match/outperform Röder et al. (2015); significance tests for Table 1 are requested.  
- Method feels ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification for using word-word embedding similarity to measure topic quality given topics and embeddings arise from different contexts.  
- Section 3’s technical descriptions are sometimes hard to follow (excessive passive voice); authors should clarify each method with explicit design choices and dedicated subsections.  
- Paper is overall clearly written but has minor language issues and could condense uninformative parts (e.g., the "trace" description) to use space for clearer explanations.  
- Key unresolved concern: sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec) and how results change with different embeddings.
