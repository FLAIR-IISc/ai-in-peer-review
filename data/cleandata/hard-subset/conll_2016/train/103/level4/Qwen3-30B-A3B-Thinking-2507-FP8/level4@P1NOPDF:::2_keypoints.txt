- Proposes a novel evaluation: cluster top-n topic words by cosine similarity of word embeddings with a sequential bucket-assignment algorithm and two variants (eigenvectors, iterative reorganization).  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets plus a weakly supervised classification task, matching or outperforming Röder et al. (2015).  
- Core idea is straightforward but lacks theoretical grounding or justification linking topic quality to word–word embedding similarity.  
- Concerns that topics (document-level) and embeddings (sequential context) are fundamentally different, making the approach feel ad hoc and potentially semantically disconnected.  
- Empirical results are promising but Table 1 needs statistical significance testing to strengthen claims.  
- Writing is generally good but Section 3 is occasionally hard to follow due to passive voice; clearer exposition and separate subsections are needed.  
- The 8-page limit isn’t fully utilized; the verbose “trace” on page 3 should be streamlined to allow deeper methodological explanation.  
- Critical omission: no analysis of sensitivity to embedding choice (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec), which weakens evaluation robustness.  
- Overall: empirically promising but requires conceptual justification, clarification of design choices, embedding-sensitivity analysis, and stronger statistical validation.
