- Proposes a novel topic-quality evaluator using word embeddings (direct or via matrix factorization) with strong benchmark performance.  
- Results are inconsistent across datasets: no single method consistently beats prior work; SVD approaches fail notably on the genomics dataset.  
- This variability raises practical concerns about deploying the method broadly.  
- Suspected cause for SVD failure: high OOV term rates in genomics; authors should investigate and consider automating method choice based on vocabulary overlap with pretrained embeddings (e.g., GloVe).  
- Method closely resembles lexical chaining techniques and should cite relevant prior work.  
- The "no parameters" claim is misleading because pretrained embeddings (e.g., GloVe) have many implicit parameters and this dependency should be acknowledged.  
- Paper does not specify OOV handling (e.g., exclusion) or its impact on evaluation robustness—this must be clarified.  
- Technical correction: cosine similarity assumes unit-length vectors; note that standard word2vec outputs are not normalized and normalization should be discussed.  
- Figure 1 visualizations are too small—improve resolution for clarity.  
- Overall: promising contribution, but addressing dataset inconsistency, OOV handling, and embedding dependency is needed to strengthen practical reliability.
