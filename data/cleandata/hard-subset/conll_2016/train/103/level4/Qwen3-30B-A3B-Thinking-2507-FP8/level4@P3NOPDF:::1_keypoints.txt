- Introduces a novel topic-quality evaluation using word embeddings and matrix factorization with strong benchmark performance.  
- Performance is inconsistent across datasets; no single approach consistently outperforms baselines.  
- SVD-based variants fail notably on the genomics dataset, undermining practical applicability.  
- High OOV rate in genomics likely explains SVD failure, but the paper does not analyze OOV effects or vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.  
- Authors do not explore automated method selection based on embedding coverage, posing a risk for practitioners.  
- Fails to engage related lexical-chaining literature (e.g., Cao et al. 2012; Liu et al. 2015) to clarify novelty.  
- Claim that the method "has no parameters" is misleading because it relies on implicitly parameterized pre-trained embeddings.  
- OOV handling is unspecified (ignored or otherwise), harming reproducibility and potentially biasing results.  
- Incorrectly assumes embedding vectors are unit-length for cosine similarity; this technical oversight affects validity.  
- Figure 1 graphs are too small, reducing interpretability of results.  
- Overall: promising idea but requires major revision to address OOV analysis, related work, parameterization, vector normalization, and presentation before publication.
