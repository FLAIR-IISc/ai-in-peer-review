- Paper proposes a word-embedding–based topic quality evaluation method (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) and achieves state-of-the-art results on standard benchmarks.  
- Major concern: substantial inconsistency in method performance across datasets despite SOTA results.  
- No single proposed variant consistently outperforms prior work; SVD-based methods perform particularly poorly on the genomics dataset.  
- Likely cause: high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term rates in genomics (authors do not analyze or explain this).  
- Suggested remedy (not explored): automatically select the best method based on vocabulary overlap with pretrained embeddings (e.g., GloVe).  
- Method strongly resembles lexical chaining approaches; related literature should be cited and discussed.  
- Paper claims “no explicit parameters” but ignores that embedding methods have many implicit parameters — this should be acknowledged.  
- OOV handling is not described (are OOV terms ignored and what is the impact on evaluation?).  
- Technical detail: Section 2.1 assumes vector length is irrelevant for cosine similarity, but pretrained and self-trained embeddings may not be unit length — clarify.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 is too small/illegible and needs resizing for readability.
