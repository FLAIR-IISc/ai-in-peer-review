- Introduces a novel embedding-based method for topic quality evaluation with strong empirical performance on standard datasets.  
- Results are inconsistent across datasets: no single method consistently outperforms others.  
- SVD-based variants perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset.  
- Inconsistency limits practical applicability for broad deployment across diverse domains.  
- Suspected cause: high proportion of OOV terms in genomics relative to GloVe; authors do not investigate this.  
- Missing mitigation: no strategy or analysis for predicting optimal method selection based on vocabulary overlap.  
- Methodological overlap with lexical chaining literature is not discussed or cited.  
- Claim of “no parameters” is misleading because underlying embeddings (e.g., GloVe) contain many parameters and should be acknowledged.  
- OOV handling is not described: unclear whether terms are ignored and what quantitative impact OOVs have on results.  
- Technical inaccuracy: cosine similarity discussion omits the need for vector normalization (pretrained embeddings are not unit-length by default).  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 graphs are too small and illegible.  
- Overall: promising idea but the OOV issue, literature omissions, parameter ambiguity, inconsistent results, and technical inaccuracies significantly weaken the contribution; these must be addressed.
