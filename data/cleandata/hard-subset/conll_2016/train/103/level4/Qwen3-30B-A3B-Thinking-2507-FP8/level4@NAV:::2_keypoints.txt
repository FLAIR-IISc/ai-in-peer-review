- Proposes clustering top-n words per topic into buckets using cosine similarity of word embeddings, assigning words sequentially or creating new buckets when similarity falls below a threshold.  
- Introduces two variants (eigenvector-based and reorganization) alongside the core sequential method.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task, achieving results that match or surpass Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method is conceptually straightforward but feels ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification for using word–word similarity to measure topic quality.  
- Raises a conceptual mismatch: topics are document-contextual while embeddings capture sequential/contextual word information, weakening the rationale.  
- Demonstrates practical effectiveness, but significance testing (e.g., for Table 1) is missing and would strengthen claims.  
- Writing is generally good but has minor language issues and occasional opacity in Section 3 due to passive voice.  
- Recommends clearer presentation: dedicate one subsection per method and remove or replace the uninformative "trace" section.  
- Specific clarity issue: design choices (e.g., how the threshold is computed) are not explained explicitly.  
- Important unresolved question: sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec) and how results change across embeddings.
