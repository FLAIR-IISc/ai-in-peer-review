- Introduces a novel bucket-based evaluation of topic models using cosine similarity of top-n word embeddings with three variants (sequential, eigenvector, reorganization) and reports strong results on three datasets plus a weakly supervised classification task, matching or beating Röder et al. (2015).  
- Core idea is straightforward but feels ad hoc; no theoretical justification is provided for measuring topic quality via word–word similarity.  
- Raises a fundamental concern about mismatch between topic/document-level semantics and the sequential/contextual basis of word embeddings.  
- Empirical results are promising, but significance testing (e.g., for Table 1) is missing and would strengthen the claims.  
- Paper is mostly clearly written, but Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and dense exposition; recommend one subsection per method and trimming the uninformative “trace.”  
- The authors should investigate sensitivity to choice of embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe) and report how results change.
