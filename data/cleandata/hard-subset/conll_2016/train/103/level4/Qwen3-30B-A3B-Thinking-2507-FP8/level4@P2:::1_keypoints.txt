- Introduces TBuckets: groups topic words into thematic buckets to measure LDA topic quality using word-embedding-based semantic similarity.  
- Proposes three bucket formation techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganization step).  
- Coherence scores derived from size/composition of the largest bucket; SVD-based methods are parameter-free and use pre-trained embeddings.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art correlation on NYT and 20NG datasets, showing strong empirical performance.  

- TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg require no hyperparameter tuning, a practical advantage over prior methods.  
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior work on two datasets with higher Pearson r correlations.  
- Demonstrates practical utility: filtering topics by TBuckets coherence improves weakly supervised text classification accuracy by 1–2%.  
- Bucketing approach aligns with human intuition—single dominant theme indicates high topic quality.  

- Performs significantly worse on Genomics (0.68 vs. 0.773), raising concerns about generalizability of SVD-based techniques.  
- Paper omits discussion of handling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) domain-specific terms (likely relevant to Genomics performance).  
- “Parameter-free” claim is misleading because method depends on pre-trained GloVe embeddings with implicit parameters.  
- Fails to cite or relate to lexical chaining literature despite strong methodological similarities.  

- Question: How are OOV terms handled (ignored/replaced/processed), and how does that affect coherence scores?  
- Question: What dataset characteristics determine which TBuckets technique works best (e.g., vocabulary overlap with embeddings)?  
- Question: How does TBuckets relate to lexical chaining work, and why was that literature omitted?  

- Section 2.1 incorrectly assumes embeddings are unit-length for cosine similarity unless normalized.  
- Figure 1 is too small to interpret effects of Max Dist for TBuckets-Clustering.  
- Paper should provide guidance on when to use Clustering vs. SVD-based techniques based on dataset traits.  

- Overall recommendation: Accept with major revisions to address Genomics performance, OOV handling, and the parameter-free claim.
