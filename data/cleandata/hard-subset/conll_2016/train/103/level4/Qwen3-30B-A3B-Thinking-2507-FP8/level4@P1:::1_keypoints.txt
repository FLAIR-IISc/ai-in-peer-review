- Summary: Introduces TBuckets, grouping topic words into semantic buckets via three methods (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) and shows SVD-Reorg outperforms prior work on NYT and 20NG while being parameter-free.

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves top results on two of three datasets without parameter tuning.

- Strength: Method has clear, intuitive justification aligned with human judgments of topic coherence and includes compelling examples.

- Strength: Demonstrates practical benefit in weakly supervised text classification by selecting higher-quality topics to improve accuracy.

- Weakness: Inconsistent performance — strong on NYT and 20NG but worse on Genomics, raising concerns for general applicability.

- Weakness: Paper does not address out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, likely relevant to poor Genomics performance.

- Weakness: Claim of being "completely parameter-free" is misleading because word embeddings (e.g., GloVe) introduce implicit parameters.

- Weakness: No analysis of how vocabulary coverage or overlap with embedding vocabularies affects performance.

- Question: How are OOV/domain-specific terms handled (ignored, mapped, or otherwise) and what is their impact on coherence scores?

- Question: Can dataset characteristics (e.g., vocabulary overlap with embeddings) predict which TBuckets variant will perform best?

- Question: How does TBuckets relate to or differ from lexical chaining approaches?

- Question: Are the GloVe embeddings unit-normalized or otherwise adjusted, given the cosine-similarity discussion about vector length?

- Additional comment: Figure 1 is too small and x-axis labels are unreadable.

- Additional comment: Paper should explicitly acknowledge that underlying word embeddings introduce implicit parameters that may affect results.
