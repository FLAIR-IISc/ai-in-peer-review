- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into semantic buckets with embeddings and three techniques (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).
- Claims TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior coherence measures on 2/3 datasets, needs no parameter tuning, and helps weakly supervised classification.

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg shows superior performance on NYT and 20NG and is parameter-free, avoiding costly tuning.
- Strength: Selecting high-coherence topics via TBuckets improves text classification accuracy substantially (e.g., >5% gaps on challenging splits like PC vs MAC).
- Strength: Evaluation follows Roder et al. (2015) datasets/metrics for direct comparison and includes illustrative topic bucketing examples.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why sequential-context embeddings should correlate with document-context topic coherence.
- Weakness: Description of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg reorganization logic and parameter calculation is unclear and hard to follow.
- Weakness: No sensitivity analysis across different word embedding models (e.g., GloVe vs word2vec), despite heavy reliance on embeddings.
- Weakness: Table 1 results lack statistical significance testing, so improvements may not be reliable.

- Question: How would TBuckets-SVD-Reorg perform with word2vec embeddings—would method rankings remain consistent?
- Question: Please clarify the reorganization threshold calculation (AWES − AWPS/AWES) and rationale for this specific formulation.
