

Summary: This paper introduces TBuckets, a novel approach for measuring topic quality in Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) models by grouping topic words into semantic buckets using word embeddings. The authors propose three techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering), TBuckets-SVD (SVD-based bucket identification), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganization of buckets). The main contributions are: (1) TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, which outperforms state-of-the-art coherence measures on two out of three datasets while requiring no parameter tuning; (2) demonstration that topic coherence can be effectively measured through semantic word grouping using embeddings; and (3) application of the coherence measure to improve weakly supervised text classification performance.

Strengths: 
1. The TBuckets-SVD-Reorg technique demonstrates superior performance on NYT and 20NG datasets compared to state-of-the-art methods, with the notable advantage of being completely parameter-free, which eliminates the need for extensive parameter tuning required by existing approaches.
2. The practical utility of the method is convincingly demonstrated through text classification experiments, where selecting high-quality topics using TBuckets leads to significantly improved classification accuracy (especially on challenging datasets like PC vs MAC), with performance differences between high and low coherence topic sets reaching over 5% in some cases.
3. The evaluation framework is well-designed, using the same datasets and metrics as Roder et al. (2015) for direct comparison, and the authors provide clear examples of topic bucketing (Table 2) that illustrate the method's intuitive nature.

Weaknesses:
1. The theoretical justification for using semantic word similarity as a measure of topic coherence is underdeveloped. The authors fail to adequately explain why word embeddings (based on sequential context) should correlate with topic quality (based on document context), which is a fundamental limitation of their approach.
2. The description of the TBuckets-SVD-Reorg technique, particularly the reorganization logic and parameter calculation, is difficult to follow. The paper's passive voice construction and lack of algorithmic clarity make it challenging to understand the exact implementation of the reorganization step.
3. The paper lacks sensitivity analysis for different word embedding models. It's unclear how the results would change with different embeddings (e.g., word2vec instead of GloVe), which is a significant concern given the method's heavy reliance on embedding quality.
4. The results in Table 1 lack statistical significance tests, making it difficult to assess whether the observed performance improvements are meaningful or could be attributed to random variation.

Questions to Authors:
1. How would the performance of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg change if word2vec embeddings were used instead of GloVe embeddings? Would the relative ranking of methods remain consistent?
2. Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the reorganization threshold calculation (AWES − AWPS/AWES) and why this particular formulation was chosen over alternatives?
