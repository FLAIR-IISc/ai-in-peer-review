- Introduces a novel evaluation method that clusters top topic words using cosine similarity of word embeddings and partitions them into "buckets" by a similarity threshold.  
- Proposes two extensions: an eigenvector-based method and a reorganization method.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, matching or exceeding prior state-of-the-art (Röder et al., 2015).  
- Strong empirical performance notwithstanding, the theoretical foundation is underdeveloped and not justified.  
- No rationale is provided for why topic quality (document-context) should correlate with word–word similarity (sequential-context embeddings).  
- Recommends adding significance tests for Table 1 to establish statistical robustness.  
- Writing is generally good but has minor language issues; excessive passive voice (especially in Section 3) obscures methodological choices.  
- Section 3’s technical descriptions are hard to follow and should be clarified with active voice and explicit design decisions.  
- Paper underuses the 8-page limit; the "trace" description is uninformative and could be condensed to improve clarity and add subsections.  
- Critical question about sensitivity to embedding choice (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe) is unaddressed and essential for assessing robustness.  
- Overall, a practical and empirically strong tool that risks appearing ad hoc without deeper theoretical justification and clearer methodological transparency.
