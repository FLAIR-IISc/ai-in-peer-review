- Introduces TBuckets: a method that measures topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings.  
- Proposes three techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering), TBuckets-SVD (SVD to find sub-themes), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD with a reorganization step).  
- Key insight: a single dominant bucket indicates high topic coherence; multiple medium-sized buckets imply lower coherence.  
- SVD-based methods are parameter-free and outperform prior methods without hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strong empirical performance on three datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics) and useful for weakly supervised text classification.  
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves highest correlation with human judgments on NYT and 20NG, beating Roder et al. (2015).  
- Parameter-free advantage is well-documented (Table 1, Figure 1) with consistent outperformance without tuning.  
- Practical benefit shown: selecting high-quality topics via TBuckets improves classification accuracy (e.g., POLITICS vs. SCIENCE 96.19% vs. 95.95%; PC vs. MAC 68.47% vs. 66.98%).  
- Weakness: no theoretical justification for why word embeddings should correlate with topic coherence; document-based topics vs. sequential-context embeddings not reconciled.  
- Weakness: methodological descriptions (especially Section 3.2 and the reorganization step) are hard to follow and lack rationale for specific formulas.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to different embedding models not evaluated—experiments use only GloVe, omitting word2vec/fastText comparisons.  
- Suggestion: restructure Section 3 with dedicated subsections for each technique and clearer design rationale; shorten overly detailed basic bucketing description.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of coherence scores to embedding choice (please test alternatives like word2vec); theoretical justification for embedding–coherence link; clarification on how the dynamic threshold (AWES - AWPS/AWES) is computed and whether it generalizes across domains.
