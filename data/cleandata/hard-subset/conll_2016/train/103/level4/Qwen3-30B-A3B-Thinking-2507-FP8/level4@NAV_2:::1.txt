

Summary: The paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach for measuring topic quality in LDA-based topic models by grouping topic words into thematic buckets. The primary contribution is TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, a parameter-free technique that outperforms state-of-the-art methods on two of three benchmark datasets (NYT and 20NG) by correlating automatically generated coherence scores with human judgments. Secondary contributions include TBuckets-SVD (parameter-free) and TBuckets-Clustering (single-parameter), both demonstrating strong performance. The method's utility for weakly supervised text classification is validated through improved classification accuracy when filtering topics by coherence.

Strengths:
1. TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art Pearson correlation (0.837 on NYT, 0.863 on 20NG) without any parameter tuning, providing a significant practical advantage over methods requiring extensive hyperparameter optimization.
2. The application to weakly supervised text classification demonstrates clear empirical utility, with classification accuracy increasing by 1.5-5.1% when using only high-coherence topics compared to random topic selection across three 20NG subsets.
3. Comprehensive evaluation across three diverse datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics) with standardized human-annotated coherence scores provides robust validation of the approach's generalizability.

Weaknesses:
1. Inconsistent performance on the Genomics dataset (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg: 0.68 vs. SOTA 0.773) lacks explanation, particularly regarding potential impacts of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms common in domain-specific corpora like Genomics, which are not addressed in the paper.
2. The claim of "parameter-free" methods is misleading, as the approach implicitly relies on pre-trained GloVe embeddings (with inherent hyperparameters), which should be acknowledged in the discussion.
3. Failure to engage with lexical chaining literature (e.g., Radev et al., 2004), which shares conceptual similarities with the bucketing procedure, weakens the novelty claim and represents a significant omission in related work.
4. No method selection guidance is provided for practitioners, leaving uncertainty about when to use TBuckets-Clustering vs. TBuckets-SVD-Reorg given their inconsistent performance across datasets.

Questions to Authors:
1. How are out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms in the Genomics dataset handled (e.g., ignored, replaced, or approximated), and what impact does this have on coherence scores?
2. Could the performance gap on Genomics be attributed to vocabulary mismatch with GloVe embeddings, and if so, what strategies could mitigate this for domain-specific applications?
3. How does the paper reconcile the "parameter-free" claim with the implicit parameter dependencies of the pre-trained embeddings used in all techniques?
