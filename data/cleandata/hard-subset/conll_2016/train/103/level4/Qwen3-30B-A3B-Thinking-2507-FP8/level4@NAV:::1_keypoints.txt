- Proposes a topic quality evaluation method using word-embedding similarity (direct or via matrix factorization) that achieves strong, state-of-the-art results on benchmarks.  
- Overall contribution is logical and significant for topic evaluation research.  
- Major concern: substantial inconsistency in performance across the three datasets; no single proposed method consistently outperforms prior art.  
- SVD-based approaches perform notably worse on the genomics dataset.  
- Lack of robustness across datasets is a practical concern for real-world deployment.  
- Likely cause: high proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms in genomics; paper fails to analyze or report this critical insight.  
- Suggestion (missing): predict optimal method choice from vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe).  
- Method closely resembles lexical chaining techniques; relevant prior work should be cited and discussed.  
- Claim of being parameter-free is misleading because underlying word-embedding methods have substantial implicit parameters that should be acknowledged.  
- Handling of OOV terms is unclear (discarded or otherwise) and their impact on evaluation robustness is not reported.  
- Technical oversight: Section 2.1 assumes vector length irrelevant for cosine similarity, but pre-trained/self-trained embeddings may not be unit-normalized—this needs clarification.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 graphs are too small and illegible.
