- Introduces TBuckets, a bucket-based topic coherence framework using word-embedding similarity with three techniques (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).
- Empirical claim: SVD-Reorg outperforms prior work on NYT and 20NG and clustering is competitive on Genomics.
- Application: selecting higher-TBuckets-coherence topic models generally improves weakly supervised text classification (macro-F1).

- Strength: Conceptually clear and interpretable—mirrors human assessment of a dominant semantic cluster.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with minimal tuning; SVD variants are parameter-free at inference.
- Strength: Practical utility shown—filtering by TBuckets improves classification, especially on harder subsets.
- Strength: Sensible scoring design—largest-bucket size, ordering weights, and eigenvector/singular-value signals are complementary.
- Strength: Resource-light and easy to adopt—needs only topic-word lists and off-the-shelf embeddings.

- Weakness: Inconsistent performance across datasets/variants; no single variant is uniformly best and SVD underperforms on Genomics.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on pre-trained embeddings and unclear OOV handling; Genomics likely affected by domain OOVs.
- Weakness: “Parameter-free” claim is overstated because embeddings introduce many implicit hyperparameters and domain-match risks.
- Weakness: Missing connection to prior lexical chaining literature; positioning and novelty relative to that work are unclear.
- Weakness: Clarity/presentation problems—Figure 1 too small and Section 2.1 omits explicit statements about vector normalization and cosine assumptions.

- Q: How are OOV words treated in each TBuckets variant, what are per-dataset OOV rates (esp. Genomics), and how do OOV handling choices affect scores?
- Q: Have domain-specific or subword embeddings (e.g., biomedical embeddings, fastText) been tested for Genomics to mitigate OOV/domain mismatch?
- Q: Can you provide guidance or an automatic criterion to choose among Clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg (e.g., based on vocabulary coverage or domain match)?
- Q: Do SVD variants change if vectors are L2-normalized, if different similarity metrics are used, and are bucket assignments stable across seeds?
- Q: Implementation detail: are word vectors and eigenvectors L2-normalized before cosine/SVD computations?
- Q: How sensitive are results to embedding model choice (GloVe vs word2vec vs fastText) and embedding dimensionality?
- Q: Ablation: what is the incremental value of WL and EVS versus using largest-bucket size alone?
- Q: Reproducibility: please enlarge Figure 1 and specify exact pre-trained embeddings, their corpora/dimensions, and preprocessing (lowercasing/tokenization).

- Suggestion: Report simple predictors (OOV rate, average IDF, domain-term ratio) and correlate them with relative method performance.
- Suggestion: Add a brief discussion contrasting TBuckets’ embedding/SVD approach with classical lexical chaining methods (thesauri/WordNet-based).
- Suggestion: Clarify Section 2.1’s treatment of cosine similarity and vector normalization for reproducibility.
