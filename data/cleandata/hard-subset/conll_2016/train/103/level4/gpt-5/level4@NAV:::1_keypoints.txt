- Proposes an embedding-based topic quality method (direct similarity or via matrix factorisation) and reports strong results on standard datasets.  
- Despite reporting state-of-the-art overall, variants show substantial inconsistency and none consistently surpasses the state of the art.  
- SVD-based variants perform particularly poorly on the genomics dataset, raising practical deployment concerns.  
- Suspect SVD weakness is tied to OOV terms / vocabulary overlap with GloVe; paper does not analyze or predict which variant will work best based on overlap.  
- Method closely resembles lexical chaining approaches; reviewers recommend citing and incorporating that literature.  
- Claim that the method has “no parameters” is misleading because pretrained embeddings contain many implicit parameters and this should be acknowledged.  
- Clarify how OOV terms are handled (especially in genomics)—are they omitted, and how does that affect performance?  
- Technical detail: discussion of cosine similarity ignores vector norms; word2vec embeddings are not inherently unit-length and this should be addressed.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 plots are too small and unreadable.
