- Introduces TBuckets: grouping a topic’s top words into embedding-space “buckets” to measure topic coherence via dominance of the largest bucket.  
- Proposes three methods: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering), TBuckets-SVD (use SVD eigenvectors as buckets), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (post-hoc word reassignments using dynamic thresholds).  
- Scores coherence from properties of the largest bucket and correlates with human judgments on three datasets; applies coherence selection to weakly supervised classification.  
- Contribution: a simple, embedding-based bucketing framework aiming to capture single-theme dominance with a parameter-free SVD variant and a reorganization heuristic.  
- Contribution: Empirical evidence that TBuckets-SVD-Reorg matches or outperforms prior best on NYT and 20NG and that coherence-based selection improves weakly supervised classification.  
- Contribution: Provides an interpretable diagnostic (bucket distributions) that aids topic error analysis.  
- Strength: Clear, intuitive bucketing metaphor that mirrors human topic assessment and improves interpretability over opaque coherence scores.  
- Strength: Strong performance on two datasets with minimal tuning, making the approach practitioner-friendly.  
- Strength: SVD-based variants reduce explicit hyperparameter tuning by avoiding threshold choices during bucket formation.  
- Strength: Demonstrated practical application where selecting higher-coherence topic runs boosts downstream classification performance.  
- Strength: Reorganization step addresses a realistic failure mode and uses dynamic thresholds (AWPS/AWES) to avoid extra hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent performance across datasets and methods; no single variant consistently dominates, and clustering needs a max-distance parameter sensitive to linkage.  
- Weakness: Dependence on pretrained embeddings and unclear OOV handling likely harms domain-specific datasets like Genomics; OOV rates and impacts are unreported.  
- Weakness: “No parameters” claim is overstated because embedding choices and reorg thresholds are implicit design decisions.  
- Weakness: Largest-bucket bias may penalize legitimately multi-theme but coherent topics, conflating multi-faceted coherence with incoherence.  
- Weakness: Limited statistical analysis and ablations—no confidence intervals, significance tests, or component-wise ablations to assess contributions.  
- Question: How are OOV words handled across methods, what are OOV rates per dataset, and how do OOVs affect correlations?  
- Question: Can you provide significance testing (e.g., Williams’ test) and confidence intervals for correlation differences vs baselines and among variants?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding choice (GloVe vs word2vec vs fastText) and to domain-specific embeddings for Genomics?  
- Question: For TBuckets-SVD, how is eigenvector stability ensured given rank constraints and sign indeterminacy—are eigenvectors normalized before similarity?  
- Question: For the weak supervision experiment, report variance across runs, statistical tests for S1 vs S2, and the annotation-time saving from labeling only top-5 runs.  
- Additional comment: Discuss connections to lexical chaining and semantic grouping literature.  
- Additional comment: Clarify cosine similarity and normalization details for reproducibility with pretrained vectors.  
- Additional comment: Improve Figure 1 readability by enlarging labels and legends.  
- Additional comment: Consider an automatic rule to select among TBuckets variants (e.g., based on OOV rate) to reduce domain risk.  
- Additional comment: Release code and evaluation scripts to aid reproducibility and adoption.  
- Overall evaluation: Well-motivated, practical approach with competitive results and a promising downstream application, but needs OOV/embedding robustness, broader sensitivity analyses, and stronger statistical/ablation evidence.
