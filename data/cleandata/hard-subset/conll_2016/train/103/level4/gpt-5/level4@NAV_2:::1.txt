This paper presents TBuckets, an embedding-based framework for assessing topic quality by grouping top topic words into semantic buckets via clustering or SVD (with and without reorganization). Similarity is computed in embedding space (directly or via matrix factorization). The approach reports strong results on public datasets.

The aspect that concerns me most is the inconsistency across datasets and variants. While you report state-of-the-art performance across the three datasets, which specific TBuckets variant succeeds varies considerably, and none of the proposed methods is uniformly superior to prior work. In particular, the SVD-based methods perform notably poorly on the Genomics set. For practical, off-the-shelf use on arbitrary corpora, this variability is problematic. My hunch is that the weaker SVD results on Genomics stem from a higher rate of OOV terms (see below). It may be feasible to predict which variant to use from simple diagnostics (e.g., vocabulary coverage against GloVe), but the paper does not discuss this.

Other issues:
- There are clear conceptual overlaps with lexical chaining (e.g., building cohesive chains/buckets of semantically related words). Please situate the work relative to that literature in a revision.
- You emphasize that the SVD-based methods are parameter-free, but the approach is still contingent on word embeddings, which themselves involve many design choices and hyperparameters. Not critical, but worth acknowledging explicitly.
- How are OOV topic words handled (e.g., in the Genomics dataset) when no pretrained embedding exists? Are they omitted or assigned a default vector? What effect does this have on both bucket formation and the final coherence score?

Low-level issues:
- In Section 2.1, you imply cosine similarity can be applied without regard to vector norms. That holds if vectors are unit-normalized; however, word2vec does not inherently return unit-length vectors (pretrained releases may be normalized post hoc, and self-trained models typically are not). Please clarify normalization before computing cosine similarities.
- The plots in Figure 1 are too small to be legible; please enlarge or reorganize them.
