- Proposes a new evaluation method for topic models that clusters each topic’s top-n words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Implements a simple greedy assignment plus two more complex variants using eigenvectors and reorganization.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task, showing results on par with or better than Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method is simple but somewhat ad hoc; authors do not justify why topic quality should be measured by word–word similarity.  
- Concern that word embeddings and topic models use different context notions (sequential vs. document-level), so similarity-based scores may be conceptually inconsistent.  
- Requests significance tests for Table 1 to support empirical claims.  
- Paper is generally clearly written but contains language issues and a confusing Section 3 description.  
- Section 3 suffers from passive voice and unclear design choices; recommends one subsection per method for clarity.  
- Paper underutilizes available space and should remove the uninformative “trace” on page 3.  
- Asks about sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec) and how scores would change.
