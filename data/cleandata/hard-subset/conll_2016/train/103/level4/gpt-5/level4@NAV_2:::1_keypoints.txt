- Presents TBuckets: embedding-based topic-quality framework that groups top words into semantic buckets via clustering or SVD (with/without reorganization) and computes similarity in embedding space.  
- Reports strong results on public datasets but variant performance is inconsistent across datasets.  
- No single TBuckets variant is uniformly superior to prior work; which variant wins varies considerably.  
- SVD-based methods perform poorly on the Genomics dataset relative to other methods.  
- Variability limits practical off-the-shelf use on arbitrary corpora.  
- Reviewer hypothesizes poor SVD results on Genomics may stem from higher OOV term rates; paper does not analyze this.  
- Suggests the authors could predict the best variant using simple diagnostics (e.g., vocabulary coverage against GloVe), but this is not discussed.  
- Needs positioning relative to lexical chaining literature (conceptual overlap).  
- Claim that SVD methods are parameter-free should acknowledge dependency on word embeddings and their hyperparameters.  
- Clarify how OOV topic words are handled (omitted or default vectors) and their effect on bucket formation and coherence scores.  
- Technical fix: clarify vector normalization before computing cosine similarity (pretrained/self-trained embeddings may not be unit-length).  
- Presentation fix: enlarge or reorganize Figure 1 because current plots are too small to read.
