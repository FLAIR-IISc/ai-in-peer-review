- Proposes a new evaluation method that clusters a topic’s top-n words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with a simple sequential algorithm plus eigenvector and reorganisation variants, tested on three datasets and weakly supervised classification, matching or beating prior work (Röder et al., 2015).  
- Core idea is straightforward but feels ad hoc; the authors do not justify why word–word embedding similarity should quantify topic quality given different contextual bases (document vs. sequential).  
- Empirical results look strong, but significance testing (e.g., for Table 1) is requested.  
- Paper is overall clear but has language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive phrasing and unclear design choices—recommend separate, transparent subsections for each method and shorten the uninformative trace.  
- Raises concern about sensitivity to embedding choice and asks how results would change using different embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe).
