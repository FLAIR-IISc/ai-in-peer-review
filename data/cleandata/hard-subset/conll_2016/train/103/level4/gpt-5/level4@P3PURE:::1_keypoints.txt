- Proposes topic-quality evaluation using word embeddings (direct or via matrix factorization) and reports strong results on standard datasets.  
- Main concern: inconsistent performance across variants and datasets—no single method consistently outperforms prior work.  
- SVD-based variants perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset.  
- Practical worry: variability undermines an off-the-shelf solution for arbitrary datasets.  
- Hypothesis: weaker SVD results on genomics may stem from high OOV term proportion; paper does not analyze this.  
- Suggestion: consider predicting the best variant automatically based on vocabulary overlap with pretrained embeddings (e.g., GloVe).  
- Related work: strong similarities to lexical chaining literature—should be reviewed and cited.  
- Parameter claim: “no parameters” is misleading because pretrained word embeddings introduce implicit hyperparameters; acknowledge this.  
- OOV handling: unclear how OOV terms (especially in genomics) are treated and how that affects scores and reliability.  
- Technical detail: cosine similarity assumes unit-length vectors—clarify normalization since word2vec vectors are not inherently unit-length.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 graphs are too small to read; enlarge them.  
- Overall: promising and timely contribution, but needs clearer discussion of robustness, OOV effects, variant-selection guidance, and connections to lexical chaining.
