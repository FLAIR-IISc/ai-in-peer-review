- Proposes evaluating topic models by clustering a topic’s top-n words into “buckets” using cosine similarity over word embeddings, with a simple sequential assignment and two advanced variants (eigenvector-based and reassignment).

- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task, matching or outperforming Röder et al. (2015).

- Core idea is straightforward but ad hoc; paper provides little theoretical justification for using pairwise word embedding similarity to measure topic quality.

- Concern that embeddings reflect local/sequential context while topics are document-level co-occurrence, calling the assumption nontrivial.

- Empirical results look promising, but Table 1 lacks statistical significance testing.

- Writing is generally clear but has language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design choices.

- Recommend restructuring Section 3 into one subsection per method to clearly explain design decisions and shorten the uninformative “trace.”

- Paper underuses the eight-page limit and could reallocate space to clearer exposition.

- Open question about sensitivity to choice of word embeddings—robustness analysis across embedding types (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe) is needed.
