- Proposes clustering top-n topic words into buckets using cosine similarity from pre-trained word embeddings, with a simple sequential assignment and two advanced variants (eigenvectors + reorganization); evaluated on three datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, matching or beating Röder et al. (2015).

- Core idea is straightforward but somewhat ad hoc and lacks a new justification for why embedding-based word–word similarity should measure topic quality, given different context notions behind topics and embeddings.

- Method empirically performs well, but the reviewer requests statistical significance tests for the comparisons reported in Table 1.

- Paper is generally clearly written but has occasional language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow and passive phrasing hides design choices.

- Recommend reorganizing presentation: dedicate subsections to each variant with explicit design decisions and motivations, and condense the uninformative trace to free space.

- Major missing analysis: sensitivity/robustness to choice of word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe) — include experiments or discussion to support generality.
