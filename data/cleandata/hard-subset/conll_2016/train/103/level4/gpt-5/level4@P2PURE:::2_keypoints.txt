- Introduces an evaluation that clusters a topic’s top-n words into “buckets” using cosine similarity of word embeddings (sequential bucket assignment and two variants based on eigenvectors and reorganization).  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task, where it matches or outperforms Röder et al. (2015).  
- Core idea is simple and somewhat ad hoc rather than theoretically motivated.  
- Paper does not justify why word–word similarity (embedding context) should measure topic quality (topics are document-level, embeddings often reflect sequential context).  
- Results table (Table 1) lacks significance testing to support performance claims.  
- Overall clear writing but contains language issues.  
- Section 3’s technical description is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design choices; needs clearer exposition and dedicated subsections for each method.  
- Paper underuses the allotted eight pages; the uninformative “trace” on page 3 could be shortened to free space.  
- Open question about sensitivity to the choice of word embeddings (e.g., how scores change with word2vec vs. GloVe).
