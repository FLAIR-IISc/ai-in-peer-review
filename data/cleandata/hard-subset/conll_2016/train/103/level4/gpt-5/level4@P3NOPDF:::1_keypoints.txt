- Strength: Clear, intuitive idea—use embedding-based similarity to assess topic coherence.
- Strength: Strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art on the three datasets.
- Strength: Conceptually simple and potentially generalizable across models and domains.

- Concern: Inconsistent performance—no single variant reliably outperforms across datasets; SVD methods fail notably on genomics.
- Concern: Likely sensitivity to vocabulary coverage and OOV terms; genomics domain mismatch may explain poor results.
- Concern: “No parameters” claim is misleading because results depend on pretrained embeddings with many implicit design choices.

- Request: Report OOV handling strategy, per-dataset OOV rates, and ablations showing performance as a function of coverage.
- Request: Provide guidance/diagnostics (coverage, domain similarity, lexical variability) to predict which variant will work best.
- Request: Analyze sensitivity to embedding choice and preprocessing; state whether vectors are L2-normalized before cosine similarity.
- Request: Discuss relation to lexical chaining and prior semantic-proximity methods, and how this approach differs.

- Minor: Improve figure readability (larger fonts, thicker lines, higher resolution; move dense plots to appendix).
- Minor: Rephrase “no parameters” to clarify reliance on pretrained embeddings and dataset-specific settings.
- Minor: Provide exact preprocessing steps, vocabulary matching/OOV mapping, SVD settings, and runtime/memory costs.

- Suggestion: Add a coverage analysis correlating embedding coverage with metric performance and run controlled coverage experiments.
- Suggestion: Include an automatic selection heuristic (e.g., coverage threshold or small held-out CV) to pick the best variant.
- Suggestion: Evaluate with domain-specific embeddings (e.g., biomedical) to isolate domain mismatch effects.
- Suggestion: Provide error analysis of topics where metrics disagree with humans to expose failure modes.
- Suggestion: Explicitly state normalization practices for vectors and SVD inputs/outputs.

- Overall: Promising, well-motivated contribution with encouraging results, but inconsistent cross-dataset performance and insufficient coverage/OOV and preprocessing analysis limit practical reliability; addressing these issues would strengthen the paper.
