- Proposes an embedding-based topic quality metric (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) with strong results on standard datasets.  
- Main concern: inconsistent performance across method variants and datasets; no single proposed variant consistently outperforms existing baselines.  
- SVD-based variants perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset.  
- This variability is problematic for practitioners wanting an off-the-shelf solution.  
- Possible explanation: high proportion of OOV terms (e.g., missing from pretrained GloVe) may drive weak SVD results, but the paper lacks analysis.  
- Suggestion: consider automatically predicting the best variant based on vocabulary overlap with pretrained embeddings.  
- Method has strong parallels to lexical chaining literature; recommend reviewing and citing that work.  
- Claiming the method has “no parameters” ignores that underlying pretrained embeddings carry many implicit parameters—acknowledge this.  
- Clarify how OOV terms are handled (e.g., ignored?) and quantify their impact on the metric.  
- Technical/detail issue: assuming vector length is unimportant is only valid for unit-normalized vectors; word2vec often yields non-uniform norms and this should be noted.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 plots are too small to be readable.
