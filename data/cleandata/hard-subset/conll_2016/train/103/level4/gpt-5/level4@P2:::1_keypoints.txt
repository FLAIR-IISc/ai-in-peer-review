- Proposes TBuckets: bucket-based topic coherence using word embeddings with three variants (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).
- Coherence estimated from size/composition of the largest bucket, optionally weighted by word order and eigenvector similarity.
- Evaluated on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics; shows strong correlations with human judgments and improves weakly supervised classification by selecting higher-coherence runs.

- Contribution: Introduces a simple, interpretable bucket-based coherence measure grounded in distributional semantics with a parameter-free SVD and an effective reorganization step.
- Contribution: Empirically shows TBuckets-SVD-Reorg matches or exceeds prior best coherence correlations on two datasets and is competitive on the third, with comparisons to Röder et al.
- Contribution: Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification through coherence-based topic-run selection.

- Strength: Conceptually clear and interpretable—the “largest coherent bucket” aligns with human coherence judgments and the reorg step addresses failure modes.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with minimal tuning; SVD methods are parameter-free and competitive, clustering needs only one threshold.
- Strength: Practical downstream gains shown in weakly supervised classification.
- Strength: Uses solid baselines and standard evaluation (Pearson correlation with human ratings).

- Weakness: Inconsistent performance across datasets and variants—best TBuckets variant varies by dataset and SVD underperforms on Genomics.
- Weakness: Relies on pretrained embeddings with unclear OOV handling; no per-topic coverage statistics reported.
- Weakness: “No parameters” claim is misleading because results depend on upstream embedding choices (model, corpus, dimensionality, normalization).
- Weakness: Related work gap—needs positioning against lexical chaining and other bucketing/grouping methods beyond distributional measures.
- Weakness: Limited robustness analysis—only top-10 words evaluated; sensitivity to word count, alternative topic models, sparsity, and runtime not reported.

- Q: How are OOV topic words handled, what proportion are OOV per dataset, and how does coverage correlate with performance?
- Q: Can one predict which TBuckets variant will work best on a new corpus using diagnostics (e.g., embedding coverage, average similarity, domain match)?
- Q: How sensitive are results to embedding choice (GloVe vs word2vec vs fastText, general vs domain-specific, dimensionality, normalization)? Were domain-specific vectors tested for Genomics?
- Q: How does performance change when using more than top-10 words per topic or with noisier/denser topics?
- Q: How stable is the dynamic reorganization threshold (AWES/AWPS) across datasets and were any failure cases observed?

- Comment: Clarify whether vectors are normalized before computing cosine similarity since pretrained vectors may not be unit length.
- Comment: Improve Figure 1 readability by enlarging or increasing resolution for axes and legends.
- Comment: Rephrase “no parameters” to something like “no method-internal hyperparameters beyond the choice of pretrained embeddings.”
