- Introduces TBuckets: groups top-n topic words into buckets by cosine similarity of word embeddings; a single large coherent bucket indicates higher topic quality.
- Proposes three variants: clustering with a similarity threshold; SVD-based assignment via eigenvectors; SVD-based assignment with a reorganization refinement step.
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification task; performance matches or exceeds Röder et al. (2015), with SVD variants being parameter-free.
- Empirical strength: TBuckets correlates with human topic coherence judgments competitively, with notable gains on NYT and 20NG.
- Practical utility: selecting topic sets by TBuckets coherence improves downstream weakly supervised classification in most settings.
- Simplicity and intuition: bucket-based rationale is straightforward, easy to implement, and SVD variants offer robust, parameter-free defaults.
- Clear evaluation protocol: follows Röder et al. (2015) enabling direct comparison and reproducibility.
- Weakness: conceptual justification is thin; paper doesn’t convincingly address why word–word embedding similarity should capture LDA topic quality given differing context types.
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing for reported Pearson correlations; confidence intervals or formal tests are needed to support superiority claims.
- Weakness: Section 3 is hard to follow; passive voice and unclear design choices obscure methods and reorganization criteria.
- Weakness: space allocation issues—lengthy “trace” example adds limited insight and could be replaced with clearer method exposition.
- Weakness: no analysis of sensitivity to embedding choice (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe, different training corpora or dimensionalities), which may affect results.
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of word embeddings (model, corpus, dimensionality)?
- Question: can the authors report statistical significance (confidence intervals or tests) for differences in Pearson correlations in Table 1?
- Question: how is the clustering similarity threshold chosen in practice, and is performance stable across reasonable threshold ranges?
- Question: can the authors provide intuition for the SVD+Reorg dynamic threshold and an ablation showing the reorganization step’s impact?
- Overall: method is simple and somewhat ad hoc but empirically strong; paper would benefit from clearer exposition, significance testing, and embedding sensitivity analysis.
