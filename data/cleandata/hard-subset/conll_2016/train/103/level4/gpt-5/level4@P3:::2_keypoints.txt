- Proposal: TBuckets — group a topic's top-N words into buckets using embedding similarity; coherence judged by dominance of the largest bucket.
- Variants: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative with distance threshold), TBuckets-SVD (first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers; parameter-free), TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (heuristic moves words into/out of largest bucket with dynamic threshold).
- Scoring: Use largest-bucket properties (size, LDA word-order weights, eigenvector similarity) with SVD methods weighted by top singular value.
- Empirical result: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves strong Pearson correlations with human judgments, outperforming Röder et al. on NYT and 20NG and competitive on Genomics.
- Application: Using TBuckets to select topic-model runs improves weakly supervised classification macro-F1 on several 20NG tasks.
- Contribution 1: A simple, interpretable, mostly parameter-free coherence family with strong empirical performance on two datasets.
- Contribution 2: A reorganization heuristic that improves SVD-based bucketing without manual tuning.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrated downstream utility for coherence-driven model selection.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance with low complexity and minimal tuning (notably high Pearson r on NYT and 20NG).
- Strength: Intuitive, interpretable output (largest-bucket notion aligns with human sense of coherence).
- Strength: Demonstrated practical downstream gains in weakly supervised classification.
- Strength: Systematic ablations of scoring choices provide insight into component contributions.
- Strength: Reproducibility potential using public datasets and off-the-shelf embeddings (GloVe 300D).
- Weakness: Limited theoretical justification for using distributional embeddings as a proxy for document-level topic coherence.
- Weakness: No analysis of sensitivity to embedding type, dimensionality, domain mismatch, or OOV handling; possible domain degradation (Genomics).
- Weakness: Lack of statistical rigor — no confidence intervals, significance tests, or tests for dependent correlation differences given small sample size.
- Weakness: Method description and organization are unclear in places; key design choices and thresholds need clearer algorithmic presentation.
- Weakness: Metric bias toward single-theme topics and fixed N=10; no robustness analysis to multi-thematic topics or different N values.
- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding type, dimensionality, training corpus, and OOV handling (e.g., word2vec, fastText, domain-specific embeddings)?
- Question: Are improvements over Röder et al. statistically significant — please provide confidence intervals and appropriate tests (Fisher r-to-z, Williams test, bootstrap)?
- Question: For TBuckets-SVD, why use the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers and not select by singular values or fewer vectors; what is computational cost per topic?
- Question: Clarify derivation and intuition of the reorganization threshold AWES − (AWPS/AWES); how often does reorganization add vs evict words and what is its ablation effect?
- Question: How robust are results to choice of N (e.g., 10 vs 20) and have you considered metrics capturing multiple sub-themes (e.g., entropy across buckets) to avoid penalizing valid multi-theme topics?
