- Proposes a new topic-quality metric that clusters each topic’s top-n words into “buckets” using cosine similarity over pre-trained word embeddings.  
- Implements a simple sequential assignment variant plus two advanced variants using SVD-derived eigenvectors and a reorganization step.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task.  
- Empirical results match or surpass the state of the art (Röder et al., 2015).  
- Core idea is simple and somewhat ad hoc, with no fresh theoretical justification for using word–word similarity to measure topic quality.  
- Questions the validity of linking topic coherence (document-level) to embedding similarity (sequential co-occurrence/contextual assumptions).  
- Requests significance testing for reported differences (e.g., Table 1).  
- Readable overall but Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive constructions; recommends clearer, method-by-method subsections and trimming uninformative procedural traces.  
- Raises concern about sensitivity to choice of embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec).
