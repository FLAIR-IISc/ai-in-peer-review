- Proposes TBuckets: group top-n topic words into semantically consistent “buckets” via word embeddings; coherence = dominance of a single bucket.  
- Implements three variants: agglomerative clustering with distance threshold, SVD-based assignment to orthogonal directions, and SVD with a reorganization step.  
- Scores coherence using largest-bucket properties (size, word-order weights, and eigenvector similarity in SVD variants).  
- Evaluates by correlating TBuckets with human coherence judgments on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics datasets.  
- Applies TBuckets to select topic runs for weakly supervised classification, improving macro-F1 in most cases.  
- Main contribution: simple embedding-based bucketization framework with two parameter-free SVD variants and a clustering baseline.  
- Empirical claim: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms or matches best configurations from Röder et al. (2015) on two of three datasets.  
- Practical contribution: demonstrates downstream utility by improving weakly supervised classification via coherence-based run selection.  
- Strength: SVD-based methods are parameter-free and easy to implement, aiding reproducibility and deployment.  
- Strength: Empirical gains on standard benchmarks (NYT, 20NG) support the single-dominant-bucket intuition.  
- Strength: Uses off-the-shelf GloVe embeddings, avoiding extra corpus alignment or external statistics.  
- Strength: Downstream application shows the measure is useful beyond correlation with human judgments.  
- Weakness: Limited conceptual/theoretical justification linking embedding proximity to human topic interpretability.  
- Weakness: Several design choices (reorganization rules, thresholds) feel ad hoc and lack ablation or sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: Section 3 is hard to follow; presentation clarity and organization need improvement.  
- Weakness: Missing statistical significance tests and robustness checks for reported correlation gains.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to Pearson correlation, top-10 words, and three datasets—lacks rank-based metrics and scaling analyses.  
- Weakness: Clustering variant depends on threshold/linkage without principled parameterization or sensitivity discussion.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to choice of embeddings (word2vec, fastText, domain-specific for Genomics)?  
- Question: Are improvements statistically significant vs. Röder et al. (2015)? Please report tests and CIs.  
- Question: Can you provide ablations of SVD-Reorg (e.g., into-only vs from-only moves) to identify drivers of gains?  
- Question: How does performance vary with number of top words per topic (e.g., 5, 15, 20)?  
- Question: What is the computational cost per variant, and how does it scale with topic size and embedding dimension?  
- Question: For clustering, can Max Dist and linkage be motivated or automated; would density-based methods help?  
- Question: Were rank-based correlations (Spearman/Kendall) evaluated; if not, please report them.  
- Question: Is code and a reproducible pipeline available, including preprocessing and exact GloVe vectors used?
