- Proposes embedding-based topic quality evaluation (direct similarity or via matrix factorisation) and reports impressive results on standard datasets.  
- Main concern: inconsistent performance across datasets/variants — best methods vary and several variants underperform prior state of the art, so no consistent overall winner.  
- SVD-based variants perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset, raising robustness concerns for practitioners.  
- Suspect poor SVD results are linked to high proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms in genomics; authors should investigate this connection.  
- Suggestion: authors could predict/select the best variant automatically using corpus–embedding vocabulary overlap (e.g., with GloVe), but this is not discussed.  
- Method is similar to lexical chaining literature; reviewers request citations and clearer positioning relative to that work.  
- Claim of “no parameters” is misleading because pretrained word embeddings introduce hyperparameters and design choices that affect results and should be acknowledged.  
- Need clarity on OOV handling (e.g., are OOV words ignored?) and analysis of its impact on metric stability and accuracy.  
- Low-level: clarify whether and how vectors are normalised before computing cosine similarity (word2vec vectors are not unit-length by default).  
- Low-level: Figure 1 graphs are too small and should be enlarged/reformatted for readability.
