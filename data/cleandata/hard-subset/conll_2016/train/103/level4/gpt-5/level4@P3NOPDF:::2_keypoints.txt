- Paper proposes an embedding-based intrinsic topic evaluation that clusters top-n topic words into buckets by cosine similarity, with greedy, eigenvector, and post-hoc reorganization variants.
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, reported to match or outperform Röder et al. (2015).

Strengths:
- Method is simple, easy to implement, and computationally light, relying on pre-trained embeddings.
- Empirical results are promising and competitive with a strong coherence baseline across datasets and a downstream task.
- Paper is generally clear, well-structured, and addresses scalable automatic topic evaluation.

Conceptual and methodological concerns:
- Limited conceptual justification for using local-context-trained embeddings as a proxy for document-level topic coherence.
- Approach feels ad hoc: bucket formation, thresholding, and variant choices lack theoretical motivation.
- Statistical rigor is insufficient: no significance testing reported to support superiority claims.
- Sensitivity to embedding choice (model, corpus, dimensionality, preprocessing) is not explored.

Clarity and presentation issues:
- Section 3 is hard to follow and uses passive voice that obscures design choices.
- Recommend reorganizing Section 3 with separate subsections, algorithmic steps, hyperparameters, complexity, and pseudocode.
- The illustrative “trace” is uninformative and could be moved to the appendix to free space for method detail.
- Minor language issues; needs careful proofreading.

Requests for clarification and additional analyses:
- Thresholding: specify how thresholds are chosen (global/per-topic/per-dataset), tuning procedure, range, and sensitivity.
- Aggregation: clarify which similarity aggregator (min/mean/median) is used for bucket membership and sensitivity to that choice.
- Order sensitivity: state whether the greedy algorithm is order-sensitive, mitigation strategies, and variance across orderings.
- Ablations: report comparisons isolating greedy, eigenvector, and reorganization components and their individual contributions.
- Variability: provide per-dataset breakdowns and standard deviations across runs.
- Embedding dependence: test different embeddings (word2vec, fastText, GloVe variants), dimensionalities, training corpora, and discuss domain mismatch.
- Embedding handling: state whether embeddings are fine-tuned or fixed, and how OOV/rare words are treated and their impact.
- Scope/sensitivity: analyze effects of top-n choice, number of topics, whether bucket counts/coherence vary monotonically with n.
- Scalability: report runtime and scalability for large topic sets and vocabularies.
- Evaluation grounding: correlate the metric with human coherence judgments or run a small human study to validate metric quality.
- Statistical reporting: include confidence intervals and appropriate significance tests for Table 1 and downstream results.

Overall assessment:
- Practical and effective embedding-based evaluation with encouraging empirical results, but needs stronger algorithm exposition, statistical testing, embedding and threshold sensitivity analyses, and presentation improvements.
