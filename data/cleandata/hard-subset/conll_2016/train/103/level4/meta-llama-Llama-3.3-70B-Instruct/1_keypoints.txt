- Proposes a novel method for assessing topic quality using word embeddings (direct similarity or matrix factorization) with strong results on benchmarks.
- Represents a logical and meaningful advancement in topic evaluation research.
- Results are inconsistent across datasets: no single proposed method consistently beats prior state of the art.
- SVD-based methods perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Inconsistency limits practical applicability to arbitrary datasets.
- Poor SVD performance may stem from high OOV term rates; paper does not explore predicting best method via vocabulary match with GloVe.
- Overlooks related work in the lexical chaining literature and should investigate/incorporate it.
- Claim of being parameter-free is misleading because underlying word-embedding methods have many implicit parameters.
- Fails to explain handling of OOV terms (e.g., in genomics) and the impact of ignoring them on performance.
- Section 2.1 incorrectly assumes vector length is irrelevant for cosine similarity given non-unit-length pre-trained vectors.
- Figure 1 graphs are too small and illegible, hindering result interpretation.
