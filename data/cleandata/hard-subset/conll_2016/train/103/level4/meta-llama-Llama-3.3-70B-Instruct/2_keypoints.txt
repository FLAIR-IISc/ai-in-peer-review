- Proposes evaluating topic models by clustering top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with a simple incremental method plus eigenvector and reorganization variants.  
- Assessed on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task, showing performance on par with or better than Röder et al. (2015).  
- Core idea is straightforward but somewhat ad hoc; the paper lacks novel justification for using word-word embedding similarity as a measure of topic quality.  
- Raises concern about mismatched notions of context (document-level topics vs. sequential-context embeddings), making the approach’s validity unclear.  
- Statistical significance tests (e.g., for Table 1) are missing and should be reported.  
- Paper is generally well-written but Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design choices; each method should be explained in its own subsection.  
- The manuscript underuses page allowance and should condense the uninformative "trace" on page 3.  
- Further analysis needed on sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe).  
- Notes minor language issues.
