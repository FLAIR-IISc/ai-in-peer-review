- Proposes a new topic-model evaluation metric that clusters a topic's top-n words into semantic "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Introduces three algorithmic variants: a simple sequential thresholding method, an eigenvector-based variant, and a variant with a reorganization step.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text classification task.  
- Reports performance on par with or better than the Röder et al. (2015) coherence metric.  
- Strength: strong empirical results showing the metric is simple yet effective.  
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and uses sound evaluation protocols and relevant downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: conceptual justification is weak—method feels ad hoc and lacks theory linking embedding-based word similarity to topic quality.  
- Weakness: does not address the mismatch between document-level co-occurrence (topic models) and local co-occurrence used to train embeddings.  
- Weakness: Section 3 is unclear and uses passive voice, obscuring design choices and algorithmic details.  
- Recommendation: reorganize methodology into separate, clearly labeled subsections and move less informative traces to an appendix.  
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing to assess whether improvements over baselines are meaningful.  
- Question for authors: how sensitive is the metric to the choice of embeddings (GloVe was used); what happens with word2vec or FastText?
