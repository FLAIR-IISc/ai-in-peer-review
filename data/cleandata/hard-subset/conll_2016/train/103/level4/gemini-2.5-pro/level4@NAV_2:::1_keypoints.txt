- Introduces TBuckets: groups a topic's top words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained embeddings; coherence = words forming a single large bucket.  
- Proposes three bucketing techniques: agglomerative clustering, SVD-based sub-theme detection, and SVD with reorganization.  
- Evaluated against human judgments on three public datasets and applied to weakly-supervised text classification, showing improved downstream performance when selecting coherent topics.  
- Main contribution: novel, intuitive TBuckets framework that operationalizes thematic consistency via group-based word similarity.  
- SVD-based variants are highlighted as practically parameter-free for the bucketing step.  
- Empirical claim: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art correlation with human judgments on NYT and 20NG.  
- Demonstrates practical utility: using TBuckets to pre-select topics improves final classification accuracy in a weakly-supervised task.  
- Strength: achieves strong, state-of-the-art results on two benchmark datasets with high Pearson correlation to human judgments.  
- Strength: moves beyond pairwise similarity to a holistic group-based assessment of topic coherence.  
- Strength: parameter-free SVD methods reduce need for tuning and ease off-the-shelf application.  
- Weakness: inconsistent performance—SVD methods perform much worse on the Genomics dataset, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis explaining dataset-dependent performance and gives no guidance on choosing the appropriate TBuckets variant.  
- Weakness: "parameter-free" claim is qualified since methods depend on pre-trained embeddings that have many hyperparameters (dimensionality, window size, training corpus).  
- Weakness: missing related-work discussion, notably connections to lexical chaining literature.  
- Question: How are out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms handled with GloVe, and could OOV handling explain the Genomics performance drop?  
- Question: What dataset/corpus characteristics determine which TBuckets variant is best, and can variant selection be predicted automatically (e.g., via vocabulary overlap)?  
- Additional comment: Clarify whether GloVe vectors were normalized prior to using cosine similarity, since vector magnitudes may matter.  
- Additional comment: Figures (Figure 1) are too small and illegible.
