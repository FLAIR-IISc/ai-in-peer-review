- Positive: Introduces a novel, systematic use of word embeddings (direct similarity and matrix factorization variants) for topic coherence evaluation with strong results on standard datasets.  
- Major concern: Results are inconsistent across datasets with no single proposed method reliably outperforming others, undermining off-the-shelf applicability.  
- Major concern example: SVD-based methods perform particularly poorly on the genomics dataset, highlighting variability across domains.  
- Suggested analysis: Investigate whether performance variation correlates with out-of-vocabulary (OOV) rates or vocabulary overlap with the pre-trained GloVe embeddings.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide a method or criteria to automatically select the best variant for a new dataset based on dataset characteristics.  
- Related work: Discuss and compare the approach to lexical chaining literature that uses semantic similarity to identify coherent word chains.  
- Parameter-free claim: Acknowledge that the approach implicitly depends on embedding hyperparameters (dimensionality, window size, training corpus) despite being parameter-free internally.  
- OOV handling: Clarify how OOV terms (e.g., in genomics) are treated and discuss their impact on results.  
- Minor technical: Clarify the cosine similarity description and note that pre-trained vectors may not be unit-normalized by default.  
- Presentation: Increase the size/readability of plots in Figure 1.
