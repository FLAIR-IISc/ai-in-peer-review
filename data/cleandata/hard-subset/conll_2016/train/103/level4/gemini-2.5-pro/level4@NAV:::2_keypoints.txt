- Introduces a novel topic-evaluation method that groups a topic’s top-n words into semantic “buckets” using cosine similarity of word vectors.  
- Basic algorithm iteratively assigns words to existing buckets or creates new ones; two advanced variants use eigenvectors and a reorganization step.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets plus a weakly-supervised classification task, showing comparable or better performance than Röder et al. (2015).  
- Method is simple but heuristic; paper lacks strong theoretical justification for using word-word similarity as a proxy for topic quality.  
- Concern that embeddings and topic models capture different context types (document-level vs local sequential), weakening the theoretical link.  
- Empirical results are compelling but would benefit from statistical significance tests for Table 1.  
- Paper is generally well-written with minor language issues; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and unclear design-choice descriptions—recommend restructuring into dedicated subsections and condensing the illustrative trace.  
- Query about sensitivity to the choice of word embeddings; suggest evaluating performance with word2vec instead of GloVe.
