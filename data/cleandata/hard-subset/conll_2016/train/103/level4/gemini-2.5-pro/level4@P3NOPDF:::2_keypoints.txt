- Proposes a new topic coherence metric that clusters top-N topic words using cosine similarity of pre-trained word embeddings, with three variants (sequential, eigenvector, reorganization).
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised classification task, performing on par or better than the state-of-the-art (Röder et al., 2015).
- Main strength: strong empirical performance and a practical, competitive automatic coherence measure.
- Main weakness: lacks theoretical motivation; unclear why embedding-derived word-word similarity should proxy topic quality given the gap between document-level co-occurrence and local-context embeddings.
- Method description (Section 3) is unclear and uses passive voice; recommend restructuring with separate subsections for each variant and clearer, active descriptions.
- Experimental results lack statistical significance tests; authors should include them to substantiate performance claims.
- Robustness concern: experiments use only GloVe embeddings; need analysis of sensitivity to different pre-trained embeddings (e.g., word2vec CBOW/Skip-gram).
- Overall: promising empirical contribution but needs stronger theoretical grounding, clearer methodology, and more rigorous statistical evaluation.
