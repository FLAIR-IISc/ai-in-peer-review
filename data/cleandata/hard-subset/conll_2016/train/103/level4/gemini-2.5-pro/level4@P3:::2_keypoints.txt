- TBuckets partitions a topic's top words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained word embeddings; a topic is coherent if words cluster into one large bucket.
- Three proposed methods: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD (parameter-free SVD), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD with dynamic reorganization).
- Evaluation: correlate TBuckets scores with human judgments on three standard datasets and apply the metric to weakly supervised text classification.
- Contribution: a novel, effective topic coherence metric that is competitive or state-of-the-art on benchmarks.
- Contribution: SVD-based variants are parameter-free, reducing the need for expensive tuning compared to prior coherence measures.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that selecting topics by TBuckets improves weakly supervised classification performance.
- Strength: strong empirical performance—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves SOTA on NYT and 20NG and is competitive on the third dataset.
- Strength: parameter-free SVD methods increase practicality, robustness, and ease of application for practitioners.
- Strength: the core idea of grouping semantically similar words into buckets is intuitive, simple, and easy to understand.
- Strength: demonstrated downstream utility by pre-selecting higher-quality topic sets for human annotation, improving classification accuracy.
- Weakness: limited theoretical grounding—no strong justification for using local-context word embeddings to measure document-level topic coherence.
- Weakness: methodology section is unclear and densely written; methods should be presented in separate subsections with clearer, less passive descriptions to aid replication.
- Weakness: results in Table 1 lack statistical significance testing, making it unclear whether improvements over baselines are significant.
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of embeddings (only 300-d GloVe used)—have other embeddings or dimensionalities been tested?
- Question: please provide detailed pseudo-code or a step-by-step algorithmic description for TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, and clarify reorganization conditions (LBPW, NBPE) and dynamic threshold calculations.
