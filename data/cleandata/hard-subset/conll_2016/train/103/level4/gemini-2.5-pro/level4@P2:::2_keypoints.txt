- Proposes TBuckets: group top topic words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained embeddings; coherence = most words in one large bucket; three variants (agglomerative, SVD, SVD-Reorg); evaluated vs human judgments and in weakly supervised classification.

- Contribution: introduces parameter-free TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg coherence measures that achieve state-of-the-art correlation with human judgments.

- Contribution: shows practical utility by using TBuckets to select better topic models for annotation, improving weakly supervised text classification.

- Strength: strong empirical performance—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG benchmarks.

- Strength: parameter-free SVD variants avoid manual hyperparameter tuning; dynamic threshold for reorganization reduces need for tuning.

- Strength: intuitive conceptual framework—grouping semantically related topic words into buckets is simple and well illustrated.

- Strength: demonstrated downstream utility—higher TBuckets scores lead to improved weakly supervised classification performance.

- Weakness: methodological description (especially SVD and reorganization steps) is unclear and hard to replicate; needs step-by-step pseudocode.

- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis—paper lacks explanation why embedding-based similarity (local context) aligns with topic coherence (global co-occurrence).

- Weakness: absence of statistical significance testing for claimed improvements over prior work.

- Weakness: insufficient sensitivity analysis—experiments use only 300-d GloVe; robustness to other embeddings, dimensions, or corpora not explored.

- Question: please provide pseudocode and the precise dynamic-threshold formula derived from AWES and AWPS.

- Question: have you performed statistical significance tests for the results in Table 1?

- Question: how sensitive is TBuckets to choice of embeddings (e.g., word2vec, FastText), embedding dimensionality, or training corpus?
