- Introduces TBuckets: groups a topic's top words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained embeddings and assesses coherence by bucket concentration, with agglomerative and two SVD-based variants (one with reorganization).  
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art correlation with human judgments on 20 Newsgroups and NYT datasets.  
- SVD-based variants are presented as parameter-free, offering practical advantages over methods that require tuning.  
- Methods show inconsistent performance: strong on NYT/20NG but much worse on the Genomics dataset, while clustering works best on Genomics.  
- The paper does not explain why different TBuckets variants perform better on different datasets or how to choose a variant a priori.  
- Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words is unclear; high OOV rates (e.g., in Genomics) may explain poor SVD performance but are not reported.  
- Claim of being "parameter-free" is overstated because performance depends on choices of pre-trained embeddings (dimension, window size, corpus), which are implicit hyperparameters.  
- Missing discussion of related work on lexical chaining and prior methods that group semantically related words.  
- Requests clarification on OOV handling, OOV rates per dataset, and the impact of OOVs on coherence scores.  
- Asks for guidance on selecting the best TBuckets variant for a new corpus or predicting the best variant from dataset characteristics.  
- Minor technical note: clarify that the method uses cosine similarity (vector direction) and does not consider vector magnitude.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 plots are too small and should be enlarged for readability.
