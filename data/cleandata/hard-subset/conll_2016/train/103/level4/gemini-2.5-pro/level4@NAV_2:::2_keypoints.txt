- TBuckets groups top topic words into semantic buckets using cosine similarity of pretrained embeddings and scores coherence by how concentrated words are in buckets; proposes agglomerative clustering plus two SVD-based variants (one with reorganization).  
- SVD-based TBuckets variants are parameter-free and achieve state-of-the-art or competitive performance on two of three benchmark datasets.  
- TBuckets scores correlate with human judgments on three datasets.  
- Using TBuckets to select topic sets for annotation improves downstream weakly-supervised text classification accuracy.  
- The core idea—grouping semantically related words to assess topic coherence—is intuitive and appealing.  
- The paper provides limited theoretical justification for using embedding-based semantic similarity as a proxy for topic coherence given different context models (LDA vs. GloVe).  
- Methodological description is unclear, especially for SVD variants and the dynamic threshold/reorganization step; presentation should be restructured and condensed.  
- Results in Table 1 are reported without statistical significance testing, leaving uncertainty about the robustness of improvements.  
- Question: How sensitive is TBuckets to the choice and dimensionality of embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec vs. FastText)?
