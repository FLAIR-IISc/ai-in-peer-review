- Proposes TBuckets: partition top-N topic words into embedding-based "buckets" (agglomerative, SVD, SVD-Reorg) where coherent topics form a single large bucket; shows strong empirical performance and utility in weakly supervised classification.

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art correlation with human judgments on NYT and 20NG, demonstrating strong empirical results.

- Strength: The bucketing idea is simple, intuitive, easy to implement, and practically useful for filtering topic models.

- Strength: SVD-based variants are effectively parameter-free, avoiding the tuning required by many existing coherence measures.

- Weakness: Theoretical justification is limited; the paper does not deeply connect why local-context embedding similarity should proxy global document-level topic coherence.

- Weakness: Method description lacks clarity and structure (Section 3 is hard to follow; passive voice and an overlong manual trace obscure algorithmic details).

- Weakness: Experimental analysis is incomplete—statistical significance of improvements over baselines is not reported.

- Question: How sensitive are TBuckets to the choice of word embeddings (e.g., different models, corpora, or dimensionalities)?
