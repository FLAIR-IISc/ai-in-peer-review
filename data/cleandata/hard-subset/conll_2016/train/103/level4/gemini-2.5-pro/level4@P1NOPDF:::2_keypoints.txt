- Proposes a new topic-model evaluation method that clusters the top-n topic words into "buckets" using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Introduces a simple sequential clustering variant plus two more sophisticated methods using eigenvectors and a reorganization step.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text-classification task.  
- Empirical results match or outperform the current state of the art (Röder et al., 2015).  
- Core idea is conceptually simple but somewhat ad-hoc without strong theoretical justification.  
- No clear argument for why word-to-word similarity (from embeddings) should measure topic quality given differing context definitions of topic models and embeddings.  
- Results need statistical significance tests (e.g., for Table 1) to strengthen claims.  
- Paper is generally well-written but has minor language issues.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow (passive voice, unclear structure); recommend restructuring with separate subsections and clearer descriptions.  
- Suggest condensing the long trace example on page 3 to save space.  
- Raises the question of sensitivity to the choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec).
