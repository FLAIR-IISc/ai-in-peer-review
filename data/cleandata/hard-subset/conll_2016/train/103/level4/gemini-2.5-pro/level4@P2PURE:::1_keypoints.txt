- Proposes evaluating topic quality using word embeddings (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) and reports strong results on standard datasets.  
- Main concern: inconsistent performance across proposed methods — no single method is consistently best, with SVD-based approaches notably underperforming on the genomics dataset.  
- This inconsistency is problematic for practitioners applying the methods to new datasets and needs discussion.  
- Possible cause: high proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms in genomics; authors do not explore automatic method selection based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.  
- Method is similar to lexical chaining literature; the paper should review and discuss these connections.  
- Claiming “no parameters” is misleading because pre-trained word embeddings implicitly inherit many parameters; this should be acknowledged.  
- Clarify how OOV terms are handled (e.g., ignored) and quantify the impact on domain-specific datasets like genomics.  
- Technical detail: cosine similarity assumes unit-length vectors, but standard embeddings (e.g., word2vec) are not guaranteed to have uniform length — clarify this point.  
- Presentation issue: graphs in Figure 1 are too small and hard to read.
