- Proposes evaluating topic models by clustering each topic’s top-n words into buckets using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with a simple sequential algorithm and two variants (eigenvector-based and reorganization).

- Evaluated on three standard datasets plus a weakly supervised text-classification task, achieving results comparable to or better than Röder et al. (2015).

- Method is conceptually straightforward but ad hoc; no theoretical justification is provided for using word–word embedding similarity to measure topic quality.

- Raises a conceptual mismatch: topic models use document-level context while word embeddings capture local sequential context, so the validity of the similarity-based measure is unclear.

- Empirical claims (Table 1) need statistical significance testing to be more convincing.

- Writing is generally clear but contains grammatical errors and awkward phrasing; Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice and should be reorganized into clearer subsections, with the execution trace condensed.

- Open question about sensitivity to choice of embeddings (e.g., how scores would change using word2vec vs. GloVe).
