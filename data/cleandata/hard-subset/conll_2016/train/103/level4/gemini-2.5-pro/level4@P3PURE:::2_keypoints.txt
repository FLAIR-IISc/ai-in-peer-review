- Proposes a new topic-model evaluation method that clusters top-n topic words into buckets using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with simple iterative and two complex (eigenvector, reorganization) variants.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly-supervised text classification task.  
- Empirically matches or outperforms prior state-of-the-art (Röder et al., 2015).  
- Core idea is straightforward but feels ad-hoc and lacks strong theoretical justification linking word-word similarity to topic quality.  
- Conceptual mismatch: topic models use document-level co-occurrence while embeddings use local/sequential context, so it’s unclear why they should align.  
- Results are promising but Table 1 should include statistical significance tests.  
- Paper is generally well written but contains minor grammatical errors.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow due to passive voice; recommend active voice, dedicated subsections per method, and clearer organization.  
- Suggest condensing or removing the uninformative illustrative "trace" on page 3 to free space.  
- Raises question about sensitivity to choice of embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe).
