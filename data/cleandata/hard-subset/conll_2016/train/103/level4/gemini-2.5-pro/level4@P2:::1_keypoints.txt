- Introduces TBuckets: grouping top topic words into semantic “buckets” using embeddings to assess coherence (one large bucket = coherent).
- Presents three bucketing methods (agglomerative clustering, TBuckets-SVD, TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) for evaluating topic quality.
- Shows SVD-based methods achieve strong performance without heavy parameter tuning.
- Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification via TBuckets-based topic selection.
- Strong empirical results: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior work on NYT and 20NG.
- Conceptually simple and interpretable: aligns with human judgment of semantic grouping.
- SVD-based variants are effectively parameter-free for the bucketing step, reducing need for dataset-specific tuning.
- Performance is inconsistent across domains: SVD-Reorg drops substantially on the Genomics dataset while clustering fares better.
- OOV handling is unspecified; missing discussion of OOV rates likely explains poor Genomics performance.
- Fails to cite/relate to prior literature on lexical cohesion and lexical chaining, which is relevant.
- Claim of “parameter-free” is overstated because choice of pre-trained embeddings is an important implicit parameter.
- Requests clarification on OOV handling and OOV rates per dataset and their correlation with performance.
- Asks for hypotheses explaining SVD underperformance on Genomics and guidance for choosing TBuckets variants per domain.
- Notes minor technical clarification: cosine similarity assumes vector length carries no information (normalization issue).
- Recommends higher-resolution figures for readability (Figure 1).
