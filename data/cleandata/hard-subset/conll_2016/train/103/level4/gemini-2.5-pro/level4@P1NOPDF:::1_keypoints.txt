- Proposes evaluating topic quality using word-embedding similarities and SVD, achieving strong results on standard datasets and representing an important advance.
- Main concern: inconsistent results across datasets—no single method consistently outperforms others, and SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Practical drawback: variability means practitioners cannot rely on one method for arbitrary datasets.
- Likely cause: high out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term proportion in the genomics dataset may explain poor SVD performance.
- Missing exploration/guidance: no automatic method-selection strategy (e.g., based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings) is examined.
- Related work omission: method closely resembles lexical chaining literature and should be discussed.
- Claim refinement: calling the approach “parameter-free” is misleading because it inherits many implicit parameters from pre-trained embeddings.
- Missing detail: paper does not state how OOV terms are handled or analyze their impact, especially for domain-specific corpora.
- Minor technical inaccuracy: description of cosine similarity implies vector length is irrelevant, but this assumes unit-length embeddings and should be clarified.
- Presentation issue: graphs in Figure 1 are too small to be easily readable.
