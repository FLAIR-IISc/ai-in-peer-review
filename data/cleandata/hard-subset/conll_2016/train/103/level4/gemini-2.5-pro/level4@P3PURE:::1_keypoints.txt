- Proposes embedding-based topic-quality evaluation (direct similarity or via matrix factorization) with strong results and an important next-step contribution.
- Overall state-of-the-art results reported, but no single proposed method consistently beats existing methods across all datasets.
- SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset, causing inconsistent performance and practical usability concerns.
- Reviewer suspects poor SVD performance is linked to a high proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms in specialized corpora.
- Paper lacks analysis/discussion of OOV impact and could benefit from predicting which method suits a dataset based on vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings.
- Method closely relates to lexical chaining literature; the authors should review and discuss that related work.
- Claim that the method has “no parameters” is misleading because underlying pre-trained embeddings have many implicit hyperparameters—this should be acknowledged.
- The paper does not state how OOV terms are handled (e.g., ignored) and needs analysis of OOV effects on performance.
- Minor: Clarify that cosine similarity ignores vector length only when vectors are unit-normalized; pre-trained word2vec vectors may not be normalized.
- Minor: Figure 1 graphs are too small and unreadable.
