- Introduces TBuckets: groups top topic words into semantic “buckets” using word vectors; coherent topics form one large bucket, incoherent topics fragment into many small buckets; evaluates agglomerative clustering + two SVD-based methods on three datasets.  
- Contribution: Novel framework that detects sub-themes within topics, going beyond pairwise word similarity; SVD-based bucketing is elegant and parameter-light.  
- Empirical result: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art correlation with human judgments on NYT and 20NG.  
- Practical utility: Using TBuckets to select higher-quality topic sets improves weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strength: Methodological novelty and soundness—leverages embeddings holistically and uses SVD to find orthogonal semantic dimensions.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance on general-domain datasets, outperforming a recent strong baseline (Röder et al., 2015).  
- Strength: SVD variants are effectively parameter-free, easing off-the-shelf use.  
- Strength: Downstream task evaluation is well-executed and demonstrates real-world benefit.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent performance and lack of robustness—SVD methods perform poorly on the Genomics dataset while clustering does better.  
- Weakness: No analysis of failure cases—missing investigation of embedding quality, OOV rates, and domain mismatch effects (likely relevant for Genomics).  
- Weakness: Missing connections to related lexical chaining literature; paper should situate TBuckets among prior grouping approaches.  
- Question: Please explain the SVD performance drop on Genomics and report OOV handling and OOV rates for each dataset.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of pre-trained embeddings (model, dimensionality, training corpus); acknowledge this implicit hyperparameter and test domain-specific embeddings.  
- Minor: Clarify the cosine similarity statement—cosine is appropriate for unit-normalized vectors, but not all embedding models produce unit-length vectors.  
- Minor: Figure 1 graphics are too small in the PDF and should be enlarged for clarity.
