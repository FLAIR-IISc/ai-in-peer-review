- Introduces embedding-based topic quality evaluation using direct comparison and matrix factorization, reporting strong benchmark performance.
- Experimental results are inconsistent: no single proposed variant outperforms the state of the art across all datasets.
- The SVD-based approach shows notable performance degradation on the genomics dataset.
- Lack of guidance for practitioners on how to choose among the proposed methods for new datasets.
- Hypothesized cause: higher proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms in genomics; automatic method selection based on vocabulary overlap with embeddings is not explored.
- Recommend reviewing and discussing related work in the lexical chaining literature due to conceptual overlap.
- The “parameter-free” claim is misleading because the method depends on pre-trained embeddings that have many hyperparameters.
- The manuscript must clarify how OOV terms are handled (e.g., ignored) and discuss the impact, especially for specialized domains.
- Minor technical correction: cosine similarity ignores magnitude only for unit-length vectors; many embeddings are not normalized by default.
- Presentation issue: plots in Figure 1 are too small to be legible.
