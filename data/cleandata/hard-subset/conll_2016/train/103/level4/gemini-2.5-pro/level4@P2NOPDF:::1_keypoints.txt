- Presents a topic-quality evaluation method using pre-trained word embeddings and matrix factorization, claiming state-of-the-art results on standard datasets.  
- Results are inconsistent across datasets: no single proposed variant outperforms the baseline on all three testbeds.  
- Different method variants top different datasets; SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset.  
- Practical drawback: unclear which method to choose for a new or arbitrary dataset.  
- Suspected cause: out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms—especially in genomics—may explain SVD’s poor performance; recommend discussing and analyzing OOV effects.  
- Suggest analyzing dataset characteristics (e.g., vocabulary overlap with pre-trained embeddings) to predict the best-performing variant.  
- Recommend situating the work relative to lexical chaining literature, which bears strong resemblance to the proposed method.  
- Note that the method is implicitly parameterized by hyperparameters of the underlying embeddings (e.g., dimensionality, window size) and should acknowledge these.  
- Paper does not specify OOV handling—report OOV rates per dataset and whether OOV terms are ignored.  
- Minor: clarify the assumption about unit-length embedding vectors and cosine similarity since vector magnitude can carry information.  
- Minor: enlarge Figure 1 graphs because current figures are too small to read.
