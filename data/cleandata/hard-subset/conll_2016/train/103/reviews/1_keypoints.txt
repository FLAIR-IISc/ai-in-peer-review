- Proposes embedding-based topic quality evaluation (direct or via matrix factorisation) and achieves strong results on standard datasets.
- Results inconsistent across methods and datasets; no proposed method consistently beats the state of the art.
- SVD-based methods perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset.
- Inconsistency raises practical concerns about applying the method to arbitrary datasets.
- Possible cause: high proportion of OOV terms (e.g., in genomics) affecting performance, but the paper lacks discussion or analysis of this.
- No mechanism or discussion for predicting which method will work best (e.g., based on vocab match with GloVe).
- Overlooks related work in the lexical chaining literature — should be cited and discussed.
- Claim that the method has “no parameters” is misleading because pretrained word embeddings carry many implicit parameters.
- Unclear treatment of OOV terms (are they ignored?) and their impact on evaluation results.
- Incorrect/oversimplified description of word embeddings: assumes vector length is unimportant when vectors may not be unit length; needs clarification about normalization and cosine use.
- Presentation issue: graphs in Figure 1 are too small to be readable.
