- Proposes evaluating topic models by partitioning top-n words into clusters ("buckets") using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Introduces three methods: a simple greedy word-by-word bucket assignment, an eigenvector-based method, and a reorganisation method.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification setting.  
- Empirically outperforms or matches the state of the art (Röder et al., 2015).  
- The idea is simple and somewhat ad hoc, with no new theoretical justification for using word–word similarity to measure topic quality.  
- Raises concern about mismatch between contexts for topics (document-level) and word embeddings (sequential context).  
- Requests significance tests for the reported results (Table 1).  
- Overall clearly written but contains some language issues.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow; passive phrasing obscures design choices.  
- Recommends clearer exposition (one subsection per method) and removal of uninformative content to fill available space.  
- Asks about sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe).
