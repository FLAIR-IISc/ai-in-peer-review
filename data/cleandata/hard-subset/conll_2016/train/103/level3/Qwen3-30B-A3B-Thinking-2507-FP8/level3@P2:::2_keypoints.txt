- Introduces TBuckets: measuring topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings.  
- Proposes three implementations: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD (eigenvector-based), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganized buckets).  
- Shows practical utility: selecting high-quality TBuckets topics improves weakly supervised text classification performance.  
- Strong empirical performance: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG without parameter tuning.  
- Practical application validated: TBuckets-based topic selection yields measurable classification gains (e.g., POLITICS vs SCIENCE).  
- Parameter-free advantage: TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg require no parameter tuning, improving usability.  
- Lacks theoretical justification: no clear rationale why embedding-based word similarity should map to human topic coherence given context mismatch.  
- No statistical validation: reported performance gains (e.g., Pearson r improvements) lack significance testing.  
- Method section is poorly structured and hard to follow; needs clearer, per-method subsections and rationale.  
- Embedding sensitivity not analyzed: only GloVe used, with no comparison to other embedding models like word2vec.  
- Request: provide statistical significance tests for Table 1 results to assess meaningfulness of differences.  
- Request: evaluate sensitivity to different word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs word2vec) to test robustness.  
- Request: offer clearer theoretical justification linking sequentially-trained embeddings to document-level topic coherence.
