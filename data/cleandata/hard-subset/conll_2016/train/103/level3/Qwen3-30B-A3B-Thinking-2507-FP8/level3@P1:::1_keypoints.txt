- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets to measure LDA topic quality.  
- Implements TBuckets in three ways: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with reorganization.  
- Demonstrates practical value by improving weakly supervised text classification via selecting high-quality topics.  

- Strong empirical gain in classification (example: POLITICS vs SCIENCE improvement), showing real-world utility.  
- Bucketing intuition aligns with human topic assessment and is conceptually clear.  
- SVD-based variants offer parameter-free topic quality measurement in practice, avoiding per-method tuning.  

- Performance is inconsistent across datasets: strong on NYT/20NG but much worse on Genomics, raising reliability concerns.  
- Claim of “no parameter tuning” is misleading because TBuckets depends on pretrained GloVe embeddings with many implicit parameters.  
- Missing analysis of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, likely explaining poor Genomics results due to domain-specific vocabulary.  
- Overlooks related lexical chaining literature that also groups words by semantic relations.  
- Incorrect/oversimplified description of word embeddings (e.g., vector length vs. need for normalization for cosine similarity).  
- Figure 1 graphs are too small, hindering interpretation of parameter sensitivity for TBuckets-Clustering.  

- Question: How do you justify “parameter-free” given reliance on pretrained GloVe embeddings and their training choices?  
- Question: What specific Genomics-domain factors caused low performance, and how can TBuckets be adapted for such domains?  
- Question: How to predict which TBuckets variant (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) will work best for a new dataset without exhaustive testing?  
- Question: How were OOV terms handled in experiments—ignored or otherwise—and how did that affect bucketing and evaluation?
