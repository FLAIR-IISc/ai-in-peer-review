

**Review**

**Summary and Contributions**  
The paper proposes "TBuckets," a novel embedding-based approach for topic quality evaluation that groups topic words into thematic buckets. The authors claim three contributions: (1) a novel topic coherence measure via word bucketing, (2) three implementation variants (clustering-based, SVD-based, SVD-reorganized), and (3) demonstrated utility for weakly supervised text classification. However, the strongest contribution is the practical application to text classification, while the core coherence measure lacks consistent superiority over prior work.

**Strengths**  
1. The paper introduces a conceptually intuitive method for topic coherence evaluation, aligning well with human interpretation patterns.  
2. The application to text classification demonstrates tangible utility: filtering high-coherence topics via TBuckets improves classification accuracy by 1-5% on challenging 20NG subsets.  
3. The SVD-based variants are parameter-free (as claimed), which is a notable advantage over state-of-the-art methods requiring parameter tuning.

**Weaknesses**  
1. **Inconsistent performance**: No method consistently outperforms the state-of-the-art across datasets (e.g., SVD-based methods fail on Genomics), undermining practical applicability.  
2. **OOV term oversight**: The paper fails to address the high OOV rate in Genomics (likely causing poor performance), lacks analysis of OOV handling, and provides no guidance for dataset-specific method selection.  
3. **Misleading "no parameters" claim**: The method relies on pretrained embeddings (with implicit parameters), which the authors incorrectly dismiss as parameter-free.  
4. **Technical inaccuracies**: The paper oversimplifies word embedding usage (e.g., ignoring vector normalization requirements for cosine similarity) and omits relevant lexical chaining literature.  
5. **Presentation flaw**: Figure 1 is unreadable due to tiny graphs, hindering evaluation of clustering behavior.

**Questions to Authors**  
1. How would you recommend selecting between TBuckets variants for a new dataset (e.g., based on vocabulary overlap with GloVe)?  
2. What specific OOV handling strategy would you recommend for domains like Genomics?  
3. Can you clarify how vector normalization impacts your cosine similarity calculations?
