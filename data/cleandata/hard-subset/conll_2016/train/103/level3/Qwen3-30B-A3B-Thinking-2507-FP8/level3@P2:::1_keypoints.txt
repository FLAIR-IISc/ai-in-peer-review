- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into semantic buckets where a single large bucket indicates high topic coherence.  
- Implements three bucket-formation techniques using word embeddings: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with reorganization.  
- Claims superior performance on NYT and 20NG and shows TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification; SVD methods asserted as parameter-free.  

- Core intuition (coherent topics form single large buckets) is well-motivated and illustrated with examples.  
- SVD-based techniques are parameter-free in practice, reducing the need for tuning.  
- Practical utility shown: filtering topics with TBuckets yields improved classification accuracy on 3 out of 4 datasets.  

- Inconsistent performance: strong on NYT/20NG but notably worse on Genomics, with no analysis of this discrepancy.  
- Claim of being "completely parameter-free" is misleading because the method depends on pretrained embeddings that have implicit parameters.  
- Related work in lexical chaining (e.g., Hearst 1992; Radev et al.) is omitted, weakening the novelty claim.  
- Handling of OOV terms is unclear and unanalyzed; no statistics on OOV proportions or their effect, critical for Genomics.  
- Incorrect treatment of embeddings: paper ignores need to normalize vectors for cosine similarity and misstates vector-length irrelevance.  

- Clarify the "parameter-free" claim given reliance on pretrained embeddings and acknowledge implicit parameters.  
- Explain how OOV terms are handled, provide OOV proportion for Genomics, and describe their impact on results.  
- Clarify relationship to lexical chaining literature and justify omission from related work.
