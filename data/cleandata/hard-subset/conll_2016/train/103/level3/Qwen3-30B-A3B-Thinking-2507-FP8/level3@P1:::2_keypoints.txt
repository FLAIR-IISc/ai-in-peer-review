- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic buckets with three variants (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg), evaluated on three datasets and applied to weakly supervised text classification.

- Primary contribution: TBuckets framework for measuring topic coherence via bucket formation.

- Secondary contribution: Parameter-free TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperform prior methods on 2/3 datasets without hyperparameter tuning.

- Tertiary contribution: Demonstrates TBuckets’ practical utility by selecting high-quality topics to improve weakly supervised classification.

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG while being parameter-free, outperforming prior results without tuning.

- Strength: Weakly supervised classification experiments show topic selection via TBuckets improves accuracy and highlights large gaps between high- and low-coherence topic sets.

- Strength: Intuitive link between bucket structure and human judgments—single large bucket indicates coherence, multiple medium buckets indicate lower coherence—supported by examples.

- Weakness: No theoretical justification for why embedding-based word grouping should correlate with human topic coherence; method seems ad hoc.

- Weakness: Does not address mismatch between embedding training contexts (sequential corpus context) and document-level/topic context, raising doubts about similarity relevance.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing for results in Table 1, so improvements may not be statistically meaningful.

- Weakness: Exposition in Section 3 is unclear; reorganization procedure (3.1) is convoluted and needs clearer organization and explanation.

- Question: Please provide statistical significance tests for Table 1 to verify reported improvements.

- Question: How sensitive is the approach to the choice of word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec); were different embeddings evaluated?

- Question: Clarify how the dynamic threshold for reorganization (AWES - AWPS/AWES) was derived and why it’s preferable to a fixed threshold.

- Question: Why is TBuckets-SVD-Reorg described as "completely parameter-free" when it uses a topic-dependent dynamic threshold?
