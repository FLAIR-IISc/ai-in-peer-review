- Proposes TBuckets: evaluates topic quality by clustering topic words into thematic buckets using cosine similarity of word embeddings.  
- Introduces three techniques: greedy word-by-word assignment, an eigenvector-based SVD method, and a reorganization refinement.  
- Presents parameter-free variants (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that claim to outperform tuned state-of-the-art and can filter high-quality topics for weakly supervised classification.  
- Empirical results show TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior methods on two of three standard datasets without parameter tuning.  
- Application to weakly supervised text classification demonstrates practical utility, notably on difficult datasets like PC vs MAC.  
- Parameter-free nature is a practical advantage over methods requiring extensive tuning.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why embedding-based (sequential context) word similarity corresponds to document-level topic coherence.  
- Section 3 is poorly structured and uses passive voice, obscuring methodological details and making the algorithm hard to follow.  
- Omits critical analyses: no significance tests reported for Table 1 and no sensitivity analysis across different word embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec).  
- Does not address the fundamental mismatch between embeddings trained on sequential context and topics defined at the document level.  
- Requests significance tests for Table 1 results.  
- Asks how performance changes with different embedding sources (word2vec vs. GloVe).  
- Seeks clarification of the theoretical rationale for using word embeddings to measure topic coherence.
