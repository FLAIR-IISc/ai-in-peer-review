- Proposes TBuckets: an embedding-based topic-quality measure that groups topic words into thematic buckets, claiming three contributions (new coherence measure, three implementation variants, utility for weakly supervised classification).  
- Authors’ strongest contribution is the practical application to weakly supervised text classification; the core coherence measure lacks consistent superiority over prior work.  
- Strength: Conceptually intuitive method that aligns with human interpretation of topics.  
- Strength: Filtering high-coherence topics via TBuckets improves classification accuracy by 1–5% on challenging 20NG subsets.  
- Strength: SVD-based variants are claimed to be parameter-free, offering an advantage over methods needing parameter tuning.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent performance across datasets; no variant consistently outperforms state-of-the-art (e.g., SVD fails on Genomics).  
- Weakness: High OOV rate in Genomics is not addressed; paper lacks OOV analysis and dataset-specific guidance.  
- Weakness: “No parameters” claim is misleading because the method depends on pretrained embeddings with implicit parameters.  
- Weakness: Technical inaccuracies—oversimplifies embedding usage, omits discussion of vector normalization for cosine similarity, and ignores relevant lexical chaining literature.  
- Weakness: Presentation flaw—Figure 1 is unreadable due to tiny graphs, hindering assessment of clustering behavior.  
- Question: How should practitioners select between TBuckets variants for a new dataset (e.g., using vocabulary overlap with GloVe)?  
- Question: What specific OOV handling strategy do the authors recommend for domains like Genomics?  
- Question: Please clarify how vector normalization is handled and how it impacts cosine similarity calculations.
