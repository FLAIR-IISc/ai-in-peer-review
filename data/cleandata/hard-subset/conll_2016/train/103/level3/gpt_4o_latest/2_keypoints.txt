- Introduces TBuckets: clusters top-n topic words into buckets using cosine similarity of word embeddings, with three variants (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).  
- SVD-based TBuckets are parameter-free, simplifying application.  
- Evaluated on 20NG, NYT, Genomics and a weakly supervised classification task, showing competitive or superior coherence and improved classification.  
- Contribution: a novel embedding-and-clustering framework for topic coherence evaluation.  
- Contribution: empirical validation achieving state-of-the-art or matching results across datasets and improving weakly supervised classification.  
- Contribution: conceptual simplicity and interpretability, requiring minimal parameter tuning.  
- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms or matches SOTA on most datasets despite being parameter-free.  
- Strength: Demonstrated practical utility by improving topic selection for weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strength: Method is simple and aligns with human interpretability of topics.  
- Strength: Reorganization step improves coherence of the largest bucket and overall results.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for using word-embedding cosine similarity as a proxy for topic coherence.  
- Weakness: No analysis of sensitivity to different pre-trained embeddings (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec or contextual embeddings).  
- Weakness: Writing and structure need improvement (Section 3 should be reorganized; minor language issues).  
- Weakness: Paper contains unused space that could better explain methods or provide examples.  
- Weakness: Results (Table 1) lack statistical significance testing.  
- Question: How does performance vary with different embeddings, including contextual models like BERT?  
- Question: Can the authors provide theoretical or empirical justification for cosine similarity as a reliable coherence measure?  
- Question: How does the method scale to larger datasets or topics with more than 10 words; are there computational bottlenecks?  
- Conclusion: Promising and practical approach with strong empirical results but limited by lack of theoretical grounding, sensitivity analysis, and clarity.
