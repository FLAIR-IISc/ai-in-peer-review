- Introduces TBuckets, a bucket-based method using word embeddings to evaluate topic coherence.
- Proposes three TBuckets variants: Clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with Reorganization.
- TBuckets achieves state-of-the-art results on 20NG and NYT and competitive performance on Genomics.
- SVD-based TBuckets variants are parameter-free, reducing hyperparameter tuning.
- Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification using high-quality topics.
- TBuckets offers interpretability through intuitive thematic buckets aligning with human judgments.
- Evaluation is comprehensive across multiple datasets with detailed comparisons to prior work.
- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg shows superior performance on two datasets, validating the approach.
- Parameter-free design is a significant advantage over methods needing extensive tuning.
- Method improves classification accuracy in weakly supervised settings by selecting coherent topics.
- SVD-based methods perform poorly on the Genomics dataset, likely due to OOV domain terms.
- Paper lacks discussion on how embedding quality or domain-specific embeddings affect performance.
- Implicit parameters of pre-trained embeddings (dimensionality, corpus) are not acknowledged.
- OOV term handling is unclear and not thoroughly analyzed, impacting domain-specific results.
- Assumes word vectors are unit-length for cosine similarity without guaranteeing normalization.
- Figure 1 visualizations are too small and hard to interpret, reducing clarity of results.
- Asks authors to clarify embedding quality influence, OOV handling, vector normalization, and links to lexical chaining literature.
- Recommends addressing generalizability, method-selection guidance, better visualizations, and transparency about embedding choices.
