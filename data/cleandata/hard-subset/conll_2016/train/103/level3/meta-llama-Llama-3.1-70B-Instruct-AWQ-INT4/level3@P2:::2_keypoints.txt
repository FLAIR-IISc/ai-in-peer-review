- Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings and evaluating clustering-, SVD-, and SVD+reorg-based bucket creation, showing improved performance on three datasets and for weakly supervised classification.
- Contribution: novel embedding-and-bucketing-based measure for topic quality.
- Contribution: evaluation of three bucket-creation techniques including a parameter-free SVD method.
- Contribution: empirical demonstration on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text-classification setting.
- Strength: simple, intuitive, and easy-to-implement approach to measuring topic quality.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on three standard datasets with comparisons to state of the art.
- Strength: demonstrated utility for weakly supervised text classification.
- Strength: clear and detailed description of methods and techniques.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for using word-word similarity to measure topic quality.
- Weakness: potential mismatch between document-level topic contexts and sequential contexts of word embeddings is not addressed.
- Weakness: no significance tests reported for results in Table 1, hindering assessment of statistical significance.
- Weakness: some language issues and passive phrasing obscure design choices.
- Weakness: Section 3 is hard to follow and contains uninformative content that should be clarified or removed.
- Question: provide theoretical justification for using word-word similarity to assess topic quality.
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe)?
- Question: can you provide significance tests for the reported results in Table 1?
- Question: how will you address the context mismatch between topics and word embeddings in future work?
- Additional comment: provide more detail on design choices and trade-offs and improve exposition to make the paper more concise and clearer.
