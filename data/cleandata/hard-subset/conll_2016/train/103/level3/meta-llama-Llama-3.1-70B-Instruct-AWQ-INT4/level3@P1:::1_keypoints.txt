- Proposes TBuckets: group topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings to measure LDA topic quality.
- Explores three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
- Evaluates methods on three public datasets and reports effectiveness for measuring topic coherence.
- Demonstrates TBuckets’ utility in weakly supervised text classification by selecting high-quality topics for annotation.
- Main contribution: a novel embedding-and-bucketing approach for topic quality evaluation with strong results on standard datasets.
- SVD-based methods with reorganization outperform prior work on NYT and 20NG datasets.
- Shows a practical application that can improve weakly supervised classification performance.
- Strength: leverages word embeddings to capture semantic relationships for coherence evaluation.
- Strength: SVD+reorganization yields promising improvements on certain datasets.
- Strength: offers a useful, application-oriented method for topic selection in annotation workflows.
- Weakness: inconsistent results across methods and datasets, raising robustness and generalizability concerns.
- Weakness: lacks analysis of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms and their impact on performance.
- Weakness: claiming SVD-based methods are parameter-free is misleading because pretrained embeddings contain implicit parameters.
- Weakness: unclear and insufficient explanation of how OOV terms are handled in evaluation.
- Weakness: presentation issues—figures (Figure 1) are too small and hard to read.
- Questions: How will authors address inconsistency across methods and datasets?
- Questions: Can authors provide analysis on the impact of OOV terms?
- Questions: How are OOV terms handled, and what are the implications of ignoring them?
- Questions: Please clarify embedding normalization and cosine similarity choices and their effects.
- Questions: How can the approach be extended to short texts or vocabularies with limited coverage?
