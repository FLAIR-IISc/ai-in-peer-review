**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach called TBuckets for measuring topic quality in Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. TBuckets groups topic words into thematic groups (buckets) using word embeddings and employs three techniques for creating buckets: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD)-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. The approach is evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification setting, outperforming or matching the state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to topic quality measurement**: TBuckets provides a new perspective on measuring topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic groups (buckets) using word embeddings.
2. **Three techniques for creating buckets**: The paper proposes three techniques for creating buckets: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
3. **Evaluation on standard datasets and text classification setting**: The approach is evaluated on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification setting, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Strengths**

1. **Simple and intuitive idea**: The idea of grouping topic words into thematic groups (buckets) is simple and intuitive, making it easy to understand and implement.
2. **Effective evaluation**: The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the approach on standard datasets and a text classification setting, demonstrating its effectiveness.
3. **Outperforms or matches state-of-the-art results**: The approach outperforms or matches the state-of-the-art results in topic quality measurement, demonstrating its competitiveness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of theoretical justification**: The paper does not provide a theoretical justification for using word-word similarity to measure topic quality.
2. **Mismatch between contexts**: There is a potential mismatch between the context of topics (document-level) and word embeddings (sequential context).
3. **Limited analysis of parameters**: The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the parameters used in the techniques, such as the maximum distance threshold in clustering-based technique.
4. **Language issues**: The paper contains some language issues, making it difficult to follow in some sections.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a theoretical justification for using word-word similarity to measure topic quality?
2. How do you plan to address the potential mismatch between the context of topics and word embeddings?
3. Can you provide a more thorough analysis of the parameters used in the techniques?
4. How do you plan to improve the language and clarity of the paper?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a novel approach to topic quality measurement, and the evaluation results are promising. However, the paper can be improved by addressing the weaknesses mentioned above. Additionally, the authors may want to consider providing more details on the implementation of the techniques and the computational complexity of the approach.
