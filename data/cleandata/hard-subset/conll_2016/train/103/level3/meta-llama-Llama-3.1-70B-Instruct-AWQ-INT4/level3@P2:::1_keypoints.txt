- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, which groups topic words into thematic buckets (via clustering, SVD, SVD+reorg) to measure LDA topic quality; evaluated on three datasets and used for weakly supervised classification, outperforming SOTA on two datasets.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel topic-quality evaluation method combining word embeddings and matrix factorization.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates improved performance over prior work on NYT and 20NG datasets.

- Contribution 3: Shows TBuckets' utility for weakly supervised text classification where high-quality topics matter.

- Strength 1: Achieves state-of-the-art results on two datasets, indicating effectiveness.

- Strength 2: Novel methodological direction by leveraging embeddings and factorization for topic evaluation.

- Strength 3: Practical demonstration of applicability to a real-world downstream task (classification).

- Weakness 1: Results are inconsistent across methods and datasets; no single method dominates everywhere.

- Weakness 2: SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset, questioning generality.

- Weakness 3: Paper lacks discussion or analysis of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms and their impact.

- Weakness 4: Claim of being "parameter-free" is misleading because pretrained embeddings encode many implicit parameters.

- Weakness 5: Unclear explanation of how OOV terms are handled in experiments.

- Question 1: How will the authors address inconsistent results across methods and datasets?

- Question 2: What is the impact of OOV terms on performance and how will they be handled?

- Question 3: Please provide details on preprocessing of embeddings, including normalization and cosine-similarity computation.

- Question 4: How will you predict which TBuckets method will work best for a given dataset, and which dataset features inform that choice?
