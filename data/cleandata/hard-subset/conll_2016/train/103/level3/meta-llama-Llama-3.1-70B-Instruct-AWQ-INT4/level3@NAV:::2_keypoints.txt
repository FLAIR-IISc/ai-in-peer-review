- Summary: TBuckets groups topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings with three methods (clustering, SVD, SVD+reorg) and matches/outperforms state-of-the-art on three datasets and a weakly supervised classification task.

- Contribution 1: Introduces TBuckets, a novel topic-quality measure based on grouping topic words into thematic buckets via embeddings.

- Contribution 2: Proposes three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.

- Contribution 3: Evaluates the approach on three standard datasets and a weakly supervised text classification setting, showing strong empirical performance.

- Strength 1: Simple and intuitive idea that is easy to understand and implement.

- Strength 2: Thorough and effective empirical evaluation across standard datasets and a classification task.

- Strength 3: Empirical results that outperform or match existing state-of-the-art methods.

- Weakness 1: Lacks theoretical justification for using word–word similarity as a topic-quality measure.

- Weakness 2: Potential mismatch between document-level topic context and word embeddings’ sequential context.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis of key parameters (e.g., maximum distance threshold in clustering).

- Weakness 4: Language and clarity issues in parts of the paper.

- Question 1: Can the authors provide a theoretical justification for using word–word similarity to measure topic quality?

- Question 2: How will the authors address the context mismatch between topics and word embeddings?

- Question 3: Can the authors supply a more thorough analysis of the methods’ parameters?

- Question 4: How do the authors plan to improve the paper’s language and clarity?

- Additional comment: Paper is promising but should add implementation details, computational complexity, and address the listed weaknesses.
