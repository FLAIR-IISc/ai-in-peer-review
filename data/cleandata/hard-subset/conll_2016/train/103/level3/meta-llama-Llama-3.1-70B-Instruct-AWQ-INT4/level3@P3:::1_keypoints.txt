- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings and matrix factorization; evaluates three bucket techniques (clustering, SVD, SVD+reorg) on three public datasets.  
- Reports strong results on standard datasets and occasional outperformance of state-of-the-art, but no method consistently beats SOTA.  
- SVD-based methods perform notably poorly on the genomics dataset, raising concerns about generalizability to arbitrary domains.  
- Results are inconsistent across methods and datasets, indicating unstable performance.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms and their impact; treatment of OOVs in evaluation is unclear.  
- Does not provide a mechanism or criteria (e.g., vocabulary match with pre-trained embeddings) to predict which method will work best for a given dataset.  
- The claim of “no parameters” is misleading because pre-trained word embeddings carry many implicit parameters.  
- Overlooks related work in the lexical chaining literature and fails to cite/discuss relevant prior work.  
- Description of word embeddings is oversimplified; unclear whether vectors are normalized and how cosine similarity is used—vector length may matter.  
- Presentation issues: figures (e.g., Figure 1) are too small and hard to read.  
- Contribution: introduces a novel embedding- and matrix-factorization-based approach for measuring topic quality.  
- Contribution: presents and evaluates three techniques for creating word buckets across three public datasets.  
- Contribution: demonstrates potential usefulness of the method for weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strength: novel approach addressing topic quality, an important task in topic modeling.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation on multiple public datasets.  
- Strength: shows promise for weakly supervised classification tasks.  
- Weakness: inconsistent empirical performance and no consistent SOTA beating method.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of OOV effects and unclear OOV handling.  
- Weakness: no guidance for selecting the best method per dataset.  
- Weakness: misses relevant lexical chaining literature.  
- Weakness: oversimplified treatment of embeddings and normalization questions.  
- Question: How will you address inconsistent results across methods and datasets?  
- Question: Can you provide detailed analysis of OOV impact on performance?  
- Question: How will you predict which method will work best for a given dataset?  
- Question: Please discuss and relate your method to the lexical chaining literature.  
- Question: How will you clarify/treat OOV terms and provide an accurate description of embedding normalization and cosine usage?
