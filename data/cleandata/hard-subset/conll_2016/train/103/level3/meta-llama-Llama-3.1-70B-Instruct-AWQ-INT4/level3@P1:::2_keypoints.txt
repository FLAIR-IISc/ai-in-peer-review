- Proposes TBuckets, a method that measures LDA topic quality by clustering top-n topic words into thematic “buckets” using word embeddings.
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: greedy word-by-word assignment, an eigenvector-based method, and a reorganization method.
- Evaluates TBuckets on three standard datasets and in a weakly supervised text classification task, showing effectiveness in measuring topic quality.
- Claims a novel evaluation approach that partitions topic words using cosine similarity of word embeddings.
- Presents three concrete bucket-creation algorithms (greedy, eigenvector, reorganization).
- Provides empirical comparisons against a state-of-the-art baseline (Röder et al., 2015).
- Demonstrates applicability of the method to weakly supervised text classification.
- Method is simple, easy to implement, and empirically effective.
- Evaluation is robust across multiple datasets and a real-world application.
- Comparison to prior work shows competitive performance.
- Lacks theoretical justification for using word-word similarity as a topic-quality measure.
- Raises concerns about mismatch between document-level topic learning and sequential-context word embeddings.
- Contains language issues that sometimes hinder clarity.
- Section 3’s exposition and organization are unclear and hard to follow.
- Requests significance tests for reported results to confirm statistical differences.
- Asks about sensitivity to choice of word embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs GloVe).
- Seeks more details on design choices behind the three bucket methods and the use of cosine similarity.
- Asks how the authors will address the context mismatch between topic learning and embedding training.
