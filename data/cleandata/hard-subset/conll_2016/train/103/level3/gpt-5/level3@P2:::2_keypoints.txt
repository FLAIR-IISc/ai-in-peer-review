- Proposes TBuckets: group a topic’s top-n words into embedding-based “buckets” and score coherence by dominance of the largest bucket.  
- Introduces three instantiations: agglomerative clustering with a distance threshold, SVD-based buckets from AᵀA eigenvectors, and SVD plus a reorganization step moving words by similarity.  
- Coherence score uses size/weighted size of the largest bucket, optionally weighted by eigenvector similarity and top singular value.  
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human judgments on 20NG, NYT, Genomics (top-10 words) and in a weakly supervised text-classification run-selection study.  
- Empirical result: TBuckets matches or beats Röder et al. (2015) on two of three datasets and yields modest classification gains.  

- Contribution: simple, embedding-based bucketing coherence framework with SVD and reorganization variants that are largely parameter-free.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence of competitive correlation with human judgments and utility for selecting annotation runs in weak supervision.  
- Contribution: offers an interpretable operationalization of “single-theme dominance” via largest-bucket properties.  

- Strength: Competitive state-of-the-art correlations with minimal tuning, useful as a robust default.  
- Strength: Conceptual simplicity and interpretability—buckets mirror human topic assessment and aid error analysis.  
- Strength: Sensible use of distributional semantics; SVD provides orthogonal sub-theme directions.  
- Strength: Demonstrates practical utility beyond intrinsic evaluation (improves run selection under fixed annotation budgets).  
- Strength: Clear empirical setup aligned with prior work (datasets, top-10 words, Pearson r).  

- Weakness: Limited theoretical grounding; scoring only the largest bucket and weighting by top singular value is ad hoc and may penalize valid multi-faceted topics.  
- Weakness: Domain sensitivity and embedding–topic context mismatch; global GloVe may not suit domain-specific topics (weaker Genomics performance).  
- Weakness: Missing statistical significance tests, confidence intervals, and effect-size reporting for reported improvements.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility ambiguities—SVD dimensionality/selection, unclear reorganization threshold formula, OOV handling, similarity normalization, and runtime details.  
- Weakness: Exposition issues in Section 3—passive phrasing, mixed narrative; needs clearer per-method descriptions and pseudo-code.  

- Ask: How are OOV topic words handled and what fraction are OOV per dataset?  
- Ask: How sensitive are results to different embeddings (word2vec, fastText, in-domain embeddings)?  
- Ask: SVD details—is the number of eigenvectors min(n,d), how are directions chosen, and how is word–eigenvector similarity computed/normalized?  
- Ask: Clarify the exact reorganization threshold formula and motivation; provide ablations for into-vs-from moves.  
- Ask: Report Spearman in addition to Pearson, statistical tests for correlation differences, and significance/CIs for macro-F1 in classification.  
- Ask: Vary top-n words (10/20/30) to test robustness; report sensitivity curves for clustering Max Dist and protocol for choosing it.  

- Suggest scoring variants combining largest-bucket size with dispersion measures (entropy/Gini) to avoid penalizing legitimate multi-theme topics.  
- Minor fixes: correct reorganization threshold typesetting, formalize “native bucket pull” terminology, fix mislabeled example states.  
- Reproducibility request: include code, embedding versions, preprocessing, and exact hyperparameters.
