- Proposal: TBuckets groups top-N topic words into semantic “buckets” via embeddings; coherence is higher when most words fall in one large bucket.
- Methods: Three realizations — agglomerative clustering over embeddings; SVD-based assignment to eigenvector directions; SVD + reorganization moving words based on similarity and a dynamic threshold.
- Evaluation: Measured Pearson correlation with human judgments on NYT, 20NG, Genomics; SVD variants largely parameter-free; showed downstream benefit in weakly supervised classification.

- Contribution 1: Embedding-based, bucket-size-oriented coherence metric with an SVD+reorg variant achieving state-of-the-art correlations on 2/3 datasets and being largely parameter-free.
- Contribution 2: Systematic empirical evaluation across three datasets, comparing scoring variants (size, WL, EVS) and prior work (Röder et al., 2015).
- Contribution 3: Practical application showing that selecting higher-coherence topic runs improves weakly supervised classification accuracy under the same annotation budget.

- Strength: Empirical effectiveness and simplicity — TBuckets-SVD-Reorg matches/exceeds best reported correlations on NYT and 20NG and is easy to implement and interpret.
- Strength: Parameter-light design — SVD variants require no tuned hyperparameters, aiding reproducibility and reducing engineering overhead.
- Strength: Practical utility — selecting more coherent topics improves macro-F1 in most classification tasks, with larger gains on harder splits.
- Strength: Sensible ablations — reporting multiple scoring variants clarifies which components drive performance and shows simple size signals are powerful when guided by SVD.

- Weakness: Limited novelty and ad hoc choices — bucketization, eigenvector-as-bucket IDs, and threshold formulas are heuristic with little theoretical justification.
- Weakness: Missing statistical testing and uncertainty — no significance tests, confidence intervals, or effect-size analyses for correlations or classification outcomes.
- Weakness: Embedding mismatch and sensitivity — reliance on pretrained GloVe may mismatch topic co-association; sensitivity to embedding choice, dimensionality, and OOV handling is unexplored.
- Weakness: Potential metric bias — metric may over-reward single-theme topics and penalize valid multi-faceted topics; failure modes and human agreement on such topics are unexamined.
- Weakness: Evaluation scope and fairness — classification study may cherry-pick runs and does not control compute budget or compare to simple embedding baselines and additional NPMI variants.

- Q: Are improvements over Röder et al. (2015) statistically significant (e.g., permutation tests) and can you provide CIs for Pearson r and macro-F1?
- Q: How do results vary with different embeddings (word2vec, fastText, domain-specific), dimensionalities, and OOV strategies, especially for Genomics?
- Q: Can you justify the reorganization threshold formula AWES − (AWPS/AWES) and report sensitivity to alternative/simpler thresholds?
- Q: Why use the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers; would using top-k (k < n) directions reduce noise or relate to rank(A)?
- Q: How does TBuckets handle intentionally bimodal or multi-theme topics, and do human raters deem such topics coherent?
- Q: Will you release code and exact preprocessing details (tokenization, stopwords, OOV, cosine settings, clustering thresholds) for reproducibility?
- Q: Can you provide runtime/compute cost comparisons for the selection experiment to address potential compute-budget bias?
