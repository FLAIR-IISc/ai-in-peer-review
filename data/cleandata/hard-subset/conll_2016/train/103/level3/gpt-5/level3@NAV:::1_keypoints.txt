- Proposes TBuckets: an embedding-based framework grouping top words into “buckets” with three variants (agglomerative, SVD, SVD-Reorg) linking largest-bucket properties to topic coherence.  
- Empirical evaluation on three datasets shows SVD-Reorg is competitive on NYT and 20NG; also demonstrates selecting high-quality topics improves weakly supervised classification.  
- Strength: conceptually simple and practical—bucket size/weights proxy coherence and pretrained embeddings lower engineering cost.  
- Strength: competitive intrinsic results (SVD-Reorg r≈0.869 on 20NG, ≈0.837 on NYT), indicating meaningful semantic cohesion capture.  
- Strength: clear downstream utility—TBuckets-based topic selection improves macro-F1 in 3/4 20NG binary tasks.  
- Strength: methodological diversity—offers clustering and SVD-based options to trade interpretability and parameterization.  
- Weakness: inconsistent performance across datasets/methods; SVD variants perform poorly on Genomics while clustering does better.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of OOV and domain mismatch—OOV rates and handling not quantified or examined.  
- Weakness: no guidance for choosing between clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg (no predictive diagnostics).  
- Weakness: “no parameters” claim is misleading—results depend on embedding choice/training and SVD-Reorg uses dynamic thresholding.  
- Weakness: technical clarity issues—unclear if embeddings are normalized for cosine, eigenvector-as-bucket explanation lacks justification, and Figure 1 is too small.  
- Question: report OOV rates per dataset/topic and describe how OOV words are handled.  
- Question: confirm whether embeddings are normalized and cosine similarity used universally, and how normalization affects outcomes.  
- Question: provide coverage- or similarity-based diagnostics to predict the best TBuckets variant.  
- Question: show results with domain-specific embeddings (e.g., biomedical) and alternative pretrained models.  
- Additional: discuss and cite lexical chaining literature.  
- Additional: temper/clarify the “parameter-free” claim and specify embedding/preprocessing dependencies.  
- Additional: enlarge Figure 1 and ensure axis labels/ticks are legible.
