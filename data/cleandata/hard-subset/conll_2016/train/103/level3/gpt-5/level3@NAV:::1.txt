Summary and Contributions
- Proposes TBuckets, an embedding-based framework to assess topic quality by grouping top words into “buckets,” with three variants: agglomerative clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization. Primary contribution: a simple, general idea linking largest-bucket properties to coherence and operationalizing it via embeddings.
- Empirical evaluation on three standard datasets with correlations to human judgments; in two datasets (NYT, 20NG) SVD-Reorg is competitive or better than strong baselines. Secondary contribution: a practical application showing that selecting high-quality topic sets improves weakly supervised classification.

Strengths
- Conceptual simplicity and practicality: Using bucket size/weights to proxy coherence is intuitive and easy to implement; relying on pretrained embeddings lowers engineering cost.
- Competitive results on NYT/20NG: SVD-Reorg reaches r≈0.869 on 20NG and ≈0.837 on NYT, rivalling or exceeding prior best settings, suggesting the approach captures meaningful semantic cohesion.
- Clear utility for downstream use: Selecting topic sets by TBuckets improves macro-F1 in 3/4 20NG binary tasks, demonstrating real-world benefit beyond intrinsic correlation.
- Methodological diversity: Offering clustering and SVD-based options increases robustness and enables trade-offs between interpretability and parameterization.

Weaknesses
- Inconsistent performance across datasets/methods: No variant consistently beats the state of the art; SVD-based methods perform notably poorly on Genomics (r≈0.68–0.70), while clustering does better (up to 0.774). This undermines claims of generality and raises practical concerns for arbitrary domains.
- Insufficient analysis of OOV and domain mismatch: Genomics likely has high OOV and poor embedding coverage (GloVe Wikipedia/Gigaword), yet the paper does not quantify OOV rates, describe handling (ignored? placeholder vectors?), or analyze impact on failures.
- No guidance for method selection: The paper offers no mechanism to predict when clustering vs SVD vs SVD-Reorg will work (e.g., vocabulary overlap with embeddings, average pairwise similarity, OOV rate).
- “No parameters” claim is misleading: Performance depends heavily on the choice and training of embeddings (implicit parameters); SVD-Reorg also defines a nontrivial dynamic thresholding scheme.
- Technical clarity issues: The treatment of embeddings assumes cosine similarity without stating whether vectors are normalized; vector length may matter if cosine is not consistently applied. The description of eigenvectors as “bucket identifiers” would benefit from justification and stability analysis. Presentation: Figure 1 graphs are too small to read.

Questions to Authors
- What are the OOV rates per dataset and per topic, and how are OOV words handled in each method?
- Do you normalize embeddings and use cosine everywhere? If not, how does normalization affect results?
- Can you provide coverage-based or similarity-based diagnostics to predict which TBuckets variant to use?
- How do results change with domain-specific embeddings (e.g., biomedical) and with alternative pretrained models?

Additional Comments
- Please discuss related lexical chaining literature and cite appropriately.
- Clarify and temper the “parameter-free” claim; specify dependencies on embedding choice and preprocessing.
- Enlarge Figure 1 and ensure axis labels/ticks are legible.
