- Proposes TBuckets: groups top-n topic words into cosine-similarity “buckets” using pre-trained embeddings to measure single-theme dominance.
- Presents three variants: agglomerative clustering with threshold, SVD-based assignment to eigenvector directions, and a reorganization heuristic that moves words into/out of the largest bucket.
- Evaluates TBuckets by Pearson correlation with human coherence on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics, and in a weakly supervised classification task for run selection.
- Claims TBuckets matches/exceeds Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG; SVD variants are presented as parameter-free.
- Contribution: defines a simple bucket-based coherence family operationalizing single-theme dominance with SVD and reorg heuristics.
- Contribution: empirical comparison to Röder et al. (2015) across three datasets and a small downstream selection demo.
- Strength: Clear, intuitive idea that aligns with human judgments and yields competitive results (notably TBuckets-SVD-Reorg on NYT and 20NG).
- Strength: SVD variants require minimal per-dataset tuning compared to Röder et al.’s large configuration space.
- Strength: Uses publicly available GloVe embeddings, aiding reproducibility and deployment.
- Strength: Demonstrates potential downstream utility by improving run selection for weakly supervised classification on tougher 20NG splits.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—using word–word similarity for coherence is established; main novelty is heuristic bucketization and reorganization without theoretical grounding.
- Weakness: Reorganization threshold and many design choices appear ad hoc and lack analysis or ablation.
- Weakness: Embedding-context mismatch not addressed—window-based embeddings may not reflect document-level topic semantics, affecting polysemy and domain-shift (e.g., Genomics).
- Weakness: Missing statistical significance and confidence intervals for reported Pearson correlations and claimed improvements versus baselines.
- Weakness: Method clarity issues—rationale for using first n eigenvectors as buckets, opaque reorganization conditions/formulae, and passive phrasing obscure choices and ablations.
- Weakness: Evaluation fairness limited—comparison mainly to Röder’s best settings instead of strong distributional baselines (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson, NPMI) under the same splits; selection experiment lacks variance estimates and may have selection bias.
- Question: Provide 95% CIs and statistical tests (e.g., Fisher r-to-z) for correlations and report significance of improvements versus Röder et al.
- Question: Report sensitivity to embedding choice (word2vec, GloVe, FastText), dimensionality, and in-domain vs. generic embeddings, especially for Genomics.
- Question: Clarify OOV handling, preprocessing (stopwords, stemming, casing), and sensitivity to n (e.g., top-5 vs top-20).
- Question: Justify using first n eigenvectors as buckets; report experiments with k < n, singular-value gap heuristics, or clustering in top-k subspace.
- Question: Clarify exact reorganization threshold formula with parentheses, provide intuition, and include ablation results without reorg and with alternative thresholds.
- Question: In classification selection, compare TBuckets-based selection to selecting by NPMI or Röder’s coherence and report variance/significance.
- Suggestion: Restructure Section 3 with separate clear subsections per method, explicit algorithms, inputs/outputs, complexity, and hyperparameters; use active voice and include ablations.
- Suggestion: Justify weighting by squared largest singular value and focusing only on the largest bucket; consider dispersion penalties or entropy-based measures for multi-theme topics.
- Suggestion: Report Spearman correlations and bootstrapped CIs in addition to Pearson.
- Suggestion: Release code, topic lists, and preprocessing details to improve reproducibility.
