- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, embedding-based topic coherence metrics that partition top-n topic words into cosine-similarity “buckets”; introduces agglomerative, SVD-based, and reorganization variants and shows strong correlation with human judgments and utility for weakly supervised classification.

- Contribution 1: Empirically shows simple embedding bucketing (especially SVD-Reorg) correlates strongly with human coherence and helps select better topic runs for downstream tasks.

- Contribution 2: Presents a practical, largely parameter-free SVD-based metric with an intuitive reorganization step improving robustness over pure SVD assignment.

- Contribution 3: Offers an interpretable bucketing perspective that yields human-readable diagnostics like largest-bucket size and contents.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance — SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. on 2/3 datasets and is competitive elsewhere with fewer tunable parameters.

- Strength: Demonstrates practical utility beyond correlation by improving weakly supervised text classification via topic-run selection.

- Strength: High interpretability — buckets and largest-bucket metrics provide intuitive explanations and error analysis hooks.

- Strength: Simple and portable implementation using off-the-shelf embeddings and cosine similarity with lightweight computation.

- Weakness: Limited theoretical grounding — using word–word embedding similarity as a proxy for document-level topic coherence is ad hoc and unprincipled.

- Weakness: Context mismatch — topics are document-level distributions while embeddings capture local sequential context; this is noted but not empirically addressed.

- Weakness: Missing statistical significance tests for correlation improvements reported in Table 1.

- Weakness: Method exposition issues — Section 3 is hard to follow, with passive phrasing obscuring design choices (e.g., V vs U, eigenvector sign, choice of first n eigenvectors).

- Weakness: Sensitivity and robustness not evaluated — reliance on 300d GloVe with no analysis of embedding choice, dimensionality, domain, OOV handling, or top-n selection.

- Question: Are correlation improvements over Röder et al. statistically significant (e.g., Williams/Steiger tests for dependent correlations)?

- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding model, dimensionality, training corpus, and OOV handling?

- Question: Why assign to eigenvectors in V rather than U; how is eigenvector sign handled; why use the first n directions?

- Question: How do choices like n (top words), order weighting, and singular-value weighting affect performance — any ablation studies?

- Question: For the clustering variant, which linkage and threshold were selected and how was tuning separated from test data?

- Question: Any runtime/scalability measurements and is code available?
