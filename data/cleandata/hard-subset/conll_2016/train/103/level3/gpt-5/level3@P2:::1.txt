Summary
This paper proposes TBuckets, a family of embedding-based measures for topic quality that group a topic’s top-N words into “buckets” meant to correspond to sub-themes. The intuition is that a single large bucket indicates a coherent topic. Three instantiations are presented: (i) TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering over word embeddings with a maximum distance parameter), (ii) TBuckets-SVD (use SVD of the word-embedding matrix and assign words to the most similar eigenvector), and (iii) TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (a deterministic reorganization step that moves words into/from the largest bucket using adaptive thresholds based on average similarities). The methods are evaluated by Pearson correlation with human topic coherence scores on three datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics), and applied to weakly supervised text classification by prioritizing topic sets with higher TBuckets coherence.

Main contributions (as I see them)
1) A simple, interpretable “word bucket” paradigm for topic coherence using distributional semantics, with two parameter-free SVD variants and one clustering variant.
2) Empirical evaluation on three standard datasets, with TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforming Röder et al. (2015) on 20NG and NYT, and a practical application to topic labeling for weakly supervised classification.

Strengths
- Intuitive, human-aligned design: The bucketing idea closely mirrors how humans group semantically related words within a topic. The qualitative examples (e.g., weather vs mixed Afghanistan/Wikileaks/mining topics) make the approach easy to grasp and potentially useful for interpretability.
- Competitive performance with minimal tuning: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves higher Pearson r than the best Röder et al. (2015) configurations on 20NG and NYT while avoiding parameter tuning. This is appealing for practitioners who want robust default settings without an expensive configuration search.
- Clear ablation of scoring signals: Reporting size, WL, EVS, and combinations helps disentangle where gains come from. Weighting by the top singular value is also grounded in the SVD geometry.
- Practical downstream impact: In the topic-labeling classification experiments, selecting the top-5 topic sets by TBuckets coherence improves macro-F1 vs the baseline strategy in 3/4 subsets, and clearly separates best vs worst coherence sets (notably on the harder PC-vs-MAC subset). This supports the utility of the measure beyond correlation against human judgments.
- Interpretability of outputs: Showing bucket assignments (Table 2) provides transparency into why a topic is judged coherent or not, which is valuable for error analysis and tool adoption.

Weaknesses
- Inconsistent results across datasets and methods: TBuckets-SVD(Reorg) performs notably worse on Genomics (e.g., ~0.68–0.69 vs 0.773 for Röder et al.), while TBuckets-Clustering can be competitive there. The paper offers no analysis for when to prefer clustering vs SVD-based methods, raising concerns about applicability to new domains.
- Likely OOV and domain-mismatch effects not analyzed: Genomics likely has a high OOV rate and specialized terminology poorly captured by GloVe Wiki+Gigaword. The paper does not report OOV handling (ignored? dropped?), embeddings coverage per dataset, or sensitivity to alternative embeddings (e.g., biomedical embeddings). This omission undermines the negative Genomics results and the generality claims.
- “No parameters” claim is overstated: While the SVD variants have no tunable hyperparameters in the algorithmic steps, they rely on large pretrained embeddings with many implicit design choices (training data, dimensionality, window sizes). At minimum, the claim should be reframed as “no hyperparameters to tune at evaluation time,” and sensitivity to embedding choice/coverage should be assessed.
- Technical clarity issues around embeddings and SVD:
  • The paper assumes cosine similarity but does not clearly state vector normalization (for word vectors and eigenvectors). Magnitude differences matter if cosine is not enforced; normalization details are needed.
  • SVD is applied to raw embedding rows; centering and whitening decisions are not discussed but can change the subspace directions.
  • The choice to use the first n eigenvectors (n = number of topic words) as bucket identifiers is heuristic; an analysis of selecting k < n based on explained variance would be more principled. 
  • The reorganization threshold formula is ambiguously typeset (AWES − AWPSAWES vs (AWES − AWPS)/AWES) and needs clarification; its statistical behavior and stability are not evaluated.
- Missing related work on lexical chaining and topical grouping: The paper should cite and position against lexical chaining and related coherence work (e.g., Morris & Hirst 1991; Barzilay & Elhadad 1997; later chain-based/topic segmentation/coherence methods). The proposed bucket grouping overlaps conceptually and methodologically with this literature.
- Presentation and experimental reporting:
  • Figure 1 is too small to be readable; parameter-sensitivity plots need to be legible.
  • TBuckets-Clustering’s dependence on Max Dist is shown but practical guidance for setting it is minimal; reporting the exact values used in Table 1 and error bars would help.
  • The classification gains are modest in some subsets; statistical significance is not reported.

Questions to Authors
- Embeddings and OOV:
  • How are OOV topic words handled? Are topics with OOV words partially evaluated, or are OOV words dropped? What fraction of words per dataset is OOV?
  • Can you report results with domain-specific embeddings for Genomics (e.g., BioWordVec, PubMed/PMC word2vec/GloVe) and with word2vec vs GloVe to assess sensitivity?
- Similarity and normalization:
  • Are word vectors and eigenvectors L2-normalized before computing similarities? Is cosine or dot-product used in every step?
  • Did you consider centering the embedding matrix before SVD?
- Model selection:
  • Can you propose a simple diagnostic (e.g., embedding coverage rate, average pairwise similarity, top singular value profile) to choose between TBuckets-SVD(Reorg) and TBuckets-Clustering on a new dataset?
  • Why use the first n eigenvectors rather than selecting k by cumulative explained variance?
- Reorganization:
  • Please clarify the threshold formula (parentheses, exact computation) and provide a sensitivity/ablation. How often do “into” vs “from” moves occur, and how do they affect correlation?
- Classification study:
  • Were annotators blind to the coherence scores? How consistent were labels across runs? Please report variance and significance tests for S1 vs S2 differences.

Additional comments
- Please correct minor typographical issues (e.g., the reorg threshold formula typesetting).
- Consider adding readable, larger plots and releasing code and evaluation scripts with OOV handling documented.
