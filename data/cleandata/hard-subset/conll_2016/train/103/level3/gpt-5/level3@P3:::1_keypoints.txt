- Proposes TBuckets: embedding-based measures that bucket a topic’s top words into coherent sub-themes (agglomerative clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) and score topics mainly via the largest bucket.
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human judgments on NYT, 20NG, Genomics, plus a weakly supervised text-classification case study.
- Main contributions: an embedding-based bucketing framework, an SVD assignment and reorganization heuristic, and empirical results showing practical utility.
- Strength: clear, intuitive, interpretable bucket metaphor—large single buckets map to human notions of coherence.
- Strength: competitive correlations on standard datasets; SVD-Reorg strong on NYT and 20NG; clustering competitive on Genomics.
- Strength: practical, low-overhead SVD variants that avoid heavy hyperparameter tuning at scoring time.
- Strength: downstream usefulness—selecting high-scoring runs by TBuckets improves macro-F1 in 3/4 classification tasks.
- Weakness: inconsistent performance across datasets and variants; no single method consistently outperforms prior state-of-the-art.
- Weakness: lack of analysis for domain mismatch and OOV effects (no OOV rates, coverage, or handling strategy reported), hindering interpretation of Genomics results.
- Weakness: “no parameters” claim is misleading because pretrained embeddings and design choices (eigenvector count, threshold formula) are implicit parameters.
- Weakness: embedding preprocessing and similarity details are underspecified (normalization, cosine vs dot, SVD comparisons), which can materially affect results.
- Weakness: missing related work discussion—links to lexical chaining and cohesion literature are not addressed.
- Weakness: limited practical guidance—no diagnostics or heuristics to predict which TBuckets variant suits a new dataset.
- Weakness: presentation/reporting issues—small/illegible Figure 1, no confidence intervals/significance tests, missing ablations and exact clustering settings.
- Question: How are OOV topic words handled and what are OOV rates per dataset (especially Genomics)? How sensitive are correlations to OOV rate?
- Question: Are word vectors L2-normalized before similarity/SVD? Are similarities computed with cosine or dot product and are eigenvectors normalized?
- Question: Why use the first n eigenvectors as buckets instead of selecting k by explained variance or singular-value thresholds; was dimensionality reduction tried?
- Question: Please justify the reorganization threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES) and report robustness to that choice.
- Question: Can you provide heuristics (e.g., embedding vocabulary coverage, domain similarity) to guide choosing clustering vs SVD variants?
- Question: Did you try domain-specific embeddings for Genomics and how did that affect performance?
- Question: In the classification case study, how much annotator effort was saved and are the F1 gains statistically significant?
- Suggestion: Enlarge figures, report OOV coverage, confidence intervals, ablations (normalization, alternative embeddings, topic-word count), and clarify reproducibility details (embedding version, tokenization, stopword handling, multiword treatment).
