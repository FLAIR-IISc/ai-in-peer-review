- Proposes TBuckets: embedding-based topic coherence measures that group a topic’s top words into “buckets” (sub-themes) and reward a single dominant bucket.  
- Implements three variants: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative on embeddings), TBuckets-SVD (SVD on topic embedding matrix), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (moves words into/out of largest bucket by similarity heuristics).  
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human coherence on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics; SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. on NYT and 20NG but not Genomics.  
- Demonstrates practical use: selecting high-coherence topic sets with TBuckets improves weakly supervised classification macro-F1 on several 20NG binary tasks.  
- Contribution: lightweight, intuitive embedding bucketing paradigm with parameter-light SVD variants.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that SVD-Reorg strongly correlates with human judgments on common benchmarks and can aid annotation-driven workflows.  
- Strength: conceptually clear and computationally cheap—does not require co-occurrence counts or heavy hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: competitive or state-of-the-art correlations on two benchmarks (NYT, 20NG) with strong reported r values.  
- Strength: practical utility beyond evaluation—ranking topics by TBuckets yields measurable annotation/classification gains.  
- Strength: SVD-Reorg addresses failure modes where semantically related words split across eigenvector-aligned buckets; dynamic thresholding aims to be parameter-agnostic.  
- Weakness: inconsistent cross-dataset performance; SVD variants perform poorly on Genomics, raising robustness concerns for specialized domains.  
- Weakness: OOV handling and domain-embedding mismatch unmeasured and unanalyzed; no reported OOV rates or experiments with domain-specific embeddings (e.g., PubMed, fastText).  
- Weakness: “no parameters” claim is overstated—pretrained embedding choices and several algorithmic thresholds act as de facto hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: methodological and reproducibility gaps—unclear normalization (unit vectors?), similarity metric (cosine vs dot), unusual choice of taking first n eigenvectors, ambiguous reorg threshold formula, and unmotivated scoring weights.  
- Weakness: missing statistical significance testing and confidence intervals for correlation comparisons.  
- Weakness: incomplete related work coverage—prior embedding-based coherence measures and lexical chaining literature not adequately compared.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—small/illegible figures, unexplained normalization of TBuckets scores vs human scales, and typographical inconsistencies.  
- Requested analyses/questions: report per-dataset and per-topic OOV rates and correlations after (a) dropping OOVs, (b) UNK vector usage, and (c) domain embeddings substitution.  
- Requested analyses/questions: justify using the first n eigenvectors vs selecting k < d (e.g., by explained variance), and report sensitivity to k.  
- Requested clarifications: state whether vectors/eigenvectors are unit-normalized and that cosine similarity is used consistently; detail exact similarity computations.  
- Requested guidance: provide criteria or meta-features (e.g., OOV rate, embedding overlap, average pairwise similarity) to decide when SVD-Reorg vs Clustering is preferable.  
- Requested reproducibility info: clarify how Röder et al. baselines were obtained (reproduced vs copied) and include statistical significance tests (e.g., bootstrap CIs).  
- Requested ablations: disentangle final scoring components (size vs WL vs EVS) and the effect of weighting by the top singular value.
