Summary: The paper proposes TBuckets, a family of embedding-based coherence metrics for topic models that partition the top-n words of each topic into “buckets” based on cosine similarity of word embeddings. Three variants are introduced: (i) an agglomerative clustering approach, (ii) an SVD-based method that assigns words to eigenvector “directions,” and (iii) a reorganization heuristic that moves words into/from the largest bucket using dynamic thresholds. On three standard datasets (20NG, NYT, Genomics), TBuckets matches or exceeds Röder et al. (2015) in Pearson correlation with human judgments, particularly with the SVD-Reorg variant, and is further shown to help in weakly supervised text classification by prioritizing high-quality topic runs.

Contributions (as I see them):
1) Empirical finding that simple embedding-based bucketing (especially SVD-Reorg) correlates strongly with human topic coherence and improves downstream weakly supervised classification by selecting better topic runs.
2) A practical, largely parameter-free SVD-based metric with an intuitive reorganization step that improves robustness over pure SVD assignment.
3) A straightforward, interpretable bucketing perspective that yields human-readable diagnostics (largest-bucket size/contents).

Strengths:
- Strong empirical performance: SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. on 2/3 datasets and is competitive elsewhere, using far fewer tunable parameters than prior work.
- Practical utility: Demonstrated benefit for weakly supervised classification (not just correlation), showing the metric’s downstream relevance.
- Interpretability: Buckets and largest-bucket size offer intuitive explanations for coherence judgments and error analysis.
- Simplicity and portability: Uses off-the-shelf embeddings and cosine similarity; implementation appears lightweight.

Weaknesses:
- Limited theoretical grounding: The use of word–word embedding similarity as a proxy for topic coherence is ad hoc; no principled link to document-level topic semantics is provided.
- Context mismatch: Topics are document-level distributions while embeddings encode local sequential context; this mismatch is acknowledged but not addressed empirically.
- Missing significance tests: Table 1 lacks statistical tests comparing correlations; improvements may not be statistically significant.
- Method exposition: Section 3 is hard to follow; passive phrasing obscures design choices (e.g., why V over U, first n eigenvectors, handling eigenvector sign).
- Sensitivity and robustness: Results rely on 300d GloVe; no sensitivity to embedding choice/domain (word2vec, fastText, in-domain, contextual) or to n (top-10) is reported.

Questions to Authors:
- Are correlation improvements over Röder et al. statistically significant (e.g., Williams/Steiger tests for dependent correlations)?
- How sensitive are results to the embedding model, dimensionality, training corpus, and OOV handling?
- Why assign to eigenvectors in V rather than U; how is eigenvector sign handled; why use the first n directions?
- How do choices like n (top words), order weighting, and singular-value weighting affect performance (ablations)?
- Can you clarify the clustering variant: which linkage and threshold were selected, and how was tuning separated from test sets?
- Any runtime/scalability data and code availability?
