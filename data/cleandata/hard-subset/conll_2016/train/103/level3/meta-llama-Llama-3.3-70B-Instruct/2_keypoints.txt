- Contribution — Introduces a minimally supervised parser that leverages external prior probabilities to boost parsing accuracy.
- Contribution — Compares delexicalized transfer parsing methods with minimally supervised parsing approaches.
- Contribution — Evaluates these methods across 32 languages from the Universal Dependencies treebank.

- Strength — The minimally supervised parser achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art delexicalized transfer parsers, notably for non-Indo-European languages.
- Strength — Uses the Universal Dependencies collection for unified, cross-lingual evaluation.
- Strength — Provides a thorough comparison of delexicalized transfer and unsupervised parsers augmented with external priors.

- Weakness — Lacks a clear explanation of why the proposed method works, especially the link between external priors and parsing accuracy.
- Weakness — Section 3 and some methodological descriptions are under-detailed and unclearly written (excessive passive voice).
- Weakness — Results lack statistical significance testing to show whether differences between methods are meaningful.

- Question — Why does the minimally supervised parser perform better on non-Indo-European languages?
- Question — How exactly do external prior probabilities affect parsing accuracy, and will other types of priors be explored?
- Question — Are there plans to extend this minimally supervised approach to other NLP tasks (e.g., POS tagging, semantic role labeling)?
