- Proposes TBuckets: group topic words into thematic “buckets” and score topic coherence using the largest bucket; presents TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg and evaluates on three datasets showing improved results and utility for weakly supervised classification.  
- Novelty: introduces a new bucket-based method to measure LDA topic quality.  
- Empirical improvement: TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art on two of three datasets.  
- Parameter-free methods: presents TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg that require no parameter tuning.  
- Uses word embeddings effectively to compute semantic similarities between topic words.  
- Simple and intuitive: the TBuckets approach is easy to understand and implement.  
- Demonstrates utility for weakly supervised text classification as a downstream application.  
- Inconsistent performance: methods do not consistently beat state-of-the-art across all datasets.  
- Out-of-vocabulary issue: paper does not adequately address impact of OOV terms on TBuckets’ performance.  
- Missing baselines: lacks comparisons with other topic coherence measures (e.g., CUCI, CUMASS).  
- Question: How will authors address TBuckets’ inconsistent cross-dataset performance?  
- Question: Can authors provide more insight into the impact of out-of-vocabulary terms?  
- Question: How do authors plan to extend TBuckets to applications like document summarization and sentiment analysis?
