- Paper proposes TBuckets, an embedding-based method that groups topic words into “buckets” to measure topic quality, with application to weakly supervised text classification.
- Main contribution: exploring embedding-based topic coherence evaluation as an alternative to co-occurrence or knowledge-base methods.
- Secondary contribution: using TBuckets to pre-select high-quality topics to improve weakly supervised classification.
- Strength: novel direction leveraging semantic relationships in word embeddings.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on three datasets, including a genomics dataset, and comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- Strength: demonstrated practical utility for weakly supervised text classification.
- Strength: SVD-based variants are parameter-free, reducing need for tuning.
- Weakness: inconsistent performance across methods and datasets, raising generalizability concerns.
- Weakness: SVD-based methods perform poorly on the genomics dataset without explanation.
- Weakness: authors fail to address out-of-vocabulary (OOV) term handling, likely important for specialized domains like genomics.
- Weakness: claim of “no parameters” for SVD methods is misleading because pre-trained embeddings contain learned parameters.
- Weakness: missing citation/discussion of related work in lexical chaining.
- Weakness: presentation issues—figures (Figure 1) are too small to read easily.
- Questions: how are OOV terms handled and how does that affect results, especially for genomics?
- Questions: what is the vocabulary overlap between GloVe training data and evaluation datasets, and does overlap correlate with performance?
- Questions: how does choice of embedding model (Word2Vec, GloVe, etc.) affect TBuckets’ performance?
- Questions: how does TBuckets relate to and differ from lexical chaining techniques?
