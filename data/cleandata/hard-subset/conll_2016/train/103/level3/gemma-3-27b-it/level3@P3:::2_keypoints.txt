- Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into embedding-based “buckets” with three creation methods: greedy, SVD, and SVD-Reorg.  
- Uses cosine similarity of word embeddings to assess topic quality, offering an intuitive alternative to existing coherence metrics.  
- Demonstrates empirical competitiveness, with SVD-Reorg often matching or outperforming prior work on standard datasets.  
- Shows practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification via topic filtering.  
- Highlights parameter-free SVD methods as easier to apply due to no tuning.  
- Clearly motivates the topic quality problem and limitations of prior approaches.  
- Presents strong empirical results, notably SVD-Reorg’s performance on NYT and 20NG.  
- Grounds evaluation with a downstream application, strengthening practical relevance.  
- Acknowledges limitations and suggests future research directions.  
- Lacks theoretical justification linking embedding similarity to human topic coherence judgments.  
- Raises context mismatch concerns between embedding training contexts and document-level topic context.  
- Section 3 is unclear and overly detailed; needs restructuring (one subsection per method).  
- Missing statistical significance tests for reported performance differences.  
- Limited ablation study: components of SVD-Reorg (size, WL, EVS) not individually assessed.  
- Asks about sensitivity to choice or fine-tuning of word embeddings (word2vec vs. GloVe, etc.).  
- Questions robustness and selection method of the reorganization threshold (AWPS/AWES vs. fixed/cross-validated).  
- Requests clarification on computational complexity and scalability of TBuckets, especially SVD-Reorg.  
- Requests more qualitative analysis showing successful and failed bucket groupings.  
- Notes minor language issues and recommends proofreading for clarity.  
- Suggests Table 2 include coherence scores to better compare with human judgments.  
- Praises the reference list as well-formatted and comprehensive.
