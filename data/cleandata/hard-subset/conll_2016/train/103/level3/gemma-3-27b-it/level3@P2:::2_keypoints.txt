- Proposes TBuckets, a method that groups topic words into semantic “buckets” using word embeddings to evaluate LDA topic quality.
- Implements three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with a reorganization step.
- Evaluated on three standard datasets (NYT, 20NG, Genomics) and shown to be competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
- Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification when selecting high-quality topics.
- Main contribution: TBuckets framework as a simple, practical metric for topic coherence using bucket formation.
- Secondary contribution: empirical evidence of TBuckets’ effectiveness on benchmarks and a downstream classification task.
- Strength — intuitive and human-aligned: bucket grouping mirrors how humans judge topic coherence and is easy to understand/adapt.
- Strength — empirical performance: outperforms prior methods on NYT and 20NG, matches on Genomics, with parameter-free SVD variants.
- Strength — practical application: selecting topics by TBuckets score yields significant gains in weakly supervised classification.
- Strength — clear presentation overall: well-structured introduction and interpretable experimental setup/results.
- Weakness — lacks theoretical grounding: no principled explanation for why embedding-based bucket partitioning should align with human coherence judgments.
- Weakness — contextual mismatch risk: pre-trained embeddings based on sequential context may not reflect document-level co-occurrence in topic models.
- Weakness — Section 3 is dense and unclear: methods description (especially reorganization) needs clearer subsections and simpler exposition.
- Weakness — missing statistical significance: reported correlation coefficients lack p-values or confidence intervals to judge significance.
- Weakness — language/concision issues: some awkward phrasing and redundancies that need editing for clarity.
- Question — did you run statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) and can you report p-values/confidence intervals?
- Question — how sensitive are results to the choice of word embeddings (pre-trained vs. dataset-trained; word2vec vs. fastText)?
- Question — what is the computational complexity of each TBuckets variant, particularly SVD and SVD-Reorg?
- Question — can you simplify and better illustrate the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg with examples?
- Additional comment — shorten the lengthy related work section to focus on the most relevant papers.
- Additional comment — include visualizations of example buckets to make bucket formation and coherence more intuitive.
