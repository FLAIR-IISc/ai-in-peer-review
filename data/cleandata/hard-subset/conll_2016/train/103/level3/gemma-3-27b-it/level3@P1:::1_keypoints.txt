- Proposes TBuckets: group topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings; coherent topics yield one large bucket, incoherent topics fragment into many smaller buckets.
- Investigates three bucket-creation methods: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.
- Shows improved performance vs. existing methods on three public datasets and utility for weakly supervised text classification.
- Main contribution: embedding- and dimensionality-reduction-based approach to topic coherence evaluation with comprehensive empirical analysis.
- Strength: leverages pre-trained embeddings to capture semantic relations beyond PMI-based approaches.
- Strength: thorough evaluation on three datasets using Pearson’s r and inclusion of a downstream classification task.
- Strength: SVD-based variants presented as parameter-free, reducing explicit tuning needs.
- Strength: improves weakly supervised classification by presenting higher-quality topics to annotators.
- Weakness: inconsistent performance across datasets (strong on NYT/20NG, weak on Genomics), raising generalizability concerns.
- Weakness: no treatment or analysis of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, likely harming domain-specific datasets like Genomics.
- Weakness: “parameter-free” claim is misleading because the approach depends on pre-trained embeddings with their own hyperparameters.
- Weakness: missing discussion of relevant lexical chaining literature, which is conceptually similar.
- Weakness: presentation and technical clarity issues—small/unclear figures and ambiguous embedding/normalization details (cosine similarity not explicit).
- Question: How are OOV terms handled and what are the OOV rates per dataset?
- Question: Why does TBuckets-SVD underperform on Genomics—can authors analyze causes (e.g., OOVs)?
- Question: How does TBuckets relate to and differ from existing lexical chaining methods?
- Question: What criteria recommend choosing Clustering vs. SVD vs. SVD-Reorg; can dataset characteristics predict best technique?
- Additional comment: Include computational complexity analysis for each TBuckets technique.
- Additional comment: Add error analysis with topic examples where TBuckets succeeds or fails and explanations.
- Additional comment: Report statistical significance testing to strengthen result claims.
