- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets using embeddings with three methods (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).
- Demonstrates improved performance over state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG and shows utility for weakly supervised text classification.
- Main contribution: embedding-based topic coherence evaluation using pre-trained embeddings and dimensionality reduction (SVD).
- Empirical evaluation across three public datasets provides comparative analysis, with mixed results.
- Novel approach: moves beyond co-occurrence to capture semantic relationships via embedding clusters.
- Strong empirical performance on some datasets (NYT, 20NG), notably with parameter-free SVD variants.
- Practical application shown: improved downstream weakly supervised classification using TBuckets.
- Methods and bucket-creation process are clearly presented and reproducible.
- Inconsistent performance and dataset dependence, notably poor SVD results on the Genomics dataset.
- No analysis of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms, which likely affect performance in specialized domains like Genomics.
- Claim of being "parameter-free" is misleading because pre-trained embeddings introduce implicit parameters.
- Oversimplified treatment of embeddings; unclear whether vectors were normalized—important for cosine similarity.
- Related work on lexical chaining is omitted and should be cited for context.
- Figure 1 is too small and hard to read; visualization clarity needs improvement.
- Questions: how were OOV terms handled and how do they affect results, especially for Genomics?
- Questions: why do SVD methods fail on Genomics and were domain-specific embeddings (biomedical) tried?
- Questions: guidance or heuristics for selecting among clustering, SVD, or SVD-Reorg for a given dataset?
- Questions: sensitivity to choice of pre-trained embeddings (Word2Vec, FastText, etc.) and any experiments comparing them?
- Additional suggestions: explore other similarity measures, discuss computational complexity/scalability, and include qualitative bucket analyses.
- Overall verdict: promising work but requires substantial revisions to address inconsistencies, OOV handling, and methodological claims; recommended weak accept contingent on revisions.
