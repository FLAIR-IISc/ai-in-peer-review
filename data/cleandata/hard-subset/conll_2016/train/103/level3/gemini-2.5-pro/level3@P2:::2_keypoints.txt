- Introduces TBuckets: partition top-N topic words into semantic “buckets” using word embeddings; coherent topics form one large bucket, incoherent topics break into many small buckets.
- Proposes three partitioning methods: agglomerative clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (which adds a reorganization step).
- Evaluated on three datasets against human judgments and shown comparable or superior correlation to prior work; used to select topic models that improve weakly supervised text classification.
- Contribution: Novel TBuckets framework leveraging pretrained embeddings; SVD-based methods are parameter-free.
- Contribution: Strong empirical results—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG.
- Contribution: Demonstrates practical downstream utility by improving classification when selecting models by coherence.
- Strength: High empirical performance with superior Pearson correlations (NYT 0.837 vs 0.806; 20NG 0.869 vs 0.859).
- Strength: Simple, intuitive approach and practical advantage of parameter-free SVD methods over heavily tuned baselines.
- Strength: Downstream task evaluation shows the metric captures meaningful model quality beyond correlation studies.
- Weakness: Method description (Section 3) is unclear and uses passive phrasing; needs step-by-step algorithm or pseudocode for reproducibility.
- Weakness: Lacks deeper theoretical justification and does not address potential mismatch between local-context embeddings and document-level topic coherence.
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported to support claims of empirical superiority.
- Question: Please perform statistical significance tests on the results in Table 1.
- Question: How sensitive is TBuckets to the choice of embeddings (e.g., word2vec, FastText, dimensionality, domain-specific training)?
