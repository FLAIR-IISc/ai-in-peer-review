- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, which assesses topic coherence by clustering top topic words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained embeddings; coherence = most words fall in one large bucket.  
- Methods: Introduces three bucketing methods — greedy clustering, SVD-based, and SVD with a reorganisation step — and presents parameter-free variants.  
- Contribution 1: Empirical claim that TBuckets achieves state-of-the-art correlation with human judgments on two of three standard datasets.  
- Contribution 2: Offers parameter-free SVD-based measures that avoid costly tuning, improving practicality.  
- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg shows strong empirical performance, matching or outperforming prior work on multiple datasets.  
- Strength: SVD-based methods are parameter-free, reducing the need for extensive parameter sweeps.  
- Strength: Demonstrates utility in a downstream weakly supervised text classification task, where higher coherence selection improves accuracy.  
- Weakness: Method feels ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification for why discrete bucketing should outperform pairwise similarity aggregation; reorganisation step is heuristic.  
- Weakness: Does not discuss potential mismatch between document-level co-occurrence signals used to generate topics and local-window context used to train embeddings.  
- Weakness: Section 3 exposition is hard to follow; passive voice and narrative structure obscure algorithmic steps and design choices.  
- Weakness: Table 1 lacks statistical significance tests, so reported improvements may not be meaningful.  
- Question: Provide significance tests for Table 1 correlations to validate improvements over the state-of-the-art?  
- Question: How sensitive is TBuckets to the choice of embedding model or training corpus (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec, domain-specific embeddings like for Genomics)?
