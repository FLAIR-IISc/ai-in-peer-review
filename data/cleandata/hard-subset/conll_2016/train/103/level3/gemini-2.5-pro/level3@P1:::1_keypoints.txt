- Paper idea: TBuckets evaluates topic coherence by grouping topic words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained word embeddings and scoring based on bucket properties (largest bucket size).
- Methods: Proposes three implementations — agglomerative clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg — all using GloVe vectors to form buckets.
- Evaluation: Measures Pearson correlation with human judgments on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior work on NYT and 20NG.
- Application: Using TBuckets to select higher-quality topic models improves performance in a downstream weakly supervised text classification task.
- Contribution 1: Introduces an intuitive, embedding-based bucketing framework for topic coherence, with novel SVD variants to capture latent semantic dimensions.
- Contribution 2: Shows practical utility by improving downstream classification via coherence-based model selection.
- Strength 1: Novel and intuitive bucketing idea that aligns with human notions of topical unity and leverages modern embeddings.
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results on standard news datasets, achieving state-of-the-art correlations for TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
- Strength 3: SVD-based variants are presented as parameter-free, offering ease-of-use compared to methods requiring extensive tuning.
- Strength 4: Demonstrates real-world utility through a downstream task, strengthening the metric's practical relevance.
- Weakness 1: Inconsistent performance across datasets — strong on NYT/20NG but substantially worse on Genomics — with no guidance on variant selection.
- Weakness 2: Lacks analysis of failure cases, especially potential domain mismatch and OOV issues for Genomics; OOV handling and rates are not reported.
- Weakness 3: "Parameter-free" claim is misleading because results depend on the choice of pre-trained embeddings, an unexamined hyperparameter.
- Weakness 4: Missing related work (lexical chaining), some imprecise embedding descriptions (cosine vs. normalization), and illegible/small figures.
- Questions for authors: How are OOV words handled and what are OOV rates per dataset; any heuristics for choosing a TBuckets variant; have you tested sensitivity to different embeddings; are GloVe vectors normalized before SVD/clustering?
