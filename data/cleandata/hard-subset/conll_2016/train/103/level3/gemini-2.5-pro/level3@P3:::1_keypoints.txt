- Paper proposes TBuckets: group a topic's top words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained embeddings; coherent topics have one dominant bucket.
- Introduces three bucket-creation methods: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD (matrix factorization for orthogonal sub-themes), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD plus refinement).
- Evaluated by correlating coherence scores with human judgments on three datasets; SVD-Reorg attains state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG.
- Contribution: novel shift from pairwise aggregation to partitioning topic words into semantic buckets as a coherence measure.
- Contribution: novel use of SVD on topic word embedding matrices to identify sub-themes and a reorganization heuristic to refine buckets.
- Contribution: strong empirical performance, notably high Pearson correlations on NYT (0.837) and 20NG (0.869).
- Strength: conceptually intuitive and aligns with human interpretation of topics by identifying core semantic clusters.
- Strength: empirical results outperform prior baselines on two datasets, supporting the dense embedding approach.
- Strength: SVD-based methods presented as simple and free of tunable hyperparameters, appealing for practitioners.
- Weakness: inconsistent performance across datasets (SVD-Reorg fails on Genomics while clustering does better) with no analysis explaining why.
- Weakness: paper omits handling and analysis of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words from pre-trained GloVe embeddings, likely causing Genomics failures.
- Weakness: misleading "parameter free" claim—choice of pre-trained embeddings is a significant implicit parameter not acknowledged.
- Weakness: unclear methodological details about embedding usage (e.g., whether vectors are L2-normalized, exact similarity metric used).
- Weakness: missing related work on lexical chaining and related methods for grouping semantically related words.
- Weakness: presentation issues—figures (e.g., Figure 1) are too small to read, hiding sensitivity analyses for clustering thresholds.
- Questions for authors: How are OOVs handled and what are OOV rates per dataset; is OOV the primary cause of Genomics performance drop?
- Questions for authors: What guidance can practitioners use to choose among Clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg for new datasets (e.g., vocabulary overlap predictor)?
- Questions for authors: Clarify and justify the "parameter free" claim and report experiments with other embeddings (Word2Vec or domain-specific) to assess sensitivity.
- Questions for authors: Specify the exact similarity metric(s) used (cosine?) and whether word/eigenvector vectors are L2-normalized before similarity computations.
