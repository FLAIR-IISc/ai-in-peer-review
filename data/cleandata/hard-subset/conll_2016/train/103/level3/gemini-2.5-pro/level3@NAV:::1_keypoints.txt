- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into semantic buckets via pretrained embeddings and offering agglomerative-clustering plus two SVD-based techniques to measure topic coherence, with empirical correlation to human judgments.  
- Strength 1: Conceptually novel and intuitive—uses embedding geometry (clustering/SVD) to capture semantic cohesion.  
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results on NYT and 20NG; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms reported state-of-the-art on those datasets.  
- Strength 3: SVD-based variants presented as parameter-free, simplifying application and removing dataset-specific tuning.  
- Weakness 1: Performance is inconsistent across datasets; no single variant reliably outperforms baselines and SVD methods perform poorly on the Genomics dataset.  
- Weakness 2: Paper fails to analyze or explain the inconsistency—likely due to OOV terms in specialized domains—with no discussion of OOV handling, prevalence, or selection guidance.  
- Weakness 3: Claim of “parameter-free” is misleading because choice of pretrained embeddings is an implicit hyperparameter; overlooks related work on lexical chaining.  
- Weakness 4: Several technical details are unclear—OOV strategy unspecified, cosine-similarity/normalization not clarified, and Figure 1 graphs are too small to read.
