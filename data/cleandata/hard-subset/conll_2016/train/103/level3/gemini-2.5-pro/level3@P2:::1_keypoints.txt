- Proposes TBuckets, an embedding-based method that groups a topic's top words into semantic "buckets" to measure topic coherence.  
- Introduces three bucketing techniques: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, evaluated against human judgments and in a weakly supervised classification task.  
- Contribution: a novel, intuitive group-based coherence metric using word embeddings that moves beyond pairwise co-occurrence measures.  
- Contribution: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art results on NYT and 20NG, showing the power of dense embeddings for coherence evaluation.  
- Strength: The bucketing idea is novel and well-motivated; using SVD to identify orthogonal sub-themes is an interesting technical choice.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance on general-domain datasets (NYT and 20NG) validates the approach.  
- Strength: Demonstrated practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification via topic selection with TBuckets.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent performance across datasets (notably poor on Genomics) with no analysis or practitioner guidance on variant selection.  
- Weakness: Fails to address Out-Of-Vocabulary (OOV) handling for embeddings, threatening replicability and likely affecting Genomics results.  
- Weakness: Misleading claim that SVD-based methods are "parameter-free" ignores the embedding choice as a major implicit hyperparameter.  
- Weakness: Omits discussion of related work on lexical chaining and semantic grouping, which is directly relevant.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—figures (e.g., Figure 1) are too small to read and hinder interpretation of parameters like Max Dist.  
- Question: How were OOV words handled and what were the OOV rates for GloVe across the three datasets?  
- Question: Why does TBuckets-SVD-Reorg perform variably across datasets, and what dataset characteristics should guide choosing a TBuckets variant?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of pre-trained embeddings (e.g., word2vec vs. GloVe, corpus, dimensionality)?
