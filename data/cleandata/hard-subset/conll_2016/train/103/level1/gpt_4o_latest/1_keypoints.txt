- Introduces TBuckets, grouping topic words into semantically similar "buckets" to measure LDA topic coherence.  
- Proposes three bucket-creation techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative), TBuckets-SVD (SVD-based sub-themes), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (reorganizes SVD buckets).  
- Evaluates methods on three datasets and reports TBuckets-SVD-Reorg correlates best with human coherence scores.  
- Shows TBuckets can improve weakly supervised text classification by selecting high-quality topics.  
- Contribution: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg as a parameter-free coherence measure that outperforms baselines on two datasets.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates practical utility for weakly supervised text classification via coherence-based topic selection.  
- Contribution: Novel use of SVD to identify interpretable sub-themes for coherence assessment.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results with state-of-the-art performance and no parameter tuning.  
- Strength: Practical applicability to text classification with measurable accuracy gains.  
- Strength: Clear, intuitive methodology with illustrative examples of bucket formation and reorganization.  
- Strength: Robust evaluation across multiple datasets and comparison to state-of-the-art, including human annotations.  
- Strength: Focus on interpretability aligns with real-world topic modeling needs.  
- Weakness: Lacks evaluation on domain-specific datasets; performance on specialized vocabularies is unclear.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparative analysis explaining why TBuckets outperforms competing methods.  
- Weakness: Scalability and computational cost of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg for large corpora are not discussed.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of alternative or additional coherence metrics beyond bucket size and eigenvector similarity.  
- Question: What is the computational complexity of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg versus other methods for large datasets?  
- Question: Has TBuckets been tested on highly domain-specific corpora (e.g., medical or legal), and how does it perform?  
- Question: Could the reorganization step introduce instability in coherence scores across runs, and how is this mitigated?  
- Conclusion: Paper is a valuable, innovative contribution with recommendation for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing scalability, domain evaluation, and deeper comparative analysis.
