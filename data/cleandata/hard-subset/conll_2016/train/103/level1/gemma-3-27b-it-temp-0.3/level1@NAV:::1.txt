## Review for "Measuring Topic Quality using Word Buckets"

This paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, for measuring topic quality in Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) models. The core idea is to group topic words into “buckets” based on semantic similarity using word embeddings, with the assumption that coherent topics will result in a single large bucket representing a unified theme. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization, and demonstrate improved performance compared to state-of-the-art methods on three public datasets, alongside a demonstration of utility in weakly supervised text classification.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the TBuckets framework itself. The intuition behind using bucket formation as a proxy for topic coherence is sound and offers a different perspective compared to existing methods relying on PMI or distributional similarity. The SVD-based techniques, particularly with reorganization, are a notable strength, as they are parameter-free, addressing a common criticism of many coherence measures requiring extensive tuning. The empirical validation on established datasets and the demonstration of improved performance are also valuable contributions.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Approach:** TBuckets offers a fresh perspective on topic coherence evaluation, moving beyond traditional statistical measures to a more conceptually aligned approach based on semantic grouping.
2. **Parameter-Free Methods:** The SVD-based techniques (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) are a significant advantage, simplifying the evaluation process and reducing the need for parameter optimization.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates that TBuckets outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on multiple datasets, providing evidence of its effectiveness.
4. **Practical Application:** The successful application of TBuckets to weakly supervised text classification highlights its practical utility beyond simply measuring topic quality.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Components:** While the combination is novel, the individual components (word embeddings, SVD, clustering) are well-established techniques. The paper doesn’t introduce any new advancements in these underlying methods.
2. **Reorganization Step Complexity:** The description of the reorganization step (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) is complex and relies on several parameters (NBPW, NBPE, LBPW, OBPW) derived from similarity scores. While the authors claim no parameter *tuning*, the logic behind these parameters and their impact on performance could be more thoroughly explained.
3. **Dataset Limitations:** The evaluation relies on datasets created specifically for topic coherence evaluation (Roder et al., 2015). Evaluating on more diverse, real-world datasets would strengthen the findings.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more intuitive explanation of the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg? Perhaps a visual example illustrating how words are shifted between buckets would be helpful.
2. How sensitive is the performance of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg to the choice of word embeddings? Have you experimented with different pre-trained embeddings or training your own?
3.  What is the computational cost of the TBuckets approach compared to existing coherence measures, particularly for large topics and datasets?
