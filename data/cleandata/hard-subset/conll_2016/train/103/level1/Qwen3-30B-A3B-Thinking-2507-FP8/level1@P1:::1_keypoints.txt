- Introduces TBuckets: groups topic words into thematic buckets and uses largest bucket size as a coherence measure.  
- Proposes three techniques: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Claims parameter-free SVD-based methods that avoid extensive tuning required by prior work.  
- Demonstrates practical utility: selecting high-quality topics with TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strong practical motivation: method aligns with human perception of coherence and is intuitively illustrated.  
- SVD techniques are effectively parameter-free and outperform state-of-the-art on two of three datasets.  
- Clear application value shown by consistent classification accuracy gains, notably +3.1% on PC vs MAC.  
- Limited novelty: clustering/SVD/reorganization are standard techniques and similarity to prior vector-space work not fully acknowledged.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why largest bucket size correlates with human coherence judgments.  
- Inadequate comparison to more recent topic coherence methods beyond Roder et al. (2015).  
- Clarify how TBuckets-SVD-Reorg’s dynamic threshold (AWPS/AWES) is computed and why it needs no tuning.  
- Explain how Max Dist for TBuckets-Clustering was chosen given performance sensitivity in Figure 1.  
- Address robustness to embedding choice (GloVe vs. Word2Vec/fastText) and its impact on coherence scores.  
- Expand discussion differentiating TBuckets from prior distributional-semantic coherence methods and test whether classification gains stem from coherence measure or the reorganization step.
