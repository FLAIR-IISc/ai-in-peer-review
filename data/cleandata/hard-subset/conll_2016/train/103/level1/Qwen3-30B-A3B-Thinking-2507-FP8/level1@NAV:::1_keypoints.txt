- Proposes TBuckets: measure topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic buckets and using the size of the largest bucket as a coherence indicator aligned with human judgment.  
- Introduces three techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering), TBuckets-SVD (SVD to find orthogonal sub-themes), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD plus reorganization step).  
- Main contributions (ranked): TBuckets concept itself; parameter-free SVD-based techniques; practical application to weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strength: TBuckets is intuitively compelling and theoretically aligns with how humans assess topic coherence.  
- Strength: SVD-based methods are effectively parameter-free and outperform baselines on 2 of 3 datasets, reducing tuning burden.  
- Strength: Using TBuckets to select high-quality topics yields up to 4.2% improvement in weakly supervised classification on difficult datasets.  
- Weakness: Claim of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg being “completely parameter free” is problematic since the reorganization threshold depends on topic-specific AWPS/AWES (implicit tuning).  
- Weakness: Comparisons are limited to metrics reported in Röder et al. (2015) and omit the broader suite of coherence measures.  
- Weakness: Classification improvements are modest and lack sufficient analysis explaining why gains occur, especially for the hardest datasets.  
- Question: How is TBuckets-SVD-Reorg truly parameter-free given the topic-specific threshold calculation?  
- Question: Can you provide a more detailed comparison with recent coherence measures beyond Table 1?  
- Question: What is the relationship between the coherence score threshold and classification performance improvement in the text classification experiments?
