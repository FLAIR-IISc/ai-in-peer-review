- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic buckets (TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) and evaluates against human coherence and in weakly supervised classification.

- Contribution 1: TBuckets framework for measuring topic coherence via thematic bucketing of topic words.

- Contribution 2: Parameter-free SVD-based techniques (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) reducing need for parameter tuning.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification when selecting high-quality topics.

- Strength 1: Method is technically sound, aligns with human intuition, uses Pearson correlation for evaluation, and outperforms baselines on NYT and 20NG with TBuckets-SVD-Reorg best.

- Strength 2: SVD-based methods require no tuning, easing practical adoption and outperforming tuned state-of-the-art methods.

- Strength 3: Practical application shows selecting high-quality topics boosts classification on 3/4 datasets, notably on PC vs MAC.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—core idea resembles prior semantic similarity grouping methods and not clearly distinguished from prior work.

- Weakness 2: Reorganization step lacks ablation/analysis to isolate its contribution and characterize which topics benefit.

- Weakness 3: Questionable "parameter-free" claim since reorganization uses a dynamic threshold from AWPS/AWES that may implicitly involve parameters.

- Weakness 4: Comparison set is limited (mainly Röder et al. 2015); lacks comparison with more recent coherence measures.

- Question 1: How does TBuckets fundamentally differ from existing semantic similarity-based coherence measures (e.g., Aletras & Stevenson 2013)?

- Question 2: Please clarify the "parameter-free" claim given the reorganization threshold calculation—how does it avoid parameter tuning?

- Question 3: Can you analyze which topic characteristics produce the largest classification gains when using high-quality topics, especially for PC vs MAC?
