

This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach for measuring topic coherence in LDA-based topic models by grouping topic words into thematic buckets. The core contribution is the intuitive concept of using bucket distribution (specifically the size of the largest bucket) as a coherence indicator, which aligns with human assessment of topic quality. The authors develop three techniques: TBuckets-Clustering (agglomerative clustering), TBuckets-SVD (using SVD to identify orthogonal sub-themes), and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (adding a reorganization step to improve SVD results). The main contributions, in order of strength, are: (1) the TBuckets concept itself as a coherent measure of topic quality; (2) the parameter-free nature of the SVD-based techniques; and (3) the practical application to weakly supervised text classification.

Strengths: First, the TBuckets concept is intuitively compelling and directly addresses how humans assess topic coherence, making it a theoretically sound contribution. Second, the parameter-free SVD-based methods (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) represent a significant practical advantage over state-of-the-art methods that require extensive parameter tuning, as evidenced by their superior performance on two out of three datasets. Third, the text classification application demonstrates tangible utility by showing that using TBuckets to select high-quality topics improves classification performance by up to 4.2% on difficult datasets like PC vs MAC.

Weaknesses: First, the paper claims TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is "completely parameter free," but the reorganization threshold depends on topic-specific calculations (AWPS and AWES), which constitutes implicit parameter tuning that isn't properly addressed. Second, the comparison with state-of-the-art methods is limited to the metrics reported in Roder et al. (2015), without comparing against the full suite of coherence measures they proposed. Third, the text classification results show only modest improvements (up to 4.2%) and don't provide sufficient analysis of why the improvement occurs, particularly for the most challenging datasets.

Questions to Authors: 1) How exactly is TBuckets-SVD-Reorg truly parameter-free given the topic-specific threshold calculation? 2) Could you provide a more detailed comparison with the most recent coherence measures beyond those reported in Table 1? 3) What is the relationship between the coherence score threshold and classification performance improvement in the text classification experiments?
