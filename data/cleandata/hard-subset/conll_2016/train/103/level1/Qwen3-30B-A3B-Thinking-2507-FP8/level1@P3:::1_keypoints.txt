- Paper proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets to measure topic coherence; claims three contributions (bucketing, parameter-free SVD methods, utility for weakly supervised classification).  
- Reviewer sees parameter-free TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg as the most significant contribution; bucketing core idea has limited novelty.  

Strengths:
- TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are parameter-free SVD-based techniques, offering practical advantages over methods that need tuning.  
- Demonstrated real-world utility: selecting high-quality topics with TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification performance.  
- Rigorous experimental evaluation across three datasets with correlation to human judgments and downstream classification results.  

Weaknesses:
- Core bucketing idea resembles prior semantic grouping/coherence measures and lacks clear novelty or differentiation.  
- Reorganization step uses an ad hoc dynamic threshold (AWES - AWPS*AWES) without sufficient justification or comparison to simpler alternatives.  
- Paper omits discussion of computational complexity; SVD-based methods may be far more expensive than baselines.  
- Limited discussion of limitations and generalization, e.g., handling multimodal topics or domain-specific word similarity (Genomics results lag).  

Questions for authors:
- How is the reorganization threshold truly parameter-free, and why couldn’t the same threshold logic be used for other coherence measures?  
- Why does TBuckets perform worse on Genomics; how would the method adapt to domain-specific word representations?  
- What is the computational complexity of TBuckets-SVD in practice (e.g., for n=100), and is it practical compared to O(n^2) baselines like CUCI/CUMASS?
