- TBuckets-SVD-Reorg: a parameter-free technique that achieves state-of-the-art coherence on 2 of 3 datasets.  
- New conceptual framework: measures topic coherence by grouping words into semantic buckets instead of using co-occurrence or vector similarity.  
- Practical utility: using TBuckets to select topics improves weakly supervised text classification performance.  

- Parameter-free measurement: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms tuned baselines without parameter tuning, a practical advantage.  
- Strong empirical validation: consistent Pearson correlations with human scores across three datasets, outperforming prior work on NYT and 20NG.  
- Real-world application: selecting high-quality topics via TBuckets yields better classification on most evaluated datasets.  
- Conceptual novelty: thematic word-buckets align more directly with human judgments of topic coherence.  

- Limited technical novelty: core methods (SVD, clustering) are established and the paper doesn’t clearly separate its innovation from standard uses.  
- Insufficient ablation studies: unclear how much improvement comes from reorganization versus the SVD component.  
- Limited evaluation scope: only three relatively small datasets with 100 topics each, so generalizability is uncertain.  
- Incomplete baseline comparison: omits more recent coherence measures beyond Roder et al. (2015).  

- Clarification needed: why the reorganization dynamic threshold (AWPS/AWES) isn’t considered parameter tuning.  
- Clarification needed: which specific reorganization elements drive the modest gains over SVD alone.  
- Clarification needed: how TBuckets compares to other coherence-based topic selection methods in weakly supervised classification.
