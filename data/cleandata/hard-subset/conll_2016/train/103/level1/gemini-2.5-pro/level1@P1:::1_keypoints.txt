- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, which groups a topic's top words into semantic "buckets" using pre-trained embeddings and three methods (agglomerative, SVD, SVD-Reorg) to score topic coherence; validated by correlation with human judgments and improved model selection for weakly supervised classification.

- Main contribution 1: Novel, parameter-free SVD-based TBuckets (and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that uses SVD on word-embedding matrices to identify latent sub-themes and bucket words.

- Main contribution 2: Demonstrates practical utility by showing TBuckets-based model selection improves weakly supervised text classification performance.

- Strength 1: Novel intuition — bucketing and SVD-derived orthogonal "sub-themes" offer a fresh, group-based take on coherence beyond pairwise similarity.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results — TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG benchmarks.

- Strength 3: Parameter-free design — avoids hyperparameter tuning common in other coherence measures, increasing robustness and usability.

- Strength 4: Downstream validation — filtering models by TBuckets coherence yields tangible improvements in a real classification task, supporting practical relevance.

- Weakness 1: Reorganization step unclear and seemingly ad-hoc — the dynamic threshold formula lacks theoretical justification and needs clearer explanation or ablation.

- Weakness 2: Reliance on pre-trained embeddings — using only GloVe (Wikipedia/Gigaword) may limit performance on domain-specific corpora; sensitivity to embedding choice not explored.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation limited to top-10 words per topic — no analysis of sensitivity to different N (e.g., 15 or 20), which could affect coherence scoring.

- Weakness 4: Incomplete comparison details — paper cites Röder et al. (2015) best settings but lacks specifics or conceptual positioning relative to the measures it outperforms.

- Question 1: Please justify the dynamic threshold formula in SVD-Reorg and report any experiments with alternative formulations or ablations.

- Question 2: Can you explain why in POLITICS vs RELIGION the S1 baseline slightly outperforms the "Highest 5 Coh. sets" despite the section's central claim?

- Question 3: Why does PC vs MAC show higher accuracy for the "Lowest 5 Coh. sets" than the S1 baseline — could selecting worst topics sometimes improve downstream performance?
