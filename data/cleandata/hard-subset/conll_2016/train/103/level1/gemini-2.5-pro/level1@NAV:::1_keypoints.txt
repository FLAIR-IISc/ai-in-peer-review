- Proposes TBuckets: measure topic coherence by embedding topic words and grouping them into “buckets”; coherence = most words in one large bucket.  
- Introduces three bucketing techniques: agglomerative clustering and two parameter-free SVD-based methods (TBuckets-SVD and SVD-Reorg, the latter with a reorganization step).  
- Evaluates methods against human judgments on three standard datasets (NYT, 20NG, Genomics) and uses TBuckets to improve a downstream weakly supervised text classification task.  
- Contribution: a novel, intuitive SVD-based framework (TBuckets-SVD and SVD-Reorg) that is parameter-free and achieves state-of-the-art coherence measurement.  
- Contribution: demonstrates practical utility by showing topic selection via TBuckets improves weakly supervised classification performance.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior methods on two of three benchmarks (NYT, 20NG).  
- Strength: Parameter-free SVD methods remove costly dataset-dependent tuning, improving robustness and usability.  
- Strength: Validated usefulness in a downstream application, showing real-world benefit beyond intrinsic evaluation.  
- Weakness: Dynamic threshold formula in SVD-Reorg lacks derivation or analysis and appears ad-hoc; needs stronger justification or ablation.  
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on external pre-trained embeddings; the impact of different or domain-specific embeddings (notably for Genomics) is not explored.  
- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to top-10 topic words; no analysis of how performance varies with different numbers of topic words (e.g., 15 or 20).
