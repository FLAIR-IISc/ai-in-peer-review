- Proposes TBuckets: group a topic's top words into semantic "buckets" using word embeddings and score coherence by the largest bucket's properties.
- Presents three bucketing techniques: agglomerative clustering, SVD, and SVD with a reorganization step.
- Evaluates TBuckets by Pearson correlation with human coherence judgments on three benchmark datasets.
- Demonstrates practical utility by using TBuckets to select coherent topic models for weakly supervised text classification, improving accuracy.
- Contribution: introduces an intuitive, non-pairwise framework that mimics human grouping of theme words.
- Contribution: uses SVD on word-embedding matrices to identify orthogonal sub-themes as bucket prototypes.
- Contribution: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg attains state-of-the-art correlations on NYT and 20NG datasets.
- Contribution: a largely parameter-free SVD-based approach, reducing tuning compared to PMI/co-occurrence methods.
- Strength: strong novelty and clear intuition for partitioning topic words into thematic buckets.
- Strength: compelling quantitative results (e.g., Pearson r ≈ 0.84 on NYT, 0.87 on 20NG).
- Strength: parameter-free design enhances practical applicability across settings.
- Strength: demonstrates downstream benefit—selecting topics by TBuckets improves classification F1.
- Weakness: reorganization dynamic threshold formula (AWES - AWPS/AWES) is heuristic and unexplained.
- Weakness: no justification for weighting coherence by the square of the highest singular value.
- Weakness: unclear process for selecting/using SVD eigenvectors (what is n?).
- Weakness: ambiguous reorganization condition (definition and computation of OBPW vs per-bucket vs global unclear).
- Weakness: heavy reliance on pre-trained embeddings (single GloVe set); sensitivity to embedding choice and domain mismatch not explored.
- Weakness: lower performance on Genomics suggests embedding-domain mismatch and insufficient analysis of this issue.
- Weakness: experiments only use top-10 words; no analysis of TBuckets' behavior for other topic sizes (N).
- Question: please explain the derivation and intuition behind the dynamic threshold formula and alternatives considered.
- Question: justify the singular-value-squared weighting and report ablations without this weighting.
- Question: clarify whether S2's improved classification stems from a larger model pool and propose fairer comparisons (e.g., matching model counts for S1).
