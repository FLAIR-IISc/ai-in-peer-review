Summary and contributions
This paper proposes TBuckets, a family of topic coherence measures that group the top-N words of an LDA topic into “buckets” using word embeddings. The intuition is that a coherent topic should yield one dominant bucket. Three implementations are presented: (i) agglomerative clustering over embeddings (TBuckets-Clustering), (ii) SVD over the word-embedding matrix with assignment of words to the most similar right-singular vector (TBuckets-SVD), and (iii) SVD followed by a heuristic reorganization step (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg). Topic coherence is then scored from properties of the largest bucket. Evaluations report Pearson correlation with human ratings on three datasets, and a downstream use case for weakly supervised classification via topic labeling.

As I see it, the main contributions (strongest first) are:
1) A simple, embedding-based operationalization of “single-theme dominance” via largest-bucket coverage, with an SVD-based, largely parameter-free instantiation that performs competitively with, and sometimes better than, strong baselines (Röder et al., 2015) on two datasets.
2) A reorganization heuristic that leverages word–word and word–eigenvector similarities to correct misassignments from SVD bucketing.
3) A small but concrete application demonstrating that selecting high-coherence topic sets (per TBuckets) can improve weakly supervised text classification performance.

Strengths
- Empirical competitiveness and simplicity: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg attains r=0.837 (NYT) and r≈0.869 (20NG), exceeding Röder et al.’s best (0.806 and 0.859 respectively), and is close on Genomics. The method is straightforward to implement, computationally light for small N (e.g., N=10), and uses off-the-shelf embeddings.
- Intuitive formulation aligned with human judgment: The “largest bucket” notion captures the human tendency to perceive a single dominant theme as coherence. The examples provided illustrate how bucket distributions reflect semantic structure.
- Avoids heavy parameter tuning: The SVD variants avoid explicit hyperparameters (in contrast to many coherence configurations in Röder et al., 2015), and the reorganization threshold is dynamically derived from topic-level statistics rather than tuned on a dev set.
- Useful downstream signal: In topic-labeling classification, selecting the top-5 topic sets (per TBuckets) modestly improves macro-F1 on 3/4 binary 20NG tasks and clearly separates top vs. bottom coherence sets (e.g., PC-MAC: 68.47 vs. 67.96/67.96 and 68.47 vs. 67.96; and large gaps between top vs. lowest in several pairs), supporting practical utility for triaging topics before annotation.
- Complements existing families of measures: TBuckets offers a geometric, bucket-based perspective distinct from PMI/NPMI- or conditional-probability-based measures, and may capture phenomena missed by purely co-occurrence-based metrics.

Weaknesses
- Missing strong baselines and ablations: The paper does not compare against simple embedding baselines such as average pairwise cosine, centroid tightness, or principal-component concentration (e.g., proportion of variance explained by PC1). Given that TBuckets relies on embeddings and SVD, such baselines are necessary to establish that bucketization adds value beyond standard similarity or PCA statistics.
- Methodological ambiguities in SVD assignment: For A ∈ R^{n×d}, V contains eigenvectors of A^T A (d×d). The paper proposes taking “the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers” and assigning each word to its most similar eigenvector. This raises questions: why n buckets rather than selecting k by explained variance or data-driven criteria? Is cosine similarity signed or absolute? How are degenerate components handled? Without these details, reproducibility and theoretical grounding are limited.
- Heuristic reorganization lacks principled justification: The into/from rules and the dynamic threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES?) are ad hoc, with unclear derivation and a typographical ambiguity. It is not obvious why this specific combination should generalize, nor is there a sensitivity analysis to these choices.
- Evaluation and significance: Reported improvements over Röder et al. are small (e.g., 0.869 vs. 0.859) and no significance testing for dependent correlations (e.g., Steiger’s test with Fisher z) is provided. Similarly, classification gains are modest and sometimes negative (POLITICS vs RELIGION), with no confidence intervals; differences may be within run-to-run variance.
- Parameter tuning on clustering appears to use gold: TBuckets-Clustering explores Max Dist values with performance plotted and reports the best per-dataset. If tuned against human ratings on the test sets, this inflates performance. A proper tuning protocol (dev split or cross-validation) is needed.
- Limited robustness analysis: Results are reported for N=10 words only. Coherence behavior can change with N (e.g., 5, 15, 20), different embeddings (word2vec vs. GloVe), or domains (notably Genomics where performance lags). No OOV handling, preprocessing, or embedding coverage analysis is reported.

Questions to the authors
- SVD design: Why choose the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers? Did you try choosing k by cumulative explained variance (e.g., 80–95%) or via scree plot/elbow? How sensitive are results to k?
- Similarity details: Is cosine computed with sign (i.e., can negative similarity lead to assignment)? Did you try absolute cosine for eigenvectors? How are ties resolved?
- Reorganization threshold: Please clarify the exact formula and rationale (parentheses/normalization). Did you perform sensitivity checks or ablations with fixed thresholds?
- Baselines: How does TBuckets compare to (a) average pairwise cosine among topic words, (b) cosine of words to the centroid, (c) variance explained by PC1, and (d) Aletras and Stevenson’s embedding-based coherence using topic-word-only contexts?
- Statistical testing: Are the correlation improvements over Röder et al. statistically significant? Please report confidence intervals or tests for dependent correlations. Likewise for classification macro-F1 differences.

Additional comments
- Clarify handling of OOV words in GloVe; report embedding coverage per dataset and its effect on topics with missing vectors.
- Provide pseudocode and release code to ensure reproducibility (including clustering linkage, distance threshold selection protocol, and SVD implementation details).
- Consider reporting robustness across different N (top-5/10/20 words) and different embedding sources. This may also explain the weaker Genomics results and guide domain-specific embeddings.
- The WL weighting 1/log(i+1) is plausible but arbitrary; an ablation of different positional weightings (or none) would help.
- Minor typos and index errors (e.g., “Bucket-1: {storm, weather}” in the second example; ambiguous threshold equation) should be corrected for clarity.
