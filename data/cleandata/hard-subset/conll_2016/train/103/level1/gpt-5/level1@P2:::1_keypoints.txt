- Proposes TBuckets: embedding-based methods that group top-N topic words into “buckets” to measure topic coherence by dominance of a single bucket.  
- Presents three instantiations: agglomerative clustering, SVD-based assignment to ATA eigenvectors, and SVD with an automatic reorganization heuristic.  
- Scores coherence from bucket properties (size, LDA-order weighting, eigenvector similarity) with SVD scores scaled by the largest singular value.  
- Evaluated by Pearson correlation with human annotations on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics, and applied to weakly supervised topic-labeling model selection.  

Contributions:
- Introduces a simple, intuitive bucketization framework including a parameter-free SVD variant and a reorganization heuristic.  
- Empirical evidence that SVD-Reorg matches or outperforms baselines on NYT and 20NG and is competitive elsewhere.  
- Shows practical utility: selecting topic models by TBuckets can improve weakly supervised topic-labeling classification.  

Strengths:
- Intuitive “largest bucket” coherence signal aligning with human judgments and presented with illustrative examples.  
- Competitive performance with minimal tuning compared to prior work requiring extensive configuration search.  
- Clever use of ATA eigenvectors as orthogonal sub-theme directions and an effective reorganization step.  
- Demonstrates downstream utility: higher TBuckets coherence correlates with better labeling accuracy in most settings.  
- Reorganization threshold is parameter-free and adapts to topic difficulty via average similarity comparisons.  

Weaknesses:
- Novelty is limited relative to prior embedding-based coherence methods; conceptual advance is incremental.  
- Several heuristic design choices (largest-bucket focus, LDA-order weighting, EVS term, singular-value weighting) lack theoretical justification or ablation studies.  
- Evaluation limited in scope and statistical rigor: only Pearson on three 100-topic datasets, no significance tests, no Spearman, and sensitivity to N not analyzed.  
- Reproducibility concerns: OOV handling, preprocessing, exact similarity metrics, SVD/clustering details, and no code release.  
- Clarity and minor errors: ambiguous threshold formula, typos, late notation, and weak explanation for preferring V over U.  

Questions for authors:
- Provide statistical significance, confidence intervals, and Spearman correlations; are gains over Röder et al. significant?  
- Run ablations: largest-bucket only; without EVS/WL; without singular-value weighting; SVD without reorg; compare to mean pairwise cosine baseline.  
- Clarify OOV handling, exact similarity function (cosine vs. dot), normalization, and fraction of missing embeddings per dataset.  
- For Genomics, provide in-domain embeddings or error analysis to explain performance drop and reorg’s role.  
- Specify SVD assignment details: number of eigenvectors used, behavior when n<d, sign ambiguity handling, and sensitivity to these choices.  
- Clarify and justify reorganization threshold formula (is it AWES − (AWPS/AWES) or (AWES − AWPS)/AWES?) and show sensitivity/scale invariance.  
- Report runtime and scalability: average time per topic for SVD and reorg (e.g., N=10, d=300) and behavior when N increases.  

Overall assessment:
- Clear, practical idea with competitive results and a useful downstream demo, but incremental novelty, heuristic choices without ablations, and limited statistical rigor weaken the submission.  
- Likely borderline reject in current form but promising if authors add significance tests, ablations, clarity on SVD/threshold/OOV handling, and stronger treatment of Genomics domain mismatch.
