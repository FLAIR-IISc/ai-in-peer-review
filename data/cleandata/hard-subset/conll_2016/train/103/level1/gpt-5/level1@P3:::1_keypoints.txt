- Proposes TBuckets: group top-N topic words into embedding-based “buckets”; coherence = dominance of a single bucket.
- Implements three variants: agglomerative clustering (TBuckets-Clustering), SVD bucketing (TBuckets-SVD), and SVD with heuristic reorganization (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg).
- Scores coherence from properties of the largest bucket; evaluates Pearson correlation with human ratings on three datasets and a weakly supervised classification use case.
- Main contribution: simple embedding-based operationalization of “single-theme dominance” with a largely parameter-free SVD instantiation that is often competitive with prior baselines.
- Contribution: a reorganization heuristic that uses word–word and word–eigenvector similarities to correct SVD misassignments.
- Contribution: application showing selecting high-coherence topics per TBuckets can modestly improve weakly supervised classification.
- Strength: empirical competitiveness and simplicity—TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves high correlations (e.g., r=0.837 NYT, r≈0.869 20NG) and is inexpensive for small N.
- Strength: intuitive formulation—largest-bucket notion aligns with human judgments and examples illustrate semantic bucket structure.
- Strength: avoids heavy parameter tuning—SVD variants are mostly parameter-free and reorg threshold is derived from topic statistics.
- Strength: useful downstream signal—selecting top-coherence topics improves macro-F1 in several binary 20NG tasks and separates top vs. bottom topic sets.
- Strength: complements existing coherence families by providing a geometric, bucket-based perspective distinct from co-occurrence measures.
- Weakness: missing simple embedding baselines (average pairwise cosine, centroid tightness, PC1 variance) to show bucketization adds value.
- Weakness: SVD assignment is ambiguous—why choose the first n eigenvectors, how is similarity computed (signed/absolute), and how are degenerate components handled?
- Weakness: reorganization heuristic is ad hoc with unclear derivation, a typographical ambiguity in the threshold formula, and no sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: evaluation lacks statistical testing—reported gains over Röder et al. are small and no significance/confidence intervals are provided.
- Weakness: clustering parameter tuning may use gold labels or test-set performance without a proper dev protocol, risking inflated results.
- Weakness: limited robustness analysis—results only for N=10, no tests across different N, embeddings (word2vec/GloVe), domains, or OOV handling/coverage.
- Question: Why select the first n eigenvectors as buckets; did you try k selection by explained variance or elbow methods and how sensitive are results to k?
- Question: Is cosine similarity computed with sign or absolute value for eigenvector assignment, and how are ties resolved?
- Question: Clarify the reorganization threshold formula and provide sensitivity checks or ablations with fixed thresholds.
- Question: Please compare TBuckets to baselines: average pairwise cosine, centroid cosine, PC1 variance explained, and prior embedding-based coherence methods.
- Question: Are correlation improvements statistically significant (e.g., Steiger’s test) and do classification F1 differences have confidence intervals?
- Additional: Clarify OOV handling for embeddings, report embedding coverage per dataset, and analyze impact of missing vectors on topics.
- Additional: Provide pseudocode and release code (clustering linkage, distance threshold protocol, SVD details) to ensure reproducibility.
- Additional: Report robustness across different top-N values and embedding sources to explain domain differences (e.g., Genomics).
- Additional: Ablate positional WL weighting (1/log(i+1)) and other weighting choices to justify design.
- Additional: Fix minor typos/index errors and ambiguous examples/equations for clarity.
