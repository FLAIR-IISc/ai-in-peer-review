- Proposes TBuckets: group top-N topic words into embedding-based “buckets” and score coherence by the size of the largest bucket, with three instantiations (agglomerative, SVD bucketing, parameter-free reorganization).

- Evaluated via Pearson correlation with human judgments on three datasets and used to select topics for weakly supervised text classification, showing improved performance.

- Contribution: a simple, largely parameter-free SVD-based coherence estimator (TBuckets-SVD and -SVD-Reorg) that correlates strongly with humans and outperforms prior best on 2/3 datasets.

- Contribution: introduces a pragmatic “largest-bucket” coherence paradigm that operationalizes single-theme intuition using an SVD-driven implementation and dynamic reorganization heuristic.

- Contribution: demonstrates that selecting topic sets with higher TBuckets coherence improves weakly supervised classification on 20NG subsets.

- Strength: strong empirical correlations with minimal tuning (e.g., TBuckets-SVD-Reorg r=0.837 NYT, 0.869 20NG), appealing for practitioners.

- Strength: clear, intuitive rationale matching human judgments; examples show detection of misplacements and benefits from reorganization.

- Strength: computationally simple and scalable (SVD on small n×d matrices and cosine ops), easy to integrate into topic pipelines.

- Strength: application evidence—selecting top topic runs by TBuckets yields equal or better macro-F1 in most tasks and large best-vs-worst gaps.

- Weakness: limited novelty vs prior embedding/distributional coherence methods; lacks ablation against centroid or k-means baselines.

- Weakness: methodological ambiguities and ad hoc choices (SVD assignment justification, unclear reorganization threshold formula, unexplained singular-value weighting).

- Weakness: domain mismatch and robustness concerns—substantial performance drop on Genomics likely due to general-purpose embeddings and OOV/coverage issues.

- Weakness: incomplete comparative evaluation and no statistical significance testing; missing comparisons to recent embedding-based coherence measures and pairwise cosine baselines.

- Weakness: application study design could be tightened—selection doubles induction cost, gains are small/negative in some cases, and annotation/run details are sparse.

- Question: clarify the reorganization threshold formula, its sensitivity, and whether alternatives (fixed margin, z-normalization) were tried.

- Question: in SVD bucketing, specify whether cosine similarity to eigenvectors is used, how sign ambiguity is handled, and relation to A V = U S projections.

- Question: why weight coherence by the squared top singular value and were other scalings (none, linear) compared?

- Question: how many topic words N were used and how does performance vary with N (e.g., 5, 10, 20)?

- Question: for Genomics, what fraction of topic words lacked good embeddings and would domain-specific embeddings mitigate the drop?

- Additional: report statistical significance for correlation comparisons (e.g., Williams/Steiger tests).

- Additional: fix minor typos and examples in the manuscript.

- Additional: add a simple centroid-similarity or average pairwise cosine baseline to contextualize SVD/Reorg gains.
