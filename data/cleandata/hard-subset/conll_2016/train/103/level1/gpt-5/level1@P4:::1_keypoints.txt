- Proposes TBuckets: grouping top-N LDA words into semantic “buckets” using embeddings; coherent topics should occupy a single dominant bucket.
- Introduces three TBuckets variants: Clustering (agglomerative with distance threshold), SVD (assign words to ATA eigenvectors), and SVD-Reorg (parameter-free heuristic moving words based on similarities).
- Coherence computed from the largest bucket using size, LDA word-order weighting, and (for SVD variants) word–eigenvector similarity, optionally weighted by top singular value squared.
- Evaluated via Pearson correlation with human judgments on 20NG, NYT, and Genomics; SVD-Reorg beats Röder et al. on 2/3 datasets and is competitive on the third.
- Demonstrates downstream utility: selecting high-TBuckets runs improves weakly supervised classification macro-F1 on 20NG subsets.
- Contribution: simple embedding-based coherence framework operationalizing “single-theme dominance,” including a parameter-free SVD reorganization.
- Contribution: empirical results showing competitive or superior correlations to a strong baseline and practical use in run selection for classification.
- Contribution: interpretable bucketing that yields human-readable groupings of topic words.
- Strength: Clear intuition and interpretability with step-by-step examples and inspectable bucket outputs.
- Strength: Competitive performance with minimal tuning; SVD variants match or surpass best Röder configurations without heavy hyperparameter searches.
- Strength: Practical relevance—TBuckets-based selection improves downstream classification performance measurably.
- Strength: Simplicity and efficiency—operates on top-10 words with pre-trained embeddings and cheap SVD on small matrices.
- Strength: Thorough exploration of variants and ablations (clustering vs SVD, multiple scoring schemes, sensitivity analysis for clustering threshold).
- Weakness: Exposition issues—typos, inconsistent dry-run examples, and ambiguous notation (e.g., reorganization threshold formula); weak justification for squaring the top singular value.
- Weakness: Potentially unfair/tuned comparisons—Clustering Max Dist may be tuned per dataset; no statistical significance testing; baseline numbers taken from literature rather than rerun.
- Weakness: Domain dependence—SVD variants underperform on Genomics likely due to embedding mismatch; no OOV quantification or domain-specific embedding tests.
- Weakness: Method choices lack stronger justification—use of ATA eigenvectors as bucket IDs, WL definition, singular-value weighting, and stability of SVD-Reorg not theoretically motivated or fully ablated.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—only Pearson’s r reported, no rank-based metrics like Spearman, no sensitivity analysis for N (top words), and missing reproducibility details (code, preprocessing, OOV handling).
- Question: Clarify the exact reorganization threshold formula and provide ablations showing its effect versus a constant threshold.
- Question: Why use the first n eigenvectors of ATA as bucket identifiers instead of U or a k chosen by explained variance; report sensitivity to number of eigenvectors.
- Question: Motivation and ablation for singular-value squared weighting; do results hold without it?
- Question: How were Clustering Max Dist values chosen—were they tuned on test sets; can results be shown with a fixed validation-chosen threshold?
- Question: Provide statistical significance (confidence intervals or p-values) for improvements over Röder et al.
- Question: Report OOV rates per dataset and test domain-specific or alternative embeddings (word2vec, fastText, contextual) for Genomics.
- Question: How does performance vary with number of top words per topic (e.g., N=5,15,20)?
- Additional comment: Report Spearman’s rho in addition to Pearson’s r.
- Additional comment: Release code and exact preprocessing details to improve reproducibility.
- Additional comment: Correct typographical errors in bucket examples and standardize notation.
