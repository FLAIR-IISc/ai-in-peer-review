- Proposes TBuckets, a novel topic coherence measure that groups topic words into thematic buckets using word embeddings.  
- Core idea: coherent topics produce a single large bucket, an intuitive measure of topic quality.  
- Introduces SVD-based bucketing (basic and reorganized) that is essentially parameter-free and improves performance.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art coherence measures on three public datasets.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification.  
- Strong empirical results: consistent higher Pearson correlation with human judgments across datasets.  
- Parameter-free SVD methods reduce the need for tuning compared to many existing measures.  
- Methodology is clear and intuitive, with helpful example walkthroughs of the bucketing process.  
- Shows downstream relevance by linking coherence improvements to better classification performance.  
- Provides a comprehensive related-work overview situating TBuckets within the literature.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of what properties of the largest bucket (e.g., pairwise similarity, embedding variance) drive human-perceived coherence.  
- Relies on pre-trained embeddings (GloVe), so performance may be affected by embedding quality, biases, or domain mismatch.  
- Reorganization heuristic uses somewhat arbitrary thresholds (AWES - AWPS) with limited explanation or exploration of alternatives.  
- Scalability concerns: computational cost (notably SVD) and performance on large vocabularies/topics are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Asks for deeper analysis of largest-bucket properties correlating with human judgments (e.g., average similarity, variance).  
- Requests experiments with alternative or fine-tuned embeddings (Word2Vec, fastText) and their impact on results.  
- Seeks clarification on computational complexity and scaling of TBuckets, especially the SVD step.  
- Requests rationale and empirical basis for the dynamic threshold formula used in the reorganization step and exploration of other strategies.  
- Notes that calling SVD techniques “completely parameter free” is misleading because embedding dimensionality (e.g., d=300) is a parameter.  
- Suggests visualizations of formed buckets for example topics to aid understanding.  
- Recommends adding discussion of recent neural topic models and their coherence measures to the related-work section.
