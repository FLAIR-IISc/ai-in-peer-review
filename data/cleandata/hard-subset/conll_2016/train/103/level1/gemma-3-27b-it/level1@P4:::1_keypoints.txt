- Proposes TBuckets: measure topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets—coherent topics yield one large bucket, incoherent topics fragment into many small ones.  
- Implements three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD with reorganization (SVD variants are parameter-free).  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art on three public datasets and helps weakly supervised text classification.  
- Novel perspective that evaluates coherence via bucket formation rather than PMI or other standard proxies.  
- Parameter-free SVD methods (TBuckets-SVD & TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) reduce the need for tuning.  
- Strong empirical results and demonstrated downstream utility for classification.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-structured, and experimentally detailed.  
- Reorganization step is insufficiently explained; criteria (NBPW, NBPE, LBPW, OBPW, thresholds) are complex and lack intuitive justification.  
- Heavy dependence on pre-trained word embedding quality; sensitivity to embedding choice and domain mismatch not investigated.  
- Comparisons are limited mainly to Roder et al. (2015); lacks evaluation against more recent coherence measures.  
- Computational complexity and scalability (especially of SVD methods) are not discussed.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis and concrete examples of formed buckets for success/failure cases.  
- Requests clearer, more intuitive explanation and visualization of bucket reorganization and sensitivity to thresholding.  
- Requests experiments showing impact of different pre-trained embeddings on TBuckets’ performance.  
- Requests analysis of computational complexity and empirical runtimes across dataset sizes.  
- Requests qualitative examples of topics where TBuckets succeeds and where it fails.
