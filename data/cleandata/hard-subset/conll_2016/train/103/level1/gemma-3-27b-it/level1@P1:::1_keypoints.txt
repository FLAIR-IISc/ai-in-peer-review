- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into semantic "buckets" to measure LDA topic quality.  
- Explores three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.  
- Claims TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art coherence measures on three public datasets.  
- Demonstrates TBuckets’ utility in improving weakly supervised text classification.  
- TBuckets offers a novel, semantics-driven perspective on topic coherence beyond co-occurrence/distributional methods.  
- Empirical results show TBuckets-SVD-Reorg has strong correlation with human judgments across datasets.  
- SVD-based methods are parameter-free, simplifying use and reducing tuning needs.  
- Shows practical value by improving classification accuracy in a downstream weakly supervised task.  
- Paper provides clear, detailed explanations and an illustrative bucket-creation walkthrough.  
- TBuckets’ performance depends on pre-trained word embeddings; robustness to different embeddings isn’t analyzed.  
- Reorganization step uses heuristic thresholds (AWPS, AWES) that lack strong theoretical justification.  
- Downstream evaluation is limited to one setting (weak supervision with LPA-TD), restricting generalizability claims.  
- No ablation study to quantify contributions of SVD vs. reorganization components.  
- Experimental results lack reported variances or confidence intervals to assess statistical significance.  
- Asks how sensitive TBuckets is to embedding choice and whether alternatives (Word2Vec, fastText) were tried.  
- Requests justification for the dynamic reorganization threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES) and its intuition.  
- Inquires about methods for selecting the optimal number of buckets beyond implicit SVD determination.  
- Seeks information on computational costs of each TBuckets technique, especially at large scale.  
- Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.  
- Paper is generally well-written, easy to understand, and clearly motivated.
