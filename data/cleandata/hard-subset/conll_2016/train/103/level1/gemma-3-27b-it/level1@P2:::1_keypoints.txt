- Core idea: TBuckets groups topic words into semantic “buckets” using word embeddings; coherent topics produce one large bucket.

- Contribution: Novel coherence measure framing topic quality as a bucketing problem using embeddings, offering a semantic alternative to co-occurrence methods.

- Contribution: Parameter-free SVD-based TBuckets variants reduce tuning needs and increase practical usability.

- Contribution: TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification, demonstrating downstream utility.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated approach linking bucket structure to topic coherence.

- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation on three public datasets using Pearson correlation with human judgments.

- Strength: Thorough, reproducible descriptions of clustering, SVD, and SVD-with-reorganization techniques.

- Strength: Practical advantage of parameter-free methods emphasized.

- Strength: Demonstrated improvement on a downstream NLP task strengthens practical relevance.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — components (embeddings, SVD, clustering) are established; position as incremental improvement needed.

- Weakness: Reliance on pre-trained embeddings; sensitivity to embedding choice or domain-specific training not explored.

- Weakness: SVD-Reorg adds complexity via a dynamic threshold that could be simplified and better justified.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of failure cases and examples where TBuckets disagrees with human judgments.

- Weakness: Paper is somewhat verbose in background/related work and could be more concise.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of pre-trained embeddings or to training embeddings on the target corpus?

- Question: Can you provide a more intuitive, simplified example of the dynamic threshold in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?

- Question: Can you show topics where TBuckets performs poorly and explain why?

- Question: How does computational cost scale with topic model size and vocabulary, especially for SVD-Reorg?

- Additional: Reference list is comprehensive.

- Additional: Experimental results are clearly presented in tables.

- Additional: Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
