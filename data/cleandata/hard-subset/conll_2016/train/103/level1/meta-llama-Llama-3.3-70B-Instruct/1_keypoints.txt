- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, which groups topic words into thematic buckets (via clustering, SVD, or SVD+reorg) to compute a novel topic coherence score and shows improved results on three datasets plus utility for weakly supervised text classification.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a new bucket-based perspective for measuring LDA topic coherence, differing from co-occurrence or semantic-similarity methods.

- Contribution 2: Proposes three bucket-creation techniques—clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization—offering multiple implementation options.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on two of three evaluated datasets, suggesting TBuckets’ effectiveness.

- Strength 1: Provides a novel and original approach to topic coherence measurement.

- Strength 2: Empirically effective at measuring topic coherence according to reported evaluations.

- Strength 3: Shows potential practical utility for weakly supervised text classification.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to only three datasets, raising concerns about generalizability.

- Weakness 2: Claims about no parameter tuning for TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg lack clarity on robustness to different parameter settings.

- Weakness 3: Comparison set is narrow, using only one state-of-the-art baseline, limiting comparative validation.

- Question 1: How will TBuckets be extended to applications beyond text classification?

- Question 2: What impact do choices of word embeddings have on TBuckets’ performance?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to broaden evaluation to better establish TBuckets’ generalizability?
