**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. The approach groups topic words into thematic buckets, where a single large bucket signifies a single coherent theme, indicating high topic coherence. TBuckets uses three techniques for creating buckets of words: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD), and SVD with reorganization. The approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets, and the results show that TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art techniques. Additionally, the paper demonstrates the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to measure topic coherence**: TBuckets proposes a new way to measure topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic buckets, which is different from existing approaches that rely on word co-occurrence or distributional semantics.
2. **Three techniques for creating buckets of words**: The paper presents three techniques for creating buckets of words, including clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization, which provides a comprehensive evaluation of different methods.
3. **Evaluation on publicly available datasets**: The paper evaluates TBuckets on three publicly available datasets, providing a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art techniques.

**Strengths**

1. **TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art techniques**: The results show that TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art techniques on two out of three datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
2. **Completely parameter-free SVD-based techniques**: The SVD-based techniques, TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, require no parameter tuning, making them more practical and efficient.
3. **Usefulness for weakly supervised text classification**: The paper demonstrates the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification, providing a new application for the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation on a specific task**: The paper only evaluates TBuckets on the task of measuring topic coherence and weakly supervised text classification. It would be beneficial to evaluate the approach on other tasks, such as topic modeling or document classification.
2. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The paper does not provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed approach, which would help to understand the underlying mechanisms and limitations of TBuckets.
3. **Dependence on word embeddings**: TBuckets relies on word embeddings, which may not always be available or accurate for specific domains or languages.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend TBuckets to other tasks, such as topic modeling or document classification?
2. Can you provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed approach, including the limitations and potential biases of TBuckets?
3. How do you plan to address the dependence on word embeddings, and what alternatives can be used when word embeddings are not available or accurate?
