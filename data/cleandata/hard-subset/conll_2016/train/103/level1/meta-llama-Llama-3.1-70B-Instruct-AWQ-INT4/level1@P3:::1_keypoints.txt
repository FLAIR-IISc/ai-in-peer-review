Summary:
- Proposes TBuckets to measure LDA topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets; a single large bucket indicates high coherence.
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques (clustering-based, SVD, SVD with reorganization) and evaluates on three public datasets, showing TBuckets outperforms SOTA and aids weakly supervised text classification.

Main contributions:
- Novel coherence measure based on thematic word buckets rather than co-occurrence or distributional semantics.
- Proposes and compares three bucket-creation methods: clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.
- Thorough empirical evaluation on three publicly available datasets against state-of-the-art techniques.

Strengths:
- TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art methods on two of three datasets.
- SVD-based variants are parameter-free, increasing practicality and efficiency.
- Demonstrates usefulness for weakly supervised text classification.

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation is limited to topic coherence and weakly supervised classification, not broader tasks like topic modeling or document classification.
- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining mechanisms, limitations, and guarantees.
- Depends on word embeddings, which may be unavailable or unreliable for some domains or languages.

Questions to authors:
- How will TBuckets be extended or applied to other tasks such as topic modeling or document classification?
- Can you provide a theoretical analysis detailing limitations and potential biases of TBuckets?
- How do you plan to address reliance on word embeddings or what alternatives exist when embeddings are unavailable/inaccurate?
