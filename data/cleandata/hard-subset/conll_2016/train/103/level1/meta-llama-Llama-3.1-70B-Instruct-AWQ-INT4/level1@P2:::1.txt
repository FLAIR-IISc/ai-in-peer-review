**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach called TBuckets to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. The approach groups topic words into thematic groups (buckets) using three different techniques: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD)-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. The TBuckets approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets and compared with state-of-the-art results. The paper also demonstrates the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to topic quality measurement**: The paper proposes a new approach to measure topic quality using word buckets, which is different from existing approaches that rely on word co-occurrence or distributional semantics.
2. **Three techniques for creating word buckets**: The paper presents three techniques for creating word buckets: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. These techniques are evaluated and compared with state-of-the-art results.
3. **Evaluation on publicly available datasets**: The paper evaluates the TBuckets approach on three publicly available datasets, providing a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art results.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty of the approach**: The TBuckets approach is a new and innovative way to measure topic quality, which is different from existing approaches.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the TBuckets approach on three publicly available datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
3. **Usefulness for weakly supervised text classification**: The paper demonstrates the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification, which is an important application area.
4. **No parameter tuning required**: The SVD-based techniques in TBuckets do not require parameter tuning, which is a significant advantage over existing approaches.
5. **Good performance**: The TBuckets approach outperforms state-of-the-art results on two out of three datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited explanation of the reorganization step**: The paper does not provide a detailed explanation of the reorganization step in the SVD-based technique, which may make it difficult to understand and replicate.
2. **No analysis of the effect of word embeddings**: The paper does not analyze the effect of using different word embeddings on the performance of the TBuckets approach.
3. **Limited comparison with other approaches**: The paper only compares the TBuckets approach with state-of-the-art results and does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other existing approaches.
4. **No discussion of the limitations of the approach**: The paper does not discuss the limitations of the TBuckets approach, such as its potential sensitivity to the choice of word embeddings or the number of topics.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the reorganization step in the SVD-based technique?
2. How does the choice of word embeddings affect the performance of the TBuckets approach?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive comparison with other existing approaches to topic quality measurement?
4. What are the limitations of the TBuckets approach, and how can they be addressed in future work?
