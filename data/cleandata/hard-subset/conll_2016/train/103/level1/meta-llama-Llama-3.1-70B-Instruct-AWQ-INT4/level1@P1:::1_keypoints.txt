- Proposes TBuckets to measure LDA topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic "buckets" using clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.
- Evaluates TBuckets on three public datasets and reports outperforming prior state-of-the-art.
- Demonstrates TBuckets’ usefulness for weakly supervised text classification by surfacing high-quality topics for annotation.

- Contribution: TBuckets as a novel metric where a single large bucket indicates high topic coherence.
- Contribution: Empirical evaluation on three publicly available datasets showing improved performance over reported baselines.
- Contribution: Application of TBuckets to improve human-in-the-loop weakly supervised text classification.

- Strength: Novel and intuitive grouping-based approach to assess topic quality.
- Strength: Reported state-of-the-art performance on evaluated datasets.
- Strength: SVD-based variants are parameter-free, avoiding tuning overhead.
- Strength: Practical utility in selecting high-quality topics for weak supervision workflows.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—only three datasets may not generalize broadly.
- Weakness: Comparisons limited to results reported in Roder et al. (2015), lacking broader baselines.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of results and parameter effects on performance.
- Weakness: No discussion of limitations or justification for key assumptions (e.g., single large bucket = single coherent theme).

- Q: Can TBuckets extend to other topic models like NMF or PLSA?
- Q: Can authors provide deeper analysis of parameter effects and performance variability?
- Q: How will the approach address the assumption that a single large bucket equals a single coherent theme?
- Q: What is the quantitative impact of using TBuckets on downstream weakly supervised classification performance?
