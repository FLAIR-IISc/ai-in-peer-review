**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel approach, called TBuckets, to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. The approach groups topic words into thematic groups (buckets) and evaluates the coherence of a topic based on the properties of the largest bucket. The authors explore three techniques for creating buckets: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD)-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. The TBuckets approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets and outperforms the state-of-the-art results. The authors also demonstrate the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to measure topic coherence**: The paper proposes a new approach to measure the coherence of LDA-based topics, which is based on grouping topic words into thematic groups (buckets).
2. **Three techniques for creating buckets**: The authors explore three techniques for creating buckets: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
3. **Evaluation on publicly available datasets**: The TBuckets approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets, and the results show that it outperforms the state-of-the-art results.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty of the approach**: The TBuckets approach is a new and innovative way to measure topic coherence, which is different from existing approaches.
2. **Good performance**: The results show that the TBuckets approach outperforms the state-of-the-art results on three publicly available datasets.
3. **Usefulness for text classification**: The authors demonstrate the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification, which is a practical application of the approach.
4. **Parameter-free**: The SVD-based techniques are completely parameter-free, which is a desirable property in machine learning models.
5. **Clear explanation**: The paper provides a clear explanation of the TBuckets approach, including the intuition behind it and the technical details.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The evaluation of the TBuckets approach is limited to three publicly available datasets, which may not be representative of all possible datasets.
2. **Comparison to other approaches**: The paper only compares the TBuckets approach to the state-of-the-art results reported in Roder et al. (2015), but it does not compare it to other existing approaches.
3. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The paper does not provide a theoretical analysis of the TBuckets approach, which would be useful to understand its strengths and limitations.
4. **Dependence on word embeddings**: The TBuckets approach relies on word embeddings, which may not always be available or accurate.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how you selected the three datasets used in the evaluation?
2. How do you plan to extend the TBuckets approach to other applications beyond text classification?
3. Can you provide more insights on why the SVD-based techniques are completely parameter-free?
4. How do you plan to address the limitation of relying on word embeddings, which may not always be available or accurate?
