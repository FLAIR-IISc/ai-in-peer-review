- Proposes TBuckets, which groups topic words into thematic buckets and scores topic coherence using properties of the largest bucket.
- Implements three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
- Evaluated TBuckets on three public datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art on two of them.
- Shows TBuckets' utility for weakly supervised text classification.
- Contribution: a novel bucket-based metric for topic coherence.
- Contribution: exploration and comparison of three bucket construction methods.
- Contribution: empirical evaluation demonstrating competitive performance.
- Strength: novel and intuitive approach to measuring topic coherence.
- Strength: empirical effectiveness on multiple datasets.
- Strength: practical usefulness for weakly supervised classification tasks.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to only three datasets, raising concerns about generality.
- Weakness: insufficient comprehensive comparison with other coherence measures.
- Weakness: calls for broader and more diverse evaluations to fully assess effectiveness.
- Question: how will the method extend to other domains like images or videos?
- Question: what is the computational complexity and how does it compare to other coherence measures?
- Question: how will TBuckets perform when applied to topic models other than LDA?
