- Proposes TBuckets, a novel method that groups topic words into thematic buckets to measure LDA topic quality.
- Implements three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
- Evaluates TBuckets on three public datasets and compares with state-of-the-art methods.
- Shows TBuckets' utility for weakly supervised text classification.
- Introduces a novel bucket-based metric for topic quality distinct from co-occurrence or distributional approaches.
- Provides three concrete techniques for creating word buckets and compares their performance.
- Offers comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple datasets.
- Demonstrates applicability to weakly supervised classification tasks.
- SVD-based techniques require no parameter tuning, simplifying use.
- TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art on two of three datasets.
- Reorganization step in the SVD-based method is under-explained and hard to replicate.
- Lacks analysis of how different word embeddings affect performance.
- Comparison is limited to state-of-the-art results and omits broader baseline comparisons.
- Does not discuss limitations such as sensitivity to embeddings or topic count.
- Requests clearer, detailed explanation of the SVD reorganization step.
- Asks for analysis of the impact of embedding choice on TBuckets' performance.
- Requests a more comprehensive comparison with other topic-quality methods and a discussion of limitations and future fixes.
