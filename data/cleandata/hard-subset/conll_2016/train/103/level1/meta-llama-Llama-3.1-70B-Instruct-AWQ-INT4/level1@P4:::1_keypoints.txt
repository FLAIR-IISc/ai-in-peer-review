Paper proposes TBuckets: group topic words into thematic buckets and measure LDA topic coherence by properties of the largest bucket.

Novel approach to measure topic coherence using thematic grouping (TBuckets).

Three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.

Evaluated on three public datasets and reported to outperform state-of-the-art.

Novelty: TBuckets is a new and different method compared to existing coherence measures.

Good empirical performance: outperforms prior methods on three datasets.

Demonstrated usefulness for weakly supervised text classification.

SVD-based techniques are parameter-free.

Paper provides clear intuition and technical details.

Evaluation limited to three datasets, which may not be representative.

Comparisons limited to Roder et al. (2015); lacks broader baselines.

No theoretical analysis of the TBuckets approach is provided.

Dependence on word embeddings, which may be unavailable or inaccurate.
