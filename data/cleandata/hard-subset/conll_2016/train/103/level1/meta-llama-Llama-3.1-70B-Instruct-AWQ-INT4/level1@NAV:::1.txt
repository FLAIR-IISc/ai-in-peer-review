**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach called TBuckets to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. The approach groups topic words into thematic groups (buckets) and evaluates the coherence of a topic based on the properties of the largest bucket. The authors explore three techniques for creating buckets of words: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD)-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. The approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets and outperforms the state-of-the-art results in two out of three datasets. The authors also demonstrate the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to topic coherence measurement**: The paper proposes a new approach to measuring topic coherence, which is based on grouping topic words into thematic groups (buckets) and evaluating the coherence of a topic based on the properties of the largest bucket.
2. **Three techniques for creating buckets of words**: The authors explore three techniques for creating buckets of words: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
3. **Evaluation on publicly available datasets**: The approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets and outperforms the state-of-the-art results in two out of three datasets.

**Weaknesses**
