**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach, called TBuckets, to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. The approach groups topic words into thematic buckets, where a single large bucket signifies a single coherent theme, indicating high topic coherence. The TBuckets approach employs three techniques for creating buckets of words: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD)-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. The authors evaluate their techniques on three publicly available datasets and demonstrate better performance than the state-of-the-art results. They also highlight the utility of TBuckets for the task of weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Approach to Measure Topic Quality**: The paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, to measure the quality of LDA-based topics by grouping topic words into thematic buckets.
2. **Three Techniques for Creating Buckets**: The authors propose three techniques for creating buckets of words: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
3. **Improved Performance over State-of-the-Art**: The authors demonstrate that their techniques outperform the state-of-the-art results on three publicly available datasets.
4. **Utility for Weakly Supervised Text Classification**: The authors highlight the utility of TBuckets for the task of weakly supervised text classification.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Approach to Measure Topic Quality**: The TBuckets approach is effective in measuring the quality of LDA-based topics, as demonstrated by its improved performance over state-of-the-art results.
2. **Three Techniques for Creating Buckets**: The authors provide three techniques for creating buckets of words, which can be useful in different scenarios.
3. **Improved Performance on Multiple Datasets**: The authors demonstrate improved performance on three publicly available datasets, which suggests that their approach is robust and generalizable.
4. **Utility for Weakly Supervised Text Classification**: The authors highlight the utility of TBuckets for the task of weakly supervised text classification, which can be useful in real-world applications.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their approach on three publicly available datasets, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate its generalizability.
2. **Lack of Theoretical Analysis**: The authors do not provide a theoretical analysis of their approach, which can make it difficult to understand its limitations and potential biases.
3. **Limited Comparison with Other Approaches**: The authors only compare their approach with the state-of-the-art results reported in Roder et al. (2015), which may not be sufficient to demonstrate its superiority over other approaches.
4. **Limited Discussion of Future Work**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of future work, which can make it difficult to understand the potential directions for improving their approach.
