**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics. TBuckets groups topic words into thematic groups (buckets) and uses three techniques to create these buckets: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD)-based, and SVD-based with reorganization. The approach is evaluated on three publicly available datasets and outperforms the state-of-the-art techniques in two out of three datasets. Additionally, the paper demonstrates the usefulness of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel approach, TBuckets, to measure topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic groups (buckets).
2. Three techniques for creating buckets: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
3. Evaluation of TBuckets on three publicly available datasets, showing better performance than state-of-the-art techniques in two out of three datasets.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel and intuitive approach to measure topic quality, which is a crucial aspect of topic modeling.
2. The evaluation of TBuckets on three publicly available datasets demonstrates its effectiveness and generalizability.
3. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the results, including examples of topics and buckets created using TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
4. The approach is completely parameter-free, which is a significant advantage over state-of-the-art techniques that require multiple parameter tuning.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of TBuckets, such as its potential failure to capture complex topics or its sensitivity to word embeddings.
2. The evaluation of TBuckets is limited to three datasets, and it would be beneficial to evaluate its performance on more datasets to further demonstrate its generalizability.
3. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how TBuckets can be used for other applications beyond text classification.
4. The paper assumes that the topic model is available de facto, but it would be beneficial to discuss how TBuckets can be used with other topic modeling techniques.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the potential limitations of TBuckets, such as its failure to capture complex topics or its sensitivity to word embeddings?
2. Can you provide more details on how TBuckets can be used for other applications beyond text classification?
3. How do you plan to evaluate the performance of TBuckets on more datasets to further demonstrate its generalizability?
4. Can you discuss how TBuckets can be used with other topic modeling techniques, such as non-parametric topic models or neural topic models?
