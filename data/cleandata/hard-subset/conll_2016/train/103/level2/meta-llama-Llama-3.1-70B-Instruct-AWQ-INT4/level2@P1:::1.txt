**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach, TBuckets, to measure the quality of Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topics by grouping topic words into thematic groups (buckets). The approach uses three techniques for creating buckets: clustering-based, singular value decomposition (SVD), and SVD with reorganization. The authors evaluate their techniques on three publicly available datasets and demonstrate better performance than the state-of-the-art results. They also highlight the utility of TBuckets for weakly supervised text classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to topic quality measurement**: TBuckets introduces a new way to measure topic quality by grouping topic words into buckets, which can be used to evaluate the coherence of a topic.
2. **Improved performance over state-of-the-art**: The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms existing state-of-the-art techniques on two out of three datasets, with the SVD-based techniques being completely parameter-free.
3. **Application to weakly supervised text classification**: The authors show that using TBuckets to evaluate topic quality can improve the performance of weakly supervised text classification.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of word embeddings**: The authors leverage word embeddings to capture semantic relationships between words, which is essential for measuring topic coherence.
2. **Simple and intuitive approach**: The TBuckets approach is easy to understand and implement, making it a practical solution for topic quality measurement.
3. **Robust evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on multiple datasets and compare it with existing state-of-the-art techniques, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited exploration of parameter tuning**: Although the authors claim that their SVD-based techniques are parameter-free, they do not explore the impact of parameter tuning on the performance of their approach.
2. **Lack of comparison with other topic modeling techniques**: The authors only compare their approach with existing topic quality measurement techniques, but do not evaluate its performance in comparison to other topic modeling techniques.
3. **Limited discussion on the limitations of TBuckets**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion on the limitations of their approach, such as its potential biases or assumptions.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more insights into the choice of word embeddings and their impact on the performance of TBuckets?
2. How do you plan to extend TBuckets to other topic modeling techniques, such as non-LDA-based approaches?
3. Can you discuss the potential limitations and biases of TBuckets, and how you plan to address them in future work?
