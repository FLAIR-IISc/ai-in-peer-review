- Proposes TBuckets, a method that groups LDA topic words into thematic buckets to measure topic quality.
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD-based, and SVD-based with reorganization.
- Evaluates TBuckets on three public datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art on two of them.
- Demonstrates TBuckets' usefulness for weakly supervised text classification.
- Novel and intuitive approach addressing topic quality in topic modeling.
- Evaluated on multiple datasets, indicating effectiveness and some generalizability.
- Provides thorough result analysis with examples of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outputs.
- Parameter-free method, avoiding extensive hyperparameter tuning required by competitors.
- Lacks detailed analysis of TBuckets' limitations, such as handling complex topics or embedding sensitivity.
- Evaluation limited to three datasets; broader testing would better establish generalizability.
- Unclear explanation of TBuckets' applicability beyond text classification.
- Assumes an available topic model without discussing compatibility with other topic modeling methods.
- Asks how authors will address limitations like complex-topic capture and embedding sensitivity.
- Requests more details on using TBuckets for applications beyond text classification.
- Suggests plans for broader evaluation on additional datasets to demonstrate generalizability.
- Asks how TBuckets could be integrated with other topic modeling techniques (non-parametric or neural).
