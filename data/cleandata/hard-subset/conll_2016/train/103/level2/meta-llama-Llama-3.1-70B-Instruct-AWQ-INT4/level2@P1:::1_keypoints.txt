- Proposes TBuckets: groups LDA topic words into thematic buckets using clustering, SVD, and SVD+reorganization to measure topic quality.  
- Evaluated on three public datasets and reportedly outperforms state-of-the-art on two of them.  
- Demonstrates TBuckets can improve weakly supervised text classification performance.

- Novel metric: introduces bucket-based topic quality measurement to assess topic coherence.  
- Empirical gains: shows improved results over prior techniques, with SVD-based variants being parameter-free.  
- Practical application: applies TBuckets to boost weakly supervised classification.

- Uses word embeddings effectively to capture semantic word relationships for coherence measurement.  
- Method is simple and intuitive, easy to implement.  
- Evaluation is robust: multiple datasets and comparisons to existing topic-quality methods.

- Does not sufficiently explore parameter tuning effects despite claiming parameter-free SVD variants.  
- Lacks comparisons against other topic modeling methods (beyond topic-quality metrics).  
- Limited discussion of TBuckets’ limitations, potential biases, and underlying assumptions.

- Request for clarification on how choice of word embeddings affects TBuckets’ performance.  
- Asks how TBuckets could be extended to non-LDA topic modeling approaches.  
- Seeks discussion of TBuckets’ potential limitations/biases and plans to address them.
