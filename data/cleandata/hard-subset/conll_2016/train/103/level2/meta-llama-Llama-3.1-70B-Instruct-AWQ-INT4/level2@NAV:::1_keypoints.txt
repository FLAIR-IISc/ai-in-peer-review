- Summary: Proposes TBuckets to measure LDA topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets (large single bucket = high coherence); implements clustering, SVD, and SVD-reorg and reports better performance than prior methods on three datasets.

- Contribution 1: Introduces the TBuckets approach for measuring topic quality via word buckets.

- Contribution 2: Presents three techniques—TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg—that outperform state-of-the-art coherence measures.

- Contribution 3: Applies TBuckets to weakly supervised text classification, showing improved classification performance.

- Strength 1: Offers a novel perspective on topic quality measurement with practical relevance to IR and text classification.

- Strength 2: Empirically outperforms existing methods on three public datasets.

- Strength 3: TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are parameter-free, increasing practicality and efficiency.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to only three datasets, raising questions about generalizability.

- Weakness 2: The bucketing process may be too simplistic to capture complex or nuanced word relationships.

- Weakness 3: Method depends on pre-trained word embeddings, which may be unavailable or unsuitable in some domains.

- Question 1: How will TBuckets be extended to capture more complex/nuanced semantic relationships between words?

- Question 2: Can the authors clarify the choice of word embeddings and quantify their impact on TBuckets’ performance?

- Question 3: How do the authors envision deploying TBuckets in real-world applications like text classification and information retrieval?

- Additional comment: Promising approach that outperforms baselines but requires further evaluation and refinement to ensure robustness and generalizability.
