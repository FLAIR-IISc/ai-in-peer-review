- Introduces TBuckets: automatically groups top-topic words into semantic "buckets" and considers a topic coherent if most words fall into one large bucket.  
- Proposes three bucket-creation methods using pre-trained embeddings: agglomerative clustering and two SVD-based methods that treat right-singular vectors as latent sub-theme representations.  
- Proposes SVD-Reorg refinement that moves words based on a dynamic similarity threshold and evaluates methods by correlating coherence scores with human judgments on three datasets, outperforming prior work.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by using TBuckets to filter topics for human annotation in a weakly-supervised classification task, improving final classification accuracy.  

- Main contribution: a novel, intuitive, and largely parameter-free SVD-based coherence metric (TBuckets-SVD and SVD-Reorg) that detects latent sub-themes rather than relying on pairwise similarity.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical evidence that the coherence metric improves downstream weakly-supervised text classification when used to select topics for annotation.  

- Strength: Intuitive and novel idea—bucketing words and using SVD to discover sub-themes is a fresh perspective versus pairwise PMI/cosine aggregation.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance—state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG, competitive on Genomics against Röder et al. (2015) benchmarks.  
- Strength: Parameter-free SVD methods offer practical advantages over methods requiring extensive configuration tuning.  
- Strength: Demonstrated real-world usefulness through improved classification accuracy when selecting higher-coherence topic sets.  

- Weakness: SVD-Reorg’s dynamic threshold formula (`AWES - AWPS/AWES`) is under-justified and appears ad-hoc, weakening the parameter-free claim.  
- Weakness: No ablation study to show contribution of individual scoring components (largest-bucket size, WL, EVS, singular-value squared weighting).  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on the logic for moving words out of the largest bucket; unclear if the same dynamic threshold applies and risk of marginal, unjustified evictions.  
- Weakness: Experimental reporting omissions—counter-intuitive results where low-coherence sets outperform high-coherence sets (MEDICAL vs SPACE; POLITICS vs RELIGION) need more discussion.  

- Question: How was the SVD-Reorg dynamic threshold designed—derived theoretically or chosen among heuristics, and what alternatives were tried?  
- Question: What motivates weighting SVD-based scores by the square of the largest singular value, and how do results look without this weighting?  
- Question: Any further insights on why low-coherence topic sets outperformed high-coherence sets in some classification pairs, and does this limit the metric’s predictive value for downstream tasks?
