- TBuckets groups a topic's top words into thematic "buckets" using pre-trained embeddings; coherence is measured by the distribution across buckets (largest bucket importance).  
- Three bucketing methods proposed: agglomerative clustering and two SVD-based variants, the second including a reorganization step.  
- Evaluated by correlating with human judgments on three datasets; TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is state-of-the-art on two of three datasets.  
- Demonstrates practical utility: filtering topics by TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification.  
- Contribution: a novel, intuitive bucketing framework for topic coherence, with SVD framing sub-themes as orthogonal dimensions.  
- Contribution: a parameter-free method (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) achieving strong empirical performance without hyperparameter tuning.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that the metric improves downstream application performance.  
- Strength: high novelty and strong intuition; SVD-based sub-theme identification is principled and elegant.  
- Strength: excellent empirical results on NYT and 20NG benchmarks.  
- Strength: parameter-free nature reduces tuning burden and aids practical adoption.  
- Strength: validated via a meaningful downstream weakly supervised classification experiment.  
- Weakness: SVD-based methodology lacks crucial details and justification (e.g., why select exactly n eigenvectors).  
- Weakness: Reorganization step uses a complex dynamic threshold (AWES - AWPS/AWES) without clear motivation or ablation.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of results—no hypothesis for poor performance on the Genomics dataset.  
- Weakness: Lacks qualitative error analysis showing cases where TBuckets disagrees with human judgments.  
- Weakness: Scoring variants and the weighting by the square of the top singular value are not well motivated or compared.  
- Question: Why use exactly n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers and were alternative eigenvector-selection strategies tested?  
- Question: Please justify the dynamic reorganization threshold (AWES - AWPS/AWES) and provide ablation vs simpler thresholds.  
- Question: What explains the Genomics performance gap—domain mismatch of general embeddings or a limitation of the bucketing approach?
