- Introduces TBuckets, a method that assesses topic coherence by grouping topic words into semantic "buckets" using word embeddings.
- Proposes three grouping methods (agglomerative clustering and two SVD-based methods), with the SVD variants being parameter-free.
- Evaluates methods by correlating coherence scores with human judgments on three datasets and shows state-of-the-art results on two.
- Demonstrates practical utility by using TBuckets to select topic models that improve weakly supervised text classification accuracy.
- Contribution: a novel, intuitive, parameter-free SVD-based grouping (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that uses embedding eigenvectors to find sub-themes.
- Contribution: shows downstream value of the metric by improving classification via selecting higher-coherence topics.
- Strength: conceptually sound and novel use of SVD to decompose topics into orthogonal semantic components.
- Strength: parameter-free approach increases robustness and ease of application compared to parameter-dependent coherence metrics.
- Strength: strong empirical performance, outperforming prior bests on NYT and 20NG using Pearson correlation with human scores.
- Strength: practical downstream demonstration strengthens the metric's real-world relevance.
- Weakness: reorganization heuristic (dynamic threshold AWES - AWPS/AWES) is poorly explained and appears ad hoc without ablation or derivation.
- Weakness: scoring uses only the largest bucket, ignoring distribution/composition of smaller buckets and potential penalties for split topics.
- Weakness: SVD methods underperform on the Genomics dataset with no analysis offered; domain mismatch of pre-trained embeddings is a plausible but unexplored cause.
- Question: request intuition or derivation for the dynamic threshold formula—empirical vs. principled origin and ablation results.
- Question: ask for hypotheses on Genomics underperformance and whether domain-specific embeddings were or could be tested.
