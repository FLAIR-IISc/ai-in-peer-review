This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach to measuring topic quality based on grouping topic words into thematic “buckets.” The core idea is that coherent topics should result in a single large bucket, while incoherent topics will yield multiple smaller buckets. The authors explore three techniques for bucket creation: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization, and demonstrate improved performance compared to state-of-the-art methods on three datasets, along with a successful application to weakly supervised text classification.

**Contributions:**

1. **A novel metric for topic coherence (TBuckets):** The central contribution is the TBuckets approach itself, providing a new way to assess topic quality by analyzing the structure of word groupings within a topic. This offers a different perspective compared to existing metrics focused on word co-occurrence or distributional similarity.
2. **Three implementations of TBuckets with varying complexity:** The exploration of clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization offers a range of options, with the SVD-based methods being particularly appealing due to their parameter-free nature. This provides flexibility and demonstrates the robustness of the core concept.
3. **Demonstration of TBuckets’ utility in a downstream task:** The application of TBuckets to weakly supervised text classification provides concrete evidence of its practical value beyond simply measuring topic quality. This establishes relevance and impact.

**Strengths:**

1. **Intuitive Approach:** The underlying idea of grouping words into thematic buckets aligns with how humans intuitively assess topic coherence. This makes the approach conceptually sound and easier to understand.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates consistently strong performance compared to state-of-the-art methods on three different datasets. This provides robust evidence of TBuckets' effectiveness. The outperformance on NYT and 20NG is particularly noteworthy.
3. **Parameter-Free SVD Methods:** The SVD-based techniques (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) are a significant advantage, eliminating the need for parameter tuning, which can be time-consuming and potentially introduce bias.
4. **Clear Explanation and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind TBuckets and provides a detailed explanation of each technique, including the rationale for the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
5. **Successful Application to Text Classification:** The demonstration of improved text classification performance when using TBuckets to select high-quality topics reinforces its practical utility and highlights its potential for broader applications.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Individual Components:** While the *combination* of ideas is novel, the individual components – word embeddings, SVD, clustering – are well-established techniques. The novelty lies in *how* these components are integrated to create TBuckets, but this should be explicitly acknowledged and further highlighted.
2. **Lack of Ablation Study:** The paper would benefit from an ablation study to assess the contribution of each component of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (e.g., the reorganization step). How much improvement is gained from reorganization compared to just using TBuckets-SVD? This would help to better understand the key drivers of performance.
3. **Dependence on Word Embedding Quality:** The performance of TBuckets is inherently tied to the quality of the word embeddings used. While the authors utilize pre-trained GloVe embeddings, the limitations of these embeddings are not discussed. Domain-specific embeddings might yield different results.
4. **Reorganization Threshold Justification:** The method for dynamically calculating the reorganization threshold (AWES − AWPS/AWES) is described, but lacks a strong justification. Why this specific formula, and what are the implications of different formulations? A more in-depth discussion of this parameter and its sensitivity would be valuable.
5. **Limited Analysis of Failure Cases:** The paper primarily focuses on successful applications. A discussion of scenarios where TBuckets performs poorly, and potential reasons for those failures, would strengthen the analysis and provide insights for future improvements.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more detailed discussion of the novelty of TBuckets, explicitly acknowledging the use of established components and highlighting the innovative aspects of their integration?
2. Would you consider performing an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?
3. How sensitive is the performance of TBuckets to the choice of word embeddings? Have you experimented with different embedding models?
4. What is the computational complexity of each of the TBuckets techniques (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg)?

**Additional Comments:**

* The references are comprehensive.
* The experimental setup is clearly described and the results are presented in a well-organized manner.
* Consider adding a visual example illustrating the bucket creation process for both a coherent and an incoherent topic. This would further enhance the clarity of the approach.
