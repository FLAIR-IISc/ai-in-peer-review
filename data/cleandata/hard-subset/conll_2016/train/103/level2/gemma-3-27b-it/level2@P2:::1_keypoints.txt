- Novel metric (TBuckets): Proposes TBuckets, measuring topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets and assessing bucket size distribution.
- Three implementations: Introduces clustering-based, SVD, and SVD-with-reorganization variants, offering a spectrum of complexity and parameter requirements.
- Downstream utility: Demonstrates TBuckets improves weakly supervised text classification, showing practical value beyond coherence scoring.

- Intuitive idea: Bucket-based grouping aligns with human intuition about topic coherence.
- Strong empirical performance: Outperforms state-of-the-art on three datasets, notably NYT and 20NG.
- Parameter-free SVD methods: TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg remove need for parameter tuning.
- Clear exposition: Motivation and methods, including the reorganization step, are well explained.
- Practical impact: Improved topic selection leads to better text classification results.

- Limited novelty of components: Uses established tools (embeddings, SVD, clustering); novelty is primarily in their integration and should be explicitly framed as such.
- No ablation study: Lacks experiments isolating the contribution of each component, especially the reorganization step.
- Embedding dependence: Performance hinges on word embedding quality (GloVe used), with no exploration of alternative or domain-specific embeddings.
- Reorganization threshold justification weak: The dynamic threshold formula (AWES − AWPS/AWES) lacks strong rationale and sensitivity analysis.
- Limited failure analysis: Paper focuses on successes with little discussion of scenarios where TBuckets performs poorly.

- Request novelty clarification: Please explicitly discuss which aspects are novel versus established components.
- Ablation request: Would you run an ablation to quantify the reorganization step’s impact?
- Embedding sensitivity: Have you tested TBuckets with different embedding models or domains?
- Complexity details: What are the computational complexities of clustering, SVD, and SVD-Reorg?

- References comprehensive.
- Experimental setup and results well organized.
- Suggest adding a visual example showing bucket creation for coherent vs. incoherent topics.
