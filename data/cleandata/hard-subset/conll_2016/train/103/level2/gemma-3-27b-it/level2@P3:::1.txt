This paper proposes TBuckets, a novel approach for measuring topic quality in Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) based topic models. The core idea is to group topic words into thematic buckets, with the assumption that a single large bucket indicates a coherent topic. Three techniques are explored for bucket creation: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization. The work demonstrates that TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art techniques on three publicly available datasets and shows its utility in weakly supervised text classification.

The primary contribution is the TBuckets approach itself, offering a new perspective on topic coherence assessment by leveraging word embeddings and bucket formation. The exploration of three different bucket creation techniques – clustering, SVD, and a reorganization step for SVD – provides a nuanced investigation of the core idea. A secondary contribution is the empirical validation of TBuckets against existing methods and its successful application to a downstream task (weakly supervised text classification), demonstrating practical relevance.

Strengths:

1. **Novelty of Approach:** The TBuckets concept of representing topic coherence through the structure of word buckets is a fresh approach compared to existing methods that often rely on statistical measures like PMI or distributional similarity. This provides a different lens for evaluating topic quality.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art methods on three publicly available datasets. This strengthens the validity of the proposed approach. The consistent outperformance, particularly of the SVD-based methods, is compelling.
3. **Parameter-Free SVD Techniques:** The SVD-based techniques are highlighted as being completely parameter-free, a significant advantage over methods requiring parameter tuning. This simplifies the application of the method and reduces the potential for bias introduced by parameter selection.
4. **Demonstrated Practical Application:** The successful application of TBuckets to weakly supervised text classification demonstrates the practical utility of the method beyond simply measuring topic coherence. This provides a compelling justification for the work.
5. **Clear Explanation of Methods:** The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the TBuckets approach and the three techniques used for bucket creation, including the rationale behind the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.

Weaknesses:

1. **Limited Discussion of Bucket Reorganization:** While the reorganization step in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is motivated and explained, the specific thresholds and calculations involved (AWPS, AWES, the threshold formula) feel somewhat ad-hoc. A more rigorous justification or sensitivity analysis of these parameters would strengthen the approach. The explanation could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of why these specific calculations were chosen.
2. **Lack of Ablation Study:** The paper does not present an ablation study to systematically evaluate the contribution of each component of TBuckets-SVD-Reorg. For example, the impact of the reorganization step alone could be quantified by comparing TBuckets-SVD with TBuckets-SVD-Reorg. This would provide a clearer understanding of which aspects of the method are most important.
3. **Limited Comparison with Baselines in Text Classification:** The text classification experiments only compare performance with one specific approach (LPA-TD) and doesn’t compare against more standard weakly supervised learning baselines. Demonstrating improvements over simpler or more widely-used methods would further strengthen the argument for the effectiveness of TBuckets.
4. **Reliance on Pre-trained Word Embeddings:** The approach relies heavily on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings (GloVe). The paper doesn't discuss the potential impact of different embedding models or the sensitivity of the results to the choice of embeddings. 

Questions to Authors:

1. Could you provide a more detailed justification for the specific formula used to calculate the dynamic threshold for reorganization in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg?
2.  Have you considered performing an ablation study to evaluate the individual contributions of the SVD and reorganization steps in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg? If so, what were the results?
3.  Were any alternative word embedding models explored, and if so, how did they impact the performance of TBuckets?
4. How sensitive are the results to the choice of the window size used for calculating PMI or NPMI when using existing coherence measures for comparison?



Additional Comments: 

- The referencing is generally good, but some references are missing years (e.g., Blei et al., 2003).
- The discussion of related work could be expanded to include more recent approaches to topic coherence assessment.
- It would be helpful to provide some example buckets generated by each of the three TBuckets techniques for a few sample topics.
