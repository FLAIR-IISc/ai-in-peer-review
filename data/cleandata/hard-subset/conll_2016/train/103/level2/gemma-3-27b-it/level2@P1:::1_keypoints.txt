- TBuckets introduces a novel coherence measure by grouping topic words into thematic “buckets,” with large single buckets indicating high coherence.
- TBuckets explores three bucket-creation techniques: clustering, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.
- TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are parameter-free, avoiding extensive parameter tuning.
- The method is shown useful beyond coherence scoring by improving weakly supervised text classification.
- Motivation and problem statement are clear, highlighting limitations of existing coherence measures.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using three public datasets and Pearson correlation with human judgments.
- Empirical results are strong: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms state-of-the-art on two datasets and is competitive on the third.
- The practical application to weakly supervised classification demonstrates real-world relevance.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with a helpful example of the bucket creation process.
- Novelty is limited at the component level since embeddings and SVD are established; the main contribution is their combination.
- The reorganization step introduces a complex dynamic threshold (AWPS/AWES) with insufficient justification and sensitivity analysis.
- The paper lacks a detailed error analysis to explain when and why TBuckets succeeds or fails.
- Evaluation on only 20NG, NYT, and Genomics may limit generalizability; more diverse datasets are needed.
- The clustering-based approach depends on a “Max Dist” parameter but lacks comprehensive analysis of its impact.
- Request clarification on TBuckets’ novelty relative to prior embedding+dimensionality-reduction topic methods.
- Ask for rationale behind AWPS/AWES thresholding and whether simpler alternatives were explored.
- Request examples of topics where TBuckets performs poorly and explanations for those failures.
- Ask whether TBuckets has been evaluated on additional domains or more diverse datasets.
- Request detailed analysis and best-practice guidance for setting the clustering “Max Dist” parameter.
- Suggest discussing how TBuckets could apply to other topic model variants.
- Recommend adding more context for some references to clarify relevance.
- Minor: check terminology consistency (e.g., “coherence score” vs. “coherence”).
