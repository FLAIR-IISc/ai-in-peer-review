- Proposes TBuckets: grouping topic words into thematic buckets (clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) where a single large bucket indicates topic coherence.  
- Shows TBuckets outperforms state-of-the-art on three public datasets and aids weakly supervised text classification.  
- Main contribution: a new coherence assessment perspective using word embeddings and bucket formation, plus empirical validation and downstream utility.  
- Novelty: TBuckets offers a fresh alternative to PMI/distributional similarity–based coherence measures.  
- Strong empirical results: consistent outperformance, especially for SVD-based variants, across datasets.  
- Parameter-free SVD techniques: no hyperparameter tuning required, simplifying application.  
- Practical application: demonstrated usefulness in weakly supervised classification beyond coherence scoring.  
- Clear exposition: methods and the rationale for the SVD reorganization step are well explained.  
- Weakness — reorganization step: thresholds and formulas (AWPS, AWES, threshold) appear ad-hoc and need stronger justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — no ablation study: paper lacks systematic evaluation of each component (e.g., SVD vs SVD-Reorg) to isolate contributions.  
- Weakness — limited classification baselines: text classification compared only to LPA-TD, missing comparisons to standard weakly supervised baselines.  
- Weakness — reliance on pre-trained embeddings: no analysis of sensitivity to embedding choice (GloVe vs alternatives).  
- Question: please justify the dynamic threshold formula used in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Question: have you performed an ablation study to isolate the effects of SVD and reorganization, and what were the results?  
- Question: were alternative word embeddings tried, and how did they affect performance?  
- Question: how sensitive are coherence comparisons to the PMI/NPMI window size used for baselines?  
- Additional: some references lack years (e.g., Blei et al., 2003) and referencing should be checked.  
- Additional: related work discussion could be expanded to include more recent topic coherence methods.  
- Additional: include example buckets produced by each TBuckets technique for sample topics to illustrate behavior.
