- Proposes TBuckets: evaluate topic quality by grouping topic words into “buckets,” where fewer, larger buckets indicate higher quality.  
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: clustering-based, SVD, and SVD with reorganization.  
- Claims to outperform state-of-the-art methods on three public datasets.  
- Demonstrates usefulness of TBuckets in a weakly supervised text classification task.  
- Main contribution: an intuitive thematic grouping approach for topic coherence.  
- SVD-based methods, especially with reorganization, perform well without parameter tuning.  
- Experiments are thorough: established datasets and comparison to Roder et al. (2015).  
- Detailed methodological explanations and rationale for the reorganization step.  
- Weakness: motivation for using word embeddings is not deeply explored; alternatives not discussed.  
- Weakness: reorganization step feels somewhat ad hoc and the dynamic threshold lacks rigorous theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: classification accuracy gains are modest and broader benchmarking against other weakly supervised methods is needed.  
- Questions for authors: why choose embeddings over other semantic representations; provide theoretical justification or sensitivity analysis for the dynamic threshold; compare classification performance to non-topic-model-based weakly supervised methods.
