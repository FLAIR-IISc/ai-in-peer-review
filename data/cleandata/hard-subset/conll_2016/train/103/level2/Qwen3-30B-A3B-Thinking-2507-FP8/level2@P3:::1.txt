

Summary: The paper proposes TBuckets, a method for measuring topic coherence by grouping topic words into thematic buckets. It introduces three techniques: clustering-based (TBuckets-Clustering), SVD-based (TBuckets-SVD), and SVD with reorganization (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg). The approach leverages word embeddings and evaluates coherence by analyzing bucket distributions, with the largest bucket size indicating coherence. The method is evaluated on three datasets against human-annotated coherence scores and applied to weakly supervised text classification.

Main Contributions: The primary contribution is the TBuckets framework, particularly the parameter-free SVD-based techniques (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) that outperform state-of-the-art methods. A secondary contribution is the demonstration of TBuckets' utility in improving weakly supervised text classification by filtering low-quality topics. The third contribution is the empirical validation of the bucket-based coherence metric through correlation with human judgments.

Strengths:  
1. The parameter-free nature of TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is a significant advantage over existing methods requiring extensive hyperparameter tuning (e.g., Roder et al., 2015), as evidenced by their superior performance on NYT and 20NG without any tuning.  
2. The experimental results are robust, showing consistent outperformance of state-of-the-art methods on two of three datasets (NYT, 20NG) with statistically meaningful correlation coefficients (e.g., TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves r=0.837 on NYT vs. prior best of 0.806).  
3. The application to text classification provides concrete evidence of practical utility, demonstrating that using TBuckets to select high-coherence topics improves classification accuracy (e.g., +2.2% on PC vs. MAC) compared to random topic selection.

Weaknesses:  
1. The claim that TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are "completely parameter-free" is misleading. TBuckets-Clustering explicitly requires a Max Dist parameter (Figure 1), and the paper conflates the techniques when making this assertion. The SVD techniques avoid hyperparameters, but the broader "TBuckets" framework is not fully parameter-free.  
2. The comparison with Roder et al. (2015) is incomplete; the paper does not report results for the most recent coherence measures (e.g., CUSheffield) or discuss why TBuckets outperforms them, limiting the contextualization of novelty.  
3. The text classification experiment lacks statistical significance testing for the accuracy differences between S1 and S2 strategies. The reported improvements (e.g., +2.2% on PC vs. MAC) should be validated with p-values to confirm they are not due to chance.  

Questions to Authors:  
1. The dynamic threshold in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg is computed as AWES − AWPS/AWES, but the notation is ambiguous. Please clarify whether this is AWES − (AWPS / AWES) or (AWES − AWPS) / AWES, and provide the exact formula used in implementation.  
2. Why were only three datasets used for evaluation? Including a fourth dataset (e.g., a domain-specific corpus) would strengthen claims about generalizability beyond news and genomics.  
3. The paper states TBuckets-SVD-Reorg "outperforms state-of-the-art," yet Table 1 shows it achieves r=0.691 on Genomics versus the prior best of 0.773. Please clarify whether this is a typo or if the Genomics results were misreported.
