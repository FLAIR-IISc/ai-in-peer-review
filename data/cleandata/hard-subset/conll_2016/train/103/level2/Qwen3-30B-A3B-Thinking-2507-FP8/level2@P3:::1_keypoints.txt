- Proposes TBuckets, a topic coherence metric that groups topic words into thematic buckets and introduces TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.  
- Uses word embeddings and bucket-size distributions (largest bucket size) to measure coherence and evaluates against human-annotated scores and in weakly supervised text classification.

Main contributions:
- TBuckets framework, with parameter-free SVD-based variants (TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg) claimed to outperform prior methods.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by using TBuckets to filter low-quality topics and improve weakly supervised text classification.  
- Empirically validates the bucket-based coherence metric via correlation with human judgments.

Strengths:
- TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg avoid hyperparameter tuning, a clear advantage over prior methods.  
- Robust experimental results showing superior performance on NYT and 20NG with strong correlation coefficients (e.g., r=0.837 on NYT).  
- Practical improvement in classification accuracy when selecting high-coherence topics (e.g., +2.2% on PC vs. MAC).

Weaknesses:
- Claim of being “completely parameter-free” is misleading since TBuckets-Clustering requires a Max Dist parameter and the paper conflates techniques.  
- Comparison with prior work is incomplete—recent coherence measures (e.g., CUSheffield) are not reported or discussed.  
- Text classification gains lack statistical significance testing; reported improvements need p-values to confirm they’re not due to chance.

Questions to authors:
- Clarify the dynamic threshold formula in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg: is it AWES − (AWPS / AWES) or (AWES − AWPS) / AWES, and provide the exact implemented formula.  
- Why were only three datasets used; can you justify generalizability or add a domain-specific corpus?  
- Explain the Genomics result discrepancy: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg shows r=0.691 vs prior best 0.773—typo or misreported result?
