- Summary: Introduces TBuckets, a thematic-bucketing coherence measure with three contributions — a parameter-free TBuckets-SVD/Reorg that outperforms prior work on two datasets, an interpretable human-aligned bucketing mechanism, and demonstrated benefit for weakly supervised text classification.

- Strength 1: Parameter-free TBuckets-SVD/Reorg is a practical advance, achieving top Pearson r on NYT (0.837) and 20NG (0.869) without hyperparameter tuning.

- Strength 2: Bucketing mechanism is intuitively human-aligned and interpretable, with examples showing bucket distributions match human coherence judgments.

- Strength 3: Shows real utility for classification — using high-coherence topics yields a 1.5% accuracy gain on the PC vs MAC task with little extra compute.

- Weakness 1: Statistical significance is not addressed — no p-values, confidence intervals, or tests to confirm observed correlation improvements are meaningful.

- Weakness 2: Comparison to prior work is incomplete/misleading — only best-reported numbers from Röder et al. were used instead of evaluating against their specific best-performing configurations.

- Weakness 3: Reorganization step lacks rigorous justification — no error analysis showing SVD failures or how often reorg fixes them, and the dynamic threshold’s derivation and implicit parameters are unclear.

- Question 1: How does the dynamic threshold in TBuckets-SVD-Reorg truly avoid parameter tuning if it is computed from topic-specific statistics that may depend on dataset properties?

- Question 2: Why does TBuckets-SVD-Reorg underperform on Genomics (0.68 vs 0.773 in Röder et al.), and what dataset-specific limitations explain this drop?
