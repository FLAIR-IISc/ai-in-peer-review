- Summary: Proposes TBuckets, grouping topic words into thematic buckets with three techniques (clustering-based, SVD-based, SVD with reorganization) and shows improved coherence on 20NG, NYT, Genomics plus utility for weakly supervised classification.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel word-bucketing framework for measuring topic coherence.

- Contribution 2: Presents parameter-free SVD-based techniques that reportedly outperform tuned state-of-the-art methods.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates practical utility by improving weakly supervised text classification via filtering high-coherence topics.

- Strength 1: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves superior performance on two of three datasets and is claimed to be completely parameter-free, aiding practical adoption.

- Strength 2: Application to text classification shows clear practical value, improving classification in 3 of 4 scenarios.

- Strength 3: Provides a thorough comparison with prior methods and clear examples of bucket formation.

- Weakness 1: Lacks discussion of limitations, notably scalability to larger topic sets and performance on topics with few words.

- Weakness 2: Claim of being "completely parameter-free" is misleading because the reorganization step uses a dynamic, topic-dependent threshold that needs explanation.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation is limited to datasets from Röder et al. (2015) and lacks tests on additional datasets or different topic modeling approaches, limiting generalizability.

- Question 1: How does TBuckets perform on topics with fewer than five words?

- Question 2: Can the authors explain why the method performs better on NYT and 20NG than on Genomics?
