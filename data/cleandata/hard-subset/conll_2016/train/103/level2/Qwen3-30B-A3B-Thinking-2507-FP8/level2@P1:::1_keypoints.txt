- Proposal: TBuckets groups topic words into thematic buckets to measure topic quality in LDA-based models, arguing single large buckets indicate high coherence.

- Contributions: coherence intuition, three bucket-creation techniques (clustering-based, SVD-based, SVD with reorganization), and SVD methods that reportedly need no tuning and beat prior work on two of three datasets.

- Strength: TBuckets is intuitively compelling and well-illustrated, offering a human-centered alternative to purely statistical coherence measures.

- Strength: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves state-of-the-art on NYT and 20NG (e.g., 0.837 vs 0.806 Pearson’s r on NYT) and requires no parameter tuning.

- Strength: Demonstrated practical utility for weakly supervised text classification—filtering by coherence improves performance by 1–5%, with a 4.86% gain on PC vs MAC.

- Strength: SVD techniques are well-motivated, the reorganization step addresses SVD limitations, and the dynamic threshold idea is elegant (though needs more explanation).

- Weakness: Performance gains are modest (0.031 on NYT, 0.006 on 20NG) and the paper doesn't justify their practical significance for topic-model selection.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to three datasets from the same source (Röder et al., 2015); broader domains (e.g., biomedical, legal) are missing.

- Weakness: The reorganization threshold formula (AWES - AWPS*AWES) is poorly explained despite claims of being parameter-free and needs a concrete example.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to more recent work beyond Röder et al. (2015) and unclear how TBuckets fits into that broader coherence landscape.

- Weakness: Computational complexity, especially for large topic models and SVD costs, is not discussed.

- Question: Provide a concrete example of how the dynamic reorganization threshold is calculated for a specific topic and justify the “parameter-free” claim.

- Question: Would TBuckets perform similarly on topics with fewer than 10 words (e.g., from different LDA hyperparameters)?

- Question: Can the authors give a qualitative analysis of the topics behind the PC vs MAC difference to show how bucketing identified problematic topics?
