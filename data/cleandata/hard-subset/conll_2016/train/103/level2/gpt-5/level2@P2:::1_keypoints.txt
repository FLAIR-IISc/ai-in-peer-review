- Proposes TBuckets: grouping top-N topic words into embedding-based “buckets” and scoring coherence by dominance of the largest bucket.  
- Introduces three variants: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg (SVD plus parameter-free reorganization).  
- Coherence scores combine largest-bucket properties (size, LDA word-order weights, eigenvector similarity) optionally weighted by top singular value.  
- Evaluated via Pearson correlation with human judgements on NYT, 20NG, and Genomics datasets.  
- Applied to weakly supervised text classification by prioritizing topic-model runs with higher TBuckets coherence for annotation.  
- Contribution: a simple, interpretable bucket-based coherence framework leveraging embeddings and a novel SVD-based bucketing plus reorganization heuristic.  
- Empirical result: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. (2015) on NYT and 20NG (Pearson r improvements reported).  
- Strength: clear human-aligned intuition and illustrative examples that match inspection workflows.  
- Strength: competitive performance with minimal parameter tuning; SVD variants are effectively parameter-free.  
- Strength: downstream utility shown—selecting high-coherence runs often improves macro-F1 and avoids very poor runs.  
- Strength: reasonable related-work coverage situating TBuckets among PMI/NPMI and distributional measures.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—embedding-based relatedness and clustering are established; main novelty is largest-bucket heuristic.  
- Weakness: SVD-based bucketing lacks theoretical justification for using eigenvectors as bucket prototypes.  
- Weakness: methodological clarity issues—ambiguous reorganization threshold formula and missing ablations for scoring components and σ1^2 weighting.  
- Weakness: design choice of using first n eigenvectors as buckets is not justified and may conflate bucket count with topic length.  
- Weakness: domain and embedding dependence—substantial performance drop on Genomics and no experiments with domain-specific or multiple embeddings.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation breadth—only Pearson reported, no Spearman, significance testing, paired tests, or confidence intervals.  
- Weakness: mixed and sometimes small downstream gains; some cases where low-coherence sets outperform high-coherence ones.  
- Questions: clarify the reorganization threshold calculation, its range, and sensitivity to negative values.  
- Questions: rationale for using principal directions as bucket prototypes and comparisons to simpler parameter-free alternatives (e.g., spherical k-means or graph community detection).  
- Questions: provide ablations removing σ1^2 and isolating contributions of size vs WL vs EVS.  
- Questions: how are OOV topic words handled with GloVe and what are OOV rates per dataset?  
- Questions: were other embeddings (word2vec, fastText, domain-specific) tested, and how do results vary?  
- Questions: why only report Pearson r—request Spearman ρ and statistical significance tests (e.g., Williams test).  
- Suggestions: release implementation details and code (cosine normalization, preprocessing, tie-breaking) for reproducibility.  
- Suggestions: evaluate bucket quality intrinsically (purity/entropy) and report full bucket-size distributions, not only largest.  
- Suggestions: proofread and fix typos/formatting issues; note limitation that multi-theme topics may be unfairly penalized.  
- Suggestion: consider additional evaluations (topic intrusion tasks or fresh human judgments) to complement dataset correlations.
