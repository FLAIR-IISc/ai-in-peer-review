- Proposes TBuckets: grouping top topic words into embedding-based “buckets” with three variants (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg).  
- Main contributions: largely parameter-free SVD bucketing with strong human-judgment correlation, interpretable largest-bucket scoring, and modest downstream gains via run selection.

- Empirical correlations with human judgments are competitive or superior to baselines (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg r≈0.83–0.87 on NYT/20NG; Clustering matches SOTA on Genomics).  
- SVD variants are effectively parameter-free, easing practical use versus tunable coherence methods.  
- Method is simple and interpretable: bucketing and largest-bucket score align with annotator intuitions.  
- Demonstrates utility beyond intrinsic evaluation: selecting high-coherence runs improves macro-F1 in some weakly supervised classification tasks.  
- Reorganization heuristic (AWPS/AWES) is clearly described and avoids manual thresholds.

- Mixed results on Genomics and overall sensitivity to embedding domain mismatch, with no experiments using in-domain embeddings.  
- Lacks statistical rigor: no confidence intervals, significance tests, or model-selection safeguards reported.  
- Reproducibility issues: unclear clustering threshold selection, OOV handling, embedding normalization, eigenvector sign/scale treatment, and choice of number of eigenvectors.  
- Coherence heavily depends on the largest bucket, potentially penalizing legitimately multi-faceted topics; no analysis of failure modes or bias.  
- Classification gains are modest and inconsistent across tasks, and the link between coherence and downstream accuracy is underanalyzed.

- How sensitive are results to embedding source/dimension and OOV handling, and can in-domain embeddings improve Genomics?  
- Why use the first n eigenvectors for bucketing; how are eigenvector sign/scale handled and are there ablations on n?  
- How is the agglomerative Max Dist chosen in practice; is there a validation protocol?  
- Are reported correlation improvements statistically significant vs Röder et al.; please provide CIs and p-values.  
- How does performance vary with number of top words N and with alternative scoring that accounts for multiple mid-sized buckets?
