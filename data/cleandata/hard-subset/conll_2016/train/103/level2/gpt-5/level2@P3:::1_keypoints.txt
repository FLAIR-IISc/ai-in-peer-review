- Paper proposes TBuckets: bucket-based topic coherence metrics using word embeddings with three variants (Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg) and scores derived from the largest bucket.
- Main contributions: interpretable bucket framework and a parameter-free SVD variant with reorganization, plus empirical correlations with human judgments and a weak-supervision application.

Strengths:
- Clear, interpretable intuition: a single large bucket maps to a coherent theme, demonstrated with examples and bucket visualizations.
- Competitive empirical performance: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms prior best settings on NYT and 20NG and is parameter-light.
- Practical utility: selecting topic models by TBuckets improves macro-F1 in several weakly supervised 20NG tasks.
- Efficient and simple: operates on small embedding matrices using off-the-shelf SVD/clustering and avoids heavy co-occurrence statistics.

Weaknesses:
- SVD bucketing under-specified: unclear rationale for using V’s first n eigenvectors as buckets and underspecified assignment details (normalization, ties).
- Reorganization criterion ambiguous: threshold expression is unclear and its motivation/scale are not analyzed; reorg’s contribution not isolated.
- Missing statistical and robustness analyses: no significance testing, sensitivity to top-N, embeddings, OOV handling; poor Genomics performance suggests domain mismatch.
- Baseline and tuning claims shaky: comparison to Röder et al. (2015) lacks protocol parity checks and TBuckets has implicit design choices acting as hyperparameters.
- Sparse classification details: number of annotators, labeling protocol, inter-annotator agreement, and significance of macro-F1 differences are not reported; selection protocol may bias results.

Questions for authors:
- Why use the first n eigenvectors of V as buckets instead of selecting k by explained variance or using U (word-space) directions?
- Clarify the reorganization threshold formula (exact parentheses) and typical AWPS/AWES values across datasets.
- How were scoring components (size, WL, EVS, WL*EVS) chosen and why weight by the squared largest singular value; are results robust without this weighting?
- Which exact pre-trained embeddings were used and how are OOV words handled; how frequent are OOVs in each dataset?
- Are correlation improvements and macro-F1 gains statistically significant (e.g., Fisher r-to-z, tests for classification)?

Essential suggestions:
- Clarify and justify SVD bucketing and reorganization threshold; add ablations isolating each component (SVD w/o reorg, w/o singular-value weighting, w/o WL/EVS).
- Provide statistical significance for correlations and classification results, and detail OOV handling and embedding choices.

Nice-to-have suggestions:
- Sensitivity analyses for top-N words, alternative/in-domain embeddings (e.g., Word2Vec, Genomics-specific), and different topic counts.
- Direct comparison to standard NPMI coherence under identical preprocessing and release code/scripts for reproducibility.
