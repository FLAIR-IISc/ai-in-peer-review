Summary and contributions:
- The paper proposes TBuckets, a family of topic coherence measures that group a topic’s top words into “buckets” using word embeddings. Three variants are described: agglomerative clustering (TBuckets-Clustering), SVD-based bucketing (TBuckets-SVD), and SVD with a parameter-free reorganization heuristic (TBuckets-SVD-Reorg).
- Core contributions (as I see them): (1) A practical, largely parameter-free SVD-based bucketing framework whose coherence correlates strongly with human judgments on NYT and 20NG; (2) An interpretable “largest-bucket” coherence scoring that connects directly to human intuitions of single-theme topics; (3) A demonstration that selecting topic runs by TBuckets coherence can modestly improve weakly supervised classification.

Strengths:
- Empirical correlation with human judgments is competitive or better than strong baselines: TBuckets-SVD-Reorg achieves r≈0.83–0.87 on NYT/20NG, outperforming Röder et al.’s best settings on 2/3 datasets; TBuckets-Clustering matches SOTA on Genomics (r≈0.774).
- The SVD-based variants are effectively parameter-free, a practical advantage over coherence frameworks requiring nontrivial configuration/tuning.
- The method is simple and interpretable: bucketing aligns with how annotators assess topicality; the “largest bucket” criterion operationalizes the single-theme intuition.
- Usefulness is illustrated beyond intrinsic evaluation: selecting higher-coherence runs improves macro-F1 on weakly supervised classification in 2–3 tasks (e.g., POLITICS vs SCIENCE from 91.09 to 96.19).
- Clear algorithmic description of the reorganization heuristic grounded in observable statistics (AWPS, AWES) is a thoughtful attempt to avoid manual thresholds.

Weaknesses:
- Mixed results on Genomics (SVD(-Reorg) r≈0.68–0.69), and overall sensitivity to embedding domain mismatch is acknowledged but not systematically addressed (no experiments with in-domain embeddings).
- Statistical rigor is lacking: no confidence intervals, significance tests, or model selection safeguards; improvements vs baselines may not be statistically robust.
- Methodological details impede reproducibility: clustering distance threshold selection is unclear; handling of OOV words, embedding normalization, eigenvector sign ambiguity, and the choice of “first n eigenvectors” lack justification/ablation.
- The coherence score relies mainly on the largest bucket, potentially penalizing legitimately multi-faceted topics; no analysis of failure modes or bias toward single-theme topics.
- The classification gains are modest/inconsistent (e.g., MED–SPACE reversed; POLITICS–RELIGION S1 > S2-high), and the link between coherence differences and downstream accuracy lacks deeper analysis.

Questions to authors:
- How sensitive are results to the embedding source/dimension and to OOV handling? Can in-domain embeddings rescue Genomics?
- Why use the first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers? How do you handle eigenvector sign/scale? Any ablations on number of eigenvectors?
- How is the agglomerative Max Dist chosen in practice? Is there a validation protocol?
- Are the reported r improvements statistically significant vs Röder et al.? Please provide CIs and p-values.
- How does performance vary with the number of top words N and with alternative scoring that accounts for multiple mid-sized buckets?
