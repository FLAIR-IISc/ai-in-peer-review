Summary and contributions
The paper introduces TBuckets, a family of methods to estimate LDA topic coherence by grouping the top-N topic words into “buckets” based on distributional semantics. Three instantiations are proposed: (a) TBuckets-Clustering, which agglomeratively clusters word embeddings; (b) TBuckets-SVD, which assigns words to buckets defined by principal directions (eigenvectors) of the embedding matrix via SVD; and (c) TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, which refines SVD buckets using a parameter-free reorganization criterion driven by word–word and word–eigenvector similarities. Topic coherence is then computed from properties of the largest bucket (size, LDA-order weighting, eigenvector similarity), with additional weighting by the largest singular value for SVD methods. Experiments on three public datasets (NYT, 20NG, Genomics) evaluate Pearson correlations against human judgments, and a weak supervision application shows that selecting topic models by TBuckets coherence can improve downstream classification.

As seen, the primary contributions are:
1) A simple, interpretable bucket-based framework (TBuckets) for topic coherence grounded in word embeddings, including a parameter-free SVD-based variant with a reorganization step.
2) Empirical results showing competitive or superior correlations with human judgments compared to the best configurations reported in Röder et al. (2015) on two datasets, and a practical application demonstrating that TBuckets-based selection improves weakly supervised text classification performance on 20NG subsets.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity and interpretability. The “single large bucket implies a single coherent theme” intuition is easy to understand and aligns with human judgments of topic interpretability. The paper demonstrates the idea with walk-through examples and shows the formed buckets for several topics, which makes the signal used by the metric transparent.

- Competitive performance with limited tuning. TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforms the best settings from Röder et al. (2015) on NYT (0.837 vs. 0.806) and 20NG (0.869 vs. 0.859), while the SVD-based methods are parameter-free. TBuckets-Clustering also performs comparably on Genomics depending on linkage and threshold. This suggests that embedding-based bucketing is a viable alternative to co-occurrence-based coherence measures.

- Practical utility for downstream tasks. The weakly supervised classification experiment shows that selecting topic sets by TBuckets-SVD-Reorg leads to better macro-F1 in 3/4 binary 20NG tasks and that the highest-coherence runs substantially outperform the lowest-coherence runs (e.g., 96.19 vs. 91.09 for POLITICS vs SCIENCE), indicating meaningful correlation between TBuckets coherence and usefulness of topics for labeling.

- Simplicity and efficiency. Operating on 10×d embedding matrices and using off-the-shelf SVD or agglomerative clustering keeps computation lightweight. The reliance on pre-trained embeddings avoids building large co-occurrence statistics, which is advantageous in resource-constrained settings.

Weaknesses
- Incomplete methodological specification for SVD bucketing. The rationale and mechanics of using V’s first n eigenvectors as bucket identifiers need clearer justification. Since V spans feature space rather than the space of word instances, it is not obvious why eigenvectors of AᵀA should define “sub-themes” and why the number of buckets should default to n (topic word count). Further, assignment by nearest-eigenvector cosine is under-specified (e.g., normalization, handling ties).

- Reorganization criterion and thresholding require clarification. The dynamic threshold is written as AWES − AWPSAWES, which is ambiguous (is it AWES − AWPS/AWES?). The motivation for subtracting AWPS/AWES from AWES and the expected scale of this quantity are not analyzed. Without ablations isolating the effect of reorganization, it is difficult to assess whether gains over TBuckets-SVD are robust or due to overfitting to heuristics.

- Lack of statistical significance and robustness analyses. Improvements over Röder et al. (2015) are modest on 20NG and NYT, and performance drops significantly on Genomics. No significance testing (e.g., Fisher r-to-z) is provided, and there is no sensitivity study on the number of top words, choice of embeddings, or OOV handling. Given that embeddings were trained on general-domain corpora, the Genomics results suggest domain mismatch; this deserves a deeper analysis.

- Comparison baselines and claims about parameter tuning. The comparison uses the “best performing settings” from Röder et al. (2015), but it is not shown whether strong, widely adopted NPMI configurations perform similarly under the exact evaluation protocols. The claim that TBuckets-SVD variants are “completely parameter free” overlooks design choices such as singular-value weighting, the number of eigenvectors considered, and the composition of scoring components (size, WL, EVS), which act as hyperparameters.

- Limited details in the classification study. The number of annotators, labeling protocol, inter-annotator agreement, and significance of macro-F1 differences are not reported. The selection protocol (best 5 of 10 runs) might inflate gains without controlling for topic/model diversity. Providing these details would strengthen the application claims.

Questions to authors
- SVD bucketing: Why are the first n eigenvectors of V used as bucket identifiers rather than selecting k < n by energy (e.g., cumulative singular value mass) or using U (word-space) directions? How sensitive is performance to this choice?

- Reorganization threshold: Please clarify the threshold formula (exact parentheses) and provide intuition for its scale. What are typical values of AWPS and AWES across datasets?

- Scoring composition: How were the combinations (size, WL, EVS, WL*EVS) selected, and why is the largest singular value squared used as a weight? Are results robust to removing this weighting?

- Embeddings and OOV handling: Which exact GloVe model/version was used? How are OOV words treated in topics, and how often do they occur in the three datasets?

- Significance: Are the correlation differences vs. Röder et al. (2015) significant under Fisher r-to-z? Are the macro-F1 differences in the classification study statistically significant?

Suggestions for improvement
Essential:
- Clarify and justify the SVD-based bucketing mechanism and the reorganization threshold, and add ablations isolating each component (SVD without reorg, with/without singular-value weighting, with/without WL/EVS).
- Report statistical significance for correlation improvements and classification gains, and detail OOV handling and embedding settings.

Nice-to-have:
- Evaluate sensitivity to (a) number of top words per topic, (b) alternative embeddings (Word2Vec; in-domain embeddings for Genomics), and (c) different topic counts/runs.
- Compare directly to standard NPMI-based coherence under identical preprocessing to contextualize gains.
- Release code and scripts for reproducibility.
