- Proposes TBuckets, grouping top-N topic words into embedding-based “buckets”; coherent topics yield one dominant large bucket; three variants: Clustering, SVD, SVD-Reorg.  
- Main contributions: new embedding-based coherence family with parameter-free SVD and reorg; empirical Pearson correlations vs human judgments; application showing improved weakly supervised classification.  
- Strength: Clear, intuitive formulation that mirrors human inspection of topic word lists.  
- Strength: Competitive empirical performance with minimal tuning; SVD-Reorg outperforms Röder et al. on NYT and 20NG.  
- Strength: Sensible use of word embeddings removes dependence on corpus co-occurrence, aiding portability.  
- Strength: Reorganization step addresses realistic failure modes by moving words based on pull metrics.  
- Strength: Demonstrated downstream utility—selecting high-TBuckets topics improves macro-F1 in most binary tasks.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance or confidence intervals reported for Pearson correlations; modest gains may be unreliable.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to key design choices (top-N, embedding type/domain, similarity metric) is underexplored.  
- Weakness: SVD-based bucket construction is somewhat ad hoc—choice of first n eigenvectors, redundancy, computational cost, and tie handling need justification.  
- Weakness: Reorganization threshold formula is heuristic and poorly motivated; alternative thresholds and sensitivity analysis are needed.  
- Weakness: Evaluation scope/fairness details lacking—unclear whether Röder et al. baselines were reproduced comparably; Spearman correlations omitted.  
- Q: Are improvements over Röder et al. statistically significant (CIs or permutation tests)?  
- Q: How sensitive are results to N (e.g., N=15 or 20) and does the largest-bucket heuristic scale?  
- Q: How are OOV topic words handled; were domain-specific embeddings tried for Genomics and with what effect?  
- Q: Why use the first n eigenvectors for buckets; consider selecting k by explained variance or clustering; what is runtime per topic?  
- Q: Can you provide an ablation quantifying incremental gains from reorg and sensitivity of the dynamic threshold?  
- Q: Rationale for weighting by the square of the top singular value vs linear/no weighting?  
- Suggestion: Report Spearman correlations in addition to Pearson for rank consistency.  
- Suggestion: Provide error analysis where TBuckets underperforms (notably Genomics) to guide domain-specific fixes.  
- Suggestion: Clarify and report stability of clustering distance threshold; ensure fair baseline tuning.  
- Suggestion: For classification experiments, report variance across runs and significance tests; release code or pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Overall: Thoughtful, well-motivated approach with promising results, but needs significance tests, sensitivity analyses, and clearer justification of SVD/Reorg design choices.
