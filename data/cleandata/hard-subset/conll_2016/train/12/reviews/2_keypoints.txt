- Proposes a simple, elegant modification to the RNN output layer that models noise by multiplying the softmax by a learned “noise” FC layer to train on both gold and projected annotations.  
- Strong empirical results on POS tagging for eight simulated low-resource languages and two real low-resource languages.  
- Method is modular and likely useful across other low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Experimental setup (training with as little as ~1000 token annotations) is unusual and may be unrealistic for many settings.  
- Lacks analysis of performance vs. amount of gold data — should plot accuracy while varying gold annotation size to show when the method helps or harms.  
- The paper only evaluates cross-lingual projection with the same tagset; potential applicability to other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tagsets is not explored.  
- Surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in the objective; should test tuning the relative contribution of the two terms.  
- Clarify the representation of projected labels \tilde{y} (token-level vs. aggregated type-level distribution) and how to handle words that are never aligned.  
- Minor editorial issues: line 357 unclear about \tilde{y} format; line 267 has a bracket typo.
