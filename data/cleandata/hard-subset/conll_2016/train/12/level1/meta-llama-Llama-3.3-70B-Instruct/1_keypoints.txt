- Proposes a deep model that jointly learns from annotated and projected data for sequence tagging.
- Introduces an explicit noise transformation layer that models mapping between clean and noisy tags.
- Evaluates on simulated and real low-resource languages, claiming state-of-the-art results.
- Novel and effective modeling of noise in cross-lingual projection.
- Bidirectional LSTM with a noise layer enables joint learning of clean and noisy tags.
- Robustness demonstrated by evaluation on both simulated and real low-resource languages.
- Experimental results outperform other state-of-the-art algorithms.
- Assumes noise can be modeled by a simple linear transformation, which may not hold universally.
- Small annotated dataset size may be insufficient to train a robust model.
- Evaluation limited to only two real low-resource languages, possibly not representative.
- Comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods may be incomplete.
- How was the optimal size of the annotated dataset determined?
- Please provide more details on the noise transformation layer initialization and update during training.
- How does the approach handle highly noisy or inconsistent projected tags?
- Can the authors analyze the learned noise transformation matrices to show what errors are being corrected?
