- Proposes a simple noise-transition (linear) layer on top of a BiLSTM to map clean tag distributions to noisy cross-lingual projected tags during training.  
- Two-stage training recipe: pretrain on gold-only then jointly train with gold + projected data for stability.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains across 8 simulated low-resource settings and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Primary contribution: explicit noise-transition layer enabling the model to learn when to trust and correct projections.  
- Secondary contributions: empirical study outperforming prior work (Duong et al., 2014a) and analyses of learned confusion matrices.  
- Noise matrices A are interpretable and capture plausible cross-lingual tag mappings (e.g., determiner↔noun).  
- Strong baselines and careful comparisons (gold-only, proj-only, union, MaxEnt, Duong et al.) strengthen empirical claims.  
- Two-stage training is practical, easy to reproduce, and appears effective in low-resource settings.  
- Visualization and analysis of A provide qualitative insight linking projection noise patterns to performance differences.  
- Claim mismatch: paper emphasizes learning with 1k gold tokens but real-language experiments use ~5k tokens, weakening the low-resource claim.  
- Conceptually incremental: label-transition matrices for noisy labels are known in prior literature; novelty is moderate given small average gains.  
- No statistical significance tests or variance reporting; small gains in some settings may not be robust.  
- Limited ablations/sensitivity analyses (pretraining vs joint, A initialization/constraints, parallel data amount/quality, alignment filtering, many-to-one handling).  
- Noise model is global and context-independent; projection errors are often context-dependent and may require conditioning on alignment/confidence.  
- Missing training/reproducibility details (dropout, early stopping, batch size, A initialization, embeddings, learning rate schedule, epochs, dev selection, tagset mapping).  
- Questions for authors: why use 5k gold tokens for real languages instead of 1k; how is A initialized/regularized; how are tags mapped to Universal tagset; results for key ablations; robustness across seeds and statistical significance?  
- Practical suggestions: refine heuristic for unaligned/multi-aligned tokens (use local context or alignment probabilities), fix reference formatting, and release code/data splits.
