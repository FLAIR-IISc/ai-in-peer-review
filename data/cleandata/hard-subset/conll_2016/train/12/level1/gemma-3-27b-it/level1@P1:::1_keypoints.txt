- Introduces a learnable noise layer that maps projected tags to the target tagset to explicitly model projection errors.  
- Proposes a joint BiLSTM training framework that combines gold-standard and noisy projected data to learn which projections to trust.  
- Validates the approach on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, showing consistent improvements and state-of-the-art results.  
- Clearly motivates the problem of noisy cross-lingual projection and frames the solution well.  
- The noise layer is a novel and effective alternative to prior filtering or correction methods.  
- Empirical results are strong, outperforming baselines and demonstrating practical value on real languages.  
- Experimental design is solid, using both simulated and real languages with fair baseline comparisons.  
- Analysis and visualization of the learned noise matrix provide qualitative insight into error patterns.  
- Limited analysis of word alignment quality (Giza++) and its influence on projection noise and performance.  
- Lacks detailed description of hyperparameter tuning (search process, validation size, sensitivity).  
- No thorough analysis of added computational cost from the noise matrix (training/inference/memory).  
- Assumes a universal tagset; robustness to very different tagset structures is not evaluated.  
- How sensitive are results to word alignment quality and were other aligners or filtering strategies tested?  
- Please provide hyperparameter search space, validation setup, and sensitivity analyses.  
- What is the computational overhead (training time, inference time, memory) introduced by the noise layer?  
- How well does the model generalize to languages with tagsets that differ substantially from the universal tagset?  
- Writing is clear overall, though some technical details (e.g., exact loss formulation) need more explanation.  
- Releasing code would improve reproducibility and facilitate follow-up research.  
- Consider discussing potential applications of the noise-layer approach beyond POS tagging.
