- Core idea: augment a BiLSTM POS tagger with a trainable “noise layer” to learn when and how much to trust noisy cross-lingual projected tags.  
- Evaluation: tested on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), achieving competitive or state-of-the-art results.  
- Primary contribution: introduction of a noise layer that explicitly models and corrects projection errors within a sequence tagging architecture.  
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive multilingual evaluation, including real-world low-resource scenarios, strengthening empirical support.  
- Analysis: visualization and analysis of learned noise transformation matrices offer insight into the types of projection errors the model corrects.  
- Strength — Novelty: explicit, trainable noise modeling moves beyond simple filtering or heuristics for projected data.  
- Strength — Empirical results: consistent improvements across diverse languages demonstrate robustness.  
- Strength — Evaluation breadth: thorough experimental setup facilitates comparison with prior work.  
- Strength — Interpretability: noise matrices provide useful interpretive insights into model behavior.  
- Weakness — Alignment: limited analysis of how alignment quality influences performance and noise modeling.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: insufficient discussion of sensitivity to learning rate, noise layer size, and required tuning.  
- Weakness — Complexity: addition of noise layer increases parameters and complexity with little discussion of computational cost or scalability.  
- Question — Hyperparameters: please detail sensitivity and tuning required for noise-layer-related hyperparameters.  
- Question — Alignment impact: did you test different aligners or filtering by alignment confidence to measure impact on performance?  
- Question — Computational cost: how does model runtime/parameter count compare to other state-of-the-art low-resource POS taggers?
