- Proposes a neural noise layer that learns to transform noisy cross-lingual projected POS tags into more accurate labels.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over state-of-the-art across eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Visualizes learned noise transformation matrices to reveal error patterns the model corrects.

- Novel, well-motivated method that explicitly models projection noise rather than ignoring or pre-cleaning it.  
- Strong empirical evaluation showing consistent gains over strong baselines, including Duong et al. (2014a).  
- Visualization of noise matrices provides interpretable insights (e.g., NUM vs DET confusion, differing noise levels across languages).  
- Practical relevance: achieves state-of-the-art performance with only ~1,000 gold tokens.

- Lacks detailed analysis of why the specific noise-layer formulation outperforms alternatives.  
- Real-language evaluation is limited to only two low-resource languages.  
- No comparison to other noise-handling techniques like heuristic filtering or alternative noise models.  
- Missing discussion of computational overhead (training time and model size) introduced by the noise layer.

- How is the noise matrix A initialized and how do you prevent poor local optima during training?  
- How does the method handle languages with very different syntactic structures or word orders?  
- Clarify how the reported “1000 tokens” of gold data were split between training and development.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
