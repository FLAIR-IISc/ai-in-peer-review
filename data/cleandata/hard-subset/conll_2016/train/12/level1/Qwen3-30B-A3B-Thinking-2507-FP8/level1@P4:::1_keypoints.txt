- Paper: neural POS tagger for low-resource languages using small gold data plus noisy cross-lingual projected data.
- Contributions: novel noise layer modeling transformation from projected to gold tags; joint training on gold and projected data; SOTA evaluation on simulated and real low-resource languages.
- Strength: Principled treatment of projection noise via a well-integrated noise layer and clear joint training objective.
- Strength: Strong empirical results—consistent SOTA across 8 simulated languages and Malagasy/Kinyarwanda, with up to 3–4% gains using only 1,000 gold tokens.
- Strength: Interpretability through learned transformation matrices that reveal language-specific noise patterns.
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to separate the noise layer’s effect from the benefit of joint training.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of why the model learns particular transformation patterns (no deeper linguistic explanation).
- Weakness: No comparison to other noisy-label techniques (noise-robust losses, data augmentation, self-training), so relative advantage is unclear.
- Question: Why is two-stage training (pretrain on gold, then joint) necessary—does end-to-end training from random init fail and were alternatives tried?
- Question: How were tagset mismatches handled in real-language experiments given the claim the noise layer can handle mismatched tagsets?
- Question: How does the amount of parallel data affect the noise layer’s ability to learn transformations, given larger gains for Malagasy than Kinyarwanda?
