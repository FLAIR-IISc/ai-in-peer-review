- Novel noise-layer that models transformation from projected tags to true tags, potentially generalizable beyond POS tagging.
- Joint training on 1,000 gold tokens plus noisy projected data using a BiLSTM with a noise transformation matrix.
- Strong empirical validation across 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, outperforming prior methods.
- Learned noise matrices provide diagnostic insight into common projection errors and reliably projected tags.
- Conceptual novelty and practical impact: explicit noise modeling enables effective use of minimal gold data and parallel corpora.
- Comprehensive evaluation with consistent improvements; reported accuracies include 86.3% (Malagasy) and 82.5% (Kinyarwanda).
- Visualization of noise matrices reveals specific error patterns (e.g., Swedish PRT vs X, Malagasy noun→adjective).
- Clear technical presentation: well-structured BiLSTM + noise layer, precise formulation, and a motivated two-stage training procedure.
- Missing ablation studies to quantify the noise layer’s contribution versus BiLSTM architecture or joint training.
- Limited discussion of failure modes, including effects of poor alignments or large source-target syntactic divergence.
- Comparisons focus on Duong et al. (2014a) and do not address more recent (2015–2016) low-resource POS methods.
- Insufficient implementation details about the two-stage training (e.g., pretraining data proportion) and sensitivity to choices.
- Request for details on two-stage training: what fraction of gold data is used for pretraining vs joint training and sensitivity?
- Request for comparisons to recent transfer/multilingual or improved-alignment approaches and for analysis of where the noise layer fails.
