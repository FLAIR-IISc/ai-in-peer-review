- Novel method: introduce a learnable noise layer (matrix A) that maps clean model tag distributions to noisy projected labels for POS tagging with mixed-quality data.  
- Model architecture: BiLSTM sequence tagger trained jointly on small gold data and larger noisy projected data, with the noise layer applied to projected outputs.  
- Contribution: principled framework for integrating distant supervision by explicitly modeling projection noise rather than treating noisy labels as ground truth.  
- Empirical validation: evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, consistently outperforming strong baselines and prior SOTA.  
- Methodological elegance: directly models systematic projection errors while sharing sequence representations with gold data.  
- Thorough experiments: controlled Europarl splits plus real-world Malagasy and Kinyarwanda tests demonstrate robustness beyond simulations.  
- Clear gains: consistent performance improvements across all ten languages; naive union of gold+proj data performs poorly.  
- Interpretability: visualization of learned noise matrices reveals strong diagonals and informative off-diagonal confusions aligned with intuition about language pairs.  
- Weakness — training ablation missing: no empirical comparison of the proposed two-stage (pretrain then joint) training vs single-stage joint training from random init.  
- Weakness — limited linguistic analysis: learned noise matrices are not deeply connected to concrete linguistic/alignment error examples.  
- Weakness — scalability concern: full K×K noise matrix may not scale to large label spaces (e.g., hundreds of labels); alternatives like low-rank approximations are not explored.  
- Question: provide quantitative results comparing two-stage training to single-stage joint training from random initialization.  
- Question: clarify projected-label generation procedure (apparent conflict between retaining only one-to-one alignments and assigning distributions for many/none-aligned tokens).  
- Question: have prominent off-diagonal entries been validated against actual frequent projection errors measured on a gold dev set?
