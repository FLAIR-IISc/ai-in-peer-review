- Novel sequence-tagging method adds an explicit noise layer to correct errors from cross-lingual tag projection.
- Joint learning framework trains on both gold-standard and projected-tag corpora using a BiLSTM with the noise layer.
- Noise layer models the mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource gold tags and is learned jointly.
- Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource settings plus Malagasy and Kinyarwanda, reporting state-of-the-art results.
- Effectively leverages noisy projected data to improve performance in low-resource scenarios.
- Demonstrates robustness to noise in projected labels.
- Achieves empirical state-of-the-art performance on the tested settings and languages.
- Evaluation is limited to a small set of languages and datasets, needing broader validation.
- Approach depends on high-quality parallel data for projection, which may not always be available.
- Joint training may incur significant computational complexity compared to other methods.
- Open questions: how to handle lower-quality parallel data, detailed computational cost comparisons, and plans for wider language/dataset evaluation.
