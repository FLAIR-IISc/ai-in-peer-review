- Introduces a noise transformation layer to model mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags.  
- Proposes a joint training objective combining two cross-entropy terms to learn from gold and projected annotations.  
- Evaluates the approach on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Effectively leverages noisy projected data to significantly improve POS tagging accuracy via the noise layer.  
- Shows robustness to different types and amounts of noise across simulations and real-language experiments.  
- Claims generalizability of the approach to other sequence tagging tasks and modeling frameworks.  
- Evaluation is limited to POS tagging and lacks experiments on other sequence tagging tasks.  
- No ablation study is provided to assess the contribution of individual components.  
- Limited analysis and visualization of the learned noise transformation matrices to interpret model behavior.  
- Request for more details on training: optimization algorithm and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Question about selection and impact of the size of the noise transformation matrix A.  
- Request for further analysis/visualization of learned noise transformation matrices to understand error correction.
