**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to sequence tagging that learns to correct errors from cross-lingual projection using an explicit noise layer. The approach is framed as joint learning over two corpora, one tagged with gold standard and the other with projected tags. The model uses a bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM) network with a noise transformation layer to encode the mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags. The paper evaluates the approach on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real low-resource languages, Malagasy and Kinyarwanda, and achieves state-of-the-art results.
