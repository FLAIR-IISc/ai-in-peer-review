- Proposes a sequence-tagging model that corrects cross-lingual projection errors via an explicit noise layer.  
- Frames learning as joint training on a gold-tagged corpus and a projected-tag corpus.  
- Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) and reports state-of-the-art results.  
- Contribution: explicitly models the transformation between tagsets within a deep neural network.  
- Contribution: jointly trains on gold and projected annotations to leverage both data sources.  
- Contribution: effectively uses parallel data to improve POS tagging for low-resource languages.  
- Strength: novel and effective noise-modeling approach for cross-lingual projection.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art empirical performance across multiple settings.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: strong experimental evaluation with multiple baselines and analysis of learned noise matrices.  
- Weakness: analysis of the learned noise transformation matrices is limited and could be deeper.  
- Weakness: method assumes availability of parallel data, which may not hold for all low-resource languages.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to other approaches (e.g., methods leveraging language relatedness).  
- Question: please provide a more in-depth analysis of the learned noise transformation matrices and their limitations.  
- Question: how does the approach perform when parallel data is limited or unavailable?  
- Question: can you compare the method to approaches that use language relatedness information (e.g., dependency parsing or lexical resources)?
