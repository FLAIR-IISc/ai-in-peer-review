- Paper proposes a BiLSTM sequence-tagging model with an explicit noise layer to correct errors from cross-lingual projection using joint learning on gold and projected corpora.  
- Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource settings plus Malagasy and Kinyarwanda, achieving state-of-the-art POS tagging results.  
- Contribution: explicit modeling of projection noise via a noise transformation layer.  
- Contribution: joint learning framework combining gold-standard and projected data for improved tagging.  
- Contribution: empirical state-of-the-art performance on multiple low-resource languages and simulations.  
- Strength: novel and meaningful approach to handling cross-lingual projection noise.  
- Strength: effectively leverages projected data to boost POS tagging accuracy.  
- Strength: achieves strong empirical results that outperform baselines and prior work.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and well-organized for readability.  
- Strength: strong experimental evaluation with comparisons to several baselines and SOTA methods.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of the noise layer and its detailed effect on model performance.  
- Weakness: evaluation is restricted to POS tagging; other tasks (NER, parsing) not tested for generalizability.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of the approach’s limitations and potential failure modes.  
- Weakness: minor textual errors (typos, missing references).  
- Question: provide deeper analysis of the noise layer and its impact on results.  
- Question: evaluate the method on additional tasks like NER and dependency parsing.  
- Question: discuss limitations, applications, and possible extensions of the approach.  
- Question: give more experimental details, including BiLSTM hyperparameters and setup.  
- Additional comment: overall a significant, novel, and effective contribution to low-resource language processing despite minor issues.
