- Summary: BiLSTM sequence-tagging model with an explicit noise layer jointly trained on gold and projected tags to correct cross-lingual projection errors; evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, matching or exceeding SOTA.

- Contribution: Introduces a noise-layer-based method to correct errors in cross-lingual tag projection for sequence tagging.

- Contribution: Proposes joint end-to-end learning on gold and projected annotations to improve POS tagging in low-resource settings.

- Contribution: Designs a noise transformation layer encoding mappings between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags.

- Strength: Effectively leverages projected annotations to improve POS tagging under low-resource conditions.

- Strength: Demonstrates robustness to noise in projected annotations by learning to correct errors.

- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art or better results across all tested simulated and real settings.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), so generalization is unclear.

- Weakness: Approach depends on word alignments, which can be noisy and may hurt performance.

- Weakness: Joint learning of gold and projected annotations increases computational complexity.

- Question: How will the method be extended to other languages and domains?

- Question: How are word alignments handled and noisy alignments mitigated?

- Question: How can the computational complexity be reduced for practical real-world use?
