**Summary and Main Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel approach to sequence tagging that learns to correct errors from cross-lingual projection using an explicit noise layer. The authors propose a deep neural network model that jointly trains on gold standard and projected annotations, allowing the model to learn from both sources of data. The main contributions of this work are:

1. The introduction of a noise transformation layer to model the mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags.
2. The development of a joint training objective that combines two cross-entropy terms to learn from both gold standard and projected data.
3. The evaluation of the proposed approach on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real low-resource languages, Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of noisy projected data**: The proposed approach demonstrates a significant improvement in POS tagging accuracy by leveraging the noisy projected data. The noise transformation layer allows the model to learn from the projected data while accounting for the errors and biases.
2. **Robustness to noise**: The authors show that the proposed approach is robust to different types and amounts of noise in the projected data, as demonstrated by the results on the eight simulated low-resource settings and the two real low-resource languages.
3. **Generalizability**: The proposed approach is general and can be applied to other sequence tagging tasks and modeling approaches, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: While the authors evaluate their approach on a range of languages and settings, the evaluation is limited to POS tagging. It would be beneficial to evaluate the approach on other sequence tagging tasks to demonstrate its generalizability.
2. **Lack of ablation study**: The authors do not provide an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of each component of the proposed approach. This would help to understand the importance of each component and identify potential areas for improvement.
3. **Limited analysis of noise transformation layer**: While the authors provide some analysis of the noise transformation layer, it would be beneficial to provide more in-depth analysis and visualization of the learned noise transformation matrices to better understand how the model is learning to correct errors from cross-lingual projection.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the training process, including the optimization algorithm and hyperparameter tuning?
2. How do you select the size of the noise transformation matrix A, and what is the impact of different sizes on the performance of the model?
3. Can you provide more analysis and visualization of the learned noise transformation matrices to better understand how the model is learning to correct errors from cross-lingual projection?
