- Introduces a BiLSTM sequence-tagging approach using cross-lingual projection with an explicit noise layer to handle projection noise.
- Primary contribution: models the transformation between projected and gold tags so the model learns when and how much to trust projected data.
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates the method on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Tertiary contribution: provides empirical analysis of the learned noise transformation matrix to reveal tag confusion patterns.
- Strength: explicit noise modeling with a learned noise matrix and de-noising during backprop addresses projection inaccuracies.
- Strength: strong empirical performance across simulated and real low-resource languages, notably SOTA on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.
- Strength: well-defined experimental setup (CoNLL data, 1,000-token training sets) enabling fair comparison to prior work.
- Strength: clear, detailed explanation of the method with helpful mathematical formulation and diagrams.
- Weakness: analysis of the noise transformation matrix is superficial and lacks linkage to specific linguistic phenomena.
- Weakness: method’s performance depends on word alignment quality but sensitivity to different aligners/parameters is not investigated.
- Weakness: two-stage pre-training strategy is used without ablation studies to justify its necessity or explore alternatives.
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis of specific tagging errors beyond overall accuracy metrics.
- Question: can you relate observed noise matrix patterns to particular linguistic differences between source and target languages?
- Question: how sensitive is performance to word alignment quality, and were different alignment tools or parameters tested?
- Question: was an ablation study done to evaluate the benefit of the two-stage training strategy, and what were the results?
- Additional comment: reference list is incomplete (e.g., "Ammar et al.,? 2016").
- Additional comment: consider adding a discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed method versus existing approaches.
