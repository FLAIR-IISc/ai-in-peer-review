- Tackles low-resource POS tagging using cross-lingual projection, which introduces noisy labels.  
- Main contribution: neural model with an explicit noise layer to learn and correct projection errors via joint training on gold and projected data.  
- Empirical claim: matches or outperforms state-of-the-art on simulated low-resource settings and Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.  
- Strength: practical solution that explicitly models projection noise instead of treating all projected data equally.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation across simulated and real-world scenarios with consistent improvements.  
- Strength: qualitative insight via visualization of the learned noise transformation matrix.  
- Weakness: insufficient motivation for why a noise layer is preferable to heuristic filtering methods for cleaning projections.  
- Weakness: choice of BiLSTM is conventional and lacks justification versus other sequence models (e.g., Transformers).  
- Weakness: experimental analysis lacks detailed breakdown of success/failure cases and which projection errors are mitigated.  
- Weakness: paper does not discuss the potential impact of word alignment quality on model performance.  
- Question: why choose a noise layer over traditional filtering based on alignment scores?  
- Question: what is the computational/training-time cost of the noise layer compared to a standard BiLSTM?  
- Question: can you provide detailed error analysis showing which types of projection errors are handled well or remain challenging?
