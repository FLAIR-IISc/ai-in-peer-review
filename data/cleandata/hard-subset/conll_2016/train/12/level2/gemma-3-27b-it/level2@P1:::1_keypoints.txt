- Proposed model: neural network for low-resource POS tagging using cross-lingual projection with an explicit learned noise transformation.
- Novel contribution: noise layer (matrix A) that models and corrects systematic projection errors.
- Strong empirical results: equals or exceeds state-of-the-art on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Clear presentation: well-written motivation and helpful model illustration (Figure 2).
- Comprehensive evaluation: comparisons to strong baselines and insightful ablation studies (Annotated, Projected, Ann+Proj).
- Weakness: limited qualitative analysis of the learned noise matrix A; lacks specific tag-confusion examples.
- Weakness: system performance depends on word alignment quality; impact of alignment errors not thoroughly explored.
- Weakness: no hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., learning rate, hidden size).
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis identifying remaining persistent mispredictions by tag.
- Question: request examples of tag confusions corrected by matrix A and reasons for frequent incorrect mappings.
- Question: ask whether different word alignment tools or filtering strategies were tested to assess alignment impact.
- Question: ask how sensitive the model is to the size of initial gold annotated data (e.g., performance with ~100 tokens).
- Question: ask whether a linear matrix A is justified or if non-linear noise transformations were considered.
- Additional comment: phrase "uniformly equals or exceeds" in abstract is slightly too strong and should be softened.
- Additional comment: discuss computational cost of the proposed model compared to alternatives.
- Additional comment: suggest noting potential applications of the noise modeling technique beyond POS tagging.
