- Introduces a learnable noise layer that models the transformation between projected and ground-truth POS tags to discount unreliable projections.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art POS tagging across eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Integrates explicit noise modeling into a BiLSTM for end-to-end training.  
- Clearly motivates the problem of noisy cross-lingual projection and its impact on low-resource POS tagging.  
- Presents a novel, data-driven noise layer that offers interpretable transformation matrices, improving over heuristic approaches.  
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation with both simulated experiments and real-language tests.  
- Compares against strong, established baselines (e.g., Duong et al. 2014a).  
- Explains the model architecture and a two-stage training procedure in detail.  
- Offers limited analysis of the learned transformation matrices; lacks deeper insight into common tag confusions and language-specific patterns.  
- Relies on word alignment quality but does not analyze sensitivity to different alignment tools or parameters.  
- Omits details on computational cost (training time, GPU/memory requirements).  
- Assumes comparable tagsets; robustness to drastically different tagging schemes is not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Question: What are the most common tag confusions observed in the learned transformation matrices across languages?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to word alignment quality and have alternative alignment tools/parameters been tested?  
- Question: What computational resources (GPU memory, training time) were required for the experiments?  
- Question: How does performance degrade when tagsets differ substantially compared to methods avoiding direct tag mapping?  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Suggest adding discussion of future work (e.g., other NLP tasks or alternative noise models).  
- Fix inconsistent reference formatting.
