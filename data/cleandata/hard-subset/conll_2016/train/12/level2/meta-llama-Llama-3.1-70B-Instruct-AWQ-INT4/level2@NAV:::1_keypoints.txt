- Proposes a sequence-tagging method that corrects cross-lingual projection errors via an explicit noise layer.  
- Frames learning jointly over a small gold-tagged corpus and a larger projected-tag corpus.  
- Uses a BiLSTM with a noise layer to map projected high-resource tags to low-resource tags.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Effectively reduces projection errors, demonstrating strong empirical performance.  
- The noise layer is a key novelty enabling learning of tag mappings.  
- The approach is generalizable to other sequence tagging tasks.  
- Assumes availability of gold-standard tags for a small subset, which may not hold in practice.  
- Requires substantial parallel data for projection, limiting applicability to some languages.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of the noise layer’s mechanics and its impact on performance.  
- Requests more details on how the noise layer is learned and influences results.  
- Asks how the method performs when no gold-standard tags are available.  
- Seeks clarification on applying the approach to other sequence tagging tasks.
