- Summary: Proposes a neural sequence-tagging model with an explicit noise layer that corrects cross-lingual projection errors, jointly trained on gold and projected data and evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource settings with strong results.

- Contribution — Novel approach: Introduces a neural model that models and corrects projection noise via an explicit noise layer.

- Contribution — Joint training: Trains the model end-to-end on both gold-standard and projected data to leverage both signal types.

- Contribution — Evaluation: Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), achieving equal or better than prior work.

- Strength — Effective use of projected data: Demonstrates projected data can improve POS tagging accuracy in low-resource scenarios.

- Strength — Robustness to noise: The explicit noise layer enables the model to learn to correct noisy projections.

- Strength — Strong empirical performance: Matches or exceeds state-of-the-art results across experiments.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Real-world evaluation is limited to only two low-resource languages, affecting generalizability.

- Weakness — Parallel-data assumption: Method assumes availability of parallel data, which may not exist for many low-resource languages.

- Weakness — Computational cost: Training requires significant computational resources, which may limit adoption.

- Question — Scaling to other languages: How will the approach extend to additional low-resource languages and what challenges are expected?

- Question — Noise matrix details: Please provide more details on the noise transformation matrix A and how it is learned during training.

- Question — Handling extreme noise/bias: How does the model handle highly noisy or biased projected data, and what mitigation strategies are available?
