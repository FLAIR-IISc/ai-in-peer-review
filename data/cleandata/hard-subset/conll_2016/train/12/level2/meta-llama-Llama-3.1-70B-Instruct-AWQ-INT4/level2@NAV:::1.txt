**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to sequence tagging that learns to correct errors from cross-lingual projection using an explicit noise layer. The approach is framed as joint learning over two corpora, one tagged with gold standard and the other with projected tags. The model uses a bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM) network with a noise layer to encode the mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags. The approach is evaluated on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real low-resource languages, Malagasy and Kinyarwanda, and achieves state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel approach to sequence tagging that learns to correct errors from cross-lingual projection using an explicit noise layer.
2. The approach is framed as joint learning over two corpora, one tagged with gold standard and the other with projected tags.
3. The model uses a bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM) network with a noise layer to encode the mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags.

**Strengths**

1. The approach is effective in correcting errors from cross-lingual projection and achieves state-of-the-art results on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real low-resource languages.
2. The use of a noise layer allows the model to learn the mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags, which is a key contribution of the paper.
3. The approach is general and can be applied to other sequence tagging tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the gold standard tags are available for a small portion of the data, which may not always be the case in real-world scenarios.
2. The approach requires a large amount of parallel data, which may not be available for all languages.
3. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the noise layer and its impact on the performance of the model.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the noise layer is learned and how it affects the performance of the model?
2. How does the approach perform when the gold standard tags are not available for a small portion of the data?
3. Can you provide more details on how the approach can be applied to other sequence tagging tasks?
