- Contribution: Proposes a sequence-tagging approach with an explicit noise layer to correct cross-lingual projection errors.
- Contribution: Frames learning as joint training over a gold-tagged corpus and a projected-tag corpus within a deep neural network.
- Contribution: Evaluates on simulated low-resource settings and real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) with strong results.

- Strength: Effectively leverages noisy projected data to significantly improve POS tagging accuracy.
- Strength: Introduces a novel noise layer that models transformations between high-resource projected tags and low-resource tags.
- Strength: Reports state-of-the-art performance on both simulated and real low-resource datasets.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of the noise layer’s behavior and its impact on overall performance.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on high-quality parallel data, which may be unavailable for many low-resource languages.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to other state-of-the-art approaches that also utilize noisy projected data.

- Question: How sensitive is the noise layer to different types of projection noise?
- Question: How can the approach be adapted to scenarios with limited or low-quality parallel data?
- Question: Can you provide more detailed comparisons to other noisy-projection state-of-the-art methods?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written with clear explanations and strong experimental results; addressing the above weaknesses would strengthen it further.
