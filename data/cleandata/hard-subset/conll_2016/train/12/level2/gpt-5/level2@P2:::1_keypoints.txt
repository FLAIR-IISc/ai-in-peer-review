- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger with a learnable linear noise layer modeling mapping from gold to projected noisy labels, jointly trained on gold and projected parallel-data projections for low-resource tagging.

- Contribution 1: Simple, explicit noise-aware training objective using a learnable K×K confusion matrix on top of a BiLSTM.

- Contribution 2: Empirical evidence of consistent gains over strong baselines and prior SOTA on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.

- Contribution 3: Analysis of learned noise matrices providing interpretable insights into projection error patterns.

- Strength: Conceptually clean, transparent noise modeling (softmax(A)) that integrates with end-to-end backprop and is easy to implement.

- Strength: Consistent empirical improvements across many settings, including notable gains on Kinyarwanda and Malagasy.

- Strength: Evaluation includes both simulated and genuinely low-resource languages, increasing practical relevance.

- Strength: Interpretability via heatmaps of A that correlate with language-specific projection errors.

- Strength: Sensible training recipe (pretrain on gold, then joint training) to avoid degenerate solutions.

- Weakness: Limited ablations and diagnostics—no quantitative results for training without pretraining, varying gold/projected weighting, or comparing the noise layer to a simple linear layer.

- Weakness: Potentially unrealistic simulated data conditions (large, filtered parallel corpora) that may overstate gains for true low-resource scenarios.

- Weakness: Missing reporting of variance or statistical significance for reported (often small) improvements.

- Weakness: Coarse handling of many-to-one/unaligned projections (sentence-level tag frequency) may inject bias and more principled alternatives are not explored.

- Weakness: Incomplete comparison to broader label-noise literature and lack of discussion on identifiability/regularization of the noise matrix A.

- Question: How sensitive are results to mixing ratios or loss weighting between gold and projected data?

- Question: Can you provide ablations for (a) no pretraining, (b) projected data without the noise layer, and (c) different A initializations/regularizations?

- Question: For Malagasy and Kinyarwanda, can you report results with only 1k gold tokens, and for simulated languages, results with less parallel data or relaxed alignment filtering?

- Question: Were multiple random seeds or statistical significance tests run for reported gains?

- Additional: Clarify NULL/many-to-one handling and consider alignment posteriors or soft projections instead of sentence-level distributions.

- Additional: Provide more details on regularization, hyperparameter tuning, and consider/ablate CRF output layer for sequence consistency.

- Additional: Fix citation formatting and better position relative to noisy-label neural network literature.

- Additional: Release code and preprocessed resources to aid reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Solid, practical, and interpretable contribution that merits acceptance but needs stronger ablations, clearer data-condition reporting, and tighter positioning versus label-noise work.
