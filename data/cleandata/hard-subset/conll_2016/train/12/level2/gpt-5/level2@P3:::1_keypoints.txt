- Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger that jointly learns from small gold data and noisy cross-lingual projected labels using a learnable K×K “noise” linear layer modeling projection distortions.  
- Trains in two stages: pretraining on gold data, then joint training on gold plus projected data with the noise layer.  
- Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource Europarl settings and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Reports consistent but sometimes modest gains over strong baselines (e.g., Duong et al.), with average improvements and higher scores on the two real languages.  
- Learned noise matrices (A) provide interpretable diagnostics showing language-specific projection errors (e.g., PRT↔X confusions).  
- Method is simple, low-parameter (K×K), practical, and scalable, requiring only parallel data, alignments, and an English tagger.  
- Two-stage training protocol is used to mitigate instability from noisy labels.  
- Evaluation is well-contextualized, comparing to neural and non-neural baselines under realistic low-resource splits.  
- Ambiguity in projection setup: conflicting descriptions about handling multi/NULL alignments vs. retaining only one-to-one links.  
- Lacks analysis of statistical significance and variance; reported gains (e.g., +0.4 avg) may be fragile without confidence intervals or multiple seeds.  
- Real-language training sizes contradict the 1k-token claim (Malagasy/Kinyarwanda use ~5k tokens); results on 1k for these languages are missing.  
- Noise model is global and context-independent; not conditioning A on alignment confidence or token features may underfit structured noise.  
- Reproducibility details are sparse: initialization of A, exact pretraining/finetuning schedules, epochs, early stopping, regularization/dropout are not specified.  
- Asks whether many-to-one/one-to-many/NULL alignments were used or discarded and requests proportions of each supervision case.  
- Queries initialization and constraints for the noise matrix A (identity/random/diagonal bias) and any stabilization techniques.  
- Requests training regimen details (epochs, early stopping, dropout) and whether results are averaged over multiple runs.  
- Suggests clarifying projection pipeline, reporting alignment-case proportions, and reconciling the 1k-token narrative with real-language setups.  
- Recommends adding training details, variance analysis across seeds, and small ablations (e.g., conditioning on alignment confidence or alignment-quality filtering).  
- Overall: a simple, well-motivated, interpretable method that yields consistent improvements, but would be stronger with clarifications and robustness/statistical reporting.
