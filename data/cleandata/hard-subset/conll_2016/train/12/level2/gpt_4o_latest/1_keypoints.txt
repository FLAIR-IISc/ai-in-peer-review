- Introduces a noise transformation layer that models the relationship between clean gold tags and noisy projected tags to handle projection inconsistencies.
- Integrates the noise model into a BiLSTM for joint training on gold-standard and projected data.
- Evaluated on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), achieving state-of-the-art performance.
- Novelty: provides explicit, interpretable noise modeling for cross-lingual projection, unlike prior implicit methods.
- Strong empirical results demonstrating effectiveness across diverse datasets and settings.
- High practical relevance for low-resource NLP where annotated data is scarce and projection is common.
- Comprehensive evaluation (simulated + real languages) and clear explanation of architecture and two-stage training.
- Limited analysis of failure cases and behavior under extremely noisy or sparse projected data.
- Scalability concerns due to reliance on parallel corpora and word alignments that may be unavailable or unreliable.
- Limited qualitative/interpretable analysis of the learned noise matrix and linguistic insights from it.
- Baseline comparisons omit recent multilingual pre-trained models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), limiting benchmarking completeness.
- Questions for authors: comparison to multilingual pre-trained models, applicability to other tasks (e.g., parsing, NER), and sensitivity to parallel corpus size/quality.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses and provide additional analyses.
