- Contribution: Proposes a neural sequence-tagging model with an explicit noise layer (transformation matrix) to model projection errors and enable joint training on small gold and large noisy data.
- Contribution: Provides strong empirical validation, achieving SOTA on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages under comparable data conditions.
- Contribution: Analyzes learned noise matrices, showing dominant diagonal signal and language-specific off-diagonal confusions.

- Strength: Elegant, well-motivated approach that learns noise patterns instead of relying on heuristic filtering; principled joint training objective.
- Strength: Thorough, rigorous evaluation with fair baselines (including strong BiLSTM) and extensive language coverage.
- Strength: Insightful interpretability via visualization of noise matrices that reveal systematic error patterns.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation and effective figures.

- Weakness: Projection strategy for tokens aligned to multiple or no English words is ambiguous and may provide weak signals; needs justification and impact analysis.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on the two-stage training procedure (pretraining criteria, sensitivity, evidence against single-stage) harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: Claim of noise-layer generality is unproven—experiments are limited to a BiLSTM and should include simpler models (e.g., MaxEnt) to isolate the noise layer's effect.

- Question: Please justify the chosen projection method for unaligned/multi-aligned tokens, compare alternatives, and report how often this occurs.
- Question: Provide detailed hyperparameters, stopping criteria, and sensitivity experiments for the two-stage training regime and quantify its impact.
- Question: Was the rectangular noise matrix feature (for mismatched tagsets) explored, and can it handle real-world tagset mismatches?
