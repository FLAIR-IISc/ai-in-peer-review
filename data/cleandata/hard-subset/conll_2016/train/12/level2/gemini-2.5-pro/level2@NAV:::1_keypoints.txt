- Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger for low-resource languages augmented with a trainable noise layer that models systematic errors between true and projected tags.  
- Trains the model jointly on small gold-standard data and larger noisy cross-lingual projection data.  
- Novel contribution: an explicit, learnable noise transformation matrix that lets the model learn when to trust projected labels instead of relying on heuristics.  
- Empirical contribution: comprehensive evaluation on ten languages (eight simulated, two real low-resource) showing consistent state-of-the-art improvements.  
- Strength: the noise layer is an elegant, well-motivated solution and visualizations show it captures meaningful cross-lingual divergences.  
- Strength: robust experimental results outperforming strong baselines including Duong et al. (2014a) across diverse languages.  
- Strength: strong practical impact for low-resource NLP by enabling effective use of parallel corpora with minimal annotation.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of limitations, e.g., dependence on word alignment quality and typological distance between languages.  
- Weakness: analysis could be deeper—missing ablation on sensitivity to amount of gold data (e.g., 100 vs 500 vs 1000 tokens).  
- Question: how does model performance correlate with underlying word alignment quality; is the method more robust when alignments are poorer?  
- Question: did you try fully joint training from scratch versus pre-training on gold data, and how much does performance degrade without pre-training?
