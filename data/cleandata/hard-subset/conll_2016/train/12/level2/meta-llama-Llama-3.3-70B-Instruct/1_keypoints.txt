- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM sequence-tagging model with an explicit noise layer that jointly learns from gold and cross-lingually projected noisy tags to correct projection errors; evaluated on eight simulated low-resource settings plus Malagasy and Kinyarwanda with SOTA results.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel error-correcting approach to sequence tagging using an explicit noise layer.

- Contribution: Frames training as joint learning over a gold-tagged corpus and a projected-tagged corpus.

- Contribution: Designs a noise transformation layer to map projected high-resource tags to low-resource tags.

- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple simulated and real low-resource languages.

- Strength: Effectively leverages noisy projected data to improve POS tagging accuracy.

- Strength: Technique is general and potentially applicable to other NLP tasks and models.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of the noise transformation layer and its specific impact on results.

- Weakness: Relies on the availability and quality of parallel data, which may be scarce or noisy.

- Weakness: Potentially high computational cost due to BiLSTM and joint training over two corpora.

- Question: Please provide deeper analysis of the noise transformation layer and its contribution to gains.

- Question: How does the method perform when parallel data is poor quality or very limited?

- Question: Provide details on computational complexity and applicability to other NLP tasks.
