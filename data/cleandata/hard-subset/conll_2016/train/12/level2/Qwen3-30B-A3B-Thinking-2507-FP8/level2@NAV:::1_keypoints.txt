- Introduces a neural POS tagger for low-resource languages that jointly learns from small gold data and noisy cross-lingual projected data.  
- Main contribution: an explicit noise transformation layer that models noise in projected tags to learn trust levels for annotations.  
- Empirical contribution: significantly outperforms baselines in simulated settings (8 languages) and real low-resource languages (Malagasy 86.3%, Kinyarwanda 82.5%).  
- Analysis contribution: learned transformation matrices reveal language-specific noise patterns useful for practitioners.  
- Strength: explicit noise modeling is novel and well-motivated compared to prior implicit approaches.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments showing consistent improvements and new state-of-the-art on target languages.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of noise matrices that sheds light on error types across languages.  
- Weakness: unclear separation of benefits from joint training versus the explicit noise layer—BiLSTM Ann+Proj baseline already improves performance.  
- Weakness: missing implementation details for the noise matrix A (initialization, size, effect on performance) hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: lacks evaluation of limitations under extremely noisy parallel data or large syntactic divergence between languages.
