1. Summary: Proposes a neural model with a noise transformation layer to jointly learn from gold and noisy projected POS tags, achieving SOTA on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages with 1000 gold tokens.

2. Contribution: Introduces a noise transformation layer that models mapping between projected and gold tags.

3. Contribution: Presents a joint learning framework combining gold and projected data without extra constraints.

4. Contribution: Provides comprehensive evaluation showing consistent improvements over prior work on simulated and real low-resource languages.

5. Strength: Addresses noisy cross-lingual projection with a well-motivated noise layer that intuitively learns when to trust projected tags.

6. Strength: Thorough evaluation across simulated (8 European) and real (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) settings demonstrates generalizability and consistent gains.

7. Strength: Clear visualizations of learned noise matrices illustrate how the model handles different noise types across languages.

8. Strength: Practical approach likely generalizable to other low-resource NLP tasks using distant supervision.

9. Weakness: Novelty is overstated; modeling label noise is not new and the paper misses key related work in noisy-label literature.

10. Weakness: Lacks discussion of practical limitations, e.g., performance with <500 gold tokens or very poor parallel data and failure cases.

11. Weakness: Insufficient training details for the noise layer (initialization of A, overfitting mitigation, rationale for two-stage training).

12. Weakness: Claims of being first to model noise are not adequately compared to existing noisy-label correction methods.

13. Question: How does performance scale with less gold data (e.g., 500 tokens) and what is the minimum gold data needed?

14. Question: What do off-diagonal patterns in the noise matrices reveal about types of projection noise across languages?

15. Question: How does the approach fare with poor-quality parallel data or very distant language pairs, and can examples of failure cases be given?

16. Overall: Strong, well-evaluated contribution to low-resource POS tagging but would benefit from better contextualization in noisy-label literature and discussion of practical limits.
