- Summary: Proposes a neural POS tagger for low-resource languages that jointly learns from a small gold dataset and noisy cross-lingual projected data using an explicit noise layer; evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real languages with consistent gains.

- Contribution 1: Introduces an explicit noise layer that models the transformation between projected tags and gold-standard tags.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates consistent performance improvements across simulated and real low-resource settings.

- Contribution 3: Analyzes the learned noise transformation matrix to provide insights into projected-tag noise.

- Strength 1: Addresses a practical NLP problem of leveraging noisy distant supervision with a novel, elegant noise-modeling approach.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple simulated languages and two real languages shows robustness.

- Strength 3: Noise-matrix analysis yields useful interpretability about how different noise types are handled.

- Weakness 1: Novelty relative to other joint-training approaches (beyond Duong et al.) is not sufficiently clarified.

- Weakness 2: Using only 1000 gold tokens may not reflect the realities of truly low-resource languages.

- Weakness 3: Paper does not assess sensitivity to word-alignment quality, despite reliance on projected tags.

- Weakness 4: Computational complexity and training time compared to baselines are not reported.

- Question 1: How sensitive is the method to word-alignment quality and divergent word order?

- Question 2: Can the approach handle mismatched POS tagsets across languages?

- Question 3: How does performance scale below 1000 gold tokens and what is the minimum gold data needed?
