- Proposes low-resource POS tagging method combining small gold data with cross-lingual projected tags and a noise transformation matrix A.  
- Uses a BiLSTM model with an additional noise layer that transforms projected tags to better match true tags.  
- Jointly trains on gold and projected data and evaluates on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages.  

- Key contribution: explicit modeling of projection noise via a learned transformation matrix A.  
- Key contribution: unified training framework integrating gold-standard and projected supervision.  
- Key contribution: comprehensive evaluation across simulated and real low-resource scenarios.  

- Strength: well-motivated and effective noise modeling; learned matrices show structured, learnable noise patterns.  
- Strength: strong and consistent empirical gains over baselines, including state-of-the-art on real languages.  
- Strength: clear problem statement and convincing demonstration that projected tags are problematic without correction.  

- Weakness: limited novelty—similar noise-handling ideas exist and comparisons to other noise methods are insufficient.  
- Weakness: lacks ablation studies isolating the noise layer’s contribution and robustness to projection quality.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and sensitivity to very low-quality or scarce parallel data and alignments.  

- Question: how does this differ from existing noise-robust methods and why is the transformation matrix preferable?  
- Question: what are ablation results versus random/noise baselines or alternative noise models (dropout, separate noise classifier)?  
- Question: how does the fixed-size matrix A scale to differing or finer-grained tagsets and mismatched tag inventories?  

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include comparisons to other noise methods, ablations, and discussions of limitations.  
- Reviewer confidence: high.
