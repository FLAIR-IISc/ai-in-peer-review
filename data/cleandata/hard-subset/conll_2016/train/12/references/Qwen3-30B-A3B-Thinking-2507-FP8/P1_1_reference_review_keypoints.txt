- Tackles low-resource POS tagging by combining small gold data with noisy cross-lingual projected tags and modeling projection noise explicitly.  
- Proposes a BiLSTM with a K×K noise transformation matrix that learns when to trust projected tags.  
- Evaluated on 8 simulated European languages and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), showing consistent improvements.

- Contribution: explicit modeling of the noise transformation between projected and true tags via a learned K×K matrix.  
- Contribution: comprehensive evaluation on both simulated and real low-resource languages demonstrating empirical gains.  
- Contribution: visual analysis of learned noise matrices revealing meaningful tag confusion patterns.

- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach showing that modeling projection noise improves performance over directly using projected tags.  
- Strength: strong empirical evaluation across diverse settings, outperforming prior state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Strength: insightful visualizations of noise matrices that reveal interpretable error patterns across languages.

- Weakness: novelty claim may be overstated given prior related work (e.g., Duong et al., 2014a) that also models projected data jointly.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation on real low-resource languages (only Malagasy and Kinyarwanda), reducing claims of broad generality.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of failure modes and limitations (e.g., poor parallel data quality, divergent language pairs).

- Question: clarify evaluation setup and why Kinyarwanda results outperform Garrette et al. (2013) — are setups comparable?  
- Question: explain linguistic reasons behind patterns in noise matrices (e.g., NUM vs DET confusions in Italian).  
- Question: provide examples/analysis showing how the model handles mismatching tagsets.

- Additional: clarify how the noise layer differs from label smoothing or confidence-weighting methods.  
- Additional: justify the two-stage training (pretrain on gold then joint training) and report results for joint training from scratch.  
- Additional: analyze for which languages or noise types the method yields the largest improvements.

- Overall: solid, empirically validated contribution that explicitly models projection noise, but needs clearer positioning vs prior work, broader real-language evaluation, and deeper failure-mode analysis.
