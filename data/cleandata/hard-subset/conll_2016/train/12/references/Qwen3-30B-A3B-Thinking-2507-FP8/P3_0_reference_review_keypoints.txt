- Proposes a learnable noise transformation matrix to model how noisy cross-lingual projected POS tags map to true tags.
- Combines small gold-standard datasets with noisy projected data in a neural model using an explicit noise layer.
- Evaluates across 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, showing consistent improvements over prior work.
- Provides visualizations of learned noise matrices revealing language-specific tag confusions and trust patterns.
- Novel methodological contribution: explicitly learning when/how much to trust projected annotations instead of cleaning or assuming reliability.
- Empirical strength: consistent accuracy gains (e.g., 91.7% vs 91.3% on simulated languages; improved Malagasy and Kinyarwanda results).
- Analysis strength: detailed examination of noise patterns (strong diagonals and interpretable off-diagonal confusions).
- Practical training: two-stage procedure (pretrain on gold, then joint training) to initialize and learn the noise matrix.
- Weakness: lacks comparison with recent multilingual transfer methods (mBERT, XLM-R) and more modern baselines.
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure modes and why performance varies across languages (e.g., Kinyarwanda vs Malagasy).
- Weakness: insufficient ablation/justification proving the necessity and unique contribution of the noise layer.
- Weakness: assumes availability of parallel data; scalability to languages without parallel corpora is not discussed.
- Weakness: computational cost and scalability of the K×K noise matrix for larger tagsets or tasks are not addressed.
- Asks for comparison results with XLM-R/mBERT and rationale for baseline choices.
- Asks how rectangular noise matrices are handled when tagsets mismatch and practical limitations of that case.
- Requests details on parallel data quality for Malagasy and Kinyarwanda and its impact on results.
- Requests concrete examples showing the model “ignoring” noisy projected verb tags and comparisons to other methods.
- Asks for evidence or examples of applying the noise-layer approach to other noisy supervision types (distant/crowdsourced).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include modern baselines and deeper failure-mode/limitation analysis.
