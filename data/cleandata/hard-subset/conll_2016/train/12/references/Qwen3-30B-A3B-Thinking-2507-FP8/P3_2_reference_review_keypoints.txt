- Paper proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger that models noise in cross-lingual projected tags via a learnable noise transformation matrix.  
- Approach combines small gold-standard data with noisy projected labels instead of relying on heuristics or direct use of projections.  
- Evaluated on 8 simulated low-resource European languages and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) with consistent improvements.  

- Key contribution: a principled noise transformation layer to map projected tags to true tags.  
- Key contribution: comprehensive empirical validation across simulated and real low-resource settings.  
- Key contribution: analysis and visualization of learned noise matrices showing systematic tag confusions.  

- Strength: novel method recognizing and modeling systematic (not random) noise in projected labels.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, improving state-of-the-art on multiple languages (e.g., Malagasy 86.3% vs 85.3%).  
- Strength: insightful visualizations revealing how the model trusts or downweights certain projections.  
- Strength: relatively simple, practical implementation potentially applicable to other noisy annotation tasks.  

- Weakness: lacks thorough ablation studies isolating the noise layer’s contribution versus joint training effects.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to other general noise-robust learning methods (e.g., symmetric cross-entropy, co-teaching).  
- Weakness: theoretical justification for the specific noise-layer formulation is weak or ad-hoc.  
- Weakness: limited number of real low-resource languages evaluated (only two), limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure cases or scenarios where the noise layer underperforms.  

- Question: how is the noise transformation matrix A initialized and does initialization affect results?  
- Question: how does the model compare to other noise-robust approaches (symmetric cross-entropy, co-teaching, Bayesian methods)?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of word alignment tool (Giza++ IBM model 3 vs alternatives like FastAlign)?  
- Question: how does parallel data size (e.g., Kinyarwanda 11k vs Malagasy 100k) affect projected tag quality and performance?  
- Question: what is the impact of using the universal POS tagset versus language-specific tagsets for low-resource languages?  

- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add ablation studies and comparisons to other noise modeling approaches.
