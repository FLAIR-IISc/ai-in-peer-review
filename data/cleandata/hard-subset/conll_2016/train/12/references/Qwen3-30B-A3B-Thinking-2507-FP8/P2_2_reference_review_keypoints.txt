- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger with an explicit noise layer to model transformation from cross-lingual projected tags to gold tags, jointly trained on noisy projected and gold data, showing improvements on simulated and real low-resource languages.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel noise layer (K×K matrix) that explicitly models noise in projected tags instead of treating them as direct supervision.

- Contribution 2: Jointly trains on gold-standard and noisy projected data so the noise layer learns when and how much to trust projections.

- Contribution 3: Provides comprehensive evaluation on 8 simulated European languages and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), outperforming prior SOTA.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated, simple, and effective approach with empirical gains and interpretable noise matrices.

- Strength 2: Strong experimental design combining fair simulation and real-world tests, showing consistent improvements.

- Strength 3: Insightful analysis of the learned noise transformation revealing language-specific noise patterns and correlation with parallel data quality.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty claim since label-noise modeling exists in supervised learning literature; lacks comparison to those methods.

- Weakness 2: Does not sufficiently explore failure cases or performance when source-target languages or parallel data are very poor or dissimilar.

- Weakness 3: Missing important training details (pretraining epochs, initialization of A, data balancing) hindering reproducibility.

- Weakness 4: No discussion of computational cost/efficiency impact from adding the noise layer.

- Question 1: How does this approach differ from standard label-noise techniques like Goldberger & Ben-Reuven (2016), and why aren’t those directly applicable?

- Question 2: How does performance degrade with extremely limited or poor parallel data (e.g., <5k tokens)?

- Question 3: What is the effect of using all alignments (many-to-one, one-to-many) vs. only one-to-one alignments on projected data quality and model performance?

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons to label-noise literature, failure-case analysis, training details, and computational considerations.
