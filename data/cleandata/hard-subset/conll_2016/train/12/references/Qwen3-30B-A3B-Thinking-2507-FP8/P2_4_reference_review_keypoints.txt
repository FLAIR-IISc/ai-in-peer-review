- Addresses low-resource POS tagging by combining small gold datasets with noisy cross-lingual projected data using a neural model that explicitly models projection noise via a transformation matrix A.
- Main contribution: explicit noise modeling of projected POS tags through a transformation matrix rather than treating projections as direct supervision.
- Main contribution: joint training framework that combines gold and projected data to learn when/how much to trust projected annotations.
- Main contribution: comprehensive evaluation on simulated low-resource European languages and real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) showing consistent improvements.
- Strength: conceptually novel and well-motivated idea; noise layer is simple, elegant, and backed by empirical analysis of projection errors.
- Strength: strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art gains (1–4%) across tested languages and insightful analyses of learned noise matrices.
- Strength: clear methodology and implementation details, with a practical two-stage training scheme (pretrain on gold, then joint training).
- Weakness: limited comparisons to more recent methods (e.g., Guo et al. 2016, transformer-based or newer transfer-learning approaches).
- Weakness: lacks thorough ablation studies to isolate the contribution of the noise layer versus other components or simple heuristics.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure cases and limitations (e.g., extreme projection noise or large syntactic divergence).
- Question: clarify handling of rectangular noise matrix A when tagsets differ and when a universal tagset is not used.
- Question: provide parameter count and computational impact of the noise matrix A relative to the rest of the model.
- Question: is there a correlation between parallel corpus size/quality and learned noise level (e.g., Kinyarwanda vs Malagasy), and how should this inform resource allocation?
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, empirically supported paper with broader applicability to noisy supervision in low-resource NLP; recommend acceptance.
