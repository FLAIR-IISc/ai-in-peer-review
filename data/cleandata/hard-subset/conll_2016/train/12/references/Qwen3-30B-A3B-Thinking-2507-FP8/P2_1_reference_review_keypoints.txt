- Contribution: Proposes a neural POS tagger with a learnable noise layer modeling transformation from projected (noisy) tags to true tags to decide how much to trust distant supervision.  
- Contribution: Evaluated on 8 simulated low-resource European languages and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), showing consistent improvements over prior methods.  
- Contribution: Analyzes the learned noise transformation matrix A to reveal tag confusion patterns and explain noise behavior.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated approach that explicitly models projection noise and outperforms baselines (e.g., 91.7% vs 91.3% on simulated languages).  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across simulated and real low-resource settings with statistically consistent improvements.  
- Strength: Insightful analysis of the noise matrix that uncovers concrete tag confusion patterns across languages.

- Weakness: Insufficient justification that a neural BiLSTM is necessary; no ablation isolating the noise layer from the neural architecture or comparison with simpler models.  
- Weakness: Limited real-world evaluation (only Malagasy and Kinyarwanda), weakening claims of generality across languages and noise types.  
- Weakness: No discussion of computational cost or training/resource requirements compared to prior, cheaper methods.
