- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger that combines small gold data with noisy cross-lingual projected tags using a learned noise layer to model projected→true tag transformations; outperforms baselines on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages.

- Contribution 1: Explicitly models noise in projected POS tags via a K×K transformation matrix.

- Contribution 2: Integrates the noise model into a BiLSTM with joint training on gold and projected data.

- Contribution 3: Provides strong empirical validation showing consistent improvements across languages and insights from learned noise matrices.

- Strength 1: Addresses an important low-resource NLP problem and leverages parallel corpora practically.

- Strength 2: Well-motivated, simple, and parameter-efficient noise-layer technical contribution.

- Strength 3: Strong and consistent empirical results, including compelling real-language accuracies (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).

- Strength 4: Straightforward implementation and practical two-stage training procedure.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—noise-as-transformation has precedent in related areas and is not fully distinguished from prior work.

- Weakness 2: Evaluation on only two real low-resource languages is insufficient to demonstrate broad generalizability.

- Weakness 3: Lacks comparison to more recent deep/Transformer-based cross-lingual transfer baselines.

- Weakness 4: No ablation studies to isolate the noise layer’s contribution or test constrained variants.

- Weakness 5: Insufficient discussion of limitations, especially for languages with strong syntactic divergence from the source.

- Question 1: How would the noise-layer approach generalize to other tasks (e.g., NER, parsing) and what modifications would be needed?

- Question 2: Why use two-stage training instead of end-to-end training, and what are the theoretical benefits?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison to existing noise-modeling techniques in the literature?

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with practical impact and strong results but limited novelty and limited real-language evaluation—recommend accept with minor revisions (expand novelty discussion, add ablations, broaden evaluation).
