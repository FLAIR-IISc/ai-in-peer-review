- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM with a learnable K×K noise layer to map noisy cross-lingual projected POS tags to gold tags, trained end-to-end and evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages with consistent improvements.

- Contribution 1: Explicitly models noise in projected POS tags via a learnable transformation matrix.

- Contribution 2: Integrates the noise model into an end-to-end deep neural network (BiLSTM) trained on both gold and projected data.

- Contribution 3: Provides a comprehensive evaluation showing consistent gains across simulated and real low-resource settings.

- Strength 1: Novel, well-motivated noise modeling that captures predictable projection errors with minimal extra parameters.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results, including notable improvements on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda and informative analyses of the learned noise matrices.

- Strength 3: Clear theoretical justification and a well-grounded training objective and architecture.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to mostly linguistically similar languages and only two real languages, lacking truly divergent language pairs.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison with more recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., multilingual BERT, transfer learning).

- Weakness 3: Insufficient explanation of the mechanism by which the model learns to "trust" or distrust specific projected tags.

- Question 1: How does the method perform when the high-resource language is not English?

- Question 2: Can the authors compare their approach to recent low-resource POS tagging methods like multilingual BERT or transfer learning?

- Question 3: How would the model learn to completely ignore consistently misaligned projected tags (e.g., verbs)?

- Overall assessment: A meaningful, well-executed contribution with strong results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand evaluations and clarify the trust-learning mechanism.
