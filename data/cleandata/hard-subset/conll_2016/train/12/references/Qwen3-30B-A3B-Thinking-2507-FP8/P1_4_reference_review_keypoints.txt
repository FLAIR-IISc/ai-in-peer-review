- Proposes a neural POS-tagger for low-resource languages combining small gold data with noisy projected tags via an explicit noise transformation layer.
- Key innovation: a noise transformation layer modeling mapping between projected and true tags to learn trust in projected data.
- Joint training procedure that combines gold annotations and noisy projections for improved performance.
- Comprehensive evaluation on 8 simulated low-resource European languages and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).
- Shows consistent and statistically significant improvements over prior state-of-the-art, e.g., 86.3% on Malagasy and 82.5% on Kinyarwanda.
- Provides clear visualizations of learned noise transformation matrices to explain model behavior.
- Novel explicit noise modeling contrasts with prior cleaning or implicit-noise methods.
- Practical significance for NLP in low-resource languages and potential extension to other distant-supervision tasks.
- Well-structured experiments including ablation studies demonstrating the noise layer’s effectiveness.
- Limited comparison to recent methods post-Duong et al. (2014a), missing modern transfer/multilingual-pretraining baselines.
- No analysis of model robustness under varying noise levels or different parallel-data quality/domains.
- Insufficient implementation details on the noise matrix A initialization and its learning dynamics during pretraining.
- Lacks analysis of failure cases and specific scenarios where the model underperforms.
- Computational complexity and overhead of adding the noise layer are not discussed, raising concerns for large tagsets or constrained settings.
- Asks for comparisons with recent low-resource POS methods (transfer learning, multilingual pretraining).
- Requests analysis of performance with varying parallel-data quality and noise levels.
- Requests details on initialization and evolution of the noise transformation matrix A during training.
- Asks which tag confusions the model handles well versus which remain challenging.
- Asks for computational-cost comparison to baseline BiLSTM models considering the noise matrix size.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with novel approach and compelling results, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address listed weaknesses.
