- Paper tackles low-resource POS tagging via cross-lingual projection and proposes a BiLSTM with a noise transformation layer to model projection noise.
- Contribution: introduces an explicit noise transformation layer that learns to map projected tags to true tag distributions (learns when/how much to trust projections).
- Contribution: shows strong empirical performance, outperforming existing methods, notably on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.
- Contribution: offers comprehensive evaluation with both simulated and real languages and informative ablation studies.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated, and theoretically sound approach that moves beyond heuristics for noisy projections.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful diagrams explaining architecture and learned parameters.
- Strength: provides implementation and dataset details, improving reproducibility.
- Strength: compares against strong baselines (e.g., Duong et al. 2014a, maxent) and includes within-model ablations.
- Strength: visualization and analysis of learned noise matrices give insight into the types of projection errors corrected.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of word alignment quality (Giza++ statistics) and correlation with tagging errors.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on English as pivot, with limited discussion of biases or alternatives for typologically distant languages.
- Weakness: no sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (BiLSTM dimensions, learning rate) to show robustness.
- Weakness: does not report computational cost or training time overhead introduced by the noise layer.
- Question: please provide Giza++ alignment statistics (one-to-one, many-to-one, one-to-many, NULL) for the language pairs.
- Question: have you evaluated using different pivot languages and the impact on typologically distant target languages?
- Question: did you perform hyperparameter tuning, and what ranges were explored for key hyperparameters?
- Question: what is the computational overhead and training time difference compared to a standard BiLSTM without the noise layer?
- Additional: recommend analysis of remaining tagging error types to guide further improvements.
- Additional: suggest exploring applicability of the noise-modeling approach to other NLP tasks beyond POS tagging.
- Overall: strong, well-executed paper with a significant contribution; recommended for acceptance.
