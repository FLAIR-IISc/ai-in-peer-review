- Tackles low-resource POS tagging using cross-lingual projection plus limited gold data, with a BiLSTM that models projection noise.  
- Core contribution: a noise layer with a learned transformation matrix A to model and compensate for inaccurate projected tags.  
- Empirical result: consistent improvements in simulated settings and state-of-the-art on Malagasy (86.3%) and Kinyarwanda (82.5%).  
- Evaluation: comprehensive experiments using both simulated and real-world data and comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Strength: novel idea to explicitly model projected-data noise rather than rely on heuristics or preprocessing.  
- Strength: substantial practical performance gains on challenging real-world languages.  
- Strength: well-designed experimental setup and controlled simulations to isolate effects.  
- Strength: clear writing and helpful model illustration (Figure 2).  
- Strength: visualization and analysis of learned noise matrix (Figures 3 & 4) provide useful insight.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis showing specific cases and remaining failure modes.  
- Weakness: reliance on Giza++ for alignments, which can be sensitive and produce suboptimal results.  
- Weakness: added complexity from the KxK noise matrix; unclear sensitivity, stability, and tuning requirements.  
- Weakness: absence of an ablation study isolating the noise layer’s true impact.  
- Question: provide detailed error examples demonstrating why the proposed model outperforms baselines.  
- Question: were alternative word alignment tools to Giza++ considered, and what were the outcomes?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the size/regularization of transformation matrix A?  
- Question: please include an ablation comparing BiLSTM with and without the noise layer.  
- Question: clarify the criteria for switching from pre-training to joint training in the two-stage process.  
- Additional comment: clarify the reference to "clab/cnn" for readers unfamiliar with the codebase.  
- Additional comment: the phrase "mild improvements" understates the observed accuracy gains.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective approach with compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
