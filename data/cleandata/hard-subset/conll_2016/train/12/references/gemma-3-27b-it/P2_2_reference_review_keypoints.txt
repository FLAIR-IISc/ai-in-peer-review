- Tackles low-resource POS tagging using cross-lingual projection and proposes a BiLSTM with a learnable noise transformation to handle projection noise.  
- Introduces a learnable “noise layer” (matrix A) to model transformation between projected and gold tags, allowing the model to adapt to systematic projection errors.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements on simulated and real low-resource languages, outperforming prior methods and achieving state-of-the-art on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.  
- Presents a practical framework for combining distant supervision with limited labeled data, applicable to broader low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Novel and well-justified approach that models noise instead of merely filtering it, with clear mathematical formulation.  
- Comprehensive evaluation using simulated experiments and two real-world languages, plus varied parallel corpora sources.  
- Clear writing and motivation, helpful diagrams, and insightful analysis of learned transformation matrices.  
- Significant practical impact and clear relevance to the CONLL community.  
- Limited ablation study; more analysis of initialization strategies for A and alternative architectures would strengthen the work.  
- Results likely sensitive to word alignment quality, but the paper lacks an explicit investigation of alignment tool/parameter effects.  
- Variability in data sources (web-sourced Malagasy vs. ARL MURI Kinyarwanda) may confound comparisons and isn’t fully discussed.  
- Two-stage training (pre-training then joint training) adds complexity; single-stage alternatives are not explored.  
- Questions: need details on hyperparameter tuning for A (learning rate, regularization), sensitivity to alignment quality, causes of performance differences across languages/data, and comparisons to alternative architectures (e.g., Transformers).  
- Suggests releasing code to improve reproducibility; overall assessment: strong, novel, well-motivated paper recommended for acceptance.
