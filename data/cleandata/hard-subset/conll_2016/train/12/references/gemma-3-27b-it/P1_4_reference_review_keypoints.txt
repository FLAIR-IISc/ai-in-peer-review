- Paper proposes a BiLSTM that models noise in cross-lingual projected POS tags via a learnable transformation matrix to combine projected and gold data.  
- Core contribution: explicit, learnable modeling of projection noise rather than heuristic filtering.  
- Core contribution: effective joint training on noisy projected data and limited gold annotations.  
- Core contribution: strong empirical results, setting state-of-the-art on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.  
- Strength: novel and well-motivated “noise layer” concept that learns systematic projection errors.  
- Strength: consistent improvements over baselines on both simulated and real low-resource datasets.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures and comprehensive related work.  
- Strength: practical relevance for low-resource NLP and leveraging parallel data.  
- Strength: insightful analysis and visualization of the learned transformation matrix.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of how word alignment quality impacts projection and tagging performance.  
- Weakness: no reported sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (embeddings, hidden size, learning rate).  
- Weakness: brief treatment of tagset mismatch handling and unclear behavior for very different tagsets.  
- Weakness: lack of discussion about computational cost relative to simpler baselines.  
- Question: How does word alignment quality (and different alignment tools) affect model performance?  
- Question: Were hyperparameter sensitivity analyses performed and how robust is the model to changes?  
- Question: How does the model perform or degrade with language pairs that have drastically different tagsets?  
- Question: What are the training and inference time costs compared to MaxEnt or BiLSTM without noise layer?  
- Additional suggestion: discuss broader applications beyond POS tagging for the noise modeling approach.  
- Additional suggestion: consider pre-training the noise matrix A on larger parallel corpora before fine-tuning.  
- Overall judgment: strong paper with compelling approach and results; recommended for acceptance.
