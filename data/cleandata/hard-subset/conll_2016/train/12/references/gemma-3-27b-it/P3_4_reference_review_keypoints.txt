- Proposes a BiLSTM model with an explicit "noise layer" to learn mappings from noisy projected tags to true tags.  
- Targets low-resource POS tagging using cross-lingual projection from English.  
- Demonstrates competitive results on simulated and real low-resource languages, outperforming prior state-of-the-art on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.  
- Main contribution: explicit noise modeling via a learnable transformation matrix (A).  
- Strong empirical validation across multiple languages and settings.  
- Comprehensive evaluation using both simulated low-resource scenarios and real languages.  
- Clear presentation and helpful figures illustrating architecture and learned matrices.  
- Realistic experimental setting leveraging parallel data and limited annotated data.  
- Comparison to strong baselines (Duong et al., 2014a; Garrette & Baldridge, 2013) showing clear improvements.  
- Weakness: limited in-depth analysis of the learned noise matrices beyond diagonal dominance.  
- Weakness: lack of discussion on hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., BiLSTM size, learning rate).  
- Weakness: no analysis of computational cost of training the noise-layer model versus baselines.  
- Weakness: reliance on Giza++ alignments without exploring more advanced alignment methods.  
- Question: request for detailed analysis of recurring tag-confusion patterns across languages in matrix A.  
- Question: request for results or sensitivity analysis over different hyperparameter settings and optimization procedures.  
- Question: request for information on computational cost and feasibility compared to baseline models.  
- Question: query about using more sophisticated word alignment techniques (e.g., NMT-based) and potential impact.  
- Additional comment: bibliography appears incomplete and should be corrected.  
- Additional comment: use of the universal tagset is beneficial for cross-lingual comparison.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with novel, effective approach; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
