- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM with a learnable “noise transformation” layer to model and correct noisy cross-lingual POS projections, showing improvements on simulated and real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).

- Contribution 1: Introduces explicit noise modeling via a learnable noise layer that maps projected tags to cleaner low-resource tags.

- Contribution 2: Validates the method on real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) and reports state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution 3: Clearly frames the low-resource POS tagging problem and motivates cross-lingual projection despite its noise.

- Strength 1: Novel and well-motivated approach that learns tag confusions rather than relying on heuristics.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results across languages and settings, favorable comparison to Duong et al. (2014a).

- Strength 3: Thorough experiments including simulated/real data, ablation studies, and visualization of the noise matrix.

- Strength 4: Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful figures illustrating model and noise transformation.

- Weakness 1: Limited qualitative and quantitative analysis of what the learned noise matrix captures linguistically.

- Weakness 2: Method appears sensitive to word alignment quality and lacks exploration of different alignment strategies or robustness.

- Weakness 3: Little detail on hyperparameter tuning or sensitivity (learning rate, LSTM size, noise matrix dimensionality).

- Weakness 4: Missing comparisons to more recent cross-lingual transfer methods using pre-trained multilingual embeddings (e.g., mBERT).

- Questions for authors: Provide deeper analysis of the noise matrix, experiments with different alignment tools/strategies, hyperparameter sensitivity details, and comparisons to multilingual pre-trained models.

- Additional comments: Code release would aid reproducibility; related work should include recent advances in pre-trained cross-lingual transfer.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, and effective paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to address the listed analyses and comparisons.
