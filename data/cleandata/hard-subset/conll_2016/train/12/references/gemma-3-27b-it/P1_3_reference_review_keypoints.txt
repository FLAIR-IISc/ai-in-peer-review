- Summary: Proposes learning a noise-transformation layer to weight and de-noise cross-lingually projected POS tags for low-resource languages, showing improved tagging on simulated and real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).

- Contribution 1: Introduces an explicit, learnable noise transformation matrix to model projection noise instead of using projected tags as hard labels.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates consistent empirical gains and state-of-the-art results across multiple languages and settings.

- Contribution 3: Uses a careful experimental setup with simulations, real-world evaluations, strong baselines, and ablation analyses.

- Strength 1: Novel and elegant noise-modelling approach that outperforms heuristic filtering, supported by visualizations of the learned matrix.

- Strength 2: Consistent performance improvements across diverse languages, including genuinely low-resource cases.

- Strength 3: Clear motivation and problem formulation linking cross-lingual projection noise to performance issues.

- Strength 4: Comprehensive experiments and insightful analyses of the noise transformation.

- Weakness 1: Added model complexity and limited interpretability of what specific noise patterns the A matrix captures.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient analysis of word alignment quality and its correlation with tagging performance.

- Weakness 3: No discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially for the noise transformation layer.

- Question 1: What specific types of noise does matrix A capture and are there consistent cross-language patterns?

- Question 2: How does model performance vary with different alignment qualities or alignment tools?

- Question 3: How sensitive is the model to hyperparameters of the noise layer (learning rate, regularization)?

- Question 4: How would this approach adapt to other noisy annotation scenarios (distant supervision, crowdsourcing) and what challenges would arise?

- Additional comment 1: Writing is generally clear though occasionally verbose.

- Additional comment 2: Related work section is well-written and comprehensive.

- Additional comment 3: Code release is recommended for reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with a novel, effective method; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address analysis and robustness questions.
