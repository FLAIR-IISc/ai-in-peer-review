- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger for low-resource languages that explicitly models noise in cross-lingual projected tags via a "noise transformation" layer to combine projected and small gold datasets.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel noise transformation layer to model discrepancies between projected and true POS tags.

- Contribution: Provides a principled method to integrate noisy distant supervision with limited gold annotations.

- Contribution: Demonstrates strong empirical gains across simulated and real low-resource languages, outperforming prior work.

- Strength: Directly addresses projection noise, a core limitation of cross-lingual projection methods.

- Strength: Uses a sensible BiLSTM architecture with a two-stage (pre-training then joint training) procedure.

- Strength: Extensive evaluation across eight simulated and two real languages with comparisons to strong baselines.

- Strength: Clear explanation and visualization of the learned noise transformation matrix A and its link to tag confusions.

- Strength: High practical relevance for building NLP systems for low-resource languages.

- Weakness: Architectural novelty is limited beyond adding the noise layer; underlying BiLSTM is standard.

- Weakness: Performance depends on the quality of word alignments; more analysis of alignment impact is needed.

- Weakness: Missing details on hyperparameter tuning (learning rate, embedding sizes, LSTM hidden sizes), affecting reproducibility.

- Weakness: Lacks an ablation study isolating the noise layer’s contribution (e.g., BiLSTM without the noise layer on combined data).

- Question: Please detail the hyperparameter tuning process, search space, and strategy (grid/random search, etc.).

- Question: How does model performance vary with word alignment quality; can alignment accuracy be correlated with POS accuracy?

- Question: Were alternative noise modeling methods considered (different layers or parameterizations of the noise matrix)?

- Question: Can the noise layer be applied to other distant supervision scenarios beyond cross-lingual projection?

- Additional: Paper is well-written, with thorough related work and generally good formatting (some figure captions are long).

- Overall assessment: Recommend acceptance; method is well-motivated and effective, though additional hyperparameter details and an ablation study would strengthen the paper.
