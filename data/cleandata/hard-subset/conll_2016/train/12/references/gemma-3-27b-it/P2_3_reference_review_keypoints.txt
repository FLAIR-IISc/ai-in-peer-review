- Proposes a BiLSTM model that learns an explicit noise-transformation matrix A to correct noisy cross-lingual POS tag projections using small amounts of gold data.  
- Demonstrates improved POS tagging on simulated and real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) versus prior methods.  
- Core contribution: explicit, learnable modeling of projection noise via a transformation matrix rather than filtering/weighting.  
- Empirically validated across diverse languages, with consistent improvements and strong baselines including Duong et al.  
- Achieves strong practical results (e.g., 86.3% Malagasy, 82.5% Kinyarwanda), showing real-world impact.  
- Model design is novel, conceptually sound, and interpretable through the noise matrix A.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive, covering both simulated low-resource setups and real languages.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and includes helpful architectural figures.  
- Visualizations of the learned noise matrix provide interpretability and evidence of learned tag relationships.  
- Needs deeper analysis of the noise matrix to explain why certain tag transformations are learned.  
- Lacks discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and whether results depend on specific tuning choices.  
- Introduces additional parameters (complexity increase) without detailed discussion of trade-offs.  
- Method depends on word alignment quality; impact of different alignment tools/strategies is underexplored.  
- Asks for more detailed analysis of recurring tag misalignments in A to diagnose projection weaknesses.  
- Requests information on hyperparameter sensitivity and whether systematic tuning was performed.  
- Requests computational cost comparison (training time, memory) versus baselines.  
- Asks whether different initializations for matrix A (e.g., identity) were tried and their effects.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, clever approach with solid results; addressing minor analyses would strengthen the paper—recommend acceptance.
