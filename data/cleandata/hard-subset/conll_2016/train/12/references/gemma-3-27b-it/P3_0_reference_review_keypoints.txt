- Summary: Proposes a neural POS tagger for low-resource languages that learns a noise-transformation layer to weight noisy cross-lingual projected tags instead of treating them as ground truth.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a principled, learnable noise-transformation layer to model and downweight unreliable cross-lingual projections.

- Contribution 2: Shows strong empirical gains over baselines on simulated and real low-resource languages, notably Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.

- Contribution 3: Integrates the noise layer within a BiLSTM architecture to capture complex relations between tokens, projected tags, and true tags.

- Strength 1: Novel idea of learning to trust noisy projections rather than filtering them, offering a conceptual advance.

- Strength 2: Thorough evaluation with fair comparisons to prior work and useful ablation studies.

- Strength 3: Clear presentation and helpful illustrations that explain the model design.

- Strength 4: High practical relevance for scalable low-resource POS tagging applications.

- Strength 5: Analysis and visualization of the learned noise matrix provide insight into model behavior.

- Weakness 1: Lacks detailed error analysis showing specific examples of successes and failures and which confusions are corrected.

- Weakness 2: Method’s performance depends on alignment quality, but the paper does not systematically study alignment tool/parameter effects.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and search procedures; more reporting could improve rigor.

- Weakness 4: No discussion of computational overhead or training-time cost introduced by the noise layer versus baselines.

- Question 1: Please provide a detailed error analysis with examples illustrating how the noise layer corrects projection errors.

- Question 2: How sensitive is performance to alignment quality and have different aligners or settings been tested?

- Question 3: What hyperparameter search strategy and value ranges were used, and how extensive was tuning?

- Question 4: What is the approximate increase in training time compared to a BiLSTM without the noise layer?

- Additional comment 1: The citation “clab/cnn” is unclear and should be specified.

- Additional comment 2: The phrase “well suited to the low-resource applications” is redundant and could be tightened.

- Additional comment 3: It would be interesting to test whether the learned noise transformation generalizes across low-resource languages.

- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — novel, effective approach with clear empirical gains; addressing the noted weaknesses would further strengthen the paper.
