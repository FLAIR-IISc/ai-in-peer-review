- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger for low-resource languages that jointly learns a noise-transformation layer to model and correct noisy cross-lingual projected tags using limited gold data.
- Empirical claim: Shows improved performance on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, outperforming baselines including Duong et al. (2014a).

- Contribution: Introduces an explicit noise transformation layer to learn systematic errors from projected tags instead of heuristic cleaning.
- Contribution: Demonstrates strong empirical gains on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda, indicating practical effectiveness.
- Contribution: Integrates the noise layer into an end-to-end BiLSTM framework enabling joint learning from projected and gold annotations.

- Strength: Novel and insightful approach that treats projection noise as a learnable signal.
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated methodology with helpful figures illustrating model structure.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple languages and strong baseline comparisons.
- Strength: Significant performance improvements supported by detailed tables and visualizations of the noise matrices.
- Strength: Well-written and organized presentation.

- Weakness: Limited quantitative analysis of the learned noise transformation matrices and linguistic patterns.
- Weakness: Method’s reliance on word alignment quality is acknowledged but not thoroughly explored or ablated.
- Weakness: Missing discussion of computational cost and how the added noise layer affects training/inference time.
- Weakness: Claim about handling mismatching tagsets is not empirically tested or discussed in depth.

- Question: Please provide quantitative analyses of Figures 3–4 to identify language-specific noise patterns.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to alignment quality; have you tried different aligners or corrupted alignments?
- Question: What are the computational costs (training and inference) compared to baselines?
- Question: How would the approach handle very different tagsets and how should the A matrix be interpreted in such cases?

- Additional: Fix truncated or incomplete bibliography entries (e.g., Ammar et al.).
- Additional: Clarify/format the Malagasy parallel data URL in Section 4.1.1.

- Recommendation: Strong accept — novel, well-executed, and empirically convincing with suggested analyses to further strengthen the paper.
