- Paper models noise in cross-lingual POS projection via a learnable K×K confusion matrix on BiLSTM outputs, trained with gold and projected data in two stages.  
- Training: pretrain on gold, then joint training on gold + projected labels using the noise layer.  
- Empirical gains shown on 8 simulated European languages and 2 real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Contribution: simple, end-to-end label-noise layer that learns when to trust projected tags.  
- Contribution: consistent empirical improvements over baselines and prior work in low-resource POS tagging.  
- Contribution: qualitative interpretability of learned confusion matrices revealing systematic projection errors.  
- Strength: explicit, differentiable modeling of projection noise instead of heuristic filtering.  
- Strength: consistent improvements, notably larger gains on truly low-resource languages.  
- Strength: strong baselines and ablative comparisons isolate the noise-layer benefit.  
- Strength: interpretability helps error analysis and linguistic insight.  
- Strength: simplicity and generality — model-agnostic layer applicable to other noisy supervision tasks.  
- Weakness: modest gains on simulated languages and no statistical significance testing reported.  
- Weakness: limited ablations/sensitivity analyses (A initialization/regularization, loss weighting, training schedule).  
- Weakness: unclear projection pipeline details and inconsistency about handling multi/NULL alignments.  
- Weakness: fairness of baselines — lacks modern neural baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF) trained on projected labels without noise layer.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns — some resources not public and sparse hyperparameter details; code not released.  
- Key question: how is A initialized/regularized and does it avoid degenerate solutions?  
- Key question: how is the joint loss weighted and was tuning a scalar weighting between datasets tried?  
- Key question: are distributional labels for many-to-one/NULL alignments actually used, and how much supervision do they contribute?  
- Key question: is two-stage training necessary versus end-to-end or curriculum methods?  
- Key question: sensitivity to amount/quality of parallel data and amount of gold data?  
- Key question: were mismatched tagsets (rectangular A) tested or how would the method behave in that case?
