- Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger with a learned K×K noise layer (softmax(A o)) to adapt noisy cross-lingual projected labels.
- Trains in two stages: pretrain on small gold data, then joint training on gold plus projected data.
- Evaluates on 8 simulated European languages and 2 genuine low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) showing consistent gains over prior work.
- Contribution: simple, explicit label-noise adaptation layer jointly trained with a sequence model for low-resource POS tagging.
- Contribution: thorough empirical evaluation across 10 languages with analyses of learned noise matrices for interpretability.
- Contribution: practical training protocol (pretrain then joint) to stabilize learning from noisy supervision.
- Strength: directly addresses noisy, biased projections from alignment errors and structural divergences.
- Strength: noise layer is parameter-efficient, easy to implement, and yields consistent improvements, notably +4.2 on Kinyarwanda.
- Strength: pretraining on gold then joint learning is well motivated and empirically validated.
- Strength: qualitative analysis of learned A matrices provides plausible diagnostic insights per language.
- Strength: evaluation balances simulated and true low-resource settings with publicly known datasets.
- Weakness: noise-layer parameterization (softmax(A o)) is unconventional and not clearly interpretable as a class-conditional transition matrix.
- Weakness: no constraints, regularization, or ablations (e.g., T o transition matrix, diagonal priors, identity init) to address identifiability concerns.
- Weakness: projection labeling heuristics for unaligned/multi-aligned tokens use sentence-level tag frequencies, which may inject bias and are untested against alternatives.
- Weakness: limited baselines and ablations—no BiLSTM-CRF, char-level features, or comparisons with confidence-weighting/pruning strategies.
- Weakness: experimental clarity issues—gold set sizes differ (1k vs ~5k), hyperparameters, dev tuning, significance, and variance across runs not reported.
- Weakness: claim about supporting mismatched tagsets (rectangular A) is untested; experiments only use universal tags.
- Question: Why use softmax(A o) instead of a row-stochastic T with õ = T o, logits-level A, or constrained/identity-initialized A?
- Question: How sensitive are results to projection heuristics for unaligned/multi-aligned tokens—please ablate sentence-prior vs uniform vs alignment-posterior methods?
- Question: What are the exact gold training sizes for Malagasy/Kinyarwanda for this method and each baseline?
- Question: Do results hold when adding CRF decoding and/or character-level features to baselines, or how would those interact?
- Question: Please report variance across seeds, statistical significance, and coverage statistics for alignment retention/filtering.
- Question: Did you try initializing A to identity or encouraging diagonal dominance, and how does that affect convergence and accuracy?
- Overall: Practical and interpretable approach with consistent gains, but unconventional noise modeling, limited ablations, and under-specified experiments leave the paper borderline acceptable pending clarifications and stronger comparisons.
