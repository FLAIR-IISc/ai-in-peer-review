- Paper proposes a learnable noise layer (K×K confusion matrix) on top of a BiLSTM tagger to model cross-lingual projection noise and jointly train on gold and projected data.
- Main contributions: explicit label-noise layer for projected tags, empirical results across 10 languages with interpretable noise matrices, and a practical training recipe (gold pretraining + joint learning).
- Strength: simple, well-motivated idea that cleanly integrates with a BiLSTM and adds few parameters.
- Strength: consistent empirical gains, especially in truly low-resource languages (notable improvements on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda).
- Strength: interpretable analyses showing plausible off-diagonal confusion patterns in learned A matrices.
- Strength: realistic data setups using large parallel corpora where available and small noisy bilingual data for real low-resource languages.
- Strength: practical two-stage training that stabilizes learning from noisy supervision.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty — the noise layer is essentially a known class-conditional confusion matrix applied to projection.
- Weakness: baseline coverage is incomplete — missing comparisons to BiLSTM-CRF and graph/constraint-based projection methods under comparable settings.
- Weakness: coarse heuristic handling of alignments (1-to-1 only; sentence-level priors for multi/unaligned tokens) rather than using alignment posteriors or confidence weighting.
- Weakness: missing ablations and sensitivity analyses (effect of pretraining, A initializations, varying gold/projected data amounts, conditioning A on alignment confidence).
- Weakness: sparse reporting of training details and no significance testing or variance across runs, making small gains uncertain.
- Question: How critical is two-stage training — ablation requested comparing joint training from scratch, pretraining with frozen A, and learning only A?
- Question: Does the noise layer help when replacing softmax tagger with a BiLSTM-CRF, and by how much?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the amount of gold data and to parallel corpus size/quality — request for learning curves (especially for Kinyarwanda)?
- Question: Can projected examples be weighted by alignment/sentence confidence, and does that improve performance?
- Question: For multi-aligned/unaligned tokens, how many tokens are affected and how do alternative heuristics compare to the sentence-level prior?
- Overall assessment: well-motivated and practical method with clear empirical and interpretability benefits, particularly in low-resource settings; acceptance is recommended pending stronger baselines and ablations.
