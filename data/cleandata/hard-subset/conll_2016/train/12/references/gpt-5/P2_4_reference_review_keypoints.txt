- Addresses low-resource POS tagging by explicitly modeling noise in projected labels with a learned linear noise layer A on top of a BiLSTM, trained with gold pretraining then joint training.  
- Contribution: simple, end-to-end noise-adaptation layer that learns when/how to trust projected tags.  
- Contribution: empirical validation on 8 simulated + 2 real languages with consistent gains and new SOTA on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.  
- Contribution: qualitative analysis via learned confusion matrices that give interpretable insights into projection noise.  

- Strength: minimal complexity (only K×K params) yet consistent, tangible improvements (e.g., +4.2 accuracy on Kinyarwanda).  
- Strength: strong experimental design and comparability to prior work, showing naive use of projected data can hurt.  
- Strength: learned noise matrices reveal plausible, language-specific tag confusions.  
- Strength: method is general and easily portable to other sequence labeling/noisy-supervision problems.  
- Strength: robust gains across both high-quality Europarl projections and noisier real-language scenarios.  

- Weakness: novelty is limited—approach is close to known label-noise techniques and A is not a properly constrained row-stochastic confusion matrix, so the interpretation is loose.  
- Weakness: missing ablations (constrained vs unconstrained A, A initialization, pretraining necessity, A regularization).  
- Weakness: missing sensitivity analyses for amount/quality of parallel data, sentence-selection filter, and the NULL/many-to-one heuristic.  
- Weakness: data realism concerns—simulations use large (200k) Europarl bitext and real gold sets (~5k tokens) are larger than the “1k token” tiny regime emphasized.  
- Weakness: baselines/architecture omit common enhancements (char embeddings, BiLSTM-CRF); unclear if gains persist with stronger encoders/decoders; no significance testing.  
- Weakness: reproducibility issues—sparse training details (epochs, early stopping, A init) and no code release; small average deltas raise stability/tuning concerns.  

- Question: How is A initialized and was it ever constrained to be a proper confusion matrix or regularized to avoid degenerate solutions?  
- Question: Is pretraining necessary—what are results for joint training from scratch and for identity vs random A initialization?  
- Question: For NULL/multi-alignments, were alternatives tried (uniform, alignment-confidence weighting, dropping tokens) and how sensitive are results?  
- Question: How do results vary with amount of parallel data, and can you report performance with only 1k gold tokens for Malagasy/Kinyarwanda?  
- Question: Do gains persist with character-level features and/or a CRF decoder, or discuss expected interactions if not tested?  
- Question: Please provide full training/optimization details (epochs, early stopping, dev selection, gradient clipping, regularization, hyperparameter tuning).
