- Proposes low-resource POS tagging that combines small gold annotations with noisy cross-lingual projected tags by modeling projection noise via a learnable K×K noise matrix A applied to model outputs.
- Model: BiLSTM tagger trained on two channels—gold tags with standard cross-entropy and projected tags after passing predictions through A; training uses gold pretraining then joint training.
- Empirical result: Outperforms baselines on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages, with state-of-the-art gains and interpretable learned noise matrices.

- Strength: Principled noise handling—learnable confusion layer models projection errors coherently as latent true tags → noisy observations.
- Strength: Consistent empirical gains over competitive baselines, notably on Kinyarwanda and Malagasy.
- Strength: Interpretability—visualizations of A show plausible, language-specific confusions.
- Strength: Simple, general approach (single linear layer) likely transferable to other noisy-supervision tasks.
- Strength: Sensible training protocol (pretrain on gold then joint training) to mitigate optimization with noisy labels.

- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to existing label-noise/confusion-matrix literature; needs clearer positioning and novelty claim.
- Weakness: A is under-specified/unconstrained (not forced stochastic or identity-biased), raising identifiability and robustness concerns; lacks ablations on initialization/regularization/loss weighting.
- Weakness: Projection heuristics (1-1 alignments, coarse handling of many-to-one/unaligned tokens) may be suboptimal and discard useful supervision.
- Weakness: Incomplete baseline coverage and fairness—missing comparisons (e.g., label propagation, posterior regularization, multi-source projection) and stronger BiLSTM enhancements (char features, CRF); no significance/variance reporting.
- Weakness: Low-resource realism concerns—gold set sizes and parallel data amounts vary; paper doesn’t disentangle effects of parallel corpus size/quality from model gains; lacks sensitivity analysis over data sizes.

- Q: How is A initialized and were identity/diagonal priors or row-stochastic constraints tried, and with what effect?
- Q: How are gold vs projected losses balanced—was a weighting coefficient tuned, and what happens if projected data dominates?
- Q: Ablations: effect of removing gold pretraining and sensitivity to gold data size (e.g., 500/1k/5k tokens)?
- Q: Was rectangular A (mismatched tagsets) evaluated in practice, and are there stability issues?
- Q: What proportion of tokens receive projected labels under 1-1 filtering, and what is the accuracy of the English tagger + mapping?
- Q: Are improvements statistically significant—please report mean/std over runs or significance tests?

- Please clarify dev set usage, early stopping, and report optimization hyperparameters (batch size, epochs, dropout).
- Recommend reporting performance with character features, since they often help low-resource POS and may interact with projection utility.
- Suggest releasing code and alignment/projection scripts to improve reproducibility.
