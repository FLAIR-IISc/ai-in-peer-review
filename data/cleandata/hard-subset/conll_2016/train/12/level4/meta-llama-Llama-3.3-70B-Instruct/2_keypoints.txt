- Proposes a novel RNN output-layer modification that models noise via an added fully connected layer to learn from both gold and projected annotations.
- Method is straightforward, elegant, modular, and shows promising POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Experimental setup is unconventional (very small gold set); more empirical validation is needed by plotting accuracy vs varying sizes of gold annotations.
- Need to evaluate performance with ample gold data, identify the gold-size threshold where the method helps, and whether that threshold is language-dependent.
- Noisy-label approach has broader applications (e.g., crowdsourcing, different tag sets), but paper only evaluates cross-lingual projections with the same tag set.
- Current training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally; authors should investigate tuning these weights.
- Description of projected data is unclear: the form of the vector representation \tilde{y} is not specified, and token-level vs type-level construction and handling of unaligned words are unanswered.
- Minor notation error: replace a closing bracket with an opening bracket at line 267.
