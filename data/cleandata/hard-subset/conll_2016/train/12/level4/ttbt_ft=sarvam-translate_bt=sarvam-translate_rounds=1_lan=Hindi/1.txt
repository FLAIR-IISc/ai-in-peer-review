I had reviewed this paper previously when it was a brief paper draft for ACL 2016. At that time, there was an error in the experiment setup, which has now been corrected. I had suggested then that I was willing to accept a draft for another *ACL program, provided the error was fixed, and I see no obstacle to doing so now. Another reviewer pointed out that if we focus on real low-resource languages, the paper's setup is somewhat artificial, related to the cost of finding and paying annotators. I believe that instead of overselling the method, it should be highlighted in the article.

There are relevant works in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, for example, Johansson et al. (2016, ACL) and Eijik et al. (2015, ACL). It would be good to consider them in the discussion of related work for completeness.

In summary, I think this is a good contribution, and I vote to accept it. It should be indicated whether the data is made available or not. I now evaluate those parts in good faith, assuming that the research is publicly available.
