- Proposes a noise-aware output layer replacing softmax by multiplying the label distribution with a learned noise-generation FC layer.  
- Method is well-motivated, elegant, modular, and broadly applicable to low-resource NLP.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on POS tagging across eight simulated and two real languages using 1,000 gold tokens plus projected data.  
- Core strength: simplicity and empirical effectiveness—projected annotations combined with sparse gold data substantially improve performance.  
- Experimental design concern: using exactly 1,000 gold tokens is atypical; authors should plot accuracy vs. gold-size (e.g., 100–20,000) to assess behavior.  
- Key experimental questions to address: does performance degrade with ample gold, what is the beneficial threshold, and how does it vary across languages?  
- Narrow focus on cross-lingual projections with identical tag sets; unclear if approach generalizes to crowdsourced or weak supervision with noisy/mismatched tags.  
- Loss weighting issue: equal weighting of gold and noisy labels seems suboptimal—need tuning/sensitivity analysis to see if upweighting gold helps.  
- Technical ambiguity: unclear form of \tilde{y} (vector vs. distribution) and whether token-level projected tags (vs. word-type aggregation) are supported.  
- Alignment limitation: current reliance on one-to-one word alignments leaves \tilde{y} undefined for unaligned words; authors should discuss remedies (e.g., back-translation, heuristics).  
- Minor typo: line 267 has a bracket error.  
- Overall verdict: promising contribution that requires deeper empirical validation and technical clarification to be a strong CONLL submission.
