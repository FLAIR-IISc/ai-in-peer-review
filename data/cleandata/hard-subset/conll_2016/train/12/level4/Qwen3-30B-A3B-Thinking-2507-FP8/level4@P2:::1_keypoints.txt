- Summary: Proposes a neural low-resource POS tagger that jointly trains on small gold data and noisy projected tags using an explicit noise layer, achieving SOTA on simulated and real languages.  
- Strength 1: Corrected prior experimental flaw and shows robust empirical gains over baselines across 8 simulated languages and Malagasy/Kinyarwanda.  
- Strength 2: Novel explicit noise modeling via a learnable matrix A that captures projection-to-true tag transformations.  
- Strength 3: Practically feasible—requires only ~1,000 gold tokens and is effective in real low-resource cases.  
- Weakness 1: Real-world evaluation may be artificial because it relies on large parallel web data, which many truly low-resource languages lack.  
- Weakness 2: Related work is incomplete—omits key papers (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015) that address similar projection/transfer ideas.  
- Weakness 3: Paper does not explicitly confirm public release of parallel and annotated datasets, risking reproducibility.  
- Question 1: Will the parallel corpora and annotated datasets (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) be publicly released?  
- Question 2: How does the approach handle scenarios with no parallel data; clarify limitations for languages without English-aligned resources.  
- Overall: Strong, novel contribution with corrected experiments and practical gains; recommend acceptance after minor revisions.
