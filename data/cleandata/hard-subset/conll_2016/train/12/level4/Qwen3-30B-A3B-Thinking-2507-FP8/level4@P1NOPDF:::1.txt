

I reviewed this paper previously as an ACL 2016 short paper draft. At that time, it contained a flaw in the experimental setup, which has since been corrected. Since I previously indicated I would support acceptance at another ACL venue upon correction of this issue, I now see no remaining objections to recommending acceptance.  

A second reviewer appropriately noted that the paper’s setup may appear somewhat artificial when considering real-world low-resource language scenarios, particularly regarding the practical trade-offs between the costs of finding versus paying annotators. This limitation should be clearly addressed in the revision to avoid overstating the method’s applicability.  

The related work section should also incorporate recent relevant contributions, specifically Johannsen et al. (2016) and Agić et al. (2015), both ACL publications on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages. Including these would strengthen the paper’s scholarly context and completeness.  

Overall, this represents a solid contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance. Additionally, please confirm whether the experimental data will be made publicly available, as this is standard practice for research in our community. I assume the data will be shared upon publication.
