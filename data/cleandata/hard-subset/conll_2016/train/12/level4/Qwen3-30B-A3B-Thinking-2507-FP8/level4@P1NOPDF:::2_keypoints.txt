- Proposes augmenting the softmax output with a noise-generation FC layer to learn from both gold and projected annotations in low-resource RNNs.  
- Method is well-motivated, simple, elegant, yields strong POS-tagging results on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, and is modularly applicable.  
- Experimental setup is questionable: using 1,000 gold tokens is uncommon and needs broader validation.  
- Missing analysis of accuracy vs. gold-data size to show whether performance degrades with more gold, the benefit threshold, and language dependence.  
- Noise-source analysis is limited to cross-lingual projections with identical tag sets; should consider other noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and mismatched tag sets.  
- Training objective equally weights gold and noisy labels; should report whether tuning these relative weights improves performance or affects sensitivity.  
- Technical ambiguity: Line 357 does not define \tilde{y} clearly (token-level vs. type-aggregated distributions) and does not specify how unaligned words are handled given only one-to-one alignments.  
- Minor typo: Line 267 has a closing bracket that should be an opening bracket.
