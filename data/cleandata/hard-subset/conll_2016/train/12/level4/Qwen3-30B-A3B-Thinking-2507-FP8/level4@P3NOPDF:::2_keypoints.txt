- Introduces a novel two-stage output-layer replacing softmax to model noise in projected labels for RNNs in low-resource NLP.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on POS tagging with 1,000 gold tokens plus large-scale projected data across eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Method is simple, modular, requires minimal changes to standard RNN pipelines, and achieves state-of-the-art results under extreme data scarcity.  
- Experimental validation is thorough for the fixed 1,000-token setting and shows practical viability beyond simulated languages.  
- Main limitation: evaluation fixed at 1,000 gold tokens lacks generalizability to other annotation budgets.  
- Missing analysis of performance scaling with varying gold sizes (e.g., 500–10,000 tokens) to identify thresholds and whether the method harms performance with more gold data.  
- Scope of label-noise sources underexplored—only cross-lingual projection with identical tag sets considered; no experiments with other noise types (e.g., crowd-sourced tags or coarse tag sets).  
- Training objective equally weights gold and noisy labels; the paper should investigate tuning relative loss weights and report sensitivity or optimal ratios.  
- Ambiguity in description of projected labels (\tilde{y})—unclear if they are distributions, token-level tags, or aggregated word-type labels, affecting reproducibility.  
- Unclear handling of unaligned words (loanwords, morphological variants) and whether token-level vs. type-level aggregation is required.  
- Minor issues: typo at line 267 and suggested tighter phrasing in the abstract (e.g., "models noise in projected labels").  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful, and broadly applicable contribution that would be elevated by added experiments on gold-data scaling, alternative noise sources, hyperparameter weighting, and clarifications for reproducibility.
