

I reviewed this paper previously as an ACL 2016 short paper draft. At that time, it contained a flaw in the experiment setup, which has now been corrected. Having previously indicated I would support acceptance at another *ACL event pending correction of this flaw, I now see no remaining obstacles to acceptance.  

Another reviewer noted that the paper's setup appears somewhat artificial when considering real-world low-resource language scenarios, particularly regarding the costs of annotator acquisition versus payment. This limitation should be explicitly addressed in the write-up to avoid overstating the method's applicability.  

The related work section should also incorporate relevant prior studies on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), for completeness.  

In summary, this represents a solid contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance. Additionally, the paper should clearly state whether the data will be made publicly available; I assume it will be and evaluate this aspect in good faith.
