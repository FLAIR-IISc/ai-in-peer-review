- Primary contribution: novel learnable noise-layer (parameter matrix) modeling transformation between gold tags and noisy cross-lingual projected tags for joint training.  
- Secondary contribution: consistently outperforms baselines in simulated low-resource settings and on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda using only 1,000 gold tokens.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, modular noise layer easily integrated into existing sequence tagging architectures.  
- Strength: strong empirical improvements across eight simulated languages and two real languages, showing practical utility with minimal gold data.  
- Strength: demonstrates generality by handling tagset mismatches and learning language-specific noise via transformation matrices.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses a single fixed gold-data size (1,000 tokens) and lacks analysis of performance scaling with varying gold amounts.  
- Weakness: restricted to cross-lingual projection with identical tagsets; unclear generalization to other noisy supervision sources or different tagset mappings.  
- Weakness: training objective weights gold and projected data equally without investigating optimal weighting per language or data quality.  
- Question for authors: How does the method perform with token-level projected labels instead of word-type aggregation?  
- Question for authors: For words that never align in the one-to-one alignment, how is the projected label vector \tilde{y} constructed?
