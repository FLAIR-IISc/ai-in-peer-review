- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger for low-resource languages that jointly learns from 1,000 gold tokens and noisy cross-lingual projected tags using an explicit noise layer to map projected→gold tags.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces an explicit noise layer to model the transformation between projected and gold tags instead of treating projected tags as clean.  
- Contribution 2: Proposes a joint training framework that combines gold-standard and projected data for POS tagging.  
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) with minimal gold data.  
- Strength 1: The noise layer captures when/how much to trust projected data, improving over methods that treat projections as clean or only implicitly correct them.  
- Strength 2: Achieves strong results with only 1,000 annotated tokens, addressing scarcity in low-resource scenarios.  
- Strength 3: Joint training effectively leverages both data sources and outperforms prior approaches that ignored or implicitly handled projection noise.  
- Weakness 1: Lacks discussion of practical limitations and a cost comparison between obtaining parallel data for projection versus annotating small gold datasets.  
- Weakness 2: Related work omits recent advances in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).  
- Weakness 3: Experimental setup for real languages needs better justification regarding the practicality and costs of sourcing parallel data versus annotators.  
- Question to authors: Will the datasets and code be released to enable reproducibility?
