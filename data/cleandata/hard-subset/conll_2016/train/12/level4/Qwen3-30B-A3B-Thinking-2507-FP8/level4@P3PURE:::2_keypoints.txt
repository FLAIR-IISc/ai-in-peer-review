- Introduces an output-layer modification: a fully connected layer modeling noise on top of softmax, enabling learning from both gold and projected annotations in low-resource RNNs.  
- Method is strong, simple, elegant, and modular, showing compelling POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Experimental setup is limited: should include accuracy vs. varying gold-data sizes to show whether performance degrades with ample gold, the threshold where the method helps, and cross-language variation.  
- Validation is narrow: only uses cross-lingual projected labels with identical tag sets, but noisy labels can come from other sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or different tag sets.  
- Training objective concern: paper uses equal weighting for gold and noisy labels; authors should report results when tuning relative weights.  
- Clarify notation and data construction (line 357): \tilde{y} is not defined—must state if it’s a distribution over projected POS tags, whether it's token-level vs. aggregated per word type, and how missing alignments are handled.  
- Minor correction: line 267 replace one closing bracket with an opening bracket.
