

Review:  
I reviewed this paper previously as a draft for ACL 2016. At that time, it contained a flaw in the experimental setup, which has since been corrected. Having previously indicated that I would support acceptance at another ACL venue upon correction of this flaw, I now see no remaining objections.  

One reviewer noted that the paper’s setup may appear somewhat artificial when considering real-world low-resource language scenarios, particularly regarding the costs associated with finding versus paying annotators. This limitation should be explicitly addressed in the writeup to avoid overstating the method’s applicability.  

The related work section should also incorporate relevant prior efforts in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016) and Agić et al. (2015). Including these would strengthen the paper’s completeness.  

In summary, this is a solid contribution. I recommend acceptance, provided the aforementioned points are addressed. I assume the data will be made publicly available, as is standard for research contributions.
