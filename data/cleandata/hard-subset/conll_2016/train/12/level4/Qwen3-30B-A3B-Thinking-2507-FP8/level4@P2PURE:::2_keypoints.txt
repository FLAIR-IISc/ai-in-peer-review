- Proposed modification: multiply standard softmax by a fully connected layer modeling noise to produce a distribution over noisy labels in RNN output layers.  
- Method enables learning from both small gold and large projected annotations for low-resource languages.  
- Strengths: simple, elegant, modular approach with robust POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Experimental concern: using as few as 1,000 gold tokens is uncommon; need accuracy vs. gold-size plots to assess performance with more gold data.  
- Key unanswered questions from such plots: (1) does the method hurt when ample gold exists? (2) what gold-size threshold makes it beneficial? (3) how does this threshold vary by language?  
- Limited evaluation scope: only cross-lingual projected labels with identical tag sets tested; other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or differing tag sets not explored.  
- Training objective issue: gold and noisy labels are equally weighted—reporting results when tuning their relative weights would be informative.  
- Clarification needed on projected-label notation (\tilde{y}): is it a distribution or token-level tag, and how are tags created for words without one-to-one alignments?
