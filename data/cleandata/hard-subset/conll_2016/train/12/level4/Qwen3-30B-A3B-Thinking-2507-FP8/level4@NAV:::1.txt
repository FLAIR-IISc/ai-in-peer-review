

I reviewed this paper previously as an ACL 2016 short paper draft. At that time, the draft contained a flaw in the experiment setup, which has now been corrected. Since I previously indicated willingness to accept the draft for another *ACL* event upon correction of this flaw, I now see no remaining barriers to acceptance. 

A second reviewer noted that the paper's setup may appear artificial when considering real-world low-resource language scenarios, particularly regarding the cost implications of annotator acquisition versus payment. This limitation should be explicitly addressed in the writeup to avoid overstating the method's applicability. 

The related work discussion should also incorporate relevant prior efforts in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), for completeness. 

In summary, this represents a valuable contribution, and I recommend acceptance. The paper should clearly state whether the data will be publicly released; I am evaluating this aspect in good faith, presuming research data availability.
