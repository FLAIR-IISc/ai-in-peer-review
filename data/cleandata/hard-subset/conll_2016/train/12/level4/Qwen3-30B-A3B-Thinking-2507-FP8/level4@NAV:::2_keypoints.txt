- Introduces an output-layer modification for RNNs that models noise from projected labels by adding a fully connected layer on top of softmax to represent noisy-label distributions.  
- Method is well-motivated, straightforward, elegant, and modular, showing strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages using small gold + large projected annotations.  
- Experimental setup is atypical (1,000-token gold); recommend plotting tagging accuracy versus varying gold sizes to assess real-world behavior.  
- Key empirical questions: does performance degrade with ample gold data, what gold-data threshold makes the method beneficial, and how does that threshold vary by target language?  
- Scope is limited to cross-lingual projections with identical tag sets; other noisy-label sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or differing tag sets are not explored.  
- Training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally—a surprising choice given small gold corpora; tuning relative weights should be investigated.  
- Line 357 is unclear about projected data (x_t, \tilde{y}); clarify what \tilde{y} represents, whether token-level projections (vs. word-type aggregates) work, and how unaligned words are handled.  
- Minor typo: Line 267 uses a closing bracket where an opening bracket is required.
