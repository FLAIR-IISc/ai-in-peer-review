I previously reviewed an earlier version of this work as a short paper submission to ACL 2016. A noted experimental issue has now been addressed. Having indicated at that time my willingness to accept a revised draft at a future ACL event contingent on this correction, I now see no reason to alter that assessment. 

Another reviewer raised a valid point regarding the potential artificiality of the setup when considering the practical realities of low-resource language annotation, specifically the differing costs associated with finding versus compensating annotators. I recommend acknowledging this limitation within the paper to avoid overstating the method’s applicability.

For a more complete related work discussion, the authors should consider referencing relevant work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL).

Overall, I believe this is a valuable contribution and recommend acceptance. Please clarify whether the data used in this research will be made publicly available, as my evaluation is based on the assumption of open access.
