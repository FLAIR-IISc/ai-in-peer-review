I had reviewed this paper before, when it was a brief draft for the 2016 ACL. At that time, there was an error in the test setup, which has now been corrected. Since I suggested at the time that I would be willing to accept the draft for another ACL event if the error was corrected, I see no reason not to do so now.

Another reviewer mentioned that, if we focus on real low-resource languages, the paper's setup is somewhat artificial, which relates to the cost of finding and paying commentators. I think this should be expressed in the writing rather than overpromoting the method.

There are relevant work streams in the annotation project for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johansen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL). It would be good to reflect those in the discussion of related work for completeness.

In summary, I think this is an excellent contribution, and I vote in favor of accepting it. It should be mentioned whether the data has been made available. I am now honestly evaluating those parts, assuming that the research has public availability.
