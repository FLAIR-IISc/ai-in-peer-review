I previously reviewed this paper when it was submitted as an ACL 2016 short paper. At that time, the experimental setup had a flaw, which has now been corrected. Since I indicated then that I would be willing to accept the paper at another *ACL venue contingent on fixing that issue, I now see no remaining obstacles to acceptance.

Another reviewer rightly noted that the paper’s setup is somewhat artificial when applied to truly low-resource languages, especially regarding the relative costs of finding versus paying annotators. This caveat should be made explicit in the write-up to avoid overselling the method.

There are also relevant lines of work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages—e.g., Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL)—that should be discussed for completeness in the related work section.

In sum, this is a nice contribution, and I recommend acceptance.

Please also clarify whether the data will be made available. I am evaluating those aspects in good faith, assuming public availability of the research.
