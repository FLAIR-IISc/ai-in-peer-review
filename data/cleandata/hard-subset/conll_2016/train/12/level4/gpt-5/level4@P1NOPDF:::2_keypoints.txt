- Proposes adding a noise-modeling fully connected layer after the softmax in RNN output to learn from both gold and projected annotations.  
- Method is well-motivated, simple, elegant, modular, and likely to be adopted for other low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Reports solid POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two true low-resource languages using small gold and large projected datasets.  
- Experimental setup (e.g., training with only ~1,000 token-level annotations) is unusual and may be a rare practical scenario.  
- Should show tagging accuracy as a function of gold-data amount to determine when the method helps, hurts, or stops providing benefits across languages.  
- Evaluation limited to cross-lingual projections with the same tag set; other realistic noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing, differing tag sets) are not tested.  
- Training objective gives equal weight to gold and noisy labels; paper should explore tuning their relative contributions.  
- Clarity issues about projected-label representation: \tilde{y}’s form (distribution vs token-level tags), aggregation over word types, and handling of unaligned tokens are not specified.  
- Minor typo: at line 267 one of the closing brackets should be an opening bracket.
