- Proposes an elegant, modular output-layer modification: a softmax over clean labels post-multiplied by a fully connected layer modeling a noise-generation channel to learn from gold and projected annotations.
- Strong empirical results: solid POS tagging performance on eight simulated and two genuine low-resource languages, method likely adoptable for other NLP low-resource tasks.
- Experimental setup is atypical (only ~1,000 token gold annotations); recommend plotting accuracy vs. amount of gold data to show when the method helps or hurts and how the threshold varies by language.
- Claims broader applicability to other noisy-label sources (e.g., crowdsourcing, differing tag sets) but evaluation is limited to cross-lingual projections using the same tag set.
- Unexpected equal weighting of gold and noisy losses in the objective; should report whether tuning their relative contributions improves results.
- Projection representation is under-specified: unclear what form \tilde{y} takes (distribution over projected tags? aggregated by type or token-level), and how tokens with no one-to-one alignment are handled.
