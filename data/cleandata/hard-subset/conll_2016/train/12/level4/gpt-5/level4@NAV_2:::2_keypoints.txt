- Proposes modifying the tagger head: softmax over clean labels followed by a learned linear transform to model noise and produce noisy-label distribution.  
- Strong, simple, and elegant approach with solid POS tagging results across eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Modular design likely useful for other low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Experimental protocol is atypical (only ~1k gold tokens); recommend plotting accuracy vs. gold-data size to show performance trends.  
- Need to evaluate whether the method hurts performance with ample gold data, identify the gold-data threshold for gains, and analyze language-specific trends.  
- Experiments only use cross-lingual projection with the same tag set, limiting evidence for broader noisy-supervision sources (e.g., crowdsourcing, differing tag inventories).  
- Surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy losses; suggest tuning the relative loss contribution to possibly improve results.  
- Clarify the form of projected label ỹ (token-level vs. type-level distributions) and handling of unaligned tokens.
