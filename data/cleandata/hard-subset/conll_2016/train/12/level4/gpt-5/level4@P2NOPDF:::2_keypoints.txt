- Proposes adding a learned “noise” layer after the softmax in RNN taggers to model corruption and train jointly on gold and projected noisy labels.
- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and likely applicable to other low-resource NLP tasks.
- Experiments (POS tagging) show solid gains using small gold sets plus large cross-lingual projected annotations across eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Experimental setup is unusual (training with as few as 1,000 annotated tokens) and may not reflect common practice.
- Recommend plotting tagging accuracy vs. gold data size to show when the noise layer helps or hurts and how the benefit threshold varies by language.
- Raises question whether the noise layer ever hurts with ample gold data and how much gold is needed before gains disappear.
- Other noisy-label sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and mismatched tag sets are not evaluated; current experiments only use cross-lingual projections with the same tag set.
- Training objective appears to weight gold and noisy labels equally; suggest tuning or learning the relative weight between them.
- Clarify the form of projected label representation \tilde{y} (distribution over tags, type-level vs. token-level) and how unaligned tokens are handled.
- Minor editorial fix: replace one of the two closing brackets with an opening bracket at line 267.
- Overall: well-motivated and promising; strengthen the paper by improving evaluation design, supervision weighting, and data-representation details.
