I previously reviewed this work when it was submitted as an ACL 2016 short paper. At that time, I identified a flaw in the experimental setup, which has now been corrected. As I had indicated that I would be willing to accept the paper for another *ACL venue contingent on that fix, I now see no obstacle to acceptance.

Another reviewer rightly noted that the experimental scenario is somewhat artificial for truly low-resource languages, particularly regarding the relative costs of finding versus paying annotators. This limitation should be made explicit in the paper to avoid overstating the practical applicability of the method.

For completeness, the related work section should more fully engage with research on annotation projection for extremely low-resource settings, such as Johannsen et al. (ACL 2016) and Agić et al. (ACL 2015), and clarify how the present approach compares to or complements those lines of work.

Overall, this is a strong contribution, and I recommend acceptance.

Please also indicate whether the data will be released. I am evaluating reproducibility in good faith with the presumption that the resources will be made publicly available.
