I previously reviewed this paper when it was an ACL 2016 short-paper draft. At that time, it had a flaw in the experimental setup, which has now been corrected. Since I had indicated I would be willing to accept the draft for another *ACL venue provided that flaw was fixed, I now see no obstacle to acceptance.

Another reviewer noted that the setup is somewhat artificial when considering truly low-resource languages, particularly regarding the costs of finding versus paying annotators. I agree this trade-off should be made explicit in the write-up to avoid overselling the method.

There are relevant lines of work in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages—for example, Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic/Agić et al. (2015, ACL). It would be helpful to reflect on these in the related work section for completeness.

In summary, this is a solid contribution, and I vote to accept. Please indicate whether the data will be made available. I evaluate those aspects in good faith, presuming public availability of the research materials.
