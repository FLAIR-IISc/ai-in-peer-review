I previously reviewed this paper when it was submitted as an ACL 2016 short paper. At that time, the experimental setup had a flaw, which has now been corrected. Since I had indicated that I would be willing to accept the paper for another *ACL venue provided the flaw was fixed, I now see no obstacle to acceptance.

Another reviewer noted that the paper’s setup is somewhat artificial when considering truly low-resource languages, particularly regarding the relative costs of finding versus paying annotators. This caveat should be clearly acknowledged in the write-up to avoid overselling the method.

There are relevant lines of work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages—e.g., Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL)—that should be discussed in the related work section for completeness.

In summary, this is a solid contribution, and I recommend acceptance. Please indicate whether the data will be made available. I am evaluating those aspects in good faith, presuming public availability of the research.
