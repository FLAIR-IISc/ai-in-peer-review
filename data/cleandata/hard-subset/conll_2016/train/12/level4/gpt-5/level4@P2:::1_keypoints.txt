- Paper presents a BiLSTM POS tagger that jointly trains on small gold-annotated data and noisy cross-lingual projected tags via an explicit noise layer modeling mapping between clean and projected tags.
- Primary contribution: integrates an explicit projection-noise transformation into a sequence tagger to learn when to trust projected labels.
- Secondary contribution: broad empirical validation showing consistent improvements over strong baselines and prior SOTA across eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).
- Experimental flaw from prior submission has been fixed, removing a previous obstacle to acceptance.
- Approach is principled and well-motivated: explicitly modeling projection noise leverages distant supervision without overwhelming scarce gold labels.
- Results are strong and consistent, improving on comparable baselines under the same evaluation protocol.
- Weakness: experimental setup is somewhat artificial for real low-resource contexts and should acknowledge practical annotation cost/logistics limitations.
- Weakness: related work should better discuss recent projection efforts targeting extremely low-resource languages (e.g., Johannsen et al. ACL 2016; Agić et al. ACL 2015).
- Question: clarify whether data, alignments/projections, and code will be released.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with requests to add discussion of practical annotation constraints and include the missing related work in the camera-ready version.
