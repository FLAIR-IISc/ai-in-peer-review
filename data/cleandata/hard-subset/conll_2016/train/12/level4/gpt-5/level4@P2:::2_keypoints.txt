- Paper proposes a BiLSTM sequence tagger with an explicit learnable noise layer to map clean tag distributions to noisy cross-lingual projected labels, trained jointly on small gold and larger projected data.
- Primary contribution: learnable noise-transformation layer that models mapping from clean tags to noisy projected labels.
- Secondary contribution: simple, end-to-end joint training scheme combining scarce gold annotations with plentiful projected labels.
- Tertiary contribution: strong empirical results across 10 language settings, showing robustness for low-resource POS tagging.
- Strength: conceptually clear and simple separation of tagger and noise via an interpretable transformation matrix.
- Strength: consistently meets or exceeds prior baselines in both simulated and true low-resource scenarios.
- Strength: practical and modular—easy to integrate into standard sequence taggers and applicable to other noisy supervision tasks.
- Strength: sensible training strategy—pretraining on gold followed by joint training mitigates harmful effects of noisy projections.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to extreme low-resource regime (~1k gold tokens); lacks learning curves across more realistic gold-data amounts.
- Weakness: objective equally weights gold and noisy losses; unclear if tuning this trade-off improves performance.
- Weakness: experiments only use cross-lingual projection with matched tagset; no evidence for other noisy sources or tagset mismatches despite claims.
- Weakness: ambiguity in description of projected-label vector ỹ—construction (token-level vs. aggregated) and handling of unaligned tokens are unclear.
- Question: how does performance vary with more gold supervision (e.g., 1k, 5k, 10k, full); does the noise layer always help or can it hurt?
- Question: were gold- and projection-based losses weighted differently in tuning, and how sensitive are results to this hyperparameter?
- Question: please clarify exactly how ỹ is defined (token-level aligned one-hot vs. sentence/type-level distribution) and how unaligned tokens are handled.
- Question: has a rectangular noise matrix been tried for tagset mismatch or other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing), and are there practical pitfalls?
- Question: what are training details—epochs for gold pretraining vs. joint training, and does initialization of the noise matrix (e.g., near-identity) matter?

- Additional comment: add an explicit definition of projected-label vector ỹ where first introduced.
- Additional comment: include ablations (no pretraining, no noise layer, tuned loss weights) if space permits to strengthen empirical claims.
