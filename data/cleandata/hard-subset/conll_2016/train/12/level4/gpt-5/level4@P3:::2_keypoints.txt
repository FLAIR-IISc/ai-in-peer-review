- Paper proposes a BiLSTM tagger augmented with an explicit noise (confusion) layer to jointly learn from small gold annotations and large noisy cross-lingual projected POS tags.  
- Training alternates between gold supervision and noisy projected supervision to learn when to trust projections.

Contributions:
- Principled, modular noise-layer formulation that models mapping between clean and noisy labels for distant supervision.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda) showing consistent gains.  
- Interpretable analysis of learned noise matrices (A) demonstrating capture of systematic projection errors.

Strengths:
- Elegant and effective modeling of projection noise with an interpretable confusion layer.  
- Strong empirical results using only ~1K gold tokens, matching or beating prior work.  
- Sensible and necessary two-stage training (pretrain on gold then joint training).  
- General, reusable approach likely transferable to other NLP distant-supervision tasks.  
- Clear interpretability via A matrix visualizations that diagnose projection quality.

Weaknesses:
- Empirical regime fixed at ~1K gold tokens; lacks learning curves across different gold sizes.  
- Joint loss equally weights gold and noisy objectives with no ablation or tuning of the trade-off.  
- Only evaluates same-tagset projection; no experiments on mismatched tagsets or other noisy sources.  
- Incomplete ablations on key design choices (pretraining, alignment filtering, A initialization/regularization).  
- Ambiguity in construction of projected supervision ỹ (token vs type aggregation, handling of unaligned tokens).  

Questions for authors:
- Can you provide learning curves across gold-token amounts (e.g., 0.5k, 1k, 2k, 5k, 10k) and show whether projected data ever hurts?  
- Did you tune a weight between gold and projected losses or use annealing; what values or schedules were effective?  
- How are projected labels ỹ constructed (token-level or type-aggregated) and how are unaligned tokens treated?  
- How is A initialized and regularized; any sensitivity to these choices?  
- What is the effect of removing pretraining, using all alignment types, or not filtering by alignment scores?  
- How sensitive are results to English tagger/alignment quality and does benefit vary with projection noise?
