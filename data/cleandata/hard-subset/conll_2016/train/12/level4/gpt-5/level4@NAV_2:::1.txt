I assessed this work previously, when it appeared as an ACL 2016 short paper draft. At that time I identified a flaw in the experimental setup; that issue has now been addressed. Since I had indicated I would support acceptance contingent on that correction, I no longer see impediments to recommending acceptance.

A fellow reviewer observed that the setup becomes somewhat artificial when considering truly low-resource languages, particularly with respect to the practical trade-off between the effort of locating annotators and the cost of compensating them. This caveat should be made explicit in the paper to avoid overselling the approach.

There are closely related efforts on annotation projection for extremely low-resource scenarios, e.g., Johannsen et al. (ACL 2016) and Agic et al. (ACL 2015). It would strengthen the related work section to situate this submission relative to those lines of work.

In sum, this is a solid contribution, and I recommend acceptance.

Please also clarify whether the data will be released. I am evaluating this aspect in good faith, assuming the resources will be made publicly available.
