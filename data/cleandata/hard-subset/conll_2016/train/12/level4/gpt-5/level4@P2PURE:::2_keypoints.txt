- Proposal: modify RNN output layer by post-multiplying softmax with a fully-connected layer that models noise, enabling joint learning from gold and projected noisy labels.  
- Strengths: method is simple, elegant, modular, and yields solid POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Experimental setup concern: training with as few as 1,000 tokens is unusual; need plots of tagging accuracy vs. amount of gold data to assess when the method helps or hurts and whether thresholds vary by language.  
- Generalization concern: noisy labels from other sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or differing tag sets are plausible but not evaluated—paper only uses cross-lingual projections with the same tag set.  
- Objective weighting concern: surprising that gold and noisy labels are weighted equally; should report tuning of their relative contributions.  
- Ambiguity in projected-label representation: paper does not specify what vector \tilde{y} is (token-level vs aggregated type-level distribution), how token-level projected tags would be handled, or how to construct \tilde{y} for unaligned words.
