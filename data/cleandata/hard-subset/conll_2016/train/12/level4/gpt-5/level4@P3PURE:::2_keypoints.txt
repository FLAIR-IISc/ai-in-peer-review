- Proposal: modify RNN output layer by composing softmax with a fully connected noise-generation layer to train from gold and projected annotations.  
- Overall verdict: strong, apt, simple, elegant, and modular—likely adoptable for other low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Experiments: solid POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages using small gold + large projected data.  
- Experimental setup concern: using only ~1,000 gold tokens is uncommon; recommend plotting accuracy vs. amount of gold data to show when the method helps and whether it hurts with ample gold, and whether thresholds vary by language.  
- Scope limitation: evaluation only on cross-lingual projection with the same tag set; other noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tag sets are not tested.  
- Objective weighting: surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels—suggest tuning relative contributions could improve performance.
