- Proposes a simple, modular noise layer on top of the softmax to model label corruption for training from gold and projected annotations.  
- Elegant and easy-to-implement idea that cleanly models noisy labels.  
- Strong empirical gains on POS tagging across eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Likely broadly applicable to other low-resource NLP tasks using weak or noisy supervision.  
- Needs experiments varying gold-data size to show when the method helps or hurts and to identify crossover points (possibly language-dependent).  
- Should report results from tuning a mixing weight between gold and noisy supervision and analyze dependence on gold size/quality, projection amount/quality, and language.  
- Lacks evaluation beyond cross-lingual projections; should test or discuss heterogeneous noisy sources (crowd, distant supervision) and label-set mismatches.  
- Clarify the form of projected supervision ỹ (token- vs. type-level, distributional vs. other) and how unaligned tokens are handled; report coverage statistics.  
- Provide training details for the noise layer: initialization strategy, regularization/constraints, stability across random seeds, and identifiability.  
- Add baselines: gold-only, naive mixing without learned noise model, and confidence-weighted projections; include per-language results and error analysis.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated, and effective method; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions and additional analyses.  
- Minor fixes: correct bracket typo (line 267), precisely define ỹ (line 357), note computational overhead is negligible, and consider adding a schematic figure.
