I previously reviewed this paper when it was an ACL 2016 short paper draft. At that time, the experimental setup had a flaw, which has now been corrected. Since I had indicated I would be willing to accept the draft for another *ACL venue provided the flaw was fixed, I now see no obstacle to doing so. Another reviewer noted that the setup is somewhat artificial when considering truly low-resource languages, particularly regarding the costs of locating versus paying annotators. I believe this should be made explicit in the write-up to avoid overselling the method.

There is relevant work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, e.g., Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL). It would be helpful to reflect on these in the related work section for completeness.

In summary, this is a solid contribution, and I recommend acceptance. Please indicate whether the data will be made available. I am evaluating those aspects in good faith, presuming public availability of the research.
