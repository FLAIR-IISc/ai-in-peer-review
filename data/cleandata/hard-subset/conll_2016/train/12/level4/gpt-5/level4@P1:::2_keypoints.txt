- Proposes adding a fully connected “noise” layer on top of a BiLSTM softmax to jointly learn from small gold data and large noisy cross-lingual projections by modeling the noise process.
- Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource languages plus Malagasy and Kinyarwanda with ~1k gold tokens and parallel data; results match or exceed prior work and the method is modular beyond POS tagging.

Strengths
- Elegant, principled idea: explicitly modeling projection noise with few extra parameters in a minimal, end-to-end trainable change.
- Strong empirical gains over BiLSTM baselines and prior SOTA across simulated and real low-resource settings despite very limited gold data.
- Interpretable: learned noise matrices reveal systematic cross-lingual confusions and capture alignment/syntactic divergence errors.
- Practically portable: design likely applicable to other low-resource sequence tagging tasks and other noisy supervision sources.

Weaknesses
- Practicality concern: only ~1k gold tokens is an uncommon setting; need learning curves to show when the method helps or hurts as gold data increases and whether thresholds vary by language.
- Limited scope: experiments only cover cross-lingual projection with the same tagset, so claims about crowdsourcing or mismatched tagsets are untested.
- Objective weighting: equal weighting of gold and noisy losses may be suboptimal; no reported experiments on weighting or curriculum/schedule.
- Missing details: unclear definition/representation of projected label ỹ and how tokens without alignments are handled, affecting reproducibility and noise-layer training.

Questions to authors
- Please provide learning curves for varying gold sizes (e.g., 500, 1k, 5k, 10k) and report where the method stops helping and whether that varies by language.
- How is ỹ defined: token-specific (one-hot or token-level distribution), type-level, or sentence-level, and how are unaligned tokens handled given one-to-one alignment filtering?
- Did you experiment with weighting gold vs. projected losses or with training schedules (e.g., gold-heavy early), and what were the effects?
- How sensitive are results to the quantity/quality of projected data and the one-to-one alignment restriction; does changing alignment filtering alter performance?
- Did you try mismatched tagsets using a rectangular noise matrix A, and if not, are there implementation constraints?
- What regularization is applied to the noise matrix A to prevent degenerate solutions, and how sensitive are results to initialization and the two-stage training procedure?
