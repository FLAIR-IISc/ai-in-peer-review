I had reviewed this paper previously when it was a brief draft for the 2016 ACL. At the time, there was an error in the test setup, which has now been corrected. Since I suggested at the time that I would be willing to accept a draft for another ACL event if the error were corrected, I see no reason not to accept it now.

Another reviewer noted that if we focus on actual low-resource languages, the paper's setup is somewhat artificial, which relates to the cost of finding and paying commenters. I think this should be expressed in the writing rather than over-promoting the methodology.

There are relevant work streams in the annotation project for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johansen et al. (2016, ACL) and Azik et al. (2015, ACL). It would be good to reflect those in the discussion of related work for completeness.

In summary, I think this is an excellent contribution, and I vote in favor of accepting it. It should be mentioned whether the data has been made available. I am now honestly evaluating those parts, assuming that the research has public availability.
