- Proposes augmenting RNN softmax with a fully connected layer to model noise and learn from gold and projected annotations.  
- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and shows promising POS tagging results on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages.  
- Experimental setup (e.g., using as few as 1,000 gold tokens) is unconventional and may be uncommon in practice.  
- Recommends plotting accuracy vs. varying gold annotation sizes to assess when the method helps, whether it harms with ample gold, and language-specific thresholds.  
- Evaluation is limited to cross-lingual projections with the same tag set; other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tag sets are unexplored.  
- The training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally; tuning their relative contributions should be investigated.  
- Clarify the nature of the projected vector representation (\tilde{y}): type-level vs token-level construction and handling of unaligned words.  
- Minor typographical error: bracket mismatch at line 267.
