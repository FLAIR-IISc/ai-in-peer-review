I previously reviewed this paper as an ACL 2016 short paper draft, at which point it contained a flaw in the experiment setup that has since been corrected. Given that I suggested I would be willing to accept the draft for another ACL event contingent upon the correction of this flaw, I now see no obstacles to doing so.

Another reviewer noted that the paper's setup may be somewhat artificial, particularly when considering real low-resource languages, as it relates to the costs of finding versus paying annotators. I concur that this limitation should be acknowledged in the write-up to avoid overselling the method.

There are relevant lines of work in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), that would enhance the related work discussion if included for completeness.

In summary, I believe this is a valuable contribution, and I vote to accept it. It would be beneficial to indicate whether the data is made available; I have evaluated this aspect in good faith, assuming public availability of research.
