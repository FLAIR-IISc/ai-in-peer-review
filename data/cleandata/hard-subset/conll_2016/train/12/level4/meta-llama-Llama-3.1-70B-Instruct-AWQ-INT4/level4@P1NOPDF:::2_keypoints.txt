- Proposes modifying RNN output layer by multiplying the softmax with a fully connected layer that models noise, yielding a distribution over noisy labels.  
- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and shows strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Modularity suggests promise for other low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Experimental setup is unconventional (uses as few as ~1000 gold tokens); a plot of accuracy vs. gold-annotation size is needed.  
- Should evaluate whether performance degrades or improves with abundant gold data and identify the gold-data threshold and language dependency for benefit.  
- Only evaluated with cross-lingual projections using the same tag set; other noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and different tag sets are not tested.  
- Training objective equally weights gold and noisy labels; tuning their relative contributions should be investigated.  
- Description of projected vector \tilde{y} is ambiguous (token-level vs. type-level distribution) and unclear how to handle unaligned words.  
- Minor typo: bracket error at line 267.
