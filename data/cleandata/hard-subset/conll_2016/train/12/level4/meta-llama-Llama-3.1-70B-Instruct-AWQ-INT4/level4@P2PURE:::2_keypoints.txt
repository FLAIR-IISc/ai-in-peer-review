- Proposed method: modify RNN output by multiplying softmax with a fully connected layer that models noise, enabling training from gold and projected annotations.  
- Strengths: simple, elegant, modular approach with strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Practical concern: experimental setup (very limited gold annotations) is unusual and may have limited real-world applicability.  
- Suggestion: plot tagging accuracy versus size of gold annotations to show when the method helps, whether it degrades with ample gold data, and the gold-data threshold by language.  
- Generality concern: only evaluated with cross-lingual projection using the same tag set; should test other noisy-label sources (e.g., crowd-sourcing) and differing tag sets.  
- Objective weighting: surprising that gold and noisy labels are equally weighted—recommend investigating tuned or weighted contributions.  
- Data representation unclear: format of projected-vector \tilde{y} not specified; clarify token-level vs aggregated word-type projections and how to handle never-aligned words.
