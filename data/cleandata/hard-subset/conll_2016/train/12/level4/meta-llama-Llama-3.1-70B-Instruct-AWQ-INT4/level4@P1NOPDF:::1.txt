I previously reviewed this paper as a draft for ACL 2016. At that time, I identified a flaw in the experimental setup, which has since been corrected. Given that I suggested I would be willing to accept the draft for another ACL event if the flaw was addressed, I now see no obstacles to accepting it.

Another reviewer noted that the paper's setup may be somewhat artificial, particularly when considering real low-resource languages and the costs associated with finding versus paying annotators. I agree that this limitation should be acknowledged in the write-up to avoid overstating the method's capabilities.

The authors may also want to consider discussing relevant work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), to enhance the completeness of the related work section.

In summary, I believe this is a valuable contribution, and I vote to accept it. It would be helpful to indicate whether the data used in the study will be made available. In evaluating the paper, I have assumed that the data will be publicly available, as is standard practice in research.
