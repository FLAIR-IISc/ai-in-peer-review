- Proposes modifying RNN output layer by multiplying softmax with a learned noise layer to model noisy (projected) labels.
- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and easy to implement.
- Shows strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages using small gold + large projected annotations.
- Experimental setup is unusual (very small gold set ~1000 tokens); lacks analysis varying gold-data size.
- Should plot accuracy vs. gold annotation size to show when the method helps or harms and whether thresholds depend on language.
- Only evaluates cross-lingual projections with the same tag set; other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tag sets are not explored.
- Training objective gives equal weight to gold and noisy labels; authors should report effects of tuning their relative contributions.
- Clarify the projected-data vector representation (\tilde{y}): is it a distribution over projected POS tags, token-level vs. type-level aggregation, and handling of unaligned words?
- Questionable handling of words never aligned under the one-to-one alignment constraint.
- Minor typo: bracket mismatch reported at line 267.
- Main claimed contributions: a new noise-layer neural model for sequence tagging, joint training on gold and projected annotations, and empirical validation on multiple low-resource languages.
