- Introduces a deep model that explicitly models noise in cross-lingual projected annotations to improve low-resource learning.
- Uses a bidirectional LSTM tagger with an explicit noise layer and joint training on gold and projected annotations.
- Evaluated on simulated settings and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Novel and effective approach to handling noisy projected annotations for low-resource NLP.
- Experimental results convincingly demonstrate effectiveness on both simulated and real languages.
- Provides thorough analysis and visualization of the noise transformation matrices, improving interpretability.
- Experimental setup may still be somewhat artificial and doesn’t fully account for annotator discovery vs. payment costs.
- Related work coverage is incomplete, missing additional lines on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages.
- Unclear whether the experimental data will be publicly released, raising reproducibility concerns.
- Request for more details on costs and challenges of obtaining gold-standard annotations and how the approach mitigates them.
- Inquiry about extending the approach to other NLP tasks and languages and potential method limitations.
- Asks whether the data will be made publicly available and how it will be shared.
