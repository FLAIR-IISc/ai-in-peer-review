I previously reviewed this paper as a draft submission to ACL 2016, where I identified a flaw in the experiment setup. The authors have since addressed this issue, and given my earlier suggestion to reconsider the paper for another ACL event upon correction, I now have no reservations about accepting it.

However, another reviewer raised a valid point regarding the paper's setup, which may be somewhat artificial when applied to real low-resource languages, particularly in terms of the costs associated with finding versus paying annotators. I agree that this limitation should be acknowledged in the paper to avoid overselling the method.

The authors may also benefit from discussing relevant work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), to enhance the completeness of their related work discussion.

In summary, I consider this to be a valuable contribution, and I vote to accept the paper. It would be helpful to indicate whether the data used in the study will be made available, as this is an important aspect of research reproducibility and transparency. I evaluate this aspect in good faith, assuming that the data will be publicly available.
