- Prior review noted an experimental flaw which has now been corrected, removing prior objections to acceptance.
- Paper proposes a sequence-tagging model that learns to correct cross-lingual projection errors via an explicit noise layer.
- Model is trained jointly on gold-standard and projected-tag corpora using a BiLSTM architecture.
- Main contribution 1: explicit modeling of the transformation between tagsets within a deep model.
- Main contribution 2: integration of that noise model into a joint training regime on annotated and projected data.
- Main contribution 3: evaluation on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages, matching or improving on baselines and achieving state-of-the-art on the two real languages.
- Strength 1: novel and significant approach to correcting projection noise in sequence tagging.
- Strength 2: broad evaluation across simulated and real low-resource languages demonstrating robustness.
- Strength 3: detailed analysis of results, including inspection of the noise transformation matrix A.
- Weakness 1: experiment setup may be somewhat artificial—assumes availability of a small annotated set that may not exist in real low-resource scenarios.
- Weakness 2: insufficient discussion of related work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages.
- Weakness 3: no clear statement about whether experimental data is publicly available, impacting reproducibility.
- Question 1: provide more details on the experiment setup and its relation to real-world low-resource settings.
- Question 2: discuss related work in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages and how this work compares.
- Question 3: clarify whether the data used is publicly available and, if so, provide access details.
- Additional comment: reviewer votes to accept, urging authors to address the noted weaknesses and provide more experimental and related-work details.
