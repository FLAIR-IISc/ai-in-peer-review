I previously reviewed this paper in its draft form for ACL 2016, where it had an experimental setup flaw that has since been addressed. Given my earlier willingness to consider the draft for another ACL event contingent on the correction of this flaw, I no longer see any barriers to acceptance.

Another reviewer noted that the paper's setup may be somewhat contrived, particularly when considering real-world low-resource languages and the associated costs of annotator recruitment versus compensation. I agree that this limitation should be acknowledged in the manuscript to provide a more balanced view of the method's applicability.

The authors may also benefit from discussing relevant research on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016) and Agic et al. (2015), to enhance the completeness of the related work section.

In summary, I consider this a valuable contribution and recommend acceptance. It would be beneficial to specify whether the data will be made publicly available, as I evaluate such aspects with the assumption of open access to research data.
