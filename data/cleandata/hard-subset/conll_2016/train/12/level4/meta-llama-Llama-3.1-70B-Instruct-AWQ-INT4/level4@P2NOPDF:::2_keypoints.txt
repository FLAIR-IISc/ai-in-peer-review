- Proposes modifying RNN output layer by multiplying softmax with a noise-modeling fully connected layer to learn from both gold and projected annotations in low-resource languages.

- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and yields strong POS-tagging results on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages using few gold and many projected annotations.

- Experimental setup is unusual (only ~1000 token gold annotations); recommends plotting accuracy vs. gold-data size to assess when the method helps and whether performance degrades with abundant gold.

- Limited evaluation scope: only cross-lingual projected labels with the same tag set; unclear applicability to other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or different tag sets.

- Surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in the training objective; should test tuning the relative contributions of the two terms.

- Description of projected label vector \tilde{y} is unclear (type-level vs token-level distribution); unclear how to construct \tilde{y} for unaligned words.

- Minor typo: bracket mismatch at line 267.
