- Summary: Proposes a sequence-tagging method with an explicit noise layer to correct cross-lingual projection errors via joint learning on gold and projected corpora; evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, matching or exceeding SOTA.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a deep model that explicitly models tagset transformation/noise within the network.

- Contribution 2: Integrates the noise model into a joint training framework combining annotated and projected data.

- Contribution 3: Evaluates across multiple simulated and real low-resource languages, showing POS accuracy improvements.

- Strength 1: Novel handling of projection noise addresses a key challenge in low-resource NLP.

- Strength 2: Demonstrated effectiveness on both simulated and real-world low-resource languages.

- Strength 3: Thorough empirical evaluation including SOTA comparisons and analysis of learned noise matrices.

- Weakness 1: Experimental setup assumes availability of some annotated data, which may be unrealistic for some low-resource languages.

- Weakness 2: Lacks detailed analysis of annotation cost versus performance trade-offs.

- Weakness 3: Related-work discussion is not comprehensive, especially for extremely low-resource annotation-projection methods.

- Question 1: Provide more details on annotation cost vs. performance trade-offs.

- Question 2: How will the approach handle cases with extremely scarce or expensive annotated data?

- Question 3: Provide deeper analysis of learned noise matrices and common error types in cross-lingual projection.
