- Proposed method: modify RNN output by multiplying softmax with a fully connected layer that models noise, yielding a distribution over noisy labels to learn from gold and projected annotations.
- Strengths: method is simple, elegant, modular, and reports strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Experimental setup concern: using only ~1000 token gold annotations is unusual; broader validation varying gold annotation size is needed.
- Missing analysis: need plots of tagging accuracy vs gold-data size to show when the method helps or hurts and whether benefits depend on target language.
- Limited evaluation: only uses cross-lingual projected labels with the same tag set, though other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or different tag sets are possible.
- Training objective issue: paper assigns equal weight to gold and noisy labels; should report whether tuning their relative contributions helps.
- Representation ambiguity: unclear what the projected label vector \tilde{y} is (e.g., tag distribution), whether it’s token- or type-level, and how unaligned words are handled.
- Minor correction: bracket typo at line 267.
- Conclusion: promising approach but requires additional experiments and analyses to fully characterize its potential and limitations.
