I previously reviewed this paper as a draft submission for ACL 2016, and at that time, it contained a flaw in the experimental setup, which has since been corrected. Given that I had expressed a willingness to accept the draft for another ACL event, contingent upon the correction of this flaw, I now see no obstacles to accepting it.

However, another reviewer raised a valid point that the paper's setup may be somewhat artificial, particularly when considering real low-resource languages, where the costs of finding versus paying annotators can be significant. I believe this limitation should be acknowledged in the write-up to avoid overselling the method.

There are relevant lines of work in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), which should be included in the related work discussion for the sake of completeness.

In summary, I think this is a nice contribution, and I vote to accept it. It would be helpful to indicate whether the data used in the study will be made available. I have evaluated this submission with the assumption that the data will be publicly available, as is standard practice in research.
