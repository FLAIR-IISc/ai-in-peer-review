- Proposes multiplying the softmax output by a fully connected layer that models noise, enabling learning from both gold and projected annotations.
- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and reports strong POS tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Experimental setup is unusual (training with as few as 1,000 token gold annotations); this scenario is rare and needs broader validation.
- Recommend plotting tagging accuracy versus gold-annotation size to answer: does performance degrade with sufficient gold? what is the gold threshold where the method helps? does this vary by language?
- Only evaluated on cross-lingual projections with the same tag set; other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tag sets are not explored.
- Surprising that the training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally; should report results when tuning the relative contribution of the two terms.
- The description of the projected vector \tilde{y} is unclear (what representation—distribution over projected POS?), and it’s unclear whether token-level vs type-level aggregation or unaligned words affect the approach.
- Minor typo: line 267 has a bracket error (one closing bracket should be an opening bracket).
