I previously reviewed this paper in its draft form for ACL 2016, where I identified a flaw in the experiment setup. I am pleased to see that this issue has been addressed in the current submission. Given that my previous assessment suggested acceptance pending correction of this flaw, I now have no reservations about recommending this paper for acceptance.

However, I do want to acknowledge a concern raised by another reviewer regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, the setup may be somewhat artificial when considering real-world applications for low-resource languages, particularly in terms of the costs associated with finding versus paying annotators. I believe it is essential to discuss this limitation in the paper to provide a more balanced view of the proposed method and avoid overstating its potential.

The paper could also benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work, particularly in the area of annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages. For example, Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL) have made relevant contributions that warrant consideration. Including these references would enhance the paper's completeness and situating the proposed method within the broader research landscape.

In summary, I consider this a valuable contribution, and I vote to accept the paper. However, I would like to see a clear indication of whether the data used in the study will be made available to the research community. I evaluate this aspect in good faith, assuming that the data will be publicly available, as is standard practice in our field.

Rating: Accept
