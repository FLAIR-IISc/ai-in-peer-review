- Proposes a novel sequence-tagging method that learns to correct cross-lingual projection errors via an explicit noise transformation layer.
- Introduces a noise transformation encoding mapping between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags.
- Integrates the noise model into a deep neural network and jointly trains on annotated and projected data.

- Well-motivated and novel use of a noise layer to model tagset transformations.
- Empirical results show the method outperforms state-of-the-art on simulated and real low-resource languages.
- Clear and concise presentation of the model, noise transformation, and joint training objective.

- Experiments still rely on simulated low-resource languages, which may not reflect real-world annotation costs and challenges.
- Discussion of related work, especially on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, is insufficient.

- Request for details on costs and challenges of working with real low-resource languages and how the method addresses them.
- Ask for comparison to other annotation projection methods (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).
- Will the experimental data be made publicly available?
