- Proposal: modify RNN output by combining traditional softmax with a fully connected layer modeling noise, enabling learning from gold and projected annotations in low-resource settings.
- Strong overall: approach is suitable, simple, elegant, shows promising POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two true low-resource languages, and is modular/likely adoptable.
- Experimental concern: setup is somewhat unusual/practically rare; paper should vary gold-annotation size and plot accuracy to show when the method helps and how it scales with more gold data.
- Limited noise sources: experiments only use cross-lingual projections with the same tag set, but noisy labels could also come from crowdsourcing or different tag sets and should be evaluated.
- Objective weighting: surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels — paper should test tuning contributions of the two terms given small gold corpora.
- Projected-data ambiguity: unclear definition of vector representation \tilde{y} (distribution vs aggregated type-level vs token-level), and unclear handling of words with no alignments.
- Minor issue: typographical bracket error at line 267.
