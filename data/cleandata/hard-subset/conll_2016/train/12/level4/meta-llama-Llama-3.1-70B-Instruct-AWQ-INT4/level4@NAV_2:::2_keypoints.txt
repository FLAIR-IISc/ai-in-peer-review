- Novel approach: sequence tagging that learns to correct cross-lingual projection errors via an explicit noise layer, trained jointly on a tiny gold corpus and larger projected data; evaluated with 1,000 gold tokens + parallel data, achieving SOTA on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages.

- Contribution: new neural model for low-resource sequence tagging that leverages both small gold annotations and distant supervision from cross-lingual tag projection.

- Contribution: uses a BiLSTM base with an added noise transformation layer to model mapping between projected high-resource tags and target low-resource tags.

- Contribution: empirically outperforms existing methods on simulated and real low-resource POS tagging tasks.

- Strength: method is simple, elegant, and modular, making it accessible and reusable for other low-resource NLP problems.

- Strength: strong empirical results on eight simulated and two truly low-resource languages.

- Weakness: experimental setup is unusual; recommendation to report tagging accuracy as a function of gold annotation size.

- Weakness: only evaluates cross-lingual projection with the same tag set; lacks exploration of other noisy label sources or differing tag sets.

- Weakness: training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally; paper should investigate tuning the relative contribution of the two terms.

- Question: request for details on vector representation of projected data and whether token-level projected POS tags suffice.

- Question: how are projected labels constructed for words that are never aligned?

- Question: have the authors experimented with tuning the relative weight of the gold vs. noisy loss terms, and what were the outcomes?
