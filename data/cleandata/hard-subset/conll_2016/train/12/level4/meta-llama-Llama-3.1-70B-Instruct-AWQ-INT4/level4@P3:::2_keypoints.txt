- Proposes a novel BiLSTM sequence-tagging model with an explicit noise layer to correct errors from cross-lingual projection.
- Frames learning as joint training on a small gold-annotated corpus and a larger projected (noisy) corpus, trainable end-to-end.
- Evaluates on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), claiming state-of-the-art results.

- Method is simple, elegant, and easy to integrate into existing NLP pipelines.
- Reports strong POS-tagging performance on both simulated and real low-resource languages.
- Model is modular and likely applicable to other low-resource NLP tasks.

- Experimental setup is unusual; unclear how performance varies with different amounts of gold annotations.
- Only tested with cross-lingual projection using the same tag set; unclear generalizability to other noisy sources or differing tag sets.
- Training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally; unclear if tuning their contributions would improve results.

- How does performance change with varying amounts of gold annotations, especially when gold data is plentiful?
- Can noisy-label vectors be constructed from token-level projected POS tags instead of aggregating by word type?
- How are noisy-label vectors constructed for words that are never aligned in projection?

- Paper needs a more detailed discussion of the noise layer and its properties.
- Should include additional baselines or comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.
- Add visualizations or illustrations to clarify the model and its components.
