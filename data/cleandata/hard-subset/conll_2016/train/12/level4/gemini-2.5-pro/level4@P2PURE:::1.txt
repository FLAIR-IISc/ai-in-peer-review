I previously reviewed a short paper version of this manuscript for ACL 2016. A significant flaw in the experimental setup that I identified at the time has now been corrected. As my primary concern has been successfully addressed, I now recommend this paper for acceptance.

One point, raised by another reviewer, merits consideration. The experimental setup could be viewed as somewhat artificial for truly low-resource languages, especially regarding the real-world trade-off between the cost of finding qualified annotators and the cost of the annotation task itself. To avoid overstating the method's applicability, the authors should acknowledge and discuss this limitation in the paper.

For completeness, the related work section would benefit from a discussion of relevant research in annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages. The work of Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), for example, should be included to better situate this contribution within the field.

In summary, this paper is a valuable contribution, and I vote for acceptance. However, the authors should explicitly state whether the data and resources used in this study will be made publicly available. My positive evaluation assumes this research will be reproducible.
