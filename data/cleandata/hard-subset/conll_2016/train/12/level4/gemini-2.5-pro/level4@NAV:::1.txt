I am familiar with this work, having reviewed a prior version submitted as an ACL 2016 short paper. My primary concern at the time was a flaw in the experimental design, which I am pleased to see has been successfully rectified in this revision. As my previous recommendation was conditional on this correction, I now see no further obstacles to its acceptance.

I concur with another reviewer's point that the experimental setup is somewhat idealized for genuinely low-resource scenarios, particularly concerning the practical trade-offs between finding and paying annotators. To avoid overstating the method's claims, the authors should transparently discuss this limitation in the paper.

Furthermore, the related work section could be strengthened by incorporating and discussing relevant literature on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL). This would provide a more complete context for the paper's contribution.

In summary, this is a valuable contribution, and I recommend acceptance. For reproducibility, the authors should clarify whether the data and code will be made publicly available; my evaluation assumes this in good faith.
