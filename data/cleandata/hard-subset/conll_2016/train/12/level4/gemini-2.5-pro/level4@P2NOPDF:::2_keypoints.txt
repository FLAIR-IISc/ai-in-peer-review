- Proposes augmenting RNN softmax with a learned fully connected noise layer and multiplying them to model distributions over noisy observed labels.  
- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and well-suited to low-resource noisy-label settings.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on POS tagging for eight simulated and two genuine low-resource languages using 1,000 gold tokens plus projected annotations.  
- Experimental setup is narrow (only 1,000 gold tokens); recommend plotting performance vs. gold-data size to assess behavior as gold data increases.  
- Key experimental questions: does the method hurt with large gold data, what gold-data threshold yields benefit, and how does this threshold vary by language?  
- Generalizability: framework likely applies to other noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and mismatched tag sets, and this broader applicability should be acknowledged.  
- Objective weighting: equal loss weighting for gold and noisy data is surprising; ablation or tuning of relative weights is needed to verify 1:1 is optimal.  
- Clarify projected data representation \tilde{y}: specify whether it is one-hot or full distribution, token- vs. type-level construction, and handling of unaligned words.  
- Minor correction: fix mismatched bracket typo at line 267.
