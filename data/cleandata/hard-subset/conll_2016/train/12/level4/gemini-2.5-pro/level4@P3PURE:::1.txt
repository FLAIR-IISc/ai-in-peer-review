I previously reviewed this submission for ACL 2016 as a short paper. At that time, it contained a significant flaw in the experimental setup, which has now been corrected. As my previous recommendation for acceptance was conditional on this correction, I am pleased to see the issue resolved and now recommend this paper for acceptance.

While the core contribution is valuable, I offer the following suggestions to further strengthen the manuscript:

1.  **Situating the Work for Low-Resource Languages:** A previous reviewer noted that the paper's setup may be somewhat artificial for truly low-resource scenarios, especially regarding the trade-off between the cost of finding annotators and the cost of paying them. I agree with this assessment. The authors should discuss this limitation explicitly in the paper to provide a more nuanced perspective and avoid overstating the method's immediate applicability.

2.  **Related Work:** The discussion of related work would be more complete with the inclusion of relevant research on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages. In particular, I suggest citing and discussing the work of Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL).

3.  **Data Availability:** The paper should clearly state whether the data and/or code will be made publicly available to support reproducibility. My evaluation assumes in good faith that the research artifacts will be released.

In summary, this is a nice contribution, and I vote to accept the paper. Addressing the minor points above will improve the final version.
