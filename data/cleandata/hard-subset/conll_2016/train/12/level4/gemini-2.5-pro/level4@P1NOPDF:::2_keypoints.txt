- Proposal: augment RNN softmax with a fully connected layer to model noise, enabling joint learning from gold and projected (noisy) annotations.  
- Strength: method is well-motivated, simple, elegant, and modular—easy to adopt for other low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Results: impressive POS-tagging improvements on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages using small gold + large projected data.  
- Experimental suggestion: vary the amount of gold data and plot tagging accuracy vs. gold set size to better demonstrate utility.  
- Key empirical questions to answer with that analysis: (1) Does the method harm performance when gold annotations are abundant? (2) When does the benefit diminish? (3) Does the threshold vary by language?  
- Noise-source limitation: experiments only use cross-lingual projection with a shared tagset; should evaluate other noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and mismatched tag sets.  
- Objective-weighting concern: training equally weights gold and noisy losses; an ablation on different weightings (e.g., upweight gold) is needed.  
- Clarification needed: the projected-data representation \tilde{y} is underspecified (token-level vs. type-level distribution) and unclear for words never aligned in the parallel corpus.
