- Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger with an explicit noise layer that learns a transformation matrix to correct systematic errors in cross-lingual projected labels, trained jointly on clean and noisy data.  
- Leverages a small gold-standard annotated corpus plus a larger noisy corpus from cross-lingual projection.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on eight simulated low-resource languages and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Main contribution: a novel, well-motivated neural architecture that explicitly models noise from distant supervision via a dedicated transformation layer.  
- Strength: elegant and effective method—principled, interpretable way to exploit large unreliable data without overwhelming clean signals.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation across multiple simulated and real languages demonstrating generalizability.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance with consistent gains over baselines and prior SOTA (Duong et al., 2014a).  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical applicability when large parallel corpora may be as hard to obtain as annotations in extremely low-resource scenarios.  
- Weakness: related work section omits relevant recent work on annotation projection and cross-lingual methods (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agić et al. 2015).  
- Question for authors: will the code, processed datasets, and experimental configurations be publicly released to facilitate reproducibility?
