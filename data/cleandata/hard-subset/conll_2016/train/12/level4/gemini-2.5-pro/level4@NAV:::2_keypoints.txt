- Proposes augmenting RNN output layer with a noise-modeling fully connected layer multiplied with the softmax to handle gold and projected noisy annotations.  
- Method is conceptually neat, modular, and suitable for low-resource learning scenarios.  
- Demonstrates strong POS-tagging performance on eight simulated and two genuine low-resource languages using small gold plus larger projected corpora.  
- Experimental setup (building a tagger from only 1000 annotated tokens) is unconventional and may be uncommon in practice.  
- Recommends evaluating performance as a function of available gold data to show when the method helps, if it degrades with ample gold data, and whether thresholds vary by language.  
- Notes broader applicability to other noise sources (e.g., crowd-sourcing) and differing tag sets, but experiments are limited to cross-lingual projections sharing the same tag set.  
- Questions the decision to weight gold and noisy data equally in the training objective and requests an ablation tuning their relative contributions.  
- Points out ambiguity in the definition of projected labels (\tilde{y}) — unclear whether they are probability distributions, token-level or type-aggregated, and how unaligned words are handled.  
- Minor typographical error: on line 267 one closing bracket should be an opening bracket.
