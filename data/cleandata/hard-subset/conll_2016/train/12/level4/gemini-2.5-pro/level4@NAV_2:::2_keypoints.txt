- Method: joint BiLSTM POS tagger using a learned "noise layer" to map clean tag distributions to noisy projected tags for low-resource learning.  
- Contribution: a simple, end-to-end noise-layer approach to incorporate noisy distant supervision into neural sequence tagging.  
- Contribution: strong empirical validation — state-of-the-art POS results on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages.  
- Strength: elegant, simple, and modular method that is easy to implement and potentially adaptable to other NLP tasks and noisy label sources.  
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements over strong baselines and prior SOTA across ten languages.  
- Strength: useful analysis — visualization of the learned noise matrix shows meaningful, language-specific error patterns.  
- Weakness: evaluation only at a single extreme low-resource point (1000 tokens); lacks learning curves across varying amounts of gold data.  
- Weakness: experiments restricted to cross-lingual projection with identical tagsets; not tested on other noisy sources or mismatched tagsets.  
- Weakness: joint loss weights gold and noisy data equally without justification and no ablation over a weighting hyperparameter.  
- Question: did you experiment with a weighting hyperparameter to control the relative influence of gold vs. noisy loss terms?  
- Question: how is the projected POS vector ỹt constructed for unaligned or multiply-aligned tokens — token-level or aggregated type-level?  
- Question: how does the method’s benefit change as more gold-annotated data becomes available; when does the noise layer stop helping?
