- Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger augmented with a trainable "noise layer" that models systematic errors from cross-lingual projection.  
- Trains jointly on a small gold dataset and a much larger noisy projected dataset to learn cleaner internal predictions.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on eight simulated European languages and two true low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Introduces a simple, elegant noise-layer matrix to learn mappings from clean to noisy labels during end-to-end training.  
- Shows strong, comprehensive empirical gains over prior baselines (e.g., Duong et al. 2014a) and standard BiLSTMs.  
- Claims the noise layer is a generic, modular component applicable to other classifiers and noisy supervision sources.  
- Provides insightful visualizations of the learned noise matrix that reveal systematic tag confusions and language-dependent noise levels.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single low-resource setting (1k gold tokens) and lacks learning curves for more annotated data.  
- The joint loss equally weights gold and projected data without exploring a weighting hyperparameter, which may be suboptimal.  
- Mentions handling mismatched tagsets via rectangular transformation matrices but does not evaluate this capability.  
- Asks whether a weighting coefficient was tried to prioritize gold data during training.  
- Requests learning-curve experiments to show how benefits change with more gold annotations (e.g., 1k→5k→10k).  
- Seeks clarification on contradictory descriptions of alignment handling for multi-aligned or unaligned tokens.
