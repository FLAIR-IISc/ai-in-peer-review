- Proposal: augment the softmax output with an extra fully connected layer to model noise from projected (noisy) labels and learn from both gold and projected annotations.  
- Strength: well-motivated, simple, elegant method with strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two truly low-resource languages; modular and easily adoptable.  
- Experimental setup concern: training from as few as 1,000 annotated tokens is unusual; recommend plotting accuracy vs. gold annotation size to show when the method helps and whether it harms performance with large gold sets or varies by language.  
- Scope limitation: evaluation only uses cross-lingual projected labels with the same tag set; broader applicability (e.g., crowdsourced noisy labels or differing tag sets) is untested.  
- Training objective issue: equal weighting of gold and noisy losses is surprising; analysis of different relative weights is needed.  
- Projected-data clarity: definition of the projected label vector \tilde{y} is ambiguous (token vs. type distribution, how it’s constructed).  
- Alignment/coverage question: method uses only one-to-one alignments, so it’s unclear how \tilde{y} is formed for tokens that are never aligned.
