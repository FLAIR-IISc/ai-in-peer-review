- Proposes a novel, simple modification to RNN output layers that models noise via a fully connected layer producing a distribution over noisy labels alongside gold labels — well-motivated and promising.

- Demonstrated on POS tagging for eight simulated low-resource languages and two real low-resource languages using small gold sets plus cross-lingually projected annotations.

- Method is modular, making it potentially useful across various low-resource NLP problems.

- Experiments would be stronger with a more comprehensive empirical analysis varying the size of the gold annotation set.

- Recommend plotting tagging accuracy versus number of gold annotations to answer: does performance degrade with ample gold data, when does the method become beneficial, and does the threshold vary by language?

- Current evaluation is limited to noisy labels from cross-lingual projections with matching tag sets; robustness to other noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or differing tag sets is not shown.

- The training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally; a sensitivity analysis of different weighting schemes is needed to find optimal settings.

- Clarify the construction of the projected-data vector ỹ (e.g., distribution over projected POS tags vs. aggregated word-type predictions) and whether token-level projected tags would work.

- Explain handling of words without one-to-one alignments and how ỹ is formed for those cases.

- Minor typographical error: a closing bracket on line 267 should be an opening bracket.

- Overall assessment: valuable, clearly articulated contribution with promising results; addressing the above points would broaden impact.
