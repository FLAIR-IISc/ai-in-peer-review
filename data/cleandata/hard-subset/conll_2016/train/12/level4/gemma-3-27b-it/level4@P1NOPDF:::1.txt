I previously reviewed this paper when it was submitted as an ACL 2016 short paper. At that time, there was a flaw in the experimental setup, which has now been addressed. Having indicated then that I would be willing to accept the revised draft for another ACL venue if this issue were resolved, I now see no reason to change that assessment. 

Another reviewer rightly pointed out that the paper’s setup feels somewhat artificial when considered in the context of truly low-resource languages, specifically regarding the relative costs of finding versus paying annotators. I believe the authors should acknowledge this limitation in the write-up to avoid overstating the method’s applicability.

There is relevant work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL). Discussing these approaches in the related work section would improve the paper’s completeness.

In summary, I believe this is a valuable contribution and I vote to accept it. The authors should clarify whether the data used in the experiments will be made publicly available. I evaluate the reproducibility aspects of the work in good faith, assuming the data will be released for research purposes.
