- Proposes augmenting RNN output layer with a noise-modeling fully connected layer to combine gold and cross-lingual projected annotations for low-resource languages.  
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, modular, and shows strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.  
- Experimental setup is specialized; recommend plotting learning curves vs. gold annotation size to test robustness as gold data varies.  
- Investigate whether the method harms performance with abundant gold data, identify the gold-data threshold where it becomes beneficial, and check variability across target languages.  
- Current experiments only use cross-lingual projections; suggest evaluating other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tag sets.  
- Surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in training; recommend experiments tuning the relative weight of the two terms.  
- Clarify technical details about ỹ: whether it is a distribution over projected POS tags, token-level vs. type-level aggregation, and handling of unaligned words given one-to-one alignments.
