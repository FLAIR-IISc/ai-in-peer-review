1. Introduces a novel noise layer that explicitly models and corrects projection errors between projected and ground-truth POS tags, with informative transformation-matrix visualizations.  
2. Demonstrates strong empirical gains and state-of-the-art results (e.g., Malagasy 86.3%, Kinyarwanda 82.5%) across eight simulated and two real low-resource settings.  
3. Provides a comprehensive evaluation including ablation studies (BiLSTM variants) that highlight the noise layer’s contribution.  
4. Well-motivated approach addressing inherent noise in cross-lingual projection with a principled learning solution.  
5. Lacks sufficient discussion of practical annotation and parallel-data acquisition/cleaning costs, making the setup potentially artificial for real-world low-resource scenarios.  
6. Related-work coverage is incomplete; should cite and compare to more extremely low-resource and projection-specific studies (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).  
7. Model complexity and specific architectural choices (e.g., hidden dimensionality) are not thoroughly justified or analyzed.  
8. Request: provide detailed breakdown of effort to acquire and clean parallel data used for Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.  
9. Request: report how performance scales with annotated-data size and whether there are diminishing returns relative to parallel-corpus size.  
10. Request: deeper analysis of learned transformation matrices to identify common tag confusions and language-specific patterns.  
11. Please state whether datasets and code will be publicly released to facilitate reproducibility.  
12. Suggest discussing limitations such as dependence on high-quality word alignments and sensitivity to tagset mismatches.
