- Introduces an explicit “noise layer” to correct errors in cross-lingual projected tags via joint training on gold and projected data.
- Uses a bidirectional LSTM to generate gold and projected tags, with the noise layer modeling the transformation between them.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).
- Primary contribution: practical, effective method for leveraging noisy, distantly supervised cross-lingual projection data.
- Noise layer is conceptually simple, principled, and clearly formulated, making it easy to adapt to other tasks.
- Evaluation is thorough across diverse simulated and real-language scenarios, compared against strong baselines.
- Limited analysis of the learned noise transformation matrices; lacks deeper insight into specific error patterns corrected.
- Training objective uses equal weighting for gold and noisy losses; impact of varying these weights is unexplored.
- Experiments focus only on cross-lingual projection with aligned tagsets; generalization to other noisy label sources is not evaluated.
- Evaluation emphasizes very small gold data (1000 tokens); no learning curve showing behavior as gold data increases.
- Asks for a detailed analysis of the learned transformation matrix A to identify captured error patterns.
- Requests results from experiments with different weighting schemes between gold and noisy losses.
