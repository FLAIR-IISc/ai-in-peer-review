I previously reviewed this paper as an ACL 2016 short paper submission. At that time, the experiment setup contained a flaw, which has now been corrected. Having indicated a willingness to accept the draft at another ACL event contingent on this correction, I now see no remaining obstacles to acceptance. 

Another reviewer rightly pointed out that the paper’s setup may be somewhat artificial when considered in the context of truly low-resource languages, specifically regarding the trade-off between the cost of finding annotators versus paying them. This limitation should be acknowledged in the write-up to avoid overstating the method’s applicability.

Relevant work exists on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL). Including a discussion of these approaches within the related work section would improve the paper's completeness.

In summary, I believe this is a valuable contribution and vote to accept. The authors should clearly state whether the data used in the experiments will be made publicly available. I evaluate the paper in good faith, assuming that the data will be released for research purposes.
