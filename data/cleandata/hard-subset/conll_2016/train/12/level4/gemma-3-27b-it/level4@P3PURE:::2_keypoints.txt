- Proposed method: multiply traditional softmax by a learned fully-connected layer modeling noise to learn from gold and projected annotations in low-resource settings.
- Strengths: method is simple, elegant, modular, and yields strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Experimental concern: using as few as 1000 gold tokens is unusual; need accuracy-vs-gold-data plots to show when the method helps, whether it ever degrades performance with more gold, and language-dependent thresholds.
- Scope limitation: experiments only use cross-lingual projection with a matching tag set; other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or differing tag sets are not evaluated.
- Objective weighting: surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels—should report whether tuning their relative contributions improves performance.
- Data/notation clarity: \tilde{y} is not explicitly defined (token-level vs aggregated word-type distributions); unclear how \tilde{y} is constructed for tokens without alignments given one-to-one alignment pruning.
