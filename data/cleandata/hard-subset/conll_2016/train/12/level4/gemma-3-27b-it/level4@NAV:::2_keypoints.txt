- Proposes a novel RNN output-layer modification that models noise via an added fully connected layer to learn distributions over noisy and gold labels.  
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, and modular.  
- Shows strong POS-tagging results on eight simulated and two genuine low-resource languages using limited gold + cross-lingual projected annotations.  
- Evaluation targets a niche scenario (extremely limited annotations, e.g., 1000 tokens) that may not be broadly representative.  
- Lacks analysis of accuracy as a function of gold annotation size; recommends plotting performance vs gold-data amount for method and baselines to identify when the method helps and cross-language variation.  
- Limited to cross-lingual projections with identical tag sets; does not explore other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or mismatched tag sets.  
- Surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in training objective; recommends reporting results when tuning their relative contributions.  
- Clarity issue in description of projected data (line 357): unclear structure of \tilde{y}, whether it’s a token-level distribution or aggregated type prediction, and how missing alignments are handled; suggests evaluating token-level projected tags.  
- Minor typographical fix needed on line 267 (closing bracket should be an opening bracket).
