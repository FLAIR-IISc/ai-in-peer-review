- Proposes modifying RNN output layer: multiply softmax label distribution by a fully connected “noise” layer to model noisy (projected) annotations.  
- Strong, simple, and elegant method with modular design likely easy to adopt across low-resource NLP tasks.  
- Reports strong POS tagging results on eight simulated low-resource languages and two truly low-resource languages using small gold and larger projected datasets.  
- Experimental setup uses very small gold annotation size (≈1000 tokens), which is uncommon and raises practical concerns about realism.  
- Recommends additional experiments plotting tagging accuracy vs. varying gold dataset size to show when the method helps or harms and whether the beneficial threshold varies by language.  
- Paper only demonstrates cross-lingual projected labels with the same tag set, but noisy labels could also come from crowdsourcing or differing tag sets—wider applicability not evaluated.  
- Surprising that training objective weights gold and noisy labels equally; tuning their relative contributions was not explored and could improve performance.  
- Notation ambiguity on line 357: the structure of the projected-label vector ̃y (e.g., token-level vs. type-aggregated distribution) is not defined, and construction for unaligned words is unclear.  
- Minor typo on line 267: a closing bracket should be an opening bracket.
