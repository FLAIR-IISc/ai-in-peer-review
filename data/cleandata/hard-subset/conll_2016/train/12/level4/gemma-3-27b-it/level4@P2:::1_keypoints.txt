- Core contribution: Introduces a noise layer in a BiLSTM to explicitly model projection noise, jointly training on gold and projected data so the model learns when to trust projections.
- Empirical claim: Matches or exceeds state-of-the-art on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).
- Analysis: Provides evidence the model learns meaningful error patterns via the learned noise transformation matrix.

- Strength: Novel noise modeling that integrates noise handling into learning rather than pre-processing.
- Strength: Strong, consistent empirical results across simulated and real low-resource scenarios.
- Strength: Well-designed experiments and evaluation (simulated subsetting, universal tagset, mixed simulated+real evaluation).

- Weakness: Experimental setup may be artificial; simulated low-resource scenarios may not reflect practical costs/constraints of true low-resource settings.
- Weakness: Related work section is incomplete—missing discussion of extremely low-resource projection work (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016, Agić et al. 2015).
- Weakness: Unclear whether data and code will be released, harming reproducibility.

- Question: What is the computational cost of training the proposed model compared to simpler baselines?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to word alignment quality and have different alignment tools/parameters been tested?
- Question: Any surprising or language-specific patterns observed in the learned noise transformation matrices?

- Additional request: Please state data and code availability clearly.
- Additional request: Add a brief discussion of limitations, notably reliance on parallel data availability.
