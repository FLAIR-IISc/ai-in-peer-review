- Proposes modifying the RNN softmax by multiplying with a fully connected layer that models noise, yielding a second output distribution over noisy labels.  
- Method is simple, elegant, modular, and well-suited to learning from gold and cross-lingually projected annotations.  
- Shows promising POS-tagging results on eight simulated low-resource languages and two genuinely low-resource languages.  
- Modularity suggests easy applicability to other low-resource NLP problems.  
- Experimental setup is unusual (training with only ~1000 token gold annotations), which may not reflect typical use cases.  
- Recommends plotting tagging accuracy versus gold annotation size to assess when the method helps, whether it degrades with abundant gold data, and per-language thresholds.  
- Broader potential to incorporate other noisy label sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tag sets, but current experiments are limited to cross-lingual projections with matching tag sets.  
- Surprised that gold and noisy labels are weighted equally in the objective; tuning their relative weights might improve performance and should be reported.  
- Description of projected label vector \tilde{y} is unclear (is it a distribution over projected POS tags?), and performance implications of token-level vs type-level aggregation are unexplored.  
- Unclear how \tilde{y} is constructed for unaligned words given only one-to-one word alignments are preserved.  
- Minor typographical error: on line 267 a closing bracket should be an opening bracket.
