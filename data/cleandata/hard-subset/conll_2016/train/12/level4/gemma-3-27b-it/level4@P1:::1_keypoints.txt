- Proposes a novel noise layer to explicitly model and correct projection noise in cross-lingual sequence tagging.
- Integrates the noise model into a BiLSTM for joint training on gold and projected data.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, matching or exceeding state-of-the-art on simulated and real low-resource languages (Malagasy 86.3%, Kinyarwanda 82.5%).
- Novelty: learnable transformation for projection noise that lets the model adaptively weight projected labels, with visualized transformation matrices.
- Strong empirical performance across eight simulated settings and two real languages, outperforming prior baselines.
- Well-designed experiments using realistic low-resource setups, CoNLL simulations, Garrette & Baldridge datasets, and clear ablation studies.
- Clear, detailed model and training descriptions with helpful architecture diagrams.
- Potentially optimistic assumption of readily available parallel data; needs more discussion of practical difficulties and cost trade-offs.
- Related work section misses some recent projection methods (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016, Agic et al. 2015).
- Lacks thorough analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, hidden dimensionality).
- Does not state whether code and data will be publicly released, raising reproducibility concerns.
- Asks authors to elaborate on challenges and trade-offs of obtaining parallel data versus paid annotation.
- Requests analysis of model performance under varying projection noise/alignment quality.
- Asks whether authors will release code and data for reproducibility.
- Minor wording suggestions: rephrase informal sentences (e.g., on projection problems), replace "mimicking" with "modeling/representing," and tighten phrasing like "Provided there are..."
