- Proposed method: modify RNN output by multiplying softmax with a fully connected layer that models noise, enabling training from both gold and projected annotations.  
- Strong submission: method is simple, elegant, modular, and yields good POS-tagging results on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages.  
- Experimental setup concern: using only ~1000 token gold annotations is unusual; need plots of tagging accuracy vs gold data size to show when the method helps or harms and how benefits vary by language.  
- Limited noisy-source validation: experiments only use cross-lingual projections with the same tag set; should evaluate other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and differing tag sets.  
- Objective weighting issue: training objective equally weights gold and noisy labels; authors should test tuning these weights to see if performance improves.
