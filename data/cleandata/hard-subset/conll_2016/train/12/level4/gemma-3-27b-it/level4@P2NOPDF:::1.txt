This paper presents a valuable contribution, and I recommend it for acceptance to CONLL 2016. I previously reviewed an earlier draft of this work as a submission to ACL 2016, at which time I identified an issue with the experimental setup. This has now been addressed satisfactorily. Given that correction, and my previous conditional acceptance statement, I see no remaining impediments to publication.

One reviewer raised a valid point regarding the practical applicability of the experimental setup to truly low-resource languages. Specifically, the cost dynamics of annotator sourcing versus annotation costs may not fully reflect the challenges faced when working with such languages. The paper would benefit from acknowledging this limitation in the discussion, to avoid overstating the generalizability of the method. Acknowledging this nuance would strengthen the presentation and demonstrate a thoughtful understanding of the broader research landscape.

To further enhance the related work section, I suggest including discussion of annotation projection techniques specifically designed for extremely low-resource settings. Relevant work includes Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL). Integrating these references would provide a more complete and contextualized overview of the field.

Finally, please clearly state whether the datasets used in this work will be made publicly available. While I assess the work in good faith assuming openness, explicit confirmation of data availability is crucial for reproducibility and continued research.
