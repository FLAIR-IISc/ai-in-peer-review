I previously reviewed this paper as an ACL 2016 short paper submission. A flaw in the experimental setup identified at that time has now been addressed. Having indicated a willingness to accept the revised draft at another ACL venue contingent on this correction, I now see no remaining impediments to acceptance. 

One reviewer raised a valid point regarding the artificiality of the paper's setup when considered in the context of truly low-resource languages, specifically concerning the relative costs of annotation and annotator acquisition. The paper should acknowledge this limitation to avoid overstating the method’s applicability.

Relevant work on annotation projection for extremely low-resource languages, such as Johannsen et al. (2016, ACL) and Agic et al. (2015, ACL), should be discussed in the related work section for a more complete presentation.

In summary, I consider this a valuable contribution and recommend acceptance. Please clarify whether the data used in the experiments will be made publicly available. I offer this evaluation with the understanding that the research data will be released for reproducibility.
