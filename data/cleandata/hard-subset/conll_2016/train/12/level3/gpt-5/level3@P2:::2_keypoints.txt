- Proposal: Add a learnable confusion (noise) layer on top of a BiLSTM POS tagger to model cross-lingual projection noise and jointly train on small gold and large projected data.
- Validation: Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource languages and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), matching or improving prior work.
- Contribution 1: Introduces explicit noise modeling for sequence tagging via a lightweight confusion matrix and a two-stage (pretrain then joint) training recipe.
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates consistent empirical gains in low-resource settings, outperforming strong baselines and prior SOTA.
- Contribution 3: Offers interpretability and modularity of the noise layer, suggesting applicability to other noisy supervision regimes.
- Strength: Conceptually simple and minimally invasive; the confusion matrix is parameter-light, interpretable, and shows meaningful error patterns.
- Strength: Strong empirical results (e.g., average 91.7% on simulated languages; improved Malagasy and Kinyarwanda scores vs. prior work).
- Strength: Thorough comparisons against relevant baselines (MaxEnt, BiLSTM variants, prior SOTA) clarify the benefit of the noise layer.
- Strength: Experimental focus is practical for low-resource scenarios, using standard alignment and sentence selection practices.
- Weakness: Loss weighting fixed to equal weighting; lacks ablation on mixing coefficient or curriculum to handle varying projection quality.
- Weakness: Missing data-efficiency curves showing performance vs. amount of gold-labeled data.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in how projected token labels ỹ are constructed for many-to-one or unaligned cases; needs explicit specification.
- Weakness: Claims about handling tagset mismatches and generality to other noisy sources are not empirically tested.
- Weakness: Inconsistency between simulated (~1k gold tokens) and real experiments (~5k gold tokens) — needs reconciliation and discussion.
- Question: Was a tunable weight between gold and projected losses tried, and how sensitive are results to this hyperparameter?
- Question: Exactly how is the projected label distribution ỹ formed for multi-aligned or NULL tokens, and are alignment confidences or token filtering used?
- Question: Is the noise layer used only during training (not at test time), i.e., are predictions taken from the clean softmax?
- Question: What happens when real-language training is restricted to ~1k gold tokens — how do performance and A matrices change?
- Question: How is A initialized and did training from scratch without two-stage pretraining cause instability?
- Additional: Clarify ỹ format in Section 3.1/3.2, report statistical significance for key improvements, and fix minor typos/formatting.
