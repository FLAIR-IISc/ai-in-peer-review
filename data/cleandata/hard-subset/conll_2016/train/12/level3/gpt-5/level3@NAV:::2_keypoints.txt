Summary: Proposes a simple K×K “noise” layer atop an RNN tagger to model systematic errors in cross-lingual POS projection, trained jointly on tiny gold and larger projected corpora, yielding consistent gains on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages.

Main contributions:
- Modular, interpretable noise-modeling layer that calibrates projected labels during joint training for robust improvements.
- Empirical validation across 10 languages with competitive or SOTA performance in low-resource regimes.
- Practical training recipe (gold pretraining then joint training) to stably exploit noisy supervision.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results across many languages, showing reliability and generality.
- Simplicity and modularity: a small pluggable K×K layer usable for other noisy-supervision scenarios.
- Interpretability via learned confusion-matrix visualizations revealing projection noise patterns.
- Sound joint objective and end-to-end training with pretraining to avoid poor local optima.

Weaknesses:
- Missing data-efficiency analysis: no performance curves vs. amount of gold data to show when method helps or hurts.
- Fixed equal weighting of gold and noisy losses without ablation on mixing coefficient.
- Limited scope of noise sources: only same-universal-tagset cross-lingual projection evaluated.
- Ambiguity in projected label representation (token-level one-hot vs. distribution) and handling of unaligned tokens.
- Experimental realism concerns: "~1000 tokens" claim conflicts with >5k gold tokens used in real-language experiments.

Questions to authors:
- Report accuracy vs. gold data size (e.g., 200–2000 tokens) and identify regimes where the noise layer helps most.
- Did you tune a mixing coefficient between gold and projected losses, and how sensitive are results?
- Precisely define ỹ: token-level one-hot vs. soft distribution; how are NULL/multi-alignments encoded?
- Can you show results with mismatched tagsets (rectangular A) and/or other noisy sources (e.g., crowd labels)?
- What is sensitivity to parallel data size/quality and to initialization of the confusion matrix A?
