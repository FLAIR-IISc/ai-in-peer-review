- Summary: Proposes POS tagging for low-resource languages using a BiLSTM plus an explicit learnable noise layer to jointly learn from tiny gold data and noisy cross-lingual projections; pretrained on gold then jointly trained, showing consistent gains on 8 simulated settings and Malagasy/Kinyarwanda.

- Primary contribution: Introduces an explicit, learnable noise transformation (confusion matrix) for projection noise that enables effective joint training and yields state-of-the-art or competitive results across 10 settings.

- Secondary contribution: Provides an empirical study (ablations: gold-only, projected-only, naive union) and insightful analyses of learned confusion matrices clarifying when projected data helps.

- Tertiary contribution: Proposes a simple two-stage training strategy (gold pretraining followed by joint training) that stabilizes learning with noisy supervision.

- Strength: Clear, general idea with strong empirical validation—consistently improves over BiLSTM baselines and prior work (e.g., Duong et al. 2014), including high scores on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.

- Strength: Faithful analysis and interpretability—visualized noise matrices reveal systematic projection biases (e.g., determiner/number confusions) supporting the modeling choice.

- Strength: Practical low-resource setup—strong performance with ~1k gold tokens and results on two truly low-resource languages boost external validity.

- Strength: Experimental rigor—comparisons to multiple baselines and prior SOTA, and correction of an earlier experimental setup flaw.

- Weakness: External validity caveat—the simulation protocol ignores real-world costs of finding vs. paying annotators and should be acknowledged/discussed.

- Weakness: Related work coverage incomplete—needs explicit citation and positioning against projection/label-transfer work (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agić et al. 2015).

- Weakness: Data/code availability unclear—paper should state whether models, projection resources, and learned noise matrices will be released.

- Weakness: Claim about handling mismatched tagsets (rectangular A) is not empirically demonstrated; an empirical or synthetic test would strengthen the claim.

- Questions: Will you release code, trained models, projection scripts, and learned A matrices for all languages?

- Questions: How robust is the method to smaller/noisier parallel corpora and different alignment choices (e.g., many-to-one alignments)?

- Questions: Please position the approach versus Johannsen et al. (2016) and Agić et al. (2015)—what does the explicit noise layer newly enable?
