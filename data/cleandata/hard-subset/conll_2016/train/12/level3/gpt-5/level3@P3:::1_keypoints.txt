- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger that jointly trains on ~1k gold tokens and noisy projected tags using a learned confusion matrix noise layer to model projection errors, showing consistent gains on 10 languages.
- Primary contribution: Introduces an explicit, simple noise model (confusion matrix A) integrated into neural tagging for end-to-end learning from gold + projected data.
- Secondary contribution: Strong empirical results across 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, with interpretable analyses of learned noise matrices.
- Tertiary contribution: Practical training recipe (gold pretraining followed by joint training) that effectively leverages very small gold datasets with projected supervision.
- Strength: Principled modeling of projection noise yields consistent improvements over gold-only, naive mixing, and prior work (Duong et al. 2014a), notably on noisy Kinyarwanda data.
- Strength: Learned A matrices are interpretable and reveal sensible confusion patterns, providing transparency into projection errors.
- Strength: Realistic low-resource setup (~1k tokens + parallel data) and results on public Malagasy/Kinyarwanda datasets demonstrate practical viability.
- Strength: Experimental issues from an earlier draft were fixed; the revised setup and comparisons are sound.
- Strength: Method is lightweight, easy to implement, and likely transferable to other sequence tagging or distant supervision tasks.
- Weakness: External validity limited when parallel data are scarce or domain-mismatched; applicability to truly low-resource settings with little parallel data is unclear.
- Weakness: Related work coverage is incomplete—should cite/discuss recent projection literature (e.g., Agić et al. 2015; Johannsen et al. 2016) and position the method relative to multi-source/improved projection pipelines.
- Weakness: Limited ablation/sensitivity analyses—missing tests for gold-data size, parallel-data/alignment quality, training stages and A initialization/regularization, and statistical significance.
- Weakness: Unclear whether code, projected datasets/alignments, and learned A matrices will be released, affecting reproducibility.
- Question: Will you release code, projection pipelines, alignments, and learned A matrices (which parts will be public)?
- Question: How sensitive are results to gold supervision size (e.g., <1k tokens or scaling with more gold)?
- Question: Can the noise layer incorporate multi-source projection, and any results or expectations for that setup?
- Question: How is A initialized and regularized; were degenerate solutions observed without gold pretraining and were any constraints used?
- Question: Were results statistically tested, especially on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda?
- Additional comment: Add discussion of limitations when parallel data are limited, domain mismatch, and annotator-cost vs. parallel-data tradeoffs.
- Additional comment: Cite and discuss Agić et al. (2015) and Johannsen et al. (2016), clarifying complementarity or improvements.
- Additional comment: Justify discarding non-1–1 alignments and the heuristic for unaligned tokens; consider ablation including many-to-one alignments.
- Additional comment: The rectangular-A idea for mismatched tagsets is interesting—add a short experiment or clarifying remark on feasibility.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, pending related-work additions, caveat acknowledgment about parallel-data requirements, and a statement on data/code release.
