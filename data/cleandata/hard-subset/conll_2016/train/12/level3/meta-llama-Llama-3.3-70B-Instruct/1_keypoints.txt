- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model that uses a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram.  
- Model requires only a Wikipedia dump and is applicable to all languages in Wikipedia.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across many languages, including low-resource languages, with improvements over strong baselines.  
- Analyzes the quality of wikifier features and how their effectiveness depends on Wikipedia size.  
- Addresses the significant NLP problem of scarce annotated data for low-resource languages.  
- Language-independent approach increases applicability across Wikipedia languages.  
- Thorough analysis of wikifier feature quality provides insight into performance factors.  
- Empirical results show the model outperforms strong baselines on several languages, including low-resource ones.  
- Risk of being artificial for true low-resource languages; this limitation needs acknowledgement.  
- Related work section should better cover annotation projection approaches for extremely low-resource languages.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of model limitations and directions for future work.  
- Overall, a valuable contribution with potential for low-resource NER if limitations are not oversold.  
- Request for details on training of the cross-lingual wikifier and its ambiguity resolution.  
- Ask how authors will address limitations for real low-resource languages and dataset collection details.  
- Suggestion to consider or integrate other multilingual NER techniques like parallel projection or auto-generated Wikipedia training data.
