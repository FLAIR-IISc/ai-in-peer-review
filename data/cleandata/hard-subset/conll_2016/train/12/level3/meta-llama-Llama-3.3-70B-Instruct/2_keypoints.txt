- Proposes modifying the RNN output layer to train on both gold and projected annotations for low-resource languages.  
- Achieves strong POS tagging performance on simulated and truly low-resource languages.  
- Method is modular, increasing likelihood of reuse across NLP tasks.  
- Main contribution: language-independent NER using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-agnostic features.  
- Main contribution: a cross-lingual NER model designed to be applicable to all Wikipedia languages.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates effectiveness across a wide range of languages, including low-resource ones.  
- Strength: method is described as strong, elegant, and empirically effective.  
- Strength: thorough experimental setup with comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Strength: detailed analysis of wikifier feature quality and its dependence on Wikipedia size.  
- Weakness: experimental setup is unusual; should vary gold annotation size and plot tagging accuracy to better characterize benefits/limits.  
- Weakness: focus on cross-lingual projections with the same tag set limits impact; other noisy label sources and tag sets should be explored.  
- Weakness: equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in training objective is surprising; the contribution of each should be tuned or analyzed.  
- Weakness: unclear how projected-data vectors are represented or constructed, especially for words never aligned.  
- Questions for authors: how will they address the same-tag-set projection limitation?  
- Questions for authors: please provide more detail on the vector representation of projected data.  
- Questions for authors: how do they plan to explore other noisy label sources and different tag sets in future work?  
- Overall: well-written, novel, and effective; with revisions addressing weaknesses it could make a significant NLP contribution.
