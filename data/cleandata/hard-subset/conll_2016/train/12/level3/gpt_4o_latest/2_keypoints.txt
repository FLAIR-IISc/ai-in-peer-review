- Introduces a noise modeling layer on BiLSTM outputs to handle discrepancies between clean and projected annotations.
- Proposes a joint training framework that integrates gold-standard and projected data in a unified objective.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).
- Method is conceptually simple and minimally invasive yet effective.
- Achieves superior performance compared to prior methods on both simulated and real datasets.
- Approach is modular and adaptable to other NLP tasks and noisy label sources.
- Practical for low-resource settings by using a small gold-standard dataset (~1,000 tokens).
- Visualization of the noise transformation matrix provides insights into annotation noise and model behavior.
- Reliance on very small gold-standard datasets may not reflect scenarios with larger annotated corpora.
- Missing sensitivity analysis: no plot of tagging accuracy as a function of gold annotation size.
- Evaluation limited to cross-lingual projection; not tested on other noisy-label sources like crowdsourcing.
- Unclear how vector representations for unaligned words in projected data are constructed.
- Training objective equally weights gold and noisy labels without exploring alternative weightings.
- Asks authors to clarify handling and vector construction for unaligned projected words.
- Asks whether different weightings for gold vs. noisy labels were tried and what the outcomes were.
- Requests a plot showing tagging accuracy versus size of the gold-standard dataset.
- Notes a typographical error (bracket mismatch) on line 267.
- Recommends adding discussion of computational efficiency relative to baselines.
- Concludes the paper is a significant, empirically strong contribution to low-resource NLP and is a strong candidate for acceptance.
