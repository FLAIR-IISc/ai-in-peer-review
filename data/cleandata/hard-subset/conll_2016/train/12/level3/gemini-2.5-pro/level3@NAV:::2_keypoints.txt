- Introduces a noise layer for BiLSTM POS taggers that models transformation from model predictions to noisy cross-lingual projected labels, enabling joint training on clean and noisy data.  
- Main contribution: a simple, modular noise-layer architecture that learns a transformation matrix capturing systematic errors in distantly supervised data.  
- Empirical contribution: state-of-the-art results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages using ~1000 gold tokens.  
- Strength: methodological elegance and modularity — the noise layer is intuitive and easily integrated into other architectures/tasks.  
- Strength: consistently strong empirical gains across all ten languages, notably Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.  
- Strength: realistic low-resource setup (~1000 gold tokens) that matches practical NLP development conditions.  
- Weakness: no analysis of how performance varies with different amounts of gold-annotated data (accuracy vs. gold data size missing).  
- Weakness: unjustified equal weighting of gold and noisy loss terms; lacks ablation/tuning of their relative contributions.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope — only cross-lingual projection noise with a shared universal tagset, untested on other noisy sources or tagset mismatches.  
- Weakness: clarity issues about representation of projected labels for unaligned or multiply aligned words, hindering reproducibility.  
- Question: request analysis showing how performance changes as the amount of gold data varies and when noisy data stops helping.  
- Question: ask whether the authors tried a weighting hyperparameter to balance gold vs. projected loss and its effect.  
- Question: request clarification on how projected label distributions are constructed for non-one-to-one alignments.
