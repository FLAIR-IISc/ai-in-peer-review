- Proposed method: BiLSTM POS tagger augmented with a learnable linear "noise layer" that models confusion between true and projected noisy labels, enabling joint training on clean and noisy data.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple, effective noise layer that explicitly models supervision noise via a learnable confusion matrix.

- Contribution 2: Achieves state-of-the-art results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages, outperforming strong baselines.

- Strength: The noise-layer design is novel and elegant, providing interpretable learned confusion matrices (visualized in Figures 3–4).

- Strength: Thorough and convincing empirical evaluation across multiple languages and baselines, demonstrating consistent gains.

- Strength: Method is model-agnostic and likely generalizable to other noisy supervision scenarios (e.g., crowdsourcing or other distant supervision).

- Weakness: Experiments use a single small gold-data size (1,000 tokens); no analysis of how performance or benefit of the noise layer scales with more gold data.

- Weakness: Joint loss equally weights projected and gold losses without exploring a weighting hyperparameter, which may be suboptimal given differing data quality and quantity.

- Weakness: Scope of evaluation is narrow—only cross-lingual projection with identical tagsets is tested; mismatched tagsets and other noise sources are not demonstrated.

- Question 1: Have you tried introducing/tuning a weighting hyperparameter to control the relative contribution of noisy vs. gold data in the joint loss?

- Question 2: Can you provide results showing accuracy as a function of gold-data size (e.g., 1k, 5k, 10k) to identify regimes where the method helps most?

- Question 3: Please clarify how projected labels ỹ_t are represented (one-hot vs. distribution) and how the cross-entropy with log softmax(A*o_t) is computed when ỹ_t is a distribution.

- Additional comment: There appears to be a citation typo for Duong et al. (2014a)/(2014b) referring to the same paper.

- Additional comment: The two-stage pre-training (pre-train on gold) seems important; include an ablation showing performance when training jointly from scratch.
