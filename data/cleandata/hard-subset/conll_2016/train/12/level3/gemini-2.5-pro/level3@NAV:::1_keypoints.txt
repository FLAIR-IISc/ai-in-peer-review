- Proposal: BiLSTM POS tagger for low-resource settings using a small gold corpus plus larger noisy corpus from cross-lingual projection.
- Core idea: add a linear “noise layer” to explicitly model systematic errors between projected and true labels and jointly train on clean and noisy data to de-noise supervision.
- Results: achieves state-of-the-art on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Contribution 1: introduction of a simple, principled noise layer to model and compensate for distant-supervision noise.
- Contribution 2: strong empirical validation showing consistent improvements across many languages.
- Strength: method is novel, principled, and generalizable to other distant-supervision tasks.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments, including real-world languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda); previous experimental flaw corrected.
- Weakness: missing discussion/citation of related work (Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).
- Weakness: assumption that large parallel corpora are easier to obtain than small direct annotation may not hold for all low-resource languages—should be acknowledged.
- Question: please clarify plans to release code and data (especially Malagasy and Kinyarwanda) for reproducibility.
