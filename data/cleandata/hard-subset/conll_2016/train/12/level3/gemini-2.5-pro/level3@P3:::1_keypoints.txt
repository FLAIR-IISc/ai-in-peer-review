- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger for low-resource languages that jointly trains on small gold datasets and large projected noisy data using a trainable linear "noise layer"; evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real languages with state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution: Introduces a simple, trainable noise layer to model systematic errors between model predictions and noisy projected tags, enabling effective use of large noisy corpora.

- Contribution: Provides a strong, comprehensive empirical evaluation showing consistent improvements over strong baselines across ten languages, including real low-resource cases.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated method that cleanly integrates gold and projected supervision in a single end-to-end model without heavy feature engineering.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance, outperforming prior state-of-the-art (Duong et al., 2014a) on average across languages.

- Strength: Authors addressed a previously identified experimental flaw, and the corrected evaluation appears rigorous and sound.

- Strength: Insightful analysis—visualizations of the learned noise matrix reveal interpretable, language-specific error patterns (e.g., clean vs. diffuse projections).

- Weakness: Framing assumes plentiful parallel data, which may be unrealistic for truly low-resource languages and should be acknowledged.

- Weakness: Related work discussion is incomplete; should cite and discuss recent relevant work (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).

- Question/Request: Reviewer requests confirmation that code, processed parallel corpora, word alignments, and final data splits (especially for Malagasy and Kinyarwanda) will be publicly released for reproducibility.
