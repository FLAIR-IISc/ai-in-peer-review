- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger for low-resource languages that jointly trains on a small clean corpus and large projected noisy data using a learnable "noise layer," showing SOTA on 8 simulated and 2 real languages.

- Contribution 1: Introduces an explicit trainable noise layer (confusion matrix) to model and de-noise cross-lingual projection errors within an end-to-end neural sequence model.

- Contribution 2: Provides strong empirical results, outperforming baselines and prior work (Duong et al., 2014a) and achieving new SOTA on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.

- Strength 1: Elegant, well-motivated model that isolates projection noise; learned noise matrices offer interpretable, language-specific error patterns.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive, rigorous evaluation across simulated and real low-resource settings with appropriate baselines and ablations.

- Strength 3: Clear, well-written paper with helpful figures and responsiveness to reviewer feedback, leading to confidence in results and publishability.

- Weakness 1: Lacks discussion of some relevant related work on annotation projection and learning from noisy data (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agić et al. 2015).

- Weakness 2: Practicality concern: assumption that large parallel corpora are easier to obtain than small annotated sets may not hold in all real-world cases and should be acknowledged.

- Question / Request: Will the processed parallel/projection data and model code (especially for Malagasy and Kinyarwanda) be publicly released to ensure reproducibility?
