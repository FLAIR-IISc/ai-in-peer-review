- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger with a learnable "noise layer" to de-noise cross-lingual projected labels by jointly training on small gold data and large noisy data; evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages with strong results.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple, interpretable noise layer (K×K linear transform) that models confusion between true and projected tags and is learned jointly with the tagger.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates state-of-the-art or superior POS tagging performance in multiple simulated and real low-resource settings.

- Strength 1: Novel, elegant, and parameter-efficient approach that integrates noise modeling directly into the model rather than relying on heuristics.

- Strength 2: Thorough and convincing empirical evaluation showing consistent improvements over strong baselines and prior work.

- Strength 3: Method is modular and potentially generalizable to other sequence-labeling tasks and noise sources (e.g., crowdsourcing, other distant supervision).

- Weakness 1: No analysis of how performance or the benefit of the noise layer varies with different amounts of gold data (no learning curve across gold-data sizes).

- Weakness 2: Joint objective equally weights gold and projected losses (after normalization) with no ablation or tuning of a weighting hyperparameter.

- Weakness 3: Limited empirical validation of claimed generality—experiments are confined to cross-lingual projection with identical tagsets and do not test other noise types or mismatched tagsets.

- Question 1: Request for analysis showing how performance changes as annotated gold data varies (e.g., 100–5000 tokens) and when projected data stops helping.

- Question 2: Ask whether a weighting hyperparameter between gold and projected loss was tried and how it affects performance.

- Question 3: Clarification requested on the representation of projected labels ỹ (are they soft distributions for unaligned/multi-aligned tokens?) and how cross-entropy is computed with soft targets.
