- Summary: Proposes a BiLSTM sequence-tagging model with an explicit learned noise layer that jointly trains on gold annotations and noisy cross-lingual projections; evaluated on 8 simulated and 2 real low-resource languages, achieving SOTA.

- Contribution — Noise modeling: Introduces an explicit noise layer that learns to correct errors from cross-lingual projection.

- Contribution — Deep neural model: Proposes a modular BiLSTM model that jointly learns from gold and noisy projected data and can generalize to other tasks.

- Contribution — Empirical results: Reports state-of-the-art POS tagging performance on the evaluated languages.

- Strength — Simple/elegant modification: Models noise by multiplying softmax outputs with a learned noise FC layer, easy to implement.

- Strength — Strong empirical results: Demonstrated effectiveness across multiple languages and settings.

- Strength — Modular design: Model architecture is generalizable to other low-resource NLP tasks.

- Weakness — Unrealistic setup: Training with only ~1,000 token annotations may be unrealistic; needs experiments varying gold-data size.

- Weakness — Lack of analysis: No performance vs. gold-data-size analysis to show trade-offs between gold and noisy data.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Only evaluates projection with the same tagset; applicability to other noisy sources and differing tagsets is unexplored.

- Question: How are projected labels represented (token-level vs. aggregated type-level), and how are unaligned words handled?

- Question: How is the relative contribution of the two objective terms decided and has it been tuned?

- Question: Can the authors provide analysis of method performance as the amount of gold data increases?

- Question: Has the approach been tested on other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or on differing tagsets?
