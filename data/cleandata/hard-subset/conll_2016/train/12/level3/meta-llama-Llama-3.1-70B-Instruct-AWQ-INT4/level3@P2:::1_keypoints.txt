- Summary: Proposes a deep neural model with an explicit noise layer to correct cross-lingual projection errors, jointly trained on gold and projected annotations, achieving SOTA on simulated settings and Malagasy/Kinyarwanda.
- Contribution: Introduces a novel noise-modeling neural approach for sequence tagging in low-resource languages.
- Contribution: Demonstrates joint end-to-end training on gold and projected annotations to improve POS tagging accuracy.
- Contribution: Matches or exceeds prior state-of-the-art across eight simulated settings and two real low-resource languages.
- Strength: Effectively utilizes noisy projected annotations to boost tagging performance.
- Strength: Empirical results show strong performance across multiple languages and settings.
- Strength: Novel and explicit modeling of projection noise is a key technical contribution.
- Weakness: Experimental setup may be artificial by assuming a small set of gold annotations in low-resource scenarios.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of related work, missing recent relevant papers (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).
- Weakness: Unclear whether the data used in experiments will be made publicly available.
- Question: How will the authors make the data used in the paper available to the public?
- Question: How do the authors plan to address the limitation of assuming available gold-standard subsets in low-resource settings?
- Question: Can the authors expand the related-work discussion to include recent annotation-projection literature (Johannsen et al., Agic et al.)?
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but could discuss limitations and future work more thoroughly.
