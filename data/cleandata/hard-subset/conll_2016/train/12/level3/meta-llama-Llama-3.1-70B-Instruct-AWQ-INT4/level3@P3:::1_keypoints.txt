- Proposes a noise layer for sequence tagging to correct cross-lingual projection errors and jointly train on gold and projected annotations end-to-end.
- Contributions: models noise in projected data, integrates it into a deep neural network for joint training, evaluates on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.
- Novelty: explicitly models tagset transformation within the network rather than using heuristics for cleaning projected tags.
- Effectiveness: method leverages noisy projected data to improve POS tagging accuracy with limited gold annotations.
- Empirical strength: achieves state-of-the-art results on the evaluated languages.
- Limitation: assumes availability of parallel data, which may not exist for many low-resource languages.
- Limitation: the proposed noise layer may not capture all types of projection noise.
- Limitation: paper does not discuss computational requirements, which could be problematic for low-resource settings.
- Suggestion: discuss implications for other projection-based NLP tasks (e.g., machine translation, sentiment analysis).
- Suggestion: explore alternative noise models (e.g., Gaussian, Laplace) to improve robustness.
- Suggestion: provide more details on hyperparameters and their tuning.
- Question: how were hyperparameters tuned for the experiments?
- Question: how will the approach be extended to other NLP tasks that rely on cross-lingual projection?
- Question: have other noise models been considered or tested to improve robustness?
- Reproducibility: authors should consider releasing code and data to facilitate replication and further research.
