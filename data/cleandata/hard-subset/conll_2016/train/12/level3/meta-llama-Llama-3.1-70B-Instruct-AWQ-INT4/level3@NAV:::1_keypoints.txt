Summary: Proposes a joint learning method with an explicit noise layer to correct cross-lingual projection errors for sequence tagging, evaluated on 8 simulated settings and Malagasy/Kinyarwanda, matching or exceeding SOTA.

Noise Layer: Introduces a noise layer that models the transformation between projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags to learn when/how much to trust projections.

Joint Learning: Trains jointly on gold-annotated and projected corpora so the model can learn from both reliable and noisy supervision.

Evaluation on Low-Resource Languages: Validated on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda (plus simulations), showing better performance than competing methods.

Effective Use of Noisy Projected Data: The method effectively leverages noisy projected data to improve POS tagging beyond direct-use baselines.

Robustness to Noise: Demonstrates robustness to projection noise, maintaining strong performance even with low-quality projected data.

Generalizability: Method appears generalizable to other low-resource languages based on empirical results.

Assumes Availability of Parallel Data: The approach requires parallel data for projection, which may not exist for many low-resource languages.

Requires Annotated Data: Relies on some annotated (gold) data, which can be expensive or unavailable for low-resource settings.

May Not Perform Well on Highly Noisy Data: Performance can suffer with very noisy projections, as observed for Kinyarwanda.

Question — Parallel data: How do you plan to handle languages without parallel data for projection?

Question — Noise layer learning: Please provide more detail on how the noise layer is learned and applied to improve POS tagging.
