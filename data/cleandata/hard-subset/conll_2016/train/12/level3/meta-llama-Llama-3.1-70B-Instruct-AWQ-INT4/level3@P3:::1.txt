**Summary and Contributions**

This paper presents a novel approach to sequence tagging that learns to correct errors from cross-lingual projection using an explicit noise layer. The authors propose a deep neural network model that jointly trains on gold standard and projected annotations, and can be trained end-to-end with backpropagation. The main contributions of this work are: (1) the study of noise in projected data in word alignments and the description of it with an additional layer model, (2) the integration of the model into a deep neural network and joint training on both annotated and projected data, and (3) the evaluation on eight simulated and two real-world low-resource languages.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to handling noise in cross-lingual projection**: The authors propose a new model that explicitly models the transformation between tagsets as part of a more expressive deep learning neural network. This approach is a significant improvement over previous methods that rely on heuristics or constraints to clean the projected tags.
2. **Effective use of noisy projected data**: The authors demonstrate that their approach can effectively utilize noisy projected data to improve POS tagging accuracy, even with a small amount of gold standard annotations.
3. **Strong empirical results**: The authors evaluate their approach on eight simulated and two real-world low-resource languages and achieve state-of-the-art results, outperforming previous methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumes availability of parallel data**: The authors assume that parallel data is available for low-resource languages, which may not always be the case.
2. **Noise layer may not capture all types of noise**: The authors acknowledge that the noise layer may not capture all types of noise in the projected data, and that further research is needed to improve the model.
3. **Computational requirements**: The authors do not discuss the computational requirements of their approach, which may be a concern for low-resource languages with limited computational resources.

**Additional Comments**

* The authors should consider discussing the implications of their approach for other NLP tasks that rely on cross-lingual projection, such as machine translation and sentiment analysis.
* The authors should also consider exploring the use of other types of noise models, such as Gaussian noise or Laplace noise, to improve the robustness of their approach.
* The authors should provide more details on the hyperparameters used in their experiments and how they were tuned.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how you tuned the hyperparameters for your experiments?
2. How do you plan to extend your approach to other NLP tasks that rely on cross-lingual projection?
3. Have you considered exploring the use of other types of noise models to improve the robustness of your approach?
