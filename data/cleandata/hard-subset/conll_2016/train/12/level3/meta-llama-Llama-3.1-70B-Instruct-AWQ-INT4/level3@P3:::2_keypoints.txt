- Proposes a novel sequence-tagging method for low-resource languages using both gold annotations and noisy cross-lingual projected annotations.
- Introduces a neural model with an explicit noise layer to learn and correct errors from cross-lingual projection, trained jointly on gold and projected data.
- Uses an LSTM-based deep learning architecture integrated into a joint training framework.
- Evaluated on eight simulated low-resource languages and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), achieving state-of-the-art POS tagging results.
- Method is modular and likely transferable to other low-resource NLP tasks.
- Experimental setup is unusual but provides a comprehensive evaluation across varied settings.
- Strong empirical results demonstrate effectiveness, especially on simulated languages and the two real languages.
- Concern: training with as little as ~1000 token annotations may be unrealistic for many real-world settings.
- Missing analysis of performance as a function of gold-data amount; unclear when the method helps or harms.
- Evaluation limited to cross-lingual projection with the same tagset; applicability to other noisy sources or differing tagsets not explored.
- Surprising choice to weight gold and noisy labels equally in the objective; tuning relative contributions was not investigated.
- Representation of projected labels and handling of words never aligned are not clearly explained.
- Minor editorial issues noted (unclear text at line 357, bracket typo at line 267).
