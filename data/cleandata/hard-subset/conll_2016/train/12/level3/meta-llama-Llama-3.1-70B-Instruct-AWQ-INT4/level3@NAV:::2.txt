**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to sequence tagging that learns to correct the errors from cross-lingual projection using an explicit noise layer. The authors introduce a new neural network model for sequence tagging in a low-resource language, suitable for training with both a tiny gold standard annotated corpus and distant supervision using cross-lingual tag projection. The model uses a bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM) and a noise transformation layer to encode the mapping between the projected high-resource tags and low-resource tags. The authors evaluate their approach on eight simulated low-resource languages and two real low-resource languages, Malagasy and Kinyarwanda, and achieve state-of-the-art results.

**Contributions of the Work**

The primary contribution of this work is the introduction of a novel approach to sequence tagging that learns to correct the errors from cross-lingual projection using an explicit noise layer. The authors also make significant contributions in the following areas:

1. **Modular and generalizable approach**: The proposed method is modular and can be easily integrated with other deep learning models, making it a useful contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors demonstrate strong empirical results on POS tagging for eight simulated low-resource languages and two real low-resource languages, Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.
3. **Robustness to noise**: The proposed method is robust to noise in the projected data and can learn to correct errors in the projected tags.

**Strengths of the Submission**

The strengths of this submission are:

1. **Novel approach**: The proposed approach is novel and addresses a significant problem in low-resource languages, where annotated data is scarce.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors demonstrate strong empirical results on a range of languages, including simulated and real low-resource languages.
3. **Modular and generalizable approach**: The proposed method is modular and can be easily integrated with other deep learning models, making it a useful contribution to the field of natural language processing.
4. **Robustness to noise**: The proposed method is robust to noise in the projected data and can learn to correct errors in the projected tags.
5. **Well-written and clear**: The paper is well-written and clear, making it easy to understand the proposed approach and its contributions.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

The weaknesses of this submission are:

1. **Limited analysis of performance vs. amount of gold data**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of how the performance of the proposed method changes with the amount of gold data.
2. **Unrealistic experimental setup**: The experimental setup, which involves training with as little as 1000 token annotations, may be unrealistic for many settings.
3. **Lack of exploration of other noisy sources**: The authors only evaluate cross-lingual projection with the same tagset and do not explore other noisy sources, such as crowdsourcing.
4. **Surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels**: The authors use equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in the objective, which may not be optimal.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the performance of the proposed method change with the amount of gold data?
2. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup and how it relates to real-world scenarios?
3. Have you explored other noisy sources, such as crowdsourcing, and if so, what were the results?
4. Can you provide more insights into the surprising equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in the objective?
