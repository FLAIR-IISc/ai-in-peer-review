- Proposes a sequence-tagging model that explicitly models noise from cross-lingual projection via an output-noising layer in a BiLSTM, jointly training on gold and projected corpora.
- Gold tags are predicted directly from the BiLSTM hidden state; projected tags are produced after a learned linear transformation that encodes tagset mapping and trust in projected data.

Contributions:
- Introduces a novel noise-modeling approach that embeds tagset transformation into a deep neural network.
- Demonstrates effective correction of projection errors using an explicit noise layer to improve POS tagging.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages.

Strengths:
- Simple, elegant modification to the RNN output layer that is easy to implement.
- Strong empirical performance across multiple low-resource languages.
- Modular design that can be adapted to other noisy-annotation NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
- Experimental setup with ~1000 token annotations may be unrealistic for many real-world scenarios.
- Missing analysis of how performance scales with varying amounts of gold data.
- Evaluation limited to projection with the same tagset; applicability to other noisy sources or differing tagsets is unexplored.

Questions for authors:
- How does performance vary with different amounts of gold data?
- How are words handled that are never aligned in the parallel data?
- Is the projected label representation \tilde{y} token-level or aggregated type-level distribution?
- Have you tested the method on other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) or differing tagsets?
- Can you tune the relative weighting of the two objective terms, and what are the effects?

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Minor editorial issues: bracket typo on line 267 and unclear wording about \tilde{y} on line 357.
