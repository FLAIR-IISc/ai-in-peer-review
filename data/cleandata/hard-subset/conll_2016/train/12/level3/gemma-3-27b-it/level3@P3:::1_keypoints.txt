- Proposes a BiLSTM POS tagger with a learned “noise layer” to model discrepancies between cross-lingual projected tags and gold tags.  
- Jointly trains on limited gold data and abundant noisy projected data to discount unreliable projections.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Main contribution: a novel, noise-aware neural architecture that explicitly models transformation between projected and gold tags.  
- Empirical contribution: consistent, significant gains over strong baselines including prior state-of-the-art.  
- Practical contribution: a usable methodology for leveraging cross-lingual projection with limited annotated data.  
- Strength: tackles a central low-resource NLP problem—leveraging noisy distant supervision effectively.  
- Strength: technically sound, simple and well-integrated noise layer with clear mathematical formulation.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation with comparisons to strong baselines and both simulated and real languages.  
- Strength: experimental setup and parameters are clearly described, aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength: authors identified and corrected an experimental flaw from a prior draft.  
- Weakness: experimental setup may be artificial; cost trade-offs between parallel data and annotation need clearer discussion.  
- Weakness: initially omitted relevant related work on annotation projection (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016, Agic et al. 2015); needs deeper engagement.  
- Weakness: analysis of the learned noise/transformation matrices is limited—more insight into common tag confusions and language-specific patterns is needed.  
- Questions: please elaborate on generalization when parallel data is costlier than annotation, and compare explicitly to Johannsen et al. (2016) and Agic et al. (2015).  
- Question: will trained models and annotated data be released for reproducibility?  
- Additional notes: writing is clear, the universal tagset choice is appropriate, and experimental details are thorough.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommended for acceptance.
