- Proposes augmenting an RNN with a learned differentiable “noise layer” to model cross-lingual projection errors and jointly train on gold and projected data.  
- Approach is well-motivated, reasonably implemented, and avoids heuristic cleaning by learning to handle noise.  
- Empirical results are competitive on both simulated and real low-resource languages.  
- Method is modular and potentially applicable to other low-resource sequence-tagging tasks.  
- Main contribution: direct integration of a noise model into the sequence tagging architecture rather than heuristic data cleaning.  
- Secondary contribution: thorough empirical validation across diverse languages, focusing on a truly low-resource setting (~1000 tokens).  
- Major weakness: no analysis of performance as a function of gold-data size; need accuracy vs. gold-annotation plot to show when the method helps or harms.  
- Limited evaluation: only tested on cross-lingual projection with identical tagsets; applicability to other noisy sources (e.g., crowdsourcing) and different tagsets is unexplored.  
- Objective weighting concern: gold and noisy labels are equally weighted without ablation/tuning; this choice seems arbitrary and needs study.  
- Clarification needed on representation of projected labels (token-level vs. aggregated type-level distribution).  
- Clarification needed on how words that are never aligned during projection are handled.  
- Minor editorial issues noted (specific line references: 357 and 267).  
- Questions for authors: provide accuracy vs. gold-size plot; evaluate on other noisy data sources; report experiments varying gold/noisy weighting; clarify projected-label representation and unaligned-word handling.
