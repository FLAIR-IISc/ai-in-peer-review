- Introduces a noise layer in a BiLSTM tagger to model and discount noisy cross-lingual projected POS tags.  
- Evaluated on eight simulated and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), often surpassing state-of-the-art.  
- Primary contribution: simple, effective noise transformation matrix that enables joint training on gold and projected data.  
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation showing robustness across diverse languages.  
- Results on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda are particularly strong and noteworthy.  
- Clear, well-motivated presentation with helpful diagrams explaining the model.  
- Practical relevance: useful for low-resource NLP and works with very small gold data (≈1,000 tokens).  
- Weakness: training with only ~1,000 tokens may be unrealistic and not representative of many real scenarios.  
- Weakness: no analysis of performance versus amount of gold data (no accuracy vs. gold-size plot).  
- Weakness: method only tested with identical tagsets; tagset mismatch scenarios are unexplored.  
- Weakness: equal weighting of gold and noisy labels is unexamined; sensitivity to weighting is missing.  
- Weakness: unclear representation of projected labels (token-level vs. type-level) and handling of unaligned words.  
- Question: how does performance vary as gold annotation size increases (request for a plot)?  
- Question: please clarify the representation of projected labels and treatment of unaligned words.  
- Question: did you try different weightings for gold vs. noisy labels, and what were the results?  
- Question: what is the computational cost of training with the noise layer compared to a standard BiLSTM tagger?
