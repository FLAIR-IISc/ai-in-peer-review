- Introduces a neural sequence-tagging model with an explicit learnable “noise layer” to correct errors in cross-lingual projected annotations, enabling joint learning from gold and projected data.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy 86.3%, Kinyarwanda 82.5%), matching or exceeding state-of-the-art.
- Provides comprehensive evaluation including baselines, ablation studies, and analysis/visualization of the learned noise transformation matrix that reveals tag confusion patterns.
- Clearly motivates the low-resource POS tagging problem and presents technical details in a concise, understandable way.
- Weakness: simulated low-resource setup (first 1000 CoNLL tokens) may be artificial and not fully reflect real low-resource costs and conditions.
- Weakness: related work discussion is limited; should engage more with recent annotation projection studies (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).
- Weakness: paper does not state whether processed data or trained models will be publicly released.
- Question: what is the computational cost of training the model with the noise layer versus a standard BiLSTM?
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the quality of word alignments, and have different alignment tools been evaluated?
- Question: please provide more detailed qualitative analysis of the A matrix, highlighting specific tag confusions the model corrects.
- Additional: writing is clear and concise.
- Additional: experimental setup and results are well-defined and clearly presented.
- Additional: authors should discuss limitations like dependence on parallel data and potential projection-induced bias.
- Overall recommendation: well-executed, impactful work — recommend acceptance.
