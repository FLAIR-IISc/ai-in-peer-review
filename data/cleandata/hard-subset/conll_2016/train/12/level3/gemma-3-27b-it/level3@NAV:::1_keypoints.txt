- Novel approach: uses cross-lingual projection and a neural “noise layer” to learn the transformation between projected and gold POS tags.
- Noise-layer idea is well-motivated and effectively implemented.
- Strong experimental results across eight simulated low-resource settings and two real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda), matching or exceeding state-of-the-art.
- Primary contribution: integrates noise modeling into deep learning to learn how to utilize noisy projected annotations rather than just cleaning them.
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation across diverse languages and strong baselines, supporting robustness and generalizability.
- Qualitative analysis of learned noise transformation matrices (Figures 3 & 4) provides useful insight into model behavior and projection errors.
- Strengths include correction of a prior experimental flaw, consistent performance on simulated and real data, and practical motivation for leveraging parallel corpora.
- Caveat: setup may be artificial for some low-resource languages since aligned parallel data can be hard or costly to obtain relative to annotation.
- Related work: the related work section should be expanded to include annotation-projection literature (e.g., Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015).
- Reproducibility: authors should explicitly state whether data and code will be made publicly available.
- Question for authors 1: clarify computational cost of training, particularly learning the noise transformation matrix A as tagset size grows.
- Question for authors 2: provide more examples of specific linguistic phenomena captured by the learned transformation beyond those in the paper.
