- Introduces a neural POS-tagger that corrects cross-lingual projection errors via an explicit noise layer, with joint learning of projected→gold tag transformations.
- Contribution: joint noise-layer framework, strong evaluation across simulated and real low-resource languages, and ability to use noisy projected data without ad-hoc cleaning.
- Strength: principled noise modeling (K×K matrix A) that learns trust in projected tags, evidenced by clear diagonal/off-diagonal patterns.
- Strength: strong, consistent empirical gains—state-of-the-art on 8 simulated languages and substantial improvements on Malagasy and Kinyarwanda.
- Strength: simple, efficient modification to BiLSTM with minimal extra parameters, yielding large performance gains.
- Strength: insightful visualizations of noise matrices revealing language-specific error patterns (e.g., PRT/X in Swedish, NUM/DET in Italian).
- Weakness: evaluation assumes readily available parallel data, not accounting for real-world costs of obtaining such data versus annotation.
- Weakness: related work misses key prior contributions (Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015) that should be cited and discussed.
- Weakness: paper does not state whether code and datasets will be publicly released, limiting reproducibility.
- Question: clarify practical availability and cost comparison of parallel data vs. annotating ~1,000 tokens for real low-resource languages.
- Question: will datasets and code be released for reproducibility?
- Question: provide details on initialization of the noise matrix A and the exact pretraining procedure mentioned.
