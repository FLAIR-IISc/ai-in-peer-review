- Summary: Proposes learning a noise layer to correct cross-lingual projected POS tags via joint training on gold and projected corpora.

- Contribution 1: Explicit modeling of the noise process affecting cross-lingual projected annotations.

- Contribution 2: Joint learning framework that integrates gold and projected data through a noise layer in a deep neural network.

- Contribution 3: Comprehensive evaluation showing state-of-the-art performance on simulated low-resource settings and real languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).

- Strength 1: Learnable transformation matrix is well-motivated, effectively implemented, and outperforms naive projected-data baselines and prior SOTA.

- Strength 2: Compelling real-language results (86.3% Malagasy, 82.5% Kinyarwanda) with useful analysis of learned noise patterns.

- Strength 3: Practical utility demonstrated by needing only 1,000 gold tokens plus parallel data for training.

- Weakness 1: Experimental setup is somewhat artificial and does not fully acknowledge practical differences between finding parallel data and paying for gold annotations.

- Weakness 2: Important related work (Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015) is omitted and should be discussed.

- Weakness 3: Unclear whether datasets and code will be publicly released; this should be specified.

- Question 1: How does noise-matrix learning handle varying quality of parallel data in real-world deployment?

- Question 2: How would performance change if projected tags come from a source language other than English?

- Question 3: Will the datasets and code be made publicly available, and if so, when?
