- Contribution: Proposes a neural POS tagger for low-resource languages that jointly learns from small gold annotations and noisy projected tags via a noise layer that models transformation/trust in projected data, showing SOTA results on eight simulated and two real languages.

- Strength 1: Noise layer offers a principled way to incorporate noisy distant supervision, addressing prior methods that treated projected tags as reliable.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive experiments demonstrate consistent improvements over baselines across multiple languages and settings.

- Strength 3: Model architecture is elegant and interpretable, with the noise transformation matrix providing insights into projection errors.

- Weakness 1: Experimental setup is somewhat artificial for real low-resource scenarios, especially concerning the cost and availability of annotators.

- Weakness 2: Related work misses key citations (Johannsen et al. 2016; Agic et al. 2015) and should be updated to properly position the contribution.

- Weakness 3: Paper does not state whether data will be made publicly available, impacting reproducibility.

- Revision note: Authors corrected the prior experimental setup flaw.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to acknowledge experimental artificiality, add missing citations, and state data availability.
