- Proposes a noise transformation layer that models errors in cross-lingual projected annotations via a simple RNN output-layer modification.  
- Enables joint training on gold-standard and projected annotations to correct projection errors.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements across eight simulated and two real low-resource languages (Malagasy, Kinyarwanda).  
- Method is elegant, simple, and requires minimal changes to standard sequence-tagging architectures.  
- Empirical results are robust and achieve state-of-the-art performance in the evaluated settings.  
- The approach is modular and potentially applicable to other low-resource NLP tasks beyond POS tagging.  
- Experimental setup uses only 1,000 gold tokens, which is unusual and may be unrealistic for many practical scenarios.  
- Lacks analysis of how performance varies with different amounts of gold data to identify regimes where the method helps or harms.  
- Equal weighting of gold and noisy labels in the loss is arbitrary; no ablation or justification for this hyperparameter is provided.  
- Evaluation only considers cross-lingual projection with identical tagsets; applicability to other noisy sources or differing tagsets is unexplored.  
- Clarification needed on projected label representation (token-level vs. type-level) and handling of never-aligned words.  
- Contains minor editorial issues (unclear notation at line 357; bracket typo at line 267) and overall weaknesses that should be addressed before acceptance.
