

This paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks (Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, Recognizing Textual Entailment, etc.) by introducing a new software implementation (dataset-sts) and addressing dataset limitations through new benchmarks (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop). The main contributions are: (1) a modular framework enabling consistent evaluation across diverse tasks; (2) new datasets that overcome critical limitations in existing benchmarks (small size, uneven candidate sets, poor validation/test splits); and (3) evidence that transfer learning across tasks can improve performance, suggesting a path toward more generalizable models.

Strengths: The unified framework with dataset-sts software is a significant contribution that will greatly improve reproducibility and comparability in this field. The new datasets directly address well-documented problems with existing benchmarks (e.g., Wang's Answer Sentence Selection dataset), and the careful evaluation methodology with 95% confidence intervals (addressing high variance in neural model results) represents a methodological advance. The paper demonstrates that transfer learning across tasks yields consistent improvements, providing a promising direction for future work.

Weaknesses: The claim of "universal semantic comprehension" is overstated—the models still lag behind state-of-the-art on most tasks, and transfer learning gains are modest (e.g., Ubu.RNN outperforms baseline RNN but not significantly better than other models). The paper fails to sufficiently differentiate its framework from existing multi-task learning approaches, and the comparison with recent state-of-the-art models is inadequate for assessing true contribution. The paper also doesn't adequately address why a unified framework is necessary when many previous papers have worked on multiple tasks.

Questions: How does your framework differ from existing multi-task learning approaches in NLP? Could you provide more details on the dataset construction process for yodaqa/large2470 to ensure no data leakage with the Wang dataset? Given that models still lag behind state-of-the-art on many tasks, how do you justify the claim of "universal semantic comprehension" rather than just improved transfer learning?
