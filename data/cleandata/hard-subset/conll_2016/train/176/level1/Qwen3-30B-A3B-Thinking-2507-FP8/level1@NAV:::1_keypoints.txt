- Proposes a unified framework and new software (dataset-sts) for consistent sentence-pair scoring evaluation.  
- Introduces new benchmarks (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) to address dataset limitations like size, candidate sets, and validation/test splits.  
- Presents evidence that transfer learning across tasks can improve performance and generalizability.  
- Dataset-sts improves reproducibility and comparability across diverse tasks.  
- New datasets specifically address problems in Wang’s Answer Sentence Selection (small size, uneven candidates, bad splits).  
- Uses 95% confidence intervals in evaluation to account for high variance in neural model results.  
- Demonstrates consistent transfer learning gains across tasks.  
- Claim of “universal semantic comprehension” is overstated; models still lag behind state-of-the-art and gains are modest.  
- Fails to sufficiently differentiate the framework from existing multi-task learning approaches.  
- Comparison with recent state-of-the-art models is inadequate to assess true contribution.  
- Does not clearly justify why a unified framework is necessary given prior multi-task work.  
- Reviewer asks how the framework differs from existing multi-task NLP approaches.  
- Reviewer requests details on yodaqa/large2470 construction to ensure no data leakage with the Wang dataset.  
- Reviewer questions the justification for claiming “universal semantic comprehension” rather than modest transfer-learning improvements.
