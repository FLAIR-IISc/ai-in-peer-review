- Proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring covering multiple NLP tasks and introduces dataset-sts for consistent implementation and evaluation.  
- Introduces two new Answer Sentence Selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address size and candidate-balance limitations.  
- Demonstrates transfer learning (Ubuntu Dialogue → other tasks), with Ubu.RNN outperforming baseline approaches.  
- Major methodological contribution: dataset-sts reduces fragmentation and enables meaningful cross-task model comparisons.  
- Careful evaluation: reports 95% confidence intervals and addresses randomness/overfitting issues in neural model evaluation.  
- Practical dataset improvements: new datasets produce more challenging, realistic benchmarks with lower baseline performance.  
- Weak evidence for the claim of “universal” semantic comprehension; transfer results are promising but limited in scope.  
- Lacks detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art on RTE and STS, making it hard to gauge progress relative to current best models.  
- Limited transfer-learning exploration: only transfers from Ubuntu Dialogue and doesn’t test multi-source or very different task types.  
- Question: How was data leakage prevented between yodaqa/large2470 and wang, especially during transfer experiments?  
- Question: Why recommend RNN-CNN as primary for f2-type tasks when Ubu.RNN transfer yields better performance?  
- Question: Please provide specific, detailed comparisons to recent state-of-the-art models for STS and paraphrasing tasks.  
- Overall: methodological rigor and datasets are valuable and likely to be widely adopted, but the paper needs stronger SOTA comparisons and broader transfer experiments to substantiate universality claims.
