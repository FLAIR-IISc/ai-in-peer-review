- Proposal: A unified framework for sentence-pair scoring with task-specific adaptation across tasks like Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, and RTE.  
- Contribution 1: dataset-sts — an open-source unified software framework enabling fair model comparison across multiple f2-type tasks.  
- Contribution 2: Two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) addressing size, split, and noise issues in existing datasets.  
- Contribution 3: Evidence for transfer learning across tasks suggesting feasibility of a universal semantic comprehension model.  

- Strength 1: Strong methodological contribution — exposes high variance in prior single-run evaluations and advocates 95% CI over multiple runs for reliable benchmarking.  
- Strength 2: Valuable datasets — larger, cleaner datasets with lower baselines that provide more room for progress.  
- Strength 3: Practical utility — modular, open-source framework (Keras-based) that improves reproducibility and ease of experimentation.  

- Weakness 1: Insufficient evidence that transfer results demonstrate a truly “universal” semantic model rather than task-specific adaptation.  
- Weakness 2: Limited demonstration that the framework produces superior models or insights compared to existing approaches or recent state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness 3: Novelty overstated — framework mainly wraps standard architectures (RNN/CNN/attention) without introducing new model/learning techniques.  

- Q1: Request for absolute performance difference between Ubu.RNN and baseline RNN (e.g., percentage points).  
- Q2: Request for direct comparison between training from scratch on target tasks versus transfer learning from Ubuntu Dialogue.  
- Q3: Request for specific baseline numbers on the new datasets compared to existing datasets.  
- Q4: Request rationale for choosing Ubuntu Dialogue as the transfer learning source dataset.  

- Overall: Strong methodological and dataset contributions that merit acceptance with minor revisions; claims of “universal semantic comprehension” should be toned down and supported with clearer performance comparisons.
