- Proposes a unified "Sentence Pair Scoring" framework claiming task-independent neural models can handle diverse sentence-pair tasks with small task-specific output layers.
- Contribution: Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation and an open-source platform unifying datasets, models, and adapters as a community benchmark.
- Contribution: Introduces two new, larger Answer Sentence Selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address weaknesses of the wang dataset.
- Contribution: Demonstrates successful cross-task transfer learning by pre-training on the Ubuntu Dialogue Corpus and fine-tuning on other sentence-pair tasks.
- Strength: High methodological rigor — reports averages over 16 runs with 95% confidence intervals to address high variance in neural results.
- Strength: Valuable community resources — new datasets and the dataset-sts open-source framework improve reproducibility and lower barriers for future work.
- Strength: Broad empirical scope — surveys multiple architectures across tasks and provides insights (e.g., CNNs better on small data, RNNs on larger datasets).
- Strength: Strong transfer-learning result — pretraining on large dialogue data yields consistent, substantial improvements, supporting transferable representations.
- Weakness: Limited architectural novelty — implements existing models (DAN, GRU, CNN) without proposing new architectures.
- Weakness: Inconsistent competitiveness — implemented baselines lag behind state-of-the-art on RTE and STS and some results were omitted from main tables.
- Weakness: Potentially overstated "universal comprehension" claim — evidence is from a single source dataset and model, more experiments needed to generalize the claim.
- Question: Please explain the performance gap on SICK and SNLI — is it due to Siamese architecture limitations, the MLP scorer, or lack of cross-sentence attention?
- Question: Have you tried reverse transfer (e.g., pretraining on SNLI and transferring to answer selection) or different architectures to strengthen the generic-comprehension claim?
- Question: How sensitive are results to BM25 pruning to top-20 candidates for answer selection, and could pruning remove difficult correct answers that hinder learning?
