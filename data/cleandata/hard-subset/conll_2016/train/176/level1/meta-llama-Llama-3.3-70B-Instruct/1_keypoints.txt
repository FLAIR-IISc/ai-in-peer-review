- Unifies various sentence-pair tasks (Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, RTE, STS) under a single framework for comprehensive understanding.
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to provide more realistic and challenging evaluation settings.
- Demonstrates feasibility of universal, task-independent sentence-pair models using cross-task transfer learning.
- Provides a thorough literature review highlighting fragmentation in sentence-pair research and need for unification.
- Shows the effectiveness of new datasets and experiments for evaluating sentence-pair scoring models.
- Uses cross-task transfer learning to empirically support the promise of universal models.
- Proposed models still lag behind state-of-the-art on some tasks (Recognizing Textual Entailment, Semantic Textual Similarity).
- Paper lacks detailed analysis of results and discussion of model/dataset limitations.
- Some experimental settings (e.g., single dataset for hyperparameter tuning) may be suboptimal.
- How will the authors address limitations of their models, especially where they underperform SOTA?
- Can the authors provide more details on dataset sizes, characteristics, collection, and preprocessing?
- How do the authors plan to extend the approach to other tasks/datasets and what are the potential applications?
