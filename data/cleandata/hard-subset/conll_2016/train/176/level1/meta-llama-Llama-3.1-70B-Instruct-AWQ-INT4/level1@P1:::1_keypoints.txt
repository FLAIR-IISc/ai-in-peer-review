- Proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring across tasks (answer selection, next utterance ranking, RTE, STS).  
- Introduces the yodaqa/large2470 dataset and an evaluation methodology that accounts for neural model randomness.  
- Evaluates RNN, CNN, and attention-based models across multiple tasks.  
- Shows models can be transferred across tasks (transfer learning effectiveness).  
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation clarifying model strengths and weaknesses.  
- New dataset and methodology enable more robust model comparison.  
- Transfer learning results indicate potential for more generalizable models.  
- No novel model architectures proposed; limited architectural innovation.  
- Analysis of results is limited; lacks detailed per-model, per-task breakdown.  
- Heavy emphasis on the Ubuntu Dialogue dataset, which may reduce domain generality.  
- Asks for a more detailed breakdown of performance by model and dataset.  
- Requests clarification on strategies to address overfitting in neural models.  
- Requests more details about yodaqa/large2470 (size, composition, preprocessing).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and situates work in existing literature.  
- Unified framework and evaluation methodology are significant contributions for robust evaluation.
