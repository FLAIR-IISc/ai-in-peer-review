**Summary and Contributions**

The paper "Sentence Pair Scoring: Towards Unified Framework for Text Comprehension" proposes a unified framework for various sentence pair scoring tasks, including answer sentence selection, next utterance ranking, recognizing textual entailment, and semantic textual similarity. The authors introduce a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, and evaluate several neural network models, including recurrent neural networks (RNNs), convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and attention-based models. The paper's primary contributions are:

1. **Unified framework**: The authors propose a single framework that can be applied to various sentence pair scoring tasks, allowing for a more comprehensive evaluation of models across tasks.
2. **New dataset**: The introduction of the yodaqa/large2470 dataset provides a larger and more challenging benchmark for answer sentence selection tasks.
3. **Cross-task evaluation**: The paper evaluates models across multiple tasks, demonstrating the importance of considering multiple tasks when developing sentence pair scoring models.

**Strengths**

1. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The paper provides a thorough evaluation of various models across multiple tasks, offering valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each model.
2. **New dataset**: The introduction of the yodaqa/large2470 dataset is a significant contribution, as it provides a larger and more challenging benchmark for answer sentence selection tasks.
3. **Unified framework**: The proposed framework allows for a more comprehensive evaluation of models across tasks, facilitating the development of more generalizable sentence pair scoring models.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited model exploration**: While the paper evaluates several models, the exploration of model architectures and hyperparameters is limited. More extensive experimentation could have provided further insights into the optimal models for each task.
2. **Lack of attention to state-of-the-art models**: The paper does not thoroughly compare its results to state-of-the-art models for each task, making it difficult to assess the relative performance of the proposed models.
3. **Insufficient analysis of results**: The paper could have benefited from a more in-depth analysis of the results, including a discussion of the implications of the findings and potential avenues for future research.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How did you select the hyperparameters for each model, and did you consider using more advanced hyperparameter tuning techniques, such as Bayesian optimization?
2. Can you provide more details on the yodaqa/large2470 dataset, including the data collection process and any preprocessing steps?
3. How do you plan to address the limitations of the current framework, such as the lack of attention to state-of-the-art models and the limited exploration of model architectures and hyperparameters?
