**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks, which involve classifying a pair of sentences based on their semantic similarity. The authors argue that there exist universal measures that can be applied to various semantic similarity tasks, and that these measures can be learned using neural network models. The paper presents a comprehensive survey of popular non-neural and neural baselines for sentence pair scoring tasks, including Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, Recognizing Textual Entailment, and Semantic Textual Similarity. The authors also introduce two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, for Answer Sentence Selection and Property Selection tasks, respectively.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unified Framework**: The paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks, which allows for easy implementation of models, dataset loaders, and task adapters in a modular fashion.
2. **New Datasets**: The authors introduce two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, which provide a more comprehensive evaluation of sentence pair scoring models.
3. **Comprehensive Survey**: The paper presents a comprehensive survey of popular non-neural and neural baselines for sentence pair scoring tasks, providing a thorough understanding of the current state-of-the-art in the field.

**Strengths**

1. **Comprehensive Survey**: The paper provides a thorough survey of popular non-neural and neural baselines for sentence pair scoring tasks, which is a valuable resource for researchers in the field.
2. **Unified Framework**: The proposed framework allows for easy implementation of models, dataset loaders, and task adapters in a modular fashion, facilitating the development of new models and evaluation of existing ones.
3. **New Datasets**: The introduction of two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, provides a more comprehensive evaluation of sentence pair scoring models and helps to address the limitations of existing datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Novelty in Models**: The paper does not propose any novel models for sentence pair scoring tasks, but rather evaluates existing models using the proposed framework and datasets.
2. **Limited Evaluation**: The paper only evaluates a limited set of models using the proposed framework and datasets, which may not be representative of the entire range of sentence pair scoring models.
3. **Overemphasis on IR Baselines**: The paper places a strong emphasis on IR baselines, which may not be the most effective approach for sentence pair scoring tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend the proposed framework to incorporate more advanced models, such as attention-based models and serial RNNs with attention?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the preprocessing steps used for the new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop?
3. How do the authors plan to address the issue of overfitting, which is mentioned in the paper as a common problem in sentence pair scoring tasks?
