- Proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring to learn universal semantic similarity measures with neural models.
- Argues that universal measures can be applied across tasks and learned via neural networks.
- Provides a comprehensive survey of non-neural and neural baselines across tasks (Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, RTE, STS).
- Introduces two new datasets: yodaqa/large2470 (Answer Sentence Selection) and wqmprop (Property Selection).
- Framework is modular, enabling easy implementation of models, dataset loaders, and task adapters.
- New datasets aim to provide more comprehensive evaluation and address limitations of existing benchmarks.
- The survey is a valuable resource summarizing current baselines and state-of-the-art approaches.
- The modular framework facilitates development and evaluation of new models.
- Paper lacks novel model architectures, focusing on evaluating existing models instead.
- Evaluation covers only a limited set of models, which may not represent the full space of approaches.
- Paper places strong emphasis on IR baselines, which may not be optimal for all sentence pair tasks.
- Question: How will the framework incorporate advanced models like attention-based architectures and serial RNNs with attention?
- Question: Please provide more details on preprocessing steps for yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop.
- Question: How do the authors plan to address overfitting, a common problem in sentence pair scoring tasks?
