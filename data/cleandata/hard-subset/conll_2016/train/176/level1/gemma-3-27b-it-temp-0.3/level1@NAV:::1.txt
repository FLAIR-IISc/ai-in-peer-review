## Review of "Sentence Pair Scoring: Towards Unified Framework for Text Comprehension"

This paper presents a comprehensive review of the sentence pair scoring task, framing it as a unifying problem across diverse NLP applications like question answering, textual entailment, and dialogue ranking. The authors aim to establish a unified framework for evaluation, introduce new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop), and demonstrate the feasibility of task-independent models through cross-task transfer learning. 

The primary contribution of this work is the **creation and release of the yodaqa/large2470 dataset**. The authors convincingly argue that existing datasets suffer from limitations like small size, uneven distributions, and noisy validation splits. This new dataset, built with a more robust methodology, provides a valuable resource for the community and addresses a clear need for better benchmarks in sentence pair scoring. A secondary contribution lies in the **thorough empirical evaluation of various neural network architectures** (RNNs, CNNs, attention mechanisms) across multiple tasks within a unified framework. This comparative analysis, including baselines, provides a useful watermark for future research and highlights the strengths and weaknesses of different approaches. Finally, the paper demonstrates **initial success with cross-task transfer learning**, showing that models trained on one task (Ubuntu Dialogue) can generalize to others, supporting the idea of universal semantic comprehension models.

**Strengths:**

1. **Dataset Contribution:** The introduction of yodaqa/large2470 is a significant contribution. The detailed explanation of its construction and the critique of existing datasets are well-justified and demonstrate a clear understanding of the challenges in this area.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper provides a broad and systematic evaluation of various models across multiple tasks. The inclusion of strong IR baselines and the reporting of confidence intervals enhance the reliability and interpretability of the results.
3. **Unified Framework:** The effort to unify the diverse landscape of sentence pair scoring tasks under a single framework is commendable. This facilitates meaningful comparisons and promotes a more cohesive research direction.
4. **Open-Source Release:** The release of the dataset-sts framework and KeraSTS library promotes reproducibility and encourages further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Models:** While the evaluation is thorough, the models themselves (RNNs, CNNs, attention) are relatively standard. The paper doesn't introduce any novel architectural innovations.
2. **Transfer Learning Results are Preliminary:** The transfer learning experiments, while promising, are limited in scope. The authors acknowledge that the chosen configuration (Ubu. RNN) might not be optimal and further investigation is needed.
3. **Lack of Ablation Studies:** The paper would benefit from ablation studies to better understand the contribution of different components within the models, particularly the added features (e.g., word overlap) in the Answer Sentence Selection task.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details on the Mechanical Turk labeling process for the yodaqa/large2470 dataset? What quality control measures were implemented to ensure the accuracy of the labels?
2.  You mention that the validation set performance is not always a good indicator of test set performance.  What strategies did you employ to mitigate overfitting and select the best model for evaluation?
3.  Regarding the transfer learning experiments, what were the specific hyperparameters used during the fine-tuning phase on the target tasks?
