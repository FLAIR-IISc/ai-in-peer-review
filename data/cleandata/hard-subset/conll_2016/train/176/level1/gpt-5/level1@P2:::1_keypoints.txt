- Contribution: Proposes a unified “Sentence Pair Scoring” framework with shared model interfaces, dataset adapters, open-source code, and improved evaluation practices (multiple restarts, CIs, strong IR baselines).
- Contribution: Releases new answer sentence selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470) and property selection dataset (wqmprop) to address wang’s size and stability issues.
- Contribution: Provides a broad empirical study of neural architectures (avg/DAN, CNN, biGRU, RNN-CNN, attention) across tasks and preliminary cross-task transfer learning (Ubuntu → others).
- Strength: Emphasizes methodological rigor and reproducibility by quantifying seed variance, averaging runs, and reporting 95% confidence intervals.
- Strength: Includes strong IR baselines (TF-IDF, BM25) and realistic ensembling/pruning setups to improve comparability.
- Strength: Offers unified, multi-task tooling (Keras-based library, dataset loaders) that lowers barriers for cross-task comparisons and replication.
- Strength: The large2470 dataset is more challenging than wang, showing lower baselines and more headroom for improvement.
- Strength: Empirical insights: CNNs perform better on small datasets, RNNs scale with data, attention helps on IR-like tasks, and transfer from Ubuntu is beneficial.
- Weakness: Limited technical novelty—models are replications/variants of known architectures; novelty is mainly in resources and evaluation.
- Weakness: Dataset construction risks (auto-labeling by answer-string matching) may introduce false negatives/positives and lexical bias; overlap/leakage with wang not rigorously quantified.
- Weakness: BM25 pruning and using BM25 as an input feature can dominate results and obscure neural model contributions; no ablations quantify each component’s impact.
- Weakness: Incomplete head-to-head comparisons with strongest per-task SOTA models (e.g., ESIM-style RTE/STS models) limit claims about competitiveness.
- Weakness: Writing, formatting, and implementation details are incomplete or scattered; key hyperparameters and early-stopping policies are not fully specified.
- Weakness: Transfer learning experiments are limited to one source and configuration with minimal ablation (e.g., freezing vs fine-tuning, dropout effects).
- Question: Provide precise yodaqa/large2470 statistics (questions, candidate distribution, positives per query, dev/test sizes), annotation QA, and estimate of false-negative rate from string matching.
- Question: Quantify overlap with wang at question and sentence levels and confirm there is no cross-split leakage or duplication.
- Question: Report what fraction of gold positives are pruned by top-20 BM25 on train/dev/test and sensitivity to cutoff (e.g., 20 vs 50); provide ablations removing BM25 feature, pruning, and token-overlap flags.
- Question: Give full Ubuntu-pretrain details (hidden sizes, training steps/epochs, optimizer) and ablations (freezing layers vs full fine-tune, dropout schedule); report attempts with other sources or multi-task training.
- Question: Given weak dev-test correlation, clarify epoch selection procedure and report fixed-epoch results to avoid implicit dev overfitting.
- Suggestion: Include stronger per-task baselines (contemporary SOTA models) with variance-aware reporting to contextualize performance.
- Overall assessment: Valuable resource paper with strong significance and soundness in methodology and datasets, but weaker on model novelty and completeness; leans toward weak accept if datasets/code and requested ablations/clarifications are provided, otherwise borderline.
