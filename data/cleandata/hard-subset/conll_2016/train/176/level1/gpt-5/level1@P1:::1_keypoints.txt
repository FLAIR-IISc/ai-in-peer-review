- Proposes unifying sentence pair tasks under a single f2(s0,s1) framework and studies task-independent neural models with task-specific adapters.  
- Implements and releases an open-source KeraSTS framework for loading datasets, running common models, and reporting robust stats (16 runs, 95% CIs) with IR baselines.  
- Introduces two new answer selection datasets: yodaqa/large2470 (Wikipedia candidates) and wqmprop (Freebase property selection).  
- Benchmarks standard neural architectures (avg/DAN, CNN, RNN, RNN-CNN, simple attention) across tasks and shows cross-task transfer, notably Ubuntu Dialogue → other tasks.  
- Primary contribution: unified evaluation framework with stronger methodology, IR baselines, and statistical reporting.  
- Primary contribution: new, larger, and more realistic answer selection datasets.  
- Primary contribution: empirical evidence that cross-task transfer (Ubuntu→others) improves performance.  
- Strength: methodological rigor and reproducibility (averaging over runs, CIs, strong non-neural baselines).  
- Strength: open-source framework and task unification that facilitates comparable multi-task experiments.  
- Strength: new datasets expose headroom (more candidates, lower baselines) and fill licensing gaps.  
- Strength: consistent cross-task transfer gains suggesting viability of universal sentence-pair models.  
- Strength: breadth of empirical study surfaces useful trends (e.g., CNNs for small data, RNNs for larger, attention helpful in IR-like settings).  
- Weakness: limited model novelty—architectures are standard and offer little algorithmic innovation.  
- Weakness: “task-independent” claim undermined by IR-specific engineering (BM25 top-20 pruning, BM25 as feature, token overlap/capitalization/number flags).  
- Weakness: lack of quantified impact/ablations for pre-filtering and token-level features (no recall@20, no feature ablation).  
- Weakness: dataset quality and potential leakage not quantified (possible overlap between yodaqa and wang, automatic labeling noise).  
- Weakness: weaker baselines and missing stronger entailment models on RTE/STS tasks limit generality claims.  
- Weakness: training and reporting choices raise concerns (heavy dropout, early overfitting, limited hyperparameter search, odd epoch sampling).  
- Weakness: presentation clarity issues (truncated abstract, malformed references, scattered stats/hyperparams).  
- Question: Will yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop be fully released with clear licenses and fixed train/dev/test splits?  
- Question: Please quantify overlap with wang and provide leak-free splits if overlap exists.  
- Question: What is BM25 recall@20 on the new datasets and how do results change without pruning or BM25 as a feature?  
- Question: Provide ablations for token overlap/capitalization/number flags and for projection vs. dot-product, with/without dropout.  
- Question: Which layers are transferred in transfer learning, and report exact mean±CI numbers for Ubu.RNN vs non-transferred baselines (and transfers from SNLI if tried).  
- Question: Detail the hyperparameter search protocol (ranges, budgets, selection), sensitivity to dropout/optimizer, and rationale for training on 1/4 data per epoch.  
- Question: For yodaqa, quantify label noise from string-matching, any manual validation/IAA; for wqmprop, report average properties per question and ambiguity/balance stats.  
- Overall: Useful resource and methodology paper with broad empirical value, but claims of task independence are weakened by IR engineering and missing ablations/leakage analyses.
