- Paper frames multiple sentence-pair tasks as a single f2(s0,s1) scoring problem and implements a shared evaluation framework.
- Releases code (dataset-sts/KeraSTS) with standardized data loaders, task adapters, and task-independent encoders.
- Advocates improved reporting: multi-seed runs (16) and 95% confidence intervals to address run-to-run variance.
- Introduces two new datasets: yodaqa/large2470 for answer sentence selection and wqmprop for property selection.
- Compares several neural encoders (avg, DAN, CNN, RNN, RNN-CNN, attention) against IR baselines (TF-IDF, BM25).
- Integrates overlap/BM25 features recognizing strong IR baselines in sentence selection.
- Empirically shows cross-task transfer: pretraining an RNN on Ubuntu next-utterance ranking improves performance on other f2 tasks.
- Framework improves reproducibility and comparability across sentence-pair tasks.
- New datasets present larger candidate pools and lower baselines, offering more discriminative evaluation than wang.
- Breadth of model coverage provides useful cross-task architecture trade-offs (e.g., CNNs for small data, RNNs for large data).
- Transfer learning results support the hypothesis that f2 encoders learn transferable comprehension signals.
- Modeling novelty is limited; main contribution is engineering, datasets, and methodology rather than new algorithms.
- Answer selection evaluation may be confounded by BM25 prefiltering and inclusion of BM25 scores as MLP inputs.
- Dataset construction risks (overlap/leakage between wang and yodaqa) are acknowledged but not quantified.
- Empirical evidence is incomplete or uneven: missing comparisons to strongest published results and omitted tasks.
- Transfer-learning experiments use a different RNN configuration, leaving ambiguity whether gains come from pretraining or hyperparameters.
- Important implementation details (tokenization, max lengths, optimizers, hyperparameter grids, CI sample sizes) are under-specified.
- Paper contains clarity/presentation issues: typos, incomplete references, and missing/uncited figures/tables.
- Extreme dropout choice (p=0.8) lacks systematic justification or analysis of convergence effects.
- Reviewer requests quantification of dataset overlaps and a leakage analysis with possible revised splits.
- Reviewer asks for ablations on BM25 prefilter sensitivity (no prefilter, top-50) and impact of including BM25 as a feature.
- Reviewer requests fair transfer comparisons: same architecture trained from scratch vs pretrained with identical hyperparameters, transfer details, and parameter counts.
- Reviewer asks for full numeric results (means and 95% CIs) for all tasks and comparisons to strongest published baselines, plus run counts.
- Reviewer requests confirmation that yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop will be released with fixed splits, candidate lists, evaluation scripts, and licensing details.
