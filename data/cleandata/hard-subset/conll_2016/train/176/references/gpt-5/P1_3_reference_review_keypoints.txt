- Proposes a unified evaluation framework and tooling (PySTS/KeraSTS) for sentence-pair (f2) tasks, aiming for task-agnostic models and cross-dataset evaluation.
- Introduces improved evaluation methodology: averaging over 16 runs and reporting 95% confidence intervals to address instability and cherry-picking.
- Releases two new answer-selection datasets: yodaqa/large2470 (larger, more realistic candidates) and wqmprop (property selection), claiming more challenging benchmarks.
- Demonstrates cross-task transfer where weights from next-utterance ranking (Ubuntu Dialogue) improve performance on other f2 tasks versus training from scratch.
- Strength: tackles reproducibility and variance issues by documenting dataset instability (e.g., wang) and providing rigorous statistical reporting.
- Strength: unifies heterogeneous datasets/models into an open-source pipeline, lowering barriers for cross-task research and fairer comparisons.
- Strength: shows that simple IR baselines remain competitive on realistic datasets, calibrating claims of neural gains.
- Strength: provides broad empirical comparisons across architectures (avg/DAN/CNN/RNN/RNN-CNN/attention) and tasks, noting CNNs perform better on smaller data while RNNs benefit from larger datasets.
- Weakness: limited model novelty—architectures are standard baselines and lag state-of-the-art on core NLI/STS tasks.
- Weakness: answer-selection evaluation is confounded by BM25 pruning and using BM25 as a feature, potentially inflating results and mixing lexical and semantic contributions.
- Weakness: dataset construction concerns—possible overlap/leakage between wang and yodaqa, noisy distant supervision from answer-string matching, and insufficient documentation of splits and noise analysis.
- Weakness: incomplete baselines and tuning—omits stronger contemporary NLI/STS architectures, tunes primarily on yodaqa which may bias results, and uses only 10% of Ubuntu training data.
- Weakness: narrow transfer evaluation—only one source task, one architecture, and limited analysis of which components transfer or comparisons to multi-task training.
- Key dataset questions: request de-duplication analysis between wang and yodaqa, noise quantification for yodaqa labeling, and complete split/candidate size/license details.
- Key evaluation questions: request ablations for BM25 pruning vs no pruning, BM25-as-feature, and token-overlap flags; clarification on per-task vs shared hyperparameter tuning and early stopping procedure.
- Key transfer questions: request layer-wise freezing/unfreezing ablations, impact of dropout mismatch, comparisons to multi-task or in-domain pretraining, and transfer in opposite directions or from other source tasks.
- Overall verdict: primary contribution is evaluation framework, datasets, and methodological guidance; needs clearer dataset documentation, fairer ablations disentangling IR features, and deeper transfer analyses to solidify claims.
