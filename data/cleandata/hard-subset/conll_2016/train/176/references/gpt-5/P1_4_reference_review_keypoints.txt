- Summary: Proposes a unified view and shared framework for sentence-pair (f2) tasks, adds two new datasets, evaluates multiple models and IR baselines, emphasizes robust evaluation (16 runs + CIs), and shows transfer gains from Ubuntu pretraining.

- Contribution 1: Provides a unified evaluation pipeline (dataset-sts/KeraSTS) that normalizes data handling, includes IR baselines, and reports robust statistics highlighting instability in prior work.

- Contribution 2: Introduces two new datasets — yodaqa/large2470 (harder answer sentence selection) and wqmprop (property selection) — to address limitations of the wang dataset.

- Contribution 3: Empirically shows cross-task transfer learning where pretraining on Ubuntu Dialogue improves performance on other f2 tasks.

- Strength 1: Methodological rigor and reproducibility via averaging over 16 runs and reporting 95% confidence intervals, demonstrating single-run unreliability.

- Strength 2: Unified framework and baselines consolidate datasets, task adapters, multiple neural models, and IR baselines, with open-source release to aid comparability.

- Strength 3: New datasets offer more realistic, harder IR-style evaluation with descriptive statistics and lower baseline performance.

- Strength 4: Demonstrates practical cross-task transfer: Ubuntu-pretrained RNN gives consistent improvements when fine-tuned.

- Strength 5: Honest analysis of pitfalls (overfitting, dev–test mismatch, lexical overlap) with pragmatic mitigations like BM25 features and candidate pruning.

- Weakness 1: Limited model novelty and omission of stronger recent approaches (advanced attention, pretrained encoders), leaving gaps versus SOTA on some tasks.

- Weakness 2: IR feature integration (BM25) and top-20 pruning change task distribution and complicate comparisons; ablations without these heuristics are missing.

- Weakness 3: Dataset construction concerns and potential label/train–test leakage for yodaqa/large2470; noise and validation procedures are under-specified.

- Weakness 4: Transfer learning analysis is underdeveloped — unclear which layers/features transfer, sensitivity to mismatches, and comparison to multi-task/joint training is lacking.

- Weakness 5: Presentation and completeness issues: some references/figures unclear, hyperparameters not fully specified per task, and missing standard Ubuntu metrics (Recall@k).

- Question 1 (dataset release/licensing): Will yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop be fully released with scripts, and what are the licensing restrictions for derived data?

- Question 2 (noise/validation): How were answer-bearing sentences labeled in yodaqa/large2470 and are there estimates of label noise or human validation?

- Question 3 (de-duplication/leakage): What steps were taken to prevent overlap between splits and across datasets, and can overlap statistics be provided?

- Question 4 (Ubuntu metrics): Can you report Recall@1/2/5 for Ubuntu (10-candidate ranking) and performance variance across seeds?

- Question 5 (BM25/overlap ablations): Please provide ablations without BM25, without token-overlap flags, and without top-20 pruning to quantify their impact.

- Question 6 (transfer specifics): Which layers are transferred vs. reinitialized, were freezing/discriminative learning rates tried, and was any negative transfer observed?

- Recommendation: Borderline accept (weak accept) — valuable for its unified, statistically sound evaluation, tooling, and datasets, but needs stronger dataset documentation, ablations, and clarity to improve comparability.
