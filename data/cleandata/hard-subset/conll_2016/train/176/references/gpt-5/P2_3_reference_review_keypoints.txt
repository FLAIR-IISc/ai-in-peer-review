- Paper frames many NLP tasks as sentence-pair scoring (f2), proposes a unified evaluation framework (KeraSTS), adds two datasets, and benchmarks neural/IR baselines showing Ubuntu pretraining transfers well.

- Contribution: open-source unified toolkit integrating datasets, adapters, consistent training/evaluation with multi-run confidence intervals and strong IR baselines.

- Contribution: two new datasets—large2470 for answer sentence selection and wqmprop for property selection—to address limitations of wang.

- Contribution: empirical study across architectures/tasks and a transfer learning result showing Ubuntu next-utterance pretraining helps other f2 tasks.

- Strength: strong reproducibility and methodology—averages over 16 runs with confidence intervals, highlights variance and dev/test mismatch issues.

- Strength: inclusion of realistic IR baselines (BM25/TF-IDF) and token-overlap features prevents overclaiming neural gains.

- Strength: breadth—implements multiple model families (avg/DAN, CNN, RNN, RNN-CNN, attention) under one codebase across diverse f2 tasks.

- Strength: proposed large2470 is more challenging than wang and wqmprop targets a useful KB-QA subtask.

- Strength: practical insights—CNNs fare better on small data, RNNs on larger data; attention helps IR-like answer selection; BM25 pruning and overlap features matter.

- Weakness: limited model novelty—architectures are standard and attention variant is basic, so originality is low for CoNLL.

- Weakness: confounding evaluation choices—BM25 top-20 pruning, adding BM25 as feature, different dropout and scorer choices complicate fair cross-model comparisons and task-independence claims.

- Weakness: dataset quality and leakage risks—automatic labeling, unclear noise rate, sparse denoising details, and possible overlaps with wang/curatedv2 lack de-duplication evidence.

- Weakness: empirical results lag SOTA on key tasks (RTE, STS); Ubuntu result is primarily a new baseline with limited training usage.

- Weakness: clarity/completeness issues—missing dataset URLs, redacted stats/figures, formatting errors, and unexplained training choices hinder reproducibility.

- Question: will you publicly release large2470 and wqmprop with construction scripts, splits, licenses, and leakage controls, and what are denoising/human validation stats?

- Question: what is the contribution of BM25 pruning and BM25-as-feature per task/model—please report ablations without pruning/features.

- Question: why use different scorers (dot vs MLP) across tasks—were scorer types tuned uniformly, and how sensitive are model comparisons to these choices?

- Question: how does Ubuntu pretraining compare to alternatives (SNLI, language-model/skip-thought pretraining, embedding-only initialization), with absolute gains and CIs per target task?

- Question: are 16-run averages and CIs reported for all models/datasets; how were random seeds and preprocessing standardized across experiments?

- Overall assessment: useful engineering and evaluation contribution with valuable resources and methodological guidance, but limited novelty, unresolved dataset quality/leakage concerns, and confounded comparisons lead to a weak-reject recommendation unless addressed.
