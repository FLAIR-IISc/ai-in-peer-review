- Paper proposes a unified view and evaluation framework for sentence pair scoring (f2) across multiple tasks with variance-aware reporting.
- Introduces two new datasets: yodaqa/large2470 for answer sentence selection and wqmprop for property selection.
- Provides a common codebase/pipeline (dataset loaders, Keras models) and standardized metrics across tasks.
- Benchmarks a suite of neural baselines (avg/DAN, CNN, biGRU, RNN-CNN, attention) alongside IR baselines (TF-IDF/BM25).
- Emphasizes variance-aware evaluation: averaging over 16 seeds and reporting t-based 95% confidence intervals.
- Demonstrates cross-task transfer where RNN pretrained on Ubuntu Dialogue improves downstream f2 tasks.
- Strength: rigorous evaluation methodology that exposes variance often larger than reported SOTA deltas.
- Strength: comprehensive, task-agnostic framework with interchangeable scorers, losses, and inclusion of IR baselines.
- Strength: new datasets are larger/more challenging, reducing overfitting to small splits and brittle metrics.
- Strength: empirical insights about model behavior (CNNs better on small data; RNNs on large; attention helps in IR-style selection).
- Strength: initial cross-task transfer results are promising and support shared f2 task commonalities.
- Weakness: modeling novelty is limited; omits stronger contemporary models (decomposable attention, ESIM, modern encoders).
- Weakness: dataset construction/validation details are insufficient; risks of false negatives and lexical bias not quantified.
- Weakness: use of BM25 for pruning/feature injection complicates comparability and may inflate neural model gains.
- Weakness: transfer claims are narrow (single source, single architecture) with limited analysis of why transfer helps.
- Weakness: reporting choices and implementation details (training schedule, heavy dropout, using first 1M Ubuntu pairs) lack justification and ablations.
- Questions: provide ablations removing token-overlap features and BM25 pruning/features across answer selection datasets.
- Questions: quantify dataset noise—manual validation, false-negative rate for yodaqa, and linguistic justification for wqmprop property paths.
- Questions: report overlap between wang and yodaqa/large2470 and provide non-overlapping splits to avoid leakage.
- Questions: justify using only first 1M Ubuntu pairs; does full dataset or SNLI pretraining change transfer outcomes?
- Questions: compare Ubuntu results (with CIs) to Lowe et al. (2015) and Kadlec et al. (2015) under identical evaluation to confirm statistical superiority.
- Overall: valuable as an evaluation/resource paper with strong methodology, but limited modeling novelty and needs stronger ablations, dataset validation, and inclusion of competitive models.
