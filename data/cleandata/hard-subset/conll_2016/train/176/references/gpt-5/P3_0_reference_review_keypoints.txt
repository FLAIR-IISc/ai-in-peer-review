- Paper proposes a unified perspective and modular evaluation framework for sentence-pair scoring tasks, introduces two new answer-selection datasets, and compares neural/IR models with cross-task transfer experiments.
- Methodological contribution: advocates multi-run evaluation with 95% confidence intervals and provides robust IR baselines.
- Data contribution: releases larger, harder answer sentence selection datasets (large2470, wqmprop) and consolidates Ubuntu next-utterance ranking treatment.
- Empirical finding: pretraining on Ubuntu dialogues improves downstream task performance, suggesting some task-agnostic transfer.
- Strength: rigorous evaluation and variance reporting (averaging across 16 runs) highlights run-to-run noise and improves result reliability.
- Strength: unified, task-agnostic pipeline and open-source release lower barriers for fair cross-task comparison.
- Strength: new datasets have many candidates per question and lower baselines, increasing difficulty for future methods.
- Strength: broad, sensible baselines include strong IR methods and simple neural models, giving grounded comparisons.
- Strength: practical transfer-learning tips (dropout, optimizer choices) are shared for practitioners.
- Weakness: modeling novelty is limited; missing stronger pairwise interaction architectures (match-LSTM, decomposable attention, ESIM, multi-perspective).
- Weakness: IR pre-filtering and injected BM25/overlap features confound claims of task-agnostic semantic learning; ablations are needed.
- Weakness: dataset construction and contamination risks are insufficiently documented; overlap removal and labeling heuristics unclear.
- Weakness: transfer-learning claims lack necessary ablations isolating pretraining from architectural/optimizer changes.
- Weakness: Ubuntu pretraining uses only 10% of data, making baseline comparability and claimed improvements unclear.
- Question: provide precise construction details, verification rates, linking accuracy, overlap lists, and scripts for large2470 and wqmprop.
- Question: report model performance without BM25 prefiltering and without BM25/overlap features, and sensitivity to varying prefilter k.
- Question: supply transfer ablations — same architecture without pretraining, embedding-only pretraining, layer freezing, and scorer/dimensionality control; test domain sensitivity (e.g., SNLI).
- Question: clarify epoch selection across 16 runs and show test metrics across checkpoints given weak dev/test correlation on wang.
- Question: justify Ubuntu 10% downsampling and report whether full-data training changes conclusions.
- Suggestion: include stronger pairwise-interaction baselines and multi-hop models to better support universality claims.
- Suggestion: add error analyses (failure modes, calibration) and per-task training budgets/hyperparameters for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: evaluate answer selection without top-k pruning on at least one dataset to allow apples-to-apples comparisons.
- Overall: main value is methodological rigor and new datasets with initial transfer evidence; modeling is incremental and requires stronger baselines, clearer datasets, and rigorous ablations to become a robust benchmark reference.
