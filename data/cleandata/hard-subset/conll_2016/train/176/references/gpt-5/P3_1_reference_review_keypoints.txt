- Paper proposes a unified view of sentence-pair (f2) tasks and provides an evaluation framework, datasets, and neural baselines across multiple tasks.
- Contributions: unified evaluation methodology with seed-averaged metrics and CIs, open-source tooling, two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop), and transfer learning experiments (Ubuntu → other f2 tasks).
- Strength: rigorous reproducibility practices (16 runs, 95% CIs) that address common variance issues in sentence-pair research.
- Strength: practical community resources — integrated framework and larger, harder answer-selection dataset.
- Strength: cross-task baseline results reveal useful patterns (CNNs better on small data, RNNs scale, IR baselines strong).
- Strength: transfer learning evidence — Ubuntu-pretrained RNN improves several target tasks; practical tips (dropout caveat) included.
- Strength: sensible IR integration (BM25, overlap features, top-20 pruning) mirrors realistic pipelines.
- Weakness: limited modeling novelty and omission of stronger cross-sentence interaction models (decomposable attention, match-LSTM, etc.).
- Weakness: potential dataset construction issues and leakage — yodaqa positives may be surface-match biased and wang/yodaqa overlap not quantified.
- Weakness: IR entanglement — BM25 used for both pruning and as model feature, making it hard to attribute gains to neural models.
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning mainly on large2470 risks overfitting and validation–test mismatch remains unresolved.
- Weakness: reporting gaps — missing direct numerical comparisons to strongest published baselines on SNLI/SICK/Ubuntu.
- Weakness: limited analysis and ablations — unclear what transfers (embeddings vs encoder vs scorer), feature effects, and no error analysis.
- Questions: quantify overlap between wang and yodaqa and provide de-dup protocol or rerun affected experiments.
- Questions: clarify yodaqa labeling (exact/partial string match), fraction of non-surface matches, and any human validation/noise estimates.
- Questions: report results without BM25 pruning and without BM25/overlap features to show neural-only performance alongside IR-augmented results.
- Questions: provide numeric R@1/2/5 comparisons (with CIs) on Ubuntu against Lowe et al. and Kadlec et al.
- Questions: provide transfer ablations — which layers are frozen vs fine-tuned, effects of freezing embeddings/encoder/scorer, and matching dropout regimes.
- Additional suggestion: add strong non-neural LTR baselines (e.g., LambdaMART) for answer selection.
- Additional suggestion: explore multi-task joint training with shared encoders and task-specific heads.
- Additional suggestion: incorporate token-level cross-attention/alignment to improve inter-sentence interactions.
- Additional suggestion: use stratified bootstrap over questions for MAP/MRR significance and report per-question candidate set size distributions.
- Overall: paper’s main value is artifacts and evaluation rigor; borderline accept pending artifact release and addressing leakage/evaluation clarifications.
