- Proposes a unified "sentence pair scoring" (f2) view covering answer selection, next-utterance ranking, entailment, and STS.  
- Releases a unified evaluation framework (dataset-sts/KeraSTS) with consistent adapters, IR baselines, and multi-seed CIs to improve comparability.  
- Introduces new answer-selection datasets: yodaqa/large2470 (larger, more realistic candidate sets) and wqmprop (property selection).  
- Empirically critiques the wang dataset as small and unstable, showing high variance in single-run metrics.  
- Evaluates simple neural architectures (avg/DAN, CNN, biGRU, RNN-CNN, attention) across tasks to compare behavior and strengths.  
- Demonstrates transfer learning: pretraining on Ubuntu Dialogue improves performance on several downstream tasks.  
- Primary claimed contributions: (1) rigorous unified evaluation methodology, (2) new realistic answer-selection datasets and instability analysis, (3) empirical evidence for cross-task transfer.  
- Strength: strong methodological rigor—multi-seed averages, 95% CIs, and inclusion of strong non-neural baselines (TF-IDF, BM25).  
- Strength: useful cross-task unification and baseline coverage yielding actionable observations (e.g., CNNs on small data, RNNs with more data, attention helps IR-style tasks).  
- Strength: yodaqa/large2470 stresses realistic IR conditions (larger, uneven candidate sets) and reveals lower baselines for more meaningful progress.  
- Strength: transfer learning result is practically impactful with concrete guidance (dropout/optimizer) and consistent gains from Ubuntu pretraining.  
- Weakness: limited modeling novelty and omission of stronger contemporary task-specific baselines, leaving gaps to SOTA on SNLI/SICK/STS.  
- Weakness: answer-selection experiments confounded by using BM25 as an input feature and pruning to BM25 top-20, obscuring pure neural-scorer contribution.  
- Weakness: dataset construction/overlap concerns—possible overlap with wang, insufficient detail on wqmprop creation, noise rates, and split/leakage safeguards.  
- Weakness: evaluation fairness issues—varying hyperparameter choices and capacities across tasks, unstable dropout usage, and unexplained use of only 10% Ubuntu training.  
- Weakness: universality claim is limited—pretraining evidence comes from one conversational corpus only, lacking multi-source or reverse-transfer analysis.  
- Key requested ablations/details: full stats and overlap analysis for yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, label-noise estimates, and split construction.  
- Key requested ablations/details: answer-selection experiments without BM25-as-feature and without BM25 top-20 pruning.  
- Key requested ablations/details: Ubuntu preprocessing/truncation details, rationale for 1M training subset, and results using full data.  
- Key requested comparisons: stronger task-specific baselines on SNLI/SICK (e.g., attentive/Tree-LSTM) and capacity-matched from-scratch baselines.  
- Key requested transfer controls: CIs for transfer gains, pretraining on SNLI/SICK or random-label baselines, reverse transfer tests, and capacity-matching checks.  
- Reproducibility request: release exact splits, code, pretraining/fine-tuning checkpoints, and clarify dataset licensing.
