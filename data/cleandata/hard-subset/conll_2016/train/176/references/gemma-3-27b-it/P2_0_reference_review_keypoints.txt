- Overall: Strong paper advocating a unified evaluation framework and new datasets; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Contribution: Proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring and provides the dataset-sts package for standardized evaluation.
- Contribution: Introduces two new, more challenging datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address weaknesses of existing benchmarks.
- Contribution: Demonstrates cross-task transfer learning (Ubuntu Dialogue → other tasks) improving performance, supporting task-independent semantic models.
- Strength: Addresses the critical need for standardized evaluation in sentence pair research.
- Strength: New datasets are carefully designed to fix issues like small size and uneven candidate distributions.
- Strength: Experiments are comprehensive, include IR baselines, and report confidence intervals to account for training randomness.
- Strength: Focuses on practically relevant tasks (QA, dialogue) and is extendable to future tasks (e.g., memory networks).
- Weakness: Models used (RNN, CNN, attention) are not novel; paper emphasizes evaluation/data over algorithmic innovation.
- Weakness: Analysis of why transfer learning works is limited and some experimental choices (dot-product scorer, dimensionality) lack justification.
- Weakness: Paper is lengthy; some model descriptions could be more concise to improve readability.
- Question: Request deeper analysis of what features or representations are transferred in cross-task learning.
- Question: Ask whether overlap between wang and yodaqa datasets was examined and how it affects comparisons to prior work.
- Question: Ask how easily dataset-sts can be extended to new datasets/tasks with different formats or metrics.
- Additional: Verify that GitHub and dataset URLs are active and accessible.
- Additional: Suggest adding a summary table of dataset characteristics (size, task type, evaluation metric).
