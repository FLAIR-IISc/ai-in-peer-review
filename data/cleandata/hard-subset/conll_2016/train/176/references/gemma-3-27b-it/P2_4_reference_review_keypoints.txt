- Paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring (f2), surveys tasks/datasets, compares baselines, adds two new datasets, and shows transfer learning benefits from Ubuntu Dialogue.  
- Contribution: comprehensive survey and unified evaluation paradigm for f2 tasks.  
- Contribution: introduces yodaqa/large2470 (and wqmprop) to address limitations of the wang dataset.  
- Contribution: extensive empirical benchmarking and demonstration of cross-task transfer learning.  
- Strength: clearly motivated problem and compelling case for a unified framework.  
- Strength: thorough coverage of baseline models, including strong IR baselines.  
- Strength: rigorous evaluation with confidence intervals and a reproducible dataset-sts framework.  
- Strength: practical dataset contribution (yodaqa/large2470) with clear creation process.  
- Strength: successful demonstration of transfer learning from Ubuntu Dialogue.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in model architectures—mostly standard models applied/compared.  
- Weakness: models lag behind state-of-the-art on some tasks (RTE, STS).  
- Weakness: overly detailed, implementation-level model descriptions that could be streamlined.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of why transfer learning from Ubuntu Dialogue works.  
- Question: rationale for selecting the specific (non-novel) models—constraints or design choices?  
- Question: details on attempts and limited success combining IR baselines with ensemble methods.  
- Question: what characteristics of the Ubuntu Dialogue dataset enable effective transfer, and were other sources explored?  
- Question: potential overlap between wang and yodaqa datasets and the risk of bias in comparisons.  
- Additional: writing is clear but some sections (e.g., Section 3) should be more concise.  
- Additional: ensure the dataset-sts framework URL is included in the final paper.  
- Overall: solid contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses and questions.
