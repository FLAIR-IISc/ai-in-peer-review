- Summary: Proposes a unified framework for sentence-pair scoring (f2) using task-independent models fine-tuned per task, introduces new datasets and a software package, and shows cross-task transfer learning is feasible.

- Contribution 1: Provides comprehensive empirical evaluation across diverse f2 tasks and introduces yodaqa/large2470 to address shortcomings of prior answer-selection benchmarks.

- Contribution 2: Proposes a unified f2 framework and releases the dataset-sts toolkit to enable reproducible experiments and easier model comparison.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates that transfer learning from the Ubuntu Dialogue dataset can improve performance on other f2 tasks, supporting the universality hypothesis.

- Strength 1: Uses rigorous evaluation by reporting confidence intervals from multiple runs, addressing randomness in neural model results.

- Strength 2: Identifies and remedies dataset limitations (e.g., small size, noisy splits) with the yodaqa/large2470 dataset.

- Strength 3: Compares neural models to strong IR baselines (TF-IDF, BM25), providing realistic performance context.

- Strength 4: Open-source release of dataset-sts lowers barrier to entry and aids reproducibility.

- Strength 5: Clear organization and systematic presentation of tasks, models, and results.

- Weakness 1: Limited architectural exploration; omits modern models like Transformers despite their strong NLP performance.

- Weakness 2: Transfer learning experiments are shallow, focusing only on Ubuntu Dialogue and lacking systematic cross-task transfer analysis.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of model components (e.g., BM25 feature).

- Weakness 4: Models underperform on STS and RTE with little analysis on why they lag state-of-the-art.

- Weakness 5: Hyperparameter tuning details (search space, optimization strategy) are insufficiently documented for full reproducibility.

- Question 1: Why were Transformer-based architectures excluded, and would they change results?

- Question 2: Have the authors performed systematic transfer learning between all tasks, and what insights were found?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide ablation studies to assess key components like BM25 in answer selection?

- Question 4: Can qualitative error analysis be provided for STS and RTE to identify error types and causes?

- Question 5: Please detail the hyperparameter tuning process, including search space and optimization strategy.

- Additional comment: Paper should discuss related work on meta-learning and multi-task learning in NLP.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable dataset, toolkit, and evaluation methodology; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing raised weaknesses.
