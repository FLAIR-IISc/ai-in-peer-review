- Proposes a unified evaluation framework for sentence-pair scoring (f2) tasks to improve comparability across models and datasets.  
- Introduces new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) to address shortcomings of existing benchmarks like the Wang dataset.  
- Demonstrates cross-task transfer learning feasibility for sentence-pair models.

- Clearly motivates the need for a unified evaluation of f2 tasks.  
- Conducts a thorough experimental setup with diverse models, strong IR baselines, and reported confidence intervals.  
- Provides rigorous analysis of Wang dataset limitations and justifies creation of yodaqa/large2470.  
- Releases open-source tools (`dataset-sts`, `KeraSTS`) to support reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized.

- Models evaluated are largely standard architectures with limited novel modeling contributions.  
- Lacks detailed ablation studies to isolate the impact of model components and training choices.  
- Transfer learning experiments are narrow in scope (single RNN/dot-product setup) with limited analysis of why transfer works.  
- Missing detailed error analysis to identify common failure modes and guide future work.  
- Evaluation is limited to English datasets, reducing multilingual generalizability.

- Did you try mitigation strategies for Wang dataset issues (e.g., k-fold CV, augmentation)?  
- What quality control was used for Mechanical Turk labeling of yodaqa/large2470?  
- Why was the specific RNN/dot-product configuration chosen for transfer learning and were alternatives tried?  
- Were model error types analyzed across datasets, and what are common failure cases?  
- What computational resources and training times are required to reproduce the experiments?

- Ensure redacted URLs are included in supplementary material.  
- Clarify and justify the introduced f1/f2 notation for readability.  
- Joint multi-task training is suggested but noted as potentially challenging due to differing objectives.

- Overall: solid contribution emphasizing a comprehensive evaluation framework, useful new dataset(s), and promising transfer results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
