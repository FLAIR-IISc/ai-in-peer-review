- Proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring, surveys tasks/baselines, introduces datasets, and releases an open-source evaluation framework.
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address small size, uneven distributions, and noisy evaluation in existing benchmarks.
- Releases the “dataset-sts” open-source framework to facilitate reproducible research and lower entry barriers.
- Provides a comprehensive benchmarking framework enabling meaningful comparisons across diverse f2-type tasks.
- New datasets are motivated to overcome limitations of prior resources, especially for answer selection.
- Open-source implementation promotes reproducibility and modular experimentation.
- Addresses a real problem in NLP: lack of standardized evaluation across semantic similarity tasks.
- Offers a thorough baseline comparison, including strong IR baselines alongside neural models.
- Dataset improvements are supported by quantitative analysis (e.g., Figure 1).
- Emphasizes statistical rigor and advocates reporting confidence intervals due to neural result instability.
- The released software is practical, modular, and likely beneficial to the community.
- Modeling contribution is limited: RNNs and CNNs are standard and lack novelty.
- Transfer learning results are preliminary, limited in scope, and configuration choices are not well justified.
- Insufficient analysis of new datasets’ characteristics (e.g., error types, difficulty distributions).
- Paper presentation is lengthy; model descriptions could be more concise to improve readability.
- Requests clarification on hyperparameter tuning and dropout use in transfer learning experiments.
- Asks whether error analysis has been performed on yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop to guide improvements.
- Questions the rationale for choosing a dot-product scorer with smaller dimensionality for transfer experiments and whether alternatives were tried.
- Seeks details on methods and results for ensembling IR baselines with neural models.
- Inquires about the scalability of “dataset-sts” to additional f2-type tasks and required modifications for new data formats.
- Notes the reference list is extensive and inclusion of GitHub URLs is excellent.
- Suggests adding a summary table of dataset characteristics and quantifying potential overlap between Wang and yodaqa.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses and questions.
