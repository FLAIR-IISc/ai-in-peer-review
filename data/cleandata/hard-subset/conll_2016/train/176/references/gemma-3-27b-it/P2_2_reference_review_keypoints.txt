- Proposes a unified evaluation framework for sentence-pair (f2) tasks, including new datasets and transfer learning experiments.
- Introduces yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop to address shortcomings of existing datasets (e.g., Wang).
- Releases the dataset-sts software package to facilitate reproducible research.
- Provides a comprehensive baseline study comparing neural and non-neural models across multiple datasets.
- Demonstrates feasibility of cross-task transfer learning with positive initial results.
- Timely and relevant effort to standardize fragmented sentence-pair evaluation practices.
- New dataset appears thoughtfully constructed and addresses known dataset biases.
- Experimental setup is thorough, with attention to statistical significance and training details.
- Open-source release has clear practical impact for the community.
- Transfer learning results lend preliminary support to a universal semantic comprehension hypothesis.
- Models lack architectural novelty (mostly RNNs/CNNs), so main contribution is benchmarking/framework.
- Reported results lag state-of-the-art on some tasks (RTE, STS), limiting immediate performance gains.
- Overemphasis on ranking tasks; broader f2 task formulations (e.g., regression) are underexplored.
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis to explain model failure modes.
- Model description sections are overly verbose and could be streamlined for readability.
- Question: Why weren't transformer-based SOTA models (BERT/RoBERTa) integrated, and what are the challenges?
- Question: Please provide detailed error analysis for yodaqa/large2470—what pairs cause failures?
- Question: Have you tried joint multi-task training, and what were the outcomes?
- Question: Was any negative transfer observed in the transfer learning experiments, and under what conditions?
- Suggestion: Condense presentation and better justify hyperparameter choices.
- Overall assessment: Solid, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
