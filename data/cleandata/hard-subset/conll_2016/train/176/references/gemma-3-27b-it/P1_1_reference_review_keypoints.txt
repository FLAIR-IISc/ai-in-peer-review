- Paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring (f2 tasks) with a shared evaluation methodology and task-independent models with task-specific adaptation.
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address shortcomings in existing benchmarks, especially for answer selection.
- Presents a comprehensive evaluation of baseline models and demonstrates feasible cross-task transfer learning.
- Provides a consolidated benchmark and open-source software (dataset-sts) to improve reproducibility and consistent comparisons.
- yodaqa/large2470 is a notable dataset improvement over the small/noisy Wang dataset and offers a more realistic answer selection benchmark.
- Empirical results show transfer learning across f2 tasks yields statistically significant, though moderate, performance gains.
- Evaluation is thorough, covering IR baselines and neural models, and includes confidence intervals to address stochasticity.
- Practical implementation and open-source release are strong contributions for adoption and reproducibility.
- Methodology is rigorous with documented experimental setup, hyperparameters, and attention to noisy validation issues.
- Models used (RNNs, CNNs) are standard; the paper lacks novel neural architectures.
- Transfer learning improvements are present but not large; analysis of which tasks benefit most is limited.
- Limited exploration of transfer strategies (e.g., freezing layers) reduces insight into best practices for transfer learning.
- Lacks detailed ablation studies to quantify contributions of components like BM25 features or scoring modules.
- Discussion of weaker STS/RTE results is insufficient; reasons and remediation strategies are not deeply analyzed.
- Potential dataset overlap between Wang and yodaqa is acknowledged but not quantified or thoroughly analyzed.
- Requests analysis of which semantic relationships transfer best between tasks and directionality of transfer effects.
- Asks for quantification of overlap between Wang and yodaqa (e.g., percentage of identical QA pairs).
- Queries whether advanced regularization techniques were tried to prevent overfitting (e.g., variational dropout, early stopping).
- Seeks explanation for Ubu.RNN’s strong transfer performance and what makes the Ubuntu dataset an effective source task.
- Requests details on computational resources required to reproduce experiments (GPU memory, training time).
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses figures/tables effectively.
- Overall assessment: solid and valuable contribution; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address listed weaknesses.
