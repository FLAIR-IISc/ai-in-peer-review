- Proposes a unified framework for sentence-pair scoring across diverse f2 tasks with task-independent modeling plus task-specific adaptation layers.  
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address limitations of existing benchmarks like Wang.  
- Emphasizes rigorous evaluation practices, including statistical confidence intervals and comparisons to strong IR baselines.  
- Presents preliminary transfer learning experiments showing feasibility of training on one f2 task and applying to others.  
- Addresses the fragmented research landscape and inconsistent evaluation in semantic similarity tasks.  
- Commended for rigorous reporting and acknowledging the impact of randomness in neural models.  
- yodaqa/large2470 is a practical, larger, and cleaner benchmark that improves over the Wang dataset.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and provides a useful survey of existing models and datasets.  
- Releases the dataset-sts framework to promote reproducibility and future research.  
- Models used (RNNs, CNNs, Attention) are relatively standard with limited architectural novelty.  
- Transfer learning experiments are narrow in scope (single configuration, limited variants).  
- Excessive focus on implementation details in the main text detracts from higher-level insights.  
- Results do not reach state-of-the-art on some tasks (RTE, STS), raising competitiveness concerns.  
- Asks for concrete examples of challenges with Wang that motivated yodaqa/large2470.  
- Requests plans for extending transfer learning experiments (different architectures, freezing strategies, pretraining sizes).  
- Seeks hypotheses and remediation plans for lagging performance on RTE and STS.  
- Inquires whether more advanced retrieval baselines (e.g., transformer-based) were evaluated.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — strong framework and dataset contribution despite limited model novelty.
