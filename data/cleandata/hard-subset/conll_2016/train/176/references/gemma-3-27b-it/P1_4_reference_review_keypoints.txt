- Proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring (f2-type tasks) with open-source dataset-sts for reproducible research.
- Introduces new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address limitations of existing datasets like wang.
- Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation of diverse models (TF-IDF, CNNs, RNNs, attention) across multiple tasks.
- Demonstrates preliminary evidence that transfer learning (from Ubuntu Dialogue) can improve performance across tasks.
- Uses rigorous methodology (confidence intervals, acknowledgment of randomness) for meaningful model comparisons.
- Integrates practical elements (BM25 with neural models) and focuses on realistic, complex datasets.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents tasks, models, and results.
- Core neural architectures used are not novel; the contribution is primarily empirical/unifying rather than architectural innovation.
- Transfer learning experiments are preliminary and lack detailed analysis of which layers or strategies work best.
- Models lag behind state-of-the-art on RTE and STS tasks, raising questions about competitive performance on those tasks.
- New dataset reporting lacks some detailed statistics (e.g., sentence length distributions, vocabulary size).
- Requests more analysis on transfer learning specifics (which layers transfer, fine-tuning vs. freezing effects).
- Asks for explanation of why models underperform on RTE/STS and potential adjustments to improve them.
- Requests quantification and mitigation details for potential overlap between wang and yodaqa datasets.
- Requests disclosure of computational resources and training time used for experiments.
- Notes the GitHub URL was redacted and should be included in the final version.
- Recommends ablation studies to measure contributions of individual model components (dropout, scoring modules).
- Suggests broader discussion of related work in cross-lingual and multi-task learning for sentence pair scoring.
- Overall: valuable, reproducible contribution with strong empirical foundations, but limited model novelty and some analyses/details missing.
