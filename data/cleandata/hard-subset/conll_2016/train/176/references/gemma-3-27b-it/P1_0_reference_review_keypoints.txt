- Paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring (f2) and argues for task-independent models fine-tuned per application.  
- Provides a comprehensive survey of tasks/datasets and establishes baselines with neural and non-neural models.  
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address limitations of existing answer selection benchmarks.  
- Releases an open-source framework (dataset-sts) to facilitate reproducible research.  
- Empirically demonstrates cross-task transfer learning, showing models trained on one f2 task can generalize to others.  
- Well-motivated problem: unifying f2 tasks is relevant given fragmented prior work.  
- Thorough experimental setup with multiple models, datasets, confidence intervals, and attention to randomness.  
- Practical contribution through open-source code improves reproducibility and accessibility.  
- Clear, detailed methodology including hyperparameters enables replication.  
- Convincing critique of existing datasets motivating the new yodaqa/large2470 dataset.  
- Core models (RNNs, CNNs, Attention) are standard; no novel architectural contributions.  
- Transfer learning gains are present but modest; optimal transfer strategies not fully explored.  
- Heavy emphasis on ranking metrics (MAP, MRR) may miss other semantic representation evaluations.  
- Missing ablation studies, making it unclear which components drive performance.  
- Limited error analysis; more detailed failure-mode investigation would guide future work.  
- Questions: rationale for choosing Ubuntu Dialogue as source task and whether other source tasks were tried.  
- Questions: how hyperparameter tuning addressed validation/test mismatch and whether k-fold or other techniques were used.  
- Questions: details on yodaqa/large2470 data curation and candidate sentence quality control.  
- Questions: hypotheses for lagging performance on RTE/STS and potential remedies.  
- Questions: strategies to mitigate overfitting across multiple runs while leveraging best-validation epochs.  
- Additional notes: writing is clear but dense in places; related work could be more comprehensive.  
- Suggestion: include dataset/code links in the main paper body rather than footnotes.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution via framework, datasets, and analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
