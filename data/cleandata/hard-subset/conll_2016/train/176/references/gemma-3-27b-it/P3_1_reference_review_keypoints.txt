- Proposes a unified framework for evaluating sentence pair scoring models and argues for task-independent semantic comprehension models.
- Surveys existing tasks/datasets, establishes neural and non-neural baselines, and releases an open-source dataset-sts framework.
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address shortcomings in existing answer-selection benchmarks.
- Provides a structured, comprehensive survey of f2 tasks, datasets, and models to standardize evaluation practices.
- Releases an open-source toolkit (dataset-sts) to streamline experimentation and reproducibility.
- Uses rigorous evaluation methodology, reporting confidence intervals and comparing against strong IR baselines like BM25.
- Clearly identifies practical problems in the fragmented sentence pair scoring landscape and justifies new datasets.
- Presents a comprehensive range of baselines from simple overlap methods to RNNs and CNNs.
- Demonstrates effective transfer learning, notably from the Ubuntu Dialogue dataset to other tasks.
- Open-source contributions and emphasis on reproducibility are significant strengths.
- Model architectures lack novelty, relying on standard RNN/CNN approaches and existing attention mechanisms.
- Paper omits reported results for RTE and STS tasks, leaving an incomplete performance picture.
- Hyperparameter tuning details are sparse, reducing reproducibility of experimental choices.
- Lacks error analysis to explain why transfer learning works and to identify common failure modes.
- Requests results for RTE and STS to complete evaluation transparency.
- Asks for more details on hyperparameter search space and optimization strategy.
- Seeks information on regularization techniques explored beyond L2 and their impact on training stability.
- Asks for insights into why Ubuntu Dialogue is particularly effective for transfer learning.
- Requests an ablation study on overlap features to quantify their contribution.
- Suggests clearer model descriptions, correction of the redacted GitHub URL, and info on computational resources needed for reproduction.
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution focused on evaluation and reproducibility; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
