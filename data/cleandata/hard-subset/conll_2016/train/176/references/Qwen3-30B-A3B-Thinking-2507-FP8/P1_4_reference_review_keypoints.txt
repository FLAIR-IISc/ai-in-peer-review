- Summary: Proposes a unified, open-source framework (dataset-sts) and two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) for consistent evaluation of sentence-pair scoring and demonstrates transfer learning across tasks.

- Contribution 1: Provides a modular open-source framework for implementing and evaluating sentence-pair scoring models across multiple tasks.

- Contribution 2: Introduces two new datasets addressing size, candidate-set, and validation/test split issues in existing resources.

- Contribution 3: Advocates rigorous evaluation with 95% confidence intervals and multiple training runs to account for neural model variance.

- Strength 1: Strong improvement to evaluation methodology by reporting confidence intervals and multiple runs, highlighting high result variance.

- Strength 2: Practical dataset improvements that remedy small/uneven candidate sets and poor splits, enabling more meaningful comparisons.

- Strength 3: Open-source Keras-based framework lowers barrier for reproducibility and cross-task comparison.

- Weakness 1: Claim of "universal semantic comprehension" is overstated; transfer results are limited and not broadly validated.

- Weakness 2: Inadequate comparison to modern state-of-the-art models (e.g., transformer-based architectures like BERT/RoBERTa).

- Weakness 3: Lacks deeper analysis explaining why certain models (CNN vs RNN) perform better on datasets of different sizes.

- Question 1: How would you more convincingly demonstrate that learned representations are truly "universal" across diverse tasks and datasets?

- Question 2: Have you compared or considered comparisons with transformer-based architectures, and what challenges would they pose for your framework?

- Question 3: Why was yodaqa/large2470 set to size 2470, and how was this size determined to be meaningful for evaluation?

- Overall recommendation: Valuable contributions via framework and datasets; accept with minor revisions to temper universal-claim and broaden model comparisons.
