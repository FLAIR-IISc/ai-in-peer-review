- Proposes a unified framework for sentence-pair tasks and cross-task transfer learning, plus new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) and a dataset-sts software toolkit.  
- Main contributions: new high-quality datasets, unified evaluation framework, and demonstration of transfer learning from Ubuntu Dialogue.  
- Strength: substantial dataset contributions addressing small size, uneven candidate sets, and noisy splits in prior benchmarks.  
- Strength: improved evaluation methodology using multiple runs and 95% confidence intervals, highlighting variance in standard single-run reporting.  
- Strength: strong transfer-learning results showing Ubuntu Dialogue-trained models outperform task-specific baselines on other tasks.  
- Weakness: overstates claims of “universal” models—evidence limited to one source task and a few targets; models still show task-specific behavior.  
- Weakness: limited technical novelty of the framework relative to existing toolkits (e.g., AllenNLP, Stanford NLP).  
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative justification that yodaqa/large2470 is superior to alternatives; harder datasets ≠ better without diversity/coverage evidence.  
- Weakness: potential dataset leakage—shared ancestry with wang questions but no proof of non-overlap provided.  
- Question: provide quantitative metrics showing new datasets better represent tasks or yield more reliable evaluation compared to alternatives like WikiQA.  
- Question: provide broader evidence that validation performance poorly approximates test performance across multiple tasks/datasets.  
- Question: have you tried transfer learning from other source tasks (e.g., SNLI) to test generality beyond Ubuntu Dialogue?  
- Question: quantify candidate set size distributions and how uneven candidate sets impact evaluation across old vs. new datasets.  
- Conclusion: valuable datasets and evaluation improvements with promising transfer results, but claims about universality and framework novelty should be tempered and dataset justifications strengthened.
