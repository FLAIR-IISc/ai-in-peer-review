- Creation of new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address small size, uneven candidate distributions, and unstable evaluation in Answer Sentence Selection.
- Development of a unified evaluation framework (dataset-sts) for systematic comparison across sentence-pair tasks.
- Demonstration of cross-task transfer learning showing models trained on Ubuntu Dialogue transfer effectively to other tasks.
- Identification and correction of significant flaws in existing Answer Sentence Selection datasets, with detailed dataset analysis and lower baselines on new datasets.
- Rigorous evaluation methodology including reporting 95% confidence intervals and highlighting high variance in neural NLP results.
- Empirical evidence that Ubuntu-trained RNN models perform consistently well across multiple classification tasks.
- No novel model architectures introduced; paper evaluates standard RNN/CNN/attention models despite claiming advances toward universal comprehension.
- Insufficient comparison with recent state-of-the-art models (e.g., transformer-based or advanced attention architectures).
- Potential dataset leakage between wang and yodaqa datasets due to common ancestry, with no clear evidence of overlap elimination.
- Superficial analysis of transfer learning results without probing why some tasks transfer better than others.
- Request clarification on how yodaqa/large2470 differs from curatedv2 and evidence of no train/test overlap with existing datasets.
- Request explanation of what aspects of the framework (beyond evaluation) enable "universal semantic comprehension" given standard model architectures.
- Request analysis of how dataset size (e.g., Ubuntu) specifically affects transfer and performance relative to other datasets.
- Request recommendations for model selection when validation performance poorly approximates test performance on complex tasks.
- Overall: valuable contributions in datasets and evaluation methodology, but limited technical advance due to lack of novel models and insufficient SOTA comparison.
- Suggested improvements: more SOTA comparisons, deeper analysis of transfer learning, and explicit evidence ruling out dataset leakage to strengthen the paper's claims.
