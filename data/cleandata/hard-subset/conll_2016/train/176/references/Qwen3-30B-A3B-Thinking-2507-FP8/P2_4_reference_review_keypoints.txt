- Summary: Proposes a unified sentence-pair scoring framework, introduces two new datasets, an open-source evaluation toolkit, and evaluates cross-task transfer learning with multiple neural models.

- Contribution 1: New datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) intended to fix small size, uneven candidates, and split issues in existing Answer Sentence Selection data.

- Contribution 2: Unified evaluation framework (dataset-sts) and KeraSTS implementation for modular, cross-task comparison and reproducibility.

- Contribution 3: Empirical demonstration of cross-task transfer learning showing modest but consistent gains when transferring models (notably from Ubuntu Dialogue).

- Strength 1: New datasets address important limitations of prior corpora and provide larger, better-curated resources for Answer Sentence Selection.

- Strength 2: Advocates improved evaluation methodology by reporting confidence intervals to account for neural model variability.

- Strength 3: Shows transfer learning yields consistent improvements, suggesting learned general sentence representations rather than purely task-specific patterns.

- Strength 4: Practical, open-source implementation increases reproducibility and usability for the research community.

- Weakness 1: Claims of “universal semantic comprehension” are overstated given modest transfer gains (typically 1–2% absolute).

- Weakness 2: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art models on each task, leaving unclear competitiveness of proposed baselines.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient evidence that new datasets fully avoid issues like train/test overlap or data leakage.

- Weakness 4: Little model innovation—evaluates standard RNN/CNN/attention architectures rather than proposing novel architectures.

- Weakness 5: Inconsistent treatment of tasks (ranking vs classification) and lack of clarity how framework handles such distinctions.

- Question 1: Provide concrete baseline numbers (e.g., BM25 MRR) for yodaqa/large2470 to support claims of lower baseline performance.

- Question 2: Quantify overlap between Wang and yodaqa datasets and explain how train/test leakage was ruled out.

- Question 3: Report specific Ubuntu Dialogue metrics (MRR, Recall@1, etc.) to back claims of top performance.

- Question 4: Provide omitted RNN-only target-task training numbers to clarify the magnitude of transfer learning gains.

- Overall assessment: Valuable contribution via datasets, evaluation framework, and methodological improvements; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to temper universal-claims and add stronger SOTA comparisons.
