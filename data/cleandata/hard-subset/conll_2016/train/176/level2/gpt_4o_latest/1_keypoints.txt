- Proposes a unified framework for evaluating sentence pair scoring across multiple f2-type tasks.
- Introduces new datasets (e.g., yodaqa/large2470) to address size, noise, and split issues in existing benchmarks.
- Presents a task-independent model architecture adaptable via fine-tuning and evaluates RNN, CNN, hybrid, and attention-based models.
- Demonstrates cross-task transfer learning, showing models trained on one task can generalize to others.
- Provides an open-source benchmarking framework and tools (dataset-sts, KeraSTS) for reproducibility.
- Highlights the utility of neural models but does not introduce novel architectures.
- Models fall short of state-of-the-art on tasks like RTE and STS, reducing practical impact.
- Training exhibits significant overfitting and high variance across runs, raising robustness concerns.
- Framework scope is incomplete, excluding notable tasks/datasets such as Memory Networks and multilingual sentence tasks.
- Asks how authors will address overfitting and instability (regularization/architecture fixes).
- Asks why certain tasks/datasets were excluded and whether they will be added in future work.
- Asks whether joint multi-task training was considered to improve generalization.
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions focused on addressing overfitting and providing stronger SOTA comparisons.
