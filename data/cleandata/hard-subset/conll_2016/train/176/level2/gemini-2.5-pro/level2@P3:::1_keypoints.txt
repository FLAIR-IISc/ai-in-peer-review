- Proposes a unified "Sentence Pair Scoring" framework to consolidate multiple sentence-pair NLP tasks and argue for transferable, task-independent models.
- Provides a comprehensive empirical study comparing standard neural models (RNN, CNN, hybrids, attention) against strong IR baselines across multiple datasets.
- Advocates robust evaluation by running models multiple times (16) and reporting mean performance with confidence intervals to address high variance.
- Introduces two new large datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) and releases an open-source experimental framework to aid reproducibility.
- Establishes a strong cross-task empirical benchmark and shows transfer learning benefits from a model pretrained on a large dialogue corpus.
- Strength: Emphasis on robust evaluation and demonstrating that single-run results can be misleading due to high variance.
- Strength: Valuable community resources—well-motivated new datasets and a unified, open-source framework lower barriers for future work.
- Strength: Coherent unifying vision supported by broad experiments and inclusion of strong IR baselines; transfer learning results are compelling.
- Weakness: Lack of architectural novelty—benchmarked models are faithful implementations of existing architectures.
- Weakness: Transfer learning baseline clarity—Ubu.RNN uses a different architecture, making it hard to isolate pretraining effects from architectural changes.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of why models underperform on RTE/STS and what architectural features (e.g., cross-sentence attention, memory) are missing.
- Question: Please include results of the Ubu.RNN architecture trained from scratch on each target task to isolate pretraining gains.
- Question: Do the RTE/STS results suggest that more complex inter-sentence attention or other inference-focused components are required?
