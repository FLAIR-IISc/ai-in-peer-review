- Proposes unifying diverse NLP tasks under "Sentence Pair Scoring" and hypothesizes a universal, task-independent text comprehension learned by neural models.
- Supports hypothesis via (1) broad empirical study of standard architectures, (2) critique and improved evaluation resources, and (3) transfer learning demonstration.
- Main contribution: advocates rigorous evaluation (multiple runs, reporting means and 95% CIs) to improve reproducibility.
- Provides valuable resources: two new answer-selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) and an open-source framework (dataset-sts).
- Demonstrates effective cross-task transfer: Ubu.RNN pretrained on Ubuntu dialogue transfers well to other tasks.
- Delivers a comprehensive cross-task benchmark by evaluating consistent models across varied tasks and datasets.
- Strength: principled statistical evaluation that highlights high result variance on benchmarks like wang.
- Strength: practical community contributions (datasets and framework) that facilitate standardized comparisons.
- Strength: strong empirical support for transferability—pretrained model ranks among best across tasks.
- Strength: wide scope—ambitious coverage of tasks, datasets, and models in a unified setup.
- Weakness: limited architectural novelty—models are re-implementations and the "unified framework" is more conceptual/software than a new model.
- Weakness: performance gaps on SNLI and SICK raise concerns about limits of the learned universal comprehension or architectural mismatch.
- Weakness: inclusion of engineered features (BM25 score, token overlap flags) confounds assessment of neural semantic contribution.
- Weakness: transfer learning analysis is brief and lacks deeper probing of what is transferred and why.
- Question: provide answer-selection results without BM25 and overlap flags to isolate neural semantic performance.
- Question: why do models (including transferred Ubu.RNN) underperform on SNLI/SICK—dialogue pretraining vs. need for compositional/inferential skills or architecture issue?
- Question: have you tried pretraining on other large-scale tasks (e.g., SNLI) and transferring to answer selection or dialogue to explore asymmetries in learned skills?
