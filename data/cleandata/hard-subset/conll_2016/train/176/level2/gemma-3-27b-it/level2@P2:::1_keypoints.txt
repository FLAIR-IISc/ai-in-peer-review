- Frames Sentence Pair Scoring as a unifying f2 task for Answer Sentence Selection, STS, and Next Utterance Ranking, aiming to unify research, improve evaluation, and show cross-task transfer learning viability.
- Contributions: consolidates existing datasets, introduces yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, surveys baselines, and releases the dataset-sts software framework.

Strengths:
- Comprehensive survey of sentence pair tasks and baselines, including neural and non-neural methods for meaningful comparison.
- Introduces yodaqa/large2470 to address wang dataset limitations (size, imbalance, noisy measures).
- Releases an open-source, modular Keras-based framework (dataset-sts) to improve reproducibility and experimentation.
- Emphasizes statistically rigorous evaluation (confidence intervals) to address high variance in benchmark results.
- Demonstrates positive transfer learning from Ubuntu Dialogue to other f2 tasks, supporting general semantic representation learning.

Weaknesses:
- No novel model architecture or training technique; relies on established RNN/CNN models.
- Superficial analysis of why models perform differently across tasks and limited justification for hyperparameter choices.
- Over-reliance on pre-trained GloVe embeddings without exploring contextual or jointly trained alternatives; adaptation during training is not quantified.
- Future work suggestions feel disconnected or insufficiently justified relative to the f2 framework.
- Unclear details on how BM25 scores are integrated into the MLP scorer.

Questions for authors:
- How exactly was yodaqa/large2470 created and what filtering/selection criteria were used for candidate sentences?
- What steps mitigated overlap between wang and yodaqa, and how might overlap have affected results?
- Which layers were transferred in transfer learning experiments, which were retrained, and what regularization was used during fine-tuning?
- Besides selecting the epoch with best validation performance, what methods were tried to avoid overfitting given validation/test mismatch?
- What computational resources were used for training and evaluation to ensure reproducibility?

Additional comments:
- Writing is generally clear but could be more concise in places.
- Add a summary table of dataset characteristics for clarity.
- Include ablation studies to quantify contributions of components (dropout, scoring modules, etc.).
