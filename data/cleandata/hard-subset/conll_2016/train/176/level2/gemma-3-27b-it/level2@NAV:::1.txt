## Review of "Sentence Pair Scoring: Towards Unified Framework for Text Comprehension"

This paper presents a comprehensive review and benchmarking effort for the task of sentence pair scoring (f2), framing it as a unified approach applicable to diverse NLP tasks like question answering, textual entailment, and dialogue ranking. The authors identify a fragmentation in research, stemming from task-specific methodologies and evaluation metrics, and advocate for task-independent models with task-specific adaptation. A significant contribution is the introduction of two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, designed to address limitations of existing resources like the standard wang dataset (small size, uneven data distribution). The paper also provides a detailed empirical evaluation of various neural and non-neural baselines across these tasks, establishing a new benchmark for future research.

**Strengths:**

1. **Unified Framework:** The framing of diverse tasks under a single f2 scoring paradigm is a valuable contribution, promoting a more holistic understanding of semantic similarity.
2. **New Datasets:** The introduction of yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop is a key strength. These datasets address practical issues in existing benchmarks, offering larger scale and improved characteristics for robust model evaluation. The detailed discussion of dataset design and rationale is commendable.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The thorough benchmarking of a wide range of models (from IR baselines to CNNs and RNNs) provides a valuable resource for the community.  The inclusion of confidence intervals in the results adds statistical rigor, addressing a common shortcoming in reported NLP performance.
4. **Practical Implementation:** The creation and open-sourcing of the `dataset-sts` and `KeraSTS` frameworks encourage reproducibility and facilitate further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Models:** While the evaluation is thorough, the models themselves are largely established architectures. The core contribution lies in the unified framework and benchmarking, rather than novel model design. The transfer learning experiment, while promising, is preliminary and requires further investigation.
2. **Dataset Overlap Concern:** The authors acknowledge potential overlap between the wang and yodaqa datasets, which could introduce bias in comparisons. A more detailed analysis of this overlap and its potential impact is needed.
3. **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions tuning primarily on yodaqa/large2470.  It's unclear how well these hyperparameters generalize to other tasks and datasets. More detailed justification for the choices would be helpful.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the overlap between the wang and yodaqa/large2470 datasets, quantifying the extent of shared questions and their potential influence on the reported results?
2.  What considerations were given to potential biases in the YodaQA retrieval process that might affect the quality of the yodaqa/large2470 dataset?
3. Could you elaborate on the specific configurations and results of the transfer learning experiments beyond the brief mention in Section 5?
