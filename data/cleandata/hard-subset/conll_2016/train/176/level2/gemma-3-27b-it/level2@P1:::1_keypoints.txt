- Paper frames sentence pair scoring as a unified f2 task covering answer selection, entailment, and next-utterance ranking.  
- Main contribution is a systematic survey and benchmarking of IR and neural models across multiple datasets.  
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address limitations of existing resources.  
- Releases the dataset-sts framework (PySTS and KeraSTS) to facilitate reproducible research.  
- Evaluates models with rigorous methodology, including confidence intervals and strong IR baselines (TF-IDF, BM25).  
- Demonstrates effective cross-task transfer learning, notably pre-training on the Ubuntu Dialogue dataset.  
- Models evaluated (RNNs, CNNs, attention) are standard and lack substantial architectural novelty.  
- Paper does not provide detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art on RTE and STS, only noting it lags behind.  
- Hyperparameter tuning appears dataset-specific (primarily on yodaqa/large2470) without full sensitivity analysis.  
- The notion of “universal semantic comprehension” is vaguely defined and not rigorously operationalized.  
- yodaqa/large2470 may inherit biases from the YodaQA retrieval system used to generate candidates.  
- Reviewer requests direct comparisons of best models to current SOTA on RTE and STS tasks.  
- Reviewer asks for clarification on dataset creation and steps taken to mitigate YodaQA-induced biases.  
- Reviewer requests analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity across datasets and tuning procedures.  
- Reviewer asks for a concrete definition and measurement strategy for “universal semantic comprehension.”  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and has comprehensive references.  
- Use of confidence intervals in reporting results is commended as an improvement over single point estimates.
