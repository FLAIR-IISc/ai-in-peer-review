- Argues for a unified view of sentence-pair scoring across tasks (answer sentence selection, next-utterance ranking, RTE, STS).
- Presents a common evaluation framework (dataset-sts/KeraSTS) and two new answer-selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop).
- Benchmarks several simple neural architectures against strong IR baselines across tasks.
- Studies cross-task transfer and shows pretraining on Ubuntu dialog improves downstream f2 tasks.

- Proposes a rigorous, cross-task evaluation with standardized training/eval, 16-run reporting and 95% CIs, and competitive IR baselines.
- Releases two new answer-selection datasets designed to fix instability and small-split issues, with public tooling.
- Provides an empirical study demonstrating practical benefits of transfer learning from a large next-utterance ranking corpus.

- Reporting over 16 seeds with 95% CIs improves reliability and highlights variance issues (e.g., wang instability).
- A consistent setup across diverse f2 tasks with clear metrics (MAP/MRR, Recall@k, Pearson r) enables meaningful comparisons.
- Strong IR baselines and pragmatic ensembling (BM25 features, token overlap flags) set a realistic application bar and show where neural models add value.
- Open-source framework and datasets are likely useful for reproducible cross-task studies.
- Pretraining on Ubuntu substantially improves downstream results, supporting feasibility of general f2 representations.

- Limited modeling novelty: architectures are standard and do not reach state-of-the-art on RTE/STS, weakening the “universal comprehension” claim.
- Task-agnostic claims are confounded by reliance on BM25 pruning and overlap features; ablations are insufficient to isolate effects.
- Dataset hygiene concerns: possible overlap between wang and yodaqa is acknowledged but not quantified, risking train–test leakage.
- Analyses are incomplete: little error analysis, efficiency reporting, or detailed ablations on dropout, candidate pruning, and feature flags; negative results underinvestigated.

- Please quantify wang–yodaqa overlap and confirm no train–test leakage in reported settings.
- What performance do models achieve without BM25 pruning and without overlap features across tasks?
- Will you release scripts for dataset construction and provide fixed evaluation splits/checksums?
- What are the training and inference costs per model and task, and how do they trade off with accuracy?
