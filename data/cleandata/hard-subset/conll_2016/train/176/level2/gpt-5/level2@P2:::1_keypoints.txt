- Paper proposes a unified framework (dataset-sts/KeraSTS) for sentence-pair tasks with normalized datasets, adapters, models, and an evaluation protocol averaging 16 seeds with CIs.
- Introduces two new answer-selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) and analyzes instability of the wang dataset.
- Reports cross-task experiments showing strong IR baselines and that RNNs pretrained on Ubuntu Dialogue transfer well to other sentence-pair tasks.
- Strength: Provides a modular, task-agnostic framework that improves experiment comparability and reuse.
- Strength: Evaluation rigor—uses 16 random seeds and 95% CIs to address run-to-run variability, especially on unstable datasets like wang.
- Strength: Includes strong IR baselines (TF-IDF/BM25/overlap) and ensembles to fairly contextualize neural model gains.
- Strength: New datasets emphasize realistic, larger candidate pools to better reflect retrieval-like settings.
- Strength: Demonstrates transfer learning benefits and provides practical tuning guidance (dropout, label balancing, RMSprop).
- Weakness: Limited algorithmic novelty—models are existing architectures and a reimplemented attention mechanism.
- Weakness: Lacks thorough ablations on BM25 prefiltering and overlap-feature effects, obscuring neural models’ standalone contribution.
- Weakness: Dataset overlap, deduplication, and reproducibility concerns; release details redacted and dataset statistics/denoising procedures under-documented.
- Weakness: Thin comparisons on some tasks—only 10% of Ubuntu data used, lagging SOTA on RTE/STS with little analysis.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—typos, truncated sentences, broken references, and incomplete citations hurting readability and verification.
- Question: Quantify exact overlap between wang and yodaqa/large2470 and report any deduplication or re-runs if leakage exists.
- Question: Report MAP/MRR with and without BM25 prefiltering, with/without BM25 as feature, and without token-overlap flags; how often does prefilter remove positives?
- Question: Provide absolute/relative gains from Ubuntu pretraining with CIs, compare to multi-task training, and report freezing vs. full fine-tuning experiments.
- Question: Why use only 10% of Ubuntu training data; how do results scale with more data and compare to prior baselines (e.g., Kadlec et al.) under identical settings?
- Question: Release full dataset statistics, annotation protocols, inter-annotator agreement, licensing, and fixed candidate retrieval pipelines for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Add per-task ablations and error analyses to show where neural models outperform IR, especially for entity-heavy/compositional cases.
- Suggestion: Use paired randomization or bootstrap tests over queries for MAP/MRR in addition to run-based CIs.
- Suggestion: Clarify per-task hyperparameters, early stopping criteria, compute/time, and consider subword/char embeddings for OOV/domain jargon.
- Overall: Valuable infrastructural and methodological contribution contingent on stronger ablations, dataset documentation, and presentation fixes; modeling is incremental.
