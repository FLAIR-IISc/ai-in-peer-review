- Proposal: Frame multiple f2 tasks as “sentence pair scoring” and provide a unified evaluation/modeling framework (dataset-sts / KeraSTS) with task adapters and standardized reporting.  
- Contribution: Release two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) claimed to be larger/more challenging than wang and extend f2 coverage to property selection.  
- Experiments: Run extensive cross-task experiments with simple neural architectures, emphasize rigorous evaluation (16 seeds, 95% CIs), and show transfer from Next Utterance Ranking (Ubuntu) helps other tasks.  

- Strength: Provides a practical, open-source unified framework that improves reproducibility and lowers fragmentation in f2 research.  
- Strength: Rigorous evaluation methodology (multiple seeds, confidence intervals) and inclusion of strong IR baselines (TF-IDF, BM25).  
- Strength: New datasets appear more challenging (larger/uneven candidate sets, lower baselines), which could reduce leaderboard noise.  
- Strength: Demonstrates effective cross-task transfer learning (Ubuntu pretraining improves downstream performance).  
- Strength: Offers useful empirical insights (CNN vs RNN scale behavior, attention usefulness, training tips) for practitioners.  

- Weakness: Limited modeling novelty and omission of stronger cross-sentence/attention architectures, leaving SOTA advances for RTE/STS unaddressed.  
- Weakness: Potential confound from BM25 pruning and including BM25 scores as input, risking overdependence on a specific IR pre-filter without sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Dataset curation and label-noise/overlap issues (with wang/WebQuestions ancestry) are not sufficiently quantified or mitigated.  
- Weakness: Evidence for a “universal” model is inconclusive since best configs vary by task and models don’t reach competitive SNLI/SICK performance.  
- Weakness: Presentation gaps (missing figures/tables, typos, incomplete hyperparameter/compute details) hinder reproducibility.  

- Clarify overlap: Report measured overlap/deduplication between wang and yodaqa/large2470 across splits.  
- Clarify dataset quality: Provide manual validation or audit estimates of label noise for yodaqa/large2470.  
- Clarify BM25 dependence: Ablate sensitivity to BM25 pruning cutoff and to including BM25 as a feature.  
- Clarify transfer protocol: Give parameter counts, learning rates/schedules, layer freezing, and whether full Ubuntu training affects transfer gains.  
- Clarify token features: Quantify the isolated impact of boolean token-level features across tasks.  

- Essential fix: Quantify/mitigate dataset overlap, release deduplication scripts, dataset stats, label-quality assessments, and licensing.  
- Essential fix: Add BM25 ablations (vary k, remove BM25 feature) and report sensitivity.  
- Essential fix: Include at least one stronger cross-sentence attention/interactions baseline (e.g., bidirectional attention or ESIM) for comparison.  
- Optional: Report parameter counts, training time, and memory per model/task.  
- Optional: Explore multi-task training and improve presentation (proofreading, complete figures/tables, final code/data URLs).  

- Overall: Valuable contribution in resources, unification, and rigorous evaluation, but needs dataset/evaluation clarifications and a stronger attention baseline to substantiate the “universal” modeling claim.
