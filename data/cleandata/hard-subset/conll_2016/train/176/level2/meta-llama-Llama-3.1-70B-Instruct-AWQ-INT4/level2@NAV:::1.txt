**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Sentence Pair Scoring: Towards Unified Framework for Text Comprehension" proposes a unified framework for various sentence pair scoring tasks, including answer sentence selection, next utterance ranking, recognizing textual entailment, and semantic textual similarity. The authors aim to develop a universal text comprehension model that can be applied to multiple tasks. They introduce a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, and evaluate several neural network models, including recurrent neural networks (RNNs), convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and attention-based models. The results show that the RNN-CNN model and the attention-based model perform well on various tasks, and that transfer learning from one task to another can be effective.

**Contributions**

1. **Unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks**: The authors propose a unified framework for various sentence pair scoring tasks, which can help to reduce the fragmentation of research in this area.
2. **New dataset**: The authors introduce a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, which can be used for answer sentence selection and other tasks.
3. **Evaluation of neural network models**: The authors evaluate several neural network models, including RNNs, CNNs, and attention-based models, on various sentence pair scoring tasks.

**Strengths**

1. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors evaluate their models on multiple tasks and datasets, which provides a comprehensive understanding of their performance.
2. **Introduction of a new dataset**: The new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, can be useful for other researchers working on sentence pair scoring tasks.
3. **Effective use of transfer learning**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of transfer learning from one task to another, which can be useful for other researchers working on related tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited novelty**: The paper builds on existing work in the area of sentence pair scoring, and the models proposed are not significantly new or innovative.
2. **Overemphasis on neural network models**: The paper focuses primarily on neural network models, which may not be the best approach for all sentence pair scoring tasks.
3. **Lack of comparison with other approaches**: The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other approaches, such as traditional machine learning methods or symbolic AI approaches.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend your work to other sentence pair scoring tasks, such as paraphrasing or question answering?
2. Can you provide more details on the yodaqa/large2470 dataset, such as its size, complexity, and any preprocessing steps that were taken?
3. How do you plan to address the issue of overfitting in your models, particularly in the context of transfer learning?
