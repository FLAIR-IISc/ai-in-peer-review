- Proposes a unified framework treating various sentence pair tasks (answer selection, next utterance ranking, RTE, STS) as semantic similarity scoring.  
- Introduces a new, larger, more challenging answer sentence selection dataset (yodaqa/large2470).  
- Evaluates multiple neural models (RNN, CNN, attention) across tasks and datasets for cross-task comparison.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation helps identify model strengths and weaknesses across tasks.  
- Unified framework facilitates consistent evaluation and comparison of models for sentence-pair tasks.  
- New dataset provides a more realistic and demanding benchmark for answer sentence selection.  
- Omits comparison to non-neural baselines (traditional ML or symbolic models).  
- Lacks detailed analysis explaining why models perform differently (error analysis/insights).  
- Provides limited discussion of future work and directions.  
- Asks how the framework will be extended to other tasks and datasets.  
- Requests more detailed result analysis, including per-model error examination.  
- Asks how the authors will address overfitting in deep models.  
- Suggests improving the paper with deeper analysis, broader model comparisons, and more discussion of future work.
