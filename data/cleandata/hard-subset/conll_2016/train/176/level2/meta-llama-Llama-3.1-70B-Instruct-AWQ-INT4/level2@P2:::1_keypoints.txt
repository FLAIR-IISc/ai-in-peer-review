- Proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring to classify semantic similarity across tasks.  
- Reviews multiple tasks (answer selection, next-utterance ranking, RTE, STS) as instances of sentence pair scoring.  
- Introduces two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, intended to address shortcomings of existing datasets.  
- Implements and evaluates a range of neural models (RNNs, CNNs, attention-based) across tasks.  
- Shows models can learn a generic text-comprehension-like task and that transfer learning can improve performance.  

- Main contribution: unified evaluation framework for sentence pair models.  
- Main contribution: two new datasets to broaden evaluation and address dataset issues.  
- Main contribution: cross-task empirical evaluation of several neural architectures.  

- Strength: comprehensive review framing many NLP tasks as sentence-pair problems.  
- Strength: valuable dataset additions (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop).  
- Strength: empirical comparison highlights strengths and weaknesses of different model families.  

- Weakness: limited comparison to only a few baseline models, reducing clarity on relative gains.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis explaining why certain models perform better.  
- Weakness: reports heavy overfitting in some models, suggesting limited generalization.  

- Asks for deeper analysis of why models differ in task performance.  
- Requests plans or methods to mitigate observed overfitting.  
- Requests detailed information on the new datasets (size, complexity, preprocessing).  

- Overall: valuable unified framework and datasets but limited by shallow comparisons and lack of theoretical insight; future work should address these gaps.
