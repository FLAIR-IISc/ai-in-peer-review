**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring, a task that involves classifying a pair of sentences based on their semantic similarity. The authors review various tasks that can be phrased as sentence pair scoring problems, including answer sentence selection, next utterance ranking, recognizing textual entailment, and semantic textual similarity. They also introduce two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, which are designed to alleviate some of the issues with existing datasets. The authors propose a range of neural network models, including recurrent neural networks (RNNs), convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and attention-based models, and evaluate their performance on various tasks. The results show that the models can learn a generic task akin to text comprehension and that transfer learning can be effective in improving performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unified Framework**: The authors propose a unified framework for sentence pair scoring, which allows for the evaluation of various models across multiple tasks.
2. **New Datasets**: The authors introduce two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, which are designed to alleviate some of the issues with existing datasets.
3. **Model Evaluation**: The authors evaluate the performance of various neural network models on multiple tasks, including answer sentence selection, next utterance ranking, recognizing textual entailment, and semantic textual similarity.

**Strengths**

1. **Comprehensive Review**: The authors provide a comprehensive review of various tasks that can be phrased as sentence pair scoring problems, which helps to establish the context and significance of the research.
2. **New Datasets**: The introduction of two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, is a significant contribution, as they are designed to alleviate some of the issues with existing datasets.
3. **Model Evaluation**: The evaluation of various neural network models on multiple tasks provides valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each model.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Model Comparison**: The authors only compare the performance of their proposed models with a few baseline models, which may not provide a comprehensive understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of each model.
2. **Lack of Theoretical Analysis**: The authors do not provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed models, which may limit the understanding of why certain models perform better than others.
3. **Overfitting**: The authors mention that they observed heavy overfitting in some of their models, which may indicate that the models are not generalizing well to unseen data.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of why certain models perform better than others on specific tasks?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of overfitting in your models?
3. Can you provide more information on the yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop datasets, such as their size, complexity, and any preprocessing steps that were taken?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing, as it proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring and introduces two new datasets. However, there are some limitations to the study, including the limited model comparison and lack of theoretical analysis. The authors should consider addressing these limitations in future work to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of each model.
