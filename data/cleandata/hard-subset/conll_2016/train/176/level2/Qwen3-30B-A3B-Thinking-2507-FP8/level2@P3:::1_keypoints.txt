1. Proposes a unified, modular framework for sentence pair scoring tasks (Answer Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, RTE, STS).  
2. Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address size and split limitations in existing resources.  
3. Establishes a comprehensive evaluation methodology including 95% confidence intervals for reported results.  
4. Demonstrates transfer learning across tasks, showing performance gains and supporting a hypothesis of universal semantic comprehension.  
5. Identifies fragmentation in NLP research on similar sentence-pair tasks and offers a practical unifying solution.  
6. New datasets effectively remedy issues like small size and uneven splits, especially for Answer Sentence Selection.  
7. Open-source framework (dataset-sts, KeraSTS) is a valuable community resource likely to facilitate future research.  
8. Claim of “universal semantic comprehension” is overstated given limited supporting evidence.  
9. Transfer learning results are limited: demonstrated only with one model type (RNN) and one source task (Ubuntu Dialogue).  
10. Lacks thorough comparison with recent state-of-the-art models, particularly for RTE where models lag behind.  
11. Insufficient theoretical explanation for why certain models (CNNs vs RNNs) perform better on specific tasks beyond dataset-size effects.  
12. Needs broader transfer experiments across multiple source tasks and architectures to substantiate universality claims.  
13. Statistical analysis is stronger than typical work, but should include effect sizes alongside confidence intervals to show practical significance.  
14. Paper would benefit from more analysis on why the new datasets are more challenging and demonstrations of easy extensibility to new tasks/architectures.  
15. Questions for authors: comparisons with other unified approaches, additional evidence for universal comprehension, quantitative comparisons to literature best results, and performance on the Ubuntu Dialogue dataset.
