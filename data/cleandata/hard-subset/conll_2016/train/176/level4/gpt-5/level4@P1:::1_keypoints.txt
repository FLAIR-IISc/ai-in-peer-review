- Proposes unifying multiple sentence-pair tasks as a single "sentence pair scoring" (f2) problem using task-independent neural models with thin task-specific adapters.
- Contributions: a unifying experimental framework with stronger statistical reporting and IR baselines comparison.
- Contributions: broad cross-task empirical results and initial transfer-learning evidence (Ubu.RNN) for reusable sentence encoders.
- Contributions: open-source framework (dataset-sts/PySTS/KeraSTS) to promote reproducibility and reuse.
- Claim of new, more challenging datasets is stated in the abstract but not clearly delivered or documented.
- Strength: Timely and useful unification of diverse tasks under a common paradigm encouraging comparable evaluation and reuse.
- Strength: Broad evaluation across CNN/RNN/RNN-CNN/attention models and IR baselines; useful finding that CNNs suit smaller data while RNNs benefit from larger data.
- Strength: Methodological rigor by averaging over multiple random seeds and reporting confidence intervals.
- Strength: Transfer-learning signal where Ubu.RNN pretrained on Ubuntu often outperforms task-specific training.
- Strength: Open-source infrastructure lowers barriers and aids extensibility.
- Weakness: Incomplete presentation—at least one results table is missing/unfinished, critical for an empirical paper.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis and interpretation—needs more cross-task trend discussion, ablations, and concrete takeaways.
- Weakness: Dataset claim vs. delivery—new dataset(s) are not clearly presented, documented, or released.
- Weakness: Limited error analysis and cross-task insight—no taxonomy explaining why models succeed/fail per task.
- Weakness: Lack of qualitative examples and hard/edge case illustrations for each dataset.
- Weakness: Ambiguity around Ubu.RNN transfer gains—unclear if gains are due to dataset size or nature.
- Question: Which new datasets are actually introduced? Provide construction, stats, splits, licensing, and examples or revise the abstract.
- Question: Complete the missing results table(s) and fully document experiments.
- Question: What are the main failure modes by task? Provide an error taxonomy or case studies.
- Question: Do tasks truly share the same f2 phenomenon? Report measurable divergences (overlap sensitivity, length effects, domain mismatch).
- Question: Can you disentangle pretraining dataset size from nature (pretrain on large non-dialogue corpus; subsample Ubuntu; report learning curves)?
- Question: Will you report confidence intervals for all tasks/models in the final version?
- Minor: Use consistent terminology ("weight"/"weighted"), fix typos ("sentene"→"sentence", "world overlap"→"word overlap"), and replace "interpunction" with "punctuation."
- Overall: Promising and timely with meaningful methodological contributions, but currently unfinished; recommend publication conditional on completing tables, expanding analysis, clarifying datasets, adding qualitative/error analysis, and resolving Ubu.RNN ambiguity.
