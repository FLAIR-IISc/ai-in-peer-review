- Novel contribution: proposes treating multiple sentence-pair scoring tasks as instances of a single problem—modeling semantic relations between two sentences—and studying generally applicable neural architectures across them.  
- Overall assessment: exciting and valuable direction, well suited to CoNLL, but paper feels unfinished and should be revised before acceptance.  
- Condition 1: finalize the incomplete first results table.  
- Condition 2: substantially expand the discussion and analysis of the many reported results to explain patterns, implications, and limitations.  
- Condition 3: resolve the abstract’s promise of a new dataset by either providing the dataset/details or revising the abstract.  
- Condition 4: investigate and discuss why performance varies across tasks, and whether variability stems from modeling choices or substantive task differences—address whether a unified framework is appropriate.  
- Condition 5: include example dataset instances (prototypical and challenging pairs) to illustrate error modes.  
- Condition 6: clarify why the Ubu. RNN transfer model is recommended—determine whether this is due to task generality or dataset size and explain how practitioners can tell.  
- Recommendation: happy to see it published if the above issues are addressed; other reviewers may disagree.  
- Minor language corrections needed: fix “weigh/weighed” → “weight/weighted,” multiple misspellings of “sentence,” clarify “interpunction,” and correct “world overlap” → “word overlap.”
