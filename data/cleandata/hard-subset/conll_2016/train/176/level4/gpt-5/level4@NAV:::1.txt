This paper introduces, to the best of my knowledge, the novel idea of treating several sentence-pair scoring problems (e.g., Answer Set Scoring, RTE, Paraphrasing, among others) as instances of a broader task: understanding semantic relations between two sentences. It also examines whether generally applicable neural network models can be learned for this family of tasks. I find this an exciting direction that deserves presentation at CoNLL and further discussion and investigation.

My primary concern is that the paper feels incomplete. It should be accepted for publication only on the condition that the following updates are made in the final version:
1 - complete the first results table
2 - given the large number of individual results, the written analysis is far too brief; substantially more interpretation and discussion are badly needed
3 - the abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset that the paper does not appear to provide; this discrepancy must be addressed
4 - the results vary considerably across tasks—please investigate when and why the models fail on some tasks and succeed on others; even absent definitive conclusions, it would be useful to hear whether you believe this stems from modeling choices or from task dissimilarity; does it truly make sense to unify them?
5 - include example instances from the datasets used, covering both typical sentence pairs and pairs that are difficult to classify
6 - the Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is recommended for new tasks; is that due to the nature of the data (greater generality) or to dataset size? How might we tease this apart?

Despite its rough edges, this is an exciting approach that could spark valuable discussion, and I would be happy to see it published—IN A MORE FINISHED FORM.
I acknowledge that other reviewers may not share this view.
