- Frames diverse sentence-pair tasks as a unified scoring function and proposes task-agnostic neural architectures with lightweight adapters.
- Presents a shared evaluation framework with standardized baselines, metrics, statistical reporting, and introduces new answer-selection datasets.
- Demonstrates cross-task transfer: Ubuntu Dialog RNN pretraining adapts strongly to other tasks.
- Strength: clear and timely unifying perspective encouraging reusable models and systematic comparison.
- Strength: solid empirical framework—multiple architectures, IR baselines, 16-run averages, and confidence intervals improve reproducibility.
- Strength: provides practical tools (open-source dataset-sts and KeraSTS) to lower replication barriers.
- Strength: transfer learning evidence suggests promise for universal sentence comprehension models.
- Major weakness: incomplete presentation—first results table is unfinished and missing entries undermine empirical claims.
- Major weakness: insufficient analysis—needs deeper interpretation of model wins/losses and sensitivity to dataset characteristics.
- Major weakness: new dataset claim under-delivered—construction details, splits, stats, examples, and licensing are missing.
- Major weakness: unexplained variability across tasks; unclear if gaps stem from modeling limits or task dissimilarity.
- Major weakness: lacks illustrative example pairs (prototypical and challenging) from each dataset to show model behavior/failures.
- Question: provide full descriptions for new datasets (acquisition, cleaning, labeling, overlaps, splits, stats, license, examples).
- Question: when will missing table entries be added and will per-task confidence intervals be provided throughout?
- Question: what evidence separates modeling limitations from task divergence; can performance be correlated with dataset properties?
- Question: is Ubu. RNN’s advantage due to dialog nature or pretraining scale; have you controlled by pretraining on other large corpora or subsampling Ubuntu?
- Question: can you quantify contributions of attention vs. overlap features and the impact of token-level supervision?
- Suggestion: expand results discussion with per-task analyses, error typologies, and ablations (e.g., remove BM25, overlap flags).
- Suggestion: clarify evaluation protocols, tuning strategy, and report test-performance distributions for high-variance datasets like wang.
- Suggestion: release all code, pretrained models, and datasets with documented preprocessing to support reproducibility.
- Minor issues: fix wording/typos—use “weight/weighted,” correct “sentene”→“sentence,” “interpunction”→“punctuation,” and “world overlap”→“word overlap.”
- Overall: promising and timely work with useful tools and transfer results, but acceptance should be conditional on completing results, expanding analysis, and fully documenting new datasets.
