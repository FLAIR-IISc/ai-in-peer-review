- Summary: Proposes unifying sentence-pair tasks as modeling semantic relations and studies whether a single transferable neural architecture can span them, with promising but incomplete empirical exploration.

- Strength: Clear, compelling motivation to unify related tasks under a semantic-relation framework.

- Strength: Ambitious empirical scope across diverse benchmarks.

- Strength: Initial transfer-learning results are promising and relevant for low-resource tasks.

- Strength: Could provide a foundation for reusable sentence-pair encoders if fully realized.

- Main concern: Paper feels unfinished—presentation, analysis, and completeness of results do not match the ambition.

- Revision 1: Complete the first results table and present full results for all models/baselines with clear metrics and data splits.

- Revision 2: Substantially expand result analysis—summarize cross-task trends, report dataset variance and statistical significance, and contrast with strong baselines/prior SOTA.

- Revision 3: Resolve the abstract–content mismatch about the “new, more challenging dataset”: either fully describe and evaluate it or revise the abstract.

- Revision 4: Provide cross-task error analysis identifying common failure modes, success cases, and whether issues are modeling vs. task heterogeneity, supported by examples.

- Revision 5: Include representative examples from each dataset showing prototypical and challenging pairs that lead to errors.

- Revision 6: Clarify why the Ubu. RNN transfer model helps—disentangle task generality from data-size/domain effects via downsampling, alternative pretraining, and learning curves.

- Revision 7: Improve experimental detail and reproducibility by documenting dataset stats, preprocessing, tokenization, hyperparameters, training schedules, evaluation protocol, ensembling, and ideally releasing code/models.

- Suggestion: Discuss whether a single shared encoder with task-specific heads suffices or whether lightweight task adaptation (adapters, adversarial training) is needed.

- Suggestion: Run ablations (e.g., with/without attention or alignment modules) to identify components driving cross-task transfer.

- Suggestion: Unify notation and define all acronyms on first use.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept contingent on completing the requested revisions for camera-ready.

- Minor language/style: Fix wording and typos—use “weight/weighted,” correct “sentene”→“sentence,” “interpunction”→“punctuation,” “world overlap”→“word overlap,” and perform thorough proofreading for grammar and dataset-name capitalization.
