This paper presents, to my knowledge, a novel framing: treating a range of sentence-pair scoring tasks (e.g., Answer Sentence Selection, RTE, Paraphrase Identification, among others) as instances of a broader problem of modeling semantic relations between two sentences. The authors also explore whether broadly applicable neural models can be learned for this family of tasks. I find this an exciting and timely direction that merits presentation at CoNLL and further discussion.

That said, the submission feels unfinished. I would support acceptance only if the final version addresses the following:

1) The first results table must be completed. Please include all missing entries and ensure consistent metrics and splits.

2) The discussion of results is far too brief given the number of experiments. Substantially expand the analysis to interpret the findings across tasks, highlight key patterns, and provide comparative insights beyond raw scores.

3) The abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset, but the paper does not appear to deliver it. Please resolve this inconsistency by either including a clear description and release details of the dataset or revising the abstract accordingly.

4) Performance varies widely across tasks. Please investigate why the models succeed on some tasks and fail on others. Even a preliminary error analysis would be valuable. Is this primarily a modeling issue or due to substantial differences among the tasks (e.g., domain, label space, or data characteristics)? To what extent is it valid to unify these tasks under a single framework?

5) Include example instances from each dataset: both prototypical sentence pairs and challenging cases that lead to misclassification. Brief commentary on what makes these examples easy or hard would improve clarity.

6) The Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is recommended for new tasks. Is this advantage driven by the generality of the source task or simply by the size of the training data? Please clarify and, if possible, provide evidence (e.g., subsampling controls or matched-size pretraining) to disentangle these factors.

Despite its rough edges, the approach is promising and could spur valuable discussion. I would be happy to see it published in a more polished form after the above issues are addressed. I recognize that other reviewers may have different views.

Minor language and typographical notes:
- “Weigh”/“weighed” are used where “weight”/“weighted” are appropriate.
- “Sentence” is misspelled as “sentene” in several places.
- Please clarify “interpunction” (did you mean “punctuation”?).
- One instance reads “world overlap” instead of “word overlap.”
