2. Reviewer finds the proposal innovative and worthy of presentation and further discussion.  
3. Paper is incomplete and should be accepted only after revisions.  
4. First results table is missing and must be completed.  
5. Discussion/analysis of results is insufficient given many experiments; more detailed interpretation is urgently needed.  
6. The abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset but the paper does not deliver — this inconsistency must be resolved.  
7. Results vary widely across tasks; authors should investigate whether failures are due to modeling limits or fundamental task differences and whether a unified treatment is valid.  
8. Include example sentence pairs from datasets, showing typical and particularly challenging instances.  
9. Clarify why the Ubu.RNN transfer model is recommended for new tasks — is it due to model generality or dataset size, and how can this be tested?  
10. Minor language issues to fix: incorrect use of "weigh"/"weighed" (should be "weight"/"weighted"), multiple misspellings of "sentence", unclear use of "interpunction", and "world overlap" should be "word overlap".
