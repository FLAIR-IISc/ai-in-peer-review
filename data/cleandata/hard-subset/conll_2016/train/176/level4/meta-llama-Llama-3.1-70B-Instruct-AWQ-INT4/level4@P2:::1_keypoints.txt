- Proposes a unified framework treating various sentence pair scoring tasks as instances of semantic relation understanding between two sentences.  
- Investigates generally-applicable neural network models meant to transfer across multiple sentence pair tasks.  
- Introduces new datasets and evaluates multiple models, including neural networks and traditional IR baselines.  
- Paper presents an exciting, worthy-to-present idea with potential for further discussion and investigation.  
- Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation comparing diverse model types.  
- The new datasets are a notable contribution to the field.  
- Paper feels unfinished: several tables and results are not fully presented or discussed.  
- Written discussion of results is brief and lacks interpretation and detailed analysis.  
- The abstract promises a more challenging dataset, but that promise is not fully realized in the paper.  
- Results are inconsistent across tasks, requiring deeper investigation into causes of success or failure.  
- Paper lacks example instances from the datasets, including prototypical and problematic sentence pairs.  
- Requests more interpretation and analysis explaining varying model performance across tasks.  
- Asks whether model failures are due to modeling choices or inherent dissimilarity between tasks.  
- Requests illustrative dataset examples, especially pairs that challenge classification.  
- Queries whether the success of the Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is due to the nature of the data or dataset size.  
- Minor language issue: "weigh" and "weighed" used where "weight" and "weighted" are needed.  
- Several misspellings of "sentence" (appearing as "sentene").  
- Uses the nonstandard term "Interpunction"; should clarify or replace with a standard term.
