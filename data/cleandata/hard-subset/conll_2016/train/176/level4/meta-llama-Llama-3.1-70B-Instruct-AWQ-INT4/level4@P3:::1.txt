This paper proposes a novel approach to treating various sentence pair scoring tasks, such as Answer Sentence Selection, Recognizing Textual Entailment, and Paraphrasing, as instances of a more general task of understanding semantic relations between two sentences. The authors also investigate the potential of learning generally applicable neural network models for this family of tasks. This proposal is exciting and worthy of presentation at CoNLL, as well as further discussion and investigation.

**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks, which involves classifying a pair of sentences based on their semantic similarity. The authors review various tasks that can be phrased as sentence pair scoring problems, including Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, Recognizing Textual Entailment, and Semantic Textual Similarity. They also propose a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, which is an extension of the curated question dataset with candidate sentences retrieved from English Wikipedia and labeled by matching gold standard answers.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unified Framework**: The authors propose a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks, which enables the development of generally applicable neural network models.
2. **New Dataset**: The paper introduces a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, which is designed to address the limitations of existing datasets in the Answer Sentence Selection task.
3. **Model Evaluation**: The authors evaluate various neural network models, including Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), and Attention-Based Models, on multiple sentence pair scoring tasks.

**Strengths**

1. **Exciting Proposal**: The paper proposes a novel approach to treating various sentence pair scoring tasks as instances of a more general task, which is exciting and worthy of further discussion and investigation.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors evaluate multiple models on various tasks, providing a comprehensive understanding of their strengths and weaknesses.
3. **New Dataset**: The introduction of a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, addresses the limitations of existing datasets in the Answer Sentence Selection task.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Unfinished Paper**: The paper feels unfinished, with some results tables incomplete and a lack of interpretation and discussion of the results.
2. **Limited Analysis**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of how and why the models fail or succeed on different tasks, which is essential to understanding the effectiveness of the proposed framework.
3. **Lack of Example Instances**: The paper does not include example instances of the various datasets used, including prototypical sentence pairs and pairs that pose problems for classification.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of how and why the models fail or succeed on different tasks?
2. How can we determine whether the success of the Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is due to the nature of the data or the size of the dataset?
3. Can you include example instances of the various datasets used, including prototypical sentence pairs and pairs that pose problems for classification?

**Minor Points**

1. "Weigh" and "weighed" are consistently used in contexts that rather require "weight" and "weighted".
2. There are several misspellings of "sentence" (as "sentene").
3. What is "interpunction"?
4. One instance of "world overlap" instead of "word overlap".
