- Treats various sentence pair scoring tasks as instances of a general semantic-relation problem and explores learning generally applicable neural models.  
- Proposes a unified framework for sentence-pair classification enabling shared neural architectures across tasks.  
- Introduces a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, extending curated QA data with Wikipedia-retrieved candidate sentences.  
- Evaluates multiple neural models (RNNs, CNNs, attention-based) across several sentence-pair tasks.  
- Strength: novel and exciting proposal that merits presentation and further study.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across models and tasks.  
- Strength: new dataset addresses limitations of existing Answer Sentence Selection resources.  
- Weakness: paper feels unfinished with incomplete result tables and sparse discussion.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of why models succeed or fail on different tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks example dataset instances, especially difficult/prototypical sentence pairs.  
- Question: provide detailed analysis of model failures and successes.  
- Question: clarify whether Ubu. RNN transfer learning gains stem from data nature or dataset size.  
- Question: include example instances of datasets, including problematic pairs.  
- Minor: incorrect use of "weigh/weighed" vs. "weight/weighted."  
- Minor: several misspellings of "sentence" as "sentene."  
- Minor: unclear term "interpunction."  
- Minor: typo "world overlap" should be "word overlap."
