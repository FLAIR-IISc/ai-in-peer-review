This paper introduces a novel approach by framing various sentence pair scoring tasks, such as Answer Set Scoring, RTE, and Paraphrasing, as instances of a broader task focused on understanding semantic relationships between two sentences. Moreover, the authors explore the potential of developing generally applicable neural network models for this family of tasks. I find this proposal intriguing and worthy of presentation at CoNLL, as well as further discussion and investigation.

However, the paper's current form feels incomplete, and I recommend its acceptance for publication contingent upon addressing the following updates for the final version:

1. The first results table should be completed to provide a comprehensive overview.
2. Given the extensive number of individual results, a more in-depth discussion and interpretation of the findings are necessary to facilitate a deeper understanding.
3. The abstract mentions the presentation of a new, more challenging dataset, which is not delivered in the paper. This discrepancy needs to be resolved.
4. The results exhibit significant variability across different tasks. An investigation into the reasons behind the models' failures and successes in various tasks would be valuable. Even if no definitive answer is found, the authors' perspective on whether this is a modeling issue or a dissimilarity between tasks would be insightful.
5. Including example instances of the various datasets, encompassing both prototypical sentence pairs and problematic pairs, would enhance the paper's clarity.
6. The Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is recommended for new tasks, but it is unclear whether this is due to the nature of the data or the dataset size. Further analysis is needed to determine the underlying reason.

Despite the paper's unpolished nature, its approach has the potential to generate interesting discussions, and I would be pleased to see it published in a more refined form.

Some minor points regarding language and clarity:

* The consistent use of "weigh" and "weighed" should be replaced with "weight" and "weighted" in the respective contexts.
* Multiple instances of "sentene" should be corrected to "sentence."
* The term "interpunction" is unclear and requires clarification.
* The phrase "world overlap" should be corrected to "word overlap."
