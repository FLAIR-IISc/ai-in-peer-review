This paper proposes a novel approach to treat various sentence pair scoring tasks as instances of a more general task of understanding semantic relations between two sentences. The authors investigate the potential of learning generally-applicable neural network models for this family of tasks. This proposal is exciting and worthy of presentation at CoNLL and further discussion and investigation.

**Summary of the Paper:**
The paper introduces a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks, such as Answer Sentence Selection, Recognizing Textual Entailment, and Paraphrasing. The authors propose a general task of understanding semantic relations between two sentences and explore the potential of learning neural network models that can be applied to multiple tasks. They also introduce new datasets and evaluate various models, including neural network models and traditional IR baselines.

**Main Contributions:**

1. Proposal of a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks.
2. Investigation of generally-applicable neural network models for this family of tasks.
3. Introduction of new datasets and evaluation of various models.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper proposes a novel and exciting approach to sentence pair scoring tasks.
2. The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of various models, including neural network models and traditional IR baselines.
3. The introduction of new datasets is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The paper feels unfinished, with several tables and results not fully presented or discussed.
2. The written discussion of results is brief and lacks interpretation and analysis.
3. The abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset, but this is not fully delivered in the paper.
4. The results vary across tasks, and more investigation is needed to understand why models succeed or fail on certain tasks.
5. The paper lacks example instances of the various datasets used, including prototypical sentence pairs and pairs that pose problems for classification.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more interpretation and analysis of the results, particularly regarding the varying performance across tasks?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of models failing on certain tasks, and do you believe this is a question of modeling or dissimilarity between tasks?
3. Can you provide example instances of the various datasets used, including prototypical sentence pairs and pairs that pose problems for classification?
4. How can we determine whether the Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is recommended due to the nature of the data or the size of the dataset?

**Minor Points:**

1. "Weigh" and "weighed" are consistently used in contexts that require "weight" and "weighted".
2. There are several misspellings of "sentence" (as "sentene").
3. "Interpunction" is not a standard term in the field; please clarify or replace with a more common term.
4. One instance of "world overlap" instead of "word overlap" was found.
