- Proposes treating various sentence-pair tasks as a unified semantic-relation problem and exploring generally applicable neural models — an exciting, worthy direction.  
- Reviewer’s main concern: the paper feels unfinished and needs substantial revision before publication.  
- First results table is incomplete and must be fully presented for a comprehensive overview.  
- Written discussion is insufficient given many individual results; deeper interpretation and analysis are required.  
- Abstract claims a new, more challenging dataset that is not provided; include the dataset or revise the abstract.  
- Results vary widely across tasks; authors should analyze why models fail on some tasks and succeed on others and discuss whether a unified approach is appropriate.  
- Include example dataset instances, both prototypical and challenging cases, to clarify dataset characteristics and model behavior.  
- Clarify the recommendation of the Ubu.RNN transfer learning model—determine if its success is due to data characteristics or dataset size and explain how to tell.  
- Reviewer would support publication if the paper is more finished, though acknowledges others may disagree.  
- Minor language/editing issues: misuse of "weigh/weighed" vs "weight/weighted," repeated "sentene" misspellings, unclear term "interpunction," and typo "world overlap" should be "word overlap."
