- Paper proposes a unified framework treating many sentence-pair tasks as instances of semantic relation understanding and investigates generally applicable neural models.  
- Authors introduce a platform for aggregate benchmarking across multiple sentence-pair datasets.  
- Review finds the proposal exciting and worthy of presentation and further discussion.  
- Main concern: the paper feels unfinished and needs substantial updates before publication.  
- Complete the first results table (currently incomplete).  
- Provide much more interpretation and discussion given the large number of individual results.  
- Resolve the mismatch where the abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset that the paper does not present.  
- Investigate why results vary significantly across tasks and whether failures are due to modeling or task dissimilarity; discuss whether tasks can truly be unified.  
- Include example instances from each dataset, showing prototypical and problematic sentence pairs.  
- Clarify whether Ubu.RNN’s transfer learning success stems from dataset nature (general task) or simply dataset size, and explain how to determine this.  
- Weaknesses summarized: unfinished manuscript, large variability in task performance, and lack of dataset examples.  
- Strengths summarized: novel unified framework, exploration of general neural models, and the benchmarking platform.  
- Minor language issues to fix: use "weight/weighted" not "weigh/weighed," correct misspellings of "sentence," clarify or replace "interpunction," and fix "world overlap" → "word overlap."  
- Questions for authors: more result interpretation, plan for handling task dissimilarity, dataset examples, and explanation of Ubu.RNN transfer effects.
