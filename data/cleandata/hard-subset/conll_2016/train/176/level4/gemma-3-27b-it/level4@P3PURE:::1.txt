This paper proposes the novel step of framing several sentence pair scoring tasks – including Answer Set Scoring, Recognizing Textual Entailment (RTE), and Paraphrasing – as instances of a more general task: understanding semantic relations between two sentences. Furthermore, the authors investigate the potential of learning generally-applicable neural network models for this family of tasks. I find this to be an exciting proposal, worthy of presentation at CoNLL and further discussion and investigation.

My primary concern with the paper is that it currently feels incomplete and should be accepted for publication only with the proviso that several updates are made to the final version:

1. The first results table requires completion.
2. Given the extensive number of individual results presented, the written discussion of these results is insufficiently detailed. Significantly more interpretation and discussion are needed.
3. The abstract promises the presentation of a new, more challenging dataset, which the paper does not appear to deliver. This incongruity must be addressed.
4. The results exhibit considerable variation across different tasks. I recommend an investigation into the factors contributing to the models’ successes and failures on specific tasks. Even without definitive answers, the authors’ perspective on whether these variations stem from modeling limitations or inherent task dissimilarities would be valuable. Specifically, does grouping these tasks into a unified framework prove effective?
5. Please include example instances of the datasets used, showcasing both prototypical sentence pairs and those that prove challenging for classification.
6. The Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is recommended for new tasks, but it is unclear whether this recommendation is due to the general nature of the task or the size of the dataset. How can this question be resolved?

Despite its unpolished nature, this is an exciting approach that could stimulate much interesting discussion, and I would be happy to see it published in a more finalized form. I acknowledge that this view may not be shared by other reviewers.

Some minor points regarding language:

* "Weigh" and "weighed" are consistently used in contexts requiring "weight" and "weighted."
* There are several misspellings of "sentence" (appearing as “sentene”).
* The term "interpunction" is unclear and should be verified.
* One instance of "world overlap" appears instead of "word overlap."
