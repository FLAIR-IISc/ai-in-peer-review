- Proposes a unified framework for diverse sentence-pair scoring tasks and studies learning general neural models across them.
- Main contributions: formalized unified evaluation, new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop), and initial cross-task transfer learning evidence.
- Strength: comprehensive multi-task evaluation with IR baselines and confidence intervals for rigorous comparison.
- Strength: new datasets address size and quality shortcomings of existing benchmarks.
- Strength: positive transfer from a model pre-trained on Ubuntu Dialogue suggests potential for task-independent representations.
- Weakness: experimental discussion is superficial, tables incomplete, and lacks in-depth analysis of performance differences.
- Weakness: abstract overstates a novel dataset; paper appears more focused on improving existing datasets.
- Weakness: questionable validity of full task unification—need deeper analysis of task heterogeneity and single-model limits.
- Weakness: paper lacks illustrative example sentence pairs showing typical and challenging cases.
- Weakness: unclear why the Ubuntu RNN transfers well—dataset properties versus size not analyzed.
- Question: please provide deeper analysis explaining performance differences across tasks and models.
- Question: clarify the status of the claimed new dataset or revise the abstract accordingly.
- Question: suggest experiments to disentangle task characteristics from model limitations.
- Question: provide challenging example sentence pairs from each dataset to illustrate difficulties.
- Question: report investigations into why Ubu. RNN transfers effectively and whether this generalizes.
- Additional comments: fix minor language errors and ensure the referenced GitHub URL is accessible.
