- Proposes a unified framework treating diverse sentence-pair scoring tasks as instances of semantic relation understanding and explores generally-applicable neural models.  
- Reviewer finds the idea exciting and worthy of presentation but the paper feels incomplete and needs revision.

Major required revisions:
- First results table is incomplete and must be finished.  
- The written discussion of the many results is too short and requires much deeper interpretation.  
- The abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset but the paper does not fully deliver; this discrepancy must be resolved.  
- Models show considerable variation across tasks; authors should investigate why models succeed or fail and discuss whether the unified framing is effective.  
- Include illustrative examples from each dataset, showing prototypical and challenging sentence pairs.  
- Explain whether the Ubu.RNN transfer learning advantage stems from the dataset’s nature or its size and how to determine that.

Minor linguistic issues:
- Incorrect use of “weigh”/“weighed” instead of “weight”/“weighted.”  
- Multiple misspellings of “sentence” as “sentene.”  
- The term “interpunction” is unclear and should be checked.  
- One instance of “world overlap” should be “word overlap.”

Contributions (key positives):
- Frames diverse sentence-pair tasks under a unified framework encouraging generalizable models and evaluation.  
- Introduces the yodaqa/large2470 dataset as a larger, more balanced alternative for Answer Sentence Selection.  
- Provides systematic evaluation across many models and tasks and demonstrates effective cross-task transfer learning with confidence intervals.

Strengths (concise):
- Unified framework promotes generalizable modeling and meaningful comparisons.  
- New yodaqa/large2470 dataset addresses shortcomings of existing datasets.  
- Comprehensive evaluation of neural and non-neural models with confidence intervals.

Weaknesses (concise):
- Incomplete results table and superficial analysis of findings.  
- Dataset novelty/difficulty not sufficiently demonstrated beyond size and origin.  
- Insufficient explanation for why Ubu.RNN transfer learning performs well.

Questions to authors:
- Provide more detailed analysis explaining performance differences between models and tasks.  
- Clarify what makes yodaqa/large2470 novel or more challenging versus existing datasets.  
- Elaborate why Ubu.RNN succeeds in transfer learning and whether dataset size or task characteristics drive this.  
- Provide example instances from each dataset showing easy and difficult cases.

Additional comment:
- Address minor grammatical errors and verify the correct meaning/spelling of “interpunction.”
