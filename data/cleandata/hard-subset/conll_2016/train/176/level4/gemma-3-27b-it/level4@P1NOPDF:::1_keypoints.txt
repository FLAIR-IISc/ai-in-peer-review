- Novel contribution: frames multiple sentence-pair tasks as a unified semantic-relation task and explores generally-applicable neural models — an exciting and valuable proposal.
- Recommendation: accept only if several substantive revisions are made before the final version.
- Results table: the first results table is incomplete and must be finished.
- Discussion: the paper presents many individual results but has disproportionately short interpretation; much more analysis is needed.
- Dataset promise: the abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset which is not delivered — this discrepancy must be resolved.
- Task variance: results vary widely across tasks; authors should investigate why models succeed on some tasks and fail on others and whether the unified framework is effective.
- Examples: include illustrative dataset examples showing prototypical and challenging sentence pairs for classification.
- Transfer learning claim: clarify whether Ubu. RNN’s strong performance is due to model generality or simply dataset size, and propose experiments to disentangle these factors.
- Overall tone: paper is unpolished but promising and could spark discussion if completed.
- Minor language issues: fix misuse of “weigh/weighted,” multiple “sentene” misspellings, unclear “interpunction” (likely “punctuation”), and “world overlap” → “word overlap.”
