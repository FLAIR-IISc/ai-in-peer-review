This paper presents a novel approach – to the best of my knowledge – by framing several sentence pair scoring tasks (including Answer Set Scoring, Recognizing Textual Entailment, and Paraphrase Identification) as instances of a broader problem: understanding semantic relations between sentences. The authors further explore the potential of developing generally applicable neural network models to address this family of tasks. I find this a promising and valuable contribution, deserving of presentation at CoNLL and further research.

However, the paper currently feels incomplete and, in my assessment, should only be accepted with the understanding that substantial revisions will be incorporated into the final version. I outline these necessary improvements below:

1. **Results Table Completion:** The first results table requires completion to provide a comprehensive overview of the findings.

2. **Expanded Results Discussion:** Given the extensive number of individual results presented, the accompanying written discussion is insufficiently detailed. A much more thorough interpretation and analysis of the results are needed. The authors should delve deeper into the implications of their findings.

3. **Dataset Clarification:** The abstract promises the introduction of a new, more challenging dataset, but the paper does not appear to deliver on this claim. This discrepancy must be addressed, either by presenting the dataset or revising the abstract accordingly.

4. **Task-Specific Performance Analysis:** The results demonstrate significant variation in performance across different tasks. The authors should investigate the reasons behind these differences – specifically, why the models succeed on some tasks and fail on others. While a definitive answer may not be possible, exploring potential causes related to model limitations or inherent task dissimilarities would be highly valuable. This would also speak to the validity of grouping these tasks under a unified framework.

5. **Illustrative Examples:** The paper would benefit from the inclusion of example instances from each dataset used. These examples should include both prototypical sentence pairs that are easily classified and those that pose significant challenges for the models, offering insights into the models’ strengths and weaknesses.

6. **Ubu.RNN Transfer Learning Analysis:** The Ubu.RNN transfer learning model is recommended for new tasks, but the rationale for this recommendation is unclear. Is its effectiveness due to the inherent generality of the learned representations, or simply the size of the Ubu dataset? Further analysis is required to disentangle these factors and provide guidance on when this approach is most appropriate.

Despite these areas for improvement, the core idea presented is compelling and has the potential to stimulate significant discussion within the research community. I believe the paper warrants publication *in a more polished state*.

I acknowledge that my assessment may differ from those of other reviewers. 

Finally, I note a few minor language issues:

* The words "weigh" and "weighed" are consistently used where "weight" and "weighted" are more appropriate.
* There are several misspellings of “sentence” (appearing as “sentene”).
* The term "interpunction" is used; please clarify if this is intended to be “punctuation.”
* One instance of “world overlap” should be corrected to “word overlap.”
