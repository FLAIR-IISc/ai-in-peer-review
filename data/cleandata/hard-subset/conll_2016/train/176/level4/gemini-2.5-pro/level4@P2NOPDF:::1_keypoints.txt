- Proposes unifying diverse sentence-pair tasks (answer selection, RTE, paraphrase) under a single neural modeling framework — promising and significant.

- Manuscript feels premature and incomplete, more like a preliminary report; acceptance should be conditional on major revisions.

- Primary results table is incomplete and the discussion of results is too brief; deeper interpretation across experiments is required.

- No error analysis or investigation into why performance varies across tasks; need to determine if issues are architectural or due to task dissimilarity.

- Abstract promises a new challenging dataset that is not presented or described; include the dataset or revise the abstract.

- Paper lacks concrete examples from datasets, including prototypical and misclassified sentence pairs, to aid intuition and clarity.

- Recommendation of Ubuntu RNN transfer learning is underjustified; authors must explain whether gains come from source-task similarity or dataset size and how to disentangle factors.

- Overall idea is innovative and impactful but execution is insufficient for a top-tier venue without substantial revision.

- Minor issues: grammatical misuse of "weigh"/"weighed" vs "weight"/"weighted", multiple misspellings of "sentence", "interpunction" should be "punctuation", and "world overlap" should be "word overlap".
