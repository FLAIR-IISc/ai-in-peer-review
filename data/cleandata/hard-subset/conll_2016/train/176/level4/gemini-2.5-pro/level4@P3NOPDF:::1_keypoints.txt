- Proposes a novel, timely unified framework for sentence-pair semantic-relation tasks and a generally-applicable neural modeling approach.  
- Paper feels preliminary and unfinished; acceptance should be conditional on major revisions.  
- Incomplete results table and overly brief discussion—needs complete tables and much deeper interpretation of experimental trends.  
- Abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset but the body does not deliver; either introduce/use the dataset or remove the claim.  
- Models show high performance variance across tasks; require deeper analysis and error investigation to explain successes and failures.  
- Include qualitative examples (prototypical successes and challenging failure cases) from datasets to clarify model behavior.  
- Unclear justification for recommending the Ubu. RNN transfer model—must disentangle effects of training data nature versus dataset size.  
- Minor language/spelling errors to fix: misuse of "weigh"/"weighed" vs "weight"/"weighted", "sentene" misspellings, "interpunction" → "punctuation", and "world overlap" → "word overlap".  
- Overall conclusion: promising and discussion-worthy work, but requires completed results, substantive analysis, and consistency fixes before publication.
