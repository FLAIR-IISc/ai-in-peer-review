- Proposes unifying diverse sentence-pair NLP tasks (Answer Selection, RTE, Next Utterance Ranking) under a single "sentence pair scoring" framework.  
- Provides an open-source framework, new larger Answer Sentence Selection datasets, and extensive empirical evaluation across architectures and tasks.  
- Releases dataset-sts framework to lower barriers and encourage task-independent model development.  
- Introduces new large-scale Answer Sentence Selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) addressing shortcomings of prior benchmarks.  
- Advocates rigorous evaluation by reporting confidence intervals over multiple runs to account for neural model variance.  
- Demonstrates effective transfer learning: pretraining on the Ubuntu Dialogue Corpus improves performance on other sentence-pair tasks.  
- Ambitious, timely direction toward universal text comprehension beyond single-task models.  
- Provides concrete community assets (datasets and code) that improve reproducibility and benchmarking.  
- Employs sound experimental methodology emphasizing statistical rigor and variance analysis.  
- Comprehensive experiments show promising empirical results, particularly with transfer-learned RNN (Ubu. RNN).  
- Lacks deep analysis and interpretation explaining why specific models perform differently across tasks.  
- Presentation feels incomplete (missing table, typos) and lacks qualitative examples illustrating model behavior.  
- Transfer learning study is limited to one source task and one architecture, leaving unclear whether gains are due to dataset scale or task nature.  
- Question: Is transfer success mainly due to Ubuntu dataset scale or inherent conversational properties vs. other large tasks like SNLI?  
- Question: Can authors explain why performance varies by task (e.g., attention helps Answer Selection; struggles on RTE/STS) and whether this challenges the unified framework?  
- Question: Will all experiments be completed and will the camera-ready include expanded results and deeper analysis?
