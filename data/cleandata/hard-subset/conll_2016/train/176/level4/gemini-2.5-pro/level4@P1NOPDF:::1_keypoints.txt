- Proposes a unified approach to sentence-pair scoring and transferable neural models; idea is novel and compelling.  
- Primary concern: paper feels preliminary and incomplete; acceptance should be conditional on substantial camera-ready revisions.  
- (1) First results table is incomplete and must be finalized.  
- (2) Experimental analysis is too brief; a much more thorough interpretation and discussion of results is needed.  
- (3) Abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset but the dataset is not presented or described in the paper; resolve this discrepancy.  
- (4) Model performance varies significantly across tasks; include analysis (error analysis, probing modeling limits vs task dissimilarities) to explain these variations.  
- (5) Add illustrative examples from datasets, including prototypical and challenging sentence pairs, to aid intuition.  
- (6) Clarify recommendation of the Ubu. RNN transfer model: determine whether effectiveness stems from Ubuntu Corpus content or its large size and discuss how to disentangle these factors.  
- Despite shortcomings, the central idea could stimulate community discussion and merits publication if revised.  
- Minor language/typo issues: misuse of "weigh"/"weighed" vs "weight"/"weighted"; misspellings of "sentence"; non-standard "interpunction" (should be "punctuation"); "world overlap" should be "word overlap".
