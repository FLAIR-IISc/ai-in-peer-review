

This paper proposes treating multiple sentence pair scoring tasks (e.g., Answer Set Scoring, RTE, Paraphrasing) as instances of a unified semantic relation understanding task, and investigates learning generalizable neural models for this family of tasks. This is an exciting and relevant approach for CoNLL, with significant potential to stimulate discussion and future work. However, the current manuscript requires substantial revision before acceptance, as it feels incomplete in several critical areas. Below are essential revisions needed for the final version:

1.  **Complete and Clarify Results:** Table 1 is incomplete. The results discussion is overly brief given the volume of data; a thorough interpretation of performance variations across tasks is essential. The authors must analyze *why* models succeed or fail on specific tasks, addressing whether performance differences stem from task dissimilarities or model limitations. This directly impacts the core thesis of unifying these tasks.

2.  **Address Abstract-Content Discrepancy:** The abstract claims the paper introduces a "new, more challenging dataset," but no such dataset is presented or described. This critical inconsistency must be resolved—either by adding the dataset or revising the abstract.

3.  **Provide Representative Examples:** Include illustrative examples from *all* datasets used, featuring both typical sentence pairs and challenging cases that expose model weaknesses. This will significantly enhance clarity and evaluation.

4.  **Clarify UBU-RNN Transfer Rationale:** The paper recommends UBU-RNN for new tasks. The justification must be strengthened: is this due to the model's inherent suitability for the *semantic relation* task, or primarily because of the *dataset size* used in their experiments? The authors should discuss how to distinguish between these factors.

5.  **Strengthen Task Unification Argument:** The observed performance variation across tasks (e.g., high accuracy on some, low on others) necessitates a more explicit discussion of whether these tasks are fundamentally compatible within a single modeling framework. The paper should present the authors' position on whether the observed differences indicate a flaw in the unification approach or reflect task-specific challenges.

The paper's core idea is promising, but the current draft lacks the necessary depth and completeness to support its ambitious claims. Addressing these points will transform it from an interesting but preliminary note into a substantial contribution worthy of publication. The revisions are not merely cosmetic; they are fundamental to validating the paper's central thesis.

**Minor Language Corrections Required:**
*   Replace all instances of "weigh" and "weighed" with "weight" and "weighted".
*   Correct all misspellings of "sentence" (e.g., "sentene").
*   Replace "interpunction" with "punctuation".
*   Correct "world overlap" to "word overlap".

While this review reflects a critical assessment, the proposed work holds significant potential. With the necessary revisions, it would make a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance *only* upon satisfactory completion of the points above, as the current state does not meet the standards for publication. I recognize that other reviewers may have differing perspectives on the required depth of revision.
