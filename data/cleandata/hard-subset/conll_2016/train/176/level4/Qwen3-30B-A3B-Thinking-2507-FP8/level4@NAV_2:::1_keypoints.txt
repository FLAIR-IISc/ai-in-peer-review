- Summary: Proposes a unified framework for sentence-pair scoring and evaluates general-purpose neural models across multiple NLP tasks.

- Contribution: Introduces a unified semantic-relationship paradigm that treats diverse sentence-pair tasks as instances of one problem.

- Contribution: Adds new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) to address dataset size, uneven candidate distribution, and noisy evaluation.

- Contribution: Shows transfer learning with Ubu.RNN (trained on Ubuntu Dialogue) can be adapted to other tasks, indicating potential for generic text-comprehension models.

- Strength: Conceptual consolidation that could unify fragmented research and provide clearer methodology for the field.

- Strength: New datasets reveal lower baseline performance and mitigate prior dataset limitations, leaving room for improvement.

- Strength: Consistent cross-task performance of Ubu.RNN provides promising evidence for transferability of models.

- Weakness: First results table is incomplete, hindering full assessment of the many experiments.

- Weakness: Discussion lacks sufficient analysis of why models succeed or fail across tasks.

- Weakness: Abstract’s claim of a more challenging dataset is not convincingly demonstrated in the paper.

- Weakness: The paper fails to deeply investigate causes of differing model performance and task-specific failures.

- Weakness: Endorsing Ubu.RNN as a primary model is unjustified; unclear if success is due to dataset nature or size.

- Question: Provide example instances from each dataset (prototypical and challenging sentence pairs) for clarity.

- Question: Clarify whether Ubu.RNN’s success is driven by data generality or simply the larger training dataset, and how to distinguish these factors.

- Minor language issues: multiple typos and word-choice errors (e.g., "weigh" vs "weight", "sentene", "interpunction"→"punctuation", "world overlap"→"word overlap").
