

This paper proposes a novel approach treating multiple sentence pair scoring tasks—such as Answer Set Scoring, RTE, and Paraphrasing—as instances of a unified semantic relation understanding problem. The authors further investigate the potential of training generalizable neural network models for this task family. I find this to be an exciting and promising direction that merits presentation at CoNLL and further scholarly discussion.

However, the current version of the paper feels incomplete and requires substantial revisions before publication. I recommend acceptance only if the authors address the following critical issues in the final version:

1. **Complete the first results table**: The table currently lacks critical entries and must be finalized.
2. **Expand results discussion**: The written analysis is excessively brief given the volume of results. A thorough interpretation of the findings across tasks is urgently needed.
3. **Resolve abstract discrepancy**: The abstract claims to present a new, more challenging dataset, yet this dataset is not described or utilized in the paper. This inconsistency must be corrected.
4. **Analyze task-specific performance variation**: Results show significant variation across tasks. The authors should investigate *why* models succeed or fail on specific tasks. Is this due to modeling limitations, or inherent differences between the tasks themselves? Clarifying whether these tasks truly form a unified family is essential.
5. **Include illustrative examples**: Provide concrete examples from all datasets, including both typical sentence pairs and challenging cases that the model struggles with.
6. **Clarify Ubu.RNN transfer learning rationale**: The paper recommends Ubu.RNN for new tasks. Is this due to the task nature (e.g., semantic relation complexity) or dataset size? The authors should explain how to distinguish between these factors.

Despite these shortcomings, the core proposal is compelling and could spark significant discussion in the community. I would strongly support publication pending substantial revisions to achieve a more polished final version.
