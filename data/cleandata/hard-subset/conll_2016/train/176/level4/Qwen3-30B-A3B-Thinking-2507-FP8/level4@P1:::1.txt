

This paper proposes treating a range of sentence pair scoring tasks (e.g., Answer Sentence Selection, Recognizing Textual Entailment, Paraphrasing) as instances of a unified semantic relation understanding problem. The authors investigate whether generally applicable neural network models can be developed for this family of tasks. I find this to be an exciting and promising direction that merits presentation at CoNLL and further investigation.

However, the paper currently feels incomplete and requires significant revisions before acceptance. Specifically:

1. The first results table is incomplete, preventing proper evaluation of the proposed framework.

2. The paper contains numerous results but offers minimal interpretation or discussion. A thorough analysis of why certain models succeed or fail across different tasks is sorely needed. The authors should explain not just what the results are, but why they matter—particularly given the substantial variation in model performance across different tasks.

3. The abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset, but the paper fails to deliver on this claim. The introduced datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) appear to be extensions of existing resources rather than substantially new challenges. This discrepancy between the abstract's promise and the paper's content needs to be addressed.

4. The paper demonstrates that model performance varies significantly across different tasks. The authors should investigate whether these differences stem from fundamental task dissimilarities or limitations in the modeling approach. A more nuanced discussion of whether these tasks truly form a unified family would strengthen the paper's central claim.

5. The paper lacks concrete examples of the datasets used. Including both prototypical sentence pairs and challenging examples (where models fail) would greatly enhance the reader's understanding of the tasks' nature and the models' limitations.

6. The authors recommend the Ubu. RNN transfer learning model for new tasks. However, it's unclear whether this recommendation stems from the task's nature or the dataset's size. The authors should clarify whether the success of transfer learning is due to the specific characteristics of the Ubuntu Dialogue dataset or the model architecture itself, and provide evidence to support their position.

Despite these significant shortcomings, the paper presents a valuable conceptual contribution that could generate meaningful discussion. I would be happy to see this work published in a more complete form. The core idea of unifying sentence pair scoring tasks and exploring transfer learning across them is compelling, and the proposed framework has merit.

Minor language issues to address:
- Consistently use "weight" instead of "weigh" and "weighted" instead of "weighed"
- Correct all instances of "sentene" to "sentence"
- Replace "interpunction" with "punctuation"
- Fix "world overlap" to "word overlap" throughout the paper
