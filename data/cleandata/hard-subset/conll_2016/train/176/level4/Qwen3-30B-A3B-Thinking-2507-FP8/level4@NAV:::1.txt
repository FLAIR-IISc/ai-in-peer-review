

This paper presents a novel (to my knowledge) approach by framing multiple sentence pair scoring tasks (e.g., Answer Set Scoring, RTE, Paraphrasing) as instances of a broader semantic relation understanding task between two sentences. It further explores the viability of developing generally applicable neural network models for this task family. I consider this a compelling proposal meriting presentation at CoNLL and further discussion.  
The primary concern is that the paper feels incomplete. Acceptance should be contingent upon the following revisions for the final version:  
1. Completion of the first results table.  
2. Substantially expanded discussion of results, as the current written analysis is overly brief given the extensive results.  
3. Resolution of the discrepancy between the abstract’s promise of a new, more challenging dataset and the paper’s actual content.  
4. Investigation into the variable model performance across tasks: an analysis of why models succeed or fail on specific tasks (e.g., whether this stems from modeling limitations or inherent task dissimilarities) is needed, even if no definitive answer is found.  
5. Inclusion of illustrative examples from all datasets, featuring both typical sentence pairs and problematic cases.  
6. Clarification on why the Ubu. RNN transfer learning model is recommended for new tasks—is this due to the task nature or dataset size? A method to distinguish between these factors should be proposed.  
Despite its current unfinished state, the approach holds significant promise for fostering meaningful discussion. I support publication **in a more finished form**.  
Minor language corrections:  
* Replace "weigh"/"weighed" with "weight"/"weighted" throughout.  
* Correct repeated misspellings of "sentence" as "sentene".  
* Replace "interpunction" with "punctuation".  
* Correct "world overlap" to "word overlap".  
I acknowledge this perspective may not align with other reviewers' assessments.
