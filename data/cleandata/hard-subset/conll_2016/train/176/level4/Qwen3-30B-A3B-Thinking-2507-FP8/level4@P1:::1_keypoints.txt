- Promising idea: unify various sentence-pair tasks as a semantic relation understanding problem and explore general neural models and transfer learning.

- Paper feels incomplete and requires significant revisions before acceptance.

- Incomplete first results table prevents proper evaluation of the proposed framework.

- Numerous results are reported but lack interpretation; need analysis explaining why models succeed or fail across tasks.

- Abstract claims a new challenging dataset, but introduced datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) appear to be extensions rather than genuinely new challenges.

- Need investigation into whether performance variation reflects fundamental task dissimilarities or limitations of the modeling approach, and whether tasks truly form a unified family.

- Paper lacks concrete dataset examples; include prototypical and challenging sentence pairs (especially failure cases) to clarify task nature and model limitations.

- Recommendation of the Ubu. RNN transfer model is unclear; clarify if its success is due to Ubuntu dataset characteristics, dataset size, or the architecture, and provide supporting evidence.

- Despite shortcomings, the conceptual contribution is valuable and merits publication in a more complete form.

- Minor language fixes needed: use "weight"/"weighted", correct "sentene"→"sentence", "interpunction"→"punctuation", and "world overlap"→"word overlap".
