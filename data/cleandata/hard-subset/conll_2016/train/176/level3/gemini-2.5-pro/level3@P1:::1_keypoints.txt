- Proposes a unified "Sentence Pair Scoring" framework covering Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, RTE, and STS, with goals to unify research, improve evaluation, and enable cross-task transfer.

- Introduces two new Answer Sentence Selection datasets and an open-source evaluation framework.

- Evaluates a suite of neural models (RNN, CNN, hybrid, attention) and strong IR baselines across multiple datasets.

- Key empirical finding: pre-training on the Ubuntu Dialogue Corpus yields a model (Ubu.RNN) that transfers well to other sentence-pair tasks, suggesting learnable general text comprehension.

- Contribution: reframing many sentence-level tasks as a single problem to encourage general semantic models and a common benchmark.

- Contribution: highlights instability of single-run evaluations on small datasets and advocates reporting means and confidence intervals over multiple runs.

- Contribution: provides larger, more challenging QA datasets and a reusable software framework to support reproducible research.

- Strength: ambitious, timely vision aligning with current pre-training and transfer-learning trends.

- Strength: strong emphasis on robust, statistically sound evaluation practices.

- Strength: valuable community resources (datasets and code) that address deficiencies in existing benchmarks.

- Weakness: results are incompletely presented and under-analyzed; paper offers little explanation for task-dependent performance differences.

- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis and example cases to substantiate claims about "text comprehension" and to illustrate model errors.

- Weakness: unclear presentation and missing/incomplete results tables; manuscript needs polishing and fuller exploration of new datasets' characteristics.

- Question: Do varying task performances imply a single universal architecture is insufficient, or that task-specific components are needed within a unified framework?

- Question: Is Ubuntu transfer success due to dataset size or conversational data properties, and how would you design experiments to separate these factors?

- Question: Please include complete results tables in the final version to enable full assessment.

- Question: Provide qualitative failure examples for Ubu.RNN across tasks to clarify current limitations of the proposed "universal" models.

- Additional: Correct typos—use "weighted" (not "weighed"), "sentence" (not "sentene"), "punctuation" (not "interpunction"), and "word overlap" (not "world overlap").
