- Proposes a unified framework treating various sentence-pair tasks as a single semantic relation scoring problem.  
- Main contributions: unified evaluation framework, two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop), and demonstration of transfer learning across tasks.  
- Strength: framework addresses fragmented research landscape and shows a single model architecture can adapt to multiple tasks with task-specific outputs.  
- Strength: new datasets aim to fix limitations of existing benchmarks (small size, uneven candidate distributions).  
- Strength: transfer learning (Ubu.RNN) shows promising cross-task gains, suggesting potential for general-purpose semantic models.  
- Weakness: Claim of a “new, more challenging dataset” is unsupported and not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Weakness: yodaqa/large2470 is not clearly shown to be significantly more challenging than existing datasets like Wang or WikiQA.  
- Weakness: Results section incomplete (missing first results table) and overall discussion of results is too brief.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why models succeed on some tasks and fail on others; unclear coherence of unified task grouping.  
- Weakness: Transfer learning claim lacks clarification on whether gains stem from dataset size, dataset characteristics, or true model generalizability.  
- Weakness: Models underperform state-of-the-art on RTE and STS tasks; this is not addressed.  
- Required revision: Provide clear justification and metrics showing how yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop improve over existing benchmarks.  
- Required revision: Complete the missing results table and expand analysis of model performance across tasks, explaining task-specific successes/failures.  
- Required revision: Strengthen transfer learning evidence with ablation studies or comparative experiments to isolate causes of transfer gains.  
- Additional minor issues: Fix language/typos (e.g., “weigh” vs. “weight,” “interpunction,” “world overlap” → “word overlap”).  
- Decision summary: Promising and novel but incomplete; requires major revisions before acceptance.  
- Questions for authors: clarify how yodaqa/large2470 addresses Wang’s limitations, analyze task-specific model performance differences, and provide evidence distinguishing dataset-size vs. dataset-characteristic vs. model-generalizability effects on Ubu.RNN’s success.
