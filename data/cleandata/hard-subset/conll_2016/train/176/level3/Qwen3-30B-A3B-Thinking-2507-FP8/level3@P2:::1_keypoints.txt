- Proposes a unified framework and software package (dataset-sts) for evaluating sentence-pair scoring across multiple tasks.  
- Introduces two new Answer Sentence Selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address limitations of existing datasets.  
- Demonstrates transfer learning: models pretrained on Ubuntu Dialogue can be fine-tuned for other sentence-pair tasks.  
- Strength: Provides a common evaluation platform to reduce fragmentation and enable meaningful cross-task comparisons.  
- Strength: Uses careful statistical analysis (95% confidence intervals) to account for randomness in neural model performance.  
- Strength: New datasets appear larger and more realistic than prior Wang dataset, promising better benchmarking.  
- Weakness: Dataset presentation is inconsistent with the abstract’s claim of a “more challenging” dataset and lacks adequate demonstration.  
- Weakness: Result discussion is shallow, offering little interpretation of why models succeed or fail across tasks.  
- Weakness: First results table is incomplete, preventing proper evaluation of model performance.  
- Weakness: Transfer learning claim (Ubu.RNN) is under-supported; unclear if gains stem from model generality or dataset size.  
- Weakness: Paper is underdeveloped and needs substantial polishing and completion before publication.  
- Question: Please clarify the discrepancy between the abstract’s dataset claims and the paper’s dataset presentation.  
- Question: Provide example instances (prototypical and problematic pairs) from each dataset to illustrate their characteristics.  
- Question: Suggest experiments to determine whether Ubu.RNN’s transfer success is due to model generality or the Ubuntu dataset’s size.
