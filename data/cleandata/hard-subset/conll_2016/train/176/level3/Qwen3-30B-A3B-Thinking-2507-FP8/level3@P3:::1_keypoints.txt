- Proposes a unified semantic-relation framework for diverse sentence-pair tasks and minimal task-specific adaptation for neural models.  
- Contributes a modular software framework (dataset-sts) for systematic model-task comparisons.  
- Introduces new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) to address limitations in existing Answer Sentence Selection data.  
- Demonstrates cross-task transfer learning with Ubu.RNN, suggesting neural models can learn general text comprehension.  
- Novel unified framework addresses fragmentation in the literature and provides a useful resource for future work.  
- New datasets meaningfully tackle size, candidate set unevenness, and noisy evaluation issues in prior datasets.  
- Transfer learning results are promising and indicate potential for universal semantic comprehension models.  
- Abstract promises a challenging new dataset but the paper fails to present or evaluate it, creating a serious inconsistency.  
- Results tables are incomplete—missing entries for the new datasets—undermining the claims.  
- Analysis of results is too brief; authors do not investigate why models succeed or fail across tasks.  
- Paper does not disentangle whether Ubu.RNN’s performance stems from dataset size, dataset nature, or transfer learning.  
- Manuscript contains numerous language errors and lacks example instances from datasets to illustrate model behavior.  
- Paper feels unfinished: underdeveloped results, shallow discussion, and needs substantial polishing.  
- Requests clarification and results for the claimed new dataset(s).  
- Requests example prototypical and problematic dataset pairs to aid interpretation.  
- Requests experiments or analysis to determine why Ubu.RNN performs best (dataset vs. method).  
- Recommendation: promising core idea but unsuitable for publication until major revisions, completion, and polishing are done.
