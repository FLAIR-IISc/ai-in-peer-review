## Review of "Sentence Pair Scoring: Towards Unified Framework for Text Comprehension"

This paper proposes a unified framework for tackling various sentence pair scoring tasks, framing them as a general semantic relation problem solvable with adaptable neural models. The core idea – treating tasks like Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, and Recognizing Textual Entailment under a single umbrella – is promising and could streamline research in this area. The authors also introduce two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, intended to address shortcomings in existing benchmarks.

The primary contribution of this work, as I see it, lies in the attempt to consolidate a fragmented research landscape. While the idea of a unified framework isn’t entirely novel, the authors’ effort to provide a common evaluation platform (dataset-sts and KeraSTS) and a comprehensive baseline comparison is valuable. A secondary contribution is the introduction of the yodaqa/large2470 dataset, which, if validated, could provide a more challenging benchmark for answer selection.

However, the paper currently feels incomplete and requires substantial revision before publication. A major issue is the inconsistency regarding the promised new datasets. The abstract claims a “more challenging dataset,” but the results presented don’t fully support this claim, and the discussion of baseline performance on these datasets is limited. Furthermore, the first results table is incomplete, hindering a proper assessment of the reported performance. 

The discussion of results is far too brief. The wide performance variability across tasks demands a deeper investigation into *why* models succeed on some and fail on others. Do these tasks genuinely form a unified group, or are there inherent differences that make a single model ill-suited for all? The authors need to analyze these discrepancies and provide insightful interpretations. The claim regarding Ubu.RNN’s strong performance needs clarification – is it due to the dataset’s generality or simply its size? Proposing experiments to disentangle these factors is crucial.

Finally, the paper suffers from minor polish issues: inconsistent use of “weigh/weighted” vs. “weight/weighted,” misspellings of “sentence,” an unclear term “interpunction,” and “world overlap” should be corrected to “word overlap.” Example instances from each dataset, showcasing both prototypical and problematic pairs, would greatly enhance understanding.



**Questions to Authors:**

*   Can you provide a completed version of the first results table?
*   Please elaborate on the characteristics of the yodaqa/large2470 dataset that make it more challenging than existing benchmarks.
*   What experiments could be conducted to determine whether Ubu.RNN’s success is due to dataset generality or size?
*   Can you provide example sentence pairs from each dataset, illustrating both successful and failing cases for your models?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The URL for the dataset-sts framework was redacted. Please provide it.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the proposed framework.
