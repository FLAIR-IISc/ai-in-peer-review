**Paper Review: Sentence Pair Scoring: Towards Unified Framework for Text Comprehension**

**Summary:**
This paper proposes a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks, which are commonly found in various NLP applications such as answer sentence selection, next utterance ranking, recognizing textual entailment, and semantic textual similarity. The authors introduce a novel approach to treat these tasks as a unified semantic-relation problem, leveraging generally applicable neural models. They also present a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, and evaluate various neural network models on multiple tasks.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Unified Framework**: The authors propose a unified framework for sentence pair scoring tasks, which enables the use of generally applicable neural models across multiple tasks.
2. **New Dataset**: They introduce a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, which is designed to alleviate the problems of existing datasets, such as small size and uneven distribution of sentence pairs.
3. **Model Evaluation**: The authors evaluate various neural network models on multiple tasks, including answer sentence selection, next utterance ranking, recognizing textual entailment, and semantic textual similarity.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel Proposal**: The authors' proposal to treat sentence pair scoring tasks as a unified semantic-relation problem is novel and exciting.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of various neural network models on multiple tasks, providing valuable insights into their strengths and weaknesses.
3. **New Dataset**: The introduction of a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, is a significant contribution to the field, as it addresses the limitations of existing datasets.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incomplete Results**: The first results table is incomplete and must be completed to provide a comprehensive understanding of the models' performance.
2. **Insufficient Analysis**: The written discussion of the results is too short and needs more interpretation and analysis to provide a deeper understanding of the models' strengths and weaknesses.
3. **Dataset Claim Mismatch**: The abstract promises a new, more challenging dataset, but the paper does not actually present it, which is an inconsistency that must be resolved.
4. **Result Variability**: The performance of the models varies widely across tasks, and the authors should investigate why models fail on some tasks and succeed on others.
5. **Lack of Examples**: The paper would benefit from including example instances from each dataset, showing prototypical pairs and problematic pairs.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on the yodaqa/large2470 dataset, including its size, distribution, and any preprocessing steps taken?
2. How do you plan to address the result variability across tasks, and what insights can be gained from this analysis?
3. Can you provide more examples of sentence pairs from each dataset to illustrate the challenges and opportunities of each task?

**Additional Comments:**
The paper would benefit from minor language and typo fixes, including incorrect uses of "weigh"/"weighed" vs. "weight"/"weighted", multiple misspellings of "sentence", and unclear terms like "interpunction".
