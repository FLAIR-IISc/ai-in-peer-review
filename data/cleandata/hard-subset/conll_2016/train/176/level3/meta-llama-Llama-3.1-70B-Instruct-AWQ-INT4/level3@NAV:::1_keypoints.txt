- Proposes a unified framework treating diverse sentence-pair tasks as a single semantic-relation problem using general neural models.  
- Introduces a new dataset, yodaqa/large2470, intended to address small size and uneven distributions in existing datasets.  
- Evaluates multiple neural network models across tasks: answer sentence selection, next utterance ranking, RTE, and STS.  
- Novel and exciting idea to unify sentence-pair scoring tasks under one framework.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation offers insights into model strengths and weaknesses across tasks.  
- The new dataset is a significant contribution if fully presented and validated.  
- First results table is incomplete and must be finished for clarity.  
- Discussion and analysis of results are too brief and need deeper interpretation.  
- Abstract promises a more challenging dataset but the paper fails to present it clearly, creating an inconsistency.  
- Model performance varies widely across tasks; the paper should investigate causes of success and failure.
