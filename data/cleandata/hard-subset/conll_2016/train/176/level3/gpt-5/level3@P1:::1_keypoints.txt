- Paper proposes a unified view of sentence-pair scoring, evaluates task-agnostic neural architectures across tasks, emphasizes rigor (multiple seeds, CIs), explores transfer from Ubuntu dialogue, and introduces new answer-selection datasets.

- Contribution: an open-source unified evaluation toolkit (dataset-sts/KeraSTS) with standardized adapters, repeated runs, and CI reporting.

- Contribution: systematic cross-task evaluation of CNN/RNN/RNN-CNN/attention models and evidence that Ubuntu-trained RNN (Ubu.RNN) transfers well to other tasks.

- Contribution: new answer-selection datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) claimed to be larger and more challenging than wang.

- Strength: clear and compelling unified problem formulation enabling meaningful cross-task comparisons.

- Strength: methodological rigor—averaging over 16 runs and reporting 95% CIs to address high variance in small datasets.

- Strength: inclusion of strong IR baselines (TF-IDF/BM25, overlap features, candidate pruning) and practical BM25+neural integration.

- Strength: breadth of model comparisons yields actionable findings (e.g., CNNs for small datasets, RNNs for larger, attention helpful in IR-style ranking).

- Strength: transfer learning signal—Ubu.RNN initialization consistently improves performance on several target tasks.

- Weakness: paper feels unfinished—missing/incomplete results tables and promised content not fully presented.

- Weakness: dataset claims are under-documented and not clearly released; need stats, splits, deduplication, overlap/leakage analysis, and examples.

- Weakness: insufficient analysis and interpretation—lack of ablations (e.g., with/without BM25/pruning), qualitative error analysis, and explanations for cross-task performance differences.

- Weakness: potential evaluation confounds—BM25 pruning and BM25-as-feature may drive gains, making neural contribution unclear; attention formulation is asymmetric and under-probed.

- Weakness: models lag behind SOTA on RTE/STS (SNLI/SICK/STS) with no exploration of causes (architecture, training objective, features).

- Question: will yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop be released with clear train/dev/test splits, deduplication, overlap audits vs. wang, statistics, and example cases?

- Question: how much answer-selection performance comes from (a) BM25 candidate pruning and (b) BM25 as an input feature—please provide ablations and trade-off discussion.

- Question: how are early stopping and model selection handled across the 16 seeds to avoid validation overfitting, and do CIs change if selecting best vs. averaging seeds?

- Question: is Ubu.RNN’s benefit due to dataset size or next-utterance supervision—can you contrast with pretraining on similarly sized non-dialog data or multi-task training?

- Question: did you evaluate symmetric or token-to-token cross-attention variants beyond attn1511, and how do they perform on long vs. short contexts?

- Additional comment: complete missing tables/figures and expand results with targeted ablations, qualitative examples, and per-task “what works and why” analysis.

- Additional comment: clarify per-task training details (token limits, padding/truncation, optimizer schedules, dropout) to ensure reproducibility.

- Additional comment: fix minor language/typos (e.g., weigh/weighed, sentence misspellings, “interpunction”→punctuation, “world overlap”→word overlap).

- Overall recommendation: timely and valuable work but recommend acceptance only with major revisions to complete results, release/document datasets, and substantially expand analysis.
