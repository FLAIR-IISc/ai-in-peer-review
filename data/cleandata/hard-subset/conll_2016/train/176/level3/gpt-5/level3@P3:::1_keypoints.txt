- Proposes unified sentence-pair scoring f2(s0, s1) across tasks and implements a modular evaluation framework (KeraSTS) with multiple neural architectures.  
- Emphasizes robust evaluation: multiple random seeds, averaging, and 95% confidence intervals.  
- Reports transfer learning where RNN pretrained on Ubuntu (Ubu.RNN) improves downstream sentence-pair tasks.  
- Introduces two new datasets (yodaqa/large2470, wqmprop) and argues for stronger IR baselines and principled ensembling.  
- Recommendation: Accept for presentation only with major revisions—novel and promising but unfinished and under-analyzed.

- Contribution 2: Cross-task transfer learning evidence suggesting shared sentence-pair representations (Ubu.RNN).  
- Contribution 3: New datasets and adapters targeting answer/property selection, though presentation is incomplete.
