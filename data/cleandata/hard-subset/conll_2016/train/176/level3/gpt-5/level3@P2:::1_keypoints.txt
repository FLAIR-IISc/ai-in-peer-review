- Proposes a unified sentence-pair framework covering Answer Sentence Selection, Next Utterance Ranking, RTE, and STS with task adapters, several neural architectures, transfer learning, new datasets, and open-source tooling.  
- Emphasizes stronger evaluation practices: multiple random seeds, 95% confidence intervals, and inclusion of IR baselines (TF-IDF/BM25).  
- Shows a transfer-learning result where Ubuntu-pretrained RNN (Ubu.RNN) often improves performance on other f2 tasks.  
- Claims to introduce new datasets (yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop) but they are under-documented in the paper.  

Strengths:
- The unifying perspective and released adapters/code aid reproducibility and cross-task benchmarking.  
- Reporting averaged results with CIs and IR baselines is a meaningful methodological improvement.  
- Broad empirical comparison across CNN/RNN/attention models gives an initial mapping of model suitability to dataset size/types.  
- Transfer-learning signal supports the idea that a general sentence-pair encoder can be seeded from large supervision.  

Weaknesses:
- Reporting is incomplete (missing entries in main results table) and inconsistent, preventing full assessment.  
- New-dataset claims lack construction details, splits, statistics, licensing, and leakage analysis (possible overlap with wang).  
- Paper lacks sufficient analysis: why models succeed/fail, role of overlap features, BM25 pruning effects, and per-task error typologies.  
- The “unified” encoder claim is not convincingly justified given wide performance variability, potential negative transfer, and sensitivity concerns.  
- Ubu.RNN improvement is ambiguous—unclear if gains stem from pretraining size, domain, or other factors without controlled ablations.  
- Presentation issues (typos, unclear terminology, missing training/tokenization details) undermine clarity and reproducibility.  

Questions for authors:
- When will the missing table entries, CIs, and number-of-seed details be provided for all experiments?  
- Please provide full documentation for yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop: construction, labeling, splits, stats, licenses, and download URLs.  
- How do you prevent train–test leakage and deduplicate across datasets (esp. wang vs yodaqa)?  
- Can you run transfer controls: pretrain on SNLI with matched sample count; sub-sample Ubuntu to SNLI size; layer-freezing/reinit ablations; multi-task vs sequential fine-tuning; and dropout consistency ablations?  
- What is the impact of BM25 top-20 pruning on recall and training bias—please compare with/without pruning and with/without overlap features.  
- Please include representative error examples and (where applicable) attention visualizations per dataset.  

Additional comments:
- Fix noted typos and improve language/polish.  
- Justify/clarify training choices (very high dropout p=0.8, training on 1/4 data per epoch, early stopping), and report tokenization, OOV handling, max sequence lengths, and truncation policy.  
- Add missing metrics: Ubuntu MRR, STS Pearson’s r and Spearman’s ρ, and significance tests against strongest published baselines.  
- Ensure all figures/tables are complete, legible, and consistently referenced.  

Recommendation:
- Borderline accept conditional on major revisions: complete results, proper dataset release/documentation, and substantially deeper analysis to support central claims.
