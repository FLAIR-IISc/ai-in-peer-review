- Proposes a unified framework framing diverse sentence-pair scoring tasks as a common semantic-relation problem solvable with task-independent neural models and task-specific output layers.  
- Introduces two new datasets, yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop, intended to address size and quality limitations of existing benchmarks.  
- Provides a broad empirical evaluation across multiple tasks and model families to establish baselines within the proposed framework.  
- Strong motivation: addresses fragmentation in sentence-pair research and insightfully treats the second sentence as a rich training label.  
- Practical contribution: releases dataset-sts and KeraSTS libraries to support reproducibility and community adoption.  
- Extensive experiments: evaluates IR baselines, simple neural nets, RNNs and CNNs and reports confidence intervals for statistical rigor.  
- Dataset creation targets real issues (small/uneven benchmarks), with yodaqa/large2470 as a concrete attempt to improve benchmarks.  
- Major issue: incomplete results table that prevents proper assessment of model performance and comparisons.  
- Analysis is superficial: lacks deep investigation into why models perform differently across tasks and into failure modes.  
- New-dataset evaluation mismatch: lower performance on yodaqa/wqmprop needs discussion on whether they are genuinely harder or ill-suited to evaluated models.  
- Transfer learning claim (Ubuntu Dialogue pretraining improves performance) requires experiments to separate effects of dataset size versus representation generality.  
- Presentation problems: multiple language, typographical, and spelling errors reduce manuscript polish.  
- Request for dataset exemplars: provide prototypical and problematic examples from yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop.  
- Request for transfer-learning ablation: test whether similar gains arise from pretraining on other large datasets to isolate causes.  
- Ask which tasks were considered but excluded and what limitations the unified framework has.  
- Ask for completion timeline of the missing results table.  
- Suggest clarifying rationale for choosing embedding dimensionality N=300.  
- Recommend reporting computational resources used for experiments.  
- Overall judgment: promising and novel direction but requires substantial revision; conditional acceptance recommended pending major fixes.
