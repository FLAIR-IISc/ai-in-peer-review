1. Proposes a unified framework treating diverse NLP tasks as sentence pair scoring using the second sentence as a “complex label.”  
2. Explicitly frames a broad range of tasks (Answer Sentence Selection, STS, Next Utterance Ranking) as a common semantic relation problem.  
3. Provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple datasets and model architectures.  
4. Releases the dataset-sts framework and libraries (KeraSTS, PySTS) to support reproducibility.  
5. Introduces two new datasets: yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop.  
6. Novelty: the unification idea could enable a single adaptable model across tasks.  
7. Extensive evaluation including IR baselines offers useful performance context.  
8. Open-source release is a strong contribution to reproducibility and community use.  
9. Shows promising transfer learning results from the Ubuntu Dialogue model (Ubu.RNN).  
10. Table 1 is incomplete and many model/dataset results are missing, hindering evaluation.  
11. Analysis and discussion of experimental results are insufficient and lack depth.  
12. Claim that new datasets are “more challenging” is unsubstantiated and needs rigorous comparison.  
13. Large variability in performance across tasks raises questions about task coherence and generalizability.  
14. Paper lacks investigation into why models succeed or fail on specific tasks and architectures.  
15. No illustrative examples or error cases are provided to diagnose model shortcomings.  
16. Requests: complete Table 1 and supply missing experimental results.  
17. Requests: justify why yodaqa/large2470 and wqmprop are more challenging than existing datasets.  
18. Requests: analyze whether Ubu.RNN’s transfer success is due to dataset size, domain, or both.  
19. Requests: provide detailed error analysis with example failure cases for each task.  
20. Requests: present evidence that diverse sentence pair tasks form a unified group suitable for one model.  
21. Minor language and formatting issues need correction (typos, punctuation terminology, “word overlap”).  
22. Overall assessment: paper feels unfinished and requires substantial polishing and deeper analysis before publication.
