

# Peer Review: Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification

## Summary
This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that leverages a cross-lingual wikifier to ground text to English Wikipedia entries, using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features. The authors demonstrate that these features can be used for direct transfer NER without requiring parallel text or native speaker interaction, and they show significant improvements over strong baselines on both high-resource (CoNLL languages) and low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).

## Main Contributions
1. The development of a novel method to generate language-independent features for cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification
2. Comprehensive evaluation showing significant performance gains over state-of-the-art direct transfer methods on multiple languages
3. Insightful analysis of how Wikipedia size affects feature quality and the applicability of the approach across different script types

## Strengths

**1. Novel Feature Engineering Approach:** The paper's core contribution is the clever application of cross-lingual wikification as a feature generation mechanism rather than as a downstream task. The insight that FreeBase types provide strong signals for NER even with imperfect disambiguation (Figure 1) is valuable and well-supported by the results. This approach creates truly language-independent features that can be applied across languages without requiring language-specific adaptations.

**2. Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The paper conducts thorough experiments across both high-resource and low-resource languages. The comparison with Täckström et al. (2012) and Zhang et al. (2016) is well-executed and shows clear advantages. The analysis of how Wikipedia size affects feature quality (Section 4.4) is particularly valuable for understanding the limitations of the approach.

**3. Practical Implementation with Minimal Requirements:** The method only requires a multilingual Wikipedia dump, making it highly practical for deployment. The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms methods requiring parallel text and native speaker interaction on low-resource languages, which is a significant practical advantage.

## Weaknesses

**1. Lack of Comparison with Modern Transformer-Based Approaches:** The paper compares with older methods (Täckström et al., 2012; Zhang et al., 2016) but doesn't compare with recent state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods based on multilingual transformers (e.g., multilingual BERT). Given that transformer-based approaches have become dominant in NLP, this omission is significant. The authors should compare their method with these modern approaches to establish its current standing.

**2. Insufficient Analysis of Wikifier Limitations:** While the paper acknowledges imperfect disambiguation (Figure 1), it doesn't systematically analyze how wikifier errors propagate to the NER system. For instance, what happens when the wikifier incorrectly identifies a person as an organization? How does this impact the NER results? A more detailed error analysis would strengthen the paper's claims.

**3. Limited Discussion of Feature Engineering Justification:** The modifications to the wikifier system (Section 3.3) are presented as necessary but not thoroughly justified. The authors state they modify the candidate generation process to avoid linking bigrams to single words (e.g., "in Germany" linking to "Germany"), but they don't provide evidence that this modification actually improves NER performance. A comparison between the original and modified wikifier would be valuable.

## Questions to Authors

1. How would your approach compare with recent transformer-based cross-lingual NER methods (e.g., using multilingual BERT or XLM-R)? Given that these approaches have become the new state-of-the-art in cross-lingual tasks, it would be valuable to see how your method performs against them.

2. Can you provide more detailed error analysis showing how wikifier errors (e.g., incorrect FreeBase type assignments) affect the NER results? This would help understand the robustness of your feature engineering approach.

3. For languages with very small Wikipedia (like Yoruba), the performance of wikifier features is significantly degraded. Could you discuss potential strategies to mitigate this limitation, such as combining with other knowledge sources or using synthetic data generation?

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a valuable contribution to cross-lingual NER by demonstrating that cross-lingual wikification can provide strong language-independent features. The experimental results are compelling, particularly for low-resource languages where the approach outperforms methods requiring parallel text. However, the lack of comparison with modern transformer-based approaches is a significant limitation that needs to be addressed. The paper would be substantially strengthened by incorporating these comparisons and providing a more detailed analysis of the wikifier's limitations and error propagation.

The work is well-executed, well-motivated, and demonstrates clear practical value for cross-lingual NER. With the addition of comparisons to recent state-of-the-art methods and a more thorough analysis of the wikifier's limitations, this paper would be a strong contribution to the field.
