

# Review: Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model that leverages cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features for NER. The authors ground words and n-grams in any language to English Wikipedia, using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as features. The model requires only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, eliminating the need for parallel text or language-specific resources.

The main contributions are:
1. A novel method to generate language-independent features for cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification, which provides strong signals even with imperfect disambiguation.
2. Comprehensive evaluation across both high-resource (CoNLL languages) and low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), demonstrating significant improvements over strong baselines.
3. Analysis showing that the quality of wikifier features correlates with Wikipedia size, and that training on multiple source languages further improves performance.

## Strengths

1. **Novel feature extraction approach**: The paper makes a compelling case that FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories from English Wikipedia provide valuable language-independent features for NER, even when disambiguation is imperfect. This insight is well-supported by Figure 1 and the experimental results, particularly for low-resource languages where lexical features fail. The authors demonstrate that these features significantly improve performance on languages with non-Latin scripts (Bengali, Tamil), which is a notable contribution.

2. **Strong empirical validation**: The paper provides thorough evaluation across multiple languages and settings. The results in Table 2 show consistent improvements over baselines, with over 10-point gains on German, Dutch, and Turkish compared to base features. The analysis in Table 3 clearly demonstrates the correlation between Wikipedia size and feature quality, adding significant depth to the claims.

3. **Resource-efficient approach**: The method requires only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, making it highly practical for real-world deployment. The authors demonstrate the approach works for languages with entirely non-Latin scripts (Bengali, Tamil), which is particularly valuable for low-resource language processing. The comparison to Zhang et al. (2016) shows significant advantages with far fewer resources.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited novelty differentiation**: While the application of wikification to NER is novel, the paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish its contribution from prior direct transfer methods like Täckström et al. (2012). The authors acknowledge this but don't provide a comprehensive comparison that clearly shows how their wikifier features represent a meaningful advancement beyond previous approaches. The paper's claim that their features are "truly delexicalized" isn't sufficiently justified against existing work.

2. **Incomplete ablation study**: The paper modifies the cross-lingual wikifier system (Tsai and Roth, 2016) for their purpose but doesn't provide an ablation study to assess the contribution of each modification. The two modifications to the candidate generation process and ranking model are mentioned but not evaluated individually, making it difficult to assess which changes are most impactful.

3. **Limited exploration of broader applicability**: While the paper focuses on NER, it doesn't explore whether the wikifier features could benefit other NLP tasks. The authors briefly mention this in the conclusion but provide no evidence or experiments to support the claim that these features might generalize to other tasks, limiting the perceived significance of the contribution.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper claims that their method is orthogonal to projection approaches (Section 2.3), but doesn't provide experiments showing how the two could be combined. Could the authors provide details on how their wikifier features could be integrated with projection methods and what performance gains might be expected?

2. In Table 3, the authors show that removing FreeBase querying (W.−FB query) significantly degrades performance. However, they don't explain why FreeBase querying is necessary for the wikifier features to be effective. Could the authors clarify the relationship between FreeBase types and the quality of the features?

3. The paper demonstrates that training on multiple source languages improves performance (Table 4), but doesn't address how the method would handle cases where source languages have different linguistic properties. How would the approach perform when training on languages with very different morphological structures (e.g., agglutinative vs. fusional languages)?

## Overall Assessment

This paper presents a practical and effective approach to cross-lingual NER that leverages cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The method is well-motivated, thoroughly evaluated, and demonstrates significant improvements over strong baselines, particularly for low-resource languages. While the novelty is somewhat limited by prior work on direct transfer, the specific application of wikifier features is novel and well-supported. The resource efficiency of the approach (requiring only Wikipedia dumps) is a major strength. The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with prior work and a deeper exploration of the feature engineering process, but the core contribution is sound and valuable for the field.
