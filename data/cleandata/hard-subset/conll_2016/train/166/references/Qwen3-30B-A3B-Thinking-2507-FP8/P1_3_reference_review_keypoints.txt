- Proposes a cross-lingual wikifier that grounds words to English Wikipedia to produce language-independent NER features (FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories).  
- Claims the method needs only a multilingual Wikipedia dump, not parallel text or target-language annotations.  
- Evaluated on high-resource (CoNLL) and five low-resource languages, showing significant gains over direct-transfer baselines.  
- Main contribution 1: a language-independent feature extraction method via cross-lingual wikification.  
- Main contribution 2: comprehensive evaluation demonstrating improvements, especially for low-resource languages.  
- Main contribution 3: analysis linking Wikipedia size to feature quality and performance.  
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated approach with clear examples showing FreeBase types help despite imperfect disambiguation.  
- Strength: Broad experimental evaluation across diverse languages and relevant baselines.  
- Strength: Insightful experiments showing Wikipedia size (and interlanguage links) drive feature quality, especially for non-Latin scripts.  
- Strength: Wikifier features also improve monolingual NER, indicating broader utility.  
- Weakness: No comparison to recent multilingual pretrained models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), which are current baselines.  
- Weakness: Poor performance on languages lacking FreeBase coverage (Bengali, Tamil, Yoruba) with no proposed remedy.  
- Weakness: Two ad-hoc wikifier modifications for n-grams are under-justified and unevaluated.  
- Weakness: No analysis of computational cost, particularly for exhaustive n-gram wikification.  
- Questions for authors: How will you handle languages with no FreeBase coverage or use alternative knowledge sources?  
- Questions for authors: Will you compare to multilingual models like XLM-R for a current baseline?  
- Questions for authors: How do you reconcile the claim of requiring only Wikipedia when FreeBase types are relied upon?  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, novel contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions if comparisons to multilingual models and solutions for non-FreeBase languages are added.
