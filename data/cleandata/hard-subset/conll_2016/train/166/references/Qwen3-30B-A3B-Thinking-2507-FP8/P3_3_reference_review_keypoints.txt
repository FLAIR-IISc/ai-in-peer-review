- Leverages cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features from FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories, grounding to English Wikipedia.

- Contributions: a practical, resource-efficient cross-lingual NER method needing only multilingual Wikipedia; empirical evidence that wikifier features are strong language-independent signals; improvements for direct transfer and monolingual NER.

- Practicality and simplicity: effective without parallel data or complex models; FreeBase types provide strong signals even with imperfect disambiguation.

- Comprehensive evaluation across high- and low-resource languages, analyzing Wikipedia size impact; particularly effective for non-Latin scripts where lexical features fail.

- Insightful analysis explaining why the approach works (correlation with Wikipedia size, multi-language training effects).

- Understates limitations for languages with very small Wikipedias and offers no proposed solutions for minimal-coverage languages.

- Implementation complexity: cross-lingual wikifier requires significant infrastructure (FreeBase API, multilingual processing) and paper omits computational/practical details.

- Incomplete comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., multilingual BERT), limiting claims of novelty and competitiveness.

- Lacks analysis of individual feature contributions (FreeBase types vs. Wikipedia categories) to performance.

- Question: How would the method handle languages with very small Wikipedias — augment with other knowledge sources?

- Question: What are the computational requirements and runtime for processing a large multilingual Wikipedia dump?

- Question: Why do Bengali and Tamil degrade more — solely Wikipedia size or other factors like script complexity?

- Overall: A practical, well-validated approach with valuable insights but constrained by low-resource coverage, implementation transparency, and comparisons to recent advances.
