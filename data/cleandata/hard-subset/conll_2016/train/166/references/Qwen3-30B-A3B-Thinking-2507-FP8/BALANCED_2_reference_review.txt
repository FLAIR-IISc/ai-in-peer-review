

# Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that uses a cross-lingual wikifier to ground words and phrases to English Wikipedia, leveraging FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features. The authors demonstrate that these features provide strong signals for NER even with imperfect disambiguation. Their key contributions are:

1. Development of a method to extract language-independent features for NER through cross-lingual wikification, requiring only multilingual Wikipedia without parallel text.
2. Comprehensive empirical validation across both high-resource (CoNLL languages) and low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), showing consistent improvements over strong baselines.
3. Systematic analysis of how Wikipedia size affects feature quality and demonstration that training on multiple source languages can further improve results.

## Strengths

1. **Novel and well-motivated core contribution**: The paper makes a strong case for using wikification as a source of language-independent features for NER. The insight that FreeBase types provide valuable signals even with imperfect disambiguation (Figure 1) is compelling and well-justified. Unlike previous approaches that require parallel data or language-specific resources, this method only needs multilingual Wikipedia, making it broadly applicable.

2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach across a diverse set of languages (both Latin and non-Latin scripts), providing a thorough analysis of performance across different language families and resource levels. The comparison with strong baselines (Täckström et al., Zhang et al.) is convincing, with significant improvements on multiple languages, particularly for non-Latin script languages where lexical features fail.

3. **Insightful analysis of Wikipedia size effects**: The analysis in Section 4.4 (Table 3) effectively demonstrates the direct relationship between Wikipedia size and feature quality, which is crucial for understanding the limitations and potential of the approach. This goes beyond simple performance claims to provide actionable insights for future work.

## Weaknesses

1. **Hybrid nature of the approach**: The paper claims to be a "direct transfer" method, but the use of language-specific gazetteers (derived from Wikipedia) makes it a hybrid approach. The authors acknowledge this in their monolingual experiments but don't sufficiently address how this hybrid nature affects the "direct transfer" claim. For a true direct transfer method, all features should be completely language-independent, which isn't the case here.

2. **Limited handling of extremely low-resource languages**: While the paper analyzes Wikipedia size effects, it doesn't provide concrete solutions for languages with very small Wikipedia size (e.g., Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil). The results for these languages remain significantly below those for CoNLL languages, and the paper lacks strategies to improve performance for such cases, which limits the practical applicability of the approach.

3. **Insufficient differentiation from prior work**: The paper mentions Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and Sil and Yates (2013) but doesn't clearly articulate how their approach differs from these works. The modifications to the wikifier (to handle n-grams) are mentioned but not thoroughly justified, making it difficult to assess the novelty of their technical contribution.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper states that their approach "works on all languages in Wikipedia," but doesn't address how it would handle languages without a Wikipedia (e.g., languages with no written form). How would the approach be adapted for such languages?

2. The results for Bengali and Tamil show significant performance gaps compared to CoNLL languages. Given that FreeBase doesn't contain entities in these languages (Table 3), what specific strategies could be integrated to improve performance for languages with non-Latin scripts?

3. How would you evaluate the trade-off between the quality of wikifier features (which depends on Wikipedia size) and the potential benefits of using language-specific gazetteers for languages with larger Wikipedia resources?

## Overall Assessment

This is a well-executed paper with a compelling core idea that makes a valuable contribution to cross-lingual NER. The empirical evaluation is thorough and provides important insights into the relationship between Wikipedia size and feature quality. However, the hybrid nature of the approach (using language-specific gazetteers) weakens the direct transfer claim, and the paper doesn't sufficiently address the limitations for extremely low-resource languages.

The paper would benefit from a clearer articulation of the approach's limitations and a more thorough comparison with prior work on wikification for NER. With these improvements, the paper would make a strong contribution to the field.

**Recommendation: Weak Accept** (with minor revisions to address the weaknesses noted above, particularly regarding the direct transfer claim and low-resource language handling). The paper is strong in its evaluation and insight but needs to better clarify the scope of its contribution.
