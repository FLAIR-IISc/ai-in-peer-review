- Core contribution: uses cross-lingual wikification to map text to English Wikipedia, using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent NER features.  
- Minimal resource requirement: method needs only multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel data or language-specific heuristics).  
- Empirical performance: outperforms direct transfer baselines on high-resource CoNLL and several low-resource languages.  
- Multilingual coverage: evaluated on 9 languages, including non-Latin scripts (Bengali, Tamil), showing consistent improvements.  
- Wikipedia-size analysis: shows feature quality and performance correlate with target-language Wikipedia size.  
- Novelty and practicality: presents a new, widely applicable approach that tolerates imperfect disambiguation yet yields strong signals.  
- Strong evaluation: comprehensive comparisons across multiple strong baselines and large F1 gains (10+ points on several languages).  
- Non-Latin applicability: successfully demonstrates viability for scripts where many cross-lingual methods fail.  
- Missing SOTA comparisons: lacks evaluation against recent multilingual pre-trained models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Limited theoretical grounding: insufficient explanation why FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories work so well for NER.  
- Insufficient wikifier details: sparse description of modifications to Tsai and Roth (2016) for n-gram handling, hurting reproducibility.  
- Limited error analysis: no detailed analysis of failure modes, especially for low-resource languages.  
- Questions for authors: how does this compare to XLM-R/mBERT, what were the n-gram wikifier modifications, and how was tokenization handled for non-Latin scripts?  
- Overall verdict: strong, practical contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to add SOTA comparisons and more implementation/theoretical details.
