

# Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

## Summary
This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model that leverages a cross-lingual wikifier to ground words to English Wikipedia, using the resulting FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features. The authors claim that these features can be used for direct transfer learning across languages without requiring parallel data or annotated target language data.

## Primary Contributions (as I see them)
1. Application of cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features for NER
2. Demonstration of these features' effectiveness in direct transfer learning across multiple languages
3. Analysis of how Wikipedia size affects feature quality

## Strengths
1. The paper addresses an important problem in low-resource NER with a potentially scalable solution.
2. The analysis of how Wikipedia size affects feature quality (Section 4.4) is valuable and well-executed.
3. The evaluation on diverse languages, including non-Latin scripts, demonstrates the approach's broad applicability.

## Weaknesses (Primary to Least Important)

### 1. Inconsistent Terminology and Methodological Ambiguities (Critical Issue)
The paper fails to clearly define key concepts. The term "wikifier features" is used throughout without precise definition. The authors claim to "ground every n-gram (n ≤ 4)" but provide no justification for why n=4 is optimal, and the example in Figure 1 shows word-level linking, not n-gram linking. The paper states "we set n to 4 in all our experiments" but never explains the rationale for this choice. The modification to the Tsai and Roth (2016) wikifier system is described as "deviating from normal usage" but the justification for this deviation is weak and not well-supported.

### 2. Inaccurate and Inconsistent Results Presentation (Critical Issue)
Table 2 contains contradictory information. The paper claims in Section 4.3 that "for both Bengali and Tamil, [Wikifier only] is the strongest signal, and gets the highest score," but the numbers in Table 2 show "Wikifier only" scores (45.96 for Bengali, 37.83 for Tamil) are substantially lower than "Base Features" (69.11 and 70.62, respectively). This is a fundamental inconsistency that undermines the paper's core claim. Additionally, the "Wikifier only" row for Yoruba shows 33.63 F1, but the paper claims it "gets the highest score" for Bengali and Tamil, which is contradicted by the table.

### 3. Problematic Evaluation Comparisons (Significant Issue)
The comparison to Täckström et al. (2012) is misleading. The paper claims to outperform their "baseline" but doesn't clarify that the Täckström baseline is actually a more limited setup (no POS features). The comparison to Zhang et al. (2016) is problematic because Zhang et al. uses "one-hour interaction with a native speaker," which the paper claims is not required, but the paper never clarifies whether this interaction is part of the training process or just for evaluation. The paper's claim of "significant improvement" on low-resource languages is questionable given the extremely low F1 scores (e.g., 6.72 for Yoruba).

### 4. Technical Errors and Omissions (Significant Issue)
The paper claims to use "the BIO labeling scheme" but states in Section 4.1 that they convert "IOB1 labeling scheme" to "BIO labeling scheme." These are different schemes with different labeling conventions. The paper also states "We use the system proposed in Tsai and Roth (2016)" but fails to provide any details about how their modifications differ from the original system, making it impossible to replicate their approach.

### 5. Inadequate Justification for Key Claims (Minor Issue)
The paper claims "the FreeBase types still provide valuable information" in Figure 1, but the example shows basic person linking ("Albrecht" and "Lehmann" to people), which is a very simple type of information that could be obtained from much simpler methods (e.g., word lists or simple rules), not requiring sophisticated wikification.

## Questions to Authors
1. Please clarify the definition of "wikifier features" and explain why grounding every n-gram (n ≤ 4) is necessary for the approach to work.
2. Please resolve the contradiction between the text claiming "Wikifier only" gets the highest score for Bengali and Tamil, and the numbers in Table 2 showing otherwise.
3. Please clarify the relationship between FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories, and explain how these were extracted from Wikipedia.

## Recommendation
Reject

The paper contains fundamental inconsistencies in its results presentation (Table 2 vs. text), methodological ambiguities, and technical errors that undermine its core claims. The contradictory results in Table 2 and the text about the "Wikifier only" performance for Bengali and Tamil are particularly problematic and cannot be reconciled. While the approach has potential, these critical flaws make the paper's claims unreliable and the results difficult to interpret. The paper fails to demonstrate the claimed superiority of their approach over baselines with sufficient clarity and rigor.
