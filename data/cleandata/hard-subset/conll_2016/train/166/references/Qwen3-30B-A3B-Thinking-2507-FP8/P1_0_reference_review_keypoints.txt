- Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to map text to English Wikipedia, using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features.  
- Shows wikifier features substantially improve cross-lingual NER without parallel data or language-specific resources.  
- Demonstrates applicability to low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), outperforming methods that require parallel/native resources.  
- Presents a unified framework where wikifier features benefit both monolingual and cross-lingual NER models.  

- Strong empirical results across high- and low-resource languages, with large F1 gains and analysis of when lexical features fail.  
- Practical approach requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, accessible for researchers without parallel corpora.  
- Analyses how Wikipedia size affects feature quality, identifying limitations tied to smaller Wikipedias.  
- Effective on non-Latin scripts (e.g., Bengali, Tamil), addressing under-studied low-resource languages.  
- Multi-source training with wikifier features improves results without needing parallel data.  

- Lacks comparison to modern cross-lingual pretrained models (mBERT, XLM-R), relying on older baselines.  
- Insufficient analysis of wikifier disambiguation errors and their specific impact on NER.  
- No clear threshold or guidance for practicality on very small Wikipedia languages (e.g., Yoruba performance concerns).  
- Omits computational complexity or scalability analysis for processing all n-grams (n ≤ 4).  
- Unclear treatment and impact of MISC tag modifications on evaluation and cross-lingual comparisons.  

- How does the method compare to modern models like XLM-R or multilingual BERT on cross-lingual NER?  
- What are the wikifier’s error rates and error patterns, and how do they affect NER performance?  
- What Wikipedia size (e.g., article count) makes the approach impractical — can you provide a threshold?  
- What is the computational overhead of wikifying all n-grams compared to standard NER pipelines?  

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons to modern models and the noted analyses/limitations.
