

# Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that leverages a cross-lingual wikifier to ground words/phrases to English Wikipedia, using Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as language-independent features. The authors claim three key contributions: (1) a novel method using cross-lingual wikification for NER feature extraction, (2) strong performance in direct transfer across languages without requiring parallel data or native speaker interaction, and (3) thorough analysis of how Wikipedia size affects feature quality.

## Strengths

1. **Practical and innovative approach:** The paper presents a simple yet effective solution that requires only multilingual Wikipedia as input, making it highly practical for real-world deployment. The core insight that imperfect wikification can still provide useful NER features (Figure 1) is well-supported by the results, particularly impressive given the method's minimal requirements.

2. **Strong empirical results on diverse languages:** The method significantly outperforms baselines on both high-resource languages (Spanish, Dutch, German) and low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil). The results on low-resource languages are particularly compelling, as the method outperforms approaches requiring parallel text and native speaker interaction, which is a significant contribution to the field.

3. **Thorough analysis of feature quality:** The paper provides valuable insights into why performance varies across languages, linking it directly to Wikipedia size. The ablation study (Table 3) convincingly demonstrates that smaller Wikipedia sizes lead to poorer feature quality, explaining the performance differences between high-resource and low-resource languages.

4. **Comprehensive evaluation framework:** The paper evaluates the method in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings, and explores training on multiple source languages. The analysis showing that training on multiple languages (EN+ES+NL+DE) improves results over training on English alone (Table 4) provides practical guidance for real-world applications.

## Weaknesses

1. **Inadequate comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods:** The paper compares against Täckström et al. (2012) and Zhang et al. (2016), but fails to include more recent approaches like multilingual transformers (mBERT, XLM-R) or other contemporary cross-lingual NER methods. Without these comparisons, it's difficult to assess the true standing of the proposed method in current literature.

2. **Misleading "wikifier only" baseline:** The paper reports "wikifier only" results (Table 2) that exclude standard NER features like previous tags. This makes the reported scores artificially low and not comparable to standard NER models, undermining the significance of the reported improvements.

3. **Limited analysis of NER type performance:** The paper doesn't analyze how the wikifier features perform for different NER types (PERSON, ORG, LOC, MISC). A more detailed breakdown would provide deeper insights into the method's strengths and weaknesses for specific entity types.

4. **Lack of discussion on language coverage limitations:** The paper states the method requires multilingual Wikipedia but doesn't address how it would handle languages with no Wikipedia (e.g., many indigenous languages). This is a significant limitation for truly global NER, and the authors should acknowledge this constraint.

## Questions to Authors

1. How would your method compare to recent multilingual transformer-based approaches (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R) on the same datasets? Given that these models have achieved strong results in cross-lingual NER, this comparison is essential for positioning your work.

2. Could you provide a detailed analysis of how wikifier features perform for different NER types (e.g., PERSON vs. ORG)? This would help understand the method's specific strengths and limitations.

3. How would you handle languages with no Wikipedia (or very small Wikipedia)? The paper's current approach is limited to languages with Wikipedia, which excludes many languages of interest.

4. Why was the "wikifier only" baseline implemented without standard NER features (like previous tags)? This makes the reported results difficult to interpret and compare with standard NER models.

## Overall Recommendation

This is a solid paper with a clear, novel idea that demonstrates practical utility for cross-lingual NER. The empirical results are strong, particularly for low-resource languages, and the analysis of Wikipedia size impact is valuable. However, the lack of comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods and the misleading baseline undermine the paper's contribution. 

**Decision: Weak Accept** (with major revisions)

The paper has significant potential, but to strengthen it, the authors should: (1) include comparisons to recent multilingual transformer approaches, (2) revise the "wikifier only" baseline to include standard NER features for fair comparison, (3) provide more detailed analysis of NER type performance, and (4) address the limitation regarding languages without Wikipedia. With these revisions, the paper would make a strong contribution to the field.
