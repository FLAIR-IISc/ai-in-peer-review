Contributions:
- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to generate language-independent features from multilingual Wikipedia only.
- Shows grounding n-grams to English Wikipedia and using FreeBase types/categories yields strong signals for NER despite imperfect disambiguation.
- Presents a direct-transfer approach requiring no parallel text or native speaker annotation and reports large gains on several low-resource languages.

Strengths:
- Novel and practical cross-lingual application of wikification to generate language-independent NER features.
- Strong empirical improvements on both high- and low-resource languages, e.g., +14 F1 on Turkish vs prior work.
- Analysis of Wikipedia size impact clarifies limitations and feature quality dependence on resource size.
- Demonstrates benefits from multi-source (multi-language) training.

Weaknesses:
- Core feature engineering (FreeBase types/Wikipedia categories) is similar to prior monolingual work and not highly novel.
- Lacks comparison to recent multilingual pretrained models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), limiting assessment against current SOTA.
- No statistical significance testing provided to support claims of “significant improvements.”
- Does not address applicability to languages without Wikipedia coverage.
- Overstates “no parallel text” claim; the employed cross-lingual wikifier relies on interlanguage links, which are a form of parallel alignment.

Questions for authors:
- How would the method handle languages without Wikipedia coverage and what alternative data sources could provide wikifier features?
- Can you provide comparisons against multilingual models like XLM-R or mBERT?
- Why do lexical features fail for Bengali and Tamil (non-Latin scripts), and how might script-specific challenges be addressed?

Overall recommendation:
- Solid, practically valuable paper with strong low-resource results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add significance testing and comparisons to recent multilingual models.
