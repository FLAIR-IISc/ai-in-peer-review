- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER that uses wikification to map words in any language to English Wikipedia categories/Freebase types, requiring only multilingual Wikipedia and showing large gains on CoNLL and five low-resource languages.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a cross-lingual NER model using a wikifier to generate language-independent features without parallel text or language-specific heuristics.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that wikifier-derived features substantially improve cross-lingual NER performance, especially for low-resource languages.

- Contribution 3: Analyzes feature quality dependence on target-language Wikipedia size and shows multi-source training further improves results.

- Strength 1: Novel approach for cross-lingual NER that does not rely on parallel data, making it applicable to low-resource languages.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical validation across high-resource and low-resource languages with notable F1 improvements over prior direct-transfer baselines.

- Strength 3: Insightful analysis linking wikifier feature effectiveness to Wikipedia size, clarifying limitations and performance variance.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty since prior work used Wikipedia categories for NER; unclear how much novelty is due to the cross-lingual framing versus wikification itself.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient evaluation of the wikifier component and lack of quantification of wikifier errors’ impact on NER performance.

- Weakness 3: Missing comparisons to recent state-of-the-art cross-lingual methods (e.g., multilingual BERT/transformers), limiting assessment of current relevance.

- Weakness 4: Only modest monolingual gains (1–2 points) from wikifier features and no clear explanation why cross-lingual gains are much larger.

- Weakness 5: Limited discussion of practical limitations for languages with very small Wikipedias and no concrete minimum-coverage guidance.

- Question 1: Why was n=4 chosen for n-grams—please provide ablation results and justification.

- Question 2: How would the approach perform on languages with very different writing systems (e.g., Chinese, Japanese) and what are potential limitations?

- Question 3: What is the minimum Wikipedia size needed for the method to be effective, and can you give examples of languages where it would fail?
