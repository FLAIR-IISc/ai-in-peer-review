- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to map text to English Wikipedia and extract language-independent features (FreeBase types, Wikipedia categories) for direct transfer without parallel data.  
- Main contribution: novel method for obtaining language-independent features via cross-lingual wikification.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive evaluation showing effectiveness on high-resource and low-resource languages.  
- Main contribution: shows wikifier features also improve monolingual NER models.  
- Strength: practical and accessible feature extraction that avoids parallel text or language-specific heuristics and leverages multilingual Wikipedia.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains across multiple languages, outperforming established baselines and prior low-resource methods.  
- Strength: thorough analysis (Wikipedia size effects, script differences) and ablation study validating feature components.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent cross-lingual NER work (e.g., multilingual BERT or recent 2019+ methods).  
- Weakness: insufficient technical detail on wikifier modifications for n-gram processing and candidate generation.  
- Weakness: lack of discussion on limitations for extremely low-resource languages with very small Wikipedias.  
- Question: please detail the modifications to the Tsai and Roth (2016) wikifier for n-gram candidate generation.  
- Question: how would the approach perform for languages with <10k Wikipedia articles and what minimum Wikipedia size is needed?  
- Question: how does this method compare to multilingual BERT-based cross-lingual NER in current state-of-the-art and resource trade-offs?  
- Overall: well-motivated, practical contribution with strong results and analyses, recommended for acceptance despite needed comparisons and implementation details.
