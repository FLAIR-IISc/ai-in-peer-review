

# Review: Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification

## Summary
This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that uses a cross-lingual wikifier to ground text to English Wikipedia, extracting language-independent features (FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories) for NER. The model is trained on one or more source languages and applied directly to target languages without requiring parallel text or language-specific rules. The authors evaluate their approach on CoNLL languages (English, German, Spanish, Dutch) and five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), demonstrating significant improvements over strong baselines.

## Main Contributions
1. A novel method for obtaining language-independent features for cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification.
2. Comprehensive evaluation showing effectiveness across both high-resource and low-resource languages.
3. Demonstration that wikifier features improve monolingual models, not just cross-lingual transfer.

## Strengths

1. **Novel and Practical Feature Extraction**: The core contribution of using Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as language-independent features is both clever and practical. The approach only requires multilingual Wikipedia, which is readily available, and avoids the need for parallel text or language-specific heuristics. This makes the method highly accessible and implementable for researchers working with low-resource languages.

2. **Strong Empirical Results**: The paper demonstrates significant improvements over strong baselines across multiple languages. For low-resource languages, the proposed method outperforms Zhang et al. (2016), which requires "one-hour interaction with a native speaker" - a substantial advantage for practical applications. The direct transfer results (62.10 F1 on English→Dutch) significantly outperform Täckström's baseline (48.4 F1), showing the value of the proposed features.

3. **Thorough Analysis**: The paper provides a thoughtful analysis of why the approach works, including how Wikipedia size affects performance and why wikifier features are particularly valuable for non-Latin script languages. The ablation study (Table 3) clearly demonstrates the importance of FreeBase and interlanguage links for feature quality.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Comparison to Recent Work**: The paper compares against Täckström (2012) and Zhang et al. (2016), but doesn't compare against more recent state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods. For example, recent work by Ruder et al. (2019) or the emergence of multilingual BERT-based approaches for cross-lingual NER should be considered to establish the paper's position in the current landscape.

2. **Insufficient Detail on Wikifier Modification**: While the paper mentions modifications to the cross-lingual wikifier for n-gram processing, it lacks technical details on how exactly the system was adapted. Without understanding these modifications, it's difficult to assess the novelty or reproducibility of the approach. The critical detail about "generating title candidates only according to the whole string, not individual tokens" should be elaborated.

3. **Lack of Discussion on Limitations**: The paper doesn't adequately address the limitations of the approach, particularly for extremely low-resource languages. While Table 3 shows the impact of Wikipedia size, it doesn't discuss how the approach would perform on languages with minimal Wikipedia coverage (e.g., languages with fewer than 10,000 articles). This is a significant practical concern.

## Questions to Authors

1. Could you provide more details about the modifications made to the Tsai and Roth (2016) wikifier for n-gram processing? Specifically, how did you handle the candidate generation for n-grams where n>1?

2. How would your approach perform on languages with extremely small Wikipedia coverage (e.g., languages with fewer than 10,000 articles)? Could you provide an estimate of the minimum Wikipedia size required for the approach to be effective?

3. How does your approach compare to multilingual BERT-based cross-lingual NER methods that have emerged since this paper's work? Given the current state-of-the-art, does your method still offer advantages, particularly in terms of resource requirements?

## Overall Assessment

This paper presents a well-motivated and practical approach to cross-lingual NER that offers significant advantages for low-resource languages. The core contribution of using Wikipedia categories as language-independent features is novel and valuable, and the empirical results are convincing. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field, particularly for researchers working with languages that lack annotated data. While the paper would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison to recent methods and greater detail on the wikifier modifications, the main contribution is strong and well-supported by the evidence presented. I recommend acceptance.
