

# Peer Review: "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

## Summary and Main Contributions
The paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that leverages cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features from FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories. The authors demonstrate that grounding words to English Wikipedia provides strong signals for NER across languages without requiring parallel data. The main contributions are: (1) a practical, resource-efficient cross-lingual NER method requiring only multilingual Wikipedia; (2) empirical demonstration that wikifier features are strong language-independent signals; and (3) showing these features improve both direct transfer and monolingual NER.

## Strengths
1. **Practicality and Simplicity**: The approach is remarkably simple yet effective. By using a cross-lingual wikifier to generate features, the method avoids the need for parallel data or complex multilingual models. The only requirement is a multilingual Wikipedia dump, making it highly accessible. The paper's key insight—that FreeBase types provide strong signals even with imperfect disambiguation (Figure 1)—is compelling and well-validated.

2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors provide thorough evaluation across both high-resource (CoNLL) and low-resource languages (REFLEX/LORELEI), including an insightful analysis of how Wikipedia size impacts performance. The demonstration that wikifier features work best for non-Latin scripts (where lexical features fail) is particularly valuable for addressing a significant gap in cross-lingual NER research.

3. **Insightful Analysis**: Beyond just reporting numbers, the authors analyze *why* their approach works. The correlation between Wikipedia size and performance quality (Table 3), the demonstration that wikifier features outperform lexical features for non-Latin scripts, and the analysis of training on multiple languages (Figure 2) provide deep insights that advance the field.

## Weaknesses
1. **Understated Limitations for Small Wikipedias**: The paper acknowledges performance drops for languages with small Wikipedias (Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) but fails to address how to handle this limitation. The analysis in Table 3 shows that removing interlanguage links degrades performance, but the authors don't propose solutions for languages with minimal Wikipedia coverage—critical for real-world deployment.

2. **Implementation Complexity**: While claiming the method "only requires multilingual Wikipedia," the cross-lingual wikifier (Tsai and Roth, 2016) requires significant infrastructure (FreeBase API access, multilingual Wikipedia processing). The paper doesn't discuss computational requirements or practical implementation challenges, limiting reproducibility and real-world applicability.

3. **Incomplete Comparison with State-of-the-Art**: The comparison with Zhang et al. (2016) is limited to the low-resource languages section. The authors don't directly compare with more recent cross-lingual NER methods like those using multilingual BERT (e.g., Zhang et al., 2020), which would better establish their method's novelty and competitiveness. The comparison with Täckström (2012) is adequate but doesn't address more recent advances.

4. **Lack of Feature Contribution Analysis**: The paper doesn't analyze how much each wikifier feature (FreeBase types vs. Wikipedia categories) contributes to performance. Understanding which signals are most valuable would guide future research and feature engineering, but the authors don't provide this critical analysis.

## Questions to Authors
1. How would you handle languages with very small Wikipedias (e.g., less than 10K articles)? Would you consider augmenting with other knowledge sources?

2. Could you provide more details on the computational requirements of your approach? How long does it take to process a large multilingual Wikipedia dump?

3. Why does the performance of wikifier features degrade more for Bengali and Tamil compared to other low-resource languages? Is it solely due to Wikipedia size, or are there other factors (e.g., script complexity)?

## Overall Assessment
This paper presents a practical and effective approach to cross-lingual NER that leverages Wikipedia as a universal resource. While the method is elegant and well-validated, its limitations for low-resource languages and incomplete comparison with recent work are notable. The core contribution—using wikifier features as language-independent signals for NER—remains valuable and could be combined with other techniques for further improvement. With minor clarifications on implementation and limitations, this work would make a strong contribution to the field.
