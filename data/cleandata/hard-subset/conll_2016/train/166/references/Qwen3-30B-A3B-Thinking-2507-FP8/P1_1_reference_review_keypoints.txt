- Proposes a cross-lingual NER method that uses a cross-lingual wikifier to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia and extract language-independent features (FreeBase types, Wiki categories).  
- Claims the method requires only multilingual Wikipedia, not parallel text or language-specific heuristics.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements across multiple languages (CoNLL and five low-resource non-Latin-script languages).  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation against strong baselines and comparisons in direct transfer and monolingual settings.  
- Analyzes how Wikipedia size and interlanguage link availability affect feature quality and NER performance.  
- Strength: novel, simple, and effective feature-engineering approach valuable for low-resource languages.  
- Strength: broad and thorough evaluation showing gains over baselines, especially for small-Wikipedia languages.  
- Strength: practical analysis linking Wikipedia coverage to feature usefulness and limitations.  
- Weakness: lacks quantification of how wikifier errors/disambiguation mistakes impact downstream NER performance.  
- Weakness: baseline comparison is incomplete/unfair—doesn't compare to Täckström et al.'s bitext-cluster results nor test combining approaches.  
- Weakness: monolingual model does not reach state-of-the-art and the paper does not explain why wikifier features underperform in monolingual settings.  
- Question: how does wikifier accuracy (e.g., a 10% error rate) quantitatively affect NER performance?  
- Question: have the authors tried or considered combining their wikification features with Täckström's bitext-induced clusters, and what are the technical challenges?  
- Question: what specific limitations of the feature set prevent reaching monolingual SOTA, and how might they be addressed?  
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address baseline comparisons and deeper analysis of wikifier impact; method is practically valuable for low-resource NER.
