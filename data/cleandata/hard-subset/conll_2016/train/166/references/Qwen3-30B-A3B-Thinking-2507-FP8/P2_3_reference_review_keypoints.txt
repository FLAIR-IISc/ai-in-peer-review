- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to ground tokens to English Wikipedia and extract language-independent features (Freebase types, Wikipedia categories).

- Claims contributions: wikifier features are strong language-independent signals, enable direct transfer to low-resource languages without parallel data, and validated across 9 languages.

- Novel/practical idea: uses wikification as features (not downstream), removing need for parallel text or language-specific heuristics and working on non-Latin scripts.

- Strong empirical validation: thorough evaluation and ablations across 9 languages showing wikifier features outperform lexical features for non-Latin scripts and improve results when combined.

- Insightful analysis: identifies when wikifier vs lexical features are useful and studies effect of Wikipedia size on performance.

- Lacks comparison to recent multilingual transformer models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), making state-of-the-art positioning unclear.

- Insufficient analysis of wikifier errors and how incorrect disambiguation affects NER performance.

- Does not address computational cost or scalability of running the wikifier on large corpora.

- Asks whether newer wikifiers (e.g., mBERT-based) would improve low-resource performance.

- Requests details on training data sizes for low-resource vs. CoNLL languages and their impact on results.

- Asks for clarification on scaling when training with multiple source languages and potential diminishing returns.

- Overall: solid, practical contribution with comprehensive evaluation; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to compare with recent models and discuss computational/wikifier limitations.
