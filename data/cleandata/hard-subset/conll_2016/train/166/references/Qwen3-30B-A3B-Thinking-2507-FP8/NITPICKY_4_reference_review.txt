

# Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

## Summary
This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach using a cross-lingual wikifier to ground words to English Wikipedia, extracting language-independent features (FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories) for direct transfer of NER models across languages. The authors claim their method outperforms existing direct transfer approaches on CoNLL languages and low-resource languages without requiring parallel text or native speaker interaction.

## Main Contributions (as I see them)
1. Demonstrating that cross-lingual wikification features can serve as effective language-independent features for NER
2. Providing evidence that these features work well even with imperfect disambiguation
3. Showing that the approach can be applied to languages with very small Wikipedia sizes (though performance drops)

## Strengths

1. **Novel application of wikification for language-independent features**: The paper correctly identifies that Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types provide valuable language-independent signals for NER, which is a useful insight that hasn't been sufficiently explored in previous work.

2. **Comprehensive evaluation across multiple language types**: The evaluation on both CoNLL languages (Spanish, Dutch, German) and five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) provides a broad assessment of the approach's applicability.

3. **Clear demonstration of the importance of Wikipedia size**: The analysis showing how Wikipedia size affects feature quality (Table 3) is valuable and provides useful insights for future work.

## Weaknesses

1. **Critical technical error in related work section**: The paper states "polyglot word embeddings (Al-Rfou et al., 2013) are used as features in their NER model. Although the features are delexicalized, the embeddings are unique to each language, and so the model cannot transfer." This is factually incorrect. Polyglot word embeddings are explicitly designed to be cross-lingual, meaning they are language-independent. This error significantly undermines the paper's credibility and suggests a fundamental misunderstanding of the cited work.

2. **Overstated claims about applicability**: The paper claims "The only requirement to build the cross-lingual wikifier model is a multilingual Wikipedia dump, and it can be trivially applied to all languages in Wikipedia." This is misleading because Table 2 clearly shows that performance drops significantly for languages with small Wikipedia sizes (Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), contradicting the claim of "trivial application" to all languages.

3. **Inconsistent and unclear methodological details**: The paper states "We modify the system in the following two ways" for the wikifier, but fails to provide clear evidence of why these modifications are necessary or how they impact performance. The description of the modification for n-grams (n>1) is particularly confusing and lacks justification.

4. **Formatting and presentation errors**: Table 2 has a critical formatting error where the header "APPROACH EN NL DE ES TR TL YO BN TA AVG" lists 9 languages but only 8 columns are provided (EN, NL, DE, ES, TR, TL, YO, BN, TA = 9, but only 8 columns shown). This is a serious formatting error that indicates poor attention to detail.

5. **Inadequate explanation of key decisions**: The paper states "We set n to 4 in all our experiments" without providing any justification or ablation study to show why n=4 was chosen over other values (n=2, 3, or 5). This lack of explanation makes it difficult to assess the methodological soundness of the approach.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper claims polyglot word embeddings are language-specific, which contradicts the original work. Can you clarify this misunderstanding and provide the correct citation for the cross-lingual nature of these embeddings?

2. Why did you choose n=4 for n-grams when most NER systems use n=1 (words) or n=2 (bigrams)? Could you provide an ablation study showing the impact of different n-gram sizes?

3. In the section on "Quality of Wikifier Features," you mention removing the FreeBase query step for high-resource languages. How did you handle this for low-resource languages where FreeBase is unavailable? What alternative approach was used?

## Conclusion

While the paper presents a potentially useful approach to cross-lingual NER, the significant technical errors, overstatements about applicability, and methodological inconsistencies are substantial flaws that undermine the paper's credibility. The critical error in the related work section (misrepresenting polyglot embeddings as language-specific) is particularly concerning as it suggests the authors may not fully understand the related work. Additionally, the formatting error in Table 2 indicates poor attention to detail, which is especially problematic for a paper claiming to be a "perfect" solution to cross-lingual NER.

These flaws, while individually minor, collectively indicate a lack of rigorous attention to detail that is required for publication at a venue like CoNLL. The paper's claims about the universality of their approach are not supported by the evidence presented, and the methodological ambiguities make it difficult to assess the true contribution of the work.

Given the paper's significant technical errors and overstatements, I recommend rejection.
