

# Peer Review: Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper proposes a novel approach for cross-lingual NER using a cross-lingual wikifier to generate language-independent features. The key insight is that by grounding words to English Wikipedia entries, FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories can serve as universal features for NER across languages. The authors evaluate their method on both high-resource (CoNLL languages) and low-resource (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) languages, demonstrating significant improvements over strong baselines.

The main contributions in order of strength:
1. The development of a language-independent feature extraction method using cross-lingual wikification that requires only multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel text or annotated data in target languages)
2. Comprehensive evaluation showing significant improvements over direct transfer baselines on both high-resource and low-resource languages
3. Insightful analysis demonstrating the correlation between Wikipedia size and feature quality, particularly for non-Latin script languages

## Strengths

1. **Novel and Well-Motivated Approach**: The paper presents a clear and compelling argument for using cross-lingual wikification as a source of language-independent features. The example in Figure 1 effectively demonstrates how FreeBase types provide useful NER signals even with imperfect disambiguation. This is a significant contribution to the cross-lingual NER field, as it addresses a key challenge (language-specific lexical features) without requiring parallel data or language-specific heuristics.

2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors evaluate on a diverse set of languages (CoNLL languages and five low-resource languages), providing strong evidence for their approach's generalizability. The comparison with relevant baselines (Täckström et al., Zhang et al.) is thorough and shows clear improvements, particularly on low-resource languages where their approach outperforms methods requiring more resources (e.g., native speaker interaction).

3. **Insightful Analysis of Wikipedia Size Impact**: Section 4.4 provides valuable empirical analysis showing that the quality of wikifier features correlates directly with Wikipedia size. The experiments removing interlanguage links (Table 3) convincingly demonstrate that Wikipedia size is the key factor affecting performance, especially for non-Latin script languages. This analysis is crucial for understanding the limitations and potential of the approach.

4. **Demonstrated Utility Beyond Transfer Learning**: The paper shows that wikifier features improve monolingual NER models as well as cross-lingual transfer, indicating the features have broader utility beyond the specific transfer setting. This strengthens the case for the general value of the approach.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Comparison with Recent Multilingual Approaches**: The paper doesn't compare with state-of-the-art multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R) that have shown strong performance in cross-lingual NER. These approaches have become the new baseline for cross-lingual transfer tasks, and the absence of such comparison weakens the paper's position in the current landscape. For instance, mBERT has achieved strong results on CoNLL languages without requiring the wikification pipeline described here.

2. **Underdeveloped Handling of Non-Latin Scripts**: While the paper demonstrates the approach works for non-Latin scripts (Bengali, Tamil), the performance on these languages remains poor (F1 around 27-28). The authors acknowledge this is due to FreeBase not containing these languages, but don't propose any solutions. This is a significant limitation, as the approach fails to deliver meaningful results for languages with no FreeBase coverage.

3. **Ad-Hoc Wikifier Modifications**: The paper describes two modifications to the existing wikifier for n-gram processing (Section 3.3), but these are presented as afterthoughts without sufficient justification or evaluation. The authors don't demonstrate that these modifications are necessary or optimal, which weakens the reproducibility of their method.

4. **Lack of Computational Analysis**: The paper doesn't address the computational cost of their approach, particularly the n-gram processing step. Processing every n-gram in a document for wikification could be computationally expensive, especially for large documents or languages with complex morphology. This is an important practical consideration for real-world applications.

## Questions to Authors

1. How do you plan to handle languages with no FreeBase coverage (Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) in a more effective way? Would you consider using alternative knowledge sources or modifying the wikification process for these languages?

2. Have you considered comparing your approach with multilingual language models (e.g., XLM-R) for cross-lingual NER? Given their strong performance on transfer tasks, this would provide a more relevant baseline for the current state of the art.

3. The paper states that "the only requirement to build the cross-lingual wikifier model is a multilingual Wikipedia dump." However, the approach relies on FreeBase for entity types, which is unavailable for many languages. How do you propose to address this limitation in your future work?

## Overall Recommendation

This is a well-written paper with a strong core idea that makes a meaningful contribution to cross-lingual NER. The methodology is sound, the evaluation is comprehensive, and the insights about Wikipedia size are valuable. The main weaknesses are the limited comparison with recent multilingual approaches and the poor performance on non-FreeBase languages. If the authors can address these limitations in the revision (particularly by comparing with current state-of-the-art multilingual models), this paper would make a strong contribution to the field.

The paper would benefit from a more balanced discussion of limitations and a clearer position relative to recent advances in multilingual NLP. Nevertheless, the core contribution of using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features is novel and valuable. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
