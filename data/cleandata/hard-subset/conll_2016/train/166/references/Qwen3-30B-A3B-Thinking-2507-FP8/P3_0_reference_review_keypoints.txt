- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER by grounding text to English Wikipedia and using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features, needing only multilingual Wikipedia and improving over direct-transfer baselines.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces wikification-based cross-lingual NER using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as features.  
- Contribution 2: Modifies the wikifier to process all n-grams (not just named entities) to extract features for every word.  
- Contribution 3: Provides comprehensive evaluation across CoNLL and multiple low-resource languages, showing consistent improvements.  
- Strength: Strong practical motivation and simplicity, requiring minimal resources and yielding large gains for non-Latin scripts (e.g., Bengali, Tamil).  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation with multiple baselines and multilingual training analyses showing tangible F1 improvements.  
- Strength: Clear evidence that wikifier-derived features are language-independent and can outperform lexical features for non-Latin scripts.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of why performance degrades for languages with small Wikipedia coverage and lack of mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness: Insufficient differentiation from prior work (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007; Sil & Yates 2013) and unclear novelty boundaries.  
- Weakness: No quantification of how wikifier/disambiguation errors impact NER performance—missing error-correlation analysis.  
- Weakness: No discussion of computational cost for processing all n-grams, which may be significant in practice.  
- Questions for authors: Provide analysis of performance vs. target Wikipedia size, effect of expanding FreeBase coverage for underrepresented languages, and which FreeBase types drive gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen limitation analysis and clarify differences from prior work.
