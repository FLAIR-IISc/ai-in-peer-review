- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to map n-grams to English Wikipedia and extract FreeBase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features, evaluated on 9 languages.

- Contribution claims: (1) method for wikification-based language-independent features, (2) model outperforming direct transfer baselines on CoNLL, (3) applicability to low-resource languages.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation arguing FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories are robust language-independent signals, with analysis showing imperfect disambiguation can still help.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across high- and low-resource languages and useful analysis showing performance correlates with Wikipedia coverage.

- Strength: Strong baseline comparisons, showing improvements over Täckström (2012) and outperforming Zhang et al. (2016) for low-resource settings.

- Weakness: Claims of "no parallel data" are misleading because interlanguage links in Wikipedia act as a form of parallel data and determine wikifier quality.

- Weakness: Insufficient evidence that FreeBase types are truly language-independent or map consistently across languages and contexts.

- Weakness: Limited effectiveness on very low-resource languages (e.g., Yoruba F1 = 6.72) with no substantive remedies proposed.

- Weakness: No comparison with modern multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), weakening claims of state-of-the-art performance.

- Question: Please clarify that interlanguage links constitute parallel data and explain relation to other Wikipedia-based cross-lingual methods.

- Question: Provide analysis showing FreeBase type mappings are consistent across languages and align with entity types in target languages.

- Question: How would the approach be modified for extremely low-resource languages or how will the paper acknowledge its fundamental limitations?

- Overall assessment: Well-executed and insightful with useful empirical findings, but overstates novelty and resource claims and omits crucial comparisons, making it an incremental contribution unless limitations are more precisely addressed.
