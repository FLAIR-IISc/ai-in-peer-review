

# Peer Review: Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification

## Summary
This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that leverages a cross-lingual wikifier to ground words to English Wikipedia, using resulting FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features. The authors demonstrate their method on CoNLL languages (Spanish, Dutch, German) and five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), showing improvements over direct transfer baselines.

## Main Contributions (as I see them)
1. Demonstrating that cross-lingual wikification provides strong language-independent features for NER, which can be used for direct transfer without parallel data
2. Comprehensive evaluation across multiple languages, including non-Latin script languages, showing the approach's viability for low-resource settings
3. Analysis of how Wikipedia size affects feature quality, providing important insights into the approach's limitations

## Strengths
1. **Novel Application of Wikification for NER Features**: The paper makes a compelling case that wikification (traditionally a downstream task of NER) can provide valuable language-independent features. The analysis in Figure 1 and Table 3 convincingly shows that even imperfect disambiguation yields useful FreeBase types that serve as strong NER signals, especially for low-resource languages. This insight is valuable for the community.

2. **Strong Empirical Evaluation**: The paper provides thorough experiments across multiple languages (including non-Latin scripts) and demonstrates consistent improvements over strong baselines. The comparison with Täckström et al. (2012) and Zhang et al. (2016) is well-justified, showing 10+ point improvements on Dutch, German, and Turkish. The monolingual experiments (Table 2) also validate that wikifier features are valuable even in monolingual settings.

3. **Insightful Analysis of Limitations**: The paper's analysis of how Wikipedia size affects performance (Table 3) is particularly valuable. It clearly explains why the approach works better for some languages than others, and the experiments with randomly removing interlanguage links demonstrate the direct relationship between Wikipedia size and feature quality. This is crucial for the community to understand the approach's applicability.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Novelty**: While the application to cross-lingual NER is valuable, the core idea of using Wikipedia categories as features for NER has been explored before (Kazama and Torisawa, 2007). The paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish how their cross-lingual extension differs from prior work beyond the multilingual aspect. The authors cite this work but don't adequately address how their approach improves upon it.

2. **Mischaracterization of "No Parallel Data" Requirement**: The paper claims their method "requires no sentence-aligned or word-aligned parallel text," but it actually depends on Wikipedia's interlanguage links, which are a form of parallel data (albeit not sentence-aligned). This is a significant oversight that weakens the paper's key claim about the approach's requirements. The authors should clarify that they use a form of parallel data (interlanguage links) but avoid sentence-level alignment.

3. **Insufficient Discussion of Scalability**: The paper doesn't address how the approach would perform for languages with virtually no Wikipedia presence (e.g., many African languages). The experiments on Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil (Table 2) already show degraded performance due to Wikipedia size limitations, but the paper doesn't propose solutions or discuss how to extend the approach to such cases.

4. **Lack of Computational Analysis**: The paper doesn't discuss the computational cost of their approach, particularly the modification to wikify all n-grams (n≤4). For large documents, this could be significantly more expensive than standard NER, which is a practical consideration for real-world deployment.

## Questions to Authors
1. How does your approach compare to recent work on multilingual NER that uses multilingual BERT or similar models (e.g., Devlin et al., 2019)? Have you considered using such models as a baseline?

2. The paper states that the method requires "only multilingual Wikipedia, no sentence-aligned or word-aligned parallel text is needed." Given that interlanguage links constitute parallel data, could you clarify the exact nature of the parallel data your method requires?

3. For languages with very small Wikipedia (like Yoruba), would incorporating other multilingual resources (e.g., multilingual knowledge bases beyond Wikipedia) improve performance? Have you considered this direction?

This paper makes a valuable contribution to cross-lingual NER, particularly in demonstrating the utility of wikification for language-independent features. While the novelty is somewhat limited, the thorough evaluation and insightful analysis of limitations significantly strengthen the paper. Addressing the weaknesses noted above would substantially improve the paper's impact.
