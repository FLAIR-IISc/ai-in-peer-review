- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to map n-grams to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features.
- Shows strong performance on CoNLL languages and several low-resource languages, outperforming baselines that use parallel data or native speakers.
- Main contribution: demonstrates wikification as a powerful feature source for zero-shot cross-lingual NER.
- Main contribution: provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation across resource-rich and low-resource languages.
- Main contribution: analyzes feature importance and the effect of Wikipedia size on performance.
- Strength: strong empirical results with clear tables and significant gains on low-resource languages.
- Strength: novel, practical approach shifting from NER-to-wikification to wikification-to-NER without needing parallel resources or language-specific rules.
- Strength: detailed ablations showing the role of Freebase and interlanguage links.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental setup and experiments combining multiple training languages.
- Strength: clear, well-written paper with helpful illustrations.
- Weakness: individual components are established; novelty is mainly in their combination and application.
- Weakness: reliance on English Wikipedia may introduce coverage and cultural biases.
- Weakness: feature set is somewhat hand-engineered and could benefit from learned feature approaches.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—unclear which entity types or languages cause failures.
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice/quality of the cross-lingual wikifier and have other wikifiers been tested?
- Question: How can reliance on Freebase be mitigated for languages with limited Freebase coverage?
- Question: Have you observed bias toward entities well-represented in English Wikipedia, and can you provide examples?
- Question: Any preliminary experiments combining this approach with parallel projection and their outcomes?
- Additional comment: Related work discussion should focus more on the intersection of wikification and NER.
- Additional comment: Provide a more detailed description of the Tsai and Roth (2016) cross-lingual wikifier for reproducibility.
- Overall: Strong paper with novel, effective approach and thorough evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
