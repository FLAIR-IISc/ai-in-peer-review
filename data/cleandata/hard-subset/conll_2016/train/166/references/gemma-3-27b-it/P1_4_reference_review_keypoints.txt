- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features.
- Shows empirical improvements over direct transfer baselines on CoNLL (ES, NL, DE) and five low-resource languages (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA).
- Demonstrates that wikifier features can boost monolingual models and benefit multi-source training.
- Main contribution: reframing wikification as a source of language-independent features for NER rather than only entity linking.
- Main contribution: strong empirical gains in low-resource settings without parallel corpora or native supervision.
- Main contribution: comprehensive experiments including ablations, Wikipedia size analysis, and multi-source training.
- Strength: novel and intuitive idea to use cross-lingual wikification features for NER.
- Strength: consistent performance improvements, notably for non-Latin-script and low-resource languages.
- Strength: well-structured experiments and insightful ablation analyses.
- Strength: clear, concise writing and helpful illustrative figure.
- Strength: method is reproducible using publicly available resources (Wikipedia, Tsai & Roth wikifier).
- Weakness: core NER architecture (Ratinov & Roth 2009) lacks substantial novelty—work focuses on feature engineering.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on English Wikipedia may introduce bias and limit performance for culturally distant languages.
- Weakness: feature interaction analysis between wikifier and baseline features is superficial.
- Weakness: lacks detailed qualitative error analysis to explain failure modes (ambiguity, incorrect wikification, etc.).
- Question: how does reliance on English Wikipedia affect languages with different cultural knowledge, and how to mitigate bias?
- Question: can the authors provide a detailed analysis of interactions between wikifier features and baseline features?
- Question: were alternative cross-lingual wikifiers tested, and how sensitive are results to the choice of wikifier?
- Question: can the authors provide qualitative error analysis with concrete examples?
- Additional comment: related work coverage is comprehensive but could more sharply state the specific gap addressed.
- Additional comment: table layouts are overly wide and could be simplified or split.
- Additional comment: report computational cost of wikification, especially for large documents.
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution with strong low-resource results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address bias and deeper feature/error analyses.
