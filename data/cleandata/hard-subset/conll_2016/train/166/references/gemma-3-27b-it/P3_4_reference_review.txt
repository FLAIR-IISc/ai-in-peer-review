## Review of “Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification”

**Summary:** This paper presents a cross-lingual NER model leveraging a cross-lingual wikifier to generate language-independent features. The core idea is to ground n-grams in test documents to English Wikipedia, utilizing the resulting Wikipedia categories and Freebase types as features for a NER model trained primarily on English data. The authors demonstrate significant performance gains over baseline methods on CoNLL datasets and achieve state-of-the-art results on several low-resource languages.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Demonstration of Wikification as a Feature Source for Cross-Lingual NER:** The paper convincingly shows that cross-lingual wikification provides surprisingly effective language-independent features for NER, enabling strong performance even with limited or no target language annotations. This is the most significant contribution.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The model achieves notable improvements over existing direct transfer approaches on both resource-rich (Spanish, Dutch, German) and low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), including outperforming a system requiring native speaker interaction.
3. **Comprehensive Analysis:**  The paper includes a valuable analysis of the impact of Wikipedia size on performance, and investigates the benefits of multi-lingual training.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel and Effective Approach:** The core idea of using wikification to create language-agnostic features is innovative.  While wikification has been explored in NLP, its application *as a feature engineering step* for direct transfer NER, rather than as a post-processing linking task, is a significant contribution. The results demonstrate this approach is not merely theoretically interesting, but practically effective.
2. **Strong Experimental Validation:**  The authors provide a thorough experimental setup, evaluating their model on a diverse set of languages, including both CoNLL datasets and several low-resource languages. The inclusion of low-resource languages is particularly valuable, as this is where cross-lingual transfer is most crucial. The comparisons to existing state-of-the-art methods are well-defined and clearly presented.
3. **Insightful Error Analysis:**  The analysis regarding Wikipedia size and its impact on feature quality (Table 3) is very helpful and provides a clear understanding of the limitations and dependencies of the approach. It shows a strong understanding of the underlying mechanisms.
4. **Well-Written and Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the methodology and understand the experimental results.  Figure 1 is a particularly effective illustration of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Components:**  While the *combination* of techniques is novel, the individual components (cross-lingual wikification, feature-based NER with averaged perceptron) are not new.  The paper relies heavily on existing systems (Tsai & Roth's wikifier, Ratinov & Roth's NER model) and doesn’t present substantial innovation in these areas. The novelty lies in the application and insightful feature engineering.
2. **Dependence on English Wikipedia:** The entire system’s performance is heavily reliant on the quality and coverage of the English Wikipedia. While this is acknowledged, the paper doesn't explore potential mitigation strategies for scenarios where the English Wikipedia is incomplete or biased.  The analysis of low-resource languages highlights this dependency.  Could other central Wikipedias (e.g., German) be used to create alternate feature sets?
3. **Feature Engineering Complexity:** The feature set, while effective, seems quite complex (multiple gazetteer features, wikifier features of varying n-grams). While the ablation studies show the benefit of the Wikifier features, a more detailed analysis of the *contribution of each individual feature* within the wikifier component would be valuable.  Are certain Freebase types more predictive than others? Are longer n-grams consistently more informative?
4. **Limited Discussion of Error Cases:** While the analysis of Wikipedia size is helpful, a deeper qualitative analysis of the types of errors the model makes would be beneficial.  Are there specific entity types that are consistently misclassified? Are there particular linguistic phenomena that pose challenges for the wikifier?



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **English Wikipedia Bias:** You acknowledge the reliance on English Wikipedia. Have you considered exploring alternative strategies, such as leveraging multiple central Wikipedias, or incorporating techniques to mitigate potential biases in the English Wikipedia data?
2. **Feature Importance:**  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the importance of different features within the wikifier feature set? Specifically, which Freebase types and n-gram lengths contribute the most to performance?
3. **Error Analysis:** Can you provide more specific examples of the types of errors the model makes, and discuss potential reasons for these errors?  A qualitative analysis of misclassified entities would be helpful.
4. **Computational Cost:** What is the computational cost of generating the wikifier features, particularly for very large documents or corpora?  Is this a practical limitation for real-world applications?




**Additional Comments:**

* The paper cites (Sil and Yates, 2013) as not being scalable due to needing a trained NER system. However, the authors are themselves using a pre-trained NER system (Ratinov and Roth, 2009) as a base. Clarifying this distinction might be helpful.
* It would be interesting to see a comparison to more recent NER models (e.g., those based on transformers) to see if the wikifier features can also improve their performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and effective approach to cross-lingual NER. While the individual components are not groundbreaking, the combination of techniques, combined with the thorough experimental evaluation and insightful analysis, makes a significant contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
