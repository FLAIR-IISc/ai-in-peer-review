- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia and use Wikipedia categories/Freebase types as language-independent features.  
- Demonstrates significant gains over direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL languages and achieves SOTA on several low-resource languages; also improves monolingual models and benefits multilingual training.  
- Main contribution: novel use of wikification as feature engineering for NER rather than as post-processing entity linking.  
- Main contribution: strong empirical results on low-resource languages without using parallel data or native-speaker input.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive experimental analysis including ablations and studies on Wikipedia size and inter-language link density.  
- Strength: simple, elegant, and scalable approach relying only on Wikipedia data.  
- Strength: consistent performance improvements across multiple languages, particularly impressive on low-resource languages.  
- Strength: thorough ablation studies that clarify the importance of Freebase and other components.  
- Strength: clear writing and helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Strength: practical and easy to scale to new languages using Wikipedia dumps.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on English Wikipedia may introduce bias and coverage limitations that aren’t fully explored.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent pre-trained multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R) to contextualize results.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of wikifier error propagation and sensitivity of NER to linking errors.  
- Weakness: feature set complexity and reliance on wikifier output details may require substantial tuning per language.  
- Question: How does English Wikipedia bias affect performance and will you incorporate other Wikipedia languages to mitigate it?  
- Question: Have you tried integrating wikifier features into pre-trained multilingual models (e.g., fine-tuning mBERT) and how do results compare?  
- Question: Can you provide a detailed error analysis of the wikifier and identify entity types prone to mis-linking?  
- Question: What is the relative importance of Base, Gazetteer, and Wikifier features, and have you performed feature selection per language?  
- Additional comment: Discussion of low-resource languages and Table 2 are strong; consider adding a limitations/future work section.  
- Overall: Strong, novel, and empirically compelling paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
