- Proposal: Use cross-lingual wikification to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features for NER.

- Empirical result: Significant improvements on CoNLL (Spanish, Dutch, German) and state-of-the-art gains in direct-transfer for low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).

- Contribution 1: Novel use of wikification as input feature engineering for NER rather than a downstream/post-processing step.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates practical, strong performance for low-resource languages without parallel data or native speaker input.

- Contribution 3: Thorough experimental validation including ablations (Freebase links, Wikipedia size) and multi-source training analyses.

- Strength: Novel reframing of wikification–NER interaction that opens new research directions.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains across diverse languages and competitive baselines.

- Strength: Detailed ablation studies that shed light on which components drive improvements.

- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation and illustrative figures.

- Strength: Practical and scalable approach relying only on multilingual Wikipedia.

- Weakness: Dependence on a single wikification system (Tsai & Roth 2016) without comparison to alternatives.

- Weakness: Possible bias from grounding to English Wikipedia; some concepts/categories may be missing or differently represented.

- Weakness: Potential feature complexity and high dimensionality; limited discussion of feature selection or overfitting risk.

- Weakness: Lack of qualitative error analysis to illustrate common failure modes.

- Question: How sensitive is NER performance to varying quality of the cross-lingual wikifier or different wikification systems?

- Question: Can the authors quantify or illustrate biases introduced by grounding to English Wikipedia across languages or entity types?

- Question: Were any feature selection or dimensionality reduction techniques used, and what was their impact?

- Question: Can the authors provide examples of common errors, especially in low-resource languages?

- Additional comment: Related work and experimental setup are comprehensive and clear.

- Additional comment: Recommend adding a table summarizing key features for reference.

- Additional comment: Encourage releasing code to aid reproducibility.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address wikifier sensitivity and English Wikipedia bias.
