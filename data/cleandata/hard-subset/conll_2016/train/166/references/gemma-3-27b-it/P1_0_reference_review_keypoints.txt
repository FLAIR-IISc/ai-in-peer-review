- Proposes cross-lingual NER that grounds n-grams to English Wikipedia and uses Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as language-independent features.
- Demonstrates significant gains over direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL (Spanish, Dutch, German) and achieves state-of-the-art on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).
- Shows wikification features also improve monolingual models and multi-source training.
- Main contribution: effective feature engineering via cross-lingual wikification for NER.
- Main contribution: strong performance on low-resource languages where annotated data is scarce.
- Main contribution: comprehensive evaluation including ablations and baseline comparisons.
- Strength: novel use of wikification as a feature generator rather than a pre-/post-processing step.
- Strength: strong empirical results across multiple languages, boosting confidence in robustness.
- Strength: detailed ablation studies that clarify component importance and wikification characteristics.
- Strength: practical and scalable approach needing only a multilingual Wikipedia dump (no parallel corpora).
- Strength: clear presentation and sufficient details to facilitate reproducibility.
- Weakness: core NER model (Ratinov & Roth 2009) is not novel; innovation is mainly in features.
- Weakness: reliance on English Wikipedia may introduce cultural and coverage bias for some languages.
- Weakness: wikification errors exist and their full impact on NER is not thoroughly analyzed.
- Weakness: limited analysis of why certain wikifier features interact well with base NER features.
- Question: How does English Wikipedia bias affect performance for languages with different cultural/conceptual contexts, and could multilingual KGs help?
- Question: Request for a more detailed error analysis of the cross-lingual wikifier and which error types most harm NER.
- Question: Have techniques been explored to identify and leverage synergistic interactions between wikifier and base NER features?
- Question: What is the computational cost and scalability of wikifying every n-gram with respect to document length and Wikipedia size, and are optimizations possible?
- Additional notes: related work coverage is comprehensive, Table 2 is helpful, and conclusions summarize contributions and future directions.
- Overall assessment: strong, practical, well-executed paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
