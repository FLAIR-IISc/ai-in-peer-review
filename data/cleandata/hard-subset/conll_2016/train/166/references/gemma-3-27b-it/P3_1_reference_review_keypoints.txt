- Proposes cross-lingual NER that grounds n-grams to English Wikipedia and uses Wikipedia categories/Freebase types as language-independent features.
- Shows strong improvements over direct transfer methods on CoNLL (ES, NL, DE) and SOTA on five low-resource languages (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA).
- Demonstrates that wikification features also help monolingual models and multi-source training.
- Key contribution: using wikification output as feature generator for NER rather than treating wikification as the end task.
- Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation across both well-resourced and low-resourced languages.
- Analyzes the impact of Wikipedia size and interlanguage links on performance, highlighting method dependencies.
- Strength: novel and intuitive use of Wikipedia disambiguation signals to inform NER despite imperfect wikification.
- Strength: consistent, compelling empirical gains, notably outperforming systems relying on native speaker interaction.
- Strength: thorough ablation studies and feature-combination analyses that clarify what helps performance.
- Strength: clear presentation and reproducibility details, including citation of the wikification system used and data sources.
- Strength: practical and scalable approach requiring only Wikipedia dumps, avoiding parallel corpora or language-specific resources.
- Weakness: limited novelty in wikification itself—relies on existing Tsai & Roth (2016) system, making the contribution incremental.
- Weakness: potential bias from grounding to English Wikipedia and English-centric FreeBase/types, with limited deeper analysis of that bias.
- Weakness: feature engineering could be suboptimal; lacks exploration of language-agnostic features like POS, char-level embeddings, or subword units.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—paper does not deeply investigate failure modes by entity type or language.
- Questions for authors: how to mitigate English-Wikipedia bias or use multilingual KBs; whether character/subword features were tried; request for detailed error analysis; rationale for limiting n-grams to size 4 and scalability trade-offs.
- Additional comments: improve table formatting, expand discussion of training/test language combination observations, and provide full LDC identifiers in references.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with strong empirical evidence; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
