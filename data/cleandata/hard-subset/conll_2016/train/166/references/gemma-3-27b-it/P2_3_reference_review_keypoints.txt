- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia, using Freebase types and categories as language-independent features.  
- Shows improvements over direct transfer baselines on CoNLL and several low-resource languages.  
- Demonstrates that wikification provides valuable signals for cross-lingual NER even with imperfect disambiguation.  
- Presents strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive performance across diverse and low-resource languages.  
- Integrates wikifier features into monolingual and multi-source training to further boost performance.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation exploring Wikipedia size, feature combinations, and multi-source training effects.  
- Novel and practical approach by using wikification as a feature source rather than only pre-processing.  
- Particularly effective in low-resource settings, outperforming methods requiring parallel data or native speaker input.  
- Ablation studies clarify the importance of Freebase links, interlanguage link density, and Wikipedia size.  
- Well-written paper with clear methodology, thorough experimental setup, and organized results.  
- Multi-source training yields consistent additional gains, enhancing practical applicability.  
- Wikification component itself is not novel, relying on existing systems (Tsai and Roth, 2016).  
- Grounding everything to English Wikipedia introduces potential bias and may limit applicability for languages with cultural/conceptual differences.  
- Feature set is complex, creating significant engineering overhead and lacking exploration of feature reduction.  
- Lacks detailed qualitative error analysis to identify consistent failure modes or challenging entity types.  
- Asks authors to discuss mitigation of English-Wikipedia bias and the possibility of using multiple anchor languages.  
- Queries whether feature selection or dimensionality reduction was explored to simplify the model.  
- Requests a more detailed error analysis outlining common error types and problematic linguistic phenomena.  
- Asks for clarification on exclusion of MISC tags and its impact on reported results.  
- Notes missing full citation for Zhang et al. (2016) and suggests clearer visualization of Figure 1 examples.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical, and effective paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
