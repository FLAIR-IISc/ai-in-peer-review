- Proposes cross-lingual NER that grounds n-grams to English Wikipedia via a wikifier, using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features.  
- Demonstrates strong performance gains on CoNLL languages (ES, NL, DE) and several low-resource languages (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA), and improvements in monolingual and multi-source setups.  

Main contributions:
- Shows wikification can be used as feature engineering for cross-lingual NER rather than only a downstream task.  
- Reports significant empirical gains on low-resource languages, outperforming baselines that require native speaker interaction.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments including ablations, Wikipedia size impact, and multi-source training analysis.  

Strengths:
- Novel, practical approach that avoids expensive parallel data and leverages broadly available Wikipedia.  
- Strong, competitive results across multiple languages and baselines.  
- Thorough evaluation with informative ablation studies on feature contributions.  
- Clear writing and sufficient methodological detail to suggest reproducibility.  
- Insightful analysis on the role of FreeBase integration and interlanguage link quality.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited analysis of the impact of wikification errors and system robustness to mislinked entities.  
- Heavy reliance on English Wikipedia may introduce English-centric bias and limit generality.  
- Complex feature set with unclear relative importance and potential overfitting risk.  
- Lacks comparison to recent multilingual pre-trained models (e.g., multilingual BERT) and newer cross-lingual transfer methods.  

Questions for authors:
- Provide qualitative error analysis showing cases where incorrect wikification caused NER failures and which languages/entities are most affected.  
- Which wikifier features (FreeBase types, categories, etc.) are most informative according to feature importance metrics?  
- Have you considered using multilingual Wikipedia embeddings (e.g., mBERT) as features or alternatives?  
- Can the framework be adapted to other multilingual knowledge bases beyond FreeBase, and what challenges would that entail?  

Additional comments:
- Improve notation consistency (e.g., clarify “W.−FB query” in Table 3).  
- Report computational cost of the wikification step.  
- Present training-language results more clearly, e.g., a concise table of best training languages per test language.  

Overall assessment:
- Strong paper with a novel, effective approach and comprehensive evaluation; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
