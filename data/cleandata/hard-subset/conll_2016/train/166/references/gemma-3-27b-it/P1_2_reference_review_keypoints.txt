- Proposes cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia, using Wikipedia categories and Freebase types as language-independent features.  
- Demonstrates improved transfer from English to CoNLL languages (Spanish, Dutch, German) and SOTA on five low-resource languages.  
- Contribution: shows wikification as an effective, practical feature source for cross-lingual NER.  
- Contribution: provides comprehensive evaluation across resource-rich and low-resource languages.  
- Contribution: analyzes impact of Wikipedia size on wikifier feature effectiveness.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains, notably for low-resource languages without parallel data or native speakers.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated idea treating wikification as feature engineering for NER.  
- Strength: thorough ablation studies revealing component contributions (e.g., FreeBase, interlanguage links).  
- Strength: practical and scalable approach relying on readily available resources, minimal language-specific rules.  
- Strength: candid discussion of limitations, especially dependence on Wikipedia size and non-Latin scripts.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in the wikifier itself—relies on prior system (Tsai & Roth, 2016).  
- Weakness: complex feature set (base, gazetteer, wikifier); lacks detailed feature importance analysis.  
- Weakness: baseline NER model (Ratinov & Roth, 2009) is dated; comparison to transformer-based models is missing.  
- Weakness: English-centric grounding may introduce bias; unclear if other central languages would perform comparably.  
- Question: request for detailed feature importance breakdown across languages and settings.  
- Question: whether alternative or more advanced wikifiers were tested and their impact.  
- Question: possibility and results of integrating features into transformer-based NER (e.g., BERT).  
- Question: whether grounding to non-English Wikipedia editions was explored to reduce English-centric bias.  
- Additional comment: improve table formatting for readability.  
- Additional comment: clarify n-gram grounding modification with a visual example.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-evaluated paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
