- Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to produce language-independent features by grounding n-grams to English Wikipedia categories and Freebase types.  
- Demonstrates improvements over direct-transfer baselines and SOTA on several low-resource languages without parallel corpora or native speakers.

Main contributions:
- Shows cross-lingual wikification as a novel, effective source of language-independent features for NER.  
- Achieves strong empirical gains on challenging low-resource languages, outperforming human-interaction baselines.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments and ablations, including analysis of feature contributions and Wikipedia size effects.

Strengths:
- Novel reframing of wikification as a feature engineering step for cross-lingual NER.  
- Strong empirical validation across multiple languages and competitive baselines.  
- Detailed feature ablation studies clarify the contribution of wikifier, gazetteer, and base features.  
- Practical and scalable approach relying only on publicly available Wikipedia dumps.  
- Rigorous experimental design including multilingual training and careful analyses.

Weaknesses:
- Limited analysis of wikification errors and their specific impact on NER performance.  
- Feature set complexity (notably n-gram features) may hinder practical implementation and could be streamlined.  
- Dependence on English Wikipedia risks bias and misrepresentation of entities from other languages.  
- Insufficient explanation for why language-independent features also improve monolingual NER.

Questions for authors:
- Can you provide a detailed error analysis of the cross-lingual wikifier by entity type and language?  
- Have you tested simplifying the feature set (e.g., reducing max n-gram length) and its performance impact?  
- How do you mitigate biases from using English Wikipedia, and have you considered multilingual Freebase or alternatives?  
- Why do language-independent features improve monolingual NER—disambiguation only or other mechanisms?

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Related work coverage is adequate and relevant.  
- Inclusion of both CoNLL and low-resource languages strengthens the evaluation.  
- Table 2 effectively summarizes data sizes and results.

Overall assessment:
- Strong, impactful paper with novel idea and solid empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing wikification error analysis and feature simplification.
