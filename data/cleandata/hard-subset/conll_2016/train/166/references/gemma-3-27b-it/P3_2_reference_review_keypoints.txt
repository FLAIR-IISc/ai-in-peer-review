- Paper proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to map n-grams to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features.  
- Demonstrates improved results on CoNLL languages and SOTA on several low-resource languages in direct transfer from English.  
- Shows wikification is a strong, language-agnostic feature source that goes beyond simple gazetteers.  
- Presents an effective direct-transfer approach requiring no parallel data or language-specific rules.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation across standard and truly low-resource languages with varied scripts.  
- Novel framing of wikification as feature engineering rather than a preprocessing requirement.  
- Strong empirical performance with ablations highlighting contributions of different feature sets.  
- Clear experimental setup and insightful analyses (e.g., Wikipedia size, removing Freebase info).  
- Practical approach relying on publicly available Wikipedia and wikifier tools.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Limited analysis of specific wikification errors and their impact on NER performance.  
- Method depends heavily on English Wikipedia, introducing potential bias for typologically different languages.  
- Feature set is relatively complex; computational cost and feature-selection strategies are not discussed.  
- Lacks comparison to modern multilingual pretrained models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R) as stronger baselines.  
- Request for detailed error analysis of wikifier mis-links and their effect on entity types.  
- Question about mitigating English-Wikipedia bias and alternatives like other anchor languages or multilingual knowledge graphs.  
- Asks for insights on computational cost and possible feature simplification or selection.  
- Seeks comparison or discussion relative to recent cross-lingual models using multilingual pretraining.  
- Figure visualizing language pairs (Figure 2) is helpful.  
- Analysis on removing Freebase and interlanguage links (Table 3) is insightful.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practical paper with minor revisions recommended to address error analysis, bias, cost, and baseline comparisons.
