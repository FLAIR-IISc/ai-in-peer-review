This paper presents a cross-lingual NER model leveraging cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea is to ground n-grams in any language to the English Wikipedia, then utilize the resulting Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as features for NER. The authors demonstrate improvements over direct transfer baselines on CoNLL datasets and achieve competitive results on low-resource languages.

The primary contribution of this work is the *concept* of using comprehensive cross-lingual wikification – grounding *every* n-gram – as a feature engineering step for cross-lingual NER. While wikification and entity linking have been explored, the authors' approach shifts the paradigm by leveraging it not as a post-processing step, but as a core component for feature extraction *prior* to NER. This is a surprisingly simple yet potentially powerful idea. A secondary contribution lies in the extensive experimental evaluation across a diverse set of languages, including low-resource scenarios. The ablation studies regarding Wikipedia size and multi-lingual training are also valuable.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Approach:** The idea of employing exhaustive cross-lingual wikification for feature generation is genuinely novel. It reframes a traditionally downstream task into a foundational component of the NER pipeline. The simplicity of the concept is appealing and suggests a potential for broader application.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The consistently reported performance gains across multiple languages, including the challenging low-resource scenarios, provide solid evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach. Outperforming baselines that require parallel corpora (Zhang et al., 2016) is particularly noteworthy.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper includes a thorough set of experiments, including ablation studies examining the impact of Wikipedia size and multi-lingual training. This demonstrates a good understanding of the factors influencing performance and strengthens the credibility of the findings.
4. **Practicality & Accessibility:** The method relies solely on multilingual Wikipedia, a readily available resource. This contrasts with approaches requiring parallel data or expensive linguistic resources, making the approach highly practical and accessible to researchers.
5. **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis in Section 4.4, demonstrating the correlation between Wikipedia size and feature quality, provides a valuable insight into the limitations of the approach and points to directions for future work.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Analysis of Wikification Errors:** While the paper acknowledges imperfect disambiguation, it lacks a detailed analysis of *what kinds* of errors the wikifier makes and how these errors impact NER performance. Investigating this would provide a more nuanced understanding of the method’s limitations.
2. **Reliance on English Wikipedia:** The grounding to the English Wikipedia introduces a potential bias. Entities that are less well-represented or conceptualized in the English Wikipedia may be disadvantaged. The paper does not sufficiently address this potential bias.
3. **Feature Engineering Details:** While the paper outlines the feature set, the details are somewhat sparse. A more detailed description of the feature representation and the rationale behind specific feature choices would be beneficial.  For example, why limit n-grams to length 4?
4. **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art:**  The comparison to existing methods is somewhat limited. While comparisons to Täckström et al. (2012) and Zhang et al. (2016) are provided, a more comprehensive comparison to more recent and sophisticated cross-lingual NER models would be valuable.
5. **Presentation:** The paper suffers from occasional awkward phrasing and could benefit from more careful editing for clarity. This doesn't impede understanding but detracts from the overall polish.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the cross-lingual wikifier and their impact on NER performance?
2. How does the reliance on the English Wikipedia potentially bias the results, and what steps could be taken to mitigate this bias?
3. What is the rationale for limiting n-grams to a length of 4? Have you experimented with other values?
4. Could you elaborate on the feature representation and the rationale behind the specific feature choices? Specifically, how are the Freebase types and Wikipedia categories encoded as features?



**Additional Comments:**

*   It would be interesting to see a visualization of the wikification process for a few example sentences.
*   The figures are a bit small and difficult to read.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some weaknesses, I believe this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of cross-lingual NER. The core idea is innovative, the experimental results are compelling, and the analysis provides insightful observations. The paper prioritizes a novel concept over methodological perfection, which I find commendable. I recommend **acceptance**, with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above. The potential of this approach warrants further investigation, and this paper provides a strong foundation for future work.
