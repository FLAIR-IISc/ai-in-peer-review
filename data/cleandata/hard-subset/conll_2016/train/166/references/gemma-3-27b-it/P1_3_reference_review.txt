## Peer Review: Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) model that leverages cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea is to ground n-grams from any language to the English Wikipedia and utilize the associated Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as features for NER. The authors demonstrate significant performance improvements on CoNLL datasets (Spanish, Dutch, German) and outperform state-of-the-art methods on low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) in a direct transfer setting (training on English, testing on others).  The paper also shows improvements on monolingual models by adding these features and the benefits of multi-source training.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Application of Cross-Lingual Wikification to NER:** The paper demonstrates a practical and effective method for integrating cross-lingual wikification *directly* into the feature engineering process for NER, going beyond the traditional pipeline of NER-then-wikification. This is a novel utilization of a previously downstream task for core model input.
2. **Strong Performance on Low-Resource Languages:** The results on the low-resource languages are compelling, consistently outperforming existing approaches that require parallel data or native speaker interaction. This demonstrates the practical value of the proposed method where labeled data is scarce.
3. **Comprehensive Analysis & Experimental Validation:** The authors conduct thorough experiments, including ablation studies (monolingual improvements, influence of Wikipedia size) and exploration of multi-source training, providing strong empirical support for their claims.



**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Approach:** The core idea of utilizing wikification *as* a feature engineering step, rather than as a post-processing step, is a significant contribution. It reframes the relationship between wikification and NER and opens up new avenues for research. 
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The reported results are consistently positive, showing substantial improvements across a diverse range of languages and experimental settings. The performance gains on low-resource languages, in particular, are noteworthy.  The comparison with strong baselines (Täckström et al., Zhang et al.) provides confidence in the method’s effectiveness.
3. **Detailed Analysis:** The ablation studies (examining the impact of Freebase links, Wikipedia size) are valuable. They provide insights into the mechanisms driving the performance gains and help understand the limitations of the approach. The analysis of different training language combinations is also insightful.
4. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The methodology, experimental setup, and results are presented in a logical and understandable manner. Figure 1 effectively illustrates the core concept.
5. **Practicality & Scalability:** The approach relies solely on multilingual Wikipedia, a readily available resource, making it highly practical and scalable to a wide variety of languages.




**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Exploration of Wikification System:** The paper relies on a specific cross-lingual wikifier (Tsai and Roth, 2016) without a comparative analysis of alternative wikification systems. The performance of the NER model is therefore tied to the quality of this specific wikifier. It would be beneficial to demonstrate robustness to variations in wikification quality or to explore the impact of different wikifiers.
2. **Dependence on English Wikipedia:** The grounding to the *English* Wikipedia introduces a potential bias.  Concepts and categories might be represented differently or be missing in the English Wikipedia, which could negatively impact performance in certain languages. The authors acknowledge this with the Wikipedia size analysis, but a more detailed investigation of potential biases would be beneficial.
3. **Feature Engineering Complexity:** While the idea is elegant, the feature set (especially considering combinations of n-grams and types) is potentially complex.  A more detailed discussion of feature selection or dimensionality reduction techniques would be valuable, especially considering the risk of overfitting.  The performance gain might be partially attributable to the sheer number of features rather than the inherent effectiveness of the approach.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:**  The paper would benefit from a qualitative error analysis.  Identifying common errors made by the model could provide valuable insights into its strengths and weaknesses, and guide future improvements.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Wikification System Sensitivity:**  How sensitive is the performance of your model to variations in the quality of the cross-lingual wikifier? Have you experimented with different wikification systems or evaluated the impact of varying the disambiguation accuracy?
2.  **English Wikipedia Bias:**  Could you elaborate on potential biases introduced by grounding all n-grams to the English Wikipedia? Are there specific languages or entity types where this bias is particularly noticeable?
3.  **Feature Selection:** Did you employ any feature selection techniques to reduce the dimensionality of the feature set? If so, could you describe the methods used and their impact on performance?
4.  **Error Analysis:** Can you provide some examples of common errors made by your model, particularly in the low-resource languages? What types of entities or linguistic structures are most challenging for the model?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The discussion of related work is comprehensive.
*   The notation and experimental setup are clearly described.
*   Consider adding a table summarizing the key features used in the model for easier reference.
*   The code could be made publicly available to facilitate reproducibility.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.  The paper presents a novel and effective approach to cross-lingual NER, demonstrates strong empirical results, and offers a thorough analysis of the proposed method. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly regarding the sensitivity to the wikification system and potential English Wikipedia bias, would further strengthen the contribution of this work.
