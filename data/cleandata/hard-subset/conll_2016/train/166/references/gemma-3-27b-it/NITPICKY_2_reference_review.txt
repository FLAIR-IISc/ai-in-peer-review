## Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model leveraging cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea is to ground n-grams from any language to the English Wikipedia and use the resulting Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as features for NER. The authors demonstrate improved performance over baseline methods on CoNLL datasets and several low-resource languages. 

The primary contribution of this work lies in systematically exploring the utility of readily available cross-lingual wikification as a source of language-independent features for NER, and demonstrating its effectiveness, particularly in low-resource scenarios. A secondary, though valuable, contribution is the thorough experimental evaluation across a diverse set of languages, including several genuinely low-resource ones, and the insightful analysis of the impact of Wikipedia size on performance.

**Strengths:**

1. **Practicality and Accessibility:** The method is refreshingly practical. It relies solely on Wikipedia, removing the need for parallel corpora or complex multilingual resources. This makes the approach easily replicable and applicable to a wide range of languages, a significant advantage for resource-scarce languages.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The results presented are compelling. The model consistently outperforms baselines, and the gains are particularly noticeable in the low-resource languages, where the advantage of language-independent features is most pronounced.  The comparative analysis with Zhang et al. (2016) is well-executed and highlights the benefit of the proposed approach over methods relying on native speaker interaction.
3. **Thorough Analysis of Wikification Quality:**  The ablation studies (Table 3) demonstrating the impact of FreeBase links and interlanguage links are insightful and provide a nuanced understanding of what aspects of the wikification process are most critical for performance. This analysis strengthens the justification for the chosen approach.
4. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The experiments cover a good range of languages, including those with non-Latin scripts, increasing confidence in the generalizability of the proposed method.  The exploration of multi-lingual training adds further value.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Idea:** While the execution is strong, the core idea – leveraging Wikipedia for cross-lingual transfer – is not entirely novel. Previous works (Kazama & Torisawa, 2007, highlighted by the authors) have explored similar concepts. The paper needs to more explicitly delineate its novel contributions *beyond* simply applying wikification to NER. The novelty appears to be in the systematic application of *every n-gram* wikification combined with feature engineering and broad multilingual evaluation. However, this nuance should be clearly articulated.
2. **Feature Engineering Details are Vague:** The description of features, particularly the wikifier features, lacks sufficient detail. The authors mention “types of wi, wi+1, and wi−1, and the types of the n-grams which contain wi,” but it’s unclear how these types are represented and integrated into the model.  A more explicit and formalized description of the feature set is necessary for reproducibility and understanding. Are they one-hot encoded? Embedded?
3. **Dependence on English Wikipedia:** The strong reliance on English Wikipedia as the central grounding point is a limitation.  Bias inherent in the English Wikipedia’s coverage and structure could negatively impact performance in languages with significantly different cultural or historical contexts.  The paper doesn't adequately address this potential bias.  Exploring grounding to other major language Wikipedias might yield improvements.
4. **Formatting inconsistencies & Minor Errors:** The paper suffers from a surprising number of minor formatting issues and typos. For example, the label in Table 2 "APPROACH" is unclear. The citation style is inconsistent. While individually minor, these errors collectively detract from the paper’s professionalism and credibility.
5. **Insufficient Justification for Parameter Choices:** The choice of n=4 for n-gram wikification is not well-justified. An ablation study varying ‘n’ would be beneficial to determine the optimal value. Similarly, the choices related to the averaging perceptron classifier (Ratinov & Roth, 2009) are taken as is without exploration in a cross-lingual setting.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed description of the feature representation for the wikifier features? Specifically, how are Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types encoded and integrated into the model?
2.  How sensitive is the performance to the quality of the cross-lingual wikifier itself? Did you evaluate different wikifiers or consider potential disambiguation errors?
3.  Have you considered exploring grounding to Wikipedias in languages other than English?  Would this mitigate potential biases or improve performance for specific languages?
4.  What is the computational cost of grounding every n-gram to Wikipedia, and how does it scale with the size of the document and the target language?
5. What is the impact of removing MISC tags from the CoNLL datasets when evaluating performance on low-resource languages? Does this affect the comparability of results?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of related work, specifically highlighting the differences and advantages of the proposed approach compared to existing methods.
*   Please address the formatting inconsistencies and typos throughout the manuscript. A careful proofread is highly recommended.
*   Consider adding a table summarizing the key hyperparameters and their values for better reproducibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite its strengths, the paper is currently not quite at the level of acceptance due to the limited novelty of the core idea, insufficient detail in the methodology, and the presence of numerous minor flaws. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly providing a more detailed explanation of the features and acknowledging the limitations of relying solely on English Wikipedia, would significantly strengthen the manuscript. I lean towards **rejection** at this time, but would be willing to reconsider with a substantial revision.
