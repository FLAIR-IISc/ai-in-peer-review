## Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

This paper presents a cross-lingual NER model leveraging a cross-lingual wikifier to generate language-independent features. The core idea – utilizing wikification as a feature engineering step for cross-lingual transfer – is conceptually sound and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness, particularly for low-resource languages. The paper is well-structured and presents a comprehensive set of experiments.

**Summary and Contributions:**

The paper addresses the challenge of NER in low-resource languages by transferring knowledge from high-resource languages using a direct transfer approach. The key contribution lies in the demonstration that grounding n-grams to the English Wikipedia, even with imperfect disambiguation, provides valuable features for cross-lingual NER. This is achieved through a cross-lingual wikifier, and the resulting Wikipedia categories and Freebase types are used as language-independent features. A secondary, but significant contribution is the comprehensive empirical evaluation across a range of languages, including both CoNLL languages and several truly low-resource languages, showcasing the model's robustness and outperformance of existing methods. The ablation studies investigating the quality of wikifier features based on Wikipedia size are also valuable. 

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty and Insightful Approach:** The paper presents a novel application of wikification. Traditionally viewed as a downstream task, the authors effectively reposition wikification as a powerful feature extraction method for NER, enabling cross-lingual transfer. This shift in perspective is a significant contribution.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The model consistently outperforms comparable direct transfer methods on both CoNLL languages and low-resource languages. The reported improvements are substantial, particularly for languages with limited resources, demonstrating the practical value of the proposed approach.  The comparisons to Zhang et al. (2016) are particularly compelling, given the resource requirements of the baseline.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The thorough experimental evaluation is a major strength. The authors test their model on a diverse set of languages, perform ablation studies to analyze the contribution of different feature sets, and investigate the impact of Wikipedia size. This rigorous evaluation builds confidence in the robustness and generalizability of the proposed method.
4. **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The methodology is described in detail, and the experimental results are presented in a clear and concise manner. Figure 1 effectively illustrates the concept of cross-lingual wikification.
5. **Useful Analysis of Wikifier Quality:** The investigation of the impact of Wikipedia size and the ablations concerning Freebase and interlanguage links (Table 3) offer valuable insights into the limitations and dependencies of the method. This self-critical analysis strengthens the paper.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Reliance on English Wikipedia:** The model's complete dependence on the English Wikipedia is a potential limitation. While the English Wikipedia is the largest and most comprehensive, it introduces a bias and may not adequately represent concepts and entities from all cultures and languages. Exploring methods to incorporate information from other language Wikipedias directly could be beneficial.
2. **Wikifier Imperfections:** The paper acknowledges that wikification is imperfect, and the performance is affected by disambiguation errors. While the results demonstrate robustness to these errors, further investigation into mitigating their impact could be valuable.  Specifically, how sensitive are the results to varying degrees of wikification accuracy?
3. **Feature Engineering Choices:** The choice of features (Ratinov & Roth 2009 base features) appears somewhat arbitrary and may be sub-optimal for the cross-lingual setting.  While the authors show that adding the wikifier features helps, a more thorough exploration of feature engineering techniques tailored to cross-lingual transfer might yield further improvements. The lack of POS tags is understandable given the difficulties in obtaining them for all languages, but the discussion of why they didn’t improve performance feels somewhat cursory.
4. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** While the quantitative results are strong, a more qualitative analysis of error cases would provide valuable insights. What types of entities are most frequently misidentified? What are the common sources of errors? This would help to identify areas for future improvement.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How does the performance of the model change when using different wikification systems (e.g., those with different disambiguation algorithms)?
2.  Have you considered incorporating information from non-English Wikipedia articles directly, perhaps by creating language-specific gazetteers or embeddings?
3.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the error cases, including examples of misidentified entities and the reasons for the errors?
4.  Is there a noticeable performance difference when using different n-gram lengths (n ≤ 4)? Were other values of *n* explored?
5. You mention that training on all but the test languages improves results. Does the order of training languages have a significant impact?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The formatting of Table 2 could be improved for readability.
*   The reference to “Schwierigkeiten beim nachvollziehenden Verstehen Albrecht Lehmann läßt Flüchtlinge und Vertriebene in Westdeutschland” in Figure 1 seems out of place and doesn't add significant value.
* Consider adding a discussion of the computational cost associated with the wikification process, particularly for large-scale datasets.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of cross-lingual NER. The proposed approach is novel, the experimental results are compelling, and the paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. **I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the questions and suggestions outlined above.**
