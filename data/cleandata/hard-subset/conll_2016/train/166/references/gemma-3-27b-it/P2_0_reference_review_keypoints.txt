- Proposes cross-lingual NER that grounds n-grams to English Wikipedia and uses Wikipedia categories and Freebase types as language-independent features.  
- Novel framing of wikification as feature engineering for direct transfer NER rather than a downstream EL step.  
- Empirical gains: outperforms baseline direct-transfer methods on CoNLL (ES, NL, DE) and achieves state-of-the-art on several low-resource languages.  
- Broad evaluation including REFLEX and LORELEI datasets strengthens claims of generalizability.  
- Analysis showing Wikipedia size and interlanguage-link availability affect wikification feature effectiveness.  
- Thorough ablation studies (e.g., removing Freebase queries) that clarify feature contributions.  
- Practical approach requiring only multilingual Wikipedia, avoiding parallel corpora or language-specific resources.  
- Detailed feature engineering description supports reproducibility.  
- Missing detailed error analysis; unclear which entity types or ambiguous cases drive failures and whether imperfect wikification causes systematic errors.  
- Reliance on English Wikipedia may introduce cultural/coverage bias that is acknowledged but not deeply explored.  
- Comparisons lack more recent strong baselines using multilingual embeddings (e.g., XLM-RoBERTa); trade-offs vs. large pretrained models are not fully discussed.  
- Potential feature overlap/redundancy unaddressed; feature selection or dimensionality reduction could improve performance and interpretability.  
- Questions posed: request for error analysis, examination of English-Wikipedia biases, comparisons to recent SOTA (even if requiring parallel data), and experiments on feature selection/dimensionality reduction.  
- Presentation strengths: clear writing, well-designed figures, and good organization.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practically relevant work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
