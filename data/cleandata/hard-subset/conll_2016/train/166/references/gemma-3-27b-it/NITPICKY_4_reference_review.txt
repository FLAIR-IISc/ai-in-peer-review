This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model leveraging a cross-lingual wikifier to generate language-independent features. The core idea is to ground n-grams from any language to English Wikipedia, utilizing the resulting Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as features for NER. The authors demonstrate improvements over direct transfer baselines on CoNLL datasets and achieve competitive results on several low-resource languages.

The primary contribution is the demonstration of the effectiveness of a wikifier-feature approach for cross-lingual NER, particularly in low-resource scenarios. A secondary contribution lies in the comprehensive experimental evaluation across a diverse set of languages, including several truly low-resource settings. The inclusion of detailed ablation studies examining the impact of Wikipedia size and training language combinations provides valuable insights.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper consistently demonstrates performance gains across a variety of languages and experimental setups. The results on the low-resource languages are particularly compelling, exceeding existing state-of-the-art methods that rely on parallel data or native speaker interaction. This practical impact is a significant strength.
2. **Thorough Experimental Evaluation:** The authors go beyond simply presenting overall numbers and perform detailed ablation studies.  The investigation into the impact of Wikipedia size (Table 3) is insightful and reveals a crucial dependency of the method’s effectiveness. The examination of different training language combinations (Figure 2) provides a nuanced understanding of transfer learning dynamics.
3. **Clear and Logical Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the methodology and experimental design. The inclusion of Figure 1, illustrating the wikification process, enhances understanding. The organization is logical, making it easy to follow the flow of ideas.
4. **Relevance to the Community:** Cross-lingual NER, especially for low-resource languages, is an active and important research area.  A method requiring only multilingual Wikipedia is highly valuable and potentially broadly applicable, offering a pragmatic solution to data scarcity.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incremental Novelty:** While the results are good, the core idea of using Wikipedia features for NER isn't entirely novel (as acknowledged in the related work, e.g., Kazama and Torisawa, 2007). The primary novelty lies in the specific application of *cross-lingual* wikification *to generate n-gram features* and the extensive empirical validation. However, this contribution, while significant, feels incremental rather than groundbreaking.
2. **Over-Reliance on English Wikipedia:** The entire approach hinges on the quality and coverage of the English Wikipedia.  The ablation study highlighting this dependency (Table 3) is commendable, but it also reveals a fundamental limitation. Performance degrades drastically when the connection to English Wikipedia is weakened, suggesting a potential bias towards concepts well-represented in the English Wikipedia. The impact of this bias on truly underrepresented languages isn't fully addressed.
3. **Feature Engineering Details:** The feature set (Table 1) is described at a high level. More details about the specific implementation of the gazetteer features and how the FreeBase types/Wikipedia categories are integrated into the model would be beneficial. The number of features derived from the wikifier is not provided, making it difficult to assess their contribution relative to other features.
4. **Limited Error Analysis:** While the ablation studies provide some insight, a more in-depth error analysis would be valuable. Understanding *why* the model fails on certain types of entities or in specific languages could inform future improvements.  Specifically, analyzing errors stemming from imperfect wikification would strengthen the paper.
5. **Formatting and Minor Issues:** There are minor formatting inconsistencies. For example, some table captions are incomplete. Additionally, the reference to "Figure 2" in section 4.4 is misleading as it only contains data and no figure.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details on the specific features derived from the wikifier? Specifically, how many features are generated per n-gram, and what is the distribution of those features across different entity types?
2. How does the performance of the model vary for different entity types (e.g., PERSON, ORG, LOC)? Are there specific entity types for which the wikifier features are particularly effective or ineffective?
3.  Given the reliance on English Wikipedia, have you explored strategies to mitigate potential bias toward concepts heavily represented in English? For example, could you incorporate features from other Wikipedia language editions?
4.  What is the computational cost associated with the cross-lingual wikification process, especially for longer texts?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a clearer discussion of the limitations of the approach, particularly the dependence on English Wikipedia and the potential for bias.
*   Please revise the reference to "Figure 2" in section 4.4 to accurately reflect its content.
*   Check for and correct any remaining formatting inconsistencies.
