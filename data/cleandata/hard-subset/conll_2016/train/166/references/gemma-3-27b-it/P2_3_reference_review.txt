## Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

**Summary:** This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model leveraging a cross-lingual wikifier to generate language-independent features. The core idea is to ground n-grams from any language to English Wikipedia, utilizing the associated Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as features for NER. The authors demonstrate improvements over baseline direct transfer methods on CoNLL datasets and several low-resource languages. They also show the benefit of incorporating these features into monolingual models and multi-source training.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Demonstration of Wikification as a Feature Source for Cross-Lingual NER:** The paper convincingly shows that grounding words in Wikipedia, even with imperfect disambiguation, provides valuable signals for NER in a cross-lingual setting. This is the most significant contribution, as it offers a relatively simple and scalable way to leverage readily available multilingual resources.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The model achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results across a diverse set of languages, including low-resource scenarios. The consistent improvements across different languages validate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation & Analysis:** The paper includes a thorough evaluation, exploring the impact of Wikipedia size, feature combinations, and multi-source training. This analysis provides valuable insights into the strengths and limitations of the proposed method.



**Strengths:**

1. **Novel and Practical Approach:** The idea of utilizing cross-lingual wikification *as a feature source* rather than a pre-processing step is a novel and insightful contribution. It cleverly inverts the traditional pipeline and leverages the inherent knowledge embedded in Wikipedia. The reliance on readily available Wikipedia data makes the approach highly practical and scalable to numerous languages.
2. **Strong Performance, Especially in Low-Resource Settings:** The consistent performance gains, especially on the low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil), are compelling.  The results demonstrate a clear benefit of the proposed method when limited annotated data is available.  Outperforming approaches requiring parallel corpora or native speaker interaction is a significant achievement.
3. **Detailed Analysis of Feature Importance:** The ablation studies (e.g., removing Freebase links, varying interlanguage link density) offer valuable insight into the components driving the performance.  These analyses clearly demonstrate the importance of Wikipedia size and feature quality.
4. **Well-Written and Thoroughly Evaluated:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the methodology. The experimental setup is comprehensive, and the results are presented in a clear and organized manner. The inclusion of comparisons to existing baselines is crucial for establishing the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.
5. **Multi-Source Training Benefits:** Demonstrating that combining training data from multiple source languages further improves performance adds practical value to the work.



**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in the Wikification Component Itself:** While the *application* of cross-lingual wikification to NER feature engineering is novel, the wikification component itself relies on existing systems (Tsai and Roth, 2016). The paper doesn’t present any novel contributions to wikification algorithms. The strength lies in the creative *use* of an existing technology.
2. **Dependence on English Wikipedia:**  The grounding of all languages to English Wikipedia introduces a potential bias. While this simplifies feature representation, it implicitly assumes English Wikipedia's coverage and organization are representative of all languages. This dependency could limit performance on languages with significant cultural or conceptual differences from English.  The analysis of this bias is limited.
3. **Feature Engineering Complexity:**  The feature set, while comprehensive, is complex. While the paper lists them, the combination of base features, gazetteer features, and wikifier features introduces a significant engineering overhead.  Further investigation into feature selection or dimensionality reduction could be valuable.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** While the quantitative results are strong, the paper lacks a detailed qualitative error analysis.  Understanding *where* the model fails would provide valuable insights for future improvements. Are there specific entity types or linguistic phenomena that consistently cause errors?




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  You mention the potential for bias introduced by grounding all languages to English Wikipedia. Could you elaborate on this issue and discuss potential strategies to mitigate this bias (e.g., using multiple anchor languages)?
2.  The feature set appears quite complex.  Have you explored any feature selection or dimensionality reduction techniques to simplify the model without sacrificing performance?
3.  Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, highlighting common error types and potential areas for improvement?  Are there specific entity types or linguistic phenomena that consistently challenge the model?
4.  In the experiments section, you mention the exclusion of MISC tags. Can you explain how many MISC tags were excluded and whether their exclusion significantly affected the reported results?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The reference to "Zhang et al. (2016)" would be helpful if a full citation was provided in the bibliography.
*   A more visual representation of the example in Figure 1, highlighting the linked terms and their associated Freebase types, could improve clarity.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to cross-lingual NER. The consistent empirical results, coupled with a thorough analysis, demonstrate the value of leveraging cross-lingual wikification for feature engineering. While the novelty of the wikification component itself is limited, the innovative application and strong performance justify acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
