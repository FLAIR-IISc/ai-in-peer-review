- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to map n-grams to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types/categories as language-independent features, improving results on CoNLL and several low-resource languages and helping monolingual/multi-source training.
- Contribution 1: Shows that cross-lingual wikification provides effective, practical language-independent features for NER without parallel corpora.
- Contribution 2: Evaluates thoroughly on both standard CoNLL languages and genuinely low-resource languages (including non-Latin scripts).
- Contribution 3: Provides informative ablation studies on Freebase, interlanguage links, and training-language combinations.
- Strength 1: Strong empirical improvements, especially for difficult low-resource languages (e.g., Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).
- Strength 2: Practical and scalable method using readily available Wikipedia resources and simple feature engineering.
- Strength 3: Thorough analysis (ablation studies and training-language experiments) that sheds light on why the approach works.
- Strength 4: Clear presentation and helpful figures (e.g., wikification illustration).
- Strength 5: Addresses an important real-world problem—NER for languages with limited annotated data.
- Weakness 1: Limited novelty in the wikification component itself; relies on existing systems without advancing wikification methods.
- Weakness 2: Heavy reliance on engineered features with limited discussion of adaptability or automated feature learning (e.g., embeddings).
- Weakness 3: Dependence on English Wikipedia raises concerns about biases and their cross-lingual impact; discussion is limited.
- Weakness 4: Lacks comparison to more recent strong baselines using multilingual embeddings or pretrained models (BERT, XLM-R).
- Question 1: What percentage of wikification attempts link to valid English Wikipedia pages per language, and how does that correlate with NER performance?
- Question 2: How might English Wikipedia biases manifest in NER results (entity-type over-representation, geographic linking errors)?
- Question 3: Have the authors tried combining wikifier features with multilingual embeddings or pretrained language models, and what were the outcomes?
- Question 4: What error modes does the wikifier exhibit and how do those errors affect NER performance?
- Additional comment 1: Improve formatting consistency for references (e.g., “et al.” usage).
- Additional comment 2: Make the description of the training/test language matrix (Figure 2) more concise.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with practical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
