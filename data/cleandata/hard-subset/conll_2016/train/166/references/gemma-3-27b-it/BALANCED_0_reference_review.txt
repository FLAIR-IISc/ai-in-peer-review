This paper presents a cross-lingual NER model leveraging cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea of grounding n-grams to the English Wikipedia and utilizing the resulting FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories as features is promising and well-motivated, addressing the challenge of NER in low-resource languages. The experimental results, particularly the performance gains on both CoNLL datasets and low-resource languages, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Effective Utilization of Cross-Lingual Wikification for NER:** The primary contribution lies in successfully demonstrating that cross-lingual wikification can be a powerful technique for generating language-independent features for NER.  The method moves beyond wikification as a downstream task and positions it as a crucial component for feature engineering. This is a novel and impactful application of wikification.
2. **Strong Empirical Results on Diverse Languages:** The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the model on a diverse set of languages, including both resource-rich (Spanish, Dutch, German) and low-resource (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) languages. The consistent performance improvements over baselines, including state-of-the-art approaches requiring parallel data, is a significant achievement.
3. **Detailed Analysis of Feature Importance:** The ablation studies and analysis regarding Wikipedia size and the impact of FreeBase links provide valuable insights into the factors influencing the performance of the model. This demonstrates a solid understanding of the underlying mechanisms.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty and Significance:** The idea of leveraging cross-lingual wikification as a core component of a NER model is novel and addresses a critical challenge in NLP – low-resource language processing. The success of this approach is significant, especially considering the simplicity of the underlying idea.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The thorough experimental evaluation on a wide range of languages provides strong evidence of the model's effectiveness and generalizability.  The comparison against strong baselines, including those requiring more resources, strengthens the claim of the method’s superiority.
3. **Clear and Well-Organized Presentation:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The methodology is clearly explained, and the experimental setup is well-defined. The inclusion of Figure 1 as a motivating example is particularly helpful.
4. **Ablation Studies and Analysis:**  The ablation studies in Section 4.3, demonstrating the influence of Wikipedia size and FreeBase links, are a strong point. They offer a deep understanding of the system and how its components contribute to overall performance.
5. **Practicality and Scalability:** The method’s reliance solely on multilingual Wikipedia makes it highly practical and scalable to numerous languages without requiring parallel corpora or extensive language-specific resources.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Feature Set:** While the wikifier features are effective, the base features used (Ratinov and Roth, 2009) are relatively simple. Exploring more sophisticated lexical and contextual features, potentially incorporating recent advancements in pre-trained language models (even in a cross-lingual way), could further improve performance.
2. **Wikifier Quality Dependence:**  The performance is heavily reliant on the quality of the cross-lingual wikifier. While the paper addresses this somewhat with analysis on Wikipedia size, the impact of noisy or ambiguous wikification results isn’t fully explored. Error analysis of mis-wikified terms and their impact on NER accuracy would be valuable.
3. **Lack of Comparison to Recent Cross-Lingual Embeddings:** The paper compares against some relevant baselines, but a comparison to more recent approaches leveraging cross-lingual word/subword embeddings (e.g., mBERT, XLM-RoBERTa) would provide a stronger benchmark and better position the work within the current landscape of cross-lingual NLP.  Directly incorporating such embeddings as features could also be explored.
4. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:** While the Wikipedia size analysis is helpful, a more general error analysis of NER errors would provide further insights. Specifically, what types of entities are most frequently misidentified, and in what languages? This could inform future improvements to the model or the wikification process.
5. **Handling of Multi-Word Entities:** The paper mentions grounding n-grams up to length 4, but doesn't discuss the challenges of identifying and correctly grounding longer, more complex multi-word entities. A discussion of this limitation and potential solutions would be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Have you considered incorporating more advanced lexical or contextual features, such as character-level embeddings or features derived from pre-trained language models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-RoBERTa)? If not, could you comment on why?
2. Could you provide a more detailed error analysis of the NER results, identifying common types of errors and languages where the model struggles the most?
3. How sensitive is the model's performance to the quality of the cross-lingual wikifier? Have you investigated the impact of mis-wikified terms on NER accuracy?
4. How does the performance of the model compare to more recent cross-lingual embedding-based approaches (e.g., using mBERT or XLM-RoBERTa)?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The formatting of Table 2 is a little dense and could be improved for readability.
*   The reference to “Zhang et al. (2016)” appears frequently; a brief description of their approach in the main text would be helpful.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the computational cost of the cross-lingual wikification step.



**Overall:**

This is a strong paper with a clear and compelling contribution. The proposed method is novel, well-motivated, and supported by comprehensive experimental results. While there are some areas for improvement, the strengths of the work significantly outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
