- Proposes cross-lingual NER that grounds every n-gram (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia via a wikifier and uses Wikipedia categories + Freebase types as language-independent features in a perceptron NER model.
- No parallel data or target-language annotation required; evaluated in monolingual and direct-transfer settings.
- Evaluated on CoNLL (EN, ES, DE, NL) and five low-resource languages (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA), showing consistent gains from wikifier features.
- Strong improvements over direct-transfer baselines (including Täckström et al. 2012) on CoNLL and improvements over Zhang et al. (2016) on low-resource languages.
- Analyses quantify the impact of Wikipedia size and interlanguage coverage on performance; multi-source training gives small extra gains.
- Main contribution: demonstrates cross-lingual wikification yields effective, language-independent features for direct-transfer NER with large gains and no parallel data.
- Contribution: analysis connecting performance to Wikipedia/Freebase coverage with ablations explaining low-resource failures.
- Contribution: practical adaptations to a wikifier to densely ground all n-grams and incorporate linker confidence into NER.
- Strength: clear, broadly applicable insight—delexicalized Wikipedia/Freebase typing is a practical transfer strategy complementary to other methods.
- Strength: substantial empirical gains on CoNLL languages (often >10 F1 on NL/DE/TR) using fewer resources than some baselines.
- Strength: thoughtful ablations and error analyses that isolate wikifier contributions and sensitivity to KB/interlanguage coverage.
- Strength: evaluation across nine languages and multiple scripts, showing robustness when lexical features fail.
- Strength: practical, extensible approach compatible with multi-source training and orthogonal to projection/embedding methods.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Freebase and interlanguage links raises durability and coverage concerns; suggests adopting Wikidata or other KBs.
- Weakness: limited comparison to contemporary cross-lingual embedding or multilingual contextual model baselines.
- Weakness: dataset label-set adaptations (removing/relabeling tags) may bias results and complicate comparability; protocol should be standardized.
- Weakness: insufficient methodological detail for reproducibility (feature inventory, training hyperparameters, linker integration, code release).
- Weakness: no runtime/efficiency or scalability analysis for grounding every n-gram up to length 4.
- Questions for authors: feasibility of porting to Wikidata/other KBs and expected impact or preliminary results.
- Questions: per-language fraction of tokens/n-grams linked, coverage statistics, and correlation with F1.
- Questions: how NIL/low-confidence links are handled, thresholding strategy, and sensitivity to top-k candidates (they use top-2).
- Questions: request for more implementation details (epochs, regularization, feature templates, decoding, exact types/categories) and plan to release code.
- Questions: request results on low-resource languages under native label sets (without remapping/removing tags) to assess harmonization impact.
- Overall: moderate but meaningful novelty; strong empirical evidence and analyses; recommends addressing durability, adding embedding baselines, clarifying protocol, and improving reproducibility; reviewer leans toward accept.
