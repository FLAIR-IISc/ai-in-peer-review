- Proposes grounding all n-grams (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia and using Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent, delexicalized NER features.  
- Two modifications to an existing wikifier make dense, all-ngrams grounding practical without parallel data.  
- Evaluated monolingually and in direct transfer across nine languages (including five low-resource/non-Latin), showing consistent gains.  
- Main contribution: simple, general way to convert cross-lingual wikification into effective NER features.  
- Strong empirical evaluation with ablations tying performance to Wikipedia size and cross-lingual linkage.  
- Demonstrates monolingual gains over base+gazetteers for all nine languages and notable direct-transfer improvements.  
- Shows multi-source training can further improve cross-lingual performance; outperforms several prior baselines using fewer resources.  
- Method requires only multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel text or native annotations), making it broadly applicable.  
- Practical wikifier changes (candidate restriction, removal of mention-embedding features) reduce errors and enable all-ngrams grounding.  
- Sensible multi-source transfer experiments reveal English often best but not always, offering deployment guidance.  
- Reproducibility-minded choices: standard R&R model, concrete implementation details (n ≤ 4, top-2 candidates, ranker confidence).  
- Key weakness: heavy dependence on Wikipedia and Freebase coverage; performance degrades for languages with sparse intersections (e.g., Yoruba).  
- Concern about Freebase deprecation and lack of discussion about substituting Wikidata or other KBs.  
- Evaluation compromises: dropping TIME/TTL and remapping MISC to LOC may bias training/evaluation toward wiki-based methods and reduce comparability.  
- Limited methodological novelty beyond applying wikifier features to an older perceptron NER framework.  
- No comparison to modern neural or embedding-based cross-lingual NER baselines or combinations with orthogonal techniques.  
- Computational/scalability concerns unaddressed: no runtime, memory, throughput, or pruning strategy reporting for grounding all n-grams.  
- Limited error analysis: lacks per-entity-type F1, boundary vs type error breakdown, NIL handling analysis, and qualitative failure modes.  
- Question: report runtime/throughput, memory usage, pruning thresholds, and scaling with document length and candidate sizes.  
- Question: provide the mapping from Freebase types/Wikipedia categories to PER/LOC/ORG and sensitivity of results to that mapping.  
- Question: describe NIL handling strategy, use of ranker confidence thresholds, and their effect on precision/recall.  
- Question: can Freebase be substituted with Wikidata (or another KB) and produce similar results, especially for low-resource languages?  
- Question: were target-language gazetteers used at test time; provide a baseline without them to isolate wikifier benefit.  
- Request: provide per-entity-type F1 and an error analysis (boundary vs type) to show what wikifier features help most.  
- Minor comments: clarify tokenization and casing for non-Latin scripts, report statistical significance, and release code/resources and the language-to-type mapping.  
- Overall verdict: clear, practical idea with solid empirical validation across languages; reviewer leans toward acceptance despite caveats.
