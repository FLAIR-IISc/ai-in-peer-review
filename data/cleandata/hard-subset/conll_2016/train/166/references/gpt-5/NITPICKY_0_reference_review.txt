Summary and main contributions
- The paper proposes using cross-lingual wikification to create language-independent features (Freebase types and Wikipedia categories) for NER. A simple averaged-perceptron BIO tagger (Ratinov & Roth, 2009) is augmented with: (i) Wikipedia-derived gazetteers per language and (ii) features from a cross-lingual wikifier (Tsai & Roth, 2016) applied to every n-gram (n ≤ 4). The model is trained on one or multiple source languages and directly applied to target languages without target-side supervision.
- Extensive experiments on CoNLL (EN, DE, ES, NL) and five low-resource languages (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA) show that wikifier features help both monolingually and in direct transfer, with larger gains for languages with larger Wikipedias. Ablations examine the role of Freebase and interlanguage link coverage. Multi-source training provides modest additional gains.
- The work empirically characterizes when to include or exclude lexical/gazetteer features based on script overlap and Wikipedia size, offering practical guidance for cross-lingual NER when supervision is absent.

Strengths
- Clear, simple idea with good empirical payoff: Using cross-lingual EL signals as delexicalized features is intuitive yet effective. The “Wikifier only” setting demonstrates a genuine cross-lingual signal that survives transfer.
- Breadth of evaluation: Nine languages across different scripts and resource levels, with monolingual, single-source transfer, and multi-source transfer. The analysis relating performance to Wikipedia size/interlanguage links is valuable and actionable.
- Comparisons to prior transfer baselines: Reported improvements over Täckström et al. (2012) and Zhang et al. (2016) (where applicable) suggest the approach is competitive despite using fewer resources than some baselines.
- Ablation insights: The experiments that remove Freebase query and interlanguage links convincingly support the claim that coverage in Wikipedia/Freebase critically impacts the effectiveness of the wikifier features.
- Practical guidance: The recommendation to rely primarily on wikifier features when scripts differ and to keep all features for Latin-script targets is useful for practitioners.

Weaknesses
- Clarity and formatting issues harm credibility and reproducibility. Examples:
  - Table 2 appears internally inconsistent: the Täckström rows have 3 numbers but the table has 9 columns; column alignment and ordering are unclear. The “Wikifier only” direct transfer row seems incomplete. “AVG” is sometimes missing or unexplained. Figure 2 and Table 4 are referenced but not provided here; Figure 1 interrupts text with a garbled inline example. Mixed hyphenation (nonlexical/non-lexical), inconsistent capitalization/spelling (FreeBase/Freebase), and typos (e.g., “as a the base model”) occur.
  - The footnote “1” for n-grams is misplaced; “We set n to 4 in all our experiments.” has no clear footnote anchor.
  - Several references are cited but not fully specified in-line; the reader cannot verify exact setups.
- Reproducibility gaps:
  - No detailed specification of feature instantiation for wikifier outputs (which categories/types, how many, how pruned, how encoded), and no ablation between Wikipedia categories vs. Freebase types (only a partial ablation via removing FB query).
  - Training details are underspecified: perceptron hyperparameters, iteration counts, initialization, early stopping, decoding specifics, and whether features are normalized.
  - Tokenization, casing, and sentence segmentation across languages are not described; capitalization and affix features likely behave inconsistently in non-Latin scripts; no explicit handling is described.
  - Gazetteer construction is described only at a high level; exact filters, normalization, alias handling, and overlap with training/test are unclear.
  - No runtime/complexity analysis for “wikify every n-gram up to 4” (potentially expensive), no mention of pruning or NIL thresholding beyond “stop words or NIL.”
- Questionable data handling and evaluation choices:
  - Label remapping: For low-resource settings, TIME/TTL removed; MISC in CoNLL remapped to LOC based on Freebase type or dropped. This can bias results and harms comparability with prior work. Later, MISC is ignored when evaluating CoNLL in Figure 2. These choices need stronger justification and consistency checks; per-type F1 is not reported.
  - Lack of statistical significance testing; many reported improvements are modest (e.g., multi-source gains).
  - Baseline fairness: Täckström comparisons are hard to interpret due to table inconsistencies; it’s unclear which columns correspond to which languages. For Zhang et al. (2016), differences in label sets and resources (e.g., your use of target-language Wikipedia gazetteers) complicate fairness claims.
- Methodological fragility/limitations:
  - Heavy dependence on Freebase and interlanguage links; for YO/BN/TA the approach degrades substantially. The claim “works on all languages in Wikipedia” is tempered by this dependency.
  - Cross-lingual wikifier modifications (e.g., disabling token-based candidate generation for n > 1) may reduce recall for multiword entities; no analysis is given. Including top-2 candidates introduces noise; the “ranker features” inclusion is mentioned but not evaluated.
  - No error analysis: failure modes (boundary errors vs. type errors, script-specific issues, morphology) are not discussed.

Questions to authors
- Table 2 clarity: Please provide an unambiguous version with precise column headers and ensure the Täckström rows are correctly aligned to target languages. Report averages consistently and include standard deviations or significance tests.
- Feature specifics: Which exact Freebase types and Wikipedia categories are used? How many per token/ngram are retained (top-k? thresholds?), how are they encoded, and how is feature explosion controlled?
- Wikifier configuration: What NIL thresholding or candidate pruning is applied? What is the impact of including top-2 candidates vs. top-1? Please provide an ablation including (i) categories only, (ii) Freebase types only, (iii) ranker features off.
- Preprocessing: How are tokenization, sentence splitting, and casing handled per language (especially for non-Latin scripts)? Are affix and capitalization features disabled for such languages?
- Gazetteers: How exactly are gazetteers constructed per language (alias resolution, redirects, disambiguation pages, normalization)? How do you avoid test leakage via Wikipedia pages created after the corpora were annotated?
- Label remapping: Please justify and quantify the impact of dropping/remapping TIME/TTL/MISC. Provide per-entity-type results and a version aligned to each dataset’s native label set to enable fair comparison.
- Efficiency: What is the runtime/memory overhead of wikifying all n-grams up to length 4 on typical documents? Are there pruning heuristics?
- Resources: Will you release code, trained models, and gazetteer lists to ensure reproducibility?
