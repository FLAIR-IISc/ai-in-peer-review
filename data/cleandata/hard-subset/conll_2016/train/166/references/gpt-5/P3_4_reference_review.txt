Summary and main contributions
- The paper proposes a direct-transfer cross-lingual NER approach that leverages cross-lingual wikification to English Wikipedia to derive language-independent features (Freebase types, Wikipedia categories, and ranker confidences) for every n-gram (n ≤ 4). These features are combined with a classic NER model (Ratinov & Roth, 2009) and Wikipedia-derived gazetteers.
- It demonstrates consistent gains from wikifier features in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings across CoNLL languages and five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), with analyses showing that improvements correlate with target-language Wikipedia size/intersection with English.
- The paper provides ablations on wikification components (Freebase query, interlanguage link coverage), and explores multi-source training.

Strengths
- Clear, simple idea with solid empirical validation: Using cross-lingual wikification as delexicalized features is an intuitive way to port NER without labeled target data. The “wikifier only” rows and cumulative ablations convincingly show that these features carry cross-lingual signal that complements gazetteers and base features.
- Broad multilingual evaluation: Results span 4 CoNLL languages and 5 genuinely low-resource languages (including non-Latin scripts). The finding that wikifier gains scale with Wikipedia size is practically useful and is backed by ablations (e.g., Table 3) that degrade interlanguage links and Freebase access.
- Orthogonal to prior direct-transfer methods: The approach does not rely on bitext, alignments, or language-specific heuristics and can co-exist with projection or multilingual clustering. The comparison to Täckström et al. (2012) aligns with this positioning; the method outperforms bitext-cluster transfer on shared languages.
- Sensible design choices and error-aware modifications: Restricting n-gram candidate generation to whole-string queries and excluding global-mention embeddings for dense n-gram wikification are pragmatic to reduce false positives and overfitting. Including linker confidence as a feature is a good safeguard.
- Multi-source training analysis: While modest in gains, the exploration of training on multiple source languages and identifying non-English best sources for some targets (e.g., Spanish for Yoruba) provides insight into data augmentation in transfer.

Weaknesses
- Limited baselines and dated modeling: The core NER model is a 2009 perceptron with greedy decoding and handcrafted features. There are no comparisons to modern cross-lingual neural baselines (e.g., cross-lingual word embeddings, multilingual contextual encoders). As a result, it is hard to judge how much of the reported gains would persist against stronger, now-standard baselines.
- Use of Freebase undermines replicability and future utility: Freebase is deprecated. The approach’s strongest features depend heavily on Freebase types. Although the paper analyzes the impact of removing Freebase lookups, the method’s practical viability should be demonstrated with Wikidata or a current KB. Without this, the contribution risks being non-reproducible.
- Evaluation protocol and label mapping may bias results: For low-resource languages, the paper removes TTL/TIME and remaps some MISC to LOC based on KB types, then ignores MISC for CoNLL in cross-lingual settings. These changes are substantial and can advantage KB-based features. There is no per-entity-type breakdown to show boundary vs. typing gains. This complicates comparability and may inflate type accuracy due to KB type leakage.
- Target-language resources at test time blur the “direct transfer” setting: The approach uses target-language Wikipedia (gazetteers and wikifier candidates via interlanguage links). While no labeled data is used, these are target-specific resources. A clearer positioning versus “unsupervised target” vs “resource-lean but Wikipedia-available” is needed, and a baseline that excludes target-language Wikipedia but keeps English-only KB would clarify the contribution of target resources.
- Computational and practical concerns not addressed: Wikifying all n-grams up to length 4 is potentially expensive and may introduce many spurious candidates. There is no analysis of runtime, throughput, or memory, nor NIL handling rates, ambiguity statistics, or an error analysis quantifying overlinking/underlinking impacts on boundary detection vs. typing.

Questions to authors
- Can you replace Freebase with Wikidata and report end-to-end results and ablations? Given Freebase deprecation, this seems essential for reproducibility.
- What is the runtime and memory footprint of wikifying all n-grams (n ≤ 4) on typical documents? How many candidate links per token on average, and what is the NIL rate per language?
- How sensitive are results to the number of top candidates (you use top-2) and to the inclusion of Wikipedia categories vs. KB types separately? Please provide ablations per feature family.
- Could you report per-entity-type precision/recall and boundary vs. type errors? This would help understand whether gains are primarily from typing or also boundary detection.
- For the multi-source training, how do you balance training data across languages and prevent source-dominance? Any evidence of negative transfer on particular targets?
- How would the system perform when restricted to English KB only (no target-language Wikipedia/gazetteers), i.e., using only interlanguage links available from English? This would help separate the effects of target-side resources.

Additional comments and suggestions
- Stronger baselines are needed: include a cross-lingual embedding-based CRF and a multilingual transformer fine-tuned on source-only (zero-shot) to contextualize the gains of wikifier features.
- Report results with standard label sets (no remapping), and in a second setting with your remapping, to ensure comparability and to quantify the dependence on KB-based relabeling.
- Consider a more structured decoder (e.g., CRF) to reduce error propagation from greedy decoding, especially when previous-tag features are important in transfer.
- Provide qualitative error analysis illustrating typical failure modes of the wikifier (e.g., common noun overlinking, metonymy, nested mentions) and their effects on NER.

Overall, the idea is elegant and empirically promising, particularly for resource-lean settings with available Wikipedia. To reach the bar for acceptance, the paper should modernize baselines, replace Freebase with a current KB, clarify the evaluation protocol, and deepen analysis of computational cost and error sources.
