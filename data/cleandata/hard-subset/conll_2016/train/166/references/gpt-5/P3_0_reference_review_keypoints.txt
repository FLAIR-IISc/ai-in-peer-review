- Proposes cross-lingual NER that wikifies every n-gram (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia and uses top-k Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features in a Ratinov & Roth-style model.  
- Demonstrates wikifier-derived features substantially improve monolingual and direct-transfer NER, outperforming prior direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL (ES/NL/DE) and five low-resource languages without parallel data or target annotations.  
- Analyzes when features help, showing dependence on Wikipedia size and English interlanguage links, and reports modest gains from multi-source training.  

- Clear, simple idea with strong empirical payoff: turning entity linking upstream yields delexicalized cross-lingual features that work across scripts and languages.  
- Minimal resource assumptions: requires only multilingual Wikipedia and an off-the-shelf cross-lingual wikifier—no parallel corpora, bilingual lexicons, or target annotations.  
- Solid empirical breadth: improves over strong baselines (including Täckström and Zhang et al.) while using fewer resources.  
- Useful analyses and ablations: includes “Wikifier only” ablation and shows how Freebase coverage/interlanguage link density affect performance; multi-source experiment is informative.  
- Practical implications: EL-derived types/categories form a robust, language-agnostic feature family that can plug into NER and other sequence tasks and combine with orthogonal signals.  

- Novelty is incremental: using Wikipedia categories/types for NER is not new, and the main technical dependency (cross-lingual wikifier) is external; the leap is moderate.  
- Evaluation choices may confound comparability: label remapping/removal (e.g., MISC→LOC, dropping TIME/TTL) and ignoring MISC in some matrices could make baseline comparisons unfair.  
- Heavy reliance on Freebase and English-centric KB signals: performance degrades for very low-resource Wikipedias and Freebase’s retirement raises sustainability concerns.  
- Limited error/type analysis: only aggregate F1 reported—no per-entity-type P/R, boundary vs typing breakdown, or qualitative error analysis to show where gains arise.  
- Missing stronger contemporary baselines: no comparisons to direct-transfer models using cross-lingual embeddings or modern neural sequence taggers (e.g., multilingual BiLSTM-CRF).  

- Clarify label mapping/evaluation: are all four CoNLL labels evaluated and consistent with Täckström baselines, and how were tags remapped for low-resource languages?  
- Wikifier coverage and efficiency: what fraction of tokens/n-grams are NIL vs linked across languages, and what is the runtime cost of wikifying all n-grams up to length 4?  
- Error profile question: do gains come from better typing or boundary detection—can you provide per-type P/R or common failure modes?  
- KB sustainability question: have you tried Wikidata or DBpedia as replacements for Freebase, and how sensitive are results to top-k candidate count?  
- Fairness to baselines: did Täckström’s baselines have access to target-language gazetteers from Wikipedia, and can you provide a variant without them?  

- Recommend using a CRF or globally normalized decoder to reduce propagation of local linking errors.  
- Suggest combining wikifier features with cross-lingual embeddings and/or silver projected data from Wikipedia as orthogonal improvements.  
- Advise reporting per-entity-type breakdowns and statistical significance to strengthen claims.  
- Propose adding a brief qualitative case study with success and failure examples for interpretability.  
- Recommend switching from Freebase to Wikidata to improve coverage and long-term viability.
