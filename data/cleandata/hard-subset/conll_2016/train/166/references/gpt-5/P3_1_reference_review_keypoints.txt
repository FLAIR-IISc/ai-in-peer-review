- Proposes direct-transfer NER using cross-lingual wikification: ground every n-gram (n≤4) to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types, Wikipedia categories, and linker confidence as language-independent features.
- Requires no parallel corpora; uses only multilingual Wikipedia for feature extraction.
- Shows wikifier-derived features substantially improve monolingual and zero-shot cross-lingual NER across nine languages, including non-Latin scripts.
- Analyzes sensitivity to Wikipedia size and availability of interlanguage links/Freebase, and shows dependence on English intersection and type coverage.
- Explores multi-source transfer and finds small but consistent gains from training on multiple annotated source languages.
- Strength: Clear, language-agnostic formulation that separates surface forms from language-independent entity semantics and complements other transfer methods.
- Strength: Broad empirical coverage across languages and informative ablations (wikifier-only, base, +gazetteers, +wikifier).
- Strength: Competitive direct-transfer results that outperform a strong baseline and a bitext-cluster model without parallel data.
- Strength: Simple integration into standard NER pipelines and compatibility with other transfer strategies.
- Strength: Multi-source transfer experiments show stacking annotated sources is generally beneficial, with high-resource donors most effective.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on Freebase and Wikipedia coverage; performance degrades for genuinely low-resource languages and Freebase is deprecated.
- Weakness: Comparisons may be unfair because the proposed method uses target-language Wikipedia resources while some baselines may not; lacks comparisons to strong multilingual transformer baselines (mBERT/XLM-R).
- Weakness: Possible evaluation leakage from Wikipedia-derived gazetteers/categories and label set alterations that complicate comparability to published results.
- Weakness: Computational cost and scalability concerns from wikifying all n-grams up to length 4; no runtime, memory, or pruning discussion.
- Weakness: Limited analysis depth—missing per-entity-type breakdowns, boundary vs. type error analysis, script-specific errors, linker confidence calibration, and negative-transfer exploration.
- Question: Please report runtime, throughput, indexing footprint, and average candidate set sizes for wikification and NER inference across languages.
- Question: How fair are comparisons to Täckström et al. (2012)? Provide a baseline using only English resources to isolate the gain from target-language Wikipedia.
- Question: Have you tried replacing Freebase with Wikidata (properties/instance-of/class membership), and how does that affect results, particularly for low-resource languages?
- Question: How do results compare to zero-shot mBERT/XLM-R baselines, and do wikifier features add value when combined with such models?
- Question: Provide per-entity-type F1 and boundary/type error decomposition, especially for non-Latin scripts.
- Overall: Valuable, well-supported idea but requires updates to resources (Wikidata), matched baselines (multilingual transformers), runtime/scalability analysis, and fairness fixes before acceptance.
