- Paper proposes direct-transfer NER using cross-lingual wikification to produce language-independent features from English Wikipedia/Freebase.  
- Wikify every n-gram (n ≤ 4) in target text and use linked English page’s Freebase types, Wikipedia categories, and gazetteers as features.  
- Trains averaged-perceptron BIO NER on source languages and applies to target languages without target supervision.  
- Empirical claim: wikification-derived semantic types are strong delexicalized features that outperform prior direct-transfer baselines and a weakly supervised system on several languages.  
- Provides a resource-light pipeline requiring only multilingual Wikipedia (no bitext or human-in-the-loop), plus wikifier modifications and ablations.  
- Broad evaluation across 9 languages (4 CoNLL + 5 low-resource, including non-Latin scripts) with analyses on Wikipedia size and interlanguage link coverage.  
- Strength: simple, practical, widely applicable approach that’s attractive for low-resource settings.  
- Strength: consistent empirical gains in mono- and cross-lingual NER, including notable F1 improvements (e.g., +10 on Dutch/German/Turkish).  
- Strength: thorough ablations (wikifier-only, removing Freebase/interlanguage links) and analysis showing performance scales with Wikipedia size.  
- Strength: applicability demonstrated on non-Latin scripts (Bengali, Tamil) where delexicalized features outperform lexical/gazetteer baselines.  
- Strength: reusability and transparency via known components and detailed feature descriptions supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: reliance on deprecated Freebase and English-centric KBs may limit longevity and coverage, with no alternative (e.g., Wikidata) evaluated.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—uses longstanding perceptron NER and prior Wikipedia/EL ideas, lacking joint NER+EL or more discriminative learning.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparisons to stronger modern baselines (multilingual embeddings, projection with better aligners, pretrained multilingual models).  
- Weakness: use of target-language gazetteers at test time blurs “direct transfer”; need separate reporting without target gazetteers.  
- Weakness: label set and evaluation choices (remapping/dropping MISC, TIME/TTL) complicate comparability and lack per-type analyses.  
- Weakness: scalability/runtime concerns unaddressed—wikifying every n-gram is potentially expensive and overlapping n-gram handling is unspecified.  
- Question: do cross-lingual experiments include target-language gazetteers, and can results be reported without them?  
- Question: what is the runtime cost of wikifying every n-gram, how are overlapping n-grams resolved, and how sensitive are results to linker k/confidence thresholds?  
- Question: have authors tried replacing Freebase with Wikidata, and how does that affect languages with poor Freebase coverage?  
- Question: how is tokenization handled for non-Latin scripts, and how sensitive is performance to tokenization quality or language-specific heuristics?  
- Question: can authors provide per-entity-type F1 and an error analysis (boundary vs type errors, NIL errors, systematic confusions)?  
- Additional comment: migrating to Wikidata and exploring joint/neural models or combining with projection/multilingual embeddings could improve novelty and robustness.
