- Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification of every n-gram (n ≤ 4) to map to English Wikipedia titles and derive Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features.
- Builds language-specific gazetteers from Wikipedia titles and uses ranker confidence features; trains on source languages and directly transfers to targets without parallel data or target supervision.
- Evaluates on CoNLL (EN/DE/ES/NL) and five low-resource languages (TR/TL/YO/BN/TA) with ablations and multi-source training analyses.
- Main methodological contribution: practical delexicalized feature set based on cross-lingual wikification and n-gram modifications (top-2 candidates, ranker features).
- Empirical claim: consistent F1 gains across nine languages in monolingual and direct-transfer settings, outperforming strong baselines and a weakly supervised system on low-resource languages.
- Analysis contribution: ablations showing performance correlates with Wikipedia size and interlanguage-link coverage, and Freebase availability impacts results.
- Strength: minimal assumptions—requires only multilingual Wikipedia and a wikifier, avoiding bitext, heuristics, or target supervision.
- Strength: sizable and consistent direct-transfer improvements (10+ F1 in several languages) and better low-resource performance with fewer resources.
- Strength: sensible, informative ablations and diagnostics that validate the role of Wikipedia coverage and Freebase.
- Strength: wikifier features improve monolingual NER and multi-source training provides modest additional gains without harming performance.
- Strength: clear methodological choices (n-gram wikification, top-2 candidates, ranker confidence) are well-motivated and effective.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on Freebase and English-centric resources; Freebase deprecation and limited non-English coverage are bottlenecks.
- Weakness: incremental novelty given prior uses of Wikipedia for NER; novelty mainly in scaling cross-lingual wikification and feature engineering.
- Weakness: scalability and computational cost of wikifying all n-grams not reported (runtime, memory, pruning/caching strategies missing).
- Weakness: potential asymmetry in direct-transfer baselines because target-language gazetteers and wikifier outputs use target Wikipedia.
- Weakness: label-space changes (remapping MISC labels via Freebase) may bias comparisons; significance testing absent.
- Key question: can Wikidata replace Freebase and how would that affect low-resource language performance?
- Key question: what is the computational cost (time/memory) of wikifying all n-grams and are there effective pruning strategies?
- Key question: how many tokens/n-grams are linked per language and what fraction of features derive from types vs categories vs ranker scores?
- Key question: what is the incremental effect of target-language gazetteers vs wikifier features in direct transfer (ablation requested)?
- Key question: sensitivity to top-1 vs top-2 vs top-k candidates and to maximum n-gram length?
- Key question: do wikifier features mainly help boundary detection or type classification (per-entity-type breakdown requested)?
- Key question: can this approach be combined with neural sequence models and cross-lingual embeddings for complementary gains?
