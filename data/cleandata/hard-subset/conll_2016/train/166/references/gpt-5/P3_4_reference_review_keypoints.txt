- Proposes direct-transfer cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification to English Wikipedia to get language-independent features (Freebase types, Wikipedia categories, ranker confidences) per n-gram, combined with a classic NER model and Wikipedia-derived gazetteers.  
- Shows consistent gains from wikifier features in monolingual and cross-lingual settings across CoNLL and five low-resource languages, with improvements correlating with target Wikipedia size/intersection with English.  
- Provides ablations on wikification components (Freebase queries, interlanguage link coverage) and explores multi-source training.  
- Strength: simple, clear idea with solid empirical validation; wikifier-only and cumulative ablations demonstrate complementary cross-lingual signal.  
- Strength: broad multilingual evaluation including non-Latin-script, low-resource languages; practical finding that wikifier gains scale with target Wikipedia size.  
- Strength: method is orthogonal to prior transfer methods (no bitext/alignments) and outperforms bitext-cluster transfer on shared languages.  
- Strength: sensible design choices to reduce false positives (whole-string queries, excluding global-mention embeddings) and use of linker confidence as safeguard.  
- Strength: multi-source training analysis provides insight into data augmentation and non-English best-source effects.  
- Weakness: core NER model is dated (2009 perceptron, greedy decoding) with no comparisons to modern cross-lingual neural baselines.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on deprecated Freebase undermines reproducibility and future utility; needs Wikidata replacement.  
- Weakness: evaluation label remapping and MISC handling may bias results toward KB-based features; lacks per-type breakdown (boundary vs typing gains).  
- Weakness: use of target-language Wikipedia resources at test time blurs the “direct transfer” claim and should be contrasted with English-only KB baseline.  
- Weakness: computational/practical concerns unaddressed—no runtime, memory, candidate count, NIL rates, or analysis of spurious candidates and overlinking.  
- Questions: replace Freebase with Wikidata and re-run ablations?  
- Questions: report runtime/memory, average candidate links per token, and NIL rates per language for n-grams (n ≤ 4).  
- Questions: sensitivity to number of top candidates (top-2) and separate ablations for Wikipedia categories vs KB types?  
- Questions: provide per-entity-type precision/recall and boundary vs type error breakdowns.  
- Questions: how is multi-source training balanced to avoid source-dominance and is there evidence of negative transfer?  
- Questions: how does the system perform with English-only KB (no target-language Wikipedia/gazetteers)?  
- Suggestions: include stronger baselines (cross-lingual embedding CRF, multilingual transformer zero-shot) and report results with standard label sets and remapped labels.  
- Suggestions: consider a structured decoder (e.g., CRF) to reduce greedy decoding errors and provide qualitative error analysis of wikifier failure modes (overlinking, metonymy, nested mentions).
