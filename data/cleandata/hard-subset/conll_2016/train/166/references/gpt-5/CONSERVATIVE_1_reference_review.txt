Summary and main contributions
- The paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that augments a standard sequence labeling model (averaged perceptron, BIO) with features derived from a cross-lingual wikifier. Words/n-grams (up to length 4) are linked to English Wikipedia; the associated Freebase types and Wikipedia categories are used as language-independent features.
- The authors demonstrate improvements in both monolingual and direct-transfer settings on CoNLL languages (EN, ES, DE, NL) and five low-resource languages (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA), with ablations showing that wikifier features add predictive power beyond base and gazetteer features.
- Analyses examine the dependency of performance on the size of target-language Wikipedia and show modest gains from multi-source training.

Strengths
- Empirical gains in direct transfer: On CoNLL non-English test sets, adding wikifier features yields substantial improvements over their own direct-transfer baseline and over Täckström et al. (2012) without bitext clusters; the proposed model is competitive with their bitext-cluster results while not relying on parallel data. This directly supports the central claim that cross-lingual wikification provides strong delexicalized signal for NER.
- Sensible ablations: The progression from base → +gazetteers → +wikifier clarifies the marginal utility of each feature family, and “wikifier-only” results are informative about how much signal the linkage itself carries across languages.
- Broad language coverage: Including five low-resource languages (including non-Latin scripts) is valuable. The analysis connecting performance degradation to Wikipedia/interlanguage link coverage is convincing and explains when the method is likely to work.
- Practical multi-source transfer: Results showing small but consistent gains from training on multiple annotated source languages are useful and align with established findings in cross-lingual transfer.
- Minimal target-side supervision: The approach avoids parallel corpora and language-specific heuristics, and in principle scales to any language with interlanguage links to English Wikipedia.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to prior work: The core idea—using Wikipedia-derived type/category features for NER—is not new (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa, 2007; Sil & Yates, 2013). The main difference here is the use of an existing cross-lingual wikifier to supply those features and applying them in direct transfer. Methodologically this is an incremental combination of established components, rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Dependence on Freebase and interlanguage links compromises generality and reproducibility: The method leans heavily on Freebase types and English interlanguage links. Freebase has been deprecated; for several low-resource languages the Freebase step fails entirely. The claim that “only Wikipedia dump is required” is therefore overstated. A viable, well-specified replacement (e.g., Wikidata) and an implementation plan are needed for reproducibility.
- Evaluation choices and potential tuning on test languages: 
  - For low-resource languages, the paper at times uses only wikifier features for non-Latin scripts and all features for Latin scripts. It is unclear how this decision would be made in a realistic zero-shot setting without target-language development data; this risks implicit tuning on the test language.
  - The label set is modified: MISC is removed for CoNLL languages in some experiments, and MISC→LOC remapping is applied using Freebase. This deviates from standard CoNLL evaluation and may complicate fairness of comparisons (including with Zhang et al., 2016). The impact of these choices is not quantified.
- Comparisons miss stronger contemporary baselines: The paper compares to Täckström (2012) and one projection-style method, but omits more competitive cross-lingual transfer approaches based on multilingual embeddings or contextual models. Even if such models are orthogonal, a strong direct-transfer baseline (e.g., multilingual word embeddings, or modern multilingual encoders if available) is essential to support claims of state-of-the-art performance.
- Scalability and error analysis are underdeveloped: 
  - Grounding every n-gram up to length 4 is potentially expensive and prone to spurious links; there is no runtime or throughput analysis, nor discussion of pruning strategies.
  - Error breakdowns (by entity type, length, or linking confidence) are limited; the paper largely attributes failures to Wikipedia size without deeper qualitative analysis of linking errors versus boundary/type errors.
- Statistical significance and robustness: Reported gains are sometimes modest; there is no statistical testing, and it is unclear how sensitive results are to choices like top-k candidates, feature thresholds, or hyperparameters of the wikifier.

Questions to authors
- Reproducibility and resources: Will you release code, the exact gazetteer construction pipeline, and the trained cross-lingual wikifier models? Given Freebase deprecation, can your features be derived from Wikidata (properties/classes) or Wikipedia categories alone? Please provide a concrete, implementable replacement.
- Model selection in zero-shot: How would a practitioner decide whether to use “wikifier-only” vs. “all features” for a new target language without a labeled dev set? Can you propose a language-agnostic, unsupervised criterion (e.g., based on script detection, coverage of interlanguage links, or wikifier confidence) and validate it?
- Label set modifications: What is the quantitative impact of removing MISC and remapping MISC→LOC on CoNLL and low-resource results? Are baselines treated identically? Please provide results under standard CoNLL scoring as well.
- Comparisons: Can you add baselines using multilingual word embeddings or off-the-shelf multilingual encoders in a direct-transfer setting, to contextualize the contribution of wikifier features?
- Efficiency: What is the computational cost of wikifying all n-grams (n ≤ 4) per document? What pruning or candidate filtering is used to keep latency manageable, and how does that affect performance?
- Error analysis: Can you provide an entity-type breakdown and an analysis of cases where linking is incorrect but still beneficial/harmful to NER, and how the ranker confidence is used to mitigate noise?

Overall, the paper demonstrates that cross-lingual wikification carries useful signal for NER and can provide meaningful gains in direct transfer. However, the methodological novelty is limited, evaluation choices need clarification, and reproducibility depends on now-defunct resources. Strengthening baselines, standardizing evaluation, and providing a Freebase-independent implementation would substantially improve the paper.
