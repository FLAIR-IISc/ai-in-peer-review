- Proposes direct-transfer cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to map all n-grams (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent NER features.
- Shows empirical gains from wikifier-derived features across 9 languages in monolingual and direct-transfer settings, outperforming prior direct-transfer and some low-resource baselines.
- Analyzes dependence of feature quality on Wikipedia size and interlanguage links and finds small gains from multi-source training.
- Strength: practical, low-resource approach requiring only multilingual Wikipedia and no bitext or target annotations.
- Strength: strong empirical evidence and careful ablations across languages and scenarios, with consistent F1 improvements.
- Strength: approach is orthogonal to projection and embedding methods and can complement other cross-lingual signals.
- Strength: insightful analysis of script effects and how feature firing relates to performance.
- Strength: multi-source transfer yields modest additional improvements, suggesting extensibility.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on deprecated Freebase and English Wikipedia categories threatens reproducibility and long-term utility.
- Weakness: core NER model (averaged perceptron, hand-crafted features) and baselines are dated compared to modern neural multilingual methods.
- Weakness: label mapping/removal decisions (e.g., MISC/TTL/TIME) complicate comparability and may bias results.
- Weakness: no analysis of computational/engineering costs for wikifying every n-gram (runtime, memory, precision/recall).
- Weakness: limited qualitative/error analysis and little breakdown by entity type or failure mode, especially for low-resource and non-Latin scripts.
- Reproducibility question: please release code, gazetteers, and wikifier configs and report whether Wikidata can replace Freebase and with what impact.
- Efficiency question: report runtime and memory footprint of wikifying every n-gram and compare inference time to the base model.
- Label harmonization question: quantify sensitivity to dropping/remapping tags and provide results under a standardized tagset for fair comparison.
- Feature-ablation question: report “Base + Wikifier” and “Gazetteers + Wikifier” and per-entity-type F1 to isolate wikifier contribution.
- Wikifier training question: clarify whether the wikifier needs supervised training beyond dumps, and how tokenization/segmentation is handled for non-Latin scripts.
- Overall: novelty moderate (cross-lingual grounding is a useful twist), soundness generally good but needs clarification on label handling and costs.
- Overall: significant practical impact for low-resource settings but should include modern baselines or demonstrate complementarity with multilingual encoders.
- Overall: clear and well organized but needs more implementation details and expanded error analysis to be a solid, impactful contribution.
