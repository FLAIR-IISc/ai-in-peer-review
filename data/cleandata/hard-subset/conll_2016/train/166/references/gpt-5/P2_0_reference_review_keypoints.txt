- Proposes direct-transfer NER using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features (Freebase types and Wikipedia categories) by grounding every n-gram (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia.  
- Builds on a strong English NER system and augments it with multilingual gazetteers plus novel wikifier-derived features, with two modifications to make dense n-gram wikification feasible.  
- Delivers substantial gains in direct transfer without parallel data, outperforming Täckström et al. (2012) on CoNLL (ES/NL/DE) and Zhang et al. (2016) on five low-resource languages; monolingual experiments also improve with wikifier features.  
- Provides thorough analyses: ablations (wikifier-only, base, +gazetteers, +wikifier), sensitivity to Wikipedia/Freebase coverage, and multi-source training showing consistent gains.  

- Strength: Simple, elegant, and practical idea that requires only a multilingual Wikipedia dump and no target-language annotation.  
- Strength: Strong, consistent empirical improvements (e.g., +10 F1 in some settings) across Latin and non-Latin scripts.  
- Strength: Clean, insightful ablations and controlled experiments tying performance to Wikipedia/Freebase coverage rather than model brittleness.  
- Strength: Multi-source training experiments and language-pair matrix provide practical guidance on source language choices.  
- Strength: Wikifier features also improve monolingual NER, indicating general semantic utility.  

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Freebase (deprecated) and noisy/uneven Wikipedia categories; ablation shows big drops without Freebase, raising sustainability concerns.  
- Weakness: No evaluation of efficiency or scalability for wikifying all n-grams (runtime, memory, throughput) and no practical pruning/caching guidance.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps—insufficient detail on modified wikifier candidate generation/ranking, exact feature templates, stopword/NIL handling, and unclear code release plans.  
- Weakness: Evaluation comparability issues—label mapping and preprocessing choices (e.g., MISC handling, LOC remapping) complicate fair comparisons with prior work.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to more recent cross-lingual representation baselines (e.g., multilingual embeddings or joint EL+NER) to contextualize gains.  

- Questions: What are the runtime/memory costs and scaling behavior of wikifying all n-grams, and are there pruning/caching heuristics or speed/accuracy trade-offs?  
- Questions: Will code, trained models, gazetteers, label-remapping scripts, and full feature templates (including ranker confidence and stopword/NIL rules) be released?  
- Questions: Can you provide finer-grained ablations (Freebase types vs. Wikipedia categories vs. ranker confidence; categories-only; top-1 vs. top-2 candidates), clarify CoNLL MISC inclusion, ensure preprocessing alignment with baselines, and detail hyperparameter tuning and multi-source balancing?
