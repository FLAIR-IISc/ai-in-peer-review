- Proposes cross-lingual NER that wikifies every n-gram (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia and uses Wikipedia categories + Freebase types as language-independent features in an averaged-perceptron BIO model, with two practical tweaks to make exhaustive linking feasible.
- Evaluates monolingual and direct-transfer settings across nine languages (including five low-resource, non-Latin scripts), analyzing wikifier feature utility, dependence on Wikipedia/interlanguage links, and multi-source training effects.
- Claims to outperform prior direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL languages and a weakly supervised method on low-resource languages without parallel corpora or target-language annotation.

- Conceptually simple and resource-efficient: a modular cross-lingual wikifier approach that avoids parallel corpora and is applicable to many languages with only Wikipedia.
- Broad, diverse evaluation across languages and scripts increases ecological validity and shows consistent monolingual gains from wikifier features.
- Informative ablations diagnose why low-resource languages benefit less (weak interlanguage links, lack of Freebase), and comparisons (wikifier-only vs all-features) give actionable guidance.
- Demonstrates compatibility with multi-source transfer, showing modest but useful gains when combining source languages.
- Practical tweaks to candidate generation/ranking reduce false positives and improve robustness when wikifying all n-grams.

- Lacks comparisons to strong modern baselines (e.g., mBERT/XLM-R zero-shot or CRF over aligned multilingual embeddings), making significance unclear.
- Heavy dependence on Freebase and English Wikipedia raises sustainability and reproducibility concerns; performance drops when Freebase or interlanguage links are missing.
- Feature calibration problems in cross-script transfer: adding lexical/gazetteer features can hurt performance (e.g., Yoruba), suggesting over-weighting of English-specific signals and no proposed mitigation.
- Some evaluation/comparison details are unclear or inconsistent (e.g., CoNLL Spanish result mapping, use of target-side gazetteers/Wikipedia at test time, label modifications for low-resource languages) which could bias comparisons.
- Practicality concerns: exhaustive n-gram wikification may be expensive, yet runtime, memory, and throughput measurements are missing.
- Preprocessing/tokenization details for non-Latin scripts are lacking and could materially affect results.

- Please add comparisons to zero-shot multilingual encoders (mBERT/XLM-R) and/or CRF with aligned multilingual embeddings to establish modern baselines.
- Clarify whether direct-transfer uses target-language gazetteers and target-side Wikipedia for feature extraction; provide ablations without target-side gazetteers.
- Can features be replicated using Wikidata types/properties instead of deprecated Freebase, and how do results change?
- Can you mitigate the “all-features hurts” calibration issue (e.g., feature-group reweighting, instance weighting, or adversarial/domain adaptation)?
- Report end-to-end runtime, memory footprint for wikifying all n-grams, and tokenization/casing handling for non-Latin scripts.

- Method is technically sound and analyses support central claims.
- Novelty is moderate: KB-derived types for NER are known, but exhaustive cross-lingual wikification and its use for direct transfer are the main contributions.
- Potentially useful in settings without modern multilingual encoders, but impact is uncertain without comparisons to current strong baselines.
- Clarity/reproducibility generally good but some evaluation details and resource dependencies need clarification; code/resource availability not specified.
- Reviewer leans borderline accept pending addition of modern baselines and clarifications on resource dependencies.
