**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) model that can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia. The model uses a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate words and phrases to the English Wikipedia, providing language-independent features for NER. The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages, including high-resource languages (Spanish, German, and Dutch) and low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil). The results show that the proposed model outperforms comparable approaches on the standard CoNLL datasets and also performs well on low-resource languages.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Cross-lingual wikifier features**: The authors propose a novel method for generating language-independent features for NER using a cross-lingual wikifier. This feature set is the primary contribution of the paper.
2. **Language-independent NER model**: The authors develop a language-independent NER model that can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia, using the cross-lingual wikifier features.
3. **Evaluation on low-resource languages**: The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages, including low-resource languages, which is a significant contribution to the field of NLP.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel feature set**: The cross-lingual wikifier features are a novel and effective way to generate language-independent features for NER.
2. **Strong evaluation**: The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages, including high-resource and low-resource languages, which demonstrates the robustness of their approach.
3. **Comparison to state-of-the-art**: The authors compare their model to state-of-the-art approaches, including the Täckström baseline and the expectation learning model proposed in Zhang et al. (2016), and show that their model outperforms these approaches.
4. **Analysis of wikifier features**: The authors provide an analysis of the wikifier features, including the impact of Wikipedia size on the quality of the features, which provides insight into the strengths and limitations of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on Wikipedia**: The authors' approach relies heavily on Wikipedia, which may not be available or up-to-date for all languages.
2. **Limited use of lexical features**: The authors do not use lexical features for non-Latin script languages, which may limit the performance of their model on these languages.
3. **No comparison to monolingual models**: The authors do not compare their cross-lingual model to monolingual models, which would provide a more complete evaluation of their approach.
4. **Limited analysis of errors**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by their model, which would provide insight into areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limitation of relying on Wikipedia, which may not be available or up-to-date for all languages?
2. Can you provide more details on the analysis of errors made by your model, including the types of errors and the languages on which they occur?
3. How do you plan to extend your approach to include other techniques for multilingual NER, such as parallel projection and generating training data from Wikipedia automatically?
4. Can you provide more details on the computational resources required to train and evaluate your model, including the time and memory requirements?
