**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a language-independent method for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier. The method grounds words and phrases in non-English text to English Wikipedia entries, providing language-independent features for NER. The authors evaluate their approach on a wide range of languages, including low-resource languages, and show significant improvements over strong baselines.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Cross-lingual wikifier features**: The authors introduce a novel approach to generating language-independent features for NER using a cross-lingual wikifier. This feature set is shown to be effective in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.
2. **Language-independent NER model**: The authors propose a language-independent NER model that can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia, requiring only a Wikipedia dump as input.
3. **Evaluation on low-resource languages**: The authors evaluate their approach on a set of low-resource languages, including Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil, and show that their method outperforms existing approaches.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of wikifier features**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their cross-lingual wikifier features in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.
2. **Robustness to low-resource languages**: The authors show that their approach can handle low-resource languages with limited annotated data.
3. **Improved performance over baselines**: The authors demonstrate significant improvements over strong baselines in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on Wikipedia size**: The authors note that the quality of wikifier features depends on the size of the Wikipedia dump, which may be a limitation for low-resource languages.
2. **Limited evaluation on high-resource languages**: The authors primarily evaluate their approach on low-resource languages, with limited evaluation on high-resource languages.
3. **Lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The authors do not compare their approach to state-of-the-art methods in the field, which may limit the understanding of their approach's strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the limitation of their approach's dependence on Wikipedia size, particularly for low-resource languages?
2. Can the authors provide more evaluation results on high-resource languages to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in these settings?
3. How do the authors plan to integrate their approach with other techniques for multilingual NER, such as parallel projection and generating training data from Wikipedia automatically?
