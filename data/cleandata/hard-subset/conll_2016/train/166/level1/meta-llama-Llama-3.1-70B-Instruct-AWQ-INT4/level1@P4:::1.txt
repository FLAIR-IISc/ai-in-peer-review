**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a language-independent model for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram. The model works on all languages in Wikipedia and only requires a Wikipedia dump. The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings, showing significant improvements over strong baselines.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Cross-lingual wikifier features**: The authors introduce a novel feature extraction method that grounds words and phrases in non-English languages to the English Wikipedia, providing language-independent features for NER.
2. **Language-independent NER model**: The authors propose a language-independent NER model that can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia, using the cross-lingual wikifier features.
3. **Evaluation on low-resource languages**: The authors evaluate their model on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil) and show that their approach performs better than a state-of-the-art model that assumes parallel text and interaction with a native speaker.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel feature extraction method**: The cross-lingual wikifier features are a novel contribution to the field of NER, and the authors demonstrate their effectiveness in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.
2. **Language independence**: The authors' model is language-independent, making it applicable to all languages in Wikipedia, which is a significant advantage over previous approaches.
3. **Improved performance on low-resource languages**: The authors' model performs better than a state-of-the-art model on low-resource languages, which is a significant contribution to the field of NER.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on Wikipedia size**: The quality of the wikifier features depends on the size of the Wikipedia for the target language, which may limit the model's performance on low-resource languages.
2. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their model on a limited set of languages, and it is unclear how well the model will perform on other languages.
3. **Comparison to other approaches**: The authors only compare their model to a few other approaches, and it is unclear how their model performs compared to other state-of-the-art models.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the limitation of their model's dependence on Wikipedia size?
2. Can the authors provide more details on how they plan to extend their model to other languages?
3. How does the authors' model perform compared to other state-of-the-art models in the field of NER?

**Additional Comments**

The authors' model is a significant contribution to the field of NER, and their evaluation on low-resource languages is a valuable addition to the field. However, the authors should consider addressing the limitations of their model and providing more comprehensive evaluations to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
