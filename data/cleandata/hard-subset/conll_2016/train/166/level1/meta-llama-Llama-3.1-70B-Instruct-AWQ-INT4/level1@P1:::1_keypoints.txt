- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model using a wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram and ground to English Wikipedia using only a Wikipedia dump.  
- Evaluated broadly across many languages in monolingual and cross-lingual settings, showing significant improvements over strong baselines.  
- Introduces cross-lingual wikifier features that ground phrases to English Wikipedia for language-independent NER features.  
- Presents a language-independent NER model applicable to all languages in Wikipedia without language-specific features or annotations.  
- Evaluates effectiveness on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Strong, thorough evaluation across many languages including low-resource settings and comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Novel feature extraction method offering a new way to obtain language-independent NER signals.  
- Flexible approach usable for any language with a Wikipedia presence.  
- Heavy reliance on Wikipedia quality and size, which may limit performance for languages with small or poor Wikipedias.  
- Limited exploitation of Wikipedia: only uses categories and Freebase types, leaving other signals (document words, inter-title relations) unexplored.  
- Simple additive combination of wikifier features with other features, which may be suboptimal.  
- Questions: how to mitigate reliance on limited Wikipedias for low-resource languages?  
- Questions: can additional Wikipedia information (words, title relations) be used to boost performance?  
- Questions: how will you combine wikifier features with other features more effectively than a simple additive approach?
