- Proposes cross-lingual NER via wikification that grounds non-English text to English Wikipedia entries.  
- Uses wikification-derived features claimed to be language-independent for training a single multilingual NER model.  
- Evaluates the model on both high-resource (EN, DE, ES, NL) and low-resource (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA) languages.  
- Reports outperforming comparable approaches on high- and low-resource languages.  
- Novel approach distinct from parallel-data or language-specific feature methods.  
- Strong empirical results reported across evaluated languages.  
- Claims broad applicability to all languages present in Wikipedia, beneficial for low-resource languages.  
- Method heavily depends on Wikipedia availability and quality, which may be lacking for some languages.  
- Evaluation covers a limited set of languages, raising questions about generalizability.  
- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art NER models, making impact assessment difficult.  
- Asks how authors will address Wikipedia availability and quality for low-resource languages.  
- Requests details on extending the approach to other languages and plans for further evaluation.  
- Seeks comparison to state-of-the-art NER methods and clarification of advantages/disadvantages.
