- Proposes a cross-lingual NER model using a wikifier to map mentions to English Wikipedia, yielding language-independent features and strong performance across languages.  
- Evaluated on high- and low-resource languages and outperforms comparable approaches on CoNLL datasets and low-resource tests.

- Main contribution: novel cross-lingual wikifier features for language-independent NER.  
- Main contribution: a language-independent NER model applicable to all Wikipedia languages.  
- Main contribution: extensive evaluation including low-resource languages.

- Strength: the wikifier-derived feature set is novel and effective for cross-lingual NER.  
- Strength: strong multilingual evaluation covering both high- and low-resource languages.  
- Strength: outperforms state-of-the-art baselines (Täckström, Zhang et al. 2016).  
- Strength: analysis of wikifier features, including the effect of Wikipedia size on feature quality.

- Weakness: heavy dependence on Wikipedia, which may lack coverage or be outdated for some languages.  
- Weakness: limited use of lexical features for non-Latin scripts, potentially hurting performance.  
- Weakness: no comparison against monolingual NER models for a fuller evaluation.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis, lacking detailed breakdowns by error type and language.

- Question: how will you address reliance on Wikipedia when coverage or recency is poor?  
- Question: can you provide detailed error analysis by error type and language?  
- Question: will you extend the approach with other multilingual NER techniques (e.g., parallel projection, auto-generated training data from Wikipedia)?  
- Question: what are the computational requirements (time, memory) for training and evaluation?
