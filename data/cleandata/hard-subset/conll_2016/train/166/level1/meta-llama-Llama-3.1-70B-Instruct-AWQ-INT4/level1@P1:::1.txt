**Summary**

This paper proposes a language-independent model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram. The model works on all languages in Wikipedia and only requires a Wikipedia dump. The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings, showing significant improvements over strong baselines.

**Contributions**

1. **Cross-lingual wikifier features**: The authors introduce a novel feature extraction method that grounds words and phrases to the English Wikipedia, providing language-independent features for NER.
2. **Language-independent NER model**: The proposed model can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia, without requiring language-specific features or annotations.
3. **Evaluation on low-resource languages**: The authors evaluate their model on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil), demonstrating its effectiveness in settings with limited training data.

**Strengths**

1. **Strong evaluation**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their model on a wide range of languages, including low-resource languages, and compare it to strong baselines.
2. **Novel feature extraction method**: The cross-lingual wikifier features introduced in this paper provide a new way to extract language-independent features for NER.
3. **Flexibility**: The proposed model can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia, making it a valuable tool for multilingual NER.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on Wikipedia**: The model relies heavily on the quality and size of Wikipedia articles, which may limit its effectiveness for languages with small or low-quality Wikipedia presence.
2. **Limited use of Wikipedia information**: The authors only use Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as features, leaving other potentially useful information (e.g., words from documents, relations between titles) unexplored.
3. **Simple feature combination**: The authors combine the cross-lingual wikifier features with other features using a simple additive approach, which may not be the most effective way to combine these features.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limitation of relying on Wikipedia, particularly for languages with small or low-quality Wikipedia presence?
2. Can you explore the use of other Wikipedia information (e.g., words from documents, relations between titles) as features to improve the model's performance?
3. How do you plan to combine the cross-lingual wikifier features with other features in a more effective way, rather than using a simple additive approach?
