- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model using a wikifier that disambiguates every n-gram to English Wikipedia, needing only a Wikipedia dump and showing improvements over strong baselines.
- Introduces cross-lingual wikifier features that ground non-English words/phrases to English Wikipedia as novel language-independent features for NER.
- Proposes a language-independent NER model applicable to all languages in Wikipedia using those wikifier features.
- Evaluates the approach on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) and reports better performance than a prior state-of-the-art method requiring parallel text and native-speaker interaction.
- Strength: novel and effective feature extraction method demonstrated in monolingual and cross-lingual settings.
- Strength: true language independence, making the model broadly applicable across Wikipedia languages.
- Strength: improved results on several low-resource languages, a meaningful contribution for scarce-resource settings.
- Weakness: wikifier feature quality depends on target-language Wikipedia size, potentially limiting performance for very low-resource languages.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a small set of languages, leaving generalization to other languages unclear.
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods, making relative performance uncertain.
- Question: how will the authors address the model’s dependence on Wikipedia size?
- Question: can the authors provide more details on extending the model to additional languages?
- Question: how does the model compare quantitatively to other SOTA NER approaches?
- Overall comment: the work is a significant contribution with valuable low-resource evaluation, but needs to address limitations and provide more comprehensive evaluations.
