- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER that uses wikification to map words/phrases to English Wikipedia/FreeBase types as language-independent features, working for high- and low-resource languages.

- Contribution 1: Novel method to generate language-independent features via cross-lingual wikification enabling English-trained NER to apply to any language with Wikipedia coverage.

- Contribution 2: Comprehensive empirical validation showing effectiveness across many languages, including low-resource ones, outperforming direct-transfer baselines.

- Contribution 3: Shows these wikification-derived features also improve monolingual NER systems across tested languages.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical validation across diverse high- and low-resource languages with consistent improvements and practical gains for low-resource cases.

- Strength 2: Novel use of wikification and FreeBase/Wikipedia category signals as robust, language-independent features despite imperfect disambiguation.

- Strength 3: Practical, low-resource implementation requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps and benefiting from multi-source training.

- Strength 4: Analysis of how Wikipedia size and interlanguage/FreeBase coverage affect feature quality and performance.

- Weakness 1: Limited comparison to prior Wikipedia-based NER work (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa, Nothman et al.), weakening the novelty claim.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient technical detail on wikifier modifications for n-gram handling and candidate generation/ranking.

- Weakness 3: Low-resource evaluation limited to five languages with insufficient analysis of why Bengali and Tamil perform poorly beyond script issues.

- Weakness 4: No comparison with more recent language-agnostic approaches using multilingual embeddings or models like XLM/mBERT.

- Question 1: How do wikifier-derived features compare to other language-independent features (e.g., character n-grams, multilingual embeddings) and what is their relative importance?

- Question 2: Provide exact implementation details for the wikifier’s n-gram candidate generation and ranking to avoid false entities.

- Question 3: How much of the Bengali/Tamil performance drop is due to lack of FreeBase support, and how would results change if FreeBase covered these languages?

- Question 4: Which source languages most improve transfer to particular targets when training on multiple sources, and why?
