

This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that leverages cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The main contributions, as I see them, are: 1) The insight that grounding every n-gram (not just named entities) to English Wikipedia via cross-lingual wikification yields strong language-independent features for NER; 2) The empirical demonstration that these features significantly improve performance across multiple languages, including low-resource ones; 3) The demonstration that these features can enhance both cross-lingual transfer and monolingual NER systems.

Strengths: The paper provides a genuinely language-independent approach that requires only Wikipedia dumps, eliminating the need for parallel data or language-specific features. The results are compelling across 9 languages, with significant improvements over strong baselines, especially for low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, etc.) where other methods fail. The analysis showing the direct correlation between Wikipedia size and feature quality provides valuable insights into the approach's limitations. The method is simple yet effective, making it accessible to the broader NLP community.

Weaknesses: The paper inadequately addresses why their approach outperforms previous work that also leverages Wikipedia (e.g., Kazama and Torisawa 2007), particularly regarding the novelty of grounding every n-gram. The handling of non-Latin script languages (Bengali, Tamil) is problematic as the method fails to extract FreeBase types for these languages, yet the paper claims "very good" performance. The evaluation of low-resource languages is limited to only five languages, making it difficult to generalize the findings. The paper doesn't sufficiently address the scalability limitations when Wikipedia size is extremely small (e.g., Yoruba).

Questions: How do you handle cases where the cross-lingual wikifier fails to find any Wikipedia entries for a given language (e.g., for languages with minimal Wikipedia coverage)? Could you provide more details about the implementation of your modified wikifier, particularly regarding the n-gram processing and candidate generation adjustments?
