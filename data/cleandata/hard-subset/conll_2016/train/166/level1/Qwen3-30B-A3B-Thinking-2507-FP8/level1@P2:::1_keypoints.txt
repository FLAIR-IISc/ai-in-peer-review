1. Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to extract language-independent features by grounding multilingual mentions to English Wikipedia entries.  
2. Uses Wikipedia categories and Freebase types as robust NER features, effective even with imperfect disambiguation.  
3. Demonstrates strong empirical results across high- and low-resource languages and analyzes Wikipedia size impact on feature quality.  
4. Novel, practical approach that requires only multilingual Wikipedia and no parallel data or target-language annotations.  
5. Consistently outperforms prior baselines across diverse languages, showing large F1 improvements (e.g., Dutch, German, Turkish).  
6. Thorough evaluation on both CoNLL and several low-resource languages, demonstrating broad applicability.  
7. Insightful analysis linking wikifier feature quality and NER performance to Wikipedia size, including ablation of interlanguage links.  
8. Language-independent features also improve monolingual NER systems across all evaluated languages.  
9. Clear comparisons with strong baselines (Täckström et al., Zhang et al.) showing resource-efficiency advantages.  
10. Lacks comparisons to more recent multilingual embedding methods (e.g., XLM, mBERT) and discussion of complementarity or redundancy.  
11. Provides insufficient detail on the modifications made to the Tsai and Roth (2016) wikifier, limiting reproducibility.  
12. Does not discuss computational complexity or resource costs of wikifying many n-grams for large-scale use.  
13. No analysis of specific failure cases to illuminate method limitations and guide improvements.  
14. Limited exploration of language-specific challenges (especially non-Latin scripts) and strategies to address them.  
15. Question: Would the proposed features complement or be redundant with current multilingual embeddings like mBERT?  
16. Question: Why do English-based gazetteers fail for non-Latin scripts beyond script differences, and how to improve matching?  
17. Question: Why does Spanish perform best as a training language for Yoruba—due to lexical similarity, Wikipedia size, or other factors?  
18. Question: How does removing MISC tags when training affect evaluation for low-resource languages, and would results change if included?  
19. Question: How would the approach scale to languages with extremely small Wikipedias (<10k articles) and what alternatives are recommended?  
20. Overall: Strong, practical contribution with convincing results and valuable analysis, deserving acceptance after addressing noted details.
