- Summary: Proposes language-independent cross-lingual NER via wikification to English Wikipedia, using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories; shows wikifier features are strong signals, an English-trained model can transfer to many languages (including some low-resource), and features help both cross-lingual and monolingual NER using only multilingual Wikipedia dumps.

- Strength: Strong empirical validation across 9 languages with consistent improvements (e.g., Dutch direct transfer 62.10 vs 48.4) and useful analysis linking performance to Wikipedia size.

- Strength: Truly language-independent feature engineering that relies only on multilingual Wikipedia and remains effective where lexical features fail (e.g., Bengali/Tamil).

- Strength: Dual utility of features — improves cross-lingual transfer (10+ points on some languages) and boosts monolingual NER (0.5–5.5 F1 improvements).

- Weakness: Limited applicability to truly low-resource languages — very poor performance on Yoruba/Bengali/Tamil and an overoptimistic claim about applicability to all Wikipedia languages.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison with contemporary neural and other recent NER methods (e.g., Lample et al. 2016), limiting assessment of novelty/significance.

- Weakness: Lack of analysis on wikifier failure modes — no quantification of disambiguation errors or their correlation with NER errors.

- Weakness: Underestimation of computational costs — running a wikifier over all n-grams may be a scalability bottleneck not discussed.

- Question: How do wikifier features compare to other language-independent features like character n-grams or morphological features in monolingual NER?

- Question: For tiny Wikipedias (e.g., Yoruba), which entity types are hardest and could combining wikifier features with semi-supervised or parallel-data methods help?

- Question: How can the absence of FreeBase entries for some languages be mitigated (alternative KBs, synthetic entity types, etc.)?

- Question: How does performance scale for languages with extremely small Wikipedias (e.g., <10K articles) where wikifier features become very sparse?

- Overall: Valuable contribution demonstrating the promise of cross-lingual wikification for NER, but needs deeper analysis of limitations, comparisons to recent methods, and scalability for very low-resource settings.
