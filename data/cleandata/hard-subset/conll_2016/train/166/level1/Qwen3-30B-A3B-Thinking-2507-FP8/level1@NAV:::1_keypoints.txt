- Ground every n-gram to English Wikipedia via cross-lingual wikification to produce language-independent NER features.  
- Empirically show these features significantly improve NER performance across multiple languages, including low-resource ones.  
- Demonstrate the features enhance both cross-lingual transfer and monolingual NER systems.  
- Approach requires only Wikipedia dumps, removing need for parallel data or language-specific features.  
- Strong results across 9 languages with notable gains over baselines, especially for low-resource languages (e.g., Turkish, Tagalog).  
- Analysis reveals a direct correlation between Wikipedia size and feature quality, clarifying limitations.  
- Method is simple and effective, making it accessible to the broader NLP community.  
- Paper does not clearly explain why it outperforms prior Wikipedia-based methods (novelty of grounding every n-gram not fully justified).  
- Handling of non-Latin scripts (Bengali, Tamil) is problematic—Freebase type extraction fails despite claims of strong performance.  
- Low-resource evaluation is limited to five languages, reducing confidence in generalizability.  
- Scalability concerns when Wikipedia is extremely small (e.g., Yoruba) are not sufficiently addressed.  
- How are cases handled when the cross-lingual wikifier finds no Wikipedia entries for a language with minimal coverage?  
- Request more implementation details of the modified wikifier, especially n-gram processing and candidate generation adjustments.
