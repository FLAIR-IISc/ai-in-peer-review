- Introduces cross-lingual wikification to produce language-independent features for NER by grounding non-English text to English Wikipedia using FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories.  
- Proposes a novel NER model that uses these wikification-derived features for both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on CoNLL (Spanish, German, Dutch) and strong results on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Shows multi-source training improves target-language NER and that the wikification features also boost monolingual NER systems.  
- Novel and practical approach that removes dependence on language-specific annotated data, making it highly scalable across languages.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse languages, including non-Latin scripts, with detailed analyses of feature contributions and Wikipedia-size impact.  
- Consistently outperforms baselines and prior methods, particularly effective in low-resource scenarios.  
- Resource-efficient: requires only a multilingual Wikipedia dump, avoiding parallel corpora or extensive language-specific preprocessing.  
- Major weakness: reliance on Wikipedia size reduces effectiveness for languages with sparse Wikipedia coverage (e.g., Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Limited exploration of additional Wikipedia-derived information (e.g., document-level context, inter-title relations) that might improve performance.  
- Evaluation is confined to NER and does not test applicability of features to other NLP tasks like entity linking or relation extraction.  
- Question: How does the model handle ambiguous or noisy wikification, especially in low-resource languages with limited coverage?  
- Question: Have other Wikipedia-derived features (document context, inter-title relations) been considered to boost performance?  
- Question: Can the approach be extended to other tasks (entity linking, relation extraction), and what changes would that require?  
- Overall: Practical, innovative, and effective approach recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address Wikipedia-dependence and broader feature/task exploration.
