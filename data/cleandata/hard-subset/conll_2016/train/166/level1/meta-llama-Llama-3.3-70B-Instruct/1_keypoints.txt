Contributions:
- Language-independent features: use a cross-lingual wikifier to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia.
- Cross-lingual NER model: applicable to all Wikipedia languages and outperforms baselines on CoNLL and low-resource languages.
- Improved monolingual NER: the features boost monolingual NER performance across 9 evaluated languages.

Strengths:
- Effective use of Wikipedia to build language-independent features across many languages.
- State-of-the-art results on several languages, including low-resource ones.
- Flexible and scalable: supports training from multiple source languages to improve performance.

Weaknesses:
- Depends on target-language Wikipedia size, limiting effectiveness for low-resource languages.
- Only leverages Wikipedia categories and Freebase types, leaving other Wikipedia information unused.
- No comparison to other multilingual NER approaches (e.g., parallel projection or auto-generated Wikipedia training data).

Questions to authors:
- How will you address dependence on Wikipedia size for low-resource languages?
- Can you integrate other multilingual NER techniques like parallel projection or auto-generated Wikipedia training data?
- How will you evaluate the model on a larger set of languages, especially those with limited Wikipedia presence?
