- Proposes a direct-transfer, language-independent NER approach that wikifies n-grams to English Wikipedia and uses Wikipedia categories/Freebase types as language-agnostic features.

- Trains on one or multiple source languages and tests on target languages with no target-language supervision.

- Evaluates on CoNLL (es, de, nl) and five low-resource languages (tr, tl, yo, bn, ta) plus monolingual experiments.

- Key contribution: shows cross-lingual wikification yields strong delexicalized features that substantially improve direct transfer and low-resource NER.

- Key contribution: provides a systematic empirical study across nine languages with ablations linking performance to Wikipedia size and Freebase coverage.

- Key contribution: demonstrates multi-source transfer gains and that wikifier-derived features also help monolingual NER.

- Strength: simple, broadly applicable idea that reverses NER→EL to use EL signals as language-independent NER features, lowering deployment barriers.

- Strength: strong empirical gains over English-trained baselines and prior bitext-cluster models, especially where transfer is hardest.

- Strength: broad coverage and insightful ablations tying gains to Freebase availability and interlanguage link density, with practical guidance.

- Strength: approach is orthogonal to projection/clustering methods and can combine with other cross-lingual techniques.

- Strength: clear methodological description of wikifier modifications and feature construction enabling reimplementation.

- Weakness: heavy dependence on Wikipedia/Freebase coverage limits effectiveness in truly low-resource and non-Latin-script languages; Freebase deprecation raises sustainability concerns.

- Weakness: label-set inconsistencies (remapping/removal of tags) may conflate system performance with label engineering and bias comparisons.

- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent cross-lingual baselines and some unclear table/reporting alignment impacting claim clarity.

- Weakness: scalability and error analysis underexplored — no runtime/memory figures or detailed boundary vs. type error breakdowns.

- Weakness: “language-independent” claim diluted because best results rely on language-specific gazetteers and lexical features in some settings.

- Questions: request per-type precision/recall and boundary vs. type error breakdowns, especially for low-resource languages.

- Questions: ask for computational cost of wikifying n-grams, sensitivity to n and top-k candidate choices.

- Questions: ask whether Freebase was replaced with Wikidata or if only Wikipedia categories/infoboxes were tried for low-coverage languages.

- Questions: request a harmonized low-resource evaluation (e.g., PER/LOC/ORG only) without Freebase remapping.

- Questions: ask how robust gains are when language-specific gazetteers/lexical features are removed to isolate delexicalized signal.

- Overall assessment: moderate but meaningful novelty, simple and well-motivated with strong empirical results and analyses; merits acceptance despite limitations.
