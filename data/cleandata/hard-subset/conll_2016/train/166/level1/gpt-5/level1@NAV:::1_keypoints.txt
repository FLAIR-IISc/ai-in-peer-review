- Summary: Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification to map n-grams to English Wikipedia and harvest Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as delexicalized features, improving monolingual and direct-transfer NER across nine languages including low-resource ones.

- Primary contribution: Shows wikification-derived types/categories are strong, truly delexicalized features that yield substantial gains in direct transfer across diverse scripts/resources.

- Secondary contribution: Provides a careful empirical study and ablations across nine languages (including low-resource), identifying factors driving performance.

- Tertiary contribution: Demonstrates practical multi-source training that gives modest but consistent improvements and also boosts monolingual NER.

- Strength: Strong cross-lingual effectiveness under minimal assumptions—training on English + multilingual Wikipedia outperforms prior direct-transfer baselines and helps low-resource targets without parallel data.

- Strength: Convincing delexicalization—wikifier-only features preserve signal across languages/scripts and produce double-digit gains in several targets (e.g., NL/DE/TR).

- Strength: Thorough analysis of resource dependencies—experiments varying interlanguage links and Freebase coverage explain weaker results for some languages and validate the causal role of wikifier features.

- Strength: Practical scalability—applies to all Wikipedias, requires no alignment, benefits multi-source training, and improves monolingual systems across languages.

- Weakness: Heavy dependence on Wikipedia/Freebase coverage—performance degrades markedly for languages with limited interlanguage links or absent Freebase types and may fail on non-Wikipedia domains.

- Weakness: Label-set and evaluation inconsistencies—mapping/removing MISC and other tags for comparability may introduce bias and affect fairness of comparisons.

- Weakness: Limited baselines and model diversity—no comparisons to contemporary multilingual embeddings or joint NER-EL/neural approaches, weakening claims relative to modern methods.

- Weakness: Efficiency/latency concerns—wikifying all n-grams (n≤4) can be computationally expensive and no runtime/throughput analysis is provided.

- Question: How costly is full-document n-gram wikification in practice (time/memory), and can it be pruned without losing accuracy?

- Question: How do results change when replacing deprecated Freebase with Wikidata?

- Question: Have you evaluated on out-of-domain text (non-news) to assess domain robustness and Wikipedia bias?

- Question: Can you quantify statistical significance for key gains and provide more granular error analysis separating linking noise from boundary/type errors?
