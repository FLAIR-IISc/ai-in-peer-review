- Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification of n-grams to English Wikipedia titles and using Wikipedia categories/Freebase types as delexicalized features.
- Requires only multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel corpora) and can train on one/multiple sources to apply to any language with a Wikipedia.
- Shows sizable gains over direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL (ES, DE, NL) and five low-resource languages, and improves monolingual NER.

Main contributions:
- Demonstrates that wikification-derived type/category features give strong language-independent signals that improve cross-lingual NER without parallel data.
- Presents a practical, scalable framework tied to Wikipedia size/interlanguage links with multi-source training benefits.
- Shows the same features consistently improve monolingual NER across nine languages, complementing lexical and gazetteer features.

Strengths:
- Strong cross-lingual performance with minimal assumptions, beating some bitext-based methods while using no parallel corpora.
- Language-independence via grounding to English Wikipedia creates a unified type/category space and yields reasonable F1 even with wikifier-only features.
- Careful ablations and analyses clarify contributions of wikifier, gazetteers, and multi-source training, including degradation studies tied to resource size.
- Broad, realistic evaluation across nine languages and both standard and low-resource settings, including multi-source training experiments.
- Conceptually simple and compatible with other transfer methods and modern neural taggers.

Weaknesses:
- Heavy dependence on Wikipedia and Freebase limits effectiveness for very small Wikipedias and is problematic given Freebase deprecation.
- Potential unfairness in low-resource comparisons due to label-set modifications (dropping TIME/TTL, removing MISC, mapping MISC to LOC) and unclear baseline harmonization.
- Computational cost concerns from wikifying every n-gram up to length 4; runtime, memory, and pruning/caching strategies are unreported.
- Limited novelty beyond prior use of Wikipedia categories/types; main novelty is cross-lingual operationalization and extensive evaluation.
- Missing implementation details and error analysis: linker thresholds, ambiguity handling, tokenization, feature templates, and qualitative error breakdowns are under-specified.

Questions to authors:
- What are results replacing Freebase with Wikidata or removing Freebase entirely, and how much does performance change?
- What is the computational cost per sentence/document to wikify all n-grams up to 4, and what fraction of n-grams get linked?
- Which disambiguation/ranking features were retained, what confidence thresholds are used, and how are NILs and low-confidence links handled?
- How are multiple categories/types per candidate aggregated, normalized, or capped to avoid bias from many-category entities?
- Did all baselines in low-resource evaluations apply the same label-set modifications; if not, can matched evaluations be provided?
- How are multi-source training instances balanced or weighted across languages, and do gains persist with much smaller non-English sources?
- Have the wikifier features been tried in neural taggers (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF), and are similar gains expected?

Additional comments:
- Clarify availability and size of target-language gazetteers in truly low-resource settings and whether they are used only at test time.
- Recommend reporting per-entity-type F1 and boundary vs. classification errors to show where cross-lingual signals help most.
