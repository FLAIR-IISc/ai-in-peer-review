- Contribution: Introduces cross-lingual wikification features (Freebase types and Wikipedia categories) grounded to English Wikipedia for language-independent NER features.
- Contribution: Presents a practical, scalable pipeline requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, evaluated across nine languages with analyses of Wikipedia size, Freebase coverage, and multi-source training.
- Contribution: Integrates multilingual Wikipedia-derived gazetteers and wikifier features into a perceptron/BIO NER model, improving both cross-lingual transfer and monolingual performance.

- Strength: Shows clear, consistent cross-lingual F1 gains from wikifier features, especially in direct transfer and low-resource languages, using only Wikipedia.
- Strength: Provides careful ablation and controlled analyses (e.g., downsampling interlanguage links, removing Freebase) to identify drivers of success.
- Strength: Demonstrates modest but consistent benefits from multi-source transfer and pooling high-resource languages.
- Strength: Method is complementary/orthogonal to prior transfer and projection methods and compares favorably to several baselines.
- Strength: Implementation and featureization are practical and easily integrated into standard NER codebases, with monolingual gains across languages.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Freebase and interlanguage link coverage; portability to Wikidata or languages with sparse links is not demonstrated.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—combines existing components rather than proposing a new joint model or principled NIL handling.
- Weakness: Label remapping (TIME/TTL removal, MISC-to-LOC) may confound comparisons if baselines aren’t remapped identically.
- Weakness: Aggressive n-gram wikification likely introduces noise; NIL rates, disambiguation accuracy, and runtime costs are not quantified.
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to modern cross-lingual embedding-based or neural sequence transfer baselines.

- Question: Are baseline results for low-resource languages evaluated with the same tag remapping (TIME/TTL removal, MISC heuristics) to ensure fair comparison?
- Question: What is the computational overhead of wikifying all n-grams (docs/sec, candidates per token, % NIL links)?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the number of top candidates k (k=2); was k tuned and how does it affect precision/recall?
- Question: Can Freebase be replaced with Wikidata classes/properties, or how does performance change when only Wikipedia categories are available?
- Question: Do wikifier features help specific entity types more (PER vs ORG vs LOC); is there a per-type analysis?
- Question: How is tokenization handled for non-Latin scripts (BN/TA), does the wikifier rely on language-specific tokenizers, and how do segmentation errors affect features?

- Additional: Report precision/recall breakdowns and provide qualitative error analysis of wikifier-induced boundary/type errors.
- Additional: Consider combining wikifier features with cross-lingual clusters or embeddings to test orthogonality in a single model.
- Additional: Exploring joint or structured models for NER+EL could further improve results and address noisy links.
