- Proposes language-independent direct-transfer NER using cross-lingual wikification to map n-grams to English Wikipedia and extract Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as delexicalized features.

- Integrates wikifier-derived features with a perceptron BIO tagger, delexicalized base features, and Wikipedia-derived gazetteers.

- Evaluates on nine languages (CoNLL and low-resource), with monolingual/cross-lingual experiments, ablations on wikifier components, and multi-source training.

- Shows wikifier-derived features enable strong direct transfer without parallel data, outperforming prior direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL and competing on low-resource languages.

- Provides empirical analysis linking performance to Wikipedia size and Freebase coverage, and shows wikifier features also help monolingual NER and multi-source training.

- Adapts a cross-lingual wikifier for dense per-n-gram grounding and integrates it practically as NER features.

- Achieves large direct-transfer F1 gains (>10 F1 in some languages) over strong baselines, supporting the efficacy of wikification signals.

- Demonstrates robustness across diverse languages and scripts, improving results even with very small Wikipedias in several cases.

- Supplies informative ablations showing impact of removing Freebase/interlanguage-link signals and exploring multi-source effects.

- Requires only multilingual Wikipedia and a wikifier (no parallel corpora, POS taggers, or language-specific rules), making it appealing for new languages.

- Modifies candidate generation and ranking in a pragmatic way to support per-n-gram grounding.

- Conceptually incremental: Wikipedia-derived semantic types for NER are not novel; main novelty is cross-lingual dense wikification and direct transfer.

- Performance degrades with small Wikipedias and sparse interlanguage links, limiting applicability for the lowest-resource languages.

- Label preprocessing (e.g., remapping MISC→LOC, removing TIME/TTL) and gazetteer overlap may confound evaluation and bias results.

- Uses an older perceptron backbone with greedy decoding and lacks comparison to contemporary neural transfer methods or combined orthogonal signals.

- Does not report runtime, throughput, or memory costs for dense wikification, raising efficiency and scalability concerns.

- Lacks detailed pruning/confidence-thresholding strategies for wikifier features and sensitivity analysis to number of candidates.

- Missing details on tokenization/capitalization handling for non-Latin scripts and robustness across tokenizers.

- Needs quantification of label modifications in low-resource setups and impact of relabeling on per-class F1; a no-relabel baseline is requested.

- Questions about multi-source training balance (avoiding English domination) and whether fine-tuning on best source was tried.

- Suggests reporting precision/recall per entity type and significance tests to strengthen empirical claims.

- Recommends releasing adapted wikifier code, feature extraction scripts, and gazetteers for reproducibility.

- Proposes combining wikification with multilingual embeddings/clusters or projection methods as a likely avenue to improve results and better position the work.
