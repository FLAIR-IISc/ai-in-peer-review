- Proposes cross-lingual NER by wikifying every n-gram (up to 4) to English Wikipedia and using associated categories/FreeBase types as language-independent features.  
- Uses English-annotated training data and directly transfers the model to nine target languages without target-language training or parallel corpora.  
- Evaluated comprehensively on CoNLL (Spanish, Dutch, German) and five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), showing strong cross-lingual performance.  
- Wikifier-based features substantially improve direct-transfer baselines and also help in monolingual and multi-source training settings.  
- Method inverts the usual pipeline (wikification before NER) and applies features to all n-grams, providing rich signals for boundary and type detection.  
- Ablation study demonstrates method performance correlates with target-language Wikipedia size and inter-language link density.  
- Outperforms strong baselines including methods that rely on parallel corpora, highlighting practicality and resource efficiency.  
- Shows applicability to non-Latin-script languages, supporting true language-independence of the features.  
- Main dependency on an external cross-lingual wikifier (Tsai & Roth, 2016), treated as a black box without error-mode analysis.  
- Paper lacks analysis of how wikifier errors propagate to NER performance or whether systematic wikifier biases affect entity-type predictions.  
- Does not explain how the model handles conflicting or redundant signals from overlapping n-gram features (feature ambiguity/resolution).  
- No discussion of feature explosion or feature selection/regularization given wikification of all n-grams and use of an averaged perceptron.  
- Pre-processing of CoNLL MISC tags uses FreeBase signals to relabel MISC to LOC or remove MISC, which may confound training and evaluation with the proposed features.  
- Questions posed: request for wikifier performance details and sensitivity of NER F1 to wikifier accuracy.  
- Questions posed: request clarification on how conflicting type information from overlapping n-grams is resolved by the learning model.  
- Questions posed: request results when English MISC tags are mapped to O (or left unchanged) to assess impact of the heuristic reassignment.
