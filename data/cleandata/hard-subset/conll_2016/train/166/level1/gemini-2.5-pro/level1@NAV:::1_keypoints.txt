- Summary: Proposes a direct-transfer cross-lingual NER that wikifies n-grams to English Wikipedia and uses Wikipedia-derived types/categories as language-agnostic features, trained on high-resource languages and evaluated on nine target languages without target annotations.

- Main contribution 1: Introduces cross-lingual wikification as a novel way to produce universal semantic features for NER by grounding n-grams to English Wikipedia entries.

- Main contribution 2: Provides extensive empirical validation across nine languages, analyzing performance dependence on Wikipedia size and link density.

- Strength 1: Elegant and novel idea to ground n-grams in a universal KB to create delexicalized, language-independent entity features.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical gains in the direct-transfer setting, with large F1 improvements on CoNLL (e.g., +15 Dutch, +13 Spanish) and wins on several low-resource languages.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive analysis and ablations (Wikipedia size, inter-language links, multi-source training) that substantiate claims and clarify limitations.

- Weakness 1: Method heavily depends on target-language Wikipedia size and link quality, limiting applicability to truly low-resource languages.

- Weakness 2: Lacks error analysis of the cross-lingual wikifier, so the impact of wikification errors on NER performance is unclear.

- Weakness 3: Limited contextual comparison to other cross-lingual transfer paradigms (e.g., annotation projection using parallel corpora).

- Question to authors: How expensive is wikifying every n-gram up to length 4 at inference time—what is the latency and how does it scale relative to standard NER models?
