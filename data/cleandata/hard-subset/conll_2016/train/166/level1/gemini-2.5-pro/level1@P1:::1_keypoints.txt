- Proposes cross-lingual NER via a wikifier that links n-grams in any language to English Wikipedia and extracts language-independent features (categories, FreeBase types).  
- Integrates these wikifier features into a perceptron NER system for direct transfer from high-resource to zero-resource languages.  
- Evaluated on nine typologically diverse languages (including low-resource and non-Latin scripts) and shows significant gains over direct-transfer baselines and benefits for monolingual NER.  

- Contribution: introduces a novel approach of reversing the pipeline by using entity linking to generate semantic NER features anchored in English Wikipedia, avoiding parallel data.  
- Contribution: provides a comprehensive empirical study across many languages, analyzes effect of Wikipedia size, and shows gains from multi-source training.  

- Strength: high novelty and strong empirical impact, with large F1 improvements (e.g., +12 Dutch, +15 German, +5 Spanish) over strong baselines.  
- Strength: scalable and practical — only requires multilingual Wikipedia, no parallel corpora or native-speaker interaction, applicable to many low-resource languages.  
- Strength: thorough experiments and ablations, including simulated smaller Wikipedias and multi-source training analysis.  

- Weakness: heavy dependence on an external wikifier (Tsai & Roth 2016) treated as a black box, with no analysis of wikifier error propagation to NER.  
- Weakness: baseline handling of non-Latin scripts is unclear — English lexical features in base model unfairly penalize non-Latin languages; a delexicalized baseline is lacking.  
- Weakness: ad-hoc preprocessing and CoNLL tag mapping (removing/mapping MISC) may confound comparisons with prior work and affect task validity.  

- Question: please provide quantitative wikifier error analysis (recall of linking and precision of FreeBase types) on representative languages like German and Yoruba.  
- Question: how do results change if trained on the original 4-class CoNLL data (PER, LOC, ORG, MISC) and transferred to low-resource languages — does removing MISC alter conclusions?  
- Question: have you tried hybrid models for non-Latin languages combining wikifier features with language-agnostic base features (word shape, capitalization) to avoid overfitting to English lexical features?
