- Proposes cross-lingual NER by grounding mentions to English Wikipedia and using categories/FreeBase types as language-independent features.  
- Novel framing: wikification used as core feature engineering for cross-lingual transfer rather than pre/post-processing.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains, especially for low-resource languages, outperforming prior methods.  
- Extensive evaluation across resource-rich and extremely low-resource languages shows robustness and broad applicability.  
- Compares against strong baselines and includes multi-source training experiments to validate improvements.  
- Ablation studies (e.g., removing FreeBase links) and analysis of Wikipedia size effects provide useful insights into feature quality.  
- Main weakness: reliance on English Wikipedia may introduce bias and uneven coverage despite interlanguage links.  
- Base NER feature set is relatively limited (Ratinov & Roth, 2009); newer feature architectures might further improve results.  
- Scalability concern: wikifying all n-grams (up to n=4) could be computationally expensive on large datasets.  
- Open question: robustness to imperfect disambiguation and strategies to mitigate noisy wikification links.  
- Open question: handling of concepts present in Wikipedia but missing FreeBase entries for low-resource languages.  
- Open question: actual computational cost of wikification and potential optimizations for scalability.
