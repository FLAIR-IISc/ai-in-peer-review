- Novel method: uses cross-lingual wikification of all n-grams to generate language-independent features grounded in English Wikipedia and Freebase.
- Strong empirical performance: outperforms prior direct-transfer approaches on CoNLL (ES, DE, NL) and shows impressive results on low-resource languages (TR, TL, YO, BN, TA).
- Features improve monolingual NER and support multi-source transfer learning, showing broader applicability.
- Comprehensive evaluation: experiments include Wikipedia-size impact, multi-source setups, and ablations (FreeBase removal, interlanguage link reduction).
- Practical and scalable: relies only on multilingual Wikipedia, enabling processing of many languages without parallel corpora.
- Potential bias from English Wikipedia: reliance on English may introduce cultural/coverage biases not fully discussed.
- Wikification errors: disambiguation is imperfect and the effect of systematic wikification errors on performance is not quantified.
- Feature generality: standard (English-oriented) features may not be optimal for all languages and need further adaptation.
- Limited error analysis: more detailed breakdowns (by entity type, language-specific failures) are missing, especially for low-resource languages.
- Question on biases: request for elaboration on how English Wikipedia biases affect different languages.
- Question on mitigation: ask what strategies were or could be used to reduce wikification errors.
- Question on feature adaptation: ask whether language-specific feature tuning was tried and with what results.
- Question on errors: request a more detailed error analysis for low-resource languages.
- Paper clarity and organization: generally well-written and clearly organized.
- Useful resources: Table 2 effectively summarizes experimental setup and results.
- Related work: inclusion is comprehensive and provides good context.
- Computational cost: recommend discussing computational cost and scalability of the wikification step for very large datasets.
