- Primary contribution: Proposes grounding mentions to English Wikipedia and using categories/FreeBase types as language-independent features for cross-lingual NER.  
- Enables training a single model on resource-rich languages and transferring to low-resource languages via wikification-based features.  
- Secondary contribution: Extensive evaluation across CoNLL datasets and multiple low-resource languages demonstrating strong performance and generalizability.  
- Additional analysis: Studies impact of Wikipedia size on feature quality and benefits of multi-source training.  
- Strength — Novelty: Introduces cross-lingual wikification as a novel, effective alternative to parallel corpora or language-specific heuristics for NER.  
- Strength — Empirical results: Shows consistent state-of-the-art or strong gains on standard and low-resource NER benchmarks.  
- Strength — Comprehensive evaluation: Includes ablations (FreeBase, interlanguage links) and experiments with different training language combinations.  
- Strength — Practicality: Relies only on multilingual Wikipedia, making the approach broadly applicable without expensive resources.  
- Weakness — Reliance on English Wikipedia: Performance is dependent on English Wikipedia coverage and may degrade for poorly represented languages.  
- Weakness — Feature complexity: Uses a fairly complex set of wikifier and gazetteer features, which may hinder reproducibility and adaptation.  
- Weakness — Limited error analysis: Lacks detailed qualitative/error-type analysis to pinpoint common failure modes.  
- Weakness — Baseline comparisons: Some comparisons omit stronger recent multilingual pretrained models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), limiting claims of superiority.  
- Question: How sensitive is NER performance to wikifier quality and wikification errors?  
- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed error analysis with examples and mitigation strategies?  
- Question: Have the authors considered augmenting features with target-language Wikipedia content to mitigate English coverage gaps?  
- Question: How does this approach compare empirically to modern multilingual pre-trained models fine-tuned for NER?  
- Question: What is the computational cost of generating wikifier features for large corpora or long documents?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Additional comment: Figures and tables are informative and references are comprehensive and relevant.  
- Additional comment: Authors should release code and data to facilitate reproducibility.
