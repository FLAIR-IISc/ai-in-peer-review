- Core idea: ground words in English Wikipedia via cross-lingual wikification to create language-independent features for NER.
- Novel technique: use wikification disambiguation results, Wikipedia categories, and FreeBase types as direct NER features rather than just preprocessing.
- Strong performance: achieves state-of-the-art results on several low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) without parallel data.
- Feature transferability: language-independent features improve cross-lingual transfer, monolingual models, and benefit from multi-source training.
- Novelty and insight: reframes wikification as a feature generator for NER in a novel and productive way.
- Empirical strength: consistent, strong results across diverse languages with supportive comparisons to prior work.
- Comprehensive experiments: thorough ablations (e.g., FreeBase removal, interlanguage link variations) and multi-source analysis.
- Clear presentation: well-written, well-organized paper with effective figures and detailed experimental descriptions.
- Practicality and scalability: method depends only on multilingual Wikipedia, making it broadly applicable without expensive resources.
- Dependence on wikifier quality: performance limited by accuracy/coverage of the wikifier; paper lacks deep analysis of wikification errors.
- Limited feature set: uses simple features (base features, gazetteers, wikifier); could benefit from character-level or contextualized embeddings.
- Limited error analysis: lacks detailed examination of failure cases (e.g., ambiguous entities, rare types).
- Freebase dependency: reliance on the deprecated FreeBase is a longer-term concern despite some robustness shown.
- Request for wikifier error details: need more information on error types and which languages/entity types suffer most.
- Question about richer representations: whether character-level embeddings or contextualized models (e.g., BERT) were tried and results.
- Labeling scheme rationale: ask why BIO was chosen and whether alternatives were considered.
- Bias concern: grounding in English Wikipedia may introduce cultural/encyclopedic biases affecting performance across languages.
- Related work: discussion is thorough and well-placed.
- Low-resource focus: strong emphasis on and contribution to low-resource language NER.
- Reproducibility: providing code/data would greatly enhance reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
