## Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

This paper presents a compelling approach to cross-lingual NER leveraging cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea – grounding mentions in all languages to English Wikipedia and utilizing the associated categories and FreeBase types – is innovative and demonstrably effective. The paper convincingly demonstrates strong performance gains, particularly on low-resource languages, surpassing existing methods.

The primary contribution lies in the successful application of cross-lingual wikification features for direct transfer learning in NER.  While wikification and entity linking have been explored before, this work uniquely positions wikification *as* the feature engineering step for cross-lingual transfer, rather than a pre- or post-processing stage. A second, significant contribution is the comprehensive experimental evaluation. The authors test their model across a diverse set of languages, including both resource-rich (Spanish, German, Dutch) and extremely low-resource (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) languages, showcasing robustness and broad applicability.  Finally, the insightful analysis of Wikipedia size's impact on feature quality and the benefit of multi-source training add valuable understanding.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The model consistently outperforms comparable approaches on both CoNLL datasets and low-resource languages, providing strong evidence of its effectiveness. The gains on low-resource languages are particularly noteworthy.
2. **Novel Application of Wikification:** Framing wikification as a core feature engineering technique for cross-lingual NER is a clever and effective approach. This bypasses the need for parallel corpora or language-specific expertise.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The extensive experimental setup, encompassing multiple languages and comparison against strong baselines (including Täckström’s work and Zhang et al.), strengthens the validity of the results.
4. **Detailed Analysis:** The ablation studies (e.g., removing FreeBase links) and the investigation of multi-source training provide valuable insights into the method's strengths and limitations. 

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Dependency on English Wikipedia:** The method's reliance on English Wikipedia as the grounding point introduces a potential bias. While mitigated by the use of interlanguage links, the quality of these links and the completeness of English Wikipedia coverage may still impact performance.
2. **Limited Feature Set:** The features used in the base NER model (Ratinov & Roth, 2009) appear somewhat limited given recent advancements in NER. Exploring more sophisticated features alongside wikification features could further enhance performance.
3. **Scalability Concerns:** While the paper demonstrates the method’s efficacy, the computational cost of wikifying every n-gram (up to n=4) might become a bottleneck for very large datasets.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the impact of imperfect disambiguation during wikification? How robust is the model to incorrect links, and are there specific strategies to mitigate the negative effects of noisy links?
2.  You mention that FreeBase does not contain entities for some low-resource languages. How does the model handle cases where a concept exists in Wikipedia but lacks a corresponding FreeBase entry?
3.  Regarding scalability, what is the computational cost of the wikification process, and have you explored any optimizations to reduce this cost?
