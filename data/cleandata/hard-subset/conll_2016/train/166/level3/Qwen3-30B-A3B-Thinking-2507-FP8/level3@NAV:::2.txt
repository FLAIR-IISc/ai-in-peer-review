

This paper proposes a cross-lingual NER approach using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The main contributions are: (1) demonstrating that wikifier features from English Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types serve as effective language-independent features for cross-lingual NER, (2) showing this approach works well across diverse languages including low-resource ones with non-Latin scripts, and (3) establishing that training on multiple source languages further improves results.

Strengths: The paper provides a coherent and convincing empirical evaluation across nine languages, including five low-resource languages with non-Latin scripts. The analysis of how Wikipedia size impacts performance is insightful, particularly for non-Latin script languages where wikifier features outperform lexical features. The demonstration that wikifier features remain effective even when lexical features are absent (for non-Latin scripts) is particularly valuable.

Weaknesses: The related-work section lacks sufficient comparison with Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and fails to clearly distinguish from other Wikipedia-based NER systems. The paper should include POS-tag features to empirically assess their utility across languages, as suggested by Tackström et al. The claim that approaches from Tackström and Nothman are orthogonal requires support through combined-model results. The paper also fails to compare with strong projection baselines that operate without parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009), making it difficult to contextualize the approach's advantages. Additionally, Table 4 lacks statistical significance tests, and the explanation for surprising language-pair results (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish) is insufficient.

Questions to Authors: Could you provide combined-model results for Tackström and your approach to validate the orthogonality claim? How would your approach compare to projection methods using bilingual semantic spaces (e.g., Vulić & Moens 2013) without parallel data? Please clarify why Dutch performs best for Spanish in your experiments.
