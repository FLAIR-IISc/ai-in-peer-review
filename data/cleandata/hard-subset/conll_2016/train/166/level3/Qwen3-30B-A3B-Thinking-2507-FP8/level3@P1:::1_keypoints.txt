- Primary contribution: introduce cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features (FreeBase types, Wikipedia categories) for NER.  
- Contribution: show grounding to English Wikipedia significantly improves cross-lingual NER performance.  
- Contribution: thorough empirical evaluation across 9 languages, outperforming state-of-the-art in direct-transfer without target annotations.  
- Contribution: analysis of Wikipedia size effects, showing smaller Wikipedias yield weaker but still useful features.  
- Strength: effective use of Wikipedia structure to create truly language-independent features, robust even with imperfect disambiguation.  
- Strength: comprehensive multilingual evaluation including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages (e.g., Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Strength: strong results for low-resource languages without relying on parallel text or native speakers.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of how Wikipedia size impacts feature quality (Table 3).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of how entities absent from Wikipedia affect model performance.  
- Weakness: lack of quantitative analysis of error propagation from wikifier to NER predictions.  
- Weakness: inadequate explanation and analysis for poor performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on handling non-Latin scripts and languages lacking FreeBase support (Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Question: how does the model handle entities not present in Wikipedia and what is the impact on NER performance?  
- Question: can the authors provide a detailed error analysis correlating wikifier errors with NER errors?  
- Question: what specific modifications are proposed to improve Tamil and Bengali performance when lexical features are included?  
- Question: how does the system handle wikifier failures to find entries (e.g., new entities), and what fallback features are used?  
- Overall: valuable and compelling idea with strong experiments for low-resource settings; addressing the noted analyses and clarifications would strengthen the paper.
