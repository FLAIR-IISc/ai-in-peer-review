- Proposes a language-independent NER approach using cross-lingual wikification as feature extraction.
- Introduces a novel feature set derived from Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types for language-independent representations.
- Demonstrates significant performance gains in monolingual and direct-transfer NER across many languages, including low-resource non-Latin scripts.
- Analyzes the relationship between Wikipedia size and feature quality, showing effectiveness even with limited Wikipedia resources.
- Novel feature engineering outperforms standard lexical features in cross-lingual settings, especially for non-Latin scripts.
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation across nine languages with comparisons to multiple baselines.
- Practical implementation requires only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, needing no language-specific modifications.
- Shows wikifier features also improve monolingual NER, increasing the method’s applicability.
- Method’s effectiveness is limited by Wikipedia coverage and may miss entities absent from Wikipedia.
- Lacks analysis of how wikifier errors propagate to NER errors, leaving robustness to wikifier inaccuracies unclear.
- Adding lexical features degrades performance on Tamil and Bengali, indicating over-reliance on lexical features without explored regularization.
- Asks how entities not in Wikipedia are handled and what the estimated Wikipedia entity coverage is for low-resource languages.
- Requests error analysis correlating wikifier errors with NER prediction errors and quantification of NER errors due to wikifier inaccuracies.
- Asks which regularization, feature-weighting, or feature-selection strategies could prevent over-reliance on lexical features across languages.
