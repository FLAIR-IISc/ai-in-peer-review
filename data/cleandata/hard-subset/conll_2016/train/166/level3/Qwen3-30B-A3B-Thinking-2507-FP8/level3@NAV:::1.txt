

This paper proposes a novel approach to cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification as a language-independent feature source. The main contributions, as I see them, are: (1) the development of a language-agnostic feature set derived from grounding target-language phrases to English Wikipedia via cross-lingual wikification, (2) demonstrating that these features significantly improve performance in both monolingual and direct-transfer settings across diverse languages, and (3) providing comprehensive evaluation that includes thorough analysis of low-resource and non-Latin languages.

The paper's strengths are substantial. First, the insight that Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types can serve as effective language-independent features for NER is both novel and well-validated through extensive experiments. Second, the evaluation across nine languages—including Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil—provides compelling evidence for the approach's generalizability, especially for low-resource languages where previous methods have struggled. Third, the detailed analysis of how Wikipedia size affects performance offers valuable practical insights for future work.

The primary weaknesses concern methodological limitations. First, the paper fails to address how entities not present in Wikipedia (which is particularly problematic for low-resource languages) are handled, potentially limiting the approach's applicability. Second, the strong dependence on the cross-lingual Wikifier is not adequately evaluated—specifically, how often Wikifier errors propagate to NER prediction errors remains unclear. Third, the paper observes poor direct-transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, yet offers no mechanism to prevent over-reliance on these features, suggesting a methodological flaw.

I would like to ask the authors to clarify: (1) how they handle entities not present in Wikipedia, particularly for low-resource languages; (2) whether they conducted error analysis to quantify the impact of Wikifier errors on final NER predictions; and (3) whether they considered regularization techniques to balance feature classes and prevent over-reliance on lexical features. The paper makes a valuable contribution to cross-lingual NER, but addressing these concerns would strengthen the work significantly.
