- Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to generate language-independent features by grounding phrases to English Wikipedia entries and using categories/FreeBase types.

- Shows that wikifier features enable direct transfer NER (train on English, test on other languages) and improve monolingual NER across 9 languages, including low-resource ones.

- Contribution: demonstrates cross-lingual wikification yields effective language-independent NER features without parallel data.

- Contribution: provides comprehensive evaluation across 9 languages with consistent improvements over strong baselines.

- Contribution: shows wikifier features are especially valuable for non-Latin script languages where lexical features fail.

- Strength: strong empirical evidence of effectiveness across multiple languages, notably Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil.

- Strength: method is simple and broadly applicable, requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps and no language-specific resources.

- Strength: analysis offers insight into why wikifier features help more for some languages and scripts.

- Weakness: related work section insufficiently compares to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and other Wikipedia-based NER systems, lacking clarity on differences in wikification and ambiguity handling.

- Weakness: omits POS-tag features in experiments despite their claimed importance for direct transfer, weakening comparisons to prior work.

- Weakness: claims of orthogonality with Tackström et al. and Nothman et al. lack empirical validation; combined-model results are needed.

- Weakness: lacks comparison with strong projection baselines that don't require parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013) using bilingual semantic spaces.

- Weakness: reported improvements (Table 4) are small/inconsistent and lack statistical significance testing (p-values).

- Question: request for preliminary combined-model results to validate orthogonality with Tackström et al. and Nothman et al.

- Question: ask for analysis explaining surprising language-pair transfer patterns (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba).

- Question: request inclusion of comparisons with bilingual semantic-space projection methods to better contextualize the approach.

- Overall: coherent story with convincing experiments, but paper would be stronger by addressing the above weaknesses and comparisons.
