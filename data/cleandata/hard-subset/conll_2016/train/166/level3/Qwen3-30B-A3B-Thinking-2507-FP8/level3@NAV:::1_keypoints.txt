- Proposes language-agnostic features for cross-lingual NER by grounding target-language phrases to English Wikipedia via cross-lingual wikification.
- Shows these wikification-derived features significantly improve monolingual and direct-transfer NER performance across diverse languages.
- Provides comprehensive evaluation including low-resource and non-Latin languages and analysis of how Wikipedia size affects performance.
- Novel insight that Wikipedia categories and Freebase types serve as effective language-independent NER features, validated by extensive experiments.
- Evaluation across nine languages (e.g., Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) demonstrates strong generalizability, especially for low-resource languages.
- Detailed analysis linking Wikipedia size to system performance offers practical guidance for future work.
- Does not address handling of entities absent from Wikipedia, limiting applicability in low-resource settings.
- Over-reliance on the cross-lingual Wikifier is not adequately evaluated; unclear how Wikifier errors propagate to NER errors.
- Observes poor direct-transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, with no mechanism proposed to prevent feature over-reliance.
- Requests clarification on how out-of-Wikipedia entities are handled, particularly for low-resource languages.
- Asks whether an error analysis was performed to quantify the impact of Wikifier mistakes on final NER predictions.
- Suggests the authors consider regularization or other techniques to balance feature classes and prevent over-reliance on lexical features.
