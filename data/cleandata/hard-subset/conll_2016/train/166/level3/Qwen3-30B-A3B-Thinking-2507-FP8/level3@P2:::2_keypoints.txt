Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to map words to English Wikipedia (FreeBase types/categories) as language-independent features.  
Validates the approach across 9 languages, including low-resource non-Latin scripts.  
Shows the wikification features also improve monolingual NER, not just transfer settings.  
Comprehensive evaluation across diverse languages with significant gains, notably for languages where lexical features fail (e.g., Bengali, Tamil).  
Simple, practical method requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps—no parallel data or language-specific resources.  
Good analysis of Wikipedia size effects and ablation studies showing the importance of FreeBase queries and interlanguage links.  
Incremental contribution with limited methodological novelty and insufficient distinction from prior Wikipedia-based NER/wikification work.  
Omits comparison to standard POS-tag features (Täckström et al., 2012), leaving unclear how the new features interact with established ones.  
Multi-source training improvements are small (+1.2 F1 average), inconsistent across languages, and lack statistical significance testing.  
Can you show that combining these features with projection-based transfer methods (e.g., Zhang et al., 2016) yields extra gains?  
Why does Spanish serve as the best source language for Yoruba while English is best for most others—please explain linguistically.  
Why were POS-tag features not included in experiments, and would their inclusion alter the results?  
Clarify what "typically" means for multi-source training improvements and identify specific language pairs with substantial gains.
