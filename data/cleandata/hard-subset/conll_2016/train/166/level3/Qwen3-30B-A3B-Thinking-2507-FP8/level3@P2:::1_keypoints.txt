- Language-independent cross-lingual NER using wikification: map target-language phrases to English Wikipedia and use categories/Freebase types as features.
- Method requires only multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel text or language-specific resources), enabling NER for languages with Wikipedia coverage.
- Empirical validation across 9 languages shows significant improvements in monolingual and cross-lingual transfer, including low-resource non-Latin-script languages.
- Wikifier feature quality correlates with Wikipedia size and script, giving stronger results for languages with larger Wikipedias.
- Strength: genuinely language-independent approach that enables NER without annotated training data.
- Strength: thorough evaluation across 9 languages and multiple settings with detailed analysis by Wikipedia size and script.
- Strength: proposed features improve both monolingual and cross-lingual systems, with 4.3–5.2 point gains over a strong baseline and superior low-resource results.
- Weakness: no clear handling strategy for entities absent from Wikipedia (NIL), which can hurt performance in low-resource languages.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of dependence on wikifier accuracy and how wikifier errors translate into NER errors.
- Weakness: unexplained degradation when lexical features are included for Bengali and Tamil; lack of investigation into non-Latin script issues.
- Weakness: evaluation omits comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods beyond older baselines.
- Weakness: limited analysis of why wikifier features help some languages more than others beyond simple Wikipedia-size correlation.
- Question: How do wikifier disambiguation errors specifically affect NER predictions—request for error analysis correlating wikifier and NER errors?
- Question: How are entities not present in Wikipedia handled, especially in low-resource languages, and what is the impact on performance?
- Question: Why do lexical features harm performance on Bengali and Tamil, and what solutions can improve robustness to non-Latin scripts?
