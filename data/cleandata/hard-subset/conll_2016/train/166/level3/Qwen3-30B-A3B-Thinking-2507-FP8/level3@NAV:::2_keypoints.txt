- Proposal: cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features.  
- Contribution: wikifier features from English Wikipedia categories and Freebase types are effective language-independent features.  
- Contribution: approach works well across diverse languages, including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.  
- Contribution: training on multiple source languages further improves results.  
- Strength: coherent, convincing empirical evaluation across nine languages, including five low-resource non-Latin scripts.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of how Wikipedia size impacts performance.  
- Strength: wikifier features outperform lexical features for non-Latin scripts and remain useful when lexical features are absent.  
- Weakness: related-work section missing comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and insufficient distinction from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Weakness: paper omits POS-tag features—should evaluate their utility across languages as suggested by Tackström et al.  
- Weakness: orthogonality claim with Tackström and Nothman lacks support from combined-model results.  
- Weakness: no comparison with strong projection baselines that work without parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009).  
- Weakness: Table 4 lacks statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: inadequate explanation for surprising language-pair results (e.g., Dutch yielding best results for Spanish).  
- Question: provide combined-model results for Tackström + this approach to validate orthogonality.  
- Question: how does the approach compare to projection methods using bilingual semantic spaces (e.g., Vulić & Moens 2013) without parallel data?  
- Question: clarify why Dutch performs best for Spanish in the experiments.
