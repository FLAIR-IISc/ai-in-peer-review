- Primary contribution: Shows wikifier-derived Freebase types and Wikipedia categories are robust, language-agnostic features enabling direct transfer from English to 9 languages without parallel data (strong empirical gains, e.g., +12.1 F1 on German).  
- Secondary contribution: Demonstrates these wikifier features also improve monolingual NER systems across all languages (up to +4.7 F1).  
- Tertiary contribution: Shows training on multiple source languages (e.g., English+Spanish) further boosts performance, particularly for low-resource targets like Yoruba.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation is comprehensive across 9 languages including low-resource ones and the model consistently outperforms strong baselines (e.g., +12.1 German, +14.3 Turkish).  
- Strength: Monolingual enhancement results are significant, with wikifier features yielding +4.7–5.2 F1 improvements across languages.  
- Strength: Method is practical and low-resource, requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps and no parallel data or language-specific heuristics.  
- Weakness: Related work section insufficiently distinguishes from Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and lacks empirical comparison, weakening the novelty claim.  
- Weakness: Omission of POS features is problematic; authors should report results using universal POS tags since POS is known to help direct transfer.  
- Weakness: Claim of being "orthogonal" to Täckström’s clustering method lacks supporting evidence; no combined-method experiments are provided.  
- Weakness: Reported marginal gains in Table 4 (e.g., +0.5 F1) lack statistical significance testing, so improvement claims are unsubstantiated.  
- Weakness: Section 3.3 model modifications (e.g., n-gram processing to avoid over-linking) are unclear and need a running example to clarify behavior.  
- Question: How does the model’s performance compare to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) on English and German?  
- Question: Can you provide results using universal POS tags to assess their impact on cross-lingual transfer?  
- Question: What are the statistical significance (p-values) for the improvements reported in Table 4?  
- Question: Can you explain the surprising language-pair results (e.g., Spanish best for Yoruba, Dutch best for Spanish) with linguistic or Wikipedia-size analysis?  
- Question: Please clarify the Section 3.3 model modification with a concrete example (e.g., processing "Albrecht Lehmann" in German).
