- Proposes language-independent cross-lingual NER features by wikifying n-grams to English Wikipedia titles and using Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as delexicalized features.
- Primary contribution: shows cross-lingual wikification yields effective, language-independent features that substantially improve NER in monolingual and direct-transfer settings.
- Secondary contribution: presents a broad empirical study across nine languages (including low-resource and non-Latin scripts) with careful ablations and analyses of Wikipedia size and interlanguage links.
- Tertiary contribution: provides practical guidance on when to rely on wikifier features versus lexical/gazetteer features and shows wikifier features also help monolingual NER.
- Strength: clear, impactful idea with strong empirical support; wikifier features alone and combined give consistent gains, sometimes >10 F1 in transfer.
- Strength: broad and rigorous evaluation beyond CoNLL, favorable comparisons to strong prior work while using fewer resources and no parallel text.
- Strength: insightful coverage and failure-mode analysis via pruning and FreeBase removal experiments that explain weaker gains on low-resource languages.
- Strength: practical and general pipeline requiring only multilingual Wikipedia, with modifications to wikify all n-grams making it a usable feature extractor.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on wikifier coverage and FreeBase, with degraded performance for languages/entities with sparse interlanguage links or missing pages.
- Weakness: limited analysis of error propagation from wikifier mistakes (wrong title/type or NIL) to NER boundary/type errors and no quantification of linker calibration effects.
- Weakness: over-reliance on lexical features harms transfer for non-Latin scripts; paper notes this but does not test remedies like group-wise regularization or feature dropout.
- Weakness: some evaluation design choices (e.g., relabeling MISC to LOC) may confound comparability and lack statistical significance testing.
- Question: report per-language coverage—what fraction of tokens/spans receive wikifier-derived features and how often do NILs occur, broken down by PER/LOC/ORG?
- Question: quantify error attribution—how many NER errors stem from wrong links, missing links, or misleading types/categories, and provide confusion matrices with/without wikifier features?
- Question: how does the system handle entities absent from Wikipedia—do gazetteer or contextual signals compensate and by how much?
- Question: did you try feature-group balancing methods (group lasso, reweighting, dropout) to reduce lexical feature dominance in transfer, and any results?
- Question: can the pipeline switch from FreeBase to Wikidata for types, and would that improve performance in low-resource languages?
- Additional comment: report per-class (PER/LOC/ORG) F1 for monolingual and transfer settings to show where wikifier features help most.
- Additional comment: try a simple feature-group reweighting or dropout schedule to improve transfer to non-Latin scripts without hurting Latin-script languages.
- Additional comment: include runtime and memory costs for wikifying all n-grams and feature extraction to assess scalability.
- Additional comment: clarify multi-source training mixture (balanced vs proportional) and whether sample weighting by Wikipedia size or target similarity helps.
- Additional comment: releasing feature extraction code and language-specific gazetteers would aid reproducibility and adoption.
- Overall: paper is clear, convincing, and practically useful; addressing coverage/error analyses and simple feature-group balancing would further strengthen the work.
