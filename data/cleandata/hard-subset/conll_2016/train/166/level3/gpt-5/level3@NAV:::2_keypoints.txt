- Primary contribution: Empirical study showing cross-lingual wikification yields strong, language-independent features that improve direct-transfer NER across nine languages.  
- Secondary contribution: Practical modifications to a wikifier to densely link n-grams and add Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as delexicalized NER features.  
- Tertiary contribution: Broad evaluation on CoNLL plus five low-resource languages and analyses linking performance to Wikipedia size and multi-source training.  

- Strength — empirical effectiveness: Wikifier features give sizable direct-transfer gains, outperforming prior baselines on CoNLL and low-resource languages with fewer resources.  
- Strength — generality and simplicity: Method needs only multilingual Wikipedia, integrates with standard NER models, and also helps monolingual NER.  
- Strength — diagnostic analyses: Useful analyses relate performance to English intersection size and include ablations removing Freebase/interlanguage links.  
- Strength — multi-source training: Adding annotated data from multiple languages is generally safe and often beneficial.  

- Weakness — limited novelty: Contribution is incremental since Wikipedia-derived NER features are known and technical edits are modest.  
- Weakness — related work gaps: Insufficient comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and unclear positioning vs. other Wikipedia-based NER systems (e.g., Nothman et al.).  
- Weakness — missing baselines/ablations: No POS-tag feature experiments and no strong projection baselines (including semantic-space projection without parallel data).  
- Weakness — unclear methodology: Section 3.3 (edits to Tsai & Roth) is hard to follow and needs a running example to clarify candidate generation/ranking.  
- Weakness — orthogonality claims untested: No combined-model results with Täckström-style clusters or Nothman silver data to support orthogonality.  
- Weakness — analysis and reporting issues: Table 4 improvements are small/inconsistent, statistical significance not reported, and surprising training–test language pair findings are unexplained.  
- Weakness — presentation problems: Several typos and awkward sentences; cited examples need correction.  

- Request: Add POS-tag feature experiments using universal tags and report impacts across languages.  
- Request: Provide combined results with (i) Täckström et al. clusters and (ii) Nothman-style silver data to test orthogonality.  
- Request: Include strong projection baselines, including semantic-space projection without parallel data, and clarify positioning versus those methods.  
- Request: Clarify Section 3.3 with a step-by-step running example of the modified candidate generation/ranking.  
- Request: Explain anomalous cross-language training results (e.g., Dutch→Spanish; Spanish→Yoruba).  
- Request: Report statistical significance (e.g., bootstrap or AR) for Table 4 and other key comparisons.  
- Request: Proofread and fix typos and incorrect cited examples.
