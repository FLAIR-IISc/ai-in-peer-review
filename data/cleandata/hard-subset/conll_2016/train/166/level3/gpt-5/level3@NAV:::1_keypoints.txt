- Proposes a language-independent NER approach mapping target-language n-grams to English Wikipedia via a cross-lingual wikifier to derive delexicalized features (Wikipedia categories + Freebase types).  
- Shows these Wikipedia-derived features improve both monolingual NER and direct transfer (English→target) across CoNLL and several low-resource/non-Latin languages.  
- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that wikifier-derived, language-agnostic features yield large gains and outperform strong direct-transfer baselines without target annotation.  
- Contribution: Practical, broadly applicable featureization pipeline requiring only multilingual Wikipedia, with modifications to wikify n-grams and expose ranker confidence as features.  
- Contribution: Careful analysis across resource levels and scripts, including ablations on Wikipedia size, Freebase availability, and multi-source training effects.  
- Strength: Strong cross-lingual gains and competitive monolingual improvements, including for languages with minimal lexical overlap (e.g., Bengali/Tamil).  
- Strength: Minimal resource requirements (only multilingual Wikipedia), avoiding language-specific heuristics or parallel corpora.  
- Strength: Thorough experimentation and informative ablations explaining performance variations (e.g., Latin vs. non-Latin scripts).  
- Strength: Demonstrates small but consistent gains from multi-source training, indicating robustness and extensibility.  
- Weakness: Coverage gaps for entities absent from Wikipedia/Freebase are not quantified, leaving NIL impact on recall and error modes unclear.  
- Weakness: Heavy dependency on the cross-lingual wikifier without analysis of wikifier error propagation or confidence calibration effects on NER.  
- Weakness: Feature-group imbalance—lexical/gazetteer features can hurt direct transfer in Tamil/Bengali, with no principled regularization explored.  
- Weakness: Reliance on deprecated Freebase and English-centric categories raises sustainability and cross-cultural bias concerns; alternatives not evaluated.  
- Question: What proportion of mentions in each dataset are NIL, and how does performance differ on NIL vs. linked mentions?  
- Question: What is the correlation between wikifier top-1 accuracy/confidence and NER precision/recall, and how much error propagates?  
- Question: Have you tried feature-group regularization (e.g., separate L2, orthogonalization, group dropout, confidence-weighted training) to prevent lexical over-reliance?  
- Question: Could Wikidata replace Freebase for typing, and what impact would that have?
