- Paper proposes direct-transfer cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification (FreeBase types + Wikipedia categories) for every n-gram, requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump.

- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that wikifier-derived semantic features are strong, language-agnostic signals that substantially improve direct-transfer NER across 9 languages.

- Contribution: a simple, scalable direct-transfer NER architecture that needs no parallel text, taggers, or language-specific heuristics; multi-source training typically helps.

- Contribution: broad experimental study including CoNLL and five low-resource languages, analyses linking performance to Wikipedia size/coverage, and evidence these features also help monolingual NER.

- Overall: method is incremental rather than novel, but coherent and supported by convincing empirical results.

- Strength: strong cross-lingual performance with minimal resources, outperforming several baselines and prior work on many languages.

- Strength: clear evidence that wikifier features are language-agnostic and complementary to other features, with interlanguage-link coverage explaining weaker gains on very small Wikipedias.

- Strength: practical and scalable pipeline requiring only Wikipedia dumps and a cross-lingual wikifier, with a conceptually simple n-gram wikification modification.

- Strength: broad evaluation and ablations across languages, settings, and analyses (e.g., script effects, Wikipedia size) that give useful practitioner guidance.

- Strength: multi-source transfer generally helps and rarely harms, supporting feature transferability.

- Weakness: missing key baselines and combinations (e.g., wikifier + multilingual clusters, + silver Wikipedia training), and no comparison with projection methods using bilingual semantic spaces.

- Weakness: Section 3.3 lacks methodological clarity — the “wikify all n-grams” modifications are tersely described and need a running example.

- Weakness: limited assessment of POS-tag features — prior POS experiments are not reported and fair comparisons to Täckström et al. are missing.

- Weakness: analysis gaps and weak statistical support — surprising cross-language results lack deeper analysis and multi-source gains need significance testing.

- Weakness: related-work positioning is thin; distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER and non-parallel projection methods should be clearer.

- Question: report experiments adding universal POS tags across languages — do results change for Latin vs. non-Latin scripts?

- Question: provide preliminary results combining wikifier features with (a) multilingual word clusters and (b) silver Wikipedia training on a subset of languages.

- Question: include a strong projection-based baseline that does not require sentence-aligned data (e.g., bilingual semantic space methods) for better contextualization.

- Question: expand Section 3.3 with a running example showing candidate generation, ranking, top-k selection, and token-level aggregation; report sensitivity to k.

- Question: provide feature ablations quantifying contributions of FreeBase types vs. Wikipedia categories vs. ranker confidence, especially for low-resource languages.

- Question: analyze anomalous language-pair results (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) with category overlap, topical distributions, gazetteer coverage, or annotation differences.

- Question: provide significance tests (e.g., bootstrap) and variance over multiple runs for key comparisons (Tables 2 and 4).

- Question: report computational cost of wikifying all n-grams (n ≤ 4) and give heuristics to prune candidates if needed.

- Additional comment: proofread the paper, fix typos and ambiguous figure/table references, correct the German sentence example, and ensure consistency of tag mappings and evaluation implications.
