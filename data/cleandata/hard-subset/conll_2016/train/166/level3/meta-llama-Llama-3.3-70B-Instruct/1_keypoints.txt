- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER using a wikifier that maps mentions to English Wikipedia, requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump.  
- Contribution: a language-independent NER model using cross-lingual wikification that achieves state-of-the-art mono- and cross-lingual results.  
- Contribution: introduces truly delexicalized wikifier features that enable direct model transfer across languages.  
- Contribution: demonstrates effectiveness across many languages, including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.  
- Strength: Significantly improves over strong baselines in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.  
- Strength: Wikifier features provide a novel and effective mechanism for cross-language transfer.  
- Strength: Analysis of wikifier feature quality and dependence on Wikipedia size yields valuable performance insights.  
- Weakness: Overall performance depends on the accuracy of the cross-lingual wikifier, which can introduce prediction errors.  
- Weakness: Approach may not handle entities that are not present in Wikipedia.  
- Weakness: Does not integrate or evaluate other multilingual NER techniques (e.g., parallel projection, automatic Wikipedia-based training data).  
- Question: How will the authors address handling entities missing from Wikipedia?  
- Question: Please provide more details and accuracy metrics for the cross-lingual wikifier used.  
- Question: How do the authors plan to incorporate other multilingual NER techniques into their approach?
