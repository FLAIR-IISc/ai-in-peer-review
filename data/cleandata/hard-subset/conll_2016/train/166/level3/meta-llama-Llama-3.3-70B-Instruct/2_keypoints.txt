- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model using a cross-lingual wikifier mapping mentions to English Wikipedia.  
- Trains on annotation in one or multiple source languages and applies to all Wikipedia languages.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across many languages, including low-resource languages, with significant gains over strong baselines.  
- Analyzes the quality of wikifier-derived features and their dependence on Wikipedia size.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on several languages, including low-resource cases.  
- Uses a novel, effective cross-lingual wikifier to obtain language-independent NER features.  
- Provides valuable analysis linking Wikipedia size to feature usefulness for cross-lingual NER.  
- Related work section is insufficiently detailed and lacks clear distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Does not report experiments with POS tags as features to assess their utility across languages.  
- Section 3.3 would benefit from a running example to clarify the modified Tsai and Roth model.  
- Results in Table 4 are not fully convincing and need statistical significance testing.  
- Paper requires more careful proofreading to fix typos and improve clarity.  
- How will the authors address Wikipedia size effects on wikifier feature quality for low-resource languages?  
- Please provide more details on the cross-lingual wikifier design and training procedure.  
- How do the authors plan to integrate other multilingual NER techniques (parallel projection, automatic Wikipedia-derived training data) into their model?
