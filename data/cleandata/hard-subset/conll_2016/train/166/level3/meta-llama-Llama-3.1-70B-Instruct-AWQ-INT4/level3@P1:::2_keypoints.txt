- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER method that uses an English Wikipedia-based wikifier to disambiguate non-English text and generate transferable features.  
- Introduces cross-lingual wikification enabling direct-transfer NER without parallel data or language-specific features.  
- Shows that wikifier-generated language-independent features are effective for NER even without language-specific inputs.  
- Evaluates the approach across many languages, including low-resource ones, demonstrating its broad applicability.  
- Effectively leverages cross-lingual wikification to produce transferable NER features.  
- Reports significant improvements over strong baselines (e.g., Tackström et al. 2012).  
- Evaluates on a wide range of languages, highlighting potential for low-resource NER.  
- Related-work section needs deeper comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and clearer distinction from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Section 3.3 (edited Tsai and Roth model) lacks clarity and would benefit from a running example.  
- Improvements reported in Table 4 are small/inconsistent and should be backed by statistical significance tests.  
- Paper contains typos and awkward sentences that require careful proofreading and editing.  
- How will the authors address limitations like the need for a large Wikipedia dump and possible wikification errors?  
- Please provide more details and comparisons for experiments that include POS-tag features.  
- How do the authors plan to combine this approach with other multilingual NER techniques (e.g., parallel projection, automatically generated Wikipedia training data)?  
- Request more analysis of surprising language-pair results (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish, Spanish best for Yoruba).
