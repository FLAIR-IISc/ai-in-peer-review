This paper proposes a novel approach to cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) by leveraging a cross-lingual wikifier to ground words and phrases in non-English languages to English Wikipedia entries. The authors demonstrate that the resulting language-independent features can be used to improve monolingual NER systems and enable direct transfer of NER models across languages.

The paper's primary contributions are:

1. The development of a language-independent method for NER using cross-lingual wikification.
2. The demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed approach in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings, with significant improvements over strong baselines.

The strengths of the paper are:

1. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including experiments on a wide range of languages, including low-resource languages with non-Latin scripts.
2. The paper presents a valuable analysis of the importance of Wikipedia size to the quality of wikifier features and explores the use of multiple source languages for training.
3. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be used to improve monolingual NER systems, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

1. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of how entities not present on Wikipedia are affected by their method.
2. The approach strongly depends on the cross-lingual wikifier, and the authors do not provide a thorough analysis of how often wikifier errors lead to NER prediction errors.
3. The paper does not explore the use of other techniques for multilingual NER, such as parallel projection and generating training data from Wikipedia automatically.

Questions to authors:

1. How do you plan to address the issue of entities not present on Wikipedia in your approach?
2. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the error propagation from the wikifier to the NER model?
3. Have you considered exploring the use of other techniques for multilingual NER in your approach?

Additional comments:

* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its contributions.
* The use of a cross-lingual wikifier is a novel and interesting approach to cross-lingual NER.
* The paper's focus on low-resource languages with non-Latin scripts is a significant contribution to the field.
* The authors' analysis of the importance of Wikipedia size to the quality of wikifier features is valuable and insightful.
* The paper's results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate significant improvements over strong baselines.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of cross-lingual NER, and the authors' approach has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in this area.
