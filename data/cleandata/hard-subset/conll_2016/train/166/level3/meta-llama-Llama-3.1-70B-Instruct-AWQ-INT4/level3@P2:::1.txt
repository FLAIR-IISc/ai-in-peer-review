This paper proposes a novel approach to cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) using a language-independent method that leverages cross-lingual wikification. The main contributions of this work are:

1. The development of a method that utilizes cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features for NER, which can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia.
2. The demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed approach in both monolingual and direct-transfer settings, with significant improvements over strong baselines.
3. The thorough analysis of the proposed method on a wide range of languages, including low-resource and non-Latin languages.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The proposed method is innovative and has the potential to significantly improve cross-lingual NER performance. The use of cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features is a novel approach that addresses the limitations of traditional methods.
2. The experiments are thorough and well-designed, with a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method on a wide range of languages. The inclusion of low-resource and non-Latin languages in the experiments adds significant value to the paper.
3. The paper provides valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method, including the impact of Wikipedia size on the quality of wikifier features.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

1. The method strongly depends on the cross-lingual Wikifier, which may not always produce accurate results. The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the impact of Wikifier errors on NER prediction errors.
2. The method may not perform well on entities that are not present on Wikipedia. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how such entities are handled.
3. The paper does not consider the impact of lexical features on the performance of the proposed method. The results show that adding lexical features can actually decrease performance in some cases, which suggests that the method may be over-relying on these features.

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of Wikifier errors on NER prediction errors? How often do Wikifier errors lead to NER prediction errors, and what are the common types of errors that occur?
2. How do you handle entities that are not present on Wikipedia? Are there any plans to incorporate additional sources of information to improve performance on such entities?
3. Have you considered regularizing feature classes differently to prevent over-reliance on lexical features? This may help to improve performance in cases where lexical features are not relevant.

Additional Comments:

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and experiments.
* The use of tables and figures is effective in presenting the results and analysis.
* The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work, which helps to situate the proposed method in the context of existing research.
