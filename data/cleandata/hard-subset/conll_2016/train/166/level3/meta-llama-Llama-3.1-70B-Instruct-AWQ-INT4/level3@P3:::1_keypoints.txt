- Proposed a language-independent NER method using a cross-lingual wikifier that grounds non-English text to English Wikipedia.
- Demonstrated the approach improves monolingual NER and enables direct cross-lingual model transfer with gains over strong baselines.
- Thorough evaluation across many languages, including low-resource languages with non-Latin scripts.
- Analyzed the impact of Wikipedia size on wikifier feature quality and explored multi-source training.
- Showed practical benefit by improving monolingual NER systems.
- Lacks analysis of how entities absent from Wikipedia are handled.
- Does not quantify how often wikifier errors propagate to NER prediction errors.
- Does not explore alternative multilingual NER methods like parallel projection or automatic Wikipedia-derived training data.
- How will entities not present on Wikipedia be addressed?
- Can you provide a detailed analysis quantifying error propagation from the wikifier to the NER model?
- Have you considered integrating other multilingual NER techniques (e.g., parallel projection, auto-generated Wikipedia training data)?
- Paper is well-written and clearly explained.
- The cross-lingual wikifier idea is novel and interesting.
- Emphasis on low-resource, non-Latin scripts is a valuable contribution.
- The analysis of Wikipedia size effects is insightful.
- Results are impressive, showing significant improvements over strong baselines.
- Overall: a significant contribution with potential to advance cross-lingual NER.
