**Summary**

This paper proposes a language-independent model for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram. The model is trained on annotated documents in one or multiple source languages and can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia. The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages, including low-resource languages, and show significant improvements over strong baselines.

**Contributions**

1. **Cross-lingual wikification for direct-transfer NER**: The authors propose a novel approach to cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification, which grounds words and phrases in non-English text into English Wikipedia entries. This approach provides language-independent features for NER.
2. **Language-independent features**: The authors show that the typing information from Wikipedia categories and FreeBase are useful language-independent features for NER.
3. **Evaluation on low-resource languages**: The authors evaluate their model on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil) and show that their approach performs better than existing methods.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of cross-lingual wikification**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of cross-lingual wikification in providing language-independent features for NER.
2. **Improved performance on low-resource languages**: The authors show that their approach performs better than existing methods on low-resource languages.
3. **Flexibility in training languages**: The authors demonstrate that their model can be trained on multiple languages, which can improve performance on certain languages.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of novelty in approach**: The authors' approach builds on existing work, and the contribution is incremental rather than technically novel.
2. **Insufficient comparison to related work**: The authors do not provide a thorough comparison to related work, such as Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and Nothman et al. (2012).
3. **Unclear explanation of edited model**: The authors' explanation of the edited model from Tsai and Roth (2016) is unclear and would benefit from a running example.
4. **Small and inconsistent improvements**: The improvements in Table 4 are small and inconsistent, and the authors do not provide statistical significance tests to support their claims.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more thorough comparison to related work, such as Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and Nothman et al. (2012)?
2. Can you provide a clearer explanation of the edited model from Tsai and Roth (2016), including a running example?
3. Can you provide statistical significance tests to support the claims in Table 4?
4. Can you discuss the potential limitations of your approach and how you plan to address them in future work?

**Additional Comments**

The paper contains several typos and awkward sentences, and the authors should perform careful proofreading to fix these errors. Additionally, the authors should consider including other techniques for multilingual NER, such as parallel projection and generating training data from Wikipedia automatically, in their model.
