**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a language-independent method for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier. The approach leverages the English Wikipedia to disambiguate words and phrases in non-English languages, generating language-independent features for NER. The method is evaluated on a range of languages, including those with limited annotated data, and demonstrates significant improvements over strong baselines.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Cross-lingual wikification for direct-transfer NER**: The paper introduces a novel approach to cross-lingual NER, leveraging the English Wikipedia to disambiguate words and phrases in non-English languages. This approach enables the transfer of a trained NER model across languages, without requiring parallel data or language-specific features.
2. **Language-independent features for NER**: The paper demonstrates that the features generated by the cross-lingual wikifier are useful for NER, even in the absence of language-specific features. This is a significant contribution, as it enables the development of NER models that can be applied to a wide range of languages.
3. **Evaluation on a range of languages**: The paper evaluates the proposed approach on a range of languages, including those with limited annotated data. This demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach in a variety of settings and highlights its potential for improving NER in low-resource languages.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of cross-lingual wikification**: The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of cross-lingual wikification for generating language-independent features for NER. This approach enables the transfer of a trained NER model across languages, without requiring parallel data or language-specific features.
2. **Significant improvements over strong baselines**: The paper reports significant improvements over strong baselines, including the approach of Tackström et al. (2012). This demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach and highlights its potential for improving NER in a variety of settings.
3. **Wide range of languages evaluated**: The paper evaluates the proposed approach on a range of languages, including those with limited annotated data. This demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach in a variety of settings and highlights its potential for improving NER in low-resource languages.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Related-work section could be improved**: The related-work section could benefit from a deeper comparison to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and clearer distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
2. **Lack of clarity in Section 3.3**: Section 3.3, which describes the edited model from Tsai and Roth, could benefit from a running example to illustrate the approach.
3. **Small and inconsistent improvements in Table 4**: The improvements reported in Table 4 are small and inconsistent, and could benefit from statistical significance tests to support the claims.
4. **Typos and awkward sentences**: The paper contains several typos and awkward sentences, which could be improved through careful proofreading and editing.

**Questions to Authors**

1. **How do you plan to address the limitations of the cross-lingual wikification approach, such as the requirement for a large Wikipedia dump and the potential for errors in the wikification process?**
2. **Can you provide more details on the experiments that include POS-tag features, and how they compare to the results reported in the paper?**
3. **How do you plan to combine the proposed approach with other techniques for multilingual NER, such as parallel projection and generating training data from Wikipedia automatically?**
4. **Can you provide more analysis on the surprising language-pair results, such as Dutch being the best training language for Spanish, and Spanish being the best training language for Yoruba?**
