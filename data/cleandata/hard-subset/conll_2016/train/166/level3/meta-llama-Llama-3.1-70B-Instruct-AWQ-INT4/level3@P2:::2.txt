This paper proposes a language-independent model for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram. The model works on all languages in Wikipedia and requires only a Wikipedia dump. The authors study a wide range of languages in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings and show significant improvements over strong baselines.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. The development of a method that makes use of cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features for NER.
2. The demonstration of the effectiveness of these features in training NER models with no annotation in the target language.
3. The proposal of a direct transfer model that can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia.

The strengths of this submission are:

1. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model on a wide range of languages, including low-resource languages.
2. The use of cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features is a novel approach that shows promising results.
3. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the quality of wikifier features and its impact on the performance of the model.

The weaknesses of this submission are:

1. The related-work section could benefit from a deeper comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and clearer distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
2. The authors do not provide a comparison with a strong projection baseline, which would help to better contextualize the strengths and limitations of the wikification-based approach.
3. The improvements reported in Table 4 are small and inconsistent, and the authors should provide statistical significance tests to support their claims.
4. The paper contains several typos and awkward sentences, and could benefit from careful proofreading.

Questions to authors:

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the surprising language-pair results, such as Dutch being the best for Spanish and Spanish being the best for Yoruba?
2. Can you provide preliminary combined-model results for some languages to support the claim that the approaches from Tackström et al. and Nothman et al. are orthogonal?
3. Can you explain why you did not use POS-tag features in your experiments, and provide a comparison with the results of Tackström et al.?
4. Can you provide more details on how you plan to incorporate other techniques for multilingual NER into your model in future work?

Additional comments:

* The authors should consider adding a comparison with a strong projection baseline to better contextualize the strengths and limitations of the wikification-based approach.
* The authors should note that projection can be done without parallel data using bilingual semantic spaces, and mention and position these methods.
* The authors should provide a running example to illustrate the edited model from Tsai and Roth in Section 3.3.
* The authors should provide a clearer explanation of the surprising language-pair results, such as Dutch being the best for Spanish and Spanish being the best for Yoruba.
