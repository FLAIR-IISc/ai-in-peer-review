- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER method using a cross-lingual wikifier to map phrases to English Wikipedia categories and Freebase types.  
- Introduces a new multilingual NER feature that links target-language phrases to English Wikipedia articles.  
- Shows improvements in both monolingual and direct-transfer (English→target) NER settings.  
- Evaluates method thoroughly on many languages, including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.  
- Leverages Wikipedia-derived typing features as an innovative cross-lingual signal.  
- Presents strong experimental results demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.  
- Analyzes impact of Wikipedia size on quality of wikifier features.  
- Method heavily depends on the cross-lingual wikifier, which may produce errors.  
- Unclear how entities absent from Wikipedia are handled or affected.  
- Possible over-reliance on lexical features, causing poorer performance for languages like Tamil and Bengali.  
- Asks how authors will address entities not present on Wikipedia.  
- Requests more detailed error analysis of the cross-lingual wikifier and its effect on NER errors.  
- Suggests considering different regularization for feature classes to avoid lexical over-reliance.  
- Paper is well-written and clear but should address the listed concerns to strengthen the contribution.
