- Proposes language-independent cross-lingual wikification features for NER applicable across all Wikipedia languages.
- Demonstrates significant improvements over strong baselines in monolingual and direct-transfer settings.
- Provides extensive analysis across many languages, including low-resource and non-Latin languages.

- Innovative approach that addresses limitations of traditional cross-lingual NER via wikification.
- Thorough, well-designed experiments with broad language coverage add significant value.
- Offers useful insights into strengths/weaknesses, notably the effect of Wikipedia size on feature quality.

- Strong dependence on the cross-lingual Wikifier without thorough analysis of how Wikifier errors affect NER.
- Likely poor coverage for entities not present in Wikipedia with no clear handling strategy described.
- Does not sufficiently consider or regularize lexical features, which can sometimes decrease performance.

- Request detailed analysis of how often and what types of Wikifier errors lead to NER mistakes.
- Ask how entities absent from Wikipedia are handled and whether additional information sources will be used.
- Suggest investigating different regularization of feature classes to prevent over-reliance on lexical features.

- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Tables and figures effectively present results.
- Comprehensive related-work review that situates the contribution.
