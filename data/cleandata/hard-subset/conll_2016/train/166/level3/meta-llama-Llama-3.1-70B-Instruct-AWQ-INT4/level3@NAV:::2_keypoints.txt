- Summary: Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model using a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate n-grams, trained on source languages and evaluated across many languages (including low-resource), showing significant gains over strong baselines.
- Contribution: Introduces language-independent features for cross-lingual NER derived from a cross-lingual wikifier.
- Contribution: Uses a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram, providing transferable, language-agnostic signals for NER.
- Contribution: Evaluates the approach on a wide range of languages, including low-resource languages, and reports substantial improvements.
- Strength: Presents a novel approach to generating language-independent features for cross-lingual NER.
- Strength: Demonstrates strong performance gains on low-resource languages.
- Strength: Shows robustness to language variation, including different scripts and lexical features.
- Weakness: Limited comparison to prior related work (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007; Nothman et al. 2012).
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of surprising language-pair results (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish; Spanish best for Yoruba).
- Weakness: Some experiments show only small improvements (e.g., adding Spanish training data besting Yoruba by a small margin).
- Question: How will you address the limited comparison to related work in future revisions?
- Question: Can you provide a more thorough analysis of surprising language-pair results?
- Question: How do you plan to further improve performance on very low-resource languages such as Yoruba and Bengali?
