- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model using a cross-lingual wikifier that disambiguates every n-gram and requires only a Wikipedia dump, applicable to all Wikipedia languages.
- Shows wikification-derived, language-independent features can train NER models with no target-language annotations.
- Proposes a direct-transfer model usable across all Wikipedia languages.

- Thorough evaluation across many languages, including low-resource ones.
- Novel use of cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features with promising results.
- Provides detailed analysis of wikifier feature quality and its effect on model performance.

- Related-work section needs deeper comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and clearer distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Lacks comparison with a strong projection baseline to contextualize strengths and limitations.
- Improvements reported (e.g., Table 4) are small/inconsistent and require statistical significance tests.
- Paper contains typos and awkward sentences and needs careful proofreading.

- Please analyze surprising language-pair results (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish; Spanish best for Yoruba).
- Provide preliminary combined-model results to support the claim that Tackström et al. and Nothman et al. approaches are orthogonal.
- Explain why POS-tag features were not used and compare with Tackström et al.’s results.
- Describe how other multilingual NER techniques will be incorporated in future work.

- Add a comparison with a strong projection baseline.
- Note and position projection methods that work without parallel data using bilingual semantic spaces.
- Provide a running example to illustrate the edited Tsai and Roth model in Section 3.3.
- Provide a clearer explanation of the surprising language-pair results.
