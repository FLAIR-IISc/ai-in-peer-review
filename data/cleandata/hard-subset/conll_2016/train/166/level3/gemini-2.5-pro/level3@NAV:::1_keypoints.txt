- Novel method: cross-lingual Wikifier maps n-grams to English Wikipedia to create language-agnostic NER features without needing parallel corpora.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across nine languages (including low-resource and non-Latin scripts) with analysis of Wikipedia size effects.  
- Core idea is elegant and effective: language-agnostic features yield substantial gains over strong baselines.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed, covering high- and low-resource cases and providing useful analysis.  
- Practical impact: outperforms prior resource-heavy approaches, valuable for low-resource language NLP.  
- Limitation: performance depends on Wikipedia coverage and fails for entities not present in Wikipedia (emerging/local entities).  
- Reliance on Wikifier accuracy: no error analysis of disambiguation/linking failures and their downstream effects.  
- Lexical features hurt direct-transfer on non-Latin scripts (e.g., Tamil, Bengali), suggesting overfitting to source-language lexical signals without proposed mitigation.
