- Paper proposes direct-transfer cross-lingual NER using a wikifier to map n-grams to English Wikipedia, generating language-independent features for training on source languages and applying to targets without annotations.
- Main contribution: coherent, empirically validated method combining cross-lingual wikification with standard NER to produce robust language-agnostic features.
- Contribution: analysis showing correlation between Wikipedia size / inter-language links and transfer quality, and exploration of multi-source training benefits.
- Strength: strong empirical results outperforming prior direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL and several low-resource languages.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental setup covering monolingual, direct-transfer, and multi-source settings including non-Latin and low-resource languages.
- Strength: insightful ablation simulating low-resource settings that clarifies limitations due to small Wikipedias.
- Weakness: insufficient positioning against prior work — brief comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and missing discussion of bilingual semantic-space projection methods.
- Weakness: no direct experimental comparison with a strong projection-based transfer baseline.
- Weakness: key methodological choices lack empirical backing — omission of POS-tag features is unsupported by reported results.
- Weakness: claim of orthogonality to methods like Täckström et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) needs evidence (e.g., combined-model experiments).
- Weakness: multi-source training gains are small/inconsistent and require statistical significance testing.
- Weakness: Section 3.3 detailing wikifier modifications is unclear; a running example from n-gram to feature vector is needed for reproducibility.
- Weakness: surprising empirical findings (e.g., unexpected best source languages) are presented without analysis or hypotheses.
- Weakness: presentation issues — typos and awkward phrasing require proofreading.
