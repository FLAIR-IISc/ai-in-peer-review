- Proposed a direct-transfer cross-lingual NER method that links n-grams to English Wikipedia via a wikifier and uses Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features for NER.
- Demonstrated the method improves monolingual NER by adding wikifier features and enables training on a high-resource language for direct application to low-resource languages.
- Evaluated the approach on nine languages (including non-Latin scripts) and showed strong empirical results.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel language-independent feature representation for NER by inverting the usual NER-then-linking pipeline and leveraging a central English KB.
- Contribution: Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation across diverse languages, resource levels, and scripts, comparing to strong baselines.
- Contribution: Analyses the dependence of performance on knowledge-base size and intersection with English Wikipedia, clarifying applicability and limits for low-resource languages.

- Strength: The cross-lingual wikification idea is novel and substantially boosts cross-lingual transfer F1 scores on several languages.
- Strength: Extensive, thorough experimentation (nine languages, monolingual/cross-lingual/multi-source settings) strengthens the empirical claims.
- Strength: Honest, valuable analysis of low-resource scenarios showing performance correlates with target-language Wikipedia size.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on the upstream wikifier with no error analysis to show how linking errors propagate to NER mistakes.
- Weakness: No treatment or analysis of entities absent from English Wikipedia, leaving unclear recall for out-of-KB entities.
- Weakness: Ad-hoc manual exclusion of lexical features for non-Latin scripts is brittle; lacks principled, automatic feature-weighting or regularization.

- Question: Please provide error analysis linking wikifier errors (wrong link or failure to link) to NER errors and common failure cases.
- Question: How does the model perform on entities not present in Wikipedia—does lacking wikifier features hurt recall, or do base/gazetteer features compensate?
- Question: Did you consider automatic methods (e.g., group regularization) to learn to down-weight unhelpful feature groups instead of manual feature removal for non-Latin scripts?
