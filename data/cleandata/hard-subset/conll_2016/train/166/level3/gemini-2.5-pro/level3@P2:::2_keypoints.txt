- Proposes using a cross-lingual wikifier to ground n-grams to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent NER features.  
- Demonstrates method on direct transfer NER from English to 9 languages, showing large gains over direct-transfer and some weakly-supervised baselines.  
- Shows wikifier feature effectiveness correlates with language Wikipedia size and inter-language link coverage.

- Grounding phrases to English Wikipedia is a simple, principled way to get language-independent semantic features without parallel corpora.  
- Empirical results are strong: state-of-the-art in direct transfer and improvements even over systems that use parallel data or human interaction.  
- Evaluation is thorough across diverse languages and scripts, with useful ablation analysis linking feature quality to Wikipedia resources.

- Lacks comparison to annotation projection and other cross-lingual paradigms, leaving contextual positioning incomplete.  
- Claims of orthogonality to cluster/silver-data methods are unverified; no experiments combining methods are provided.  
- Exclusion of POS features is asserted but not empirically justified with presented ablation results.  
- Modified wikifier description is too brief and lacks reproducible detail (candidate generation/ranking for all n-grams).  
- Interesting cross-source transfer patterns (e.g., Dutch→Spanish) are reported but unexplained.  
- Multi-source training gains are small and reported without statistical significance testing.

- Clarify how the Tsai & Roth (2016) wikifier was modified for all n-grams, with a concise example.  
- Provide results combining wikifier features with cross-lingual word clusters (e.g., Täckström et al.) for at least one CoNLL language.  
- Explain unexpected source-target synergies in Figure 2 with linguistic or data-driven hypotheses.  
- Report statistical significance testing for the small multi-source training improvements.
