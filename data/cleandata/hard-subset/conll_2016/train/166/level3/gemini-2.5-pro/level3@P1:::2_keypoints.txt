- Summary (approach): Proposes direct-transfer NER using a cross-lingual wikifier to map n-grams to English Wikipedia entries and derive language-independent Freebase types and Wikipedia category features.  
- Summary (evaluation): Trains on high-resource languages and evaluates on nine languages (CoNLL + five low-resource), showing significant improvements over comparable direct-transfer baselines.

- Contribution: Demonstrates that cross-lingual wikification yields powerful, language-independent features for direct-transfer NER and analyzes feature quality relative to Wikipedia size.  
- Contribution: Provides a broad empirical evaluation across nine diverse languages, including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains, notably large F1 improvements on CoNLL languages and outperforming a resource-heavy prior on low-resource languages without parallel data.  
- Strength: Method is simple, intuitive, and generalizable, avoiding parallel corpora or language-specific tools.  
- Strength: Insightful analysis linking wikifier feature quality to target Wikipedia size and interlanguage link coverage.

- Weakness: Insufficient positioning against prior work (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007) and omission of methods using bilingual semantic spaces or cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Weakness: Missing key baselines and ablations (e.g., universal POS tag baseline, strong projection-based baseline) to contextualize gains.  
- Weakness: Methodological clarity issues (opaque Section 3.3, superficial analysis of language-transfer matrix, small/inconsistent multi-source gains lacking significance testing).  
- Weakness: Claim of orthogonality with Täckström et al.'s bitext clustering is speculative and untested.

- Question: Provide a step-by-step example of how the modified wikifier processes an n-gram like "in Germany" and avoids incorrect linking.  
- Question: Have statistical significance tests been performed for the modest multi-source training improvements?  
- Question: Can you hypothesize why surprising transfer patterns occur (e.g., Spanish better for Yoruba than English; Dutch best for Spanish)?  
- Question: Can you provide results combining your wikifier features with Täckström et al.'s bitext clusters on at least one CoNLL language to support orthogonality claims?

- Additional comment: Paper is generally well-written but needs proofreading to fix typos and awkward phrasing.
