- Proposes a direct-transfer cross-lingual NER using a cross-lingual wikifier that grounds n-grams to English Wikipedia so models trained on a high-resource language transfer to others without parallel data.
- Main contribution is a strong empirical demonstration of the method’s effectiveness across nine languages, including low-resource ones.
- Technical novelty is incremental, but the paper presents a coherent system with convincing results.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation on CoNLL and genuinely low-resource languages (e.g., Yoruba, Tamil), validating robustness and broad applicability.
- Strength: method is well-motivated and elegant, using only a Wikipedia dump as a minimal resource, making it practical for many languages.
- Weakness: lacks crucial baselines—no comparison to a strong projection-based baseline—and related work is insufficiently distinguished from prior Wikipedia-based systems.
- Weakness: several claims are unsubstantiated—needs statistical significance tests (e.g., multi-source training gains), evidence for orthogonality to other methods, and analysis of surprising results (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish).
- Weakness: technical description (notably Section 3.3 on wikifier modifications) is unclear and would benefit from a running example.
- Weakness: paper contains typos and awkward sentences and requires careful proofreading.
