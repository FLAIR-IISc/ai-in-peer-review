**Summary and Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel approach for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) by generating language-independent features using a cross-lingual Wikifier. The core idea is to map n-grams in any source language to English Wikipedia articles, thereby leveraging Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as powerful, delexicalized features. The authors demonstrate that this method improves performance in both monolingual and direct-transfer settings across a wide range of languages.

The main contributions of this work are:
1.  A novel and effective method for creating language-agnostic NER features using cross-lingual Wikification. This approach cleverly bypasses the need for parallel corpora, relying only on the multilingual structure of Wikipedia.
2.  A comprehensive empirical evaluation across nine diverse languages, including valuable analysis on low-resource and non-Latin script languages. This sheds light on the method's real-world applicability and its performance characteristics under different resource conditions.

**Strengths**

1.  The primary strength of this paper is its core idea. Using a cross-lingual Wikifier to generate features is an elegant and effective solution for the direct-transfer NER problem. The features are truly language-agnostic and, as shown in Table 2, lead to substantial performance gains over strong baselines.
2.  The experiments are thorough and well-designed. The authors provide a convincing evaluation on a wide spectrum of languages, from high-resource CoNLL languages to low-resource ones like Yoruba and Tamil. The analysis on how Wikipedia size affects feature quality provides excellent insight into the method’s dependencies.
3.  The work has significant practical implications, particularly for low-resource languages. The model outperforms prior work that requires more resources (e.g., parallel text, native speaker interaction), making it a valuable tool for expanding NLP capabilities to a wider range of languages.

**Weaknesses**

1.  The model’s performance is fundamentally dependent on the coverage of Wikipedia. A key concern is how the method handles named entities that are not present in Wikipedia, such as emerging entities or local persons and organizations. The paper does not address this limitation, which is critical for real-world deployment.
2.  The entire feature generation pipeline relies heavily on the accuracy of the upstream cross-lingual Wikifier. The paper lacks an error analysis exploring how often incorrect disambiguation or a failure to link by the Wikifier results in a downstream NER error. Understanding this error propagation is crucial for assessing the model’s robustness.
3.  The authors observe that including lexical features drastically hurts direct-transfer performance on non-Latin script languages like Tamil and Bengali. This suggests the model may be overfitting to source-language lexical features. The paper would be stronger if it explored methods to mitigate this, such as regularizing different feature classes to prevent over-reliance on non-transferable signals.
