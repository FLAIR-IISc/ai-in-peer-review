- Summary: Proposes language-independent NER features via cross-lingual Wikification mapping n-grams to English Wikipedia categories/Freebase types and evaluates monolingual and direct-transfer NER on nine languages, showing consistent improvements.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel upstream feature-generation method that grounds n-grams in English Wikipedia to produce delexicalized, cross-lingual NER features.

- Contribution 2: Provides a comprehensive empirical study across nine languages (including low-resource and non-Latin scripts) demonstrating strong direct-transfer performance without parallel data.

- Strength 1: Elegant, effective idea that avoids parallel data and yields large direct-transfer F1 gains (e.g., Dutch 49.66→62.10; German 55.04→60.97).

- Strength 2: Thorough, broad-ranging experiments with analyses (including correlation with Wikipedia size) that support the method’s generalizability.

- Strength 3: Particularly strong results on low-resource languages, outperforming stronger-resource baselines.

- Weakness 1: Heavy reliance on Wikipedia coverage; no analysis of performance on entities absent from Wikipedia (local/new entities).

- Weakness 2: Lacks quantitative analysis of error propagation from Wikifier disambiguation mistakes to final NER errors.

- Weakness 3: Adding English lexical/gazetteer features catastrophically degrades transfer to Bengali/Tamil; only a manual feature-selection fix is suggested instead of principled remedies.

- Question 1: How does the model handle entities not in Wikipedia—provide in-Wikipedia vs out-of-Wikipedia recall analysis?

- Question 2: Can you quantify how often Wikifier wrong/no links directly cause NER errors?

- Question 3: Have you tried principled approaches (e.g., different regularization or group lasso) to prevent over-reliance on source-specific lexical features during transfer?
