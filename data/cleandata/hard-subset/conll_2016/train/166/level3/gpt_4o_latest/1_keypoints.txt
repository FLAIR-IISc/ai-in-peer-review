- Introduces cross-lingual Wikifier features that ground non-English text to English Wikipedia to improve multilingual NER, showing strong monolingual and direct-transfer gains.  
- Primary contributions: novel Wikifier features, comprehensive evaluation on low-resource/non-Latin languages, demonstration of multilingual training benefits, and analysis of Wikipedia-size impact.  
- Novelty/practicality: method is simple and scalable, requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump without parallel data or native speakers.  
- Extensive evaluation: experiments cover nine languages including five low-resource/non-Latin languages, with insightful analysis of Wikipedia coverage effects.  
- Strong baselines: outperforms prior state-of-the-art methods in both monolingual and direct-transfer settings.  
- Multilingual training: combining multiple source languages improves performance, especially for low-resource targets.  
- Weakness — Wikipedia dependence: performance degrades for languages with sparse Wikipedia (e.g., Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Weakness — out-of-Wikipedia entities: struggles to recognize entities absent from Wikipedia, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Weakness — Wikifier errors: disambiguation/linking mistakes can propagate and harm NER performance.  
- Weakness — lexical over-reliance: poor transfer on Tamil and Bengali suggests excessive dependence on lexical features; needs better feature regularization.  
- Questions for authors: how ambiguous multi-entry links are handled; whether augmenting with external KBs/embeddings was considered for low-resource coverage; and whether out-of-Wikipedia entities can be addressed via contextual embeddings or unsupervised clustering.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: meaningful, scalable contribution with strong results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
