- Novel approach: use cross-lingual wikification to ground mentions in English Wikipedia for language-independent NER features.  
- Core idea: map non-English mentions to English Wikipedia entries to extract categories and Freebase types as features.  
- Method avoids parallel corpora and annotation projection by framing NER as a grounding problem.  
- Evaluation: extensive experiments on CoNLL languages and multiple low-resource languages with ablation studies.  
- Empirical result: Wikipedia-derived features consistently improve NER, outperforming comparable and some state-of-the-art methods.  
- Benefit: features also improve monolingual NER and multi-source training scenarios.  
- Contribution 1: introduces wikification-based cross-lingual NER as a conceptual shift.  
- Contribution 2: demonstrates effectiveness of Wikipedia/Freebase-derived features across languages.  
- Contribution 3: provides comprehensive evaluation, including Wikipedia size impact and multi-source analysis.  
- Strength: innovative use of Wikipedia as language-independent knowledge source.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains, especially in low-resource settings.  
- Strength: robust experimental design and informative ablation/analysis.  
- Strength: practical applicability—only requires a multilingual Wikipedia dump.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with useful figures/tables.  
- Weakness: reliance on wikifier—potential error propagation to NER not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: handling of out-of-Wikipedia (NIL) entities is underexplored and impact unclear.  
- Weakness: lexical features can hurt performance in low-resource cases, suggesting need for better regularization.  
- Question: quantify wikifier error types and correlation with NER errors.  
- Question: how are NIL (unlinked) mentions handled and mitigated?  
- Question: were different regularization techniques for lexical features experimented with?  
- Additional comment: report computational cost of the wikification step.  
- Additional comment: consider issues from evolving knowledge bases (e.g., Freebase inconsistencies or outdated data).
