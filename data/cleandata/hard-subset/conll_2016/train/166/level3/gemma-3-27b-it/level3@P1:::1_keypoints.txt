- Proposes grounding mentions in English Wikipedia via cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent NER features.  
- Demonstrates improvements over strong baselines in monolingual and cross-lingual transfer, including low-resource and non-Latin script languages.  
- Shows wikification-as-feature (rather than downstream) is effective and can boost monolingual NER too.

- Novel and effective idea: grounding to English Wikipedia provides valuable signals even with imperfect disambiguation.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse languages (CoNLL and low-resource languages) showing generalizability.  
- Detailed analyses of Wikipedia size effects and benefits of multi-source training illuminate method behavior.  
- Strong comparisons to relevant baselines, with consistent gains especially on low-resource languages.  
- Practical and scalable approach relying only on multilingual Wikipedia with demonstrated computational efficiency.

- Main dependence on wikifier accuracy without thorough analysis of how wikifier errors affect NER performance.  
- Unclear handling and impact of entities missing from English Wikipedia and limited coverage of Freebase types.  
- Lexical features can dominate and harm performance on low-resource languages (e.g., Bengali, Tamil), suggesting over-reliance on lexical transfer.  
- Limited exploitation of other Wikipedia signals (document context, title relations) that could improve features.

- Request a detailed error analysis quantifying what fraction of NER errors are due to incorrect wikification.  
- Ask how the model handles entities absent from English Wikipedia, their test-set frequency, and their effect on results.  
- Ask whether feature regularization or weighting was tried to reduce lexical feature dominance and what the outcomes were.

- Writing is clear and the paper is well-organized.  
- Figure 1 is a helpful illustration of wikification and features.  
- Related work discussion is comprehensive and well-contextualized.  
- Suggest adding a summary table of main experimental results for quick reference.
