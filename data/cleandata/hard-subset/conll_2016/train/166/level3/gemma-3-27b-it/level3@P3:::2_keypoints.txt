- Systematic evaluation of cross-lingual wikification as language-independent features for NER across CoNLL and many low-resource languages.  
- Grounding mentions to a common English Wikipedia space is a sound, intuitive approach with competitive results.  
- Main contribution is extensive evaluation and analysis rather than novel algorithms.  
- Demonstrates strong cross-lingual transfer: English-trained models perform well on many target languages, especially low-resource ones.  
- Analysis of Wikipedia feature quality shows Wikipedia size and Freebase links materially affect performance.  
- Broad language coverage (CoNLL + Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) strengthens real-world applicability.  
- Thorough experiments, including monolingual gains, transfer configurations, and ablations, support the findings.  
- Clear writing and motivation; figures (e.g., Figure 1) effectively illustrate grounding.  
- Method achieves competitive or superior performance to strong baselines (e.g., Zhang et al. 2016) in low-resource settings.  
- Technical novelty is limited; approach builds on established wikification and feature-based NER techniques.  
- Related-work comparison is shallow, lacking detailed contrasts with Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and other Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Omission of POS-tag experiments is a significant gap; authors should at least try imperfect taggers to assess impact.  
- Section 3.3 (modified Tsai & Roth 2016 system) is unclearly described; needs a running example for n-gram candidate generation/ranking.  
- No combined-model experiments to support the claim that approaches (Tackström et al., Nothman et al.) are orthogonal.  
- Statistical significance testing is missing for small/inconsistent improvements (e.g., Table 4).  
- Related work overly focused on parallel projection; missing discussion of non-parallel projection methods (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009, Vulić & Moens 2013).  
- Request for a detailed comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) on wikification, features, and experiments.  
- Request for a running example showing Tsai & Roth (2016) modifications for n-gram wikification.  
- Request for preliminary combined-model results incorporating Tackström et al. or Nothman et al. elements.  
- Request to perform statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) for reported improvements.  
- Question about surprising language-pair results (Dutch best for Spanish; Spanish best for Yoruba) and possible linguistic/Wikipedia explanations.  
- Notes on presentation issues: typos and awkward phrasing (e.g., “Latin Script Non-Latin Script”); inconsistent citation formatting for Zhang et al. (2016).  
- Clarify whether reported F1 scores are micro-averaged or macro-averaged.
