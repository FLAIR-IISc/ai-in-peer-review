- Core idea: use cross-lingual wikification to ground mentions in English Wikipedia and extract language-independent NER features.  
- Contribution: demonstrates effectiveness of wikification features for direct-transfer NER across CoNLL and low-resource languages.  
- Contribution: provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation across nine languages, including non-Latin scripts.  
- Contribution: shows wikification-derived features also help monolingual NER systems.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, notably outperforming baselines on Spanish, German, Dutch and several low-resource languages.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation and well-designed ablation studies that reveal feature importance.  
- Strength: practical applicability—relies only on multilingual Wikipedia, no parallel corpora needed.  
- Strength: clear evidence that wikification features are informative even in isolation and correlate with Wikipedia size.  
- Strength: multi-source training yields additional performance improvements.  
- Weakness: limited technical novelty; use of Wikipedia for cross-lingual transfer is not entirely new.  
- Weakness: related work comparisons are incomplete; needs clearer distinctions from Kazama & Torisawa (2007), Nothman et al. (2012), and projection methods without parallel data.  
- Weakness: unclear implementation details in Section 3.3 (modifications to Tsai & Roth 2016 wikifier)—needs a running example.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests for multi-source training improvements (Table 4).  
- Weakness: unexplained, counterintuitive language-pair results (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish) lack analysis.  
- Weakness: missing experiments on POS tag usage and its impact compared to related work.  
- Question: provide a running example showing how the wikifier modifications affect disambiguation and feature generation.  
- Question: supply statistical significance tests for Table 4 to confirm multi-source gains.  
- Question: explain factors behind surprising language-pair transfer results in Figure 2.  
- Question: have you combined this approach with strong projection baselines; if so, present results, if not, explain why.  
- Additional comment: paper has typos and awkward phrasing—needs proofreading and clearer presentation of examples/figures.  
- Additional comment: consider discussing limitations, such as Wikipedia biases and challenges with highly ambiguous mentions.
