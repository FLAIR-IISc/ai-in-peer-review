- Core idea: Ground mentions in any language to English Wikipedia and use resulting categories and Freebase types as language-independent NER features — innovative and effective.
- Experimental setup: Thorough evaluation across diverse languages, including low-resource and non-Latin script languages.
- Primary contribution: Successful application of cross-lingual wikification features for direct-transfer NER with strong results, especially in low-resource scenarios.
- Empirical contribution: Comprehensive evaluation showing effectiveness across languages and analyzing the impact of Wikipedia size on feature quality.
- Broader benefit: Wikification features also improve monolingual NER systems.
- Strength: Strong experimental design with ablation studies and comparisons to solid baselines.
- Strength: Valuable analysis of low-resource languages and Wikipedia-size effects.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation.
- Weakness: Dependence on the Wikifier — unclear how Wikifier errors propagate to NER and how robust the method is to incorrect/ambiguous wikifications.
- Weakness: Lack of quantification of what percentage of NER errors are attributable to the Wikifier.
- Weakness: Adding lexical features harms performance for languages like Tamil and Bengali, indicating possible over-reliance on lexical cues.
- Question: Provide detailed analysis of Wikifier error rates on test sets and correlation with NER errors.
- Question: Explore regularization or feature-weighting strategies to reduce over-reliance on lexical features.
- Question: How does the method handle entities not present in Wikipedia, and does absence of an entry limit detection?
- Additional comment: A qualitative error analysis would help link model errors to wikification limitations.
- Additional comment: Discuss potential biases introduced by using English Wikipedia as the central grounding point.
