- Presents a cross-lingual NER model that uses wikification to map non-English mentions to English Wikipedia, using categories and Freebase types as language-independent features.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across standard CoNLL datasets and several low-resource languages, showing consistent improvements over strong baselines.
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation analyzing feature combinations, Wikipedia size, and multi-source training effects.
- Method is practical and scalable because it relies only on multilingual Wikipedia, avoiding parallel corpora or language-specific resources.
- Ablation studies (e.g., removing Freebase, varying interlanguage links) show the robustness and inner workings of the wikifier features.
- Core technical novelty is incremental; builds on established cross-lingual wikification and direct transfer techniques rather than proposing a major algorithmic advance.
- Related work comparison is insufficient, particularly lacking detailed contrast with Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Excludes POS-tag features; missing experiments or discussion on POS effects despite prior work suggesting potential benefits.
- Section 3.3 (modifications to Tsai and Roth wikifier) is unclear and would benefit from a running example and clearer delineation of performance impact.
- Reported improvements in some tables are small and inconsistent, and the paper lacks statistical significance testing (p-values/confidence intervals).
- Limited analysis of why certain training languages work best for particular test languages; explanations for cross-language discrepancies are missing.
- Questions about computational cost of wikification, handling of ambiguous mentions, and scalability for very large texts remain unanswered.
- Needs clarification on steps taken to mitigate Wikipedia biases (demographic/topic coverage) and how these affect NER performance.
- Should provide a concrete example showing how the modified wikifier handles complex cases and improves NER.
- Must report statistical significance for Table 4 results to validate claimed improvements.
- Should discuss limitations for languages with very different grammatical structures or writing systems compared to English.
- Paper has typos and awkward sentences; requires thorough proofreading and citation/terminology consistency checks.
- Recommend adding discussion of ethical considerations around Wikipedia data biases and potential applications beyond NER (e.g., IE, QA, MT).
