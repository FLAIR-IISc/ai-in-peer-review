- Overall: Proposes grounding mentions in English Wikipedia to extract language-independent features for cross-lingual NER; solid application with thorough multilingual experiments though not a novel algorithmic advance.

Strengths:
- Broad experimental evaluation across many languages, including low-resource ones, supporting generalizability.
- Convincing results that outperform direct transfer baselines and are competitive on low-resource languages.
- Detailed analysis of factors like Wikipedia size and benefits of multi-source training, offering useful insights.
- Practical approach that relies only on Wikipedia, avoiding parallel corpora or heavy language-specific resources.

Weaknesses:
- Incremental novelty: combines existing techniques (wikification, feature engineering, direct transfer) without a fundamentally new method.
- Related work section omits detailed comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and insufficiently distinguishes other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- No experiments including POS tag features, despite prior work suggesting their potential benefit.
- Claim of orthogonality with Tackström et al. is unsupported by combined-model results.
- Table 4 reports small, inconsistent improvements without statistical significance testing.

Questions for authors:
- Provide a detailed comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007), highlighting differences in wikification and features.
- Explain challenges of integrating POS tag features across languages and share any preliminary results.
- Conduct and report statistical significance tests (p-values) for the results in Table 4.
- Give a concrete example of the edited model from Tsai and Roth (Section 3.3) with sample input and output.

Additional comments:
- Paper needs careful proofreading to fix typos, awkward sentences, and some inaccurate cited examples.
