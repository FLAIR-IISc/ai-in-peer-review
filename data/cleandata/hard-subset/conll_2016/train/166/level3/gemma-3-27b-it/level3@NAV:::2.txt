## Review of "Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification"

This paper presents a cross-lingual NER approach leveraging cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea – grounding mentions in English Wikipedia to utilize category and Freebase type information – is sound and builds upon existing work in wikification and transfer learning. The contribution is a coherent application of existing techniques to a cross-lingual NER problem, validated by a comprehensive set of experiments across a diverse range of languages, including several low-resource scenarios. While not a technically groundbreaking innovation, the thoroughness of the experimentation and the convincing results represent a valuable contribution to the field.

**Strengths:**

1. **Broad Experimental Evaluation:** The authors evaluate their approach on a substantial number of languages, including both resource-rich (Spanish, German, Dutch) and low-resource (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) languages. This broad evaluation strengthens the claims about the generalizability of the proposed method.
2. **Convincing Results:** The paper demonstrates consistently positive results, outperforming existing direct transfer approaches and achieving competitive performance, particularly on low-resource languages where data scarcity is a significant challenge.
3. **Detailed Analysis:** The inclusion of experiments analyzing the impact of Wikipedia size and the benefits of multi-source training adds depth to the study and provides valuable insights into the method’s strengths and limitations.
4. **Practical Approach:**  The method’s reliance solely on Wikipedia is a significant advantage, removing the need for parallel corpora or extensive language-specific resources.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incremental Novelty:** The core technical contribution is incremental. The paper skillfully combines existing techniques (wikification, feature engineering, direct transfer) rather than introducing a fundamentally new algorithm or representation.
2. **Related Work Gaps:** The related work section lacks a sufficiently detailed comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and doesn’t adequately distinguish the proposed approach from other Wikipedia-based NER systems. A more nuanced discussion of prior art is needed.
3. **Lack of POS Tag Experiments:** The decision to exclude POS tag features, despite the suggestion of their potential benefit by Tackström et al., weakens the analysis.  Empirical evaluation of POS tags is crucial to justify their omission.
4. **Insufficient Combined Model Results:**  The claim of orthogonality with Tackström et al.’s work is not adequately supported. Preliminary results combining their approach with the presented method would strengthen this claim.
5. **Statistical Significance:** Table 4 presents small and inconsistent improvements without reporting statistical significance tests. This makes it difficult to assess the reliability of the reported gains.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007), specifically outlining the key differences in their wikification process and feature engineering?
2.  Could you elaborate on the challenges encountered when attempting to integrate POS tag features across all languages, and provide some preliminary results if possible?
3.  Would you be willing to perform statistical significance tests on the results presented in Table 4 and report the p-values?
4.  Can you provide a concrete example illustrating the Section 3.3 (the edited model from Tsai and Roth) with sample input and output?



**Additional Comments:**

The paper contains several typos and awkward sentences that require careful proofreading.  For example, in the abstract, “strong languageindependent features” should be “strong language-independent features.” Also, some cited examples in the text are inaccurate and need correction.
