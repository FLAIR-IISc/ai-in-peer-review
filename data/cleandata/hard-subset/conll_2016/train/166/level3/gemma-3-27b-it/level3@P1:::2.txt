## Review of Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Wikification

This paper presents a cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) model leveraging cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea is to ground mentions in non-English text to English Wikipedia entries, utilizing the resulting categories and Freebase types as features for a NER model. While the approach builds on existing work in cross-lingual wikification and direct transfer, the paper demonstrates its effectiveness across a diverse set of languages, including several low-resource ones, and shows improvements over strong baselines. The most significant contribution lies in a comprehensive empirical evaluation demonstrating the viability and robustness of wikification-based features for cross-lingual NER.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents compelling experimental results on both standard CoNLL datasets (Spanish, German, Dutch) and a diverse set of low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil). The consistent performance gains over comparable approaches, especially in the low-resource setting, highlight the effectiveness of the proposed method. The consistent improvements across multiple languages demonstrate the generalizability of the approach.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their approach, considering various aspects such as the impact of different feature combinations (base features, gazetteers, wikifier features), the influence of Wikipedia size, and the benefits of multi-source training. This detailed analysis provides valuable insights into the strengths and limitations of the method.
3. **Practicality and Scalability:** The approach relies solely on multilingual Wikipedia, eliminating the need for parallel corpora or language-specific resources. This makes it highly practical and easily scalable to a large number of languages. The system's dependence on readily available resources is a significant advantage.
4. **Demonstrated Robustness:** The ablation studies (e.g., removing Freebase information, varying interlanguage links) demonstrate the robustness of the wikifier features and offer valuable insights into their inner workings. This detailed analysis is crucial for understanding the method's strengths and weaknesses. 

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incremental Novelty:** While the paper presents a solid and well-executed approach, the core technical novelty is incremental. Cross-lingual wikification and direct transfer are established techniques, and this work primarily demonstrates their effective combination for NER. The approach's strength is more in its thorough application and comprehensive evaluation than in a groundbreaking algorithmic innovation.
2. **Insufficient Comparison to Related Work:** The related work section lacks a sufficiently detailed comparison to existing approaches, particularly Kazama and Torisawa (2007). A deeper discussion of the differences and advancements offered by the current work would strengthen the paper. The distinctions between this work and other Wikipedia-based NER systems are not clearly articulated.
3. **Missing POS-Tag Feature Experiments:** The authors explicitly exclude POS tags due to difficulties obtaining them across languages. However, the related work (e.g., Tackström et al., 2012) suggests that POS tags can be beneficial in direct transfer settings. Running and reporting experiments with POS tags, even if they yield negative results, would strengthen the analysis and address a potential limitation.
4. **Unclear Explanation of Model Modifications:** Section 3.3, describing the modifications to the Tsai and Roth (2016) wikifier, lacks clarity. A running example illustrating how these modifications work in practice would significantly improve understanding. The impact of these modifications on the performance is not clearly delineated.
5. **Statistical Significance Testing:** The improvements reported in Table 4 are relatively small and inconsistent. The lack of statistical significance testing makes it difficult to assess whether these improvements are truly meaningful. Providing p-values or confidence intervals would strengthen the claims.
6. **Limited Analysis of Language-Pair Results:** The observed discrepancies in the best training languages for different test languages (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish, Spanish best for Yoruba) are not adequately explained. A paragraph or two of analysis delving into the potential linguistic or data-related reasons for these results would enhance the paper.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you elaborate on the computational cost of the wikification process, particularly for very large texts or when dealing with ambiguous mentions?
2. What steps were taken to address potential biases in the Wikipedia data (e.g., demographic biases, uneven coverage of topics) that could affect NER performance?
3. In Section 3.3, could you provide a specific example showing how the modified wikifier handles a complex case and how this leads to improved NER performance?
4.  Regarding Table 4, can you provide statistical significance tests to validate the reported improvements?
5.  What are the limitations of this approach when applied to languages with significantly different grammatical structures or writing systems compared to English?

**Additional Comments:**

* The paper contains several typos and awkward sentences. A thorough proofreading is recommended. For example, double-check the citations and ensure consistency in terminology.
* Consider including a discussion of potential ethical considerations related to the use of Wikipedia data, such as the potential for perpetuating biases.
* The paper would benefit from a discussion of potential applications beyond NER, such as information extraction, question answering, or machine translation.



Overall, this is a solid and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of cross-lingual NER. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
