- Proposes cross-lingual wikification to map mentions to English Wikipedia and extract language-independent features for NER, enabling direct transfer to low-resource languages.
- Uses Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as features, training on resource-rich languages and applying to others to improve NER performance.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements across CoNLL datasets and five low-resource languages, sometimes improving monolingual performance.
- Novel contribution: innovative use of cross-lingual wikification to create language-agnostic semantic features for NER.
- Strong empirical evaluation across diverse languages, including non-Latin scripts, with comparisons to strong baselines.
- Shows particular effectiveness in low-resource settings (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), outperforming prior state-of-the-art.
- Robust experimental setup with ablation studies (e.g., removing FreeBase queries) and multi-language training exploration.
- Thorough analyses, including the impact of Wikipedia size on performance, adding depth to method assessment.
- Clear, concise writing and straightforward presentation of methods and results.
- Practical relevance: relies on widely available Wikipedia data, making the approach accessible for many languages.
- Strong baseline comparisons that highlight advantages over methods requiring parallel data or native speaker interaction.
- Weakness: overall performance depends on wikifier accuracy; lacks detailed quantification of how wikifier errors drive NER mistakes.
- Weakness: struggles with entities absent from Wikipedia (OOV entities) and lacks exploration of mitigation strategies.
- Weakness: over-reliance on lexical features in some languages (Tamil, Bengali), indicating a need for feature-class regularization or reweighting.
- Asks for analysis quantifying what percentage of NER errors stem from incorrect wikifications.
- Requests evaluation or ideas for handling non-Wikipedia entities and OOV performance.
- Queries whether the authors tried different regularization or down-weighting of lexical features for low-resource, non-Latin languages.
- Additional comment: improve Figure 2 color scheme for clarity.
- Additional comment: references are well-curated and relevant.
