This paper presents a cross-lingual NER approach leveraging cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The core idea—grounding mentions in any language to English Wikipedia—is compelling and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, particularly for low-resource languages. The paper is well-structured and provides a thorough exploration of the proposed method, including ablation studies and analyses of feature importance.

**Contributions:**

1. **Novel Feature Engineering:** The most significant contribution is the demonstration of cross-lingual wikification as a source of effective, language-independent features for NER. Utilizing Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types obtained through wikification proves to be a strong signal, especially in low-resource scenarios.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides convincing experimental results on both standard CoNLL datasets and a diverse set of low-resource languages, consistently outperforming comparable approaches. This validation demonstrates the practical utility of the proposed method.
3. **Comprehensive Analysis:** The ablation studies and analyses on the impact of Wikipedia size and multi-source training offer valuable insights into the strengths and limitations of the approach. These analyses deepen the understanding of the method and guide potential future research.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear and Concise Presentation:** The paper is written in a clear and understandable style. The core concept is introduced effectively, and the experimental setup is well-described.
2. **Thorough Experimental Evaluation:** The extensive experimental evaluation, covering multiple languages and ablation studies, strengthens the validity of the findings. The inclusion of low-resource languages is particularly commendable.
3. **Practical Relevance:** The approach addresses a significant challenge in NLP – NER for low-resource languages – and offers a practical solution that requires minimal language-specific resources (only a Wikipedia dump).
4. **Insightful Analysis of Wikipedia Size:** The analysis in Section 4.4 clearly links Wikipedia size to feature quality and provides actionable insights for improving performance in low-resource scenarios.
5. **Comparison to Existing Work:** The related work section is well-structured and provides a good overview of existing approaches to cross-lingual NER, effectively positioning the proposed method within the broader research landscape.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Reliance on English Wikipedia:** The method’s dependence on grounding everything to English Wikipedia introduces a potential bias and assumes the completeness and accuracy of the English Wikipedia for all languages. While acknowledged in the discussion, a more in-depth investigation into the potential limitations caused by this reliance would be beneficial.
2. **Wikifier Error Propagation:** The quality of the wikification process directly impacts the performance of the NER model. Errors in wikification can propagate and introduce noise into the features. While the paper shows the importance of Wikipedia size, it doesn’t extensively analyze the *types* of errors made by the wikifier and their impact.
3. **Feature Engineering Complexity:** Although the core idea is elegant, the feature engineering process (especially around wikifier features – Table 1) seems somewhat complex and potentially difficult to replicate. More clarity on the feature selection and combination process would be helpful.
4. **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** The paper doesn’t discuss the computational cost associated with wikifying all n-grams, particularly for larger documents. This is an important consideration for real-world applications.
5. **Monolingual Performance Gains:** While the paper demonstrates improvements in monolingual NER using these features, the magnitude of improvement is relatively small compared to the gains observed in the cross-lingual setting. A more detailed analysis of why these features are less impactful in monolingual scenarios would be useful.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. What steps were taken to mitigate potential biases introduced by relying solely on English Wikipedia?
2. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the wikifier and their impact on the NER performance?
3. Is there a trade-off between the size of 'n' in n-gram wikification and performance? What is the optimal value for 'n' and how was it determined?
4. What is the computational cost associated with wikifying all n-grams (up to n=4) for a typical document?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper could benefit from a brief discussion of potential ethical implications, such as the propagation of biases present in Wikipedia.
*   Consider adding a visualization of the feature space to illustrate how the wikifier features contribute to the separation of different entity types.
*   In Table 2, it would be helpful to include the F1-score for a baseline monolingual NER model trained and tested on each language to provide a clearer benchmark for the improvements achieved by the proposed method.
