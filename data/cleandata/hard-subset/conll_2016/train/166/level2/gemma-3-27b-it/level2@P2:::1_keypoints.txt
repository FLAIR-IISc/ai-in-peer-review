- Proposes cross-lingual wikification to produce language-independent features by grounding mentions to English Wikipedia for NER.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on CoNLL and multiple low-resource languages, often outperforming prior methods.  
- Analyzes impact of Wikipedia size and shows Freebase links are important via ablation studies.  
- Novel idea: using wikification as a feature-engineering step for NER rather than post-processing.  
- Comprehensive evaluation that includes many low-resource languages, strengthening applicability claims.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with detailed experimental setup.  
- Thorough description of feature engineering, including gazetteer and modified n-gram wikifier features.  
- Strong ablation studies that convincingly show component importance (Freebase, interlanguage links).  
- Lacks detailed error analysis to show where and why the model fails (entity types or specific languages).  
- Reliance on English Wikipedia may introduce bias and miss semantic nuances of other languages.  
- Complex feature set may hurt interpretability and risk overfitting; feature importance/selection not fully addressed.  
- Missing comparisons to recent strong baselines using multilingual pre-trained models (mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Asks authors for detailed error analysis highlighting common error patterns and weak languages.  
- Asks about strategies to mitigate English-Wikipedia bias (e.g., multilingual knowledge graphs).  
- Requests feature ablation results to identify the most important features.  
- Requests performance comparison to recent multilingual pre-trained NER methods (mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Suggests clarifying Freebase types in Figure 1.  
- Recommends specifying which features "Wikifier only" includes in Table 2.  
- Suggests using the term "Roman script" instead of "Latin script".
