- Proposes cross-lingual wikification grounding words to English Wikipedia using categories and Freebase types for NER.
- Core idea is compelling and well-motivated, creating language-independent features for cross-lingual transfer.
- Shows significant improvements over existing methods on CoNLL datasets and several low-resource languages.
- Primary contribution: effective direct-transfer NER using wikification without parallel corpora or language-specific features.
- Secondary contribution: extensive evaluation across diverse and low-resource languages demonstrating practical value.
- Shows language-independent features can also enhance monolingual NER systems.
- Strengths: clear presentation, comprehensive experiments, and insightful analysis.
- Ablation studies highlight the impact of components, especially the Freebase connection.
- Multilingual experiments reveal benefits of multi-source training and the influence of Wikipedia size.
- Weakness: only modest gains on some low-resource languages (e.g., Yoruba); needs deeper analysis beyond Wikipedia size.
- Weakness: potential bias from grounding all languages in English Wikipedia is not addressed.
- Weakness: limited discussion of mitigating lexical subtleties; lacks exploration of character-level or subword features.
- Questions for authors: clarify biases from English Wikipedia, explain challenges for languages like Yoruba, and whether character/subword features were considered.
