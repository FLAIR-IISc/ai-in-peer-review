- Proposes cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent NER features by grounding mentions to English Wikipedia.  
- Demonstrates that Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types are strong, language-agnostic signals, especially for low-resource languages.  
- Shows strong empirical results on CoNLL and multiple low-resource languages, outperforming comparable methods.  
- Provides ablation studies and analyses (including Wikipedia size and multi-source training) that clarify method strengths and limitations.  
- Paper is clearly written with a well-described experimental setup.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, covering many languages and including useful ablations.  
- Method is practically relevant for low-resource NER and requires only a Wikipedia dump as an external resource.  
- Analysis links Wikipedia size to feature quality and offers actionable insights for low-resource settings.  
- Related work section effectively situates the approach within existing cross-lingual NER research.  
- Reliance on English Wikipedia may introduce bias and assumes English Wikipedia’s completeness and accuracy for all languages.  
- Errors in the wikification step can propagate noise to NER features; error types and impacts are not deeply analyzed.  
- The wikifier feature engineering appears somewhat complex and may be hard to replicate without clearer feature-selection details.  
- Computational cost of wikifying all n-grams (especially for larger documents) is not discussed.  
- Monolingual performance gains are modest compared to cross-lingual improvements; reasons for this are not fully analyzed.  
- Requests clarification on mitigation of biases from using only English Wikipedia.  
- Asks for a detailed analysis of wikifier error types and their effect on NER performance.  
- Queries about trade-offs and optimal choice of n in n-gram wikification and how it was determined.  
- Requests information on computational cost of wikifying up to n=4 for typical documents.  
- Suggests adding discussion of ethical implications, such as propagation of Wikipedia biases.  
- Recommends visualizing the feature space to show how wikifier features separate entity types.  
- Advises including baseline monolingual F1 scores in Table 2 to better contextualize improvements.
