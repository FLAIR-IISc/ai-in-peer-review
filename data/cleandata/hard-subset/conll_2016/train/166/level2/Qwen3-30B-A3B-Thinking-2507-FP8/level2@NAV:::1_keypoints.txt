- Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to generate language-independent features grounded to English Wikipedia entries.  
- Primary contribution: grounding n-grams to English Wikipedia enables an English-trained model to transfer well across languages without target-language annotations.  
- Secondary contribution: wikification features improve both cross-lingual transfer and monolingual NER systems.  
- Method is truly language-independent, requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps and no parallel data or target annotations.  
- Experiments are comprehensive across high-resource (CoNLL) and low-resource (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) languages.  
- Shows statistically significant gains over strong baselines (e.g., +12.5 F1 on German vs. Täckström et al.).  
- Analysis of how Wikipedia size affects performance offers practical guidance for practitioners.  
- Weakness: does not assess the impact of wikifier inaccuracy, especially for low-resource languages with small Wikipedias.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent multilingual transformer models (e.g., mBERT), limiting positioning against state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness: claim of outperforming Zhang et al. (2016) is underexplained and doesn’t justify why native-speaker interaction is inferior.  
- Needs clearer discussion of trade-offs between wikifier features and lexical features for non-Latin scripts.  
- Unclear whether improvements stem mainly from wikifier features alone or the combined feature set, particularly for languages with large Wikipedia coverage.  
- Asks for wikifier accuracy rates by language, especially low-resource ones.  
- Asks how the method would perform for target languages with no Wikipedia (e.g., endangered languages).
