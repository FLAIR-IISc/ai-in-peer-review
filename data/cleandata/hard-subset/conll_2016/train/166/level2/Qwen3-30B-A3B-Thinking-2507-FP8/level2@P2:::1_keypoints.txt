- Introduces cross-lingual NER using cross-lingual wikification to create language-independent features from Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types, enabling direct transfer without target annotations.  
- Shows these wikification-based features can improve monolingual NER systems across multiple languages.  
- Analyzes how Wikipedia size affects feature quality and demonstrates multi-source training improves results.

- Novel approach requiring no parallel data or language-specific heuristics; wikification provides strong language-independent features.  
- Simple, effective method using only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, supported by experiments.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across high- and low-resource languages with significant gains over strong baselines, especially on low-resource languages.  
- Analysis of Wikipedia size effects clarifies limitations and explains performance gaps between languages.

- Claim of “language-independent features” is misleading since features derive from English Wikipedia and depend on target-language Wikipedia size/quality.  
- Comparison with Täckström et al. is problematic because their method (parallel-text-induced clusters) is orthogonal and the baseline choice isn’t justified.  
- Paper does not adequately explain why it outperforms Zhang et al. despite both using wikification; lacks evidence that feature use for boundary detection vs. types is the key difference.  
- Claim that method “can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia” is overstated given poor performance on very small Wikipedias and no clear solution for languages with no content.

- How would the approach handle languages with no Wikipedia content; could it be combined with projection or automatic data generation?  
- For Yoruba/Bengali/Tamil, how much of the performance gap is due to lack of FreeBase types versus smaller Wikipedia size?  
- Why does training on multiple source languages improve results — more data, better feature coverage, or another reason?
