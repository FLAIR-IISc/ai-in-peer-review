- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification (Wikipedia categories + Freebase types from cross-lingually grounded n-grams) to create language-independent features for direct transfer, improving cross-lingual and monolingual NER and benefiting from multi-source training.

- Strength 1: Shows Wikipedia categories and Freebase types are strong, language-independent NER features, effective even with imperfect disambiguation.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive evaluation across CoNLL and five low-resource languages, with analysis linking wikifier effectiveness to Wikipedia size.

- Strength 3: Method is simple and low-resource, requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps and no parallel text or language-specific heuristics.

- Strength 4: Demonstrates that wikifier features also improve monolingual NER systems.

- Strength 5: Analysis shows training on multiple source languages yields further improvements without major downsides.

- Weakness 1: Insufficiently detailed comparison to Täckström et al. (2012); unclear if gains are due to wikifier features or other model differences.

- Weakness 2: Low-resource evaluation focuses on languages with substantial Wikipedias (e.g., Turkish); unclear performance for languages with very small (<10k) article counts.

- Weakness 3: Lacks discussion and measurements of the computational cost and scalability of the wikification process for large-scale use.

- Weakness 4: Claim that method applies to “all languages in Wikipedia” is not addressed for languages with minimal or no Wikipedia coverage.

- Weakness 5: Baselines omit more recent cross-lingual NER work post-2016, limiting contextual comparison.

- Question 1: Why is Spanish the best training language for Yoruba in Figure 2—due to language-family similarity, data artifacts, or other reasons?

- Question 2: Why does the “Wikifier only” model outperform lexical-feature models for Bengali and Tamil, and would this generalize to other non-Latin script languages?

- Question 3: What are the computational requirements and scalability characteristics of the wikification process across document size and languages?
