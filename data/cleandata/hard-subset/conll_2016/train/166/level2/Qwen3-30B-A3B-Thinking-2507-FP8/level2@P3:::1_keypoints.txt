- Proposes a cross-lingual NER approach using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features.

- FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories derived from wikification provide strong language-independent features for NER.

- These wikification-derived features improve both direct transfer cross-lingual NER and monolingual NER systems.

- Wikipedia size directly affects feature quality, yielding significant improvements for low-resource languages.

- Comprehensive evaluation across 9 languages (high-resource and low-resource non-Latin scripts) shows consistent improvement over strong baselines.

- Analysis of Wikipedia size effects (Table 3) offers useful insights into limitations and deployment requirements.

- Demonstrates that language-independent features also enhance monolingual NER (Table 2), showing broader applicability.

- Method has minimal requirements (only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, no parallel text), making it practical and accessible.

- Lacks sufficient explanation for why performance varies across languages beyond Wikipedia size (e.g., Yoruba vs. Spanish discrepancy).

- Comparison with related work is superficial; more detail on methodological differences and why results improve is needed.

- Does not address potential failure cases from wikifier disambiguation errors, especially when grounding to English Wikipedia may be misleading.

- Evaluation of low-resource languages lacks detailed error analysis to identify the most challenging entity types.

- Why does Spanish outperform English for Yoruba training—linguistic similarity or Wikipedia content differences?

- Why does the "Wikifier only" model work better on Bengali and Tamil despite non-Latin scripts, contrary to the script-based argument?

- How does the system handle missing FreeBase entries for languages like Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil when the wikifier fails to find a FreeBase ID?

- How does this method compare to Wikipedia-based training-data generation approaches (e.g., Nothman et al., 2012) on the same low-resource languages?
