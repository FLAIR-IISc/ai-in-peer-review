**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes a language-independent model for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram. The model works on all languages in Wikipedia and requires only a Wikipedia dump. The main contributions of this paper are:

1. **Cross-lingual wikifier features**: The authors introduce a novel set of features that can be used for NER across languages. These features are obtained by grounding every n-gram to the English Wikipedia, which provides language-independent information for NER.
2. **Direct transfer model**: The authors propose a direct transfer model that can be trained on one language and applied to another language without requiring any parallel data or language-specific features.
3. **Improved performance on low-resource languages**: The authors show that their model outperforms state-of-the-art approaches on low-resource languages, including Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to cross-lingual NER**: The authors propose a new approach to cross-lingual NER that does not require parallel data or language-specific features. This approach has the potential to improve NER performance on low-resource languages.
2. **Improved performance on low-resource languages**: The authors show that their model outperforms state-of-the-art approaches on low-resource languages, which is a significant contribution to the field of NLP.
3. **Extensive experiments**: The authors conduct extensive experiments on multiple languages, including high-resource languages such as English, German, Spanish, and Dutch, as well as low-resource languages such as Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on Wikipedia**: The authors' approach relies heavily on Wikipedia, which may not be available or up-to-date for all languages.
2. **Limited analysis of errors**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of errors made by their model, which could help to identify areas for improvement.
3. **No comparison to other direct transfer models**: The authors do not compare their direct transfer model to other direct transfer models, which could help to establish the effectiveness of their approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the issue of Wikipedia not being available or up-to-date for all languages?
2. Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of errors made by their model, including examples of incorrect predictions and the reasons for these errors?
3. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to other NLP tasks, such as part-of-speech tagging or dependency parsing?

**Additional Comments**

The authors' approach to cross-lingual NER is novel and has the potential to improve NER performance on low-resource languages. However, the dependence on Wikipedia may limit the applicability of this approach to languages with limited Wikipedia coverage. Additionally, the authors could benefit from providing a more detailed analysis of errors made by their model and comparing their direct transfer model to other direct transfer models.
