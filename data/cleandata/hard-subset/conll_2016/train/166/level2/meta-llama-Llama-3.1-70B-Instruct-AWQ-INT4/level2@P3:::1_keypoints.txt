- Summary: Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER using a wikifier that maps every n-gram to English Wikipedia, trained on source languages and evaluated across many languages with significant gains.

- Contribution: Introduces a cross-lingual wikifier that grounds non-English words/phrases to English Wikipedia entries as features.

- Contribution: Proposes a language-independent NER model applicable to all languages covered by Wikipedia.

- Contribution: Evaluates the approach on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) showing notable improvements over baselines.

- Strength: Novel feature extraction that provides language-independent grounding via English Wikipedia.

- Strength: A broadly applicable, language-independent NER model design.

- Strength: Strong empirical results across many languages and in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.

- Weakness: Model quality depends on the test language’s Wikipedia size, limiting performance for low-resource languages.

- Weakness: Limited low-resource evaluation (only five languages); broader evaluation needed.

- Weakness: No comparison to state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER models, making relative performance unclear.

- Question: How does the model perform for languages with limited Wikipedia coverage, and are there plans to extend it to such languages?

- Question: Can the model/feature approach be adapted to other NLP tasks (e.g., POS tagging, dependency parsing)?

- Question: Will the authors release the code and data to facilitate reproducibility?

- Additional comment: Promising approach but requires further evaluation on low-resource languages and mitigation of dependence on Wikipedia coverage.
