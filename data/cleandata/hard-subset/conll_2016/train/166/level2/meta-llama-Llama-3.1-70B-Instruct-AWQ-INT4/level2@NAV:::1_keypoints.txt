- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model that grounds non-English text to English Wikipedia via a cross-lingual wikifier.  
- Produces language-independent features enabling direct transfer of NER models across languages.  
- Evaluates on many languages in monolingual and cross-lingual settings, showing significant gains over strong baselines.  
- Method generates language-independent NER features applicable to all languages covered by Wikipedia.  
- Outperforms comparable approaches on CoNLL (Spanish, German, Dutch) and improves performance on low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Analyzes wikifier feature quality and finds it depends on the target language’s Wikipedia size.  
- Novel approach distinct from traditional cross-lingual NER methods.  
- Strong, wide-ranging experimental evaluation demonstrating improvements over baselines.  
- Provides detailed analysis of results, including wikifier feature assessment.  
- Limited analysis of method limitations, e.g., reliance on large Wikipedia dumps.  
- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER models.  
- Omits discussion of potential applications, especially for low-resource languages.  
- Questions: how to address the large-Wikipedia requirement?  
- Questions: can you compare to state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER models?
