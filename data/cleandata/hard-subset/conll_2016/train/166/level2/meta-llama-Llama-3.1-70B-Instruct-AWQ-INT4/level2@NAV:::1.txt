**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a language-independent model for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) using a cross-lingual wikifier. The model grounds words and phrases in non-English languages to the English Wikipedia, providing language-independent features for transferring an NER model directly. The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings and show significant improvements over strong baselines.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. **Language-independent features**: The authors propose a method to generate language-independent features for NER using a cross-lingual wikifier, which can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia.
2. **Improved performance**: The authors show that their model outperforms comparable approaches on the standard CoNLL datasets (Spanish, German, and Dutch) and also performs well on low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil).
3. **Analysis of wikifier features**: The authors analyze the quality of wikifier features and show that it depends on the Wikipedia size of the test language.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to cross-lingual NER using a cross-lingual wikifier, which is different from traditional methods.
2. **Strong evaluation**: The authors evaluate their model on a wide range of languages and show significant improvements over strong baselines.
3. **Analysis of results**: The authors provide a detailed analysis of their results, including an analysis of the quality of wikifier features.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited analysis of limitations**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of their approach, such as the requirement of a large Wikipedia dump.
2. **No comparison with state-of-the-art models**: The authors do not compare their model with state-of-the-art models in cross-lingual NER.
3. **No discussion of potential applications**: The authors do not discuss potential applications of their approach, such as its use in low-resource languages.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limitation of requiring a large Wikipedia dump for your approach?
2. Can you provide a comparison of your model with state-of-the-art models in cross-lingual NER?
3. What are the potential applications of your approach, and how do you plan to explore them in future work?
