- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to create language-independent features (Wikipedia categories, Freebase types) by grounding n-grams to English Wikipedia, yielding strong transfer and monolingual gains across 9 languages; performance linked to Wikipedia size and interlanguage links.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple, language-agnostic featureization for NER via cross-lingual wikification that enables direct transfer without parallel data.

- Contribution 2: Provides comprehensive evaluation including low-resource languages, multi-source training, and ablations isolating Freebase and interlanguage link effects.

- Contribution 3: Shows the same wikification-derived features also improve monolingual NER.

- Strength 1: Achieves large, consistent cross-lingual gains using only multilingual Wikipedia, outperforming prior direct-transfer baselines on CoNLL and several low-resource languages.

- Strength 2: Features are delexicalized and orthogonal to projection/embedding methods, making them easy to combine with other approaches.

- Strength 3: Solid analyses and ablations (e.g., removing Freebase, downsampling links) convincingly explain performance variation via Wikipedia coverage.

- Strength 4: Demonstrates practical utility by improving monolingual NER in all nine evaluated languages.

- Weakness 1: Heavy reliance on Freebase and Wikipedia coverage harms performance for low-resource languages and raises maintainability issues given Freebase is deprecated.

- Weakness 2: Heuristic label remapping/removal (MISC, TTL, TIME) may bias comparisons and complicate interpretation of cross-lingual scores.

- Weakness 3: Lacks comparisons to modern neural cross-lingual baselines (multilingual embeddings/pretraining), no significance testing, and omits runtime/memory reporting.

- Weakness 4: Robustness to wikifier errors, NIL handling thresholds, and domain shift impacts are acknowledged but not thoroughly quantified.

- Question 1: Can the method be migrated from Freebase to Wikidata types/properties, and are there preliminary results?

- Question 2: What are the runtime and memory costs of wikifying all n-grams per document, and how does inference latency scale with Wikipedia size?

- Question 3: How are NIL/linking decisions thresholded in practice, and how sensitive are results to linker confidence?

- Question 4: Are the reported gains statistically significant across runs, and can significance vs. key baselines be reported?

- Question 5: In multi-source training, how are languages weighted and does curriculum or sample balancing affect results?
