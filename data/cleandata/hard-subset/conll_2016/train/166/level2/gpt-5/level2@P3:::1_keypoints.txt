- Proposes a cross-lingual NER that wikifies every n-gram (n≤4) to English Wikipedia titles and uses Freebase types, Wikipedia categories, and linker confidence as language-independent features in an averaged perceptron BIO model.

- Main contribution 1: Shows wikifier-derived type/category features are strong delexicalized signals that substantially improve both direct transfer and monolingual NER without parallel data.

- Main contribution 2: Presents a practical dense-span cross-lingual wikification setup with candidate/ranker modifications and analyzes the impact of Freebase access and interlanguage link coverage.

- Main contribution 3: Shows multi-source training gives modest additional gains and that the method outperforms prior cross-lingual baselines under similar or weaker resources.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains in monolingual and transfer settings across nine languages, with consistent F1 improvements when adding wikifier features.

- Strength: Outperforms or matches prior cross-lingual baselines (Täckström et al. 2012; Zhang et al. 2016) while requiring no parallel text or target-language annotation.

- Strength: Careful resource-dependence analysis (ablations on Freebase and interlanguage links) explains poor performance on languages with small Wikipedias and provides actionable guidance.

- Strength: General and practical approach needing only multilingual Wikipedia and no language-specific preprocessing; same features help boundary detection and typing.

- Weakness: Heavy dependence on Wikipedia and Freebase coverage; performance degrades for languages with sparse interlanguage links or Freebase types.

- Weakness: Computational and precision concerns from wikifying every n-gram; paper omits runtime, throughput, NIL-rate stats, and rejection thresholds.

- Weakness: Label alignment/evaluation choices (remapping/dropping MISC) complicate comparability with prior CoNLL results and may affect reported scores.

- Weakness: Scope of baselines is limited—no contemporaneous neural cross-lingual baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF with multilingual embeddings/clusters) for comparison or combination.

- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps in feature template specifics, NIL rejection, overlapping-span handling, and training hyperparameters; code release urged.

- Question: What proportion of tokens/spans receive candidate links per language and what are NIL rates; how is linker confidence threshold chosen and how sensitive are results?

- Question: What is end-to-end runtime for wikifying every n-gram and how does it scale with Wikipedia size; are there pruning strategies that retain most gains?

- Question: What is the marginal utility of longer n-grams (n=2,3,4) versus unigrams—any ablation by n?

- Question: In multi-source training, are languages weighted equally or by data size, and does weighting by similarity improve results?

- Question: For low-resource scripts lacking Freebase coverage, have Wikidata or category-only features been tested as substitutes?

- Additional comment: Report per-entity-type F1 (PER/LOC/ORG) and statistical significance to strengthen empirical claims.

- Additional comment: Augmenting wikifier features with graph-based signals (relations, page text) is promising and worth piloting.

- Additional comment: Combining these features with multilingual embeddings or cluster features could improve robustness when Wikipedia coverage is thin.
