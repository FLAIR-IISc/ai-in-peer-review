- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER using wikification to map target-language n-grams (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia titles and extract Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as features.  
- Trains on one or multiple source languages and directly applies to targets without parallel data or target annotations.  
- Shows wikifier-derived features enable effective direct transfer across languages.  
- Evaluates on nine languages (including five low-resource) with ablations and analyses.  
- Outperforms prior direct-transfer baselines (e.g., Täckström et al.) on CoNLL and is competitive with Zhang et al. (2016) in low-resource settings while using fewer resources.  
- Analyzes dependence on Wikipedia size and Freebase coverage and shows multi-source training gives small consistent gains.  
- Demonstrates wikifier features also improve monolingual NER.  
- Conceptually simple and resource-lean: no parallel data needed, only multilingual Wikipedia, with sensible n-gram wikifier modifications.  
- Strong empirical results with thorough feature ablations; wikifier features consistently help, especially for Latin-script targets.  
- Competitive comparisons to older baselines and bitext-based clusters show substantial gains.  
- Informative analyses (coverage ablations, train–test matrix) give guidance on source selection.  
- Broad relevance: approach applies to all languages in Wikipedia and can complement other transfer methods.  
- Reliance on Wikipedia size and Freebase coverage causes degraded performance for small Wikipedias (e.g., Yoruba) and limits applicability to very low-resource languages.  
- Uses Freebase (deprecated) and does not explore replacements like Wikidata.  
- Label-set adjustments (dropping/remapping entity types) complicate comparability; per-type breakdowns or standardized sets are needed.  
- Baselines could be stronger—lacks comparison to modern neural transfer methods (cross-lingual embeddings, BiLSTM-CRF).  
- Missing implementation details hinder reproducibility (wikifier thresholds, overlapping n-gram handling, tokenization, NIL handling, hyperparameters, gazetteer cleaning).  
- No statistical significance reporting for improvements.  
- Computational and error-propagation concerns: wikifying all n-grams may be expensive and noisy; paper lacks runtime/memory analysis and detailed error modes.  
- Questions: can Freebase be replaced with Wikidata or other KBs, and are there preliminary results?  
- Questions: what are runtime/memory footprints, how are overlapping n-grams/conflicts/pruning handled, and are confidence thresholds used?  
- Questions: frequency and handling of NILs, namespace filtering for categories/types, and sensitivity to top-1 vs top-2 candidates?  
- Questions: tokenization/segmentation strategy for non-Latin scripts and its effect on recall?  
- Questions: report per-entity-type F1 and clarify label-set modifications’ impact; any experiments keeping MISC or TTL/TIME?  
- Questions: have wikifier features been combined with cross-lingual embeddings or modern neural NER architectures?  
- Questions: in multi-source training, how are languages weighted/balanced and were sampling/adaptive weighting strategies tried?  
- Suggest migrating from Freebase to Wikidata, adding detailed error analysis (boundary vs type errors), and releasing code/models for reproducibility.
