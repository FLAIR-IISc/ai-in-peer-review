- Proposes a language-agnostic NER featureization: ground every n-gram (n ≤ 4) to English Wikipedia and use Freebase types + Wikipedia categories as delexicalized features for zero-shot transfer.  
- Enables direct transfer without parallel data or target-language annotation.  
- Empirical study across nine languages shows substantial direct-transfer gains on CoNLL, competitiveness with weakly supervised methods on low-resource languages, and consistent gains when augmenting monolingual models.  
- Analyzes dependence of performance on English–target Wikipedia intersection and finds multi-source training typically helps.  
- Strength: conceptually clean and generally applicable—cross-lingual entity types/categories are intuitive and orthogonal to projection/embedding methods.  
- Strength: strong empirical improvements on English→Dutch/German/Spanish with ablations isolating each feature group's contribution.  
- Strength: broad evaluation across scripts with informative monolingual vs. transfer and ablation studies.  
- Strength: practical guidance that wikifier-only may outperform lexicalized features for non-Latin scripts.  
- Strength: multi-source training yields modest but generally positive benefits.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on English–target interlanguage links and Freebase typing; performance drops sharply as intersection shrinks and Freebase coverage gaps hurt some languages.  
- Weakness: reproducibility/future-applicability concern because Freebase is sunset; recommends moving to Wikidata.  
- Weakness: evaluation protocol alters tagsets across languages (e.g., TIME/TTL/MISC handling), complicating comparability and requiring per-type F1 reporting.  
- Weakness: comparisons to prior work (Täckström et al., Zhang et al.) are unclear or potentially unfair; should report best-per-language configurations.  
- Weakness: insufficient wikifier details (feature set, thresholds, NIL handling, ranker training) and missing runtime/memory costs for grounding all n-grams.  
- Weakness: missing modern baselines (cross-lingual embeddings, neural NER) and no statistical significance testing for reported gains.  
- Weakness: unclear, non-principled feature-selection/tuning per language; aggregate numbers may conflate configuration choices.  
- Question: clarify column mapping in Table 2 and confirm comparisons (e.g., Spanish vs. bitext clusters) and best configurations per language.  
- Question: provide training data, features, code, and frozen KB snapshot for the modified wikifier and explain NIL handling.  
- Question: report run-time costs, memory usage, and any pruning heuristics for grounding n-grams (n ≤ 4).  
- Question: how robust are results if Freebase is replaced by Wikidata instance-of/subclass-of types, and would this improve low-resource languages?  
- Question: provide per-entity-type F1 and analysis of boundary vs. type errors to clarify where wikifier features help/hurt.  
- Suggestion: report a regression of F1 vs. English–target intersection size to quantify dependency.  
- Suggestion: run oracle ablations using only types vs. only categories to determine dominant signal.  
- Suggestion: consider CRF or constrained decoding to avoid invalid BIO sequences from greedy decoding.  
- Minor: fix terminology/typos (Freebase capitalization, footnotes, “languageindependent”) and release code, gazetteers, and wikifier resources to increase impact.
