- Proposes cross-lingual wikifier features for direct-transfer NER, grounding n-grams to English Wikipedia and using categories/Freebase types as language-independent features.
- Requires only a multilingual Wikipedia dump (no parallel corpora) to create powerful pivot features.
- Demonstrates substantial performance gains in direct-transfer NER across high- and low-resource languages and improves monolingual NER as well.
- Provides extensive evaluation across nine languages and writing systems, establishing a new state-of-the-art for direct-transfer NER under the tested constraints.
- Shows performance correlates with target-language Wikipedia size and benefits from multi-source training.
- Novel and simple idea that reverses the typical NER-then-linking pipeline and leverages widely available Wikipedia resources.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms Täckström et al. (2012) on CoNLL and Zhang et al. (2016) on several low-resource languages without needing parallel data.
- Thorough analysis and evaluation, including non-Latin scripts and experiments limiting inter-language links to explain performance differences.
- Dependency on wikifier quality: performance hinges on the underlying cross-lingual wikifier and Wikipedia coverage.
- Lacks qualitative error analysis to show how wikification failures propagate to specific NER error types (e.g., boundaries vs. types).
- Uses an older linear classifier; should discuss or demonstrate how wikifier features integrate with modern neural architectures (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF).
- Feature representation description (Section 3.3) is unclear and would benefit from concrete token-level examples for replicability.
- No experimental comparison to projection-based cross-lingual methods; should discuss trade-offs when small parallel corpora are available.
