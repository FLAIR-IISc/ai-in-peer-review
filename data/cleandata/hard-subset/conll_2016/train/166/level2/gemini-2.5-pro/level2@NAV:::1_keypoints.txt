- Proposes a direct-transfer cross-lingual NER method that wikifies n-grams to English Wikipedia and uses Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features.  
- Core contribution: a novel, effective feature-generation technique using cross-lingual wikification that removes the need for parallel corpora.  
- Empirical contribution: extensive experiments across nine languages (including low-resource, non-Latin scripts) showing strong improvements over direct-transfer baselines.  
- Novelty and elegance: simple yet powerful reversal of the usual pipeline—wikify first, then use language-agnostic features for NER.  
- Strong empirical performance: large F1 gains on CoNLL datasets (+12–15 over Täckström) and outperformance of resource-heavy methods on several low-resource languages.  
- Thorough analysis: ablation study linking feature quality to target-language Wikipedia size and explaining cross-language performance variation.  
- Dependency on the wikifier: method relies entirely on an external wikification system and lacks analysis of how wikifier errors affect NER.  
- Computational concerns: wikifying every n-gram (up to n=4) may be expensive; paper omits inference-time and cost comparisons.  
- Non-Latin script handling: lexical features hurt non-Latin languages and the proposed ad-hoc fix (use only wikifier features) is not a principled solution.
