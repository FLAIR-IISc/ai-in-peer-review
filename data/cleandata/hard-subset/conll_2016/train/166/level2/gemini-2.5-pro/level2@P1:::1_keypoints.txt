- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER by wikifying n-grams to English Wikipedia and using Wikipedia categories/FreeBase types as language-independent features; trained on high-resource language and directly transferred to nine languages with strong gains.

- Contribution 1: Novel method using cross-lingual wikification as delexicalized NER features, grounding surface forms to a shared KB.

- Contribution 2: Thorough empirical study across nine diverse languages with analyses (e.g., impact of Wikipedia size) showing effectiveness and limitations.

- Strength 1: Elegant, simple inversion of the NER→wikification pipeline to produce truly language-agnostic features.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical performance, outperforming strong baselines on CoNLL and multiple low-resource languages.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive and insightful analysis (ablation studies, simulated low-resource experiments) that identifies Wikipedia size as a key factor.

- Strength 4: Broad practicality—no parallel corpora required and applicable wherever a target-language Wikipedia exists.

- Weakness 1: Performance capped by wikifier and Wikipedia quality; needs more qualitative analysis of wikifier failure modes and resulting NER errors.

- Weakness 2: Unclear handling of out-of-knowledge-base entities (non-notable/local entities); lacks in-KB vs out-of-KB performance analysis.

- Weakness 3: Dense wikification of all n-grams may introduce noise and computational cost; paper lacks discussion of signal-to-noise ratio and filtering.

- Question 1: How does the model recognize or generalize to entities that cannot be grounded to Wikipedia?

- Question 2: Did you try strategies (e.g., different regularization) to mitigate harmful lexical transfer for typologically distant/non-Latin languages?

- Question 3: What are the runtime costs and scalability of wikifying every n-gram compared to baselines without wikifier features?
