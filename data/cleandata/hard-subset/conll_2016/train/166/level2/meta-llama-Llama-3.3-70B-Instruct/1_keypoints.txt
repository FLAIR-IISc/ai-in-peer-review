- Proposes a language-independent NER method using a cross-lingual wikifier that maps words/phrases to English Wikipedia.
- Model operates on all Wikipedia languages and requires only a Wikipedia dump as resource.
- Evaluated across many languages (monolingual and cross-lingual) and shows significant improvements over strong baselines.
- Introduces language-independent features produced by grounding to English Wikipedia via a cross-lingual wikifier.
- Proposes a cross-lingual NER model applicable to all Wikipedia languages, outperforming comparable approaches on CoNLL and low-resource datasets.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on low-resource languages (e.g., Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) with limited annotated data.
- Effectively leverages Wikipedia as a source for creating language-independent features.
- Demonstrates strong performance gains on low-resource languages that lack annotated training data.
- Offers flexibility to train on multiple source languages to augment annotated training data.
- Performance depends on the size/coverage of the target language’s Wikipedia, limiting effectiveness for small Wikipedias.
- Only uses Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types, leaving other signals (document words, title relations) unexplored.
- Acknowledges need for further work to incorporate other multilingual NER techniques (parallel projection, auto-generated Wikipedia training data).
