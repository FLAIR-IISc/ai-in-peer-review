- Proposes a cross-lingual NER model using language-independent features from cross-lingual wikification grounding to English Wikipedia entries.  
- Extracts Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as features, eliminating need for parallel corpora or language-specific rules.  
- Shows robustness across nine languages, including low-resource settings, and improves performance in direct transfer and monolingual NER with multi-source training.  
- Novel use of cross-lingual wikification as a generalizable feature-extraction mechanism requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in direct transfer, especially for low-resource languages with little lexical overlap.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse high- and low-resource languages, with detailed feature analyses and assessment of Wikipedia size impact.  
- Practical utility: leverages widely available resources (Wikipedia, FreeBase) and can enhance existing monolingual models.  
- Performance heavily depends on target-language Wikipedia size and quality, limiting gains for languages with small Wikipedias (e.g., Yoruba, Tamil).  
- Limited comparison to hybrid alternatives (projection-based methods or silver-standard data), leaving relative strengths underexplored.  
- Reproducibility concerns due to absence of publicly available code or pre-trained models.  
- Weaker handling of non-Latin scripts (e.g., Bengali, Tamil) without proposed concrete solutions.  
- Question: How does the method handle languages with extremely small or no Wikipedia; can Wikidata or other knowledge graphs supplement features?  
- Question: Have multilingual embeddings (mBERT, XLM-R) been considered as additional features to address non-Latin script challenges?  
- Question: Can the method be extended to domain-specific NER where Wikipedia coverage is sparse?  
- Overall recommendation: Acceptance with minor revisions to address reproducibility and scalability concerns.
