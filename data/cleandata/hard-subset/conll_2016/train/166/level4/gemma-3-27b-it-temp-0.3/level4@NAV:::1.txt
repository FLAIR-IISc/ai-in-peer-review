This paper introduces a novel approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition leveraging features derived from Wikipedia. The method utilizes a cross-lingual Wikifier to map phrases in a target language to corresponding English Wikipedia articles, and then incorporates features extracted from these articles. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this new feature set, improving performance in both monolingual and, notably, direct transfer scenarios where a model trained on English is evaluated on other languages. I found this to be a strong contribution; the paper presents a new feature for NER and provides a comprehensive evaluation of its benefits. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly insightful.

However, the method’s behavior with respect to named entities not present in Wikipedia warrants further investigation. Reporting results on such entities would provide a more complete picture of the approach’s limitations. 

Furthermore, the reliance on a cross-lingual Wikifier introduces a potential source of error. It would be valuable to understand the impact of Wikifier errors on the final NER predictions – what is the frequency of prediction errors attributable to inaccuracies in the Wikification step?

Finally, the observed performance degradation on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included suggests a potential issue of over-reliance on these features. Exploring differential regularization of feature classes could mitigate this, preventing the model from being unduly influenced by lexical information in these cases.
