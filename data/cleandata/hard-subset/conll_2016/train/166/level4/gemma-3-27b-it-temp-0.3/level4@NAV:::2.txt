This paper investigates cross-lingual NER transfer leveraging a recent cross-lingual wikification model. While the core concept of combining direct transfer with cross-lingual wikification is not groundbreaking, the work represents a valuable incremental contribution by effectively integrating these two research areas. The paper is generally well-structured and presents a clear narrative, supported by sufficient experimentation and compelling results. However, some aspects of the presentation could be improved.

Regarding related work, a more thorough discussion of the connection to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) is needed. Furthermore, the paper should explicitly delineate its differences from other NER systems that utilize Wikipedia knowledge, even though these distinctions are currently mentioned; emphasizing them would enhance clarity and contextualization.

The authors’ rationale for excluding POS tags is understandable, but including experiments with POS tag features, similar to those conducted by Tackstrom et al., would provide valuable empirical insight into their utility across different languages – particularly those with available universal POS tagsets.

Section 3.3 would benefit from a concrete example to clarify the functionality of the Tsai and Roth model. The current description lacks sufficient detail for complete understanding.

Given the authors’ assertion that their approach is orthogonal to and combinable with methods like those of Tackstrom et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012), presenting preliminary results combining these models across a selection of languages would strengthen the discussion. Similarly, a comparison against a strong projection baseline would further contextualize the results and demonstrate the benefits of a wikification-based approach.

The observed language-specific transfer performance – Dutch to Spanish and Spanish to Yoruba – warrants further investigation. Exploring potential reasons beyond statistical chance, and dedicating a paragraph or two to this analysis, would be insightful.

The improvements reported in Table 4 are modest and not consistently observed. Performing a statistical significance test on these results would provide stronger support for the claims.

Minor comments:

- Section 2.1: The discussion of projection should acknowledge methods that do not rely on parallel data, such as those by Peirsman and Pado (NAACL 2009) and Vulic and Moens (EMNLP 2013), which utilize bilingual semantic spaces.
- The paper would benefit from careful proofreading to correct several identified typos (e.g., “as a the base model” in Section 3, “They avoid the traditional pipeline of NER then EL by…” on Page 3, and “to disambiguate every n-grams” on Page 8).
