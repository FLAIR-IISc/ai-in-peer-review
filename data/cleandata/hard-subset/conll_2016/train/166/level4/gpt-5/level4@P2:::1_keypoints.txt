- Proposes a cross-lingual NER method that wikifies every n-gram (n ≤ 4), maps mentions to English Wikipedia, and uses Wikipedia categories and Freebase types as delexicalized features.
- Demonstrates wikifier-derived features improve both monolingual NER and direct transfer across nine languages, including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.
- Examines multi-source training and analyzes how Wikipedia size and KB coverage affect performance.
- Contribution: simple, language-agnostic, delexicalized feature set for NER effective in monolingual and cross-lingual settings.
- Contribution: extensive empirical evaluation across nine languages with ablations and analyses (e.g., impact of Wikipedia size and Freebase coverage).
- Contribution: shows multi-source training modestly improves cross-lingual NER and wikifier features consistently help monolingual models.
- Strength: low-resource assumptions and generalizable idea—requires only multilingual Wikipedia and no target annotation or language-specific tools.
- Strength: strong empirical coverage and careful ablations showing consistent gains from wikifier features, including large direct-transfer F1 improvements.
- Strength: analyzes failure modes and resource sensitivity, linking performance drops to KB/interlanguage-link coverage.
- Strength: multi-source training generally helps or does not hurt, indicating robustness of features.
- Strength: practical utility—wikifier features complement lexical and gazetteer features in monolingual NER.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on wikifier and KB coverage; performance drops for small Wikipedias and long-tail entities.
- Weakness: lacks quantified analysis for NIL/OOV entities and per-mention in-KB vs out-of-KB performance breakdowns.
- Weakness: upstream wikification may cause error propagation; no quantification or exploration of joint NER–EL modeling or backoff strategies.
- Weakness: feature-class imbalance in transfer to non-Latin scripts (lexical features can harm), with no attempted remedies like group regularization.
- Weakness: practicality and efficiency concerns—no runtime, throughput, or memory reporting for wikifying all n-grams.
- Question: report fraction of gold entities covered in Wikipedia/Freebase and in-KB vs out-of-KB performance and NIL/low-confidence rates.
- Question: quantify NER errors due to wikifier mistakes (e.g., oracle experiment with gold links).
- Question: did you try feature-group regularization or per-language rescaling to prevent over-reliance on lexical cues in transfer?
- Question: provide preprocessing/inference costs (tokens/sec), average candidates per n-gram, memory usage, and sensitivity to n-gram max length.
- Question: can the method port to Wikidata (Freebase deprecated) and yield similar improvements?
- Question: in multi-source training, how are examples balanced across languages and are there source/domain effects on transfer?
- Overall: well-executed, language-agnostic approach with broad validation and clear benefits, but limited by KB dependency, missing NIL/error-propagation analyses, and lack of runtime reporting; reviewer leans positive.
