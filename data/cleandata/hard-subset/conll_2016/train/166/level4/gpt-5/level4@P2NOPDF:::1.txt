This paper presents a multilingual named entity recognition approach that augments models with features derived from Wikipedia. Using a cross-lingual wikifier, spans in a target language are linked to English Wikipedia pages, and features from the linked entries are incorporated into the NER model. Experiments indicate that these Wikipedia-based features improve performance both in monolingual settings and, more notably, in direct cross-lingual transfer, where a model trained on English is evaluated on a different target language.

Overall, I enjoyed this paper. The feature design is novel and well-motivated, and the empirical evaluation is fairly thorough. The analyses on low-resource scenarios and non-Latin-script languages are especially compelling.

I have a few questions and suggestions:

- Coverage for unseen entities: How does the method handle entities that are missing from Wikipedia? It would be helpful to include a breakdown or analysis of performance on mentions that do not receive a Wikipedia link, along with coverage statistics, to understand the method’s behavior when Wikipedia does not contain the entity.

- Error propagation from wikification: The approach relies heavily on accurate cross-lingual wikification. To what extent do wikifier errors affect NER predictions? Please quantify this dependence, for example by reporting the proportion of incorrect or missing links, and the corresponding impact on NER. An ablation with oracle links (where possible) or robustness experiments with noisy links would clarify the sensitivity of the model to this component.

- Regularization of feature classes: In the direct-transfer experiments on Tamil and Bengali, adding lexical features appears to hurt performance. Could the model benefit from differential regularization across feature groups to prevent over-reliance on lexical cues (e.g., stronger penalties or dropout for lexical features relative to Wikipedia-derived features)? This might stabilize transfer when lexical signals are less reliable across languages.

In summary, the paper introduces a clear and useful idea with solid empirical support. Addressing the above points—especially the treatment of out-of-Wikipedia entities and the quantification of wikifier-induced errors—would strengthen the claims and clarify the method’s robustness in truly low-resource and cross-lingual settings.
