- Contribution is incremental with limited conceptual novelty, mainly integrating direct-transfer NER and cross-lingual wikification.  
- Paper is well organized with clear narrative, substantial experiments, and persuasive results.  
- Expand related work: provide detailed comparison to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and other Wikipedia-derived NER systems to clarify distinctions.  
- Although POS features were omitted and justified, report experiments with universal POS tags (e.g., Tackström et al. style) for empirical insight.  
- Section 3.3 is unclear; include a concrete running example to show how the modified Tsai and Roth model operates.  
- Since Tackström et al. and Nothman et al. are orthogonal, include preliminary combinations with those approaches to strengthen contributions.  
- Add a comparison to a strong projection-based baseline (including non-parallel projection methods) to better situate wikification results.  
- Analyze language-pair anomalies (e.g., why Dutch best for Spanish, Spanish for Yoruba) to determine if findings are statistical quirks or have substantive explanations.  
- Table 4 gains are modest and inconsistent; report statistical significance tests to support claims.  
- Minor: acknowledge projection methods that do not need parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vuletic/Vulic & Moens 2013) in Sect. 2.1.  
- Minor: fix multiple typos and awkward sentences (several examples noted in Sections 2–3 and Page 8).
