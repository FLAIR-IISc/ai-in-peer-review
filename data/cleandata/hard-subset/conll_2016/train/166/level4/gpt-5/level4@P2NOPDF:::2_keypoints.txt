- Studies cross-lingual direct transfer for NER using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; solid incremental contribution combining direct transfer and wikification.  
- Central idea not highly novel but paper is clear, coherent, and supported by sufficient experiments and evidence.  
- Related work: explain relationship to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) in more detail.  
- Related work: more explicitly differentiate from prior NER systems that leverage Wikipedia/encyclopedic knowledge.  
- POS features: run and report experiments adding POS features (e.g., as in Tackström et al.) for languages with universal POS to evaluate their utility.  
- Section 3.3: provide a running, step-by-step example to clarify the modified Tsai & Roth–based model.  
- Combining methods: include preliminary combined results with orthogonal approaches (Täckström et al., Nothman et al.) and add a strong projection-based baseline for better comparison.  
- Language transfer patterns: analyze why uncommon training→target pairs (Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) perform best—consider typology, Wikipedia coverage, script, entity distribution, or chance.  
- Table 4: gains are modest and inconsistent; report statistical significance testing to substantiate claims.  
- Minor methodological note: mention projection without parallel data and cite Peirsman & Pado (2009) and Vulić & Moens (2013).  
- Minor edits: careful proofreading needed—several unclear sentences/typos (e.g., “as a the base model,” unclear sentence on Page 3, “to disambiguate every n-grams”).
