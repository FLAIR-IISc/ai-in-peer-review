This paper introduces a multilingual named entity recognition approach that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. Using a cross-lingual wikification component, it links phrases in a target language to English Wikipedia pages and extracts features from the corresponding entries. Experiments indicate that this feature improves performance not only in monolingual settings but also in the more interesting direct transfer scenario, where a model trained on English is evaluated on a target language.

I liked this paper. It proposes a novel feature for NER and presents a fairly comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating its utility. The analysis on low-resource languages and those with non-Latin scripts is particularly compelling.

That said, what about named entities that are absent from Wikipedia? In addition to the reported results, it would be valuable to see an analysis of how such entities are impacted by the proposed method.

The approach is also highly dependent on the cross-lingual wikifier. With this extra component in the pipeline, how frequently do errors in wikification lead to downstream prediction errors?

Given the degraded direct transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, it would be worth exploring whether different feature classes could be regularized separately to prevent the model from over-relying on lexical features.
