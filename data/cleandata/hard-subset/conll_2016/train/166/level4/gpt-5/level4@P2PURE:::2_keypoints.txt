1. Addresses cross-lingual direct transfer for NER using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; a solid incremental contribution combining direct transfer and wikification.

2. Well-motivated and clearly presented with sufficient experiments and generally convincing empirical results despite limited novelty.

3. Related work needs stronger comparison to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and clearer distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.

4. POS features: authors should report experiments adding POS tags (e.g., in Tackström et al. style) to empirically assess their utility across languages.

5. Section 3.3 is unclear—add a running example to clarify how the modified Tsai and Roth model operates.

6. Orthogonal methods/baselines: include preliminary combined results with Tackström et al. and Nothman et al., and compare to a strong projection baseline to better contextualize strengths/limits.

7. Language pairing observations need analysis (e.g., why Dutch best trains Spanish and Spanish best trains Yoruba)—determine if artifact or meaningful pattern.

8. Table 4 gains are small and inconsistent; report statistical significance tests to strengthen claims.

9. Minor: note projection approaches without parallel data (Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013) and fix several typos/proofreading issues (examples provided).
