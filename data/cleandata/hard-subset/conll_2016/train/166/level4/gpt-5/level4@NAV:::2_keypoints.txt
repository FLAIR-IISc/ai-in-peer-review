- Central idea is incremental rather than novel, combining direct transfer for downstream NLP with recent cross-lingual wikification.  
- Paper presents a coherent narrative with sufficient experiments and convincing empirical results.  
- Related work section needs stronger comparison to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and clearer differentiation from other Wikipedia-derived NER systems.  
- Authors should report experiments including POS tag features (e.g., as in Täckström et al.) to assess their utility across languages.  
- Section 3.3 is unclear and would benefit from a running example to explain the modified Tsai and Roth model.  
- Provide preliminary results combining this method with orthogonal approaches (Täckström et al., Nothman et al.) to show complementarity.  
- Include a comparison against a strong projection baseline to better contextualize wikification-based strengths and limitations.  
- Explain or analyze why Dutch is the best source for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba (statistical artifact or deeper reason).  
- Table 4 shows only small, inconsistent improvements; report statistical significance tests to substantiate claims.  
- Note that projection can be done without parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013) and cite relevant work.  
- Address multiple typos and proofreading issues throughout the paper for clarity.
