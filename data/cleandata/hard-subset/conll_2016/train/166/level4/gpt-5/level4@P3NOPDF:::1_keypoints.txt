- Proposes multilingual NER that augments standard features with English Wikipedia-derived features via cross-lingual wikification.  
- Demonstrates improved performance in monolingual and especially zero-shot (English→target) transfer settings.  
- Particularly strong gains for low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.  
- Clear, practical idea: import rich, language-agnostic knowledge through cross-lingual entity linking.  
- Solid empirical evaluation across multiple languages and transfer scenarios.  
- Shows Wikipedia-derived features are complementary to standard NER features.  
- Coverage/long-tail concern: unclear performance on mentions not present in Wikipedia or on rare/unpopular entities.  
- Need stratified analysis by linkability, entity frequency/popularity, and entity types to attribute gains.  
- Wikifier error propagation: success depends on linking quality; errors may cause wrong types, boundaries, or false positives.  
- Recommend controlled studies (gold links subset, confidence thresholds, back-off) to quantify dependency on linking quality.  
- Over-reliance on lexical features in transfer (Tamil/Bengali degradation); suggests overfitting to source-language surface forms.  
- Proposed mitigations: per-feature-group regularization, feature dropout/stochastic masking, character/subword features, adversarial/domain-invariant training, and target dev-set calibration.  
- Practical deployment question: runtime, throughput, model size, and pipeline complexity of cross-lingual wikification are not discussed.  
- Suggested experiments to strengthen paper: breakdown by linkability/popularity, error analysis isolating wikifier-induced errors with mitigations, ablations for feature-group regularization/dropout, and gold-link upper-bound results.  
- Overall: well-motivated and empirically grounded with notable low-resource results; main concerns (coverage and error propagation) are addressable, leaning toward acceptance pending additional diagnostics and clarifications.
