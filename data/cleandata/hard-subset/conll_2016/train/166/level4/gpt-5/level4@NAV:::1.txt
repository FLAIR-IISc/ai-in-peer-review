This paper introduces a multilingual named entity recognition approach that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. By using a cross-lingual Wikifier, it links phrases in a target language to English Wikipedia articles and extracts features from the associated entries. Experiments indicate that this new feature improves performance not only in the monolingual setting but also in the more compelling direct transfer scenario, where an English-trained model is evaluated on a target language.

I found this paper strong. It presents a novel feature for named entity recognition and provides a fairly comprehensive experimental study demonstrating its benefits. The analysis on low-resource and non-Latin languages is particularly noteworthy.

But what about named entities that are absent from Wikipedia? In addition to the reported results, it would be useful to see how such entities are affected by the proposed approach.

The method is highly dependent on the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier. With this extra step in the pipeline, how frequently do prediction errors stem from Wikifier mistakes?

Given the poor direct transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included, I wonder whether one could regularize different feature classes separately, so the model does not become overly reliant on lexical features.
