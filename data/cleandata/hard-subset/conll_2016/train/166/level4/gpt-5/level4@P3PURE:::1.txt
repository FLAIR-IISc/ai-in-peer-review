This paper proposes a multilingual named entity recognition (NER) approach that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. Using a cross-lingual Wikifier, it links phrases in a target language to their corresponding English Wikipedia articles and incorporates features based on those entries. Experiments indicate that this feature improves performance not only in monolingual settings but also in the more challenging direct transfer scenario, where a model trained on English is applied to a target language.

Overall, I found the paper strong and compelling. It introduces a novel feature for NER and presents a fairly thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating its utility. The analysis on low-resource and non-Latin scripts is particularly interesting.

That said, I have several questions and suggestions:
- Coverage for out-of-Wikipedia entities: What happens to named entities that are not present in Wikipedia? In addition to the reported overall results, it would be informative to isolate and report performance specifically on such entities to assess how the proposed method affects them.
- Dependence on the cross-lingual Wikifier: The approach hinges on the success of the cross-lingual Wikifier. With this additional component in the pipeline, how often do prediction errors stem from Wikifier errors? Some quantitative error attribution (e.g., error rates or a breakdown of failures traceable to Wikifier mistakes) would clarify this dependency.
- Feature regularization in direct transfer: Given the poor direct-transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included, can the model regularize different feature classes separately to reduce over-reliance on lexical features? Exploring differential regularization (or related techniques) could help retain the benefits of the Wikipedia-based features while mitigating adverse effects from lexical features in cross-lingual transfer.
