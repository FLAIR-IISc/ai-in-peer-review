This paper presents a cross-lingual named entity recognition approach that derives features from Wikipedia. Using a cross-lingual wikifier, it maps phrases in a target language to English Wikipedia pages and leverages properties of those pages as features. Experiments indicate that these features boost performance not only in monolingual settings but, more importantly, in direct transfer where a model trained on English is applied to other languages.

I found the work compelling. It introduces a simple, language-agnostic feature set for NER and supports its utility with a broad and careful experimental study. The analysis on low-resource and non-Latin-script languages is particularly insightful.

What about entities that are absent from Wikipedia? Beyond the reported results, it would be useful to quantify how such cases affect the proposed method’s precision and recall. The approach also relies heavily on the cross-lingual wikifier; how frequently do wikification errors lead to downstream NER mistakes, and can you attribute errors to the wikifier versus the NER model? Given the poor direct-transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included, could you regularize or weight feature groups differently to avoid over-emphasizing lexical features?
