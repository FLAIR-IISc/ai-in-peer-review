- Tackles cross-lingual NER in direct-transfer setting using a cross-lingual wikifier to produce language-independent n-gram features (Wikipedia categories, Freebase types, ranker scores).  
- Main contributions: showing wikification yields strong delexicalized features that improve direct-transfer NER, an extensive nine-language empirical study, and practical adaptations to support dense n-gram grounding.  
- Strong empirical evidence across CoNLL and five low-resource languages with consistent and often large F1 gains from wikifier features.  
- Resource-light approach: only requires multilingual Wikipedia and outperforms methods using parallel data or manual effort.  
- Insightful analyses of Wikipedia size and English–target intersections explaining weaker gains on very low-resource Wikipedias.  
- Features are delexicalized, broadly applicable, and orthogonal to other cross-lingual techniques, enabling potential combinations.  
- Multi-source training exploration shows adding annotated data in other languages generally helps and rarely harms, though gains are modest.  
- Limited novelty: primarily an empirical/integrative contribution combining known ideas (direct transfer + wikification).  
- Clarity gap in Section 3.3: modifications to the Tsai & Roth wikifier and the n-gram feature extraction process are not fully transparent.  
- Missing baselines and combinations that would strengthen claims: POS-feature variant, a strong projection baseline, and combinations with multilingual word clusters or silver Wikipedia data.  
- Statistical rigor concern: small and inconsistent improvements (e.g., Table 4) need significance testing.  
- Related work positioning should better articulate differences from prior Wikipedia-driven NER (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007).  
- Language-pair findings (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) require deeper analysis to rule out chance and explain causes (script, domain, linkage density, training composition).  
- Request for a concrete example in Section 3.3 showing n-gram candidate generation, filtering, linking, and how categories/types/scores become token features.  
- Ask for evaluation of POS features where universal POS is available to quantify their impact.  
- Request preliminary results combining wikifier features with multilingual word clusters and/or silver Wikipedia-derived training data.  
- Ask for comparison to a strong projection system under comparable resources.  
- Ask whether multi-source training gains in Table 4 are statistically significant and when multi-source helps most.  
- Request hypotheses or evidence explaining anomalous training-language performance (Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba).  
- Suggest mentioning projection methods that do not require parallel corpora (bilingual semantic spaces/induced lexicons) to broaden related work.  
- Recommend proofreading to fix grammatical issues and standardize terminology (e.g., “Freebase”).  
- Ask to state key hyperparameters and wikifier resources (version, dumps, intersection sizes) and clarify script/tokenization handling for non-Latin languages for reproducibility.
