- Paper studies cross-lingual direct transfer for NER using a cross-lingual wikification model; approach is incremental but coherent and empirically supported.
- Strength: clear problem formulation linking wikification to NER transfer.
- Strength: broad, careful experimental setup with generally convincing results.
- Strength: sensibly positioned as complementary to prior transfer methods (projection, distant supervision).
- Major: Expand related work to explicitly compare to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and earlier Wikipedia-aware NER methods, clarifying novel contributions.
- Major: Report supplementary experiments adding POS features (e.g., Tackström et al. 2012 style) for languages with reliable POS to test redundancy or complementarity with wikification.
- Major: Improve clarity of Section 3.3 with a running example that shows input sentence → candidates → disambiguation → NER feature integration.
- Major: Substantiate claims of orthogonality by providing preliminary combined results with methods like Tackström et al. and Nothman et al.; include a strong projection baseline.
- Major: Analyze why certain language-pair transfer patterns occur (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) using corpus statistics or hypotheses about coverage, lexical overlap, or annotation conventions.
- Major: Report statistical significance or variance (bootstrap/approximate randomization or multiple runs) for modest gains shown in Table 4.
- Minor: Note projection methods that do not require parallel corpora (Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vučić & Moens 2013) in Section 2.1.
- Minor: Proofread to fix typos and awkward phrasing (several examples cited).
- Minor: When claiming complementarity, briefly characterize expected error profiles to guide when combinations help.
- Minor: If space permits, report wikification coverage stats across languages (link density, entity-type coverage) to relate to transfer performance.
- Overall: Incremental but well-executed and empirically grounded; revise positioning, clarity, baselines, significance testing, and language-pair analysis to strengthen the contribution.
