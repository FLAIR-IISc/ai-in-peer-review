- Proposes a cross-lingual NER method that maps target-language phrases to English Wikipedia pages via a cross-lingual wikifier and uses page properties as features.  
- Empirical results show these Wikipedia-derived features improve performance in monolingual settings and notably in direct transfer from English to other languages.  
- Introduces a simple, language-agnostic feature set supported by a broad and careful experimental study.  
- Provides insightful analysis for low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts.  
- Concern: how entities missing from Wikipedia affect the method — need quantification of impact on precision and recall.  
- Concern: heavy reliance on the cross-lingual wikifier — need estimates of how often wikification errors cause downstream NER mistakes and error attribution between wikifier vs. NER model.  
- Observation: direct-transfer performs poorly on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included — consider regularizing or reweighting feature groups to avoid over-emphasizing lexical features.
