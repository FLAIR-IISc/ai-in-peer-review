- Summary: Proposes multilingual NER that grounds target-language mentions to English Wikipedia and uses Wikipedia categories/Freebase types as language-independent features, improving monolingual and direct-transfer performance across multiple languages including low-resource/non-Latin scripts.

- Contribution 1: Introduces wikifier-derived, language-independent features from English Wikipedia/Freebase that enable cross-lingual NER without parallel data.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates these features substantially improve direct-transfer and monolingual NER, with analyses showing benefits for low-resource and non-Latin languages.

- Contribution 3: Provides a thorough empirical study with ablations (wikifier-only, base, +gazetteer, +wikifier) and multi-source training to clarify when and why features help.

- Strength: Clear, novel feature design using cross-lingual wikification that is simple, broadly applicable, and language-independent.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across 9 languages and controlled ablations that show consistent gains even over strong baselines.

- Strength: Valuable focus on low-resource and non-Latin-script languages, highlighting practical deployment considerations.

- Strength: Practical resource requirements—only multilingual Wikipedia needed, no parallel corpora or target annotations.

- Strength: Shows multi-source training typically yields modest target performance improvements, supporting wider applicability.

- Weakness: Unclear handling and performance breakdown for entities not present in Wikipedia or lacking interlanguage links/Freebase types, risking coverage bias.

- Weakness: Dependence on the wikifier risks error propagation; the paper lacks quantification of how many NER errors stem from wikifier disambiguation/NIL mistakes or sensitivity to linker confidence.

- Weakness: Model can overweight non-transferable lexical features (notably in Bengali and Tamil); lacks experiments on group-wise regularization or feature reweighting to mitigate this.

- Question: Please provide F1 stratified by (a) correctly linked entities, (b) linked but incorrectly disambiguated, and (c) NIL/unlinked entities.

- Question: What fraction of NER errors are attributable to wikifier errors; can you analyze masking/perturbing linker outputs or using gold links?

- Question: Have you tried group-wise regularization or different per-feature-class weighting to reduce over-reliance on lexical signals for non-Latin targets?

- Question: Can you report per-language wikifier statistics (linking coverage, NIL rate, top-1 accuracy/confidence) on the NER test sets and correlate them with NER gains?

- Overall assessment: Positive—introduces a useful language-independent feature family with solid experiments and insights; addressing coverage and wikifier-error attribution would further strengthen the work.
