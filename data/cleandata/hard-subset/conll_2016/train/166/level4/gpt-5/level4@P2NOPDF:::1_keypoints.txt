- Paper proposes multilingual NER that augments models with English Wikipedia-derived features via a cross-lingual wikifier.  
- Experiments show Wikipedia-based features improve monolingual NER and notably direct cross-lingual transfer (English→other languages).  
- Feature design is novel and well-motivated, with thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Analyses on low-resource settings and non-Latin-script languages are compelling.  
- Coverage for unseen entities is unclear—need breakdown of performance and coverage statistics for mentions that receive no Wikipedia link.  
- Wikifier errors may propagate to NER—quantify the proportion of incorrect/missing links and their impact; include oracle-link ablation or noisy-link robustness tests.  
- In direct-transfer for Tamil and Bengali, lexical features can hurt performance—consider differential regularization (stronger penalties or dropout for lexical features) to prevent over-reliance.  
- Addressing out-of-Wikipedia entities and quantifying wikifier-induced errors would strengthen claims about robustness in low-resource/cross-lingual scenarios.
