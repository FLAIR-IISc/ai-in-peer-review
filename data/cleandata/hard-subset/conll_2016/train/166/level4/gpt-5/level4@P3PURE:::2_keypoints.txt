- Paper studies cross-lingual direct transfer for NER using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; contribution is incremental but coherent with convincing experiments.  
- Core novelty is limited — it combines direct transfer and wikification rather than introducing a fundamentally new technique.  
- Related work: provide a more detailed discussion of Kazama and Torisawa (2007).  
- Related work: more explicitly distinguish this approach from other Wikipedia-based NER systems to clarify positioning.  
- Features/ablations: run experiments including POS features (e.g., per Täckström et al.) to empirically assess their utility across languages.  
- Clarity: Section 3.3 needs a running example to make the adapted Tsai & Roth model operation clear.  
- Orthogonality: include preliminary combined results with orthogonal methods (Täckström et al. 2012, Nothman et al. 2012) to strengthen the claim of combinability.  
- Baselines: compare against a strong projection baseline to better contextualize the gains from wikification-based methods.  
- Analysis: investigate why Dutch is the best source for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba (chance vs. linguistic/resource/wikification factors).  
- Results/statistics: Table 4 improvements are modest/inconsistent — include statistical significance testing to substantiate claims.  
- Minor: note that projection can be done without parallel data (see Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013) for low-resource applicability.  
- Minor: careful proofreading needed to fix typos and unclear sentences (several examples cited).  
- Overall: well-executed, clearly presented empirical study with convincing results but incremental novelty; addressing the above points would substantially improve the paper.
