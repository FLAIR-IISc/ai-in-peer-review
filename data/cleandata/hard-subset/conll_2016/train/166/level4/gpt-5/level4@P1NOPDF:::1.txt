This paper proposes a multilingual named entity recognition (NER) approach that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. Using a cross-lingual wikifier, it links target-language phrases to English Wikipedia articles and extracts features from the corresponding entries. Experiments show that these features improve performance not only in monolingual settings but also in the more interesting direct-transfer scenario, where a model trained on English is applied to a target language.

I enjoyed this paper. It introduces a new feature source for NER and presents a fairly thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating its utility. The analyses on low-resource and non-Latin-script languages are particularly engaging.

However, I have several questions and suggestions:

- Coverage gaps: What about entities that are not present in Wikipedia? In addition to the reported results, it would be valuable to see an analysis of how the method performs on mentions that cannot be linked, including error rates and whether the approach biases predictions toward entities with Wikipedia entries.

- Error propagation from the wikifier: The proposed method heavily depends on the accuracy of the cross-lingual wikifier. With this extra step in the pipeline, how frequently are final NER errors attributable to wikification errors? A breakdown of performance conditioned on correct vs. incorrect links, or an error attribution analysis, would help clarify this dependency.

- Feature regularization: Given the poor direct-transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, is it possible to regularize different feature classes separately so the model does not over-rely on lexical cues? Feature-specific regularization or tuning penalties by feature group might mitigate this issue.

Overall, this is a solid contribution with a clear idea and careful experiments. Addressing the above points—especially the impact on entities absent from Wikipedia, the robustness to wikifier errors, and the sensitivity to lexical features in low-resource, cross-lingual transfer—would strengthen the paper further.
