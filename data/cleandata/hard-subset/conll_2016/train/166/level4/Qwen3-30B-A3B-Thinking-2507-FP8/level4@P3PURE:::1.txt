

This paper presents a novel approach to multilingual named entity recognition (NER) that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. By utilizing a cross-lingual Wikifier to map target-language phrases to corresponding English Wikipedia articles, the method extracts features based on the linked Wikipedia entries. Experimental results demonstrate that this feature set enhances performance not only in monolingual settings but also in the more challenging direct transfer scenario, where an English-trained model is evaluated on target languages. The paper makes a strong contribution through its innovative feature design and comprehensive experimental evaluation, particularly in low-resource and non-Latin script languages, which are compelling aspects of the work.

The authors should address two important considerations. First, the approach assumes all relevant entities are covered in Wikipedia; an analysis of how entities absent from Wikipedia are handled and their impact on performance would strengthen the evaluation. Second, the method's reliance on the cross-lingual Wikifier introduces a potential error source. It is critical to assess the error propagation rate from Wikifier inaccuracies to final NER predictions, as this dependency could significantly affect robustness. Finally, the observed degradation in direct transfer performance for Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included suggests the model may over-rely on these features. Exploring differential regularization strategies across feature classes to mitigate this over-reliance would be valuable.
