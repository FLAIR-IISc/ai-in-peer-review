- Introduces a multilingual NER approach using cross-lingual Wikipedia features via a wikifier to map target-language phrases to English Wikipedia for feature extraction.  
- Extracted semantic and contextual Wikipedia features improve NER accuracy in monolingual and direct-transfer settings.  
- Thorough evaluation with valuable insights into low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts.  
- Novel feature set and demonstrated utility across diverse linguistic settings, especially resource-constrained scenarios.  
- Practical and scalable feature-engineering approach with real-world applicability.  
- Concern 1: Method depends on Wikipedia coverage—need analysis of performance when entities are absent from Wikipedia.  
- Concern 2: Reliance on the cross-lingual wikifier risks error propagation—require quantification of how wikifier errors affect final NER predictions.  
- Concern 3: Adding lexical features degrades performance in Tamil and Bengali, suggesting over-reliance; explore differential regularization or reweighting of lexical features.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, well-motivated, and a strong candidate for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing coverage, error analysis, and robustness.
