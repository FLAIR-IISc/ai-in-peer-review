- Paper proposes language-independent cross-lingual NER by grounding phrases to English Wikipedia and using Wikipedia categories/Freebase types as language-independent features that improve NER and transfer performance.

- Shows strong empirical gains in both monolingual and English→target transfer settings, with particularly large improvements on low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across nine languages including five low-resource non-Latin-script languages, demonstrating wikifier features help where lexical features fail.

- Strength: Simple, elegant method requiring only multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel data or language-specific resources), making it broadly applicable.

- Strength: Insightful analysis of how Wikipedia size/coverage affects wikifier quality and downstream NER performance.

- Weakness: Does not quantify how wikification errors propagate to NER; missing wikification error rates and correlation with NER mistakes.

- Weakness: Fails to analyze handling of entities absent from Wikipedia (new/obscure/recent), leaving unclear system behavior on such cases.

- Weakness: For non-Latin scripts (Bengali, Tamil) lexical features dominate and hurt performance; paper lacks exploration of feature weighting/regularization to rebalance features.

- Question: How often does the cross-lingual wikifier mis-disambiguate, and what is the breakdown of resulting NER errors (FP/FN/incorrect types)?

- Question: Can you provide examples of entities not in Wikipedia and explain how the system handles them compared to covered entities?

- Question: Would alternative feature weighting or regularization improve performance on non-Latin scripts, and what is the computational cost of such adjustments?
