- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER method using wikification to map any language to English Wikipedia, extracting Freebase types and categories as features.  
- Requires only multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel data or language-specific resources) to generate language-independent NER features.  
- Shows these features improve cross-lingual NER on CoNLL languages (Spanish, German, Dutch) and low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), and improve monolingual NER across 9 languages.

- Language-independent approach needs only Wikipedia, making it broadly applicable to languages with Wikipedia coverage and valuable for low-resource settings.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across high- and low-resource languages, with analysis of Wikipedia size effects.  
- Demonstrates wikifier features enhance monolingual NER and multi-source training further boosts performance.

- Method heavily depends on wikifier quality but the paper lacks quantitative analysis of how wikifier errors affect NER performance.  
- Does not address handling of named entities absent from Wikipedia or expected performance when entities have no Wikipedia representation.  
- Fails to explain why adding lexical features degrades performance for Bengali and Tamil and offers no mitigation strategies.  
- Comparison to recent state-of-the-art low-resource NER methods (e.g., multilingual pre-trained models) is incomplete.

- How often do wikifier errors propagate to NER errors; please provide an error-correlation analysis.  
- How does the method handle entities with no Wikipedia coverage and what is expected performance on such entities?  
- Why do lexical features hurt Bengali and Tamil performance, and what potential mitigations (e.g., feature weighting/regularization) can be applied?  
- How does this approach compare to recent low-resource NER methods using multilingual pre-trained models (e.g., multilingual BERT)?
