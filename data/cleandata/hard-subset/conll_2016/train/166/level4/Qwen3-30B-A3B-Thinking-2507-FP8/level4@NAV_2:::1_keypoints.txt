- Introduces a cross-lingual NER approach using cross-lingual wikification to create language-independent features from Freebase types and Wikipedia categories.  
- Claims these features significantly improve monolingual and cross-lingual NER performance.  
- Analyzes the effect of Wikipedia size on feature quality and shows benefits of multi-source training.  
- Strong empirical evaluation across high-resource (Spanish, German, Dutch) and low-resource non-Latin-script languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Valuable analysis of how Wikipedia size influences feature quality and method limitations.  
- Shows language-independent features also improve monolingual NER, extending utility beyond cross-lingual transfer.  
- Fails to address handling of entities not present in Wikipedia, a major practical limitation.  
- Lacks analysis of how wikification errors propagate to NER performance, especially for languages with small Wikipedias.  
- Shows poor performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included but does not explore fixes like feature-specific regularization.  
- Asks how the method handles non-Wikipedia entities and the expected performance impact.  
- Requests analysis of wikifier error effects on NER for small-Wikipedia languages and whether feature-specific regularization could mitigate poor lexical-feature performance while preserving results for Latin-script languages.
