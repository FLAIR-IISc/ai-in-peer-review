

This paper explores cross-lingual direct transfer for NER using a recent cross-lingual wikification model. While the core approach is incremental rather than highly innovative—merely combining established direct transfer techniques for NER with recent advances in cross-lingual wikification—the paper presents a coherent, well-supported argument with thorough empirical evaluation. The results are convincing, though several aspects of presentation and analysis warrant improvement.

The related work section should explicitly contrast this work with Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and clarify how it differs from other NER systems leveraging Wikipedia knowledge. The distinctions are mentioned but require stronger emphasis to properly position this contribution within the field.

The authors justify omitting POS tags as features, but including experiments with POS features (as in Tackstrom et al.) would provide valuable empirical insight into their utility across languages with available universal POS tags. This would strengthen the analysis of feature importance.

Section 3.3 would benefit from a concrete example to clarify how the modified Tsai and Roth model functions, as the current description remains somewhat opaque.

The paper repeatedly states that approaches like Tackstrom et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) are orthogonal and combinable. Reporting preliminary results on language combinations using such hybrids would significantly enrich the discussion. Similarly, comparing against a strong projection baseline (e.g., using bilingual semantic spaces as in Peirsman and Pado, NAACL 2009, or Vulic and Moens, EMNLP 2013) would better contextualize the benefits and limitations of the wikification-based approach.

The choice of Dutch as the optimal training language for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba warrants deeper analysis. Is this pattern coincidental, or do linguistic relationships (e.g., historical, typological) explain these pairings? A brief discussion would add valuable insight.

The results in Table 4 show only marginal improvements across scenarios. A statistical significance test would be necessary to substantiate the claimed gains, as the current presentation does not sufficiently support the assertion of meaningful progress.

Minor points:
- Section 2.1: Note that projection can be achieved without parallel data (e.g., via bilingual semantic spaces as in Peirsman and Pado, NAACL 2009; Vulic and Moens, EMNLP 2013), broadening applicability to low-resource language pairs.
- Proofreading: The paper contains several minor errors requiring correction (e.g., "as a the base model" in Sect. 2.1; grammatically awkward phrasing in the first sentence of Sect. 3; "They avoid the traditional pipeline of NER then EL by..." on p. 3; "to disambiguate every n-grams" on p. 8). Careful proofreading would substantially improve readability.
