

This paper explores cross-lingual direct transfer of NER models leveraging a recent cross-lingual wikification approach. While the core technical contribution is incremental rather than highly innovative, it effectively integrates two established research threads: (1) direct transfer for downstream NLP tasks (e.g., NER, parsing, POS tagging), and (2) recent advances in cross-lingual wikification. Despite the incremental nature, I found the paper well-motivated, with a coherent narrative supported by sufficient experiments and convincing empirical results. I offer the following specific suggestions for improvement:

**Related Work:** A more detailed discussion is needed on how this work relates to Kazama and Torisawa (2007), explicitly clarifying the distinctions from prior NER systems that utilized Wikipedia knowledge. The differences are noted in the text but should be more prominently highlighted to better position the contribution within the field.

**POS Features:** While the rationale for excluding POS tags as features is provided, reporting experiments with POS features (as in Tackstrom et al.) would strengthen the analysis. This would offer empirical insight into the utility (or lack thereof) of POS features across languages with available universal POS tags.

**Section 3.3 Clarity:** The description of the modified Tsai and Roth model remains ambiguous. A concrete running example would clarify the operational details.

**Orthogonal Approaches:** The claim that Tackstrom et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) are orthogonal to this work is noted. Reporting preliminary results combining these approaches on select languages would significantly enhance the discussion. Similarly, including a comparison with a strong projection baseline would provide deeper context for evaluating the efficacy of wikification-based methods.

**Language Selection Analysis:** The observation that Dutch yields the best results for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba warrants deeper exploration. A brief analysis (paragraph or two) discussing whether these patterns reflect statistical coincidence or meaningful linguistic/structural relationships would be valuable.

**Table 4 Results:** The marginal gains reported in Table 4 (not observed across all scenarios) would be more compelling with statistical significance testing to support the claims.

**Minor Comments:**
- Section 2.1: Projection can also be achieved without parallel data (e.g., via bilingual semantic spaces, as in Peirsman and Pado, NAACL 2009; Vulic and Moens, EMNLP 2013), broadening applicability to languages lacking parallel resources.
- Typos require correction: Section 3 first sentence ("as a the base model"), Section 3 sentence structure (ungrammatical), Page 3 ("They avoid the traditional pipeline of NER then EL by..."), Page 8 ("to disambiguate every n-grams"). Careful proofreading is recommended.
