

This paper addresses cross-lingual NER model transfer via a recent cross-lingual wikification framework. While the core approach lacks significant novelty—merely merging two established research threads (direct transfer for NLP tasks and recent cross-lingual wikification advances)—the work presents a coherent narrative supported by thorough experimentation and compelling empirical results. I found the paper well-structured and convincing overall, though specific clarifications are needed.  

**Related Work:** A more detailed discussion is required on how this work relates to Kazama and Torisawa (2007), including explicit differentiation from prior Wikipedia-based NER systems. The distinctions mentioned should be emphasized to better contextualize the contribution.  

**POS Features:** Although the rationale for excluding POS tags is provided, reporting experiments with POS features (as in Tackstrom et al.) would offer valuable empirical insight into their utility across languages with universal POS coverage.  

**Section 3.3:** A concrete running example would clarify the modified Tsai and Roth model, as the current description remains ambiguous.  

**Combination Experiments:** The claim that Tackstrom et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) are orthogonal to this approach merits preliminary results demonstrating their integration, enhancing the discussion. Similarly, comparing against a strong projection baseline would better contextualize the value of wikification-based methods.  

**Language Selection Analysis:** The observation that Dutch optimizes Spanish NER and Spanish optimizes Yoruba NER warrants deeper analysis—whether this reflects statistical coincidence or meaningful linguistic patterns.  

**Table 4 Results:** The modest gains reported (with no consistent improvements across all settings) would be strengthened by statistical significance testing.  

**Minor Comments:**  
- Section 2.1: Projection can also leverage non-parallel methods (e.g., Peirsman and Pado, NAACL 2009; Vulic and Moens, EMNLP 2013), broadening applicability to low-resource languages.  
- Typos require correction (e.g., "as a the base model" in Section 2.1; ungrammatical phrasing on Page 3; "to disambiguate every n-grams" on Page 8).
