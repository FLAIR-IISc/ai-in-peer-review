

This paper introduces a novel approach for multi-lingual named entity recognition (NER) leveraging features derived from Wikipedia. By employing a cross-lingual Wikifier to map target-language phrases to corresponding English Wikipedia articles, the method extracts features from these entries. Experiments demonstrate that this feature enhances performance not only in monolingual settings but also in the more challenging direct transfer scenario, where an English-trained model is evaluated on target languages.  

The paper presents a compelling new feature for NER and validates its utility through a comprehensive experimental setup. The analysis of low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly valuable and well-executed.  

However, the method’s reliance on Wikipedia coverage raises a critical question: how does it handle named entities absent from Wikipedia? It would be valuable to assess the impact of this limitation, including whether the proposed approach systematically fails for such entities or if alternative strategies could mitigate this issue.  

Furthermore, the pipeline’s dependence on the cross-lingual Wikifier introduces a potential vulnerability. Since errors in the Wikifier’s article mapping directly propagate to the NER system, it is essential to quantify how frequently inaccuracies in the wikifier lead to downstream NER errors. A failure analysis of this nature would strengthen the evaluation.  

Finally, the observed performance drop in Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added suggests a risk of over-reliance on these features. It would be worthwhile to investigate whether differential regularization across feature classes—such as applying stronger regularization to lexical features—could prevent this over-reliance and improve robustness in low-resource settings. Addressing this could further enhance the method’s applicability to languages where direct transfer struggles.
