- Paper introduces a cross-lingual NER transfer method using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; contribution is incremental but well-executed and empirically grounded.  
- Major: Clarify positioning against Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and other Wikipedia-based NER systems with a dedicated contrast paragraph.  
- Major: Add ablation studies including POS features (and report per-language differences) to justify excluding POS and strengthen feature analysis.  
- Major: Report preliminary results combining the approach with orthogonal methods (e.g., Tackström et al., Nothman et al.) to demonstrate complementarity.  
- Major: Include a strong projection baseline (e.g., unsupervised Vulic & Moens, 2013) to contextualize wikification-based gains.  
- Major: Analyze surprising language-pair results (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) to determine if patterns are statistical or linguistically motivated.  
- Major: Provide statistical significance testing (e.g., paired t-test) for marginal F1 improvements to show practical impact.  
- Minor: Note that projection can avoid parallel data via bilingual semantic spaces (Peirsman & Pado; Vulic & Moens), broadening applicability.  
- Minor: Fix typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript via careful proofreading.  
- Minor: Clarify Section 3.3 with a concrete running example to show how the wikification layer integrates with the NER pipeline.  
- Overall: Strengths are clear methodology and thorough evaluation; addressing the above would elevate the paper’s impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
