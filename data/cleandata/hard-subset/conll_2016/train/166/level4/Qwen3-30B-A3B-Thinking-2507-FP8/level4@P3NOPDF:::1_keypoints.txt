- Proposes a novel multilingual NER method that maps target-language phrases to English Wikipedia articles via a cross-lingual Wikifier and extracts features from those entries.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements in both monolingual and challenging direct-transfer settings using Wikipedia-derived features.  
- Shows strong empirical gains on low-resource and non-Latin scripts (e.g., Tamil, Bengali), highlighting practical utility for under-resourced languages.  
- Presents a well-structured experimental design with clear ablation studies and insightful language-specific analyses.  
- Assumes target entities exist in English Wikipedia; missing local, recent, or domain-specific entities could undermine performance—needs quantitative analysis of such cases.  
- Relies on cross-lingual Wikifier accuracy; mapping errors may propagate to NER—suggests measuring wikification error rates and comparing to an idealized oracle.  
- Lexical features significantly harm direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali, indicating over-reliance and motivating exploration of feature-specific regularization.  
- Overall a strong contribution, but addressing coverage, wikification sensitivity, and feature regularization would strengthen the paper’s claims and applicability.
