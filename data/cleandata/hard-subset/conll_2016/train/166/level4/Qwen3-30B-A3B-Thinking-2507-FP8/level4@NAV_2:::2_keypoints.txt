- Proposes a language-independent NER model that uses cross-lingual wikification to ground non-English tokens to English Wikipedia entries and uses Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types as features.  
- Shows the model outperforms direct transfer on CoNLL languages (Spanish, German, Dutch) and improves performance on low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) without target annotations.  
- Demonstrates that language-independent wikification features also boost monolingual NER across nine languages.

- Strong empirical evidence with comprehensive experiments across multiple language settings and detailed analysis of feature contributions and Wikipedia size effects.  
- Conceptually sound and practical approach requiring only multilingual Wikipedia dumps, no parallel text or language-specific resources.  
- Thorough analysis of the relationship between Wikipedia size and feature quality, offering insights for low-resource settings.

- Related work section insufficiently distinguishes this approach from Kazama and Torisawa (2007) who used Wikipedia category features.  
- Paper does not explain why Dutch is the best source for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba, missing deeper analysis of transfer patterns.  
- Table 4 improvements from multi-source training lack statistical significance testing to support claims.  
- No experiments including POS tag features to justify their omission or assess their cross-lingual utility.  
- Section 3.3 (Cross-lingual Wikifier Features) lacks a concrete example clarifying how the modified wikifier handles n-grams.

- Please analyze why Dutch is the best training language for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba and whether similar patterns hold for other language pairs.  
- Please provide statistical significance tests for the multi-source training results in Table 4.  
- Please provide a concrete example showing how the modified cross-lingual wikifier processes n-grams (n>1) to avoid incorrect entity linking.
