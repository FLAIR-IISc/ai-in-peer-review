

This paper addresses cross-lingual direct transfer of NER models via a recent cross-lingual wikification approach. While the core contribution is incremental rather than highly innovative—merely combining existing research paths on direct transfer for downstream NLP tasks (e.g., NER, parsing, POS) and recent cross-lingual wikification advances—the paper presents a compelling, coherent narrative supported by thorough experimentation and convincing empirical results. I found the work well-motivated and executed, though several presentation improvements would strengthen it.  

**Major Comments:**  
1. **Related Work:** A more explicit comparison with Kazama and Torisawa (2007) is needed. The paper must clearly articulate how its approach differs from prior NER systems leveraging Wikipedia knowledge, beyond the brief mentions in the text. This is essential for contextualizing the contribution.  
2. **POS Feature Analysis:** Although the authors justify omitting POS tags, reporting experiments with POS features (as in Tackstrom et al.) would provide valuable empirical insight into their utility across languages (particularly where universal POS resources exist).  
3. **Section 3.3 Clarity:** A concrete running example would clarify the modified Tsai and Roth model, as the current description is insufficiently precise.  
4. **Orthogonal Methods:** The claim that Tackstrom et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) are orthogonal merits validation via preliminary results combining their models with the proposed approach. This would enrich the discussion. Similarly, comparing against a strong projection baseline (e.g., using bilingual semantic spaces as in Peirsman and Pado, NAACL 2009; Vulic and Moens, EMNLP 2013) would better contextualize the value of wikification-based transfer.  
5. **Language Selection Analysis:** The observation that Dutch optimizes Spanish NER and Spanish optimizes Yoruba NER warrants deeper analysis. Is this linguistic proximity or a statistical artifact? A dedicated discussion would add significant insight.  
6. **Table 4 Results:** The marginal improvements reported (not consistently observed across scenarios) require statistical significance testing to substantiate claims.  

**Minor Comments:**  
- Section 2.1: Note that projection can be achieved without parallel data (e.g., via bilingual semantic spaces; Peirsman and Pado, NAACL 2009; Vulic and Moens, EMNLP 2013), broadening applicability to low-resource language pairs.  
- Typos: "as a the base model" (Section 2.1), "They avoid the traditional pipeline of NER then EL by..." (Page 3), "to disambiguate every n-grams" (Page 8). A careful proofread is recommended.
