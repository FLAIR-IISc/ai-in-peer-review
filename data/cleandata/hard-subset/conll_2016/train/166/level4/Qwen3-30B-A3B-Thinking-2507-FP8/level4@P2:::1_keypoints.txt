- Summary: Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER method that uses a cross-lingual wikifier to map phrases to English Wikipedia and extract category/Freebase-type features for direct English-to-target transfer without language-specific resources.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel language-agnostic feature set derived from cross-lingual wikification enabling direct transfer of English NER models to other languages.

- Contribution 2: Provides comprehensive empirical validation across high- and low-resource languages, showing significant gains over strong baselines.

- Contribution 3: Shows the wikification-derived features also improve monolingual NER systems, offering dual utility.

- Strength 1: Simple and effective approach that requires only multilingual Wikipedia, avoiding parallel data or language-specific resources.

- Strength 2: Thorough experiments including CoNLL and multiple low-resource, non-Latin-script languages, plus analysis of Wikipedia size impact on features.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates practical utility with state-of-the-art results and benefits for both cross-lingual transfer and monolingual tasks.

- Weakness 1: Lacks analysis of dependency on wikifier accuracy and how wikifier errors propagate to NER errors.

- Weakness 2: Fails to address entities missing from Wikipedia and how limited Wikipedia coverage affects performance.

- Weakness 3: Does not investigate feature-weighting imbalance that causes performance drops (e.g., Bengali and Tamil) when lexical features are added.

- Question 1: How does performance degrade for entities without Wikipedia entries, and can you provide examples or statistics?

- Question 2: What is the cross-lingual wikifier error rate and how often do those errors impact NER performance?

- Question 3: How could the model automatically weight features by language characteristics to avoid manual feature selection for non-Latin-script languages?
