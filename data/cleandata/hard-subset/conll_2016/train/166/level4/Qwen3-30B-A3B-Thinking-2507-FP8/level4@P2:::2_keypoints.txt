- Proposes a cross-lingual NER method using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features (Freebase types and Wikipedia categories) by grounding non-English text to English Wikipedia.  
- Trains model on English and applies it to multiple high- and low-resource languages (Spanish, German, Dutch, Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil).  
- Contributions: language-independent NER model using wikification features, empirical validation across diverse languages with strong improvements, and demonstration that these features boost monolingual NER.  
- Strength: clear, coherent approach with strong empirical validation across high- and low-resource languages and scripts.  
- Strength: convinces that wikifier-derived features are effective and outperform state-of-the-art across evaluated languages, including where Wikipedia is small.  
- Strength: shows versatility—features work both for cross-lingual transfer and to enhance monolingual systems.  
- Weakness: core innovation is incremental—combines existing techniques rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from Kazama and Torisawa (2007); needs to clarify how robustness to ambiguity is addressed.  
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing (e.g., for results in Table 4), weakening claims about consistent improvements.  
- Question: clarify key differences from Kazama and Torisawa (2007), especially regarding robustness to ambiguity.  
- Question: include statistical significance testing for Table 4 results to support improvement claims.  
- Question: would adding POS tag features (where available) improve results, as in Täckström et al. (2012)?  
- Additional: paper contains minor grammatical errors and typos that should be fixed.  
- Additional: intriguing observation about best training language pairs (Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) lacks deeper analysis and would benefit from exploration.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed incremental contribution with strong empirical results but needs clearer differentiation from prior work and statistical validation.
