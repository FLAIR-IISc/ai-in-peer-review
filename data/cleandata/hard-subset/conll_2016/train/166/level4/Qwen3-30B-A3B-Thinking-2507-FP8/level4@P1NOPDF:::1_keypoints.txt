- Introduces a novel multilingual NER approach using features derived from English Wikipedia via a cross-lingual Wikifier.  
- Extracted Wikipedia-based features improve performance in both monolingual and direct transfer (English-trained → target) settings.  
- Comprehensive experimental validation, with valuable analysis of low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts.  
- Major concern: reliance on Wikipedia coverage — unclear handling and impact for entities missing from Wikipedia.  
- Pipeline vulnerability: errors in the cross-lingual Wikifier can propagate to NER; frequency and impact of such mapping errors are not quantified.  
- Empirical issue: adding lexical features causes performance drops in Tamil and Bengali, indicating potential over-reliance on lexical/Wikipedia-derived features.  
- Suggested remedy: investigate differential regularization for feature classes (e.g., stronger regularization on lexical features) to improve robustness in low-resource languages.
