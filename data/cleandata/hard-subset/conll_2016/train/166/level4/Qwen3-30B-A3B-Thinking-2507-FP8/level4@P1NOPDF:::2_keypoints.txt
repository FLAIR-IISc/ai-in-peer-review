- Studies cross-lingual direct transfer for NER using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; approach is incremental (combines established transfer techniques with wikification).
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and results convincing, but presentation and analysis require improvement.
- Related work should explicitly contrast this work with Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and clarify differences from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Include experiments with POS tag features (e.g., as in Täckström et al.) to evaluate their utility across languages.
- Section 3.3 needs a concrete example to clarify how the modified Tsai & Roth model functions.
- Report preliminary hybrid experiments combining this approach with methods like Täckström et al. or Nothman et al. to support the claim of orthogonality/combination.
- Compare against strong projection baselines (e.g., bilingual semantic spaces from Peirsman & Pado 2009 or Vulić & Moens 2013) to better contextualize the wikification approach.
- Provide analysis explaining why Dutch is optimal for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba—investigate whether linguistic/historical/typological factors explain these pairings.
- Perform statistical significance testing for the results in Table 4, since improvements reported are marginal.
- Note that projection can be achieved without parallel data (via bilingual semantic spaces), which broadens applicability to low-resource language pairs.
- Proofread and correct several minor grammatical errors and awkward phrasings throughout the paper.
