- Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification to map non-English text to English Wikipedia for language-independent FreeBase type and category features.  
- Shows English-trained NER with wikification features can be applied to multiple target languages without target annotations.  
- Demonstrates that wikification improves cross-lingual and monolingual NER, is broadly applicable using only multilingual Wikipedia, and benefits from larger Wikipedia size and multi-source training.

- Empirical results show substantial gains, especially for low-resource and non-Latin script languages, outperforming prior resource-heavy methods on several languages.  
- The method is simple and language-agnostic, requiring only Wikipedia dumps and no parallel data or language-specific heuristics.  
- Thorough analysis links Wikipedia size to feature quality and explains why wikifier features dominate when lexical cues fail.

- Lacks comparison with strong projection baselines that do not require parallel text, limiting contextualization of results.  
- Reported improvements from multi-language training are marginal (≈0.3–1.0 F1) and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Claims of orthogonality and complementarity with projection methods are untested; no experiments show combined gains.

- Why does Spanish work best as a source for Yoruba and for Dutch-to-Spanish transfer instead of English—coincidence or linguistic reason?  
- Please provide statistical significance tests for the small F1 improvements reported in multi-language training (Table 4).  
- Have you tried combining wikification features with projection methods (e.g., Vulic & Moens) and, if so, what are the preliminary results?

- Clarify how the wikifier was modified to handle n-gram processing (Section 3.3).  
- Strengthen comparison with Kazama and Torisawa (2007) to highlight differences and robustness.  
- Fix minor grammatical errors and typos throughout the paper before publication.
