

Summary: This paper introduces a cross-lingual NER approach that leverages cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent features. The main contributions are: (1) Development of a language-independent feature set based on FreeBase types and Wikipedia categories obtained through cross-lingual wikification, (2) Demonstration that these features significantly improve both monolingual and cross-lingual NER performance, and (3) Analysis showing how Wikipedia size affects feature quality and the benefits of training on multiple source languages.

Strengths: 
1. The paper provides comprehensive evaluation across both high-resource languages (Spanish, German, Dutch) and low-resource languages with non-Latin scripts (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil), demonstrating the method's broad applicability.
2. The analysis of how Wikipedia size influences feature quality (Section 4.4) is particularly valuable for understanding the method's limitations and potential improvements.
3. The demonstration that language-independent features enhance monolingual NER systems (Section 4.2) represents a significant contribution beyond just cross-lingual transfer, as it shows the features have broader utility.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper does not adequately address how the method handles named entities that are not present in Wikipedia, which constitutes a critical limitation for practical NER applications where many entities may lack Wikipedia coverage.
2. The approach's performance is heavily dependent on the accuracy of the cross-lingual wikifier, but the paper lacks analysis of how wikification errors propagate to NER results, particularly for languages with smaller Wikipedia sizes.
3. The paper shows poor performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included, yet fails to explore potential solutions such as feature-specific regularization to prevent over-reliance on lexical features that perform poorly for non-Latin scripts.

Questions to Authors:
1. How does the proposed method handle named entities that are not present in Wikipedia, and what is the expected impact on performance for such entities?
2. Can you provide an analysis of how errors in the wikifier affect the final NER results, particularly for languages with smaller Wikipedia sizes where wikification accuracy is likely lower?
3. Given the poor performance on Tamil and Bengali with lexical features, would feature-specific regularization improve results, and how might this be implemented while maintaining performance on languages with Latin scripts?
