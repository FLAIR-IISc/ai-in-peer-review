- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER using wikification-derived Freebase types and Wikipedia categories as language-independent features enabling direct transfer without parallel data, showing strong results on CoNLL and low-resource languages.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence across nine languages showing consistent improvements, especially on low-resource languages, outperforming methods that use parallel data or native interaction.  
- Strength: Method is simple and practical, requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump and no language-specific rules, parallel text, or heavy feature engineering.  
- Strength: Analysis of Wikipedia size impact clarifies limitations and explains variable gains for low-resource languages.  
- Weakness: Contribution seems incremental, mainly combining existing techniques; novelty is limited given prior work (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007).  
- Weakness: Insufficient differentiation from Kazama & Torisawa (2007); unclear how this method addresses their ambiguity-handling limitations.  
- Weakness: Reported small improvements (Table 4) lack statistical significance testing, making practical importance unclear.  
- Weakness: Claimed orthogonality to Täckström et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) needs stronger justification and experiments showing combined gains.  
- Question: Please provide a detailed comparison with Kazama & Torisawa (2007), clarifying how ambiguity handling and monolingual focus are addressed.  
- Question: Would you run experiments including POS-tag features (as in Täckström et al.) for languages with universal POS availability to assess added utility?  
- Question: Can you explain why Dutch transfers best to Spanish and Spanish to Yoruba, and whether those patterns are statistically significant or coincidental?  
- Comment: Clarify the modified wikifier in Section 3.3 with a concrete example to improve technical clarity.  
- Comment: Explicitly state the n-gram maximum (n=4) early and consistently when mentioning "n-grams."  
- Comment: Better acknowledge that some cross-lingual wikification approaches use parallel data to more precisely position your contribution.  
- Comment: Fix minor grammatical errors and awkward phrasings throughout (e.g., "as a the base model", "disambiguate every n-grams").
