- Uses cross-lingual wikification to produce language-independent features by linking source n-grams to English Wikipedia and using categories/FreeBase types.  
- Trains on a high-resource language and directly transfers the NER model to target languages without target-language annotations.  
- Extensive evaluation on nine languages (CoNLL and low-resource) showing significant improvements over comparable baselines, especially on non-Latin scripts.  
- Strong empirical results with large F1 gains versus direct-transfer and low-resource baselines (e.g., >10 F1 on Dutch and German).  
- Simple, general method needing only a multilingual Wikipedia dump, enabling broad applicability to many languages.  
- Thorough experimental analysis, including feature ablations, monolingual/cross-lingual tests, multi-source training, and simulated reduced interlanguage links showing feature quality depends on Wikipedia size.  
- Limited conceptual novelty—an incremental application combining direct transfer and an existing wikifier rather than a new algorithm or representation.  
- Insufficient contextualization and comparison—missing comparisons to strong projection-based baselines and a clearer distinction from prior Wikipedia-based NER work.  
- Technical description lacks concrete detail and examples for the modified wikifier (Sec. 3.3) and how features are assigned from overlapping n-grams.  
- Multi-source training analysis is underdeveloped: gains are modest or negative, no significance tests, and surprising transfer patterns are unexplained.  
- Question: Could linguistic typology or language family effects (not just Wikipedia size) explain transfer patterns like Spanish→Yoruba?  
- Question: Are the reported multi-source improvements statistically significant versus the best single-source model (EN+ES)?  
- Question: Please provide a concrete walkthrough of feature generation (e.g., n-grams, top wikifier candidates, and FreeBase/Wikipedia category features for the token "Albrecht" in "Albrecht Lehmann").
