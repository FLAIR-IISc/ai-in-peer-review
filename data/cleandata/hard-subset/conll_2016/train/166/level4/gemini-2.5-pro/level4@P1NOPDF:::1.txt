This paper proposes an approach for multilingual named entity recognition (NER) that incorporates features derived from Wikipedia. Using a cross-lingual Wikifier, the method identifies English Wikipedia articles corresponding to phrases in a target language and then extracts features from those entries for use in an NER model. The experiments demonstrate that this new feature set improves performance not only in the monolingual setting but also in the more challenging direct transfer scenario, where a model trained on English is applied to a target language without target-specific training data.

This is a well-executed paper that introduces a novel and useful feature for multilingual NER. The authors conduct a thorough set of experiments to validate the utility of their proposed feature. The analysis of the feature's impact in low-resource settings and on non-Latin script languages is particularly insightful.

However, I have a few questions and suggestions for improvement:

1.  A key question is how the proposed method handles named entities that do not have a Wikipedia entry. An analysis of the model's performance on these out-of-Wikipedia entities would be a valuable addition, clarifying the feature's limitations and overall impact.

2.  The proposed pipeline's performance is heavily reliant on the accuracy of the upstream cross-lingual Wikifier. The paper would be strengthened by an error analysis that quantifies how often Wikifier errors propagate and lead to incorrect NER predictions. This would provide a clearer picture of the method's robustness.

3.  Given the performance degradation reported for direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, the model may be overfitting to source-language lexical items. Have the authors considered applying different regularization strengths to different feature classes? This could prevent the model from becoming over-reliant on lexical features that do not transfer well across languages.
