- Summary: Proposes cross-lingual NER by linking n-grams to English Wikipedia via a wikifier and using Freebase types/Wikipedia categories as language-independent features, achieving SOTA direct-transfer results for Spanish, German, and Dutch and strong performance on several low-resource languages.

- Strength 1: Novel, effective feature engineering—repurposes cross-lingual wikification to produce language-independent semantic features that yield large F1 gains (e.g., >12 points for Dutch and German) in ablations.

- Strength 2: Rigorous empirical evaluation across nine languages (including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages) with strong baselines, demonstrating broad generalizability without parallel corpora.

- Strength 3: Insightful analysis showing correlation between Wikipedia size and feature quality, benefits from multi-source training, and utility of wikifier features for monolingual NER.

- Weakness 1: Method implicitly depends on Wikipedia coverage and lacks analysis of performance on entities not present in Wikipedia (out-of-knowledge-base entities).

- Weakness 2: Pipeline design risks error propagation from the wikifier to NER, but the paper provides no quantification of how wikification errors affect final NER performance.

- Weakness 3: Suboptimal direct transfer for typologically distant, non-Latin-script languages (Bengali, Tamil); including English lexical features can catastrophically hurt performance and the model cannot automatically ignore such non-transferable features.

- Question 1: Please provide recall/scores for in-Wikipedia versus out-of-Wikipedia entities on the test sets.

- Question 2: Please quantify how many NER errors (false positives/negatives) are attributable to incorrect or missing wikifier links.

- Question 3: Have you considered learning strategies (e.g., group-wise regularization) to down-weight or ignore feature groups like English lexical features that do not transfer to typologically distant languages?
