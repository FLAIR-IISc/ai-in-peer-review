- Introduces a cross-lingual wikifier that links n-grams to English Wikipedia to produce language-independent features (Freebase types, categories) for NER.  
- Applies these wikifier features to improve monolingual NER and enable direct transfer NER from high-resource to low-resource languages without target annotations.  
- Main contribution: a novel feature representation framing wikification as an upstream source of semantic/type features for cross-lingual NER.  
- Main contribution: strong empirical results across nine languages, outperforming prior direct-transfer methods and competing with resource-heavy systems.  
- Main contribution: extensive analysis of feature utility and correlation with Wikipedia size, including effects on low-resource languages.  
- Strength: effective, intuitive approach that yields substantial F1 gains in both monolingual (+1.5–+4.2) and direct-transfer (+1.2–+12.4) settings.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation over diverse languages (high- and low-resource, non-Latin scripts) and comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Strength: insightful analyses (e.g., simulated smaller Wikipedias, impact of lexical features in non-Latin scripts) that inform applicability.  
- Weakness: no analysis of model performance on entities absent from Wikipedia (out-of-Wikipedia/NIL entities) and potential false negatives.  
- Weakness: unquantified impact of wikifier errors and error propagation from incorrect entity disambiguation to NER.  
- Weakness: brittle handling of feature classes in cross-script transfer—model over-relies on lexical features, causing severe failures on Bengali and Tamil unless manually restricted to wikifier features.  
- Questions for authors: provide recall analysis for entities with NIL wikifier outputs and their proportion in test sets.  
- Questions for authors: consider mechanisms to automatically down-weight or regularize feature groups (e.g., lexical features) that do not transfer across scripts.  
- Questions for authors: quantify how many NER errors are attributable to incorrect wikifier links (error analysis on dev data).
