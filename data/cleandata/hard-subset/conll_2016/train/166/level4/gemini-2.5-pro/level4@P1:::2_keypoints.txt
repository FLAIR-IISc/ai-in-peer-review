- Contribution is incremental: combines direct transfer for downstream NLP with recent cross-lingual wikification rather than proposing a highly novel idea.
- Paper is well-executed with a clear narrative and comprehensive, convincing experiments.
- Related work needs stronger comparison to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and clearer distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Although POS exclusion is justified, experiments including POS features (as in Täckström et al., 2012) should be reported to assess their utility.
- Section 3.3’s description of the modified wikification model is unclear and would benefit from a running example.
- Claim of orthogonality to Täckström et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) should be reinforced by reporting (even preliminary) combined-model results on a few languages.
- Include a strong projection-based baseline for a more complete comparison and to clarify advantages/limitations of the wikification approach.
- Analyze surprising source-target results (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) to determine if they are statistical coincidences or have linguistic/coverage explanations.
- Multi-source training yields only modest and inconsistent improvements; report statistical significance tests to substantiate claims of benefit.
- Note that projection can be done without parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013), which broadens applicability to low-resource languages.
- Paper needs careful proofreading to fix typos and grammatical errors (several examples provided).
