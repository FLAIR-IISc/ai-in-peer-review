1. Novel language-independent NER features via cross-lingual wikification mapping n-grams to English Wikipedia and using categories/Freebase types.  
2. Extensive empirical evaluation on nine languages showing substantial improvements in monolingual and direct-transfer settings.  
3. Wikifier features provide a strong delexicalized signal, yielding consistent and often large F1 gains (e.g., ~+12 in some direct-transfer cases).  
4. Thorough, diverse evaluation including high- and low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts, demonstrating generalizability.  
5. Useful analyses: ablation linking performance to target Wikipedia size/intersection and demonstration of multi-source training gains.  
6. Method depends on Wikipedia coverage; no analysis of performance on out-of-Wikipedia entities.  
7. Heavy reliance on the upstream wikifier with no error-propagation analysis showing how wikifier mistakes affect NER.  
8. Handling of lexically dissimilar language pairs is heuristic (dropping lexical features) rather than a principled, automatic approach.  
9. Question: How does performance compare on entities not present in Wikipedia versus a baseline without wikifier features?  
10. Question: Can you quantify the proportion of NER errors attributable to incorrect or missing wikifier links?  
11. Question: Have you considered automatic approaches (e.g., feature-group-specific regularization) to down-weigh non-transferable lexical features instead of manual exclusion?
