- Proposes grounding all n-grams to English Wikipedia via a cross-lingual wikifier and using Wikipedia categories/FreeBase types as language-independent features for NER.
- Applies these wikifier-derived features both to enhance monolingual NER and to enable direct transfer from high-resource to low-resource languages.
- Evaluates the approach across nine languages (including non-Latin scripts) and reports large improvements, especially in direct transfer.
- Contribution: a novel, delexicalized feature-generation method that leverages Wikipedia structure and avoids needing parallel corpora.
- Contribution: thorough empirical validation showing strong gains and a clear correlation between feature quality and target-language Wikipedia size/interlanguage links.
- Strength: substantial F1 improvements in direct transfer, outperforming prior state-of-the-art baselines on multiple languages.
- Strength: simple and broadly applicable approach requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump, easy to integrate into existing NER systems.
- Strength: insightful analysis linking performance to Wikipedia coverage and exploring multi-source training effects.
- Weakness: effectiveness depends on Wikipedia coverage—entities absent from Wikipedia (local or emerging entities) won’t receive the cross-lingual signal.
- Weakness: missing analysis of wikifier error propagation; no breakdown of NER errors caused by incorrect or missing wikification.
- Question/suggestion: consider differential regularization (e.g., down-weighting English lexical features) to force reliance on language-independent wikifier features for typologically distant/script-different languages.
