This paper proposes an approach for multi-lingual named entity recognition that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. The method uses a cross-lingual Wikifier to identify English Wikipedia articles corresponding to phrases in a target language and then extracts features from these articles. The experiments demonstrate that this new feature improves performance not only in the monolingual setting but also in the direct transfer setting, where a model trained on English is tested on a target language.

This is a solid paper that introduces a novel and useful feature for named entity recognition. The authors conduct a thorough set of experiments to validate the utility of their proposed feature. The analysis on low-resource and non-Latin languages is particularly insightful.

However, there are a few points that could be clarified or strengthened:

*   The proposed method relies on the existence of entities in Wikipedia. It would strengthen the paper to include an analysis of how the system performs on named entities that do not have a corresponding Wikipedia entry. An evaluation of how these specific entities are affected by the proposed method would provide a more complete picture of the feature's impact.

*   The performance is heavily dependent on the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier. An error analysis detailing how often Wikifier errors lead to downstream NER prediction errors would be a valuable addition to quantify the impact of this pipeline component.

*   Given the poor performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, it suggests the model may be overfitting to source-language lexical items. Have the authors considered applying differential regularization to the various feature classes? This could prevent the model from becoming over-reliant on lexical features that do not transfer well across languages.
