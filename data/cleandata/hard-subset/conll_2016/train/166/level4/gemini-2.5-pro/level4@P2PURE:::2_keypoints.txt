- Central idea leverages cross-lingual wikification for direct-transfer NER but is largely incremental rather than introducing new core technology.  
- Paper is well-executed with clear narrative, thorough experiments, and convincing empirical evidence.  
- Related work must more explicitly compare to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and clearly differentiate from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Experiments should report results including POS tag features (as in Täckström et al., 2012) to assess their utility across languages.  
- Section 3.3 needs a running example to clarify the modified Tsai and Roth model.  
- Claiming orthogonality to Täckström et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) requires empirical support—report preliminary combined-model results on some languages.  
- Include a strong projection-based baseline to better contextualize advantages/limitations of the wikification-based transfer.  
- Provide deeper analysis of cross-lingual transfer anomalies (e.g., why Dutch best for Spanish, Spanish best for Yoruba) to determine if findings are coincidental or linguistically/data-driven.  
- Results in Table 4 show small/uneven improvements; report statistical significance tests to substantiate claimed gains.  
- Related discussion of projection should acknowledge non-parallel-data methods (e.g., Peirsman & Pado, 2009; Vulić & Moens, 2013) useful for low-resource languages.  
- Paper needs careful proofreading to fix grammatical errors and typos (several examples cited).
