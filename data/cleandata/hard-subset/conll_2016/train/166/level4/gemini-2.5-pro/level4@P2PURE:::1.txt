This paper presents an approach for multi-lingual named entity recognition that incorporates features derived from Wikipedia. By leveraging a cross-lingual Wikifier, the method identifies English Wikipedia articles corresponding to phrases in a target language and uses them to generate features. The experiments effectively demonstrate that this new feature improves performance not only in monolingual settings but also in the direct transfer setting, where an English-trained model is applied to a target language.

The paper's primary strength is its proposal of a novel and effective feature for named entity recognition. The authors conduct a thorough set of experiments that convincingly demonstrate the feature's utility. The analysis of its impact on low-resource and non-Latin languages is particularly insightful.

However, a few points could be clarified:

1.  A key question is how the proposed method performs on named entities that do not have a corresponding Wikipedia entry. An analysis of the model's performance on this specific subset of entities would be a valuable addition, clarifying the feature's impact on out-of-Wikipedia mentions.

2.  The proposed approach is highly dependent on the performance of the cross-lingual Wikifier. Since this component introduces a potential source of error, it would be beneficial to include an analysis of error propagation. Specifically, how frequently do errors from the Wikifier lead to incorrect NER predictions?

3.  Given the performance degradation in the direct transfer setting for Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, it seems the model may become over-reliant on these language-specific cues. Have the authors considered applying differential regularization to the various feature classes? This could potentially prevent the model from overfitting to source-language lexical features and improve cross-lingual transferability.
