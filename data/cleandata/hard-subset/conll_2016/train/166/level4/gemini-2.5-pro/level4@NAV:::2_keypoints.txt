- Paper studies direct cross-lingual transfer for NER using a state-of-the-art cross-lingual wikification system.  
- Contribution is incremental: it integrates existing direct-transfer and cross-lingual wikification lines rather than introducing a new method.  
- Presentation and experiments are coherent, clearly written, and empirically convincing.  
- Related work needs expansion to discuss Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and explicitly distinguish the method from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Section 3.3 (modified Tsai & Roth model) is unclear and would benefit from a running example.  
- Authors should evaluate inclusion of POS-tag features (as in Täckström et al.) to empirically test their usefulness across languages.  
- To support claims of orthogonality, provide preliminary hybrid results combining this approach with Täckström et al. and Nothman et al., and compare to a strong projection-based baseline.  
- Investigate and analyze surprising source-target transfer patterns (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) to determine if they are artifacts or linguistically/data-driven.  
- Improvements in Table 4 are marginal and inconsistent; report statistical significance tests.  
- Sect. 2.1 should acknowledge projection methods that do not require parallel corpora (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013).  
- Manuscript contains typographical and grammatical errors and requires thorough proofreading.
