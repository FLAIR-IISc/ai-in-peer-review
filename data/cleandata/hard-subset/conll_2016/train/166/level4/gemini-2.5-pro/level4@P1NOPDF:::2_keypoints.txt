- Paper is an incremental but well-executed combination of direct transfer for NLP and recent cross-lingual wikification, producing convincing empirical results.
- Related work section needs clearer connection to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and explicit distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Provide experiments including POS tag features (as in Täckström et al. 2012) to empirically assess their usefulness across languages.
- Section 3.3 is unclear; include a running example to illustrate how the edited Tsai and Roth model is applied.
- Include preliminary experiments combining this method with orthogonal approaches (Täckström et al., Nothman et al.) to demonstrate complementarity.
- Add a strong projection-based baseline comparison to better contextualize advantages and limitations of wikification-based transfer.
- Offer deeper analysis of surprising cross-lingual transfer patterns (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) to determine linguistic or data-driven causes.
- Report statistical significance tests for the improvements (Table 4) to substantiate claims given modest gains.
- Expand discussion of projection methods to include approaches that don’t require parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulic & Moens 2013).
- Proofread and fix typos/grammatical errors throughout the paper (several examples provided).
