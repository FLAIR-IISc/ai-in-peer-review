1. Overall: The paper combines cross-lingual wikification with direct transfer for NER—more a logical synthesis than a novel technique—but is very well executed with convincing experiments.

2. Related work/positioning: Strengthen related work by explicitly comparing to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and consolidating differences from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.

3. POS ablation: Include an experiment adding universal POS features (as in Tackström et al. 2012) to empirically validate the authors’ decision to exclude POS tags.

4. Model clarity: Improve the description of the adapted Tsai and Roth model (Section 3.3), e.g., with a running example to clarify mechanics.

5. Combined baselines: Provide preliminary results for models combining the proposed features with Tackström et al. (2012)/Nothman et al. (2012) and compare to a strong projection-based baseline.

6. Results analysis: Offer deeper analysis of surprising cross-lingual pairings (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) to determine if they reflect linguistic/data phenomena or chance.

7. Statistical significance: Report significance tests for the modest improvements (Table 4) to substantiate the claimed benefits of the features.

8. Projection-based methods note: In related work, mention projection-style methods that do not require parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Padó 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013).

9. Proofreading/typos: Fix multiple typos and grammar issues (examples provided from Sections 3 and 8 and Page 3).
