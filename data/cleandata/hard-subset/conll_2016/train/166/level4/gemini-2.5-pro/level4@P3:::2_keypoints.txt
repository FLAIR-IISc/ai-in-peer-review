- Proposes using cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent, delexicalized NER features by grounding n-grams to English Wikipedia entries.  
- Trains a standard NER model on a source language and directly transfers to target languages using Wikipedia-derived features.  
- Evaluated on nine languages (including non-Latin scripts) with strong performance on CoNLL datasets and notable gains for five low-resource languages.  
- Main contribution: novel application of wikification as a feature source for direct-transfer cross-lingual NER.  
- Extensive empirical validation demonstrates robustness and correlation of performance with target-language Wikipedia size.  
- Practical approach requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump—no parallel corpora, alignments, or language-specific tools.  
- Elegant, coherent method and well-structured presentation with insightful analysis of Wikipedia-size effects.  
- Incremental technical novelty: combines existing ideas (delexicalized transfer + wikification) rather than introducing a new learning algorithm.  
- Insufficient contextualization: related-work discussion (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007) needs expansion and clearer differentiation.  
- Missing comparisons: lacks a strong projection-based baseline (on available languages) to better contextualize results.  
- Analysis gaps: multi-source training gains lack statistical significance testing and some cross-language transfer findings (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) are unexplained.  
- Request for clarification: provide a concrete running example of how all n-grams are handled by the modified wikifier and how features are generated.  
- Suggestion to test synergy: consider (even preliminary) experiments combining this approach with cross-lingual clusters or silver-standard data.  
- Request for hypothesis/analysis on surprising transfer patterns (e.g., why Spanish transfers better to Yoruba than English).  
- Additional comments: paper needs proofreading for minor typos and should note that projection can sometimes be done without parallel corpora (e.g., via bilingual semantic spaces).
