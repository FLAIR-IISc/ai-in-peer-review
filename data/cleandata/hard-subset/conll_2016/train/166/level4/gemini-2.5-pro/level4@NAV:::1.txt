This work introduces a method for multi-lingual named entity recognition that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. The approach utilizes a cross-lingual Wikifier to link phrases in a target language to corresponding English Wikipedia articles, from which features are then extracted. The experiments demonstrate that this novel feature enhances performance not only in monolingual tasks but also in the more challenging direct transfer setting, where a model trained on English is evaluated on a target language.

This paper is a valuable contribution. Its primary strength is the proposal of a novel, effective feature for named entity recognition, which is validated through a comprehensive set of experiments. The analysis focusing on low-resource and non-Latin script languages is particularly insightful and underscores the method's potential impact.

However, the paper could be strengthened by addressing the handling of named entities that lack a Wikipedia entry. An analysis of how the proposed method affects the performance on these out-of-Wikipedia entities would provide a more complete picture of its capabilities.

Furthermore, the proposed method's performance is critically dependent on the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier. As this introduces an additional component into the pipeline, it would be beneficial to include an error analysis that quantifies how inaccuracies in the Wikifier propagate and contribute to final prediction errors.

Finally, considering the performance degradation observed in the direct transfer setting for Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are included, it raises a question about overfitting. It would be worth exploring whether applying different regularization strengths to various feature classes could mitigate the model's over-reliance on lexical features that do not generalize across languages.
