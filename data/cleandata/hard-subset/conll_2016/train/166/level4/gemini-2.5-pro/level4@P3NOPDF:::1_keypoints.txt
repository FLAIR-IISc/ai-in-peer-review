- Proposes a novel method that uses a cross-lingual Wikifier to map target-language phrases to English Wikipedia and extract language-agnostic features for multilingual NER.  
- The Wikipedia-grounded feature set is creative and effective, especially useful for low-resource languages.  
- Extensive experiments across multiple languages and settings show significant gains in monolingual and zero-shot direct-transfer scenarios.  
- Analysis specifically examines performance and challenges on low-resource and non-Latin script languages (e.g., Tamil, Bengali).  
- Unclear handling of out-of-Wikipedia (OOW) entities—need analysis of model performance on entities without Wikipedia entries and whether missing features hurt generalization.  
- Introduces dependency on a cross-lingual Wikifier—requires error analysis and quantification of how Wikifier failures propagate to NER errors and impose a performance ceiling.  
- Evidence of overfitting to English lexical features in direct transfer; recommends exploring differential regularization (stronger penalties on lexical features, weaker on Wikipedia features) to improve robustness.  
- Overall a well-motivated, elegant, and empirically strong contribution; addressing the OOW, Wikifier-error, and regularization concerns would strengthen the paper further.
