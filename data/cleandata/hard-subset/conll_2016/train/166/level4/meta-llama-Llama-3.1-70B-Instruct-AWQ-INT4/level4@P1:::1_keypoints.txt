- Proposes a cross-lingual wikifier-based feature for multi-lingual named entity recognition.  
- Identifies English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and uses those entries as features.  
- Model is language-independent and requires only a Wikipedia dump to work across languages.  
- Experiments show improvements in both monolingual and direct-transfer (English→target) settings.  
- Thorough evaluation covering many languages, including low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.  
- Analysis on low-resource and non-Latin languages is particularly insightful.  
- Strength: novel feature with comprehensive experimental validation.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on the cross-lingual wikifier, which may introduce errors.  
- Weakness: no analysis of how entities absent from Wikipedia are handled or affected.  
- Weakness: direct transfer performance drops for Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added.  
- Suggestion: analyze error propagation from the wikifier to the NER model to assess robustness.  
- Suggestion: explore different regularization strategies for feature classes to reduce over-reliance on lexical features.  
- Questions for authors: impact of non-Wikipedia entities, error propagation analysis, and regularization experiments for non-Latin scripts.
