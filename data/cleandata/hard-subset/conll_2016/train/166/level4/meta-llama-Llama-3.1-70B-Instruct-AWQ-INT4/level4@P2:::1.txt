This paper proposes a novel approach for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) by leveraging a language-independent method that utilizes cross-lingual wikification. The authors demonstrate that this approach can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia, with the only requirement being a multilingual Wikipedia dump.

**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces a cross-lingual NER model that can be trained on a source language (or multiple source languages) and applied to a target language. The model relies on a cross-lingual wikifier to disambiguate words and phrases in the target language to English Wikipedia entries. The authors show that the corresponding Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types can be used as language-independent features for NER.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Cross-lingual wikification**: The authors propose a method for cross-lingual wikification, which grounds words and phrases in a non-English text to English Wikipedia entries. This allows for the extraction of language-independent features for NER.
2. **Language-independent NER model**: The authors develop a language-independent NER model that can be applied to all languages in Wikipedia. The model uses features based on the Wikipedia categories and FreeBase types of the wikified entities.
3. **Experiments on low-resource languages**: The authors evaluate their model on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, and Tamil) and demonstrate significant improvements over strong baselines.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel approach for cross-lingual NER that leverages cross-lingual wikification. This approach has the potential to improve NER performance for low-resource languages.
2. **Thorough experiments**: The authors conduct a thorough set of experiments to evaluate their model on various languages, including low-resource languages.
3. **Analysis on low-resource languages**: The authors provide an analysis of the performance of their model on low-resource languages, which is particularly interesting.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on cross-lingual wikifier**: The proposed method is strongly dependent on the success of the cross-lingual wikifier. Errors in the wikifier may propagate to the NER model.
2. **Named entities not on Wikipedia**: The authors do not provide results on how named entities that are not on Wikipedia are affected by the proposed method.
3. **Over-reliance on lexical features**: The poor performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added suggests that the model may become over-reliant on lexical features.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the issue of named entities that are not on Wikipedia?
2. Can you provide more details on the error analysis of the cross-lingual wikifier and how it affects the NER model?
3. Have you considered regularizing the various feature classes differently to prevent over-reliance on lexical features?
