This paper proposes an approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition that leverages features from Wikipedia. By utilizing a cross-lingual Wikifier, it identifies English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and extracts features based on the Wikipedia entry. The experimental results demonstrate that this new feature is beneficial not only in the monolingual case but also in the more interesting direct transfer setting, where the English model is tested on a target language.

I found this paper to be compelling, as it introduces a novel feature for named entity recognition and conducts a fairly thorough set of experiments to showcase the feature's utility. The analysis on low-resource languages and non-Latin languages is particularly noteworthy.

However, it would be beneficial to investigate the impact of the proposed method on named entities that do not have a Wikipedia entry. In addition to the results presented in the paper, it would be interesting to see an analysis of how these entities are affected by the proposed method.

The success of the proposed method is heavily dependent on the performance of the cross-lingual Wikifier. Given the additional step in the pipeline, it would be valuable to examine the frequency of errors in prediction due to errors in the Wikifier.

Furthermore, considering the poor performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added, it is worth exploring whether regularizing the various feature classes differently could prevent the model from becoming over-reliant on the lexical features.
