This paper proposes a novel approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition by leveraging features from Wikipedia. The method relies on a cross-lingual Wikifier to identify English Wikipedia articles corresponding to phrases in a target language, and subsequently utilizes features derived from these Wikipedia entries. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this new feature, not only in the monolingual setting but also in the more challenging direct transfer setting, where an English model is evaluated on a target language.

I found this paper to be engaging, as it introduces a new feature for named entity recognition and conducts a comprehensive set of experiments to validate its utility. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly noteworthy.

However, it would be beneficial to investigate the impact of the proposed method on named entities not present in Wikipedia. Supplementing the existing results with an analysis of how these entities are affected would provide a more complete understanding of the method's strengths and limitations.

The success of the proposed method is heavily dependent on the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier. Given the additional step introduced in the pipeline, it is essential to examine the frequency of errors in prediction caused by errors in the Wikifier.

Furthermore, the poor performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added raises questions about the potential benefits of regularizing different feature classes separately. This could prevent the model from becoming overly reliant on lexical features, potentially leading to improved performance.
