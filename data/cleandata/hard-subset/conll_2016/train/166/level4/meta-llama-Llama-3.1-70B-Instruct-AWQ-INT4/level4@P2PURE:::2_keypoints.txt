- Paper explores cross-lingual direct transfer for NER using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; idea is incremental but the combination of two research paths is a useful contribution.  
- Presentation is generally clear and experiments are sufficient and convincing, though novelty is limited.  
- Related work: expand discussion of Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and more clearly emphasize differences from prior Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Run experiments including POS tag features (e.g., universal POS) to empirically justify excluding POS as features.  
- Section 3.3 (modified Tsai and Roth model) is unclear and would benefit from a running example.  
- Report preliminary results combining this approach with Tackstrom et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) and compare to a strong projection baseline to strengthen claims.  
- Provide deeper analysis of surprising cross-lingual training results (e.g., why Dutch best for Spanish, Spanish best for Yoruba) to determine if patterns are meaningful or coincidental.  
- Results in Table 4 show only small improvements and are not consistently strong; include statistical significance testing for those results.  
- Minor: Section 2.1 should mention projection methods that do not require parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulic & Moens 2013).  
- Minor: Paper needs careful proofreading to fix typos and ungrammatical sentences (several examples cited).
