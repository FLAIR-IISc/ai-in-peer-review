- Proposes a novel cross-lingual NER approach that leverages Wikipedia features via a cross-lingual Wikifier mapping target-language phrases to English articles.  
- Uses Wikipedia-derived features for each n-gram and requires only a Wikipedia dump, making the method language-independent.  
- Demonstrates that the Wikipedia feature improves performance in both monolingual and direct-transfer (English→target) settings.  
- Conducts a comprehensive experimental evaluation across many languages, including low-resource and non-Latin scripts.  
- Shows competitive performance against strong baselines and significant improvements in several settings.  
- Analysis of low-resource and non-Latin languages is a notable and interesting contribution.  
- Raises concern about handling named entities that are absent from Wikipedia and how that affects performance.  
- Method depends on the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier, posing risk of prediction errors when the Wikifier mislinks.  
- Observes poor direct-transfer performance on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added.  
- Suggests different regularization for feature classes to prevent overreliance on lexical features, especially for Tamil and Bengali.  
- Main contributions summarized: novel Wikifier-based feature, broad experimental validation, and focused analysis on low-resource/non-Latin languages.  
- Strengths summarized: novel feature, thorough experiments, and strong empirical results.  
- Weaknesses summarized: reliance on Wikifier accuracy, unclear handling of non-Wikipedia entities, and degraded transfer performance for some languages with lexical features.  
- Questions for authors: impact of entities missing from Wikipedia, frequency/impact of Wikifier errors, and whether feature-class regularization would help Tamil/Bengali.
