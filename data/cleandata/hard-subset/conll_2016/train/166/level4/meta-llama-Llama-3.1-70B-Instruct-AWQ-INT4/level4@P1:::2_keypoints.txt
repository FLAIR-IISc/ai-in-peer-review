- Summary: Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model using a wikifier applied to every n-gram, evaluated across many languages with convincing results.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces cross-lingual wikification to generate language-independent NER features.  
- Contribution 2: Presents a direct-transfer NER model that works for all Wikipedia languages and outperforms comparable approaches on CoNLL and low-resource languages.  
- Contribution 3: Shows that training on multiple source languages can further improve NER performance.  
- Strength 1: Effective use of cross-lingual wikification yields strong language-independent features and competitive performance.  
- Strength 2: Extensive experimentation across languages and settings provides convincing empirical support.  
- Strength 3: Demonstrates notable improvements on low-resource languages.  
- Weakness 1: Key idea has limited novelty, building on prior direct transfer and wikification work.  
- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison with strong projection baselines for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis of why certain training languages work better for particular targets.  
- Question 1: Request for a detailed explanation and running example of the edited Tsai and Roth model.  
- Question 2: Have the authors tried combining this approach with orthogonal methods (e.g., projection or Wikipedia-generated training data)?  
- Question 3: Request for statistical significance tests (e.g., for Table 4) to support multi-source improvements.  
- Additional comment: Paper needs careful proofreading and should discuss implications for selecting training languages.
