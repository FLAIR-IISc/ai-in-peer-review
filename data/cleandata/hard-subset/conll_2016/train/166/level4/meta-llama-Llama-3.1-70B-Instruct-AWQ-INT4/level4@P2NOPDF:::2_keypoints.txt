- Paper combines direct cross-lingual NER transfer with recent cross-lingual wikification; valuable despite limited methodological novelty.
- Overall presentation is coherent and results are convincing, supported by experiments and empirical evidence.
- Related work needs expansion: explicitly relate this work to Kazama and Torisawa (2007).
- More clearly emphasize differences between this approach and prior NER systems that used Wikipedia knowledge.
- Include experiments with POS tag features (as in Täckström et al.) to assess their usefulness across languages.
- Add a running example in Section 3.3 to clarify the edited Tsai and Roth model description.
- Report preliminary results combining this approach with orthogonal methods (Täckström et al., Nothman et al.) for select languages.
- Compare against a strong projection baseline to contextualize the benefits/limitations of wikification-based transfer.
- Discuss surprising language-pair findings (Dutch best for Spanish; Spanish best for Yoruba) to determine coincidence vs. underlying causes.
- Conduct statistical significance tests (e.g., for results in Table 4) to support claims about improvements.
- Note that projection can be done without parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013) and cite these alternatives.
- Perform careful proofreading to fix typos and awkward phrasing throughout the paper.
