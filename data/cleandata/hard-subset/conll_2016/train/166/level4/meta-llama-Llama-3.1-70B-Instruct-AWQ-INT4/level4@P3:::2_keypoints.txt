- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model that grounds non-English text to English Wikipedia entries via wikification.  
- Evaluates on multiple languages, including low-resource ones, showing significant gains over strong baselines.  
- Main contribution: novel method to generate language-independent NER features using cross-lingual wikification.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates effective direct transfer of NER models across languages using these features.  
- Main contribution: improves NER performance for low-resource languages.  
- Strength: paper presents a clear, coherent narrative with sufficient empirical support.  
- Strength: experimental results are convincing across a range of languages.  
- Strength: novel application of cross-lingual wikification to NER.  
- Weakness: limited core technical novelty—mainly combines existing direct-transfer and wikification approaches.  
- Weakness: related work discussion is sparse; should better situate with prior Wikipedia-based NER (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007).  
- Weakness: lacks experiments including POS-tag features despite arguing against them.  
- Request: provide a detailed, running-example explanation of the edited Tsai and Roth model.  
- Request: explain why Dutch is best for Spanish and Spanish best for Yoruba—statistical fluke or systematic reason?  
- Request: report preliminary results combining this approach with orthogonal methods (e.g., Täckström et al. 2012; Nothman et al. 2012).  
- Request: include statistical significance testing for the results in Table 4.  
- Additional: paper needs careful proofreading to remove typos and improve clarity.  
- Additional: compare against a strong projection baseline for context.  
- Additional: exploring combinations with other multilingual NER techniques is a promising future direction.
