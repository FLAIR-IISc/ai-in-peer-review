- Proposes a cross-lingual NER approach that leverages cross-lingual wikification to map non-English text to English Wikipedia entries.
- Requires only a multilingual Wikipedia dump, enabling application to all languages present in Wikipedia.
- Trains a model on one or more source languages and applies it to target languages using language-independent features.
- Uses Wikipedia categories and Freebase types of wikified entities as language-independent NER features.
- Evaluates on five low-resource languages (Turkish, Tagalog, Yoruba, Bengali, Tamil) and reports significant improvements over strong baselines.
- Strength: Novel method that could improve NER for low-resource languages via wikification.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple languages.
- Strength: Provides focused analysis on performance in low-resource language settings.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on the quality of the cross-lingual wikifier; errors can propagate to NER.
- Weakness: No reported analysis or results for named entities absent from Wikipedia.
- Weakness: Evidence of over-reliance on lexical features, hurting direct transfer performance in Tamil and Bengali.
- Question: How will the method handle named entities not present in Wikipedia?
- Question: Can the authors provide detailed error analysis of the cross-lingual wikifier and its impact on NER?
- Question: Have the authors considered differential regularization of feature classes to reduce over-reliance on lexical features?
