This paper proposes a novel approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition by leveraging features from Wikipedia. The method utilizes a cross-lingual Wikifier to identify corresponding English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and extracts features based on these entries. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this new feature in both monolingual and direct transfer settings, where an English model is applied to a target language.

I found this paper to be compelling, as it introduces a new feature for named entity recognition and conducts a comprehensive set of experiments to validate its utility. The analysis on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly noteworthy.

However, it would be beneficial to investigate the impact of the proposed method on named entities not present in Wikipedia. Supplementing the paper's results with an analysis of these entities would provide a more complete understanding of the method's performance.

The success of the proposed method heavily relies on the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier. Given the additional step in the pipeline, it would be useful to examine the frequency of errors in prediction caused by errors in the Wikifier.

The poor performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added raises questions about the potential for regularization of different feature classes. This could prevent the model from becoming overly reliant on lexical features, potentially improving its overall performance.
