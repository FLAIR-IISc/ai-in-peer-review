- Proposes a language-independent cross-lingual NER model that grounds non-English text to English Wikipedia using typing info from Wikipedia categories and FreeBase.
- Trains on a source language and directly transfers to all Wikipedia languages without parallel data or language-specific features.
- Uses cross-lingual wikification to provide language-independent features for NER.
- Demonstrates improved performance over comparable methods on CoNLL datasets and low-resource languages.
- Paper is well-structured with convincing empirical results and a coherent narrative.
- Evaluates across a wide range of languages in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.
- Provides robust evaluation on multiple datasets including CoNLL and low-resource languages.
- Key idea lacks strong novelty, combining existing direct transfer and wikification approaches.
- Limited gains when training on multiple languages (small improvements in Table 4 may be insignificant).
- Paper contains typos and would benefit from more careful proofreading.
- Request to clarify relationship to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Ask why POS tags were omitted and request experiments including POS features.
- Request a running example illustrating the edited Tsai and Roth model.
- Question why Dutch is the best source for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba—statistical fluke or systematic reason?
- Ask for preliminary results combining this approach with orthogonal methods (e.g., Tackström et al. 2012, Nothman et al. 2012).
