This paper introduces a novel approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition, leveraging features extracted from Wikipedia. By utilizing a cross-lingual Wikifier, the method identifies corresponding English Wikipedia articles for target language phrases and incorporates features derived from these entries. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this new feature in both monolingual and direct transfer settings, where an English model is applied to a target language.

I found this paper to be compelling, as it presents an innovative feature for named entity recognition and conducts a comprehensive set of experiments to validate its utility. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly noteworthy.

However, it would be beneficial to investigate the impact of the proposed method on named entities not present in Wikipedia, in addition to the results presented in the paper. Furthermore, given the method's reliance on the cross-lingual Wikifier, it is essential to assess the error propagation from the Wikifier to the prediction pipeline and quantify the frequency of errors caused by Wikifier inaccuracies.

The poor performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are incorporated raises questions about the potential benefits of regularization across different feature classes. This could prevent the model from becoming overly dependent on lexical features, thereby improving its robustness and generalizability.
