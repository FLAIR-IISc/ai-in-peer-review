This paper proposes an innovative approach for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) by leveraging features from Wikipedia. The method relies on a cross-lingual Wikifier to identify English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and utilizes features based on the Wikipedia entry. The experiments demonstrate that this new feature is beneficial not only in the monolingual case but also in the more interesting direct transfer setting, where the English model is tested on a target language.

I found this paper engaging, as it introduces a novel feature for NER and conducts a comprehensive set of experiments to showcase the feature's utility. The analysis on low-resource languages and non-Latin languages is particularly noteworthy.

However, it would be interesting to investigate how named entities not present on Wikipedia are affected by the proposed method. Additionally, the method's dependence on the success of the cross-lingual Wikifier raises questions about the frequency of errors in prediction due to errors in the Wikifier.

The poor performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added suggests that it might be beneficial to regularize the various feature classes differently, preventing the model from becoming overly reliant on lexical features.

Summary of the paper:
The paper proposes a language-independent method for cross-lingual NER using a cross-lingual Wikifier to disambiguate every n-gram. This model works on all languages in Wikipedia and requires only a Wikipedia dump. The authors study a wide range of languages in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings, demonstrating significant improvements over strong baselines.

Main contributions:
1. A novel approach for cross-lingual NER using a cross-lingual Wikifier.
2. A comprehensive set of experiments showcasing the feature's utility in both monolingual and cross-lingual settings.
3. An analysis of the method's performance on low-resource languages and non-Latin languages.

Strengths:
1. The paper proposes a novel feature for NER and conducts a thorough set of experiments to demonstrate its utility.
2. The analysis on low-resource languages and non-Latin languages is particularly interesting.
3. The method's performance is competitive with strong baselines.

Weaknesses:
1. The method's dependence on the success of the cross-lingual Wikifier may lead to errors in prediction.
2. The performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are added is poor.
3. The method's ability to handle named entities not present on Wikipedia is unclear.

Questions to authors:
1. How do named entities not present on Wikipedia affect the proposed method's performance?
2. What is the frequency of errors in prediction due to errors in the Wikifier?
3. Would regularizing the various feature classes differently improve the model's performance on Tamil and Bengali?
