- Paper studies cross-lingual direct-transfer NER using cross-lingual wikification; coherent approach with strong empirical validation though not highly novel.
- Strength: Strong empirical results — outperforms baselines on CoNLL languages and yields large gains on low-resource languages with a solid experimental setup.
- Strength: Effective use of cross-lingual wikification — language-independent features grounded in English Wikipedia are well-motivated and validated by ablations.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation — includes monolingual and cross-lingual transfer, Wikipedia-size analysis, multi-source training, comparisons to relevant baselines, and low-resource languages.
- Weakness: Limited novelty — mainly an incremental application of existing direct-transfer and wikification ideas to NER.
- Weakness: Related work coverage is insufficiently detailed, especially regarding prior encyclopedic-knowledge NER systems (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007).
- Weakness: Limited exploration of feature combinations — more experiments (e.g., with POS tags) or explanations for omitted features are needed.
- Weakness: Modest and inconsistent gains from multi-source training; significance testing and deeper analysis of limited improvements are missing.
- Question: Please analyze why Dutch best transfers to Spanish and Spanish to Yoruba — are language or data characteristics explaining this?
- Question: Provide a concrete example showing the modified wikifier in action (grounding n-grams and using FreeBase types).
- Question: Have you tried combining this approach with methods like Täckström et al. (2012) or Nothman et al. (2012)? If so, report results; if not, explain challenges.
- Question: Would results change if a strong projection-based baseline were included in comparisons?
- Additional comment: Acknowledge projection methods that don’t need parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013) in Section 2.1.
- Additional comment: Paper contains minor typos/grammatical errors — careful proofreading recommended.
