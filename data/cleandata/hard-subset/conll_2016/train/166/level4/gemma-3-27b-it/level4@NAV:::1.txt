This paper introduces a novel approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition that leverages features extracted from Wikipedia. The method utilizes a cross-lingual Wikifier to map phrases in a target language to corresponding English Wikipedia articles, subsequently employing features derived from these articles. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this feature, improving performance in both monolingual and, notably, direct transfer scenarios where a model trained on English is evaluated on other languages. 

I found this to be a strong paper, presenting a new and well-motivated feature for NER, supported by a comprehensive experimental evaluation. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly insightful.

However, the method’s behavior when encountering named entities not present in Wikipedia warrants further investigation. Reporting results on such entities would be valuable. 

Furthermore, the reliance on a cross-lingual Wikifier introduces a potential source of error. Quantifying the impact of Wikifier errors on the final prediction accuracy would strengthen the analysis. 

Finally, the observed performance degradation in direct transfer for Tamil and Bengali with the addition of lexical features raises a question about feature regularization. Exploring differential regularization strategies for various feature classes may mitigate over-reliance on lexical information and improve overall performance.
