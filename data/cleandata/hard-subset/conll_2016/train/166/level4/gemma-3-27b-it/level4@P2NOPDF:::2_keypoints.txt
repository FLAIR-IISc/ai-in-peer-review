- Core idea (combining direct transfer with cross-lingual wikification) is incremental but well-supported, with thorough experiments showing effectiveness.
- Related work needs a more detailed connection to Kazama & Torisawa (2007) and clearer differentiation from prior Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Authors should include experiments with POS-tag features (per Täckström et al.) to evaluate their usefulness across languages with Universal POS tagsets.
- Section 3.3 is unclear; add a running example to illustrate the Tsai and Roth model’s functionality.
- Claim of orthogonality to methods like Täckström et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) should be supported with preliminary combination results across languages.
- Include a comparison against a strong projection baseline to better contextualize benefits of the wikification approach.
- The finding that Dutch is best for Spanish training and Spanish for Yoruba is intriguing and warrants deeper analysis to determine if it’s anomalous or meaningful.
- Improvements in Table 4 are modest and inconsistent; report statistical significance tests to strengthen claims.
- Section 2.1 should acknowledge projection methods that don’t require parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013).
- Paper needs careful proofreading to correct typos (e.g., "as a the base model", "They avoid the traditional pipeline of NER then EL by...", "to disambiguate every n-grams").
