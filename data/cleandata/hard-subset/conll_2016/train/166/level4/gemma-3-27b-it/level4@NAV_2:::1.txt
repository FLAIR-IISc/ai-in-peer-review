This paper proposes an approach for multi-lingual named entity recognition using features derived from Wikipedia. By leveraging a cross-lingual Wikifier, the method identifies corresponding English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and utilizes features based on the associated Wikipedia entry. Experiments demonstrate that these novel features enhance performance not only in monolingual scenarios but also, crucially, in the direct transfer setting, where a model trained on English is evaluated on target languages.

I find this paper to be a strong contribution. It introduces a novel feature set for named entity recognition and presents a comprehensive set of experiments to validate its effectiveness. The analysis conducted on low-resource languages and languages with non-Latin scripts is particularly insightful.

However, the method’s reliance on Wikipedia raises a concern: how does it perform on named entities not present in Wikipedia? Including results evaluating performance on such entities would strengthen the paper. 

Furthermore, the proposed method introduces a cross-lingual wikifier as an additional step in the pipeline. Assessing the frequency of prediction errors stemming from inaccuracies within the wikifier itself would be valuable.

Finally, observing the diminished performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are incorporated, I question whether employing differential regularization of the various feature classes could prevent the model from becoming overly dependent on lexical information.
