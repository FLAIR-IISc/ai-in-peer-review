- Paper is incremental (no core new technology) but presents a coherent story with sufficient experiments and convincing results.
- Related work section needs a more detailed comparison to Kazama and Torisawa (2007).
- Clearly emphasize differences from other NER systems that use Wikipedia/encyclopedic knowledge to aid contextualization.
- Authors should report experiments including POS tag features (as in Täckström et al.) to assess their usefulness across languages.
- Section 3.3 (modified Tsai and Roth model) is unclear and would benefit from a running example.
- Provide preliminary results combining the proposed approach with orthogonal methods (Täckström et al., Nothman et al.) to show complementarity.
- Compare against a strong projection baseline to better contextualize the usefulness/limitations of wikification-based transfer.
- Discuss unexpected language pair results (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish, Spanish best for Yoruba) with deeper analysis rather than leaving it unexplained.
- Results in Table 4 show only small, inconsistent improvements; report statistical significance tests to support claims.
- Note that projection can be done without parallel data; cite Peirsman & Pado (2009) and Vulić & Moens (2013) as alternatives using bilingual semantic spaces.
- Address numerous typos and proofreading issues (several ungrammatical sentences and wording errors).
- Overall presentation could be improved in clarity and contextual comparisons despite the generally positive empirical contribution.
