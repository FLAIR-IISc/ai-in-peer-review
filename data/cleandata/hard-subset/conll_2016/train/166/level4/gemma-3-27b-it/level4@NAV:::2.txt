This paper investigates cross-lingual NER transfer leveraging a recent cross-lingual wikification model. While the core concept of combining direct transfer with cross-lingual wikification is incremental rather than fundamentally novel, the work presents a coherent narrative, supported by comprehensive experiments and convincing results. Overall, I find the paper well-executed and valuable. However, I have several suggestions regarding its presentation.

Regarding related work, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of its relationship to Kazama and Torisawa (2007). Additionally, a clearer articulation of the distinctions between this work and other NER systems utilizing Wikipedia knowledge is needed, even though these differences are touched upon; emphasizing them will enhance the paper’s context and readability. 

Although the authors provide justification for excluding POS tags, including experiments with POS tag features—similar to those conducted by Tackstrom et al.—would offer valuable empirical insight into their utility across different languages, particularly those with available universal POS tagsets.

Section 3.3 could be improved with a concrete, illustrative example to clarify the mechanics of the Tsai and Roth model. The current description lacks sufficient clarity. 

Given the authors’ assertion that their approach is orthogonal to, and combinable with, existing work such as Tackstrom et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012), providing preliminary results demonstrating this combination across a selection of languages would strengthen the discussion. Similarly, comparing performance against a strong projection baseline would further contextualize the findings and highlight the benefits of a wikification-based approach.

The observation that Dutch is the optimal training language for Spanish, and Spanish for Yoruba, is intriguing. A more in-depth analysis of these results – moving beyond a simple statistical observation – would be highly beneficial.

The improvements reported in Table 4 are marginal and inconsistent. Performing statistical significance testing on these results would provide stronger support for the presented claims.

Minor comments:

- Section 2.1: It is important to note that projection methods are not limited to those requiring parallel data. Approaches like Peirsman and Pado (NAACL 2009) and Vulic and Moens (EMNLP 2013) utilize bilingual semantic spaces for transfer, increasing their applicability to resource-scarce languages.
- The paper would significantly benefit from careful proofreading to address several identified typographical errors (e.g., Section 3, first sentence: "as a the base model"; Page 3: "They avoid the traditional pipeline of NER then EL by..."; Page 8: "to disambiguate every n-grams").
