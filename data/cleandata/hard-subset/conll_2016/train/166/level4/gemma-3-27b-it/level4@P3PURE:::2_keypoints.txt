- Paper studies cross-lingual direct-transfer NER using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; contribution is incremental but valuable.  
- Writing, narrative, experiments, and empirical support are generally solid and convincing.  
- Related work needs deeper discussion of Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and clearer articulation of differences from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.  
- Justification for excluding POS features is given but authors should run experiments including POS features (e.g., as in Täckström et al.) to empirically validate that choice.  
- Section 3.3 (Tsai and Roth model) is unclear and would benefit from a running example to improve comprehension.  
- Authors claim orthogonality with Täckström et al. and Nothman et al.; they should report preliminary results combining these approaches on some languages.  
- Include a comparison against a strong projection baseline to better contextualize the benefits/limits of the wikification approach.  
- Unexpected language-specific transfer patterns (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) merit analysis and hypotheses beyond statistical coincidence.  
- Improvements in Table 4 are modest and inconsistent; perform and report statistical significance tests.  
- Broaden the projection-methods discussion to include non-parallel approaches (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013).  
- Address numerous typographical and phrasing errors through careful proofreading (several example sentences cited).
