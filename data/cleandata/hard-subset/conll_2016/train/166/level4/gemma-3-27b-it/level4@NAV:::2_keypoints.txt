- Overall assessment: paper is well-executed with a coherent narrative, comprehensive experiments, and convincing results, but the core idea is incremental rather than fundamentally novel.
- Related work: expand discussion of Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and more clearly articulate distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- POS tags: include experiments with POS tag features (e.g., as in Täckström et al.) to evaluate their utility across languages.
- Section 3.3 clarity: add a concrete illustrative example to clarify the mechanics of the Tsai and Roth model.
- Method combinations: provide preliminary results combining the proposed approach with prior methods (Täckström et al., Nothman et al.) to support claims of orthogonality and complementarity.
- Baseline comparisons: compare against a strong projection baseline to better contextualize the benefits of the wikification-based approach.
- Language transfer analysis: perform deeper analysis of intriguing transfer patterns (e.g., Dutch→Spanish, Spanish→Yoruba) beyond simple statistics.
- Results robustness: the improvements in Table 4 are marginal/inconsistent; conduct statistical significance testing to strengthen claims.
- Projection methods note: acknowledge that projection approaches are not limited to parallel data and cite alternatives using bilingual semantic spaces (Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013).
- Presentation polish: carefully proofread to fix typographical and grammatical errors throughout the paper.
