This paper proposes an approach for multi-lingual named entity recognition leveraging features derived from Wikipedia. By employing a cross-lingual Wikifier, the method identifies corresponding English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and utilizes features extracted from these entries. Experiments demonstrate that this novel feature improves performance not only in monolingual settings, but also—and more notably—in direct transfer learning, where a model trained on English is evaluated on a target language.

I found this to be a strong paper. It introduces a new feature for named entity recognition and provides a fairly thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating its utility. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly insightful.

However, the method’s reliance on Wikipedia raises a question: how does it perform on named entities not present in Wikipedia? It would be beneficial to see results assessing the impact of the proposed method on such entities, in addition to the findings already presented.

Furthermore, the proposed approach is critically dependent on the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier. Given the addition of this component to the pipeline, it is important to understand the frequency with which errors in the Wikifier contribute to errors in named entity prediction.

Finally, considering the degraded performance observed in direct transfer for Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are incorporated, I wonder if differential regularization of the various feature classes could be explored. This might prevent the model from becoming overly reliant on lexical features and potentially improve generalization.
