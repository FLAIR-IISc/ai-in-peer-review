This paper introduces a novel approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition by incorporating features derived from Wikipedia. Leveraging a cross-lingual Wikifier, the method identifies corresponding English Wikipedia articles for phrases in the target language and utilizes features extracted from these entries. Experimental results demonstrate that this new feature improves performance not only in monolingual settings, but also—and more notably—in direct transfer scenarios where an English model is evaluated on a target language. 

I found this to be a strong paper. It presents a valuable new feature for named entity recognition and supports its utility with a comprehensive set of experiments. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly insightful.

However, the approach’s reliance on Wikipedia raises a question: how does it perform with named entities that lack corresponding Wikipedia pages? Reporting results on the impact of this method on such entities would be a worthwhile addition. 

Furthermore, the method’s performance is intrinsically tied to the accuracy of the cross-lingual Wikifier. It would be helpful to understand the frequency with which Wikifier errors contribute to prediction errors in the overall pipeline.

Finally, given the degraded performance observed in direct transfer for Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are incorporated, I suggest exploring differential regularization of feature classes to mitigate potential over-reliance on lexical information.
