This paper proposes an approach for multilingual named entity recognition using features derived from Wikipedia. By leveraging a cross-lingual Wikifier, the method identifies corresponding English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and utilizes features based on these entries. Experiments demonstrate that this novel feature benefits performance not only in monolingual settings but also, and more notably, in the direct transfer scenario, where a model trained on English is evaluated on a target language.

I found this paper to be strong and well-executed. It introduces a new feature for named entity recognition and provides a comprehensive set of experiments to demonstrate its utility. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and those with non-Latin scripts is particularly insightful.

However, the method's reliance on Wikipedia raises a question: how does the approach handle named entities that are not present on Wikipedia?  Beyond the results already presented, it would be valuable to see an analysis of the impact of this limitation on performance.

Furthermore, the proposed method introduces an additional step – the cross-lingual wikifier – into the pipeline.  Understanding the error propagation is crucial; how often do prediction errors stem from inaccuracies in the wikifier itself? 

Finally, given the degraded performance of direct transfer on Tamil and Bengali when lexical features are incorporated, I wonder if differential regularization of the various feature classes might be beneficial. This could prevent the model from becoming overly reliant on lexical features, which appear less effective in these languages.
