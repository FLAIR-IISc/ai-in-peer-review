- Paper investigates cross-lingual NER transfer using a recent cross-lingual wikification model; well-executed with coherent narrative and convincing empirical results.
- Core idea not highly novel — incremental contribution combining direct transfer for NER/POS/parsing with recent wikification advances.
- Related work needs deeper discussion, especially connection to Kazama and Torisawa (2007).
- Explicitly articulate distinctions between this system and other Wikipedia-based NER systems to improve clarity.
- Although POS tag exclusion is justified, include experiments with POS features (e.g., following Täckström et al.) to evaluate their utility across languages.
- Section 3.3 (Tsai and Roth model) should include a running example to clarify its operation.
- Claim of orthogonality to Täckström et al. (2012) and Nothman et al. (2012) would be strengthened by preliminary combined-model results on some languages.
- Compare with a strong projection baseline as additional context despite orthogonality to show benefits/limitations of wikification approach.
- The Dutch→Spanish and Spanish→Yoruba transfer observations are intriguing and warrant a deeper analysis to determine if they are coincidental or systematic.
- Improvements in Table 4 are modest and inconsistent; report statistical significance tests for those results.
- Minor: acknowledge projection methods that do not rely on parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013).
- Minor: careful proofreading needed to fix several typos and ungrammatical sentences throughout the paper.
