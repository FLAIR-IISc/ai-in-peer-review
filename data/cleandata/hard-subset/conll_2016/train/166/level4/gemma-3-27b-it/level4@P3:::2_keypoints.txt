- Proposes cross-lingual NER via wikification to English Wikipedia to produce language-independent features for transfer from resource-rich to low-resource languages.
- Demonstrates effectiveness of wikification features with strong performance on CoNLL and several low-resource languages.
- Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation across nine languages, analyzing Wikipedia size effects and multi-source training benefits.
- Presents a practical, easily-replicable methodology that relies only on multilingual Wikipedia (no parallel corpora or language-specific rules).
- Shows consistently strong empirical results, outperforming comparable approaches on standard and low-resource datasets.
- Conducts insightful ablation studies (e.g., removing FreeBase queries, varying interlanguage link density) and examines gazetteer and Wikipedia-size effects.
- Paper is generally clear and coherent, with a well-motivated introduction and organized experimental setup.
- The core idea is incremental; novelty over prior direct-transfer and wikification work is limited and should be more explicitly positioned.
- Related work lacks detailed comparison with other Wikipedia/knowledge-based NER systems (e.g., Kazama & Torisawa 2007).
- Limited investigation of feature interactions with other techniques (projection, silver-standard data); needs systematic combination experiments and strong projection baselines.
- Multi-source training improvements are modest, presented without statistical significance testing or analysis of which language combinations help most.
- The observed language-specific training effects (e.g., Dutch best for Spanish, Spanish for Yoruba) are unexplained and require further analysis.
- Request to provide statistical significance tests for Table 4 results.
- Ask for explanation of why Dutch/Spanish are especially effective training languages for the noted targets.
- Request clarification of computational cost of the cross-lingual wikification process for large datasets.
- Ask for a clearer, example-driven description of the edited Tsai and Roth (2016) system used in section 3.3.
- Suggest mentioning projection approaches that don’t require parallel data (e.g., bilingual semantic spaces by Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulic & Moens 2013).
- Recommend proofreading to fix typos (examples noted).
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable incremental contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
