- Proposes multilingual NER using cross-lingual wikification to extract language-independent Wikipedia-derived features.  
- Shows improved performance in monolingual and direct transfer (English→target) settings.  
- Contribution: demonstrates Wikipedia-based features as an alternative to parallel corpora or language-specific rules.  
- Contribution: comprehensive evaluation across resource-rich and low-resource languages, including non-Latin scripts.  
- Contribution: analyzes impact of Wikipedia size and explores multi-source training for practical insights.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, outperforming comparable approaches on CoNLL and low-resource languages.  
- Strength: thorough experiments varying features, training languages, and language sets, increasing confidence in generalizability.  
- Strength: insightful error analysis probing Wikipedia size, FreeBase link removal, and interlanguage connections.  
- Strength: practical methodology requiring only a multilingual Wikipedia dump, reducing need for parallel data or annotations.  
- Strength: clear, well-explained presentation with helpful illustrative figure.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on the cross-lingual wikifier with insufficient quantification of wikification errors and their effect.  
- Weakness: struggles with entities absent from Wikipedia and lacks dedicated evaluation or mitigation strategies for these cases.  
- Weakness: lexical features hurt performance on Tamil and Bengali, indicating a need for feature weighting or regularization exploration.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods.  
- Question: what is the frequency of incorrect wikifications and how much do they contribute to overall NER errors?  
- Question: how does the method perform on named entities not present in Wikipedia (results on a dedicated test set)?  
- Question: were feature regularization methods tried to address lexical feature degradation on low-resource languages, and what were the results?  
- Comment: clarify the notation "W.−FB query" in Table 3.  
- Comment: expand the multi-source training discussion and include a table showing gains from each added language.  
- Comment: discuss computational cost of cross-lingual wikification for large documents or many languages.
