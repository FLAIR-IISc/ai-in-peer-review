This paper presents an approach to multi-lingual named entity recognition that leverages features derived from Wikipedia. The method utilizes a cross-lingual Wikifier to identify corresponding English Wikipedia articles for phrases in the target language, and then employs features extracted from those articles. Experiments demonstrate that these new features are beneficial not only in monolingual scenarios, but also—and more significantly—in a direct transfer setting, where a model trained on English is evaluated on a target language. 

I found this to be a strong paper. It introduces a novel feature for named entity recognition and provides a comprehensive set of experiments to validate its effectiveness. The analysis focusing on low-resource languages and non-Latin scripts is particularly insightful.

However, the paper does not address the performance of the method when encountering named entities not present in Wikipedia. It would be valuable to see results exploring the impact of this limitation on the proposed approach. 

The method’s reliance on a successful cross-lingual Wikifier is a key consideration. Introducing this additional step into the pipeline raises the question of how often errors in the Wikifier contribute to errors in the final prediction. Quantifying this effect would strengthen the analysis.

Finally, the observed performance degradation with the addition of lexical features during direct transfer for Tamil and Bengali prompts an interesting question. Would it be possible to apply differential regularization to the various feature classes, preventing the model from becoming overly dependent on lexical information? Investigating this could potentially improve performance in these challenging scenarios.
