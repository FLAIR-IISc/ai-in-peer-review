- Proposes a novel multilingual NER method that uses a cross-lingual Wikifier to map phrases to English Wikipedia and extract Wikipedia-derived features.  
- Shows that Wikipedia-based features improve NER performance in monolingual settings and notably in direct-transfer (English-to-target) evaluations.  
- Paper is a valuable, well-motivated contribution with comprehensive experiments, reproducibility details, and insightful analysis for low-resource and non-Latin-script languages.  
- Major limitation: heavy reliance on Wikipedia coverage; lacks analysis/discussion of how the system handles entities absent from Wikipedia and whether performance degrades gracefully.  
- Raises concern about the cross-lingual Wikifier quality; the paper should quantify Wikifier error rates and how those errors propagate to incorrect NER predictions.  
- Suggests analyzing types of Wikifier errors (wrong links vs. failed links) and measuring their individual impacts on NER robustness.  
- Notes that adding lexical features decreased performance for Tamil and Bengali in direct transfer, indicating potential over-reliance on lexical cues or misbalanced features.  
- Recommends exploring feature-specific regularization or dynamic weighting schemes to better balance feature contributions and improve results on challenging languages.
