This paper proposes a method for identifying multilingual entities using features from Wikipedia. Relying on an interlingual Wikifire, it identifies English Wikipedia articles for phrases in a target language and uses features based on Wikipedia entries. Experiments show that this new feature helps not only in monolingual cases but also in more interesting direct translation settings, where the English model is tested in a target language.

I liked this paper. It proposes a new feature for identifying named entities and conducts several thorough experiments to demonstrate the usefulness of the feature. The analysis on low-resource and non-Latin languages is particularly interesting.

But what about named entities that are not on Wikipedia? In addition to the results of the paper, it would be interesting to see the results of how these entities are affected by the proposed method.

The proposed method strongly depends on the success of interlingual Wikifire. With this additional step in the pipeline, how often do we get errors in predictions due to Wikifire errors?

Considering the poor performance of direct translation after adding lexical features in Tamil and Bengali, I am wondering if it is possible to regularize the different feature classes differently, so that the model does not become overly dependent on lexical features.
