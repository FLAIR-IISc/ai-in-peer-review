This paper proposes an approach for multilingual named entity recognition using features from Wikipedia. Relying on a cross-lingual Wikifier, it identifies English Wikipedia articles for phrases in the target language and uses features based on the Wikipedia entry. Experiments show that this new feature not only helps in monolingual cases but also in more interesting direct translation settings, where the English model is tested on a target language.

I liked this paper. It proposes a new feature for named entity recognition and conducts a fairly extensive set of experiments to demonstrate the utility of the feature. The analysis on low-resource and non-Latin languages is particularly interesting.

But what about named entities that are not on Wikipedia? Besides the results in the paper, it would be interesting to see how the proposed method affects these entities.

The proposed method relies heavily on the success of the cross-lingual Wikifier. With this additional step in the pipeline, how often do we encounter errors in predictions due to errors in the Wikifier?

Given the poor performance of direct translation on Tamil and Bengali when adding literal features, I wonder if it is possible to regulate different feature classes differently, so that the model does not become overly dependent on literal features.
