- Main idea is incremental: uses cross-lingual wikification for NER transfer but lacks fundamental novelty.
- Paper is well-structured with a clear narrative and convincing empirical evidence.
- Related work needs stronger connection to Kazama and Torisawa (2007) and clearer distinctions from other Wikipedia-based NER systems.
- Include experiments with POS-tag features (e.g., Tackström et al.) to empirically assess their utility across languages.
- Section 3.3 is unclear; add a running example to clarify the modified Tsai and Roth model.
- Authors claim orthogonality with Tackström et al. and Nothman et al.; provide preliminary combined-results for some languages.
- Compare the approach to a strong projection-based baseline to better contextualize wikification methods.
- Investigate why Dutch works best for Spanish and Spanish for Yoruba—discuss possible linguistic or structural reasons vs. coincidence.
- Table 4 shows minor and inconsistent improvements; report statistical significance tests to support claims.
- Note that projection can be achieved without parallel data (e.g., Peirsman & Pado 2009; Vulić & Moens 2013) and should be mentioned.
- Paper contains typos and grammatical issues; needs careful proofreading (examples cited).
