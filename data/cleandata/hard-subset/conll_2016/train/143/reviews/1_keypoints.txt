- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (dictionary->multilingual clusters), multiCCA (project languages into English anchor via bilingual dictionaries), multiSkip (extend bilingual skip objective to all parallel corpora), and Translation Invariance (low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment terms, possibly bilingual-only).  
- Evaluation: modified QVEC using CCA to align embeddings with linguistic features, producing rotation- and linear-invariant scores and extended to multilingual evaluation.  
- Paper is generally well written and clearly described.  
- Major issue 1: Unclear novelty relative to Gardner et al.'s translation invariance — need explicit explanation of what is new (e.g., multilingual extension).  
- Major issue 2: Using supersense annotations across multiple languages risks a tiny intersection of features — how is this addressed beyond footnote 9?  
- Major issue 3: Coverage likely affects scores in Table 2 (e.g., dependency parsing shows differing coverage for multiCluster vs multiCCA with similar scores) — need analysis of coverage impact.  
- Major issue 4: Table 3 results are inconsistent: intrinsic metrics often do not favor multilingual techniques, translation invariance wins word-translation by far, and multiCluster (which discards inter-cluster semantics) performs best on extrinsic tasks — this is puzzling and needs explanation.  
- Question 1: What is the loss in performance when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser, and what gains occur from simply replacing random embeddings with these pretrained embeddings in the LSTM parser?  
- Question 2: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Question 3: Are there advantages to multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings and then using multi-CCA to project between spaces?  
- Question 4: Dictionary extraction via parallel alignments or Google Translate may not reflect real lexicons — did the authors evaluate with real multilingual dictionaries?
