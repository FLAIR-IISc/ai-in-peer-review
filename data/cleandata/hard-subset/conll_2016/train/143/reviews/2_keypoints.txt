- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-motivated).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC’s correlation with CCA and incorporates multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluates methods on a wide range of tasks via the authors’ web portal; proposed methods sometimes outperform baselines.
- Paper is well written and meticulously documented.
- Main criticism: the paper favors breadth over depth and lacks thorough discussion of results and design choices.
- Requests deeper analysis of the conflicting MultiCluster results between the 59- and 12-language setups.
- Requests investigation of the effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.
- Recommends including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (e.g., in Table 1) to support the QVEC-CCA improvement claim.
- Minor wording issue: phrasing “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” (l.304) is misleading.
- Formatting issue: two equations on p.3 exceed the column margin.
- Citation inconsistency: Lines 121/147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip, but section 2.3 only mentions Luong et al.; clarify correspondences.
- Suggests adding more related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context (lists several relevant papers).
- Concludes paper has high practical value (improved QVEC measure, web portal) but provides limited new research insights.
