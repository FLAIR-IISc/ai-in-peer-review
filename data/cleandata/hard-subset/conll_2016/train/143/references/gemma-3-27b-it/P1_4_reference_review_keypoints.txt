- Paper proposes dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), an improved evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a public web portal for evaluation.  
- Strongest contribution is the publicly available evaluation portal providing standardized benchmarks and upload/evaluation capabilities.  
- multiQVEC-CCA and analysis of existing metrics address shortcomings of QVEC and better correlate with downstream tasks.  
- Demonstrates practical application of dictionary-based methods at scale across 59 languages.  
- Clear motivation and problem statement advocating scalable methods that avoid heavy parallel data reliance.  
- Comprehensive evaluation using diverse intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, including document classification and dependency parsing.  
- Practicality and reproducibility are emphasized via released code, portal, and detailed data/training descriptions.  
- Rigorous correlation analysis shows multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA align better with downstream performance.  
- Scalability shown by applying methods to 59 languages, extending prior scope.  
- Methods lack fundamental algorithmic novelty, mainly extending existing clustering and CCA techniques.  
- Reported performance gains (e.g., multiCCA vs. alternatives) are inconsistent and may lack statistical significance.  
- Heavy dependence on bilingual dictionary quality, with concerns about noise/bias from Google Translate-generated dictionaries.  
- Extrinsic task setup is oversimplified by removing all features except embeddings, potentially unrealistic and limiting.  
- Missing detailed error analysis to explain when and why methods succeed or fail.  
- Reviewer requests: analysis of dictionary quality/filtering and its impact, statistical significance tests for results, justification of extrinsic task design, qualitative error examples, and computational cost breakdown.  
- Additional notes: paper is well-written with comprehensive related work; recommend adding broader limitations discussion.  
- Overall: solid, valuable contribution (portal and evaluation metrics especially); recommend acceptance contingent on addressing concerns.
