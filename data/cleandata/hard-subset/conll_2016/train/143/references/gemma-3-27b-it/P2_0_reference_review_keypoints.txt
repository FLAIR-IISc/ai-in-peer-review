- Paper proposes two methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for embeddings across 59 languages and adapts QVEC to multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, plus releases an evaluation web portal.  
- The evaluation portal and comprehensive intrinsic/extrinsic metrics are the paper's most significant contribution and a valuable community resource.  
- multiCCA sensibly extends bilingual CCA to multilingual settings, projecting into English space and showing consistent performance.  
- The empirical experiments across many languages and metrics provide a useful baseline and insightful analysis.  
- The authors perform a rigorous evaluation, including correlation analysis and improvements to QVEC (multiQVEC-CCA).  
- Training and evaluating embeddings at the scale of 59 languages is a substantial and useful effort.  
- The paper is clearly written with a logical flow and understandable methodology.  
- multiCluster lacks novelty, underperforms, and may introduce semantic ambiguity through clustering translational equivalents.  
- Both methods depend heavily on bilingual dictionary quality; the impact of noisy or incomplete dictionaries is insufficiently explored.  
- Using Google Translate to create dictionaries for 47 languages is a potential weakness due to variable MT quality.  
- The paper lacks qualitative error analysis explaining why methods succeed or fail on specific linguistic phenomena.  
- Comparisons to other methods are limited; more direct benchmarking against recent transformer-based multilingual approaches is needed.  
- Questions: sensitivity to dictionary quality and noise, limitations of Google Translate-derived dictionaries, effects of projecting into non-English central languages, and plans for updating/maintaining the portal.  
- Additional notes: code/data availability is excellent; minor edits could improve clarity; add discussion of computational costs.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—multiCCA and the evaluation portal (especially the portal) make the paper a valuable contribution despite multiCluster’s limited novelty.
