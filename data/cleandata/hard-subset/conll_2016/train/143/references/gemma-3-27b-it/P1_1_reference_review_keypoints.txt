- Paper studies dictionary-based multilingual embeddings for 59 languages, proposing multiCluster and multiCCA, and introduces multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA plus a public web evaluation portal.  
- Major contribution: a comprehensive evaluation framework (multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA) and a web portal that standardizes and improves multilingual embedding evaluation.  
- Major contribution: demonstrates scalability of dictionary-based methods to 59 languages, an important engineering result despite limited methodological novelty.  
- Major contribution: provides a strong empirical baseline and releases training/evaluation data to support reproducibility.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA addresses shortcomings of prior intrinsic metrics and correlates with downstream tasks, validating its usefulness.  
- Strength: the public web portal lowers barriers for researchers and includes dev/test datasets for consistent comparisons.  
- Strength: experiments are thorough, using multiple metrics, many languages, ablations, coverage analysis, and released code/data.  
- Strength: the paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Weakness: the embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) are incremental extensions of existing techniques and deliver modest performance gains.  
- Weakness: heavy sensitivity to bilingual dictionary quality, with concerns about noise and bias from Google Translate–generated dictionaries and a fixed τ threshold that may not be optimal across pairs.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases—insufficient exploration of why methods vary by language family, resource level, or dictionary size/quality.  
- Weakness: downstream tasks (document classification, dependency parsing) are standard and may not fully capture benefits for tasks like MT or cross-lingual IR.  
- Question: request for detailed analysis of dictionary quality, mitigation of noise/bias from auto-generated dictionaries, and per-pair performance variation.  
- Question: was the τ threshold tuned or varied, and how sensitive are results to different τ values?  
- Question: what were the primary computational bottlenecks when scaling to 59 languages and what optimizations are possible?  
- Question: given the incremental novelty, what future directions do the authors foresee for more innovative multilingual embedding algorithms?  
- Additional comment: ensure the GitHub URL is included in the final version.  
- Additional comment: add a discussion of limitations and potential biases of the proposed evaluation metrics.  
- Additional comment: include a table summarizing resources per language (monolingual data size, dictionary size).
