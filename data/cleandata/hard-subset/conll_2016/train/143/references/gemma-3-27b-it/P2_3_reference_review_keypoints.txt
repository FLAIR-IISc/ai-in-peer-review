- Paper introduces two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings across 59 languages and a new evaluation metric/portal (multiQVEC-CCA).
- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA metric and public evaluation portal provide standardized, robust evaluation for multilingual embeddings.
- Contribution: multiCCA and multiCluster offer scalable, dictionary-based approaches that avoid reliance on parallel corpora, aiding low-resource languages.
- Contribution: Thorough empirical comparisons and correlation analysis between intrinsic and extrinsic metrics clarify method strengths/weaknesses.
- Strength: Fills a critical gap by providing reproducible, comparable evaluation resources and shows improved correlation of multiQVEC-CCA with downstream tasks.
- Strength: Practical and scalable approach based on bilingual dictionaries allows coverage of 59 languages without expensive parallel data.
- Strength: Well-designed experiments with multiple metrics and reported coverage percentages increase credibility of results.
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with well-explained equations and methodology.
- Weakness: Core methods build heavily on prior work, so technical novelty is limited; main novelty is scale and evaluation.
- Weakness: Noticeable performance gap and coverage differences between multiCCA and multiCluster, with insufficient analysis of why multiCluster underperforms.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on bilingual dictionary quality (some from Google Translate) introduces potential noise and bias without sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Lack of detailed error-case analysis to identify which translation pairs or phenomena are poorly represented.
- Question: Please analyze why multiCluster underperforms versus multiCCA at 59 languages—semantic ambiguity, cluster size, dictionary quality, or other factors?
- Question: Were any methods used to mitigate coverage differences in evaluation (e.g., intersected vocabularies or weighting) beyond reporting coverage?
- Question: How sensitive are results to dictionary quality and were alternative dictionary sources or reliability assessments explored?
- Question: What computational resources are required for training/evaluating the 59-language setup to assess practicality for limited-resource researchers?
- Additional: Update placeholder links to software and datasets before final version.
- Additional: Ensure consistent reference formatting throughout the paper.
- Overall assessment: Recommend Accept with minor revisions—the evaluation framework and resources are valuable despite limited methodological novelty.
