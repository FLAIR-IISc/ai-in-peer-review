- Paper proposes learning massively multilingual embeddings from monolingual data plus bilingual dictionaries, introducing multiCluster and multiCCA approaches.  
- Major contribution is multiQVEC-CCA and a publicly available evaluation portal to standardize intrinsic evaluation.  
- multiQVEC-CCA is theoretically justified and empirically validated as a more robust intrinsic metric.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation across 59 languages provides a useful benchmark and highlights scaling challenges for dictionary-based methods.  
- Addresses an important, practical problem by avoiding dependence on parallel corpora and using readily available resources.  
- Demonstrates rigorous evaluation with both intrinsic and extrinsic tasks and correlation analyses.  
- Releases code, data, and an evaluation portal, improving reproducibility and accessibility.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and situates contributions within related work.  
- Provides insightful error analysis, notably on semantic ambiguity affecting multiCluster.  
- Novelty of embedding methods is limited—the main innovation is their application at massive multilingual scale rather than new algorithms.  
- Reliance on automatically generated dictionaries (Google Translate) raises concerns about noise, bias, and the arbitrary τ=0.1 threshold.  
- Paper lacks detailed analysis of computational scalability, memory, and training-time bottlenecks as languages scale.  
- Performance varies across metrics with insufficient explanation of why methods succeed on particular tasks.  
- Requests clarification on dictionary quality evaluation and the impact of dictionary noise.  
- Asks for detailed scalability metrics (memory, CPU time) and how training time scales with languages/corpus size.  
- Seeks justification/experiments for choosing τ=0.1 and whether adaptive thresholds would help.  
- Queries sensitivity of multiCluster to maximum cluster size and alternative clustering strategies.  
- Suggests experiments combining embeddings with other features for extrinsic tasks to assess embeddings’ standalone value.  
- Recommends discussing potential biases in training data and adding a table summarizing corpora/dictionaries per language.  
- Suggests improving documentation for the evaluation portal; overall recommendation: accept pending responses to the authors’ questions.
