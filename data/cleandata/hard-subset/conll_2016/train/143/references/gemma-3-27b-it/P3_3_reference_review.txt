## Review of "Massively Multilingual Word Embeddings"

**Summary:** This paper investigates methods for creating word embeddings across 59 languages using dictionary-based approaches, namely `multiCluster` and `multiCCA`.  A key component is the development and evaluation of new metrics for assessing the quality of these multilingual embeddings – `multiQVEC` and `multiQVEC-CCA` – and the release of a public evaluation portal.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Comprehensive Evaluation Framework:** The development of `multiQVEC` & `multiQVEC-CCA` along with the publicly available evaluation portal is the most significant contribution.  Existing evaluation methods for multilingual embeddings were lacking, and this work provides a much-needed, standardized, and accessible resource for the community.
2. **Demonstration of Dictionary-Based Methods at Scale:** Successfully applying dictionary-based alignment to create embeddings for 59 languages is a substantial engineering achievement.  It highlights the viability of this approach for resource-rich multilingual scenarios.
3. **Empirical Analysis of Evaluation Metric Correlation:** The correlation study between intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation metrics provides valuable insight into which metrics are most reliable indicators of downstream task performance.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Motivation & Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the need for massively multilingual embeddings and positions this work within the broader context of multilingual NLP.  The benefits of cross-lingual transfer and improved machine translation are well-established and provide a strong rationale for the research.
2. **Thorough Experimental Setup:** The experiments are comprehensive, including a substantial number of languages, multiple evaluation metrics, and comparisons to established baselines. The availability of code and data via the evaluation portal is a huge benefit to reproducibility and further research.
3. **Well-Justified Evaluation Metrics:**  The critique of existing evaluation methods (specifically QVEC) and the development of `multiQVEC-CCA` to address these shortcomings are compelling. The explanations of the theoretical issues and the improvements are clear.
4. **Practical Impact of the Portal:**  The evaluation portal is a significant contribution to the field. It lowers the barrier to entry for researchers working on multilingual embeddings and promotes standardized evaluation practices.
5. **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the methodology and understand the results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Embedding Methods:** While `multiCluster` and `multiCCA` are reasonable approaches, they build directly upon existing methods (CCA and clustering) and don’t introduce significant algorithmic novelty. The core innovation resides in the scale of application and the evaluation framework.  The paper acknowledges this, but it’s worth explicitly stating the limitations.
2. **Dictionary Quality Dependency:** The performance of the dictionary-based methods is heavily reliant on the quality of the bilingual dictionaries. The paper acknowledges the issue of dictionary creation using Google Translate, but a more detailed analysis of the impact of dictionary quality on the results would be beneficial. The use of automatically generated dictionaries introduces potential noise and bias.
3. **Extrinsic Task Limitations:**  The extrinsic tasks chosen (document classification and dependency parsing) are somewhat limited in their scope. While valuable, they don’t fully capture the potential benefits of multilingual embeddings in all downstream applications (e.g., machine translation). A more diverse set of downstream tasks would strengthen the evaluation.
4. **Coverage Issues & Vocabulary Discrepancies:** The coverage percentages reported in Table 3 highlight a substantial difference in vocabulary coverage between the different methods.  While the authors attempt to address this, the impact on the evaluation is still a concern. A more robust comparison would involve restricting the evaluation to a common vocabulary.
5. **Lack of Ablation Studies:**  The paper would benefit from ablation studies to understand the contribution of different components of the proposed methods. For example, how does the choice of cluster size in `multiCluster` affect performance?



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the quality of the automatically generated dictionaries (particularly for languages beyond the 12 with parallel data) and how this might affect the results?  What steps were taken to filter noisy translations?
2.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of the threshold τ in the dictionary creation process? Did you explore different values of τ and their impact on performance?
3.  Regarding the coverage issues in Table 3, what specific steps were taken to address vocabulary discrepancies when comparing the methods? Could you provide results with a restricted common vocabulary?
4.  Are you planning to expand the evaluation portal to include other downstream tasks (e.g., machine translation) and/or other evaluation metrics?
5.  Could you elaborate on the computational cost associated with training these embeddings, particularly for the `multiCCA` method and for the full 59-language setup?



**Additional Comments:**

* The paper would benefit from a discussion of the limitations of dictionary-based methods in handling out-of-vocabulary words and morphologically rich languages.
* Consider adding a section on potential ethical implications of using machine translation for dictionary creation (e.g., potential biases in the translation models).



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of multilingual NLP. The development of the evaluation framework and the publicly available portal are particularly significant. While the embedding methods themselves lack significant novelty, the comprehensive evaluation and practical impact of the work outweigh this limitation.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
