- Paper develops dictionary-based multilingual embeddings for 59 languages using multiCluster and multiCCA.  
- Introduces new intrinsic evaluation metrics multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA and releases a public evaluation portal.  
- Main contribution: standardized, publicly available multilingual evaluation framework.  
- Demonstrates feasibility of dictionary-based alignment at large scale (59 languages).  
- Provides empirical analysis correlating intrinsic metrics with extrinsic task performance.  
- Strong motivation and clear problem framing for massively multilingual embeddings.  
- Thorough experimental setup with many languages, multiple metrics, baselines, and released code/data.  
- Well-justified critique of QVEC and clear rationale for multiQVEC-CCA.  
- Evaluation portal has practical impact by lowering barriers and promoting standardized evaluation.  
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized.  
- Limited algorithmic novelty: methods extend existing CCA and clustering approaches.  
- Methods depend heavily on bilingual dictionary quality, with potential noise from automatic translations.  
- Extrinsic evaluation is limited to document classification and dependency parsing, missing tasks like MT.  
- Coverage and vocabulary discrepancies across methods raise concerns about fair comparisons.  
- Lacks ablation studies to quantify contributions of components (e.g., cluster size effects).  
- Requests analysis of automatically generated dictionary quality and noise filtering procedures.  
- Asks about sensitivity to the dictionary threshold τ and exploration of alternative values.  
- Requests clarification on how vocabulary coverage discrepancies were handled and results on a common vocabulary.  
- Inquires whether the portal will be expanded to more downstream tasks and metrics.  
- Seeks details on computational cost, especially for multiCCA across 59 languages.  
- Suggests discussing limitations for OOV words and morphologically rich languages.  
- Recommends addressing ethical implications of using MT for dictionary creation (biases).  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution due to evaluation framework and portal; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
