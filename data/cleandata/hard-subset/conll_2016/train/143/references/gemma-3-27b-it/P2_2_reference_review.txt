## Review for CoNLL Paper

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of estimating massively multilingual word embeddings, proposing two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) and a refined evaluation framework (multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA).  A key aspect of the work is the creation of a publicly available evaluation portal for multilingual embeddings. The authors evaluate their methods across a range of intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Comprehensive Evaluation Framework:** The development and justification of multiQVEC-CCA as a more robust intrinsic evaluation metric, addressing shortcomings in existing methods, is a significant contribution. The correlation analysis demonstrating its improved connection to downstream tasks is convincing.
2. **Publicly Available Evaluation Portal:** The creation and release of a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings is a valuable resource for the community. It lowers the barrier to entry for researchers and promotes standardized evaluation practices.
3. **Empirical Study of Dictionary-Based Methods:** The large-scale empirical evaluation of multiCCA and multiCluster across 59 languages provides useful insights into their performance characteristics and limitations.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Motivation & Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the importance of multilingual embeddings for NLP tasks like machine translation and transfer learning, establishing a compelling motivation for the research.
2. **Rigorous Evaluation:** The authors employ a diverse set of intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation metrics, providing a comprehensive assessment of the proposed methods. The correlation analysis effectively demonstrates the value of the new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA).
3. **Practical Contribution – Evaluation Portal:**  The publicly accessible evaluation portal is a highly practical contribution that will benefit the research community by facilitating standardized and reproducible evaluation of multilingual embeddings.  The provision of benchmark results further enhances its utility.
4. **Thorough Experimentation:** The work includes experiments on a substantial number of languages (59), providing a broad assessment of the methods' generalizability. The authors also provide detailed ablation studies (e.g., coverage analysis) in the appendix.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Methods:** While the multiCCA and multiCluster methods are well-described and implemented, their core ideas (CCA for bilingual embeddings, clustering for mapping) are not entirely novel. The main contribution lies in scaling these techniques to a large number of languages and the comprehensive evaluation.  The paper would benefit from a stronger discussion of how their implementation differs significantly from prior work.
2. **Performance Gaps & Dependence on Dictionary Quality:**  The results show that multiCCA outperforms multiCluster, but the absolute performance levels aren’t overwhelmingly strong, particularly in extrinsic tasks. The reliance on automatically generated dictionaries (via Google Translate) for many languages raises concerns about the quality of the training data and its impact on performance. This is acknowledged but could be further investigated.
3. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** The paper lacks a detailed analysis of *why* certain methods perform better or worse in specific scenarios. Investigating error cases and identifying linguistic factors that influence performance would strengthen the findings.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Regarding the Google Translate-generated dictionaries:  What steps were taken to mitigate potential errors introduced by the translation process? Did you perform any quality control on these dictionaries?
2.  The paper highlights the importance of coverage. Could you discuss the tradeoffs between coverage and embedding quality, and whether there are strategies to improve coverage without sacrificing quality?
3.  You mention that the evaluation portal is mirrored on GitHub.  Is the code for training the embeddings also available, and if so, could you provide a link?
4.  Regarding multiQVEC-CCA, what are the computational costs associated with this evaluation metric compared to multiQVEC?

**Additional Comments:**

* The reference list is extensive and well-formatted.
* The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, although some sections (particularly those with equations) could benefit from further clarification.
* The figures and tables are clear and informative.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper.  While the core methods may not be radically novel, the comprehensive evaluation framework, the practical contribution of the evaluation portal, and the large-scale empirical study represent significant contributions to the field of multilingual embeddings. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly through more in-depth error analysis and a clearer articulation of the methods' novelty, would further strengthen the paper.
