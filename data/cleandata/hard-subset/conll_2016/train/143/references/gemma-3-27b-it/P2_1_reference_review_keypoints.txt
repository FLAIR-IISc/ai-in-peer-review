- Paper proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) and new evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) plus an evaluation portal for massively multilingual embeddings.
- Main contribution: release of multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA metrics and a public evaluation portal with correlation analysis showing superiority over existing metrics.
- Contribution: Practical implementation and scaling of multiCCA and multiCluster to 59 languages, providing a useful engineering baseline.
- Contribution: Publicly released embeddings, data, and code serving as empirical baselines for future work.
- Strength: Strong motivation and relevance to multilingual NLP applications like MT and transfer learning.
- Strength: Rigorous, comprehensive evaluation using intrinsic and extrinsic metrics with thorough correlation analysis.
- Strength: Public availability of portal, code, and pre-trained embeddings promotes reproducibility and community use.
- Strength: Clear presentation and well-explained technical details with helpful result tables.
- Strength: Successfully identifies and addresses limitations of prior QVEC metrics, improving evaluation fidelity.
- Weakness: Limited algorithmic novelty—the methods adapt established techniques (clustering, CCA) rather than introduce new algorithms.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on bilingual dictionary quality, with insufficient analysis of dictionary noise and Google Translate limitations.
- Weakness: Extrinsic task performance (document classification, dependency parsing) shows only modest gains and may not be practically significant.
- Weakness: Comparisons in Tables 2 & 3 are tempered by coverage and vocabulary overlap issues; broader consistent baselines are needed.
- Question: How sensitive are multiCCA and multiCluster to dictionary quality and dictionary noise?
- Question: Have alternative dictionary construction methods for low-resource languages been explored, and what are their trade-offs?
- Question: How do extrinsic gains compare to baselines that do not use pre-trained embeddings to contextualize impact?
- Question: Why does multiQVEC-CCA correlate better with document classification while multiQVEC correlates better with dependency parsing?
- Additional comment: Ensure the evaluation portal URL is publicly accessible during review.
- Additional comment: Supplementary material referenced in the paper should be made available to reviewers.
- Overall assessment: Solid, useful contribution with valuable resources and rigorous evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
