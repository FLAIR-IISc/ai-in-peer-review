- Main contribution: release of a multilingual embedding evaluation portal with data and benchmarks.  
- Methodological contribution: proposal of multiQVEC-CCA to fix QVEC’s theoretical issues and extend it to multilingual evaluation.  
- Empirical contribution: large-scale application and comparison of dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) across 59 languages.  

- Strength: clear motivation and problem statement for scalable multilingual embeddings.  
- Strength: comprehensive intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations with correlation analysis.  
- Strength: resource contribution (portal, datasets) improves reproducibility and adoption.  
- Strength: careful identification and correction of QVEC limitations.  
- Strength: thorough experimental setup and transparent reporting of data, hyperparameters, and coverage.  

- Weakness: core methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) have limited algorithmic novelty.  
- Weakness: reliance on automatically translated dictionaries raises concerns about dictionary quality and sensitivity to threshold τ.  
- Weakness: mismatch between intrinsic and extrinsic results—multiQVEC-CCA correlates best but multiCCA is not consistently superior.  
- Weakness: insufficient error analysis of why multiCluster underperforms at large scale (cluster size/coherence not examined).  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art multilingual models (e.g., LASER, mBERT, XLM-R).  

- Question: details on dictionary quality, any manual inspection/filtering, and sensitivity to τ?  
- Question: average cluster size for 59 languages, examples of incoherent clusters, and possible strategies to improve clustering?  
- Question: why does multiQVEC-CCA correlate better with downstream tasks despite mixed intrinsic performance of multiCCA—are intrinsic datasets biased?  
- Question: were comparisons to LASER/mBERT/XLM-R considered or run, and if not, why?  
- Question: plans to include evaluations of recent multilingual models in the portal?  

- Additional comments: paper is well-written; citation style inconsistent; the portal link in the introduction is broken and must be fixed.  
- Overall assessment: solid, community-useful paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions if weaknesses are addressed.
