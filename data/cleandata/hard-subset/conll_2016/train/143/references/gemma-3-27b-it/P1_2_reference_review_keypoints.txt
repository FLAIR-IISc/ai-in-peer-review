- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for 59 languages and avoids reliance on parallel corpora.  
- Introduces evaluation metrics multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA and a public web portal for multilingual embedding evaluation.

- Main contribution: new multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA metrics and an evaluation portal as a community benchmark.  
- Main contribution: scalable dictionary-based approach to build embeddings for many languages using bilingual dictionaries.  
- Main contribution: empirical analysis showing better correlation of the proposed metrics with extrinsic tasks.

- Strength: Addresses scaling multilingual embeddings to low-resource languages using readily available dictionaries.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation with intrinsic–extrinsic correlation analysis supporting the new metrics.  
- Strength: Public web portal, data, and code improve reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation of methods and metrics.  
- Strength: Thorough comparisons to existing baselines (e.g., multiSkip, translation-invariance).

- Weakness: Core algorithmic novelty is limited; multiCCA and multiCluster extend existing techniques incrementally.  
- Weakness: Reliance on automatically generated dictionaries (e.g., Google Translate) risks noise and bias, with limited analysis of impact.  
- Weakness: Extrinsic evaluation is narrow (document classification, dependency parsing) and omits other relevant tasks like IR or MT.  
- Weakness: Varying coverage across languages due to dictionary availability raises fairness and comparability concerns.

- Question: Please provide a detailed analysis of the quality and validation steps for automatically generated dictionaries.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the dictionary threshold τ, and were alternative τ values evaluated?  
- Question: What are the computational bottlenecks and scalability limits when extending to more languages?  
- Question: Why were document classification and dependency parsing chosen as extrinsic tasks, and how representative are they?  
- Question: Why is English used as the hub for multiCCA, were other hubs considered, and how does hub choice affect performance?

- Additional: Include discussion of dictionary-based approach limitations (e.g., polysemy, lack of context).  
- Additional: Supplementary material should provide per-language dictionary size and coverage statistics.  
- Additional: Ensure URLs to code, data, and the evaluation portal are publicly available in the final version.
