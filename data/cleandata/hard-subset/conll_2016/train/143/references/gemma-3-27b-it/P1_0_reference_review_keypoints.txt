- Paper presents two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), new intrinsic metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA), and a public evaluation portal.
- Major contribution is the comprehensive evaluation framework and portal that standardizes multilingual embedding evaluation and correlates intrinsic metrics with downstream tasks.
- Large-scale empirical study across 59 languages demonstrating performance and limitations of dictionary-based methods.
- multiQVEC-CCA is a theoretically motivated improvement over QVEC that addresses basis invariance and normalization issues.
- Practical utility: the public web portal lowers barriers and supports reproducible research with both intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.
- Thorough empirical evaluation with many languages and metrics, including correlation analysis between intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations.
- Improved evaluation metric empirically correlates better with downstream tasks than existing metrics.
- Clear presentation and logical organization, with acknowledgment and partial mitigation of evaluation biases (e.g., vocabulary coverage).
- Limited novelty of the embedding methods themselves—the contribution is largely in scale and application rather than new algorithms.
- Concerns about dictionary quality from Google Translate and potential biases, with insufficient analysis of sensitivity to dictionary errors.
- Extrinsic tasks (document classification, dependency parsing) are limited and the parsing setup (removing non-embedding features) is artificially restrictive.
- Ablation studies are incomplete; more analysis of design choices (e.g., cluster size, dictionary thresholds) is needed.
- Question: provide precision/recall or error analysis for automatically generated dictionaries and sensitivity of results to dictionary quality.
- Question: justify choice of extrinsic tasks and rationale for removing all non-embedding features in parsing; hypothesize effects of including other features.
- Question: clarify scalability limits and computational bottlenecks for extending to 100+ languages.
- Question: address potential bias from multiCCA relying on English as the central space and report experiments using other central languages if available.
- Comment: ensure the GitHub repository is public and well-documented.
- Comment: discuss ethical implications and potential biases of using machine translation for dictionary creation.
- Comment: make supplementary material (e.g., correlation values) available and clearly organized.
- Overall: a solid contribution with valuable evaluation resources and empirical analysis; addressing noted weaknesses would strengthen the work.
