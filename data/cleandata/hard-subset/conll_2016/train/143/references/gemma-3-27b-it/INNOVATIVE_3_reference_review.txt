This paper tackles the challenging problem of massively multilingual word embeddings, proposing two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) and a refined evaluation framework (multiQVEC-CCA). The primary contribution is the systematic exploration of dictionary-based approaches for scaling multilingual embeddings to a significantly larger number of languages (59) than typically seen in prior work, coupled with a dedicated evaluation suite and portal to facilitate future research. A secondary contribution lies in the proposed multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric, aiming to address shortcomings in existing evaluation methodologies. Finally, the provision of a publicly available evaluation portal is a valuable resource for the community.

**Strengths:**

1. **Bold Scope & Practicality:** The paper tackles a genuinely hard problem – scaling multilingual embeddings beyond the typical two or handful of languages. This ambition, and the focus on leveraging readily available (though imperfect) resources like bilingual dictionaries, is a significant strength.  The focus on dictionary-based methods is a pragmatic choice given the scarcity of parallel corpora for many language pairs.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors don’t just propose methods; they invest heavily in evaluating them, not only with existing metrics but also by developing a new one (multiQVEC-CCA) and crucially, building a publicly available evaluation portal. This facilitates reproducibility and future work.  The correlation analysis between intrinsic and extrinsic metrics is insightful and valuable.
3. **Community Resource:** The evaluation portal is a strong positive. Providing a centralized platform for evaluating multilingual embeddings will undoubtedly accelerate progress in the field. The inclusion of benchmarks and the ability to upload and evaluate custom embeddings are particularly useful.
4. **Clear Presentation & Experimental Thoroughness:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed methods and experimental setup. The extensive set of experiments across multiple languages and metrics demonstrates a commitment to rigorous evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Methodological Limitations & Incremental Nature:** While the scope is ambitious, the proposed methods themselves (multiCluster and multiCCA) feel somewhat incremental. MultiCCA is a relatively straightforward extension of existing bilingual CCA approaches, and multiCluster, while a valid idea, suffers from inherent limitations related to cluster ambiguity. The paper acknowledges these limitations, but they are significant. The performance gains over simpler baselines aren't dramatic enough to suggest a breakthrough.
2. **Dictionary Quality Dependence:** The performance is heavily reliant on the quality of the bilingual dictionaries. The authors acknowledge this, particularly concerning the dictionaries created through Google Translate, but the sensitivity to dictionary quality isn't fully explored. A more detailed analysis of the impact of dictionary noise would be beneficial.
3. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** The paper presents quantitative results but lacks a qualitative analysis of where the methods succeed and fail.  Understanding *why* certain languages or word types pose challenges would provide valuable insights.
4. **Coverage vs. Performance Trade-off:** The focus on broad language coverage (59 languages) inevitably involves trade-offs with performance. The paper doesn’t adequately address this tension – is it better to have decent embeddings for many languages, or high-quality embeddings for a smaller subset?

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of dictionary quality on the performance of multiCCA and multiCluster? Specifically, how does performance degrade as the quality (precision/recall) of the dictionary decreases?
2.  The paper mentions that multiCluster suffers from semantic ambiguity due to clustering. Have you explored alternative clustering algorithms or strategies to mitigate this issue?
3.  Could you provide some example error cases – i.e., examples of word pairs that are incorrectly aligned or have poor embedding similarity – and discuss why the methods failed in these instances?
4.  Regarding the evaluation portal: Will it be actively maintained and updated with new datasets and metrics? What mechanisms are in place to ensure the quality and reliability of user-submitted embeddings and evaluations?
