## Review for CoNLL Submission: "Estimating Massively Multilingual Word Embeddings"

**Summary:** This paper investigates methods for learning word embeddings across a large number of languages (59) using dictionary-based approaches. The authors propose two methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, and adapt an existing evaluation metric (QVEC) to the multilingual setting, introducing multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA. A significant contribution is the release of a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings.

**Main Contributions (as I see them, ordered by strength):**

1. **The Multilingual Embedding Evaluation Portal:** This is the most valuable contribution. Providing a standardized, accessible platform for evaluating multilingual embeddings with diverse metrics is critical for advancing the field. The availability of data and benchmarks alongside the evaluation tools will significantly benefit researchers.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA:**  Addressing the theoretical shortcomings of the original QVEC metric and extending it to a multilingual setting is a valuable refinement.  The authors demonstrate better correlation with downstream tasks, suggesting improved evaluation quality.
3. **Empirical Exploration of Dictionary-Based Methods at Scale:** While the methods themselves (multiCluster and multiCCA) aren't groundbreakingly novel, applying them to a large number of languages (59) and providing a comprehensive empirical comparison is a significant undertaking.  The results, even if nuanced, offer valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of these approaches.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Motivation & Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly articulates the importance of multilingual embeddings for tasks like machine translation and transfer learning. The need for scalable methods beyond bilingual approaches is well-justified.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors employ a diverse suite of intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation metrics, demonstrating a rigorous assessment of their methods.  The correlation analysis between intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations is particularly insightful.
3. **Resource Contribution:** The web portal and accompanying datasets are a substantial contribution to the community. This facilitates reproducibility and comparison of future work. The clear documentation and ease of use will encourage adoption.
4. **Addressing Limitations of Existing Metrics:** Identifying and correcting the shortcomings of QVEC with multiQVEC-CCA showcases a thoughtful approach to evaluation methodology. 
5. **Thorough Experimental Setup:** The authors detail their experimental setup, including data sources, hyperparameters, and the handling of vocabulary coverage. This transparency is crucial for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Methods:** MultiCluster and multiCCA build upon existing techniques (clustering and CCA). While the application to a large number of languages is valuable, the core algorithmic innovation is relatively modest. The paper would benefit from a more critical discussion of this limitation.
2. **Dictionary Quality & Impact:** The reliance on automatically translated dictionaries for the 59-language setup raises concerns about dictionary quality. While acknowledged, the impact of noisy dictionaries on embedding quality isn't thoroughly investigated. The paper doesn't explore sensitivity to the dictionary creation threshold (τ).
3. **Discrepancy between Intrinsic and Extrinsic Results:** While multiQVEC-CCA correlates best with downstream tasks, multiCCA's performance, as measured by intrinsic metrics, is not consistently superior.  This disconnect requires further investigation and discussion. It raises questions about the reliability of intrinsic evaluations in this setting. 
4. **Insufficient Analysis of MultiCluster Performance:** MultiCluster consistently underperforms multiCCA, especially with the larger language set. The paper doesn't provide a detailed error analysis to understand *why* it struggles. Are the clusters too large and semantically heterogeneous? This would be valuable insight.
5. **Limited Comparative Analysis with Recent State-of-the-Art:**  The comparison is primarily to earlier work. A comparison to more recent, powerful multilingual embedding approaches (e.g., LASER, mBERT, XLM-R) would provide valuable context and demonstrate the competitiveness (or lack thereof) of the proposed methods.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dictionary Quality:** Can you provide more details on the quality of the automatically translated dictionaries?  Did you perform any manual inspection or filtering?  How sensitive are the results to the threshold parameter τ?
2. **MultiCluster Analysis:** What is the average cluster size in the 59-language setup?  Can you provide examples of clusters that exhibit poor semantic coherence? What strategies could be employed to improve cluster quality (e.g., more sophisticated clustering algorithms)?
3. **Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Correlation:** Why do you think multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than other intrinsic metrics, despite multiCCA not consistently outperforming in all intrinsic evaluations? Is there a bias in the intrinsic datasets?
4. **Comparison to SOTA:**  Have you considered comparing your methods to recent, pre-trained multilingual models like LASER, mBERT, or XLM-R? If so, what were the results? If not, why not?  
5. **Portal Future Development:**  Do you have plans to incorporate support for evaluation against other multilingual models (e.g., those mentioned above) within the evaluation portal?



**Additional Comments:**

* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*  Minor: The citation style is inconsistent (e.g., sometimes "et al." is used, sometimes not).
* The link to the web portal in the introduction is broken.  (This needs to be fixed.)




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and valuable contribution to the field of multilingual NLP. While the core methods lack significant novelty, the comprehensive evaluation, the development of multiQVEC-CCA, and, most importantly, the publicly available evaluation portal make this paper worthy of acceptance. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above during the rebuttal phase would further strengthen the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
