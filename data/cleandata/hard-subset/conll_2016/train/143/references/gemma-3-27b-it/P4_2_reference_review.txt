This paper introduces methods for estimating massively multilingual word embeddings, proposes new evaluation metrics for these embeddings, and provides a web portal for evaluating them. The authors claim three main contributions: (1) two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) for estimating embeddings in 59 languages, (2) adaptations of QVEC (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA) for evaluating multilingual embeddings that correlate better with downstream tasks, and (3) an easy-to-use web portal for evaluating embeddings.

The most significant contribution of this work is the development and release of the multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metrics. The authors convincingly argue that existing intrinsic evaluation metrics are poorly correlated with downstream performance. Their proposed metrics, particularly multiQVEC-CCA, demonstrate stronger correlations, suggesting they offer a more reliable way to assess the quality of multilingual embeddings. This is a valuable addition to the field, addressing a critical need for better evaluation methodologies.

The provision of a publicly available evaluation portal is another strong point. This resource lowers the barrier to entry for researchers working on multilingual embeddings and promotes reproducibility. The ability to easily evaluate embeddings against a standardized set of metrics is highly beneficial.

The proposed embedding methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, are a logical extension of existing bilingual embedding techniques to a multilingual setting. While the methods themselves are not groundbreaking in terms of novel algorithmic ideas, their implementation and demonstration on a large number of languages (59) is a substantial effort and provides a useful benchmark for future research. The comparative analysis against other methods highlights the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.

However, the paper suffers from a few weaknesses. Firstly, the empirical evaluation is somewhat limited by the choice of downstream tasks. While document classification and dependency parsing are relevant, they may not fully capture the diversity of applications for multilingual embeddings. Exploring tasks like cross-lingual information retrieval or machine translation could provide a more comprehensive assessment.

Secondly, the ablation study and analysis of the proposed methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) could be more thorough. The authors mention the impact of cluster size in multiCluster, but a more detailed exploration of different parameter settings and their effect on performance would be valuable. Similarly, a deeper analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of multiCCA, particularly its reliance on English as the central vector space, would strengthen the contribution.

Finally, while the authors acknowledge the limitations of using supersense tags for multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA (specifically, inconsistencies across languages), they don’t fully explore alternative approaches or discuss potential solutions. This weakens the generalizability of the evaluation metrics to languages without readily available supersense annotations.

Questions to Authors:

1.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the performance of multiCluster and multiCCA on different language families or typological groups?
2.  How sensitive are the results of multiQVEC-CCA to the choice of the English supersense tagset? Have you considered using a more universal or language-agnostic semantic resource?
3.  The paper focuses primarily on dictionary-based methods. Have you explored the possibility of incorporating parallel corpora, even limited ones, into the multiCluster and multiCCA frameworks to potentially improve performance?
4.   Could you provide the code for the evaluation portal?

Additional Comments:

The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of the relevant literature. The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized. The experimental setup is adequately described, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner. The supplementary material would be valuable to include alongside the submission.
