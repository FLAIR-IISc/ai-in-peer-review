- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA and a public evaluation portal as a standardized, improved intrinsic evaluation for massively multilingual embeddings.
- Trains and evaluates embeddings across 59 languages, creating a large-scale benchmark and dataset.
- Introduces multiCCA, an extension of CCA projecting many languages into an English space, showing good performance.
- Tackles the bottleneck of parallel data by using dictionary-based methods for multilingual embedding learning.
- Demonstrates rigorous evaluation with correlation analysis between intrinsic metrics and downstream tasks, supporting multiQVEC-CCA.
- Releases code, data, and a web portal to promote reproducibility and community use.
- Designs thorough experiments comparing multiple methods on intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, with coverage and vocabulary-mismatch considerations.
- Presents the work clearly with well-motivated methods and a helpful related work overview.
- Core methods (e.g., multiCluster) show limited novelty and are largely straightforward extensions.
- Reliance on English as the pivot may introduce bias and is not deeply analyzed or compared to other pivots.
- Dictionaries built from Google Translate for many languages may be noisy, risking degraded embedding quality.
- Paper lacks sufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (e.g., cluster size, embedding dimensionality).
- Downstream tasks are standard (document classification, dependency parsing) and could include stronger cross-lingual transfer tasks.
- Requests clarity on sensitivity of multiCCA to pivot language choice and experiments with other pivots.
- Asks for analysis/validation of Google Translate–derived dictionaries and error rates from manual checks.
- Seeks information on computational cost (training time, memory) as languages scale.
- Wants justification and experiments for chosen multiCluster maximum cluster sizes and their impact.
- Questions how methods handle polysemy and whether multilingual training conflates senses.
- Suggests adding discussion of potential biases in data and methods.
- Recommends including a table of computational resources used for training.
- Requests supplementary material with the full correlation matrix and additional ablation studies.
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution mainly for evaluation framework and resources; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
