- Paper proposes multiCluster and multiCCA for massively multilingual word embeddings and adapts QVEC to multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA, plus provides a public evaluation portal and benchmarks.
- The public evaluation portal and standardized benchmarks are the paper’s most significant contribution, addressing a key bottleneck in the field.
- multiQVEC-CCA improves on QVEC’s theoretical shortcomings and shows better correlation with downstream tasks.
- Extensive empirical evaluation across 59 languages offers valuable analysis of dictionary-based embedding approaches.
- The evaluation uses both intrinsic and extrinsic metrics and attempts to control for vocabulary coverage, strengthening validity.
- Experiments are comprehensive and compare proposed methods against multiple baselines, demonstrating scalability.
- The paper is generally clear and well-written with well-established motivation and articulated contributions.
- Identifying and addressing QVEC’s limitations is a noteworthy contribution to embedding evaluation.
- Proposed methods lack substantial algorithmic novelty, largely building on existing clustering and CCA techniques.
- multiCCA only occasionally and not consistently outperforms baselines, raising questions about when it helps.
- Using English as the hub in multiCCA may introduce bias; deeper analysis of hub choice effects is needed.
- Performance depends heavily on bilingual dictionary quality; use of Google Translate may introduce noise and bias.
- The paper does not provide a sensitivity analysis on dictionary quality or error analysis of translated dictionaries.
- Computational costs for training/evaluating 59-language embeddings are not detailed, which may hinder reproducibility and adoption.
- The reviewer requests examples of embedding failures and explanations for translational mismatches.
- The reviewer asks whether other intrinsic evaluation metrics were considered and why they weren’t included.
- Recommend adding discussion of method limitations, future research directions, error bars/statistical tests, and typological language comparisons.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution due to the portal and multiQVEC-CCA; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
