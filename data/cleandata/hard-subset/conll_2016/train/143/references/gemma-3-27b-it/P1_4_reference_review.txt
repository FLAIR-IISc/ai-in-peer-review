## Peer Review for CoNLL - Multilingual Word Embeddings Paper

**Summary:**

This paper presents methods for estimating massively multilingual word embeddings using dictionary-based approaches (multiCluster and multiCCA). The authors also contribute a refined evaluation framework (multiQVEC-CCA) and a publicly available web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings. The core idea is to leverage bilingual dictionaries and monolingual data to align word representations across numerous languages, avoiding the need for extensive parallel corpora.

**Main Contributions (as I see them, ordered by strength):**

1. **The publicly available evaluation portal:** This is the strongest contribution. A standardized, accessible evaluation suite is crucial for advancing the field of multilingual embeddings. The provision of benchmarks and the ability for researchers to upload and evaluate their own models is a valuable resource.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA and the analysis of existing evaluation metrics:**  Identifying shortcomings in existing evaluation methods (QVEC) and proposing a theoretically sound and empirically better alternative (multiQVEC-CCA) is a significant step towards more reliable evaluation in this domain. The correlation analysis with downstream tasks is insightful.
3. **Practical demonstration of dictionary-based methods at scale:** While the methods themselves (multiCluster, multiCCA) build on existing techniques, successfully applying them to 59 languages and demonstrating performance is a valuable engineering effort and expands the scope of multilingual embedding research.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Motivation & Clear Problem Statement:** The paper clearly articulates the need for scalable methods for creating multilingual embeddings, highlighting the limitations of relying solely on parallel data. 
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors perform a thorough evaluation using a diverse set of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, including document classification and dependency parsing.  The inclusion of both established and novel metrics allows for a nuanced assessment of the proposed methods.
3. **Practicality and Reproducibility:** The publicly available evaluation portal and code promote reproducibility and accelerate future research. The detailed description of the data processing and training procedures is helpful.
4. **Rigorous Analysis of Evaluation Metrics:** The correlation analysis between intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation metrics is particularly insightful.  The demonstration that multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA correlate better with downstream performance than previous metrics is a strong argument for their adoption.
5. **Scalability:** Successfully applying the methods to 59 languages demonstrates a practical capability that is beyond the scope of many previous studies.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Embedding Methods:** While the multiCluster and multiCCA methods are well-described and implemented, they are extensions of existing techniques (clustering and CCA) and don't introduce fundamentally new algorithmic innovations.  The primary contribution lies in the *application* and scaling of these methods.
2. **Performance Gains Not Always Clear:** The performance gains of multiCCA over other methods, especially the translation-invariance method, are not consistently significant across all tasks. The paper sometimes relies on small differences in scores, which could be sensitive to hyperparameters or random initialization.  Further investigation into the statistical significance of these results is needed.
3. **Dictionary Quality Dependence:** The performance of the dictionary-based methods is highly dependent on the quality of the bilingual dictionaries.  The reliance on Google Translate for dictionary creation in the 59-language setup raises concerns about potential noise and bias.  The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t fully address the impact on results.
4. **Oversimplification in Extrinsic Tasks:** Removing all features *except* the embeddings in the extrinsic tasks (document classification & dependency parsing) feels overly restrictive.  While it isolates the effect of the embeddings, it might not reflect realistic NLP scenarios and could lead to artificially low performance.
5. **Missing Error Analysis:**  There's a lack of detailed error analysis to understand *why* the different methods succeed or fail in specific scenarios. Understanding the types of errors made by each method would provide valuable insights for future improvement.


**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Dictionary Quality:**  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the quality of the dictionaries used for the 59-language setup (specifically those generated using Google Translate)?  Did you perform any filtering or quality control on these dictionaries?  What is the impact of dictionary noise on your results?
2. **Statistical Significance:** Can you provide statistical significance tests (e.g., t-tests) for the performance differences reported in Table 3? This will help clarify whether the observed improvements are statistically meaningful.
3. **Extrinsic Task Justification:**  Why was it decided to remove *all* other features in the extrinsic tasks?  Would the results change if a more realistic feature set was used?
4. **Error Analysis:** Can you provide some examples of specific words or phrases where the different embedding methods perform well or poorly? A qualitative error analysis would be insightful.
5. **Computational Cost:** Could you provide a more detailed breakdown of the computational resources (time and memory) required to train the embeddings for 59 languages? This information would be helpful for researchers considering using your methods.

**Additional Comments:**

* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
* The related work section is comprehensive.
* Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the proposed methods, beyond the dictionary quality issue.



Overall, this is a solid and valuable contribution to the field of multilingual embeddings. The evaluation portal and the analysis of evaluation metrics are particularly significant.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above will further strengthen the paper and its impact. I recommend acceptance, contingent on addressing the reviewers' concerns.
