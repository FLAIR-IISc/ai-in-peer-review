- Summary: Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings across 59 languages, new evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA), and an evaluation web portal.
- Contribution 1: Introduces multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA metrics and a portal, showing better correlation with downstream tasks than existing intrinsic metrics.
- Contribution 2: Trains and releases multilingual embeddings for 59 languages as a community resource.
- Contribution 3: Applies multiCluster and multiCCA at large scale to provide solid baselines despite limited algorithmic novelty.
- Strength 1: Clearly motivated problem statement emphasizing benefits for translation and transfer learning.
- Strength 2: Rigorous evaluation with both intrinsic and extrinsic metrics and correlation analysis across multiple languages.
- Strength 3: Valuable community resources (portal, data, code) that support reproducibility.
- Strength 4: Thorough experiments with fair baseline comparisons and attempts to account for coverage biases.
- Strength 5: Identifies and addresses theoretical shortcomings in prior QVEC metrics.
- Weakness 1: Estimation methods lack substantial algorithmic novelty beyond existing CCA and clustering approaches.
- Weakness 2: Heavy reliance on automatically translated dictionaries for many languages without deep analysis of their quality impact.
- Weakness 3: Insufficient discussion of computational costs and scalability for training 59-language embeddings.
- Weakness 4: Coverage vs. performance trade-offs are handled ad-hoc (e.g., evaluating vocabulary intersections) rather than systematically.
- Weakness 5: Missing qualitative or error analysis to illuminate method-specific strengths and failure modes.
- Question 1: Request for detailed analysis of automatically generated dictionary precision/recall and its correlation with embedding performance.
- Question 2: Ask for training time, memory usage, and strategies to improve scalability for even more languages.
- Question 3: Inquire about hyperparameter sensitivity and whether hyperparameter optimization was performed.
- Question 4: How do embeddings perform for very low-resource languages and what strategies could help them?
- Question 5: Request qualitative examples demonstrating translational equivalence or semantic similarity captured by the embeddings.
- Additional comments: Paper is well-written, references are comprehensive, provided URLs and GitHub mirror aid transparency and reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable evaluation framework and resources; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
