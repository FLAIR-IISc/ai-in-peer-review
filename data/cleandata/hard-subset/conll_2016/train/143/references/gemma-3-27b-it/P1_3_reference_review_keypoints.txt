- Paper introduces two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings across 59 languages and new evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) plus a web portal.
- Contribution: release of multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA and an evaluation portal addressing prior evaluation gaps and correlating better with downstream tasks.
- Contribution: scalable dictionary-based approach showing feasibility of building embeddings for 59 languages, with implemented methods and public code/data.
- Contribution: comprehensive experiments and public release of training data and embeddings establishing a baseline for future work.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem definition emphasizing importance for MT, transfer learning, and multilingual NLP.
- Strength: rigorous evaluation including intrinsic and extrinsic tasks with insightful correlation analysis between them.
- Strength: valuable resource contribution — evaluation portal, data, and trained embeddings improve reproducibility.
- Strength: well-defined experimental setup with attention to coverage in evaluation.
- Strength: clear, comprehensive presentation enabling reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited novelty in core methods—extensions of existing techniques rather than fundamentally new algorithms.
- Weakness: use of Google Translate to create bilingual dictionaries raises concerns about dictionary quality and its impact.
- Weakness: multiCluster underperforms relative to multiCCA at scale, suggesting issues like semantic ambiguity and insufficient cluster analysis.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on computational cost and scalability for training 59-language embeddings.
- Weakness: reliance on English as pivot may introduce bias; alternative pivot or fully multilingual approaches are not explored.
- Question: please analyze dictionary quality (precision/recall vs. human dictionaries) and sensitivity of results to dictionary quality.
- Question: please provide deeper analysis of why multiCluster fails at scale and whether alternative clustering strategies were tried.
- Question: what computational resources (CPU/GPU/memory) and training time were required, and how does this scale with number of languages?
- Question: were other pivot languages tried in multiCCA and did pivot choice significantly affect performance?
- Question: provide error analysis with examples of translationally equivalent pairs that failed to align and common error types.
- Additional: discuss related work on zero-shot cross-lingual transfer as a direct application of multilingual embeddings.
- Additional: include more supplementary details on hyperparameters and hardware configurations.
- Additional: clarify manual effort and choice of threshold τ used for dictionary creation.
