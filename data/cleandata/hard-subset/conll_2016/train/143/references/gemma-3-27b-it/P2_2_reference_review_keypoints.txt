- Proposes dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) and a refined intrinsic evaluation (multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA) with a public evaluation portal.
- multiQVEC-CCA is presented as a more robust intrinsic metric with improved correlation to downstream tasks.
- Public web portal released to standardize and lower barriers for multilingual embedding evaluation.
- Large-scale empirical evaluation across 59 languages offers broad insights into method performance.
- Strong motivation and clear problem definition emphasizing multilingual embeddings’ importance.
- Rigorous evaluation using diverse intrinsic and extrinsic metrics and correlation analysis.
- Practical contribution: benchmark results and an accessible evaluation infrastructure.
- Thorough experimentation including ablation studies (e.g., coverage analysis) in the appendix.
- Core methodological novelty is limited; main contribution is scaling and comprehensive evaluation.
- Performance gaps: multiCCA outperforms multiCluster but absolute extrinsic results are not very strong.
- Heavy dependence on automatically generated (Google Translate) dictionaries raises data-quality concerns.
- Lack of detailed error analysis explaining why methods succeed or fail in specific scenarios.
- Question about quality control steps taken for Google Translate-generated dictionaries.
- Question about tradeoffs between coverage and embedding quality and strategies to improve coverage.
- Question whether training code for embeddings is available (and request for a link).
- Question about the computational cost of multiQVEC-CCA compared to multiQVEC.
- References are extensive and well formatted.
- Paper is generally well-written; some equation sections need clearer exposition.
- Figures and tables are clear and informative.
- Overall recommendation: accept — contributions in evaluation framework, portal, and large-scale study outweigh limited novelty, but more error analysis and clearer novelty discussion would strengthen the paper.
