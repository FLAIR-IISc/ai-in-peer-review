

# Review of "Multilingual Word Embeddings"

## Summary of the Paper
This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) for estimating multilingual word embeddings that only require monolingual data and pairwise parallel dictionaries (not parallel corpora). The authors also develop improved evaluation metrics (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA) that better correlate with downstream tasks, and create a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings. The main contributions are: (1) novel evaluation metrics that better correlate with extrinsic tasks, (2) dictionary-based estimation methods for multilingual embeddings, and (3) a web portal to facilitate future research.

## Main Contributions
The authors' primary contribution is the development of evaluation metrics (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA) that significantly outperform existing intrinsic metrics in correlating with extrinsic tasks. The proposed estimation methods are secondary contributions, and the web portal is a valuable practical resource.

## Strengths

1. **Significant Improvement in Evaluation Metrics**: The paper's most compelling contribution is the demonstration that multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA better correlate with downstream tasks than existing metrics. Table 1 shows that multiQVEC-CCA achieves a Pearson correlation of 0.896 with document classification, compared to 0.386 for cross-lingual word similarity and 0.066 for word translation. This is a substantial improvement that directly addresses a critical limitation in current evaluation practices.

2. **Practical Community Resource**: The web portal is an exceptionally valuable contribution that will substantially facilitate future research in multilingual NLP. It provides a standardized way to evaluate embeddings across multiple languages and metrics, addressing a major pain point in the field.

3. **Demonstration of Dictionary-Based Methods**: The paper convincingly shows that dictionary-based methods can produce high-quality multilingual embeddings without requiring parallel corpora, which is particularly valuable for low-resource languages where parallel data is scarce. The consistent outperformance of multiCCA over multiCluster across metrics (especially for 59 languages) is a significant practical contribution.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Novelty of Proposed Methods**: The core contribution (the embedding estimation methods) lacks sufficient novelty. multiCCA is a straightforward extension of Faruqui and Dyer's bilingual CCA approach, and multiCluster is a natural extension of cluster-based methods. The paper doesn't clearly articulate how these approaches differ from or improve upon previous work. For instance, the related work section notes that Hermann and Blunsom (2014) already showed how to extend bicvm to multiple languages, though their implementation only supported bilingual embeddings.

2. **Insufficient Analysis of Google Translate Reliance**: For 47 of the 59 languages, the authors rely on Google Translate to create dictionaries, which introduces potential errors. The paper doesn't adequately analyze how these translation errors affect embedding quality or provide error analysis to demonstrate the reliability of this approach. This is a significant limitation for a paper claiming to create embeddings for 59 languages.

3. **Lack of Comparison with State-of-the-Art Methods**: The comparison with baselines (translation-invariance and multiSkip) is limited. The paper doesn't compare with recent state-of-the-art methods like multilingual BERT or other transformer-based approaches that have become dominant in multilingual NLP. This omission makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of the proposed methods in the current landscape.

4. **Insufficient Discussion of Computational Complexity**: The paper doesn't address the computational complexity of the proposed methods, especially for large numbers of languages. For example, the multiCCA method requires estimating projection matrices for each language into English space, which becomes increasingly complex as the number of languages grows. This is a critical consideration for practical application.

## Questions to Authors

1. How do the proposed methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) specifically differ from previous work, particularly from Hermann and Blunsom (2014) who already demonstrated a method for extending bilingual embeddings to multiple languages?

2. What analysis have the authors performed to assess the impact of using Google Translate for dictionary creation on the quality of the embeddings, especially for languages with less reliable translation services?

3. How do the proposed methods scale computationally to larger numbers of languages (e.g., 100+), and what are the practical limitations for real-world applications?

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the field through its improved evaluation metrics and the web portal resource. The demonstration that dictionary-based methods can produce high-quality multilingual embeddings is also useful. However, the limited novelty of the embedding estimation methods and insufficient analysis of the Google Translate reliance are significant weaknesses. The paper would be substantially strengthened by comparing with more recent state-of-the-art methods and providing a thorough analysis of the translation errors introduced by Google Translate.

While the paper's contribution to evaluation methodology is strong, the novelty of the core methodological contribution is limited. The paper would be more compelling with a clearer articulation of novelty, better analysis of the Google Translate reliance, and comparison with current state-of-the-art methods. The web portal is an excellent practical contribution that would be valuable to the community even if the core methods are not particularly novel.

**Decision: Weak Accept**  
The paper makes a valuable contribution through its evaluation metrics and web portal, but the core methodological contributions lack sufficient novelty. With the suggested improvements, this would be a strong paper for acceptance.
