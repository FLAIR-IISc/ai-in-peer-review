- Paper proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) and improved evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) plus a web evaluation portal.  
- Major contribution: multiQVEC-CCA is a theoretically sound metric that correlates much better with downstream tasks than prior metrics.  
- Major contribution: The web portal enables standardized, easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings across multiple metrics.  
- Major contribution: Empirical results show dictionary-based methods can be competitive with parallel-corpus-based methods across many languages.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA addresses invariance to linear transforms and unnormalized scoring, yielding high correlation with extrinsic tasks (e.g., 0.896 with document classification).  
- Strength: The web portal is a practical tool likely to lower barriers and improve reproducibility in multilingual embedding research.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments across 59 languages, with attention to vocabulary coverage, support the empirical claims.  
- Weakness: Estimation methods have limited novelty, largely adapting existing techniques without strong justification for the specific adaptations.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks theoretical analysis explaining why multiCCA (and other proposals) should work better than alternatives.  
- Weakness: Claim of benefit from "massively multilingual" (59 languages) is not clearly justified or shown to be better than smaller-scale approaches.  
- Weakness: multiQVEC-CCA depends on supersense annotations that are inconsistent across languages and unavailable for many languages, limiting applicability.  
- Question: Why does multiCCA consistently outperform multiCluster—what are the theoretical and practical advantages of the projection approach?  
- Question: How do the authors justify treating ~1.6% coverage difference between multiCluster and multiCCA as “relatively small,” and how were coverage differences handled in correlations?  
- Question: How can multiQVEC-CCA be adapted or evaluated for languages lacking supersense annotations?  
- Overall assessment: Strong practical contributions (metrics, portal, empirical validation) warrant acceptance with minor revisions to address theoretical grounding and clarify the massively multilingual claims.
