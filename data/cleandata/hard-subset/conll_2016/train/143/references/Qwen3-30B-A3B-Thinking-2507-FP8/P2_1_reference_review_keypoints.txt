- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings using only monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries.  
- Introduces two evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) that better correlate with downstream tasks than existing intrinsic metrics.  
- Provides a web portal and downloadable tools for evaluating multilingual embeddings.  

- Strong evaluation methodology: multiQVEC-CCA shows much higher correlation with downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 vs. 0.386).  
- Comprehensive experiments across 59 languages, multiple metrics, and estimation methods, with clear presentation and reproducibility resources.  
- Solid theoretical motivation addressing QVEC limitations and careful extension to multilingual settings.  

- Limited novelty: multiCCA is a straightforward extension of bilingual CCA and multiCluster resembles prior clustering work.  
- Insufficient analysis explaining why multiCCA outperforms multiCluster and lack of qualitative failure-case analysis.  
- Limited generalizability: multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA depend on supersense annotations available for few languages.  
- Lacks comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., MUSE, LASER), making competitiveness unclear.  

- Why does multiCCA consistently outperform multiCluster despite multiCluster’s direct alignment approach?  
- What alternative linguistic resources could extend multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA beyond languages with supersense annotations?  
- How would the methods perform on low-resource languages with scarce or unreliable dictionaries?  

- Overall: valuable contribution to multilingual embedding evaluation with strong metrics and tooling, but limited methodological novelty and scope without broader analysis and comparisons.
