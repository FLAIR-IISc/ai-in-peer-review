

# Review of "Estimating Multilingual Word Embeddings"

## Summary and Main Contributions

The paper proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) for estimating multilingual word embeddings that require only monolingual data and pairwise parallel dictionaries, rather than parallel corpora. It also introduces new evaluation metrics (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA) that purportedly better correlate with downstream task performance than existing metrics. The authors additionally present a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings.

From my perspective, the strongest contributions are:
1. The web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which will facilitate future research
2. The demonstration that multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than existing intrinsic metrics
3. The exploration of dictionary-based approaches that avoid the need for parallel corpora

## Strengths

1. **Web Portal for Evaluation**: The development of a comprehensive web portal to evaluate multilingual embeddings across nine metrics is a significant contribution. This will substantially facilitate future research in the area by providing a standardized evaluation framework, which is particularly valuable given the lack of such resources in multilingual NLP.

2. **Evaluation Metric Analysis**: The paper provides compelling evidence that their proposed multiQVEC-CCA metric correlates much better (0.896) with downstream document classification performance than existing metrics like cross-lingual word similarity (0.386) or word translation (0.066). This addresses a real gap in the literature and demonstrates a clear methodological improvement in evaluation practices.

3. **Practical Approach to Multilingual Embeddings**: The paper demonstrates that dictionary-based approaches can effectively create multilingual embeddings without requiring parallel corpora, which is valuable given the scarcity of parallel data for many language pairs. The authors successfully train embeddings for 59 languages using this approach.

## Weaknesses

1. **Inadequate Comparison with Established Baselines**: The paper fails to provide a comprehensive comparison with the most established methods for multilingual embeddings. The baselines used (multiSkip, translation-invariance) are not state-of-the-art, and the paper doesn't compare with methods that have demonstrated strong performance in the literature (e.g., MUSE, LASER, or the approaches by Artetxe et al. or Conneau et al.). This weakens the claim that their methods "work well" and makes it difficult to assess whether they represent a meaningful advancement.

2. **Lack of Consistent Superiority Across Evaluation Metrics**: The results in Table 3 show that the proposed methods don't consistently outperform baselines across all metrics. For instance, multiCCA ranks first in only 3 of 9 evaluation metrics, and multiCluster ranks first in just 1. The paper claims "multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster according to all evaluation metrics" (for 59 languages), but this is contradicted by the data in Table 2. This inconsistency undermines the paper's main claim of proposing superior methods.

3. **Insufficient Validation of Theoretical Claims**: The paper claims multiQVEC-CCA "addresses a theoretical shortcoming" of multiQVEC, but doesn't provide adequate empirical evidence that this theoretical improvement translates to practical benefits. The paper shows that multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than multiQVEC (0.896 vs. 0.635), but doesn't demonstrate that this correlation difference is statistically significant or that it leads to better embedding quality in practical applications.

4. **Limited Data Quality for 47 Languages**: For 47 of the 59 languages, the authors use Google Translate to generate dictionaries, which introduces potential errors. The paper doesn't adequately address how these errors affect embedding quality or provide any analysis of the quality of these automatically generated dictionaries. This significantly weakens the credibility of results for these languages.

5. **No Demonstration of Downstream Task Improvement**: The paper shows that their evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA) correlates better with downstream tasks than existing metrics, but doesn't demonstrate that their embedding methods (multiCluster/multiCCA) actually lead to better downstream task performance. This is a critical gap since the ultimate goal of embeddings is to improve downstream tasks.

## Questions to Authors

1. Why didn't you compare your methods against state-of-the-art multilingual embedding approaches (e.g., MUSE, LASER) that have demonstrated strong performance in the literature?

2. How do you explain the inconsistent ranking of your methods across different evaluation metrics (Table 3)? What does this imply about the suitability of different metrics for different applications?

3. Given that you used Google Translate to generate dictionaries for 47 languages, what evidence do you have that the resulting embeddings are meaningful rather than simply reflecting translation errors?

4. Can you provide statistical significance testing for the correlation differences between your proposed metrics and existing metrics?

5. Why did you choose to use only English as the "anchor" language for multiCCA, and how might this affect performance for languages with very different structures?

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a valuable contribution through its evaluation portal and its analysis of evaluation metrics. However, the proposed embedding methods lack sufficient validation against established baselines and do not consistently demonstrate superiority across evaluation metrics. The paper's claims about the effectiveness of multiCluster and multiCCA are not fully supported by the evidence presented. While the work is interesting and useful in some aspects, it falls short of demonstrating a meaningful advance in the field of multilingual embeddings. The paper would benefit from more rigorous comparison with state-of-the-art methods and stronger evidence supporting the superiority of the proposed approaches.
