- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) to learn multilingual embeddings for 59 languages using monolingual data plus bilingual dictionaries.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an improved evaluation metric that better correlates with downstream tasks (0.896 correlation).  
- Provides a public web portal for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings with development and test modes.  
- Claims dictionary-based approaches can achieve competitive results without parallel corpora.  
- Theoretical contribution: fixes QVEC’s issues (linear transformation invariance, normalization) and demonstrates stronger downstream correlation.  
- Practical contribution: the web portal lowers barriers and promotes consistent, reproducible evaluation.  
- Empirical contribution: shows multiCCA often outperforms baselines and ranks first on multiple evaluations.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent SOTA embedding methods (e.g., Artetxe 2018, Lample 2018, Conneau 2018).  
- Weakness: claim of “no parallel data required” is misleading because 47/59 dictionaries were created via Google Translate (which relies on parallel corpora).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why multiCluster underperforms and whether its semantic-ambiguity issues can be mitigated.  
- Weakness: inconsistent evaluation protocol and unclear handling of vocabulary coverage differences across methods.  
- Question: How do the methods compare empirically to recent SOTA alignment approaches—were those reproduced?  
- Question: How were errors from Google Translate-derived dictionaries accounted for, and can you quantify their error rate?  
- Question: Can you provide a theoretical justification for why invariance to linear transformations explains multiCCA’s superior performance?  
- Overall: Valuable contributions in evaluation methodology and tooling, but misleading data-claims and missing SOTA comparisons need addressing.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify parallel-data claims and include comparisons with recent SOTA.
