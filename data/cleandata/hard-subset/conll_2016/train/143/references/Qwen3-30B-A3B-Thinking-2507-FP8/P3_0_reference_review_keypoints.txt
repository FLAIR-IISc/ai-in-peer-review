- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for 59 languages without requiring parallel corpora.  
- Contributions: new intrinsic metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) that better correlate with downstream tasks, demonstration that dictionary-based methods work, and a web evaluation portal.  
- Strength: Rigorous evaluation showing existing intrinsic metrics correlate poorly with downstream tasks while multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA correlate strongly (e.g., Pearson 0.896 with document classification).  
- Strength: Web portal provides reproducible, standardized infrastructure for evaluating multilingual embeddings across metrics and languages.  
- Strength: Demonstrates scalability and practical utility of creating multilingual embeddings from monolingual data plus bilingual dictionaries, beneficial for low-resource languages.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—multiCCA is a simple extension of prior work and multiCluster builds on established clustering methods; distinctions from related work are unclear.  
- Weakness: The claim of requiring only monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries is misleading because high-quality bilingual dictionaries often rely on parallel corpora.  
- Weakness: multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA depend on consistent supersense annotations not available for many languages; using POS tags would shift evaluation to syntax, reducing semantic validity.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent method rankings across metrics (e.g., multiCCA often best but worst on word translation) with no clear rationale for metric prioritization.  
- Question: How do you address the practical difficulty of obtaining high-quality bilingual dictionaries and how do resource needs compare to parallel-corpus methods?  
- Question: How would you adapt multiQVEC-CCA for languages lacking consistent supersense annotations, and how would such adaptation affect metric validity?  
- Question: Why does multiCluster’s performance degrade when scaling from 12 to 59 languages—semantic ambiguity from clusters or other causes?  
- Overall: Important contribution for evaluation methodology and tools; core methods lack novelty but results and portal are valuable, paper would benefit from clarifying evaluation limitations and methodological choices.
