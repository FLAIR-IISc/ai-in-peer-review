- Paper proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings across 59 languages, introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, and provides an evaluation web portal.
- Major contribution: multiQVEC-CCA corrects QVEC’s theoretical flaws and shows much stronger correlation with downstream tasks.
- Major contribution: an evaluation portal offering standardized intrinsic and extrinsic benchmarks to improve reproducibility.
- Major contribution: multiCCA, a CCA-based projection into English space, outperforms other methods across metrics.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA is theoretically sound (CCA, normalized score) and empirically correlates far better with extrinsic tasks (e.g., 0.896 vs 0.635).
- Strength: the evaluation portal is a practical resource that lowers the barrier for fair comparison in multilingual NLP.
- Strength: multiCCA is a well-motivated extension of bilingual CCA that maintains semantic consistency without needing parallel corpora.
- Weakness: Claim of “59 languages” is misleading because 47 languages use Google Translate-derived dictionaries that likely introduce noise and lower-quality embeddings.
- Weakness: multiCluster’s justification is underdeveloped; forcing translational equivalents to share vectors induces semantic ambiguity and its practical use cases are unclear.
- Weakness: Comparison with multiSkip is unfair because multiSkip uses only parallel data while other methods also leverage large monolingual corpora; this should be clarified or corrected.
- Weakness: No analysis is provided of the quality (precision/recall) of the Google Translate-based dictionaries, leaving reliability of many embeddings unassessed.
- Question: How was the quality of the Google Translate dictionaries assessed, and what evidence shows embeddings trained on them are of practical quality?
- Question: Given multiCluster’s context-ignorance and multiCCA’s superior performance, in what scenarios would multiCluster be preferable?
- Question: Both methods use the same dictionaries—what specific aspects of multiCCA’s projection explain its much better performance?
- Overall: Valuable contributions in evaluation metrics and tooling, but the “massively multilingual” claim overstates scope/quality; recommend acceptance with major revisions to clarify embedding quality and scope.
