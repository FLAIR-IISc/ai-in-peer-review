- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) and two new evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) for multilingual embeddings.  
- Claims applicability across 59 languages using monolingual data + pairwise dictionaries, without parallel corpora.  
- Main contribution: multiQVEC-CCA corrects QVEC's lack of invariance to linear transforms and unnormalized scores, yielding normalized [-1,1] scores.  
- Empirically, multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA correlate much better with downstream tasks than prior intrinsic metrics.  
- Secondary contribution: a web portal for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation with dev/test modes.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation including correlation analysis and ablation studies across many languages and tasks.  
- Strength: the web portal fills a critical community need for standardized evaluation.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., MUSE, LASER, XLM).  
- Weakness: quality concerns about the 59-language dataset because 47 languages use Google Translate–derived dictionaries.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical justification for why multiCCA outperforms multiCluster.  
- Question: How do Google Translate–generated dictionaries compare to manually curated ones, and how do they affect embedding quality?  
- Question: Are the reported multiCCA vs. multiCluster differences statistically significant or within margin of error?  
- Question: Why was maximum cluster size increased for 59 languages (10,000) vs. 12 languages (1,000), and how did this choice affect results?  
- Overall: valuable contributions in evaluation metrics and tooling; suitable for acceptance with minor revisions if comparisons to recent methods and dictionary-quality issues are addressed.
