- Summary: Proposes multiCluster and multiCCA for multilingual embeddings, introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metrics, and provides a web portal for standardized evaluation across 59 languages.  
- Claim: multiQVEC-CCA better correlates with downstream tasks and multiCCA outperforms other methods on intrinsic metrics.

- Contribution — multiQVEC-CCA: Fixes QVEC’s lack of invariance and unnormalized scores by using CCA to produce normalized, rotation-invariant evaluation.  
- Contribution — multiCCA: Dictionary-based embedding method reporting consistent intrinsic-metric gains across 59 languages.  
- Contribution — Web portal: Practical platform for uploading embeddings and running standardized intrinsic/extrinsic evaluations.

- Strength — Theoretical metric improvement: multiQVEC-CCA addresses fundamental flaws in QVEC and shows strong empirical correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Strength — Practical portal: Provides reproducible, standardized evaluation and a development/test mode to encourage sound practice.  
- Strength — Broad experiments: Thorough validation across multiple metrics, 59 languages, and several embedding methods with correlation analyses.

- Weakness — Downstream-task evidence missing: No direct demonstration that multiCCA embeddings improve actual downstream NLP task performance, only intrinsic correlations.  
- Weakness — No SOTA comparisons: Fails to compare to recent strong baselines like XLM or MUSE, leaving relative performance unclear.  
- Weakness — Dictionary quality concerns: Uses Google Translate for 47 languages without assessing precision/recall or impact of translation errors.  
- Weakness — Overemphasis on coverage: Reports coverage percentages that may not reflect true embedding quality and could be misleading.

- Question 1: Provide actual downstream task results for multiCCA versus other methods, not just correlation coefficients.  
- Question 2: Why were XLM and MUSE excluded, and how would the authors expect their methods to compare?  
- Question 3: How was the quality of Google Translate–derived dictionaries evaluated (precision/recall vs. human dictionaries)?  
- Question 4: Why different max cluster sizes (1,000 vs 10,000) across experiments and how does that affect comparability?  
- Question 5: Why emphasize coverage over downstream task performance, given coverage’s limitations?

- Overall recommendation: Significant methodological advance (multiQVEC-CCA) and useful portal, but needs SOTA comparisons and concrete downstream-task evidence for multiCCA to be a strong contribution.
