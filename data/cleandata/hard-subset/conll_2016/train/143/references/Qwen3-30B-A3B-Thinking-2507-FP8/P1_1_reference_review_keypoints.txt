- Paper proposes creating multilingual embeddings for 59 languages using monolingual corpora plus pairwise dictionaries, without parallel corpora.  
- Main contributions claimed: two dictionary-based embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), two improved intrinsic evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA), and a web evaluation portal.  
- Reviewer views the improved evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA) as the most significant contribution.  
- Reviewer views the web portal for standardized evaluation as a major practical contribution.  
- Embedding methods are considered incremental extensions of prior work rather than highly novel.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows substantially better correlation with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics.  
- Strength: The web portal offers practical, standardized evaluation across intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.  
- Strength: Paper includes thorough empirical analysis and correlation experiments across languages and tasks.  
- Strength: Clear demonstration that existing intrinsic metrics (word similarity, word translation) correlate poorly with extrinsic performance.  
- Weakness: multiCluster and multiCCA are straightforward extensions of prior methods and lack clear novelty.  
- Weakness: multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA depend on supersense annotations, which are inconsistent or unavailable for many languages.  
- Weakness: Incomplete comparison to state-of-the-art multilingual embedding methods (limited baselines).  
- Weakness: Comparison to parallel-data methods uses only 12 (European) languages despite training on 59, limiting generalizability.  
- Question: How does multiCCA specifically differ from Hermann and Blunsom (2014)’s multilingual extension?  
- Question: Why were maximum cluster sizes (1,000 for 12 languages, 10,000 for 59) chosen for multiCluster, and how do they affect embedding quality?  
- Question: What is the theoretical justification that multiCCA better handles word sense variation exacerbated by multiCluster?  
- Question: How will the supersense-dependency limitation be addressed for languages without annotations or via alternative evaluation methods?  
- Overall: Valuable contributions in evaluation methodology and tooling warrant acceptance with minor revisions; embedding methods are incremental and comparisons/annotation limitations should be addressed.
