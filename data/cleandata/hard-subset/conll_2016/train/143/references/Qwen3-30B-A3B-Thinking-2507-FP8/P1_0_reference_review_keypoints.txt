- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that require monolingual corpora and pairwise dictionaries and scales to 59 languages.  
- Introduces improved evaluation metrics (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA) that better correlate with downstream tasks.  
- Provides a public web portal for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings across 59 languages.  
- multiQVEC-CCA addresses QVEC’s issues (non-invariance to linear transforms and unnormalized scores) and shows much higher correlation with downstream tasks.  
- The web portal is a practical community resource that lowers barriers and supports reproducible, standardized evaluation.  
- Empirical results show multiCCA consistently outperforms baselines and that the new intrinsic metrics correlate strongly with extrinsic performance.  
- Core embedding methods are incremental extensions of prior work (e.g., multiCCA builds on Faruqui & Dyer; multiSkip is a variant), limiting novelty.  
- Lacks comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., multilingual BERT / mBERT and other neural approaches), making current standing unclear.  
- Does not sufficiently analyze limitations such as sensitivity to dictionary quality or performance for languages with sparse translation resources.  
- Downstream evaluation is narrow (only document classification and dependency parsing); broader task coverage is needed to validate metric correlations.  
- Requests comparison with mBERT/multilingual BERT and other recent methods.  
- Asks for clarification on why multiCluster ignores contextual semantics yet performs reasonably for some languages and its limitations for larger sets.  
- Notes supersense annotations used for multiQVEC-CCA are limited to a few languages and asks how to address annotation sparsity across languages.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—valuable contributions in evaluation and resources, but address methodological limitations and include comparisons to recent SOTA.
