- Main contribution: multiQVEC-CCA as an improved evaluation metric for multilingual embeddings, plus two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) and a web evaluation portal.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA addresses QVEC flaws (invariance to linear transforms, normalized [-1,1]) and shows much stronger correlation with downstream tasks.
- Strength: The web portal is a valuable community resource enabling standardized intrinsic/extrinsic evaluation with dev/test modes.
- Strength: Empirical results over 59 languages show dictionary-based approaches work at scale, with multiCCA consistently outperforming multiCluster.
- Weakness: Estimation methods offer limited novelty—straightforward extensions of prior work without strong justification of significance.
- Weakness: 59-language dataset relies on Google Translate for dictionaries, introducing potential noise and lowering embedding quality.
- Weakness: No comparison to recent contextual multilingual models (mBERT, XLM, XLM-R), making it hard to assess SOTA performance.
- Question: How do the proposed methods compare empirically to multilingual BERT, XLM, XLM-R, and other recent approaches?
- Question: What is the impact of Google-Translate-derived dictionaries versus parallel-corpus-derived dictionaries (12-language set) on embedding quality?
- Question: Have context-aware or more sophisticated clustering methods been considered to mitigate multiCluster’s ignoring of sense distinctions?
- Additional: The intrinsic–extrinsic correlation analysis (Table 1) is strong; authors should address the limited 12-language evaluation and emphasize the web portal in the final paper.
