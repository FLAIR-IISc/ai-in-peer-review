- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), new evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA), and a web evaluation portal; trains embeddings for 59 languages using monolingual data plus pairwise dictionaries and claims better correlation with downstream tasks.

- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA substantially improves correlation with downstream performance and fixes QVEC’s limitations (invariance to linear transforms and normalized scoring).

- Strength: The web portal is a valuable, reproducible resource supporting standardized multilingual embedding evaluation and sound experimental practices.

- Strength: Extensive empirical validation across 59 languages, multiple metrics, and comparison of several estimation methods supports robustness of findings.

- Weakness: No comparison with recent transformer-based multilingual models (mBERT, XLM, XLM-R), leaving unclear how methods stack up against current SOTA.

- Weakness: Language set is skewed toward European languages and many dictionaries were generated via Google Translate, raising concerns about generalizability and dictionary quality/bias.

- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations, especially context-sensitivity issues from multiCluster and implications for real-world use or mitigation strategies.

- Weakness: Inconsistent best-performing methods across metrics are reported but not adequately analyzed or explained.

- Question: How do your dictionary-based methods compare empirically to multilingual BERT and XLM-R on downstream tasks?

- Question: How was the quality of Google Translate-based dictionaries evaluated, and what error analysis was performed on translation pairs?

- Question: How would the methods perform on more typologically diverse languages (different scripts, morphology, syntax)?

- Overall: Important contributions in evaluation metrics and tooling that benefit the community, but claims of “massively multilingual” impact are weakened by limited language diversity and missing SOTA comparisons; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
