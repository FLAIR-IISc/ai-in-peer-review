- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) requiring only monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries.  
- Introduces improved evaluation metrics (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA) that better reflect downstream performance.  
- Provides a web portal for standardized, easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings.  
- Shows multiQVEC-CCA correlates far better with downstream tasks than prior intrinsic metrics (e.g., 0.896 and 0.273 vs 0.007–0.386).  
- Main contributions: novel evaluation metrics, an evaluation web portal, and dictionary-based embedding methods.  
- Strength: rigorous correlation analysis demonstrating previous intrinsic metrics poorly predict downstream performance.  
- Strength: practical evaluation portal lowers barriers and standardizes multilingual embedding evaluation.  
- Strength: clearly articulates why prior metrics (cross-lingual similarity, word translation) are inadequate.  
- Weakness: embedding estimation methods show limited novelty and are sometimes outperformed by existing approaches.  
- Weakness: evaluation metrics depend on supersense annotations that are inconsistent and unavailable for many languages, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with more recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., fastText and other newer multilingual approaches).  
- Question: request evidence/explanation for claim that multiQVEC-CCA is invariant to basis rotation.  
- Question: ask how much multiCCA’s advantage over translation-invariance methods is due to coverage versus intrinsic quality.  
- Question: request exact coverage percentages for multiCluster vs. multiCCA in the 59-language evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — strong evaluation contribution and portal, but address metric limitations and provide fuller comparisons for embedding methods.
