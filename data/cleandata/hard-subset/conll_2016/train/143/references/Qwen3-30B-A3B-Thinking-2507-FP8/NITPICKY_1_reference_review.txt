

# Review of "Multilingual Word Embeddings: Methods and Evaluation"

## Summary of Paper and Main Contributions

This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings (multiCluster and multiCCA), introduces improved evaluation metrics (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA), and develops an evaluation web portal. The authors claim to train embeddings across 59 languages using only monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries. While the paper presents an interesting evaluation framework, several critical methodological and technical issues undermine its contributions.

## Strengths

1. **The web portal for evaluation is a valuable contribution** that will substantially facilitate future research in multilingual NLP. The portal provides a much-needed infrastructure for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings, addressing a genuine gap in the field.

2. **The proposed multiQVEC-CCA metric addresses a legitimate issue** with the original QVEC metric regarding invariance to linear transformations. While the implementation has flaws, the core insight about the need for invariance is valid and important.

3. **The paper provides a comprehensive set of evaluation metrics** that go beyond traditional word similarity and translation tasks, offering a more nuanced approach to evaluating multilingual embeddings.

## Weaknesses

1. **Mathematical and technical errors in the evaluation metric descriptions**: The paper contains significant mathematical errors in describing QVEC-CCA. Specifically, the authors state "QVEC-CCA = CCA(X>,S>) = maxv,w r(X>v,S>w)" which is incorrect. CCA finds the vectors v and w that maximize the correlation, but the correlation itself is r(X>v,S>w), not the max. This fundamental error invalidates their theoretical claims about the metric.

2. **Inconsistent and problematic threshold selection**: The authors state they "fixed τ = 0.1 early on based on manual inspection of the resulting dictionaries" for constructing bilingual dictionaries. This is methodologically unsound - the threshold should be determined through a proper validation procedure, not manual inspection, as it directly impacts dictionary quality and downstream results.

3. **English-centric bias without proper justification**: The authors state they "let the vector space of the initial (monolingual) English embeddings serve as the multilingual vector space" without addressing the inherent English bias this creates. For a paper claiming to work with 59 languages, this English-centric approach is problematic and the paper fails to discuss how this bias affects the multilingual embeddings.

4. **Unsubstantiated claims about correlation significance**: The paper claims that "multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA correlate better with both downstream tasks" based on Table 1. However, the correlation between multiQVEC-CCA and parsing (0.273) is only marginally better than word translation (-0.292), and the correlation for multiQVEC with document classification (0.635) is not significantly better than the monolingual word similarity (0.386) in a statistically meaningful way. The paper provides no statistical significance tests to support these claims.

5. **Inconsistent reporting of coverage metrics**: The paper reports coverage percentages in square brackets but fails to clarify whether these are coverage of the vocabulary in the training data or the evaluation data. This ambiguity makes it difficult to interpret the results, particularly when comparing methods with different coverage rates.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper states "We developed multiSkip independently of Coulmance et al. (2015) and Guo et al. (2016)" but describes a method that appears to be a direct extension of Luong et al. (2015b). Please clarify how your implementation differs from these prior works and provide evidence of independent development.

2. The claim that "multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster according to all evaluation metrics" for 59 languages is based on small differences (e.g., 66.6 vs. 61.0 in multilingual word similarity). Please provide statistical significance tests for these differences.

3. The paper states that "multiQVEC-CCA is invariant to linear transformations," but the mathematical description (QVEC-CCA = CCA(X>,S>)) is incorrect as described in Section 3.3. Please provide the correct mathematical formulation.

## Overall Assessment

This paper presents an interesting evaluation framework for multilingual embeddings, but the numerous technical and methodological flaws significantly undermine its contributions. The mathematical errors in describing the proposed metrics, the unsound threshold selection procedure, and the English-centric bias without proper justification are serious issues that cannot be easily overlooked. While the web portal is a valuable contribution, it is not sufficient to outweigh the methodological weaknesses in the core technical content of the paper.

The paper's claims about the superiority of their proposed metrics are not adequately supported by the data, and the experimental results are not presented with sufficient statistical rigor. Given the stringent standards of CoNLL and the requirement for methodological precision in computational linguistics, I cannot recommend acceptance of this paper in its current form.

I recommend rejection due to the substantial technical flaws that fundamentally undermine the paper's core contributions, despite the potentially valuable evaluation portal. The authors would need to thoroughly revise the paper to address these issues before it could be considered for publication.
