- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings across 59 languages, plus new intrinsic metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) and an evaluation web portal.  
- Main contribution is the evaluation methodology (metrics and portal); embedding methods are incremental.

- Major strength: multiQVEC-CCA addresses basis dependence and normalization and shows dramatically better correlation with downstream tasks (0.896 vs 0.386).  
- Practical strength: the web portal standardizes evaluation and improves reproducibility.  
- Empirical strength: thorough correlation analysis (Table 1) convincingly shows the new metrics outperform previous ones.

- Weakness: proposed embedding methods are not novel and offer only incremental improvements over prior work.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons — paper omits recent transformer-based multilingual baselines (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why multiCCA outperforms multiCluster or why multiQVEC-CCA correlates better.  
- Weakness: evaluation coverage uses vocabulary intersections, which may bias results and not represent full vocabularies.

- Question: How do the methods compare to transformer-based multilingual models like XLM-R?  
- Question: Can you provide a theoretical explanation for multiQVEC-CCA’s better correlation with downstream tasks?  
- Question: Is multiCluster’s degradation with more languages due to semantic ambiguity from clustering or other factors?

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — valuable evaluation contributions and portal, but needs comparison to recent SOTA and clarification on weaknesses.
