- Paper proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), new evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA), and a web evaluation portal.  
- Methods require only monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries (no parallel corpora).  
- Main claimed contribution: multiQVEC-CCA strongly correlates with downstream tasks and addresses theoretical shortcomings of prior metrics.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: 59 languages, intrinsic/extrinsic tasks, showing multiCCA > multiCluster and multiQVEC-CCA as best metric.  
- Web portal is a practical community resource enabling standardized evaluation across nine metrics with dev/test modes.  
- multiCluster and multiCCA offer limited methodological novelty, being straightforward extensions of prior work.  
- multiQVEC(-CCA) depend on supersense annotations available for very few languages, limiting applicability and adoption.  
- Evaluation lacks comparisons to several relevant state-of-the-art multilingual embedding methods, weakening claims of improvement.  
- Use of Google Translate to build dictionaries for 47/59 languages may bias results and affect generalizability.  
- Question: need stronger theoretical justification for why multiCCA extends well to multiple languages beyond empirical results.  
- Question: how to extend multiQVEC-CCA to languages without supersense annotations (e.g., using universal POS tags)?  
- Question: please compare against more recent state-of-the-art methods (at least two from past ~2 years).  
- Overall: valuable contributions in evaluation metrics and tooling, but limited novelty in estimation methods and incomplete comparison to recent work; addressing these would strengthen the paper.
