- Primary contribution: multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA — multilingual extensions of QVEC with CCA to fix basis invariance and normalize dimensionality, showing stronger alignment with downstream performance.
- Secondary contribution: Two scalable, dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods — multiCCA (English-pivot CCA) and multiCluster (connected-component clustering + skip-gram) covering up to 59 languages.
- Tertiary contribution: A public evaluation portal and benchmark suite for intrinsic and extrinsic multilingual assessment.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated evaluation advance addressing QVEC’s basis dependence and unnormalized scores.
- Strength: Pragmatic, reproducible methods that require only monolingual corpora plus bilingual dictionaries, enabling broad language coverage.
- Strength: Solid evaluation suite across nine metrics including two extrinsic tasks, with explicit coverage reporting and discussion of coverage bias.
- Strength: Honest analysis of method behavior (e.g., multiCluster degradation with many languages, multiSkip’s weaknesses) and coverage confounds.
- Strength: Potential community impact via standardized data, metrics, and an upload-and-evaluate portal.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty — multiCCA and multiCluster are incremental extensions of existing ideas.
- Weakness: Potential evaluation leakage and bias from using Google Translate to build both training dictionaries and test lexicons, risking inflated performance.
- Weakness: Correlation analysis is narrow (three languages, 17 embeddings, two downstream tasks) and relies on multilingual supersense resources available for few languages.
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons may be unfair — multiSkip lacks monolingual data and the translation-invariance baseline used a smaller context window for computational reasons.
- Weakness: Pivot-to-English design may introduce pivot bias and symmetry distortions; CCA choices and matrix inversions could be numerically unstable.
- Weakness: Extrinsic tasks are limited and averaged results mask per-language variation; parsing setup removes POS/morph features, possibly reducing realism.

- Q: Dictionary/evaluation leakage — what proportion of evaluation pairs overlap with training dictionaries, and can you rerun with strictly disjoint dictionaries?
- Q: CCA details — how are projections regularized/whitened, constrained (orthogonal?), any numerical issues or condition numbers, and do lower-dimensional projections help?
- Q: Pivot bias — did you try multi-view CCA or joint shared-space approaches, and does pivoting through lower-resource languages degrade performance?
- Q: Hubness/NN effects — did you assess hubness or use alternative retrievals (mean-centered cosine, inverted softmax), and how sensitive are results?
- Q: Sensitivity to dictionary noise and τ — how sensitive are multiCluster and multiCCA to dictionary precision/recall and to noisy machine-translated dictionaries?
- Q: Extrinsic analyses — please provide per-language results, variance across seeds; in document classification how is class imbalance handled; in parsing how is language weighting handled?
- Q: Resource longevity — will the portal have a stable, citable URL and archival copy of benchmarks and code?

- Additional comment: Include a baseline that combines monolingual and parallel signals (e.g., TransGram/BiSkip variants) to strengthen comparisons.
- Additional comment: Report intrinsic metric results on vocabulary intersections in the main text to mitigate coverage confounds.
- Additional comment: Clarify monolingual corpora sources and sizes per language for the 59-language setup.
