- Proposes two dictionary-based methods for massively multilingual embeddings: multiCCA (pivot-English CCA projection) and multiCluster (cross-lingual clusters + skip-gram).
- Adapts QVEC to multilingual settings (multiQVEC) and introduces QVEC-CCA/multiQVEC-CCA to fix basis-variance and dimensionality issues.
- Trains embeddings for 59 languages (dictionary-only) and 12 languages (with parallel corpora baselines) and releases data/splits and an evaluation portal for reproducibility.
- Primary evaluation contribution: multiQVEC-CCA and multilingual variant show stronger correlation with downstream tasks than common intrinsic metrics.
- Primary resource contribution: wide-coverage multilingual embeddings, standardized dev/test splits, and an evaluation portal.
- Primary methodological contribution: straightforward multilingual extension of bilingual CCA and a simple clustering-based approach.
- QVEC-CCA is well-motivated (basis-invariance, normalization) and correlates better with downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 with document classification).
- Large-scale training and released resources lower barriers for future work and reporting of coverage improves fairness in comparisons.
- multiCCA is simple, scalable, requires only monolingual embeddings and dictionaries, and performs strongly at large scale; dictionary-based methods are competitive with parallel-based baselines for 12 languages.
- Experimental design isolates embedding utility (e.g., disabling parser fine-tuning) to clarify embeddings’ contribution to downstream performance.
- Limited methodological novelty: multiCCA and multiCluster are straightforward extensions of prior work, with the main advance in evaluation/resources.
- Baselines may be unfair or incomplete: multiSkip excludes monolingual data and some baselines have constrained settings, complicating comparison claims.
- Heavy reliance on Google Translate dictionaries for 47 languages raises concerns about noise, bias, and potential evaluation/train circularity without quantifying dictionary quality.
- Correlation study is limited in scope (17 embeddings, three languages) with modest effect sizes for parsing and lacks statistical significance or robustness analysis.
- Multilingual design choices under-analyzed: English-pivot may induce hubness/typological bias and alternatives (non-English pivots, multi-pivot, joint projections) are not explored.
- MultiCluster’s performance drop at 59 languages is expected but lacks ablation analysis (cluster size, dictionary precision/recall, sense-splitting) to explain failure modes.
- Minor issues: possible breach of double-blind review via portal IP and redacted code references; formatting and reference inconsistencies.
- Ask authors to report sensitivity of multiCCA to dictionary size/quality and pivot choice, and provide comparisons with non-English or multi-pivot strategies.
- Request quantification of Google Translate dictionary noise and analysis of its impact (e.g., perturbation/filtering) and concerns about using it for evaluation.
- Recommend adding stronger baselines: dictionary-based orthogonal Procrustes mapping and multiSkip variants that use monolingual data for apples-to-apples comparisons.
- Clarify CCA implementation details: projection dimensionality, handling of rank deficiencies, and inversion in ECCA(m, v).
- Provide confidence intervals/significance tests for correlations and extrinsic task differences and expand correlation study to more languages/tasks.
