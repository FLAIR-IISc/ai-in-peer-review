- Primary contribution: multiQVEC-CCA, a basis-invariant, dimension-normalized CCA-based intrinsic metric and its multilingual supersense variant that correlates better with extrinsic tasks than common intrinsic metrics.  
- Secondary contribution: a public evaluation portal and datasets/scripts for standardized, multi-metric assessment of multilingual embeddings with coverage reporting.  
- Tertiary contribution: two simple dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCCA and multiCluster) evaluated across up to 59 languages.  

- Strength: Convincing correlation analysis showing multiQVEC(-CCA) aligns much better with document classification and parsing than word similarity/translation metrics.  
- Strength: Large-scale, practical utility—training/evaluating embeddings for 59 languages and explicitly reporting coverage to address intrinsic-eval biases.  
- Strength: Methods are simple, effective, and reproducible; multiCCA often outperforms baselines on extrinsic tasks.  
- Strength: Careful extrinsic experimental design that isolates embedding effects (fixed pretrained embeddings, reduced feature set in parsing).  
- Strength: Honest discussion of known limitations (semantic conflation, training-data constraints, coverage confounds).  

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—multiCCA is a straightforward extension of prior bilingual CCA work.  
- Weakness: Baseline weaknesses and fairness issues (multiSkip excludes monolingual data; translation-invariance baseline constrained by window size; missing strong baselines like orthogonal Procrustes or multiway CCA).  
- Weakness: Correlation study limited in scope and stability—only 17 systems across 3 languages, no CIs or significance tests, and reliance on supersense resources for those languages.  
- Weakness: Dictionary quality and potential coupling—extensive use of Google Translate for training/evaluation raises noise and leakage concerns not quantified.  
- Weakness: Technical clarity and correctness concerns about CCA invertibility, rank-deficiency, numerical stability, and missing discussion of regularization/pseudoinverses.  
- Weakness: Anonymity breach (raw IP address) and lack of statistical significance/variance reporting for extrinsic results.  

- Question: Under what conditions are the CCA projection matrices invertible; how is rank deficiency handled (regularization, truncation, pseudoinverse) and what ranks are observed with small lexica?  
- Question: Why not include orthogonal Procrustes and a monolingual-data-enabled multiSkip baseline to strengthen comparisons?  
- Question: Can you report confidence intervals/significance for Pearson correlations and replicate the study on other language sets or with different linguistic resources to test robustness?  
- Question: Can you quantify Google Translate dictionary noise and its impact (e.g., by comparing against Wiktionary/Europarl lexicons or varying dictionary size/quality), and ensure no training/evaluation leakage?  
- Question: Does the portal standardize coverage handling (intersection evaluation option), provide variance estimates for extrinsic tasks, and will all training/evaluation scripts, seeds, and preprocessing be released?
