- Proposes multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA to extend QVEC to multilingual settings and address rotation/dimensionality issues, plus releases an evaluation portal and benchmarks.
- Introduces multiCCA (pivot via CCA to English) and multiCluster (cluster translational equivalents then train skip-gram) to build multilingual embeddings for up to 59 languages without parallel corpora.
- Compares multiCCA/multiCluster to multiSkip and a translation-invariance baseline across 12- and 59-language settings, multiple intrinsic metrics, and two downstream tasks.
- QVEC-CCA resolves theoretical issues of QVEC (rotation invariance, comparability across dimensions) and correlates better with some extrinsic tasks than traditional word similarity metrics.
- Reproducible engineering: trains 59-language embeddings using monolingual corpora and dictionaries and releases models/datasets to the community.
- MultiCCA is a simple, effective baseline leveraging monolingual embeddings and bilingual dictionaries, performing strongly especially at scale.
- Evaluation reports coverage alongside scores and includes diverse intrinsic/extrinsic measures, improving transparency.
- Qualitative insight: multiCluster can worsen polysemy by collapsing translational equivalents, explaining performance drops when scaling languages.
- Limited methodological novelty: multiCCA is mainly a direct extension of bilingual CCA and multiCluster is a straightforward pipeline; main novelty is in evaluation.
- Correlation study is underpowered and narrow (17 variants, 3 languages, 2 tasks), with unstable Pearson correlations and no confidence intervals or significance testing.
- Potential evaluation bias: Google Translate was used for both training and test dictionaries in the 59-language setup, risking inflated performance.
- Baseline parity concerns: multiSkip omitted monolingual data and translation-invariance used smaller context windows, likely disadvantaging them and weakening comparisons.
- MultiQVEC(-CCA) limited by supersense resource availability (en/da/it), constraining generality; using only one canonical correlation may be suboptimal.
- Insufficient sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (alignment threshold, cluster caps, CCA regularization) and unclear claims about CCA non-singularity/numerical stability.
- Request confidence intervals/significance tests and robustness checks (Spearman vs Pearson, resampling, more languages/tasks) for correlation results.
- Ask why only the first canonical correlation was used in QVEC-CCA and whether sums/averages of top k were tried and how they performed.
- Request analysis of overlap between training and test pairs when Google Translate is used and replication with independent lexicons for a subset of languages.
- Ask for fairer baselines: multiSkip using monolingual corpora and translation-invariance with matched context windows/training budgets.
- Clarify CCA implementation details: regularization, dimensionality reduction, and how invertibility/numerical stability of projections are ensured.
- Request sensitivity analyses showing robustness of multiCluster/multiCCA to τ, cluster size, embedding dimensionality, and normalization.
- Suggest adding statistical significance/variance for downstream results, ablations with noisier dictionaries, and alternative parsing setups (fine-tuning/POS) to better assess practical utility.
- Recommend documenting portal dataset/metric versioning and ensuring long-term availability to maximize community impact.
