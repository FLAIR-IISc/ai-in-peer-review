- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCCA and multiCluster) to induce shared multilingual embeddings without parallel corpora.  
- Introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA as intrinsic evaluation metrics for multilingual embeddings, with multiQVEC-CCA addressing basis invariance and dimensionality issues.  
- Provides an evaluation portal, trained embeddings, and extensive comparisons across up to 59 languages including extrinsic tasks.

- multiQVEC-CCA shows strong correlation with downstream multilingual document classification and multiQVEC correlates better with parsing than word similarity/translation metrics.  
- The multiCCA method is simple, scalable, resource-light (monolingual data + dictionaries), and competitive across 59 languages.  
- Experiments are thorough, reporting intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations, training details, and explicitly tracking coverage to mitigate confounds.  
- Releases useful resources (code, portal, embeddings, datasets) that aid reproducibility and comparability.  
- multiCCA (pivoting through English) is an elegant, easy-to-implement baseline that performs well empirically.

- Correlation analysis is limited (17 embedding variants, 3 languages, 2 extrinsic tasks), lacks confidence intervals, and may not reflect typical downstream usage due to frozen embeddings and delexicalized models.  
- Some baselines (e.g., multiSkip) are unfairly weak or trained with constrained settings, creating uneven comparisons.  
- Training and evaluation use Google Translate-derived dictionaries for many languages, risking circularity or bias favoring dictionary-aligned methods.  
- multiCluster’s one-vector-per-cluster design exacerbates polysemy across many languages and underperforms; more analysis is needed on when it helps.  
- Non-anonymized URLs, formatting issues, and RCV licensing limit double-blindness and full reproducibility.

- Request confidence intervals/significance tests and stability analyses for the reported correlations (different seeds, language subsets, additional tasks).  
- Ask for clarification and results ensuring no overlap between training dictionaries and evaluation lexicons.  
- Suggest adding stronger baselines that leverage both monolingual and parallel data to match the data used by proposed methods.  
- Question sensitivity to pivot choice (English) and whether joint/multi-way alignment alternatives were tried.  
- Request analysis of dictionary noise and performance as a function of dictionary size/quality for auto-generated dictionaries.  
- Ask to report coverage-normalized intrinsic scores (e.g., intersection vocabulary results) in the main text for fairer comparisons.
