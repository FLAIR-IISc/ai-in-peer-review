- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: multiCCA (pivoting to English via CCA) and multiCluster (cross-lingual clusters with shared embeddings).
- Introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, intrinsic multilingual evaluation metrics addressing QVEC’s rotation and normalization issues.
- Trains multilingual embeddings for up to 59 languages and provides an evaluation portal and packaged resources for reproducible benchmarking.
- Claims the main contributions are the evaluation framework (multiQVEC-CCA and correlation study), the 59-language resources/portal, and pragmatic scalable methods (multiCCA, multiCluster).
- Strong emphasis on evaluation quality with empirical correlation between intrinsic metrics and downstream tasks, showing multiQVEC(-CCA) correlates better than common alternatives.
- CCA-based correction addresses theoretical shortcomings of QVEC (basis dependence and lack of normalization).
- Practical, scalable approaches that require only monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries, enabling broad language coverage.
- multiCCA is computationally light, conceptually simple, and performs strongly across several metrics.
- Solid extrinsic evaluations on multilingual document classification and UD parsing with frozen embeddings to isolate their effects.
- Careful reporting of coverage and discussion of its impact on results.
- Provides community resources (portal, embeddings, data splits) that aid reproducibility and standardization.
- Clear exposition, sensible baselines, and justified design choices (e.g., English pivot).
- Methodological novelty is limited; multiCCA is a straightforward extension and multiCluster is a simple clustering + skip-gram approach prone to polysemy issues.
- Comparisons may be unfair: multiSkip uses only parallel data while related work uses both parallel and monolingual corpora, and translation-invariance was run with a smaller context window for efficiency.
- Using Google Translate-derived dictionaries for training and evaluation across many languages risks overlap and may inflate results or not reflect realistic lexicon induction.
- Correlation study has limited scope and potential bias: 17 points heavily dominated by multiCluster variants, only three languages used, and Pearson correlations reported without robustness checks.
- Some intrinsic evaluations rank within a limited candidate set, which can inflate accuracy versus full-vocabulary retrieval; coverage and frequency effects need deeper analysis.
- multiCluster-specific analyses (cluster semantic coherence, max cluster size impact) and systematic intersection-based comparisons are lacking.
- Asks whether GT-derived training dictionaries overlap with evaluation dictionaries and suggests alternative sources (Wiktionary, PanLex) to reduce concerns.
- Requests Spearman correlations, confidence intervals, and robustness checks (balancing/removing multiCluster variants) for the correlation study.
- Asks how multiCCA performs when pivoting through other languages or projecting into a learned shared space, especially for non-Indo-European languages.
- Requests ablations on dictionary size/quality and monolingual corpus size to characterize multiCCA data requirements.
- Asks for quantification of performance loss from removing POS/morphological features in document classification and parsing to contextualize absolute scores.
- Notes double-blind review compliance for the portal URL, suggests releasing scripts to regenerate GT-based dictionaries, and requests clarification of word translation evaluation protocol (candidate set size, tie-breaking).
- Overall recommendation: weak accept based on strong evaluation contributions and valuable resources, despite moderate methodological novelty.
