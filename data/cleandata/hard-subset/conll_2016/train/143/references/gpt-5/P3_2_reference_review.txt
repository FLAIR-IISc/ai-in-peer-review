Summary
This paper studies massively multilingual word embeddings with limited supervision. It proposes two dictionary-based estimation methods: (i) multiCluster, which builds cross-lingual clusters via dictionaries and trains skip-gram on cluster IDs; and (ii) multiCCA, which learns CCA projections from each language’s monolingual space into English, yielding a shared space. It further adapts QVEC to the multilingual setting (multiQVEC) and introduces QVEC-CCA to address basis-variance and dimensionality issues. The authors evaluate intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, analyze correlation between them, train embeddings for up to 59 languages, and release an evaluation portal.

Main contributions (as I see them)
1) Evaluation framework and metric: multiQVEC-CCA, a theoretically better-behaved intrinsic metric that correlates more strongly with downstream performance than commonly used similarity/translation tasks; plus a correlation study and an evaluation portal.
2) A simple, scalable CCA-based approach (multiCCA) to build multilingual embeddings from monolingual embeddings and bilingual dictionaries, shown to work competitively at scale.
3) A broad empirical study across up to 59 languages with multiple intrinsic and two extrinsic tasks, including careful coverage reporting.

Strengths
- Clear evaluation insight: The paper carefully examines the limitations of standard intrinsic metrics and provides multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA, which remedy QVEC’s basis dependence and dimensionality confounds. The correlation analysis (albeit small-sample) convincingly shows stronger links to downstream tasks than word similarity or word translation.
- Practical, scalable estimation: multiCCA is simple, resource-lean (monolingual corpora plus bilingual dictionaries), and scales to 59 languages. The empirical results show strong coverage and consistent performance relative to alternatives.
- Transparent reporting: The paper highlights coverage effects, reports coverage alongside scores, and discusses vocabulary intersection analyses—important for fair intrinsic comparisons.
- Useful infrastructure: The evaluation portal, data packaging, and standardized benchmarks will likely accelerate research and improve reproducibility.
- Honest analysis of methods: The weaknesses of multiCluster (semantic drift from connected components, exacerbated at 59 languages) are acknowledged and analyzed.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty in methods: multiCCA is a straightforward extension of Faruqui and Dyer’s bilingual CCA by selecting English as a hub space; multiCluster is a naive connected-components clustering that is known to induce semantic chaining. The methodological contribution is incremental relative to prior bilingual mapping literature.
- Ambiguity in CCA formulation: The claim that CCA projection matrices are “guaranteed to be non-singular” is questionable without rank conditions and regularization, especially with high-dimensional embeddings and small dictionaries. The mapping ECCA(m,v) = T^{-1}_{en→m} T_{m→m,en} Em(v) needs clearer justification versus projecting both sides to a canonical subspace.
- Baseline comparability: The multiSkip baseline is at a disadvantage (trained only on parallel data) relative to methods that exploit much larger monolingual corpora; translation-invariance is run with a smaller window (3 vs 5) for computational reasons; hyperparameters are not matched across methods. This weakens head-to-head conclusions.
- Potential evaluation leakage: For 59 languages, Google Translate is used to create both training dictionaries (top-20k) and the evaluation dictionaries for word translation. If there is vocabulary overlap, this risks circularity in intrinsic evaluation. The paper should ensure disjointness or use independent gold resources.
- Extrinsic setup is narrow and somewhat contrived: Document classification is limited in languages; parsing removes POS/morphology and freezes embeddings, emphasizing embeddings but diverging from typical practice and potentially underestimating downstream utility. No evaluations on other common cross-lingual tasks (e.g., NER, XNLI, MT lexicon induction) where cross-lingual geometry matters.
- multiQVEC(-CCA) resource dependence: Reliance on supersense annotations for only three languages limits generality; the paper notes inconsistencies but this undermines claims of “multilingual” evaluation. Correlation is computed over 17 models without confidence intervals or statistical testing and within the same small language triad.
- Anonymity and presentation: The portal’s IP address and some reference formatting appear to break double-blind and reduce polish.

Questions to the authors
- CCA mapping: Please clarify the mathematical rationale for ECCA(m,v) = T^{-1}_{en→m,en} T_{m→m,en} Em(v). Under what conditions are these matrices invertible? Did you regularize CCA, reduce dimensionality, or enforce orthogonality? How sensitive are results to these choices?
- Data leakage: For the 59-language word translation evaluation, did you ensure the evaluation pairs are disjoint from (and built independently of) the GT-derived training dictionaries? If not, can you re-run with disjoint vocabularies or Wiktionary-based tests?
- Baselines: Can you include a mapping baseline based on orthogonal Procrustes (e.g., regress to English with orthogonality constraint) for a more direct comparison to multiCCA? Also, can you provide multiSkip results that leverage monolingual corpora (as in related work) to level the playing field?
- Robustness: How do results vary with embedding dimensionality and CCA target dimension? Can you report confidence intervals/significance for the correlation analyses, and Spearman as well as Pearson?
- multiCluster design: Did you try limiting cluster transitivity (e.g., only star-shaped clusters around English, or restricting degree/edge confidence) to mitigate semantic chaining? Any ablations on threshold τ or maximum cluster size?

Additional comments
- Reporting coverage intersections for all intrinsic metrics strengthens claims; consider including those main-table variants. 
- The extrinsic parsing setup is informative as a stress test; adding a more realistic setting (e.g., with POS or fine-tuning embeddings) would better reflect practical utility.
- Please ensure anonymization (remove portal identifiers) and fix reference formatting.
