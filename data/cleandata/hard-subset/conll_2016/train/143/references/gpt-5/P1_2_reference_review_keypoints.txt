- Paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for massively multilingual embeddings: multiCCA (CCA projection into English) and multiCluster (cluster translational equivalents and train skipgram).
- Introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA as multilingual intrinsic evaluation metrics aligning embeddings to linguistic property matrices and addressing QVEC’s basis/dimensionality issues.
- Releases an evaluation portal with datasets, scripts, and reproducible infrastructure for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation.
- multiQVEC-CCA shows substantially better correlation with downstream tasks than common intrinsic metrics (e.g., 0.896 with document classification) across 17 systems and 2 extrinsic tasks.
- Broad multilingual scope: embeddings trained for 59 languages with reported coverage analysis and careful experimental methodology.
- multiCCA is simple, scalable, and competitive, often outperforming multiCluster and matching or exceeding parallel-corpus baselines in several settings.
- Thoughtful experimental design mixing intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations, with analyses isolating embedding quality (e.g., frozen embeddings, POS/morphology removal).
- Clear exposition and reproducibility: methods and hyperparameters are well-documented to facilitate replication.
- multiSkip baseline implementation omits monolingual data, and translation-invariance uses a smaller context window, potentially weakening baseline comparisons.
- Possible train–test bias: Google Translate dictionaries used for training and evaluation in 47 of 59 languages may inflate translation metrics due to circularity.
- Modeling novelty is limited: multiCCA and multiCluster are straightforward extensions of prior bilingual and cross-lingual methods.
- Correlation study scope is limited: correlations computed over 17 systems in only 3 languages and 2 tasks, with no reported statistical significance or confidence intervals.
- multiQVEC methods depend on limited manually annotated linguistic resources (supersense annotations in only three languages), constraining applicability and introducing potential bias.
- Request for correlation robustness checks across random seeds, different monolingual corpora, and additional downstream tasks (e.g., NER/POS or zero-shot transfer) with significance testing.
- Ask for analysis of dictionary overlap between training and evaluation in the 59-language setup and results using disjoint evaluation dictionaries.
- Suggest ablations on multiCCA sensitivity to dictionary noise, size, and threshold τ for dictionary extraction.
- Question pivot choice: why English only, and whether multi-pivot or joint CCA projections were tried to reduce English-specific biases.
- Recommend strengthening baselines: run multiSkip with monolingual corpora and match translation-invariance window sizes to fairly isolate architectural differences.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept, leaning accept, contingent on clarifications and minor additional experiments.
