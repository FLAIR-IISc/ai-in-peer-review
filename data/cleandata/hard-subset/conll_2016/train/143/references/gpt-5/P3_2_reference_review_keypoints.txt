- Paper proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (cluster IDs + skip-gram) and multiCCA (per-language CCA projections into English).
- Adapts QVEC to multilingual settings with multiQVEC and introduces QVEC-CCA to address basis and dimensionality issues.
- Provides an evaluation framework, correlation study showing multiQVEC-CCA better correlates with downstream tasks, and an evaluation portal.
- Empirical study trains embeddings for up to 59 languages and reports multiple intrinsic and two extrinsic tasks with coverage reporting.
- multiQVEC-CCA is claimed to be a theoretically better-behaved intrinsic metric that correlates more with downstream performance than similarity/translation metrics.
- multiCCA is simple, scalable, and resource-lean, performing competitively at large scale.
- The paper transparently reports coverage effects and vocabulary intersection analyses to enable fair intrinsic comparisons.
- The released portal, data packaging, and benchmarks improve reproducibility and research acceleration.
- Authors honestly analyze multiCluster’s weaknesses, noting semantic drift from connected components at scale.
- multiCCA’s methodological novelty is limited; it extends bilingual CCA by choosing English as a hub, and multiCluster is a known naive clustering approach.
- The CCA formulation claims (e.g., guaranteed nonsingular projection matrices) are ambiguous and may not hold without rank conditions or regularization.
- The mapping formula ECCA(m,v) = T^{-1}_{en→m} T_{m→m,en} Em(v) needs clearer justification versus canonical subspace projection.
- MultiSkip baseline is disadvantaged (trained only on parallel data) and hyperparameters are not consistently matched across methods.
- Translation-invariance baseline used a smaller window (3 vs 5) for computational reasons, weakening direct comparisons.
- Potential data leakage: Google Translate was used to build both training and evaluation dictionaries for 59 languages, risking circularity if vocabularies overlap.
- Extrinsic evaluation is narrow and somewhat contrived: limited languages for document classification and parsing that removes POS/morphology and freezes embeddings.
- No evaluations on other common cross-lingual tasks (e.g., NER, XNLI, MT lexicon induction) where cross-lingual geometry matters.
- multiQVEC(-CCA) depends on supersense annotations available for only three languages, limiting the claimed multilingual generality.
- Correlation analyses use 17 models without confidence intervals, statistical testing, or Spearman correlations reported.
- Portal’s IP address and reference formatting break double-blind anonymity and reduce presentation polish.
- Authors are asked to clarify CCA invertibility, regularization, dimensionality choices, and sensitivity of results to these choices.
- Reviewer requests assurance that evaluation dictionaries are disjoint from GT-derived training dictionaries or re-running with disjoint/Wiktionary-based tests to avoid leakage.
- Suggest adding an orthogonal Procrustes mapping baseline and stronger multiSkip baselines that use monolingual corpora for fairer comparison.
- Request robustness checks: vary embedding and CCA dimensions, report confidence intervals/significance, and Spearman correlations.
- Suggest mitigating multiCluster semantic chaining via limited transitivity (e.g., star clusters, degree/edge thresholds) and report ablations on τ or max cluster size.
- Recommend reporting coverage intersections for all intrinsic metrics, adding more realistic extrinsic settings (e.g., with POS or fine-tuning), and fixing anonymization and reference formatting.
