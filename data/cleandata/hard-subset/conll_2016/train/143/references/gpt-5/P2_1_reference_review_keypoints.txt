- Primary contribution: Proposes multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, fixing QVEC’s basis dependence and dimensionality bias, and showing much better correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Secondary contribution: Implements and evaluates two scalable dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCCA and multiCluster) across 59 languages with detailed 12-language comparisons.  
- Tertiary contribution: Provides a web portal, datasets, code, and standardized automated evaluations for community use.

- Strength: Correct and principled critique of QVEC with a solid CCA-based fix and clear multilingual extension using supersense tag matrices.  
- Strength: Broad and thorough empirical evaluation across 59 languages and a 12-language subset, including extrinsic tasks with embeddings as sole token features.  
- Strength: multiCCA is simple, scalable, robust, and offers strong coverage and competitive accuracy at large language scales.  
- Strength: Careful treatment and reporting of coverage confounds, improving interpretability of intrinsic evaluations.  
- Strength: Good reproducibility and potential community impact via provided hyperparameters, code, and portal.

- Weakness: Limited novelty in estimation methods—multiCCA and multiCluster are straightforward extensions/engineering rather than algorithmic innovations.  
- Weakness: Some baselines may be unfair or weak (multiSkip excluding monolingual data; restricted context window for translation-invariance), complicating comparisons.  
- Weakness: Correlation study is limited in scope (17 embeddings, two extrinsic task setups) and lacks confidence intervals or significance testing; correlations vary across tasks.  
- Weakness: 59-language translation evaluation may suffer from training–evaluation dependence due to reliance on Google Translate for both training dictionaries and evaluation.  
- Weakness: Multilingual supersense resource limited to en/da/it with inconsistencies, restricting breadth and possibly biasing intrinsic evaluations.

- Question: Please report confidence intervals or p-values for Pearson correlations and test significance of differences between metrics.  
- Question: How sensitive is multiCCA to pivot language choice; did you try joint multi-view CCA or multiple pivots?  
- Question: Can you include a multiSkip variant using monolingual data or re-run translation-invariance with a larger context window to ensure fair baselines?  
- Question: Can you quantify or mitigate Google Translate training–evaluation dependence in the 59-language experiments (e.g., disjoint seeds or alternative dictionaries)?  
- Question: For multiCluster, did you try sense disambiguation, subword/character features, or varying cluster size thresholds to reduce semantic conflation?

- Overall: High significance for evaluation practice, moderate for methods; generally sound but needs stronger baselines and statistical reporting; reproducibility and clarity are good.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer leans positive based on evaluation contribution, empirical breadth, and practical impact, with suggested improvements for baselines and statistical rigor.
