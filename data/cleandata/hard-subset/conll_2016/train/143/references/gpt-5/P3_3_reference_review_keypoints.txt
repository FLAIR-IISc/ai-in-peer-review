1) Paper studies massively multilingual word embeddings and introduces multiCluster and multiCCA.  
2) Proposes intrinsic metrics multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA to better correlate with downstream tasks.  
3) Provides a public evaluation portal, code, and datasets for standardized evaluation.  
4) Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA as a rotation/scale-invariant intrinsic metric aligned to linguistic features.  
5) Contribution: scalable, dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCCA, multiCluster) up to 59 languages.  
6) Contribution: community resources (portal, released models, datasets).  
7) Strength: Correct critique of QVEC’s basis dependence and dimensionality bias, with a principled CCA fix.  
8) Strength: Correlation analysis showing common intrinsic metrics can be weak predictors of downstream performance.  
9) Strength: multiCCA is a simple, effective extension of bilingual CCA via an English pivot with strong empirical results.  
10) Strength: Broad evaluation across intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, 12- and 59-language settings, and coverage reporting.  
11) Strength: Reproducibility and practical impact through released models, portal, and clear training details.  
12) Strength: Useful negative result showing multiCluster degrades at scale due to sense conflation.  
13) Weakness: Baselines may be unfairly handicapped (multiSkip without monolingual data; translation-invariance with smaller context window).  
14) Weakness: Training dictionaries and evaluation pairs for 59 languages both come from Google Translate, risking leakage and optimistic scores.  
15) Weakness: Correlation study limited to three languages, 17 related embeddings, and single Pearson coefficients without significance or CIs.  
16) Weakness: multiCluster risk of over-merging senses into large heterogeneous clusters; no cluster purity analysis.  
17) Weakness: multiCCA uses non-orthogonal projections and English pivoting that may distort monolingual geometry and propagate noise; orthogonal mappings not analyzed.  
18) Weakness: Downstream experiments freeze embeddings and remove features, under-representing practical use and lacking true cross-lingual transfer/zero-shot tests.  
19) Question: How much overlap exists between GT-derived training dictionaries and evaluation pairs for the 59-language translation metric?  
20) Question: Have you tried orthogonality constraints (e.g., Procrustes) or summing top-k canonical correlations in multiQVEC-CCA, and how do results change?  
21) Question: Can you report per-language or non-English pair results to assess English pivot effects?  
22) Question: How sensitive are multiCCA/multiCluster to dictionary size, noise, and alignment threshold τ—any ablations?  
23) Question: For the correlation analysis, can you provide confidence intervals (e.g., bootstrap) and test robustness across embedding dimensionalities and corpora?  
24) Additional: Please specify portal maintenance plans and consider adding open corpora given RCV licensing constraints.  
25) Additional: Provide intersection-only scores in main text to address coverage confounds.  
26) Additional: Discuss related orthogonal mapping/Procrustes methods and hubness mitigation even if contemporaneous.  
27) Overall: Solid and useful contribution on evaluation methodology and scalable multilingual embeddings, but baseline fairness and evaluation leakage need addressing.
