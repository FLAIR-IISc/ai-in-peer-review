- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings across 59 languages.
- Introduces improved evaluation metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA), with multiQVEC-CCA showing superior correlation to downstream tasks.
- Provides a web portal and open-source code for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation.

- multiQVEC-CCA achieves strong correlation with document classification (0.896), outperforming prior intrinsic metrics.
- The web portal and dev/test design promote standardized evaluation and reproducibility.
- Demonstrates feasibility of training embeddings for 59 languages using only dictionaries; multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster.

- Claim of “no parallel data” is misleading because bilingual dictionaries are required and may be scarce for low-resource languages.
- multiQVEC-CCA shows low correlation with parsing (0.273), raising doubts about its utility for syntactic tasks and lacking an explanation.
- Paper omits comparisons to recent unsupervised multilingual methods (e.g., Conneau et al. 2017), limiting assessment of novelty.

- How does the approach handle low-resource languages with no or scarce bilingual dictionaries?
- Why is the correlation with parsing performance much lower than with document classification?
- How do these methods compare empirically to unsupervised dictionary-free approaches (Conneau et al., Artetxe et al.)?
