- Proposes multilingual word embedding methods for 59 languages using only dictionaries and monolingual data.  
- Introduces two estimation approaches: multiCluster and multiCCA.  
- Proposes a new intrinsic evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA.  
- Claims main contribution: a reliable evaluation metric that better correlates with downstream tasks.  
- Claims main contribution: a web portal for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings.  
- Claims main contribution: demonstration that dictionary-based methods scale to 59 languages.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows strong correlation with downstream tasks (0.896 with document classification, 0.273 with parsing).  
- Strength: the web portal improves reproducibility and standardization.  
- Strength: experiments across 59 languages demonstrate scalability of the approach.  
- Strength: thorough correlation analysis between intrinsic and extrinsic metrics supports the evaluation framework.  
- Weakness: limited novelty of estimation methods, with multiCCA building directly on prior work (Faruqui & Dyer 2014).  
- Weakness: evaluation comparisons are confounded by coverage differences across methods (e.g., multiCCA’s higher coverage vs. translation-invariance).  
- Weakness: no comparison to recent transformer-based multilingual models (e.g., multilingual BERT variants).  
- Weakness: inconsistent supersense annotations across languages are not fully addressed as a limitation of the evaluation metric.  
- Recommendations: clarify comparisons to transformer-based multilingual approaches, discuss coverage effects on evaluation, propose standard handling for coverage, and provide stronger theoretical justification for the estimation methods.
