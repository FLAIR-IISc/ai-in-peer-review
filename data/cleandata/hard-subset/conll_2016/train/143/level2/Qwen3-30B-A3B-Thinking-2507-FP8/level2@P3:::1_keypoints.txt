- Introduces dictionary-based methods to estimate multilingual embeddings for 50+ languages using monolingual data instead of parallel corpora.  
- Proposes multiCCA, extending bilingual CCA approaches to many languages via dictionaries.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation metric that better correlates with downstream tasks.  
- Provides a web portal for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings.  
- Empirically shows multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 for document classification) than prior metrics.  
- Strong motivation and clear advancement beyond bilingual-focused embedding work.  
- The web portal is a valuable, accessible resource likely to facilitate future research.  
- Lacks analysis explaining why multiCluster degrades significantly when scaling to 59 languages versus 12.  
- Limited evaluation language sets (59 and 12 languages) without sufficient justification of representativeness.  
- multiQVEC-CCA depends on supersense annotations that may be unavailable for many languages, with no proposed remedy.  
- Asks how evaluation can be adapted for languages lacking supersense annotations.  
- Asks what linguistic phenomena cause multiCluster's poorer performance at large scale and how to mitigate them.  
- Asks about computational cost and scalability of training multiCCA for 59 languages versus smaller sets.  
- Notes the claim that multiCCA outperforms multiCluster is supported but calls for deeper analysis of semantic ambiguity effects and portal applicability to languages outside the current set.
