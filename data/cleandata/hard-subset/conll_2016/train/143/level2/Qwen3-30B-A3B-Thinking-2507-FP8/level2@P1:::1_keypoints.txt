- Paper introduces multilingual embedding estimation across 59 languages using dictionaries and monolingual data; contributions: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiQVEC-CCA, and a web evaluation portal.

- multiQVEC-CCA is a major contribution, showing much stronger correlation with downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 doc classification, 0.273 parsing) than prior metrics.

- multiCCA is well-motivated and consistently outperforms multiCluster in the 59-language setting by extending CCA to multilingual alignment.

- The web portal offers standardized evaluation tools, training-data downloads, and embedding uploads, improving reproducibility and comparability.

- Claim of "no parallel data" is misleading because dictionaries were extracted from parallel corpora (Europarl) and Google Translate was used for 59 languages.

- The paper does not adequately analyze why multiCluster underperforms in the 59-language setting and lacks supporting evidence for the stated reasons.

- multiQVEC-CCA’s reliance on supersense annotations (available for only a few languages) limits its applicability and is not properly addressed.

- Reported coverage differences (multiCCA vs translation-invariance) confound performance comparisons, and no clear method for fair comparison is provided beyond bracketed percentages.

- Evaluation of multiCluster is limited to a single baseline (12 languages) and lacks analysis of why it fares better on smaller sets.

- Question: Justify cluster size thresholds in multiCluster (1,000 for 12 languages, 10,000 for 59 languages) and report any ablation studies.

- Question: How should evaluations be handled when methods have significantly different coverage percentages?

- Question: Clarify the theoretical shortcoming of QVEC that multiQVEC-CCA is intended to address.

- Overall comment: Solid empirical and practical contributions, but the paper should expand discussion of limitations—particularly supersense dependence and coverage issues.
