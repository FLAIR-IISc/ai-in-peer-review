- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic metric that fixes QVEC's rotation and normalization issues and shows much higher correlation with downstream tasks.
- Proposes two estimation methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, to build multilingual embeddings from monolingual corpora plus bilingual dictionaries, avoiding parallel data.
- Releases a public web portal providing datasets and tools for standardized, reproducible evaluation of multilingual embeddings.

- multiQVEC-CCA is theoretically sound (uses CCA) and empirically correlates far better with downstream performance than prior intrinsic metrics.
- Dictionary-based methods (multiCCA/multiCluster) scale to many languages and are competitive with or superior to parallel-data methods on several metrics.
- Experiments are thorough across intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, multiple language sets (up to 59), and carefully address vocabulary coverage issues.
- The open-source evaluation portal is a valuable community resource that lowers barriers and promotes standardized comparisons.

- multiCCA offers limited algorithmic novelty, being a straightforward hub-and-spoke extension of prior bilingual CCA work.
- Both methods depend heavily on bilingual dictionaries and an English pivot, with no analysis of sensitivity to pivot choice or dictionary quality.
- multiQVEC-CCA relies on scarce manually created linguistic resources (supersense-tagged corpora), limiting its applicability to many low-resource languages.

- How sensitive are results to using English as the pivot, and have non-pivot or multi-pivot (decentralized) approaches been tested?
- Can multiCluster's performance drop when scaling to 59 languages be mitigated by finer-grained or sense-aware clustering, or by limiting cluster size?
