- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a new intrinsic evaluation metric that fixes theoretical flaws of prior methods and correlates better with downstream tasks.  
- Proposes two scalable estimators, multiCluster and multiCCA, to build shared embeddings for up to 59 languages using monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries.  
- Releases a public web portal and open-source toolkit to standardize evaluation and accelerate multilingual embedding research.  
- multiQVEC-CCA is robust and validated, showing high correlation with extrinsic task performance and offering a reliable proxy for evaluation.  
- multiCCA is scalable and accessible, enabling embeddings for low-resource languages by relying on bilingual dictionaries rather than parallel corpora.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive and careful, comparing multiple methods across nine intrinsic and extrinsic tasks with controls for variables like vocabulary coverage.  
- The public portal, code, and datasets are a significant community contribution that enhances reproducibility and future work.  
- The estimation methods have limited technical novelty, mainly extending and combining existing bilingual techniques rather than introducing fundamentally new algorithms.  
- Results are inconclusive: proposed methods are competitive but are outperformed by a translation-invariance baseline on several tasks, limiting claims of superiority.  
- The pivot-based multiCCA depends on English as a single pivot, introducing potential bias and dependency on English dictionaries that the paper does not sufficiently discuss.
