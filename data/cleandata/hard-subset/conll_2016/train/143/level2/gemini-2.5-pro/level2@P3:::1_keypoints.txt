- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a basis-invariant, normalized intrinsic metric that correlates better with downstream tasks than prior intrinsic metrics.  
- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, scalable to 59 languages without parallel corpora.  
- Provides a public evaluation portal and releases code, data, and trained embeddings to support reproducible research.

- Rigorous empirical evaluation showing multiQVEC-CCA has higher correlation with extrinsic task performance than word similarity/translation metrics.  
- Scalable, resource-light estimation methods (multiCCA/multiCluster) that work with monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries, enabling coverage of many low-resource languages.  
- Thorough experimental design that controls confounds (e.g., parsing task setup) and addresses vocabulary-coverage effects.  
- Valuable community contribution via a public portal and shared resources for standardized evaluation.

- multiQVEC-CCA depends on supersense annotations available for only three languages, limiting its direct applicability across all 59 languages.  
- multiCCA’s projection formula (using T⁻¹_en→m,en * T_m→m,en) lacks clear justification or comparison to simpler alternatives.  
- multiCluster performs well in 12-language settings but degrades in the 59-language setting, suggesting semantic ambiguity and robustness issues as language count increases.  
- Baseline comparisons are uneven: multiSkip uses only parallel corpora while proposed methods use large monolingual data, making direct comparisons less fair.

- Please clarify the intuition and advantages of the multiCCA projection formulation versus simpler single-matrix projections.  
- Can multiQVEC-CCA be extended using more widely available annotations (e.g., universal POS) and how do such variants correlate with downstream tasks compared to results in Table 1?
