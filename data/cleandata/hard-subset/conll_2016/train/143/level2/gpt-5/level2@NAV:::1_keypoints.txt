- Proposes scalable dictionary-only methods for massively multilingual word embeddings (multiCCA and multiCluster) and introduces improved intrinsic evaluation (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA) with a public evaluation portal and resources.
- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA as an improved intrinsic metric showing stronger correlation with downstream tasks than word similarity/translation metrics.
- Contribution: multiCCA, a practical dictionary-based pipeline that scales to 59 languages and competes with parallel-data methods on several metrics.
- Contribution: released standardized datasets, trained models, and a web portal to facilitate reproducible evaluation.
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence that multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA better predict downstream performance (e.g., high correlation with document classification).
- Strength: Broad, practical approach enabling embeddings for 59 languages using only monolingual data and dictionaries, valuable for low-resource settings.
- Strength: Thorough experimental comparisons with clear baselines across intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.
- Strength: Emphasis on reproducibility and community impact via released resources and a web portal.
- Strength: Sensible discussion and partial control of coverage effects in evaluation.
- Weakness: Moderate methodological novelty—multiCCA extends prior bilingual CCA and multiCluster worsens polysemy as languages increase.
- Weakness: Intrinsic evaluation depends on supersense annotations for only en/da/it, limiting generality; weak correlation with parsing.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Google Translate for dictionaries raises concerns about noise and circularity, with no analysis of robustness to dictionary quality.
- Weakness: Coverage confounds persist; some results compare differing vocabularies despite acknowledgments.
- Weakness: Correlation study uses 17 models and limited downstream tasks, reducing statistical robustness; more tasks would strengthen claims.
- Question: How sensitive are multiCCA and multiCluster to dictionary size, noise, and coverage?
- Question: Would using a non-English hub or multi-hub/joint CCA change results for typologically distant languages?
- Question: Can multiQVEC(-CCA) be extended beyond supersenses (e.g., UD POS/morphology) to broaden language coverage?
- Question: Can evaluation avoid GT-derived test pairs (e.g., use Wiktionary-only) to reduce bias from Google Translate?
- Question: Could subword modeling mitigate multiCluster’s sense conflation and improve performance for low-resource languages?
