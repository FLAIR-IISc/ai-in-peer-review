- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCCA, multiCluster) for massively multilingual embeddings without parallel corpora.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a principled intrinsic evaluation metric addressing basis-variance and normalization and showing stronger downstream correlation.  
- Provides a public evaluation portal with datasets, code, and trained models to standardize comparisons.  
- multiQVEC-CCA shows high correlation (0.896) with multilingual document classification and outperforms common intrinsic metrics.  
- Demonstrates scalable training for 59 languages using only monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries; multiCCA scales more robustly than multiCluster.  
- Empirical setup is thorough and balanced: compares dictionary- and parallel-based methods, reports intrinsic/extrinsic metrics, and analyzes coverage effects.  
- Provides practical impact via downloadable tooling, datasets, and trained models with splits for reproducibility.  
- Includes sensible baselines and analyses (translation-invariance factorization, multilingual skip-gram) and discusses when each method wins.  
- Risk of evaluation-data leakage: many dictionaries and eval pairs derived from Google Translate, possibly inflating results.  
- multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA depend on supersense annotations available for only a few languages, limiting metric applicability and biasing correlations.  
- multiSkip baseline is not comparable to prior work because it omits monolingual data, potentially understating parallel-based methods.  
- Several methodological choices lack ablation/sensitivity analysis (dictionary threshold, pivot choice, CCA regularization, corpus size/quality).  
- Claim that multiCCA “consistently” outperforms multiCluster at 59 languages needs per-language breakdowns and robustness checks.  
- Extrinsic gains are mixed and lack statistical significance testing and per-language granularity.  
- Question: How sensitive is multiCCA to the pivot language and would non-English or multi-pivot alignment change results?  
- Question: What CCA regularization is used and how sensitive are results to regularization and dimensionality?  
- Question: How sensitive are methods to dictionary noise and size; report performance versus dictionary coverage and τ threshold?  
- Question: Does using Google Translate for both training and evaluation inflate results; can sources be cleanly separated?  
- Question: Can parallel-data methods be rerun with monolingual corpora included to ensure resource parity with prior work?  
- Question: Are reported extrinsic improvements statistically significant; please add significance tests or discuss variability?  
- Question: Can intrinsic scores be recomputed on shared vocabularies to quantify coverage effects beyond percent reporting?  
- Suggest per-language extrinsic results and typological analysis to see which languages benefit most from each method.  
- Suggest exploring polysemy effects of cluster-sharing and considering sense-disambiguated or token-level alternatives.  
- Recommend including alternative intrinsic evaluations that don’t require language-specific annotations (e.g., independent gold dictionaries).  
- Ensure evaluation portal and URLs are compatible with double-blind review and clarify concrete training details (corpus sources/sizes, tokenization, CCA constraints).  
- Overall: Valuable methodological and evaluation advances with strong tooling; addressing leakage, baseline parity, and robustness analyses would strengthen the paper and support acceptance.
