- Proposes two dictionary-based methods for massively multilingual embeddings: multiCCA (per-language CCA into English) and multiCluster (cluster translation-equivalent types, train skip-gram on cluster IDs).  
- Introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, multilingual intrinsic evaluation metrics aligning embeddings with linguistic-feature matrices and addressing QVEC’s basis-rotation and dimensionality issues.  
- Releases a web portal, code, and pretrained embeddings for up to 59 languages to standardize training and evaluation.  
- Main contribution 1: QVEC-CCA and its multilingual variant are principled intrinsic metrics that improve correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Main contribution 2: multiCCA is a practical recipe to build a single shared embedding space for many languages using bilingual dictionaries and monolingual corpora.  
- Main contribution 3: Resources — a public evaluation portal and multilingual embeddings.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated intrinsic evaluation with empirical support showing higher correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Strength: Pragmatic multilingual scaling without parallel data, suitable for low-resource settings and demonstrated on 59 languages.  
- Strength: Thorough, diverse evaluation including coverage reporting, intersection-of-vocab control, and two extrinsic tasks.  
- Strength: Honest analysis noting multiCluster’s amplified type-level ambiguity and degraded performance with many languages.  
- Strength: Community impact via public datasets and an evaluation portal with dev/test splits.  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons may be unfair—multiSkip lacks monolingual data and translation-invariance used smaller windows, disadvantaging baselines.  
- Weakness: Potential bias from using Google Translate for both training and evaluation dictionaries in the 59-language experiments, risking leakage.  
- Weakness: Limited correlation study scope (17 models, 3 languages) which may inflate metric correlations due to overlap with linguistic resources.  
- Weakness: Hub-based multiCCA projects all languages to English, which may harm non-English–non-English distances and lacks deep diagnostic analysis.  
- Weakness: Methodological novelty is incremental; main novelty is evaluation and resources rather than modeling.  
- Question: Provide multiSkip results that also use monolingual corpora and translation-invariance scaled to comparable hyperparameters?  
- Question: How much overlap exists between Google-Translate-derived training dictionaries and evaluation dictionaries; can results be reported on disjoint evaluation sets?  
- Question: Why compose mappings via T_en→m,en inverse with T_m→m,en in multiCCA rather than mapping to a shared canonical space; clarify orthogonality and numerical stability of inverses.  
- Question: How does performance vary with dictionary size/quality and embedding dimensionality; any sensitivity or ablation studies?  
- Question: Provide analyses for non-English–non-English pairs and for typologically distant/morphologically rich languages to test the hub assumption.  
- Additional comment: Ensure GT-derived dictionaries and evaluation lists are redistributable or provide scripts to recreate them.  
- Additional comment: Add significance tests for downstream comparisons and report runtime/memory for practical guidance.  
- Additional comment: Fix minor reference formatting issues for the camera-ready.
