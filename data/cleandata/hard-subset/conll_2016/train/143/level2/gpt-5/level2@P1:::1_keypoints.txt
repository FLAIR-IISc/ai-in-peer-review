- Contribution: Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, a new intrinsic evaluation metric fixing QVEC's basis dependence and normalization and extending it to multilingual settings.  
- Contribution: Introduces scalable, dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCCA and multiCluster) trained for up to 59 languages without sentence-aligned corpora.  
- Contribution: Provides a systematic empirical comparison of intrinsic vs. extrinsic metrics and releases an evaluation portal with code and data.

- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows strong correlation with downstream tasks (e.g., ~0.90 with multilingual document classification), outperforming common intrinsic metrics.  
- Strength: Practical, parallel-free methods scale to 59 languages; multiCCA is simple, reproducible, and robust at scale while multiCluster degrades due to sense conflation.  
- Strength: Thorough multi-metric evaluation with clear baselines across 12- and 59-language settings, yielding nuanced findings about method trade-offs.  
- Strength: Thoughtful handling of coverage confounds in intrinsic evaluation and provision of a portal to standardize reporting.  
- Strength: Community impact through released resources and an evaluation portal that lowers barriers for future work.

- Weakness: multiQVEC-CCA is limited by linguistic matrices based on supersense annotations for only three languages (en, da, it), restricting generality and statistical confidence.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on noisy/proprietary dictionaries (Google Translate and fast_align-derived) for many languages, with no sensitivity analysis of dictionary quality or thresholds.  
- Weakness: Hub-based multiCCA mapping into English may bias representations toward English; alternative mappings and mitigations are not explored.  
- Weakness: Extrinsic evaluations are simplified (no POS/morphology, frozen embeddings) and limited in language/task scope, possibly understating practical downstream effects.  
- Weakness: multiCluster exacerbates polysemy by collapsing cross-lingual senses at large scale, with no proposed mitigations (sense induction or context-aware methods).
