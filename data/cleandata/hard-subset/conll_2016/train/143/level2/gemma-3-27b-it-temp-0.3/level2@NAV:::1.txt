## Review of "Estimating and Evaluating Multilingual Embeddings"

This paper introduces methods for estimating and evaluating word embeddings across 59 languages within a shared embedding space. The core contributions are two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA), a refined evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a publicly available web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings. 

The strongest aspect of this work is the comprehensive evaluation framework. The authors identify limitations in existing evaluation metrics and propose multiQVEC-CCA, which demonstrably correlates better with downstream tasks (text categorization and parsing) than previous methods. The development and release of the evaluation portal is also a significant contribution, providing a valuable resource for the research community. The experimental setup, including the use of both intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations across multiple languages, is thorough and strengthens the validity of the findings. 

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. While the proposed estimation methods are presented, their performance isn’t consistently superior to existing baselines, particularly the translation-invariance method. The results indicate that multiCCA performs well, but the reasons for its success (and the relative underperformance of multiCluster) aren’t deeply explored. The paper acknowledges the limitations of dictionary-based methods, but doesn’t sufficiently address how these limitations impact the quality of embeddings for languages with limited or noisy dictionary resources. The discussion of related work, while extensive, lacks critical analysis of how this work builds upon or diverges from existing approaches in a novel way. 

Furthermore, the paper’s presentation could be improved. The numerous equations and abbreviations make it difficult to follow the technical details without repeated reference to earlier sections. The results section is dense and could benefit from clearer visualizations and more concise summaries of key findings.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the specific challenges encountered when applying multiCluster to languages with limited dictionary resources, and what strategies could be employed to mitigate these challenges?
2.  The paper highlights the better correlation of multiQVEC-CCA with downstream tasks. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of *why* this metric is more effective, beyond simply stating it addresses a theoretical shortcoming of multiQVEC?
3.  Given the computational cost of training embeddings for 59 languages, could you provide a more detailed breakdown of the computational resources required and the training time for each method?
