- Paper introduces multiCluster and multiCCA for multilingual embeddings without parallel data, plus multiQVEC-CCA evaluation, validated on 59 languages.
- Contribution: two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for shared-space multilingual word embeddings.
- Contribution: new evaluation metric multiQVEC-CCA that aims to fix QVEC’s limitations and better correlate with downstream tasks.
- Contribution: a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings with multiple metrics to support reproducible experiments.
- Strength: multiCCA consistently outperforms alternatives across most evaluation metrics.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows improved correlation with downstream tasks, offering a more reliable intrinsic metric.
- Strength: extensive experiments across 59 languages and varied intrinsic/extrinsic metrics demonstrate robustness.
- Weakness: paper lacks detailed analysis of the multiCluster method, limiting understanding and adoption.
- Weakness: methods depend on dictionary quality, which may degrade performance in practice.
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other existing multilingual embedding methods, leaving relative strengths unclear.
- Question: please provide more implementation details and limitations of the multiCluster method.
- Question: how will you mitigate or address dependence on dictionary quality?
- Question: can you include a more comprehensive comparison to other existing multilingual embedding approaches?
