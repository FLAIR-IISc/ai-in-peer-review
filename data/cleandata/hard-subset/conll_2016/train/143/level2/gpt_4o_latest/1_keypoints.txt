- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, that do not require parallel corpora.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a new intrinsic evaluation metric that better correlates with downstream multilingual tasks.  
- Releases a public web portal for standardized intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation and reproducibility.  
- Demonstrates scalability by producing embeddings for 59 languages, including low-resource ones.  
- multiCluster and multiCCA are novel and applicable to low-resource settings where parallel corpora are scarce.  
- multiQVEC-CCA addresses limitations of prior intrinsic metrics and shows improved correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation across multiple intrinsic and extrinsic metrics with clear comparative results.  
- The evaluation portal enhances transparency and enables community benchmarking.  
- multiCCA generally outperforms multiCluster and some baselines but underperforms on some intrinsic metrics (e.g., word translation) versus translation-invariance methods.  
- Reliance on bilingual dictionaries may limit applicability in truly low-resource languages lacking reliable dictionaries.  
- Intrinsic experiments focus mainly on English, Italian, and Danish, raising questions about generalizability across other languages.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis and qualitative investigation of failure cases.  
- Requests clarity on performance with sparse/noisy dictionaries and potential adaptation to noisy resources.  
- Asks whether multiQVEC-CCA can be extended to include syntactic properties where supersense annotations are missing.  
- Seeks information on the computational efficiency of multiCluster and multiCCA relative to parallel-corpus methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address dictionary reliance, generalizability, and error-analysis concerns.
