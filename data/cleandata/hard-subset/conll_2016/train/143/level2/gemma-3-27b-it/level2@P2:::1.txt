This paper introduces methods for estimating and evaluating multilingual word embeddings. The authors propose two dictionary-based embedding estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA), a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings.

The primary contribution of this work is the multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric and the demonstration that it correlates better with downstream tasks compared to previously used intrinsic metrics. The detailed analysis correlating intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation metrics (Table 1) is a valuable contribution. The development and release of the evaluation portal is a useful secondary contribution, facilitating further research in the area. While the proposed estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) are relatively straightforward extensions of existing techniques, the comprehensive experimental evaluation across a large number of languages is a notable undertaking.

**Strengths:**

1. **Improved Evaluation Metric:** The multiQVEC-CCA metric addresses theoretical shortcomings of QVEC and demonstrates improved correlation with downstream tasks (document classification and dependency parsing). This is a significant step forward in developing more reliable intrinsic evaluation methods for multilingual embeddings. The analysis in Table 1 convincingly demonstrates the superiority of multiQVEC-CCA over existing methods.
2. **Comprehensive Experimental Setup:** The authors evaluate their methods across a large number of languages (59 for estimation, 12 for comparison with baselines) and multiple evaluation metrics, providing a thorough assessment of their performance.  The consideration of both intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations is commendable.
3. **Useful Resource – Evaluation Portal:** The development and release of a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings is a valuable contribution to the community, lowering the barrier to entry for researchers in this area. Providing the data and benchmarks along with the portal further enhances its utility.
4. **Clear Presentation and Motivation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation behind the work, the proposed methods, and the experimental setup. The connection to existing literature is also well-established.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Estimation Methods:** The proposed estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) are relatively straightforward extensions of existing techniques. While the experimental evaluation is thorough, the core algorithmic contributions are incremental.  The paper does not convincingly demonstrate significant advantages of these methods over existing approaches, especially considering the complexity of the evaluation setup.
2. **Reliance on Google Translate Dictionaries:** The use of Google Translate to create dictionaries for a substantial number of languages raises concerns about the quality and potential biases of these dictionaries. While acknowledged, the impact of this on the results is not sufficiently investigated. The inconsistency in dictionary sourcing (Europarl vs. Google Translate) might also introduce confounding factors.
3. **Coverage Issues and Vocabulary Discrepancies:** The authors acknowledge and attempt to address coverage issues when comparing methods, but the problem of varying vocabularies across embeddings remains a significant concern, particularly for intrinsic evaluations. Restricting the evaluation to the intersection of vocabularies introduces its own biases, as highlighted in the paper. A more robust solution to address this issue is needed.
4. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** While the paper presents a comprehensive set of experimental results, it lacks in-depth analysis of error cases. Understanding *why* certain methods perform better or worse in specific scenarios would provide valuable insights and guide future research.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Regarding the Google Translate dictionaries, what measures were taken to assess and mitigate potential biases or inaccuracies introduced by this data source? Did you explore alternative dictionary creation methods?
2.  Could you elaborate on the specific characteristics of the languages where multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster? Are there any linguistic properties that might explain this difference in performance?
3.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of hyperparameters (e.g., embedding dimension, context window size, threshold τ)? Did you perform a systematic hyperparameter search?
4.  The paper mentions that QVEC and QVEC-CCA rely on supersense annotations. Could you discuss the limitations of using English supersense tags for evaluating embeddings in other languages? Are there plans to incorporate supersense tags for additional languages?



**Additional Comments:**

*  The references section appears to be incomplete (e.g. missing authors for some publications). Please verify and complete the references.
*  The supplementary material referenced in the paper is crucial for understanding the full scope of the experiments. Ensure this material is readily available and clearly documented.
* A more detailed discussion of the computational resources required to train and evaluate the embeddings would be helpful for reproducibility.
