- Presents methods for estimating and evaluating word embeddings across 59 languages using dictionaries and monolingual data.
- Proposes two estimation methods: multiCluster and multiCCA.
- Introduces a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, that better correlates with downstream tasks than existing intrinsic metrics.
- Releases a publicly available web portal and open-source code for evaluating multilingual embeddings.
- Primary contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation and introduction of multiQVEC-CCA.
- Large-scale experimental coverage (59 languages) provides robust assessment at scale.
- multiQVEC-CCA demonstrates improved correlation with downstream tasks compared to prior intrinsic metrics.
- Public resources improve reproducibility and facilitate further research.
- Experiments include rigorous correlation analysis and comparison against strong baselines.
- Limited absolute performance gains over baselines; practical impact of improvements is not always substantial.
- Dictionary creation for 47 languages relies on Google Translate, introducing potential noise and bias.
- Coverage varies across methods, creating bias from evaluating different vocabulary subsets.
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis of specific failure cases or per-language weaknesses.
- Requests: detailed error-type analysis by estimation method, including problematic language pairs or domains.
- Requests: analysis of sensitivity to threshold (τ) in dictionary extraction from parallel corpora.
- Requests: consider strategies to mitigate Google Translate bias (e.g., back-translation or human validation).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses and questions.
