- Introduces two multilingual embedding estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), a refined evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a public web portal for evaluation.  
- multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics and is supported by thorough intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations.  
- Providing tools, benchmarks, and a web portal is a valuable community resource that facilitates future work.  
- Focuses on estimating embeddings for many languages using only dictionary data, addressing a gap where parallel corpora are scarce.  
- Adapts known techniques (clustering, CCA) to the multilingual setting; contributions are useful but not radically novel.  
- Empirical validation is strong, with convincing comparisons across tasks and a detailed analysis of method strengths and weaknesses.  
- Authors acknowledge metric limitations and the impact of vocabulary coverage, strengthening the experimental claims.  
- The novelty of the estimation methods is incremental; clearer articulation of unique, non-trivial adaptations is needed.  
- Dictionary creation from Google Translate lacks sufficient justification; the fixed threshold (τ = 0.1) is arbitrary and MT biases aren’t addressed.  
- Analysis of error cases is limited; more insight is needed into when multiCCA vs. multiCluster performs better and which linguistic properties affect performance.  
- Question: What steps were taken to mitigate biases from Google Translate dictionaries and was any manual validation performed?  
- Question: How sensitive are QVEC and QVEC-CCA to the quality and consistency of supersense tag annotations across languages?  
- Question: What are the computational costs of each method as the number of languages scales?  
- Paper is well-written, well-referenced, and inclusion of code and data is a strong positive; suggest discussing future work like leveraging monolingual data or other dictionary resources.
