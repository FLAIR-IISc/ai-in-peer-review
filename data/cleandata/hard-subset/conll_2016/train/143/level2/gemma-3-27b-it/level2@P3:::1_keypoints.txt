- Paper introduces two multilingual embedding estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web portal across 59 languages.  
- Strength: Comprehensive multilingual evaluation approach and valuable released resource for the community.  
- Strength: multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA are strong contributions that correlate better with downstream tasks than prior intrinsic metrics.  
- Strength: Release of a web portal with benchmark datasets is a significant practical contribution enabling comparability.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments (intrinsic/extrinsic) with clear results and ablation studies increase credibility.  
- Strength: Analysis of correlations between intrinsic metrics and downstream performance provides useful insights.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on data sources and preprocessing for the 59 languages, limiting reproducibility and adoption.  
- Weakness: Reliance on Google Translate for many bilingual dictionaries raises quality and bias concerns that aren’t analyzed.  
- Weakness: multiCluster description lacks detail; cluster size parameter (1000/10000) impact and sensitivity are not fully explored.  
- Weakness: Performance gains from multiCCA/multiCluster are modest and lack analysis of specific scenarios where they excel.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to existing methods may be unfair (e.g., multiSkip’s restriction to parallel data).  
- Weakness: Heavy focus on English as the central language; performance with other central languages is not explored.  
- Weakness: Supersense tag consistency across languages is acknowledged but not robustly addressed or mitigated.  
- Question: What is the computational cost and scalability of each embedding method as language count increases?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters like embedding dimension and learning rate?  
- Question: How does coverage (reported percentages) affect evaluation outcomes in detail?  
- Question: What steps ensured quality and consistency of bilingual dictionaries from different sources?  
- Question: How robust are the methods to noise or errors in bilingual dictionaries?  
- Additional: Supplementary material should include detailed data sources and preprocessing for all 59 languages.  
- Additional: Provide a detailed error analysis for downstream task failures.  
- Additional: Discuss potential broader applications of the multilingual embeddings beyond presented tasks.
