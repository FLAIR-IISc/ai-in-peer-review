- Primary contributions: introduces multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric, two dictionary-based estimators (multiCluster, multiCCA), and a multilingual embedding evaluation web portal.
- multiQVEC-CCA is claimed to better correlate with downstream tasks than prior intrinsic metrics, supported by analysis in Table 1.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across many languages (59 for estimation, 12 for baseline comparison) and multiple intrinsic/extrinsic metrics.
- The evaluation portal and released data/benchmarks are useful community resources that lower research barriers.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, method descriptions, and literature connections.
- multiCluster and multiCCA are relatively straightforward extensions of existing techniques, so estimation-method novelty is limited.
- The paper does not convincingly show substantial advantages of the proposed estimators over existing approaches given the complex evaluation setup.
- Reliance on Google Translate to build dictionaries raises concerns about quality and bias, and the impact is insufficiently investigated.
- Inconsistent dictionary sources (Europarl vs. Google Translate) may introduce confounding factors in comparisons.
- Coverage and vocabulary discrepancies across embeddings remain problematic for intrinsic evaluations; intersecting vocabularies introduces bias.
- The paper lacks in-depth error analysis to explain why methods succeed or fail in specific scenarios.
- Question: What measures were taken to assess/mitigate biases or inaccuracies from Google Translate dictionaries, and were alternative dictionary methods explored?
- Question: What language characteristics explain cases where multiCCA outperforms multiCluster?
- Question: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters (embedding dimension, context window, threshold τ), and was systematic tuning performed?
- Question: What are the limitations of using English supersense annotations for evaluating other languages, and are there plans to add supersense tags for more languages?
- Additional comment: Complete and verify the references (some entries missing authors).
- Additional comment: Ensure supplementary material is readily available and clearly documented.
- Additional comment: Provide more detail on computational resources required for training and evaluation to aid reproducibility.
