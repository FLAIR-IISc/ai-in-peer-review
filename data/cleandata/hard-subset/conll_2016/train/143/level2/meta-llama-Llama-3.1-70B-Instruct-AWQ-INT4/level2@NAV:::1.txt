**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces new methods for estimating and evaluating multilingual word embeddings in a single shared space. The authors propose two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual embeddings without requiring parallel data. They also introduce a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which correlates better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics. Additionally, they develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Main Contributions**

1. Two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings: multiCluster and multiCCA.
2. A new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of previous intrinsic metrics.
3. A web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes novel methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings that do not require parallel data, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their methods using a variety of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.
3. The web portal developed by the authors provides a valuable resource for the research community, facilitating the evaluation and comparison of multilingual embeddings.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the bilingual dictionaries used for estimating multilingual embeddings are of high quality, which may not always be the case.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their methods, which could be a concern for large-scale applications.
3. The paper relies heavily on the use of supersense tag annotations for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which may not be available for all languages.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the issue of low-quality bilingual dictionaries, which could impact the performance of their methods?
2. Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their methods, including any potential optimizations?
3. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to languages that do not have supersense tag annotations available?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of multilingual word embeddings, proposing novel methods for estimation and evaluation that do not require parallel data. The web portal developed by the authors is a valuable resource for the research community, and the thorough evaluation of their methods provides a strong foundation for future research. However, the authors should address the potential limitations of their approach, including the quality of bilingual dictionaries and the computational complexity of their methods.
