**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes two novel methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings in a shared vector space without requiring parallel data. The authors also introduce a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, which extends the QVEC metric to multilingual settings. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods and evaluation metric through experiments on 59 languages, showing that they outperform existing methods in various evaluation tasks.

**Main Contributions**

1. **MultiCluster and multiCCA methods**: The paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data. These methods are shown to be effective in various evaluation tasks.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric**: The paper introduces a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, which extends the QVEC metric to multilingual settings. This metric is shown to correlate better with downstream tasks than existing intrinsic metrics.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The paper presents a web portal for evaluating multilingual word embeddings, which provides a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics for evaluating the quality of word embeddings.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings**: The proposed methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, are shown to be effective in various evaluation tasks, outperforming existing methods.
2. **Improved evaluation metric**: The proposed evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, is shown to correlate better with downstream tasks than existing intrinsic metrics.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The web portal provides a convenient and standardized way for researchers to evaluate their multilingual word embeddings.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation on downstream tasks**: The paper only evaluates the proposed methods and evaluation metric on two downstream tasks, document classification and dependency parsing. More evaluation on other tasks would be beneficial.
2. **Dependence on bilingual dictionaries**: The proposed methods rely on bilingual dictionaries, which may not be available for all language pairs.
3. **Computational complexity**: The proposed methods may be computationally expensive, especially for large-scale datasets.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the proposed methods perform on other downstream tasks, such as machine translation and question answering?
2. Can the proposed methods be extended to work with other types of linguistic resources, such as monolingual corpora and multilingual dictionaries?
3. How can the computational complexity of the proposed methods be reduced, especially for large-scale datasets?
