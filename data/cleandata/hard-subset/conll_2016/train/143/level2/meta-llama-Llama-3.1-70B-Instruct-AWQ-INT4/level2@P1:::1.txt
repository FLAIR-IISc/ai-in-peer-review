This paper introduces two novel methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings using monolingual data and bilingual dictionaries. The authors also propose a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which extends QVEC to multilingual settings. Additionally, they develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings. The paper's main contributions are:

1. **MultiCluster and multiCCA methods**: The authors propose two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings. These methods are novel and have the potential to improve the performance of multilingual NLP tasks.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA evaluation method**: The authors extend QVEC to multilingual settings, addressing its limitations and providing a more robust evaluation metric. This method can be useful for evaluating multilingual word embeddings.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors develop a web portal that allows researchers to evaluate their multilingual embeddings using a suite of evaluation tasks. This portal can facilitate research in multilingual NLP.

Strengths:

1. **Novel methods**: The authors propose two novel methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which can potentially improve the performance of multilingual NLP tasks.
2. **Robust evaluation metric**: The authors extend QVEC to multilingual settings, providing a more robust evaluation metric for multilingual word embeddings.
3. **Web portal**: The authors develop a web portal that can facilitate research in multilingual NLP by providing a suite of evaluation tasks.

Weaknesses:

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their methods on a limited set of languages and tasks. More extensive evaluation on diverse languages and tasks is needed to demonstrate the effectiveness of their methods.
2. **Comparison to baselines**: The authors do not provide a comprehensive comparison of their methods to existing baselines. This makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of their methods.
3. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The authors do not provide a theoretical analysis of their methods, which can make it difficult to understand why their methods work and how they can be improved.

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide more details on the construction of the bilingual dictionaries used in your methods?
2. How do you plan to extend your methods to more languages and tasks?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive comparison of your methods to existing baselines?
4. Can you provide a theoretical analysis of your methods to help understand why they work and how they can be improved?
