- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA) and an improved supersense similarity measure QVEC-CCA with multilingual evaluation.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation across tasks via a custom web portal, showing competitive performance in specific settings.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, meticulously documented, and addresses timely research challenges.  
- Practical contributions (QVEC-CCA and the evaluation portal) are valuable to the community.  
- The work favors breadth over depth; key results lack sufficient analysis explaining why methods perform differently.  
- Conflicting performance of MultiCluster between 59- and 12-language setups (Section 4.2) needs deeper investigation.  
- Sensitivity of MultiCluster/MultiCCA to parameter choices and estimation decisions (dictionary quality, CCA dimensionality) is underdiscussed.  
- Without deeper analysis, the paper’s methodological novelty and theoretical insight are limited.  
- Recommendation: add monolingual QVEC vs. QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 to substantiate improvement claims.  
- Recommendation: rephrase dataset description (Line 304) to indicate aligned language pairs from a unified benchmark.  
- Recommendation: resolve MultiSkip baseline citation discrepancies and clarify relationships among cited works (e.g., Luong et al. 2015 vs. Guo et al. 2014).  
- Recommendation: expand Related Work to include listed papers on multilingual embeddings, sense-specific embeddings, and translational context (Chandar et al., Hill et al., Faruqui & Dyer, Suster et al., Guo et al., Diab & Resnik).  
- Minor fixes: adjust equations that exceed column margins and clarify why 59-language vs. 12-language setups were chosen for MultiCluster.  
- Overall: strong practical/implementation contribution that needs substantially expanded analysis of inconsistent results and parameter sensitivity to become a significant scientific advancement.
