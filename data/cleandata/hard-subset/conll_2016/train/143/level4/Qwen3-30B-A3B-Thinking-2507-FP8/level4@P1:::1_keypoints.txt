1. multiQVEC-CCA shows much stronger correlation with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics (e.g., 0.896 with document classification).  
2. The web portal provides standardized, reproducible multilingual embedding evaluation with dev/test modes.  
3. Dictionary-based methods produce embeddings for 59 languages using only monolingual data and dictionaries, avoiding parallel corpora.  
4. The multilingual extension to translation-invariance is insufficiently justified and may be a straightforward generalization of prior bilingual work.  
5. The evaluation depends on supersense annotations whose intersection across languages can be very small; the proposed consistent-tag fix is only mentioned in a footnote.  
6. Table 3 results are inconsistent: translation-invariance often outperforms the proposed methods, while multiCluster unexpectedly does best on extrinsic metrics, needing explanation.  
7. Coverage differences across methods (e.g., 70.9% vs. 69.3%) can bias comparisons; results on intersection vocabularies are needed for fairness.  
8. How does fixing embeddings in the dependency parser compare to training embeddings jointly, and what gains over random embeddings were observed?  
9. Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings in Section 5.1, or does it represent a different set of embeddings?  
10. What are the advantages of multiSkip versus first learning bilingual embeddings and then applying multiCCA to align spaces?  
11. Did the authors test with real multilingual dictionaries (rather than Google Translate/parallel corpora), and how did those results compare?
