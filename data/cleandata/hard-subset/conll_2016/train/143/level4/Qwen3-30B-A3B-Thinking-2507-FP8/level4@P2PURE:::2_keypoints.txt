- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: clustering-based MultiCluster and CCA-based MultiCCA.
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC's correlation with CCA and adds multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluates methods via an author-developed web portal across many tasks, showing improvements over two baselines in some cases.
- Paper is very well-written, timely, and carefully documented.
- Main critique: work is too broad and lacks depth in analysis.
- Needs more thorough discussion of results, e.g., conflicting MultiCluster performance between 59-language and 12-language setups.
- Should analyze the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Practical contributions (improved QVEC and web portal) are valuable, but the paper provides limited new insights on the research questions.
- Suggestion: report monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to support the improvement claim.
- Minor wording issue: “combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” misleading since datasets are of the same type.
- Formatting problem: two equations exceed the column margin on page 3.
- Citation/clarification needed for MultiSkip: Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. are cited, but Section 2.3 only references Luong et al.; clarify relationships.
- Recommend adding related-work citations on multilingual embeddings and clustering: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013).
- Recommend adding citations on multilingual training improving source-language embeddings: Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014).
- Recommend adding broader translational-context citation: Diab & Resnik (2002).
