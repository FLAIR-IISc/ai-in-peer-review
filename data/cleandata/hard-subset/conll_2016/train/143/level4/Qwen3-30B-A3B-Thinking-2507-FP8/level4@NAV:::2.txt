

This paper introduces two dictionary-based approaches for multilingual word embedding estimation: MultiCluster, inspired by clustering, and MultiCCA, derived from canonical correlation analysis. It further proposes a supersense similarity measure that enhances QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation. The evaluation employs a comprehensive set of tasks via the author-developed web portal, demonstrating that the proposed methods outperform two baselines in certain scenarios.  

The paper is exceptionally well-written, reflecting substantial effort. The representation-learning and evaluation methodologies are timely, and the meticulous documentation is commendable. However, the work prioritizes breadth over depth, resulting in insufficient analysis of key results—particularly the contradictory MultiCluster performance across 59- vs. 12-language configurations and the impact of estimation parameters. While the paper offers high practical value (e.g., improved QVEC, web portal), it lacks significant new insights into addressed research questions.  

Concrete suggestions:  
- Include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to support the claim of QVEC-CCA’s superiority (lines 326–328).  
- Minor: Line 304’s phrasing ("combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets") misrepresents these as heterogeneous; they are homogeneous across languages.  
- Page 3: Two equations exceed column margins.  
- Lines 121/147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip, but Section 2.3 references Luong et al. Clarify the relationship.  

Additional relevant works for citation:  
*Multilingual embeddings/clustering*: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013).  
*Multilingual training for source-language embeddings*: Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014).  
*Broader translational context*: Diab & Resnik (2002).
