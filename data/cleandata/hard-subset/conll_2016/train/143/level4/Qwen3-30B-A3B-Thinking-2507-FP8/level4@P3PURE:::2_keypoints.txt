- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-motivated).  
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure that improves QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation.  
- Evaluation across many tasks via the authors' web portal shows the proposed methods outperform two baselines in some cases.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, represents substantial effort, and is meticulously documented.  
- Critique: the paper favors breadth over depth, leaving key findings insufficiently explored.  
- Needs a deeper discussion of the conflicting MultiCluster results between the 59- and 12-language setups.  
- Needs analysis of the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- Practical contributions (improved QVEC measure, web portal) are valuable, but the paper provides limited novel research questions or resolutions.  
- Concrete suggestion: include monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA to support the improvement claim.  
- Minor wording: line 304 suggests heterogeneous datasets though they are the same task across languages.  
- Formatting issue: two equations exceed the column margin on page 3.  
- Clarify citation correspondence for the MultiSkip baseline (Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. vs. Luong et al.).  
- Recommend adding related-work references: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013), Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014), and Diab & Resnik (2002).
