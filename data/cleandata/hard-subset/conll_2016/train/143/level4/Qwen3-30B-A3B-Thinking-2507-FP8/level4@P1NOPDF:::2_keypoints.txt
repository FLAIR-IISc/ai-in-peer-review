- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: clustering-based MultiCluster and CCA-based MultiCCA.  
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure that improves QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and enabling multilingual evaluation.  
- Evaluates methods across many tasks via a custom web portal and shows the proposed representations outperform two baselines in some settings.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, timely, and well-documented; practical contributions (improved QVEC and web portal) are clear.  
- Breadth of the paper overshadows depth; the manuscript needs deeper analysis to be more foundational.  
- Conflicting MultiCluster performance between the 59- and 12-language setups requires further investigation and explanation.  
- Need analysis of the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- Table 1 is incomplete: include correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA for context.  
- Clarify phrasing in line 304 to indicate these are multiple monolingual datasets translated to the same language pair.  
- Resolve citation inconsistency: clarify relationship between Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. regarding the MultiSkip baseline.  
- Related work should be expanded to include multilingual clustering and multilingual training literature (e.g., Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Faruqui & Dyer 2013; Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014) and broader translational context (Diab & Resnik 2002).  
- Minor technical fix: adjust equations that exceed the column margin.
