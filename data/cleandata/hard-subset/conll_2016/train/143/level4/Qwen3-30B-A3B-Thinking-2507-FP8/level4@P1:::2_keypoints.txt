- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that need only monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries, no parallel data.  
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation metric that fixes QVEC’s theoretical issues and better correlates with downstream tasks.  
- Releases a web portal and open-source implementations for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA addresses invariance and normalization problems of QVEC and shows stronger correlation with document classification and dependency parsing.  
- Strength: Methods are practical and accessible, relying on widely available resources rather than costly parallel data.  
- Strength: Web portal provides standardized metrics, dev/test sets, and an upload platform to boost reproducibility and research.  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis explaining why multiCluster performs poorly with 59 languages but is competitive with 12 languages.  
- Weakness: Does not explore sensitivity to parameter choices (e.g., cluster size, dictionary extraction threshold τ), leaving robustness unclear.  
- Weakness: Results show inconsistent rankings across evaluation metrics with no clear guidance on which method is generally preferable.  
- Question: What specifically causes multiCluster’s degraded performance with 59 languages versus 12 languages?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to τ and cluster size, and would parameter tuning improve performance?  
- Question: How would multiQVEC-CCA work for languages lacking supersense annotations, and what alternative resources or approaches could be used?
