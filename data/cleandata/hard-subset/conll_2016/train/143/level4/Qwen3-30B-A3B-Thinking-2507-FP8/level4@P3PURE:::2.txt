

This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings: MultiCluster, motivated by clustering, and MultiCCA, motivated by canonical correlation analysis. Additionally, it introduces a supersense similarity measure that improves upon QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation. The evaluation, conducted across a wide range of tasks using the authors' web portal, demonstrates that the proposed methods outperform two baselines in certain cases.  

The paper is exceptionally well-written and represents substantial effort. The presented representation-learning and evaluation approaches are timely, and the meticulous documentation is commendable. However, the paper prioritizes breadth over depth, resulting in insufficient exploration of key findings. Specifically, a more thorough discussion of the conflicting MultiCluster results between the 59- and 12-language setups, as well as the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster/CCA, is needed. While the paper offers high practical value (e.g., improved QVEC measure, web portal), I have not learned much regarding novel research questions or their resolution.  

Below are concrete suggestions:  
- Include correlation results (Table 1) for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA to support the claim (lines 326–328) that QVEC-CCA improves upon QVEC.  
- Minor: Line 304 ("a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets") implies datasets of different types, whereas they are homogeneous (same task, different languages).  
- Page 3: Two equations exceed the column margin.  
- Lines 121 and 147 reference Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 cites Luong et al. Clarify the correspondence between these works.  
- While the related work is reasonably covered, these additional references would strengthen the context:  
  - Multilingual embeddings and clustering: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013).  
  - Multilingual training for better source-language embeddings: Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014).  
  - Broader translational context: Diab & Resnik (2002).
