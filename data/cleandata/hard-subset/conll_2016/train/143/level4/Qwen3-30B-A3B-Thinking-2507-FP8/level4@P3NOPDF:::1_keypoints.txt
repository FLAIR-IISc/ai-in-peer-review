- Novelty gap: Translation Invariance seems closely related to Gardner et al.; paper must explicitly justify technical novelty beyond multilingual scaling.  
- Super-sense coverage sparsity: Reliance on cross-lingual super-sense features (QVEC) risks severe sparsity—authors must explain mitigation, especially for low-resource languages.  
- Coverage-performance disconnect: multiCluster and multiCCA show large coverage differences but similar downstream scores; authors must analyze how coverage affects results.  
- Inconsistent empirical findings: Translation Invariance tops QVEC (intra-language) while multiCluster outperforms on extrinsic tasks, contradicting the paper’s goal of preserving cross-lingual structure.  
- Question on parser training: How much does fixing vs. fine-tuning embeddings affect dependency parsing, and what gain do these embeddings give over random initialization?  
- Table 1 clarity: Is Table 1 averaged across the 17 embeddings from Section 5.1? If so, state this explicitly.  
- multiSkip justification: What does multiSkip offer over a simpler two-step approach (learn bilingual embeddings, then multiCCA projection)?  
- Dictionary realism: Dictionaries were extracted from parallel data/Google Translate—did authors test with real multilingual lexicons (e.g., BabelNet), and how would dictionary realism affect applicability?  
- Overall assessment: Well-written and technically sound, but claims need stronger justification—Translation Invariance novelty, coverage limits, and inconsistent results must be resolved for the contribution to be convincing.
