- Paper: Proposes four methods for multilingual word embeddings and a modified QVEC metric for evaluation.  
- multiCluster: Maps words to multilingual clusters via a dictionary; uses cluster embeddings as word representations.  
- multiCCA: Anchors embeddings in English by projecting other-language monolingual embeddings via bilingual dictionaries (extends Faruqui & Dyer 2014).  
- multiSkip: Extends Luong et al. (2015b) by expanding the skip objective to use parallel corpora across all languages.  
- Translation invariance: Uses low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment constraints; multilingual extension appears limited relative to bilingual form.  
- Evaluation: Uses CCA to align embeddings with linguistic annotations (e.g., super-senses) and derives rotation-invariant scores, generalized to multilingual settings.  
- Writing: Paper is well-written and methodologies are clearly described.  
- Concern 1 (novelty): Need clarification on how the translation-invariance method is genuinely extended to multilingual settings versus prior bilingual work.  
- Concern 2 (super-sense coverage): Cross-lingual super-sense annotation sparsity may cause severe feature sparsity—authors should explain handling beyond footnote 9 (e.g., smoothing or language-specific subsets).  
- Concern 3 (coverage effect on Table 2): Large coverage differences between multiCluster and multiCCA may drive comparable dependency-parsing scores; request sensitivity analysis on coverage.  
- Concern 4 (inconsistent Table 3 results): Intrinsic metrics do not clearly favor multilingual embeddings; translation invariance surprisingly dominates word translation, and multiCluster (which ignores inter-cluster semantics) yields the best extrinsic results—these inconsistencies need explanation.  
- Q1: How much performance loss occurs when fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and how much do these embeddings improve the LSTM stack parser versus random initialization?  
- Q2: Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Q3: What are the advantages of multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings first and then applying multiCCA projections?  
- Q4: Were embeddings evaluated using real multilingual dictionaries (e.g., BabelNet) rather than dictionaries extracted from parallel corpora or Google Translate?
