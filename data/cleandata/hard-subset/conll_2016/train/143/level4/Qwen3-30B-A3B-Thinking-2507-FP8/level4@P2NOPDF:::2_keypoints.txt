- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-based) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Proposes QVEC-CCA, an improved supersense similarity metric replacing QVEC’s correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.
- Provides a custom web portal and evaluates methods across diverse tasks, showing gains over two baselines in some scenarios.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-documented, and reflects substantial effort.
- The refined QVEC-CCA metric and the evaluation portal are timely and offer clear practical value to the community.
- Work emphasizes breadth over deep analytical exploration, limiting theoretical insights.
- Contradictory results for MultiCluster between 59-language and 12-language setups need explicit analysis.
- Impact of parameter choices and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and MultiCCA should be examined in greater depth.
- Limited new insights into core research questions about multilingual representation learning despite useful tools and data.
- Suggestion: include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 to substantiate performance claims.
- Suggestion: clarify wording about dataset combinations to indicate they are all cross-lingual similarity datasets differing by language pairs.
- Suggestion: fix equation formatting on page 3 to fit column margins for readability.
- Suggestion: resolve citation inconsistencies around MultiSkip (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Luong et al.) to clarify relationships.
- Suggestion: expand related work to include clustering-based multilingual embeddings (Chandar et al., Hill et al., Faruqui & Dyer), multilingual training works (Suster et al., Guo et al.), and translational context (Diab & Resnik).
- Overall assessment: valuable practical contributions and strong execution, but needs deeper analysis of results and methodological sensitivities; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
