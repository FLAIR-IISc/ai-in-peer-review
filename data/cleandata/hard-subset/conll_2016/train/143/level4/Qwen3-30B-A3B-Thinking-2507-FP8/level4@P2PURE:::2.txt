

This paper introduces two dictionary-based approaches for estimating multilingual word embeddings: one clustering-based (MultiCluster) and one based on canonical correlation analysis (MultiCCA). Additionally, it proposes a supersense similarity measure that enhances QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation. The evaluation employs a web portal developed by the authors across a broad range of tasks, demonstrating that the proposed methods outperform two baselines in certain cases.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and represents substantial effort. The presented representation-learning and evaluation methods are timely, and I commend the authors for their meticulous documentation. However, I believe the work expands too broadly at the expense of depth. A more thorough discussion of results is needed—for instance, addressing the conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between the 59-language and 12-language setups, and analyzing the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster/CCA. While the paper offers high practical value (improved QVEC measure, web portal), I have not gained substantial new insights regarding the research questions addressed and resolved.

Concrete suggestions follow:
- Include correlation results (Table 1) for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA to support the claim in lines 326–328 that QVEC-CCA improves upon QVEC.
- Minor: Line 304 states "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets." This implies datasets of different types, whereas they are identical in nature (just different languages).
- Page 3: Two equations exceed the column margin.
- Lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip, but Section 2.3 references only Luong et al. Clarify the relationship between these works.
- While related work is reasonably covered, these additional citations would strengthen the context:
  - Multilingual embeddings and clustering: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013).
  - Multilingual training for improved source-language embeddings: Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014).
  - Broader translational context: Diab & Resnik (2002).
