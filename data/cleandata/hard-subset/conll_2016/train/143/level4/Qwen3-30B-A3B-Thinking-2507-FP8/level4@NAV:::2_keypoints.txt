- Paper introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-inspired) and MultiCCA (CCA-derived).  
- Proposes supersense similarity measure that improves QVEC by replacing correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.  
- Evaluation uses a comprehensive task set via the authors’ web portal and shows improvements over two baselines in some settings.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and thoroughly documented.  
- Methods and evaluation are timely and of high practical value (improved QVEC, web portal).  
- Work favors breadth over depth, lacking deep analysis of key results.  
- Insufficient analysis of contradictory MultiCluster performance between 59- and 12-language settings.  
- Missing investigation of the impact of estimation/training parameters on results.  
- Paper offers limited new theoretical insights into the addressed research questions.  
- Suggestion: report monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to support claims of QVEC-CCA’s superiority.  
- Minor wording fix: line 304’s phrase mischaracterizes cross-lingual similarity datasets as heterogeneous.  
- Formatting fix: two equations on page 3 exceed column margins.  
- Clarify citation/relationship for MultiSkip: Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. are inconsistently referenced.  
- Suggested additional citations for related work in multilingual embeddings and translational context (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013; Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014; Diab & Resnik 2002).
