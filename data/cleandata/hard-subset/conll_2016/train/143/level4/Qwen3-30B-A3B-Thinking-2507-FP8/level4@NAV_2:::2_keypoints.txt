- Summary: Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) needing only monolingual data plus pairwise dictionaries, proposes multiQVEC-CCA which better correlates with downstream tasks, and provides an evaluation web portal.

- Contribution 1: multiQVEC-CCA evaluation method addressing theoretical shortcomings of prior metrics and showing stronger correlation with downstream tasks.

- Contribution 2: A web portal providing a standardized framework and datasets for evaluating multilingual embeddings with intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

- Contribution 3: multiCluster and multiCCA estimation methods enabling training embeddings across 59 languages without parallel corpora.

- Strength 1: multiQVEC-CCA shows strong empirical correlation with downstream tasks (0.896 for document classification, 0.273 for parsing), filling a key evaluation gap.

- Strength 2: The web portal is a practical, reproducibility-boosting resource that lowers barriers for multilingual embedding research.

- Strength 3: Thorough, large-scale evaluation across 59 languages demonstrating scalability and comparative performance of multiple methods.

- Weakness 1: Lack of explanation for why multiCluster performs well in the 12-language setting but degrades substantially with 59 languages.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient discussion of trade-offs between estimation methods, particularly why multiCCA outperforms multiCluster as language count increases.

- Weakness 3: Potentially unfair comparison to multiSkip because the authors’ implementation may not use monolingual data as in the original multiSkip, which could disadvantage that baseline.

- Question 1: Please explain in detail why multiCluster’s performance differs between the 12-language and 59-language settings and what causes the cluster approach to degrade.

- Question 2: Please elaborate on what properties of the multilingual embedding space make CCA-based methods more robust than clustering-based methods in the 59-language case.

- Question 3: How will the evaluation portal be extended or limited in the future to support more advanced evaluation scenarios and address potential shortcomings?
