- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (dictionary-driven word→cluster embeddings), multiCCA (projects languages into English anchor via bilingual dictionaries), multiSkip (extends bilingual context objective to all parallel corpora), and Translation Invariance (low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment; possibly limited to bilingual settings).  
- Proposes a modified QVEC evaluation using CCA to derive rotation-invariant aligned basis vectors and extends this metric to multilingual evaluation.  
- Paper is well-structured and method descriptions are clear.  
- Major concern 1: Novelty unclear relative to Gardner et al.’s translation invariance—if multilingual extension is the contribution, the paper needs a concise clarification.  
- Major concern 2: Super-sense annotation sparsity across languages may produce extremely sparse feature intersections; the handling beyond footnote 9 is not explained.  
- Major concern 3: Coverage rates strongly affect results (Table 2); unclear why multiCluster and multiCCA have similar dependency-parsing scores despite markedly different coverage.  
- Results are inconsistent: multilingual methods often underperform on intrinsic metrics despite the goal of improving word translation; translation invariance (not designed for this task) achieves best intrinsic results.  
- Extrinsic results: multiCluster, which discards inter-cluster semantic info, performs best on downstream tasks—this surprising outcome needs explanation.  
- Question: What is the performance degradation from fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and what incremental gain do these embeddings provide over random embeddings in the LSTM parser?  
- Question: Is Table 1 an average across the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Question: What advantage does multiSkip provide over the simpler pipeline of learning bilingual embeddings then projecting them with multiCCA?  
- Question: Dictionary extraction via parallel alignments or Google Translate may not reflect real-world lexicons—did the authors evaluate using standard multilingual dictionaries?
