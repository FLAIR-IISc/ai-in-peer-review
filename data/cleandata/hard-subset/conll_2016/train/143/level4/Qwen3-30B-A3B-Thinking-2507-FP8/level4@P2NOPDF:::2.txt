

This paper introduces two dictionary-based approaches for estimating multilingual word embeddings: MultiCluster, grounded in clustering, and MultiCCA, derived from canonical correlation analysis. It further proposes an enhanced supersense similarity measure, QVEC-CCA, which improves upon QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation. The authors evaluate their methods across diverse tasks using a custom web portal, demonstrating performance gains over two baselines in specific scenarios.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, reflecting substantial effort and meticulous documentation. The methods presented—particularly the refined QVEC-CCA metric and the evaluation portal—are timely and offer clear practical value to the research community. However, the work leans heavily toward breadth at the expense of deeper analytical exploration. A more thorough discussion of key results would significantly strengthen the contribution. For instance, the contradictory outcomes for MultiCluster across the 59-language and 12-language setups warrant explicit analysis. Similarly, the impact of parameter choices and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and MultiCCA on performance should be examined in greater depth. While the paper delivers useful tools and data, it leaves the reader with limited new insights into core research questions about multilingual representation learning.

**Specific Suggestions:**
1. **Table 1 Enhancement:** Include correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA to substantiate the claim (lines 326–328) that QVEC-CCA outperforms QVEC.
2. **Terminology Precision:** On line 304, revise "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" to clarify that these datasets share the same nature (cross-lingual similarity) but differ in language pairs.
3. **Equation Formatting:** Equations on page 3 exceed column margins; adjust formatting for readability.
4. **Citation Consistency:** Lines 121 and 147 reference Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip, but Section 2.3 cites Luong et al. Clarify the relationship between these works to avoid confusion.
5. **Related Work Expansion:** The discussion of related work could be strengthened by incorporating additional relevant studies:
   - *Multilingual embeddings via clustering:* Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Faruqui & Dyer (2013).
   - *Multilingual training for improved source embeddings:* Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014).
   - *Broader translational context:* Diab & Resnik (2002) on unsupervised sense tagging.

**Overall Assessment:** The paper makes a valuable contribution through its practical innovations (QVEC-CCA, evaluation portal) and robust empirical validation. The writing and execution are strong, and the work aligns well with current research trends. However, the limited depth in analyzing the core findings—particularly regarding methodological sensitivities and contradictory results—reduces its theoretical impact. Addressing these gaps would elevate the paper from a high-quality technical contribution to a more foundational advance in multilingual representation learning. I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to strengthen the analysis and citations.
