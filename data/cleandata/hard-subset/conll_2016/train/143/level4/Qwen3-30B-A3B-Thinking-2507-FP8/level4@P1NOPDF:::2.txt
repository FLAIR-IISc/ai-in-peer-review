

This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings: one clustering-based (MultiCluster) and another grounded in canonical correlation analysis (MultiCCA). It also introduces a supersense similarity measure that improves QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation. The authors evaluate these methods across a broad range of tasks using their custom web portal, demonstrating that the proposed representations outperform two baselines in certain settings.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and represents substantial, meticulous work. The methods are timely, and the authors' documentation is commendable. However, the paper’s breadth somewhat overshadows its depth. A more thorough analysis of results would strengthen the contribution—particularly regarding the conflicting MultiCluster performance between the 59- and 12-language setups, and the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster/CCA. While the practical value is clear (e.g., the improved QVEC measure and web portal), I gained limited novel insights into the core research questions addressed.

Below are specific suggestions:

1.  **Table 1 completeness**: Include correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA. As stated in lines 326–328, QVEC-CCA is an improvement over QVEC, so these results are essential for context.
2.  **Line 304 phrasing**: "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" implies heterogeneous datasets. Clarify that these are multiple *monolingual* datasets translated to the same language pair (e.g., "multiple cross-lingual word similarity datasets from the same language pair").
3.  **Citation consistency**: Lines 121 and 147 reference Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 cites Luong et al. Please clarify the relationship between these works.
4.  **Additional relevant literature**: The related work section could be expanded to include:
    *   *Multilingual embeddings via clustering*: Chandar et al. (2014, NIPS), Hill et al. (2014, arXiv), Faruqui & Dyer (2013, ACL).
    *   *Multilingual training for improved source embeddings*: Suster et al. (2016, NAACL-HLT), Guo et al. (2014, COLING).
    *   *Broader translational context*: Diab & Resnik (2002, ACL).

Minor technical notes:
*   Page 3: Adjust equations exceeding the column margin.
*   The paper’s core contribution—MultiCCA and the supersense measure—remains strong, but deeper analysis of *why* results vary across language sets would elevate the work from practical to foundational. The current evaluation, while comprehensive, lacks the interpretive depth needed to fully justify the methodological choices.
