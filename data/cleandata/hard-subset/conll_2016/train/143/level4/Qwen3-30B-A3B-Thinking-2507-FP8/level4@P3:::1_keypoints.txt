- Introduces methods for estimating and evaluating multilingual word embeddings across 59 languages in a shared vector space.  
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, a novel intrinsic evaluation metric that correlates better with downstream tasks.  
- Provides a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings to encourage reproducible research.  
- Presents two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) that require only monolingual data and dictionaries.  
- multiQVEC-CCA achieves high correlation with document classification (0.896), outperforming existing metrics (e.g., 0.386).  
- The evaluation portal supports nine metrics with dev/test modes and an open-source code/data release, lowering barriers for future work.  
- Extends bilingual embedding techniques to multilingual via multiCCA; multiCluster remains competitive in some settings, with analysis explaining its semantic-ambiguity limitations.  
- Unclear novelty and justification over Gardner et al. (2015) and how translation-invariance is applied to 59 languages.  
- Inconsistent results: translation-invariance outperforms proposed methods on word translation while multiCCA outperforms on downstream tasks, undermining key claims.  
- Coverage differences across methods are not systematically controlled and may confound performance comparisons.  
- Supersense annotations are inconsistent across languages and no scalable solution is proposed, limiting the evaluation’s generalizability.  
- Limited testing with real-world curated dictionaries (e.g., BabelNet), reducing practical relevance of the dictionary construction approach.  
- How does multiCCA’s extension of translation-invariance differ from Gardner et al. (2015), and why is it applicable to 59 languages?  
- Why does translation-invariance dominate word translation while multiCCA wins downstream tasks—does this indicate misalignment of the word-translation metric with practical utility?  
- How would results change if evaluation were restricted to the vocabulary intersection across all methods to eliminate coverage bias?  
- Can authors provide examples where multiCluster’s cluster-level embeddings improve downstream performance despite claimed semantic ambiguity?
