- Introduces multiCluster and multiCCA, dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual embeddings across 59 languages without parallel data.  
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation metric that fixes QVEC’s basis dependence and unnormalized scoring and better correlates with downstream tasks.  
- Provides a web portal with standardized benchmarks, nine metrics, dev/test modes, and open-source implementations to improve reproducibility.  
- multiQVEC-CCA substantially improves evaluation reliability and shows stronger correlation with text categorization and parsing (Table 1).  
- The evaluation portal and shared datasets lower barriers for future multilingual embedding research.  
- Comprehensive empirical validation across 59 languages shows multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster at large scale.  
- The paper lacks analysis explaining why multiCluster performs well in 12 languages but poorly in the 59-language setting (possible causes: language diversity, dictionary quality, clustering limits).  
- No parameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., τ = 0.1, cluster size thresholds), limiting understanding of robustness and practical applicability.  
- multiQVEC-CCA evaluation is limited to English, Italian, and Danish; broader, more typologically diverse languages are needed to establish general validity.  
- Question: What specific factors explain multiCluster’s divergent performance between 12 and 59 languages and how can they be mitigated?  
- Question: How does the quality of Google Translate–constructed dictionaries affect method performance and what error margins are expected?  
- Question: How does multiCluster exacerbate semantic ambiguity in the 59-language setting and can the method be modified to address this?  
- Additional: Clarify the relation between MultiSkip and works by Coulmance et al. (2015) and Guo et al. (2016), and correct the claim that cross-lingual word similarity “datasets” are distinct rather than variations of the same dataset.
