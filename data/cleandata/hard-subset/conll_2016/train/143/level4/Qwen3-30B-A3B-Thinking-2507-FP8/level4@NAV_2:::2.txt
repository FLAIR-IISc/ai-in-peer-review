

Summary: This paper introduces two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings (multiCluster and multiCCA) that require only monolingual data and pairwise parallel dictionaries, and proposes a new evaluation method (multiQVEC-CCA) that demonstrates better correlation with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics. The authors also develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which will significantly facilitate future research in this area.

Main contributions (as assessed by the reviewer):
1. The multiQVEC-CCA evaluation method, which addresses theoretical shortcomings of previous metrics and demonstrates superior correlation with downstream tasks (document classification and dependency parsing).
2. The web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which provides a standardized framework for researchers to test their methods using multiple intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.
3. The multiCluster and multiCCA estimation methods, which enable training embeddings across 59 languages without requiring parallel data.

Strengths:
1. The multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric is a significant contribution that directly addresses a critical gap in the field. The paper provides strong empirical evidence showing that multiQVEC-CCA correlates more strongly with downstream task performance (0.896 with document classification and 0.273 with parsing) compared to previous intrinsic metrics, which is a major advancement for the field.
2. The web portal is a highly valuable practical contribution that will substantially lower the barrier for future research in multilingual embeddings. It provides standardized datasets, evaluation tools, and a framework for replicable results, which is particularly important given the challenges in evaluating multilingual embeddings.
3. The paper thoroughly evaluates multiple estimation methods across a wide range of languages and tasks, providing comprehensive results that demonstrate the relative strengths and weaknesses of each approach. The 59-language evaluation is particularly impressive and demonstrates the scalability of the proposed methods.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper does not sufficiently explain the conflicting performance patterns of multiCluster across the 12-language and 59-language settings. The authors note that multiCluster performs well with 12 languages but significantly worse with 59 languages, yet fail to provide a detailed analysis of why this discrepancy occurs, which is a critical gap in understanding the method's limitations.
2. The paper lacks sufficient discussion of the trade-offs between the estimation methods. For example, the authors report that multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster across metrics in the 59-language setting, but do not thoroughly analyze why the cluster-based approach (multiCluster) struggles with larger language sets while maintaining competitive performance with fewer languages.
3. The comparison with multiSkip appears potentially misleading, as the authors' implementation does not leverage monolingual data (unlike the original multiSkip method described in Luong et al., 2015b), which may artificially disadvantage this baseline method. The paper should clarify this implementation detail to provide a fair comparison.

Questions to Authors:
1. Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation for the performance difference of multiCluster between the 12-language and 59-language settings, particularly regarding how the cluster-based approach becomes less effective as the number of languages increases?
2. The paper mentions that multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster across all metrics in the 59-language setting. Could the authors elaborate on the specific characteristics of the multilingual space that make the CCA-based approach more robust than the clustering-based approach?
3. The evaluation portal is a major contribution, but the paper does not discuss potential limitations or future extensions to this framework. How do the authors envision the portal evolving to support more advanced evaluation scenarios in the future?
