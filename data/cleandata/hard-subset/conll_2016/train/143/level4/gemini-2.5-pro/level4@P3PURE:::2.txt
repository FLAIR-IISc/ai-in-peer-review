This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for learning multilingual word embeddings: MultiCluster, which is based on clustering, and MultiCCA, which is based on canonical correlation analysis. The authors also introduce a supersense similarity measure that improves upon QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating a multilingual evaluation framework. The evaluation, conducted on a wide range of tasks via a web portal developed by the authors, demonstrates that the proposed representation methods outperform two baselines in several cases.

The paper is very well-written and represents a substantial amount of work. The proposed representation-learning and evaluation methods are timely, and the authors are to be applauded for their meticulous documentation.

My main concern, however, is that the paper favors breadth at the expense of depth. A more thorough discussion of the results is needed. For instance, the paper should elaborate on the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups, and analyze the effect of different estimation parameters and design choices within MultiCluster and MultiCCA. Consequently, while the paper offers high practical value to the research community through its improved QVEC-CCA measure and web portal, it provides limited insight into the underlying research questions it aims to address.

Below are some more specific remarks.

To substantiate the claim that the proposed QVEC-CCA is an improvement over QVEC (lines 326–328), the authors should include correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA in Table 1.

**Minor Issues:**

*   Line 304: The phrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" could be misleading, as it suggests datasets of different types. It would be clearer to state that these are datasets of the same kind, but for different language pairs.
*   Page 3: Two equations exceed the column margin.
*   The paper should clarify the relationship between the MultiSkip baseline and the cited works. Lines 121 and 147 mention Coulmance et al. and Guo et al., while Section 2.3 refers only to Luong et al.
