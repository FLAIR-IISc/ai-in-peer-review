- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC’s correlation component with CCA and adds a multilingual evaluation framework.
- Develops a web portal and evaluates representations across many tasks, showing the proposed methods outperform two baselines in several cases.
- Paper is well-written, substantial, and meticulously documented.
- Main concern: paper favors breadth over depth and lacks thorough discussion of results.
- Needs deeper analysis of conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between the 59- and 12-language setups.
- Should analyze the effects of different estimation parameters and design choices within MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Provides high practical value (QVEC-CCA measure and web portal) but limited insight into core research questions.
- To substantiate QVEC-CCA’s improvement, include monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA in Table 1.
- Minor: rephrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" to avoid misleading wording about dataset types.
- Minor: two equations exceed the column margin.
- Minor: clarify the relationship and citations for the MultiSkip baseline (inconsistent references to Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al.).
- Suggests adding related citations on multilingual embeddings/clustering, multilingual training effects, and translational context (lists several specific papers to include).
