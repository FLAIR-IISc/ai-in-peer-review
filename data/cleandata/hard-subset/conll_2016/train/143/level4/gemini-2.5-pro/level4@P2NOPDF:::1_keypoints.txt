- Paper presents four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and a proposed multilingual extension of Translation Invariance.  
- Proposes a multilingual QVEC intrinsic evaluation using CCA to measure correlation with manual linguistic features; score is rotation- and linear-invariant.  
- Paper is well-written and methods are clearly described.  

Major issues:
- Unclear novelty/technical contribution for the Translation Invariance method; paper does not detail how the bilingual approach is extended to multilingual.  
- Scalability concern: reliance on cross-lingual supersense annotations may not scale as languages increase and may exclude low-resource languages.  
- Inconsistent and unconvincing experimental results: bilingual translation invariance outperforms multilingual methods on intrinsic tests, and simple multiCluster often beats more complex methods on parsing, undermining motivation for the new methods.  
- Ambiguity about impact of vocabulary coverage on results (Table 2); need clarity on whether and how scores are comparable across methods with different coverage.

Points needing clarification:
- Dependency parsing: effect of freezing vs fine-tuning pre-trained embeddings, and baseline gain over random embeddings in the LSTM parser.  
- Table 1: confirm whether reported results are averages over all 17 language pairs.  
- multiSkip vs pipelined approach: justify theoretical/practical advantages of joint training over learning bilingual embeddings then mapping them (e.g., via multiCCA).  
- Dictionary realism: how would performance change using real-world, noisier, less comprehensive lexical resources versus automatically extracted dictionaries?
