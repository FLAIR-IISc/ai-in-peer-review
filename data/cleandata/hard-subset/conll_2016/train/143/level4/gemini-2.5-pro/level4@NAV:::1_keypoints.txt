- Proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (dictionary-based word clusters), multiCCA (English-anchored projection of monolingual embeddings), multiSkip (parallel-context objective across corpora), and translation-invariance (low-rank PMI decomposition guided by bilingual alignments).  
- Introduces a modified QVEC metric using CCA to produce rotation- and linear-invariant quality scores, extended to multilingual evaluation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presented.  
- Clarify novelty of the translation-invariance method relative to Gardner et al.; explicitly state if the main contribution is a multilingual extension.  
- Concern that relying on super-sense annotations across many languages yields a vanishing intersection of features; request mitigation beyond footnote 9.  
- Ask how vocabulary coverage affects reported Table 2 scores, noting similar dependency-parsing results despite different coverage for multiCluster and multiCCA.  
- Table 3 lacks a clear, consistent narrative: most proposed methods underperform on intrinsic metrics, translation-invariance dominates word translation (a central goal), and multiCluster unexpectedly excels on extrinsic tasks—explain these outcomes.  
- Request dependency-parsing details: performance drop when embeddings are fixed during training and gain from initializing the LSTM parser versus random initialization.  
- Ask whether Table 1 values are averages over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1.  
- Request justification for multiSkip over a two-stage pipeline that learns bilingual embeddings then maps them with multiCCA.  
- Question the dictionary extraction protocol: were noisier, real-world multilingual dictionaries considered instead of aligned parallel text or Google Translate-derived pairs?
