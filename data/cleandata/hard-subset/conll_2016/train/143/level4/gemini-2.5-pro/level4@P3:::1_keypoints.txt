- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) requiring only monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation metric with higher correlation to downstream tasks.  
- Releases a public web portal and embeddings for 59 languages to enable standardized, reproducible evaluation.  
- Strong analysis showing existing intrinsic metrics poorly predict downstream performance and multiQVEC-CCA correlates well (Pearson r=0.896 with document classification).  
- Impressive scale and open-science contribution by training and releasing embeddings for 59 languages and evaluation infrastructure.  
- multiCCA is simple, scalable, and effective for low-resource scenarios, outperforming multiCluster in many settings.  
- Results across methods are inconsistent and sometimes counter-intuitive (translation-invariance often outperforms proposed methods; multiCluster best on extrinsic tasks), weakening clear takeaways.  
- multiQVEC-CCA relies on parallel, manually annotated linguistic resources (supersense tags) available for very few languages, limiting scalability.  
- Differences in vocabulary coverage across methods confound comparisons, requiring intersection-based analysis to ensure fair evaluation.  
- Request to report parser performance with (a) random embeddings and (b) fine-tuned pretrained embeddings to contextualize results.  
- Asks for intuition why multiCluster’s conflation of translations yields strong extrinsic performance despite losing semantic distinctions.  
- Seeks clarification on whether the translation-invariance baseline was extended to multilingual settings or applied pairwise.  
- Questions how the methods would perform using real-world, manually curated dictionaries with different noise and coverage characteristics.
