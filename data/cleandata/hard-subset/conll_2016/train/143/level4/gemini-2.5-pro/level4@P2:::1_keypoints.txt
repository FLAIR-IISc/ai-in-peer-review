- Contribution: Proposes multiCluster and multiCCA dictionary-based methods and compares them to multiSkip and translation-invariance.
- Contribution: Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a rotation-invariant, normalized intrinsic metric that correlates better with downstream tasks.
- Contribution: Provides a public web portal and open-source code for standardized, reproducible multilingual embedding evaluation.
- Strength: Rigorous, replicable evaluation—multiQVEC-CCA and correlation analysis strongly justify the new metric and the portal aids comparability.
- Strength: Scalability and practicality—dictionary-based methods (especially multiCCA) avoid parallel corpora and scale to 59 languages.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments—four methods evaluated across nine intrinsic/extrinsic tasks, revealing task-specific method strengths.
- Weakness: Inconsistent performance—proposed methods do not dominate; translation-invariance wins several metrics and multiCluster’s strong extrinsic results are unexplained.
- Weakness: Vocabulary coverage confounds comparisons—translation-invariance has much lower coverage and intersection analysis is not in the main paper.
- Weakness: Practical limits of multiQVEC-CCA—requires manually annotated linguistic resources that are rare, limiting applicability to many languages.
- Question: Is the translation-invariance implementation a direct application of Gardner et al. (2015) or extended for multilingual use?
- Question: How much does freezing pre-trained embeddings in the parser hurt performance versus allowing fine-tuning?
- Question: How would dictionary-based methods like multiCCA degrade with noisier, lower-coverage lexical resources (e.g., Wiktionary)?
