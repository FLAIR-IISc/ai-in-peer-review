- Paper presents a comprehensive comparison of four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation Invariance) and proposes a multilingual QVEC (CCA-based) evaluation metric invariant to linear transforms.  
- Strength: well-written, clearly structured, and a broad large-scale comparative study.  
- Major: Clarify novelty of the Translation Invariance method relative to Gardner et al. (2015), explicitly state and explain any extension to the multilingual setting.  
- Major: Discuss scalability limits of super-sense-based evaluation as languages increase and the intersection of features shrinks, and propose mitigation strategies beyond the footnote.  
- Major: Analyze relationship between embedding coverage and downstream performance (e.g., differing coverage but similar parsing F1 for multiCluster vs. multiCCA) to contextualize trade-offs.  
- Major: Explain inconsistent and counter-intuitive results (intrinsic losses for multilingual methods, strong bilingual invariance on translation, multiCluster excelling extrinsically despite coarse clusters) with deeper analysis.  
- Minor: Report dependency parsing ablation (fixed embeddings during training) and provide an LSTM baseline with random embeddings to contextualize gains.  
- Minor: Clarify whether Table 1 scores are averaged across all 17 languages or a subset.  
- Minor: Discuss conceptual/empirical advantages of integrated multiSkip versus a pipeline (bilingual then multiCCA) and consider comparing them.  
- Minor: Address dictionary realism—evaluate robustness to noisier, standard multilingual dictionaries rather than high-coverage parallel-extracted ones.
