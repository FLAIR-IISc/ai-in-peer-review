- Paper proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (CCA).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC's correlation with CCA and extended to multilingual evaluation.
- Authors provide a web portal and evaluate methods across many tasks; new methods sometimes outperform baselines.
- Paper is well written, thoroughly documented, and represents substantial, timely work.
- Main concern: breadth over depth — insufficient analysis and discussion of results.
- Recommend deeper analysis of conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between 59- and 12-language setups.
- Suggest analyzing effects of estimation parameters and design choices within MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Practical contributions (improved QVEC measure, web portal) are valuable, but novel research insights are less clear.
- Request to include monolingual correlation results comparing QVEC and QVEC-CCA (Table 1) to substantiate improvement claim.
- Minor wording/formatting issues: clarify phrase about dataset combination and fix two equations exceeding column margins.
- Citation inconsistency for MultiSkip baseline: reconcile references to Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al.
- Literature review solid but should include additional relevant work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (several cited papers).
- Also recommend adding literature on multilingual training for improved source-language embeddings and broader use of translational context.
