- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation metric addressing QVEC’s theoretical issues and showing stronger correlation with downstream tasks.
- Releases a public web portal and trained embeddings for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation.
- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that do not require parallel corpora and evaluates them up to 59 languages.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA fixes basis invariance and normalization problems and empirically correlates better with downstream performance than common intrinsic metrics.
- Strength: public evaluation portal and released resources improve reproducibility and lower the barrier for benchmarking.
- Strength: large-scale, comprehensive experiments evaluating four methods across nine tasks and two language sets (12 and 59 languages).
- Strength: dictionary-based methods are novel, accessible, and competitive with methods that rely on parallel data.
- Weakness: analysis is superficial—results are reported without deep investigation into causes (e.g., multiCluster’s poor performance on 59 languages).
- Weakness: lacks ablation and sensitivity analyses for key design choices and hyperparameters (e.g., max cluster size, choice of English as pivot).
- Weakness: related work coverage is incomplete—omits relevant autoencoder, deep correlation, and sense-specific bilingual embedding literature.
