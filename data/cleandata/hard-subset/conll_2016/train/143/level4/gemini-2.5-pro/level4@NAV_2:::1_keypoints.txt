- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, a new intrinsic evaluation metric that correlates more strongly with downstream NLP tasks than prior intrinsic metrics.  
- Releases a public evaluation portal, open-source code, and pre-trained models to support reproducible, standardized multilingual embedding research.  
- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that scale to 59 languages using only monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries.  
- Provides a thorough correlation analysis between intrinsic metrics and extrinsic task performance, demonstrating limitations of existing metrics.  
- The public web portal and resources lower barriers to entry and promote consistent benchmarking in the field.  
- Demonstrates impressive scalability by training embeddings for 59 languages without relying on parallel corpora.  
- Experimental results do not consistently show the proposed methods as superior; a baseline (translation-invariance) outperforms on several intrinsic tasks.  
- multiCluster—collapsing all translations into a single vector—surprisingly achieves the best extrinsic-task performance, raising questions about evaluation sensitivity to semantic distinctions.  
- multiQVEC-CCA depends on supersense tag annotations, which are available for very few languages, limiting the metric’s applicability in massively multilingual settings.  
- Comparison across methods is confounded by large differences in vocabulary coverage, complicating fair score comparisons.  
- The novelty and adaptation of baseline methods (e.g., translation-invariance) to the multilingual setting are unclear and need clarification.  
- Question: Why were embeddings kept fixed during dependency parsing, and how would fine-tuning affect results?  
- Question: Why does multiCluster perform so well on extrinsic tasks despite discarding intra-cluster semantic distinctions?  
- Question: How would using manually curated lexicons (vs. automatically extracted dictionaries) affect performance, and were such resources tested?
