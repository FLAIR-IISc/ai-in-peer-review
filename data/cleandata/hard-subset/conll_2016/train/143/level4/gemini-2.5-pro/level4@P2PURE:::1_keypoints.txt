- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and a Translation Invariance approach.  
- multiCluster: groups words into multilingual clusters via a dictionary and assigns a single learned embedding per cluster.  
- multiCCA: extends bilingual CCA to multilingual by anchoring to English and projecting other monolingual spaces into it.  
- multiSkip: extends bilingual skip-based alignment to incorporate signals from all parallel corpora in a single objective.  
- Translation Invariance: uses low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment frequency; unclear if it generalizes beyond bilingual case.  
- Proposes a modified QVEC evaluation using CCA for rotation- and linear-invariant alignment of embeddings with linguistic feature vectors, extended to multilingual evaluation.  
- Paper is well-written and methods are clearly described.  
- Major concern 1: Novelty of the Translation Invariance method relative to Gardner et al. is unclear; need explicit claim if the contribution is multilingual extension.  
- Major concern 2: Reliance on super-sense annotations risks severe feature sparsity across languages; paper should address this more than a footnote.  
- Major concern 3: Analysis omits impact of coverage (e.g., multiCluster vs multiCCA similar parsing scores despite different coverage); authors should clarify coverage effects.  
- Major concern 4: Results lack a coherent narrative—intrinsic metrics often don’t favor proposed methods, Translation Invariance unexpectedly excels at word translation, and multiCluster unusually strong on extrinsic tasks; needs analysis/intuition.  
- Additional Q1: How much does parsing performance drop if pretrained embeddings are fixed, and what gain do they provide over random initialization?  
- Additional Q2: Do Table 1 results represent averages over all 17 languages from Section 5.1?  
- Additional Q3: What are conceptual/empirical advantages of multiSkip versus a pipeline of bilingual embeddings followed by multiCCA mapping?  
- Additional Q4: Dictionaries were auto-extracted from parallel corpora/MT; have the authors evaluated methods with manually-curated lexicons to reflect coverage/noise challenges?
