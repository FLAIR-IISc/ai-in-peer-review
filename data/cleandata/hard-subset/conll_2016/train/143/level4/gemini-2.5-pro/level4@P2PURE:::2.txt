This paper introduces two dictionary-based methods for learning multilingual word embeddings: MultiCluster, which is motivated by clustering, and MultiCCA, which is based on canonical correlation analysis. The authors also propose QVEC-CCA, a new supersense similarity measure that extends QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA to enable multilingual evaluation. An extensive evaluation across a range of tasks, facilitated by a web portal developed by the authors, demonstrates that the proposed methods outperform two baselines in some scenarios.

The paper is well-written, timely, and represents a substantial amount of work. The authors are to be commended for the meticulous documentation and the development of the accompanying web portal.

However, the paper's primary weakness is its breadth, which comes at the expense of depth. The analysis of the results lacks a thorough discussion. For instance, the paper does not explain the conflicting performance of MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language settings, nor does it explore the impact of key parameters and design choices for the MultiCluster and MultiCCA methods. Consequently, while the contributions are of high practical value to the community (e.g., the improved QVEC-CCA measure and the web portal), the paper offers limited insight into the underlying research questions.

Below are some more specific points:

*   To substantiate the claim that QVEC-CCA is an improvement over the original QVEC (lines 326-328), the authors should include correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA in Table 1.

Minor issues:

*   l. 304: The phrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" could be misinterpreted. Consider rephrasing to clarify that these are datasets of the same type, but for different language pairs.
*   p. 3: Two equations exceed the column margin.
*   The paper creates some confusion regarding the MultiSkip baseline. Lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al., but Section 2.3 only mentions Luong et al. Please clarify the relationship between these works and the implemented baseline.
*   The related work section could be strengthened by including other relevant publications, such as:

    *   Hill, F., Cho, K., Jean, S., Devin, C., and Bengio, Y. (2014). Embedding word similarity with neural machine translation. arXiv preprint arXiv:1412.6448.
    *   Lu, A., Wang, W., Bansal, M., Gimpel, K., & Livescu, K. (2015). Deep multilingual correlation for improved word embeddings. In NAACL.
    *   Faruqui, M., & Dyer, C. (2013). An Information Theoretic Approach to Bilingual Word Clustering. In ACL.
