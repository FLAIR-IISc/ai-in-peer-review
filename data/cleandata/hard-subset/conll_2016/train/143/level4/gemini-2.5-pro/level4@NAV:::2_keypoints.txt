- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual word representation methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).  
- Proposes QVEC-CCA, replacing QVEC’s correlation component with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.  
- Conducts comprehensive evaluation across diverse tasks via a custom web portal, showing the methods outperform baselines in some settings.  
- Manuscript is well-written, substantial, and meticulously documented.  
- Main concern: the paper emphasizes breadth over analytical depth.  
- Requires deeper discussion of conflicting MultiCluster results in the 59- vs. 12-language settings.  
- Needs analysis of how estimation parameters and design choices affect MultiCluster and MultiCCA performance.  
- Practical contributions (enhanced QVEC measure and the web portal) are valuable to the community.  
- Overall, the paper delivers limited new insights into its core research questions.  
- Suggest adding monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA in Table 1 to substantiate improvement claims.  
- Minor wording issue: clarify that the “combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” means same dataset type differing by language.  
- Minor formatting issue: two equations extend beyond the column margin.  
- Clarify the MultiSkip baseline citation—relationship among Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. is confusing.  
- Recommend adding related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).  
- Recommend adding related work on multilingual training for improved source-language embeddings (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014).  
- Recommend adding work on broader use of translational context (Diab & Resnik 2002).
