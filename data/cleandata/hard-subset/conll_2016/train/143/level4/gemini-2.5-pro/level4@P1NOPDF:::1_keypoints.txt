- Paper presents four methods for multilingual word embeddings and a modified QVEC metric (QVEC-CCA) for evaluation.  
- multiCluster: maps words into multilingual clusters via a dictionary and learns a single embedding per cluster.  
- multiCCA: extends Faruqui & Dyer (2014) by using English as an anchor and projecting other monolingual spaces into it via bilingual dictionaries.  
- multiSkip: extends Luong et al. (2015b) by incorporating source/target context from word alignments across all available parallel corpora for a multilingual objective.  
- Translation Invariance: uses low-rank decomposition of the word PMI matrix with bilingual alignment frequencies; novelty relative to Gardner et al. is unclear and multilingual extension is uncertain.  
- QVEC-CCA: replaces QVEC with a CCA-based score that maximizes correlation between embeddings and linguistic features, yielding rotation- and linear-transformation-invariant scores and a multilingual extension.  
- Paper is well-written and methods are clearly described.  

Major concerns:
- Unclear novelty of the translation invariance method relative to prior work (Gardner et al.); if novelty is multilingual extension, this must be explicitly argued.  
- Reliance on super-sense annotations for multilingual evaluation is problematic due to small intersections across languages and potential sparsity; authors should explain handling beyond footnote 9.  
- Results (Table 2) are hard to interpret without accounting for vocabulary coverage differences; coverage effects (e.g., similar parsing scores despite different coverage) need clarification.  
- Inconsistencies in Table 3: novel multilingual methods often do not outperform baselines on intrinsic metrics, the baseline translation invariance does best on word translation, and multiCluster unexpectedly tops extrinsic tasks, indicating a disconnect between intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations that needs discussion.  

Minor questions/clarifications:
- For dependency parsing, report performance drop when embeddings are fixed vs fine-tuned and gains over random initialization in the same parser.  
- Confirm whether Table 1 results are averaged across all 17 languages in Section 5.1.  
- Explain conceptual/empirical advantages of joint-training multiSkip versus a pipeline of bilingual training followed by multiCCA projection.  
- Dictionaries were auto-extracted from parallel corpora/Google Translate; have authors tested with publicly available, manually curated multilingual dictionaries to reflect real/noisy resource challenges?
