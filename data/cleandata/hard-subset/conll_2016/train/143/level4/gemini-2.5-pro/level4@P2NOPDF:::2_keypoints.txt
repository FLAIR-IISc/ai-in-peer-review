- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).  
- Proposes QVEC-CCA, an improved supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC's correlation with CCA and extending it for multilingual evaluation.  
- Develops a web portal and evaluates methods across tasks, showing competitive performance against two baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, timely, and thoroughly documented; accompanying web portal is a strong practical contribution.  
- Main weakness: breadth over depth—many methods and evaluations but superficial result discussion.  
- Needs deeper analysis of conflicting outcomes, e.g., MultiCluster performance with 59 vs 12 languages.  
- Should provide more insight into effects of parameter choices and design decisions for MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- Practical contributions (QVEC-CCA and portal) are valuable, but the paper offers limited scientific insight into specific research questions.  
- Request: include monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 to substantiate improvement claim.  
- Request: clarify relationship and citations for the MultiSkip baseline (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Luong et al.).  
- Suggest expanding related work coverage, including papers on multilingual embeddings/clustering, multilingual training for source-language embeddings, and translational context.  
- Minor: rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” to avoid implying heterogeneity.  
- Minor: two equations on page 3 exceed the column margin and should be fixed.
