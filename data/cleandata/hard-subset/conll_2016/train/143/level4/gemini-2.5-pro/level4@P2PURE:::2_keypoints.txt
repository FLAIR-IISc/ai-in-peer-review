- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).  
- Proposes QVEC-CCA, extending QVEC by replacing correlation with CCA for multilingual supersense evaluation.  
- Provides an extensive empirical evaluation and a publicly available web portal; methods outperform two baselines in some settings.  
- Paper is well-written, timely, and meticulously documented; the web portal is a strong practical contribution.  
- Main weakness: the paper prioritizes breadth over depth, offering limited analysis and insight.  
- Fails to explain conflicting MultiCluster performance between the 59- and 12-language settings.  
- Does not analyze the impact of key parameters and design choices for MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- While QVEC-CCA and the portal are practically valuable, the work gives limited understanding of underlying research questions.  
- Requests inclusion of monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 to substantiate improvement claims.  
- Minor issues: ambiguous phrasing at l.304 and two equations that exceed the column margin.  
- Confusion about the MultiSkip baseline citations—clarify which prior works correspond to the implemented baseline.  
- Recommends strengthening related work by citing additional relevant papers (e.g., Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013; Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014; Diab & Resnik 2002).
