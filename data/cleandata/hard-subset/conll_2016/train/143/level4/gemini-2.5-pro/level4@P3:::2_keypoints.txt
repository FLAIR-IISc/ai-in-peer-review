- Proposes multiCluster and multiCCA to learn shared-space multilingual word embeddings from monolingual corpora plus bilingual dictionaries.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation metric invariant to linear transforms and producing normalized scores.  
- Releases a public web portal with datasets, evaluation scripts (nine metrics), and trained embeddings for 59 languages.  
- Trains embeddings for 59 languages and compares methods on a 12-language subset against baselines.  
- Demonstrates multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA correlate better with parsing and document classification than existing intrinsic metrics.  
- Shows multiCCA often outperforms parallel-data-based methods on downstream extrinsic tasks.  
- Emphasizes rigorous evaluation methodology, including assessing the quality of evaluation metrics themselves.  
- Provides high practical value through open-source code, data, and a standardized evaluation platform for reproducible research.  
- Conducts large-scale experiments across nine intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, offering a valuable benchmark.  
- Lacks deep analysis of why multiCluster degrades from 12 to 59 languages beyond a brief “semantic ambiguity” hypothesis.  
- Key comparisons are confounded by differing vocabulary coverage across methods, weakening comparative claims.  
- Embedding estimation methods are incremental extensions of prior work, with limited novelty emphasized.  
- Requests further analysis of multiCluster’s degradation (e.g., impact on polysemous words and hyperparameter effects).  
- Asks for monolingual correlation scores to support the claim that QVEC-CCA improves over QVEC.  
- Seeks clarification on the exact relationship between the multiSkip implementation and prior works (Luong et al., Coulmance et al., Guo et al.).  
- Notes a minor wording issue about "combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" needing clarification.  
- Suggests expanding related work to include autoencoder-based and other correlation-based multilingual embedding methods.
