- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (CCA-motivated).  
- Proposes QVEC-CCA, an improved supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC’s correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.  
- Evaluated across many tasks and shows improvements over baselines in some settings; authors provide a web portal for evaluation.  
- Paper is well-written, timely, and practically valuable; documentation and released tools are commendable.  
- Main concern: focus on breadth at the expense of analytical depth; discussion of results is often superficial.  
- Lack of analysis for conflicting MultiCluster results between 59- and 12-language setups.  
- Paper needs deeper investigation of key parameters and design choices for MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- Recommend adding monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results to Table 1 to substantiate the claimed improvement.  
- Inconsistent citation for the MultiSkip baseline (mentions Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al.); clarify relationships and correct citations.  
- Related work section could be strengthened by including several suggested papers on multilingual embeddings, bilingual training, and translational context.  
- Minor wording: line 304’s phrase about “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” can be clearer about dataset types/language pairs.  
- Minor formatting: two equations on page 3 exceed the column margin and should be fixed.
