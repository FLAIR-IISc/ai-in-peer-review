- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (groups translationally equivalent words) and MultiCCA (multilingual extension of bilingual CCA).
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation metric shown to correlate better with downstream parsing and document classification than existing intrinsic metrics.
- Releases a public web portal with standardized datasets and evaluation scripts for nine intrinsic and extrinsic tasks to enable reproducible comparisons.
- Evaluates methods across up to 59 languages on a wide range of tasks, showing competitive or superior performance in several settings.
- High practical value: multiQVEC-CCA and the evaluation portal are likely to have substantial community impact.
- Extensive experiments: large-scale empirical study across 59 languages and nine evaluation tasks provides a valuable benchmark.
- Clear writing and thorough documentation, with released code and data supporting reproducibility.
- Lacks deep analysis of why methods perform as they do; results are broad but underexplained (e.g., MultiCluster’s differing performance on 12 vs 59 languages).
- Offers limited new scientific insight beyond engineering and resource contributions; central research questions are underexplored.
- The argument for multiQVEC-CCA would be strengthened by including monolingual correlation results for direct comparison with QVEC.
- Clarify the exact relationship and citation lineage of the MultiSkip baseline across prior works (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Luong et al.).
- Wording issue: clarify the phrase about "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" to indicate aggregation across language pairs.
- Formatting issue: two equations in Section 2.3 appear to exceed the column margin.
- Missing related work: cite additional autoencoder/correlation and bilingual-signal methods (e.g., Chandar et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Guo et al. 2014; Suster et al. 2016) and early translational-context work (Diab & Resnik 2002).
