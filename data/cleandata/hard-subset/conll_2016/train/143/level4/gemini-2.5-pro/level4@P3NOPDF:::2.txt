This paper introduces two dictionary-based methods for learning multilingual word embeddings: MultiCluster, which is based on clustering, and MultiCCA, which is motivated by canonical correlation analysis. The authors also propose QVEC-CCA, an improved supersense similarity measure that replaces the correlation component of QVEC with CCA and incorporates multilingual evaluation. The proposed methods are evaluated on a wide range of tasks, and the authors demonstrate that they can outperform existing baselines in certain settings. The evaluation is facilitated by a web portal also developed by the authors.

The paper is very well-written, clearly presenting a substantial body of work. The methods for representation learning and evaluation are timely and relevant to the community. The authors should also be commended for their meticulous documentation and for making their evaluation tools available via a web portal, which is a valuable practical contribution.

My main concern with the paper, however, is its focus on breadth, which seems to come at the expense of analytical depth. While the paper presents several new methods and an extensive evaluation, the discussion of the results is somewhat superficial. This leaves the reader with interesting empirical findings but without a deeper understanding of the underlying phenomena. For instance, the paper reports conflicting outcomes for the MultiCluster method between the 59- and 12-language setups but does not offer a detailed analysis or hypothesis for this discrepancy. Similarly, a more in-depth discussion on the effect of key parameters and design choices for MultiCluster and MultiCCA would significantly strengthen the paper's scientific contribution. As it stands, while the paper offers high practical value (particularly the improved QVEC measure and the web portal), its contribution in terms of answering specific research questions and providing new insights is less pronounced.

Below are some more specific points for improvement:

*   To substantiate the claim that QVEC-CCA is an improvement over the original QVEC (lines 326-328), it would be beneficial to include the monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1. This direct comparison would make the demonstrated improvement more explicit.

*   There appears to be an inconsistency in the citation for the MultiSkip baseline. Lines 121 and 147 refer to Coulmance et al. and Guo et al., while Section 2.3 refers to Luong et al. Please clarify the relationship between these works and ensure the citation is consistent and accurate.

*   The related work section is reasonable but could be strengthened by including other relevant work. I suggest considering the following:

    **Multilingual embeddings and clustering:**
    *   Chandar A P, S., Lauly, S., Larochelle, H., Khapra, M. M., Ravindran, B., Raykar, V. C., and Saha, A. (2014). An autoencoder approach to learning bilingual word representations. In NIPS.
    *   Hill, F., Cho, K., Jean, S., Devin, C., and Bengio, Y. (2014). Not all languages are created equal: A neural view of multilingual embeddings. arXiv preprint arXiv:1405.5861.
    *   Lu, A., Wang, W., Bansal, M., Gimpel, K., & Livescu, K. (2015). Deep multilingual correlation for improved word embeddings. In NAACL.
    *   Faruqui, M., & Dyer, C. (2013). An Information Theoretic Approach to Bilingual Word Clustering. In ACL.

    **Multilingual training for improved source-language embeddings:**
    *   Suster, S., Titov, I., and van Noord, G. (2016). Bilingual learning of multi-sense embeddings with discrete autoencoders. In NAACL-HLT.
    *   Guo, J., Che, W., Wang, H., and Liu, T. (2014). Learning sense-specific word embeddings by exploiting bilingual resources. In COLING.

    **Broader use of translational context:**
    *   Diab, M., & Resnik, P. (2002). An unsupervised method for word sense tagging using parallel corpora. In ACL.

**Minor Points:**

*   Line 304: The phrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" could be slightly misleading, as it suggests the datasets are of different types. It might be clearer to state that these are datasets of the same type but for different language pairs.
*   Page 3: Two equations appear to exceed the column margin, which should be corrected for formatting.
