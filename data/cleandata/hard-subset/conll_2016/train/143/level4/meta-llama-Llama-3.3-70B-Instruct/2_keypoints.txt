- Paper introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).
- Authors propose a supersense similarity measure that improves QVEC by replacing correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluation across multiple tasks via an author-developed web portal shows the new methods beat two baselines in some cases.
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly documented, and presents timely, relevant representation and evaluation methods.
- Concern: breadth of coverage appears to sacrifice depth; results need deeper analysis and interpretation.
- Important unresolved issue: conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between 59- and 12-language setups require explanation.
- Need clearer discussion of the impact of estimation parameters and methodological choices in MultiCluster and CCA.
- Paper offers high practical value (improved QVEC measure, web portal) but does not substantially advance theoretical understanding.
- Specific request: include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) for a fuller comparison.
- Minor issues: clarify cross-lingual word similarity dataset descriptions and fix equations that exceed column margins.
- Inconsistency in citing the MultiSkip baseline; clarify correspondence between the different references.
- Suggest adding citations to related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (e.g., Chandar, Hill, Lu, Faruqui, Suster, Guo, Diab).
