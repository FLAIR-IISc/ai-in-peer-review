- Paper presents four multilingual embedding methods and a modified QVEC metric for evaluation.  
- multiCluster: uses dictionaries to form multilingual clusters and assigns cluster embeddings as word representations.  
- multiCCA: projects monolingual embeddings into an English anchor space using bilingual dictionaries (extension of Faruqui & Dyer).  
- multiSkip: extends Luong et al.’s aligned source–target context skip model to the multilingual setting by combining all parallel corpora in the objective.  
- Translation invariance: low-rank decomposition of the PMI matrix with bilingual alignment frequency terms; may be mainly bilingual.  
- Evaluation: uses CCA to align embeddings with linguistic annotations, producing rotation- and linear-invariant scores and extended to multilingual evaluation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Concern: novelty of the translation invariance approach vs Gardner et al. is unclear; need clarification if multilingual extension is the contribution.  
- Concern: super-sense annotations across languages may have very small feature intersections; authors should explain how they handle this beyond a footnote.  
- Concern: coverage effects may bias Table 2 scores (e.g., big coverage gap between multiCluster and multiCCA with similar parsing scores); needs investigation.  
- Concern: Table 3 shows inconsistent results—multilingual methods often don’t excel on intrinsic metrics, invariance method dominates word-translation metrics, and multiCluster’s strong extrinsic performance is surprising given loss of inter-cluster semantics.  
- Question: What is the performance loss when embeddings are fixed in dependency parsing, and what gain do they provide over random embeddings in the LSTM stack parser?  
- Question: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in §5.1?  
- Question: What advantages does multiSkip offer compared to learning bilingual embeddings and then applying multi-CCA across spaces?  
- Question: Were real multilingual dictionaries used instead of dictionaries extracted from parallel corpora or Google Translate, and what were the findings?
