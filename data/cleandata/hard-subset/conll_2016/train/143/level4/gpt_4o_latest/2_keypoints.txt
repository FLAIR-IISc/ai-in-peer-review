- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-inspired) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Proposes a supersense similarity measure that improves QVEC by replacing its correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.
- Provides a web portal and evaluates methods across diverse tasks, showing the proposed representations outperform two baselines in some scenarios.
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly documented, and presents timely, relevant representation-learning and evaluation techniques.
- Paper emphasizes breadth over depth; lacks detailed analysis of results and underlying reasons.
- Needs deeper discussion of the conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between the 59-language and 12-language setups.
- Requires analysis of the effects of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- High practical value (improved QVEC measure and web portal) but less effective at answering specific research questions.
- Request to include monolingual correlation results (Table 1) for QVEC and QVEC-CCA to support improvement claims.
- Minor wording issue: phrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” can be misleading.
- Layout issue: two equations exceed the column margin.
- Clarification needed on MultiSkip baseline citations (relationship among Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al.).
- Suggests adding related references on multilingual embeddings/clustering, multilingual training for improved source-language embeddings, and translational context methods.
