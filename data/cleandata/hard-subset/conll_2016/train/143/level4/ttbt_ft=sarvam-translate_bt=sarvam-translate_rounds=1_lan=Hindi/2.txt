This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embedding, one inspired by clustering (multicluster) and the other by Canonical Correlation Analysis (Multilingual CCA).

Additionally, a supersense similarity measure is proposed that improves on QCEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and taking multilingual evaluation into account.

The evaluation is conducted on a wide range of tasks using a web portal developed by the authors; it has been shown that in some cases, the proposed representation methods perform better than two other baselines (e.g., regarding conflicting results between the 59- and 12-language setups for multicast; regarding the impact of estimation parameters and decisions in multicast/CCA). Therefore, while I feel this paper has high practical value for me and the research community (better QCEC measures, web portal), I haven't really learned that much from reading it, that is, in terms of the research questions addressed and answered.
