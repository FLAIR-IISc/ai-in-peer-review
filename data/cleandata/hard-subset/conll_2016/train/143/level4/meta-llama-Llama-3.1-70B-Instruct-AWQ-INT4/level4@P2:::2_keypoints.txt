- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that use monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries without requiring parallel corpora.  
- Proposes multiQVEC adaptation and a new multiQVECCCA evaluation metric that better correlates with downstream tasks like text categorization and parsing.  
- Develops a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings with a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across many tasks and metrics showing effectiveness in some scenarios.  
- Strength: Thorough discussion of limitations of prior methods and advantages of the proposed approaches.  
- Strength: Web portal is a useful community resource for evaluation and comparison.  
- Weakness: Broad scope may reduce depth; results discussion (e.g., conflicting MultiCluster outcomes for 59 vs 12 languages) is insufficient.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of how estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster/CCA affect performance.  
- Weakness: Minor errors and inconsistencies (equation formatting, citations) detract from clarity.  
- Question: Please provide a more detailed explanation of the conflicting MultiCluster results between the 59- and 12-language setups.  
- Question: How do estimation parameters and implementation decisions in MultiCluster/CCA influence performance?  
- Question: Clarify the correspondence between the MultiSkip baseline and the works of Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al.  
- Additional comment: Add more related works on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and embedding training for stronger context.  
- Additional comment: Consider deeper exploration of translational context as examined in other studies.  
- Additional comment: Improve formatting and citation consistency to enhance readability.
