This paper presents two novel dictionary-based approaches for estimating multilingual word embeddings, namely MultiCluster, which is grounded in clustering, and MultiCCA, rooted in canonical correlation analysis. Furthermore, the authors propose a supersense similarity measure that enhances QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and considering multilingual evaluation. The evaluation, conducted via a web portal developed by the authors, demonstrates the proposed representation methods' superior performance over two baseline methods in certain tasks.

The paper is well-written and reflects a substantial amount of work. The introduced representation-learning and evaluation methods are timely and well-documented. However, upon reviewing the paper, I feel that it may have sacrificed some depth for breadth. A more in-depth discussion of the results would be beneficial, particularly regarding the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups and the impact of estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster/CCA.

While the paper holds high practical value for the research community, particularly with the improved QVEC measure and the web portal, it does not provide substantial insights into research questions addressed and answered.

Some specific remarks are as follows:

- Including correlation results (Table 1) for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA would be beneficial, as the proposed QVEC-CCA is stated to be an improvement over QVEC.
- Minor corrections include rephrasing "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" to reflect that the datasets are of the same nature but in different languages.
- Equations on page 3 exceed the column margin and should be adjusted.
- The references to Coulmance et al, Guo et al, and Luong et al in relation to the MultiSkip baseline should be clarified.
- Additional relevant works on multilingual embeddings and clustering, as well as multilingual training of embeddings, could be included in the citations, such as those by Chandar et al, Hill et al, Lu et al, Faruqui et al, Suster et al, Guo et al, and Diab et al.
