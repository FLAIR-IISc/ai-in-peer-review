- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC's correlation with CCA and extending evaluation to multilingual settings.
- Evaluation across many tasks via a web portal shows the proposed methods sometimes outperform two baselines.
- Paper is well-written with thorough documentation and useful practical contributions (improved QVEC measure and web portal).
- Paper may be too broad, sacrificing depth in analysis and discussion of results.
- Requests deeper analysis of conflicting results (e.g., MultiCluster performance in 59- vs 12-language setups).
- Requests analysis of the effect of estimation parameters and methodological choices in MultiCluster and CCA.
- Reviewer did not gain significant new theoretical insights despite high practical value.
- Suggests including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) for comparison.
- Minor wording/format issues: rephrase line 304, two equations exceed column margin, and clarify citations for the MultiSkip baseline (Coulmance/Guo vs Luong).
- Recommends adding related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).
- Suggests citing papers on multilingual training for better source-language embeddings (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014).
- Recommends broader citation on translational context methods (Diab & Resnik 2002).
