- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC’s correlation with CCA and supports multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluates methods on many tasks via an author-built web portal; proposed methods outperform two baselines in some cases.
- Paper is well-written and meticulously documented; evaluation tools and improved QVEC measure have high practical value.
- Paper sacrifices depth for breadth; overall results discussion is insufficient.
- Calls for deeper analysis of conflicting MultiCluster results between 59-language and 12-language setups.
- Requests examination of how estimation parameters and modeling decisions affect MultiCluster and CCA results.
- Paper provides practical contributions but offers limited novel research insights or clearly answered research questions.
- Asks to report monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) since QVEC-CCA is claimed as an improvement.
- Minor wording: rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” (line 304) to avoid implying different dataset natures.
- Formatting issue: two equations exceed the column margin on page 3, reducing readability.
- Citation clarification needed: align MultiSkip references (lines 121,147) with Section 2.3 (Coulmance, Guo, Luong).
- Suggests adding related-work citations: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013), Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014), and Diab & Resnik (2002).
- Recommends incorporating the additional references and addressing the above points to improve the paper’s depth and clarity.
