This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, one motivated in clustering (MultiCluster) and another in canonical correlation analysis (MultiCCA). In addition, a supersense similarity measure is proposed that improves on QVEC by substituting its correlation component with CCA, and by taking into account multilingual evaluation.

The evaluation is performed on a wide range of tasks using the web portal developed by the authors; it is shown that in some cases the proposed representation methods outperform two other baselines. I think the paper is very well written, and represents a substantial amount of work done. The presented representation-learning and evaluation methods are certainly timely. I also applaud the authors for the meticulous documentation.

My general feel about this paper, however, is that it goes (perhaps) in too much breadth at the expense of some depth. I'd prefer to see a thorougher discussion of results (e.g. regarding the conflicting outcome for MultiCluster between 59- and 12-language set-up; regarding the effect of estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster/CCA). So, while I think the paper is of high practical value to me and the research community (improved QVEC measure, web portal), I frankly haven't learned that much from reading it, i.e. in terms of research questions addressed and answered.

**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, multiCluster and multiCCA. These methods use dictionaries and monolingual data to estimate embeddings for words in multiple languages. The paper also proposes a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which improves upon previous intrinsic evaluation metrics. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their methods by training embeddings for 59 languages and evaluating them on various tasks.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. **MultiCluster and MultiCCA**: The authors propose two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings. These methods use dictionaries and monolingual data to estimate embeddings for words in multiple languages.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA**: The authors propose a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which improves upon previous intrinsic evaluation metrics. This method uses CCA to measure the correlation between word embeddings and a linguistic matrix.
3. **Web Portal**: The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual word embeddings. The portal allows users to upload their embeddings and evaluate them on various tasks.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Well-written and well-organized**: The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow and understand.
2. **Timely and relevant**: The paper addresses a timely and relevant topic in the field of natural language processing.
3. **Meticulous documentation**: The authors provide meticulous documentation of their methods and experiments.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of depth**: The paper covers a wide range of topics, but lacks depth in some areas. For example, the authors could provide more discussion on the results and the effect of estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster/CCA.
2. **Limited analysis**: The authors could provide more analysis of their results, such as comparing the performance of their methods to other state-of-the-art methods.
3. **Minor errors**: There are some minor errors in the paper, such as the equations exceeding the column margin and the inconsistent citation of related works.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more discussion on the results and the effect of estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster/CCA?
2. How do your methods compare to other state-of-the-art methods in the field?
3. Can you provide more analysis of the results, such as comparing the performance of your methods on different tasks and languages?

**Additional Comments**

1. The authors could consider including more related works in their literature review, such as the papers mentioned in the review.
2. The authors could provide more details on the implementation of their methods, such as the software and hardware used.
3. The authors could consider providing more examples of how their methods can be applied in real-world scenarios.
