This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, namely MultiCluster, which is motivated by clustering, and MultiCCA, which is based on canonical correlation analysis. Additionally, the authors introduce a supersense similarity measure that improves upon QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and considering multilingual evaluation. The evaluation is performed on a wide range of tasks using a web portal developed by the authors, demonstrating that the proposed representation methods outperform two other baselines in some cases.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have invested considerable effort into the presented representation-learning and evaluation methods, which are certainly timely. The meticulous documentation is also praiseworthy. However, upon reading the paper, I feel that it may have sacrificed some depth for the sake of breadth. A more thorough discussion of the results would be beneficial, particularly regarding the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups, as well as the effect of estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.

While I believe the paper holds high practical value for me and the research community, particularly with the improved QVEC measure and the web portal, I did not gain substantial insights from reading it in terms of research questions addressed and answered. Below are some specific remarks:

It would be informative to include the correlation results (Table 1) for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA as well, given that the proposed QVEC-CCA is stated to be an improvement over QVEC (lines 326-328).

Minor comments:

* Line 304: The phrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" could be rephrased, as it implies that the datasets are of different natures, whereas they are actually of the same kind, just in different languages.
* Page 3: Two equations exceed the column margin, which may affect readability.
* Lines 121 and 147 only mention Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. when referring to the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 only mentions Luong et al. It would be helpful to clarify the correspondence between these works.
* Although the paper provides reasonable coverage of related works, there are additional relevant studies that could be included:
    + Multilingual embeddings and clustering: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), and Faruqui and Dyer (2013).
    + Multilingual training of embeddings for better source-language embeddings: Suster et al. (2016) and Guo et al. (2014).
    + More broadly, translational context has been explored in Diab and Resnik (2002).

Incorporating these additional references and addressing the mentioned points would enhance the paper's depth and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed methods and their contributions to the field.
