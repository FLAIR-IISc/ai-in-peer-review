- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC’s correlation component with CCA and incorporates multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluates methods across many tasks using a web portal and shows the proposed representations outperform two baselines in some cases.
- Paper is well written, thorough, and meticulously documented.
- Criticism: paper covers too much breadth at the expense of depth.
- Requests deeper analysis of results, e.g., conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between 59- and 12-language setups.
- Requests analysis of the effects of estimation parameters and modeling decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.
- Practical contributions (improved QVEC measure, web portal) are valuable, but the paper provides limited insight into addressed research questions.
- Suggests including monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA in Table 1 to substantiate the claimed improvement.
- Minor wording fix: rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets.”
- Formatting issue: two equations exceed the column margin.
- Clarify citation correspondence for the MultiSkip baseline (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., vs. Luong et al.).
- Recommends adding related literature on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).
- Recommends additional citations on multilingual training and translational context (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014; Diab & Resnik 2002).
