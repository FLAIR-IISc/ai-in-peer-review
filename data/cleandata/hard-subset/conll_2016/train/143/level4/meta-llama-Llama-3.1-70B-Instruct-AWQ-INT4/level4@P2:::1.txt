**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces four methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings and a modified QVEC metric for evaluating their efficacy. The embedding methods are multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and Translation Invariance. The evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, uses Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA) to maximize the correlation between word embeddings and linguistic data.

**Contributions**

1. The authors propose two novel dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual embeddings.
2. They adapt QVEC to evaluating multilingual embeddings and propose a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA.
3. They develop an easy-to-use web portal for evaluating arbitrary multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Strengths**

1. The paper is well-written and clearly describes the work.
2. The proposed methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, are novel and show promising results.
3. The evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, is an improvement over the original QVEC and provides a more robust evaluation metric.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper does not clearly explain the novelty of the Translation Invariance approach with respect to Gardner et al.'s work.
2. The use of supersense annotations across multiple languages may lead to a small number of features in the intersection of multiple languages, which could be a problem.
3. The results in Table 3 do not tell a consistent story, and the multilingual embedding techniques do not always perform the best.
4. The coverage of the embeddings affects the score in Table 2, but the impact is not clearly explained.

**Questions to Authors**

1. What is the loss in performance by fixing the word embeddings in the dependency parsing task? What was the gain by simply using these embeddings as alternatives to the random embeddings in the LSTM stack parser?
2. Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in section 5.1?
3. Are there any advantages of using the multi-Skip approach instead of learning bilingual embeddings and performing multi-CCA to learn projections across the distinct spaces?
4. Did you explore the use of any real multilingual dictionaries instead of extracting dictionaries from parallel corpora or using Google Translate?
