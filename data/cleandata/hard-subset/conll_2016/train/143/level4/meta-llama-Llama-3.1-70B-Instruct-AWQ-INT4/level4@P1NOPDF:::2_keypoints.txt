- Paper proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (MultiCluster and MultiCCA) and a supersense similarity measure QVEC-CCA that replaces QVEC's correlation with CCA and extends evaluation to multilingual settings.

- Evaluation uses a wide range of tasks via the authors' web portal and shows the proposed methods outperform two baselines in some cases.

- Paper is well-written, documents work meticulously, and is timely with high practical value (improved QVEC, web portal).

- Main critique: paper favors breadth over depth and lacks thorough discussion of results and underlying research questions.

- Request for deeper analysis: explain conflicting MultiCluster performance between 59- and 12-language setups and effects of estimation parameters/decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.

- Suggest including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to substantiate the claimed improvement.

- Minor wording issue: rephrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" to avoid implying different dataset types.

- Formatting issue: two equations exceed column margins and hurt readability.

- Citation clarification needed: lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip but Section 2.3 cites only Luong et al.; clarify correspondence.

- Recommend adding related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).

- Recommend adding work on multilingual training for better source-language embeddings (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014).

- Note broader related work on translational context and unsupervised sense tagging (Diab & Resnik 2002).
