- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC’s correlation component with CCA and supports multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluation on many tasks via an author-developed web portal; proposed methods outperform two baselines in some cases.
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly documented, and of high practical value (measure and web portal).
- Main criticism: the paper sacrifices depth for breadth and lacks thorough analysis of results and addressed research questions.
- Needs deeper discussion of conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between 59- and 12-language setups and the effect of estimation parameters/decisions.
- Recommend reporting monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA (Table 1) to support the claimed improvement.
- Minor wording issue: rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” (line 304) to avoid implying heterogeneous dataset types.
- Formatting issue: two equations on page 3 exceed the column margin.
- Citation/clarification issue: lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance and Guo for MultiSkip, but section 2.3 only mentions Luong et al.; clarify correspondences.
- Related-work omissions recommended: include works on multilingual embeddings/clustering and multilingual training (e.g., Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013; Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014; Diab & Resnik 2002).
- Overall: valuable practical contributions but limited new theoretical or research insights.
