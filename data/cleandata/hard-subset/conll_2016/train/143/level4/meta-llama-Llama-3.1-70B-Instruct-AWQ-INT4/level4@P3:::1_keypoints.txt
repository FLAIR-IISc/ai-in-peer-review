- multiCluster: maps words to multilingual clusters via a dictionary and uses cluster embeddings as word embeddings.  
- multiCCA: projects non-English monolingual embeddings into an English anchor space using bilingual dictionaries (extension of Faruqui & Dyer 2014).  
- multiSkip: extends Luong et al. (2015b) by incorporating source–target context from all available parallel corpora into a multilingual objective.  
- Translation invariance: learns embeddings via low-rank decomposition of the PMI matrix with bilingual alignment terms; may be limited to bilingual settings.  
- Evaluation: adapts QVEC with CCA to align embeddings to linguistic features, producing a rotation- and linear-invariant multilingual evaluation (multiQVEC-CCA).  

Contributions:
- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that need only monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries.  
- Adapts QVEC to multilingual evaluation (multiQVEC) and introduces multiQVEC-CCA to address a theoretical issue.  
- Provides a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings with intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.  

Strengths:
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive across intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.  
- The evaluation web portal is a useful community resource.  

Weaknesses:
- Novelty of the translation invariance approach relative to Gardner et al. (2015) is unclear.  
- Supersense annotations across languages may yield few intersecting features, harming evaluation robustness.  
- Table 3 results are inconsistent and multilingual methods do not uniformly outperform alternatives.  
- The paper omits discussion of performance loss from fixing embeddings in the dependency parsing task.  

Questions for authors:
- What is the novelty of the translation invariance approach compared to Gardner et al. (2015)?  
- How will you address sparse supersense annotation intersections across languages?  
- Can you explain why multilingual embedding techniques underperform in some Table 3 results?  
- Did you try real multilingual dictionaries rather than extracting from parallel corpora or Google Translate?  
- How much performance is lost by keeping embeddings fixed in the dependency parsing experiments?  
- Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings from section 5.1?  
- What are the advantages of multiSkip versus learning bilingual embeddings and using multi-CCA to align them?
