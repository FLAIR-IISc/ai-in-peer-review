Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).  
Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity metric that replaces QVEC's correlation with CCA and incorporates multilingual evaluation.  
Provides comprehensive evaluation across monolingual/cross-lingual similarity, word translation, and downstream tasks, and offers a web portal for standardized evaluation.  
Strength: Methods do not require parallel data, a useful contribution for multilingual NLP.  
Strength: multiQVEC-CCA better addresses QVEC’s limitations and correlates more with downstream task performance.  
Strength: Thorough evaluation across a wide range of intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.  
Strength: The web portal facilitates future research by providing a suite of evaluation metrics.  
Weakness: Lacks deeper analysis of conflicting MultiCluster results between the 59- and 12-language setups.  
Weakness: Insufficient details on estimation parameters and design choices for MultiCluster and MultiCCA and their impact.  
Weakness: Related work coverage is incomplete; missing relevant papers on multilingual embeddings and clustering.  
Weakness: Web portal implementation and usage details are under-specified.  
Question: Please provide more details on estimation parameters and decisions for MultiCluster and MultiCCA and their effect on performance.  
Question: How will you resolve or explain the conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between the 59- and 12-language experiments?  
Question: Can you give implementation and usage details for the web portal so other researchers can adopt it?  
Question: How can the proposed methods be extended to more languages and tasks, and what are their potential limitations?
