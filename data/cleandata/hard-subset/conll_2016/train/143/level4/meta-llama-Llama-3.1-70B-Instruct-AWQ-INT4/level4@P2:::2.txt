**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces two novel methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, namely multiCluster and multiCCA, which utilize dictionaries and monolingual data without requiring parallel data. Additionally, the authors propose a new evaluation method, multiQVECCCA, which improves upon previous intrinsic metrics by correlating better with downstream tasks such as text categorization and parsing. The authors also develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings that only require monolingual data and pairwise parallel dictionaries.
2. The authors adapt QVEC to evaluating multilingual embeddings (multiQVEC) and develop a new evaluation method, multiQVECCCA, which addresses the theoretical shortcomings of multiQVEC.
3. The authors create an easy-to-use web portal for evaluating arbitrary multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods using a wide range of tasks and metrics, demonstrating their effectiveness in certain scenarios.
2. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the limitations of previous methods and the advantages of their proposed approaches.
3. The web portal developed by the authors is a valuable resource for the research community, facilitating the evaluation and comparison of multilingual embeddings.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper attempts to cover a broad range of topics, which may come at the expense of depth in certain areas. For example, the discussion of results could be more thorough, particularly regarding the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups.
2. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the effect of estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster/CCA on the performance of the proposed methods.
3. Some minor errors and inconsistencies were noted, such as the formatting of equations and the citation of related works.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Could you provide a more detailed discussion of the results, particularly regarding the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups?
2. How do the estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster/CCA affect the performance of the proposed methods?
3. Could you clarify the correspondence between the MultiSkip baseline and the works of Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al.?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper could benefit from the inclusion of additional related works, such as those on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and training of embeddings for better source-language embeddings.
2. The authors may consider exploring the translational context in more depth, as it has been explored in other works.
3. The paper's formatting and citations could be improved to enhance readability and consistency.
