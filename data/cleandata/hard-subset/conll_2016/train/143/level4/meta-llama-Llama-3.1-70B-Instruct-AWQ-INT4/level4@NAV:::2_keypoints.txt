- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-based) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).  
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure that improves QVEC by replacing correlation with CCA and enabling multilingual evaluation.  
- Evaluation via a web portal shows the proposed methods outperform two baselines on some tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, documents substantial, timely representation-learning and evaluation work.  
- Criticism that the paper favors breadth over depth and needs deeper analysis of results.  
- Requests deeper discussion of conflicting MultiCluster results between 59- and 12-language setups and the influence of estimation parameters/decisions in MultiCluster/CCA.  
- Notes high practical value (improved QVEC and web portal) but limited insight into answered research questions.  
- Suggests including monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA (Table 1).  
- Recommends rephrasing the description of datasets to indicate they are the same dataset types in different languages.  
- Points out formatting issue: equations on page 3 exceed column margins.  
- Asks to clarify references to Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. regarding the MultiSkip baseline.  
- Recommends adding related citations on multilingual embeddings and training (e.g., Chandar et al., Hill et al., Lu et al., Faruqui et al., Suster et al., Guo et al., Diab et al.).
