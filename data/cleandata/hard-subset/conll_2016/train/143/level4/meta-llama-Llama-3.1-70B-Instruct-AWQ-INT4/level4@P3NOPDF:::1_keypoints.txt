- Paper introduces four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation invariance) and a modified QVEC evaluation using CCA extended to multilingual evaluation.  
- multiCluster: maps words to multilingual clusters via a dictionary and uses cluster embeddings.  
- multiCCA: projects other-language embeddings into an English anchor space using bilingual dictionaries.  
- multiSkip: extends bilingual skip objectives to multilingual by incorporating all parallel corpora.  
- Translation invariance: uses low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment terms but may be primarily bilingual.  
- Evaluation: uses CCA to align embeddings with linguistic features, producing rotation- and scale-invariant scores, extended to multilingual settings.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear.  
- Major issue: unclear novelty relative to Gardner et al.’s translation invariance approach—need to clarify what is new (especially for multilingual extension).  
- Major issue: supersense annotations across languages may yield very small intersection feature sets; authors must explain how they handle this beyond footnote 9.  
- Major issue: unclear effect of coverage on Table 2 scores (e.g., dependency parsing shows similar scores despite different coverage).  
- Major issue: Table 3 results are inconsistent—intrinsic metrics often do not favor the proposed multilingual methods, and translation invariance unexpectedly dominates word-translation; multiCluster surprisingly best on extrinsic tasks despite discarding inter-cluster semantics.  
- Questions for authors: What performance loss occurs when fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and what gain from using these embeddings vs. random in the LSTM parser?  
- Questions for authors: Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings from Section 5.1?  
- Questions for authors: Any advantage of multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings then using multi-CCA to connect spaces?  
- Questions for authors: Did authors evaluate with real multilingual lexicons (vs. alignments or Google Translate) to reflect realistic dictionary challenges?  
- Overall: interesting contribution but requires clarifications and addressing the above issues to strengthen impact.
