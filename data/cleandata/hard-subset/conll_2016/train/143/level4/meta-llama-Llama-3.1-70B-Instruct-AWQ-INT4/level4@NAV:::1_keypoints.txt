- multiCluster: uses a dictionary to map words into multilingual clusters and uses cluster embeddings as word representations.
- multiCCA: extends Faruqui & Dyer’s bilingual CCA to multilingual space by anchoring on English and projecting other languages via bilingual dictionaries.
- multiSkip: generalizes Luong et al.’s source/target context (alignment) objective to multiple languages by adding terms for all available parallel corpora.
- Translation invariance: low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment-frequency terms; may be mainly effective for bilingual embeddings.
- Evaluation: modified QVEC/CCA aligns embeddings with linguistic features to get rotation-/linear-invariant scores, extended to multilingual evaluation.
- Paper clarity: paper is well-written and clearly describes methods.
- Concern — novelty: unclear how translation invariance differs from Gardner et al.; need clarification, especially if contribution is merely multilingual extension.
- Concern — super-sense annotations: intersection of features across languages may be tiny; authors must explain handling beyond footnote 9.
- Concern — coverage effects: Table 2’s coverage numbers (e.g., dependency parsing) don’t clearly explain similar scores across methods; more analysis required.
- Concern — inconsistent results: Table 3 shows multilingual techniques often not best on intrinsic metrics; translation invariance dominates word-translation, and multiCluster surprisingly best on extrinsic tasks—needs explanation.
- Question — parsing setup: what is the performance loss when fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and what gain from using these embeddings instead of random ones in the LSTM parser?
- Question — Table 1: is it an average over the 17 embeddings described in §5.1?
- Question — multi-Skip vs bilingual+multi-CCA: what are the advantages of multi-Skip over training bilingual embeddings then aligning with multi-CCA?
- Question — dictionaries: did authors evaluate with real multilingual lexicons (not just alignments or Google Translate) to reflect realistic lexicon challenges?
