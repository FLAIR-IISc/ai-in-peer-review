- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).  
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure improving QVEC by replacing correlation with CCA and enabling multilingual evaluation.  
- Provides a web portal and evaluates methods across many tasks, showing proposed methods outperform baselines in some cases.  
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly documented, and represents substantial, timely work.  
- Breadth of the paper may sacrifice depth; results discussion is insufficient in places.  
- Conflicting MultiCluster results between 59- and 12-language setups are unexplained and need analysis.  
- Effects of estimation parameters and methodological choices in MultiCluster/CCA are not fully explored.  
- Suggests including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 for comparison.  
- Requests clarification of the nature and description of the cross-lingual word similarity datasets to avoid misleading readers.  
- Formatting issues: ensure equations fit column margins and are properly formatted.  
- Ask for clearer correspondence among works cited in the MultiSkip baseline (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Luong et al.).  
- Recommends citing additional related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al., Hill et al., Lu et al., Faruqui et al., Suster et al.).  
- Strengths summarized: two new multilingual embedding methods, improved QVEC metric, and a useful evaluation web portal.  
- Key weaknesses summarized: insufficient depth in analysis, unexplained experimental discrepancies, and limited exploration of parameter/estimation impacts.  
- Questions to authors: explain the MultiCluster 59 vs 12-language discrepancy; detail effects of estimation parameters/decisions; clarify correspondence of MultiSkip-related works.
