1. Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and Translation Invariance (low-rank PMI with bilingual alignment).

2. multiCluster maps words via a dictionary to multilingual clusters and uses cluster embeddings as word embeddings.

3. multiCCA extends bilingual CCA to multilingual settings by anchoring spaces in English.

4. multiSkip generalizes source–target context skipgram approaches to multiple languages.

5. Translation Invariance performs low-rank decomposition of the PMI matrix with bilingual alignment frequency terms.

6. Evaluation uses CCA between embeddings and linguistic annotations to produce rotation- and linear-invariant scores and extends QVEC to multiQVEC-CCA.

7. Main contributions: two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), the multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric, and a web portal for multilingual embedding evaluation.

8. Strength: clear, well-structured description of methods and evaluation metrics.

9. Strength: thorough analysis including correlations between intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

10. Strength: the web portal is a valuable community resource.

11. Weakness: novelty of the Translation Invariance approach relative to Gardner et al. (2015) is not clearly explained.

12. Weakness: using supersense annotations across languages may yield a small intersecting feature set; this limitation needs addressing.

13. Weakness: embedding coverage affects scores (Table 2) and requires more detailed analysis.

14. Weakness: results in Table 3 are inconsistent and need deeper discussion on implications.

15. Question: What is the performance loss when fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and what gains occur versus random embeddings in the LSTM parser?

16. Question: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?

17. Question: Are there advantages to multiSkip versus learning bilingual embeddings then applying multi-CCA to align spaces?

18. Question: Did the authors try real multilingual dictionaries instead of extracting from parallel corpora or using Google Translate?
