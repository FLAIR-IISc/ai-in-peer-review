- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (dictionary → multilingual clusters → cluster embeddings used as word embeddings).  
- multiCCA: extends Faruqui & Dyer (2014) by using English as an anchor and bilingual dictionaries to project other languages into the English space.  
- multiSkip: generalizes Luong et al. (2015b) by modifying the skip objective to incorporate source/target context from all available parallel corpora.  
- Translation-invariance: low-rank decomposition of the PMI matrix with bilingual alignment frequency terms; may be limited to bilingual embeddings.  
- Evaluation: a modified QVEC metric using CCA to align embeddings with linguistic feature vectors, producing rotation-/linear-invariant scores, extended to multilingual evaluation.  
- Writing: paper is well-written and clear.  
- Major issue 1: need to clarify novelty of translation-invariance approach relative to Gardner et al.; if novelty is only multilingual extension, state and justify it.  
- Major issue 2: super-sense annotation across multiple languages may yield a very small intersection of features; authors must explain how they handle this beyond footnote 9.  
- Major issue 3: unclear role of coverage in Table 2—coverage differs (e.g., dependency parsing for multiCluster vs multiCCA) yet scores are similar; explain coverage’s impact.  
- Major issue 4: Table 3 results are inconsistent—multilingual methods don’t dominate intrinsic metrics; translation-invariance wins word-translation significantly; multiCluster unexpectedly best on extrinsic tasks—need explanation.  
- Q1: What is the performance loss when fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and what gain is obtained by replacing random embeddings in the LSTM stack parser?  
- Q2: Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Q3: Are there advantages of multiSkip versus learning bilingual embeddings and applying multi-CCA to align spaces?  
- Q4: Given dictionary extraction via alignments/Google Translate may not mirror real lexicons, did the authors test with real multilingual dictionaries?
