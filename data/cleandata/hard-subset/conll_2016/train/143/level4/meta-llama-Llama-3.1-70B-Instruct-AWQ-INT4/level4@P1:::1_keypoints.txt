1. Paper describes four embedding methods: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and a translation-invariance approach (possibly bilingual only).  
2. multiCluster: maps words to multilingual clusters via a dictionary and uses cluster embeddings as word embeddings.  
3. multiCCA: projects monolingual embeddings into an English anchor space using bilingual dictionaries (extension of Faruqui & Dyer).  
4. multiSkip: extends Luong et al.’s bilingual skip objective to the multilingual case by adding objectives for all parallel corpora.  
5. Translation-invariance: low-rank decomposition of PMI with bilingual alignment terms; may be limited to bilingual embeddings.  
6. Evaluation: proposes multiQVEC-CCA, using CCA to align embeddings with linguistic features for rotation- and linear-invariant scoring, extended to multilingual evaluation.  
7. Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
8. Major issue 1: unclear novelty over Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance approach — need explicit explanation of contributions beyond multilingual extension.  
9. Major issue 2: using supersense annotations across languages risks very small intersection feature sets; unclear mitigation beyond footnote 9.  
10. Major issue 3: coverage differences likely affect scores (Table 2); need analysis of coverage impact, e.g., parsing example where coverage differs between methods.  
11. Major issue 4: Table 3 results are inconsistent—intrinsic metrics often don’t favor multilingual methods, and translation-invariance dominates word-translation, contrary to goals.  
12. Concern: surprising that multiCluster, which discards inter-cluster semantic relations, performs best on extrinsic tasks.  
13. Question: what is the performance loss when fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and the gain from initializing the LSTM parser with these embeddings?  
14. Question: is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
15. Question: any advantages of multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings then applying multi-CCA to align spaces?  
16. Question: dictionary extraction via alignments or Google Translate may not reflect real lexicon challenges—did the authors test on real multilingual dictionaries?  
17. Summary: paper proposes two novel dictionary-based multilingual methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) and a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), plus a public evaluation portal.  
18. Main contributions: two dictionary-based embedding methods, multiQVEC-CCA that better correlates with downstream tasks, and an evaluation web portal.  
19. Strengths: novel non-parallel-data methods, improved intrinsic metric correlation with downstream tasks, and a useful evaluation portal.  
20. Weaknesses: supersense annotation coverage across languages, inconsistent empirical results (Table 3), and potential loss of cross-lingual semantics in multiCluster.
