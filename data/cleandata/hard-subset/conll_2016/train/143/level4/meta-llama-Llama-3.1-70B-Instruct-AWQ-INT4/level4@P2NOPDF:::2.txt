This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings: MultiCluster, which is motivated by clustering, and MultiCCA, which is based on canonical correlation analysis. Additionally, the authors introduce a supersense similarity measure that improves upon QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and considering multilingual evaluation. The evaluation is performed on a wide range of tasks using the web portal developed by the authors, demonstrating that the proposed representation methods outperform two other baselines in some cases.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have clearly invested a substantial amount of work in the presented representation-learning and evaluation methods. These methods are timely, and the authors' meticulous documentation is commendable. However, my overall impression is that the paper may be too broad, sacrificing some depth in the process. I would prefer to see a more thorough discussion of the results, such as the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups and the effect of estimation parameters and decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.

While I believe the paper has high practical value for me and the research community, particularly with regards to the improved QVEC measure and the web portal, I did not gain significant new insights from reading it in terms of research questions addressed and answered.

Below are some specific remarks:

It would be beneficial to include the correlation results (Table 1) for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA as well, as the proposed QVEC-CCA is stated to be an improvement over QVEC (lines 326-328).

Minor points:

* Line 304: The phrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" could be rephrased, as it implies that the datasets are of different nature, whereas they are actually of the same kind, just in different languages.
* Page 3: Two equations exceed the column margin.
* Lines 121 and 147 only mention Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. when referring to the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 only mentions Luong et al. It would be helpful to clarify the correspondence between these works.

While the paper does a reasonable job of citing related works, there are some additional relevant papers that could be included:

* Multilingual embeddings and clustering:
	+ Chandar et al. (2014): An autoencoder approach to learning bilingual word representations.
	+ Hill et al. (2014): Embedding word similarity with neural machine translation.
	+ Lu et al. (2015): Deep multilingual correlation for improved word embeddings.
	+ Faruqui and Dyer (2013): An Information Theoretic Approach to Bilingual Word Clustering.
* Multilingual training of embeddings for better source-language embeddings:
	+ Suster et al. (2016): Bilingual learning of multi-sense embeddings with discrete autoencoders.
	+ Guo et al. (2014): Learning sense-specific word embeddings by exploiting bilingual resources.
* More broadly, translational context has been explored in:
	+ Diab and Resnik (2002): An unsupervised method for word sense tagging using parallel corpora.
