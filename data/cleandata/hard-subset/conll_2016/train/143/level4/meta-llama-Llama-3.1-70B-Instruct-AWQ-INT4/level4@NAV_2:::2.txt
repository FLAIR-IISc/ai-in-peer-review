This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, one motivated in clustering (MultiCluster) and another in canonical correlation analysis (MultiCCA). In addition, a supersense similarity measure is proposed that improves on QVEC by substituting its correlation component with CCA, and by taking into account multilingual evaluation. The evaluation is performed on a wide range of tasks using the web portal developed by the authors; it is shown that in some cases the proposed representation methods outperform two other baselines.

Summary of the paper and main contributions:
The paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, MultiCluster and MultiCCA. The authors also propose a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of the existing QVEC method. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods on a wide range of tasks, including monolingual and cross-lingual word similarity, word translation, and downstream tasks such as document classification and dependency parsing. The authors also develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual word embeddings, which provides a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics for evaluating the quality of word embeddings.

Strengths:
1. The paper proposes two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data. This is a significant contribution to the field of multilingual NLP.
2. The authors propose a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of the existing QVEC method. This method is shown to correlate better with downstream tasks than existing intrinsic metrics.
3. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods on a wide range of tasks, including monolingual and cross-lingual word similarity, word translation, and downstream tasks such as document classification and dependency parsing.
4. The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual word embeddings, which provides a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics for evaluating the quality of word embeddings. This portal will facilitate future research in this area.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the results, particularly with regards to the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups.
2. The authors could provide more details on the estimation parameters and decisions used in MultiCluster and MultiCCA, and how these affect the performance of the methods.
3. The paper could include more related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering, such as the work by Chandar et al. (2014) and Hill et al. (2014).
4. The authors could provide more details on the web portal, such as how it is implemented and how it can be used by other researchers.

Questions to authors:
1. Can you provide more details on the estimation parameters and decisions used in MultiCluster and MultiCCA, and how these affect the performance of the methods?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups?
3. Can you provide more details on the web portal, such as how it is implemented and how it can be used by other researchers?
4. How do you think the proposed methods can be extended to other languages and tasks, and what are the potential challenges and limitations of these methods?
