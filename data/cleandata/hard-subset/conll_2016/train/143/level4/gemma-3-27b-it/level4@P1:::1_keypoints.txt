- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation-invariance) and a modified QVEC evaluation metric.
- multiCluster: maps words to multilingual clusters via a dictionary and uses cluster embeddings as word embeddings.
- multiCCA: projects monolingual embeddings into an English anchor space using bilingual dictionaries (extension of Faruqui & Dyer 2014).
- multiSkip: generalizes Luong et al. (2015b) by extending source-target context objective across all parallel corpora.
- Translation-invariance: low-rank decomposition of PMI with bilingual alignment terms; may be limited to bilingual embeddings.
- Evaluation: uses CCA to align embeddings with linguistic features, producing a score invariant to rotation/linear transforms and extended to multilingual evaluation.
- Paper is well written and clearly described.
- Clarification needed on what is novel in the translation-invariance method compared to Gardner et al., especially regarding multilingual extension.
- Concern about supersense annotations: feature intersection across languages may be very small—more than footnote 9 is needed on handling this.
- Question about coverage effects on Table 2: how much do differing coverage rates (e.g., multiCluster vs multiCCA) influence scores?
- Inconsistency in Table 3: intrinsic metrics do not consistently favor the proposed multilingual methods; translation-invariance dominates word translation while multiCluster unexpectedly excels on extrinsic tasks.
- Curious why multiCluster, which discards inter-cluster semantic information, performs best on extrinsic metrics.
- Request for dependency parsing details: performance loss when fixing embeddings and gain from using these embeddings vs random in the LSTM parser?
- Is Table 1 averaged across the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?
- Does multiSkip offer advantages over learning bilingual embeddings and then using multiCCA to align spaces?
- Question about dictionary sources: did authors evaluate using existing multilingual lexicons rather than parallel-alignment or Google Translate-extracted dictionaries?
