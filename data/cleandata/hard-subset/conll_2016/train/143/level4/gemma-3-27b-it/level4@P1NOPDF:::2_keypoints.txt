- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC’s correlation component with CCA and extends evaluation to the multilingual setting.
- Evaluates methods across multiple tasks using an author-developed web portal and shows the proposed representations outperform two baselines in some scenarios.
- Writing and documentation are excellent; the work is substantial, timely, and practically valuable.
- Main concern: the paper emphasizes breadth over depth and lacks thorough analysis of results.
- Requests deeper discussion of inconsistent MultiCluster performance between the 59-language and 12-language setups.
- Asks for analysis of the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Recommends including monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results (e.g., in Table 1) to substantiate the claimed improvement.
- Minor wording: “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” could be misread; clarify these are same-type datasets for different languages.
- Formatting issue: two equations extend beyond the column margin on page 3.
- Citation inconsistency: Lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 only cites Luong et al.; clarify correspondences.
- Suggests adding related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).
- Suggests adding work on multilingual training to improve source-language embeddings (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014).
- Recommends citing broader translational-context work such as Diab & Resnik (2002).
