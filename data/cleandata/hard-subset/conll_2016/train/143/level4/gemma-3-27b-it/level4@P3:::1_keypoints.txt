- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and a translation-invariance PMI decomposition.  
- Evaluation uses a modified QVEC metric (multiQVEC-CCA) with CCA extensions for multilingual evaluation and provides a web portal.  
- Contribution: a comprehensive evaluation framework (multiQVEC-CCA) and a public portal for reproducible multilingual embedding evaluation.  
- Contribution: scales dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods to 59 languages.  
- Contribution: empirical analysis of correlations between intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations, highlighting reliability differences.  
- Strength: rigorous, multi-metric evaluation across monolingual and cross-lingual tasks.  
- Strength: practical resource — the web portal enables standardized, reproducible evaluations.  
- Strength: clear mathematical exposition allowing replication of methods.  
- Strength: detailed experimental setup with hyperparameters, preprocessing, and evaluation details.  
- Strength: convincingly identifies limitations of existing intrinsic evaluation metrics and motivates multiQVEC-CCA.  
- Weakness: translation-invariance extension to multilingual case is under-explained and needs more detail on modifications/challenges.  
- Weakness: reliance on supersense tags raises coverage and sparsity concerns across languages without sufficient mitigation discussion.  
- Weakness: vocabulary coverage effects are not adequately analyzed, complicating interpretation of results (e.g., Table 2).  
- Weakness: inconsistent results (Table 3) — intrinsic vs extrinsic performance unclear, and multiCluster’s strong extrinsic performance is unexplained.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of dictionary quality, potential biases, and effects of machine-translated vs curated lexicons.  
- Question: report dependency parsing ablation — how much does fixing pretrained embeddings degrade performance and how much gain vs random init?  
- Question: clarify Table 1 — does it show average correlation across the 17 embeddings in Section 5.1?  
- Question: when is multiSkip preferable to bilingual-then-multiCCA approaches, and what are the computational tradeoffs?  
- Question: were curated multilingual lexicons (e.g., Open Multilingual Wordnet) tested, and how would higher-quality dictionaries affect results?  
- Question: what explains multiCluster’s strong extrinsic performance despite discarding inter-cluster semantic info?  
- Suggestion: add a table summarizing vocabulary coverage per method and task.  
- Suggestion: update references to include more recent multilingual embedding work.  
- Minor edit: replace “intra-language” (p.26) with a clearer phrase like “cross-lingual word translation.”
