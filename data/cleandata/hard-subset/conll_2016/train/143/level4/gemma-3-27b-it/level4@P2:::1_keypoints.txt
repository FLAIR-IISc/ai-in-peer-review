- Paper introduces four methods for multilingual word embeddings (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation invariance) and a novel evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA.  
- multiQVEC-CCA is a correlation-based metric intended to better align intrinsic evaluation with downstream task performance.  
- Main contributions: a comprehensive multilingual evaluation framework with multiQVEC-CCA, a public web portal and benchmark datasets, and dictionary-based methods applied to 59 languages.  

Strengths:
- multiQVEC-CCA is a theoretically motivated and empirically validated improvement over prior intrinsic metrics.  
- Authors provide resources (code, datasets, web portal) that enhance reproducibility and comparability.  
- The work covers a large scale of 59 languages, including exploration for resource-poor languages.  
- Paper presentation is clear, well-structured, and technically accessible.

Weaknesses:
- The embedding methods largely adapt existing approaches and offer limited methodological novelty.  
- Experimental results are inconsistent and lack a clear interpretive narrative (e.g., translation-invariance outperforming proposed methods on intrinsic tasks).  
- Heavy reliance on bilingual dictionaries (including MT-derived) raises concerns about quality, bias, and coverage effects.  
- Missing ablation studies and broader comparisons to stronger baselines to isolate design choices and advantages.

Questions for authors:
- What specific multilingual adaptations were made to the translation-invariance method and how much do they contribute?  
- How can supersense tagging issues and low feature intersection across languages be mitigated—are alternative resources viable?  
- Can you analyze how embedding coverage affects evaluation scores, especially for dependency parsing?  
- Why does the simplified multiCluster perform well on extrinsic tasks—do hypotheses or further task evaluations explain this?  
- What are the computational costs and scaling behavior of each method as the number of languages increases?

Additional comments:
- Clarify details about the 17 embeddings used in Table 1 (hyperparameters, data) to interpret correlations.  
- Testing with higher-quality multilingual dictionaries could strengthen evaluation robustness.  
- Quantify performance loss/gain when fixing embeddings in dependency parsing versus using random initialization.
