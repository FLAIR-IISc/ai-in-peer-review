- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Proposes a supersense similarity measure (QVEC-CCA) that replaces QVEC’s correlation component with CCA and adds multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluation uses a publicly available web portal and shows the proposed methods outperform several baselines in some settings.
- Paper is well-written, documents a significant body of work, and provides meticulous documentation.
- Main weakness: prioritizes breadth over depth, lacking deeper analysis of results and design choices.
- Requests more discussion on conflicting MultiCluster performance between the 59- and 12-language setups.
- Requests analysis of the impact of estimation parameters and design choices in both MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Practical contributions (improved QVEC and web portal) are valuable, but the paper is less insightful on fundamental research questions.
- Suggests including monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results to support the improvement claim.
- Minor wording issue: “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” is misleading about dataset types.
- Formatting issue: two equations extend beyond the column margin on page 3.
- Citation inconsistency: MultiSkip baseline citations (Coulmance et al., Guo et al.) differ from Section 2.3’s Luong et al. reference and need clarification.
- Related-work omissions: recommends citing Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013), Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014), and Diab & Resnik (2002).
