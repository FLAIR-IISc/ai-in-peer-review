- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an improved supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC's correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.
- Releases a publicly available evaluation suite and web portal for multilingual embeddings.
- Empirical evaluation across many tasks/languages shows the proposed methods outperform baselines in some scenarios.
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly documented, and represents substantial, timely work.
- Main contributions: evaluation suite/web portal, demonstration of dictionary-based methods (notably MultiCCA) at scale, and extensive empirical comparisons.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including intrinsic and extrinsic tasks and many languages.
- Strength: improved metric (multiQVEC-CCA) that correlates better with downstream tasks.
- Strength: practical evidence that dictionary-based approaches can be effective, especially with many languages.
- Weakness: analysis feels superficial and prioritizes breadth over depth.
- Weakness: limited novelty in individual algorithmic components (relies on established methods like CCA and skip-gram).
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis and qualitative examination of where methods fail.
- Request: provide correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA.
- Request: explain contrasting MultiCluster performance between 59- and 12-language setups and effects of cluster size/dictionary quality.
- Request: clarify which parts of MultiSkip derive from Luong et al., Coulmance et al., and Guo et al.
- Request: describe how vocabulary intersection was constructed and discuss potential evaluation bias from differing vocabularies.
- Minor comments: rephrase line 304 for clarity, fix equations exceeding margins, and consider citing additional related work (several cited papers).
