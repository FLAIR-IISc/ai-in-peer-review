This paper introduces two dictionary-based methods, MultiCluster and MultiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings. It also proposes an improved evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, building upon QVEC using canonical correlation analysis to address identified shortcomings. A key component of the work is the development of a web portal to facilitate evaluation and dissemination of multilingual embeddings and associated resources.

The primary contribution of this work is a comprehensive and readily accessible framework for evaluating multilingual embeddings. The multiQVEC-CCA metric demonstrably improves upon existing evaluation methods by addressing theoretical limitations and correlating better with downstream tasks. The accompanying web portal is a significant practical contribution, providing a valuable resource for researchers in the field by offering data, evaluation tools, and a platform for sharing results. A secondary, but still noteworthy, contribution lies in the implementation and comparison of dictionary-based embedding methods across a large number of languages (59), providing empirical insights into their performance.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel and Effective Evaluation Metric:** The development of multiQVEC-CCA is a strong point. Addressing the limitations of QVEC with CCA demonstrably improves correlation with downstream tasks (document classification and parsing), as shown in Table 1. This addresses a critical need for robust evaluation in multilingual embedding research.
2. **Practical and Accessible Resource:** The web portal is a highly valuable deliverable. Providing the data, code, and a unified evaluation platform significantly lowers the barrier to entry for researchers and promotes reproducibility. This fosters further research and accelerates progress in the field.
3. **Broad Empirical Evaluation:** The work evaluates the proposed methods and baselines across a substantial number of languages (59), providing a comprehensive assessment of their effectiveness in diverse linguistic contexts. This wide-ranging evaluation strengthens the validity of the findings.
4. **Clear and Well-Written Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly articulates the methodology and results. The organization is logical, and the presentation of technical details is understandable.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Depth of Analysis:** While the scope of the work is broad, the analysis of results lacks depth. The conflicting performance of MultiCluster across different language set sizes (59 vs. 12) warrants a more thorough investigation, including a discussion of the underlying reasons and potential mitigating strategies. Further exploration of the impact of key parameter settings and design choices within MultiCluster and MultiCCA would also be beneficial.
2. **Incomplete Reporting of Correlation Results:** The omission of correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA (as mentioned on line 326-328) hinders a complete understanding of the improvement offered by multiQVEC-CCA. Including these results would provide a more comprehensive comparison and strengthen the justification for the proposed method.
3. **Clarification Needed Regarding Baselines:** The relationship between Coulmance et al. and Luong et al. as they relate to the MultiSkip baseline is unclear. Section 2.3 only mentions Luong et al., while lines 121 and 147 cite both. A clear explanation of the implementation details and connections between these works is required.
4. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:** The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the types of errors made by the different embedding methods. This could reveal specific strengths and weaknesses of each approach and inform future research directions.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide the correlation results for the monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA metrics, as referenced on lines 326-328?
2. Please clarify the relationship between the MultiSkip baseline and the work of both Luong et al. and Coulmance et al. Specifically, which implementation details are used from each?
3. What specific factors contribute to the performance difference observed between MultiCluster when trained on 59 languages versus 12 languages? Can you elaborate on potential reasons for this discrepancy and any steps taken to address it?
4. Could you provide further details regarding the choice of the threshold τ = 0.1 in the dictionary creation process, and whether other values were investigated?

**Additional Comments:**

* Line 304: "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" could be rephrased to "different cross-lingual word similarity datasets" as they all share the same general methodology.
* Pages 3: The equations exceed the column margin and should be formatted for better readability.
* The paper would benefit from a more extensive discussion of related work, including the cited papers by Chandar et al., Hill et al., Lu et al., Faruqui & Dyer (2013), Suster et al., Guo et al. (2014), and Diab & Resnik.
