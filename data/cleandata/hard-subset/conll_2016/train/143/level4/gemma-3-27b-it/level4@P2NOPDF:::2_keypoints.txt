- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-inspired) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, an enhanced supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC’s correlation with CCA and extending to multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluates methods across diverse tasks via a publicly available web portal, showing gains over several baselines in some settings.
- Paper is well-written, documents substantial research effort, and provides a valuable, well-documented web portal.
- Representation learning and evaluation approaches are relevant and timely.
- Main weakness: paper favors breadth over depth, with result discussion feeling superficial.
- Requests deeper analysis of why MultiCluster performs differently in 59-language vs 12-language setups.
- Calls for more detailed investigation of parameter choices and implementation decisions in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Notes limited new theoretical insight despite high practical value (QVEC-CCA and portal).
- Suggests adding monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results (as in Table 1) to support improvement claims.
- Recommends clarifying wording about the “combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” to note they differ only by language.
- Points out inconsistent attribution for the MultiSkip baseline across sections; requests clarification of citations and correspondence.
- Advises expanding related work to include additional relevant papers on multilingual embeddings, clustering, multilingual training, and translational context (lists several specific references).
