- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA) and a supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC’s correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluated on many tasks via the authors’ web portal; in some cases the proposed methods outperform two baselines.
- Paper is well written and meticulously documented.
- Main critique: paper favors breadth over depth and lacks deeper analysis of results.
- Requests explanation for conflicting MultiCluster performance between the 59- and 12-language setups.
- Requests analysis of the effect of estimation parameters and methodological choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Suggests including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to support the claimed improvement.
- Minor wording issue: “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” may misleadingly imply heterogeneous dataset types rather than language variants.
- Minor formatting issue: two equations on page 3 exceed the column margin.
- Minor clarification needed: inconsistent attribution for the MultiSkip baseline (Coulmance/Guo cited in lines 121/147, but section 2.3 cites Luong).
- Missing related-work references on multilingual embeddings and clustering: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013).
- Missing related-work references on multilingual training for improving source-language embeddings: Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014).
- Suggests including broader translational-context work such as Diab & Resnik (2002).
