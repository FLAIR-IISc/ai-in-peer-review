- Paper proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, an improved supersense similarity measure replacing QVEC’s correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluation is comprehensive, using a publicly accessible web portal developed by the authors.
- Methods outperform several baselines in specific scenarios.
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly documented, and provides a useful evaluation platform.
- Main critique: paper favors breadth over depth and lacks deeper analysis of underlying research questions.
- Request for more thorough discussion of results, especially MultiCluster’s differing performance in 59-language vs. 12-language setups.
- Need deeper analysis of the impact of parameter choices and design decisions in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Suggest including monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results to justify claimed improvements.
- Recommend rephrasing dataset description to avoid implying heterogeneous dataset types.
- Ask for clarification of MultiSkip baseline citations and the specific implementation used.
- Recommend expanding Related Work to include several specific papers (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013; Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014; Diab & Resnik 2002).
- Formatting issue: equations exceed column margins and should be adjusted.
- Overall: valuable contribution; addressing the above suggestions would strengthen the paper’s impact.
