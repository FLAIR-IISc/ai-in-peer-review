- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (dictionary-driven multilingual clusters → cluster embeddings).
- multiCCA extends Faruqui & Dyer’s bilingual CCA approach to multilingual space using English as an anchor via bilingual dictionaries.
- multiSkip generalizes Luong et al.’s source/target context skip-gram idea to multilingual settings by combining objectives across parallel corpora.
- Translation-invariance uses low-rank decomposition of the PMI matrix with bilingual alignment terms, but may be limited to bilingual embeddings.
- Evaluation adapts QVEC via CCA to align embeddings with linguistic (super-sense) annotations, producing rotation-/linear-invariant scores and extended to multilingual evaluation.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Major concern: unclear novelty relative to Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance method—need explicit statement if the main contribution is the multilingual extension.
- Major concern: super-sense annotation sparsity across languages—intersection of features may be tiny; authors need to address this beyond footnote 9.
- Major concern: coverage effects on Table 2 are not analyzed—similar extrinsic scores despite differing coverage (e.g., multiCluster vs. multiCCA) need explanation.
- Major concern: Table 3 results are inconsistent—multilingual methods often don’t beat baselines; translation-invariance wins translation metric by a large margin.
- Major concern: surprising that multiCluster (which discards inter-cluster cross-lingual semantics) performs best on extrinsic tasks—needs analysis.
- Question: what performance loss/gain results from fixing embeddings in dependency parsing vs. random embeddings in the LSTM parser?
- Question: is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?
- Question: are there advantages of multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings first and then applying multiCCA to align them?
- Question: dictionary extraction (from alignments or GT) may not reflect real-world lexicons—did authors evaluate with existing curated multilingual dictionaries?
