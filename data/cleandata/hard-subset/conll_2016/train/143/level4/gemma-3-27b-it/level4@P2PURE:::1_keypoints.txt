- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation-invariance) and a modified QVEC metric using CCA for multilingual evaluation.  
- The manuscript is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Novelty is unclear: authors must state what is new over Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance work and whether the multilingual extension is the main contribution.  
- Super-sense annotations across languages can drastically reduce shared features; the brief footnote treatment is insufficient and mitigation strategies should be discussed.  
- Coverage effects on reported scores (Table 2) are unclear—e.g., multiCluster and multiCCA have very different coverage but similar dependency parsing scores.  
- Table 3 results are inconsistent: multilingual embeddings rarely beat baselines on intrinsic metrics, while translation-invariance performs best by a large margin, contrary to the paper’s stated goals.  
- It is surprising that multiCluster performs strongly on extrinsic tasks despite discarding inter-cluster semantic relations; this needs explanation.  
- Clarify whether the translation-invariance method is practically limited to bilingual embeddings or properly extends to multilingual settings.  
- Question: how much does fixing pretrained embeddings hurt dependency parsing, and what improvement do these embeddings give over random embeddings in the LSTM parser?  
- Question: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Question: What are the advantages of multiSkip compared to training bilingual embeddings and then applying multi-CCA to align them?  
- Question: The dictionary extraction via alignments or Google Translate may not reflect real-world lexicons—did the authors evaluate with existing manually curated multilingual dictionaries?
