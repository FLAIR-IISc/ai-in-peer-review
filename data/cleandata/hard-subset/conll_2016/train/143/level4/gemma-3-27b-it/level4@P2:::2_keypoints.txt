- Introduces two dictionary-based methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA), an improved evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web portal for multilingual embeddings and resources.
- multiQVEC-CCA addresses QVEC shortcomings using CCA and shows better correlation with downstream tasks.
- The web portal provides data, code, unified evaluation tools, and a platform for sharing results, improving accessibility and reproducibility.
- Empirical evaluation spans 59 languages, offering broad comparative insights into dictionary-based embedding methods.
- Paper is generally clear, well-written, and logically organized.
- Analysis lacks depth: conflicting MultiCluster performance (59 vs. 12 languages) needs deeper investigation and discussion of parameter/design choices.
- Missing reported correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA limits assessment of multiQVEC-CCA’s improvement.
- Unclear relationship and implementation details between the MultiSkip baseline and works by Luong et al. and Coulmance et al.; clarification needed.
- Limited error analysis; more detailed examination of method-specific errors would be valuable.
- Request to provide monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results.
- Request clarification on which implementation details for MultiSkip are adopted from Luong et al. vs. Coulmance et al.
- Request explanation for why MultiCluster performs differently when trained on 59 vs. 12 languages and any mitigation steps.
- Request justification for the dictionary threshold τ = 0.1 and whether alternative values were tested.
- Minor editorial suggestions: rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets,” fix equations that exceed column margins, and expand the related work discussion (several cited papers).
