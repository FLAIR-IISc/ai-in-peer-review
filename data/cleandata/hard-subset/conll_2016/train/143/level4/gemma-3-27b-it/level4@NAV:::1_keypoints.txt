- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation invariance) and a modified QVEC metric for evaluation.  
- multiCluster: uses bilingual dictionaries to form multilingual clusters and represents words by cluster embeddings.  
- multiCCA: projects monolingual embeddings into English anchor space via bilingual dictionaries (extension of Faruqui & Dyer).  
- multiSkip: generalizes alignment-based skip-gram objectives to multilingual settings using all parallel corpora.  
- Translation invariance: low-rank decomposition of PMI with bilingual alignment components, with unclear multilingual extension and potential bilingual limitation.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly explained.  
- Concern: novelty and multilingual extension of the Translation invariance method need clearer differentiation from Gardner et al.  
- Concern: cross-lingual super-sense annotations may suffer severe feature sparsity across languages; proposed mitigation needs more detail beyond footnote 9.  
- Concern: coverage effects on Table 2 scores should be analyzed, since multiCluster and multiCCA score similarly despite large coverage differences in parsing.  
- Concern: Table 3 results are inconclusive—multilingual methods seldom beat baselines; unexpected strong performance of invariance for word translation and multiCluster on extrinsic tasks needs explanation.  
- Clarification requested: trade-offs of fixing embeddings during dependency parsing and benefits versus random initialization in the LSTM parser.  
- Clarification requested: whether Table 1 reports an average over the 17 embeddings from Section 5.1.  
- Clarification requested: advantages of multiSkip versus learning bilingual embeddings then using multiCCA for projection.  
- Clarification requested: limitations of using dictionaries from parallel corpora or Google Translate and whether real-world multilingual dictionaries were considered.
