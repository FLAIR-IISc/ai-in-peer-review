- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).  
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure (QVEC-CCA) that replaces QVEC’s correlation component with CCA and extends evaluation to multilingual settings.  
- Provides comprehensive multi-task evaluation via a public web portal, showing competitive performance and outperforming some baselines in certain cases.  
- Paper is very well written, documents methods and experiments thoroughly, and addresses a relevant, timely problem.  
- Main critique: work emphasizes breadth over depth, lacking deeper analysis of results and design choices.  
- Requests more in-depth discussion of MultiCluster’s differing performance in the 59- vs. 12-language setups.  
- Requests detailed analysis of the impact of estimation parameters and design choices for MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- Finds practical contributions valuable (improved QVEC measure and web portal) but views novel research contributions as limited.  
- Suggests adding monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to support improvement claims.  
- Minor wording issue: “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” may be misleading about dataset types.  
- Formatting issue: several equations extend beyond the column margin.  
- Citation inconsistency: Lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip, but Section 2.3 only cites Luong et al.; clarify correspondences.  
- Recommends including additional related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).  
- Recommends citing work on multilingual training improving source-language embeddings (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014).  
- Suggests acknowledging translational context literature such as Diab & Resnik (2002).
