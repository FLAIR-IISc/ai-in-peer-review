- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).  
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure improving QVEC by replacing correlation with CCA and adding multilingual evaluation.  
- Provides a web portal and extensive evaluations showing the proposed methods outperform two baselines in some settings.  
- Paper is well-written, documented, and represents a substantial, timely body of work with high practical value.  
- Main critique: paper emphasizes breadth over depth and lacks deeper analysis of results and underlying causes.  
- Requests deeper discussion on conflicting MultiCluster performance between 59- and 12-language setups.  
- Requests analysis of the effects of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- Suggests including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to support claims of improvement.  
- Minor wording issue: line 304’s phrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” is misleading.  
- Layout problem: two equations on page 3 exceed the column margin.  
- Citation inconsistency: lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip while section 2.3 cites only Luong et al.; clarify correspondence.  
- Recommends adding related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (several specific papers provided).  
- Also suggests citing work on multilingual training for better source-language embeddings and translational context methods.
