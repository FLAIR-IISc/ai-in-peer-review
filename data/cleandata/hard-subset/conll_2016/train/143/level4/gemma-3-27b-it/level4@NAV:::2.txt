This paper introduces two dictionary-based methods for multilingual word embedding estimation – MultiCluster, based on clustering, and MultiCCA, utilizing canonical correlation analysis. Additionally, a novel supersense similarity measure is presented, refining QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation. The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation across various tasks via a publicly available web portal, demonstrating that their proposed methods achieve competitive performance, outperforming several baselines in certain scenarios.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly demonstrates a significant research effort. The proposed representation learning and evaluation techniques are highly relevant and timely, and the meticulous documentation is commendable. However, my overall impression is that the work prioritizes breadth over depth. I would have appreciated a more in-depth discussion of the results, particularly regarding the contrasting performance of MultiCluster in the 59- and 12-language setups, and a more detailed analysis of the impact of estimation parameters and design choices within MultiCluster and MultiCCA. Despite this, I believe the paper holds substantial practical value for both myself and the research community – particularly through the improved QVEC measure and the provided web portal – though its contribution to novel research questions answered feels somewhat limited.

Here are some specific suggestions for improvement:

Including the correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA (Table 1) would strengthen the claims regarding the improvement of QVEC-CCA, as stated in lines 326-328. 

Minor points:

* Line 304:  The phrasing "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" could be misinterpreted as implying datasets of different types, when they are in fact variations of the same dataset across different languages.
* Page 3: Several equations extend beyond the column margin.
* Lines 121 and 147 mention Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. in relation to the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 only cites Luong et al. Clarification of this correspondence is needed.

While the paper provides a reasonable overview of related work, several relevant publications could be included to provide a more complete context:

Multilingual embeddings and clustering:
Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013).

Multilingual training of embeddings for improved source-language embeddings:
Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014).

More broadly, the utilization of translational context, as explored in Diab & Resnik (2002), could also be acknowledged.
