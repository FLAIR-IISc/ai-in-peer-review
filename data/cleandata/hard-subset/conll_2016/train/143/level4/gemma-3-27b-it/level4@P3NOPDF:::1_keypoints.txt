- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation Invariance) and a rotation/translation-invariant QVEC/CCA evaluation; paper is clearly written.  
- The novelty and specific contribution of the Translation Invariance approach relative to Gardner et al. are not clearly highlighted.  
- Super-sense annotation usage likely suffers from multilingual data sparsity; the paper needs concrete mitigation strategies and analysis beyond a footnote.  
- Table 2 results lack analysis of coverage effects—it's unclear why multiCluster and multiCCA yield similar dependency-parsing scores despite very different coverage.  
- Table 3 results are inconsistent: multilingual methods do not uniformly outperform baselines; Translation Invariance underwhelms for word-translation goals.  
- The strong extrinsic performance of multiCluster is puzzling given it ignores inter-cluster semantic information and requires explanation.  
- For dependency parsing, the paper should quantify performance loss from fixing pretrained embeddings and the gain from replacing random initialization with these embeddings.  
- Clarify Table 1 aggregation: is it an average over the 17 embeddings in Section 5.1, and how was aggregation computed?  
- Explain advantages of multiSkip compared to learning bilingual embeddings then applying multiCCA for projection.  
- The dictionary extraction (parallel alignments/Google Translate) may not reflect real multilingual lexicons; evaluate or compare with curated multilingual dictionaries.
