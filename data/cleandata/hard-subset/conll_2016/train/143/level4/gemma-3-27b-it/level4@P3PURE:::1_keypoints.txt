- multiCluster: uses a dictionary to map words to multilingual clusters and derives cluster embeddings as word representations.  
- multiCCA: extends Faruqui & Dyer (2014) to multilingual settings by projecting monolingual embeddings into an English anchor space using bilingual dictionaries.  
- multiSkip: extends alignment-based embedding objectives (Luong et al.) to multiple languages by adding parallel-corpus alignment components.  
- Translation invariance: low-rank decomposition of the PMI matrix with bilingual alignment frequencies in the objective, potentially limited to bilingual embeddings.  
- Evaluation: modified QVEC/CCA metric aligning embeddings with linguistic data to produce rotation- and linear-invariant scores, extended to multilingual evaluation.  
- General: paper is well-written and clearly describes methods.  
- Major issue 1: Unclear novelty of the translation invariance approach relative to Gardner et al.; need clarification if multilingual extension is the novel contribution.  
- Major issue 2: Super-sense annotation overlap across languages may be too small (intersection problem); authors need to explain how this is handled beyond a footnote.  
- Major issue 3: Coverage effects on Table 2 are unclear—similar downstream scores arise despite very different coverage numbers for multiCluster and multiCCA.  
- Major issue 4: Table 3 is inconclusive—multilingual methods often do not beat baselines; translation invariance surprisingly best on word-translation, and multiCluster unexpectedly best on extrinsic metrics despite discarding inter-cluster semantics.  
- Question 1: How much performance change results from fixing pretrained embeddings in the dependency parser versus random initialization?  
- Question 2: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Question 3: What advantages does multiSkip offer over training bilingual embeddings followed by multi-CCA projections?  
- Question 4: Did the authors evaluate using real-world multilingual dictionaries (instead of parallel-alignment or Google Translate–based extraction)?
