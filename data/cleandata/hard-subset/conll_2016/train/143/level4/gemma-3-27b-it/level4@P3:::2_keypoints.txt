- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA) and a new supersense similarity metric (multiQVEC-CCA) with a supporting web evaluation portal.  
- Evaluation across intrinsic and extrinsic tasks shows the proposed representations sometimes outperform baselines.  
- Paper is well-written and practically valuable, especially the improved QVEC metric and the publicly released evaluation portal.  
- Paper is broad in scope but lacks depth in analysis and does not present a clear narrative of novel research questions answered.  
- Primary contribution is the multiQVEC-CCA metric and the evaluation infrastructure; embedding methods offer limited novelty.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA addresses QVEC limitations and shows substantially higher correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation and a public web portal with data and trained embeddings enhance reproducibility.  
- Strength: Experimental setup is thorough, comparing multiple baselines and language sets (12 vs 59) and including intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.  
- Weakness: MultiCluster and MultiCCA build on existing techniques and don’t show consistent advantages across all tasks.  
- Weakness: Conflicting MultiCluster results between 12 and 59 languages raise concerns about robustness and scalability.  
- Weakness: Analysis is superficial; lacks deeper exploration of parameter impacts, ablation studies, and reasons for performance discrepancies.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—missing comparative results (monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA), unclear phrasing, formatting problems, and unclear citation relationships.  
- Question: Provide detailed analysis of why MultiCluster behaves differently when trained on 59 vs 12 languages and how to improve scalability/robustness.  
- Question: Clarify which algorithmic design choices in MultiCCA/MultiCluster most affect performance and whether ablation studies are possible.  
- Question: Justify using English supersense annotations for QVEC/QVEC-CCA and discuss limitations of single-language annotations for multilingual evaluation.  
- Additional: Include suggested related citations (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013; Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014; Diab & Resnik 2002).  
- Additional: Verify the web portal link is functional and ensure consistent citation formatting.
