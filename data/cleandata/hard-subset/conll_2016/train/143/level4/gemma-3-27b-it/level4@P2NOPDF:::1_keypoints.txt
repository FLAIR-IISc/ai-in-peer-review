- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation invariance) and a refined QVEC evaluation metric.  
- Evaluation uses CCA to derive rotation- and linear-transform-invariant scores and extends this to multilingual settings.  
- Paper is clearly written and techniques are well explained.  
- Novelty of the Translation invariance method (and its multilingual extension) is unclear and should be compared to Gardner et al.  
- Cross-lingual super-sense annotations may suffer from feature sparsity due to small intersections across languages; need more detail on mitigation.  
- Coverage effects on Table 2 need investigation—similar dependency parsing scores for multiCluster and multiCCA despite large coverage differences require explanation.  
- Table 3 results are inconclusive: multilingual methods often fail to beat baselines on intrinsic metrics, and Translation invariance unexpectedly outperforms others on word translation.  
- multiCluster’s strong extrinsic performance is counterintuitive given it discards inter-cluster semantic information and needs discussion.  
- Request for clarification: impact of fixing word embeddings on dependency parsing performance and gains from replacing random embeddings with the proposed embeddings in the LSTM parser.  
- Request for clarification: whether Table 1 reports averages across all 17 embeddings from Section 5.1.  
- Request for clarification: what advantages multiSkip offers over training bilingual embeddings then applying multiCCA for cross-space projections.  
- Request for clarification: how dictionaries were extracted and whether manually curated multilingual lexicons were evaluated instead of (or in addition to) alignments/Google Translate.
