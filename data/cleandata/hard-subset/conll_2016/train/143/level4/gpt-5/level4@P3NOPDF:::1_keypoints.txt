- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation-invariance) and a rotation-invariant CCA-based QVEC evaluation metric with a multilingual extension.

- multiCluster: clusters translationally equivalent words via bilingual dictionaries and shares a single cluster embedding.

- multiCCA: projects non-English languages linearly into an English anchor space using bilingual CCA projections.

- multiSkip: multilingual extension of skip-gram that incorporates aligned source/target context across parallel corpora.

- Translation-invariance: factorizes a word-PMI matrix with bilingual alignment frequency terms; seems primarily bilingual-focused.

- Strength: Clear unified presentation of multiple multilingual methods.

- Strength: Proposes a rotation- and linear-invariant CCA-based intrinsic evaluation metric and extends it to multilingual settings.

- Strength: Broad empirical evaluation on both intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.

- Major concern: Novelty unclear relative to prior translation-invariance work — need explicit statement of what is new for multilingual extension (objective, constraints, training regime, theoretical/empirical properties).

- Major concern: Reliance on cross-lingual supersense annotations risks severe feature sparsity; need methods and sensitivity analysis to mitigate/interrogate sparsity.

- Major concern: Coverage differences across methods may explain similar extrinsic scores; request controlled analyses quantifying impact of coverage (intersection-only results, coverage-bucketed performance, ablations).

- Major concern: Inconsistent empirical story — intrinsic metrics often do not favor multilingual techniques, translation-invariance dominates translation task, and coarse multiCluster performs well extrinsically; request explanations (domain/frequency/morphology effects) and significance testing.

- Request: Report parsing results with frozen vs fine-tuned embeddings and compare initialization benefits vs random for LSTM parser.

- Request: Clarify Table 1 aggregation protocol (which embeddings averaged, weighting, per-language variances).

- Request: Discuss empirical/practical trade-offs of multiSkip vs pipeline of bilingual training + multiCCA projection (data, compute, robustness, downstream performance).

- Request: Discuss dictionary realism and robustness — tests with curated lexicons (PanLex, Wiktionary) or expected sensitivity to dictionary quality/coverage.

- Request: Provide per-language-pair resource details: dictionary sizes, evaluation coverage, parallel corpus sizes, alignment quality, OOV rates.

- Request: Give construction details of the multilingual linguistic matrix for QVEC: how features are shared/aligned, handling of language-specific features, per-language correlations and variances.

- Request: Include statistical significance testing (across runs or bootstrapped samples) for intrinsic and extrinsic results.

- Overall: Important and clearly written work with a valuable evaluation contribution, but needs stronger empirical analysis and clarification of methodological details to resolve mixed findings.
