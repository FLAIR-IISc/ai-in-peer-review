- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA) and QVEC-CCA, a CCA-based supersense intrinsic evaluator, with broad evaluation and public portal.  
- Strength: Clear problem setting; MultiCluster and MultiCCA are simple, reproducible, and practically useful.  
- Strength: QVEC-CCA sensibly improves QVEC by addressing correlation issues and enabling multilingual evaluation.  
- Strength: The evaluation portal and thorough documentation are valuable community resources.  
- Strength: Broad empirical evaluation across many languages and tasks.  

- Main concern: Paper favors breadth over depth and often lacks explanation for when/why methods work or fail.  
- Need analysis of MultiCluster’s conflicting outcomes in 59- vs 12-language setups (e.g., language-family makeup, dictionary coverage, homonymy/polysemy, corpus differences).  
- Require sensitivity analyses for key estimation choices (dictionary size/quality, clustering criteria, CCA dimensionality/regularization, frequency thresholds) with ablations and learning curves.  
- Assess stability across language families and scripts to determine whether gains are uniform or concentrated in particular typological groups.  
- Increase statistical rigor: report confidence intervals, significance tests, and effect sizes for key comparisons.  
- QVEC-CCA evaluation: include monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results to substantiate claimed improvements.  
- Clarify baselines/prior work provenance (Coulmance, Guo, Luong) and specify exactly what MultiSkip implementation and deviations were used.  
- Provide hypotheses or diagnostics explaining task/language-specific “wins” vs “losses” (e.g., alignment quality, domain mismatch, vocabulary coverage).  

- Minor: Rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” if tasks are homogeneous across languages.  
- Minor: Fix formatting where two equations exceed the column margin.  
- Minor: Add more reproducibility details (hyperparameters, preprocessing, dictionary filtering rules) in appendix/portal.  

- Related work suggested for citation: Chandar et al. (2014), Hill et al. (2014), Lu et al. (2015), Faruqui & Dyer (2013); Suster et al. (2016), Guo et al. (2014); Diab & Resnik (2002).  
- Overall: Strong, timely practical contributions and documentation; main weakness is limited analytical depth—addressing diagnostics, sensitivity analyses, clearer baselines, and monolingual QVEC comparisons would substantially strengthen impact.
