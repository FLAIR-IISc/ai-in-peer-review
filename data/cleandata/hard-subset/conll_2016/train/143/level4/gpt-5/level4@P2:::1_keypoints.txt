- Paper studies four multilingual embedding estimators (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation-invariance MF) and introduces multiQVEC / multiQVEC-CCA plus a public evaluation portal.

- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA is a basis-invariant intrinsic metric that correlates better with downstream tasks than common intrinsic metrics.

- Contribution: Dictionary-based estimator multiCCA scales to 59 languages without parallel text and shows strong coverage/performance.

- Contribution: Public benchmark, code, data, and trained models to standardize multilingual intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation.

- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA (and multiQVEC) show substantially higher correlation with downstream performance than word similarity or translation metrics.

- Strength: Dictionary-based methods are practical and scalable, enabling training for many languages without parallel corpora.

- Strength: Broad experimental design covering multiple intrinsic metrics and two extrinsic tasks, with attention to coverage effects.

- Strength: Released web portal and resources improve reproducibility and future comparisons.

- Weakness: Novelty unclear relative to Gardner et al. (2015) translation-invariance approach; paper doesn’t detail what is new for multilingual extension.

- Weakness: Reliance on supersense annotations for the linguistic matrix raises coverage and cross-lingual consistency concerns that may bias intrinsic scores.

- Weakness: Coverage differences confound comparisons (e.g., multiCluster vs multiCCA in parsing); evaluations should normalize or ablate for coverage effects.

- Weakness: Mixed results in Table 3 — translation-invariance is best on word translation, while multiCluster wins extrinsic tasks, requiring deeper analysis of when cluster conflation helps.

- Weakness: multiSkip baseline is disadvantaged by not using monolingual corpora, making comparisons with parallel-data methods less clear.

- Question: What, if any, specific novelty or multilingual extension was introduced for the translation-invariance (Gardner et al.) baseline?

- Question: How sensitive are multiQVEC / multiQVEC-CCA correlations to the size and cross-lingual consistency of the supersense matrix, and were alternatives (universal POS, harmonized guidelines) tried?

- Question: Can you quantify coverage effects on dependency parsing (e.g., masking to equalize coverage or controlled ablations)?

- Question: In parsing, what is the performance difference when embeddings are frozen vs fine-tuned, and what gain comes from initializing with these embeddings vs random under the no-POS/morphology setup?

- Question: For Table 1 correlations, were coefficients computed across the 17 systems (one point per system per metric), and could outliers be affecting results?

- Question: Are there practical advantages of training a multilingual skip-gram model vs training bilingual embeddings and aligning via multiCCA (compute cost, coverage, ease of adding languages)?

- Question: Beyond alignments and Google Translate, were curated bilingual lexicons evaluated, and how do dictionary noise/domain mismatch affect multiCCA/multiCluster performance and coverage?
