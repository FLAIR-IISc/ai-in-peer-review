- Proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (cluster translational equivalents; one embedding per cluster).
- multiCCA: project each language into English embedding space using bilingual dictionaries (multilingual extension of Faruqui & Dyer).
- multiSkip: generalizes skip-gram with cross-lingual alignment contexts across multiple parallel corpora.
- Translation-invariance: factorizes PMI with bilingual alignment terms; appears limited to bilingual embeddings.
- Proposes a modified QVEC using CCA to produce rotation-invariant correlations between embeddings and linguistic features, extended to multilingual evaluation.
- Paper is clearly written and methods are well described.
- Major concern: unclear novelty over Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance embeddings—need to state what is technically new beyond a straightforward multilingual generalization.
- Major concern: supersense feature sparsity across languages may yield a tiny intersection of shared features—need mitigation and justification for cross-lingual comparability.
- Major concern: coverage differences (e.g., in Table 2) may affect scores—require quantification or normalization to match coverage levels.
- Major concern: inconsistent intrinsic vs extrinsic results (Table 3), notably translation-invariance beating others for translation and multiCluster excelling extrinsically despite collapsing distinctions—need analysis reconciling these findings.
- Question: quantify impact of fixing vs fine-tuning embeddings in dependency parsing and the gain from pretrained embeddings versus random initialization.
- Question: clarify how Table 1 is aggregated—whether it averages the 17 embeddings from §5.1 or uses another protocol.
- Question: compare practical/performance trade-offs of training multiSkip directly on parallel corpora versus training bilingual embeddings then aligning via multiCCA (accuracy, data, cost).
- Question: realism of dictionaries—did experiments use curated multilingual dictionaries or only aligner/Google Translate-derived ones, and how robust are methods to noisy/limited lexicons?
- Summary: useful set of multilingual methods and invariant evaluation, but needs clarification on translation-invariance novelty, coverage and sparsity issues, and explanation for intrinsic/extrinsic mismatches.
