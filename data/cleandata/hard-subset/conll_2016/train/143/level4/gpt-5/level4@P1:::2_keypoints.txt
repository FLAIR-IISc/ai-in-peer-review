- Paper introduces MultiCluster (shared vectors for translational equivalents) and MultiCCA (CCA projection into English-pivot space) for multilingual embeddings and proposes multiQVEC-CCA as an improved intrinsic evaluation metric.
- Contributions: improved intrinsic metrics (multiQVEC / multiQVEC-CCA), two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods, and released evaluation portal, datasets, and trained models across 59 languages.

- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA is well motivated and shows higher correlation with downstream tasks than common intrinsic metrics.
- Strength: Evaluation portal, datasets, and trained embeddings improve reproducibility and practical impact.
- Strength: Broad empirical evaluation across nine metrics, multiple language settings (12 and 59 languages), and diverse baselines, with attention to coverage reporting.
- Strength: Clear exposition and thorough documentation with reasonable training details and thoughtful discussion of evaluation goals.
- Strength: Methods are scalable and resource-efficient, leveraging monolingual corpora and bilingual lexicons without parallel data.

- Weakness: Paper favors breadth over depth, leaving key analyses underexplored, especially discrepancies between 12- and 59-language MultiCluster results.
- Weakness: Sensitivity analyses for MultiCluster and MultiCCA (graph construction, thresholds, cluster size, pivot choice, training params) are missing.
- Weakness: Table 1 omits correlations for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA, weakening the empirical claim that QVEC-CCA improves QVEC.
- Weakness: Baseline description (multiSkip) and its relation to Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. is unclear and needs reconciliation.
- Weakness: Related work is incomplete; several relevant papers on multilingual embeddings and cross-lingual clustering are not discussed.
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues (equations overflowing margins; ambiguous phrasing about datasets).

- Question: Please analyze why MultiCluster performs well on 12 languages but degrades on 59 languages (e.g., dictionary noise, cluster connectivity, max cluster size).
- Question: How sensitive are MultiCluster and MultiCCA to hyperparameters and design choices (τ, window size, epochs, pivot language)?
- Question: Clarify the exact implementation and differences of the multiSkip baseline versus Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al., including monolingual data use.
- Question: Add correlations for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA to Table 1 to substantiate QVEC-CCA’s improvements.

- Minor: Rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” to avoid implying dataset type differences.
- Minor: Fix formatting so equations do not exceed column margins.
- Minor: Reconcile citations and naming of the multiSkip baseline across referenced works and §2.3.

- Additional refs: Several relevant works (e.g., bilingual autoencoders, translation-based embedding methods, bilingual clustering and sense modeling) should be cited to better situate the paper.

- Overall: Strong and timely paper with practical contributions and solid methods, but would benefit from deeper empirical analysis and clarifications on baselines and omitted correlations.
