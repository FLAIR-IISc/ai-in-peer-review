- Paper introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), two intrinsic metrics (multiQVEC, multiQVEC-CCA), a public evaluation portal, and large-scale experiments on 59 languages (12-language focused comparisons where parallel data exist).

- Main contributions: a better-correlated intrinsic evaluation framework and portal; scalable dictionary-based embedding methods that avoid parallel corpora; an empirical study of intrinsic–extrinsic metric correlations.

- Strength: Demonstrates that conventional intrinsic metrics correlate weakly with downstream tasks, while multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA show substantially better correlation.

- Strength: Presents practical, scalable methods (especially multiCCA) that train embeddings for 59 languages using monolingual data plus bilingual dictionaries with strong coverage and performance.

- Strength: Provides reproducibility resources (web portal, shared benchmarks) and a clear experimental setup.

- Strength: Broad empirical comparison across four estimation methods, multiple intrinsic/extrinsic metrics, and reporting of coverage alongside scores.

- Strength: Paper is well written and technically accessible.

- Weakness: Analysis favors breadth over depth; limited investigation into why multiCluster is competitive on 12 languages but much weaker on 59 (attributed briefly to ambiguity).

- Weakness: Use of Google Translate dictionaries for 47 languages in both training and evaluation risks dictionary noise and evaluation leakage; dictionary quality and impact are not quantified.

- Weakness: Table 1 omits monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlations, leaving incomplete intrinsic–extrinsic comparison.

- Weakness: Baseline clarity issue: multiSkip is described inconsistently across citations and the implemented variant uses parallel data, needing precise description and differentiation from prior work.

- Weakness: Coverage confounds remain since differing vocabularies influence intrinsic comparisons and key intersection-of-vocabulary evaluations are only in the supplement.

- Question: Provide ablations for multiCluster on maximum cluster size, dictionary threshold τ, dictionary source (Europarl vs. Google Translate), and monolingual corpus size to explain performance drop at 59 languages.

- Question: How sensitive is multiCCA to the pivot language choice, and were other high-resource pivots or multi-pivot CCA tried?

- Question: Are there manual quality estimates or spot checks for the Google Translate dictionaries, and were any filtering/consistency checks applied beyond excluding identical/multiword translations?

- Question: In downstream tasks (e.g., document classification), were embeddings fixed or fine-tuned, were dimensionality/preprocessing identical across methods, and were hyperparameters tuned equivalently?

- Question: Can monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA be added to Table 1, and why does multiQVEC-CCA correlate less with parsing than multiQVEC?

- Question: Given supersense availability for only en/da/it, how will multiQVEC(-CCA) scale—plans to expand annotations or use alternative resources (e.g., universal POS)?

- Minor: Clarify that “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” refers to multiple same-type datasets across language pairs.

- Minor: Fix two equations that exceed column margins.

- Minor: Harmonize multiSkip description and citations (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Luong et al.) and specify exactly which variant was implemented.

- Minor: Consider adding related work (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013; Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014; Diab & Resnik 2002).

- Minor: Ensure the portal and datasets remain accessible post-review and provide guidance for reproducing RCV-based experiments given licensing constraints.

- Overall: Strong, timely, and well-executed paper with practical method and evaluation contributions; recommend acceptance after addressing depth/clarity issues, especially multiCluster analysis, dictionary quality, and completeness of correlation results.
