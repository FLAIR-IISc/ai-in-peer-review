This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings: one clustering-motivated approach (MultiCluster) and another based on canonical correlation analysis (MultiCCA). In addition, it introduces a supersense similarity measure that extends QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and by incorporating multilingual evaluation. The evaluation spans a wide range of tasks using the authors’ web portal, and the results show that in some cases the proposed representations outperform two baselines.

The paper is very well written and represents substantial work. The proposed representation-learning and evaluation methods are timely, and the documentation is meticulous. I also value the practical contributions, notably the improved QVEC-style measure and the public web portal.

That said, the paper favors breadth over depth. I would have liked a more thorough discussion of the results, in particular:
- The conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups.
- The effects of estimation parameters and design decisions in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.

As a result, while the work is of clear practical value to me and the community, I learned relatively little in terms of research questions that are posed and answered by the study.

Concrete remarks:
- It would be useful to include correlation results (Table 1) for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA as well, especially since lines 326–328 state that QVEC-CCA improves on QVEC.
- Lines 121 and 147 mention Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. when referring to the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 mentions only Luong et al. Please clarify the correspondence among these works.
- Line 304: “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” suggests heterogeneous datasets; if they are of the same type across different languages, please rephrase to avoid that implication.
- Page 3: two equations exceed the column margin; please fix the formatting.
