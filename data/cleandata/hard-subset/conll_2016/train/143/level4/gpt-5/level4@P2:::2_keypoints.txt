- Primary contribution: multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation replacing QVEC alignment-and-sum with CCA and extended to multilingual embeddings, shows higher correlation with downstream tasks.
- Secondary contribution: Two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) that avoid parallel corpora and scale to 59 languages.
- Tertiary contribution: Public evaluation portal, code, and data splits to standardize intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA correlates more consistently with downstream tasks than common intrinsic metrics, addressing intrinsic–extrinsic gaps.
- Strength: multiCCA and multiCluster are resource-efficient and scalable, useful for low-resource languages.
- Strength: Broad, transparent evaluation comparing many methods across intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, with coverage reported alongside scores.
- Strength: The evaluation portal and documentation improve reproducibility and community utility.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why multiCluster degrades from 12 to 59 languages; lack of diagnosis for semantic conflation, cluster-size effects, dictionary noise, and imbalance.
- Weakness: Missing systematic sensitivity and ablation studies for key hyperparameters (τ, max cluster size, window size, hub choice, corpus size/quality).
- Weakness: Unclear baseline positioning—relationship among Coulmance, Guo, Luong, TransGram/BiSkip variants and which training signals are used is not well specified.
- Weakness: Evaluation confounds from differing vocabularies and dictionary quality; coverage and Google Translate noise may bias results, especially for clustering and translation tasks.
- Weakness: Presentation issues and incomplete reporting—monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlations with downstream tasks are not shown; minor formatting/clarity problems.
- Question: Why does multiCluster degrade with more languages, and can limiting cross-lingual connectivity or subclustering mitigate semantic conflation?
- Question: How sensitive are multiCluster and multiCCA to hyperparameters (τ, max cluster size, context window, negative sampling) and monolingual corpus quality?
- Question: What is the effect of using English as the hub in multiCCA; were alternative hubs or joint alignments tried and how do they affect non-English alignments?
- Question: Can authors report monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlations with downstream tasks alongside multilingual results?
- Question: For 59 languages, how noisy are Google Translate-derived dictionaries, what filtering was applied, and how does this affect translation and clustering outcomes?
- Question: For fairness under varying coverage, can main-text results include vocabulary intersections across methods and quantify rank changes?
- Minor: Clarify wording about cross-lingual word similarity datasets to avoid implying heterogeneity where pairs are of the same kind.
- Minor: Fix formatting overflow for two equations on p.3.
- Minor: Make correspondence explicit among cited baselines (Coulmance, Guo, Luong) regarding training signals and use of monolingual/parallel data.
- Suggestion: Cite related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational sense disambiguation (e.g., Chandar et al., Hill et al., Lu et al., Faruqui & Dyer, Suster et al., Guo et al., Diab & Resnik).
- Suggestion: Add a short data-quality analysis (precision/recall vs a gold subset) for dictionaries to improve interpretability.
- Overall assessment: Strong, practically valuable work with notable contributions to evaluation and resources, but would benefit from deeper analysis, clearer baselines, and fuller reporting.
