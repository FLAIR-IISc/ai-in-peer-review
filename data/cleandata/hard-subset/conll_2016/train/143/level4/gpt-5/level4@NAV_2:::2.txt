This paper presents two dictionary-based approaches for learning multilingual word embeddings, one grounded in clustering (MultiCluster) and the other in canonical correlation analysis (MultiCCA). It also introduces a supersense-based evaluation that improves upon QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and extending it to a multilingual setting. The authors evaluate across a broad suite of tasks using their web portal and show that, in some settings, the proposed representations outperform two baselines.

The paper is clearly written and reflects a substantial effort. The proposed representation-learning and evaluation techniques are timely, and the documentation is careful. My overall impression, however, is that the paper favors breadth over depth. I would have liked a more in-depth analysis of the results (e.g., the divergent behavior of MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language configurations; the impact of estimation choices and hyperparameters in MultiCluster/CCA). Thus, while the paper is of high practical value to me and the community (improved QVEC variant, web portal), I did not learn as much as I hoped in terms of posed and answered research questions.

Below are some more specific remarks.
It would be helpful to include the correlation results (Table 1) for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA as well, since lines 326–328 claim QVEC-CCA improves over QVEC.

Minor:
l. 304: “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” — this reads as if the datasets differ in nature, but they seem to be the same type across different language pairs, correct?
p. 3: two equations run past the column margin.
Lines 121 and 147 mention Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. when referring to the MultiSkip baseline, but §2.3 cites only Luong et al. Please clarify how these are connected.

While the related work is generally well covered, additional relevant works could be cited:
Multilingual embeddings and clustering:
Chandar A P, S., Lauly, S., Larochelle, H., Khapra, M. M., Ravindran, B., Raykar, V. C., and Saha, A. (2014). An autoencoder approach to learning bilingual word representations. NIPS.
Hill, F., Cho, K., Jean, S., Devin, C., and Bengio, Y. (2014). Embedding word similarity with neural machine translation. arXiv:1412.6448.
Lu, A., Wang, W., Bansal, M., Gimpel, K., and Livescu, K. (2015). Deep multilingual correlation for improved word embeddings. NAACL.
Faruqui, M., and Dyer, C. (2013). An Information Theoretic Approach to Bilingual Word Clustering. ACL.
Multilingual training of embeddings to improve source-language embeddings:
Suster, S., Titov, I., and van Noord, G. (2016). Bilingual learning of multi-sense embeddings with discrete autoencoders. NAACL-HLT.
Guo, J., Che, W., Wang, H., and Liu, T. (2014). Learning sense-specific word embeddings by exploiting bilingual resources. COLING.
More broadly, on translational context:
Diab, M., and Resnik, P. (2002). An unsupervised method for word sense tagging using parallel corpora. ACL.
