- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-motivated) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Introduces a supersense similarity measure that extends QVEC by using CCA and enabling multilingual evaluation.
- Evaluates methods across many tasks via the authors’ web portal, with proposed representations sometimes outperforming baselines.
- Paper is well written, substantial, timely, and meticulously documented.
- Provides useful practical contributions: improved QVEC-style measure and a public web portal.
- Paper favors breadth over depth and lacks deeper analysis of results.
- Needs explanation for conflicting MultiCluster outcomes between 59- and 12-language setups.
- Requires analysis of the effects of estimation parameters and design choices in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Reviewer felt the study did not clearly pose and answer focused research questions.
- Concrete suggestion: include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 to support the claimed improvement.
- Clarify correspondence among Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. when referring to the MultiSkip baseline.
- Reword “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” if datasets are homogeneous across languages to avoid implying heterogeneity.
- Fix formatting where two equations exceed the column margin.
- Suggested related work to cite on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).
- Suggested related work on multilingual training to improve source-language embeddings (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014).
- Suggested related work on translational context and unsupervised sense tagging (Diab & Resnik 2002).
