The paper introduces two dictionary-based approaches to learning multilingual word embeddings: a clustering-based method (MultiCluster) and a canonical correlation analysis method (MultiCCA). It also proposes a supersense-based similarity measure that extends QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and incorporating multilingual evaluation.

The methods are evaluated across a broad range of tasks via the authors’ web portal, and in several cases the proposed representations outperform two baselines.

The paper is clearly written and reflects substantial effort. The proposed representation-learning and evaluation methods are timely, and the documentation is meticulous. However, the work seems to favor breadth over depth. I would have liked a more thorough analysis of the results—e.g., an explanation of the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster in the 59- vs. 12-language setups, and a discussion of how estimation choices and hyperparameters affect MultiCluster and MultiCCA. Thus, while the paper offers high practical value (the improved QVEC measure and the web portal), I did not learn as much as I had hoped in terms of explicit research questions posed and answered.

Below are some more concrete remarks.
- It would be helpful to include the correlation results in Table 1 for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA as well, given that lines 326–328 claim QVEC-CCA improves over QVEC.

Minor:
- Line 304: “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” suggests heterogeneous datasets, whereas they appear to be of the same type in different languages—please clarify.
- Page 3: two equations exceed the column width.
- Lines 121 and 147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. when referring to the MultiSkip baseline, but Section 2.3 mentions only Luong et al. Please clarify the correspondence among these works and the exact baseline used.

While the paper generally does a reasonable job citing related work, additional relevant references could be included:
- Multilingual embeddings and clustering:
  Chandar A P, S., Lauly, S., Larochelle, H., Khapra, M. M., Ravindran, B., Raykar, V. C., and Saha, A. (2014). An autoencoder approach to learning bilingual word representations. In NIPS.
  Hill, F., Cho, K., Jean, S., Devin, C., and Bengio, Y. (2014). Embedding word similarity with neural machine translation. arXiv preprint arXiv:1412.6448.
  Lu, A., Wang, W., Bansal, M., Gimpel, K., & Livescu, K. (2015). Deep multilingual correlation for improved word embeddings. In NAACL.
  Faruqui, M., & Dyer, C. (2013). An Information Theoretic Approach to Bilingual Word Clustering. In ACL.
- Multilingual training of embeddings to improve source-language embeddings:
  Suster, S., Titov, I., and van Noord, G. (2016). Bilingual learning of multi-sense embeddings with discrete autoencoders. In NAACL-HLT.
  Guo, J., Che, W., Wang, H., and Liu, T. (2014). Learning sense-specific word embeddings by exploiting bilingual resources. In COLING.
- More broadly, work on translational context:
  Diab, M., & Resnik, P. (2002). An unsupervised method for word sense tagging using parallel corpora. In ACL.
