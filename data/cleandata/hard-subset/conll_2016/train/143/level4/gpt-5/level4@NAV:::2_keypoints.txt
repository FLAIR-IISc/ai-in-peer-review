- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-grounded) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).
- Proposes a supersense similarity metric (QVEC-CCA) that replaces QVEC’s correlation with CCA and adds multilingual evaluation.
- Provides broad evaluation across many tasks via the authors’ web portal.
- Results show the proposed representations outperform two baselines in some settings.
- Paper is very well written and thoroughly documented; methods and portal are practical and timely.
- Reviewers feel the work favors breadth over depth and lacks clear, focused research questions.
- Main missing analyses: conflicting MultiCluster results between 59- and 12-language setups.
- Missing sensitivity analysis on estimation choices and hyperparameters for MultiCluster and MultiCCA.
- Suggests including monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA (Table 1) to support the claim of improvement.
- Minor wording issue: line 304’s phrasing implies heterogeneous dataset types but they appear homogeneous across languages.
- Formatting issue: two equations extend beyond the column margin on page 3.
- Citation inconsistency: MultiSkip baseline references differ across lines/sections and need alignment (Coulmance, Guo, Luong et al.).
- Recommends additional relevant related-work citations on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context (several papers listed).
