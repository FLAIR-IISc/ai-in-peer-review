1. Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-based) and MultiCCA (CCA-based).  
2. Introduces a supersense-based evaluation that replaces QVEC's correlation with CCA and extends QVEC to multilingual settings.  
3. Evaluates methods across many tasks via a web portal and reports that the proposed representations outperform two baselines in some settings.  
4. Paper is clearly written, carefully documented, and of high practical value (improved QVEC variant, web portal).  
5. Main critique: the paper favors breadth over depth and lacks deeper analysis of results.  
6. Requests analysis of MultiCluster's divergent behavior between the 59- and 12-language configurations.  
7. Requests analysis of the impact of estimation choices and hyperparameters in MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
8. Asks to include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (Table 1) to support the claim that QVEC-CCA improves over QVEC.  
9. Minor wording issue: line 304's phrasing about “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” is ambiguous.  
10. Formatting issue: two equations run past the column margin on page 3.  
11. Citation/clarification needed: lines 121 and 147 reference Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for the MultiSkip baseline, but §2.3 cites only Luong et al.; clarify connections.  
12. Suggests adding related-work citations on multilingual embeddings and clustering (Chandar et al. 2014; Hill et al. 2014; Lu et al. 2015; Faruqui & Dyer 2013).  
13. Suggests adding citations on multilingual training to improve source-language embeddings (Suster et al. 2016; Guo et al. 2014).  
14. Suggests adding a citation on translational context methods (Diab & Resnik 2002).
