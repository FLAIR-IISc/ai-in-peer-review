- Paper proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA), a supersense-based intrinsic evaluation (QVEC-CCA), and a public evaluation portal; shows gains over baselines in some settings.
- Strength: clearly written, substantial effort, timely approaches and evaluation framework.
- Strength: meticulous documentation and a useful web portal plus an improved QVEC variant as resources.
- Concern: Conflicting MultiCluster results between 59-language and 12-language settings need explanation (e.g., dictionary coverage, language families, token frequency).
- Concern: Lack of sensitivity analysis for key choices (clustering granularity, normalization, dictionary size/quality, CCA dimensionality/regularization).
- Concern: Missing ablations to separate contributions from the dictionary, monolingual data, and multilingual signals.
- Recommendation: Provide deeper analysis to strengthen scientific insights beyond practical contributions.
- Evaluation measure: Table 1 should include monolingual QVEC vs. QVEC-CCA correlations to isolate the effect of CCA from multilingual evaluation.
- Clarity/minor: Rephrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” if datasets are homogeneous across languages.
- Clarity/minor: Fix two equations that exceed column margins.
- Clarity/minor: Clarify MultiSkip baseline attribution and which implementation/variant is used (relations among Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Luong et al.).
- Related work: Add citations on multilingual embeddings, clustering, multilingual training for source-language embeddings, and translational context (several suggested papers).
- Overall: Practical, well-engineered contributions that would benefit from expanded analysis, parameter sensitivity studies, clearer baseline lineage, and added monolingual QVEC comparisons to maximize impact.
