- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (clustering-inspired) and MultiCCA (canonical correlation analysis).
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a supersense-based evaluation measure that replaces QVEC’s correlation with CCA and supports multilingual evaluation.
- Provides a public web evaluation portal and evaluates models across a wide range of tasks, showing improvements over baselines in some settings.
- Paper is clearly written with careful documentation and useful community resources.
- Main weakness: prioritizes breadth over depth, lacking deeper analysis and definitive takeaways.
- Requests deeper analysis of conflicting results for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups.
- Requests more thorough discussion of estimation choices and hyperparameter effects for MultiCluster and MultiCCA (e.g., dictionary coverage, clustering criteria, CCA dimensionality, regularization, preprocessing).
- Suggests adding monolingual correlation results for QVEC and QVEC-CCA to Table 1 to substantiate the claim that QVEC-CCA improves upon QVEC.
- Minor wording/formatting issues: clarify phrasing about cross-lingual datasets (Line 304), fix equations that exceed column margin, and clarify MultiSkip citations/implementation.
- Notes missing related work that should be cited for broader context (lists several relevant papers on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context).
- Recommends a deeper error and sensitivity analysis (impact of dictionary coverage/quality, domain mismatch, noise in lexical resources).
- Urges clearer explanation for why MultiCluster diverges between large and small language setups (language coverage, domain mismatch, lexical resource noise).
- Encourages more explicit articulation of research questions and hypotheses and analyses that yield concrete, generalizable insights beyond task-wise performance.
