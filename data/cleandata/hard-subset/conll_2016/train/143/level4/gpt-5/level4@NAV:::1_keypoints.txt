- Paper introduces four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (dictionary-based clusters), multiCCA (English-anchored CCA projections), multiSkip (multilingual extension of skip-gram with alignments), and a translation-invariance PMI factorization (potentially bilingual-limited).  
- Evaluation uses a modified QVEC with CCA to align embeddings to linguistic resources, yielding rotation- and linear-invariant scores, extended to multilingual settings.  
- Paper is clearly written and methods are well explained.  
- Major issue 1: Unclear novelty relative to Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance approach; need clarification if the contribution is mainly the multilingual extension.  
- Major issue 2: Supersense annotations across languages may yield very small feature intersections; mitigation beyond footnote 9 is not explained.  
- Major issue 3: Scores in Table 2 may be sensitive to coverage differences (e.g., multiCluster vs multiCCA in dependency parsing); robustness to coverage is questioned.  
- Major issue 4: Inconsistent results—multilingual methods often not top on intrinsic metrics, translation-invariance dominates word-translation, and multiCluster unexpectedly excels on extrinsic tasks despite discarding inter-cluster semantics.  
- Other question 1: What is the performance drop when freezing embeddings in the dependency parser, and what gains from replacing random embeddings with these pretrained ones in the LSTM parser?  
- Other question 2: Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Other question 3: Are there benefits to multiSkip versus training bilingual embeddings and then applying multiCCA to align separate spaces?  
- Other question 4: Did the authors evaluate using real multilingual dictionaries instead of dictionaries induced from parallel data or Google Translate, which may not reflect real lexicon challenges?
