- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: clustering-based MultiCluster and CCA-based MultiCCA.  
- Proposes a supersense-based similarity measure (QVEC-CCA) that replaces QVEC’s correlation with CCA and adds multilingual evaluation.  
- Evaluates methods across many tasks via the authors’ web portal; in several cases the proposed representations outperform two baselines.  
- Paper is clearly written, meticulously documented, and timely, offering high practical value (improved QVEC and a web portal).  
- Reviewer feels the work favors breadth over depth and lacks sufficiently deep analysis and explicit research questions.  
- Requests explanation for conflicting MultiCluster results between the 59-language and 12-language setups.  
- Requests discussion of how estimation choices and hyperparameters affect MultiCluster and MultiCCA.  
- Concrete request: include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 to support the claim in lines 326–328.  
- Minor: clarify “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets,” which may misleadingly suggest heterogeneous datasets.  
- Minor: two equations on page 3 exceed the column width and need formatting fixes.  
- Minor: clarify citation correspondence and exact MultiSkip baseline used (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. are referenced inconsistently).  
- Recommends adding related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (several specific references provided).  
- Recommends adding related work on multilingual training to improve source-language embeddings (several specific references provided).  
- Recommends citing broader work on translational context (e.g., Diab & Resnik 2002).
