- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation-invariance) and a modified CCA-based QVEC metric for multilingual evaluation.

- multiCluster: groups translation-equivalent words via dictionaries into shared cluster embeddings used for all cluster members.

- multiCCA: projects monolingual spaces into an English anchor space using bilingual dictionaries, extending Faruqui & Dyer to multilingual settings.

- multiSkip: extends bilingual skip-based cross-lingual objectives to multilingual settings by adding cross-lingual context terms from parallel corpora.

- Translation-invariance: uses low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment-frequency components, apparently focused on bilingual embeddings.

- Evaluation: introduces a rotation- and linear-invariant CCA variant of QVEC with an extension to multilingual evaluation using aligned linguistic resources.

- Writing: paper is clearly written and methods are described straightforwardly.

- Major concern—novelty: unclear what is new beyond Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance work; authors should explicitly state technical contributions and whether the novelty is merely a multilingual extension.

- Major concern—supersense sparsity: intersecting linguistic features across languages may yield very small shared feature sets; more than a footnote is needed to address sparsity.

- Major concern—coverage effects: reported scores (e.g., Table 2 dependency parsing) may be sensitive to coverage differences; a controlled analysis of coverage vs. performance is requested.

- Major concern—inconsistent intrinsic results: Table 3 shows inconsistent intrinsic metrics, translation-invariance dominates word-translation while simple methods often beat others on extrinsic tasks, and multiCluster’s strong extrinsic performance is puzzling.

- Question—parsing setup: need numbers for performance loss when fixing embeddings during parser training and gains from replacing random embeddings with the proposed embeddings in the LSTM parser.

- Question—Table 1 aggregation: clarify whether Table 1 averages the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1.

- Question—multiSkip vs multiCCA: clarify advantages of using multiSkip directly versus learning bilingual embeddings then projecting with multiCCA.

- Question—dictionaries: ask whether curated multilingual dictionaries (beyond alignments or Google Translate) were tried, since induced/MT dictionaries may not reflect real lexicon challenges.

- Summary recommendation: improve positioning relative to prior translation-invariance work, analyze coverage and feature sparsity more deeply, and explain why simpler methods outperform on extrinsic tasks to strengthen contributions.
