- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (cluster words via bilingual dictionaries, learn one vector per cluster).
- multiCCA: extend bilingual CCA to multilingual by projecting each language’s embeddings into an English target space via dictionary-driven linear maps.
- multiSkip: extend bilingual skip-gram to multilingual by summing aligned context objectives over all parallel corpora.
- Translation-invariance: low-rank factorization of a PMI matrix with bilingual alignment frequency terms (primarily bilingual-oriented).
- Introduces a revised QVEC-style evaluation using CCA to maximize correlation between embedding dimensions and linguistic feature matrices, extended to multilingual settings.
- Paper is clearly written and technically coherent.
- Major concern 1: Clarify novelty relative to Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance approach and justify the nontriviality of any multilingual extension.
- Major concern 2: Supersense annotation sparsity/inconsistency across languages may severely limit shared feature intersection—how is this mitigated beyond the footnote?
- Major concern 3: Results may be sensitive to coverage differences (e.g., multiCluster vs multiCCA in dependency parsing have different coverage but similar scores)—please analyze sensitivity.
- Major concern 4: Table 3 is hard to interpret—multilingual methods often don’t top intrinsic metrics, translation-invariance dominates word translation, and multiCluster’s strong extrinsic performance is counterintuitive.
- Q1 to authors: In parsing, what is the performance drop from freezing pretrained embeddings, and what gain do these embeddings give over random initialization in the stack-LSTM parser?
- Q2 to authors: Does Table 1 report correlations aggregated over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?
- Q3 to authors: What advantages does multiSkip offer versus learning bilingual embeddings and then applying multiCCA to align them?
- Q4 to authors: Did you evaluate with real curated multilingual dictionaries (not just alignment-derived or Google Translate dictionaries), and if so, what were the results?
