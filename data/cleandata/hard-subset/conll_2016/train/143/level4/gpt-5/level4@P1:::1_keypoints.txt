- Paper proposes methods for multilingual word embeddings and a modified intrinsic evaluation metric.

- multiCluster: clusters translationally equivalent word types via bilingual dictionaries and trains one embedding per cluster from concatenated monolingual corpora.

- multiCCA: projects monolingual embeddings into an English anchor space using CCA with bilingual dictionaries, extending Faruqui & Dyer (2014) to multilingual.

- multiSkip: extends bilingual skip-gram to multilingual by summing bilingual skip-gram objectives over parallel corpora.

- Translation-invariance: factorizes a PMI matrix with terms from bilingual alignment frequencies (based on Gardner et al., 2015); may be limited to bilingual settings.

- Evaluation: adapts QVEC to multilingual (multiQVEC) and proposes QVEC-CCA/multiQVEC-CCA to align embeddings to linguistic features via CCA, yielding rotation-invariant, normalized intrinsic scores.

- Contribution 1: Two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that do not require parallel corpora.

- Contribution 2: Improved intrinsic evaluation (QVEC-CCA) and multilingual extension intended to correlate better with downstream tasks.

- Contribution 3: Comprehensive empirical comparison across many languages, intrinsic/extrinsic tasks, and coverage reporting.

- Strength: Clear exposition and thorough description of methods and CCA-based evaluation.

- Strength: Sound evaluation design using both intrinsic and extrinsic tasks and analysis of correlations; QVEC-CCA addresses basis dependence and normalization issues.

- Strength: Ambitious, practically relevant multilingual extensions and large-scale experiments with coverage reporting.

- Weakness: Novelty unclear regarding translation-invariance—need precise explanation of what, if anything, is new for multilingual extension.

- Weakness: Reliance on supersense annotations across languages is problematic due to sparse/inconsistent tag intersections; requires discussion of mitigation (e.g., tag mappings or alternative resources).

- Weakness: Coverage varies across methods and may confound reported scores; authors should quantify coverage effects and compare on shared vocabularies.

- Weakness: Inconsistent results across metrics—translation-invariance excels at word translation while multiCluster often wins extrinsic tasks—needs reconciliation and guidance on method choice.

- Q1: How much performance is lost by fixing pretrained embeddings in the parser versus fine-tuning, and how much do pretrained embeddings beat random initialization?

- Q2: Clarify Table 1: which exact set of models and metrics (the 17 multilingual embeddings?) were used to compute correlations?

- Q3: When does multiSkip outperform a pipeline of bilingual embeddings plus multiCCA (consider data needs, compute cost, or task-specific benefits)?

- Q4: Did you evaluate with human-curated multilingual dictionaries instead of alignment/Google Translate extraction, and how do results differ?

- Q5: Did you attempt a true multilingual extension of translation-invariance (e.g., multiple alignment matrices or unified operator), and what were the outcomes?
