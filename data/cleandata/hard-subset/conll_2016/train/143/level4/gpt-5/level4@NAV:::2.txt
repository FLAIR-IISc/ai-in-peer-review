Review- This paper introduces two dictionary-based approaches for inducing multilingual word embeddings, one grounded in clustering (MultiCluster) and another based on canonical correlation analysis (MultiCCA). In addition, it proposes a supersense similarity metric that extends QVEC by replacing its correlation component with CCA and by incorporating multilingual evaluation.

The evaluation spans a broad set of tasks via the authors’ web portal; the results show that in some settings the proposed representations outperform two other baselines.

The paper is very well written and reflects substantial effort. The proposed representation-learning and evaluation methods are timely, and I commend the thorough documentation. My overall impression, however, is that it may favor breadth over depth. I would have liked a more detailed analysis of the results (e.g., the conflicting outcomes for MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups; the impact of estimation choices and hyperparameters in MultiCluster/CCA). Thus, while I view the paper as highly practical for me and the community (improved QVEC measure, web portal), I did not come away having learned much in terms of clearly posed and answered research questions.

More concrete remarks follow.

Multilingual embeddings and clustering:

Lu, A., Wang, W., Bansal, M., Gimpel, K., & Livescu, K. (2015). Deep multilingual correlation for improved word embeddings. In NAACL.
Faruqui, M., & Dyer, C. (2013). An Information Theoretic Approach to Bilingual Word Clustering. In ACL.
Multilingual training of embeddings to improve source-language embeddings:
Suster, S., Titov, I., and van Noord, G. (2016). Bilingual learning of multi-sense embeddings with discrete autoencoders. In NAACL-HLT.
Guo, J., Che, W., Wang, H., and Liu, T. (2014). Learning sense-specific word embeddings by exploiting bilingual resources. In COLING.
More broadly, translational context has been explored, e.g.:
Diab, M., & Resnik, P. (2002). An unsupervised method for word sense tagging using parallel corpora. In ACL.
