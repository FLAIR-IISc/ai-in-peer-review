- Paper presents four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (dictionary-based clusters with one embedding per cluster).
- multiCCA: extends Faruqui & Dyer to multilingual by using English as an anchor and projecting other languages’ embeddings via bilingual dictionaries.
- multiSkip: extends Luong et al. by adding objective components for all available parallel corpora to learn multilingual skip-gram style embeddings.
- Translation invariance: low-rank PMI decomposition with bilingual alignment-frequency terms; may be limited to bilingual embeddings.
- Evaluation: modified QVEC using CCA to maximize correlation between embeddings and linguistic feature vectors, producing rotation-invariant scores and extended to multilingual evaluation.
- Paper is clearly written.
- Major issue 1: Unclear novelty relative to Gardner et al.’s translation invariance—if novelty is the multilingual extension, authors must explain what is new.
- Major issue 2: Supersense annotations across languages are problematic due to small feature intersections; need a concrete solution beyond a footnote.
- Major issue 3: Coverage likely affects Table 2 results—e.g., multiCluster and multiCCA have different coverage but similar dependency parsing scores; clarify coverage impact.
- Major issue 4: Table 3 is inconsistent—multilingual methods often do not win intrinsic metrics, translation invariance wins translation tasks by a wide margin, and multiCluster oddly does best on extrinsic metrics despite discarding inter-cluster semantics.
- Additional Q1: What is the performance loss from fixing embeddings in dependency parsing, and what gain from using them to initialize the LSTM stack parser?
- Additional Q2: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?
- Additional Q3: Are there advantages to multiSkip versus training bilingual embeddings separately then applying multiCCA to align spaces?
- Additional Q4: Dictionaries from alignments or Google Translate may not reflect real lexicons—did the authors evaluate with real multilingual dictionaries?
