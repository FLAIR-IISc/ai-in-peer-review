## Review of "Estimating and Evaluating Multilingual Embeddings"

This paper presents methods for estimating and evaluating word embeddings across 59 languages in a shared embedding space. The authors propose two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) and a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), alongside a web portal for facilitating research in this area.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the comprehensive evaluation framework. The development of multiQVEC-CCA, and the demonstration that it correlates better with downstream tasks than existing metrics, is a significant step forward. The creation of a publicly available web portal with benchmarks and tools for evaluating multilingual embeddings is also a valuable contribution to the community, lowering the barrier to entry for researchers in this field. Finally, the extensive experimentation across a large number of languages (59) provides a robust assessment of the proposed methods.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Evaluation Framework:** The introduction of multiQVEC-CCA and its superior correlation with downstream tasks (document classification and parsing) provides a more reliable method for assessing the quality of multilingual embeddings. This is crucial for driving progress in the field.
2. **Community Resource:** The web portal is a highly valuable resource. Providing a platform for easy evaluation and comparison of different methods will undoubtedly accelerate research in multilingual embeddings. The open-source release of methods further enhances this contribution.
3. **Large-Scale Experimentation:** Evaluating the methods across 59 languages demonstrates the scalability and generalizability of the proposed approaches. This broad coverage is a significant advantage over studies limited to a few language pairs.
4. **Thorough Comparison:** The paper provides a detailed comparison against existing methods, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of each approach. The ablation studies and analysis of coverage percentages add to the rigor of the evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Estimation Methods:** While multiCluster and multiCCA are presented as novel, they build heavily on existing techniques (CCA, skipgram). The improvements over these baselines, while demonstrated, are not groundbreaking.
2. **Dictionary Quality Dependence:** The performance of multiCluster and multiCCA is heavily reliant on the quality of the bilingual dictionaries used. The paper acknowledges this, but a more in-depth analysis of the impact of dictionary quality would be beneficial.
3. **Extrinsic Task Simplification:** Removing all features *except* the embeddings in the downstream tasks (classification and parsing) is a rather drastic simplification. While it isolates the impact of the embeddings, it may not reflect real-world scenarios where embeddings are used in conjunction with other features.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of dictionary quality on the performance of multiCluster and multiCCA? Specifically, how does performance vary with dictionary size and precision?
2.  Regarding the simplification of the downstream tasks, do you anticipate the relative ranking of the methods would change if other features were included?
3.  The paper mentions the limitations of using supersense tags for languages other than English. Do you have plans to explore alternative linguistic resources or methods for cross-lingual evaluation?
