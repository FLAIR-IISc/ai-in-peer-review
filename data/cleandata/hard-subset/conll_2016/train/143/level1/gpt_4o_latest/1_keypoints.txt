- Introduces multiCluster and multiCCA for multilingual embeddings across 59 languages using monolingual corpora + bilingual dictionaries (no parallel corpora).
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation metric addressing basis invariance and normalization, with better correlation to downstream tasks.
- Provides a web portal and open-source tools for standardized evaluation and benchmarking of multilingual embeddings.
- multiCluster and multiCCA are scalable and suitable for low-resource languages; multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster.
- multiQVEC-CCA better correlates with downstream tasks and fixes theoretical issues of prior QVEC.
- Evaluates methods comprehensively on intrinsic and extrinsic tasks (word similarity, translation, document classification, dependency parsing).
- Presents detailed correlation analysis showing limitations of existing intrinsic metrics and advantages of multiQVEC-CCA.
- Open-source release and portal promote reproducibility and practical impact.
- Comparison to parallel-data-based methods is limited to 12 languages; broader comparison across all 59 is needed.
- multiCluster suffers semantic ambiguity from shared embeddings for translationally equivalent words, reducing performance at scale.
- multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA rely on supersense annotations, limiting applicability to languages without those resources.
- Question: How robust are multiCluster and multiCCA to noisy or incomplete bilingual dictionaries?
- Question: Can multiQVEC-CCA be adapted for languages lacking supersense annotations, and how?
- Question: Will the evaluation portal be extended to additional downstream tasks (e.g., MT, cross-lingual IR)?
- Conclusion: Significant contributions to multilingual NLP with practical tools and methods; recommended for acceptance despite noted limitations.
