- Introduces multiCluster and multiCCA to produce shared-space word embeddings for many languages using monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries (no parallel text).
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation metric shown to correlate more strongly with downstream task performance.
- Conducts extensive experiments across 59 languages and releases an open-source evaluation portal and resources.
- Contribution: validates multiQVEC-CCA as a better predictor of extrinsic performance than prior intrinsic metrics.
- Contribution: demonstrates scalable dictionary-based embedding methods at massive scale (59 languages) with empirical comparisons to parallel-corpus methods.
- Contribution: provides a public evaluation portal to promote reproducibility and standardized benchmarking.
- Strength: rigorous analysis showing multiQVEC-CCA achieves high correlation (0.896) with document classification.
- Strength: exceptionally thorough experimental evaluation—four methods, nine tasks, up to 59 languages.
- Strength: high practical impact through released code, trained embeddings, and a public portal.
- Weakness: proposed estimation methods are inconsistent and do not clearly outperform baselines; translation-invariance sometimes outperforms them.
- Weakness: multiCCA’s reliance on English as a pivot may introduce anglocentric bias and suboptimality for typologically distant languages, not addressed.
- Weakness: methodological novelty is incremental—main innovation is large-scale application rather than new algorithms.
