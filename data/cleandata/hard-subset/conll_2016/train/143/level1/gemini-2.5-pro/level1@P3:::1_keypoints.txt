- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that don't require parallel corpora.  
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation metric that correlates much better with downstream tasks than prior metrics.  
- Performs large-scale empirical comparisons across up to 59 languages and nine evaluation metrics.  
- Releases an evaluation web portal, open-source code, and pre-trained embeddings for the community.  
- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA is a significantly better proxy for downstream performance (strong Pearson correlations reported).  
- Contribution: comprehensive experiments and public resources enable reproducible, large-scale multilingual evaluation.  
- Contribution: multiCCA and multiCluster are simple, scalable dictionary-based methods; multiCCA is particularly robust.  
- Strength: rigorous treatment of evaluation exposing poor correlation of popular intrinsic metrics and proposing a superior alternative.  
- Strength: ambitious scale (59 languages) and community-facing artifacts (portal, code, models).  
- Strength: well-structured, thorough experimental design covering intrinsic and extrinsic tasks and multiple language settings.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—approaches are incremental extensions of existing ideas (bilingual CCA, cross-lingual clustering).  
- Weakness: baseline comparisons may be unfair/confounded—multiSkip uses only parallel data, and vocabulary-coverage differences skew results.  
- Weakness: multiQVEC-CCA depends on scarce supersense annotations (only available for 3 European languages), limiting applicability to massively multilingual settings.  
- Question: How sensitive are multiCCA results to pivoting through English, and have non-pivot or multi-pivot alternatives been tried?  
- Question: Why was multiSkip limited to parallel data, and how would results change if it also used monolingual corpora like other methods?  
- Question: How feasible is replacing supersense annotations with universal POS tags for multiQVEC-CCA, what would the S matrix look like, and would it still proxy semantic quality?
