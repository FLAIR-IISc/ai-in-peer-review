- Proposes two scalable multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that require only monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries, enabling embeddings for 59 languages.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a theoretically improved intrinsic evaluation metric addressing QVEC's lack of invariance and normalization, and shows strong correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Releases code, data, pre-trained models, and a public evaluation portal to standardize and lower the barrier for multilingual embedding research.  

Strengths:
- multiQVEC-CCA is a significant advance in evaluation, showing very high correlation (Pearson 0.896) with document classification.  
- Methods are practical and scalable by using dictionaries instead of scarce parallel corpora, demonstrated via 59-language embeddings.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive, separating metric evaluation from method evaluation and comparing against strong baselines with multiple metrics.  
- The public portal and released resources provide high community value for reproducibility and standardized benchmarking.  

Weaknesses:
- Comparison to parallel-data baselines is unfair because the multiSkip baseline was not given monolingual data that similar prior work used.  
- The estimation methods show limited algorithmic novelty, largely extending prior bilingual CCA and lexicon-clustering ideas.  
- Results for multiCluster are inconsistent: strong in the 12-language setting but reportedly hurt in the 59-language setting, without clear reconciliation.  
- multiQVEC-CCA’s practical applicability is limited by reliance on manually annotated linguistic resources available for only a few languages.  

Questions to authors:
- Why was monolingual data excluded from the multiSkip baseline, and how would allowing monolingual data affect comparisons?  
- Can multiCluster’s degradation in the 59-language setting be mitigated (e.g., better clustering or constraints) rather than abandoning the approach?  
- Have you considered validating multiQVEC-CCA with more widely available resources (e.g., universal POS tags) to extend its applicability to more languages?
