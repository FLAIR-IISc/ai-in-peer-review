- Summary: Proposes multiCluster and multiCCA to build multilingual embeddings from monolingual corpora plus bilingual dictionaries for 59 languages, introduces multiQVEC-CCA as an improved intrinsic metric, and releases code/data plus an evaluation portal.
- Contribution 1: Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a correlation-based intrinsic metric that empirically correlates better with downstream task performance than prior intrinsic metrics.
- Contribution 2: Presents two scalable, dictionary-based embedding estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) that avoid parallel corpora and scale to 59 languages.
- Contribution 3: Provides a public evaluation portal and open-source releases of methods and embeddings to enable standardized, replicable research.
- Strength 1: Theoretical critique of QVEC and principled replacement with CCA is well-motivated and empirically validated with stronger correlations to extrinsic tasks.
- Strength 2: Experiments are large-scale and thorough, training embeddings for 59 languages and evaluating across nine metrics including extrinsic tasks.
- Strength 3: Paper is clear, methods are simple and practical, and the focus on dictionary-based approaches is useful for lower-resource languages.
- Strength 4: Results analysis is careful and nuanced, acknowledging that no single method dominates and discussing plausible causes for differences.
- Weakness 1: Estimation methods are incremental extensions of existing ideas (cross-lingual clustering, bilingual CCA) rather than novel algorithms.
- Weakness 2: multiQVEC-CCA depends on scarce linguistic resources (supersense tags available for only 3 languages), limiting its current multilingual applicability.
- Weakness 3: multiSkip baseline is disadvantaged by using only parallel data while other methods use large monolingual corpora, making comparisons potentially unfair.
- Weakness 4: multiCCA’s use of English as a single pivot is unexamined and may harm quality for non-English-centric language pairs; alternatives are not explored.
- Question 1: Could the authors justify or analyze English-as-pivot drawbacks and consider alternatives like averaged pivots or graph-based multi-way projections?
- Question 2: Can multiQVEC-CCA be extended to more languages for semantic evaluation using automatically induced resources or mappings via multilingual KBs like BabelNet?
- Question 3: What are the size differences between the parallel data for multiSkip and the monolingual corpora used by other methods, and why was a stronger monolingual-augmented multiSkip baseline not included?
