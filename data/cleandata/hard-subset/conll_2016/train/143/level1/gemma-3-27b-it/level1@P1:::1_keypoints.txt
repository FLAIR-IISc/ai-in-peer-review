- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA as a theoretically sound, empirically validated intrinsic metric that correlates better with downstream tasks than prior metrics.  
- Proposes scalable estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings and applies them across 59 languages.  
- Releases a web portal and open-source code/data to facilitate reproducibility and further research.  
- Strong empirical validation: Section 5.1 shows statistically significant correlation improvements of multiQVEC-CCA.  
- Large-scale scope (59 languages) offers broader insights than prior small-language studies.  
- Comprehensive evaluation using multiple intrinsic and extrinsic metrics strengthens result reliability.  
- Practical resources (portal, code, data) lower barriers for the community and encourage adoption.  
- Clear problem statement and motivation linking multilingual embeddings to MT and transfer learning.  
- Estimation methods show limited novelty—they adapt existing techniques (CCA, skipgram) without major algorithmic innovation.  
- Dictionary creation via Google Translate for 47 languages raises concerns about noise and bias not fully analyzed.  
- Vocabulary coverage varies across methods; using vocabulary intersections may discard substantial information and its impact is under-discussed.  
- Lacks deeper qualitative/error analysis for specific languages or failure cases.  
- Parameter choices (cluster size, context window) are insufficiently explored for sensitivity or justification.  
- Asks authors to detail steps taken to mitigate Google Translate biases, including any filtering or post-editing.  
- Requests analysis of word types covered by different methods to explain coverage-driven performance differences.  
- Inquires about sensitivity to using English as the pivot in multiCCA and whether alternative pivots change results.  
- Seeks clarification on computational costs and runtime scaling with language count and corpus size.  
- Writing is clear and references are comprehensive, and code/data availability is commendable.  
- Suggests more detailed ablation studies on multiCCA and multiCluster components.  
- Notes a missing citation: "Luong et al., 2015a" appears absent from references.
