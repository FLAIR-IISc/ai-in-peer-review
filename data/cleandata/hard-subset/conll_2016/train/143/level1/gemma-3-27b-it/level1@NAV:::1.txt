## Review of "Estimating and Evaluating Multilingual Embeddings"

This paper presents methods for estimating and evaluating word embeddings across 59 languages in a shared space. The authors propose two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) and a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), alongside a web portal for facilitating research in this area.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the comprehensive evaluation framework and the associated web portal. Existing evaluation methods often lack correlation with downstream tasks, and the authors successfully demonstrate that their proposed multiQVEC-CCA metric exhibits a stronger correlation with both document classification and dependency parsing. The availability of a readily accessible web portal and open-source code significantly enhances the reproducibility and advancement of research in multilingual embeddings, representing a valuable resource for the community. A secondary contribution is the systematic exploration of dictionary-based methods for multilingual embedding estimation, though the methods themselves appear less novel than the evaluation framework.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Evaluation Framework:** The development and validation of multiQVEC-CCA, demonstrating improved correlation with downstream tasks compared to existing metrics, is a significant contribution. The authors convincingly demonstrate the inadequacy of previously used metrics and provide a more reliable tool for assessing embedding quality.
2. **Community Resource:** The publicly available web portal and open-source code are highly valuable. They lower the barrier to entry for researchers and promote reproducibility of results in this complex field.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Setup:** The authors conducted experiments across a large number of languages (59) and multiple evaluation tasks, providing a robust assessment of their methods. The inclusion of both intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations strengthens the findings.
4. **Thorough Analysis & Justification:** The paper provides a detailed rationale for the design choices of their methods and evaluation metrics, addressing potential shortcomings and limitations. The careful consideration of coverage issues in evaluation is particularly commendable.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Estimation Methods:** While multiCluster and multiCCA are well-described, they largely build upon existing techniques (CCA, Skipgram). The core ideas lack substantial novelty compared to the evaluation framework.
2. **Dictionary Quality Dependence:** The performance of multiCluster and multiCCA is heavily reliant on the quality of the bilingual dictionaries used for training. The paper acknowledges this, but the impact of dictionary noise on the results isn't fully explored. The Google Translate dictionaries for many languages are a potential source of error.
3. **Scalability Concerns:** While the evaluation is performed on 59 languages, the computational cost of training and evaluating embeddings for such a large number of languages is not explicitly discussed. This could limit the practical applicability of the methods.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive are the results to the threshold 'τ' used in constructing the bilingual dictionaries from parallel corpora?
2.  Could you provide more detail on the computational resources required to train the embeddings for the 59 languages, and any optimizations implemented to address scalability concerns?
3.  What are the limitations of the current evaluation portal (e.g., supported embedding formats, maximum embedding size)?
