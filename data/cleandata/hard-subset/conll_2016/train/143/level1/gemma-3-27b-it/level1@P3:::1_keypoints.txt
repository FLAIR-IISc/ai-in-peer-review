- Released multilingual evaluation portal and benchmarks for 59 languages, providing a standardized platform for comparing embeddings.  
- Proposed multiQVEC-CCA, fixing QVEC’s theoretical issues and showing better correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Presented two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) scaled to 59 languages, demonstrating practical scalability.  
- Large-scale multilingual setup (59 languages) expands beyond typical bilingual-focused work.  
- Rigorous evaluation using multiple intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, with correlation analysis highlighting multiQVEC-CCA’s strengths.  
- Public release of code, embeddings, and portal promotes reproducibility and community use.  
- Clear problem motivation linking massively multilingual embeddings to MT and transfer learning.  
- Estimation methods lack core algorithmic novelty, primarily extending existing clustering and CCA techniques.  
- Experimental gains over baselines are modest; comparisons to stronger parallel-data methods are missing.  
- Baseline choice (e.g., multiSkip) is potentially weak, which may overstate proposed methods’ advantages.  
- Reliance on dictionaries (many from Google Translate) raises data quality and bias concerns, with fixed threshold τ=0.1 appearing arbitrary.  
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis to explain where and why methods fail across languages or word types.  
- Questions on mitigation of Google Translate biases in dictionary creation.  
- Question whether multiSkip can be augmented with monolingual data and how that would affect comparisons.  
- Inquiry about interpreting low correlation coefficients between intrinsic and extrinsic metrics and contributing factors.  
- Request for detailed computational resource breakdown for reproducibility (multiCCA, multiSkip).  
- Writing is generally good but some technical explanations should be more concise.  
- Supplementary material is essential and must be reviewed; ensure the web portal remains active and maintained.
