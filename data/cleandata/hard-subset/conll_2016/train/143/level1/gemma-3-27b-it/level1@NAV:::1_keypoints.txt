- Primary contribution: Proposes a comprehensive evaluation framework (multiQVEC-CCA) and web portal for multilingual embeddings, showing stronger correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Secondary contribution: Systematic exploration of dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), though methods are incremental.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA demonstrates improved correlation with document classification and dependency parsing versus prior metrics.  
- Strength: Public web portal and open-source code enhance reproducibility and accessibility for researchers.  
- Strength: Large-scale experiments across 59 languages with both intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations yield robust assessment.  
- Strength: Detailed analysis and justification of design choices, including attention to coverage issues in evaluation.  
- Weakness: Estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) lack substantial novelty, mainly extending existing techniques.  
- Weakness: Performance depends heavily on bilingual dictionary quality; impact of noisy dictionaries (e.g., Google Translate) is underexplored.  
- Weakness: Scalability and computational cost for training/evaluating embeddings across many languages are not clearly addressed.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the threshold τ used when constructing bilingual dictionaries from parallel corpora?  
- Question: What computational resources and optimizations were used to train embeddings for the 59 languages?  
- Question: What are the limitations of the evaluation portal (supported formats, maximum embedding size, etc.)?
