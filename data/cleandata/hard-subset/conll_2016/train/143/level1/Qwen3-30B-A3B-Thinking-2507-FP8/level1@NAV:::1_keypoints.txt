- Introduces methods for estimating and evaluating word embeddings across 59 languages in a single shared space.
- Proposes multiQVECCCA, an evaluation metric that addresses flaws in prior methods and shows superior correlation with downstream tasks.
- Provides a web portal for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings.
- Presents dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) as incremental improvements over existing work.
- Strength: multiQVECCCA is theoretically sound and highly correlated with downstream tasks (0.896 vs 0.386 for previous metrics).
- Strength: The web portal is a valuable practical tool that will facilitate future research.
- Strength: Thorough correlation analysis between intrinsic metrics and downstream tasks gives important community insights.
- Weakness: Estimation methods lack sufficient novelty compared to prior approaches like translation-invariance and multiSkip.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Google Translate to construct dictionaries for 47 languages is a significant limitation that may harm embedding quality.
- Weakness: Paper does not demonstrate scalability to truly low-resource languages or effective handling of polysemy.
- Weakness: Comparisons to state-of-the-art are limited and benchmarking is not comprehensive.
- Question: How will the authors address limitations of Google Translate-based dictionaries for low-resource language pairs?
- Question: Can the authors provide analysis of performance on languages with very different grammatical structures?
