- Paper introduces dictionary-based multilingual embeddings for 59 languages (multiCluster, multiCCA) and a new intrinsic evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA) plus an evaluation web portal.  
- Major contributions (by strength): multiQVEC-CCA metric, comprehensive evaluation portal, and the multiCCA estimation method.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows much better correlation with downstream tasks than previous metrics, addressing a key evaluation gap.  
- Strength: The evaluation portal standardizes metrics and languages, improving replicability and comparability in multilingual embedding research.  
- Strength: multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster across multiple metrics and languages, showing strong practical utility.  
- Weakness: Proposed estimation methods lack novelty — multiCCA is a direct extension of prior work and multiCluster resembles earlier clustering-based approaches.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on Google Translate for dictionaries (47/59 languages) without analysis of translation quality or error propagation into embeddings.  
- Weakness: multiCluster’s single-vector-per-cluster exacerbates polysemy, with insufficient analysis of its impact on downstream performance.  
- Question: How does multiCCA compare to parallel-data methods when controlling for the amount of parallel data used?  
- Question: Please provide analysis of Google Translate dictionary quality (e.g., precision/recall vs. curated dictionaries) and its effects on embeddings.  
- Question: What analyses show whether polysemy from multiCluster disproportionately harmed its performance compared to multiCCA?  
- Overall: The paper delivers a significant advance in intrinsic evaluation and a useful portal, but would benefit from stronger comparisons to prior work and analyses of dictionary quality and polysemy limitations.
