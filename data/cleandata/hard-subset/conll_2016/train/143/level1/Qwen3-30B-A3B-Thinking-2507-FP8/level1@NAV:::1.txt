

This paper introduces methods for estimating and evaluating word embeddings across 59 languages in a single shared space. The main contributions, as I see them, are: 1) the multiQVECCCA evaluation metric, which addresses a critical flaw in previous evaluation methods and shows superior correlation with downstream tasks; 2) the web portal for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings; and 3) the dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA), which are incremental improvements over existing work.

Strengths: The most significant contribution is multiQVECCCA, which provides a theoretically sound evaluation method that correlates strongly with downstream tasks (0.896 correlation with document classification, compared to 0.386 for previous metrics). This addresses a fundamental problem in the field and represents a substantial methodological advance. The web portal is a valuable practical contribution that will facilitate future research by providing standardized evaluation tools. The thorough correlation analysis between intrinsic metrics and downstream tasks provides important insights for the community.

Weaknesses: The estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) lack sufficient novelty compared to existing work like translation-invariance and multiSkip, with the authors themselves acknowledging they build on prior approaches. The use of Google Translate to construct dictionaries for 47 languages is a significant limitation that could compromise embedding quality and is not adequately addressed. The paper fails to demonstrate how the methods scale to truly low-resource languages or handle polysemy effectively, which is critical for multilingual embeddings. The comparison to state-of-the-art methods is limited to specific baselines rather than comprehensive benchmarking.

Questions to Authors: How would you address the limitations of Google Translate-based dictionaries for low-resource language pairs? Could you provide additional analysis on how the methods perform on languages with very different grammatical structures?
