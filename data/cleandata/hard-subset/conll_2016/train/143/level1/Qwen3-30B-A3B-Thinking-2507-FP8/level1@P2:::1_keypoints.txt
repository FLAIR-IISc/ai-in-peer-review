1. Introduces dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) requiring only monolingual data and dictionaries.  
2. Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, a new intrinsic evaluation metric that correlates strongly with downstream tasks.  
3. Provides a web portal for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation.  
4. multiQVEC-CCA shows substantially improved correlation with downstream performance (e.g., 0.896 with document classification).  
5. The web portal is a practical community resource that promotes reproducible, multilingual evaluation.  
6. Methods are evaluated at scale (59 languages); multiCCA consistently outperforms multiCluster across metrics.  
7. Embedding estimation methods are largely incremental extensions of prior work with limited novelty.  
8. multiQVEC-CCA depends on supersense annotations available for few languages, limiting its broad applicability.  
9. Comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines are incomplete (missing methods like Al-Rfou et al., Guo et al.).  
10. Question: How will the authors address reliance on scarce supersense annotations or use alternative resources (e.g., universal POS) to extend evaluation?  
11. Question: Can the authors provide analysis showing whether performance gaps between multiCluster and multiCCA persist when vocabulary coverage is normalized?  
12. Question: Please clarify the implementation of multiSkip and its claimed (non)use of monolingual data relative to prior descriptions.  
13. Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address evaluation limitations and stronger comparisons to prior work.
