- Summary: Paper proposes dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings, a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web evaluation portal for 50+ languages.
- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA as a better intrinsic evaluation metric correlating more strongly with downstream tasks.
- Contribution: A public web portal and released data/code to standardize multilingual embedding evaluation.
- Contribution: Two estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for shared multilingual embedding spaces.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows substantially higher correlation with downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 for document classification) than prior intrinsic metrics.
- Strength: The web portal and released resources improve reproducibility and provide a standardized evaluation framework.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across 59 languages and multiple intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, with multiCCA showing consistent performance.
- Weakness: Estimation methods are incremental; multiCCA and multiCluster mainly extend prior work without clearly novel advances.
- Weakness: Inconsistent evaluation results across metrics—no single estimation method dominates across all tasks.
- Weakness: multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA rely on supersense annotations available for few languages, limiting applicability.
- Question: Please clarify how multiCCA specifically differs from and extends Hermann and Blunsom (2014) and related prior work.
- Question: Please clarify the claim about multiSkip not using monolingual data versus using monolingual contexts from parallel corpora.
- Question: Why is the correlation between multiQVEC-CCA and dependency parsing low (0.273) versus document classification, and how might it be improved?
