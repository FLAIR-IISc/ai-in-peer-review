- Paper introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web evaluation portal across 50+ languages.  
- Primary contribution is multiQVEC-CCA; estimation methods are incremental extensions.  

- multiQVEC-CCA shows strong empirical correlation with downstream tasks, outperforming prior intrinsic metrics.  
- The web portal provides practical, standardized infrastructure for multilingual embedding evaluation.  
- Dictionary-based methods can be competitive with parallel-data methods and scale better across many languages.  

- multiCluster and multiCCA lack novelty and are minor extensions of prior work (e.g., Faruqui & Dyer; clustering approaches).  
- 59-language results are of questionable significance because they are evaluated mainly with intrinsic metrics that may not reflect downstream utility.  
- The paper insufficiently compares its evaluation portal to existing tools and lacks detail on how it improves prior monolingual evaluation methods.  

- Request clarification on how multiQVEC-CCA addresses multiQVEC’s theoretical shortcomings (invariance to linear transforms and unnormalized scores).  
- Ask why coverage differences between multiCluster and multiCCA are small despite multiCluster forcing shared vectors for translational equivalents.  
- Ask whether multiCCA’s reported superiority over multiCluster is statistically significant.  

- Note limitation: multiQVEC-CCA depends on supersense annotations across languages, which are inconsistent or unavailable for many languages.  
- Suggest stronger comparison of dictionary-based vs. parallel-data methods over more languages to substantiate claims.  
- Overall: well-written paper with a strong evaluation contribution and useful portal, but estimation-method novelty is overstated and some claims need stronger empirical support.
