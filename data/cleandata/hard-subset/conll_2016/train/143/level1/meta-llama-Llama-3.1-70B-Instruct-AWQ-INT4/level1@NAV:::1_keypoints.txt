- Paper proposes dictionary-based multilingual embedding estimation methods plus a new intrinsic evaluation and an evaluation web portal.  
- MultiCluster and multiCCA: novel dictionary-based methods that don’t require parallel corpora, trained on monolingual data plus pairwise dictionaries across 59 languages.  
- MultiQVEC-CCA: a new intrinsic evaluation metric that reportedly correlates better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics.  
- Web portal: a public evaluation site providing a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics for multilingual embeddings.  
- Strength: methods are novel and more widely applicable because they avoid requiring parallel data.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA is a meaningful improvement in intrinsic evaluation correlation with downstream performance.  
- Strength: the web portal is a useful resource for benchmarking and comparison.  
- Strength: experiments are extensive and demonstrate effectiveness across multiple tasks.  
- Weakness: multiQVEC-CCA still depends on supersense tag annotations, limiting its applicability.  
- Weakness: performance of the proposed methods may hinge on the quality of the dictionaries used.  
- Weakness: computational requirements may be high for large-scale multilingual embedding estimation.  
- Question: how will the authors address multiQVEC-CCA’s reliance on supersense tag annotations?  
- Question: please provide more details on dictionary construction and its impact on performance.  
- Question: how do the authors plan to extend their methods to low-resource languages or those with limited dictionary resources?
