- Summary: Proposes multiCluster and multiCCA for shared multilingual embeddings without parallel data, introduces multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric and a web portal, and validates them with experiments.

- Contribution — multiCluster and multiCCA: Two novel dictionary-based methods for multilingual embeddings that do not require parallel data.

- Contribution — multiQVEC-CCA: A new intrinsic evaluation metric claimed to better correlate with downstream tasks than prior metrics.

- Contribution — Web portal: A publicly available evaluation portal offering a standardized suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics for multilingual embeddings.

- Strength — Novel methods: The paper introduces new approaches (multiCluster, multiCCA) that avoid the need for parallel corpora.

- Strength — Improved metric: multiQVEC-CCA addresses limitations of earlier intrinsic metrics and shows better downstream correlation.

- Strength — Extensive experiments: The methods and metric are validated through a broad set of experiments.

- Strength — Evaluation portal: The web portal facilitates standardized evaluation and future research.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation of multiCluster: multiCluster is only evaluated in limited settings and lacks comprehensive comparisons.

- Weakness — Dependence on English vector space: multiCCA relies on English as the multilingual space, which may limit applicability to languages distant from English.

- Weakness — Limited extrinsic evaluations: Only two extrinsic tasks (document classification, dependency parsing) are tested; broader task evaluation is missing.

- Weakness — Lack of comparisons to SOTA: The paper does not sufficiently compare the proposed methods against other state-of-the-art multilingual embedding approaches.

- Question — OOV handling: How do the proposed methods handle out-of-vocabulary words?

- Question — Clustering choices: Could multiCluster be improved using more sophisticated clustering algorithms?

- Question — Alternative base languages: How does multiCCA perform if a language other than English is used as the multilingual vector space?

- Question — Applicability to other tasks: Can these methods be applied effectively to tasks like machine translation or question answering?
