**Summary**

This paper proposes two novel methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings in a shared vector space without requiring parallel data. The authors also introduce a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of previous intrinsic evaluation metrics. Additionally, they develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods and evaluation metric through extensive experiments on various tasks.

**Contributions**

1. **MultiCluster and multiCCA methods**: The authors propose two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data. These methods are a significant contribution to the field of multilingual NLP.
2. **multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric**: The authors introduce a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of previous intrinsic evaluation metrics. This metric is shown to correlate better with downstream tasks than previous metrics.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics. This portal will facilitate further research in this area by providing a standardized evaluation framework.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel methods for estimating multilingual embeddings**: The authors propose two novel methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, which do not require parallel data. These methods are a significant contribution to the field of multilingual NLP.
2. **Improved evaluation metric**: The authors introduce a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of previous intrinsic evaluation metrics. This metric is shown to correlate better with downstream tasks than previous metrics.
3. **Extensive experiments**: The authors conduct extensive experiments to evaluate the proposed methods and evaluation metric. These experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods and evaluation metric.
4. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics. This portal will facilitate further research in this area by providing a standardized evaluation framework.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation of multiCluster method**: The authors do not provide a detailed evaluation of the multiCluster method, which is one of the two proposed methods. This method is only evaluated in a limited setting, and its performance is not compared to other methods in all evaluation metrics.
2. **Dependence on English vector space**: The multiCCA method relies on the English vector space as the multilingual vector space. This may limit the applicability of the method to languages that are not closely related to English.
3. **Limited evaluation of extrinsic tasks**: The authors only evaluate the proposed methods on two extrinsic tasks, multilingual document classification and multilingual dependency parsing. More extrinsic tasks should be evaluated to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.
4. **Lack of comparison to other methods**: The authors do not compare the proposed methods to other state-of-the-art methods for estimating multilingual embeddings. This comparison is necessary to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the proposed methods handle out-of-vocabulary words?
2. Can the multiCluster method be improved by using more sophisticated clustering algorithms?
3. How does the performance of the multiCCA method change when using a different language as the multilingual vector space?
4. Can the proposed methods be applied to other NLP tasks, such as machine translation and question answering?
