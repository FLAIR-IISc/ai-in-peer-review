**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces new methods for estimating and evaluating multilingual word embeddings in a shared vector space. The authors propose two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, which do not require parallel data. They also introduce a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which correlates better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics. Additionally, the authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Main Contributions**

1. **MultiCluster and multiCCA methods**: The authors propose two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings. These methods use monolingual data and pairwise parallel dictionaries to train embeddings in 59 languages.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA evaluation method**: The authors introduce a new intrinsic evaluation method that addresses the limitations of previous methods. MultiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics. This portal facilitates further research in this area.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel methods for estimating multilingual embeddings**: The authors propose two new methods that do not require parallel data, making them more widely applicable.
2. **Improved evaluation method**: MultiQVEC-CCA is a significant improvement over previous intrinsic metrics, correlating better with downstream tasks.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The portal provides a valuable resource for researchers to evaluate and compare different multilingual embedding methods.
4. **Extensive experimental evaluation**: The authors conduct a thorough experimental evaluation of their methods, demonstrating their effectiveness in various tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited scope of evaluation metrics**: While multiQVEC-CCA is an improvement over previous intrinsic metrics, it still has limitations, such as relying on supersense tag annotations.
2. **Dependence on dictionary quality**: The performance of the proposed methods may depend on the quality of the dictionaries used.
3. **Computational requirements**: The methods proposed in this paper may require significant computational resources, particularly for large-scale multilingual embedding estimation.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the limitations of multiQVEC-CCA, such as its reliance on supersense tag annotations?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the dictionary construction process and how it affects the performance of the proposed methods?
3. How do the authors plan to extend their methods to handle low-resource languages or languages with limited dictionary resources?
