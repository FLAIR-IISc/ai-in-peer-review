**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes new methods for estimating and evaluating word embeddings in a shared space across multiple languages. The authors introduce two dictionary-based estimation methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, which use monolingual data and bilingual dictionaries to train embeddings for 59 languages. They also propose a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which correlates better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics. Additionally, the authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Main Contributions**

1. **MultiCluster and multiCCA estimation methods**: The authors propose two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data. These methods are capable of training embeddings for a large number of languages.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA evaluation method**: The authors propose an improved intrinsic evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of previous metrics and correlates better with downstream tasks.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which provides a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, as well as a platform for researchers to upload and evaluate their own embeddings.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel estimation methods**: The authors propose two new dictionary-based estimation methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, which are capable of training embeddings for a large number of languages without requiring parallel data.
2. **Improved evaluation metric**: The authors propose an improved intrinsic evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of previous metrics and correlates better with downstream tasks.
3. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their methods using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, including multilingual document classification and dependency parsing.
4. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which provides a platform for researchers to upload and evaluate their own embeddings.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation of multiCluster**: The authors only evaluate multiCluster on a limited set of languages and tasks, which may not be representative of its performance on other languages and tasks.
2. **Comparison to baselines**: The authors only compare their methods to a limited set of baselines, which may not be representative of the state-of-the-art in multilingual word embeddings.
3. **Lack of analysis on semantic ambiguity**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the semantic ambiguity introduced by the multiCluster method, which may affect its performance on certain tasks.
4. **Limited evaluation of multiQVEC-CCA**: The authors only evaluate multiQVEC-CCA on a limited set of languages and tasks, which may not be representative of its performance on other languages and tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the semantic ambiguity introduced by the multiCluster method and its impact on performance?
2. How do you plan to address the limitations of multiQVEC-CCA, such as its reliance on supersense tag annotations?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive evaluation of multiCluster and multiCCA on a larger set of languages and tasks?
4. How do you plan to extend the web portal to support more evaluation metrics and tasks?
