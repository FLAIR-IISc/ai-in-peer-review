- Summary: Proposes dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for 59 languages, a new intrinsic metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web portal for evaluation.

- Contribution: multiCluster and multiCCA—two dictionary-based estimation methods that train embeddings for many languages without parallel data.

- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA—an improved intrinsic evaluation metric claimed to correlate better with downstream tasks.

- Contribution: A web portal that hosts intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation metrics and allows researchers to upload and evaluate embeddings.

- Strength: Novel estimation methods capable of training embeddings for many languages using only bilingual dictionaries and monolingual data.

- Strength: Improved evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA) addressing limitations of prior metrics and showing better correlation with downstream tasks.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation using multiple intrinsic and extrinsic tasks including document classification and dependency parsing.

- Strength: Public web portal providing a platform for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation.

- Weakness: multiCluster evaluated on a limited set of languages and tasks, leaving its generality unclear.

- Weakness: Comparisons are limited to a small set of baselines, potentially missing state-of-the-art methods.

- Weakness: No detailed analysis of semantic ambiguity introduced by multiCluster and its impact on performance.

- Weakness: multiQVEC-CCA evaluated on a limited set of languages and tasks and relies on supersense tag annotations.

- Question: Please provide a detailed analysis of semantic ambiguity from multiCluster and its performance impact.

- Question: How will you address multiQVEC-CCA’s reliance on supersense tag annotations and its limitations?

- Question: Can you expand evaluation of multiCluster and multiCCA to a larger, more diverse set of languages and tasks?

- Question: How do you plan to extend the web portal to support additional evaluation metrics and tasks?
