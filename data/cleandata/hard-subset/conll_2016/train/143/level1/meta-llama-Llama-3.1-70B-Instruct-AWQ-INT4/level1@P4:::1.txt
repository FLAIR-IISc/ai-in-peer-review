**Summary**

The paper proposes two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings in a shared vector space. The authors also introduce a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses limitations of previous intrinsic evaluation metrics. Additionally, they develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods and evaluation metrics through experiments on 59 languages.

**Contributions**

1. **MultiCluster and multiCCA methods**: The paper proposes two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data.
2. **MultiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric**: The authors introduce a new intrinsic evaluation metric that addresses limitations of previous metrics, such as sensitivity to linear transformations and unnormalized scores.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The paper presents a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics, facilitating further research in this area.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel methods for multilingual embeddings**: The paper proposes two new methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which are shown to be effective in experiments.
2. **Improved evaluation metric**: The authors introduce a new intrinsic evaluation metric that addresses limitations of previous metrics, providing a more robust way to evaluate multilingual embeddings.
3. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods and evaluation metrics, using a large number of languages and metrics.
4. **Web portal for evaluation**: The paper provides a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, making it easier for researchers to compare and evaluate their methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited analysis of multiCluster method**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the multiCluster method, which is shown to perform poorly in some experiments.
2. **Dependence on English vector space**: The multiCCA method relies on the English vector space as the multilingual vector space, which may not be optimal for all languages.
3. **Limited evaluation of web portal**: The paper does not provide a detailed evaluation of the web portal, including its usability and effectiveness in facilitating research.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the multiCluster method, including its strengths and weaknesses?
2. How do you plan to address the limitation of relying on the English vector space in the multiCCA method?
3. Can you provide more information on the web portal, including its design, implementation, and evaluation?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and evaluation metrics.
* The experiments are comprehensive and well-designed, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed methods and evaluation metrics.
* The web portal is a valuable resource for the research community, and its development is a significant contribution of the paper.
