Contributions:
1. Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) to learn multilingual embeddings from monolingual data and bilingual dictionaries.
2. Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation using canonical correlation analysis to better align embeddings with linguistic matrices.
3. Provides a web portal for uploading and evaluating multilingual embeddings on a standardized suite of metrics.

Strengths:
4. Novel, effective estimation methods that perform well, especially across many languages.
5. multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than prior intrinsic metrics.
6. The web portal facilitates reproducible comparison and future research.

Weaknesses:
7. Limited empirical comparison to other methods, notably those using parallel data.
8. Reliance on high-quality bilingual dictionaries, which may not exist for all language pairs.
9. Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large datasets.

Questions for authors:
10. How do multiCluster and multiCCA compare empirically to parallel-data methods like multiSkip?
11. How were the bilingual dictionaries constructed for experiments (sources, quality, coverage)?
12. How are out-of-vocabulary words handled and what impact does that have on downstream tasks?
