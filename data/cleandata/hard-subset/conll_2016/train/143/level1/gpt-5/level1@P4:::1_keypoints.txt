- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster (cluster translational equivalents, train skip-gram on cluster IDs) and multiCCA (project monolingual embeddings into English via CCA with bilingual dictionaries).
- Introduces intrinsic evaluation metrics multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, with multiQVEC-CCA addressing basis-variance and dimensionality issues via CCA.
- Shows multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with two downstream tasks (document classification, dependency parsing) than widely used intrinsic metrics, and releases a web portal and embeddings for up to 59 languages.
- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA is a basis-invariant intrinsic metric with stronger empirical correlations to downstream tasks.
- Contribution: multiCCA is a simple, scalable dictionary-based method that projects monolingual embeddings into a shared English space and works across 59 languages.
- Contribution: Public release of trained embeddings, datasets, and an evaluation portal to standardize multilingual embedding evaluation.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA directly addresses QVEC’s rotation and dimensionality issues and shows substantially higher correlation (e.g., 0.896 with document classification).
- Strength: MultiCCA scales without parallel corpora and outperforms multiCluster at large scale, useful for low-resource languages.
- Strength: Comprehensive, transparent empirical study across multiple intrinsic metrics and two extrinsic tasks, reporting coverage and discussing confounds.
- Strength: Provides community resources (portal, data, embeddings) that lower barriers and standardize evaluation.
- Strength: Solid baselines and analysis contrasting dictionary-based, parallel-only, and matrix factorization approaches, showing when each excels.
- Weakness: Correlation experiments limited to three languages and two tasks; supersense resources and weak parsing correlation (0.273) limit generalizability.
- Weakness: Baseline comparability concerns—multiSkip omits monolingual data and translation-invariance uses a smaller context window, potentially disadvantaging baselines.
- Weakness: Reliance on Google Translate-derived dictionaries for 59 languages may be noisy, hard to reproduce, and evaluation restricts candidate sets, possibly inflating scores.
- Weakness: Theoretical clarity of multiCCA mapping is lacking—CCA yields lower-dimensional projections, so assumptions about invertibility and numerical stability are not fully justified.
- Weakness: Method novelty is modest—multiCluster is straightforward and may worsen polysemy; multiCCA’s pivoting is simple though practically useful.
- Q: Clarify dimensionality/rank assumptions ensuring invertibility of CCA-based mappings and how numerical stability is ensured.
- Q: How sensitive are results to the choice of English pivot; have other pivots or joint latent alignments been tried?
- Q: Provide results for multiSkip augmented with monolingual data and translation-invariance with larger windows or tuned hyperparameters.
- Q: What is the coverage/noise level of Google Translate-derived dictionaries for 59 languages, and do alternative dictionaries change results?
- Q: How do word-translation results change with full target vocabularies or hubness mitigation methods like CSLS?
- Comment: Report variances (standard deviations) over multiple runs to quantify robustness of correlations and extrinsic results.
- Comment: Consider sense-aware clustering or soft alignment to mitigate multiCluster’s polysemy-induced degradation at large scale.
- Comment: Clarify portal longevity and hosting/mirroring plans to ensure reproducibility.
