- Proposes dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that avoid parallel corpora.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation metric addressing QVEC’s basis-variance and normalization issues, and adapts it for multilingual use.  
- Releases an evaluation portal, datasets, and resources to standardize and reproduce multilingual embedding research.  
- Experiments across up to 59 languages, multiple intrinsic metrics, and two extrinsic tasks show multiQVEC(-CCA) correlates better with downstream performance than common intrinsic metrics.  
- Key contribution: principled intrinsic metric (multiQVEC-CCA) with high correlation to downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 with document classification).  
- Key contribution: comprehensive, standardized evaluation framework and cross-metric/coverage analyses.  
- Key contribution: scalable, dictionary-based embedding estimation (multiCCA, multiCluster) that is competitive and practical for many languages.  
- Strength: strong evidence that intrinsic metrics matter—multiQVEC(-CCA) outperforms word similarity/translation metrics in downstream correlation.  
- Strength: breadth and rigor—large language coverage, multiple methods and metrics, and attention to coverage biases.  
- Strength: practicality—multiCCA scales using English as a hub and avoids parallel-data dependence.  
- Strength: methodological clarity—QVEC’s issues are well-argued and CCA-based fix is theoretically sound.  
- Strength: reproducibility—datasets, models, and portal lower friction for future work.  
- Weakness: limited algorithmic novelty—multiCCA is a straightforward bilingual-CCA extension and multiCluster can increase polysemy.  
- Weakness: dictionary quality and potential evaluation leakage from extensive use of Google Translate dictionaries.  
- Weakness: extrinsic evaluations are narrow/artificial (document classification, delexicalized frozen-embedding parsing) and lack common tasks like MT/NER.  
- Weakness: multilingual intrinsic metrics depend on supersense resources available for only a few languages, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: some experimental choices seem ad hoc and significance/confidence reporting is missing; correlation analysis uses only 17 models.  
- Questions for authors: report statistical significance/confidence intervals and nonparametric correlations?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is multiCCA to pivot language choice (non-English pivots)?  
- Questions for authors: provide a multiSkip baseline leveraging monolingual data for fair comparison?  
- Questions for authors: what safeguards prevent Google Translate leakage into training and evaluation?  
- Questions for authors: could the portal include standardized multilingual supersense/POS resources to broaden multiQVEC(-CCA) applicability?
