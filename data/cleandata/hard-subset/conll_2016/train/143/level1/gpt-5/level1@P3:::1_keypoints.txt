- Proposes two dictionary- and monolingual-corpus-based methods for multilingual embeddings: multiCluster and multiCCA.
- Introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA as multilingual intrinsic evaluation metrics using supersense annotations.
- Evaluates embeddings on multiple intrinsic metrics and two extrinsic tasks (document classification, dependency parsing) and releases an evaluation portal.
- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA is a principled, basis-invariant, normalized intrinsic metric with stronger reported downstream correlation.
- Contribution: Scalable, dictionary-based multilingual embedding estimation covering 59 languages with released resources and evaluation portal.
- Contribution: Simple yet strong multilingual CCA baseline pivoting through English; analysis highlights weaknesses in common intrinsic evaluations.
- Strength: Clear and well-motivated evaluation advance; multiQVEC-CCA fixes QVEC’s basis and normalization issues.
- Strength: Strong practical value and breadth—consistent pipelines, standardized splits, coverage reporting, and reproducibility focus across 59 languages.
- Strength: Sensible, simple algorithms (multiCCA, multiCluster) offering good performance/coverage trade-offs and illustrating polysemy/merging trade-offs.
- Strength: Honest assessment of baseline limitations, coverage biases, and confounds, with mitigation attempts.
- Weakness: Correlation study is limited (17 embeddings, 3 languages) raising concerns about statistical power, generality, and possible circularity with shared supersense resources.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on English as pivot and on scarce supersense resources, which may not generalize to distant or low-resource languages.
- Weakness: Evaluation dictionaries from Google Translate risk overlap with training data and bias word-translation evaluation; no audit or sensitivity analysis provided.
- Weakness: multiCluster’s hard clustering across dense multilingual dictionary graphs causes semantic conflation and error propagation with no mitigation offered.
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons suffer from hyperparameter and training-data asymmetries (e.g., window sizes, absence of monolingual data for multiSkip).
- Weakness: Insufficient ablations and analysis of key design choices (pivot selection, τ threshold, cluster caps, embedding dimensionality, monolingual data amounts).
- Question: Provide confidence intervals and significance tests for reported correlations and show robustness across more languages and extrinsic tasks.
- Question: Report overlap between Google Translate-derived training dictionaries and evaluation word-translation lists or re-run with held-out/different evaluation sources.
- Question: Clarify construction of the multilingual linguistic matrix for multiQVEC(-CCA): shared vs language-specific properties, alignment method, and frequency-weighting/coverage control.
- Question: Justify exclusive English pivot choice and report experiments with orthogonal Procrustes, multiple pivots, averaged projections, or joint multilingual CCA.
- Question: Provide sensitivity analyses for dictionary precision/recall (τ), dictionary size per language, and alignment noise effects on multiCluster/multiCCA.
- Question: Report experiments with soft clustering/weighted translations, community detection, or other mitigations to reduce semantic conflation in multiCluster.
- Overall: Valuable evaluation methodology and resources with strong practical impact; main novelty is evaluation and scale rather than modeling.
- Overall recommendation: Lean toward acceptance contingent on clarifying correlation generality, evaluation independence, and baseline parity.
