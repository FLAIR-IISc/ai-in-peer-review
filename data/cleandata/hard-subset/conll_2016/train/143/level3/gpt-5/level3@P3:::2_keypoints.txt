- Primary contribution: Proposes QVEC-CCA (and multilingual variant) to fix QVEC’s basis dependence and dimensionality bias, showing stronger correlation with downstream tasks; extends supersense-based evaluation to multiple languages.
- Secondary contribution: Releases a public evaluation portal, datasets, and code to improve reproducibility and comparability for multilingual embeddings research.
- Tertiary contribution: Introduces two simple dictionary-based estimators (MultiCCA and MultiCluster) that scale to 59 languages without parallel corpora and sometimes outperform strong baselines.

- Strength: Broad and rigorous evaluation across intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, 12- and 59-language settings, plus a benchmark web portal—strong community contribution.
- Strength: QVEC-CCA is well-motivated and empirically yields substantially higher correlations with downstream document classification than prior intrinsic metrics.
- Strength: MultiCCA and MultiCluster are practical, simple, require only monolingual data + dictionaries, scale to many languages, and are effective in several settings.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well-documented about setups, metrics, and training details.

- Weakness: Paper favors breadth over depth; many design choices (English hub, dictionary extraction, corpus weighting, post-normalization) lack ablation or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: MultiCluster shows unexplained performance drop in the 59-language setting vs. competitive results in 12-language tasks; potential causes (sense conflation, dictionary noise, cluster size, rare-word handling, corpus imbalance) are not probed.
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of CCA methodology (hub choice, inversion/ten→m,en usage, regularization, dimensionality, whitening, vocabulary/dictionary sparsity) and lack of comparisons to alternative CCA formulations.
- Weakness: Evaluation/reporting gaps—monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlations with extrinsic tasks are omitted; coverage-controlled comparisons are relegated to supplementary and statistical significance is not consistently reported.
- Weakness: Related-work and citation inconsistencies (multiSkip citations, relation to Coulmance/Guo/Luong, misleading phrasing about dataset combinations) and minor formatting issues (overfull equations).

- Question: Can you analyze MultiCluster by cluster size distribution to see whether large connected components cause sense conflation?
- Question: How does dictionary source (Europarl vs. Google Translate) affect MultiCluster performance?
- Question: How sensitive are MultiCluster results to τ threshold for translation pair selection and to a cap on maximum cluster size?
- Question: What happens if you restrict to high-confidence dictionary edges or prune cross-language edges that induce large polysemous components?
- Question: In MultiCluster, do you weight sentences/languages equally when concatenating corpora, and does reweighting to equalize per-language contribution reduce cluster frequency dominance?
- Question: What are the CCA regularization settings and target dimensionality, are projections whitened, and how sensitive are results to using English as hub vs. other hubs or multi-view CCA?
- Question: What are results using Ten→m,en directly (without inversion) or using symmetric/joint mappings to a shared latent space?
- Question: For Table 3, can you present both full-coverage and intersection-vocabulary results in the main text with standard errors and significance tests (e.g., Fisher z, bootstrap)?
- Question: Please add monolingual QVEC and monolingual QVEC-CCA correlations with extrinsic tasks alongside multiQVEC results to substantiate the QVEC-CCA improvement claim.

- Minor comment: Replace vague phrase about “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” with a precise list and aggregation protocol.
- Minor comment: Fix formatting so two equations on p.3 do not exceed column margins.
- Minor comment: Clarify how “multiSkip” relates to Coulmance et al. (TransGram), Guo et al., and Luong et al., and ensure consistent citations.
- Minor comment: Align context-window choices across methods, report random seeds, and show variance over multiple runs for key scores.
- Minor comment: Expand related work discussion on dictionary-based clustering and translational-context methods to better situate MultiCluster and MultiCCA.

- Overall recommendation: Accept if space allows for the practical impact (QVEC-CCA and evaluation resources), but the paper needs deeper methodological analyses and the requested experiments to strengthen scientific contribution.
