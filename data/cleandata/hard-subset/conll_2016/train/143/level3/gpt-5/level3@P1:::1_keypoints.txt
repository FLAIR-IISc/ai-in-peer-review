- Paper studies massively multilingual embeddings and introduces dictionary-based estimators (multiCluster, multiCCA) that avoid parallel corpora.
- Proposes multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA intrinsic metrics that better correlate with downstream tasks and extends them to multilingual settings.
- Releases an evaluation portal with datasets, scripts, and pretrained models to support reproducibility.
- Contribution: shows multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA correlate substantially better with downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 with document classification).
- Contribution: presents practical, scalable dictionary-based methods (especially multiCCA) to build shared-space embeddings across 59 languages without parallel data.
- Contribution: provides resources and benchmarks enabling replicable, community-wide evaluation.
- Strength: very strong empirical scope and rigor across many languages, embeddings, and both intrinsic/extrinsic tasks.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA addresses QVEC’s basis-variance and dimensionality issues and normalizes scores for fairer comparisons.
- Strength: multiCCA is simple, scalable, uses English as hub, and achieves strong coverage and performance when parallel data are scarce.
- Strength: reproducibility focus and reporting of coverage alongside scores is valuable.
- Strength: clear writing and accessible presentation of methods and protocol.
- Weakness: methodological novelty is incremental—multiCCA is a natural extension and some baselines replicate prior work.
- Weakness: coverage/OOV confounds are under-analyzed and may drive some observed differences in performance.
- Weakness: inconsistent and puzzling result patterns (e.g., multiCluster outperforming in some extrinsic tasks) lack deeper ablation/explanation.
- Weakness: multilingual supersense features may be English-centric and inconsistent across languages, risking bias.
- Weakness: dictionary quality (Google Translate/alignments) may introduce noise, domain bias, or circularity and needs validation against curated lexicons.
- Question: report absolute parser performance drop from freezing embeddings vs. trainable embeddings and gains over random fixed embeddings, ideally per language.
- Question: confirm correlation computations (sample size across 17 embeddings) and provide confidence intervals/significance tests for Table 1.
- Question: clarify advantages of training multiSkip directly vs. mapping bilingual embeddings with multiCCA (efficiency, coverage, extrinsic quality).
- Question: did you validate dictionaries with curated lexicons (Wiktionary, PanLex) or run small-scale checks to rule out GT-derived bias?
- Question: provide OOV rates per method and re-run comparisons on intersection vocabularies and frequency-matched subsets to see if rankings change.
- Question: provide ablations for multiCluster (cluster size, τ) and sensitivity analyses for multiCCA (hub choice, dictionary noise).
- Overall recommendation: solid, practical paper with valuable evaluation and resources; lean toward acceptance pending clarifications on novelty, coverage/OOV analysis, and dictionary/supersense concerns.
