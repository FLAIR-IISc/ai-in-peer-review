- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that do not require parallel corpora.  
- Proposes multilingual intrinsic evaluation metrics (multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA) that address QVEC’s rotation/dimensionality issues and aim to correlate with downstream tasks.  
- Provides a public evaluation portal, standardized datasets/protocols, and open-source implementations for reproducible multilingual embedding research.  
- Experiments span up to 59 languages and include both intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, with correlation analyses across 17 embedding settings.  
- multiQVEC-CCA shows strong correlation with downstream tasks (e.g., document classification 0.896), while common intrinsic metrics correlate poorly.  
- multiQVEC correlates best with parsing (0.444).  
- Both multiCCA and multiCluster are scalable and low-resource-friendly, using monolingual corpora plus bilingual dictionaries.  
- Broad, transparent experiments report multiple metrics (similarity, translation, QVEC variants, parsing, classification) and compare to parallel-data baselines.  
- The paper is clearly written and appears reproducible, with methods and resources easy to follow.  
- multiCCA has limited methodological novelty—essentially a pivot-based extension of bilingual CCA—and its novelty vs. prior projection methods needs clearer justification.  
- Coverage differences confound some comparisons; the paper needs stronger, quantitative control analyses for vocabulary coverage effects.  
- Inconsistencies across metrics (e.g., translation-invariance excelling at word translation vs multiCluster winning on extrinsic tasks) are underexplained and require further analysis.  
- Reliance on Google Translate–derived dictionaries for training and evaluation raises bias and circularity concerns; independent lexicons are needed.  
- multiQVEC(-CCA) depends on supersense annotations with cross-lingual inconsistencies; the effect of tag inconsistencies and limited feature intersections needs analysis.  
- Request: quantify parser performance for (a) random embeddings, (b) pretrained fine-tuned embeddings, and (c) fixed pretrained embeddings.  
- Request: confirm correlation computation details in Table 1 (computed over 17 embeddings), and report N, p-values, or confidence intervals.  
- Request: discuss whether multiSkip trained on parallel data can outperform projection pipelines in some scenarios, and comment on convergence/stability.  
- Request: report results with manually curated multilingual dictionaries (e.g., Wiktionary, PanLex) to assess bias vs Google Translate–derived dictionaries.  
- Request: provide controlled comparisons on vocabulary intersections and plots of score vs coverage to quantify how rankings change with coverage.  
- Request: analyze multiCluster-induced polysemy (how often clusters merge non-synonymous translations) and run ablations varying cluster size or dictionary precision thresholds.  
- Ask for clarification on novelty relative to Gardner et al.’s translation-invariance and whether any multilingual extension/implementation changes were made.  
- Suggest discussing alternative projection objectives (e.g., orthogonal Procrustes) and whether they differ materially from CCA in multilingual settings.  
- Suggest adding a note on the portal’s long-term hosting plan and intentions to include independent, non-MT-derived evaluation lexicons.
