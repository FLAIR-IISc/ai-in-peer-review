- Primary contribution: QVEC-CCA, a CCA-based intrinsic evaluation that fixes QVEC’s basis- and scale-dependence and its multilingual extension, showing stronger downstream correlations.  
- Secondary contribution: A public evaluation portal with datasets, scripts, and upload capability for standardized multilingual embedding benchmarking.  
- Tertiary contribution: Two dictionary-based multilingual methods (MultiCCA pivoted to English; MultiCluster cluster-and-share) trained on up to 59 languages, with sometimes incremental improvements.  

- Practical impact: The portal, packaged data, and released code materially lower barriers and should catalyze progress and comparability.  
- Improved intrinsic evaluation: QVEC-CCA is well-motivated (basis-invariance, normalization), multilingualized, and empirically correlates better with extrinsic tasks.  
- Breadth of evaluation: Experiments cover 59- and 12-language settings, multiple intrinsic metrics, and two extrinsic tasks (document classification, parsing).  
- Clear exposition: The paper is well written, meticulously documented, and appears highly reproducible.  

- Breadth over depth: The paper favors wide coverage over focused ablations and error analyses, leaving many design choices and failure modes under-explored.  
- Unexplained inconsistency: MultiCluster’s strong results in 12 languages versus degradation in 59 are noted but not analyzed (e.g., cluster chaining, polysemy, dictionary noise).  
- Limited parameter sensitivity: Key hyperparameters (τ for dictionary extraction, max cluster size, CCA dimensionality/regularization, pivot choice, coverage filtering) lack sensitivity analysis.  
- Evidence gap in correlations: Need monolingual QVEC vs. QVEC-CCA correlations with downstream tasks to substantiate the improvement claim.  
- Related work and citations: Inconsistencies in MultiSkip citations and insufficient positioning relative to multilingual clustering and translational-context methods.  

- Question: What drives MultiCluster’s divergence between 12 and 59 languages; can you quantify cluster chaining, polysemy, and dictionary noise effects?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to τ, cluster size caps, and CCA regularization/dimensionality—please provide sensitivity plots?  
- Question: Why fix English as pivot in MultiCCA; would alternative pivots or multi-pivot/joint CCA change results?  
- Question: Can you report monolingual QVEC vs. QVEC-CCA correlations with each extrinsic task (e.g., scatter plots)?  

- Wording: Specify exactly which cross-lingual word similarity datasets and the aggregation protocol referred to.  
- Formatting: Fix two equations that exceed the column margin.  
- Citations: Clarify correspondences for MultiSkip and expand related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context.  
- Overall: High practical value (QVEC-CCA and the portal) but limited new methodological insight without deeper analyses.
