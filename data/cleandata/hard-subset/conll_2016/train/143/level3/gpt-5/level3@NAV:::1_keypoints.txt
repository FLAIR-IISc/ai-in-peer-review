- Primary contribution: multiQVEC-CCA, a multilingual intrinsic metric that aligns embeddings to supersense features via CCA, yielding rotation- and dimensionality-invariant scores with stronger downstream-task correlation than standard intrinsic metrics.
- Secondary contribution: Two dictionary-based scaling methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that work for 59 languages without parallel corpora; multilingual CCA anchored in English is simple and effective.
- Tertiary contribution: Public evaluation portal and a comprehensive experimental suite covering 12–59 languages.
- Strength: CCA-based reformulation fixes known QVEC issues (basis sensitivity, dimensionality bias) and shows notably stronger correlations with document classification and reasonable parsing correlations.
- Strength: Large-scale training/evaluation for 59 languages and release of code/portal improves accessibility and reproducibility.
- Strength: Dictionary-only methods are practical where parallel data is scarce; multiCCA’s English-as-anchor design achieves strong coverage.
- Strength: Thorough experiments including multiple intrinsic/extrinsic evaluations, coverage reporting, and multilingual settings.
- Weakness: Novelty unclear relative to Gardner et al. (2015); need explicit delineation of what is new beyond translation-invariance.
- Weakness: Supersense feature coverage across languages is limited (en/da/it) and per-language overlap and impact are not quantified, risking biased scores.
- Weakness: Coverage differences between methods confound results (e.g., multiCluster vs multiCCA); central, coverage-controlled analyses and frequency stratification are needed.
- Weakness: Inconsistent results (e.g., translation invariance dominating word translation, multiCluster winning several extrinsic tasks) require explanation (possible factors: denoising, hubness/anisotropy, alignment noise, objective-task mismatch).
- Question: What is the parsing performance loss when embeddings are fixed vs. fine-tuned, and what gains come from replacing random with pretrained embeddings in the LSTM parser?
- Question: Is Table 1 computed over the 17 embeddings in §5.1, and how are correlations aggregated (per-embedding pairs vs. per-setting averages)?
- Question: Does multiSkip offer advantages over training bilingual embeddings and then using multiCCA to map into a shared space—please compare directly?
- Question: Beyond alignments and Google Translate, were curated multilingual lexicons evaluated to assess robustness to dictionary noise?
