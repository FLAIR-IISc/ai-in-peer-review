- Paper studies massively multilingual word embeddings, proposing dictionary-based multiCluster and multiCCA, introduces multiQVEC-CCA intrinsic evaluation, compares to multiSkip and translation-invariance, and releases resources/portal.

Contributions:
- multiQVEC-CCA intrinsic evaluation that better correlates with downstream classification and parsing.
- Reproducible evaluation suite and resource release (portal, benchmarks, multilingual embeddings).
- Two scalable dictionary-based estimators (multiCCA, multiCluster) with competitive multilingual performance.

Strengths:
- Rigorous, broad evaluation across methods, metrics, and up to 59 languages, with analysis of intrinsic–extrinsic correlations.
- Practical, scalable methods (multiCCA) requiring only monolingual corpora plus bilingual dictionaries and extensive resource release.
- Clear exposition of estimation objectives and evaluation methods.
- Competitive empirical results: multiCCA leads on 59 languages; dictionary methods often top extrinsic tasks for 12-language set.

Weaknesses:
- Limited methodological novelty: multiCCA and multiCluster are straightforward extensions of prior work; main novelty is scale and evaluation.
- Supersense-based multilingual evaluation may suffer sparsity and inconsistencies; authors should quantify shared types/features and sensitivity to intersections.
- Coverage confounds: differing vocabulary coverage across methods may bias results; need per-method OOV/token rates and controlled intersection analyses.
- Inconsistent patterns in results (e.g., translation-invariance dominating word-translation, multiCluster doing well on parsing) lack explanation and targeted ablations.
- Need experiments to test hypotheses about tying, English-anchoring, and invariance effects (e.g., en↔X vs X↔Y, varying cluster sizes, symmetric shared-space alignment).
- Parsing setup departs from standard practice by freezing embeddings; must quantify loss vs fine-tuning and gains over random initialization.

Questions for authors (requested analyses):
- What is the absolute LAS/UAS loss from freezing embeddings vs allowing fine-tuning, and gains from pretrained embeddings in a standard trainable stack-LSTM parser?
- How were the correlations in Table 1 computed precisely (are they over the 17 configs and how are the 102 values formed)?
- Does training a single multiSkip have advantages over per-pair bilingual skip-gram models projected with multiCCA; any matched-data comparison?
- Were curated bilingual dictionaries (e.g., Wiktionary) evaluated vs Europarl/Google Translate, and what is their effect?
- What are sizes and intersections of multilingual supersense matrices per language, and how sensitive are multiQVEC(-CCA) scores to these intersections?
- Please report per-language OOV token rates for parsing and document classification per method, and rerun tasks with fixed UNK handling and intersection-of-coverage vocabularies.
- Have you tried symmetric shared-space alignment (joint CCA or Procrustes barycenter) instead of anchoring to English, and does it improve non-English pair translation and multiQVEC(-CCA)?
