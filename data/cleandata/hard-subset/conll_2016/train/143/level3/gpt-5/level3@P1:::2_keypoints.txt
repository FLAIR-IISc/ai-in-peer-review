- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods: MultiCluster (shared clusters of translationally equivalent word forms) and MultiCCA (project monolingual embeddings into a shared English-pivot space via CCA).
- Introduces multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, multilingual intrinsic evaluation metrics based on supersense annotations, and provides an evaluation web portal.
- Experiments across up to 59 languages show multiQVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than word similarity/translation metrics, and MultiCCA is generally more robust than MultiCluster at scale.
- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA substantially improves intrinsic–extrinsic correlation, addressing an evaluation gap with community value.
- Contribution: Public evaluation portal and resources standardize benchmarking across intrinsic/extrinsic metrics and languages, improving reproducibility.
- Contribution: Practical dictionary-based methods that avoid parallel corpora and scale to 59 languages, with MultiCCA offering strong coverage/performance.
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence that multiQVEC-CCA better reflects downstream utility (high correlation with document classification).
- Strength: Broad, large-scale evaluation suite (59 languages; multiple intrinsic and extrinsic tasks) increases generality of findings.
- Strength: Public release, web portal, and careful documentation enhance practicality and reproducibility.
- Strength: Clear exposition, concrete design choices, and nuanced caveats increase trustworthiness.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of why methods behave differently (e.g., why MultiCluster degrades from 12 to 59 languages).
- Weakness: Insufficient sensitivity analyses on key design choices (cluster size, τ, frequency cutoffs, pivot choice, mapping conditioning, dimensionality).
- Weakness: Evaluation confounds—training data differences and use of Google Translate dictionaries for both training and evaluation may bias comparisons.
- Weakness: Incomplete empirical support for QVEC-CCA’s claimed advantages—monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA comparisons are missing.
- Weakness: Dependence on English resources/pivoting and limited supersense annotations across languages limit generality.
- Question: Can authors analyze MultiCluster failures in terms of cluster-size distributions, sense mixing, and dictionary precision/recall at τ=0.1?
- Question: Why use an English pivot for MultiCCA instead of joint multi-view CCA, and how sensitive are results to pivot choice, orthogonality constraints, and numerical stability?
- Question: How do hyperparameters (window size, dimensionality, frequency thresholds, normalization) and random seeds affect conclusions?
- Question: Could authors provide intersection-vocabulary (coverage-controlled) results for intrinsic and extrinsic metrics to isolate method quality?
- Question: Portal details—will it accept private uploads, are datasets versioned, and are metric scripts publicly available?
- Additional: Clarify which MultiSkip variant is implemented and how it maps to cited prior work.
- Additional: Expand related work connections to cross-lingual word classes, orthogonal bilingual mappings, and CCA-based lexicon induction.
- Additional: Include monolingual correlation results to support QVEC-CCA’s basis-invariance and normalization claims.
- Additional: Specify which cross-lingual word similarity datasets are combined (line 304 ambiguity).
- Additional: Fix formatting issues (equations exceeding margins) and check CCA notation/conditioning and matrix index consistency.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — strong community contribution via improved evaluation and resources, but needs deeper analysis and sensitivity studies.
