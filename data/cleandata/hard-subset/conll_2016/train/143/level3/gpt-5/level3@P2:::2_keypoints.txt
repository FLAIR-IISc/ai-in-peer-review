Paper proposes multiCluster and multiCCA for dictionary-based multilingual embeddings, adapts QVEC to multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, and releases an evaluation portal and datasets for 50+ languages.  
Main contributions ranked: (1) multiQVEC-CCA and evaluation portal/datasets, (2) large-scale dictionary-based embeddings across 59 languages, (3) two estimation methods with multiCCA being simple and effective.

Strengths:
Strong practical impact via open web portal, data release, standardized dev/test splits, and emphasis on replicability.  
multiQVEC-CCA shows much stronger correlation with downstream tasks than word similarity/translation metrics.  
Comprehensive empirical evaluation across intrinsic/extrinsic tasks, methods, and many languages.  
Methods are simple and scalable: multiCCA’s pivot-to-English CCA and multiCluster clustering are easy to implement and broadly applicable.  
Paper is clearly written with explicit setups, hyperparameters, and thoughtful coverage-bias discussion.

Weaknesses:
Paper favors breadth over depth and lacks deep analyses explaining multiCluster’s drop in performance at 59 languages.  
Insufficient ablation and sensitivity studies for key design choices (dictionary threshold, cluster sizes, window/epoch settings, normalization, pivot choice, dictionary quality).  
Comparisons between dictionary- and parallel-based methods are confounded by differing resource quality/coverage; fairer matched-resource experiments are needed.  
Missing reported correlations for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA to support claims about QVEC-CCA improvements.  
Minor clarity/consistency issues: unclear citation mapping for multiSkip, vague dataset description, and formatting problems.

Questions for authors:
What drives multiCluster’s degradation at scale—dictionary noise, cluster-size limits, polysemy/hub nodes, or coverage—and can you provide ablations?  
How sensitive are results to pivot language, monolingual embedding choices (dimensions/algorithms), dictionary size/quality, skip-gram hyperparameters, and alignment constraints (orthogonality/Procrustes)?  
Can you run controlled experiments where all methods use the same bilingual signal to isolate method effects from resource differences?  
Please add monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlations with extrinsic tasks to substantiate improvement claims.  
Clarify precisely how multiSkip differs from Coulmance et al., Guo et al., and Luong et al. in objectives and resource use.

Additional comments:
Broaden related-work discussion to contrast with Klementiev et al., BilBOWA, Luong et al., Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Al-Rfou et al., Täckström et al., Och, and Haghighi et al.  
Relying on supersense annotations for only en/da/it limits multiQVEC/CCA generality; discuss plans for broader linguistic resources or harmonization.  
Despite limited novelty of methods, the improved intrinsic metric and evaluation portal/datasets make a valuable community contribution.
