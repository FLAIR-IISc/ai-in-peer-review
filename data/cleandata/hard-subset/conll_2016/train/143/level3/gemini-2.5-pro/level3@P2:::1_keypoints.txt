- Problem tackled: building and evaluating shared-space word embeddings for many languages (59) using resource-light methods.  
- Methods proposed: multiCluster (clusters translationally equivalent words) and multiCCA (linear projections from monolingual spaces into an English-anchored space using bilingual dictionaries).  
- New metric: multiQVEC-CCA uses CCA to align embeddings with linguistic feature space, is invariant to linear transforms, normalized, and correlates better with downstream tasks.  
- Resources released: 59-language pre-trained embeddings, monolingual data, dictionaries, and a public web portal for standardized evaluation.  

- Strength: identifies evaluation gap and shows multiQVEC-CCA better predicts downstream task performance via thorough correlation analysis.  
- Strength: dictionary-based multiCCA is scalable and practical, requiring only monolingual corpora and bilingual dictionaries (no parallel corpora).  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental design comparing four estimation methods across nine intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.  
- Strength: significant community contribution through open evaluation portal and released resources enabling reproducible research.  

- Weakness: inconsistent and counter-intuitive results (Table 3) with unclear narrative—multiCluster unexpectedly best on downstream tasks without adequate explanation.  
- Weakness: potential bias from differing vocabulary coverage across models; evaluations reported on unequal test subsets and intersection analysis is relegated to supplementary material.  
- Weakness: practical limits of multiQVEC-CCA due to reliance on multilingual supersense annotations available for few languages; reported correlations use only EN, IT, DA, raising generalization concerns and suggesting alternatives (e.g., universal POS) should be explored.  

- Question: please provide deeper analysis/hypotheses explaining inconsistent performance patterns, especially why multiCluster outperforms on extrinsic tasks while translation-invariance model dominates word translation.  
- Question: how much do embeddings contribute to parser performance—report baselines with random initialization and results when pre-trained embeddings are fine-tuned.  
- Question: how robust are multiCCA and multiCluster to noise/quality variation in input dictionaries; any analysis with smaller, high-quality lexicons?  
- Question: have you tested using universal POS tags instead of supersense tags for multiQVEC-CCA, and do they correlate well with semantic downstream tasks like document classification?
