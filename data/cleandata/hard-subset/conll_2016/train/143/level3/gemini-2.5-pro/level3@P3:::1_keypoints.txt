1. Summary: Proposes and compares four methods for single-space massively multilingual embeddings, introduces multiQVEC-CCA as a rotation-invariant intrinsic metric, evaluates across up to 59 languages, and provides a public evaluation portal.

2. Contribution: Provides a large-scale empirical comparison of dictionary-, parallel-, and matrix-factorization-based multilingual embedding methods across many tasks and languages.

3. Contribution: Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a CCA-based intrinsic evaluation that addresses QVEC's rotation sensitivity and shows stronger correlation with downstream tasks.

4. Contribution: Releases code, trained embeddings, and a web portal to support reproducible multilingual embedding evaluation.

5. Strength: Tackles an ambitious, relevant scale (59 languages) beyond typical bilingual or small-scale multilingual work.

6. Strength: multiQVEC-CCA is well-motivated, invariant to linear transformations, and shows high correlation (0.896) with document classification.

7. Strength: Paper is clear and commits to reproducibility through open-sourcing resources.

8. Weakness: Experimental results are inconsistent and counter-intuitive across tasks, with no deep analysis explaining why different methods win on different tasks.

9. Weakness: Unclear novelty of the translation-invariance method—paper does not clearly state what was newly contributed beyond Gardner et al. (2015).

10. Weakness: Evaluation is confounded by uncontrolled variables, making method comparisons hard to interpret.

11. Confound: Vocabulary coverage varies across methods (e.g., large accuracy differences with very different coverage), but comparisons are reported without controlled-vocabulary analyses.

12. Confound: multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA rely on supersense annotations across languages that may be inconsistent and have a small intersection, potentially making scores noisy.

13. Question: For dependency parsing, what are baseline numbers—how much do fixed vs. fine-tuned embeddings differ, and what gain do pretrained embeddings give over random initialization?

14. Question: What exactly is novel in your implementation of translation-invariance relative to Gardner et al. (2015)—was it extended to a joint multilingual objective, and if so how?

15. Question: How was the multilingual linguistic feature matrix for multiQVEC-CCA constructed from inconsistent supersense tags—what is the intersection size and its effect on metric stability?

16. Question: Have you evaluated the impact of dictionary quality (Google Translate vs. manually curated dictionaries) on embedding performance for the 47 languages using machine translations?

17. Question: What are the theoretical advantages of end-to-end multiSkip versus a two-stage pipeline (bilingual embeddings followed by multi-CCA projection into English)?
