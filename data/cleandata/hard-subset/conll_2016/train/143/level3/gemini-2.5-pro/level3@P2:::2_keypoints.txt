- Introduces multiCluster and multiCCA methods to build multilingual embeddings for up to 59 languages using monolingual data plus bilingual dictionaries.  
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic evaluation metric that better correlates with downstream tasks, and provides a public web evaluation portal.  
- Main contributions ranked: (1) multiQVEC-CCA + portal, (2) dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods, (3) large-scale empirical benchmark across tasks and languages.  
- High practical value: multiQVEC-CCA shows strong correlation with extrinsic tasks (e.g., 0.896 with document classification) and the portal promotes reproducibility.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across nine tasks and two language sets (12 and 59 languages), including a meta-evaluation of intrinsic metrics.  
- Methods are simple, intuitive, don’t require parallel corpora, and are competitive with parallel-data approaches.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and meticulously documented.  
- Prefers breadth over depth: lacks analysis explaining why methods differ in performance across tasks.  
- multiCluster suffers a large performance drop from 12 to 59 languages with only a vague “semantic ambiguity” explanation and no deeper analysis.  
- Missing sensitivity analyses for design choices (e.g., maximum cluster size for multiCluster; choice of English as pivot for multiCCA).  
- Empirical support for QVEC-CCA’s improvement is incomplete; needs direct monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation comparison.  
- Requests for authors: analyze whether multiCluster degradation is gradual or has a tipping point and whether cross-family clustering drives ambiguity.  
- Requests sensitivity experiments: effect of max cluster size and choice of pivot language (other pivots tested?).  
- Requests Table 1 to include monolingual QVEC vs monolingual QVEC-CCA correlations.  
- Minor clarifications: specify that “combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” refers to RG65 across six pairs.  
- Presentation fixes: equations exceeding column margins should be corrected.  
- Clarify the relationship between multiSkip and the cited prior works (Coulmance et al., Guo et al., Luong et al.).  
- Strengthen related work by discussing multilingual clustering (e.g., Täckström et al., 2012) and translational-context methods from parallel data (e.g., Gouws et al., Coulmance et al.).
