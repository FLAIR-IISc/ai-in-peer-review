- Proposes MultiCluster and MultiCCA for massively multilingual dictionary-based embeddings and evaluates them in 12- and 59-language settings, with mixed performance.
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a basis-invariant, normalized intrinsic metric that correlates strongly with downstream tasks, and releases a public evaluation portal.
- Main contributions: QVEC-CCA + evaluation platform (strongest contribution) and two scalable dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods.
- Strength: High practical value—QVEC-CCA shows strong correlation with document classification (r=0.896) and the portal enhances reproducibility.
- Strength: Thorough experiments across multiple intrinsic and extrinsic tasks and both small- and large-scale multilingual settings.
- Strength: Well-written paper with released code, embeddings, and evaluation data.
- Weakness: Prioritizes breadth over depth, offering limited analysis and few generalizable insights about what drives multilingual embedding success.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of key experimental results and design choices, e.g., unexplained MultiCluster degradation from 12→59 languages and lack of ablations on cluster size or pivot choice.
- Weakness: Claim of QVEC-CCA superiority is not fully supported—missing direct monolingual QVEC vs. QVEC-CCA correlation comparisons.
- Question: Please analyze why MultiCluster degrades from 12 to 59 languages (cluster size/coherence, graceful degradation, qualitative cluster differences).
- Question: Please report sensitivity to design choices—effect of maximum cluster size, pivot-language choice, and dictionary quality on MultiCCA/MultiCluster.
- Question: Add monolingual correlation scores for QVEC and QVEC-CCA to substantiate the claimed improvement.
- Additional: Strengthen related work coverage on multilingual embeddings, cross-lingual clustering, and translational context learning.
- Additional: Clarify citation inconsistencies around “multiSkip” implementation and related works.
- Additional: Fix vague wording about combined datasets and correct formatting issues with equations running past the margin.
