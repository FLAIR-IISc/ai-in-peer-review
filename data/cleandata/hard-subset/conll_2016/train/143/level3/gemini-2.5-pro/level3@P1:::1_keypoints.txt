Paper studies multilingual embedding estimation without parallel corpora and introduces dictionary-based methods multiCluster and multiCCA applied to 59 languages.  
Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an improved intrinsic metric that corrects QVEC’s theoretical flaws and correlates better with downstream tasks.  
Provides a public web portal with standardized datasets and evaluation scripts to promote reproducibility.  
Large-scale empirical comparison of dictionary-based vs. parallel-corpus-based methods across nine intrinsic and extrinsic tasks for up to 59 languages.  
Main contribution: a principled evaluation framework (multiQVEC-CCA) and a community resource for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation.  
Main contribution: one of the first broad empirical analyses of multilingual embedding techniques at large language scale.  
Strength: ambitious scope and valuable resource provision (59 languages + open evaluation portal).  
Strength: principled development and validation of multiQVEC-CCA using CCA to address basis sensitivity and normalization.  
Strength: thorough, multi-faceted experimental design comparing four estimation methods on diverse tasks.  
Strength: clear writing and presentation with well-articulated motivation and methods.  
Weakness: methodological novelty is unclear—multiCCA is a simple extension of prior work and some baseline implementations are insufficiently distinguished from originals.  
Weakness: inconsistent, puzzling experimental results (e.g., multiCluster outperforms on extrinsic tasks; translation-invariance excels only in word translation), hurting interpretability.  
Weakness: evaluation confounded by large differences in vocabulary coverage across models, requiring more central treatment than in supplementary material.  
Weakness: practical limits of multiQVEC-CCA due to dependence on cross-lingual linguistic annotations (supersense tags) that may be inconsistent or sparse.  
Question: report parser performance when embeddings are fine-tuned and when initialized randomly as baselines.  
Question: clarify exactly how Pearson correlation coefficients in Table 1 were computed from the 17 embeddings.  
Question: explain remaining advantages of joint training approaches (multiSkip) over two-step "train-then-project" pipelines like multiCCA.  
Question: how sensitive are multiCluster and multiCCA to dictionary quality; any experiments with manually curated high-precision bilingual dictionaries?
