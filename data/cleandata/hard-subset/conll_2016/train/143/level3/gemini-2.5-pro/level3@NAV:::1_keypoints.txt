- Summary: Proposes and evaluates four multilingual embedding methods (two dictionary-based: multiCluster, multiCCA; two parallel-data-based), introduces multiQVEC-CCA as a rotation-/linear-invariant intrinsic metric, trains embeddings for up to 59 languages, and releases a public evaluation portal.

- Contribution 1: Introduces and validates multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic metric invariant to linear transformations that better correlates with downstream tasks.

- Contribution 2: Provides a wide empirical comparison of dictionary-based vs. parallel-corpus-based multilingual embedding methods across many tasks, languages, and metrics.

- Contribution 3: Proposes two practical dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that do not require parallel corpora.

- Strength 1: Paper is very well written with clear descriptions of methods and evaluation framework.

- Strength 2: Rigorous focus on evaluation methodology, proposing an improved intrinsic metric and validating it against downstream tasks.

- Strength 3: Extensive experiments offering valuable comparative analysis of four embedding techniques on intrinsic and extrinsic tasks.

- Strength 4: Release of an open evaluation portal that aids reproducibility and lowers barriers for future research.

- Weakness 1: Novelty of the "Translation Invariance" method is unclear relative to Gardner et al. (2015); the paper should clarify its specific contribution (e.g., full multilingual extension).

- Weakness 2: Results in Table 3 are inconsistent/counter-intuitive, notably that multiCluster outperforms others despite collapsing semantics, requiring deeper analysis.

- Weakness 3: Experiments do not disentangle vocabulary coverage effects from true model performance, creating a confound in comparisons (Table 3).

- Weakness 4: multiQVEC-CCA depends on supersense annotations, raising scalability concerns and questions about handling tiny intersections of linguistic features across many languages.

- Question 1: For dependency parsing, what is the gain from using the pretrained embeddings vs. random initialization, and what is the performance drop when keeping embeddings fixed vs. fine-tuning?

- Question 2: Are the correlation scores in Table 1 averaged over all 17 embedding models mentioned in Section 5.1?

- Question 3: Are there theoretical or empirical advantages of the end-to-end multiSkip approach versus a two-stage pipeline of bilingual embeddings followed by multi-CCA projection?

- Question 4: Have the authors evaluated methods with professionally curated multilingual lexicons to measure the impact of dictionary quality versus extracted or machine-translated dictionaries?
