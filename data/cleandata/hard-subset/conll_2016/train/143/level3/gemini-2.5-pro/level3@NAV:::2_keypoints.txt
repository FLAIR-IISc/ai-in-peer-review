- Introduces MultiCluster and MultiCCA, two dictionary-based methods for shared-space multilingual embeddings, plus an improved intrinsic metric QVEC-CCA and a public evaluation portal.  
- QVEC-CCA and the web portal are the paper's strongest contributions, improving intrinsic evaluation and reproducibility.  
- MultiCluster and MultiCCA provide alternatives to parallel-data approaches and are competitive in some evaluation settings.  
- Strong practical value: the improved metric and portal offer useful tools for the community.  
- Extensive evaluation across up to 59 languages and nine intrinsic/extrinsic tasks gives broad empirical coverage.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and thorough documentation; data and code release is commendable.  
- Lacks depth in analysis: many results are reported without explanations for why methods succeed or fail, notably the MultiCluster discrepancy between 12- and 59-language setups.  
- Missing ablation and sensitivity studies on key design choices (e.g., maximum cluster size for MultiCluster, pivot language choice for MultiCCA).  
- Some claims lack supporting evidence (missing monolingual QVEC baselines) and there are minor issues (inconsistent citations, formatting errors, unclear phrasing).  
- Asks for deeper analysis of MultiCluster's performance drop across language counts and whether semantic ambiguity or cluster-size thresholds explain it.  
- Requests sensitivity analysis of MultiCluster and MultiCCA to parameters/design choices such as cluster size limits and pivot-language selection.
