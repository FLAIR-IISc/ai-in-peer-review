- Presents study of multilingual word embeddings for up to 59 languages, proposing two dictionary-only methods: multiCluster and multiCCA.
- multiCluster groups translationally equivalent words into clusters before training embeddings.
- multiCCA projects monolingual embedding spaces into a shared space by pivoting on English using CCA.
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation metric using CCA that fixes QVEC’s rotation and normalization issues.
- Conducts extensive experiments comparing methods on nine intrinsic and extrinsic tasks across 12 and 59 languages.
- Releases an open-source web portal, code, data, and pretrained embeddings for 59 languages to support standardized evaluation and reproducibility.
- Main contribution: principled intrinsic evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA) showing strong correlation with downstream performance.
- Main contribution: public evaluation portal and large multilingual resource release that aids reproducible research.
- Main contribution: two simple, effective dictionary-based methods for massively multilingual embeddings without parallel corpora.
- Strength: provides valuable community resources and demonstrates strong commitment to reproducibility.
- Strength: solid methodological improvement in evaluation, empirically supported by high correlation with downstream tasks.
- Strength: impressive experimental breadth and scale demonstrating scalability of methods.
- Strength: paper is clear, well-written, and well organized.
- Weakness: favors breadth over depth, with limited analysis explaining why results occur.
- Weakness: conflicting and unexplained performance of multiCluster between 12- and 59-language settings without deeper investigation.
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of design choices and hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., pivot language, cluster size).
- Question: request deeper analysis disentangling dictionary quality vs. semantic drift or scaling issues for multiCluster’s performance drop.
- Question: ask for monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results to confirm improvements in the monolingual setting.
- Question: ask rationale for choosing English as pivot in multiCCA and sensitivity to other pivot languages.
- Additional comment: clarify wording about aggregating cross-lingual word similarity datasets.
- Additional comment: clarify relationship of the multiSkip implementation to prior cited works.
- Additional comment: fix formatting of skipgram objective equations that exceed column width.
- Additional comment: expand related work to include other multilingual embedding methods using parallel corpora and multilingual clustering.
