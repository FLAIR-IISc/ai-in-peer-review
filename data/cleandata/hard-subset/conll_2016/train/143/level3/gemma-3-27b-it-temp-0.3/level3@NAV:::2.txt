## Review of “Estimating and Evaluating Multilingual Embeddings”

This paper presents two dictionary-based methods, MultiCluster and MultiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings, alongside a new evaluation metric, QVEC-CCA, and a web portal for evaluating such embeddings. The core contribution lies in the practical tooling – the web portal and improved QVEC measure – rather than groundbreaking algorithmic advancements. While the authors demonstrate a broad exploration of methods and evaluation across a substantial number of languages, the analysis feels somewhat superficial, favoring breadth over depth.

The strengths of this submission are clear. The authors provide a meticulously documented and well-written paper. The web portal is a valuable resource for the community, facilitating further research in multilingual embeddings. The introduction of QVEC-CCA, addressing limitations of the original QVEC, is a positive step towards more robust evaluation. The extensive experimental setup, covering 59 languages, is commendable. 

However, several weaknesses hinder the paper’s impact. The results are often inconclusive or lack detailed analysis. For example, the conflicting performance of MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups is not thoroughly investigated. The impact of key parameters within MultiCluster and MultiCCA (e.g., cluster size, threshold τ) is not discussed. The claim of QVEC-CCA improvement would be strengthened by including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (e.g., in Table 1) to demonstrate the improvement beyond the multilingual setting. The phrasing "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" (l.304) is misleading – it implies a composite dataset rather than separate evaluations. Minor formatting issues (equations exceeding column margins) and citation inconsistencies (lines 121/147) also detract from the presentation. Finally, the related work section feels incomplete, missing key references on multilingual embeddings (e.g., Hermann & Blunsom, 2014) and translational context.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more in-depth analysis of why MultiCluster performs differently in the 59-language versus 12-language setups? What specific factors contribute to this discrepancy?
2.  How sensitive are the results of MultiCluster and MultiCCA to variations in key parameters (e.g., cluster size, threshold τ)? Have you conducted any sensitivity analysis?
3.  Please include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results to support the claim that QVEC-CCA is an improvement over QVEC.
4.  Please clarify the correspondence between the citations of Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip (lines 121/147) and the description in section 2.3.
