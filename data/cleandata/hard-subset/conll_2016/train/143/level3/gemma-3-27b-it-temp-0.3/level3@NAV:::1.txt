## Review of "Estimating and Evaluating Multilingual Embeddings"

This paper presents a study on estimating and evaluating multilingual word embeddings for 59 languages. The authors propose four methods – multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and Translation Invariance – for embedding words into a shared space. Crucially, they also introduce multiQVEC-CCA, an improved evaluation metric, and provide a web portal for facilitating further research. The paper is generally well-written and clearly describes the proposed methods and experimental setup.

The primary contribution of this work, in my view, is the comprehensive evaluation framework, including both the multiQVEC-CCA metric and the publicly available evaluation portal. The development of a standardized, accessible evaluation suite is a significant step forward for the field, enabling more rigorous and reproducible research in multilingual embeddings. The extension of QVEC to a multilingual setting and addressing its theoretical shortcomings is a valuable addition. A secondary contribution lies in the empirical exploration of different dictionary-based embedding methods, providing insights into their relative performance across various languages and tasks.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. The novelty of the proposed methods, particularly concerning Translation Invariance, is not clearly articulated. The authors need to explicitly detail how their multilingual extension differs from and improves upon Gardner et al.’s work. A major concern is the use of supersense annotations across multiple languages. While acknowledged in a footnote, the potential for a tiny intersection of features and its impact on evaluation are not adequately addressed. Furthermore, the analysis of coverage effects on the results in Table 2 is lacking. The differing coverage between multiCluster and multiCCA, despite similar scores, raises questions about the fairness of the comparison. 

Perhaps the most puzzling aspect is the inconsistent results in Table 3. The fact that multiCluster, which discards inter-cluster semantics, performs best on extrinsic tasks while intrinsic metrics often favor other methods requires a more thorough explanation. The disconnect between intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations suggests potential issues with the evaluation setup or the methods themselves.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  What is the performance loss when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser, and what gains are observed from simply replacing random embeddings with these pretrained embeddings in the LSTM parser? This would help quantify the value of the pretraining.
2.  Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1? Clarifying this is crucial for understanding the statistical significance of the reported correlations.
3.  Are there advantages to multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings and then using multi-CCA to project between spaces? A comparative analysis would be insightful.
4.  Did the authors evaluate the methods with real, curated multilingual dictionaries, beyond those extracted via parallel alignments or Google Translate? This would strengthen the validity of the results.

**Additional Comments:**

The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the Google Translate-derived dictionaries used for some languages. A sensitivity analysis regarding the threshold τ used in dictionary creation would also be valuable.
