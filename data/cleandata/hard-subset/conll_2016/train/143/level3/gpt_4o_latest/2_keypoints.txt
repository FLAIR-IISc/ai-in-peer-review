- Introduces MultiCluster (clustering-based) and MultiCCA (CCA-based) methods for multilingual embeddings using monolingual data and bilingual dictionaries (no parallel corpora).  
- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an improved multilingual evaluation metric addressing QVEC’s sensitivity to basis rotation and dimensionality.  
- Develops a web portal for standardized evaluation and benchmarking of multilingual embeddings.  
- Evaluates methods on multiple intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, showing competitive performance in some cases.  
- Strength: Novel dictionary-based approaches broaden applicability to resource-scarce languages.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA meaningfully improves evaluation correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Strength: Practical contribution of a web portal promotes reproducibility and standardization.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly structured.  
- Weakness: Analysis lacks depth—conflicting MultiCluster results and hyperparameter effects are insufficiently explored.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical insight into why MultiCluster vs. MultiCCA perform differently.  
- Weakness: Missing correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA.  
- Weakness: Related work coverage omits several important prior studies in multilingual embeddings and clustering.  
- Weakness: Minor phrasing and formatting issues (e.g., terminology and equations exceeding margins).  
- Questions: Request more analysis on factors explaining MultiCluster’s variable performance across tasks.  
- Questions: Ask about sensitivity to bilingual dictionary quality/size and robustness in low-resource settings.  
- Questions: Why were monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlations omitted, and can they be included?  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address analysis depth, theoretical insights, missing results, and presentation issues.
