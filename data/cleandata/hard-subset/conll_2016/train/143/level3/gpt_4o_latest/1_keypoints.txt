- Presents four multilingual embedding estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, translation-invariance) and a novel intrinsic metric multiQVEC-CCA.  
- multiCluster builds multilingual clusters from dictionaries; multiCCA extends bilingual CCA to a multilingual English-anchored space.  
- Adapts Luong et al.'s bilingual method (multiSkip) and applies a translation-invariance approach via low-rank PMI alignment.  
- Proposes multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA to address QVEC’s theoretical issues and shows better correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Releases a web portal with evaluation tasks, data, embeddings, and tools to support reproducibility.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA is a significant, better-correlated intrinsic evaluation metric.  
- Strength: Dictionary-based methods scale to many languages (59) and do not require parallel corpora.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation combining intrinsic and extrinsic metrics with correlation analysis.  
- Strength: Open-source resources and a web portal promote reproducibility and community use.  
- Weakness: Translation-invariance method’s novelty is unclear and seems derivative of prior work.  
- Weakness: Evaluation scores may be confounded by differing coverage across methods; impact not fully resolved.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent results across metrics (e.g., multiCluster’s poor intrinsic but strong extrinsic performance) are unexplained.  
- Weakness: No exploration of curated multilingual dictionaries as alternatives to extracted/translated dictionaries.  
- Questions for authors: quantify performance loss when fixing embeddings in parsing versus random embeddings; clarify Table 1 averaging and embedding choices; justify multiSkip vs bilingual+multiCCA advantages; report experiments or challenges using curated dictionaries.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions pending clarification of translation-invariance novelty and answers to the reviewer’s questions.
