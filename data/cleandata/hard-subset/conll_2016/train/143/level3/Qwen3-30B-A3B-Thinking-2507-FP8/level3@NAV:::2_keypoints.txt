- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA) and a new QVEC-CCA evaluation metric for multilingual contexts.  
- Provides a comprehensive web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings.  
- Main claimed contributions: novel dictionary-only multilingual methods, improved QVEC-CCA that better correlates with downstream tasks, and a practical evaluation portal.  
- Strength: clearly written and meticulously documented paper.  
- Strength: web portal is a valuable practical resource that will facilitate future research.  
- Strength: QVEC-CCA shows better correlation with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics.  
- Strength: evaluation is broad across multiple languages and tasks, with competitive method performance on several metrics.  
- Weakness: favors breadth over depth and lacks sufficient analysis of key findings.  
- Critical missing analysis: no explanation for conflicting MultiCluster performance between 59-language and 12-language setups.  
- Missing results: monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA results are omitted from Table 1, weakening the claim of improvement.  
- Presentation/citation issues: inconsistent citations for MultiSkip and a misleading phrase about dataset combination.  
- Formatting issues: two equations exceed column margins.  
- Methodological gaps: lacks discussion of parameter sensitivity and choices in MultiCluster and CCA.  
- Suggested fixes: provide deeper analysis of conflicting MultiCluster results, add monolingual evaluations, and correct/clarify citation and wording issues.  
- Overall: practical contributions are valuable but the paper needs stronger theoretical and empirical discussion to establish clearer research insights.
