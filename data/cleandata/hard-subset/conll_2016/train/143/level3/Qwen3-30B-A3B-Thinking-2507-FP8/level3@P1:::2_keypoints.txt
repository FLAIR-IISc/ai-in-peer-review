- Proposes multiQVEC-CCA, an intrinsic evaluation metric using CCA to address QVEC's theoretical shortcomings.  
- Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding estimation methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) that do not require parallel data.  
- Provides a web portal for standardized, reproducible evaluation of multilingual embeddings with dev/test modes.  
- multiQVEC-CCA shows substantially higher correlation with downstream tasks (document classification, dependency parsing) than prior intrinsic metrics.  
- The web portal will lower the barrier to entry and encourage sound experimentation practices in multilingual embedding research.  
- multiCCA achieves strong empirical performance, often outperforming multiCluster and other baselines, especially in the 59-language setup using English as a reference.  
- Major weakness: insufficient analysis of why multiCluster performs well on 12 languages but poorly on 59, with no discussion of mitigation strategies.  
- Major weakness: lacks thorough investigation of sensitivity to estimation parameters and methodological choices (e.g., cluster size, dictionary threshold τ).  
- Major weakness: claim that multiQVEC-CCA improves over QVEC is under-supported due to missing monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA comparison results.  
- Minor issues: misleading wording about dataset composition, equations exceeding column margins, and inconsistent citations for MultiSkip.  
- Paper is well written and meticulously documented; the portal and metric have clear practical value.  
- Overclaim concern: assertion that massively multilingual embeddings will be crucial in the future is not convincingly supported by the modest empirical gains reported.  
- Questions requested: detailed analysis of multiCluster scaling behavior, sensitivity to cluster-size and τ, inclusion of monolingual QVEC/QVEC-CCA results, and recommendations for standardizing supersense annotations.
