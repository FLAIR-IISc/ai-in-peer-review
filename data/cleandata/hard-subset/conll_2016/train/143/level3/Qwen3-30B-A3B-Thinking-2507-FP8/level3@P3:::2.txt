

# Review of "MultiCluster and MultiCCA: Dictionary-Based Multilingual Embeddings" (CONLL 2016)

## Summary
This paper introduces two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings (multiCluster and multiCCA), a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings. The authors evaluate these methods across a range of intrinsic and extrinsic tasks, demonstrating that their proposed evaluation metric correlates better with downstream tasks than previous metrics.

## Main Contributions
1. **Web portal for multilingual embedding evaluation**: A practical resource that will significantly facilitate future research in multilingual NLP by enabling easy evaluation of embeddings across multiple metrics.
2. **Improved evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA)**: A novel metric that addresses theoretical shortcomings of previous evaluation methods and demonstrates better correlation with downstream tasks.
3. **Dictionary-based embedding methods**: Two methods (multiCluster and multiCCA) for estimating multilingual embeddings using dictionaries and monolingual data, without requiring parallel corpora.

## Strengths
1. **High practical value**: The web portal is a substantial contribution that will benefit the entire community by providing a standardized evaluation framework. The portal's design (with development/test modes) encourages sound experimentation practices, and the open-source release of all methods is highly commendable.

2. **Superior evaluation metric**: The multiQVEC-CCA metric demonstrates significantly better correlation with downstream tasks (0.896 with document classification and 0.273 with parsing) compared to previous intrinsic metrics (e.g., 0.386 for word similarity with document classification). This is a meaningful contribution to the evaluation methodology of multilingual embeddings.

3. **Well-written and meticulously documented**: The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with thorough documentation of methods, data, and evaluation protocols. This makes the work highly reproducible and accessible to researchers.

## Weaknesses
1. **Breadth over depth**: The paper presents a wide range of experiments but lacks sufficient depth in analyzing results. For example, the paper doesn't adequately explain why multiCluster performs poorly with 59 languages (Table 2) but competes well with 12 languages (Table 3). The authors should investigate whether the semantic ambiguity from clustering is the root cause and provide a more nuanced analysis.

2. **Insufficient parameter analysis**: The paper doesn't thoroughly investigate how estimation parameters (e.g., cluster size in multiCluster, threshold τ for dictionary construction) affect performance. This is particularly important given that multiCluster's performance varies significantly between the 59- and 12-language setups.

3. **Lack of supporting evidence for metric improvement**: The claim that multiQVEC-CCA improves upon QVEC is not fully supported. The paper should include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (e.g., in Table 1) to demonstrate the improvement. Without these, the claim appears unsubstantiated.

4. **Minor issues**: The phrasing "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" (l.304) is misleading as it suggests a single combined dataset rather than multiple separate datasets. Two equations on p.3 exceed the column margin, and there's a citation inconsistency between Lines 121/147 (citing Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip) and Section 2.3 (only mentioning Luong et al.).

## Questions to Authors
1. Could you provide a deeper analysis of why multiCluster performs so poorly with 59 languages (Table 2) compared to the 12-language setup (Table 3)? Is the semantic ambiguity from clustering the primary issue, and how might this be mitigated?

2. Would it be possible to include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results in Table 1 to better support your claim that multiQVEC-CCA is an improvement over QVEC?

3. Could you investigate the sensitivity of your methods to key parameters (e.g., cluster size in multiCluster, threshold τ for dictionary construction) and share how these choices impact performance?

## Overall Assessment
The paper has high practical value with its web portal and improved evaluation metric, making it a useful resource for the community. However, it provides limited new research insights due to its breadth-over-depth approach. While the methods are well-implemented and evaluated, the paper lacks the depth of analysis needed to fully understand the strengths and limitations of the proposed approaches. The practical contributions are strong enough to warrant acceptance, but the authors should address the weaknesses outlined above in a revision.
