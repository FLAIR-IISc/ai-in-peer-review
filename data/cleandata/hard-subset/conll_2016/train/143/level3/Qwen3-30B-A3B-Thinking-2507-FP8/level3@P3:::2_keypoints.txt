- Summary: Introduces two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), and a web portal; shows the metric correlates better with downstream tasks.

- Contribution: Web portal for standardized multilingual embedding evaluation that facilitates reproducible research.

- Contribution: multiQVEC-CCA, a proposed intrinsic metric claimed to fix theoretical issues and correlate better with downstream tasks.

- Contribution: Two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that estimate multilingual embeddings from dictionaries and monolingual data without parallel corpora.

- Strength: High practical value—portal and open-source release encourage sound experimentation and community use.

- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows substantially better correlation with downstream tasks than prior intrinsic metrics.

- Strength: Paper is well-written, thoroughly documented, and highly reproducible.

- Weakness: Breadth over depth—insufficient analysis of why multiCluster performs poorly with 59 languages but well with 12 languages.

- Weakness: Insufficient parameter analysis—effects of cluster size, dictionary threshold τ, and other hyperparameters are not explored.

- Weakness: Claim that multiQVEC-CCA improves over QVEC is under-supported; monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlations are missing.

- Weakness: Minor presentation issues and citation inconsistencies (misleading phrasing about datasets, equations exceeding margins, inconsistent citations for MultiSkip).

- Question: Please analyze why multiCluster fails for 59 languages and whether semantic ambiguity from clustering is the cause and how to mitigate it.

- Question: Please add monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results (e.g., in Table 1) to substantiate metric improvement claims.

- Question: Please report sensitivity analyses for key parameters (cluster size, τ) and their impact on performance.

- Overall assessment: Strong practical contribution (portal and metric) deserving acceptance, but lacks depth of analysis on method limitations and parameter sensitivity.
