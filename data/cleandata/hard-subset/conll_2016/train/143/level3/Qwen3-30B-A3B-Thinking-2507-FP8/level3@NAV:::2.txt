

This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods for multilingual word embeddings (MultiCluster and MultiCCA) and introduces QVEC-CCA, an improved evaluation metric that replaces QVEC's correlation with CCA for multilingual contexts. The authors also present a comprehensive web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings.

The primary contributions are: (1) the novel multilingual embedding estimation methods that require only dictionaries and monolingual data; (2) the improved QVEC-CCA evaluation metric that better correlates with downstream tasks; and (3) the practical web portal enabling easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings.

Strengths include the paper's excellent writing and meticulous documentation, the practical value of the web portal (which will facilitate future research), and the demonstration that QVEC-CCA correlates better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics. The evaluation across multiple languages and tasks is thorough, and the proposed methods show competitive performance in several metrics.

However, the paper's main weakness is its tendency to favor breadth over depth. The authors present results across many languages and metrics but lack sufficient analysis of key findings. Most critically, they fail to explain the conflicting MultiCluster performance between the 59-language and 12-language setups, which is essential for understanding the method's limitations. Additionally, the paper omits monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA results in Table 1, which would strengthen the claim that QVEC-CCA improves upon QVEC.

Other weaknesses include citation inconsistencies (Lines 121/147 cite Coulmance et al. and Guo et al. for MultiSkip while Section 2.3 only mentions Luong et al.), a misleading phrase about "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" (l.304), and two equations exceeding column margins. The paper also misses opportunities to discuss parameter sensitivity and methodological choices in MultiCluster and CCA.

The authors should address these issues by providing deeper analysis of the conflicting MultiCluster results, adding the requested monolingual evaluation results, and clarifying the citation inconsistencies. While the practical contributions are valuable, the paper would benefit from more rigorous theoretical and empirical discussion to establish stronger research insights.
