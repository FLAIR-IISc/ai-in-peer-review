- Proposes multiCluster and multiCCA to learn multilingual embeddings for 59 languages using only monolingual data and bilingual dictionaries (no parallel corpora).
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a novel intrinsic evaluation metric that reportedly correlates much better with downstream tasks than previous metrics.
- Releases a web portal and infrastructure for standardized evaluation of multilingual embeddings, including data and upload interface.
- multiQVEC-CCA shows high correlation with document classification (0.896) and dependency parsing (0.273), outperforming prior intrinsic metrics.
- The web portal is positioned as a community resource that will lower barriers for reproducible evaluation and comparison.
- Methods (multiCCA projecting into English anchor space; multiCluster grouping translationally equivalent words) are clearly described and accessible.
- Novelty of the estimation methods is unclear relative to prior work (e.g., Gardner et al. 2015) and needs clearer differentiation.
- Multilingual supersense annotations used for evaluation are limited and inconsistent across languages, potentially undermining the metric’s validity.
- Evaluation results are inconsistent: translation-invariance scores highest on word translation while multiCluster scores highest on extrinsic tasks, with no convincing explanation.
- Coverage differences between methods (e.g., vocabulary intersection rates) materially affect evaluation scores but are not sufficiently analyzed in the main paper.
- Supplementary analysis on intersecting vocabularies is mentioned but not discussed in the main text, leaving open questions about fair comparisons.
- Question: Why does multiCluster, which forces translationally equivalent words to share one vector (losing context), yield better downstream task performance?
- Question: How do the authors reconcile multiQVEC-CCA’s reported higher correlation with downstream tasks with Table 3 showing multiCCA as best for document classification?
- Question: Did the authors assess the impact of using Google Translate-derived dictionaries versus manually curated dictionaries on embedding quality?
- Question: How do the authors justify generalizing results to 59 languages when the main evaluation in Table 2 covers only English, Italian, and Danish?
