- Proposes methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) to estimate shared word embeddings for 50+ languages using monolingual data and pairwise dictionaries.
- Introduces a new intrinsic evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, claimed to correlate better with downstream tasks.
- Provides a practical web portal supporting nine evaluation metrics, standard datasets, and easy method comparison.
- Strong empirical evidence that multiQVEC-CCA correlates much better (0.896) with downstream document classification than prior intrinsic metrics.
- The web portal will lower the barrier for multilingual embedding evaluation and has immediate practical value.
- Demonstrates scalability by training multilingual embeddings across 59 languages, with multiCCA performing well at this scale.
- Paper lacks deep analysis explaining why multiCluster degrades from 12 to 59 languages.
- Insufficient exploration of design choices and parameter sensitivity (e.g., cluster size, threshold τ) affecting results.
- Weak support for the claim that multiQVEC-CCA improves over QVEC due to missing monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation results.
- Requests from authors: analyze multiCluster scalability issues and propose fixes.
- Requests from authors: provide parameter sensitivity analysis for multiCluster and other methods.
- Requests from authors: include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results to substantiate the metric claim.
- Minor issues: misleading phrasing about dataset combination, two equations exceed column margin, and a citation inconsistency for MultiSkip references.
- Suggests adding related work on multilingual embeddings (e.g., Täckström et al. 2012, Haghighi et al. 2008) and recent clustering/translational-context papers.
- Overall: valuable practical contributions (metric and portal) but needs deeper analysis and stronger methodological support.
