- Summary: Proposes four methods for multilingual word embeddings (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation Invariance) across 59 languages, introduces multiQVEC-CCA evaluation, and provides a web evaluation portal.

- Contribution 1: multiQVEC-CCA — a new intrinsic evaluation metric that addresses theoretical flaws in prior metrics and shows better correlation with downstream tasks.

- Contribution 2: Dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) — practical approaches for multilingual embeddings that don't require parallel data, though their novelty is limited.

- Contribution 3: Web evaluation portal — a practical resource to standardize and facilitate multilingual embedding evaluation.

- Strength 1: Strong evaluation evidence — multiQVEC-CCA correlates much better with downstream tasks (e.g., 0.896 with document classification) than standard intrinsic metrics.

- Strength 2: Clear presentation and practicality — well-written paper with thorough explanations and a useful public evaluation tool.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive analysis — thorough comparison showing standard intrinsic metrics poorly predict downstream performance compared to the proposed metric.

- Weakness 1: Unclear novelty in translation invariance extension — the multilingual extension of Gardner et al. (2015) is not clearly distinguished as novel.

- Weakness 2: Supersense annotation limitations — cross-language supersense intersection is tiny and not adequately addressed, risking misleading evaluation.

- Weakness 3: Inconsistent results (Table 3) — multiCluster performs best on extrinsic tasks despite low intrinsic scores, requiring explanation.

- Weakness 4: Coverage effects unaddressed — paper notes coverage differences between methods but doesn't analyze how coverage impacts results, especially for parsing.

- Question 1: What is the performance loss when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser, and what gains come from replacing random embeddings with these pretrained embeddings in the LSTM parser?

- Question 2: Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?

- Question 3: Does multiSkip offer advantages over learning bilingual embeddings and then applying multi-CCA to align spaces?

- Question 4: Were real multilingual dictionaries used to evaluate dictionary extraction, or only parallel alignments/Google Translate-derived lexicons?
