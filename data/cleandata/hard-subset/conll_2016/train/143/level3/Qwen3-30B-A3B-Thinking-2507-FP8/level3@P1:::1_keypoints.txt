- Contributions: introduces multiQVECCCA evaluation metric, a web portal for multilingual embedding evaluation, and two dictionary-based estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for 59 languages.  
- multiQVECCCA strength: addresses theoretical shortcomings of prior metrics and shows stronger correlation with downstream tasks (0.896 doc classification, 0.273 dependency parsing).  
- Web portal strength: provides standardized evaluation metrics and dev/test data across many languages, aiding reproducibility.  
- Practical contribution: demonstrates feasiblity of building multilingual embeddings across 59 languages using only dictionaries and monolingual data.  
- Novelty concern: multiCCA’s novelty is unclear relative to Gardner et al. (2015) translation-invariance; theoretical/practical differences are not clearly explained.  
- Supersense annotation limitation: multilingual supersense features may have a small intersection across languages (EN, IT, DA), potentially limiting evaluation effectiveness and not fully analyzed.  
- Coverage effects: differences in word coverage across methods (e.g., multiCluster 70.9% vs. multiCCA 69.3%) likely affect scores but are not adequately analyzed (no intersection-restricted evaluation).  
- Inconsistent results: puzzling mismatch where multiCluster wins extrinsic tasks while translation invariance dominates word translation; the paper lacks explanation for this disconnect.  
- Dependency parsing question: request for comparison of fixed vs. trainable embeddings and the gain from replacing random embeddings with pretrained ones in LSTM parser.  
- Table 1 clarification: ask whether Table 1 averages over the 17 embeddings in Section 5.1 or reflects a different setup.  
- multiSkip question: ask whether multiSkip has advantages over learning bilingual embeddings then projecting with multiCCA, given multiSkip’s reliance on parallel data.  
- Dictionary source question: ask if methods were evaluated using real multilingual dictionaries (e.g., thesauri) instead of Google Translate–extracted dictionaries.  
- Overall judgment: valuable contributions in evaluation metric and portal, but needs clearer novelty claims for estimation methods, deeper analysis of coverage effects, and explanations for intrinsic/extrinsic inconsistencies.
