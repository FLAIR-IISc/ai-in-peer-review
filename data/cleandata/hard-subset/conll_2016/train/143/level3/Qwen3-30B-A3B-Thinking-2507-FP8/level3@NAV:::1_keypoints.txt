- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation Invariance) and introduces multiQVEC-CCA as a new evaluation metric.  
- Main contributions: (1) multiQVEC-CCA addressing theoretical shortcomings and better correlating with downstream tasks, (2) a web portal for evaluation, (3) dictionary-based methods to estimate multilingual embeddings without parallel data.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA effectively provides a rotation-invariant intrinsic metric and shows stronger correlation with extrinsic tasks (Table 1).  
- Strength: The web portal is a valuable practical resource that will facilitate future research.  
- Strength: The paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Weakness: Novelty claim is problematic because the translation invariance method largely overlaps with Gardner et al. (2015) and needs explicit clarification of the multilingual extension.  
- Weakness: The supersense annotation approach risks tiny feature intersections across languages, which the paper does not adequately address.  
- Weakness: Coverage differences significantly affect results (Table 2), but the authors do not analyze this effect.  
- Weakness: Inconsistency in Table 3—multiCluster performs best on extrinsic tasks despite discarding inter-cluster semantics, while translation invariance dominates word translation—creates a puzzling pattern.  
- Question: What performance loss occurs when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser?  
- Question: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?  
- Question: Are there advantages to multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings first and then using multi-CCA?  
- Question: Did the authors evaluate with real multilingual dictionaries rather than Google Translate?
