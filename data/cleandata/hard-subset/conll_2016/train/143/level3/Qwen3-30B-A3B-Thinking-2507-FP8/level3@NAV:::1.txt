

This paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and Translation Invariance) and introduces multiQVEC-CCA, an evaluation method designed to better correlate with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics. The main contributions are: (1) the improved evaluation method multiQVEC-CCA that addresses theoretical shortcomings of previous metrics and shows stronger correlation with extrinsic tasks; (2) a web portal for easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings; and (3) dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual embeddings without requiring parallel data.

Strengths: The paper's most significant contribution is multiQVEC-CCA, which effectively addresses the lack of rotation-invariant evaluation metrics for multilingual embeddings. The evaluation shows clear improvements over existing intrinsic metrics in correlating with downstream tasks (Table 1). The web portal is a valuable practical contribution that will facilitate future research in this area. The paper is well-structured and clearly written.

Weaknesses: The novelty claim is problematic as the translation invariance method largely overlaps with Gardner et al. (2015), requiring explicit clarification of the multilingual extension. The supersense annotation approach (footnote 9) risks tiny feature intersections across languages, which isn't adequately addressed. Coverage differences significantly impact results (Table 2), yet the authors don't analyze this effect. Most concerning is the inconsistency in Table 3, where multiCluster performs best on extrinsic tasks despite discarding inter-cluster semantics, while translation invariance dominates word translation, creating a puzzling pattern.

Questions to authors: 1) What performance loss occurs when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser? 2) Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1? 3) Are there advantages to multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings first and then using multi-CCA? 4) Did authors evaluate with real multilingual dictionaries rather than Google Translate?
