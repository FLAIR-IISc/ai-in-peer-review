- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding estimation methods: multiCluster and multiCCA.
- Introduces a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, using CCA to improve on QVEC.
- Provides a publicly available web portal and releases code/data for standardized evaluation.
- Demonstrates feasibility of estimating embeddings for 59 languages using only bilingual dictionaries and monolingual data.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and practical tools rather than novel theory.
- Strength: meticulous documentation and public release increase practical value.
- Weakness: paper lacks depth in analysis despite broad technical scope.
- Inconsistencies in Table 3 across evaluation metrics are not sufficiently analyzed.
- multiCluster shows degraded performance when scaling from 12 to 59 languages; causes (cluster size, dictionary quality, low-resource influence) are unexplored.
- No sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters or methodological choices (e.g., threshold τ = 0.1 and embedding dimensionality 512) and no justification given.
- Claim that multiQVEC-CCA outperforms QVEC is under-supported; monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlations are missing for direct comparison.
- Minor issues: misleading phrasing about dataset combination, equations exceeding column margins, and citation/attribution inconsistencies for MultiSkip.
- Related work coverage is limited; should include additional literature on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context (e.g., Al-Rfou et al., Hermann & Blunsom, Täckström et al.).
- Questions for authors: explain multiCluster performance difference between 59- and 12-language setups.
- Questions for authors: justify τ = 0.1 and provide sensitivity analysis over threshold and dictionary construction methods.
- Questions for authors: provide correlation results for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA analogous to Table 1.
- Questions for authors: clarify the attribution/citation relationship for MultiSkip between Luong et al., Coulmance et al., and Guo et al.
- Conclusion: valuable practical contribution with improved evaluation and resources, but limited scientific impact due to insufficient analysis and justification of design choices.
