## Review of “Estimating and Evaluating Multilingual Embeddings”

This paper introduces methods for estimating and evaluating multilingual word embeddings, proposing two dictionary-based estimation techniques – MultiCluster and MultiCCA – and a refined evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA. A key contribution is the development of a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, providing a valuable resource to the community.

The primary strength of this work lies in the practical value of the evaluation portal and the improved QVEC-CCA metric. The portal lowers the barrier to entry for researchers working with multilingual embeddings and facilitates more standardized evaluation. While the improvements over existing QVEC methods appear modest, the focus on improving evaluation is commendable. Furthermore, the exploration of dictionary-based approaches to multilingual embedding is a worthwhile endeavor, especially given the limited availability of parallel corpora.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of depth and analysis. While breadth is apparent in the evaluation across numerous languages and tasks, the discussion of results is often superficial. The conflicting performance of MultiCluster between the 59- and 12-language setups is particularly concerning and warrants a more thorough investigation. The authors should clarify why the increased semantic ambiguity from the larger cluster size negatively impacts performance, and what adjustments could mitigate this. 

Further, a deeper analysis of the effects of estimation parameters (e.g., cluster size, SGD epochs) and methodological decisions (e.g., dictionary creation) is needed. Including monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results, as suggested, would provide a stronger baseline to support the claims of improvement for multiQVEC-CCA. The phrasing regarding "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" is indeed misleading and should be clarified. Additionally, the citation inconsistencies regarding MultiSkip (lines 121/147 vs. section 2.3) need to be addressed. 

Finally, the related work section feels incomplete. Key papers on multilingual embedding techniques beyond those specifically cited, as well as more recent work on clustering for cross-lingual transfer and the use of translational context, should be discussed.

In conclusion, while this paper provides a useful resource and proposes some interesting ideas, it ultimately prioritizes breadth over depth, resulting in limited new research insights.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a detailed analysis of the conflicting results for MultiCluster in the 59- vs. 12-language setups? What factors contribute to the performance decrease with a larger number of languages?
2.  How sensitive are the performance of MultiCluster and MultiCCA to variations in the estimation parameters (e.g., cluster size, learning rate, number of epochs)? Please provide an ablation study.
3.  Can you clarify the discrepancies in citations regarding MultiSkip (lines 121/147 vs. section 2.3)?
4.  Please provide monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results to better contextualize the improvements achieved by multiQVEC-CCA.
