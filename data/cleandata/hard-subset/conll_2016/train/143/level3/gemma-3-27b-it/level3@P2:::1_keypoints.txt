- Introduces multiCluster and multiCCA for dictionary-based multilingual embedding estimation, and extends QVEC to multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA; releases a web portal, code, and data.  
- Main contribution is a systematic multilingual evaluation framework and the novel multiQVEC-CCA metric combining intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations.  
- Public release of embeddings, code, and an evaluation portal improves reproducibility and lowers barrier to entry.  
- Large-scale experiments training embeddings for 59 languages provide a substantial benchmark and insights into multilingual embedding challenges.  

Strengths:  
- Comprehensive evaluation combining intrinsic and extrinsic tasks with careful attention to metric limitations.  
- multiQVEC-CCA and the correlation analysis (Table 1) offer evidence for improved evaluation over prior metrics.  
- Resource creation (59-language embeddings, code, portal) and clear documentation benefit the community.  
- Clear, well-written exposition with adequate technical and experimental detail.  
- Authors explicitly discuss limitations of existing intrinsic metrics (coverage, vocabulary mismatch).  

Weaknesses:  
- Estimation methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) are incrementally novel and insufficiently differentiated from prior work (e.g., Gardner et al.).  
- Supersense-tag evaluation may be biased by feature overlap across languages; mitigation is inadequately explained.  
- Experimental results are inconsistent and puzzling (intrinsic metrics often not favoring multilingual methods; unexpected extrinsic performance), lacking deeper analysis.  
- Coverage effects are critical but under-explored; vocabulary-intersection analyses are mentioned but not presented.  

Questions for authors:  
- How much does parser performance degrade when embeddings are fixed, and what gains occur replacing random embeddings with these pretrained embeddings?  
- Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings in Section 5.1, and are the reported correlations statistically significant?  
- What concrete advantages does multiSkip provide over learning bilingual embeddings then projecting with multiCCA, and in which scenarios?  
- Were evaluations repeated with high-quality curated bilingual dictionaries to assess robustness to noisy dictionary extraction?  

Additional suggestions:  
- Provide a more detailed discussion of limitations for each estimation method.  
- Include a deeper error analysis on extrinsic tasks to clarify method strengths and weaknesses.  
- Add visualizations of the learned embedding spaces to aid interpretation.
