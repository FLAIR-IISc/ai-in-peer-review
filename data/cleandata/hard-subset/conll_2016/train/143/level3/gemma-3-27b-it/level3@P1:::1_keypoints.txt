- Paper proposes dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA), extends QVEC to multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA, and releases a web portal and code across 59 languages.  
- Strength: Extensive empirical evaluation across many languages, intrinsic/extrinsic metrics, and downstream tasks providing a useful benchmark.  
- Strength: Novel evaluation metric multiQVEC-CCA addresses QVEC’s theoretical issues (rotation/linear invariance) and shows better correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Strength: Public web portal, code, and trained embeddings improve reproducibility and accessibility for future research.  
- Weakness: Novelty of multiCluster and multiCCA is limited or not clearly distinguished from prior work (e.g., Gardner et al. 2015); need clearer articulation of what is truly new.  
- Weakness: Supersense annotation inconsistencies across languages may bias evaluations; impact of these inconsistencies is not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Weakness: Results may be skewed by vocabulary coverage differences; inconsistent findings (e.g., intrinsic metrics not favoring multilingual methods) need deeper investigation.  
- Weakness: Unclear empirical advantage of multiSkip over a simpler pipeline of bilingual embeddings plus multiCCA projection; complexity justification is lacking.  
- Question: What is the performance change when freezing embeddings in dependency parsing, and how much improvement comes from initializing with pretrained multilingual embeddings?  
- Question: Does Table 1 report correlations averaged across the 17 embeddings from Section 5.1, and what is the statistical basis for those coefficients?  
- Question: Are there scenarios or language pairs where multiSkip outperforms bilingual+CCA, and if so, which ones?  
- Question: Were evaluations done with curated real-world lexicons or only dictionaries extracted from parallel corpora/MT, and how does dictionary quality affect results?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written with ambitious experiments, but addressing novelty, coverage/annotation quality, and explaining inconsistent results would strengthen it.
