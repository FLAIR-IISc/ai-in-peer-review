- Introduces four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation Invariance) and a new evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA).
- multiQVEC-CCA fixes QVEC’s rotation and normalization issues and correlates well with downstream tasks.
- Extends bilingual embedding techniques to a multilingual setting across 59 languages with implementations.
- Provides a web portal and open-source code to support reproducible research and benchmarking.
- Paper is clear and well-written with methods and experimental setup described comprehensively.
- Broad experimental evaluation across intrinsic and extrinsic tasks and many languages.
- multiQVEC-CCA shows strong correlation with document classification and dependency parsing.
- Public availability of code and portal improves resource accessibility for the community.
- Thorough comparisons to relevant baselines enable nuanced assessment of methods.
- Novelty of the translation invariance extension is unclear relative to Gardner et al. (2015); specific novel aspects are not explained.
- Supersense annotation intersection across languages may be minimal, potentially biasing multiQVEC/multiQVEC-CCA scores; impact not analyzed.
- Vocabulary coverage effects are not sufficiently analyzed; differing coverage (e.g., multiCluster vs. multiCCA) raises fairness concerns.
- Some results are inconsistent/puzzling (e.g., multiCluster’s strong extrinsic performance despite discarding inter-cluster semantics).
- Translation invariance excels at word translation but performs weaker on other metrics, and overall inconsistent preferences for multilingual techniques need explanation.
- What is the dependency parser performance drop when embeddings are fixed, and what gain comes from replacing random embeddings with these pretrained ones?
- Is Table 1 averaged over the 17 embeddings in Section 5.1, and if so, how was the averaging done?
- Are there advantages or trade-offs (performance, computational cost) of multiSkip versus learning bilingual embeddings then applying multiCCA?
- Did the authors evaluate method performance using manually curated multilingual dictionaries to assess the impact of dictionary quality?
