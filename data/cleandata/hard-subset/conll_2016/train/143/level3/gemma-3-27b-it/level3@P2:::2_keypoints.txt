- Introduces multiCluster and multiCCA dictionary-based estimation methods, a new evaluation metric multiQVEC-CCA, and a publicly available web portal for multilingual embedding research.

- Main contribution is multiQVEC-CCA and the web portal; the proposed estimation methods are reasonable but offer incremental and inconsistent gains.

- multiQVEC-CCA is a novel, well-motivated metric that addresses QVEC's basis invariance and normalization issues using CCA.

- The web portal and public release of data/code significantly improve practical accessibility, standardization, and reproducibility.

- Experiments are comprehensive across many languages and tasks, including intrinsic/extrinsic evaluations and correlation analyses.

- Paper is thoroughly documented and reproducible, with clear implementation and experimental details.

- multiCluster and multiCCA produce only modest, inconsistent improvements over baselines and lack explanation for varied success.

- Notable performance discrepancy for multiCluster between 59-language and 12-language setups is reported but not deeply analyzed.

- Key parameters (e.g., dictionary threshold τ, cluster size) lack thorough sensitivity analysis and tuning discussion.

- Correlation analysis (Table 1) omits monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA baselines, weakening comparative claims.

- Wording in Section 3.4 ("combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets") is misleading — datasets were evaluated separately.

- Request: provide detailed analysis of why multiCluster differs between 59- and 12-language settings, focusing on dictionary quality and data sparsity.

- Request: report sensitivity of multiCluster and multiCCA to parameters (cluster size, τ) and any tuning experiments.

- Request: include correlation coefficients for monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA in Table 1 for completeness.

- Additional: Equations (1) and (2) exceed the column margin and should be fixed.

- Additional: Clarify inconsistent citations for the MultiSkip method across the paper.

- Additional: Consider adding related work on multilingual embeddings and clustering (e.g., Al-Rfou et al. 2013; Haghighi et al. 2008; Täckström et al. 2012).

- Overall assessment: Valuable contribution mainly due to the new metric and portal; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
