- Primary contribution: multiQVEC-CCA evaluation metric and an accompanying web portal are valuable resources for the multilingual NLP community.  
- The proposed embedding methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA) are less novel and lack deep analysis.  
- multiQVEC-CCA addresses QVEC’s basis dependence and unnormalized scores using a principled CCA-based improvement.  
- The web portal, data, and benchmarks facilitate reproducible research and easy comparison of multilingual embeddings.  
- Experimental evaluation is broad, covering 59 languages and multiple downstream tasks, offering a wide performance overview.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-documented, aiding understanding and reproducibility.  
- Authors correctly identify limitations of existing evaluation metrics and propose a metric intended to better correlate with downstream tasks.  
- Paper emphasizes breadth over depth, presenting descriptive results without sufficient statistical testing or detailed error analysis.  
- Conflicting MultiCluster results between 59- and 12-language setups are reported but not adequately investigated.  
- Key parameter sensitivity (e.g., dictionary threshold τ, embedding dimensionality, monolingual model choice) is not systematically analyzed.  
- Claimed improvement of multiQVEC-CCA lacks direct monolingual QVEC vs QVEC-CCA correlation comparisons to support it.  
- Some phrasing is misleading (e.g., unclear whether cross-lingual word similarity datasets are combined or used separately).  
- Requests for clarification include detailed analysis of MultiCluster discrepancies, parameter sensitivity results, and citation correspondences for MultiSkip.  
- Authors should report computational resources and training time, especially for the 59-language experiments.  
- Minor issues: fix equation formatting and expand related work to include recent multilingual embedding, clustering, and translation-context papers.
