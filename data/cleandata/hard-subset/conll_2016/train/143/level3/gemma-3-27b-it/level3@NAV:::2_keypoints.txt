- Paper proposes two dictionary-based estimation methods (MultiCluster, MultiCCA) and a refined evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA), plus a web evaluation portal.
- Web portal and improved QVEC-CCA metric are practical strengths that lower barriers and standardize multilingual embedding evaluation.
- Exploration of dictionary-based multilingual embedding estimation is valuable given limited parallel corpora.
- Improvements over existing QVEC methods are modest, though the focus on evaluation is positive.
- Paper lacks depth and analysis despite broad evaluation across many languages and tasks.
- Discussion of results is often superficial and needs more thorough interpretation.
- Conflicting performance of MultiCluster between 59- and 12-language setups is concerning and requires detailed investigation.
- Authors should explain why larger cluster size/increased semantic ambiguity harms performance and propose mitigations.
- Need deeper analysis of estimation parameter effects (e.g., cluster size, learning rate, SGD epochs) and methodological choices (e.g., dictionary creation).
- Include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results to better contextualize multiQVEC-CCA improvements.
- Phrase “a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets” is misleading and should be clarified.
- Citation inconsistencies for MultiSkip need correction (lines 121/147 vs. section 2.3).
- Related work section is incomplete; add key multilingual embedding papers, recent clustering-for-transfer work, and translational-context studies.
- Minor fixes: correct equation formatting on page 3 and clearly mark the web portal URL as supplementary.
