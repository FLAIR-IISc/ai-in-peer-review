This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings, and introduces a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA. The authors also develop a web portal for evaluating arbitrary multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

The main contributions of this work are: (1) two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual embeddings, (2) a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the shortcomings of the previous QVEC method, and (3) a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings.

The strengths of this paper are: (1) the proposal of two new methods for estimating multilingual embeddings, which can be useful for various NLP tasks, (2) the introduction of a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which can provide more accurate evaluation results, and (3) the development of a web portal, which can facilitate future research in this area.

The weaknesses of this paper are: (1) the lack of thorough discussion of the results and design choices, (2) the favoring of breadth over depth, which makes it difficult to fully understand the proposed methods and their implications, and (3) the need for more analysis of the conflicting results between the 59- and 12-language setups.

The paper could be improved by: (1) providing a deeper analysis of the results and design choices, (2) investigating the effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in multiCluster and CCA, and (3) including more related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context.

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide more details on the design choices and parameters used in the multiCluster and CCA methods?
2. How do you plan to address the conflicting results between the 59- and 12-language setups?
3. Can you include more related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the field?
4. How do you plan to extend the web portal to include more evaluation metrics and tasks?
5. Can you provide more details on the implementation of the multiQVEC-CCA method and its advantages over the previous QVEC method?

Additional Comments:

* The paper is well-written and meticulously documented.
* The proposed methods and web portal have high practical value for various NLP tasks.
* The paper provides a good overview of the field and the challenges associated with multilingual word embeddings.
* The formatting issue with the two equations on p.3 should be addressed.
* The citation inconsistency in section 2.3 should be clarified.
* The phrasing "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" (l.304) is misleading and should be rephrased.
