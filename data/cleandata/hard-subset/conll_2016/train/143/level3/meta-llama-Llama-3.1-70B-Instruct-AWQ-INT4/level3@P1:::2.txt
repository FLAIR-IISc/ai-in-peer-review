**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings in a shared vector space. The authors also introduce a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which extends the QVEC metric to multilingual evaluations and addresses its theoretical shortcomings. The paper evaluates the proposed methods on a range of tasks, including text categorization and parsing, and provides a web portal for easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods**: The authors propose two novel methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, which use dictionaries and monolingual data to estimate multilingual embeddings. These methods do not require parallel data and can be used for a large number of languages.
2. **Improved evaluation metric**: The authors introduce multiQVEC-CCA, a supersense similarity measure that replaces QVEC's correlation with CCA and incorporates multilingual evaluation. This metric is shown to correlate better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors provide a web portal for easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings, which facilitates further research in this area.

**Strengths of the Submission**

1. **Practical value**: The paper has high practical value, providing improved methods for estimating multilingual embeddings and a web portal for easy evaluation.
2. **Well-written and documented**: The paper is well-written and meticulously documented, making it easy to follow and understand.
3. **Wide range of evaluations**: The authors evaluate their methods on a wide range of tasks, including text categorization and parsing, providing a comprehensive assessment of their performance.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

1. **Lack of depth**: The paper favors breadth over depth, lacking thorough discussion of results and design choices.
2. **Limited new research insights**: The paper provides limited new research insights, building on existing work in the field.
3. **Methodological decisions**: The authors do not thoroughly discuss the effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a deeper analysis of the conflicting MultiCluster results between the 59- and 12-language setups?
2. How do estimation parameters and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and CCA affect the performance of these methods?
3. Can you include monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results to support the QVEC-CCA improvement claim?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of inconsistent supersense annotations across languages in future work?
5. Can you provide more related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context to situate your research in the broader field?
