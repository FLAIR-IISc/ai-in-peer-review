This paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and Translation Invariance. The authors also introduce a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which extends QVEC to multilingual evaluation. The paper is well-written and clearly described, making it easy to follow.

**Contributions**

1. The authors propose two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual embeddings that only require monolingual data and pairwise parallel dictionaries.
2. The authors adapt QVEC to evaluating multilingual embeddings and develop a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which addresses the theoretical shortcomings of QVEC.
3. The authors develop an easy-to-use web portal for evaluating arbitrary multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

**Strengths**

1. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their proposed methods, including a correlation analysis with downstream tasks.
2. The authors introduce a new evaluation method, multiQVEC-CCA, which is shown to correlate better with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics.
3. The authors develop a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which will facilitate future research in this area.

**Weaknesses**

1. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the novelty of their methods relative to Gardner et al.'s translation invariance.
2. The authors use supersense annotations across multiple languages, which may result in a tiny intersection of features. It is unclear how this is addressed beyond a footnote.
3. The authors do not provide an analysis of the impact of coverage on the scores in Table 2.
4. The results in Table 3 are inconsistent, with intrinsic metrics often not favoring multilingual techniques, translation invariance winning word-translation by far, and multiCluster performing best on extrinsic tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. What is the loss in performance when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser, and what gains occur from simply replacing random embeddings with these pretrained embeddings in the LSTM parser?
2. Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?
3. Are there advantages to multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings and then using multi-CCA to project between spaces?
4. Dictionary extraction via parallel alignments or Google Translate may not reflect real lexicons — did the authors evaluate with real multilingual dictionaries?

**Additional Comments**

The authors should consider providing more details on the preprocessing steps, such as tokenization, stemming, or lemmatization, and how these steps affect the results. Additionally, the authors should consider providing more information on the computational resources required to train the models, as this can be an important factor in the choice of method.
