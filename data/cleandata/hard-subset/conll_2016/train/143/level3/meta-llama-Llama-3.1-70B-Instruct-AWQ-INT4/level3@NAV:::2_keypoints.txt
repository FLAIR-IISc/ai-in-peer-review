- Summary: Proposes dictionary-based MultiCluster and MultiCCA for multilingual embeddings, introduces QVEC-CCA evaluation, evaluates broadly and provides a web portal.

- Contribution 1: MultiCluster and MultiCCA—novel dictionary-based methods that do not require parallel data.

- Contribution 2: QVEC-CCA—new evaluation addressing limitations of QVEC for more robust multilingual embedding assessment.

- Contribution 3: Web portal—online toolkit for easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings.

- Strength 1: Novel methods enable multilingual embeddings without parallel corpora.

- Strength 2: Improved evaluation metric (QVEC-CCA) for more reliable intrinsic assessment.

- Strength 3: Public web portal facilitates replication and further research.

- Weakness 1: Paper favors breadth over depth, lacking detailed discussion of results and design choices.

- Weakness 2: Conflicting results between 59-language and 12-language setups are reported but not investigated.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.

- Question 1: Please provide detailed analysis of the conflicting 59-language vs. 12-language results.

- Question 2: How will you address QVEC-CCA’s reliance on supersense tag annotations and its limitations?

- Question 3: Can you provide a thorough analysis of the effects of estimation parameters and methodological choices in MultiCluster and CCA?
