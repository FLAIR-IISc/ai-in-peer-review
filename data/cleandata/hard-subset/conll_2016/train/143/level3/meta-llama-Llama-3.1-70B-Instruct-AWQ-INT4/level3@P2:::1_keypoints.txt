- Proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation Invariance) and a new multilingual evaluation multiQVEC-CCA; paper is clearly written.
- Contribution: Introduces two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that require only monolingual data and pairwise parallel dictionaries.
- Contribution: Adapts QVEC for multilingual evaluation and proposes multiQVEC-CCA to address QVEC’s theoretical shortcomings.
- Contribution: Provides a web portal for evaluating arbitrary multilingual embeddings with intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation of proposed methods, including correlation analysis with downstream tasks.
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA shows better correlation with downstream tasks than previous intrinsic metrics.
- Strength: Web portal will facilitate future research by making evaluation accessible.
- Weakness: Novelty relative to Gardner et al.’s translation invariance is not clearly explained.
- Weakness: Use of supersense annotations across languages may yield tiny feature intersections; handling is only briefly noted.
- Weakness: No analysis of how coverage impacts scores reported in Table 2.
- Weakness: Inconsistent results in Table 3—intrinsic metrics don’t favor multilingual techniques, translation invariance dominates word-translation, and multiCluster best on extrinsic tasks.
- Question: How much performance is lost when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser, and what gains come from replacing random embeddings with these pretrained ones in the LSTM parser?
- Question: Is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?
- Question: Are there advantages of multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings then using multi-CCA to project between spaces?
- Question: Were real multilingual dictionaries used to evaluate dictionary extraction, or only parallel alignments/Google Translate?
- Additional comment: Provide more details on preprocessing (tokenization, stemming, lemmatization) and their effects.
- Additional comment: Report computational resources required to train the models, as this affects method choice.
