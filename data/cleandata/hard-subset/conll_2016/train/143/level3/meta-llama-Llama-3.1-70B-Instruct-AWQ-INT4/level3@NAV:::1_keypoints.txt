- Proposes four multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, Translation Invariance) and a new multilingual evaluation metric (multiQVECCCA).
- Introduces multiQVECCCA, an extension of QVEC for multilingual evaluation that correlates better with downstream tasks.
- Provides comprehensive intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation (word similarity, word translation, document classification, dependency parsing).
- Claims the proposed methods outperform existing methods in some settings.
- Contribution: two new dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that estimate multilingual embeddings without parallel data.
- Contribution: improved multilingual evaluation metric (multiQVECCCA) addressing limitations of QVEC.
- Contribution: extensive empirical evaluation across multiple metrics and tasks.
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly describes methods and metrics.
- Strength: evaluation is comprehensive across intrinsic and extrinsic measures.
- Strength: multiQVECCCA appears to improve correlation with downstream performance.
- Strength: novel dictionary-based approaches that avoid requiring parallel corpora.
- Weakness: unclear novelty compared to Gardner et al.'s translation invariance method.
- Weakness: using supersense annotations across languages may yield a tiny intersection of features and is not fully addressed.
- Weakness: coverage issues likely affect scores in Table 2 and are not adequately discussed.
- Weakness: inconsistent or puzzling results reported in Table 3 that need explanation.
- Question: what is the performance loss when fixing embeddings in the dependency parser, and what gains from replacing random embeddings with these pretrained ones in the LSTM parser?
- Question: is Table 1 an average over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1?
- Question: are there advantages of multiSkip over learning bilingual embeddings and then using multi-CCA to project between spaces?
- Question: were real multilingual dictionaries (rather than parallel alignment or Google Translate) used for dictionary extraction and evaluation?
