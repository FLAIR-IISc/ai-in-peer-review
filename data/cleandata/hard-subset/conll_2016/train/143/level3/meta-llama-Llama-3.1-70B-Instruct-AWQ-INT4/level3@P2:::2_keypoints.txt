- Proposes two dictionary-based methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, for estimating multilingual embeddings.  
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, a new evaluation method addressing shortcomings of QVEC.  
- Develops a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings with intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.  
- Strength: novel methods that can benefit multiple NLP tasks.  
- Strength: multiQVEC-CCA may provide more accurate intrinsic evaluation than prior QVEC.  
- Strength: web portal facilitates reproducible evaluation and future research.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of results and design choices.  
- Weakness: paper favors breadth over depth, limiting understanding of methods and implications.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of conflicting results between 59- and 12-language setups.  
- Improvement: provide deeper analysis of results and rationale for design choices.  
- Improvement: investigate effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in multiCluster and CCA.  
- Improvement: include more related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context.  
- Question: provide more details on design choices and parameters used in multiCluster and CCA.  
- Question: how will the authors address the conflicting 59- vs 12-language results?  
- Question: can the authors add more related work to contextualize their contributions?  
- Question: plans to extend the web portal with additional evaluation metrics and tasks?  
- Question: provide implementation details of multiQVEC-CCA and clarify its advantages over QVEC.
