This paper proposes two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, multiCluster and multiCCA, and introduces a new evaluation metric, QVEC-CCA. The authors also develop a web portal for evaluating arbitrary multilingual embeddings using a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

The paper's primary contributions are:

1. The proposal of two dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data.
2. The introduction of QVEC-CCA, a new evaluation metric that addresses the limitations of the existing QVEC metric.
3. The development of a web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings, which provides a suite of intrinsic and extrinsic metrics.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The proposed methods, multiCluster and multiCCA, are well-motivated and show promise in evaluating multilingual word embeddings.
2. The introduction of QVEC-CCA is a significant contribution, as it addresses the limitations of the existing QVEC metric and provides a more robust evaluation method.
3. The web portal is a valuable resource for the research community, providing a standardized platform for evaluating multilingual embeddings.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

1. The paper favors breadth over depth, with some sections feeling somewhat superficial.
2. The results are not thoroughly discussed, and some of the design choices are not fully justified.
3. The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the conflicting results between the 59- and 12-language setups.
4. The effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions on the performance of multiCluster and CCA are not fully explored.

Some questions for the authors:

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the conflicting results between the 59- and 12-language setups?
2. How do the estimation parameters and methodological decisions affect the performance of multiCluster and CCA?
3. Can you provide more information on the supersense tag annotations used in the multiQVEC and multiQVECCCA metrics?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of inconsistent supersense annotations across languages?

Additional comments:

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context.
2. The formatting issue on page 3, where two equations exceed the column margin, should be addressed.
3. The citation inconsistency on lines 121 and 147 should be clarified.
4. The minor wording issue on line 304, where the phrase "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets" is misleading, should be corrected.

Overall, this paper makes significant contributions to the field of multilingual word embeddings, but could benefit from a more in-depth analysis and discussion of the results and design choices.
