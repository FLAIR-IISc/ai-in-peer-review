- Proposes two dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) that do not require parallel data.  
- Introduces QVEC-CCA, a new evaluation metric intended to remedy limitations of QVEC.  
- Develops a web portal for standardized intrinsic and extrinsic evaluation of multilingual embeddings.  
- multiCluster and multiCCA are well-motivated and show promise for multilingual embedding estimation.  
- QVEC-CCA is a meaningful improvement, providing a more robust evaluation over QVEC.  
- The web portal is a valuable community resource for standardized assessment.  
- Paper favors breadth over depth, leaving some sections feeling superficial.  
- Results and several design choices are not thoroughly discussed or justified.  
- Conflicting results between the 59- and 12-language setups are not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions on multiCluster and CCA performance are underexplored.  
- Request a detailed analysis of the 59- vs 12-language conflicting results.  
- Request clarification on how estimation parameters and methodological choices affect multiCluster and CCA.  
- Ask for more information on the supersense tag annotations used in multiQVEC and multiQVEC-CCA.  
- Ask how inconsistent supersense annotations across languages will be addressed.  
- Recommend a more detailed related-work discussion on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context.  
- Point out formatting issue: two equations exceed the column margin on page 3.  
- Note citation inconsistency on lines 121 and 147 needs clarification.  
- Suggest correcting misleading wording on line 304 about "a combination of several cross-lingual word similarity datasets."  
- Overall: paper makes significant contributions but requires deeper analysis and discussion of results and design choices.
