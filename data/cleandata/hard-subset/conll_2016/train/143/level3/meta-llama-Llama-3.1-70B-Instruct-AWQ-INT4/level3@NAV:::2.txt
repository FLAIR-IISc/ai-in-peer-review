**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes two dictionary-based methods, MultiCluster and MultiCCA, for estimating multilingual word embeddings. The authors also introduce a new evaluation method, QVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of the existing QVEC method. The paper evaluates the proposed methods on a wide range of tasks and provides a web portal for easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings.

**Main Contributions**

1. **MultiCluster and MultiCCA methods**: The authors propose two novel dictionary-based methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data.
2. **QVEC-CCA evaluation method**: The authors introduce a new evaluation method, QVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of the existing QVEC method and provides a more robust evaluation of multilingual embeddings.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors provide a web portal for easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings, which facilitates further research in this area.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel methods for multilingual embeddings**: The authors propose two novel methods for estimating multilingual word embeddings, which do not require parallel data.
2. **Improved evaluation method**: The authors introduce a new evaluation method, QVEC-CCA, which addresses the limitations of the existing QVEC method and provides a more robust evaluation of multilingual embeddings.
3. **Web portal for evaluation**: The authors provide a web portal for easy evaluation of multilingual embeddings, which facilitates further research in this area.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of depth in results and design choices**: The paper favors breadth over depth and lacks thorough discussion of results and design choices.
2. **Conflicting results**: The paper presents conflicting results between the 59-language and 12-language setups, which need to be further investigated.
3. **Limited analysis of estimation parameters**: The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and CCA.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the conflicting results between the 59-language and 12-language setups?
2. How do you plan to address the limitations of the QVEC-CCA method, such as its reliance on supersense tag annotations?
3. Can you provide a more thorough analysis of the effects of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in MultiCluster and CCA?
