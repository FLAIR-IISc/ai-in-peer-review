- Proposes two dictionary-based methods (multiCluster, multiCCA) for multilingual embeddings using dictionaries and monolingual data, without parallel data.
- Introduces multiQVEC-CCA, replacing QVEC’s correlation with CCA and extending QVEC to multilingual evaluation, claiming better correlation with downstream tasks.
- Evaluates methods on multiple tasks (text categorization, parsing) across many languages and provides a web portal for evaluation.

- Contribution: dictionary-based multilingual embedding methods scalable to many languages.
- Contribution: improved intrinsic evaluation metric (multiQVEC-CCA) for multilingual embeddings.
- Contribution: public web portal to facilitate multilingual embedding evaluation.

- Strength: high practical value—improved methods plus an evaluation portal.
- Strength: well-written and meticulously documented.
- Strength: wide-ranging empirical evaluations across tasks and languages.

- Weakness: favors breadth over depth, lacking thorough discussion of results and design choices.
- Weakness: limited novel research insights, mainly building on existing work.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of estimation parameters and methodological decisions in multiCluster and CCA.

- Question: explain conflicting multiCluster results between the 59- and 12-language setups.
- Question: how do estimation parameters and methodological choices affect multiCluster and CCA performance?
- Question: provide monolingual QVEC and QVEC-CCA correlation results to support the claimed improvement.
- Question: how will inconsistent supersense annotations across languages be addressed in future work?
- Question: include more related work on multilingual embeddings, clustering, and translational context to better situate the research.
