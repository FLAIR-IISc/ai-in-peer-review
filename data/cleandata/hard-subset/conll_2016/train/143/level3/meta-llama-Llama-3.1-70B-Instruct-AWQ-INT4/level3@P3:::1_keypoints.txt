- Paper proposes four multilingual embedding methods: multiCluster, multiCCA, multiSkip, and Translation Invariance.
- Authors introduce a new evaluation metric, multiQVEC-CCA, to address limitations of QVEC.
- A web portal for evaluating multilingual embeddings is developed and released.
- Paper is well-written and methods/metrics are clearly described.
- Novelty of multiCluster and multiCCA is not clearly justified; need detailed comparison to prior work (e.g., Gardner et al.).
- Supersense annotations across languages may yield a small feature intersection; the handling of this issue is insufficiently explained beyond a footnote.
- Coverage differences in evaluation metrics may bias scores (e.g., Table 2 dependency parsing shows different coverage for multiCluster vs. multiCCA).
- Results in Table 3 are inconsistent: multiCluster outperforms on extrinsic tasks despite discarding inter-cluster semantics, requiring explanation.
- multiCCA shows promising results on multilingual embedding tasks.
- multiQVEC-CCA provides a more robust evaluation approach for multilingual embeddings.
- The web portal is a valuable community resource.
- Evaluation metrics are not comprehensive; more metrics should be included for a complete assessment.
- Paper lacks detailed analysis of impact on downstream tasks (e.g., machine translation, cross-lingual IR).
- Authors should clarify the performance impact of fixing pretrained embeddings in the dependency parser versus replacing random embeddings in the LSTM parser.
- Clarify whether Table 1 averages over the 17 embeddings described in Section 5.1.
- Explain if multiSkip has advantages over learning bilingual embeddings first and then applying multi-CCA to project between spaces.
- Report whether evaluation used real multilingual dictionaries or relied on dictionary extraction via parallel alignments/Google Translate.
