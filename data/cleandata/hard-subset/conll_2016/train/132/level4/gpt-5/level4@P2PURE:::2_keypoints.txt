- Paper proposes a neural-style topic model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings and enforce sparsity for topic-like outputs; examples are promising.
- Major flaw: no empirical evaluation—assessment relies solely on example tables with no evidence of representativeness or comparisons to baselines.
- Without quantitative evaluation it is impossible to judge the model’s value; paper is difficult to accept as-is.
- Suggested evaluation 1: use document-level topic representations in supervised document classification against a baseline topic model with same number of topics.
- Suggested evaluation 2: evaluate on a dataset with document similarity annotations using pairwise comparisons over topic vectors.
- Including a GitHub link compromises anonymity (potential grounds for immediate rejection).
- Unclear how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected; showing only a subset risks cherry-picking.
- Section 2.2.1’s suggestion to compute topic–word pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary seems computationally prohibitive—need scalability details.
- Tokenization/chunking is unclear: how is SpaCy used to identify “tokens” and which chunk types are extracted, given use of pre-trained word2vec with few MWEs?
- OOV handling is not addressed: how are unseen terms in deployment handled, and how are pre-trained vs. updated embeddings kept comparable?
- Claim that 20 topics worked best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; recommend simple evaluations (e.g., information-theoretic measures vs. true labels) for direct comparison with LDA.
- Need comparisons to several relevant neural topic models (specific citations provided).
- Low-level issues: typos/grammar (“it’s similarity” → “its similarity”), unclear phrasing about “topic basis,” Figure 5 caption wording, and inconsistent/incomplete reference formatting.
