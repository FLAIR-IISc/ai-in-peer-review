- Paper introduces lda2vec: combines SGNS (word2vec) with document vectors as simplex mixtures of topic vectors regularized by a Dirichlet prior.
- Word, topic, and document vectors share the same space; context vector = pivot word vector + document vector.
- Implementation uses automatic differentiation with GPU support and the code is released open-source.
- Contribution: unified objective that jointly learns word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures.
- Contribution: practical architecture preserving embedding linearities while enabling topic-level interpretability.
- Contribution: open-source implementation to ease experimentation with interpretable embeddings.
- Strength: conceptual clarity and simplicity — adding a document mixture to the context is intuitive and ties to embedding linearities.
- Strength: interpretability — simplex constraint plus Dirichlet sparsity produces LDA-like sparse document-topic proportions.
- Strength: practicality — integrates cleanly with autodiff and negative sampling, making reproduction/extensions easy.
- Strength: qualitative results — compelling examples on 20NG and Hacker News showing coherent topics and corpus-specific analogies.
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative evaluation and lack of baselines (no comparisons to LDA, neural topic models, paragraph vectors, or downstream tasks).
- Weakness: risk of cherry-picking — figures show hand-selected topics without full topic lists or aggregate quality statistics.
- Weakness: computational feasibility concerns for exhaustively computing topic–word similarities on large vocabularies; needs complexity analysis and scalable alternatives.
- Weakness: preprocessing ambiguity — unclear tokenization/lemmatization/phrase detection and mapping to pretrained vectors.
- Weakness: OOV and deployment unclear — need procedure for inferring document mixtures on unseen data and handling OOVs given fine-tuned vectors.
- Weakness: choice of number of topics not justified beyond dataset idiosyncrasy; should compare alignment with gold labels and LDA.
- Weakness: inclusion of a public GitHub link breaks double-blind policy and must be anonymized at submission.
- Questions: how were example topics selected and can the authors provide full topic lists with per-topic coherence scores?
- Questions: can authors report Cv/NPMI and downstream classification vs LDA/NTM baselines (same K)?
- Questions: what are time/memory complexities, throughput numbers, and do you use batching/ANN for scalability?
- Questions: clarify preprocessing for 20NG (unigrams vs phrases) and mapping to pretrained word2vec.
- Questions: describe inference for unseen documents — freeze vectors vs optimize proportions, and OOV embedding strategy.
- Questions: report sensitivity to Dirichlet α/λ, dropout, negative sampling power β, and number of topics.
- Minor comments: fix typos/grammar (e.g., “it’s”→“its”), clarify “topic basis,” consider “terms” in Figure 5 caption, normalize reference formatting, and ensure anonymized code link.
