Summary:
The paper introduces lda2vec, a model that augments the SGNS (word2vec) objective with document vectors constructed as mixtures of latent topic vectors constrained to the simplex and regularized by a Dirichlet prior to induce sparsity. Word, topic, and document vectors live in the same space; the context vector is the sum of a pivot word vector and a document vector. The authors report qualitative topics for 20 Newsgroups and Hacker News, some topic coherence scores (Cv) on 20NG, and demonstrate corpus-specific word analogies. The implementation leverages automatic differentiation and is released as open source.

Contributions (as I see them):
- A simple, unified objective that jointly learns word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures by combining SGNS with a Dirichlet-regularized topic simplex.
- A practical architecture that retains word-embedding linearities while enabling topic-level interpretability, implemented in an autodiff framework with GPU support.
- An open-source implementation that may lower the barrier to experimenting with interpretable embedding models.

Strengths:
- Conceptual clarity and simplicity: Summing a document mixture vector with a pivot word to form the context vector is intuitive and ties interpretability (topics) to embedding linearities.
- Interpretability: The simplex constraint with Dirichlet sparsification promotes sparse document-topic proportions reminiscent of LDA, making topics and document mixtures easy to inspect.
- Practicality: The approach fits cleanly into standard autodiff toolchains and negative sampling, suggesting it is easy to reproduce and extend.
- Qualitative evidence: The examples on 20NG and Hacker News are compelling and demonstrate that the model can recover coherent, human-interpretable themes and corpus-specific analogies.

Weaknesses:
- Lack of quantitative evaluation and baselines: Apart from reporting Cv for different hyperparameters on 20NG, there is no comparison to standard topic models (e.g., LDA, HDP) or to neural topic models, nor to embedding-based document models (e.g., paragraph vectors). Without downstream or held-out metrics, it is hard to assess the model’s value.
  • Suggested evaluations: (a) use document-topic mixtures as features for supervised document classification (e.g., on 20NG) and compare against LDA with the same number of topics; (b) intrinsic topic quality comparisons (Cv/NPMI) against LDA/NTM; (c) document similarity benchmarking with human annotations; (d) perplexity or held-out likelihood where appropriate; (e) analogy/similarity evaluations relative to SGNS/GloVe baselines.
- Risk of cherry-picking: Figures 3–6 present hand-selected topics and examples without reporting coverage (e.g., full topic lists, distribution of Cv across topics). This undermines representativeness. Please include complete topic inventories and aggregate quality statistics.
- Computational feasibility: Computing topic–word similarities by exhaustively scoring every topic against every word can be expensive for large vocabularies. The paper should quantify complexity and discuss scalable alternatives (e.g., matrix multiplication with batched normalization, ANN search, or precomputing normalized vectors).
- Preprocessing ambiguity: Section 3.1 states tokens are identified with SpaCy, but it is unclear whether multiword expressions are included (as in 3.2) or only lemmas. This matters because 20NG uses pretrained word2vec (primarily unigrams). Please clarify tokenization, lemmatization, phrase detection, and how these map to pretrained vectors.
- OOV and deployment: How are OOV terms handled at inference time on unseen documents? Since word vectors are fine-tuned during training, fixed pretrained vectors for OOVs may be misaligned. Describe inference for new documents (e.g., freezing word/topic vectors, optimizing document proportions only) and any OOV strategy.
- Choice of number of topics: Finding that 20 topics works best on 20NG is unsurprising given the dataset’s structure. A simple sanity check would be to measure information-theoretic alignment or supervised accuracy against the gold newsgroup labels and compare with LDA.
- Anonymity: Including a public GitHub link in the submitted version breaks double-blind policy and can warrant rejection. Please ensure anonymity-compliant code release (e.g., anonymous repository) at submission time.

Questions to authors:
- Example selection: How were the topics/examples in Figures 3–6 chosen? Can you provide the full list of topics with their top words and per-topic coherence scores?
- Quantitative baselines: Can you report Cv/NPMI compared to LDA and recent neural topic models, as well as 20NG classification using document-topic mixtures vs LDA (same K)?
- Efficiency: What is the time/memory complexity for computing topic–word similarities and training on large vocabularies? Do you use batching/ANN? Please provide throughput numbers.
- Preprocessing: For 20NG, did you use only lemmatized unigrams, or also noun phrases/entities? How were tokens mapped to pretrained word2vec when phrases were present?
- OOV/inference: How do you infer document mixtures for unseen documents? Do you freeze word/topic vectors and optimize only proportions? How do you embed OOV tokens?
- Sensitivity: How sensitive are results to α and λ in the Dirichlet term, dropout on word/document vectors, negative sampling power β, and the number of topics?
