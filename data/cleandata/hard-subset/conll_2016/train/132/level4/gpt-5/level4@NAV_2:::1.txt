Blending word2vec-style embeddings with LDA is potentially promising. The central issue with the current submission is that the technical exposition is extremely hard to follow. Section 2 needs a complete rewrite so that a reader familiar with word2vec and LDA can quickly grasp the high-level mechanism by which the two are integrated. As written, it does not provide that clarity.

More detailed comments:
The third paragraph of the introduction is opaque. “requires deriving a new approximation” — an approximation to what, exactly? Why does this make prototyping time-consuming? In what sense does it make feature evaluation easier?
Why are the same word vectors used for both pivot and target roles (in contrast to the original word2vec formulation)? What is the rationale for this choice?
What does it mean to “separate” words from a marginal distribution?
What is meant by “co-adaptation” here?
“If we only included structure up to this point” — what “structure” specifically?
“it's similarity” -> “its”
Footnote 1 compromises anonymity.
There appears to be no substantive evaluation. Merely showing a few example clusters is no longer acceptable in NLP. Figure 2 looks like it might report a quantitative metric, but it is explained only in an overly long caption.
The conclusion’s claim that the model solves word analogies overstates the evidence; the paper presents only a few cherry-picked examples of the familiar kind.
The Chang citation lists the venue as “Advances in ...” — please provide the actual venue.
