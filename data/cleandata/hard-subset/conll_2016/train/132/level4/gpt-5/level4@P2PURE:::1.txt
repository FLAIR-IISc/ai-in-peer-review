Combining word2vec with LDA could be interesting, but the paper’s technical exposition is largely unintelligible. Section 2 needs a complete rewrite so that a reader already familiar with word2vec and LDA can readily grasp, at a high level, how the models are integrated. The current presentation does not provide that clarity.

Detailed comments:
- The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. “Requires deriving a new approximation” — an approximation to what, exactly? Why is it time-consuming to develop prototypes? Why is it easier to evaluate features?
- Why are the same word vectors used for both pivot and target roles (unlike word2vec, which separates them)? What is the motivation for this choice?
- What does it mean to “separate words from a marginal distribution”?
- Please define “co-adaptation.”
- “If we only included structure up to this point” — what specific structure is being referred to?
- Typo: “it’s similarity” should be “its similarity.”
- Footnote 1 violates anonymity.
- There appears to be no real evaluation. The days of presenting only a few example clusters are over in NLP. Figure 2 looks like it might report a quantitative evaluation, but it is discussed only in an overly long caption; it needs proper presentation and explanation in the main text.
- The conclusion claims the model solves word analogies, which overstates the results; the paper shows only a few cherry-picked examples of the king + queen type.
- The Chang reference lists the venue as “Advances in ...” — please provide the actual conference or journal name.
