- Paper proposes lda2vec: a hybrid of SGNS word embeddings and LDA-style document-topic mixtures where context = pivot word vector + simplex-constrained document mixture of shared topic vectors, trained with negative sampling and autodiff, and a Dirichlet term for sparsity.
- Claimed contributions: joint embedding of words, documents, and topics with additive context; Dirichlet sparsity for interpretability; straightforward autodiff implementation.
- Conceptually appealing: combining sparse, interpretable topic mixtures with dense word vectors in a shared space is an interesting direction.
- Interpretability benefit: topics live in the same vector space as words, enabling direct nearest-neighbor inspection for topic words.
- Qualitative results: example topics on 20NG and Hacker News look coherent and domain-appropriate.
- Major weakness — clarity and rigor: Section 2 is unclear/underspecified (missing precise definitions, objectives, dimensions, constraints, and derivations).
- Specific technical ambiguities: unclear “new approximation,” rationale for tying input/output embeddings, meaning of “separate words from the marginal distribution,” and undefined “co-adaptation” and “structure up to this point.”
- Major weakness — evaluation: lacks rigorous quantitative baselines (no LDA or neural topic comparisons), downstream tasks, ablations (Dirichlet term, tied embeddings, additive context), or standard analogy benchmarks.
- Reproducibility/anonymity issues: code link in footnote breaks anonymity and many training/preprocessing details are scattered or missing for reproduction.
- Presentation issues: important methodological details buried in figure captions, incomplete/malformed references, and stylistic/typo errors.
- Requests for authors — model spec: provide full objective derivation, minibatch scaling of Ld, parameterization/constraints of pjk, topic/document normalization, and justification/experiments for tied embeddings and dropout choices.
- Requests for authors — hyperparams & ablations: give how α and λ were chosen, sensitivity plots, quantitative effect of removing Dirichlet term, and any regularization on topic vectors.
- Requests for authors — evaluation: compare topic coherence to LDA on same data, report per-topic Cv distributions, add downstream tasks (e.g., 20NG classification) and systematic analogy evaluation with coverage statistics.
- Anonymity request: remove code links and identifying info for camera-ready submission.
- Minor fixes: correct typos (e.g., “it’s”→“its”), define symbols at first use, move methodological details into main text, and fix incomplete citations.
