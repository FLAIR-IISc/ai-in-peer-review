- Proposal: Introduces lda2vec, combining SGNS with a Dirichlet prior to embed words, topics, and documents in a shared space where document vectors are convex combinations of topic vectors for sparsity and interpretability.
- Contributions: Practical neural topic model integrating SGNS and simplex-constrained document mixtures, joint autodiff implementation, and open-source code release (which breaches anonymity).
- Strength: Conceptual simplicity and interpretability from summing document and word vectors and enforcing simplex/non-negativity constraints to yield readable document-topic mixtures.
- Strength: Practical and extensible objective that fits standard SGNS tooling and autodiff frameworks, enabling scalable joint training of words, topics, and documents.
- Strength: Qualitative results show coherent topics on 20NG and Hacker News plus meaningful embedding analogies; clear exposition of model and loss.
- Strength: Use of topic coherence (Cv) to report human-correlated topic quality.
- Weakness: Insufficient empirical evaluation — lacks quantitative baselines (LDA, neural topic models), downstream tasks (classification/retrieval), per-topic coherence distributions, and topic diversity metrics.
- Weakness: Potential cherry-picking of examples and analogies with no selection methodology or coverage statistics, undermining evidence.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in preprocessing and tokenization (SpaCy usage vs. multiword expressions) and mismatch with pretrained word2vec not addressed.
- Weakness: No explanation of handling OOV terms or inference for new documents (whether embeddings are fixed or fine-tuned at test time).
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to related neural/embedding-enhanced topic models, leaving novelty and advantages unclear.
- Weakness: Anonymity breach due to public GitHub URL, which conflicts with double-blind review policy.
- Weakness: Minor clarity and feasibility issues — computational cost of topic–word similarity (O(KV)), unexplored hyperparameter sensitivity (α, λ, K, dropout), and unsupported hyperparameter choices.
- Questions for authors: Request classification/retrieval experiments, per-topic coherence and diversity, ablations on λ/α/K/dropout, and comparisons to LDA and prior neural models.
- Questions for authors: Clarify tokenization details, reconciliation with pretrained embeddings, OOV handling, test-time encoding procedure, and practical efficiency for computing topic–word similarities.
- Minor editorial comments: Several wording and reference-formatting fixes suggested (typos, unclear sentences, caption wording, and inconsistent bibliography entries).
- Overall assessment: Promising, practically motivated model with compelling qualitative results but insufficient quantitative evidence and anonymization issues; cannot recommend acceptance without stronger experiments and proper anonymization.
