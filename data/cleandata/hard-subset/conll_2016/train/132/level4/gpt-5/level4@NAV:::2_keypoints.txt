- Introduces a neural-style topic model extending the word2vec objective to jointly learn document embeddings and enforce sparsity to emulate topic outputs.
- Model is compelling and provided examples are promising.
- Major shortcoming: no quantitative empirical evaluation—assessment relies solely on example tables with unknown representativeness and no comparisons to standard or neural topic models.
- Suggested evaluations: (1) use document topic representations in supervised classification to compare representations; (2) evaluate on datasets with document similarity annotations via pairwise comparisons.
- Including a GitHub link breaks anonymity and could justify rejection.
- Concern: Figures 3–6 may be cherry-picked—unclear how examples/topics were selected.
- Scalability concern: computing word-topic representations via pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary (Section 2.2.1) may be computationally infeasible for realistic vocabularies and topic counts.
- Unclear tokenization: need details on how SpaCy was used to identify “tokens” and whether noun chunks or other chunk types were extracted.
- Unclear handling of OOV terms and comparability of updated vs. non-updated embeddings when applying the model to new documents.
- Result that 20 topics works best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; suggest information-theoretic comparison against true labels and comparison to LDA.
- Recommend comparisons to additional neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Low-level issues: minor grammar (“it’s similarity” → “its similarity”), unclear phrasing about the “topic basis,” Figure 5 caption should use “terms” not “words,” and reference formatting is inconsistent (Roder et al. missing conference name).
