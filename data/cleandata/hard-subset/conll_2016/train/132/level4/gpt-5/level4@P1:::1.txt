Summary
The paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model that integrates Skip-gram with Negative Sampling (SGNS) for word embeddings with Dirichlet-distributed, simplex-constrained document-topic mixtures. The context vector is the sum of a document vector and a pivot word vector, tying local word co-occurrences to global document themes. Document vectors are constrained to be convex combinations of learned topic vectors; a Dirichlet likelihood with a small concentration parameter promotes sparse, interpretable document–topic proportions. The model is implemented in an automatic differentiation framework and evaluated qualitatively on Twenty Newsgroups and a large Hacker News comments corpus; topic coherence scores (Cv) are reported for Twenty Newsgroups, and several word analogies are illustrated.

Contributions (as I see them)
1) A modeling formulation that jointly learns word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document–topic mixtures by combining SGNS with a Dirichlet-induced simplex constraint on document weights over learned topic vectors.
2) A straightforward implementation in an autodiff framework that enables joint training of word, topic, and document vectors, with qualitative demonstrations on two corpora and limited topic coherence evaluation.

Strengths
- Potentially useful hybrid idea: Combining SGNS with sparse topic mixtures is conceptually appealing and could bridge interpretability (topic models) with the empirical strength of word embeddings. The choice to represent document vectors as mixtures over topic vectors (which share the embedding space with words) is a sensible way to support both interpretability and compositionality.
- Sparse, interpretable document representations: The Dirichlet-simplex constraint encourages non-negative, normalized mixtures and yields topic vectors that can be interpreted via nearest neighbors. The qualitative topics from Twenty Newsgroups align with known categories, indicating the intended behavior.
- Practical training in autodiff frameworks: Leveraging automatic differentiation and GPUs makes prototyping simpler and faster. If clearly presented, this could lower the barrier for practitioners wishing to extend or adapt topic–embedding hybrids.
- Some attempt at quantitative topic quality: Reporting Cv coherence on Twenty Newsgroups is a step beyond purely anecdotal topics, and the results suggest the model can recover coherent themes.

Weaknesses
- Technical exposition is unclear and incomplete (most critical). Section 2 needs a thorough rewrite. Several statements are ambiguous or undefined, impeding understanding and reproducibility. Examples include: “requires deriving a new approximation” (approximation to what, and how is this relevant here?); motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target (unlike standard word2vec tying); “separate words from a marginal distribution” (what separation is meant?); “co-adaptation” is invoked without definition or citation context; “If we only included structure up to this point” (which structure?). These issues make the main idea hard to follow despite familiarity with LDA and SGNS.
- Insufficient evaluation and lack of baselines. Beyond showing example topics and a table of average coherence, there is no rigorous comparative evaluation. There are no comparisons to LDA, doc2vec, neural topic models, or hybrid baselines; no downstream tasks (e.g., document classification or retrieval), no perplexity or alternative topic quality/diversity metrics, and no ablations (e.g., effect of the Dirichlet term, α, λ, tying embeddings, or using the sum c = w + d versus alternatives). The analogy examples are cherry-picked; there is no standardized analogy/evaluation set.
- Claims overstate evidence. The conclusion claims the model “solves word analogies,” but the paper only shows a few handpicked analogies. Without a standardized benchmark or systematic evaluation, this claim is not supported.
- Presentation and figure/table issues. Figure 2 appears to be the key quantitative result but is described primarily in a long caption rather than in the main text, with limited discussion of experimental setup, significance, or variance. The discussion of coherence does not clarify the choice and impact of the external corpus versus the model’s vocabulary (especially for the Hacker News tokenization). Figure 1’s variables are not consistently defined in the text.
- Anonymity and citation problems. The code link (Footnote 1) violates double-blind reviewing. There are typographical issues (“it’s similarity” should be “its similarity”), and several references are incomplete or malformed (e.g., the Chang et al. venue listed as “Advances in …”). These detract from the paper’s professionalism.

Questions to Authors
- Clarify the phrase “requires deriving a new approximation”: Are you contrasting variational/posterior approximations in probabilistic topic models with your direct optimization of an objective in autodiff? What exactly is the “approximation” your approach avoids?
- Why tie the pivot and target embeddings? What empirical or theoretical motivation supports this design relative to the two-matrix setup in SGNS? Please include an ablation.
- What exactly do you mean by “separating context-word pairs from the marginal distribution”? Please formalize the intended property in terms of the SGNS objective and negative sampling distribution u^β.
- Define “co-adaptation” in this context and justify the specific dropout scheme on both document and pivot vectors. Provide dropout rates and an ablation.
- Precisely specify the document mixture: Are topic vectors learned parameters in the same space as word embeddings, with document mixtures obtained via softmax over free logits? How are p_jk parameterized and initialized? Please include the exact parameterization and constraints.
- Report comprehensive evaluations: (a) Compare against LDA, doc2vec, GloVe+LDA hybrids, and at least one neural topic model. (b) Provide downstream performance (e.g., classification on Twenty Newsgroups using learned document representations). (c) Include topic diversity metrics and human judgments if possible. (d) Standardized word analogy and similarity benchmarks, or at least a robust in-domain analogy protocol.
- Provide ablations for α and λ in the Dirichlet term, as well as the effect of removing L_d (α = 1), varying β, number of negatives, and using c = w + d versus other combinations (e.g., concatenation, learned gating).
