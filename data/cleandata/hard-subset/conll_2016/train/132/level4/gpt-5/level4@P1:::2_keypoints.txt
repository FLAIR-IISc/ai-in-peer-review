- Summary: Introduces lda2vec, combining SGNS word2vec with simplex-constrained sparse document-topic mixtures using a Dirichlet prior by summing document and pivot word vectors.
- Contribution 1: Neural formulation that yields interpretable, sparse document-topic mixtures while jointly learning word and topic vectors without bespoke inference.
- Contribution 2: Practical autodiff-based implementation with qualitative demos on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.
- Contribution 3: Code release to facilitate reuse.

- Strength: Conceptual elegance and practicality — integrates SGNS and Dirichlet-sparse topic mixtures in an intuitive, easy-to-integrate design.
- Strength: Interpretability by design via non-negativity, simplex constraints, and sparsity-inducing Dirichlet prior.
- Strength: Compelling qualitative results (coherent topics and word analogies) on Hacker News and Newsgroups.
- Strength: Open-source code improves reproducibility and adoption.

- Weakness: No quantitative evaluation or baselines — lacks comparisons to LDA or neural topic models and downstream tasks.
- Weakness: Breach of double-blind review due to included GitHub link.
- Weakness: Risk of cherry-picking — selected topics/examples lack justification or representativeness.
- Weakness: Methodological clarity gaps around tokenization, noun-chunk usage, and compatibility with pre-trained word2vec multiword entries.
- Weakness: Unclear handling of OOV words and comparability of updated vs. frozen embeddings at inference time.
- Weakness: Computational feasibility concerns for deriving top words per topic via pairwise similarity over large vocabularies.

- Question: Provide quantitative comparisons to LDA and neural topic models (e.g., topic coherence, classification, document similarity) — at minimum LDA with same topic count on 20 Newsgroups.
- Question: How were topics/examples in Figures 3–6 selected; report systematic metrics (e.g., average coherence) and full/randomized topic lists.
- Question: Specify exact tokenization in Section 3.1 — noun chunks or standard tokenization/lemmatization, and how this aligns with pre-trained vectors.
- Question: How are OOV tokens handled at inference and how is embedding comparability ensured if embeddings are updated during training?
- Question: What is the computational complexity of deriving top words per topic, and are approximate NN or other optimizations used?
- Question: Have you evaluated information-theoretic measures or supervised classification across topic counts to verify n=20 isn’t merely matching label cardinality?

- Additional: Remove/anonymize GitHub link for submission to preserve double-blind review.
- Additional: Compare/discuss related work (Cao et al. AAAI 2015; Nguyen et al. TACL 2015; Shamanta et al. ECML PKDD 2015).
- Minor issues: Typos and wording fixes (Line 315 “it’s”→“its”; clarify “topic basis are”; Figure 5 caption “words”→“terms”; standardize reference formatting).

- Overall: Model is appealing and qualitatively interesting but needs quantitative evaluation, methodological clarifications, and anonymity fix to be a stronger submission.
