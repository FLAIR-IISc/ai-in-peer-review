- Proposes lda2vec: a hybrid joining SGNS word embeddings with Dirichlet-constrained, sparse document–topic mixtures via summed word+document context vectors.
- Main claimed contributions: simple SGNS+topic formulation, autodiff-friendly joint learning of word/topic/document vectors, and empirical qualitative/coherence results on two corpora.
- Strength: conceptually simple and practically useful—injects LDA-like interpretability into embedding models via an intuitive context-as-sum formulation.
- Strength: unifying representation space allows direct inspection of topic–word similarities and interpretable document memberships.
- Strength: reports topic coherence (Cv) on Twenty Newsgroups and follows negative-sampling best practice (β = 0.75).
- Strength: provides implementation/training details (window, negatives, dropout) and GPU-compatible autodiff objective.
- Major weakness: exposition is unclear and imprecise—section 2, notation, and several claims are poorly defined or overloaded.
- Major weakness: insufficient evaluation—no comparisons to LDA, doc2vec, or related hybrids, no downstream tasks, and only cherry-picked analogy examples.
- Major weakness: inadequate related-work positioning and lack of engagement with prior topic+embedding research.
- Major weakness: anonymity violations (links reveal authors) and reproducibility issues—missing ablations, underspecified hyperparameters and training details.
- Presentation problems: malformed references, figure captions containing key methods, and hard-to-parse figures (Figure 1); grammar/typos throughout.
- Methodological clarity needed: explain tying input/output embeddings and its effect versus separate matrices used in SGNS.
- Methodological clarity needed: justify dropout on document and word vectors and explain “co-adaptation” claims.
- Optimization question: clarify how the Dirichlet prior is optimized through the softmax, gradient behavior for sparse proportions, and sensitivity to α and λ.
- Evaluation requests: report Cv for LDA with matched preprocessing and run downstream document-level tasks comparing lda2vec to LDA/doc2vec.
- Evaluation requests: provide quantitative topic coherence or extrinsic evaluation on Hacker News instead of only qualitative examples.
- Clarify negative sampling: explain the statement about “separating observed words from the marginal” and which negative distribution (u^β) was used per experiment.
- Minor requests: remove identifying links for anonymous submission, fix notation/grammar, move key method details from figure captions into text, and include an algorithmic summary.
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially useful idea but requires substantial revision for clarity, stronger baselines/evaluation, reproducibility, and presentation improvements.
