- Proposed method: extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and applies sparsification to approximate topic distributions; intuitive with promising qualitative examples.
- Strength: Clear, appealing modeling idea that bridges distributed representations and sparse topic-like structure.
- Strength: Well-written exposition with illustrative examples.
- Strength/issue: Code release aids reproducibility but inclusion of a live GitHub link violates double-blind anonymity.
- Major concern: Lacks quantitative evaluation—relies almost entirely on qualitative results, preventing assessment of effectiveness, robustness, and generalizability.
- Major concern: Anonymity violation via live GitHub link must be removed or anonymized for conference policy compliance.
- Suggestion: Add supervised document classification/clustering evaluations (e.g., 20 Newsgroups) comparing sparse representations to LDA and neural topic models with multiple seeds.
- Suggestion: Evaluate document similarity/retrieval performance (e.g., MAP, AUC) on datasets with relatedness annotations.
- Suggestion: Report topic coherence metrics (e.g., NPMI, UCI) to quantify interpretability relative to baselines.
- Suggestion: Systematically vary number of topics (K) and analyze stability and performance rather than reporting a single K.
- Suggestion: Include ablation studies (with/without sparsification, fine-tuned vs fixed embeddings, dimensionality effects).
- Baselines: Must compare to classical models (LDA) and neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. AAAI 2015; Nguyen et al. TACL 2015; Shamanta et al. ECML/PKDD 2015).
- Clarification needed: Describe selection criteria for shown qualitative topics and provide a random sample or full appendix to avoid cherry-picking.
- Clarification needed: Discuss computational complexity of computing topic–word representations and any approximations used (top-k pruning, ANN, negative sampling).
- Clarification needed: Specify tokenization and multiword handling (SpaCy settings, noun chunks, vocabulary construction) and interaction with pre-trained embeddings.
- Clarification needed: Explain OOV handling and generalization to unseen documents if embeddings are fine-tuned (freeze, subword, retrofitting, UNK strategies).
- Clarification needed: Justify choosing 20 topics for 20 Newsgroups and report alignment metrics with ground-truth (e.g., mutual information, purity) versus baselines.
- Overall assessment: Interesting idea with encouraging qualitative results, but current lack of quantitative validation and anonymity breach preclude acceptance; fix these to make it a strong contribution.
- Minor comments: Fix grammatical errors (e.g., “it’s similarity” → “its similarity”), clarify “topic basis,” adjust Figure 5 caption for multiword units, and standardize/complete citation formatting.
