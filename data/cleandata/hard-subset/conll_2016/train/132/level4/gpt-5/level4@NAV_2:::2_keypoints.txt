- Paper proposes a neural topic model by extending the word2vec objective to jointly learn document embeddings and sparsify them to mimic topic outputs.  
- Proposed model and qualitative examples are appealing, but the paper lacks any quantitative empirical evaluation or comparisons to baselines.  
- Without quantitative results it’s impossible to judge merit; add evaluations such as supervised document classification using inferred document-topic features.  
- Alternatively evaluate directly on datasets with document similarity annotations (e.g., pairwise similarity from topic mixtures).  
- Releasing a GitHub link in the submission breaks anonymity and risks desk rejection.  
- Clarify how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected to avoid concerns about cherry-picking.  
- Discuss the scalability and possible approximations for computing topic–word nearest neighbors over large vocabularies and many topics.  
- Specify exactly how tokens are identified with SpaCy (noun chunks or another strategy) and how tokenization aligns with pretrained word2vec multiword coverage.  
- Explain handling of OOV terms at inference, given that token embeddings are fine-tuned during training and may not align for unseen tokens.  
- The claim that 20 topics work best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; provide information-theoretic or clustering-based evaluations (mutual information, clustering metrics) for validation and direct LDA comparison.  
- Compare against related neural topic models (references provided) to situate contributions against prior work.  
- Low-level fixes: grammar (“it’s similarity” → “its similarity”), clarify phrasing around “topic basis,” change Figure 5 caption “words” → “terms,” and make references formatting consistent (fill missing venue names).
