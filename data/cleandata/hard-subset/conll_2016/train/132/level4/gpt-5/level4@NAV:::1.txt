Review- Combining word2vec and LDA could be promising. The main
issue with the current paper is that the technical exposition is opaque.
Section 2 needs a complete rewrite so that a reader familiar
with word2vec and LDA can readily grasp a high-level picture of how
the models are being integrated. The current presentation does not achieve this.
More detailed comments:
The third paragraph of the introduction is unintelligible to me. "requires
deriving a new approximation" — an approximation to what? why is it time-consuming
to build prototypes? Why is it easier to evaluate features?
Why use the same word vectors for pivot and target (unlike in word2vec)? What's
the rationale for that choice?
what does it mean to "separate words from a marginal distribution"?
what is co-adaptation?
"If we only included structure up to this point" — what structure?
"it's similarity" -> its
Footnote 1 compromises anonymity.
There appears to be no evaluation. The days when it was acceptable to present
only a few example clusters are long gone in NLP. Figure 2 looks like it might be
a quantitative evaluation, but it's only described in an overly long caption.
The statement in the conclusion that the model solves word analogies is
overstated given what was shown, which was just a few cherry-picked examples of the king
+ queen variety.
The Chang reference lists the venue as "Advances in ..." Am I supposed
to guess the venue?
