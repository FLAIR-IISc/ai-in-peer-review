This paper explores a potentially interesting combination of word2vec and LDA. However, the primary issue is that the technical exposition is largely incomprehensible. Section 2, in particular, needs a complete rewrite so that a reader familiar with word2vec and LDA can readily understand the high-level design, the modeling assumptions, and how the components are integrated. The current presentation does not achieve this.

More detailed comments:
- The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. What does “requires deriving a new approximation” refer to—an approximation to which objective, likelihood, or posterior? Why is it “time consuming to develop prototypes”? Why is it comparatively “easier to evaluate features”? Please make the claims concrete and justify them.
- Why are the same word vectors used for both pivot and target positions, unlike word2vec which maintains separate input/output embeddings? What is the motivation for this design choice, and what are its consequences?
- What does it mean to “separate words from a marginal distribution”? Please define the term precisely and explain how this operation is performed in the model.
- What is meant by “co-adaptation” in this context? Provide a clear definition and, if relevant, a formal description of the phenomenon and how the model addresses it.
- “If we only included structure up to this point” — what specific structure is being referred to here? Spell out exactly which dependencies or components you mean.
- Typo: “it’s similarity” should be “its similarity.”
- Footnote 1 breaks anonymity.
- There appears to be no substantive evaluation. Presenting example clusters is no longer acceptable evidence in NLP. Figure 2 looks like it might report a quantitative evaluation, but it is only described in an overly long caption and not integrated into the main text with a clear experimental setup, metrics, and baselines.
- The conclusion’s claim that the model “solves word analogies” is overstated. The paper shows only a few cherry-picked examples of the king–queen type, which is insufficient to support such a claim.
- The Chang citation lists the venue as “Advances in …” Please provide the complete and correct conference/journal name.

In its current form, the paper’s core idea is obscured by unclear technical writing and the lack of a proper evaluation. A thorough rewrite of Section 2 for clarity and a meaningful, quantitative evaluation are necessary before the contribution can be adequately assessed.
