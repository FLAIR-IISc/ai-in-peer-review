- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, combining SGNS word embeddings with Dirichlet-constrained simplex document–topic mixtures where document vectors are convex combinations of learned topic vectors and context = pivot + document.

- Contribution 1: Joint modeling formulation that learns word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document–topic mixtures by combining SGNS with a Dirichlet simplex constraint.

- Contribution 2: Autodiff-based implementation enabling joint training of words, topics, and documents with qualitative demos on two corpora and limited Cv coherence reporting.

- Strength: Conceptually appealing hybrid bridging interpretability of topic models with strengths of word embeddings by sharing embedding space between words and topics.

- Strength: Dirichlet-simplex constraint yields sparse, interpretable document representations and topic vectors interpretable via nearest neighbors.

- Strength: Practical implementation in automatic-differentiation/GPU frameworks lowers prototyping barriers.

- Strength: Some quantitative evaluation (Cv coherence on Twenty Newsgroups) goes beyond pure anecdotes.

- Weakness: Technical exposition is unclear and incomplete; many statements are ambiguous or undefined, impeding understanding and reproducibility.

- Weakness: Insufficient empirical evaluation and missing baselines (no comparisons to LDA, doc2vec, neural topic models, hybrids) and no downstream tasks or ablations.

- Weakness: Claims overstate evidence (e.g., asserting the model “solves word analogies” based on cherry-picked examples).

- Weakness: Presentation issues — key quantitative results relegated to captions, unclear experimental setup, undefined variables in figures, and unclear impact of external coherence corpora.

- Weakness: Anonymity and citation problems — code link violates double-blind review, typos, and incomplete/malformed references.

- Question: Clarify what is meant by “requires deriving a new approximation” and which approximation is being avoided or contrasted.

- Question: Why tie pivot and target embeddings rather than use separate matrices as in SGNS; request ablation or justification.

- Question: Clarify “separating context-word pairs from the marginal distribution” and formalize with respect to the SGNS objective and negative sampling distribution u^β.

- Question: Define “co-adaptation” here, justify the dropout scheme on document and pivot vectors, and provide dropout rates and ablation.

- Question: Precisely specify document mixture parameterization — are topic vectors learned in the same space as words, how are p_jk parameterized and initialized, and what constraints are used.

- Question: Request comprehensive evaluations: comparisons to LDA/doc2vec/GloVe+LDA/neural topic models, downstream tasks (e.g., classification), topic diversity/human judgments, and standardized analogy/similarity benchmarks.

- Question: Request ablations for α and λ in the Dirichlet term, effect of removing L_d (α=1), varying β/negative samples, and different compositions for c (sum vs concatenation or gating).

- Additional comment: Remove identifying links for anonymized review, fix typos, and complete all references with correct venues and years.
