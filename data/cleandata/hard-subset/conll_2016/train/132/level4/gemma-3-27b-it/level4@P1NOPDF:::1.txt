A combination of word2vec and LDA is a potentially interesting direction. However, the main weakness of this paper is the lack of clarity in its technical details. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to allow readers familiar with word2vec and LDA to easily grasp a high-level understanding of how these models are being integrated. The current presentation fails to achieve this. 

More detailed comments:

The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. The phrase “requires deriving a new approximation” is vague – approximation of *what*? Furthermore, the text states that developing prototypes is time-consuming, but doesn’t explain why. Conversely, it claims it is easier to evaluate features, without justification. 

The decision to use the same word vectors for both pivot and target words, diverging from the standard word2vec approach, is not motivated. Why was this choice made? 

The meaning of “separating words from a marginal distribution” is unclear. Similarly, the term “co-adaptation” requires further explanation.

The phrase “If we only included structure up to this point” lacks context; what specific structure is being referred to? 

There’s a minor grammatical error: “it’s similarity” should be “its similarity.”

Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission.

Crucially, the paper lacks a robust evaluation. The practice of relying solely on example clusters is no longer acceptable within the NLP community. Figure 2 *appears* to present a quantitative evaluation, but this is only conveyed through an excessively long caption. The figure itself isn’t discussed in the main text.

The conclusion’s claim that the model solves word analogies is an overstatement. The paper only presents a few selectively chosen examples (e.g., “king + queen”), which does not constitute a comprehensive demonstration of analogical reasoning.

Finally, the citation for Chang includes only “Advances in…” – the complete conference or journal name is missing, making it difficult to locate the source.
