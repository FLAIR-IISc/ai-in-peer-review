- Proposes a neural topic model extending word2vec with sparsification; examples are promising but lack quantitative backing.
- Complete absence of empirical evaluation or comparisons to established (traditional and neural) topic models prevents robust assessment and hinders acceptance.
- Suggested evaluations: use learned document embeddings in supervised document classification vs a topic model with equal topic cardinality, or evaluate on a dataset with document similarity annotations via pairwise topic-vector comparisons.
- Code release is commendable, but including a GitHub link in the submission breaks anonymity and may warrant rejection.
- Selection criteria for the examples in Figures 3–6 are unclear, raising the possibility of cherry-picking.
- Sec 2.2.1’s approach of computing topic word representations via pairwise comparisons is computationally expensive for realistic vocabularies/topics and needs feasibility justification.
- Tokenization is underspecified: Sec 3.1 claims SpaCy tokens but doesn’t clarify whether noun chunks only (as in Sec 3.2) or another method is used, conflicting with pretrained word2vec’s limited multiword terms.
- No handling strategy for out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is discussed; applying the model to novel documents may require updating embeddings and invalidate pretrained comparisons.
- Reporting 20 topics as optimal on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; recommend an information-theoretic comparison to true labels to enable direct comparison with LDA.
- Missing baseline comparisons to prior neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Minor issues: typo ("it's" → "its"), awkward phrasing about impact on the “topic basis,” Figure 5 caption should use “terms” not “words,” and reference formatting is inconsistent/incomplete (e.g., Roder et al.).
