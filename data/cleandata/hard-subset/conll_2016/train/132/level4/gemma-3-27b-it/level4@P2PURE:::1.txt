The combination of word2vec and LDA has the potential to be an interesting approach. However, a major weakness of this paper is the lack of clarity in its technical presentation. Section 2 requires a substantial revision to provide readers familiar with word2vec and LDA with a clear, high-level understanding of how the models are integrated; the current explanation fails to achieve this. 

More specific comments:

The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. The phrase “requires deriving a new approximation” is vague – approximation of what specifically? The reasons given for the time required to develop prototypes, and the ease of feature evaluation, are not justified. The decision to utilize identical word vectors for pivot and target words, diverging from the standard word2vec approach, lacks clear motivation. The meaning of “separating words from a marginal distribution” is also undefined. Furthermore, the term “co-adaptation” is not explained. The phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" is ambiguous; what specific structure is being referenced? There is a minor grammatical error: "it's similarity" should be "its similarity." Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission.

Critically, the paper lacks a robust evaluation. Simply presenting example clusters, as was sometimes acceptable in the past, is no longer sufficient in the field of NLP. While Figure 2 appears to present quantitative results, these are only described within the excessively lengthy caption. The claim in the conclusion that the model solves word analogies is an overstatement, as the paper only demonstrates performance on a limited set of curated examples (e.g., “king + queen” analogies). Finally, the citation for Chang uses an incomplete venue name (“Advances in…”); the complete venue should be specified.
