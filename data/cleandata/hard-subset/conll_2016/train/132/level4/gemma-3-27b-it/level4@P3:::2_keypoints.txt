- Proposes lda2vec: jointly learn word and document embeddings with a Dirichlet prior to yield interpretable, topic-like document representations.  
- Primary contribution: a novel, differentiable combination of word2vec skip-gram and topic modeling enabling end-to-end training; secondary contributions: Chainer implementation and public code release.  
- Strength: novel model formulation that bridges dense embeddings and sparse, interpretable topic mixtures via a Dirichlet prior.  
- Strength: practical implementation using Chainer with GPU support, facilitating rapid prototyping and reproducibility.  
- Strength: demonstrated semantic learning and analogical reasoning on the Hacker News dataset, showing domain-specific token relationships.  
- Weakness: no quantitative empirical evaluation or comparisons to baselines (LDA, NMF, other neural topic models), making effectiveness unsubstantiated.  
- Weakness: potential cherry-picking of presented topics (manual selection for Figures 3 and 4) without systematic evaluation.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns about computing word similarities over the full vocabulary for topic interpretation.  
- Weakness: no discussion of handling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms for new documents.  
- Question: what quantitative metrics, datasets, and baselines will be used for rigorous evaluation, and why were they omitted here?  
- Question: what criteria were used to select topics shown in the paper, and can topic quality be supported with coherence scores?  
- Question: please provide a detailed scalability analysis of word-similarity computations and any optimization strategies.  
- Question: how does the model handle OOV terms and what strategies (subword/character models) are considered?
