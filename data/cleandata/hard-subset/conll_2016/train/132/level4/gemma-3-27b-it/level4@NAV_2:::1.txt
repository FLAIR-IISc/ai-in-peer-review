This paper proposes lda2vec, a model combining the strengths of word embeddings (word2vec) and topic models (LDA) to learn dense word vectors alongside sparse, interpretable document representations. The primary contribution is a method for jointly training word and document vectors within a unified framework leveraging automatic differentiation, enabling efficient model development and experimentation. A secondary contribution lies in the successful application of this framework to both the Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News datasets, demonstrating the model’s ability to learn coherent topics and meaningful semantic relationships. Finally, the work demonstrates the ability to learn linear regularities between words, a property inherited from word2vec.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel Combination of Approaches:** The core idea of integrating word embeddings with topic modeling to achieve both representational power and interpretability is promising.  The approach addresses a key limitation of purely dense representations by aiming for sparse, human-understandable document mixtures.
2. **Practical Implementation & Framework:**  The use of automatic differentiation frameworks (Chainer) is a significant advantage, allowing for rapid prototyping and efficient computation, particularly with GPU acceleration. This lowers the barrier to entry for researchers exploring similar hybrid models.
3. **Demonstrated Performance on Multiple Datasets:** The application of lda2vec to both Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News provides evidence of the model’s generalizability. The qualitative results presented, particularly the coherent topics discovered in both corpora, are encouraging. The Hacker News experiments showing learned semantic relationships are also compelling.
4. **Retention of Word Vector Properties:** The model effectively leverages the desirable properties of word embeddings, such as the ability to capture linear regularities between words, as demonstrated by the analogy examples.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Rigorous Evaluation:** The empirical evaluation is limited. While topic coherence is reported for Twenty Newsgroups, a more comprehensive quantitative evaluation is needed. This includes comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., standard LDA, doc2vec) using standard metrics (e.g., perplexity, topic diversity, downstream task performance). The claim of solving word analogies is overstated; a more systematic evaluation of this capability is required.
2. **Presentation and Clarity:** Section 2, describing the model, is difficult to follow. The notation is dense, and the explanation of how word2vec and LDA are combined lacks sufficient clarity for readers familiar with both techniques. A clearer, more intuitive explanation of the core mechanics is essential. Specific concepts, such as the motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target words, and the purpose of separating words from a marginal distribution, are not adequately explained.
3. **Missing Details & Justification:** Several design choices lack clear justification. For example, the specific values chosen for parameters like λ (Dirichlet likelihood strength) and α (Dirichlet concentration parameter) are not adequately motivated. Similarly, the explanation of “co-adaptation” is insufficient without further elaboration.
4. **Anonymity Violation:** Footnote 1 reveals a link to the authors’ code repository, which violates the conference’s anonymity policy.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more intuitive explanation of the model, focusing on how the word2vec and LDA components interact? A diagram or pseudocode might be helpful.
2. What is the motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target words, as opposed to the standard word2vec approach?
3. Please elaborate on the rationale behind the chosen values for the key hyperparameters (λ, α, and beta). How sensitive are the results to these parameters?
4. Could you provide a more comprehensive quantitative evaluation of lda2vec, including comparisons to relevant baseline models and standard evaluation metrics?
5. How does the performance of lda2vec scale with larger datasets and vocabularies?

**Additional Comments:**

*  Please provide the full venue information for the Chang et al. (2009) reference.
*  The claim in the conclusion about “solving word analogies” should be toned down, as it is based on a limited set of examples.
