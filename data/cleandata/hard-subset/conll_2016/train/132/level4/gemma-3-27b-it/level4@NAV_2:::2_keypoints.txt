- Proposes a neural-styled topic model that extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparsifies them to mimic topic models.  
- Model is well-motivated and the illustrative examples are promising.  
- Major limitation: no quantitative empirical evaluation — relies solely on illustrative examples, hindering assessment of real value.  
- Suggested evaluations: use document-topic vectors in supervised document classification versus traditional topic models; or evaluate directly on datasets with document similarity annotations.  
- Releasing code is commendable, but including a GitHub link breaks anonymity and could justify rejection.  
- Unclear selection criteria for presented topic examples — risk of cherry-picking.  
- Section 2.2.1’s method of computing topic word representations via pairwise comparisons with the entire vocabulary may be computationally infeasible for large vocabularies/topics.  
- Use of SpaCy for tokenization is not fully explained (e.g., whether limited to noun chunks), which matters given reliance on pre-trained word2vec with limited multi-word terms.  
- OOV handling is not discussed; applying the model to new documents raises issues with updated vs. non-updated embeddings.  
- Reporting that 20 topics worked best on Twenty Newsgroups is unsurprising; recommend an information-theoretic comparison with true labels to objectively compare with LDA.  
- Paper should compare against related neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).  
- Minor issues: typo “it’s similarity” → “its similarity”; awkward phrasing “topic basis are affected”; Figure 5 caption “words” → “terms”; inconsistent and incomplete reference formatting.
