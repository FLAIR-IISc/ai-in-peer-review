- Paper proposes a neural-styled topic model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparsify them to mimic topic-model outputs.  
- Model is promising with encouraging illustrative examples, but the paper lacks any empirical evaluation.  
- Reliance on example tables without representativeness discussion risks cherry-picking and prevents assessing true merit.  
- Suggested evaluations: use topic representations in supervised document classification vs. comparable topic models.  
- Suggested evaluations: evaluate on datasets with document similarity annotations via pairwise topic-vector comparisons.  
- Inclusion of a GitHub link breaks anonymity (potential grounds for rejection) despite code release being commendable.  
- Selection criteria for examples (Figures 3–6) are unclear, raising cherry-picking bias concerns.  
- Feasibility concerns about computing topic-to-word representations via pairwise comparisons for realistic vocabularies and topic counts.  
- Unclear how “tokens” are identified with SpaCy (e.g., noun chunks or other), especially given pre-trained word2vec’s limited multi-word coverage.  
- No discussion of handling OOV terms or the impact of updating word2vec embeddings on comparability with untuned OOV embeddings.  
- The finding that 20 topics is optimal on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; suggest direct information-theoretic comparison to true labels (e.g., vs. LDA).  
- Authors should compare against relevant neural topic models (lists three specific papers to consider).  
- Minor issues: typo ("it's similarity" → "its similarity"), awkward phrasing re: “topic basis” and verb agreement.  
- Minor issues: change Figure 5 caption “words” to “terms” and fix inconsistent/incomplete reference formatting (e.g., Roder et al.).
