- Proposes lda2vec: jointly learn word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures using automatic differentiation.

- Contribution: integrates a Dirichlet-style topic mixture into a word2vec framework, a novel joint optimization direction.

- Contribution: highlights practical benefits of using automatic differentiation frameworks (Chainer) for rapid topic-model experimentation.

- Contribution: enforces sparse document-topic distributions via a Dirichlet prior to retain interpretability.

- Strength: offers potential for more interpretable document representations than purely dense embeddings.

- Strength: well-motivated rationale for combining topic models and word embeddings, with clear discussion of complementary limitations.

- Strength: problem is relevant to the CONLL community focused on semantic understanding and interpretability.

- Strength: authors provide an open-source implementation facilitating reproducibility.

- Weakness: technical presentation is unclear and convoluted; key equations (e.g., loss derivation) lack sufficient explanation.

- Weakness: evaluation is weak—only cherry-picked examples shown, with no rigorous quantitative comparisons or baselines.

- Weakness: several design choices (e.g., sharing pivot and target word vectors) are unmotivated and unexplained.

- Weakness: footnote breaks double-blind review (anonymity violation) and should be removed.

- Weakness: informal and imprecise language and grammatical issues detract from paper quality.

- Question: clarify the model architecture with a step-by-step explanation and a diagram of information flow.

- Question: justify key design decisions and hyperparameter choices (e.g., sharing vectors, α, λ, β).

- Question: perform a rigorous evaluation on standard datasets with baselines (LDA, word2vec, doc2vec) and statistical tests, and show downstream task benefits.

- Question: explain and justify the choice of topic coherence metric (Cv) for evaluating learned topics.

- Additional: paper needs a more concise, focused presentation that centers on core contributions and evaluation.

- Additional: careful proofreading is required to eliminate grammatical errors and improve clarity.
