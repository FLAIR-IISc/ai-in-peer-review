- Combining word2vec and LDA is an interesting direction but the paper lacks technical clarity.
- Section 2 needs a complete rewrite so readers familiar with word2vec and LDA can grasp the integration at a high level.
- Introduction’s third paragraph is vague—“requires deriving a new approximation” doesn’t say what is being approximated.
- The claim that developing prototypes is time-consuming and that features are easier to evaluate is asserted without justification.
- The choice to use the same word vectors for pivot and target words (deviating from standard word2vec) is not motivated.
- The phrase “separating words from a marginal distribution” is unclear and needs explanation.
- The term “co-adaptation” is used without definition or clarification.
- The sentence “If we only included structure up to this point” lacks context about what structure is meant.
- Minor grammar error: “it’s similarity” should be “its similarity.”
- Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission.
- The paper lacks a robust evaluation; relying solely on example clusters is insufficient for current NLP standards.
- Figure 2 appears to contain quantitative evaluation but is only explained in a long caption and not discussed in the main text.
- The conclusion overstates results by claiming the model “solves” word analogies based on a few selective examples.
- The citation for Chang is incomplete (only “Advances in…”), making the source hard to locate.
