- Proposes lda2vec: a hybrid that jointly learns dense word vectors and Dirichlet-distributed document-topic mixtures, implemented in Chainer and evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and a Hacker News corpus.  
- Strength: Novel hybrid approach combining interpretability of topic models with representational power of embeddings.  
- Strength: Practical, efficient implementation using Chainer with GPU acceleration, easing prototyping and training.  
- Strength: Qualitative evidence of semantic learning and domain-specific relationships (e.g., word analogies) on Hacker News.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited and largely qualitative; lacks quantitative comparisons to LDA, word2vec, and modern baselines on standard metrics.  
- Weakness: Presentation and technical clarity are poor, especially Section 2 and explanations of key concepts.  
- Weakness: Several design choices (shared pivot/target vectors, α = n−1, λ = 200) are unjustified and lack ablation studies.  
- Question: Why use the same vectors for pivot and target words, and how does this choice affect performance?  
- Question: What quantitative metrics and baseline comparisons were used beyond the reported topic coherence examples?  
- Question: Please clarify the “co-adaptation” issue and provide an intuitive explanation of how the dropout mechanism addresses it.  
- Question: What is the computational complexity of lda2vec versus traditional LDA and word2vec, and how well does it scale?  
- Comment: Remove Footnote 1 to preserve submission anonymity.  
- Comment: The claim that the model “solves” word analogies is overstated; examples appear cherry-picked and not comprehensive.  
- Comment: Provide complete venue information for the Chang et al. reference.  
- Comment: Expand the related work to cover recent advances in neural topic modeling.
