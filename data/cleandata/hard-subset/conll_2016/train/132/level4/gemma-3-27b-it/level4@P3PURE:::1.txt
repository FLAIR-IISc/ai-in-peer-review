A combination of word2vec and LDA holds potential interest. However, the primary weakness of this paper lies in the incomprehensibility of its technical details. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to provide a reader familiar with word2vec and LDA with a clear, high-level understanding of how the models are being integrated. The current presentation fails to achieve this. 

More detailed comments:

The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. The phrase “requires deriving a new approximation” is vague – approximation *of what*? The statement regarding the time required to develop prototypes is also unclear; why is prototype development considered time-consuming? Conversely, why is feature evaluation described as easier? The decision to use the same word vectors for both pivot and target words, unlike in standard word2vec, lacks justification. What is the motivation behind this choice?

Several statements require clarification. What is meant by “separating words from a marginal distribution”? The term “co-adaptation” is used without definition. The phrase “If we only included structure up to this point” is ambiguous; what specific structure is being referred to? In the sentence discussing similarity, “it’s similarity” should be corrected to “its similarity.” 

Footnote 1 inadvertently breaks anonymity.

Critically, the paper lacks a proper evaluation. Presenting example clusters, while potentially illustrative, is insufficient by current standards in NLP. Figure 2 *appears* to present quantitative evaluation results, but these are relegated to an excessively long caption and not discussed in the main text.

The conclusion’s claim that the model solves word analogies is an overstatement. The evidence presented consists only of a few selectively chosen examples, such as “king + queen,” and does not demonstrate a general ability to solve analogy tasks.

Finally, the citation for Chang includes an incomplete venue name (“Advances in ...”). The full conference or journal name should be provided for clarity.
