- Proposes a neural-styled topic model learning document embeddings via an extended word2vec objective and sparsification to mimic topic models.  
- Model is compelling and examples are promising, but the paper lacks empirical evaluation, relying only on illustrative examples.  
- Suggested evaluation 1: use document topic representations in supervised document classification, comparing against a topic model with the same number of topics.  
- Suggested evaluation 2: evaluate on a dataset with document similarity annotations using pairwise comparisons of topic vectors.  
- Releasing code is good, but including a GitHub link breaks anonymity and could be grounds for rejection.  
- Unclear how Figures 3–6 were selected; presenting a subset risks cherry-picking bias.  
- Computational feasibility concern: computing topic word representations via pairwise comparison with every vocabulary word seems expensive for realistic vocabularies/topics.  
- Tokenization is unclear: how SpaCy "tokens" were identified (noun chunks only or other types) and how that interacts with pre-trained word2vec multi-word terms.  
- OOV handling is not addressed; applying the model to novel documents is problematic if word embeddings are updated during training.  
- The claim that 20 topics work best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; suggest information-theoretic comparison with true labels (e.g., vs. LDA).  
- Paper should compare against other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. AAAI 2015; Nguyen et al. TACL 2015; Shamanta et al. MLKD 2015).  
- Minor issues: typo "it's similarity" → "its similarity"; unclear phrasing about how the "topic basis" is affected.  
- Minor suggestions: change Figure 5 caption from “words” to “terms”; fix inconsistent reference formatting and add missing conference for Roder et al.
