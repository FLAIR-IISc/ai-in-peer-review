Combining word2vec and LDA presents a potentially interesting direction for research. However, the primary weakness of this paper lies in the lack of clarity in its technical presentation. Section 2 requires a substantial revision to clearly convey, at a high level, how these models are integrated – even for readers already familiar with word2vec and LDA. The current explanation fails to provide this necessary understanding. 

Specific points of concern include: the third paragraph of the introduction, which is currently unintelligible; several undefined concepts ("approximation of what?", "time consuming to develop prototypes?", "easier to evaluate features?", "separate words from a marginal distribution?", "co-adaptation?"); ambiguous phrasing ("If we only included structure up to this point…", "it's similarity"); and a grammatical error ("its"). Additionally, Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the review process.

Critically, the paper lacks a rigorous evaluation. Simply presenting example clusters is insufficient. While Figure 2 *appears* to show quantitative results, these are relegated to the figure caption and are not discussed within the main text. The conclusion’s claim that the model "solves word analogies" is an overstatement, supported only by a limited set of curated examples. Finally, the citation for Chang is incomplete, lacking the full conference or journal name ("Advances in…").
