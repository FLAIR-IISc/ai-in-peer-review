- Proposes a neural-styled topic model that extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and enforces sparsity to mimic topic model outputs.
- Model is promising and examples are encouraging, but the paper lacks empirical evaluation.
- Recommend evaluations: (1) use topic representations for supervised document classification vs a traditional topic model with same topic count.
- Recommend evaluations: (2) direct evaluation on datasets with document similarity annotations using pairwise comparisons of topic vectors.
- Open-source code release is commendable but the GitHub link in the paper breaks anonymity (possible ground for rejection).
- Unclear how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected; risk of cherry-picking.
- Computing topic word representations via pairwise comparison with the full vocabulary seems computationally expensive—feasibility concerns.
- Tokenization unclear: how SpaCy was used and whether noun-chunk extraction or multiword term handling was applied.
- Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is not addressed for applying the model to new documents.
- Finding that 20 topics works best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; suggest simple information-theoretic comparisons to true labels and LDA.
- Paper should compare against neural topic models such as Cao et al. (2015), Nguyen et al. (2015), and Shamanta et al. (2015).
- Minor issues: typo ("it's" → "its"), awkward phrasing on Line 361, Figure 5 caption should possibly use "terms" not "words," and inconsistent reference formatting.
