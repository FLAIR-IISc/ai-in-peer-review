- Positive: Core idea of extending word2vec to learn document embeddings with sparsification to approximate topic behavior is compelling and examples are promising.  
- Major flaw: No quantitative empirical evaluation—reliance on example tables prevents assessment of representativeness and performance versus established topic models.  
- Suggested evaluations: (1) use learned document representations in supervised document classification versus comparable-topic models; (2) evaluate document similarity via pairwise comparisons on datasets with similarity annotations.  
- Anonymity breach: Inclusion of a GitHub link compromises blind review and technically warrants rejection (final decision with PCs).  
- Unclear example selection: Criteria for examples in Figures 3–6 are not specified, suggesting possible cherry-picking.  
- Scalability concern: Section 2.2.1’s method of computing topic word representations via pairwise comparisons with the vocabulary may be computationally infeasible for realistic vocabularies and topic counts.  
- Tokenization ambiguity: Section 3.1 references SpaCy for token identification but does not specify the method (e.g., noun chunks vs. tokens), which matters given multiword terms.  
- OOV handling omitted: The paper does not address out-of-vocabulary terms or how the model handles novel documents with updated vs. pre-trained embeddings.  
- Dataset-topic coincidence: Finding 20 topics optimal for 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; an information-theoretic comparison to true labels (and direct comparison with LDA) is recommended.  
- Missing related work: Should cite and discuss Cao et al. (AAAI 2015), Nguyen et al. (TACL 2015), and Shamanta et al. (MLKD 2015).  
- Minor edits: line 315 "it's similarity" → "its similarity"; line 361 clarify and rephrase "topic basis" effect; Figure 5 caption replace "words" with "terms".  
- Reference issues: Inconsistent reference formatting (title capitalization, missing conference names) throughout the manuscript.
