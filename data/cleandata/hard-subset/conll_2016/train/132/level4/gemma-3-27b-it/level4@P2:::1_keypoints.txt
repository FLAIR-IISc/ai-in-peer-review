- Paper proposes lda2vec: jointly learning dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures in a single differentiable model.
- Primary contribution: seamless integration of word2vec-style embeddings with LDA-like topic mixtures to combine semantic representation and interpretability.
- Secondary contribution: empirical demonstrations on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News showing learned topics and word analogies.
- Reported topic coherence scores and qualitative examples suggest promising but not definitive results.
- Strength: novel integration of established techniques bridging dense embeddings and sparse topic models, implemented in an automatic-differentiation framework.
- Strength: demonstrated adaptability across datasets, with Hacker News experiments showing domain-specific representations.
- Strength: qualitative topic and analogy examples provide initial insight into model behavior and include topic coherence metrics.
- Weakness: technical presentation lacks clarity—Section 2 is hard to follow and notation/motivations need clearer exposition.
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative evaluation—needs rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art topic models and downstream tasks.
- Weakness: some claims are overstated (e.g., "solve word analogies") and examples appear cherry-picked without systematic evaluation.
- Weakness: anonymity breach via a footnote linking to the authors’ code repository.
- Question: please explain intuitively why the same word vectors are used for pivot and target words instead of the standard separate vectors.
- Question: clarify the specific challenges of deriving approximations for new topic models and how lda2vec addresses them.
- Question: what metrics and systematic evaluations were used for the claimed word analogy performance?
- Question: provide detailed experimental setup information, including hyperparameter tuning and computational resources.
- Additional: correct typographical errors (e.g., "it's similarity" → "its similarity") and complete bibliographic entries.
- Additional: clarify phrases such as "separating words from a marginal distribution," "co-adaptation," and the meaning of “structure” in the referenced sentence.
