- Proposes a neural topic model that extends word2vec to learn document embeddings which are sparsified to mimic topic distributions; core idea is promising with compelling qualitative examples.
- Primary weakness: complete lack of quantitative empirical evaluation; claims are unsupported by metrics or comparisons.
- Recommend evaluation 1: use document embeddings as features in supervised document classification to compare representation quality against standard topic models with same topic count.
- Recommend evaluation 2: directly evaluate topic vectors on a dataset with human document similarity annotations, measuring correlation with judgments.
- Positive: authors’ commitment to releasing code is good, but including a GitHub link in the submission breaks anonymity and may be a fatal flaw for review.
- Example selection is unclear; must justify and transparently report how examples (Figures 3–6) were chosen to avoid cherry-picking.
- Computational feasibility concern: computing topic word representations via pairwise comparisons with every vocabulary word is likely prohibitive at realistic scales and needs addressing.
- Tokenization needs clarification: explain precisely how “tokens” are extracted with SpaCy (noun chunks only or other methods), especially given pre-trained word2vec’s limited multi-word coverage.
- OOV handling is not addressed; need to explain behavior for novel documents and differences between updated vs. fixed embeddings.
- 20 Newsgroups result is unsurprising; propose information-theoretic comparison to true labels and direct comparison to LDA for more insight.
- Missing related work: cite and compare to recent neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Minor issues to fix: typo line 315 ("it's" → "its"), clarify awkward phrasing at line 361 about the "topic basis," change Figure 5 caption to "terms" if appropriate, and standardize/complete reference formatting.
