This paper explores a potentially promising combination of word2vec and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). However, the primary weakness lies in the lack of clarity in the technical presentation. Section 2 requires a substantial rewrite to clearly articulate how these models are integrated, even for readers already familiar with both word2vec and LDA. The current explanation is insufficient to provide a high-level understanding of the approach.

Specifically, several points within the paper require clarification. The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. The phrase “requires deriving a new approximation” lacks context – what is being approximated? Furthermore, the statements regarding the time required for prototype development and the ease of feature evaluation are unsubstantiated and require explanation. 

Several design choices lack sufficient motivation. The paper does not justify the decision to utilize identical word vectors for both pivot and target words, a departure from standard word2vec practice. The meaning of “separating words from a marginal distribution” is also unclear. The term “co-adaptation” is used without definition, hindering comprehension. The phrase “If we only included structure up to this point” requires specification – what ‘structure’ is being referenced? A minor correction is needed: “it’s similarity” should be “its similarity”.

A breach of anonymity occurs in Footnote 1.

A critical deficiency is the absence of rigorous evaluation. Qualitative examples, such as cluster displays, are no longer considered sufficient justification in the NLP research community. Figure 2 appears to present quantitative results, but this information is buried within a lengthy caption rather than being formally discussed in the main text.

The conclusion’s claim that the model “solves word analogies” is an overstatement. The paper demonstrates only a limited number of carefully selected examples (e.g., “king + queen”), which do not constitute a comprehensive evaluation of analogical reasoning ability. 

Finally, there is a bibliographic error in the citation for Chang; the venue is listed as "Advances in …". Please provide the full and accurate conference or journal name.
