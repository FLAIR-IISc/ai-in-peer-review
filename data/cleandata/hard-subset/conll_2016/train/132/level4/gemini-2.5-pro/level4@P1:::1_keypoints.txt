- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, which jointly learns word embeddings and Dirichlet-constrained document-topic mixtures by adding a document-topic component to the SGNS objective and a Dirichlet sparsity loss.  
- Model detail: Context vector = pivot word vector + document vector; document vector = weighted sum of topic vectors; Dirichlet prior encourages sparse, interpretable topics.  
- Strength: Novel, intuitive synthesis of dense word embeddings and interpretable topic models in a single vector space.  
- Strength: Implemented in a modern autodiff framework (Chainer) with open-source code, easing extension and experimentation.  
- Weakness: Technical description is unclear; Section 2 needs rewriting for a clearer high-level exposition.  
- Weakness: Introduction paragraph is confusing about the claimed "new approximation" and why modern frameworks alleviate evaluation difficulties.  
- Weakness: Key terms (e.g., "co-adaptation") are undefined and vague phrases hinder understanding.  
- Weakness: Design choices (e.g., using same vectors for pivot and target) are not motivated or justified.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is insufficient—relies on qualitative examples and cherry-picked analogies without rigorous quantitative comparisons or downstream-task baselines.  
- Weakness: Topic coherence results are only briefly shown in a figure caption and not discussed or compared to baselines.  
- Weakness: Anonymity breach—GitHub link reveals author identity, violating double-blind review.  
- Weakness: Overclaiming in conclusions (e.g., "solves word analogies") unsupported by comprehensive evaluation.  
- Weakness: Minor presentation errors (incomplete references, grammatical mistakes).  
- Question: Clarify the “approximation” referenced in the intro and explain how the framework makes evaluating model features easier.  
- Question: Why use the same word vectors for pivot and target; were separate input/output embeddings tried?  
- Question: Define “co-adaptation” in this context and explain how dropout on document/word vectors prevents it.
