- Introduces lda2vec: jointly learns dense word vectors and Dirichlet-distributed topic mixtures, integrating skip-gram negative sampling with sparse topic mixtures to embed words, documents, and topics in one space.
- Main contributions: a unified model combining embeddings and sparse topic modeling, and demonstration of implementing it using automatic differentiation rather than bespoke inference algorithms.
- Strength: novel hybrid approach that could combine semantic power of embeddings with interpretable, sparse document representations.
- Strength: qualitative results (Twenty Newsgroups, Hacker News) show coherent topics and plausible corpus-specific word similarities/analogies.
- Strength: practical implementation ease—shows complex probabilistic models can be built with automatic differentiation, lowering development barriers.
- Weakness: technical description is unclear and lacks motivation for key choices (e.g., single embedding for pivot/target, unexplained terms like “co‑adaptation”).
- Weakness: evaluation is inadequate and claims are overstated—mostly cherry-picked qualitative examples, no rigorous quantitative baselines, and topic coherence is presented without context.
- Weakness: breach of anonymity via a GitHub link in a footnote violates double-blind review policy.
- Question: why use a single embedding matrix for both pivot and target words—what are the theoretical/empirical benefits?
- Question: which traditional approximation methods (e.g., variational inference, Gibbs sampling) does the approach avoid, and how does AD-based optimization compare?
- Question: how do the reported topic coherence scores compare to standard LDA on the same preprocessed Twenty Newsgroups dataset?
