The proposed synthesis of word2vec and LDA is a promising research direction. However, the paper's primary weakness is the incomprehensibility of its technical exposition. Section 2, in particular, requires a complete revision. As currently written, it is difficult for a reader already familiar with both word2vec and LDA to formulate a high-level understanding of how the two models are being integrated. The current presentation fails to provide this necessary clarity.

More specific comments:

*   The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. The manuscript mentions the need for "deriving a new approximation," but it does not specify what is being approximated. The claims regarding the time-consuming nature of prototype development and the relative ease of feature evaluation are also presented without justification.
*   The rationale for using identical word vectors for both pivot and target words, a departure from the standard word2vec approach, is not explained. The motivation for this design choice should be provided.
*   The phrase "separate words from a marginal distribution" is ambiguous and requires clarification.
*   The term "co-adaptation" is used without a clear definition in the context of the model.
*   The manuscript refers to including "structure up to this point," but the specific type of structure being referenced is not specified.
*   A grammatical correction is needed: "it's similarity" should be "its similarity."
*   Footnote 1 compromises the double-blind review process.
*   The paper lacks a formal quantitative evaluation. Presenting qualitative examples of clusters is insufficient for the current standards in NLP research. While Figure 2 appears to contain quantitative results, this information is confined to a lengthy caption and is not properly discussed or contextualized within the main body of the paper.
*   The conclusion's claim that the model solves word analogies is an overstatement of the results. The evidence provided consists only of a few curated examples and is not sufficient to support such a strong assertion.
*   The reference to Chang et al. is incomplete, listing the venue as "Advances in ...". The full conference or journal title should be provided.
