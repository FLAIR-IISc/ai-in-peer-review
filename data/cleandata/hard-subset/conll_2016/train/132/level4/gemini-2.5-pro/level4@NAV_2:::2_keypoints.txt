- Proposal: lda2vec jointly learns word vectors and Dirichlet-distributed sparse topic mixtures by defining context as pivot word vector plus document topic mixture, embedding words, topics, and documents in one space.
- Main contribution: novel, elegant architecture integrating SGNS with topic modeling to capture token-level semantics and interpretable document-level themes simultaneously.

Strengths:
- Novel & intuitive formulation: simple cj = wj + dj injects document-level thematic info into word embedding learning.
- Promising qualitative results: coherent topics on 20NG and Hacker News and preserved semantic linearities in word vectors.
- Modern implementation: built with automatic differentiation, publicly released code, enhancing reproducibility and extensibility.

Weaknesses:
- No quantitative evaluation or comparisons to baselines (e.g., LDA or neural topic models) on intrinsic or downstream tasks.
- Risk of cherry-picking: qualitative examples lack description of selection method, so representativeness is unclear.
- Insufficient related-work comparison: omits several relevant neural topic/embedding works (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Violation of anonymity: public GitHub link reveals author identity, breaching double-blind review rules.

Questions to authors:
- How were the example topics, tokens, and analogy queries in Figures 3–6 selected (random vs. quality-driven)?
- How does the model handle OOV terms when inferring topic distributions for new, unseen documents?
- Why not provide a simple quantitative evaluation (e.g., compare document-topic distributions to 20NG labels using purity or NMI)?
- For Hacker News with ~110k tokens, how is the topic-to-vocabulary similarity computed and how were “Top tokens” generated computationally?
- Clarify tokenization in the 20 Newsgroups experiment: single-word tokens or multi-word phrases, and how this interacts with pre-trained embeddings.
