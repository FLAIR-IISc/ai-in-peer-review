- Proposes a neural topic model extending the word2vec objective to jointly learn word and document embeddings, with document embeddings sparsified to mimic traditional topic models.
- Model is conceptually interesting and qualitative examples look promising.
- Major weakness: no quantitative empirical evaluation provided, relying solely on example tables.
- Recommendation: include indirect evaluation via supervised document classification using topic features versus baselines (e.g., LDA).
- Recommendation: include direct evaluation using datasets with document similarity annotations to assess document representation quality.
- Procedural issue: manuscript includes a public GitHub link, compromising double-blind review and potentially warranting rejection.
- Example selection is unexplained, raising concerns about cherry-picking of topics in Figures 3–6.
- Scalability concern: computing topic word representations via pairwise comparisons with every vocabulary word seems computationally infeasible for realistic vocabularies/topics.
- Tokenization is unclear: ambiguity whether SpaCy tokens mean noun chunks or other tokens, and how multi-word expressions are handled given pre-trained word2vec.
- OOV handling is not addressed; fine-tuned embeddings on training data may not be comparable for unseen words in new documents.
- Missed opportunity: trivial quantitative evaluation on 20 Newsgroups (e.g., NMI between topics and labels) was not performed.
- Missing comparisons to relevant neural topic-model literature (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Minor issues: typo ("it's" → "its"), awkward phrasing about "topic basis," inaccurate Figure 5 caption terminology ("terms" vs "words"), and inconsistent/erroneous reference formatting.
