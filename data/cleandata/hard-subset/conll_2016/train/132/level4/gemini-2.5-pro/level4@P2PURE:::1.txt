The proposed combination of word2vec and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is a potentially interesting research direction. However, the primary weakness of the paper in its current form is the lack of clarity in its technical presentation, which makes the proposed model difficult to understand. Section 2, in particular, requires a complete revision to provide a reader familiar with word2vec and LDA with a clear, high-level understanding of how the two models are integrated. The current description does not achieve this.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   The third paragraph of the introduction is difficult to follow, and several claims are not substantiated. For instance, what is the "new approximation" being derived for? Why is developing prototypes described as "time-consuming," and what makes evaluating features "easier" in this context?
*   The paper deviates from the standard word2vec formulation by using the same vectors for both pivot and target words. The motivation for this significant design choice is not explained.
*   The manuscript uses several key terms without sufficient definition or context. For example:
    *   What is meant by "separating words from a marginal distribution"?
    *   The term "co-adaptation" is used without a clear definition.
    *   The phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" is ambiguous; it is not clear what "structure" is being referred to.
*   The paper lacks a quantitative evaluation, which is a significant shortcoming. Presenting a few example clusters is insufficient to validate the model's effectiveness by modern NLP standards. While Figure 2 may represent a quantitative analysis, it is only described in a lengthy caption and is not properly presented or discussed in the main body of the paper.
*   The claim in the conclusion that the model "solves word analogies" is an overstatement. The evidence provided consists of a few cherry-picked examples, which is not sufficient to support such a strong claim. A systematic evaluation on a standard analogy dataset is required.

**Other Issues:**

*   Footnote 1 appears to violate the anonymity requirements of the conference.
*   The citation for Chang et al. is incomplete, listing the venue simply as "Advances in...". The full conference or journal name should be provided.
*   There is a grammatical error on page X: "it's similarity" should be "its similarity".
