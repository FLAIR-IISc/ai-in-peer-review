- Proposes combining word2vec-style embeddings with LDA to create topic-aware word representations; the idea is promising.  
- Paper has two major problems: lack of technical clarity and almost no empirical evaluation.  
- Technical description (especially Section 2) is opaque and needs a complete rewrite.  
- A reader familiar with word2vec and LDA should be able to understand the integration, but the exposition leaves many unanswered questions.  
- Empirical evaluation is nearly absent; qualitative clusters are insufficient by current NLP standards.  
- Authors should evaluate on standard intrinsic tasks (word similarity, analogy) and extrinsic tasks (text classification, sentiment) and compare to baselines like word2vec and LDA features.  
- Figure 2 appears quantitative but is not described in the main text and is relegated to a long caption, making it hard to interpret.  
- Unclear claim: what is the “new approximation” being derived, and why is it needed?  
- Unclear design choice: why use the same word vectors for pivot and target words instead of separate vectors?  
- Undefined terms/phrases (e.g., “separate words from a marginal distribution,” “co-adaptation”) need formal definitions.  
- Ambiguous wording: clarify what “structure” refers to in “If we only included structure up to this point.”  
- Overstated claim that the model “solves word analogies”; evidence is limited to cherry-picked examples without systematic evaluation.  
- Footnote 1 may breach anonymity and should be removed or rephrased.  
- Minor fixes: grammatical error (“it's similarity” → “its similarity”) and incomplete Chang et al. reference (venue missing).  
- Summary: idea has merit but paper is not ready for publication; requires major revision to address clarity and evaluation.
