- Proposes a neural topic model extending the word2vec objective to learn document embeddings and sparsifying them to mimic sparse document-topic distributions.
- Model is conceptually appealing and qualitative examples are compelling.
- Major shortcoming: complete lack of quantitative empirical evaluation.
- Examples shown may be unrepresentative; no information on selection or prevalence of presented topics.
- No direct comparisons to standard or other neural topic models, making contribution hard to assess.
- Suggestion: perform indirect task-based evaluation (e.g., use document-topic features for supervised classification vs. LDA baseline).
- Suggestion: perform direct evaluation on datasets with ground-truth document similarity using pairwise comparisons of topic vectors.
- Inclusion of a public GitHub link violates double-blind review policy and is a serious issue.
- Method for selecting examples in Figures 3–6 is not described, raising cherry-picking concerns.
- Section 2.2.1’s pairwise comparison with every vocabulary word seems computationally infeasible; scalability needs discussion.
- Tokenization procedure is ambiguous (SpaCy tokens vs. noun chunks), unclear handling of multi-word expressions vs. pre-trained word2vec coverage.
- No discussion of handling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms for applying the model to new documents.
- The 20-topic result on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; recommend information-theoretic cluster vs. label comparison for quantitative assessment.
- Related work discussion is incomplete and should compare with other neural topic models (several cited papers recommended).
- Minor language/grammar issues: "it's similarity" → "its similarity"; unclear phrasing "the topic basis are affected."
- Figure 5 caption should use "terms" instead of "words" to account for multi-word expressions.
- Reference formatting is inconsistent and some citations are incomplete (e.g., Roder et al. missing venue).
