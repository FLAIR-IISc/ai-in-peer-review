- Proposal: neural topic model extending the word2vec objective to learn document embeddings that are sparsified to mimic traditional topic distributions (elegant, promising qualitative examples).  
- Major flaw: complete lack of quantitative empirical evaluation—reliance on a few selected examples prevents assessment or comparison with baseline/topic models.  
- Suggested extrinsic evaluation: use learned document vectors as features in supervised classification and compare against baselines (e.g., LDA) with the same number of topics.  
- Suggested intrinsic evaluation: evaluate document similarity against human judgments (e.g., cosine similarity on a dataset with similarity annotations).  
- Anonymity breach: inclusion of a GitHub link in the manuscript violates anonymity policy and may warrant rejection.  
- Example selection unclear: Figures 3–6 lack a stated selection methodology, raising concerns about potential cherry-picking.  
- Computational feasibility concern: Section 2.2.1’s pairwise topic-word comparisons with every vocabulary word appears infeasible for realistic vocabulary/topic sizes and needs clarification or an efficient alternative.  
- Tokenization ambiguity: Section 3.1’s use of SpaCy to identify “tokens” is unclear (noun chunks vs. single tokens), especially given use of pre-trained word2vec embeddings—tokenization strategy must be clarified.  
- OOV handling missing: paper does not explain how out-of-vocabulary terms are handled, nor how fine-tuned embeddings compare to original pre-trained embeddings for OOVs.  
- Missed simple quantitative check: reporting that 20 topics works best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising and could have been validated with measures like NMI against ground-truth labels for direct comparison with LDA.  
- Related work gap: paper should compare to recent neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).  
- Minor issues—typos and clarity: "it's similarity" → "its similarity" (Line 315); unclear phrasing "topic basis are affected" (Line 361).  
- Minor issue—figure terminology: Figure 5 caption should use "terms" rather than "words" for multi-word expressions.  
- Minor issue—references: bibliography formatting is inconsistent and incomplete (e.g., abbreviated titles, missing conference names); needs cleanup.
