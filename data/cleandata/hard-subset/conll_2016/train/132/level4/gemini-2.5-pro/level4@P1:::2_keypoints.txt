- Paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid that jointly learns word embeddings, topic vectors, and sparse document-topic distributions by summing word and document vectors in a skip-gram objective.  
- Elegant and intuitive model that unifies word2vec-style embeddings with LDA-like interpretable topic mixtures.  
- Promising qualitative results: coherent topics and domain-specific analogies on 20 Newsgroups and Hacker News.  
- Authors provide an open-source implementation, aiding reproducibility.  
- Major weakness: no quantitative empirical evaluation — results are exclusively qualitative and prevent objective comparison to baselines.  
- Reviewer recommends extrinsic (e.g., classification) and intrinsic (e.g., document similarity) evaluations to measure performance.  
- Insufficient comparison to related neural topic-model literature; paper omits comparison/positioning against recent works (e.g., Cao 2015, Nguyen 2015, Shamanta 2015).  
- Risk of cherry-picking: examples may be unrepresentative and selection process is not described.  
- Procedural flaw: submission breaches double-blind anonymity via a public GitHub link — serious issue that may warrant rejection.  
- Question on OOV handling: how are unseen words in new documents handled given fine-tuned embeddings and potential transductive limitation?  
- Question on preprocessing: clarify tokenization and mapping to pre-trained embeddings (SpaCy tokens vs. multi-word expressions).  
- Question on computational feasibility: computing topic similarity to all vocabulary words (e.g., 110k) may be costly — is there an efficient approximation?  
- Suggestion for quantitative 20 Newsgroups eval: compare discovered topics to ground-truth labels (purity/NMI) and against LDA.  
- Minor edits requested: fix typo ("it's"→"its"), clarify "topic basis" phrasing, label Figure 5 terms/tokens vs words, and correct inconsistent/incomplete bibliography formatting.
