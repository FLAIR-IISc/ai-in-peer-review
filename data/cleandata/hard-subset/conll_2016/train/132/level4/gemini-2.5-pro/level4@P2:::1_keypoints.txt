- Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model that learns word, document, and topic vectors by combining SGNS (word2vec) with LDA-style sparse document-topic mixtures.
- Context vectors are formed by summing pivot word vectors and document vectors, with a Dirichlet term encouraging sparse, interpretable document-topic weights.
- Shows qualitative results on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News demonstrating coherent topics and semantic relationships.
- Main contribution: a unified architecture that jointly learns embeddings for words, documents, and topics in a shared space.
- Main contribution: demonstrates implementation and optimization of the hybrid model using automatic differentiation frameworks.
- Strength: novel and intuitive combination of word embeddings and topic modeling that may yield richer representations.
- Strength: explicit focus on interpretability via a Dirichlet sparsity objective producing coherent, understandable topics.
- Weakness: model description is unclear and hard to follow; several terms and motivations are not sufficiently explained.
- Weakness: confusing claims about automatic differentiation and unclear use of terms like “co-adaptation.”
- Weakness: evaluation is largely qualitative and lacks rigorous quantitative comparisons to baselines.
- Weakness: topic coherence results are insufficiently discussed and not compared against LDA or other baselines.
- Weakness: claim that the model “solves word analogies” is overstated and supported only by a few examples.
- Weakness: key design choices (e.g., single embedding for pivot and target words, Dirichlet strength λ=200) lack justification or sensitivity/ablation studies.
- Question: why use a single embedding for input and output words instead of separate input/output embeddings?
- Question: please provide quantitative comparisons to standard LDA (topic quality/perplexity) and SGNS (word similarity/analogy) on the same datasets.
- Question: clarify what “co-adaptation” refers to in this model and how dropout prevents it.
- Additional: remove Footnote 1 linking to a public GitHub that breaks anonymity.
- Additional: complete the incomplete reference for Chang et al. (2009).
- Additional: fix minor typo: “it's similarity” → “its similarity” in Section 2.2.1.
