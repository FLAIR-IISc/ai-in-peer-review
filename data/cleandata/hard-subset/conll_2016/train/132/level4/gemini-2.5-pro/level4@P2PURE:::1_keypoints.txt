- Combination of word2vec and LDA is interesting but the technical presentation is unclear; Section 2 needs a complete revision to explain the integration.  
- Third paragraph of the introduction is hard to follow and contains unsubstantiated claims (e.g., what is the "new approximation"? why are prototypes “time-consuming”? why is feature evaluation “easier”?).  
- The model departs from standard word2vec by using the same vectors for pivot and target words, but this design choice is not motivated.  
- Several key terms are used without definition or context (e.g., "separating words from a marginal distribution", "co-adaptation", ambiguous use of "structure").  
- Paper lacks quantitative evaluation; showing a few example clusters is insufficient and Figure 2 is only described in a caption rather than discussed in the main text.  
- The claim that the model "solves word analogies" is an overstatement based on cherry-picked examples; a systematic evaluation on standard analogy datasets is required.  
- Footnote 1 may breach the conference anonymity requirements.  
- Citation for Chang et al. is incomplete (venue given as "Advances in...").  
- Typographical/grammatical error: "it's similarity" should be "its similarity".
