- Contribution: Proposes a neural topic model extending the word2vec objective to jointly learn document embeddings which are sparsified into topic-like representations.
- Positive: Model is conceptually interesting and qualitative examples look promising.
- Major weakness: No quantitative evaluation — assessment relies entirely on qualitative examples with no empirical comparisons to baseline or existing topic models.
- Suggested quantitative evaluations: use learned document embeddings for supervised document classification vs LDA or evaluate embeddings on document similarity tasks with human annotations.
- Policy violation: Inclusion of a public GitHub link compromises anonymity and is a serious review-process breach that could justify rejection.
- Clarify figure selection: Authors must explain how example topics in Figures 3–6 were chosen to avoid concerns of cherry-picking.
- Scalability concern: Section 2.2.1’s pairwise comparison of topics with every vocabulary word appears computationally prohibitive; authors should discuss scalability and complexity.
- Tokenization ambiguity: Section 3.1 states tokens are identified using SpaCy but does not specify whether noun chunks or another method are used, creating confusion about preprocessing.
- OOV handling: The paper does not explain how to handle out-of-vocabulary terms for new documents given fine-tuned word embeddings; a strategy for inferring topics with unseen words is needed.
- Missed evaluation opportunity: The use of 20 topics on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising and could have supported a simple quantitative comparison (e.g., information-theoretic match to ground-truth labels, compared to LDA).
- Related work incomplete: Expand discussion to include other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015) and similar prior work.
- Minor issues: grammar fixes ("it's" → "its"; rephrase line 361), caption wording ("terms" vs "words"), and inconsistent/missing reference formatting (e.g., Roder et al.).
