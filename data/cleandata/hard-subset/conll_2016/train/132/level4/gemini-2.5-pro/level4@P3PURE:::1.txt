This paper proposes a model that combines word2vec and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), which is a potentially interesting research direction. However, the main problem with the current submission is that the technical details are largely incomprehensible. Section 2, in particular, requires a complete rewrite. A reader familiar with both word2vec and LDA should be able to gain a high-level understanding of how the models are being combined, but the current presentation fails to achieve this.

**Major Concerns**

1.  **Lack of Clarity:** The description of the model and its motivation is extremely difficult to follow. Several key concepts and claims are introduced without adequate explanation. For example:
    *   The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. What "new approximation" needs to be derived? Why is it "time consuming to develop prototypes," and what makes it "easier to evaluate features"? These claims need justification.
    *   What does it mean to "separate words from a marginal distribution"?
    *   The term "co-adaptation" is used without a definition.
    *   The paper states, "If we only included structure up to this point..." without specifying what "structure" is being referred to.

2.  **Insufficient Evaluation:** The paper lacks any form of rigorous quantitative evaluation. In modern NLP, presenting a few example clusters as the sole form of results is no longer sufficient. While Figure 2 appears to present a quantitative evaluation, it is only described in an overly long caption and is not properly introduced or discussed in the main body of the paper.

3.  **Overstated Claims:** The conclusion claims that the model "solves word analogies." This is a significant overstatement of what has been shown, which is merely a few cherry-picked examples (e.g., king/queen). Such a strong claim requires validation on standard, comprehensive analogy benchmarks.

**Questions on Methodology**

*   The paper deviates from the standard word2vec formulation by using the same vectors for both pivot and target words. What is the motivation for this design choice?

**Minor Issues**

*   **Anonymity:** Footnote 1 breaks the anonymity of the review process and should be removed.
*   **Incomplete Reference:** The reference for Chang et al. lists the venue as "Advances in ...". This is not a complete or valid venue name and needs to be corrected.
*   **Typo:** "it's similarity" should be corrected to "its similarity".
