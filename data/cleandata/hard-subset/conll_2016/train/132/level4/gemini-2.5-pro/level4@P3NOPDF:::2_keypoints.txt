- Introduces a neural topic model that jointly learns word and document embeddings and sparsifies document embeddings to produce topic-like outputs.  
- Conceptually interesting and elegant, with promising qualitative examples.  
- Major weakness: no quantitative empirical evaluation; reliance solely on selected topic examples prevents objective assessment or comparison to prior work.  
- Suggests extrinsic evaluation: use document-topic representations in supervised tasks (e.g., classification) and compare accuracy/F1 against LDA and neural baselines.  
- Suggests intrinsic evaluation: evaluate document similarity against annotated datasets using cosine similarity comparisons to ground-truth scores.  
- Procedural issue: submission includes a public GitHub link, violating double-blind review policy and typically grounds for rejection.  
- Request clarification on how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected to avoid perceived cherry-picking and show representativeness.  
- Computational concern: computing topic representations via pairwise comparisons with every vocabulary word may be prohibitively expensive—need feasibility and cost analysis.  
- Preprocessing is unclear: clarify tokenization pipeline (SpaCy tokens vs. noun chunks) and compatibility with pre-trained word2vec single-word/multi-word expressions.  
- OOV handling is not addressed; fine-tuning embeddings raises issues comparing learned embeddings to original pre-trained vectors for unseen terms.  
- 20 Newsgroups evaluation is unsurprising; recommend comparing discovered topics to ground-truth labels using information-theoretic measures (e.g., NMI) and baselines like LDA.  
- Missing related work: should discuss/compare to prior neural topic models (Cao et al. AAAI 2015; Nguyen et al. TACL 2015; Shamanta et al. ECML PKDD 2015).  
- Minor issues and typos: grammatical errors, Figure 5 caption wording, inconsistent reference formatting, and other small edits needed.
