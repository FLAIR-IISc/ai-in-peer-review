- Proposes a neural topic model extending word2vec to jointly learn word and sparse document embeddings; idea is interesting and qualitative examples look promising.
- Major flaw: no empirical evaluation—no quantitative or comparative results to assess performance.
- Paper should compare against standard topic models (e.g., LDA) and other neural topic models.
- Recommend extrinsic evaluation (e.g., use document vectors for supervised classification).
- Recommend intrinsic evaluation (e.g., compare model document similarities to annotated similarity datasets).
- Procedural issue: submission breaks double-blind review by including a GitHub link that compromises anonymity.
- Example selection (Figures 3–6) is unexplained, raising concerns of cherry-picking.
- Scalability concern: computing topic word representations via pairwise comparisons with the entire vocabulary seems computationally prohibitive.
- Tokenization pipeline is unclear—spaCy noun-chunk extraction vs. pre-trained word2vec single-word embeddings needs clarification.
- OOV handling is not addressed; fine-tuning embeddings complicates application to unseen documents.
- 20 Newsgroups result (20 topics) is unsurprising; suggest information-theoretic evaluation (e.g., NMI) against ground-truth labels.
- Related work comparisons are missing; should discuss prior neural topic models that use distributed word representations (e.g., Cao 2015, Nguyen 2015, Shamanta 2015).
- Minor issues: grammar/wording errors (e.g., "it's similarity", awkward phrasing), caption wording ("terms" vs "words"), and inconsistent reference formatting.
