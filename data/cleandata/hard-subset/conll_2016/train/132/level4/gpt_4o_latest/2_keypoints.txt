- Paper proposes an innovative neural-inspired topic model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings constrained by sparsification to emulate topic model outputs.
- Positive: examples provided are compelling and show promise.
- Major concern: no empirical evaluation — reliance on illustrative examples without representative evidence or comparisons undermines acceptability.
- Suggested evaluations: use document classification with topic representations to benchmark against topic models with the same number of topics.
- Suggested evaluations: perform direct evaluation on datasets with document similarity annotations (pairwise topic-vector comparisons).
- Anonymity breach: including the GitHub link in the submission compromises double-blind review and could justify rejection.
- Concern about cherry-picking: how were examples in Figures 3–6 selected; presenting only a subset raises selection bias concerns.
- Computational feasibility: Section 2.2.1’s pairwise comparisons between topics and every vocabulary word may be prohibitive for large vocabularies/topics — is it practical?
- Tokenization clarity: Section 3.1 needs clarification on SpaCy usage — were only noun chunks extracted (as in 3.2) or other chunk types included, and how were multiword terms handled given pre-trained word2vec limits?
- OOV handling: paper lacks details on handling out-of-vocabulary terms when applying the trained model to new documents and alignment with tuned embeddings.
- Evaluation interpretation: finding 20 topics best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; an information-theoretic comparison with true labels (e.g., to compare with LDA) would be simple and informative.
- Comparison to prior work: should compare against other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Minor issues: grammar and phrasing errors (Line 315 "it's similarity" → "its similarity"; Line 361 unclear wording about "topic basis" and verb agreement).
- Minor issues: Figure 5 caption: consider replacing "words" with "terms."
- Minor issues: inconsistent reference formatting (e.g., missing conference names and inconsistent venue formatting).
