- Model is well-motivated and example outputs look promising, but the paper lacks rigorous empirical evaluation.  
- Major flaw: relies solely on illustrative examples (Figures 3–6) without quantitative comparisons, so claimed advantages are unverified.  
- Required evaluation: supervised document categorization (e.g., 20 Newsgroups) comparing topic representations with equal topic cardinality.  
- Required evaluation: direct evaluation on datasets with annotated document similarity using pairwise topic-vector comparisons.  
- Critical anonymity violation: GitHub link in main text breaches double-blind policy and alone may warrant rejection.  
- Example selection is unspecified, risking cherry-picking; authors must clarify selection criteria or include diverse examples.  
- Technical concern: computing topic word vectors by pairwise vocabulary comparisons is computationally prohibitive for large vocabularies/topics and needs justification or an alternative.  
- Tokenization ambiguity: use of SpaCy and noun chunks conflicts with word2vec’s single-word embeddings—tokenization pipeline and embedding alignment must be clarified.  
- OOV handling: training on the full corpus avoids OOV in experiments but fails for novel documents; propose aggregation or dynamic OOV strategies.  
- 20 Newsgroups baseline is unsurprising (20 topics ≈ 20 classes); suggest using topic coherence or label-based information gain and direct LDA comparisons.  
- Missing comparisons to recent neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. AAAI 2015; Nguyen et al. TACL 2015; Shamanta et al. ECML-PKDD 2015).  
- Minor issues: typos (e.g., "it's"→"its"), wording (e.g., "topic basis are"→"is"), figure caption term choice, and inconsistent reference formatting.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: conceptually promising but unsubstantiated; recommend rejection pending substantial revisions to provide rigorous evaluation and clarify technical ambiguities.
