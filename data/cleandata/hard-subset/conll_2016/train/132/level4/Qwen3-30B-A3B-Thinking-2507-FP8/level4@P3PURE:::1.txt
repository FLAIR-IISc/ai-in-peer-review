

The paper proposes combining word2vec and LDA, which holds potential interest. However, the current technical presentation is incomprehensible, preventing readers familiar with both methods from grasping the core approach. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to provide a clear high-level overview of the model combination. The current description fails to achieve this.

Specific concerns:
1.  The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. What is being approximated by "deriving a new approximation"? Why is developing prototypes time-consuming? Why is evaluating features easier?
2.  Why are identical word vectors used for both pivot and target words (unlike standard word2vec)? What is the motivation for this design choice?
3.  What does "separating words from a marginal distribution" mean?
4.  What is "co-adaptation" in this context?
5.  The phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" refers to an unspecified type of structure; clarify.
6.  "it's similarity" should be "its similarity".
7.  Footnote 1 violates anonymity.
8.  The paper lacks any formal evaluation. The practice of presenting only example clusters (as in Figure 2) is long outdated in NLP. Figure 2's caption describes a quantitative evaluation, but the description is overly lengthy and insufficiently integrated into the main text.
9.  The conclusion's claim that "the model solves word analogies" is overstated; the paper only demonstrates a few cherry-picked examples (e.g., king + queen), not a robust solution.
