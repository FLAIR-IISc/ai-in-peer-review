

The combination of word2vec and LDA presents potential interest, but the paper suffers from severe technical incomprehensibility. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to enable a reader familiar with word2vec and LDA to grasp the high-level model integration. The current presentation fails to achieve this.  

Specific concerns:  
- The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear: "requires deriving a new approximation" — approximation of which quantity? Why is prototype development time-consuming? Why is evaluating features easier?  
- Why are identical word vectors used for pivot and target (unlike standard word2vec)? What motivates this choice?  
- What does "separating words from a marginal distribution" mean?  
- What is "co-adaptation" in this context?  
- "If we only included structure up to this point" — what structure is referenced?  
- "it's similarity" → "its similarity" (typo).  
- Footnote 1 violates anonymity.  
- No rigorous evaluation is provided. It is no longer acceptable in NLP to present only example clusters; Figure 2 appears quantitative but is described only in an excessively verbose caption.  
- The conclusion's claim that "the model solves word analogies" is overstated; the paper demonstrates only a few cherry-picked examples (e.g., king + queen).  
- The Chang reference lists the venue as "Advances in..." — the correct venue (NeurIPS) should be specified, not left for guesswork.
