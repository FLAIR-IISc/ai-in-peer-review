

Summary: This paper introduces lda2vec, a model that integrates Dirichlet topic modeling with word embeddings to learn dense word vectors jointly with document-level topic mixtures. The primary contribution is the formulation of a non-negative simplex constraint that produces sparse, interpretable document representations while maintaining the ability to learn semantic relationships between words. The secondary contribution is the demonstration of this framework's applicability to two different corpora (Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments) with meaningful topic discovery.

Strengths:
1. The model effectively leverages automatic differentiation frameworks (Chainer) to simplify development and enable GPU acceleration, making it more accessible for researchers to experiment with hybrid topic modeling approaches.
2. The approach successfully demonstrates interpretable topic representations in the Twenty Newsgroups dataset, with topic coherence scores (0.55-0.57) that correlate with human evaluations of topic quality.
3. The model learns meaningful semantic relationships specific to the Hacker News corpus, as evidenced by the ability to perform domain-specific word analogies (e.g., "Silicon Valley" ≈ "California" + "technology").

Weaknesses:
1. The technical presentation is insufficiently clear, particularly in Section 2, which fails to provide a coherent explanation of how the model combines word2vec and LDA components. A reader familiar with both word2vec and LDA would struggle to grasp the model's architecture from the current description.
2. The evaluation is inadequate for a modern NLP paper. The paper presents only qualitative topic examples and lacks proper quantitative evaluation metrics beyond topic coherence scores (which are presented in a lengthy caption rather than the main text).
3. The claim that the model "solves word analogies" is overstated; the paper only demonstrates a few cherry-picked examples (like "king - man + woman = queen") rather than providing systematic evaluation of the model's ability to solve analogies.

Questions to Authors:
1. Please clarify the technical details of how the document mixture model integrates with the word embedding space, particularly why the same word vectors are used for both pivot and target words (unlike standard word2vec).
2. How does the model's performance compare to established topic modeling approaches (e.g., LDA, LDA2vec) on standard metrics beyond topic coherence?
3. Please provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the model's ability to solve word analogies rather than relying on a few example cases.
