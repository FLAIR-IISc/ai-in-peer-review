

Summary: This paper introduces lda2vec, a model that combines Dirichlet topic models with distributed word embeddings to create interpretable document representations. The authors claim to bridge the gap between the interpretability of topic models and the semantic richness of word embeddings while maintaining computational efficiency through automatic differentiation frameworks.

Main Contributions (as I see them):
1. A novel framework for jointly learning word embeddings and topic-based document representations within a single model
2. A simple implementation approach that leverages modern automatic differentiation frameworks
3. Demonstrated topic coherence in two distinct corpora (Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News)

Strengths:
1. The conceptual integration of topic modeling with word embeddings is promising and addresses a meaningful gap in the literature. The paper successfully demonstrates that document representations can be both interpretable (through sparse topic mixtures) and semantically rich (through learned word vectors).
2. The implementation approach is straightforward for practitioners familiar with deep learning frameworks, which is a significant practical contribution given the complexity of developing new probabilistic models from scratch.
3. The topic coherence results on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset (0.567 for 20 topics with β=0.75) are reasonable and comparable to established methods, suggesting the model can produce meaningful topics.

Weaknesses:
1. The technical exposition is fundamentally flawed. Section 2 is confusing and fails to explain the core model architecture in a way that would be accessible to readers familiar with both LDA and word2vec. The paper repeatedly refers to "structure" without specifying what is being referred to, and fails to clarify how the model combines these two approaches. For instance, the claim that "the same word vectors are used in both the pivot and target representations" lacks motivation, and the explanation of "separating words from a marginal distribution" is unintelligible.
2. The evaluation is insufficient and misleading. The paper presents only qualitative examples of discovered topics and cherry-picked word analogy results without proper statistical evaluation. The claim that "lda2vec solves word analogies" is overstated given that the paper only shows a few examples (e.g., "king - man + woman = queen") without demonstrating systematic performance. The topic coherence results in Figure 2 are described only in the caption, not in the main text, which is inappropriate for a research paper.
3. The introduction contains several confusing statements that undermine the paper's credibility. The claim that "crafting a new probabilistic topic model requires deriving a new approximation" is unclear and unsupported, and the justification for why this makes "prototypes time-consuming to develop" is not explained. The paper also fails to properly contextualize its contribution relative to existing work on topic modeling and word embeddings.
4. The paper contains technical inaccuracies, including the incorrect statement "it's similarity" (should be "its similarity") and the inappropriate use of a footnote that breaks anonymity.
5. The evaluation of word vector quality using the 3COSMUL measure is not properly contextualized, and the paper fails to demonstrate how the learned vectors compare to established methods like word2vec or GloVe.

Questions to Authors:
1. Please clarify the motivation for using the same word vectors for both pivot and target representations, as this differs from the standard word2vec approach and lacks justification.
2. Explain the technical meaning of "separating words from a marginal distribution" as used in Section 2.1.
3. Provide a more thorough evaluation of the model's performance, including comparison to baseline methods on standard metrics and a proper analysis of the word analogy results.
4. Justify the claim that "lda2vec solves word analogies" by presenting systematic results rather than a few cherry-picked examples.
5. Please rewrite Section 2 to clearly explain how the model combines LDA and word embeddings in a way that would be understandable to readers familiar with both paradigms.
