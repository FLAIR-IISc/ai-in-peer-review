- Introduces lda2vec, combining Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings to produce sparse, interpretable document representations.  
- Claims two contributions: a joint architecture learning word, topic, and document-topic vectors; and sparse, human-interpretable topic mixtures via a Dirichlet likelihood.  
- Strength: elegant integration of embeddings and topic modeling, leveraging additive word-vector properties to bridge dense and sparse representations.  
- Strength: straightforward implementation in automatic-differentiation frameworks; open-source code and documentation promote reproducibility.  
- Strength: qualitative results (Figures 3–6) show meaningful topics and preserved semantic regularities, including linear token relationships.  
- Weakness: completely lacks quantitative empirical evaluation and statistical comparisons (e.g., topic coherence, classification accuracy) to assess performance.  
- Weakness: submission compromises anonymity by including a GitHub link, violating conference review guidelines.  
- Weakness: unclear selection criteria for example topics in figures, raising concerns about potential cherry-picking.  
- Weakness: does not address handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms for applying the model to new documents.  
- Weakness: fails to compare against several relevant neural topic models published since 2015 (e.g., Cao et al., Nguyen et al., Shamanta et al.).  
- Question: please provide quantitative topic coherence scores for lda2vec vs. LDA and neural topic models on Twenty Newsgroups and correlation with human judgments.  
- Question: clarify preprocessing and SpaCy tokenization methodology for the Hacker News corpus.  
- Question: how will OOV terms be handled when applying the trained model to novel documents and what is the impact on representation quality?  
- Question: provide an information-theoretic comparison (e.g., mutual information with true labels) to show the choice of 20 topics for Twenty Newsgroups is not an artifact.  
- Question: how feasible is the O(V×K) pairwise computation of topic word representations for large-scale vocabularies and applications?
