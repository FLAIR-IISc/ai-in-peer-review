

The paper proposes combining word2vec and LDA, but its technical presentation is fundamentally unclear, rendering the core contribution difficult to assess. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to provide a coherent high-level explanation of the model integration for readers familiar with both techniques. The current description fails to achieve this, leaving critical design choices opaque.

Specific concerns include:
*   **Introduction (Paragraph 3):** The phrase "requires deriving a new approximation" lacks context—approximation of what? The claim that developing prototypes is "time-consuming" and that evaluating features is "easier" is unsupported and confusing. The motivation for using identical word vectors for pivot and target (contrary to standard word2vec practice) is not justified.
*   **Methodology:** Key concepts like "separating words from a marginal distribution" and "co-adaptation" are undefined and unclear. The phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" refers to unspecified structural elements, creating ambiguity.
*   **Grammar & Clarity:** "it's similarity" should read "its similarity."
*   **Evaluation:** The paper lacks any rigorous evaluation. Relying solely on illustrative clusters (as in Figure 2) is insufficient by current NLP standards. The figure caption is overly verbose and describes quantitative results, but the main text does not present or discuss these results, making the evaluation non-existent.
*   **Conclusion:** Overstates the model's capability by claiming it "solves word analogies," based only on selective, cherry-picked examples (e.g., "king + queen"). This misrepresents the limited evidence provided.
*   **References:** The Chang reference omits the venue name ("Advances in..."), requiring the reviewer to guess the publication source, which violates submission guidelines.

The paper's core idea holds potential, but its current execution is unpresentable. The technical exposition must be fundamentally restructured to clarify the model architecture, motivation for key design choices, and the actual contribution relative to existing work. Crucially, the absence of any quantitative evaluation or meaningful results renders the proposed method's utility unverifiable. The authors must either provide a rigorous evaluation or substantially revise the claims to match the evidence presented. The current draft does not meet CONLL standards for clarity, technical soundness, or empirical validation.
