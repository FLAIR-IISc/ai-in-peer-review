- Summary: Introduces lda2vec, extending skip-gram to jointly learn word vectors and Dirichlet-distributed sparse topic mixtures for interpretable document representations.  
- Contribution: Unified embedding of word, topic, and document vectors in the same space with Dirichlet constraint for sparsity.  
- Contribution: Practical implementation using automatic differentiation frameworks with GPU acceleration and open-source code.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates coherent topic discovery and semantic regularities on specialized corpora but lacks rigorous quantitative validation.  

- Strength: Produces sparse, human-readable topic mixtures using a Dirichlet constraint (α = n⁻¹), improving interpretability over dense embeddings.  
- Strength: Embeds words, topics, and documents in a single semantic space, enabling direct, intuitive topic interpretation.  
- Strength: Straightforward integration into frameworks like Chainer with available code and GPU support, lowering adoption barriers.  

- Weakness: Lacks empirical validation and statistical comparisons to baselines; claims (e.g., "20 topics work best") lack significance testing.  
- Weakness: Violates anonymity by referencing a GitHub repository, which alone may warrant rejection under CONLL rules.  
- Weakness: Tokenization and preprocessing are ambiguous—conflict between SpaCy noun-phrase extraction and pretrained word2vec usage undermines reproducibility and OOV handling.  
- Weakness: Examples appear cherry-picked (Figures 3–6) without a protocol for selection, risking overestimation of performance.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons with recent neural topic models and absence of quantitative metrics (e.g., coherence, classification accuracy) make novelty and performance unclear.  

- Question: What protocol was used to select the topics shown in Figures 3–6 to avoid cherry-picking?  
- Question: How are OOV terms in novel documents handled—are word vectors updated at inference and how does that affect comparability?  
- Question: Why was Cv topic coherence not reported for the Hacker News corpus while used for Twenty Newsgroups?  
- Question: How does lda2vec compare to LDA (same topic count) on standard metrics like perplexity and classification accuracy?  
- Question: Please clarify tokenization—does SpaCy extract only noun phrases and how does that align with pretrained word2vec embeddings?
