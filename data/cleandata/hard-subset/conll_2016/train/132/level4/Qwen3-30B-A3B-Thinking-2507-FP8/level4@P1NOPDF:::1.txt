

The combination of word2vec and LDA is promising, but the current paper fails to communicate its technical approach clearly. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to enable a reader familiar with both word2vec and LDA to grasp the high-level methodology of their integration. The present exposition does not achieve this.

Specific concerns include:  
- The third paragraph of the introduction lacks clarity. The phrase "requires deriving a new approximation" is ambiguous—approximation of what? Additionally, it is unclear why developing prototypes is time-consuming or why evaluating features would be easier.  
- The motivation for using identical word vectors for both pivot and target terms (contrary to standard word2vec practice) is not justified.  
- The phrase "separate words from a marginal distribution" requires precise definition.  
- The term "co-adaptation" is undefined and needs elaboration.  
- The statement "If we only included structure up to this point" refers to an unspecified type of structure.  
- "it's similarity" should be "its similarity."  
- Footnote 1 violates double-blind review by revealing authorship.  

Critically, the paper lacks rigorous evaluation. NLP has long moved beyond qualitative examples; presenting only illustrative clusters (e.g., Figure 2) is insufficient. While Figure 2 *appears* to show quantitative results, its description is confined to an overly verbose caption, obscuring the actual methodology and metrics. The conclusion’s claim that "the model solves word analogies" is misleading—the paper only demonstrates a few selectively chosen examples (e.g., king + queen), not a robust solution.  

Finally, the citation for Chang et al. omits the venue name ("Advances in ..."), requiring the reviewer to guess the conference or journal. This undermines academic rigor.  

In summary: the core idea merits attention, but the paper’s technical exposition is incoherent, evaluation is absent, and critical details are omitted. A thorough revision addressing these points is essential before reconsideration.
