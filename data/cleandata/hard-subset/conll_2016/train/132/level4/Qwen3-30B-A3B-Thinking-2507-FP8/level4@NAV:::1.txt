

The integration of word2vec and LDA presents a potentially valuable direction, but the manuscript's technical exposition is difficult to follow. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to provide a clear, high-level overview of the model combination for readers familiar with both techniques; the current presentation fails to achieve this. More detailed comments:  
The third paragraph of the introduction lacks clarity—the phrase "requires deriving a new approximation" is ambiguous (approximation of what?), and the rationale for why prototype development is time-consuming and why feature evaluation is simpler is unclear. The motivation for using identical word vectors for pivot and target terms (contrary to standard word2vec practice) is not justified. The phrase "separating words from a marginal distribution" is undefined. The term "co-adaptation" appears without explanation. The statement "if we only included structure up to this point" refers to unspecified structural elements. "It's similarity" should read as "its similarity." Footnote 1 violates anonymity. No rigorous evaluation is provided; the field has moved beyond merely presenting example clusters. Figure 2 appears quantitative but is described solely in an overly detailed caption. The conclusion’s claim that the model "solves word analogies" overstates the results, which are based on selectively chosen examples (e.g., "king + queen" analogies). The Chang reference omits the venue name (e.g., "Advances in..."), forcing the reviewer to infer the publication.
