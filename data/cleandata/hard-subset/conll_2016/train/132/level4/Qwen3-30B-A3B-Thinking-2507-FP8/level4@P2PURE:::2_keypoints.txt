- Proposed neural topic model extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparsifies them to mimic topic outputs; approach is compelling with promising examples.  
- Major weakness: no empirical evaluation beyond illustrative examples, so model efficacy is unestablished.  
- Suggested indirect evaluation: use document-topic vectors in a supervised classification task against a topic model with the same number of topics.  
- Suggested direct evaluation: test on datasets with document similarity annotations and compare pairwise topic vector similarity.  
- Code release in the submission breaks anonymity and may be grounds for rejection per conference policy.  
- Selection criteria for Figures 3–6 are unclear, raising risk of cherry-picked example topics.  
- Section 2.2.1’s proposal to compute topic word representations via pairwise vocabulary comparisons is computationally infeasible at realistic vocab and topic sizes.  
- Tokenization method (Section 3.1) using SpaCy is unspecified (e.g., noun chunks vs. others), yet multiword terms matter given pretrained word2vec excludes many MWEs.  
- Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is unaddressed; full-corpus training hides deployment issues where OOVs require embedding updates and comparability problems.  
- The finding that 20 topics fits 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; a direct information-theoretic comparison to true labels (and to LDA) is needed.  
- Missing comparisons to other neural topic models (Cao et al. AAAI 2015; Nguyen et al. TACL 2015; Shamanta et al. ECML/PKDD 2015).  
- Low-level issues: typos ("it's similarity" → "its similarity"), awkward phrasing ("topic basis are" → "is"), figure caption wording ("words"→"terms"), and inconsistent reference formatting.
