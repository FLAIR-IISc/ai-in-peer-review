Summary: Introduces lda2vec, combining Dirichlet topic modeling with word embeddings via a non-negative simplex constraint to produce sparse, interpretable document representations and demonstrates it on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.

Strength 1: Leverages automatic differentiation (Chainer) and GPU acceleration, easing development and experimentation.

Strength 2: Produces interpretable topics on Twenty Newsgroups with topic coherence scores ~0.55–0.57 that align with human judgments.

Strength 3: Learns meaningful, corpus-specific semantic relationships on Hacker News, demonstrated by domain-specific analogies.

Weakness 1: Technical presentation is unclear—Section 2 does not coherently explain how word2vec and LDA components are combined.

Weakness 2: Evaluation is inadequate—relies on qualitative examples and coherence scores in a caption rather than comprehensive quantitative metrics.

Weakness 3: Claim of "solving word analogies" is overstated, supported only by a few cherry-picked examples rather than systematic evaluation.

Question 1: Please clarify how the document mixture model integrates with the word embedding space and why the same vectors are used for pivot and target words.

Question 2: How does the model compare to established topic models (e.g., LDA) on standard metrics beyond topic coherence?

Question 3: Please provide a comprehensive, systematic evaluation of the model's ability to solve word analogies.
