- Introduces a conceptually interesting neural topic model that extends word2vec to learn sparsified document embeddings resembling traditional topics.  
- Promising qualitative examples shown, but lack of rigorous empirical evaluation undermines the contribution.  
- Core weakness: no quantitative evaluation or statistical evidence of representativeness for the qualitative examples.  
- Missing direct comparisons to standard and neural topic models (e.g., LDA; Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).  
- Suggested evaluations: supervised categorization using document representations, datasets with document similarity annotations, or information-theoretic measures (e.g., mutual information with 20 Newsgroups).  
- Unclear example selection criteria for Figures 3–6, raising risk of cherry-picking.  
- Computational feasibility concern: computing topic embeddings via pairwise vocabulary comparisons is likely intractable for typical vocabularies.  
- Tokenization ambiguity: SpaCy usage and whether multiword expressions or noun chunks are included is unspecified.  
- OOV handling is not described; real-world deployment would require clear strategy for updating or comparing embeddings.  
- Anonymity violation: included GitHub link compromises double-blind review (formal grounds for rejection).  
- Minor issues: typos/phrasing errors, inconsistent reference formatting, and terminology (e.g., "words" vs. "terms").
