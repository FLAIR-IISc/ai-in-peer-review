- Proposes a compelling neural topic model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparsify them to mimic topic outputs; examples are promising.  
- Major shortcoming: lacks empirical evaluation beyond illustrative examples, with no indication of example representativeness.  
- No direct comparisons to standard or neural topic models, leaving the model's true value unclear.  
- Suggested evaluations: use document-topic representations in supervised categorization (compare against topic model with same topic cardinality).  
- Suggested evaluations: perform direct evaluation on datasets with annotated document similarity (pairwise topic vector comparisons).  
- Code release is commendable but the GitHub link in the anonymized submission compromises anonymity (possible grounds for rejection).  
- Figures 3–6 show selected examples but selection method is unspecified, raising concerns about selective presentation.  
- Section 2.2.1’s pairwise vocabulary comparison for topic word representations appears computationally infeasible for typical vocabularies and topic counts.  
- Section 3.1 tokenization is unclear (SpaCy usage): are noun chunks extracted or other chunk types? clarify given multiword term coverage issues.  
- Out-of-vocabulary handling is unaddressed; applying the model to new documents would require updating word2vec, making embeddings incomparable.  
- The 20-topic result on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; an information-theoretic comparison against true labels (allowing direct LDA comparison) would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Authors should compare against key neural topic models (Cao et al. AAAI 2015; Nguyen et al. TACL 2015; Shamanta et al. ECML/PKDD 2015).  
- Low-level issues: typos and grammar (Line 315 "it's"→"its"; Line 361 subject-verb), Figure 5 caption "words"→"terms", and inconsistent/missing reference formatting.
