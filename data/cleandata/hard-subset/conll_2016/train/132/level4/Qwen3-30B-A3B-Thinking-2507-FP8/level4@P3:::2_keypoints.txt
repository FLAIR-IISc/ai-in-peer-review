- Paper introduces lda2vec, a hybrid model combining Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings to produce interpretable document representations.  
- Contributions: joint learning of dense word vectors and sparse document-topic mixtures in one framework.  
- Contributions: practical implementation using automatic differentiation frameworks (e.g., Chainer) to simplify development and optimization.  
- Contributions: empirical results showing discovery of coherent topics and domain-specific semantic relationships in word vectors.  

- Strength: Conceptual innovation bridging topic modeling and distributed word representations via a non-negative simplex constraint for interpretability.  
- Strength: Elegant extension of skip-gram to incorporate document-level context.  
- Strength: Practical, concise implementation that leverages autodiff/GPU and open-sourced code.  
- Strength: Empirical demonstration of strong topic coherence (e.g., 0.567 for 20 topics) and qualitative examples of meaningful topics.  

- Weakness: Lacks rigorous empirical comparisons to baselines (e.g., LDA, neural topic models) and downstream task evaluations like document classification.  
- Weakness: Explicit GitHub link violates conference anonymity and is a serious procedural flaw.  
- Weakness: Insufficient methodological detail on selection of top tokens, tokenization of multi-word phrases, and handling of OOV terms, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Omits comparisons with recent neural topic models (e.g., Nguyen et al., 2015; Shamanta et al., 2015) that are directly relevant.  
- Weakness: Overstates topic-quality claims (e.g., 20-topic coherence on 20 Newsgroups may be unremarkable without proper baselines).  

- Question: Clarify how example topics in Figures 3–6 were selected (cherry-picked or random sample?).  
- Question: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms during inference on new documents?  
- Question: Provide comparisons of lda2vec’s topic coherence and document classification performance against LDA and a neural topic model on Twenty Newsgroups.  
- Question: Specify exact tokenization procedure for the Hacker News corpus, including treatment of multi-word terms and alignment with tools like Palmetto.
