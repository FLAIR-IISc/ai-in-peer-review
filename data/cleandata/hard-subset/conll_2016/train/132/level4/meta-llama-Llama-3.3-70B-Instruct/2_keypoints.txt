- Proposes a neural-styled topic model using a word2vec objective to learn document embeddings refined by sparsification.  
- Paper is intriguing and examples look promising but lacks any empirical evaluation.  
- Evaluation consists only of example tables with no indication of representativeness or baseline comparisons.  
- Recommend empirical tests: use topic representations in supervised document classification or datasets with document similarity annotations.  
- Without empirical evaluation, the model’s value is hard to assess and the paper is difficult to accept.  
- Authors broke anonymity by including a GitHub link in the submission.  
- Example selection in Figures 3–6 is unclear, risking cherry-picking.  
- Section 2.2.1’s pairwise word-vocabulary comparisons to compute topic word representations may be computationally expensive for realistic vocabularies and topic counts.  
- Use of SpaCy for tokenization is underspecified, especially regarding interaction with pre-trained word2vec embeddings.  
- Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is not described and may cause problems on novel documents.  
- The claim that 20 topics work best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; a simple information-theoretic comparison with true labels could compare to LDA.  
- Authors should compare against other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).  
- Minor issues: fix "it's" → "its" (Line 315); clarify "topic basis" and awkward sentence (Line 361); Figure 5 caption should say "terms" not "words"; standardize reference formatting.
