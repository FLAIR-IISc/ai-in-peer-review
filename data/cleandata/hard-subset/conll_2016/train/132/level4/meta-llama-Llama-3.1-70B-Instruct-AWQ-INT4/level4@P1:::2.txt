This paper proposes a neural-styled topic model, extending the objective of word2vec to also learn document embeddings, which it then constrains through sparsification, hence mimicking the output of a topic model.

**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions:**

The authors present a novel model, lda2vec, which combines the strengths of word embeddings and topic models. The model extends the word2vec objective to learn document embeddings, which are then constrained to produce sparse and interpretable topic representations. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A new model that jointly learns word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space.
2. A sparsification technique that encourages the document vectors to be sparse and interpretable, similar to traditional topic models.
3. An evaluation of the model on two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments, demonstrating its ability to discover salient topics and learn semantic relationships between words.

**Strengths:**

1. The model is well-motivated and combines the strengths of word embeddings and topic models in a novel way.
2. The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the model and its components.
3. The evaluation on two datasets demonstrates the model's ability to discover salient topics and learn semantic relationships between words.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The paper lacks empirical evaluation of the model, relying on tables of examples rather than quantitative metrics.
2. The authors do not compare their model to other neural topic models or traditional topic models.
3. The paper has several minor issues, such as inconsistent reference formatting and unclear explanations of certain techniques.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. How did you select the examples in Figures 3-6? Could you provide more information on the process of selecting these examples?
2. How does your model deal with out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms? Could you provide more information on how the model handles OOV terms in novel documents?
3. Could you provide more information on the sparsification technique used in the model? How does it encourage the document vectors to be sparse and interpretable?
4. How do you plan to evaluate the model in future work? Could you provide more information on potential evaluation metrics or datasets?

**Additional Comments:**

* The authors should consider adding more empirical evaluation of the model, such as quantitative metrics or comparisons to other models.
* The paper could benefit from more minor revisions, such as consistent reference formatting and clearer explanations of certain techniques.
* The authors should consider adding more information on the model's limitations and potential future work.
