- Proposes a neural-styled topic model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings constrained via sparsification to mimic topic models.  
- Presented examples are promising but lack evidence of representativeness.  
- Major omission: no empirical evaluation or quantitative comparison with standard or neural topic models.  
- Suggested evaluation: use topic representations in supervised document categorization to compare against topic models with same topic cardinality.  
- Alternative evaluation suggestion: test on datasets with document similarity annotations using pairwise comparisons over topic vectors.  
- Releasing the GitHub link in the submission broke anonymity and is a serious submission violation.  
- Unclear how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected, raising concern about cherry-picking.  
- Calculating topic word representations via pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary appears computationally expensive/scalability concern.  
- Tokenization details using SpaCy are unclear, especially given use of pre-trained word2vec embeddings.  
- Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is not addressed, which could hurt generalization to new documents.  
- Reporting 20 topics as best for 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; a simple information-theoretic evaluation against true labels could have compared to LDA.  
- Authors should compare against other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).  
- Minor issues: Line 315 "it's similarity" → "its similarity."  
- Minor issues: Line 361 wording "topic basis" unclear and sentence awkward.  
- Minor issues: Figure 5 caption should use "terms" instead of "words."  
- Minor issues: Reference formatting inconsistent with errors and missing information.
