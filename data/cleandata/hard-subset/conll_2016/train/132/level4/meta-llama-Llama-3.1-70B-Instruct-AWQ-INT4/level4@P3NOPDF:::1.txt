The integration of word2vec and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is a promising approach that warrants further exploration. However, the current paper falls short in its technical presentation, making it challenging for readers to grasp the core concept. Specifically, Section 2 requires a thorough revision to provide a clear, high-level overview of how the two models are combined, assuming a basic familiarity with word2vec and LDA.

Several aspects of the paper necessitate clarification and improvement. The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear, particularly the statement "requires deriving a new approximation." It is essential to specify what is being approximated and why developing prototypes is time-consuming. Furthermore, the evaluation of features is mentioned as being easier, but the reasoning behind this claim is not provided.

The decision to use the same word vectors for pivot and target languages, unlike in traditional word2vec, lacks motivation. Additionally, the concept of separating words from a marginal distribution is not adequately explained. The term "co-adaptation" is also unclear and requires definition.

Minor errors and inconsistencies were found throughout the paper. For instance, the phrase "it's similarity" should be replaced with "its similarity." Moreover, Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission.

A notable omission in the paper is a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed model. In the current state of natural language processing (NLP), it is no longer acceptable to rely solely on example clusters or cherry-picked instances. While Figure 2 appears to present a quantitative evaluation, the description is confined to the overly lengthy caption, which is insufficient.

The conclusion contains an exaggerated claim regarding the model's ability to solve word analogies. The provided examples, such as "king + queen," are limited and do not substantiate the assertion. A more thorough evaluation is necessary to support such claims.

Lastly, the reference to Chang's work is incomplete, as the conference or journal name is truncated to "Advances in...". Providing the full venue name is essential for accurate citation and further research.

In summary, while the idea of combining word2vec and LDA is intriguing, the paper's technical presentation and evaluation require significant improvement to effectively communicate the proposed approach and its potential benefits.
