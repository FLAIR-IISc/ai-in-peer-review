This submission proposes a combination of word2vec and LDA, which could be a potentially interesting approach. However, the technical details presented in the paper are unclear and require a complete rewrite to facilitate understanding. Specifically, Section 2 needs to be rewritten to provide a high-level picture of how the models are being combined, making it accessible to readers familiar with word2vec and LDA.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. The development of a hybrid model that combines word2vec and LDA, allowing for the joint learning of word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space.
2. The use of a Dirichlet likelihood term to encourage sparse document weights, leading to more interpretable document-to-topic proportions.
3. The demonstration of the model's ability to learn linear relationships between words and solve word analogies in a specialized vocabulary.

Strengths:

1. The proposed model has the potential to leverage the strengths of both word2vec and LDA, allowing for the capture of semantic regularities between words while still yielding sparse and interpretable document-to-topic proportions.
2. The use of a Dirichlet likelihood term is a novel approach to encouraging sparse document weights, which can lead to more interpretable results.
3. The model's ability to learn linear relationships between words and solve word analogies in a specialized vocabulary is a promising result.

Weaknesses:

1. The technical details presented in the paper are unclear and require significant rewriting to facilitate understanding.
2. The evaluation of the model is limited, with no quantitative evaluation of the model's performance on a standard benchmark.
3. The statement in the conclusion that the model solves word analogies is overstated, as only a few cherry-picked examples are presented.

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide a clearer explanation of how the models are being combined, and what are the key differences between the proposed approach and existing methods?
2. How do you plan to evaluate the model's performance on a standard benchmark, and what metrics will you use to assess its performance?
3. Can you provide more details on the Dirichlet likelihood term and how it encourages sparse document weights?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of co-adaptation in the model, and what are the implications of using the same word vectors for pivot and target?
5. Can you provide more examples of the model's ability to learn linear relationships between words and solve word analogies in a specialized vocabulary?
