- Proposed neural-styled topic model is intriguing and example outputs look promising.
- Paper lacks thorough empirical evaluation and relies only on unrepresentative example tables.
- No quantitative comparisons to standard or neural topic models, making assessment of merit difficult.
- Recommend evaluating document topic representations via supervised categorization or pairwise document-similarity annotations.
- Releasing code is good, but including the GitHub link breaks anonymity and is a serious concern.
- Selection process for example figures (Figures 3–6) is unclear, raising cherry-picking concerns.
- Computing word representations for topics via pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary seems computationally expensive and potentially infeasible.
- Tokenization method using SpaCy is unspecified and its relation to noun-chunk extraction and multi-word terms is unclear.
- The paper does not address handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms and incompatibility between updated and non-updated embeddings.
- Reporting that 20 topics worked best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; no information-theoretic comparison to true labels or to LDA is provided.
- No comparisons made to other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Several low-level issues: grammar errors ("it's" → "its"), unclear phrasing ("the topic basis is affected"), and awkward word usage.
- Figure caption wording should use "terms" instead of "words" for accuracy.
- References and citation formatting are inconsistent and missing venue information for some works.
