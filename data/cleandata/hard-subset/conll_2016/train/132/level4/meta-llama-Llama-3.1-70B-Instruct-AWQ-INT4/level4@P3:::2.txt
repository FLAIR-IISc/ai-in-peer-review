**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural-styled topic model, lda2vec, which extends the objective of word2vec to learn document embeddings. The model constrains the document embeddings through sparsification, mimicking the output of a traditional topic model. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on two datasets: Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Extension of word2vec to learn document embeddings**: The authors propose a novel approach to learning document embeddings by extending the word2vec objective.
2. **Sparsification of document embeddings**: The authors introduce a sparsification technique to constrain the document embeddings, making them more interpretable and similar to traditional topic models.
3. **Joint training of word, topic, and document vectors**: The authors demonstrate that their model can jointly train word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space.

**Strengths of the Submission**

1. **Novel approach to learning document embeddings**: The authors propose a unique approach to learning document embeddings by extending the word2vec objective.
2. **Effective use of sparsification**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their sparsification technique in making the document embeddings more interpretable.
3. **Joint training of multiple vectors**: The authors show that their model can jointly train word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

1. **Lack of empirical evaluation**: The authors do not provide a comprehensive empirical evaluation of their model, relying on examples and tables instead of quantitative comparisons with other models.
2. **Limited comparison with other models**: The authors do not compare their model with other neural topic models or traditional topic models, making it difficult to assess its true worth.
3. **Methodological concerns**: The authors do not provide sufficient details on how they selected the examples in Figures 3-6, and their approach to handling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is unclear.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How did you select the examples in Figures 3-6, and can you provide more information on the methodology used to choose these examples?
2. Can you provide more details on how your model handles OOV terms, and how you plan to address this issue in future work?
3. Can you compare your model with other neural topic models, such as those proposed in Cao et al. (2015), Nguyen et al. (2015), and Shamanta et al. (2015)?
4. Can you provide more information on the topic basis and how it is affected by the sparsification technique?
5. Can you clarify the reference formatting and provide a consistent format for all references?

**Additional Comments**

* The authors should consider providing more details on the methodology used to select the examples in Figures 3-6 to avoid the perception of cherry-picking.
* The authors should clarify their approach to handling OOV terms and provide more information on how they plan to address this issue in future work.
* The authors should consider comparing their model with other neural topic models and traditional topic models to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their approach.
