- Proposes a neural-styled topic model that extends word2vec to jointly learn document embeddings constrained via sparsification to mimic topic-model outputs.
- Model aims to combine word embedding semantic regularities with interpretable, sparse document/topic representations.
- Paper is well-motivated and presents promising illustrative topic examples on 20 Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.
- Lacks empirical evaluation and quantitative comparison to standard or neural topic models.
- Relies heavily on example tables without indicating representativeness, risking cherry-picking.
- No supervised document categorization or document-similarity evaluation provided to validate utility.
- Computational feasibility of computing topic word representations via pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary is unclear for realistic vocab sizes and topic counts.
- Method for token identification via spaCy (Section 3.1) is insufficiently described.
- Paper does not address handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms for novel documents.
- Claim that 20 topics works best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising and lacks rigorous, information-theoretic comparison to true labels.
- Paper should compare against other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Authors compromised review anonymity by including a GitHub link in the submission.
- Several low-level issues: grammatical errors, inconsistent reference formatting, and unclear figure/table captions.
- Main contributions: extending word2vec to learn document embeddings, sparsifying document embeddings to mimic topic models, and example topics on two corpora.
- Strengths: combines embeddings and topic modeling strengths, promising examples, and good motivation.
- Weaknesses: lack of quantitative evaluation, dependence on examples, missing OOV treatment, and anonymity breach.
- Questions for authors: how were Figures 3–6 examples selected; how will OOVs be handled; can you provide supervised or similarity-based evaluations; how will you compare to prior neural topic models?
