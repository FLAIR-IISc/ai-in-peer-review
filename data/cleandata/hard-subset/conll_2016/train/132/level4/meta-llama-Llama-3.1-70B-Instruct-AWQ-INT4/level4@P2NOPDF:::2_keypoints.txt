- Proposes a neural-styled topic model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings constrained via sparsification to mimic topic-model outputs.
- Model is innovative and provided examples are promising.
- Paper lacks empirical evaluation and comparison to standard or neural topic models, relying only on example tables.
- This omission makes it difficult to assess the model's value and to accept the paper.
- Suggested evaluation: supervised document categorization using topic representations vs. a topic model with same topic count.
- Suggested evaluation: direct assessment on datasets with document similarity annotations using pairwise comparisons of topic vectors.
- Code release is commendable, but including a GitHub link breaks anonymity and must be removed for blind review.
- Unclear how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected; risk of cherry-picking topics.
- Topic-word calculation via pairwise comparison with the entire vocabulary (Section 2.2.1) seems computationally expensive; feasibility needs clarification.
- Use of SpaCy for token identification (Section 3.1) is insufficiently explained (which chunk types are used, how multiword terms handled).
- Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is not addressed; tuned embeddings vs. non-updated OOV embeddings may be incomparable when applied to new documents.
- Reporting that 20 topics worked best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; a simple information-theoretic comparison against true labels (and LDA) would be appropriate.
- Authors should compare against prior neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Low-level issues: Line 315 grammar ("it's" → "its"), Line 361 unclear phrasing, Figure 5 caption should use "terms" not "words", and references have inconsistent/missing conference formatting.
