A combination of word2vec and LDA is a potentially interesting approach. However, the primary issue with the current paper is that the technical details are unclear, making it difficult to understand the methodology. Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to provide a high-level overview of how the models are combined, allowing readers familiar with word2vec and LDA to grasp the concept easily. The current presentation falls short of achieving this goal.

More detailed comments:

The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear, specifically the statement "requires deriving a new approximation." It is uncertain what this approximation refers to and why developing prototypes is time-consuming. Additionally, it is not explained why evaluating features is easier. The decision to use the same word vectors for pivot and target languages, unlike in traditional word2vec, lacks motivation. The concept of separating words from a marginal distribution is also unclear, as is the notion of co-adaptation. The phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" is ambiguous, as it does not specify what kind of structure is being referred to. Furthermore, there is a grammatical error in the text ("it's" instead of "its").

Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission. Moreover, the paper lacks a thorough evaluation, relying instead on example clusters, which is no longer acceptable in the field of NLP. Figure 2 appears to present a quantitative evaluation, but the description is confined to an overly long caption. The conclusion's claim that the model solves word analogies is an overstatement, as the paper only presents a few cherry-picked examples of analogies such as "king + queen." The reference to Chang is incomplete, as the conference/journal name is truncated to "Advances in ...," making it difficult to identify the venue.
