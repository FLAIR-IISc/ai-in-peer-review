- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid of word2vec and LDA that jointly learns dense word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document-topic weights via an extended SGNS objective, evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.

- Contribution: Hybrid model combining word2vec and LDA to produce joint dense word vectors and sparse document representations.

- Contribution: Extension of the SGNS objective to incorporate document-wide feature vectors that learn continuous document weights onto topic vectors.

- Contribution: Empirical evaluation on a large Hacker News corpus (and 20NG) showing topic discovery and linear word relationships.

- Strength: Produces sparse, interpretable document representations compared to traditional dense document vectors.

- Strength: Enables joint learning of word and document vectors, facilitating discovery of salient topics and linear word relations.

- Strength: Demonstrates scalability to large corpora.

- Weakness: Technical details are insufficiently clear, making the model components and their integration hard to follow.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited—relies on a few example clusters and a single quantitative dataset, which may be inadequate.

- Weakness: Some claims (e.g., solving word analogies) appear overstated and supported by cherry-picked examples.

- Question: Please clarify the SGNS extension and the exact mechanism for joint learning of word and document vectors.

- Question: How will you address co-adaptation, and what motivates using the same word vectors for pivot and target?

- Question: Can you provide a more comprehensive evaluation with quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art models?

- Question: How do you plan to resolve the anonymity issue mentioned in Footnote 1?
