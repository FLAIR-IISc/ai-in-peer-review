- Paper proposes lda2vec: extends word2vec to learn document embeddings with sparsification; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.
- Contribution: extends the word2vec objective to produce document embeddings.
- Contribution: introduces sparsification of document embeddings for interpretability akin to topic models.
- Contribution: jointly trains word, topic, and document vectors in a shared representation space.
- Strength: novel approach to learning document embeddings by extending word2vec.
- Strength: sparsification improves interpretability of document embeddings.
- Strength: demonstrates joint training of multiple vector types in one space.
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive empirical evaluation and quantitative comparisons with baselines.
- Weakness: limited comparisons with other neural and traditional topic models.
- Weakness: methodological gaps—insufficient detail on example selection (Figures 3–6) and unclear OOV handling.
- Question: How were the examples in Figures 3–6 selected; please detail the selection methodology?
- Question: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms and how will this be addressed in future work?
- Question: Can you compare lda2vec with other neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015)?
- Question: Provide more information on the topic basis and how sparsification affects it.
- Question: Please clarify and standardize reference formatting.
- Additional comment: Provide more methodological detail to avoid the appearance of cherry-picking examples.
- Additional comment: Clarify OOV handling and planned remedies in future work.
- Additional comment: Include comparisons with other neural and traditional topic models for a more comprehensive evaluation.
