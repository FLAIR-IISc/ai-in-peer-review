- Proposes a neural-styled topic model extending the word2vec objective to learn document embeddings that are sparsified to mimic traditional topic models.
- Model appears promising with illustrative examples showing potential.
- Major limitation: no empirical evaluation — results are only example tables with no representativeness or baseline comparisons.
- Suggested evaluations: supervised document categorization comparing topic representations at equal topic cardinality, or direct evaluation on datasets with pairwise document similarity annotations.
- Anonymity breach: GitHub link included in submission, compromising double-blind review.
- Unclear selection process for examples in Figures 3–6, raising risk of cherry-picking.
- Computational feasibility concern: deriving word representations for topics via pairwise comparisons with entire vocabulary may be prohibitively costly.
- Processing/tokenization unclear: how SpaCy, noun-chunk extraction, and pretrained word2vec embeddings are used is not specified.
- OOV handling is not described, risking failures on novel documents.
- Reporting that 20 topics worked best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; a simple information-theoretic comparison to true labels would strengthen evaluation.
- Missing comparisons to other neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).
- Minor issues: typo "it's"→"its" (Line 315); unclear phrase "topic basis" and awkward "are" (Line 361); use "terms" instead of "words" in Figure 5 caption; inconsistent reference formatting.
