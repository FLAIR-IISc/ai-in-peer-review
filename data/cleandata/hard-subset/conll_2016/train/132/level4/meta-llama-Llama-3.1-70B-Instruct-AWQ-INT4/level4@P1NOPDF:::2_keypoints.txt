1. Proposes a neural-styled topic model that extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and enforces sparsity to mimic traditional topic model outputs.

2. Model is promising and examples are compelling, but the paper lacks any empirical evaluation beyond selected example tables.

3. Reliance on examples without measures of representativeness or direct comparisons makes it impossible to assess the model's true quality.

4. Recommend evaluations: use topic representations in supervised document classification to compare against topic models with the same topic count.

5. Recommend evaluations: perform direct evaluation on datasets with document similarity annotations using pairwise comparisons over topic vectors.

6. Public GitHub link in submission breaks anonymity, which is a serious submission rule violation and could justify rejection.

7. Selection process for example topics (Figures 3–6) is unclear and risks perceived cherry-picking.

8. Section 2.2.1’s proposed word-by-word pairwise computation for topic word representations would be extremely expensive; feasibility is unclear.

9. Use of SpaCy for token extraction (Section 3.1) is underspecified — unclear whether noun chunks or other methods are used and how multiword terms are handled.

10. Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is not explained; this will cause problems when applying the model to new documents due to embedding updates.

11. The finding that 20 topics works best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; suggest information-theoretic comparison to true labels (e.g., vs. LDA) as a simple quantitative check.

12. Authors should compare against existing neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).

13. Minor issues: "it's similarity" → "its similarity"; unclear phrasing at Line 361; Figure 5 caption should use "terms" not "words"; references formatting and missing conference names need correction.
