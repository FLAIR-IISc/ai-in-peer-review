**Summary of the Paper:**

The paper proposes a new model, lda2vec, which combines word2vec and LDA to build unsupervised document representations. The model extends the Skipgram Negative-Sampling (SGNS) objective to utilize document-wide feature vectors while simultaneously learning continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors. The authors evaluate the model on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset and a large corpus of Hacker News comments.

**Main Contributions:**

1. The combination of word2vec and LDA to build unsupervised document representations.
2. The extension of the SGNS objective to utilize document-wide feature vectors.
3. The evaluation of the model on two large datasets.

**Strengths:**

1. The model combines the strengths of word2vec and LDA to build unsupervised document representations.
2. The extension of the SGNS objective is a novel contribution.
3. The evaluation on two large datasets provides a comprehensive understanding of the model's performance.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The technical details are incomprehensible, making it difficult to understand the model.
2. The evaluation is limited to example clusters and a few cherry-picked examples of word analogies.
3. The paper lacks a clear explanation of the motivation behind using the same word vectors for pivot and target.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a clear explanation of the motivation behind using the same word vectors for pivot and target?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of co-adaptation in the model?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance on the two datasets?
4. How do you plan to extend the model to other tasks, such as information retrieval and parsing?
