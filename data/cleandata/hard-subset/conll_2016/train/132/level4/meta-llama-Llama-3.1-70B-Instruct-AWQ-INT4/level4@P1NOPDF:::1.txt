A combination of word2vec and LDA could be a potentially interesting approach. However, the main problem with the current paper is that the technical details are not clearly presented. Section 2, in particular, requires a complete rewrite to provide a high-level overview of how the models are being combined, making it accessible to readers familiar with word2vec and LDA. The current presentation falls short in achieving this goal.

More detailed comments:

The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear. The statement "requires deriving a new approximation" lacks context - what exactly is being approximated? Additionally, it is not evident why developing prototypes is time-consuming or why evaluating features is easier. Furthermore, the decision to use the same word vectors for pivot and target languages (unlike in traditional word2vec) is not adequately motivated.

The concept of separating words from a marginal distribution is also unclear. Moreover, the term "co-adaptation" is not explicitly defined. The phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" is ambiguous - what kind of structure is being referred to? A minor point, but "it's similarity" should be corrected to "its similarity".

Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission.

A notable omission in the paper is a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach. The practice of presenting only example clusters without quantitative evaluation is no longer acceptable in NLP. Figure 2 appears to be a quantitative evaluation, but the description is confined to the overly long caption. The conclusion's claim that the model solves word analogies is an overstatement, as the paper only presents a few cherry-picked examples (e.g., king + queen).

Lastly, the Chang reference is incomplete, with the conference/journal name listed as "Advances in ...". The full venue name should be provided to facilitate accurate citation.
