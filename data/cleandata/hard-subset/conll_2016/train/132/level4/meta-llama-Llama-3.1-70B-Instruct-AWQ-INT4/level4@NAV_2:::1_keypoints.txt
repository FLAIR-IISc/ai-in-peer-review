- Overall: Approach (word2vec + LDA) is potentially interesting but technical details are unclear and Section 2 needs a rewrite to explain the model combination to readers familiar with word2vec and LDA.

Contributions:
- Hybrid model that jointly learns word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space.
- Introduction of a Dirichlet likelihood term to encourage sparse, more interpretable document-to-topic weights.
- Demonstration that the model can learn linear word relationships and solve word analogies in a specialized vocabulary.

Strengths:
- Leverages strengths of word2vec (semantic regularities) while producing sparse, interpretable document-topic proportions via LDA.
- Novel use of a Dirichlet likelihood term to induce sparsity in document weights.
- Promising ability to capture linear analogies in a domain-specific vocabulary.

Weaknesses:
- Technical presentation is unclear and requires significant rewriting for comprehension.
- Evaluation is limited; no quantitative benchmarking on standard datasets.
- Claim that the model “solves” word analogies is overstated and based on a few cherry-picked examples.

Questions to authors:
- Please clarify how the models are combined and how the approach differs from existing methods.
- How will you evaluate the model on standard benchmarks and which metrics will you use?
- Provide more details on the Dirichlet likelihood term and how it enforces sparse document weights.
- How will you address co-adaptation and the implications of using the same word vectors for pivot and target?
- Can you supply more examples demonstrating the model’s learned linear relationships and analogy solving?
