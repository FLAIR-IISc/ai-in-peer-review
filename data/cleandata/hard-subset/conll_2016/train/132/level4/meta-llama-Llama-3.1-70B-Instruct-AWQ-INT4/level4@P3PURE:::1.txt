A combination of word2vec and LDA could be potentially interesting, but the current paper falls short in its technical presentation. The main issue lies in the lack of clarity in the technical details, making it challenging for readers familiar with word2vec and LDA to grasp the high-level concept of how the models are being combined. To address this, Section 2 requires a complete rewrite to provide a clear and concise explanation.

More detailed comments:

The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear, particularly the statement "requires deriving a new approximation." It is essential to specify what is being approximated and why developing prototypes is time-consuming. Furthermore, it is unclear why evaluating features is easier. Additionally, the decision to use the same word vectors for pivot and target languages, unlike in word2vec, needs to be motivated.

The concept of separating words from a marginal distribution is not well-explained and requires further clarification. The term "co-adaptation" is also not defined, making it difficult to understand its relevance to the proposed model. In Section 2, the phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" is ambiguous, and it is unclear what kind of structure is being referred to.

There are also several minor errors, including a typo in the phrase "it's similarity," which should be "its similarity." Moreover, Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission.

A major concern is the lack of evaluation in the paper. In the field of NLP, it is no longer acceptable to present only example clusters without any quantitative evaluation. While Figure 2 appears to provide some quantitative results, the description is buried in an overly long caption. Furthermore, the conclusion overstates the model's capabilities, claiming that it solves word analogies, when in fact, only a few cherry-picked examples are presented.

Finally, the reference to Chang's work is incomplete, as it lacks the conference or journal name, making it difficult to identify the venue.
