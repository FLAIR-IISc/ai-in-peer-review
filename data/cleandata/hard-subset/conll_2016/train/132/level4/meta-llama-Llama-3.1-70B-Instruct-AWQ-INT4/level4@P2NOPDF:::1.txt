A combination of word2vec and LDA has the potential to be an interesting and innovative approach. However, the current paper falls short in its technical presentation, making it challenging for readers to comprehend the proposed model. Specifically, Section 2 requires a thorough rewrite to provide a clear, high-level overview of how the authors combine word2vec and LDA, allowing readers familiar with these concepts to easily grasp the underlying idea.

Several aspects of the paper need clarification. The third paragraph of the introduction is unclear, particularly the statement "requires deriving a new approximation." It would be beneficial to specify what is being approximated and why developing prototypes is time-consuming. Additionally, the authors should explain why evaluating features is easier and provide a clear motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target languages, unlike in traditional word2vec.

The authors should also define what it means to separate words from a marginal distribution and explain the concept of co-adaptation. Furthermore, the phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" lacks context, and it is unclear what kind of structure is being referred to. A minor correction is also needed: "it's similarity" should be replaced with "its similarity."

Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission, which should be revised to maintain the author's confidentiality. Moreover, the paper lacks a thorough evaluation of the proposed model. While example clusters may have been sufficient in the past, the field of NLP has moved beyond this approach. Figure 2 appears to present some quantitative evaluation, but the description is buried in an overly long caption.

The conclusion overstates the model's capabilities, claiming that it solves word analogies. However, the provided examples are limited to a few cherry-picked instances, such as "king + queen." The authors should be more precise in their claims and provide a more comprehensive evaluation to support their assertions.

Lastly, the reference to Chang's work is incomplete, as the conference or journal name is truncated to "Advances in...". The authors should provide the full venue name to facilitate accurate citation and referencing.
