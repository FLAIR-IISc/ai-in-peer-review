- Proposes a neural-styled topic model that learns document embeddings via a word2vec-like objective and sparsifies them to mimic topic outputs.  
- Examples shown are promising but the paper lacks any empirical evaluation or quantitative comparisons to assess representativeness.  
- Without empirical evaluation, the paper’s worth is unclear and is hard to accept.  
- Suggested evaluations: use topic representations in supervised document classification against a topic model with same topic count.  
- Alternative evaluation suggestion: use datasets with document similarity annotations to directly compare topic-vector similarities.  
- Authors broke anonymity by including a GitHub link in the submitted version; this must be removed.  
- Selection process for Figures 3–6 is unclear and may indicate cherry-picking; explain how examples were chosen.  
- Section 2.2.1’s pairwise topic-word comparison approach appears computationally infeasible for realistic vocabularies and topic counts.  
- SpaCy tokenization details (how noun chunks and other chunk types are extracted) are not specified and need clarification.  
- Handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms is not addressed; applying the model to new documents could produce non-comparable embeddings.  
- The claim that 20 topics worked best on 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; an information-theoretic comparison against true labels (and LDA) would be more informative.  
- Compare the model to existing neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).  
- Minor editorial issues: fix "it's" → "its" (Line 315), clarify "the topic basis is affected"/awkward "are" (Line 361), change "examples" → "terms" in Figure 5 caption, and standardize reference formatting.
