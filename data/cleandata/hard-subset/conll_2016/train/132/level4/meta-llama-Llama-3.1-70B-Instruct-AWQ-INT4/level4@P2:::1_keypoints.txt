- Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid of word2vec and LDA to learn dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document representations.
- Extends the Skipgram Negative-Sampling (SGNS) objective to include document-wide feature vectors and continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors.
- Demonstrates the model on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.
- Novel combination enabling simultaneous learning of word and document representations.
- Produces sparse, interpretable document representations compared to traditional dense representations.
- Uses the Chainer automatic-differentiation framework for rapid development and training.
- Interpretable representations are a significant advantage.
- The model and codebase are flexible and easily extendable via Chainer.
- Shows empirical effectiveness on two datasets.
- Technical details in the paper are unclear, making the architecture and implementation hard to follow.
- Paper lacks thorough quantitative evaluation and relies heavily on example clusters.
- Claims about solving word analogies are overstated and supported by cherry-picked examples.
- Requests clearer explanation of Section 2, especially how word2vec and LDA are combined.
- Asks how the authors will address the lack of quantitative evaluation.
- Requests more examples or experiments to validate the model's word-analogy capabilities.
- Recommends clearer and more concise presentation of technical details.
- Suggests adding more quantitative evaluations to better demonstrate effectiveness.
- Recommends consistent reference formatting with full venue names instead of abbreviations.
