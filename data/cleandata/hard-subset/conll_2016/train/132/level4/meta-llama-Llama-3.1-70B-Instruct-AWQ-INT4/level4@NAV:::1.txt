A combination of word2vec and LDA could be a promising approach. However, the paper's technical details are unclear, hindering comprehension. A thorough revision of Section 2 is necessary to provide a clear, high-level overview of the model combination for readers familiar with word2vec and LDA.

Detailed comments:

The third paragraph of the introduction lacks clarity. Specifically, the phrase "requires deriving a new approximation" is ambiguous - what is being approximated, and why is prototype development time-consuming? Additionally, the rationale behind using the same word vectors for pivot and target, unlike in word2vec, is unclear.

Several terms and concepts are not adequately explained, including the separation of words from a marginal distribution, co-adaptation, and the type of structure referred to in the phrase "if we only included structure up to this point." Furthermore, the statement "it's similarity" contains a grammatical error.

Footnote 1 compromises the anonymity of the submission. Moreover, the paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation, relying instead on example clusters. Figure 2 appears to present a quantitative evaluation, but the description is confined to the lengthy caption. The conclusion's claim that the model solves word analogies is overstated, as only a few selective examples are provided.

Lastly, the reference to Chang is incomplete, omitting the conference or journal name, which should be fully specified for clarity.
