**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a hybrid model, lda2vec, that combines the strengths of word2vec and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) to learn dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document representations. The model extends the Skipgram Negative-Sampling (SGNS) objective to incorporate document-wide feature vectors and learns continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets: Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Hybrid Model**: The paper proposes a novel combination of word2vec and LDA, allowing for the simultaneous learning of word and document representations.
2. **Sparse Document Representations**: lda2vec produces sparse, interpretable document representations, which is a key advantage over traditional dense document representations.
3. **Automatic Differentiation Framework**: The authors utilize the Chainer framework to quickly develop and train the model, making it easier to evaluate and refine the architecture.

**Strengths**

1. **Interpretable Representations**: lda2vec provides sparse and interpretable document representations, which is a significant advantage over traditional dense representations.
2. **Flexibility**: The model can be easily extended and modified using the Chainer framework.
3. **Effectiveness**: lda2vec demonstrates good performance on two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Technical Details**: The paper's technical details are unclear, making it difficult for readers to understand the model's architecture and implementation.
2. **Lack of Evaluation**: The paper relies heavily on example clusters and lacks a thorough quantitative evaluation.
3. **Overstated Claims**: The authors overstate the model's ability to solve word analogies, presenting only a few cherry-picked examples.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clearer explanation of the technical details in Section 2, particularly the combination of word2vec and LDA?
2. How do you plan to address the lack of quantitative evaluation in the paper?
3. Can you provide more examples or experiments to demonstrate the model's ability to solve word analogies?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper would benefit from a clearer and more concise presentation of the technical details.
* The authors should consider adding more quantitative evaluations to demonstrate the model's effectiveness.
* The paper's references could be formatted more consistently, with full venue names instead of abbreviations.
