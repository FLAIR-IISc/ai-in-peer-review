Combining word2vec and LDA presents a potentially interesting direction. However, the primary weakness of this paper lies in the lack of clarity in its technical presentation. Section 2 requires a substantial revision to provide a clear, high-level overview of the model combination for readers already familiar with both word2vec and LDA. The current explanation fails to achieve this. 

Specific concerns include: the third paragraph of the introduction is unclear – the “approximation” being referenced needs to be defined, and the justifications for the claimed time consumption in prototype development and ease of feature evaluation are missing. The decision to utilize identical word vectors for both pivot and target words (contrary to standard word2vec practice) lacks sufficient motivation. Further clarification is needed regarding the meaning of “separating words from a marginal distribution” and the concept of “co-adaptation.” The phrase “If we only included structure up to this point” is ambiguous and requires specification of the “structure” being discussed. A minor grammatical correction is needed: “it’s similarity” should be “its similarity.” Footnote 1 compromises reviewer anonymity.

Critically, the paper lacks a rigorous evaluation. Qualitative examples of clusters are insufficient for a modern NLP publication. While Figure 2 *appears* to present quantitative results, these are only described within the lengthy caption and not formally analyzed in the text. The conclusion’s claim that the model “solves” word analogies is an overstatement, given the presentation of only a limited set of selected examples. Finally, the citation for Chang is incomplete, omitting the full conference or journal name (“Advances in…” is insufficient).
