- Paper proposes lda2vec: combine SGNS and LDA-style document-topic mixtures where context c = word vector + document vector and document vectors are convex combinations of learnable topic vectors with Dirichlet-style sparsity.
- Jointly trains word, topic, and document vectors with automatic differentiation and evaluates on 20 Newsgroups (topic coherence) plus qualitative analysis on a large Hacker News corpus.
- Contribution: a simple, end-to-end trainable augmentation of SGNS that yields interpretable document-topic mixtures via sparse softmax over topic vectors.
- Contribution: topics, words, and documents share the same embedding space allowing direct nearest-neighbor inspection of topics.
- Contribution: provides qualitative evidence of coherent topics (Cv ≈ 0.56 on 20NG) and preserves embedding linearities; open-source GPU/AD implementation.
- Strength: elegant integration of interpretability into SGNS with additive context separating long-term (document) and short-term (word) signals.
- Strength: interpretable shared embedding space improves transparency vs. Doc2Vec and enables direct inspection of topic-word relations.
- Strength: convincing qualitative topic examples on 20NG with plausible Cv scores.
- Strength: scalable and practical—trained with negative sampling on a 75M-token Hacker News corpus and released code supports reproducibility.
- Strength: clear, intuitive motivation for additive composition and sparsity-inducing Dirichlet-style regularizer.
- Weakness: lacks quantitative baselines and ablations—no direct comparison to LDA, Doc2Vec, topical embedding methods, or SGNS-with-post-hoc-topics.
- Weakness: Hacker News evaluation is entirely qualitative; coherence metrics (e.g., NPMI) and systematic analogy benchmarks are missing.
- Weakness: limited downstream validation—no document classification, retrieval, or clustering experiments to justify document vector utility.
- Weakness: ambiguous probabilistic framing—the Dirichlet term acts as a regularizer rather than a proper probabilistic topic model; minibatch scaling and sensitivity to λ/α are unclear.
- Weakness: several methodological choices are under-justified (tied input/output embeddings, negative sampling params, dropout, exponent) and topic redundancy/diversity is unaddressed.
- Weakness: presentation and related-work coverage need improvement; some figures/references are messy or incomplete and key related models are under-cited.
- Question: provide direct Cv comparisons to a tuned LDA baseline on 20NG and ablations (no Dirichlet, c=d only, c=w only, varied λ/α) to quantify component contributions.
- Question: evaluate document vectors on 20NG classification (e.g., linear SVM) and compare to Doc2Vec and LDA.
- Question: why tie input and output embeddings, and what is the effect of using separate matrices as in standard SGNS?
- Question: for Hacker News, can coherence be computed on the same corpus (e.g., NPMI) and can analogy accuracy be reported on a curated domain-specific set?
- Question: how are unseen/new documents handled at test time, is there an efficient inference procedure for topic proportions, and how stable are inferred proportions?
- Question: how sensitive are coherence and sparsity to the number of topics, and what diagnostics/recommendations exist for selecting topic count?
- Additional comment: report training time and memory compared to LDA/Doc2Vec to substantiate practicality claims.
- Additional comment: discuss topic diversity regularizers and failure cases (topic collapse, diffuse mixtures).
- Additional comment: improve references and figure readability to strengthen presentation.
