Summary and claimed contributions
The paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid objective that augments Skip-gram with Negative Sampling (SGNS) by adding document vectors constructed as convex mixtures of learned topic vectors, together with a Dirichlet prior encouraging sparse document–topic proportions. The context vector for predicting a target word is the sum of the pivot word embedding and the document embedding, ensuring that word, topic, and document vectors lie in the same space. Training is done end-to-end with automatic differentiation, using a regularization term corresponding to the Dirichlet log-likelihood on the simplex-normalized document–topic weights. The evaluation includes topic coherence (Cv) on 20 Newsgroups and qualitative topic and analogy results on a large Hacker News (HN) comments corpus.

As I see them, the main contributions are:
1) A simple, end-to-end trainable objective that combines SGNS with sparse document–topic mixtures to produce interpretable topics and document representations in the same embedding space as words.
2) Qualitative evidence that the model recovers plausible topics and preserves linear word regularities; some quantitative topic coherence on 20NG.
3) Open-source implementation facilitating reproducibility and extension.

Strengths
- Conceptual simplicity and practicality: The model is easy to implement in modern autodiff frameworks and scales to large corpora. The objective is straightforward (SGNS + Dirichlet regularizer) and does not require bespoke variational derivations or sampling schemes. This lowers the barrier for practitioners compared with bespoke probabilistic topic models.
- Unified embedding space for words, topics, and documents: Summing document and word vectors ties all components to a common geometry. This enables convenient interpretability (nearest words to a topic vector) and supports semantic composition (e.g., topical sense disambiguation of words in context) without extra machinery.
- Interpretability via explicit sparsity: The Dirichlet-inspired regularization encourages sparse document–topic mixtures, aligning with LDA-style interpretability. The qualitative topics on 20NG and HN are coherent and map well to recognizable domains.
- Qualitative transfer of SGNS properties: The paper shows that analogical structure emerges in-domain (e.g., HN-specific relations), indicating that the addition of document vectors does not destroy the desirable properties of SGNS embeddings.
- Resource release: Public code and preprocessing pipelines are useful to the community, and the authors discuss practical details (negative sampling exponent, dropout, initialization), which aids reproducibility.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to prior art integrating topics and embeddings: There is substantial prior work combining topic models with distributed representations or neural language models (e.g., TWE/Topical Word Embeddings, DocNADE/NADE-based topic models, Gaussian LDA linking topics to embedding spaces, neural variational document/topic models). The paper does not situate itself against these models with comparisons or detailed discussion of conceptual differences (e.g., discriminative SGNS-style objective vs. probabilistic generative objectives; per-topic word embeddings vs. shared embeddings + topic vectors).
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation and lack of baselines: On 20NG, only Cv is reported, with no comparison to LDA, NMF, DocNADE, NVDM, or even Doc2Vec + LDA as baselines. No downstream tasks (classification, retrieval, clustering) are provided to assess the utility of document or topic representations. Claims about sparsity and interpretability could be supported by entropy of topic mixtures, topic diversity, and human evaluation. For HN, the paper foregoes coherence entirely; however, coherence can be computed with an in-domain co-occurrence matrix rather than relying on Palmetto’s external corpus.
- Ambiguities and clarity issues in the method description: 
  - The text says “set α = n−1” for sparsity, which reads as alpha = n-1 (typically >1) rather than 1/n (<1); this must be clarified.
  - It states “softmax maps latent vectors initialized in R300 onto the simplex defined by pjk,” but p has dimension equal to the number of topics, not 300. The dimensionalities of topic mixtures, embeddings, and latent parameters should be clearly distinguished.
  - Dropout is applied to the document/pivot vectors; this can change the interpretation of mixture-based document vectors and may require justification or ablation.
- Modeling limitations and missing ablations: The objective is not a coherent probabilistic model of documents; the Dirichlet term acts as a regularizer on softmax outputs rather than part of an ELBO. There are no ablations for (i) removing the document vector, (ii) removing the Dirichlet term, or (iii) varying λ and α to quantify trade-offs between coherence and SGNS quality. The choice of sharing input/output embeddings deviates from common SGNS practice; an ablation could test its impact.
- Reporting and reproducibility gaps: Important hyperparameters (dropout rates, learning rates/schedules, subsampling thresholds, negative sampling counts) are only partially specified. There is no reporting of training time, memory, convergence behavior, or stability across seeds, especially for the large HN run.

Questions to authors
- Please clarify α: do you set α = 1/n (which encourages sparsity) or α = n-1? If the former, please correct the notation. How sensitive are results to α and λ?
- What is the exact dimensionality of the per-document latent variables that go through softmax to produce p_j? Is it R^n (n topics)? The text mentions R300, which seems inconsistent.
- Can you report baselines on 20NG: LDA coherence, Doc2Vec document classification accuracy, and a neural topic model (e.g., DocNADE or a variational neural topic model) for coherence and/or perplexity? Even a simple LDA baseline for Cv would contextualize your 0.56–0.57.
- Can you compute coherence on HN with in-domain co-occurrence statistics (e.g., NPMI on the HN corpus) rather than Palmetto’s external corpus?
- Ablations: (i) remove the Dirichlet term (α=1) and quantify the drop in Cv and sparsity; (ii) use c_j = w_j only (no document vector) to quantify the value of the document component; (iii) compare shared vs. separate input/output embeddings.
- What dropout rates were used on w and d, and how sensitive are results to these choices?

Additional comments
- Consider reporting topic sparsity (mean entropy of p_j), topic diversity, and topic overlap metrics alongside Cv.
- Downstream evaluation (e.g., if document vectors improve 20NG classification vs. baselines) would strengthen the case for utility.
- Some references are missing or misformatted, and related work coverage should be expanded to include neural autoregressive topic models and topical word embeddings.
- Numerical stability: since log p appears in the loss, did you need epsilon-clipping? Please mention any safeguards.
- Minor: clarify whether topic and word vectors are L2 normalized before cosine similarity; this affects nearest-neighbor interpretability.
