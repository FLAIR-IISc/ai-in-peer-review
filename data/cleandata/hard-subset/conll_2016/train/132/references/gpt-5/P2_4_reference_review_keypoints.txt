- Proposes lda2vec: augment SGNS with document vectors constrained as convex combinations of topic vectors and a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity penalty.  
- Documents, topics, and words live in a shared embedding space; context = pivot word vector + document vector; trained with SGD + negative sampling.  
- Claimed contributions: ties SGNS to sparse doc–topic mixtures, yields interpretable topic vectors in word space, demonstrates coherent topics and SGNS-like analogies, and provides an easy-to-extend implementation.  
- Strength: conceptually simple and implementable in autodiff toolkits, scales with negative sampling, and code is GPU-ready.  
- Strength: interpretability by design—document vectors on the simplex and topics directly comparable to words; shown recognizable topics on 20NG and HN.  
- Strength: joint word–topic–document space enables direct topic queries and linear analogies not available in vanilla LDA/doc2vec.  
- Strength: practical training recipe that can be faster and more straightforward than LDA/Gibbs approaches.  
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative evaluation and baselines—no head-to-head comparisons (LDA, NMF, doc2vec, neural models) on the same preprocessing and metrics.  
- Weakness: HN results are only qualitative; 20NG reports Cv only for lda2vec variants, making significance unclear.  
- Weakness: methodological ambiguities and apparent errors (e.g., α stated <1 then set to n−1—likely a typo), unclear softmax dimensionality, and under-specified optimization/hyperparameter details.  
- Weakness: missing ablations for key claims—no experiments showing effects of removing the Dirichlet term, varying α/λ, changing context composition, or using pretrained vs random embeddings.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical grounding—little connection to PMI factorization or probabilistic topic models, and no discussion of identifiability or when additive contexts may harm topics.  
- Weakness: presentation and formatting issues (figures, formulas, references) that hurt clarity and reproducibility.  
- Key question: clarify the Dirichlet concentration α (is it 1/n or n−1?), which λ values were tried, and sensitivity to these hyperparameters.  
- Key question: what is the dimensionality of the logits before the softmax for p_jk and how is mapping from R^300 to the n-simplex implemented?  
- Key question: provide quantitative baselines on 20NG with identical preprocessing for Cv, topic diversity, and a downstream task (e.g., document classification).  
- Key question: run ablations—(i) remove Ld, (ii) vary α, (iii) disable summation of doc and pivot vectors, (iv) separate input/output embeddings—and report effects.  
- Key question: supply full training details (learning rate, batch size, epochs, negative sampling exponent, runtime/memory, stability across seeds, overfitting controls).  
- Overall: idea is appealing and practical but the paper lacks rigorous evaluation, ablations, and clear methodology; reviewer leans toward rejection pending stronger experiments and corrected exposition.
