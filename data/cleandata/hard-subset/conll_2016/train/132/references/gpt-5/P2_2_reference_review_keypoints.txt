- Proposes lda2vec: augment SGNS/word2vec with document vectors constrained as sparse mixtures over shared topic vectors, summing pivot and document vectors for context.  
- Claims joint embedding of words, topics, and documents in a shared space with Dirichlet-style sparsity on document-topic proportions.  
- Provides experiments on 20 Newsgroups (topic coherence, qualitative topics) and Hacker News (qualitative topics, analogies) and releases code.

- Contribution: simple joint embedding framework tying interpretable sparse document-topic mixtures to SGNS-style training.  
- Contribution: demonstrates easy implementation with autodiff, scalability to large corpora, and provides open-source code.  
- Contribution: qualitative evidence that topics are coherent and embeddings capture domain-specific linear regularities.

- Strength: design choices (sum of pivot+doc, doc as mixture over topics) give clear interpretability; topics live in same space as words for easy inspection.  
- Strength: reuses SGNS/negative sampling with light regularization, making the method simple and scalable without complex variational inference.  
- Strength: compelling qualitative examples on 20NG and Hacker News show coherent topics and community-specific semantics.  
- Strength: publicly released code and preprocessing scripts aid reproducibility and adoption.

- Weakness: quantitative evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to baselines (LDA, NMF, HDP, neural topic models, doc2vec), making reported coherence hard to interpret.  
- Weakness: omits extrinsic task evaluations (e.g., classification, retrieval, clustering) to demonstrate utility beyond qualitative inspection.  
- Weakness: methodological clarity problems—use of a “Dirichlet likelihood” as regularizer, inconsistent parameter descriptions (mention of R300 vs. n_topics), and no sensitivity analysis for α and λ.  
- Weakness: missing ablations to disentangle effects of (a) adding document vector, (b) constraining to topic mixtures, and (c) Dirichlet sparsity; pretrained initialization confounds results.  
- Weakness: presentation and training details are incomplete and contain typographical/formatting issues, hindering reproducibility.

- Question: provide quantitative baselines on 20NG (LDA with same preprocessing and topic count, NMF, doc2vec) and at least one extrinsic task using learned topic proportions.  
- Question: clarify p_jk parameterization—are softmax logits per document in R^n_topics? fix inconsistent references to R300 and report exact parameter shapes.  
- Question: report sensitivity of coherence and interpretability to α and λ, and quantify sparsity (e.g., entropy or average number of topics per document >5%).  
- Question: provide ablations for variants: (i) no document vector added to pivot, (ii) unconstrained document vectors, (iii) with/without Dirichlet regularizer, (iv) without pretrained embeddings on 20NG.  
- Question: compute NPMI-based coherence using counts from the Hacker News corpus to enable quantitative comparison.

- Overall: neat, practical idea with good qualitative demos and released code, but falls short due to weak quantitative comparisons, missing extrinsic evaluations, limited ablations, and clarity/reproducibility issues.
