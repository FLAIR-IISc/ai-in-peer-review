- Proposes lda2vec: context vector = pivot word vector + document vector, where document vector is a convex combination of shared topic vectors with simplex weights and a Dirichlet-style sparsity regularizer, trained end-to-end with negative sampling and Adam.  
- Main contribution: a simple, differentiable objective that ties SGNS embeddings with sparse, interpretable document–topic mixtures in a shared embedding space.  
- Main contribution: empirical evidence of coherent topics on 20 Newsgroups and qualitative topic/analogy results on a large Hacker News corpus, plus open-source code.  
- Strength: conceptually simple and GPU-friendly architecture that is easy to implement in autodiff frameworks.  
- Strength: unified embedding space for words, topics, and documents enables interpretable operations (e.g., nearest words to topics).  
- Strength: enforces sparse, normalized doc-topic mixtures via softmax plus Dirichlet-like regularizer, improving interpretability over dense doc vectors.  
- Strength: qualitative topics look plausible across datasets and analogy examples show corpus-specific semantic structure.  
- Strength: sensible training choices (tied embeddings, negative sampling exponents, phrase-aware tokenization) improve practicality.  
- Weakness: limited novelty because prior work (TWE, TopicVec, Gaussian LDA, LFTM, joint topic–embedding models, doc2vec) already couples topics and embeddings or learns document vectors from context.  
- Weakness: weak empirical evaluation—no comparisons to standard LDA, CTM, topical-embedding baselines, or downstream tasks like document classification/retrieval.  
- Weakness: Hacker News results are purely qualitative; coherence metrics omitted due to tokenization mismatch and no alternative intrinsic metrics reported.  
- Weakness: missing ablations and sensitivity analyses for the Dirichlet regularizer (λ, α), free vs. mixture doc vectors, and composition choice c = w + d.  
- Weakness: dependence on pretrained vectors (used on 20NG) is not analyzed; effect of initialization is unclear.  
- Weakness: probabilistic framing is misleading—Dirichlet term acts as a regularizer and the model is not generative, so held-out likelihood/perplexity and posterior inference are not available.  
- Weakness: reporting and clarity issues—underspecified implementation details (dropout, subsampling, learning rates, normalization), hard-to-parse figures, and reference/formatting problems.  
- Question: please report Cv coherence for matched-preprocessing baselines (LDA, TWE/TopicVec) on 20 Newsgroups and evaluate doc vectors on classification/retrieval vs. doc2vec/LDA.  
- Question: provide ablations of λ and α, distributions of doc-topic weights, and comparisons of (i) free doc vectors, (ii) mixtures without Dirichlet, and (iii) full model; quantify c = w + d vs. other compositions.  
- Question: how much do pretrained word vectors help on 20 Newsgroups—can you train from scratch and report the gap?  
- Question: report training times, hardware, and key hyperparameters (dropout rate, subsampling, window strategy); confirm if input/output embeddings are tied and whether norms are constrained.  
- Question: do topics collapse or become redundant—please report topic diversity or decorrelation measures and consider orthogonality penalties.  
- Question: how are doc-topic proportions inferred for unseen documents with fixed topics/word vectors, and what is the computational cost?  
- Overall: idea is appealing and useful but needs stronger positioning against prior work and substantially more rigorous empirical evaluation and ablations to justify acceptance.
