Summary and claimed contributions
- Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid objective that augments skip-gram with negative sampling (SGNS) by adding a document vector to the pivot word, and constrains each document vector to be a convex combination of learned topic vectors, with a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity regularizer on document–topic proportions.
- Demonstrates qualitative topic interpretability and reports topic coherence (Cv via Palmetto) on 20 Newsgroups; presents qualitative topics and word analogies on a large Hacker News comments corpus.
- Provides an implementation using an automatic differentiation framework (Chainer) with GPU support.

Strengths
- Simple, implementable idea that attempts to bring sparse, interpretable document–topic structure into the SGNS/doc2vec family while keeping word/document/topic vectors in a shared space.
- Some evidence of interpretability: reported Cv scores on 20 Newsgroups and coherent-looking topic word lists on both datasets. The qualitative topics correspond to expected newsgroups/themes.
- Scales to a nontrivial corpus (Hacker News; 75M tokens, 66k documents) and produces domain-specific linear regularities in the embedding space, suggesting the model can learn reasonable word representations in situ.
- Open-source code and a clear high-level description of the training objective will aid reproducibility and adoption.
- The use of a sparsity-inducing prior on document–topic proportions aligns with established practice for topic interpretability.

Weaknesses
- Lack of rigorous comparison to established baselines. There is no quantitative head-to-head with LDA/CTM/NMF for coherence/perplexity, nor with doc2vec/paragraph vectors, or with neural topic models, on standard datasets. Reporting only lda2vec’s Cv without an LDA baseline on the same data leaves unclear whether the proposed method improves, matches, or degrades interpretability.
- Limited novelty relative to known methods. The “document + word vector predicts target” setup closely mirrors the Distributed Memory variant of paragraph vectors (doc2vec). The main novelty is constraining the document vector to be a simplex-weighted mixture of topic vectors and adding a Dirichlet-style regularizer. This reads as an engineering regularization of doc2vec rather than a new modeling principle, and the paper does not empirically show that this constraint yields better topics or downstream performance than doc2vec + post-hoc topic induction or LDA alone.
- Methodological justification is weak. The objective is a sum of the SGNS loss and a scaled Dirichlet “likelihood” over softmaxed document–topic weights. This is not derived as an ELBO or a principled probabilistic model; key choices (e.g., λ=200, α=1/n) appear ad hoc, and there is no ablation demonstrating their necessity or robustness. The tying of input/output embeddings in SGNS is nonstandard and not justified. No discussion of identifiability of topic vectors in a shared embedding space (rotation/scale), or of how the additive context interacts with negative sampling.
- Evaluation is largely qualitative on the larger corpus. For Hacker News, coherence is omitted due to tokenization mismatch, leaving only hand-picked topics and analogy examples. There is no comparison to SGNS trained on the same data, to doc2vec, or to LDA on a phrase-tokenized corpus, so it is unclear whether lda2vec adds value beyond established baselines.
- Clarity and technical detail issues. The description of mapping “latent vectors initialized in R300 onto the simplex defined by pjk” is confusing—document–topic proportions should be length-n, not 300. Notation is inconsistent in places (e.g., indices and ranges). Training details (epoch count seems very high for large corpora; runtime/memory; negative sampling details; whether input/output embeddings are tied) and hyperparameter selection/tuning are insufficiently specified. Pretrained word vectors are used on 20NG but not on HN, confounding comparisons.

Questions to authors
- Please provide quantitative baselines on 20 Newsgroups: Cv and, if possible, UMass/NPMI for standard LDA (e.g., Gibbs/VI), CTM, NMF, and doc2vec-based document clustering. Does lda2vec outperform or match these?
- How does lda2vec compare to doc2vec (DM and DBOW) trained under the same settings, both in topic coherence (e.g., clustering and labeling) and downstream tasks (e.g., document classification with the learned doc vectors)?
- Ablations: What happens if you remove the Dirichlet regularizer (α=1 or λ=0)? If you allow dense doc vectors (no simplex constraint)? If you do not tie input/output embeddings? Please report Cv and any downstream metrics.
- Clarify the construction of document–topic proportions: what is the dimensionality of the softmaxed vector per document—n topics or 300? If the latter, how is it mapped to n topics? Please correct/clarify the notation.
- Hyperparameters: How were λ and α chosen? How sensitive are results to these values, to dropout, to number of negatives, to window size, and to β (beyond the small sweep shown)?
- For Hacker News, can you compute coherence using an external corpus that supports your phrase vocabulary (or map phrases to unigrams) to provide a quantitative comparison to LDA and SGNS baselines?
- Training details: epochs, batch sizes, negative sampling distribution, optimizer settings, wall-clock time and hardware. Did you use separate input/output embeddings or tie them? If tied, why?

Overall, while the idea is intuitively appealing and the qualitative results are reasonable, the paper lacks rigorous empirical validation against strong, established baselines and offers limited methodological novelty. A more thorough, controlled evaluation and clearer, principled justification of the objective would be needed to justify acceptance.
