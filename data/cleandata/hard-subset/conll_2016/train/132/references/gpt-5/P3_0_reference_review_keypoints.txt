- Proposal: lda2vec combines SGNS-style word embeddings with LDA-like topic mixtures where context = pivot word vector + document vector and document vectors are softmax-constrained mixtures of K topic vectors with a Dirichlet-like sparsity regularizer.
- Training/eval: trained with negative sampling and Adam in Chainer; evaluated on 20 Newsgroups (Cv coherence, qualitative topics) and a large Hacker News corpus (qualitative topics and word analogies).
- Claimed contributions: unified embedding space for words, topics, and documents; interpretable sparse-ish topic mixtures without variational inference; empirical coherence on 20NG and corpus-specific semantics on HN; open-source implementation.
- Strength — simplicity: model is conceptually simple and straightforward to implement in autodiff frameworks using familiar components.
- Strength — interpretability: topic vectors live in the same space as words, enabling intuitive diagnostics like nearest words to topics.
- Strength — sparse mixtures without VI: Dirichlet-inspired regularizer provides a pragmatic way to encourage sparse document-topic mixtures while retaining end-to-end training.
- Strength — qualitative alignment: 20NG topics and HN topics/analogies align with domain expectations (e.g., sci.space, Mt. Gox, DDG).
- Strength — reproducibility: authors release code and preprocessing details.
- Weakness — limited novelty: approach is close to PV-DM/doc2vec and other topical-embedding hybrids (TWE, DocNADE, Gaussian LDA), so novelty must be better positioned and justified.
- Weakness — narrow evaluation and missing baselines: lacks direct comparisons to LDA, NMF, PV-DM, TWE, etc., no document-level downstream tasks, perplexity, diversity metrics, or human evaluations.
- Weakness — confounded by pretrained embeddings: using pretrained word vectors likely boosts results and no ablation contrasts pretraining vs random init.
- Weakness — imprecise math/exposition: mislabels priors as likelihoods, ignores softmax Jacobian/constants, dimensional inconsistency (softmax described over embedding dim instead of K topics), and overloaded notation.
- Weakness — not truly sparse: softmax assigns positive mass to all topics; alternatives (sparsemax/entmax, temperature annealing, stick-breaking) are not explored.
- Weakness — hyperparameter/stability issues: sparsity weight λ, α, and other hyperparameters are chosen heuristically with no sensitivity analysis or error bars; dropout mentioned but not evaluated.
- Question — novelty/positioning: how does lda2vec differ methodologically and empirically from PV-DM plus post-hoc topic discovery or TWE; can you include direct comparisons?
- Question — pretraining ablation: what are coherence and topic quality when starting from random embeddings?
- Question — topic-word scoring: how are top words selected/scored (dot product vs cosine) and what exact scores were used for Palmetto?
- Question — parameterization clarity: please fix the softmax-over-K description, resolve notation overload (n for topics vs negatives, j indexing), and clarify dimensions.
- Question — regularization details: how was λ chosen and what sensitivity analyses were performed for λ, α, K, β, negatives, and dropout?
- Question — true sparsity experiments: did you try sparsemax/entmax or temperature-annealed softmax and what were the trade-offs?
- Question — downstream tasks: can you report 20NG document classification using p_j or doc vectors and compare word analogy performance to SGNS on the same data?
- Suggestion — additional evaluations: report topic diversity and intruder-word evaluations, include standard deviations over multiple runs.
- Minor suggestion — manuscript fixes: correct reference formatting, clarify notation, and state how topic vectors are initialized and updated.
