- Contribution: Proposes lda2vec, combining SGNS with simplex-constrained mixtures of topic vectors and a Dirichlet sparsity regularizer to produce sparse, interpretable document–topic mixtures while preserving word2vec semantics.
- Contribution: Demonstrates joint word/topic/document embedding quality (interpretable topics and analogies) on 20NG and Hacker News, with an autodiff implementation that scales to large corpora.

- Strength: Principled, simple design replacing dense doc vectors with convex topic mixtures encourages LDA-like interpretability.
- Strength: Additive context vector (pivot word + document) is intuitive and leverages compositional embedding properties.
- Strength: Joint embedding space enables direct inspection of topics via nearest neighbors and supports meaningful analogical reasoning.
- Strength: Practical and scalable implementation using negative sampling, GPUs, and open-source code aids reproducibility.
- Strength: Dirichlet regularizer produces sparse document proportions, aligning with topic-model interpretability.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—approach closely resembles PV-DM/doc2vec; main novelty is simplex constraint + Dirichlet term, and prior related work is under-discussed.
- Weakness: Insufficient quantitative evaluation and baselines—only Cv on 20NG, no comparisons to LDA, doc2vec variants, or downstream tasks like classification/retrieval.
- Weakness: Methodological clarity issues—Dirichlet used as a regularizer rather than from a generative model, and many training/architecture details are underspecified or inconsistent.
- Weakness: Use of pretrained embeddings on 20NG without ablation complicates fairness of results.
- Weakness: Presentation problems—formatting/citation errors and missing related work at the intersection of topics and embeddings.
- Weakness: Risk of scale/degeneracy—topic vector norms and mixture scaling are not addressed.

- Question: How does lda2vec compare quantitatively to LDA and PV-DM/doc2vec on Cv, topic sparsity (entropy/effective topics), held-out likelihood, and downstream tasks?
- Question: Can you provide ablations over α and λ showing effects on coherence, sparsity (Gini/concentration), and run-to-run stability?
- Question: Please clarify architecture/training details: embedding dimensionality, tied input/output embeddings, frequency subsampling, dropout, negative sampling schedule, topic normalization, and exact mapping to the K-simplex.
- Question: On Hacker News, can coherence be computed using an internal co-occurrence matrix or adapted Palmetto for phrases to enable comparisons?
- Question: Do topic vector norms drift during training, and have you considered unit-normalizing topics or using cosine-based losses to prevent scale-dominated mixtures?

- Comment: Idea is promising and practical but needs stronger baselines, clearer exposition, and better related-work coverage.
- Comment: Reporting runtime, memory, and stability across seeds would improve reproducibility.

- Recommendation: Weak Reject—interesting and useful but incremental over PV-DM with insufficient evaluation and clarity; would be stronger with rigorous comparisons and clearer methodology.
