- Proposal: lda2vec — combine SGNS word2vec with document vectors constrained as sparse softmax mixtures over learned topic vectors, using a Dirichlet sparsity term; trained end-to-end with negative sampling and Adam; code and preprocessing provided.

Contributions:
- Joint embedding framework blending SGNS with interpretable, sparse document-topic mixtures learned in a shared space.
- Empirical claim that the model yields coherent topics and corpus-specific semantic regularities without bespoke inference, via autodiff optimization.
- Open-source implementation and large-scale qualitative case study (Hacker News) showing topics, nearest neighbors, and analogies.

Strengths:
- Conceptual simplicity and interpretability: retains additive SGNS semantics while enforcing LDA-like simplex and sparsity on document-topic proportions.
- Practicality and ease of implementation: replaces variational inference with standard optimization plus Dirichlet regularizer; code lowers barrier to adoption.
- Coherence-oriented evaluation on 20 Newsgroups: reports Cv over topic and negative-sampling hyperparameter sweeps.
- Scalability and qualitative depth: Hacker News case study demonstrates domain-specific topics and meaningful joint word/topic space for neighbors and analogies.
- Clear intuitions and design choices: additive context c = w + d and Dirichlet sparsity provide direct interpretability mechanisms.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty and incomplete related work: similar ideas exist (doc2vec, topical embeddings, ETM, Gaussian LDA); paper lacks theoretical/empirical comparison to these baselines.
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation and missing baselines: no direct comparisons of Cv/perplexity or downstream tasks against LDA/NMF/ETM/TWE/doc2vec under identical preprocessing; Hacker News results purely qualitative.
- Methodological ambiguities and potential inconsistency: unclear parameterization (mention of R300 seems wrong), unclear whether Dirichlet is a true prior or regularizer on softmax outputs.
- Limited analysis of design choices: no study of additive weighting/norms (c = w + d may let one term dominate), no ablations for λ, α, dropout, negative samples; confounded use of pretrained embeddings across datasets.
- Reporting and reproducibility gaps: no runtimes, memory, stability, multiple seeds, error bars, or sensitivity analyses; hyperparameter choices appear ad hoc.

Questions to authors:
- Provide quantitative baselines: how does lda2vec Cv compare to LDA/NMF/ETM/TWE on 20NG with identical preprocessing and topic counts; any downstream classification/retrieval comparisons to doc2vec/LDA?
- Clarify document-topic parameterization: is p_j in R^n (n topics)? Fix the “R300” typo and explain how the Dirichlet term is applied to softmax outputs and whether it’s treated as a prior or regularizer.
- Ablations requested: report metrics for variants without (a) the Dirichlet term, (b) the topic-mixture constraint (free document vectors), and (c) addition vs learned weighted sum c = w + γ d or norm constraints.
- Stability/sensitivity: how sensitive are results to λ, α, number of negatives, β, dropout, and initialization; do you enforce vector norm constraints?
- Hacker News evaluation: can you provide quantitative human or automatic evaluations (topic intrusion, coherence) or comparison to LDA on the same tokenization?

Additional comments:
- Expand related work to include topical word embeddings and neural topic models, and clarify differences from prior art.
- Consider reporting analogy accuracy versus standard SGNS on the same corpora to quantify tradeoffs between interpretability and embedding quality.
