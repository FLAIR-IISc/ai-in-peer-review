Summary and claimed contributions
The paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model that augments SGNS (word2vec) with document representations formed as mixtures over learned topic vectors. The model jointly learns word, topic, and document vectors in a shared space, and adds a Dirichlet-based regularizer to encourage sparse document–topic proportions. Experiments on 20 Newsgroups and a large Hacker News comments corpus provide qualitative topic examples and analogy-style vector arithmetic, and report topic coherence (Cv) for 20NG. Code is released.

As I see it, the main contributions are:
1) A simple neural formulation that ties SGNS to interpretable document representations by constraining document vectors to be convex combinations of topic vectors, with a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity term.
2) A demonstration that such a model can be implemented and trained end-to-end with automatic differentiation, producing qualitatively coherent topics and plausible word analogies.
3) Open-source implementation and qualitative case studies on two corpora.

Strengths
- Conceptual simplicity and interpretability: Constraining document vectors to lie in the simplex over topic vectors yields an interpretable decomposition while retaining SGNS’ ability to learn useful word representations. The shared space makes topic–word similarity queries direct and intuitive.
- Practical implementability: The model is straightforward to implement in modern autodiff frameworks and may be attractive for practitioners who already rely on SGNS-style training but want per-document topic mixtures.
- Qualitative results align with intuition: On 20NG, top tokens per topic align with known newsgroups; on HN, topic labels plausibly capture community themes; analogy-style examples show that the embeddings capture salient relations.
- Open-source release: Availability of code lowers the barrier to reproduction and extension.

Weaknesses
- Methodological inconsistencies and apparent errors:
  • Dirichlet concentration α: The text explicitly claims sparsity when α < 1, but then sets α = n−1 (for n topics), which is typically >> 1 and encourages homogeneity. This directly contradicts the stated goal of inducing sparsity and undermines the central regularizer. I suspect α = 1/n was intended. This must be corrected, and results need to be revisited accordingly.
  • Mini-batch scaling of Ld: You state that Ld is “adjusted to be proportional to the minibatch size divided by the size of the total corpus.” For an objective originally summed over all documents, the unbiased mini-batch estimator should scale by N/B (not B/N). As written, the regularizer is grossly underweighted. Please clarify and fix.
  • Softmax dimensionality: You write that a “softmax transform maps latent vectors initialized in R300 onto the simplex defined by pjk.” The logits for the document–topic proportions should be R^n (number of topics), not R^300 (embedding size). This is a non-trivial notation/implementation confusion.
  • Shared input/output embeddings: You use the same word vectors for pivot and target, diverging from standard SGNS that uses separate input/output embeddings. This design choice can materially affect embedding quality; no justification or ablation is provided.
  • Dropout details are vague and potentially problematic: You mention dropout on “the unnormalized document vector ~dj” and on ~wj, but do not specify where/how masks are applied (per-token, per-document, per-dimension), the dropout rates used, or effects on training stability. Given that ~dj is reused across many contexts in a document, dropout semantics matter.

- Weak and incomplete evaluation:
  • No baselines: There is no comparison to standard LDA (topic coherence/perplexity), doc2vec/paragraph vectors (document retrieval/classification), or other topic-embedding hybrids. Claimed “high” coherences (e.g., 0.567 Cv) are uninterpretable without baselines or statistical testing.
  • No quantitative downstream tasks: No document classification, retrieval, or held-out likelihood/perplexity; no quantitative analogy accuracy; no human evaluation of topic interpretability beyond Cv (and Cv isn’t reported for HN).
  • Pretrained embeddings used on 20NG: Initializing with pretrained vectors can inflate coherence; without an LDA or doc2vec baseline under the same preprocessing, improvements cannot be attributed to the proposed architecture.

- Presentation and formatting issues that hinder clarity and reproducibility:
  • Numerous citation/reference errors (question marks, backslashes, malformed years, wrong authors for scikit-learn, incomplete venue details). The bibliography is not publication-ready.
  • Missing or broken figures/tables: Figure 1 appears corrupted; the text refers to Figure 6 but only Figures 1–5 are present. There is ASCII-like noise embedded in Section 1. Equation formatting is inconsistent.
  • Notation inconsistencies and typos throughout (e.g., R300 vs R^n, pjn indices, “do- mains,” “F lugdienst,” etc.).

- Limited novelty and insufficient positioning vs prior work: The model is essentially SGNS with a convex document-topic layer and Dirichlet regularization. Related neural topic models and hybrid approaches combining embeddings with topics are not adequately discussed or contrasted. The connection to standard doc2vec (DBOW/DM) and how the mixture constraint changes behavior is not analyzed.

- Reproducibility gaps and training details:
  • “Two hundred epochs” over large corpora is atypical; no wall-clock times, convergence diagnostics, early stopping, or sensitivity analyses are provided.
  • Critical hyperparameters are under-specified (dropout rates, λ ablation, negative sampling schedule, learning rate/decay, batch size). No seeds or variance across runs are reported.

Questions to authors (for rebuttal)
1) Did you intend α = 1/n rather than α = n−1? Please provide the correct derivation of Ld and confirm the exact α and λ used in all experiments.
2) How exactly did you scale Ld in mini-batch training? If you used B/N, please re-run with the correct N/B scaling (or equivalent) and report impacts.
3) Why did you tie input/output embeddings? Provide an ablation comparing tied vs untied embeddings on coherence and analogy tasks.
4) Please add quantitative baselines: LDA (same preprocessing) with Cv and, if feasible, held-out likelihood; doc2vec and LDA for document classification/retrieval; analogy accuracy on a standard set (or a curated HN set) to substantiate the qualitative examples.
5) Clarify dropout: rate(s), where the mask is applied (per token/document/dimension), and its effect on results. Provide λ and dropout ablations.
6) Fix figures (especially the missing Figure 6) and bibliography; ensure all equations/notations are correct (R^n vs R^300, indices).

Given the above methodological inconsistencies (notably the α and Ld scaling issues), lack of baselines/quantitative evaluation, and substantial presentation problems, I cannot recommend acceptance in its current form despite the appealing idea and open-source release. A careful revision that corrects the technical issues and adds strong baselines could make this a solid contribution.
