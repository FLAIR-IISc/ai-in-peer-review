Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid objective that augments SGNS with document vectors constrained to be mixtures over shared topic vectors, with a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity term on document-topic proportions. Word, topic, and document vectors are learned jointly in a shared embedding space.
- Empirical demonstrations on Twenty Newsgroups (reporting topic coherence) and a large Hacker News corpus (qualitative topics and analogies). The authors emphasize interpretability (sparse topic mixtures) alongside word-embedding regularities.
- An open-source implementation using an automatic differentiation framework.

Main contributions as I see them:
1) A practical formulation that ties document embeddings to sparse mixtures over learned topic vectors while training with SGNS, yielding a shared space for words, topics, and documents.
2) Qualitative evidence that the approach discovers reasonable topics and preserves word analogy structure; limited quantitative coherence results on 20NG.
3) Public code that may ease adoption.

Strengths
- Simple, pragmatic design. The model’s core idea—sum of pivot word and document vector where the document vector is a convex combination of topic vectors—is straightforward and easy to implement. This is attractive for practitioners and fits nicely within autodiff/SGNS tooling.
- Shared embedding space enhances interpretability. Being able to retrieve nearest words to topic vectors and directly inspect topic-word affinities without a separate decoder is convenient and aligns with common embedding workflows.
- Qualitative results look plausible. On both 20NG and HN, the listed top words per topic appear coherent and on-topic, and the reported analogies on HN are sensible for that domain.
- Open-source implementation. Availability of code and preprocessing scripts contributes to reproducibility and may facilitate comparative studies.
- Scalability to larger corpora. Training on ~75M tokens with 40 topics and 256 dimensions suggests the method is computationally feasible.

Weaknesses
- Limited and incomplete evaluation against strong baselines. There is no quantitative comparison to standard topic models (e.g., LDA with tuned hyperparameters, CTM/HDP) on topic coherence, nor to recent neural topic models or to simple baselines such as LDA + SGNS or NMF on GloVe. On 20NG, the paper reports average Cv numbers without baseline references, confidence intervals, or statistical testing. On HN, coherence is omitted due to tokenization mismatch, leaving only qualitative inspection. Without rigorous baselines, it is difficult to assess whether lda2vec improves topic quality or interpretability.
- No downstream or intrinsic evaluations of document representations. For a model that claims improved document representations, there are no classification/clustering retrieval experiments (e.g., 20NG label prediction) and no comparison to doc2vec or averaging word embeddings. The analogy demonstrations are qualitative; there is no quantitative analogy or word similarity evaluation, nor a comparison to plain SGNS trained on the same corpus.
- Missing ablations and sensitivity analyses. The role of the Dirichlet term (λ, α), the choice to tie input/output embeddings, dropout on embeddings, the number of topics, and the context-window size are not systematically explored. The paper asserts that α<1 encourages sparsity and λ=200 “works well,” but there is no study of topic coherence/interpretability versus these hyperparameters. An ablation without the Dirichlet term is mentioned qualitatively but not quantified.
- Methodological clarity and probabilistic grounding. The “Dirichlet likelihood” is applied directly to softmax-normalized proportions pjk but the overall objective is not a coherent generative likelihood; it is a regularized SGNS objective. This is fine as an engineering choice, but claims to “optimize a Dirichlet” are somewhat misleading without a principled probabilistic formulation or evidence that this regularizer reliably yields better topics than standard alternatives (e.g., L1 or entropic regularization). Also, Section 2.2.1 states that a softmax “maps latent vectors initialized in R300 onto the simplex defined by pjk” though the simplex should be of size equal to the number of topics n, not the embedding dimension; this needs clarification.
- Novelty relative to prior work is unclear. There has been a body of work connecting topic models and word embeddings or jointly learning topics and embeddings. The paper does not position itself against such approaches nor compare empirically. Without this, the contribution reads as an incremental combination of SGNS with a sparse document-topic mixture.
- Reporting and reproducibility details. Important training details are under-specified: precise dropout rate(s), learning rate schedule and early stopping criteria, initialization and constraints for pjk, stability across random seeds, runtime/memory versus LDA/SGNS. The decision to tie pivot and target embeddings in SGNS deviates from common practice; the impact is not studied.

Questions to authors
- Baselines: Can you report topic coherence (Cv and/or NPMI) against tuned LDA and, if possible, a neural topic model baseline on 20NG for the same number of topics? For HN, can you compute coherence on a compatible reference corpus or report human word-intrusion/topic-intrusion scores?
- Document representations: How do your document vectors perform on 20NG document classification (e.g., logistic regression over frozen document vectors), compared to doc2vec, LDA topic proportions, and averaged SGNS embeddings?
- Ablations and sensitivity: Please provide ablations removing (a) the Dirichlet term, (b) the mixture constraint (free dense document vectors), and (c) tying input/output embeddings; and sensitivity curves for α and λ showing effects on sparsity, coherence, and topic diversity.
- Clarification: In Section 2.2.1, should the softmax map from R^n (n = #topics) rather than R^300? If not, how is pjk parameterized?
- Stability and reproducibility: How sensitive are results to random seed and initialization (especially with/without pretrained embeddings on 20NG)? Please report mean±std over multiple runs.
- Efficiency: What are the training times and memory footprints relative to SGNS plus LDA on the same data? How does negative sampling with tied embeddings impact convergence?

Overall recommendation: While the idea is intuitive and the implementation practical, the current submission lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation and comparisons to established baselines necessary to validate a new method. I would encourage a more thorough empirical study (with baselines, ablations, and downstream tasks) and clarifications before publication.
