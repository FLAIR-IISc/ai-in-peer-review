Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model that augments the SGNS (word2vec) objective with document vectors constrained to be convex combinations of shared topic vectors. Document-topic mixture weights are encouraged to be sparse via a Dirichlet-inspired regularizer. The context vector is the sum of a pivot word vector and a document vector, tying local and global context.
- The model is trained end-to-end with automatic differentiation and negative sampling, with word, topic, and document vectors living in the same embedding space. Topics are interpreted by nearest-neighbor words; document-topic proportions resemble LDA.
- Empirical evidence is provided via topic coherence (Cv) on 20 Newsgroups and qualitative analyses (topic lists, word analogies) on 20NG and a large Hacker News comments corpus. Code and preprocessing are released.

Contributions as I see them (most significant first)
1) A simple neural formulation that constrains document embeddings to be sparse convex mixtures of shared topic vectors within an SGNS framework, enabling topic interpretability in the same space as word embeddings.
2) Empirical demonstration that this yields coherent topics on 20NG and sensible qualitative topics/analogies on a large web corpus, with an easy-to-implement autodiff training recipe.
3) Practical design choices (tying input/output embeddings, dropout on embeddings, Dirichlet-style sparsity) and open-source implementation.

Strengths
- Conceptual simplicity and implementability: The method integrates a well-known predictive objective (SGNS) with a convex mixture constraint on document vectors. This is easy to implement with autodiff, avoids bespoke variational inference, and can leverage GPUs. This lowers the barrier to prototyping “interpretable embeddings.”
- Interpretability via shared space: Making topic, document, and word vectors cohabit the same space allows straightforward interpretation of topics by nearest-neighbor words and direct semantic inspection of topic-word relations—an appealing property absent in many neural topic models.
- Sparsity mechanism for document-topic proportions: The Dirichlet-like log p regularizer (α<1) and simplex constraint encourage sparse, LDA-style document mixtures, addressing a common criticism of dense doc vectors (e.g., doc2vec). The paper gives qualitative evidence that sparsity improves interpretability.
- Reasonable topic coherence on 20NG: Reported mean Cv around 0.56–0.57 for 20 topics is in the typical range for moderately coherent topics on this corpus, suggesting the approach is viable as a topic model.
- Large-scale qualitative demonstration: The Hacker News experiment (with phrase/entity tokenization) shows the model can scale and learns corpus-specific semantics and analogies, indicating the approach is not limited to small datasets.

Weaknesses
- Limited and incomplete baselines: The paper does not quantitatively compare to strong baselines: LDA (with the same preprocessing and Cv), doc2vec (PV-DM/PV-DBOW), neural topic models (e.g., NVDM), or embedding+topic hybrids (e.g., topical word embeddings, Gaussian LDA). Without such comparisons, it is hard to assess novelty and gains. In particular, PV-DM already sums word and doc vectors to predict targets; the key difference here (convex topic mixture with sparsity) needs empirical ablations against PV-DM and LDA to establish its value.
- Evaluation is mostly qualitative on the large corpus and lacks task-based metrics: For 20NG, there is no document classification or clustering against newsgroup labels using the learned topic proportions; for HN, coherence is omitted rather than computed on the in-domain corpus (one can compute NPMI on the training corpus). No quantitative word analogy or similarity benchmarks are reported relative to SGNS baselines, despite claims about linear regularities.
- “Dirichlet likelihood” is a heuristic regularizer: The term λ sum_k (α−1) log p_jk is presented as a likelihood, but λ is a free scaling not tied to a proper Bayesian objective, and p_jk are outputs of a softmax. This is fine as regularization, but calling it likelihood is misleading. Moreover, the choice α = n−1 is ambiguous (do you mean 1/n?), and there is no sensitivity analysis for α and λ. The lack of ablations leaves unclear how much sparsity contributes to coherence and whether topics degrade without it (authors state they do, but provide no numbers).
- Methodological and notation clarity issues: There are several confusing or inconsistent statements: “softmax transforms latent vectors initialized in R300 onto the simplex” (should be R^n where n is number of topics), ambiguous notation in Eq. (4) with ellipses mixed into a sum, unclear whether vectors are normalized before summation, and what dropout rates are used. The training details for SGNS (subsampling, dynamic window, learning rate schedule) are not specified, though known to materially impact results.
- Confounds in 20NG: Using pretrained word vectors for 20NG but random initialization for HN complicates interpretation. It remains unclear if the reported coherence depends on pretraining rather than the proposed architecture. No report of runtime, convergence, or stability across seeds.

Questions to Authors
- Baselines: Can you report Cv and (optionally) UMass or NPMI for classic LDA and PV-DM/PV-DBOW on 20NG with identical preprocessing? Also report a neural topic model baseline (e.g., NVDM) if feasible. For HN, compute NPMI on the in-domain corpus to quantify topic coherence.
- Ablations: Please provide ablations removing (a) the Dirichlet regularizer, (b) the convex topic mixture (i.e., free dense doc vectors), and (c) weight tying for pivot/target embeddings. How do these affect topic coherence and downstream document classification?
- Clarify α and λ: Do you mean α=1/n? How sensitive are results to α and λ? Can you report sparsity statistics (e.g., average number of topics with p_jk > 0.05) and coherence vs. sparsity curves?
- Relation to doc2vec PV-DM: PV-DM sums doc and word vectors to predict targets. Beyond the convex mixture/sparsity constraint, are there other key differences (e.g., weight tying, negative sampling choices)? Can you show where lda2vec outperforms PV-DM quantitatively?
- Implementation specifics: Did you use word subsampling, dynamic windows, or vector normalization? What dropout probability is used on embeddings? Are topic vectors constrained/normalized?

Additional comments
- The approach is promising and practical. Strengthening the empirical section with solid baselines, ablations, and in-domain coherence would substantially improve the paper.
- Improve notation and clean up reference formatting. Include runtime and convergence behavior, and discuss stability across random seeds.
