- Proposal: lda2vec augments SGNS with document vectors formed as convex mixtures of learned topic vectors and a Dirichlet prior to encourage sparse document–topic proportions.
- Training: end-to-end autodiff optimization combining SGNS loss with a Dirichlet log-likelihood regularizer on simplex-normalized document–topic weights.
- Evaluation: topic coherence (Cv) on 20 Newsgroups, qualitative topics and analogies on Hacker News, and open-source implementation.

Strengths
- Simple, practical objective that’s easy to implement in modern autodiff frameworks and scales to large corpora.
- Unified embedding space for words, topics, and documents enabling interpretability and semantic composition.
- Dirichlet-inspired regularization encourages sparse, LDA-like document–topic mixtures and yields coherent qualitative topics.
- Retains SGNS properties: analogical structure and in-domain relations appear preserved.
- Public code and operational details (negative sampling, dropout, initialization) aid reproducibility.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: lacks comparison and positioning relative to existing topic+embedding models (TWE, DocNADE, Gaussian LDA, neural variational models).
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation and baselines: only Cv on 20NG reported, no comparisons to LDA, NMF, DocNADE, NVDM, Doc2Vec+LDA, or downstream task results.
- Ambiguities in method description: unclear α notation (1/n vs n−1), inconsistent dimensionality statements (R300 vs topic dimension), and undocumented dropout rationale.
- Modeling limitations: objective is a regularizer-driven hybrid, not a coherent probabilistic model or ELBO, and lacks key ablations (remove document vector, remove Dirichlet term, vary λ/α, shared vs separate embeddings).
- Reporting/reproducibility gaps: several hyperparameters (dropout, learning rate/schedule, subsampling, negatives), training time, memory, convergence behavior, and seed variability are not fully reported.

Questions to authors
- Clarify α: do you set α = 1/n (sparsity-inducing) or α = n−1? How sensitive are results to α and λ?
- What is the exact dimensionality of the latent variables passed through softmax to produce p_j (R^n with n topics?), and what does R300 refer to?
- Please report baselines on 20NG (LDA Cv, Doc2Vec classification, neural topic models) to contextualize reported Cv ≈ 0.56–0.57.
- Can coherence on HN be computed using in-domain co-occurrence statistics (e.g., NPMI on HN) instead of Palmetto’s external corpus?
- Provide ablations: (i) α=1/remove Dirichlet term, (ii) c_j = w_j only (no document vector), (iii) shared vs separate input/output embeddings.
- What dropout rates were used on word and document vectors, and how sensitive are results to these choices?

Additional comments
- Report topic sparsity metrics (mean entropy of p_j), topic diversity, and topic overlap alongside Cv.
- Add downstream evaluations (e.g., 20NG classification, retrieval, clustering) to demonstrate utility of document/topic representations.
- Expand related work to include neural autoregressive topic models and topical word embeddings; fix missing/misformatted references.
- Mention numerical stability measures for log p (epsilon-clipping) if used.
- Clarify whether topic and word vectors are L2-normalized before computing cosine nearest neighbors.
