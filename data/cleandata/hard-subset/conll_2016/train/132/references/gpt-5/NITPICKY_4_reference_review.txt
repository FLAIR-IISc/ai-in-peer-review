Summary and claimed contributions
This paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid objective that augments the word2vec Skipgram with Negative Sampling (SGNS) loss by (i) introducing a document vector that is added to the pivot word vector to form the context, and (ii) constraining the document vector to be a convex combination of shared topic vectors via a simplex parameterization with a Dirichlet-style sparsity regularizer. The model is trained end-to-end with automatic differentiation. Empirical evaluation consists of average Cv topic coherence on 20 Newsgroups and qualitative analyses (top words, nearest neighbors, analogies) on 20NG and a large Hacker News comments corpus.

As I see it, the main contributions are:
1) A simple hybrid objective combining SGNS with a document-topic simplex mixture regularized by a Dirichlet-like term, yielding joint embeddings for words, topics, and documents in one space.
2) Qualitative evidence that the learned topics are interpretable and that learned word vectors exhibit domain-specific analogies.
3) An implementation in an autodiff framework with code release.

Strengths
- Simple, intuitively motivated architecture. Summing a document vector with a pivot word vector reuses a well-known compositional property of embeddings; parameterizing the document as a mixture over shared topic vectors encourages interpretability and offers a clean way to inspect topics by nearest neighbors.
- Interpretability emphasis. The use of a simplex constraint and a subunit concentration parameter to encourage sparse document-topic proportions directly targets interpretability; reporting Cv (albeit on one dataset) is aligned with common topic quality evaluation practice.
- Joint space for words, topics, and documents. Having aligned vectors enables straightforward inspection (nearest tokens to topics, topic labels) and supports analogy-style probing that reflects corpus-specific structure.
- Scales to a larger corpus. The Hacker News experiment (75M tokens, 110k types) suggests the approach is at least implementable at reasonable scale, and the noun-phrase tokenization is a thoughtful attempt to adapt to domain-specific vocabulary.
- Open-source implementation. Availability of code and use of autodiff frameworks lowers the barrier to reproduction and extension.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty and missing positioning versus closely related work. The core training signal (doc vector + word vector predicts target) is extremely close to the Distributed Memory variant of paragraph vectors (Le & Mikolov, 2014), which the paper cites but does not compare against or clearly differentiate from. The idea of integrating topics/Dirichlet structure with embeddings also appears in prior work (e.g., Topical Word Embeddings, Gaussian LDA variants, neural variational topic models), none of which are discussed or compared. Without a thorough related work treatment and empirical comparisons, the incremental contribution is unclear.
- Inadequate empirical evaluation and lack of baselines. For 20 Newsgroups, only average Cv is reported, with no comparisons to LDA, doc2vec, or other neural topic models; no statistical significance, no per-topic analysis, and no downstream evaluations (classification, clustering, retrieval). For the Hacker News corpus, evaluation is purely anecdotal (top words, nearest neighbors, cherry-picked analogies) with no quantitative metrics due to tokenization mismatch. The paper does not demonstrate that lda2vec delivers better topic quality, better embeddings, or better document representations than strong baselines.
- Technical and notation issues that obscure correctness. Several inconsistencies/ambiguities:
  - The Dirichlet “likelihood” term is written as Ld = λ Σjk (α−1) log pjk, but this is a prior over p, not a likelihood; its scaling with λ is ad hoc. The claim “we set α = n−1” is ambiguous: do the authors mean α = 1/n (sparse) or α = n−1 (non-sparse for n>2)? This is critical to understanding sparsity.
  - The softmax is said to map “latent vectors initialized in R300 onto the simplex defined by pjk,” but the simplex over topics is Rn with n topics, not R300. This suggests a dimensionality mismatch or a typographical error.
  - Equation (4) mixes indices and bounds (pjn with n as an index vs number of topics), and k/n are overloaded throughout.
  - The model uses a single set of word embeddings for both input and output roles in SGNS, diverging from standard word2vec practice; this is not justified or ablated.
  - The minibatch approximation to Ld is described as “proportional to the minibatch size divided by the size of the total corpus” without precise derivation; given document-specific parameters, more care is needed.
- Missing inference story for unseen documents. Document-topic proportions appear as free parameters learned only for training documents. There is no description of how to infer p for new documents (e.g., optimizing logits with fixed word/topic vectors, amortized inference, or MAP with the same regularizer), nor any runtime or convergence considerations.
- Hyperparameterization and ablations are underspecified. Key choices (dropout rates, batch size, learning rate schedule, vector dimensions per experiment, negative sampling exponent effects, α and λ sensitivity) are not systematically explored. The claim that α=1 leads to incoherent topics is not substantiated with metrics.
- Presentation and formatting quality are poor. The manuscript contains numerous typographical errors, malformed references (e.g., “Mikolov et al.,? \Q2013\E”, duplicated/incorrect author lists), inconsistent notation, and figures that are difficult to read (ASCII artifacts, missing captions details). These issues impede reproducibility and clarity.

Questions to authors
- Please clarify the Dirichlet term: is α set to 1/n or to n−1? How sensitive are results to α and λ? Why is this called a “likelihood”?
- What is the dimensionality of the logits fed to the softmax for p? Rn (number of topics) or R300? If the latter, how is the projection to n topics defined?
- How is inference performed for unseen documents? Provide algorithmic details, objective, initialization, and wall-clock/runtime behavior.
- Why use a single embedding matrix for both input and output words in SGNS? Does using separate matrices (as in word2vec) affect topic coherence or analogy performance?
- Please provide quantitative baselines: LDA (with Cv), doc2vec (document classification), and a neural topic model. Can you report perplexity and/or downstream task performance?
- Can you include ablations on dropout, β, α, λ, and the effect of pretrained vs random initialization?
- For the Hacker News corpus, can you devise a quantitative evaluation compatible with your tokenization (e.g., intrinsic coherence computed on your corpus, extrinsic classification), rather than relying solely on qualitative examples?

Overall, while the idea is appealing and the implementation practical, the manuscript’s novelty relative to doc2vec and topical embedding literature is insufficiently established, and the empirical evidence is too weak and anecdotal. The numerous technical and presentation issues further reduce confidence.
