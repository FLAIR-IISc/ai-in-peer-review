- Paper summary: Proposes lda2vec, augmenting SGNS with document vectors constrained as sparse convex mixtures of shared topic vectors so words, topics, and documents share an embedding space.

- Contribution 1: Neural formulation that forces document embeddings to be sparse convex combinations of topic vectors within an SGNS framework for interpretable topics.

- Contribution 2: Empirical evidence of coherent topics on 20 Newsgroups and qualitative topics/analogies on Hacker News, with an autodiff training recipe and released code.

- Contribution 3: Practical design choices (weight tying, embedding dropout, Dirichlet-style sparsity) and an open-source implementation.

- Strength: Conceptually simple and easy to implement with autodiff/GPUs, avoiding complex variational inference.

- Strength: Interpretability via shared embedding space enabling topic inspection by nearest-neighbor words.

- Strength: Sparsity mechanism (Dirichlet-like regularizer + simplex) encourages LDA-style sparse document-topic mixtures improving interpretability.

- Strength: Reasonable topic coherence on 20NG (mean Cv ≈ 0.56–0.57) indicating viability as a topic model.

- Strength: Scales to large corpora (Hacker News) and shows corpus-specific semantics and analogies.

- Weakness: Limited/incomplete baselines — no quantitative comparison to LDA, PV-DM/PV-DBOW, neural topic models, or other hybrid methods.

- Weakness: Evaluation is mostly qualitative for large corpus and lacks task-based metrics (classification/clustering, in-domain coherence, analogy benchmarks).

- Weakness: “Dirichlet likelihood” is a heuristic regularizer with unclear grounding; α and λ choices and sensitivity are ambiguous and unanalyzed.

- Weakness: Methodological and notation clarity issues (confusing statements, ambiguous equations, missing training details like subsampling/windowing/learning rate and dropout).

- Weakness: Confounds in 20NG results due to pretrained word vectors vs random init on HN; no runtime or stability (seed) reporting.

- Question: Please report Cv/NPMI/UMass for classic LDA and PV-DM/PV-DBOW on 20NG with identical preprocessing, and NPMI for HN.

- Question: Provide ablations removing (a) Dirichlet regularizer, (b) convex topic mixture, and (c) weight tying, and report effects on coherence and downstream tasks.

- Question: Clarify α and λ (do you mean α=1/n?), report sensitivity, and give sparsity stats (e.g., average topics with p_jk>0.05) and coherence vs sparsity curves.

- Question: Clarify relation to doc2vec PV-DM beyond convex sparsity and show quantitative comparisons where lda2vec outperforms PV-DM.

- Question: Provide implementation specifics: word subsampling, dynamic windows, learning rate schedule, embedding normalization, dropout probability, and any constraints on topic vectors.

- Additional comment: Approach is promising but needs stronger empirical baselines, clearer notation, runtime/convergence reporting, and stability analysis across seeds.
