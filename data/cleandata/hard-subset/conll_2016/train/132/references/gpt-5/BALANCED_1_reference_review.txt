Summary
This paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model that augments the Skip-gram with Negative Sampling (SGNS) objective by adding a document vector to the context representation. Each document vector is constrained to be a convex mixture over shared topic vectors, and a Dirichlet-inspired regularizer encourages sparse document–topic proportions for interpretability. The model is trained end-to-end with automatic differentiation, learning word, topic, and document embeddings in a shared space. Experiments on 20 Newsgroups report topic coherence (Cv) and qualitative examples; a larger Hacker News corpus is used for qualitative topic and analogy demonstrations.

Main contributions (as I see them)
- A simple SGNS-based objective that yields interpretable, sparse document–topic mixtures by expressing document vectors as convex combinations of shared topic vectors and regularizing with a Dirichlet-style term.
- Joint training of word, topic, and document embeddings in a shared space that preserves word2vec-like linear regularities while enabling LDA-like topic interpretability.
- An open-source implementation demonstrating that such hybrid topic–embedding models can be trained with standard autodiff and GPUs.

Strengths
- Conceptual simplicity and interpretability: The architecture (context = word + document; document = mixture over topic embeddings) is intuitive and directly supports inspection of topic vectors via nearest words, akin to LDA topic-word lists. This is appealing to practitioners seeking interpretable embeddings.
- Practicality and accessibility: The model uses standard components (SGNS with negative sampling, softmax-constrained mixtures, Adam) and autodiff, lowering barriers to implementation and extension. Code availability is a plus for reproducibility.
- Qualitative evidence is persuasive: The topics shown for 20 Newsgroups map well to known categories, and the Hacker News examples capture community-specific vocabulary, topics, and analogies (e.g., font families, tech hardware, compensation).
- Sparse mixture regularization: The Dirichlet-inspired penalty provides a clear knob to promote sparsity and interpretability in document-topic proportions, addressing a common pain point in dense doc embeddings and some neural topic models.

Weaknesses
- Limited empirical rigor and missing baselines: There is no quantitative comparison to strong baselines such as LDA/ProdLDA, NVDM/Neural LDA, ETM/TopicVec/TWE, GloVe+LDA pipelines, or doc2vec. On 20 Newsgroups, reporting Cv without direct baselines (same preprocessing and number of topics) makes it difficult to assess whether lda2vec improves topic quality. On Hacker News, no quantitative metrics are reported at all.
- Evaluation breadth: The paper focuses almost exclusively on topic coherence and qualitative analogies. It omits commonly reported evaluations for such models: (i) document-level classification or retrieval using the learned proportions; (ii) held-out likelihood/perplexity (or ELBO-like proxy) for topic models; (iii) standard word similarity/analogy benchmarks (or in-domain analogies with quantitative accuracy); (iv) topic diversity measures and stability across runs.
- Regularizer lacks principled probabilistic treatment: The “Dirichlet likelihood” is effectively a regularizer on softmaxed proportions with tunable strength λ, not a proper probabilistic model with inference. This is fine pragmatically, but the paper blurs the distinction and does not analyze sensitivity to α and λ, the effect on learned topics, or alternatives (e.g., entmax/sparsemax, L1 on logits).
- Ablation and design choices under-explored: Key choices are not ablated—including tying input/output embeddings (SGNS typically uses separate matrices), summing word and doc vectors vs. other compositions, the necessity of pretrained word vectors on 20NG, dropout rates, number of negatives, and the impact of α=1/n. The claim that removing the Dirichlet term leads to incoherent topics is not backed by quantitative evidence.
- Positioning w.r.t. prior work: The related work omits or under-positions closely related neural topic/embedding hybrids and neural topic models (e.g., TWE/TopicVec, NVDM, ProdLDA, ETM, SCHOLAR). The proposed formulation is close in spirit to learning topic embeddings tied to word embeddings; the novelty relative to these lines is unclear without direct discussion and empirical comparison.

Questions to authors
- Can you provide direct Cv comparisons on 20 Newsgroups against: (i) LDA (same preprocessing, n topics), (ii) ProdLDA or NVDM, and (iii) ETM/TopicVec/TWE? How does lda2vec fare in topic quality and diversity?
- How sensitive are results to α and λ? Please include a sweep and report effects on sparsity (e.g., average number of topics per document above threshold), Cv, and qualitative topic interpretability.
- What is the impact of tying input and output embeddings in SGNS? Does untying improve word-level performance or topics?
- Could you quantify document representation quality via a simple linear classifier for 20 Newsgroups labels trained on the learned document proportions? This would illuminate whether the mixtures capture discriminative structure.
- On 20NG, how much do pretrained word vectors contribute? Please report performance with random initialization to separate benefits of the model from external pretraining.
- Training efficiency: What are runtime and memory characteristics vs. LDA/ETM on the reported corpora? Any issues with negative sampling scaling for large vocabularies?

Recommendation
Weak reject. The paper presents a clean and potentially useful hybrid that encourages interpretable document–topic mixtures within an SGNS framework, and the qualitative results are promising. However, the current empirical evaluation is too limited to support acceptance at a venue with CoNLL’s standards: there are no head-to-head baselines, little quantitative breadth beyond Cv (and none on the large corpus), no ablations, and insufficient positioning relative to closely related neural topic models and topic–embedding hybrids.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add rigorous comparisons to LDA/ProdLDA/NVDM/ETM/TopicVec/TWE on 20NG (Cv, diversity, classification) and at least one larger corpus with quantitative metrics.
- Provide ablations for α, λ, pretraining, tied vs. untied embeddings, and the sum composition choice.
- Clarify the regularizer’s role as a sparsity prior, include sensitivity and stability analyses across random seeds, and consider alternative sparsity-inducing mechanisms.
- Include runtime and scalability measurements.
- Expand related work and clearly articulate novelty and tradeoffs.
