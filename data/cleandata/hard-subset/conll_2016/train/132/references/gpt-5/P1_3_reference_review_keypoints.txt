- Proposes lda2vec: add a document vector to the SGNS pivot word vector and constrain document vectors as convex mixtures of shared topic vectors, with a Dirichlet-style regularizer for sparse document–topic proportions.  
- Learns word, topic, and document vectors jointly in one embedding space using autodiff; demonstrates qualitative topics and analogies on 20 Newsgroups and Hacker News.  
- Releases code and preprocessing scripts.  
- Central contribution: simple architecture tying SGNS to interpretable, LDA-like sparse topic mixtures via additive composition.  
- Shows qualitative interpretability of topics and embedding operations (nearest neighbors, analogies) on both general and domain-specific corpora.  
- Empirical observation: adding a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity regularizer improves topic interpretability/coherence.  
- Conceptual strengths: simplicity, practicality, and minimal architectural changes with reproducible implementation.  
- Interpretability strength: convex topic mixtures and sparsity yield human-interpretable topic proportions and easy topic inspection.  
- Scalability strength: trains with negative sampling and Adam on GPUs; scales to a 75M-token corpus and handles phrase/entity tokenization.  
- Unified embedding space advantage: words, topics, and documents live in the same space enabling simple downstream operations and labeling.  
- Good empirical hygiene: reasonably described hyperparameters and use of Palmetto Cv for topic coherence on 20NG.  
- Main novelty concern: limited novelty relative to prior topical-embedding and neural topic model literature and insufficient related work coverage.  
- Lacks quantitative baselines: no comparisons to LDA, doc2vec, neural topic models, or downstream tasks; Cv scores lack comparable baselines.  
- Dirichlet regularizer ambiguity: objective not derived from a coherent generative model and minibatch scaling of the global term is ad hoc without systematic ablation.  
- Methodological confounds: different initialization strategies (pretrained vs random) across datasets and lack of ablations for hyperparameters and architectural choices.  
- Missing ablations: no clear quantification of contributions from document vector vs Dirichlet term, or effects of tying embeddings, dropout, topic count, and β exponent.  
- Clarity issues: imprecise notation/equations, unclear embedding dimensionalities (300 vs 256), and confusing figures.  
- Questions requested: quantitative comparisons to LDA/doc2vec/neural topic models, sensitivity analysis for α and λ, exact embedding dimensionalities and tying, pretrained vs random init effects, minibatch scaling of Ld, and ablations removing components.  
- Suggested improvements: stronger probabilistic framing (e.g., amortized inference or connection to ProdLDA), alternative coherence evaluation for Hacker News, and more rigorous baselines and ablations.  
- Overall verdict: practical and appealing idea, but empirical evidence, positioning, and ablation/benchmarking are insufficient for acceptance in current form.
