Summary
The paper proposes lda2vec, a simple hybrid that augments skip-gram with negative sampling (SGNS) by adding a document vector to the pivot word vector when predicting targets, and constraining each document vector to be a convex mixture of shared topic vectors. A Dirichlet-inspired regularizer encourages sparse document–topic proportions for interpretability. Word, topic, and document vectors are learned jointly in a common embedding space using automatic differentiation. Experiments on 20 Newsgroups (with pretrained word vectors) and a large Hacker News comments corpus (random init) show qualitative topics and word analogies; the 20NG topics are evaluated with Palmetto Cv coherence.

Contributions (as I see them)
- A simple architecture that ties SGNS word embeddings with interpretable document-topic mixtures in the same space via additive composition of document and word vectors, encouraging sparse, LDA-like proportions. This is the central technical idea and is straightforward to implement with autodiff.
- Qualitative demonstration that the resulting topic vectors are interpretable and that the joint space supports standard embedding operations (similarity, analogies), including on a large, domain-specific corpus. Code and data preprocessing scripts are released.
- An empirical observation that adding a Dirichlet-style sparsity regularizer over softmax-normalized document-topic weights noticeably improves interpretability/coherence.

Strengths
- Conceptual simplicity and practicality: The sum of a document vector and pivot word vector is an intuitive way to inject global document information into local SGNS context prediction while maintaining the linear properties of embeddings. The approach requires minimal architectural changes and can be reproduced in standard frameworks; code is released.
- Interpretability via sparse mixtures: Constraining document vectors to be convex combinations of shared topic vectors and promoting sparsity yields proportions that are human-interpretable and allow direct inspection of topics by nearest-neighbor words. The qualitative topics on 20NG (e.g., sci.space, sci.crypt) and Hacker News (e.g., Bitcoin, compensation) are coherent.
- Scalability and applicability: The method trains with negative sampling and Adam on GPUs and scales to a 75M-token corpus. Using phrase/entity tokenization on Hacker News demonstrates the model’s flexibility in specialized vocabularies and its ability to learn meaningful domain-specific relations and analogies.
- Unified embedding space: Placing words, documents, and topics in the same space enables simple downstream operations (topic labeling via nearest neighbors, analogies like Silicon Valley ≈ California + technology). This is a practical advantage over classic LDA where topics are distributions.
- Empirical hygiene and transparency: Implementation details (window size, negatives, dropout, β exponent), preprocessing pipelines, and hyperparameters are reasonably described. The use of Palmetto Cv for topic coherence on 20NG follows common practice.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to prior art and unclear positioning: The paper does not sufficiently compare to or cite related hybrids such as topical word embeddings (e.g., TWE), Gaussian LDA/embedding-informed topic models, neural topic models (NVDM/ProdLDA), or doc2vec. The core ideas—adding document vectors to word contexts and imposing simplex/sparsity over topic proportions—are incremental and overlap with established lines of work; a thorough related work section and empirical comparisons are needed to establish novelty.
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation and baselines: On 20NG, only lda2vec coherence is reported; there is no comparison to LDA, doc2vec+clustering, GloVe+topic models, or neural topic baselines. No held-out likelihood/perplexity, no downstream tasks (classification/retrieval), and no human evaluation are provided. The reported average Cv (e.g., ~0.56) is not interpretable without baselines under the same preprocessing. On Hacker News, topic coherence is omitted due to tokenization mismatch, leaving only qualitative anecdotes.
- Ambiguity of the “Dirichlet likelihood” objective: The regularizer is applied directly to softmax-normalized proportions as λ Σ (α−1) log pjk. This is not derived from a coherent generative model; minibatch scaling of this global term is ad hoc. There is no ablation quantifying its effect beyond a qualitative claim that α=1 yields incoherence. Sensitivity to α and λ is not explored systematically.
- Methodological confounds: On 20NG, word vectors are initialized from pretrained SGNS and updated; on Hacker News, random init is used. This makes it difficult to attribute coherence gains to the architecture vs. pretrained embeddings. The effect of tying input/output embeddings, dropout on document/word vectors, number of topics/hidden units, and negative sampling exponent β is not ablated beyond a small β sweep.
- Clarity and presentation issues: Some equations and notations are imprecise (e.g., summation indices, using the same embeddings for input/output without discussion, the role of 256 “hidden units” for topics vs embedding dimension). Figures include hard-to-parse artifacts. The related work section is sparse and misses key references.

Questions to authors
- Can you provide quantitative comparisons on 20NG against LDA (same number of topics), doc2vec (for document clustering/classification), and a neural topic model (e.g., ProdLDA/NVDM) using Cv and, if possible, human judgments?
- How sensitive are results to α and λ? Please include an ablation varying α ∈ {1/n, 0.1, 1.0} and λ across orders of magnitude, and report coherence and sparsity (e.g., entropy of p_j).
- What is the exact embedding dimensionality for words/topics/documents in each experiment (the text mentions 300 and 256)? Are input and output embeddings tied? Do topic vectors have additional regularization?
- How do pretrained vs. random initialization affect 20NG topic coherence? Please report with/without pretrained embeddings.
- How is Ld scaled in minibatches to approximate the full-corpus objective? Does this introduce bias for varying document lengths/frequencies?
- Can you include an ablation removing the document vector (pure SGNS) and removing the Dirichlet term to quantify their individual contributions?

Additional comments
- A more rigorous probabilistic framing (e.g., amortized inference over simplex with a proper prior, or connections to ProdLDA) would strengthen the model’s foundations.
- If Palmetto mismatch prevents coherence on Hacker News, consider computing intrinsic coherence with an external reference corpus aligned to your vocabulary or reporting human topic ratings.
- The qualitative results are promising; with stronger baselines and ablations, this could be a solid contribution.

Overall, the idea is practical and appealing, but the current empirical evidence and positioning are not yet sufficient for acceptance.
