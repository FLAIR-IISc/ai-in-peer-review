- Introduces lda2vec: a hybrid model combining word embeddings and LDA to learn document vectors; implemented in Chainer and evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.
- Principled hybridization: integrates document and word vectors using SGNS and a Dirichlet prior to promote interpretable, sparse topic mixtures.
- Practical implementation: Chainer-based, GPU-accelerated code improves scalability and reproducibility.
- Demonstrated on diverse datasets: shows competitive topic coherence and meaningful word analogies/visualizations on both benchmark and real-world corpora.
- Clear motivation: addresses the gap between semantic-rich embeddings and interpretable topic models.
- Sound methodology: well-defined formulation, Dirichlet prior for sparsity, and use of dropout to prevent co-adaptation.
- Empirical validation: quantitative coherence scores and qualitative examples support effectiveness.
- Reproducibility: code release enables verification and extension by other researchers.
- Efficient implementation: leverages automatic differentiation and GPU training.
- Limited novelty: similar embedding–topic hybrids exist and related work discussion is incomplete.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity & limited ablation: lacks systematic studies on λ, α, β, dropout, and negative sampling.
- Evaluation narrowness: relies mainly on topic coherence and would benefit from downstream task evaluations (e.g., classification, IR).
- Preprocessing confound on Hacker News: noun-phrase tokenization may inflate results; comparison to standard tokenization is needed.
- Request to authors: expand related-work comparison to prior embedding–topic hybrids.
- Request to authors: provide ablation/sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters and training choices.
- Request to authors: evaluate learned representations on downstream tasks (document classification, IR).
- Request to authors: report results using standard word tokenization for Hacker News to assess preprocessing impact.
- Figure/layout suggestion: clarify Figure 1 to better show information flow.
- Explain Dirichlet choice: expand on why a low concentration parameter (α < 1) is chosen for interpretability.
- Add complexity discussion: include brief analysis of computational complexity.
- Overall: solid, practical contribution with reproducible code; minor revisions recommended to address novelty, ablations, and broader evaluation.
