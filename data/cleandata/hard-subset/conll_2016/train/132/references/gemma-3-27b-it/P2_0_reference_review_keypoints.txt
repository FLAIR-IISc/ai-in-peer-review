- Introduces lda2vec: a hybrid model combining Skip-gram word embeddings with Dirichlet-regularized topic mixtures to learn document and word vectors in a shared space.  
- Practical, relatively simple integration implemented in Chainer, demonstrating feasibility.  
- Jointly learns document and word representations so semantic word vectors influence document-level understanding.  
- Empirical evaluation on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News with coherence metrics and baselines showing interpretable topics.  
- Clear motivation to combine topic models’ interpretability with embeddings’ semantic power.  
- Sound methodology leveraging Skip-gram, Dirichlet likelihood for sparsity, and automatic differentiation.  
- Demonstrated interpretability with convincing qualitative examples and topic coherence scores (Figure 3).  
- Successful application to Hacker News, capturing domain-specific relationships (Figures 4–5).  
- Open-source code provided for reproducibility.  
- Limited novelty: combining embeddings and topic models is not highly novel and needs clearer differentiation from prior work (e.g., NTMs).  
- Evaluation overly focused on topic coherence; lacks downstream task evaluations like classification or retrieval.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity poorly analyzed; choices (e.g., λ=200, α=n−1) seem arbitrary.  
- Lacks comparison to stronger baselines, especially neural or embedding-based topic models (e.g., NVDM).  
- Requests ablation study on sensitivity to Dirichlet concentration α and regularization λ.  
- Asks whether pre-trained embeddings (GloVe/Word2Vec) were tried and their impact.  
- Questions scalability to very large datasets and possible optimizations.  
- Suggests porting implementation to other frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow) beyond Chainer.  
- Minor issues: cramped figure formatting, inconsistent reference formatting, and introduction could better summarize existing hybrid approaches.  
- Overall: valuable, well-implemented contribution with promising results and open code; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
