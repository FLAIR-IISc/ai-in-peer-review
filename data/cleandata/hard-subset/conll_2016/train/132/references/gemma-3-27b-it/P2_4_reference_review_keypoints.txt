- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, combining skip-gram embeddings with Dirichlet-constrained topic mixtures to produce dense yet interpretable document representations, demonstrated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.

- Contribution 1: Conceptually simple hybrid that sums word and document vectors and constrains document vectors as mixtures of topic vectors.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates adaptability by applying the method to both a standard benchmark and a real-world Hacker News corpus.

- Contribution 3: Uses a Dirichlet prior on document-topic distributions to encourage sparsity and interpretability.

- Strength 1: Clear and well-motivated explanation of why to combine topic models and word embeddings.

- Strength 2: Sound experimental methodology using topic coherence and semantic relationship evaluations.

- Strength 3: Code is available, documented, and unit tested, supporting reproducibility.

- Strength 4: Effective use of automatic differentiation (Chainer) to simplify implementation and training.

- Strength 5: Qualitative results show convincing topics and interesting semantic relationships on Hacker News.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—core ideas are not entirely new and related work coverage is insufficient.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison to strong, recent baselines like neural variational document models or advanced embedding methods.

- Weakness 3: Overreliance on topic coherence metric and insufficient evaluation on downstream tasks (e.g., classification, retrieval).

- Weakness 4: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (Dirichlet alpha, negative sampling exponent beta) and no ablation study.

- Weakness 5: Limited discussion of scalability to very large datasets beyond the presented corpora.

- Question 1: Please expand related work comparisons to other embedding–topic hybrid approaches and clarify differentiation.

- Question 2: Can you compare lda2vec to recent topic models (e.g., NVDM) and embeddings (e.g., Sentence-BERT) on standard benchmarks?

- Question 3: How sensitive is lda2vec to hyperparameters like alpha and beta—can you provide an ablation study?

- Question 4: How does training cost scale with dataset size and number of topics, and what are scalability limits?

- Question 5: How does noun-phrase tokenization for Hacker News affect comparability to standard word-level embeddings and topic interpretability?

- Additional comment 1: Improve Figure 1 with clearer labels and better design.

- Additional comment 2: Reference formatting is inconsistent and should be fixed.

- Overall assessment: Paper is a valuable, well-executed contribution with reproducible code; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen baselines and related work.
