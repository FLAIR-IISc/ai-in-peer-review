- Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model combining LDA-style topic mixtures with word embeddings to get interpretable document vectors that aid word prediction.  
- Jointly trains word embeddings and topic models, preserving semantic relationships and producing coherent topics.  
- Uses automatic differentiation (Chainer) for efficient training and provides an open-source implementation.  
- Evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News, showing coherent topics and domain-specific vocabulary learning.  
- Clear motivation: convincingly argues benefits of combining topic models and embeddings.  
- Sound methodology: mathematical formulation is clear and includes a Dirichlet likelihood term for sparsity and interpretability.  
- Strong empirical results: qualitative word analogies and dataset-specific demonstrations support effectiveness.  
- Open-source code, documentation, and tests enhance reproducibility and community uptake.  
- Thoughtful design choices such as dropout and negative sampling parameter selection (beta=0.75).  
- Limited baselines: lacks comparisons to recent neural topic models (e.g., NTM, ProdLDA) and other hybrid approaches.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper lacks ablation/sensitivity analysis for lambda, alpha, and dropout.  
- Coherence metric reliance: depends mainly on Cv metric; should include other metrics (UMass, NPMI) or human evaluation.  
- Scalability unclear: needs runtime/memory benchmarks and discussion of performance on larger datasets.  
- Requests ablation study on hyperparameters and their impact on coherence and qualitative topics.  
- Asks for direct comparisons to recent neural topic models in coherence and downstream tasks.  
- Seeks computational cost details (training time and memory) across dataset sizes.  
- Queries effect of training word vectors from scratch versus using pre-trained vectors.  
- Suggests porting implementation to more common frameworks (PyTorch/TensorFlow) for broader accessibility.  
- Diagrams, writing, and references are helpful and clear.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated contribution with minor revisions requested; recommends acceptance.
