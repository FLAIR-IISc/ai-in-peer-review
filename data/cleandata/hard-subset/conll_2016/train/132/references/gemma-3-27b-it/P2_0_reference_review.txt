## Review for "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

**Summary:**

This paper presents *lda2vec*, a model that combines Skip-gram word embeddings with Dirichlet-optimized topic mixtures. The core idea is to leverage the strengths of both approaches: the semantic representation power of word embeddings and the interpretability of topic models. The model learns document vectors as mixtures of topic vectors, while simultaneously training word embeddings, effectively embedding both into a shared space.  The authors demonstrate the method on the Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News datasets.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A practical and relatively simple integration of word embeddings and topic models:** The paper presents a functional hybrid model. While the idea of combining these approaches isn't entirely novel, *lda2vec* provides a concrete implementation using modern deep learning frameworks (Chainer) and demonstrates its feasibility.
2. **Demonstration of joint learning of document and word representations:** The paper successfully shows that word vectors and document representations can be learned simultaneously, creating a shared embedding space. This is a key element in allowing the semantic relationships captured in word embeddings to influence document-level understanding.
3. **Empirical evaluation on two datasets:** The experiments on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News, along with appropriate baselines and coherence metrics, provide evidence for the method's effectiveness, particularly in topic interpretability.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind combining topic models and word embeddings, highlighting the benefits of each and the potential for a more comprehensive representation. The authors effectively frame the problem as a need to overcome the limitations of both purely sparse (topic models) and purely dense (word embeddings) representations.
2. **Sound Methodology:** The model design is logically structured and builds upon established techniques (Skip-gram, LDA). The use of the Dirichlet likelihood to encourage sparsity in document mixtures is a sensible addition, improving interpretability. The framework leveraging automatic differentiation is also a strong point, facilitating experimentation.
3. **Demonstrated Interpretability:**  The results on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset, coupled with the topic coherence scores and example topics provided, show that the model can learn interpretable topics. The qualitative analysis in Figure 3 is convincing.
4. **Successful Application to a Real-World Dataset:** Applying *lda2vec* to the Hacker News dataset is a significant strength.  The results in Figures 4 and 5 demonstrate the model's ability to capture domain-specific knowledge and learn meaningful relationships within a complex, evolving corpus.
5. **Open-Source Code:** The availability of the code base is a valuable asset for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty:** While the implementation is solid, the underlying idea of combining word embeddings and topic models isn't groundbreaking. The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work and a clearer articulation of what specifically differentiates *lda2vec* from existing approaches.  For example, existing work like NTMs (Neural Topic Models) or approaches utilizing pre-trained embeddings *within* topic models aren't given sufficient attention.
2. **Evaluation Primarily Focused on Coherence:** While topic coherence is a useful metric, it doesn't fully capture the performance of a topic model.  The paper would be strengthened by including downstream task evaluations (e.g., document classification, information retrieval) to demonstrate the practical utility of the learned representations.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions some hyperparameter tuning but doesn't provide a detailed analysis of the model’s sensitivity to different parameter settings. This makes it difficult to assess the robustness of the results and provides little guidance for practitioners wanting to apply the model to new datasets. The selection of lambda=200 and alpha = n-1 feels somewhat arbitrary.
4. **Lack of Comparison with Stronger Baselines:** The paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison with state-of-the-art topic modeling techniques, especially those that also incorporate word embeddings or neural networks. Specifically, comparison with Neural Variational Document Models (NVDM) or similar approaches would be helpful.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of the Dirichlet concentration parameter α and the regularization strength λ? Could you provide a more detailed ablation study exploring these parameters?
2.  Have you explored incorporating pre-trained word embeddings (e.g., GloVe, Word2Vec) into *lda2vec*?  If so, what impact did this have on performance and interpretability?
3.  Do you foresee any challenges scaling *lda2vec* to very large datasets (e.g., billions of tokens)?  What optimizations could be employed to address these challenges?
4.  You mention the use of Chainer. Have you considered porting the implementation to other deep learning frameworks like PyTorch or TensorFlow?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The formatting of some of the figures is a little cramped.
*   The references section could benefit from being more consistently formatted.
*   The introduction could benefit from a more concise summary of existing hybrid approaches.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some weaknesses related to novelty and evaluation, I believe this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of topic modeling. The *lda2vec* model is a practical and well-implemented approach that effectively combines the strengths of word embeddings and topic models. The experimental results are promising, and the open-source code makes the work readily accessible to the research community.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
