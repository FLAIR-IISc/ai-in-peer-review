- Presents lda2vec: adds document vectors to SGNS context and enforces sparse document-topic mixtures via a Dirichlet prior; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.  
- Contribution: a practical, simple hybrid that integrates topic-model interpretability with word-embedding representational power.  
- Contribution: uses a Dirichlet prior to produce sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures.  
- Contribution: demonstrates effectiveness on both benchmark and real-world corpora with qualitative examples.  
- Strength: clear motivation and well-framed problem connecting topic models and embeddings.  
- Strength: conceptually sound model design (document vector addition, Dirichlet constraint, dropout).  
- Strength: strong empirical results showing coherent topics and domain-specific relationships.  
- Strength: open-source implementation and detailed preprocessing for reproducibility.  
- Strength: well-written and well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: core components are not novel—the novelty lies mainly in their integration.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and ablation studies to isolate component contributions.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies mainly on topic coherence and lacks downstream-task comparisons against strong baselines.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of model properties (e.g., effect of Dirichlet prior, vector additivity).  
- Question: why does beta=0.75 work better and how sensitive is performance to beta?  
- Question: what are the training time and memory costs compared to word2vec and LDA (complexity analysis)?  
- Question: were other tokenization strategies tested beyond noun-phrase tokenization on Hacker News, and how did they perform?  
- Question: can the authors provide negative results or failure cases and explanations?  
- Question: what types of linear regularities/relationships does the model capture compared to standard word embeddings?  
- Additional: figures are clear, related work is comprehensive, and the paper should discuss limitations and future directions.
