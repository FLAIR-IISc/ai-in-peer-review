## Review of "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

**Summary:**

This paper presents `lda2vec`, a model that combines the strengths of topic models (LDA-style interpretability) and word embeddings (dense, distributed representations). It builds upon the Skip-gram Negative Sampling (SGNS) architecture by incorporating document vectors that are constrained to be mixtures of topic vectors, enforced through a Dirichlet likelihood. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on the Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News datasets, showing coherent topic discovery and the ability to learn semantic relationships between words.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A practical and relatively simple method for integrating topic modeling with word embeddings:** The core idea of directly injecting topic proportions into the word embedding learning process, using a Dirichlet prior to encourage sparsity and interpretability, is a valuable contribution. It provides a concrete framework for combining these previously disparate approaches.
2. **Demonstration of coherent topic modeling within a neural embedding framework:** The paper successfully demonstrates that the `lda2vec` model can discover interpretable topics in two datasets, evaluated through topic coherence metrics.
3. **Empirical evidence of learning semantic relationships in a specialized corpus:** The Hacker News experiments showcase the model’s ability to capture domain-specific relationships between words, going beyond simply reproducing general-purpose embeddings.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The introduction effectively articulates the trade-offs between topic models and word embeddings, highlighting the benefits of a hybrid approach. The desire for interpretable document representations alongside the power of dense embeddings is well-justified.
2. **Solid Technical Foundation:** The model is built on well-established techniques (SGNS, LDA), making it relatively easy to understand and build upon. The mathematical formulation is clear and the explanation of the Dirichlet likelihood and its role in inducing sparsity is well presented.
3. **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experiments on two distinct datasets (Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News) provide a good level of confidence in the model’s generalizability. The inclusion of both quantitative (coherence scores) and qualitative (topic examples, word analogies) evaluations is a strength.
4. **Practical Implementation and Open Source Availability:** The authors' commitment to open-sourcing the code is commendable and promotes reproducibility and further research.  The provided links to the code, preprocessed datasets, and tokenization procedure are valuable.
5. **Successful Integration of Interpretability:** The combination of the Dirichlet prior and topic vector projection is an elegant way to bridge the gap between the interpretability of LDA and the representational power of neural networks. The demonstrated topic coherence and word similarity examples support this claim.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* is valuable, the individual components (SGNS, LDA-style topic modeling) are well-established. The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of how this work compares to other attempts to combine topic models and embeddings (e.g., incorporating topic embeddings as features in a downstream task, using topic models to initialize embeddings).
2. **Lack of Baseline Comparison with Stronger Embedding Methods:** The paper primarily compares against the general concept of topic models vs. dense embeddings but lacks a comparison against more modern embedding techniques (e.g., BERT, Sentence Transformers) that also incorporate contextual information. This makes it difficult to assess the true added value of `lda2vec`.
3. **Sensitivity to Hyperparameters:** The paper mentions experimenting with hyperparameters but doesn’t provide a detailed analysis of their impact. A more systematic hyperparameter study and ablation analysis (e.g., removing the Dirichlet prior) would strengthen the results.  The choice of λ=200 seems somewhat arbitrary and could benefit from justification.
4. **Limited Discussion of Scalability:** While the authors mention using GPUs, the computational cost of the model isn't thoroughly addressed.  The scaling properties of `lda2vec` to truly large datasets remain unclear.
5. **Coherence Metric Limitations:** The reliance on topic coherence (Cv) is reasonable, but it’s known to have limitations and doesn’t always perfectly align with human judgment. A brief discussion of these limitations would be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How does `lda2vec` compare to existing methods that combine topic models and embeddings, particularly those that utilize pre-trained embeddings or attention mechanisms? Please provide a more detailed comparison and discuss the trade-offs.
2.  What is the impact of the Dirichlet prior’s concentration parameter (α) on the quality of the learned topics? Did you experiment with different values of α, and if so, what were the results?
3.  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of `lda2vec`, including training time and memory requirements, and how it scales with dataset size and the number of topics?
4.  You mention dropout. Was the dropout rate optimized? If so, what was the optimal rate and why?
5.  In the Hacker News corpus, you use noun phrase tokenization. Did you experiment with other tokenization schemes, and how did they affect the results?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The diagrams are helpful but could benefit from clearer labeling of the key components.
*   Consider including a table summarizing the key hyperparameters used in each experiment.
*   The writing is generally clear and concise.




Overall, this is a well-written and interesting paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of topic modeling and word embeddings. While the core components aren’t entirely novel, the integration is well-executed, and the experimental results are promising. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
