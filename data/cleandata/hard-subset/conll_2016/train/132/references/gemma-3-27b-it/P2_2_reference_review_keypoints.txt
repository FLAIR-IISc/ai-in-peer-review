- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, integrating Dirichlet topic proportions into the skip-gram objective to produce dense, interpretable document vectors; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News with coherent topics and semantic relationships.

- Contribution — hybrid model: Novel and intuitive integration of Dirichlet priors with word embeddings to add document-level structure to embeddings.

- Contribution — topic coherence: Demonstrates coherent topic discovery on Twenty Newsgroups with reported coherence scores.

- Contribution — practical implementation: Clear training procedure, quantitative and qualitative analyses, and open-sourced code and tokenization.

- Strength — clear motivation: Well-articulated limitations of standard topic models and word embeddings motivating the approach.

- Strength — sound technical approach: Directly integrating a Dirichlet prior into the skip-gram loss is clever and efficiently implemented (automatic differentiation, Chainer).

- Strength — empirical validation: Results on two datasets show versatility and meaningful topics; Hacker News shows domain-specific vocabulary and semantic analogies.

- Strength — reproducibility and presentation: Paper is well-written and authors provide code and data for replication.

- Strength — semantic relationships: Learned embeddings capture meaningful analogies and semantic structure.

- Weakness — limited novelty: Core building blocks (LDA, skip-gram) are established; novelty is mainly in integration rather than new fundamentals.

- Weakness — weak baseline comparisons: Lacks comprehensive comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., NMF, Doc2Vec, BERT-based topic models).

- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient analysis of sensitivity to key hyperparameters (α, λ, embedding dimension).

- Weakness — reliance on single coherence metric: Uses Cv only; would benefit from multiple coherence measures or human evaluation.

- Question — baseline comparison: Please compare lda2vec to Doc2Vec and transformer-based topic/document embedding methods.

- Question — hyperparameter analysis: Provide detailed sensitivity analysis and guidelines for choosing α, λ, and embedding size.

- Question — scalability: How does computational cost scale with dataset size and what optimizations exist for large corpora?

- Question — ablation study: Did authors ablate the Dirichlet prior and sparse membership regularization to show their individual contributions?

- Additional comment — figure clarity: Figure 1 would benefit from clearer illustration.

- Additional comment — notation clarity: Equations (1) and (2) need more explicit variable definitions.

- Overall assessment: Valuable and promising contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focused on stronger baselines and hyperparameter analysis.
