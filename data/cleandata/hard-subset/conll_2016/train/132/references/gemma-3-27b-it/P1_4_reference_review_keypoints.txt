- Proposes lda2vec: combines SGNS word embeddings with Dirichlet-constrained document/topic vectors added to word context prediction.
- Contribution: a practical, implementable hybrid that bridges topic models and word embeddings.
- Contribution: demonstrates joint training yields coherent topics while preserving semantic relationships in embeddings.
- Contribution: applies model to a large, domain-specific Hacker News corpus showing domain-relevant semantics.
- Strength: clearly motivated and well-defined problem of combining interpretability and semantic representation.
- Strength: technically sound integration of SGNS with a Dirichlet prior and use of autodiff frameworks.
- Strength: empirical validation on both Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News with coherence metrics and qualitative topics.
- Strength: open-source code provided to support reproducibility.
- Strength: demonstrates meaningful semantic relationships beyond topic identification (e.g., entity associations).
- Weakness: limited novelty in core components; main novelty is integration rather than new algorithms.
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparison to strong baselines that combine topic models and embeddings.
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (limited exploration of topics, beta, learning rates, etc.).
- Weakness: reliance on automated Palmetto coherence metric without any human interpretability evaluation.
- Weakness: choice of negative sampling parameter β (e.g., 0.75) not thoroughly justified.
- Question: please compare lda2vec in detail to other methods that combine topic models and embeddings and clarify advantages.
- Question: elaborate hyperparameter tuning process and provide a more thorough sensitivity analysis.
- Question: quantify computational cost vs. SGNS and how training scales with dataset size and topic count.
- Question: consider adding a small-scale human evaluation of topic interpretability to complement automated metrics.
- Question: justify the Dirichlet parameter choice (α = n-1) and explain its effect on document-topic sparsity.
- Comment: figures are generally clear and helpful.
- Comment: writing is organized and clear but occasionally dense.
- Comment: references are comprehensive.
- Overall: promising, well-executed hybrid approach but needs stronger baselines, deeper hyperparameter analysis, and interpretability validation to be more convincing.
