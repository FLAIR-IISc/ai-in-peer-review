- Presents lda2vec: integrates LDA-style topic interpretability with word embeddings by making document vectors mixtures of topic vectors via a Dirichlet prior in an SGNS framework.
- Contribution: a practical, simple method for injecting topic proportions into embedding learning to encourage sparsity and interpretability.
- Contribution: demonstrates coherent topic discovery within a neural embedding framework using coherence metrics.
- Contribution: shows empirical evidence of capturing domain-specific semantic relationships on Hacker News.
- Strength: clear motivation and well-articulated problem trade-offs between topic models and embeddings.
- Strength: solid technical foundation using established SGNS and LDA components with clear mathematical exposition of the Dirichlet role.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News with quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- Strength: practical implementation with open-source code, preprocessed data, and tokenization procedures provided.
- Strength: elegant integration of interpretability via Dirichlet prior and topic vector projection supported by coherence and similarity examples.
- Weakness: limited novelty because core components are well-known; needs comparison to prior hybrid approaches.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to stronger/contextual embedding baselines (e.g., BERT, Sentence Transformers).
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter analysis and ablation studies; λ=200 is unexplained and sensitivity to settings is unclear.
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability and computational cost for large datasets despite GPU use.
- Weakness: reliance on Cv coherence metric without discussing its known limitations relative to human judgment.
- Question: how does lda2vec compare to other methods combining topics and embeddings, especially those using pre-trained embeddings or attention?
- Question: what is the impact of the Dirichlet concentration parameter α; were different α values tested?
- Question: provide detailed computational cost analysis (training time, memory, scaling with dataset size and topic count).
- Question: was dropout rate optimized; if so, what was the optimal rate and rationale?
- Question: did different tokenization schemes (beyond noun phrase tokenization) affect Hacker News results?
- Additional comments: diagrams need clearer labeling and include a table summarizing key hyperparameters used per experiment.
- Overall: well-written, interesting contribution with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
