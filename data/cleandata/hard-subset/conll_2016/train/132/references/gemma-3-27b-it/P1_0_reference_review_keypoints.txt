- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, combining Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings via skip‑gram negative sampling and automatic differentiation; demonstrated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.  
- Contribution: Practical, streamlined hybrid approach using autodiff to lower barrier to exploring topic+embedding models.  
- Contribution: Jointly learns interpretable topic mixtures (Dirichlet) and dense word/document embeddings in a single model.  
- Contribution: Empirical validation with qualitative analyses on two datasets showing coherent topics and semantic relationships.  
- Strength: Addresses a relevant problem by combining interpretability of topic models with representational power of embeddings.  
- Strength: Practical and accessible implementation (Chainer) and open‑source code release.  
- Strength: Clear model description and helpful architecture diagram.  
- Strength: Compelling empirical results (topic coherence on 20NG and domain‑specific word similarities on Hacker News).  
- Strength: Thorough related work discussion.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—similar hybrid ideas exist and the paper doesn’t sufficiently differentiate itself beyond implementation.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited—lacks comprehensive quantitative comparisons to strong baselines and downstream task experiments.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity not thoroughly explored; tuning procedure for λ and β is underreported and ablations are missing.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of scalability and computational bottlenecks for very large corpora.  
- Weakness: Presentation of results lacks statistical significance measures (e.g., standard deviations/confidence intervals).  
- Question: How does lda2vec specifically differ from prior approaches that embed topic proportions into embedding spaces?  
- Question: Why weren’t standard LDA and doc2vec used as baselines, and how would lda2vec compare on coherence and downstream tasks?  
- Question: What was the hyperparameter selection process for λ and β, and were ablation studies performed?  
- Question: What are the major computational bottlenecks and how does the method scale with corpus size; any planned optimizations?  
- Question: Was α = n−1 chosen theoretically or empirically, and how do different α values affect learned topic mixtures?  
- Additional: Code availability is excellent for reproducibility.  
- Additional: Writing is clear but could be streamlined; suggest adding a table summarizing experiment hyperparameters.
