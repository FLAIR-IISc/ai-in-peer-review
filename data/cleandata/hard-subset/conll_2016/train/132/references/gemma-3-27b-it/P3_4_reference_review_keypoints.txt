- Presents lda2vec: a hybrid model combining Dirichlet topic modeling and SGNS word embeddings by embedding words and documents in a shared space with a Dirichlet prior for sparsity.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News with competitive topic coherence and qualitative semantic relationships.
- Contribution: principled integration of topic modeling into the word embedding learning objective rather than using embeddings as features.
- Contribution: provides a practical Chainer implementation and evaluation using coherence metrics and qualitative analyses.
- Contribution: shows semantic regularities and analogy-like behavior in a domain-specific corpus (Hacker News).
- Strength: clearly motivated trade-off between interpretability of topics and representational power of embeddings.
- Strength: technically sound novel integration of the Dirichlet prior into SGNS with sensible regularization (dropout).
- Strength: empirical results are promising, with strong coherence on Twenty Newsgroups and convincing qualitative Hacker News examples.
- Strength: code release and clear presentation aid reproducibility and adoption.
- Strength: clear writing and helpful visualizations (e.g., Figure 1).
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to recent strong baselines (e.g., NVDM, BERTopic, transformer-based models or fine-tuned BERT).
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (λ, α, β) and lack of ablation (what if λ=0).
- Weakness: scalability and computational requirements for large datasets (e.g., 1M docs) are not discussed.
- Weakness: relies heavily on topic coherence as the sole quantitative metric for Twenty Newsgroups; needs more holistic/human evaluations.
- Question: please compare to recent state-of-the-art topic models (NVDM, BERTopic) on Twenty Newsgroups.
- Question: what is the effect of removing the Dirichlet prior (λ=0) and varying α and β?
- Question: what are training time and memory requirements for much larger datasets and strategies to improve scalability?
- Question: can you provide qualitative topic listings for Twenty Newsgroups comparable to the Hacker News analysis?
- Question: did you try other negative sampling exponents besides u^(3/4), and what changed?
- Additional: references are appropriate and code availability is a significant plus.
- Additional: consider discussing broader applications (document clustering, information retrieval).
- Overall: solid, novel, and well-presented contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to address baseline comparisons and hyperparameter/scalability analyses.
