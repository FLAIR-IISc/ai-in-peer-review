- Summary: Introduces lda2vec, a hybrid model combining Dirichlet topic mixtures with SGNS word embeddings to produce interpretable topics and dense document vectors, evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.

- Contribution 1: Presents a practical, differentiable hybrid that jointly learns topic mixtures and word embeddings using modern deep learning frameworks.

- Contribution 2: Incorporates a Dirichlet prior to produce more interpretable, sparse document-topic representations than pure dense embeddings.

- Contribution 3: Provides empirical validation on both a benchmark dataset and a real-world corpus to demonstrate generalizability.

- Strength 1: Novel integration of SGNS and Dirichlet-optimized topic mixtures bridging traditional topic models and neural embeddings.

- Strength 2: Emphasis on interpretability via sparse topic mixtures supported by qualitative examples.

- Strength 3: Practical implementation using Chainer/GPU with code availability for reproducibility.

- Strength 4: Demonstrates learned semantic regularities and meaningful word relationships in the learned embeddings.

- Strength 5: Clear writing and helpful diagrams that explain model architecture and training.

- Weakness 1: Limited comparative evaluation — lacks thorough baselines against LDA, NMF, Doc2Vec, etc.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., λ and α) and no detailed ablation.

- Weakness 3: Scalability concerns not fully addressed; training cost and limits on larger datasets are unclear.

- Weakness 4: Reliance on a single topic coherence metric (Cv) rather than multiple complementary metrics.

- Weakness 5: Hacker News experiment provides expected results without deeper analysis explaining why lda2vec outperforms alternatives.

- Question 1: Please provide quantitative and qualitative comparisons to LDA and Doc2Vec (perplexity, coherence, classification accuracy).

- Question 2: Describe methodology for selecting λ and α and provide a sensitivity/ablation study.

- Question 3: Report training time and scalability bottlenecks for the Hacker News dataset and possible optimizations.

- Question 4: Justify choice of Cv coherence metric and discuss whether other metrics (UMass, NPMI) change conclusions.

- Question 5: Explain rationale and experiments behind using unigram frequency exponent 3/4 for negative sampling versus other exponents.

- Additional comment 1: References section is incomplete (missing publication years and full citations).

- Additional comment 2: The open-source software statement should include a direct repository link in references/acknowledgements.
