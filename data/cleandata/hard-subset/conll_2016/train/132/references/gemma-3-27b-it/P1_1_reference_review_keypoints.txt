- Paper introduces lda2vec: combines SGNS word embeddings with Dirichlet-based topic mixtures by adding document vectors to word context vectors for joint training.
- Main contribution: principled integration of topic models and word embeddings to combine interpretability with distributed representations.
- Main contribution: demonstration of joint training of word and document vectors using automatic differentiation in a single framework.
- Main contribution: empirical validation on Twenty Newsgroups and a large Hacker News corpus showing topic coherence and semantic relationships.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated approach that targets interpretability plus representational power.
- Strength: technically sound formulation leveraging SGNS and Dirichlet priors; implementation-friendly via automatic differentiation.
- Strength: positive empirical results with competitive topic coherence and useful semantic analogies.
- Strength: open-source, documented, and unit-tested code enhancing reproducibility.
- Strength: effective visualization (Figure 1) clarifying model architecture.
- Weakness: core idea’s novelty is limited given prior work combining topics and embeddings; positioning relative to existing work is weak.
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive baseline comparisons (e.g., standard LDA, NMF, Doc2Vec) to assess relative performance.
- Weakness: topic interpretation (especially Hacker News) is subjective; needs more rigorous evaluation beyond coherence scores.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness.
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability, training time, and memory requirements for large corpora.
- Question: please expand related work comparing lda2vec to other topic+embedding approaches and clarify distinctions.
- Question: provide baseline comparisons to LDA, Doc2Vec, and other relevant methods on both datasets.
- Question: supply a detailed hyperparameter analysis (learning rate, embedding dim, Dirichlet concentration, etc.).
- Question: report training time and peak memory usage on the Hacker News dataset and how resources scale with data size.
- Question: consider additional topic evaluation methods (human evaluation or downstream task performance).
- Additional comment: notation in eq. (5) is unconventional; explicitly define summation indices for clarity.
- Additional comment: reference list is incomplete and contains abbreviated citations that need full bibliographic details.
- Additional comment: add a limitations/future work section to discuss shortcomings and possible extensions.
