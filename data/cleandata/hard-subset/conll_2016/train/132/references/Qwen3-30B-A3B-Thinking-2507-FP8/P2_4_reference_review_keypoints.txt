- Introduces lda2vec: combines LDA topic modeling with word embeddings to embed words and documents in the same vector space for interpretability and semantic richness.
- Main contributions: unified lda2vec model; interpretable topics with good coherence on standard datasets; evidence of corpus-specific semantic relationships (Hacker News).
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated model design that sums document and word vectors to form context vectors.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News with topic coherence (Palmetto) and qualitative topic analysis.
- Strength: Reproducibility — authors released code and data.
- Strength: Demonstrates semantic relationships and strong word analogy results.
- Strength: Practical implementation in automatic differentiation frameworks (Chainer) lowers implementation barriers.
- Weakness: Fails to compare with prior hybrid approaches, notably omitting Mimno et al. (2017) LDA2Vec, questioning novelty.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for architectural choice of summing vectors versus alternatives.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation on Hacker News — unable to use Palmetto and provided no alternative or manual evaluation.
- Weakness: Unclear novelty claim — appears as straightforward adaptation without clear advancement over prior work.
- Weakness: Claims about reduced implementation complexity via autodiff are overstated without evidence of efficiency gains.
- Question: How does this work relate to or differ from Mimno et al. (2017) LDA2Vec?
- Question: What evidence shows summing document and word vectors is better than concatenation or element-wise multiplication?
- Question: How would you evaluate Hacker News topic quality without Palmetto, and will you perform a manual evaluation?
- Overall: Well-motivated model with strong dataset evaluation but weakened by missing comparisons, limited theoretical justification, and incomplete Hacker News evaluation.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — reasonable contribution but requires addressing prior work comparison and key design justifications in revision.
