

# Review of "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper introduces lda2vec, a hybrid model that combines Dirichlet-optimized topic mixtures with word embeddings. The authors aim to create interpretable document representations that preserve semantic relationships from word embeddings. The main contributions as I see them are:

1. A practical implementation of a hybrid model that successfully integrates LDA's interpretability with word2vec's semantic richness using automatic differentiation frameworks.
2. A method for enforcing sparse document-topic memberships through a Dirichlet likelihood term with low concentration parameter.
3. Demonstration of the model's ability to learn meaningful topics and semantic relationships in domain-specific corpora.

## Strengths

1. **Practical implementation approach**: The authors effectively leverage modern automatic differentiation frameworks (Chainer) to simplify model development, avoiding the need for complex custom inference algorithms. This makes the approach more accessible to practitioners compared to traditional topic modeling methods that require deriving new approximations.

2. **Strong interpretability demonstration**: The paper provides compelling evidence of topic interpretability through both quantitative (topic coherence metrics) and qualitative (topic examples) evaluations. The Twenty Newsgroups results (0.567 coherence for 20 topics) align well with human evaluations as shown in Röder et al. (2015), and the qualitative examples (Figure 3) clearly match expected newsgroup categories.

3. **Domain-specific semantic learning**: The Hacker News experiments demonstrate the model's ability to capture domain-specific semantic relationships (Figure 6), showing that lda2vec learns meaningful analogies like "Silicon Valley - California + technology" and "Bitcoin - currency + digital" that are relevant to the corpus's domain.

4. **Clear model exposition**: The paper provides an excellent explanation of the model architecture with Figure 1 being particularly effective in illustrating how document vectors, word vectors, and topic vectors interact within the same space.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited novelty and insufficient comparison**: The paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate lda2vec from existing hybrid models. Previous work like LDA2Vec (which the authors cite) already attempts similar integration, but the paper doesn't provide a direct comparison. Without clear comparison to strong baselines (LDA, LDA2Vec, standard word2vec), it's hard to assess whether the proposed approach offers meaningful improvements.

2. **Inadequate evaluation**: The Hacker News corpus evaluation lacks topic coherence metrics due to vocabulary differences, which is a significant omission. The paper also doesn't compare against relevant baselines on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset, making it difficult to determine if the observed coherence scores are genuinely better than existing methods.

3. **Missing ablation studies**: The paper claims the Dirichlet likelihood term is crucial for sparsity, but doesn't provide ablation results showing what happens when this term is removed or when α is set to different values. Similarly, it doesn't demonstrate how much the document vector component contributes versus standard word2vec.

4. **Lack of scalability analysis**: While the paper mentions the Hacker News corpus has 75 million tokens, it doesn't provide computational efficiency metrics or compare training time with traditional LDA or other topic models. This is a significant omission given the claim that "modern automatic differentiation frameworks... dramatically improve computational speed."

5. **No discussion of limitations**: The paper fails to address potential limitations, such as performance on short documents, handling of out-of-vocabulary words, or how the model scales to extremely large corpora beyond the Hacker News dataset.

## Questions to Authors

1. How does lda2vec specifically differ from LDA2Vec (the model introduced by the same authors in their previous work), and how does it improve upon it?

2. The Hacker News corpus evaluation omits topic coherence metrics due to vocabulary differences. Have you considered adapting the coherence metric for domain-specific vocabularies, or using alternative evaluation metrics for this corpus?

3. Could you provide ablation results showing the impact of removing the Dirichlet likelihood term (Ld) on topic coherence and sparsity?

4. What is the computational complexity of lda2vec compared to traditional LDA, and how does training time scale with corpus size?

## Overall Assessment

This paper presents a practical approach to combining topic modeling with word embeddings that demonstrates promising results on two datasets. The implementation is well-structured, and the interpretability claims are well-supported for the Twenty Newsgroups dataset. However, the lack of comparative evaluation against relevant baselines, insufficient ablation studies, and limited discussion of limitations prevent this work from making a strong contribution to the field. With more thorough evaluation and clearer differentiation from existing work, this could be a valuable contribution.

**Decision: Weak Reject** (with potential for acceptance after major revisions addressing the weaknesses above)
