- Paper introduces "lda2vec": a hybrid model combining LDA-style topic mixtures with word embeddings by extending skip-gram with document-level topic vectors and a Dirichlet sparsity term.
- Main contributions: unified vector space merging interpretability and semantic richness; Dirichlet likelihood to enforce sparse document-topic mixtures; empirical demonstration of interpretable topics and semantic relations in specialized corpora.
- Strength: Successfully integrates topic modeling and word embeddings, producing coherent topics while preserving semantic relationships; Dirichlet sparsity is well-motivated.
- Strength: Strong empirical validation on multiple domains (Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News) with comparable or better topic coherence and convincing qualitative results.
- Strength: Clear evidence of semantic regularities and domain-specific analogies learned by the model, beyond typical topic modeling.
- Weakness: Lacks meaningful comparison with recent neural topic models and prior implementations, making it hard to judge improvement over state-of-the-art.
- Weakness: Questionable novelty given similarity to earlier LDA2Vec work; paper does not acknowledge or clearly differentiate from prior work.
- Weakness: Insufficient ablation studies—no analysis of model variants (e.g., without Dirichlet sparsity or without document-topic mixture) to isolate component contributions.
- Question: Please clarify how this work differs from the original LDA2Vec paper and what novel contributions are introduced.
- Question: Provide a detailed comparison with traditional LDA (same number of topics) to show whether the model improves standard topic modeling.
- Question: Quantify how topic coherence or interpretability changes when the Dirichlet sparsity term is removed.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—well-motivated and empirically convincing model, but weakened by lack of comparisons, unclear novelty, and missing ablations.
