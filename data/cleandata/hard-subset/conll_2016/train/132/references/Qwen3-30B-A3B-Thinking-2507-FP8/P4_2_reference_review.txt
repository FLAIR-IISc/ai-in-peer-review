

# Review of "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper introduces "lda2vec," a hybrid model that combines Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings. The authors propose a unified framework that extends Skipgram Negative Sampling (SGNS) to learn document representations as sparse mixtures of topics while simultaneously learning word and topic vectors in the same space. The key technical contributions are:

1. A novel model architecture that integrates topic modeling with word embeddings using automatic differentiation frameworks, enabling simpler model development and GPU acceleration.
2. A sparsity-inducing Dirichlet likelihood term (Ld) that encourages interpretable document-topic proportions through low concentration parameters.
3. Demonstration of the model's effectiveness in discovering coherent topics and learning semantic relationships in two distinct text corpora.

## Strengths

1. **Clear and well-motivated model architecture**: The paper provides a thorough explanation of the model's components and rationale. The use of document vectors as a sum of topic vectors (Equation 4) and context vectors as the sum of document and word vectors (Equation 3) is well-justified with intuitive examples. The figure (Figure 1) effectively illustrates how the model combines elements of topic models and word embeddings. The authors clearly explain the technical motivation for each design choice, making the model accessible to readers.

2. **Strong empirical validation of interpretability**: The paper demonstrates that lda2vec produces meaningful, interpretable topics in both the Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News datasets. The topic coherence scores (0.567 with 20 topics) are competitive with traditional LDA, and the qualitative analysis of topics (Figures 3 and 4) convincingly shows that the model discovers relevant themes. The Hacker News analysis particularly demonstrates the model's ability to capture domain-specific concepts like "Bitcoin" and "X Windows" that would be difficult to identify with traditional topic models.

3. **Demonstration of semantic relationships**: The paper convincingly shows that lda2vec learns semantic regularities within the specialized vocabulary of the Hacker News corpus (Figure 6). The word analogy results demonstrate that the model captures meaningful relationships between tokens (e.g., "Silicon Valley" - "California" + "technology"), which is a significant achievement for an unsupervised model. This shows the model successfully preserves the semantic properties of word embeddings while adding interpretability.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of clear novelty differentiation**: The paper claims "lda2vec" as a new model but fails to clearly distinguish it from prior work. Tang et al. (2015) already proposed LDA2VEC, a nearly identical model that combines topic models with word embeddings. The paper should explicitly state how this work differs from Tang et al.'s approach and why it constitutes a meaningful advancement rather than just an implementation.

2. **Insufficient evaluation and comparison**: The evaluation is limited to two datasets without thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art topic models. The paper does not compare against LDA2VEC or other hybrid models, nor does it provide ablation studies to demonstrate the contribution of the Dirichlet sparsity term. The Hacker News corpus evaluation lacks topic coherence scores due to vocabulary mismatch with the Palmetto tool, which weakens the evaluation of this dataset.

3. **Poor justification of key parameters**: The choice of α = n-1 for the Dirichlet concentration parameter is not theoretically justified but rather stated as "to drive interpretability." The paper does not provide ablation studies or analysis showing why this specific value is optimal. Similarly, the choice of λ = 200 for the Dirichlet likelihood strength lacks explanation or empirical validation.

## Questions to Authors

1. How does this work specifically differ from Tang et al.'s LDA2VEC (2015)? The paper should clearly articulate the technical distinctions that make this contribution novel.

2. Why was the Dirichlet concentration parameter set to α = n-1? What evidence supports this choice over other values, and how sensitive is the model to this parameter?

3. The paper uses pretrained word vectors for Twenty Newsgroups but random initialization for Hacker News. What is the rationale for this difference, and how would using pretrained vectors for Hacker News affect the results?

## Additional Comments

The paper's technical approach is sound and the implementation appears well-executed. The use of automatic differentiation frameworks to simplify topic model development is a practical contribution that could benefit the community. However, the novelty claim needs strengthening through clearer differentiation from prior work and more comprehensive evaluation.

The paper's main contribution is the demonstration of a model that successfully combines the interpretability of topic models with the semantic richness of word embeddings, but the novelty claim is weakened by the lack of clear differentiation from prior work. With stronger justification for design choices and more thorough evaluation, this work could make a meaningful contribution to the field.
