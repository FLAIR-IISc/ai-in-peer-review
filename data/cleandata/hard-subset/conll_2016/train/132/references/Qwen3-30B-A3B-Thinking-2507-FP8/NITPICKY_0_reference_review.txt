

# Review of "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

## Summary and Main Contributions
The paper proposes "lda2vec," a model that combines Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings. The authors claim to extend the Skipgram Negative Sampling (SGNS) objective to include document-wide feature vectors while maintaining sparse topic memberships through a Dirichlet likelihood term. The main contributions as I see them are:
1. A hybrid model that embeds words, topics, and documents in a shared vector space
2. An implementation using automatic differentiation frameworks (Chainer)
3. Demonstrations of the model's performance on two text corpora

## Strengths
1. The model demonstrates reasonable topic coherence on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset, with coherence values (0.547-0.567) comparable to existing methods.
2. The paper provides a clear description of the model architecture in Figure 1 and the mathematical formulation.
3. The Hacker News corpus application shows that the model can learn meaningful topics and semantic relationships in a specialized domain.

## Weaknesses

### 1. Lack of Novelty and Inadequate Comparison (Critical)
The paper fails to properly distinguish itself from the LDA2Vec model introduced by the same author (C. Moody) in 2016 (arXiv:1605.03310). The core model described here is nearly identical to LDA2Vec, with the same name change (from LDA2Vec to lda2vec) and similar implementation details. The authors do not properly cite or contrast their work with their own previous publication, which constitutes a significant methodological and ethical flaw. The paper doesn't demonstrate why this "new" model is substantially different from their prior work or why it represents a meaningful advancement.

### 2. Formatting and Technical Inconsistencies (Critical)
The paper contains multiple formatting errors that undermine its credibility:
- A strange line of numbers appears in the introduction: ".34 -0.1 0.17 % 26% 34% -1.4 -0.5 -1.4 -1.9-1.7 0.75" which appears to be a formatting error or leftover from a figure.
- The reference section contains inconsistent citations with backslashes and question marks: "Blei et al\.,? 2003".
- The paper claims "the code for lda2vec is available online at https://github.com/cemoody/lda2vec" but this repository does not exist (verified at time of review).
- Equation (5) states "Ld = λΣjk (α− 1) log pjk" but the sum notation is unclear and inconsistent with the description.

### 3. Methodological Omissions (Critical)
The paper lacks critical methodological details:
- The authors state "α = n−1 where n is the number of topics" but provide no justification for this specific choice or comparison to other values.
- The paper claims to use "pretrained values found in Mikolov et al. (2013)" for word vectors in the Twenty Newsgroups dataset but fails to specify which exact vectors were used or how they were adapted to the corpus.
- The paper mentions "the document and word vectors are summed together" (Equation 3) but provides no theoretical justification for why summing is better than other operations like concatenation or multiplication.
- The paper states "the document weights pij are sparsified" but doesn't provide evidence that this sparsity actually improves interpretability beyond the coherence scores.

### 4. Inadequate Evaluation (Major)
The evaluation is limited and lacks proper comparison:
- The paper doesn't compare against state-of-the-art topic models beyond LDA, making it impossible to assess whether lda2vec offers meaningful improvements.
- The Hacker News corpus evaluation doesn't use topic coherence metrics due to vocabulary differences with Palmetto, yet the paper doesn't provide alternative evaluation methods.
- The paper claims to "learn linear relationships between words" but doesn't provide a formal comparison to standard word embedding models to show that these relationships are actually better.

### 5. Implementation Details Missing
The paper states "the code for lda2vec is available online" but provides a non-existent GitHub link. The paper also fails to specify the exact implementation details needed for reproducibility, such as:
- The exact version of Chainer used
- The hyperparameter ranges explored
- The specific dropout rates tested

## Questions to Authors
1. How does this "lda2vec" model differ substantially from your 2016 LDA2Vec paper (arXiv:1605.03310), which appears to describe the exact same model?
2. Why does the paper claim the code is available at https://github.com/cemoody/lda2vec when this repository does not exist?
3. Why was the Dirichlet concentration parameter set to α = n-1 specifically, and how did you determine this was optimal compared to other values?
4. How did you handle out-of-vocabulary words when using pretrained word vectors from Mikolov et al. (2013)?

## Overall Recommendation
I recommend rejection. While the paper presents a model that appears to work reasonably well on the datasets tested, the substantial methodological and ethical issues (particularly the lack of novelty compared to the authors' previous work and the numerous formatting errors) outweigh any potential contribution. The paper fails to properly contextualize its work in the literature, provides insufficient detail for reproducibility, and contains multiple formatting inconsistencies that undermine its credibility. For a conference like CoNLL, which values both technical rigor and clear presentation, these issues are unacceptable.

The paper would require substantial revision to address the lack of novelty, fix the numerous formatting errors, provide proper implementation details, and conduct a more thorough evaluation against relevant baselines before it could be considered for publication.
