- Summary: Introduces lda2vec, combining LDA-like Dirichlet topic sparsity with word2vec skip-gram embeddings by summing pivot and document (mixture of topic) vectors and adding a Dirichlet term; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News showing interpretable topics and semantic relationships.

- Contribution 1: Novel integration of document-topic sparsity from LDA with word embeddings to produce interpretable document representations that preserve semantics.

- Contribution 2: Practical implementation using automatic differentiation frameworks, lowering barrier to adoption by avoiding complex custom approximations.

- Contribution 3: Empirical demonstrations that lda2vec learns meaningful topics and semantic relations in both general and domain-specific corpora.

- Strength 1: Successfully bridges probabilistic topic modeling and distributed word representations with a well-motivated architecture and clear visualization.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical validation with competitive topic coherence on Twenty Newsgroups and qualitative domain-specific results on Hacker News.

- Strength 3: Emphasizes implementation simplicity and accessibility by leveraging standard autodiff tools (Chainer).

- Weakness 1: Lacks comparisons to recent state-of-the-art neural topic models (e.g., NTM, DTM), making relative performance unclear.

- Weakness 2: Limited sensitivity and behavior analysis (e.g., effects of α, hidden units, dropout, training time, scalability) leaving limits and tuning unclear.

- Weakness 3: Reproducibility concerns due to incomplete code URL and insufficient hyperparameter/training detail.

- Question 1: Request for estimated implementation complexity (lines of code) for lda2vec versus standard LDA or Neural Topic Model.

- Question 2: Request to add comparisons with recent neural topic models (e.g., NTM, DTM) in revision.

- Question 3: Ask for explanation of why negative sampling exponent β = 0.75 yields slightly higher topic coherence than β = 1.0.

- Question 4: Ask for sensitivity analysis showing how topic coherence varies with different Dirichlet concentration α values (α currently set to n-1).

- Question 5: Request discussion of model scalability and computational resource needs given larger corpora (Hacker News ~50× larger than Twenty Newsgroups).

- Additional comment: Writing and figures are clear; strengthen comparisons with other hybrid approaches and provide a complete, accessible code repository link.
