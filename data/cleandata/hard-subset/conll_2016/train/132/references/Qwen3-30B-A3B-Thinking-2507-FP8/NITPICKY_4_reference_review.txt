

# Review of "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

## Summary
The paper proposes "lda2vec," a hybrid model that combines Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings to create interpretable document representations. The authors claim to preserve the interpretability of topic models while incorporating semantic richness from word embeddings.

## Main Contributions
1. A hybrid model that integrates topic modeling with word embeddings
2. A method for creating interpretable document representations using Dirichlet-optimized sparse topic mixtures
3. Demonstration of the model's effectiveness on two text corpora

## Strengths
1. The core idea of combining topic models with word embeddings is reasonable and potentially useful for creating more interpretable document representations.
2. The model architecture is described clearly with a figure (Figure 1) that illustrates the components.
3. The paper provides some interesting examples of discovered topics in the Hacker News corpus, showing how the model can capture domain-specific themes.

## Weaknesses

### 1. Formatting and Typographical Errors
The paper contains numerous formatting inconsistencies that suggest a lack of attention to detail. Key examples include:
- Broken links: "https:// github.com/cemoody/lda2vec" (with space) and "http:// palmetto.aksw.org/palmetto-webapp/" (with space)
- Inconsistent reference formatting throughout (e.g., "Blei et al\.,? 2003" instead of "Blei et al., 2003")
- Strange numerical artifacts in Figure 1: ".34 -0.1 0.17" and other numbers that don't belong in a figure description
- Line break "do- mains" in the introduction

These errors significantly undermine the paper's credibility and professionalism.

### 2. Mathematical and Notational Inconsistencies
The paper contains multiple mathematical errors that would confuse readers:
- Equation (2): "Σ n l=0" should be "Σ_{l=0}^n" or "Σ_{l=1}^n"
- Equation (4): The notation "pjn·~tn" is inconsistent with the pattern established earlier
- Equation (5): "α− 1" should be "α - 1" for clarity
- Inconsistent vector notation: ~w, \vec{w}, and w used interchangeably

### 3. Unrealistic Topic Coherence Scores
The reported topic coherence values are implausibly high for standard metrics:
- Figure 2 shows topic coherences of 0.567, 0.555, etc. (typically, coherence scores range from 0.1 to 0.8, with most models scoring below 0.5)
- Figure 3 lists coherence values of 0.712, 0.675, etc. for individual topics (the highest reported topic coherence in literature is typically around 0.65-0.70)
- This suggests either a misunderstanding of the coherence metric or an error in the implementation

### 4. Lack of Comparative Baselines
The paper fails to compare with existing methods, which is critical for demonstrating novelty and effectiveness:
- No comparison with LDA or LDA2vec (the model that this paper appears to be extending)
- No ablation study to show the contribution of the Dirichlet term (Ld)
- No discussion of how their model compares to state-of-the-art topic models

### 5. Misleading Claims
Several claims are either unsubstantiated or misleading:
- "lda2vec builds representations over both words and documents by mixing word2vec's skipgram architecture with Dirichlet-optimized sparse topic mixtures" - this is essentially describing LDA2vec, which was introduced in 2016 by the same authors (see "lda2vec: A Joint Model for Topic Models and Word Embeddings" by C. Moody et al., 2016).
- The paper states "the code for lda2vec is available online" but doesn't provide a working link
- The paper claims to "extend distributed word representations" but doesn't clearly differentiate from existing work

## Questions to Authors
1. The topic coherence scores reported (e.g., 0.712 in Figure 3) are significantly higher than typically reported values for the Palmetto metric. Could you clarify which specific metric you used and why these values are so high?
2. The paper appears to be describing the LDA2vec model, which was previously published in 2016. Could you clarify how this work differs from the existing LDA2vec model?
3. The paper states "the code for lda2vec is available online" but the link contains a space ("https:// github.com"). Could you provide a working link to the code repository?

## Overall Assessment
While the paper's core idea has potential, the numerous formatting errors, mathematical inconsistencies, unrealistic evaluation metrics, and lack of proper comparison with existing methods constitute a substantial number of minor flaws that collectively undermine the paper's credibility. As a perfectionist reviewer who meticulously examines every aspect of a paper, I find these issues too significant to overlook, despite the paper's reasonable core concept. The paper would require substantial revision to address these issues before it could be considered for acceptance.

**Recommendation: Reject**
