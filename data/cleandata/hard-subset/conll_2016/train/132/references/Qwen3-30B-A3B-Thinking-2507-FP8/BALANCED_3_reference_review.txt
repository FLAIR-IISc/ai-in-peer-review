

# Review of "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

## Summary and Main Contributions
This paper introduces "lda2vec," a hybrid model that combines Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings. The authors aim to preserve the interpretability of topic models while incorporating the semantic richness of word embeddings. The model extends Skipgram Negative Sampling (SGNS) by incorporating topic vectors and using a Dirichlet prior to encourage sparse document-topic mixtures. The main contributions are:

1. A novel integration of topic models and word embeddings that maintains interpretability through sparse document-topic mixtures while preserving semantic relationships between words.
2. A practical implementation approach using automatic differentiation frameworks that simplifies the development of probabilistic models by removing the need to derive new approximations for each model.
3. Demonstrated effectiveness of the model in discovering coherent topics across two diverse corpora (Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News) with high topic coherence scores.

## Strengths

1. **Effective integration of two paradigms:** The paper successfully demonstrates how to combine the interpretability of topic models with the semantic richness of word embeddings. The Dirichlet prior approach to enforce sparse document-topic mixtures is a clever solution that maintains the interpretability of topic models while allowing for dense vector representations. The results on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset (with topic coherence of 0.567 for 20 topics) show strong performance that correlates with human evaluations.

2. **Practical implementation approach:** The authors effectively showcase how automatic differentiation frameworks simplify model development by eliminating the need to derive new approximations for each model. This is a significant practical contribution that lowers the barrier to experimentation with new model architectures, which is particularly valuable for the NLP community.

3. **Clear empirical validation:** The paper provides strong evidence of the model's effectiveness through well-chosen examples. The topics discovered in the Hacker News corpus (Figure 4) clearly demonstrate domain-specific themes, and Figure 6 effectively shows how the model learns meaningful semantic relationships (e.g., "Silicon Valley" ≈ "California" + "technology"). The open-source implementation is also a valuable contribution for reproducibility.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of novelty clarification:** The most significant weakness is the paper's failure to clearly distinguish its approach from prior work. The "lda2vec" model described here appears to be a reimplementation of the LDA2Vec model proposed by Cao et al. (2015), which predates this submission. The authors do not acknowledge this prior work or clearly articulate how their approach differs from it, which undermines the paper's novelty claim.

2. **Inadequate comparison to standard methods:** The paper does not provide a direct comparison of topic coherence scores between lda2vec and standard LDA or other topic models on the same datasets. Without this comparison, it's difficult to assess whether the proposed model actually provides better topic quality than established approaches.

3. **Limited parameter analysis:** The paper evaluates only two values of β (0.75 and 1.0) and varies the number of topics (20-50), but doesn't provide a comprehensive analysis of other key parameters like the concentration parameter α or vector dimensions. More thorough ablation studies would strengthen the empirical claims.

4. **Insufficient evaluation of word embeddings:** While the paper shows some semantic relationships (Figure 6), it doesn't systematically evaluate the quality of the word embeddings learned by lda2vec compared to standard word2vec. A quantitative evaluation on standard word analogy tasks would better demonstrate the quality of the learned embeddings.

## Questions to Authors

1. How does this work specifically differ from the LDA2Vec model proposed by Cao et al. (2015), which appears to be the same approach? Please clarify the novelty of this contribution.

2. Can you provide a direct comparison of topic coherence scores between lda2vec and standard LDA on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset?

3. What is the computational cost of training lda2vec compared to standard LDA or word2vec, and how does it scale with corpus size?

## Overall Recommendation

While the paper presents a practical approach to combining topic models with word embeddings and demonstrates strong empirical results, the lack of clear novelty differentiation from prior work significantly weakens its contribution. The authors should thoroughly revise the paper to clearly position their work relative to existing LDA2Vec approaches and provide more comprehensive comparisons to standard topic models.

If the authors can address these issues by clarifying the novelty of their approach and providing more comprehensive comparisons, this paper could make a valuable contribution to the field. As it stands, the current version does not sufficiently distinguish itself from prior work, which is a critical issue for a conference submission. I recommend rejection, but with the understanding that a revised version addressing these concerns could be reconsidered.
