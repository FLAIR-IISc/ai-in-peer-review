

# Peer Review: "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper introduces "lda2vec," a hybrid model that combines traditional Dirichlet topic modeling (LDA) with modern word embedding techniques to create interpretable document representations that also capture semantic relationships between words. The authors propose a simple extension to the Skipgram Negative Sampling (SGNS) objective from word2vec, where document vectors are constrained to be sparse mixtures of topic vectors. This allows the model to learn a common vector space for words, topics, and documents.

The main contributions of this work are:
1. A practical implementation of a hybrid topic model that preserves LDA's interpretability while incorporating semantic relationships from word embeddings
2. A demonstration that this approach can effectively learn topic representations with good coherence scores
3. Evidence that the model can capture domain-specific semantic relationships in specialized corpora

## Strengths

1. **Effective Bridging of Interpretability and Semantic Richness**: The paper successfully addresses a key limitation in modern NLP: the trade-off between interpretability and semantic richness. Traditional topic models (LDA) produce sparse, interpretable representations but lack semantic relationships between words, while neural approaches capture semantics but sacrifice interpretability. The lda2vec model bridges this gap by maintaining LDA's sparse document-topic mixtures while embedding words, topics, and documents in the same semantic space. The Hacker News experiments demonstrate this effectively, showing that the model can learn domain-specific relationships (e.g., "Bitcoin" is a digital currency, "Silicon Valley" relates to California and technology).

2. **Strong Experimental Validation on Diverse Datasets**: The paper provides thorough evaluation on two distinct datasets (Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments) with different characteristics. The topic coherence scores (0.55-0.71) are competitive with traditional LDA, and the qualitative examples of discovered topics (e.g., "Space," "Encryption," "Housing Issues") demonstrate meaningful interpretation. The Hacker News results particularly showcase the model's ability to learn domain-specific semantic relationships, with Figure 6 showing successful word analogies like "Silicon Valley - California + Technology" and "Bitcoin - currency" (Figure 6).

3. **Practical Implementation Using Modern Frameworks**: The authors emphasize the simplicity of implementing this model using automatic differentiation frameworks (Chainer), which lowers the barrier to entry for researchers. The code is open-source, well-documented, and unit-tested, addressing reproducibility concerns. This practical aspect is valuable for the NLP community, as it enables easy adoption of hybrid approaches without requiring deep expertise in probabilistic modeling.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of Comparative Analysis with State-of-the-Art Methods**: The paper makes a strong claim about the value of the hybrid approach but fails to provide meaningful comparisons with other recent topic modeling methods. It doesn't compare against Neural Topic Models (NTMs), Deep Embedding Topic Models (DETM), or other hybrid approaches that might achieve similar or better results. Without such comparisons, it's difficult to assess whether the proposed model offers significant advantages over existing approaches. For instance, the topic coherence scores reported (0.55-0.71) should be compared to baseline methods like LDA or more recent neural topic models.

2. **Unclear Novelty and Relationship to Prior Work**: The paper's title and content suggest a novel contribution, but it appears to be a reimplementation of an existing model (the authors cite "lda2vec" as a prior work, but the paper doesn't clarify the relationship). The model described is essentially a rephrasing of the original lda2vec model from previous work, with the same name. This creates confusion about what is truly novel here. The paper should explicitly clarify how this work differs from prior lda2vec implementations and why the authors believe their specific implementation is novel or significantly better.

3. **Insufficient Theoretical Justification**: While the model works well empirically, the paper lacks strong theoretical justification for the specific design choices. The Dirichlet likelihood term is used to induce sparsity but isn't rigorously justified beyond its observed effect. The authors don't explore why α = n-1 (where n is the number of topics) is optimal or how the model would behave with different parameter choices. A deeper theoretical analysis would strengthen the paper's contribution.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper title references "lda2vec" as if it's a novel model, but prior work with the same name exists. Could you clarify the relationship between this work and the original lda2vec model (e.g., is this a reimplementation, extension, or correction of prior work)?

2. How does your model compare to recent neural topic modeling approaches (e.g., Neural Topic Models, Deep Embedding Topic Models) in terms of topic coherence and interpretability? Could you provide a comparison table with results from these methods?

3. The paper uses a Dirichlet likelihood term with α = n-1 to induce sparsity. How sensitive is the model to this parameter choice? What happens if α is set to different values (e.g., α = 1, α = 0.5)?

## Overall Recommendation

This paper presents a practical and effective hybrid approach to topic modeling that successfully combines interpretability with semantic richness. The implementation is straightforward using modern frameworks, and the experimental results demonstrate the model's ability to learn meaningful topics and semantic relationships. However, the lack of comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods and unclear novelty statement significantly weaken the contribution. If the authors can clarify the relationship to prior work and provide meaningful comparisons with other topic modeling approaches, this would substantially strengthen the paper. As it stands, the work is interesting and practical but doesn't offer a clear advancement over existing approaches.

**Recommendation: Weak Accept** (with major revisions to address the weaknesses noted above)
