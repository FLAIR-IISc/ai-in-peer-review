- Summary/Main contribution: Proposes lda2vec, combining Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings to jointly learn sparse document-topic mixtures and dense word vectors, implemented simply with automatic differentiation and empirically shown to produce coherent topics and semantic relations.

Strengths:
- Novel integration: Achieves interpretable sparse document-topic mixtures while retaining semantic richness via word embeddings, producing human-interpretable topics.
- Practical implementation: Demonstrates efficient, easy-to-implement training using modern autodiff frameworks, lowering adoption barriers.
- Semantic regularities: Shows the model captures meaningful token relationships (analogy-style semantics) beyond typical topic models.

Weaknesses:
- Lack of comparative analysis: No head-to-head comparisons with LDA, CTM, or recent neural topic models on standard metrics like topic coherence.
- Missing key related work: Fails to cite/discuss Tang et al.'s LDA2VEC (ACL 2016) and does not clarify differences or novelty relative to it.
- Unjustified hyperparameter choice: Sets Dirichlet α = n⁻¹ without ablation or theoretical justification to support sparsity claims.
- Limited scalability analysis: Omits training time, memory usage, and scaling behavior with corpus size or topic count.

Questions to authors:
- How does lda2vec compare quantitatively to LDA2VEC and other topic models on standard metrics?
- How does sharing topic vectors across documents differ theoretically and practically from standard topic model formulations?
- How sensitive is performance to the fixed context window size (five), and would dynamic windows help?
- How does the semantic quality of jointly trained word vectors compare quantitatively to standalone word embedding methods?
