- Proposes lda2vec, combining Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings to produce interpretable document representations.  
- Adds a sparsity-inducing Dirichlet likelihood term to the SGNS objective to produce sparse document-topic mixtures.  
- Demonstrates that learned representations preserve semantic relationships and topic coherence.

- Novel integration of topic modeling and word embeddings in a unified, easy-to-implement framework.  
- Dirichlet likelihood with α < 1 effectively induces sparse, interpretable topic mixtures (e.g., high coherence for a "Space" topic).  
- Model preserves semantic relationships enabling corpus-specific analogies (examples shown for Hacker News).  
- Practical contribution: open-source code, documentation, and unit tests that lower adoption barriers.

- Lacks comparative evaluation with recent baselines that integrate embeddings and topics (insufficient contrast with LDA2Vec and others).  
- Evaluation is limited to topic coherence and lacks downstream task assessments (e.g., classification, clustering).  
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of Dirichlet term and shared pivot/target word vectors.  
- Unclear novelty and insufficient positioning relative to prior work (especially LDA2Vec).

- How does lda2vec specifically differ from and improve upon LDA2Vec and similar prior work?  
- Have document representations been evaluated on downstream tasks, and how do they compare to LDA and other models?  
- Can you provide ablation studies showing the Dirichlet term’s contribution and the effect of shared word vectors?  
- How do you address biases and sensitivity of topic coherence to vocabulary size and corpus characteristics?
