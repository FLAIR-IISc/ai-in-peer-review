1. Proposes lda2vec: combines SGNS word embeddings with document vectors as convex mixtures over learned topic vectors, using pivot+document context and a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity regularizer; evaluated on 20NG and Hacker News (Cv reported).

2. Contribution: simple architectural coupling of SGNS with sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures in a shared embedding space.

3. Contribution: empirical exploration showing reasonable topic coherence on 20NG and qualitative analyses on Hacker News, with released code.

4. Contribution: practical recipe to induce sparsity in document-topic proportions via a Dirichlet log-likelihood regularizer on softmax-normalized mixture weights.

5. Strength: conceptual clarity and simplicity—bridges SGNS-style embeddings and LDA-like interpretable mixtures without variational inference.

6. Strength: shared embedding space allows easy inspection/diagnostics (nearest words, topic–word linearities, word-topic analogies).

7. Strength: qualitative results appear convincing (coherent topics, Cv ≈0.56 average, strong examples).

8. Strength: reproducibility and practicality—autodiff/GPU implementation and released preprocessing/code.

9. Weakness: limited novelty—approach closely resembles PV-DM and overlaps with prior topical embedding and neural topic models; sharper positioning needed.

10. Weakness: inadequate evaluation—no quantitative baselines (LDA, PV-DM, DocNADE, NVDM, etc.), no downstream document-task results, and anecdotal analogy evidence only.

11. Weakness: methodological ambiguities and clarity issues—softmax dimensionality phrasing, tying of input/output embeddings, missing SGNS choices (subsampling, dynamic windows), unclear dropout details.

12. Weakness: lack of ablations and sensitivity analyses—no quantitative sparsity metrics or experiments varying α, λ, topic count, negative sampling exponent, or embedding tying.

13. Weakness: missing probabilistic grounding and likelihood evaluation—model not presented as coherent generative model and perplexity/held-out likelihood are absent.

14. Question: clarify softmax output dimensionality—should map to K topics, not R300 (typo/clarification requested).

15. Question: provide quantitative comparisons to LDA (Cv and classification), PV-DM/doc2vec (classification/interpretability), and DocNADE/NVDM using identical preprocessing.

16. Question: report quantitative sparsity of document-topic mixtures (entropy, top-3 mass) and how it varies with α and λ; show coherence–sparsity trade-off.

17. Question: justify tying input and output embeddings and report results for untied embeddings on coherence and analogy tasks.

18. Question: state whether subsampling of frequent tokens and dynamic window sizes were applied and their effects.

19. Question: provide LDA baseline Cv on 20NG (same preprocessing) and statistical comparison to lda2vec results.

20. Question: do topics collapse onto frequent words without extra regularization, and were decorrelation/orthogonality penalties tried to reduce redundancy?

21. Overall: idea is appealing and useful but needs stronger baselines, ablations, and clearer methodological presentation to be a strong conference submission.
