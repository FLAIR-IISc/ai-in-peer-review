- Proposes lda2vec: joint objective that learns SGNS word embeddings and sparse simplex-constrained document–topic mixtures in the same vector space.
- Context vector equals pivot word vector plus document vector (document = simplex mixture over learned topic vectors).
- Adds a Dirichlet log-likelihood regularizer to encourage sparsity in document–topic proportions.
- Implemented with autodiff, negative sampling, dropout, and Adam; evaluated on 20 Newsgroups and a large Hacker News corpus.
- Contribution: practical, easily implementable joint model coupling SGNS with interpretable sparse topic mixtures.
- Contribution: empirical finding that Dirichlet sparsity regularization improves qualitative interpretability and topic coherence.
- Contribution: open-source implementation demonstrating scalability to moderately large corpora.
- Strength: simple, practical, and adoptable due to reliance on standard components and auto-diff frameworks.
- Strength: simplex constraint yields sparse, interpretable document mixtures and allows inspecting topic vectors in embedding space.
- Strength: shows reasonable topic coherence on 20NG and qualitatively coherent Hacker News topics while preserving SGNS analogy properties.
- Strength: training recipe and scalability demonstrated on a 75M-token corpus; minibatch scaling of Dirichlet term is pragmatic.
- Strength: reproducibility aided by released code, preprocessing scripts, and explicit hyperparameters.
- Weakness: limited novelty—combines known ideas (Doc2Vec-like additive document vectors and LDA-like mixtures) with a Dirichlet regularizer.
- Weakness: weak baselines and incomplete evaluation—no comparisons to LDA, Doc2Vec, GloVe+LDA, neural topic models, or downstream tasks.
- Weakness: Dirichlet term treated heuristically as a regularizer with ad-hoc hyperparameters and insufficient sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: lacking ablation studies (Dirichlet effect, additive vs alternative composition, tied vs untied embeddings, initialization).
- Weakness: unclear procedure for inferring document–topic proportions for unseen documents and limited guidance on topic number selection.
- Questions: provide quantitative comparisons to LDA, Doc2Vec, and two-stage embedding+LDA pipelines and at least one downstream task.
- Questions: report sensitivity of results to α and λ and training stability (topic collapse vs diffuseness).
- Questions: describe inference procedure, initialization, stopping criteria, and efficiency for unseen documents.
- Questions: analyze impact of tying vs untying embeddings and additive vs alternative composition on coherence and analogies.
- Questions: report training time and memory usage compared to SGNS and LDA and discuss scalability limits.
- Overall: method is sound as an objective and practical, moderately original, and potentially useful but would be strengthened by stronger baselines, ablations, and clearer justification of the Dirichlet regularizer and inference.
