Summary
This paper proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model that augments Skip-gram with Negative Sampling (SGNS) by adding document vectors constrained to be sparse mixtures over learned topic vectors on the probability simplex. The context vector is the sum of a pivot word vector and a document vector, and sparsity is encouraged via a Dirichlet regularization term. The model is implemented in an autodiff framework and evaluated qualitatively and with topic coherence on 20 Newsgroups and qualitatively on a large Hacker News corpus.

Contributions (as I see them)
1) A practical joint training scheme that embeds words, topics, and documents in a shared space, combining SGNS with simplex-constrained, Dirichlet-regularized document-topic mixtures to yield interpretable document representations.
2) Empirical evidence that the shared space enables direct interpretability of topics via nearest-neighbor words and preserves linear word regularities on in-domain data.
3) An accessible implementation leveraging automatic differentiation and GPUs.

Strengths
- Interpretability without discarding SGNS benefits: The simplex-constrained document representations yield sparse mixtures whose top tokens are coherent and human-interpretable, while the embedding space preserves analogical structure (e.g., domain-specific analogies on Hacker News).
- Simplicity and implementability: The approach is architecturally simple (sum of word and document vectors plus a Dirichlet term), easily implementable in autodiff frameworks, and should be scalable; open-source code is provided.
- Positive coherence on 20NG: Reported average Cv ≈ 0.56 with best configuration (n=20, β=0.75), and example topics align well with known newsgroups (e.g., Space, Encryption), supporting the interpretability claim.
- Unsupervised learning over large corpus: The Hacker News experiment (75M tokens) demonstrates scalability and domain adaptation, including meaningful phrase-level tokens.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: The model is a straightforward coupling of SGNS with a softmax-simplex document mixture and an ad hoc Dirichlet regularizer, lacking a deeper probabilistic formulation or new inference technique.
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation and baselines: No comparisons to strong topic/embedding baselines (e.g., LDA/NMF/CTM for coherence, doc2vec/TWE/GloVe for embeddings), no downstream tasks (e.g., document classification, retrieval), and no statistical testing.
- Missing ablations/sensitivity: No study of the impact of λ, α, topic count, dropout, initialization (pretrained vs random), or the design choice of summation vs alternatives. The minibatch scaling of the Dirichlet term is introduced but not analyzed.
- Coherence only on one corpus: Coherence for Hacker News is omitted due to tokenization mismatch; alternative coherence computations or an internal reference corpus could mitigate this.
- Potential confounds: Use of pretrained embeddings on 20NG may inflate coherence/NN quality; effects are not isolated.

Questions to Authors
- How does lda2vec’s coherence and topic sparsity compare to LDA (and NMF) on 20NG under identical preprocessing?
- What is the effect of λ and α on coherence, sparsity, and downstream performance? Please provide ablations.
- Can you report runtime/memory vs LDA and doc2vec, and show downstream tasks (e.g., doc classification or retrieval) to quantify utility of the learned representations?
