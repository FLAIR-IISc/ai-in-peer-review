- Proposes lda2vec: augment SGNS by adding simplex-constrained, Dirichlet-regularized document vectors summed with word vectors to produce interpretable document-topic mixtures.
- Jointly trains words, topics, and documents in a shared embedding space, enabling sparse, interpretable document representations.
- Shows that topics are directly interpretable via nearest-neighbor words and that embedding linearities are preserved in-domain.
- Implements the model in an autodiff/GPU framework and releases accessible code.
- Demonstrates reasonable topic coherence on 20 Newsgroups (Cv ≈ 0.56 for best config) with example topics matching known newsgroups.
- Scales to a large Hacker News corpus (75M tokens) and handles phrase-level tokens, showing domain adaptation qualitatively.
- Limited novelty: essentially a straightforward coupling of SGNS with simplex document mixtures and an ad hoc Dirichlet regularizer without deeper probabilistic grounding.
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation and baselines: no comparisons to LDA/NMF/CTM for coherence or doc2vec/TWE/GloVe for embeddings, no downstream task results, and no statistical testing.
- Missing ablations/sensitivity analyses (λ, α, topic count, dropout, initialization, sum vs alternative composition) and no analysis of minibatch scaling for the Dirichlet term.
- Coherence reported only for 20NG; Hacker News coherence omitted due to tokenization mismatch, limiting quantitative claims on large corpus.
- Potential confound: use of pretrained embeddings on 20NG may inflate coherence and nearest-neighbor quality without isolating effects.
- Requests to authors: compare coherence and sparsity to LDA/NMF under identical preprocessing, provide λ/α ablations, and report runtime/memory plus downstream task performance (classification/retrieval).
