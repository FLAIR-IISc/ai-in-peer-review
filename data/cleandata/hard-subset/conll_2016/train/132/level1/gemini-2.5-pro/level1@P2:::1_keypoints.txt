- Summary: Introduces lda2vec, which augments SGNS by summing word and document vectors where document vectors are mixtures of global topic vectors, trained end-to-end with a Dirichlet term to induce sparse, interpretable topics; evaluated on 20 Newsgroups and Hacker News showing coherent topics and quality word embeddings.

- Contribution 1: Novel, simple integration of document-level topic structure into SGNS via context = word vector + document vector, embedding words, topics, and documents in one space.

- Contribution 2: Practical hybrid framework leveraging automatic differentiation and a Dirichlet log-likelihood term to avoid complex variational/Gibbs inference.

- Strength 1: Conceptually clear and elegant modification that injects document semantics and enforces topic sparsity similar to LDA.

- Strength 2: Unified vector space for words, topics, and documents improves interpretability and topic-word retrieval.

- Strength 3: Strong qualitative results, especially on Hacker News, showing coherent domain topics and preserved word-embedding analogies.

- Strength 4: Code released and built on standard tools (Chainer, Adam), aiding reproducibility and practicality.

- Weakness 1: Lacks quantitative comparisons to baselines (LDA, doc2vec, other joint models) and no extrinsic downstream evaluations like classification or retrieval.

- Weakness 2: Key hyperparameters (λ=200, α=1/n) are not justified or analyzed for sensitivity and cross-dataset robustness.

- Weakness 3: Limited ablation study—no quantitative evidence showing impact of removing Dirichlet term, varying α, or other components like dropout or the vector-sum design.

- Weakness 4: Ambiguity in minibatch approximation for the global Dirichlet loss (scaling by minibatch_size/corpus_size) with no analysis of its theoretical impact or bias on convergence.

- Question 1: Please provide quantitative comparisons against LDA and Paragraph Vectors on 20 Newsgroups, including topic coherence and downstream task performance.

- Question 2: How was λ=200 chosen and how sensitive are topic coherence and word-vector quality to this hyperparameter; does the optimal value transfer across datasets?

- Question 3: Please justify and analyze the minibatch scaling used for the Dirichlet likelihood (minibatch_size/corpus_size) and its effect on gradients and convergence.
