Summary:
- Proposes lda2vec: augments SGNS by predicting context = word vector + document vector, where document vector is a sparse mixture of shared topic vectors.
- Encourages topic sparsity via a Dirichlet log-likelihood term; model is end-to-end trainable with automatic differentiation.
- Evaluated quantitatively (topic coherence on 20NG) and qualitatively (topics and word analogies on Hacker News).

Main contributions:
- Novel hybrid that unifies neural word embeddings (SGNS) and topic models (LDA) by embedding words, topics, and documents in a shared vector space.
- Practical implementation showing such generative+neural models can be optimized with standard autodiff tools, avoiding bespoke inference algorithms.
- Qualitative demonstrations that the model finds coherent, domain-specific topics and captures fine-grained semantic relations in word embeddings.

Strengths:
- Conceptually simple and elegant additive composition of word and document vectors grounding word prediction in document context.
- Unified representation space enabling direct comparison and interpretation of words, topics, and documents.
- Reproducible and practical: straightforward implementation in autodiff frameworks and open-source code provided.
- Convincing qualitative results (coherent Hacker News topics and domain-specific word analogies).

Weaknesses:
- No quantitative comparison to baseline topic models (e.g., Mallet/Gensim LDA) or to standard word-embedding benchmarks, so relative gains are unclear.
- Insufficient ablation/sensitivity analysis on the Dirichlet loss and hyperparameters (e.g., λ=200 appears arbitrary).
- Ambiguity in model specification: unclear what latent vectors are input to the softmax producing document-topic proportions p_jk.
- Missing precise formulation for adjusting the corpus-level Dirichlet term during mini-batch training.

Questions to authors:
- Please provide topic coherence scores for a standard LDA baseline (e.g., Cv on 20 Newsgroups) for comparison.
- Specify mathematically how document-topic proportions p_jk are generated: what are the "latent vectors" and how are they learned?
- Report quantitative effect on topic coherence on 20NG when the Dirichlet sparsity term is removed (λ=0).
