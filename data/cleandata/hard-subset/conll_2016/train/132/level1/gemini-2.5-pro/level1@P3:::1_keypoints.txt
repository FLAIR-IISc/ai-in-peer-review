- Introduces lda2vec: jointly learns word and topic vectors plus sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures by extending SGNS with document-specific context vectors and a Dirichlet regularizer.
- Core contribution: a novel, intuitive hybrid that unifies dense embeddings’ predictive power with LDA-style interpretability via shared vector space and additive context.
- Practical contribution: shows how a probabilistic Dirichlet constraint can be implemented as a neural-network regularizer within automatic differentiation, avoiding specialized inference algorithms.
- Strength: well-motivated, clean model design (context = word vector + document vector) with topics embedded in the same space as words for direct interpretation.
- Strength: methodological significance—integrates topic modeling into deep learning frameworks, making the approach accessible and extensible.
- Strength: convincing qualitative and quantitative results (high Cv coherence on 20 Newsgroups, interpretable topics, and domain-specific analogies on Hacker News).
- Weakness: lacks direct quantitative comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., tuned LDA for topic coherence, SGNS for embedding quality on same corpora).
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter and ablation analysis—no sensitivity study for λ or reported coherence when λ=0 to justify the Dirichlet term’s effect.
- Weakness: potential scalability and modeling-choice concerns—minibatch approximation of corpus-level Dirichlet loss lacks theoretical justification, and the choice of vector addition vs alternatives is not explored.
- Question: please provide baseline comparisons (Cv for LDA, embedding task scores for SGNS) on the same preprocessed datasets.
- Question: did the authors test whether document vectors can dominate pivot word vectors in strong-topic documents, and does dropout fully mitigate this?
- Question: how was λ=200 chosen—was it grid-searched, and can the authors provide coherence vs λ results to guide practitioners?
