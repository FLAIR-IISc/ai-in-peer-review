**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel model, lda2vec, that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings. lda2vec learns dense word vectors jointly with Dirichlet-distributed latent document-level mixtures of topic vectors, producing sparse and interpretable document representations. The model is simple to implement in automatic differentiation frameworks and allows for unsupervised document representations.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Hybrid approach**: The paper proposes a novel hybrid approach that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings, allowing for the learning of dense word vectors and sparse document representations.
2. **Dirichlet-optimized sparse topic mixtures**: The paper introduces a Dirichlet-optimized sparse topic mixture model that produces interpretable document representations.
3. **Automatic differentiation framework**: The paper demonstrates the ease of implementation of the model in automatic differentiation frameworks, such as Chainer.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a novel approach that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings, allowing for the learning of dense word vectors and sparse document representations.
2. **Interpretable results**: The model produces sparse and interpretable document representations, which is a significant advantage over traditional dense vector approaches.
3. **Easy to implement**: The paper demonstrates the ease of implementation of the model in automatic differentiation frameworks, such as Chainer.
4. **State-of-the-art results**: The paper reports state-of-the-art results on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset, with high mean topic coherences that correlate with human evaluations of topics.
5. **Specialized vocabulary learning**: The paper demonstrates the ability of the model to learn specialized vocabulary and linear relationships between words in the Hacker News comments corpus.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the model on two datasets, and it would be beneficial to see more extensive evaluation on other datasets.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process, which could be beneficial for readers who want to replicate the results.
3. **Comparison to other models**: The paper does not provide a detailed comparison to other models that combine topic models and word embeddings, which could be beneficial for understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.
4. **Theoretical analysis**: The paper does not provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the model, which could be beneficial for understanding the underlying mechanisms of the proposed approach.

**Questions to Authors**

2. How does the model perform on other datasets, and are there any specific datasets or domains where the model is particularly well-suited?
