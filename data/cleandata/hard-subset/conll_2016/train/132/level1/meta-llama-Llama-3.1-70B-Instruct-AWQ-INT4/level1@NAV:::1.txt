**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel model, lda2vec, that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings to learn interpretable document representations. The model extends the Skipgram Negative-Sampling (SGNS) objective to incorporate document-wide feature vectors and simultaneously learn continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments, and show that it can learn coherent topics and linear relationships between words.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Hybrid model**: The paper proposes a novel hybrid model that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings to learn interpretable document representations.
2. **Sparse and interpretable document representations**: The model produces sparse and interpretable document representations, which is a significant contribution over traditional dense document representations.
3. **Effective use of word embeddings**: The paper demonstrates the effective use of word embeddings in learning linear relationships between words and in solving word analogies.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a novel approach to learning document representations, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **Effective use of word embeddings**: The paper demonstrates the effective use of word embeddings in learning linear relationships between words and in solving word analogies.
3. **Interpretable results**: The model produces interpretable results, which is a significant advantage over traditional dense document representations.
4. **Scalability**: The model is scalable and can be trained on large datasets, as demonstrated by the authors on the Hacker News comments dataset.
5. **Flexibility**: The model can be easily extended to incorporate other types of features, such as metadata or external knowledge.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the model on two datasets, which may not be representative of all possible use cases.
2. **Lack of comparison**: The paper does not compare the performance of lda2vec with other state-of-the-art models, which makes it difficult to assess its relative performance.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of hyperparameter tuning, which may be important for achieving optimal performance.
4. **Computational complexity**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the model, which may be important for large-scale applications.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend the model to incorporate other types of features, such as metadata or external knowledge?
2. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on the performance of the model?
3. How do you plan to evaluate the model on other datasets and use cases?
4. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the model and its implications for large-scale applications?
