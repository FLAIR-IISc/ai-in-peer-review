**Summary**

This paper presents lda2vec, a hybrid model that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings to learn dense word vectors jointly with Dirichlet-distributed latent document-level mixtures of topic vectors. The model is designed to produce sparse, interpretable document mixtures through a non-negative simplex constraint, while also learning word vectors and the linear relationships between them. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments, and show that the model can discover coherent topics and learn linear relationships between words.

**Contributions**

1. **Hybrid model**: The authors propose a novel hybrid model that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings, allowing for the learning of dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document mixtures.
2. **Extension of word embeddings**: The authors extend the word embedding model to include document-wide feature vectors, enabling the learning of document representations that capture long-term themes.
3. **Interpretable document representations**: The authors design the model to produce sparse, interpretable document mixtures, making it easier to understand the underlying structure of the documents.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective topic discovery**: The authors demonstrate that lda2vec can discover coherent topics in two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments, and that the topics are interpretable and meaningful.
2. **Learning of linear relationships**: The authors show that lda2vec can learn linear relationships between words, enabling the model to solve word analogies in a specialized vocabulary.
3. **Simple implementation**: The authors note that the model is simple to implement in automatic differentiation frameworks, making it accessible to a wide range of researchers and practitioners.
4. **Flexibility**: The authors demonstrate that lda2vec can be applied to different datasets and can learn different types of relationships between words.
5. **Interpretability**: The authors design the model to produce sparse, interpretable document mixtures, making it easier to understand the underlying structure of the documents.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of lda2vec, which may be a concern for large-scale datasets.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process for lda2vec, which may be a concern for researchers and practitioners who are not familiar with the model.
3. **Comparison to other models**: The authors do not provide a detailed comparison of lda2vec to other hybrid models that combine topic models and word embeddings, which may be a concern for researchers and practitioners who are interested in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of different models.
4. **Evaluation metrics**: The authors primarily use topic coherence as an evaluation metric, which may not capture all aspects of the model's performance.
5. **Limited analysis of document representations**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the document representations learned by lda2vec, which may be a concern for researchers and practitioners who are interested in understanding the underlying structure of the documents.

**Questions to Authors**
