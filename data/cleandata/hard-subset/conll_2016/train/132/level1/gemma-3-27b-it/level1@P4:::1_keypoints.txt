- Presents lda2vec: joint model combining Dirichlet topic models with word embeddings using automatic differentiation.  
- Claims interpretability of traditional topic models while leveraging semantic richness of embeddings.  
- Primary contribution: seamless integration of topic models and embeddings enabling fast prototyping (Chainer) and open-source code.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical demos on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News showing coherent topics and preserved embedding semantics.  

Strengths:
- Novel integration of Dirichlet topic models and word embeddings within a differentiable joint-learning framework.  
- Practical implementation that leverages automatic differentiation (Chainer) to simplify probabilistic modeling and speed experimentation; code released.  
- Empirical validation across diverse datasets with topic coherence scores and example analogies, notably Hacker News-specific semantics.  
- Clear writing and motivation with helpful figures (e.g., model architecture).  

Weaknesses:
- Core components (Skipgram embeddings, Dirichlet topic modeling) are not novel; joint training challenges and their solutions are under-detailed.  
- Lacks comprehensive quantitative comparisons to strong baselines (LDA, Doc2Vec, NMF) on the same datasets.  
- Insufficient ablation studies on the Dirichlet likelihood and sparsity parameter α; rationale for chosen α (n−1) is weak.  
- Overreliance on topic coherence metric; needs human qualitative evaluation to validate interpretability.  

Questions for authors:
- What challenges arose during joint training and what techniques ensured stable convergence?  
- How does lda2vec quantitatively compare to LDA, Doc2Vec, NMF on the evaluated datasets?  
- How sensitive is performance to the Dirichlet α; please provide ablation results varying α.  
- Were human qualitative evaluations of topic interpretability performed; if so, what was the methodology and outcome?  

Additional comments:
- Equation (5) notation is unclear—clarify the summation’s purpose.  
- Provide concise details of noun-phrase tokenization used for the Hacker News corpus in the main paper.
