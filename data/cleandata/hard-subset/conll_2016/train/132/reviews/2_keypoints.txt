- Paper proposes a neural-styled topic model that extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparsifies them to mimic topic models.  
- Reviewer likes the model and examples but the paper lacks any empirical evaluation or direct comparison to standard/neural topic models.  
- Suggests evaluation via supervised document classification using topic representations or direct document-similarity datasets.  
- Publishing the GitHub link breaks anonymity and may warrant rejection.  
- Unclear how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected; potential cherry-picking.  
- Concern that computing topic-word representations by pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary is computationally infeasible.  
- Unclear tokenization details using SpaCy — which token types or noun-chunks are extracted and how multiword terms are handled.  
- No discussion of handling OOV terms when applying the trained model to novel documents after embedding updates.  
- Finding that 20 topics fits 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; propose an information-theoretic comparison to true labels and direct LDA comparison.  
- Must compare against prior neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015).  
- Minor issues: grammar/phrasing errors (e.g., "it's" vs "its", awkward "topic basis" sentence), Figure 5 caption should say "terms" not "words".  
- Reference formatting is inconsistent and some entries (e.g., Roder et al.) miss conference information.
