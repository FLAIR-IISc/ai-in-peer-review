- Proposes lda2vec to combine word embeddings with sparse, Dirichlet-distributed topic mixtures to bridge dense and interpretable document representations.  
- Uses automatic differentiation (Chainer) to jointly learn word vectors and topic mixtures, easing implementation and experimentation.  
- Demonstrates model on Hacker News, showing learned semantic relationships, but the demonstration is limited.  
- Strength: Well-motivated combination of embeddings and topic models for richer, interpretable document representations.  
- Strength: Practical use of AD frameworks to remove manual derivations and accelerate development.  
- Strength: Hacker News examples indicate the model can capture domain-specific token relationships.  
- Strength: Paper clearly frames the trade-offs between dense and sparse document representations.  
- Weakness: Section 2 is unclear and dense; notation and explanations lack sufficient intuition and need rewriting.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is insufficient—only example topics shown and coherence reported only in a figure caption, with no baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness: Several design choices (e.g., shared pivot/target vectors, hyperparameters λ and α) are unjustified.  
- Weakness: Anonymity violation via repository link, plus minor writing and formatting errors and an unclear intro paragraph.  
- Weakness: Conclusion overstates results (e.g., claiming it "solves word analogies") based on cherry-picked examples.  
- Question: Request for an intuitive explanation of the loss function and the meaning of “separating words from a marginal distribution.”  
- Question: Why use the same word vectors for pivot and target words instead of separate vectors as in standard word2vec?  
- Question: Please provide quantitative comparisons to LDA, NMF, doc2vec, and Sentence Transformers across datasets with metrics (perplexity, coherence, downstream tasks).  
- Question: Explain choices for λ, α, β and provide a sensitivity analysis showing their impact.  
- Additional: Recommend adding a high-level architecture diagram to clarify the model.  
- Additional: Reference list has a formatting error (Chang et al. missing venue).  
- Additional: Dirichlet likelihood term (Ld) is tersely described and needs a more detailed explanation.
