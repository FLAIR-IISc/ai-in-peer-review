- Proposes lda2vec: jointly learn word vectors and sparse document-topic mixtures to combine word2vec-style embeddings with LDA interpretability.  
- Main contribution is practical integration/demonstration within automatic differentiation frameworks rather than novel methodology.

- Strength: Demonstrates feasible integration of embeddings and topic models that simplifies training and experimentation.  
- Strength: Empirical examples show the model captures meaningful semantic relationships in word vectors.  
- Strength: Dirichlet prior yields sparse, more interpretable document-topic mixtures aligning with known categories.

- Weakness: Technical description is dense, notationally heavy, and hard to follow, impairing understanding and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited and qualitative; lacks comprehensive quantitative comparisons to LDA, NMF, Doc2Vec, and downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: Several design choices (e.g., shared pivot/target vectors, ambiguous phrases like “separate words from a marginal distribution” and “co-adaptation”) are insufficiently motivated or explained.  
- Weakness: Footnote linking to code violates conference anonymity and should be removed.

- Q: Please provide a clear high-level explanation/diagram of how word2vec and LDA components interact.  
- Q: Why use the same word vectors for pivot and target words instead of separate vectors as in standard word2vec?  
- Q: How does lda2vec perform versus established topic models and document embeddings on standard datasets and downstream tasks?  
- Q: Can you clarify terms like “separate words from a marginal distribution” and “co-adaptation” in your model context?  
- Q: What is the role of the Dirichlet hyperparameter λ and how sensitive are results to its value?
