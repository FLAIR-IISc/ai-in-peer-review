- Paper proposes lda2vec: joint model combining word2vec-style embeddings with Dirichlet topic models to learn dense word vectors and sparse interpretable document representations.
- Idea is potentially interesting and promising, especially the unified framework and open-source release.
- Major clarity issues: Section 2 is largely incomprehensible and lacks a clear high-level overview linking to word2vec and LDA.
- Several concepts are introduced without explanation (e.g., “separate words from a marginal distribution”, “co-adaptation”).
- No justification provided for using the same word vectors for pivot and target words, deviating from standard word2vec practice.
- Ambiguous phrasing throughout (e.g., “If we only included structure up to this point”) and minor typos (e.g., "it's similarity" → "its").
- Footnote 1 breaks anonymity and must be removed.
- Primary weakness: complete absence of rigorous quantitative evaluation; qualitative examples and clusters are insufficient.
- Figure 2’s quantitative information is buried in the caption, preventing proper analysis.
- Conclusion overstates results (claims to “solve word analogies”) based on cherry-picked examples.
- Reference formatting errors (Chang et al. missing venue) need correction.
- Questions for authors: provide a clear high-level model explanation and clarity on how it builds on word2vec and LDA.
- Questions for authors: justify using the same pivot/target vectors and explain differences from standard word2vec.
- Questions for authors: supply detailed quantitative evaluation (beyond topic coherence) including word similarity and downstream tasks.
- Questions for authors: clarify the meaning of “separate words from a marginal distribution” and explain “co-adaptation” and how the approach addresses it.
