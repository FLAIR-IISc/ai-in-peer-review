- Proposes lda2vec: combines SGNS word embeddings with a Dirichlet-based topic model to learn dense word vectors and interpretable document-topic mixtures; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.

Main contributions:
- Integrates word2vec-style training with a Dirichlet topic model to combine semantic embeddings and interpretable topics.
- Demonstrates the model on a large, domain-specific Hacker News corpus to show domain-specific semantic relationships.
- Implements the model using an automatic differentiation framework (Chainer) for practical training and experimentation.

Strengths:
- The integration of embeddings and topic modeling is an interesting and relevant direction for representation learning.
- Experiments on Hacker News suggest the model can learn meaningful domain-specific representations and analogies.
- Use of Chainer/automatic differentiation aids reproducibility and ease of implementation.
- Figure 1 gives a clear high-level model overview.

Weaknesses:
- Section 2 is unclear and technically convoluted; needs a complete rewrite for clarity on design and integrations.
- Several design choices lack justification (e.g., using the same vector for pivot and target, unclear notion of “separating words from a marginal distribution”).
- Ambiguous phrasing and typos reduce clarity (example: “If we only included structure up to this point”; “it's similarity”).
- Footnote 1 breaks author anonymity, violating double-blind review policy.
- Evaluation is insufficient and largely qualitative; lacks standard quantitative topic-modeling metrics and rigorous analogy tests.
- Conclusions overstate results (claiming it “solves word analogies”) based on cherry-picked examples.
- Citation formatting error: Chang et al. (2009) missing venue.

Questions to authors:
- Please provide a simplified, intuitive explanation of the model architecture (especially Section 2) and how it relates to standard word2vec and LDA.
- Why use identical vectors for pivot and target words instead of separate input/output vectors as in word2vec?
- What exactly is meant by “separating words from a marginal distribution” and which technique is used?
- Will you add comprehensive quantitative evaluations (standard topic metrics and rigorous analogy assessments)?
- How sensitive are reported topic coherence scores to preprocessing and hyperparameter choices?

Additional comments:
- Discuss the impact of using noun-phrase preprocessing on model performance.
- Include details on computational resources (training time, memory usage).
