- Novel idea: jointly learning word embeddings with sparse, interpretable Dirichlet document mixtures is promising.
- Clear presentation: model architecture and diagrams are well explained and helpful.
- Qualitative examples: Figures 3–5 show compelling, meaningful topics and semantic relationships.
- Major flaw — no empirical evaluation: lacks quantitative results or comparisons to LDA, NMF, or neural topic models.
- Missing baselines: no supervised classification or document-similarity benchmarks to validate representations.
- Anonymity breach: including the GitHub link violates double-blind submission rules.
- Potential cherry-picking: example selection is unclear and may be biased without systematic evaluation.
- Scalability concern: computing topic–word representations via pairwise comparisons with full vocabulary seems computationally expensive.
- Missing implementation details: unclear tokenization choices with SpaCy and handling of multiword terms.
- OOV handling absent: no description of how out-of-vocabulary terms are managed for new documents.
- Topic-number claim weak: stating 20 topics fit 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising without information-theoretic or LDA comparison.
- Questions for authors: request quantitative evaluations, justification/systematic topic quality measures, computational cost/scale strategies, SpaCy tokenization details, and OOV handling.
- Minor issues: grammatical errors, incorrect Figure 5 caption ("terms" not "words"), and inconsistent/incomplete reference formatting.
