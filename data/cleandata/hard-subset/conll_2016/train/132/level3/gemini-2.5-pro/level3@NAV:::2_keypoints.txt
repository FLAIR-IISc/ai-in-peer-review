- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, a joint word–document–topic neural model that extends skip-gram with document vectors as weighted mixtures of topic vectors and uses a Dirichlet prior for sparse, interpretable topic weights, trained end-to-end in an autodiff framework.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel architecture that merges dense word embeddings with sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures in a single jointly-trained model.
- Contribution 2: Shows implementation of the hybrid model within a modern automatic-differentiation framework, avoiding custom inference derivations.
- Strength 1: Conceptually appealing and intuitive fusion of skip-gram objectives with sparse document-topic mixtures to learn multi-level representations.
- Strength 2: Qualitative results on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News show coherent topics and meaningful semantic regularities/analogies.
- Weakness 1: Lacks quantitative empirical evaluation and comparisons to baselines (e.g., LDA, neural topic models) or downstream-task validation.
- Weakness 2: Violates double-blind review policy by including a public GitHub link that reveals author identities.
- Weakness 3: Methodological details are missing, hindering reproducibility and suggesting possible cherry-picking of qualitative examples; absent details on tokenization, OOV handling, and computational feasibility.
- Weakness 4: Presentation problems (grammatical errors, inconsistent references, incorrect figure caption) detract from the manuscript.
