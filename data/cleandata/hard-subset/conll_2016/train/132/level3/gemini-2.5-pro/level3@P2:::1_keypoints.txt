- Proposal: lda2vec jointly learns word embeddings and interpretable document/topic vectors by combining SGNS-style word2vec with a Dirichlet-inspired topic mixture, training end-to-end.

- Contribution 1: Novel architecture that unifies dense word embeddings and sparse topic models in a shared continuous space for words, documents, and topics.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that complex hybrid probabilistic models can be implemented and trained using automatic differentiation frameworks without custom inference.

- Strength 1: Conceptually appealing and novel idea enabling direct interpretability by comparing topic vectors to word vectors.

- Strength 2: Compelling qualitative results showing coherent topics, domain-specific word similarities, and some linear analogies in the learned embeddings.

- Strength 3: Practicality and reproducibility via implementation in a standard deep learning framework and an open-source release.

- Weakness 1: Model description is unclear and under-motivated (Section 2), with undefined terms and questionable design choices (e.g., single embedding matrix for pivot/target).

- Weakness 2: Insufficient quantitative evaluation—mostly cherry-picked qualitative examples and topic coherence scores without comparisons to standard baselines or benchmark tasks.

- Weakness 3: Some claims are overstated or unsubstantiated (e.g., "solves word analogies") and broader assertions about automatic differentiation lack supporting evidence.

- Question 1: Why use a single word embedding matrix for pivot and target words—were separate matrices tried and how did they affect performance?

- Question 2: Clarify the probabilistic interpretation of the `Ld` "Dirichlet-likelihood" applied to softmax outputs—does it act as a sparsity-inducing regularizer rather than a true likelihood?

- Question 3: Provide baseline comparisons (e.g., vanilla LDA for topic coherence, SGNS for analogy/similarity) to contextualize results and quantify trade-offs.

- Question 4: Define "preventing co-adaptation" in the model context and explain the role/effect of dropout in achieving it.
