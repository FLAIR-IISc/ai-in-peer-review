- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, which jointly learns word embeddings and sparse document-topic mixtures by predicting words from the sum of a pivot word vector and a document vector (document = mixture of topic vectors) with a Dirichlet loss to encourage sparsity; trained end-to-end and shown qualitatively on 20NG and Hacker News.

- Contribution 1: Novel hybrid architecture combining SGNS-style word embeddings with a decomposed document vector of topic mixtures, creating a unified embedding space for words, topics, and documents.

- Contribution 2: Practical demonstration of incorporating a Dirichlet prior as a loss term within an automatic-differentiation framework to simplify prototyping of probabilistic components.

- Strength 1: Conceptually elegant and novel—the additive context (~wj + ~dj) and topic decomposition yield interpretable global context alongside local word context.

- Strength 2: Strong qualitative results—topics appear coherent and the model retains SGNS semantic/syntactic regularities (similarity and analogy examples).

- Strength 3: Implementation and reproducibility potential through use of an autodiff framework and released code (noting procedural issue).

- Weakness 1: Major lack of quantitative evaluation and baseline comparisons (e.g., LDA, neural topic models); no downstream or intrinsic metrics reported.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient discussion and comparison to related neural topic-modeling prior work.

- Weakness 3: Risk of cherry-picking and vague experimental/implementation details (selection of examples, how top words per topic are computed, tokenization).

- Weakness 4: Violation of double-blind policy by including a GitHub link in the submission.

- Question 1: Please provide quantitative comparisons to LDA and other neural topic models on benchmarks and downstream tasks.

- Question 2: Clarify how the figures/examples were selected to address potential cherry-picking.

- Question 3: Explain the practical method and scalability for identifying top-N words for each topic.

- Question 4: Have you evaluated alignment of learned document-topic distributions with ground-truth labels (e.g., NMI) and the choice of topic number?

- Question 5: Describe how representations for new/unseen documents are inferred—retraining vs. fixed-embedding inference.
