- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid that jointly learns word and topic embeddings plus sparse Dirichlet document-topic mixtures by combining SGNS (word2vec) with LDA-style document structure.
- Contribution: Main contribution is the unified model and objective that merges neural embeddings with probabilistic topic modeling, implemented with automatic differentiation.

- Strength — Novelty: Elegant and promising hybrid of word2vec and LDA that could let word-level semantics inform topics and document themes contextualize word meanings.
- Strength — Practicality: Implementation in an automatic-differentiation framework (Chainer) avoids bespoke inference algorithms, making the approach accessible and extensible.
- Strength — Qualitative results: Presented coherent, interpretable topics and plausible word analogies on sample datasets, indicating the model can meet its goals qualitatively.

- Weakness — Opaque technical description: Section 2 lacks clear high-level intuition or generative explanation; key mechanics (e.g., formation of context vector) are poorly explained and use confusing phrasing.
- Weakness — Missing quantitative evaluation: No comparisons to baselines (e.g., standard LDA, SGNS) for topic coherence or word-vector benchmarks, no extrinsic tasks, and overclaimed analogy results based on cherry-picked examples.
- Weakness — Unclear motivation for design choices: Important choices (using same vector for pivot and target, claims in the introduction, and the use of dropout for “co-adaptation”) are not justified or explained.

- Question: Please provide a clearer, step-by-step/high-level or generative description of the model and the forward pass from document+pivot to loss.
- Question: Why use the same vectors for pivot and target words instead of separate input/output vectors, and what is the empirical impact of this choice?
- Question: Provide quantitative comparisons to relevant baselines for topic coherence, word-vector quality (e.g., WordSim/analogy benchmarks), and extrinsic tasks (classification/IR).
- Question: Clarify what is meant by “co-adaptation” between document and pivot vectors and why dropout is the appropriate mitigation.

- Additional: Footnote links to a public GitHub break reviewer anonymity by revealing the author’s name.
- Additional: Typo — “it's similarity” should be “its similarity.”
- Additional: Missing/incorrect reference formatting for Chang et al. (2009); venue is not listed.
