- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, which combines SGNS (word2vec) with LDA-like sparse topic mixtures to jointly learn word, topic, and document vectors; uses a Dirichlet-like regularizer and is implemented with automatic differentiation, demonstrated qualitatively on 20NG and Hacker News.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a hybrid model that unifies dense word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures in a single neural framework.

- Contribution 2: Shows a simple way to impose Dirichlet-like sparsity via a regularization term in a neural network, avoiding complex variational/Gibbs inference.

- Strength 1: Elegant and novel formulation (context = word vector + document-topic mixture) that injects document-level theme into SGNS.

- Strength 2: Explicit design for interpretability through sparse, non-negative document-topic mixtures producing coherent, inspectable topics.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates semantic regularities and analogy-like structure in learned embeddings, especially on domain-specific data.

- Weakness 1: No quantitative evaluation or comparisons to baselines (LDA, Doc2Vec, neural topic models) or standard tasks (classification, retrieval, perplexity).

- Weakness 2: Risk of cherry-picking and vague experimental details (selection of examples, tokenization specifics, and how nearest words to topic vectors were computed).

- Weakness 3: Paper violates double-blind review by linking to a public GitHub repository.

- Weakness 4: Does not explain how to infer topic proportions for new/out-of-sample documents without retraining.

- Question 1: Please provide quantitative comparisons to established baselines on standard downstream tasks.

- Question 2: How were the topics, words, and analogy examples chosen—was selection systematic or potentially cherry-picked?

- Question 3: What is the procedure and computational cost for retrieving nearest words to a topic vector?

- Question 4: How are document-topic proportions inferred for unseen documents without retraining embeddings?

- Question 5: Please address the inclusion of the public GitHub link and the resulting anonymity policy violation.
