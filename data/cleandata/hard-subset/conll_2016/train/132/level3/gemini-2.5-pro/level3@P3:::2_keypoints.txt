- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model combining word2vec-style embeddings with sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures trained end-to-end with a Dirichlet regularizer; demonstrated qualitatively on two corpora.

- Contribution: Introduces an elegant architecture that unifies dense word vectors and sparse topic-based document representations via document vectors expressed as sparse mixtures of topic vectors.

- Strength: Novel and intuitive architecture: context = pivot word vector + document vector, with document vector decomposed into topic mixtures embedding words, documents, and topics in a shared space.

- Strength: Emphasis on interpretability: Dirichlet-based regularizer promotes sparse, LDA-like document-topic distributions producing coherent, human-readable topics.

- Strength: Compelling qualitative results: coherent topics on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News and preserved word-vector analogies specialized to domain.

- Weakness: No quantitative evaluation or baselines: lacks downstream task performance (e.g., classification, retrieval) and comparisons to LDA, doc2vec, or other benchmarks.

- Weakness: Missing comparison to prior neural topic models: fails to situate and empirically compare against related neural-topic work (e.g., Cao et al., Nguyen et al., Shamanta et al.).

- Weakness: Possible cherry-picking and weak experimental design: unclear selection of examples, unsurprising choice of 20 topics for 20 Newsgroups, and no rigorous cluster-label comparison metrics.

- Weakness: Unclear methodological and practical details: scalability of finding top words for topics, tokenization and multi-word phrase handling, and out-of-vocabulary (OOV) handling for new documents are not addressed.

- Question: Why were no quantitative, task-based evaluations performed and are there plans to compare lda2vec to standard baselines on such tasks?

- Question: How were the example topics and word analogies selected—randomly or cherry-picked?

- Question: How are top words per topic computed in practice—does it require a full dot product against the vocabulary, and what are the computational costs at scale?

- Question: Please clarify the tokenization and multi-word phrase procedure for Hacker News and how multi-word phrases are represented during training.

- Additional comment: Inclusion of a public GitHub link violates double-blind review and may warrant rejection.

- Additional comment: Paper contains minor grammatical and phrasing errors that need correction.

- Additional comment: Bibliography formatting is inconsistent and several entries are incomplete or missing venue information.
