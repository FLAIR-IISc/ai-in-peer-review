- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, combining skip-gram word embeddings with Dirichlet-constrained sparse document-topic mixtures to yield semantically meaningful and interpretable document representations.

- Strength: Elegant, simple model design that leverages automatic differentiation and GPUs for efficient hybrid model prototyping.

- Strength: Example topics show strong coherence and domain-specific semantic regularities on the Hacker News corpus.

- Strength: Dirichlet likelihood with low concentration effectively sparsifies document-topic mixtures, improving interpretability over dense representations.

- Weakness: Lacks empirical baselines and direct comparisons to LDA and neural topic models; coherence scores are reported without significance or baselines.

- Weakness: GitHub link in the paper violates double-blind review policy and should be removed for resubmission.

- Weakness: Topic selection methodology for Figures 3-6 is unclear, raising concerns about cherry-picking examples.

- Weakness: Computational feasibility of computing topic-word similarities over the full vocabulary is not addressed; potential O(n²) cost for large corpora.

- Weakness: No discussion of handling out-of-vocabulary terms or details on tokenization and multiword term treatment for deployment.

- Question: How were the specific topics in Figures 3-6 selected—please provide a clear selection methodology?

- Question: What is the computational complexity of topic-word similarity calculations and how does this scale to large corpora?

- Question: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary terms during inference when word vectors are not updated?

- Question: Can you provide direct comparisons to LDA and other neural topic models on standard benchmarks for coherence and downstream tasks?

- Minor: Paper contains grammatical errors and awkward phrasing (e.g., "it's" vs "its," "topic basis"); Figure 5 caption should use "terms" not "words."

- Minor: Reference formatting is inconsistent and some entries lack conference details (e.g., Röder et al. 2015).

- Minor: The claim about 20 topics fitting 20 Newsgroups needs information-theoretic justification or direct LDA comparison to support the observation.
