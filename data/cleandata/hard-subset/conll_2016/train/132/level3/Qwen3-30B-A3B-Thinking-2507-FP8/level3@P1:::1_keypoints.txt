- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, combining word embeddings with Dirichlet-distributed topic mixtures to jointly learn word, topic, and document-topic vectors in a common space.

- Contribution 1: Simple hybrid model implementation that leverages automatic differentiation for rapid prototyping of topic–embedding architectures.

- Contribution 2: Uses a Dirichlet likelihood with low concentration to enforce sparsity in document-topic mixtures for interpretable representations.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates the model can learn domain-specific semantic relationships in specialized corpora.

- Strength 1: Simplicity — avoids deriving new inference algorithms by using modern autodiff frameworks, lowering the barrier to experiment with topic models.

- Strength 2: Produces interpretable topics on standard datasets (Twenty Newsgroups) with competitive coherence scores (0.567) and alignment with newsgroup categories.

- Strength 3: Shows promising domain-specific semantic relations (Hacker News), capturing meaningful analogies and topical structure.

- Weakness 1: Technical exposition is unclear and confusing (Section 2 hard to follow; key concepts and motivations poorly explained).

- Weakness 2: Lacks proper quantitative evaluation and comparisons to established topic models; coherence scores and Hacker News results are insufficiently analyzed.

- Weakness 3: Claims of "solving word analogies" are overstated and based on cherry-picked examples without systematic benchmarking.

- Weakness 4: Contains technical errors and ambiguities (typos, unclear "co-adaptation" explanation, inadequate justification for Dirichlet term, incomplete citation).

- Weakness 5: Footnote linking code repository breaks double-blind anonymity, which is a serious procedural issue.

- Question 1: Why use the same word vectors for pivot and target words instead of separate vectors as in standard word2vec?

- Question 2: Please provide quantitative topic coherence comparisons against established topic models on Twenty Newsgroups.

- Question 3: How does model performance and computational efficiency scale with corpus size compared to other topic models?

- Question 4: Provide a clearer explanation of "co-adaptation" and how dropout mitigates it.
