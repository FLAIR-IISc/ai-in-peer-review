- Proposed "lda2vec": extends word2vec to learn document embeddings with Dirichlet-induced sparsity to mimic topic models.
- Contributions: joint learning of document, topic, and word vectors in a shared space.
- Contributions: method for producing interpretable topic representations comparable to traditional topic models.
- Contributions: implementation designed to integrate with automatic differentiation frameworks.
- Strength: conceptually innovative blend of topic models and word embeddings, offering sparse document-topic mixtures with dense word vectors.
- Strength: example topics (e.g., Space, Encryption, X Windows) appear meaningful and suggest practical interpretability.
- Strength: implementation simplicity and ease of integration with autodiff tools.
- Weakness: no direct empirical comparison to LDA or neural topic models; coherence scores lack contextual baselines.
- Weakness: anonymity violation by including a public GitHub link.
- Weakness: unclear selection of example topics and potential cherry-picking; claim that 20 topics fits 20 Newsgroups is unsubstantiated.
- Weakness: computational/scalability concerns from pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary are unaddressed.
- Weakness: technical gaps in tokenization details (SpaCy usage, multiword terms) and omission of OOV handling for new documents.
- Question: how will you perform empirical comparisons to standard and neural topic models, and which metrics/baselines will be used?
- Question: please clarify the tokenization procedure for the Hacker News corpus and handling of multiword terms relative to Palmetto.
- Question: how are out-of-vocabulary terms handled when applying the trained model to novel documents?
- Question: how is scalability addressed for computing topic-word representations with large vocabularies?
- Question: provide an anonymized implementation link to verify reproducibility while maintaining anonymity.
- Minor: fix language errors ("it's" vs "its"), awkward phrasing, and change Figure 5 caption to "terms" not "words."
- Minor: inconsistent reference formatting and missing conference info; topic coherence results need comparison to LDA.
