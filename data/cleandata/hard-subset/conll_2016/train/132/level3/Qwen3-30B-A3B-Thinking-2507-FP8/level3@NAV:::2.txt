

**Review**

**Summary and Contributions**  
The paper introduces lda2vec, a model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings with sparsified topic representations. The primary contribution appears to be a hybrid approach combining neural word embeddings with Dirichlet-constrained topic mixtures, aiming for interpretable document representations. However, the claimed novelty is limited by the lack of rigorous evaluation and comparison to existing work.

**Strengths**  
1. The model design conceptually bridges neural word embeddings with probabilistic topic models, offering a potentially interpretable alternative to dense document representations.  
2. The visual examples (Figures 3-6) demonstrate reasonable topic coherence and semantic relationships in specialized corpora, suggesting the model captures meaningful patterns.  

**Weaknesses**  
1. **Critical lack of empirical evaluation**: The paper provides no direct comparison to standard topic models (LDA) or neural topic models (Cao et al. 2015; Nguyen et al. 2015; Shamanta et al. 2015), and no quantitative evaluation via document classification or similarity metrics. The topic coherence scores alone are insufficient for claiming superiority.  
2. **Anonymity violation**: The GitHub link in the paper breaches double-blind review, warranting rejection.  
3. **Methodological concerns**: The approach to compute topic-word representations via pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary appears computationally infeasible for large vocabularies. Tokenization details using SpaCy are unclear, and OOV handling is unaddressed.  
4. **Unsubstantiated claims**: The finding that 20 topics fits 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising (as it matches the number of classes), and no information-theoretic comparison to true labels or LDA is provided.  
5. **Technical flaws**: Figure 5 caption incorrectly uses "words" instead of "terms," and reference formatting is inconsistent (e.g., missing conference details for Röder et al.).  

**Questions to Authors**  
1. How do you address the computational infeasibility of pairwise topic-word comparisons for large vocabularies?  
2. Why is the 20-topic result for 20 Newsgroups considered meaningful without comparison to LDA or neural topic models?  
3. Please clarify the SpaCy tokenization process, especially regarding multiword terms and noun-chunk handling.
