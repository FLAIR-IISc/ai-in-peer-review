- Proposal: lda2vec jointly learns SGNS word embeddings and sparse, Dirichlet-regularized simplex document-topic mixtures, using pivot-word + document vectors as the prediction context.
- Contribution: Simple hybrid objective that combines SGNS with LDA-like sparse document mixtures, co-training word and topic vectors.
- Contribution: Implementation-friendly formulation for AD/GPU frameworks with publicly available code (anonymity concern noted).
- Contribution: Qualitative demonstrations on 20NG and Hacker News and limited quantitative topic coherence reporting.
- Strength: Retains SGNS linearities while enforcing interpretable, LDA-like sparse document representations.
- Strength: Conceptually simple and easy to implement/extend in automatic-differentiation frameworks.
- Strength: Learned topics appear qualitatively coherent on the shown corpora.
- Strength: Aligning document and word vectors enables direct inspection of topic–word similarity.
- Strength: Paper lists practical training/details (negative sampling, dropout, hyperparameters) aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: Major clarity issues in Section 2—key terms and design choices (shared embeddings, “separate words from a marginal distribution,” “co-adaptation,” and modular model structure) are unclear or undefined.
- Weakness: Insufficient quantitative evaluation—no comparisons to baselines (LDA, neural topic models, doc2vec), no ablations, no downstream tasks, and Cv reported only in a caption without variance/significance.
- Weakness: Overstated claims about solving word analogies based on cherry-picked examples without benchmarked comparisons.
- Weakness: Anonymity breach—footnoted code link reveals author identity, violating double-blind review.
- Weakness: Presentation and reference problems (malformed citations, typos, key numeric results relegated to figure captions).
- Question: Why tie input and output embeddings instead of using separate matrices, and was this compared experimentally?
- Question: Precisely define “co-adaptation” here and explain how dropout prevents it.
- Question: Clarify mathematically what is meant by “separate words from the marginal distribution” in relation to negative sampling and u^β.
- Question: Explicitly list model components present before adding the topic-mixture constraint and clarify the “approximation” referenced in the introduction and how AD obviates it.
- Evaluation requests: Provide quantitative topic-coherence comparisons (Cv, NPMI) vs LDA and neural baselines, multiple seeds/significance, ablations (α, simplex, shared vs separate embeddings, dropout), downstream tasks (classification/retrieval), and standard analogy benchmarks.
- Practical requests: Report training time/memory relative to separate SGNS+LDA, cost of maintaining document-topic mixtures, and sensitivity analyses for λ and α.
- Additional comments: Remove/reblind the code link, fix typos and references, move key numeric results into the main text, and consider adding topic visualizations and diversity metrics.
- Overall: Promising and practical idea but requires major clarifications and substantially stronger quantitative evaluation to meet publication standards.
