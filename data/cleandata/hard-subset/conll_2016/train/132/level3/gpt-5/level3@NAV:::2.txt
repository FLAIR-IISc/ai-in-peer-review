Summary and contributions
- Proposes lda2vec: a skip-gram/negative-sampling model that augments word2vec with a Dirichlet-simplex over document-level topic mixtures, yielding sparse, interpretable document representations while jointly learning word/topic/document embeddings in a shared space.
- Demonstrates qualitative interpretability via nearest-neighbor tokens for topics and corpus-specific analogies.
- Provides a practical recipe implementable in autodiff frameworks; conceptual simplicity is a plus, but methodological novelty is moderate relative to prior neural topic models.

Strengths
- Conceptual elegance and interpretability: The simplex-constrained document mixtures are easy to understand and align with topic-model semantics while preserving word2vec additivity.
- Qualitative evidence suggests meaningful topics and analogies on 20NG and HN, indicating the approach learns corpus-aware semantics.
- Practicality: The objective integrates cleanly with SGNS/Adam and modern frameworks; the sparsity-promoting Dirichlet prior is a straightforward mechanism to encourage interpretable mixtures.

Weaknesses
- No empirical evaluation or baselines: Lacks quantitative comparisons to LDA and neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al., 2015; Nguyen et al., 2015; Shamanta et al., 2015). Suggested: supervised document classification using topic proportions, document similarity retrieval, topic coherence vs LDA/NVDM/ProdLDA, and information-theoretic alignment to 20NG labels.
- Anonymity breach: Publishing the GitHub link compromises double-blind review and is grounds for rejection under most conference policies.
- Methodological opacity and risk of cherry-picking: Selection criteria for Figures 3–6 are unclear; without protocol (random seeds, how many runs, which topics shown), results may be cherry-picked.
- Scalability concerns: Computing topic–word associations via pairwise similarity over the full vocabulary is potentially infeasible at scale; no discussion of complexity or use of approximations (e.g., ANN/top-k pruning).
- Preprocessing/generalization gaps: SpaCy tokenization choices (token types, noun-chunk rules, multiword handling) are unspecified; OOV handling at inference, especially after embedding updates, is not discussed. The claim that 20 topics fit 20NG is unsurprising; no direct LDA comparison is provided.

Questions to authors
- Will you add quantitative evaluations (topic coherence vs LDA/NTM baselines, supervised classification, doc similarity) and comparisons to neural topic models (Cao; Nguyen; Shamanta)?
- How were topics/examples in Figures 3–6 selected? Please describe selection protocol, seeds, and number of runs.
- How do you compute topic–word lists efficiently at scale? Any ANN index or top-k screening?
- Specify tokenization: which SpaCy token types, noun-chunk rules, and treatment of multiword terms? How are OOVs handled at inference?
- How will you remedy the anonymity violation from the GitHub link?
