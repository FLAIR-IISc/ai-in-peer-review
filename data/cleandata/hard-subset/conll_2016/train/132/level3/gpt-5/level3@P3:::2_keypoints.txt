- Proposal: lda2vec augments SGNS (word2vec) with sparse, simplex-constrained document vectors via a Dirichlet prior, sharing word/topic/doc embeddings in one space.  
- Contribution: Simple neural architecture enforcing sparse document–topic mixtures while jointly learning word, topic, and document embeddings.  
- Contribution: Empirical illustrations of interpretable topics, corpus-specific analogies, and an autodiff-friendly implementation recipe.  
- Strength: Conceptual clarity and simplicity—additive (word+doc) context preserves word2vec compositionality and Dirichlet induces sparsity.  
- Strength: Interpretability—topics appear coherent and topic–term similarity is inspectable in the shared embedding space.  
- Strength: Implementation accessibility—fits standard autodiff toolchains for easy prototyping and extension.  
- Strength: Potential utility as a middle ground between dense doc embeddings and probabilistic topic models for interpretability-needing applications.  
- Weakness: Lack of quantitative baselines—no comparisons to LDA, CTM, or prior neural topic models and only Cv reported without baselines or significance testing.  
- Weakness: Missing downstream evaluations—no supervised classification, retrieval, clustering (NMI/ARI), or information-theoretic alignment to validate usefulness.  
- Weakness: Anonymity breach—public GitHub and dataset links violate double-blind review and should be removed/anonymized.  
- Weakness: Selection bias/cherry-picking—hand-picked qualitative examples lack protocol or overall distributional quality metrics.  
- Weakness: Computational scalability concerns—topic–word similarity is O(KVd) with no discussion of top-k retrieval, ANN, or batching strategies.  
- Weakness: Preprocessing/tokenization underspecified—SpaCy details, multiword terms, lemmatization, casing, and filtering are unclear.  
- Weakness: OOV and inference for new documents not addressed—no procedure for inferring proportions or handling unseen tokens.  
- Weakness: Reference and presentation issues—inconsistent references, missing venue info, grammar problems, and figure caption errors.  
- Request: Provide quantitative baselines (Cv/Umass), supervised classification, retrieval, clustering metrics, and mutual information comparisons.  
- Request: Ablations—report effects of removing/varying the Dirichlet term (α), λ, and using word+doc sum vs. doc-only.  
- Request: Describe inference for new documents—optimization procedure, initialization, stopping criteria, runtime, and OOV handling.  
- Request: Specify SpaCy tokenization—what is filtered, how multiword terms/noun-chunks are normalized, and lemmatization/casing rules.  
- Request: Explain scalability—methods for efficient top-k term retrieval (batched matmul, FAISS, inverted indices) and time/memory for V≈100k, K≥100.  
- Request: Remove/anonymize identifying links to preserve double-blind review.  
- Additional: Fix grammar/phrasing and reference formatting, report full topic coherence distribution and proportion of coherent topics, provide training/inference runtimes and hyperparameter sensitivity, and release anonymized code/data for camera-ready.  
- Overall: Idea is appealing and useful, but the paper requires substantial empirical rigor, baselines, evaluations, and clarity before publication.
