- Paper proposes lda2vec: a hybrid that jointly learns SGNS word embeddings and simplex-constrained sparse document-topic mixtures, using context = word vector + document vector.  
- Document vectors are convex combinations of learned topic vectors with sparsity encouraged by a Dirichlet-inspired penalty.  
- Experiments: reported Cv topic coherence on 20 Newsgroups and qualitative topics and analogies on a larger Hacker News corpus.  
- Contribution: simple architecture tying SGNS to LDA-style mixtures to produce a unified embedding space for words, topics, and documents.  
- Contribution: practical training recipe using autodiff, negative sampling, dropout, and a Dirichlet-like sparsity penalty.  
- Strength: clear and useful idea—adds global document context to local lexical context via an additive context vector.  
- Strength: interpretability—topics, words, and documents share a space enabling easy nearest-neighbor topic inspection and percentage-style document attributions.  
- Strength: empirical signals show plausible topic coherence and sensible qualitative topics on both corpora.  
- Strength: implementation feasible in standard autodiff/SGD frameworks without bespoke variational inference.  
- Weakness: Section 2 is unclear and poorly written; many terms are imprecise or undefined and exposition needs rewriting.  
- Weakness: modeling choices lack justification—shared input/output embeddings, Dirichlet penalty form and hyperparameters (α=1/n, λ=200), and dropout are not motivated or ablated.  
- Weakness: evaluation is weak—no baselines (LDA, doc2vec, separate embeddings), no ablations, and analogy claims are unsupported by standard benchmarks.  
- Weakness: missing reproducibility details (batch sizes, learning rates, window/subsampling, negative sampling distribution, rare-word handling) and mixing pretrained/random init confounds.  
- Weakness: inference for unseen documents (how to obtain topic proportions) is not described.  
- Weakness: presentation and policy errors (typo, non-anonymous code link in footnote, reference formatting/citation inconsistencies).  
- Requests: rewrite Section 2 to state learnable parameters, objective, and interaction of simplex constraint with SGNS.  
- Requests: justify and/or ablate using a single word vector set vs separate input/output embeddings and report effects on performance.  
- Requests: clarify terms like “separate words from the marginal distribution” and “co-adaptation” with formal or intuitive definitions.  
- Requests: justify Dirichlet regularizer choices, provide sensitivity analyses (coherence and sparsity vs α, λ), and report actual sparsity levels.  
- Requests: provide quantitative comparisons (coherence vs LDA under same preprocessing, embedding quality vs SGNS/doc2vec) and ablations (no-doc vector, dense docs, no dropout).  
- Requests: describe inference for unseen documents (optimization vs encoder) and compute coherence for Hacker News with a compatible external corpus or internal PMI.  
- Overall: idea is appealing and practically motivated but needs much clearer exposition and a stronger, controlled empirical evaluation with baselines and ablations.
