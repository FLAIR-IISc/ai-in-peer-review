- Proposal: lda2vec combines SGNS word embeddings with LDA-style simplex document-topic mixtures; document vectors are convex combinations of topic vectors and context = pivot word + document vector with a Dirichlet sparsity prior.
- Contribution 1: Simple, interpretable formulation marrying SGNS and LDA-like document mixtures with shared word/topic/document space.
- Contribution 2: Practical end-to-end training recipe using autodiff, negative sampling, and GPU-friendly minibatching, plus qualitative case studies.
- Contribution 3: Design choice of additive composition (document + pivot) enabling direct topic-word similarity and domain-specific analogies.
- Strength: Conceptual clarity — simplex-constrained proportions and shared embedding space give familiar, LDA-like interpretability.
- Strength: Practicality and simplicity — small modification to SGNS, easy implementation with standard optimizers and components.
- Strength: Qualitative plausibility — example topics on 20NG and HN look sensible and capture domain-specific semantics.
- Strength: Sensible modeling choices — additive context, sparse Dirichlet prior, and shared space support inspection and interpretability.
- Weakness: Lack of quantitative baselines — no systematic comparisons to LDA/NMF, doc2vec, neural topic models, or extensive extrinsic tasks.
- Weakness: Missing extrinsic evaluations — need document classification, clustering (NMI/AMI/ARI), retrieval, and ablation studies.
- Weakness: Possible cherry-picking — hand-picked topics/analogies without selection protocol; need full lists, random samples, and aggregate metrics.
- Weakness: Anonymity violation — GitHub and dataset links reveal authorship and violate double-blind review.
- Weakness: Missing related work — needs positioning and comparison to prior neural/topic hybrid and neural topic models.
- Weakness: Unclear inference for new documents — no described test-time procedure for inferring document-topic proportions.
- Weakness: OOV handling unspecified — no description of handling unseen tokens, subword methods, or UNK strategy.
- Weakness: Sensitivity/stability not reported — no analysis of hyperparameters (α, λ), initialization stability, or negative sampling choices.
- Question: Can the authors report Cv and topic diversity vs LDA/NMF and performance vs doc2vec and 2015 neural topic models?
- Question: What is the test-time inference procedure for p on unseen documents, its runtime, and stability?
- Question: How are OOVs handled at test time — UNK, subword, or retrofitting?
- Question: What was the selection protocol for the shown topics/analogies; can random samples and aggregate metrics be provided?
- Question: How are top tokens per topic and coherence computed efficiently for large vocabularies; what are training/inference runtimes vs baselines?
- Question: Please report hyperparameter sensitivities and ablation results (no Dirichlet, dense docs, no additive context, tying embeddings).
- Reproducibility: Need LDA baselines with identical preprocessing and alignment metrics to ground-truth labels across topic counts.
- Reproducibility: Tokenization and preprocessing details must be fully specified (spaCy settings, lemmatization, stopwords, phrase handling).
- Minor fixes: Correct writing issues, clarify ambiguous sentences, and fix Figure 5 caption terminology.
- References: Ensure consistent formatting, complete venue info, and correct disambiguated citations.
- Ethics/policy: Remove de-anonymizing links and provide an anonymized code archive or defer release until after acceptance.
- Overall evaluation: Promising, practical idea with good qualitative results, but current submission lacks quantitative rigor and violates anonymity; lean toward rejection unless addressed.
