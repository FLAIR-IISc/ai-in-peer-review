- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, blending SGNS word embeddings with Dirichlet-constrained document–topic mixtures, adding document mixture to pivot word vectors and training with SGNS and a Dirichlet prior; implemented in Chainer and evaluated on 20NG and Hacker News.

- Contribution 1: Simple objective fusing SGNS with sparse, interpretable document–topic mixtures embedding words, topics, and documents in a shared space.

- Contribution 2: Qualitative evidence of interpretable topics via nearest-neighbor words and limited quantitative Cv scores on 20NG.

- Contribution 3: An autodiff-based implementation that eases prototyping but breaks anonymity.

- Strength: Conceptually appealing hybrid that blends local (word) and global (document) semantics via additive context.

- Strength: Topic vectors live in the same space as words, enabling intuitive inspection and interpretability.

- Strength: Includes some quantitative topic coherence (Cv) and examines sensitivity to negative sampling exponent.

- Strength: Training is scalable and simple to implement with negative sampling and autodiff.

- Weakness: Technical exposition is opaque; Section 2 needs rewriting and clearer high-level explanation and motivations.

- Weakness: Uses the same word vectors for pivot and target contrary to SGNS practice without motivation, theory, or ablation.

- Weakness: Ambiguous statement about “separating observed words from the marginal distribution” needs formalization in SGNS terms.

- Weakness: “Co-adaptation” used to justify dropout is unexplained and dropout rates are not reported.

- Weakness: Ambiguous phrasing like “If we only included structure up to this point” should be clarified (e.g., model without topic-mixture constraint).

- Weakness: Insufficient empirical evaluation — no comparisons to strong baselines (LDA, paragraph vectors, SGNS+post-hoc topics, neural doc-topic models).

- Weakness: Figure 2 reports only average Cv with no baselines, variance, significance, downstream tasks, analogy metrics, or ablations.

- Weakness: Unclear claims in Introduction about avoiding “deriving a new approximation” via autodiff; connection to variational/sampling inference is not justified.

- Weakness: Key hyperparameters (α = 1/n, λ = 200) lack principled motivation or sensitivity analyses; minibatch scaling of Dirichlet term is ad hoc and unevaluated.

- Weakness: Overstates results by claiming the model “solves word analogies” based on cherry-picked examples without quantitative benchmarks.

- Weakness: Anonymity broken by public repo link; presentation issues like typos and reference formatting errors.

- Question: Why use a single shared embedding matrix for pivot and target roles — provide theoretical/empirical justification and an ablation.

- Question: What precisely is meant by “separating observed words from the marginal distribution” — restate in SGNS log σ(c·w) / log σ(−c·w−) terms.

- Question: What are the dropout rates for document and word vectors, how sensitive are results, and what co-adaptation is observed?

- Question: Clarify whether “structure up to this point” refers to the model without the topic-mixture constraint and Dirichlet term.

- Question: What “approximation” in traditional topic models does this method avoid — are you replacing variational/sampling inference with point-estimate optimization?

- Question: How were α and λ selected — provide sensitivity analyses; how is Ld scaled in minibatches and does this bias the objective?

- Question: Provide quantitative baselines: LDA coherence on the same preprocessing, doc2vec or SGNS+doc vectors, classification/clustering accuracy, and standard word analogy accuracy.

- Additional: Anonymize code/data links for review, fix typo “it’s similarity” → “its similarity,” and correct reference formatting.

- Additional: Clarify full training setup (dimensions, window, negatives, learning rate, early stopping, initialization, dropout probabilities).

- Additional: Improve Figure 2 by moving caption content into figure/main text, adding error bars over multiple runs, and comparing to LDA baselines.
