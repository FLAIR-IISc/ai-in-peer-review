- Paper proposes lda2vec: combines SGNS/word2vec with Dirichlet-regularized sparse document mixtures over learned topic vectors in a shared embedding space.
- Main claimed contributions: simple SGNS+Dirichlet architecture, qualitative case studies (20 Newsgroups, Hacker News), and an autodiff-friendly implementation with code.
- Strength: Conceptual simplicity and practicality—easy to implement and extend in modern autodiff frameworks.
- Strength: Interpretability via sparse simplex-constrained document-topic proportions and intuitive topic inspection by nearest tokens.
- Strength: Joint semantic space enables topic inspection and word-analogy probing, with plausible Hacker News examples.
- Strength: Potential for reproducibility and adoption if code is properly released/anonymized.
- Weakness: Lacks rigorous empirical evaluation and comparisons to LDA and contemporary neural topic models, and no extrinsic downstream tasks.
- Weakness: Only a single coherence metric reported and no clustering/classification metrics or statistical significance tests.
- Weakness: Anonymity violation due to GitHub/data links—this alone could justify rejection unless fixed.
- Weakness: Evidence appears cherry-picked; no per-topic coherences, distributions, or full topic listings provided.
- Weakness: Missing computational and scalability details (runtime, memory, method for top-k token retrieval over large vocabularies).
- Weakness: Preprocessing and inference details underspecified (tokenization, phrase handling, OOV handling, inference for unseen documents).
- Questions: Add quantitative baselines (LDA, neural topic models), coherence metrics (Cv, UMass, UCI), clustering (NMI/ARI), and document classification results.
- Questions: Provide sensitivity analyses/ablations for λ, α, topic count, negative sampling, and Dirichlet term effects.
- Questions: Clarify inference for unseen documents/OOV handling and whether p is optimized or an encoder is used.
- Questions: Report training times, hardware, memory, and efficient methods for computing top-k words per topic (use of ANN?).
- Additional comments: Improve writing and grammar, standardize and complete references, and anonymize code link for review.
- Reporting suggestion: Provide full top-10 words for all topics, per-topic coherence distributions, and consider human intrusion tests for interpretability claims.
- Overall: Idea is neat and promising, but paper needs stronger quantitative baselines, clearer experimental details, and anonymization before being compelling.
