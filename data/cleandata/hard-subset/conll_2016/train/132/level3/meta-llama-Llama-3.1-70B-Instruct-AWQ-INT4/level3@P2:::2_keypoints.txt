- Proposes lda2vec, a neural topic model that extends word2vec to learn sparse, interpretable document embeddings.
- Claims the model captures semantic regularities while providing interpretable topics; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.
- Contribution: novel neural-styled topic model that sparsifies document embeddings to mimic topic models.
- Contribution: combines word embeddings and topic modeling to produce interpretable document representations.
- Contribution: demonstrates topic coherence and learned linear word relationships on two datasets.
- Strength: simple to implement and compatible with automatic differentiation frameworks.
- Strength: clear, concise explanation of the model and its components.
- Strength: thorough related-work discussion and acknowledgment of model limitations.
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparison to standard and neural topic models, hindering performance assessment.
- Weakness: unclear selection criteria for examples in Figures 3–6, suggesting possible cherry-picking.
- Weakness: no discussion of handling out-of-vocabulary terms for new documents after embedding updates.
- Weakness: omission of comparisons to prior neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al., Nguyen et al., Shamanta et al.).
- Weakness: minor issues in grammar, phrasing, and inconsistent reference formatting.
- Question: please explain how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected and whether they represent overall performance.
- Question: how will OOV terms be handled when applying the model to novel documents?
- Question: provide comparisons against prior neural topic models (Cao et al., Nguyen et al., Shamanta et al.).
- Question: how will you address computational infeasibility of topic-word representations computed via pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary?
- Question: provide details on SpaCy tokenization choices, noun-chunk extraction, and handling of multiword terms.
- Additional comment: paper has potential but needs more rigorous evaluation and comprehensive comparisons to existing models.
