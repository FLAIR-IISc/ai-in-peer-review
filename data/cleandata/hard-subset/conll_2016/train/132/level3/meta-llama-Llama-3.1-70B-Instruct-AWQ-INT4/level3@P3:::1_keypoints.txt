- Proposes lda2vec, a novel model that jointly learns dense word vectors and Dirichlet-distributed document-topic mixtures.  
- Uses a Dirichlet likelihood term to encourage sparse, interpretable document representations.  
- Demonstrates the model on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News, showing coherent topics and some linear word relationships.  
- Technical section (Section 2) is currently incomprehensible and needs a full rewrite to provide a clear high-level picture.  
- Introduction’s third paragraph is unclear; must explain the derived approximation, why prototype development is time-consuming, and why feature evaluation is easier.  
- Motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target is not justified and needs explanation.  
- Phrases like “separate words from a marginal distribution,” “co-adaptation,” and “If we only included structure up to this point” are ambiguous and must be clarified.  
- Typo: “it's similarity” should be “its similarity.”  
- Footnote 1 breaks anonymity and should be removed.  
- Paper lacks quantitative evaluation; relying on example clusters is insufficient and Figure 2’s quantitative claims need expansion.  
- Conclusion overstates results (claims solving word analogies) based on cherry-picked examples and should be toned down.  
- Reference formatting error: Chang citation missing venue name.  
- Strength: Combining word2vec and LDA is a novel, promising idea that leverages strengths of both methods.  
- Strength: Dirichlet term for sparsity is a good approach for interpretable document representations.  
- Strength: Empirical application shows the model can find coherent topics and some semantic relationships.
