- Paper proposes lda2vec: a neural-styled topic model that extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparse document-to-topic proportions.
- Contribution: Hybrid topic model combining traditional topic models with neural word embeddings.
- Contribution: Joint training of word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space.
- Contribution: Produces sparse, interpretable document mixtures via a non-negative simplex constraint.
- Strength: Generates effective document representations capturing local and global semantic relationships.
- Strength: Produces interpretable topics and word distributions.
- Strength: Easily integrated into automatic differentiation frameworks (practical to implement).
- Weakness: Lacks thorough empirical evaluation and direct comparisons to standard and neural topic models.
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics for topic and document quality are not clearly specified.
- Weakness: Examples shown may be cherry-picked, risking overly optimistic presentation of results.
- Question: How were the examples in Figures 3–6 selected and was there a selection criterion?
- Question: Provide detailed SpaCy tokenization procedure—which token types/noun-chunks and handling of multiword terms.
- Question: How will out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms be handled when applying the model to new documents after embedding updates?
- Question: Request a more comprehensive evaluation of lda2vec including direct comparisons to baselines.
- Additional: Paper needs a more thorough related-work discussion, especially on prior neural topic models.
- Additional: Reference formatting is inconsistent and some entries lack conference information.
- Additional: Several minor grammar and phrasing errors throughout the paper.
- Additional: Figure 5 caption should be corrected from "words" to "terms".
