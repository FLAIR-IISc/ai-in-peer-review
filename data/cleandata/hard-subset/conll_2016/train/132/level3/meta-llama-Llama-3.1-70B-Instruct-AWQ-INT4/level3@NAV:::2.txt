**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural-styled topic model called lda2vec, which combines word2vec with Dirichlet-distributed latent document-level mixtures of topic vectors. The model learns dense word vectors jointly with document embeddings and sparsifies them to mimic topic models. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets: Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes a novel neural topic model that combines the strengths of word2vec and traditional topic models.
2. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets, showing that it can discover coherent topics and learn linear relationships between words.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a simple and effective way to extend word2vec to learn document embeddings and topic models.
2. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the model and its components.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks empirical evaluation or direct comparison to standard/neural topic models.
2. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the examples in Figures 3-6 were selected, which may be a result of cherry-picking.
3. The computational feasibility of computing topic-word representations by pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary is unclear.
4. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how to handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms when applying the trained model to novel documents after embedding updates.
5. The reference formatting is inconsistent, and some entries are missing conference information.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to evaluate the effectiveness of lda2vec in comparison to standard/neural topic models?
2. Can you provide more details on how the examples in Figures 3-6 were selected?
3. How do you plan to handle OOV terms when applying the trained model to novel documents after embedding updates?
4. Can you provide more details on the computational feasibility of computing topic-word representations by pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper could benefit from a more thorough evaluation of lda2vec in comparison to standard/neural topic models.
* The authors should consider providing more details on the experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning.
* The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential applications of lda2vec.
