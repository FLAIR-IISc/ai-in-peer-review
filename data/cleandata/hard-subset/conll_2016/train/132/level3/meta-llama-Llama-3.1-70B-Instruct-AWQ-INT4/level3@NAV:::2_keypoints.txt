1. Proposes lda2vec, combining word2vec with Dirichlet-distributed document-level topic mixtures to learn sparse topic-like document embeddings.
2. Evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.
3. Contribution: novel neural topic model that blends word2vec and traditional topic models.
4. Contribution: demonstrates lda2vec can discover coherent topics and linear word relationships.
5. Strength: Simple, effective extension of word2vec to learn document embeddings and topics.
6. Strength: Clear and concise explanation of model and components.
7. Strength: Experimental results support lda2vec’s effectiveness on two datasets.
8. Weakness: Lacks empirical evaluation or direct comparisons to standard/neural topic models.
9. Weakness: Unclear how examples in Figures 3–6 were selected; potential cherry-picking.
10. Weakness: Unclear computational feasibility of computing topic-word representations via pairwise comparisons with full vocabulary.
11. Weakness: No clear strategy for handling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms when applying the model to new documents.
12. Weakness: Reference formatting inconsistent and some entries missing conference information.
13. Question: How will you evaluate lda2vec against standard/neural topic models?
14. Question: Provide details on selection criteria for examples in Figures 3–6.
15. Question: How will OOV terms be handled when applying the trained model to novel documents after embedding updates?
16. Question: Clarify computational feasibility of computing topic-word representations by pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary.
17. Additional: Paper needs a more thorough comparative evaluation to standard/neural topic models.
18. Additional: Authors should provide more details on experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning.
19. Additional: Paper would benefit from a fuller discussion of limitations and potential applications.
