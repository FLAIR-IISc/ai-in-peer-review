- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, combining word2vec and LDA by extending SGNS with document-wide feature vectors to learn dense word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document representations; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News.  
- Contribution: Hybrid model that merges word2vec and LDA to obtain dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document representations.  
- Contribution: Extension of the SGNS objective to incorporate document-wide feature vectors and jointly learn continuous document-topic weights.  
- Contribution: Produces sparse, interpretable document representations via a non-negative simplex constraint.  
- Strength: Novel combination of two popular models (word2vec and LDA) with potential for new NLP insights.  
- Strength: Empirically effective at discovering coherent topics on two datasets.  
- Strength: Can capture linear relationships between words, improving over traditional topic models.  
- Weakness: Technical presentation (Section 2) is unclear and needs a rewrite for readers familiar with word2vec and LDA.  
- Weakness: No clear motivation given for using the same word vectors for pivot and target, deviating from standard word2vec practice.  
- Weakness: Lacks thorough quantitative evaluation, relying mainly on example clusters and limited captions.  
- Weakness: Conclusion overstates results by claiming solved word analogies based on a few cherry-picked examples.  
- Question: Please justify using the same word vectors for pivot and target in lda2vec.  
- Question: How will you address and clarify the unclear technical details in Section 2?  
- Question: Can you provide a more thorough quantitative evaluation (e.g., perplexity, topic coherence, word analogy accuracy)?  
- Question: How do you plan to extend lda2vec to other tasks like document classification and clustering?
