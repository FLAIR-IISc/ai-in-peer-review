- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, a model that combines word2vec-style embeddings with Dirichlet-like sparsity to jointly learn word, topic, and document vectors, demonstrated on 20 Newsgroups and Hacker News.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel model combining word embeddings and topic models to produce coherent topics and interpretable document representations.

- Contribution 2: Emphasizes the model's simplicity and ease of implementation in automatic differentiation frameworks.

- Contribution 3: Shows applicability on two diverse datasets, suggesting domain adaptability.

- Strength 1: The model is well-motivated with clear design intuitions.

- Strength 2: Using a common representation space for words, topics, and documents is an important innovation for interpretability.

- Strength 3: Provides empirical demonstrations that lda2vec discovers salient topics and word linearities.

- Weakness 1: No empirical comparison to standard or neural topic models, making relative performance unclear.

- Weakness 2: Example selection procedure for Figures 3–6 is unspecified, raising cherry-picking concerns.

- Weakness 3: Computational cost of computing topic-word representations via pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary is unclear and unanalyzed.

- Weakness 4: Tokenization details with spaCy are vague—unspecified token types, noun-chunk extraction, and multiword handling.

- Weakness 5: No discussion of handling out-of-vocabulary terms when applying the trained model to new documents.

- Weakness 6: Claim that 20 topics fits 20 Newsgroups is unsurprising; authors should compare to true labels and LDA.

- Weakness 7: Fails to compare to prior neural topic models (Cao et al., 2015; Nguyen et al., 2015; Shamanta et al., 2015).

- Weakness 8: Minor grammar/phasing issues and inconsistent reference formatting.

- Question 1: How will the authors add empirical evaluations and direct comparisons to standard/neural topic models?

- Question 2: How were examples in Figures 3–6 selected, and how will cherry-picking concerns be addressed?

- Question 3: How will the computational feasibility of pairwise topic-word comparisons be improved or analyzed?

- Question 4: Can the authors provide full spaCy tokenization details, including token types, noun-chunk extraction, and multiword term handling?

- Question 5: How will OOV terms be handled when applying the model to novel documents after embedding updates?

- Question 6: Can the authors provide an information-theoretic comparison to true labels and a direct LDA baseline?

- Question 7: How will the authors compare lda2vec to prior neural topic models (Cao et al., Nguyen et al., Shamanta et al.)?

- Additional comment 1: The provided GitHub link breaks anonymity and may warrant rejection.

- Additional comment 2: Authors should add preprocessing details (stop-word removal, stemming/lemmatization, special-character handling).

- Additional comment 3: Provide analysis of robustness to hyperparameter settings and their impact on performance.
