This paper proposes a novel approach to combining word2vec and LDA, two popular methods for learning representations of words and documents, respectively. The proposed model, lda2vec, learns dense word vectors jointly with Dirichlet-distributed latent document-level mixtures of topic vectors. This approach has the potential to leverage the strengths of both methods, capturing semantic regularities between words and forming interpretable representations over documents.

The paper's main contributions are:

1. The development of a novel model that combines word2vec and LDA, allowing for the joint learning of word and document representations.
2. The use of a Dirichlet likelihood term to induce sparse document representations, making it easier to interpret the results.
3. The application of the model to two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments, demonstrating its ability to discover coherent topics and learn linear relationships between words.

However, there are several weaknesses in the paper that need to be addressed:

1. The technical details in Section 2 are incomprehensible and need a complete rewrite to provide a clear high-level picture for readers familiar with word2vec and LDA.
2. The introduction's third paragraph is unclear and needs to be rewritten to explain the approximation being derived, why prototype development is time-consuming, and why feature evaluation is easier.
3. The motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target is unclear and needs to be justified.
4. The meaning of "separate words from a marginal distribution" is unclear and needs to be explained.
5. The concept of "co-adaptation" is not clearly defined and needs to be explained in the context of the model.
6. The phrase "If we only included structure up to this point" is ambiguous and needs to be clarified.
7. There is a typo in the text: "it's similarity" should be "its similarity".
8. Footnote 1 breaks anonymity and should be removed.
9. The paper lacks quantitative evaluation and relies on example clusters, which are insufficient. Figure 2's quantitative content is only in the caption and needs to be expanded.
10. The conclusion overstates the results by claiming the model solves word analogies based on a few cherry-picked examples.
11. There is a reference formatting error: the Chang citation is missing the venue name.

Strengths:

1. The combination of word2vec and LDA is a novel and interesting approach that has the potential to leverage the strengths of both methods.
2. The use of a Dirichlet likelihood term to induce sparse document representations is a good idea, making it easier to interpret the results.
3. The application of the model to two datasets demonstrates its ability to discover coherent topics and learn linear relationships between words.

Weaknesses:

1. The technical details are unclear and need to be rewritten.
2. The introduction's third paragraph is unclear and needs to be rewritten.
3. The motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target is unclear and needs to be justified.
4. The paper lacks quantitative evaluation and relies on example clusters, which are insufficient.
5. The conclusion overstates the results and needs to be rewritten.

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide a clear and concise explanation of the model's technical details?
2. Can you justify the use of the same word vectors for pivot and target?
3. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the concept of "co-adaptation" in the context of the model?
4. Can you provide more quantitative evaluation of the model's performance on the two datasets?
5. Can you revise the conclusion to provide a more accurate representation of the model's capabilities?
