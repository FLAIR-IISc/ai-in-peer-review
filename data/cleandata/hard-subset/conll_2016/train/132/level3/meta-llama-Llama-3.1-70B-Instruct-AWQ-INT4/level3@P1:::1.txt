**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel model, lda2vec, that combines the strengths of word2vec and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) to learn dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document representations. The model extends the Skipgram Negative-Sampling (SGNS) objective to incorporate document-wide feature vectors and simultaneously learn continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments, and show that it can discover coherent topics and learn linear relationships between words.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Hybrid model**: The paper proposes a novel hybrid model that combines the strengths of word2vec and LDA to learn dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document representations.
2. **Extension of SGNS objective**: The authors extend the SGNS objective to incorporate document-wide feature vectors and simultaneously learn continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors.
3. **Sparse document representations**: The model produces sparse, interpretable document representations through a non-negative simplex constraint, which is a significant improvement over traditional dense document representations.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel combination of word2vec and LDA**: The paper proposes a novel combination of two popular models, which has the potential to lead to new insights and improvements in natural language processing.
2. **Effective in discovering coherent topics**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec in discovering coherent topics in two datasets, Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.
3. **Ability to learn linear relationships between words**: The model can learn linear relationships between words, which is a significant improvement over traditional topic models.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Technical details are unclear**: Section 2 of the paper is unclear and needs a complete rewrite to provide a clear high-level picture for readers familiar with word2vec and LDA.
2. **Motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target is unclear**: The authors do not provide a clear motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target, which is contrary to the traditional word2vec approach.
3. **Lack of quantitative evaluation**: The paper does not provide a thorough quantitative evaluation of the model's performance, relying on example clusters and a limited quantitative content in Figure 2's caption.
4. **Conclusion overstates results**: The conclusion overstates the results by claiming that the model solves word analogies based on a few cherry-picked examples.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target in the lda2vec model?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of unclear technical details in Section 2 of the paper?
3. Can you provide a more thorough quantitative evaluation of the model's performance, including metrics such as perplexity, topic coherence, and word analogy accuracy?
4. How do you plan to extend the lda2vec model to other tasks, such as document classification and clustering?
