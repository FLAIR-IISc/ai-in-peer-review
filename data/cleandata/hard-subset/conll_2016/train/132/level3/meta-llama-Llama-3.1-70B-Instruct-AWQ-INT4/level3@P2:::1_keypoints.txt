- Summary: Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid of word2vec and LDA that jointly learns word, topic, and document vectors; evaluated on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a hybrid model combining word2vec and LDA for joint word, topic, and document embeddings.
- Contribution 2: Enforces sparse, interpretable document representations via a Dirichlet-likelihood term.
- Contribution 3: Claims the model captures linear token relationships usable for word analogies.
- Strength 1: Novel and interesting combination of two popular models with potential new applications.
- Strength 2: Effective use of automatic differentiation frameworks for model development and training.
- Strength 3: Good qualitative results shown through example topics and word analogies.
- Weakness 1: Technical exposition (Section 2) is unclear and needs a full rewrite for clarity.
- Weakness 2: No clear motivation for using the same word vectors for pivot and target, contrary to word2vec practice.
- Weakness 3: Insufficient quantitative evaluation; relies on example clusters and cherry-picked analogies.
- Weakness 4: Conclusion overstates results by claiming analogy-solving ability based on limited examples.
- Question 1: Please justify using identical word vectors for pivot and target.
- Question 2: Provide more details on the Dirichlet-likelihood term and how it promotes sparsity.
- Question 3: Include thorough quantitative evaluations (e.g., perplexity, topic coherence).
- Question 4: Provide more systematic examples and evaluations of word analogy solving.
