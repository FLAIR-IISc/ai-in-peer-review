**Paper Review: Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a neural-styled topic model, lda2vec, that extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparsifies them to mimic topic models. The model jointly trains word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space, yielding coherent topics and interpretable document-to-topic proportions. The authors evaluate lda2vec on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset and a large corpus of Hacker News comments, demonstrating its ability to discover salient topics and learn linear relationships between words.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel model that combines the strengths of word embeddings and topic models, allowing for the discovery of coherent topics and interpretable document representations.
2. The model is simple to implement in automatic differentiation frameworks, making it accessible to a wide range of researchers and practitioners.
3. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two diverse datasets, showcasing its ability to adapt to different domains and tasks.

**Strengths**

1. The model is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the intuitions behind its design.
2. The use of a common representation space for word, topic, and document vectors is a key innovation, allowing for the discovery of coherent topics and interpretable document representations.
3. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of lda2vec on two datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in discovering salient topics and learning linear relationships between words.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks any empirical evaluation or direct comparison to standard/neural topic models, making it difficult to assess the relative performance of lda2vec.
2. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the examples in Figures 3-6 were selected, raising concerns about cherry-picking.
3. The computational feasibility of computing topic-word representations by pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary is unclear, and the authors do not provide any analysis of the computational complexity of their model.
4. The tokenization details using SpaCy are unclear, and the authors do not specify which token types or noun-chunks are extracted and how multiword terms are handled.
5. The authors do not discuss how to handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms when applying the trained model to novel documents after embedding updates.
6. The finding that 20 topics fits the 20 Newsgroups dataset is unsurprising, and the authors should consider an information-theoretic comparison to true labels and a direct LDA comparison.
7. The authors do not compare lda2vec to prior neural topic models (Cao et al., 2015; Nguyen et al., 2015; Shamanta et al., 2015), which is a significant omission.
8. There are minor issues with grammar and phrasing throughout the paper, and the reference formatting is inconsistent.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the lack of empirical evaluation and direct comparison to standard/neural topic models?
2. Can you provide more details on how the examples in Figures 3-6 were selected, and how you plan to address concerns about cherry-picking?
3. How do you plan to improve the computational feasibility of computing topic-word representations by pairwise comparisons with the full vocabulary?
4. Can you provide more details on the tokenization details using SpaCy, including which token types or noun-chunks are extracted and how multiword terms are handled?
5. How do you plan to handle OOV terms when applying the trained model to novel documents after embedding updates?
6. Can you provide an information-theoretic comparison to true labels and a direct LDA comparison to evaluate the performance of lda2vec?
7. How do you plan to compare lda2vec to prior neural topic models (Cao et al., 2015; Nguyen et al., 2015; Shamanta et al., 2015)?

**Additional Comments**

1. The GitHub link provided in the paper breaks anonymity and may warrant rejection.
2. The authors should consider adding more details on the preprocessing steps, including the removal of stop words, stemming or lemmatization, and the handling of special characters.
3. The authors should consider providing more analysis on the robustness of lda2vec to hyperparameter tuning and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the model.
