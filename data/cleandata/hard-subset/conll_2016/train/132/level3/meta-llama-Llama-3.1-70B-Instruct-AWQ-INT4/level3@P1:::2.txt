**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel neural-styled topic model, lda2vec, which extends word2vec to learn document embeddings and sparsifies them to mimic traditional topic models. The model jointly trains word, topic, and document vectors in a common representation space, preserving semantic regularities between learned word vectors while yielding sparse and interpretable document-to-topic proportions. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets: the Twenty Newsgroups corpus and the Hacker News comments corpus.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Hybrid Topic Model**: lda2vec combines the strengths of traditional topic models and neural network-based word embeddings, providing a novel approach to document representation.
2. **Joint Training**: The model jointly trains word, topic, and document vectors, allowing for a more comprehensive understanding of the relationships between these entities.
3. **Sparse Document Representations**: lda2vec produces sparse, interpretable document mixtures through a non-negative simplex constraint, making it easier to understand the underlying structure of the data.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Document Representations**: lda2vec generates high-quality document representations that capture both local and global semantic relationships.
2. **Interpretable Results**: The model provides interpretable results, allowing for easy understanding of the topics and their corresponding word distributions.
3. **Flexibility**: lda2vec can be easily integrated into existing automatic differentiation frameworks, making it a practical choice for many applications.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Empirical Evaluation**: The paper lacks a thorough empirical evaluation, including direct comparisons to standard and neural topic models.
2. **Unclear Evaluation Metrics**: The authors do not provide clear evaluation metrics for the quality of the learned topics and document representations.
3. **Potential Cherry-Picking**: The examples in Figures 3-6 may have been cherry-picked, which could lead to an overly optimistic assessment of the model's performance.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How did you select the examples in Figures 3-6? Was there a specific criteria used to choose these examples?
2. Can you provide more details on the tokenization process using SpaCy? Which token types or noun-chunks were extracted, and how were multiword terms handled?
3. How do you plan to handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) terms when applying the trained model to novel documents after embedding updates?
4. Can you provide a more comprehensive evaluation of lda2vec, including direct comparisons to standard and neural topic models?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the related work, including prior neural topic models.
* The reference formatting is inconsistent, and some entries are missing conference information.
* Minor grammar and phrasing errors were found throughout the paper.
* The Figure 5 caption should be corrected to say "terms" instead of "words".
