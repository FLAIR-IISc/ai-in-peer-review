- Proposes lda2vec, a hybrid model combining word2vec and LDA to learn dense word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document-topic mixtures.
- Claims the model embeds word, topic, and document vectors in a shared space enabling interpretable document-topic proportions while preserving word semantics.
- Contribution: novel integration of skip-gram word2vec architecture with LDA-inspired sparse topic mixtures.
- Contribution: demonstrates interpretable document representations and coherent topics on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News datasets.
- Contribution: shows ability to capture semantic relationships and solve word analogies within specialized corpora.
- Strength: novel approach that bridges dense embeddings and interpretable topic models with potential practical applications.
- Strength: introduces sparse document-topic proportions to add interpretability often missing in neural methods.
- Strength: compatible with automatic differentiation and GPU acceleration, supporting scalability.
- Strength: learns domain-specific vocabulary and semantics, as shown on the Hacker News dataset.
- Weakness: technical description (Section 2) is unclear and needs a complete rewrite to explain model architecture and component interplay.
- Weakness: several terms (e.g., “co-adaptation,” “separating words from a marginal distribution,” “structure”) are vague or undefined.
- Weakness: insufficient motivation for sharing the same word vectors for pivot and target words and unclear impact of that choice.
- Weakness: evaluation is weak by modern standards—example clusters and coherence scores are insufficient and Figure 2 is poorly described.
- Weakness: claims about solving word analogies are overstated and appear cherry-picked without statistical rigor.
- Weakness: footnote violates review anonymity, a serious procedural issue.
- Question: request clearer explanation of integration between word2vec and LDA, including the loss function and Dirichlet prior role.
- Question: ask for motivation and performance impact of using identical pivot and target word vectors versus separate embeddings.
- Question: request elaboration on “co-adaptation” and how dropout mitigates it.
- Question: ask for quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art topic models on standard benchmarks.
- Additional: correct typographical error “it's similarity” → “its similarity.”
- Additional: fix incomplete/ambiguous Chang reference to clarify venue and professionalism.
- Recommendation: promising idea but major rewrite and stronger evaluation required; currently recommend rejection.
