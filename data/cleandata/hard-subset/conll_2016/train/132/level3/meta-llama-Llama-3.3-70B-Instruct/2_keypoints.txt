- Proposes a neural-styled topic model extending word2vec to learn document embeddings but lacks adequate empirical evaluation.
- Model effectiveness is undetermined without comparison to standard or neural topic models via supervised categorization or similarity-annotated datasets.
- Paper should compare to existing neural topic models (e.g., Cao et al., Nguyen et al., Shamanta et al.).
- Unclear tokenization: selection of "tokens" via SpaCy and handling of multiword terms is not explained.
- Out-of-vocabulary (OOV) handling is unspecified, and updating word2vec embeddings may make OOVs non-comparable.
- Example selection in figures appears cherry-picked and the selection process is unclear.
- Proposes a transition-based broad-coverage semantic parser (BSP) supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units.
- Experiments with UCCA parsing, presented as a novel contribution.
- BSP parser achieves comparable primary-edge F-scores to parsers like MaltParser and UPARSE and can predict some remote edges.
- Code release is a strength but publishing the GitHub link compromises review anonymity.
- Lacks comparisons to other neural topic models and baseline topic modeling evaluations.
- Several low-level issues: grammatical errors, inconsistent reference formatting, and unclear terminology.
- Recommendations: add empirical evaluation, clarify example selection, tokenization, multiword/OOV handling, and compare to relevant neural models.
- Key questions for authors: how will you address empirical evaluation, comparisons to neural topic models, OOV/multiword handling, and paper clarity/consistency?
