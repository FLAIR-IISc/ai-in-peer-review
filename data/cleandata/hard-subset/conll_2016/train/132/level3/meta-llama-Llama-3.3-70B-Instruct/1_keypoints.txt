- Paper proposes BSP, a novel transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminal nodes, and discontinuous units for broad-coverage semantic structures (BSS).
- Authors also explore a conversion-based approach applying existing parsers to converted BSS representations.
- First experiments on UCCA parsing showing BSP achieves a primary edge F-score of 69.2%.
- Strength: BSP addresses limitations of existing parsers for handling BSS phenomena.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation including comparison with conversion-based methods and feature-set analysis.
- Strength: Potential impact on downstream applications like machine translation and summarization.
- Weakness: Some technical details are unclear and need more explanation and context.
- Weakness: The introduction’s third paragraph is confusing about deriving a new approximation and evaluating features.
- Weakness: Using the same word vectors for pivot and target is questionable and lacks clear motivation.
- Weakness: Concepts like separating words from a marginal distribution and co-adaptation are unclear and need clarification.
- Weakness: Evaluation is insufficient—paper lacks quantitative comparisons to other methods and example clusters/figure captions are inadequate.
- Suggestion: Authors should provide clearer explanations, justify design choices, and expand the quantitative evaluation.
- Question: How does BSP compare to other state-of-the-art parsers on BSS tasks?
- Question: Can the conversion-based approach be improved with more sophisticated conversion procedures or target representations?
- Question: How can the approach be extended to handle implicit nodes and inter-sentence relations in UCCA annotations?
