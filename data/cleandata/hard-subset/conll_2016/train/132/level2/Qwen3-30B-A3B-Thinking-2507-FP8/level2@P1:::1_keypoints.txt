Introduces lda2vec, jointly learning Dirichlet-sparse document-topic mixtures and dense skip-gram word embeddings.  
Novel integration: bridges interpretable topic models (LDA) and dense embeddings using Dirichlet sparsity (α < 1).  
Strong empirical validation: high topic coherence on Twenty Newsgroups and meaningful semantic regularities in Hacker News.  
Practical implementation: straightforward AD-framework (Chainer) code with open-source release and clear training details.  
Insufficient baseline comparisons to recent neural topic models (e.g., Neural LDA), leaving relative gains unclear.  
Topic coherence metric concerns: Palmetto’s external-corpus measure may be inappropriate for domain-specific Hacker News vocabulary.  
Missing ablation: no experiment removing the Dirichlet constraint to confirm it causes sparsity and interpretability.  
Inconsistent word initialization: pretrained vectors used for Twenty Newsgroups but random init for Hacker News without analysis of effects.  
Question: how does lda2vec’s topic coherence compare directly to Neural LDA on Twenty Newsgroups?  
Question: why use Palmetto for Hacker News—can authors report Hacker News–derived coherence (e.g., corpus NPMI) or human evaluations?  
Additional comment: include discussion of computational efficiency (training time vs. LDA) and sensitivity analysis for λ (e.g., λ = 200).
