## Review of "Mixing Dirichlet Topic Models and Word Embeddings to Make lda2vec"

This paper introduces lda2vec, a model combining the strengths of word embeddings (word2vec) and topic models (LDA) to generate both dense word vectors and interpretable document representations. The core idea is to jointly learn word vectors and document mixtures of topic vectors, leveraging automatic differentiation for efficient training. The authors aim to address the limitations of both traditional topic models (lack of semantic richness) and dense document representations (lack of interpretability).

The primary contribution is the lda2vec model itself, which effectively integrates word embeddings with a Dirichlet-distributed topic mixture, resulting in sparse, interpretable document representations. A secondary contribution lies in demonstrating the practical application of this model on both the Twenty Newsgroups dataset and a large Hacker News corpus, showcasing its ability to learn coherent topics and meaningful word relationships. Finally, the authors contribute a readily available, well-documented, and unit-tested open-source implementation of the model.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Combination:** The hybrid approach of combining word embeddings and topic models is a sensible and potentially powerful idea. The authors successfully implement this integration within a modern automatic differentiation framework.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both datasets demonstrate the model’s ability to learn meaningful representations. The topic coherence results on Twenty Newsgroups are promising and the qualitative analysis on Hacker News provides compelling evidence of learned semantic relationships.
*   **Interpretability:** The model explicitly aims for interpretability through the use of sparse document mixtures, a key advantage over purely dense representations.
*   **Open-Source Release:** Providing a well-documented and tested implementation significantly enhances the reproducibility and potential impact of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Baselines:** The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison to existing state-of-the-art topic modeling or document embedding techniques. A more thorough evaluation against established methods would strengthen the claims of improvement.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The impact of key hyperparameters (e.g., Dirichlet concentration parameter, dropout rates) is not thoroughly investigated. A more detailed ablation study would be beneficial.
*   **Hacker News Analysis Depth:** While the qualitative analysis on Hacker News is interesting, it lacks quantitative evaluation. Metrics beyond topic labeling would provide a more rigorous assessment of the model’s performance on this dataset.
*   **Lack of Theoretical Justification:** The paper primarily focuses on the practical implementation and empirical results. A more detailed theoretical analysis of the model’s properties and convergence guarantees would be valuable.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the model’s performance to the choice of the Dirichlet concentration parameter (α)?
2.  Could you provide a more detailed comparison of lda2vec to other topic modeling techniques (e.g., NMF, LDA) on the Twenty Newsgroups dataset, using standard evaluation metrics?
3.  What is the computational cost of training lda2vec compared to standard word2vec or LDA models, particularly for large datasets like Hacker News?
