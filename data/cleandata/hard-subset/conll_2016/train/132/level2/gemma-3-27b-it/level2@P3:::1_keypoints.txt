- Introduces lda2vec, jointly learning dense word embeddings and sparse, interpretable document mixtures by combining Skipgram negative sampling with a Dirichlet-likelihood term and simplex constraint.  
- Demonstrates the approach on Twenty Newsgroups and a Hacker News corpus and provides open-source, practical implementation details.  

- Novel hybrid: successfully integrates word embeddings with topic modeling, using a Dirichlet term to enforce sparsity and interpretability.  
- Empirical validation: shows coherent topics (with coherence scores on 20 Newsgroups) and domain-specific semantic relationships on Hacker News.  
- Practicality and reproducibility: open-source code, clear documentation, and use of automatic differentiation frameworks make the method accessible.  
- Clear presentation: well-motivated exposition and helpful figures that explain architecture and trade-offs between dense and sparse representations.  

- Limited baselines: lacks experimental comparisons to standard LDA and recent neural topic models (e.g., NTM, ProdLDA).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: no systematic analysis of key hyperparameters (Dirichlet α, regularization λ) or ablation studies.  
- Scalability concerns: unclear how the model scales to very large corpora; limited runtime/memory analysis.  
- Topic interpretation subjectivity: reliance on manual labeling and inspection; quantitative metrics beyond coherence are limited.  

- How do document representations compare to LDA/other topic models on downstream tasks (e.g., classification)?  
- What are training time and memory requirements for very large corpora (millions of documents)?  
- Why choose α = n−1 for the Dirichlet prior—empirical choice or theoretical justification?  
- How sensitive are results to word vector initialization and does pre-training help consistently?  

- Additional comment: paper claims unit-tested code but does not describe the test suite or testing strategy.
