- Proposes lda2vec: combines Skipgram Negative Sampling with Dirichlet-distributed topic mixtures to jointly learn word vectors and document representations.
- Hybrid Model (lda2vec): integrates Skipgram embeddings with a Dirichlet topic model in a single differentiable framework enabling joint optimization.
- Joint Learning & Interpretability: produces semantically rich and interpretable document representations via sparse topic mixtures and word embeddings.
- Empirical Validation: demonstrates coherent topics and semantic relationships on Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News, including topic coherence and analogy evaluations.
- Clear Motivation: well-articulated rationale for combining topic models and word embeddings to balance interpretability and semantic richness.
- Sound Technical Approach: technically sound integration of Skipgram with Dirichlet priors and practical use of automatic differentiation.
- Comprehensive Experiments: experiments on two datasets with coherence metrics, analogy tests, and noun-phrase tokenization for Hacker News.
- Reproducibility: code is open-source, documented, and unit-tested, enhancing reproducibility.
- Addresses Co-adaptation: uses dropout on document and word vectors to mitigate co-adaptation.
- Limited Novelty: combining distributional and topic models is not entirely new and needs clearer differentiation from related hybrids.
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity: performance appears sensitive to topic count and negative sampling exponent; λ=200 lacks strong justification.
- Evaluation Limitations: topic coherence scores and limited analogies are insufficient—needs human qualitative analysis and downstream task evaluation.
- Lack of Strong Baselines: missing comparisons to competitive document embedding methods like Doc2Vec and transformer-based models.
- Question: What is the computational cost of lda2vec compared to other document embedding techniques on large datasets?
- Question: How does lda2vec performance change with different initialization strategies for topic vectors?
- Question: How does varying the Dirichlet concentration parameter α affect sparsity and interpretability of learned topics?
- Question: How do lda2vec document embeddings compare to Doc2Vec or other methods on downstream tasks like document classification?
- Figure 1 is cluttered and would benefit from clearer labeling and visual organization.
- Palmetto should be introduced up front as a topic coherence evaluation tool rather than explained later.
- Recommend adding discussion of lda2vec limitations, including sensitivity to data quality and challenges interpreting topics in complex domains.
