- Paper proposes lda2vec: joint learning of Skip-gram word embeddings and Dirichlet-distributed sparse document-topic mixtures to gain interpretable document representations.
- Primary contribution: method for jointly learning word and document representations in automatic-differentiation frameworks for faster prototyping.
- Secondary contribution: empirical demonstrations on Twenty Newsgroups (good topic coherence) and Hacker News (domain-specific semantics and analogies).
- Practical contribution: open-source implementation (Chainer) and insightful analysis of learned representations.
- Strength: novel and reasonable fusion addressing interpretability of dense embeddings.
- Strength: strong empirical results with competitive coherence and qualitative word-analogy examples.
- Strength: practical reproducibility via automatic-differentiation implementation and released code.
- Strength: clear motivation and comprehensive related-work positioning.
- Weakness: limited comparative analysis against stronger baselines (e.g., NMF, advanced LDA inference).
- Weakness: insufficient discussion/analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., Dirichlet alpha, learning rate).
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability and computational complexity for very large corpora.
- Weakness: components are largely established; novelty mainly in their combination and needs clearer delineation of new aspects.
- Questions: sensitivity to Dirichlet concentration parameter α and its effect on coherence/interpretability?
- Questions: computational cost of training lda2vec vs. traditional LDA as topics or corpus size scale?
- Questions: rationale for choosing Chainer over other automatic-differentiation frameworks?
- Overall recommendation: solid paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
