- Contribution: Proposes lda2vec, combining Dirichlet-style topic modeling with word embeddings to learn dense word vectors and sparse interpretable document-topic mixtures.
- Contribution: Provides a practical implementation using an automatic differentiation framework (Chainer) for GPU-accelerated development.
- Contribution: Empirically evaluates lda2vec on Twenty Newsgroups and a Hacker News corpus, reporting topic coherence and qualitative examples.

- Strength: Clearly motivates bridging limitations of traditional topic models (lack of semantics) and dense embeddings (lack of interpretability).
- Strength: Technically sound design—uses Dirichlet likelihood to encourage sparse document mixtures and Skipgram for word embeddings.
- Strength: Emphasizes reproducibility by providing code and preprocessed datasets.
- Strength: Demonstrates learning of meaningful semantic relationships and analogies in Hacker News experiments.
- Strength: Well-written paper with helpful diagrams illustrating the model architecture.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., LDA, NMF, Doc2Vec, biterm models).
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, dropout, Dirichlet concentration, etc.).
- Weakness: Evaluation relies mainly on topic coherence and lacks downstream task assessments (classification, retrieval).
- Weakness: Limited justification and analysis for the chosen Dirichlet concentration parameter (α = n−1).
- Weakness: Vague description of noun phrase tokenization for the Hacker News corpus and its effect on results.

- Question: Please compare lda2vec to baseline methods (LDA, Doc2Vec, NMF) on Twenty Newsgroups.
- Question: How sensitive are results to the Dirichlet concentration parameter α; can you show results for different α values?
- Question: Have you evaluated learned document representations on downstream tasks (classification, retrieval); what are the results?
- Question: Provide detailed description of the noun phrase tokenization procedure for Hacker News and its impact.
- Question: What is the computational cost (training time, memory) of lda2vec versus standard LDA or Doc2Vec?

- Additional comment: Reference list appears incomplete with missing cited works.
- Additional comment: Figure formatting could be improved for clarity.

- Overall: Promising and well-motivated work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing evaluation, hyperparameter analysis, tokenization details, and references.
