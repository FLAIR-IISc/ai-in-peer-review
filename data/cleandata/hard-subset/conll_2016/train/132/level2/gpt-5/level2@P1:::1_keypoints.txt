Model: lda2vec augments SGNS by representing documents as sparse, nonnegative mixtures of learned topic vectors in a shared embedding space, enforced via a Dirichlet regularizer.  
Contributions: joint SGNS + sparse interpretable document–topic mixtures; autodiff/GPU implementation with released code; empirical evidence of coherent topics on 20NG and interpretable embeddings on Hacker News.

Strength: Conceptually simple and intuitive extension of SGNS where document vectors are convex combinations of topic vectors.  
Strength: Sparsity via simplex-constrained softmax and Dirichlet regularizer improves topic interpretability and concise document mixtures.  
Strength: Practical and reproducible implementation (Chainer, Adam, negative sampling) with released code and tests.  
Strength: Qualitative and coherence evidence shows meaningful topics on 20NG and domain-specific analogies on Hacker News.

Weakness: Limited novelty — similar ideas exist (doc2vec, topical embeddings); paper needs clearer positioning of what is genuinely new.  
Weakness: Evaluation omits baselines (LDA, GloVe+LDA, doc2vec+LDA) and quantitative ablations; HN results are only qualitative.  
Weakness: Methodological clarity issues (softmax dimensionality ambiguity, tying input/output embeddings without justification, fixed hyperparameters with little sensitivity analysis).  
Weakness: Claims about the Dirichlet’s impact on coherence lack quantitative backing, variance reporting, or examples.  
Weakness: Scalability/efficiency details absent — training time, hardware, and minibatch scaling of the Dirichlet term are not reported.

Questions: How does lda2vec compare conceptually and empirically to topical word embeddings (TWE) and LDA+word2vec/doc2vec pipelines?  
Question: Clarify the softmax input dimensionality in Section 2.2.1 — should latent vectors be R^n (topics) rather than R^300?  
Question: Did you compare tied vs. untied input/output word embeddings, and how does tying affect coherence and analogy performance?  
Question: Provide ablations/sensitivity analyses for λ and α and quantitative evidence that α=1 degrades coherence.  
Question: Please report topic coherence for standard baselines on 20NG and an extrinsic task (e.g., document classification) using identical preprocessing.  
Question: Can HN topic coherence be computed using an intrinsic PMI/NPMI estimator over the HN corpus instead of Palmetto?  
Question: Provide training details: time per epoch, total time, hardware specs, negative sampling settings (β sensitivity, number of negatives).
