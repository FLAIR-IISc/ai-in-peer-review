- Proposes lda2vec: augments SGNS/word2vec with document representations as convex mixtures over learned topic vectors, trained end-to-end with negative sampling and dropout.  
- Context vector is additive (cj = wj + dj); document vectors are simplex-constrained via softmax and sparsified with a Dirichlet prior (α < 1).  
- Experiments: quantitative Cv topic coherence on 20 Newsgroups, qualitative topics and analogies on a large Hacker News corpus; code released.

Contributions:
- Jointly learns word embeddings, interpretable topic vectors, and sparse document–topic mixtures by combining SGNS with Dirichlet-regularized document mixtures.  
- Uses an additive context tying document-wide signals and local co-occurrence, placing words, topics, and documents in a shared vector space.  
- Demonstrates coherent topics on 20NG and preserves embedding regularities with an autodiff-based, reproducible implementation.

Strengths:
- Clear, pragmatic, and interpretable objective that replaces dense document vectors with sparse simplex-constrained mixtures.  
- Principled Dirichlet regularization to induce sparsity in document-topic proportions.  
- Topics live in the same space as words, enabling intuitive nearest-neighbor inspection and theme interpretation.  
- Reports Cv and sensitivity to negative sampling exponent, with coherent example topics on 20NG.  
- Scalable, GPU-friendly implementation with open-source code and practical engineering choices.  
- Qualitative evidence that the model captures domain-specific terminology and analogies on a large corpus.

Weaknesses:
- Missing quantitative baselines: no comparison of Cv to standard LDA, DocNADE, or other topic models under matched settings.  
- Lacks ablations for key design choices (Dirichlet prior, softmax mixing, tying embeddings, dropout).  
- Limited positioning against closely related work (TWE, Gaussian LDA, NVDM, doc2vec) and insufficient contrast of objectives/novelty.  
- Large-corpus evaluation is purely qualitative; no alternative quantitative metrics reported for Hacker News.  
- Terminology is imprecise: the “Dirichlet-likelihood” is a prior, and the model is not a generative LDA-style topic model.  
- Practical training details (runtime, epochs, memory, seed stability) are not reported.

Questions for rebuttal:
- How does lda2vec’s Cv compare to standard LDA on 20NG with matched preprocessing and topic counts?  
- What is the quantitative impact of the Dirichlet prior (λ, α) on coherence and sparsity (e.g., entropy of pjk)?  
- How are new/unseen documents inferred—what is the practical procedure to obtain pjk at test time?  
- Are topic vectors regularized for diversity (orthogonality/decorrelation), and is redundancy observed?  
- What are the training time and resource requirements, and sensitivity to tying vs. untied embeddings?  
- Any failure modes when removing the Dirichlet term or changing α/λ?

Additional suggestions:
- Essential: add LDA comparison on 20NG, ablation on λ/α, and clarify likelihood/ non-generative claims.  
- Recommended: include at least one quantitative metric on Hacker News (analogy accuracy, retrieval, or intrusion tests).  
- Nice-to-have: discuss relations to TWE/Gaussian LDA, and report topic sparsity and diversity statistics.
