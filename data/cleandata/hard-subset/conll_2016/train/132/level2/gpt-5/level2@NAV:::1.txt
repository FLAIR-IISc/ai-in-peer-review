Summary and contributions
- A unified model (lda2vec) that augments SGNS with document vectors constrained to be simplex mixtures over learned topic vectors, yielding sparse, interpretable document-topic proportions while sharing a vector space with words.
- A practical training recipe implementable in standard autodiff frameworks (code released), combining negative sampling, dropout, and a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity term.
- Empirical demonstration that topics are coherent on 20 Newsgroups (best Cv≈0.567 with 20 topics, β=0.75) and that the shared space enables intuitive topic-word inspection and corpus-specific analogies (Hacker News).

Strengths
- Interpretability by design: Projecting document vectors onto a nonnegative simplex over topics, with a Dirichlet-like objective, induces sparse mixtures that are easy to inspect; examples on 20NG (e.g., “Space,” “Encryption”) show clear themes and reasonable Cv scores.
- Joint space for words, topics, and documents: The ability to compute topic–word similarities directly (argmax over topic·word) is compelling and distinguishes the approach from LDA with post-hoc embeddings; the qualitative Hacker News topics and analogies illustrate practical usefulness.
- Simplicity and implementability: The formulation is lightweight (SGNS + linear mixing + prior), fits neatly into minibatch training with Adam and negative sampling, and the open-source implementation and preprocessing scripts support reproducibility.
- Sensible engineering choices: Use of dropout on both document and pivot vectors and minibatch scaling of the prior term show awareness of optimization pitfalls.

Weaknesses
- Limited empirical validation: No quantitative comparison against strong baselines (e.g., LDA/ProdLDA, doc2vec, TWE) on topic coherence, downstream classification, retrieval, or analogy benchmarks; HN results are purely qualitative.
- Novelty relative to prior art is under-argued: Mixing embeddings with topic models and learning joint spaces has precedent; the paper would benefit from a clearer differentiation and ablation showing the unique impact of the Dirichlet-simplex constraint vs standard doc2vec or unconstrained document vectors.
- Prior and training details need justification: The choice of α=n−1 and λ=200 appears ad hoc; no sensitivity analysis, stability discussion, or failure modes (e.g., topic redundancy/collapse) are provided.
- Coherence reporting lacks context: 20NG Cv≈0.56 is reported without baseline numbers or statistical significance; the decision not to evaluate HN coherence could be mitigated with alternative internal metrics.

Questions to authors
- How does lda2vec compare to LDA, doc2vec, and topical word embedding baselines on 20NG in Cv and in a standardized downstream task (e.g., 20NG classification)? Please include numbers.
- Sensitivity: How do α, λ, K, and dropout affect sparsity, coherence, and analogy quality? Any guidelines for selection?
- Does the shared space hurt/help word-level semantics versus SGNS alone? Report standard analogy/similarity scores.
- What is the computational cost vs LDA and doc2vec on 20NG (epochs, wall-clock, GPU/CPU), and how does it scale?
- How do you prevent topic duplication or drift into single high-frequency words? Any regularization beyond the Dirichlet term?
