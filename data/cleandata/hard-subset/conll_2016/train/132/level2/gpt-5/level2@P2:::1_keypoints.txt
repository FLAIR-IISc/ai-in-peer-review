- Proposes lda2vec: augment SGNS with document-level vectors constrained as sparse softmax mixtures over a small set of latent topic vectors, using a Dirichlet-like regularizer.
- Jointly trains word, topic, and document vectors in a shared embedding space with context vectors formed by summing pivot and document vectors.
- Demonstrates interpretability on 20 Newsgroups (Cv coherence) and qualitatively on Hacker News, and releases autodiff/GPU code.
- Contribution: simple, differentiable objective combining SGNS with sparse, interpretable document-to-topic mixtures.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that topic vectors are interpretable while preserving word-level linear regularities; open-source implementation provided.
- Strength: Conceptually simple and implementable, leveraging autodiff/GPUs and reproducible regularizers; released code is practical for the community.
- Strength: Interpretability via simplex-constrained, sparsity-inducing document mixtures and ability to retrieve nearest words to topic vectors.
- Strength: Joint embedding space enables direct inspection of topic-word relations and preserves vector arithmetic in domain-specific corpora.
- Strength: Scales to large corpora (75M tokens, 110k vocab) and adapts to domain-specific semantics.
- Weakness: Missing quantitative baselines — no comparisons to LDA, NMF, GloVe+LDA, or doc2vec-based topics under controlled settings.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation of representation utility — no downstream document classification or standard word-level analogy/similarity benchmarks.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in “Dirichlet” treatment and sensitivity — softmax parameterization plus Dirichlet penalty is not a full generative model and α/λ sensitivity is untested.
- Weakness: Lack of ablations for key design choices (sum vs alternatives, tied embeddings, dropout, initialization) and unquantified claims about regularizer effects.
- Weakness: Hacker News topic quality is only qualitative; quantitative diagnostics (PMI, diversity, held-out coherence) are missing.
- Question: How does lda2vec’s coherence compare to LDA and doc2vec+clustering on 20NG under matched preprocessing and topic counts?
- Question: Can you report 20NG document classification performance using learned mixtures compared to LDA/doc2vec baselines?
- Question: How sensitive are coherence and embedding quality to α and λ — please provide a small grid with coherence and word analogy scores?
- Question: Are input/output SGNS embeddings tied, and what is the performance impact of tying vs not tying?
- Question: How are unseen documents handled at inference — can topics/word vectors be fixed and document mixtures efficiently optimized and stably inferred?
- Question: Do you apply topic diversity/orthogonality regularization, and what empirical redundancy issues arise?
- Additional comment: Clarify dropout and training hyperparameters and report training time comparisons to LDA for scalability claims.
- Additional comment: Report topic-vector norm distribution and whether vectors are normalized, since large-norm topics can dominate mixtures.
- Additional comment: Fix misformatted references and figures; proofread the manuscript.
- Overall recommendation: Idea is appealing and useful but empirical evaluation is insufficient; needs quantitative baselines, downstream tasks, ablations, and sensitivity analyses to be a compelling contribution.
