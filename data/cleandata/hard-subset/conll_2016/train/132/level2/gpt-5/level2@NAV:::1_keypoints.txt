- Unified model (lda2vec): augments SGNS with document vectors constrained as simplex mixtures over learned topic vectors, yielding sparse, interpretable document-topic proportions in the same vector space as words.
- Practical training recipe: combines negative sampling, dropout, and a Dirichlet-inspired sparsity term; code released and implementable in standard autodiff frameworks.
- Empirical claims: topics appear coherent on 20 Newsgroups (Cv≈0.567 with 20 topics, β=0.75) and the shared space yields intuitive topic–word inspection and corpus-specific analogies (Hacker News).
- Strength — interpretability by design: simplex projection and Dirichlet-like objective induce sparse, inspectable mixtures with clear thematic topics (examples and Cv scores shown).
- Strength — joint space for words, topics, documents: enables direct topic–word similarity and corpus analogies, distinguishing it from LDA plus post-hoc embeddings.
- Strength — simplicity and implementability: lightweight SGNS + linear mixing + prior fits minibatch Adam training; open-source code and preprocessing aid reproducibility.
- Strength — sensible engineering choices: use of dropout on document and pivot vectors and minibatch scaling of the prior indicate awareness of optimization issues.
- Weakness — limited empirical validation: no quantitative comparisons to strong baselines (LDA/ProdLDA, doc2vec, TWE) on coherence, classification, retrieval, or analogy benchmarks; HN results are qualitative only.
- Weakness — novelty under-argued: prior work mixes embeddings and topic models; paper lacks clear differentiation and ablation to show unique impact of the Dirichlet-simplex constraint.
- Weakness — prior and training details need justification: hyperparameters (α=n−1, λ=200) appear ad hoc with no sensitivity analysis, stability discussion, or failure-mode exploration (e.g., collapse/redundancy).
- Weakness — coherence reporting lacks context: 20NG Cv≈0.56 is reported without baselines or statistical significance and HN coherence is unevaluated.
- Question — comparative performance: how does lda2vec compare quantitatively to LDA, doc2vec, and topical word embeddings on 20NG Cv and on a standardized downstream task (e.g., 20NG classification)?
- Question — sensitivity and guidance: how do α, λ, K, and dropout affect sparsity, coherence, and analogy quality, and what are selection guidelines?
- Question — effect on word semantics: does the shared space help or hurt word-level semantics versus SGNS alone; report standard analogy/similarity scores.
- Question — computational cost and scaling: what are epochs, wall-clock time, hardware (GPU/CPU) comparisons vs LDA and doc2vec, and how does it scale with data/topic count?
- Question — topic collapse/duplication prevention: how are duplicate topics or topics dominated by single high-frequency words avoided; is there regularization beyond the Dirichlet term?
