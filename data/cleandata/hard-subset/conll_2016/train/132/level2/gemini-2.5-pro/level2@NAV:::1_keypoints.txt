- Introduces lda2vec: jointly learns word, topic, and document vectors by summing word and document vectors, with document vectors as sparse mixtures of topic vectors.  
- Contribution: integrates topic modeling directly into word embedding training to capture local syntax and global themes.  
- Contribution: shows building a complex probabilistic model using an automatic differentiation framework, avoiding custom inference.  
- Strength: model elegance and novelty—cj = wj + dj unifies word and topic representations in a shared, interpretable space.  
- Strength: strong qualitative evaluation—coherent topics on 20 Newsgroups and Hacker News and meaningful domain-specific word analogies.  
- Strength: reproducibility and practicality—implemented in an autodiff framework with open-source code.  
- Weakness: lacks quantitative comparisons to standard LDA (e.g., Mallet) and benchmarked word embeddings (word2vec/GloVe).  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation and hyperparameter analysis, particularly sensitivity of the Dirichlet weight λ.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns not addressed—computational complexity and training time compared to separate LDA+word2vec are unclear.  
- Question: request quantitative topic coherence comparisons (e.g., Cv or NPMI) against standard LDA on 20 Newsgroups.  
- Question: ask how λ=200 was chosen and how sensitive topic coherence and word analogy results are to this hyperparameter.
