- Introduces lda2vec, which jointly learns word and document vectors by combining skip-gram negative sampling with LDA-style mixed-membership document representations.
- Core innovation: define skip-gram context as pivot word vector plus document vector, where document vectors are sparse mixtures of topic vectors learned end-to-end.
- Demonstrates building a hybrid neural–probabilistic model using automatic differentiation (Chainer), avoiding custom inference algorithms.
- Shows qualitative and quantitative results: coherent topics on 20 Newsgroups (Cv) and strong domain-specific embeddings on a Hacker News corpus.
- Strength: simple and novel formulation that captures local word and global document contexts simultaneously.
- Strength: effectively bridges neural embeddings and probabilistic topic models, allowing topic and word vectors in the same space for direct inspection.
- Strength: strong qualitative results (interpretable topics, word similarities, and analogies) on real-world corpora.
- Strength: practical and reproducible implementation with released code.
- Weakness: lacks quantitative comparisons to baselines—no LDA or other neural topic model baselines for topic coherence.
- Weakness: word embedding evaluation is only qualitative; needs intrinsic benchmarks (e.g., WordSim, analogy tasks) versus SGNS.
- Weakness: key hyperparameters (e.g., λ = 200, α = 1/n) are inadequately justified and need sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: ambiguous minibatch approximation of the global Dirichlet term Ld with unclear effects on convergence or bias.
- Questions for authors: provide LDA baseline coherence (same preprocessing), quantitative evaluation of embeddings versus SGNS, and rationale/analysis of the minibatch Dirichlet approximation.
