1. Summary: Introduces lda2vec, jointly learning word embeddings, topic vectors, and document-topic mixtures by extending SGNS with document vectors formed from weighted topic vectors and a Dirichlet sparsity term, trained end-to-end.

2. Contribution: Proposes a unified model that places word, document, and topic vectors in a shared semantic space, combining dense embeddings with sparse, interpretable topic representations.

3. Contribution: Demonstrates practical implementation of a hybrid probabilistic-neural model using automatic differentiation, lowering the barrier to developing such models.

4. Strength: Novel and elegant synthesis of SGNS and LDA that links word-level semantics to document-level themes via sparse topic-mixed document vectors.

5. Strength: Produces more interpretable document representations by enforcing sparse, non-negative document-topic proportions, with qualitatively coherent topics.

6. Strength: Strong qualitative evaluation showing coherent topics and high-quality word embeddings that retain semantic/syntactic regularities.

7. Strength: Practical and reproducible implementation with code released using standard autodiff frameworks.

8. Weakness: Lacks quantitative comparisons to established baselines (e.g., standard LDA, SGNS, GloVe) on topic coherence and word embedding benchmarks.

9. Weakness: Key hyperparameters and design choices (e.g., Dirichlet weight λ=200, dropout, context summation) are not justified or analyzed for sensitivity; no ablation for the sparsity term.

10. Weakness: Ambiguous minibatch training procedure for the corpus-level Dirichlet term; unclear how out-of-batch document-proportion vectors are handled and updated.

11. Question: Please provide quantitative topic coherence comparisons against a well-tuned LDA baseline on Twenty Newsgroups.

12. Question: Clarify the minibatch update procedure—are document-proportion vectors for all documents updated or only those in the batch, and how is the corpus-wide Ld approximated?

13. Question: How sensitive are topic coherence and word vector quality to the Dirichlet strength λ=200; please report effects of varying λ.
