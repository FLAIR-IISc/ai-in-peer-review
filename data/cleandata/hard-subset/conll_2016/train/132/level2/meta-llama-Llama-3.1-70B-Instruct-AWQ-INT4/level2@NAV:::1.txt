**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a new model called lda2vec, which combines the strengths of Dirichlet topic models and word embeddings to learn interpretable document representations. The model extends the Skipgram Negative-Sampling (SGNS) objective to include document-wide feature vectors and learns continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets: Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.

**Main Contributions**

1. **lda2vec model**: The authors propose a new model that combines the strengths of Dirichlet topic models and word embeddings to learn interpretable document representations.
2. **Extension of SGNS objective**: The authors extend the SGNS objective to include document-wide feature vectors and learn continuous document weights loading onto topic vectors.
3. **Experimental results**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on two datasets: Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments, showing that the model can learn coherent topics and linear relationships between words.

**Strengths**

1. **Interpretable document representations**: lda2vec learns interpretable document representations that can be easily understood by humans.
2. **Combination of strengths**: The model combines the strengths of Dirichlet topic models and word embeddings, allowing it to learn both sparse and dense representations.
3. **Effective on large datasets**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of lda2vec on a large dataset (Hacker News comments), showing that the model can handle large amounts of data.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of lda2vec, which may be a limitation for very large datasets.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process, which may be important for achieving good results.
3. **Comparison to other models**: The authors do not provide a detailed comparison to other models that combine topic models and word embeddings, which may be important for understanding the strengths and weaknesses of lda2vec.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of lda2vec and how it compares to other models?
2. How did you tune the hyperparameters of lda2vec, and what are the most important hyperparameters to tune?
3. Can you provide a detailed comparison to other models that combine topic models and word embeddings, such as Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) and Word2Vec?
