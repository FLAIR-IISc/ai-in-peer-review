**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes a new model, lda2vec, that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings to form interpretable document representations and dense word vectors. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A novel model that jointly learns dense word vectors and sparse document representations using a combination of Skipgram Negative-Sampling (SGNS) and Dirichlet-optimized sparse topic mixtures.
2. A method that leverages automatic differentiation frameworks to simplify the development and evaluation of the model.
3. Experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of lda2vec in discovering coherent topics and learning linear relationships between words in two datasets: Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective combination of topic models and word embeddings**: The paper successfully integrates the strengths of both approaches, allowing for the formation of interpretable document representations and dense word vectors that capture semantic regularities.
2. **Simplified development and evaluation**: The use of automatic differentiation frameworks enables the rapid development and evaluation of the model, making it easier to experiment with different architectures and hyperparameters.
3. **Strong experimental results**: The paper presents convincing experimental results on two datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of lda2vec in discovering coherent topics and learning linear relationships between words.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited comparison to existing methods**: While the paper compares lda2vec to SGNS and LDA, it would be beneficial to include comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods, such as GloVe and Paragraph Vectors.
2. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The paper primarily focuses on empirical results, but a more in-depth theoretical analysis of the model's properties and limitations would be valuable.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process, which could be important for reproducing the results.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the range of values explored and the criteria used for selection?
2. How does lda2vec perform on other datasets, such as Wikipedia or BookCorpus?
3. Have you considered applying lda2vec to other NLP tasks, such as text classification or sentiment analysis?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the model and experimental results. The use of figures and tables to illustrate the results is helpful. However, some minor issues with formatting and punctuation were noticed.
