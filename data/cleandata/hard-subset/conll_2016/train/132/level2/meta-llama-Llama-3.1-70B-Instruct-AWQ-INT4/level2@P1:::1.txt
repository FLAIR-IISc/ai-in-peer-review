**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a hybrid model, lda2vec, that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings. The model learns dense word vectors jointly with Dirichlet-distributed latent document-level mixtures of topic vectors. This formulation produces sparse, interpretable document mixtures through a non-negative simplex constraint. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on two datasets: Twenty Newsgroups and Hacker News comments.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Hybrid model**: The paper proposes a novel hybrid model that combines the strengths of topic models and word embeddings. This is a significant contribution, as it allows for the learning of dense word vectors and sparse, interpretable document mixtures.
2. **Joint learning of word and document vectors**: The model learns word and document vectors jointly, which allows for the capture of semantic regularities between words and documents.
3. **Sparse and interpretable document mixtures**: The model produces sparse and interpretable document mixtures, which is a significant advantage over traditional dense document representations.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of word embeddings**: The paper effectively uses word embeddings to capture semantic regularities between words.
2. **Interpretable document mixtures**: The model produces sparse and interpretable document mixtures, which is a significant advantage over traditional dense document representations.
3. **Flexibility**: The model can be easily extended to other datasets and tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The model requires significant computational resources, especially for large datasets.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The model requires careful tuning of hyperparameters, which can be time-consuming and challenging.
3. **Limited evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the model on two datasets, which may not be representative of all possible use cases.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the computational complexity of the model, especially for large datasets?
2. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and how you selected the optimal values?
3. Have you considered evaluating the model on other datasets or tasks, such as text classification or information retrieval?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed model and its advantages. The experimental results are promising, and the model shows potential for capturing semantic regularities between words and documents. However, the computational complexity and hyperparameter tuning requirements may limit the model's applicability to large-scale datasets.
