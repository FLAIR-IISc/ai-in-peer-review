- Paper augments text-based sentiment analysis with eye-tracking features (basic fixation/saccade stats and graph-based gaze-saliency), evaluated on two datasets with sarcasm/irony, includes ablations and mobile deployment discussion.

- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that gaze-derived features provide complementary signal for sentiment classification, especially on complex pragmatic phenomena.

- Contribution: Careful, interpretable feature-set comparison (Sn, Sr, Gz) with significance analysis clarifying when and how cognitive features help.

- Contribution: Preliminary feasibility analysis for practical eye-tracking data collection and deployment (Section 7).

- Strength: Conceptual novelty — using eye-tracking as a cognitive proxy to address limitations of text-only models on sarcasm/irony.

- Strength: Thorough ablations and interpretability; clear Sn vs Sn+Sr vs Sn+Sr+Gz comparisons and notable relative gains on complex constructs (e.g., +6.1% relative F on irony).

- Strength: Feasibility discussion appropriately addresses practicality, user consent, and mobile sensing routes.

- Strength: Methodological clarity — well-defined basic and graph-based gaze features, multiple classifier comparisons, and organized feature categories aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness: Domain conflation — datasets mix movie reviews and tweets; domain-separated training/evaluation is needed to assess domain sensitivity.

- Weakness: Modest absolute gains from gaze overall — Gz adds only 0.6–1.5 F1 over Sn+Sr; cost-benefit should be discussed.

- Weakness: Baseline context unclear — reported F1s seem low vs Pang & Lee movie-review SOTA; need clearer positioning against domain-appropriate and modern neural baselines.

- Weakness: Potential data overlap/leakage — unclear whether snippets overlap between baseline training sources or across D1/D2 folds; deduplication must be clarified.

- Weakness: Minor presentation issue — excessive use of bold is distracting.

- Question: Do D1 and D2 contain any snippets overlapping with Pang & Lee or Sentiment140 sources or across train/test folds; please clarify deduplication?

- Question: Can you report domain-separated training/testing (movie-only vs tweet-only) and cross-domain transfer results?

- Question: How robust are gaze features to lower sampling rates and mobile noise; can you provide per-device or per-reader variability analyses?
