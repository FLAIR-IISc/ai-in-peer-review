- Introduces using eye-movement (gaze) cognitive features alongside textual and sarcasm features for sentiment and sarcasm detection, reporting statistically significant F-score gains on two datasets.
- Main contribution: proposes eye-tracking–derived cognitive features as a novel modality for core NLP tasks.
- Strength: Novel idea bridging psycholinguistics and computational linguistics by applying eye-tracking to sentiment analysis.
- Strength: Well-written, clearly structured paper with transparent feature descriptions and reproducible experimental setup.
- Strength: Cognitive features notably improve classification on ironic/sarcastic texts (6.1% F-score gain in held-out ironic data).
- Weakness: Overall performance improvement is marginal (0.6–1.5% over a strong textual baseline), questioning practical value given costly data collection.
- Weakness: Lacks strong theoretical justification that specific gaze features capture sentiment polarity rather than general reading difficulty or complexity.
- Weakness: Feasibility and real-world deployment claims are speculative and fail to address privacy, consent, and scalability concerns.
