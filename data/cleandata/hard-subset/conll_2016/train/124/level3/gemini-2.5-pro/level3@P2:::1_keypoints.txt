- Introduces eye-tracking "cognitive features" as a new modality for sentiment analysis.
- Provides empirical evidence that adding gaze features yields a small but statistically significant F-score improvement, especially for sarcastic/ironic text.
- High novelty with potential to inspire similar multimodal approaches for other challenging NLP tasks.
- Strong experimental design: two datasets, multiple classifiers, systematic feature ablation, and held-out irony analysis.
- Clear writing and detailed feature engineering make the work accessible and reproducible.
- Performance gains are marginal (e.g., +0.6% and +1.5% F-score), raising questions about practical benefit.
- Theoretical grounding is weak; gaze features may reflect general reading difficulty rather than sentiment-specific processing.
- Serious scalability and deployment concerns since eye-tracking data collection is costly and impractical at scale.
- Request for focused feature analysis on the ironic subset to identify which cognitive features drive gains.
- Ask whether gains persist after controlling for readability to rule out general cognitive-load confounds.
- Seek clarification on deployment: require gaze data per new text or plan to predict gaze from text for practical use.
