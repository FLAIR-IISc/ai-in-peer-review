

This paper proposes augmenting traditional sentiment analysis features with cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of readers, introducing an interesting novel approach to handling complex sentiment constructs like sarcasm and irony. The authors claim their enhanced feature set improves sentiment polarity detection by up to 9.3% on two datasets compared to traditional features alone.

The main contributions I identify are: (1) introducing eye-tracking features as cognitive proxies for sentiment analysis (a novel methodological contribution), (2) conducting thorough feature significance analysis demonstrating cognitive features' efficacy for complex constructs (a significant empirical contribution), and (3) addressing feasibility of the approach through discussion of mobile eye-tracking technology (a valuable practical contribution).

Strengths include the novel cognitive feature approach that directly addresses semantic/pragmatic challenges in sentiment analysis, the clear and helpful comparison of feature sets showing how different components contribute to performance, and the appropriate discussion of feasibility in Section 7, which acknowledges practical limitations while outlining promising applications.

Key weaknesses are: (1) datasets conflate movie reviews and tweets, which undermines interpretability and training effectiveness by mixing domains with different linguistic properties; (2) results in Table 2 appear low relative to Pang & Lee's state-of-the-art (though more reasonable when compared to Twitter data), raising questions about the baseline; (3) lack of clarification on whether training and test sets contain overlapping snippets (even from same sources), which could compromise results; and (4) excessive use of bold formatting that distracts from the content.

Questions for authors: Please clarify whether there are overlapping snippets between training and test sets in Datasets 1 and 2. Also, explain why results are lower than Pang & Lee's state-of-the-art despite the authors' claim of significant improvement.
