- Contribution: Introduces a novel sentiment analysis approach combining traditional features with cognitive features from eye-tracking data.
- Contribution: Provides empirical evidence of marginal classification improvements, especially for complex constructs like sarcasm.
- Contribution: Offers detailed analysis showing cognitive features are effective for linguistically nuanced text.

- Strength: Well-structured, clearly written paper that is accessible to readers.
- Strength: Thorough discussion of lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic challenges in sentiment analysis.
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation using multiple classifiers, two datasets, and a held-out dataset for complex constructs.
- Strength: Feature significance analysis shows many top-ranked features are gaze-based (10/20 for Dataset 1).
- Strength: Cognitive features yield larger improvements on complex sentiments (6.1% on irony vs. 2.1% on simple texts).

- Weakness: Weak theoretical motivation linking specific eye-movement patterns to sentiment processing beyond reading ease.
- Weakness: Marginal overall performance gains (0.6% and 1.5% over best baseline) undermine practical impact.
- Weakness: Feasibility and real-world deployment argument (e.g., e-commerce example) is unconvincing given eye-tracking requirements.
- Weakness: No discussion of whether cognitive features can be approximated from other, more practical signals.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to recent state-of-the-art deep learning methods that automatically learn text features.

- Question: How can you strengthen the theoretical connection between eye-movement patterns and specific sentiment phenomena (e.g., sarcasm)?
- Question: How do you justify the complexity and data requirements of eye-tracking given only marginal performance gains?
- Question: Why were comparisons to deep learning baselines (e.g., CNN/RNN methods) not included?

- Overall: Interesting idea with potential, but current work lacks sufficient theoretical justification, compelling gains, and practical feasibility to establish a strong contribution.
