- Proposes TBuckets: a novel method that measures LDA topic quality by grouping topic words into thematic buckets and scoring coherence from the largest bucket.
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg, evaluated on three public datasets with reported improvements over prior methods.
- Claims TBuckets-SVD and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg are parameter-free, removing the need for tuning and improving efficiency versus state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- The bucket-based idea is interesting and may generalize to other NLP tasks.
- Empirical results show better performance than baselines on three datasets.
- Review criticizes the paper for lacking clear justification for using cognitive features (eye-movement patterns) for sentiment and sarcasm tasks.
- Notes that adding cognitive features yields only marginal performance gains (e.g., Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr).
- Finds the discussion on feasibility unconvincing, with unclear practical usefulness in the provided example (section 7.2).
- Asks for stronger justification of eye-movement/cognitive features for sentiment and sarcasm detection.
- Requests plans to address the marginal improvement when using cognitive features.
- Requests additional examples demonstrating TBuckets' usefulness in other NLP tasks.
