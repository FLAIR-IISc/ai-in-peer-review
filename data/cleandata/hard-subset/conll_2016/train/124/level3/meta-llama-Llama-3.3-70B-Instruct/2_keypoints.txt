- Paper proposes TBuckets, a novel method that groups topic words into thematic buckets to compute LDA topic coherence.
- Introduces three bucket-creation techniques: TBuckets-Clustering, TBuckets-SVD, and TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
- Evaluates methods on three public datasets and reports TBuckets-SVD-Reorg outperforming prior state-of-the-art on two datasets.
- Contribution: new TBuckets approach offering an alternative measure of topic coherence.
- Contribution: three concrete techniques for building buckets to operationalize the approach.
- Contribution: empirical evaluation demonstrating effectiveness, especially for TBuckets-SVD-Reorg.
- Strength: novel perspective on measuring topic coherence.
- Strength: thorough evaluation across three datasets.
- Strength: comparison against state-of-the-art showing competitive performance.
- Strength: demonstrated application to weakly supervised text classification.
- Weakness: unclear whether training/test datasets 1 and 2 have overlapping snippets, which may bias results.
- Weakness: distracting use of bold text in places, hurting readability.
- Weakness: mixing domains in datasets may weaken interpretability; single-domain evaluation could improve analysis.
- Question: please provide dataset details (number of documents and topics per dataset).
- Question: how was the optimal number of buckets per topic determined and how sensitive is performance to this choice?
- Question: please elaborate on TBuckets' application to weakly supervised classification, including benefits and limitations.
