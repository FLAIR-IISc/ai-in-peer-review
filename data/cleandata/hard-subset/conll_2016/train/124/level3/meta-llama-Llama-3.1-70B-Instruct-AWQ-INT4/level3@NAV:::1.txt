**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of readers. The authors claim that this is the first use of cognitive features in natural language processing (NLP) for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. The paper presents a comprehensive review of existing sentiment analysis techniques, highlighting the challenges posed by lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic nuances in user-generated text. The authors introduce a new feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data. The experimental results show a significant improvement in sentiment polarity detection, especially for complex texts with irony and sarcasm.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel use of cognitive features**: The paper introduces a new approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of readers.
2. **Improved sentiment polarity detection**: The experimental results show a significant improvement in sentiment polarity detection, especially for complex texts with irony and sarcasm.
3. **Comprehensive review of sentiment analysis techniques**: The paper provides a thorough review of existing sentiment analysis techniques, highlighting the challenges posed by lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic nuances in user-generated text.

**Strengths**

1. **Interesting idea**: The use of cognitive features for sentiment analysis is a novel and interesting approach that has the potential to improve the accuracy of sentiment analysis systems.
2. **Well-written paper**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.
3. **Comprehensive review**: The paper provides a comprehensive review of existing sentiment analysis techniques, highlighting the challenges posed by lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic nuances in user-generated text.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Weak motivation**: The motivation for using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not clearly explained, and the authors could have provided more evidence to support their hypothesis.
2. **Marginal improvement**: The improvement in sentiment polarity detection is marginal, and the authors could have provided more analysis to explain the significance of the results.
3. **Unconvincing feasibility discussion**: The discussion on the feasibility of the approach is unconvincing, and the authors could have provided more evidence to support their claims.

**Questions to Authors**

3. Can you provide more analysis to explain the significance of the results, especially for complex texts with irony and sarcasm?
