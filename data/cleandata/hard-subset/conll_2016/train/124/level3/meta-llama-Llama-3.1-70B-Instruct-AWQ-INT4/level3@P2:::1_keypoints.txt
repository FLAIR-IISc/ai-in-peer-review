- Proposes combining eye-movement (cognitive) features with linguistic features for sentiment and sarcasm detection, claiming up to 9.3% accuracy improvement on two datasets.

- Introduces cognitive (eye-movement) features as a novel input for sentiment and sarcasm analysis.

- Evaluates the approach on two public datasets and reports improved sentiment polarity detection.

- Conducts feature significance analysis showing cognitive features help capture irony and sarcasm.

- Innovative idea integrating cognitive signals into NLP tasks.

- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Work could inspire further research using cognitive features in NLP.

- Motivation for why cognitive features should help sentiment polarity is weak and lacks theoretical backing.

- Reported accuracy improvement is marginal and may not justify the added complexity of using gaze data.

- Feasibility discussion (e.g., Section 7.2 example) is unconvincing and does not clearly demonstrate benefit.

- Please provide stronger theoretical justification for why cognitive features aid sentiment detection.

- How do you plan to address or justify the marginal accuracy gains from adding cognitive features?

- Provide more convincing, practical examples or scenarios where incorporating gaze data is beneficial and feasible.
