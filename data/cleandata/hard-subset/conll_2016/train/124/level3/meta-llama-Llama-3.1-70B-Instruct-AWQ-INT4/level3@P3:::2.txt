This paper proposes an innovative approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors introduce a new feature set that combines traditional linguistic features with gaze-based features, achieving significant improvements in sentiment polarity detection, especially for complex texts with irony and sarcasm.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. The introduction of cognitive features as a novel approach to sentiment analysis, which has the potential to improve the accuracy of sentiment polarity detection, especially for complex texts.
2. The development of a comprehensive feature set that combines traditional linguistic features with gaze-based features, which can be used to improve the performance of sentiment analysis systems.
3. The evaluation of the proposed approach on two publicly available datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving sentiment polarity detection accuracy.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The innovative approach to sentiment analysis using cognitive features, which has the potential to improve the accuracy of sentiment polarity detection.
2. The comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach on two publicly available datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving sentiment polarity detection accuracy.
3. The clear and helpful discussion and comparison of feature sets, which provides valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of different feature combinations.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

1. The datasets used in the evaluation are not separated by domain, which may affect the generalizability of the results. It would be beneficial to evaluate the approach on datasets from different domains to improve the robustness of the results.
2. The results in Table 2 appear to be low compared to the state-of-the-art results reported by Pang and Lee. However, when compared to Twitter data, the results are more reasonable. It would be helpful to provide a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art results to better understand the performance of the proposed approach.
3. There is a need for clarification on whether there are overlapping snippets between the training and test sets in datasets 1 and 2, even if they are from the same sources. This information is crucial to ensure the validity of the results.

Questions to the authors:

1. Can you provide more information on the preprocessing steps used to extract the gaze-based features from the eye-tracking data?
2. How did you select the specific gaze-based features used in the study, and are there any other features that could be explored in future work?
3. Can you provide more details on the statistical significance test used to compare the performance of different feature combinations, and how the results were interpreted?

Additional comments:

* The use of bold text throughout the paper is excessive and distracting. It would be better to use bold text only when necessary to emphasize important information.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results and the potential applications of the proposed approach in real-world scenarios.
* The authors may want to consider exploring the use of deep learning techniques to automatically learn features from the eye-tracking data, rather than relying on hand-crafted features.
