- Proposes combining traditional sentiment features with cognitive features from readers' eye-movements for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
- Claims this is the first use of eye-movement–derived cognitive features in NLP and reports significant accuracy improvements over traditional feature sets.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and methodologically explained.
- Contribution: introduces a novel feature set merging textual and cognitive signals.
- Contribution: empirically demonstrates improved accuracy on two datasets for sentiment and sarcasm detection.
- Contribution: presents a new direction beyond purely textual features for sentiment analysis.
- Strength: approach is novel and potentially impactful for improving sentiment analysis.
- Strength: evaluation is thorough, using two datasets and showing performance gains.
- Strength: explanations and presentation are clear and easy to follow.
- Weakness: motivation for using cognitive features beyond reading ease is weak and underexplained.
- Weakness: reported improvement from cognitive features appears marginal in some comparisons and significance is not discussed.
- Weakness: feasibility examples and real-world applicability (Section 7.2) are unconvincing and lack concrete benefits.
- Question: please explain why cognitive features are relevant to sentiment analysis beyond reading ease.
- Question: how will you collect eye-movement data at scale for large-scale sentiment tasks?
- Question: provide concrete real-world application examples (e.g., product reviews, social media monitoring).
- Additional comment: include more details on the eye-tracking technology and data processing.
- Additional comment: add visualizations of eye-movement data to illustrate cognitive features.
- Additional comment: discuss potential applications beyond sentiment analysis, like summarization or QA.
