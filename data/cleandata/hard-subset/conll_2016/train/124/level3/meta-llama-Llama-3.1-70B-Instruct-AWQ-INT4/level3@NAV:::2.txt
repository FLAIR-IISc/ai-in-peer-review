**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Leveraging Cognitive Features for Sentiment Analysis" proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often struggle with nuanced texts, such as those containing sarcasm, irony, or implicit sentiment. By augmenting traditional features with cognitive features, they demonstrate improved performance on two datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of cognitive features**: The authors propose a new set of features derived from eye-movement patterns, which capture the cognitive processes involved in reading and understanding text.
2. **Improved sentiment analysis performance**: The authors demonstrate that their approach improves sentiment analysis performance on two datasets, particularly for texts with complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.
3. **Feature significance analysis**: The authors perform a feature significance analysis, which shows that cognitive features are effective in capturing complex sentiment nuances.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to sentiment analysis, which leverages cognitive features to improve performance.
2. **Improved performance**: The authors demonstrate improved performance on two datasets, particularly for texts with complex constructs.
3. **Feature significance analysis**: The authors perform a thorough feature significance analysis, which provides insights into the effectiveness of cognitive features.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dataset limitations**: The authors use two datasets that conflate domains (movie reviews and tweets), which may limit the generalizability of their approach.
2. **Overlapping snippets**: The authors do not clarify whether there are overlapping snippets between training and test sets in datasets 1 and 2, which may affect the performance of their approach.
3. **Comparison to state-of-the-art**: The authors' comparison to the state-of-the-art (Pang & Lee) is limited, as their results are lower relative to this baseline.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify whether there are overlapping snippets between training and test sets in datasets 1 and 2?
2. How do you plan to address the dataset limitations and conflation of domains in future work?
3. Can you provide more details on the feature significance analysis and how you selected the top-ranked features?

**Additional Comments**

The authors' use of bold formatting is excessive and distracting. The paper could benefit from a more concise and clear writing style.
