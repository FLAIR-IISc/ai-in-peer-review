This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of readers. The authors claim that this is the first use of such features in NLP, and their results show a significant improvement in accuracy over traditional feature sets.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the motivation, methodology, and results. The idea of using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is interesting and could inspire work on other NLP tasks.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. The proposal of a novel feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns.
2. The demonstration of the effectiveness of this feature set in improving the accuracy of sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
3. The introduction of a new approach to sentiment analysis that goes beyond traditional textual features.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The novelty of the approach: The use of cognitive features for sentiment analysis is a new and innovative idea that has the potential to improve the accuracy of sentiment analysis.
2. The thorough evaluation: The authors have evaluated their approach on two datasets and have shown significant improvements in accuracy over traditional feature sets.
3. The clarity of the explanation: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the methodology and results.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

1. The motivation for using cognitive features is weak and unclear beyond reading ease. The authors could have provided more explanation on why cognitive features are relevant to sentiment analysis.
2. The improvement from adding cognitive features is marginal (Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr). The authors could have discussed the significance of this improvement and how it relates to the overall performance of the system.
3. The feasibility discussion (especially example in Section 7.2) is unconvincing and does not demonstrate a clear benefit. The authors could have provided more concrete examples of how their approach could be applied in real-world scenarios.

Questions to authors:

1. Can you provide more explanation on why cognitive features are relevant to sentiment analysis beyond reading ease?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of collecting eye-movement data for large-scale sentiment analysis tasks?
3. Can you provide more concrete examples of how your approach could be applied in real-world scenarios, such as product review analysis or social media monitoring?

Additional comments:

* The authors could have provided more details on the eye-tracking technology used to collect the data and how it was processed.
* The paper could have benefited from more visualizations of the eye-movement data to help illustrate the concept of cognitive features.
* The authors could have discussed the potential applications of their approach beyond sentiment analysis, such as in text summarization or question answering.
