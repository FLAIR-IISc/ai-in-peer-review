This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating eye-movement (cognitive) features derived from human annotators' gaze data. The authors claim that this is the first time such features have been used in NLP tasks.

**Summary of the Paper:**

The paper addresses the challenges of sentiment analysis, particularly in handling nuances at lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic levels. The authors propose augmenting traditional sentiment features with cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. They use two publicly available datasets, which contain text snippets with complex constructs like sarcasm and irony. The authors evaluate the effectiveness of their approach using various classification techniques and feature combinations, demonstrating a significant improvement in sentiment polarity detection over traditional feature sets.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Novel use of cognitive features**: The authors propose using eye-movement features derived from human annotators' gaze data for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection, which is a new approach in NLP.
2. **Improved sentiment polarity detection**: The authors demonstrate a significant improvement in sentiment polarity detection using their enhanced feature set, particularly for text with complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.
3. **Feature significance analysis**: The authors perform a feature significance analysis, which shows that cognitive features are effective in capturing complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.

**Strengths:**

1. **Well-written and easy to follow**: The paper is well-organized and easy to understand, making it accessible to a broad audience.
2. **Interesting idea**: The use of cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection is an innovative approach that could inspire work on other NLP tasks.
3. **Thorough evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach using various classification techniques and feature combinations, demonstrating the effectiveness of their enhanced feature set.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Weak motivation**: The authors' motivation for why cognitive features should help detect sentiment polarity is unclear and lacks a strong theoretical foundation.
2. **Marginal improvement**: The improvement from adding cognitive features is marginal, particularly when compared to the Sn+Sr feature combination.
3. **Unconvincing feasibility discussion**: The authors' discussion on the feasibility of their approach, particularly the example in Section 7.2, is unconvincing and does not demonstrate a clear benefit.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of why cognitive features should help detect sentiment polarity, and what specific aspects of cognitive processing are being captured by these features?
2. How do you plan to address the marginal improvement from adding cognitive features, and what additional experiments or analyses would you conduct to further demonstrate the effectiveness of your approach?
3. Can you provide more concrete examples or scenarios where your approach would be particularly useful or beneficial, and how it would be integrated into existing NLP systems or applications?
