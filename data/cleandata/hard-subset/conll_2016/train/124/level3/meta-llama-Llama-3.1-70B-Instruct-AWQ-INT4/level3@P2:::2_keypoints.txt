- Summary: Proposes combining cognitive features from annotators' eye-movement with traditional sentiment features, showing improved polarity detection on two datasets.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces eye-tracking-derived cognitive features as novel inputs for sentiment analysis.  
- Contribution 2: Provides a comprehensive feature set combining traditional sentiment, sarcasm/thwarting, and cognitive (eye-tracking) features.  
- Contribution 3: Evaluates the approach on two publicly available nuanced datasets (implicit sentiment, sarcasm, thwarting) and reports effectiveness.  
- Strength 1: Innovative use of eye-tracking as cognitive proxies to capture nuanced sentiment processing.  
- Strength 2: Clear, detailed discussion and comparison of the proposed feature set with traditional methods.  
- Strength 3: Feasibility discussion included (mobile eye-trackers, possible applications in e-commerce and online learning).  
- Weakness 1: Datasets conflate domains (movie reviews and tweets), which may affect training and interpretability.  
- Weakness 2: Reported results appear lower than Pang & Lee state-of-the-art; needs explanation and context.  
- Weakness 3: Unclear whether training and test sets contain overlapping snippets; clarification required.  
- Question 1: How was eye-tracking data preprocessed (noise filtering, missing-value handling)?  
- Question 2: How will the authors address domain conflation to improve robustness?  
- Question 3: Can the authors explain the discrepancy between their results and Pang & Lee's state-of-the-art?
