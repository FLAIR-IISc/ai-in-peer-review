- Summary: Proposes using cognitive features from readers' eye-movement to improve sentiment analysis, reporting up to 3.7% and 9.3% gains on two datasets.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces eye-movement–based cognitive features as a novel input for sentiment models.  
- Contribution 2: Shows improved polarity detection on texts with lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic subtleties.  
- Contribution 3: Discusses feasibility of data collection using mobile/non-intrusive eye-trackers.  
- Strength 1: Innovative and fresh approach to sentiment analysis.  
- Strength 2: Clear, helpful comparison and discussion of different feature sets.  
- Strength 3: Thorough, well-reasoned discussion of practical feasibility.  
- Weakness 1: Some reported results (Table 2) are lower than prior state of the art (Pang & Lee).  
- Weakness 2: Unclear whether training and test sets contain overlapping snippets in datasets 1 and 2.  
- Weakness 3: Excessive and distracting use of bold formatting in the paper.  
- Question 1: Are there overlapping snippets between training and test sets in datasets 1 and 2?  
- Question 2: How will the authors address potential overlap between training and test sets?  
- Question 3: How exactly will eye-tracking data be collected from mobile devices non-intrusively?  
- Question 4: How will the approach be evaluated across different domains (e.g., movie reviews, tweets)?
