**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating eye-movement (cognitive) features into traditional linguistic features. The authors argue that cognitive features can capture linguistic nuances better than traditional features alone. They conduct experiments on two datasets, demonstrating an improvement in sentiment polarity detection accuracy by up to 9.3% over state-of-the-art systems.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Introduction of cognitive features for sentiment analysis**: The authors propose the use of eye-movement features, derived from human annotators' gaze patterns, to improve sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. This is a novel contribution to the field of NLP.
2. **Experimental evaluation on two datasets**: The authors conduct experiments on two publicly available datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach in improving sentiment polarity detection accuracy.
3. **Feature significance analysis**: The authors perform a feature significance analysis, showing that cognitive features are indeed useful in capturing complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to sentiment analysis, incorporating cognitive features into traditional linguistic features.
2. **Well-written and easy to follow**: The paper is well-organized and easy to understand, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.
3. **Potential for inspiring further work**: The idea of using cognitive features for NLP tasks is interesting and could inspire further research in this area.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Weak motivation for cognitive features**: The authors' motivation for why cognitive features should help detect sentiment polarity is unclear and lacks strong theoretical backing.
2. **Marginal improvement**: The improvement in accuracy from adding cognitive features is marginal, which may not be significant enough to justify the added complexity of incorporating gaze data.
3. **Unconvincing feasibility discussion**: The authors' discussion on the feasibility of their approach, particularly the example in Section 7.2, is unconvincing and does not demonstrate a clear benefit.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more theoretical backing for why cognitive features should help detect sentiment polarity?
2. How do you plan to address the marginal improvement in accuracy from adding cognitive features?
3. Can you provide more convincing examples or scenarios where the incorporation of gaze data would be beneficial and feasible?
