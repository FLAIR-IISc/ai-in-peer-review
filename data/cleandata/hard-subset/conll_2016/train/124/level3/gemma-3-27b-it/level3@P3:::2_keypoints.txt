- Novel approach: Augments sentiment analysis with cognitive features from eye-tracking to capture readers' nuanced sentiment perception, improving F-score on two datasets.
- Contribution — Eye-tracking integration: Demonstrates feasibility and potential benefits of using gaze-derived features in sentiment pipelines.
- Contribution — Feature engineering: Provides a comprehensive, well-categorized set of traditional, sarcasm/thwarting, and novel gaze-based features.
- Contribution — Empirical validation: Shows initial experimental improvements on two datasets, indicating promise for complex linguistic constructs.
- Strength — Novelty: Applying eye-tracking as proxies for cognitive processes in sentiment classification is a genuinely new direction.
- Strength — Detailed features & comparison: Thorough feature description, comparative analyses, and PCA-based dimensionality reduction strengthen the study.
- Strength — Feasibility discussion: Authors acknowledge practical limitations and discuss growing availability of mobile eye-trackers and application scenarios.
- Strength — Error analysis: Includes an error analysis noting potential information loss from aggregating individual gaze patterns.
- Strength — Statistical testing: Uses McNemar’s test to confirm significance of F-score improvements.
- Weakness — Domain conflation: Mixing movie reviews and tweets may hurt generalization and interpretability; domain-specific splits are recommended.
- Weakness — Low baselines: Relatively low baseline F-scores (especially Dataset 1) raise concerns; stronger baselines (e.g., fine-tuned pre-trained models) are needed.
- Weakness — Overlap risk: Paper doesn’t clarify possible overlapping snippets between training and test sets, which could inflate results.
- Weakness — Presentation: Excessive use of bold text is distracting and reduces readability.
- Question — Dataset overlap: Is there any partial or full overlap between train and test snippets in Datasets 1 and 2?
- Question — Domain separation: Have authors tried separate models for movie reviews vs. tweets, or plan to explore this?
- Question — Stronger baselines: Can results be compared against fine-tuned pre-trained language models (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa)?
- Question — Individual gaze data: Have authors explored leveraging participant-specific gaze patterns or multi-instance approaches instead of aggregating?
- Additional — Table notation: Clarify P, R, F notation in Table 2 caption.
- Additional — Abbreviations & figures: Remove unnecessary abbreviations and improve figure labels/annotations for clarity.
