- Novel idea: Augmenting sentiment analysis with cognitive features from eye-tracking; claims first use for sentiment/sarcasm and reports up to 9.3% F-score gains on two datasets.
- Primary contribution: Integration of eye-tracking into a sentiment pipeline and creation of a dataset of cognitive features alongside benchmarks.
- Strong feature engineering: Comprehensive set of traditional, sarcasm-related, cognitive, and readability features.
- Good presentation: Clear writing, organization, and a thorough related-work survey in sentiment and eye-tracking literature.
- Weak theoretical grounding: The link between specific eye-movement patterns (regressions, fixations) and sentiment/sarcasm understanding is not convincingly established.
- Marginal empirical gains: Cognitive features yield only modest improvements (0.6–1.5% F-score over Sn+Sr), questioning impact relative to added complexity.
- Questionable feasibility: Practical deployment depends on future inexpensive mobile eye-trackers and optimistic user-consent assumptions, making applications unconvincing.
- Limited analysis: Paper lacks a detailed error analysis to show what errors cognitive features actually help fix.
- Questions for authors: Clarify hypothesized cognitive processes behind improvements, sensitivity to eye-tracker quality, and provide a detailed error breakdown.
