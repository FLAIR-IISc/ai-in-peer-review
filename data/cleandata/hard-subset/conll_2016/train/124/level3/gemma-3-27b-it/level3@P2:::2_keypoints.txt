- Proposes augmenting sentiment analysis with cognitive features from eye-tracking, reporting up to 9.3% F-score improvement.
- Primary contribution: novel integration of eye-tracking-derived cognitive features into a sentiment pipeline.
- Secondary contribution: thorough feature analysis showing cognitive features help handle irony and sarcasm.
- Strength: Novel idea with potential to improve accuracy on nuanced or complex sentiment cases.
- Strength: Comprehensive, well-categorized feature set with gaze features ranking highly in importance.
- Strength: Proactive feasibility discussion about mobile eye-trackers and user consent considerations.
- Strength: Thorough experiments across two datasets and multiple classifiers with statistical significance testing.
- Weakness: Combined movie reviews and tweets may limit generalizability and confound domain-specific effects.
- Weakness: Relatively low baseline F-scores raise questions; comparison with more recent SOTA methods is needed.
- Weakness: Possible overlap between training data and evaluation snippets could inflate performance; needs clarification.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—excessive bolding and repetitive text reduce readability.
- Question: Is there overlap between Pang & Lee / Sentiment140 training data and Datasets 1/2; if so, quantify it?
- Question: Would separating movie reviews and tweets change results; please provide per-domain outcomes.
- Question: How were cognitive features aggregated across participants in the saliency graph; how were individual differences handled?
- Additional: Include ablation studies to isolate contributions of individual cognitive features.
- Additional: Replace the unconventional “Gz” abbreviation with a clearer label for gaze features.
- Additional: Briefly address privacy implications of collecting and using eye-tracking data.
