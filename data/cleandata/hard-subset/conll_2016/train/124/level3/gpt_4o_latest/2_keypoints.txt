- Introduces eye-tracking–derived cognitive features as a proxy for cognitive load to improve sentiment analysis on complex constructs.  
- Proposes an augmented feature set combining traditional sentiment, sarcasm-related, and gaze-based cognitive features.  
- Reports F-score improvements up to 3.7% and 9.3% on two datasets when adding cognitive features.  
- Provides detailed feature significance analysis and rankings to show individual contributions.  
- Discusses feasibility and potential for real-world deployment using mobile eye-tracking technology.  
- Novelty: first use of eye-tracking cognitive features for sentiment, addressing sarcasm and implicit sentiment.  
- Clear and comprehensive feature analysis supported by statistical significance tests.  
- Robust experimental design: evaluated on two datasets and a held-out set, showing gains for complex constructs.  
- Feasibility/applicability discussion adds practical relevance given mobile eye-tracker advances.  
- Weakness: combined datasets from different domains (movies and tweets) may introduce noise and reduce interpretability.  
- Weakness: lower performance on Pang & Lee dataset compared to state-of-the-art raises competitiveness concerns for simpler text.  
- Weakness: unclear whether training and testing datasets overlap (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment140), risking inflated results.  
- Weakness: excessive bold formatting detracts from readability and professionalism.  
- Question: was dataset overlap between training and testing (particularly Pang & Lee and Sentiment140) checked and ruled out?  
- Question: have you evaluated domain-separated datasets to understand domain-specific impact of cognitive features?  
- Question: what is the computational cost and scalability of extracting gaze-based features for large-scale, real-world use?  
- Additional: promising direction but needs improved dataset handling and benchmarking against state-of-the-art to strengthen contributions.  
- Additional: feasibility section is valuable but practical issues like user consent and data privacy should be explored further.
