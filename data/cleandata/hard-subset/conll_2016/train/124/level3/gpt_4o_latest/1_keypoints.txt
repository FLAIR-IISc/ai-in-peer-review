- Proposes novel use of gaze-based cognitive features from eye-tracking for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.  
- Shows empirical improvement in F-score (up to 3.7% and 9.3% on two datasets) when combining cognitive and linguistic features.  
- Performs feature importance analysis highlighting gaze-based features' contribution, especially for irony and sarcasm.  
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology, experiments, and results.  
- The idea of integrating eye-tracking cognitive features into NLP is innovative and could inspire follow-up work.  
- Provides extensive experimental evidence, statistical tests, and feature analyses to support claims.  
- Fails to convincingly justify why cognitive features are necessary or should outperform deep learning representations.  
- Reported performance gains are modest relative to the cost and complexity of collecting eye-tracking data.  
- Feasibility and scalability concerns: mobile eye-tracker use cases are speculative and lack implementation details.  
- Asks for stronger theoretical justification for cognitive features over other approaches.  
- Questions how the approach can scale to large, real-world datasets given data collection challenges.  
- Suggests exploring alternatives to simulate or approximate cognitive features without hardware.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing motivation, scalability, and real-world applicability.
