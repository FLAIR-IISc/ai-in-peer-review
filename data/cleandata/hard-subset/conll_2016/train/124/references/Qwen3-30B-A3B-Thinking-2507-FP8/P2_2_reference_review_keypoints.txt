- Novel approach: uses eye-tracking–derived cognitive features to improve sentiment analysis, especially for complex cases like sarcasm and irony.
- Data collection: eye-tracking data gathered from annotators while reading sentiment-bearing texts and combined with traditional features.
- Empirical gains: adding cognitive features yields notable improvements, e.g., 6.1% F-score boost for ironic texts vs. 2.1% for simple texts.
- Contribution 1: first integration of eye-tracking cognitive features into sentiment analysis.
- Contribution 2: rigorous evaluation on two datasets specifically containing challenging sentiment cases.
- Contribution 3: quantification that cognitive features disproportionately help complex sentiment.
- Strength 1: genuinely novel and promising direction with detailed description of cognitive features for reproducibility.
- Strength 2: strong evaluation setup using datasets with many sarcastic/ironic examples and meaningful baselines.
- Strength 3: ablation study showing cognitive features specifically improve performance on complex cases.
- Strength 4: thorough feature engineering, including gaze-saliency graph features.
- Weakness 1: no comparison with state-of-the-art deep learning/transformer models, limiting assessment of competitiveness.
- Weakness 2: practicality and feasibility of deploying eye-tracking in real-world applications are speculative and under-evidenced.
- Weakness 3: potential data leakage because annotators who provided labels also provided eye-tracking data.
- Weakness 4: lacks analysis explaining why cognitive features help or which specific cognitive mechanisms are at work.
- Weakness 5: relatively small datasets and few annotators (5–7), limiting generalizability and robustness.
- Question 1: how do you mitigate the potential data leakage between labels and eye-tracking signals?
- Question 2: why no comparison with transformer-based or other deep learning state-of-the-art models like BERT?
- Question 3: can you provide concrete feasibility details (cost, implementation, adoption) for real-world deployment?
- Question 4: clarify how the “carefully curated held-out dataset” was created and its relation to Dataset 1.
- Overall verdict: promising and innovative but hampered by lack of SOTA comparisons and practical feasibility concerns; needs these addressed for top-conference acceptance.
