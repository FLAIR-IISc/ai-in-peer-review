- Proposes using eye-tracking–derived cognitive features to improve sentiment analysis, especially for sarcasm and irony.  
- Reports a 6.1% relative F-score improvement on irony-containing texts when cognitive features are added.  
- Provides curated datasets with eye-tracking data specifically for sentiment analysis of complex linguistic constructs.  
- Novel conceptual approach linking cognitive processes (eye movements) to NLP for handling complex sentiment phenomena.  
- Strong empirical improvement on challenging cases, supported by statistical significance (McNemar test, p=0.0001).  
- Thorough experimental design validating dataset difficulty and systematically testing feature combinations with a held-out set.  
- Discusses practical feasibility and potential applications, noting emergence of mobile eye-trackers.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why specific eye-movement features should correlate with sentiment complexity.  
- Potential data leakage: eye-tracking was recorded during annotation, so features may reflect annotation behavior, not text properties.  
- Does not compare against state-of-the-art deep learning baselines (CNNs/RNNs), limiting evaluation relevance.  
- Small participant pool (7 and 5) raises concerns about generalizability across populations and contexts.  
- No qualitative analysis or concrete examples showing how cognitive features corrected model predictions on sarcastic/ironic texts.  
- Asks authors to clarify handling of potential data leakage and whether eye-tracking was recorded before/after labeling.  
- Requests explanation for omission of deep learning baselines and comparisons to modern approaches.  
- Wants specific examples demonstrating how particular cognitive features (e.g., RSF, LREG) distinguish sarcasm.  
- Seeks details on hardware requirements and feasibility of real-world deployment without specialized eye-trackers.  
- Recommends clarifying annotation timing, adding qualitative examples, and including a deep learning comparison to strengthen the paper.
