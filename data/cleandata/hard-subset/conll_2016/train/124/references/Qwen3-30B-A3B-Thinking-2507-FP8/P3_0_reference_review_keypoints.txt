- Novel approach: Incorporates cognitive features from eye-movement patterns into sentiment analysis, reporting up to 9.3% overall accuracy gain and 6.1% gain on irony/sarcasm cases.
- Contribution 1: First application of eye-tracking-derived cognitive features as input features for sentiment analysis.
- Contribution 2: Empirical validation on two datasets curated for complex sentiment expressions (sarcasm, irony, thwarted expectations).
- Contribution 3: Detailed feature-significance analysis showing cognitive features rank highly among top features.
- Strength 1: Genuinely novel core idea opening a new research direction linking psycholinguistics and sentiment analysis.
- Strength 2: Carefully chosen challenging datasets with low baseline F-scores, validating the focus on complex cases.
- Strength 3: Strong empirical improvements specifically for irony/sarcasm, supporting the paper’s hypothesis.
- Strength 4: Thorough chi-squared feature analysis showing many cognitive features among top-ranked features.
- Weakness 1: Lacks strong theoretical justification for why eye-movements should correlate with sentiment nuances like sarcasm/irony.
- Weakness 2: Potential circularity: eye-tracking data collected from annotators performing sentiment labelling, which may bias features.
- Weakness 3: Omits comparisons to state-of-the-art deep learning methods (CNNs/RNNs), weakening claims of superiority.
- Weakness 4: Feasibility concerns for scaling due to reliance on eye-tracking hardware, privacy, and adoption barriers; mobile eye-tracker discussion is speculative.
- Weakness 5: Complex gaze features (gaze-saliency graphs) are insufficiently explained and motivation for chosen graph metrics is unclear.
- Question 1: Provide stronger, specific theoretical evidence linking eye-movement patterns to sentiment processing, especially for sarcasm/irony.
- Question 2: How do you address circularity—would features generalize to non-annotator readers?
- Question 3: How would cognitive features integrate with or improve deep learning architectures for sentiment analysis?
- Question 4: What concrete steps would make large-scale mobile eye-tracking data collection feasible and practical?
- Question 5: Explain construction of gaze-saliency graphs and why graph properties (edge density, weighted degrees) should reflect sentiment complexity.
- Overall assessment: Promising and novel with encouraging results for complex cases, but undermined by theoretical, methodological, and comparison shortcomings.
- Recommendation: Major Revision required to address theory, circularity, SOTA comparisons, and feasibility/explanation of complex features.
- Additional comment: Authors should provide a concrete feasibility pathway for deployment and clearer, more transparent feature-engineering descriptions (esp. section 4.5).
