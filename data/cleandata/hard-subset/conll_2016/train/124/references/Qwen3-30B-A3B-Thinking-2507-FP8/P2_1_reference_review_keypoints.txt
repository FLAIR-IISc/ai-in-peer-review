- Proposal: use eye-tracking-derived cognitive features to improve sentiment analysis, focusing on sarcasm/irony; datasets: 994 and 1059 snippets.  
- Features: fixation duration, saccade patterns, regression counts, gaze-saliency graphs combined with standard sentiment features.  
- Results: adding cognitive features yields +3.7% and +9.3% accuracy on Dataset 1 and 2, and +6.1% for complex cases like irony.  
- Contribution: novel integration of cognitive (eye-movement) features into sentiment analysis, a first for sarcasm/irony detection in NLP.  
- Contribution: systematic extraction and rigorous evaluation of cognitive features with feature significance analysis.  
- Contribution: demonstration that cognitive features significantly improve performance on complex sentiment constructs.  
- Strength: genuinely novel application of eye-tracking from psycholinguistics to sentiment/sarcasm detection.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation with larger gains on challenging (sarcastic/ironic) texts.  
- Strength: thorough feature-significance analysis showing many top features are gaze-based.  
- Strength: clear discussion of feasibility, limitations, and potential real-world applications.  
- Weakness: no comparison to state-of-the-art deep learning methods, leaving competitiveness unclear.  
- Weakness: practicality/scalability concerns—requiring eye-tracking data is likely infeasible for most real-world systems.  
- Weakness: insufficient theoretical grounding linking specific eye-movement patterns to sentiment/cognitive processes.  
- Weakness: potential confound—features may reflect annotators’ decision process rather than generalizable signals.  
- Weakness: relatively small datasets limit statistical power and generalizability of findings.  
- Questions: how would cognitive features be integrated into deep learning (e.g., input features or attention) and how would performance compare to SOTA?  
- Questions: provide concrete examples where cognitive features enabled correct sarcasm/irony detection and explain why traditional features failed.  
- Recommendation: weak accept—novel and promising but needs deep-learning comparisons and stronger theoretical/practical justification.
