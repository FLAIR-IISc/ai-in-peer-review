- Proposal: Introduces eye-tracking–derived cognitive features for sentiment analysis, claimed as a novel approach to capture nuances like sarcasm and irony.

- Contribution: Shows up to 9.3% accuracy improvement when combining cognitive features with traditional sentiment and sarcasm features.

- Contribution: Demonstrates cognitive features specifically improving performance on complex constructs (e.g., 6.1% gain on irony/sarcasm).

- Strength: Novel solution to handling sarcasm/irony by leveraging human cognitive signals, with convincing targeted improvements.

- Strength: Rigorous evaluation across multiple datasets with statistical tests (McNemar p=0.0001) and feature significance analysis.

- Strength: Careful dataset choice including challenging sarcastic/ironic and sentiment-heavy corpora that validate experimental difficulty.

- Strength: Comprehensive feature engineering and grounding in cognitive science literature for the cognitive features.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why specific eye-tracking features correlate with sentiment complexity (features appear arbitrary).

- Weakness: Practical feasibility is questionable because eye-tracking data collection is expensive and consumer-grade trackers may be insufficient.

- Weakness: No comparison to mainstream deep learning methods (CNNs/RNNs), weakening claims of improvement over state of the art.

- Weakness: Cognitive features yield marginal overall gains (0.6–1.5%) compared to larger gains from sarcasm features, overstating their impact.

- Weakness: No detailed error analysis identifying which cognitive features or hardware limitations cause failures.

- Question: How do you separate cognitive signals of sentiment processing from general reading difficulty or complex grammar effects?

- Question: Can you demonstrate that cognitive features work with consumer-grade mobile eye-trackers (lower sampling rates/accuracy)?

- Question: Why not develop a single theoretically grounded cognitive feature for interpretability and effectiveness instead of many combined features?

- Overall: Interesting and promising contribution but claims are overstated; acceptance recommended with minor revisions addressing feasibility, theoretical grounding, comparisons to deep learning, and clearer attribution of feature importance.
