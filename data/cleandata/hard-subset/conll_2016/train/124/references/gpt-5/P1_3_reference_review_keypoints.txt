- Paper augments text-based sentiment analysis with cognitive features from eye-tracking and shows modest F1 gains, larger on sarcastic/ironic texts.
- Contribution: empirical finding that gaze features modestly improve SA (overall +0.6/+1.5 F1; +4.2 on complex subset).
- Contribution: introduces a reusable suite of gaze-derived features, including novel saliency-graph degree metrics.
- Contribution: cross-dataset evaluation with feature-importance analysis showing gaze features among top predictors.
- Strength: Targets hard semantic/pragmatic phenomena (sarcasm/irony) where textual features struggle, with held-out analysis showing larger gains.
- Strength: Clear ablation across feature sets and classifiers illustrating incremental value of gaze features.
- Strength: Novel gaze-saliency graph features plausibly grounded in psycholinguistic theory.
- Strength: Uses public datasets, statistical tests, and shows many gaze features rank highly in D1.
- Strength: Controls for confounds via readability and length features.
- Weakness: Requires gaze at test time; feasibility and realistic deployment scenarios (single reader, noisy devices) are not evaluated.
- Weakness: Overall gains are modest and gaze-only models perform poorly, limiting practical value.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to contemporary neural SA baselines (CNN/RNN/transformers).
- Weakness: Possible methodological/reporting issues (e.g., Table 2 anomaly where Sn+Gz equals Gz-only on D2).
- Weakness: Unclear training/data provenance for the “Lexical polarity (LP)” feature and potential domain overlap.
- Weakness: No clear normalization strategy across different eye-trackers/participants; device heterogeneity not addressed.
- Weakness: Cross-validation and averaging per-reader may mask inter-reader variance; reader effects may be uncontrolled.
- Weakness: Limited qualitative/error analysis and lack of concrete examples showing where gaze helps or fails.
- Question: What minimal gaze signal is needed at test time (single reader vs. averaged multiple; single pass vs. full reading)?
- Question: How are gaze measures normalized across readers/sessions/devices, and do results vary with normalization?
- Question: On what data is the LP classifier trained and does it overlap with D1/D2; can LP be ablated?
- Question: Are McNemar tests and significance reported over aggregated CV folds; can confidence intervals or repeated-runs be reported?
- Question: Please verify the D2 SVM Sn+Gz scores and explain why adding Sn to Gz does not improve SVM on D2.
- Question: How consistent are gaze-derived predictions across annotators; do features show inter-reader reliability or reading-speed effects?
- Question: Can the authors include modern transformer-based SA baselines to contextualize gaze contribution?
- Question: If gaze is unavailable at test time, can a text-only student model be distilled from a gaze-augmented teacher; any preliminary evidence?
