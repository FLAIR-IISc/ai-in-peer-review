- Paper explores using eye-tracking–derived cognitive features to improve sentiment analysis, especially for sarcasm and irony.
- Authors augment traditional sentiment features with eye-movement patterns and show improved accuracy on two datasets.
- Contribution: Introduces a novel set of eye-tracking–based features integrated into a standard sentiment classification pipeline.
- Contribution: Empirically demonstrates statistically significant accuracy gains, notably on nuanced sentiment cases.
- Contribution: Evaluates approach on two public eye-tracking datasets and highlights their difficulty for standard SA methods.
- Strength: Idea is novel and could substantially improve handling of subtle expressions if scalable.
- Strength: Experimental setup is sound (10-fold CV, multiple classifiers, held-out validation) and compares against baselines.
- Strength: Combines traditional, sarcasm/thwarting, and cognitive features, with clear feature explanations.
- Strength: Reports statistical significance (McNemar test) validating observed improvements.
- Strength: Authors discuss feasibility and practical considerations of collecting eye-tracking data.
- Weakness: Absolute improvement (3–9% F-score) is modest, questioning cost-effectiveness of eye-tracking data collection.
- Weakness: Datasets are small with moderate inter-annotator agreement, raising generalizability concerns.
- Weakness: Cognitive features (e.g., gaze-saliency graphs) are complex; paper lacks discussion of computational cost and scalability.
- Weakness: Error analysis is limited; unclear where cognitive features most help in correcting errors.
- Weakness: Unclear consistency of cognitive features across annotators and potential information loss from aggregation.
- Question: Request for a detailed cost-benefit analysis including acquisition, preprocessing, and computational costs.
- Question: How sensitive are results to eye-tracker quality (sampling rate, accuracy) and were dataset differences observed?
- Question: Provide detailed error analysis showing examples where cognitive features corrected misclassifications.
- Question: Discuss inter-annotator variability in eye-tracking data and impact on aggregated features.
- Question: Have alternative methods for deriving cognitive features (different graphs or aggregations) been tried?
- Additional: Paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and comprehensively referenced.
- Additional: Related work discussion is appropriate.
- Overall assessment: Promising, novel approach with modest gains and some concerns; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
