- Summary: The paper integrates eye-tracking–derived cognitive features into sentiment analysis and shows improved accuracy on two datasets, especially for sarcasm/irony.

- Contribution — Novel Feature Integration: Introduces the use of eye-tracking features in a standard sentiment pipeline, an unexplored application area.

- Contribution — Empirical Validation: Provides experiments on two datasets showing statistically significant performance gains when adding cognitive features.

- Contribution — Dataset Utilization: Uses complex datasets containing sarcasm and irony to demonstrate the value of cognitive features.

- Strength: Strong motivation and clear hypothesis linking eye movements to cognitive processing in sentiment tasks.

- Strength: Comprehensive feature engineering including basic gaze metrics and more complex graph-based features.

- Strength: Solid experimental setup with baselines, held-out tests, and significance testing.

- Strength: Demonstrated (modest but significant) improvements, especially on complex sentences involving sarcasm/irony.

- Strength: Feasibility discussion addressing data-collection challenges and potential solutions like mobile eye-tracking.

- Weakness: Limited novelty in methodology since individual eye-tracking features are established; novelty is primarily in application.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why specific cognitive features help (lack of deeper cognitive interpretation).

- Weakness: Results depend on annotation and eye-tracking quality; more discussion of annotator bias and agreement needed.

- Weakness: Performance gains are modest; the paper should better contextualize practical significance.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art deep learning approaches and lacking discussion of integration with such models.

- Question: Please elaborate on the cognitive mechanisms linking specific gaze patterns to sarcasm/irony detection with concrete examples.

- Question: How might annotator interpretation variability affect eye-tracking signals and model performance; was inter-annotator reasoning analyzed?

- Question: In which real-world scenarios are the modest performance gains likely to be practically valuable?

- Question: Have you explored integrating cognitive features into deep learning architectures and what challenges/opportunities do you anticipate?

- Additional: Improve table and reference formatting for readability.

- Additional: Feasibility section is strong and proactively addresses key concerns.

- Overall assessment: Solid, novel application with encouraging results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
