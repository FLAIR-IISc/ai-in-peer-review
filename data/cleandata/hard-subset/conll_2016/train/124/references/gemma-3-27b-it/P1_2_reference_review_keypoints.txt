- Novel feature engineering: integrates cognitive features from eye-tracking into sentiment analysis.
- Empirical validation: shows effectiveness of cognitive features on challenging sarcasm/irony datasets.
- Impact on complex constructs: cognitive features improve performance specifically on sarcasm and irony.

- Novelty of approach: combining eye-tracking with SA is an unexplored, compelling idea.
- Rigorous experimental setup: uses cross-validation, baselines, and two datasets for generalization.
- Statistically significant results: F-score improvements are reported as significant.
- Detailed feature description: comprehensive listing of traditional and cognitive features, including gaze-saliency graphs.
- Feasibility discussion: authors acknowledge eye-tracking limitations and discuss mobile eye-tracker prospects.

- Limited generalizability of eye-tracking setup: differing tracker qualities across datasets may introduce variability.
- Lack of ablation study detail: missing analysis of individual cognitive feature/group contributions.
- Feature redundancy concerns: many features risk redundancy and overfitting despite PCA use.
- Limited comparison to state-of-the-art: no comparisons to recent deep learning (CNN/RNN) SA models.
- Subjectivity in eye-tracking interpretation: linking eye movements to cognitive processes could have alternative explanations.

- Eye-tracker quality: how did differing sampling rates/accuracy affect derived features and were discrepancies mitigated?
- Ablation study: can you show per-feature/group contributions (basic gaze vs. graph-based)?
- Feature selection: why select 500 principal components and were alternative dimensionality reduction methods considered?
- Deep learning comparison: rationale for excluding DL models and potential for integrating cognitive features into them?
- Cognitive interpretation: can alternative interpretations of eye-movement patterns be discussed?

- Paper is well-written and clearly structured.
- Add a visualization of the gaze-saliency graph for a representative sentence.
- Fix formatting inconsistencies in table captions (e.g., use of "˙" instead of ".").
