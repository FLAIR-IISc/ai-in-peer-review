- Novelty: Introduces eye-tracking–derived cognitive features into sentiment analysis pipelines, a novel application beyond psycholinguistic studies.  
- Empirical gains: Augmenting traditional features with cognitive signals yields measurable accuracy improvements across SVM, NB, and NN on two datasets.  
- Feature engineering: Proposes both basic gaze metrics and a graph-based saccade representation to capture nuanced cognitive patterns.  
- Motivation: Clearly motivates the need for cognitive features to handle nuanced language like sarcasm and irony.  
- Feature set: Combines traditional sentiment features with comprehensive gaze-based features, showing thoughtful design.  
- Evaluation: Performs multiple-classifier experiments, statistical significance testing, and a held-out dataset experiment to support claims.  
- Feasibility: Discusses practical considerations and future applicability (e.g., mobile eye-trackers).  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and clearly structured.  
- Dataset generalizability: Used datasets are small (994 and 1059 instances), raising concerns about broader applicability.  
- Confounds in eye-tracking: Does not sufficiently address non-cognitive factors (reading habits, screen settings, calibration) that can affect gaze data.  
- Feature complexity: Complex gaze features risk reduced interpretability and potential overfitting; need clearer identification of the most impactful features.  
- Deep learning omission: Lacks comparisons or integration with state-of-the-art deep models (Transformers/BERT), limiting contextual relevance.  
- Annotation reliability: Moderate Fleiss kappa (0.62, 0.68) suggests label noise that may affect evaluation validity.  
- Generalizability question: Have you evaluated or planned evaluation on larger, more diverse datasets?  
- Confounding controls question: What steps were taken to control for individual and technical confounds in eye-tracking data?  
- Interpretability question: Can you explain why specific gaze features correlate with sentiment and identify consistent sarcasm/irony patterns?  
- Deep learning integration question: Have you tried or considered incorporating cognitive features into deep learning models, and what challenges are expected?  
- Annotation effect question: How might inter-annotator disagreement have influenced results, and how will this be mitigated?  
- References/formatting: Reference list is extensive but inconsistently formatted.  
- Promotional content: Mention of a patent ("Cogisen") appears promotional and should be removed or reframed.  
- Visualization suggestion: Add example gaze plots aligned with text snippets to illustrate observed patterns.  
- Recommendation: Overall solid and promising; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
