- Paper explores using eye-tracking–derived cognitive features to improve sentiment analysis, especially for sarcasm/irony.
- Hypothesis: eye-movement patterns reflect cognitive processes relevant to sentiment understanding.
- Experiments on two datasets show performance gains when adding cognitive features.

- Contribution: novel application of eye-tracking features to improve sentiment classification.
- Contribution: empirical validation that cognitive features boost accuracy on linguistically complex texts.
- Contribution: use and analysis of two datasets containing text and eye-tracking data as a resource.

- Strength: clear motivation linking psycholinguistics to sentiment analysis limitations.
- Strength: comprehensive feature set covering traditional, sarcasm/irony cues, cognitive features, and readability metrics.
- Strength: rigorous evaluation with two datasets, multiple classifiers, 10-fold CV, and statistical significance testing.
- Strength: feature significance analysis (Chi-squared) highlights informative gaze-based features.
- Strength: feasibility discussion addresses eye-tracking accessibility and practical applications.

- Weakness: limited generalizability due to small, domain-specific datasets (reviews and normalized tweets).
- Weakness: cognitive feature descriptions (gaze-graph features) are complex and reduce readability.
- Weakness: limited error analysis; lacks detailed cases where the system still fails (e.g., types of sarcasm).
- Weakness: averaging gaze data across participants may lose informative individual patterns.
- Weakness: no comparison to deep learning approaches and limited discussion of integrating cognitive features with DL.

- Question: provide example sentences where cognitive features significantly improved classification and explain why.
- Question: sensitivity of results to parameters in gaze-saliency graph construction and alternative graph methods tried.
- Question: anticipated challenges for integrating cognitive features into deep learning models.
- Question: elaborate on privacy concerns of collecting/using eye-tracking data and mitigation strategies.

- Additional: paper is well-written and organized.
- Additional: references are extensive and thorough.
- Additional: recommend adding a feature-summary table for quick reference.

- Overall assessment: solid, interesting contribution with empirical gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
