- Summary: Uses eye-tracking–derived cognitive features to augment sentiment analysis, claiming improved accuracy especially on sarcasm/irony.

- Contribution 1: Novel integration of eye-movement/cognitive signals directly into sentiment analysis models.

- Contribution 2: Empirical results showing statistically significant accuracy gains across classifiers and datasets, notably on complex constructs.

- Contribution 3: Advanced feature engineering from eye-tracking, including gaze-saliency graph features.

- Strength 1: Clear problem motivation linking cognitive signals to challenges in sentiment analysis.

- Strength 2: Rigorous experimental setup with multiple datasets, classifiers, and 10-fold cross-validation.

- Strength 3: Use of statistical significance testing (McNemar’s test) to support results.

- Strength 4: Comprehensive, well-organized description of feature categories and individual features.

- Strength 5: Creative exploration of gaze-saliency graphs as a cognitive feature source.

- Weakness 1: Limited practical generalizability due to the need for per-text eye-tracking data and potential annotator bias.

- Weakness 2: Large, potentially redundant feature set with insufficient analysis to identify the most critical cognitive features.

- Weakness 3: Lack of comparison to recent deep learning baselines limits assessment against state-of-the-art methods.

- Weakness 4: Absence of detailed qualitative error analysis showing where cognitive features fail or help.

- Weakness 5: Insufficient discussion of inter-participant variability and robustness of cognitive features to individual reading differences.

- Question 1: Can you apply rigorous feature selection (e.g., recursive feature elimination) to isolate impactful cognitive features and reduce dimensionality?

- Question 2: Will you explore integrating cognitive features into deep learning models, and what are the expected challenges/benefits?

- Question 3: How sensitive are cognitive features to individual reading patterns, and have normalization techniques been investigated?

- Question 4: Can you provide examples of instances where cognitive features did not improve classification and analyze causes?

- Question 5: Did differences in eye-tracker quality between Dataset 1 and Dataset 2 affect performance or feature effectiveness?

- Additional comment 1: Writing is clear and well-organized.

- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive.

- Additional comment 3: Recommend including visualizations of gaze-saliency graphs to clarify the feature representation.
