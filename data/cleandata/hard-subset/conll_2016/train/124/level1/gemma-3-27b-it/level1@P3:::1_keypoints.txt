- Proposes augmenting traditional sentiment features with cognitive features from eye-tracking to improve detection of nuanced sentiment (e.g., sarcasm/irony).  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements up to 9.3% F-score on two datasets, with feature significance analysis and a held-out experiment.  
- Novel contribution: first attempt to integrate eye-tracking data directly into a sentiment classification pipeline.  
- Significant engineering: rich set of gaze-based features including graph-based representations and reading-difficulty controls.  
- Rigorous evaluation using two datasets, feature significance testing, and McNemar statistical tests.  
- Strength: idea is novel and potentially impactful for handling subtle sentiment phenomena.  
- Strength: comprehensive and thoughtful feature engineering that captures diverse cognitive signals.  
- Strength: evaluation setup and analyses strengthen the validity of the reported gains.  
- Strength: authors acknowledge practical and ethical considerations (mobile trackers, consent).  
- Weakness: reliance on eye-tracking limits scalability, generalizability, and real-world adoption given cost/intrusiveness.  
- Weakness: many complex gaze features reduce interpretability and hinder theoretical understanding.  
- Weakness: baseline comparisons exclude modern deep-learning (transformer) SOTA, weakening assessment of true contribution.  
- Weakness: datasets are relatively small and annotation quality/agreements need deeper discussion.  
- Q: Why were SOTA deep learning sentiment models not used for baseline comparisons or feature integration?  
- Q: Can you provide a visual example of the gaze-saliency graph and detail how weighted degrees are computed?  
- Q: Have you considered textual proxies for cognitive effort (e.g., complexity, ambiguity) as alternatives to eye-tracking?  
- Q: Please provide detailed error analysis showing which sentence types benefit or suffer from cognitive features.  
- Suggestion: make the introduction more concise and focused.  
- Suggestion: streamline Section 4 (Enhanced Feature Set) for readability.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of eye-tracking technology (calibration errors, participant fatigue) and potential impact on results.
