- Proposes augmenting traditional sentiment features with cognitive features from readers' eye-movements, improving polarity detection especially for irony and sarcasm, evaluated on two datasets.
- Introduces novel cognitive features derived from eye-tracking gaze patterns that capture readers' cognitive processes.
- Combining traditional and cognitive features yields F-score gains of 3.7% and 9.3% on two datasets.
- Approach is particularly effective on complex constructs (irony/sarcasm) with a 6.1% relative improvement on held-out data.
- Novel perspective by incorporating eye-movement–based cognitive features into sentiment analysis.
- Demonstrates statistically significant performance gains, especially on complex text.
- Robust evaluation across two datasets including statistical significance tests and feature significance analysis.
- Relies on scarce eye-tracking data that may not be readily available for many applications.
- Gaze-feature extraction is complex and may require substantial computational resources.
- Potentially limited generalizability across languages and domains, needing adaptation and fine-tuning.
