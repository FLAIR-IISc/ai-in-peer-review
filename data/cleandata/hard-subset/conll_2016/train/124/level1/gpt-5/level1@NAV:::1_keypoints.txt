- Summary: Paper augments sentiment analysis with gaze-derived cognitive features from two eye-tracked datasets, reporting F-score gains (up to 3.7% and 9.3% in CV; 6.1% on held-out ironic texts) and showing gaze features among top predictors.

- Contribution: Empirical evidence that gaze signals improve sentiment classification on linguistically complex short texts, especially irony/sarcasm.

- Contribution: Novel, interpretable gaze feature set (including saliency-graph measures capturing saccades and regressions) with analysis of their importance.

- Contribution: Careful ablation and significance testing demonstrating incremental value of gaze beyond standard sentiment and sarcasm/thwarting features.

- Strength: Larger gains on hard cases (irony/sarcasm), with held-out analysis showing +6.1% F for ironic texts versus +2.1% for non-ironic.

- Strength: Interpretable, theory-motivated features (fixations, regressions, saccade-based graph degrees) with several ranked highly by chi-squared tests.

- Strength: Sound ablation and statistical testing (stepwise gains Sn→Sn+Sr→Sn+Sr+Gz and McNemar tests) supporting robustness.

- Strength: Results hold across datasets collected with different eye-trackers (Eyelink-1000 vs Tobii T120), suggesting some sensor robustness.

- Weakness: Practical deployment is unclear since method requires gaze at inference time and mobile eye-tracking proposals are speculative and unvalidated.

- Weakness: Limited baselines—no comparisons to strong modern text-only models (CNN/RNN/transformers) to show additive benefit over state-of-the-art text models.

- Weakness: Dataset scale and generalization concerns—small datasets (<1.1k each), within-dataset CV, no cross-domain/leave-device/leave-subject validation, and held-out set is a subset of Dataset 1.

- Weakness: Possible confounds from different hardware and annotation protocols; sparse details on hyperparameter tuning and PCA choices.

- Question: Can you report results with stronger text-only baselines (e.g., BiLSTM/CNN/transformer fine-tunes) and on cross-device/leave-user splits?

- Question: Can gaze be used only at training time (e.g., multitask or knowledge distillation) to produce deployable text-only models?

- Question: How sensitive are results to saliency-graph construction (weighting schemes) and to gaze aggregation across annotators?
