- Augments feature-based sentiment analysis with cognitive features from readers’ eye movements and evaluates their impact alongside traditional text features on two eye-tracking datasets.
- Claims up to 3.7% and 9.3% absolute F-score gains overall and larger gains on a sarcasm/irony subset; feature importance shows gaze features among the most informative.
- Primary contributions: introduction and systematic evaluation of gaze-based cognitive features, novel graph-based gaze-saliency features, evidence of gaze benefits for pragmatic phenomena (sarcasm/irony), and feature-significance analysis showing unique signal from gaze.

- Clear empirical finding that gaze features add predictive signal, especially on complex sarcasm/irony examples (e.g., +4.2 F-score for irony on held-out subset).
- Novel methodological contribution with comprehensive gaze feature set including graph-derived degree measures and edge-density metrics.
- Sensible controls for confounds from reading difficulty via readability and sentence-length features.
- Thorough comparisons across classifiers (SVM, NB, simple NN) and ablation studies that attribute gains to sarcasm features first and gaze second.
- Use of two publicly available datasets with different tracker qualities and content types, adding robustness to results.

- Limited practical applicability at inference time because the approach assumes availability of gaze data at prediction, and feasibility discussion is speculative without quantified costs/privacy analysis.
- Modest incremental gains from gaze over strong textual sarcasm/thwarting features on main datasets (+0.6 and +1.5 F1), with most gains driven by textual sarcasm features and the larger gains shown on a small (131-example) held-out subset.
- Limited external validity and generalization tests: no cross-dataset or domain transfer experiments and relatively small, specialized datasets (~1K sentences each).
- Incomplete methodological clarity and reproducibility details: some gaze feature definitions ambiguous, PCA and sarcasm-feature construction not fully specified, and unclear subject-level normalization and fold leakage controls.
- Baselines omit stronger contemporary models (dependency-based features, CNN/RNNs), limiting claims that gaze adds value over the best text-only systems.

- Inference-time scenario: do you expect gaze during prediction (real-time per-user) or training-with-gaze/testing-without, and can you show models trained with gaze but tested without (e.g., privileged information methods)?
- Cross-dataset generalization: can you report train-on-D1/test-on-D2 and vice versa, and sensitivity to tracker quality (500 Hz vs 120 Hz)?
- Feature and preprocessing clarity: please detail computation of graph-based features (F1H/F2H, PSDH/PSDS, RSDH/RSDS), edge-weight aggregation, normalization per sentence/subject, and how PCA was fit within cross-validation.
- Subject variability: did you analyze inter-participant variability, consider per-reader features or mixed-effects models, and assess impact on results?
- Stronger baselines: can you add dependency-based and modern CNN/RNN baselines to contextualize gaze’s incremental value?

- Specify whether reported F-scores are macro or micro and provide confidence intervals alongside significance tests.
- Fix minor typos/inconsistencies and align feature labels and definitions across text and tables to improve clarity.
