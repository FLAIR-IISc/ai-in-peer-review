- Novel feature set combining cognitive (eye-tracking) features with traditional sentiment features.  
- Cognitive features improve performance on complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.  
- Detailed analysis identifying which cognitive (gaze) features are most significant.  
- Strong motivation: clearly identifies limitations of traditional sentiment methods for sarcasm, irony, and thwarted expectations.  
- Comprehensive feature engineering covering traditional, sarcasm/irony, and a wide range of gaze-derived metrics.  
- Effective experimental validation: two datasets, 10-fold CV, held-out complex dataset, with statistically significant improvements (e.g., p=0.0001; 6.1% gain on complex texts).  
- Practical feasibility is questionable due to reliance on eye-tracking hardware and speculative deployment scenarios.  
- Missing comparison with state-of-the-art deep learning models (e.g., BERT/transformers), leaving relative gains unclear.  
- Insufficient theoretical or empirical explanation of why specific cognitive features aid sentiment analysis.  
- Novelty may be overstated since prior work has used eye-tracking for related tasks and distinctions are not clearly drawn.  
- How will you address practical deployment given limited availability/accuracy of mobile eye-trackers?  
- Why were deep learning approaches excluded, and would cognitive features complement or be redundant with them?  
- Please provide concrete held-out examples where cognitive features corrected failures of traditional features.  
- Which specific gaze features matter for different sentiment complexities (e.g., sarcasm vs. implicit sentiment), and why?
