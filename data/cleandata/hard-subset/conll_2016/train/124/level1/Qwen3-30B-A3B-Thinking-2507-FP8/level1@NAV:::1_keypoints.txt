- Introduces cognitive features from eye-tracking data as a novel approach to sentiment analysis, especially for complex constructs.
- Reports performance improvements up to 9.3%, with notable gains for sarcasm and irony.
- Provides feature significance analysis indicating cognitive features help capture linguistic nuances.
- Held-out experiment (Table 4) shows 6.1% F-score improvement for ironic texts versus 2.1% for simpler texts.
- Feature ranking (Table 3) finds 10 of the top 20 features are gaze-related for Dataset 1, supporting their relevance.
- Emphasizes addressing semantic/pragmatic challenges that traditional methods struggle with.
- Fails to adequately address practical feasibility of requiring eye-tracking data for real-world deployment; mobile tech is currently insufficient and proposed solution is speculative.
- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art deep learning approaches that learn features without specialized data collection.
- Eye-tracking methodology concerns: moderate inter-rater agreement (kappa 0.62–0.68) and inconsistent hardware quality across datasets may affect reliability.
- Question: How will the authors address practical challenges of collecting eye-tracking data in real-world applications?
- Question: How might cognitive features be integrated with deep learning architectures to further enhance performance?
