- Novel cognitive feature set: introduces eye-tracking–derived features (basic gaze metrics and gaze-saliency graph features) not previously used in sentiment analysis.  
- Empirical validation: adding cognitive features yields up to 9.3% F-score improvement on two datasets with complex sentiment constructs.  
- Feature significance analysis: chi-squared ranking shows many cognitive features among the top features (10/20 in Dataset 1, 7/20 in Dataset 2).  
- Strength — novel approach: addresses sarcasm/irony with cognitive signals rather than only linguistic features, showing compelling empirical gains.  
- Strength — rigorous experiments: uses two challenging datasets, three classifiers, multiple feature combos, and statistical tests (McNemar), plus a held-out irony test.  
- Strength — detailed feature analysis: provides table-backed evidence that cognitive features contribute meaningful signal beyond accuracy metrics.  
- Weakness — weak theoretical grounding: insufficient explanation of why eye-movement patterns should correlate with sentiment complexity.  
- Weakness — practicality concerns: speculative discussion of mobile eye-tracker feasibility and understates privacy, cost, adoption, and device-quality issues.  
- Weakness — missing deep-learning comparison: omits evaluation against modern DL baselines (CNNs/RNNs), limiting assessment of relative effectiveness.  
- Weakness — possible feature leakage: unclear whether gaze was recorded during annotation (risk that features reflect annotator behavior rather than text complexity).  
- Question — recording context: were eye-tracking recordings made during sentiment annotation or in a separate reading task?  
- Question — hardware/sampling impact: how did differences between Eyelink-1000 (500Hz) and Tobii T120 (120Hz) affect results and feature reliability?  
- Question — qualitative examples: can authors show specific examples where cognitive features helped (e.g., sarcasm) and where they failed?  
- Additional comment: paper has a clear question and strong empirical evidence but needs stronger theoretical justification and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
