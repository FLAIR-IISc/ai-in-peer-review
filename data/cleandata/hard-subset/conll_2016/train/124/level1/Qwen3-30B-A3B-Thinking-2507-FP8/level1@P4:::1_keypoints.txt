- Summary: Augments traditional sentiment features with cognitive features from readers' eye movements, claiming improvements across lexical/syntactic/semantic/pragmatic levels and reporting up to 3.7% and 9.3% F-score gains on two eye-tracking datasets.

- Contribution 1: Proposes a novel feature set incorporating cognitive (eye-movement) features into sentiment analysis.

- Contribution 2: Provides experimental validation showing significant improvements for complex sentiment expressions (irony/sarcasm).

- Contribution 3: Performs feature significance analysis demonstrating the relevance of cognitive features for nuanced sentiment analysis.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated approach that clearly articulates limitations of current methods and why cognitive features could help with nuanced expressions.

- Strength 2: Sound experimental design with multiple classifiers (SVM, NB, NN), two datasets, and statistical significance testing (McNemar).

- Strength 3: Held-out analysis shows larger gains for complex texts (6.1%) versus simple texts (2.1%), supporting usefulness for difficult cases.

- Weakness 1: Novelty is questionable given prior eye-tracking work (Joshi et al. 2014; Mishra et al. 2016) and inadequate differentiation from those studies.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient methodological detail on how cognitive features, especially graph-based features, are computed from raw eye-tracking data, harming reproducibility.

- Weakness 3: Feasibility discussion is weak—paper fails to address practical barriers like hardware costs, user consent, and data privacy for large-scale deployment.

- Question 1: How does your cognitive feature set differ from the gaze features used by Mishra et al. (2016) and what specific improvements does it offer?

- Question 2: Please provide detailed methods for computing cognitive features from raw eye-tracking data, especially the graph-based features, to enable reproducibility.

- Question 3: Given practical challenges of eye-tracking at scale, what specific real-world applications justify the implementation cost and privacy/consent trade-offs?

- Overall: Interesting and promising results for complex sentiment, but limited by unclear novelty, insufficient methodological detail, and weak practical viability arguments.
