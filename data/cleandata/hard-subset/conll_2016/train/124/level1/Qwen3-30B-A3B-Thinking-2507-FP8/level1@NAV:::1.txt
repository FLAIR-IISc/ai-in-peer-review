

This paper proposes augmenting traditional sentiment analysis features with cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns. The main contributions are: (1) introducing cognitive features from eye-tracking data as a novel approach to sentiment analysis, particularly for complex constructs; (2) demonstrating significant performance improvements (up to 9.3%) specifically for texts with sarcasm and irony; and (3) providing empirical evidence through feature significance analysis that cognitive features effectively address linguistic nuances.

Strengths: The paper makes a compelling case for cognitive features' value in handling complex sentiment constructs, with the held-out dataset experiment (Table 4) showing a 6.1% F-score improvement for ironic texts versus only 2.1% for simpler texts. The feature significance analysis (Table 3) strongly supports that gaze-based features are meaningful contributors to the improved performance, with 10 out of top 20 features being gaze-related for Dataset 1. The paper's focus on addressing semantic/pragmatic challenges (which remain difficult for traditional methods) is particularly valuable for advancing sentiment analysis.

Weaknesses: The paper fails to adequately address the practical feasibility of requiring eye-tracking data for real-world deployment, despite mentioning it briefly in Section 7. Current mobile eye-tracking technology is insufficient for widespread adoption, and the proposed solution (Section 7.1) remains speculative. The work does not compare against state-of-the-art deep learning approaches that have dominated sentiment analysis recently, which learn features automatically without specialized data collection. Additionally, the eye-tracking methodology has limitations: moderate inter-rater agreement (kappa 0.62-0.68) and inconsistent eye-tracking hardware (high-quality vs. low-quality devices) across datasets may affect feature reliability.

Questions to Authors: How do you plan to address the practical challenges of collecting eye-tracking data in real-world applications given current technological limitations? Could you elaborate on how your cognitive features might be integrated with deep learning architectures to potentially enhance performance further?
