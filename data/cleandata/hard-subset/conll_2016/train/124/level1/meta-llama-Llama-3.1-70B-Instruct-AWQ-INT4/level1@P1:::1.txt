**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often fail to capture nuances in user-generated text, such as sarcasm and irony. To address this, they augment traditional features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data, which they claim can improve sentiment analysis performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel feature extraction method**: The authors propose a new method for extracting cognitive features from eye-tracking data, which they claim can capture nuances in user-generated text that traditional features miss.
2. **Improved sentiment analysis performance**: The authors demonstrate that their approach can improve sentiment analysis performance on two datasets, with a maximum improvement of 3.7% and 9.3% in F-score.
3. **Feature significance analysis**: The authors perform a feature significance analysis, which shows that cognitive features are indeed useful for sentiment analysis, particularly for text with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors' approach is novel and innovative, and has the potential to improve sentiment analysis performance on text with complex nuances.
2. **Thorough evaluation**: The authors perform a thorough evaluation of their approach, using multiple datasets and feature combinations, which provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of their method.
3. **Feature significance analysis**: The authors' feature significance analysis provides valuable insights into the importance of cognitive features for sentiment analysis.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited generalizability**: The authors' approach relies on eye-tracking data, which may not be readily available for all text datasets. This limits the generalizability of their approach.
2. **Complexity of feature extraction**: The authors' feature extraction method is complex and requires specialized equipment (eye-trackers). This may make it difficult to replicate their approach.
3. **Lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The authors do not compare their approach to state-of-the-art sentiment analysis methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate the relative performance of their approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limited generalizability of your approach, given the reliance on eye-tracking data?
2. Can you provide more details on the complexity of your feature extraction method, and how it can be replicated by other researchers?
3. How do you plan to compare your approach to state-of-the-art sentiment analysis methods in future work?

**Additional Comments**

The authors' approach is innovative and has the potential to improve sentiment analysis performance on text with complex nuances. However, the limited generalizability and complexity of their feature extraction method are significant limitations that need to be addressed. Additionally, a comparison to state-of-the-art sentiment analysis methods would provide valuable insights into the relative performance of their approach.
