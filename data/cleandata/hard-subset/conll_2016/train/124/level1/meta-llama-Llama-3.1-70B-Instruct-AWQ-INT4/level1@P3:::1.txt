**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by leveraging cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of readers. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often struggle with nuances at lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic levels, and that incorporating cognitive features can improve performance. They introduce a new feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data, and demonstrate its effectiveness on two publicly available datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel feature set**: The authors propose a new feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data. This feature set is shown to improve performance on sentiment analysis tasks, particularly for texts with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.
2. **Cognitive feature extraction**: The authors develop a method for extracting cognitive features from eye-tracking data, including features such as average first-fixation duration, average fixation count, and regression count. These features are shown to be effective in capturing linguistic nuances that are difficult to detect using traditional sentiment analysis methods.
3. **Improved performance on complex texts**: The authors demonstrate that their approach improves performance on texts with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm, which are often challenging for traditional sentiment analysis methods.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to sentiment analysis that leverages cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of readers. This approach has the potential to improve performance on sentiment analysis tasks, particularly for texts with complex constructs.
2. **Effective feature extraction**: The authors develop a method for extracting cognitive features from eye-tracking data that is effective in capturing linguistic nuances that are difficult to detect using traditional sentiment analysis methods.
3. **Improved performance on complex texts**: The authors demonstrate that their approach improves performance on texts with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm, which are often challenging for traditional sentiment analysis methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited datasets**: The authors only evaluate their approach on two publicly available datasets, which may not be representative of all sentiment analysis tasks.
2. **Small sample size**: The authors only use a small sample size of eye-tracking data, which may not be sufficient to train robust models.
3. **Lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The authors do not compare their approach to state-of-the-art sentiment analysis methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the issue of limited datasets and small sample size in future work?
2. Can you provide more details on how you extracted cognitive features from eye-tracking data, and how you selected the specific features used in your approach?
3. How do you plan to compare your approach to state-of-the-art sentiment analysis methods in future work?

**Additional Comments**

The authors' approach is innovative and has the potential to improve performance on sentiment analysis tasks, particularly for texts with complex constructs. However, more work is needed to evaluate the effectiveness of their approach and to address the limitations of the current study.
