**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often fail to capture nuanced sentiment expressions in user-generated text, and that cognitive features can help improve the accuracy of sentiment classification. The paper presents a comprehensive study of sentiment analysis using a combination of traditional and cognitive features, and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach on two publicly available datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Feature Design**: The paper proposes a new feature design that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. This is a significant contribution to the field of sentiment analysis.
2. **Improved Accuracy**: The paper demonstrates that the proposed feature design improves the accuracy of sentiment classification on two publicly available datasets, with an overall improvement of 3.7% and 9.3% respectively.
3. **Feature Significance Analysis**: The paper performs a feature significance analysis and shows that cognitive features are indeed useful in capturing complex sentiment expressions, particularly in text with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.

**Strengths**

1. **Comprehensive Study**: The paper presents a comprehensive study of sentiment analysis using a combination of traditional and cognitive features, and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach on two publicly available datasets.
2. **Novel Feature Design**: The paper proposes a new feature design that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators.
3. **Improved Accuracy**: The paper demonstrates that the proposed feature design improves the accuracy of sentiment classification on two publicly available datasets.
4. **Feature Significance Analysis**: The paper performs a feature significance analysis and shows that cognitive features are indeed useful in capturing complex sentiment expressions.
5. **Potential Applications**: The paper highlights the potential applications of the proposed approach in various domains, including e-commerce, online learning, and gaming.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Dataset**: The paper uses only two publicly available datasets, which may not be representative of all types of text data.
2. **Eye-Tracking Technology**: The paper relies on eye-tracking technology, which may not be widely available or affordable for all users.
3. **Feature Engineering**: The paper requires significant feature engineering efforts to extract cognitive features from eye-movement patterns, which may be time-consuming and labor-intensive.
4. **Interpretability**: The paper does not provide a clear interpretation of the cognitive features and how they relate to sentiment expressions.
5. **Scalability**: The paper does not discuss the scalability of the proposed approach to larger datasets or more complex sentiment analysis tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limitations of eye-tracking technology, such as cost and availability?
2. Can you provide more details on the feature engineering process and how you extracted cognitive features from eye-movement patterns?
3. How do you plan to interpret the cognitive features and relate them to sentiment expressions?
4. Can you discuss the scalability of the proposed approach to larger datasets or more complex sentiment analysis tasks?
5. How do you plan to address the potential biases in the eye-movement patterns of human annotators?
