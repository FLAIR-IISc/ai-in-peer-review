- Proposes a novel sentiment-analysis approach that augments traditional features with cognitive features from annotators' eye-movement patterns, evaluated on two public datasets.
- Introduces a combined feature set (traditional + cognitive) and reports performance improvements on the evaluated datasets.
- Defines cognitive features such as fixation duration, saccade length, and regression count to capture linguistic nuances missed by traditional features.
- Tests the approach on two challenging datasets containing constructs like irony and sarcasm.
- Strength: Innovative departure from traditional sentiment methods by incorporating human cognitive signals.
- Strength: Presents a comprehensive feature set that empirically improves sentiment-analysis performance.
- Strength: Evaluation targets challenging phenomena (irony/sarcasm), demonstrating potential robustness.
- Weakness: Limited generalizability due to evaluation on only two datasets.
- Weakness: Reliance on eye-tracking data, which may be scarce or impractical for many applications.
- Weakness: Heavy feature engineering for cognitive features may be time-consuming and require expertise.
- Question: How will the authors address generalizability to other sentiment-analysis tasks and datasets?
- Question: Request for more details on eye-tracking data collection and preprocessing procedures.
- Question: How can the feature set be made accessible to practitioners without eye-tracking resources or feature-engineering expertise?
- Additional comment: Paper needs more detailed result analysis, including error analysis and explicit discussion of limitations.
- Additional comment: Should clarify practical applications and guidance for real-world use of the proposed approach.
