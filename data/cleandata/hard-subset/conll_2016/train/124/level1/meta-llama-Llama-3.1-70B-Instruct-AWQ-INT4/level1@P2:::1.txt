**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often struggle with nuances at lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic levels, and that cognitive features can help improve the accuracy of sentiment polarity detection. The paper presents a comprehensive study on the effectiveness of cognitive features in sentiment analysis, using two publicly available datasets and a range of machine learning classifiers.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of cognitive features**: The paper introduces a new set of features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators, which can be used to improve the accuracy of sentiment analysis.
2. **Evaluation of cognitive features**: The authors evaluate the effectiveness of cognitive features in sentiment analysis using two publicly available datasets and a range of machine learning classifiers.
3. **Improved accuracy**: The paper shows that the inclusion of cognitive features can improve the accuracy of sentiment polarity detection, particularly for text with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features, which can help improve the accuracy of sentiment polarity detection.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors evaluate the effectiveness of cognitive features using two publicly available datasets and a range of machine learning classifiers, providing a comprehensive understanding of the approach.
3. **Improved accuracy**: The paper shows that the inclusion of cognitive features can improve the accuracy of sentiment polarity detection, particularly for text with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited datasets**: The paper uses only two publicly available datasets, which may not be representative of all types of text data.
2. **Limited classifiers**: The authors evaluate the effectiveness of cognitive features using only three machine learning classifiers, which may not be representative of all types of classifiers.
3. **Lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods**: The paper does not compare the proposed approach with state-of-the-art methods in sentiment analysis, making it difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of the approach in relation to existing methods.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators compare with other types of features used in sentiment analysis, such as linguistic features or stylistic features?
2. Can the proposed approach be extended to other types of text data, such as longer texts or texts from different domains?
3. How do the cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators relate to other cognitive processes, such as attention or memory?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a comprehensive study on the effectiveness of cognitive features in sentiment analysis, and the results are promising. However, the limitations of the study, such as the limited datasets and classifiers, need to be addressed in future work. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results and the potential applications of the proposed approach.
