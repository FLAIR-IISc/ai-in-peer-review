- Summary: Augments sentiment analysis with cognitive features from eye-tracking to better capture nuances like sarcasm and irony, claiming improved performance.

- Main contribution — novel feature extraction: Proposes a method to extract cognitive features from eye-movement data to capture reading signals missed by traditional features.

- Main contribution — improved performance: Reports F-score gains up to 3.7% and 9.3% on two datasets when adding cognitive features.

- Main contribution — feature significance analysis: Shows cognitive features are useful, especially for texts with complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.

- Strength — innovative approach: Novel idea with potential to improve handling of complex linguistic phenomena.

- Strength — thorough evaluation: Uses multiple datasets and feature combinations to support effectiveness.

- Strength — feature insights: Analysis provides valuable information on which cognitive features aid sentiment analysis.

- Weakness — limited generalizability: Dependence on eye-tracking data limits applicability across datasets and real-world settings.

- Weakness — extraction complexity: Feature extraction is complex and requires specialized equipment, making replication difficult.

- Weakness — missing SOTA comparison: No comparison to state-of-the-art sentiment methods, so relative performance is unclear.

- Question to authors — generalizability: How will you address reliance on eye-tracking data to broaden applicability?

- Question to authors — replication details: Can you provide more details on the complexity and reproducibility of your feature extraction pipeline?

- Question to authors — SOTA benchmarking: How do you plan to compare your method against state-of-the-art sentiment analysis approaches?

- Additional comment: Approach is promising but needs to address generalizability, complexity, and include SOTA comparisons to validate its practical value.
