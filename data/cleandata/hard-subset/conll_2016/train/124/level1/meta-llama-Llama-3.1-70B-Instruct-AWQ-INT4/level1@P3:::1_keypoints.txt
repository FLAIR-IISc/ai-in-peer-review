- Proposes a novel sentiment-analysis approach that incorporates cognitive features from readers' eye movements.  
- Argues traditional methods miss lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic nuances that cognitive signals can capture.  
- Introduces a combined feature set merging traditional sentiment features with eye-tracking–derived cognitive features.  
- Presents a method for extracting cognitive features (e.g., first-fixation duration, fixation count, regression count).  
- Demonstrates improved performance on two public datasets, especially for complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.  
- Strength: Innovative use of eye-movement data to augment sentiment analysis.  
- Strength: Effective cognitive feature extraction that captures hard-to-detect linguistic nuances.  
- Strength: Empirical gains on texts containing irony and sarcasm.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to only two datasets, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: Small eye-tracking sample size may prevent robust model training.  
- Weakness: No comparison to state-of-the-art sentiment-analysis methods, hindering assessment of relative performance.  
- Questions: How will the authors address limited datasets and small sample sizes in future work?  
- Questions: Request for more details on cognitive-feature extraction and feature-selection criteria.  
- Questions: How do the authors plan to compare their approach to state-of-the-art methods?  
- Overall comment: Approach is promising and innovative but requires broader evaluation and stronger comparisons to validate effectiveness.
