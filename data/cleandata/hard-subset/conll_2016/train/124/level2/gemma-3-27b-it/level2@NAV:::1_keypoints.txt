- Core claim: Incorporating eye-tracking derived cognitive features improves sentiment polarity detection (up to 9.3% F-score gain).  
- Primary contribution: Demonstrates eye-movement signals enhance sentiment analysis, especially for complex constructs like sarcasm.  
- Secondary contribution: Presents comprehensive feature engineering combining textual features with diverse eye-movement metrics, including novel graph-based features.  
- Experimental validation: Shows statistically significant improvements across multiple classifiers on two datasets, including a held-out complex-case set.  
- Strength — Novelty: Introducing cognitive features from eye-tracking to sentiment analysis is a genuinely novel approach.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Thorough experiments and statistically significant results support the claims.  
- Strength — Feature engineering: Detailed and extensive design of gaze-based and traditional features.  
- Strength — Motivation: Clear problem statement and rationale for incorporating cognitive insights.  
- Strength — Limitations acknowledged: Authors discuss feasibility and practical constraints of the approach.  
- Weakness — Dataset size: Datasets are relatively small, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness — Eye-tracker variability: Use of different-quality eye-trackers is noted but its impact isn’t deeply analyzed.  
- Weakness — Graph feature complexity: Gaze-saliency graph descriptions are complex and need clearer explanation, visualization, and per-feature impact analysis.  
- Weakness — Practicality: Dependence on eye-tracking data raises adoption hurdles despite discussion of emerging mobile solutions.  
- Questions: Sensitivity to gaze-saliency graph construction parameters; computational cost for real-time use; effect of individual differences in eye-movement patterns.  
- Overall recommendation: Promising, well-motivated work with strong empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focusing on scalability, robustness to eye-tracker variability, and deeper analysis of graph-based features.
