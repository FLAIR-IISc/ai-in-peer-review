- Novel integration of cognitive (eye-tracking) features with traditional sentiment features to improve sentiment analysis, especially for sarcasm/irony.  
- Empirical validation showing statistically significant F-score improvements on two datasets using the combined feature set.  
- Feature significance analysis supports that cognitive features help handle complex linguistic constructs and explains why the method works.  
- Clear motivation and problem definition highlighting challenges of subtle sentiment and rationale for cognitive features.  
- Comprehensive and well-described feature set, including novel gaze-saliency graph features.  
- Rigorous experimental setup with two datasets, multiple classifiers, 10-fold CV, and a held-out test for complex constructs.  
- Statistical significance testing (McNemar) provided to show improvements are not by chance.  
- Feasibility discussion addressing practical limitations and potential of mobile eye-trackers.  
- Dataset size and moderate inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss kappa) are limitations that may introduce noise.  
- Using datasets from different eye-trackers (Eyelink vs. Tobii) may confound results due to differing data quality.  
- Averaging gaze data across participants may lose individual signal; participant-specific modeling could help.  
- Limited comparison to modern deep learning baselines; stronger benchmarks would strengthen claims.  
- Possible feature redundancy in the large feature set; formal feature selection or dimensionality reduction is needed.  
- Question: How do averaged gaze limitations affect results and what alternatives (participant-specific models) were considered?  
- Question: How did differing eye-tracker quality between datasets impact cognitive feature extraction and results?  
- Question: Are there plans to integrate cognitive features into deep learning sentiment models and what challenges are expected?  
- Question: What preprocessing/filtering was applied to eye-tracking data to remove artifacts or noise?  
- Writing is clear and well-organized.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive.  
- Recommend adding a dataset statistics table and a visual of the gaze-saliency graph for clarity.
