- Summary: Augments traditional sentiment features with cognitive features from readers' eye-movement patterns and reports significant accuracy gains on two datasets.
- Contribution: Introduces a novel set of cognitive features derived from eye movements to capture reading-related linguistic nuances.
- Contribution: Demonstrates that combining cognitive and traditional features improves sentiment analysis accuracy.
- Contribution: Shows cognitive features are especially effective for complex texts (e.g., irony, sarcasm).
- Strength: Novel approach—leveraging eye-movement patterns is innovative for sentiment analysis.
- Strength: Reports a significant improvement in accuracy over traditional systems.
- Strength: Robust evaluation with two datasets, plus error analysis and feature significance analysis.
- Weakness: Limited availability of eye-tracking data may restrict real-world applicability.
- Weakness: Need for user consent for eye-tracking poses scalability and deployment challenges.
- Weakness: Aggregating eye-tracking parameters to form cognitive features may cause information loss.
- Question: How will you address limited eye-tracking data availability and user-consent challenges?
- Question: Please provide more details on the construction of the gaze-saliency graphs.
- Question: How do you plan to extend this cognitive-feature approach to other NLP tasks (e.g., emotion analysis, summarization)?
