- Summary: Proposes augmenting sentiment analysis with cognitive features from annotators' eye movements to better handle nuanced text (e.g., sarcasm, irony).
- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel feature set combining traditional sentiment features with eye-movement–derived cognitive features.
- Contribution 2: Applies eye-tracking data in a new way to derive cognitive signals for sentiment analysis.
- Contribution 3: Reports accuracy improvements of 3.7% and 9.3% on two datasets using the proposed approach.
- Strength 1: Innovative use of human cognitive signals to address linguistic subtleties.
- Strength 2: Demonstrated empirical accuracy gains, especially on complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.
- Strength 3: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and classification methods.
- Weakness 1: Limited scope—unclear if the method generalizes beyond sentiment analysis.
- Weakness 2: Heavy dependence on eye-tracking data raises concerns about availability and scalability.
- Weakness 3: Complex feature set hampers interpretability and understanding of why improvements occur.
- Question 1: How will the approach be scaled given reliance on eye-tracking data?
- Question 2: Can the authors provide clearer insight into feature interpretability and their contributions to accuracy?
- Question 3: Have the authors considered applying this method to other NLP tasks (e.g., summarization, QA)?
