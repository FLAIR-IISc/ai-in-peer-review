**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by leveraging cognitive features derived from the eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often struggle with nuanced texts that contain subtleties at lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic levels. To address this, they introduce a new feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two publicly available datasets and show that their method improves the performance of polarity detection by a maximum of 3.7% and 9.3% over traditional methods.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Feature Set**: The authors introduce a new feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data. This feature set is shown to be effective in improving the performance of polarity detection.
2. **Cognitive Features**: The authors propose a set of cognitive features that are derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. These features are shown to be effective in capturing nuanced texts that contain subtleties at lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic levels.
3. **Improved Performance**: The authors demonstrate that their approach improves the performance of polarity detection by a maximum of 3.7% and 9.3% over traditional methods on two publicly available datasets.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative Approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to sentiment analysis that leverages cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators.
2. **Effective Feature Set**: The authors demonstrate that their feature set is effective in improving the performance of polarity detection on two publicly available datasets.
3. **Robust Evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach using a robust evaluation methodology, including a comparison with traditional methods and an analysis of the importance of cognitive features.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Generalizability**: The authors' approach is limited to datasets that contain eye-tracking data, which may not be widely available.
2. **Complexity of Feature Extraction**: The authors' feature extraction process is complex and may require specialized expertise and equipment.
3. **Limited Analysis of Cognitive Features**: The authors' analysis of cognitive features is limited to a few examples, and further research is needed to fully understand the relationship between cognitive features and sentiment analysis.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limited generalizability of your approach, given the requirement for eye-tracking data?
2. Can you provide more details on the complexity of feature extraction and how it can be simplified for practical applications?
3. How do you plan to extend your analysis of cognitive features to better understand their relationship with sentiment analysis?
