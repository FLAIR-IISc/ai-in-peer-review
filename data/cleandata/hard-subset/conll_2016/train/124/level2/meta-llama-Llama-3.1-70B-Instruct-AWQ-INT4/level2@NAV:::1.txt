**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often struggle with nuanced text, such as sarcasm and irony, due to limitations in capturing linguistic subtleties. By leveraging eye-tracking data, they aim to improve the accuracy of sentiment analysis.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel feature set**: The authors introduce a new feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns. This feature set is shown to improve the accuracy of sentiment analysis, particularly for text with complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.
2. **Eye-tracking data**: The authors utilize eye-tracking data to derive cognitive features, which are used to augment traditional sentiment features. This is a novel application of eye-tracking data in sentiment analysis.
3. **Improved accuracy**: The authors demonstrate that their approach improves the accuracy of sentiment analysis, with an overall improvement of 3.7% and 9.3% on two datasets.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to sentiment analysis, leveraging eye-tracking data to derive cognitive features.
2. **Improved accuracy**: The authors demonstrate that their approach improves the accuracy of sentiment analysis, particularly for text with complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.
3. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their approach, using multiple datasets and classification methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited scope**: The authors focus on sentiment analysis, and it is unclear whether their approach can be applied to other natural language processing tasks.
2. **Dependence on eye-tracking data**: The authors' approach relies on eye-tracking data, which may not be readily available or scalable.
3. **Interpretability**: The authors' feature set is complex, and it may be challenging to interpret the results and understand why their approach is effective.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the scalability of your approach, given the reliance on eye-tracking data?
2. Can you provide more insight into the interpretability of your feature set and how it contributes to the improved accuracy of sentiment analysis?
3. Have you considered applying your approach to other natural language processing tasks, such as text summarization or question answering?
