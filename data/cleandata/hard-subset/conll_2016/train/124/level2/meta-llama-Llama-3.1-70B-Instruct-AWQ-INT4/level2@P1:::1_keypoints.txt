- Proposes augmenting traditional sentiment features with cognitive features derived from readers' eye-movement patterns to better capture nuances like sarcasm and irony.  
- Evaluates the approach on two datasets containing complex constructs and reports significant accuracy improvements.  
- Main contribution: introducing cognitive (eye-tracking) features into sentiment analysis.  
- Main contribution: demonstrating effectiveness on datasets with sarcasm and irony.  
- Main contribution: performing feature significance analysis to identify impactful features.  
- Strength: novel, promising approach that may improve handling of complex textual constructs.  
- Strength: evaluation on realistic, complex datasets supports practical relevance.  
- Strength: feature significance analysis provides insights into which features drive improvements.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to only two datasets, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: only a limited set of cognitive features was considered; other potentially useful features were not explored.  
- Weakness: reliance on eye-tracking data presents technical and applicability limitations.  
- Question: how will the authors address the practical/technical limitations of requiring eye-tracking data?  
- Question: which specific cognitive features were most important according to the feature significance analysis?  
- Question: how do the authors plan to extend this cognitive-feature approach to other NLP tasks (e.g., emotion analysis, summarization)?
