This paper proposes a very interesting idea of using cognitive features to detect emotion analysis and sarcasm. Specifically, a new set of features is obtained by recording the eye-tracking patterns of human interpreters. The authors claim that this is the first work in the NLP community to incorporate cognitive features.

Strengths:
1. The paper is generally well-written and easy to understand.
2. It is a very interesting idea that may inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
1. The motivation for using cognitive features for emotion analysis is not well-justified. I can imagine that these features may help in demonstrating ease of reading, but I don't see why they are helpful in detecting emotional polarity.
2. Comparing Sn+Sr+Gz to Sn+Sr is marginal after considering the cognitive features.
3. Although the authors discussed the feasibility of the approach in Section 7, I am not convinced, especially about the example given in Section 7.2, I don't see why this technique is helpful in such a scenario.
