This paper is about introducing eye-tracking features as a type of cognitive feature for emotion analysis. I think the idea of introducing eye-tracking features as a proxy for cognitive weight for emotion analysis is interesting.

I think the discussion on the comparison of features and feature sets is clear and very helpful. I also like that the feasibility of the approach is addressed in Section 7.

I wonder if this would help in evaluation if the datasets do not confuse different domains, for example, a movie review corpus and a tweet corpus.

For example, it could improve the prediction of movie reviews (i.e., tweets) if tweets (i.e., movie reviews) were not in training. This would also make the results easier to understand. The results in Table 2 may seem quite low compared to state-of-the-art results for the Pang and Lee data, but look much better compared to the results of the Twitter data.

In Section 3.3, there are no overlapping snippets in the training data and test data of datasets 1 and 2, right? Even if they come from the same sources (e.g., Pang and Lee and Sentiment 140).
