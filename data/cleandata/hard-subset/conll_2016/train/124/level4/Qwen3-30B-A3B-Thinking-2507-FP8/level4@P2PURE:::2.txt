

This paper introduces eye-tracking features as cognitive proxies for sentiment analysis, presenting an interesting approach. The discussion of features and comparison of feature sets is clear and highly valuable, and addressing the feasibility of the approach in Section 7 is commendable.  

However, the evaluation would benefit from separating distinct domains. Combining movie reviews and tweets in the datasets confounds results; training and testing on disjoint domains (e.g., excluding tweets from movie review training and vice versa) would improve prediction performance for each domain and enhance result interpretability. Table 2 results appear relatively low compared to state-of-the-art on Pang and Lee data but appear more competitive when benchmarked against Twitter-specific results.  

In Section 3.3, are there no overlapping snippets between the training and testing data for Datasets 1 and 2, even when derived from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140)?  

Minor: The excessive use of bold text is distracting.
