- Proposes using eye-tracking features as cognitive proxies for sentiment analysis, bridging cognitive science and NLP.  
- Feature engineering and comparative analysis (Section 3) are well-structured and clearly explained.  
- Practical feasibility discussion (Section 7) strengthens real-world applicability.  
- Major concern: evaluation mixes domains (movie reviews and tweets) in training/testing, obscuring performance interpretation.  
- Mixing likely inflates Pang & Lee results and underrepresents Twitter performance; domain-separated evaluation is needed.  
- Recommend re-evaluating with strictly domain-separated splits (train on one domain, test on the other) to test true generalization.  
- Data partitioning worry: authors must confirm no overlapping snippets across datasets to avoid data leakage and ensure reproducibility.  
- Minor issue: inconsistent formatting and excessive bolding distracts from content and should be streamlined.  
- Overall: meaningful contribution with strong methods, but results require careful interpretation until domain-mixing and partitioning issues are resolved.
