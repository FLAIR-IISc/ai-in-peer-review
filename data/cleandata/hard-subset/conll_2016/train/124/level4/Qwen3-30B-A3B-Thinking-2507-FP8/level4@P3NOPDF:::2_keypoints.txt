- Novel approach: incorporates eye-tracking features as proxies for cognitive load to improve sentiment analysis, well-motivated and promising.  
- Theoretical grounding: strong integration of cognitive science insights into NLP and user engagement measurement.  
- Feature engineering: Section 3 provides clear, well-structured guidance on feature design and comparative analysis.  
- Feasibility: Section 7 offers a thoughtful assessment of practical implementation challenges.  
- Major methodological concern: training and test sets conflate domains (movie reviews vs. tweets), obscuring true model performance and interpretability.  
- Recommended fix: run domain-separated experiments (train/test on Pang & Lee only, and separately on Sentiment140) to validate within-domain gains.  
- Data-leakage risk: unclear whether datasets are disjoint across sources—authors must explicitly confirm no overlap between all datasets.  
- Minor presentation issues: reduce excessive bolding in figures/tables and tighten phrasing in Section 3.3 for precision.  
- Overall verdict: promising contribution hindered by evaluation flaws; addressing domain separation and disjointness would substantially strengthen the paper.
