- Paper proposes combining cognitive features from eye-tracking with sentiment and sarcasm detection; authors claim this is the first such integration in NLP.  
- Strength: manuscript is well-structured and clearly presented.  
- Strength: proposed methodology is novel and could motivate future work across NLP tasks.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for using cognitive features for sentiment—their specific utility for polarity is unclear despite correlations with reading ease.  
- Weakness: performance gains from adding cognitive features (Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr) are marginal and do not demonstrate significant added value.  
- Weakness: feasibility argument in Section 7 (especially the example in 7.2) is unconvincing and fails to show practical utility.
