

This paper presents an intriguing approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotators' eye-movement patterns. The authors claim this represents the first use of cognitive features within the NLP community.

Strengths:
1. The paper is well-written and accessible.
2. The core idea is highly innovative and holds significant potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
1. The motivation for employing cognitive features in sentiment analysis is inadequately justified. While these features might reflect reading ease, their specific utility for detecting sentiment polarities remains unclear.
2. The performance improvement achieved by adding cognitive features (comparing Sn+Sr+Gz to Sn+Sr) is marginal.
3. Although the authors address feasibility in Section 7, their argument is unconvincing, particularly the example in Section 7.2. The utility of this technique in the described scenario remains unclear.
