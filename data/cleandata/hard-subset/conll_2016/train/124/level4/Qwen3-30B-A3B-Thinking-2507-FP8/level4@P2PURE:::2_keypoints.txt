- Introduces eye-tracking features as cognitive proxies for sentiment analysis — an interesting and novel approach.  
- Discussion and comparison of feature sets is clear and highly valuable.  
- Section 7’s treatment of feasibility is commendable.  
- Evaluation should separate domains; combining movie reviews and tweets confounds results and hurts interpretability.  
- Recommend training/testing on disjoint domains (e.g., exclude tweets from movie-review training and vice versa) to improve domain-specific performance.  
- Table 2 results seem low relative to Pang & Lee state-of-the-art but more competitive against Twitter benchmarks.  
- Clarify whether Datasets 1 and 2 contain overlapping snippets between training and testing when derived from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee, Sentiment140).  
- Minor: excessive use of bold text is distracting.
