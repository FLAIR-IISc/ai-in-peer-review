

This paper introduces eye-tracking features as cognitive indicators for sentiment analysis. The concept of employing eye-tracking metrics as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis is intriguing. The discussion of feature engineering and comparative analysis of feature sets is well-structured and highly informative. The inclusion of feasibility considerations in Section 7 is also commendable.  

It would be beneficial to evaluate the approach using domain-separated datasets, as the current combination of movie reviews and tweets may confound results. Separating these domains—e.g., training on movie reviews and testing on tweets exclusively—would likely enhance prediction performance for each domain and improve result interpretability. Table 2's reported performance appears modest relative to state-of-the-art benchmarks on the Pang & Lee dataset but aligns more favorably with Twitter sentiment analysis results.  

In Section 3.3, are there no overlapping text snippets between the training and testing splits for Datasets 1 and 2, even when sourced from the same corpora (e.g., Pang & Lee, Sentiment 140)?  

Minor: The excessive use of bold formatting is occasionally distracting.
