- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Novel idea: incorporating cognitive (eye-movement) features for sentiment and sarcasm detection with potential to inspire other NLP work.  
- Motivation is weak: authors do not justify why reading/ease-related cognitive features should indicate sentiment polarity.  
- Empirical benefit is marginal: adding cognitive features yields only slight improvement (Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr).  
- Feasibility arguments (Section 7), especially the example in Section 7.2, are unconvincing and don’t demonstrate practical utility.
