- Introduces eye-tracking features as cognitive proxies for sentiment analysis — an interesting and novel approach.  
- Feature design and comparative analysis of feature sets are clear and highly valuable.  
- Exploration of practical feasibility in Section 7 is a strong contribution.  
- Major methodological concern: datasets conflate distinct domains (movie reviews vs. tweets), complicating interpretation and possibly inflating results.  
- Recommendation: separate domains and run cross-domain experiments (train on movies/test on tweets and vice versa) to clarify per-domain performance.  
- Clarify benchmarking: the Pang & Lee results appear modest versus general SOTA but are more competitive when compared to Twitter-specific (Sentiment140) results; conflation obscures this nuance.  
- Critical to confirm no overlapping text snippets between Dataset 1 and Dataset 2 to rule out data leakage and contamination.  
- Minor stylistic note: excessive bold text is distracting and unnecessary.
