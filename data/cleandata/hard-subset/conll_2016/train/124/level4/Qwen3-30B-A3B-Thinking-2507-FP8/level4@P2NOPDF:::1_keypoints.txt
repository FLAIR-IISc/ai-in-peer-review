- Novel approach: integrates eye-movement–derived cognitive features for sentiment and sarcasm detection, claimed as a first in NLP.  
- Strength: manuscript is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Strength: interdisciplinary idea of leveraging cognitive processes via eye-tracking is compelling and potentially inspiring for other NLP work.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why eye-movements should correlate with sentiment polarity or sarcasm.  
- Weakness: marginal performance gains (≤0.5% F1) do not justify the added complexity and cost of eye-tracking.  
- Weakness: feasibility and real-world applicability arguments are unconvincing, ignoring scalability, user discomfort, and noisy environments.  
- Required revisions: rigorously justify the cognitive–sentiment link with task-specific hypotheses.  
- Required revisions: evaluate against stronger, state-of-the-art linguistic baselines.  
- Required revisions: provide concrete, realistic use cases addressing scalability and user constraints.  
- Overall: intriguing novelty but insufficiently validated; contribution is speculative and not yet ready for publication.
