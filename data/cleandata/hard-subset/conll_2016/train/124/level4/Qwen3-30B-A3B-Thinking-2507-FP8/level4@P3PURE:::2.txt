

This paper introduces eye-tracking features as cognitive proxies for sentiment analysis, presenting an interesting approach. The discussion of features and comparative analysis of feature sets is clear and highly beneficial. Additionally, the feasibility of the approach is well-addressed in Section 7.  

I recommend evaluating the model on domain-specific data to avoid conflating domains (e.g., separating movie reviews and tweets). Training on mixed domains may hinder performance on individual domains—such as movie reviews if tweets are included in training—and complicate result interpretation. The results in Table 2 appear relatively low compared to state-of-the-art on Pang and Lee data but appear more reasonable when benchmarked against Twitter-specific results.  

In Section 3.3, are there no overlapping snippets between the training and testing data for Datasets 1 and 2 (even when sourced from the same corpora, e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140)?  

Minor note: Some excessive bolding may be distracting.
