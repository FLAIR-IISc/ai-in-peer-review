- Novelty: Introduces a novel integration of eye-tracking–derived cognitive features into sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection — a notable conceptual contribution.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and logically structured.  
- Promise: Leveraging eye-movement data to capture cognitive processing is a promising idea that could inspire broader NLP work.  
- Theoretical gap: Insufficient mechanistic justification for how eye-movement patterns specifically inform sentiment polarity rather than general comprehension.  
- Weak empirical gains: Performance improvements from cognitive features are marginal (e.g., 0.5–1.2% F1) and lack robust statistical significance or detailed feature-contribution analysis.  
- Feasibility concerns: Real-world scalability and practicality of using eye-tracking (especially for real-time/high-volume social media) are unconvincing and not adequately addressed.  
- Overall: Intriguing methodological angle but requires stronger theoretical grounding, more compelling empirical validation, and a clearer path to real-world applicability.
