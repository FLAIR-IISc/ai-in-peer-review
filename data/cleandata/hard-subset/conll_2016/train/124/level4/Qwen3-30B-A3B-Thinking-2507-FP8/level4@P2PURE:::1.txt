

This paper proposes an intriguing approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotators' eye-movement patterns. The authors assert this represents the first use of cognitive features in the NLP community.  

Strengths:  
1. The paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
2. The core idea is novel and could inspire research in other NLP tasks.  

Weaknesses:  
1. The motivation for employing cognitive features in sentiment analysis is inadequately justified. While these features might reflect reading ease, the authors fail to establish why they are relevant for determining sentiment polarity.  
2. The performance gain from adding cognitive features (comparing Sn+Sr+Gz to Sn+Sr) is marginal.  
3. The feasibility argument in Section 7 remains unconvincing, particularly the example in Section 7.2, which does not demonstrate how this technique would be beneficial in the described scenario.
