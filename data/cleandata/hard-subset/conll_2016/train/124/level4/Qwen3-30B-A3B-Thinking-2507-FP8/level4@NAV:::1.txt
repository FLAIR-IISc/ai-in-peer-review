

This paper introduces an intriguing approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotators' eye-movement patterns. The authors assert this represents the first integration of cognitive features within the NLP domain.  
Strengths:  
1. The manuscript is well-structured and clearly presented.  
2. The proposed methodology offers a novel perspective that could motivate future work across diverse NLP tasks.  
Weaknesses:  
1. The rationale for employing cognitive features in sentiment analysis lacks sufficient justification. While these features may correlate with reading ease, their specific utility for determining sentiment polarity remains unclear.  
2. The performance gain from incorporating cognitive features (comparing Sn+Sr+Gz against Sn+Sr) is marginal and fails to demonstrate significant added value.  
3. Despite addressing feasibility in Section 7, the argument remains unconvincing, particularly the example in Section 7.2, which does not establish practical utility for the proposed technique.
