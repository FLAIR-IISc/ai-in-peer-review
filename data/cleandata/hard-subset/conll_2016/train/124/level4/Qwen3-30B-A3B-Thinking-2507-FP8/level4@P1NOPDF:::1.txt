

This paper presents a novel and compelling approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotators' eye-movement patterns. The authors position this as the first integration of such cognitive metrics into NLP, which represents a significant conceptual contribution.  

**Strengths:**  
1. The paper is clearly written and logically structured, making it accessible to readers.  
2. The core idea—leveraging eye-tracking data to capture cognitive processes—holds promise and could stimulate research in other NLP domains requiring nuanced human-like processing.  

**Weaknesses:**  
1. The justification for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is insufficiently grounded. While these features might correlate with reading ease or cognitive load, the paper fails to establish a clear mechanistic link to sentiment polarity detection. It remains unclear how eye-movement patterns specifically inform the identification of positive/negative sentiment rather than general comprehension.  
2. The performance gains from adding cognitive features (i.e., comparing Sn+Sr+Gz to Sn+Sr) are marginal. The reported improvements (e.g., 0.5–1.2% F1 in Table 3) do not convincingly demonstrate that cognitive features provide substantial value beyond existing sentiment and sarcasm features. Without robust statistical significance or analysis of feature contribution, the practical utility of these additions is questionable.  
3. The feasibility discussion in Section 7, particularly the example in Section 7.2 (real-time social media monitoring), is unconvincing. The authors suggest cognitive features could enhance speed or accuracy in dynamic settings, but the example lacks concrete evidence. It is unclear how eye-tracking data—typically requiring controlled environments—would scale to real-world, high-volume applications without introducing significant latency or usability barriers. The argument remains speculative without addressing these practical constraints.  

Overall, while the paper introduces an intriguing methodological angle, its core claims require stronger theoretical justification, more compelling empirical validation, and a clearer path to real-world applicability. The marginal gains and unresolved feasibility concerns limit the paper’s impact despite its conceptual novelty.
