- Proposes combining traditional sentiment features with cognitive (eye-tracking) features, showing improved detection of sarcasm/irony and providing feature significance analysis.

- Clear, well-structured integration of cognitive science and NLP.

- Empirical results show statistically significant improvements (up to 9.3% F-score) on complex sentiment constructs.

- Feature significance analysis suggests cognitive features help detect nuanced sentiment rather than providing a general performance boost.

- Lacks theoretical justification tying eye-movement patterns causally to sentiment processing rather than to general reading difficulty.

- Marginal improvements (0.6–1.5% F-score) when adding cognitive features to the augmented feature set question practical significance.

- Feasibility discussion (especially the e-commerce example) is unconvincing and doesn’t clearly show added value over text-based analysis.

- Request for a theoretical framework or additional evidence that eye-movements specifically correlate with sentiment polarity, not just reading difficulty.

- How do you justify the extra complexity and cost of collecting eye-tracking data given the marginal performance gains?

- In the e-commerce scenario, how would the system distinguish regressions due to reading difficulty from regressions signaling sentiment polarity?
