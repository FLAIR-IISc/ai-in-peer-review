This paper explores the incorporation of eye-tracking data as cognitive features to enhance sentiment analysis, a promising and interesting approach. 

The presentation of features and the comparative analysis of feature sets are well-executed and particularly insightful. The inclusion of a feasibility analysis in Section 7 is also a valuable contribution.

Regarding the evaluation, I suggest considering the potential benefits of decoupling datasets from different domains (e.g., movie reviews and tweets). Training and testing on separate domain-specific datasets could lead to improved performance for each domain and facilitate clearer interpretation of the results. Specifically, the reported results in Table 2 appear modest when benchmarked against state-of-the-art performance on the Pang and Lee dataset, but are more competitive when considered in the context of Twitter sentiment analysis.

I have a clarifying question regarding data separation in Section 3.3: could the authors confirm that there is no overlap between the training and testing data for datasets 1 and 2, even when utilizing data from the same original sources (such as Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140)? 

Finally, a minor point: the extensive use of bold formatting occasionally feels distracting.
