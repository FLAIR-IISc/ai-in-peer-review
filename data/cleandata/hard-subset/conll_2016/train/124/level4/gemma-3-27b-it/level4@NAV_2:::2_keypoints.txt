- Paper proposes using eye-tracking-derived cognitive features to improve sentiment analysis by capturing readers’ cognitive processing during interpretation.
- Main contribution: augmenting traditional features with cognitive (eye-movement) features yields up to a 9.3% F-score improvement on tested datasets.
- Secondary contribution: extensive feature engineering (sentiment, sarcasm/thwarting, cognitive, reading difficulty) with thorough ablation studies.
- Experimental strength: evaluation on two datasets, multiple classifiers, a held-out complex dataset (irony/sarcasm), and statistical significance testing.
- Practical strength: discussion of feasibility and applications given rising availability of mobile eye-trackers (e-commerce, online learning).
- Novelty: eye-tracking for direct improvement of sentiment classification is a fresh, valuable perspective linking psycholinguistics and NLP.
- Feature engineering: rich, nuanced feature set beyond lexicon approaches, with ablation results highlighting individual feature contributions.
- Rigor: solid experimental setup and validation strengthen confidence in the reported improvements.
- Weakness: dataset heterogeneity (mixing movie reviews and tweets) may introduce domain biases and obscure true effectiveness of cognitive features.
- Weakness: limited generalizability due to specific eye-tracking hardware and inter-subject variability in eye-tracking signals.
- Weakness: averaging eye-tracking parameters across participants may lose individual-level signal; alternative aggregation could help.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art/deep learning baselines (e.g., on Pang and Lee) to contextualize gains.
- Question: please clarify the impact of dataset heterogeneity and whether domain-specific training/evaluation was performed.
- Question: how sensitive are results to the particular eye-tracking devices and participant variability, and can this be mitigated?
- Question: did you try alternative aggregation methods (participant-specific features or advanced statistical models) for eye-tracking data?
- Question: how do your results compare to recent deep learning approaches on Pang and Lee in terms of F-score and other metrics?
