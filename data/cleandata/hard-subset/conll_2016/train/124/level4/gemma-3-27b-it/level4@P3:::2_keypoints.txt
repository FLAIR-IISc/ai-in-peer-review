- Core idea: Use eye-tracking-derived cognitive features (eye-movement patterns) to improve sentiment analysis by capturing cognitive load during sentiment interpretation.
- Primary contribution: Demonstrates that incorporating eye-tracking features measurably improves sentiment accuracy, especially on complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.
- Secondary contribution: Detailed feature analysis identifying which eye-movement metrics are most informative for sentiment detection.
- Strength — empirical gains: Shows statistically significant F-score improvements (up to 9.3% on Dataset 2) with rigorous 10-fold CV and significance testing.
- Strength — novelty: Novel integration of cognitive (gaze) features grounded in cognitive science, moving beyond purely linguistic features.
- Strength — feature analysis: Comprehensive feature-significance results (Table 3) that guide future research on informative gaze metrics.
- Strength — feasibility discussion: Pragmatic Section 7 addressing data acquisition, mobile eye-trackers, user consent, and real-world application scenarios.
- Weakness — dataset heterogeneity: Combining movie reviews and tweets may introduce noise and limit generalizability; separate or more homogeneous evaluations are needed.
- Weakness — limited SOTA comparison: Lacks thorough comparison to recent state-of-the-art (especially deep learning) sentiment models.
- Weakness — potential data dependency: Cognitive features come from specific annotators; generalizability across populations or tasks is unclear.
- Weakness — averaging gaze data: Averaging across participants may lose individual cognitive strategy information; alternative approaches could help.
- Question — data overlap: Clarify how training/testing overlap was prevented between Datasets 1 and 2 and source datasets (Pang & Lee, Sentiment140).
- Question — feature sensitivity: How sensitive are results to parameters for deriving cognitive features (e.g., edge density thresholds); were sensitivity analyses done?
- Question — bias controls: What steps controlled for participant variability in reading habits or visual acuity in the eye-tracking data?
- Additional comment: Reduce distracting bold text and include a table summarizing dataset statistics (size, sentiment distribution, avg. sentence length).
