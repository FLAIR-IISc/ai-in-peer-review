- Uses eye-tracking features as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis — interesting and potentially valuable idea.
- Clear and insightful discussion and comparison of different feature set configurations.
- Section 7’s practical feasibility analysis is a strong, commendable part of the paper.
- Major concern: evaluation conflates distinct domains (movie reviews vs tweets), which may hurt performance and complicate interpretation.
- Recommend separating datasets by domain to enable focused training/testing and clearer comparisons (results look low vs Pang & Lee but more competitive for Twitter).
- Require confirmation that there is no train/test contamination even when data originate from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee, Sentiment140); absence of overlap is crucial.
- Minor stylistic issue: frequent use of bold text is distracting and should be reduced.
