This paper presents a novel and interesting approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotators’ eye-movement patterns. The authors assert this is the first work to integrate such features within the NLP community.

Strengths:
1. The paper is generally well-written and clear.
2. The core idea is highly interesting and has the potential to stimulate further research in other NLP areas.

Weaknesses:
1. The motivation for leveraging cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not strongly justified. While these features may correlate with reading ease, the connection to identifying sentiment polarity remains unclear.
2. The improvement achieved through the addition of cognitive features, as evidenced by the comparison between Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr, is marginal.
3. Despite the discussion of the approach’s feasibility in Section 7, I remain unconvinced, particularly regarding the example presented in Section 7.2. The benefits of this technique in that scenario are not readily apparent.
