This paper introduces eye-tracking features for sentiment analysis as a type of cognitive feature. I believe the idea of incorporating eye-tracking features as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis is interesting.

The discussion of features and comparison of feature sets is clear and very helpful. I also appreciate that the feasibility of the approach is addressed in Section 7.

I wonder if the evaluation would benefit from using datasets that are more domain-specific. For instance, it might improve the prediction accuracy for movie reviews (respectively, tweets) if the training data did not include tweets (respectively, movie reviews). This would also make the results easier to interpret. The results in Table 2 appear relatively low compared to state-of-the-art results for the Pang and Lee dataset, but look more competitive when compared to results for Twitter data.

In Section 3.3, can the authors confirm that there are no overlapping snippets between the training and testing data for datasets 1 and 2, even though they originate from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140)?

Minor comment: the frequent use of bold text is somewhat distracting.
