This paper explores the incorporation of eye-tracking data as cognitive features for sentiment analysis, a conceptually interesting approach that leverages eye movements as a proxy for cognitive load. 

The discussion of features and the comparison of different feature sets are clear and informative. The inclusion of a feasibility analysis in Section 7 is also a strength of the paper.

I question whether the evaluation would benefit from using datasets that are not mixed-domain. Specifically, separating the movie review and tweet corpora could improve performance—potentially allowing for more accurate prediction for each domain when the other is excluded from the training data—and would facilitate clearer interpretation of the results. The results presented in Table 2 appear relatively low when compared to state-of-the-art performance on the Pang and Lee dataset, but are more competitive when assessed against results for Twitter data.

Regarding Section 3.3, could the authors confirm that there is no overlap between the training and testing data for datasets 1 and 2, even when the data originates from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140)?

Finally, the frequent use of bold text is occasionally distracting.
