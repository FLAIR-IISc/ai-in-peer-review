This paper presents an intriguing approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human eye-tracking data. The core idea – leveraging patterns in annotator eye movements – is novel and potentially impactful. The authors assert this is the first work to integrate such cognitive features into the NLP pipeline, which, if true, represents a significant contribution.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clarity and Readability:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to understand. The authors effectively convey their methodology and results.
2. **Novelty and Potential Impact:** The proposed approach is innovative and has the potential to inspire research extending beyond sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection to other NLP tasks involving human cognitive processes.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Motivation and Justification:** The central motivation for employing cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not fully convincing. While the authors suggest a link to reading ease, the rationale for *why* these features should improve sentiment polarity detection remains underdeveloped. A more detailed explanation of the hypothesized connection between specific eye-movement patterns and underlying sentiment would strengthen the paper.  The link between cognitive effort and sentiment expression needs further articulation.
2. **Marginal Improvement:** The performance gains achieved by incorporating cognitive features appear modest. The comparison between results using Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr indicates a limited improvement, raising questions about the practical benefit of the added complexity of acquiring and processing eye-tracking data. A more thorough analysis of the statistical significance of these improvements is warranted.
3. **Feasibility and Application Scenario:** The discussion of the approach's feasibility in Section 7 does not fully address my concerns. Specifically, the example provided in Section 7.2 does not convincingly demonstrate how this technique would be helpful in the presented scenario. The connection between the observed eye-movement patterns and the successful resolution of ambiguity feels tenuous and requires stronger justification. The example doesn't clearly illustrate a situation where cognitive features uniquely contribute to improved performance compared to traditional NLP methods. 

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper introduces a valuable, albeit preliminary, investigation into the use of cognitive features for sentiment analysis. The novelty of the approach is commendable, and the paper is well-presented. However, the lack of a strong theoretical justification for the hypothesized link between eye movements and sentiment, coupled with the marginal performance improvements and a less-than-compelling feasibility demonstration, limit the overall impact. 

**Recommendations:**

* **Expand the Motivation:**  Provide a more robust theoretical grounding for the use of cognitive features, perhaps drawing on cognitive psychology literature regarding the relationship between attention, emotional processing, and language comprehension.
* **Statistical Rigor:** Conduct a more rigorous statistical analysis to determine the significance of the observed improvements.
* **Illustrative Examples:**  Replace or augment the example in Section 7.2 with a more persuasive demonstration of the technique’s utility in a real-world scenario where cognitive features offer a clear advantage. Consider exploring scenarios where subtle emotional cues or nuanced language understanding are crucial.
* **Error Analysis:** A detailed error analysis, identifying cases where the cognitive features *specifically* contributed to correct classifications or failed to prevent misclassifications, would provide valuable insight.
