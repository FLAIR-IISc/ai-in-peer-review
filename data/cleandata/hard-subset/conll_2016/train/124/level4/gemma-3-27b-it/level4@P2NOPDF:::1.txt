This paper presents a novel and intriguing approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotator eye-tracking data. The authors assert this is the first work to integrate such features into the NLP pipeline. 

**Strengths:**

1. The paper is clearly written and generally easy to understand.
2. The core idea is highly interesting and has the potential to inspire further research across a range of NLP tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The motivation for utilizing cognitive features in sentiment analysis requires further justification. While these features may plausibly reflect aspects of reading difficulty or cognitive load, the connection to accurately identifying sentiment polarity is not sufficiently established. The paper would benefit from a more detailed explanation of *how* these features are expected to contribute to sentiment understanding.

2. The performance improvement achieved by adding cognitive features appears marginal. The comparison between results using Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr suggests a limited gain, raising questions about the practical impact of this approach. A more substantial and statistically significant improvement would strengthen the findings.

3. The discussion of the approach’s feasibility in Section 7 is not entirely convincing. Specifically, the example provided in Section 7.2 does not clearly demonstrate the benefits of this technique in the presented scenario. The rationale for how eye-tracking data would be beneficial in that particular case is unclear, and a more compelling demonstration would be needed to support the authors’ claims about broader applicability.
