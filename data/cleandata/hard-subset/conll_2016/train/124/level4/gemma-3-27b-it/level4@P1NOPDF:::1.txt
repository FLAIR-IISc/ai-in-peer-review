This paper presents a very interesting idea: leveraging cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. Specifically, the authors record eye-movement patterns of human annotators to derive a novel set of features. They claim this is the first work to incorporate cognitive features into the NLP pipeline.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
2. The core idea is highly interesting and has the potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis isn’t fully justified. While I can see how these features might reflect reading ease, the connection to detecting sentiment polarity isn't clear.
2. The performance improvement gained by incorporating cognitive features appears marginal, as evidenced by the comparison between results using Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of their approach in Section 7, I remain unconvinced. Specifically, the example provided in Section 7.2 doesn’t demonstrate a clear benefit of this technique; it’s not obvious why it would be helpful in that scenario.
