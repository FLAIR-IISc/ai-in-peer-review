This paper proposes an interesting approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors claim this is the first work to integrate such features into the NLP pipeline.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
2. The core idea is novel and has the potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The motivation for utilizing cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not strongly justified. While these features may reflect reading ease, the connection to detecting sentiment polarity remains unclear.
2. The performance improvement gained by incorporating cognitive features—as evidenced by the comparison between Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr—is marginal.
3. Despite the discussion of feasibility in Section 7, I remain unconvinced, particularly regarding the example presented in Section 7.2. The rationale for how this technique would be helpful in that specific scenario is not persuasive.
