This paper presents a novel and intriguing approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotators’ eye-movement patterns. The authors position this work as a pioneering effort in integrating cognitive data within the NLP field.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper is clearly written and readily understandable.
2. The central idea is highly innovative and has the potential to stimulate further research across various NLP applications.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The rationale for leveraging cognitive features specifically for sentiment analysis requires further elaboration. While these features may offer insights into reading effort, the connection to identifying sentiment polarity remains unclear.
2. The performance gains achieved through the inclusion of cognitive features appear limited, as evidenced by the modest difference between results using Sn+Sr+Gz versus Sn+Sr.
3. The feasibility discussion in Section 7 does not fully address my concerns. Specifically, the example presented in Section 7.2 fails to convincingly demonstrate the utility of this technique in the described scenario.
