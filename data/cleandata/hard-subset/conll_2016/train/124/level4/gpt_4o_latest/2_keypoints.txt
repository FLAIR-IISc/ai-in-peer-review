- Paper proposes using eye-tracking features as cognitive signals for sentiment analysis; the idea is intriguing.
- The feature descriptions and comparisons are clear and informative.
- The feasibility of the proposed approach is thoroughly addressed (Section 7).
- Suggestion: avoid conflating domains (movie reviews vs. tweets); training and testing should be separated by domain to potentially improve accuracy and interpretability.
- Results in Table 2 are relatively low versus Pang & Lee state-of-the-art but more competitive on Twitter data.
- Clarification requested: ensure no overlapping snippets between training and testing for datasets 1 and 2, even if from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment140).
- Minor: excessive use of bold text is somewhat distracting.
