This paper presents a novel and intriguing approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotator eye-movement patterns. The authors posit that this is a pioneering effort to integrate such features within the NLP field.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper is clearly written and readily understandable.
2. The core idea is highly innovative and has the potential to stimulate further research across various NLP applications.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The rationale for leveraging cognitive features in sentiment analysis requires further substantiation. While these features may correlate with reading effort, the connection to discerning sentiment polarity remains unclear.
2. The performance gains achieved through the inclusion of cognitive features appear limited, as evidenced by the modest difference between results using Sn+Sr+Gz versus Sn+Sr.
3. The discussion of the approach’s broader feasibility in Section 7 is not entirely persuasive. Specifically, the example provided in Section 7.2 does not convincingly demonstrate the benefits of this technique in the presented scenario.
