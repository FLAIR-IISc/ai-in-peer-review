This paper explores the incorporation of eye-tracking data as cognitive features to enhance sentiment analysis, a promising and interesting approach. 

The presentation of features and the comparison of different feature set configurations are well-articulated and provide valuable insight. The inclusion of a feasibility analysis in Section 7 is also a strength of the work.

I suggest considering the potential benefits of evaluating the approach on datasets separated by domain. Specifically, training and testing on distinct corpora (e.g., movie reviews vs. tweets) may lead to improved performance and more interpretable results for each domain. The reported performance in Table 2 appears relatively low when benchmarked against state-of-the-art results on the Pang and Lee dataset, but is more competitive when considered in the context of Twitter sentiment analysis.

Regarding data handling, could the authors confirm that there is no overlap between the training and testing data for datasets 1 and 2, even when originating from the same sources (such as Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140)? 

Finally, a minor point: the frequent use of bold text is somewhat distracting and could be reduced.
