This paper proposes an interesting idea: using cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. Specifically, the authors record human annotators’ eye-movement patterns to derive a new set of features. They claim this is the first work to incorporate cognitive features into NLP.

Strengths:
1. The paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
2. The idea is intriguing and may inspire research on other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
1. The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not well justified. While such features might correlate with reading ease or processing difficulty, it is unclear why they should help determine sentiment polarity.
2. The performance gains from adding cognitive features are marginal, as shown by the comparison of Sn+Sr+Gz with Sn+Sr.
3. Although Section 7 discusses feasibility, I remain unconvinced. In particular, the example in Section 7.2 does not demonstrate why this technique would be helpful in that scenario.
