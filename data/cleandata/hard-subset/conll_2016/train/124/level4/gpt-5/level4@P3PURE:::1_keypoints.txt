- Proposes using human eye-movement (cognitive) features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
- Claims to be the first work to incorporate cognitive features into NLP models.
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.
- Idea is novel and could inspire research in other NLP tasks.
- Motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is weak and the link to sentiment polarity is unclear.
- Performance gains from adding cognitive features are marginal (Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr).
- Feasibility argument in Section 7 is unconvincing.
- Example in Section 7.2 does not clearly demonstrate the technique's usefulness.
