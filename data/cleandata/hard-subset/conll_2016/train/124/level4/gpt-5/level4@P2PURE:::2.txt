This paper introduces eye-tracking features for sentiment analysis as a form of cognitive signal. The idea of using eye-tracking as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis is interesting. The discussion of the features and the comparison of feature sets are clear and informative, and I appreciate that Section 7 addresses feasibility.

For evaluation, I wonder whether it would be better not to conflate domains—for example, mixing the movie review corpus with the tweet corpus. Excluding tweets from training when predicting movie reviews (and vice versa) might improve within-domain performance and would also make the results easier to interpret. In Table 2, the reported scores appear rather low relative to state-of-the-art results on the Pang and Lee dataset, but they look much more competitive when compared with results on Twitter data.

Regarding Section 3.3, please confirm that there are no overlapping snippets between the training and test sets for datasets 1 and 2, even though they are drawn from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140).

Minor: the extensive use of boldface is somewhat distracting (or perhaps that’s just me).
