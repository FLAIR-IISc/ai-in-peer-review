- Paper proposes using eye-tracking features as cognitive signals for sentiment analysis; idea is interesting and well-motivated.
- Feature descriptions and comparisons across feature sets are clear and informative.
- Authors appropriately discuss feasibility of the approach (Section 7).
- Concern: datasets conflate domains (movie reviews vs tweets); training on mixed domains may hurt performance and interpretability.
- Observation: reported results (Table 2) look low compared to state-of-the-art on Pang & Lee data but more competitive on Twitter data.
- Request: confirm there are no overlapping snippets between training and test sets for datasets 1 and 2 despite shared sources.
- Minor: excessive use of boldface is distracting.
