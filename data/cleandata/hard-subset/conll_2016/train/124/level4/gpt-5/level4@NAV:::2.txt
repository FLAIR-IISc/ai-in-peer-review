This paper proposes using eye-tracking features for sentiment analysis as a form of cognitive signal. I find the idea of leveraging eye-tracking as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis to be an interesting one.

The discussion of the features and the comparison across feature sets are clear and very useful. I also appreciate that the feasibility of the approach is considered in Section 7.

I wonder whether the evaluation would benefit from not mixing domains across datasets, e.g., the movie review corpus and the tweet corpus. For one, predictions on movie reviews (resp. tweets) might improve if tweets (resp. movie reviews) were excluded from training. It would also make the results easier to interpret. The numbers in Table 2 seem rather low relative to state-of-the-art results on the Pang and Lee data, but look considerably better when compared to results reported for Twitter data.

In Section 3.3, there are no overlapping snippets between the training and test sets for datasets 1 and 2, correct? Even if they come from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140).
