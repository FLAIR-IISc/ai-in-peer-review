This paper proposes incorporating eye-tracking signals as cognitive features for sentiment analysis. Using gaze-derived indicators as a proxy for cognitive load in polarity prediction is a compelling idea.

The presentation of the feature inventory and the comparisons across feature sets are clear and informative. I also appreciate that the paper explicitly discusses feasibility in Section 7.

It might strengthen the evaluation to avoid mixing domains (e.g., movie reviews and tweets) within training and testing. Isolating domains could improve in-domain performance and make results easier to interpret. As presented, the scores in Table 2 look low relative to state-of-the-art numbers on the Pang and Lee dataset, but are more competitive when viewed against typical Twitter baselines.

In Section 3.3, please confirm there is no overlap between training snippets and the test instances in Datasets 1 and 2, even if they originate from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee, Sentiment140).

Minor: the extensive use of boldface is somewhat distracting (possibly just my preference).
