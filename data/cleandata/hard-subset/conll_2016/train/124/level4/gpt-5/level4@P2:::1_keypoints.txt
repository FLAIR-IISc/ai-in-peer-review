- Empirical contribution: Gaze-derived cognitive features added to textual features yield small but statistically significant F-score gains on two datasets (max +0.6 and +1.5 F).
- Feature contribution: Introduces a concrete set of gaze features (basic and graph-based “gaze-saliency” measures) integrated into a standard supervised sentiment-analysis pipeline.
- Targeted benefit: Gaze features show larger improvements on complex phenomena (irony/sarcasm) in a held-out evaluation.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well written and clearly organized with straightforward descriptions of datasets, features, and models.
- Strength — novelty: Leveraging eye-tracking for cognitive signals is an interesting, potentially influential idea for pragmatics-related NLP tasks.
- Strength — methodology: Evaluation across two datasets, multiple classifiers, significance testing, and feature-importance analysis is reasonable and thorough.
- Weakness — motivation: Paper does not convincingly explain why gaze signals should predict sentiment polarity (vs. only reading difficulty or ambiguity).
- Weakness — marginal gains: Improvements over strong textual baselines are modest, limiting practical impact given added complexity and data needs.
- Weakness — feasibility: Deployment scenarios (mobile eye-tracking) are not convincingly practical; privacy, consent, device variability, and in-the-wild data collection are underaddressed.
- Question: Can gaze contributions be disentangled from readability and length effects (e.g., via partial correlations or covariate regression)?
- Question: Which specific error types are corrected uniquely by gaze features — provide representative examples showing gaze-driven corrections.
- Question: How robust are gaze features across hardware (SR Research vs. Tobii) and sampling rates, and do gains persist across devices?
- Question: How do gaze features compare against stronger text-only baselines (e.g., recent neural models) or ensembles including them?
- Question: What are anticipated costs, scale, privacy safeguards, and expected data-quality drop-off for practical deployment?
- Overall: Creative and carefully executed initial evidence that gaze features can help, especially for complex cases, but conceptual justification, practical impact, and deployment feasibility need stronger support.
