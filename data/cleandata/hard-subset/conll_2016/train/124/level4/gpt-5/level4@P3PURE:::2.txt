This paper introduces eye-tracking features for sentiment analysis as a type of cognitive feature. The idea of using eye-tracking as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis is interesting.

The discussion of features and the comparison of feature sets are clear and helpful. I also appreciate that the feasibility of the approach is addressed in Section 7.

One concern is that the evaluation conflates different domains (e.g., the movie review corpus and the tweet corpus). Separating these domains could help in two ways: first, it might improve prediction for movie reviews if tweets are not included in training (and vice versa); second, it would make the results easier to interpret. As written, the results in Table 2 appear rather low compared to state-of-the-art results for the Pang and Lee data, but look much better when compared to results for Twitter data.

In Section 3.3, please confirm that there are no overlapping snippets between the training and testing data of datasets 1 and 2, even if they come from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment140).

Minor: some of the extra use of boldface is distracting (or maybe it’s just me).
