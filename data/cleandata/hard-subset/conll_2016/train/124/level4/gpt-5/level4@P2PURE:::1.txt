This paper proposes an intriguing approach that leverages cognitive features—specifically, eye-movement patterns recorded from human annotators—to derive new features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. The authors claim this is the first work in NLP to incorporate cognitive features.

Strengths:
- The paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
- The idea is interesting and may inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
- The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not well justified. While these features may reflect reading ease, it is unclear why they would help detect sentiment polarity.
- The performance improvement after adding cognitive features is marginal; Sn+Sr+Gz shows little gain over Sn+Sr.
- Although the feasibility is discussed in Section 7, I remain unconvinced. In particular, the example in Section 7.2 does not make clear why this technique would be helpful in that scenario.
