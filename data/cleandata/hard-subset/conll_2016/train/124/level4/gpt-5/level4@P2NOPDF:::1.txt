This paper proposes an intriguing approach that incorporates cognitive features—specifically, eye-movement patterns from human annotators—into sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. The authors claim this is the first NLP work to integrate such cognitive signals.

Strengths:
- The paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
- The idea is novel and could inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
- The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not sufficiently justified. While these features may reflect reading difficulty or attention, the connection to sentiment polarity detection is unclear and not convincingly argued.
- The performance gains from adding cognitive features are marginal, as seen when comparing Sn+Sr+Gz to Sn+Sr.
- Although Section 7 discusses feasibility, the argument is unconvincing. In particular, the example in Section 7.2 does not clearly demonstrate how the proposed technique would be beneficial in that scenario.
