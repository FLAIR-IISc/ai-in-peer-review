- Summary: Proposes using eye-tracking–derived gaze features as cognitive signals to improve sentiment analysis, with clear feature discussion and feasibility considerations.  
- Strength: Novel contribution linking gaze-based cognitive signals with NLP sentiment tasks.  
- Strength: Clear and well-presented feature engineering and comparisons across feature families.  
- Strength: Thoughtful feasibility discussion on collecting or approximating eye-tracking in practice (Section 7).  
- Concern: Domain conflation—mixing movie reviews and tweets likely obscures performance and interpretation.  
- Request: Report in-domain results separately (train/test on movie reviews only; train/test on tweets only).  
- Request: Report cross-domain transfer experiments (train on one domain, test on the other) to assess robustness.  
- Note: Pooled-domain results can be kept but per-domain results should be primary for interpretability.  
- Concern: Table 2 results need context—numbers look low versus Pang & Lee SOTA but competitive on Twitter.  
- Request: Provide direct comparisons to strong text-only baselines under the same splits (e.g., bag-of-ngrams + linear models or neural baselines).  
- Request: Include ablation studies quantifying incremental benefit of gaze features over text-only models.  
- Request: Compare against established benchmarks or cite comparable-split results to frame improvement magnitudes.  
- Concern: Confirm train/test separation and absence of overlapping or near-duplicate snippets; describe deduplication if needed.  
- Request: Report statistical significance for differences among feature sets and gaze improvements (e.g., paired bootstrap, McNemar).  
- Concern: Control for confounds like sentence length and lexical difficulty that affect both gaze and sentiment.  
- Request: Clarify how multi-reader gaze data were aggregated and whether normalization for individual reading speed was applied.  
- Request: Discuss data efficiency—how much gaze data is required and how performance scales with less gaze supervision.  
- Feasibility suggestion: Consider using predicted gaze (text-only models trained on a small gaze subset) to scale to larger corpora.  
- Feasibility suggestion: Consider substituting cognitive proxies (reading times, surprisal) and compare their utility to real gaze features.  
- Question: Confirm no overlap between training and test snippets in Datasets 1 and 2, including near-duplicates.  
- Question: How many readers contributed eye-tracking per instance and how were inter-reader differences handled?  
- Question: Are gaze features normalized for individual differences and text length?  
- Question: Do gaze-feature gains persist across both domains in in-domain evaluations?  
- Minor: Reduce excessive boldface; reserve for key takeaways.  
- Minor: Ensure tables clearly indicate domains, splits, and whether results are in-domain, cross-domain, or pooled.  
- Minor: If space allows, add a short error analysis illustrating cases where gaze features help (e.g., sarcasm, negation).  
- Overall assessment: Well-motivated with clear feature design, but experimental setup conflating domains and lacking stronger baselines/ablations limits interpretability and impact.
