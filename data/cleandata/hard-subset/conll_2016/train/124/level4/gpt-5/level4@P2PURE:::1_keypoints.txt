- Proposes using cognitive features (eye-movement patterns from annotators) to derive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection; claims to be first NLP work using cognitive features.
- Paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
- The idea is interesting and may inspire research in other NLP tasks.
- Motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is weak—unclear why reading/ease-related signals would indicate sentiment polarity.
- Performance gains from adding cognitive features are marginal; Sn+Sr+Gz shows little improvement over Sn+Sr.
- Feasibility remains unconvincing despite discussion in Section 7; the example in Section 7.2 fails to demonstrate clear usefulness.
