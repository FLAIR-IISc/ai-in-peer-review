Review- This paper presents a compelling idea of leveraging cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. More precisely, it captures eye-movement patterns from human annotators to derive a novel feature set. The authors state that this is the first work to bring cognitive features into the NLP community.
Strength:
1. The paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
2. The idea is intriguing and may inspire research across other NLP tasks.
Weakness:
1. The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not well substantiated. While these features might reflect reading ease, it is unclear why they would help determine sentiment polarity.
2. The improvement after adding cognitive features is marginal, as shown by comparing Sn+Sr+Gz with Sn+Sr.
3. Although the authors discuss feasibility in Section 7, I am not persuaded, especially by the example in Section 7.2; it is not clear how this technique is beneficial in that scenario.
