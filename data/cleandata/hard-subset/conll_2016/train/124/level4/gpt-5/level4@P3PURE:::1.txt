This paper proposes an interesting idea: leveraging cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. Specifically, the authors record the eye-movement patterns of human annotators to derive a new set of features. They claim this is the first work to incorporate cognitive features into NLP models.

Strengths:
- The paper is generally well written and easy to follow.
- The idea is novel and may inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
- The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not well justified. While such features may capture reading ease or processing difficulty, the connection to detecting sentiment polarity is unclear.
- The performance gains from adding cognitive features appear marginal, as evidenced by the comparison of Sn+Sr+Gz with Sn+Sr.
- Although Section 7 discusses feasibility, the argument is unconvincing. In particular, the example in Section 7.2 does not clearly demonstrate why this technique would be helpful in that scenario.
