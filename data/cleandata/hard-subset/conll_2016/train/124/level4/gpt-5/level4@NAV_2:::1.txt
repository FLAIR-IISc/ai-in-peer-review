This paper introduces an intriguing approach: augmenting sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection with cognitive features derived from readers’ eye-movement patterns. Specifically, the authors record annotators’ gaze behavior and convert it into a feature set used alongside traditional linguistic features. They claim, to the best of their knowledge, that this is the first work in NLP to incorporate such cognitive signals.

Strengths:
1. The manuscript is clearly written and straightforward to follow.
2. The central idea is novel and thought-provoking, with potential to stimulate research across other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
1. The motivation for applying cognitive features to sentiment polarity classification is not sufficiently substantiated. While these signals may reflect reading difficulty or processing effort, the link to reliably inferring positive versus negative sentiment remains unclear.
2. The incremental gains from adding gaze features appear small when comparing Sn+Sr+Gz against Sn+Sr.
3. Although Section 7 discusses feasibility, the application scenario in Section 7.2 is not convincing; it is hard to see how the proposed technique would concretely help in that example.
