- Novelty: Using eye-tracking features as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis is an interesting idea.
- Clarity: Feature descriptions and comparisons across feature sets are clear and useful; feasibility is appropriately discussed (Section 7).
- Evaluation concern: Avoid mixing domains across datasets (movies vs tweets); excluding cross-domain examples from training could improve performance and interpretation.
- Results: Table 2 scores look low relative to Pang & Lee state-of-the-art but compare better to reported Twitter results.
- Data question: Confirm that datasets 1 and 2 have no overlapping snippets between training and test sets, even if from the same sources.
- Minor issue: Excessive use of boldface is distracting.
