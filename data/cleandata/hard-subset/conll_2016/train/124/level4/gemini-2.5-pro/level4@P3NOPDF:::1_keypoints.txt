- Novel approach: introduces cognitive features from eye-tracking for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection, claimed as first of its kind.  
- High potential impact: creative idea that could inspire new research directions linking human cognition to NLP.  
- Clear presentation: paper is well-written with an understandable methodology, data collection, and experiments.  
- Weak motivation: insufficient theoretical justification or psycholinguistic evidence tying gaze patterns specifically to sentiment labeling rather than processing difficulty.  
- Marginal empirical gains: only modest performance improvements from gaze features, raising doubts about their practical utility versus collection cost.  
- Unconvincing feasibility: scalability and real-world applicability are underdeveloped; proposed use-case scenarios and alternatives to direct eye-tracking are not compelling.
