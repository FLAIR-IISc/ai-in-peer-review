- Novel approach: augments linguistic features with cognitive features from eye-tracking to improve sentiment analysis, especially for complex constructs like sarcasm and irony.  
- Empirical gains: engineered gaze features from two datasets yield up to 9.3% F-score improvement over textual baselines and help on a held-out ironic-text set.  
- Contribution: introduces a novel set of cognitive (gaze) features, from basic fixation/regression stats to graph-based gaze-saliency metrics.  
- Contribution: provides empirical evidence that cognitive features help handle sarcasm, irony, and thwarted expectations.  
- Strength: idea is novel and bridges psycholinguistics with computational sentiment analysis.  
- Strength: clear, detailed feature engineering with logical feature categories and an ablation study showing incremental value.  
- Strength: proactive discussion of practical feasibility and deployment (mobile eye-trackers, user consent).  
- Weakness: evaluation mixes domains (reviews, tweets, quotes), making results hard to interpret and compare to prior work.  
- Weakness: mixed-domain training risks negative transfer; domain-specific evaluations are needed to assess true effectiveness and generalizability.  
- Question for authors: confirm there is no overlap between the baseline training corpus (10,662 snippets) and the eye-tracking test instances to rule out data leakage.  
- Additional comment: excessive use of boldface is visually distracting.
