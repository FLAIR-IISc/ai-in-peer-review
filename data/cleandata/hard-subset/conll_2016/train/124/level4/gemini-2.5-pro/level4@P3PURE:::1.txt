This paper proposes the novel idea of using cognitive features for the tasks of sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. Specifically, the authors derive a new set of features from the eye-movement patterns of human annotators recorded during the reading process. The authors claim this is the first work to incorporate such features into the NLP domain.

**Strengths:**
1. The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
2. The core idea is novel and has the potential to inspire further research in other NLP tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The motivation for using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not sufficiently justified. While it is plausible that these features reflect processing difficulty or reading ease, the connection to detecting sentiment polarity is not clearly established by the authors.
2. The empirical results show that the inclusion of cognitive features yields only marginal improvements. Specifically, the performance gain when comparing the Sn+Sr+Gz model with the Sn+Sr baseline is minimal.
3. The discussion on the feasibility of the proposed approach (Section 7) is unconvincing. In particular, the example provided in Section 7.2 does not clearly demonstrate how this technique would be beneficial in the described scenario.
