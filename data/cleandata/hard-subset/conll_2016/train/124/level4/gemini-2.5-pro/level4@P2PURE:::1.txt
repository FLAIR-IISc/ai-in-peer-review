This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection that incorporates cognitive features derived from the eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors claim this is the first work to introduce such features to the NLP community.

**Strengths:**
1.  The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and easy to follow.
2.  The core idea of leveraging cognitive signals is innovative and could inspire new lines of research in other NLP tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
1.  The motivation for using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not sufficiently justified. While it is plausible that these features reflect reading ease or processing difficulty, the paper does not establish a clear link between them and the detection of sentiment polarity.
2.  The empirical results demonstrate only a marginal performance improvement from the inclusion of cognitive features, as shown in the comparison between the 'Sn+Sr' and 'Sn+Sr+Gz' models.
3.  The discussion in Section 7 regarding the practical feasibility of the approach is unconvincing. Specifically, the example provided in Section 7.2 fails to demonstrate the utility of this technique in the described scenario.
