- Introduces eye-tracking features for sentiment analysis as a novel proxy for cognitive load.  
- Core idea is interesting and original.  
- Discussion and comparison of feature sets are clear and helpful.  
- Inclusion of Section 7 on practical feasibility is appreciated.  
- Main concern: evaluation conflates different domains (movie reviews and tweets) into single train/test sets, making results hard to interpret.  
- Drawback 1: Single-domain performance may be improved by training on in-domain data rather than mixed-domain data.  
- Drawback 2: Mixed-domain setup complicates comparison with prior work (e.g., Table 2 looks low vs. Pang & Lee but more competitive vs. Twitter results).  
- Recommendation: perform domain-specific evaluation for clearer contribution and direct benchmark comparisons.  
- Clarifying question: confirm there are no overlapping text snippets between training and testing sets for datasets 1 and 2 from the same original sources.  
- Minor point: excessive bold formatting is distracting.
