- Well-structured and clearly written paper with methods and experiments easy to follow.  
- Innovative idea of using cognitive (eye-movement) signals for sentiment and sarcasm detection, potentially inspiring broader NLP work.  
- Weak motivation: insufficient theoretical justification linking eye-tracking features to sentiment polarity or sarcasm beyond general reading difficulty.  
- Marginal empirical gains: gaze-based features give only modest improvement over syntactic/semantic baselines, raising questions about practical utility.  
- Scalability/feasibility concerns: reliance on specialized eye-tracking for annotators and an unconvincing deployment scenario limit real-world applicability.
