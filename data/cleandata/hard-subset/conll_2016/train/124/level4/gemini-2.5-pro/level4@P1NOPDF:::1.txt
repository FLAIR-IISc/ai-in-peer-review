This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features. Specifically, the authors derive a new feature set from the eye-movement patterns of human annotators recorded during reading. The authors state that this is the first work to introduce such cognitive features to the NLP field.

**Strengths:**

1.  The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.
2.  The central idea is novel and intriguing, with the potential to inspire new lines of research for other NLP applications.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not sufficiently justified. While one can imagine how these features might reflect reading ease or text complexity, the paper does not establish a clear theoretical link between eye-movement patterns and the detection of sentiment polarity.
2.  The reported performance improvement from adding cognitive features is marginal. A direct comparison between the `Sn+Sr` and `Sn+Sr+Gz` models reveals only a minor gain, which calls into question the practical value of these features, especially considering the significant overhead required for data collection.
3.  The authors' discussion of the approach's real-world feasibility in Section 7 is not persuasive. Specifically, the example given in Section 7.2 fails to convincingly demonstrate the utility of this technique in the proposed scenario.
