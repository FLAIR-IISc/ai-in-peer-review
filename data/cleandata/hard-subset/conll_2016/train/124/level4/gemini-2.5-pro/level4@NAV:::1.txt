This paper introduces a novel and interesting approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features. The core of their method involves deriving a new feature set from the recorded eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors assert that their contribution is the first of its kind to integrate cognitive features within the field of Natural Language Processing.

**Strengths:**
1. The manuscript is well-structured and clearly written, making it straightforward to comprehend.
2. The central idea is highly original and has the potential to stimulate new lines of inquiry in other areas of NLP.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The rationale for applying cognitive features to sentiment analysis lacks sufficient justification. While these features could plausibly capture aspects of reading difficulty, their direct relevance to determining sentiment polarity is not clearly established.
2. The empirical results show only a marginal performance gain. Specifically, the comparison between the Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr models demonstrates that the addition of cognitive features yields minimal improvement.
3. The discussion on the practical feasibility of the proposed method in Section 7 is unconvincing. The specific example provided in Section 7.2, in particular, fails to adequately demonstrate the utility of this technique in a real-world scenario.
