- Introduces a novel, well-motivated approach using eye-tracking features as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis.  
- Exploration and comparison of cognitive/feature sets are clear and insightful.  
- Section 7’s discussion of practical feasibility is a strong, valuable addition.  
- Major concern: datasets conflate domains (movie reviews and tweets); training/testing should be done separately by domain to avoid negative transfer.  
- Domain-specific evaluation would make results easier to interpret and enable fairer comparison with prior work (current Table 2 is hard to contextualize).  
- Request clarification (Section 3.3): confirm no overlapping text snippets between training and testing for datasets 1 and 2 despite shared sources.  
- Minor: excessive use of bold formatting is distracting and may hinder readability.
