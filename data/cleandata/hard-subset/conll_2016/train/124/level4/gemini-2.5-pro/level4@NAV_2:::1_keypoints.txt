- Introduces using eye-tracking–derived cognitive features alongside text for sentiment analysis.
- Hypothesizes that gaze signals help interpret linguistically complex texts (sarcasm, irony).
- Shows gaze features improve F-score on two datasets.
- Contribution: new modality bridging psycholinguistics and computational linguistics for sentiment tasks.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that gaze+linguistic features yield performance gains, especially on sarcastic/ironic text.
- Strength: highly novel idea with potential to inspire similar work in other NLP tasks.
- Strength: thorough experimental design—two datasets, multiple classifiers, ablation studies, feature significance, and irony-focused analysis.
- Strength: clear presentation and detailed feature descriptions enabling understanding and replication.
- Weakness: modest performance gains (≈0.6% and 1.5% F-score), raising cost–benefit concerns given eye-tracking effort.
- Weakness: unclear mechanistic link between specific gaze patterns and sentiment polarity beyond signaling processing difficulty.
- Weakness: practical feasibility limited by dependence on eye-tracking data and reliance on speculative future hardware.
- Question: please provide more analysis on how specific gaze features differentiate positive vs. negative sentiment in non-sarcastic texts.
- Question: consider transfer learning or distillation—can models trained with gaze be converted to text-only models that generalize to new data?
