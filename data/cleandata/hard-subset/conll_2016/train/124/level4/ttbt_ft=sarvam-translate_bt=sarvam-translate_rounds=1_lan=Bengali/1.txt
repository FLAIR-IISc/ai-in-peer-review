This paper proposes a very interesting idea of using cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. More specifically, the authors record the eye movements of human commentators to create new features. The authors claim that this is the first time cognitive features have been incorporated into the NLP community.

Strengths:
1. The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
2. A very interesting idea that could inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
1. The purpose of using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not very well supported. I can imagine that these features could help reflect readability, but I don't understand why they are helpful in identifying sentiment polarity.
2. The improvement is slight after considering cognitive features by comparing Sn+Sr with Sn+Sr.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of the method in section 7, I am not sure, especially about the examples given in section 7.2. I don't understand why this technique is helpful in this situation.
