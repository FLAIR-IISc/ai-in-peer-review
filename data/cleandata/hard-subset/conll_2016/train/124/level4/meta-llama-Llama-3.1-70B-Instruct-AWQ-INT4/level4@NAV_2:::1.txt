This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from the eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors claim that this is the first work to integrate cognitive features into the NLP community.

**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions:**

The paper addresses the challenges of sentiment analysis at lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic levels. The authors propose augmenting traditional features with cognitive features obtained from eye-tracking data to improve sentiment polarity detection. The main contributions of this work are:

1. Introduction of cognitive features from eye-movement patterns to enhance sentiment analysis.
2. Development of a feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features.
3. Experimental evaluation of the proposed approach on two datasets, demonstrating improved performance over traditional feature sets.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
2. The idea of incorporating cognitive features into NLP tasks is innovative and has the potential to inspire further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not thoroughly justified. While these features may reflect reading ease, their relevance to detecting sentiment polarities is not clearly established.
2. The performance improvement achieved by incorporating cognitive features is marginal, as seen in the comparison between Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr.
3. The discussion on the feasibility of the approach in Section 7 is not convincing, particularly with regards to the example provided in Section 7.2. The benefits of using this technique in such a scenario are not clearly demonstrated.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more insight into the relationship between cognitive features and sentiment polarities? How do you envision these features capturing nuances in sentiment expression?
2. How do you plan to address the marginal performance improvement achieved by incorporating cognitive features? Are there any potential modifications or extensions to the approach that could enhance its effectiveness?
3. Can you elaborate on the example provided in Section 7.2? How do you envision this technique being applied in real-world scenarios, and what benefits do you expect it to bring?
