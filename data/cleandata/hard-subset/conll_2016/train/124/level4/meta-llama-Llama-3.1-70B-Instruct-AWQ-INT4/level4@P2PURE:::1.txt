This paper proposes an intriguing idea of incorporating cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. Specifically, the authors utilize the eye-movement patterns of human annotators to derive a novel set of features, claiming to be the first to integrate cognitive features into the NLP community.

Strengths:
1. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.
2. The concept presented is innovative and has the potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:
1. The motivation behind using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not adequately justified. While these features may reflect reading ease, it is unclear how they contribute to detecting sentiment polarities.
2. The improvement achieved by considering cognitive features, as shown by comparing Sn+Sr+Gz with Sn+Sr, is marginal.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of their approach in Section 7, the provided example in Section 7.2 is unconvincing, and it is unclear how this technique would be beneficial in such a scenario.
