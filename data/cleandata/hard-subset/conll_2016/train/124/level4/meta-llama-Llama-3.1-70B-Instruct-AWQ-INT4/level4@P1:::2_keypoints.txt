- Proposes using eye-tracking features as cognitive signals to improve sentiment analysis.  
- Novel idea: eye-tracking as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment classification.  
- Clear, thorough explanation and comparison of feature sets (sentiment, sarcasm/irony, eye-movement, reading difficulty).  
- Experimental setup and results are well-presented and easy to follow.  
- Section 7 addresses feasibility, applications, and limitations of using eye-tracking features.  
- Datasets mix different domains (e.g., movie reviews and tweets), which may harm generalization and accuracy.  
- Recommends evaluating model performance separately on movie reviews and tweets for clearer interpretation.  
- Asks whether there are overlapping snippets between training and testing data across datasets 1 and 2.  
- Minor distraction: excessive bold formatting in the paper.  
- Main contributions summarized: introduction of eye-tracking features, comprehensive feature comparison, and discussion of feasibility.
