- Paper introduces a novel sentiment analysis and sarcasm-detection method using cognitive features from annotators' eye-movements and claims to be first to do so in NLP.
- Strength: The manuscript is well-structured and clearly written, making the approach easy to follow.
- Strength: The idea could inspire follow-up research and offer a fresh perspective for other NLP tasks.
- Weakness: The paper does not convincingly justify why cognitive/eye-movement features are relevant specifically for detecting sentiment polarity.
- Weakness: The empirical gains from adding cognitive features (Sn+Sr+Gz vs. Sn+Sr) are relatively small.
- Weakness: The feasibility discussion (Section 7), particularly the example in Section 7.2, is unpersuasive and leaves doubts about practical usefulness.
