- Proposes using cognitive features from eye-tracking to improve sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
- Combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive (eye-movement) features into a unified feature set.
- Evaluated on two datasets and reports improved performance over traditional feature sets.
- Paper is well-written and accessible.
- Introducing cognitive features into NLP is an innovative idea with potential to inspire further work.
- Motivation for why cognitive features should reflect sentiment polarity is insufficiently justified.
- Reported performance improvements are marginal (e.g., Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr).
- Feasibility discussion, especially the example in Section 7.2, is unconvincing and benefits are unclear.
- Request clarification on how cognitive features relate to and capture sentiment nuances.
- Ask for plans to address marginal gains and possible extensions to boost effectiveness.
- Ask for elaboration on Section 7.2 example, real-world application, and expected benefits.
