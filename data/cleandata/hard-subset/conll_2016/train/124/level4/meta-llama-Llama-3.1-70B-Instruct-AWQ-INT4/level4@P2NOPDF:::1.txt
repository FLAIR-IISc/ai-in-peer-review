This paper proposes an intriguing idea of incorporating cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. Specifically, the authors record the eye-movement patterns of human annotators to derive a novel set of features. They claim that this is the first work to integrate cognitive features into the NLP community.

Strengths:

1. The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
2. The idea of utilizing cognitive features is innovative and has the potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:

1. The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not adequately justified. While these features may reflect reading ease, it is unclear why they are beneficial for detecting sentiment polarities. A more thorough explanation of the connection between cognitive features and sentiment analysis would strengthen the paper.
2. The improvement achieved by incorporating cognitive features is marginal, as evident from the comparison between Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr. This raises questions about the significance of the proposed approach.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of their approach in Section 7, the example provided in Section 7.2 is unconvincing. It is unclear why this technique would be helpful in such a scenario, and further clarification is needed to demonstrate the practicality of the proposed method.

Overall, while the paper presents an interesting idea, it requires more thorough justification and convincing results to demonstrate the effectiveness of cognitive features in sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
