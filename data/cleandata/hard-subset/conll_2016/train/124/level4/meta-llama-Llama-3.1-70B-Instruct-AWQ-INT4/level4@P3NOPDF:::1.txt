This paper proposes an intriguing idea of leveraging cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. The authors record the eye-movement patterns of human annotators to derive a novel set of features, claiming that this is the first work to incorporate cognitive features into the NLP community.

Strengths:

1. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
2. The idea of utilizing cognitive features is innovative and has the potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:

1. The motivation behind using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not thoroughly justified. While these features may reflect reading ease, it is unclear why they are beneficial for detecting sentiment polarities. A more comprehensive explanation of the underlying cognitive processes and their connection to sentiment analysis would strengthen the paper.
2. The improvement achieved by incorporating cognitive features is marginal, as evident from the comparison of Sn+Sr+Gz with Sn+Sr. This raises questions about the practical significance of the proposed approach.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of their approach in Section 7, the argument is not entirely convincing. The example provided in Section 7.2 is particularly unconvincing, as it is unclear why this technique would be helpful in such a scenario. A more compelling demonstration of the approach's feasibility and potential applications would be beneficial.

Additional suggestions:

* To enhance the paper's impact, the authors could provide more detailed information about the cognitive processes underlying their approach and how they relate to sentiment analysis.
* A more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with state-of-the-art approaches, would help to establish its effectiveness.
* The authors may want to consider providing more concrete examples or case studies to illustrate the potential benefits and applications of their approach.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting idea, but its impact is limited by the marginal improvement achieved and the lack of convincing justification for the use of cognitive features in sentiment analysis. With further development and refinement, this approach has the potential to contribute meaningfully to the NLP community.
