This paper proposes an intriguing idea of incorporating cognitive features into sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. Specifically, the authors record the eye-movement patterns of human annotators to derive a novel set of features. They claim that this is the first work to integrate cognitive features into the NLP community.

Strengths:

1. The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
2. The idea of utilizing cognitive features is very interesting and may inspire research in other NLP tasks, potentially leading to innovative approaches.

Weaknesses:

1. The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not thoroughly justified. While these features may help reflect reading ease, it is unclear why they are beneficial for detecting sentiment polarities. A more convincing explanation is needed to support the inclusion of cognitive features in this context.
2. The improvement achieved by considering cognitive features is marginal, as evident from the comparison between Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr. This raises questions about the significance of incorporating cognitive features into the model.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of their approach in Section 7, the example provided in Section 7.2 is not convincing. It is unclear why this technique would be helpful in such a scenario, and further clarification is necessary to demonstrate its practical applications.

Overall, while the paper presents an innovative idea, it requires more robust justification and convincing results to demonstrate the effectiveness of cognitive features in sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
