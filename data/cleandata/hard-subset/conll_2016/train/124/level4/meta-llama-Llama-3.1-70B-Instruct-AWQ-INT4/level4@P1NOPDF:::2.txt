This paper introduces eye-tracking features as a type of cognitive feature for sentiment analysis, a notion that I find intriguing. The idea of leveraging eye-tracking features as a proxy for cognitive load in sentiment analysis is particularly interesting.

The discussion on the features and comparison of feature sets is clear and very helpful. I also appreciate that the authors address the feasibility of their approach in Section 7.

However, I wonder if the evaluation would benefit from using datasets that do not conflate different domains. For instance, separating the movie review corpus from the tweet corpus could improve the prediction performance for each individual domain. This is because training on a mixed dataset may not be optimal for either domain. Additionally, this separation would make the results easier to interpret. As it stands, the results in Table 2 seem low compared to state-of-the-art results for the Pang and Lee data, but appear more favorable when compared to results for Twitter data.

In Section 3.3, I assume there are no overlapping snippets in the training and testing data of datasets 1 and 2, even though they come from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140). Could the authors confirm this?

On a minor note, some of the extra use of bold formatting can be distracting; it may be worth reconsidering its use to improve the overall readability of the paper.
