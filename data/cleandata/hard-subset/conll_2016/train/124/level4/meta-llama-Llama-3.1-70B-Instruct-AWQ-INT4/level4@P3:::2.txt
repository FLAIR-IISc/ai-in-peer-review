**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors argue that traditional sentiment analysis methods often struggle with nuanced text, such as sarcasm and implicit sentiment, due to the complexity of lexical, syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic levels. To address this, they introduce a new feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Introduction of cognitive features for sentiment analysis**: The authors propose a novel approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators.
2. **Development of a new feature set**: The authors create a feature set that combines traditional sentiment features with cognitive features extracted from eye-tracking data, which improves the performance of sentiment analysis.
3. **Evaluation on two datasets**: The authors evaluate their approach on two datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their method in handling complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to sentiment analysis**: The authors propose a unique approach to sentiment analysis by incorporating cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns of human annotators.
2. **Improved performance**: The authors demonstrate that their approach improves the performance of sentiment analysis, especially in handling complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.
3. **Clear discussion on features and comparison**: The authors provide a clear discussion on the features and comparison of feature sets, which is helpful in understanding their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dataset limitations**: The authors use two datasets that conflate different domains, which may affect the prediction results. It would be helpful to evaluate the approach on datasets that are more homogeneous.
2. **Low accuracy**: The accuracy results in Table 2 seem low compared to state-of-the-art results for similar datasets.
3. **Lack of comparison to deep learning methods**: The authors do not compare their approach to deep learning methods, which are commonly used in sentiment analysis.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the eye-tracking data was collected and preprocessed?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of dataset limitations and conflation of different domains?
3. Can you provide a more detailed comparison of your approach to deep learning methods for sentiment analysis?
4. How do you plan to extend your approach to other NLP tasks, such as emotion analysis and text summarization?
