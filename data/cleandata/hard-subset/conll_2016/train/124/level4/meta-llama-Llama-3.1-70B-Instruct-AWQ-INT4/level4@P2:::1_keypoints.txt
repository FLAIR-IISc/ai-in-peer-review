- Summary: Proposes using cognitive features from annotators' eye movements for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection, claiming to be the first to introduce such features to NLP.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a new set of cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns for sentiment and sarcasm tasks.
- Contribution 2: Presents a comprehensive evaluation of these cognitive features' effect on classification accuracy.
- Contribution 3: Discusses feasibility and potential real-world applications of the approach.
- Strength 1: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Strength 2: The idea of using cognitive/eye-tracking features is innovative and could inspire further NLP research.
- Strength 3: Provides thorough analysis and comparisons with traditional methods.
- Weakness 1: Motivation for why cognitive features should help detect sentiment polarity (beyond reading ease) is not well-justified.
- Weakness 2: Reported accuracy improvements are marginal and may not justify the added complexity.
- Weakness 3: Feasibility arguments and the specific example in Section 7.2 are unconvincing.
- Question 1: Please justify more clearly why cognitive features help detect sentiment polarities beyond reflecting reading ease.
- Question 2: How will the authors address the marginal accuracy gains and further improve effectiveness?
- Question 3: Provide concrete real-world application examples and explain the tangible benefits of the approach.
