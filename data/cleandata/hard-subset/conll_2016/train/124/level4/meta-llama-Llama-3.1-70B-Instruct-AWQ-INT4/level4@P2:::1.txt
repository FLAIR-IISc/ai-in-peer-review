**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from the eye-movement patterns of human annotators. The authors claim that this is the first work to introduce cognitive features into the NLP community.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a new set of cognitive features derived from eye-movement patterns, which can be used to improve sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
2. The paper presents a comprehensive analysis of the effectiveness of these cognitive features in improving the accuracy of sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection.
3. The authors discuss the feasibility of their approach and its potential applications in real-world scenarios.

**Strengths**

1. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.
2. The idea of using cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection is innovative and has the potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the effectiveness of their approach, including a comparison with traditional methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. The motivation for using cognitive features for sentiment analysis is not well-justified. While the authors argue that these features can reflect reading ease, it is unclear why they are helpful in detecting sentiment polarities.
2. The improvement in accuracy achieved by incorporating cognitive features is marginal, which may not be sufficient to justify the additional complexity of the approach.
3. The authors' discussion of the feasibility of their approach is not convincing, particularly with regards to the example provided in Section 7.2. It is unclear why this technique would be helpful in such a scenario.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more justification for why cognitive features are helpful in detecting sentiment polarities, beyond their ability to reflect reading ease?
2. How do you plan to address the marginal improvement in accuracy achieved by incorporating cognitive features, and what additional steps can be taken to improve the effectiveness of your approach?
3. Can you provide more concrete examples of how your approach can be applied in real-world scenarios, and what benefits it can bring to these applications?
