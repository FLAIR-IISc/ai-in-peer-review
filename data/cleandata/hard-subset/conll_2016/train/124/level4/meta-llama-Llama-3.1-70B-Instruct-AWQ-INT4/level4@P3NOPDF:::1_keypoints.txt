- Paper is well-written and accessible.
- Using cognitive (eye-movement) features for sentiment/sarcasm detection is an innovative idea.
- Motivation for why cognitive features help sentiment polarity is not well justified.
- Connection between underlying cognitive processes and sentiment analysis needs clearer explanation.
- Empirical gains from cognitive features are marginal (Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr), raising practical significance concerns.
- Feasibility argument in Section 7 is unconvincing and the provided example (Section 7.2) is unclear.
- More detailed analysis of cognitive mechanisms underlying the features is needed.
- Require a more comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Provide concrete examples or case studies to better illustrate benefits and applications.
