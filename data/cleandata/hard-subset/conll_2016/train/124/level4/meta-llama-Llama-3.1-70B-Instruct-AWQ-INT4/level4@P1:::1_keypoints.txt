- Proposes using cognitive features derived from annotators' eye-movement patterns for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection, claiming to be first in NLP to do so.  
- Aims to augment traditional lexical/syntactic/semantic features with eye-tracking features to handle complex constructs like irony and sarcasm.  
- Main contributions: introduction of eye-movement–based cognitive features and empirical demonstration of their effectiveness for SA and sarcasm detection.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and accessible.  
- Strength: the idea of leveraging cognitive signals is novel and could inspire further NLP research.  
- Weakness: motivation is weak—unclear why reading/ease signals map to sentiment polarity.  
- Weakness: performance gains from cognitive features are marginal, especially versus strong baselines (Sn+Sr+Gz vs Sn+Sr).  
- Weakness: feasibility/example in Section 7.2 is unconvincing and does not clearly show practical utility.  
- Question: request stronger justification for why cognitive features should aid sentiment polarity detection.  
- Question: ask how authors will address the marginal performance improvement observed.  
- Question: request more convincing examples or scenarios demonstrating feasibility and usefulness of the approach.
