This paper proposes a very interesting idea of utilizing cognitive features for sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection. More specifically, the eye-movement patterns of human annotators are recorded to derive a new set of features. The authors claim that this is the first work to incorporate cognitive features into the NLP community.

Strengths:

1. The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
2. The idea presented is very interesting and may inspire research in other NLP tasks.

Weaknesses:

1. The motivation for using cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not well justified. While these features may help reflect reading ease, it is unclear why they are beneficial for detecting sentiment polarities.
2. The improvement achieved by considering cognitive features is marginal, as evident from the comparison of Sn+Sr+Gz with Sn+Sr.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of the approach in Section 7, the explanation is not convincing, particularly with regards to the example provided in Section 7.2. It is unclear why this technique would be helpful in such a scenario.
