This paper presents a novel approach to sentiment analysis and sarcasm detection by incorporating cognitive features derived from human annotators' eye-movement patterns. The authors assert that this work is the first to integrate cognitive features into the NLP community.

Strengths:

1. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to comprehend.
2. The proposed idea has the potential to inspire research in other NLP tasks, offering a fresh perspective.

Weaknesses:

1. The justification for utilizing cognitive features in sentiment analysis is not thoroughly convincing. While these features may indicate reading ease, their relevance to detecting sentiment polarities is not clearly established.
2. The performance improvement achieved by incorporating cognitive features, as evidenced by the comparison between Sn+Sr+Gz and Sn+Sr, is relatively minor.
3. Although the authors discuss the feasibility of their approach in Section 7, the provided example in Section 7.2 is not persuasive, leaving doubts about the technique's usefulness in such scenarios.
