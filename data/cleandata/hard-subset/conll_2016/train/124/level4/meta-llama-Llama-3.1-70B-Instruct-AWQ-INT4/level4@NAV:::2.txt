This paper explores the integration of eye-tracking features into sentiment analysis as a cognitive feature, a concept that holds promise. The authors' discussion on feature sets and their comparative analysis is clear and informative. Additionally, the feasibility assessment in Section 7 is a valuable contribution.

The evaluation could be strengthened by using domain-specific datasets, rather than combining disparate domains such as movie reviews and tweets. This approach might enhance the predictive power of the model for each domain and facilitate more interpretable results. For instance, the results in Table 2 appear lower than state-of-the-art results for the Pang and Lee dataset but are more comparable to Twitter data results.

Regarding Section 3.3, it is assumed that there is no overlap between the training and testing data snippets for datasets 1 and 2, even though they originate from the same sources (e.g., Pang & Lee and Sentiment 140). 

A minor suggestion is to reconsider the use of bold formatting, as it can be distracting at times.
