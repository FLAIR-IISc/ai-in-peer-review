- Proposes MDL-based method to discover cross-language word correspondences, modeling substring-matching rules and optimizing model vs data description length.  
- Trains model with EM and evaluates on 13 Slavic languages using distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.  
- Motivation and formulation are sensible and MDL/EM choices are well-motivated, though some derivations are unclear.  
- Suggests empirical comparison with Bayesian phylogenetic inference tools (e.g., MrBayes) due to resemblance to Bayesian objectives.  
- Lacks comparison to related work on borrowing and cognate detection (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen; List & Moran 2013).  
- Alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck, currently limiting comparisons to at most 5 languages.  
- Phylogenetic trees built with neighbor joining—method has known disadvantages (e.g., manual rooting); suggests more sophisticated phylogenetic methods.  
- Unclear whether EM is run to convergence or uses another stopping criterion.  
- Mixing cognate dataset with non-cognate Swadesh lists may affect results and should be considered.  
- Using orthographic data risks conflating script changes with linguistic correspondences; phonetic transcriptions would be preferable.  
- Two technical points are unclear: the meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and the merging step in the maximization update.  
- Minor editorial issue: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).  
- Provides specific literature references to cite and/or compare against (List & Moran 2013; Kitchen et al. 2009).
