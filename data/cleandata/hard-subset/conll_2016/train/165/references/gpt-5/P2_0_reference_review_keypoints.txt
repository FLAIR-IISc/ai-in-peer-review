- Proposes an MDL-based deterministic method to discover string-level correspondences across >2 languages using EM that alternates exact multiway alignments and greedy rule merges.  
- Introduces a two-part MDL code for rule strings, rule-usage distributions, and per-symbol codes, with seed-rule initialization and a data-overweighting knob.  
- Empirically applies the method to Slavic data, deriving NCD trees that match plausible phylogeny and a shared-description-length (sDL) decomposition for 4-way subset analysis.

- Strength: Conceptual generality — models free-form multi-string rules without distributional assumptions, yielding interpretable phonological/orthographic patterns.  
- Strength: Principled deterministic objective — clear MDL trade-off and reproducible training (null init, greedy merges, exact alignments).  
- Strength: Clean multiway alignment formulation — casts N-way optimal alignment as a shortest-path problem enabling exact alignments.  
- Strength: Empirical findings align with known structure (Slavic subgroupings, orthography effects) and sDL surfaces plausible cross-subset regularities.  
- Strength: Practical knobs — seed rules and data-overweighting allow practitioner control when evidence is marginal.

- Weakness: Limited empirical validation and lack of baselines — mainly qualitative results, no comparisons to PMI/pair-HMM/ALINE, no quantitative tree accuracy or extrinsic tasks.  
- Weakness: Orthography confounds and data curation — raw orthography and non-cognate Swadesh entries may conflate script/spelling with phonology/etymology.  
- Weakness: Coding-scheme clarity issues — L(Π) and L(D|M) roles unclear, reused terminology for code(π)/code(σ), and undefined “weak number composition.”  
- Weakness: Algorithmic details and scalability under-specified — candidate-merge enumeration, stopping criteria, smoothing interaction, re-estimation frequency, and coarse complexity claims; currently scales only to ~5 languages.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility and presentation gaps — code/data availability, hyperparameters, schedules missing; some references and equations imprecise.

- Question: Please precisely define the “data-to-model code” in L(Π), role of LN(TΠ)+log(...) and whether ML plug-in or composition codes are used.  
- Question: How are merge candidates generated/pruned, what is the stopping criterion, and do you re-align after each accepted merge?  
- Question: How was cognacy ensured in Swadesh lists, and did you test robustness to non-cognates/noise?  
- Question: Can you compare against PMI-based or pair-HMM baselines and report quantitative tree distances; can sDL be validated against linguistic judgments?  
- Question: How do results change if Cyrillic is romanized or data mapped to phonemic transcriptions?

- Overall novelty: Moderate — interesting MDL application and sDL idea but related pairwise work exists.  
- Overall quality: Mixed — promising core idea and examples, but limited validation and unclear technical details.  
- Overall clarity: Moderate — high-level ideas clear, formal derivations and references need tightening.  
- Overall significance: Potentially meaningful for unsupervised multilingual form analysis if strengthened with baselines, clearer derivations, controlled data, and quantitative evaluation.
