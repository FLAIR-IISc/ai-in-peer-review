- Proposes an MDL-based, deterministic method to discover multi-language substring correspondences from raw orthography with no prior rule-shape distribution.  
- Defines a two-part code over multi-language substring correspondences and computes optimal alignments via shortest-path (Dijkstra) given a rule table.  
- Learns rules via a deterministic EM-like greedy merge procedure and introduces a “data over-weighting” knob to favor larger, specific correspondences.  
- Demonstrates the method on Slavic cognate lists, producing pairwise NCDs that recover known phylogenetic groupings and a multi-way shared-description-length (sDL) analysis.  
- Contribution: MDL formulation and deterministic algorithm for N>2 substring correspondences with exact alignment given a rule table.  
- Contribution: Information-theoretic sDL metric enabling fine-grained, beyond-pairwise similarity analysis across language subsets.  
- Contribution: Empirical case study showing linguistically plausible correspondences and control over rule granularity.  
- Strength: Conceptual novelty in moving beyond pairwise models and allowing sparse multi-way rules that capture orthographic/phonological regularities.  
- Strength: Principled, deterministic alignment subproblem (shortest-path) and reproducible greedy training with option to seed linguistic rules.  
- Strength: sDL provides a useful, fine-grained analysis and the four-language example reveals expected subgroupings and asymmetries.  
- Strength: Empirical results align with known Slavic subgrouping and plausibly capture correspondences and script effects.  
- Strength: Data over-weighting is a practical mechanism to surface longer rules for downstream tasks despite its subjectivity.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical validation—small datasets, no quantitative baselines (PMI, pair HMMs, Levenshtein/PMI phylogenies), no rule quality metrics or statistical tests.  
- Weakness: Under-specified coding and reproducibility details (data-to-model code, multinomial compositions, many algorithmic hyperparameters and heuristics missing).  
- Weakness: Modeling choices (code design, over-weighting, orthographic input) introduce subjectivity and conflate script with phonology.  
- Weakness: Scalability and convergence concerns—alignment memory use, runtime limits (practical N≤5), greedy merges with no guarantees or stability analysis.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—notation inconsistencies, typos, broken/incomplete references, unclear relation to prior MDL/Bayesian work.  
- Questions: Provide exact derivations for data-to-model components, encoding of rule usage and symbol unigrams, and handling of zero counts.  
- Questions: Clarify precise training schedule, stopping criteria, number of merges per M-step, overlap handling, tie-breaking, gaps, and partial order reduction.  
- Questions: Report sensitivity/ablation to Laplace smoothing, alpha over-weighting, and initialization.  
- Questions: Compare against at least two baselines (PMI-based correspondences and Levenshtein distances) and evaluate correspondences against a small gold set.  
- Questions: Will code and processed datasets (with transliterations) be released for replication?  
- Overall assessment: Promising and original approach with appealing qualitative results, but currently lacks sufficient experiments and methodological clarity—lean reject for main conference, potential for workshop/revision.
