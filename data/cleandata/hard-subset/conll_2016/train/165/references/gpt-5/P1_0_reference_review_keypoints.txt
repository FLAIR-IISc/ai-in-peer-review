- Paper proposes an MDL-based framework to discover systematic string-level correspondences across multiple languages without parametric rule assumptions, using a two-part code, shortest-path alignment, and greedy EM-style merges.
- Contribution: formal MDL model and deterministic learning algorithm for multi-language correspondence induction scaling beyond language pairs.
- Contribution: information-theoretic toolkit for multiway similarity using NCD for pairwise distances and shared Description Length (sDL) for arbitrary subsets.
- Contribution: practical data over-weighting knob to control rule granularity when data are sparse.
- Strength: clear MDL formulation with explicit coding choices and attention to reproducibility.
- Strength: deterministic, multi-language correspondence induction addresses a gap beyond pairwise or stochastic methods.
- Strength: alignment cast as exact shortest-path search (Dijkstra), giving an intuitive E-step.
- Strength: learned rules are interpretable and sDL enables novel multiway analyses beyond tree structures.
- Strength: empirical signals (NCD phylogeny) broadly match expected Slavic groupings and detect known outliers.
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation and no comparisons to established baselines or standard tasks (cognate detection, held-out compression).
- Weakness: reliance on raw orthography and ad hoc data choices risks conflating orthographic and phonological patterns.
- Weakness: scalability/practicality not demonstrated beyond small N; hours of runtime and memory limits reported.
- Weakness: data over-weighting departs from MDL objective and risks overfitting without guidance on choosing α.
- Weakness: several modeling and coding details (rule-table derivations, merge compatibility, Laplace correction, ε-handling) need clarification.
- Question: can you compare against established methods (PHMM/PMI/ALINE/LexStat) on cognate identification or predictive compression?
- Question: have you tried phonemic transcriptions or controlling for script/diacritics, and how do results change?
- Question: how are “compatible” rules defined for merging, is the merge search local/global, and are alignments recomputed after each merge?
- Question: please provide full derivations for rule/code lengths and specify Laplace smoothing effects on code lengths and alignments.
- Question: how should users select the over-weighting α, and can you show sensitivity/stability analyses across α and resamples?
- Question: what are typical |R| and A values, runtime/memory for the four-language experiment, and possible optimizations?
- Additional: please release code and preprocessing scripts and clarify handling of missing entries vs. claim of gap-agnosticism.
- Additional: improve presentation—fix references, number equations, and define symbols at first use.
- Additional: add quantitative evaluations of rule quality (precision/recall vs curated correspondences) and robustness checks (bootstrap sDL/NCD).
- Overall recommendation: borderline—interesting MDL and multiway sDL ideas, but insufficient evaluation, orthography dependence, and scalability/details need strengthening.
