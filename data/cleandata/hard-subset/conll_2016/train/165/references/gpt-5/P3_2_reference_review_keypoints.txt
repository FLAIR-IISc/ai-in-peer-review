- MDL-based two-part-code framework to learn variable-length correspondence rules across N≥2 languages from cognate tuples, with no parametric rule-distribution assumptions.  
- Model encodes rule strings per language subset, aligns data by shortest-path search, and refines rules via a deterministic EM-like procedure with greedy merges.  
- Information-theoretic analyses: pairwise NCD from learned codes for Slavic phylogeny and a “shared Description Length” (sDL) metric attributing model complexity to language subsets.  
- Case study on Slavic languages (13 pairwise, 4-way CS–PL–RU–BG) showing linguistically plausible correspondences and an α “data over-weighting” knob to induce larger rules.  
- Clear, flexible MDL formulation that avoids strong parametric priors and accounts for sparsity through subset-specific rule encoding.  
- Ability to discover multi-language (non-pairwise) rules and visualize subset-shared structure (sDL) is distinctive and useful.  
- Deterministic, interpretable learning trajectory (singleton init, Laplace smoothing, greedy MDL merges) that linguists can inspect or seed.  
- Method captures script/orthography effects (e.g., Latin vs. Cyrillic clustering), useful for transliteration/orthography tasks though confounding for phylogeny.  
- Practical α knob is a pragmatic control to elicit larger rules when data is limited or for reconstruction tasks.  
- Evaluation is limited and partly confounded: no quantitative comparisons to established baselines and no standard phylogeny metrics; small Swadesh lists and internationalisms risk conflating borrowing/orthography with inheritance.  
- Scalability concerns: alignment graphs can be memory-intensive (practical limit ~N≈5), E-/M-step complexities suggest problems for longer words, larger alphabets, and higher N without pruning or approximations.  
- MDL code specification needs tighter justification: unclear separation of L(Π), L(Σi) and L(D|M), potential double-counting, and unclear role of weak composition terms.  
- Over-weighting α departs from MDL optimality without formal analysis of induced bias or sensitivity studies.  
- Missing related work and context: lacks discussion/comparison with ALINE, PMI/PHMM, LexStat/SCA, Bayesian phylogenetic lexical models, and recent multilingual cognate/sound correspondence work.  
- NCD computation needs clarification: how L(X,X) and L(X,Y) are computed, training/encoding splits, and whether code tables are re-estimated per condition.  
- MDL code details requested: full derivation of L(Π) and L(Σi), justification for data-to-model terms being in L(M), and avoidance of double-counting usage information.  
- sDL definition questioned: why sum L(π) rather than using each rule’s contribution to L(D|M), and whether the alternatives were compared.  
- Sensitivity and robustness: request ablations for initialization, Laplace constant, α, and variance across seeds (if randomized).  
- Scalability metrics requested: typical |R|, |A|, wall-clock, peak memory, dataset stats, and any heuristics (beam/A*/caps) to scale beyond five languages.  
- Baseline comparisons requested: ALINE/PMI/PHMM/LexStat on the same data and quantitative agreement with a reference Slavic tree.  
- Suggestion to include phonemic data to decouple orthography from phonology and treat Latin/Cyrillic as script variants for robustness testing.  
- Reproducibility request: release code, data splits, and learned rule tables.
