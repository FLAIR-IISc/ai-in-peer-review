- Proposes an MDL-based framework to induce multi-language correspondence rules from orthographic cognate tuples without parametric correspondence forms.  
- Models a two-part code over a rule table and alignments; alignments are shortest paths and parameters optimized via EM-like E-step (optimal alignments) and M-step (greedy rule merges).  
- Produces pairwise NCD distances and an information-theoretic shared description length (sDL) for all language subsets.  

- Contribution: deterministic MDL formulation and alignment-as-shortest-path algorithm for arbitrary-length multi-language correspondence rules.  
- Contribution: decomposition of model description length (sDL) to quantify similarity among all language subsets (multi-way analysis).  
- Contribution: empirical Slavic case study showing linguistically sensible rules and NCD-derived phylogeny matching known groupings.  

- Strength: Conceptual clarity and interpretability; produces directly interpretable multi-language rules and supports seeding for expert-in-the-loop use.  
- Strength: Principled MDL formulation bridging pattern mining and alignment, avoiding ad hoc penalties and priors.  
- Strength: Natural N>2 generalization enabling multi-way regularity discovery beyond pairwise methods.  
- Strength: Empirical plausibility—NCD phylogeny and four-way analyses align with linguistic expectations and reveal meaningful patterns.  
- Strength: Practical flexibility—data overweighting knob helps surface near-significant rules for sparse data.  

- Weakness: Limited empirical validation and no comparisons to standard baselines (PMI/LexStat/pair-HMMs/Bayesian methods) or intrinsic/extrinsic metrics like precision/recall.  
- Weakness: Reliance on raw orthography confounds phonological signal with script/orthographic conventions; lacks phonemic normalization or G2P.  
- Weakness: Theoretical and implementation details of code lengths are under-specified or inconsistent, threatening reproducibility and soundness.  
- Weakness: M-step candidate generation and overall scalability/search strategy are underdefined; experiments are small-scale with no runtime/scaling analysis.  
- Weakness: sDL may misrepresent shared structure because it sums rule costs without considering rule usage in encoding, risking misleading similarity interpretations.  

- Question: Provide precise formulas for all code components, clarify enumerative code usage, and ensure prefix-freeness/Kraft conditions.  
- Question: How are empty (ε) entries in rules penalized, are all-empty/near-empty rules disallowed, and what prevents proliferation of language-internal substrings?  
- Question: Precisely specify M-step candidate generation: which merges are considered, compatibility criteria, adjacency constraints, and stopping criteria?  
- Question: How is the overweighting parameter α chosen, and can you report sensitivity analyses on its impact on rule inventories and NCD/sDL?  
- Question: Can you add comparisons to standard baselines and/or a gold mapping to estimate precision/recall, and perform held-out DL or cross-validation for generalization?  

- Additional: Correct typos, clarify ambiguous statements, and provide implementation details (max rule length, Laplace parameter, partial order reduction) with code/data release.  
- Additional: Run phonemic transliteration/G2P experiments to mitigate orthography confounds.  
- Additional: Consider augmenting sDL with usage-weighted contributions or normalizing by total description length per subset.  

- Overall: Thoughtful and promising MDL framing for multilingual correspondences; strengthening theoretical specification and adding comparative/predictive evaluations would improve impact.
