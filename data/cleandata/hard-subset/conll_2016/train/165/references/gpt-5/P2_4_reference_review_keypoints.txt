- Proposes a principled two-part MDL framework (PS-2) to discover multi-language correspondence rules from raw orthographic strings without parametric rule assumptions.
- Jointly optimizes model and data cost using shortest-path alignment per item and a deterministic EM-like training with greedy rule merges.
- Provides an analysis toolkit: pairwise Normalized Compression Distance (NCD) for phylogeny and shared Description Length (sDL) to quantify structure in any subset of languages.
- Case study on Slavic languages recovers plausible correspondences, a reasonable phylogeny, subgroup structure, and script/orthography effects.
- Introduces data over-weighting as a practical knob to surface longer or rarer patterns in small corpora.

- MDL formulation is principled and interpretable, connecting improvements to actual compression and minimizing strong priors.
- Multi-language capability and sDL are novel, enabling analysis beyond pairwise comparisons and revealing which correspondences hold at different arities.
- Deterministic algorithm with option to seed expert rules supports replicable, linguist-friendly workflows.
- Results align with linguistic expectations; extracted rules and tree structure have face validity.
- Data over-weighting offers practical control of granularity while preserving statistical ordering.

- Evaluation lacks quantitative rigor: no baselines, intrinsic metrics, extrinsic tasks, or statistical support for phylogenies.
- Reproducibility is weak: missing precise hyperparameters, algorithmic details, code, processed datasets, and runtime/memory profiling.
- Coding design choices (two-part code specifics, data-to-model codes) lack justification and ablation to assess sensitivity.
- Optimization is heuristic with no convergence guarantees and no sensitivity analysis to initialization, merge schedule, or over-weighting.
- Limited scope and preprocessing: raw orthography conflates phonology and script, gaps are excluded, and robustness to noisy or larger datasets is untested.

- Please release code, processed data, and exact hyperparameters to enable replication.
- How sensitive are results to coding choices and have you performed ablations on L(Π) components?
- How is the over-weighting factor α chosen, and can its effect on false positives/overfitting be quantified?
- How robust is the method to noise and missing entries; can you report controlled experiments including gaps?
- Can you provide quantitative comparisons to standard pairwise methods to demonstrate multi-language advantages?
- What are the practical scaling limits (N, word length, dataset size) and are there approximation strategies for efficiency?

- Originality: moderate—applying MDL to multi-language correspondence and sDL is useful though MDL pattern discovery is known.
- Soundness: conceptually sound but some coding choices and heuristics need deeper justification and validation.
- Clarity: generally clear with good intuition, but more algorithmic detail required for replication.
- Significance: promising for multi-language analysis but limited by weak empirical evaluation.
- Recommendation: borderline accept contingent on stronger empirical comparisons and improved reproducibility.
