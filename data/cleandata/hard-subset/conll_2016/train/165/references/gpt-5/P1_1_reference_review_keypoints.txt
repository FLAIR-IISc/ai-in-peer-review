- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover multi-language substring correspondence rules with a two-part code and an EM-like learning loop (E: shortest-path alignment, M: greedy merges).
- Contribution: MDL formulation and deterministic greedy learning for multi-string correspondence induction extending serial-episode MDL to multi-string alignment.
- Contribution: Practical E-step alignment via shortest-path search over rule applications enabling simultaneous multi-language alignment.
- Contribution: Information-theoretic analysis of language similarity via shared description lengths across all subsets (sDL), demonstrated on four Slavic languages.
- Strength: Clear, interpretable MDL objective that balances model cost and data fit, avoiding ad-hoc priors.
- Strength: Multi-language capability beyond pairwise methods, producing rules defined on any subset of languages.
- Strength: Deterministic, transparent learning with traceable greedy merges and exact shortest-path E-step.
- Strength: Qualitative induced correspondences look linguistically plausible (phonological/orthographic correspondences and inflectional endings).
- Strength: Pairwise NCD tree matches known Slavic groupings and reflects script effects, suggesting the model captures real regularities.
- Weakness: Evaluation is small-scale and lacks held-out compression, systematic baselines, and quantitative benchmarks against established methods.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on raw orthography and mixed scripts conflates orthographic idiosyncrasies with phonology; transliteration/phonemic input needed.
- Weakness: Ambiguities in code design (L(M), L(D|M)), unclear computation of code(π)/code(σ), and possible double-counting/leakage between model and data terms.
- Weakness: Greedy EM is path-dependent, may lock in suboptimal merges, no annealing/restarts or guarantees reported.
- Weakness: "Data over-weighting" introduces arbitrary α that undermines MDL justification and risks overfitting without sensitivity analysis.
- Question: Provide a precise, self-contained derivation of L(Π) and L(D|M), clarify code(σ)/code(π) computation and whether counts are re-estimated each EM iteration.
- Question: Justify LN(TΠ)+log((TΠ−1 choose |Π|−1)) in L(Π) and clarify how data statistics are prevented from leaking into model encoding.
- Question: Explain efficient construction of the alignment graph for multi-language rules, the partial order reduction used, and runtime scaling with N and vocabulary.
- Question: What is R (max rule applications) in practice, and how does it affect performance?
- Question: How are ties broken when multiple merges yield identical DL reductions, and how deterministic are alignments given fixed rule costs?
- Question: How is the over-weighting parameter α selected; provide sensitivity analyses or held-out evaluations across α.
- Question: Compare to established methods (ALINE, LexStat/PMI, PHMM aligners, Bayesian sound change models, LingPy) on public datasets with quantitative metrics (tree accuracy, alignment scores, held-out compression).
- Additional: Release code, data, and parameter settings for reproducibility; clean formatting/section numbering.
- Additional: Claim of handling missing data is untested—recommend controlled experiments with missing entries.
