- Proposes an MDL-based framework that discovers multi-way, variable-length string correspondences from cognate lists by minimizing a two-part MDL objective with an EM-like alternation of shortest-path alignments and greedy rule merges.

- Contribution 1: Presents a deterministic MDL formulation and algorithm for multi-language string correspondences (shortest-path alignment + MDL-guided greedy merging).

- Contribution 2: Offers native multi-language analysis and an sDL decomposition to quantify shared structure among arbitrary language subsets.

- Contribution 3: Provides qualitative Slavic experiments showing plausible correspondences, a broadly consistent phylogeny, and a practical “over-weighting” knob to surface near-significant rules.

- Strength: Principled, assumption-light objective—two-part MDL yields interpretable, reproducible rule selection without strong parametric priors.

- Strength: N-ary capability—model handles more than pairwise alignments and sDL helps identify subset-specific shared structure and asymmetries.

- Strength: Clear, interpretable modeling—human-readable multi-character rules, deterministic merging, and support for expert seeding produce linguistically meaningful patterns.

- Strength: Empirical plausibility—the NCD phylogeny and four-way analyses largely match linguistic intuitions and reveal expected outliers and dependencies.

- Strength: Methodologically extensible—connections to MDL pattern mining and shortest-path alignment suggest paths for scaling and hybrid approaches.

- Weakness: Limited quantitative evaluation and missing baselines—no comparisons to established aligners, no intrinsic alignment metrics, and no extrinsic task evaluation.

- Weakness: Orthography vs phonology confound—experiments use raw orthography across scripts, risking conflation of orthographic conventions with phonological correspondences.

- Weakness: Scalability and practicality concerns—experiments up to N=4, multi-hour runtimes, vague complexity parameters, and no runtime/convergence plots or pruning strategies detailed.

- Weakness: Method-detail gaps—model and coding terms are terse or nonstandard, merge candidate generation/tie-breaking/stability and Laplace correction effects are under-specified.

- Weakness: Departure from principled MDL via α over-weighting—pragmatic weighting undermines objectivity with no guidance on choosing α or analysis of overfitting tradeoffs.

- Weakness: Reproducibility and presentation issues—no code/data release indicated, inconsistent references/figures, and some formulae lack definitions, hindering replication.

- Question: Can you provide quantitative comparisons to baseline correspondence/aligners and intrinsic/extrinsic evaluations (alignment accuracy, cognate prediction, reconstruction)?

- Question: How sensitive are results to phonemic vs orthographic input—can you report experiments with phonetic transcriptions or normalized alphabets?

- Question: Precisely how are L(X,X) and L(X,Y) computed for NCD and how is symmetry ensured; why sum model code lengths in sDL rather than usage-weighted measures?

- Question: Please detail merge candidate generation, tie-breaking, pruning/stopping criteria, and provide runtime/memory scaling and convergence behavior for larger |D| and N.

- Question: How should the over-weighting parameter α be selected; can you show its impact on downstream performance and offer principled alternatives to preserve objectivity?

- Question: Will you release code, processed datasets, and scripts for reproducing the phylogeny and sDL plots to improve reproducibility?
