- Proposes an MDL-based framework for discovering linguistic correspondences without arbitrary distributional assumptions.  
- Introduces a deterministic EM-based algorithm that learns correspondence rules directly from data.  
- Presents an efficient method for simultaneous multi-language correspondence discovery, handling more than two languages.  
- Demonstrates linguistically meaningful insights via comprehensive Slavic language analysis, including phylogenetic alignment and example correspondences.  
- Strength: Novel and principled use of MDL to address limitations of previous statistical approaches.  
- Strength: Effective multi-language analysis that reveals correspondences across subsets of languages beyond pairwise methods.  
- Strength: Clear experimental validation with phylogenetic results and concrete correspondence examples.  
- Weakness: No thorough comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., neural or probabilistic models).  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to Slavic languages, raising questions about generalizability to other language families and typologies.  
- Weakness: The "data over-weighting" technique is insufficiently justified and may contradict the objective MDL framework.  
- Question: How does the method compare empirically to recent neural-network and probabilistic approaches for correspondence discovery?  
- Question: How would the method perform on languages with different writing systems or morphological typologies (e.g., Arabic, Chinese, agglutinative languages)?  
- Question: Please justify the data over-weighting technique and provide guidelines for choosing appropriate weights.  
- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution with promising results, but needs broader comparisons and validation beyond Slavic languages to strengthen claims.
