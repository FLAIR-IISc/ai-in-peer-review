- MDL framework proposed for objective, distribution-free discovery of linguistic correspondences.
- Algorithm that quantifies similarity across all language subsets rather than only pairwise.
- Data over-weighting extension to control granularity of discovered correspondences.

- MDL formulation is a principled alternative to prior statistical methods and avoids prior assumptions.
- Approach reveals multi-subset similarities, capturing nuanced relationships beyond pairwise analyses.
- Discovered correspondences are linguistically plausible and the phylogenetic output aligns with Slavic classifications.

- No rigorous quantitative comparison with state-of-the-art baselines to substantiate performance claims.
- Experiments are limited to closely related Slavic languages and Swadesh data, limiting generalizability.
- Data over-weighting contradicts the paper’s claim of objectivity and lacks justification or selection criteria.
- Computationally challenging beyond five languages and no complexity scaling analysis is provided.
- Absence of linguistic expert validation to confirm the meaningfulness of discovered patterns.

- Please provide quantitative comparisons with established methods (e.g., Snyder & Barzilay 2008, Wettig et al. 2011).
- How does the method handle languages with different scripts or vastly different morphological complexity?
- Clarify why Czech’s many diacritics increase its individual MDL description length.
- How do you reconcile data over-weighting with MDL objectivity and how should the weight parameter be chosen?

- Promising MDL-based contribution and useful multi-subset insights, but needs stronger baselines, broader datasets, justification for over-weighting, scalability analysis, and linguistic validation.
