- Proposes an MDL-based framework to discover linguistic correspondences without assuming specific correspondence distributions.  
- Presents an algorithm that quantifies linguistic similarity across all subsets of languages (not just pairwise).  
- Empirically validates the method on Slavic languages, recovering known subgroupings and subtle relationships.

- Novel methodological approach addressing the limitation of pairwise or assumption-heavy methods using an objective MDL formulation.  
- Strong empirical validation: phylogenetic results align with established Slavic classifications and reveal nuanced similarities.  
- Key contribution: ability to quantify similarity across all language subsets simultaneously, offering richer insights than pairwise methods.  
- Technical rigor and reproducibility: sufficient implementation and MDL encoding details are provided.

- Evaluation limited to Slavic languages, reducing claims about generalizability to other families.  
- No direct comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, making relative performance unclear.  
- Data over-weighting introduces subjectivity and lacks guidance for choosing the weight parameter.  
- Insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability beyond processing up to five languages on a desktop.

- How does the method compare to recent neural multi-language correspondence approaches?  
- Please provide detailed analysis of computational complexity scaling with languages and dataset size.  
- How should linguists choose the data over-weighting parameter to preserve objectivity while capturing subtle correspondences?

- Overall: well-motivated, technically sound approach that meaningfully advances multi-language correspondence discovery; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (expand evaluations, comparisons, and methodological clarifications).
