

# Peer Review: "Discovering Correspondences between Multiple Languages by MDL"

## Summary and Main Contributions

This paper proposes an MDL-based approach for discovering linguistic correspondences between multiple languages without making assumptions about the distribution or shape of these correspondences. The authors present an efficient algorithm that can handle more than two languages simultaneously, allowing for detailed quantification of linguistic similarities among all subsets of languages. The main contributions, in order of strength, are:

1. A novel application of MDL principles to multi-language linguistic correspondence discovery, providing an objective, unbiased framework that avoids the limitations of existing statistical approaches.
2. A practical algorithm for multi-language analysis that enables detailed quantification of similarities across all language subsets, not just pairwise comparisons.
3. A thorough experimental evaluation on Slavic languages demonstrating the approach's ability to identify linguistic subgroups and produce phylogenetic trees consistent with established linguistic classifications.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Soundness and Novelty of MDL Application**: The paper makes a strong case for using MDL as a principled approach to linguistic correspondence discovery. The authors clearly articulate why existing methods have limitations (arbitrary assumptions, lack of linguistic knowledge integration, pairwise-only constraints) and how MDL addresses these issues. The theoretical foundation is solid, with clear explanations of how MDL principles translate to linguistic correspondence discovery. The paper successfully adapts MDL to a novel problem domain, which is a significant contribution to computational linguistics.

2. **Multi-Language Analysis Capability**: The paper's most compelling contribution is its ability to analyze more than two languages simultaneously. This is a major limitation of existing approaches that the paper effectively demonstrates. The four-language analysis (Czech, Polish, Russian, Bulgarian) shows how the method can quantify similarities across all subsets of languages, providing insights that pairwise analyses would miss. The results align well with linguistic expectations (e.g., grouping Czech and Polish together, identifying Bulgarian's similarity to Russian), which demonstrates the practical utility of the approach.

3. **Comprehensive Evaluation on Slavic Languages**: The evaluation is thorough and well-structured, using both pairwise analyses (with NCD and phylogenetic trees) and four-way analysis. The results consistently align with linguistic classifications, providing strong evidence that the approach produces meaningful results. The paper's demonstration of how the method can identify linguistic subgroups (e.g., Bulgarian as an outlier in the south Slavic group) shows the approach's ability to capture nuanced linguistic relationships.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of Comparative Evaluation**: The paper does not provide a rigorous comparison with state-of-the-art methods. While it mentions that existing approaches have limitations, it fails to demonstrate how the proposed method outperforms them. The paper should compare its results with at least one or two relevant existing approaches (e.g., Wettig et al., 2011; Snyder and Barzilay, 2008) to quantify the improvement. Without this, it's difficult to assess the true contribution of the work.

2. **Limited Linguistic Analysis of Discovered Correspondences**: While the paper presents example correspondences, it doesn't deeply analyze their linguistic significance. The examples show phonological and orthographic rules, but the paper doesn't discuss how these align with established linguistic patterns or whether they reveal new insights. For instance, the paper doesn't explain why certain palatalizations or metatheses are discovered, or how they relate to historical linguistic changes. A more detailed linguistic analysis would strengthen the paper's contribution.

3. **Underdeveloped Discussion of Data Over-Weighting**: The paper introduces "data over-weighting" as a technique to handle cases with insufficient statistical evidence, but this section lacks critical analysis. The authors acknowledge this technique moves results away from the objective MDL framework and introduces subjectivity, yet they don't provide sufficient evidence that it's beneficial in practice or discuss its potential drawbacks. A more balanced discussion of when and how to use this technique would be valuable.

## Questions to Authors

1. The paper claims the approach works well for multiple languages, but doesn't provide a clear benchmark for comparison. Could the authors provide a quantitative comparison with at least one existing method (e.g., Wettig et al., 2011) on the same Slavic dataset?

2. The paper mentions that the approach is "deterministic" but also states that "it is easy to integrate non-determinism." How does this affect the reproducibility of results, and what evidence do the authors have that the deterministic approach provides better results than a randomized one?

3. The paper shows that the algorithm can identify linguistic subgroups, but the results for Bulgarian and Macedonian as "outliers" seem to contradict some linguistic knowledge. Could the authors elaborate on why these languages appear as outliers in their analysis, and how this aligns with established linguistic classifications?

## Additional Comments

The paper is well-written and presents a theoretically sound approach with practical applications. The implementation details are thorough, and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive. The authors make a compelling case for the value of their approach, particularly for multi-language analysis. With better comparison to existing methods and deeper linguistic analysis of the discovered correspondences, this paper could be significantly strengthened. The data over-weighting technique needs more careful consideration, as it seems to introduce subjectivity that conflicts with the paper's objective MDL framework.
