- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover linguistic correspondences across multiple languages without strong distributional assumptions.  
- Presents an efficient algorithm that handles more than two languages and quantifies similarities across all language subsets.  
- Empirically evaluates on Slavic languages, producing subgroupings and phylogenetic trees consistent with linguistic classifications.

- Strong theoretical motivation and novel application of MDL to linguistic correspondence discovery.  
- Capability to analyze multiple languages simultaneously, revealing subgroup patterns that pairwise methods miss.  
- Thorough, well-structured evaluation on Slavic languages with results aligning with linguistic expectations.

- Lacks rigorous comparative evaluation against state-of-the-art methods to quantify improvement.  
- Provides limited linguistic analysis of discovered correspondences and insufficient discussion of their linguistic significance.  
- Data over-weighting technique is introduced without sufficient critique or evidence of its benefits and drawbacks.

- Request for quantitative comparison with at least one existing method (e.g., Wettig et al., 2011) on the same dataset.  
- Clarification needed on determinism vs. non-determinism: reproducibility and whether deterministic yields better results.  
- Explanation requested for why Bulgarian and Macedonian appear as outliers relative to established classifications.

- Well-written and implemented; stronger comparisons, deeper linguistic analysis, and careful treatment of data over-weighting would strengthen the paper.
