- Novel MDL-based method to discover linguistic correspondences across multiple languages without assuming correspondence distributions.  
- Empirical demonstration on 13 Slavic languages that identifies linguistic subgroups and quantifies similarities; pairwise NCD and four-way analyses align with linguistic classifications.  
- Deterministic, efficient algorithm presented to infer correspondence rules from data.  
- Strong motivation and clear problem statement explaining limitations of existing methods and why MDL is appropriate.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation with substantial dataset and detailed analyses (pairwise phylogeny, shared description lengths).  
- Novel handling of multiple languages simultaneously, able to find correspondence rules linking only subsets of languages (beyond pairwise).  
- Lacks direct comparison to state-of-the-art baselines, so actual performance advantages are unclear.  
- Limited theoretical analysis: no convergence discussion for the EM-based algorithm, no formal complexity or scalability analysis.  
- Questionable novelty of core algorithm — appears to be a straightforward adaptation of Tatti & Vreeken (2012) without clear distinctions or justifications.  
- Question for authors: provide direct comparisons in accuracy and efficiency to strong baseline methods.  
- Question for authors: specify theoretical time complexity, scalability with number of languages, and main computational bottlenecks.  
- Question for authors: clarify how data over-weighting affects statistical significance and guidance for choosing over-weighting levels.  
- Overall: well-motivated and empirically validated approach; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add comparisons and stronger theoretical analysis.
