

# Review of "Discovering Correspondences between Multiple Languages by MDL"

## Summary and Contributions

This paper proposes an MDL-based approach for discovering string-level correspondences between multiple languages without making arbitrary assumptions about correspondence distributions. The authors develop an efficient deterministic algorithm to infer correspondence rules directly from data, enabling analysis beyond pairwise comparisons. The main contributions, in order of strength, are:

1. A novel application of MDL for discovering objective string-level correspondences across multiple languages without restrictive assumptions about their distribution.
2. A deterministic algorithm that learns correspondence rules according to statistical significance, growing from simple to complex patterns.
3. A framework for quantifying linguistic similarity across all subsets of analyzed languages, not just pairwise comparisons.

## Strengths

1. **Strong theoretical foundation**: The authors effectively leverage MDL, a well-established principle in information theory, to avoid the arbitrary assumptions that plague existing approaches. Their adaptation of MDL for correspondence discovery is well-motivated and theoretically sound, providing a principled way to identify the most succinct description of linguistic data.

2. **Thorough experimental validation**: The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation across multiple dimensions - pairwise analysis (with phylogenetic tree construction), four-language analysis, and the introduction of "data over-weighting" to control granularity. The results align well with established linguistic classifications, demonstrating the method's ability to capture meaningful linguistic structure.

3. **Novel technical contribution**: The "data over-weighting" technique is particularly valuable, allowing linguistic experts to control the level of detail in discovered correspondences. This practical feature bridges the gap between purely statistical methods and expert linguistic knowledge.

4. **Clear exposition**: The paper provides a well-structured technical exposition of the MDL code and algorithm, with sufficient mathematical detail for reproducibility while remaining accessible to linguists and computational linguists.

5. **Demonstrated capability for multi-language analysis**: The paper successfully shows how their method can quantify similarities across all subsets of languages simultaneously, which is a significant advancement over existing pairwise approaches.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of comparison with state-of-the-art**: While the authors mention that existing approaches are limited to pairs of languages, they don't provide a quantitative comparison of their method against current approaches. Without this, it's difficult to assess the relative advantages of their technique, which is a significant omission for a methodological contribution.

2. **Insufficient discussion of computational complexity**: The paper states that their implementation can process up to five languages within hours on modest hardware, but doesn't provide a formal complexity analysis or compare computational demands with existing methods. This is crucial for assessing practical applicability.

3. **Superficial linguistic interpretation**: The paper identifies linguistic patterns (e.g., "liquid metatheses such as (ла,оло)"), but doesn't provide in-depth linguistic analysis of why these patterns are significant or how they relate to historical sound changes. The connection between the technical results and linguistic theory remains somewhat underdeveloped.

4. **Handling of different writing systems**: The paper mentions Serbo-Croatian in Latin vs. Cyrillic script as a case where the method works as expected, but doesn't explain how the algorithm handles the fundamental differences between these writing systems. This is a significant gap given the Slavic language focus.

5. **Limited discussion of determinism's implications**: While the authors present determinism as a strength, they don't explore how this might limit the method's ability to capture inherent uncertainty in linguistic phenomena, which could be a drawback in some applications.

## Questions to Authors

1. Could you provide quantitative comparisons between your method and existing pairwise correspondence discovery approaches, particularly regarding accuracy or information-theoretic measures?

2. What are the practical limitations for scaling your method to larger language sets (e.g., 10+ languages), and how might these be addressed in future work?

3. In the four-language analysis, you mention that "the internationalism specjalny is analyzed with the three-way i correspondence plus a Polish singleton rule (j,,,)." Could you elaborate on why this particular segmentation was chosen over alternative segmentations?

4. How does your method handle languages with fundamentally different writing systems (e.g., Cyrillic vs. Latin script), and what implications does this have for the discovered correspondences?

5. Could you discuss potential applications of your method in historical linguistics or language teaching, beyond the current demonstration?

## Overall Assessment

This is a well-motivated and technically sound paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of computational historical linguistics. The methodological approach is principled and the experimental results are promising. However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the weaknesses noted above, particularly by providing comparative results with existing methods, better explaining the linguistic significance of the discovered patterns, and clarifying how the method handles practical challenges like different writing systems.

The paper demonstrates a clear advancement in the ability to discover string-level correspondences across multiple languages without restrictive assumptions, making it a strong candidate for acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses noted above to ensure the paper makes the strongest possible contribution to the field.
