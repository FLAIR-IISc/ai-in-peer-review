- Summary: MDL-based deterministic method to infer correspondence rules across multiple languages, demonstrated on Slavic languages to identify subgroups and quantify similarity.

- Contribution: Introduces an MDL framework for multi-language correspondence discovery, a deterministic rule-inference algorithm, and an empirical demonstration on Slavic data.

- Strength: Novel, purely data-driven MDL application that avoids preset rule shapes and assumptions.

- Strength: Solid theoretical grounding in MDL, clarifying the trade-off between model complexity and data description length.

- Strength: Algorithm clearly described (EM, singleton initialization, graph-based alignment) and reproducible.

- Strength: Informative empirical results (pairwise NCD, phylogenetic trees, sDL, example correspondences) consistent with linguistic knowledge.

- Strength: Data over-weighting mechanism allows incorporation of linguistic expertise and control over granularity.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns—memory and runtime bottlenecks limit experiments to around five languages.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—only Slavic languages, so generalizability to other families is untested.

- Weakness: No rigorous quantitative comparison to existing pairwise correspondence methods (e.g., accuracy/F1).

- Weakness: Heavy dependence on input data quality and Swadesh lists/cognate choices; potential biases and coverage issues.

- Weakness: Some sections (detailed MDL math) are overly long and could be more concise.

- Question: What optimizations will you implement to address computational bottlenecks and scale to more languages/datasets?

- Question: Are there plans or preliminary results for evaluation on non-Slavic language families?

- Question: Can you provide quantitative comparisons to baseline correspondence-discovery methods?

- Question: How sensitive are learned correspondences to cognate selection and noisy/incomplete data, and how will you mitigate this?

- Question: Besides MDL score, what other metrics or linguistic criteria will you use to evaluate correspondence rule quality?

- Additional: Figures are effective, references are comprehensive, and overall writing is good but could be more concise.

- Overall assessment: Novel, theoretically sound contribution with promising empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability and comparative evaluation.
