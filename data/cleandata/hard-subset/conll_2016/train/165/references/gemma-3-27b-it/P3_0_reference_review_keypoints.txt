- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover multilingual correspondences without strong distributional assumptions, using a deterministic EM algorithm and demonstrated on Slavic languages.  
- Main contribution: an assumption-minimal, principled MDL framework for multi-way correspondence discovery.  
- Main contribution: a deterministic, reproducible algorithm (EM-based) that learns rules and alignments.  
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration on Slavic languages including pairwise/four-way analyses and phylogenetic trees.  
- Strength: novel application of MDL to correspondence discovery beyond prior etymological work.  
- Strength: sound, rigorous theoretical foundation linking MDL and coding theory.  
- Strength: practical algorithm and clear implementation details addressing model complexity and alignment.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation, notably the valuable four-way analysis and illustrative correspondences.  
- Strength: clear writing and accessible structure for readers in linguistics and information theory.  
- Weakness: limited dataset scope (small Swadesh lists, cognate-focused) raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: no direct quantitative comparison to existing statistical or cognate-identification methods.  
- Weakness: claim of objectivity is undermined by subjective data choices and the data over-weighting mechanism.  
- Weakness: computational scalability is limited (practical only up to ~5 languages) and needs optimization.  
- Weakness: over-reliance on orthography; phonetic/morphological information is not incorporated and could improve robustness.  
- Question: provide quantitative comparison (precision/recall/F1) to existing methods on the same dataset.  
- Question: how sensitive are learned correspondences to different input data sources or cognate selection methods?  
- Question: can morphological information (stems/affixes) be integrated, and how would it affect model complexity and runtime?  
- Question: what guidelines should linguists follow to choose data over-weighting levels to balance objectivity and detail?  
- Question: what specific optimizations or parallelization strategies are planned to improve scalability?  
- Additional comment: include a glossary of symbols for clarity.  
- Additional comment: specify sources and selection criteria for internationalisms and pan-Slavic words to aid reproducibility.  
- Additional comment: improve Figure 1 to more clearly reflect NCD-based branch lengths.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and theoretically sound paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions addressing data scope, comparisons, and scalability.
