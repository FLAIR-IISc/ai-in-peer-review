Main contributions:
- Applied MDL to discover correspondences across multiple languages simultaneously, moving beyond pairwise comparisons.
- Introduced a deterministic algorithm that avoids prior distributional assumptions.
- Demonstrated the approach on Slavic languages, quantifying string-level similarities and subgroupings via shared description lengths.

Strengths:
- Novel, theoretically sound MDL formalization with a clear rationale.
- Deterministic method improves reproducibility and interpretability.
- Capable of multi-language analysis (demonstrated up to four languages).
- Informative experiments (pairwise NCD and four-way shared description length) that reveal linguistic subgroups.
- Clear, well-written presentation with illustrative examples.

Weaknesses:
- Relies on limited Swadesh list data; generalizability is uncertain and weighting adds subjectivity.
- Computational complexity and scalability are concerns; current implementation limited to ~5 languages.
- Evaluation focused on internal consistency; lacks comparison to external cognate databases or benchmarks.
- Data over-weighting introduces a subjective parameter without principled selection guidance.
- Insufficient error analysis—missing discussion of common error types and causes.

Questions to authors:
- Please provide detailed computational complexity analysis relative to number of languages (N) and cognate tuple lengths.
- How sensitive is the algorithm to noisy or inconsistent input data?
- What criteria or principled method determines appropriate data over-weighting?
- Have you compared results to external linguistic knowledge or databases; any preliminary findings?
- Can you provide more examples of rules linking only two or three languages and their linguistic significance?

Additional comments:
- References are appropriate.
- Paper structure is logical and easy to follow.
- Releasing code and data would enhance reproducibility.

Overall assessment:
- Strong, innovative paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions addressing data dependency, scalability, and evaluation concerns.
