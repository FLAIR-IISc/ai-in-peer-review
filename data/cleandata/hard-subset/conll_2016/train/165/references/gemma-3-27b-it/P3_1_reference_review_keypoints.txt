- Presents a novel MDL-based deterministic method to discover systematic correspondences across multiple languages from data.  
- Infers correspondences without assuming underlying change distributions and demonstrates on Slavic languages.

- Applies MDL to multi-language correspondence discovery, addressing combinatorial scaling beyond pairwise comparisons.  
- Introduces a deterministic, transparent, and reproducible algorithm for correspondence inference.  
- Quantifies similarity between subsets of languages, enabling fine-grained linguistic analysis.

- Novel combination of MDL and deterministic inference fills a gap in the literature.  
- Strong, mathematically sound MDL formulation with clear connections to coding theory and an EM-like optimization.  
- Empirical validation on Slavic languages shows plausible cognate alignments and a phylogenetic tree consistent with linguistic classifications.  
- Four-way analysis and shared description length (sDL) provide useful measures of subgroup similarity and shared complexity.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and technically accessible.

- Reliance on Swadesh lists and pre-identified cognates limits representativeness and may bias results.  
- Scalability concerns: algorithmic complexity (O(|D| · |R|^2)) and few-language experiments leave applicability to larger families uncertain.  
- Lacks direct comparison to existing statistical baseline methods to demonstrate relative performance.  
- Data over-weighting introduces subjectivity with insufficient justification for chosen weighting factors.  
- Insufficient error analysis—types and causes of algorithmic errors are not deeply explored.

- How sensitive are results to EM initialization and alternative initialization strategies?  
- Request for detailed computational complexity and memory analysis and planned scalability optimizations.  
- How does the method handle typologically distant languages or corpora with few cognates, and can it be adapted?  
- Need for principled justification and sensitivity analysis for data over-weighting factors.  
- Will the authors release code and data to support reproducibility?

- Figures, tables, and references are well done and relevant.  
- The sDL metric for language relatedness is promising and warrants further study.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions contingent on addressing raised questions and improvements.

- Overall: strong, theoretically sound contribution with empirical support, tempered by data, scalability, and comparative-evaluation limitations.
