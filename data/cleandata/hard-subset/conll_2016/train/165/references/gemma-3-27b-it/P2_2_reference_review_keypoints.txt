- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover systematic correspondences between languages without strong prior assumptions.  
- Presents a deterministic algorithm to infer correspondence rules, promoting reproducibility and interpretability.  
- Extends the approach to handle correspondences across more than two languages.  
- MDL provides a sound theoretical foundation connecting coding theory and information content to the task.  
- The combination of unsupervised, MDL-based discovery with a deterministic algorithm is novel.  
- Algorithm and EM/alignment procedure are described clearly with attention to computational complexity.  
- Empirical validation on Slavic languages shows plausible correspondences and supports phylogenetic reconstruction.  
- Authors address data sparsity via an explored data over-weighting parameter.  
- Evaluation is limited to Slavic languages and Swadesh/cognate lists, so generalizability is unclear.  
- Lacks direct comparison to strong existing baselines for cognate identification and reconstruction.  
- Computational bottlenecks and scalability concerns for larger numbers of languages/data are acknowledged.  
- Presentation of learned correspondences (e.g., Table 3) is hard to parse and could be clearer/visualized better.  
- Requests a direct baseline comparison with precision/recall/F1 to assess performance gains.  
- Asks for theoretical complexity as a function of number of languages and data, and planned scalability optimizations.  
- Questions generalizability to typologically different language families and necessary modifications.  
- Seeks justification and sensitivity analysis for the chosen data over-weighting factor.  
- Notes the paper is well-written, references are relevant, and non-deterministic extensions are an intriguing future direction.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with minor revisions recommended; recommends acceptance.
