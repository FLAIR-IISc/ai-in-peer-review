- Summary: Proposes an MDL-based deterministic method to infer correspondence rules among multiple languages and demonstrates it on Slavic languages.  
- Contribution 1: Applies MDL to discover correspondences without imposing prior rule-structure assumptions.  
- Contribution 2: Introduces a deterministic algorithm for inferring correspondence rules, improving reproducibility and interpretability.  
- Contribution 3: Extends analysis beyond pairwise comparisons and defines shared Description Length (sDL) to quantify multi-language similarity.  
- Strength 1: Novel approach that lets data drive correspondence discovery rather than biased prior models.  
- Strength 2: Strong theoretical grounding in MDL with a clear two-part formulation.  
- Strength 3: Algorithm description is clear, including EM steps, alignment, and partial order reduction.  
- Strength 4: Experiments on Slavic languages yield plausible correspondences and a phylogenetic tree aligning with linguistic classifications.  
- Strength 5: sDL provides a useful, quantifiable metric for structural similarity across language subsets.  
- Weakness 1: Scalability is limited (practical up to ~5 languages), restricting applicability to larger datasets.  
- Weakness 2: Experiments rely on small Swadesh lists, raising concerns about robustness and statistical significance.  
- Weakness 3: The data over-weighting parameter introduces subjectivity and lacks sensitivity analysis or selection guidelines.  
- Weakness 4: Evaluation is primarily intrinsic and lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art methods on tasks like cognate identification or MT.  
- Weakness 5: Presentation is occasionally verbose and could be tightened, especially explanations of established concepts.  
- Question 1: What are the main algorithmic bottlenecks and potential avenues for parallelization or approximation?  
- Question 2: How sensitive are results and the phylogeny to the over-weighting parameter α, and can you provide an analysis?  
- Question 3: Have you tried datasets beyond Swadesh lists, and what challenges arose?  
- Question 4: How does your method perform compared to SOTA approaches on standard cognate identification or MT metrics?  
- Question 5: How could randomized search methods (e.g., simulated annealing) be integrated and what benefits would they offer?  
- Additional comment 1: References are comprehensive and well-cited.  
- Additional comment 2: Some equations and presentations could be clarified for readability.  
- Overall assessment: Promising and novel contribution with sound theory and encouraging results; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing scalability, dataset breadth, evaluation, and parameter sensitivity.
