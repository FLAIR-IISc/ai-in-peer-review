- Summary: Proposes an MDL-based, assumption-free deterministic algorithm to discover correspondences across multiple languages, demonstrated on Slavic data.

- Contribution — MDL framework: Introduces a principled, assumption-free MDL formulation for linguistic correspondences focused on succinct data description.

- Contribution — Deterministic algorithm: Presents a deterministic, reproducible algorithm for inferring correspondence rules with EM-style updates.

- Contribution — Multilingual extension: Extends analysis beyond pairwise comparisons to jointly model multiple languages and quantify shared structure.

- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Strong justification via MDL, coding theory, and accessible explanation of two-part MDL.

- Strength — Clear methodology: Detailed algorithmic description including EM steps, encoding scheme, alignment procedures, and complexity discussion.

- Strength — Empirical results: Experiments on Slavic languages yield plausible phylogenies and illustrative learned correspondences.

- Strength — Novel similarity metric: Proposes “shared Description Lengths” (sDL) for granular similarity quantification among language subsets.

- Strength — Overfitting discussion: Addresses data scarcity and granularity via data over-weighting and discusses trade-offs with linguistic expertise.

- Weakness — Scalability: Current approach is limited (claims up to five languages) with unclear performance on larger datasets and complex languages.

- Weakness — Data dependency: Heavy reliance on Swadesh lists; cognate selection and representativeness may bias results.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Evaluation focuses on phylogeny/NCD; lacks quantitative comparisons to established cognate-identification and phylogenetic methods.

- Weakness — No ablation study: Missing analysis of individual MDL components (e.g., integer codes, Laplace correction) and their impact.

- Weakness — Presentation verbosity: Paper is repetitive and could be streamlined, especially Section 4.

- Question — Scalability: What are the main computational bottlenecks and planned optimizations to scale to more languages/larger datasets?

- Question — Data sensitivity: How sensitive are results to cognate choices and different datasets or subsets?

- Question — Baseline comparisons: How does the method compare quantitatively to Levenshtein, Bayesian phylogenetics, and other cognate-identification methods in accuracy and cost?

- Question — Over-weighting impact: How does varying data over-weighting change correspondences, and is there a principled selection method?

- Question — Alignment alternatives: Were alternative alignment metrics explored and why was the chosen metric selected?

- Additional comment — Notation: Improve notation for clarity.

- Additional comment — Reproducibility: Provide a public implementation to encourage reproducibility.

- Additional comment — Objectivity caveat: Clarify that “objective results” are still influenced by data and model choices.

- Recommendation: Promising, theoretically solid work recommended for acceptance pending responses to questions and minor clarifications.
