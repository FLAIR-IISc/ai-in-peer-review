- Summary: Proposes an MDL-based, EM-driven deterministic method to discover correspondences across multiple languages without strong prior assumptions, demonstrated on Slavic languages.

- Contribution — MDL framework: Applies the MDL principle to multi-language correspondence discovery, offering a theoretically grounded, assumption-light approach.

- Contribution — Deterministic algorithm: Introduces an EM-based deterministic algorithm for inferring correspondence rules, enabling reproducible results.

- Contribution — Empirical demonstration: Validates the approach on Slavic languages and explores data weighting to control granularity of discovered correspondences.

- Strength — Novelty: Combines MDL with a deterministic inference procedure, distinguishing it from similarity- or probabilistic-based methods.

- Strength — Multi-language scalability: Explicitly designed to handle more-than-pairwise language comparisons, allowing holistic analyses.

- Strength — Theoretical clarity: Presents a rigorous, well-motivated mathematical formulation of the method.

- Strength — Convincing results: Experimental outputs (phylogenetic tree and example correspondences) are persuasive and show practical promise.

- Strength — Reproducibility: Determinism enhances reproducibility and trustworthiness of findings.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Experiments are confined to Slavic languages, leaving generalizability to other families untested.

- Weakness — Implementation and scalability: Current implementation has computational bottlenecks and unclear costs for larger datasets or many languages.

- Weakness — Dependence on cognate lists: Requires pre-identified cognates, making results sensitive to input quality and completeness.

- Weakness — Subjective data weighting: Weighting controls granularity but introduces subjectivity and lacks clear guidance for selection.

- Weakness — Limited comparisons: Lacks direct quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art cognate reconstruction or other established methods.

- Question — Scalability: Requests detailed complexity analysis, identified bottlenecks, and planned optimizations for scaling.

- Question — Data weighting: Asks for objective criteria or automated procedures to choose weighting factors.

- Question — Cognate identification: Inquires about integrating automatic cognate detection and anticipated challenges.

- Question — Method comparison: Requests quantitative comparison with established methods on the same dataset.

- Question — Linguistic insights: Seeks novel linguistic findings from discovered rules beyond established knowledge.

- Additional comment — Writing and presentation: Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and includes a helpful phylogenetic visualization.

- Additional comment — Terminology caution: Urges clarification of the term “objective,” noting inevitable subjectivity in data and implementation choices.

- Overall recommendation: Strong, novel submission recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
