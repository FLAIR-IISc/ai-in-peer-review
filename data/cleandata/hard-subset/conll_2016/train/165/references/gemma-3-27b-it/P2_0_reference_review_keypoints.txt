- Presents an MDL-based method for discovering multi-language correspondences using associated strings without pre-defined distributional assumptions.
- Introduces an efficient, deterministic EM-based algorithm with optimizations for correspondence inference.
- Demonstrates multi-way analysis and quantifies linguistic similarity with experiments on Slavic languages, including pairwise and four-way analyses.
- Shows data over-weighting can control the granularity of discovered correspondences.
- Contribution: principled, data-driven MDL framework that avoids biasing structural assumptions.
- Contribution: practical, deterministic algorithm using EM and optimizations for moderate numbers of languages.
- Contribution: capability for multi-way correspondence discovery and subgroup identification beyond pairwise methods.
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding in MDL balancing model complexity and data fit.
- Strength: novel direct multi-way analysis capability distinguishing it from pairwise-focused methods.
- Strength: deterministic algorithm improves reproducibility and interpretability.
- Strength: demonstrated practicality and reasonable runtime on a standard desktop for tested datasets.
- Strength: informative experiments with phylogenetic validation and illustrative correspondence examples.
- Weakness: limited scalability and insufficiently detailed complexity analysis for larger numbers of languages and lexicons.
- Weakness: sensitivity to data quality and reliance on Swadesh lists without robust analysis of noise effects.
- Weakness: lacks direct comparative evaluation against other multi-way or adapted phylogenetic methods.
- Weakness: operates solely on orthographic forms, ignoring phonetic and morphological information.
- Question: need detailed analysis of runtime and memory scaling with languages and lexicon size and main bottlenecks.
- Question: how sensitive is the algorithm to noisy or inaccurate cognate data and are robustness techniques tested?
- Question: request for comparative evaluation versus other multi-way or phylogenetic approaches.
- Question: have phonetic or morphological features been considered or what are the pros/cons of integrating them?
- Question: what principled criteria determine the level of data over-weighting and how to choose it optimally?
- Additional: paper is generally well-written with helpful figures and sample correspondences.
- Additional: notation could be improved slightly for readability.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability, robustness, and comparative evaluation.
