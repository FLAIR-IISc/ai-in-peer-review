- Proposes an MDL-based method to find correspondences across Slavic languages by coding substring-match rules and balancing model complexity vs data encoding cost.  
- Learns model parameters with EM and evaluates on 13 Slavic languages using distance metrics, a phylogenetic tree, and qualitative correspondence examples.  
- Motivation and problem formulation are sound; MDL is appropriate and the EM justification is clear, though some mathematical derivations are hard to follow.  
- Highlights the MDL–Bayesian connection and recommends an empirical comparison with Bayesian phylogenetic tools like MrBayes.  
- Related work section is weak; should discuss computational historical linguistics methods (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen) and toolkits such as List & Moran (2013).  
- Also recommends comparisons to biological phylogenetics tools applicable to linguistic data (PAUP*, MrBayes).  
- Alignment procedure is computationally expensive and currently limits comparisons to at most five languages, harming scalability for multi-language analysis.  
- Use of neighbor-joining for tree construction has known limitations (e.g., rooting); consider more sophisticated phylogenetic inference methods.  
- Request clarification on the EM stopping criterion (full convergence vs other threshold).  
- Dataset mixes cognate sets with Swadesh lists; authors should discuss how this mixture may impact results.  
- Reliance on orthographic data and mixed scripts is a weakness—may learn script conversions instead of true phonetic correspondences; recommend phonetic transcriptions.  
- Unclear: the phrase “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286) needs explanation.  
- Unclear: the description of the merging process in the maximization step is difficult to follow and needs more detail.  
- Minor wording correction: change “focus in on” (line 440) to “focus on.”  
- References cited as relevant: List & Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009).
