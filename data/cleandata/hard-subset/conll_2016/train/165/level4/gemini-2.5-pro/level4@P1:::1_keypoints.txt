- MDL-based method to discover string-level correspondences across languages, trained with EM and evaluated on 13 Slavic languages (pairwise distances, phylogeny, learned correspondences).  
- Contribution: principled, non-parametric MDL approach that discovers patterns directly from data without strong priors.  
- Contribution: intuitive EM-based training that alternates aligning data (E-step) and merging rules (M-step) to improve the model.  
- Contribution: novel "shared description length" metric to quantify similarity among arbitrary subsets of languages (beyond pairwise).  
- Strength: sound information-theoretic MDL framework balancing model complexity and data fit.  
- Strength: clear motivation and exposition; EM-like procedure is well explained and intuitive.  
- Strength: experimental analysis is insightful—the phylogeny matches known Slavic classification and multi-language analyses are promising.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to related work in computational historical linguistics and Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes, Kitchen et al.; List & Moran; Bouchard-Cote, Warnow, Nakhleh).  
- Weakness: severe memory/runtime scalability limits (analysis effectively capped at five languages), undermining the multi-language claim.  
- Weakness: reliance on orthographic forms risks capturing script-level rather than phonetic correspondences; phonetic transcriptions would be more robust.  
- Weakness: use of neighbor-joining for phylogeny is dated and requires manual rooting; needs justification or a stronger method.  
- Weakness: unclear data handling—how Swadesh lists and cognate sets were combined and whether non-cognates were filtered is not discussed.  
- Question: please provide a detailed description of the M-step merging process—how are two patterns selected and combined to maximize description length reduction?  
- Question: what stopping criterion is used for EM—fixed iterations or convergence on total description length?  
- Question: clarify the "optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules": what is this unigram distribution and how is it computed?  
- Question: given the scalability bottleneck, what are your plans to improve efficiency to support analysis of more than five languages?
