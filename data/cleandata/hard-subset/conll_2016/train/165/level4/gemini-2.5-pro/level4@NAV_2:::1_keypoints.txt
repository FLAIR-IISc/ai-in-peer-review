- MDL-based method to discover string-level correspondences across multiple languages using correspondence rules that match substrings in cognates.  
- MDL objective balances rule-set complexity with data-description cost and is optimized with an EM algorithm.  
- Primary contribution: a novel MDL framework for discovering correspondences among more than two languages.  
- Secondary contribution: quantitative multi-language similarity analysis via shared Description Length (sDL), offering more nuance than pairwise comparisons.  

- Strength: Principled MDL framework prevents overfitting and avoids strong prior assumptions; EM optimization is well-motivated.  
- Strength: Empirical validation: pairwise analysis on 13 Slavic languages yields a phylogenetic tree largely consistent with linguistic classifications.  
- Strength: Novel multi-way analysis and sDL visualization reveal nuanced relationships among language subsets (e.g., Czech, Polish, Russian, Bulgarian).  

- Weakness: Limited comparison to related work—no conceptual or empirical comparisons to established cognate detection, borrowing analysis, or phylogenetic tools.  
- Weakness: Scalability issues—current implementation limited to up to five languages due to memory/runtime bottlenecks in alignment.  
- Weakness: Reliance on orthographic data rather than phonetic transcriptions risks capturing spelling conventions instead of true linguistic correspondences.  
- Weakness: Mixing Swadesh lists (shared meaning but not necessarily cognates) with known cognate data without discussing impacts may confound results.
