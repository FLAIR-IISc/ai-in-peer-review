- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover shared-word correspondences across Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated on 13 languages.
- MDL motivation and EM justification are sound, though some mathematical derivations are hard to follow.
- Lacks thorough comparison to existing borrowing and cognate-detection methods (e.g., work by Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen; List & Moran toolkit).
- Should include empirical comparisons to Bayesian/biological phylogenetic tools (e.g., PAUP*, MrBayes) given the MDL–Bayesian connection.
- Scalability is a major issue: the alignment procedure is a memory/runtime bottleneck, limiting experiments to at most five languages.
- Phylogenetic analysis relies on neighbor-joining; more sophisticated methods and root-placement considerations should be discussed or used.
- EM stopping criterion is not specified—unclear whether EM is run to convergence or for a fixed number of iterations.
- Model uses orthographic data, risking conflation of orthographic conventions with phonological correspondences; phonetic transcriptions are preferable.
- Dataset mixes known-cognate data with Swadesh lists where cognacy isn’t guaranteed; the impact of this mixing should be addressed.
- The term “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” is unclear and needs explanation.
- The merging procedure in the maximization step is not clearly described and requires more detail.
- Minor typo: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).
