- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover correspondences between words across 13 Slavic languages using substring-matching rules, EM training, distance metrics, a phylogenetic tree, and illustrative examples.  
- Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL is appropriate and EM use is justified, though some mathematical derivations are hard to follow.  
- Recommend empirical comparison to established Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) given the MDL–Bayes connection.  
- Related work coverage is insufficient—need stronger comparison to computational historical linguistics methods for borrowing and cognate detection (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen).  
- Mention and consider established biological phylogenetics tools (PAUP*, MrBayes) as relevant baselines for tree inference.  
- Alignment procedure has high memory/runtime complexity, limiting comparisons to at most five languages; scalability strategies should be discussed.  
- Neighbor-Joining for tree construction has known limitations (e.g., manual root specification); consider more sophisticated phylogenetic inference methods.  
- Clarify the EM stopping criterion (convergence threshold versus fixed iteration count).  
- Mixing datasets (known cognates vs Swadesh lists without guaranteed cognacy) may affect results and should be discussed.  
- Using orthographic representations (especially across scripts) is a limitation; phonetic transcriptions would better capture true sound correspondences.  
- Clarify the meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286).  
- Clarify the procedure for merging rules during the maximization step.  
- Minor typographical correction: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).
