- Proposes MDL-based method to discover cross-language correspondences, modeling substring-matching rules and optimizing model+data description length via EM on 13 Slavic languages.  
- Motivation and use of EM are sound, but several derivations lack sufficient detail and clarification.  
- Authors note resemblance to Bayesian phylogenetic inference; an empirical comparison with Bayesian tools (e.g., MrBayes, PAUP) would be valuable.  
- Major omission: no comparison to existing borrowing/cognate-detection or computational historical-linguistics methods (e.g., work by Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen; List & Moran).  
- Alignment procedure is a major limitation—memory/runtime bottlenecks restrict comparisons to at most five languages and hinder scaling to more languages.  
- Neighbor-joining for phylogenies has drawbacks (e.g., manual root specification); more sophisticated tree methods should be considered.  
- Unclear training details: whether EM is run to convergence or uses other stopping criteria is not specified.  
- Dataset composition (mix of cognate lists and Swadesh lists) may affect results and should be discussed.  
- Using orthographic data can mask real correspondences and conflate script changes with linguistic change; phonetic transcriptions would be preferable.  
- Specific unclear points: meaning of "optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules" and the merging step in the maximization phase need clearer explanation.  
- Minor editorial issue: grammatical error ("focus in on" → "focus on" at line 440).  
- Overall conclusion: interesting and promising approach but needs methodological clarifications, scalability fixes, and comparisons to prior work.
