- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-language correspondences by learning substring-matching rules and an MDL objective balancing model and data.  
- Uses EM for training and evaluates on 13 Slavic languages with distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.  
- Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL and EM choices are reasonable, but some mathematical derivations are unclear.  
- Suggests empirical comparison with Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes, PAUP) due to resemblance to Bayesian inference.  
- Recommends comparing to existing borrowing/cognate-detection and computational historical-linguistics work (e.g., Bouchard-Cote, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen; List & Moran 2013).  
- Alignment procedure scales only up to 5 languages because of memory/runtime limits, posing a major bottleneck for multi-language analyses.  
- Using neighbor-joining for phylogenies has drawbacks (e.g., manual root choice); more sophisticated tree inference methods should be explored.  
- Unclear whether EM is run to convergence or stopped by another criterion; stopping conditions should be clarified.  
- Data mixing (cognate dataset + Swadesh lists) may bias results and should be justified or controlled.  
- Working in orthographic form can hide phonetic correspondences and conflate script changes with linguistic change; phonetic transcriptions are preferable.  
- Specific technical clarifications needed: the meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and the merging step in the maximization update.  
- Minor wording edit: change “focus in on” to “focus on.”
