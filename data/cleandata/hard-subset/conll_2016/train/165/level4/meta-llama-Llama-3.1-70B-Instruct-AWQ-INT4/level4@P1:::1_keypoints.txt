- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-lingual word correspondences among Slavic languages.  
- Models substring-matching rules across languages and formulates an MDL objective balancing model and data description.  
- Trains the model with Expectation-Maximization (EM).  
- Evaluates on 13 Slavic languages, reporting distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.  
- Motivation and MDL formulation are sensible and appropriate for the task.  
- Explanation for using EM is clear.  
- Some mathematical derivations are unclear and need more detailed explanation.  
- Notes resemblance of the MDL objective to Bayesian inference and suggests comparing empirically to phylogenetic Bayesian methods (e.g., MrBayes).  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to other borrowing/cognate-detection or historical-linguistics computational methods.  
- Alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck, currently limiting comparisons to at most five languages.  
- Uses simple neighbor-joining for phylogeny, which has known drawbacks; more sophisticated methods could be considered.  
- Uses orthographic data, which may obscure correspondences, especially across different scripts.  
- Asks authors about plans to fix the alignment bottleneck, choice of phylogenetic method, EM stopping criteria, impact of phonetic vs orthographic data, and clarification of the "optimal unigram" and maximization-step merging.
