- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover correspondences between words across multiple Slavic languages by learning substring-matching rules and an MDL objective.

- Trains the model with EM and evaluates on 13 Slavic languages using distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.

Contributions:
- Introduces a novel MDL formulation for discovering cross-language correspondences.
- Develops an efficient algorithm to infer correspondence rules directly from data.
- Provides experiments demonstrating discovery of non-trivial language associations.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated approach with a clear explanation of MDL application.
- Promising experimental results that find meaningful correspondences.
- EM training is well-justified and the algorithm is clearly described.

Weaknesses:
- No comparison to other borrowing, cognate-detection, or historical-linguistics methods.
- Alignment procedure has a significant memory/runtime bottleneck, limiting scalability.
- Uses neighbor-joining for phylogeny despite known disadvantages; more advanced methods advisable.

Questions to authors:
- How will the alignment memory/runtime bottleneck be addressed to scale to more languages?
- Have you considered Bayesian or maximum-likelihood methods for phylogenetic inference?
- What is the EM stopping criterion and how do you ensure convergence?

Minor issues:
- Typo: replace "focus in on" with "focus on" (line 440).
- Some derivations need clearer explanations or illustrative examples.
