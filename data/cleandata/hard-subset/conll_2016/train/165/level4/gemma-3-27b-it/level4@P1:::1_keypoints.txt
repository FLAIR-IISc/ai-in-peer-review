- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover multilingual correspondences across Slavic languages using substring-matching rules, trained with EM and evaluated on 13 languages.
- Motivation and EM approach are sound; core ideas are clear though some derivational details are unclear.
- Suggests comparing the MDL objective to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) for empirical insights.

Contributions:
- Applies MDL to discover multilingual correspondences without strong priors, enabling relationships among any subset of languages.
- Introduces an efficient deterministic algorithm for inferring correspondences directly from data.
- Provides experimental evaluation showing the method finds meaningful linguistic groupings and example correspondences.

Strengths:
- Novel application of MDL that lets data drive correspondence discovery rather than imposing strong structural assumptions.
- Efficient deterministic algorithm capable of handling multiple languages simultaneously, avoiding pairwise combinatorial blowup.
- Comprehensive evaluation using NCD, phylogenetic trees, and qualitative examples.
- Produces quantifiable similarity measures via shared description length for nuanced linguistic analysis.

Weaknesses:
- Scalability issues: memory/runtime bottleneck limits comparisons to at most five languages in practice.
- Phylogenetic method choice (neighbor joining) is potentially suboptimal and requires manual rooting; EM stopping criterion is unclear.
- Data mixing (Swadesh lists + other cognates) may introduce inconsistencies or bias; reliance on orthography may hide phonetic correspondences.
- Limited comparison to established computational historical-linguistics methods and related prior work.

Questions to authors:
- What strategies will you use to address the scalability limitations of the alignment procedure?
- What is the EM stopping criterion—convergence threshold or fixed iterations?
- How sensitive are results to mixing Swadesh lists with other cognate data; have you tried separate analyses?
- Have you tried phonetic transcriptions instead of orthographic forms, and what was the impact?
- What do you mean by “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286)?
