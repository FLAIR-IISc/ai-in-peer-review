- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-lingual word correspondences, modeled as substring-matching rules and optimized with EM on 13 Slavic languages.
- Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL and EM are reasonable choices, though some derivations are unclear.
- Authors note resemblance to Bayesian inference; an empirical comparison with Bayesian phylogenetic tools (e.g., MrBayes) would be valuable.
- Related-work coverage is incomplete: lacks comparisons to borrowing/cognate detection and historical-linguistics methods (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen, List & Moran).
- Alignment procedure has high memory/runtime costs, currently limiting evaluation to ≤5 languages; scalability strategies are needed.
- Using Neighbor Joining for phylogeny has known drawbacks (e.g., manual rooting); more sophisticated tree inference methods should be considered.
- EM algorithm details are incomplete: stopping criterion (convergence vs alternative) is not specified.
- Dataset composition mixes cognate datasets and Swadesh lists (which may not ensure cognacy); potential impact on results should be addressed.
- Working from orthographic forms may obscure true phonological correspondences and confound script-related changes; phonetic transcriptions would mitigate this.
- Some technical points are unclear: definition of the “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286).
- The merging process in the maximization (M) step of EM is not clearly explained.
- Suggests comparing to biological phylogeny tools (PAUP*, MrBayes) and mentioning List & Moran (2013) among related resources.
- Minor editorial issue: replace “focus in on” with “focus on” (line 440).
