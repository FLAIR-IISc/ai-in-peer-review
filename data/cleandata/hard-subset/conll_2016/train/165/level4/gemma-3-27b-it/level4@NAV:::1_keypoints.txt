- Proposes an MDL-based method to identify cross-linguistic substring correspondences across 13 Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated via distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.  
- Motivation for MDL and EM is sound, but some mathematical derivations are not fully clear.  
- Should compare empirically to established Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes).  
- Related-work discussion is thin; needs coverage of cognate detection, borrowing identification, and key studies/tools (Bouchard-Cote, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen; List & Moran 2013; PAUP, MrBayes).  
- Alignment procedure is computationally limited to comparisons of up to five languages, which is a significant bottleneck for phylogenetic applications.  
- Using neighbor-joining for tree construction has known limitations (including manual rooting); more sophisticated phylogenetic methods should be considered.  
- EM stopping criterion is unclear and needs specification (convergence threshold or other metric).  
- Mixing a cognate dataset with a Swadesh list may bias results; the impact of dataset composition should be investigated.  
- Reliance on orthographic forms rather than phonetic transcriptions risks capturing script changes instead of true linguistic correspondences; phonetic data is preferable.  
- The phrase “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286) is unclear and needs clarification.  
- The merging process in the maximization step requires more detailed explanation.  
- Minor editorial fix: “focus in on” should be “focus on” (line 440).
