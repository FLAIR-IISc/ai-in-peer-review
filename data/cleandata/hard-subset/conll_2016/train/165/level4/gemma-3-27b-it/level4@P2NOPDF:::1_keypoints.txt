- Presents a novel MDL-based method to discover cross-linguistic correspondences across Slavic languages, modeling substring-matching rules and optimizing an MDL objective via EM.  
- Evaluated on 13 Slavic languages with distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and illustrative correspondence examples.  
- Motivation and use of MDL and EM are reasonable, but some mathematical derivations lack clarity.  
- Recommend empirical comparison with Bayesian inference methods (e.g., MrBayes) given the MDL–Bayesian connection.  
- Related work section is incomplete; missing comparisons to cognate detection, borrowing identification, and tools/studies by Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, List & Moran (2013), Kitchen, and phylogenetic tools like PAUP*/MrBayes.  
- Major computational bottleneck: memory/runtime limits comparison to at most five languages, raising scalability concerns for larger phylogenies.  
- Choice of neighbor-joining for tree construction is questionable; more sophisticated phylogenetic methods should be considered.  
- Clarify EM termination condition (convergence vs. alternative stopping criterion).  
- Data mixing of cognate datasets and Swadesh lists may affect results and needs discussion regarding differing data quality.  
- Using orthographic data can obscure true correspondences; phonetic transcriptions are recommended to reduce script-related artifacts.  
- Unclear points needing clarification: meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and details of the merging process in the maximization step.  
- Minor wording correction: replace “focus in on” with “focus on” (line 440).  
- References suggested for inclusion: List & Moran (2013) toolkit and Kitchen et al. (2009) Bayesian phylogenetic analysis.
