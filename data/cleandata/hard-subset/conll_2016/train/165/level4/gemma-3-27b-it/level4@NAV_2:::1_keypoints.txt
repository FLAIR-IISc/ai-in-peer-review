- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover multilingual correspondences between words across Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated on 13 languages.  
- Primary contribution: novel application of Minimum Description Length to multilingual correspondence discovery with an efficient algorithm.  
- Secondary contribution: practical demonstration on Slavic languages with quantitative (NCD phylogeny) and qualitative (example correspondences) results.  
- Introduces and evaluates data over-weighting as a way to control correspondence granularity.  
- Strength: MDL provides a principled balance between model complexity and data fit, avoiding arbitrary assumptions.  
- Strength: Method can handle more than two languages simultaneously, capturing broader cross-linguistic patterns.  
- Strength: Algorithm and EM/MDL code are clearly and thoroughly presented, facilitating reproducibility and extension.  
- Strength: Experiments produce linguistically plausible correspondences and a phylogenetic tree aligning with known classifications.  
- Weakness: Scalability is limited by memory and runtime bottlenecks, restricting practical use to about five languages.  
- Weakness: Use of neighbor-joining for tree inference is potentially problematic (long-branch attraction, manual rooting); more advanced methods may be preferable.  
- Weakness: Dataset mixing Swadesh lists and other cognates may introduce bias, and reliance on orthographic forms rather than phonetic transcriptions is a limitation.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to existing methods in cognate detection, borrowing identification, and historical reconstruction weakens situating of the work.  
- Question: What strategies are planned to address memory and runtime bottlenecks and improve scalability?  
- Question: Were more sophisticated phylogenetic methods (e.g., Bayesian inference) considered, and why was neighbor-joining chosen?  
- Question: Did the authors test the effect of using only one data source (Swadesh vs other cognates) on results?  
- Question: Please clarify “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286).  
- Question: Please provide more detail on the merging process in the maximization step of the EM algorithm.  
- Minor comment: Fix grammatical issue “focus in on” → “focus on” (line 440); added references to List & Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009) are helpful.
