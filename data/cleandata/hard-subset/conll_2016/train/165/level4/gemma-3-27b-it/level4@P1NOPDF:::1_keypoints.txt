- Paper proposes MDL-based method with EM to discover cross-language word correspondences, tested on 13 Slavic languages.
- Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL and EM are reasonable choices, but some derivations are unclear.
- Reviewer suggests empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) given resemblance to Bayesian inference.
- Lacks comparison to existing borrowing/cognate-detection and computational historical-linguistics methods (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen) and to List & Moran (2013).
- Alignment procedure has high memory/runtime requirements, limiting comparisons to at most five languages; scalability is a major concern.
- Using neighbor-joining for phylogenetic trees has known disadvantages (e.g., manual root specification); more sophisticated methods should be explored.
- Unclear whether EM is run to convergence or stopped by another criterion; clarify stopping condition.
- Dataset mixes known-cognate data with Swadesh lists that may not be cognate-only, which could affect results.
- Using orthographic forms can obscure true correspondences (esp. across scripts); phonetic transcriptions are recommended.
- Two unclear technical points to clarify: the meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and the merging step during maximization.
- Minor language-editing issue: “focus in on” should be “focus on.”
- Reviewer cites relevant references to be included/considered (List & Moran 2013; Kitchen et al. 2009).
