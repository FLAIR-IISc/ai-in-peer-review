- Proposes an MDL-based method for cross-lingual correspondence discovery across 13 Slavic languages, learning substring-matching rules and training via EM, evaluated with distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.
- Motivation is sound and MDL is appropriate; EM justification is well presented but some mathematical derivations lack clarity.
- Notes similarity between the MDL objective and Bayesian inference and recommends empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes).
- Fails to compare against existing borrowing-detection and cognate-identification work (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen) and omits discussion of List & Moran (2013).
- Should consider tools from biological phylogeny (paup, MrBayes) as additional baselines or comparisons.
- Alignment procedure is a major computational bottleneck, currently limiting analyses to at most five languages; scalability solutions are needed.
- Using neighbor joining for tree construction is limited (e.g., manual rooting); more sophisticated phylogenetic methods should be investigated.
- Unclear whether the EM algorithm is run to convergence or stopped by another criterion; stopping criterion must be specified.
- Dataset mixes cognate and potentially non-cognate Swadesh items; the effect of this mixture on results should be analyzed.
- Reliance on orthographic data risks capturing script or orthography changes rather than true phonetic correspondences; using phonetic transcriptions is recommended.
- Specific unclear technical points: the phrase “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and the merging step in the maximization procedure need clarification.
- Minor editorial issue: replace “focus in on” with “focus on” (line 440).
- Recommend adding references: List & Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009) (and other relevant literature) to situate the work.
