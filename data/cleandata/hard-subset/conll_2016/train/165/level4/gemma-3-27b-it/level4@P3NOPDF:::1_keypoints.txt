- Proposes a novel MDL-based method for discovering cross-linguistic correspondences in 13 Slavic languages using rule-based substring matching and EM training.  
- Motivation and use of MDL/EM are well-justified, though some mathematical derivations need clearer exposition.  
- Given the MDL–Bayesian connection, an empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) is recommended.  
- Related work section is incomplete; should discuss cognate detection, borrowing, and key researchers (Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen) and include List & Moran (2013).  
- Computational/alignment limitations: current implementation appears limited to comparisons among ≤5 languages, causing scalability, memory, and runtime concerns.  
- Using neighbor joining for phylogeny is problematic (manual rooting); consider more advanced phylogenetic methods and clarify the EM stopping criterion.  
- Dataset composition (mixing cognates and possible non-cognate Swadesh lists) could bias results and should be discussed.  
- Reliance on orthographic forms across scripts may conflate script variation with linguistic change; phonetic transcriptions would strengthen validity.  
- Clarify technical points: meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and details of the merging step in the M-step.  
- Minor stylistic correction: change “focus in on” to “focus on.”  
- Overall: interesting and promising contribution, but needs improvements in scalability, phylogenetic methodology, dataset handling, and clarity to increase impact.
