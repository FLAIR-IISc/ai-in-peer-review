- Proposes an MDL-based method to identify cross-language correspondences using substring-alignment codes and EM, evaluated on 13 Slavic languages with distance metrics, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.  
- Motivation and theoretical framework are sound; MDL is appropriate and the rationale for EM is well-argued.  
- Some mathematical derivations are unclear and need clearer exposition.  
- Suggests an empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic inference (e.g., MrBayes) to contextualize the MDL objective.  
- Lacks comparison to existing borrowing and cognate-detection methods and computational historical-linguistics work (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen, List & Moran).  
- Recommends considering tools from biological phylogenetics (PAUP*, MrBayes) as baselines or comparisons.  
- Alignment procedure appears memory- and runtime-limited, restricting comparisons to at most five languages at a time — scalability concerns.  
- Using neighbor-joining for phylogenetic trees has known drawbacks (e.g., manual rooting); consider more advanced tree-building methods.  
- Unclear whether EM is run to convergence or stopped by another criterion; clarify stopping rule.  
- Combining cognate datasets with non-cognate Swadesh lists may bias or affect results; discuss impact.  
- Presenting data in orthographic form may hide true correspondences or reflect script changes; phonetic transcriptions are recommended.  
- Unclear terminology: clarify “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286).  
- The merging process in the maximization step is insufficiently explained and needs clarification.  
- Minor edit: replace “focus in on” with “focus on” (line 440).  
- Suggested references to include/List: Johann-Mattis List & Steven Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009).
