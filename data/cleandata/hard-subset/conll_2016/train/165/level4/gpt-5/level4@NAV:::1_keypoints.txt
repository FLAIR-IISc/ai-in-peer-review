- Paper proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-language word correspondences, modeling substring-alignment rules and optimizing an MDL objective via EM, evaluated on 13 Slavic languages.  
- Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL is appropriate and EM rationale is clear, though some derivations lack transparency.  
- Suggest empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) given the MDL–Bayes connection.  
- Missing related-work comparisons to borrowing/cognate detection and computational historical-linguistics methods (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen; List & Moran 2013).  
- Alignment procedure has memory/runtime limits that restrict comparisons to at most five languages, which undermines multi-language analysis; strategies to scale up are needed.  
- Phylogenetic tree construction uses neighbor joining, which has known drawbacks (e.g., root specification); stronger tree inference methods should be considered.  
- Clarify EM stopping criterion: whether EM is run to convergence or stopped by another rule.  
- Dataset mixing concern: combining a cognate dataset with Swadesh lists may bias results and should be analyzed.  
- Data in orthographic form may mask true linguistic correspondences or conflate script conversion with phonological correspondences; phonetic transcriptions would help.  
- Two unclear methodological points: meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286) and the merging procedure in the M-step.  
- Minor language edit: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).  
- Recommended citations to include: List & Moran (2013) toolkit and Bayesian phylogenetic analyses (e.g., Kitchen et al. 2009).
