- Contribution: MDL-based framework (PS-2 code) and deterministic EM-like training to discover multi-way substring correspondences with shortest-path alignment and greedy MDL-guided merging.  
- Contribution: Toolkit using Normalized Compression Distance (NCD) for pairwise distances and shared description length (sDL) to quantify shared structure across language subsets.  
- Contribution: Slavic case study showing linguistically interpretable rules, a plausible phylogeny, and a practical data over-weighting knob to control rule granularity.

- Strength: Principled, assumption-light modeling—MDL trades model complexity and data fit and yields interpretable, comparable objectives.  
- Strength: Supports correspondences over arbitrary language subsets, enabling beyond-pairwise analyses and fine-grained sDL decomposition.  
- Strength: Deterministic, interpretable pipeline (shortest-path alignment + greedy merging) that recovers meaningful phonological/orthographic/morphological patterns.  
- Strength: Empirical plausibility—NCDs and neighbor-joining tree align with known Slavic groupings; four-language analysis reveals sensible subgroups.  
- Strength: Practical flexibility—initialization and data over-weighting let users inject linguistic bias and control granularity.

- Weakness: Scalability bottlenecks in alignment graph and EM limit practical analysis to ~5 languages, constraining multi-language and phylogenetic applications.  
- Weakness: Use of orthography (mixed scripts) conflates script conversion with phonological correspondences, possibly obscuring true sound changes.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical comparisons to established cognate-detection and phylogenetic methods and tools, weakening contextual evaluation.  
- Weakness: Methodological underspecification—details missing on optimal unigram, M-step merge mechanics/search space, and EM stopping criteria, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Dataset heterogeneity—mixing Swadesh lists and cognate sets may introduce biases and make cross-dataset comparisons unclear.

- Question: How will you scale alignment/EM beyond five languages (e.g., A*, pruning, beam search, indexing), and how does runtime scale with vocabulary/alphabet size?  
- Question: What are the EM stopping criteria, sensitivity to them, and do you use restarts?  
- Question: How are candidate merges generated/evaluated in the M-step—constraints on contiguity, length, frequency, and defenses against merge-order/local minima?  
- Question: What precisely is the “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules”—global vs. per-language estimate and handling of zero-count symbols in coding?  
- Question: Have you tried script normalization or phonetic transcriptions, and can you quantify rules that are script-conversion vs. true sound correspondences?  
- Question: Are Swadesh and cognate datasets ever combined, or always analyzed separately—can results be stratified by source for robustness?  
- Question: Can you compare the NCD-based tree to trees from standard phylogenetic toolkits (MrBayes, PAUP) and compare cognate identification to established methods or annotations?  
- Question: Will you release code, data splits, and parameters (e.g., over-weighting α) to enable replication?  
- Question: Any empirical evidence or ablations showing sensitivity to initialization, over-weighting, or hyperparameters?
