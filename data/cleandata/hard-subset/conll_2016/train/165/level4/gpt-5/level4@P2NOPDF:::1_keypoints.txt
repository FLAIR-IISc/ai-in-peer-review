- Presents an MDL-based method for discovering cross-linguistic substring correspondence and word-level alignments among multiple Slavic languages using an MDL objective and EM training.
- Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL is reasonable and the rationale for EM is clear, though some derivations are hard to follow.
- Notes a connection between the MDL objective and Bayesian inference; an empirical comparison to established Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) is warranted.
- Lacks comparisons to prior work on borrowing/cognate detection and computational historical linguistics (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen, and List & Moran 2013).
- Omits discussion of biological phylogenetics tools (e.g., PAUP, MrBayes) that could serve as baselines or points of comparison.
- Major memory/runtime bottleneck limits experiments to at most five languages, which undermines the multi-language and phylogenetic claims; discuss pruning, blocking, approximate inference, or parallelization as mitigations.
- Neighbor-joining for phylogenetic inference has drawbacks (manual rooting, sensitivity to distance estimates); consider more robust Bayesian or maximum-likelihood methods.
- Clarify EM training details: do you run to convergence, and specify stopping criteria (relative improvement threshold, held-out validation, or max iterations).
- Data mixture concern: combining cognate-annotated data with non-cognate Swadesh lists may bias inference and evaluation; a sensitivity analysis is needed.
- Using orthographic forms can conflate script conversion with true sound correspondences; discuss feasibility and impact of using phonetic transcriptions.
- Clarity request: formally define the “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and explain its role in the objective.
- Clarity request: provide a step-by-step description or pseudocode of the M-step merging procedure, including merge criteria, conflict resolution, and effect on code length.
- Minor editorial fix: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).
- References suggested to include List & Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009) for relevant background and comparison.
