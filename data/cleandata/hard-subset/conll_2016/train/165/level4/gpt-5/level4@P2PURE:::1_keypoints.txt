- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-linguistic substring correspondences across Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated on 13 languages.  
- Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL and EM rationale is clear, though some derivations are not fully clear.  
- The MDL–Bayesian connection invites empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes).  
- Lacks comparisons to related work in borrowing/cognate detection and historical linguistics (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen; List & Moran 2013).  
- Alignment memory/runtime bottleneck limits comparisons to at most five languages; needs strategies to scale for multi-language analysis.  
- Neighbor-joining for phylogenetic inference has known drawbacks (e.g., manual root choice); consider more sophisticated phylogenetic methods.  
- EM convergence/stopping criterion is unclear—need to state whether EM runs to convergence or uses another stopping rule.  
- Combining cognate data with non-cognate Swadesh lists may affect results; the impact of this dataset mixture should be assessed.  
- Using orthographic forms may conflate script changes with linguistic correspondences; phonetic transcriptions would mitigate this.  
- Two unclear technical points require clarification: the meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and the merging procedure in the M-step.  
- Minor edit: replace “focus in on” with “focus on” (line 440).  
- Suggested references to cite/compare: List & Moran (2013) toolkit and Kitchen et al. (2009) Bayesian phylogenetic analysis.
