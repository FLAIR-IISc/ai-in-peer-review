- Paper proposes an MDL-based method for discovering cross-linguistic correspondences, coding substring-matching rules and training with EM on 13 Slavic languages.
- Motivation and overall formulation are sensible and EM rationale is clear, but some derivations are not fully clear.
- The resemblance between the MDL objective and Bayesian inference suggests an empirical comparison with Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes).
- The paper lacks comparison to borrowing and cognate-detection methods and other computational historical-linguistics work (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen).
- Relevant prior work such as List and Moran (2013) is not discussed and should be cited.
- Biological phylogeny tools (e.g., PAUP, MrBayes) could serve as baselines or points of reference but are not considered.
- Major memory/runtime bottleneck in the alignment procedure restricts experiments to at most five languages; need strategies for scaling (pruning, approximation, batching, compact representations).
- Use of neighbor-joining for phylogenetic reconstruction has known disadvantages (e.g., manual root choice); more sophisticated methods should be considered.
- EM stopping criterion is not specified — clarify whether EM runs to convergence or uses another stopping rule and its runtime/performance impact.
- Mixing cognate-labeled and Swadesh (not-necessarily-cognate) datasets may affect results and should be assessed.
- Working in orthography can obscure genuine correspondences and may capture script conversion; phonetic transcriptions would mitigate this limitation.
- The phrase “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” is unclear and needs clarification.
- The merging procedure in the maximization step is not fully explained; a detailed description or worked example is requested.
- Minor wording correction: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).
- Recommended references to include: List & Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009).
