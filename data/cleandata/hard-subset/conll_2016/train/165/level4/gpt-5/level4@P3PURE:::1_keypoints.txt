- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-language substring correspondences in 13 Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated via distances, phylogenetic tree, and examples.  
- Motivation and formulation are sensible and MDL + EM is reasonable, but some derivations are unclear.  
- Notes resemblance to Bayesian inference; recommends empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes).  
- Lacks comparison to prior work on borrowing/cognate detection and computational historical linguistics (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen); must discuss List & Moran (2013).  
- Alignment procedure has a memory/runtime bottleneck limiting comparisons to ≤5 languages; needs scalable solutions to include more languages.  
- Building trees with neighbor joining has known drawbacks (e.g., manual rooting); consider more sophisticated phylogenetic methods.  
- EM stopping criterion is unclear—does EM run to convergence or use another stopping rule?  
- Mixing cognate dataset with Swadesh lists may bias or confound experimental results.  
- Using orthographic forms can obscure correspondences and reflect script changes; phonetic transcriptions would mitigate this.  
- Unclear terminology/steps: clarify “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and the merging performed in the maximization step.  
- Minor edit: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).  
- Cited relevant references to include/discuss: List & Moran (2013); Kitchen et al. (2009).
