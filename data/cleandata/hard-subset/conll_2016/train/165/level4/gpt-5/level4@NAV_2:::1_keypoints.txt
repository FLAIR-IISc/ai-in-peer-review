- Proposes MDL-based method to discover cross-linguistic correspondences across Slavic languages, using substring-alignment rules and EM for training.
- Motivation and setup are sensible; MDL is plausible and EM rationale is clear.
- Some derivations are not fully transparent and need clearer exposition.
- Paper notes MDL–Bayesian relationship; should include empirical comparisons to Bayesian phylogenetic approaches (e.g., MrBayes).
- Missing comparisons to borrowing/cognate detection and computational historical linguistics work (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen, List & Moran).
- Alignment procedure has memory/runtime bottlenecks limiting analyses to about five languages; scalability strategies are needed.
- Neighbor-joining for phylogenetic inference has known drawbacks (e.g., manual rooting); consider more advanced tree inference methods.
- Clarify EM stopping rule: run to convergence or use another criterion?
- Combining cognate dataset with possibly non-cognate Swadesh lists may affect results; discuss impacts.
- Using raw orthography can obscure correspondences and capture script differences; phonetic transcriptions would help.
- Clarify what “the optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” means (line 286).
- Explain the merging operation in the maximization step more clearly.
- Minor wording fix: “focus in on” → “focus on” (line 440).
- Include or cite relevant references such as List & Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009).
