- Proposal: MDL-based method to discover cross-lingual substring correspondence rules across Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated via distances, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.  
- Strength: Motivation and formulation are sound; MDL is appropriate and EM justification is well-presented.  
- Weakness: Some mathematical derivations remain unclear and need clarification.  
- Suggestion: Compare empirically to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) due to the MDL–Bayesian resemblance.  
- Related work gap: Missing comparisons to borrowing detection, cognate identification, and computational historical linguistics (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen).  
- Related tools: Acknowledge List & Moran (2013) toolkit and biological phylogenetics tools (PAUP*, MrBayes) even if not directly applicable.  
- Scalability: Alignment memory/runtime bottleneck restricts comparisons to ≤5 languages; need a plan to scale to more languages.  
- Phylogenetic method: Neighbor-joining has limitations (e.g., manual rooting); consider maximum likelihood or Bayesian inference for tree construction.  
- EM details: Stopping criterion (convergence vs fixed iterations) is unspecified and should be defined.  
- Data composition: Mixed cognate and non-cognate Swadesh lists may affect results; clarify impact.  
- Orthography vs phonetics: Using orthographic data may confound genuine linguistic correspondences (script effects); phonetic transcriptions recommended.  
- Specific clarifications needed: Explain “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and the merging step in the maximization phase.  
- Minor edit: Change “focus in on” to “focus on.”
