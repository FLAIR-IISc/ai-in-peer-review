- Introduces MDL-based method for discovering cross-lingual substring correspondences among Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated via distances, a phylogenetic tree, and examples.  
- Motivation and MDL formulation are sound and EM use is justified, but some mathematical derivations are unclear.  
- Authors draw parallels between MDL and Bayesian inference; an empirical comparison with Bayesian phylogenetic tools (e.g., MrBayes) is recommended.  
- Related-work coverage is incomplete—missing established computational historical-linguistics methods for borrowing/cognate detection and relevant phylogeny tools (e.g., Bouchard-Côte, Warnow, Nakhleh, List & Moran, PAUP, MrBayes).  
- Alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck limiting comparisons to ≤5 languages; scalability needs to be addressed.  
- Phylogenetic tree construction uses neighbor-joining with manual root specification; more robust methods (e.g., maximum likelihood) should be considered.  
- EM stopping criterion is unclear—state whether EM runs to convergence or uses another stopping rule.  
- Mixing cognate and non-cognate datasets (e.g., Swadesh lists) may bias results; the impact should be analyzed.  
- Reliance on orthographic data can obscure true linguistic correspondences across scripts; phonetic transcriptions are preferable.  
- Specific unclear points: clarify the meaning of the “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” (line 286).  
- Maximization-step merging process is insufficiently explained and needs clearer description.  
- Minor editorial fix: change “focus in on” to “focus on” (line 440).  
- Provide/add missing bibliographic references cited in the review (e.g., List & Moran 2013; Kitchen et al. 2009).
