- MDL-based algorithm infers objective, statistically significant word correspondences across multiple languages without assuming predefined distributions.  
- Novel two-part MDL formulation with deterministic EM yields a statistically rigorous, reproducible method for correspondence discovery.  
- Method enables multi-language (beyond pairwise) analyses, revealing nuanced groupings and producing phylogenetic trees aligning with known classifications.  
- Data over-weighting (α ≠ 1) offers practical control over granularity, allowing expert-driven refinement while preserving MDL ordering.  
- Reliance on orthographic data risks conflating script changes with phonological correspondences, obscuring true sound patterns.  
- Alignment procedure has combinatorial scalability limits (practical to ≤5 languages) with no concrete mitigation proposed.  
- Insufficient benchmarking against established historical-linguistics tools and limited empirical validation of the Bayesian analogy.  
- Question: How would phonetic transcriptions (e.g., IPA) change discovered correspondences, especially for script-divergent pairs?  
- Question: What specific computational optimizations could extend the method to ≥6 languages without prohibitive runtime?  
- Question: Can the data-weighting scheme (α > 1) be formalized to incorporate linguistic priors (e.g., known sound laws) while retaining MDL objectivity?
