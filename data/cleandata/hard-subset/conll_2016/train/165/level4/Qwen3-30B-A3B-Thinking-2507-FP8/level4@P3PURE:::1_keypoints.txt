- Positive: Approach is well-motivated; MDL is a reasonable objective and EM rationale is clear, though some derivations are unclear.  
- Suggestion: Empirically compare the MDL/Bayesian parallel to established Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes).  
- Missing comparisons: Paper lacks comparison to borrowing detection, cognate ID, and historical-linguistics methods (e.g., work by Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen et al.).  
- Missing citation/tool: Cite and compare to List & Moran (2013) open-source toolkit for quantitative historical linguistics.  
- Algorithmic bottleneck: Alignment procedure has memory/runtime limits restricting comparisons to ≤5 languages; authors should explain plans to scale to more languages.  
- Tree construction: Using neighbor-joining is suboptimal (e.g., manual root choice); consider likelihood-based or Bayesian phylogenetic methods.  
- EM details: Paper does not state whether EM runs to convergence or what stopping criterion is used.  
- Data composition: Mixing cognate and non-cognate (Swadesh) lists may bias results; authors should assess this impact.  
- Orthography issue: Using orthographic forms can obscure true correspondences and conflate script differences; phonetic transcriptions are recommended.  
- Unclear technical points: Clarify the meaning of “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules.”  
- Method detail missing: Explain the merging procedure used in the maximization step.  
- Minor edit: Typo—change “focus in on” to “focus on.”
