- Proposes MDL-based method to discover cross-lingual substring correspondences across Slavic languages, trained with EM and evaluated on 13 languages (distance measures, phylogenetic tree, examples).  
- Motivation and use of MDL and EM are well-justified, but some derivations are unclear.  
- Authors draw parallels between their MDL objective and Bayesian inference; empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes) is recommended.  
- Paper lacks comparison to established historical-linguistics methods for cognate detection and borrowing (e.g., Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen et al. 2009).  
- Should benchmark against open-source tools/frameworks like List & Moran (2013) and phylogenetic tools from biology (PAUP*, MrBayes).  
- Alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck limiting comparisons to ≤5 languages; authors should propose solutions to scale to more languages.  
- Phylogenetic tree inference uses neighbor joining and requires manual rooting; more robust methods (e.g., maximum likelihood) should be considered.  
- EM convergence behavior is unclear—whether it runs to full convergence or uses another stopping criterion.  
- Evaluation mixes cognate (Swadesh) and non-cognate data, which may confound results and needs clarification.  
- Using orthographic forms risks capturing script/orthography differences rather than true phonological correspondences; phonetic transcriptions are suggested.  
- Unclear technical details: meaning of "optimal unigram for symbol usage across all rules" (line 286).  
- The merging process in the maximization step is insufficiently explained.  
- Minor wording issue: line 440 "focus in on" → "focus on."
