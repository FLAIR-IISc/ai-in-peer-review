- Proposes an MDL-based framework (PS-2 code) to discover string-level correspondences across multiple languages, allowing rules active on arbitrary subsets and trained with shortest-path alignment and a deterministic EM-like merge procedure.
- Contributions: enables subset-wise multi-language correspondence discovery and quantification via shared description length (sDL).
- Contributions: practical deterministic learning combining optimal alignment (Dijkstra) with greedy rule merging without parametric rule-shape assumptions.
- Contributions: empirical study on Slavic languages yielding plausible phylogeny and interpretable correspondences.
- Strength: general, assumption-light framework supporting heterogeneous rule shapes and arbitrary-length correspondences across subsets.
- Strength: interpretable information-theoretic analyses (NCD phylogeny aligns with known groupings; sDL gives fine-grained shared-structure measures).
- Strength: deterministic, reproducible learning with option to seed rules to incorporate prior knowledge.
- Strength: alignment formulated as shortest-paths gives exact E-steps and transparent costs tied to the MDL objective.
- Weakness: limited evaluation and missing baselines — no quantitative comparisons to cognate/alignment methods, no precision/recall or held-out compression.
- Weakness: uses orthography-only inputs without phonemic normalization or controlled transliteration, conflating spelling with sound change.
- Weakness: scalability concerns — E-step complexity and memory limit experiments to small datasets and few languages, with no scaling curves.
- Weakness: subjective α-weighting departs from MDL objectivity and lacks principled selection, risking cherry-picked granularity.
- Weakness: insufficient ablations — no sensitivity analyses for smoothing, code choices, merge order, or local-optima behavior; coding-term correctness not stress-tested.
- Question: can you report quantitative baselines (cognate ID accuracy, alignment F1) and held-out compression?
- Question: how do results change under transliteration or phonemic normalization?
- Question: provide runtime and memory scaling with number of languages, vocabulary size, and rule set size.
- Question: how is α chosen and can granularity be tied to MDL-based model selection or MDL-normalized significance tests?
- Question: will code and data be released to ensure reproducibility?
