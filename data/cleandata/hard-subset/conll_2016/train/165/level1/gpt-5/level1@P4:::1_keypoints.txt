- Proposes an MDL-based framework to discover string-level correspondences across multiple languages using a two-part code, shortest-path alignment, and EM-like greedy rule merging.

- Contribution: MDL formulation and deterministic algorithm (PS-2 two-part code + shortest-path alignment) enabling EM-style optimization of correspondence rules.

- Contribution: Methodology for multi-language similarity via shared Description Lengths over any language subset, allowing analyses beyond pairwise measures.

- Contribution: Practical data over-weighting knob (α) to control rule granularity and surface near-significant correspondences when data are scarce.

- Strength: Principled, assumption-light modeling with explicit code and MDL regularization producing interpretable correspondences.

- Strength: Supports rules over arbitrary subsets of languages, naturally producing 2–3-way rules when 4-way evidence is insufficient.

- Strength: Deterministic, interpretable learning path from singleton rules via greedy merging; allows seeding with known rules.

- Strength: Empirical results (pairwise NCD phylogeny and multi-way DL) align with known Slavic linguistic structure and show meaningful qualitative correspondences.

- Strength: Clear presentation of computational procedures (shortest-path alignment, E/M complexities) and illustrative qualitative examples.

- Weakness: Limited quantitative evaluation and no comparisons to established baselines, held-out tests, or human-validated gold correspondences.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns due to high E-/M-step complexity, memory bottlenecks, and lack of analysis on growth of |R| and A or pruning guarantees.

- Weakness: Orthography-driven analysis risks confounding script/orthography with phonology; results may reflect script choices rather than linguistic change.

- Weakness: Some coding details (L(Π), LN(TΠ), code(σ)/code(π), number composition) are under-explained or not formally derived.

- Weakness: Introducing α departs from pure MDL objectivity without principled guidance, sensitivity analysis, or bounds on its effects.

- Weakness: Reproducibility and presentation issues: sparse data/preprocessing details, no code release, inconsistent references, and unclear notation.

- Question: Please provide formal derivations for data-to-model coding terms in L(Π) and L(Σi) and the enumeration behind the weak number composition.

- Question: What constraints or pruning heuristics restrict rule lengths/merges, and can you characterize approximation guarantees against combinatorial explosion?

- Question: How sensitive are results to Laplace smoothing, initialization (null vs seeded), merging schedule, and do you have convergence diagnostics or multiple-start experiments?

- Question: How is the over-weighting parameter α chosen in practice, and can you provide a sensitivity analysis showing stability across α?

- Question: Can you disentangle script effects via transliteration or phonemic normalization, and does the phylogeny remain consistent under such normalization?

- Question: Can you include comparisons to baseline methods and evaluation against a gold set of expert correspondences to quantify accuracy?
