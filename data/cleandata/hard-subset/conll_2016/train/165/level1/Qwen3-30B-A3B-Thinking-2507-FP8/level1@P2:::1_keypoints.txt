- Proposes an MDL-based method for discovering correspondences across multiple languages without assuming a specific correspondence distribution.  
- Presents an efficient algorithm that can infer correspondence rules for more than two languages simultaneously.  
- Introduces data over-weighting to control the granularity of discovered correspondences.  

- The MDL formulation is theoretically sound and avoids arbitrary assumptions of prior methods.  
- Experiments on Slavic languages show strong empirical validation, recovering expected linguistic groupings and a plausible phylogeny.  
- Data over-weighting is a practical mechanism to select desired levels of detail in correspondences.  
- Provides clear qualitative examples of discovered correspondences at phonological, orthographic, and morphological levels.  

- No quantitative comparison with state-of-the-art methods is provided to demonstrate improvement.  
- Lacks analysis of computational complexity scaling with number of languages and dataset size.  
- Evaluation is limited to similar Slavic languages, leaving performance on diverse language families untested.  
- Does not provide quantitative metrics to define or measure "non-trivial" correspondences.  

- Request detailed analysis of runtime/complexity scaling and specific implementation optimizations.  
- Ask for quantitative comparisons (accuracy/precision/recall) against established baselines.  
- Ask how the method handles languages with different scripts or very different morphological structures.
