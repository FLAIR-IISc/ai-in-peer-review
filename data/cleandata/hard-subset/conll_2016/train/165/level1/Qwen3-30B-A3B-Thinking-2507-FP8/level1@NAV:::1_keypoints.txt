- Presents an efficient algorithm to analyze multiple languages simultaneously and quantify similarities across all subsets.  
- Demonstrates practical utility via a Slavic language analysis revealing meaningful linguistic insights.  
- MDL foundation offers a statistically principled method that avoids arbitrary correspondence assumptions.

- Data over-weighting technique lets users control detail level, balancing statistical significance and linguistic utility.  
- Lacks rigorous comparison to state-of-the-art correspondence discovery methods, hindering assessment of relative performance.  
- Evaluation limited to Slavic languages, leaving generalizability to other families untested.  
- Computational complexity and scalability are unclear; current implementation reportedly handles up to five languages only.
