- Summary: Proposes an MDL-based, deterministic EM-like method to discover orthographic correspondences across multiple languages, evaluated on 13 Slavic languages and introducing the sDL metric for subgroup similarity.

- Contribution 1: Applies MDL to N-way correspondence discovery, enabling assumption-light identification of statistically significant patterns across arbitrary language subsets.

- Contribution 2: Introduces shared Description Length (sDL), a novel metric that quantifies complexity of patterns shared exclusively by specific language subgroups.

- Contribution 3: Provides an end-to-end deterministic algorithm with initialization, EM-style optimization, and a practical `data over-weighting` control for rule granularity.

- Strength 1: Methodological novelty and soundness — MDL offers a principled, assumption-light approach that avoids strong prior assumptions about rule distributions.

- Strength 2: Genuine multi-language generalization — can analyze more than two languages simultaneously, demonstrated with compelling four-way analyses and sDL visualizations.

- Strength 3: Strong experimental validation — pairwise NCD reproduces known Slavic phylogeny and learned rules are linguistically plausible in qualitative examples.

- Strength 4: Practical controllability — `data over-weighting` provides a useful exploratory knob for users to tune rule significance levels.

- Weakness 1: Scalability concerns — formulation and algorithm include 2^N-1 subset terms and authors note bottlenecks for N>5, limiting applicability to larger families.

- Weakness 2: Lack of quantitative evaluation — discovered correspondences are evaluated qualitatively; no comparison to a gold standard for accuracy or linguistic validity.

- Weakness 3: MDL formulation clarity — unclear rationale for including rule usage counts in model code L(M) rather than data code L(D|M), conflating model and data costs.

- Weakness 4: Ambiguous over-weighting parameter — α is set heuristically ("until no singleton rules"), making results hard to reproduce and sensitivity unexplored.

- Question 1: Please analyze time and space complexity in N, identify primary bottlenecks, and discuss possible approximations to scale to larger N (e.g., N≥10).

- Question 2: Please clarify the theoretical justification for placing total rule usage TΠ in L(M) rather than L(D|M), and whether this matches a known MDL variant.

- Question 3: Could sDL be used to probe linguistic hypotheses (e.g., compare sDL of a subgroup vs. its supersets to distinguish innovations from shared inheritance)?
