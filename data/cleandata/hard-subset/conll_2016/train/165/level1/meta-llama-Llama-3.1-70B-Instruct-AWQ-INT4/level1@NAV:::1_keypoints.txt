- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover correspondences across multiple languages with an efficient inference algorithm evaluated on Slavic languages.  
- Demonstrates automatic discovery of non-trivial linguistic associations enabling quantitative and qualitative analyses.  

- Novel application of the MDL principle to multi-language correspondence discovery.  
- Introduces an efficient algorithm capable of handling multiple languages.  
- Presents a method to quantify linguistic similarity between languages.  

- MDL approach is robust to noisy data.  
- Algorithm is flexible for both pairwise and multi-language analyses.  
- Results are interpretable and yield linguistic insights.  

- Algorithm has high computational complexity, limiting scalability to large datasets.  
- Risk of overfitting, especially with limited data.  
- Lacks a thorough linguistic evaluation to validate findings.  

- How will overfitting be addressed in the algorithm?  
- Can the authors provide more details and validation for the linguistic evaluation?  
- How will the algorithm scale to handle more than five languages?
