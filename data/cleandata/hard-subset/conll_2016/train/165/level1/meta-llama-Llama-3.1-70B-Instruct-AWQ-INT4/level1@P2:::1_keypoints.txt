Novel application of the MDL principle to discover word correspondences across multiple languages, claiming to find statistically significant correspondences without arbitrary assumptions.

A novel MDL-based formulation for multilingual correspondence discovery.

An efficient algorithm for inferring correspondence rules that scales to more than two languages.

Experimental validation showing effectiveness on a set of Slavic languages.

Strength: Innovative use of MDL in this problem domain.

Strength: Algorithmic efficiency and ability to handle multiple languages.

Strength: Clear and well-presented experimental results on Slavic languages.

Weakness: Evaluation limited to Slavic languages and not representative of typologically diverse languages.

Weakness: No direct comparison to existing correspondence-discovery methods, making impact unclear.

Weakness: Heavy reliance on MDL without sufficient discussion of its limitations or suitability across language types.

Question: How will the approach scale to more than five languages given current computational limits?

Question: Can the authors provide a detailed comparison to existing correspondence-discovery methods?

Question: How will the method address potential MDL limitations, such as sensitivity to model class choice?

Additional comment: The paper is well-written and clear but would be strengthened by broader evaluation and comparisons.
