- Proposes an MDL-based method to automatically discover correspondences between words across multiple languages, implemented with a two-part MDL code and EM, evaluated on Slavic languages.  
- Novel application of MDL to multilingual correspondence discovery, claimed as a significant contribution.  
- Presents an efficient algorithm capable of handling multiple languages for linguistic analysis.  
- Produces objective, unbiased results by not assuming an underlying distribution for correspondences.  
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in the MDL principle.  
- Algorithmic efficiency and multi-language scalability highlighted as strengths.  
- Experimental results demonstrate efficacy on a set of Slavic languages.  
- Flexible approach that can integrate linguistic knowledge for fine-grained similarity analysis.  
- High computational complexity may limit applicability to large datasets.  
- Requires large amounts of data, posing challenges for under-resourced languages.  
- Risk of overfitting, particularly with over-represented data.  
- Assumes correspondences are statistically significant, which may not always hold.  
- Question: How will computational complexity be addressed for large datasets?  
- Question: How does the method handle under-resourced languages and limited data scenarios?  
- Question: What strategies are proposed to prevent overfitting with skewed data?  
- Question: Can you clarify the linguistic assumptions and their potential impact on results?  
- Additional comment: The approach is valuable and potentially widely applicable but needs work on complexity, data requirements, and robustness to be broadly useful.
