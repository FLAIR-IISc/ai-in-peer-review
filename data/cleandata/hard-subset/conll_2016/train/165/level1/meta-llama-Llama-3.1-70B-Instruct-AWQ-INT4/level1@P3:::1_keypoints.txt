- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover string-level correspondences between multiple languages without imposing arbitrary distributional assumptions.  
- Introduces a statistically grounded MDL framework to identify the best correspondence model for given data.  
- Presents an efficient, deterministic algorithm to infer correspondence rules directly from data and analyze multiple languages simultaneously.  
- Evaluates the method on Slavic languages and shows it can find non-trivial associations and quantify linguistic similarity.  
- Novel use of MDL for language correspondence discovery, addressing limitations of existing methods.  
- Algorithm is efficient and practical for multi-language analysis, useful to linguists and NLP researchers.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including comparisons with linguistic classifications and correspondence analysis.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to Slavic languages, leaving generalization to other families unclear.  
- Method heavily depends on data quality and quantity, raising concerns about performance on noisy or limited datasets.  
- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art methods, making it hard to judge relative performance and significance.  
- Requests clarification on strategies to handle noisy or limited data and reduce over-reliance on data.  
- Asks for more detailed linguistic analysis of discovered correspondences and discussion of potential applications.  
- Suggests extending the approach to handle more complex phenomena like morphological or syntactic correspondences.  
- Notes MDL motivation is sound and the approach could apply to tasks like machine translation, language modeling, and linguistic analysis.
