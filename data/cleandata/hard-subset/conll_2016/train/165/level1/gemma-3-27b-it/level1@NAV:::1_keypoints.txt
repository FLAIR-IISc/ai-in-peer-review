- Proposes a novel MDL-based method to discover string-level correspondences across multiple languages without pre-defined shape/distribution assumptions.  
- Core contribution: efficient, deterministic algorithm capable of handling more than two languages simultaneously.  
- Secondary contribution: applied to Slavic languages, producing a phylogenetic tree and quantified linguistic similarities across subsets.  
- Introduces data over-weighting to incorporate linguistic knowledge and control correspondence granularity.  
- Strength: MDL grounding provides strong theoretical justification for the approach.  
- Strength: Deterministic algorithm enhances reproducibility and interpretability.  
- Strength: Experiments recover expected linguistic relationships and reveal subtle multi-language similarities beyond pairwise analyses.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—claims up to five languages but lacks detailed computational cost and memory analysis.  
- Weakness: Experimental setup underdescribed, especially the choice and handling of Swadesh lists.  
- Weakness: Does not adequately address data quality issues or potential biases in the dataset.  
- Weakness: Lacks quantitative comparisons to existing cognate-identification or language-similarity methods on the same datasets.  
- Q1: Request for detailed analysis of computational complexity and memory requirements as number of languages increases.  
- Q2: Ask about sensitivity to input data quality/completeness and strategies for missing or ambiguous cognates.  
- Q3: Ask how results change when compared to or integrating established linguistic resources.  
- Q4: Ask what experiments are planned for quantitative comparison with existing approaches.
