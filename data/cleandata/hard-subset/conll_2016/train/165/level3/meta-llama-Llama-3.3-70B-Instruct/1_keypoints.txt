- Proposes BSP, a novel transition-based parser supporting multiple parents, non-terminals, and discontinuous units for DAG semantic structures.
- Explores a conversion-based approach that uses existing parsers to produce alternate representations which are converted back to broad-coverage semantic structures.
- Reports the first experiments on UCCA parsing, a cross-linguistically applicable semantic representation scheme.

- Provides a clear, detailed description of the proposed parser and the conversion-based approach.
- Empirical results show BSP is competitive with state-of-the-art parsers and the conversion-based method offers a useful upper bound.
- Highlights the value of exploring different semantic representations and parsing approaches to improve semantic parsing.

- Lacks a thorough comparison with related work on semantic parsing, particularly for UCCA.
- Does not provide a detailed error analysis to reveal the parser’s limitations.
- Could offer a more detailed discussion of potential applications for the parser and the UCCA representation.

- How will the authors address BSP’s limitations in handling discontinuous units and non-terminal nodes?
- Can the authors provide more details on the conversion procedures and how they can be improved?
- How do the authors envision BSP being applied in real-world tasks like machine translation or summarization?
