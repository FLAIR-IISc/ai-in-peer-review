Summary: Proposes an MDL-based method modeling substring-matching rules to discover cross-language word correspondences, trained with EM and evaluated on 13 Slavic languages using distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.

Main contributions:
- Novel application of MDL to discover cross-language word correspondences.
- An efficient algorithm for identifying statistically important correspondences.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 13 Slavic languages.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated methodology with clear justification for MDL and EM.
- Thorough evaluation on a large Slavic-language dataset.
- Insightful results revealing non-trivial associations and linguistic sub-groups.

Weaknesses:
- Limited comparison to prior work on borrowing and cognate detection.
- Alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck, restricting comparisons to at most five languages.
- EM stopping criterion is unspecified (unclear if run to convergence).
- Use of orthographic data may conflate script changes with linguistic correspondences.

Questions to authors:
- Please specify the EM stopping criterion and its impact on results.
- How will you address the alignment bottleneck to scale to more languages?
- Can you provide a more comprehensive comparison to borrowing and cognate-detection literature?
- How do you plan to evaluate effectiveness on other language families?
