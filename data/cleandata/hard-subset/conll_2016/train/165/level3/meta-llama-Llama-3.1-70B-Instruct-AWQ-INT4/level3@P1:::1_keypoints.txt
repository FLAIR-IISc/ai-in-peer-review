- Proposes a novel MDL-based method for discovering cross-language word correspondences.
- Introduces an efficient EM-based algorithm using a two-part code to infer correspondence rules.
- Evaluates the method on several Slavic languages and finds non-trivial cross-language associations.
- Contribution: MDL formulation for correspondence discovery is novel and relevant to linguistics/NLP.
- Contribution: Presents an algorithm claimed to be efficient for inferring statistically significant rules.
- Contribution: Provides experimental results demonstrating the approach’s effectiveness on Slavic data.
- Strength: The MDL-based approach is well-motivated and appropriate for the task.
- Strength: The algorithm is efficient and supports simultaneous analysis of multiple languages.
- Strength: Experimental results support the method’s ability to find meaningful correspondences.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to related work on borrowing and cognate detection.
- Weakness: Alignment procedure has a significant memory/runtime bottleneck, limiting analyses to ≤5 languages.
- Weakness: Some mathematical derivations in the paper are unclear and hard to follow.
- Weakness: Minor editorial issues (e.g., phrasing like “focus in on”).
- Question: Request for more details on the merging step in the maximization update.
- Question: How is EM convergence ensured or what stopping criterion is used?
- Question: How would mixing cognate datasets with non-cognate Swadesh lists affect results?
- Question: Would phonetic transcriptions instead of orthographic data improve outcomes, and why?
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but should address editorial fixes and compare to related work for a fuller evaluation.
