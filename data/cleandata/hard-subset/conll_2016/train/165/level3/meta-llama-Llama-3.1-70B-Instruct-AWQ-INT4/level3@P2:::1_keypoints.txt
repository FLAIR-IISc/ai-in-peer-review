- Proposes an MDL-based method to automatically discover cross-language word correspondences by modeling substring-matching rules and optimizing model+data description length.
- Introduces an efficient EM-based algorithm to infer statistically significant correspondence rules under the MDL objective.
- Evaluates the method on 13 Slavic languages using distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.

- Well-motivated use of the MDL principle for selecting the best model for the data.
- Presents an efficient inference algorithm that optimizes model vs. data description length.
- Offers a comprehensive quantitative and qualitative evaluation across multiple Slavic languages.

- Lacks comprehensive comparison to prior work on borrowing and cognate detection.
- Alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck, currently limiting comparisons to at most five languages.
- Several derivations and technical points (e.g., “optimal unigram for symbol usages” and the merging step in maximization) are unclear and need clarification.

- How will the authors resolve the memory/runtime bottleneck to scale beyond five languages?
- Can the authors provide more comprehensive comparisons to borrowing and cognate-detection literature?
- How will the authors clarify unclear derivations like “optimal unigram” and the merging step in the M-step?

- Provide more detail on EM algorithm usage (convergence vs. stopping criterion).
- Consider using phonetic transcriptions instead of orthographic data to avoid conflating script changes with linguistic correspondences.
