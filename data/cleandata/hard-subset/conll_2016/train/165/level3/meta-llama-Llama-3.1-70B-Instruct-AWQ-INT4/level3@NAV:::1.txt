**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a Minimum Description Length (MDL) based method to discover cross-language word correspondences, modeling substring-matching rules and optimizing model vs data description length. The authors train their model using Expectation-Maximization (EM) and evaluate it on 13 Slavic languages using distance measures, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Application of MDL**: The authors apply MDL to discover cross-language word correspondences, which is a novel application of the principle.
2. **Efficient Algorithm**: The authors propose an efficient algorithm for discovering statistically important correspondences, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
3. **Evaluation on Slavic Languages**: The authors evaluate their method on 13 Slavic languages, providing a comprehensive analysis of the correspondences between these languages.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-Motivated Methodology**: The authors provide a clear motivation for using MDL and EM, and their choices are well-justified.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors evaluate their method on a large dataset of Slavic languages, providing a thorough analysis of the correspondences between these languages.
3. **Insightful Results**: The authors provide insightful results, including the discovery of non-trivial associations between languages and the identification of linguistic sub-groups.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Comparison to Related Work**: The authors do not provide a comprehensive comparison to related work on borrowing and cognate detection.
2. **Alignment Procedure Bottleneck**: The authors note that the alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck, limiting comparisons to at most 5 languages.
3. **Unclear EM Stopping Criterion**: The authors do not specify whether EM is run to convergence or uses another stopping criterion.
4. **Potential Script Changes Conflation**: The authors use orthographic data, which may conflate script changes with linguistic correspondences.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the EM stopping criterion and how it affects the results?
2. How do you plan to address the alignment procedure bottleneck to scale up to more languages?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive comparison to related work on borrowing and cognate detection?
4. How do you plan to evaluate the effectiveness of your method on other language families?
