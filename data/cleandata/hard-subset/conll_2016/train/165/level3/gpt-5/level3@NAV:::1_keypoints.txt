- Primary contribution: MDL-based formulation for discovering cross-language substring correspondences via EM that alternates optimal alignments and rule-table updates.
- Secondary contribution: Quantitative evaluation on Slavic data (pairwise and 4-way) using NCD distances, NJ tree, illustrative rules, and shared-description-length analyses for subsets.
- Tertiary contribution: Deterministic learning setup with a tunable data overweighting parameter to control rule granularity.
- Strength: Conceptual clarity—two-part MDL framing avoids strong parametric assumptions and EM coupling of alignment and rule-cost estimation is natural.
- Strength: Novelty in learning rules over arbitrary language subsets and introducing shared description length as a diagnostic.
- Strength: Empirical plausibility—pairwise NCDs and resulting tree broadly recover known Slavic subgroupings and capture phonological/orthographic/morphological regularities.
- Strength: Interpretability and determinism—inspectable rule lists, ability to seed with linguistic knowledge, and analyze subset contributions.
- Weakness: Scalability—alignment graph causes memory/runtime bottlenecks limiting practical comparisons to ≤5 languages with no pruning/approximation strategy provided.
- Weakness: Data representation—using raw orthography risks conflating script/orthographic conventions with linguistic correspondences, especially with mixed Latin/Cyrillic scripts and no phonetic normalization.
- Weakness: Missing methodological comparisons—no empirical comparison to Bayesian phylogenetic inference (e.g., MrBayes) or related work on borrowing/cognate detection.
- Weakness: Experimental design concerns—mixing curated cognates with Swadesh lists may distort distances and neighbor joining with manual rooting is suboptimal versus principled phylogenetic methods.
- Weakness: Clarity issues in derivations—unclear definition/estimation of the “optimal unigram,” lack of detail on the maximization/merge step (candidate generation, constraints, complexity), and unspecified EM stopping criterion.
- Question: How is EM terminated—what convergence criterion, initializations are used, and are results stable across runs?
- Question: Precisely define and explain estimation of the “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” and how counts are pooled.
- Question: Describe the merge operation—eligibility of pairs, enumeration/pruning of candidates, and computational complexity.
- Question: How are script effects controlled—are transliteration or IPA/phonetic transcriptions used or evaluated?
- Question: Have distances/trees been compared to MrBayes or other Bayesian tools and to cognate/borrowing models (e.g., List & Moran 2013)?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the overweighting parameter α and to mixing Swadesh with cognate sets?
