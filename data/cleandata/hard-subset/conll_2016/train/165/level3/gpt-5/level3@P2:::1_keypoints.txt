- MDL-based framework with deterministic EM: Dijkstra-based alignment and merge-based M-step to discover substring-level correspondences across languages.
- Introduces sDL, an information-theoretic metric for structure shared by any subset of languages.
- Empirical Slavic study: plausible correspondences, NCD-based phylogenetic signal, and a tunable data over-weighting parameter for granularity control.

- Clear MDL formulation that avoids strong priors, producing rules spanning phonological, orthographic, and morphological patterns.
- Supports multi-language subset rules, enabling nuanced correspondences beyond pairwise analyses.
- Produces interpretable rules plus quantitative analyses (NCD matrices/trees and sDL decompositions) aligning with known Slavic groupings.
- Deterministic training with a reproducible EM+merge procedure and a practical knob to control rule detail.

- Missing comparisons to established phylogenetic and cognate-detection methods (e.g., MrBayes/BEAST/RevBayes; List & Moran; Kitchen et al.).
- Alignment approach is memory/time intensive and appears limited to N≈5; multiway experiments are few and small (four languages).
- Reliance on orthographic forms risks confounding script/orthography effects; phonetic transcriptions or normalization are needed.
- Methodological clarity gaps: unclear definition of “optimal unigram,” unclear merge selection/application in M-step, and unspecified EM stopping criteria/convergence behavior.
- Evaluation design concerns: mixing Swadesh and cognate-specific sets may bias results; neighbor-joining is manually rooted and has known limitations.

- What EM stopping criterion is used, and how stable is convergence across runs/seeds?
- How are M-step merge candidates generated, prioritized, and can merges be undone or split?
- Please clarify “optimal unigram”: how symbol probabilities are estimated and how this interacts with TΠ.
- What are peak memory and runtime as functions of |D|, average word length, and N; any heuristics tried to scale beyond N=5?
- Have you tried IPA/phoneme-level transcriptions or script normalization, and how do results change?
- How is the over-weighting parameter α chosen in practice; any validation curves relating complexity to downstream utility?

- Compare against MrBayes/BEAST/RevBayes and cognate/borrowing-aware methods and cite relevant work (e.g., List & Moran 2013; Kitchen et al. 2009).
- Replace or complement neighbor-joining with model-based phylogenies providing support values and principled rooting.
- Include ablations/baselines (edit distance, off-the-shelf compressor NCD) to contextualize MDL gains.
- Fix minor editorial issues and incomplete bibliography entries.
- Release code, data, and detailed runtime/memory logs to improve reproducibility.
