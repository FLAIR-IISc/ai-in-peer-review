- Summary: Proposes an MDL-based, nonparametric method to discover multi-language substring correspondences via rule tables, shortest-path alignments, and EM-like merges, evaluated on Slavic languages with an adjustable data-overweighting knob.

- Contribution 1: MDL code and deterministic EM-with-merging algorithm that discovers multi-language substring rules and aligns cognate tuples via exact shortest-paths.

- Contribution 2: Introduces shared description length (sDL) to quantify multiway shared structure among arbitrary language subsets.

- Contribution 3: Empirical Slavic case study producing interpretable correspondences, pairwise distances, a neighbor-joining tree, and a tunable rule granularity via α.

- Strength: Conceptually clean, assumption-light MDL formulation that balances model size and fit and yields interpretable rule inventories.

- Strength: Learns true multiway correspondences (not just pairwise) and quantifies shared structure, demonstrated in a four-language analysis.

- Strength: Deterministic learning with exact shortest-path alignments and merge heuristics that favor statistically useful longer rules.

- Strength: Empirical plausibility on Slavic data — recovers known groupings, outliers, and script effects sensibly.

- Strength: Practical interpretability control via data-overweighting α to trade off complexity and granularity.

- Weakness: Lacks quantitative comparisons to strong baselines in cognate/sound correspondence and phylogenetic inference.

- Weakness: Scalability and memory/runtime bottlenecks limit joint analyses to ≈5 languages, undermining multiway scalability claims.

- Weakness: Phylogeny methodology (neighbor-joining, manual rooting) lacks support values and principled uncertainty treatment.

- Weakness: Data/representation confounds—mixing curated cognates with Swadesh lists and using raw orthography may conflate orthographic/script effects with phonology.

- Weakness: Underspecified technical details around unigram coding, merge compatibility/overlap, convergence guarantees, and smoothing/α effects.

- Question: Please define precisely the unigram code for rule symbols—count source, normalization, and exact data-to-model enumeration.

- Question: Specify the merge operation: compatibility criteria, count/probability updates, objective delta, and whether k>2 merges or beam search are considered.

- Question: What is the EM stopping criterion, and how sensitive are results to initialization and Laplace smoothing?

- Question: How does α quantitatively affect rule inventory, sDL, and NCD—please provide sensitivity analyses and guidelines.

- Question: Have you tried phonemic/IPA transliteration to separate orthographic from phonological signals, and how do results change?

- Question: Can you compare your distances/trees to Bayesian phylogenetic baselines (MrBayes/BEAST) and sequence-alignment baselines (ALINE/PMI, LingPy/LEXSTAT) including uncertainty/support?

- Question: Will you release code and data for reproduction and broader comparisons?

- Additional comment: Consider more scalable alignment strategies (A* with admissible heuristics, pruning/beam search, memoization, or bounded rule lengths).

- Additional comment: Provide bootstrap/posterior support for tree edges and avoid manual rooting where possible.

- Additional comment: Clarify Swadesh cognacy vetting and how borrowings are handled.

- Editorial: Replace “focus in on” with “focus on.”
