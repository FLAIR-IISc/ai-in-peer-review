Contributions: Formulates cross-language correspondence discovery with an MDL framework and an EM implementation; applies it to 13 Slavic languages to demonstrate multi-language analysis and linguistic insights.

Strength — Theoretical foundation: MDL gives a principled, simplicity-focused justification well-suited to discovering linguistic correspondences and connects sensibly to Bayesian inference.

Strength — Multi-language capability: Can analyze more than two languages simultaneously, enabling quantification of similarity across arbitrary language subsets.

Strength — Deterministic & controllable: Deterministic algorithm supports reproducibility and interpretation; data over-weighting lets users control detail level of discovered correspondences.

Strength — Empirical evaluation: Experiments on 13 Slavic languages, NCD-based phylogeny, and example correspondences provide relevant validation and illustrative results.

Strength — Clear MDL presentation: MDL code (model and data) and encoding choices are explained clearly, including universal codes and Laplace correction.

Weakness — Scalability bottleneck: Alignment procedure has memory/runtime limits restricting comparisons to ≤5 languages, hampering applicability to larger families.

Weakness — Phylogenetic method choice: Use of neighbor joining is sensitive to long-branch attraction and manual rooting; more robust methods (e.g., Bayesian inference) are preferable.

Weakness — Data mixing concerns: Mixing cognate datasets with non-cognate Swadesh lists may bias results; dataset curation and sensitivity analyses are needed.

Weakness — Orthographic rather than phonetic data: Reliance on orthography risks conflating script changes with true linguistic correspondences; phonetic transcriptions would improve accuracy.

Weakness — EM implementation details unclear: Stopping criterion and convergence behavior of the EM algorithm are not well specified, raising stability concerns.

Weakness — Limited comparisons to prior work: Lacks comprehensive comparison to established borrowing/cognate and phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes, Bouchard-Côté, Warnow, Nakhleh, Kitchen, List & Moran).

Question — Scalability fixes: What optimizations or alternative algorithms are planned to overcome the alignment bottleneck for larger datasets?

Question — Phylogenetic choices: Why was neighbor joining chosen over more sophisticated methods like Bayesian inference?

Question — Data sensitivity: Have you tested the impact of including non-cognate Swadesh lists, or run sensitivity analyses using only cognate data?

Question — EM stopping criterion: Is the EM stopped by convergence (e.g., log-likelihood threshold) or by a fixed number of iterations?

Question — Unigram model: What is the “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” — is it frequency-based and how is it updated during EM?

Question — Merging step details: How are rules chosen to merge in the M-step, and how is decrease in description length computed for merges?
