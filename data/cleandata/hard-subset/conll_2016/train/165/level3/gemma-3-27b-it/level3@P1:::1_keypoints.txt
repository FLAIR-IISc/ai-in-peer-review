- Proposes an MDL-based method using EM to discover cross-language substring correspondences, evaluated on 13 Slavic languages.
- Main contribution: deterministic, statistically grounded approach to multi-language correspondence discovery without strong priors.
- Secondary contribution: extends MDL modeling to >2 languages, enabling finer quantification of linguistic similarity.
- Strength: Novel application of MDL offers principled model selection, determinism, and interpretability.
- Strength: Handles multi-lingual alignments beyond pairwise comparisons, demonstrated on several Slavic languages.
- Strength: Data over-weighting controls granularity, allowing trade-offs between statistical rigor and nuanced patterns.
- Weakness: Limited comparison to related work and lack of benchmarking against Bayesian phylogenetic and borrowing-detection methods.
- Weakness: Alignment procedure is a computational bottleneck, currently limiting scalability to about five languages.
- Weakness: Methodological concerns — reliance on orthographic data, use of neighbor-joining trees, and unclear EM convergence/update details.
- Question: What is the EM convergence criterion (fixed iterations, log-likelihood change, or other)?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the over-weighting parameter and is there a principled selection method?
- Question: What is the computational complexity of the alignment procedure and how can the bottleneck be mitigated?
- Question: How would results differ with phonetic transcriptions and were any phonetic experiments conducted?
- Additional: Fix editorial issue “focus in on” → “focus on”.
- Additional: Cite/compare List & Moran (2013) and Kitchen et al. (2009) for related work engagement.
- Additional: Provide more details on data selection and the impact of mixing cognate datasets with Swadesh lists.
