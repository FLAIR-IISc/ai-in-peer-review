- Core idea: use MDL to discover string-level correspondences across multiple languages by balancing rule complexity and data encoding.
- Algorithm: iterative EM-like optimization with E-step aligning data given rules and M-step merging rules to reduce description length.
- Evaluation: applied to 13 Slavic languages; pairwise NCD used for phylogeny and a novel four-way "shared Description Length" (sDL) analysis.

- Contribution: formulates multi-language correspondence discovery as a non-parametric MDL problem that handles more than two languages.
- Contribution: introduces sDL to quantify shared information among language subsets and demonstrates method on diverse correspondences (chars to stems).

- Strength: MDL framework is well-motivated, balancing model complexity and data fit and reducing overfitting without arbitrary priors.
- Strength: method generalizes beyond pairwise analysis, enabling holistic multi-language and four-way analyses.
- Strength: EM-like optimization is intuitive, deterministic, and reproducible.

- Weakness: insufficient comparison to prior work, especially Bayesian phylogenetic methods (e.g., MrBayes/Kitchen et al.) and computational cognate detection literature (e.g., List & Moran).
- Weakness: alignment procedure is a computational bottleneck (limits to ~5 languages), undermining multi-language claims.
- Weakness: phylogenetic reconstruction uses neighbor-joining, which has known drawbacks versus modern methods and manual rooting.
- Weakness: "data over-weighting" introduces a subjective parameter that departs from the objective MDL principle.
- Weakness: experiments use orthographic forms rather than phonetic transcriptions, risking script-driven artifacts (e.g., Cyrillic vs Latin).
- Weakness: data mixes Swadesh lists with internationalisms/pan-Slavic words, potentially biasing discovered correspondences.

- Question: clarify "optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules" — how is this unigram derived and encoded?
- Question: detail the maximization step — how are pattern pairs selected for merging and what is the merging operation?
- Question: what is the EM stopping criterion — fixed iterations or convergence of total description length?
- Question: have you considered using phonetic transcriptions (e.g., IPA) and how would that affect disentangling orthographic artifacts from linguistic correspondences?
