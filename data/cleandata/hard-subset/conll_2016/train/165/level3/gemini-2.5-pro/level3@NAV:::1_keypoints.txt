- Proposes an MDL-based unsupervised method using substring-matching rules and EM to discover cross-linguistic correspondences, applied to 13 Slavic languages with a shared description length metric.
- Strength: Elegant, principled MDL formulation that infers patterns without strong structural priors.
- Strength: Able in theory to model more than two languages simultaneously, enabling nuanced subgroup analyses.
- Strength: Qualitative results show linguistically plausible correspondences across Slavic languages.
- Weakness: Fails to engage with related work or compare to established cognate-detection and phylogenetic methods, leaving performance relative to the state of the art unclear.
- Weakness: Scalability is limited by an alignment bottleneck, restricting experiments to at most five languages and undermining the multi-language claim.
- Weakness: Methodological choices are questionable—use of raw orthography, outdated neighbor-joining for trees, possible confound from mixed cognate/non-cognate data, and unclear EM details (stopping criterion, merging step).
- Question: How is the M-step’s complexity managed—specifically, how is the optimal pair of rules to merge found efficiently?
- Question: What concrete strategies do the authors propose to make alignment tractable for larger numbers of languages (e.g., >10)?
