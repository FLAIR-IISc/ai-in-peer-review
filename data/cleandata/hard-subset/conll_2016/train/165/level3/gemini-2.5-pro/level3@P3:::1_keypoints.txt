- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover substring correspondences across multiple languages by balancing model complexity and data encoding, optimized with an EM algorithm.  
- E-step aligns data under current rules; M-step merges rules that most reduce total description length.  
- Evaluated on 13 Slavic languages to derive pairwise distances, a phylogenetic tree, and qualitative multi-language correspondences.

- Contribution: a non-parametric MDL formulation for multi-language correspondence discovery without strong prior assumptions.  
- Contribution: introduces "shared Description Length" (sDL) to quantify n-way linguistic similarity among subsets of languages.

- Strength: MDL framework is sensible and well-motivated, naturally balancing complexity and fit; EM is an appropriate optimization choice.  
- Strength: Empirical results on Slavic languages are promising, producing a phylogeny largely consistent with known classifications.  
- Strength: Provides detailed n-way analyses that reveal subgroup-specific and cross-group correspondences beyond pairwise methods.

- Weakness: Fails to compare to or cite substantial related work in computational historical linguistics, making novelty and performance unclear.  
- Weakness: Major scalability limitations in alignment procedure, experiments limited to at most five languages despite n-way claims.  
- Weakness: Phylogenetic evaluation is oversimplified (uses neighbor-joining); lacks comparison to established probabilistic/Bayesian phylogenetic models.  
- Weakness: Uses orthographic data, risking learning script-based superficial correspondences instead of true phonological patterns.  
- Weakness: Several technical details are unclear (e.g., "optimal unigram" meaning, candidate merge selection, EM stopping criterion).

- Question: Do you have concrete plans to improve alignment efficiency to scale to >10 languages?  
- Question: Please elaborate how candidate pattern pairs are chosen for merging and how the "optimal unigram for symbol usages" is computed/used.  
- Question: What stopping criterion was used for EM, did it converge, and how many iterations were typical?  
- Question: How does the MDL objective compare to Bayesian MAP phylogenetic models that jointly infer alignments and trees; can you provide empirical comparisons?  
- Question: Can you comment on confounding effects of orthographic data and whether the method would transfer straightforwardly to phonetic transcriptions?

- Additional: Treat the "data over-weighting" discussion as an exploratory heuristic, not part of the MDL foundation.  
- Additional: Mixing pan-Slavic cognate sets with Swadesh lists may bias results; analyze these datasets separately.  
- Minor editorial: change "focus in on" to "focus on" (line 440).
