- Paper introduces an MDL-based method to discover multi-language substring correspondences and evaluates it on 13 Slavic languages for distance, phylogeny, and n-way shared description length (sDL).
- Contribution: a principled non-parametric MDL framework for learning multi-way substring correspondences and an information-theoretic sDL metric for n-way language similarity.
- Contribution: empirical demonstration on 13 Slavic languages showing linguistically plausible correspondences and a reasonable phylogenetic grouping.
- Strength: well-motivated MDL formulation that balances model complexity and data fit and is optimized via an EM-like procedure.
- Strength: method supports simultaneous analysis across arbitrary numbers of languages, offering richer multi-way insights than pairwise approaches.
- Strength: learned correspondences and NCD-based tree largely align with known Slavic linguistic classifications and produce intuitive rule examples.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to related work in cognate detection, borrowing detection, and standard Bayesian phylogenetic methods, limiting assessment of novelty and performance.
- Weakness: scalability is poor—the alignment procedure is memory- and runtime-bound and limited empirically to at most five languages.
- Weakness: reliance on raw orthographic forms risks artifacts from writing systems; phonetic/phonemic transcriptions would be more appropriate.
- Weakness: phylogenetic inference uses neighbor-joining, a relatively weak method; more robust Bayesian or standard phylogenetic tools should be compared.
- Weakness: key methodological details are under-specified, hindering reproducibility.
- Lack of clarity: unclear stopping criterion for the EM procedure—fixed iterations or convergence of description length?
- Lack of clarity: unclear how candidate rule merges are generated and searched efficiently, since naive search would be intractable.
- Lack of clarity: ambiguous definition of L(π) and the “optimal unigram for symbol usages” used to encode characters in rules.
- Question: please elaborate the search strategy for selecting the best pair of rules to merge and how this is made efficient.
- Question: clarify what events the “optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules” is defined over and how its costs are computed.
- Question: what stopping criterion was used for EM—was the objective tracked to convergence?
- Question: discuss conceptual and empirical differences between this MDL approach and established Bayesian phylogenetic inference methods (e.g., MrBayes).
- Question: have you considered the impact of mixing Swadesh-core vocabulary with internationalisms and pan-Slavic words that may contain many borrowings?
