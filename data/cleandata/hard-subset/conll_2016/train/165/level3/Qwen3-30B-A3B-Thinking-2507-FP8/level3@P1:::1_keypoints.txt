- Contribution: Proposes an MDL-based framework for objective, assumption-free discovery of string-level correspondences across languages.
- Contribution: Introduces an efficient EM-based algorithm for learning substring-matching correspondence rules.
- Contribution: Provides empirical validation showing the method captures linguistic structure across multiple Slavic languages.

- Strength: MDL approach is well-motivated for finding objective correspondences without arbitrary distributional assumptions.
- Strength: Method automatically discovers non-trivial linguistic patterns, evidenced by a phylogenetic tree that correctly groups Slavic languages.
- Strength: Four-way analysis offers a novel, detailed quantification of linguistic similarity that surpasses traditional pairwise methods.
- Strength: Method can handle multiple languages simultaneously, valuable for linguistic analysis.
- Strength: Paper clearly explains the MDL framework, making it accessible to linguists.
- Strength: Empirical correspondences align with known linguistic patterns, supporting method efficacy.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to related work on cognate detection and borrowing (e.g., List & Moran 2013; Kitchen et al. 2009).
- Weakness: Alignment procedure has a major memory/runtime bottleneck, currently limiting analysis to at most five languages.
- Weakness: Using orthographic data rather than phonetic transcriptions risks conflating script changes with linguistic correspondences and adding noise.
- Weakness: Mixing cognate datasets with Swadesh lists may bias results since these datasets serve different linguistic purposes.
- Weakness: Technical details are unclear for the "optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules" and the merging step in the maximization update.
- Weakness: EM algorithm stopping criterion is unspecified, leaving convergence and stopping behavior unclear.

- Question: How does the MDL approach compare to Bayesian phylogenetic inference tools like MrBayes given resemblance to Bayesian objectives?
- Question: What specific advantages does this method offer over existing cognate detection approaches?
- Question: How would using phonetic transcriptions instead of orthographic data affect results, especially for languages with different scripts?
- Question: What is the precise stopping criterion for the EM algorithm and how do you ensure sufficient convergence?
