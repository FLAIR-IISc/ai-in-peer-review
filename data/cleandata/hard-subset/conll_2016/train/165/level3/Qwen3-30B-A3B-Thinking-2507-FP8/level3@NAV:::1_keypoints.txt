- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-language substring-matching correspondences, balancing model complexity and data description length.  
- Uses Expectation-Maximization to train the model and evaluates on 13 Slavic languages with distance measures, phylogenetic trees, and example correspondences.  
- Primary contribution: principled, assumption-light MDL approach enabling objective, unbiased string-level linguistic analysis.  
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates quantifying similarity across all language subsets simultaneously, not only pairwise.  
- Tertiary contribution: experimental validation showing discovery of meaningful linguistic patterns and phylogenetic trees that align with known classifications.  
- Strength: well-motivated MDL framework for correspondence discovery.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation on Slavic languages identifying meaningful patterns.  
- Strength: ability to quantify similarity across all language subsets rather than just pairwise comparisons.  
- Major weakness: no comparison to related work on borrowing and cognate detection (e.g., List & Moran 2013, Kitchen et al. 2009), weakening novelty claims.  
- Major weakness: alignment procedure has a severe memory/runtime bottleneck, limiting analysis to at most five languages and hurting practical applicability.  
- Major weakness: reliance on orthographic data instead of phonetic transcriptions risks conflating script changes with true linguistic correspondences.  
- Major weakness: phylogenetic trees built with neighbor joining, which has known disadvantages (e.g., manual rooting) and may be suboptimal.  
- Major weakness: unclear whether EM runs to convergence or uses another stopping criterion.  
- Technical clarifications needed: meaning of "optimal unigram for symbol usages in all rules" and details of the merging step in the maximization update.  
- Suggested improvement: compare to Bayesian phylogenetic inference tools (e.g., MrBayes) given the resemblance to Bayesian objectives.  
- Data concern: address mixing of cognate and non-cognate Swadesh lists in the dataset.  
- Editorial correction: change "focus in on" (line 440) to "focus on."
