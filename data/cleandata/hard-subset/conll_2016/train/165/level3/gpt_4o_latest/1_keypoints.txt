- Paper proposes an MDL-based, substring-matching method with an EM implementation to discover linguistic correspondences and is tested on 13 Slavic languages.

Contributions:
- Introduces a statistically grounded, deterministic MDL framework for identifying string-level correspondences without prior shape/distribution assumptions.
- Enables simultaneous multi-language analysis rather than just pairwise comparisons, allowing richer quantification of similarities.
- Provides empirical validation on Slavic languages with pairwise distances, a phylogenetic tree, and example correspondences.

Strengths:
- Novel and theoretically rigorous use of MDL linked to Bayesian inference.
- Practically relevant multi-language capability useful for historical linguistics and computational phylogenetics.
- Comprehensive experiments with results (phylogeny and correspondences) that align with known linguistic classifications.
- Flexible framework allowing deterministic results and potential integration of linguistic knowledge or non-deterministic extensions.

Weaknesses:
- Scalability: current implementation limited to five languages due to memory and runtime constraints.
- Lacks empirical comparisons to existing borrowing/cognate-detection methods and phylogeny tools (e.g., MrBayes, PAUP).
- Uses orthographic data instead of phonetic transcriptions, which may obscure true linguistic correspondences.
- Some derivations and algorithmic steps (e.g., merging in maximization, "optimal unigram") are unclear and hinder reproducibility.
- Relies on neighbor-joining for tree construction, a method with known limitations; more robust alternatives were not used.

Questions to authors:
- Please clarify the merging process in the maximization step and its role in model improvement.
- How does the method handle noisy data or irregular correspondences?
- Have you considered using phonetic transcriptions, and what challenges would that entail?
- Why was neighbor-joining chosen for phylogenetic inference, and were alternative methods explored?
- Can you provide empirical comparisons to tools like MrBayes or PAUP to contextualize performance?

Additional comments:
- Minor typo: change "focus in on" to "focus on."
- Expand the related-work discussion to include detailed comparisons to existing methods and tools.
- Future work should explore scalability and applicability to non-Slavic languages or larger datasets.

- Overall: promising and innovative approach that would be strengthened by addressing scalability, clarity, data representation, and comparative evaluation.
