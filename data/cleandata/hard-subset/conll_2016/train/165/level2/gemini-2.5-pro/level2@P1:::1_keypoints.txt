- Novel unsupervised MDL-based method for discovering orthographic correspondence rules from cognate lists across multiple languages simultaneously.  
- Deterministic EM-like algorithm that builds complex rules by iteratively merging simpler single-character rules.  
- Evaluated on 13 Slavic languages and shown to recover known phylogenetic relationships and subgroup insights.  
- Contribution: principled multi-language framework that models correspondences across any subset of languages rather than just pairwise.  
- Contribution: new "shared Description Length" (sDL) metric for fine-grained, quantitative analysis of similarities among language groups.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration yielding deeper subgroup structure insights (e.g., West, East, South Slavic comparisons).  
- Strength: multi-way analysis captures patterns shared by subsets of languages, offering a more holistic view than pairwise methods.  
- Strength: MDL provides a theoretically sound objective that balances model complexity and fit, reducing reliance on strong priors.  
- Strength: sDL decomposition gives richer information than a single tree, illuminating shared model complexity across subgroups.  
- Strength: practical "data over-weighting" control lets users adjust rule granularity to surface linguistically meaningful patterns in limited data.  
- Weakness: scalability/runtime limits (implementation up to ~5 languages; core alignment complexity appears to grow exponentially with N).  
- Weakness: no direct comparisons or baselines against alternative methods (e.g., lexical similarity or phylogenetic software) to assess advantage.  
- Weakness: MDL formulation clarity issues—encoding of rule counts in L(M) vs. L(D|M) is confusing and needs clearer explanation or examples.  
- Weakness: method depends on orthography; script effects (e.g., Latin vs Cyrillic Serbo-Croatian) may conflate orthographic and phonological correspondences.  
- Question: clarify rationale for including rule-count encoding in the model cost L(M) rather than in the data likelihood L(D|M).  
- Question: how sensitive is the deterministic algorithm to noisy inputs (e.g., a small percentage of non-cognates)?  
- Question: provide more detail on the partial order reduction used in alignment and its effectiveness for managing combinatorial complexity.
