- Introduces an unsupervised MDL-based method to discover orthographic correspondence rules simultaneously across multiple languages.  
- Proposes the Shared Description Length (sDL) metric for fine-grained quantitative analysis of structural similarity among language subsets.  
- Novel, well-motivated extension of MDL to multi-language discovery without imposing prior linguistic assumptions.  
- Strong experimental validation: pairwise analysis across 13 Slavic languages yields a phylogeny aligning with established classifications.  
- Four-way sDL analyses reveal nuanced subgroup relationships (e.g., CS-PL and RU-BG) that hierarchical clustering would miss.  
- Scalability is limited: current implementation bottleneck restricts practical use to about five languages.  
- The data over-weighting mechanism appears ad-hoc and subjective (weighting parameter α lacks theoretical justification).  
- Deterministic training ensures reproducibility but risks convergence to local optima and missing subtle correspondence rules.
