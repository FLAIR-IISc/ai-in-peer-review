- MDL-based framework for discovering string-level correspondences across multiple languages using a "Shared Description Length" (sDL) metric.  
- Deterministic EM-like, greedy algorithm that iteratively merges patterns to infer correspondence rules.  
- Data over-weighting extension lets users encourage discovery of rarer, longer correspondence rules for exploratory analysis.  
- Novelty: moves beyond pairwise analysis to quantify shared information across any subset of languages, enabling richer linguistic insights.  
- Methodologically sound: MDL two-part code formulation and EM-like optimization balance model complexity and fit.  
- Convincing experiments: 13 Slavic-language evaluations; NCD reconstructs expected phylogeny and sDL visualizations reveal subtle subgroup relations.  
- Practical: deterministic algorithm ensures reproducibility and the over-weighting parameter supports expert-guided granularity.  
- Scalability concern: current implementation limited to ~5 languages; exponential subset growth and alignment bottlenecks need complexity analysis and pruning heuristics.  
- Greedy M-step limitation: single-best-merge strategy risks local optima and may miss complex rules requiring non-greedy merges.  
- Clarity issue: parts of the MDL/code-length formulation (Section 4.1) are dense and need more intuitive explanation of final terms.  
- Missing baselines: no direct experimental comparison to pairwise-only models or established alignment methods to quantify joint-model benefits.  
- Question: Have alternative optimization strategies (e.g., beam search) been considered to mitigate local optima?  
- Question: Can the authors provide principled guidance for setting the data over-weighting parameter α or methods to detect diminishing returns?
