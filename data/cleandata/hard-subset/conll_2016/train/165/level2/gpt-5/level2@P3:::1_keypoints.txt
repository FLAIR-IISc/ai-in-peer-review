- MDL-based framework and deterministic EM-plus-merge algorithm for discovering multilingual, subset-specific string correspondence rules without predefining rule shapes.
- Information-theoretic analyses: NCD-based pairwise distances yielding phylogeny and sDL for measuring structure shared across four languages.
- Practical system: shortest-path alignment, partial-order reduction, Laplace smoothing, and an overweighting knob to control granularity.

- Clear, principled two-part MDL objective with explicit alphabet and sparse rule encoding.
- Well-motivated EM-like training with greedy merges grounded in MDL and frequency considerations.
- Supports rules over arbitrary language subsets, capturing mixed two-/three-way and singleton patterns.
- NCD experiments recover expected Slavic groupings and sensible script/outlier effects.
- sDL highlights shared vs language-specific model complexity and individual overheads (e.g., diacritics).
- Usable from orthography alone and compatible with seeded or singleton initializations.

- Evaluation is mainly qualitative/indirect; no precision/recall against expert correspondences or comparisons to strong baselines.
- Scalability unclear: reported practical limit ≈5 languages, high computational complexity, no pruning/parallelization or scaling curves.
- Modeling raw orthography conflates script and phonology; no transliteration or phonemic normalization experiments.
- sDL definition and tree lack uncertainty estimates; several implementation details (merge-compatibility, stopping, partial-order reduction) are underspecified.
- Overweighting parameter α is subjective and unsupported by principled selection or sensitivity analysis.

- How are merge candidates defined/tested for “compatibility,” and is there a bound on candidates per iteration?
- For NCD, are joint/concatenated models re-trained for L(X,Y) and are L(X,X)/L(Y,Y) trained identically for fair comparison?
- What Laplace smoothing magnitude is used and how sensitive are results to it?
- What is the stopping criterion and were multiple restarts or alternative initializations tried?
- Have you tested a usage-weighted sDL (e.g., sum count(π)·L(code(π))) and how would conclusions change?

- Add quantitative validation: compare NCD phylogeny to baselines, evaluate correspondences vs gold sets, and show multilingual vs pairwise benefits.
- Provide robustness analyses: bootstrap support for trees/sDL, sensitivity to α, initialization, and smoothing.
- Address orthography vs phonology: experiments with transliteration and/or phonemic normalization and report effects.
- Discuss scalability and engineering: runtime/memory curves, pruning/heuristics (A*/beam), caching, and parallelization strategies.
- Improve reproducibility: release code/data, give pseudocode, and specify all hyperparameters and merge/PO-reduction details.

- Overall: principled and promising MDL approach with compelling qualitative results; strengthen with quantitative baselines, robustness analyses, clearer implementation details, and scalability work to improve impact.
