- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover multi-language correspondences without prior shape assumptions; validated on Slavic languages.
- Formulates correspondence discovery as an MDL problem and provides an efficient, deterministic algorithm.
- Method naturally scales to more than two languages, demonstrated in a four-way analysis (Czech, Polish, Russian, Bulgarian).
- Introduces Shared Description Length (sDL) to quantify fine-grained shared linguistic structure among subsets of languages.
- MDL provides a principled statistical foundation that avoids arbitrary modeling assumptions.
- Deterministic algorithm ensures reproducibility and interpretability of results.
- Empirical results align with established linguistic classifications and reveal detailed similarity patterns.
- Paper clearly explains the algorithm, EM steps, alignment procedure, and the data over-weighting technique.
- sDL offers a novel metric that goes beyond simple pairwise distance measures.
- Computational bottlenecks and scaling challenges limit applicability to many languages or large datasets.
- Experiments rely on relatively small Swadesh lists and cognate sets, raising questions about robustness to larger or different data sources.
- Reliance on NCD as an evaluation metric may not capture nuanced linguistic relationships; alternative metrics could help.
- Limited direct comparison to existing correspondence-discovery methods weakens contextual evaluation.
- Paper lacks thorough error analysis or discussion of failure cases and spurious correspondences.
- Question: what is the algorithmic complexity as a function of number of languages (N) and dataset size (D), and what are runtime measurements?
- Question: how sensitive are results to the initial null alignment and were alternative initializations tried?
- Question: how does performance change with input data size/quality and have other data sources or augmentations been tested?
- Question: can the authors elaborate on the linguistic significance and any unexpected findings in the four-way correspondences?
- Question: have existing statistical-significance methods been considered or adapted beyond the MDL framework?
- Improve formatting of Table 3 for readability.
- Reference list appears incomplete; some citations lack full details.
- The explanation of data over-weighting is strong and highlights potential for linguistic expert input.
