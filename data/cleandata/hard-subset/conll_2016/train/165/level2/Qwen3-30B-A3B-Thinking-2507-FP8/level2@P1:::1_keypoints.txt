- Introduces an MDL-based framework to discover string-level correspondences across multiple languages without restrictive distributional/shape assumptions.
- Contributions: principled MDL framework, an efficient rule-growing algorithm based on statistical significance, and experiments quantifying linguistic similarities and subgroups.

- Addresses a gap by enabling multi-language correspondence discovery with a statistically grounded MDL approach.
- Algorithmic design is thorough, including clear MDL code, training, alignment, and a useful data over-weighting control.
- Strong experimental validation: reconstructs known groupings, performs four-way analyses, and builds phylogenetic trees aligning with linguistic classifications.
- Produces qualitative insights showing phonological and orthographic correspondences at multiple granularities and demonstrates model flexibility.
- Clear, accessible technical presentation with sufficient detail for replication.

- Evaluation is limited to closely related Slavic languages; broader language-family tests are needed for generalizability.
- Lacks detailed comparison to existing correspondence-discovery methods, especially multi-language approaches.
- Limited computational complexity and scalability analysis beyond claiming feasibility for up to five languages.
- Does not address handling of very different writing systems or morphological types (e.g., Arabic vs. Latin, agglutinative vs. fusional).

- How does the method perform on languages with different scripts or morphological structures, and how might it be adapted?
- Please provide direct comparisons with existing multi-language correspondence methods and clarify specific improvements.
- Provide more scalability details for larger numbers of languages (10+) and larger datasets, and identify main computational bottlenecks.

- Paper is well-written, promising for historical linguistics and multilingual NLP; recommend addressing scalability and testing on more diverse language families; data over-weighting is a notable strength.
