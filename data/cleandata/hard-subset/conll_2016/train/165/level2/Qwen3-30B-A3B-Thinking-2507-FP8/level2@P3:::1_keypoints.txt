- Summary: MDL-based method for discovering linguistic correspondences across multiple languages without distributional/shape assumptions; efficient algorithm handles any language subset and is evaluated on Slavic languages, recovering meaningful correspondences and phylogenetic trees.

- Contribution 1: MDL framework for multilingual correspondence discovery that avoids restrictive distributional assumptions.

- Contribution 2: Efficient algorithm that can handle multiple languages simultaneously and control detail via data over-weighting.

- Contribution 3: Demonstration of quantifying similarity across all subsets of analyzed languages.

- Strength: Addresses a notable gap by providing a theoretically grounded MDL approach for multi-language correspondence discovery.

- Strength: Comprehensive Slavic evaluation showing linguistically meaningful correspondences and phylogenies consistent with established classifications.

- Strength: Data over-weighting offers practical control over granularity of discovered correspondences.

- Strength: Simultaneous quantification of all language subsets provides richer insights than pairwise methods.

- Weakness: No direct quantitative comparison with state-of-the-art multi-language correspondence methods, leaving novelty/significance unclear.

- Weakness: Insufficient computational complexity and scalability analysis; only reports experiments up to five languages on modest hardware.

- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to Slavic languages with no discussion of challenges for typologically different families or writing systems.

- Question: How does the method compare quantitatively to existing multi-language correspondence approaches?

- Question: What are the computational bottlenecks when scaling beyond five languages, and how can they be mitigated?

- Question: Can the authors provide examples or evaluations on non-Slavic language families to show broader applicability?

- Additional comment: Need more discussion of handling very different writing systems or linguistic structures.

- Additional comment: The theoretical justification for data over-weighting should be clarified, as it departs from strict MDL.

- Additional comment: Strengthen connections to linguistic theory with specific examples of how discovered correspondences inform hypotheses.

- Additional comment: Expand evaluation of robustness to noisy or incomplete data, a common challenge in linguistic datasets.
