- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover cross-lingual word correspondences using a deterministic two-part MDL code.  
- Presents an efficient algorithm to infer statistically significant correspondence rules.  
- Validated the approach with pairwise and four-way experiments on Slavic languages.  
- Novel application of the MDL principle to correspondence discovery.  
- Algorithm is efficient and scalable to large datasets.  
- Experiments provide linguistic insights for Slavic languages.  
- Evaluation is limited to Slavic languages, leaving generality unclear.  
- No comparison to state-of-the-art correspondence discovery methods.  
- Paper lacks discussion of limitations (e.g., handling noisy or incomplete data).  
- Asks how the method handles noisy/incomplete data and whether extensions are planned.  
- Questions applicability to other language families and required modifications.  
- Requests comparison to SOTA methods and a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Paper is well-written and clear but needs broader experiments and SOTA comparisons.
