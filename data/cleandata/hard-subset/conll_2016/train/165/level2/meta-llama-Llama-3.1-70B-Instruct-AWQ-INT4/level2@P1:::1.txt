**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to discovering correspondences between multiple languages using the Minimum Description Length (MDL) principle. The authors introduce an efficient algorithm for inferring statistically significant correspondence rules from data, which can be used for pairwise and multi-language analysis. The approach is based on the idea of treating correspondences as associated strings of characters without assuming any underlying distribution. The authors evaluate their method on a dataset of Slavic languages and demonstrate its effectiveness in discovering non-trivial associations and quantifying linguistic similarity.
