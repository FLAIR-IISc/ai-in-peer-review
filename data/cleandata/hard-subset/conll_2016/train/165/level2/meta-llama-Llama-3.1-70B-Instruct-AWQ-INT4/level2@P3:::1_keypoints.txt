- Proposes an MDL-based method to discover correspondences between multiple languages.  
- Introduces an efficient, deterministic algorithm for inferring statistically significant correspondence rules.  
- Algorithm can be applied to any subset of languages without shape/distribution assumptions.  
- Enables quantification of linguistic similarity beyond pairwise comparisons.  
- Evaluated on a Slavic-language dataset, showing discovery of non-trivial associations.  
- Novel application of MDL to a complex NLP problem.  
- Algorithm is efficient and suitable for large-scale applications in principle.  
- Provides more detailed analysis of language relationships than pairwise methods.  
- Computational complexity may limit scalability to very large or high-dimensional datasets.  
- Results may be sensitive to input data quality; data quality analysis is missing.  
- Evaluation is limited to one Slavic dataset, so generalizability is unclear.  
- Asks authors to address scalability, provide detailed data-quality impact analysis, and evaluate on other languages/datasets.  
- Overall promising approach but requires further evaluation and refinement.
