- Problem & approach: Proposes an MDL-based deterministic method to discover string-level correspondences across languages without prior distributional or shape assumptions.
- Contribution — MDL framework: Introduces a two-part MDL approach balancing model complexity and data description length for correspondence discovery.
- Contribution — algorithm: Presents a deterministic EM + graph-based alignment algorithm that can handle multiple languages simultaneously.
- Contribution — experiments: Validates method on Slavic languages, producing phylogenetic trees and similarity measures that align with linguistic classifications.
- Contribution — utility: Enables quantitative and qualitative analyses with potential applications in historical linguistics, language teaching, and multilingual NLP.
- Strength: Novel, statistically grounded, and interpretable methodology with deterministic reproducibility.
- Strength: Scalability beyond pairwise analysis to multi-language correspondence discovery.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments that lend empirical credibility.
- Strength: Data over-weighting allows adjustable granularity of discovered correspondences.
- Strength: Method could be extended to other domains (e.g., bioinformatics, comparative genomics).
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to structurally similar Slavic languages; generalizability to diverse families is unclear.
- Weakness: Computational bottlenecks — runtimes of several hours for five languages raise scalability concerns.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of linguistic interpretation and implications of discovered correspondences.
- Weakness: Data over-weighting introduces subjectivity, reducing objectivity and comparability across studies.
- Weakness: Insufficient implementation and dataset details, raising reproducibility concerns.
- Question: How does the method perform on typologically diverse language families (e.g., Indo-European vs. Sino-Tibetan)?
- Question: Can the algorithm be parallelized to mitigate computational bottlenecks for larger datasets or more languages?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of initial alignment (null vs. greedy), and when is one preferable?
- Question: Can authors provide more linguistic examples and practical applications (e.g., MT, historical linguistics)?
- Conclusion: Promising and innovative approach with solid experimental support, but needs validation on diverse/larger datasets, stronger linguistic interpretation, and improved reproducibility.
