- Task and method: Proposes a semi-supervised, graph-based min-cut framework to assign temporal orientations (past/present/future/atemporal) to WordNet synsets using glosses and relations.  
- Dual-view approach: Combines supervised individual scores and association scores from WordNet relations for improved classification.  
- Resource contribution: Produces an improved time-annotated WordNet with higher accuracy than existing resources (e.g., TempoWordNet).  
- Data efficiency: Achieves competitive performance with much less labeled data (e.g., 400 examples vs. 1264).  
- Empirical performance: Shows substantial, statistically significant improvements in intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations (including TempEval-3).  
- Scalability and flexibility: Graph formulation integrates multiple views and unlabeled data, handling the full WordNet hierarchy.  
- Thorough evaluation: Includes intrinsic/extrinsic tests, feature ablations, and experiments varying labeled-data amounts.  
- Practical applicability: Resource useful for QA, IR, clinical narratives, and is publicly released.  
- Ambiguity handling lacking: Limited discussion and no concrete strategies for ambiguous synsets (e.g., “dinosaur”).  
- Real-world evaluation limited: Needs broader testing across diverse domains (e.g., clinical or economic texts).  
- WordNet dependence: Heavy reliance on WordNet may hinder applicability to languages or domains without such resources.  
- Extension questions: Unclear handling of multi-oriented synsets (e.g., “monthly”) and how the framework generalizes to other languages/domains.  
- External resources: Suggestion to consider incorporating distributional embeddings or other external resources to improve association scores.
