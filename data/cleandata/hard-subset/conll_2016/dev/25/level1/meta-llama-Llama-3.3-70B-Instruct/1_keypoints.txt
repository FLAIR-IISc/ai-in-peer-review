- Novel semi-supervised graph-based method that augments WordNet senses with temporal information using definitions and conceptual/lexical relations.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art temporal vs. atemporal and past/present/future classification, outperforming prior non-graph semi-supervised methods.  
- Creates a crowdsourced gold standard and demonstrates the ability to produce a high-quality temporal lexical resource.  
- Leverages WordNet relations to construct a graph capturing temporal relationships between synsets, improving classification accuracy.  
- Semi-supervised design effectively uses limited labeled data plus unlabeled data to boost performance.  
- Conducts extensive intrinsic and extrinsic evaluations to validate the approach.  
- The task is inherently complex—judging synset temporal dimensions from definitions is difficult.  
- The graph-based approach may be less interpretable than traditional models, making decision rationale harder to understand.  
- Heavy dependence on WordNet could limit applicability to other languages or domains.  
- How will the authors address task complexity and improve interpretability of the graph-based model?  
- Request for more details on gold-standard dataset construction and the crowdsourcing process.  
- How do the authors envision applying this temporal lexical resource in real-world scenarios (e.g., time-oriented clinical narratives)?
