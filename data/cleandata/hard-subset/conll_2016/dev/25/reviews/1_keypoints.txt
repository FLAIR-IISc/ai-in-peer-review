- Proposes a graph-based semi-supervised method to tag WordNet synset senses with temporal labels (past/present/future/atemporal) and produces a full WordNet annotation.  
- Paper is generally well-constructed, clear, and methodologically sound, producing a reusable fine-grained temporal resource.  
- Reports improvements of ~11% accuracy over a baseline/time-tagger and ~10% F1 on TempEval-3; system performs well with only 400 labeled examples.  
- Extrinsic evaluation section lacks sufficient task description and an example to clarify what the system predicts.  
- Unclear definition of "entity pairs" and the features used—need clarification of entity attributes and feature construction.  
- Ambiguity about POS representation for a pair (single or per entity) and whether lemmas were obtained automatically.  
- Labels description is confusing—unclear meanings of “event to document creation time” and “event to same sentence event” and whether these are relations.  
- Footnote about retaining 14 relations is unclear—why other relations were ignored and what makes a mapping “too complex.”  
- Unclear whether reported scores are macro- or micro-averaged.  
- Ablation study shows Lexica and Entity feature sets give similar scores—possible redundancy that needs explanation.  
- Questions about SVM usage: which parameters were optimized for extrinsic evaluation, was the SVM inside the MinCut framework optimized, and cite LibSVM if used.  
- Request counts for fine-grained labels are missing; only coarse counts (127 temporal vs 271 atemporal) are provided.  
- Request an estimate of how many words are temporally ambiguous (e.g., “present”).  
- Claim of “significantly better” in Table 3 lacks any statistical test description or p-values.  
- Related-work clarifications needed: what task did Filannino & Nenadic (2014) perform, provide a reference for “post-calibration procedure,” and explain precisely how other models differ.  
- Presentation and minor issues: enlarge and simplify Table 3 and its caption, consider graph for Table 4, correct 1064→1264, add TempEval-3 reference, reorder scores in Table 6 for clarity, and fix the typo “atemporal)”.
