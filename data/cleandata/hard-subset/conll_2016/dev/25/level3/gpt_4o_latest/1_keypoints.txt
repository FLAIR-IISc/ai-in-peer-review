- Proposes a novel graph-based semi-supervised algorithm to tag WordNet senses with temporal labels (past, present, future, atemporal).  
- Breaks the task into binary temporal/atemporal classification followed by finer-grained past/present/future labeling.  
- Leverages WordNet structure and unlabeled data using a minimum-cut graph framework.  
- Fully annotates WordNet via prior labeled data and crowd-sourcing, producing a fine-grained temporal lexical resource.  
- Achieves an 11% accuracy improvement over SUTime and prior work using only 400 labeled examples.  
- Demonstrates a 10% F1 improvement on TempEval-3, showing extrinsic utility for temporal tasks.  
- Resource has broad application potential in temporal relation extraction, IR, and clinical narratives.  
- Significant empirical gains highlight the efficiency and robustness of the semi-supervised graph approach.  
- Novel methodology: use of WordNet relations in a minimum-cut framework and hierarchical finer-grained classification.  
- Comprehensive evaluation including intrinsic/extrinsic tests and a feature ablation study.  
- Extrinsic evaluation lacks detail on TempEval-3 setup, features, entity pair selection, and label mappings.  
- Unclear rationale for ignoring certain WordNet relations and for the complexity of the relation mapping.  
- Averaging choice (macro vs. micro) is unspecified and could affect reported results.  
- Ablation study shows redundancy between Lexica and Entity features but does not explain why.  
- No details on SVM parameter optimization or reference to LibSVM, affecting reproducibility.  
- Claims of statistical significance are unsupported by significance tests or p-values.  
- Requests clarification on TempEval-3 features/label mappings and entity pair selection.  
- Asks why specific WordNet relations were ignored and what makes the mapping complex.  
- Requests explanation for feature redundancy observed in ablation results.  
- Seeks details on SVM parameter tuning and the absence of LibSVM reference.  
- Asks for per-label example counts for past/present/future and handling of ambiguous terms like "present."  
- Overall assessment: significant and novel contribution with strong results, but clarity and reproducibility issues should be addressed before final acceptance.
