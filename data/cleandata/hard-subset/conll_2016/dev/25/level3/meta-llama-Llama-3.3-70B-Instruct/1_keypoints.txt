- Presents a graph-based semi-supervised classification algorithm for temporal tagging of word senses, reporting ~11% accuracy improvement and ~10% F1 gain on TempEval-3.  
- Combines item-specific information with WordNet structure and leverages unlabeled data to annotate WordNet fully.  
- Contribution: a semi-supervised graph-based paradigm for recognizing the temporal dimension of word senses.  
- Contribution: integration of WordNet relations, glosses, and other attributes into the classification process.  
- Contribution: creation of a temporal lexical resource usable for temporal information extraction tasks.  
- Strength: clear, well-constructed presentation.  
- Strength: effective use of semi-supervised learning to exploit unlabeled data.  
- Strength: evaluation via crowd-sourcing and comparison to prior work demonstrates significant improvements.  
- Weakness: experimental settings lack sufficient detail, especially about the extrinsic evaluation task.  
- Weakness: unclear how SVM was used and how its parameters were optimized.  
- Weakness: potential redundancy between Lexica and Entity features not addressed.  
- Weakness: results presentation missing details about gold data and number of ambiguous words.  
- Weakness: minor errors such as missing references and unclear technical terms need correction.  
- Question: How were SVM parameters optimized?  
- Question: How was redundancy between Lexica and Entity handled?  
- Question: Can you provide more information on the extrinsic evaluation task?
