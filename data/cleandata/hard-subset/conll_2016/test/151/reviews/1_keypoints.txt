- Paper proposes a new deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and evaluates it across many languages.
- Core algorithm uses POS-based head rules to define permitted dependencies.
- Words are ranked by a biased PageRank over a graph defined by those permitted dependencies.
- Each word is linked to its closest allowable head that preserves tree structure and respects directionality.
- Paper is interesting and clearly presented but only slightly different from Søgaard (2012).
- Request for analysis of head rules in relation to the corpus (e.g., frequency of disconnected graphs and component sizes).
- Concern that head directionality was tuned on the test set rather than training/dev, though decision converges quickly.
- Ask how PageRank tie-breaking is handled and whether ties affect performance.
- Suggest reporting oracle results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show upper-bound impacts.
- Minor editorial suggestions: fix phrasing in 4.1 ("we obtain [the] rank"), reorder Table 5 columns to match Table 7 (prefer Table 7 order), and fix wording in 6.1 ("isolate the [contribution] of both").
