- Paper introduces UDP: a deterministic, unsupervised UD parser using personalized PageRank and a small set of dependency head rules, requiring no training data.  
- Contributions: adapts head rules to UD and enforces UD constraints (e.g., function words as leaves).  
- Contributions: uses personalized PageRank to rank content words and identify main predicates.  
- Contributions: estimates adposition attachment direction on test data, removing language-specific parameters.  
- Contributions: evaluates across 32 languages and domains, showing robustness to domain shift and POS errors.  
- Strength: novel, simple, linguistically grounded, and computationally efficient unsupervised approach that serves as a robust cross-lingual baseline.  
- Strength: strong cross-lingual and domain robustness, particularly for low-resource and domain-shift settings.  
- Strength: competitive empirical results (e.g., outperforming MSD on Hindi) with detailed POS and domain breakdowns.  
- Strength: code and datasets released, supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: method resembles prior work (Søgaard 2012); novelty over past PageRank-based methods needs clearer distinction.  
- Weakness: missing analysis of disconnected graphs produced by dependency rules and their frequency across languages.  
- Weakness: tie-breaking in PageRank scores is not explained and its effect on performance is unclear.  
- Weakness: lacks oracle experiments for constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) to quantify component contributions.  
- Weakness: minor presentation issues (unclear phrasing in Sections 4.1/6.1, inconsistent table column orders).  
- Questions: how are disconnected graphs handled in PageRank and how often do they occur across languages?  
- Questions: can the authors provide oracle results for individual constraints to assess their impact?  
- Questions: how are PageRank ties resolved and what is the impact of tie-breaking on accuracy?  
- Conclusion: promising, efficient unsupervised parser with strong robustness and reproducibility; address analyses and novelty clarification to strengthen the paper.
