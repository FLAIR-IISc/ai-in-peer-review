- Paper presents UDP: a training-free unsupervised UD parser using personalized PageRank and simple hard-coded head rules, estimating syntactic traits from test data.
- Evaluated on 32 languages, UDP shows domain robustness and outperforms a multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD) on some languages (e.g., Hindi).
- Contribution 1: Novel unsupervised approach using personalized PageRank to rank content words and a two-step decoder enforcing UD constraints.
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates strong domain robustness, making UDP a practical baseline for multilingual parsing under domain shift.
- Contribution 3: Entirely unsupervised and freely available, making it accessible for low-resource languages and practical applications.
- Strength 1: Advances unsupervised dependency parsing by achieving competitive results with minimal linguistic assumptions.
- Strength 2: Robust to domain shifts and noisy POS tags, showing greater stability than MSD.
- Strength 3: Paper is well-written with clear methodology and comprehensive experimental setup across languages and scenarios.
- Strength 4: Identifies limitations (e.g., MWEs, coordination) and opens avenues for future research.
- Weakness 1: Struggles with complex syntactic phenomena such as relative clauses, coordination, and punctuation attachment.
- Weakness 2: Reliance on UD-specific constraints may limit adaptability to other syntactic frameworks or non-UD treebanks.
- Weakness 3: Evaluation focuses on UAS only; lack of LAS limits assessment of labeled parsing performance.
- Question 1: How does UDP handle languages with significant syntactic divergence from UD, like free word order languages?
- Question 2: Could PageRank be extended to directly score dependency edges instead of words, and what challenges would that pose?
- Question 3: Have the authors considered partial edge labeling to assess potential for labeled parsing?
- Conclusion: UDP is a robust, practical unsupervised parser with notable strengths and some limitations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
