- Paper introduces UDP: a deterministic unsupervised UD parser using POS-based universal head rules, personalized PageRank to bias predicates, and a two-step decoding attaching content then function words.  
- Main contributions: extensive empirical evaluation across 32 languages and a clear adaptation of Søgaard (2012b) to UD with personalized PageRank and UD-aware decoding.  

- Strength: very comprehensive evaluation (32 treebanks, gold/predicted/naive POS, cross-domain analysis).  
- Strength: method is simple, intuitive, clearly described, and reproducible with a worked example.  
- Strength: shows notable robustness—less sensitive to domain shift and POS tagging errors than a competitive delexicalized transfer baseline.  

- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—approach is an incremental extension of Søgaard (2012b).  
- Weakness: head directionality for adpositions is estimated on the test set, effectively tuning on test data and violating evaluation norms.  
- Weakness: analysis lacks deeper investigation of core components (e.g., properties of graphs induced by head rules like disconnected components and sizes).  

- Question: how are ties handled in PageRank ranking and sorting, how often do ties occur, and do tie-breaks affect performance?  
- Question: have the authors considered reporting oracle results isolating each constraint (head rules, directionality, closest-head) to quantify component-wise performance ceilings?
