- Presents UDP: a training-free unsupervised UD parser combining POS-based hard rules with personalized PageRank and a two-step decoder enforcing UD content/function distinctions.  
- Builds a head-dependent graph, uses PageRank to identify central content words (near root), attaches content words first then function words as leaves.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages versus a strong multi-source delexicalized transfer parser, showing competitive performance and superior robustness to POS errors and domain shifts.

- Novel, simple, and elegant method: leverages UD priors, a few high-precision rules, and PageRank to produce a transparent, strong baseline without training.  
- Strong empirical results: average UAS only 6.4% below the transfer parser across 32 languages and better than baseline on some languages (e.g., Hindi); analyses across POS-quality scenarios included.  
- Exceptional cross-domain robustness: substantially lower performance variance across domains than the data-driven baseline, demonstrating better stability and generalizability.  
- Clear, reproducible presentation with detailed methodology, parsing example, and commitment to releasing code and data.

- Heavy reliance on high-quality POS tags undermines the "training-free" claim; performance drops sharply with naive POS, limiting true low-resource applicability.  
- Potential brittleness from hand-crafted, English-tuned rules (e.g., DET failure in Basque); rules may not generalize to typologically diverse languages or uncovered constructions.  
- Limited comparison to other modern unsupervised parsing approaches (e.g., DMV extensions, neural unsupervised models), leaving UDP’s position in the broader grammar-induction landscape unclear.

- How sensitive is PageRank personalization (5x weight to first verb/content word) and the “first word” heuristic, especially for verb-final languages?  
- Could on-the-fly, data-driven estimation be extended to other function-word attachment directions (e.g., DET, CONJ) to improve language-agnosticism?
