- Presents UDP, a fully unsupervised, deterministic dependency parser for UD that uses POS-based head-rule constraints, personalized PageRank, and a two-step decoding ensuring function words are leaves.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages and multiple POS/tagging scenarios, showing competitive performance vs. a multi-source delexicalized system and greater robustness to domain shifts.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation and robustness analysis of a simple unsupervised UD baseline across languages and domains.  
- Main contribution: effective adaptation of rule-and-rank parsing to UD via personalized PageRank for root identification and a two-step decoding enforcing UD constraints.  
- Strength: Extensive and rigorous evaluation on a broad set of languages with a strong comparison against a competitive MSD baseline.  
- Strength: Excellent robustness analysis demonstrating lower variance and better cross-domain stability than data-driven MSD, under both gold and predicted POS.  
- Strength: Method is clear, simple, deterministic, and easy to reproduce, making it a strong candidate baseline for UD parsing.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—mostly an incremental adaptation of prior PageRank/head-rule work (e.g., Søgaard 2012b) rather than a fundamentally new parsing method.  
- Weakness: Uses test-set information to estimate adposition directionality, violating standard train/dev/test separation and undermining methodological rigor.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper ablation and error analysis (e.g., coverage and impact of head rules, oracle analyses for directionality/distance) to reveal sources of error and improvement potential.  
- Question: How does the deterministic decoder break ties when PageRank assigns identical scores, and does tie-breaking affect performance?  
- Question: How sensitive is the parser to misclassification of adposition direction (pre- vs. postpositional), and what is the performance drop when misclassified?  
- Question: How often are constructed head-rule graphs disconnected, and what are the connected component size distributions—impacting the role of PageRank teleport probability?
