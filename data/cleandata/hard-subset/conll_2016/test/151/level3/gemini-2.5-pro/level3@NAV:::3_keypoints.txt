- Presents UDP, a training-free unsupervised dependency parser using only POS tags and a graph of potential dependencies.  
- Uses a modified PageRank that downweights stop words and personalizes to boost the main predicate.  
- Primary contribution: novel application of personalized PageRank to bias main predicate identification; secondary: extensive evaluation across 32 languages showing competitiveness and domain robustness.  
- Strength: elegant simplicity and training-free design make it a strong baseline and useful for low-resource settings, with demonstrated cross-domain robustness.  
- Strength: conceptually interesting use of personalized PageRank to inject linguistic priors by boosting the main predicate.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation across 32 languages and under gold vs. predicted POS conditions.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—approach closely mirrors Søgaard (2012) and offers only incremental contribution over prior work.  
- Weakness: personalization component under-explored and poorly justified—choice of weight (5) is arbitrary with no ablation/sensitivity analysis and the focus on only the main predicate lacks motivation.  
- Weakness: evaluation presentation relies on large tables that obscure trends; visualizations (e.g., bar charts) would improve interpretability.  
- Weakness: for predicted-POS experiments, POS tagging accuracy is not reported, preventing analysis of error propagation.  
- Minor: typo at l.207 ("The the main idea" → "The main idea").
