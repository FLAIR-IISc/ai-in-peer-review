- Paper presents UDP: a training-free, rule-based dependency parser for Universal Dependencies using POS-based head-dependent rules, personalized PageRank biased to an estimated main predicate, and a two-step decoder (content words then function words).
- Evaluated on 32 UD languages and compared to a multi-source delexicalized supervised parser (MSD), with robustness tests for predicted POS tags and cross-domain settings.

- Contribution: Extensive, multilingual empirical evaluation showing a simple rule-based parser can approach supervised delexicalized performance without training data.
- Contribution: Detailed analysis demonstrating UDP is more robust to domain shifts and POS-tagging errors than the MSD baseline.

- Strength: Simple, effective design (small rule set + PageRank + decoding) achieving 57.5% average UAS vs 63.9% for MSD.
- Strength: Thorough multilingual and cross-domain evaluation (32 languages) showing lower performance variance across domains.
- Strength: Truly unsupervised and language-agnostic—requires only POS tags (or word frequency in a naive variant) and estimates adposition directionality on the fly.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty; approach largely builds on prior PageRank-based POS-rule parsing (Søgaard 2012b) with incremental UD-specific adaptations.
- Weakness: Arbitrary parameter choices and insufficient exploration of personalization (weight set to 5 without sensitivity analysis; only a single predicate boosted).
- Weakness: Presentation issues and missing analyses—results mainly in large tables with no visualizations and no per-language POS accuracies reported.

- Question: Why was the PageRank personalization weight set to 5, and is there any sensitivity analysis or ablation for this parameter?
- Question: Were alternative personalization schemes considered (e.g., smaller boosts to all verbs or distributed weighting across POS categories)?
- Question: Can the authors provide per-language POS tagging accuracies for the UDPP/MSDP experiments to analyze parser degradation sources?

- Additional comment: Minor typo in Section 3.1 ("The the main idea..." → "The main idea...").
