- Introduces UDP: a training-free unsupervised UD parser combining personalized PageRank to find content words with a small set of UD-compliant hard-coded head rules and a two-step decoding that attaches content then function words.  
- Contribution: a simple, linguistically motivated unsupervised algorithm that integrates UD priors with graph-based ranking as a strong baseline for low-resource parsing.  
- Contribution: extensive empirical evaluation over 32 languages and multiple domains showing competitiveness with delexicalized transfer and improved robustness to domain shift.  
- Strength: significant advancement in unsupervised parsing—elegant, intuitive, and impressive performance for a training-free method.  
- Strength: paper is exceptionally clear and reproducible, with detailed explanations and specific rules provided.  
- Strength: thorough, insightful evaluation (gold vs predicted POS, cross-domain tests) demonstrating robustness, especially to domain shifts.  
- Weakness: relies on hand-crafted head rules developed from the English treebank, introducing indirect supervision and limiting purity of grammar induction.  
- Weakness: still lags behind delexicalized transfer on average (~6.4 UAS gap on gold POS), limiting applicability when source data exist.  
- Weakness: limited linguistic coverage—fixed rule set fails on complex constructions (e.g., relative clauses), causing systematic errors for some structures.
