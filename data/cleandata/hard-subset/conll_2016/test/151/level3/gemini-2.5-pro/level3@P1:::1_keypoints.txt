- Introduces UDP: an unsupervised, deterministic UD parser using POS-based head-selection rules, Personalized PageRank, and two-step decoding with no training data.  
- Evaluated on 32 UD languages and compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline (MSD); UDP is competitive and more robust across domains.  
- Contribution: comprehensive empirical analysis across gold/predicted/naive POS settings and cross-domain evaluation showing greater stability than data-driven transfer.  
- Contribution: a simple, reproducible algorithm combining POS constraints, personalized PageRank, and nearest-attachment decoding, adapted to UD (function words forced as leaves).  
- Strength: extensive multilingual evaluation and strong cross-domain robustness (lower std. dev. across domains than MSD).  
- Strength: clarity and simplicity; deterministic, no-training method with a clear worked example that aids replication.  
- Strength: strong unsupervised performance—competitive with MSD, better on some languages (e.g., Hindi), and small degradation from gold- to predicted-POS.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—largely builds on prior PageRank-based dependency work; personalization and two-step decoding are incremental.  
- Weakness: methodological flaw—adposition head-directionality estimated from test data, undermining the zero-resource claim.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the POS-based head rules (coverage across languages, graph connectivity issues, reliance on teleport probability).  
- Question: how are ties in PageRank scores handled deterministically, and does tie-breaking affect parsing performance?  
- Question: quantify impact of estimating adposition direction on test data—performance with a default or oracle direction?  
- Question: would an oracle PageRank ranking (isolating ranking vs. decoding) notably change UAS, to identify error sources?  
- Minor edits: wording fixes suggested ("we obtain the rank of each word"; "contribution" spelling).  
- Minor issue: inconsistent column order across tables; recommend a consistent ordering for readability.
