- Summary: UDP is a deterministic, unsupervised UD parser that builds a POS-based directed graph, ranks content words with personalized PageRank (verb-biased), and decodes heads in two steps to keep function words as leaves; evaluated on 32 languages and shows strong cross-domain robustness.

- Contribution 1: Large-scale, cross-lingual evaluation demonstrating that simple, universal POS-based head rules plus PageRank work well across typologically diverse languages.

- Contribution 2: Provides a clear, reproducible, parameter-light baseline for unsupervised/low-resource UD parsing combining explicit head rules, graph centrality, and UD-specific decoding constraints.

- Strength 1: Method is very clear and simple, with explicit head rules and transparent algorithmic steps aiding reproducibility.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive evaluation (32 languages, gold vs predicted vs naive POS) offering insight into realistic performance scenarios.

- Strength 3: Important robustness finding: UDP shows lower variance and greater stability under domain shift than a delexicalized transfer parser.

- Strength 4: Strong unsupervised results — competitive with a multi-source delexicalized transfer system despite using no training data.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty — approach closely resembles Søgaard (2012b); personalized PageRank and UD-tailored decoding are incremental rather than paradigm-changing.

- Weakness 2: Questionable evaluation practice — determining adposition head directionality on the test set constitutes tuning on test data and violates standard evaluation protocol.

- Weakness 3: Lack of deeper analysis — no investigation of properties of the constructed graphs (e.g., disconnected components, component sizes) to explain when the method succeeds or fails.

- Weakness 4: Missing oracle experiments — absence of oracle analyses to quantify the performance ceiling for rules, ranking, and proximity heuristics.

- Question 1: How are PageRank ties handled when sorting content words for decoding, and does tie-breaking (e.g., sentence order) affect performance?

- Question 2: Why was adposition directionality estimated on the test set instead of using development sets, and would tuning on dev change results materially?

- Question 3: Please provide statistics on graphs induced by the head rules across languages (frequency of disconnected graphs and typical connected component sizes).
