- Introduces UDP: a training-free UD parser using linguistically-motivated hard-coded rules plus personalized PageRank; parses content words first (with main-predicate boost) then attaches function words as leaves; evaluated across 32 languages and shows competitive accuracy and strong domain robustness.

- Contribution: A simple, effective, completely training-free UD parsing algorithm that sets a strong unsupervised/zero-shot baseline.

- Contribution: Novel use of personalized PageRank to determine content-word attachment order, with a predicate personalization heuristic grounding the parse.

- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that a rule-based, parameter-light parser is more robust to domain shifts than a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline.

- Strength: Paper is very clear and reproducible, with detailed algorithm description and worked examples.

- Strength: The approach is novel and significant, showing strong results from linguistic knowledge rather than large data-driven models.

- Strength: Evaluation is thorough (32 languages, predicted POS experiments) and includes valuable cross-domain analysis.

- Strength: Method is simple and transparent, offering many promising extensions and utility as a platform for future research.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on hand-crafted rules developed on English limits adaptability to language-specific or typologically different constructions.

- Weakness: Limited empirical comparisons — primarily compared to a supervised delexicalized transfer parser and not to established unsupervised parsers like DMV variants.

- Weakness: Some core heuristics are simplistic and brittle (e.g., first-verb predicate identification, fixed function-word attachment directions), causing errors in some languages (e.g., Basque).

- Question: Have you tested developing head rules on typologically different languages, or deriving a minimal rule set automatically from diverse languages, and how does that affect performance?

- Question: Can you provide qualitative analysis of which attachment types (e.g., PPs, adverbials) drive UDP’s superior cross-domain robustness compared to MSD?

- Question: Any concrete ideas for handling coordination or symmetric conjunctions within the PageRank/decoding framework without supervised training?
