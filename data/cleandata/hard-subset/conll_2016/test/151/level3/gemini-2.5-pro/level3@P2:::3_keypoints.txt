- Summary: UDP is a fully unsupervised, POS-only UD parser using POS-based rules, personalized PageRank to find content roots, and a two-step decoding; evaluated on 32 languages and shown competitive and robust to domain shift.
- Contribution 1: Provides an extensive, reproducible unsupervised baseline across 32 languages, evaluated with gold, predicted, and naive POS settings.
- Contribution 2: Shows the rule-based method is significantly more robust to domain shift than a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline (lower variance).
- Contribution 3: Adapts PageRank to UD with personalization for root identification and a two-step decoding enforcing UD leaf constraints for function words.
- Strength 1: Very thorough, wide-ranging evaluation that measures sensitivity to POS quality and provides realistic scenarios.
- Strength 2: Empirical evidence of strong cross-domain stability compared to a data-driven baseline.
- Strength 3: Simple, training-free approach that is language-agnostic and surprisingly competitive (≈6.4% UAS behind transfer baseline with gold POS; wins on Hindi).
- Weakness 1: Core method has limited novelty and closely parallels Søgaard (2012b); lacks direct empirical comparison to that prior work.
- Weakness 2: Personalization choices (e.g., predicate boost weight=5) appear arbitrary and lack sensitivity analysis or justification.
- Weakness 3: Presentation relies heavily on large tables; needs visualizations and reporting of per-language POS accuracies to clarify trends and error propagation.
- Question 1: Why was a personalization weight of 5 chosen for the main predicate, and what is the sensitivity of performance to this hyperparameter?
- Question 2: Can you provide a direct empirical comparison to Søgaard (2012b) on the UD datasets to isolate the gains from your changes?
- Question 3: Please report per-language POS tagging accuracies from the TnT tagger used in the predicted-POS experiments.
- Additional comment: Minor typo at line 207: "The the main idea" should be "The main idea".
