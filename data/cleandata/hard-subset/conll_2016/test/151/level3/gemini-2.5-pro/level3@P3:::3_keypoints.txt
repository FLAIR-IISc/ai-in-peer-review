- Paper introduces UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using only POS tags, linguistically-motivated head rules, and a personalized PageRank to build trees.  
- Method personalizes PageRank by boosting a heuristically-identified main predicate, then decodes in two steps: attach content words by rank, then attach function words as leaves.  
- No training data required; evaluated on 32 languages and shown competitive with a multi-source delexicalized transfer system and more robust to domain shift.  
- Main contribution: extensive empirical evaluation showing a simple rule-based method can be a strong, accessible baseline for UD parsing across many languages.  
- Secondary contribution: cross-domain robustness analysis demonstrating lower variance across domains compared to a data-driven multi-source delexicalized parser.  
- Strength: simplicity and effectiveness — conceptually simple, easy to implement, and within 6.4 UAS points of a strong baseline on average.  
- Strength: comprehensive multilingual evaluation on 32 languages, testing with gold POS, predicted POS, and a naive content/function tagset.  
- Strength: valuable cross-domain analysis showing the rule-based system is more stable and predictable on unseen domains.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — approach heavily builds on Søgaard (2012b); personalization and two-step decoding are incremental improvements.  
- Weakness: personalization strategy under-justified — boosting a single main predicate and fixing weight=5 lacks ablation/sensitivity analysis or principled motivation.  
- Weakness: incomplete error analysis — per-language POS tagging accuracies not reported (only average 94.1%), preventing verification of POS quality impact on UAS drops.  
- Weakness: presentation issues — large tables (e.g., Table 5) impede comparisons; visualizations like bar charts would improve clarity.  
- Question: how was the personalization weight of 5 chosen, was it tuned (e.g., on English dev), and how sensitive is performance to this hyperparameter?  
- Question: did you try alternative personalization strategies (e.g., boosting all verbs or softer weighting schemes across content POS tags)?  
- Question: can you provide per-language POS tagging accuracies for the UDPP setting to analyze POS vs. parsing errors?  
- Minor comment: typo in Section 3.1 (“The the main idea” → “The main idea”).
