- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser combining PageRank with POS-based head rules to parse without training data.  
- Demonstrates competitive parsing accuracy, approaching delexicalized transfer performance and useful for low-resource languages.  
- Evaluates the method across 32 languages and analyzes domain sensitivity, showing strong generalizability and robustness.  
- Adapts dependency head rules to UD POS relations and applies function word restrictions to incorporate linguistic constraints.  
- Achieves strong results vs. a delexicalized transfer baseline (MSD), with an average gap of 6.4% and notably good performance on Hindi.  
- Shows superior robustness to domain shift compared to MSD, with lower UAS variance across domains.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and includes a helpful example run and thorough experimental setup.  
- Uses standard UAS metric and explores performance under gold, predicted, and naive POS tagging scenarios for nuanced evaluation.  
- Releases the parser and annotated test sets to support reproducibility and future research.  
- Limited novelty: approach largely builds on Søgaard (2012), with personalized PageRank and two-step decoding as incremental extensions.  
- Problematic tuning: head directionality parameter was tuned on the test set, risking data contamination.  
- Method omits how PageRank ties are broken during decoding, which could affect performance and reproducibility.  
- Lacks oracle ablation results for constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) to quantify each component’s impact.  
- Asks authors how PageRank ties are resolved and whether tie-breaking affects performance.  
- Requests a detailed analysis of frequencies and component-size distribution for disconnected graphs in PageRank construction.  
- Suggests re-tuning head directionality on a separate development set to avoid contamination concerns.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “we obtain rank” to “we obtain the rank”; reorder Table 5 columns to match Table 7; fix “isolate the of both” to “isolate the contribution of both”.
