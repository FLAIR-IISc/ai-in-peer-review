- Presents UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using POS-based head rules + personalized PageRank and deterministic decoding without training data.
- Core idea: build a rule-based dependency graph, rank content words with personalized PageRank, assign each word to its closest allowable head.
- Primary contribution: surprisingly effective entirely unsupervised parsing that is competitive without any training data.
- Secondary contribution: novel adaptation of personalized PageRank to prioritize predicates in parsing.
- Empirical contribution: thorough evaluation across 32 languages and domain-adaptation experiments; code/data released.
- Strength: often outperforms a strong delexicalized transfer baseline despite no training.
- Strength: notable robustness to domain shift, indicating real-world applicability.
- Strength: paper is well-written with a detailed example run that aids understanding.
- Weakness: method builds heavily on prior work (Søgaard 2012b); core approach (PageRank + head rules) is not entirely novel.
- Weakness: adposition directionality estimated on test data risks data contamination and questions about generalizability to unseen languages.
- Weakness: reliance on UD formalism may make performance sensitive to UD annotation quality and consistency across languages.
- Request: provide detailed analysis of head-rule coverage — frequency of non-connecting rules and sizes of disconnected components.
- Request: clarify tie-breaking strategies used in PageRank ranking and dependency assignment.
- Request: report oracle results isolating contributions of head rules, directionality, and distance (performance if each constraint were perfect).
- Request: provide computational complexity analysis, especially PageRank cost for longer/larger sentences.
- Minor edit: change "we obtain the rank" to "we obtain the ranks" in Section 4.1.
- Minor edit: make Table 5 column order consistent with the preferable order in Table 7.
- Minor edit: fix typo in Section 6.1 from “constribution” to “contributions.”
