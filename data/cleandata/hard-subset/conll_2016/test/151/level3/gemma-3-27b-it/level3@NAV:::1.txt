## Review: Parsing for Universal Dependencies without training

This paper presents UDP, a novel unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm leveraging POS-based head rules and a biased PageRank approach. The core idea – utilizing the constraints inherent in Universal Dependencies (UD) to guide parsing without training – is promising and the paper clearly articulates the motivation and methodology. The evaluation across a substantial number of languages (32) is a significant undertaking and a strength of the submission.

The primary contribution is a competitive, entirely unsupervised parsing system that requires no training data and demonstrates robustness to domain shift, outperforming a delexicalized transfer system on certain languages. A secondary, though valuable, contribution is the detailed examination of how to effectively integrate UD constraints (function word placement, POS-based rules) into a graph-based parsing framework. Finally, the comprehensive evaluation across a large, diverse language set allows for a broad assessment of the method’s generalizability.

However, the novelty of the approach feels incremental. As the authors acknowledge, the work builds heavily on Søgaard (2012b), and the advancements – personalization of PageRank and two-step decoding – appear as refinements rather than fundamental departures. The system’s performance, while competitive, doesn't represent a significant leap beyond existing techniques. 

Several points require clarification. The authors should analyze the frequency of disconnected graphs encountered during PageRank application and provide statistics on component sizes. This would help understand the impact of the head rule limitations. The tuning of head directionality on the test set is a concern; while the authors state convergence is rapid, a more principled approach (using a dedicated development set) is preferable. Clarification on how PageRank ties are handled is also necessary – do ties impact performance, and if so, how? Finally, reporting oracle results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) would provide a useful upper-bound estimate of the potential gains achievable with perfect information. 

**Minor Editorial Suggestions:**

*   4.1: Change "we obtain [the] rank" to "we obtain the rank".
*   Table 5: Reorder columns to match Table 7.
*   6.1: Change "isolate the [contribution] of both" to "isolate the contribution of both".

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide statistics on the frequency of disconnected graphs and the sizes of their components?
2.  Why was head directionality tuned on the test set rather than a development set? Would a different approach yield significantly different results?
3.  How are ties handled during PageRank ranking? Does the tie-breaking method affect parsing performance?
4.  Could you report oracle results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to provide an upper-bound estimate of the potential performance gains?
