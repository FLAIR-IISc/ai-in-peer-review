- Paper introduces UDP, a fully unsupervised UD parser using PageRank and UD-specific head rules that requires no training data.
- Core contribution: a surprisingly effective unsupervised parser that attains competitive accuracy without supervised or cross-lingual training.
- Uses PageRank on a dependency graph plus UD-informed rules to estimate head selection and word relevance.
- Demonstrates robustness to domain shift, showing less degradation across genres compared to delexicalized transfer parsers.
- Evaluated comprehensively across 32 languages, with experiments using both gold and predicted POS tags.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough methodological explanation and illustrative examples.
- Authors release parser and datasets, supporting reproducibility and further research.
- Dependence on POS tagging accuracy: performance drops with predicted POS tags, indicating sensitivity to POS errors.
- Limited handling of complex phenomena (coordination, long-distance dependencies) due to a small set of hard-coded rules.
- Some rule engineering and minor language-specific choices (e.g., adposition direction) introduce potential bias/error.
- PageRank teleport/probability parameters tuned on English dev set; generalizability of these hyperparameters to other languages is unclear.
- Question: Which linguistic constructions are consistently misparsed due to incomplete head rules in Table 1?
- Question: What experiments determined the 5x personalization weight for the estimated main predicate, and how sensitive is performance to it?
- Question: Why does UDP show greater stability across domains—simpler rules, avoidance of data-driven biases, or other causes?
- Question: Why is UDP performance notably low on Bulgarian bulletin data—domain, language specifics, or other factors?
- Suggestion: Include a detailed error analysis with concrete misparsed examples to pinpoint common failure modes.
- Suggestion: Compare UDP against other state-of-the-art unsupervised parsing methods beyond delexicalized transfer.
