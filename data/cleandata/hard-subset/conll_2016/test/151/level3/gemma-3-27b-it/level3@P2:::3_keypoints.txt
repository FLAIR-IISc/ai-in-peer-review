- Presents UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank plus dependency head rules to parse without training data.  
- Claims contributions: adapting head rules to UD, UD function-word constraint, personalized PageRank for predicate ID, and estimating adposition attachment direction on test data.  
- Strong empirical result: surprisingly strong, linguistically motivated baseline across 32 languages, often beating delexicalized transfer.  
- Thorough experimental setup: wide multilingual and cross-domain evaluation and analysis with predicted POS tags.  
- Personalized PageRank for main predicate identification is a useful, effective adaptation of prior PageRank-based methods.  
- Estimating adposition attachment direction on test data is a practical robustness improvement.  
- Limited novelty: method builds heavily on prior work (Søgaard 2012) and improvements are incremental.  
- Personalization appears ad-hoc: weight of 5 lacks justification and scope (only main predicate) is unexplained.  
- Major presentation weakness: no visualizations (charts/plots) to make multilingual/domain results easy to interpret.  
- Missing analysis: no POS tagging scores reported, so parsing degradation with predicted POS cannot be correlated to tagging errors.  
- Overstated claims: “completely free of language-specific parameters” is misleading because head-direction rules encode language-specific knowledge.  
- Requested clarifications: justify personalization weight choice and alternatives, consider personalizing other content words, provide POS tag P/R/F1 for predicted-mode, and show example dependency trees (successes/failures).  
- Minor/Edit suggestions: fix typo at Line 207 and add a more nuanced limitations discussion for complex languages and ambiguous POS tagging.  
- Suggestion: include comparison to more recent/sophisticated cross-lingual parsers to better contextualize performance.
