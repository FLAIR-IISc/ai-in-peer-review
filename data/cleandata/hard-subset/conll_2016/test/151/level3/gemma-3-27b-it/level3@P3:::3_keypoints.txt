- Paper proposes UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using POS constraints and personalized PageRank, no training data, evaluated on 32 languages.  
- Claims competitive performance with delexicalized transfer systems and notable stability across domain shifts.  
- Contribution: a practical implementation showing UD constraints can drive unsupervised parsing and serve as a baseline.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration that UDP is robust to domain change compared to a supervised transfer baseline.  
- Contribution: use of personalized PageRank biased toward the likely predicate to improve predicate identification.  
- Strength: achieves competitive parsing accuracy for an unsupervised method.  
- Strength: strong robustness to domain change, with convincing results (e.g., Table 6).  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across 32 languages and multiple POS scenarios (gold vs predicted).  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and detailed method description.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — approach closely mirrors Søgaard (2012b) and extensions feel minor.  
- Weakness: personalization is under-explored; applying it only to a single element and using weight 5 lacks justification.  
- Weakness: results are presented mainly in tables; missing visualizations that would aid interpretation.  
- Weakness: missing analysis of correlation between POS tagging errors and parsing degradation when using predicted POS.  
- Weakness: minor typo at line 207 ("The the main idea").  
- Question: why was a personalization weight of 5 chosen and were other weights tested?  
- Question: what happens if personalization targets multiple candidate predicates (e.g., top-N verbs)?  
- Question: will the authors add cross-language/per-language visualizations comparing UDP and the baseline?  
- Question: have the authors analyzed the correlation between POS tagging accuracy and parsing accuracy with predicted POS?  
- Additional: references are extensive and well-curated.  
- Additional: parser and datasets are made available, which is commendable.
