- Proposes UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank plus hard-coded UD rules, achieving competitive performance without training and strong domain robustness.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear explanations of design choices like PageRank personalization and two-step decoding.  
- Addresses cross-lingual dependency parsing and domain adaptation by ranking content words with PageRank and applying UD-based dependency rules in two stages (content then function words).  
- Contribution: a competitive, truly unsupervised UD parser matching delexicalized transfer systems without training, useful for low-resource languages.  
- Contribution: effective integration of UD constraints (function word restrictions, POS tags) is central to UDP’s success.  
- Contribution: demonstrates robustness to domain shift, outperforming delexicalized transfer in cross-domain settings.  
- Strength: rigorous evaluation against a strong baseline across 32 languages with domain adaptation analysis using standard UAS metrics.  
- Strength: clear and detailed explanations, including an informative figure illustrating the decoding algorithm.  
- Strength: well-motivated design choices leveraging UD framework and personalized PageRank.  
- Strength: practical impact—parser and annotated test sets released for reproducibility.  
- Strength: insightful error analysis by POS and domain, highlighting specific weaknesses like PUNCT tagging.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in core components—PageRank and rule-based parsing are not individually novel; novelty lies in their integration.  
- Weakness: strong dependence on POS tagging quality; performance drops substantially with predicted POS tags.  
- Weakness: simplistic handling of complex structures (e.g., relative clauses), limiting applicability to complex sentences.  
- Weakness: unclear parameter sensitivity beyond teleport probability; needs more detailed sensitivity analysis.  
- Question: what is the computational cost of running PageRank on long sentences and are there practical sentence length limits?  
- Question: can UDP be adapted for languages without word forms (e.g., Japanese) via subword or character-based tokenization?  
- Question: are there plans and expected challenges to expand rule set to handle relative clauses?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the PageRank personalization weighting (factor of 5) and how was it chosen?  
- Additional comment: include discussion of limitations of the UD framework and how they affect UDP.  
- Additional comment: compare UDP with other recent unsupervised parsing approaches beyond the delexicalized transfer baseline.
