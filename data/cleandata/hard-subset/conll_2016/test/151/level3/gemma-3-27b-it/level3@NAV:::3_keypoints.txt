- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using POS tags, dependency head rules, and a modified PageRank for cross-lingual parsing.  
- Core idea is interesting but novelty is limited, largely mirroring Søgaard (2012).  
- Authors claim contributions (UD rule adaptation, function-word restrictions, personalized PageRank, adposition direction estimation), but main novelty is showing competitive performance without training.  
- UDP achieves competitive UAS, rivaling a delexicalized transfer parser despite no training.  
- System shows cross-lingual robustness across diverse languages, supporting the UD formalism.  
- Method is linguistically motivated and grounded in UD constraints.  
- Personalization boosting of the main predicate is useful but under-explored; the chosen weight of 5 is arbitrary and unjustified.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive by language but overly reliant on tables; visualizations (charts) are needed for clarity.  
- Missing POS tagging scores in prediction mode—reporting them would clarify error propagation effects on parsing.  
- Overall contribution is incremental rather than a substantial methodological or performance advance over prior work.  
- Minor typo to fix: "The the main idea" on line 207.  
- Questions for authors: rationale and tuning for personalization weight 5, request for visual result representations, and what is the parser's computational complexity?
