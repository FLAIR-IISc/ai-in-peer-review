- Paper introduces UDP: an unsupervised dependency parser using personalized PageRank plus linguistically motivated POS-based head rules, deterministic and training-free, evaluated on 32 languages.
- Main contribution: a practical, effective unsupervised parser grounded in Universal Dependencies that matches delexicalized transfer performance without training data.
- Secondary contribution: demonstrated robustness to domain shifts, making it useful for cross-lingual/domain parsing.
- Extensive empirical evaluation and ablation studies across many languages provide solid evidence and insight into component contributions.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results: competitive UAS scores and convincing cross-domain robustness against supervised baselines.
- Linguistic grounding: explicit UD constraints and head rules reduce spurious correlations and improve handling of function words and head direction.
- Comprehensive evaluation: comparisons to strong baselines, detailed ablations, and POS/domain analyses.
- Clear presentation: well-written exposition and a helpful detailed example.
- Resources released: parser and annotated datasets made publicly available for reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: approach largely builds on prior work (e.g., Søgaard 2012b) with incremental improvements rather than a fundamentally new method.
- Test-set driven parameter tuning: head direction estimation performed on test data risks overfitting and inflating reported performance.
- Tie-breaking in PageRank unspecified: no explanation of how equal ranks are resolved, which can affect determinism and final parses.
- Lack of oracle studies: no results showing upper bounds if individual constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) were perfect.

Questions for authors:
- What tie-breaking mechanism is used for PageRank rank ties, and how sensitive is performance to that choice?
- What percentage of sentences produce disconnected graphs under the head rules, and what are typical connected component sizes?
- Can you provide distributions and frequencies of the estimated adposition directions across languages?

Additional minor comments:
- Improve phrasing: change “we obtain the rank” to “we obtain the ranking” in Section 4.1.
- Reorder Table 5 and 7 columns for consistency with Table 7’s preferred order (BLG, UDPG, MSDG, UDPP, MSDP).
- Edit Section 6.1 wording: replace “isolate the contribution of both” with “isolate the contributions of each.”
