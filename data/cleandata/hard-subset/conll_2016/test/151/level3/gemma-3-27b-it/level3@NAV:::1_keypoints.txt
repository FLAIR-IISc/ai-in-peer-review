- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using POS-based head rules and a biased PageRank; idea and methodology are clearly presented.
- Evaluation over 32 languages is extensive and a major strength.
- Main contribution: a competitive, training-free parser robust to domain shift and sometimes outperforming delexicalized transfer.
- Secondary contribution: detailed integration of UD constraints (function-word placement, POS rules) into a graph-based parsing framework.
- Comprehensive multilingual evaluation supports claims of generalizability.
- Novelty is incremental; approach builds heavily on Søgaard (2012b) and proposed changes (personalized PageRank, two-step decoding) are refinements rather than fundamental innovations.
- System performance is competitive but not a substantial improvement over existing methods.
- Request for analysis/statistics on frequency of disconnected graphs and sizes of PageRank components to assess head-rule limitations.
- Concern that head directionality was tuned on the test set; recommend tuning on a development set and justification of current choice.
- Need clarification on how PageRank ties are broken and whether tie-breaking affects parsing performance.
- Ask for oracle results per constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to provide upper-bound performance estimates.
- Minor editorial fixes: wording in section 4.1 and 6.1, and reorder columns of Table 5 to match Table 7.
