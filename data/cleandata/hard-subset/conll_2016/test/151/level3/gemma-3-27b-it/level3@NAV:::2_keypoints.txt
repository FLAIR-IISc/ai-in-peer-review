1. Proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD parser that requires no training data and uses PageRank on dependency graphs constrained by UD rules.  
2. Core idea—combining linguistically-informed UD constraints with graph-based ranking—is well-executed and clearly explained.  
3. PageRank personalization is used effectively to identify main predicates; adposition attachment direction is estimated directly on test data.  
4. Demonstrates competitive performance versus a delexicalized transfer baseline.  
5. Shows greater robustness to domain shift compared to the transfer system.  
6. Experimental setup is strong: many languages evaluated, clear decoding example, and thorough explanation.  
7. Error analysis is insightful, especially the discussion of domain robustness.  
8. Primary weakness: main evaluation relies on gold POS tags; performance drops substantially with predicted POS.  
9. Suggests need for methods to mitigate POS sensitivity (e.g., incorporate POS info/confidence into PageRank or decoding).  
10. Comparison limited to delexicalized transfer — lacking evaluation against more recent/sophisticated transfer approaches.  
11. Limited exploration of error types beyond domain shift; broader error analysis would strengthen the paper.  
12. Questions: whether alternate PageRank personalization strategies were tried, if POS-confidence can be integrated, and the impact of excluding the NOUN->VERB head rule (and whether weighted inclusion could help).
