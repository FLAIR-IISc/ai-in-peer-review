- Main contribution: UDP is an unsupervised dependency parser using PageRank and UD head rules that achieves competitive parsing without any training data and shows domain robustness.
- Secondary contribution: Effective adaptation of dependency head rules to the UD framework, incorporating UD restrictions on function words.
- Evaluation: Thorough, multilingual experimental evaluation across 32 languages with detailed domain-shift analysis.
- Novelty: Demonstrates strong parsing performance without data-driven training, important for low-resource scenarios.
- Empirical strength: Competitive UAS versus a delexicalized transfer system and outperforms supervised baselines on some languages (e.g., Hindi).
- Robustness: Greater resilience to domain change compared to delexicalized transfer approaches.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear methodology, intuition (PageRank personalization), and a step-by-step example.
- Comprehensive comparisons: Includes relevant baselines, analyses for gold vs. predicted POS, automatic metrics, and some error analysis.
- Weakness — error analysis: Current error analysis is limited; deeper examination of failure cases and structural errors is needed.
- Weakness — POS sensitivity: Performance degrades significantly with predicted POS tags; mitigation strategies should be investigated.
- Weakness — rule engineering: Initial head rules were tuned on English; selection/tuning process and sensitivity across languages need clearer description.
- Weakness — scalability: PageRank computation may be expensive; practical scalability to very long sentences or large datasets is not fully addressed.
- Question: Provide a more detailed breakdown of specific error types and underlying causes.
- Question: Have you tried mitigating POS-tag errors (e.g., POS confidence scores or more robust taggers)?
- Question: Elaborate on how initial head rules were selected/tuned and how sensitive performance is to rule variations.
- Question: What is the computational cost of PageRank for long sentences (>100 tokens) and are optimizations planned?
- Additional: Ensure the provided dataset link is complete and not redacted.
- Additional: Expand discussion of related work to include recent unsupervised parsing advances.
- Additional: Note potential limitations of the UD framework that could affect parser performance.
- Overall: Well-executed, impactful paper that merits acceptance subject to addressing the minor weaknesses above.
