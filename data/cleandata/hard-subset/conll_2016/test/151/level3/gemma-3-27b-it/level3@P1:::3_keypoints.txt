- Presents UDP: an unsupervised dependency parser using PageRank and UD head rules that requires no training data.  
- Claimed contributions: adapting head rules to UD, function-word restrictions, personalized PageRank for predicate ID, and language-agnostic adposition attachment.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates a surprisingly strong unsupervised parser within UD that approaches delexicalized transfer parser performance.  
- Secondary contribution: systematic incorporation of UD constraints into parsing rules via tailored head rules.  
- Evaluation: broad empirical assessment across 32 languages using existing treebanks.  
- Strength: shows resilience to domain shift, performing more robustly than a supervised baseline across genres.  
- Strength: simple, interpretable, and adaptable approach relying on a small set of rules and a clear algorithm.  
- Strength: comprehensive multilingual evaluation and appropriate positioning relative to prior work.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—method builds on prior work (e.g., Søgaard 2012) and improvements (notably personalization) feel incremental.  
- Weakness: PageRank personalization (weight=5) is arbitrary and lacks sensitivity analysis or ablation to justify its impact.  
- Weakness: insufficient error analysis—needs more granular investigation into common parsing errors (beyond punctuation and coordination).  
- Weakness: evaluation presentation could be improved with visualizations and reporting POS-tagging accuracy with predicted tags to explain parsing degradation.  
- Specific methodological concern: adposition direction estimation is simplistic and could be enhanced with more sophisticated methods.  
- Potential bias: initial development and parameter tuning on English may introduce language bias.  
- Requests to authors / misc.: justify personalization weight and alternatives, provide detailed error analysis, report POS tagging accuracy with predicted tags, fix typo, add charts, and discuss head-rule limits for complex syntax (coordination, relative clauses).
