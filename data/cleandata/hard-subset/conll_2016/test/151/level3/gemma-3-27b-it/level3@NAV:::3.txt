## Review of "Parsing for Universal Dependencies without training"

This paper presents UDP, an unsupervised parser for Universal Dependencies (UD) leveraging PageRank and dependency head rules, aiming for a linguistically sound and robust cross-lingual parsing solution. The core idea of using POS tags and a modified PageRank to establish dependencies without training is interesting, but its novelty is limited, closely mirroring the approach by Søgaard (2012). While the authors claim several contributions – adapting rules to UD, incorporating function word restrictions, personalized PageRank, and language-agnostic adposition direction estimation – the most significant contribution is arguably the demonstration of competitive performance *without* training, achieving results comparable to a delexicalized transfer system. 

The personalization boosting of the main predicate is a useful addition, but it feels under-explored. The chosen weight of 5 is presented without justification and appears arbitrary; a more thorough investigation of this parameter's impact would strengthen the work. Furthermore, the experimental evaluation, while comprehensive across languages, suffers from a reliance on tables. Visualizations (e.g., charts comparing performance across different conditions) would significantly aid interpretation and highlight key findings.  

A critical addition to the evaluation would be reporting POS tagging scores in prediction mode. This would allow assessment of whether any degradation in parsing performance correlates with potential errors introduced by the POS tagger, offering insight into the system’s robustness. The paper mentions error propagation, but directly demonstrating this relationship would be more convincing. 

Overall, while the system is well-designed and achieves solid results, the contribution over Søgaard (2012) feels incremental. The method builds upon existing work without a substantial leap forward in methodology or performance. The minor typo on line 207 ("The the main idea") should be addressed.

**Strengths:**

*   **Competitive Performance:** The parser achieves respectable UAS scores, rivaling a delexicalized transfer parser without requiring training data.
*   **Cross-Lingual Robustness:** The system demonstrates relative stability across a diverse set of languages, indicating the efficacy of the UD formalism.
*   **Linguistic Motivation:** The grounding in UD constraints provides a sound foundation for the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty:** The core approach closely resembles Søgaard (2012) with incremental improvements.
*   **Arbitrary Parameter Selection:** The personalization weight of 5 lacks justification and may impact performance.
*   **Evaluation Presentation:** The exclusive use of tables hinders effective interpretation of results.
*   **Missing POS Tagging Evaluation:** Lack of POS tagging performance metrics prevents assessment of error propagation.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  What was the rationale behind choosing the personalization weight of 5? Was any parameter tuning performed, and if so, what were the results?
2.  Could you provide a visual representation of the results (e.g., bar charts) to facilitate a clearer understanding of the performance differences across languages and evaluation setups?
3.  What is the computational complexity of the parser?
