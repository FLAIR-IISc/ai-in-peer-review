- Paper presents UDP: a training-free UD parser using only POS tags and a small set of UD-consistent head rules.
- Method ranks content words with personalized PageRank over a POS-rule graph, then decodes in two steps: attach content words (single root) then function words (leafness).
- ADP attachment direction is estimated at test time from ADP–NOMINAL bigrams.
- Evaluated on 32 UD languages and multiple domain splits, compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser (MSD).

- Contribution: broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing a simple, UD-constrained, training-free parser can be competitive and more domain-robust than delexicalized transfer.
- Contribution: practical, language-agnostic design using UD-tailored head rules, two-step decoding enforcing function-word leafness, and test-time ADP direction estimation.
- Contribution: modest methodological tweak over prior work (Søgaard 2012b) by using personalized PageRank to bias root and content-word attachment order.

- Strength: strong empirical breadth and robustness — UDP with gold POS is within ~6.4 UAS of MSDG on average and shows lower domain-wise UAS variance.
- Strength: with predicted POS UDP degrades ~2.2 UAS vs. ~2.7 for MSD and shows lower error propagation (0.37 vs. 0.46).
- Strength: minimal supervision and simplicity — only POS tags required, no parallel data or source models; easy to port.
- Strength: clear ablation evidence — personalized PR adds ~9 UAS and decoding/rules add ~4 UAS over baseline.
- Strength: practical baseline for low-resource scenarios; outperforms adjacency baselines even with crude content/function tagging.

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty — approach closely mirrors Søgaard (2012b) with modest UD-specific adjustments.
- Weakness: personalization heuristic (boosting first verb/content word by factor 5) is ad hoc and under-explored; no sensitivity analysis or alternative schemes.
- Weakness: evaluation presentation lacks visual summaries; missing per-language POS scores and correlation analyses between POS accuracy and UAS.
- Weakness: baseline coverage incomplete — DMV omitted and no direct UD adaptation of Søgaard for precise calibration of incremental gains.
- Weakness: cross-linguistic rule choices tuned on English dev (fixed directions for AUX/DET/SCONJ/CONJ) introduce English bias and fail in languages like Basque.

- Rebuttal request: provide sensitivity analysis for personalization weights (e.g., {1,2,3,5,10}) and alternatives (seed all verbs, estimate priors from data).
- Rebuttal request: run a UD adaptation of Søgaard (2012b) and add UDP components incrementally to quantify contributions of each change.
- Rebuttal request: report per-language TnT POS accuracies in the predicted POS setting and correlations between POS accuracy and UAS degradation; identify dominant POS confusions.
- Rebuttal request: clarify graph construction and runtime — multi-edge weighting, frequency of disconnected PR graphs, effect of teleport probability (0.05), average runtime per sentence and PR convergence iterations.
- Rebuttal request: explore estimating directions for AUX/DET/SCONJ/CONJ at test time (beyond ADP) and report effects on languages like Basque.

- Additional: fix minor typo (“The the main idea” → “The main idea”).
- Additional: add visualizations (ablation bar plots, per-language deltas vs. MSD, domain-wise UAS boxplots).
- Additional: report how often back-off (dropping δ and/or κ) is invoked and its impact on non-projectivity.
- Additional: consider reporting labeled attachment scores or discuss feasibility of adding UD labels.
