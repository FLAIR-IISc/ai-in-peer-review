- Paper presents UDP: a deterministic, training-free UD parser using POS head rules, personalized PageRank over allowed edges, and two-phase decoding enforcing function-word headedness.
- Evaluation covers 32 UD languages, gold/predicted POS, and multiple domain splits, showing competitive UAS and robustness to domain shift and POS errors.
- Main contributions: thorough multilingual/cross-domain evaluation establishing a strong training-free baseline.
- Main contributions: a deterministic unsupervised algorithm combining POS head rules, PR ranking, and two-step decoding.
- Main contributions: empirical finding that test-time adposition directionality estimation converges quickly and is robust across languages.
- Strength: Broad, robust evaluation with low variance across domains and reduced error propagation from POS to dependencies.
- Strength: Simple, deterministic, fast, and reproducible method valuable as a community baseline, especially for low-resource settings.
- Strength: Competitive performance without training—within ~6–7 UAS of delexicalized transfer and beating it on some languages (e.g., Hindi).
- Strength: Clear ablation studies quantifying PR’s contribution (~+9 UAS) and two-step decoding gains (~+4 UAS).
- Strength: Clear presentation with worked examples and alignment to UD design constraints.
- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to Søgaard (2012); personalization and two-step decoding are incremental improvements.
- Weakness: Potential test-set tuning/data snooping by estimating adposition directionality from the test set, raising fairness concerns.
- Weakness: Missing diagnostics on PR graph structure—no stats on disconnected graphs, component sizes, or correlation with UAS.
- Weakness: Missing reporting on decoding back-offs—frequency and impact of dropping constraints (δ, κ) are not provided.
- Weakness: Tie-breaking policy is unspecified for equal PR scores or equidistant head candidates, threatening determinism and reproducibility.
- Weakness: No per-constraint oracle upper bounds to attribute errors to head-rule coverage, directionality constraints, or distance minimization.
- Question: What fraction of sentences yield disconnected PR graphs, component size distributions, correlation with UAS, and sensitivity to teleport prob 0.05?
- Question: How often are δ and/or κ dropped during decoding across languages, and what are UAS with/without back-offs?
- Question: What tie-breaking rules are used for PR ties and equidistant heads, and do alternatives change UAS?
- Question: Can directionality be estimated from train/dev or raw unlabeled text instead of test data, and how quickly do estimates stabilize per language?
- Question: Please report oracle UAS for (a) perfect ADP directionality, (b) perfect head-rule coverage, (c) ignoring distance minimization, and (d) oracle PR ranking.
- Question: How sensitive are results to perturbations of the head-rule inventory; do low-performing languages need language-specific rule tweaks?
- Additional comment: Add corpus statistics on disconnected graphs, head-rule coverage, back-off usage rates, and per-language ADP directionality vs. gold.
- Additional comment: Re-estimate global decisions from train/dev (or cross-validate) to avoid test-time peeking and improve experimental hygiene.
- Additional comment: Consider estimating directionality for DET/AUX/SCONJ per language rather than fixing from English dev; Basque DET is a counterexample.
- Minor edits: reorder table columns for consistency, fix typos (“The the main idea”, “we obtain rank”, “constribution”, “adpositon”), and other wording fixes.
