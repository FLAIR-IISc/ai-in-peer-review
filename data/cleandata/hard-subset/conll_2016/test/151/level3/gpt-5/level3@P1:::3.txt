Summary and Contributions
The paper proposes UDP, a training-free parser for Universal Dependencies that relies solely on POS tags, a small set of UD-inspired head rules, and a personalized PageRank (PR) scheme to prioritize likely main predicates and guide a two-step decoding that enforces function-word leafness. It also estimates adposition attachment direction on-the-fly from test sentences via simple bigram statistics. The system is evaluated on 32 languages in UD v1.2, under gold and predicted POS, and in multiple domains, and is compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline (MSD).

As I see them, the main contributions are:
1) A simple, robust, unsupervised UD parser that combines UD-conformant head rules, content/function separation with leaf constraints, and personalized PR for root identification and ranking of content words, delivering competitive UAS to delexicalized transfer while showing better domain robustness.
2) A lightweight, data-free estimation of adposition directionality at test time, which works reliably across languages.
3) A cross-domain evaluation showing lower variance than a delex transfer baseline and an analysis of error propagation from POS to dependencies.

Strengths
- Strong cross-lingual and cross-domain robustness with minimal resources: UDP achieves UAS close to MSD with gold POS (average gap 6.4%) and even outperforms MSD on Hindi. Importantly, UDP exhibits consistently lower standard deviation across domains (Table 6), suggesting reduced sensitivity to genre and sampling bias. This is valuable for low-resource and out-of-domain scenarios where transfer methods often degrade.
- Simple, linguistically grounded design aligned with UD: The two-step decoding that first attaches content words and then function words enforces UD’s function-word leafness and produces well-formed trees. The ADP-direction estimation is elegant and effective (ADP UAS ≈ 75% on average), avoiding language-specific parameters.
- Competitive performance under predicted POS and favorable error propagation: With TnT-tagged POS (≈94.1% average accuracy), UDP’s UAS drops only ~2.2 points versus gold, compared to ~2.7 for MSD; the estimated error propagation (0.37 for UDP vs. 0.46 for MSD) supports the claim that UDP is less brittle to POS noise.
- Clear ablation showing PR’s impact: The UDP vs. UDPNoPR comparison indicates PR contributes ~9 UAS points on average, while decoding/tree constraints add ~4 points over the rule baseline. This is a convincing demonstration that the ranking is essential.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to Søgaard (2012): The core idea—PR-based ranking for unsupervised parsing—is very close to prior work. The main additions are UD-specific constraints (function-word leafness via two-step decoding), a simple ADP direction heuristic, and personalization to the presumed main predicate. While these are practical and useful, they constitute modest methodological advancement.
- Personalization design is under-explored and under-justified: The choice to boost only the first verb (or first content word) by a fixed weight of 5 appears ad hoc. There is no sensitivity analysis over the weight, no comparison to alternative personalization schemes (e.g., distributing mass across all verbs, using a learned or data-driven prior, or exploiting morphological cues), nor an assessment across typologically diverse languages (e.g., verb-final, pro-drop).
- Missing or sparse analyses that would strengthen claims: 
  - POS impact: While a single average POS accuracy is reported, per-language POS tagging scores and correlation with UAS degradation are not presented. This would clarify whether UDP’s robustness under predicted POS is consistent cross-lingually.
  - Visualization and interpretability: The results are table-heavy; trends (e.g., per-language deltas vs. MSD, domain variances) are difficult to digest without plots.
  - Graph-building choices: The PR graph relies on hand-selected head rules. A deeper study (e.g., alternative edge sets, including/excluding function words, or edge reification) could illuminate when and why PR helps, beyond the single ablation.
- Coverage limitations and structural biases: The system does not handle some key phenomena (e.g., coordination scope, relative clauses due to excluding NOUN→VERB as heads). These design choices likely cap upper-bound performance and may explain low UAS on PUNCT and CONJ. While acknowledged as future work, the current evaluation would benefit from quantifying these error types.
- Minor presentation issues: A typo at line 207 (“The the main idea”) and occasional phrasing that could be tightened. Some methodological hyperparameters (teleport probability, personalization weight) lack principled justification.

Questions to Authors
- Personalization:
  - Why weight = 5? Please provide a sensitivity analysis across weights and languages. Does distributing personalization mass over multiple verb candidates (e.g., all verbs in the sentence) change performance, especially in verb-final languages?
  - How often is the “first verb” not the actual root? Can personalization be conditioned on simple morpho-syntactic cues (e.g., AUX vs. VERB distinctions) to improve root accuracy?
- PR graph construction:
  - Are function words included as nodes in the PR graph, and if so, how do they impact centrality? Did you try excluding them entirely from graph construction, given leafness constraints?
  - Could edge reification or weighting by linear distance improve the ranking, and did you experiment with this?
- POS effects:
  - Please report per-language POS tagging accuracies and the correlation between POS accuracy and UAS delta (gold→predicted) for both UDP and MSD.
  - How sensitive is UDP to coarse-to-fine POS mappings (e.g., UD UPOS only vs. language-specific features)?
- Hyperparameters and efficiency:
  - The teleport probability is fixed at 0.05. How sensitive are results to this choice? Any runtime and convergence statistics by sentence length?
- Error analysis:
  - Can you quantify errors due to coordination and relative clauses, to validate the proposed future work prioritization?

Additional Comments
- Consider adding summary plots (e.g., per-language UAS deltas vs. MSD, domain-wise variance bars) to make the cross-lingual and cross-domain stories easier to interpret at a glance.
- Please fix the typo at l.207 (“The the main idea”).
- It would help to release per-language hyperparameter-free scripts and the domain split metadata referenced, to facilitate reproducibility.
