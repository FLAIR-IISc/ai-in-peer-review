Summary and Contributions
This paper proposes an unsupervised UD parser that relies only on POS tags and a PageRank-based ranking over a rule-licensed graph, with (i) downweighting of function words via two-step decoding and (ii) personalization to boost the main predicate. It also estimates adposition attachment direction from test data. As contributions, I see:
1) A careful reimplementation and small extension of Søgaard (2012)-style PR parsing to the UD setting, including POS-only inputs and function-word handling (incremental).
2) A broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing competitive UAS against delexicalized transfer and improved robustness to domain shift (empirical contribution).
3) A simple heuristic for estimating ADP directionality from test data.

Strengths
- Strong cross-lingual scope and robustness: Evaluations on 32 languages and multiple domains demonstrate stability to domain shifts and surprisingly competitive UAS for a training-free method, which is valuable as a baseline and for low-resource settings.
- Simplicity and transparency: The method is easy to reproduce and interpret (POS-only, explicit rules, deterministic decoding), making error analysis and deployment straightforward.
- Practical exploitation of UD constraints: Enforcing function words as leaves and coupling PR ranking with UD-compliant head rules provides a linguistically grounded, language-agnostic baseline that often narrows the gap to delex transfer.
- Personalization idea is useful: Boosting the main predicate in PR improves root identification and convergence speed.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: The core idea and PR setup closely mirror Søgaard (2012); the extensions (UD-specific rules, function-word leafness, ADP direction heuristic, main-predicate personalization) are incremental.
- Under-explored personalization: Personalization is only applied to the main predicate; no ablations or extensions (e.g., propagating personalization to other content heads) are explored, leaving performance potential untapped.
- Arbitrary hyperparameter: The personalization weight of 5 is not justified or tuned systematically; no sensitivity analysis is provided.
- Presentation limits interpretability: Heavy reliance on large tables; key takeaways (e.g., error propagation, domain robustness) would benefit from visualizations (scatter plots, bar charts with error bars).
- Missing POS-reporting details: Predicted-POS results lack per-language POS tagger accuracies; correlation plots between POS accuracy and UAS degradation are needed to substantiate claims.

Questions to Authors
- Precisely delineate novelty over Søgaard (2012): what fails if we remove each of your additions (UD rules, two-step decoding, stop-word downweighting, personalization)?
- How sensitive are results to the personalization weight and to which token is boosted (e.g., last verb, finite verb, morphologically marked predicates)?
- Can you report per-language POS accuracies (prediction mode) and provide UAS vs POS-accuracy correlation plots?
- Did you try personalizing other content words or edge-level PR (reified graph)? Any gains?
- Please include charts summarizing cross-domain variance and ablations. Also, minor: l.207 typo (“The the main idea”).
