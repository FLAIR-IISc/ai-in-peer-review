- Proposes UDP: a training-free UD-specific parser combining POS-based head rules with personalized PageRank over sentence graphs to rank content words guiding decoding.  
- Enforces UD constraints via two-step decoding (attach content words then function words) and estimates adposition head-direction from test data.  
- Evaluated on 32 UD languages under gold/predicted POS and cross-domain, compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline (MSD).

- Contribution: simple, linguistically grounded, training-free parser achieving competitive UAS and strong robustness to domain shift.  
- Contribution: decoding strategy that guarantees single-rootedness and function-word leafhood without training.  
- Contribution: lightweight, test-time estimation of adposition direction improving cross-lingual applicability.

- Strength: Achieves UAS close to supervised delexicalized parser (≈6–7 point gap with gold POS), with PR giving ~9 UAS gain over rules-only.  
- Strength: Lower performance variance across domain splits, showing robustness to domain shift.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and reproducibility, with justified personalization in PR and informative ablations.  
- Strength: Broad multilingual evaluation (32 languages) and candid error analysis (issues with punctuation and coordination).

- Weakness: Methodological novelty is incremental over prior rule-aided unsupervised parsing (e.g., Søgaard 2012b).  
- Weakness: Reported predicted-POS results use supervised taggers trained on target language, weakening the “no training” claim for low-resource scenarios.  
- Weakness: Fixed head rules limit structural expressivity (poor handling of relative clauses, coordination, punctuation), and method is unlabeled and UD-specific.  
- Weakness: Adposition direction estimation is transductive and may be brittle for languages/domains with mixed or rare ADP patterns; other function-word directions are globally fixed.  
- Weakness: Baseline scope is limited; stronger projection-based or multilingual representation baselines are not included.

- Question: Can results be reported with cross-lingual or projected POS tags to better reflect true low-resource scenarios and compare UDP vs MSD?  
- Question: How stable is the bigram-based adposition direction estimate in languages with scarce or mixed ADP usage; were confidence thresholds or per-sentence adaptations considered?  
- Question: Why personalize PR to the first verb/content word; how does performance change in verb-final languages (e.g., using last verb) or with simple learned position priors?  
- Question: Did authors try adding a few targeted rules (e.g., limited NOUN→VERB for relative clauses) to improve CONJ/PUNCT without harming overall performance?  
- Question: Any preliminary attempts at lightweight label assignment (rule-based or POS-pattern-based) to convert unlabeled trees to labeled parsing, and what labeled accuracy can be recovered?
