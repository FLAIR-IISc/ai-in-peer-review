- Paper introduces UDP: a deterministic, unsupervised UD parser using POS-based head rules, personalized PageRank over a rule-induced graph, two-step decoding, and test-time adposition direction inference.

- Contribution: a strong, training-free baseline for UD parsing, broadly multilingual and cross-domain, competitive with delexicalized transfer.

- Contribution: a simple UD-aware decoding design (personalized PR + two-step attachment enforcing function-word leafness) that yields consistent gains over rule-only baselines.

- Contribution: language-agnostic, test-time estimation of adposition head direction that is simple, fast, and effective.

- Strength: Robust, competitive performance without training—on average within ~6–7 UAS of MSD and better on some languages (e.g., Hindi); smaller drop with predicted POS than MSD.

- Strength: Cross-domain stability—consistently lower standard deviation across domains than MSD with both gold and predicted POS.

- Strength: Clear ablations and interpretable design—PR yields ~9 UAS gain; rule-based decoding adds ~4 UAS; UD constraints are explicit and linguistically motivated.

- Strength: ADP direction estimation converges quickly (10–15 sentences) and correctly identifies pre/post positions across languages.

- Strength: Deterministic, resource-light, reproducible implementation with transparent hyperparameter choices.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty relative to prior PR-based unsupervised work (e.g., Søgaard 2012); needs deeper positioning and stronger empirical advantages over earlier PR baselines.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on hand-crafted, English-tuned head rules and directionality settings, risking missed language-specific phenomena (e.g., Basque determiners); forbidding NOUN→VERB limits handling of relative clauses.

- Weakness: Test-time estimation of ADP direction blurs model/evaluation boundary; fairness and reproducibility concerns without a frozen/dev-only protocol or sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness: Missing analysis of PR graph properties and tie-breaking—no quantification of disconnected graphs, component sizes, tie frequency, or their effect on PR stability/UAS.

- Weakness: Scope/evaluation limits—poor handling of coordination/relative clauses, no labeled attachment scores, limited comparisons to recent unsupervised/weakly supervised baselines, and no sensitivity analysis for PR hyperparameters.

- Q: How frequently are PR graphs disconnected across languages/domains, what are component sizes, and do these correlate with UAS?

- Q: How are PR rank ties and distance ties resolved during decoding, how often do they occur, and what is their impact on UAS?

- Q: How sensitive are results to teleport probability and the 5x personalization weight, and does predicate heuristic choice (first vs. last verb) affect root/UAS?

- Q: Can ADP directionality be fixed using development-only or small unlabeled target samples to assess fairness vs. MSD, and can other function-word directions be inferred similarly (helpful for Basque)?

- Q: Please provide oracle upper bounds for individual constraints (oracle head direction, oracle rule coverage, oracle nearest-head distance) to pinpoint remaining error sources.

- Additional comments: Minor editorial fixes and table reorder suggestions; request to report runtime per sentence, proportion of non-projective attachments, labeled results if feasible, and comparisons to stronger recent baselines.
