- Summary: Deterministic, training-free UD parser combining POS-based head rules with a biased PageRank and nearest allowable-head attachment; evaluated on 32 languages and multiple domains, competitive with delexicalized transfer.

- Contribution: Simple deterministic UD parser integrating POS head rules with personalized PageRank for root selection and content-word ordering.

- Contribution: Large-scale multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing robustness to POS noise and domain shift.

- Contribution: Test-time heuristic to estimate ADP head directionality without language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Strong empirical coverage — results on 32 languages (gold and predicted POS), on average within 6.4 UAS of a multi-source delexicalized baseline and better on Hindi.

- Strength: Robustness — smaller degradation with predicted POS and lower cross-domain variance than the supervised transfer baseline.

- Strength: Clarity and simplicity — transparent, fast, easy to reproduce, making it a useful baseline for typologically diverse settings.

- Strength: Sensible UD alignment — enforcing function-word leafness and single-root constraints produces linguistically coherent structures.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — approach is close to Søgaard (2012) with personalization and two-step decoding as incremental changes.

- Weakness: Test-time tuning risks evaluation leakage — estimating ADP directionality on the test set is problematic; dev-based or within-corpus unsupervised protocols preferred.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of constraints — lacks oracle upper bounds per constraint and limited statistics on rule coverage and back-off usage.

- Weakness: Graph properties underexplored — PageRank may operate on disconnected graphs but disconnection frequency and component-size distributions are not reported.

- Weakness: Ambiguity handling unspecified — tie-breaking in PageRank and attachment is not described and its effect on accuracy is unclear.

- Question: How are PageRank and attachment ties broken, how often do ties occur, and do they measurably affect UAS?

- Question: Report the proportion of sentences with disconnected graphs and the component-size distributions.

- Question: Report the frequency of dropping δ/κ constraints during back-off.

- Question: Can ADP direction be estimated on a held-out split within the test corpus to avoid leakage?

- Additional comment: Minor wording corrections suggested in Section 4.1 (“we obtain the rank.”) and Section 6.1 (“isolate the contribution of both.”).
