Summary: The paper introduces a deterministic, training-free dependency parser for UD that combines POS-based head rules with a biased PageRank over a graph of permitted dependencies, then attaches each token to its closest allowable head while preserving tree structure and head-direction constraints. The method is evaluated on 32 languages and multiple domains, showing competitive UAS to delexicalized transfer.

Contributions (as I see them):
1) A simple, deterministic UD parser that integrates POS head rules with personalized PageRank for root selection and content-word ordering.
2) A large-scale multilingual and cross-domain evaluation demonstrating robustness to POS noise and domain shift.
3) A test-time heuristic for estimating ADP head directionality without language-specific parameters.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical coverage: Results on 32 languages with both gold and predicted POS; on average within 6.4 UAS of a multi-source delexicalized baseline, and better on Hindi. This breadth and the domain analyses increase the paper’s practical relevance.
- Robustness: Smaller degradation with predicted POS and lower cross-domain variance than the supervised transfer baseline, supporting claims of stability.
- Clarity and simplicity: The algorithm is transparent, fast, and easy to reproduce, making it a useful baseline for UD parsing and typologically diverse settings.
- Sensible UD alignment: Enforcing function-word leafness and single-root constraints yields linguistically coherent structures across languages.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: The approach is close to Søgaard (2012), with personalization and two-step decoding as incremental changes; a deeper comparative analysis to Søgaard’s variants is warranted.
- Test-time tuning: Estimating ADP directionality on the test set risks evaluation leakage, even if convergence is quick; a dev-based or unsupervised-within-corpus protocol would be cleaner.
- Insufficient analysis of constraints: No oracle upper bounds per constraint (head rules, directionality, distance), and limited statistics on rule coverage and back-off usage.
- Graph properties underexplored: PageRank operates on possibly disconnected graphs; frequencies of disconnection and component-size distributions are not reported.
- Ambiguity handling: Tie-breaking in PageRank and during attachment is unspecified; the impact of ties on accuracy is unclear.

Questions to Authors:
- How are PageRank and attachment ties broken, and how often do ties occur? Do they measurably affect UAS?
- Please report: (a) proportion of sentences with disconnected graphs and component sizes; (b) frequency of dropping δ/κ constraints during back-off.
- Provide oracle analyses isolating each constraint to quantify their upper-bound contributions.
- Can ADP direction be estimated on a held-out split within the test corpus to avoid leakage?
