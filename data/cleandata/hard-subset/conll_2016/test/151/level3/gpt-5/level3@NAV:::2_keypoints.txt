- Introduces UDP: a training-free, unsupervised UD parser using personalized PageRank over sentence graphs, UD-compliant head rules, and a two-step decoding enforcing function-word leafness; estimates adposition attachment direction from test data and needs only POS tags, evaluated on 32 languages and multiple domains.

- Contribution: a linguistically grounded, training-free parser combining personalized PageRank with UD-informed constraints and test-time adposition direction estimation.

- Contribution: broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing competitive accuracy with delexicalized transfer and improved robustness to POS noise and domain shift.

- Contribution: provides a clear, reproducible rule set and analyses positioning UDP as a strong low-resource baseline for unsupervised parsing.

- Strength: competitive multilingual performance with minimal assumptions, nearly matching multi-source delexicalized transfer and outperforming it on Hindi despite no training data.

- Strength: robustness to noisy POS and domain shift, showing smaller accuracy drops with predicted POS and lower cross-domain variance than transfer methods.

- Strength: methodological clarity and linguistic grounding—UD constraints (single root, function-word leafness) and two-step decoding are well motivated and transparent.

- Strength: solid analytical evidence from ablations that isolate gains from PageRank personalization and the decoding strategy.

- Strength: practical and easy to deploy—no reliance on parallel corpora or source models, suitable for low-resource/cross-domain settings.

- Weakness: moderate novelty, building on prior rule-based/PageRank methods; main advance is UD-specific integration and evaluation breadth rather than a new paradigm.

- Weakness: dependence on POS tagging—best results use strong POS tags, and predicted POS still needs supervised taggers per language, limiting fully zero-resource use.

- Weakness: limited linguistic coverage and evaluation—only unlabeled attachment and known gaps in handling coordination, relative clauses, and punctuation.

- Weakness: potential English-centric tuning—some attachment directions and rule choices were decided on English and may not generalize cross-lingually.

- Question: how sensitive is performance to personalization weight and teleport probability in PageRank across languages?

- Question: can directionality estimation be extended beyond ADP (e.g., DET/AUX/SCONJ) to reduce English-biased heuristics?

- Question: what is UDP’s runtime profile versus delexicalized transfer at scale, and can edge-level PR (reification) be made efficient?
