- Introduces UDP: a simple, training-free UD parser using POS-based head rules, personalized PageRank to rank predicates, and a two-stage decoding enforcing UD priors (single root, function-word-as-leaf).  
- Data-driven on-the-fly estimation of adposition head-direction from test data, avoiding language-specific parameters.  
- Evaluated across 32 UD v1.2 languages, three POS regimes, and multiple domains with clear ablations and POS-level analyses.  
- Competitive cross-lingual performance: with gold POS ~6.4 UAS behind multi-source delexicalized parser and degrades minimally with predicted POS.  
- Robustness to POS noise and domain shift: lower POS-to-dependency error propagation (0.37 vs 0.46) and lower UAS variance across domains than MSDG.  
- PageRank personalization and two-step decoding are primary accuracy drivers (+9 UAS from PR, +4 UAS from decoding).  
- PR improves root accuracy (~69% on average) including in verb-final languages; PR setup and hyperparameters are documented (teleport 0.05, 5x personalization).  
- Design tightly integrates UD structural constraints, yielding deterministic parses given POS and easy reproducibility/extension.  
- Paper is well-written, transparent, and discusses future directions (MWEs, coordination, edge reification, partial labeling).  
- Limited coverage of key syntactic phenomena: excluding NOUN→VERB harms relative clauses; coordination, punctuation, and proper names perform weakly.  
- Dependence on reasonably accurate POS tags; performance collapses in extreme content/function-only settings and may suffer in very low-resource/noisy-tag scenarios.  
- Several function-POS directionality assumptions are fixed (DET, AUX, SCONJ right-attaching; CONJ, PUNCT left-attaching) based on English, causing cross-lingual errors.  
- Evaluation scope omits some alternative unsupervised/weakly supervised baselines and focuses on UAS without labeled relation evaluation.  
- Hyperparameters and graph-construction choices are tuned on English and may inject bias; limited cross-lingual sensitivity analysis.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to personalization/teleport/graph rules across languages; extending test-time direction estimation to other function POS; rationale for dropping NOUN→VERB and possible back-off fixes.  
- Further questions: sentence-level runtime and scalability; ability to produce coarse UD labels (LAS/partial labels); performance with noisy POS from projection or tiny tagger training sets and potential morphological cues.  
- Overall: a substantial, timely, training-free UD parsing baseline that leverages UD priors effectively, with strong cross-lingual breadth and robustness but limited syntactic coverage and some language-specific assumptions.
