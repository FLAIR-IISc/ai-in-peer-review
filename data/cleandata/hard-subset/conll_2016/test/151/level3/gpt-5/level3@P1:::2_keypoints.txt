- Introduces UDP: a training-free, unsupervised UD parser using UD-aligned head rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and a two-step decoder enforcing function-word leafness.

- Combines on-the-fly adposition head-direction estimation from test sentences via simple bigram counts to avoid language-specific parameters.

- Contribution: a practical, rule-guided, training-free UD parser encoding UD constraints and separating content vs. function attachment.

- Contribution: rigorous multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing UDP approaches delexicalized transfer performance and is more domain-robust.

- Contribution: simple data-driven adposition direction estimator that converges with few sentences and yields strong ADP performance.

- Strength: competitive empirical performance (57.5 UAS with gold POS) close to multi-source delexicalized transfer, with modest drop using predicted POS.

- Strength: principled exploitation of UD constraints (single root, function words as leaves) operationalized via two-step decoding.

- Strength: ablations show PageRank is crucial (~9 UAS loss without PR) and decoding adds ~4 UAS over head-rule baseline; personalized PR improves root selection.

- Strength: demonstrably lower variance across domains than delexicalized transfer, indicating better domain robustness.

- Strength: simple and resource-efficient—no training data or parallel corpora required; ADP estimator needs only ~10–15 sentences.

- Weakness: limited structural coverage by design (e.g., excludes many relative clauses, weak coordination/punctuation handling, no MWE/proper-name treatment).

- Weakness: strong dependence on POS quality and mapping; performance degrades with coarse or noisy tags and annotation inconsistencies.

- Weakness: tuning on English dev data and estimating ADP direction from test sentences raise concerns about fairness and potential evaluation leakage.

- Weakness: greedy decoding without global optimization and evaluation limited to unlabeled UAS; no labeled parsing or comparisons to recent induction baselines.

- Weakness: limited per-language error analysis beyond ADP direction and a few exceptions, leaving typology-specific failures underexplored.

- Question: Which hyperparameters and rule choices were tuned on English dev, and how sensitive are results to different development languages?

- Question: Is ADP direction estimated once per language on held-out text or recomputed per test fold, and what if estimated on development data only?

- Question: How does performance change with modern cross-lingual taggers, projected tags, or controlled POS noise levels?

- Question: Have simple rules for coordination and punctuation been tried, and what are the precision/recall trade-offs?

- Question: Can PageRank be extended to reified edge graphs for labeled attachments, and are there any preliminary results?

- Question: What are typical runtimes per sentence/treebank for UDP, and can PR costs be further reduced without accuracy loss?

- Overall verdict: A clear, substantive paper showing a strong, simple, training-free UD baseline that is competitive and domain-robust despite structural and POS-dependence limitations; leaning toward acceptance.
