- Paper proposes UDP: a deterministic, training-free UD parser using a POS-constrained multigraph, personalized PageRank (PR) to rank content words, and two-stage decoding (attach content words with single-root constraint, then attach function words as leaves).
- Contributions: simple unsupervised UD parser competitive with delexicalized transfer, linguistically motivated UD-constrained decoding with PR guidance, and on-the-fly, language-agnostic adposition (ADP) direction inference from test data.

- Broad evaluation across 32 languages and domains with standard UAS shows UDP is within ~6.4 UAS of multi-source delexicalized parsing and sometimes better (e.g., Hindi).
- Demonstrated cross-domain robustness and lower performance variance than delexicalized transfer in both gold and predicted POS settings.
- Clear, reproducible algorithmic description with deterministic two-stage decoding, complexity discussion, and informative ablations showing PR and decoding contributions.
- Language-agnostic and training-free design requires no source models or parallel data, suitable as a low-resource baseline.
- Empirical UD insights: PR personalization toward the first verb improves root accuracy and ADP direction estimation yields strong ADP UAS (~75%).

- Novelty is incremental relative to Søgaard (2012); differences mainly PR personalization and UD-specific two-stage decoding—needs direct comparison to Søgaard under UD.
- Potential test-set tuning/ leakage: ADP direction inferred from test data and function-word directions fixed from English dev may risk unfair cross-lingual evaluation.
- Graph construction and PR details underspecified: no analysis of disconnected graphs, component sizes, or effect on PR stability and attachments.
- Missing oracle/upper-bound analyses for components (ADP direction, function-word directionality, root detection, head-rule licensing) to quantify error ceilings.
- Tie-breaking and determinism in PR/decoding not fully specified; unclear frequency and impact of ties or back-off behavior when constraints prune options.

- Ask authors to report per-language ADP direction convergence: sentences/types needed, inferred vs. ground-truth accuracy, and fraction of sentences before convergence.
- Ask for graph connectivity stats: frequency of disconnected sentence graphs, distribution of component counts/sizes, and correlation with PR root selection and UAS.
- Ask how PR ties are handled, whether tie-breaking is deterministic across platforms, frequency of ties, and impact on UAS.
- Request oracle/ablation results for correct ADP direction, global function-word directions, oracle root/main predicate, and oracle head-rule licensing, plus direct reimplementation comparison to Søgaard (2012b) under UD v1.2.
- Request sensitivity analysis for teleport probability and personalization weight (set to 5).

- Editorial fixes: multiple typos and wording issues (e.g., "we obtain the rank", "isolate the contribution of both parts", stray duplications and misspellings).
- Table and formatting suggestions: reorder Table 5 to match Table 7, clean LaTeX artifacts and reference formatting.
- Recommend direct comparison to Søgaard (2012b) given conceptual proximity rather than older DMV baselines.
- Suggest quantitative error breakdowns for known limitations (coordination, punctuation, NOUN→VERB exclusions).
- Reproducibility requests: clarify code availability, random seeds (if any), and exact PR implementation details to ensure determinism.
