## Review of “Parsing for Universal Dependencies without training”

This paper presents UDP, a novel unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm leveraging POS-based head rules and a biased PageRank approach. The core idea – to exploit the constraints inherent in Universal Dependencies (UD) to achieve competitive parsing without training – is interesting and well-motivated, particularly given the challenges of cross-lingual transfer learning. The paper is clearly written and the method is presented in a logical and accessible manner.

The primary contribution lies in the effective combination of existing techniques – PageRank and rule-based parsing – tailored specifically to the UD framework. Adapting dependency head rules to UD constraints and incorporating the function word restriction are sensible modifications. The use of personalized PageRank to improve predicate identification is a clever addition. A secondary contribution is the comprehensive evaluation across 32 languages, demonstrating the robustness of the approach. The inclusion of domain adaptation experiments further strengthens the evaluation.

However, the novelty of the method is somewhat incremental. As the authors acknowledge, the work builds heavily on Søgaard (2012), and the improvements, while valuable, feel evolutionary rather than revolutionary. The performance gains, while competitive with a delexicalized transfer system, aren’t substantial enough to warrant a major shift in the field. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Broad Language Coverage:** The evaluation across 32 languages is a significant undertaking and provides strong evidence for the generalizability of the approach.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the method and experimental setup.
*   **Robustness to Domain Shift:** The domain adaptation experiments demonstrate a key advantage of the approach – its stability across different text genres.
*   **Competitive Performance:** Achieving performance comparable to a delexicalized transfer system without any training data is a noteworthy achievement.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty:** The method builds heavily on existing work (Søgaard, 2012) and the improvements are incremental.
*   **Test Set Tuning of Directionality:** Tuning head directionality on the test set raises concerns about potential overfitting. While the authors state convergence is quick, it’s still a questionable practice.
*   **Tie-Breaking in PageRank:** The paper doesn’t detail how ties are handled during PageRank tie-breaking, and it’s unclear whether this impacts performance.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on how ties are handled during the PageRank ranking process? Does the tie-breaking mechanism affect the final parse tree, and if so, how?
2.  Regarding the head directionality tuning, could you provide the results of tuning on a development set, even if it’s small? This would address concerns about test set contamination.
3.  Could you provide an analysis of the frequency of disconnected graphs encountered during the PageRank setup, and the average size of the connected components? This would help understand the impact of the head rules and teleport probability.

**Additional Comments:**
