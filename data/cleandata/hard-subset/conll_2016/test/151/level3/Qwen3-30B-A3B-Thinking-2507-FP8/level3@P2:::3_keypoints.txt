- Contribution: A linguistically grounded unsupervised parser that leverages UD constraints (function words as leaves, single-root trees).  
- Contribution: A practical method to estimate adposition direction from test data, removing language-specific parameters.  
- Contribution: Shows competitive performance with supervised cross-lingual transfer parsers and robustness across domains.

- Strength: Uses UD formal constraints to produce a theoretically grounded and interpretable approach, with demonstrated domain robustness.  
- Strength: Strong performance without training data — +4% over rule baselines and within 6.4% of supervised MSD on average.  
- Strength: Empirically more robust to domain shifts than data-driven methods, with lower standard deviations across domains.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — method closely follows Søgaard (2012) with minor UD-specific adaptations and PageRank, so claims of being unprecedented are overstated.  
- Weakness: Arbitrary parameter choice — personalization weight of 5 has no justification or ablation study.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of POS dependency — POS tagging accuracy for UDPP is not reported and no correlation with parsing performance is analyzed.  
- Weakness: Lack of visualizations — results presented only in tables, making cross-language trends hard to interpret.  
- Weakness: Underexplored personalization — personalized PageRank is applied only to the main predicate and not investigated for other uses.

- Question: What is the POS tagging accuracy of the TnT tagger used in the UDPP setup?  
- Question: Why was the personalization weight set to 5; can you provide an ablation showing performance across weights?  
- Question: Is the 0.1% UAS difference on Hindi versus delexicalized parsing statistically significant?  
- Question: How would the approach handle languages with non-standard UD conventions where function words are not consistently leaves?  
- Question: How would the two-step decoding handle multiword expressions and what modifications would be required?
