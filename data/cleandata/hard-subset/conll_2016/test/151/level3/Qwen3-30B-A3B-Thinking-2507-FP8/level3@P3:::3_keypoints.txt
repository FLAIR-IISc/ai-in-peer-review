- Presents UDP, a rule-based unsupervised UD parser using only POS tags and a modified PageRank, claiming delexicalized-transfer-level performance and cross-domain robustness.  
- Primary contributions: a rule-based unsupervised parser competitive with delexicalized transfer, and demonstrated robustness to domain changes.  
- Novelty is limited: approach closely follows Søgaard (2012) with minor modifications (personalized PageRank, UD head-rule adaptations).  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with detailed algorithm description and UD-specific decoding steps.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across 32 languages and three POS setups, illuminating robustness and POS–parsing relationships.  
- Cross-domain analysis shows lower variance across domains than supervised transfer, supporting stability claims.  
- Novelty claim is weak; authors don’t clearly justify how their changes substantially improve over Søgaard (2012).  
- Personalization weight of 5 is arbitrary and unjustified; no ablation or sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Results lack visualizations, making cross-language and setup trends hard to interpret.  
- POS tagging accuracy in prediction mode is not reported, hindering analysis of parsing degradation sources.  
- Minor proofreading issue noted (typo at line 207).  
- Questions: provide detailed comparison with Søgaard (2012) highlighting real novelties.  
- Questions: justify choice of personalization weight (5) with empirical ablation.  
- Questions: include visualizations (charts/graphs) to clarify comparative results.  
- Questions: report POS tagging accuracies for predicted-POS setups to assess correlation with parsing errors.
