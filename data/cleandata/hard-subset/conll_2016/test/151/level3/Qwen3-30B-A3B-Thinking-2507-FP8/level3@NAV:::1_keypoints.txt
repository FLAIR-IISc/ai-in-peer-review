- Contributions: adapts dependency head rules to UD POS relations, uses personalized PageRank to improve main predicate identification, and estimates adposition attachment direction on test data to create a language-parameter-free system.  
- Strength: remarkably simple yet competitive, achieving average UAS within 6.4% of supervised baselines.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across 32 languages and three POS setups shows strong cross-lingual performance.  
- Strength: analysis shows superior cross-domain robustness compared to data-driven approaches.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—approach is only slightly different from Søgaard (2012) and paper doesn’t justify why modifications matter.  
- Weakness: methodological concern—head directionality was tuned on the test set rather than training/dev despite quick convergence.  
- Weakness: missing analysis of head rules’ corpus behavior (e.g., frequency of disconnected graphs) to clarify algorithmic limitations.  
- Question: how is PageRank tie-breaking handled and does it impact performance?  
- Question: please report oracle results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show upper-bound impacts.  
- Question: clarify rationale for tuning head directionality on test data and whether this affects generalization to unseen domains.  
- Minor edits: fix phrasing in 4.1 ("we obtain [the] rank"), reorder Table 5 columns to match Table 7, and correct wording in 6.1 ("isolate the [contribution] of both").  
- Overall: interesting idea with robust evaluation, but limited novelty and test-set tuning issues weaken its contribution.
