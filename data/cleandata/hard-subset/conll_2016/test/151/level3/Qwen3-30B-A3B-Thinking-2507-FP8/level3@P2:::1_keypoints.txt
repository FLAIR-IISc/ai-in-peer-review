- Systematic adaptation of dependency head rules to UD enforcing function-word constraints (e.g., determiners/adpositions must be dependents of content words).
- Deterministic unsupervised parser using personalized PageRank and two-step decoding, claimed competitive across 32 languages and robust to domain shifts.
- Method to estimate adposition directionality from a small number of test sentences, removing language-specific parameters.
- Strong empirical evaluation across 32 languages, multiple POS scenarios, and domains showing competitive performance and lower domain variance.
- Practical utility: competitive with supervised transfer (≈6.4% avg UAS gap) and more resilient to POS errors (lower error propagation).
- Linguistically principled approach that leverages UD constraints to avoid common data-driven pitfalls and handle cross-lingual word order differences.
- Limited novelty: largely an extension of Søgaard (2012) with modest additions (UD head rules, PageRank, two-step decoding).
- Estimating adposition direction on test data undermines the claim of being fully unsupervised and may be methodologically problematic.
- Insufficient analysis of head-rule limitations (e.g., graph connectivity, component sizes) and limited error analysis for poor-performing languages/POS.
- Lack of comparison to more recent unsupervised parsers (circa 2012–2015), making it hard to assess true state-of-the-art standing.
- Question: Is estimating adposition direction on 10–15 test sentences acceptable for an unsupervised parser, and how are very short test sets handled?
- Question: How are PageRank ties broken and does tie-breaking affect parsing performance?
- Question: Can the authors provide oracle/upper-bound results isolating the impact of each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance)?
- Question: How would development using a language other than English affect results and the claim of language independence?
