- Paper introduces UDP: an unsupervised dependency parser for UD using POS tags and a modified (personalized) PageRank, claiming four contributions (UD head-rule adaptation, function-word-leaf constraint, personalized PageRank, language-free adposition direction estimation).

- Contribution (a): Adapts dependency head rules to UD-compliant POS relations.

- Contribution (b): Incorporates UD constraint that function words are leaves.

- Contribution (c): Uses personalized PageRank to improve identification of the main predicate.

- Contribution (d): Removes language-specific parameters by estimating adposition direction from test data.

- Strength: Strong cross-lingual robustness with consistently good performance across 32 languages.

- Strength: Greater stability to domain shifts than supervised baselines (MSD), shown across multiple domains.

- Strength: Simple, training-free system suitable for resource-constrained settings.

- Weakness: Method closely mirrors Søgaard (2012) with only minor modifications, so novelty is limited.

- Weakness: Personalization weight set to 5 appears arbitrary and lacks justification or ablation studies.

- Weakness: Results presented in dense tables without visualizations, making interpretation difficult.

- Weakness: Missing POS tagging scores in prediction mode, preventing analysis of whether parsing degradation is due to tagging errors.

- Questions: Why choose personalization weight = 5 and can ablation studies show effect of different weights?

- Questions: Please include visualizations to better illustrate performance differences across languages and evaluation setups.
