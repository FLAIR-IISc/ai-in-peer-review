- UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using UD constraints and personalized PageRank to identify main predicates without training data.  
- Competitive and robust: achieves performance within 6.4% of a supervised transfer parser and is more resilient to domain shifts.  
- Training-free cross-lingual design: estimates adposition direction on-the-fly to avoid language-specific parameter training.  
- Strength — UD constraints effective: exploiting function-word-as-leaf and head-dependent UD rules yields a 4–9 UAS gain over a rule-based baseline and narrows gap to supervised systems.  
- Strength — domain robustness: UDP (gold POS) shows lower cross-domain std. dev. (2.5) than MSD (4.2), indicating greater stability.  
- Strength — thorough evaluation: tested on 32 languages across three POS setups with detailed error analysis and a strong MSD baseline comparison.  
- Weakness — head-rule justification: authors give insufficient rationale for chosen head rules or their generalizability beyond UD.  
- Weakness — artificial POS scenario: the naive POS (UDPN) setup is unrealistic and under-justified, weakening those results.  
- Weakness — language-specific claim questionable: claiming no language-specific parameters conflicts with reliance on adposition direction information.  
- Question — reconcile claim of no language-specific parameters with need to estimate pre-/postposition direction per language?  
- Question — why does UDP outperform MSD on Hindi despite Hindi being verb-final where first verb may not be main predicate?  
- Question — clarify evaluation: why was punctuation excluded and exactly how was UAS computed?
