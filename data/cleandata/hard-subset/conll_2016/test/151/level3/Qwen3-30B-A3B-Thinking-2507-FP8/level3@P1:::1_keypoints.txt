- Paper presents UDP: a deterministic, fully unsupervised UD parser using POS-based head rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and closest allowable-head assignment with directionality constraints.

- Main contribution: a linguistically grounded, completely unsupervised dependency parser that leverages UD formal constraints.

- Main contribution: empirical demonstration of robust cross-lingual parsing performance across 32 languages without language-specific training.

- Main contribution: demonstration of substantially reduced sensitivity to domain shifts compared to supervised transfer methods.

- Strength: competitive performance—UDPG (gold POS) averages only 6.4% behind MSDG and even outperforms MSDG on Hindi.

- Strength: stronger robustness to domain shifts—lower standard deviation across domains than MSD (e.g., 2.5 vs. 4.2 for UDPG vs. MSDG).

- Strength: extremely simple and transparent method using only UD constraints and a small set of head rules, with no language-specific parameters.

- Weakness: limited novelty—the algorithm is a direct extension of Søgaard (2012b) with insufficient differentiation from prior work.

- Weakness: adposition head directionality is estimated on test data (data leakage); should be tuned on a proper development set.

- Weakness: missing analysis of head-rule coverage and corpus properties, notably how often the constructed graph is disconnected and component sizes.

- Weakness: lacks oracle analysis for individual constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) to assess each constraint's upper bound contribution.

- Question: how are PageRank ties broken, and does tie-breaking affect determinism or performance?

- Question: what is the frequency of disconnected graphs in the corpora and the sizes of their components?

- Question: can the authors provide oracle results for each constraint to show the upper-bound impact on final scores?
