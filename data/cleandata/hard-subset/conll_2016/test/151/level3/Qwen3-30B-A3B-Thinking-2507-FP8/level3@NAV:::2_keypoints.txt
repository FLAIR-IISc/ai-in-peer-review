- Introduces UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser requiring no training data.  
- Combines personalized PageRank on content words with a small set of UD-compliant head rules to build trees.  
- Primary contribution: a training-free algorithm leveraging UD constraints to produce high-quality, language-agnostic parses.  
- Secondary contribution: automatic estimation of adposition direction from test data, avoiding language-specific tuning.  
- Presents a two-step decoding algorithm that enforces UD convention of function words as leaves.  
- Claims competitive performance with supervised transfer systems (within 6.4% average difference).  
- Thorough evaluation across 32 languages and multiple POS scenarios.  
- Demonstrates strong robustness to domain shifts with lower standard deviation than supervised baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with clear linguistic intuition and explanations.  
- Evaluation limited by excluding languages without word forms (e.g., Japanese) and certain annotation practices.  
- Performance drop with predicted POS (2.2%) is less meaningful given high POS accuracy (94.1%).  
- Unclear how method generalizes to radically different syntactic types (additional SOV languages).  
- Comparison to recent unsupervised methods (post-2012) is incomplete, possibly overstating novelty.
