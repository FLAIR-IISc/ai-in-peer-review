- Summary: UDP is an unsupervised UD parser requiring no training that uses POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank ranking, and a two-step decoder to enforce content-as-head and function-as-leaf; evaluated on 32 languages in three POS settings and shown competitive and domain-robust.

- Contribution 1: Proposes a linguistically motivated, training-free parsing approach leveraging UD constraints (function words as leaves, content words as heads).

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates a simple rule-based parser can match supervised transfer systems while being more robust to domain shifts.

- Contribution 3: Provides a practical, easy-to-implement baseline useful for low-resource UD parsing.

- Strength 1: Strong linguistic motivation aligning method closely with UD formalism.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive evaluation across 32 languages, multiple POS scenarios, and cross-domain analysis.

- Strength 3: Clear presentation with detailed examples, component analysis, and an explained two-step decoding algorithm.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—largely builds on Søgaard (2012) with incremental UD-specific modifications.

- Weakness 2: Methodological concern—head directionality (e.g., adpositions) estimated from test data, risking evaluation bias.

- Weakness 3: Missing analysis of head-rule-related graph properties (e.g., disconnected graphs, component sizes) to show limitations.

- Weakness 4: No discussion of PageRank tie-breaking when ranks are equal and potential impact on deterministic outputs.

- Weakness 5: Lacks oracle/ablation upper-bound results for individual constraints (head rules, directionality, distance).

- Question 1: Clarify how much of the test set was used to estimate head directionality and how that compares to dev tuning.

- Question 2: Explain PageRank tie-breaking procedure and whether tie cases affect performance.

- Question 3: Provide statistics on frequency and sizes of disconnected graphs and failures of head rules to form connected graphs.

- Question 4: Report oracle results for each constraint to quantify their upper-bound contributions.

- Minor edit 1: Fix phrasing in Section 4.1 ("we obtain [the] rank").

- Minor edit 2: Reorder Table 5 columns to match Table 7 (prefer BLG, MSDG, UDPG, MSDP, UDPP).

- Minor edit 3: Fix wording in Section 6.1 ("isolate the [contribution] of both" → "isolate the contribution of both").
