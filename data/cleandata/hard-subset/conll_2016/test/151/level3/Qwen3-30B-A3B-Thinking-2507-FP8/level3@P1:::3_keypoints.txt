- Summary: Proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using only POS tags and a modified PageRank, adapting Søgaard (2012) with UD-specific constraints (function words as leaves, main predicate identification, adposition direction).
- Claimed contributions: (1) UD-tailored head rules, (2) enforcing function words as leaves, (3) personalized PageRank for main predicate, (4) parameter-free, but mainly an adaptation of Søgaard (2012) with limited novelty.
- Main novelty critique: Primary contribution is limited to adapting prior work to UD; personalized PageRank is the most substantive new piece but under-explored.
- Strength 1: Shows strong cross-lingual robustness, outperforming delexicalized transfer (MSD) on average by 6.4% and with much lower domain-standard deviation.
- Strength 2: Evaluation across gold, predicted, and naive POS scenarios gives a realistic view and shows lower error propagation than MSD despite using predicted POS tags.
- Strength 3: Simplicity and no training requirement make UDP a valuable baseline for low-resource parsing.
- Weakness 1: Minimal novelty; personalized PageRank applied narrowly to main predicate with an unjustified arbitrary weight of 5 and no sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness 2: Lacks visualizations to interpret results and trends across languages and setups (e.g., Table 5 is hard to assess).
- Weakness 3: POS tagging accuracy in predicted-mode is not reported, leaving parsing degradation unexplained and claims about marginal drops under-contextualized.
- Weakness 4: Paper contains minor proofreading issues (typo "The the main idea").
- Question 1: Why was personalization weight set to 5 and how sensitive are results to this parameter?
- Question 2: Can the authors provide visual comparisons (e.g., bar charts) of UDPG vs MSDG across languages?
- Question 3: Please report POS tagging accuracy for languages in the predicted POS setup (UDPP) to contextualize parsing results.
