- Introduces UDP, a training-free unsupervised dependency parser for UD that is competitive with delexicalized transfer systems.
- Novel training-free parsing approach achieving competitive performance vs delexicalized transfer.
- Principled adaptation of dependency head rules to UD, enforcing that function words are always leaves.
- Uses personalized PageRank for main predicate identification and a two-step decoding to keep function words as leaves.
- Substantial empirical contribution: competitive across 32 languages, only ~6.4% UAS gap vs delexicalized transfer with gold POS and better on some languages.
- Strong evaluation and robustness analysis across gold/predicted/naive POS and domains, showing superior domain robustness (std dev 1.8 vs 4.5).
- Clear, well-motivated methodology with justified use of personalized PageRank and two-step decoding to enforce UD constraints.
- Limited handling of language-specific phenomena (e.g., verb-final languages like Japanese/Korean excluded; first-verb assumption may fail).
- Overreliance on POS tag quality; tested with ~94% POS accuracy but performance under lower POS accuracy (<90%) not explored.
- Limited error analysis: insufficient investigation into failures on PUNCT/CONJ and handling of multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names.
- Question: How to modify the system for verb-final languages or use alternative personalization strategies?
- Question: How would UDP be adapted for completely unseen, noisy domains (e.g., social media)?
- Question: How could the parser be extended to handle multiword expressions within the current training-free, rule-based framework?
