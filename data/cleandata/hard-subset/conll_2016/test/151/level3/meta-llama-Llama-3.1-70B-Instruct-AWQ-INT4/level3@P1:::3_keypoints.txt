- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using only POS tags and a modified PageRank to score content words.  
- Requires no training data and is competitive with delexicalized transfer systems.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages and shown to be robust to domain change.  
- Contribution: unsupervised parsing method for UD treebanks that avoids annotated training data.  
- Contribution: a modified PageRank that downweights stop words and boosts the main predicate.  
- Contribution: demonstrated cross-lingual and cross-domain robustness.  
- Strength: competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art delexicalized transfer.  
- Strength: robust to domain change across multiple languages.  
- Strength: simple, linguistically motivated, and easy to implement.  
- Weakness: limited novelty, closely mirrors Søgaard (2012).  
- Weakness: personalization limited to the main predicate and under-explored.  
- Weakness: personalization weight set to 5 without justification or tuning.  
- Weakness: lacks visualizations to aid interpretation of results.  
- Weakness: does not report POS tagging scores in prediction mode to assess correlation with parsing degradation.  
- Question: How was the personalization weight of 5 chosen and were other values explored?  
- Question: Please provide technical details on how PageRank was modified to downweight stop words.  
- Question: Have you tried applying personalization to other POS (e.g., nouns or verbs)?  
- Question: Can you report POS tagging scores in prediction mode and their correlation with parsing performance?  
- Question: Did you explore alternative parsing approaches, such as machine learning or additional linguistic features?
