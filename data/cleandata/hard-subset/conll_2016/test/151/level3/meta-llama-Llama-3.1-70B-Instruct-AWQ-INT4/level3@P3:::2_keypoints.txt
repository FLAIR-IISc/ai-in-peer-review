- Proposed UDP: an unsupervised dependency parser using PageRank plus a small set of hard-coded rules that requires no training.  
- UD compliance: the algorithm is designed to produce Universal Dependencies parses.  
- Evaluation: tested on 32 languages and shown competitive with a delexicalized transfer system.  
- Domain robustness: claimed to be robust to domain change and less affected by deteriorating POS tags than delexicalized transfer.  
- Well-written: paper is clear and explains the intuition behind the approach.  
- Substantial contribution: significant addition to unsupervised parsing research with cross-language/domain potential.  
- Future work potential: authors propose extending the parser to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names.  
- Competitive performance: achieves strong results on most languages and outperforms a multi-source delexicalized parser for Hindi.  
- Limited evaluation: only 32 languages evaluated, leaving generalization to more languages/domains uncertain.  
- POS dependence: parser relies on POS tags, which may be unreliable for low-resource languages.  
- Limited linguistic coverage: current UDP does not handle MWEs, coordination, or proper names well.  
- Request for clarification: how will UDP be extended to handle MWEs, coordination, and proper names?  
- Request for clarification: provide more details on domain-robustness experiments (which domains and languages).  
- Request for clarification: how will authors address POS-dependence and limited handling of linguistic phenomena?  
- Overall: innovative approach with promise but needs further evaluation and refinement.
