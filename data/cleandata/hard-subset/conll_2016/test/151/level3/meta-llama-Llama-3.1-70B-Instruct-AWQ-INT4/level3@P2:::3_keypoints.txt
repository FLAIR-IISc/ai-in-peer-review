- Summary: Proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using a modified PageRank and a few head-dependent rules, requiring no training data and estimating adposition direction from test data; evaluated on 32 languages vs a delexicalized transfer baseline.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel unsupervised dependency parsing approach that leverages UD constraints.

- Contribution 2: Develops a modified personalized PageRank algorithm to boost the main predicate.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates effectiveness across many languages, including low-resource ones.

- Strength 1: Novel approach that is competitive with supervised methods.

- Strength 2: Personalized PageRank is a useful innovation that improves performance.

- Strength 3: Thorough multilingual evaluation, including low-resource languages.

- Weakness 1: Using PageRank for unsupervised parsing is not new and closely mirrors Søgaard (2012).

- Weakness 2: The personalization weight of 5 seems arbitrary and is not justified.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation presentation could benefit from visualizations (charts) for clarity.

- Weakness 4: POS tagging scores in prediction mode are not reported, obscuring whether parsing drops stem from tagging errors.

- Question 1: Please justify the choice of personalization weight = 5.

- Question 2: How will the parser be extended to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?

- Question 3: Provide more implementation details on the PageRank modification and personalization.

- Question 4: How will you address parsing degradation in low-resource languages?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but has limited novelty and needs more justification for hyperparameters and better result visualization.
