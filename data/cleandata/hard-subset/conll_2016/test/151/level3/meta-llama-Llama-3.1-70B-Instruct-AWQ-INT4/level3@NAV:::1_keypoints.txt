- Proposes UDP, a deterministic unsupervised dependency parser that requires no training data.
- Uses POS-based head rules and a biased PageRank over allowed dependency edges to rank words and assign heads while preserving tree structure and directionality.
- Evaluated across 32 languages and compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer (state-of-the-art supervised) system.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel training-free dependency parsing algorithm relevant to low-resource settings.
- Contribution: Combines POS-based head rules with biased PageRank as a unique approach to dependency parsing.
- Contribution: Provides a large-scale multilingual evaluation that highlights strengths and weaknesses.

- Strength: Novel and interesting approach to the challenging problem of unsupervised dependency parsing.
- Strength: Thorough multilingual evaluation and direct comparison to a strong supervised baseline.
- Strength: Offers detailed performance analysis, including error analysis and result discussion.

- Weakness: Performance does not substantially outperform the state-of-the-art supervised system, limiting practical impact.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of the POS-based head rules and their effect on performance.
- Weakness: Evaluation is restricted to unlabeled attachment score, which may not fully capture parser behavior.

- Question: Please provide a more detailed analysis of the head rules and their impact on results.
- Question: How will the algorithm be extended to handle complex phenomena like multiword expressions and coordination?
- Question: How does UDP compare to other unsupervised dependency parsing algorithms?

- Comment: The paper is well-written and clearly explains the algorithm and evaluation.
- Comment: References are comprehensive and helpful for contextualizing the work.
- Comment: The paper would benefit from more detailed performance analysis and expanded error discussion.
