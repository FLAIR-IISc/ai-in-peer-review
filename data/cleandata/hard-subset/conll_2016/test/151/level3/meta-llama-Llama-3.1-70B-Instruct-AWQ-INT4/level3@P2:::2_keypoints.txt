- Paper presents UDP: an unsupervised dependency parser using PageRank + small set of head rules, requires no training and is competitive with delexicalized transfer.
- Evaluated on 32 languages in three POS-reliability setups and shows increased stability to domain change versus delexicalized transfer.

- Contribution: unsupervised dependency parsing without any training data.
- Contribution: novel use of PageRank combined with hard-coded dependency head rules.
- Contribution: demonstrated robustness to domain change compared to a supervised delexicalized transfer parser.

- Strength: novel and promising unsupervised parsing approach.
- Strength: competitive performance versus a supervised delexicalized transfer baseline.
- Strength: more resilient to domain shifts than the compared supervised system.
- Strength: well-written paper that clearly explains the added prior information.
- Strength: identifies promising directions for future work.

- Weakness: limited evaluation scope (only 32 languages).
- Weakness: heavy dependence on POS tags, which may be noisy or unavailable.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other unsupervised parsers.
- Weakness: no detailed error analysis provided.

- Question: how will the system handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?
- Question: can the authors provide a detailed error analysis to clarify strengths and failures?
- Question: how will the method address POS tag accuracy and availability issues?
- Question: can the authors compare UDP to other unsupervised parsers in the field?
- Question: how will UDP be adapted for languages with different word orders or grammatical structures?
