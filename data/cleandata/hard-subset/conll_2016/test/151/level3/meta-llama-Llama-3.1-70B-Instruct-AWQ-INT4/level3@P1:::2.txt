**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an unsupervised, training-free algorithm for Universal Dependencies parsing, called UDP. The algorithm uses PageRank on words combined with a small set of hard-coded rules to perform parsing. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by evaluating it on 32 languages and comparing it to a supervised cross-lingual system. The results show that UDP is a competitive system, achieving an average difference of 6.4% compared to the supervised system, and even outperforming it on one language. The authors also evaluate the robustness of UDP to domain changes and show that it is less affected by deteriorating POS tags and more resilient to domain changes compared to the supervised system.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unsupervised, training-free algorithm for Universal Dependencies parsing**: The authors propose a novel approach to unsupervised parsing that uses PageRank and a small set of hard-coded rules to perform parsing without requiring any training data.
2. **Effective use of prior information**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of incorporating prior information, such as the PageRank algorithm and the hard-coded rules, to improve the performance of the parser.
3. **Robustness to domain changes**: The authors evaluate the robustness of UDP to domain changes and show that it is less affected by deteriorating POS tags and more resilient to domain changes compared to the supervised system.

**Strengths**

**Weaknesses**

3. **Limited handling of complex sentences**: The authors' approach may not be effective in handling complex sentences or sentences with multiple clauses.
