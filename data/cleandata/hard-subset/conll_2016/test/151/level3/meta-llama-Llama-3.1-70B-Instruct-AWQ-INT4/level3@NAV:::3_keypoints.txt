- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using only POS tags and a modified personalized PageRank.
- UDP requires no training data and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system.
- Evaluated on 32 languages and shown to be robust to domain change across languages.
- Main contribution: an unsupervised dependency parsing method that eliminates the need for labeled training data.
- Main contribution: incorporation of a personalized PageRank variant to improve main predicate identification.
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration of robustness to domain shift, sometimes outperforming delexicalized transfer.
- Strength: achieves competitive parsing performance across a wide set of languages.
- Strength: exhibits notable robustness to domain change compared to data-driven approaches.
- Strength: completely unsupervised approach is a valuable resource-light contribution.
- Weakness: limited novelty, closely related to Søgaard (2012).
- Weakness: personalization weight of 5 is arbitrary and not justified.
- Weakness: paper lacks visualizations (charts) to aid result interpretation.
- Weakness: does not report POS tagging scores in prediction mode, hindering analysis of parsing vs tagging degradation.
- Question: justification for choosing personalization weight = 5?
- Question: how does UDP perform on languages with non-standard word order or unusual grammatical structures?
- Question: details requested about the development set used to tune PageRank parameters and function-word directionality.
