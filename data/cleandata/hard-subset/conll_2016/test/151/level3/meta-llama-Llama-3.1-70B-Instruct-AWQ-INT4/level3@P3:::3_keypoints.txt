- UDP is an unsupervised dependency parser using only POS tags, UD constraints, and a modified PageRank; evaluated on 32 languages against a multi-source delexicalized system.

- Main contribution: an unsupervised parsing method needing no training data, relying solely on POS and UD constraints.

- Main contribution: a modified PageRank that downweights stop words and uses personalization to boost the main predicate.

- Main contribution: a cross-lingual evaluation across 32 languages comparing to delexicalized transfer.

- Strength: achieves competitive performance on most UD languages and outperforms the multi-source delexicalized parser on Hindi.

- Strength: more robust to domain changes and to deteriorating POS tag quality.

- Strength: simple, linguistically motivated rules make it a strong, interpretable baseline.

- Weakness: limited novelty, largely resembling Søgaard (2012).

- Weakness: personalization weight of 5 is arbitrary and unjustified.

- Weakness: paper lacks visualizations to make results easier to interpret.

- Weakness: does not report POS tagging scores in prediction mode, obscuring correlation between tagging and parsing degradation.

- Question: please justify the choice of personalization weight 5 and report results for other values.

- Question: how will the parser be extended to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?

- Question: provide details on the development set used to tune PageRank parameters and function-word directionality.

- Additional comment: include a more detailed comparison with Søgaard (2012) to clarify differences and contributions.

- Additional comment: improve evaluation by adding more languages and domains to better test robustness.

- Additional comment: consider exploring other constraints or formalisms beyond assuming UD constraints are sufficient.
