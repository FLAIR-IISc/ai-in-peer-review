- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised, training-free UD parsing algorithm using PageRank on words plus a small set of hard-coded rules, evaluated on 32 languages.  
- Shows UDP is competitive with a supervised cross-lingual system, with an average gap of 6.4% and outperforming it on one language.  
- Claims UDP is more robust to domain changes and deteriorating POS tags than the supervised system.  
- Main contribution: a novel training-free parsing approach combining PageRank and linguistic priors.  
- Main contribution: effective incorporation of prior information improves unsupervised parsing performance.  
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration of robustness to domain shifts.  
- Strength: novel, simple approach to unsupervised parsing that requires no training data.  
- Strength: clear and detailed exposition of the intuition and methods.  
- Strength: effective use of priors (PageRank and rules) yielding promising results across languages.  
- Strength: demonstrated robustness to domain changes compared to a supervised baseline.  
- Strength: identifies promising directions for future work in unsupervised parsing.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to 32 languages, possibly not representative of global language diversity.  
- Weakness: reliance on a small set of hard-coded rules that may not generalize across languages or domains.  
- Weakness: potential difficulty handling complex sentences or multi-clause structures.  
- Question: how will the method be extended to handle complex or multi-clause sentences?  
- Question: how were the hard-coded rules selected and validated?  
- Question: how will robustness to domain changes be evaluated more comprehensively?  
- Question: what is the computational complexity and how does it compare to other unsupervised parsers?  
- Question: how could additional prior information or features be incorporated to improve performance?
