**Summary**

The paper proposes an unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm, UDP, that uses only part-of-speech (POS) tags and a modified PageRank algorithm to parse Universal Dependencies (UD) treebanks. The algorithm relies on explicit POS constraints imposed by UD and uses a small set of specific dependency head rules. The authors evaluate UDP on 32 languages and compare its performance to a multi-source delexicalized transfer system.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unsupervised dependency parsing**: The paper presents an unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm that requires no training data and relies solely on POS tags and UD constraints.
2. **Modified PageRank algorithm**: The authors propose a modified PageRank algorithm that downweights stop words and uses personalization to boost the main predicate.
3. **Cross-lingual evaluation**: The paper evaluates UDP on 32 languages and compares its performance to a multi-source delexicalized transfer system.

**Strengths**

1. **Competitive performance**: UDP achieves competitive performance on all but two UD languages, and even beats a multi-source delexicalized parser on Hindi.
2. **Robustness to domain changes**: The paper shows that UDP is less affected by deteriorating POS tags and is more resilient to domain changes.
3. **Simple and linguistically sound**: The algorithm is simple and relies on linguistically sound rules, making it a robust baseline for unsupervised dependency parsing.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited novelty**: The idea of using PageRank for unsupervised dependency parsing is not new, and the paper closely mirrors Søgaard (2012).
2. **Arbitrary personalization weight**: The chosen personalization weight of 5 appears arbitrary and unjustified.
3. **Lack of visualizations**: The paper would benefit from visualizations (charts) to make the results easier to interpret.
4. **No POS tagging scores**: The paper does not report POS tagging scores in prediction mode, making it difficult to assess whether parsing degradation correlates with tagging degradation.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you justify the choice of personalization weight 5 and explore other values to see if they improve performance?
2. How do you plan to extend the parser to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?
3. Can you provide more details on the development set used to tune PageRank parameters and function-word directionality?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison with Søgaard (2012) to highlight the differences and contributions of the current work.
2. The evaluation setup could be improved by including more languages and domains to test the robustness of UDP.
3. The paper assumes that the UD constraints are sufficient for unsupervised dependency parsing, but it would be interesting to explore other constraints or formalisms.
