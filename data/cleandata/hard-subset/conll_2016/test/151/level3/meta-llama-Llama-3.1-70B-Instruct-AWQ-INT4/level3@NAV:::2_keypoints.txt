- Proposes UDP: an unsupervised, training-free UD parser using PageRank on words plus hard-coded linguistic rules.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages and shown competitive with a delexicalized transfer system.  

Main contributions:
- Novel unsupervised dependency parsing method requiring no training data.  
- Uses PageRank to estimate content-word relevance for tagging.  
- Shows greater robustness to domain change than delexicalized transfer.  

Strengths:
- Competitive performance across most UD languages, outperforming a multi-source delexicalized parser on Hindi.  
- Less affected by degrading POS tags and more resilient to domain shifts.  
- Simple, linguistically motivated rules that avoid the need for training.  

Weaknesses:
- Fails to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names.  
- Uses an over-simplified POS tagging approach that may not generalize.  
- Evaluation limited in language and domain coverage.  

Questions for authors:
- How can UDP be extended to handle MWEs, coordination, and proper names?  
- Would more sophisticated POS tagging improve the algorithm?  
- Can evaluation be broadened to more languages and domains to better demonstrate robustness?
