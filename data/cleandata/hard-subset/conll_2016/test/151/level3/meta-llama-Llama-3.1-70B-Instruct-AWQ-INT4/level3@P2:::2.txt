**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents UDP, an unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies (UD) that uses PageRank and a small set of specific dependency head rules. The parser requires no training data and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system. The authors evaluate their system on 32 languages in three setups, depending on the reliability of available POS tags, and compare it to a multi-source delexicalized transfer system. The results show that UDP is a solid and competitive system for all UD languages, with increased stability to domain change across languages.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unsupervised dependency parsing without training**: The authors propose a novel approach to unsupervised dependency parsing that requires no training data, making it a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Use of PageRank and hard-coded rules**: The authors' use of PageRank and a small set of hard-coded rules to parse UD languages is a substantial contribution to unsupervised parsing research.
3. **Robustness to domain change**: The authors demonstrate that their system is more resilient to domain changes than a supervised delexicalized transfer parser, which is a significant advantage in real-world applications.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to unsupervised parsing**: The authors' approach to unsupervised parsing is innovative and shows promise for future research in the field.
2. **Competitive performance**: The authors' system is competitive with a supervised delexicalized transfer parser, which is a significant achievement for an unsupervised system.
3. **Robustness to domain change**: The authors' system is more resilient to domain changes than a supervised delexicalized transfer parser, which is a significant advantage in real-world applications.
4. **Well-written and detailed paper**: The paper is well-written and clearly explains the intuition behind the added prior information, making it easy to follow and understand.
5. **Promising possibilities for future work**: The authors present promising possibilities for future work that could spur additional research in the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their system on 32 languages, which may not be representative of all languages.
2. **Dependence on POS tags**: The authors' system relies heavily on POS tags, which may not always be accurate or available.
3. **No comparison to other unsupervised parsers**: The authors do not compare their system to other unsupervised parsers, which makes it difficult to evaluate its performance in the context of the field.
4. **No error analysis**: The authors do not provide a detailed error analysis of their system, which would be helpful in understanding its strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions to Authors**
