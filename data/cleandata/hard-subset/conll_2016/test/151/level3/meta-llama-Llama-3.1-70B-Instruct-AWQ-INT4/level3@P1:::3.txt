**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an unsupervised parsing algorithm, UDP, for Universal Dependencies (UD) treebanks. The algorithm relies solely on part-of-speech (POS) tags and a modified PageRank algorithm to estimate the relevance of content words in a sentence. The parser requires no training data and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system. The authors evaluate their system on 32 languages and demonstrate its robustness to domain change across languages.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Unsupervised parsing for Universal Dependencies**: The authors propose a novel approach to parsing UD treebanks without requiring any training data. This is a significant contribution, as most existing parsing algorithms rely on large amounts of annotated data.
2. **Modified PageRank algorithm**: The authors modify the PageRank algorithm to downweight stop words and boost the main predicate, which improves the accuracy of the parser.
3. **Robustness to domain change**: The authors demonstrate the robustness of their parser to domain change across languages, which is an important aspect of parsing in multilingual settings.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Competitive performance**: The authors demonstrate that their parser is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system, which is a state-of-the-art approach in cross-lingual parsing.
2. **Robustness to domain change**: The authors show that their parser is robust to domain change across languages, which is an important aspect of parsing in multilingual settings.
3. **Simple and linguistically sound approach**: The authors propose a simple and linguistically sound approach to parsing UD treebanks, which is easy to understand and implement.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited novelty**: The idea of using a modified PageRank algorithm for parsing is not new and closely mirrors the work of Søgaard (2012). The authors could have explored more innovative approaches to parsing.
2. **Under-explored personalization**: The authors only apply personalization to the main predicate, which may not be sufficient to capture the complexity of sentence structures.
3. **Arbitrary personalization weight**: The authors choose a personalization weight of 5 without justification, which may not be optimal for all languages or sentence structures.
4. **Lack of visualizations**: The authors could have included visualizations, such as charts, to make the results easier to interpret.
5. **No POS tagging scores**: The authors do not report POS tagging scores in prediction mode, which would have helped to understand whether parsing degradation correlates with tagging degradation.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How did you choose the personalization weight of 5, and have you explored other values?
2. Can you provide more details on how you modified the PageRank algorithm to downweight stop words?
3. Have you considered applying personalization to other parts of speech, such as nouns or verbs?
4. Can you provide more information on the POS tagging scores in prediction mode and how they correlate with parsing degradation?
5. Have you explored other approaches to parsing UD treebanks, such as using machine learning algorithms or other linguistic features?
