**Paper Review: Parsing for Universal Dependencies without training**

**Summary**

The paper presents an unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies (UD), called UDP, which uses PageRank and a small set of hard-coded rules. The algorithm requires no training data and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system. The authors evaluate UDP on 32 languages and demonstrate its robustness to domain change.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm**: The authors propose a novel unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm that uses PageRank and a small set of hard-coded rules to parse sentences.
2. **Universal Dependencies compliance**: The algorithm is designed to comply with the Universal Dependencies formalism, which provides a consistent representation of linguistic structures across languages.
3. **Robustness to domain change**: The authors demonstrate that UDP is robust to domain change, outperforming a delexicalized transfer system in some cases.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-written and detailed paper**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the intuition behind their approach.
2. **Substantial contribution to unsupervised parsing research**: The authors' contribution to unsupervised parsing research is substantial, and their approach has the potential to be applied to other languages and domains.
3. **Promising possibilities for future work**: The authors present promising possibilities for future work, including extending the parser to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names.
4. **Competitive performance**: UDP achieves competitive performance on all but two UD languages, and even beats a multi-source delexicalized parser on Hindi.
5. **Robustness to domain change**: The authors demonstrate that UDP is less affected by deteriorating POS tags than a delexicalized transfer system and is more resilient to domain changes.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors' evaluation is limited to 32 languages, and it is unclear how UDP would perform on other languages or domains.
2. **Dependence on POS tags**: UDP relies on POS tags, which may not always be accurate, especially for low-resource languages.
3. **Limited handling of linguistic phenomena**: The authors acknowledge that UDP does not handle certain linguistic phenomena, such as multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend UDP to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the robustness of UDP to domain change, including the specific domains and languages used in the evaluation?
3. How do the authors plan to address the limitations of UDP, including its dependence on POS tags and limited handling of linguistic phenomena?

**Additional Comments**

The authors' approach to unsupervised dependency parsing is innovative and has the potential to be applied to other languages and domains. However, further evaluation and refinement are needed to fully realize the potential of UDP.
