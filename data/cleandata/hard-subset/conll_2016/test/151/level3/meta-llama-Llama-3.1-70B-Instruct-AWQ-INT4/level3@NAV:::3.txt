**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies (UD) that uses only POS tags and a modified PageRank algorithm. The algorithm, called UDP, does not require any training data and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system. The authors evaluate UDP on 32 languages and show that it is robust to domain change across languages.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unsupervised dependency parsing**: The paper proposes an unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm that does not require any training data.
2. **Use of personalized PageRank**: The algorithm uses a modified PageRank algorithm that incorporates personalization to improve main predicate identification.
3. **Robustness to domain change**: The paper shows that UDP is robust to domain change across languages, outperforming a delexicalized transfer system in some cases.

**Strengths**

1. **Competitive performance**: UDP achieves competitive performance with a delexicalized transfer system on 32 languages.
2. **Robustness to domain change**: UDP is robust to domain change across languages, which is a significant advantage over data-driven approaches.
3. **Unsupervised approach**: The algorithm does not require any training data, which makes it a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited novelty**: The paper's approach is closely related to Søgaard (2012), with limited novelty.
2. **Arbitrary personalization weight**: The chosen personalization weight of 5 appears arbitrary and unjustified.
3. **Lack of visualizations**: The paper would benefit from visualizations (charts) to make the results easier to interpret.
4. **POS tagging scores**: The paper does not report POS tagging scores in prediction mode, which makes it difficult to assess the correlation between parsing degradation and tagging degradation.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more justification for the chosen personalization weight of 5?
2. How does the algorithm perform on languages with non-standard word order or grammatical structures?
3. Can you provide more details on the development set used to tune the PageRank parameters and function-word directionality?
