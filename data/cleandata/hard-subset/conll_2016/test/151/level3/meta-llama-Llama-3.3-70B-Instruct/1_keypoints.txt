- Introduces UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser that combines personalized PageRank with head-dependent rules to rank words and link each word to the closest allowed head under directionality constraints to form trees.

Contributions:
- Proposes a linguistically grounded unsupervised parser requiring no training data and competitive with delexicalized transfer systems.
- Integrates personalized PageRank for better main-predicate identification and a small set of POS-based head-dependent rules to constrain dependencies.
- Evaluates the parser on 32 languages, showing robustness to cross-domain variation.

Strengths:
- Achieves competitive performance on almost all UD languages despite being training-free.
- Uses a simple, linguistically sound rule-based approach that reduces sensitivity to sampling bias and domain shifts.
- Provides comprehensive evaluation across three POS setups and multiple domains.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty, building on prior rule-aided unsupervised parsing work (e.g., Søgaard 2012).
- Insufficient analysis of head rules' behavior relative to the corpus (e.g., rule frequency and component sizes when graphs disconnect).
- Unclear tie-breaking strategy for words with equal PageRank scores, which could affect parsing decisions.

Questions for authors:
- How will you address the perceived lack of novelty and what extensions would make the approach more distinct?
- Can you provide detailed analysis of head-rule usage, frequencies, and component sizes when rules fail to yield a connected graph?
- How is tie-breaking handled for equal PageRank scores, and what impact do you expect tie-breaking choices to have on performance?
