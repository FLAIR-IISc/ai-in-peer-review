- Summary: Proposes UDP, a fully unsupervised UD parser needing no training; uses UD constraints, personalized PageRank for predicate ID, adposition direction estimation from test data, and two-step decoding; evaluated on 32 languages and domain robustness.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates UD constraints alone can yield a competitive, training-free dependency parser.

- Contribution 2: Shows UDP is more robust to domain shift than a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser.

- Contribution 3: Adapts personalized PageRank to improve content-word identification and parsing accuracy.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results with competitive UAS across 32 languages and a solid baseline comparison.

- Strength 2: Well-motivated use of UD constraints as an alternative for low-resource scenarios.

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation across POS setups (gold, predicted, naive) and domains.

- Strength 4: Clear, well-written paper with helpful example runs.

- Strength 5: Parser and annotated datasets are publicly available for reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Individual components (PageRank, head rules, UD constraints) lack novelty; combination is the main novelty.

- Weakness 2: Tuning relies on English, raising concerns about performance on typologically distant languages.

- Weakness 3: Naive POS content/function split (UDPN) is oversimplified and may understate potential with better POS tags.

- Weakness 4: Head rules do not cover all POS combinations, causing fallbacks and limiting rule coverage.

- Question 1: Request for detailed comparison with existing rule-based dependency parsers to highlight specific innovations.

- Question 2: Ask about sensitivity of PageRank parameters and function-word directionality to language and alternative initialization strategies.

- Question 3: Seek clarification on the impact of limited head rule coverage and plans to address it.

- Question 4: Request results for Japanese using an available POS tagger even if word forms are missing.

- Additional comment 1: Recommend expanding error analysis to identify common error patterns and causes.

- Additional comment 2: Suggest clarifying notation in Figure 1 (define H and D upfront).

- Additional comment 3: Ask authors to include links to the publicly available parser and datasets.

- Overall verdict: Strong, well-executed paper with practical contributions and robustness merits acceptance.
