- Presents a truly unsupervised UD parser (UDP) using UD constraints and personalized PageRank, requiring no training data.  
- Achieves competitive performance with a delexicalized transfer parser across 32 languages and shows robustness to domain shift.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates that encoding linguistic constraints yields surprisingly effective unsupervised parsing.  
- Main contribution: effectively operationalizes UD formal constraints, especially content vs. function word distinction and function-word attachment.  
- Main contribution: shows UDP’s stability across domains, suggesting less overfitting than data-driven methods.  
- Strength: strong empirical results across diverse languages, often rivaling or exceeding a supervised transfer baseline.  
- Strength: clear, logical methodology including PageRank personalization and a two-step decoding with head-direction estimation.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation under multiple POS scenarios (gold, predicted, naive) and across domains.  
- Strength: insightful error analysis by POS and discussion of weaknesses (e.g., coordination).  
- Strength: reproducible work with parser and datasets made available.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in individual techniques (PageRank, rule-based parsing); novelty primarily in their UD application.  
- Weakness: reliance on English for parameter tuning may bias results and isn’t fully investigated.  
- Weakness: the "naive" content/function POS scenario is overly simplistic and may not reflect real POS tagger errors.  
- Weakness: error analysis lacks detailed breakdown of specific error types (e.g., modifier attachment, root misidentification).  
- Question: what challenges and language-specific adjustments arose for typologically different languages beyond ADP directionality estimation?  
- Question: have you tried mitigating English tuning bias with multilingual dev data or adaptive tuning methods?  
- Question: will you evaluate UDP on more diverse, real-world domains beyond available UD treebank splits?  
- Question: can you provide concrete examples and frequencies of the parser’s most common dependency errors?  
- Additional: paper is well-written, tables are informative, references comprehensive, and related work discussion thorough.  
- Overall: strong paper; valuable contribution demonstrating feasible training-free parsing — recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
