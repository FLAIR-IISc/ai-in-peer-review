- Presents an unsupervised dependency parser (UDP) that uses UD constraints and personalized PageRank for predicate ID, requiring no training data and performing competitively across 32 languages with robustness to domain shift.
- Contribution: demonstrates a practical, effective unsupervised parsing approach that leverages UD structure without training.
- Contribution: shows robustness to domain shift and to noisy (predicted) POS tags, suggesting better generalizability than data-hungry methods.
- Contribution: provides a comprehensive evaluation across 32 languages, indicating broad applicability for low-resource languages.
- Strength: strong empirical results, often approaching or surpassing delexicalized transfer baselines despite no training data.
- Strength: clear, linguistically motivated design (PageRank personalization, two-step decoding) with well-explained decisions.
- Strength: resilient to POS tagging errors, with minimal performance degradation compared to supervised baselines.
- Strength: thorough multilingual and domain evaluations, including in-house English datasets, enhancing credibility.
- Strength: practical impact and reproducibility—parser and test datasets are made available.
- Weakness: individual components (PageRank, rule-based parsing) are well-known, so novelty mainly lies in their combination; paper should better position itself relative to prior work.
- Weakness: binary content/function POS distinction is overly simplistic and likely limits performance in low-resource settings.
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis on specific linguistic phenomena (e.g., coordination, relative clauses) and explanations for observed failures.
- Weakness: dependency on English for parameter tuning (PageRank, function-word directionality) may introduce bias; needs more language-independent parameter selection.
- Question: please provide detailed error analysis on constructions where the parser struggles and explain common error patterns.
- Question: was PageRank personalization limited to the first verb compared to other strategies (all verbs, content words), and what were the results?
- Question: how sensitive is performance to English-optimized head rules, and could automatic rule discovery be used instead?
- Question: why choose the extremely coarse content/function POS split, and were more fine-grained unsupervised POS distinctions explored?
- Additional comments: some table captions are unclear; paper is well-written and comprehensively referenced.
- Overall: strong, well-designed paper with significant contribution to unsupervised parsing; recommended for acceptance.
