- Paper presents a fully unsupervised dependency parser (UDP) for Universal Dependencies that uses UD constraints and no training data.  
- Uses personalized PageRank (biased toward first verb/content word) to improve predicate/root identification.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages, compared to a delexicalized transfer baseline, and tested for domain robustness.  
- Key contribution: shows a rule-based UD-constrained parser can be competitive with supervised transfer methods.  
- Key contribution: effective, pragmatic use of personalized PageRank for root/predicate identification.  
- Key contribution: demonstrates greater robustness to domain shift than a delexicalized transfer parser.  
- Strength: strong empirical results — competitive UAS across many languages without training data.  
- Strength: principled design by explicitly encoding UD linguistic constraints to avoid spurious data-driven correlations.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful run example and well-defined experiments.  
- Strength: thorough robustness analysis validating cross-domain stability.  
- Strength: resources (parser and annotated tests) are openly released for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis identifying specific linguistic failure modes or error categories.  
- Weakness: simplistic head-direction heuristics (esp. for adpositions) relying on on-the-fly bigram frequencies.  
- Weakness: notable dependence on POS tagging accuracy; performance degrades with predicted tags.  
- Weakness: potential bias from tuning parameters on English development data rather than language-agnostic tuning.  
- Question: provide detailed error analysis by phenomena, POS, or dependency relation?  
- Question: have more sophisticated head-direction methods or small language-specific dictionaries been explored?  
- Question: how can the parser be made more robust to POS tagging errors (error correction, rule adaptation)?  
- Question: how sensitive are tuned parameters (PageRank weighting, function-word directionality) to the choice of development language?  
- Question: how are coordination and multiword expressions handled, and what is their impact on performance?  
- Additional comment: discuss limitations of the UD formalism and their effect on parser performance.  
- Additional comment: reference list has formatting inconsistencies (missing venue info).  
- Overall recommendation: strong, innovative work with minor addressable weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
