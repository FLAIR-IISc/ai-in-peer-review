- Summary: Proposes UDP, a training-free dependency parser using UD constraints, head rules, personalized PageRank, and test-time adposition direction estimation; evaluated on 32 languages against a delexicalized transfer baseline and domain shifts.

- Contribution: Shows a surprisingly strong unsupervised baseline competitive with delexicalized transfer methods.

- Contribution: Demonstrates effective exploitation of UD constraints (function-word handling, single-root trees) to enable parsing without training.

- Contribution: Shows UDP is more robust to domain shift than the transfer baseline, suggesting utility in low-resource settings.

- Strength: Strong empirical results across 32 languages, with UAS comparable to or exceeding the transfer system.

- Strength: Clear, concise methodology with a well-explained two-step decoder, PageRank use, and ablation studies.

- Strength: Solid linguistic motivation tying design choices to UD principles, yielding interpretability.

- Strength: Detailed robustness analysis across domains and POS tag reliability conditions.

- Weakness: Individual components (PageRank, rule-based parsing, POS tagging) are not novel; novelty is primarily in their UD-specific integration.

- Weakness: Heavy dependence on POS tag quality; performance degrades substantially with predicted POS tags.

- Weakness: Some rule engineering and language-specific choices (adposition/head directions, function word attachments), partly tuned on English dev data.

- Weakness: Limited, shallow error analysis—needs deeper analysis of systematically misparsed constructions.

- Question: Why was Japanese excluded and have other languages with unclear word forms or rich morphology been considered?

- Question: Why choose a PageRank teleport probability of 0.05; were alternatives tried and how did they affect performance?

- Question: How sensitive is performance to errors in language-specific head direction estimation; would a conservative default help?

- Question: Can you give examples of domain-specific parsing errors and explain how UDP avoids them relative to the transfer baseline?

- Comment: Paper is well-written and organized.

- Comment: Releasing code and data would improve reproducibility.

- Comment: Comparison to other unsupervised parsing methods (beyond delexicalized transfer) would strengthen evaluation.

- Comment: The term "leaves" for function words is technically incorrect—function words are dependents but not necessarily leaves.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed paper with impressive results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
