- Summary: Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using UD constraints and personalized PageRank, evaluated on 32 languages and showing competitive, domain-robust performance without training.

- Main contribution 1: Shows a strong unsupervised baseline by exploiting Universal Dependencies constraints.

- Main contribution 2: Adapts personalized PageRank to prioritize content words (esp. verbs) for root selection, improving accuracy.

- Main contribution 3: Demonstrates robustness to domain shift, often outperforming a supervised delexicalized baseline.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results across 32 languages, often beating supervised baselines especially with predicted POS tags.

- Strength 2: Clear, reproducible presentation with a logical two-step decoding algorithm and helpful examples/tables.

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation including gold vs. predicted POS tags and domain-shift analyses.

- Strength 4: Practical relevance for low-resource settings where labeled data is scarce.

- Strength 5: Emphasizes value of linguistic formalism (UD) over purely data-driven methods.

- Weakness 1: Individual components (PageRank, rule-based parsing, UD constraints) are not novel; novelty is mainly in their integration.

- Weakness 2: Performance depends on POS tagging quality and degrades with predicted POS tags.

- Weakness 3: Simplistic treatment of complex structures (coordination, relative clauses) limits coverage.

- Weakness 4: Error analysis is limited; more in-depth investigation of failure cases is needed.

- Weakness 5: Development and parameter tuning relied on English; generalization of parameters to other languages is unclear.

- Question 1: Request for detailed error analysis by dependency relation or POS combinations.

- Question 2: How sensitive are PageRank parameters across languages and could they be adaptively tuned?

- Question 3: How can the system be extended to better handle coordination and relative clauses?

- Question 4: Did alternative POS taggers get evaluated and how did they affect parsing performance?

- Question 5: How does UDP compare to other unsupervised parsers that do not use UD constraints?

- Additional comment 1: Code and data should be released for reproducibility.

- Additional comment 2: Include a table of POS tag accuracies per language for predicted tags.

- Additional comment 3: Clarify the “error propagation” calculation and its connection to POS accuracy.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated paper with practical impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses.
