- Paper presents UDP: a completely unsupervised UD parser using UD constraints (function words as leaves), personalized PageRank for predicate ID, rule-based dependencies, and estimated adposition direction, evaluated on 32 languages.  
- Main contribution: shows a surprisingly effective truly unsupervised parser competitive with delexicalized transfer parsing without training.  
- Main contribution: systematically integrates UD formalism constraints into the parser design.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates greater robustness to domain shift compared to a supervised baseline.  
- Strength: strong empirical results — competitive UAS across 32 languages with detailed appendix tables.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated approach with good explanations of PageRank personalization and head-direction estimation.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation covering gold/predicted/naive POS and domain-shift experiments.  
- Strength: high practical value for low-resource settings since no labeled training data is required.  
- Strength: well-written and accessible presentation with helpful figures.  
- Weakness: individual components (PageRank, head rules) lack novelty; contribution is mainly their integration.  
- Weakness: performance depends on POS tagging quality; notable drop with predicted POS tags.  
- Weakness: adposition direction estimation via bigram frequency is simplistic and may not generalize to complex languages.  
- Weakness: absence of detailed qualitative error analysis to identify frequent or systematic mistakes.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of which specific UD formalism aspects help or hinder the approach.  
- Questions: request detailed error analysis by language or relation and impact analysis.  
- Questions: ask about sensitivity to PageRank teleport probability and alternatives for setting it.  
- Questions: ask how multiword expressions and coordination would be handled in future work.  
- Questions: ask how adposition-direction estimation performs when adpositions are distant from their nouns.  
- Questions: ask about computational costs compared to the delexicalized transfer parser, especially on long sentences.  
- Additional comments: recommend discussing UD formalism limitations, prominently linking code/datasets, and improving Figure 1 notation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
