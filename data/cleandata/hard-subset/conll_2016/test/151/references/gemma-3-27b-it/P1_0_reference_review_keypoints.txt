- Paper presents a truly unsupervised UD parser using UD constraints and Personalized PageRank, evaluated on 32 languages with strong cross-domain stability.  
- Key contribution: effective parsing without any training data via UD constraints and PageRank.  
- Key contribution: empirical evidence that UD’s design supports unsupervised/rule-based parsing.  
- Key contribution: extensive multilingual evaluation and domain-robustness analysis against a strong baseline.  
- Strength: novel combination of UD constraints, Personalized PageRank, and two-step decoding.  
- Strength: competitive UAS scores versus delexicalized transfer and stable performance across languages/domains.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with a helpful detailed example.  
- Strength: thorough analysis (PageRank contribution, POS breakdown, domain robustness).  
- Strength: parser and annotated evaluation datasets are publicly available.  
- Weakness: lacks deep qualitative error analysis (specific dependency relations, long-distance errors).  
- Weakness: PageRank and head-directionality tuned on English; sensitivity to typologically diverse languages is unclear.  
- Weakness: CONTENT/FUNCTION POS split is overly simplistic and may not reflect realistic noisy POS scenarios.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to other recent unsupervised or weakly-supervised parsers.  
- Q to authors: provide detailed error analysis identifying recurring problematic relations/structures.  
- Q to authors: how sensitive is performance to PageRank parameters when tuned per-language versus on English?  
- Q to authors: any systematic performance patterns by language family or typological features?  
- Q to authors: can the approach be adapted for languages lacking explicit word segmentation (e.g., Japanese)?  
- Additional: paper aligns well with UD goals and balances positive/negative results.  
- Additional: include discussion of computational complexity, especially PageRank costs.  
- Overall: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
