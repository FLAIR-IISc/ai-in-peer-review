- Presents a training-free UD parser using UD constraints, head rules, personalized PageRank, and dynamic adposition attachment direction estimation.  
- Demonstrates strong, surprisingly effective parsing performance achieved solely through linguistic constraints.  
- Evaluated across 32 languages, showing competitive results and improved robustness to domain shift vs. a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline.  
- Clever combination of PageRank personalization and two-step decoding (content words first, then function words).  
- Novel contribution: a completely unsupervised parser with potential impact for low-resource languages.  
- Comprehensive evaluation (multiple languages, gold vs. predicted POS, domain-shift analysis) strengthens the findings.  
- Clear, well-justified methodology with linguistic motivation and an illustrative example run.  
- Resources (parser and datasets) are made available for reproducibility.  
- Error analysis is limited and lacks detailed breakdowns by dependency relation or POS tag.  
- System performance heavily depends on POS tagging quality, with notable drops for languages with poor POS accuracy.  
- No direct empirical comparison to recent unsupervised methods or simple baselines, making relative advantages unclear.  
- Scalability concerns due to reliance on hand-crafted rules for typologically diverse languages.  
- Asks for a more detailed error analysis identifying frequent error types and challenging relations/POS combos.  
- Questions sensitivity to PageRank teleport probability and its effect on performance.  
- Inquires about handling non-projective dependencies given the algorithm can produce them.  
- Requests evaluation on typologically divergent languages (e.g., free word order, ergative-absolutive).  
- Paper is well-written, well-referenced, and includes helpful algorithmic description and example.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses and questions.
