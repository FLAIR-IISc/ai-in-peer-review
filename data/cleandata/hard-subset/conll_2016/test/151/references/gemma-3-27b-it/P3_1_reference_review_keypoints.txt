- UDP is a fully unsupervised, rule-based UD parser using UD constraints and personalized PageRank, evaluated on 32 languages and competitive with delexicalized transfer parsers.
- Contribution: Demonstrates that a strong dependency parser can be built without training by leveraging linguistic constraints.
- Contribution: Effectively operationalizes UD formalism (function words as leaves, single-rooted trees) into a practical parsing system.
- Contribution: Shows robustness to domain shift compared to data-driven approaches.
- Strength: Strong empirical results across 32 languages, often matching or exceeding a reasonable transfer baseline.
- Strength: Clear, well-documented methodology (head direction estimation, two-step decoding) with code and data released.
- Strength: Thoughtful error analysis, including POS-based breakdowns and domain robustness evaluation.
- Strength: Particularly relevant for low-resource languages due to no training-data requirement.
- Strength: Well-situated in related work, distinguishing from grammar induction and cross-lingual transfer literature.
- Weakness: Potential scalability and complexity issues — PageRank and O(n × |C|) decoding may bottleneck long/complex sentences.
- Weakness: Performance depends on POS tagging quality; the use of TnT may be suboptimal for some languages.
- Weakness: Simplifications of UD (e.g., not modeling coordination/relative clauses) limit coverage of certain structures.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to stronger or recent unsupervised/neural parsing baselines.
- Question: What is the computational complexity of PageRank as sentence length grows, and could it be a bottleneck?
- Question: How would coordination and relative clauses be incorporated into the current framework and head rules?
- Question: How was the 5× weighting for main predicates in PageRank chosen, and how sensitive are results to this value?
- Question: How are ties handled in decoding when multiple words are equally "closest," and does this affect determinism/reproducibility?
- Question: Could small amounts of target-language POS adaptation improve performance on POS-predicted data?
- Comment: Paper is well-written with clear figures/tables and reproducible code/data.
- Comment: More detailed error analysis with concrete examples would be beneficial.
- Comment: Consider discussing ethical implications and potential biases from head rules or POS taggers.
- Overall: Strong, valuable contribution to unsupervised dependency parsing; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
