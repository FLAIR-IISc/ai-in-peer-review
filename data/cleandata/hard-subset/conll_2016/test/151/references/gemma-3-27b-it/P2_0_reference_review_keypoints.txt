- Paper presents a completely unsupervised dependency parser (UDP) for Universal Dependencies using UD constraints and personalized PageRank, requiring no training data.  
- UDP is compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser and shows competitive performance, especially across domains.  

Main contributions:
- Demonstrates a viable, competitive unsupervised parsing approach within the UD framework.  
- Shows practical exploitation of UD constraints (e.g., function words as leaves) to build a parser.  
- Provides evidence of robustness to domain shift compared to a supervised transfer baseline.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results: competitive UAS across 32 languages and outperforming the baseline in some cases.  
- Thorough evaluation across gold vs. predicted POS and multiple domains, including in-house domain datasets.  
- Clear, well-motivated design with convincing rationale for personalization of PageRank.  
- Code and datasets are made available, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Insightful error analysis with POS-breakdown and cross-domain consistency discussion.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty in individual components; novelty lies mainly in their combination.  
- High dependence on POS tag accuracy; performance drops noticeably with predicted tags.  
- Simplistic handling of linguistic phenomena like coordination and relative clauses.  
- Dense result tables that are hard to parse quickly; could use visual highlighting or clearer summaries.  

Questions for authors:
- How sensitive are head-rule choices to the development language; were other languages tried?  
- What are the characteristics of the POS taggers used per language (rule-based, statistical, neural, training data)?  
- Are there specific rule modifications that could improve coordination and relative clause handling without losing unsupervised status?  
- Why was 0.05 chosen as the PageRank teleport probability and was a systematic search over values performed?  

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written with comprehensive references.  
- Provided dataset link is useful.  

Overall recommendation:
- Recommend accept: combination of techniques, strong results, and thorough evaluation make this a valuable contribution despite limited component-level novelty.
