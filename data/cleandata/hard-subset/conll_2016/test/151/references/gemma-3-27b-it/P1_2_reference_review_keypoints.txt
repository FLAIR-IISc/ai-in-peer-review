- Paper presents an entirely unsupervised dependency parser (UDP) using only POS constraints, personalized PageRank, and dynamic adposition direction estimation, evaluated on 32 languages and domain shifts.

- Demonstrates a strong unsupervised baseline that is competitive in cross-lingual dependency parsing without training data.

- Effectively leverages UD constraints (e.g., function words as dependents) to achieve robust parsing without learned parameters.

- Shows greater robustness to domain shift compared to a delexicalized transfer baseline.

- Empirically strong results: competitive UAS across 32 languages, often outperforming delexicalized transfer, with reported statistical significance.

- Novel combination of techniques: personalized PageRank focused on content words plus a two-step decoding tailored to UD.

- Thorough evaluation: analyses by POS tag, domain robustness, and both gold and predicted POS settings.

- Clear presentation with helpful algorithm figures and examples; code and datasets promised to be released.

- Limitation in methodological novelty: core components (PageRank, rule-based parsing) are established methods combined rather than individually novel.

- Heavy dependence on POS tagging accuracy, limiting applicability to truly low-resource languages with poor taggers.

- Simplifying assumptions and rule limitations (e.g., handling of relative clauses) constrain handling of complex linguistic phenomena.

- Lacks detailed error analysis identifying which relations are most often misparsed and why.

- Question about PageRank personalization: whether alternative predicate identification methods (e.g., frequency heuristics) were tried and how they compare.

- Question about reducing POS dependency: potential for self-training, bootstrapping, or other approaches to mitigate POS tag errors.

- Question about extending the system to handle complex structures like relative clauses and possible approaches for that.

- Request for more granular error examples categorized by POS or dependency relation.

- Suggestion to publicly release the in-house annotated datasets alongside the parser code.

- Minor request to clarify notation for functions (γ, δ, κ) in the text.

- Overall recommendation: accept — valuable contribution establishing a strong unsupervised baseline, with noted weaknesses to address.
