## Review for "Parsing for Universal Dependencies without training"

**Summary:** This paper presents a completely unsupervised dependency parser (UDP) for Universal Dependencies (UD) that leverages the constraints imposed by the UD formalism, particularly the distinction between content and function words, and utilizes personalized PageRank for improved predicate identification. The system requires no training data and achieves competitive performance compared to a delexicalized transfer parser across 32 languages. The authors also demonstrate its robustness to domain shift.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Demonstration of a surprisingly effective, truly unsupervised parsing approach:** Achieving performance comparable to a delexicalized transfer parser *without any training data* is a significant result.  This highlights the power of encoding linguistic constraints within a parsing system.
2. **Practical application of UD constraints:** The paper effectively translates the theoretical benefits of UD (consistent representation, formalized constraints) into a working parsing system.  The explicit focus on function word attachment and leveraging the UD formalism is a key strength.
3. **Robustness to domain shift:**  The observed stability of UDP across different domains is noteworthy, suggesting the system is less prone to overfitting than data-driven methods, and demonstrates the benefit of constraint-based approaches.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The parser performs remarkably well across a diverse set of languages, often rivaling or exceeding the performance of a supervised transfer baseline (MSD) on the standard UD evaluation. This is a compelling demonstration of the approach's effectiveness. The extensive evaluation across 32 languages is also a major strength.
2. **Clear and Logical Methodology:** The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the methodology, including the use of PageRank personalization and the two-step decoding process.  The detailed description of head direction estimation is particularly valuable.
3. **Thorough Evaluation:** The authors go beyond standard UAS metrics by evaluating the system under various POS tagging scenarios (gold, predicted, naive) and analyzing its performance across different domains. This demonstrates a rigorous and thoughtful evaluation process.
4. **Insightful Error Analysis:** The discussion section provides valuable insights into the parser's strengths and weaknesses, analyzing performance breakdowns by POS tag and highlighting areas for future improvement (e.g., handling coordination).
5. **Reproducibility:** The availability of the parser and datasets facilitates further research and allows for independent verification of the results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Techniques:** While the combination of techniques is effective, the individual components (PageRank, rule-based parsing) are not novel. The key contribution lies in the *application* of these techniques within the specific framework of UD, but the paper could benefit from a more nuanced discussion of the novelty relative to existing work.  The authors state they go "beyond" existing rule-aided parsing, but the extent of that advancement isn't fully articulated.
2. **Dependency on English for Parameter Tuning:** The reliance on English data for tuning PageRank parameters and directionality rules introduces a potential bias.  While the authors acknowledge this, the impact of this bias on the performance of other languages isn’t fully investigated. Ideally, a more cross-lingually informed tuning process would be explored.
3. **Simplified POS Scenario:** The "naive" content/function POS scenario, while demonstrating robustness, is overly simplistic and may not accurately reflect real-world POS tagging errors.  The results from this scenario should be interpreted with caution. Exploring more realistic noisy POS scenarios (e.g., POS errors representative of typical tagger performance) would be more informative.
4. **Limited Discussion of Error Types:** While the paper discusses broad POS-based error analysis, a more detailed analysis of specific error types (e.g., incorrect attachment of modifiers, misidentification of the root) would provide a deeper understanding of the parser's limitations.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the specific challenges encountered when applying the system to languages with significantly different syntactic structures compared to English? Were any language-specific adjustments required beyond the ADP directionality estimation?
2.  You mention the potential for a bias introduced by tuning parameters on English data. Have you explored methods to mitigate this bias, such as using a small set of development data from other languages, or utilizing a more adaptive tuning process?
3.  The paper highlights the robustness of UDP to domain shift. However, the domain splits available in the UD treebanks may not fully represent the range of real-world text types.  Do you have any plans to evaluate the system on more diverse and challenging domains?
4.  Can you provide some examples of the types of errors the system makes, and discuss why these errors occur? For instance, what are the most frequent incorrect dependency relations produced by the parser?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and easy to understand.
*   The tables are informative and clearly presented.
*   The reference list is comprehensive and up-to-date.
*   The discussion of related work is thorough and provides good context for the presented research.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of unsupervised dependency parsing. The authors have successfully demonstrated the feasibility of building a competitive parser without relying on any training data, by leveraging the constraints of the Universal Dependencies framework. While the individual techniques are not entirely novel, the combination and application within this specific context are significant. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
