- Novel unsupervised parser (UDP) that leverages UD constraints (function words as dependents, single-rooted trees) and outperforms delexicalized transfer parsers on many languages.  
- Demonstration that a rule-based parser can be more robust to domain shifts, showing lower variance across domains than data-driven baselines.  
- Practical, language-agnostic implementation working competitively across 32 languages without requiring language-specific parameters.  

- Strong empirical validation across 32 languages and three POS scenarios; UDP with gold POS is only ~6.4% UAS behind multi-source delexicalized parser.  
- UDP shows significantly lower standard deviation across domains (2.5 vs 4.2 for gold POS), indicating robustness to domain shifts.  
- Clear methodological use of UD constraints: PageRank for content-word ranking and two-step decoding (content first, function second).  
- Practical utility: freely available, simple implementation, useful for resource-constrained settings without training data.  

- Limited methodological novelty: core approach (PageRank + rule-based parsing) largely builds on Søgaard (2012b), adaptation to UD may be incremental.  
- Misleading "no training" claim: English treebank data was used during development to tune parameters and head rules.  
- Missing comparisons to other unsupervised parsers (e.g., Klein & Manning 2004, Søgaard 2012b), making relative gains unclear.  
- Overly optimistic domain robustness claims without thorough analysis of failure cases or concrete examples.  
- Naive content/function POS scenario (UDPN) is unrealistic and of limited practical relevance despite being evaluated.  

- How much English treebank data was used for development and how does that affect the "no language-specific parameters" claim?  
- How frequent are relative clauses in UD treebanks and what is the accuracy impact of excluding the NOUN→VERB head rule?  
- Why does UDP outperform MSD on Hindi — dataset properties or language-specific phenomena?  
- Estimating adposition direction on test data: how is this different from having language-specific parameters and why is it acceptable?  
- Why exclude the NOUN→VERB head rule in development and what is its impact on parsing relative clauses and related constructions?  

- Overall comment: well-written and valuable work, but should clarify relation to prior unsupervised parsing approaches and be transparent about use of English development data.
