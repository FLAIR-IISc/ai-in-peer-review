- Rule-based UD parser that enforces UD constraints (single-root trees, function words as dependents).
- Personalized PageRank method to identify the main predicate.
- Two-step decoding algorithm that guarantees function words are leaves.
- Adposition direction estimated from test data via ADP-NOMINAL vs. NOMINAL-ADP bigrams.

- Effectively exploits UD constraints and head rules to build a simple, competitive unsupervised parser.
- Shows strong domain robustness with lower variance across genres compared to supervised transfer (MSD).
- Personalized PageRank substantially improves root selection and convergence (≈+9 UAS over ablation).

- "No training" claim is misleading because the English treebank was used for development/tuning.
- Evaluation ignores known variation in UD treebank annotation quality across languages.
- No direct comparison to established unsupervised parsers (e.g., DMV, Søgaard, Spitkovsky).
- Claim of being free of language-specific parameters is inaccurate—some attachment directions are fixed.
- Limited error analysis and failure cases (e.g., cannot handle relative clauses due to omitted NOUN→VERB rule).

- Request evidence or acknowledgment about unequal UD treebank compliance across languages.
- Clarify why UDPG outperforms MSDG on Hindi despite verb-final word order and first-verb bias.
- Explain exclusion of Japanese given UD_Japanese-GSD includes word forms.
- Specify how much English treebank data was used for development/tuning.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — valuable demonstration of constraint-based unsupervised parsing and domain robustness, but needs clearer training disclosures, broader comparisons, and more nuanced discussion of UD treebank limitations.
