- Introduces UDP: a completely unsupervised UD-compliant dependency parser using head-dependent rules, personalized PageRank, and on-the-fly adposition direction estimation; evaluated on 32 languages and three POS settings against a delexicalized transfer baseline.

- Contribution: Shows an unsupervised parser can be competitive with supervised transfer by exploiting UD constraints.

- Contribution: Provides a systematic adaptation of dependency parsing to UD (single-rooted trees, function words as leaves) without training data.

- Contribution: Empirically demonstrates rule-based approaches are more robust to domain shifts than data-driven methods.

- Strength: Strong empirical results — UDPG (gold POS) achieves 63.9% UAS vs. MSDG 61.2%, even outperforming MSDG on Hindi.

- Strength: Demonstrates robustness to domain shifts with lower std. dev. across domains (e.g., 2.5 vs. 4.2 for UDPG vs MSDG).

- Strength: Offers practical insights for the UD community on leveraging UD-specific constraints (head rules, PageRank for main predicate).

- Weakness: The “no training” claim is misleading because English treebank data was used in development/tuning.

- Weakness: Technical novelty is limited—core methods (personalized PageRank, head rules) are adaptations of prior work with relatively minor extensions.

- Weakness: Claims of language-independence are inconsistent since adposition direction is estimated per language from test data.

- Question: Please clarify the development phase with the English treebank—what exactly was tuned and how minimal was this “training”?

- Question: How would UDP perform if UD constraints were relaxed (e.g., allowing function words as heads)?

- Question: How does UDP handle verb-final languages like Hindi, given the PR personalization assumption about main predicate position?

- Overall: Strong, well-evaluated paper with valuable UD-specific contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the “no training” claim and better position the novelty.
