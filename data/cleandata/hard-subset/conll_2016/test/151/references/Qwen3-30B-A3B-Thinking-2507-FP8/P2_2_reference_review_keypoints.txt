- Summary: Introduces UDP, a dependency parser that requires no parser training and uses UD POS constraints, personalized PageRank for content words, function-word leaf constraints, and adposition direction estimated from test data; evaluated on 32 languages vs. a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser.

- Contribution 1: Shows a rule-based, training-free parser leveraging UD constraints can achieve competitive results with supervised transfer parsers and be robust across languages and domains.

- Contribution 2: Provides a systematic method for adapting UD formalism constraints (single-root trees, function words as dependents) to parsing.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates practical techniques (e.g., estimating adposition direction from test data) for cross-lingual parsing without language-specific training.

- Strength 1: Novel use of UD constraints to build a principled, language-agnostic parsing approach that avoids reliance on resource-rich languages or parallel corpora.

- Strength 2: Thorough evaluation across 32 languages, three POS scenarios, and multiple domains with convincing baseline comparisons and domain-robustness analysis.

- Strength 3: Practical implementation details showing UD constraints can be directly encoded in a parsing algorithm and handle common cross-lingual challenges.

- Weakness 1: Claim of "no training" is misleading because the pipeline depends on a trained POS tagger (TnT), undermining applicability to truly low-resource settings.

- Weakness 2: Performance claims are overstated; differences to the strong MSD baseline are modest and may lack statistical significance, yet are presented as major wins.

- Weakness 3: Limited analysis of key limitations (e.g., inability to infer relative clauses, very low PUNCT UAS) and their prevalence or impact across languages.

- Weakness 4: Contradiction between claiming language independence and using language-specific attachment directions for function words without sufficient explanation.

- Question 1: How do the authors reconcile the "no language-specific parameters" claim with the use of language-specific function-word attachment directions?

- Question 2: How would the full pipeline perform in truly low-resource settings where no POS tagger training data is available?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide concrete examples and frequency estimates of parsing errors caused by excluding rules like NOUN→VERB (relative clauses)?

- Question 4: What linguistic properties explain UDP's small domain advantages over MSD, and can those properties inform improvements in supervised methods?

- Overall assessment: Valuable and well-executed demonstration that UD constraints enable competitive rule-based parsing with strong domain stability, but overstated "training-free" claims and underanalyzed limitations weaken the contribution.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept — paper deserves attention but should clarify training requirements and more fully discuss limitations before final acceptance.
