- Summary: Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using only UD constraints, PageRank for predicate identification, UD head rules, and adposition direction estimated from test data; evaluated on 32 languages and three POS settings against a multi-source transfer parser.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a rule-based unsupervised parser leveraging UD constraints (single-rooted trees, function words as dependents) with no training data.

- Contribution 2: Proposes estimating adposition attachment direction directly from test data, avoiding language-specific parameters.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates that a simple UD-aligned rule-based system attains competitive UAS (≈6.4% gap) and greater domain robustness versus supervised transfer.

- Strength 1: Effectively leverages UD formal constraints to achieve cross-lingual consistency and strong unsupervised performance.

- Strength 2: Shows substantially superior robustness to domain shifts compared to supervised transfer methods, with lower variance across domains.

- Strength 3: Achieves competitive performance with minimal resources and no language-specific tuning, outperforming supervised transfer on some languages (e.g., Hindi).

- Weakness 1: Core method is incremental rather than novel, closely related to Søgaard (2012b) with only modest tweaks (PageRank personalization, two-step decoding).

- Weakness 2: Ablation lacks a clear separation between benefits from UD constraints and benefits from PageRank; no comparison to PageRank-only variants without UD constraints.

- Weakness 3: Limited analysis of which specific UD constraints drive performance and why the approach outperforms other unsupervised methods.

- Question 1: How much of the gain is due to UD constraints versus PageRank alone—was a PageRank-without-UD baseline evaluated?

- Question 2: How are UD edge cases handled where function words can be heads in some languages—were head rules modified for exceptions?

- Question 3: How does adposition-direction estimation behave on very short texts (<10–15 sentences)—is there a fallback and how does it affect performance?

- Recommendation: Accept — practical, well-evaluated contribution showing UD-aligned rule-based parsing is competitive and robust, despite incremental novelty.
