- Summary: Presents an unsupervised UD dependency parser using UD-specific POS constraints and personalized PageRank, evaluated on 32 languages and competitive with supervised transfer parsers.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces a principled unsupervised parser exploiting UD constraints without training data.  
- Contribution 2: Proposes a practical, unified method to handle language-specific differences by estimating adposition direction from test data.  
- Contribution 3: Shows rule-based parser is more robust to domain shifts than supervised transfer approaches.  
- Strength 1: Conceptual novelty in leveraging UD-specific constraints (function words as leaves, single-rooted trees) for parsing.  
- Strength 2: Strong multilingual empirical results across 32 languages and robustness across POS tag scenarios.  
- Strength 3: Demonstrated lower sensitivity to domain shift with smaller performance variance than supervised methods.  
- Strength 4: Practical implementation with thorough error analysis and clear two-step decoding algorithm.  
- Weakness 1: Inconsistent and inaccurate performance claims (e.g., reported 6.4% vs. actual 2.7% UAS gap; incorrect Hindi claim).  
- Weakness 2: Missing comparison to recent state-of-the-art unsupervised dependency parsers (e.g., Zhang et al. 2020, Wang et al. 2021).  
- Weakness 3: Limited and shallow analysis of failure modes (e.g., impact of not modeling relative clause attachments).  
- Weakness 4: Questionable and under-justified explanation/calculation of error propagation from POS to dependencies.  
- Question 1: Please clarify the claimed 6.4% average difference between UDPG and MSDG versus the 2.7% shown in Table 5.  
- Question 2: Please clarify the claim that UDPG outperforms MSDG on Hindi given Table 5 shows equal scores.  
- Question 3: How does the approach compare directly to recent unsupervised parsers (Zhang et al. 2020; Wang et al. 2021) and why would it outperform them?  
- Question 4: Please clarify the claimed 4% improvement over adjacency baselines in the two-POS scenario versus the much larger gap in Table 5.  
- Question 5: Provide a detailed derivation and justification for the reported error propagation rates (0.37 vs. 0.46).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on fixing performance inconsistencies and adding recent unsupervised comparisons.  
- Additional Comment 1: Expand discussion contrasting UD constraints with other dependency formalisms to motivate rule-based approach.  
- Additional Comment 2: Clarify improvements over Søgaard (2012b) and the relation to that prior work.  
- Additional Comment 3: Explain whether excluding Japanese is a UD treebank limitation or an approach limitation.  
- Additional Comment 4: Provide context for absolute performance levels to interpret relative gaps (e.g., 6.4% significance).  
- Additional Comment 5: Clarify abbreviations in Table 5 (e.g., UDPP, UDPN).  
- Additional Comment 6: Provide more details about the TnT POS tagger training setup used for predicted POS.
