- Introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD-constrained parser requiring no training data and relying only on UD constraints and POS tags.  
- Key contributions: UD-constrained rule-based parsing, adposition direction estimated from test data, and PageRank-based root identification.  
- Novel use of UD formalism to build a high-performing rule-based parser close to supervised performance (UDPG vs MSDG).  
- Practical method to infer adposition direction directly from test data, removing language-specific parameters.  
- Personalized PageRank for root selection significantly improves parsing over simpler heuristics.  
- Demonstrates strong domain robustness with lower variance across domains compared to MSD and significant domain analysis.  
- Clear implementation with open-source release and reproducible evaluation setup.  
- Missing comparisons to recent strong unsupervised parsers, relying instead on outdated baselines like Klein & Manning (2004).  
- Overstates "no training" claim because English treebank was used for development and tuning, undermining full language-independence.  
- Limited error analysis; unclear whether errors stem from POS tags, head-direction estimation, or UD constraint limitations.  
- Question: How would the parser perform on truly low-resource languages with no treebank used during development?  
- Question: Why were recent supervised and neural parsers excluded from comparisons, and how would UDP fare against them?  
- Question: How sensitive is adposition-direction estimation to the number of test sentences, and what is the minimum data needed for reliable estimation?
