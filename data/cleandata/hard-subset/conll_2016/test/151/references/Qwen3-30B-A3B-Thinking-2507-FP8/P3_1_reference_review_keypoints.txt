- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using only UD constraints, PageRank, and rule-based decoding without conventional training data.
- Evaluated on 32 UD treebanks across three POS scenarios, achieving average UAS 63.9% with gold POS and competitive performance vs supervised transfer.
- Contribution: demonstrates how UD constraints (function words as leaves, content words as heads) can be exploited for unsupervised parsing.
- Contribution: achieves strong performance with minimal assumptions—within 6.4% average UAS of supervised transfer and outperforming transfer on some languages.
- Contribution: shows greater robustness to domain shifts than data-driven parsers, with lower cross-domain variance.
- Strength: solid theoretical motivation linking UD conventions to rule-based parsing and clearly motivated head rules and two-step decoding.
- Strength: competitive empirical results (e.g., average UAS 63.9% with gold POS; notable performance on Hindi relative to baselines).
- Strength: convincing cross-domain evaluation demonstrating stability of rule-based UD-constrained parsing.
- Strength: provides implementable practical details (adposition direction estimation, personalized PageRank, two-step decoding).
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—approach largely builds on Søgaard (2012b) and modifications may not be framed as significant advances.
- Weakness: claim of “no training” is misleading because English treebank was used for development/tuning, implying some language-specific parameterization.
- Weakness: insufficient error analysis—missing detailed examination of failure cases (e.g., relative clauses, punctuation performance).
- Weakness: inadequate comparison to recent unsupervised parsing work, leaving unclear position relative to latest methods.
- Question: how would head-direction estimation scale to languages with no English dev data—can directionality be estimated without language-specific tuning?
- Question: why was NOUN→VERB head rule excluded, and how would including it affect performance on languages with relative clauses?
- Question: clarify discrepancy about Hindi results—UDPG reported lower than MSDG in Table 5, contradicting text.
- Question: how could the system be modified to better handle punctuation given low PUNCT UAS (~21%)?
- Additional: clarify distinction between UD (formalism) and UDP (parser) to avoid confusion.
- Additional: specify exact number of evaluated languages (32 mentioned but Japanese excluded—likely 31 actually evaluated).
- Additional: rephrase claims about "no training" to reflect minimal development tuning; clarify PageRank/root description and POS tagger training details.
- Additional: briefly explain what the UD validation script checks when claiming produced trees always pass it.
- Overall: solid, well-motivated paper with strong empirical and practical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty and “no training” claims.
