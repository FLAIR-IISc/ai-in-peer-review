- Presents UDP, a dependency parser requiring no training that leverages Universal Dependencies constraints with personalized PageRank and two-step decoding.  
- Evaluated on 32 UD languages across gold, predicted, and naive two-POS scenarios, compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser (MSD).  

Main contributions:
- Shows unsupervised parsing can be effective by systematically exploiting UD constraints.  
- Demonstrates rule-based UD-driven parsing is robust to domain shifts.  
- Implements UD constraints practically to build a working cross-lingual parser.  

Strengths:
- Large-scale, multilingual evaluation (32 languages) across realistic POS settings.  
- Strong evidence of cross-domain robustness with lower variance than supervised transfer.  
- Clever two-step decoding ensuring function words are leaves per UD.  
- Competitive performance without training data; close to supervised transfer in many cases.  
- Clear, well-motivated design choices and reproducible methodology.  

Weaknesses:
- Core techniques (PageRank, two-step decoding) are adaptations of prior work with limited novelty.  
- Limited error analysis and lack of concrete examples quantifying specific failure modes.  
- Uses UAS without discussing how UD constraints may affect metric interpretation.  
- Insufficient discussion of performance on languages with divergent word orders and English-centric development.  
- Lacks comparisons to other unsupervised parsers, making positioning in prior work unclear.  

Questions for authors:
- Clarify the reported Hindi result (UDPG 55.3 vs. MSDG 55.7) — is there a typo or different metric?  
- How were English-developed head rules and parameters validated for typologically different languages?  
- How robust is adposition direction estimation on very small test sets (<10–15 sentences)?  
- Quantify the impact of excluding the NOUN→VERB head rule and other structures the system struggles with.  
- How could the approach be adapted for languages without accessible word forms (e.g., Japanese)?  

Overall recommendation:
- Accept: valuable empirical contribution showing UD-constrained unsupervised parsing is practical and robust, pending revisions addressing analysis, comparisons, and language-specific issues.
