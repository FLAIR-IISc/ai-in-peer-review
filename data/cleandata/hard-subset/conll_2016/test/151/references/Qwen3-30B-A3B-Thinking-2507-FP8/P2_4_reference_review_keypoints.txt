- Presents a rule-based UD dependency parser requiring no training data by exploiting UD constraints (function words as dependents).  
- Key contributions: unsupervised parser, method to estimate adposition direction from test data, personalized PageRank for main predicate identification.  
- Strong application of UD formal constraints, leveraging the innovation that function words are leaves for unsupervised parsing.  
- Competitive performance: UDPG (gold POS) achieves 63.9% UAS, only ~6.4% below supervised MSDG, with robustness across languages.  
- Demonstrates superior domain robustness: much lower performance variance across domains than supervised systems.  
- Practical simplicity and transparency: no training required at inference, minimal parameters, open code and data, useful for low-resource languages.  
- Limited novelty in technical approach: PageRank and two-step decoding largely follow prior work (Søgaard 2012b); main novelty is applying UD constraints.  
- Overstated "no training" claim: English treebank data was used for development/tuning, making the claim technically inaccurate.  
- Insufficient error analysis: lacks quantification and examples for recognized limitations (e.g., handling relative clauses).  
- Limited evaluation scope: only 32 UD v1.2 languages evaluated, excludes some languages (e.g., Japanese) and doesn’t assess v2.0 or varied structures.  
- Question: Clarify the role of English development data and its impact on the “no training” claim.  
- Question: Explain the claimed outperformance on Hindi when published numbers show a tie (55.3% vs. 55.3%).  
- Question: How will function-word direction rules learned from English generalize to languages with different attachment patterns without language-specific tuning?  
- Overall assessment: A compelling, practical unsupervised parsing contribution with impressive robustness; merits acceptance after minor revisions to clarify novelty and the “no training” claim.
