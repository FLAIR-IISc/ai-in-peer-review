- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD parser requiring no training, using UD constraints, personalized PageRank to find predicates, and UD head rules to build trees.  
- Claims competitive performance vs supervised transfer parsers and greater robustness to domain shift.  

Main contributions:
- Shows a simple rule-based UD-constrained approach can achieve competitive parsing without training data.  
- Provides a broad robustness analysis across 32 languages and multiple POS scenarios.  
- Produces a parser applicable to many languages without training data.  

Strengths:
- Practical contribution for truly low-resource languages, achieving within ~6.4% of a supervised multi-source delexicalized parser.  
- Strong, thorough empirical evaluation across 32 languages, three POS scenarios, and multiple domains.  
- Demonstrates domain robustness (lower std dev across domains for UDP vs MSD).  
- Clear, well-explained methodology with an instructive example and effective two-step decoding enforcing UD function-word-as-leaf constraints.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: builds on Søgaard (2012b) PageRank idea and UD conventions; adaptations not clearly positioned as major advances.  
- Misleading language-independence claim: English dev data used to set function-word attachment directions, introducing language-specific bias.  
- Insufficient error analysis: lacks deep investigation into poor-performing languages (e.g., Arabic, Tamil).  
- Development data bias: tuning on English treebank may create English-centric behavior despite cross-lingual claims.  

Questions for authors:
- How were function-word attachment directions determined from English dev data, and how was potential cross-lingual bias evaluated?  
- How do you reconcile the use of English data with the claim of being "completely free of language-specific parameters," and what changes would make the system truly language-agnostic?  
- Why exclude the NOUN→VERB head rule, how would including it affect performance across languages, and why accept the trade-off?  
- How was the POS→dependency error-propagation calculation performed (0.37 vs 0.46), and does this indicate a fundamental advantage of UDP?  

Overall assessment:
- Well-executed paper with strong empirical results and practical impact; novelty and language-independence claims are overstated.  
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify novelty and address language-specific parameter usage.
