- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser that requires no training and uses UD constraints plus POS tags, with personalized PageRank for root detection and a two-step decoder.
- Main contribution: a practical unsupervised parser achieving competitive performance without training data, useful for low-resource languages.
- Main contribution: method to estimate adposition direction from test data (bigram frequencies), aiding truly low-resource scenarios.
- Main contribution: thorough evaluation across 32 languages and multiple domain shifts, showing constraint-based robustness to domain changes.
- Strength: demonstrates near-parity with a supervised cross-lingual parser (avg. UAS gap ~6.4%), showing practical value for low-resource settings.
- Strength: compelling evidence that UDP is more domain-robust than supervised MSD (lower across-domain std dev: 1.8 vs 4.5).
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across gold/predicted/naive POS scenarios and detailed POS-category error analysis.
- Weakness: inadequate comparison to recent state-of-the-art unsupervised parsers (e.g., CoNLL 2017), weakening claims about competitiveness.
- Weakness: insufficient justification for purportedly language-independent design—some attachment-direction choices were set using English dev data.
- Weakness: potential overfitting to UD formalism; unclear how UDP would perform on non-UD or non-conforming treebanks.
- Question: clarify the apparent typo or metric reference for UDP vs MSD on Hindi (Table 5 shows equal scores).
- Question: explain and provide evidence that attachment-direction choices (AUX/DET/SCONJ right; CONJ/PUNCT left) are truly language-independent.
- Question: clarify exclusion of Japanese from evaluation count—why excluded from main eval but included in overall language count.
- Additional: paper is well-written and technically clear, but stronger justification of language-independence is needed.
- Additional: domain robustness results are notable and should be better contextualized; recommend stronger comparisons with unsupervised baselines.
