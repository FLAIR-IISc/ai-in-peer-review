- Paper introduces UDP, a rule-based, training-free UD dependency parser using Personalized PageRank (PR) and UD constraints.
- Method: PR over a rule-induced multigraph to rank content words and identify the main predicate.
- Method: two-step deterministic decoding—attach content words to form a well-formed tree, then attach function words as leaves with directionality constraints.
- Method: test-time estimation of adposition (ADP) direction from unlabeled test text using simple bigram counts.
- Evaluated on 32 UD v1.2 languages under gold POS, predicted POS (TnT), and naïve content/function tags; compared to multi-source delexicalized transfer (MSDG/TurboParser) and a rule baseline.
- Contribution: a simple, language-agnostic, zero-training UD parser achieving competitive cross-lingual UAS (avg 57.5 UAS, within 6.4 points of MSDG; beats MSDG on Hindi).
- Contribution: thorough robustness analysis showing lower error propagation from POS noise and lower cross-domain variance than the supervised transfer baseline.
- Contribution: clear ablations isolating gains from PR personalization and the two-step decoding.
- Strength: strong cross-lingual performance with minimal assumptions and small UD-aligned rule set.
- Strength: UD-specific design (two-step decoding and head rules) enforces function-word leafness and single-rooted trees, passing UD validation.
- Strength: PR personalization substantially improves performance (≈+9 UAS from PR; +4 from decoding; personalization improves root accuracy).
- Strength: robust to POS noise and domain shift with smaller UAS drops and lower variance than MSD.
- Strength: deterministic, lightweight, transparent, reproducible design with broad language coverage and detailed analyses.
- Weakness: limited novelty over Søgaard (2012b); contributions are incremental UD-specific tweaks rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Weakness: potential test-set leakage from estimating ADP direction directly on test data; unclear whether estimation peeks at whole test set.
- Weakness: some rule directions tuned on English dev (AUX/DET/SCONJ/CONJ/PUNCT), causing bias and errors (e.g., Basque DET failure).
- Weakness: “without training” claim is misleading since predicted POS uses supervised per-language TnT; needs explicit clarification for fair comparisons.
- Weakness: coverage limits critical UD phenomena (e.g., excludes NOUN→VERB heads, weak coordination and punctuation handling); no LAS or non-projectivity stats reported.
- Question: how is ADP direction estimated (per-sentence, incremental, or on whole test set) and is it re-estimated per domain?
- Question: what proportion of attachments required backoff (dropping δ/κ) per language?
- Question: how sensitive is UDP to misestimated ADP direction (request for perturbation experiment)?
- Question: can DET/AUX/SCONJ/CONJ directions be estimated per language from unlabeled text to fix outliers like Basque?
- Question: what fraction of produced trees are non-projective compared to UD gold?
- Additional comment: tighten clarity—fix typos, define δ/κ/γ consistently, and align examples with rules.
- Additional comment: consider stronger baselines (UD head-rule baseline or UDPipe-style headfinder) and report runtime.
- Additional comment: report POS accuracies per language for TnT, include significance tests/confidence intervals, and compare directly to Søgaard (2012b) on overlapping data.
