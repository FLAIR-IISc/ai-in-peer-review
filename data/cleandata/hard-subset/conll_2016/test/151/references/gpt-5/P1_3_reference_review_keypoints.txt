- Proposes UDP, a rule-based, training-free UD parser using UD-compliant head–dependent rules.  
- Ranks content words and identifies the main predicate via personalized PageRank (PR).  
- Uses a two-step decoding that attaches content words first, then function words to enforce UD function-word leafness.  
- Estimates adposition (ADP) attachment direction from test data on the fly.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages with gold and predicted POS and multiple domain splits; achieves competitive UAS vs delexicalized TurboParser and lower domain sensitivity.

- Main contribution: a strong cross-lingual, no-treebank baseline that attains competitive UAS and robustness to POS noise and domain shift.  
- Main contribution: a simple UD-aware decoding combining PR over a rule-induced multigraph with two-phase decoding and ADP-direction estimation.  
- Main contribution: broad multilingual and multi-domain evaluation with ablations showing PR’s impact and POS→dependency error analysis.

- Strength: competitive cross-lingual and cross-domain performance; small UAS gap to supervised delexicalized parser and smaller relative drop under predicted POS.  
- Strength: decoding design tightly aligned with UD principles; two-step decode elegantly enforces function-word leafness.  
- Strength: PR is effective and supported by ablation (~9 UAS gain) and root-identification accuracy (~69%).  
- Strength: extensive evaluation across POS regimes and domains showing lower cross-domain variance than supervised baseline.  
- Strength: clear specification of rules and parameters, fast and reproducible approach.

- Weakness: limited novelty—approach closely follows Søgaard (2012b) with incremental differences (PR personalization, two-step decode, ADP estimation).  
- Weakness: “no training” claim overstated—several hyperparameters and head-rule choices tuned on English dev, and predicted-POS uses supervised taggers.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons—no evaluation against modern cross-lingual methods (multilingual embeddings/projection) or stronger unsupervised baselines.  
- Weakness: incomplete method details and sensitivity analysis—lacks PR parameter sensitivity, language-specific settings, head-rule coverage, and analysis of back-off frequency/effect.  
- Weakness: known linguistic limitations not quantified—cannot attach VERB under NOUN, poor coordination/punctuation performance, and no analysis of structural properties or labeled attachment.

- Question: which parameters and rule choices were tuned on English (head rules, function-word directions, PR teleport, personalization weight)?  
- Question: how frequently does decoding back-off drop δ/κ per language/POS, and does this correlate with errors?  
- Question: is ADP direction decided once per language/test set or adaptively; how many sentences needed for stable estimates and how handle mixed pre-/postposition languages?  
- Question: report runtime and PR convergence stats (iterations, failures, behavior on short sentences).  
- Question: can you compare to stronger multi-source baselines (multilingual embeddings or projection-based models) to better position UDP?  
- Question: can UDP be evaluated with projected or cross-lingual POS (no target-language POS training) to support the “no training” claim?

- Overall: paper provides a clean, practical UD-aware training-free baseline with strong empirical performance, but would benefit from stronger comparisons, clearer accounting of tuning, and deeper sensitivity/limitation analyses.
