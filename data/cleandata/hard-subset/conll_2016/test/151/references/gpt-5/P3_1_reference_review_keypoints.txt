- Paper introduces UDP: an unsupervised, rule-based UD parser using a small set of UD-compliant head rules and a two-step decoder (attach content words, then function words as leaves).
- Uses personalized PageRank to prioritize main predicates and guide head attachment among content words.
- Estimates adposition (pre/postposition) attachment direction from test data via ADP–NOMINAL bigram frequencies.
- Evaluated on 32 UD v1.2 languages under three POS-tagging scenarios, compared to a multi-source delexicalized TurboParser baseline, with ablations and cross-domain analysis.
- Contribution: a simple zero-training parser leveraging UD constraints, personalization, and a deterministic two-phase decoding.
- Contribution: thorough multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing competitive UAS and greater robustness to domain shifts than the learned baseline.
- Contribution: lightweight, test-time ADP direction estimation removing language-specific parameters while maintaining accuracy.
- Strength: strong exploitation of UD design (content/function distinction, single root, function words as dependents) enforced by the two-step decoding.
- Strength: competitive multilingual results (within ~6 UAS points of transfer baseline with gold POS, surpassing for some languages) and lower degradation with predicted POS.
- Strength: clear ablations (e.g., UDPNoPR) demonstrating the contribution of personalized PageRank (~9 UAS points) and useful diagnostics (root accuracy, ADP success, POS-wise breakdown).
- Strength: minimal language-specific tuning and attractiveness for low-resource settings since no treebank or parallel data are required.
- Strength: authors intend to release code and test splits, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to Søgaard (2012b); personalization and two-step decoding are incremental innovations and need direct comparison to Søgaard’s setup.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on POS quality; the “predicted POS” setting uses target-language training (not realistic for low-resource) and the UDPN split is too crude.
- Weakness: test-time ADP estimation and oracle global direction for baseline peek at test data (transductive/oracular choices) and fairness concerns; non-oracle results requested.
- Weakness: limited head-rule coverage omits important constructions (e.g., NOUN→VERB, coordination), and forcing all function words to be leaves conflicts with MWEs and some UD cases, limiting ceiling.
- Weakness: baselines and evaluation choices need updating—delexicalized transfer has improved, punctuation-included UAS reduces comparability, and significance testing is missing.
- Question: report ADP directionality estimated on a held-out slice instead of full test set and remove the oracle global direction from the baseline; assess sensitivity to ADP misestimation.
- Question: evaluate UDP with realistic low-resource POS (projected or multilingual taggers) and report UAS as a function of POS accuracy.
- Question: provide language-wise breakdown of the effect of removing PR personalization (first-verb heuristic), especially for verb-final languages.
- Question: report UAS without punctuation, projective vs. non-projective breakdowns, and some labeled evaluations to gauge extensibility.
- Question: report number of PR iterations, typical per-sentence runtime, and sentence-length sensitivity.
- Additional: clarify exact PR graph edge set and whether direction matters; contrast graph construction with Søgaard (2012b) visually.
- Additional: investigate surprising results (e.g., high Estonian BLG) via qualitative inspection to build trust.
- Minor: note that UD’s “function words are dependents” does not strictly imply leafness; clarify this nuance and motivate future work on MWEs/coordination.
