Summary and contributions
This paper proposes an unsupervised, training-free dependency parser for Universal Dependencies (UD) that uses (i) a small set of POS-based head rules, (ii) a personalized PageRank (PR) scheme to prioritize content words and identify the main predicate, and (iii) a two-step decoding procedure that attaches content words first and then forces function words to be leaves, consistent with UD conventions. Adposition attachment direction is estimated at test time from POS bigrams. The parser is evaluated on 32 UD v1.2 languages under gold and predicted POS, and under domain shifts, and compared primarily to a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline (MSD).

As I see it, the main contributions are:
1) A simple, language-agnostic, rule-based UD parser that is competitive with delexicalized transfer on UAS across many languages, with strong robustness to domain shift.
2) A pragmatic use of personalized PageRank plus a two-stage decoding regime to enforce UD-inspired constraints, with ablations indicating PR is a substantial contributor.
3) A test-time estimation of adposition direction from unannotated test POS sequences, removing language-specific directionality parameters.

Strengths
- Broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation: The paper evaluates on 32 UD languages and multiple domains (including curated splits). The macro-level comparison of standard deviations across domains is informative; UDP consistently exhibits lower variance than MSD, supporting the claim of higher robustness under domain shift.

- Competitive performance without training: With gold POS, UDP trails MSD by ~6.4 UAS on average and even outperforms MSD on Hindi. Given the simplicity and lack of supervised parsing training, this is noteworthy. The predicted-POS results show a mild degradation relative to gold POS (2.2%), smaller than MSD’s (2.7%), with a useful error-propagation analysis.

- Clear algorithmic design grounded in UD constraints: The two-phase decoding (content first, function second) enforces the UD principle that function words are dependents and often leaves. The attachment search with backoffs is simple and yields trees by construction. The ADP direction heuristic is sensible and converges quickly.

- Ablations that isolate PR’s contribution: The comparison between UDP, UDPNoPR, and BL suggests PR yields a substantial gain (~9 UAS), while decoding/tree constraints add ~4 UAS over a rules-only baseline. This improves interpretability of where gains come from.

Weaknesses
- Novelty is limited relative to prior work: Søgaard (2012b) already explored unsupervised parsing without training using PR. The incremental additions here—PR personalization toward the first verb/content word and a two-step decoding to enforce UD-style leafness—are sensible but modest. The reliance on a very small set of POS head rules is not new per se. The paper should more rigorously position its contributions relative to Søgaard (2012b) and related rule-aided approaches, including a stronger empirical comparison on shared settings.

- Test-time estimation on the test set and fairness concerns: Estimating ADP direction from the test set itself uses target test POS statistics. While unsupervised, this can be seen as tuning on test data, and it advantages UDP vs. MSD in scenarios where no target data should be assumed. Similarly, in the predicted-POS setup, UDP uses supervised taggers trained on the target language (TnT on its training set), whereas MSD excludes the target language during training. This asymmetry should be clearly acknowledged and controlled (e.g., provide results with cross-lingual POS, or with ADP direction estimated only from non-test target text or source languages).

- Baseline choices and evaluation conventions: 
  - The rule-based baseline (BL) uses an oracle best branching direction per language and can produce disconnected structures. This muddies interpretability and is not a fair operational baseline.
  - Including punctuation in UAS contradicts common UD evaluation practice (often punctuation is excluded), and the very low PUNCT UAS heavily penalizes scores. Provide UAS excluding punctuation and, ideally, undirected UAS to align with unsupervised parsing literature.
  - No statistical significance testing is reported (e.g., over sentences or bootstrap over test sets), despite many small deltas.

- Methodological and clarity gaps:
  - The PR graph construction is under-specified (edge directions, weighting of multi-edges, normalization across POS types), and the rationale for teleport 0.05 “for convergence” is weak—PageRank with teleport always converges; discuss sensitivity to teleport and personalization weight.
  - The fallback/backoff strategy (dropping δ then κ) may create linguistically implausible attachments; provide statistics on how often backoffs occur and their impact.
  - The assumption that all function words should be leaves is not always true in UD (e.g., auxiliaries and determiners can exceptionally take dependents). Quantify the mismatch and its effect.
  - Excluding NOUN→VERB to avoid relative clauses is a strong modeling choice; quantify the resulting error on relative clause constructions and overall.

- Numerous presentation and formatting issues that impede readability and raise concerns about polish:
  - Typos and word choice: “The the main idea,” “assignations” (should be “assignments”), “constribution,” “overal,” “adpositon,” etc.
  - Table/figure inconsistencies and references: “Table 5” is referred to as “head rules,” but head rules are in Table 1; “Table 3 shows … come” while the example verb is “had”; Figure references cannot be verified; footnotes in text are misplaced.
  - References have missing years/placeholders (e.g., “?,” “\Q1999\E”), inconsistent formatting, and duplicated author listings. URLs appear mid-text (e.g., github link) and “ANONYMIZED” placeholders remain.
  - Claims “no training” conflict with using English dev to select rules and tune PR/directionality; this should be stated as development/tuning, not “no training.”

Questions to authors
- Provide full details of the PR graph: edge direction, weight of multi-edges, whether edges are typed/weighted by POS relations, normalization, and sensitivity to teleport and personalization weight (ablation curves would help).
- Report UAS excluding punctuation, and undirected UAS, to align with UD and unsupervised parsing practices. Also include significance testing for key comparisons.
- Control for fairness: (a) estimate ADP direction from non-test target text (e.g., unlabeled target dev or raw text) or source-side; (b) provide results with cross-lingual POS (e.g., projected tags) to remove reliance on supervised target POS.
- Quantify backoff usage and its impact; report how often δ/κ constraints are dropped. Also, analyze errors due to forbidding NOUN→VERB (relative clauses).
- Clarify availability: provide a real (non-anonymized) code/data link upon camera-ready; ensure tables/figures and references are corrected and consistent.
