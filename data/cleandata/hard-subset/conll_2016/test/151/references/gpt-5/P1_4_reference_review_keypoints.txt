- Presents UDP: a fully rule-based, training-free UD parser combining personalized PageRank, POS-based head rules, two-step decoding enforcing function-word leafness, and test-time ADP direction estimation.
- Main contribution 1: A simple UD-compliant, training-free parsing algorithm that attains competitive UAS vs. delexicalized transfer baselines across 32 languages.
- Main contribution 2: Thorough multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing notable robustness to POS noise and domain shift compared to a learned delexicalized baseline.
- Main contribution 3: A small set of generally applicable head rules plus test-time adposition direction estimation, demonstrating effectiveness of minimal language-agnostic heuristics.
- Strength: Strong cross-lingual coverage and competitive performance (UDPG 57.5 UAS vs. MSDG 63.9) despite no training and POS-level constraints.
- Strength: More robust to POS degradation and domain shifts than MSD, with smaller error propagation and lower domain-wise variability.
- Strength: Ablations clearly show PageRank’s importance (removing PR drops UAS ~9 points) and decoding benefits (~4 points over rule baseline).
- Strength: Simple, transparent, well-motivated UD design choices (function-word leafness, NOUN→VERB prohibition, ADP estimation) that are inspectable and explainable.
- Strength: Broad analysis including per-language results, POS-specific observations, and multiple POS prediction scenarios.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty over prior rule-aided unsupervised parsing (notably Søgaard 2012b); innovations are incremental (personalization, two-step decoding, ADP estimation).
- Weakness: “No training” claim weakened by English dev tuning of head rules and hyperparameters, introducing potential English bias.
- Weakness: Evaluation choices limit comparability—UAS includes punctuation and predicted POS use supervised in-language tagger (TnT), which is unrealistic for low-resource settings.
- Weakness: Incomplete handling of key UD phenomena (relative clauses, coordination, punctuation, MWE, names), likely capping performance and downstream utility.
- Weakness: Comparison set is limited—no projection-based baselines or comparisons to modern unsupervised objectives beyond Søgaard’s line; ADP ablations lacking.
- Question: How sensitive are results to personalization weight and teleport probability; were standard PR defaults tried?
- Question: Can authors report UAS excluding punctuation and whether ranking vs. MSD changes under that metric?
- Question: Do conclusions hold when using cross-lingual/projected or multilingual taggers instead of supervised in-language TnT?
- Question: How often does the back-off (dropping δ, then κ) trigger, and how does it affect non-projectivity and errors?
- Question: For languages with postpositions/mixed head orders, how stable is the ADP direction estimator and does sentence-level vs. corpus-level estimation matter?
- Additional comment: Release rule set, code, and domain splits for reproducibility.
- Additional comment: Report per-POS F1/head-attachment accuracy for challenging categories and an error typology to guide extensions.
- Additional comment: Edge-level PR or reified-edge scoring is a promising direction to increase novelty and accuracy while staying training-free.
- Overall: Clear, well-executed, and robust contribution with moderate novelty; lean toward acceptance if evaluation choices are clarified and more realistic POS/punctuation metrics are provided.
