- Proposes UDP: a training-free, language-agnostic UD parser that ranks content words with personalized PageRank and decodes in two passes to attach content words first and force function words to be leaves.  
- Introduces on-the-fly, data-driven estimation of adposition attachment direction from ADP–NOMINAL bigrams in test sentences; other function-word directions use fixed heuristics.  
- Evaluates on 32 UD v1.2 languages across gold/predicted/naive POS settings and cross-domain splits, showing competitiveness with a delexicalized TurboParser baseline and greater domain robustness.  
- Strong multilingual and cross-domain evaluation with consistent results across POS regimes and domains, showing lower domain variance for UDP.  
- Principled use of UD constraints: two-step decoding enforces function-word leafness and personalization improves predicate identification (+9 UAS vs. no PR).  
- Minimal language-specific rules and deterministic, efficient algorithm with interpretable failure modes; ADP direction is inferred automatically.  
- Thorough ablations and error analysis, including POS-wise behavior, root accuracy, and personalization effects across word-order types.  
- Transductive ADP estimation on test data introduces test-time look-ahead and potential data leakage; should report non-transductive variants.  
- Supervision mismatch: UDPP uses a POS tagger trained on target-language data while the delexicalized baseline uses only source data, biasing comparisons.  
- Novelty is incremental relative to Søgaard (2012b); key differences (PR, two-step decoding, ADP estimation) are modest and need direct comparison/reproduction.  
- Evaluation choices limit interpretability: punctuation included in UAS, lack of significance tests, and rule tuning on English may bias results for typologically different languages.  
- Missing runtime and structural statistics: wall-clock performance, memory, non-projective arc rates, and well-formedness details are not reported.  
- Request to quantify how much UAS gain comes from test-set ADP estimation and to report dev/unlabeled-corpus alternatives and fixed-direction baselines.  
- Ask for truly low-resource POS experiments (e.g., projected or multilingual taggers) instead of target-trained POS for UDPP.  
- Request UAS excluding punctuation, significance testing, and clarity on whether MSD used the same punctuation regime.  
- Recommend direct reimplementation/comparison to Søgaard (2012b) and stronger cross-lingual baselines (projection/multilingual embeddings).  
- Ask about sensitivity to teleport probability and personalization weight, and whether personalization harms some languages (e.g., verb-final).  
- Request reporting of non-projective arc rate and average runtime per sentence.  
- Overall: clear, well-executed, moderately novel paper showing UD constraints yield a robust training-free parser, but transductive ADP estimation and supervision mismatches should be addressed before acceptance.
