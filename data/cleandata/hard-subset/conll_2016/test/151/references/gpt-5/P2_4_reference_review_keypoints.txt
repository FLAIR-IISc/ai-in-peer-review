- Paper proposes UDP, a fully training-free UD parser using only UD constraints and POS tags, built from rule-based graphs, personalized PageRank (PR), and two-step decoding.
- Main claim: UDP is competitive with multi-source delexicalized transfer across 32 languages and notably robust to domain shift.
- Key methods: UD-informed rule graph over content/function POS, PR to rank content words (find root/guide heads), two-step decoding enforcing function-word leafness, adposition direction inferred from test data.
- Evaluation: 32 UD v1.2 languages under gold POS, predicted POS (TnT), and coarse content/function tags; compared to a multi-source delex TurboParser baseline and cross-domain analyses.
- Contribution 1: A simple, training-free parser that serves as a practical, robust UD baseline.
- Contribution 2: Effective combination of UD rules, personalized PR, and two-step decoding as a deterministic parser design.
- Contribution 3: Broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation with ablations and error diagnostics.
- Strength: Strong empirical coverage and robustness — UDP avg UAS 57.5 (gold POS) vs. 63.9 for delex baseline, smaller drop with predicted POS, and lower cross-domain variance.
- Strength: Sensible exploitation of UD constraints — function-word leafness and ADP direction heuristic work well (ADP UAS ~75%).
- Strength: Clear ablations show PR is crucial (~–9 UAS without PR), decoding adds ~+4 UAS, and PR improves root accuracy (~+2%).
- Strength: Reproducible, deterministic, no training required, few hyperparameters, code/data promised to be released.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty over prior rule-aided, training-free parsers (e.g., Søgaard 2012b); novel elements feel incremental.
- Weakness: Dependence on POS quality and supervised taggers (TnT); coarse content/function experiment insufficient to simulate real zero-resource POS scenarios.
- Weakness: Rules and some heuristics tuned on English, risking design bias and language-specific limitations (e.g., exclusion of NOUN→VERB edges).
- Weakness: Evaluation scope limited to unlabeled UAS (punctuation included); missing comparisons to other unsupervised/lightly-supervised grammar induction methods and runtime quantification.
- Weakness: PR personalization heuristic (5x weight for first verb) may be typologically brittle; need more typological analysis (OV vs VO, free word-order).
- Questions: Please report runtime (PR iterations, wall-clock per sentence), memory, and comparison to delex baseline; how does PR personalization affect convergence?
- Questions: How often does the back-off step trigger and what fraction of edges come from back-off; does this correlate with errors or POS types?
- Questions: Have you tried estimating attachment direction for DET/AUX/SCONJ from test data and does that help languages like Basque?
- Questions: Can you provide results using realistic cross-lingual POS taggers (projection or multilingual) to simulate zero-resource targets?
- Questions: How sensitive are results to the 5x personalization weight and the “first verb” heuristic; any typology-wise breakdown for root accuracy?
- Overall assessment: Well-executed and practically useful baseline with modest novelty but valuable empirical findings; needs deeper POS sourcing analysis and stronger comparisons to prior training-free parsers.
