- Proposes a training-free UD parser (UDP) that builds a directed multigraph with POS-based head rules, uses personalized PageRank to rank content words and find the root, and decodes in two passes enforcing function words as leaves.

- Introduces an unsupervised, on-the-fly per-language estimate of adposition attachment direction using ADP–NOMINAL bigrams from test data.

- Evaluates UDP across 32 UD languages (gold POS, predicted POS, coarse CF tags) and cross-domain settings, comparing to multi-source delexicalized transfer and ablations (e.g., UDPNoPR).

- Two-step decoding and restricted head rules closely operationalize UD constraints, providing linguistically motivated, cross-lingually stable behavior.

- UDP shows strong multilingual coverage and competitive UAS: within ~6–7 points of delexicalized transfer with gold POS and outperforming it on some languages (e.g., Hindi).

- Predicted POS results are competitive and exhibit lower error propagation from POS to dependencies than the transfer baseline.

- Cross-domain experiments indicate substantially lower performance variance across genres than supervised delexicalized models, suggesting better domain robustness.

- Ablations (UDPNoPR) show personalized PageRank contributes around ~9 UAS points; POS-wise breakdowns yield informative insights (e.g., high DET UAS, low PUNCT/CONJ stability).

- Method is simple, fast, transparent, reproducible, and requires only POS tags plus a small rule set.

- Core ideas (PR ranking and rule-based decoding) are incremental relative to prior work (notably Søgaard 2012b); novelty is limited.

- Reliance on high-quality POS tags and English-tuned parameters (teleport probability, functional POS directions) undermines the claim of being fully training-free and limits low-resource applicability.

- Estimating ADP direction from the test set “peeks” at the test distribution and may be impractical when only small or sentence-level inputs are available.

- Baselines are weak (hand-crafted baseline may not produce well-formed trees), and no comparisons to other modern unsupervised/weakly supervised or projection-based methods are provided.

- Evaluation reports only UAS; missing metrics include LAS, projectivity, attachment length distributions, structural analyses, and statistical significance tests.

- Systematic coverage limitations: design choices (e.g., forbidding NOUN→VERB) prevent handling relative clauses, coordination and certain constructions perform poorly (low CONJ UAS), and fixed English-derived directions hurt some languages (e.g., Basque).

- Missing direct replication/comparison to Søgaard (2012b) on the same UD setup to quantify gains from PR personalization and two-step decoding beyond UDPNoPR.

- Questions about sensitivity to PR personalization weight and teleport probability and whether these can be learned unsupervised per language.

- Suggestion to estimate attachment directionality for other function categories (DET, AUX, SCONJ, CONJ) from data analogous to ADP to avoid English-tuned defaults.

- Request for analysis of how large a test corpus must be to reliably infer ADP direction and performance under short-document or sentence-level parsing.

- Requests for additional evaluations: LAS and punctuation-excluded scores, statistical significance of differences and domain variance, release of full head-rule set, and detailed error analysis on constructions like relatives, coordination, and auxiliaries.
