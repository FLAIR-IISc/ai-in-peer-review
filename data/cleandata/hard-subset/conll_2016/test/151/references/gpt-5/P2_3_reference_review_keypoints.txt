- Proposal of a training-free UD parser using POS-based head rules, a two-step decoding (content-first, function-word leafness), and personalized PageRank to prioritize content-word attachments; only language-specific signal is adposition direction estimated unsupervised from test bigrams.  
- Large empirical study across 32 UD languages, three POS-tagging regimes (gold, supervised predicted, coarse content/function), and cross-domain evaluation.  
- Competitive performance: within ~6–7 UAS of delexicalized multi-source transfer (MSD), better for some languages (e.g., Hindi), and lower variance across domains.  
- Ablation shows PR contributes ~+9 UAS and decoding/rules ~+4 UAS over baselines; POS-wise error analysis highlights weaknesses (coordination, punctuation).  
- Method is simple, linguistically motivated, allows non-projectivities, requires no parallel data or training (aside from POS tags), and is fast with few hyperparameters.  
- Strong reproducibility and practicality claims with code/datasets promised.  
- Novelty is limited—approach is an incremental extension of prior rule-aided/PR-based work (e.g., Søgaard 2012b); main contribution is empirical.  
- Heavy reliance on POS tags and on supervised tagger (TnT) in the realistic setup weakens the “no training” claim; the coarse CF tag setup is an unconvincing lower bound.  
- Test-time (transductive) estimation of adposition direction from test bigrams may inflate UAS and should be evaluated more rigorously.  
- Baselines and metrics are limited: missing projection-based and newer cross-lingual methods, and evaluations beyond UAS (e.g., punctuation-excluded, undirected scores) would be informative.  
- Hand-picked rule set omits some relations (e.g., NOUN→VERB for relative clauses), is tuned on English dev, and may bias or cap performance on phenomena like coordination and RCs.  
- Question: sensitivity of ADP direction estimation to amount of test text and transductive bias—report first-k-sentences experiments and per-language curves.  
- Question: choice of 5x PR personalization—did authors tune this cross-linguistically or try other personalization targets (all verbs or highest in-degree node)?  
- Question: effect of stronger cross-lingual/multilingual POS taggers or fully no-target-language POS-training on UDPP results.  
- Question: include projection-based baselines (where bitext exists) and compare directly to Søgaard (2012b) on UD.  
- Question: robustness to mixed adposition systems and free word order—would local per-sentence/bigram decisions help without harming stability?  
- Question: rate of multiple roots before enforcement, incidence of non-projective arcs, and impact of sentence length on error patterns.  
- Overall: clear, practical, and well-evaluated contribution with moderate novelty; valuable as a robust baseline for UD parsing without heavy training.
