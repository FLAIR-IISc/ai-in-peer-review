- Paper introduces UDP: a training-free, rule-based UD parser using only POS tags, PageRank for content-word ranking, two-stage UD-compliant decoding, and test-time ADP head-direction estimation.
- Main contribution 1: A simple, UD-compliant parser competitive with delexicalized transfer across 32 languages and robust to domain shift (gold POS UAS 57.5 vs. 63.9 MSD; small drop with predicted POS).
- Main contribution 2: Principled use of personalized PageRank to prioritize content words and pick roots, with ablation showing large gains (+9 UAS from PR, +4 from decoding).
- Main contribution 3: Simple test-time ADP head-direction estimation and domain-robustness analysis showing lower cross-domain variance than delexicalized baseline.
- Strength: Very broad and thorough evaluation — 32 languages, three POS settings, and multi-genre domain split analysis with per-language std devs.
- Strength: Competitive, robust baseline — UDPG close to MSDG on average, surpasses on some languages, and shows less error propagation from noisy POS.
- Strength: Modeling well-aligned with UD — two-step decoding enforces function-word leaf constraint and ADP estimation is interpretable and effective (~75% ADP UAS).
- Strength: Informative ablations and diagnostics — isolates PR and decoding impacts, reports root accuracy and failure cases (punctuation, coordination, relative clauses).
- Strength: Practical and efficient — no parser training, sub-quadratic decoding, minimal per-language tuning; useful fallback for low-resource/cross-domain settings.
- Weakness: Limited novelty — approach builds on prior PR and rule-based parsing; main additions are incremental.
- Weakness: Dependence on supervised POS taggers undermines “training-free” claim for truly low-resource languages (uses per-language TnT trained on UD).
- Weakness: Parameter selection and data usage concerns — English dev used to set many rules/parameters; ADP direction estimated on test sentences; baseline directions sometimes given oracle information.
- Weakness: Coverage gaps — excludes NOUN→VERB heads (hurting relative clauses), weak on coordination and punctuation, no MWE handling, and only unlabeled attachments reported.
- Weakness: Baseline and statistical rigor — comparison lacks recent transfer/projection baselines and no significance testing reported.
- Questions: Clarify PageRank specifics — edge weighting, handling parallel edges, convergence criteria, runtime impact, and sensitivity to personalization/teleport parameters and tuning on other languages.
- Questions: ADP direction details — tokenization/filtering for bigrams, sentence-internal estimation, document-level flips, and whether other POS directions could be estimated per language.
- Questions: Low-resource POS usage — how to use UDP without target-language POS training data and results with cross-lingual or multilingual taggers?
- Questions: Decoding tie-breaking and back-off rates — how ties are resolved, frequency of back-off to relaxed constraints, and correlation with errors.
- Questions: Baselines and significance — whether statistical tests were run and comparisons to projection-based or typology-aware transfer methods.
