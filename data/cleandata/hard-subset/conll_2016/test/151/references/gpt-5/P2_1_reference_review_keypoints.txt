- Paper proposes UDP: a rule-based, training-free dependency parser for Universal Dependencies using personalized PageRank, two-step decoding (content-first, function-word leaf constraint), and test-time ADP–NOMINAL direction estimation.  
- Contribution: a simple, UD-aligned, training-free parser that's competitive with a strong delexicalized transfer baseline and robust across domains.  
- Contribution: a UD-motivated decoding design (function-word leafness + content-first decoding + PR personalization) with ablations quantifying PageRank and decoding effects.  
- Contribution: broad multilingual (32 languages) and cross-domain evaluation showing stability and lower variance than supervised transfer baselines.  
- Strength: strong, simple baseline with broad multilingual coverage; competitive UAS with gold POS and robust to POS noise and domain shifts.  
- Strength: linguistically motivated UD-aligned design yields high ADP UAS and sensible behavior without supervision.  
- Strength: informative ablations and analysis quantify PR (~+9 UAS) and decoding (~+4 UAS) contributions and discuss failure modes.  
- Strength: reproducible and practical: compact rule set, deterministic decoding given POS, no training or parallel data required; code/data to be released.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—core PR idea predates the paper; additions are incremental rather than a deeper algorithmic advance.  
- Weakness: fairness and comparison concerns—ADP direction estimated on test data, predicted POS trained per target language, and baselines limited (no modern projection/representation-based comparisons).  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to unlabeled UAS; lack of labeled attachment (LAS) or partial labeling limits claims about UD-specific advantages.  
- Weakness: coverage gaps—fails on relative clauses (verbs under nouns), coordination, punctuation, and uses fixed directionality for some function POS despite cross-linguistic variation.  
- Weakness: clarity/details lacking—typos, unclear graph construction/edge weighting, unspecified back-off frequencies, non-projectivity prevalence, and no runtime measurements.  
- Weakness: statistical rigor missing—no significance testing for language-wise or domain-variance claims.  
- Question: Are PR edges weighted by multiplicity or unweighted, and do graphs include only rule-licensed edges or also proximity edges?  
- Question: How do POS errors affect graph connectivity and PageRank stability?  
- Question: How much does estimating ADP direction on test data help vs. estimating on training/held-out data; could this be applied to DET/AUX/SCONJ?  
- Question: How often are δ (rule) and κ (direction) constraints relaxed during decoding, and what proportion of final attachments use back-off?  
- Question: Any correlation between back-off events and non-projectivity or parsing errors?  
- Question: How does personalization favoring the first verb affect verb-final languages, and can position-aware or language-specific priors improve root accuracy?  
- Question: Can you report labeled attachment (or LAS for rule-covered edges) and provide runtime per sentence/treebank?  
- Question: Will the in-house cross-domain English splits (bible, magazine, questions) be released, how large are they, and are domain differences significant?
