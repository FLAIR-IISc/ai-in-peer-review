- Proposal: UDP, a training-free UD parser using UD-compliant POS head rules, hard constraints, Personalized PageRank over a rule-induced graph, and two-step decoding separating content and function words.  
- Contribution 1: A simple UD-aware, training-free parsing algorithm enforcing function-word leafness and using personalized PageRank to rank content heads.  
- Contribution 2: A language-agnostic, test-time method to infer adposition attachment directionality from target data.  
- Contribution 3: Broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation on 32 treebanks showing competitive UAS and strong robustness to domain shift and POS noise.  

Strengths:
- Linguistically grounded design: two-step decoding matches UD principles and yields interpretable, well-formed trees.  
- Robust multilingual evaluation: thorough experiments with small drop from gold to predicted POS and lower error propagation than MSD.  
- Effective use of Personalized PageRank: improves root detection and content-word attachment with clear ablation gains.  
- Practical ADP direction estimation: simple, fast convergence from test data and strong ADP UAS without language metadata.  
- Transparent error analysis: candid discussion of limitations and POS-wise accuracies increases credibility.  

Weaknesses:
- Incremental novelty: similar PR-based, rule-guided parsing exists (Søgaard 2012b); missing direct controlled comparison on UD weakens novelty claims.  
- “No training” overstated: English-tuned head rules/parameters and reliance on supervised POS taggers blur the training-free claim.  
- Baseline/evaluation choices could be stronger: MSD is dated, punctuation included in UAS, and no significance testing reported.  
- Limited linguistic coverage: excludes NOUN→VERB heads, struggles with coordination, punctuation, and some language-specific directionality errors.  
- Sensitivity and methodological gaps: ad hoc PR hyperparameters, disconnected graphs, non-projectivity frequency, and backoff usage not sufficiently analyzed or reported.  

Questions / requested analyses:
- Report standard UD scores excluding punctuation and provide significance tests versus MSD.  
- How often are δ/κ constraint backoffs invoked and do they correlate with POS or languages?  
- Frequency of non-projective arcs and correlation with language typology?  
- Sensitivity analysis for PR teleport probability and personalization weight across languages?  
- Include a direct reimplementation comparison to Søgaard (2012b) on UD with the same POS setup.  
- For languages where the rule baseline outperforms UDP (e.g., Estonian, Tamil), what error types cause this and would estimating function POS direction from test data help?  
- Is estimating ADP direction on the test set fair—would using a held-out dev set change results?  
- Root selection accuracy by clause/word-order type and whether personalization ever hurts in verb-final languages?
