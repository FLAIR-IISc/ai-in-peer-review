- Proposes UDP: a training-free, rule-based UD parser using only POS tags, UD-inspired head–dependent constraints, personalized PageRank, two-pass decoding, and adposition direction from test bigram counts.
- Ranks content words with personalized PageRank on a POS-rule-derived graph to identify main predicates and prioritize attachments.
- Decodes in two passes to enforce UD’s “function words as leaves” constraint and enforces single-root deterministically.
- Estimates adposition (ADP) attachment direction on-the-fly from test data using simple bigram counts to reduce language-specific parameters.
- Evaluated on 32 UD v1.2 languages under gold POS, predicted POS (TnT trained per language), and a crude content/function split; compared to multi-source delexicalized transfer (MSD).
- Shows competitive multilingual performance: UDP lags MSD by only 6.4 UAS on average and outperforms MSD on some languages (e.g., Hindi) despite no treebank training.
- Demonstrates robustness to domain shift with consistently lower UAS variance than MSD across domains and POS settings.
- Method is simple, fast, interpretable, deterministic given POS, and enforces UD constraints explicitly.
- Careful ablative analysis isolates contributions of PageRank vs. decoding and quantifies POS-to-dependency error propagation.
- Broad evaluation choices: standard UAS, 32 languages, multiple POS scenarios, cross-domain slicing, and comparison to a strong delexicalized baseline.
- “Without training” claim weakened because predicted-POS setting uses TnT trained on target language data, undermining the no-training narrative.
- Limited baseline comparisons to truly unsupervised parsers; lacks direct comparisons to prior unsupervised/weakly supervised UD methods.
- Head rules and PageRank parameters tuned on English dev data; ADP direction estimated on test sets, raising concerns about test-set peeking and English-centric tuning.
- Structural coverage gaps: excludes NOUN→VERB heads (affecting relative clauses) and shows weaknesses on coordination and punctuation, causing systematic failures.
- Evaluation scope limited: only UAS reported, missing label analyses, head/root accuracy by sentence length or typology, and no significance testing.
- Question about practicable POS sources in low-resource settings given per-language TnT training—suggest reporting cross-lingual POS tagging results.
- Asks for stability analysis of ADP direction heuristic (10–15 sentences) across languages/domains and comparison to UD metadata.
- Requests analysis of PageRank personalization behavior in V-final/head-final languages and root accuracy by language family with/without personalization.
- Wants statistics on decoding backoff frequency, proportion of edges violating rules/directions, and correlation with errors.
- Requests per-sentence runtime and memory costs for PR + decoding and notes any failure cases (e.g., disconnected PR graphs) beyond teleport fix.
- Notes the rule-based baseline used oracle L/R for backoff, which may be stronger than UDP’s estimated ADP; asks for fairness clarification and non-oracle baseline.
- Recommends reporting projectivity of predicted trees and error breakdowns by sentence length due to cross-lingual skew.
- Encourages releasing code and domainwise splits with scripts reproducing PR parameters, direction estimation, and seeds for stability.
