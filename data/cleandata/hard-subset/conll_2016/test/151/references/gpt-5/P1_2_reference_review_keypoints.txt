- Summary: Proposes UDP, a training-free UD parser using personalized PageRank on a POS-rule graph, two-step content→function decoding, and test-time ADP direction estimation; evaluated on 32 treebanks and competitive with a delexicalized transfer parser (MSD) with lower variance.

- Contribution: A simple, language-agnostic, training-free pipeline exploiting UD constraints (PR + two-step decoding) that attains competitive cross-lingual UAS and robustness.

- Contribution: A practical, data-free method to infer adposition head direction from raw test text, with analysis of cross-domain stability.

- Contribution: Empirical claim that a UD-conformant rule-based method can rival delexicalized transfer and be less sensitive to POS noise and domain shifts.

- Strength: Zero-resource practicality—no treebanks or parallel data required, only POS at test time.

- Strength: Broad and clear evaluation across 32 languages, gold/predicted POS, and standard UAS metrics; modest average gap to MSD and wins on some languages (e.g., Hindi).

- Strength: Demonstrated cross-domain robustness—lower standard deviation across genres vs MSD under gold and predicted POS.

- Strength: Informative ablations showing sizable gains from PR (~+9 UAS) and two-step decoding (~+4 UAS), and POS-wise attachment analyses.

- Strength: Deterministic decoding that enforces UD constraints (single root, function-word leafness) producing valid trees.

- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to prior PR-based rule parsers (Søgaard 2012); new elements are incremental and need explicit comparison to prior variants.

- Weakness: Transductive estimation of ADP direction on the test set risks optimistic bias and weakens the “no training” claim.

- Weakness: Predicted POS are from TnT trained on target labeled data, which is not low-resource and may favor UDP; modern cross-lingual taggers would be a better stress test.

- Weakness: Limited baselines—comparison only to one delexicalized transfer system and trivial adjacency; no stronger recent transfer or projection baselines and no significance testing.

- Weakness: Method details underspecified (incomplete rule list, tuned PR teleport/personalization on English, tie-breaking, handling of disconnected components) raising overfitting concerns.

- Weakness: Linguistic coverage gaps—cannot capture relative clauses, struggles with coordination and punctuation, and uses fixed global directions for some phenomena.

- Question: Provide the complete head–dependent rule set, confirm if rules for PR and decoding are identical, explain conflict/tie resolution, and release code/rules.

- Question: How many sentences/documents are used to estimate ADP direction, and what are results when estimating from a separate dev or small unlabeled sample?

- Question: Report sensitivity of results to PR parameters (personalization weight and teleport) with cross-lingual ranges.

- Question: Add a predicted-POS setting using cross-lingual/projected tags to better simulate low-resource conditions.

- Question: Include stronger baselines (e.g., density-driven delex transfer, projection methods) and report statistical significance.

- Question: Report per-language root accuracy and ADP direction accuracy, especially for verb-final languages.

- Additional: Fix typos/formatting, complete references/tables, and release domain split scripts and annotations for reproducibility.
