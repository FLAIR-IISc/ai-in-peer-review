- Summary: Proposes an unsupervised, training-free UD parser using POS tags, personalized PageRank on a rule-induced graph, two-step decoding (content-tree then attach function words), and test-time ADP direction estimation.

- Contribution 1: A simple UD-constrained, training-free parsing algorithm that is competitive with multi-source delexicalized transfer (MSD) on UAS and more robust to POS noise and domain shifts.

- Contribution 2: Effective use of personalized PageRank focused on content words plus a two-phase decoding enforcing function-word leafness, with ablation showing substantial PR gains.

- Contribution 3: A practical, test-time bigram heuristic to estimate adposition (ADP) head-directionality without language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation on 32 languages with gold and predicted POS and per-domain variance reporting.

- Strength: Linguistically motivated, clear UD-aligned design (two-step decoding and head-direction rules) that is simple and interpretable.

- Strength: Solid ablation and error analyses quantifying contributions from PR, decoding, root accuracy, and POS-specific performance.

- Strength: Competitive performance with minimal supervision—only 6.4 UAS points behind MSD with gold POS and smaller error propagation under predicted POS.

- Strength: Authors intend to release code and test sets; algorithm is specified sufficiently for reimplementation.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—approach is incremental over prior PR-based unsupervised parsing (Søgaard 2012) with modest new elements.

- Weakness: “Without training” claim is weakened by reliance on supervised POS taggers, English dev tuning for PR and rules, and head-rule selection using English.

- Weakness: ADP direction estimation from the test set risks corpus/domain-specific bias and peeking; a cleaner approach would use external unlabeled data.

- Weakness: Baselines and evaluation choices could be stronger—missing projection/cross-lingual baselines, punctuation handling differs from common UD practice, adjacency baseline lacks quantitative numbers, and no LAS/partial labeling reported.

- Weakness: Limited handling of key constructions (e.g., NOUN→VERB disallowed, weak coordination and punctuation attachments) with insufficient downstream impact analysis.

- Question: How sensitive are results to PR personalization weight (5x) and teleport probability (0.05), and were sentence- vs corpus-level personalization or verb-final priors tried?

- Question: Is ADP direction estimated once on the whole test set (peeking) or incrementally/foldwise, and would external raw text produce different estimates or require how many sentences for stability?

- Question: Clarify the “without training” use case—are target-language POS taggers allowed, and can results with projected or unsupervised POS be shown to match the claim?

- Question: Can authors provide UAS excluding punctuation and any LAS or partial-labeling experiments to improve comparability and verify label-addition claims?

- Question: Why use 20k MSD training sentences fixed across languages, how was TurboParser tuned, and how do MSD results vary with different source selections or data sizes?

- Overall evaluation: Well-written, empirically strong paper showing practical, robust UD-aware parsing with moderate conceptual novelty; addressing POS supervision, test-time estimation, and stronger baselines/reporting would strengthen the contribution.
