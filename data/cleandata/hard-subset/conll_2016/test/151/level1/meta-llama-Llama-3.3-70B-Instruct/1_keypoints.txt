- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser for UD that requires no training data and relies on UD POS constraints.
- Leverages personalized PageRank to score content words, improving identification of the sentence’s main predicate.
- Shows robustness to domain change across languages and sometimes outperforms a multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD).
- Achieves competitive performance on most UD languages and beats MSD on Hindi.
- Is less sensitive to degraded POS tag quality, with only a ~2.2% average drop using predicted POS tags.
- Exhibits greater domain stability than MSD, with lower performance variance across languages.
- Does not handle multiword expressions, coordination, or proper names, limiting performance for some languages/domains.
- Relies heavily on UD constraints, which may not generalize to all languages or linguistic formalisms.
- Only evaluates unlabeled attachments; lacking edge labeling that could boost performance.
