- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser for Universal Dependencies using personalized PageRank and linguistically motivated head-dependent rules.
- UDP requires no training data and estimates adposition attachment direction from test data, offering a robust alternative to supervised/delexicalized parsers.
- Main contribution: a fully unsupervised UD-constrained parsing framework combining head-dependent rules with personalized PageRank to rank content words and identify predicates.
- Main contribution: demonstrated domain robustness, showing higher resilience to domain shifts than supervised delexicalized systems.
- Main contribution: novel method to estimate adposition attachment direction directly from test data, removing language-specific parameters.
- Strength: linguistically sound approach by adhering to UD constraints and explicit head-dependent rules.
- Strength: competitive performance close to multi-source delexicalized (MSD) parsers, outperforming MSD on Hindi and strong across 32 languages.
- Strength: strong domain generalization with lower performance variability across domains compared to MSD.
- Strength: simple, efficient, training-free, and interpretable due to a small set of rules.
- Strength: rigorous evaluation including gold/predicted POS, domain tests, and multiple baselines.
- Weakness: struggles with complex constructions like coordination, relative clauses, and proper names.
- Weakness: performance sensitive to POS accuracy; degrades under extreme POS noise (e.g., naive content-function tagging).
- Weakness: evaluation focused mainly on UAS; lacks LAS or partial edge labeling for more complete assessment.
- Weakness: no mechanism for handling multiword expressions, which affects many languages.
- Question: how does UDP perform on languages with free word order or highly agglutinative morphology beyond reported results?
- Question: can the adposition direction estimation method be extended to other syntactic phenomena (e.g., verb-final or S-O order)?
- Question: have you considered integrating handling for multiword expressions or named entities in future UDP versions?
- Additional comment: UDP is a well-motivated, competitive unsupervised parser that fills a gap for cross-lingual and low-resource parsing despite limitations on complex syntactic phenomena.
