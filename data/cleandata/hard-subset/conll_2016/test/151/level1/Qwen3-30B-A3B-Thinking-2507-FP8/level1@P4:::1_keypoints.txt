- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD parser requiring no training data that uses UD constraints, PageRank for main predicates, adposition direction estimated from test data, and two-step decoding for content vs function words.
- Contributions: UD-specific unsupervised parsing exploiting linguistic constraints; test-time adposition direction estimation removing language-specific parameters; competitive performance and improved domain robustness versus supervised transfer.

Strengths
- Clear, technically sound method description (PageRank setup, head direction estimation, two-step decoding) with rigorous evaluation.
- Thorough multilingual evaluation across 32 languages and three POS setups, and comparison to a strong multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline (MSD).
- Competitive performance: close to supervised MSD (avg 6.4% UAS gap) and better on Hindi in reported results.
- Demonstrated domain robustness: lower variance across domains than MSD, supporting stability under domain shift.
- Simplicity and accessibility: no training data needed, freely available, applicable to low-resource and diverse language families.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: approach largely adapts Søgaard (2012b) with incremental UD-specific modifications and insufficient differentiation from prior rule-aided unsupervised work.
- POS dependency: notable performance drop with predicted POS tags and limited discussion of practical implications of POS errors.
- Incomplete head-rule coverage: some POS categories (e.g., X, SYM) handled ad-hoc, risking inconsistent cross-lingual behavior.
- Evaluation limited to UAS: lacks broader linguistically-oriented evaluation and detailed dependency-type error analysis.

Questions for authors
- How do the Table 1 head rules differ from prior UD-compliant parsing rules, and why were these specific rules chosen?
- Can you provide deeper error analysis for Hindi to explain why UDP outperforms MSD there?
- Do you expect the observed domain robustness to generalize to the other UD languages beyond the three-treebank subset tested?
