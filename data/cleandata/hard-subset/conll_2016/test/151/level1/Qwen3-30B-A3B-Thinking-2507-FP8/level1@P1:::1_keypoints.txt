- Adapts dependency head rules to UD, enforcing function words as leaves to fit UD formalism.
- Uses personalized PageRank to identify sentence main predicate (root), improving over naive rankings.
- Estimates adposition attachment direction from test data, avoiding language-specific parameters.

- Truly unsupervised parser with strong performance close to a supervised transfer baseline.
- Demonstrates robustness to domain shifts with lower variance across domains than supervised systems.
- Linguistically grounded design leverages UD constraints to avoid common unsupervised parser errors.
- Practical, efficient implementation with available code and test sets.

- Claim of "no training" is misleading because English treebank was used for development/tuning.
- Limited analysis of POS tagger impact; POS errors and low-resource taggers' effects are underexplored.
- Major linguistic limitation: cannot handle relative clauses due to exclusion of NOUN→VERB head rule.
- Description of personalized PageRank is vague, especially how main predicate is chosen for verb-less sentences.

- How much of the English treebank was used for development/tuning and how was it split?
- How robust is adposition direction estimation on very small/low-resource test sets and what is the minimum data needed?
- Why was the NOUN→VERB head rule excluded, and how would performance change if it were included?
- Why does UDP outperform MSD on Hindi—what specific linguistic phenomena explain this result?
