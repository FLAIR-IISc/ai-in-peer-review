- Presents UDP, an unsupervised UD parser that uses only UD linguistic constraints (e.g., function words as leaves).
- Key contributions: linguistically principled constraint-based parsing, a novel personalized PageRank for main predicate ID, and strong cross-lingual/domain robustness.
- Personalized PageRank for main predicate identification significantly outperforms naive ranking.
- Achieves competitive performance across 32 languages (within 6.4% UAS of supervised delexicalized systems) without training data.
- Shows notably lower domain-shift variance than supervised baselines (e.g., std dev 1.8 vs. 4.5 for MSDG), indicating robustness.
- Clear, principled handling of UD-specific constraints (function word leaf status, adposition direction) aligned with UD formalism.
- Claim of “no training” is misleading since English treebank was used to tune head rules and PageRank parameters.
- Estimating adposition direction requires 10–15 sentences, which may fail on short texts or very low-resource languages.
- Evaluation of the “naive POS” (UDPN) scenario is simplistic and may overestimate robustness to realistic POS tagging errors.
- Overall contribution: demonstrates that leveraging linguistic constraints can yield competitive, robust unsupervised parsing without conventional training.
