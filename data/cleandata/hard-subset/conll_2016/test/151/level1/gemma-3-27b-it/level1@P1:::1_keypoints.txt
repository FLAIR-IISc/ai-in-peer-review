- Demonstrates UDP: a fully unsupervised UD parser using UD constraints and PageRank that achieves competitive performance without training data.  
- Combines linguistically motivated dependency head rules with PageRank and a two-step decoding (content vs function words) for improved parsing.  
- Shows robustness to domain shift, maintaining performance across domains better than supervised baselines.  

- Achieves strong empirical results across 32 languages with UAS comparable to delexicalized transfer systems.  
- Methodology is clear and well-motivated, including PageRank personalization and two-step decoding choices.  
- Evaluation is thorough, covering gold vs predicted POS, domain analyses, automatic metrics, and qualitative error analysis.  
- Parser and datasets are publicly released, supporting reproducibility.  
- Provides insightful error analysis highlighting strengths and stability vs data-driven parsers.  

- Individual components (PageRank, head rules) are not novel; novelty is in their UD-specific integration.  
- Parser depends on POS tags, limiting fully unsupervised claims and performance when POS quality is low.  
- Use of a limited head-rule set oversimplifies some linguistic phenomena (e.g., relative clauses).  
- Error analysis could be deeper, especially by POS tag or specific dependency relation types.  
- Results presentation is dense; key takeaways could be highlighted more concisely for readability.  

- Request clarification on how dependency head rules in Table 1 were selected and evaluated during development.  
- Ask whether personalization weight for the main predicate (5x) was tuned for languages beyond English and what results emerged.  
- Inquire about plans and approaches to extend the parser to handle more complex structures like relative clauses.  
- Request examples of linguistic features in "difficult" domains (e.g., bulletin, legal) that hurt parsing accuracy.  
- Ask whether the parser’s determinism for a given POS sequence could be a drawback when multiple valid parses exist.  

- Reference list is extensive and well-grounded in relevant literature.  
- Writing is clear and concise, making the paper easy to follow.  
- Figures and tables effectively illustrate methodology and results, though some results could be more concisely presented.
