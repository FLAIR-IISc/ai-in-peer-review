- UDP is an unsupervised UD parser using personalized PageRank plus linguistically motivated head rules to parse without training.  
- Main contribution: successful application of personalized PageRank within UD constraints yielding competitive performance across 32 languages.  
- Secondary contribution: detailed analysis showing the system’s relative robustness to domain shift versus a supervised baseline.  
- Strength: broad, diverse evaluation across many languages.  
- Strength: strong performance in low-resource scenarios compared to delexicalized transfer.  
- Strength: well-executed ablation study demonstrating the contribution of PageRank.  
- Strength: comprehensive error and cross-domain analysis; parser and datasets are released for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—PageRank and head rules are not fundamentally new techniques.  
- Weakness: insufficient investigation and unclear rationale for the specific head-rule choices.  
- Weakness: domain-shift evaluation uses unevenly represented domains, potentially biasing conclusions.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on POS tags; sensitivity to POS tagging accuracy is underexplored despite some experiments with predicted tags.  
- Weakness: claim of being “completely free of language-specific parameters” is misleading because adposition directionality is estimated on the test data.  
- Question: provide deeper justification for chosen head rules and error analysis of rules considered but excluded.  
- Question: report sensitivity to PageRank teleport probability and whether systematic tuning was performed.  
- Question: clarify/rephrase the “no language-specific parameters” claim given the adposition estimation step.  
- Question: elaborate on challenges and a possible roadmap for extending the method to multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names.
