- Paper introduces UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank and dependency head rules, claiming competitive performance to delexicalized transfer without training and greater domain robustness.  
- Four claimed contributions: adapting dependency head rules to UD, adding a UD function-word constraint, using personalized PageRank, and estimating adposition attachment direction on test data.  
- Main significance: demonstration of an effective, truly unsupervised parser within UD, notably resilient to domain shift; code and detailed analyses are publicly released.  

Strengths:
- Strong baseline and competitive results: UDP often matches or outperforms the delexicalized supervised MSD system, especially under domain shift.  
- Domain robustness: extensive cross-domain evaluation shows lower UAS variability for UDP vs MSD.  
- Comprehensive evaluation and analysis across 32 languages and multiple POS-tag conditions, with detailed error breakdowns.  
- Clear, reproducible methodology with public code and datasets.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty of individual components; novelty mainly in their combination and UD application.  
- Reliance on English for initial parameter tuning raises potential bias and unclear sensitivity to that choice.  
- The naive two-POS (CONTENT/FUNCTION) scenario is overly simplistic and may not reflect real low-resource conditions.  
- Insufficient analysis of failure cases and languages with notably poor performance.  

Questions for authors:
- How sensitive are PageRank parameters and function-word direction settings to using English for tuning, and would other languages change results?  
- Besides Japanese, were other languages excluded and was any adaptation attempted for different morphological types (e.g., agglutinative languages)?  
- Can you provide annotation details for the in-house English datasets (e.g., IAA, guidelines)?  
- By how much does personalized PageRank speed up convergence (quantitative measure)?  

Additional comments:
- Clarify the meaning of "L" and "R" head-direction labels in Table 5.  
- Reference list formatting is inconsistent.  
- Recommend using the term "zero-shot" rather than "without training."
