- Core idea: UDP is an unsupervised dependency parser for UD that uses PageRank and head-dependent rules to build trees without training data.  
- Primary contribution: A practical, training-free parser achieving competitive accuracy for many languages.  
- Secondary contribution: Empirical demonstration that a linguistically informed, training-free approach yields strong domain robustness.  
- Evaluation: Comprehensive evaluation across 32 languages with ablation studies and experiments using predicted POS tags.  
- Strong performance: Achieves competitive UAS, often surpassing a delexicalized transfer baseline without any annotated training data.  
- Robustness to domain shift: Shows smaller UAS variance across domains and superior cross-domain stability versus transfer systems.  
- Ablations and analysis: PageRank contribution and POS-tag robustness analyzed, providing insights into system behavior and limitations.  
- Presentation: Clear, well-structured paper with illustrative example runs and transparent methodology.  
- Limitation — complex syntax: Likely struggles with coordination, long-distance dependencies, and non-projective structures due to simple head-dependent rules.  
- Limitation — POS dependence: Performance degrades with predicted POS tags, indicating sensitivity to tag quality.  
- Limitation — rule engineering: Head rules and adposition heuristics reflect manual linguistic engineering and some language-specific tuning.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify how head rules were derived/refined, plans for handling coordination, and reasons for cross-language variation in domain robustness.  
- Additional suggestions: Expand comparisons to classic unsupervised parsers, ensure dataset URL works, and discuss the parser’s computational complexity.
