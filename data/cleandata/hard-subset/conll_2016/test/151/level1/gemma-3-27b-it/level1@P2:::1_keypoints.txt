- Presents UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using dependency head rules and personalized PageRank to avoid any training data.  
- Adapts dependency head rules to the UD framework, enabling a rule-based parsing approach across languages.  
- Uses personalized PageRank to improve main predicate identification, a key step for unsupervised parsing.  
- Shows strong empirical results: competitive UAS (~61.2%) across 32 languages without language-specific training, close to delexicalized transfer (MSDG ~63.9%).  
- Demonstrates robustness to domain change with lower variance than supervised baselines, suggesting greater stability.  
- Clear and thorough presentation with helpful decoding algorithm, worked example, and comprehensive experimental setups.  
- Rule coverage is limited and incomplete; reliance on fallbacks may introduce errors and expansion methodology is unclear.  
- Performance is sensitive to POS tagging: predicted POS causes ~10 UAS point drop on average, indicating error propagation issues.  
- Lacks detailed qualitative error analysis to pinpoint failure modes (e.g., coordination, relative clauses, specific POS tags).  
- Question: How were the initial dependency head rules determined and refined beyond English development data?  
- Question: Which types of POS tagging errors most significantly degrade parsing accuracy?  
- Question: How could UDP be extended to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names within its framework?  
- Suggestion: Improve notation in Fig. 1 by explicitly defining functions γ, δ, and κ for clarity.  
- Positive: References are extensive and relevant, and parser plus datasets are made available.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with novel, effective unsupervised approach and practical benefits for low-resource languages; recommend acceptance.
