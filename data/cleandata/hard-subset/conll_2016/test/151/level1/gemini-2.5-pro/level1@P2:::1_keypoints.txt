- Introduces UDP, an unsupervised, no-training-data dependency parser tailored to the Universal Dependencies (UD) formalism using hand-crafted head-selection rules.  
- Core method: personalized PageRank on a graph from head rules to identify main predicate and rank content words.  
- Core method: two-step decoding that first attaches content words then function words, enforcing UD constraint that function words are leaves.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages from UD v1.2 and compared to a rule-based baseline and a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser (MSD).  
- Extensive analysis of robustness to POS tagging errors and domain shifts across genres.  
- Primary contribution: a simple, transparent, effective no-training-data baseline competitive with supervised transfer systems and robust across domains.  
- Secondary contribution: novel use of personalized PageRank to bias toward the sentence’s first verb for main predicate ID and content ordering.  
- Contribution: empirical case for leveraging UD’s explicit structural constraints and a small set of universal rules to rival data-driven methods.  
- Strength: strong performance for an unsupervised method (57.5% UAS with gold POS vs 63.9% for MSD), outperforming MSD on Hindi.  
- Strength: excellent robustness analysis showing lower cross-domain variance and less error propagation from predicted POS tags than MSD.  
- Strength: simplicity and interpretability from a few explicit rules and minimal parameters, making decisions and errors easy to analyze.  
- Strength: thorough, broad evaluation across 32 diverse languages with gold/predicted/naive POS experiments.  
- Weakness: development choices and some parameters were set on English, introducing English-centric bias and undermining universality claims.  
- Weakness: fixed attachment directions for several function-word categories (e.g., DET right-attaching) are problematic for languages with different typology.  
- Weakness: overly minimalist head-rule set imposes structural limits (e.g., excluding NOUN->VERB prevents generating relative clauses).  
- Weakness: framing as “unsupervised” is misleading since the system relies heavily on hand-crafted linguistic knowledge and UD guidelines—better described as knowledge-based or zero-shot.  
- Question: how do English-tuned fixed function-word attachment directions affect performance on typologically different languages, and can on-the-fly direction estimation be extended beyond adpositions?  
- Question: why was the NOUN->VERB rule excluded—did including it reduce precision elsewhere, and which structures were affected?  
- Question: sensitivity of the PageRank personalization factor (set to 5)—does it risk forcing the first verb as root in complex sentences?
