- Summary: Proposes UDP, a fully unsupervised UD parser combining simple POS-based head-selection rules, personalized PageRank, two-step decoding separating content/function words, and on-the-fly adposition direction estimation; evaluated on 32 treebanks against rule-based and delexicalized transfer baselines with cross-domain analysis.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel, training-free unsupervised parser that combines lightweight linguistic rules with personalized PageRank as an effective zero-shot baseline for UD parsing.

- Contribution 2: Provides a thorough cross-domain evaluation showing the rule-based system is significantly more robust to domain shift than a competitive data-driven parser.

- Strength 1: Method is simple yet effective, achieving 57.5% UAS with gold POS using few head rules and personalized PageRank, making it an accessible baseline.

- Strength 2: Demonstrates impressive robustness to domain shift with consistently lower performance variance (avg std 2.5 vs 4.5) than the delexicalized transfer parser.

- Strength 3: Evaluation is extensive and realistic (32 languages, predicted POS experiments), showing lower error propagation from POS mistakes than the MSD parser.

- Strength 4: Offers novel technical choices (personalized PageRank for root detection, two-step decoding) with ablation evidence that PageRank adds ~9 UAS points.

- Weakness 1: Key design choices (teleport prob, head rules, fixed function-word directions) were tuned on English, undermining language-agnostic claims and hurting languages like Basque.

- Weakness 2: Head-rule set is sparse and misses important constructions (e.g., NOUN→VERB for relatives), limiting syntactic coverage and capping potential performance.

- Weakness 3: Main baseline (BLG) may produce disconnected trees, making the comparison potentially unfair since UDP enforces valid single-rooted trees.

- Question 1: Have you considered extending the on-the-fly bigram-based direction estimation used for adpositions to other function words to reduce English bias?

- Question 2: Why were restrictive head rules (like excluding NOUN→VERB) chosen—did inclusion empirically harm development performance or was it a deliberate trade-off?

- Question 3: Can you clarify whether BLG UAS was computed on potentially disconnected outputs or if post-processing was applied to form trees?
