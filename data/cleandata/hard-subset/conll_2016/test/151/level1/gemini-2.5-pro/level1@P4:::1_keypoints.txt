- Proposes UDP: a training-free UD parser using POS-based head-selection rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and a two-step decoding that attaches content words first and enforces function words as leaves.  
- Claims three main contributions: a novel combination of PageRank and UD-based decoding, extensive 32-language evaluation close to supervised delexicalized transfer, and evidence that linguistically-informed rules improve domain robustness.  

Strengths:
- Method is technically simple, interpretable, and transparent (small rule set + PageRank + clear decoding).  
- On-the-fly estimation of adposition directionality increases adaptability without language-specific data.  
- Competitive unsupervised performance: average UAS 57.5 across 32 languages, only 6.4 points behind MSDG and better than MSDG on Hindi.  
- Robustness: smaller drop with predicted POS tags (2.2% vs 2.7% for MSDG) and much lower cross-domain variability (std dev ~2.5 vs 4.5).  
- Broad empirical scope: evaluation across 32 typologically diverse languages and multiple POS settings.

Weaknesses:
- Not entirely "training-free": several design choices (head rules, PageRank personalization 5x, teleport prob 0.05, fixed function-word directions) were tuned on English dev data, risking English bias.  
- Limited novelty: core ingredients (head-selection rules, PageRank) build on prior work; contributions are incremental adaptations to UD.  
- Overly constrained rule set reduces linguistic coverage (e.g., NOUN->VERB rule excluded, preventing analyses like relative clauses) with no detailed qualitative error analysis.  
- Baseline comparison issues: BLG sometimes produces ill-formed/ disconnected trees, making it unclear how much UDP's gains stem from PageRank versus a more robust decoding that always yields valid trees.

Questions posed to authors:
- How do English-fixed attachment directions for function words affect typologically different languages, and could on-the-fly direction estimation be extended beyond adpositions?  
- What is the quantitative trade-off of excluding the NOUN->VERB rule (impact on dev UAS and which constructions/error types are most affected)?  
- Why does UDPNoPR (linear-order processing) outperform BLG by 4 UAS points—is the gap due to BLG's inability to guarantee well-formed trees or other algorithmic differences?
