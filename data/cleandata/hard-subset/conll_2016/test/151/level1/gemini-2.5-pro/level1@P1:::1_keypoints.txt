- Introduces UDP, a completely unsupervised UD parser using a small set of head-attachment rules and personalized PageRank to identify central content words.  
- Uses a two-step decoding: parse content words by PageRank then attach function words as leaves, estimating adposition directionality from test data.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages and compared against a strong supervised multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD), showing competitive results and greater robustness to POS errors and domain shift.  
- Main contribution: a simple, transparent, no-training-data unsupervised baseline for UD parsing that performs strongly across languages.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive cross-lingual and cross-domain evaluation demonstrating broad applicability and stability under POS noise and domain shift.  
- Main contribution: novel use of personalized PageRank to up-weight the likely main predicate within a two-step decoding process.  
- Strength: elegant simplicity and strong empirical performance (within 6.4 UAS points of MSD on average).  
- Strength: rigorous evaluation across 32 languages, testing both gold and predicted POS tags and cross-domain robustness.  
- Strength: truly unsupervised and practical for low-resource languages, with only adposition directionality estimated from test data.  
- Strength: clear, reproducible presentation with code and data release.  
- Weakness: relies on a hand-crafted head-rule set tuned on English, with no sensitivity analysis or rule-induction experiments.  
- Weakness: many function-word head directions are hard-coded from English (e.g., DET, CONJ), reducing true language-independence and harming languages like Basque.  
- Weakness: comparison limited to one baseline paradigm (MSD); lacks comparisons to other unsupervised or projection-based transfer methods (e.g., DMV, annotation projection).  
- Weakness: evaluation uses only unlabeled attachment score (UAS); no labeled-dependency results or qualitative error analysis of UD relations.  
- Question: how were the head rules developed, how sensitive is performance to rule changes, and can rules be induced automatically?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the 5x PageRank personalization weight and does the optimal value vary by word-order typology?  
- Question: can on-the-fly directionality estimation be extended to other function categories (DET, CONJ) and would that improve performance on languages with non-English patterns?
