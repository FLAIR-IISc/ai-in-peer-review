- Presents UDP: a fully unsupervised UD parser using linguistically-motivated head-attachment rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and a two-step decoder that makes function words leaves.
- Main contribution: rigorous evaluation showing UDP is significantly more stable across domains than a strong supervised delexicalized transfer system (MSD).
- Main contribution: a simple, effective and transparent unsupervised algorithm combining personalized PageRank and two-step decoding as a strong baseline.
- Strength: exceptionally thorough cross-lingual evaluation across 32 UD languages with solid comparisons to MSD.
- Strength: strong robustness to domain shift, exhibiting much lower performance variance across domains than MSD.
- Strength: simplicity and accessibility—requires no training data, easy to understand, reproduce, and apply to new languages.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty, largely building on prior rule-aided parsing and PageRank ideas with incremental adaptations.
- Weakness: not fully language-agnostic—key head rules and attachment directions were tuned on English dev data, risking anglocentric bias.
- Weakness: struggles with complex syntactic constructions (relative clauses, conjunctions, punctuation), reducing utility for deep syntactic downstream tasks.
