- Proposes UDP, a fully training-free UD parser combining UD-motivated head-selection rules, personalized PageRank (PR) for clause head ranking, two-stage decoding (content then function words), and unsupervised ADP direction estimation, evaluated on 32 languages.  
- Contribution: A simple, training-free multilingual parser competitive with a strong delexicalized transfer baseline and more robust under domain shift.  
- Contribution: Effective use of personalized PageRank per sentence plus UD-aligned two-stage decoding, with PR contributing ≈9 UAS on average.  
- Contribution: Lightweight unsupervised on-the-fly adposition direction estimation and analyses of POS error propagation and domain variance.  
- Strength: Broad multilingual coverage and thorough evaluation across 32 UD treebanks, with UDP within ~6–7 UAS of the MSD baseline and surpassing it on Hindi.  
- Strength: Greater robustness to POS noise and domain shifts (lower POS error propagation and lower std.dev across domains than MSD).  
- Strength: Clear, linguistically grounded, interpretable design enforcing function-word leafness and single-root constraints; ADP direction inference is simple and effective (~75% ADP UAS).  
- Strength: Informative ablations and error analyses showing PR and decoding contributions and POS-wise diagnostics.  
- Strength: Simple, few hyperparameters, and likely reproducible with promised code/data.  
- Weakness: Missing direct comparison to Søgaard (2012b) and other stronger cross-lingual baselines to establish novelty and empirical gains.  
- Weakness: “Predicted POS” condition uses a target-language supervised tagger (TnT), weakening low-resource cross-lingual claims.  
- Weakness: ADP direction estimated on test sentences (peeking at test distribution), potentially inflating performance.  
- Weakness: Some rule choices tuned on English and hard-coded for function POS; not all function-word directions are learned and some design choices (e.g., NOUN→VERB exclusion) may hurt certain languages.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of structural properties, significance testing, non-projectivity, edge-lengths, error types, and parameter sensitivity.  
- Question: Can you compare to Søgaard (2012b) on UD v1.2 with matched POS, or explain infeasibility and expected differences?  
- Question: How do results change if ADP direction is estimated on held-out dev or unlabeled data instead of the test set?  
- Question: Have you tried estimating directionality for other function POS (DET, AUX, SCONJ, CONJ) as done for ADP, and what are the effects?  
- Question: Please provide sensitivity analysis for PR teleport probability and personalization weight; do verb-final languages benefit equally from “first verb” personalization?  
- Question: Can you run experiments with cross-lingual/projected or multilingual POS taggers to better simulate low-resource conditions and report UDP vs MSD under those tags?  
- Overall: A clear, simple, and strong unsupervised UD parser with valuable multilingual and robustness findings; acceptance is recommended pending clarification on baselines and test-dependence.
