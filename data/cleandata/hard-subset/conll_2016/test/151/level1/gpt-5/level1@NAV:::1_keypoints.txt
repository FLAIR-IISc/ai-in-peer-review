- Introduces UDP: a training-free UD parser combining UD head rules, personalized PageRank for content-word ranking, and two-step decoding enforcing function-word leafness.  
- Estimates adposition (ADP) attachment direction from test POS bigrams and evaluates on 32 languages and multiple domains.  
- Contribution: a strong, simple, language-agnostic unsupervised baseline with broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation.  
- Contribution: effective combination of UD constraints with personalized PageRank and two-step decoding encoding function-word leaf constraint.  
- Contribution: lightweight, test-time estimation of adposition directionality from POS bigrams, avoiding language-specific parameters.  
- Strength: Competitive accuracy without training—UAS within ~6–7 points of MSD (gold POS), outperforming MSD on Hindi, and lower POS error propagation.  
- Strength: Cross-domain stability—lower UAS variance across genres than MSD, showing robustness to domain shift.  
- Strength: Clear ablations and analysis—PR gives ~9 UAS gain and POS-wise/root accuracy breakdowns support design choices.  
- Strength: Simplicity and portability—minimal assumptions, no parallel data, near-constant hyperparameters, easy reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—rule-aided parsing and PR ranking are incremental over prior work.  
- Weakness: English-tuned decisions and ad hoc hyperparameters (e.g., function-word directions, teleport=0.05, weight=5) risk language bias.  
- Weakness: Strong dependence on POS quality—performance collapses with coarse CONTENT/FUNCTION tags; more realistic noisy-POS tests needed.  
- Weakness: Structural coverage gaps—excluding NOUN→VERB blocks relative clauses/limits coordination; evaluation is unlabeled only.  
- Question: Can AUX/DET/SCONJ attachment directions be estimated per language to reduce English bias?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to PR personalization weight and teleport probability across languages?  
- Question: Is ADP direction estimated per document or corpus-wide, and how many sentences are needed for reliable estimation in low-resource settings?  
- Question: What is the runtime profile and scalability (PR vs. decoding), and how often are outputs non-projective?
