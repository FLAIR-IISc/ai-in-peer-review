- Paper proposes UDP: a training-free, UD-compliant dependency parser combining UD head rules, personalized PageRank (PR), two-step decoding, and a transductive ADP-direction heuristic.  
- Main contribution: a strong, language-agnostic unsupervised UD baseline competitive with multi-source delexicalized transfer and more robust to domain shift.  
- Contribution: a simple linguistically-motivated pipeline (personalized PR + two-step decoding + UD constraints) that enforces UD design choices without training.  
- Contribution: an empirical study of domain robustness in UD parsing with curated domain splits and POS-noise analysis.  

- Strength: achieves competitive accuracy without training and shows lower POS error propagation and cross-domain variance than supervised transfer.  
- Strength: clear, linguistically grounded design—content-first decoding and function-word leaf enforcement align model bias with UD.  
- Strength: thoughtful analysis and ablations quantifying PR and decoding contributions and POS-wise breakdowns of strengths/weaknesses.  
- Strength: broad multilingual evaluation across 32 languages, with gold/predicted POS and realistic multi-genre domain experiments.  

- Weakness: limited novelty—approach is incremental relative to prior rule-aided/PR-based unsupervised parsers (e.g., Søgaard 2012b).  
- Weakness: transductive estimation of ADP direction on test data complicates fair comparison to supervised baselines and needs sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: small ruleset causes systematic blind spots (no NOUN→VERB for relatives, poor coordination/punctuation handling) with unquantified per-construction impact.  
- Weakness: baselines/reporting could be stronger—omits some prior unsupervised baselines, lacks per-language analysis where BLG outperforms UDPG, and reports no significance testing.  
- Weakness: methodological/reproducibility details are insufficient—PR graph construction, full head-rule list, edge weighting, and hyperparameter sensitivity across languages are under-specified.  

- Q: Provide the full head-rule inventory, δ constraints, and precise PR multigraph edge directions/weights—are dependents equally weighted?  
- Q: How sensitive is UDP to personalization weight and teleport probability across languages; were no-personalization or different seeds tried?  
- Q: How many sentences are used to estimate ADP direction per language, does estimation timing affect results, and can you ablate fixing direction from development?  
- Q: For languages where BLG > UDPG (e.g., Estonian), what failure modes cause PR to worsen decisions; is this linked to word-order or POS distributions?  
- Q: Can directionality for AUX/DET/SCONJ be estimated from data like ADP; any preliminary experiments?  
- Q: What are runtime and memory footprints for PR and decoding, and how do they scale with sentence length?  

- Suggest reporting results excluding punctuation for standard UD comparability alongside inclusive scores.  
- Suggest a labeled attachment experiment for a subset of edges (e.g., function-word labels) to show what simple constraints can achieve.  
- Suggest expanding coordination analysis and trying simple heuristics (e.g., attach cc/conj to highest-ranked conjunct).  
- Note formatting issues in references and tables that should be standardized for camera-ready.
