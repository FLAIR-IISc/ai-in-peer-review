- Paper introduces UDP: a training-free UD parser combining POS-based head rules, personalized PageRank (PR), two-step decoding enforcing function-word leafness, and test-time ADP direction estimation.

- Evaluated on 32 UD languages and multiple domains, compared mainly to a multi-source delexicalized (MSD) transfer baseline.

- Contribution: a simple UD-compliant unsupervised parser with two-step decoding that attains competitive UAS across languages and domains.

- Contribution: a personalized PageRank setup to rank content words and identify roots, with ablation showing substantial gains over rules-only.

- Contribution: on-the-fly ADP attachment direction estimation from test data, reducing language-specific parameters and improving robustness.

- Strength: broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation; UDP is surprisingly competitive with MSD and shows smaller drop with predicted POS.

- Strength: UDP exhibits lower cross-domain variance than MSD, suggesting reduced sensitivity to domain shift.

- Strength: Ablations isolate PR’s impact (~+9 UAS), decoding impact (~+4 UAS), and PR personalization (~+2 UAS) with ~69% average root accuracy.

- Strength: Minimal language-specific parameters (ADP estimated from data; fixed function-word directions generally effective).

- Strength: Methodological transparency and simplicity—precise algorithm description, deterministic behavior for a POS sequence, and complexity analysis.

- Strength: Quantified error propagation from POS to dependencies showing relative robustness (0.37 vs 0.46 at 94.1% POS accuracy).

- Weakness: Dependence on supervised POS tagging (TnT trained on target) undermines claims of true low-resource operation and complicates fair comparisons.

- Weakness: Incremental novelty—core ideas (PR ranking, rule-aided decoding) predate this work; novelties are mainly UD adaptation and engineering choices.

- Weakness: Limited syntactic coverage and reliance on back-offs; poor punctuation and coordination handling (PUNCT UAS ~21%, CONJ ~38%) and potential local violations of UD constraints.

- Weakness: Baselines and evaluation choices incomplete—missing comparison to other cross-lingual/typology methods, rule-based baseline limitations, and no significance testing reported.

- Weakness: Development choices tuned on English (head rules, PR params), risking bias and imperfect transfer (e.g., Basque determiner issue).

- Question: Can UDP operate with cross-lingually projected or unsupervised POS tags, and how does performance compare to the TnT setup?

- Question: How sensitive are results to PR teleport probability and personalization weight, and can these be estimated per language/sentence without dev tuning?

- Question: Have you tried estimating directionality for other function POS (DET, AUX, SCONJ) from test data, and what trade-offs arise?

- Question: What is the runtime profile per sentence length and language (including PR) and how does it compare to MSD at test time?

- Question: How often do back-off cases occur where rules/directionality are dropped, and what proportion of attachments then violate UD leafness/direction constraints?

- Question: Can the method be extended to labeled parsing via simple heuristics, and what label accuracy is expected?

- Additional: Report per-language significance against MSD and UDPNoPR to quantify robustness claims.

- Additional: Given low PUNCT performance, report UAS with and without punctuation for completeness.

- Additional: Clarify minor inconsistencies/typos and release code/data to ensure reproducibility.
