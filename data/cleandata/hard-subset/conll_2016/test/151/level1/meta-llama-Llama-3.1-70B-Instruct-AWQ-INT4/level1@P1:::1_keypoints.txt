- Contribution: Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using personalized PageRank and a small set of head-dependent rules within the UD formalism.
- Contribution: Requires no training data and estimates adposition direction from test data.
- Contribution: Competitive performance on most UD languages, outperforming a multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD) on Hindi.
- Strength: Novel, linguistically grounded approach that leverages UD constraints and differs from traditional data-driven methods.
- Strength: Strong empirical results across languages, with high performance except for two languages.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation across POS setups and domains, giving insight into strengths and weaknesses.
- Strength: Robust to domain changes and less affected by domain shifts than the MSD baseline.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on the UD formalism may limit applicability to languages or domains that don’t conform to UD.
- Weakness: Small set of head-dependent rules may be insufficient to capture the complexity of all languages; rule coverage and limits not analyzed.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art unsupervised dependency parsers beyond the DMV baseline.
- Weakness: No thorough analysis of error propagation or detailed quantification of POS deterioration effects.
- Question: How will the parser be extended to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?
- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the head-dependent rules’ coverage and limitations?
- Question: How do the authors plan to adapt the parser to languages or domains that do not conform to the UD formalism?
- Question: Can the authors provide a more comprehensive evaluation against other state-of-the-art unsupervised parsers?
- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of error propagation rates and their impact on parser performance?
