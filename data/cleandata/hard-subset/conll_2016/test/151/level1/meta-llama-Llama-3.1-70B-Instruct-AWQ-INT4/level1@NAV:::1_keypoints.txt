- UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using rule-based head selection and PageRank, evaluated on 32 languages, competitive and more domain-robust than a delexicalized transfer system.
- Contribution: a novel unsupervised dependency parsing method that requires no training data.
- Contribution: introduces PageRank-based scoring of content words to identify the sentence’s main predicate.
- Contribution: demonstrates linguistic soundness and robustness across languages and domains versus a transfer baseline.
- Strength: achieves competitive performance with a state-of-the-art multi-source delexicalized transfer system.
- Strength: shows strong robustness to domain shifts and cross-lingual variation.
- Strength: enforces UD-compliant POS relations and function-word constraints for linguistically plausible parses.
- Strength: computationally efficient via PageRank scoring and a two-step decoding procedure.
- Weakness: may degrade on complex sentences with multiple clauses or nested dependencies.
- Weakness: relies on POS tag quality, so errors in tagging can hurt performance.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to 32 languages, leaving generalization to other languages uncertain.
