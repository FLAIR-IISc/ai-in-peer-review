- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD-compliant parser using personalized PageRank and a small set of head-dependent rules; requires no training and infers adposition direction from test data.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages in three POS-reliability setups and compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer system.  
- Contribution: novel unsupervised dependency parsing approach based on personalized PageRank and simple linguistic rules.  
- Contribution: produces Universal Dependencies–compliant parses, aiding cross-lingual consistency.  
- Contribution: demonstrates greater robustness to domain change than a delexicalized transfer baseline.  
- Strength: achieves competitive performance with delexicalized transfer across 32 languages.  
- Strength: more robust to domain shifts across languages.  
- Strength: simple, linguistically motivated method that is easy to apply.  
- Strength: requires no annotated training data, useful for low-resource languages.  
- Strength: shown applicable across a wide set of languages (32).  
- Weakness: evaluation uses only unlabeled attachment score (UAS), omitting other metrics.  
- Weakness: no comparisons to other unsupervised parsers, limiting contextual performance assessment.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed error analysis to explain failure modes.  
- Weakness: no explicit out-of-domain evaluation despite claims of robustness.  
- Question: How does UDP perform on morphologically complex languages (e.g., Arabic, Russian)?  
- Question: Can UDP handle non-projective dependencies?  
- Question: How robust is UDP to incorrect or missing POS tags?  
- Question: Can UDP parse texts from different genres or domains effectively?  
- Question: How does UDP compare to other unsupervised approaches (graph-based or neural) in practice?
