- Summary: UDP is an unsupervised UD parser using POS constraints and personalized PageRank, needs no training, estimates adposition direction from test data, and is evaluated on 32 languages showing competitive, domain-robust performance.

- Contribution 1: Proposes a novel unsupervised dependency parsing method that exploits Universal Dependencies constraints.

- Contribution 2: Introduces a personalized PageRank technique to estimate content-word relevance and improve main predicate identification.

- Contribution 3: Presents a simple, linguistically motivated rule-based cross-lingual parsing approach claimed to be robust to domain shifts.

- Strength: Well-motivated, innovative approach that leverages UD formalism effectively.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation across 32 languages, demonstrating competitiveness with a supervised transfer system.

- Strength: Detailed analyses across parts of speech and domains provide useful insights into strengths and weaknesses.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on POS tag quality, which may degrade performance for low-resource languages.

- Weakness: Performance sensitive to chosen head rules and adposition direction estimation, which may be inaccurate.

- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparison to other unsupervised dependency parsing methods, limiting assessment of novelty and impact.

- Question: How will POS tag quality issues, especially for low-resource languages, be addressed?

- Question: Please provide more details on head-rule choices and adposition direction estimation and their effect on performance.

- Question: How do the authors envision UDP being used in practice and what are its potential applications?

- Additional comment: The paper is clearly written and analyzed but would benefit from a more detailed comparison with other unsupervised parsers.
