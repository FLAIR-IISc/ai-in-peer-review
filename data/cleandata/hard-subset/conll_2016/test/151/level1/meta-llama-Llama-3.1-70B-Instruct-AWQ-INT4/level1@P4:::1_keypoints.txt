- Summary: Proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using personalized PageRank plus a small set of head-dependent rules, needing no training and estimating adposition direction from test data; evaluated on 32 languages against a delexicalized transfer baseline.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel unsupervised dependency parsing method that requires no training data.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates effective use of personalized PageRank combined with simple head-dependent rules to achieve competitive performance.

- Contribution 3: Shows robustness to domain shifts, with lower standard deviation across domains compared to a multi-source delexicalized parser.

- Strength 1: Achieves competitive performance on most UD languages and outperforms the baseline on Hindi.

- Strength 2: More stable performance across domains, indicating good generalization.

- Strength 3: Resource-efficient approach suitable for low-resource languages since it needs no training data.

- Weakness 1: Limited coverage of complex linguistic phenomena (e.g., relative clauses) due to reliance on a small rule set.

- Weakness 2: Performance depends on POS tag quality, which can be problematic in low-resource settings.

- Weakness 3: Lacks an explicit error analysis to clarify failure modes and strengths.

- Question 1: How will UDP be extended to handle more complex linguistic constructions like relative clauses?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide deeper insight or comparisons on why personalized PageRank is effective versus other ranking methods?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to mitigate UDP's reliance on POS tags, especially for low-resource languages?
