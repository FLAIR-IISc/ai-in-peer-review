This paper presents a method for parsing trees (i.e., universal dependency trees) using a modified version of PageRank, which relies solely on POS (Part of Speech) to give more importance to certain meaningful words (as opposed to stop words). The idea is interesting, although similar to the work done in Sogard's (2012) paper. The personalization factor, which gives more importance to the main predicate, is good, but it would be better to take it to the next level. As far as I can tell, personalization is only used for the main predicate, and its weight seems arbitrary at 5.

Regarding evaluation and detailed analysis, some charts would be beneficial, as it is sometimes difficult to extract insights from tables. Finally, it would be interesting to see whether a decline in parsing performance is highly correlated with a decline in tagging performance (which we would expect) in prediction mode, obtaining POS tagging scores.

Overall, the paper is interesting but falls short of Sogard's (2012) work.
