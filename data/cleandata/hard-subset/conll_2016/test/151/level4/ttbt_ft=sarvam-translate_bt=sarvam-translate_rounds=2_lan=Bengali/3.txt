This paper presents a way to parse trees using a modified version of PageRank (i.e., a Universal Dependency Tree Bank), relying solely on POS (Part of Speech) to give more weight to certain meaningful words (as opposed to stop words). The idea is interesting, although it closely resembles what was done in Sogaard's (2012) paper. While more weight is good in the main rule, it could be improved. As far as I know, personalization is only used in the main rule and its weight is 5, which seems intentional.

In terms of evaluation and detailed analysis, some charts would be beneficial, as extracting the key points from the table can sometimes be difficult. Finally, it would be good to get POS tagging scores in prediction mode, to see if the decline in parsing performance is closely related to the decline in tagging performance (which we hope for).

Overall, the paper is interesting, but its improvement is slight compared to Sogaard's (2012) work.
