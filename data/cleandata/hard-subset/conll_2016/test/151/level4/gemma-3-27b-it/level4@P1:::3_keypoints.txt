- Proposes an unsupervised UD parser using only POS tags and a modified PageRank to prefer content words over function words.
- Personalization gives extra weight to the main predicate (factor set to 5), but the choice and scope of personalization seem arbitrary and underexplored.
- Approach is closely related to Søgaard (2012); incremental improvement over that prior work appears modest.
- Suggests robustness to domain shift and competitive performance compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser (MSD), even outperforming MSD on Hindi.
- Demonstrates effective exploitation of UD constraints (single-rooted trees, function word roles) for rule-based unsupervised parsing.
- Evaluation is broad and thorough (32 languages, gold vs predicted POS, cross-domain), supporting generalizability claims.
- Claims lower sensitivity to domain changes than MSD, evidenced by lower standard deviation across domains.
- Lack of systematic justification or tuning analysis for the personalization parameter (value 5).
- Missing visualizations; reliance on tables makes extracting key trends harder.
- Did not report POS tagging accuracy in the predicted-POS setup, preventing assessment of parsing degradation vs tagging errors.
- Requests clearer, detailed comparison to Søgaard (2012) to delineate novel contributions.
- Minor issue: typo "The the main idea" (Line 207).
