- Introduces UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank plus dependency head rules, requiring no labeled training data.  
- Main contribution: a robust, competitive unsupervised parsing approach that reduces reliance on language-specific training.  
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates greater robustness to domain shift compared to supervised baselines.  
- Extensive evaluation: tested across 32 languages and multiple POS-tagging setups (gold, predicted, naive).  
- Strength — Competitive performance: often matches or outperforms multi-source delexicalized transfer (MSD), e.g., on Hindi.  
- Strength — Robustness to domain shift: lower UAS variance across domains than MSD, indicating stability on unseen data.  
- Strength — Comprehensive evaluation: thorough experiments across languages and POS conditions strengthen validity.  
- Strength — Clear exposition: well-written paper with detailed algorithm explanation, decoding figure, and parsing example.  
- Strength — Accessibility/reproducibility: parser and annotated datasets released publicly.  
- Weakness — Limited handling of complex structures: no explicit treatment of coordination or multiword expressions.  
- Weakness — Reliance on initial head rules: rules derived from English may limit cross-lingual generalization; sensitivity noted.  
- Weakness — Vulnerable to POS errors: performance drops under naive POS tags, indicating dependence on POS quality.  
- Weakness — Personalization bias: PageRank personalization to the first verb may misidentify main predicates in complex sentences.  
- Question — Sensitivity to PageRank teleport probability: how much does performance vary with teleport parameter?  
- Question — Automatic adaptation of head rules: can head rules be induced or adapted from unlabeled data for other languages?  
- Question — Mitigating POS errors: what strategies can reduce dependence on high-quality POS without stronger taggers?  
- Question — Plans to handle coordination/MWEs: are extensions planned to cover coordination and multiword expressions?  
- Additional comment: paper is thorough and clear, representing a significant advancement in unsupervised dependency parsing.
