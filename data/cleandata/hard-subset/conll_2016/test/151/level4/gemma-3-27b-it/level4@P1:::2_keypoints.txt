- Contribution: Proposes an unsupervised UD parser requiring no training data, useful for low-resource languages.  
- Contribution: Combines linguistically motivated rules with PageRank to achieve competitive performance vs delexicalized transfer.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates robustness to domain change, reducing overfitting compared to supervised approaches.  

- Strength: Achieves strong, consistent performance across 32 languages, nearing delexicalized transfer (Table 5).  
- Strength: Shows lower performance variance across domains than supervised baselines, indicating domain robustness (Tables 6–7).  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written with detailed methodology and an illustrative example parse, aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength: Method is linguistically grounded (POS constraints, function words as leaves), avoiding spurious data-driven correlations.  

- Weakness: Fixed rule set limits expressiveness and ability to capture complex syntactic phenomena (e.g., exclusion of NOUN → VERB).  
- Weakness: Performance is sensitive to POS tagging quality; predicted POS tags degrade results despite some robustness.  
- Weakness: Heuristic head-direction determination (adposition frequency) may not generalize across languages, causing variable performance (e.g., Basque).  

- Question: What experiments were run including the NOUN → VERB rule, and what specific problems led to its exclusion?  
- Question: Have the authors considered a feedback loop to refine POS tags using the dependency parse?  
- Question: Have alternative PageRank personalization strategies been explored (e.g., weighting other content words by frequency or semantic role)?  

- Additional: Links to data and code are appreciated.  
- Additional: The ablation study (Section 6) is insightful for component contributions.  
- Additional: Experimental setup and evaluation metrics are clearly and thoroughly explained.
