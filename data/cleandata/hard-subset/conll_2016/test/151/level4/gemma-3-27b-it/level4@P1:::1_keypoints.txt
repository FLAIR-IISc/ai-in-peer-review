- Proposes a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm evaluated across multiple languages.  
- Algorithm core: rules defining permitted dependencies based on POS tags.  
- Ranks words using a slightly biased PageRank over a graph whose edges are the permitted dependencies.  
- Constructs trees by linking each word to the closest permitted head that preserves treehood and obeys a directionality constraint.  
- Paper is interesting and clearly presented but seems only slightly different from Søgaard (2012).  
- Request to analyze head rules against the corpus (e.g., how often they yield disconnected graphs and component sizes).  
- Notes head-directionality was chosen using test data rather than train/dev, though decision converges quickly.  
- Asks how ties in PageRank scores are broken and whether tie-breaking affects performance.  
- Suggests reporting oracle results per constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show upper bounds and guide future work.  
- Minor editorial points: wording fixes (4.1, 6.1) and inconsistent column orders between Table 5 and Table 7.
