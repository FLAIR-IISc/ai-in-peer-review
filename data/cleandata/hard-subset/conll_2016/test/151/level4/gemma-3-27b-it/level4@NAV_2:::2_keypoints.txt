- Paper introduces UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank plus dependency head rules, requiring no training data.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates a viable, competitive unsupervised UD parsing approach comparable to delexicalized transfer without training.  
- Combines PageRank personalization for predicate identification with a two-step decoding that separates content vs function words.  
- Shows robustness to domain shifts across languages, suggesting better generalization than typical data-driven methods.  

- Strength: Competitive performance — average UAS 63.9% across languages, close to multi-source delexicalized transfer (MSDG).  
- Strength: Robustness to domain shift — lower UAS variance across domains than supervised MSDG.  
- Strength: Clear and detailed exposition of algorithm, design choices, and implementation.  
- Strength: Effective incorporation of UD constraints (single-root trees, peripheral function words) improving accuracy and generalization.  
- Strength: Practicality — parser and evaluation datasets released for reproducibility.  

- Weakness: Sensitive to POS tagging — significant performance drop with predicted or noisy POS tags.  
- Weakness: Limited handling of complex phenomena (multiword expressions, coordination, proper names).  
- Weakness: Reliance on pre-defined head rules may limit cross-lingual applicability and introduce biases.  
- Weakness: Performance varies considerably across languages; some languages have much lower UAS.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to UAS; lacks labeled metrics (LAS) or other dependency quality measures.  

- Question: How would handling multiword expressions and coordination affect UDP performance?  
- Question: Can head rules be learned or refined from unlabeled data to improve cross-lingual generalization?  
- Question: What is the impact of POS tagging errors on UDP for languages with low POS accuracy?  
- Question: What challenges arise when extending UDP to languages with substantially different syntax or word order?
