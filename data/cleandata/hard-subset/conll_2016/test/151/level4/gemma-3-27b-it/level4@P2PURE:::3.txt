This paper introduces a method for parsing dependency trees (specifically, Universal Dependency treebanks) that relies solely on part-of-speech tags and employs a modified PageRank algorithm to prioritize meaningful words over stop words. This approach is interesting, though conceptually similar to previous work by Søgaard (2012). The personalization factor, which increases the weight given to the main predicate, is a positive addition, but its potential impact could have been further explored. Specifically, the personalization appears to be applied only to the main predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 seems empirically determined rather than theoretically justified.

Concerning the evaluation and analysis, the inclusion of charts would have improved clarity and facilitated interpretation of the results presented in the tables. Furthermore, reporting the part-of-speech tagging accuracy in the prediction setup would be valuable to determine whether the observed decrease in parsing performance correlates with a decrease in tagging performance, as would be expected.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting contribution, but the improvement over Søgaard (2012) is incremental.
