The authors propose an unsupervised algorithm for Universal Dependencies that requires no training data. The approach leverages PageRank for word tagging, supplemented by a small set of hard-coded rules.

This article is well-written, detailed, and clearly explains the rationale behind incorporating prior information into the model. 

I believe the contribution to the field of unsupervised parsing is substantial. Furthermore, the authors’ suggestions for future work offer promising avenues for further research.

The experimental setup is comprehensive, covering a variety of languages and datasets. The reported results demonstrate competitive performance compared to existing unsupervised methods, particularly considering the simplicity of the proposed approach. However, the performance gap compared to supervised systems remains significant, which is to be expected in an unsupervised setting. It would be beneficial to include a more direct comparison to strong baseline systems, perhaps including recent neural unsupervised parsing models, to better contextualize the results.

My main concern lies with the reliance on hard-coded rules. While the authors acknowledge this limitation, the number and impact of these rules on the overall performance warrant further investigation. A more thorough ablation study, systematically removing or modifying these rules, could help quantify their contribution and identify potential areas for improvement or generalization. It is not entirely clear how sensitive the system is to these rules, or how easily they could be adapted to new languages without significant manual effort.

The analysis of the errors made by the system is insightful. The authors correctly identify challenges related to long-range dependencies and ambiguous word senses. Addressing these issues could involve incorporating more sophisticated techniques for disambiguation or exploring alternative graph-based representations that better capture contextual information. 

The presentation of the results is clear, and the tables and figures are well-designed and easy to understand. The authors also provide a useful discussion of the computational complexity of the algorithm.

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a novel and promising approach to unsupervised parsing. The method is conceptually elegant and achieves competitive results. Addressing the concerns regarding the hard-coded rules and providing a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art baselines would further strengthen the work. I recommend accepting this paper for publication.
