This paper introduces a method for parsing dependency trees (specifically, Universal Dependency Treebanks) that utilizes solely part-of-speech tags and a modified PageRank algorithm. This modification prioritizes informative words over stop words. The core idea is appealing, however, it bears strong resemblance to previous work by Søgaard (2012). The incorporation of a personalization factor to emphasize the head predicate is a positive step, but further development in this area would be valuable. Specifically, the personalization appears to be limited to the primary predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 lacks clear justification. 

Concerning the evaluation and analysis, the inclusion of charts would enhance clarity and facilitate quicker comprehension of the results presented in the tables. Furthermore, reporting POS tagging scores in a predictive setting would be informative, allowing for an assessment of whether declines in parsing performance correlate with any degradation in tagging accuracy – a relationship one would reasonably expect.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach, but the advancements over Søgaard (2012) are incremental.
