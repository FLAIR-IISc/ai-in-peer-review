This paper describes a new deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and analyses its behaviour across a range of languages. The core of the algorithm is a set of rules defining permitted dependencies based on POS tags. The algorithm begins by ranking words using a slightly biased PageRank over a graph with edges defined by these permitted dependencies. Stepping through the ranking, each word is linked to the closest word that maintains a tree structure and is permitted by the head rules and a directionality constraint. Overall, the paper is interesting and clearly presented, though it appears to differ only slightly from Sogaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training".

I have a few questions and suggestions:

Regarding the Head Rules (Table 1), it would be beneficial to include some analysis of these rules in relation to the corpus used. For example, Section 3.1 mentions that the rules do not always lead to a connected graph, but does not quantify how frequently this occurs, or the typical size of the resulting components. I was also surprised that head direction was chosen using the test data rather than training or development data. While the rapid convergence of the decision (10-15 sentences) mitigates this as a major issue, it remains a somewhat unexpected choice. 

Could the authors elaborate on how tie-breaking is handled for words with identical PageRank scores? Does this process impact performance significantly, or are ties rare enough to be negligible? Furthermore, the various constraints – head rules, directionality, and distance – inherently impose upper bounds on the system’s possible performance. Including oracle results for each constraint would be informative, demonstrating the extent to which they limit the maximum achievable score. This would be particularly valuable in guiding future work and identifying areas for potential modification of the system.

Minor comments:

- In Section 4.1, change "we obtain [the] rank" to "we obtain the rank".
- Table 5 and Table 7 present columns in different orders. I found the arrangement in Table 7 to be clearer.
- In Section 6.1, change "isolate the [contribution] of both" to "isolate the contribution of both".
