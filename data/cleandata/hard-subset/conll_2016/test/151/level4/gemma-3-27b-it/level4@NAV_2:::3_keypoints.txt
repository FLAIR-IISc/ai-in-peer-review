- Unsupervised UD parser using PageRank and dependency head rules; requires no training data.
- Core idea: leverage POS constraints and PageRank to prioritize content words; closely related to Søgaard (2012).
- Personalization of PageRank to emphasize the main predicate is a useful extension, but the chosen weight (5) seems arbitrary.
- Contribution: robust, unsupervised parsing approach competitive with delexicalized transfer systems across many UD languages.
- Contribution: demonstrates greater stability to domain shift compared to supervised transfer learning.
- Contribution: effectively integrates UD constraints (notably function word placement) into an unsupervised framework.
- Strength: Competitive performance across 32 languages, often rivaling MSD delexicalized transfer.
- Strength: Greater robustness to domain changes, indicating reduced sensitivity to genre variation.
- Strength: Effective use of UD linguistic constraints enhances robustness and generalizability.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across languages, POS setups (gold/predicted), domains, and ablation studies.
- Weakness: Incremental novelty relative to prior work (especially Søgaard 2012); personalization could be explored further.
- Weakness: Arbitrary selection of PageRank personalization weight without justification or systematic analysis.
- Weakness: Dense numerical tables make interpretation difficult; visualizations would improve clarity.
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of correlation between POS tagging accuracy and parsing performance in predicted POS setup.
- Question: What is the rationale for weight=5 in PageRank personalization and were alternatives explored?
- Question: What is the computational complexity of the algorithm, particularly PageRank for long sentences?
- Question: Have alternative approaches been considered for languages (e.g., Japanese) with different data representations or lacking word forms?
- Additional: Typo on line 207 ("The the main idea") should be fixed.
- Additional: Paper should discuss limitations (e.g., handling long-distance dependencies or complex syntax).
- Additional: Releasing the parser and annotated datasets is a valuable resource for the community.
