- Introduces UDP: unsupervised UD parsing using POS constraints and personalized PageRank to identify main predicates, aiming for parsing without training data (related to Søgaard 2012).  
- Personalization of PageRank (weight=5) is a useful addition but the chosen weight lacks strong justification.  
- Contribution: shows competitive unsupervised parsing across many languages using a simple rule-based, UD-grounded approach.  
- Contribution: incorporates personalized PageRank to improve main-predicate identification and parsing accuracy.  
- Contribution: demonstrates robust cross-domain parsing with greater stability than a delexicalized transfer (MSD) baseline.  
- Strength: strong cross-lingual performance on 32 languages without language-specific training data.  
- Strength: robustness to domain shift, with lower performance variance across domains than the supervised baseline.  
- Strength: principled, linguistically motivated approach leveraging UD constraints; interpretable and generalizable without large ML models.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation (gold vs predicted POS, many languages) and comparison to a strong transfer baseline.  
- Weakness: incremental novelty over Søgaard (2012); needs deeper exploration and justification of the PageRank weighting scheme.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on tables; paper would benefit from visualizations to clarify results across languages/domains.  
- Weakness: missing POS tagging accuracy reporting for the predicted POS setup and lack of correlation analysis between POS and parsing errors.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis—more detailed identification of common error patterns and causes is needed.  
- Question: why was personalization weight set to 5 for the main predicate and were other values evaluated?  
- Question: please provide POS tagging accuracy for the predicted POS setup, preferably broken down by language.  
- Question: what types of errors does UDP most frequently make, with examples and possible mitigation strategies?  
- Question: what is the computational complexity of the system, especially the PageRank step, and is it scalable to very large datasets?  
- Additional comment: correct typo “The the main idea” → “The main idea.”  
- Additional comment: discuss limitations, e.g., handling complex syntactic structures or typologically divergent languages.
