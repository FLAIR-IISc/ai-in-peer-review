This paper presents a method for parsing dependency trees – specifically, Universal Dependency treebanks – by leveraging only part-of-speech (POS) tags and employing a modified version of the PageRank algorithm. This modification prioritizes meaningful words over stop words. The core idea is interesting, though closely related to the work presented in Søgaard (2012). The personalization factor, which assigns greater weight to the main predicate, is a positive addition, but could have been explored further. As far as I can determine, personalization is applied solely to the main predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 appears arbitrary.

Concerning the evaluation and detailed analyses, the inclusion of charts would have been beneficial, as extracting key insights from the tables can be challenging at times. Furthermore, reporting the POS tagging scores in the prediction mode would be valuable. This would allow an assessment of whether degradation in parsing performance correlates with degradation in tagging performance, as one would anticipate.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach, but the advancement over Søgaard (2012) is incremental.
