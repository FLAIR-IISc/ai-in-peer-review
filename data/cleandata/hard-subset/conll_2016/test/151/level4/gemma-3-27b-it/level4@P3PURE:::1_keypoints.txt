- Paper proposes a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank over permitted-dependency graphs, and nearest-permitted-head linking under tree/direction constraints.  
- The method appears to differ only slightly from Søgaard (2012) "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training."  
- Request analysis of head-rule behaviour on the corpus: how often the rules produce a disconnected graph and typical component sizes.  
- Concern that head-directionality was chosen using test data rather than training/dev data, despite rapid convergence (10–15 sentences).  
- Ask how ties in PageRank scores are broken and whether tie-breaking materially affects performance.  
- Recommend reporting oracle/upper-bound results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show how they limit maximum achievable performance.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: fix wording in Section 4.1 ("we obtain the rank"), make Table 5/7 column orders consistent (prefer Table 7 order), and fix phrasing in Section 6.1 ("isolate the contribution of both").
