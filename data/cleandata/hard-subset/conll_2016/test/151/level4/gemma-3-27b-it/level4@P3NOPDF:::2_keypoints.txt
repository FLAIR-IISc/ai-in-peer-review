- Proposes an unsupervised UD parsing algorithm that requires no training data.
- Core method applies PageRank over sentence words combined with a small set of manual linguistic rules.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, detailed, and easy to follow.
- Authors clearly explain the intuition for incorporating prior linguistic knowledge.
- Algorithm description, implementation details, and results presentation are thorough and clear.
- Contribution is significant for unsupervised parsing and useful for resource-poor languages or rapid prototyping.
- Eliminating the training step is a major practical advantage.
- Reported results are competitive with existing unsupervised methods, with effective analysis of strengths and limitations.
- Authors acknowledge limitations: dependence on accurate word segmentation and weaker performance on highly ambiguous sentences.
- Useful future directions suggested: different PageRank weighting schemes, automatic rule learning, and extensions for complex linguistic phenomena.
- Clarity about how PageRank and linguistic rules interact is commendable, aided by illustrative examples.
- Minor improvements recommended: more extensive comparison to supervised baselines for context.
- Minor improvements recommended: deeper error analysis with concrete failing parse examples and explanations.
- Recommendation to add a brief discussion motivating why PageRank is particularly well-suited to dependency parsing.
- Overall assessment: strong, insightful contribution; recommend acceptance.
