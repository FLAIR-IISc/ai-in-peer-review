1. Deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules and a slightly biased PageRank to rank words, linking each word to the nearest permitted head while preserving tree constraints.

2. Main contribution: competitive unsupervised parser leveraging UD constraints that approaches supervised transfer performance without training data.

3. Novel technical contribution: personalized PageRank combined with a two-step decoding that separately handles content vs. function words per UD guidelines.

4. Demonstrated robustness: strong cross-domain stability with lower UAS variance compared to supervised MSD baselines.

5. Competitive empirical results: UDP often matches or exceeds MSDG and maintains a small performance gap when using predicted POS tags.

6. Robustness to domain shift: extensive cross-domain evaluation shows greater resilience and less domain-specific bias than supervised methods.

7. UD-grounded design: explicit use of UD conventions (POS relations, function words as leaves) contributes to generalizability and robustness.

8. Efficient and deterministic: avoids probabilistic training, offering a straightforward, predictable parsing process via two-step decoding and PageRank.

9. Limited novelty: constituent methods (PageRank, rule-based parsing, UD constraints) are not novel; contribution is primarily their integration.

10. Sensitivity to POS quality: parser performance significantly degrades with noisy/predicted POS tags.

11. Head-rule limitations: current head rules (Table 1) are limited and fail on constructions like relative clauses, reducing cross-language coverage.

12. Insufficient error analysis: more in-depth investigation of error types and causes is needed to guide improvements.

14. Question: how are ties in PageRank scores broken and what effect does tie-breaking have on performance?
