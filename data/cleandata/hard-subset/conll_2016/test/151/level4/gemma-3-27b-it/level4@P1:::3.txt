This paper presents a method for parsing Universal Dependency treebanks without training, relying solely on part-of-speech tags and a modified version of PageRank to prioritize content words over function words. This approach is interesting, though closely related to previous work by Søgaard (2012). The personalization factor, which gives more weight to the main predicate, is a valuable addition, but could be further developed. As far as I can tell, the personalization is solely applied to the main predicate, and the weight of 5 appears somewhat arbitrary.

Regarding the evaluation and detailed analyses, visualizations such as charts would be beneficial, as extracting key insights from the tables can be challenging. Furthermore, it would have been insightful to report the POS tagging accuracy in the prediction mode, allowing for an assessment of whether the observed degradation in parsing performance correlates with the degradation in tagging performance – a relationship one would expect. 

Overall, the paper is interesting, but the incremental improvement over Søgaard (2012) seems relatively modest.

**Contributions:** The primary contribution of this work is a competitive, unsupervised parsing approach for Universal Dependencies that leverages the constraints of the UD formalism and demonstrates robustness to domain shift. A secondary contribution lies in the adaptation of PageRank personalization to improve main predicate identification, and the direct estimation of adposition attachment direction on test data.

**Strengths:**

1. **Competitive Performance:** The UDP parser achieves remarkably competitive results, closely approaching the performance of a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser (MSD) across a wide range of languages (Table 5). This demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach and its potential as a viable alternative to supervised methods. The fact that it outperforms MSD on Hindi is particularly noteworthy.

2. **Robustness to Domain Shift:** The experiments demonstrate that UDP is less sensitive to domain changes compared to MSD (Table 6). This robustness is a significant advantage, especially for low-resource languages or applications where training data from the target domain is unavailable. The lower standard deviation in performance across domains for UDP, compared to MSD, strongly supports this claim.

3. **Effective Use of UD Constraints:** The parser intelligently exploits the constraints inherent in the Universal Dependencies framework, such as the single-rooted tree structure and the role of function words. This allows for a surprisingly effective unsupervised parsing system, validating the authors' hypothesis that UD lends itself well to rule-based approaches.

4. **Clear and Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper presents a thorough evaluation using a large number of languages (32) and various experimental setups, including gold POS tags, predicted POS tags, and cross-domain analysis. This comprehensive evaluation provides strong evidence for the effectiveness and generalizability of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty:** While the results are impressive, the core idea of combining rule-based parsing with PageRank builds directly upon existing work, particularly Søgaard (2012). The paper does not fully articulate how the presented approach significantly advances the state-of-the-art beyond this prior work. A more detailed comparison and justification of the specific contributions compared to Søgaard (2012) would be beneficial.

2. **Arbitrary Parameter Choices:** The personalization factor of 5 for the main predicate in the PageRank algorithm seems arbitrary and lacks a clear justification. While the results demonstrate its effectiveness, a more systematic investigation of this parameter, or a data-driven method for determining its optimal value, would strengthen the work.

3. **Lack of Visualizations:** The paper relies heavily on tables to present the results. The inclusion of charts and visualizations would make it easier to grasp the key findings and trends, especially for readers unfamiliar with the specific languages and datasets.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed comparison to Søgaard (2012), specifically outlining the key differences and the rationale behind the design choices made in UDP?
2. What is the justification for choosing a personalization factor of 5 for the main predicate in the PageRank algorithm? Were other values explored, and if so, what were the results?
3. Could you provide the POS tagging accuracy scores for the predicted POS setup? This would allow us to assess the correlation between tagging accuracy and parsing performance.
