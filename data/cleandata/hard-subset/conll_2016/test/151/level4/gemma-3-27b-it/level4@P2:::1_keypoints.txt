- Presents a deterministic unsupervised dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank over permitted dependencies, and iterative decoding to form trees.  
- Main contribution: a competitive, training-free parser leveraging Universal Dependencies (UD) constraints, achieving mean UAS 55.3% across languages.  
- Shows robustness to domain shift, with lower UAS variance across domains compared to a supervised multi-source delexicalized baseline.  
- Demonstrates practical method to estimate adposition head-direction on test data, removing need for language-specific parameters.  
- Effectively incorporates UD constraints and explicit head rules to produce linguistically principled and generalizable parses.  
- Novelty appears incremental relative to Søgaard (2012); paper needs clearer articulation of differences and specific innovations.  
- Head rules (Table 1) are central but under-analyzed; missing statistics on how often they produce disconnected graphs and component sizes.  
- Test-data estimation of adposition direction seems ad-hoc; authors should justify method and examine potential bias or alternatives.  
- Question: Provide a detailed comparison to Søgaard (2012) highlighting innovations and improvements.  
- Question: Report frequency and typical sizes of disconnected graph components caused by head rules.  
- Question: Explain tie-breaking when multiple words share the same PageRank score and any mitigation of its impact.  
- Question: Report oracle results per constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to quantify their individual contributions.  
- Minor edits: wording fixes in Sections 4.1 and 6.1, and standardize column order across Tables 5 and 7.  
- Suggestion: include discussion of computational complexity of the algorithm.
