This paper introduces a parsing approach for Universal Dependency Treebanks that leverages Part-of-Speech (POS) tags and a modified PageRank algorithm. The core idea – weighting more meaningful words to mitigate the influence of stop words – is interesting, though conceptually similar to previous work, particularly Søgaard (2012). The personalization factor, which prioritizes the main predicate, is a valuable addition, but its potential feels somewhat limited in its current implementation. Specifically, the focus on only the main predicate and the assigned weight of 5 appear arbitrary and could benefit from further investigation or justification.

The evaluation section presents results comprehensively, however, the inclusion of charts alongside the tables would significantly enhance clarity and facilitate a quicker understanding of the key findings. Visual representations would be particularly helpful for highlighting trends and relative performance differences. 

Furthermore, reporting POS tagging performance in a prediction setup would provide crucial insight. Assessing whether a decline in parsing accuracy correlates with a decrease in POS tagging accuracy – as one might expect – would strengthen the analysis and offer a more complete picture of the system’s strengths and weaknesses. This would help determine if parsing errors are directly attributable to tagging inaccuracies.

Overall, this is an interesting paper, but the advancement over Søgaard (2012) feels incremental. While the personalization strategy shows promise, its limited scope and the lack of empirical justification for its specific parameters restrict its impact. The addition of visualizations and a more detailed analysis of POS tagging performance would strengthen the evaluation and contribute to a more compelling presentation of the work.
