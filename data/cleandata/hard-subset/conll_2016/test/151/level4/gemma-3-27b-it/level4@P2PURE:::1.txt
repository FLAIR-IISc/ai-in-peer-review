This paper introduces a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and evaluates its performance across multiple languages. The algorithm centers on a rule-based system that defines allowable dependencies based on part-of-speech tags. It initiates parsing by ranking words using a biased PageRank algorithm applied to a graph where edges represent these permissible dependencies.  The algorithm then iteratively links each word to its nearest compatible head, ensuring a tree structure is maintained and adhering to predefined head rules and a directionality constraint. The paper is generally well-written and presents an interesting approach, though its advancements appear incremental compared to Sogaard (2012), “Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training”. 

I have several questions and suggestions for improvement:

Regarding the Head Rules (Table 1), providing an analysis of these rules in the context of the corpora used would be beneficial. While Section 3.1 notes that the rules do not consistently yield a connected graph, the frequency of this occurrence and the typical size of the resulting components are not reported. I also found the choice to determine head direction using the test data, rather than dedicated training or development data, unexpected. Although the rapid convergence of the decision process (10-15 sentences) mitigates this concern, it remains a noteworthy design choice. 

Could you clarify how ties are resolved for words with identical PageRank scores, and whether such ties demonstrably affect overall performance? Furthermore, the imposed constraints – head rules, directionality, and distance – inherently limit the potential performance of the system. Including oracle results for each constraint would help quantify the performance reduction caused by each, and thereby guide future development efforts.

Finally, several minor points:

- In Section 4.1, consider revising "we obtain [the] rank" for improved clarity.
- Tables 5 and 7 present columns in different orders; I found the arrangement in Table 7 more intuitive.
- In Section 6.1, replace "isolate the [contribution] of both" with a more precise phrasing.
