- Presents UDP: an unsupervised dependency parser that uses PageRank personalization and linguistically motivated head rules within the Universal Dependencies framework, requiring no training data.  
- Demonstrates feasibility of competitive unsupervised parsing under UD constraints, achieving performance comparable to supervised transfer and robust to domain shift.  
- Effectively combines PageRank personalization (to prioritize predicates/content words) with UD head rules.  
- Evaluated thoroughly across 32 languages and multiple POS-tag reliability scenarios, providing a broad benchmark.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical performance: competitive UAS, often surpassing multi-source delexicalized transfer, and stable across genres/domains.  
- Novel integration of linguistic constraints with graph-based ranking, with clear motivation and benefits.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: multiple POS scenarios, domain adaptation tests, and use of in-house and public datasets.  
- Robustness to domain shift: consistently more resilient than the supervised baseline (MSD).  
- Clear and detailed methodology with helpful examples and a transparent two-step decoding algorithm.

Weaknesses:
- Limited handling of complex syntactic structures (e.g., coordination, non-projectivity) due to simple head rules.  
- Sensitivity to POS tagging errors: significant performance drop with predicted POS tags.  
- Lacks edge labeling capability; only reports unlabeled attachment scores.  
- Head rules were developed/tuned on English, raising potential bias for typologically different languages.

Questions for authors:
- Can you provide a detailed error analysis identifying the specific structures UDP struggles with?  
- How sensitive is performance to the PageRank teleport probability and why was 0.05 chosen?  
- What are the computational costs (time and memory) compared to the delexicalized transfer parser?

Additional comments:
- Extensive referencing and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Availability of the parser and annotated datasets is a valuable community resource.  
- Overall well-written and accessible presentation.
