- Proposes an unsupervised Universal Dependencies algorithm using PageRank for tagging plus a small set of hard-coded rules.
- Paper is well-written, detailed, and clearly explains the rationale for incorporating prior information.
- Contribution to unsupervised parsing is substantial and conceptually elegant.
- Suggestions for future work are promising and offer useful research directions.
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, covering multiple languages and datasets.
- Results are competitive with existing unsupervised methods given the approach’s simplicity.
- Performance still lags significantly behind supervised systems, as expected.
- Recommendation to include direct comparisons to strong baselines, including recent neural unsupervised parsers.
- Main concern: reliance on hard-coded rules; their number and impact need further investigation.
- Requests a thorough ablation study to quantify rule contributions and assess sensitivity/adaptability to new languages.
- Error analysis is insightful, identifying issues with long-range dependencies and ambiguous word senses.
- Presentation is clear with well-designed tables/figures and a useful discussion of computational complexity.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, promising approach; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing rule-related concerns and stronger baseline comparisons.
