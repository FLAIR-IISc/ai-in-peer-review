- Introduces a deterministic, rule-based dependency parsing algorithm using POS-based permissible dependencies and modified PageRank for word ranking.  
- Algorithm iteratively links words to nearest permissible heads to form a tree while enforcing head rules and directionality constraints.  
- Paper is well-written and presents an interesting approach but seems an incremental improvement over Søgaard (2012).  
- Request more detailed analysis of head rules’ relation to the corpora and their limitations.  
- Quantify how often head rules produce disconnected graphs and the typical sizes of resulting components.  
- Question the unusual choice of determining head direction on the test set; ask for rationale or alternative (train/dev) justification.  
- Ask for clarification on tie-breaking for identical PageRank scores and whether ties affect performance.  
- Recommend reporting oracle results per imposed constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show performance upper bounds and which constraints are most restrictive.  
- Suggest this constraint analysis to guide future development by pinpointing impactful modifications.  
- Minor wording corrections: revise “we obtain [the] rank” in Section 4.1 and rephrase “isolate the [contribution] of both” in Section 6.1.  
- Consistency suggestion: make column order in Table 5 match the more intuitive arrangement in Table 7.
