This paper introduces a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and evaluates its performance across multiple languages. 

The algorithm’s foundation lies in a rule-based system that specifies allowable dependencies according to part-of-speech tags. It initiates by ranking words using a slightly weighted PageRank calculation on a graph constructed from these permitted dependencies. Subsequently, the algorithm iterates through the ranked words, linking each to its nearest compatible head, ensuring a tree structure while adhering to defined head rules and a directionality constraint.

The paper is well-written and presents an intriguing approach. However, the advancements appear incremental when compared to existing work, such as Sogaard (2012), “Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training.”

I have several questions and recommendations for improvement:

Regarding the Head Rules (Table 1), it would strengthen the analysis to include corpus-based evaluation of these rules. Specifically, while Section 3.1 notes instances where the rules generate disconnected graphs, quantifying the frequency of such occurrences and the typical size of resulting components would be valuable. The selection of head direction based on test data, rather than training or development data, was unexpected. While the rapid convergence (10-15 sentences) mitigates this concern, the rationale for this choice warrants further explanation. 

Clarification is also needed on the tie-breaking mechanism for words with equivalent PageRank scores, and an assessment of its potential impact on performance. Are ties infrequent enough to be negligible, or do they measurably affect results? Furthermore, the imposed constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) inherently limit the system’s potential performance. Including oracle results for each constraint, demonstrating the resulting performance reduction, would be insightful. This would effectively highlight areas for future development and guide optimization efforts.

Finally, a few minor points:

- In Section 4.1, consider changing “we obtain [the] rank” for clarity.
- Tables 5 and 7 present columns in a different order; I found the arrangement in Table 7 more intuitive.
- In Section 6.1, I suggest revising to “isolate the [contribution] of both.”
