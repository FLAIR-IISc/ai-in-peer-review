This paper presents a method for parsing Universal Dependency treebanks that relies solely on Part-of-Speech (POS) tags. The approach uses a modified PageRank algorithm to assign greater weight to content words over stop words.

While the core idea is interesting, it is very similar to the work presented by Søgaard (2012). The introduction of a personalization factor to give additional weight to the main predicate is a novel contribution. However, this concept feels underexplored. As I understand it, the personalization is applied only to the main predicate, and the choice of a weight value of 5 seems arbitrary and lacks justification.

Regarding the evaluation and detailed analysis, the presentation would have benefited significantly from charts or other visualizations. It is difficult to interpret the key trends and takeaways from the tables alone.

Finally, the analysis would be more complete if the authors reported the POS tagging accuracy when using predicted tags. This would allow readers to assess the extent to which the drop in parsing performance correlates with errors in POS tagging, which is an expected outcome.

In summary, while the paper presents an interesting line of work, its incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is limited.
