This paper presents a method for parsing Universal Dependency treebanks that relies solely on Part-of-Speech (POS) tags. The approach employs a modified PageRank algorithm to assign greater weight to lexically significant words over function words.

While the core idea is interesting, it is very similar to the work of Søgaard (2012). The personalization factor, which gives more weight to the main predicate, is a welcome addition, but its implementation feels underdeveloped. As described, the personalization is applied exclusively to the main predicate, and the choice of a weight of 5 lacks clear justification and appears arbitrary.

Regarding the evaluation, the detailed analyses presented in tabular format are often difficult to interpret. The inclusion of charts or other visualizations would have significantly improved the clarity and accessibility of the results.

Furthermore, the analysis would be strengthened by including POS tagging accuracy scores when the system is run in prediction mode. This would enable an investigation into the extent to which parsing performance degradation correlates with tagging errors, which is an expected outcome for such a pipeline.

In summary, while the paper presents an interesting direction, its incremental contribution over the work of Søgaard (2012) is limited.
