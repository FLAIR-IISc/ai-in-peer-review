- Proposes a novel training-free unsupervised method for inducing UD POS tags and dependency labels by combining word-level PageRank with a small set of language-agnostic hard-coded rules.

- Novelty/significance: offers an elegant, effective alternative to data-heavy supervised methods, valuable for low-resource languages and theoretical syntax study.

- Clarity/presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written and explains each component and motivation clearly.

- Potential for future research: modular approach invites extensions (other centrality measures, refined rules, bootstrapping supervised models).

- Needs an ablation study to quantify the necessity and impact of each hard-coded rule.

- Needs a detailed qualitative error analysis to identify systematic failures (e.g., long-distance dependencies, complex coordination, non-projectivity).

- Should better contextualize results against historical and contemporary unsupervised/minimally supervised parsing baselines.

- Overall: high-quality, novel, and well-executed work whose strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; strong candidate for acceptance.
