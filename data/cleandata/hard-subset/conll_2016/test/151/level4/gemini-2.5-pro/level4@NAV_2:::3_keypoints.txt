- Method: UDP is a fully unsupervised UD parser using POS-based head-dependency rules and personalized PageRank, decoding in two steps (attach content words by PageRank, then attach function words as leaves).  
- Contribution: A simple yet competitive unsupervised parser achieving near-supervised delexicalized transfer performance across 32 languages.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates strong robustness to domain shift, with lower performance variance than a data-driven transfer baseline.  
- Contribution: Novel application of personalized PageRank to bias ranking toward the sentence’s main predicate, improving structure.  
- Strength: Impressive empirical performance—average UAS only 6.4% below a competitive supervised baseline and outperforms it on Hindi.  
- Strength: Thorough, convincing evaluation across 32 languages and three POS conditions, plus cross-domain analysis.  
- Strength: Method is simple, language-independent, requires no annotated data, and estimates adposition directionality on the fly.  
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—builds heavily on prior PageRank parsing work (e.g., Søgaard 2012b) with mostly adaptations to UD.  
- Weakness: Personalization is under-explored and arbitrary (single predicate up-weighted by 5 tuned on English); lacks analysis or principled setting.  
- Weakness: Results presentation is dense and hard to parse at a glance; visualizations (charts/graphs) would improve readability.  
- Question to authors: Please report per-language POS tagging accuracies from the TnT tagger to assess whether parser degradation correlates with tagging difficulty.
