- Paper proposes a deterministic, POS-based head-rule dependency parser using a biased PageRank ranking and constrained nearest-head attachments.
- Well-written, clearly motivated, and strong evaluation across many languages; valuable exploration of rule-based unsupervised parsing.
- Needs clearer differentiation from Sogaard (2012); discuss novel contributions relative to that prior work.
- Provide deeper analysis of the hand-crafted head rules, including how often the induced graph is disconnected and typical sizes/number of components per language.
- Run oracle analyses to quantify upper-bound accuracy under (a) head rules alone and (b) head rules plus directionality, to identify main bottlenecks.
- Justify or replace using test-set samples to set head directionality (prefer dev set or held-out training data) and explain rationale for the current choice.
- Specify tie-breaking strategy for identical PageRank scores, report tie frequency, and assess impact on parsing accuracy.
- Minor fixes: correct wording in Section 4.1 ("we obtain the rank") and Section 6.1 ("isolate the contribution of both").
- Make Table 5 and Table 7 column ordering consistent (use the clearer arrangement from Table 7).
