- Presents UDP: a deterministic, unsupervised UD-tailored parser using POS-based head-dependent rules, graph construction, personalized PageRank ranking, and two-step decoding.
- Contribution: a simple, effective rule-based unsupervised parser adapted to Universal Dependencies.
- Contribution: extensive empirical evaluation across 32 languages showing competitiveness with multi-source delexicalized transfer.
- Contribution: analysis showing the rule-based system is more robust to domain shift than a data-driven baseline.
- Strength: algorithm is simple, intuitive, requires no training data, and is clearly presented with a reproducible example.
- Strength: thorough evaluation including gold and predicted POS comparisons across many languages and domains.
- Strength: convincing robustness experiments on domain splits demonstrating stability under domain shift.
- Weakness: limited novelty—largely an incremental/engineering adaptation of prior work (e.g., Søgaard 2012b) rather than a fundamental advance.
- Weakness: methodological concern that adposition head direction is estimated from test data, conflating test and development.
- Weakness: lacks oracle or component-wise analysis to quantify performance ceilings from head rules, directionality, and distance metrics.
- Question: please analyze the head rules’ properties and coverage (e.g., frequency of non-connected graphs and component sizes).
- Question: how are PageRank tie-breaks handled, how often do ties occur, and what is their impact on parsing performance?
