- Paper: Proposes UDP, a deterministic, rule-based unsupervised UD parser that builds POS-based candidate graphs, uses personalized PageRank to prioritize predicates, then a two-step decoding attaching content then function words to enforce UD leaf constraint.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple PageRank + two-step decoding unsupervised parser that is competitive with strong delexicalized transfer baselines without any training data.

- Contribution 2: Provides a broad empirical evaluation on 32 languages showing better robustness to domain shift and POS errors than data-driven baselines.

- Strength 1: Elegant, linguistically-motivated simplicity that yields strong multilingual performance and serves as a solid baseline for unsupervised/cross-lingual parsing.

- Strength 2: Demonstrated robustness to domain shift via cross-genre experiments, with consistently lower performance variance than the MSD parser.

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation including gold vs. predicted POS, naive tag ablations, an ablation isolating PageRank's contribution, and POS-wise performance breakdowns.

- Weakness 1: Incremental novelty—core ideas closely follow prior work (e.g., Søgaard 2012b), with main innovations being PageRank personalization and the two-step decoding.

- Weakness 2: No oracle or ceiling analysis quantifying the performance limit imposed by fixed head rules, directionality heuristics, and closest-attachment decoding.

- Weakness 3: Unconventional use of test data to estimate adposition directionality, which risks conflating parameter estimation with evaluation.

- Question 1: Request statistics on how often head rules produce disconnected graphs for PageRank and typical component sizes across languages.

- Question 2: Ask why adposition directionality is estimated from test data instead of a universal/dev setting and whether an English-derived setting would generalize.

- Question 3: Ask how PageRank ties between content words are broken, how often ties occur, and whether tie-breaking affects final accuracy.

- Question 4: Request an oracle experiment: upper-bound UAS if an oracle picked the correct head among candidates allowed by the head rules to separate rule-set limits from decoding errors.
