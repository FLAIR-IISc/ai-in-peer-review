This paper presents a novel deterministic algorithm for dependency parsing and evaluates its performance across a diverse set of languages. At the heart of this method is a collection of part-of-speech (POS) based rules that define allowable dependencies. The process begins by ranking words via a lightly biased PageRank algorithm applied to a graph where edges are determined by these permitted dependencies. Iterating through this ranking, the algorithm attaches each word to the nearest available candidate that maintains the tree structure and adheres to both the head rules and a directionality constraint.

Overall, the work is interesting and well-presented. However, its novelty appears incremental when compared to Sogaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training".

I have several questions and suggestions for improvement:

*   **Head Rules (Table 1):** A deeper, corpus-based analysis of these rules would strengthen the paper. For instance, Section 3.1 notes that the rules can result in disconnected graphs; it would be valuable to quantify the frequency of this issue and the typical size of the resulting components.
*   The choice to determine head direction using test data, rather than a dedicated training or development set, is unusual. While this is not a significant concern due to the rapid convergence of this decision (within 10-15 sentences), the methodology is nonetheless unexpected.
*   Could the authors clarify the tie-breaking mechanism for words that receive identical PageRank scores? Furthermore, an analysis of the frequency of such ties and their impact on overall performance would be beneficial.
*   The system's constraints—namely the head rules, directionality, and distance—inherently impose a performance ceiling. Including oracle experiments for each constraint would be highly informative. Such an analysis would reveal the performance cost of each component, thereby highlighting the most promising avenues for future improvements to this approach.

Minor Corrections:
*   In Section 4.1, consider changing "we obtain rank" to "we obtain the rank".
*   The column ordering in Tables 5 and 7 is inconsistent. For improved clarity, the layout of Table 7, which I found more intuitive, could be adopted for both.
*   In Section 6.1, the phrase "isolate the of both" appears to be missing a word; I believe it should be "isolate the contribution of both".
