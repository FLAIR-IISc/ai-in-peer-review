This paper presents a method for parsing Universal Dependency treebanks that relies solely on Part-of-Speech (POS) tags. The approach uses a modified PageRank algorithm to assign greater weight to meaningful words, such as main predicates, over stopwords.

While the core idea is interesting, it is very similar to the work presented by Søgaard (2012). The use of a personalization factor to increase the weight of the main predicate is a nice concept, but it could have been explored more thoroughly. As presented, the personalization appears to be used exclusively for the main predicate, and the choice of a weight of 5 seems arbitrary and lacks justification.

Regarding the evaluation, the detailed analyses would have been more accessible with the inclusion of charts or graphs, as it is difficult to discern key trends from the tables alone. Furthermore, it would have been valuable for the authors to report the POS tagging accuracy in prediction mode. This would allow for an analysis of how degradation in parsing performance correlates with degradation in tagging performance, a relationship that would be expected.

In summary, the paper is interesting, but its incremental contribution over the work of Søgaard (2012) is small.
