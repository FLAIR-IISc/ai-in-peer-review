- Introduces UDP: a deterministic, training-free unsupervised UD parser using POS-based head-selection rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and a two-step decoding (content backbone then function-word leaves).

- Major contribution: extensive empirical evaluation and robustness analysis across 32 languages, showing UDP is more stable under domain shift and POS noise than a delexicalized transfer parser.

- Presents a simple, UD-tailored unsupervised parsing method that enforces UD principles (e.g., function words are leaves) and combines PageRank with two-step decoding.

- Demonstrates strong performance for a training-free system, outperforming an adjacency baseline and narrowing the gap to a supervised delexicalized parser (6.4% UAS gap with gold POS), even beating MSD on Hindi.

- Strength: thorough, multilingual evaluation including experiments with predicted POS tags and cross-domain tests that strengthen claims of robustness.

- Strength: method is simple, interpretable, and reproducible, relying on a small set of rules and a standard algorithm (PageRank).

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty, largely building on Søgaard (2012b); proposed refinements feel incremental rather than fundamentally new.

- Weakness: estimates adposition head directionality from the test data (using bigram counts), violating standard evaluation protocol and tainting the “training-free” claim.

- Weakness: insufficient analysis of core components—the coverage and impact of the head rules, frequency of back-off decoding, and an oracle upper-bound study are missing.

- Question: how are ties in PageRank scores handled—are secondary sorting keys used, and do ties materially affect performance?

- Question: why estimate adposition directionality from the test set, and could this be done from a small dev set or training sentences to avoid peeking?

- Question: were the head rules developed on the English treebank, and what evidence is there for their generalization across the 32 languages (coverage/error rates)?

- Minor edits: fix typos (“the rank of each word”, “contribution”) and consider reordering Table 5’s columns to match Table 7 for readability.
