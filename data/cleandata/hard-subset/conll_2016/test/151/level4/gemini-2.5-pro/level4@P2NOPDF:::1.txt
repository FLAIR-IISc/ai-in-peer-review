This paper introduces a novel deterministic algorithm for dependency parsing and evaluates its performance across a diverse set of languages. At its core, the algorithm relies on a predefined set of head rules that specify permissible dependencies between Part-of-Speech (POS) tags. The parsing process begins by constructing a dependency graph where edges are defined by these rules. A biased PageRank algorithm is then used to rank the words in the sentence. Subsequently, the algorithm iterates through the ranked words, attaching each to the nearest available head that satisfies the head rules, a directionality constraint, and maintains a valid tree structure.

The paper is well-written and presents an interesting approach to unsupervised dependency parsing. However, the primary contribution appears to be an incremental improvement over the work of Sogaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training." The authors should more clearly delineate the novel aspects of their algorithm in comparison to this prior work, as the current description suggests a very close relationship.

I have several suggestions and questions that could help strengthen the paper:

**Analysis of Constraints and Oracle Performance**
The paper would be significantly strengthened by a more in-depth analysis of the proposed head rules (Table 1) and their impact on the parsing process. For instance, Section 3.1 mentions that the rules do not always yield a connected graph. It would be valuable to quantify the frequency of this occurrence across the different corpora and the typical size of the resulting graph components.

More broadly, the system's performance is upper-bounded by its various constraints (head rules, directionality, distance). To better understand these limitations and guide future research, I recommend including an oracle analysis. This would quantify the performance ceiling imposed by each individual constraint, revealing which components offer the most promising avenues for improvement. Such an analysis would be particularly helpful for contextualizing the current results and informing future modifications to the system.

**Methodological Clarifications**
I have a few questions regarding the experimental setup and algorithm details:
1.  The choice to determine head directionality using the test set is surprising. While the authors note that this decision converges quickly (within 10-15 sentences), using development data would be a more standard and rigorous practice. Could the authors comment on the rationale for this choice?
2.  The paper does not specify how tie-breaking is handled for words that receive identical PageRank scores. It is unclear whether ties are rare enough to be insignificant or if the resolution method has a tangible impact on parsing accuracy. A brief clarification on this point would be helpful.

**Minor Suggestions**
Finally, I have a few minor suggestions for correction:
*   Section 4.1: "we obtain rank" should be "we obtain **the** rank".
*   Section 6.1: "isolate the of both" should be "isolate the **contribution** of both".
*   The column ordering in Table 7 is more intuitive than in Table 5; standardizing the presentation across tables would improve readability.
