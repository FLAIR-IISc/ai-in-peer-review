- Introduces UDP: a fully rule-based, training-free UD parser using POS-based dependency rules, personalized PageRank to highlight predicates, and a two-step decoding attaching function words as leaves.
- Contribution: a simple, unsupervised parser that leverages UD constraints to produce competitive parses without training data.
- Contribution: extensive evaluation on 32 languages and cross-domain tests showing greater stability across domain shift than a delexicalized multi-source transfer baseline.
- Strength: simplicity and interpretability (small rule set + PageRank) yielding robustness to domain shift (lower SD across domains).
- Strength: thorough evaluation across languages and POS-tagging conditions, with detailed cross-domain analysis.
- Strength: surprisingly strong performance for an unsupervised system—on average only ~6.4 UAS behind MSD in gold POS and better on one language (Hindi).
- Weakness: limited novelty—approach is an incremental adaptation of prior PageRank/POS-rule methods (e.g., Søgaard 2012b).
- Weakness: personalization is underdeveloped—uses a hard-coded weight (5) on a single heuristically chosen node with no exploration or justification.
- Weakness: results presentation is dense and could be clearer (too many large tables; graphs/charts would help).
- Question to authors: report per-language POS tagging accuracies of the TnT tagger in the UDPP setting to contextualize parsing degradation.
