- Proposes dependency parsing of Universal Dependencies using only POS tags with a modified PageRank that down-weights stop words.  
- Core idea is interesting but largely builds on Søgaard (2012), so novelty is limited.  
- Introduces a personalization factor in PageRank to emphasize the main predicate, but implementation is underdeveloped.  
- Personalization is applied only to the main predicate and the chosen weight of 5 appears arbitrary and unjustified.  
- Paper needs ablation studies or empirical tuning to justify the personalization weight.  
- Suggests exploring personalization for other key syntactic elements to strengthen the contribution.  
- Evaluation presentation is hard to read from raw tables; charts/graphs are needed to highlight trends across languages/phenomena.  
- Missing analysis of POS tagging accuracy in the prediction setting to examine error propagation and its effect on parsing.  
- Overall contribution is incremental over prior work; paper is generally well-written but lacks in-depth exploration of new components.  
- Minor issue: typo at Line 207 ("The the main idea" → "The main idea").
