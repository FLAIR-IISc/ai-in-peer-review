- Summary: Introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using POS-based head-selection rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and a two-step decoding to enforce UD constraints; evaluated on 32 languages and shown to be robust to domain shifts.

- Contribution 1: Provides extensive cross-lingual and cross-domain evaluation showing a simple rule-based parser can be more stable across genres than a delexicalized transfer baseline.

- Contribution 2: Offers a transparent, reproducible unsupervised UD baseline that, despite no training, attains competitive UAS (≈6.4 points below supervised delexicalized on gold POS).

- Strength 1: Experimental setup is comprehensive and insightful, including realistic predicted-POS experiments and compelling cross-domain stability analysis.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical performance for an unsupervised method, outperforming adjacency baselines and sometimes exceeding the delexicalized supervised system.

- Strength 3: Method is simple, interpretable, linguistically motivated, and uses a small set of clear POS rules plus PageRank.

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty; approach is a direct extension of prior PageRank-based work with incremental UD-specific adaptations.

- Weakness 2: Personalization of PageRank (predicate identification and weight=5) is heuristic and under-justified, lacking sensitivity analysis or principled alternatives.

- Weakness 3: Incomplete reporting: predicted-POS experiments lack per-language POS tagging accuracies for the TnT tagger, limiting interpretation of error propagation.

- Question 1: How was the personalization weight of 5 chosen, how sensitive are results to it, and were more sophisticated predicate-identification heuristics tested (esp. for verb-final languages)?

- Question 2: Please clarify conceptual distinctions from Søgaard (2012b) to highlight what drives the reported performance improvements.

- Question 3: Can you include per-language TnT POS tagging accuracies in the UDPP setting to contextualize error propagation?

- Additional comment 1: Consider visualizing cross-domain stability (e.g., bar charts) to better highlight differences between UDP and MSD.

- Additional comment 2: Minor typo: Section 3.1 reads "The the main idea..." and should be "The main idea...".
