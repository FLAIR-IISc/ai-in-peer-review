- Paper presents a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, PageRank-based word ranking, and greedy linking to nearest valid heads.  
- Contribution seems incremental, improving on Søgaard (2012) rather than presenting a major new paradigm.  
- Head rules need quantitative analysis: report how often rules produce disconnected graphs and the typical sizes of resultant components.  
- Head direction decision made on the test set (not a development set), which is an unorthodox choice despite quick convergence.  
- Tie-breaking for identical PageRank scores is unspecified; clarify mechanism and assess its impact on performance.  
- Provide oracle analyses for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to quantify their individual upper-bound effects.  
- Minor fixes: phrasing in Section 4.1 ("we obtain [the] rank") and Section 6.1 ("isolate the [contribution] of both") should be corrected.  
- Table formatting: column ordering is inconsistent between Tables 5 and 7; adopt the clearer arrangement (as in Table 7).
