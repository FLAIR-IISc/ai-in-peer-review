- Proposes a dependency parser that uses only POS tags and a personalized PageRank to emphasize content words (e.g., main predicate).  
- Core idea is interesting but contribution is incremental and closely resembles Søgaard (2012).  
- Paper lacks an explicit comparison highlighting distinctions from Søgaard (2012).  
- Personalization (boosting the main predicate) is promising but underdeveloped and applied only to the main predicate.  
- Choice of personalization weight (value 5) is arbitrary and lacks empirical justification.  
- Needs systematic exploration: try personalization on other content words and/or perform ablation/weight-search to find optimal schemes.  
- Results are presented only in tables; visualizations (charts) are needed to highlight trends and takeaways.  
- Missing evaluation of POS-tagging accuracy with predicted tags and error analysis linking tagging errors to parsing degradation.  
- Overall novelty is limited, novel components are not fully explored, and presentation could be improved.  
- Minor typo: Line 207 "The the main idea" → "The main idea".
