This paper presents a new deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and analyzes its performance across a range of languages. The core of the algorithm is a set of rules based on POS tags that define permitted dependencies. The process begins by ranking words using a biased PageRank algorithm over a graph where edges are defined by these permitted dependencies. Then, iterating through the words in ranked order, the algorithm links each word to the closest head that maintains a tree structure and is permitted by the head rules and a directionality constraint.

Overall, the paper is interesting and clearly presented. However, the proposed method appears to differ only slightly from Sogaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training".

I have the following questions and suggestions:

*   **Analysis of Head Rules:** Regarding the head rules in Table 1, an analysis of their properties in relation to the corpus would be beneficial. For example, Section 3.1 mentions that the rules do not always lead to a connected graph, but it does not specify how frequently this occurs or the typical size of the resulting components.

*   **Use of Test Data for Parameter Tuning:** The choice to determine head direction using the test data, rather than a training or development set, is surprising. Although this decision reportedly converges quickly (within 10-15 sentences), making it less of a major issue, this use of the test set is an unconventional choice.

*   **PageRank Tie-Breaking:** The paper should clarify the tie-breaking mechanism for words that receive the same PageRank score. It would also be useful to know whether this choice significantly impacts performance or if ties are sufficiently rare as to be negligible.

*   **Oracle Analysis of Constraints:** The system's various constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) necessarily impose an upper bound on its performance. It would be highly informative to include oracle results for each constraint to quantify the extent to which each one limits the maximum achievable score. Such an analysis would be particularly valuable for guiding future work by highlighting which components of the system offer the most potential for improvement.

**Minor Points:**

*   Section 4.1: "we obtain rank" should be "we obtain **the** rank".
*   The column order in Tables 5 and 7 is inconsistent. The arrangement in Table 7 is clearer and would be better for consistency.
*   Section 6.1: "isolate the of both" should be "isolate the **contribution** of both".
