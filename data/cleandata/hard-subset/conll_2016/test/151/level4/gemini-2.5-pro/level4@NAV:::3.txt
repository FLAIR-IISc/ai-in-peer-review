This paper proposes a method for parsing Universal Dependency treebanks that relies exclusively on Part-of-Speech (POS) information. The approach utilizes a modified PageRank algorithm to assign greater weight to content words over stop words. While the concept is compelling, it bears a strong resemblance to the approach previously introduced by Søgaard (2012). The use of a personalization factor to up-weight the main predicate is a valuable addition, but this aspect of the model could be further developed. The personalization appears to be applied exclusively to the main predicate, and the specific weight value of 5 is presented without justification, making it seem arbitrary.

Regarding the evaluation and detailed analyses, the presentation of results would be significantly enhanced by including charts or other graphical visualizations. The current reliance on tables makes it challenging for the reader to readily interpret the main findings.

Finally, the analysis would be more complete if it included the POS tagging performance scores when using predicted tags. This would allow for an investigation into the correlation between the degradation in tagging accuracy and the resulting parsing performance, which is an expected relationship.

In summary, while the paper presents an interesting line of work, its incremental contribution beyond the work of Søgaard (2012) appears to be modest.
