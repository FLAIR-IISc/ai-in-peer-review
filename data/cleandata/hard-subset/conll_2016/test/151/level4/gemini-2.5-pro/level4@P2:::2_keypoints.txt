- Introduces UDP, a training-free unsupervised dependency parser for Universal Dependencies using linguistically-motivated head-selection rules.
- Uses personalized PageRank to rank content words and a heuristic to boost the main predicate.
- Employs a two-step decoding: build a content-word skeleton then attach function words as leaves respecting UD constraints.
- Evaluated on 32 languages from UD v1.2 and compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser.
- Claims competitive performance, with particular robustness to domain shift and to predicted (vs. gold) POS tags.
- Contribution: a coherent, end-to-end rule-based UD parser combining PageRank and content/function word distinction.
- Contribution: extensive empirical analysis showing stability across 32 languages and cross-domain settings.
- Strength: elegant, linguistically principled approach that avoids complex statistical models.
- Strength: comprehensive and realistic evaluation including predicted POS experiments and cross-domain tests.
- Strength: clear exposition, pseudocode, example, and commitment to release code and data for reproducibility.
- Weakness: several heuristics (e.g., 5x weight for first verb/content word) feel ad-hoc and may favor certain word orders.
- Weakness: limited and small set of head rules that miss phenomena like relative clauses and coordination, risking arbitrary attachments.
- Weakness: “training-free” claim is overstated because key rules and directions were tuned on English development data.
- Question: request for details on how head rules in Table 1 were selected and ablation results (e.g., impact of excluding NOUN -> VERB).
- Question: ask whether more typologically neutral predicate/root identification heuristics were explored beyond the "first verb" rule.
- Question: suggest inferring attachment direction for other function words (like was done for ADP) to make the parser more language-agnostic.
