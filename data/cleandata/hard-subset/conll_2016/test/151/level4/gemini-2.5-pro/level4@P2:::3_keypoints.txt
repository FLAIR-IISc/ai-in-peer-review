- Introduces UDP, a fully unsupervised UD parser using hand-crafted POS-based head-dependent rules and no training data.  
- Uses a two-step decoding to separate content and function words, enforcing function words as leaves per UD guidelines.  
- Employs personalized PageRank to identify the main predicate and rank content words for attachment.  
- Evaluated on 32 UD languages and compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline, with robustness analyses for POS noise and domain shift.  

- Contribution: Presents a simple, transparent, linguistically-motivated unsupervised parser serving as a strong baseline requiring no training.  
- Contribution: Provides extensive empirical evaluation across languages and domains, analyzing robustness to domain shift and noisy POS tags.  

- Strength: Demonstrates strong robustness to domain shift, with lower performance variance across domains than the delexicalized baseline.  
- Strength: Competitive performance for an unsupervised system—on average 6.4 UAS points below the multi-source delexicalized system and outperforming it on some languages (e.g., Hindi).  
- Strength: Simplicity and interpretability via a small explicit rule set and transparent decoding make the system easy to analyze and extend.  

- Weakness: Limited novelty over prior work (notably Søgaard 2012b); key idea of PageRank-based scoring is not fundamentally new.  
- Weakness: Personalization of PageRank is applied narrowly and arbitrarily (fixed factor of 5), with little justification or principled selection.  
- Weakness: Presentation of results is dense and hard to interpret; lacks visualizations to highlight key trends and correlations.  

- Question: How was the personalization factor of 5 chosen and how sensitive is performance to different values (e.g., 2, 10, or none)?  
- Question: Can you provide per-language POS tagging accuracies for the UDPP setting to analyze correlations between POS accuracy and parsing degradation?
