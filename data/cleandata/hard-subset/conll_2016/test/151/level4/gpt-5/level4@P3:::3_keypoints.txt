- Proposes UDP, a training-free UD parser using UD-aligned head rules, two-step decoding enforcing function words as leaves, and personalized PageRank to prioritize main predicates.
- Estimates adposition attachment direction from test data with simple bigram statistics.
- Evaluated on 32 languages under three POS-tagging regimes and compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser (MSD).
- Contribution: a simple, language-agnostic, training-free parser operationalizing UD constraints (single root, function-word leaves).
- Contribution: uses personalized PageRank to improve root/head selection, with ablation (UDPNoPR) showing substantial UAS gains.
- Contribution: practical on-the-fly ADP direction estimation removing language-specific tuning and converging with few sentences.
- Strength: strong cross-lingual and cross-domain robustness, competitive with MSD, less sensitive to POS errors and domain shifts.
- Strength: linguistically motivated two-step decoding that enforces UD formalism and is easy to reason about.
- Strength: parameter-free ADP adaptation works empirically and supports portability.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation (gold/predicted POS, content/function extremes, many languages, domain splits) with useful ablations.
- Strength: clear, deterministic algorithm with near-quadratic runtime and straightforward reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to Søgaard (2012); key additions are incremental and need deeper comparison to prior work.
- Weakness: heuristic hyperparameters (personalization weight, teleport prob) tuned on English without cross-language sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: personalization heuristic (first verb) may be fragile for non-verbal or verb-final languages/genres.
- Weakness: head-rule selection from English dev risks English biases; known limitations (no NOUN→VERB, coordination, punctuation) not quantified or mitigated.
- Weakness: evaluation gaps—missing per-language POS accuracies, correlation with UAS drop, and no statistical significance testing; large tables need clearer visualization.
- Weakness: practicality concerns—predicted-POS relies on in-language supervised taggers, limiting claims about unsupervised applicability for truly low-resource languages.
- Question: report per-language POS-tagging accuracies for predicted-POS and quantify correlation with UAS degradation; clarify error-propagation metric per language.
- Question: how sensitive is performance to personalization weight and teleport probability; have alternatives (e.g., learned multi-root personalization) been tried?
- Question: can directionality constraints for other function words be estimated from data like ADP to improve outliers (e.g., DET in Basque)?
- Question: provide error breakdown attributable to rule-set limitations (relative clauses, coordination, punctuation) and potential headroom if addressed.
- Question: how stable is bigram-based ADP-direction estimation for languages with mixed or multiword adpositions; provide per-language or learning-curve analysis.
- Additional: add graphical summaries (per-language UDP vs MSD scatter plots, domain variance, ablation deltas) for readability.
- Additional: minor typo noted and request to ensure code, domain-split scripts, and datasets are linked/versioned for reproducibility.
