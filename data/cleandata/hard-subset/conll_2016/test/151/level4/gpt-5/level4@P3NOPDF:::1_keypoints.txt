- Summary: Deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank over permitted-dependency graph, and greedy rank-based attachment; novelty relative to Søgaard (2012) is unclear and needs stronger comparison/analysis.

- Strength: Clear problem setup and algorithmic description.

- Strength: Sensible use of graph-based ranking to guide greedy attachment.

- Strength: Broad cross-lingual evaluation is valuable.

- Head-rules analysis needed: report how often the induced dependency graph is disconnected, component sizes, coverage of gold arcs by the rules, and which POS–POS pairs are blocked and how that correlates with per-language performance.

- Head-direction tuning issue: choosing directionality on the test set is test-time tuning—move to a development set or use cross-validation, clarify whether the initial sentences are excluded from evaluation and whether the procedure is per-language.

- Tie-breaking: specify tie-breaking strategy for identical PageRank scores, quantify tie frequency, and assess impact on performance/variance across runs.

- Upper bounds and oracle analyses: run oracles (head rules only; head rules + directionality; all constraints except ranking) and per-language upper bounds, and consider ablation studies removing constraints to identify bottlenecks.

- Relation to prior work: clearly state methodological differences from Søgaard (2012) (PageRank bias, attachment heuristic, constraint set), and provide a controlled empirical comparison and rationale for expected improvements.

- Presentation/clartiy: define “closest” precisely (linear distance/projectivity), formally state how well-formedness is maintained, and give implementation details (graph construction, PageRank parameters, convergence criteria) for reproducibility.

- Minor fixes: correct phrasing errors, standardize table column ordering, and (if space permits) report runtime/memory and random seed policy.
