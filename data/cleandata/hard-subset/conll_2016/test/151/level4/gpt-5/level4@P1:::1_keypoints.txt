- Summary: UDP is a deterministic, training-free UD parser using POS-based head rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and two-step decoding (attach content words enforcing single root, then function words as leaves), estimating adposition direction from test data; evaluated on 32 languages with strong cross-domain robustness.

- Contribution 1: Presents a simple, training-free UD parser competitive with delexicalized transfer and robust across many languages and domains.

- Contribution 2: Adapts personalized PageRank for UD parsing and pairs it with two-step decoding that enforces function-word leaf constraints.

- Contribution 3: Introduces a lightweight, test-time heuristic to estimate adposition attachment direction without language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Broad evaluation on 32 languages with gold/predicted POS and cross-domain analysis supports generality and stability.

- Strength: Robust to POS errors and domain shift, with small drops from gold to predicted POS and lower error propagation and cross-domain variance than MSD.

- Strength: Ablation shows PageRank substantially improves content-word attachment (~9 UAS) and two-step decoding adds further gains (~4 UAS).

- Strength: Method is deterministic, reproducible, does not require parallel/cross-lingual data, and enforces UD constraints by design.

- Weakness: Limited novelty beyond prior work (Søgaard 2012), mainly differing by PageRank personalization and UD-specific decoding; paper should better articulate fundamental novelties.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of head-rule-induced graph connectivity—no quantification of how often graphs are disconnected or component size distributions across languages/domains.

- Weakness: Using test-set estimation for adposition direction blurs unsupervised claims; paper should report results when direction is fixed from dev/train and sensitivity to sample size.

- Weakness: Tie-breaking for PageRank ranks is unspecified—no description of how ties are resolved or their frequency/impact.

- Weakness: No oracle analyses isolating constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) to show upper bounds and identify main bottlenecks.

- Question: How often are PR graphs disconnected across languages/domains, what are component size distributions, and does disconnection correlate with performance drops?

- Question: Why choose adposition direction from the test set rather than dev/train, how do results change if set from a held-out set, and how sensitive is estimation to number of sentences?

- Question: How are PageRank ties handled in practice, how frequently do they occur, and do different tie-break strategies affect UAS?

- Question: Can the authors report oracle upper bounds by relaxing one constraint at a time (oracle directionality, oracle head-rule licensing, oracle content-word ordering)?

- Minor comments: Fix typos/phrasing in Section 4.1 and 6.1, unify table column orders (Table 5 vs Table 7), and ensure consistent naming (UDP vs UPD) and Søgaard spelling.
