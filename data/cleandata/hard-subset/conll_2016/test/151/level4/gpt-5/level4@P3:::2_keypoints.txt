- Proposal: UDP, an unsupervised UD parser requiring no training that uses UD-compliant head rules, personalized PageRank, a two-step decoder, and an adposition direction heuristic, evaluated on 32 languages vs a delexicalized baseline.

- Contribution 1: A simple, training-free UD parser competitive with multi-source delexicalized transfer and more robust to domain shift and POS noise.

- Contribution 2: A principled use of personalized PageRank plus two-step decoding to improve root selection and content-word attachments without supervision.

- Contribution 3: A language-agnostic heuristic to estimate adposition (ADP) directionality from input, removing language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Strong cross-lingual performance—UAS close to the delexicalized baseline (avg gap ~6.4% with gold POS) and better on some languages (e.g., Hindi); PageRank gives ~9 UAS points over rules-only.

- Strength: Robustness to domain shift—lower performance variance across domains compared to the delexicalized baseline.

- Strength: Resilience to POS degradation—small performance drop with predicted POS (~2.2%) and lower POS-to-dependency error propagation than the baseline.

- Strength: Linguistically grounded, interpretable design explicitly encoding UD constraints (function words as dependents, single-root) and separating content/function words.

- Strength: Transparent methodology with algorithmic detail, complexity analysis, walkthroughs, language/POS-wise results, and ablations isolating contributions.

- Weakness: Test-time ADP estimation blurs strict unsupervised claim by using test-side signals and needs clarification for practical deployment.

- Weakness: Limited syntactic coverage—fixed rules miss attachments (e.g., NOUN→VERB for relative clauses) and struggle with coordination and punctuation.

- Weakness: Dependence on UD POS quality and conventions; English-tuned function-word directions may be suboptimal for some languages.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to unlabeled attachment and compared mainly to a single baseline; relation-specific accuracy and more baselines would strengthen claims.

- Weakness: Personalization heuristic for PageRank may bias root selection and shows variable root accuracy across languages (e.g., Arabic, Tamil).

- Question: How is ADP direction estimated—per language, per document, or per sentence batch—and how is streaming/online parsing handled?

- Question: Sensitivity to PageRank hyperparameters (teleport prob, 5× personalization); any language-specific alternatives or cases where personalization harms?

- Question: Portability of the rule set across typologically diverse languages and whether other function-word directions (DET/AUX/SCONJ) could be estimated from test data.

- Question: Empirical breakdown of errors due to coordination, MWEs, relative clauses, and which proposed extensions yield the largest gains.

- Question: Runtime and scalability—average runtimes per sentence/language, PageRank scaling with sentence length and graph sparsity, and practical constraints for large-scale use.

- Overall recommendation: Clear, well-motivated, effective unsupervised UD parsing baseline; competitive, robust, and worthy of acceptance despite noted limitations.
