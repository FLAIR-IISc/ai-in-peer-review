- Paper introduces UDP, a deterministic, training-free UD parser using POS-based head rules and personalized PageRank (PR) to rank content words and find the main predicate.
- Decoding is two-phase: attach content words guided by PR, then attach function words enforcing leafness; adposition attachment direction is estimated from ADP–NOMINAL bigrams.
- Evaluated on 32 UD languages under gold/predicted/coarse POS settings and compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline (MSD), showing competitive UAS and lower domain-shift variance.

- Contribution: A simple, language-agnostic, training-free UD parser that operationalizes UD constraints via two-step decoding and POS head rules.
- Contribution: Uses personalized PageRank to prioritize main predicate identification and guide attachment order, improving over rule-only baselines.
- Contribution: Provides extensive multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing rule-based approaches can rival delexicalized transfer and be more robust to domain shifts.

- Strength: Strong multilingual baseline with minimal supervision; competitive UAS across 32 languages and less error propagation from POS than MSD under predicted POS.
- Strength: Cross-domain robustness with consistently lower standard deviation across domains and better performance in several hard-domain cases.
- Strength: Clear algorithmic design with ablations quantifying PR’s ~9 UAS point contribution and decoding’s ~4 point contribution over a rule baseline.
- Strength: Practical and reproducible: requires only POS tags, deterministic behavior for a POS sequence, and code/data stated to be available.

- Weakness: Limited novelty versus prior work (e.g., Søgaard 2012); PR ranking and rule-guided decoding are modest extensions without strong methodological innovation.
- Weakness: ADP head-direction estimated from test data risks data leakage; estimation should be restricted to development or strictly unsupervised methods and quantified.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of rule-induced graph properties—disconnection rates, component sizes, rule coverage and violation rates are not reported.
- Weakness: Missing oracle/upper-bound analyses to identify which constraints (rules, directionality, PR, distance) limit performance.
- Weakness: Evaluation focuses on unlabeled UAS; lacks labeled performance, non-projectivity rates, and breakdowns for hard UD structures (coordination, punctuation, MWE, proper names).

- Q: Report how often rule-induced graphs are disconnected and distribution of component sizes per language before teleportation.
- Q: Explain PR tie-breaking and distance tie rules, their frequencies, determinism implications, and impact on UAS.
- Q: Clarify exactly how ADP direction is estimated (per-language on full test set or online); provide results when estimation uses only dev/training data.
- Q: Provide oracle upper bounds per constraint and combinations to identify main bottlenecks.
- Q: Report rate of non-projective attachments from the one-pass decoder, variation by language, and correlation with errors.
- Q: Have AUX/DET/SCONJ directions been estimated per language like ADP; if so, report UAS changes and whether it fixes language-specific issues (e.g., Basque DET).

- Minor: Several wording/typos (e.g., “we obtain the rank”, “isolate the contribution of both”).
- Minor: Table ordering inconsistency; consider aligning column orders (e.g., Table 5 with Table 7).
- Minor: Clarify end-to-end complexity (decoding noted as O(n × |C|) but include PR cost and typical runtime per sentence).
- Minor: Fix reference formatting/placeholders in bibliography.
