- Paper presents UDP: a deterministic, unsupervised dependency parser using POS-based head rules, two-step decoding (content then function words), and personalized PageRank over a rule-induced graph.
- Adposition attachment direction is estimated on the fly from test data using simple bigram statistics.
- Evaluated on 32 languages with gold and predicted POS, compared to a multi-source delexicalized transfer baseline; competitive UAS and more robust to domain shift.
- Main contribution: a simple, language-agnostic, training-free UD parser combining POS rules with personalized PageRank to identify roots and head order.
- Main contribution: a two-step decoding enforcing UD’s function-word leaf constraint and a practical test-time heuristic for adposition direction.
- Main contribution: broad multilingual and cross-domain evaluation showing surprising robustness versus a delexicalized supervised baseline.
- Strength: conceptually simple, clearly described, deterministic given POS, and easy to reproduce and analyze.
- Strength: competitive performance without training, achieving UAS close to a strong delexicalized baseline and modest degradation under predicted POS.
- Strength: lower cross-domain variance indicating robustness to domain shifts relative to the supervised transfer system.
- Strength: personalized PageRank improves ranking and convergence compared to rule-only variants per ablation results.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty relative to Søgaard (2012); innovations are incremental (personalization, UD leaf constraint, test-time ADP direction).
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of head rules—no quantification of graph disconnection rates, component sizes, coverage, or cross-lingual variation.
- Weakness: estimating adposition direction from test data conflates inference and tuning; needs comparison to development-set estimation and extension to other function words.
- Weakness: missing oracle/upper-bound diagnostics isolating the impact of rules, directionality, distance, and PR ranking.
- Weakness: limited syntactic coverage (e.g., forbidding NOUN→VERB blocks relative clauses; coordination and punctuation issues); frequency and error impact not analyzed.
- Question: how often are rule-induced sentence graphs disconnected and what are typical connected component sizes across languages?
- Question: how are ties in PR scores broken, how frequent are ties, and how sensitive is UAS to tie-breaking?
- Question: sensitivity of results to personalization weight and teleport probability; were alternatives or language-specific settings tested?
- Question: why prefer test-time ADP direction estimation over development-set estimation, and have other function-word directions been considered?
- Question: can the authors report oracle results for perfect PR ranking, directionality, and rule licensing to isolate ceilings?
- Question: are there remaining sources of nondeterminism (numeric precision, tie-breaking), and will code and exact settings be released?
- Minor: several typos and wording issues; inconsistent table column orders; clarify determinism given POS and direction settings; specify number of excluded languages up front.
- Minor: add brief note on computational cost since PR dominates runtime—provide typical sentence-level runtimes.
- Overall: well-executed, clear, and strong unsupervised baseline; recommend acceptance if authors expand rule analyses, justify test-time direction estimation, and add oracle/ablation diagnostics.
