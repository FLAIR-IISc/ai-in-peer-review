- Paper proposes a training-free UD parser using only POS tags and head rules plus a personalized PageRank that upweights content words and an estimated main predicate; evaluated against delexicalized transfer across many languages/domains.

- Main contributions: a simple POS-based, training-free parser with personalized PageRank to identify a main predicate; broad multilingual and domain-shift empirical comparison to delexicalized transfer.

- Strength: conceptually simple, easy to implement, leverages UD POS constraints; PageRank prioritization of content words is sensible.

- Strength: personalization for the main predicate is a useful twist on prior PageRank parsing methods.

- Strength: extensive multilingual and domain analyses that assess robustness.

- Weakness: limited novelty over Søgaard (2012); the personalization addition feels incremental rather than a clear next step.

- Weakness: personalization is underexplored—applied only to the main predicate, chosen weight (5) seems arbitrary, and no sensitivity analysis is shown.

- Weakness: results presentation is table-heavy; key findings would be clearer with charts for per-language/domain variance and error propagation.

- Weakness: missing details on predicted POS setup—POS tagging accuracy per language and analysis of how tagging errors affect parsing are not reported.

- Questions for authors: why weight the main predicate by exactly 5, did you try other values or tuning, and how sensitive are results?

- Questions for authors: did you consider personalizing beyond the main predicate (other content words or edge-level priors) or using a more principled prior?

- Questions for authors: please report POS tagging accuracy per language for predicted-POS experiments and quantify the correlation between POS accuracy and UAS or provide error-propagation analysis.

- Request: add charts to visualize cross-language and cross-domain variance and the effect of personalization vs no-personalization.

- Overall: interesting and appealing idea but incremental over prior work; needs stronger justification/extension of personalization and clarification of POS-accuracy effects.

- Minor: typo at line 207: “The the main idea” → “The main idea.”
