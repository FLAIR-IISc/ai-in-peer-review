- Proposes a training-free UD parser using PageRank over sentence graphs plus a small set of deterministic rules to produce dependencies.
- Paper is clearly written, motivates design choices, and explains priors and constraints well.
- Method is simple, practical, and a reproducible baseline for low-resource or domain-shift settings.
- Conceptually novel use of PageRank as central scoring combined with minimal rule-based constraints.
- Potential impact: fast bootstrapping of annotations, initialization for weak supervision, or fallback parsers in low-resource pipelines.
- Reliance on hand-crafted rules risks encoding language- or annotation-specific biases that may hurt cross-lingual generalization.
- Evaluation is limited; needs broader multilingual coverage (head-initial/-final, rich morphology, nonprojective) and stronger baselines (standard unsupervised, simple heuristics, delexicalized supervised).
- Reporting lacks clear metrics/settings (UAS/LAS, punctuation handling), significance testing, and per-language breakdowns.
- Missing ablation/sensitivity analysis: contributions of PageRank vs. each rule, graph construction choices, damping/convergence, and edge definitions/weights.
- Unclear what “unsupervised” means in practice given potentially injected UD/linguistic priors; clarify avoided supervision vs. injected knowledge.
- Unclear enforcement of well-formed tree constraints (single head, acyclicity, connectivity) and handling of nonprojective dependencies and tie-breaking.
- Reproducibility concerns: urge release of code, rules, configurations or a full algorithmic description and rule list in appendices.
- Suggest expanding experiments to more languages/domains, per-language results, thorough ablations, strong baselines, and significance testing.
- Suggest providing detailed algorithmic specs: graph construction, PageRank parameters/stopping, head selection, labeling, and tree constraint enforcement.
- Recommend error analysis by relation, sentence length, punctuation, discussion of failure modes, and interaction between rules and PageRank.
- Advise reporting computational complexity and runtime scalability to long sentences and large corpora.
- Recommend clearer positioning relative to prior unsupervised parsing and graph-ranking work, delineating novelty and limitations.
- Overall: a well-motivated, simple, potentially impactful contribution that needs more comprehensive evaluation, stronger baselines, and detailed ablations to be a robust benchmark.
