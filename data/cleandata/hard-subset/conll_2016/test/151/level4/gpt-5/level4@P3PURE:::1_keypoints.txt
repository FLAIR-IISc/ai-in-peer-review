- Presents a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, constructing a permitted-dependency graph, computing a biased PageRank for word ranking, then iteratively linking words under tree- and directionality-constraints.  
- Contribution seems only slightly different from Søgaard (2012) “Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training.”  
- Paper is interesting and clearly presented.  
- Ask for analysis of head rules vs. the corpus: quantify how often the rules produce a disconnected graph and characterize component size distributions.  
- Question why head direction was selected on test data instead of training/dev data (surprising despite quick convergence).  
- Ask how PageRank ties are broken and whether tie-breaking materially affects performance.  
- Recommend reporting oracle upper bounds for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show how much each constraint reduces maximum attainable performance.  
- Minor comment: fix wording in Section 4.1 (“we obtain [the] rank”).  
- Minor comment: Table column order differs between Table 5 and Table 7; Table 7’s arrangement is clearer.  
- Minor comment: fix wording in Section 6.1 (“isolate the [contribution] of both”).
