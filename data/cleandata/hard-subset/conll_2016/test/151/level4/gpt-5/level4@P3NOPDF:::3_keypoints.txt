- Proposal: lightweight POS-only dependency parser using personalized PageRank with higher prior on “meaningful” tokens and the sentence’s main predicate.
- Strength: Well-motivated POS-only baseline needing minimal supervision and lexica.
- Strength: Elegant, easy-to-implement recasting of parsing as personalized PageRank.
- Strength: Predicate-centered prior is intuitive and linguistically motivated.
- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to Søgaard (2012); method feels like a modest extension.
- Weakness: Predicate personalization weight (set to 5) is ad hoc with no justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Unclear how the “main predicate” is identified using only POS tags, especially across languages with auxiliaries or non-finite predicates.
- Weakness: Evaluation presentation (large tables) is hard to digest and obscures key trends and errors.
- Weakness: No quantified connection between POS tagging quality and parsing performance; predicted POS accuracy isn’t reported or correlated with parsing metrics.
- Weakness: Unclear operationalization of “meaningful words” vs. “stop words” using UPOS tags and whether the scheme is universal or language-specific.
- Weakness: Insufficient reproducibility details for PageRank hyperparameters (damping, convergence, initialization) and graph construction.
- Suggestion: Explore richer personalization schemes (multiple verbal heads, systematic content/function weighting, IDF-like frequencies, or learned tag priors).
- Suggestion: Provide ablations (no personalization, predicate-only, content-only, combinations) to isolate effects.
- Suggestion: Include sensitivity analyses for predicate weight, damping factor, and edge-weighting, with validation curves.
- Suggestion: Clarify methodology: predicate detection rules, stop-word/tag list, graph topology, candidate head edges, and positional biases.
- Suggestion: Improve evaluation: report gold vs predicted POS, tagger accuracy, correlations with UAS/LAS, and add plots (UAS by length, POS-accuracy bins, relation groupings).
- Suggestion: Provide error analysis (e.g., auxiliaries, prepositions, coordination) and discuss cross-lingual trends and prior robustness.
- Suggestion: Compare to stronger/recent POS-only baselines (including Søgaard) and include an oracle-root upper bound.
- Reproducibility: Provide exact hyperparameters, convergence thresholds, random seeds, and release code or detailed pseudocode.
- Overall: Neat, resource-light baseline with modest methodological increment; needs stronger experimental justification and clearer visualization to increase impact.
