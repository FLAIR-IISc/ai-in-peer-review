- Paper summary: Proposes UDP, a training-free UD parser using only POS tags, UD head rules, personalized PageRank for content-word/root selection, and test-time adposition-direction estimation; evaluated on 32 languages vs delexicalized transfer and tested for domain robustness.

- Contribution: A two-phase, rule-based decoding that enforces content–function separation and leafness of function words without training.

- Contribution: A personalized PageRank scheme to rank content words and improve root selection, yielding competitive UAS vs delexicalized transfer.

- Contribution: Lightweight, test-time estimation of adposition attachment direction from ADP–NOMINAL bigrams, removing language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Simplicity and generality — no training data, only POS tags, minimal language-specific rules, suitable for low-resource settings.

- Strength: Competitive cross-lingual performance with gold POS and modest drop with predicted POS, indicating robustness to tagging noise.

- Strength: Better domain robustness and lower variance across domains compared to supervised delexicalized transfer.

- Strength: Clear ablations showing the benefit of personalization/PageRank (~9 UAS improvement) and the two-step decoding.

- Strength: Practical adposition-direction estimation that converges quickly and improves cross-lingual applicability.

- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to prior work (Søgaard 2012); personalization toward the main predicate is incremental.

- Weakness: Personalization design choices (weight=5, single-word personalization) appear ad hoc with no sensitivity analysis or alternatives explored.

- Weakness: Fixed head-rule set excludes some constructions (e.g., relative clauses), and coordination/punctuation handling is weak, limiting ceiling performance.

- Weakness: Potential development bias from English-tuned rules/parameters; other function-word directions could be estimated data-drivenly like ADP.

- Weakness: Presentation issues — heavy tables make trends hard to digest; predicted-POS per-language tagging accuracies are not reported.

- Question: How sensitive are results to personalization weight (5) and teleport probability (0.05), and were alternatives (multiple predicates or learned weights) tried?

- Question: In verb-final or copula-drop languages, how often does the “first verb or first content word” predicate heuristic fail, and can simple heuristics improve it?

- Question: Did you try estimating directionality for other function-word classes (DET, AUX, CONJ/SCONJ) as done for ADP?

- Question: Please report per-language POS-tagging accuracy in the predicted-POS setting and the correlation between POS accuracy and UAS.

- Question: Have you tried simple post-processing heuristics for punctuation and coordination, and what effects were observed?

- Additional comment: Add visual summaries (e.g., bar charts) for average performance, domain variance, and ablation results to improve readability.

- Additional comment: Clarify code/data availability (including in-house domain datasets) and provide scripts to reproduce bigram-based directionality estimation.
