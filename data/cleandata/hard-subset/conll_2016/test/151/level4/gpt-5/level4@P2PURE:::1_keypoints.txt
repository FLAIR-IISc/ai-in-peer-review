- Paper proposes a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a permitted-dependency graph, biased PageRank word ranking, and nearest-candidate attachment that preserves tree structure and directionality.
- Presentation is clear and the idea is interesting but appears only slightly different from Søgaard (2012).
- Request to analyze head rules against the corpus: report how often the permitted-dependency graph is disconnected and typical component sizes.
- Concern that head-direction choices were selected using test data rather than training/dev data; convergence is fast (10–15 sentences) but the choice is unexpected.
- Ask how ties in PageRank scores are broken and whether tie-breaking materially affects performance.
- Suggest computing oracle upper bounds for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to quantify how much each limits achievable performance.
- Minor corrections: fix phrasing “we obtain [the] rank” (Sec 4.1), make table column orders consistent (prefer Table 7’s order), and correct wording “isolate the [contribution] of both” (Sec 6.1).
