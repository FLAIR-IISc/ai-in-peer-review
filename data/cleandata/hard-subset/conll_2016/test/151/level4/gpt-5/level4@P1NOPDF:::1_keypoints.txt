- Introduces a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank over permitted dependency edges to rank words, and a closest-candidate attachment respecting tree validity and head-direction constraints.

- Paper is interesting and well-written but appears only slightly different from Søgaard (2012), requiring clearer differentiation.

- Head-rule analysis: provide empirical corpus-level analysis of when rules yield disconnected graphs and the typical sizes of resulting components across languages and tagsets.

- Head-direction selection: justify choosing head-direction based on test data, clarify potential bias or leakage, and report an alternative/dev-based or robust selection protocol.

- PageRank tie-breaking: explain how ties in PageRank scores are resolved and report sensitivity of performance to different tie-breaking strategies (left-to-right, right-to-left, random with seed).

- Constraint-induced upper bounds: include oracle analyses for individual and combined constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) to quantify how much each limits maximum attainable performance.

- Minor edits: fix phrasing (“we obtain [the] rank”, “isolate the [contribution] of both”) and make table column orders consistent (Tables 5 and 7).
