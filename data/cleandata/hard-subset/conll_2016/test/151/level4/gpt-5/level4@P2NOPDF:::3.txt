This paper proposes a POS-only approach to parsing Universal Dependencies treebanks, using a modified PageRank to upweight semantically informative words relative to stop words. The idea is appealing, but it is very close to Søgaard (2012).

The personalization that increases the weight of the main predicate is a sensible direction, yet it feels underdeveloped. As far as I can tell, personalization is applied only to the main predicate, and the fixed weight of 5 appears arbitrary. A justification or ablation (e.g., varying the weight, personalizing additional content words, or learning weights) would strengthen the claim and clarify the contribution beyond Søgaard (2012).

Regarding evaluation, some plots would help; several tables are dense and make it hard to quickly grasp trends and effect sizes. Visualizing performance by language, POS ambiguity, or sentence length, for example, could make the analysis clearer.

It would also be useful to report POS tagging accuracy in the prediction setting and analyze its relationship with parsing performance. This would let readers assess whether drops in parsing accuracy are primarily driven by POS errors, as one might expect, and would contextualize the usefulness of the POS-only constraint.

Overall, the paper is interesting but represents a relatively small incremental step over Søgaard (2012). A stronger empirical justification for the personalization scheme and clearer presentation of results would improve the paper’s impact.
