This paper presents a new deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and analyzes its behavior across multiple languages. The core of the method is a set of POS tag–based head rules that define permitted dependencies. The algorithm first constructs a graph whose edges reflect these permitted dependencies and computes a slightly biased PageRank to obtain a word ranking. It then iterates through this ranking, linking each word to the closest word that both preserves tree well-formedness and is allowed by the head rules and a directionality constraint. The paper is interesting and clearly presented, though the contribution appears to differ only slightly from Sogaard (2012), “Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training.”

Questions and suggestions:
- Head Rules (Table 1). Please include an analysis of these rules relative to the corpus. In Section 3.1 you note that the rules do not always yield a connected graph, but you do not quantify how often this occurs or characterize the typical sizes of the resulting components.
- Head direction selection. I was surprised that head direction was chosen using the test data rather than training or development data. Given how quickly the decision converges (10–15 sentences), this is not a major issue, but it is still a surprising choice.
- Tie-breaking in PageRank. How are ties handled for words with the same PageRank score? Does tie-breaking materially affect performance, or are ties rare enough to be negligible?
- Constraint-induced upper bounds. The combination of constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) imposes an upper bound on attainable performance. It would be very informative to report oracle results for each constraint to show how much each one reduces the maximum possible score. This would also help guide future work by indicating where modifications are most likely to pay off.

Minor comments:
- Section 4.1: “we obtain [the] rank”
- Table 5 vs. Table 7: the column orders differ; the arrangement in Table 7 is clearer.
- Section 6.1: “isolate the [contribution] of both”
