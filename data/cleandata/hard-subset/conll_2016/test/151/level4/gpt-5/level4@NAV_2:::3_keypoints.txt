Paper proposes UDP: a training-free UD parser using only POS tags and UD-compliant head rules, with personalized PageRank to prioritize heads and constraints forcing function words to be leaves.

Contribution: a simple, UD-aware, rule-based parser requiring no training data and using POS-driven constraints.

Contribution: modest extension of PageRank-based unsupervised parsing via personalization that biases the main predicate (first verb/content word).

Contribution: broad empirical study across languages and POS scenarios and domain robustness evaluation, though methodological gain over Søgaard (2012) is limited.

Strength: provides a clear, language-agnostic training-free baseline that avoids parallel data or model transfer.

Strength: uses linguistically grounded UD constraints (function words as dependents and POS-only head rules).

Strength: personalized PageRank is a reasonable, supervision-free way to bias root selection.

Strength: extensive empirical coverage helps position the method relative to delexicalized transfer.

Weakness: limited novelty relative to prior PageRank-based work; personalization feels incremental.

Weakness: personalization design is narrow and the 5× weight appears ad hoc with no sensitivity analysis or justification.

Weakness: presentation is table-heavy and would benefit from figures/plots to summarize cross-language and cross-domain trends.

Weakness: missing per-language POS tagging accuracies and no analysis of how POS errors correlate with parsing UAS drops.

Question: How was the 5× personalization weight selected; was a sensitivity analysis performed and how does UAS vary across values and languages?

Question: Why restrict personalization to the presumed main predicate; were broader schemes (all verbs or content-word neighborhoods) tried and how did they perform?

Question: Please report per-language POS tagging accuracies for the predicted-POS condition and quantify their correlation with UAS degradation.
