- Proposal of UDP: a training-free UD parser using POS-based head rules plus personalized PageRank to rank content words and find the main predicate.
- Two-step decoding: attach content words first by PageRank and rules, then attach function words with directionality heuristics enforcing UD’s function-word-as-leaf constraint.
- Adposition (ADP) attachment direction is estimated at test time via a simple bigram heuristic.
- Evaluated on 32 languages (UD v1.2) under gold POS, predicted POS, and coarse content/function tags, showing competitiveness and robustness to domain shifts.
- Contribution: a simple, training-free baseline that approaches multi-source delexicalized transfer performance across languages and domains.
- Contribution: practical use of personalized PageRank plus two-step decoding to improve root/head selection and enforce UD constraints without supervision.
- Contribution: lightweight, test-time adposition direction estimation removing language-specific parameters.
- Strength: clear, principled, easily reproducible design tied to UD constraints.
- Strength: broad, thorough evaluation including ablations and error propagation analyses quantifying PageRank and decoding contributions.
- Strength: competitive accuracy and greater stability than a delexicalized transfer baseline, with smaller degradation from gold to predicted POS.
- Strength: minimal per-language tuning aside from English-based rule choices and ADP handling inferred from test data.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on POS tag quality; “unsupervised” claim depends on supervised POS availability and content/function-only setup hurts performance.
- Weakness: test-time ADP estimation may constitute target-domain peeking and could be brittle for small/skewed test samples.
- Weakness: limited rule coverage misses phenomena (e.g., NOUN→VERB relative clauses), with variable performance on PUNCT, CONJ and language-specific DET direction issues.
- Weakness: comparisons limited to a delexicalized transfer baseline; missing baselines like projection-based or typology-informed parsers and neutralized-direction metrics.
- Weakness: missing reporting details — frequency of constraint backoffs, non-projective rates, statistical significance, and runtime breakdown (PageRank vs decoding).
- Question: sensitivity of results to PageRank teleport parameter and 5× personalization weight, and whether language- or sentence-length-specific settings were explored.
- Question: minimum number of sentences needed to estimate ADP direction reliably and robustness under distribution/domain shifts.
- Question: whether DET/AUX/SCONJ directionality could be estimated per-language at test time to fix issues like Basque DET.
- Question: how often the algorithm drops rule/direction constraints, correlation with errors, and fraction of non-projective trees produced.
- Question: availability of per-language-family analyses (e.g., SOV vs SVO, case-rich vs analytic) to explain when PageRank and two-step decoding help or hurt.
- Overall: well-written, transparent, and a strong, interpretable training-free baseline with compelling empirical coverage and clear extensions; reviewer recommends acceptance.
