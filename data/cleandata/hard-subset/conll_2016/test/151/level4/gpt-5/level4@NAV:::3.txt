This paper proposes a method for parsing trees (specifically, the Universal Dependencies treebanks) that relies solely on POS tags and employs a modified PageRank to assign higher importance to content words (as opposed to stop words). The idea is appealing but very close to what Søgaard (2012) presented. The personalization that boosts the main predicate is a nice addition, but it would have been preferable to push this idea further. As far as I can tell, personalization is applied only to the main predicate, and the chosen weight of 5 appears arbitrary.

For the evaluation and detailed analyses, some figures would have been helpful, as it can be hard to extract the main takeaways from the tables alone. It would also be useful to report POS tagging scores in prediction mode to check whether the drop in parsing accuracy is strongly correlated with the drop in tagging accuracy (as one would expect).

Overall, the paper is interesting, but the improvement over Søgaard (2012) is modest.
