- Introduces a deterministic dependency-parsing algorithm using POS-based head rules, a slightly biased PageRank to rank words, and connecting each word to the nearest valid head while preserving tree structure and directionality constraints.  
- Paper is generally engaging and well-written but seems only marginally different from Søgaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training."  
- Head rules (Table 1) need corpus-based analysis: how often do they produce a disconnected graph and what are the typical sizes of resulting components?  
- Determining head direction from test data is surprising and unconventional, even if it converges quickly (10–15 sentences).  
- Paper does not explain tie-breaking for equal PageRank scores—need clarity on method and whether ties affect performance.  
- The imposed constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) create upper bounds on performance; include oracle results per constraint to quantify these limits and guide future improvements.  
- Minor issues: typo in Section 4.1 ("we obtain [the] rank"), inconsistent column order between Tables 5 and 7 (Table 7 clearer), and typo in Section 6.1 ("isolate the [contribution] of both").
