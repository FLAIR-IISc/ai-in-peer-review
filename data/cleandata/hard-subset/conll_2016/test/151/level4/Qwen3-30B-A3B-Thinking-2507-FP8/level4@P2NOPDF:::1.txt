

This paper introduces a deterministic dependency parsing algorithm based on POS-tagged head rules and analyzes its cross-lingual performance. The algorithm employs a biased PageRank ranking over a graph defined by permitted dependencies, then constructs a tree by sequentially linking each word to the closest valid head that satisfies head rules, directionality constraints, and tree formation requirements. While the approach is clearly presented and well-motivated, it exhibits significant conceptual overlap with Sogaard (2012), particularly in its unsupervised parsing strategy and rule-based dependency constraints. The novelty appears incremental rather than transformative.

The following points require clarification or expansion:

1.  **Head Rules Analysis:** Table 1 presents the head rules, but the paper lacks analysis of their corpus-specific behavior. Section 3.1 notes that the rules sometimes fail to produce connected graphs, yet does not quantify this occurrence (e.g., frequency across languages) or characterize the typical size of disconnected components. A brief corpus analysis would strengthen the justification for the chosen rules.

2.  **Head Directionality Decision:** The choice to determine head directionality using test data (rather than training or development data) is methodologically unusual. Given the rapid convergence observed (within 10-15 sentences), this is not a critical flaw, but it warrants explanation to align with standard unsupervised learning practices.

3.  **Tie-Breaking Mechanism:** The paper does not specify how ties in PageRank scores are resolved. Clarifying this mechanism and its impact on performance (e.g., whether ties are rare or significantly affect parsing accuracy) is necessary for reproducibility and understanding the algorithm's robustness.

4.  **Constraint Impact Assessment:** The constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) inherently limit the system's performance ceiling. To better contextualize results and guide future improvements, the authors should include oracle analyses demonstrating the maximum achievable score *under each constraint individually*. This would quantitatively show the performance penalty imposed by each constraint and identify the most promising targets for modification.

Minor Corrections:
*   Section 4.1: "we obtain [the] rank" (remove "[the]").
*   Table 5 vs. Table 7: The column ordering differs between these tables. Table 7's ordering is more intuitive for comparing results.
*   Section 6.1: "isolate the [contribution] of both" (remove "[contribution]"; likely meant "isolate the contribution of both factors").

The paper makes a reasonable contribution to unsupervised parsing, but the limited novelty relative to Sogaard (2012) and the lack of deeper analysis regarding constraint impacts and rule behavior are notable weaknesses. Addressing the points above—particularly the oracle analysis and head rule corpus statistics—would significantly strengthen the work and clarify its position within the field. The core algorithm is sound, but the evaluation and discussion need greater depth to justify its significance.
