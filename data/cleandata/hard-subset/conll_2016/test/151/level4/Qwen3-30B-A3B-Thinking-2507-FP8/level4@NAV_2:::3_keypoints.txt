- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using only POS tags and a modified PageRank.
- Main contributions: adapt dependency head rules to UD POS relations, enforce UD function-word-as-leaf restriction, and show robustness to domain changes versus delexicalized transfer.
- Competitive performance vs delexicalized transfer (MSD) across 32 languages with a 6.4% average UAS gap and better results on Hindi.
- Demonstrates greater domain robustness: lower standard deviation across domains (0.7–1.0) compared to MSD (1.5–2.5).
- Shows lower error propagation from POS to parsing (0.37) than MSD (0.46) via evaluation across POS scenarios.
- Approach is highly similar to Søgaard (2012b); novelty largely limited to UD adaptation.
- Personalization weight of 5 for main predicate is unjustified and appears arbitrary.
- Paper lacks visualizations/charts, making key findings hard to interpret from tables alone.
- Evaluation of UD compliance is superficial; no detailed analysis of challenging UD constructions (e.g., relative clauses, coordination).
- Requests justification and sensitivity analysis for the personalization weight choice.
- Requests plotting of parsing performance across domains to substantiate robustness claims.
- Requests POS tagging accuracy for the predicted POS setup to clarify POS-to-parsing error relationships.
