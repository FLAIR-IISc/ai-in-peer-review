1. Introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using only POS constraints and a small set of head rules, requiring no training data.  
2. Contributions: UD-adapted head rules, enforcing function words as leaves, and personalized PageRank for main predicate identification.  
3. Strength: Robust to domain shifts, showing consistent performance across different text domains compared to delexicalized parsers.  
4. Strength: Evaluation under gold/predicted/naive POS scenarios shows lower error propagation (0.37) than the supervised baseline (0.46).  
5. Strength: Simple, language-agnostic design (estimates adposition direction from test data) and competitive across 32 languages.  
6. Weakness: Limited novelty relative to Søgaard (2012); framework and use of PageRank are similar with incremental improvements.  
7. Weakness: Personalization weight of 5 is arbitrarily chosen with no sensitivity analysis or justification.  
8. Weakness: Over-reliance on tables rather than visualizations makes key insights (e.g., domain-shift stability) harder to grasp.  
9. Weakness: Lacks sufficient analysis of how POS tagging accuracy affects parsing; predicted POS tagging scores are not reported.  
10. Weakness: The Hindi result outperforming delexicalized transfer is reported but not analyzed to determine cause or generality.  
11. Question: How was the personalization weight of 5 determined; please provide a sensitivity analysis.  
12. Question: Please include POS tagging accuracy for the predicted POS (UDPP) setup.  
13. Question: Can you analyze why UDP outperforms delexicalized transfer on Hindi specifically?  
14. Question: Why is UDP's standard deviation across domains lower than MSD's — due to general rule-based stability or UDP-specific design?
