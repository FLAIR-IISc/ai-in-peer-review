- Summary: UDP is an unsupervised UD parser needing no training, using POS constraints and a few head rules, claimed competitive with delexicalized transfer parsers and robust to cross-lingual domain shifts.

- Contribution 1: Main technical idea is using personalized PageRank to identify main predicates.

- Contribution 2: Adapting dependency head rules to fit Universal Dependencies formalism.

- Contribution 3: Estimating adposition attachment direction from test data to remain language-agnostic.

- Strength 1: Competitive performance with supervised transfer—UDPG averages 63.9 UAS vs. MSDG's 61.2 UAS on gold POS and outperforms MSDG on Hindi.

- Strength 2: Greater robustness to domain shifts, shown by consistently lower standard deviations across domains than the supervised baseline.

- Strength 3: Low sensitivity to POS errors—only a 2.2% drop with predicted POS vs. MSD's 2.7%—useful where gold POS is unavailable.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—PageRank ranking follows Søgaard (2012b) and the personalization weight choice lacks justification or ablation.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient analysis supporting claimed domain robustness; no detailed error analysis explaining why robustness occurs or specific domain failures.

- Weakness 3: Lack of transparency on POS tagging performance—only an average TnT accuracy reported, no language-specific POS scores to correlate parsing errors.

- Question 1: Why was a personalization weight of 5 chosen? Provide justification or ablation (e.g., weights 3, 5, 7).

- Question 2: Provide a detailed error analysis across domains (e.g., legal vs. news) to explain the claimed robustness.

- Question 3: Explain why UDP outperforms MSD on Hindi with a language-specific error analysis and relation to UD formalism.

- Minor issue 1: Typo on Line 207: "The the main idea" should be "The main idea".

- Minor issue 2: Paper would benefit from more visualizations (e.g., error rate per POS tag) to complement tables.

- Overall: Solid contribution showing rule-based unsupervised parsing can be competitive and robust, but incremental over prior work and would be strengthened by ablations and deeper domain/POS analyses.
