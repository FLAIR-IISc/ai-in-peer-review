- Presents UDP: an unsupervised, training-free UD parser using deterministic algorithms and personalized PageRank over a graph built from dependency head rules.

- Core contributions: (1) linguistically grounded unsupervised method exploiting UD constraints, (2) personalized PageRank for main predicate ID, (3) adposition direction estimated from test data without language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Competitive performance vs. supervised delexicalized transfer (avg UAS gap 6.4% over 32 languages) and outperforms multi-source delexicalized on Hindi.

- Strength: High robustness to domain change, showing much lower standard deviation across domains than data-driven baselines.

- Strength: Simple and resource-light—relies only on POS tags and UD constraints, making it suitable for low-resource deployment.

- Weakness: Limited novelty, largely building on Søgaard (2012) with incremental UD-specific modifications and insufficient evidence of overcoming prior limitations.

- Weakness: Evaluation omits critical analysis of head-rule coverage, frequency of disconnected graphs, and sizes of disconnected components.

- Weakness: Unjustified choice to estimate adposition direction from test data instead of development data, despite directionality being learnable from few sentences.

- Question: What is the frequency of disconnected graphs and the size distribution of disconnected components produced by the head-rule graph construction?

- Question: How are ties in PageRank scores broken and what is the effect of the tie-breaking strategy on parser performance?

- Question: Can the authors provide oracle (upper-bound) results for individual constraints—head rules, directionality, and distance—to quantify theoretical limits?

- Overall assessment: Well-written and clearly presented with strong domain evaluation, but limited novelty and insufficient justification/analysis of key methodological choices weaken the paper's contribution.
