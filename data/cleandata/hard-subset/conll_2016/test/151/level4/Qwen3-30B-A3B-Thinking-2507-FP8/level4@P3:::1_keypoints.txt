- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD dependency parser requiring no training data and showing competitive performance across 32 languages.  
- Contribution: linguistically motivated rule-based parser that rivals supervised transfer systems in many languages.  
- Contribution: demonstrates strong cross-domain robustness with much lower UAS variance than supervised transfer baselines.  
- Contribution: proposes a method to estimate adposition (pre/postposition) direction from a small number of test sentences, removing the need for gold directionality.  

- Strength: UDP (gold POS) achieves close-to-supervised UAS (63.9% vs. 69.7% MSDG), even outperforming MSDG on some languages (e.g., Hindi).  
- Strength: Substantially lower standard deviation across domains (1.8 UAS for UDP vs. 4.5 for MSD), showing practical stability under domain shift.  
- Strength: Adposition direction estimation from ~10–15 sentences attains ~75% ADP UAS, making the approach practical for low-resource settings.  

- Weakness: Novelty is limited; approach is a straightforward extension of Søgaard (2012) with only modest changes (personalized PageRank, two-step decoding).  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of head rules—no quantification of how often they produce disconnected graphs or the sizes of disconnected components.  
- Weakness: Choice to estimate head direction from test data rather than development data is unexplained and needs justification.  
- Weakness: Missing oracle analyses for individual constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) to show theoretical upper bounds and where improvements would help most.  

- Q: Provide statistics on how often head rules produce disconnected graphs and the typical sizes of disconnected components.  
- Q: Why estimate head direction from test data instead of development data, and what is the performance impact of using development data?  
- Q: How are ties in PageRank scores broken for words with identical scores, and does tie-breaking materially affect performance?  
- Q: Provide oracle results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to clarify theoretical limits.  

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written, methodologically clear, evaluated across many languages and domains, and code availability supports reproducibility.  
- Additional comment: Authors should better differentiate from Søgaard (2012) by clearly explaining how personalized PageRank and two-step decoding address prior limitations and by providing a more detailed baseline comparison.
