- Proposes a POS-only dependency parsing method using a modified PageRank to prioritize meaningful words over stop words.  
- Core idea is conceptually interesting but the contribution is modest relative to prior work.  
- Strong resemblance to Søgaard (2012) which also used POS tags and personalized PageRank.  
- Personalization scheme (weight of 5 for main predicate) is thoughtful but unjustified and appears arbitrary.  
- No exploration of alternative weights or richer personalization criteria (e.g., other syntactic roles).  
- Evaluation presentation is dense and hard to interpret; needs clearer visuals (e.g., bar charts, breakdowns by relation or word type).  
- Critical omission: no POS tagging accuracy reported for the pipeline, preventing attribution of parsing errors.  
- Authors should explicitly correlate tagging and parsing performance to validate claims about POS-driven degradation.  
- Lack of comparison with more recent POS-based or neural parsers weakens the novelty claim.  
- Minor issues: typo ("The the main idea"), vague term "meaningful words", and unclear how stop words are excluded/suppressed.  
- Overall: technically sound but incremental; needs stronger justification, deeper evaluation, and clearer positioning against Søgaard (2012) and contemporary methods.
