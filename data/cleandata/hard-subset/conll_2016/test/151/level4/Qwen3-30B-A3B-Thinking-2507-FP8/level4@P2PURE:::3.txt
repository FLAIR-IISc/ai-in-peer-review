

This paper proposes a method for parsing universal dependency treebanks using only part-of-speech (POS) tags, leveraging a modified PageRank algorithm that assigns greater weight to meaningful words (as opposed to stop words). While the idea is interesting, it is very close to the approach presented in Søgaard (2012). The personalization factor that prioritizes the main predicate is a nice touch, but it would have been better to extend this approach further. As far as I can determine, the personalization is applied solely to the main predicate, and the weight of 5 appears arbitrary. Regarding evaluation and detailed analysis, including supplementary charts would have been beneficial, as the tables alone make it difficult to grasp key insights. Additionally, it would have been valuable to report the POS tagging scores in the prediction mode to assess whether the decline in parsing performance correlates strongly with the decline in tagging performance (which is the expected relationship). Overall, the paper is interesting, but the incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is small.
