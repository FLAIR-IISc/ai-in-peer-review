- Introduces a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank ranking over permitted-dependency graphs, and iterative linking to the nearest valid preceding head.  
- Paper is well-written and interesting but seems only marginally different from Søgaard (2012), raising novelty concerns.  
- Head rules (Table 1) need empirical analysis: quantify how often they produce disconnected graphs and the typical sizes of disconnected components.  
- Head directionality is chosen using test data rather than train/dev; this unexpected choice needs justification despite fast convergence.  
- Tie-breaking for identical PageRank scores is unspecified; report the tie-breaking mechanism and whether ties materially affect performance.  
- Constraint analysis is lacking: provide oracle upper-bound results for head rules, directionality, and distance constraints to show their impact on achievable scores.  
- Minor copyedits: fix "we obtain [the] rank" → "we obtain the rank"; harmonize column orders between Tables 5 and 7 (Table 7 clearer); fix "isolate the [contribution] of both" → "isolate the contribution of both."
