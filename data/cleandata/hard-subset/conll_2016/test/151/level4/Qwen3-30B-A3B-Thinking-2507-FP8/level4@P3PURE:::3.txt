

This paper proposes a dependency parsing method for universal dependency treebanks that relies solely on part-of-speech (POS) tags, utilizing a modified PageRank algorithm to assign greater weight to meaningful words (as opposed to stop words). While the idea is interesting, it is very close to the approach presented in Søgaard (2012). The personalization factor that prioritizes the main predicate is a thoughtful touch, but it would have been more compelling to extend this approach further. As far as I can determine, the personalization is applied exclusively to the main predicate with a fixed weight of 5, which appears arbitrary. Regarding evaluation, supplementary charts would have been beneficial to complement the tables, as key insights are sometimes difficult to discern from tabular data alone. Additionally, reporting the POS tagging scores in the prediction mode would have allowed for a clearer assessment of whether the parsing performance degradation is strongly correlated with tagging performance degradation—precisely the relationship one would expect. Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach, but the incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is modest.
