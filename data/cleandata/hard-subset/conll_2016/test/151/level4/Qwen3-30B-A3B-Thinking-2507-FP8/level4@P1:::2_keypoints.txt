1. Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser that requires no training and leverages UD constraints.  
2. Contribution: linguistically grounded approach exploiting UD formalism (single-rooted trees, function words as dependents).  
3. Contribution: personalized PageRank method to improve main predicate identification.  
4. Contribution: method to estimate adposition attachment direction from test data without language-specific parameters.  
5. Contribution: two-step decoding algorithm that treats content and function words differently per UD formalism.  
6. Strength: UDP matches or rivals a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser (MSD) across 32 languages, sometimes outperforming it (e.g., Hindi).  
7. Strength: Strong robustness to domain shifts, showing lower variance across domains than data-driven parsers.  
8. Strength: Thorough evaluation across POS scenarios showing lower error propagation from POS to dependency errors than MSD.  
9. Strength: Methodological clarity and reproducibility, with well-explained PageRank and two-step decoding and deep UD constraint analysis.  
10. Weakness: The claim that UDP "compares favorably" to MSD is overstated given the marginal average performance gap and inconsistent per-language wins.  
11. Weakness: Insufficient treatment of languages/treebanks that deviate from UD formalism; evaluation assumes equal compliance across treebanks.  
12. Weakness: Lack of analysis for the 2.2% UAS drop with predicted POS tags relative to MSD despite lower error propagation—POS tagger quality may be limiting.  
13. Weakness: No discussion of computational complexity in practical terms (e.g., wall-clock comparisons to existing parsers).  
14. Question: How would parser performance change if evaluation were restricted to treebanks with high UD formalism compliance?  
15. Question: How do you explain cases where UDP underperforms MSD in specific domains (e.g., Bulgarian legal documents) despite claimed domain robustness?  
16. Question: Can you provide more nuanced analysis of where UDP consistently excels relative to MSD beyond isolated cases like Hindi?
