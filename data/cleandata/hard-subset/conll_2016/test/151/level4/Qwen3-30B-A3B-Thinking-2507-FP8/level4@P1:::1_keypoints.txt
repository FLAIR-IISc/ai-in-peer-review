- Summary: UDP is an unsupervised UD parser needing no training, using POS-based head rules, personalized PageRank, and on-the-fly adposition direction estimation; shows competitive performance and domain robustness.

- Strength 1: Demonstrates strong robustness to domain shifts, maintaining consistent performance across genres unlike data-driven MSD.

- Strength 2: Simple, training-free design benefits low-resource languages and leverages UD constraints (e.g., function words as leaves).

- Strength 3: Evaluation is thorough across gold/predicted/simplified POS and domains; small performance drop with predicted POS (2.2%).

- Weakness 1: Novelty is limited compared to Søgaard (2012); contributions appear incremental and need clearer differentiation.

- Weakness 2: Head rules (Table 1) lack quantitative analysis—coverage and frequency of disconnected graphs are unreported.

- Weakness 3: Estimating adposition direction from test data (10–15 sentences) is unconventional and needs justification over using dev data.

- Question 1: Request statistics on head rule coverage across 32 languages—percent words covered and rate of disconnected graphs.

- Question 2: Ask how PageRank ties are deterministically broken and how tie-breaking affects performance.

- Question 3: Request clarification on the claimed Hindi result where UDP allegedly outperforms MSD despite table values showing MSDG > UDPG.

- Question 4: Ask authors to justify the claim that UDP is "competitive" with delexicalized transfer given UDPG (69.7) is below MSDG (73.5).

- Question 5: Suggest standardizing column order between Table 5 and Table 7 for consistency.
