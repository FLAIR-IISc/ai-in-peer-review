- Proposes a POS-only dependency parsing method using a modified PageRank to prioritize content words, but closely resembles Søgaard (2012) and offers modest novelty.  
- Personalization that increases the main predicate’s weight (set to 5) is a reasonable idea but underdeveloped and arbitrarily chosen without justification.  
- Personalization is applied only to the main predicate; method would be stronger if extended to other syntactic roles or weights derived from linguistic principles.  
- Evaluation tables are clear but lack visualizations (e.g., bar plots) that would better summarize trends and highlight strengths/weaknesses.  
- Missing POS tagging performance metrics in prediction mode prevents assessing whether parsing drops are due to tagging errors or the parser itself.  
- Reported gains (around 0.5–1.0 F1) are marginal given methodological overlap with prior work and do not convincingly demonstrate substantial improvement.  
- Authors fail to argue convincingly why their specific personalization strategy (single-predicate focus, weight=5) outperforms or differs meaningfully from prior approaches.  
- Minor editorial issue: Line 207 contains "The the main idea" → should be "The main idea."  
- Summary recommendation: empirically justify personalization weights, extend personalization beyond main predicate, include POS tagging metrics, and add visualizations to clarify results.
