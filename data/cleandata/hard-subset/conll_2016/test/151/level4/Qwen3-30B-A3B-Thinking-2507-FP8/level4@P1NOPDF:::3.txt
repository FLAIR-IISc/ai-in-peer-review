

This paper proposes a parsing approach for universal dependency treebanks that relies solely on part-of-speech (POS) tags, using a modified PageRank algorithm to assign greater weight to meaningful words (e.g., content words over stop words). While the core idea is interesting, it closely resembles Søgaard (2012), making the contribution feel incremental. The personalization factor prioritizing the main predicate is a thoughtful touch, but extending this concept would have strengthened the work. Specifically, the personalization is applied exclusively to the main predicate with a weight of 5, which appears arbitrary without justification or exploration of alternative values.  

The evaluation section would benefit from visualizations. While the tables present results clearly, supplementary charts (e.g., error analysis breakdowns or performance comparisons across categories) would make key insights more immediately accessible than dense tabular data.  

A critical omission is the lack of POS tagging scores under the prediction setup. Reporting these alongside parsing results would clarify whether the observed parsing performance drop correlates strongly with tagging accuracy degradation—a relationship the authors presumably anticipate. Including this would significantly strengthen the analysis of their pipeline’s dependencies.  

Overall, the paper presents a plausible parsing method grounded in a reasonable modification of existing techniques. However, the incremental advance over Søgaard (2012) is minimal, particularly given the limited exploration of the personalization parameter and the absence of critical performance context for the POS component. The approach demonstrates feasibility but does not substantially advance the state of the art in dependency parsing under the described constraints.
