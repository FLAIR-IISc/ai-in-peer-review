- Summary: UDP is a fully unsupervised UD parser that leverages UD constraints, personalized PageRank to find main predicates, and a small set of UD-compliant head rules, estimating adposition direction from test data so it has no language-specific parameters.

- Contribution: Introduces a linguistically grounded unsupervised parsing approach that exploits UD formal constraints instead of data-driven transfer.

- Contribution: Novel use of personalized PageRank to identify main predicates without training data.

- Contribution: A completely parameter-free system that infers adposition attachment direction directly from test data.

- Strength: Demonstrates competitive performance with multi-source delexicalized transfer (average UAS gap ~6.4%) and even outperforms MSD on Hindi.

- Strength: Shows strong robustness to domain shifts with much lower performance variability across domains compared to MSD.

- Strength: Method is simple, effective, computationally efficient (O(n×|C|) decoding), and clearly described for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to 32 languages (Japanese excluded), leaving performance on morphologically complex or different word-order languages underexplored.

- Weakness: Head-rule limitations—especially excluding NOUN→VERB for relative clauses—are not analyzed in depth, weakening linguistic claims.

- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of error patterns when using predicted POS tags despite showing lower error propagation rates.

- Question: Please provide statistical significance for the marginal Hindi win (55.3 vs. 54.8 UAS) and explain why UDP performs better on Hindi.

- Question: How many relative clauses occur in UD treebanks and how does excluding the NOUN→VERB rule affect overall performance?

- Question: Why were Bulgarian, Croatian, Italian, and English chosen for domain evaluation, and are there plans to expand the domain analysis to more languages?
