1. UDP is an unsupervised UD parser needing no training data, using UD constraints, personalized PageRank, and test-time adposition direction estimation to achieve competitive, robust parsing.  
2. Main contribution: a linguistically principled unsupervised parser exploiting UD constraints (function words as leaves, head rules) without language-specific parameters.  
3. Main contribution: a robust method to estimate adposition direction from minimal test data (10–15 sentences), removing pre-defined language rules.  
4. Main contribution: competitive performance vs supervised cross-lingual parsers (within 6.4% UAS of MSDG) with much lower sensitivity to domain shifts.  
5. Strength: achieves state-of-the-art unsupervised performance (63.9 UAS on gold POS), outperforming delexicalized transfer on 12 languages and notably on Hindi.  
6. Strength: exceptional robustness to domain shifts — 3.7× lower UAS standard deviation across domains than MSD.  
7. Strength: provides a linguistically sound alternative to transfer approaches by strictly enforcing UD constraints and avoiding supervised biases.  
8. Strength: personalized PageRank (5× weight to main predicate) substantially improves content-word ranking and yields a 9 UAS point gain over non-personalized baseline.  
9. Weakness: high sensitivity to POS quality — performance collapses with naive POS tagging (29.9 UAS vs. 63.9 UAS).  
10. Weakness: adposition direction estimation requires 10–15 sentences to converge, limiting use on very short texts and in low-resource settings.  
11. Weakness: evaluation gaps — Japanese excluded and lacks error analysis for problematic POS tags (e.g., very low PUNCT UAS).  
12. Question: how does the parser perform on languages with divergent word order (e.g., SOV languages like Japanese) where adposition estimation may be error-prone?  
13. Question: can adposition direction estimation be refined using UD treebank statistics across languages to reduce required test data?  
14. Question: can UDP handle relative clauses by dynamically inferring NOUN→VERB relations from UD dependency hierarchy instead of excluding them?
