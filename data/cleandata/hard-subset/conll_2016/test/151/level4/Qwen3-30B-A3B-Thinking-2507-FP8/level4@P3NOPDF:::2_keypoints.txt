- Proposes a novel unsupervised UD annotation method using PageRank on lexical co-occurrence graphs plus a small set of hand-crafted linguistic rules.  
- Requires no labeled training data, deriving dependency structures via iterative ranking of word co-occurrences.  
- Uses targeted rule-based constraints to resolve ambiguities (e.g., negation, clausal boundaries).  
- Leverages statistical properties of language instead of data-intensive supervised approaches.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-structured and clearly written.  
- Authors thoroughly justify design choices, including PageRank weight derivation and specific lexical resources/rules.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance against established baselines on multiple UD treebanks without supervision.  
- Aligns with UD’s cross-lingual goals by relying on universal linguistic principles rather than language-specific training.  
- Provides a coherent theoretical framework explaining why the method works, enhancing interpretability.  
- Suggests concrete future directions: integrate morphological features, refine rule sets cross-linguistically, and extend to other tasks like semantic role labeling.  
- Minor limitation: performance depends on quality and coverage of lexical resources, which may affect scalability in under-resourced languages.  
- Overall recommendation: robust, principled contribution recommended for acceptance at CONLL_2016.
