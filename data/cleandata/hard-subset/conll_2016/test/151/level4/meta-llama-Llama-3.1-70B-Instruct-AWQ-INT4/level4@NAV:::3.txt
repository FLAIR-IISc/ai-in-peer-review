This paper proposes a novel approach to parsing trees, specifically universal dependency treebanks, utilizing solely part-of-speech (POS) information and a modified PageRank algorithm. This method prioritizes meaningful words over stop words, which is an intriguing concept that bears some resemblance to the work presented in Søgaard (2012). The incorporation of a personalization factor to emphasize the primary predicate is a welcome addition, although its potential could have been further explored. Notably, the personalization appears to be limited to the main predicate, with an assigned weight of 5 that seems somewhat arbitrary.

The evaluation and in-depth analysis would have benefited from the inclusion of visual aids, such as charts, to facilitate a clearer understanding of the data presented in the tables. Furthermore, it would be enlightening to see the POS tagging scores in prediction mode, allowing for an assessment of whether the decline in parsing performance is strongly correlated with the degradation in tagging performance, as intuitively expected.

In conclusion, while the paper presents an interesting idea, the incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is relatively minor.
