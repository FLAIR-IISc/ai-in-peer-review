- Paper proposes a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank ranking, and nearest-tree linking with head/directionality constraints.  
- Contribution seems mainly an extension of Søgaard (2012) rather than a wholly new paradigm.  
- Request analysis of head rules: report how often they produce disconnected graphs and typical component sizes.  
- Concern that head directionality is inferred from test data rather than train/dev, which is counterintuitive despite rapid convergence.  
- Ask for clarification on PageRank tie-breaking and whether ties materially affect performance.  
- Recommend reporting oracle upper bounds per constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to quantify their impact on achievable performance.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: rephrase "we obtain [the] rank" (Sec. 4.1), make column orders consistent between Table 5 and Table 7 (prefer Table 7 order), and reword "isolate the [contribution] of both" (Sec. 6.1).  
- Overall paper is well-written and engaging.
