**Summary of the Paper**

The authors present an unsupervised parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies (UD) that relies on PageRank and a small set of specific dependency head rules. The parser, called UDP, requires no training and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system. The authors evaluate UDP on 32 languages and show that it is robust to domain change across languages.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Unsupervised parsing without training**: The authors propose an unsupervised parsing algorithm that does not require training data, making it a significant contribution to the field of unsupervised parsing.
2. **Use of PageRank and hard-coded rules**: The authors use PageRank to score words and a small set of hard-coded rules to determine the dependency structure of sentences.
3. **Robustness to domain change**: The authors show that UDP is robust to domain change across languages, making it a valuable contribution to the field of cross-lingual parsing.

**Strengths**

1. **Substantial contribution to the field**: The authors' contribution is substantial to the field of unsupervised parsing, and the possibilities for future work presented by the authors give rise to additional research.
2. **Well-written and detailed article**: The article is well-written, very detailed, and the intuition behind all prior information being added to the model is explained clearly.
3. **Competitive performance**: UDP's performance is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system, making it a valuable contribution to the field of cross-lingual parsing.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate UDP on 32 languages, and it is unclear how well the parser will perform on other languages.
2. **Dependence on hard-coded rules**: The parser relies on a small set of hard-coded rules, which may not be sufficient to capture the complexity of all languages.
3. **Limited handling of multiword expressions and coordination**: The parser does not handle multiword expressions and coordination, which are important aspects of natural language processing.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend UDP to handle multiword expressions and coordination?
2. Can you provide more details on how you plan to expand the use of PageRank to directly score potential dependency edges?
3. How do you think UDP will perform on languages that are not part of the UD project?
