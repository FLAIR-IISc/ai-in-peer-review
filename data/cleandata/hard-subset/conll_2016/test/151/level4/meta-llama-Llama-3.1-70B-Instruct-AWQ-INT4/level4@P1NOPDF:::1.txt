This paper presents a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and evaluates its performance across a range of languages. The algorithm's core component is a set of rules defining permitted dependencies based on POS tags. It begins by ranking words using a slightly biased PageRank over a graph with edges defined by the permitted dependencies. Then, stepping through the ranking, each word is linked to the closest word that maintains a tree structure and adheres to the head rules and a directionality constraint.

Overall, the paper is well-written and engaging, although its contribution appears to be an extension of Sogaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training". I have several questions and suggestions to further improve the paper:

Regarding the Head Rules (Table 1), it would be beneficial to provide an analysis of these rules in relation to the corpus. For instance, in section 3.1, the authors mention that these rules do not always yield a connected graph, but they do not discuss the frequency of this occurrence or the typical size of the resulting components. I was also surprised to find that the head direction was determined using the test data rather than the training or development data. Although the decision converges rapidly (within 10-15 sentences), this choice seems counterintuitive. 

Another point of clarification is required regarding tie-breaking for words with the same PageRank score. Does this have a significant impact on performance, or are ties rare enough to be negligible? The various types of constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) will inevitably lead to upper bounds on the system's possible performance. To provide a clearer understanding of these limitations, it would be informative to include oracle results for each constraint, illustrating how much they affect the maximum achievable score. This information would be particularly helpful in guiding future work by identifying areas where the system could be improved.

Minor suggestions include:

- In section 4.1, consider rephrasing "we obtain [the] rank" for improved clarity.
- The column orders in Table 5 and Table 7 differ. I found the arrangement in Table 7 to be clearer and more intuitive.
- In section 6.1, rephrase "isolate the [contribution] of both" to improve readability.
