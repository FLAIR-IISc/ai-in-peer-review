This paper proposes a method for parsing universal dependency treebanks using only part-of-speech (POS) tags and a modified version of PageRank, which assigns more weight to meaningful words over stop words. Although this idea is interesting, it bears a strong resemblance to the approach presented in Søgaard (2012). The introduction of a personalization factor that prioritizes the main predicate is a welcome addition; however, its potential could be further explored. Notably, the personalization appears to be solely applied to the main predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 seems arbitrary.

In terms of evaluation and detailed analysis, the inclusion of charts would have facilitated a clearer understanding of the results, as the tables can be somewhat difficult to interpret. Furthermore, it would have been insightful to report the POS tagging scores in prediction mode to determine whether the decline in parsing performance is strongly correlated with the degradation in tagging performance, which is a expected outcome.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach, but the incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is relatively small.
