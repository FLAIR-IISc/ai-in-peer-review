- Presents UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using personalized PageRank plus a small set of dependency head rules, requiring no training.
- Claims competitive performance with delexicalized transfer systems and robustness across languages and domains.
- Contribution: novel algorithm combining personalized PageRank with head-dependent rules for UD parsing.
- Contribution: small, UD-adapted head rules that enforce UD constraint of function words being leaves.
- Contribution: adposition attachment direction estimated on test data, making the method language-parameter free.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated approach leveraging UD constraints.
- Strength: personalized PageRank + few rules yields competitive results across UD languages.
- Strength: evaluation on 32 languages and three POS setups shows robustness and generalizability.
- Strength: outperforms/compares favorably to multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD) under noisy POS and domain shifts.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of how head rules relate to corpus evidence and why they work.
- Weakness: surprising choice to estimate head direction from test data, which may be suboptimal.
- Weakness: missing oracle ablations per constraint to show upper-bound contributions.
- Weakness: evaluation could be broader (more languages and POS setups).
- Q: Clarify how head rules were selected and adapted to UD POS relations.
- Q: Provide more details on estimating adposition attachment direction on test data.
- Q: Explain handling when head rules produce a disconnected graph.
- Q: Describe tie-breaking mechanism for identical PageRank scores.
- Additional: paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Additional: tables and figures effectively illustrate performance and behavior.
- Additional: comparison to Søgaard (2012) helps contextualize novelty.
