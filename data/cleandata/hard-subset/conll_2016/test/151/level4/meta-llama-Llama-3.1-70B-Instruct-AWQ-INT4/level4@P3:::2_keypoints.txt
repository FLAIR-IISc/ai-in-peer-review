Summary: Proposes UDP, a training-free unsupervised UD parser using PageRank to rank content words and a few head rules, competitive with delexicalized transfer.

Contribution 1: Introduces a novel unsupervised, no-training UD parsing algorithm.

Contribution 2: Uses PageRank to identify relevant content words combined with a small set of linguistic head rules.

Contribution 3: Evaluates on 32 languages, showing competitiveness with delexicalized transfer systems.

Strength 1: Algorithm and components are clearly and thoroughly explained.

Strength 2: PageRank for main predicate identification is a novel, linguistically motivated idea.

Strength 3: Robust multilingual evaluation across 32 languages supports generality.

Strength 4: Provides detailed analyses (POS breakdown, domain-robustness).

Weakness 1: Relies on a small set of hard-coded rules that may not generalize across languages/domains.

Weakness 2: PageRank may struggle with sentences having multiple main predicates or complex structures.

Weakness 3: Does not handle multiword expressions, coordination, or proper names, limiting applicability.

Question 1: How will the system be extended to handle MWEs, coordination, and proper names?

Question 2: How were the specific dependency head rules selected?

Question 3: How will the method be evaluated on languages without UD treebanks?

Question 4: How was the error propagation rate from POS to dependency errors calculated?
