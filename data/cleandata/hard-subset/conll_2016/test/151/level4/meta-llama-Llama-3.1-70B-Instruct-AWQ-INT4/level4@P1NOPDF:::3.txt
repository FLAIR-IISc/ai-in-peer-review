This paper proposes a method for parsing universal dependency treebanks using only part-of-speech (POS) information, and a modified version of PageRank to prioritize meaningful words over stop words. While this idea is intriguing, it bears a strong resemblance to the work presented in Søgaard (2012). The incorporation of a personalization factor to emphasize the main predicate is a welcome addition, but its potential could be further explored. Notably, the personalization appears to be solely applied to the main predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 seems arbitrary.

The evaluation and detailed analyses would benefit from the inclusion of charts to supplement the tables, as the latter can be challenging to interpret at times. Furthermore, it would be enlightening to report the POS tagging scores in prediction mode, allowing readers to assess whether the decline in parsing performance is closely correlated with the degradation in tagging performance, as one would expect.

In conclusion, while the paper presents an interesting approach, the incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is relatively minor.
