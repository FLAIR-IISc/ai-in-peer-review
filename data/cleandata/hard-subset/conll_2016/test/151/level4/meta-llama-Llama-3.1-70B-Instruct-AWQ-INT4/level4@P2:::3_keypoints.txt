- Presents UDP: an unsupervised UD-compliant dependency parser using only POS constraints, adapting head rules, treating function words as leaves, using personalized PageRank for main predicate identification, and estimating adposition attachment direction on test data; competitive with delexicalized transfer and robust across domains.

- Contribution: an unsupervised dependency parsing method leveraging UD constraints to achieve competitive performance without training data.

- Contribution: adaptation of dependency head rules to UD-compliant POS relations for better cross-lingual handling.

- Contribution: use of personalized PageRank to improve main predicate identification.

- Strength: achieves competitive performance without requiring annotated training data, useful for low-resource languages.

- Strength: robust to domain shifts across languages, aiding real-world applicability.

- Strength: handles language-specific nuances by adapting head rules to UD POS tags.

- Weakness: incremental over prior work (Søgaard 2012); PageRank idea is not novel.

- Weakness: personalization factor that boosts main predicate weight seems arbitrary and needs further exploration.

- Weakness: evaluation/analysis could use more visualizations (e.g., charts) for easier result interpretation.

- Question: rationale for the personalization factor weight of 5—was it optimized or chosen arbitrarily?

- Question: how will the approach handle limitations like multi-word expressions, coordination, and proper names?

- Question: can the authors provide POS tagging scores in prediction mode to assess correlation between POS tagging quality and parsing degradation?
