This paper presents a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and evaluates its performance across multiple languages. The algorithm's core relies on a set of rules defining permitted dependencies based on POS tags. It begins by ranking words using a biased PageRank over a graph with edges defined by the permitted dependencies. Then, stepping through the ranking, each word is linked to the closest word that maintains a tree structure and adheres to the head rules and directionality constraint.

Overall, the paper is well-structured and engaging, although its contribution appears to be relatively minor compared to Sogaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training". To further enhance the paper, I have a few suggestions and questions:

The head rules (Table 1) would benefit from a more in-depth analysis in relation to the corpus. For instance, while Section 3.1 mentions that these rules do not always yield a connected graph, it would be useful to provide more details on the frequency of this occurrence and the typical size of the resulting components. I was also surprised to find that head direction was determined using the test data rather than the training or development data. Although the decision converges rapidly (within 10-15 sentences), this choice seems unconventional. 

Another point of clarification is required regarding tie-breaking for words with the same PageRank score. Does this affect performance significantly, or are ties rare enough to have a negligible impact?

The various constraints (head rules, directionality, and distance) impose upper bounds on the system's potential performance. Including oracle results for each constraint would provide valuable insights into how these constraints affect the maximum achievable score. This information would be particularly helpful in guiding future work by identifying areas where the system could be improved.

Minor corrections and suggestions:

- In Section 4.1, consider rephrasing "we obtain [the] rank" for improved clarity.
- The column orders in Table 5 and Table 7 differ. I found the arrangement in Table 7 more intuitive.
- In Section 6.1, rephrase "isolate the [contribution] of both" for better readability.

These revisions should enhance the overall quality and clarity of the paper.
