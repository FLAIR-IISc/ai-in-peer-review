- Presents a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm using POS-based head rules and a slightly biased PageRank to rank words.  
- Algorithm links each word to the nearest compatible head while preserving tree structure and directionality constraints.  
- Paper is well-written, engaging, and offers a fresh perspective despite resemblance to Søgaard (2012).  
- Head rules (Table 1) need deeper analysis relative to the corpus, including frequency of disconnected graphs and component size statistics.  
- Surprising choice to determine head direction using test data rather than training/dev data; authors should justify or explore alternatives.  
- Tie-breaking for identical PageRank scores is unspecified and should be clarified or shown to be negligible.  
- Constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) impose upper bounds on performance; include oracle results per constraint to quantify their impact.  
- Minor typographical errors and inconsistencies (unnecessary definite articles, differing column orders in Table 5 vs. Table 7) should be corrected.
