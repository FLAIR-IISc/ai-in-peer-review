This paper proposes an unsupervised parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies (UD) based on PageRank and a small set of specific dependency head rules. The parser requires no training and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system.

**Main Contributions**

1. An unsupervised parsing algorithm for UD that leverages PageRank and a small set of dependency head rules.
2. A method for estimating adposition direction directly from test data.
3. A two-step decoding algorithm that ensures the leaf status of function words.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to unsupervised dependency parsing that is competitive with supervised methods.
2. The use of PageRank and personalized PageRank is a clever way to identify the main predicate and assign dependencies.
3. The evaluation is thorough, with a comparison to a strong baseline and an analysis of the parser's performance across different languages and domains.
4. The parser's robustness to domain change is a significant advantage over supervised methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. The increment over the work of Søgaard (2012) is relatively small, and the paper could benefit from more significant innovations.
2. The personalization factor is only used for the main predicate, and its weight of 5 seems arbitrary.
3. The evaluation could be improved with the inclusion of charts to help visualize the results.
4. The paper could benefit from an analysis of the correlation between POS tagging performance and parsing performance in the prediction mode.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the choice of the personalization factor and its weight?
2. How do you plan to extend the parser to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?
3. Can you provide an analysis of the correlation between POS tagging performance and parsing performance in the prediction mode?
4. How do you plan to improve the parser's performance on languages with non-standard word orders?
