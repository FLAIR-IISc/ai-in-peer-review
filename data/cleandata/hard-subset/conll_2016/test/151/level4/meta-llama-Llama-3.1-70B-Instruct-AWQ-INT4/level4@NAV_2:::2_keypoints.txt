- Proposes UDP: an unsupervised UD parsing algorithm using personalized PageRank and a small set of dependency head rules.  
- UDP requires no training data and competes with delexicalized transfer parsers.  

- Contribution: adapts dependency head rules to UD-compliant POS relations.  
- Contribution: enforces UD constraint that function words are leaves in the parse.  
- Contribution: uses personalized PageRank to improve main predicate identification.  
- Contribution: estimates adposition attachment direction on test data, removing language-specific parameters.  

- Strength: achieves high UAS across 32 languages, competitive with state-of-the-art delexicalized transfer.  
- Strength: robust to domain change and outperforms delexicalized transfer in some languages.  
- Strength: unsupervised, eliminating need for training data—beneficial for low-resource settings.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation including baseline and state-of-the-art comparisons and POS-wise analysis.  

- Weakness: heavy reliance on POS tag quality, which may be poor in low-resource languages.  
- Weakness: potential performance degradation on out-of-domain data or languages with unusual grammatical structures.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of time complexity for large-scale parsing.  

- Question: How will POS tag quality issues be addressed, especially for low-resource languages?  
- Question: Can the authors provide more insight into performance on languages with non-linear or unique word order?  
- Question: How will the algorithm be extended to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and other complex phenomena?
