This paper proposes a novel approach to parsing universal dependency treebanks, relying solely on part-of-speech (POS) tags and a modified version of the PageRank algorithm. The modification gives more weight to meaningful words, as opposed to stop words, which is an interesting idea. However, this concept bears a strong resemblance to the work presented in Søgaard (2012). While the incorporation of a personalization factor to emphasize the main predicate is a nice touch, it feels like an opportunity was missed to further develop this idea. Upon closer inspection, it appears that the personalization is only applied to the main predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 seems arbitrary.

The evaluation and detailed analyses presented in the paper would have greatly benefited from the inclusion of charts to supplement the tables. This would have facilitated a clearer understanding of the results, as the tables can be dense and difficult to interpret at times. Furthermore, it would have been enlightening to report the POS tagging scores in prediction mode, enabling an assessment of whether the decline in parsing performance is strongly correlated with the degradation in tagging performance, as one would intuitively expect.

In conclusion, while the paper presents an interesting approach, the incremental contribution over the work of Søgaard (2012) is relatively minor. With some further development and refinement, this idea could have been taken to the next level.
