This paper proposes a method for parsing universal dependency treebanks using only part-of-speech (POS) tags and a modified version of PageRank, which prioritizes meaningful words over stop words. Although the idea is interesting, it bears a strong resemblance to the work presented in Søgaard (2012). The incorporation of a personalization factor to emphasize the main predicate is a welcome addition, but its potential could be further explored. In its current form, the personalization appears to be solely applied to the main predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 seems arbitrary.

The evaluation and detailed analyses could be improved with the inclusion of charts, as the tables are sometimes difficult to interpret. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to report the POS tagging scores in prediction mode, allowing for an assessment of whether the decline in parsing performance is strongly correlated with the degradation in tagging performance, as would be expected.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach, but the incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is limited.
