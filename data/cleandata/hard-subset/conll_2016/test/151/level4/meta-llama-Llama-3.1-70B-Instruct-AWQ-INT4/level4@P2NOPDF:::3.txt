This paper proposes a novel approach to parsing universal dependency treebanks, relying solely on part-of-speech (POS) tags and a modified version of PageRank to emphasize meaningful words over stop words. While this idea is intriguing, it bears a strong resemblance to the work presented in Søgaard (2012). The introduction of a personalization factor, which assigns greater weight to the main predicate, is a welcome addition. However, its potential is not fully exploited, and the arbitrary weight of 5 assigned to the main predicate could be optimized.

The evaluation and detailed analyses would benefit from the inclusion of charts to supplement the tables, as the latter can be difficult to interpret at times. Furthermore, it would be enlightening to report the POS tagging scores in prediction mode, allowing for an assessment of the correlation between the degradation in parsing performance and the degradation in tagging performance, which is a expected outcome.

In conclusion, the paper presents an interesting approach, but the incremental contribution over Søgaard (2012) is limited.
