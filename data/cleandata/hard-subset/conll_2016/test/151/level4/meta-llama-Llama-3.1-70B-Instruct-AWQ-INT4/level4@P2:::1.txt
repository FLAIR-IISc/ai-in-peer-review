**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents UDP, an unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies (UD) that uses PageRank and a small set of specific dependency head rules. The parser requires no training and is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system. UDP is designed to be linguistically sound and robust to domain changes across languages.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm that uses personalized PageRank and a small set of head-dependent rules to achieve competitive performance on UD languages.
2. The use of a small set of rules that are adapted to UD-compliant POS relations, incorporating the UD restriction of function words being leaves.
3. The estimation of adposition attachment direction directly on test data, making the parser completely free of language-specific parameters.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a clear and well-motivated approach to unsupervised dependency parsing, leveraging the constraints of the UD formalism.
2. The use of personalized PageRank and a small set of head-dependent rules allows the parser to achieve competitive performance on UD languages.
3. The evaluation of the parser on 32 languages and three POS setups demonstrates its robustness and generalizability.
4. The comparison to a multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD) shows that UDP is less affected by deteriorating POS tags and more resilient to domain changes.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the head rules in relation to the corpus, which could help to understand their effectiveness.
2. The choice of using test data to determine head direction is surprising and may not be optimal.
3. The paper does not provide oracle results for each constraint, which could help to understand the upper bounds of possible performance.
4. The evaluation of the parser could be more comprehensive, including more languages and POS setups.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How did you select the head rules, and what was the process of adapting them to UD-compliant POS relations?
2. Can you provide more details on the estimation of adposition attachment direction directly on test data?
3. How does the parser handle cases where the head rules do not lead to a connected graph?
4. Can you provide more information on the tie-breaking mechanism for words with the same PageRank score?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the algorithm and its components.
* The use of tables and figures is helpful in illustrating the parser's performance and behavior.
* The comparison to Søgaard (2012) is useful in understanding the novelty of the approach.
