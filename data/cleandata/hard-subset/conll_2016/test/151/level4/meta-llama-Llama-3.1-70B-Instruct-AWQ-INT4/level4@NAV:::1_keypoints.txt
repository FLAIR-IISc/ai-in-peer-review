- Introduces a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm evaluated on multiple languages.  
- Algorithm uses POS-based head rules to define permissible dependencies.  
- Ranks words via a slightly biased PageRank on a graph whose edges are the permitted dependencies.  
- Iteratively links each word to the closest word that preserves a tree structure and satisfies head rules and directionality.  
- Paper is well-structured and engaging but appears similar to Sogaard (2012).  
- Head rules need deeper corpus analysis — frequency of disconnected graphs and typical component sizes are not reported.  
- Using test data to determine head direction is unexpected; authors should justify this choice despite rapid convergence.  
- Tie-breaking for identical PageRank scores is unspecified and its impact on performance should be assessed.  
- Head rules, directionality, and distance constraints impose upper bounds; provide oracle results per constraint to show maximum achievable performance.  
- Minor edits: rephrase “we obtain [the] rank” (Sect. 4.1), standardize column order in Tables 5 & 7, and rephrase “isolate the [contribution] of both” (Sect. 6.1).
