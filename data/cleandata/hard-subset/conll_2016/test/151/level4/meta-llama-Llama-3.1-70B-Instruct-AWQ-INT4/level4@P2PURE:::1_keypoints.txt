- Presents a deterministic dependency parsing algorithm evaluated on multiple languages.
- Core component: a set of POS-based head rules that define permitted dependencies.
- Words are ranked using a slightly biased PageRank over a graph whose edges come from permitted dependencies.
- Parsing links each word to the closest word that preserves a tree, respects head rules, and meets a directionality constraint.
- Paper is well-written and engaging, but contribution appears to be a minor extension of Søgaard (2012).
- Request to analyze head rules against the corpus: frequency of disconnected graphs and typical component sizes.
- Concern that head directionality was determined using test data rather than training/dev data; ask for justification.
- Question about PageRank tie-breaking: how ties are resolved and whether ties materially affect performance.
- Suggestion to report oracle results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show upper bounds on performance.
- Minor editorial suggestions: rephrase “we obtain [the] rank” (Sec. 4.1), make column orders consistent between Tables 5 and 7 (prefer Table 7 ordering), and rephrase “isolate the [contribution] of both” (Sec. 6.1).
