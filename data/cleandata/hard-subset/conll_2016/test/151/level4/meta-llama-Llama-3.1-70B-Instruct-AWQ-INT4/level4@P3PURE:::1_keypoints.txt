- Introduces a deterministic dependency parser using POS-based head rules, a biased PageRank to rank words, and linking to the nearest permitted head maintaining a tree and directionality constraint.  
- Paper is interesting and clearly presented but appears only slightly different from Søgaard (2012), requiring clearer distinction.  
- Request analysis of Head Rules (Table 1) against the corpus: how often they produce disconnected graphs and typical component sizes.  
- Surprised that head directionality was chosen on the test set rather than training/dev; decision converges quickly (10–15 sentences) but the choice is counterintuitive.  
- Ask for clarification on tie-breaking for equal PageRank scores and whether ties significantly impact performance or are rare.  
- Recommend reporting oracle results for each constraint (head rules, directionality, distance) to show upper bounds on achievable performance.  
- Minor issues: fix typos/phrasing ("we obtain [the] rank" in §4.1; "isolate the [contribution] of both" in §6.1) and harmonize column orders in Tables 5 and 7 (prefer Table 7 layout).
