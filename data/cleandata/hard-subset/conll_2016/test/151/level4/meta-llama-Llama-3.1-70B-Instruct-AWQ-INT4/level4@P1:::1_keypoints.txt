- Paper proposes a deterministic, rule-based dependency parser that ranks words via a slightly biased PageRank over permitted-dependency edges and links each word to the closest permitted head that preserves tree structure and directionality constraints.  
- Presentation is clear and the approach is interesting.  
- Method appears only slightly different from Søgaard (2012); authors’ novel contributions relative to that work are not clearly delineated.  
- Paper should include a more detailed comparison with Søgaard (2012).  
- Strength: competitive, training-free system across all Universal Dependencies languages.  
- Strength: use of personalized PageRank improves main predicate identification.  
- Strength: system shows robustness to domain change across languages.  
- Weakness: head rules (Table 1) need deeper analysis — frequency of application and typical component sizes produced.  
- Weakness: unclear why head-direction is estimated using test data rather than train/dev, and the decision-making process should be explained.  
- Weakness: constraints used by the algorithm need more detailed analysis and per-constraint oracle results to show maximum attainable scores.  
- Question: provide detailed analysis of Table 1 head rules and their application.  
- Question: justify the decision to estimate head direction using test data rather than training/development data.  
- Question: include oracle results for each constraint to guide future modifications.  
- Question: explain tie-breaking for identical PageRank scores and whether it materially affects performance.  
- Minor suggestions: rephrase "we obtain [the] rank" in §4.1; make Table 5 column order match the clearer arrangement in Table 7; rephrase "isolate the [contribution] of both" in §6.1.
