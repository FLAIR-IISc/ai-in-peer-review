- Paper proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank and a few hand-crafted head rules, requiring no training and competitive with delexicalized transfer.  
- Contribution: an unsupervised parsing algorithm that needs no annotated training data.  
- Contribution: uses PageRank scores plus a small set of hard-coded dependency head rules to build parses.  
- Contribution: demonstrates robustness to domain shifts across 32 evaluated languages.  
- Strength: substantial and novel contribution to unsupervised parsing with clear future directions.  
- Strength: well-written, detailed paper with clear explanation of injected prior information.  
- Strength: competitive performance compared to delexicalized transfer systems.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to 32 languages, leaving generalization to other languages unclear.  
- Weakness: reliance on a small set of hard-coded rules may not capture diverse language complexities.  
- Weakness: limited handling of multiword expressions and coordination structures.  
- Question: How will UDP be extended to handle multiword expressions and coordination?  
- Question: Can PageRank be expanded to directly score potential dependency edges—how would that work?  
- Question: How is UDP expected to perform on languages outside the UD project?
