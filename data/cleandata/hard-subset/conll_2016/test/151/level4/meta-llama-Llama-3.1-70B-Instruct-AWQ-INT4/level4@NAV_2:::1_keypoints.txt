- Novel unsupervised parser (UDP) using PageRank + a small set of dependency head rules, requiring no training data, evaluated on 32 languages versus delexicalized transfer.

- Contribution: a linguistically grounded unsupervised alternative to cross-lingual UD parsing competitive with delexicalized transfer.

- Contribution: a parser claimed to be robust to domain change across languages.

- Contribution: a new method to estimate adposition direction directly from test data.

- Strength: approach is well-motivated and clearly explained.

- Strength: thorough evaluation across 32 languages and comparison to a strong baseline.

- Strength: results show competitiveness with supervised transfer and greater domain robustness.

- Weakness: reliance on a small set of hand-crafted rules may limit generalizability to other languages/domains.

- Weakness: using PageRank to score content words may not be optimal; alternatives should be explored.

- Weakness: evaluation lacks detailed analysis on specific linguistic phenomena.

- Question: how were the specific dependency head rules selected and validated?

- Question: please provide more detail on the adposition direction estimation and comparison to using train/dev data.

- Question: how are tie-breaks handled for identical PageRank scores and does this affect performance?

- Question: have you considered alternative content-word scoring methods (ML models or other graph methods)?

- Question: can you provide detailed performance analysis on phenomena like verb-final order or complex morphology?
