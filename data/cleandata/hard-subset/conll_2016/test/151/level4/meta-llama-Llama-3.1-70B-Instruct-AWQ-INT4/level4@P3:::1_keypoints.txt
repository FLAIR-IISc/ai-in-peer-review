- Summary: Introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using a small set of head rules and personalized PageRank, requiring no training and evaluated on 32 languages in three POS-reliability setups; competitive with delexicalized transfer and stable across domains.

- Contribution 1: Adapts dependency head rules to UD-compliant POS relations for unsupervised parsing.

- Contribution 2: Enforces UD restriction that function words are leaves and uses personalized PageRank to improve main predicate identification.

- Contribution 3: Estimates adposition attachment direction on test data, making the parser free of language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Clear, well-structured description of the algorithm and components.

- Strength: Comprehensive multilingual evaluation across many languages and setups showing robustness.

- Strength: Competitive performance compared to a supervised cross-lingual (MSD) system.

- Weakness: Lacks in-depth analysis of how the head rules interact with corpus properties and affect performance.

- Weakness: Surprising choice to use test data to determine head direction; needs further justification.

- Weakness: No discussion of tie-breaking strategy for words with identical PageRank and its effect on performance.

- Question: How frequent and how large are disconnected graphs produced by the head rules?

- Question: How exactly does using test data for head-direction estimation affect parser performance, and are alternative methods being considered?

- Question: Can the authors provide oracle results for each constraint to show their individual impact on maximum achievable scores?

- Additional comment: Minor grammar and formatting errors throughout (e.g., missing "the" in Section 4.1).

- Additional comment: Inconsistent column ordering between Table 5 and Table 7; Table 7’s arrangement is clearer.

- Additional comment: Rephrase Section 6.1 (“isolate the [contribution] of both”) for clarity.
