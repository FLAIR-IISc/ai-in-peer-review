- Unsupervised UD parser using PageRank plus a small set of dependency head rules; no training and competitive with delexicalized transfer.
- Method for estimating adposition direction directly from test data.
- Two-step decoding that enforces function words (adpositions, etc.) to be leaves.

- Novel unsupervised approach that is competitive with supervised methods.
- Clever use of PageRank/personalized PageRank to identify main predicate and assign dependencies.
- Thorough evaluation against a strong baseline with language- and domain-level analyses.
- Demonstrated robustness to domain change compared to supervised methods.

- Incremental improvement over Søgaard (2012) is relatively small; limited novelty.
- Personalization is applied only to the main predicate and the chosen weight (5) seems arbitrary.
- Evaluation presentation could be improved with charts/visualizations.
- Lacks analysis of correlation between POS tagging quality and parsing performance in prediction mode.

- Clarify choice and justification for the personalization factor and its weight.
- How will the parser handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?
- Provide analysis of correlation between POS tagging performance and parsing in prediction mode.
- How will the approach be improved for languages with non-standard word order?
