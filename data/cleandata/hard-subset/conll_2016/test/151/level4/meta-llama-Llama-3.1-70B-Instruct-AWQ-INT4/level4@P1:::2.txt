The authors propose an unsupervised algorithm for Universal Dependencies that does not require training, leveraging PageRank for word ranking and a small set of hard-coded rules. 

The main contributions of this work are: 
1. An unsupervised dependency parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies that requires no training data.
2. A linguistically sound approach to cross-lingual UD parsing that is distinctly robust to domain change across languages.
3. A competitive system that outperforms a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser on one language and achieves close performance on all but two UD languages.

The strengths of this submission are:
1. The authors provide a thorough and detailed explanation of the intuition behind the algorithm, making it easy to follow and understand.
2. The evaluation setup is comprehensive, comparing the proposed system to multiple baselines and evaluating its performance across different POS setups and domains.
3. The results show that the proposed system is less affected by deteriorating POS tags and is more resilient to domain changes compared to a multi-source delexicalized parser.

The weaknesses of this submission are:
1. The system relies heavily on the quality of the POS tags, which may not always be accurate, especially for low-resource languages.
2. The use of hard-coded rules may not be generalizable to all languages, and the system may not perform well on languages with vastly different grammatical structures.
3. The evaluation metrics used are limited to unlabeled attachment score, and it would be beneficial to include other metrics, such as labeled attachment score or parsing accuracy, to provide a more comprehensive picture of the system's performance.

Questions to Authors:
1. How do the authors plan to extend the parser to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names, as mentioned in the future work section?
2. Can the authors provide more details on how the bigram heuristic for determining adposition direction is calculated, and how it affects the overall performance of the system?
3. How do the authors plan to address the issue of POS tag quality, especially for low-resource languages, and its impact on the system's performance?
