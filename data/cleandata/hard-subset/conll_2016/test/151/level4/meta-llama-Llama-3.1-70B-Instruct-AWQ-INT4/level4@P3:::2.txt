**Summary of the Paper**

The authors propose an unsupervised parsing algorithm for Universal Dependencies (UD) that does not require training. The algorithm, called UDP, uses PageRank to estimate the relevance of content words and a small set of specific dependency head rules. The parser is competitive with a delexicalized transfer system and offers a linguistically sound unsupervised alternative to cross-lingual UD parsing.

**Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel unsupervised parsing algorithm for UD that does not require training data, making it a significant contribution to the field of unsupervised parsing.
2. The use of PageRank to estimate the relevance of content words and the incorporation of a small set of specific dependency head rules provide a linguistically sound approach to cross-lingual parsing.
3. The authors evaluate their system on 32 languages and demonstrate its competitiveness with a delexicalized transfer system, making it a valuable contribution to the field of cross-lingual parsing.

**Strengths**

1. The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their algorithm and its components, making it easy to follow and understand.
2. The use of PageRank to estimate the relevance of content words is a novel approach that provides a linguistically sound way to identify the main predicate of a sentence.
3. The evaluation of the system on 32 languages demonstrates its robustness and competitiveness with a delexicalized transfer system.
4. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the system's performance, including a breakdown by POS and an evaluation of its robustness to domain change.

**Weaknesses**

1. The system relies on a small set of hard-coded rules, which may not be applicable to all languages or domains.
2. The use of PageRank may not be suitable for all types of sentences, such as those with multiple main predicates or complex sentence structures.
3. The system does not handle multiword expressions, coordination, or proper names, which may limit its applicability in certain domains.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend their system to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?
2. Can the authors provide more details on how they selected the specific dependency head rules used in their system?
3. How do the authors plan to evaluate their system on languages that do not have a UD treebank available?
4. Can the authors provide more details on how they calculated the error propagation rate from POS to dependency errors?
