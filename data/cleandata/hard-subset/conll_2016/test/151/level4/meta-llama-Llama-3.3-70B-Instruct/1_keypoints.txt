- Manuscript proposes a deterministic, rule-based dependency parser using a biased PageRank to rank words and attach heads while enforcing tree structure, head rules, and directionality.  
- Paper is engaging and well-structured but closely resembles Sogaard (2012) with only minor differences.  
- Head rules (Table 1) need deeper corpus analysis: report how often they produce disconnected graphs and typical component sizes.  
- Choosing head-directionality from test data is unexpected (rather than train/dev), even if it converges quickly (10–15 sentences).  
- Tie-breaking for identical PageRank scores is not described; clarify the mechanism and whether ties materially affect performance.  
- The imposed constraints (head rules, directionality, distance) cap achievable performance; provide oracle results per constraint to show upper bounds and guide improvements.  
- Minor edits: rephrase “we obtain [the] rank” in Section 4.1, make Table 5/7 column ordering consistent (Table 7 ordering preferred), and reword “isolate the [contribution] of both” in Section 6.1.
