This paper introduces a method for parsing dependency trees (specifically, universal dependency treebanks) that leverages part-of-speech tags and a modified PageRank algorithm, weighting meaningful words more heavily than stop words. The core concept is intriguing, though closely related to previous work by Søgaard (2012). The incorporation of a personalization factor to emphasize the main predicate is a positive step, but could benefit from further development. Specifically, the personalization appears limited to only the main predicate, and the assigned weight of 5 feels empirically determined rather than theoretically justified.

The evaluation and detailed analyses are presented clearly, but would be enhanced by the inclusion of illustrative charts to facilitate quicker comprehension of the results presented in the tables. Furthermore, reporting the POS tagging accuracy in a predictive setting would be valuable. This would allow assessment of the extent to which any observed decrease in parsing performance correlates with a decrease in tagging performance, as anticipated. 

Overall, the paper presents an interesting contribution, but the advancement over Søgaard (2012) is incremental.
