This paper introduces a novel deterministic dependency parsing algorithm and provides an analysis of its performance across multiple languages. The algorithm centers around a rule-based system defining allowable dependencies based on part-of-speech tags. It initiates by ranking words using a modified PageRank algorithm applied to a graph constructed from these permitted dependencies.  The parsing process then iteratively links each word to its nearest compatible head, ensuring a tree structure is maintained and adhering to defined head rules and directionality constraints. The paper is well-written and presents an interesting approach, although the advancements appear incremental when compared to Sogaard (2012), "Unsupervised Dependency Parsing without Training". 

I have several questions and suggestions for improvement:

Regarding the Head Rules (Table 1), a more detailed analysis relating these rules to the characteristics of the corpora used would be beneficial. While section 3.1 notes the potential for disconnected graphs, quantifying the frequency of this occurrence and the typical size of resulting components would provide valuable insight. The choice of using test data for determining head direction is somewhat unexpected; while the rapid convergence (10-15 sentences) mitigates this concern, the rationale behind this decision warrants further explanation.  Clarification on the tie-breaking mechanism for words with equal PageRank scores would be helpful. Assessing the impact of this mechanism on overall performance, or demonstrating its infrequent occurrence, would strengthen the analysis.  

The imposed constraints – head rules, directionality, and distance – inherently limit the system’s potential performance. Including oracle results for each constraint, demonstrating the performance degradation caused by each, would be highly informative. This would be particularly useful in guiding future research efforts by highlighting areas where modifications could yield the greatest improvements.

Minor points:

- In section 4.1, consider revising “we obtain [the] rank” for improved readability.
- Tables 5 and 7 currently present columns in a different order. I found the arrangement in Table 7 to be more intuitive.
- In section 6.1, suggest changing “isolate the [contribution] of both” for clarity.
