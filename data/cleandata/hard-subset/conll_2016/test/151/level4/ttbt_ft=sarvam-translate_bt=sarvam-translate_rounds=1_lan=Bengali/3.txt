This paper presents a way to parse trees (i.e., universal dependency tree banks) using a modified version of PageRank, relying solely on POS (Part of Speech), so that some meaningful words (as opposed to stop words) are given more importance. The idea is interesting, although it is very similar to what was done in Søgaard's (2012) paper. While it's good to give more importance to the main predicate, it could be improved further. As far as I know, personalization is only used for the main predicate, and its weight is 5, which seems intentional.

In terms of evaluation and detailed analysis, some charts would be beneficial, as it can sometimes be difficult to extract the main point from the table. Finally, it would be good to get POS tagging scores in prediction mode, to see if the decline in parsing performance is closely related to the decline in tagging performance (which we hope for).

Overall, the paper is interesting, but its improvement is slight compared to Søgaard's (2012) work.
