This paper presents a method for parsing trees (i.e., universal dependency trees) using a modified version of PageRank, relying solely on POS (Part of Speech), to give more importance to certain meaningful words (as opposed to stop words). The idea is interesting, although it is very similar to the work done in Søgaard's (2012) paper. The personalization factor, which gives more importance to the main predicate, is good, but it would be better to take it to the next level. As far as I can tell, personalization is only used for the main predicate, and its weight seems arbitrary at 5.

Regarding evaluation and detailed analysis, some charts would be beneficial, as it is sometimes difficult to extract the essence from tables. Finally, it would be interesting to obtain POS tagging scores in prediction mode to see if a decline in parsing performance is highly correlated with a decline in tagging performance (which we would expect).

Overall, the paper is interesting but falls short of Søgaard's (2012) work.
