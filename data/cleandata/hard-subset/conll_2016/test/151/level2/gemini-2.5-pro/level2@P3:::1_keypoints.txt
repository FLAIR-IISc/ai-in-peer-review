- Paper presents UDP: a rule-based, unsupervised UD parser using personalized PageRank to rank content words and a two-step decoding; only language-specific parameter (adposition direction) is estimated on-the-fly.

- Contribution: a simple, effective zero-shot parser that combines UD content/function distinctions with PageRank, establishing a strong training-free baseline.

- Contribution: extensive cross-lingual and cross-domain evaluation (32 languages) showing UDP is more robust to domain shift than a delexicalized transfer parser.

- Strength: simplicity and interpretability—small POS-based rules and transparent decoding make behavior and errors easy to analyze.

- Strength: strong empirical performance—57.5 UAS on gold POS across 32 languages, close to delexicalized transfer (63.9) and outperforming it on some languages (e.g., Hindi).

- Strength: demonstrated robustness—much lower performance variance across domains compared to the delexicalized baseline.

- Strength: effective use of UD framework—leverages content/function word distinction and a two-step decoding aligned with UD principles.

- Weakness: potential English-centric bias—head rules and fixed function-word directions were developed on English dev data, risking reduced language-independence.

- Weakness: inability to model certain valid syntactic structures (e.g., relative clauses, likely coordination) due to rule constraints; lacks detailed cross-linguistic error analysis.

- Weakness: comparisons to baselines are incomplete—missing direct quantitative comparisons to prior and more recent unsupervised/data-driven baselines.

- Question: how well does the PageRank personalization heuristic (boost first verb/first content word) work for typologically different word orders (VSO, SOV)?

- Question: how sensitive/stable is the on-the-fly adposition direction estimation to the size of test data, especially for languages with very small test sets?
