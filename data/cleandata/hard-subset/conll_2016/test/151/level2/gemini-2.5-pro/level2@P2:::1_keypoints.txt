- UDP is a fully unsupervised UD parser using a small set of POS-based head rules, personalized PageRank to rank content words (biasing the first verb), a two-step decoder (content backbone then function-word leaves), and on-the-fly adposition-direction estimation.
- Contribution: Proposes a simple, effective, linguistically-informed unsupervised parser that is surprisingly competitive with a supervised delexicalized multi-source parser.
- Contribution: Provides an extensive cross-lingual and cross-domain evaluation showing robustness and lower sensitivity to POS tagging errors and domain shift.
- Strength: Simplicity and high performance — few rules + PageRank + decoding achieve strong UAS (only ~6.4% behind the supervised baseline on gold POS, outperforming on Hindi).
- Strength: Extensive, rigorous evaluation across 32 languages and under gold/predicted POS settings, well contextualized against a competitive baseline.
- Strength: Demonstrated robustness to domain shift — consistently lower UAS variance than the data-driven MSD parser.
- Strength: Truly training-free and language-agnostic design — on-the-fly adposition direction estimation and only a POS tagger required.
- Weakness: Very limited head-rule set restricts linguistic coverage (e.g., no NOUN->VERB prevents producing relative-clause analyses), likely contributing to the performance gap.
- Weakness: Key design choices (head rules, PageRank factor, fixed function-word attachments) were tuned on English dev data, undermining the claim of no language-specific parameters and raising typological sensitivity concerns.
- Weakness: Decoding tie-breaking is unspecified (how argmin linear distance ties are resolved), hurting full reproducibility.
- Question: Do fixed function-word attachment directions (e.g., AUX, DET) generalize typologically, and could on-the-fly estimation be extended beyond adpositions?
- Question: What was the empirical trade-off of excluding rules like NOUN->VERB for relative clauses — did adding them help verbs but harm overall accuracy via spurious attachments?
