- Method: UDP is a fully unsupervised UD parser using a small set of hand-crafted head-selection rules combined with personalized PageRank and a two-step decoding (content-word parse then attach function words).
- Contribution 1: A simple, training-free parsing method that is surprisingly effective and demonstrates the value of exploiting formalism constraints.
- Contribution 2: Thorough empirical evaluation across 32 languages showing competitiveness with supervised delexicalized transfer and greater robustness to domain shifts.
- Strength 1: Strong performance for an unsupervised system—57.5 UAS with gold POS across 32 languages, closing much of the gap to a delexicalized baseline (63.9 UAS).
- Strength 2: Demonstrated robustness—much lower variance across genres in cross-domain evaluation, making it more reliable for out-of-domain text.
- Strength 3: Simplicity and reproducibility—clearly described, easy to implement, and a useful baseline for low-resource/unsupervised parsing.
- Weakness 1: Fixed hand-crafted rule set limits expressivity and cannot capture phenomena like relative clauses, placing a hard ceiling on accuracy.
- Weakness 2: Heavy dependence on input POS tag quality; performance drops substantially under naive content/function tagging and offers no mechanism for POS ambiguity or errors.
- Weakness 3: Head rules and PageRank parameters tuned on English dev set, introducing potential Anglocentric bias and uncertainty about dev-language influence on cross-lingual performance.
