- Summary: UDP is an unsupervised UD-based dependency parser needing no training data, using personalized PageRank for content-word ranking and a two-step decoder to make function words leaves; evaluated on 32 languages with competitive, robust results.

- Contribution: Proposes a linguistically grounded unsupervised parser that fully leverages UD constraints without language-specific parameters.

- Contribution: Presents a method to estimate adposition direction directly from test data without language-specific parameters.

- Contribution: Introduces a two-step decoding algorithm that enforces UD-style handling of content vs. function words.

- Strength: Achieves strong cross-lingual performance (UDPG with gold POS reaches ~92% of supervised MSDG on average across 32 languages).

- Strength: Shows greater cross-domain robustness than supervised baselines (e.g., lower UAS std dev: 1.8 vs 4.5).

- Strength: Tolerant to predicted POS tags with small performance drop (2.2% vs MSD’s 2.7%) and quantifies POS error propagation (~0.37 dependency errors per POS error).

- Weakness: Fails to sufficiently address adaptation to languages with syntactic structures very different from the development language (e.g., verb-final languages).

- Weakness: Claim of being free of language-specific parameters is weakened by the need for 10–15 test sentences to estimate adposition direction, with no fallback for extremely low-resource settings.

- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of which head rules drive performance across language families and how rules were selected.

- Question: How does the system handle complex morphological phenomena (e.g., agglutinative languages where morphemes affect dependency relations)?

- Question: How does excluding the NOUN→VERB head rule (preventing inference of relative clauses) affect performance in languages with frequent relative clauses, and can this rule be reintroduced selectively?

- Question: How would the system perform under the updated UD v2.0 formalism with its expanded set of dependency relations?
