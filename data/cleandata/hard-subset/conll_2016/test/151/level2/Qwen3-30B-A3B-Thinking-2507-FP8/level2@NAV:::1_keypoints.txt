Introduces UDP: an unsupervised UD dependency parser that requires no training data and uses only UD-specific constraints.

Main claims: competitive with supervised delexicalized transfer, robust to cross-domain shifts, and novel use of UD linguistic constraints.

Primary contribution: adapts dependency head rules to UD formalism, notably the leaf status constraint for function words.

Secondary contributions: uses personalized PageRank to find main predicates and estimates adposition direction on-the-fly from test data.

Experiments: extensive evaluation across 32 languages and three POS scenarios to support claims.

Empirical result: UDP is within 6.4% UAS of a supervised baseline on average.

Robustness: shows lower cross-domain variance (std 2.5 vs 4.2) and stable performance across language-specific domains.

Linguistic grounding: approach leverages UD formal constraints rather than relying on data-driven heuristics.

Weakness: missing analysis for why UDP outperforms the supervised baseline on Hindi (the sole language it beats supervision).

Weakness: high sensitivity to POS quality—naive POS scenario (UDPN) drops to 29.9% average UAS.

Weakness: very low punctuation performance (21% UAS) not adequately analyzed or addressed.

Positioning: paper is well-situated in related work with comprehensive discussion.

Requested clarifications: detail the personalization strategy for non-verb-final languages and how UDP handles cases where the first verb isn't the main predicate.

Requested clarifications: explain specific linguistic properties causing poor punctuation performance and potential remedies.
