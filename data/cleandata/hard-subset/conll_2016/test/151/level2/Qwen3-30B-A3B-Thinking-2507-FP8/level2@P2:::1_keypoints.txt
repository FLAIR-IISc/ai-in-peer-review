- Presents UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using only UD constraints (function words as leaves, content words as heads).  
- Main contributions: linguistically-motivated unsupervised parser, strong empirical performance vs baselines/MSD, and robustness to domain shifts.  

Strengths:  
- Leverages UD-specific constraints (function/content word roles) novelly rather than generic unsupervised methods.  
- Comprehensive evaluation on 32 languages and three POS setups; competitive with MSD (≈6.4% UAS gap) and outperforms MSD on Hindi.  
- Demonstrates domain robustness with significantly lower cross-domain std. dev. than MSD (Table 6).  
- Uses personalized PageRank to identify main predicate, yielding a ~9 UAS point improvement over non-PR variant.  

Weaknesses:  
- Claim of "no training" undermined by tuning on English treebank for head rules and PageRank parameters.  
- Lacks thorough comparison to other unsupervised parsers; unclear contribution of UD constraints vs PageRank.  
- Insufficient error analysis, e.g., impact of excluding NOUN→VERB head rule (affects relative clause parsing) not quantified.  
- Domain-robustness claim is based on limited languages/domains (Bulgarian, Croatian, Italian); evidence not comprehensive.  
- Claim of being free of language-specific parameters contradicted by adposition-direction estimation that requires language-specific tuning on test data.  

Questions for authors:  
- How much development data (sentences/languages) is required in practice, and what precisely do you mean by "no training"?  
- Why does UDP outperform MSD on Hindi—what linguistic or dataset factors explain this case?  
- What is the quantitative UAS impact across languages of excluding the NOUN→VERB head rule?
