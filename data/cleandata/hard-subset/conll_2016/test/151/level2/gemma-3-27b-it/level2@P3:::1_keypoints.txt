- Paper introduces UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank + dependency head rules that claims competitive performance without training and robustness to domain change.

- Primary contribution: a fully unsupervised parsing approach leveraging UD constraints via adapted head rules, function-word restrictions, personalized PageRank, and test-time estimation of adposition attachment direction.

- Secondary contribution: extensive evaluation across 32 languages, domain-sensitivity analysis, and release of a public parser plus annotated test sets.

- Strength: Achieves strong UAS performance without training, often approaching supervised systems and benchmarked against a multi-source delexicalized transfer (MSD) baseline.

- Strength: Shows robustness to domain change with lower performance variance across domains compared to the supervised MSD baseline (supported by Table 6).

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including multiple POS scenarios (gold/predicted/naive) and additional English datasets, providing insight into input-sensitivity.

- Weakness: Core techniques (PageRank, head rules) are not novel; novelty mainly in their UD-specific adaptation and application.

- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative error analysis beyond domain shifts; lacks identification of linguistic phenomena the parser struggles with (e.g., long-distance dependencies, non-projectivity, coordination).

- Weakness: Presentation issues—Table 5 is dense and could better highlight statistical significance and clarify “R”/“L” backoff direction labels.

- Question: Please elaborate linguistic motivations for the dependency head rules (Table 1) and note any rules that were especially effective or problematic.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to the PageRank personalization weight (5× for estimated main predicate) and was a broader hyperparameter search performed?

- Question: How does relying on the first verb/content word as the main predicate affect performance in languages with non-canonical or verb-final word order?

- Comment: Include a visual parsed-sentence example to illustrate head-rule application and PageRank behavior.

- Comment: Define abbreviations (UAS, MSD, PR) at first occurrence.

- Comment: Provide explicit links to code and data in the final paper.
