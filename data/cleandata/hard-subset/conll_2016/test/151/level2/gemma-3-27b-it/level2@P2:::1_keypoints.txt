- Proposal: UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using personalized PageRank and UD-based head rules to avoid any training.  
- Main claim: A reasonably accurate parser can be built without training by leveraging UD constraints and a careful algorithm.  
- Contributions claimed: adapting head rules to UD, adding a function-word constraint, using personalized PageRank for predicate ID, and eliminating language-specific parameters.  

Strengths:
- Novelty: Fully training-free dependency parsing is an uncommon and significant departure from standard approaches.  
- Robustness: UDP shows greater resilience to domain shift than a delexicalized transfer baseline, important for low-resource settings.  
- Evaluation breadth: Thorough experiments across 32 languages plus domain adaptation and predicted POS settings.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Method description (personalized PageRank and two-step decoding) is clear; code and data are available.  
- Baseline comparison: Competitive results against a delexicalized transfer system, sometimes outperforming it.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited error analysis: Lacks detailed breakdown of error types and systematic failure modes.  
- Reliance on gold POS: Primary results use gold POS tags; performance degrades with predicted POS, showing sensitivity to tagging quality.  
- Rule engineering: Head rules require manual selection/engineering, weakening the claim of no language-specific parameters and unclear replicability.  
- Simplified metrics: Evaluation relies mainly on UAS; additional metrics or qualitative analyses would give a fuller picture.  
- Limited unsupervised comparisons: Insufficient comparison to other unsupervised parsing methods beyond DMV.  

Questions for authors:
- Provide a detailed error analysis highlighting common UDP errors.  
- How were head rules selected and can the process be automated or made more data-driven?  
- Any methods explored to improve robustness to noisy POS tags (e.g., POS confidence integration)?  
- How sensitive is UDP to the PageRank teleport probability and why was 0.05 chosen?  
- What is the computational cost and parsing time compared to the delexicalized transfer system?  

Additional comments:
- Reference list is extensive and appropriate.  
- Figure 1 notation needs clarification (define γ, δ, κ explicitly).  
- Explain the personalization vector choice (why a factor of 5 and whether it was tuned).
