## Review of “Parsing for Universal Dependencies without training”

This paper presents UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser for Universal Dependencies (UD) that leverages PageRank and a set of dependency head rules, requiring no training data. The core idea – exploiting the constraints inherent in the UD formalism for cross-lingual parsing – is promising and well-motivated. The authors claim contributions in adapting head rules to UD, incorporating the function word restriction, using personalized PageRank, and achieving language-agnostic adposition attachment. 

The strongest contribution is the demonstrated performance of UDP. The parser achieves competitive UAS scores across 32 languages, comparing favorably to a delexicalized transfer system, and exhibits greater robustness to domain change.  The comprehensive evaluation, spanning multiple languages and domains, is a significant strength, providing compelling evidence for the method’s effectiveness. The detailed ablation study (Section 6) convincingly demonstrates the contribution of PageRank and the head rules. Finally, releasing both the parser and annotated datasets is highly valuable for the community.

However, the novelty of the approach is somewhat limited. While the combination of techniques is effective, each component (PageRank, rule-based parsing, UD constraints) has been explored individually in prior work. The personalized PageRank, while providing a performance boost, isn’t a fundamentally new algorithmic contribution.  A key weakness is the lack of in-depth error analysis. While the authors touch on difficulties with punctuation and coordination, a more detailed investigation into common error patterns would provide valuable insights for future improvements. Additionally, the reliance on the English treebank for parameter tuning (PageRank, function word direction) introduces a potential bias, despite the authors' attempt to mitigate this. The paper would benefit from exploring alternative tuning strategies or demonstrating robustness to different tuning datasets.  Finally, the presentation could be streamlined; some sections feel repetitive, and the detailed trace of the parsing example (Section 4) feels excessively granular.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the rationale behind choosing the specific head rules in Table 1?  Was there an iterative refinement process, and if so, what criteria were used?
2.  How sensitive is the performance of UDP to the choice of the teleport probability in PageRank? Did you perform a more extensive parameter search?
3.  The paper mentions the limitation regarding relative clauses. Do you have plans to address this limitation in future work?
