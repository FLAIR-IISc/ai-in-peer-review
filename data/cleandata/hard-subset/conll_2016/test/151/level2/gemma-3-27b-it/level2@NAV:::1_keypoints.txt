- Presents UDP: an unsupervised UD parser using PageRank and dependency head rules with no training data.  
- Core idea: exploit UD formalism constraints for cross-lingual parsing — promising and well-motivated.  
- Claimed contributions: adapting head rules to UD, incorporating a function-word restriction, using personalized PageRank, and language-agnostic adposition attachment.  
- Strongest result: competitive UAS across 32 languages and greater robustness to domain changes.  
- Comprehensive multilingual and cross-domain evaluation is a significant strength.  
- Ablation study convincingly demonstrates the contributions of PageRank and the head rules.  
- Releasing the parser and annotated datasets is valuable for the community.  
- Novelty is limited since PageRank, rule-based parsing, and UD constraints have been explored previously.  
- Personalized PageRank yields gains but is not a fundamentally new algorithmic contribution.  
- Lacks in-depth error analysis; only brief mentions of issues with punctuation and coordination.  
- Reliance on the English treebank for parameter tuning may introduce bias; alternative tuning/robustness checks are needed.  
- Presentation could be streamlined; some sections are repetitive and the detailed parsing trace (Section 4) is overly granular.  
- Questions raised: rationale and refinement process for the specific head rules in Table 1.  
- Questions raised: sensitivity to the PageRank teleport probability and extent of parameter search.  
- Questions raised: any plans to address limitations with relative clauses in future work.
