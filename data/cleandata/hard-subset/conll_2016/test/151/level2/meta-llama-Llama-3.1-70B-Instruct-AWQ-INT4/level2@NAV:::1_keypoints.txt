- Paper proposes UDP: an unsupervised dependency parser using PageRank and a small set of head rules, requiring no training.
- UDP is designed to be compatible with the Universal Dependencies (UD) framework for cross-lingual consistency.
- Evaluated on 32 languages and shown to be competitive with a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser.
- Contribution: a novel unsupervised parsing algorithm combining PageRank with specific dependency head rules.
- Contribution: explicit design for UD-compliant parsing across languages.
- Contribution: empirical demonstration of competitive performance on a large multilingual benchmark.
- Strength: novel training-free approach to dependency parsing.
- Strength: UD-compatible design facilitating cross-lingual application.
- Strength: extensive multilingual evaluation covering 32 languages.
- Strength: demonstrated robustness to domain change versus delexicalized transfer.
- Weakness: no handling of multiword expressions (MWEs).
- Weakness: lacks coordination handling.
- Weakness: only supports unlabeled attachments; no partial edge labeling.
- Question: How will UDP be extended to handle MWEs and coordination?
- Question: How does UDP perform on languages with complex morphology (e.g., Arabic, Hebrew)?
- Question: What are plans to improve handling of punctuation and conjunctions?
- Additional: Well-written paper with thorough evaluation and promising robustness, but limited by MWE/coordination and labeling shortcomings.
