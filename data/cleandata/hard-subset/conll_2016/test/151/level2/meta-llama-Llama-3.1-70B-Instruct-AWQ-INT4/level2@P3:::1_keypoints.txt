- Summary: Proposes UDP, an unsupervised UD parser using a few dependency head rules and personalized PageRank to rank content words; no training data and competitive with delexicalized transfer, robust across domains.

- Contribution 1: Introduces an unsupervised dependency parsing approach leveraging UD constraints.

- Contribution 2: Applies personalized PageRank to improve main predicate identification.

- Contribution 3: Uses a simple, linguistically motivated rule-based method for cross-lingual parsing.

- Strength 1: Competitive performance on most UD languages, outperforming a delexicalized parser on Hindi.

- Strength 2: More robust than supervised parsers to deteriorating POS tags and domain shifts.

- Strength 3: Unsupervised design needs no training data and can be extended for MWEs, coordination, and names.

- Weakness 1: Reliance on a small, language-specific set of head rules may not generalize across languages/domains.

- Weakness 2: Personalized PageRank (and fixed teleport probability 0.05) may be suboptimal for some languages.

- Weakness 3: Poor handling of punctuation, reflected in low UAS for PUNCT.

- Question 1: How will the parser be extended to handle multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names?

- Question 2: Have the authors considered alternative methods (e.g., edge reification) to directly score dependency edges?

- Question 3: What are the plans to improve performance on punctuation and other problematic POS tags?

- Additional comment: The approach is novel and promising but needs improvements for punctuation, problematic POS tags, and support for MWEs/coordination/names.
