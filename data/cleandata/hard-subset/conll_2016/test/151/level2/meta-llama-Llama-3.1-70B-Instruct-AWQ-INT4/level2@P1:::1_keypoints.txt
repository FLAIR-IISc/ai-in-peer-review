- Paper presents UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser for UD using personalized PageRank and a small set of head-dependent rules, requiring no training and estimating adposition direction from test data.  
- Contribution: Unsupervised parsing without training data, relying only on UD POS constraints.  
- Contribution: Uses personalized PageRank to rank content words, improving robustness and performance across domains.  
- Contribution: Employs a small, UD-adapted set of head-dependent rules for linguistically informed parsing.  
- Strength: Competitive performance on most of 32 evaluated UD languages and outperforms a multi-source delexicalized parser on Hindi.  
- Strength: More robust to domain changes than the baseline, with lower performance variance across domains.  
- Strength: Designed to satisfy general UD syntactic properties, making it linguistically sound.  
- Weakness: Strong dependence on accurate POS tags; performance drops with predicted tags.  
- Weakness: Simplistic treatment of function words may be insufficient for many languages.  
- Weakness: Does not handle multiword expressions, causing parsing errors.  
- Question: How will the parser be extended to handle multiword expressions and coordination?  
- Question: Please provide details on the development set used to tune PageRank parameters and function-word directionality.  
- Question: How might parser performance change with a more sophisticated approach to handling function words?
