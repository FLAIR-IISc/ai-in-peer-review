- Adapts dependency head rules to UD POS, enforces UD function-word-as-leaf constraint, and uses personalized PageRank for main predicate identification.
- Estimates adposition attachment direction on test data, making the parser free of language-specific parameters.
- Competitive performance on most UD languages and outperforms a multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD) on Hindi.

- Novel, linguistically sound unsupervised dependency parsing approach.
- Thorough evaluation on 32 languages with comparison to a supervised cross-lingual system (MSD).
- Detailed error analysis illuminating strengths and weaknesses.

- Heavy reliance on POS tag quality, problematic for low-resource languages.
- Performance drops significantly with predicted POS tags, limiting real-world applicability.
- Missing detailed comparison with other unsupervised dependency parsing systems, making impact assessment difficult.

- How will authors address POS tag quality as a major bottleneck?
- Why does the system perform poorly on Tamil and why is it an outlier?
- How will the approach be extended to handle multi-word expressions, coordination, and proper names?

- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of approach and results.
- Use of personalized PageRank plus dependency head rules is innovative.
- Evaluation results are promising, with particularly strong performance on Hindi.
- Releasing the parser and in-domain annotated test sets is commendable and useful for future research.
