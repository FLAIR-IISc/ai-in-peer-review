- Summary: UDP is an unsupervised dependency parser using personalized PageRank plus a small set of head-dependent rules, requiring no training and estimating adposition direction from test data; evaluated on 32 languages and shown competitive with (and sometimes better than) a multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD).
- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel unsupervised parsing approach combining personalized PageRank with head-dependent rules to parse without training data.
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that UDP achieves competitive performance with MSD across most evaluated languages and outperforms it on some.
- Contribution 3: Shows UDP is more robust to domain changes than MSD, with lower performance variance across domains.
- Strength 1: Presents a novel and conceptually simple method that avoids supervised training.
- Strength 2: Empirically competitive results across a multilingual evaluation.
- Strength 3: Greater resilience to domain shift compared to MSD.
- Weakness 1: Evaluation limited to 32 languages, leaving generalization to other languages uncertain.
- Weakness 2: Uses a small/simplistic set of head-dependent rules that may not capture complex linguistic phenomena.
- Weakness 3: Does not address handling of multiword expressions, an important parsing challenge.
- Question 1: How will UDP be extended to handle multiword expressions and other complex linguistic structures?
- Question 2: How were the head-dependent rules selected—what was the selection process and rationale?
- Question 3: What specific improvements do the authors foresee to boost UDP’s performance on languages where it underperforms?
