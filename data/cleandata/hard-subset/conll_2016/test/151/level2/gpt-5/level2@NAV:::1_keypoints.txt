- Summary: UDP is a training-free UD parser combining UD-compliant head rules, personalized PageRank, and two-step decoding with test-time adposition direction estimation; evaluated on 32 languages and multiple domains, showing UAS near multi-source delexicalized transfer and better domain robustness.

- Contribution — baseline: Presents a strong, language-agnostic unsupervised UD parsing baseline that approaches delexicalized transfer performance and is robust across domains.

- Contribution — methodology: Introduces refinements over prior rule/PR-based parsing—personalized PageRank for predicate identification and two-step decoding to enforce UD function-word leafness.

- Contribution — heuristics: Proposes a simple test-time heuristic to infer adposition attachment direction from test data, avoiding language-specific parameters.

- Strength: Evaluation breadth and rigor: extensive results on 32 languages, gold vs predicted POS, ablations, and cross-domain analysis with reported variance.

- Strength: Empirical support: Ablations show PR adds ~9 UAS and two-step decoding+rules add ~4 UAS; root accuracy and POS-wise breakdowns provide behavioral insight.

- Strength: Practical usefulness: No training and only POS tags required, making UDP a dependable baseline for low-/zero-resource and rapid cross-lingual deployment.

- Strength: UD-aware design: Enforcing function-word leafness and head-direction estimation produces linguistically plausible UD structures.

- Weakness: Limited novelty: Largely builds on Søgaard (2012); personalization and UD-specific tweaks are incremental rather than conceptual advances.

- Weakness: POS and UD sensitivity: Performance falls with coarse POS; DET direction exceptions (e.g., Basque) and poor PUNCT/CONJ handling indicate brittleness.

- Weakness: Incomplete modeling: Coordination, relative clauses (NOUN→VERB disabled), MWEs not handled; only unlabeled parsing reported.

- Weakness: Comparison scope: Needs a UD reproduction of Søgaard (2012) and stronger projection baselines to better contextualize gains.

- Weakness: Limited sensitivity analysis: Teleport and personalization weights tuned on English only; broader robustness checks across languages not reported.

- Question: Report UAS sensitivity to teleport probability and personalization weight across typologically diverse languages?

- Question: How often does the ADP direction heuristic fail, and could similar data-driven estimation be applied to DET/AUX/SCONJ?

- Question: Can you add a UD reproduction of Søgaard (2012) to contextualize gains from personalization and two-step decoding?

- Question: What is the impact of enabling NOUN→VERB (to capture relatives) on overall UAS and clause-attachment errors?
