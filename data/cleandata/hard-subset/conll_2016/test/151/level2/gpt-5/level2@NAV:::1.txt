Summary
The paper presents UDP, a training-free dependency parser for Universal Dependencies that combines a small set of UD-compliant head rules with personalized PageRank and a two-step decoding procedure that enforces function-word leafness. It estimates adposition attachment direction directly from test data. UDP is evaluated on 32 languages under gold and predicted POS settings, and on multiple domains, showing UAS close to multi-source delexicalized transfer, with improved robustness to domain shift.

Main contributions (as I see them)
1) A strong, language-agnostic unsupervised baseline for UD parsing that approaches delexicalized transfer performance and is robust across domains.
2) Methodological refinements over prior rule/PR-based unsupervised parsing: personalized PR for predicate identification and two-step decoding to enforce UD leaf constraints.
3) A simple, test-time heuristic to infer adposition directionality, removing language-specific parameters.

Strengths
- Breadth and rigor of evaluation: Results on 32 languages with both gold and predicted POS, ablations (UDP vs. UDPNoPR vs. baseline), and a cross-domain analysis with reported variance convincingly support the claims of competitiveness and robustness.
- Clear empirical support for architectural choices: Ablation shows PR contributes ~9 UAS and two-step decoding + rules add ~4 UAS over the baseline; root accuracy and POS-wise breakdown give insight into behavior.
- Practical usefulness: With no training and only POS tags, UDP offers a dependable baseline for low-resource/zero-resource scenarios and for rapid cross-lingual deployment.
- UD-aware design: The enforcement of function-word leafness and head-direction estimation aligns well with UD conventions, yielding linguistically plausible structures.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty: The approach substantially builds on Søgaard (2012) PR-based unsupervised parsing; the main additions (personalization, UD-specific rules, two-step decoding) are incremental rather than conceptual leaps.
- Dependence on POS quality and UD idiosyncrasies: Performance degrades with coarse POS; DET direction exceptions (e.g., Basque) and poor PUNCT/CONJ handling suggest brittle spots.
- Incomplete modeling: No treatment of coordination, relative clauses (explicitly disabled NOUN→VERB), or MWEs; only unlabeled parsing is reported.
- Comparison scope: While MSD is a reasonable baseline, a direct replication of Søgaard (2012) on UD and stronger projection baselines would solidify the competitiveness claim.
- Limited sensitivity analysis: Teleport and personalization weights are tuned on English; broader robustness checks across languages are not reported.

Questions to Authors
- Can you report sensitivity of UAS to the teleport probability and personalization weight across several typologically diverse languages?
- How often does the ADP direction heuristic fail, and could analogous data-driven estimation be applied to DET/AUX/SCONJ?
- Can you add a UD reproduction of Søgaard (2012) to contextualize gains from personalization and two-step decoding?
- What is the impact of enabling NOUN→VERB (to capture relatives) on overall UAS and clause-attachment errors?
