- Paper presents UDP: a rule-driven, training-free UD parser using a dependency-informed graph, personalized PageRank to rank content words, and two-pass decoding (content first, function words as leaves), requiring only POS tags.
- Key contribution 1: a simple UD-aware unsupervised algorithm combining personalized PageRank with POS-typed head rules and two-step decoding enforcing function-word leafness.
- Key contribution 2: broad empirical evaluation across 32 languages and domains showing competitive UAS vs multi-source delexicalized transfer (MSD) and robustness ablations for PR’s impact.
- Key contribution 3: a practical, language-agnostic heuristic to infer ADP head direction from test data, reducing language-specific tuning.
- Strength: strong cross-lingual coverage and competitive performance (UDPG averages 57.5 UAS, within 6.4 points of MSDG, outperforming MSDG on Hindi).
- Strength: robustness to POS noise and domain shift—smaller UAS drops with predicted POS and lower error propagation and variance across genres than MSD.
- Strength: clear methodology and informative analyses—explicit decoding, PR setup, and ablations showing PR and decoding contributions to UAS.
- Strength: minimal resource needs and interpretability—no training/parallel data/external lexicons, human-readable rules, inspectable ADP inference.
- Strength: UD-conformant design (function-word leafness and UD POS constraints) that aligns with evaluation conventions.
- Weakness: “unsupervised” claim overstated—several design choices tuned on English dev and predicted POS uses supervised taggers trained on target data.
- Weakness: limited rule coverage blocks phenomena (e.g., NOUN→VERB relative clauses), weak coordination/punctuation handling, and lacks MWE/proper name treatment; nearest-head fallback risks idiosyncratic attachments.
- Weakness: unclear test-time ADP estimation protocol (10–15 sentences) — whether online, per-slice, or whole-test tuning affects replicability and fairness.
- Weakness: comparative baseline (MSD) could be stronger or better contextualized; missing comparisons to stronger delexicalized/typology-aware baselines.
- Weakness: narrow evaluation—only UAS reported, no LAS, projectivity/length breakdowns, significance testing, or runtime/PR vs decoding cost numbers.
- Question: how exactly is ADP direction estimated at test time (fixed initial sentences, entire test set beforehand, or updated online), and what about languages with scarce ADP bigrams?
- Question: can other function-word directions (e.g., DET, SCONJ) be estimated from test data to fix issues like Basque DET, and would that improve UAS?
- Question: how sensitive is performance to PR personalization weight (5x) and teleport probability (0.05); request sensitivity analysis on diverse languages.
- Question: what proportion of arcs come from back-off steps (dropping δ then κ) and what are their error rates vs rule-licensed arcs?
- Question: what feature templates and hyperparameters were used for MSD, and would adding simple typological priors narrow the performance gap?
- Suggestion: allow constrained NOUN↔VERB attachments (e.g., relative clause cues) or learnable exceptions to recover relative clauses without breaking hierarchies.
- Suggestion: explore edge-centric (reified) PR to better disambiguate attachments in coordination and nominal modifiers.
- Suggestion: estimate more function-word directions from test data per language; this is low-cost and likely beneficial.
- Suggestion: report per-length UAS, non-projectivity rates, runtime table, and consider rerunning on newer UD releases to test stability.
