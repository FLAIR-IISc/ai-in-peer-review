- Presents UDP: a training-free UD-oriented dependency parser using UD-compliant head rules, personalized PageRank, and a two-step decoding attaching content words then function words to enforce UD leaf constraints.
- Introduces unsupervised, test-time ADP attachment direction estimation via ADP–NOMINAL bigram counts to avoid language-specific parameters.
- Evaluates broadly on 32 UD v1.2 languages with ablations and cross-domain analyses, showing competitive performance versus multi-source delexicalized transfer and improved domain robustness.
- Two-step decoding aligning function-word leaves with UD and PageRank prioritization is conceptually elegant and effective; PageRank contributes ~9 UAS over no-PR.
- Achieves competitive cross-lingual performance without target-side training: on gold POS averages 6.4 UAS below supervised transfer and surpasses it on Hindi; modest degradation with predicted POS.
- Shows greater robustness to domain shift than supervised delexicalized systems, with consistently lower standard deviations across domains.
- Minimal language-specific assumptions: adposition direction inferred from test data (converges in ~10–15 sentences); fixed directions for other function classes are simple and largely effective.
- Algorithm is clear, deterministic given POS, near O(n×|C|) runtime per sentence, and reproducible with head rules, ablations, and code provided.
- Depends on POS quality and resource assumptions: main evaluation uses gold or TnT POS trained on target data, weakening low-resource claims; CF tag granularity is sensitive.
- Compact head rule set omits constructions (coordination scope, relative clauses, MWEs), harming PUNCT and CONJ UAS and limiting certain syntactic captures.
- Teleport probability (0.05) and personalization weight (5×) tuned on English only; lacks systematic sensitivity analysis across languages and domains.
- Global single pre/postposition decision is coarse for languages with mixed adposition behaviors or register variation and may misclassify ADP tokens.
- Evaluation limited to UAS; lacks LAS, sentence-length/projectivity breakdowns, and statistical significance testing to substantiate claims.
- Question: How sensitive are results to teleport probability and personalization weight, and are there languages needing different settings?
- Question: What is the empirical non-projectivity rate from the two-step decoding and how does it compare to gold and error patterns?
- Question: For mixed ADP languages, how often does the global decision misclassify tokens and would per-sentence/local estimation help?
- Question: Can directionality for other function classes (DET, SCONJ, CONJ) be estimated at test time and what is the ablation impact vs fixed directions?
- Question: What are per-sentence runtimes and overall throughput including PageRank, and are there scalability issues on long sentences?
- Question: How do results change with alternative POS taggers (projection-based or multilingual neural) to simulate low-resource conditions?
- Essential suggestion: Add a low-resource POS condition (projection or multilingual without target training) and report UAS/robustness/error propagation.
- Essential suggestion: Provide a sensitivity analysis for PageRank parameters and the adposition estimation threshold with language-specific deviations.
- Recommended: Extend rules or add heuristics/post-processing for coordination scope, relative clauses, and punctuation to improve CONJ/PUNCT UAS.
- Recommended: Report LAS (or partial labels), breakdowns by sentence length/projectivity, and perform significance testing.
- Optional: Explore edge-reified or local PageRank over candidate edges to refine head selection while preserving simplicity.
