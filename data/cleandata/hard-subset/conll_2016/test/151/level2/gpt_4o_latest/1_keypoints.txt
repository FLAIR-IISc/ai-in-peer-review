- Unsupervised Parsing Framework: UDP is a fully unsupervised dependency parser using personalized PageRank and a small set of UD-adapted head-dependent rules.
- Domain Robustness: UDP shows robustness to domain shifts and can outperform a multi-source delexicalized transfer parser in some cases.
- Innovative Use of PageRank: The method personalizes PageRank to prioritize content words and uses a two-step decoding to enforce UD constraints (e.g., function words as leaves).
- Adposition Direction Estimation: UDP estimates adposition attachment direction from test data, making it language-agnostic and useful for low-resource settings.
- Novelty: Combining linguistically motivated rules with personalized PageRank is an innovative and effective approach to unsupervised parsing.
- Practical Utility: UDP is resource-efficient and practical for low-resource languages because it requires no training data and handles domain shifts well.
- Comprehensive Evaluation: The authors evaluate on 32 languages and multiple POS setups (gold, predicted, naive), including cross-domain tests.
- Reproducibility: The paper provides implementation details and releases the parser and annotated datasets, supporting reproducibility.
- Limited Handling of Complex Structures: UDP struggles with coordination, relative clauses, and multiword expressions (e.g., excluding NOUN→VERB hurts relative clause parsing).
- Dependency on POS Tags: The parser relies on accurate POS tags, which can be a bottleneck for low-resource languages with poor tagging.
- Lack of Labeled Parsing: The work focuses only on unlabeled attachment (UAS) and does not tackle labeled dependency parsing.
- Evaluation Bias: Head-dependent rules were developed on English data, potentially biasing performance toward languages with similar syntax.
- Question — Free Word Order: How does UDP handle languages with free word order where adjacency-based head-dependent rules may fail?
- Question — Labeled Parsing Extension: Can the approach be extended to labeled dependency parsing, and what modifications would that require?
- Question — Additional Linguistic Features: Have the authors considered using morphology or other linguistic features to help morphologically rich languages?
- Conclusion & Recommendation: The paper is a significant, well-motivated contribution to unsupervised parsing with minor limitations; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
