- Models event linking with CNNs: word embeddings → CNN + max-pooling → mention vectors, concatenated with surrounding embeddings and pairwise features to compute coreference score.  
- Main contribution: neural approach combining word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features; embeddings alone are insufficient.  
- Performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems on ACE and an expanded ACE++ dataset.  
- Experimental concern: gold trigger words are used instead of predicted ones, making comparisons to systems using predicted triggers unfair.  
- Experimental concern: different papers use different train/test splits; authors should stick to previous splits when possible.  
- Unclear: the final claim about the need for cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is not clearly explained.  
- Unclear: how positional embeddings are generated—randomly initialized or lexicalized—and why the same relative position should share an embedding across words.  
- Unclear: how left vs right neighbors are represented/used (lines 307–311); is this only affecting max-pooling?  
- Unclear: why exactly one word before and after the trigger are appended to the representation (arbitrary window choice).  
- Unclear: how the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is used, since later sections reference only v_{sent+lex}.  
- Unclear: encoding of pairwise features in section 3.2 (binary vector?) and handling of continuous features like distance; are these parameters updated during training?  
- Suggestion: evaluate on entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with more prior work.  
- Question: the square function nonlinearity—is this novel and broadly applicable?  
- Dataset concern: ACE++ is not public—will it be released? comparisons on this dataset to feature-rich systems are missing.  
- Results: many numbers are close; significance testing is needed to support claims.  
- Related work: should cite Wiseman et al. (2015) neural coreference approach.  
- Minor: line 143 has a redundant "that."  
- Minor: inconsistency in baseline naming—"same type" in Table 6 vs "same event" in text (line 670).
