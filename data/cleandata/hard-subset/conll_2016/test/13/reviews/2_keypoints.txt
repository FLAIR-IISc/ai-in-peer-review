- Paper proposes event entity linking using sentential CNN features and a two-part model for mention representation and coreference scoring.
- Paper is well-written, with useful analysis of argument sharing in ACE and dataset-size motivation for creating ACE++.
- Major issue: authors do not motivate or analyze why CNNs were chosen for mention representations and provide no comparisons (e.g., RNNs); 2–3 point F1 gains need stronger architectural evidence.
- Major issue: novelty is unclear—using CNNs over sentences for classification is not new; paper should compare to existing continuous-space event-linking methods and expand baselines in Table 3.
- Major issue: ACE++ data collection is not described—unclear event-type coverage and biases; need to show earlier methods fail where theirs succeeds and consider releasing ACE++.
- Minor: report model performance without gold references in Table 3.
- Minor: explore augmenting the model with off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) as in the line-687 example.
- Minor: evaluate on additional resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) and consider joint entity/event coreference experiments given ACE’s small size.
- Minor: Table 5 implies pairwise features remain important—report which features mattered, how they were chosen, and what alternatives were tried.
