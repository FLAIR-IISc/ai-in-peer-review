1. Proposes CNN-based sentential feature extraction for event linking without relying on external knowledge bases.  
2. Reports competitive results on ACE 2005 and a newly introduced ACE++ dataset.  
3. Main contribution: shows robust event linking is possible without extensive external semantic features.  
4. Method: combines word embeddings with pairwise and local lexical features; uses CNNs for sentence representation and logistic regression for classification.  
5. Creation of the ACE++ dataset is a valuable contribution, though currently not publicly available.  
6. Secondary contribution: demonstrates CNNs effectively capture sentence-level context for event coreference.  
7. Major weakness: uses gold trigger words instead of predicted triggers, giving an unfair advantage over full-pipeline systems.  
8. Inconsistent use of train/test splits; does not adhere to established splits for fair comparison.  
9. Model design details are unclear—choice of a one-word window around triggers is arbitrary and unexplained.  
10. Handling of left vs. right neighbor words and their impact on max-pooling is not clearly described.  
11. Purpose and use of the intermediate event representation v_e are unclear.  
12. Claim that cross-sentential information is necessary is unsupported by the presented analysis.  
13. No statistical significance testing presented despite many close performance numbers.  
14. Related work section is incomplete—missing citation to Wiseman et al. (2015).  
15. Unavailability of ACE++ hinders reproducibility and broader impact.  
16. Key questions for authors: experiments with different window sizes; clarification of neighbor representations and pooling; role of v_e; encoding and training of continuous pairwise features; ACE++ public release; performance with predicted triggers.  
17. Minor issues: redundant word "that" (line 143) and inconsistent baseline naming ("same type" vs. "same event").
