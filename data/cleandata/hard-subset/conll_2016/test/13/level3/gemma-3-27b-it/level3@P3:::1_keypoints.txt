- Proposed CNN-based event linking that learns sentential features and achieves competitive performance without heavy external semantic resources.
- Creation and use of an extended ACE++ dataset to supplement ACE 2005 for evaluation.
- Analysis of different feature combinations (pairwise, lexical, sentential) to understand model behavior and feature importance.

- Competitive performance on ACE 2005 and promising results on ACE++, validating the approach.
- Demonstrates reduced reliance on expensive domain-specific resources (e.g., WordNet/FrameNet), aiding portability and low-resource settings.
- Clear, well-explained model architecture from embeddings through CNN feature extraction to coreference scoring.
- Comprehensive experiments and clustering strategy comparisons to probe model sensitivity and configuration choices.
- Detailed error analysis identifying hard cases (e.g., pronoun resolution) and directions for improvement.

- Use of gold trigger words is a major limitation that likely overestimates performance compared to realistic predicted-trigger pipelines.
- Inconsistent dataset splits across papers hinder fair comparison and reproducibility.
- Several design choices lack justification or clarity (e.g., 1-word window size, positional embedding details, left/right neighbor handling in max-pooling).
- Possible underuse or inconsistent description of the event representation v_e, suggesting a missed modeling opportunity.
- No statistical significance testing reported to support claims about small result differences.

- Question: How would performance change when using predicted triggers instead of gold triggers?
- Question: Are positional embeddings randomly initialized or lexicalized, and why share embeddings across same relative positions?
- Question: How are left/right neighbors represented and used in max-pooling, and does this only affect pooling?
- Question: How is v_e ultimately integrated into coreference scoring beyond v_{sent+lex}?
- Question: How are pairwise features encoded (binary vs. continuous, scaling/discretization) and are their parameters trained?
- Question: Will the ACE++ dataset be publicly released?

- Minor typo: redundant "that" on line 143.
- Inconsistent baseline naming: “same type” (Table 6) vs. “same event” (text).
- The square-function nonlinearity choice is interesting and needs more discussion and potential generality.
- Suggest evaluating on entity coreference datasets (e.g., OntoNotes) for broader comparison.
- Recommend citing Wiseman et al. (2015) for relevant neural coreference prior work.
