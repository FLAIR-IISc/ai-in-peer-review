- Summary: Proposes an event-linking model using sentential CNN features and a two-part architecture, claiming SOTA on ACE and ACE++ without external features.
- Contribution: Introduces sentential CNN features for event mention representation.
- Contribution: Designs a two-part model separating mention representation and coreference scoring.
- Contribution: Reports state-of-the-art results on ACE and ACE++ without external feature sources.
- Strength: Paper is well-written and explanations are clear.
- Strength: Provides useful analysis of argument sharing and motivation for creating ACE++.
- Strength: Demonstrates strong empirical performance on two datasets.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification and architectural evidence for choosing CNNs; no comparison to RNNs/alternatives.
- Weakness: Novelty is unclear because CNNs over sentences for classification are not new.
- Weakness: Limited set of baselines (Table 3) prevents thorough performance evaluation.
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed description of ACE++ (event-type coverage, biases, collection).
- Question: Provide more evidence justifying CNNs for mention representations and comparisons to other architectures like RNNs.
- Question: How do you address the critique that sentence-level CNNs are not novel and what evidence shows your approach is novel?
- Question: Can you include a more comprehensive set of baselines, including other continuous-space event-linking methods?
- Question: Provide a detailed description of ACE++ (event-type coverage, biases, collection methodology).
- Question: How will you address model limitations such as missing fine-grained discourse structure and pronoun-resolution failures?
