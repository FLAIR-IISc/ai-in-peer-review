- Proposal: model for event linking using sentential CNN features and a two-part architecture that avoids external semantic features.  
- Claim: achieves state-of-the-art on two datasets, one publicly available.  
- Contribution: provides error analysis and motivates ACE++ via argument-sharing analysis.

- Strength: paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the model and components.  
- Strength: useful analysis of argument sharing in ACE and motivation for ACE++.  
- Strength: competitive performance on ACE, outperforming some prior systems.

- Weakness: no clear motivation for choosing CNNs over alternatives like RNNs.  
- Weakness: limited novelty since sentence-level CNNs for classification are established.  
- Weakness: ACE++ dataset is not described in detail (event-type coverage, biases).  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to existing continuous-space event-linking methods and expanded baselines (Table 3).  
- Weakness: does not report model performance without gold references in Table 3.

- Question: why were CNNs chosen over other architectures for mention representations?  
- Question: how will pronoun resolution be addressed in event linking?  
- Question: please provide ACE++ details (event-type coverage, biases).  
- Question: have you compared to existing continuous-space event-linking methods?  
- Question: what features are used in the pairwise comparison and how were they chosen/alternatives tried?

- Suggestion: evaluate on additional corpora such as EventCorefBank.  
- Suggestion: consider augmenting with off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP).  
- Suggestion: consider joint entity/event coreference experiments given ACE’s small size.
