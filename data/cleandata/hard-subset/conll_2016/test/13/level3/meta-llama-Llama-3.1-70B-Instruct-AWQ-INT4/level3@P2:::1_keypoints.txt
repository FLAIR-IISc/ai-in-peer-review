- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that combines sentential features and pairwise features, claiming state-of-the-art results on ACE and ACE++ without external features.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a neural event linking approach combining word embeddings and a small set of linguistic features via CNNs.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that CNN-generated sentential features capture contextual information useful for event linking.

- Contribution 3: Reports competitive performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems on ACE and ACE++.

- Strength 1: Novel approach leveraging CNNs to capture contextual information for event linking.

- Strength 2: Empirical results show competitive performance against feature-rich baselines.

- Strength 3: Provides a detailed error analysis offering insights into model limitations.

- Weakness 1: Experimental concerns: uses gold triggers (not predicted) and inconsistent train/test splits, making comparisons unfair.

- Weakness 2: Lack of clarity in model details: positional embeddings, left vs right neighbor representation, and why one-word context is appended are not explained.

- Weakness 3: Dataset concerns: ACE++ is not public and comparisons to feature-rich systems on ACE++ are missing.

- Question 1: How are positional embeddings generated and why should the same relative position share an embedding across words?

- Question 2: How are left vs right neighbors represented and used in the model, and does this only affect max-pooling?

- Question 3: Why append exactly one word before and after the trigger—is this window choice arbitrary?

- Question 4: Clarify how the event-mention representation v_e is used versus v_{sent+lex} referenced later.

- Question 5: How are pairwise features encoded in section 3.2, and are those parameters updated during training?

- Additional comment 1: Evaluate the model on entity coreference datasets (e.g., OntoNotes) for broader comparison.

- Additional comment 2: Provide more details on the square-function nonlinearity and its novelty/applicability.

- Additional comment 3: Consider releasing ACE++ publicly to facilitate reproducibility and comparisons.

- Additional comment 4: Include significance testing to support claims of competitive performance.
