- Proposes a two-part model using CNNs to learn event-mention representations and combining them with pairwise features for event linking.  
- Main contribution: neural approach that combines word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on ACE and ACE++, outperforming prior feature-rich systems.  
- Provides thorough analysis including error analysis that highlights pronoun resolution issues and need for finer discourse-structure info.  
- Uses gold trigger words instead of predicted triggers, which may make comparisons to other systems unfair.  
- Lacks clarity on generation of positional embeddings, representation of left/right neighbors, and encoding of pairwise features.  
- Suggests evaluating on entity coreference datasets (e.g., OntoNotes) for broader comparison with prior work.  
- Recommends exploring other architectures (recursive/recurrent networks) for generating sentential features.  
- Recommends releasing the ACE++ dataset to facilitate comparisons with feature-rich systems.  
- Asks whether the square function nonlinearity is novel and broadly applicable.  
- Asks how continuous features (e.g., distance) are handled and whether those parameters are updated during training.  
- Overall: a significant contribution that could be strengthened with minor revisions and clarifications.
