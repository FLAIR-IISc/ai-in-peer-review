- Proposes a CNN-based neural model for event linking that creates sentential features from event mentions and context and combines them with pairwise features to score coreference.  
- Does not rely on external semantic resources, increasing applicability to low-resource domains.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on ACE and ACE++ and outperforms prior feature-rich systems.  
- Contribution: combines word embeddings with CNN-generated sentential features for event linking.  
- Contribution: two-part architecture—generate event-mention representations, then compute coreference scores with pairwise features.  
- Contribution: strong empirical performance on standard datasets without external semantic features.  
- Strength: CNN extraction of sentential features from mentions and context is a notable methodological advance.  
- Strength: Independence from external semantic features improves domain applicability.  
- Strength: Empirical results demonstrate the model’s effectiveness on ACE and ACE++.  
- Weakness: Uses gold trigger words rather than predicted triggers, limiting comparability to other systems.  
- Weakness: Different train/test splits versus other studies may affect result comparability.  
- Weakness: Final claim about the need for cross-sentential information is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Generation and sharing of positional embeddings are unclearly explained.  
- Weakness: No significance testing reported, making it hard to assess statistical robustness of results.  
- Question: Please clarify the claim about needing cross-sentential information and how it’s incorporated.  
- Question: How are positional embeddings generated, and why share the same relative position embedding across words?  
- Question: How are left vs. right neighbors represented and used in the model?  
- Question: Why append exactly one word before and after the trigger—why that window size?  
- Question: How is the event-mention representation v_e used later in the model, since it’s not referenced subsequently?  
- Question: How are pairwise features encoded in Section 3.2, and are those parameters trained/updated?  
- Additional: Paper would benefit from a more detailed description of the CNN architecture and sentential feature generation.  
- Additional: Using a robust evaluation metric like BLANC is a positive choice.  
- Additional: Comparison against feature-rich prior systems is a useful contribution.  
- Additional: A more detailed error analysis would help identify directions for improvement.
