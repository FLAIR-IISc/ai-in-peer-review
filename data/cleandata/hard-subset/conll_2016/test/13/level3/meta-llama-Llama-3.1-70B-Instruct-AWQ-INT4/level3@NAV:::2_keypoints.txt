- Proposes using sentential features from CNNs for event linking, generating latent representations for event mentions.
- Introduces a two-part model combining CNN-generated representations with pairwise features for coreference decisions.
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on ACE and ACE++ without relying on external semantic features.
- Novel application of CNNs to event linking, claimed as unexplored prior work.
- Effective use of CNNs demonstrated for producing latent event mention features.
- Thorough evaluation including comparisons to prior systems and analysis of different feature classes.
- Lacks clear motivation for choosing CNNs over other neural architectures like RNNs or recursive networks.
- Limited novelty concern: CNNs for classification are common and distinctions from existing methods are not well explained.
- ACE++ evaluation is unclear due to missing description of dataset’s event-type coverage and potential biases.
- Requests clearer motivation for selecting CNNs instead of alternative architectures.
- Asks for clarification on how this approach differs from prior CNN-based classification methods.
- Requests a more detailed description of the ACE++ dataset, including event-type coverage and biases.
