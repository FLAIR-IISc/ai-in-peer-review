- Summary: Proposes a two-step event linking model using CNN-based sentential features, achieving SOTA on ACE 2005 and on a new larger dataset (ACE++) created by the authors.

- Contribution: Shows event linking can reach SOTA without external semantic resources (e.g., WordNet/FrameNet).

- Contribution: Creates and releases ACE++, a substantially larger dataset (1950 docs vs 599) that improves recall and generalization.

- Contribution: Provides error analysis identifying key challenges, especially pronoun resolution.

- Strength: Demonstrates domain portability by avoiding reliance on external semantic resources.

- Strength: Empirically shows larger training data (ACE++) substantially improves recall while keeping competitive precision.

- Strength: Error analysis offers useful insights and concrete examples (pronoun-related false negatives) for future work.

- Weakness: No clear justification or empirical comparison for choosing CNNs over RNNs or other sequence models.

- Weakness: Novelty claim is weak because CNN sentence representations are not new and comparisons omit recent neural event-linking baselines.

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset construction lacks critical details (event-type coverage, annotation consistency, potential web-source biases).

- Weakness: Table 3 omits results with gold references, making it hard to separate model vs extraction-pipeline effects.

- Weakness: Does not evaluate integrating off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) with the linking model.

- Question: What specific properties of event mentions make CNNs more suitable than RNNs, and what experiments support this?

- Question: Please provide detailed ACE++ creation info: event-type coverage, document sources, annotation consistency checks, and bias analysis.

- Question: How does the model perform on other resources like EventCorefBank used in prior work?

- Question: What are the anticipated benefits and evaluation plan for jointly resolving entity and event coreference?
