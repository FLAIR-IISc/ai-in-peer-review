- Contribution: Introduces ACE++, a larger event coreference dataset to address ACE's small size.  
- Contribution: Proposes a CNN-based sentence representation and two-part architecture for mention representation and coreference scoring that avoids external semantic features.  
- Contribution: Analyzes argument-sharing patterns in event mentions (noting 23% incomplete mentions).  

- Strength: ACE++ dataset improves scalability over ACE and motivates the task via argument-sharing analysis.  
- Strength: Model obtains 2–3 point F1 gains over prior work without external semantic resources, aiding cross-domain applicability.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written with clear technical exposition and error analysis highlighting pronoun resolution issues.  

- Weakness: Novelty is limited—CNN sentence representations are not new and the paper lacks comparison to existing continuous-space methods (e.g., Liu et al., 2014) or justification for choosing CNNs over RNNs/transformers.  
- Weakness: ACE++ dataset description is insufficient—event-type coverage, biases, and creation methodology are unclear and the dataset should be released.  
- Weakness: Improvement claims lack architectural evidence—pairwise features still matter and the paper does not specify which features or alternatives were tried.  
- Weakness: Baselines are inadequate—missing gold-reference performance, missing comparisons to continuous-space methods, and evaluation on EventCorefBank and off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) is not provided.  

- Question: Why choose CNNs over RNNs or transformers for sentence representation?  
- Question: Is ACE++ balanced across event types and free of domain biases—can you provide evidence?  
- Question: How would integrating off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) affect model performance?
