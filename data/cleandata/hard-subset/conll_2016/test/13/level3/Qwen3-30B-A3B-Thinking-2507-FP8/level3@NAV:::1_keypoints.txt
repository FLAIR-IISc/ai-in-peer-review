- Proposes a neural event coreference model using CNNs for sentential features plus lexical and pairwise features to compute coreference scores.  
- Authors claim state-of-the-art performance on ACE and ACE++ without external semantic features.  
- Main contribution: a CNN-based architecture to capture sentential context for event linking.  
- Main contribution: empirical claim that embeddings alone are insufficient for event linking (but experimental setup is questionable).  
- Main contribution: extension of the ACE dataset to ACE++ (dataset not publicly available).  
- Strength: reduces dependence on external semantic resources and tackles an important problem.  
- Strength: error analysis provides insight into pronoun resolution and boundary-case challenges.  
- Strength: results on ACE are promising and comparable to feature-rich systems.  
- Weakness: uses gold trigger words rather than predicted triggers, making comparisons unfair.  
- Weakness: ACE++ is not released and lacks comparisons to feature-rich systems on that dataset.  
- Weakness: many implementation details are unclear (positional embeddings, one-word context window choice, handling of pairwise features).  
- Weakness: no significance testing reported despite close performance numbers.  
- Weakness: claim about need for cross-sentential information is not well explained and reliance on sentence-level features is insufficiently justified.  
- Questions for authors: how are positional embeddings generated, why choose one word of context on each side, and will ACE++ be made public?
