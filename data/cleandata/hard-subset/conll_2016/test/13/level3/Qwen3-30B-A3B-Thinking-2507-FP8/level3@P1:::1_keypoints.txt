- Summary: Proposes a two-step neural event-linking model using CNN-derived sentential features instead of external semantic resources, reporting SOTA on ACE and a new ACE++ dataset.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a domain-agnostic neural approach that eliminates reliance on external semantic resources (WordNet/FrameNet).

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that sentential features from CNNs are sufficient to achieve state-of-the-art event linking performance.

- Contribution 3: Provides empirical evidence that increased training data (ACE++) substantially improves recall with little precision loss.

- Strength 1: Scales with more data—ACE++ yields 83.21% recall vs. 75.16% on ACE, showing real-world potential.

- Strength 2: Includes a thorough error analysis highlighting concrete challenges (e.g., pronoun resolution).

- Strength 3: Outperforms prior resource-dependent methods (e.g., Liu et al., 2014), making the approach more broadly applicable.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation uses gold-standard triggers rather than predicted ones, making comparisons to prior work unfair.

- Weakness 2: ACE++ is not publicly available, preventing reproducibility and external verification.

- Weakness 3: No statistical significance testing reported, so observed improvements may be within error margins.

- Weakness 4: Technical details are unclear (positional embeddings generation, rationale for one-word context, encoding/handling of pairwise features).

- Weakness 5: The claim about the necessity of cross-sentential information is not clearly explained or supported by presented data.

- Question 1: Will the ACE++ dataset be made publicly available?

- Question 2: Why exactly one word before and after the trigger—are there ablation results to justify this choice?

- Question 3: How are relative positional embeddings generated (randomly initialized or lexicalized), and why share embeddings across words with the same relative position?

- Question 4: What statistical tests were performed to confirm significance of improvements over prior work?
