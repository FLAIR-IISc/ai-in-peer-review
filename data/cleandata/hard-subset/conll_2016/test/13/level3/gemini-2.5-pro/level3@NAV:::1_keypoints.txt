- Proposal: CNN-based model builds event-mention vectors from word and positional embeddings and combines them with local lexical and pairwise features for coreference scoring; contributions: a KB-light neural architecture and empirical evidence that learned sentential features must be combined with linguistic features.
- Strength: Reduces reliance on external semantic resources (e.g., WordNet/FrameNet) while achieving competitive performance.
- Strength: Ablation study shows integrating sentential embeddings, local lexical features, and pairwise features is crucial for strong results.
- Strength: Provides detailed error analysis and multiple baseline comparisons to contextualize performance and limitations.
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are unsound—uses gold event triggers, different train/test splits from prior work, and lacks statistical significance testing for close results.
- Weakness: Key architectural details are unclear (positional embeddings, piecewise max-pooling, pairwise feature encoding, one-word context rationale), hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns due to use of a new ACE++ dataset without confirmation of release and omission of a relevant prior citation (Wiseman et al., 2015).
- Question: Please clarify the novelty and intuition behind using a square function as the nonlinearity in the similarity computation.
- Question: Will the ACE++ dataset be publicly released to enable reproduction and benchmarking?
- Question: How do you justify claims about needing cross-sentential information when the model builds mention representations from single sentences?
