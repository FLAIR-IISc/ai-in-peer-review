- Proposed CNN-based model learns sentential representations for event mentions and combines them with local lexical and pairwise features for within-document event coreference.
- Evaluated on ACE 2005 and a new proprietary ACE++ dataset, achieving performance comparable to prior heavily feature-engineered SOTA without external KBs.
- Contribution: a knowledge-lean event linking approach that replaces WordNet/FrameNet reliance with learned distributed representations.
- Contribution: a principled combination of learned CNN sentential features with curated local and pairwise features, shown complementary via ablation.
- Strength: reduces dependence on external semantic resources, improving domain portability.
- Strength: strong empirical results on ACE 2005, outperforming prior work on several metrics (e.g., BLANC, MUC F1).
- Strength: thorough ablation study and useful error analysis identifying issues like pronominal anaphora and annotation ambiguities.
- Weakness: experiments use gold event triggers/arguments, making results unrealistic for end-to-end settings and inflating performance.
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns because ACE++ is not public and train/test splits differ from prior work, complicating fair comparisons.
- Weakness: small performance gains over prior work (~1% F1) require statistical significance testing to substantiate SOTA claims.
- Weakness: unclear model/implementation details — positional embeddings, encoding/learning of continuous pairwise features, and justification for single-word local window are not specified.
- Question: clarify how continuous pairwise features (e.g., distance) are encoded and whether their representations are learned.
- Question: resolve inconsistency around v_e in Equation 3 versus use of v_sent+lex in Equation 4 — which representation is used?
- Question: justify use of the square function as a non-linearity and provide comparisons to standard activations (ReLU, tanh).
- Question: do you plan to release the ACE++ dataset and exact document lists for the ACE 2005 splits to aid reproducibility?
- Additional: related work should cite more recent neural coreference models (e.g., Wiseman et al., EMNLP 2015).
- Additional: minor naming inconsistency between "same event" (text) and "Same type" (Table 6).
