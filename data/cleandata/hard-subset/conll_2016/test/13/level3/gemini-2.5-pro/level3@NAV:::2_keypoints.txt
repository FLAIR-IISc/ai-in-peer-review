- Proposes a two-part event coreference model that uses CNN-learned sentential features combined with lexical and pairwise features to score coreference.
- Provides an analysis of ACE 2005 showing low argument sharing between coreferential mentions and motivates the creation of a larger ACE++ dataset.
- Shows a neural model that achieves state-of-the-art/competitive event linking performance without external KBs, improving cross-domain portability.
- Paper presents clear writing, well-structured explanations, and accessible model details.
- Dataset analysis insightfully highlights limitations of relying on intrinsic event properties and supports using sentential/contextual features.
- Empirical results are strong, notably achieving high performance without handcrafted semantic features from external resources.
- Fails to justify the choice of CNNs over other sequence models (RNNs/LSTMs) and offers no comparative architectural experiments.
- Limited technical novelty—applying CNNs to sentence-level tasks is not new—and evaluation lacks comparisons to other continuous-space event linking methods and stronger neural baselines.
- ACE++ is poorly described: no collection/annotation details, unclear quality, coverage, bias, or release plans, and no error analyses showing where prior methods fail but the authors' method succeeds.
