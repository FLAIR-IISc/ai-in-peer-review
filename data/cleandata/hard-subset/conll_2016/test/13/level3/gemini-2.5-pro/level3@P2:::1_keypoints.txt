- Paper proposes a neural model for within-document event coreference that uses a CNN over event sentences plus local lexical and pairwise features to score mention pairs.  
- Evaluated on ACE 2005 and a new ACE++ dataset, claiming state-of-the-art or competitive results without external KBs (WordNet/FrameNet).  
- Contribution: an effective, portable neural architecture that avoids reliance on external semantic resources.  
- Contribution: an ablation study showing sentential, local lexical, and pairwise features are complementary and each improves performance.  

- Strength: Strong empirical performance on ACE 2005, matching or surpassing prior systems while avoiding heavy feature engineering.  
- Strength: Well-motivated model design (CNN for sentential context plus lexical/pairwise features) with ablation evidence supporting the hybrid approach.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical analysis including impact of training data size, clustering strategy comparisons, baselines, and qualitative error analysis (pronouns, annotation ambiguity).  

- Weakness: Experimental setup limits reproducibility and realism — uses gold event triggers rather than predicted triggers.  
- Weakness: Major results rely on proprietary ACE++ dataset, hindering reproducibility and community validation.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent ACE train/test splits complicate direct comparisons to prior work.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported for small metric differences, weakening claims of superiority.  
- Weakness: Some design choices lack justification (e.g., why one-word left/right lexical window) and notation is occasionally ambiguous.  
- Weakness: Related work is incomplete; missing comparisons to contemporary neural coreference approaches (e.g., Wiseman et al. 2015).  

- Question: Why use a square function as the non-linearity in the similarity layer, and how does it compare to ReLU/tanh in practice?  
- Question: How are the pairwise features encoded (one-hot for categorical, raw vs. binned for distances) and are these representations learned?  
- Question: How are positional embeddings implemented (randomly initialized and learned?), and why use a single embedding per relative distance?  
- Question: Will the ACE++ dataset be released to the research community?  
- Question: How can the sentence-level CNN capture cross-sentential context needed for pronoun resolution beyond simple pairwise distance features?  

- Minor: Typo on line 143 — redundant "that."  
- Minor: Inconsistency in baseline naming between Table 6 ("Same type") and text (line 670, "same event").
