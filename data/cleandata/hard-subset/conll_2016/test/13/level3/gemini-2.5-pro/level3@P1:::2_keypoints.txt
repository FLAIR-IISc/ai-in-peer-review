- Two-stage neural model: CNN with piecewise max-pooling produces sentential event mention vectors; pairwise feed-forward network scores coreference using these plus standard features.  
- Evaluated on ACE 2005 and a new larger ACE++ dataset; claims state-of-the-art performance without external resources (WordNet/FrameNet).  
- Contribution: shows learned sentential features can match prior systems that relied on external semantic KBs, improving portability across domains.  
- Contribution: creation/use of ACE++ (1351 additional web docs) which reportedly improves recall and generalization.  
- Contribution: dataset analysis (low argument overlap among coreferent mentions) motivating sentential context and error analysis highlighting challenges like pronominal event anaphora.  
- Strength: achieves competitive or superior results on ACE 2005 across metrics (BLANC, MUC) with a clear ablation showing benefits of pairwise, local, and sentential features.  
- Strength: avoids external semantic resources, offering a more domain-portable approach than prior engineered-feature systems.  
- Strength: well-written, clearly motivated paper and sensible creation of ACE++ to address ACE sparsity.  
- Weakness: no justification/comparison for choosing CNN over other architectures (e.g., RNN/LSTM) that capture sequential, long-range dependencies.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—using CNN sentence encoders is standard; insufficient comparison to other neural coreference or continuous-space models.  
- Weakness: ACE++ lacks crucial details (sources, annotation process, IAA, event-type distribution), raising reproducibility and bias concerns; release encouraged.  
- Weakness: experiments use gold event mentions; missing evaluation with system-predicted mentions to assess robustness to pipeline errors.  
- Weakness: pairwise features remain crucial but their selection and relative importance are underspecified in the paper.  
- Questions to authors: justify CNN choice and report experiments with RNNs/LSTMs; provide full ACE++ provenance, annotation details, and release plans.  
- Questions to authors: whether augmenting with external coreference tools could handle pronominal event anaphora; clarify which pairwise features were most predictive and how they were chosen.
