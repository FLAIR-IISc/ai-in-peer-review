- Proposes a neural model for within-document event coreference that avoids external semantic resources by learning mention representations via a CNN over sentence word and relative position embeddings.  
- Builds final mention vectors by concatenating CNN-based sentential representations with trigger and neighbor embeddings, and scores mention pairs using combined vectors plus a few pairwise features through a square-activation hidden layer and logistic output.  
- Evaluated on ACE 2005 and a new ACE++ dataset, achieving performance comparable to prior feature-engineered state-of-the-art.  
- Contribution: a hybrid neural architecture that integrates learned dense representations with a small set of discrete linguistic features for event linking.  
- Contribution: shows competitive performance without relying on external knowledge bases, suggesting better portability and domain-independence.  
- Contribution: provides an ablation study demonstrating complementary contributions of sentential, local lexical, and pairwise features, and introduces the larger ACE++ corpus.  
- Strength: achieves strong performance on ACE while avoiding heavy external resources, improving generalizability versus prior handcrafted-feature systems.  
- Strength: principled model design combining global sentential context (CNN), local lexical context, and pairwise relational features, with ablation validating each component.  
- Strength: provides a thorough manual error analysis highlighting hard cases like pronominal event anaphora and annotation ambiguities.  
- Weakness: experimental validity concerns—use of gold triggers gives an unfair advantage over end-to-end systems using predicted triggers.  
- Weakness: lack of statistical significance testing on close numeric results makes it unclear whether improvements are meaningful.  
- Weakness: inconsistent ACE dataset splits across papers complicate direct comparisons, and the authors' split choice weakens claims of outperforming prior work.  
- Weakness: reproducibility problems—ACE++ is not public and several model details (e.g., exact use of v_e, encoding of pairwise features) are underspecified.  
- Weakness: claim about benefits of sentential features for distant coreference is unsupported because the model encodes only single sentences per mention.  
- Weakness: incomplete related work discussion—missing citation to Wiseman et al. (2015) on neural entity coreference.  
- Question: clarify the relationship between v_e and v_{sent+lex}—which representation is used for scoring?  
- Question: how are pairwise features encoded (one-hot for discrete, binning/encoding for continuous like sentence distance)?  
- Question: clarify how positional embeddings are generated, initialized, learned, and why a single embedding per relative position is shared across words.  
- Question: is the square activation in the similarity layer a novel contribution and has its applicability to other similarity tasks been investigated?  
- Question: justification for using exactly a one-word window around the trigger—was this tuned on development data?  
- Question: will the authors release the ACE++ dataset and implementation code to enable reproducibility?
