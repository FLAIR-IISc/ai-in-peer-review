- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using personalized PageRank and a small set of head-dependent rules.  
- Requires no training data and estimates adposition direction directly from test data.  
- Evaluated on 32 languages and compared to a multi-source delexicalized parser (MSD).  
- UDP is competitive with MSD on most languages (worse on two) and less affected by noisy POS tags and domain shifts.  

Contributions:
- Unsupervised parser achieving competitive UD performance without training data.  
- Uses personalized PageRank to score content words and infer adposition direction from test data.  
- Thorough evaluation across three POS setups (gold, predicted, naive) and multiple domains.  

Strengths:
- Novel unsupervised approach that performs competitively across many languages.  
- Effective use of personalized PageRank for content-word scoring and direction estimation.  
- Robust evaluation demonstrating resilience to POS deterioration and domain changes.  

Weaknesses:
- No comparison with other unsupervised dependency parsers, limiting assessment of novelty and impact.  
- Limited per-language and per-domain analysis to explain where and why it succeeds or fails.  
- Lacks discussion of practical applications and future extensions.  

Questions for authors:
- How does UDP compare quantitatively and qualitatively to other unsupervised dependency parsers?  
- Can you provide deeper analyses of performance on specific languages and domains?  
- What are the potential applications and planned future directions or improvements for UDP?
