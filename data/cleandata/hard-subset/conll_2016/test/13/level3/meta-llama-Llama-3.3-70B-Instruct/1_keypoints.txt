- Proposes UDP, an unsupervised dependency parser using personalized PageRank plus a small set of head-dependent rules requiring no training data.
- Adapts traditional dependency head rules to Universal Dependencies (UD)-compliant POS relations.
- Incorporates UD-specific restrictions on function words and estimates adposition direction directly from test data.
- Uses personalized PageRank to improve main predicate identification.
- Achieves competitive performance on most UD languages (fails on two languages).
- Demonstrates robustness to domain shifts and sampling bias.
- Simple design and few heuristics make the parser easy to apply across scenarios.
- Thorough evaluation across three POS setups and multiple domains.
- Experimental limitations include reliance on gold POS tags.
- Lacks comparison to other unsupervised dependency parsers and broader prior work.
- Several methodological details are unclear (generation of position embeddings; handling of left vs right neighbors).
- Results lack significance testing.
- Questions about handling of multiword expressions, coordination, and proper names remain unanswered.
- Suggests possible extensions: using PageRank to score edges, testing on different word orders/grammars, and augmenting with partial edge labeling.
