- Proposes a CNN-based event coreference model using word embeddings and limited linguistic features to compute coreference without external semantic resources.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on ACE and ACE++ datasets, demonstrating scalability and efficacy.  
- Introduces the square function as a novel nonlinearity for similarity computation.  
- Provides error analysis identifying challenges like pronoun resolution and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Strength: independence from external semantic resources enhances cross-domain portability.  
- Strength: competitive or superior performance compared to feature-rich systems on ACE and ACE++.  
- Strength: thorough error analysis offering useful insights for future work.  
- Strength: novel use of square nonlinearity could inspire related tasks.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses gold-standard event triggers, limiting real-world applicability and fair comparisons.  
- Weakness: insufficient methodological details on cross-sentential information, position embeddings, neighbor features, and event mention representation (v_e).  
- Weakness: ACE++ dataset is not publicly available, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: inconsistent train/test splits across papers complicate direct comparisons with prior work.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported despite close performance numbers.  
- Question: How does the model perform with predicted triggers instead of gold triggers?  
- Question: Please clarify how cross-sentential information and position embeddings are incorporated and whether they are learned.  
- Question: Are there plans to release the ACE++ dataset for reproducibility?  
- Additional: Paper should discuss related neural coreference work (e.g., Wiseman et al., 2015) in more detail.  
- Additional minor issues: redundant word on line 143 and inconsistent baseline naming between Table 6 and line 670.
