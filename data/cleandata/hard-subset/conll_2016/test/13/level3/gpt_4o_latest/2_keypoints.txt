- Proposes a CNN-based event coreference model that avoids external semantic resources and scores coreference with sentential and pairwise features.
- Introduces ACE++, an extended ACE 2005 dataset intended to mitigate data scarcity for event coreference.
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on ACE and shows sentential features can replace external knowledge for event linking.
- Provides analysis of argument sharing and dataset size effects to motivate ACE++.
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear motivation and justification for ACE++.
- Strength: achieves competitive, robust results on ACE without external semantic features.
- Strength: includes thoughtful error analysis highlighting pronoun resolution and annotation issues.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation with ablations and baseline comparisons.
- Weakness: choice of CNNs is not well-justified and lacks comparison to RNNs or transformers.
- Weakness: limited novelty—CNNs for event linking are not new and comparisons to continuous-space methods are insufficient.
- Weakness: ACE++ lacks key details (event coverage, annotation process, release plans), limiting reproducibility.
- Weakness: evaluation is restricted to ACE, leaving generalizability to datasets like EventCorefBank untested.
- Weakness: results rely on gold references; performance without gold mentions/arguments is not reported.
- Question: Why choose CNNs over RNNs/transformers and were alternatives compared?
- Question: Provide ACE++ details—event types, annotation protocol, and public availability.
- Question: How does the model perform without gold references for mentions and arguments?
- Question: Have you evaluated the model on other datasets (e.g., EventCorefBank) to test robustness?
- Conclusion: solid empirical study with SOTA results but weakened by limited novelty, incomplete dataset documentation, and narrow evaluation scope.
