- Task: within-document event coreference without external semantic resources.  
- Model: two-part system — CNN-based mention encoder (sentential representation conditioned on trigger) + pairwise scorer with hand-crafted features, clustered via transitive closure.  
- Empirical claim: state-of-the-art results on ACE without external KBs.  
- Data contribution: introduction of ACE++ (expanded ACE-style corpus), showing more data mainly improves recall.  
- Analysis: ablations on clustering strategies and feature classes; error analysis highlights pronoun-related and annotation-boundary failures.  
- Contribution 1: shows sentential CNN features + lightweight pairwise features can match/exceed prior ACE results, supporting portability to KB-poor domains.  
- Contribution 2: ACE++ dataset used to motivate sentence-level modeling via argument sharing, trigger mismatch, and distance stats.  
- Contribution 3: detailed ablations and error analysis diagnosing core failure modes.  
- Strength: clear problem setup, pipeline perspective, and transparent architecture presentation.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance achieved without WordNet/FrameNet/VerbOcean features.  
- Strength: thorough ablations (transitive closure vs. BestLink; feature class contributions).  
- Strength: qualitative error analysis provides actionable insights (discourse signals, pronoun/event anaphora).  
- Strength: sensible engineering (position embeddings, piecewise pooling, learned similarity) and documentation of dev/test splits.  
- Weakness: CNN encoder choice not adequately justified or compared to BiLSTM/TreeLSTM/attention; modest gains need controlled encoder comparisons and significance testing.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — CNN sentence encoders for events are not new; need positioning against Siamese/metric-learning approaches and broader baselines.  
- Weakness: ACE++ is under-specified (sampling, event-type coverage, domain mix, IAA, biases); release or detailed datasheet needed for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited — relies on gold triggers/arguments, lacks end-to-end results, and missing external datasets (ECB/ECB+) or joint entity/event experiments.  
- Weakness: pairwise feature reporting is thin — unclear which specific features matter or how they were selected/evaluated.  
- Question: Why choose CNNs? Provide controlled encoder and similarity-function comparisons with significance tests.  
- Question: ACE++ specifics — sampling, covered event types, domain distribution, IAA, known biases, and release plans.  
- Question: Add baselines — Siamese BiLSTM, avg-embed cosine, attention-based encoder, and entity-coref-style neural architectures.  
- Question: End-to-end robustness — performance with predicted triggers/arguments (no gold upstream components).  
- Question: Pairwise features — which ones contributed most, how selected, and what alternatives (discourse cues, section headers, temporal signals) were tried?  
- Question: Can augmenting with off-the-shelf entity coref (e.g., CoreNLP) improve argument overlap cases?  
- Additional comment: report variance across random seeds and statistical significance for key comparisons.  
- Additional comment: evaluate (or at least subset) on ECB/ECB+ and consider a joint entity/event experiment to validate discourse hypotheses.  
- Additional comment: ablate learned similarity (square activation) vs. cosine/bilinear to quantify its benefit.  
- Additional comment: if ACE++ cannot be released, at minimum release models, features, and detailed label distributions for reproducibility.
