- Primary contribution: neural event coreference model combining sentential CNN features with lexical and pairwise features; embeddings alone insufficient but together match feature-rich systems.
- Secondary contribution: empirical study on ACE and private ACE++ with ablations quantifying sentential vs. local vs. pairwise feature contributions and an error analysis exposing discourse/pronominal challenges.
- Tertiary contribution: a simple clustering pipeline and evidence that square-activated similarity outperforms several alternatives in this setup.

- Strength: competitive performance without external KBs; ablations show sentential CNN features add measurable value over lexical/pairwise signals.
- Strength: careful feature ablations and clustering comparisons that clarify what drives performance and when BestLink helps or hurts.
- Strength: thoughtful error analysis that surfaces hard phenomena (pronouns, borderline annotations) and contextualizes performance ceilings.
- Strength: clear, modular, reproducible architecture (CNN + pooling for mentions; simple similarity + logistic output) and reasonable training procedure.

- Weakness: fairness/comparability issues—use of gold triggers and non-standard splits undermine comparisons to prior work; significance testing is missing.
- Weakness: dataset concerns—ACE++ is not public, making claims hard to verify and lacking comparisons to feature-rich baselines on ACE++.
- Weakness: limited novelty—CNN-based sentential features are established; square similarity’s general utility is not demonstrated beyond this task.
- Weakness: clarity gaps about positional embedding initialization/sharing, left/right context representation, single-neighbor token choice, how ve relates to vsent+lex, encoding/training of pairwise features, and weak linkage of cross-sentential claims to experiments.
