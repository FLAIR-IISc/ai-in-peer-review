- Summary: Proposes a CNN + piecewise pooling architecture combining sentential embeddings, trigger lexical context, and lightweight pairwise features for within-document event coreference, clustering by transitive closure and evaluated on ACE and a new ACE++ dataset.

- Contribution 1: Simple, effective neural model that avoids external KBs by combining CNN-based sentential features with a few linguistic/pairwise features.

- Contribution 2: Ablation evidence showing pretrained embeddings/sentential features alone are insufficient; lexical trigger context and pairwise features are critical.

- Contribution 3: Larger ACE++ evaluation demonstrating gains from more training data, plus analyses of clustering strategies and an error analysis.

- Strength: Competitive accuracy without external semantic resources, matching or exceeding prior feature-rich systems on ACE.

- Strength: Clear, modular architecture with informative ablations demonstrating complementary contributions of feature groups.

- Strength: Sensible evaluation choices (BLANC, clustering strategy comparisons) and useful error analysis highlighting hard cases like pronominal event anaphora.

- Strength: Model scales with more data (ACE++ training yields sizable recall gains).

- Strength: Practical design avoids heavy syntactic/semantic preprocessing and supports multiple mentions per sentence via piecewise pooling.

- Weakness: Evaluation fairness/replicability concerns due to custom ACE splits (9:1) and use of gold triggers rather than predicted triggers or standard splits.

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset not public and comparisons on ACE++ to prior systems are missing, weakening claims tied to the new data.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported despite small and mixed metric improvements.

- Weakness: Insufficient clarity on positional embeddings (initialization, sharing, updating) and rationale for shared distance embeddings.

- Weakness: Unclear left/right handling—whether filters are shared, how piecewise pooling distinguishes sides, and handling of multiple mentions per sentence.

- Weakness: Questionable choice of context window (only ±1 token) without tuning or exploration of larger windows or attention alternatives.

- Weakness: Ambiguity in representation flow—unclear whether full ve or only vsent+lex is fed into similarity layer; Eq. (3)/(4) inconsistency.

- Weakness: Lack of detail on pairwise feature encoding (binary/one-hot, distance binning, learned jointly or fixed).

- Weakness: Claims about cross-sentential information are vague; model seems to rely mainly on sentential encodings plus pairwise metadata.

- Weakness: Square activation in similarity layer is under-motivated and not adequately compared to alternatives like cosine, bilinear, or MLPs.

- Question: Report results on a standard ACE split or provide split lists for exact replication and include significance testing.

- Question: Provide performance delta when using predicted triggers or simulate realistic trigger noise.

- Question: Will ACE++ be released (data and splits), or can a public surrogate be provided?

- Question: Clarify positional embedding details (initialization, max distance/clipping, sharing benefit).

- Question: Explain left/right context handling (different filters, pooling behavior, overlapping contexts).

- Question: Justify context window size and report performance for different windows or attention-based alternatives.

- Question: Reconcile which mention vector is used in the similarity layer (ve vs vsent+lex).

- Question: Provide encoding details for pairwise features and whether their parameters are trained jointly.

- Question: Give quantitative ablations comparing square activation against ReLU/tanh/bilinear/cosine on ACE and ACE++.

- Additional comment: Cite and discuss Wiseman et al. (2015) on neural mention-ranking coreference work.

- Additional comment: Consider evaluating on entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to assess generality.

- Additional comment: Minor edits needed (redundant word, baseline naming inconsistencies).
