- Model: learns sentential event-mention representations with CNNs and scores mention pairs via a lightweight similarity layer plus pairwise features, avoiding external KBs.  
- Results: competitive performance on ACE; introduces a larger ACE++ collection to improve recall.  
- Analysis: empirical study of argument overlap, trigger mismatch, and long-distance links motivates sentence-level context modeling.  
- Primary contribution: a strong, KB-free neural baseline for within-document event coreference with clear ablations showing sentential features' value.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical characterization of ACE/ACE++ that informs model design and evaluation.  
- Tertiary contribution: provision of a larger ACE++ evaluation set, though details are lacking.  
- Strength: clear, reproducible two-part architecture and well-written presentation.  
- Strength: ablations (Table 5) show sentential features add measurable gains over pairwise/lexical-only variants.  
- Strength: useful corpus and error analysis identifying pronoun/event anaphora and annotation ambiguity as key challenges.  
- Weakness: weak architectural motivation—no empirical comparison of CNNs vs. biRNNs/transformers/PCNN/self-attention or analyses of window/pooling choices.  
- Weakness: novelty unclear since sentence-CNNs are established; comparisons to prior continuous-space event-linking methods and stronger baselines are missing.  
- Weakness: ACE++ is under-specified—no protocol, event-type coverage, domain bias, inter-annotator agreement, or release plan reported.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies on gold event mentions; no end-to-end results, additional datasets (e.g., EventCorefBank), or joint entity–event experiments provided.  
- Weakness: feature transparency—which pairwise features drive gains, how were they selected, and which alternatives were tried remain unreported.  
- Questions for authors: justify CNN choice with comparisons to biRNNs/transformers and window/pooling analyses.  
- Questions for authors: provide ACE++ construction protocol, event-type/domain distribution, inter-annotator agreement, and release plan.  
- Questions for authors: report results without gold triggers/arguments and with off-the-shelf coref augmentation.  
- Questions for authors: specify which pairwise features are most important and any tried-but-abandoned alternatives.  
- Questions for authors: evaluate on EventCorefBank and/or in a joint entity–event coref setting to test generality.
