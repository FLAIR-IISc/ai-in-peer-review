- Resource-light neural model combining CNN sentential encoders with minimal lexical/pairwise features for within-document event coreference.
- Sentential features substantially improve performance over trigger-local and pairwise features; transitive-closure clustering outperforms BestLink here.
- Curated a larger ACE++ dataset and provided an error analysis highlighting difficult phenomena like pronominal event references.
- Strong results achieved without external KBs, suggesting suitability for low-resource or cross-domain settings.
- Systematic ablations demonstrate the empirical importance of sentential features and show BestLink reduces recall in this setup.
- Sensible metric choices (BLANC, B^3, MUC, positive links) and discussion of class imbalance improve result interpretation.
- Manual error analysis identifies pronoun-mediated references and borderline annotations as major error sources, suggesting discourse/pronominal modeling.
- Model is conceptually simple and reproducible (CNN encoder + learned similarity; TensorFlow implementation and hyperparameter search described).
- Comparisons may be unfair: evaluations use gold triggers and differing train/test splits versus some prior work.
- Several architectural details are underspecified (position embedding initialization/sharing, left/right context representation, ±1 token window rationale, use of ve, pairwise feature encoding).
- ACE++ dataset availability and lack of strong baselines on ACE++ limit external validity of reported ACE++ results.
- Novelty is incremental relative to prior neural coreference/extraction work; paper should better position itself and justify technical novelties (e.g., square nonlinearity).
- No statistical significance testing reported despite close scores across systems and settings.
- Questions on position-embedding learning/sharing and potential lexicalization of position features.
- Questions about left/right handling sensitivity and why the token window is exactly ±1 word.
- Clarify where the event-mention vector ve is used versus vsent+lex in the similarity computation.
- Specify exact encoding, normalization, and parameterization of pairwise features (binary vs. continuous, embeddings, trainable transforms).
- Justify and empirically compare the square activation against ReLU/tanh and cosine/dot-product, with significance.
- Will ACE++ and document splits be released, can results be reported on standard ACE splits, and can end-to-end results with predicted triggers be provided?
- Can the model generalize to entity coreference or joint entity-event coreference, and how does it compare to neural coref baselines (e.g., Wiseman et al., 2015)?
- Clarify cross-sentential claim with concrete evidence/ablations and cite/discuss Wiseman et al. (2015) and related neural coref work.
- Minor fixes: remove redundant “that,” standardize baseline naming (“same event” vs. “same type”), and report confidence intervals or bootstrap significance tests.
