- Summary: Proposes a two-part model for within-document event coreference using a CNN-based sentential encoder plus a pairwise scorer with hand-engineered features and transitive-closure clustering, reporting SOTA/on-par results on ACE and ACE++.

- Contribution — empirical: Shows strong event linking performance without external semantic KBs, suggesting sentential neural features can replace KB-derived features.

- Contribution — data analysis: Provides data-driven insights on ACE/ACE++ (sparse argument overlap, trigger mismatches, long-distance links) motivating sentential modeling over locality heuristics.

- Contribution — engineering: Presents a simple, effective architecture (CNN encoders, pairwise features, transitive closure) with ablations clarifying feature and clustering roles.

- Strength: Matches or surpasses feature-rich prior systems without external KBs, improving portability to domains lacking resources.

- Strength: Convincing corpus analysis demonstrating many coreferent pairs lack argument overlap or trigger similarity and are discourse-distant.

- Strength: Thorough ablations and diagnostics that isolate contributions of sentential, trigger-local, and pairwise features and justify transitive-closure clustering.

- Strength: Clear writing, multiple coreference metrics reported, hyperparameter details, and informative error analysis (pronoun issues, annotation inconsistencies).

- Strength: Simple, reproducible implementation using standard components (pretrained embeddings, Adam, TensorFlow).

- Weakness: Weak architectural motivation and comparisons — CNN choice is under-justified and lacks controlled comparisons to BiLSTMs, RCNNs, attention, or dependency-aware encoders and significance testing.

- Weakness: Novelty unclear relative to prior neural IE work; needs comparisons to continuous-space event coreference methods and stronger non-neural baselines.

- Weakness: ACE++ is poorly described (sourcing, annotation protocol, event distribution, IAA, domain mix) and its unavailability hampers reproducibility and validity of broader claims.

- Weakness: Evaluation relies on gold mentions/arguments; no end-to-end evaluation with predicted triggers/arguments, limiting realism and comparability.

- Weakness: Role of pairwise features underexplored — unclear which features matter, how selected, or whether alternatives were tried.

- Weakness: Squared similarity choice is only loosely justified; lacks systematic comparison to bilinear, cosine, absolute-value, or other similarity functions.

- Question: Why choose CNNs for mention encoding — can authors provide controlled comparisons to BiLSTM/RCNN/attention encoders with matched capacity and significance tests?

- Question: What exactly is ACE++ — please provide document collection details, annotation protocol/ontology, IAA, event-type distribution, domain coverage, and release/licensing plans.

- Question: How does the model perform end-to-end with predicted triggers/mentions (no gold arguments) from a standard event extractor, and what is the error propagation?

- Question: Which pairwise features contributed most; were alternatives like dependency paths, discourse cues, or lexical overlap variants evaluated and how were features selected?

- Question: Is the squared similarity critical — how does it compare to cosine, bilinear, or absolute-value activations under matched capacity?

- Question: Can incorporating an external entity/pronoun coreference system reduce pronoun-related errors; any preliminary experiments?

- Additional: Evaluate on other public event coreference resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) and consider joint entity/event coreference experiments.

- Additional: Report calibration (PR curves) and statistical significance versus baselines in Table 3.

- Additional: For reproducibility, release code, trained models, exact splits (including ACE++), and report training variance across seeds.

- Additional: Provide pipeline runtime and memory footprint to inform practical deployment.
