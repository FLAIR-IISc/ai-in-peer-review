- Paper uses CNNs to generate sentential features for event linking, avoiding external semantic resources, and reports state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005 and a new ACE++ dataset.  
- Demonstrates competitive event linking performance without external knowledge bases, improving portability and scalability.  
- Introduces the larger ACE++ dataset as a more substantial testbed for event linking research.  
- Provides a manual error analysis highlighting key challenges (pronoun resolution, annotation disagreement).  
- Strong experimental results, surpassing prior state-of-the-art on some metrics.  
- Model design choices are well-motivated, with dataset analysis justifying architecture decisions.  
- Thorough evaluation including ablation studies to measure contributions of feature classes.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures and tables.  
- Insightful error analysis that identifies limitations and future research directions.  
- Limited methodological novelty: CNN sentence representation is incremental in this context.  
- Unclear whether and when the ACE++ dataset will be publicly released, affecting reproducibility.  
- Results are sensitive to clustering strategy (transitive closure vs. BestLink) without a full explanation for the difference.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details are insufficiently described for full reproducibility.  
- Question: Will ACE++ be made publicly available, and if so, when?  
- Question: Why did BestLink perform worse than transitive closure; any hypotheses on model-strategy interaction?  
- Question: Have you considered using separate entity coreference systems or other techniques to address pronoun resolution?  
- Question: Please provide more details on the hyperparameter search strategy and number of configurations evaluated.  
- Additional: References are comprehensive and evaluation metrics are appropriate.  
- Additional: Consider adding qualitative examples of successful and failed linking decisions.  
- Overall: Solid paper with valuable contributions (performance without external resources, new dataset, error analysis); recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
