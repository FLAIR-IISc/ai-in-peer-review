- Paper proposes CNN-based sentential representations for event linking that avoid external lexical resources.
- Model combines CNN sentence features with pairwise features and a logistic regression classifier.
- Shows state-of-the-art or competitive performance on ACE and a newly created ACE++ dataset.
- Includes ablation studies demonstrating the contribution of different feature classes.
- Provides a manual error analysis highlighting pronoun resolution and annotator disagreement issues.
- Main contribution: effective event linking without relying on external semantic resources.
- Contribution: comprehensive experimental evaluation across two datasets.
- Contribution: detailed error analysis that suggests future directions.
- Strength: novel use of CNNs to learn contextualized event representations for coreference.
- Strength: strong empirical results, with notable gains on ACE and ACE++.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation that aids reproducibility.
- Strength: insightful error analysis revealing inherent task ambiguity.
- Strength: thorough ablation studies clarifying model component importance.
- Weakness: limited exploration of alternative architectures (e.g., RNNs, Transformers).
- Weakness: heavy reliance on the relatively small ACE dataset; ACE++ helpful but not sufficient for generalizability claims.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to recent end-to-end neural event extraction/linking models.
- Weakness: surprising finding that BestLink clustering underperforms transitive closure, requiring deeper analysis.
- Question: Why choose CNNs over RNNs/Transformers and were alternatives tried?
- Question: Plans to evaluate on additional, more diverse datasets to test generalizability?
- Question: Have you considered incorporating pronoun/entity coreference features to address pronoun errors?
- Question: Can you provide more analysis on BestLink’s failure modes and model confidence behavior?
- Question: Why was no direct comparison to end-to-end event extraction/linking systems included?
- Additional comment: clarify some notation/equations (e.g., Equation 2); reproducibility link is useful.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor revisions recommended to address architectural, dataset, and comparison concerns.
