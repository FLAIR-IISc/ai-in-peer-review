- Proposes CNN-based sentential feature learning for event linking, avoiding reliance on external semantic resources.  
- Combines CNN-derived sentence representations with pairwise features for coreference decisions.  
- Demonstrates competitive or state-of-the-art performance on ACE 2005 and a new, larger ACE++ dataset.  
- Releases the ACE++ dataset, contributing valuable data for the community.  
- Provides a thorough evaluation and insightful error analysis highlighting challenges like pronoun resolution and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Achieves strong empirical results and clear comparisons to established baselines.  
- Avoids dependence on external knowledge bases, improving portability and domain adaptability.  
- Presents a clear, well-structured model description with motivating design choices and equations.  
- Error analysis offers concrete directions for future work and acknowledges annotation subjectivity.  
- CNN architecture is fairly standard and lacks architectural novelty beyond the application to event linking.  
- Insufficient comparison to recent transformer-based or neural relation extraction models.  
- Model performance is sensitive to clustering strategy (BestLink vs. transitive closure) without a full exploration of causes.  
- Ablation study lacks granular analysis (e.g., filter sizes, pooling strategies) that could clarify contributions of design choices.  
- Requests clarification on which CNN design choices were crucial for gains and how the approach compares to transformers in efficiency and robustness.  
- Asks for explanation of clustering results, potential for graph-based clustering, and plans to address pronoun resolution errors.  
- Notes the paper is well-written, reproducible (document lists provided), and recommends releasing code and data; overall recommendation: accept.
