## Review of "Event Linking with Sentential Features from Convolutional Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a system for event linking, aiming to resolve coreferences between event mentions in text. The core idea is to leverage sentential features extracted using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to represent event mentions, combined with pairwise features for coreference decision making. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on two datasets (ACE and a newly created ACE++) and provide an error analysis.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Demonstration of Effective Sentential Feature Utilization:** The paper effectively demonstrates that sentential features, learned via CNNs, are a strong signal for event linking and can achieve competitive performance *without* relying on costly and often domain-specific external semantic resources like WordNet or FrameNet.
2. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors perform a thorough empirical evaluation on two datasets, including a new, larger corpus (ACE++), and conduct ablation studies to analyze the contribution of different feature sets.
3. **Error Analysis:** The inclusion of a detailed error analysis provides valuable insights into the limitations of the current approach and potential directions for future work.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The reported performance on ACE and, particularly, ACE++ is competitive with existing state-of-the-art methods. Achieving SOTA on a new, larger dataset (ACE++) is a significant achievement.
2. **Methodological Soundness:** The two-stage architecture – learning event representations followed by coreference decision making – is logically sound and well-motivated. The use of CNNs for capturing sentential context is appropriate and effectively implemented.
3. **Ablation Studies Provide Insight:** The ablation studies clearly demonstrate the importance of the different feature components (pairwise, lexical, sentential) and justify the design choices made by the authors. This is crucial for understanding *why* the model works.
4. **Detailed Error Analysis:** The error analysis isn’t just a cursory look at mistakes; it identifies specific challenges (e.g., pronoun resolution, annotation inconsistencies) and provides actionable insights for future research. This demonstrates a deep understanding of the task and the model’s behavior.
5. **Clear and Well-Written Paper:** The paper is generally well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. The motivation for the work and the details of the model are presented in a comprehensive manner.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core CNN Architecture:** While the *application* of CNNs to event linking with sentential features is valuable, the CNN architecture itself isn't particularly novel. The paper doesn’t present any significant innovations in the CNN design. This makes the contribution more incremental than groundbreaking.
2. **Dependence on Gold Event Mentions:** The reliance on gold-standard event mentions in the experimental setup, while acknowledged, limits the real-world applicability of the system. A fully end-to-end pipeline that jointly performs event extraction and linking would be more robust and practical.
3. **Potential for Dataset Bias:**  The authors note inconsistencies in the ACE++ annotations during the error analysis.  While this is acknowledged, the impact of these inconsistencies on the reported results isn’t fully quantified.  It’s possible the model is exploiting these annotation artifacts.
4. **Clustering Strategy:** The chosen transitive closure clustering strategy was outperformed by BestLink in other works, and the explanation provided for this discrepancy (the model already weeds out low-confidence links) feels somewhat speculative and could benefit from further investigation.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Annotation Consistency:** Can you provide more detail on the nature of the annotation inconsistencies observed in ACE++? Specifically, how did you identify these inconsistencies, and how might they be affecting the reported results?
2. **Pronoun Resolution:**  Given the identified difficulty with pronoun resolution, have you considered incorporating any explicit pronoun resolution mechanisms or features into your model?
3. **End-to-End Performance:** Do you have any preliminary results or plans for evaluating the system in a fully end-to-end pipeline that includes event extraction?
4. **CNN Architecture Exploration:** Did you experiment with different CNN architectures (e.g., deeper networks, different pooling strategies) during your development process? If so, could you briefly summarize those experiments and explain why you chose the final architecture?




**Additional Comments:**

* The reference list is comprehensive.
* The provided code link (for the document lists) is very helpful.
* The analysis of data properties (e.g., percentage of coreferential pairs sharing no arguments) is a strong point of the paper, justifying the design choices.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of event linking. While the core CNN architecture isn't novel, the effective application of sentential features, combined with a rigorous experimental evaluation and insightful error analysis, justifies acceptance. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the questions and weaknesses outlined above.
