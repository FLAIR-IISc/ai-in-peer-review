- Overall: Solid, well-executed application of CNN-based sentential features for event linking with strong empirical results; recommend accept with minor revisions.

- Contribution: Integration of sentential features via CNNs effectively captures contextual information for event linking without heavy external knowledge.

- Contribution: Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive performance on ACE 2005 and ACE++ datasets.

- Contribution: Provides thorough error analysis identifying key challenges like pronoun resolution and annotation inconsistencies.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated approach with justified design choices (CNN architecture, two-step process).

- Strength: Strong experimental evaluation including comparisons to prior work, ablation studies, two datasets, and clustering strategy analysis.

- Strength: Detailed qualitative error analysis offering insights for future work.

- Strength: Reproducibility aided by hyperparameter and data split details.

- Strength: Practical significance by reducing reliance on external knowledge sources, aiding domain/language portability.

- Weakness: Limited novelty in core methods—CNNs and pairwise/two-step architecture are established techniques.

- Weakness: Reliance on gold event mentions simplifies the task and limits realism of evaluation.

- Weakness: Dataset size (ACE 2005) is small, raising concerns about generalization to more diverse/larger datasets.

- Weakness: Pronoun resolution is identified as a major challenge but not explored with potential solutions.

- Question: Were pre-trained embeddings fine-tuned during training and what was the impact?

- Question: What is the computational cost of CNN-based feature extraction compared to hand-crafted features?

- Question: Why did BestLink perform worse than transitive closure for this model, contrary to prior findings?

- Question: Are there plans to integrate pronominal coreference techniques to address pronoun-related errors?

- Additional: Paper is well-written with adequate related work; suggest adding a hyperparameter summary table for reproducibility.
