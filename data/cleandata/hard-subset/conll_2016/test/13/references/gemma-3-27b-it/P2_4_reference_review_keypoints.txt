- Paper proposes a two-step event linking system using CNN-generated sentential features to reduce reliance on external semantic resources, reporting strong results on ACE and ACE++.
- Main contribution: shows competitive event linking can be achieved without extensive external semantic features, aiding portability and scalability.
- Main contribution: provides detailed ablation studies revealing the relative importance of pairwise, trigger-local, and sentential features.
- Main contribution: includes a comprehensive manual error analysis that identifies challenges like pronoun resolution and annotator disagreement.
- Strength: achieves strong empirical performance on standard (ACE) and larger (ACE++) datasets.
- Strength: well-motivated design explaining why sentential features and CNNs suit the task (distant mentions, limited argument overlap).
- Strength: thorough experimentation exploring clustering strategies, feature combinations, and evaluation metrics.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful diagrams illustrating the model.
- Strength: error analysis connects errors to inherent task difficulties and annotation ambiguity.
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—application of CNNs + logistic classifier rather than a radical new technique.
- Weakness: insufficient details about the ACE++ dataset creation, annotation quality control, and event type distribution.
- Weakness: model performance is sensitive to clustering strategy (BestLink vs. transitive closure) and needs deeper investigation.
- Weakness: comparisons to more recent neural approaches (e.g., transformers) are missing and would strengthen the paper.
- Question: request details on ACE++ annotation process, quality control, and event-type distribution relative to ACE.
- Question: ask for explanation of why the model effectively filters low-confidence links and which features/components enable this.
- Question: inquire whether transformer or other modern architectures were tried for sentential features and how they compare to CNNs.
- Question: suggest considering explicit pronoun/coreference resolution techniques to address pronoun-related errors.
- Additional comment: the ACEvalid/ACEtest document list link is appreciated.
- Additional comment: references are comprehensive.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with valuable empirical analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses and questions.
