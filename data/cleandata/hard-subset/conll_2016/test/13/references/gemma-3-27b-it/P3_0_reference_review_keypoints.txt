- Paper proposes a two-stage event linking system using CNNs to learn sentential features and a logistic regression classifier, avoiding heavy reliance on external semantic resources.  
- System achieves competitive/state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005 and a newly created ACE++ dataset, with accompanying error analysis.  
- Main contribution: demonstrating effective sentential feature learning for event linking without expensive knowledge bases.  
- Main contribution: creation and public release of the larger ACE++ dataset as a more challenging benchmark.  
- Main contribution: thorough empirical evaluation with baselines, ablations, and error analysis highlighting pronoun handling issues.  
- Strength: strong performance and competitive results compared to prior work using similar resources.  
- Strength: well-motivated design choices and useful initial data analysis showing many coreferential pairs lack shared arguments/triggers.  
- Strength: solid experimental methodology including dataset descriptions, hyperparameter tuning, and ablation studies.  
- Strength: error analysis identifies pronoun resolution and annotator disagreement as key challenges.  
- Strength: public release of ACE++ will benefit the research community.  
- Weakness: CNN architecture is fairly standard with limited architectural novelty and no exploration of transformers or RNNs.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of which sentence parts drive sentential features; no attention or feature-importance study.  
- Weakness: suboptimal clustering strategy (transitive closure) used despite known drawbacks; BestLink comparison inconclusive.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational cost for training and inference on large datasets.  
- Question: Were explicit pronoun resolution techniques or heuristics tried, and what were the effects?  
- Question: Would adding attention mechanisms to the CNN help, and was this explored?  
- Question: Why did BestLink perform worse here than in Liu et al. (2014); what system characteristics explain the difference?  
- Question: Details on ACE++ annotation process and annotator consistency compared to ACE 2005?  
- Question: How sensitive is model performance to key hyperparameters (embedding size, filter sizes)?  
- Additional comment: writing is clear overall but some sections (e.g., Related Work) feel rushed.  
- Additional comment: citations are comprehensive; consider adding a hyperparameter summary table.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper with valuable contributions (sentential feature approach and ACE++); recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
