- Presents an event linking system using CNN-derived sentential features without external lexical resources.  
- Combines local, sentential, and pairwise features for coreference decisions and reports SOTA on ACE and ACE++.  
- Demonstrates effective event linking without WordNet/FrameNet by relying on learned CNN representations.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation including two datasets, ablation studies, and error analysis.  
- Releases an expanded dataset (ACE++) to support event linking research.  
- Strong empirical results matching or exceeding prior state-of-the-art on ACE and improving on ACE++.  
- Design is well-motivated by observed challenges like missing shared arguments and the importance of sentential context.  
- Ablation studies clearly show the contributions of pairwise, lexical, and sentential features.  
- Error analysis identifies key issues such as pronoun resolution and annotator disagreement.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides reproducible detail with clear figures and tables.  
- Core methodology (CNNs + logistic regression) is not highly novel; main novelty is application to event linking.  
- Lacks comparison to stronger, more recent baselines using pre-trained language models (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa).  
- Sensitivity to BestLink clustering hurts recall; explanation is weak and needs deeper investigation.  
- Does not discuss computational cost of training and inference or scalability with long documents.  
- Question: Have you considered using pre-trained language models for embeddings or sentence encoding and their impact?  
- Question: Please analyze why BestLink clustering reduces recall and whether other clustering methods were tried.  
- Question: Any concrete ideas for addressing pronoun resolution within the event linking framework?  
- Question: Can you provide estimates of training/inference time and scaling behavior on ACE++?  
- Suggestion: Include ACEvalid/ACEtest document lists ( appreciated ) and a hyperparameter summary table.  
- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper with valuable contributions and analyses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
