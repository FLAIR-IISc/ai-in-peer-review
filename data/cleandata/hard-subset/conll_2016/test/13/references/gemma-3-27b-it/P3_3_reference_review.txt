## Review of "Event Linking with Sentential Features from Convolutional Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a system for event linking (coreference resolution between event mentions) that leverages sentential features extracted using convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The core idea is to represent each event mention with its surrounding sentence, processed by a CNN to capture contextual information, and then use these representations along with pairwise features for a coreference decision. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on two datasets (ACE and a newly created ACE++), and include an error analysis.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Demonstration of Effective Sentential Feature Learning for Event Linking:** The paper successfully demonstrates that CNNs can effectively learn sentential features that are valuable for event linking, achieving competitive performance without relying on external knowledge resources like WordNet or FrameNet. This is a significant step towards building more portable and domain-independent event linking systems.
2. **Creation and Release of the ACE++ Dataset:** The introduction of the ACE++ dataset, consisting of additional web documents annotated for event linking, provides a valuable resource for the research community.  Its larger size compared to ACE allows for more robust evaluation.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation and Error Analysis:** The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed model, including ablation studies and detailed error analysis, offering insights into the strengths and weaknesses of the approach.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The model achieves state-of-the-art (or competitive) results on both ACE and ACE++, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The performance gains over existing methods, particularly those relying on external resources, are noteworthy.
2. **Well-Motivated Design Choices:** The authors convincingly argue for the use of sentential features, highlighting the limitations of relying on external knowledge and presenting evidence that many coreferential event mentions lack shared arguments or trigger words, necessitating a more contextual understanding.  The data analysis presented in Section 3.1 is particularly strong.
3. **Clear and Detailed Methodology:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the model architecture, training procedure, and experimental setup. The inclusion of Figure 1 is helpful in visualizing the system.  The detailed explanation of feature engineering (Section 3.2) is commendable.
4. **Insightful Error Analysis:** The error analysis identifies key challenges in event linking, such as pronoun resolution and annotator disagreement, providing valuable directions for future research. The specific examples given are very illustrative.
5. **Publicly Available Dataset:**  The release of ACE++ is a valuable contribution to the community, fostering further research in event linking.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in the Core Methodology:** While the *application* of CNNs to learn sentential features for event linking is valuable, the underlying CNN architecture and training process are relatively standard. The core technical innovation is not groundbreaking in the field of neural networks. A stronger claim to novelty would require a more innovative architectural component.
2. **Sensitivity to Clustering Strategy:** The contrasting results with different clustering strategies (transitive closure vs. BestLink) raise concerns about the robustness of the overall system.  The paper doesn't fully explain *why* BestLink performed worse in their specific setup, leaving open questions about the optimal post-processing step.  More investigation into this area is warranted.
3. **Lack of Comparison to Stronger Neural Baseline:**  While comparisons to older systems are provided, a comparison to more recent neural network-based event linking systems (if they exist) would strengthen the paper.  Simply stating that the proposed method doesn't rely on external resources isn't sufficient to demonstrate superiority over other end-to-end neural approaches.
4. **Dataset Bias:** The ACE and ACE++ datasets focus on specific event types (Conflict, Movement, Life). The generalizability of the results to other domains and event types is not fully explored. The paper acknowledges the limitations of the dataset, but a more explicit discussion of potential domain adaptation challenges would be beneficial.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Clustering Strategy:**  Could you elaborate on why BestLink performed worse in your experiments compared to the findings of Liu et al. (2014)?  What specific characteristics of your model or dataset might explain this difference?
2. **Ablation Study Depth:** You mention experimentation with various hyperparameters, but the details are limited. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of different convolutional filter sizes or pooling strategies on performance?
3. **Pronoun Resolution:**  Given that pronoun resolution appears to be a significant source of error, have you considered incorporating explicit pronoun resolution mechanisms into the model (e.g., a separate pronoun resolution module)?
4. **Generalizability:** How do you anticipate your model would perform on event linking tasks in different domains (e.g., biomedical text, financial news)? What adjustments would be necessary?
5. **Computational Cost:** Can you provide more detail on the computational cost of training and inference, particularly the memory requirements of processing full sentences with CNNs?


**Additional Comments:**

* The code for the model and the ACE++ dataset should be made publicly available to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
* The references section is comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of event linking. The demonstration of effective sentential feature learning, the creation of the ACE++ dataset, and the thorough evaluation are all significant strengths. While the core methodology lacks substantial novelty, the practical impact and clear presentation make this a worthy contribution.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
