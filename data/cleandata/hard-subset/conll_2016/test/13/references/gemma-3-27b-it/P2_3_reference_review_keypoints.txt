- Summary: Proposes event linking using CNN-extracted sentential features plus pairwise features, showing SOTA on ACE and new ACE++ with error analysis.
- Contribution 1: Shows sentential features from CNNs are strong signals for event linking without external semantic resources.
- Contribution 2: Provides comprehensive experiments including a new larger ACE++ corpus and ablation studies.
- Contribution 3: Includes detailed error analysis highlighting limitations and future directions.
- Strength 1: Strong empirical results, achieving competitive/SOTA performance, especially on ACE++.
- Strength 2: Methodologically sound two-stage design (representation learning then coreference decision) with appropriate use of CNNs.
- Strength 3: Ablation studies clearly quantify the importance of pairwise, lexical, and sentential features.
- Strength 4: Error analysis identifies concrete challenges (pronoun resolution, annotation inconsistencies) and offers actionable insights.
- Strength 5: Paper is clear, well-organized, and well-written with solid motivation and model description.
- Weakness 1: CNN architecture itself lacks novelty; application is incremental rather than architecturally innovative.
- Weakness 2: Experiments rely on gold event mentions, limiting real-world applicability without an end-to-end pipeline.
- Weakness 3: Possible dataset bias from ACE++ annotation inconsistencies; impact on results not fully quantified.
- Weakness 4: Clustering strategy (transitive closure) underperforms alternatives like BestLink; explanation provided is speculative.
- Question 1: Please detail the annotation inconsistencies in ACE++—how identified and how they affect results.
- Question 2: Have you considered adding explicit pronoun resolution mechanisms or features?
- Question 3: Any preliminary results or plans to evaluate the system end-to-end including event extraction?
- Question 4: Did you experiment with alternative CNN architectures (depth, pooling), and why choose the final design?
- Additional: Reference list is comprehensive and code/document lists are helpful; analysis of data properties strengthens design choices.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses and questions.
