- Paper presents an event linking system using CNNs to learn sentential features, avoiding external semantic resources, and evaluates on ACE and a new ACE++ dataset with error analysis.  
- Main contribution: empirical evidence that CNN-learned sentential representations are effective for event linking without external knowledge.  
- Main contribution: release of a larger ACE++ dataset to provide a stronger benchmark.  
- Main contribution: detailed error analysis highlighting pronoun resolution and annotation ambiguity challenges.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive performance with clear ablation studies.  
- Strength: novel application of CNNs to generate sentential features for event linking, reducing reliance on lexical resources.  
- Strength: thorough experimental setup with train/dev/test splits, multiple baselines, and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: insightful and well-illustrated error analysis that identifies key failure modes.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—standard CNN architecture and training applied to a new task.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to recent transformer-based baselines (e.g., BERT), leaving relative performance unclear.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to clustering strategy (BestLink vs. transitive closure) is counterintuitive and underexplained.  
- Weakness: no discussion of computational cost or runtime compared to simpler baselines.  
- Question: will the authors compare to transformer-based models and report those results?  
- Question: please explain why transitive closure outperforms BestLink and provide illustrative examples.  
- Question: provide training/inference time estimates and comparisons to baseline costs.  
- Question: how do the authors plan to address pronoun resolution in future work?  
- Additional comment: include a link to the ACE++ dataset in the camera-ready version.  
- Additional comment: expand related work discussion to cover recent neural relation/event extraction advances.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with practical impact and valuable dataset release; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
