- Tackles within-document event coreference with a two-step model: CNN-based sentential mention representations plus pairwise features and a shallow similarity layer, clustered via transitive closure.
- Avoids external semantic resources (WordNet/FrameNet), relying on learned sentential features and simple lexical/pairwise cues.
- Achieves competitive or state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005 and an expanded ACE++ dataset, with detailed ablations.
- Main contribution: resource-light event coreference model using CNN sentential features that match prior SOTA without external semantics.
- Main contribution: empirical study on ACE++ with ablations showing the impact of sentential, lexical, pairwise features, and clustering strategies.
- Main contribution: error analysis and dataset statistics motivating design choices (many positives lack shared arguments, triggers differ, long-distance links common).
- Strength: Strong empirical performance with reduced feature engineering, compelling comparison to semantically-rich prior work (e.g., Liu et al. 2014).
- Strength: Well-motivated architecture (CNN sentence encoder with position embeddings and piecewise pooling) and clear, reproducible conceptual design.
- Strength: Thorough ablations and analyses quantifying feature/clustering contributions and dataset properties.
- Strength: Sensible evaluation choices (reporting BLANC) and candid discussion of caveats (splits, preprocessing).
- Strength: Error analysis identifies pronoun/anaphora and annotation ambiguities as key failure modes.
- Weakness: Methodological novelty is incremental; CNN + pairwise + clustering is a straightforward extension with modest tweaks (square activation).
- Weakness: Ambiguity about use of gold information (argument overlap/realis match) and fairness of comparisons across differing splits/preprocessing.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—ACE++, code, and trained models are not clearly released, limiting impact.
- Weakness: Limited global modeling—transitive closure can propagate local errors and no cluster-level or joint constraints are explored.
- Weakness: Evaluation scope is limited to within-document ACE/ACE++; no cross-document or end-to-end (predicted triggers/arguments) experiments and limited comparison to stronger encoders.
- Questions: Are gold event arguments used for argument overlap/realis match at test time, and how does this align with compared systems?
- Questions: Will ACE++ and code/trained models be released to enable replication?
- Questions: What are absolute numbers and sensitivity for similarity function comparisons (square vs cosine/bilinear) and dsim?
- Questions: How are multiple triggers in a sentence handled in piecewise pooling and is there context leakage between mentions?
- Questions: Were improvements over Liu et al. (2014) tested for statistical significance?
- Overall: Well-executed empirical paper with moderate novelty; acceptance recommended if gold-use clarifications and dataset/code release are provided.
