- Paper proposes a two-stage model for within-document event coreference: a CNN-based sentential encoder plus a pairwise classifier with squared-activation similarity and transitive-closure clustering, evaluated on ACE and ACE++.

- Contribution: Shows sentence-level CNN representations improve event coreference without external semantic resources, achieving competitive ACE results.

- Contribution: Provides ablation demonstrating complementary value of sentential and trigger-local features and compares clustering strategies (transitive closure vs BestLink).

- Contribution: Builds and uses a larger ACE++ dataset to study scaling effects and support hyperparameter selection.

- Strength: Clear data-motivated rationale: many coreferent pairs lack argument overlap/different triggers and are often far apart, motivating sentential context.

- Strength: Simple, well-engineered architecture with solid baselines and ablations isolating sentential feature contributions; pragmatic exploration of square nonlinearity.

- Strength: Competitive performance without external semantic resources, supporting portability across domains.

- Strength: Sensible evaluation choices (BLANC usage) and informative clustering comparison showing classifier-level precision control.

- Strength: Useful error analysis identifying event anaphora/pronominal references and annotation inconsistencies as bottlenecks.

- Weakness: Experimental realism concerns—likely reliance on gold event triggers, types, realis, and arguments inflates results and limits end-to-end conclusions.

- Weakness: Limited modeling novelty—CNN senti- encodings and pairwise classification with transitive closure are incremental; no global clustering optimization.

- Weakness: Reproducibility/dataset issues—ACE++ details, annotation quality, IAA, and public availability are unclear, preventing verification.

- Weakness: Evaluation lacks breakdowns by distance, trigger similarity, argument sharing, and no significance testing; clustering tuning is limited.

- Weakness: Potential feature leakage—strong pairwise features (type, realis, argument overlap) may overshadow sentential features if gold, or their predicted accuracy is unreported.

- Question: Which annotation layers are gold vs predicted at train/test (trigger spans, event types, realis/modality, arguments) and is entity coreference used for arguments?

- Question: Can you provide results with predicted triggers/types/arguments to estimate end-to-end performance impact?

- Question: How is modality/realis obtained and operationalized in pairwise features; what are the label sets and prediction accuracies if used?

- Question: What are ACE++ annotation protocol, annotators, IAA, and will the dataset or splits/guidelines be released?

- Question: Can you report performance breakdowns by sentence distance, same vs different trigger lemma, and shared vs no shared arguments to confirm sentential benefits?

- Question: Did you explore class-imbalance strategies beyond minibatch sampling, and how sensitive are results to thresholding and the square activation vs alternatives?

- Question: Any evidence left/right piecewise pooling outperforms standard max-pooling or PCNN-style pooling; include ablation on pooling choice.

- Suggestion: Report LEA in addition to BLANC/B3/MUC and include statistical significance testing.

- Suggestion: Discuss computational efficiency and scaling with document length and whether pruning antecedent candidates affects performance.

- Suggestion: If code cannot be released, provide detailed hyperparameters, preprocessing, and ACE splits for comparability.

- Suggestion/Future work: Explore global objectives or joint entity-event coreference to address pronoun/anaphora errors.

- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept — valuable empirical finding but tempered by reliance on gold annotations and limited novelty; realism and clarity improvements needed.
