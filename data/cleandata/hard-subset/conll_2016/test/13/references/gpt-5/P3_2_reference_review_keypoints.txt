- Proposes a two-step within-document event coreference model: sentence-level CNN encoder with trigger-local lexical features, plus a pairwise scorer using simple features and a “distributed similarity” layer; clustering via transitive closure.
- Claims: (1) CNN-derived sentential features can replace external semantic resources and achieve SOTA on ACE 2005; (2) provides extensive ablations and clustering/ error analyses; (3) introduces ACE++ to study data-scaling and shows recall gains.

Strengths
- Clear framing that sentential features reduce reliance on external resources, supported by ablation gains.
- Strong empirical results on ACE metrics and ACE++ demonstrating data-driven recall improvements.
- Thoughtful analysis comparing transitive-closure vs BestLink clustering and its precision/recall trade-offs.
- Detailed feature ablations showing pairwise, lexical, and especially sentential features all contribute.
- Error analysis identifies pronoun-mediated references and annotation inconsistencies as key failure modes.

Weaknesses
- Possible unrealistic supervision: likely reliance on gold triggers/types/arguments at test time; realism and impact on pipelines unclear.
- Limited modeling novelty: architecture largely standard; “square” similarity is a minor, under-justified tweak.
- Reproducibility concerns: ACE++ not clearly released and comparisons use different splits without significance tests.
- Baselines incomplete: lacks global/coreference-aware and stronger neural mention-ranking baselines for comparison.
- Missing implementation/evaluation details: hyperparameters, ablation of similarity functions, significance testing, runtime/complexity, and performance vs document length not reported.

Questions to authors
- Which annotations are gold vs predicted at test time (triggers, types, realis, arguments), and if predicted, which systems/accuracies were used; please report results with predicted annotations.
- What fraction of positive pairwise examples share event type/arguments, and how much does the model rely on type agreement vs sentential encoder?
- Provide quantitative comparisons of the “square” similarity vs ReLU/tanh/cosine and sensitivity analyses for dsim.
- Is ACE++ publicly available; if not, can you provide ACE-only stratified results to separate data-quantity from architectural gains?
- Were improvements tested for statistical significance across ACE metrics; if so, please report?

Additional comments
- Sentence-only CNN may miss cross-sentential/discourse cues; document-level/hierarchical encoders could address pronoun-mediated errors.
- Recommend reporting performance by sentence-distance buckets given prevalence of long-distance links.
- Suggest evaluating simple global clustering objectives (best-first thresholding, correlation clustering) fed by pairwise scores to better explore precision-recall trade-offs.
- Please clarify training example generation, class imbalance, and any negative sampling strategy.

Overall recommendation
- Borderline accept: valuable empirical contribution demonstrating CNN sentential features, but needs clarification on gold annotation usage, stronger/more global baselines, and reproducibility of ACE++ findings.
