- Paper addresses within-document event coreference by learning CNN-based sentential representations around triggers, combining them with trigger-local embeddings and pairwise features, and clustering via transitive closure.
- Claims state-of-the-art on ACE 2005 without external semantic resources; shows sentential features improve performance and analyzes errors and clustering strategies.
- Contribution: a practical event linking system that replaces heavy external resources with CNN-based sentential features while remaining competitive with prior SOTA.
- Contribution: empirical study showing sentential features’ utility via ablations and dataset-size analyses, and comparison of clustering strategies.
- Contribution: error analysis highlighting limitations (e.g., pronoun-mediated coreference) and annotation ambiguities to guide future work.
- Strength: achieves competitive/better F1 on multiple metrics without WordNet/FrameNet, improving portability to new domains.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated two-stage architecture (mention representation then pairwise scoring) and sensible piecewise pooling around triggers.
- Strength: convincing ablations showing sentential features substantially boost F1 and dataset-size study showing gains with more data.
- Strength: informative clustering comparison showing strong pairwise scoring can reduce need for complex downstream filtering.
- Strength: solid qualitative error analysis identifying pronoun-mediated links as a key failure mode.
- Strength: evaluation uses multiple coreference metrics and reports baselines and hyperparameter search.
- Weakness: limited technical novelty—standard CNN encoder and pairwise scorer adapted from existing relation/event extraction work.
- Weakness: unclear whether event type, realis, and argument features are gold or predicted, which affects realism and comparability.
- Weakness: ACE++ dataset details (annotation protocol, IAA, availability) are missing, limiting reproducibility and verification.
- Weakness: evaluation caveats—different ACE split, no significance testing, missing strong non-neural baselines, and no per-type/distance analyses.
- Weakness: model is purely local and lacks global inference, entity-event interactions, and explicit modeling of pronominal/nominal event anaphora.
- Question: Are event types, realis/modality, and arguments used in pairwise features gold or predicted—if predicted, report upstream model details and accuracy.
- Question: How is “argument overlap” computed (role match, head-string, coref-normalized entities) when arguments are missing?
- Question: How is severe class imbalance handled during training—loss reweighting, negative sampling, or threshold tuning?
- Question: Clarify ACE and ACE++ preprocessing: tokenization, sentence splitting, parsing, and any external NER/coref used to normalize arguments.
- Question: Provide ACE++ details: source selection, annotation protocol, IAA, and plans for releasing data or scripts.
- Suggestion: report significance tests and per-type/per-distance breakdowns to characterize where gains come from.
- Suggestion: include a strong non-neural baseline trained on the same features (without CNN) to isolate CNN contribution.
- Suggestion: discuss role of pre-trained embeddings and whether domain-specific embeddings affect results.
- Suggestion: explore lightweight global inference or cluster-level representations to mitigate over/under-merging.
- Overall: solid empirical paper with modest novelty showing CNN sentential features can substitute for external semantics, but clarity on gold features and reproducibility would strengthen the work.
