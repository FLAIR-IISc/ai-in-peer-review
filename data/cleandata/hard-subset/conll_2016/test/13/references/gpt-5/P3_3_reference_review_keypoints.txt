- Proposes a two-step within-document event coreference model: sentence-level CNNs with position and trigger-local embeddings, plus a pairwise scorer using lightweight features, squared-activation similarity, logistic classifier, and transitive-closure clustering.

- Claims comparable/better performance to prior SOTA on ACE without WordNet/FrameNet, provides ablations showing sentential feature gains, introduces an ACE++ corpus, and supplies error analysis.

- Empirically strong and resource-lean: achieves SOTA BLANC/MUC/positive-link F1 on ACE without external semantic resources.

- Sensible sentential modeling: piecewise pooling around triggers captures mention-local and sentential cues when arguments are sparse or missing.

- Clear ablations and analysis: isolates contributions of pairwise, trigger-local, and sentential features and compares BestLink vs transitive-closure behavior.

- Thoughtful error analysis: identifies hard cases like pronominal event anaphora and annotation ambiguity that current models struggle with.

- Appropriate experimental metrics: uses BLANC for class-imbalance and reports multiple coreference metrics while discussing dataset differences.

- Heavy reliance on gold annotations (triggers/types/arguments) weakens end-to-end practical relevance and complicates fair comparisons.

- Domain portability claims are unsupported: no cross-domain evaluation and still depends on pretrained word2vec.

- ACE++ dataset is non-public and under-described (no guidelines, IAA, or release plan), limiting reproducibility and verification.

- Limited document-level modeling despite many long-distance links; clustering is simple transitive closure with no global consistency/modeling.

- Evaluation/analysis gaps: no statistical significance testing, unclear thresholding/calibration of pairwise scores, weakly motivated/quantified square activation choice, and no discussion of O(n^2) scaling or negative sampling.

- Which gold annotations are assumed at inference (triggers, types, arguments)? Please report results with predicted annotations.

- How is the pairwise decision threshold chosen and are scores calibrated?

- How is class imbalance handled during training (loss weighting/sampling) and what positive/negative ratio is used per minibatch?

- Provide ACE++ annotation details: guidelines, event-schema alignment with ACE, annotator training, IAA, and plans to release the data or derived labels/features.

- Did you try multi-sentence or document-level encoders given many long-distance links; how do simple context extensions affect performance?

- Quantify and justify the square activation versus alternatives (ReLU/abs/cosine) and whether the similarity layer learns interpretable facets.

- Paper is well written; please clarify whether code/hyperparameters will be released and include statistical tests for key comparisons.
