- Task and model: Proposes within-document event coreference using a CNN sentence encoder for mentions plus a pairwise scorer with standard features and transitive-closure clustering.
- Avoids external resources: Claims competitive/state-of-the-art performance on ACE 2005 without WordNet/FrameNet and introduces a larger ACE++ dataset.
- Main empirical finding: Sentential CNN features combined with simple pairwise features match or exceed prior SOTA without external semantics, reducing domain dependence.
- Empirical analysis: Provides careful ablations (feature classes, clustering strategies), dataset size effects (ACE vs ACE++), and a thoughtful error analysis.
- Dataset contribution: Constructs a larger ACE++ collection and reports useful statistics motivating design (argument sparsity, trigger variability, long-distance links).
- Motivation clarity: Quantifies argument sparsity (~51% no shared arguments), lexical variability (~47% different triggers), and long-distance links (~55% >5 sentences), justifying sentence-level encoding.
- Competitive results: Achieves comparable or better BLANC/MUC F1 than semantic-rich baselines while dropping external semantics.
- Thoroughness: Systematic studies show sentential features are critical and BestLink clustering reduces recall in their setting.
- Simplicity and reproducibility: Straightforward architecture (CNN + shallow pairwise head) with clear design choices (position embeddings, piecewise pooling, squared similarity).
- Evaluation choices: Uses BLANC to mitigate negative-link skew and reports multiple metrics with transparent split caveats.
- Reliance on gold annotations: All results use gold event mentions (and possibly gold arguments), inflating performance versus end-to-end pipelines.
- Limited methodological novelty: Model adapts existing CNN and pairwise scoring ideas; main novelty is empirical rather than algorithmic.
- Reproducibility concerns: Unclear whether ACE++, code, and preprocessing will be released, hindering verification and follow-up work.
- Comparability issues: Results confounded by different splits and use of gold vs predicted arguments; stronger or controlled baselines would help.
- Limited global modeling: Pairwise decisions with transitive closure lack global inference or document-level discourse modeling, likely hurting pronoun and ambiguous cases.
- Question: Are gold event arguments used for the argument-overlap feature, or is an extractor used—what are end-to-end results without gold arguments?
- Question: Can you report results with predicted triggers to quantify end-to-end robustness?
- Question: Will ACE++, code, and models be released; if not, why and can derived resources be shared?
- Question: How sensitive are results to encoder choice (CNN vs BiLSTM or hybrids)?
- Question: How was the decision threshold (0.5) selected and was it tuned for BLANC F1?
- Question: Any evidence of cross-domain generalization (train/test ACE vs ACE++ without fine-tuning)?
- Overall evaluation: Solid empirical paper with clear motivation and thorough analyses; limited novelty and reliance on gold annotations make this a moderate accept pending clarifications and resource release.
