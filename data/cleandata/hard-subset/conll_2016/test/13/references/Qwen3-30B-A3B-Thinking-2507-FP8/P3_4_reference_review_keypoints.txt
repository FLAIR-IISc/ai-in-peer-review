- Problem: event linking (coreference) is important because ~23% of ACE 2005 event mentions are argument-incomplete.
- Approach: CNN-based sentential feature generator for event mentions that avoids external semantic resources (e.g., WordNet, FrameNet).
- Model: two components — a CNN for sentence-level event representations and a pairwise classifier for coreference decisions.
- Contribution 1: resource-light method that achieves comparable performance without external semantic resources.
- Contribution 2: detailed analysis of event mention characteristics (e.g., 50.97% pairs share no arguments; 55.42% >5 sentences apart).
- Contribution 3: comprehensive evaluation showing feature-class impacts and thorough error analysis.
- Strength: strong empirical justification for the problem via rigorous ACE 2005 corpus analysis.
- Strength: well-designed evaluation and ablation studies that clarify feature contributions.
- Strength: practical value in eliminating dependence on external semantic resources for new domains.
- Strength: careful comparison with prior work and supported claims on the ACE++ benchmark.
- Weakness: the "state-of-the-art" claim is questionable given an unfair comparison to Liu et al. (2014)'s resource-rich model.
- Weakness: limited handling of discourse-level phenomena, notably pronoun resolution, causing many false negatives.
- Weakness: insufficient justification and annotation detail for the ACE++ dataset added to ACE 2005.
- Weakness: limited novelty of the CNN + pairwise classifier architecture relative to recent neural coreference methods.
- Question: clarify exact experimental setup and comparability with Liu et al. (train/test splits, gold mentions).
- Question: specify concrete strategies to address pronoun/discourse-level coreference beyond vague "discourse structure" mentions.
- Question: provide annotation process details for ACE++ to ensure consistency with ACE 2005.
- Overall assessment: solid, practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to temper the "state-of-the-art" claim and justify ACE++.
