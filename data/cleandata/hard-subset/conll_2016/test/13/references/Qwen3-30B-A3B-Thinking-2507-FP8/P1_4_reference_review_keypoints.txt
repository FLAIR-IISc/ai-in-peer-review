- Contribution: Introduces a CNN-based method that uses sentential features for event linking, avoiding external semantic resources.
- Contribution: Provides a comprehensive ablation analysis of pairwise, trigger-local lexical, and sentential feature classes.
- Contribution: Extends ACE 2005 with 1,351 web documents (ACE++), showing increased training data improves recall.

- Strength: Demonstrates sentential features capture context where arguments or triggers differ (50.97% no shared arguments, 47.29% different triggers).
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art BLANC F1 on ACE 2005 (73.31%) and ACE++ (76.90%) without 105 prior semantic features.
- Strength: Thorough ablation study showing progressive gains from pairwise → trigger-local → sentential features (56.69 → 64.12 → 76.90 BLANC F1).
- Strength: Creation of ACE++ dataset with empirical evidence that more data boosts recall (75.16% → 83.21%).

- Weakness: Fails to compare against recent neural/transformer-based event coreference models (e.g., BERT-based approaches).
- Weakness: Error analysis is brief and insufficiently systematic, limiting insight into model failure modes.
- Weakness: Omits discussion of computational complexity and runtime cost of the CNN approach versus prior methods.
- Weakness: Clustering strategy and claims about confidence-based link pruning are not well-justified or evidenced.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison with stronger/simple neural baselines (e.g., sentence embeddings from BERT).

- Question: How does the CNN approach compare empirically to recent transformer-based models for event coreference?
- Question: Can the authors detail the 17 false negatives attributed to annotator disagreement and how common such ambiguities are?
- Question: Can you provide evidence (e.g., confidence score distributions) to support the claim that the model weeds out low-confidence links?
- Question: Please elaborate and justify the choice of the square function as network non-linearity over other activations.
- Question: Given 55.42% of coreferential mentions are >5 sentences apart, how does the model handle long-range dependencies and what architectural fixes might help?

- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions contingent on addressing the noted comparisons, analyses, and justifications.
