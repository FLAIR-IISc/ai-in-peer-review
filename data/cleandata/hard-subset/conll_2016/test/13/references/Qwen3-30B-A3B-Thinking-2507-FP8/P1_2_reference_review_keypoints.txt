- Neural event-linking model using sentential CNN features achieves state-of-the-art without external semantic resources (WordNet/FrameNet).
- Data analysis: ~50.97% of coreferential mentions share no arguments and ~47.29% have different trigger words, motivating sentential features.
- Two-step architecture: CNN-based event representation learning followed by a coreference decision module.

- Practical contribution: shows external semantic resources are unnecessary and improves cross-domain deployability.
- Thorough ACE 2005 analysis that strongly supports the methodological choice for sentential features.
- Clear ablation study demonstrating each feature class meaningfully contributes to performance.
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple metrics (BLANC, MUC, B-CUBED, positive links) and datasets (ACE, ACE++).

- Unfair comparison to Liu et al. (2014) due to differing clustering strategies (BestLink vs. transitive closure), weakening claimed superiority.
- Insufficient implementation/hyperparameter details, harming reproducibility.
- Limited error analysis (only 100 examples) is too small to fully characterize model weaknesses.
- Inconsistent evaluation protocols across prior work (different partitions, preprocessing, use of gold mentions) complicate comparisons.

- How would the model perform with BestLink clustering and would that close the gap with Liu et al.?
- Please provide the exact ACE train/test split used to enable reproducibility.
- Why does the pairwise+sentential-only model yield higher precision but lower recall than the full model?
- Provide details on the 17 false negatives attributed to annotator disagreement and whether those disagreements are systematic.

- Overall: well-written and valuable contribution, but needs more implementation details, reproducibility information, and deeper error analysis before publication.
