- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based model that generates sentential features for event coreference resolution and a pairwise classifier, claiming state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005/ACE++ without external semantic resources.

- Contribution 1: Introduces CNN-generated sentential features for event linking, arguing external semantic resources are unnecessary for strong performance.

- Contribution 2: Provides a thorough ablation study quantifying each feature class’s contribution.

- Contribution 3: Shows that increasing training data (ACE++) substantially improves recall for event linking.

- Strength 1: Addresses an important gap by avoiding reliance on domain-specific semantic resources, improving potential cross-domain applicability.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results showing competitive or superior performance to prior work (e.g., Liu et al. 2014) without external resources.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive ablation study demonstrating sentential features are the most impactful; removing them greatly reduces F1.

- Strength 4: Practical implications highlighted: easier generalization with more annotated data rather than building new semantic resources.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient novelty—uses CNN sentential features similar to prior neural relation/event extraction work and lacks clear differentiation from recent neural approaches.

- Weakness 2: Limited cross-domain evaluation—tests only on ACE-derived datasets, so cross-domain claims are unproven.

- Weakness 3: Incomplete comparison with recent state-of-the-art neural models (e.g., attention-based architectures) for coreference/event linking.

- Weakness 4: Superficial error analysis—analysis of 100 false positives/negatives is too small and lacks diagnostic depth (e.g., why pronoun errors occur).

- Weakness 5: Questionable interpretation of clustering strategy results—claims about BestLink vs. transitive closure lack fair comparative evidence on confidence calibration.

- Question 1: How were dataset split and preprocessing differences handled to ensure a fair comparison with Liu et al. (2014)?

- Question 2: How would the model perform on a truly different domain (e.g., medical or legal) with no prior annotation or external resources?

- Question 3: How does the proposed model differ from recent neural relation-extraction approaches (e.g., Zeng et al., dos Santos) and why might those be less effective for event linking?

- Additional comment 1: Paper is technically solid with valuable empirical evidence, but needs clearer positioning relative to recent neural work.

- Additional comment 2: The paper would be strengthened by a cross-domain evaluation and deeper analysis of model failures and architectural limitations.
