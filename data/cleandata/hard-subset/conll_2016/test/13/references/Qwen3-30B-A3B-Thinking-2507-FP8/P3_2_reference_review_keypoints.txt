- Summary: Proposes a two-step event coreference model using CNN-generated sentential features plus pairwise features, evaluated on ACE 2005 and new ACE++, achieving strong results without external semantic resources; error analysis highlights pronoun resolution issues.

- Contribution — neural sentential features: Shows CNN-based sentential features can replace external semantic resources for event linking, achieving strong performance.

- Contribution — feature analysis: Provides comprehensive ablation studies showing the relative importance of pairwise, trigger-local, and sentential features.

- Contribution — dataset: Extends ACE 2005 with ACE++, adding 1351 annotated web documents as a resource for future work.

- Strength — motivation: Strong problem motivation with ACE statistics and clear argument against reliance on costly, limited-coverage semantic resources.

- Strength — experiments: Thorough experimental evaluation and comparisons to prior systems, with ablations and error analysis supporting claims.

- Strength — error analysis: Detailed, example-driven error analysis that acknowledges ambiguous cases and annotator disagreements.

- Weakness — baselines: Lacks comparison to recent neural coreference models (e.g., Lee et al. 2017; Joshi et al. 2017; Clark & Manning 2016), weakening the state-of-the-art claim.

- Weakness — ACE++ details: Provides minimal information about ACE++ annotation procedures, inter-annotator agreement, selection criteria, and quality control.

- Weakness — architectural justification: Insufficient justification or comparison for choosing CNNs over other architectures (RNNs/LSTMs/Transformers); no ablation of alternatives.

- Weakness — efficiency: No discussion of computational complexity, training/inference time, or memory requirements for real-world scalability.

- Question — comparisons to recent neural coref: How does your model compare to/why prefer it over recent neural coreference approaches adapted to event linking?

- Question — ACE++ annotation details: What guidelines, annotator counts, inter-annotator agreement, document selection, and QC procedures were used for ACE++?

- Question — CNN design rationale: How does your CNN architecture differ from or improve upon prior sentential/relation models (e.g., Zeng et al., Nguyen & Grishman)?

- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing weaknesses in camera-ready (add comparisons to recent neural baselines, provide ACE++ annotation details, justify architecture and discuss efficiency).
