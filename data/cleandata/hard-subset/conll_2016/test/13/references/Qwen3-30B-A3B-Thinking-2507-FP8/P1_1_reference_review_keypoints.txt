- Proposes a CNN-based model that generates sentential features for event mentions to perform event coreference without external semantic resources, evaluated on ACE 2005 and a new ACE++ dataset with reported state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution: shows external semantic resources (WordNet/FrameNet) are not necessary for high-performing event coreference when using CNN-based sentential features.

- Contribution: provides a systematic empirical analysis of feature classes (pairwise, trigger-local lexical, sentential) and their positive contributions to performance.

- Contribution: compares clustering strategies (transitive closure vs. BestLink) and finds transitive closure empirically superior for their model.

- Strength: makes a significant methodological claim that CNN-based sentential features can replace expensive external semantic resources and generalize across domains.

- Strength: delivers a comprehensive experimental evaluation across ACE and ACE++, with detailed feature, dataset-size, clustering analyses and useful error analysis.

- Strength: presents a clear, well-motivated two-step model design (representation learning then coreference decision) with transparent rationale.

- Weakness: comparisons to prior work may be unfair due to differing data splits and preprocessing, undermining the state-of-the-art claim.

- Weakness: insufficient detail about the ACE++ dataset creation and annotation protocol, leaving dataset quality and comparability unclear.

- Weakness: inadequate discussion of limitations and future remedies, particularly regarding pronoun resolution failures and why the model struggles there.

- Question: request for the specific ACE document splits used to enable reproducibility and fair comparison.

- Question: ask for detailed annotation procedures, guidelines, and annotator expertise for the 1351 ACE++ web documents.

- Question: ask for more insight into why the model weeds out low-confidence links (making BestLink unnecessary) and whether this is a general property of CNN-based coreference models.

- Additional: paper is well-structured and experimentally thorough, but would be strengthened by addressing fairness of comparisons and by providing more ACE++ documentation.

- Additional: the claim that lack of semantic resources can be overcome is important but needs more rigorous comparisons accounting for preprocessing and split differences.
