- Summary: Proposes a two-step CNN-based event linking system using sentential features to remove reliance on external semantic resources, achieving state-of-the-art on ACE 2005 and ACE++.

- Contribution: Introduces a neural event-linking approach that eliminates domain-specific external resources.

- Contribution: Provides comprehensive empirical validation showing improved performance over prior work.

- Contribution: Offers thorough feature ablation and error analysis, highlighting pronoun resolution as a key challenge.

- Strength: Eliminates dependency on costly external semantic resources, improving domain portability.

- Strength: Strong empirical results with detailed ablation studies demonstrating each feature class’s contribution.

- Strength: Insightful error analysis identifying pronoun resolution and annotation boundary disagreements.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to more recent neural event-coreference models (e.g., RNNs/transformers).

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset construction and annotation process lack sufficient justification and quality-control details.

- Weakness: Paper omits discussion of computational efficiency, training, and inference costs relative to prior methods.

- Question: Please detail the ACE++ annotation process, alignment with ACE 2005 scheme, and quality control.

- Question: How does the model compare to recent neural/transformer-based event-coreference approaches?

- Question: Provide computational complexity and runtime (training/inference) comparisons to previous approaches.

- Overall assessment: Strong, practical, domain-agnostic contribution with convincing results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, dataset justification, and efficiency.
