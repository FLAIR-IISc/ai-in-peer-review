- Proposes a CNN-based event coreference system using sentential features and a pairwise classifier, aiming to avoid external semantic resources.

- Contribution: Demonstrates state-of-the-art event linking performance without external semantic resources, easing domain transfer.

- Contribution: Builds and validates ACE++ by adding 1351 web documents to ACE 2005 to create a larger training set.

- Contribution: Provides comprehensive data/model analysis, including coreference distance stats and a detailed error analysis.

- Strength: Strong empirical evidence and ablation studies showing CNN-generated sentential features suffice for high performance.

- Strength: Thorough data analysis highlighting issues like incomplete event arguments and long-range coreference distances.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation with multiple metrics, strong baselines, ablations, and comparison of clustering strategies.

- Strength: Insightful manual error analysis identifying pronoun resolution and annotation ambiguities as key challenges.

- Strength: Practical contribution of ACE++ (1950 vs 599 docs) with demonstrated recall improvements from more data.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—architecture is similar to prior relation-extraction CNN approaches and not clearly differentiated.

- Weakness: Insufficient description of ACE++ creation—document selection, annotation guidelines, and agreement metrics are missing.

- Weakness: Overstates elimination of external resources while using pre-trained Word2Vec embeddings.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to recent neural/transformer-based coreference or event extraction approaches.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of limitations and generalizability, e.g., cross-domain and cross-document applicability.

- Question: Clarify distinction between "external semantic features" and pre-trained word embeddings—what counts as external semantics?

- Question: How were the 1351 ACE++ documents selected and annotated, and what were the annotation guidelines and inter-annotator agreement?

- Question: Why does the model beat Liu et al. (2014) on BLANC, MUC, and positive-links but lag on B-CUBED—what does that imply?

- Question: Have you considered integrating discourse parsing or specialized pronoun-resolution methods to address pronoun-related errors?

- Question: How should we interpret "55.42% of coreferential mentions are more than five sentences apart" relative to typical news article length and task difficulty?
