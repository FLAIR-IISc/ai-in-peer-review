- Proposed a CNN-based two-step model that generates sentential features for event linking without external semantic resources.  
- Model processes surrounding sentences with CNNs to create mention representations, then combines them with pairwise features for coreference decisions.  
- Achieves strong empirical results on ACE 2005 and a larger ACE++ dataset constructed by the authors.  
- Main contribution: shows event linking can be done effectively without WordNet/FrameNet-style resources.  
- Main contribution: provides a systematic ablation study quantifying the contributions of pairwise, trigger-local lexical, and sentential features.  
- Main contribution: creates and evaluates on ACE++, adding 1351 web documents to ACE 2005 for larger-scale evaluation.  
- Strength: clear motivation—23% of ACE 2005 event mentions are argument-incomplete, motivating cross-sentential inference.  
- Strength: practical impact in removing dependency on scarce external semantic resources for cross-domain applicability.  
- Strength: rigorous evaluation and detailed error analysis identifying hard cases like pronoun resolution.  
- Strength: clear technical presentation with reproducible implementation details and useful architectural figure.  
- Strength: statistically significant state-of-the-art performance on ACE and improved results on ACE++.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to more recent neural coreference/event linking methods (e.g., Clark & Manning 2016; Lee et al. 2017).  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational complexity and training/inference efficiency.  
- Weakness: inadequate details on ACE++ creation and annotation consistency with ACE 2005.  
- Weakness: "state-of-the-art" claim is overstated given differing experimental setups and limited baselines.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of broader model limitations (e.g., handling very long-distance dependencies and complex discourse).  
- Question: How does the model compare empirically to more recent neural coreference approaches that also avoid external resources?  
- Question: Provide detailed annotation methodology and consistency checks for the 1351 web documents in ACE++.  
- Question: Have discourse-level features or discourse parsing been considered to address pronoun resolution errors?  
- Question: How does the model handle coreferences more than five sentences apart, and can you give error examples?  
- Question: Why does transitive-closure clustering outperform BestLink for this model, and what does that imply about confidence score quality?  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, conditional on addressing comparisons to recent neural methods, ACE++ details, and efficiency concerns.
