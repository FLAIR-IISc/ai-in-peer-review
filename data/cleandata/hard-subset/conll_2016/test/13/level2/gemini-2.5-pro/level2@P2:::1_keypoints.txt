1. CNN-based model for event coreference that combines trigger-local lexical features, sentence-level CNN features, and standard pairwise features to score mention pairs.  
2. Claims state-of-the-art performance on ACE 2005 and ACE++ without relying on external knowledge bases (WordNet/FrameNet).  
3. Main contributions: novel application of CNNs to event linking, SOTA results without external KBs, and a thorough empirical analysis (ablations, clustering, error analysis).  
4. Strength: reduces reliance on external knowledge, improving portability to new domains without curated resources.  
5. Strength: strong empirical results—outperforms a system using 105 semantic features; ablation study shows each feature class helps.  
6. Strength: comprehensive analysis, including clustering strategy comparison and insightful manual error analysis identifying challenges like pronominal event anaphora.  
7. Weakness: limited architectural novelty—the CNN setup largely follows prior relation-extraction work, novelty is mainly in application.  
8. Weakness: "distributed similarity" layer and use of square activation are poorly motivated and lack empirical comparison to other interaction functions.  
9. Weakness: SOTA comparison is inconsistent—different preprocessing/data splits and use of gold arguments versus competitors’ predicted arguments weaken the claim.  
10. Question: please justify the square function choice and report experiments with alternative combination methods (e.g., element-wise product or subtraction).  
11. Question: how could the sentence-level CNN be extended to capture discourse-level phenomena needed for pronominal event resolution?  
12. Question: how would the model perform end-to-end with predicted triggers/arguments, and do sentential features confer robustness to upstream errors?
