- Proposes a two-stage neural model for within-document event coreference that replaces hand-engineered features with CNN-derived sentential representations plus local lexical and pairwise features.
- Contribution: Shows a neural approach achieves state-of-the-art event linking without external KBs (WordNet/FrameNet), improving portability to low-resource domains.
- Contribution: Provides a thorough empirical evaluation including an ablation study quantifying the impact of pairwise, trigger-local, and CNN-derived sentential features.
- Strength: Addresses dependency on external semantic resources by learning features directly from text, increasing scalability and generalizability.
- Strength: Achieves competitive or state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005, outperforming a strong hand-engineered baseline.
- Strength: Offers comprehensive analysis and error analysis (e.g., pronoun resolution), validating design choices and identifying future challenges.
- Weakness: Limited architectural novelty—CNN sentence encoder largely adapted from prior relation classification work and not clearly positioned relative to existing models.
- Weakness: No comparison or justification versus other sentence-encoding architectures (e.g., LSTMs/GRUs), weakening the argument for CNNs.
- Weakness: ACE++ dataset description is insufficient—missing document sources, annotation guidelines, and inter-annotator agreement details needed to assess dataset quality.
