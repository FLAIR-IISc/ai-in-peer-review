- Proposes a CNN-based within-document event coreference model that combines sentential CNN encodings, local lexical embeddings, and pairwise features into a similarity network.
- Evaluated on ACE 2005 and a newly annotated larger ACE++ dataset, achieving state-of-the-art performance on ACE without external semantic resources.
- Contribution: shows learned sentential features can replace rich hand-crafted semantic features for event linking, improving domain portability.
- Contribution: attains competitive/SOTA F1 scores (e.g., BLANC, MUC) on ACE despite not using the prior 105 semantic features.
- Contribution: introduces the larger ACE++ dataset and analyzes the effect of increased training data on generalization.
- Strength: well-motivated research direction addressing reliance on domain-specific resources and promoting portability.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation including comparisons, ablation studies, clustering analyses, and training-data impact experiments.
- Strength: strong and convincing empirical results supporting the paper’s main claims.
- Strength: insightful manual error analysis highlighting challenges like pronominal event references and annotation ambiguities.
- Weakness: limited architectural novelty—CNN design largely adopted from relation classification literature.
- Weakness: insufficient justification for choosing a CNN over architectures (e.g., LSTMs) potentially better for long-range dependencies.
- Weakness: under-specified pairwise features (e.g., “Overlap in arguments” and “(Bagged) distance” are ambiguous), hurting reproducibility.
- Weakness: unclear whether ACE++ will be publicly released, which limits reproducibility and community impact.
- Question: please justify the choice of CNN vs alternative encoders like LSTMs for capturing long-range context.
- Question: please provide implementation details for pairwise features, specifically “Overlap in arguments” and “(Bagged) distance.”
- Question: will the ACE++ annotated documents be made publicly available?
