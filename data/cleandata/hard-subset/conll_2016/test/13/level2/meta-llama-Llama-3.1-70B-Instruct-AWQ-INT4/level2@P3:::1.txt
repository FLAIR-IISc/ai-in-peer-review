**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a model for event linking that uses sentential features from convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to resolve coreferences between event mentions in a document. The model consists of two parts: the first part generates a representation for a single event mention, and the second part calculates a coreference score for a pair of event mentions. The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, ACE and ACE++, without relying on external semantic features.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to event linking that uses sentential features from CNNs to resolve coreferences between event mentions.
2. The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, ACE and ACE++, without relying on external semantic features.
3. The paper provides an error analysis that highlights the challenges of event linking and identifies areas for further research.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to event linking that uses sentential features from CNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, ACE and ACE++, which demonstrates its effectiveness.
3. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the model's performance, including an error analysis that highlights the challenges of event linking.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the event mentions are already identified and annotated, which may not always be the case in real-world applications.
2. The model relies on a specific architecture and hyperparameter settings, which may not be optimal for all datasets or tasks.
3. The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models or approaches, which would be useful for understanding the model's strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions to Authors**
