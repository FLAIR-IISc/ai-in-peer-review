- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based sentential feature approach for event linking with a two-part system (single-event representation + pairwise coreference scoring) and reports SOTA on ACE and ACE++.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel event-linking method using CNN sentential features without external semantic resources.
- Contribution 2: Designs a two-part architecture that produces per-event representations and computes pairwise coreference scores.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on ACE and ACE++ datasets.
- Strength 1: Avoids reliance on external semantic features that have limited domain coverage.
- Strength 2: Effectively captures event semantics and contextual information essential for linking.
- Strength 3: Empirically validated on two datasets with strong results.
- Weakness 1: No detailed error analysis (missing false positive/negative examples and insights).
- Weakness 2: Lacks comparisons to other neural-network-based approaches.
- Weakness 3: Does not detail hyperparameters or their optimization procedure.

- Question 3: Please supply a detailed discussion of hyperparameters and how they were optimized.
