- Proposes a CNN-based sentential feature model for event linking to resolve coreferences between event mentions in a document.  
- Model comprises per-mention representation and pairwise coreference scoring components.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on ACE and ACE++ without using external semantic features.  
- Novel sentential CNN approach is a significant contribution to event linking.  
- Empirical effectiveness demonstrated by top performance on two benchmark datasets.  
- Includes a thorough performance and error analysis highlighting event-linking challenges.  
- Assumes event mentions are pre-identified and annotated, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Depends on a specific architecture and hyperparameter settings that may not generalize.  
- Does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models for contextualization.  
- How will the model be extended to handle unannotated/unidentified event mentions?  
- Have alternative architectures or hyperparameter searches been explored for robustness?  
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparative analysis versus other leading approaches?  
- Results are promising but limitations and further research directions need to be addressed.  
- Authors should explore other architectures/hyperparameters and include more thorough comparisons.
