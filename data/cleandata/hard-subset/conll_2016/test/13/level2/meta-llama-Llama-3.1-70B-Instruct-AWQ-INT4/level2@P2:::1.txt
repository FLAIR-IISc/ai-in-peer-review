**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to event linking, a task that involves resolving coreferences between recognized event mentions in a document. The authors design a system that uses sentential features from convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to model event mentions, without relying on external semantic features. The system consists of two parts: the first part generates a representation for a single event mention, while the second part calculates a coreference score for pairs of event mentions. The authors evaluate their approach on two datasets, ACE and ACE++, and achieve state-of-the-art performance on both datasets.

3. The authors evaluate their approach on two datasets, ACE and ACE++, and achieve state-of-the-art performance on both datasets.
