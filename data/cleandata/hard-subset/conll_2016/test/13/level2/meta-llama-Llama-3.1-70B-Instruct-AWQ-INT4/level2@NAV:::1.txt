**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach for event linking, a crucial task in information extraction, using sentential features from convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The model consists of two parts: the first part generates a representation for a single event mention, while the second part takes two event-mention representations and pairwise features to calculate a coreference score. The authors claim that their approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, one of which is publicly available, without relying on external semantic features.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel approach for event linking using sentential features from CNNs, which achieves state-of-the-art performance without relying on external semantic features.
2. The model is designed to handle the challenges of event linking, including the need to consider both local and sentential features, as well as the problem of pronoun resolution.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a novel and effective approach for event linking, which achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets.
2. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the model's performance, including an error analysis that highlights the challenges of pronoun resolution.
3. The paper demonstrates the usefulness of neural models for generating latent-feature representations for sentences.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on the ACE 2005 dataset, which may not be representative of other domains or datasets.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art systems, which makes it difficult to evaluate the model's performance in a broader context.
3. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the model handles cases where there are multiple event mentions in a single sentence.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the model handles cases where there are multiple event mentions in a single sentence?
2. How do you plan to extend the model to handle cross-document event linking?
3. Can you provide more information on the error analysis, including the types of errors that were most common and how the model can be improved to address these errors?

**Additional Comments**

The paper presents a novel and effective approach for event linking, which achieves state-of-the-art performance without relying on external semantic features. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art systems and a clearer explanation of how the model handles cases where there are multiple event mentions in a single sentence. Additionally, the authors could provide more information on the error analysis and how the model can be improved to address the challenges of pronoun resolution.
