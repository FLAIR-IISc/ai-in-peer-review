- Proposes a novel CNN-based sentential feature approach for event linking without external semantic features.  
- Designs a two-part model: single-event representation and pairwise coreference scoring.  
- Evaluates on ACE and ACE++ and reports state-of-the-art performance.  
- Contribution: CNN sentential-feature representation for event mentions.  
- Contribution: Two-stage model architecture for representation and coreference scoring.  
- Contribution: Empirical SOTA results on two benchmarks.  
- Strength: Avoids reliance on external semantic features, improving domain robustness.  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation across two datasets with detailed performance analysis.  
- Strength: Includes error analysis highlighting task challenges.  
- Weakness: Assumes event mentions and semantic types are pre-identified, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art models.  
- Weakness: Unclear how model handles cross-sentence or cross-paragraph event mentions.  
- Question: How does the model handle event mentions across sentences or paragraphs?  
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other SOTA methods and their trade-offs?  
- Question: How will the model be extended to joint trigger identification and coreference resolution?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and valuable but needs clarifications and broader comparisons.
