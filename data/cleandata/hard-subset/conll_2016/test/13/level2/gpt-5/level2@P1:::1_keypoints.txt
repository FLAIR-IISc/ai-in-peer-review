- Summary: Proposes a resource-lean CNN sentential encoder plus pairwise scorer for within-document event coreference, producing clusters via transitive closure and achieving competitive SOTA on ACE/ACE++ without external KBs.  
- Contribution 1: Demonstrates a CNN-based sentential representation for event mentions that attains strong performance without WordNet/FrameNet.  
- Contribution 2: Empirically shows sentential context is crucial for event linking and compares clustering strategies (transitive closure vs. BestLink).  
- Contribution 3: Provides practical evaluation on ACE and ACE++ with ablations and error analysis.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance on ACE/ACE++ (BLANC F1 73.31 / 76.90) without external semantic resources.  
- Strength: Clear evidence that sentential features substantially improve linking (ablation numbers show large gains).  
- Strength: Thoughtful modeling choices (piecewise pooling, square activation) and informative clustering comparison.  
- Strength: Useful manual error analysis highlighting pronoun/coreference and annotation variability issues.

- Weakness: Unclear which upstream annotations (event type, realis, arguments) are gold vs. predicted, potentially overstating end-to-end applicability.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—many components adapt established neural IE techniques rather than introduce new coreference models.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns: differing ACE splits, missing significance tests, limited ACE++ details, and lack of released code/hyperparams.  
- Weakness: Potential overfitting risk given long training (2000 epochs) on small data and incomplete regularization reporting.  
- Weakness: Narrow evaluation scope—uses gold triggers, no predicted-trigger/end-to-end or cross-document experiments.

- Question: Which upstream annotations are gold vs. predicted at test time (event type, realis, arguments), and can results with predicted labels be reported?  
- Question: Please provide full hyperparameters, validation curves, regularization/early-stopping details, and evidence of overfitting control.  
- Question: What is ACE++’s annotation protocol, IAA, availability, and will it be released for reproducibility?  
- Question: Were significance tests conducted for ACE and ablations (e.g., bootstrap over documents); if not, can they be added?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the square similarity activation and to each pairwise feature (type, realis); can you provide pairwise-feature ablations?

- Additional comment: Report BestLink vs. transitive closure under a fixed operating point and include precision-recall curves.  
- Additional comment: Add experiments with predicted triggers/arguments from a standard ACE extractor to show practical relevance.  
- Additional comment: Consider alternative encoders (biLSTM/attention) or joint modeling with entity coreference/argument prediction to address pronoun errors.  
- Additional comment: Release code, model configs, and the exact ACE split used to aid adoption.

- Overall: Solid empirical paper with clear analysis; addressing gold-vs-predicted inputs and reproducibility would materially strengthen impact.
