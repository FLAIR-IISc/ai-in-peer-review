- Proposes a two-stage within-document event coreference model: CNN-based sentential mention encoders + learned pairwise similarity and transitive-closure clustering.  
- Contribution 1: Shows sentence-level neural representations can replace external semantic resources while achieving competitive/SOTA results on ACE 2005 and ACE++.  
- Contribution 2: Provides careful empirical analysis: ablations (pairwise vs lexical vs sentential), clustering comparisons, dataset-size effects, and baselines.  
- Contribution 3: Offers error analysis highlighting major failure modes (e.g., pronoun-mediated links, annotation edge cases).  
- Strength: Empirically strong results with reduced reliance on external semantic resources, outperforming prior resource-heavy systems on several metrics.  
- Strength: Modeling choices are well-justified (sentence-level CNNs, positional/lexical cues, square similarity) and aligned with data properties.  
- Strength: Thorough ablation studies showing the importance and interactions of sentential and lexical features; dataset-size study underscores scalability.  
- Strength: Finding that transitive-closure clustering outperforms BestLink in this setup is notable and interpretable.  
- Strength: Clear problem framing and use of complementary metrics (BLANC, B^3, MUC, positive-link) with discussion of metric validity.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether event types, modality/realis, and argument overlaps used as pairwise features are gold or predicted, which may inflate performance.  
- Weakness: ACE++ corpus lacks sufficient description (annotation protocol, IAA, license) and may hinder reproducibility if not released.  
- Weakness: Missing standard coreference metrics (CEAF/CoNLL average) and no threshold-sensitivity analysis for the 0.5 link decision.  
- Weakness: Limited document-level context—sentence-only encoding struggles with pronoun-mediated links, suggesting need for discourse/entity signals or wider context.  
- Weakness: Architectural novelty is incremental; main contribution is empirical/integrative rather than a new algorithm.  
- Question: Are event types, realis, and argument spans/roles gold or predicted, and if predicted, which tools and accuracies were used?  
- Question: Will ACE++ (documents, annotations, splits) be released, and can annotation protocol and IAA be provided?  
- Question: How are multi-token triggers handled in position embeddings and pooling, and how are multiple mentions per sentence disambiguated?  
- Question: How was the 0.5 decision threshold chosen—tuned on validation—and can precision–recall or threshold sensitivity be reported?  
- Question: Can quantitative comparisons for the square activation vs cosine, ReLU/tanh, bilinear, etc., be added to the ablation table?  
- Suggestion: Add CEAF metrics and the CoNLL average to improve comparability with broader coreference work.  
- Suggestion: Provide per-type results and performance by sentence distance and trigger agreement to show where sentential features help most.  
- Suggestion: Consider lightweight document-level extensions (nearby-sentence attention, entity coreference features, joint entity-event modeling) to address pronoun links.  
- Suggestion: Release code, trained models, and ACE split lists; if ACE++ cannot be released, provide a detailed conversion pipeline or subset for reproducibility.
