- Demonstrates CNNs can learn sentential features for event linking, removing dependence on external knowledge bases.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on ACE and competitive results on the larger ACE++ dataset.  
- Provides a thorough experimental evaluation including ablations and comparison to baselines.  
- Introduces ACE++ as a larger, more challenging dataset for evaluation.  
- Includes a detailed error analysis that pinpoints weaknesses like pronoun resolution.  
- Outperforms existing approaches on ACE and is competitive on ACE++, showing strong empirical performance.  
- Novel feature engineering by replacing KB-based features with learned CNN sentential features, improving portability.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, with architecture explained and illustrated.  
- Error analysis gives actionable insights for future work, especially on pronoun/coreference issues.  
- Ablation studies and baseline comparisons convincingly show component contributions.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details are limited, reducing reproducibility (search space and justification not provided).  
- Datasets (ACE, ACE++) may be biased or limited in domain, raising questions about generalization.  
- Lacks comparison to more recent neural event-linking methods, limiting benchmarking completeness.  
- The chosen transitive-closure clustering strategy is potentially suboptimal and contradicts prior findings (BestLink discrepancy).  
- Heavy reliance on gold event mentions may mask issues in event trigger identification and end-to-end performance.  
- Request for detailed hyperparameter tuning procedures, search space, and rationale.  
- Ask authors to explain why BestLink underperformed compared to transitive closure in their experiments.  
- Inquire about robustness to event trigger identification errors and potential end-to-end evaluation with a trigger detector.  
- Request commentary on computational cost relative to baselines.  
- Suggest exploring mechanisms to address pronoun resolution (e.g., attention or coreference integration).  
- Recommend expanding related work discussion to include recent neural event-linking advances.  
- Suggest adding a dataset statistics table (event counts, avg. sentence length) for clarity.  
- Advise improving notation consistency (clarify symbols like 'dc' and 'dw').
