- Proposes a novel event linking model that uses sentential features from convolutional neural networks (CNNs).  
- Motivated approach leverages contextual information encoded by CNNs, aligning with neural trends in relation/event extraction.  
- Main contribution: competitive, state-of-the-art performance on two datasets without external semantic features.  
- Secondary contribution: thorough empirical evaluation including ablation studies for pairwise, lexical, and sentential features.  
- Ablation studies clarify the contribution of each model component.  
- Error analysis provided, highlighting pronoun resolution as a notable challenge.  
- Paper strengths: clear presentation, comprehensive experiments, and strong baseline comparisons.  
- Authors demonstrate strong understanding of related work and articulate advantages of their approach.  
- Weakness: performance gains over existing methods are modest.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational complexity (training and inference costs), especially for the CNN component.  
- Weakness: does not discuss potential dataset biases (ACE and ACE++) that could affect performance.  
- Weakness: error analysis could be expanded with more examples and systematic error categorization.  
- Questions raised: provide computational cost comparisons to baselines.  
- Questions raised: investigate potential biases in ACE and ACE++ datasets.  
- Questions raised: consider incorporating coreference resolution or other techniques to address pronoun resolution.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
