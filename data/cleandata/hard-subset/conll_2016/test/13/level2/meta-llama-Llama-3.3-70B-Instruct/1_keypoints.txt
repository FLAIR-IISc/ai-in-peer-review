- Proposes an event coreference model using sentential features from CNNs, avoiding external semantic features.  
- Model has two parts: single-event mention representation and pairwise coreference scoring.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, including a public benchmark.  
- Novel approach that eliminates reliance on external semantic features.  
- Empirically effective, outperforming prior methods on evaluated datasets.  
- Includes error analysis highlighting pronoun resolution and boundary-case challenges.  
- Dependence on quality of event mention recognition and argument identification.  
- Potentially poor performance on small or domain-diverse datasets.  
- Lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models.  
- Question: How does the model handle inaccurate event mention recognition or argument identification?  
- Question: Can the model be adapted for cross-document event coreference?  
- Question: How does the model perform with limited training data or across diverse domains?
