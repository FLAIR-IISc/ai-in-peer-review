- Proposed CNN-based event coreference model that generates sentential features without external semantic resources and uses a trainable similarity function with lexical/pairwise features; achieves state-of-the-art on ACE 2005 and ACE++.

Contributions:
- Domain-independent event linking model that attains competitive performance without external semantic features.
- Use of CNNs to produce sentential features that capture document-level context for event mentions.
- Thorough evaluation on multiple datasets (including ACE++) and an error analysis highlighting challenges like pronoun resolution.

Strengths:
- Novel and practical approach adaptable to diverse domains due to independence from external resources.
- State-of-the-art or competitive performance compared to methods that rely on extensive external features.
- Comprehensive evaluation with detailed ablation studies isolating contributions of feature classes.
- Insightful error analysis pointing out pronoun resolution issues and annotation inconsistencies.
- Reproducible: sufficient implementation details and hyperparameters provided.

Weaknesses:
- Limited comparison to simpler non-neural baselines in terms of computational efficiency and scalability.
- Struggles with pronoun resolution and lacks proposed remedies or concrete future directions to address it.
- Evaluation uses gold-annotated event mentions, which may overestimate real-world performance with noisy detectors.
- Focus restricted to within-document coreference; cross-document applicability is unexplored.

Questions to authors:
- How does the model perform when integrated into a full pipeline with automatically detected (non-gold) event mentions?
- Could discourse-level features (rhetorical structure, entity coreference chains) help mitigate pronoun resolution errors?
- Have you considered extending the approach to cross-document event coreference, and what changes would be needed?

Overall assessment:
- Significant, well-written contribution with minor revisions recommended to address identified weaknesses and clarify broader applicability.
