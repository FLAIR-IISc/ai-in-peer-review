- Summary: Proposes a resource-light CNN-based event coreference model using sentential features, achieves SOTA on ACE and ACE++, and flags pronoun resolution as a key challenge.

- Strength: Demonstrates practical significance by matching/exceeding methods that use external semantic resources, improving domain adaptability.

- Strength: Rigorous ablation study shows sentential features are essential, with the full model reaching 76.90% BLANC F1 on ACE++.

- Strength: Error analysis meaningfully identifies pronoun-based coreference failures and suggests directions like discourse structure integration.

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset construction lacks detail on annotation methodology and inter-annotator agreement, harming reproducibility and result interpretation.

- Weakness: Comparison to Liu et al. (2014) is unclear because dataset/train-test split alignment is not specified, reducing fairness of the comparison.

- Weakness: Pronoun failure mode is underexplored—no analysis of why CNN sentential features fail for long-distance or discourse-dependent pronouns.

- Question: How were the 1351 web documents in ACE++ annotated—provide guidelines, annotator training, and inter-annotator agreement scores?

- Question: Did you use the same ACE train/test document splits as Liu et al. (2014); if not, provide the exact splits used?

- Question: For pronoun errors, does the model fail to identify pronoun referents or to assess event scope, and would discourse parsing/coreference-chain features help?
