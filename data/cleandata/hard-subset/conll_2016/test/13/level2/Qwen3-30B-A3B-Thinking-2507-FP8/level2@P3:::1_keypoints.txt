- Summary: Two-step neural model uses CNNs to create sentential event representations plus a pairwise scoring function, achieving strong results on two datasets without external semantic resources.

- Strength: Avoids reliance on external semantic resources (WordNet/FrameNet), enabling broader, domain-agnostic applicability.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation with ablation studies and detailed error analysis; shows improved generalization with more training data (ACE++).

- Strength: Model design is data-motivated (many coreferent pairs lack shared arguments) and effectively combines local and sentential features.

- Strength: Demonstrates neural models can learn useful latent sentential features for information extraction tasks.

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset construction is under-documented—annotation process, guidelines, and quality control are not provided.

- Weakness: Claimed state-of-the-art is undermined by methodological inconsistencies in comparisons (different train/test splits, preprocessing, use of gold mentions).

- Weakness: Novelty claim about sentential features is overstated; insufficient differentiation from prior relation-extraction work (Zeng et al., Nguyen & Grishman).

- Weakness: Architecture (CNN + similarity) is conventional and not justified against alternatives (e.g., RNNs); design choices not sufficiently motivated.

- Question: Provide details on ACE++ annotation: how the 1351 web documents were annotated, guidelines used, and how disagreements were resolved.

- Question: How should the community assess the SOTA claim given varying train/test splits and preprocessing across compared systems?

- Question: How does using sentential features for event linking specifically differ from Zeng et al. (2014) and Nguyen & Grishman (2015), and what task-specific adaptations were needed?

- Additional: Pronoun resolution limitation should be better contextualized within broader event coreference challenges.

- Additional: Annotator disagreement on some false negatives suggests annotation guidelines may need refinement.
