- Proposes a CNN-based model that uses sentential features for event linking without external semantic resources (WordNet/FrameNet).  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on ACE and ACE++ datasets without external semantic features.  
- Ablation study (Table 5) shows sentential features provide the largest performance improvement.  
- Thorough error analysis (Section 6.2) identifies pronoun resolution as a major limitation.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by achieving competitive results without domain-specific external resources.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art baselines is undermined by inconsistent dataset partitioning.  
- Paper does not adequately contrast with recent neural approaches (e.g., RNNs, attention), limiting assessment of novelty.  
- The “state-of-the-art” claim is weakened by lack of comparison to the most recent neural event-linking methods.  
- Question about scalability: how the model performs on documents with many event mentions.  
- Question about adaptation to cross-document event linking and handling longer-range dependencies.  
- Overall: solid, domain-agnostic contribution that would be stronger with improved comparative methodology and better contextualization among recent neural advances.
