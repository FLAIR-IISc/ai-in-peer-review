- Clear, well-structured manuscript with straightforward presentation.  
- Strong analysis of argument sharing among coreferent events in the ACE corpus.  
- Useful examination of dataset size effects motivating the new ACE++ dataset.  
- Major concern: No clear motivation for choosing a CNN to generate mention representations or discussion of pros/cons versus alternatives (e.g., RNNs).  
- Major concern: Modest 2–3 point F1 gains require stronger justification that the proposed architecture is superior.  
- Major concern: Novelty of using sentential features is unclear; authors must position work relative to other continuous-space models and stronger baselines.  
- Major concern: Baseline selection (Table 3) is not comprehensive enough to establish improvements.  
- Major concern: ACE++ dataset collection/composition details are missing; scope, event-type coverage, and potential biases must be described and the dataset should be released for reproducibility.  
- Major concern: Need empirical demonstration that the model is less dependent on domain-specific resources (e.g., show cases where resource-dependent methods fail).  
- Minor issue: Table 3 should report performance without gold references for a more realistic evaluation.  
- Minor issue: Consider integration with standard entity coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) and discuss how that signal could be used.  
- Minor issue: Evaluate on additional benchmarks (e.g., EventCorefBank) to demonstrate robustness and generalizability beyond ACE.  
- Minor issue: Pairwise features appear important (Table 5); include an ablation study analyzing feature importance, selection rationale, and other feature configurations.
