- Proposes a CNN with piecewise max-pooling for event-mention representations plus pairwise features and a squared-activation similarity layer, achieving strong within-document event coreference results on ACE2005 and ACE++ without external KBs.
- Main contribution: a resource-light neural architecture that matches prior state-of-the-art while avoiding handcrafted semantic resources, improving portability across domains.
- Main contribution: thorough empirical evaluation, including an ablation study, creation of the ACE++ dataset, and insightful error analysis.
- Strength: matches/exceeds prior work without external knowledge bases, making the approach more generalizable.
- Strength: principled integration of pairwise, trigger-local, and sentential features; ablation shows each feature class contributes positively.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments showing gains from more data, informative clustering-strategy comparison, and valuable error analysis highlighting pronoun problems and annotation issues.
- Weakness: experimental comparability is limited because the model is evaluated with gold triggers/arguments while some prior work uses system-predicted inputs, potentially biasing comparisons.
- Weakness: fragmentation of ACE dataset splits (authors use/release their own split) complicates fair benchmarking and tracking progress.
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported for small performance differences, making SOTA claims uncertain.
- Weakness: some model design choices lack justification—trigger-local window size of one is unexplained.
- Weakness: use of the squared activation in the similarity layer is not well motivated or empirically compared to standard activations.
- Question: clarify how position embeddings are initialized, trained, and whether they are lexicalized or shared by relative position.
- Question: clarify whether the final representation used is v_e (hidden layer output) or vsent+lex (input), due to an apparent inconsistency in the paper.
- Question: how are pairwise features encoded (binned one-hot vs scalar) and are those encodings fixed or learned?
- Question: will the ACE++ dataset be released publicly to enable standardized comparisons?
- Additional suggestion: discuss applying the model to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to broaden impact.
- Additional suggestion: cite related neural entity coreference work (Wiseman et al., ACL 2015).
- Minor issue: naming inconsistency—baseline called "same event" in text but "same type" in Table 6.
