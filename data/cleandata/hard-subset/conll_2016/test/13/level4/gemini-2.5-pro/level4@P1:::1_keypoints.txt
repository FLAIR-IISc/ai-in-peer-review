- Paper proposes a neural event coreference model using a CNN over sentence words with piecewise max-pooling around the trigger and local lexical features, scored pairwise for coreference.

- Main contribution: a portable neural architecture combining sentence-level CNN features with local lexical and pairwise features to match state-of-the-art without external KBs.

- Strength: achieves competitive/SOTA performance without relying on external semantic resources like WordNet or FrameNet.

- Strength: demonstrates complementary value of sentential CNN features, local lexical context, and traditional pairwise features (supported by ablation study).

- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation on ACE and an expanded ACE++ dataset, plus analysis of dataset size, clustering strategies, and error analysis.

- Weakness: experimental comparability concerns—uses gold-standard triggers, which is unrealistic and complicates comparison to systems using predicted triggers.

- Weakness: differing train/test splits of ACE in prior work make reported score comparisons unreliable; authors should use or acknowledge standard splits.

- Weakness: unclear model details—position embeddings generation and learning are vaguely described and need clarification.

- Weakness: arbitrary design choice—local lexical context limited to one word on each side without justification or experiments for window size.

- Weakness: confusing notation/usage—final event vector v_e introduced but possibly unused; need to clarify which representation is used in similarity scoring.

- Weakness: reproducibility issues—major experiments use a private ACE++ dataset, limiting reproducibility and preventing fair baseline comparisons on that data.

- Question: clarify which event representation (v_e or v_sent+lex) is input to the similarity function in Section 3.2.

- Question: explain how position embeddings are initialized, updated, and whether they depend on the trigger or are relative-position-only.

- Question: detail encoding of pairwise features (e.g., how non-binary features like distance are represented) and whether they are learned or fixed.

- Question: justify or contextualize the use of a square non-linearity in the similarity layer and whether this is novel or transferable to other tasks.

- Question: will the authors release the ACE++ dataset to enable reproducibility and comparisons?

- Additional: suggest situating work relative to entity coreference literature (e.g., Wiseman et al. 2015) and potential application to OntoNotes.

- Additional: recommend reporting statistical significance tests for small performance differences.

- Additional: note minor textual inconsistency between Table 6 caption ("same type") and main text ("same event").
