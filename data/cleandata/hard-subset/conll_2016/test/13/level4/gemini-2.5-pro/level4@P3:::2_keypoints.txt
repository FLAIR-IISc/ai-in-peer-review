- Proposes a neural within-document event coreference model that replaces external KB features with learned sentential features, using a CNN encoder and a pairwise scorer; reports state-of-the-art on ACE 2005 and a new ACE++ dataset.

- Contribution: demonstrates that learned distributed representations enable domain-independent event linking without curated lexical-semantic resources.

- Contribution: introduces a larger ACE++ dataset and shows model recall improves substantially with more training data.

- Strength: reduces dependency on external knowledge bases, improving portability to new domains.

- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation, including ablation studies and experiments on ACE++, supporting the model’s effectiveness and data-scaling behavior.

- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with useful dataset analysis and architecture diagram.

- Weakness: insufficient motivation for using a CNN over sequential models (e.g., RNNs); no ablation/comparison to alternative encoders.

- Weakness: novelty is limited in the context of existing neural representation methods and competing baselines in Table 3 are incomplete.

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset lacks documentation on sourcing, annotation, and event-type distribution; reproducibility and validity are unclear without release.

- Question: have you considered augmenting or comparing the model with an off-the-shelf anaphora resolver to handle pronominal event references?

- Question: please detail the pairwise feature selection process—which engineered features were most impactful and which were discarded?

- Question: how robust is the model in an end-to-end pipeline with predicted triggers/arguments, and how sensitive is it to upstream extraction errors?
