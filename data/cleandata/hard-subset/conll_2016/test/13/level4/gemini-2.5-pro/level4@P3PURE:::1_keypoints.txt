- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that combines CNN-derived mention vectors, context embeddings, and pairwise features in a single-layer NN, matching prior feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution: a neural approach that fuses word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Strength: limited feature set that avoids manually-crafted external resources.

- Use of gold trigger words is a major concern; results with predicted triggers are needed for fair comparison.
- Non-standard train/test splits impede direct comparison; evaluate on established splits for reproducibility.

- Cross-sentential information evidence is convincing, but a key statement (lines 65–70) is unclear and needs clarification.
- Position embeddings are under-specified—clarify initialization, lexicalization, and why relative positions share embeddings across words.
- Explain how left/right neighbors are incorporated beyond max-pooling—are they used elsewhere in the model?
- Justify the choice of appending only one word before and after the trigger instead of other window sizes.
- Clarify the role of event-mention representation v_e and how/if it is used relative to v_{sent+lex} in the final model.
- Section 3.2 must detail encoding of pairwise features, handling of continuous features (e.g., distance), and whether these representations are trainable.

- Consider applying the approach to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with broader prior work.
- Discuss the square-function non-linearity: is it novel, what are its properties, and is it applicable elsewhere?
- State whether the ACE++ dataset will be released; if not, provide baseline comparisons on that dataset.
- Include statistical significance testing because many reported results are numerically close.
