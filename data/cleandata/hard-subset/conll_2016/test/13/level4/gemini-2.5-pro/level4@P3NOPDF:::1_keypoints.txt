- Strength: Proposes a neural hybrid for event coreference that reduces hand-crafted features and shows that adding a small set of linguistic features to embeddings yields a substantial boost.

- Major concern: Uses gold event triggers for training/testing, limiting real-world applicability and making comparisons with systems using predicted triggers unfair; recommend reporting results with predicted triggers.

- Major concern: Uses a non-standard train/test split, hindering reproducibility and comparability; urge use of established dataset splits.

- Clarification: Rephrase/elaborate the unclear concluding sentence about the need for cross-sentential information (lines 65–70).

- Clarification: Describe position embeddings in detail—initialization, training, whether they are lexicalized, and why relative-position embeddings are shared across anchors.

- Clarification: Explain how left and right context neighbors are used to build the final representation—are left/right distinctions only through column selection in max-pooling or is there another mechanism?

- Clarification: Justify the choice of appending one word before and after the trigger; indicate if window size was chosen empirically or via ablation.

- Clarification: Resolve the apparent inconsistency between v_e and v_{sent+lex} (is v_e unused or a typo?).

- Clarification: Detail integration of pairwise features—encoding of binary features, handling/discretization/normalization of continuous features, and whether these feature representations are learned or fixed.

- Suggestion: Consider applying the model to nominal/pronominal entity coreference to compare with more neural coreference systems (e.g., OntoNotes benchmarks).

- Suggestion: Discuss the use and novelty of the square-function non-linearity, its properties, and potential applicability to other NLP tasks.

- Suggestion: Release the ACE++ dataset or at least report baseline results on it to improve reproducibility and comparisons.

- Suggestion: Include statistical significance testing for reported performance differences.

- Suggestion: Expand related work to include other neural coreference approaches (e.g., Wiseman et al., 2015).
