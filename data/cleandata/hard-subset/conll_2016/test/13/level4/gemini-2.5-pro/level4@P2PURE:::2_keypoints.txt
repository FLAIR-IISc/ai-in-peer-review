- Proposes a CNN-based model using sentential features for event entity linking with separate mention representation and coreference scoring components.  
- Paper is well-written and includes a strong analysis of argument sharing in ACE and dataset-size effects motivating ACE++.  
- Fails to justify the choice of a CNN; no discussion of pros/cons or comparison to alternatives (e.g., RNN), despite modest F1 gains.  
- Novelty is unclear because CNNs with sentential features are well-established; insufficient positioning against other continuous-space methods and limited baselines.  
- Provides no details on how the ACE++ dataset was created; unknown event types undermine claims about coverage and reproducibility—ACE++ should be described and released.  
- Experiments should report performance without gold references (missing in Table 3).  
- Should consider augmenting with nominal coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) as a baseline or feature source.  
- Needs evaluation on additional datasets (e.g., EventCorefBank) to bolster claims and motivate joint entity-event coreference modeling.  
- Table 5 shows handcrafted pairwise features are very effective; requires ablation/analysis of which features matter, why they were chosen, and what alternatives were tried.
