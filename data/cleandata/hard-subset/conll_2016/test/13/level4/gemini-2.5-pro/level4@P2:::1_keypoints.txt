- Paper proposes a two-stage neural model for event coreference: CNN-based sentence representations with piecewise max-pooling plus local trigger context, combined with pairwise features and a neural similarity layer evaluated on ACE2005 and private ACE++.
- Main contribution: shows a knowledge-free neural architecture can match state-of-the-art event linking without external semantic resources.
- Main contribution: provides an ablation study showing learned sentential features, local lexical features, and pairwise features all contribute, with sentential features being critical.
- Strength: achieves strong performance comparable to feature-rich systems while reducing reliance on domain-specific resources.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation including dataset-size analysis, clustering comparisons, and detailed ablation studies.
- Strength: includes manual error analysis highlighting challenges like pronominal event references and annotation boundary inconsistencies.
- Weakness: experimental setup limits comparability — uses gold event triggers and non-standard train/test splits which may make comparisons to prior work unfair.
- Weakness: model description lacks clarity — position embeddings generation/initialization is vague.
- Weakness: model description lacks justification for using exactly one word before and after the trigger as local context.
- Weakness: unclear role and usage of final event-mention representation v_e versus the intermediate v_sent+lex used in pairwise scoring.
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns — heavy reliance on private ACE++ dataset prevents verification and wider use.
- Weakness: evaluation lacks statistical significance testing, making small performance differences hard to interpret.
- Question: clarify how position embeddings are generated, initialized, and shared across contexts.
- Question: justify or provide experiments for choosing a one-word context window around the trigger.
- Question: clarify the role and use of v_e from Equation 3 versus v_sent+lex in subsequent steps.
- Question: explain how non-binary pairwise features (distance, argument overlap) are encoded before concatenation.
- Question: justify the use of the square function as the nonlinearity in Eq. 4 and whether it outperforms standard activations.
- Additional: suggest adapting the model to entity coreference to compare against broader neural coreference work.
- Additional: recommend citing Wiseman et al. (ACL 2015) on neural mention representations for ranking.
- Additional: note a minor labeling inconsistency between Table 6 and the text ("Same type" vs "same event").
