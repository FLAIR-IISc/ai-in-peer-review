- Proposes a two-stage neural model for event coreference: CNN-based sentential encodings of triggers + pairwise features fed to a classifier.  
- Claims state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005 and a newly created, larger ACE++ dataset.  
- Contribution: created ACE++ by adding 1351 documents to ACE 2005 to address data scarcity and improve recall.  
- Contribution: demonstrates competitive performance without relying on external semantic resources (e.g., WordNet, FrameNet).  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation, including ablation study isolating pairwise, trigger-local, and sentential features.  
- Strength: useful qualitative error analysis identifying challenges like pronominal anaphora and annotation inconsistencies.  
- Strength: well-motivated dataset creation to reduce reliance on trigger/argument overlap and leverage sentential context.  
- Strength: credible goal and empirical support for reducing dependency on external knowledge, improving portability.  
- Weakness: paper lacks adequate justification for choosing a CNN over sequential models (RNN/LSTM) given modest gains.  
- Weakness: novelty unclear because CNN classification is common; limited comparison to existing neural coreference methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on ACE++ (source, annotation process, event type distribution, release plans) limits reproducibility and assessment.  
- Question: request deeper analysis of which pairwise features drive performance and how they were selected.  
- Question: ask for results using system-predicted event mentions to assess end-to-end practicality.  
- Question: ask whether incorporating general-purpose entity coreference features could help pronominal event references.  
- Question: suggest evaluating on other corpora (e.g., EventCorefBank) to demonstrate robustness beyond ACE/ACE++.
