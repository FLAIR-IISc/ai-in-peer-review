- Proposed neural model: CNN with piecewise max-pooling produces sentential event representations which are concatenated with local lexical features and pairwise features, fed into a similarity network for coreference scoring; evaluated on ACE2005 and ACE++.

- Contribution: Achieves strong event linking performance without external semantic resources (e.g., WordNet/FrameNet), improving portability and reducing dependency on manual KBs.

- Contribution: Shows via ablation that combining CNN-derived sentential features, trigger-local lexical features, and standard pairwise features is crucial for high performance.

- Strength: Reaches state-of-the-art performance while avoiding external knowledge bases, making the approach more generalizable across domains.

- Strength: Convincing ablation study demonstrates the additive value of sentential, local lexical, and pairwise features.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation on ACE and ACE++ plus manual error analysis highlights remaining challenges (e.g., pronominal event references) and the benefits of more data.

- Weakness: Evaluation uses gold event triggers and a different ACE train/test split than prior work, creating an unrealistic setting and complicating fair comparisons.

- Weakness: Key architectural and implementation details are underspecified (position embeddings, piecewise pooling implementation, encoding of non-binary pairwise features, justification of one-word context), harming reproducibility.

- Weakness: ACE++ evaluation only compares variants of the authors' model; lacking baseline results (e.g., Liu et al., 2014) on ACE++ weakens claims about scalability with more data.

- Question: How are position embeddings generated and learned—randomly initialized and trained or otherwise?

- Question: How exactly is the piecewise max-pooling partitioning defined relative to triggers, especially for multi-word triggers?

- Question: Clarify which vector (hidden-layer v_e vs. concatenated vsent+lex) is actually fed into the similarity network.

- Question: How are non-binary pairwise features (e.g., sentence distance) encoded before concatenation?

- Question: Is using the square function as the similarity-layer nonlinearity novel for this task, and might it generalize to other similarity modeling scenarios?

- Question: Will the ACE++ dataset be released publicly to enable future comparisons and replication?
