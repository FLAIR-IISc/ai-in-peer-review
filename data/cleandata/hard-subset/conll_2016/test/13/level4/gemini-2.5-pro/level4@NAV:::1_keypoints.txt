- Paper proposes a CNN-based event linking model that combines mention representations, neighbor word embeddings, and pairwise features to compute coreference scores.  
- Main contribution: a neural architecture integrating word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features; embeddings alone are insufficient.  
- Achieves performance comparable to heavily feature-engineered prior systems on ACE and ACE++ datasets.  

- Experimental concern: evaluation uses gold-standard triggers instead of system-predicted triggers, yielding unrealistically high performance and unfair comparison to systems using predicted triggers.  
- Experimental concern: authors use different train/test splits than prior work, harming comparability; should use established splits.  

- Unclear: final statement in paragraph about need for cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is confusing.  
- Unclear: how position embeddings are generated, initialized, trained, and whether they are lexicalized or context-independent.  
- Unclear: precise role of left/right neighbor embeddings—are they only used within max-pooling or elsewhere?  
- Unclear: justification for using only the single-word immediate neighbors instead of a different window size.  
- Unclear: role and usage of the event-mention representation v_e versus v_{sent+lex}, since later sections only reference v_{sent+lex}.  
- Unclear: how pairwise features are encoded (binary and continuous), and whether their representations are fixed or learned during training.  

- Suggestion: consider adapting approach to entity coreference to enable comparisons on larger datasets like OntoNotes.  
- Suggestion/question: is the square function nonlinearity a novel choice here and might it generalize to other tasks?  
- Reproducibility issue: ACE++ dataset is not public—ask whether authors will release it and whether comparisons to feature-rich systems on ACE++ are possible.  
- Suggestion: include statistical significance testing due to close performance numbers.  
- Related work: discuss Wiseman et al. (2015) neural approach to entity coreference.  

- Minor: remove redundant "that" at line 143.  
- Minor: inconsistent baseline name—“same type” in Table 6 vs “same event” in text (line 670).
