- Proposes a CNN-based neural model for event coreference that combines CNN-learned mention representations with immediate context embeddings and a small set of linguistic pairwise features.
- Evaluated on ACE 2005 and an expanded ACE++ dataset, achieving performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution is a neural approach that effectively blends learned representations with simple linguistic features; finding that word embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Strength: linguistic features are simple and do not rely on extensive manually engineered external resources.
- Experimental concern: model is evaluated with gold-standard event triggers rather than predicted triggers, which may overestimate performance and unfairly compare to systems using predicted triggers.
- Experimental concern: uses a non-standard train/test split, complicating direct comparison and reproducibility; authors should report results on established splits.
- Clarification requested: final statement in paragraph (lines 65–70) motivating cross-sentential information is unclear and needs rewording.
- Clarification requested: insufficient detail on how position embeddings are generated, initialized, and whether they are lexicalized or dependent on the word.
- Clarification requested: unclear how left and right context windows contribute to the final representation beyond max-pooling—need details on their usage.
- Clarification requested: choice of a single-word window for immediate context seems arbitrary; authors should report experiments with other window sizes and their impact.
- Clarification requested: role and usage of event-mention representation v_e is unclear—state whether v_e is used in the final model and how it relates to v_{sent+lex}.
- Clarification requested: Section 3.2 lacks detail on incorporation of pairwise features—how are binary vs. continuous features encoded and are these feature representations learned or fixed?
- Suggestion: consider applying the approach to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to enable broader comparisons with prior work.
- Suggestion: comment on the novel choice of a square function as nonlinearity and its potential applicability to other NLP tasks.
- Dataset concern: ACE++ is not publicly available—authors should plan to release it and include comparisons on ACE++ with feature-rich systems.
- Results concern: many performance differences are small; authors should perform statistical significance testing to support claims of improvement.
- Related work: strengthen by discussing recent neural approaches to entity coreference, e.g., Wiseman et al. (2015).
- Minor: redundancy—remove redundant "that" at line 143.
- Minor: inconsistent terminology—baseline referred to as "same type" in Table 6 but "same event" in text (line 670).
