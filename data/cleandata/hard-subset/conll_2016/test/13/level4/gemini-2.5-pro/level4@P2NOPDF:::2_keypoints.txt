- Paper proposes an event entity linking model using CNN-generated sentential features with separate mention representation and pairwise coreference scoring components.
- Review praises clear writing, insightful analysis of argument sharing in ACE, and analysis motivating the new ACE++ dataset.
- Major concern: no justification for choosing a CNN over alternatives (e.g., RNN) and no comparative experiments to support the architectural choice given modest gains.
- Major concern: novelty is unclear—using CNNs for text representations is well-known; the paper must situate itself relative to existing continuous-space/neural event linking work and expand baselines.
- Major concern: ACE++ dataset is under-described—missing creation, scope, annotation details, event type coverage, and a public release to ensure reproducibility.
- Request to validate claim that the approach is domain-independent by showing performance where knowledge-based methods fail and describing ACE++ event types.
- Minor: include results for systems that do not use gold event mentions to reflect realistic performance.
- Minor: discuss integrating standard entity coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) and how such links could improve or inform the model/baseline.
- Minor: evaluate on additional corpora (e.g., EventCorefBank) to demonstrate robustness and motivate joint entity-event coreference work.
- Minor: ablation shows pairwise features are crucial—provide analysis of the most effective features, feature selection process, and features tried but discarded.
