- Proposes a two-stage event entity linking model: CNN-based sentential mention encoder plus a similarity module with lexical and pairwise features.  
- Manuscript is clearly written; the analysis of argument overlap and dataset-size effects (motivating ACE++) is valuable.  
- Insufficient motivation/analysis for choosing CNNs; no comparisons to alternatives (e.g., RNN/biRNN) despite modest (~2–3 F1) gains.  
- Novelty of using sentential CNN features for event linking is unclear; must compare to existing continuous-space/neural event coreference approaches and expand baselines.  
- ACE++ creation lacks detail: need which event types are included, curation methodology, and how ACE’s type constraints were addressed.  
- Must empirically show that methods relying on external semantic resources underperform on ACE++ to support the claim of domain independence.  
- Recommend releasing ACE++ publicly to enable future comparisons.  
- Report results without gold annotations (predicted triggers/arguments) to assess end-to-end robustness.  
- Consider augmenting or comparing to standard coreference resolvers (e.g., Stanford CoreNLP) as baselines or auxiliary signals.  
- Given ACE’s small size, evaluate on additional resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) and investigate joint entity-event coreference.  
- Pairwise features appear important; report which pairwise features are most impactful, how they were selected, and what other features were tried.
