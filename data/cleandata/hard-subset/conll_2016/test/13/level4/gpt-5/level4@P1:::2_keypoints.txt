- Replaces external semantic resources with CNN-learned sentential features for within-document event coreference, achieving state-of-the-art on ACE and ACE++.
- Model architecture: CNN-based sentence encoder for mentions plus a similarity module using learned representations and pairwise features.
- Main contributions: SOTA event linking without external KBs; empirical analyses (argument sharing, distances, ablations, clustering); creation/use of larger ACE++ dataset showing more data improves recall.
- Clear, well-structured presentation and training/evaluation explanation.
- Informative data analyses motivating sentential features and exploring clustering strategies and dataset scaling.
- Strong empirical results on several metrics (BLANC, MUC, positive-link F1) without WordNet/FrameNet/VerbOcean.
- Thorough ablations showing sentential, trigger-local lexical, and pairwise features all matter; sentential features aid recall.
- Useful qualitative error analysis highlighting pronoun-mediated links and annotation ambiguities.
- Limited motivation for choosing CNNs over alternatives (BiLSTM/GRU/tree models) and no comparisons to them.
- Novelty unclear because CNN sentence encoders are established; baselines in Table 3 not comprehensive enough to support strong novelty claims.
- Insufficient detail about ACE++ collection, annotation, event types, distribution differences, and availability.
- Reliance on gold annotations (e.g., gold triggers); lacks end-to-end results without gold inputs.
- Continued dependence on pairwise features; paper lacks per-feature importance or detailed feature selection rationale.
- Question: Why CNNs for mention encoding and were RNN/recursive/hybrid encoders evaluated?
- Question: What are ACE++ sources, annotation process, event types, QA, inter-annotator agreement, and release plans?
- Question: Exactly which gold inputs are used on ACE/ACE++; can results without gold references be reported?
- Question: Which pairwise features were most impactful and were feature ablations/importances run?
- Question: Can off-the-shelf nominal/pronominal coreference be integrated to handle pronoun-mediated event links?
- Suggestion: Include non-gold (end-to-end) results in Table 3 or separately.
- Suggestion: Evaluate on additional datasets (e.g., EventCorefBank) and explore joint entity–event coreference.
- Suggestion: Incorporate simple pronoun-resolution baselines (using coreference outputs) and consider releasing ACE++ to support portability claims.
