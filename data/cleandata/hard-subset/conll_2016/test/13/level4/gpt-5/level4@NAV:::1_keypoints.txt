- Method: CNN over word embeddings + max-pooling to encode event mentions, concatenated with contextual embeddings and pairwise features, fed to a single-layer NN to compute coreference scores.  
- Main contribution: a neural event-linking model that combines word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features; embeddings alone were insufficient but added features are limited and not resource-heavy.  
- Evaluation: tested on ACE and a private ACE++ dataset, achieving comparable performance to prior feature-rich systems.  
- Experimental concern: authors use gold trigger words—results with predicted triggers are needed since one competing system uses predicted triggers, making current comparisons potentially unfair.  
- Experimental concern: different train/test splits across papers are used; authors should follow established splits where possible.  
- Unclear: the final statement in the second paragraph (lines 65–70) about cross-sentential information is unclear.  
- Unclear: how position embeddings are generated—are they randomly initialized or lexicalized, and why should the same relative position across different words share an embedding?  
- Unclear: how left versus right neighbors are incorporated into the representation (lines 307–311)—is this only affecting max-pooling?  
- Unclear: choice of appending exactly one token before and after the trigger feels arbitrary—why that window size?  
- Unclear: role of event-mention representation v_e (line 330)—later text only references v_{sent+lex}; how is v_e used?  
- Unclear: encoding and use of pairwise features in Section 3.2—are binary features encoded as a binary vector, how is distance handled, and are features updated during training?  
- Suggestion: try applying the approach to entity coreference to enable comparisons with more prior work and datasets like OntoNotes.  
- Question: the use of a square nonlinearity is intriguing—is this novel and broadly applicable to other tasks?  
- Dataset transparency: ACE++ is not public—do authors plan to release it; at minimum, provide comparisons with feature-rich systems on ACE++.  
- Results: several reported numbers are very close—statistical significance testing would strengthen claims.  
- Related work: cite Wiseman et al. (2015) among neural approaches to (entity) coreference.  
- Minor issues: remove unnecessary “that” (line 143) and fix inconsistent baseline labeling (“same type” vs “same event”) in Table 6/text.  
- Reference: Wiseman, Rush, Weston, and Shieber (ACL 2015) on learning anaphoricity and antecedent ranking features for coreference resolution.
