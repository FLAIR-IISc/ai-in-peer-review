- Paper tackles within-document event coreference using CNN-learned sentential features instead of external semantic resources.
- Model has two parts: a CNN-based event-mention encoder with positional/trigger-local info, and a pairwise scorer combining mention representations with hand-crafted pairwise features followed by clustering.
- Contribution: shows a resource-free model achieves state-of-the-art on ACE and strong results on an expanded ACE++ dataset.
- Contribution: provides ablation and design studies on sentential, trigger-local, and pairwise features, plus analyses of dataset size and clustering strategies.
- Contribution: offers task diagnostics (argument overlap, trigger mismatch, mention distance) and error analysis highlighting pronoun-related challenges; ACE++ could be valuable if released.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and motivating corpus analysis supporting sentence-level features.
- Strength: competitive results without external lexicons, indicating better portability to domains lacking such resources.
- Strength: thorough experiments including multiple metrics, ablations, data-scale analysis, and clustering strategy comparisons.
- Strength: useful error analysis that identifies pronoun-linked failures and limits of sentence-only representations.
- Weakness: insufficient justification for choosing CNNs; lacks comparisons to BiLSTMs, attention models, or other encoders.
- Weakness: limited novelty/positioning since CNN sentence features are established in IE; competitor set is not broad enough.
- Weakness: ACE++ is under-described — collection method, event types, guidelines, quality, and release plans are missing.
- Weakness: reliance on gold annotations (triggers/arguments) undermines assessment of end-to-end robustness and portability.
- Weakness: pairwise hand-crafted features appear critical, but the paper provides limited analysis of which features matter or why.
- Question: why were CNNs chosen — please compare to at least one strong neural alternative and/or provide efficiency/stability evidence.
- Question: provide ACE++ construction details, covered event types/domains, annotation guidelines, inter-annotator agreement, and release plans.
- Question: can the authors evaluate competing systems that use external semantic features on ACE++ to support the portability claim?
- Question: report non-gold (predicted trigger/argument) results to assess end-to-end performance and error propagation.
- Question: can signals from general coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP linking pronouns) be integrated to reduce pronoun-related errors?
- Question: which pairwise features are most impactful — provide per-feature ablations or learned-importance analyses and describe feature selection process.
- Overall: well-written and empirically careful paper with practical promise, but needs architectural comparisons, clearer ACE++ documentation, and non-gold evaluations to strengthen claims.
