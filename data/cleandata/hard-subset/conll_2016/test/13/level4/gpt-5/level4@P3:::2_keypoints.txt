- Empirical claim: sentence-level neural representations can replace external semantic resources and achieve state-of-the-art within-document event coreference on ACE.
- Model contribution: two-stage architecture with a CNN-based mention encoder and a similarity module combining mention representations and pairwise features.
- Clustering insight: transitive-closure clustering outperforms BestLink for this model; ACE++ improves recall scaling.
- Strength: competitive performance without external KBs, especially higher BLANC F1.
- Strength: clean ablations show complementary contributions of pairwise, trigger-local, and sentential features; sentential features boost precision and recall.
- Strength: clear, quantitative analysis of data properties (argument overlap, trigger mismatch, mention distance) motivating modeling choices.
- Strength: useful comparison of clustering strategies showing BestLink can hurt recall.
- Strength: informative error analysis identifying pronoun resolution and borderline annotation cases as key failure modes.
- Weakness: CNN encoder choice is under-motivated and lacks comparison to BiLSTMs or hybrid sequence models.
- Weakness: novelty/positioning unclear—using CNN sentence encoders is established and relation to prior neural/continuous approaches is not well situated.
- Weakness: ACE++ construction and annotation details (sources, event types, IAA, quality control) are insufficiently described, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: evaluation relies on gold triggers/types; lacks end-to-end experiments with predicted triggers/types and broader uniform baseline comparisons.
- Weakness: dependence on engineered pairwise features—unclear how much gain comes from neural sentential representations versus those features.
- Question: Why CNNs over BiLSTMs/hybrids; can you provide controlled encoder comparisons?
- Question: Why use a square nonlinearity in the similarity layer; comparisons to cosine, ReLU/tanh MLPs, or bilinear scoring?
- Question: How was ACE++ collected/annotated, what event types/distributions and IAA, and will ACE++ and code be released?
- Question: Are type-agreement features gold or predicted; can you report results with predicted triggers/types for robustness?
- Question: Can you expand comparisons to additional neural/continuous-space event coreference baselines and run them on your splits?
- Question: Which pairwise features drive performance; please provide feature importance or pairwise ablations and selection rationale?
- Question: Have you tried integrating standard entity/coreference outputs to mitigate pronoun-resolution errors (even as a simple pipeline)?
