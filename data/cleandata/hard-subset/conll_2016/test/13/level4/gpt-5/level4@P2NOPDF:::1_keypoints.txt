- Models event linking with CNNs over word embeddings + max-pooling, concatenated with neighbor word embeddings and pairwise features into a single-layer NN for scoring.  
- Main contribution: a neural event-linking approach combining embeddings with a small set of linguistic features; finding that embeddings alone are insufficient.  
- Achieves performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems on ACE and ACE++.  
- Experiments appear to use gold triggers; results with predicted triggers are needed for fair comparison to some baselines.  
- Different train/test splits are used across papers; recommend adhering to established splits for fair comparison.  
- Clarify the final claim about the importance of cross-sentential information (lines 65–70).  
- Explain how position embeddings are generated—randomly initialized and learned, or tied to lexical content—and justify sharing across different adjacent words.  
- Clarify how left vs. right neighbors are incorporated (does the distinction affect only max-pooling or also convolutional filters/concatenation?).  
- Justify using exactly one word before/after the trigger as context; was window size tuned on development data?  
- Clarify the role of the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) and where it is used versus v_{sent+lex} in final scoring.  
- Describe how pairwise features are encoded/used: concatenated binary vector? how is distance handled (bucketing/normalization)? are feature parameters learned?  
- Suggest evaluating/applying the approach to entity coreference to enable comparisons on larger datasets like OntoNotes.  
- Question the use of the square function as a nonlinearity—novelty and expected generalization to other tasks.  
- ACE++ is not public; request release of ACE++ or at least comparisons against feature-rich baselines on public datasets for reproducibility.  
- Recommend statistical significance testing for small result differences.  
- Related work: cite neural coreference work such as Wiseman et al. (2015).
