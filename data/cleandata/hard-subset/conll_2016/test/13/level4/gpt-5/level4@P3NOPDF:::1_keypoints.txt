- Presents a CNN-based event coreference model combining convolutional word-embedding encoders, neighbor-word embeddings, and a small set of pairwise linguistic features with a single-layer similarity scorer, achieving competitive results on ACE and ACE++.
- Strength: Clear, modular neural architecture that integrates distributed representations with a compact set of linguistic features and avoids heavy hand-crafted resources.
- Strength: Empirical finding that word embeddings alone are insufficient, while modest feature augmentation yields competitive performance.
- Strength: Evaluation on ACE and an expanded ACE++ dataset helps probe generality.
- Concern: Uses gold triggers, complicating fair comparison since some baselines use predicted triggers; results with predicted triggers or normalized comparisons are needed.
- Concern: Uses nonstandard data splits; please adopt widely used splits or additionally report on them for comparability.
- Clarification: The claim about cross-sentential information helping is unclear in one paragraph and needs a clearer restatement and a concrete example.
- Clarification: Position embeddings need specification—initialization, whether learned end-to-end, absolute vs. relative, shared vs. contextualized, and rationale for identical embeddings for same relative positions.
- Clarification: Explain how left and right neighbors are handled—separate filters or only reflected in pooling/concatenation—and whether asymmetry affects convolution, pooling, or only final concatenation.
- Clarification: Justify the single-word left/right context window and report experiments with wider windows or learned attention.
- Clarification: Specify how representation v_e is used vs. v_sent+lex and where each enters the scoring network.
- Clarification: Precisely describe pairwise feature encoding (distance binning/normalization/embedding), whether feature embeddings are learned, and how features are integrated with neural representations.
- Results: Report statistical significance tests for close scores to strengthen comparisons.
- Results: Include an ablation isolating contributions of neighbor words, position, and pairwise features, given claim that embeddings alone underperform.
- Results: Provide error analysis highlighting common failure modes (e.g., cross-sentence links, lexical variation, argument compatibility).
- Modeling: Discuss motivation and stability of using a square nonlinearity, compare to ReLU/tanh, and include ablation on activation choice.
- Modeling: Claim that approach adapts to entity coreference; demonstrate or preliminarily evaluate on an entity coreference benchmark to broaden impact.
- Reproducibility: Commit to releasing ACE++ or scripts to reproduce it, or otherwise strengthen comparison on public ACE splits.
- Reproducibility: Provide exact preprocessing, tokenization, resources (pretrained embeddings), and hyperparameters to facilitate replication.
- Related work: Expand discussion of neural coreference literature, e.g., Wiseman et al. (2015), and position relative to antecedent-ranking architectures.
- Minor: Remove redundant “that” (line 143) and standardize terminology between Table 6 and text (e.g., “same type” vs. “same event”).
- Minor: Tighten notation consistency for v_e vs. v_sent+lex and define all symbols at first use.
- Overall: Solid, timely contribution with competitive performance, but would be stronger with predicted-trigger results/normalized comparisons, standard splits or ACE++ release, statistical tests and targeted ablations, and clarifications on representations and feature integration.
