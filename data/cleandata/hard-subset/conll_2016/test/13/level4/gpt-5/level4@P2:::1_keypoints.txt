- Paper proposes a neural within-document event coreference model using sentential CNNs plus local lexical and pairwise features, achieving competitive performance without external semantic resources.  
- Contributions: (1) CNN-based event-linking model that replaces heavy semantic features, (2) thorough ablations and clustering-strategy comparison, (3) use of a larger ACE++ dataset with error analysis (ACE++ not released).  
- Strength: Competitive results without external KBs, improving portability to low-resource domains.  
- Strength: Strong ablation study showing sentential + local + pairwise features are necessary; word embeddings alone insufficient.  
- Strength: Insightful comparison of clustering strategies (transitive closure vs. BestLink) and its impact on recall/pruning.  
- Strength: Useful qualitative error analysis highlighting pronoun issues and annotation ambiguities.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses gold triggers and non-standard splits, harming comparability; should report predicted-trigger results and release splits.  
- Weakness: Model description lacks clarity on position embeddings, left/right context handling, choice of one-token local window, actual use of v_e, and encoding/normalization of pairwise features.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—CNN encoders are established; square activation’s novelty and generality are not convincingly justified.  
- Weakness: ACE++ is not public; lack of statistical significance testing and missing comparisons with feature-rich systems on ACE++.  
- Weakness: Scope limited—no evaluation on entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to situate method in broader coreference literature.  
- Key question: Please report results with predicted triggers and the trigger detector used.  
- Key question: How are position embeddings initialized/trained and are large distances clipped?  
- Key question: Precisely where is v_e used versus v_sent+lex in the scoring pipeline?  
- Key question: How are pairwise features encoded/scaled; are any embedded/learned?  
- Key question: Did you test larger local windows or alternative context encodings (sensitivity analysis)?  
- Key question: Is the square activation novel here; comparisons to ReLU/tanh/bilinear/cosine and significance tests?  
- Key question: Will you release ACE++ and the exact train/valid/test split document lists?  
- Key question: Have you tried applying the framework to entity coreference (OntoNotes), or what adaptations would be needed?  
- Minor comments: Rephrase unclear motivation (lines 65–70), fix small wording issues, standardize baseline labels in Table 6, cite prior neural coreference work (e.g., Wiseman et al. 2015), and provide significance testing and split document IDs.
