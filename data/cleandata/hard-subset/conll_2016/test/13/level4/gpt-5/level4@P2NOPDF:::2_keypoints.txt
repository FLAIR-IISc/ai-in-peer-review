Paper proposes event entity linking using CNN-based sentential representations with an encoder and pairwise scorer

Strength: clearly written and well organized

Strength: insightful analysis of argument sharing in ACE

Strength: compelling investigation of dataset size effects motivating ACE++

Major issue: Insufficient motivation and empirical justification for using CNNs over RNNs/biLSTMs or transformers

Major issue: Lacks ablations on encoder type, context window, training efficiency, and robustness to support architectural choice

Major issue: Unclear novelty relative to existing continuous-space/neural approaches and incomplete baseline comparisons

Major issue: Table 3’s baselines appear incomplete; need comparisons to prior neural event linking methods or clearer positioning

Major issue: ACE++ lacks documentation on collection, annotation, event-type coverage, domains, and label distribution

Major issue: Claim that prior approaches are domain-tied is unsupported without experiments showing degradation of resource-reliant systems on ACE++

Major issue: Recommend releasing ACE++ to enable reproducibility and future comparisons

Minor: Report end-to-end performance without gold references (predicted mentions/links)

Minor: Explore leveraging off-the-shelf coreference signals (e.g., CoreNLP) as features or baselines

Minor: Evaluate on additional corpora (e.g., EventCorefBank) and consider joint entity-event coreference modeling

Minor: Pairwise features seem highly effective; report which features matter, selection process, and include a feature ablation

Recommendations: Provide architectural rationale for CNNs, include encoder/type/context ablations, pretrained embedding and data-scaling experiments, and learning curves

Recommendations: Provide error analysis separating gains from sentential representations vs pairwise features and dataset effects

Recommendations: Clarify evaluation settings (use of gold annotations) and report variance across runs

Overall: Promising and well-presented work but novelty and empirical support are insufficient; address stronger baselines, encoder comparisons, feature ablations, and detailed ACE++ reporting to strengthen the paper
