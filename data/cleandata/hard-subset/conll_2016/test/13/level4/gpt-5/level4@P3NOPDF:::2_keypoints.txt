- Clear problem setup and well-structured presentation.
- Insightful analysis showing argument sharing across coreferent events.
- Empirical finding that training data size matters, motivating ACE++.
- Motivation for using CNNs is underdeveloped and needs conceptual/empirical justification versus RNNs, bi-LSTMs, bag-of-ngrams, or attention encoders.
- Recommend ablation experiments replacing CNN with RNN/bi-LSTM and bag-of-ngrams, and testing different context window sizes and filter widths.
- Novelty relative to prior continuous-space/neural event coreference/linking work is unclear; baselines appear incomplete.
- Paper should enumerate and directly compare to existing neural approaches and clarify what is conceptually new (architecture, sentential-only inputs, or training paradigm).
- ACE++ dataset description is insufficient: construction methodology, annotation guidelines, annotator expertise, quality control, and inter-annotator agreement are missing.
- Need ACE++ coverage details (event types, domains, genres), train/dev/test splits, licensing/access, and a public release commitment.
- Provide evidence that methods relying on external semantic resources degrade on ACE++ while the proposed approach remains robust.
- Limited analysis of feature contributions; pairwise features seem highly effective and require finer-grained ablation.
- Describe feature selection process, alternatives considered, and provide error analysis by event type, argument structure, and discourse phenomena.
- Report end-to-end performance without gold annotations (no gold mentions/references) to quantify practical utility.
- Consider leveraging off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) as augmentations or features.
- Evaluate on additional benchmarks (especially ECB/ECB+) to assess generalization and compare to broader prior work.
- Provide full implementation details for replication: hyperparameters, training regimen, negative sampling, and model selection criteria.
- Clarify evaluation protocol and metrics, including how mention detection interacts with linking and whether evaluations use gold or predicted mentions.
- Overall: promising direction reducing reliance on external knowledge, but needs stronger CNN justification, broader baselines/ablations, detailed ACE++ documentation, and cross-domain evaluations to increase impact.
