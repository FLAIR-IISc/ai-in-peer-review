- Paper proposes a CNN-based event linking model that combines word-embedding-derived vector representations with linguistic features and uses a single-layer network to score coreference, evaluated on ACE and an expanded ACE++ dataset with results comparable to feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution: a neural entity-linking approach showing that word embeddings alone are insufficient and that limited, no-external-resource linguistic features improve performance and efficiency.
- Experimental concern: model uses gold trigger words (not predicted), which may make comparisons with other systems unfair despite the authors' justification.
- Experimental concern: inconsistent train/test splits across papers; authors should use established splits when possible.
- Unclear statement: the paper's claim about the need for cross-sentential information (second paragraph, lines 65–70) is not clearly explained.
- Unclear method: how position embeddings are generated—whether randomly initialized or lexicalized—and rationale for sharing embeddings across relative positions is unspecified.
- Unclear method: the process of creating representations from left and right neighbors (lines 307–311) is poorly explained, including whether this only affects max-pooling.
- Unclear design choice: appending word embeddings of one word before and after triggers appears arbitrary and lacks justification.
- Unclear usage: the role of the event-mention representation ve (line 330) is unclear since later sections refer only to v{sent+lex}.
- Unclear documentation: encoding and treatment of pairwise features in section 3.2—how binary features and the distance feature are represented and whether they are fixed during training—are not described.
- Suggestion: explore applying the approach to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to enable broader comparisons with existing work.
- Suggestion: the use of a square function as nonlinearity is interesting and its novelty and transferability to other tasks merit investigation.
- Suggestion: release of the ACE++ dataset and comparisons with feature-rich systems on it would aid reproducibility and benchmarking.
- Suggestion: include significance testing to strengthen claims about performance differences.
- Related work: add discussion of neural approaches to coreference such as Wiseman et al. (2015).
- Minor issues: remove redundant "that" (line 143) and fix inconsistent baseline labeling ("same type" in Table 6 vs. "same event" in text, line 670).
