- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that creates mention vectors via CNN+max-pooling, concatenates surrounding word embeddings, and combines these with pairwise features to compute coreference scores.
- Evaluated on ACE and ACE++, achieving performance comparable to previous feature-rich systems.
- Primary contribution: a neural approach that combines word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Using gold trigger words instead of predicted triggers is questionable; results with predicted triggers are needed for fair comparison with some competitors.
- Different papers use different train/test splits; authors should adhere to prior splits for consistency.
- The claim about the necessity of cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is unclear and needs clarification.
- Method for generating position embeddings is unclear (randomly initialized vs. lexicalized) and why relative positions share embeddings across different words is not justified.
- The effect of left vs. right neighbor representations is unclear—does it only affect max-pooling or more?
- Rationale for appending exactly one word before and after triggers is arbitrary; justification for window size is missing.
- The event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is unclear and seems unused later, with only v_{sent+lex} appearing in subsequent sections.
- Encoding and handling of pairwise features (binary vs. distance features) are unclear, and it’s not stated whether these features are learned or fixed during training.
- Recommend exploring applicability to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with more prior work and datasets.
- The use of a square function as nonlinearity is unusual and should be discussed for novelty and broader applicability.
- ACE++ is not publicly available; authors should release it or at least compare against feature-rich systems on it to facilitate comparisons.
- Significance testing is needed because reported results are close and may not be meaningfully different.
- Related work should cite the neural coreference approach by Wiseman et al. (2015).
- Minor typo: redundant "that" on line 143.
- Inconsistency: baseline labeled "same type" in Table 6 but referred to as "same event" in the text (line 670).
