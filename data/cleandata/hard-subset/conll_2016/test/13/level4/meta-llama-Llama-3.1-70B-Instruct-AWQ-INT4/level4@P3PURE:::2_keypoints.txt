- Paper is well-written and easy to follow; ACE corpus analysis of argument sharing and dataset-size analysis motivating ACE++ are strong contributions.
- Major issue: Authors do not motivate or analyze why CNNs were chosen for mention representations nor compare to alternatives (e.g., RNNs), despite small F1 gains.
- Major issue: Novelty is unclear—using sentential CNN features for classification is not new; need explicit comparison to existing continuous-space event linking methods.
- Major issue: Table 3’s method choices are incomplete and lack thorough baselines/competitors.
- Major issue: ACE++ dataset collection details are missing—event types covered, construction methodology, and biases are not described.
- Major issue: Claim that approach is domain/resource-independent is unsubstantiated; authors should show prior methods fail where theirs succeeds and consider releasing ACE++.
- Minor: Report performance without gold references in Table 3.
- Minor: Explore augmenting the model with an off-the-shelf coreference system (e.g., CoreNLP linking “It” to “bombing”) and report effects.
- Minor: Results should be validated on additional resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) and consider joint entity-event coreference modeling.
- Minor: Pairwise features appear important; authors should state which features were most effective, how features were selected, and what alternatives were tried.
