- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that combines word embeddings, CNN + max-pooling, and surrounding word embeddings, then concatenates pairwise features via a single-layer NN to score coreference.
- Evaluated on ACE and an expanded ACE++ dataset, achieving performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution: a neural approach that integrates word embeddings with limited linguistic features; results show embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Experimental concern: model uses gold trigger words whereas some competitors used predicted triggers, making comparisons unfair; results with predicted triggers should be reported.
- Experimental concern: inconsistent train/test splits across papers; authors should follow previous splits when possible.
- Unclear statement: the final sentence in paragraph about cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is unclear and needs clarification.
- Unclear: how position embeddings are generated — are they randomly initialized or lexicalized, and why would relative positions next to different words share embeddings?
- Unclear: the role of left vs. right neighbors in representation (lines 307–311) is unclear — does it only affect max-pooling or more?
- Unclear: appending one word before and after triggers seems arbitrary; rationale for selecting one neighbor on each side is not justified.
- Unclear: usage of the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is unclear since only v_{sent+lex} appears used later.
- Unclear: how pairwise features are applied (section 3.2) — are binary features binary vectors, how is distance encoded, and are these features fixed during training?
- Suggestion: test applicability to entity coreference resolution to compare with more prior work and datasets (e.g., OntoNotes).
- Suggestion: the use of a square nonlinearity is interesting — authors should discuss novelty and potential applicability to other tasks.
- Suggestion: release the ACE++ dataset or at least compare on it against feature-rich systems to aid reproducibility and comparison.
- Suggestion: perform statistical significance testing, as some reported differences are small.
- Related work: include Wiseman et al. (2015) neural approach to (entity) coreference in the related work discussion.
