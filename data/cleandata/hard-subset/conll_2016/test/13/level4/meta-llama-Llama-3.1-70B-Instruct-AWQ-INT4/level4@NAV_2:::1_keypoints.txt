- Proposes a CNN-based two-stage model that generates vector representations for event mentions and combines them with pairwise features for event linking.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on ACE and ACE++ without external feature sources.  
- Main contribution: a neural event linking approach that fuses word embeddings with limited linguistic features.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates comparable performance to feature-rich prior systems.  
- Strength: novel CNN-based approach effectively combines embeddings and limited linguistic cues.  
- Strength: thorough results analysis including error analysis highlighting task challenges.  
- Strength: achieves strong performance without relying on external features.  
- Weakness: relies on gold trigger words, which may not reflect real-world use.  
- Weakness: comparisons to prior work may be unfair due to differing train/test splits and preprocessing.  
- Weakness: some model components (relative position embeddings, embedding concatenation) lack clear explanation.  
- Question: request for details on generation and necessity of relative position embeddings.  
- Question: how will the authors address using predicted rather than gold trigger words in future work?  
- Question: will the ACE++ dataset be released and can more details be provided?  
- Question: have the authors considered applying this approach to entity coreference and its challenges/benefits?  
- Question: please clarify the choice and generality of the square function as the nonlinearity.
