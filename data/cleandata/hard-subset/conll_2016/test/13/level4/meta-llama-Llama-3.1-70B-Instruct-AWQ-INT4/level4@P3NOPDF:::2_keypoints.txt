- Proposal: CNN-based sentential features for event entity linking with a two-part model (mention representation + coreference scorer).  
- Strength: Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and easy to follow.  
- Strength: Insightful analysis of argument sharing in ACE and compelling discussion on dataset size motivating ACE++.  
- Major issue: No clear motivation for using CNNs; lacks analysis of pros/cons and comparisons to alternatives (e.g., RNNs).  
- Major issue: Small improvement (2–3 F1) over state-of-the-art requires stronger evidence that the CNN architecture is superior.  
- Major issue: Novelty unclear—using CNNs on sentential features for classification is not new; authors must explicitly state contributions and compare to continuous-space event linking methods.  
- Major issue: Table 3’s method selection is incomplete; comparisons need to be more comprehensive.  
- Major issue: ACE++ dataset collection details are missing; unknown event-type coverage undermines claims of robustness and domain-independence.  
- Suggestion: Make ACE++ public to enable meaningful future comparisons.  
- Minor issue: Report model performance without gold references in Table 3 for completeness.  
- Minor suggestion: Explore augmenting with a vanilla coreference system (e.g., CoreNLP) to assess gains from entity coref integration.  
- Minor suggestion: Evaluate on other resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) to demonstrate robustness and motivate joint entity-event coreference modeling.  
- Minor issue: Provide a detailed analysis of pairwise features (Table 5), indicating which features were most effective and how they were selected.
