- Proposes a neural event-linking model using CNNs + max-pooling to create event-mention vectors combined with surrounding word embeddings.  
- Incorporates pairwise features fed through a single-layer network to compute similarity vectors and a coreference score.  
- Main contribution: neural event linking combining word embeddings and linguistic features without hand-crafted external resources.  
- Evaluated on ACE and an expanded ACE++ dataset, achieving performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems.  
- Strength: novel, well-structured approach that effectively uses CNNs and avoids manual resources.  
- Weakness: experiments use gold trigger words, which may not reflect real-world performance with predicted triggers.  
- Weakness: inconsistent train/test splits across papers raise fairness concerns for comparisons.  
- Clarification needed: the claim about cross-sentential information is unclear (last sentence of paragraph two).  
- Clarification needed: how position embeddings are generated and why the same relative position should share embeddings across different words.  
- Clarification needed: unclear use of left/right neighbor embeddings and how the event-mention representation is used downstream.  
- Clarification needed: pairwise features are not well-explained—how they are encoded and used during training is unclear.  
- Suggestion: explore the square function nonlinearity further and test its applicability in other tasks.  
- Suggestion: release the ACE++ dataset to enable reproducible comparisons.  
- Related work: should cite neural coreference work by Wiseman et al. (2015).  
- Suggestion: include significance testing to substantiate model comparisons.  
- Minor issues: redundant word (“that”) on line 143 and inconsistent naming of the “same type” baseline in Table 6 vs. text.
