- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that creates mention vectors via word-embedding CNN + max-pooling, concatenates neighboring embeddings, adds pairwise features, and computes a coreference score with a single-layer network.  
- Achieves performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems on ACE and an expanded dataset, showing embeddings alone are insufficient and limited linguistic features help.  
- Uses gold trigger words in experiments; performance with predicted triggers is not reported, making comparisons with systems using predicted triggers unfair.  
- Different papers use different train/test splits; authors should adhere to previous splits to enable accurate comparisons.  
- The claim about the need for cross-sentential information is supported by numbers, but a specific statement in the paper (lines 65–70) is unclear.  
- The method for generating position embeddings is vague: unclear whether they are randomly initialized or lexicalized and why identical embeddings apply to different lexical contexts.  
- The role of left vs right neighbors in the representation (and whether it only affects max-pooling) is not well explained.  
- Appending one word before and after triggers appears arbitrary; no justification for the chosen window size is given.  
- The event-mention representation v_e is not clearly defined or consistently used; later sections only reference v_{sent+lex}.  
- Encoding and treatment of pairwise features is unclear (binary vs other encoding, how distance is represented, and whether features are fixed during training).  
- Suggests exploring application to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with more prior work.  
- Questions novelty and generality of using a square function as nonlinearity and whether it applies to other tasks.  
- Uses two datasets including a non-public ACE++; asks whether ACE++ will be released and requests comparisons of feature-rich baselines on that dataset.  
- Recommends reporting significance testing due to close result numbers.  
- Suggests citing Wiseman et al. (2015) neural approach for (entity) coreference in related work.  
- Minor edits: remove redundant "that" (line 143) and fix inconsistent baseline naming ("same type" vs "same event" in table/text).
