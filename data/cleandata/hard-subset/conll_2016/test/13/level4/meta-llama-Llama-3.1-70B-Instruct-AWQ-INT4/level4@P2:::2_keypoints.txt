- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based sentential-feature model for event linking with a two-part architecture (mention representation + pairwise coreference scoring), evaluated on ACE 2005 and a new ACE++ dataset.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a sentential-CNN approach that claims state-of-the-art event linking without external knowledge sources.

- Contribution 2: Constructs and evaluates on a new extended dataset, ACE++, derived from ACE 2005.

- Contribution 3: Analyzes the ACE corpus for argument sharing between event coreferences to motivate sentential features.

- Strength 1: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Strength 2: The ACE corpus analysis is interesting and provides solid motivation for the approach.

- Strength 3: Evaluation on ACE++ gives a more comprehensive view of model performance.

- Weakness 1: Lacks motivation and analysis for using CNNs over alternatives (e.g., RNNs); no comparison with other representation models.

- Weakness 2: Novelty of using sentential features is not clearly positioned against existing continuous-space event linking methods.

- Weakness 3: ACE++ dataset collection methodology and covered event types are not described, which is a major omission.

- Weakness 4: No comparison with other approaches on datasets where the method is claimed to succeed, weakening the claim about not needing restricted-domain resources.

- Question 1: Please provide details on ACE++ collection and the event types it covers.

- Question 2: How were pairwise comparison features chosen, and what alternatives were tried?

- Question 3: Can you compare your approach to other methods on a dataset where you claim superiority?

- Question 4: How will you handle pronoun resolution, a known challenge for event linking?
