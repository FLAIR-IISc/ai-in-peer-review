- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that creates mention vectors via CNN + max-pooling and concatenates them with surrounding word embeddings and pairwise features to compute coreference scores.
- Primary contribution: a neural approach that effectively combines word embeddings with linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Achieves performance comparable to feature-rich past systems on ACE and ACE++.
- Concern: evaluation uses gold trigger words rather than predicted ones, potentially inflating results and making comparisons unfair.
- Concern: inconsistent train/test splits across papers; authors should adhere to previous splits for fair comparison.
- Unclear: the final claim about cross-sentential information in lines 65–70 is not well explained.
- Unclear: how position embeddings are generated—randomly initialized or lexicalized—and why adjacent relative positions share embeddings.
- Unclear: the role of left/right neighbors in representation (lines 307–311) and whether they only affect max-pooling.
- Unclear: rationale for appending exactly one word before and after triggers rather than an alternative context window.
- Unclear: how the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is used, since subsequent sections only reference v_{sent+lex}.
- Unclear: encoding and training of pairwise features (e.g., distance); whether non-binary features are encoded and if features are fixed during training.
- Suggestion: evaluate applicability to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with more prior work.
- Suggestion: discuss and justify the use of a square function as nonlinearity and its potential novelty and broader applicability.
- Dataset issue: ACE++ is not public—authors should release it or at least compare ACE++ results with feature-rich systems.
- Suggestion: perform statistical significance testing for reported results given close numbers.
- Related work: cite neural coreference work like Wiseman et al. (2015).
- Minor: redundant word "that" at line 143.
- Minor: inconsistent baseline naming—"same type" in Table 6 vs. "same event" in text (line 670).
