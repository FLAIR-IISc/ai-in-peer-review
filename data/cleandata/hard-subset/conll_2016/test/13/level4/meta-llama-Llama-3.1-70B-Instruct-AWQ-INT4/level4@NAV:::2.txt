This paper introduces a novel approach to event entity linking by leveraging sentential features from convolutional neural networks (CNNs) instead of relying on external knowledge sources. The proposed two-part model consists of an event mention representation learning component and a coreference score calculation component. The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and easy to follow. The analysis of argument sharing between event coreferences in the ACE corpus is commendable, and the discussion on the impact of dataset size is a compelling motivation for creating the ACE++ dataset.

However, there are several major concerns that require attention:

* The authors do not provide sufficient justification for choosing CNNs over other architectures, such as recurrent neural networks (RNNs), for generating mention representations. A more comprehensive analysis of the pros and cons of using CNNs is necessary, especially given the modest improvement in F1 score compared to state-of-the-art methods.
* The novelty of using sentential features for event linking is not clearly established, as this approach is not new in the context of classification tasks. The authors should explicitly compare their method to existing continuous space methods for event linking and provide a more thorough evaluation in Table 3.
* The collection process and characteristics of the ACE++ dataset are not described. Given the limitations of the original ACE dataset, it is essential to know what event types are covered in ACE++ and how it addresses the biases in the original dataset. Making the ACE++ dataset publicly available would also facilitate future comparisons.

Some minor issues to consider:

* Including the performance of the model without gold references in Table 3 would provide additional insight.
* Exploring the potential augmentation of the model with a vanilla coreference resolution system could be beneficial, as demonstrated by the example in line 687.
* Testing the model on other available resources, such as EventCorefBank, is crucial for demonstrating its robustness and motivating the simultaneous consideration of entity and event coreference.
* The effectiveness of pairwise features in Table 5 suggests that feature engineering remains important for achieving a competitive model. A more detailed discussion on the selection of features and the exploration of alternative options would be valuable.
