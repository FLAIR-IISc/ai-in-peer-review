- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that creates event mention vectors via CNN + max-pooling, concatenates neighbor embeddings and pairwise features, and computes a coreference score with a single-layer NN.
- Evaluated on ACE and ACE++ and achieves performance comparable to previous feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution: a neural entity-linking approach combining word embeddings with limited linguistic features; word embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Linguistic features used are few and do not require manually crafted external resources.
- Experimental concern: model uses gold trigger words rather than predicted triggers — results with predicted triggers are needed for fair comparison to some baselines.
- Experimental concern: inconsistent train/test splits across papers; authors should adhere to established splits for fair comparisons.
- Unclear statement: the final claim about the need for cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) needs clearer explanation.
- Unclear methodology: how position embeddings are generated (random init vs. lexicalized vs. shared for relative positions) is not specified.
- Unclear methodology: how left/right neighbor embeddings are used to create representations (and whether this only affects max-pooling) is not well explained.
- Design choice unclear: appending one-word-before/after trigger embeddings seems arbitrary and needs justification.
- Presentation issue: event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is not clearly used later; only v_{sent+lex} appears in subsequent sections.
- Feature encoding unclear: incorporation of pairwise features (binary vs. non-binary like distance) and whether they are trainable during training is not described.
- Suggested extension: evaluate/apply the approach to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with more prior work.
- Novelty/question: use of a square function as the nonlinearity is unusual and its rationale or broader applicability is unclear.
- Reproducibility/request: authors should consider releasing the ACE++ dataset or at least compare baselines on it for fair evaluation.
- Statistical rigor: include significance testing for reported results since some numbers are close.
- Related work omission: Wiseman et al. (2015) neural coreference approach should be cited and discussed.
- Minor issues: redundant word "that" at line 143.
- Minor inconsistency: a baseline is labeled "same type" in Table 6 but referred to as "same event" in the text (line 670).
