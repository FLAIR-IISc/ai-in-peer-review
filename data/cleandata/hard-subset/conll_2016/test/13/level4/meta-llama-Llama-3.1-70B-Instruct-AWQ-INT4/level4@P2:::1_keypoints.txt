- Paper proposes a CNN-based event linking model that creates mention vectors via word embeddings + CNN + max-pooling and scores coreference with a single-layer NN using concatenated features.  
- Evaluated on ACE (and an expanded version), achieving performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems.  
- Main contribution: neural approach combining word embeddings with additional linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient.  
- Demonstrates that added linguistic features improve performance without requiring manually crafted external resources.  
- Strength: novel use of CNNs to capture sentential features for event linking.  
- Strength: model attains competitive results relative to previous systems.  
- Strength: relies on limited linguistic features, increasing practicality by avoiding heavy manual resources.  
- Weakness: relies on gold trigger words, which may not be available in real-world settings.  
- Weakness: uses a different train/test split from prior work, complicating direct comparisons.  
- Weakness: some model details are unclear (e.g., generation/use of position embeddings and left vs. right neighbor handling).  
- Question: please detail how position embeddings are generated and incorporated.  
- Question: how will the model perform with predicted (non-gold) trigger words, and how will this be addressed?  
- Question: please clarify comparisons to prior work given differing train/test splits.  
- Additional suggestion: provide a more detailed comparison with prior studies and discuss implications of different train/test splits.  
- Additional suggestion: evaluate on other datasets (e.g., OntoNotes) to further validate generality.  
- Additional comment: the use of a square function nonlinearity is notable and could be discussed for novelty and wider applicability.
