- Proposes a CNN-based model for event linking that combines CNN-derived mention vectors with surrounding word embeddings and pairwise features to compute coreference scores.
- Achieves performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems on ACE and an expanded ACE++ dataset.
- Main contribution: a neural approach that combines word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features; shows embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Linguistic features used are limited and do not require manually crafted external resources.
- Experimental concern: uses gold trigger words, which may make comparisons unfair to systems using predicted triggers; authors should report results with predicted triggers.
- Experimental concern: inconsistent train/test splits across papers hinder fair comparison and reproducibility; authors should adhere to established splits.
- Unclear: the final claim about needing cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is not clearly explained.
- Unclear: position embeddings generation is vague—are they randomly initialized, lexicalized, or shared across positions?
- Unclear: how left/right neighbors are used to create representations (lines 307–311) and whether this only affects max-pooling is not explained.
- Unclear/arbitrary: appending one word before and after triggers seems arbitrary; rationale for this window size is missing.
- Unclear: the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is not clearly used later, where only v_{sent+lex} appears.
- Unclear: pairwise features in Section 3.2 are under-explained—how is distance encoded and are these features updated during training?
- Suggestion: evaluate/apply the approach to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with more prior work.
- Suggestion: discuss the choice and novelty of using a square function as nonlinearity and its potential wider applicability.
- Data/reproducibility: consider releasing ACE++ or at least compare feature-rich systems on it to facilitate benchmarking.
- Results: include statistical significance testing to substantiate comparisons.
- Related work: cite and discuss Wiseman et al. (2015) neural approach for (entity) coreference resolution.
- Minor issues: redundant "that" at line 143; inconsistency between "same type" (table 6) and "same event" (line 670).
