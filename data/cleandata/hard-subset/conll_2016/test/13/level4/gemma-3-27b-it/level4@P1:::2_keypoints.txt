- Paper is well-written and easy to follow; strong analyses of ACE corpus argument sharing and dataset-size effects motivate ACE++.
- Authors fail to motivate why CNNs were chosen for mention representations and do not compare to alternatives (e.g., RNNs); 2–3 F1 gain needs stronger architectural evidence.
- Novelty is unclear: using sentential CNN features for classification is not new; authors must situate and compare to existing continuous-space event-linking methods.
- Baseline selection (Table 3) is incomplete — more comprehensive comparisons to related methods are required.
- No information on how the ACE++ dataset was created or what event types it covers; this undermines claims about domain independence and limits reproducibility.
- Authors should demonstrate that prior methods fail where their approach succeeds and consider releasing ACE++ publicly.
- Missing evaluation without gold-standard event mentions in Table 3.
- Not explored whether off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) can augment or improve event linking; example in paper suggests potential gains.
- Model evaluated only on ACE; need experiments on other resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) and consideration of joint entity+event coreference.
- Pairwise feature effectiveness (Table 5) suggests feature engineering still matters; authors should report which features worked best, how features were selected, and what other features were tried.
