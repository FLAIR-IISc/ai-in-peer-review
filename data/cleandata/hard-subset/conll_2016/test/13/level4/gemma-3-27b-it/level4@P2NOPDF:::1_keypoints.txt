- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model: CNN+max-pool mention vectors concatenated with contextual embeddings and pairwise features fed to a single-layer NN, achieving competitive ACE2005/ACE++ results.  
- Main contribution: a neural event coreference model that combines word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features without heavy feature engineering or external resources.  

Experimental setup concerns:
- Uses gold trigger words rather than predicted triggers, which may make comparisons to other systems unfair.  
- Inconsistent train/test splits across prior work reduce reproducibility; authors should use established splits where possible.  

Clarification needed:
- The claim about the need for cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is unclear and needs elaboration.  
- Positional embeddings: unclear how they are generated (randomly initialized vs. learned, lexicalized or not) and why identical embeddings are used for the same relative positions regardless of surrounding words.  
- Role of left/right neighbor embeddings (lines 307–311) is unclear—do they only affect max-pooling or more?  
- Rationale for including only one word before/after the trigger (window size) appears arbitrary and needs justification.  
- Use of event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is unclear since later text seems to use only v_sent+lex.  
- Pairwise features: are binary features encoded as a binary vector, how are continuous features (e.g., distance) handled, and are these features fixed or learned during training?  

Suggestions and potential extensions:
- Evaluate applicability to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with broader prior work.  
- Investigate the squared nonlinearity choice for novelty and potential usefulness in other tasks.  
- Publicly release ACE++ or at minimum compare directly against feature-rich baselines on ACE++; perform statistical significance testing given small reported differences.  

Related work:
- Include and discuss Wiseman et al. (2015) neural coreference model in related work.  

Minor issues:
- Line 143: remove redundant “that.”  
- Table 6 vs. text inconsistency: “same type” (table) vs. “same event” (line 670) should be consistent.
