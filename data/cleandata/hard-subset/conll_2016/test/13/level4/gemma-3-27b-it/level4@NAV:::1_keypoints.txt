- Paper studies event linking with CNNs: word-embedding-based mention vectors + surrounding-word embeddings, combined with pairwise features to score coreference; achieves competitive results on ACE and ACE++.

- Main contribution: a neural event-linking method that integrates word embeddings with selected linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient and features can be incorporated without heavy manual engineering.

- Experimental concern: model uses gold trigger words rather than predicted triggers; results with predicted triggers are needed for fair comparison to competing systems.

- Experimental concern: inconsistencies in train/test splits across papers; authors should adhere to standard splits for comparability.

- Unclear: the argument for needing cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is not clearly stated.

- Unclear: how positional embeddings are generated—randomly initialized or lexicalized—and why the same embedding is used for a relative position regardless of adjacent words.

- Unclear: how left versus right neighbor embeddings are incorporated (lines 307–311)—does this only affect max-pooling or more of the architecture?

- Unclear: rationale for appending only the immediately preceding and following word embeddings to the trigger (why that window size).

- Unclear: role and usage of the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) versus v_sent+lex, since later sections seem to use only v_sent+lex.

- Unclear: how pairwise features are integrated—binary feature encoding vs. continuous features (e.g., distance), and whether these features are updated during training.

- Suggestion: evaluate applicability to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to broaden comparison with prior work.

- Suggestion: further investigate the use of a square activation/nonlinearity—its novelty and transferability to other tasks.

- Dataset/access concern: ACE++ is not public; releasing it would enable broader research and fairer comparisons; compare feature-rich systems on ACE++ as well.

- Evaluation suggestion: perform statistical significance testing given close reported results.

- Related-work omission: include the neural coreference approach by Wiseman et al. (2015) in the related work.

- Minor edits: remove redundant "that" (line 143); fix Table 6 label inconsistency ("same type" vs. "same event").
