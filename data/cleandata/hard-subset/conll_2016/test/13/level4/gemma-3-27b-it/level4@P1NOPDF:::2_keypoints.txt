- Paper proposes event entity linking using sentential CNN features instead of external knowledge, with a two-part model (mention representation + pairwise coreference scoring).
- Review praises clarity, writing, and the ACE corpus analysis of argument sharing and dataset-size analysis motivating ACE++.
- Major concern: authors do not motivate or analyze why CNNs were chosen for mention representations.
- Major concern: no comparison to alternative architectures (e.g., RNNs); small 2–3 F1 gain needs stronger architectural evidence.
- Novelty concern: applying CNNs to sentential features for classification is not new; unclear what is novel about this event linking approach.
- Baselines issue: paper should explicitly compare to existing continuous-space event linking methods; Table 3’s method selection is incomplete.
- Dataset concern: ACE++ collection process and covered event types are not described, raising questions about domain bias and generality.
- Claim support: need examples showing earlier methods fail where this approach succeeds to substantiate domain-independence claims.
- Reproducibility suggestion: authors should consider releasing ACE++ to enable future comparisons.
- Minor: report model performance without gold references in Table 3.
- Minor: explore augmenting with standard coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) as baselines or components.
- Minor: evaluate on additional datasets (e.g., EventCorefBank) to strengthen claims and motivate combined entity/event coreference approaches.
- Minor: pairwise features are effective—report which features were most useful, rationale for feature selection, and other tried features.
