- Paper proposes an event entity linking model using sentential CNN features and trains in two stages: mention representation learning and pairwise coreference scoring.
- Paper is well-written, with valuable ACE corpus analysis on argument sharing and a useful dataset-size analysis motivating ACE++.
- Major concern: authors do not motivate or analyze why CNNs are chosen for mention representations or compare to alternatives (e.g., RNNs); a 2–3 F1 gain is insufficient evidence of architectural superiority.
- Major concern: novelty is unclear since applying CNNs to classification is not new; the paper should explicitly compare to existing continuous-space event linking methods and expand baselines beyond those in Table 3.
- Major concern: ACE++ dataset collection methodology is not described; authors must list covered event types, justify claims about domain-independence, demonstrate prior methods’ failures where theirs succeeds, and consider releasing the dataset.
- Minor: report model performance without gold references in Table 3.
- Minor: explore augmenting the model with standard coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) as these can provide useful mention linking signals.
- Minor: evaluate on additional resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) and consider joint entity-event coreference due to ACE’s small size.
- Minor: Table 5 indicates pairwise feature importance—identify which features were most effective, explain feature selection rationale, and report other explored features.
