- Paper proposes CNN-based event linking: CNNs on word embeddings + max-pooling produce mention vectors combined with lexical and pairwise features to score coreference.  
- Primary contribution: competitive neural event linking that minimizes reliance on external semantic resources.  
- Secondary contribution: thorough experimental analysis including error analysis that elucidates strengths and weaknesses.  

Strengths:
- Strong performance on ACE and ACE++ without external KBs, making the approach domain- and resource-agnostic.  
- Well-designed two-stage architecture (event representation via CNNs, then coreference decision) with sensible use of context and feature integration.  
- Comprehensive evaluation comparing to state-of-the-art and analyzing feature/clustering impacts.  

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation uses gold event triggers, limiting realism and comparability to end-to-end systems that predict triggers.  
- Inconsistent dataset splits across literature reduce fairness and reproducibility of comparisons.  
- Several design choices (e.g., one-word context window, squared activation) lack clear justification or ablation support.  

Questions to authors:
- Please detail position embedding implementation: initialization and learning procedure.  
- Is v_e actually used in the system or was it removed post-design; if removed, why?  
- How is the distance pairwise feature encoded (discrete vs. continuous, scaling/normalization)?  
- Do you plan to release the ACE++ dataset to the community?
