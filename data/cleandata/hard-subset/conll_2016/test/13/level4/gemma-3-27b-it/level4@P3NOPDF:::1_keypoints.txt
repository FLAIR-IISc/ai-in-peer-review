- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that builds mention vectors via CNN+max-pooling, concatenates context embeddings, and combines pairwise features with a single-layer NN; matches prior feature-rich systems on ACE 2005/ACE++.

- Main contribution: integrates word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient and avoiding heavy external resources.

- Experimental concern: evaluation uses gold trigger words instead of predicted triggers; should report results with predicted triggers for fair comparison.

- Experimental concern: inconsistent train/test splits across papers harm comparability and reproducibility; use established splits.

- Unclear: the claim about the need for cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) requires clarification.

- Unclear: positional embeddings description is vague—are they randomly initialized, lexicalized, and why are position embeddings independent of the referenced word?

- Unclear: how left/right neighbor representations (lines 307–311) interact with the max-pooling operation is not explained.

- Unclear: rationale for appending only one word before/after the trigger (window size choice) is arbitrary and needs justification.

- Unclear: role of the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is unclear since later sections use v_sent+lex instead.

- Unclear: handling of pairwise features (binary encoding, treatment of continuous distance feature, and whether features are fixed or learned) is not specified.

- Suggestion: evaluate the approach on entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with a broader set of prior work.

- Suggestion: discuss the novelty and potential benefits of using the square function as a nonlinearity and its applicability to other tasks.

- Dataset suggestion: release ACE++ publicly or at least include comparisons of feature-rich systems on ACE++ for better benchmarking.

- Evaluation suggestion: perform statistical significance testing because many reported results are close.

- Related work: cite and discuss Wiseman et al. (2015) neural coreference approach.

- Minor issues: remove redundant “that” (line 143) and use consistent terminology in Table 6 / line 670 (“same type” vs “same event”).
