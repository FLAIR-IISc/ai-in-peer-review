- Proposal: model for event entity linking using sentential features from CNNs, with components for mention representation and pairwise coreference scoring.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of argument sharing in ACE corpus.  
- Strength: dataset-size analysis motivates creation of the ACE++ dataset.  
- Major concern: authors do not justify choice of CNNs or compare to alternative architectures (e.g., RNNs).  
- Major concern: modest 2–3 F1 gains warrant stronger evidence that the proposed architecture is superior.  
- Novelty concern: using sentential features and CNNs is not clearly novel; lack of focused comparison to continuous-space event-linking methods.  
- Evaluation concern: selection of baselines (Table 3) appears incomplete and lacks comprehensive comparison.  
- Dataset concern: insufficient detail on construction and event types in ACE++; potential bias from limited original ACE event types.  
- Claim support: authors must demonstrate cases where their model outperforms resource-dependent prior methods and justify the claim that their approach is less domain-specific.  
- Reproducibility: authors should strongly consider releasing the ACE++ dataset to enable comparisons.  
- Minor suggestions: report results without gold references, explore augmenting with standard coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP), evaluate on additional datasets (e.g., EventCorefBank), and provide more detail on pairwise feature selection and most impactful features.
