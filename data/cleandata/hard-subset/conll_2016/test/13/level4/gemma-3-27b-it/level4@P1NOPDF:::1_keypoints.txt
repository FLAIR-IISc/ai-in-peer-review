- Models event linking with CNNs: word embeddings → CNN + max-pooling → concatenated with surrounding embeddings and pairwise features → single-layer NN for similarity and coreference scoring, matching feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution: a neural approach combining word embeddings with a small set of linguistic features; pure embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Uses gold trigger words rather than predicted triggers; requests results with predicted triggers for fairer comparison to competing systems.
- Different train/test splits from prior work raise comparability concerns; should adhere to established splits.
- The claim about the need for cross-sentential information (final statement in second paragraph) is unclear and needs clarification.
- Unclear how positional embeddings are generated—randomly initialized or lexicalized—and why the same relative position would share embeddings across different words.
- Unclear how left/right neighbor embeddings are used to create the representation (lines 307–311) and whether this only affects max-pooling.
- Appending only the immediate preceding and following words to the trigger representation seems arbitrary; rationale for this choice is needed.
- Unclear how the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is used, since subsequent sections appear to use only v_sent+lex.
- Pairwise feature encoding ambiguous: how is distance represented and are these features updated during training or kept fixed?
- Suggest evaluating whether the approach generalizes to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with broader prior work.
- The squared function used as a nonlinearity is interesting; inquire whether this is novel and broadly applicable.
- ACE++ dataset is not publicly available—request release of ACE++ or at least comparisons of feature-rich systems on it.
- Reported result differences are small; recommend significance testing to support comparisons.
- Related work omission: cite Wiseman et al. (2015) on neural approaches to (entity) coreference resolution.
- Minor typo: redundant “that” on line 143.
- Inconsistent baseline naming: “same type” in table 6 vs. “same event” in text (line 670).
- Reference to include: Wiseman, Rush, Weston, & Shieber (2015), ACL 2015.
