- Paper proposes a neural model for event linking using CNN-derived word-embedding representations concatenated with surrounding word embeddings and pairwise features, achieving performance comparable to feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution: neural event linking that combines word embeddings with limited linguistic features; embeddings alone are insufficient for strong performance.
- Experimental concern: authors use gold trigger words rather than predicted ones, which may make comparisons to systems using predicted triggers unfair.
- Experimental concern: differing train/test splits across papers; authors should use established splits when possible.
- Unclear: the final statement about the necessity of cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is not clear.
- Unclear: how position embeddings are generated (“similar to word embeddings”) — initialization, lexicalization, and semantics of relative-position embeddings are unspecified.
- Unclear: how left vs right neighbors are used in creating representations (lines 307–311); does this only affect max-pooling?
- Unclear: appending only the single word before and after the trigger seems arbitrary — rationale for this window size is not given.
- Unclear: role and usage of the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is unclear since later sections use only v_{sent+lex}.
- Unclear: how pairwise features (mostly binary) are encoded (e.g., distance feature), and whether these features are fixed or trained.
- Suggestion: consider applying the approach to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with a broader set of prior work.
- Question: the use of a square function as a nonlinearity is novel and interesting — authors should discuss its generality and potential for other tasks.
- Dataset issue: ACE++ is not public; authors should release it or at least compare feature-rich systems on ACE++ to aid reproducibility.
- Results issue: many reported numbers are close; statistical significance testing should be reported.
- Related work: cite and discuss neural coreference approaches such as Wiseman et al. (2015).
- Minor: Line 143 has a redundant “that”; inconsistent naming of a baseline (“same type” vs “same event” in Table 6/text).
