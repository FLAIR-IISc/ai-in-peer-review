- Paper proposes event entity linking using sentential CNN features with separate components for mention representations and pairwise coreference scoring.  
- Well-written and clearly presented; insightful analysis of argument sharing in ACE and useful investigation of dataset-size effects motivating ACE++.  
- Insufficient justification for choosing CNNs: no analysis of pros/cons or comparison to alternatives (e.g., RNNs), yet only modest (2–3 F1) gains are shown.  
- Novelty is unclear because CNNs for classification are standard; authors must compare explicitly to existing continuous-space event-linking methods and expand baselines in Table 3.  
- ACE++ dataset construction is not described; missing details on event types and potential ACE bias—authors should document methods, provide failure cases of prior approaches, and release ACE++.  
- Report model performance without gold references in Table 3 to give a realistic evaluation.  
- Explore augmenting the model with off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) to handle pronoun linking and strengthen baselines.  
- Validate on additional resources (e.g., EventCorefBank) given ACE’s small size to establish robustness and support simultaneous entity/event coreference claims.  
- Table 5 shows pairwise features are highly influential; authors should identify the most effective features, justify the chosen feature set, and describe features considered but discarded.
