- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model that creates event mention vectors via CNN + max-pooling, concatenates surrounding context embeddings, and scores coreference with a single-layer network plus pairwise features; achieves performance comparable to feature-rich systems on ACE and ACE++.
- Main contribution: a neural event linking approach combining word embeddings with a limited set of linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Linguistic features used are limited in scope and do not rely on extensive manually-built external resources.
- Experimental limitation: uses gold trigger words rather than predicted triggers, which may make comparisons to competing systems unfair.
- Experimental concern: inconsistencies in train/test splits across papers; authors should adhere to established splits for comparability.
- Unclear statement in lines 65–70 about the need for cross-sentential information despite convincing quantitative evidence.
- Positional embeddings description is vague: unclear whether they are randomly initialized or lexicalized, and why the same embedding is assigned to a relative position regardless of adjacent word.
- The method for incorporating left/right neighbor embeddings (lines 307–311) is unclear—does it only affect max-pooling?
- Appending only the single word before and after the trigger seems arbitrary; motivation for this window size is not justified.
- Usage of event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is unclear since later sections reference only v_sent+lex.
- Pairwise feature implementation needs elaboration: how continuous features (e.g., distance) are encoded, and whether features are fixed during training.
- Suggest evaluating applicability to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to enable broader comparisons.
- The use of a squared function as a nonlinearity is unusual; authors should clarify novelty and potential usefulness in other tasks.
- ACE++ dataset is not public; releasing it or comparing feature-rich systems on ACE++ would improve reproducibility and evaluation.
- Results are numerically close; statistical significance testing is recommended to validate comparisons.
- Related work omission: cite the neural coreference approach by Wiseman et al. (2015).
- Minor edits: remove redundant "that" (line 143) and reconcile terminology inconsistency between “same type” (Table 6) and “same event” (line 670).
