- Paper proposes CNN-based sentential features and a two-part model for event mention representation and pairwise coreference scoring.  
- Manuscript is well-written and the analyses of argument sharing and dataset size (motivating ACE++) are valuable.  
- Authors do not justify choosing CNNs over alternatives (e.g., RNNs) or provide comparative experiments, despite only marginal (2–3 pt) F1 gains.  
- Novelty is unclear because CNN/continuous-space approaches for similar tasks exist; the method must be explicitly contrasted with prior work and compared more thoroughly.  
- ACE++ dataset collection methodology and covered event types are not described, undermining claims about domain-agnostic coverage and comparability.  
- Authors must demonstrate that prior methods fail on ACE++ while their approach succeeds, and publicly release ACE++ for reproducibility.  
- Table 3 should report model performance without gold references to give a realistic baseline.  
- Explore integration with off-the-shelf coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) to strengthen baseline analysis.  
- Evaluate on additional corpora (e.g., EventCorefBank) to test robustness and justify simultaneous entity-event coreference modeling.  
- Clarify the role of pairwise features (which features mattered, selection process, and alternatives), since Table 5 suggests feature engineering remains critical on ACE-scale data.
