- Summary: Proposes a two-stage, resource-agnostic event coreference model using CNN-derived sentential representations plus lexical/pairwise features, achieving SOTA on ACE and released ACE++.

- Contribution: Resource-agnostic approach that avoids external knowledge bases while maintaining state-of-the-art performance.

- Contribution: Ablation and error analyses show sentential features are essential and identify pronoun resolution as a major failure mode.

- Contribution: Public release of an expanded ACE++ dataset to improve data volume and reproducibility.

- Strength: Empirical analysis shows 23% of event mentions require cross-sentential resolution, highlighting a previously overlooked challenge.

- Strength: Ablation study demonstrates all feature classes are necessary, with a 13.8% F1 drop when sentential features are removed.

- Strength: ACE++ expands ACE (1950 vs. 599 docs) and yields an 8.0% absolute F1 improvement, supporting scalability claims.

- Weakness: No justification or comparison for using CNNs vs. sequence models (RNNs/LSTMs); marginal SOTA gains (2–3 F1) make this choice unclear.

- Weakness: Novelty is undercut by prior neural/CNN relation/event extraction work; key continuous-space baselines are omitted from comparisons.

- Weakness: ACE++ construction/annotation details (event type coverage, guidelines) are missing, weakening generalization and dataset-claim support.

- Question: What additional event types are included in ACE++ and how do they address ACE’s category bias?

- Question: Could integrating an external coreference system (e.g., CoreNLP) mitigate the model’s pronoun-resolution errors?

- Question: What criteria guided selection of pairwise features and which alternative features were considered or discarded?
