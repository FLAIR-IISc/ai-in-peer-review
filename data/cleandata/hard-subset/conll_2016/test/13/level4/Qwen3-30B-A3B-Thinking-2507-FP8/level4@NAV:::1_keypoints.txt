- Model: CNN-based event mention representations concatenated with contextual embeddings and pairwise features produce a coreference score via a single-layer NN.  
- Evaluation: Tested on ACE and proprietary ACE++, achieving performance comparable to prior feature-rich systems.  
- Contribution: Proposes a neural event linking approach that fuses word embeddings with minimal linguistic features; embeddings alone are insufficient.  

Experimental setting concerns:
- Uses gold trigger words; must report results with predicted triggers for fair comparison to systems using predicted triggers.  
- Train/test splits are inconsistent with prior work; authors should adopt standard splits where possible.  

Unclear points requiring clarification:
- Final claim about cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is unclear and needs rewording or justification.  
- Position embeddings: unclear initialization/derivation (random, lexicalized, or other) and why relative position embeddings should be consistent across different words.  
- Handling of left/right neighbors (lines 307–311): clarify whether this affects only max-pooling or other parts of the model.  
- One-word context window (appending one word before/after triggers) appears arbitrary; justify this design choice.  
- Event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is ambiguous since later sections use v_sent+lex; clarify differences and usage.  
- Pairwise features (Section 3.2): explain binary feature encoding, distance feature treatment, and whether features are fixed or updated during training.  

Other issues and suggestions:
- Discuss applicability to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to enable broader comparisons.  
- Explain the novel square nonlinearity and its potential use beyond this task.  
- ACE++ dataset not publicly released; share ACE++ or at least report feature-rich baselines on ACE++ for reproducibility.  
- Report statistical significance for marginal performance differences.  
- Related work omission: cite Wiseman et al. (2015) on neural coreference resolution.
