- Proposes a CNN-based event linking model: mention vectors from word embeddings + CNN max-pooling, augmented with one-word context embeddings and pairwise features, scored by a single-layer NN.  
- Main contribution: shows a neural model that integrates embeddings with minimal, non-manual linguistic features and that embeddings alone are insufficient.  

- Experimental validity concern: use of gold trigger words (not predicted) undermines fair comparison to baselines that use predicted triggers; results with predicted triggers are needed.  
- Experimental validity concern: inconsistent train/test splits (ACE vs. ACE++) harm reproducibility; authors should adhere to established splits.  

- Unclear: Position embeddings—initialization and rationale (shared relative positions vs. context-specific) are not specified.  
- Unclear: Neighbor representation—ambiguous whether left/right neighbor handling affects only max-pooling or the input representation.  
- Unclear: Contextual embedding choice—appending exactly one word before/after trigger seems arbitrary and needs justification or ablation.  
- Unclear: Event representation ve is introduced but not used later; its purpose/integration must be clarified.  
- Unclear: Pairwise feature handling—encoding of non-binary features (e.g., distance), and whether they are trainable or fixed, is omitted.  

- Suggestion: Extend approach to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with neural baselines like Wiseman et al. (2015).  
- Suggestion: Justify/unpack the unusual square nonlinearity and discuss broader applicability beyond event linking.  
- Suggestion: Release ACE++ dataset (or at least report baselines on it) to enable reproducibility and fair benchmarking.  
- Suggestion: Report statistical significance (e.g., t-tests) for marginal performance differences.  

- Minor: Fix wording redundancy (line 143) and standardize terminology ("same event" vs. "same type" in Table 6).  
- Minor: Add citation to relevant neural coreference work (Wiseman et al., 2015).  

- Conclusion: Method is plausible and promising, but methodological gaps (gold triggers, splits), unclear technical details, and reproducibility issues must be addressed before publication.
