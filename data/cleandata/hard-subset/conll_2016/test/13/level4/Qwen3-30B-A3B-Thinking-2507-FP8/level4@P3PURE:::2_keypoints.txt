- Paper proposes CNN-based sentential features for event entity linking with a two-part model: mention representation and pairwise coreference scoring.  
- Manuscript is well-written and includes useful analysis of argument sharing and dataset-size motivation for ACE++.  
- Major concern: no justification for choosing CNNs over alternatives (e.g., RNNs) or empirical comparison, especially given only 2–3 F1 gains.  
- Major concern: novelty unclear since sentential/CNN-based approaches exist; authors must contrast with prior continuous-space event linking methods and expand Table 3 comparisons.  
- Major concern: ACE++ dataset collection methodology is not described; event-type coverage and potential domain bias must be specified to support claims of broader coverage.  
- Major concern: need evidence that prior methods fail on ACE++ while the proposed model succeeds, and consideration of publicly releasing ACE++ for reproducibility.  
- Minor: Table 3 should report performance without gold event mentions to assess real-world utility.  
- Minor: Authors should consider augmenting or comparing with standard coreference systems (e.g., CoreNLP) as a stronger baseline.  
- Minor: Evaluate on additional datasets (e.g., EventCorefBank) to validate robustness beyond ACE’s small size.  
- Minor: Table 5 suggests pairwise features are highly effective—authors should clarify which features mattered most, how features were selected, and what alternatives were tested.
