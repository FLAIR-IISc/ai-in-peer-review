- Proposes a CNN-based neural model for event linking that combines CNN-derived mention vectors with contextual embeddings and pairwise features, scoring coreference with a single-layer NN; achieves comparable results to feature-rich systems on ACE and ACE++.

- Main contribution: shows a neural framework integrating word embeddings with limited linguistic features without external resources, and that embeddings alone are insufficient.

- Using gold trigger words is justified but weakens comparability to baselines that use predicted triggers; report results with predicted triggers too.

- Inconsistent train/test splits across studies undermine comparability; the authors should use standard splits where possible.

- The claim about the necessity of cross-sentential information is convincing but the final statement (lines 65–70) is unclear.

- Position embeddings for relative positions are underspecified: unclear initialization/learning and why positions relative to different words would share embeddings.

- Handling of left vs. right neighbors (lines 307–311) is ambiguous — clarify whether this only affects max-pooling or other processing.

- Appending exactly one word before/after triggers seems arbitrary; justify choice or evaluate different window sizes.

- The event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is introduced but not used later; clarify its role versus v_sent+lex.

- Pairwise features are underdescribed: specify how distance is encoded (continuous or binned) and whether features are fixed during training.

- Suggest evaluating extension to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with established baselines like Wiseman et al. (2015).

- The use of a square function as a nonlinearity is noted but not contextualized; clarify novelty and generalizability to other tasks.

- ACE++ is not publicly available; release would aid reproducibility, and ACE++ results should be compared to feature-rich baselines if kept private.

- Small reported performance differences require statistical significance testing to support improvement claims.

- Related work should include Wiseman et al.’s neural coreference approach (ACL 2015) alongside other entity coreference literature.

- Minor: remove redundant "that" at line 143.

- Minor: make terminology consistent — Table 6’s "same type" vs. line 670’s "same event."

- Reference to include: Wiseman, Rush, Weston & Shieber (2015), ACL.
