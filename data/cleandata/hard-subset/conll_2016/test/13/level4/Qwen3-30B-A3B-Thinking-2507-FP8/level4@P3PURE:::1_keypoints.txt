- Paper models event linking with CNN-based mention representations plus surrounding-word embeddings and pairwise features to score coreference, evaluated on ACE and ACE++ with competitive performance.
- Main contribution: a neural event-linking approach combining word embeddings and limited linguistic features; embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Experimental concern: evaluation uses gold trigger words; results with predicted triggers are needed for fair comparison to some baselines.
- Experimental concern: inconsistent train/test splits across prior work; authors should follow established splits where possible.
- Unclear: the concluding claim about the necessity of cross-sentential information (lines 65–70) is not clearly stated or justified.
- Unclear: how position embeddings are generated — random, lexicalized, or normalized across triggers — is not specified.
- Unclear: the mechanism for handling left vs. right neighbors (lines 307–311) is ambiguous — does this only affect max-pooling?
- Unclear: appending one word before and after the trigger appears arbitrary and lacks justification.
- Unclear: the event-mention representation v_e (line 330) is introduced but not clearly used later; only v_sent+lex is referenced.
- Unclear: pairwise features (Section 3.2) lack detail — are binary features concatenated as a vector, how are continuous features (e.g., distance) handled, and are they fixed during training?
- Suggestion: method could be extended to entity coreference (e.g., OntoNotes) to compare with established datasets and prior work.
- Question: using a square function as nonlinearity is unusual — is this novel and does it generalize beyond this task?
- Reproducibility: ACE++ dataset is not public; authors should release it or at least report feature-rich baseline results on ACE++.
- Evaluation: close numerical differences in results require statistical significance testing to support claims.
- Related work: should cite Wiseman et al. (2015) for neural coreference resolution.
- Minor: line 143 contains a redundant "that."
- Minor: inconsistency between Table 6 ("same type") and text (line 670, "same event")
