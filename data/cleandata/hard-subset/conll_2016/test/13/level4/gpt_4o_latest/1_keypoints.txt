- Proposal: CNN-based event linking that generates mention vectors via CNN+max-pooling, concatenates surrounding word embeddings and pairwise features, and scores coreference with a single-layer NN; matches prior feature-rich systems.
- Main contribution: introduces a neural method combining word embeddings with minimal linguistic features, showing embeddings alone are insufficient.
- Experimental concern: evaluation uses gold trigger words rather than predicted ones, making comparison to baselines that use predicted triggers less equitable.
- Experimental concern: authors use different train/test splits than prior work; they should adhere to established splits for consistency.
- Unclear claim: the statement that cross-sentential information is necessary is supported numerically but a concluding sentence (lines 65–70) is unclear.
- Unclear method: how position embeddings are generated (“similar to word embeddings”) — random initialization or lexicalized — is not specified, and sharing embeddings across different words is unexplained.
- Unclear method: how left/right neighbor words are incorporated (lines 307–311) — whether they only affect max-pooling or more — is unclear.
- Design choice questioned: appending one word before and after the trigger seems arbitrary; rationale for this specific context window is not provided.
- Representation clarity: the role of the event-mention representation ve (line 330) is unclear since later sections focus on vsent+lex without mentioning ve.
- Feature encoding unclear: how pairwise features are represented (binary vectors?), how distance is encoded, and whether these features are fixed during training need clarification.
- Suggestion: consider extending the approach to entity coreference resolution to compare with more prior work and datasets like OntoNotes.
- Question: using a square function as a nonlinearity is interesting — is this novel and might it generalize to other tasks?
- Dataset availability: ACE++ is not public; authors should state plans to release it and compare feature-rich systems on it to aid future comparisons.
- Evaluation rigor: many reported results are close; statistical significance testing should be performed to strengthen claims.
- Related work: add mention of neural coreference resolution by Wiseman et al. (2015).
- Minor copy edits: remove redundant "that" at line 143.
- Minor consistency issue: baseline label mismatch — "same type" in Table 6 vs. "same event" in text (line 670) should be reconciled.
- Reference noted: Wiseman, Rush, Weston, and Shieber (ACL 2015) on learning anaphoricity and antecedent ranking features for coreference.
