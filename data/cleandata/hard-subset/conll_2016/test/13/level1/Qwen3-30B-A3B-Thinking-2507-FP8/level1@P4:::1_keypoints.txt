- Proposes a neural event-linking model using only sentential CNN features, removing reliance on external semantic resources.  
- Model uses two CNN steps: generate event-mention representations, then make coreference decisions with lexical/pairwise features.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on ACE 2005 and a newly created, publicly available ACE++ dataset.  
- Contribution: novel model achieving SOTA without external semantic features.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that CNN-derived sentential features suffice for high-quality event linking.  
- Contribution: error analysis identifying challenges (e.g., pronoun resolution) for future work.  
- Strength: removes dependency on limited-coverage external resources (WordNet/FrameNet), increasing domain applicability.  
- Strength: comprehensive ablation study showing sentential features are critical and pairwise features alone are weak.  
- Strength: thorough error analysis noting specific failure modes and that 22% of false positives appear plausible to humans.  
- Weakness: comparisons to prior work are methodologically inconsistent due to differing splits and preprocessing, hindering fair evaluation.  
- Weakness: insufficient CNN architecture and training details (filter sizes, counts, hidden dims, training regime, compute) for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: ACE++ creation lacks annotation/process details and analysis of distributional differences, raising dataset-bias concerns.  
- Weakness: paper does not clearly explain why sentential CNN features outperform external semantic features.  
- Question: How do ACE++ web documents compare to ACE 2005 in annotation consistency and domain characteristics?  
- Question: Please provide full CNN architecture and hyperparameter details to enable reproducibility.  
- Question: How could the model be modified to better handle pronoun-based event coreference?
