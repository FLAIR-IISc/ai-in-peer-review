- Proposal: use CNN-based sentential features for event coreference to avoid external semantic resources, combining per-mention CNN representations with pairwise scoring; reports SOTA on two datasets and highlights pronoun resolution errors.

- Contribution: demonstrates high-performance event coreference without external semantic resources, enabling domain-agnostic application.

- Contribution: provides an ablation study showing sentential, trigger-local lexical, and pairwise features each materially improve performance.

- Contribution: offers error analysis that identifies pronoun resolution as a major challenge and direction for future work.

- Strength: strong empirical motivation showing limitations of external resources (ACE statistics: cross-sentence, argumentless pairs, differing triggers).

- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art metrics compared to prior work (e.g., Liu et al. 2014) without using 3rd-party semantic features.

- Strength: ablation results convincingly demonstrate the necessity of each feature class; removing sentential features notably degrades performance.

- Strength: thorough error analysis quantifies pronoun-related false negatives and annotator-disagreement cases, aiding interpretation.

- Weakness: SOTA claim is weakened by inconsistent train/test splits and preprocessing differences across compared systems.

- Weakness: lacks comparison to other neural architectures (e.g., RNNs, recursive nets) and justification for choosing CNNs.

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset creation/annotation procedures are under-specified, raising questions about data quality and consistency.

- Weakness: paper omits discussion of computational efficiency and training/deployment costs relative to prior methods.

- Weakness: identifies pronoun resolution as a key issue but provides no concrete ideas or extensions to address it.

- Question: Have you compared CNNs to RNNs/recursive networks for this task, and why choose CNNs?

- Question: How were the 1,351 ACE++ web documents annotated and aligned with ACE 2005 guidelines?

- Question: How confident are you that performance gains stem from the model rather than differing splits or preprocessing?

- Question: What preliminary approaches do you envision to integrate pronoun resolution into the current architecture?
