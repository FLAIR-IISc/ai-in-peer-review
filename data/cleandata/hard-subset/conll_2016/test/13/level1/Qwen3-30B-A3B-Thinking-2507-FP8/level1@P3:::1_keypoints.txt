- Proposes a CNN-based event coreference model that uses sentential features instead of external semantic resources and releases the ACE++ dataset.
- Claims three contributions: CNN-generated sentential features, state-of-the-art performance without external semantics, and a larger ACE++ dataset.

- Eliminating reliance on external semantic resources is practically significant and achieves comparable BLANC F1 to prior work.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: ablation studies, error analysis, multiple metrics, and justified use of gold event mentions.
- The ACE++ dataset (1950 docs) is a valuable resource and shows model gains with more training data.

- The model struggles with pronoun resolution (17% of false negatives) and the paper lacks proposals to address this limitation.
- Comparisons are outdated, omitting more recent neural coreference models (e.g., Lee et al., Clark & Manning, BERT-based approaches).
- The choice of CNNs is insufficiently justified over other architectures (LSTMs, attention-based models) and lacks discussion of alternatives.

- How would the model be extended to handle pronoun resolution, and what features or architectural changes are proposed?
- How does the approach compare to recent neural/coreference models using contextual embeddings like BERT, and would sentential features still help?
- Why are pre-trained Word2Vec embeddings acceptable while other external semantic resources are deemed undesirable?

- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to address pronoun resolution, update comparisons to recent neural models, and better justify architectural choices; ACE++ and practical contribution support acceptance.
