- Proposed a two-stage event coreference model using CNN-extracted sentential features to avoid external semantic resources.
- Demonstrated state-of-the-art performance without domain-specific semantic resources and analyzed feature contributions.
- Performed error analysis identifying key challenges (notably pronoun resolution) and directions for future work.

- Addresses practical limitation of prior work by removing dependency on domain-specific external resources, justified by ACE statistics.
- Strong empirical validation with SOTA results on ACE and ACE++, ablation studies showing feature contributions, and clustering strategy analysis.
- Insightful error analysis highlighting pronoun resolution and annotation inconsistency issues with concrete examples.
- Clear model architecture and rationale for sentential features, supported by figures and statistics on long-distance coreference.

- Comparison to prior work is weakened by experimental inconsistencies and differing preprocessing/annotations across systems.
- Claim of not using external semantic resources is misleading because pre-trained word embeddings were used.
- Insufficient justification that the neural approach is necessary compared to potentially stronger non-neural lexical methods.
- Lacks evidence or discussion of cross-domain transfer and robustness beyond ACE/ACE++.
- Omits analysis of computational complexity and runtime compared to previous methods.

- How would the model be modified to handle pronoun resolution (specific architectural or training changes)?
- How do the authors differentiate acceptable external resources (e.g., word embeddings) from unacceptable ones (e.g., WordNet); what criteria define permissibility?
- How is the model expected to perform on completely different domains (e.g., medical text) without adaptation?
