- Paper proposes a two-stage neural model for within-document event coreference using position-aware sentence-level CNNs and a learned pairwise similarity scorer with transitive-closure clustering.  
- Claims SOTA or competitive results on ACE 2005 and a larger ACE++ corpus while avoiding external semantic resources.

Main contributions:
- Demonstrates sentence-level CNN representations plus light pairwise features can reach SOTA-level event coreference without external KBs.  
- Presents a simple two-step architecture (position embeddings, piecewise pooling, squared nonlinearity) with ablations showing component effects.  
- Introduces a larger ACE++ dataset for training/analysis and provides an error analysis highlighting difficult phenomena.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results with minimal external resources, supporting portability to new domains.  
- Model design is well-motivated for the task (trigger-focused position embeddings, piecewise pooling, squared similarity).  
- Thorough ablations and comparisons (BestLink vs transitive closure) clarify where gains arise.  
- Transparent experimental details (training, hyperparameters, metrics) and published ACE split IDs.  
- Insightful error analysis pointing to pronoun-based references and annotation inconsistencies.

Weaknesses:
- Reliance on gold annotations (triggers, possibly arguments/modality) may overestimate real-world performance and comparability.  
- Comparisons are weakened by differing data splits and omitted metrics (no CEAF or CoNLL average).  
- Limited methodological novelty—largely adapts existing CNN encoders and standard pairwise/clustering approaches.  
- ACE++ is under-described (guidelines, IAA, availability) despite being central to claims.  
- Clustering uses fixed 0.5 threshold and transitive closure with no learned/global clustering or threshold sensitivity analysis.  
- Class imbalance (90% negative links) handling during training is not described (no sampling/weighting/PR analysis).

Questions to authors:
- Are argument-overlap and modality features based on gold annotations or predicted outputs?  
- Will ACE++ be released, and can you provide annotation guidelines, IAA, and dataset composition?  
- How sensitive are results to the 0.5 clustering threshold and was it tuned on validation?  
- Did you try learned/global clustering methods (e.g., agglomerative with learned linkage)?  
- How did you handle class imbalance during training (weighting, negative sampling, threshold tuning)?  
- Can you report ablations removing argument-overlap, modality, and “antecedent in first sentence” features and provide CEAF/CoNLL scores?

Additional comments:
- Report end-to-end performance with predicted triggers to show practical relevance.  
- Compare the squared activation to alternatives (absolute value, bilinear similarity).  
- Provide qualitative cases showing when sentential features compensate for different triggers or missing arguments.
