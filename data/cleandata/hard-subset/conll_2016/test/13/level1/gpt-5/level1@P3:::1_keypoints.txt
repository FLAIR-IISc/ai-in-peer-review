- Summary: Tackles within-document event coreference without external KBs via a sentence-level CNN mention encoder + pairwise scorer using engineered pairwise features, clustering by transitive closure, evaluated on ACE2005 and ACE++.

- Contribution 1: Shows CNN-learned sentential features can substitute for external KB-dependent semantic features, achieving SOTA/competitive ACE results.

- Contribution 2: Provides careful ablations isolating sentential, trigger-local, and pairwise features, clustering strategies, and training-data effects.

- Contribution 3: Builds a larger ACE++ dataset and analyzes gains from more data (dataset not publicly released).

- Strength: Strong empirical results with reduced reliance on external resources, competitive especially on BLANC and potentially more portable across domains.

- Strength: Clear, modular architecture (position embeddings, piecewise pooling) inspired by relation extraction that effectively encodes trigger-centric sentence context.

- Strength: Thorough ablations and analyses show sentential features add substantial value; useful comparisons of clustering methods and data-size effects.

- Strength: Detailed dataset characterization and error analysis highlight issues like pronominal event anaphora and annotation ambiguities.

- Strength: Practical and simple implementation using standard embeddings and straightforward pairwise scoring + clustering.

- Weakness: Pairwise features (event type, realis, argument overlap) appear to rely on gold annotations, likely inflating performance and reducing real-world comparability.

- Weakness: Limited novelty beyond applying a CNN encoder and heavy reliance on engineered pairwise features; no joint modeling of triggers and arguments or document-level encoding.

- Weakness: Sentence-local encoder may miss long-distance links (>5 sentences); gains for such links likely driven by non-neural pairwise features.

- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns: ACE++ not released, annotation details and IAA not reported, and some training/hyperparameter details are underspecified.

- Weakness: Clustering is heuristic (transitive closure); no global inference or learned clustering, and conclusions may be threshold-dependent.

- Q: Are event types, realis, and argument spans/roles gold or predicted? Please report results with predicted values and an ablation without these features.

- Q: How was ACE++ annotated (who, guidelines, IAA) and will it / splits be released?

- Q: Provide exact hyperparameters (dw, dp, dc, n, de, dsim), regularization, early stopping, and sensitivity to random seeds.

- Q: Can you report bucketed performance by trigger identity agreement, type agreement, argument overlap, and sentence distance to show where sentential features help?

- Q: Why use a squared activation in the similarity layer versus element-wise product/abs-diff + MLP? Any empirical comparison?

- Q: Please include CEAF_e/phi4 and CoNLL F1 and report cluster properties (avg cluster size, singleton rate).

- Suggestion: Augment encoder with argument-aware representations (attention to entity mentions or dependency paths) to internalize argument compatibility without gold features.

- Suggestion: Consider document-level encoders (hierarchical BiLSTMs / transformers) to capture long-distance links and pronominal event anaphora.

- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost and candidate pruning since pairwise scoring is O(n^2) in mentions per document.
