- Paper tackles within-document event coreference using a neural mention-pair model with CNN-learned sentential features instead of external KBs.

- Architecture: sentence-level event-mention representations (word + position embeddings, CNN, piecewise max-pooling, hidden layer), pairwise features, squared-similarity scoring, logistic regression, transitive-closure clustering.

- Experiments on ACE2005 and an expanded ACE++ dataset show state-of-the-art or comparable results; ablations examine sentential feature contributions.

- Main contribution 1: Shows CNN-based sentential features can match prior KB-dependent systems on ACE, improving domain portability.

- Main contribution 2: Ablation demonstrates sentential features provide complementary signal beyond trigger-local and pairwise features.

- Main contribution 3: Empirical comparison of clustering strategies shows classifier strength makes BestLink unnecessary compared to transitive closure in this setting.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance without external semantic resources, reducing engineering and portability concerns.

- Strength: Modeling choices are data-motivated (many coreferent pairs differ in triggers/arguments and are far apart), justifying sentence-level context and piecewise pooling.

- Strength: Careful ablations and baseline coverage isolate feature contributions and provide informative naive baselines.

- Strength: Useful error analysis identifying pronoun event anaphora and annotation ambiguity as major failure modes.

- Strength: Implementation and training details (TensorFlow, hyperparameter search) improve reproducibility relative to similar NN work.

- Weakness: Reliance on gold annotations for pairwise features (types, modality/realis, arguments) weakens the resource-free claim and end-to-end applicability.

- Weakness: ACE++ dataset is insufficiently described and apparently not publicly released, limiting reproducibility and impact.

- Weakness: Limited comparison set and mismatched experimental settings (different splits/preprocessing) complicate attributing gains to sentential features alone.

- Weakness: Model uses local pairwise decisions with transitive closure, lacking global/document-level inference to correct over/under-merging.

- Weakness: Some modeling choices (squared similarity, piecewise pooling) are under-ablated and statistical significance/variance over seeds is not reported.

- Questions for authors: Which pairwise features use gold annotations and can results with predicted types/arguments be reported?

- Questions for authors: Will ACE++ be released with guidelines and agreement stats, or can splits/statistics be shared for reproducibility?

- Questions for authors: Please provide ablations for similarity activation choices and pooling variants, plus variance/significance tests.

- Questions for authors: How sensitive is performance to CNN window size, position embeddings, and word-embedding fine-tuning?

- Questions for authors: Any results with predicted triggers/arguments to assess end-to-end robustness vs KB-heavy systems?

- Overall assessment: Moderately novel and well-motivated with valuable empirical evidence, but dependence on gold annotations and unreleased ACE++ limit practical impact; reviewer is positive but cautious.
