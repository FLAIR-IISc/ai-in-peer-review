- Paper proposes event linking without external KBs by encoding mentions with sentence-level CNNs, trigger-local features, a learned similarity module with pairwise cues, and transitive-closure clustering.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art BLANC F1 on ACE and competitive results on other metrics; ablations on ACE++ show sentential features are crucial.  

Contributions:
- Shows sentential CNN features plus lightweight pairwise cues can match/exceed prior KB-dependent methods, improving portability.  
- Provides systematic analyses: dataset diagnostics, ablations separating sentential vs lexical vs pairwise features, and clustering strategy comparisons.  
- Presents a simple, practical architecture including a learned multi-dimensional similarity with square activation that is easy to deploy.  

Strengths:
- Strong performance without WordNet/FrameNet, suggesting broader applicability where KBs are unavailable.  
- Convincing ablations and dataset diagnostics that motivate modeling choices (e.g., many positive pairs lack shared arguments or have different triggers).  
- Informative clustering study showing BestLink can hurt recall here and that classifier calibration matters.  
- Meaningful error analysis identifying pronominal events and annotation inconsistencies as major error sources.  
- Reproducibility-friendly: clear training setup, hyperparameters, published ACE splits, and use of pre-trained embeddings.  

Weaknesses:
- Relies on gold triggers (and likely gold arguments) limiting ecological validity; no end-to-end evaluation with predicted mentions.  
- Comparability issues: differing ACE splits/preprocessing and no significance testing make SOTA claims less definitive.  
- Sentence-only encoding may miss discourse cues needed for long-distance links.  
- Transitive-closure clustering can propagate errors and over-merge due to lack of global/joint inference.  
- Insufficient details on ACE++ release/annotation quality, limiting verification and impact.  

Questions for authors:
- Are pairwise features computed from gold arguments, and how does performance change with predicted triggers/arguments?  
- Will ACE++ be released, and what are its annotation guidelines and quality controls?  
- Were results tested for statistical significance and sensitivity to thresholds or the square activation?  
- Can the model incorporate multi-sentence/document context without external resources, and does that help long-distance cases?
