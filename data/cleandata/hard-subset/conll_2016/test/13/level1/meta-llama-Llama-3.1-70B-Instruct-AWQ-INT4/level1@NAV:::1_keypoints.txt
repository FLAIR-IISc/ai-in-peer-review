Summary: Proposes an event-linking model that uses CNN-derived sentential features plus local features (no external semantic features) and achieves state-of-the-art results on two datasets.

Contribution 1: Novel application of CNN-based sentential features for event linking, an approach not previously explored for this task.

Contribution 2: Empirically achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, demonstrating effectiveness.

Contribution 3: Claims improved robustness to domain change by avoiding reliance on external semantic features.

Strength 1: Effectively leverages CNNs to generate useful sentential representations for event linking.

Strength 2: Provides a robust evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art systems across two datasets.

Strength 3: Includes error analysis that identifies limitations and points toward future work.

Weakness 1: Limited generalizability due to evaluation on only two datasets that may not cover diverse domains.

Weakness 2: Lacks interpretability—no detailed analysis of the learned sentential features or how they drive predictions.

Weakness 3: Heavy dependence on hyperparameter tuning to reach strong performance, which may limit practical applicability.

Question 1: Request for more details on the learned sentential features and their role in prediction.

Question 2: Inquiry about performance on additional datasets and plans to evaluate more diverse text types.

Question 3: Ask about limitations of CNNs for sentential feature generation and whether other neural architectures might perform better.
