- Proposes CNN-generated sentential features for event linking, trained on ACE and ACE++, achieving state-of-the-art results.  
- Contribution: Uses CNN-based sentential features to capture contextual information for event-mention representations.  
- Contribution: Introduces a two-step architecture: generate event-mention representations, then determine coreference.  
- Contribution: Achieves SOTA on ACE and ACE++ without relying on external feature sources.  
- Strength: Novel use of CNN sentential features improves contextual representation of event mentions.  
- Strength: Two-step architecture is well-designed and effectively leverages generated representations for coreference.  
- Strength: Empirically strong — state-of-the-art performance on two datasets.  
- Strength: Provides a thorough evaluation and error analysis.  
- Weakness: Model performance depends heavily on the quality of generated event-mention representations.  
- Weakness: No use of external feature sources, which may limit performance across domains/datasets.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models.  
- Weakness: Error analysis highlights problems but offers no clear remedies.  
- Question: How will pronoun resolution errors be addressed?  
- Question: How does the model handle event mentions that span different sentences or paragraphs?  
- Question: How can the model be extended to cross-document event linking?  
- Question: Provide more detail on hyperparameter tuning and selection.  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and promising, but reliance on representation quality and absence of external features may limit applicability.
