- Proposes an event coreference model that removes reliance on external semantic resources by using CNN-based sentential feature representations plus lexical and pairwise features.
- Uses a two-step architecture combining sentential and pairwise features to compute coreference scores.
- Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive performance on ACE 2005 and ACE++ datasets.
- Provides a detailed error analysis highlighting challenges like pronoun resolution and annotation inconsistencies.
- Contribution: model design that maintains strong performance without WordNet/FrameNet.
- Contribution: empirical results demonstrating robustness across multiple metrics and datasets.
- Contribution: thorough error analysis identifying future research directions.
- Strength: independence from external semantic resources improves domain portability.
- Strength: strong empirical performance compared to prior systems that use external features.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including multiple datasets, metrics, clustering strategies, and ablations.
- Weakness: CNN choice for sentential features lacks novelty and justification versus RNNs/transformers.
- Weakness: poor handling of pronoun resolution with no concrete remediation proposed.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to ACE and ACE++, lacking more diverse or cross-document datasets.
- Weakness: occasional unclear delineation between novel contributions and prior work.
- Questions: Why were CNNs chosen over recurrent/transformer models and were alternatives explored?
- Questions: How does the model perform for cross-document coreference and would it generalize to such settings?
- Questions: Could discourse-level features or pre-trained models (e.g., BERT) improve pronoun resolution, and were they considered?
- Overall comment: Strong contribution showing external resources aren’t necessary for SOTA, but needs better pronoun resolution and broader evaluation.
