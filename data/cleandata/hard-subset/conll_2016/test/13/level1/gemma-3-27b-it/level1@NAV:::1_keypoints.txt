- Proposes event linking using CNN-extracted sentential features without external knowledge bases, combined with lexical/pairwise features and a similarity function.
- Main contribution: demonstrates competitive event linking can be achieved without WordNet/FrameNet, improving portability and domain-independence.
- Architectural contribution: thoughtful integration of CNN sentential representations with traditional features and justified design choices (e.g., dual max-pooling, trigger-local feature handling).
- Empirical result: achieves state-of-the-art on ACE and competitive performance on ACE++, with accompanying error analysis.
- Strength: Eliminates reliance on external resources, broadening applicability across domains.
- Strength: Well-motivated architecture grounded in preliminary analyses and related work.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance supporting the approach’s effectiveness.
- Strength: Insightful error analysis identifying concrete challenges (pronoun resolution, annotator disagreement).
- Weakness: Limited novelty in applying CNNs for sentence representation; main novelty is in integration within the pipeline.
- Weakness: Dataset size (ACE) is small, raising questions about generalizability; ACE++ helps but larger diverse corpora would strengthen claims.
- Weakness: Clustering choice (rejecting BestLink in favor of transitive closure) needs deeper justification and analysis.
- Question: Why did BestLink clustering perform worse here — was it sensitive to hyperparameters or other setup differences?
- Question: How might the model be extended to better handle pronoun resolution (e.g., incorporating entity-level coreference)?
- Question: What is the computational cost of this model, especially relative to approaches using many external features?
