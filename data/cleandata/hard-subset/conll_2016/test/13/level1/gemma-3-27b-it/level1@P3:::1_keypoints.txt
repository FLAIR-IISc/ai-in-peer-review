- Novel application of CNNs to learn event representations directly from sentence context, avoiding reliance on external knowledge bases and improving domain portability.
- Combines sentential CNN features with lexical and pairwise features, explicitly incorporating trigger-local and sentence-level information.
- Strong empirical validation: state-of-the-art results on two datasets, ablation studies, and error analysis identifying pronoun resolution as a key challenge.

- Tackles domain dependence of prior event linking methods by reducing external KB reliance.
- Achieves SOTA performance on both datasets and uses a public dataset to aid reproducibility.
- Well-designed two-stage architecture (learn event representations, then coreference decision) with max-pooling to handle multiple mentions.
- Manual error analysis is insightful, comparing annotator disagreements to model errors and highlighting concrete failure modes.
- Clear, well-organized presentation that makes the approach and experiments easy to follow.

- Limited novelty in the CNN component itself; innovation is primarily in the application rather than new CNN architecture.
- Lacks comparisons to strong recent baselines using pre-trained contextual models (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa).
- Still relies on handcrafted pairwise features, limiting the claimed move toward end-to-end learning.
- Clustering strategy (transitive closure vs. BestLink) is not fully justified and may contribute to errors.
