- Paper proposes an event linking system using CNNs to generate sentential features, combining those representations with lexical/pairwise features, and reports SOTA results plus error analysis.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results — achieves state-of-the-art on two datasets including ACE.  
- Strength: Novel feature engineering — effective use of CNNs (positional embeddings, max-pooling) to avoid reliance on external knowledge.  
- Strength: Comprehensive error analysis identifying key failure modes (e.g., pronoun resolution, annotation issues).  
- Strength: Thorough experimental setup with ablation studies, baseline comparisons, and clustering strategy analysis.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning (search space and selection criteria not described).  
- Weakness: Dataset bias and generalizability concerns due to heavy reliance on the small, domain-specific ACE corpus.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to recent neural architectures (e.g., transformers) and limited coverage of recent related work.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to clustering strategy (BestLink vs. transitive closure) with unclear justification for chosen approach.  
- Question: Request for detailed hyperparameter tuning methodology and search space.  
- Question: Have newer architectures like transformers been explored and how do they compare?  
- Question: Why did BestLink perform worse on ACE++, and were there specific error patterns causing this?  
- Question: What challenges are expected when adapting the model to datasets with different event types or domains?  
- Additional: Figure 1 is helpful, writing is clear, dataset code link appreciated, but related work could more fully cover recent neural advances.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
