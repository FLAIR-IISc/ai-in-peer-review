- Paper proposes an event linking system using CNN-derived sentential features from context, avoiding external knowledge bases.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates competitive event linking performance without external semantic resources (WordNet/FrameNet).  
- Secondary contribution: empirically validates CNN-based sentential features for capturing relevant context.  
- Provides thorough experimental evaluation and error analysis, establishing a useful benchmark.  

- Strength: addresses practical limitation of resource-dependent systems, increasing portability and applicability.  
- Strength: solid experiments on ACE and ACE++ with standard metrics and informative ablation studies.  
- Strength: detailed error analysis highlighting pronoun resolution issues and comparison to human annotation discrepancies.  
- Strength: clear model description and architecture presentation (CNN layers, feature concatenation, similarity function).  

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—CNN usage for sentence representation is not novel and architecture choices are under-justified.  
- Weakness: small dataset (ACE, 599 docs) raises generalization concerns; impact of dataset size and ACE++ results need more discussion.  
- Weakness: clustering findings (transitive closure > BestLink) are not fully analyzed; reasons for BestLink’s degradation are unexplored.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to more recent transformer-based baselines (e.g., BERT).  

- Question: what is the computational cost compared to methods using extensive external features?  
- Question: have you considered adding coreference resolution preprocessing to address pronoun resolution errors?  
- Question: why did BestLink hurt performance—what characteristics of the model/output explain this?  
- Question: have you tried replacing CNNs with transformer encoders (BERT) and how do results compare?  

- Additional: ACE document list link is useful.  
- Additional: related work coverage is adequate but should emphasize recent neural event extraction/linking advances.  
- Additional: typographical error—“intermin-gles” should be “intermingles.”
