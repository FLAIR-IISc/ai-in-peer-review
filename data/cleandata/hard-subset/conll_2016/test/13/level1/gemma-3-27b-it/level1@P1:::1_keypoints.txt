- Primary contribution: CNN-based sentential features enable competitive event linking without external knowledge bases.  
- Secondary contribution: Empirical analysis of feature combinations, clustering strategies, and released dataset splits for reproducibility.  
- Strength: Eliminates reliance on external resources (WordNet/FrameNet), improving portability across domains.  
- Strength: Effective use of CNNs with position embeddings to encode sentential context for event coreference.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results — state-of-the-art on ACE and competitive on ACE++; informative ablation studies.  
- Strength: Thorough experimentation and comparisons to baselines; brief error analysis highlights model weaknesses.  
- Weakness: CNN architecture offers limited novelty; contribution is mainly application of existing techniques.  
- Weakness: Error analysis is shallow (only 100 FPs and 100 FNs) and lacks quantitative breakdown of error types.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness; Table 2 is hard to parse.  
- Weakness: Dataset limitations — ACE and ACE++ are small and domain-specific, limiting generalizability.  
- Question: Why use a square non-linearity for polarity invariance — how does polarity matter for event similarity?  
- Question: Can authors provide quantitative distribution of error types (e.g., pronoun resolution vs. annotator disagreement)?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to different word embedding initializations or to fine-tuning embeddings?  
- Question: Any initial experiments or plans to incorporate discourse-level information (discourse parsing/RST)?  
- Additional comment: Improve formatting/readability of Table 2.  
- Additional comment: Fix inconsistent citation style.  
- Additional comment: Qualify the "state-of-the-art" claim given dataset and experimental setup limitations.  
- Additional comment: Clarify implementation/details of transitive closure clustering and tie-breaking for merges.
