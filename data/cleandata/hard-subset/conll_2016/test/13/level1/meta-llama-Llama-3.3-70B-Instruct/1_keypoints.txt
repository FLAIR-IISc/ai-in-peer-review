- Proposes a two-part neural model (CNN-based sentential features + pairwise decision model) for event coreference without external semantic features.
- Uses convolutional neural networks to generate latent sentential representations instead of hand-crafted semantic features.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, one of which is publicly available.
- Presents an error analysis that identifies key challenges and suggests directions for future work.
- Clear and detailed model description that facilitates understanding and replication.
- Thorough experimental evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art systems and ablation of feature and clustering choices.
- Error analysis provides valuable insights into model limitations and hard cases.
- Lacks a detailed comparison to other neural models for event coreference to situate its contributions.
- Relies on pre-trained word embeddings, which may limit applicability across languages and domains.
- Does not discuss potential applications beyond event coreference resolution.
- Asks how authors will address pronoun resolution, a major error source identified.
- Requests more details on hyperparameter tuning and model sensitivity to settings.
- Inquires how the model can be extended to other languages or domains without pre-trained embeddings.
