- Proposes a two-stage neural model for within-document event coreference: a CNN to encode sentential context + local trigger features, then a pairwise NN to score coreference; evaluated on ACE2005 and a new ACE++ dataset.  
- Contribution: demonstrates effective KB-independent event linking by learning sentential features with a CNN, improving domain portability.  
- Contribution: introduces a larger ACE++ dataset (adds 1351 documents) to boost training data for event linking.  
- Contribution: provides comprehensive empirical evaluation, including SOTA comparisons, ablation studies, and detailed error analysis.  

- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art BLANC F1 (73.31% on ACE) without external semantic resources, outperforming prior feature-rich systems.  
- Strength: Well-motivated two-part architecture; ablation shows sentential CNN features markedly improve performance (e.g., to 76.90% on ACE++).  
- Strength: Thorough experimental analysis—shows benefits of more data, compares clustering strategies, and provides useful error analysis (pronouns, annotation issues).  

- Weakness: Lacks crucial details about ACE++ creation (document sources, annotation guidelines, inter-annotator agreement), undermining reproducibility and interpretability of gains.  
- Weakness: Limited architectural novelty—CNN design and techniques (position embeddings, piecewise pooling) closely follow prior work; main novelty is application to event linking.  
- Weakness: State-of-the-art claim is ambiguous due to differing data splits/preprocessing and lack of a strong non-neural baseline on the same splits.  

- Question: Please provide ACE++ provenance, annotation protocol, and inter-annotator agreement statistics.  
- Question: Please justify the use of the square activation in the similarity layer and report comparisons to alternatives (e.g., ReLU, element-wise product/difference).  
- Question: Does the model handle pronominal event mentions, and could incorporating entity coreference help address pronoun-related failures?
