- Presents a neural model for event coreference that replaces external KB features with CNN-learned sentential representations.  
- Primary contribution: shows sentence-level neural features can match feature-rich systems and achieve strong results on ACE 2005.  
- Secondary contribution: introduces a larger ACE++ dataset and provides a thorough ablation study.  
- Strength: Reduces dependency on domain-specific external resources, improving portability to new domains.  
- Strength: Rigorous evaluation with comparisons to multiple baselines, ablations isolating sentential/local/pairwise feature contributions, and clustering strategy analysis.  
- Strength: ACE++ dataset and error analysis highlight remaining challenges (pronoun resolution, annotation ambiguities) and guide future work.  
- Weakness: Limited architectural novelty—CNN setup largely follows prior sentence-level relation models without clear new architectural innovations.  
- Weakness: SOTA claim is ambiguous due to differing data splits/preprocessing and underperformance on the B-CUBED metric.  
- Weakness: Non-standard square activation in the similarity layer is under-justified and lacks supporting quantitative comparisons.
